Rosa rivalis

Rosa rivalis, commonly known as the "rival rose," is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America, particularly found in the eastern United States and parts of Canada. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces red-orange hips in the fall.

Rosa rivalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
