Rubus schleicheri microgene caeruleicaulis

Rubus schleicheri microgene caeruleicaulis, commonly known as the blue-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green stems and dark, glossy leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally grown in gardens and agroforestry systems.
