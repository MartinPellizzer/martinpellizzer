Racosperma niveum

Racosperma niveum, commonly known as the white-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Racosperma niveum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
