Rosa omeiensis f. aculeatoeglandulosa

Rosa omeiensis f. aculeatoeglandulosa, commonly known as the hairy-fruited rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa omeiensis species.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and glandular hairs, which are distinctive features of its morphology. The plant typically produces pink to red flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
