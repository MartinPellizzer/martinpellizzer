Rumex montevidensis

Rumex montevidensis, commonly known as the Uruguayan sorrel, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common sight in moist, temperate environments.

The species is also valued for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor similar to other sorrel species.
