Rubus × phaneronothus

Rubus × phaneronothus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a result of crossbreeding between Rubus ursinus and Rubus glaucus, combining traits from both parent species. It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
