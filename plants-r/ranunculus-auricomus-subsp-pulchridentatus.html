Ranunculus auricomus subsp. pulchridentatus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

pulchridentatus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals that are often adorned with a distinctive white or greenish hue.

The plant has trifoliate leaves that are palmately divided, with serrated edges and a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both ornamental value and ecological benefits.
