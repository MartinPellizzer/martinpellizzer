Rubus schmidelyanus subsp. teretiusculus

Rubus schmidelyanus subsp.

teretiusculus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
