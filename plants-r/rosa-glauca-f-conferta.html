Rosa glauca f. conferta

Rosa glauca f. conferta, commonly known as the dense blue rose, is a compact shrub with densely packed, semi-evergreen foliage.

Its flowers are typically pale blue to white, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its short, wiry stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy bloom. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens.

This form of Rosa glauca is particularly hardy and adaptable, making it suitable for a variety of growing conditions.
