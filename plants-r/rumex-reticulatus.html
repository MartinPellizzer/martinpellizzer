Rumex reticulatus

Rumex reticulatus, commonly known as the net-veined dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, with both male and female flowers present on the same plant.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
