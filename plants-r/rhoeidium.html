Rhoeidium

Rhoeidium, commonly known as the waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves.

Its leaves are spirally arranged around the stem, resembling the spokes of a wheel, which helps in maximizing light absorption. The plant is adapted to slow-moving or still freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Rhoeidium is also notable for its ability to trap and retain water, which aids in its survival in wetland habitats.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing shelter for small aquatic organisms.
