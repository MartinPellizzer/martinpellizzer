Rhynchosia clivorum subsp. pycnantha

Rhynchosia clivorum subsp.

pycnantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved climbing bean, is a perennial leguminous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets.

The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, while the pods are slender and contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a forage plant.
