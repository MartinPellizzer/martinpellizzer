Rubus × rhodanthemus

Rubus × rhodanthemus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus hirtellus, resulting in a shrub that exhibits characteristics of both parents. It is native to the western United States and is known for its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in coastal and woodland environments.
