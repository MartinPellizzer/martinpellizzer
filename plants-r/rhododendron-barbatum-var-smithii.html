Rhododendron barbatum var. smithii

Rhododendron barbatum var. smithii, commonly known as the Smith's rosebay rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features dense, leathery foliage with elliptical leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray on the underside. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes in suitable climates.
