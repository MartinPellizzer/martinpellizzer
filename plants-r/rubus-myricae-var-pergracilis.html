Rubus myricae var. pergracilis

Rubus myricae var. pergracilis, commonly known as the slender blackberry bush, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, dark berries that ripen in the fall. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild flavor in its fruit.
