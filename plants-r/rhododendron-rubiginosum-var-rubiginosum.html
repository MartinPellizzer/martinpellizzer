Rhododendron rubiginosum var. rubiginosum

Rhododendron rubiginosum var. rubiginosum, commonly known as the rusty rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shady, humid environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, with a distinctive reddish or rusty underside.

The rusty rhododendron is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as part of natural landscapes.
