Rosa alpina var. bourgaeana

Rosa alpina var. bourgaeana, commonly known as the Alpine rose or Bourgogne rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a shrub that typically grows in alpine environments, characterized by its hardy nature and ability to thrive in cold climates. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of white to grayish-white hairs. Its flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
