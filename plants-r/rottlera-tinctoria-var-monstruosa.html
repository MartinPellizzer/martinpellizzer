Rottlera tinctoria var. monstruosa

Rottlera tinctoria var. monstruosa, commonly known as the giant rottlera, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, making it a dramatic addition to tropical gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is often variegated with shades of green, yellow, and white, adding visual interest.

Due to its size and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in warm, humid climates where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
