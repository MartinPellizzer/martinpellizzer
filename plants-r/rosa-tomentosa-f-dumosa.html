Rosa tomentosa f. dumosa

Rosa tomentosa f. dumosa, commonly known as the woolly rose or shrub rose, is a compact, thorny shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage and small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces oval, red-orange hips that are edible and contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a hedge.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
