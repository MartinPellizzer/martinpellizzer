Rosa inaltata

Rosa inaltata, commonly known as the tall rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and its stems are typically covered in thorns.

Rosa inaltata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
