Rhododendron rhombicum

Rhododendron rhombicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are often borne on long, slender pedicels.

Rhododendron rhombicum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific ecological niches.
