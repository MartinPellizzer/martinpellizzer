Rhamnus arguta var. betulifolia

Rhamnus arguta var. betulifolia, commonly known as the dwarf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by black, spherical berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The species is notable for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
