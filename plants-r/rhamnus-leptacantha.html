Rhamnus leptacantha

Rhamnus leptacantha, commonly known as the slender-fruited buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its narrow, spiny stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
