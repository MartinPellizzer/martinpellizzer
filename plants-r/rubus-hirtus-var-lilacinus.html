Rubus hirtus var. lilacinus

Rubus hirtus var. lilacinus, commonly known as the lilac raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically pink to reddish in color, with a tart flavor. The plant features trifoliate leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its flowers are usually pale pink or white, adding ornamental value to the landscape.
