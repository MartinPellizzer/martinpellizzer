Rosa dumalis var. verticillacantha

Rosa dumalis var. verticillacantha, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, which helps it withstand harsh alpine conditions. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, providing insulation against cold temperatures.

This variety is well adapted to rocky soils and high altitudes, making it a resilient and ornamental plant in specialized garden settings.
