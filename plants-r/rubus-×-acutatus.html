Rubus × acutatus

Rubus × acutatus, commonly known as the brambleberry or dewberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, usually white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically small, tart, and have a high acidity. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable species within the Rubus genus.
