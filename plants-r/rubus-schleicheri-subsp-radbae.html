Rubus schleicheri subsp. radbae

Rubus schleicheri subsp.

radbae, commonly known as the Radbae blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically clustered together.

The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its fruit is valued for its tart flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
