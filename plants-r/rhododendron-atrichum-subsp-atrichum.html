Rhododendron atrichum subsp. atrichum

Rhododendron atrichum subsp.

atrichum, commonly known as the purple rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal. It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and pink.

The leaves are typically elliptical, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous forest understories.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
