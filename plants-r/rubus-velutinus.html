Rubus velutinus

Rubus velutinus, commonly known as the velvet raspberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
