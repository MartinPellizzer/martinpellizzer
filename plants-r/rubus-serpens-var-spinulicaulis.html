Rubus serpens var. spinulicaulis

Rubus serpens var. spinulicaulis, commonly known as the spiny-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native range.
