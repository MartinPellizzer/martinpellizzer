Robinia melanocarpa

Robinia melanocarpa, commonly known as black locust, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a feathery appearance. The tree produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in racemes during the spring, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. Its bark is thick, fissured, and grayish in color, providing a distinctive appearance.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and hard, heavy wood, which makes it valuable for construction and furniture-making.
