Rubus crataegifolius f. xanthocarpus

Rubus crataegifolius f. xanthocarpus, commonly known as the yellow-fruited bramble, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to edible berries, which are typically orange to red in color. Its fruit is notable for its high vitamin C content and is often used in local culinary traditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
