Rosa afzeliana var. brevipedunculata

Rosa afzeliana var. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stalked shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its relatively short peduncles, which distinguish it from other varieties of Rosa afzeliana. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and adaptability to various climates.
