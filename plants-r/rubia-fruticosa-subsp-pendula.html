Rubia fruticosa subsp. pendula

Rubia fruticosa subsp.

pendula, commonly known as the hanging bugleweed, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in sunny or partially shaded locations. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also serve as a habitat for various insects.

The species is part of the Rubiaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants.
