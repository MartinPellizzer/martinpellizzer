Rhynchospora tenuiseta

Rhynchospora tenuiseta, commonly known as the slender-hair sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
