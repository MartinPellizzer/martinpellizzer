Rosa blanda var. acicularioides

Rosa blanda var. acicularioides, commonly known as the slenderleaf rock rose, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, often hairy stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that give it its common name and help it thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. It is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in gardens.
