Rubus podocarpus

Rubus podocarpus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas and is characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin.

Rubus podocarpus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
