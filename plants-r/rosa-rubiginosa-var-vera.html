Rosa rubiginosa var. vera

Rosa rubiginosa var. vera, commonly known as the Turkish rose or Damask rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that produces fragrant, pink to red flowers with five petals. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of rose essential oils.

The flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.
