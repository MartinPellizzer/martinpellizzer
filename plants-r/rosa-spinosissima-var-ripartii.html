Rosa spinosissima var. ripartii

Rosa spinosissima var. ripartii, commonly known as the Ripartii prickly rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is well adapted to harsh, arid environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

This variety is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
