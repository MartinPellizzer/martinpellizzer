Ranunculus simpsonii

Ranunculus simpsonii, commonly known as Simpson's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. It has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, and its stems are often hairy.

Simpson's buttercup is a short-lived perennial that thrives in cool, temperate climates.
