Ranunculus auricomus subsp. modestipilosus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

modestipilosus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features showy yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often adorned with a distinctive orange or reddish hue at the base.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are typically hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for garden cultivation and ecological studies.
