Racosperma dietrichianum

Racosperma dietrichianum, commonly known as the Grey Box, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has greyish bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, yellow flowers in dense clusters, followed by elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Racosperma dietrichianum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
