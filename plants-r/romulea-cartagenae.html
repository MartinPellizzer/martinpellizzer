Romulea cartagenae

Romulea cartagenae, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in exotic flora.
