Rosa lutea var. discolor

Rosa lutea var. discolor, commonly known as the yellow rose with variegated leaves, is a cultivar of the Rosa lutea species.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to orange flowers and distinctive variegated foliage that features white and green patterns. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique leaf coloration and delicate floral display.
