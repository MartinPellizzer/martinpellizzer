Rubus leptodytus

Rubus leptodytus, commonly known as the slenderleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, tart berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a finely serrated edge.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many other species of brambles and blackberries.
