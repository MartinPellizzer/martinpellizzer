Ranunculus acris var. alpestris

Ranunculus acris var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb with basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
