Ranunculus auricomus subsp. adunans

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

adunans, commonly known as the golden crowncup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a distinctive greenish center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Ranunculaceae family.
