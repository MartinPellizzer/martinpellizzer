Rubus purpuratus microgene rufispinus

Rubus purpuratus microgene rufispinus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a species of bramble within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of numerous small drupelets. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the berries are bright red when mature.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and has been cultivated for both culinary and commercial purposes.
