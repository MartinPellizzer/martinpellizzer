Rubus ulmifolius proles peduncularis

Rubus ulmifolius proles peduncularis, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves with serrated edges, and small white or pink flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color in late summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a common and adaptable plant in temperate regions.
