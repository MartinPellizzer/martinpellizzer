Rosa officinalis

Rosa officinalis, commonly known as the officinal rose, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia and is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant flowers. The plant produces pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and serrated. Rosa officinalis is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and for the extraction of rose oil, which is used in perfumery and aromatherapy.

The plant also has historical uses in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
