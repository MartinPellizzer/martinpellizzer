Rubus × fulvescens

Rubus × fulvescens, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rubus genus in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many brambles. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations.
