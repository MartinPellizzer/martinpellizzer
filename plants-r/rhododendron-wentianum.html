Rhododendron wentianum

Rhododendron wentianum, commonly known as the Yunnan rhododendron, is a species native to China, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers, which range in color from pink to red, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral displays.
