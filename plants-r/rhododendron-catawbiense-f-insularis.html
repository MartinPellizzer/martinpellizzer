Rhododendron catawbiense f. insularis

Rhododendron catawbiense f. insularis, commonly known as the Catawba Island Rhododendron, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Appalachian Mountains of the United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and red during late spring. The plant is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and robust, upright growth habit. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun for optimal flowering.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
