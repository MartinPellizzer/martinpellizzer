Rhododendron degronianum var. intermedium

Rhododendron degronianum var. intermedium, commonly known as the Intermediate Rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a slightly pubescent underside. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland settings.
