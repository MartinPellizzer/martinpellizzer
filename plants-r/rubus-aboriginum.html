Rubus aboriginum

Rubus aboriginum, commonly known as the Western prickly pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western regions of North America, particularly California and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, a type of berry, is edible and has a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions.
