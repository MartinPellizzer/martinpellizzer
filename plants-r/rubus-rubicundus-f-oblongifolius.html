Rubus rubicundus f. oblongifolius

Rubus rubicundus f. oblongifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its elongated, oblong-shaped leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, dark red to black berries, which are typically edible. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows. Its botanical features include thorny stems and a dense growth habit, making it a hardy, adaptable species.

The leaves are usually serrated and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
