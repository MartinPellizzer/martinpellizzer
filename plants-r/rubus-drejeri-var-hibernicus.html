Rubus drejeri var. hibernicus

Rubus drejeri var. hibernicus, commonly known as the Hibernian blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus drejeri species native to northern regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and edible berries.
