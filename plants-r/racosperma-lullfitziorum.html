Racosperma lullfitziorum

Racosperma lullfitziorum, commonly known as the Lullfitz's acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and waxy leaf surfaces. It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen into the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.

Additionally, it is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
