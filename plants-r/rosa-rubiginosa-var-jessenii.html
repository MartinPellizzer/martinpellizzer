Rosa rubiginosa var. jessenii

Rosa rubiginosa var. jessenii, commonly known as the Jessen's rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces oval, green leaves with serrated edges, and its fruit is a red, hairy rose hip. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
