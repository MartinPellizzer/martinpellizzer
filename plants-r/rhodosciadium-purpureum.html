Rhodosciadium purpureum

Rhodosciadium purpureum, commonly known as the purple rhodes grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with purple or pink flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Rhodosciadium purpureum is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
