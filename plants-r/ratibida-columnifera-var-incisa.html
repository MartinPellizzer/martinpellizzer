Ratibida columnifera var. incisa

Ratibida columnifera var. incisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow-orange flower heads that are surrounded by narrow, tubular rays. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for pollinators and a popular choice for native gardens.
