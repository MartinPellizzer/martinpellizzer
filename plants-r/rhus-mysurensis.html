Rhus mysurensis

Rhus mysurensis, commonly known as the Mysore sumac, is a small deciduous tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the poison ivy and cashew. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are divided into several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by clusters of red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species.

Rhus mysurensis is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
