Ranunculus bupleuroides subsp. cherubicus

Ranunculus bupleuroides subsp.

cherubicus, commonly known as the cherubic buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with deeply lobed petals, typically appearing in early spring.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are covered in a soft, downy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to alpine gardens.
