Rubus rivularis var. grandibracteosus

Rubus rivularis var. grandibracteosus, commonly known as the large-bract raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink. The fruit, a aggregate berry, is edible and has a tart flavor.

It is characterized by its large, showy bracts that surround the flower cluster, contributing to its ornamental value.
