Rapanea sodiroana

Rapanea sodiroana, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic wood species. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its wood is valued for its fine grain and durability, making it useful in furniture-making.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.
