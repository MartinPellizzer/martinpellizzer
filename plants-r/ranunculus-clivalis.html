Ranunculus clivalis

Ranunculus clivalis, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, glandular stem. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are palmate, with a distinctive lobed structure, and it produces small, greenish sepals beneath the petals.

Ranunculus clivalis is a perennial species that blooms in late spring to early summer.
