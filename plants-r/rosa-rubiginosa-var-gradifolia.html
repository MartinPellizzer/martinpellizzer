Rosa rubiginosa var. gradifolia

Rosa rubiginosa var. gradifolia, commonly known as the creeping rose or the shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces oval, glossy leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its fruit is a red, fuzzy rosehip that contains seeds.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
