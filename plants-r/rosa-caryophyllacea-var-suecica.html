Rosa caryophyllacea var. suecica

Rosa caryophyllacea var. suecica, commonly known as the Swedish rose, is a shrub native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape and have a hairy underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a red, hairy hip that contains achenes, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and hardiness.
