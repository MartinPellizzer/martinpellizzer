Rudgea nodosa var. dupasquieri

Rudgea nodosa var. dupasquieri, commonly known as the golden star bush, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow, star-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically small, elliptical, and arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. This species thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant blooms and compact form, which make it a popular choice among gardeners.
