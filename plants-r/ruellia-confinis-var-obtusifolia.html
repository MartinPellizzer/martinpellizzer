Ruellia confinis var. obtusifolia

Ruellia confinis var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often broader at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its adaptability and attractive foliage make it a valuable addition to both tropical and subtropical landscapes.
