Ranunculus multicaulis var. multicaulis

Ranunculus multicaulis var. multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features a short, upright stem with a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves that are finely toothed. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with numerous stamens, typically blooming in early spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its foliage and flowers are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
