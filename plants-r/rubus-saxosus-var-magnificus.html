Rubus saxosus var. magnificus

Rubus saxosus var. magnificus, commonly known as the splendid rock raspberry, is a variant of the shrub Rubus saxosus, characterized by its striking floral and foliar features.

It is native to rocky, alpine regions and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red berries that are highly valued for their tart flavor and ornamental appeal. Its leaves are typically broad and serrated, with a deep green color that contrasts beautifully with its vibrant flowers.

This variant is noted for its resilience and ability to flourish in challenging environments, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
