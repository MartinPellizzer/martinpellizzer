Rhododendron cloiophorum subsp. leucopetalum

Rhododendron cloiophorum subsp.

leucopetalum, commonly known as the white-petaled rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive elliptical shape, while the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
