Rhododendron adenobracteum

Rhododendron adenobracteum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has a dense root system that helps stabilize soil in mountainous regions.
