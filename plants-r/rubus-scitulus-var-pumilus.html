Rubus scitulus var. pumilus

Rubus scitulus var. pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The berries are often used in jams and pies, though they are less commonly cultivated than other blackberry varieties.
