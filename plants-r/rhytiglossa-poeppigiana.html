Rhytiglossa poeppigiana

Rhytiglossa poeppigiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The velvetleaf is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
