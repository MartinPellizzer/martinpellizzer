Rhamnus disticha

Rhamnus disticha, commonly known as the silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and small, elliptical leaves that are typically silver-white on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Rhamnus disticha thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
