Rabdosia rivularis

Rabdosia rivularis, commonly known as the water rabdosia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest understories. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rabdosia rivularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
