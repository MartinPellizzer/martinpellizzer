Ricinocarpos ledifolius

Ricinocarpos ledifolius, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features alternate, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers are borne in umbels and bloom during the rainy season.

The plant is known for its toxic properties, containing compounds that can be harmful to livestock and humans.
