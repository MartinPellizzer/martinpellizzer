Ranunculus auricomus subsp. crassitruncus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

crassitruncus, commonly known as the broad-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with five broad, rounded petals and a distinctive golden center.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of soft, white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
