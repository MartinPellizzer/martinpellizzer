Rhipsalis trigona

Rhipsalis trigona, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its segmented, branching stems that resemble leaves. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the holiday season. Rhipsalis trigona thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooms make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
