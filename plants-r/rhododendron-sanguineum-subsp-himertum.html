Rhododendron sanguineum subsp. himertum

Rhododendron sanguineum subsp.

himertum, commonly known as the Himertum rhododendron, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and red.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
