Rhamnus rubra subsp. nevadensis

Rhamnus rubra subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

The plant has small, oval leaves that are usually gray-green in color, and it produces yellowish-white flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a small, smooth, black berry that ripens in the fall.

The species is characterized by its adaptability to harsh conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
