Rubus vestitus var. leucanthemus

Rubus vestitus var. leucanthemus, commonly known as the white-flowered blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its distinctive flower color and thorny stems.
