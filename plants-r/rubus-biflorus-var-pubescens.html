Rubus biflorus var. pubescens

Rubus biflorus var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, known as blackberries, are aggregate fruits formed from multiple small drupelets.

This variety is native to parts of North America and is often found in moist, wooded areas.
