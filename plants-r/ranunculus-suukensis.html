Ranunculus suukensis

Ranunculus suukensis, commonly known as the Suukensis buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of numerous small petals arranged in a cup-like structure.

Ranunculus suukensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
