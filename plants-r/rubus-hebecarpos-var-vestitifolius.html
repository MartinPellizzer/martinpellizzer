Rubus hebecarpos var. vestitifolius

Rubus hebecarpos var. vestitifolius, commonly known as the velvet-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist woodlands and thickets. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The botanical features of this plant make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
