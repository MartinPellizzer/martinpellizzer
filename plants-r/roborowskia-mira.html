Roborowskia mira

Roborowskia mira, commonly known as the alpine snowbell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a compact rosette of blue-gray, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, providing a silvery appearance. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale blue during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Roborowskia mira is often cultivated in rock gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
