Rubus adornatoides

Rubus adornatoides, commonly known as the adorned raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to South Africa, characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, though they may vary in color depending on the cultivar. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a pubescent surface.

Rubus adornatoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
