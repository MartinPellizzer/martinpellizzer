Rubus serpens var. vogesiacus

Rubus serpens var. vogesiacus, commonly known as the Vogesian blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible and often used in jams and preserves. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has a woody stem covered in thorny protrusions.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
