Rubus perpetiolulatus var. sublividus

Rubus perpetiolulatus var. sublividus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that are typically small and tart. The leaves are distinctive, with a silvery or bluish-white underside that gives the plant its common name. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
