Rubus impolitus

Rubus impolitus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to its ecological niche and contribute to its role in local ecosystems.
