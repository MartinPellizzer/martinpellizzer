Rubus hirtus var. emersidens

Rubus hirtus var. emersidens, commonly known as the prickly raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and a dense covering of fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep red color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its thorny stems and edible fruits make it both a challenging and valuable plant in natural ecosystems.
