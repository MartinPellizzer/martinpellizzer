Ranunculus buddii

Ranunculus buddii, commonly known as the Himalayan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features simple, palmately lobed leaves and produces yellow or white flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific growing conditions to flourish.
