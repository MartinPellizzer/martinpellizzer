Rosa coruscans

Rosa coruscans, commonly known as the sparkling rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, silvery-white petals that reflect light like a shimmering surface. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a delightful fragrance.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, Rosa coruscans is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
