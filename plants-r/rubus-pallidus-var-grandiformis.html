Rubus pallidus var. grandiformis

Rubus pallidus var. grandiformis, commonly known as the pale bramble or large-fruited raspberry, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically pink to red in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
