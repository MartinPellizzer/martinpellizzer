Rhipsalis cruciformis

Rhipsalis cruciformis, commonly known as the cross-shaped mistletoe, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its branching, leafless stems that often form a cross-like shape, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Rhipsalis cruciformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
