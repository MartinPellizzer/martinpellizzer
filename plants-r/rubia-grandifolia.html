Rubia grandifolia

Rubia grandifolia, commonly known as greater cowplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 12,000 species of flowering plants. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Rubia grandifolia is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
