Rubus serpens microgene longiglandulosus

Rubus serpens microgene longiglandulosus, commonly known as the long-glandular blackberry.

This plant is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and is covered in fine, glandular hairs. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
