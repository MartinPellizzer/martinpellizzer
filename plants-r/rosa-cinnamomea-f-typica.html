Rosa cinnamomea f. typica

Rosa cinnamomea f. typica, commonly known as the cinnamon rose, is a hybrid rose species native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery due to their strong, sweet aroma.

The cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
