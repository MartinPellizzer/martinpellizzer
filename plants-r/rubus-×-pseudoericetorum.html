Rubus × pseudoericetorum

Rubus × pseudoericetorum, commonly known as the creeping raspberry.

It is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
