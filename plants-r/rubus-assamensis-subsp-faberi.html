Rubus assamensis subsp. faberi

Rubus assamensis subsp.

faberi, commonly known as the Assam blackberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some use in traditional medicine.
