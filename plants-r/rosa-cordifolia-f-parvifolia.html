Rosa cordifolia f. parvifolia

Rosa cordifolia f. parvifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaved rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically velvety in texture, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking a unique and resilient rose variety.
