Rubus tomentosus f. pentaphyllus

Rubus tomentosus f. pentaphyllus, commonly known as the five-leaf raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, red-orange berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has a sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are usually pentagonal, with five lobes, giving it its distinctive appearance.

The flowers are white and clustered in umbels, adding to its ornamental value.
