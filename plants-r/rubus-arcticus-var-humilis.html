Rubus arcticus var. humilis

Rubus arcticus var. humilis, commonly known as the Arctic bramble, is a low-growing shrub native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and edible, tart berries. The plant has slender, hairy stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tundra ecosystems.
