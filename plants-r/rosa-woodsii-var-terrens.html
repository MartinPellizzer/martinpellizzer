Rosa woodsii var. terrens

Rosa woodsii var. terrens, commonly known as the desert rose or wood's rose, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy texture. This plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring deep root systems and the ability to conserve water efficiently.

It is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
