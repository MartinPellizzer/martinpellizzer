Rhynchospora flexuosa

Rhynchospora flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with three-ranked leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Rhynchospora flexuosa thrives in moist, acidic soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
