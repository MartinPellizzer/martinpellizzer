Rubus andegavensis

Rubus andegavensis, commonly known as the Andegavensis raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it is closely related to other raspberries in the Rubus genus, sharing similar floral and fruit structures.
