Rhynchospora nivea

Rhynchospora nivea, commonly known as the snowbeak sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Rhynchospora nivea thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small vertebrates.
