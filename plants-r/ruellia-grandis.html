Ruellia grandis

Ruellia grandis, commonly known as the large ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, arranged in terminal clusters. Ruellia grandis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
