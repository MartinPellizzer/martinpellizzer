Rosa × centifolia var. pomponia

Rosa × centifolia var. pomponia, commonly known as the Pompon rose, is a hybrid rose species prized for its compact growth and abundant flowering.

It features multiple layers of fragrant, pink or white petals that bloom in clusters, creating a pompon-like appearance. The plant is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in autumn, and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. It is a popular choice for gardens due to its ornamental value and hardiness.

Its flowers are often used in perfumery and floral arrangements.
