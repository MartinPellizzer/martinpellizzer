Rosa canina f. platyphylloides

Rosa canina f. platyphylloides, commonly known as the flat-leaf dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its broader, flatter leaves compared to the typical rose species. The plant produces fragrant pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fuzzy hips. It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

This form is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and floral display.
