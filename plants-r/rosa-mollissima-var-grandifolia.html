Rosa mollissima var. grandifolia

Rosa mollissima var. grandifolia, commonly known as the soft-hairy rose or grandiflorous rose, is a species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its densely hairy stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
