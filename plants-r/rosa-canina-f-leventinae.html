Rosa canina f. leventinae

Rosa canina f. leventinae, commonly known as the Leventina dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive pale pink to white flowers and thorny stems. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces red, fuzzy hips that contain seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
