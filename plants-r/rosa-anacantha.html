Rosa anacantha

Rosa anacantha, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems covered in sharp, thorn-like prickles. The plant produces fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

The fruit is a red, hairy rosehip that contains seeds and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
