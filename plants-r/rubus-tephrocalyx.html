Rubus tephrocalyx

Rubus tephrocalyx, commonly known as the ash-gray bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a rough, serrated edge.

The fruit, a small aggregate berry, is usually red or black when mature and is edible.
