Rosa graveolens subsp. nuda

Rosa graveolens subsp.

nuda, commonly known as the naked rose, is a species of shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its simple, thornless stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in pink or white.

The plant produces rose hips that are orange-red in color and contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
