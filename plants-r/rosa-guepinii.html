Rosa guepinii

Rosa guepinii, commonly known as the shrub rose, is a vigorous, thorny shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink or red, appearing in clusters. The plant is known for its strong growth habit and ability to spread rapidly, making it both ornamental and invasive in some regions. It produces hips, which are the fruit of the plant, containing seeds that can remain viable for several years.

Rosa guepinii is often cultivated for its fragrance and ornamental value in gardens.
