Rhododendron brachycarpum subsp. fauriei

Rhododendron brachycarpum subsp.

fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's rhododendron, is a shrub native to the Himalayas. It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
