Rubus mulsantii

Rubus mulsantii, commonly known as the Mulsant blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa, particularly in countries like Namibia and South Africa. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
