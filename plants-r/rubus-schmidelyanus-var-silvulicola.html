Rubus schmidelyanus var. silvulicola

Rubus schmidelyanus var. silvulicola, commonly known as the silvulicola raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
