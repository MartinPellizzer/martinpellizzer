Rubus antennifer

Rubus antennifer, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a deep red color, often with a slight whitish bloom. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Rubus antennifer is valued for its edible berries and its role in local ecosystems.
