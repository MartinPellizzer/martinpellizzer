Rubus hirtus subsp. elegantulus

Rubus hirtus subsp.

elegantulus, commonly known as the elegant trailing raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the Rosaceae family.
