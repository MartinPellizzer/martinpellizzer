Rosa coriifolia var. subvacillans

Rosa coriifolia var. subvacillans, commonly known as the variable shrub rose, is a less commonly cultivated variant of the shrub rose.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to elliptic leaflets that have a slightly serrated margin. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of China and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

It is relatively hardy and can thrive in full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
