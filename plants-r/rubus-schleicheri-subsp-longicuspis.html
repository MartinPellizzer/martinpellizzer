Rubus schleicheri subsp. longicuspis

Rubus schleicheri subsp.

longicuspis, commonly known as the long-cusped blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
