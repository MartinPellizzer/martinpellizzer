Rhododendron trichopodum

Rhododendron trichopodum, commonly known as the creeping rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are typically pink or red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
