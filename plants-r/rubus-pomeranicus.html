Rubus pomeranicus

Rubus pomeranicus, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a species of brambles native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates.

Rubus pomeranicus is also known for its ornamental value and ability to form dense thickets.
