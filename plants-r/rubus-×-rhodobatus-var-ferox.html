Rubus × rhodobatus var. ferox

Rubus × rhodobatus var. ferox, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained, fertile ground. It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to several meters in height, often forming dense thickets.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits, which are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
