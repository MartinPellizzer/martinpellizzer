Rhododendron japonoheptamerum var. japonoheptamerum

Rhododendron japonoheptamerum var. japonoheptamerum, commonly known as the Japanese heptamerum rhododendron, is a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically produces clusters of pink to reddish-purple blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
