Ranunculus auricomus subsp. platyodon

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

platyodon, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features showy yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals, often with a distinctive dark central vein.

The plant has a short, thick rhizome and hairy stems that are typically branched. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a basal rosette of lobed, hairy foliage.

It prefers moist, shady habitats and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
