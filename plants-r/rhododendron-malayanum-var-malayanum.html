Rhododendron malayanum var. malayanum

Rhododendron malayanum var. malayanum, commonly known as the Malayan rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height, featuring dense, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are usually pink to red in color and bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary and secondary forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
