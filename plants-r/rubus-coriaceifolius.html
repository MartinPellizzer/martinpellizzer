Rubus coriaceifolius

Rubus coriaceifolius, commonly known as the hard-leaved blackberry, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
