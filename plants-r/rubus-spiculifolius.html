Rubus spiculifolius

Rubus spiculifolius, commonly known as the prickly bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, known as blackberries, are edible and often used in culinary applications.

Rubus spiculifolius is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in temperate climates.
