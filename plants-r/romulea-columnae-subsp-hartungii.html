Romulea columnae subsp. hartungii

Romulea columnae subsp.

hartungii, commonly known as Hartung's columnar romulea, is a member of the Iridaceae family and is native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces striking, pale yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive columnar structure.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight blue or greyish hue.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
