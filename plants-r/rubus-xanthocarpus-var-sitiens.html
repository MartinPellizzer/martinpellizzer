Rubus xanthocarpus var. sitiens

Rubus xanthocarpus var. sitiens, commonly known as the yellow-fruited bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color, which are edible but often tart. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
