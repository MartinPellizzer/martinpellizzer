Rhododendron goyozanense

Rhododendron goyozanense, commonly known as the Goyozan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
