Rosa montana subsp. caballicensis

Rosa montana subsp.

caballicensis, commonly known as the Caballic Mountain rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments.

The plant features dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it produces small, red berries in the autumn.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience in harsh climates.
