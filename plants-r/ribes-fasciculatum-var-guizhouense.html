Ribes fasciculatum var. guizhouense

Ribes fasciculatum var. guizhouense, commonly known as Guizhou gooseberry, is a variant of the gooseberry plant native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions with moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are usually yellow or white.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its potential medicinal uses.
