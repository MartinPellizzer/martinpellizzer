Rubus obscurus proles rubicundus

Rubus obscurus proles rubicundus, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, and the fruit is aggregate, formed from multiple drupes.

This species is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
