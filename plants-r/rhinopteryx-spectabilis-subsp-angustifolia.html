Rhinopteryx spectabilis subsp. angustifolia

Rhinopteryx spectabilis subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rhinopteryx, is a flowering plant characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
