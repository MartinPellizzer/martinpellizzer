Ranunculus pilisiensis

Ranunculus pilisiensis, commonly known as the Pilis buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Hungary.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically feature yellow petals with a dark center. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. It has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are usually hairy.

Ranunculus pilisiensis is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
