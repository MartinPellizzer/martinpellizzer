Rhododendron apiense

Rhododendron apiense, commonly known as the Philippine rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of the Philippines.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of funnel-shaped, bell-like flowers in shades of pink to purple. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many other ornamental and ecologically significant plants.

Rhododendron apiense is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
