Ribes andicola

Ribes andicola, commonly known as the Andean gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various shrubs and vines, many of which are cultivated for their edible berries. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries that are typically consumed raw or used in culinary applications. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in moist, temperate regions with well-drained soil.

The leaves are usually alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
