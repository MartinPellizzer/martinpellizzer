Rhipsalis floccosa subsp. oreophila

Rhipsalis floccosa subsp.

oreophila, commonly known as the cloudy rhipsalis, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family. It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy stems that give it a distinctive appearance.

This subshrub is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Brazil. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
