Rytigynia lenticellata

Rytigynia lenticellata, commonly known as the smoothleaf rytigynia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Rytigyniaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and smooth, often hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a glossy surface and may have small, raised pores called lenticels.

The flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
