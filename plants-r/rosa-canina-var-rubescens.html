Rosa canina var. rubescens

Rosa canina var. rubescens, commonly known as the red rose hip.

This variant of the dog rose is characterized by its deep red, elongated fruits that develop after the flowering season. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rosa genus. It produces fragrant pink or white flowers in the spring, followed by the distinctive red hips in autumn.

The hips are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional herbal medicine and culinary applications.
