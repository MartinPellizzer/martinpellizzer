Rubus ulmifolius var. congestus

Rubus ulmifolius var. congestus, commonly known as the dense-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are white and clustered, giving way to dark purple to black fruits that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible berries, which have a rich, sweet flavor.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
