Rubus ulmifolius var. rusticanus

Rubus ulmifolius var. rusticanus, commonly known as the Rustic blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry species.

It is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valued plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
