Rosa pimpinellifolia var. argentea

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. argentea, commonly known as silverleaf rock rose, is a variant of the rock rose species native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and drought tolerance.
