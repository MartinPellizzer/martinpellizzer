Rubus mucronifer subsp. fuscicortex

Rubus mucronifer subsp.

fuscicortex, commonly known as the dark-barked blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant produces edible berries that are dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest edges or disturbed soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker, thicker bark compared to other Rubus species.
