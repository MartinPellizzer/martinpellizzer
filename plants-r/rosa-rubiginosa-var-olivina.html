Rosa rubiginosa var. olivina

Rosa rubiginosa var. olivina, commonly known as the olive rose, is a shrub-like perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces rosehips that are typically orange or red, containing a high concentration of vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
