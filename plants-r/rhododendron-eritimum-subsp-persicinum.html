Rhododendron eritimum subsp. persicinum

Rhododendron eritimum subsp.

persicinum, commonly known as the Persian rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils at high altitudes. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
