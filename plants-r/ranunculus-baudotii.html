Ranunculus baudotii

Ranunculus baudotii, commonly known as the Baudot's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. The plant features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its often toxic compounds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
