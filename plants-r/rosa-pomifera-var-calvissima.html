Rosa pomifera var. calvissima

Rosa pomifera var. calvissima, commonly known as the smooth rose or calvissima rose, is a rare and distinctive species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces large, fuzzy fruits known as rose hips, which are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its rarity and unique botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
