Rosa gentilis

Rosa gentilis, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Rosa gentilis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Its historical use in gardens and herbal medicine highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
