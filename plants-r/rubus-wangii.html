Rubus wangii

Rubus wangii, commonly known as the Wang's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits, known as berries, are typically red and edible, though they are not as commonly consumed as other Rubus species.

Rubus wangii is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
