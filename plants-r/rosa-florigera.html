Rosa florigera

Rosa florigera, commonly known as the Brazilian garden rose, is a species native to Brazil and is characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The flowers are usually pink to red in color, with a deep, velvety texture and a strong, sweet fragrance. It blooms in spring and summer, producing clusters of flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
