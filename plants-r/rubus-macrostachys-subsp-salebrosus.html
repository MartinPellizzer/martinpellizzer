Rubus macrostachys subsp. salebrosus

Rubus macrostachys subsp.

salebrosus, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, serrated leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This subspecies is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional medicine.
