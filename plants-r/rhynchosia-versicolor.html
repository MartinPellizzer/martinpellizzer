Rhynchosia versicolor

Rhynchosia versicolor, commonly known as the variable cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, hairy stipules and produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several edible seeds, which are used as a food source.

Rhynchosia versicolor is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural use.
