Rhipsalis

Rhipsalis, commonly known as the mistletoe cactus, is a genus of epiphytic cacti native to tropical regions of the Americas.

These plants typically grow on trees or other structures, using their roots to anchor themselves without deriving nutrients from their host. Their stems are usually slender and branched, often covered in small, hair-like structures called trichomes. Rhipsalis species produce small, white or green flowers that bloom during the wet season, followed by berries that are often eaten by birds.

Due to their unique appearance and ease of care, they are popular houseplants in many parts of the world.
