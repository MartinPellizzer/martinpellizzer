Rosa communis var. beatricis

Rosa communis var. beatricis, commonly known as the Beatrice rose, is a cultivar of the common rose, characterized by its deep pink petals and lush, fragrant blooms.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves, typical of the Rosa genus. The flowers are usually borne in clusters and are prized for their ornamental value in gardens. This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of climates, making it a popular choice for horticultural enthusiasts.

Its botanical features align closely with those of its parent species, Rosa communis, but it stands out for its unique coloration and floral display.
