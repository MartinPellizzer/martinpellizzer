Rosa gentilis f. globifera

Rosa gentilis f. globifera, commonly known as the globeflower rose, is a unique cultivar of the Rosa genus characterized by its distinctive, rounded flower heads.

This plant is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The flowers are typically pink or red, with a globular shape that sets it apart from other rose varieties. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial sunlight, making it suitable for garden borders or container planting.

Due to its rare and specific appearance, it is a prized addition to specialized botanical collections.
