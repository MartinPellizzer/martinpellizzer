Ranunculus auricomus subsp. chlorellus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

chlorellus, commonly known as the yellow water crowfoot, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often with a greenish center.

The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, such as ponds, lakes, and stream edges. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, and it is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

The subspecies chlorellus is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and habitat preferences compared to the typical form.
