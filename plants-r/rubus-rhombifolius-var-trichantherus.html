Rubus rhombifolius var. trichantherus

Rubus rhombifolius var. trichantherus, commonly known as the Appalachian bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are often red or black in color. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
