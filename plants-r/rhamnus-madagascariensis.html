Rhamnus madagascariensis

Rhamnus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar buckthorn, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often glandular leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including dry forests and rocky areas, and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of Madagascar.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the region's flora.
