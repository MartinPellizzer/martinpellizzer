Rosa willmottiana

Rosa willmottiana, commonly known as the Willmott's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe, including parts of the Czech Republic and Austria. This shrub typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features fragrant, pale pink to white flowers. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets that are serrated and hairy.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
