Ranunculus amansii

Ranunculus amansii, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to seven petals, typically in shades of yellow, white, or orange. The plant has a short, hairy stem and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ranunculus amansii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
