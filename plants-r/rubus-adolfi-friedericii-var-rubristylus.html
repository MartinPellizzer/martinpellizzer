Rubus adolfi-friedericii var. rubristylus

Rubus adolfi-friedericii var. rubristylus, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive red-colored stems.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically larger and sweeter than those of other blackberry varieties. Its thorny stems and dense foliage make it a hardy shrub that thrives in temperate climates.

It is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value due to its attractive appearance.
