Rosa pimpinellifolia subsp. alpina

Rosa pimpinellifolia subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the alpine rock rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family. It is a shrub characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and thorny stems.

This subspecies is typically found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it produces pink to red flowers in late summer.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
