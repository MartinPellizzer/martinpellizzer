Rubus camusianus

Rubus camusianus, commonly known as the Camusian bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Native to specific regions in Europe, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
