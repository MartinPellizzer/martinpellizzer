Ranunculus sardous var. cordigerus

Ranunculus sardous var. cordigerus, commonly known as the Sardinian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sardinia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a deep green color and a hairy texture.

It is a short-lived perennial that blooms in spring and is noted for its ornamental value in horticulture.
