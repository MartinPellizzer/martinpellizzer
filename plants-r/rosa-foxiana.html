Rosa foxiana

Rosa foxiana, commonly known as the Fox's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges.

Rosa foxiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
