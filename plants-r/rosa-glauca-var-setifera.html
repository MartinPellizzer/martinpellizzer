Rosa glauca var. setifera

Rosa glauca var. setifera, commonly known as the bluebelle rose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its pale blue to lavender flowers and grayish-green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant. It produces fragrant blooms in late spring to early summer.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and small spaces.
