Rhododendron phaeochrysum

Rhododendron phaeochrysum, commonly known as the golden rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in high-altitude forests and alpine meadows. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are dark green with a waxy surface, and its flowers are showy, with five petals that are typically yellow or golden in color. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
