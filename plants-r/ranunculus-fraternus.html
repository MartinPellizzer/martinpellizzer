Ranunculus fraternus

Ranunculus fraternus, commonly known as the brother's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for various pollinators and its ornamental value in gardens.
