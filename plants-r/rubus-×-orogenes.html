Rubus × orogenes

Rubus × orogenes, commonly known as the alpine bramble, is a hybrid plant species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces aggregate fruits, typically red or black berries, which are edible and used in local cuisines. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky soils at high elevations.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important role in stabilizing mountainous terrain and supporting local wildlife.
