Raphia maxima

Raphia maxima, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a large, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its massive, fan-shaped leaves that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant has a robust trunk with a fibrous, spongy interior that provides structural support. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, with separate male and female flowers on different plants.

Raphia maxima plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
