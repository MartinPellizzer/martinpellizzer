Rosa communis proles lusana

Rosa communis proles lusana, commonly known as the Lusana rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its botanical features include a woody structure and the ability to produce hips, which are the fruit of the plant.

The Lusana rose is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and aromatic qualities.
