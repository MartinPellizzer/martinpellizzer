Richeria grandis var. gardneriana

Richeria grandis var. gardneriana, commonly known as the Gardner's richeria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palm-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its foliage is thick and leathery, providing a dense canopy that helps retain moisture in the soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
