Reseda lutea var. pumila

Reseda lutea var. pumila, commonly known as dwarf yellowrocket, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, typically from May to June. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in horticultural settings.
