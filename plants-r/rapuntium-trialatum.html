Rapuntium trialatum

Rapuntium trialatum, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
