Ranunculus schennikovii

Ranunculus schennikovii, commonly known as the Schennikov's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
