Ranunculus trichophyllus proles paucistamineus

Ranunculus trichophyllus proles paucistamineus, commonly known as the few-amine Ranunculus, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, usually yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers have five to eight sepals that are often mistaken for petals.

The species is notable for its reduced number of stamens, which is a key feature in its scientific name.
