Rhododendron smithii

Rhododendron smithii, commonly known as the Olympic Peninsula rhododendron, is a species native to the western United States, particularly the Olympic Peninsula in Washington state.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in cool, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing dense foliage that offers habitat for various wildlife species.

Rhododendron smithii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
