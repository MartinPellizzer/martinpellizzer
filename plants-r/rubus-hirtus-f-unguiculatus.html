Rubus hirtus f. unguiculatus

Rubus hirtus f. unguiculatus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry.

This plant is a flowering shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and are covered in fine, stiff hairs. The fruit, a small, dark purple to black berry, is edible and often used in jams and desserts.

It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
