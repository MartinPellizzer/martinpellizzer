Rubus braeuckeri

Rubus braeuckeri, commonly known as the Braeucker's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple or black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
