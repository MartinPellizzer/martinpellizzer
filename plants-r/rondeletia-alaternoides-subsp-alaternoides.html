Rondeletia alaternoides subsp. alaternoides

Rondeletia alaternoides subsp.

alaternoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf rondeletia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
