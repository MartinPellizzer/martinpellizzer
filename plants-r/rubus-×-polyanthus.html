Rubus × polyanthus

Rubus × polyanthus, commonly known as the everbearing raspberry.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus idaeus (red raspberry) and Rubus strigosus (blackberry). This plant is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that ripen throughout the growing season. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the leaves are trifoliate with serrated edges.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.
