Rubus pittieri

Rubus pittieri, commonly known as the Chilean blackberry, is a species of brambles native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions and mountainous areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
