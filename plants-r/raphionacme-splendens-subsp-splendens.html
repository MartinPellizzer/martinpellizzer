Raphionacme splendens subsp. splendens

Raphionacme splendens subsp.

splendens, commonly known as the splendid rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
