Rosa × complicata var. subcanina

Rosa × complicata var. subcanina, commonly known as the rose of Sharon, is a hybrid species within the rose family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant features thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is adaptable to various soil types and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
