Rubia pubescens

Rubia pubescens, commonly known as hairy woodruff, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 13,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Rubia pubescens is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.
