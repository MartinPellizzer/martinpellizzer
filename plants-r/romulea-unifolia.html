Romulea unifolia

Romulea unifolia, commonly known as the single-flowered romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Romulea unifolia is also noted for its ability to thrive in Mediterranean climates.
