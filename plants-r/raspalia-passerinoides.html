Raspalia passerinoides

Raspalia passerinoides, commonly known as the silverleaf raspberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color, resembling raspberries in texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
