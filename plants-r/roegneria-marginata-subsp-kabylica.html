Roegneria marginata subsp. kabylica

Roegneria marginata subsp.

kabylica, commonly known as the Kabylic grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous pale green to brownish florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

This subspecies is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil.
