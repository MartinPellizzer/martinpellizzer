Rotang viminalis

Rotang viminalis, commonly known as white mangrove, is a species of mangrove tree native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and its ability to thrive in saline and tidal environments. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches. Its flowers are usually small and white, clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces woody, elongated fruits that contain a single seed.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
