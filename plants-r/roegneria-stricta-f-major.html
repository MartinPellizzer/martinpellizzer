Roegneria stricta f. major

Roegneria stricta f. major, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often clustered in panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
