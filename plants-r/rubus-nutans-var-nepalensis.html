Rubus nutans var. nepalensis

Rubus nutans var. nepalensis, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

This variant is closely related to other Rubus species and is often cultivated for its edible berries.
