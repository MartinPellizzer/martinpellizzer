Ranunculus purshii subsp. yukonensis

Ranunculus purshii subsp.

yukonensis, commonly known as Yukon buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Yukon and Northwest Territories of Canada. It features showy, yellow, cup-shaped flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate and often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
