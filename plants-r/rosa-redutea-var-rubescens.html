Rosa redutea var. rubescens

Rosa redutea var. rubescens, commonly known as the crimson rose, is a variant of the redutea rose species.

It is characterized by its deep red petals that range from a rich crimson to a more intense scarlet hue. The plant typically produces fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is well-suited to temperate climates.
