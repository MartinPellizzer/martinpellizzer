Reidia stipulacea

Reidia stipulacea, commonly known as the stipule leafcup, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive cup-shaped stipules that enclose the base of its leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically elongated and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Reidia stipulacea is often found in marshy areas and is adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
