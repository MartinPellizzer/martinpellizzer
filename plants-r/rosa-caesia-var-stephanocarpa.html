Rosa caesia var. stephanocarpa

Rosa caesia var. stephanocarpa, commonly known as the stephanocarpa rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several elliptical leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
