Rhipsalis bauchienii

Rhipsalis bauchienii, commonly known as the silver mist cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, thin, branching stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its unique growth habit allows it to thrive in shaded, humid environments, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Unlike many cacti, Rhipsalis bauchienii does not have true leaves but instead relies on its stems for photosynthesis.
