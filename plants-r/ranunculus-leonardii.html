Ranunculus leonardii

Ranunculus leonardii, commonly known as the Leonard's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many species of buttercups and other herbaceous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its flowers are bright yellow with numerous petals, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Ranunculus leonardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
