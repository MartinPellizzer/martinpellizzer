Rubus vestitifolius

Rubus vestitifolius, commonly known as the velvet-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, dark green leaves and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in the understory of forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
