Rubus aragonensis

Rubus aragonensis, commonly known as Aragon blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or dry soils.

Due to its unique flavor and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable plant in botanical collections.
