Rubus koehneanus

Rubus koehneanus, commonly known as the Koehnean raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and thorned stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
