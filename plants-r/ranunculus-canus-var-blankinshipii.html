Ranunculus canus var. blankinshipii

Ranunculus canus var. blankinshipii, commonly known as the Blankinship's crowfoot, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and stems that are hairy and upright. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with five to eight petals and numerous stamens.
