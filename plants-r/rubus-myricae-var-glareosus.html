Rubus myricae var. glareosus

Rubus myricae var. glareosus, commonly known as the shining blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its flavorful fruit and is often cultivated in home gardens and commercial settings.
