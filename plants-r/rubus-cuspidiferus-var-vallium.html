Rubus cuspidiferus var. vallium

Rubus cuspidiferus var. vallium, commonly known as the Virginia blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The berries are valued for their flavor and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
