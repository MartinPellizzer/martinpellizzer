Rhynchosia adenantha

Rhynchosia adenantha, commonly known as the African bean or yellow cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are used as a food source in some regions. Rhynchosia adenantha is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural systems.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climates contributes to its widespread distribution across its native range.
