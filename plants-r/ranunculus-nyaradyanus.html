Ranunculus nyaradyanus

Ranunculus nyaradyanus, commonly known as the Hungarian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically arranged in a cup-like shape. The plant has a hairy stem that is often tinged with red or purple, and its leaves are deeply lobed with a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
