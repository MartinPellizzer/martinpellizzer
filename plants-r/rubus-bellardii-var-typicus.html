Rubus bellardii var. typicus

Rubus bellardii var. typicus, commonly known as the Bellard's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Georgia and Florida. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
