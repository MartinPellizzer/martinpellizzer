Rubus omalus subsp. scitulus

Rubus omalus subsp.

scitulus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native ecosystems.
