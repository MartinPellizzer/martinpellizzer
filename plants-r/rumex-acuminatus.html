Rumex acuminatus

Rumex acuminatus, commonly known as sharpleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist soils, often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive sharp tip, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
