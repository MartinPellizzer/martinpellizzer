Rothmannia microphylla

Rothmannia microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf rothmannia, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and thrives in well-drained soil.

Rothmannia microphylla is often used in rock gardens and as a container plant due to its ornamental value and resilience.
