Ranunculus auricomus subsp. nicklesi

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

nicklesi, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers with five petals and a distinctive, spurred nectary.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often hairy.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
