Ranunculus indivisus var. indivisus

Ranunculus indivisus var. indivisus, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to eleven sepals that resemble petals, and it typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant has a short, stout rhizome and hairy stems that are often reddish in color. Its leaves are palmately divided into three to five lobes, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers and is often found in shaded, damp environments.
