Rubus monensis

Rubus monensis, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
