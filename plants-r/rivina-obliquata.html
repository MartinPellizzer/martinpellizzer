Rivina obliquata

Rivina obliquata, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Rivina obliquata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
