Rosa moschata var. densa

Rosa moschata var. densa, commonly known as the dense musk rose, is a variety of the musk rose species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant produces small, oval-shaped hips that are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, with thorny stems typical of the Rosa genus.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and culinary applications.
