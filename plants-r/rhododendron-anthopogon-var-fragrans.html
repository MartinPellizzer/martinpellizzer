Rhododendron anthopogon var. fragrans

Rhododendron anthopogon var. fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its flowers emit a pleasant fragrance, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The varietal name 'fragrans' refers to its aromatic blooms, which are often used in traditional herbal remedies.
