Rosa rubiginosa var. comosa

Rosa rubiginosa var. comosa, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage and clusters of fragrant, pale pink to reddish flowers. The plant produces oval, orange-red hips that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
