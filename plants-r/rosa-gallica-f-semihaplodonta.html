Rosa gallica f. semihaplodonta

Rosa gallica f. semihaplodonta, commonly known as the Semi-Halploid Rose, is a botanical variant of the Gallica rose, characterized by its unique genetic structure.

This form exhibits a reduced chromosome count, making it a valuable subject in plant genetics research. It typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, similar to its parent species. The plant is deciduous, with thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.
