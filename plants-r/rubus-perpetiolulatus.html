Rubus perpetiolulatus

Rubus perpetiolulatus, commonly known as the evergreen bramble, is a shrub native to parts of East Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or forested areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a source of edible berries.
