Rivina gracilis

Rivina gracilis, commonly known as slender rivina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Rivina gracilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
