Rubus nitidus var. holerythros

Rubus nitidus var. holerythros, commonly known as the red-berried bramble, is a variant of the common blackberry species.

It is characterized by its vibrant red berries that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates, often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. Its leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets, and the stems are thorny.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
