Rosa tomentosa subrubiginosa

Rosa tomentosa subrubiginosa, commonly known as the woolly rose or the red-tipped rose, is a shrub-like species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with thorny stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are dark green and covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Rosa tomentosa subrubiginosa is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
