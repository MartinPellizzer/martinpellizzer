Rubus hamorensis

Rubus hamorensis, commonly known as the Hamorensis blackberry, is a species of brambles native to South America, specifically found in Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often described as having a tart and slightly bitter flavor.
