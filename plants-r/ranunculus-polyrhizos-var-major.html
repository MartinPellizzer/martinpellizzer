Ranunculus polyrhizos var. major

Ranunculus polyrhizos var. major, commonly known as the giant water crowfoot, is a aquatic plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has finely divided, floating leaves that help it capture sunlight for photosynthesis. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five to eight petals and numerous stamens.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
