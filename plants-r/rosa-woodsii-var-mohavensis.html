Rosa woodsii var. mohavensis

Rosa woodsii var. mohavensis, commonly known as the Mohave rose, is a subspecies of the wood's rose native to the southwestern United States.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, red or orange fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
