Rhombophyllum rhomboideum

Rhombophyllum rhomboideum, commonly known as the diamond cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed, cylindrical stems. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Rhombophyllum rhomboideum is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
