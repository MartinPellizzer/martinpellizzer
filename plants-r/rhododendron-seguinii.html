Rhododendron seguinii

Rhododendron seguinii, commonly known as the Seguin's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often borne in clusters.

Rhododendron seguinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
