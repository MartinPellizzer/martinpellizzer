Rhipsalis regnellii

Rhipsalis regnellii, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its branching, leaf-like stems that are often segmented. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the holiday season. Its unique growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Rhipsalis regnellii prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
