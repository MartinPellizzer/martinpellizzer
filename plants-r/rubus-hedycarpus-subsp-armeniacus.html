Rubus hedycarpus subsp. armeniacus

Rubus hedycarpus subsp.

armeniacus, commonly known as the Armenian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region. It produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems. Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by small, edible fruits.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.
