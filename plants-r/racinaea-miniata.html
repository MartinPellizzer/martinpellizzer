Racinaea miniata

Racinaea miniata, commonly known as the miniature racinaea, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes plants like philodendrons and peace lilies. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish or purple tint on the underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often hidden beneath a modified leaf called a spathe.

Racinaea miniata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
