Romulea cuprea

Romulea cuprea, commonly known as the copper iris, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes many ornamental flowering plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces vivid orange-red flowers with a distinctive, spurred petal. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Romulea cuprea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and borders.
