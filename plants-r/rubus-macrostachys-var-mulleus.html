Rubus macrostachys var. mulleus

Rubus macrostachys var. mulleus, commonly known as the purple-leafed raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple foliage, which contrasts with its typical red or green leaves in other Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
