Rosa × armatissima

Rosa × armatissima, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has dark green, lobed leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its botanical features include a woody structure and the ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Rosa × armatissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
