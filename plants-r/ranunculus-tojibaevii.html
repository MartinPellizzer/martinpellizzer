Ranunculus tojibaevii

Ranunculus tojibaevii, commonly known as the Tojibaev's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to the landscape.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
