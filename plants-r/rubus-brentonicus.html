Rubus brentonicus

Rubus brentonicus, commonly known as the Brenton's blackberry, is a shrub native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and hedgerows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
