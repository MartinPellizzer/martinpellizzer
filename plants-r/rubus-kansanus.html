Rubus kansanus

Rubus kansanus, commonly known as the Kansas blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in prairie and woodland habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife.
