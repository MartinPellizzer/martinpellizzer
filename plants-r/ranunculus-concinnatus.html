Ranunculus concinnatus

Ranunculus concinnatus, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a serrated edge.

Ranunculus concinnatus is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
