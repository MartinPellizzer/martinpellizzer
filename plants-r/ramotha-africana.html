Ramotha africana

Ramotha africana, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and grey, often exfoliating to reveal a lighter inner layer.

Ramotha africana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
