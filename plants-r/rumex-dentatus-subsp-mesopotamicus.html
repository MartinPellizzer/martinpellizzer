Rumex dentatus subsp. mesopotamicus

Rumex dentatus subsp.

mesopotamicus, commonly known as the Mesopotamic dock, is a perennial herb native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a robust root system.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and floodplains. It produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by triangular, hard seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
