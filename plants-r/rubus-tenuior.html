Rubus tenuior

Rubus tenuior, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval and hairy.

Rubus tenuior thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
