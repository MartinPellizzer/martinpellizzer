Rosa alpina var. pyriformis

Rosa alpina var. pyriformis, commonly known as the alpine rose or Pyriformis rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. The leaves are simple, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of soft, grayish-white hairs. This variety is well adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in garden settings.
