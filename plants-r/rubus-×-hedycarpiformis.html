Rubus × hedycarpiformis

Rubus × hedycarpiformis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin with a hairy underside.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruits.
