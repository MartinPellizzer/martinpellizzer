Rubus villicaulis var. rectangulatus

Rubus villicaulis var. rectangulatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinct leaf shape and robust growth habits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are broad and have a slightly serrated edge, distinguishing it from other Rubus species.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
