Rubus insericatus var. purpurascens

Rubus insericatus var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn from green to a deep purple or black when mature. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its unique fruit color and flavor, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
