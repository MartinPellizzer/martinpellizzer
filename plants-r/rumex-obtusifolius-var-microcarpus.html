Rumex obtusifolius var. microcarpus

Rumex obtusifolius var. microcarpus, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a wavy margin. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in waste areas.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive leaf shapes and edible leaves in some species.
