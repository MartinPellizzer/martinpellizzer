Rabdosia flavida

Rabdosia flavida, commonly known as the yellow rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Rabdosia flavida is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
