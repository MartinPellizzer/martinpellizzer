Rosa riparia var. amoena

Rosa riparia var. amoena, commonly known as the Amoena river rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with serrated edges, providing a lush green backdrop to the blooms. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils near waterways and is often found in riparian habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
