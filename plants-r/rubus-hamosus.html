Rubus hamosus

Rubus hamosus, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
