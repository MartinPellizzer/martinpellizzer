Rosa × lupulina

Rosa × lupulina, commonly known as the musk rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa moschata.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow-orange flowers and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in making perfume and essential oils.

Its stems are thorny, and it produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. Rosa × lupulina is native to eastern Asia and has been widely introduced to other parts of the world.
