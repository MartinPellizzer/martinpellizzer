Rubus obsessus var. unilaris

Rubus obsessus var. unilaris, commonly known as the single-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its unique morphology.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are usually simple and have a slightly serrated edge, distinguishing it from other Rubus species.

The plant is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.
