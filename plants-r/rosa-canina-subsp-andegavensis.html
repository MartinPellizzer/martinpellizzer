Rosa canina subsp. andegavensis

Rosa canina subsp.

andegavensis, commonly known as the Andegavensis rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by orange-red hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
