Rosa cuspidata var. pseudocuspidata

Rosa cuspidata var. pseudocuspidata, commonly known as the western prickly rose, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces oval, hairy leaves with serrated edges, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is notable for its hardiness and adaptability to harsh conditions.
