Rubeola linearifolia

Rubeola linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaf rubeola, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique leaf structure, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
