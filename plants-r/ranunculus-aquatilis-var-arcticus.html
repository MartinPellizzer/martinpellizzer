Ranunculus aquatilis var. arcticus

Ranunculus aquatilis var. arcticus, commonly known as the Arctic water crowfoot, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and streams. The plant features finely divided, feather-like leaves that help it float on the water's surface. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with five petals and numerous stamens.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small fish.
