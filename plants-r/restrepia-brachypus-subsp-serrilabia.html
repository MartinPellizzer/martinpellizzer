Restrepia brachypus subsp. serrilabia

Restrepia brachypus subsp.

serrilabia, commonly known as the serrated-labied restrepia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes of South America. It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and long, strap-like leaves.

The flowers are typically greenish-yellow with red or brown markings, and they bloom in late winter or early spring. This subspecies is characterized by its serrated labellum, which gives it its common name.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
