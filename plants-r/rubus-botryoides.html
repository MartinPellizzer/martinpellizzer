Rubus botryoides

Rubus botryoides, commonly known as the sweet dewberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial shrub that produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are white with five petals. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in areas with partial shade.

The fruit is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor, making it a popular plant for foraging and cultivation.
