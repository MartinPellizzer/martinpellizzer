Rhynchospora laevigata

Rhynchospora laevigata, commonly known as the smooth spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Rhynchospora laevigata thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
