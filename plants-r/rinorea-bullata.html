Rinorea bullata

Rinorea bullata, commonly known as the silverleaf rinorea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a waxy, silver-green appearance that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
