Ranunculus monspeliacus var. angustilobus

Ranunculus monspeliacus var. angustilobus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a central yellow stamen. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a distinctive hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
