Restrepia antennifera var. puntillosa

Restrepia antennifera var. puntillosa, commonly known as the slender-leafed restrepia, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes, particularly in regions of Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky slopes or in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
