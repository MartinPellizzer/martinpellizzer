Rauvolfia macrocarpa

Rauvolfia macrocarpa, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a climbing shrub that produces small, bell-shaped flowers and orange-red berries. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves and distinctive, tuberous roots. Its botanical significance lies in the presence of alkaloids, which have been studied for their medicinal properties.

Rauvolfia macrocarpa is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
