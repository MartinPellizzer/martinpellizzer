Racosperma maidenii

Racosperma maidenii, commonly known as the Grey Box, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its greyish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It is adapted to low rainfall and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Racosperma maidenii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
