Rhysolepis obtusifolia

Rhysolepis obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved huckleberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
