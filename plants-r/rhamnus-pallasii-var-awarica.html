Rhamnus pallasii var. awarica

Rhamnus pallasii var. awarica, commonly known as the Florida buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and is adapted to moist, acidic soils. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are clustered in small, greenish-white inflorescences, and it produces small, black berries that are edible for some wildlife.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native wetland ecosystems.
