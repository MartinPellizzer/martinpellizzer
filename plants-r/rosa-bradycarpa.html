Rosa bradycarpa

Rosa bradycarpa, commonly known as the slow-fruited rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, followed by small, round, red berries that mature in the fall. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem with bark that is dark brown and fissured, and it is often found in rocky or arid habitats.

Rosa bradycarpa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
