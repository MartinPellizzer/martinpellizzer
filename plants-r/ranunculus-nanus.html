Ranunculus nanus

Ranunculus nanus, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a small herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, featuring delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Ranunculus nanus is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
