Rapistrum rugosum var. linnaeanum

Rapistrum rugosum var. linnaeanum, commonly known as the Linnaean sowthistle, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its rough, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically lobed or toothed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
