Restio miser

Restio miser, commonly known as the weeping restio, is a unique member of the Restionaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with long, drooping inflorescences that give it a weeping or cascading effect. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like structures.

Restio miser is often used in ornamental horticulture for its distinctive visual appeal and adaptability to coastal environments.
