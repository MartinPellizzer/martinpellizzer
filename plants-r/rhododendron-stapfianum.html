Rhododendron stapfianum

Rhododendron stapfianum, commonly known as the Stapf's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist, and shaded environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in the spring, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.

Rhododendron stapfianum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing mountainous soils and supporting local biodiversity.
