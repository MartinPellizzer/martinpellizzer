Rubia argyi

Rubia argyi, commonly known as Chinese madder, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its roots are the primary source of a red dye used in traditional dyeing practices.

Rubia argyi is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands.
