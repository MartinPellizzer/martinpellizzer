Rafnia capensis

Rafnia capensis, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. Rafnia capensis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its unique blooming pattern and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
