Rubus uncinatus subsp. discerptus

Rubus uncinatus subsp.

discerptus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
