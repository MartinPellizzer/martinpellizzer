Rubus latistipulatus

Rubus latistipulatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves with serrated edges and dark purple to black berries that ripen in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowering period occurs in early summer, producing clusters of white to pale pink flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in local ecosystems as a food source for wildlife.
