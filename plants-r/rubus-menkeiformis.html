Rubus menkeiformis

Rubus menkeiformis, commonly known as the Menke's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its vigorous growth habit.
