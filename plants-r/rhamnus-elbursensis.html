Rhamnus elbursensis

Rhamnus elbursensis, commonly known as the Elburz buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, dark berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
