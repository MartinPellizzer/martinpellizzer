Ruellia capitata

Ruellia capitata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ruellia capitata thrives in partial to full sunlight and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage and blooms.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period contribute to its appeal in both landscape design and container gardening.
