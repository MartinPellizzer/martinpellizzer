Rhamnoides hippophae

Rhamnoides hippophae, commonly known as the sea buckthorn, is a shrub native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, typically blooming in early spring.

Rhamnoides hippophae thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found near coastal areas.
