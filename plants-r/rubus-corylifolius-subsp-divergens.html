Rubus corylifolius subsp. divergens

Rubus corylifolius subsp.

divergens, commonly known as the broad-leafed blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and thorny stems.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black achenes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus.
