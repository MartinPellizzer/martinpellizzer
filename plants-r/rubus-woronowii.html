Rubus woronowii

Rubus woronowii, commonly known as the Korean blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in forested areas and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
