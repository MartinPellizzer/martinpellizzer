Rhododendron metternichii subsp. yakushimanum

Rhododendron metternichii subsp.

yakushimanum, commonly known as the Yakushima azalea, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the subtropical forests of Yakushima Island, Japan. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red.

The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.

It plays a significant role in the ecological and cultural landscape of Yakushima.
