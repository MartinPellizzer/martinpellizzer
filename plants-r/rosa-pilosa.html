Rosa pilosa

Rosa pilosa, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fuzzy, glandular hairs that cover its leaves and stems. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Rosa pilosa is native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
