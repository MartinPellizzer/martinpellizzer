Rosa sempervirens var. pilosula

Rosa sempervirens var. pilosula, commonly known as the silky rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery foliage. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its common name. It produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in moderate climates.
