Rubus rufolanatus

Rubus rufolanatus, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and have a tart flavor. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has thorny stems that are often reddish in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Rubus rufolanatus is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its use in local cuisines.
