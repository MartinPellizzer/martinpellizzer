Rhododendron adenogynum

Rhododendron adenogynum, commonly known as the woolly rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that provides insulation against cold temperatures. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high elevations. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, appearing in clusters and blooming in the spring.

Rhododendron adenogynum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
