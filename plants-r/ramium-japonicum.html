Ramium japonicum

Ramium japonicum, commonly known as Japanese clover, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

Ramium japonicum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
