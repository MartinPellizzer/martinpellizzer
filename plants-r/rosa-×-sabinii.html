Rosa × sabinii

Rosa × sabinii, commonly known as the Rosa sabinii or the Sabin's rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the cross between Rosa bracteata and Rosa rugosa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and its ability to thrive in coastal and saline environments. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine, silvery hairs.

Rosa × sabinii is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
