Rubus scaberrimus microgene finitimus

Rubus scaberrimus microgene finitimus, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
