Rosa clinophylla var. glabra

Rosa clinophylla var. glabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf dogwood rose, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, which are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, and it is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic value.

The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
