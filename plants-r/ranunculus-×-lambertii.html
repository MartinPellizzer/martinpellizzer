Ranunculus × lambertii

Ranunculus × lambertii, commonly known as the Lambert's buttercup, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with trifoliate leaves and a hairy stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens with their bright hues.
