Rosa rubiginosa var. decipiens

Rosa rubiginosa var. decipiens, commonly known as the "false rose" or "bog rose," is a shrub native to the wetlands of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark red to purplish stems and small, fragrant pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in heathland and marshy areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions, making it a common sight in wetland ecosystems.
