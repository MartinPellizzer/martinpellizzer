Rosa communis var. analoga

Rosa communis var. analoga, commonly known as the common rose variant analoga, is a botanical variety characterized by its delicate pink flowers and thorny stems.

It is native to temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight. The plant produces fragrant blooms throughout the growing season, typically from spring to autumn. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
