Restio consimilis

Restio consimilis, commonly known as the similar restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, grass-like foliage that grows in dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
