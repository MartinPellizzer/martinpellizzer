Rhodomyrtus novoguineensis

Rhodomyrtus novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea rose myrtle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Rhodomyrtus novoguineensis is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its resilience to pests and diseases.
