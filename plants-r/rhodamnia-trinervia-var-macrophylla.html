Rhodamnia trinervia var. macrophylla

Rhodamnia trinervia var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf rhodamnia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in a trifoliate pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, usually pink or white, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or semi-coastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
