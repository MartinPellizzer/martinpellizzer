Rhododendron sperabile var. sperabile

Rhododendron sperabile var. sperabile, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five petals and a showy, glandular corolla.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
