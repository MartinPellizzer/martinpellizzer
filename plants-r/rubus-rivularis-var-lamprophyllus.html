Rubus rivularis var. lamprophyllus

Rubus rivularis var. lamprophyllus, commonly known as the shining leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces bristly, thorny stems.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
