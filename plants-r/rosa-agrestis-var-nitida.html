Rosa agrestis var. nitida

Rosa agrestis var. nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf rose, is a variant of the wild rose species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in various garden settings.
