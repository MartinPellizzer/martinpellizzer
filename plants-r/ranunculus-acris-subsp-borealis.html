Ranunculus acris subsp. borealis

Ranunculus acris subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the northern buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America and Eurasia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

The plant features bright yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, upright stem. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure.

The subspecies is characterized by its smaller, more compact flowers compared to the typical Ranunculus acris.
