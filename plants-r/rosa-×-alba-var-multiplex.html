Rosa × alba var. multiplex

Rosa × alba var. multiplex, commonly known as the white multiflora rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit.

It features multiple stems and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to eastern Asia and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and its thorny stems provide structural support.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
