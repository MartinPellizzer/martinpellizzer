Rapanea courboniana

Rapanea courboniana, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically dark purple when mature. It is valued for its durable timber and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened in some regions.
