Ranunculus platanifolius

Ranunculus platanifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar morphological features.

Ranunculus platanifolius is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
