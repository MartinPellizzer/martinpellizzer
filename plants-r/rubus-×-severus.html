Rubus × severus

Rubus × severus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, oval leaves with serrated edges and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a common sight in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
