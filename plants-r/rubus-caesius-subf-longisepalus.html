Rubus caesius subf. longisepalus

Rubus caesius subf.

longisepalus, commonly known as the long-sepaled blackberry, is a subspicies of the blackberry genus Rubus. It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated margins.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five sepals that are elongated and prominent. The fruit, a aggregate berry, is usually dark purple to black when mature.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
