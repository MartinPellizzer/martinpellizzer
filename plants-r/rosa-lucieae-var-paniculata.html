Rosa lucieae var. paniculata

Rosa lucieae var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and the production of large, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It is native to the southern United States and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
