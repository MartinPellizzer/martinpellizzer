Rhynchosia amabilis

Rhynchosia amabilis, commonly known as the sweetpea vine, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Rhynchosia amabilis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
