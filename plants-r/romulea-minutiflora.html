Romulea minutiflora

Romulea minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf romulea, is a small, bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It produces delicate, yellow flowers with a distinctive hooded structure, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
