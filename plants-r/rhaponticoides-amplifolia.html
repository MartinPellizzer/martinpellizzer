Rhaponticoides amplifolia

Rhaponticoides amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved rhapontic, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a cool, temperate climate.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
