Rubus omalus var. suprapilosus

Rubus omalus var. suprapilosus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are white and borne in clusters, adding to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued for both its ecological role and its use in local traditional medicine.
