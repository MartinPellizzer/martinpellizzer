Roegneria cheniae

Roegneria cheniae, commonly known as the Chinese wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many grasses. It is often found in moist, temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.

Roegneria cheniae is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health properties.
