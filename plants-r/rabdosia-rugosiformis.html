Rabdosia rugosiformis

Rabdosia rugosiformis, commonly known as the rough-leaved rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, serrated margin, giving it its common name. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or violet, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Rabdosia rugosiformis is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
