Rubus bonus

Rubus bonus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
