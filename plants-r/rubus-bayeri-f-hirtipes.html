Rubus bayeri f. hirtipes

Rubus bayeri f. hirtipes, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its hairy texture, particularly on the stems and leaves, is a distinguishing feature of this botanical variant.
