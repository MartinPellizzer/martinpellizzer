Rosa australis

Rosa australis, commonly known as the Australian rose, is a species native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green foliage. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets.

Rosa australis is adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in coastal and inland regions of Australia.
