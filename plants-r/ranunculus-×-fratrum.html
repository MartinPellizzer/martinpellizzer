Ranunculus × fratrum

Ranunculus × fratrum, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
