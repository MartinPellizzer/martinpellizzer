Rhododendron dichropeplum

Rhododendron dichropeplum, commonly known as the two-colored rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and thrives in acidic soil conditions. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive two-colored appearance on their upper and lower surfaces.

Rhododendron dichropeplum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
