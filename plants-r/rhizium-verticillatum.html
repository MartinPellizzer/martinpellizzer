Rhizium verticillatum

Rhizium verticillatum, commonly known as the spiral root plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive spiraling root structure.

This plant is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its roots form intricate, helical patterns that aid in anchoring the plant and absorbing nutrients efficiently. The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in soil stabilization.
