Rosa glauca var. jaccardii

Rosa glauca var. jaccardii, commonly known as the Jaccard's blue rose, is a rare and striking variation of the blue rose.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in areas of Kazakhstan and Tajikistan. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. Its foliage is characterized by a bluish-green hue, which adds to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its unique color and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its rare beauty.
