Rubus orthacanthus subsp. villarsianus

Rubus orthacanthus subsp.

villarsianus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible blackberries in the summer. Its fruit is valued for its flavor and is often used in jams and desserts.

The species is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
