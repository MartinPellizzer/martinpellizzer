Rubus granulatus subsp. munkacsensis

Rubus granulatus subsp.

munkacsensis, commonly known as the Hungarian raspberry, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Basin. It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, red berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
