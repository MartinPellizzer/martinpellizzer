Ranunculus arizonicus

Ranunculus arizonicus, commonly known as Arizona buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and desert areas. It has basal leaves that are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

Ranunculus arizonicus is an herbaceous perennial that produces a single flower per stem, usually blooming in spring.
