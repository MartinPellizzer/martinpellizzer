Rhododendron simiarum var. versicolor

Rhododendron simiarum var. versicolor, commonly known as the Variable Rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape.

The variety 'versicolor' is notable for its variable flower colors, which can change subtly depending on environmental conditions.
