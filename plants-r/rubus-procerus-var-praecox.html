Rubus procerus var. praecox

Rubus procerus var. praecox, commonly known as early bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during early spring. Its fruit, a red or black berry, ripens in late summer and is edible.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
