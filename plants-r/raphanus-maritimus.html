Raphanus maritimus

Raphanus maritimus, commonly known as sea radish, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Atlantic coasts of Europe and North Africa.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant features a thick, fleshy root that is often used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable. Its yellow flowers are borne in clusters and attract various pollinators.

Raphanus maritimus is well adapted to salt spray and can thrive in harsh maritime environments.
