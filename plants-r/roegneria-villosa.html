Roegneria villosa

Roegneria villosa, commonly known as the hairy bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, blue-green flowers.

Roegneria villosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
