Rumex angulatus

Rumex angulatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf dock, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and reddish stems. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The plant has been used historically in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
