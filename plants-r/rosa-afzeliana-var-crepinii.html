Rosa afzeliana var. crepinii

Rosa afzeliana var. crepinii, commonly known as the Crepin's rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fragrant, single or semi-double pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has glossy, ovate leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rosa genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
