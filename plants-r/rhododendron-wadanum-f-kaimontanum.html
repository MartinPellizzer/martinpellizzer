Rhododendron wadanum f. kaimontanum

Rhododendron wadanum f. kaimontanum, commonly known as the Kaimanawa rhododendron, is a hybrid form native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in native forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
