Roscoea purpurea var. auriculata

Roscoea purpurea var. auriculata, commonly known as the purple flame plant, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a rhizomatous perennial with thick, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces showy, orchid-like flowers in shades of pink and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blossoms make it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.
