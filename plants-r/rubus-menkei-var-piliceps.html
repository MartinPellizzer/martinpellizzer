Rubus menkei var. piliceps

Rubus menkei var. piliceps, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
