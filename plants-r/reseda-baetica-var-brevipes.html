Reseda baetica var. brevipes

Reseda baetica var. brevipes, commonly known as the Iberian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender stems and small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in early summer.

Reseda baetica var. brevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
