Ranunculus commemorans

Ranunculus commemorans, commonly known as the commemorated buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
