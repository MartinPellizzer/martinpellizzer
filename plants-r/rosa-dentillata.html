Rosa dentillata

Rosa dentillata, commonly known as the toothed rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are pinnately compound, with finely serrated leaflets that give the plant its common name. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to many landscapes.
