Rindera lanata

Rindera lanata, commonly known as the woolly rindera, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Rindera lanata thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and horticultural contexts.
