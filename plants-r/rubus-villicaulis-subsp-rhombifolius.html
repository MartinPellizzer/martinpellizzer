Rubus villicaulis subsp. rhombifolius

Rubus villicaulis subsp.

rhombifolius, commonly known as the rhombifolius blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of eastern North America. It is characterized by its rhomboid-shaped leaves, which are typically hairy and have a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, often recognized for its ornamental and ecological value.
