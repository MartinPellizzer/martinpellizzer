Rebutia rovidana

Rebutia rovidana, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to Mexico.

It features a swollen, colorful tuberous stem that often resembles a different species, giving it its unique appearance. The plant has small, spiny stems and produces yellow or orange flowers during the growing season. It is well-suited for rock gardens and containers due to its compact size and drought tolerance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, water-storing tissues.
