Rosa tomentella subsp. blondaeana

Rosa tomentella subsp.

blondaeana, commonly known as the silky blushing rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, almost fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, depending on the variety. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
