Rubus × cuneatifrons

Rubus × cuneatifrons, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (the cloudberry). The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and the fruits are usually dark red to black when mature.

This hybrid is often found in moist, temperate environments and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
