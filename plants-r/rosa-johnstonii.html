Rosa johnstonii

Rosa johnstonii, commonly known as the Johnston's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include simple, alternate leaves and aggregate fruits known as rose hips.

Rosa johnstonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
