Rubus procumbens var. roribaccus

Rubus procumbens var. roribaccus, commonly known as the creeping blackberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its flowers are white and clustered in heads, and it is known for its vigorous spreading habit through runners.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
