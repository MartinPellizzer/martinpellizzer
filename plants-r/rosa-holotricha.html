Rosa holotricha

Rosa holotricha, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and the Tian Shan. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and thorny stems, which provide protection against herbivores. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the rose genus.

Rosa holotricha is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
