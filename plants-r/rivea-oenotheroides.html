Rivea oenotheroides

Rivea oenotheroides, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, yellow flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant produces small, round, greenish-yellow berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

Rivea oenotheroides is often grown for its ornamental value and is adapted to full sun to partial shade.
