Rothmannia malleifera

Rothmannia malleifera, commonly known as the African milkwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Rothmannia malleifera is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
