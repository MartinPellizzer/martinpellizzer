Rubus villosus var. engelmannii

Rubus villosus var. engelmannii, commonly known as Engelmann's bramble, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, edible berries in the summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, rocky or sandy soils.

This species is often found in open woodlands, along stream banks, and in mountainous regions.
