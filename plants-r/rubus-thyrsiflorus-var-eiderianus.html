Rubus thyrsiflorus var. eiderianus

Rubus thyrsiflorus var. eiderianus, commonly known as the Eiderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, which are edible and have a tart flavor. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The Eiderberry is also valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
