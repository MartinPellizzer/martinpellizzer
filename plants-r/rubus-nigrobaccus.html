Rubus nigrobaccus

Rubus nigrobaccus, commonly known as the black-barked raspberry.

It is a species of bramble belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, usually red or black berries that are edible, though they are often described as having a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its vigorous growth habit.
