Rubus microthyrsus

Rubus microthyrsus, commonly known as the small-thyrsus blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southern United States, particularly in regions of Texas and Louisiana. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
