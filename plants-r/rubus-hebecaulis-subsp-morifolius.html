Rubus hebecaulis subsp. morifolius

Rubus hebecaulis subsp.

morifolius, commonly known as the purple-leaf bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, trifoliate leaves that often display a striking purple or reddish hue.

The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, edible berries. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
