Rosa orbicantula

Rosa orbicantula, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in cold, rocky environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Rosa orbicantula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
