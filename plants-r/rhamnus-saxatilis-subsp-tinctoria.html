Rhamnus saxatilis subsp. tinctoria

Rhamnus saxatilis subsp.

tinctoria, commonly known as the dwarf elder, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its fruit is a small, black drupe that ripens in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
