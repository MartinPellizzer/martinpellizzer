Reynoutria weyrichii

Reynoutria weyrichii, commonly known as Chinese water chestnut, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and the production of edible, starchy tubers. The plant typically grows in wet or aquatic environments, such as marshes and ponds. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, often floating on the water's surface.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three sepals and three petals, typically in shades of white or pale yellow.
