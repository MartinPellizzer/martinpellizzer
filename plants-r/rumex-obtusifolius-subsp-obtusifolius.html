Rumex obtusifolius subsp. obtusifolius

Rumex obtusifolius subsp.

obtusifolius, commonly known as broad-leaved dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family. It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along streambanks.

Its leaves and stems are sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent properties.
