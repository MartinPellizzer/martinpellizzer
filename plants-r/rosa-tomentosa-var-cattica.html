Rosa tomentosa var. cattica

Rosa tomentosa var. cattica, commonly known as the Cattica rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, thorny stems covered in a woolly, grayish pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets that are hairy on both sides. Its flowers are typically pale pink to white, with a faint scent, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
