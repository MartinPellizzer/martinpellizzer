Rhododendron viscosum f. nitidum

Rhododendron viscosum f. nitidum, commonly known as the glossy alpine azalea, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It features dense, dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a subtle, sweet fragrance. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
