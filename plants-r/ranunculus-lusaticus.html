Ranunculus lusaticus

Ranunculus lusaticus, commonly known as the Lusatian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features palmately divided, bright green leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions and is protected in others.
