Rubus lasiodermis

Rubus lasiodermis, commonly known as the rough-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are white and have five petals, and it thrives in moist, wooded areas.

Rubus lasiodermis is an important species for both wildlife and ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in local ecosystems.
