Rosa communis glabrae

Rosa communis glabrae, commonly known as the smooth rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, smooth stems and fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces oval, green leaves with serrated edges, and its hips, or fruit, are typically red and contain seeds. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.

The smooth rose is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
