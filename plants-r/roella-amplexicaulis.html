Roella amplexicaulis

Roella amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping roella, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, clasping leaves that encircle the stem. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Roella amplexicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
