Rhododendron ebianense

Rhododendron ebianense, commonly known as the Ebian rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, preferring acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to deep red.

Rhododendron ebianense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
