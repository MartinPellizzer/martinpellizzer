Rapuntium rugulosum

Rapuntium rugulosum, commonly known as the rough chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, wrinkled leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in gardens. It produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across various habitats.

Rapuntium rugulosum is valued for its edible leaves, which can be used in salads or cooked as a vegetable.
