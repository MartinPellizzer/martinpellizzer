Restrepia contorta

Restrepia contorta, commonly known as the twisted restrepia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a short, stout rhizome and a dense rosette of narrow, leathery leaves. The plant produces inflorescences with several small, greenish-white flowers that have a distinctive, intricate structure. It prefers cool, humid environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
