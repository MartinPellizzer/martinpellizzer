Rollinia puberula

Rollinia puberula, commonly known as the rough custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, hairy, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are clustered in branched inflorescences. Its fruit is a spherical, spiky berry that turns from green to yellow when ripe.

The fruit contains a juicy, aromatic pulp with numerous seeds, making it both ornamental and edible.
