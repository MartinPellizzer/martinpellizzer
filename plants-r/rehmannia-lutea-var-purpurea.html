Rehmannia lutea var. purpurea

Rehmannia lutea var. purpurea, commonly known as purple-flowered Chinese angelica, is a variant of the Rehmannia lutea species native to China.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers and fleshy, reddish roots that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is part of the gentian family (Gentianaceae). Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and a tall, flowering stem.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its medicinal properties.
