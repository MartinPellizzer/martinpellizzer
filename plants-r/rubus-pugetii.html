Rubus pugetii

Rubus pugetii, commonly known as the Olympic mountain raspberry, is a species of brambles native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Rubus pugetii plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinator populations.
