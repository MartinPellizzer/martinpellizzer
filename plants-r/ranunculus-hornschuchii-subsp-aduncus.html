Ranunculus hornschuchii subsp. aduncus

Ranunculus hornschuchii subsp.

aduncus, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest understories. Its botanical characteristics include a short, stout stem and a basal rosette of leaves.

It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
