Rhipsalis floccosa subsp. floccosa

Rhipsalis floccosa subsp.

floccosa, commonly known as the woolly mistletoe, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems that give it a fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruits are small, yellow berries that contain seeds dispersed by birds.

This species is often found growing as an epiphyte on trees in humid, tropical environments.
