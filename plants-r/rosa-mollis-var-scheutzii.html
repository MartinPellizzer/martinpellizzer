Rosa mollis var. scheutzii

Rosa mollis var. scheutzii, commonly known as the Scheutz's soft rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a soft, velvety pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
