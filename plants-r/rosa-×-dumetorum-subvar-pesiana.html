Rosa × dumetorum subvar. pesiana

Rosa × dumetorum subvar. pesiana, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of white down.

This species is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.
