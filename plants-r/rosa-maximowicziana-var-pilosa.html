Rosa maximowicziana var. pilosa

Rosa maximowicziana var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, hairy foliage, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with oval-shaped leaflets and a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
