Rinorea chevalieri

Rinorea chevalieri, commonly known as the purple-flowered rinorea, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a shrub native to Madagascar, characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Rinorea chevalieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
