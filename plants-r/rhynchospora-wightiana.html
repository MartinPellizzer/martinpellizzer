Rhynchospora wightiana

Rhynchospora wightiana, commonly known as the Wight's spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it relies on wind pollination.

Rhynchospora wightiana plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
