Rubus caesius subsp. vollmannii

Rubus caesius subsp.

vollmannii, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a shrub native to North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, oval leaves.

The plant produces bright red, aggregate fruits that are edible and widely used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
