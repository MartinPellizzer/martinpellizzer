Rubus macrostachys var. micradenes

Rubus macrostachys var. micradenes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and have a tart flavor. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often armed with sharp prickles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in local ecosystems.
