Rhipidoglossum pachyrhizum, commonly known as the thick-rooted tongue fern, is a unique member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick rhizomes that store water, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Rhipidoglossum pachyrhizum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in maintaining soil stability and supporting local biodiversity.
