Raphanus raphanistrum subsp. fugax

Raphanus raphanistrum subsp.

fugax, commonly known as the wild radish, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its erect stem, lobed leaves, and small white or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces round, smooth-seeded fruits that mature in late summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
