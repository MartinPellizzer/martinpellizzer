Rubus obscurus microgene obscuriformis

Rubus obscurus microgene obscuriformis, commonly known as the dark form of the blackberry plant, is a species characterized by its small, dark-colored berries.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of North America. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and thickets. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, though small, is edible and has a tart flavor, often used in local culinary traditions.
