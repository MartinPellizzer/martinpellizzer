Ranunculus subfirmicaulis

Ranunculus subfirmicaulis, commonly known as the firm-stemmed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves with a hairy surface and produces showy yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is characterized by its erect, somewhat woody stems. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy flowers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
