Rubus ursinus var. eastwoodianus

Rubus ursinus var. eastwoodianus, commonly known as the Eastwood's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and local biodiversity.
