Rubus phoenicolasius f. aureiceps

Rubus phoenicolasius f. aureiceps, commonly known as the golden-tipped raspberry, is a variant of the red raspberry species characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow bracts.

This plant is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are typically pink to white, and the fruit, which is similar to regular raspberries, has a slightly sweeter flavor. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, making it a popular choice for gardens.

It requires well-drained soil and full sun to flourish.
