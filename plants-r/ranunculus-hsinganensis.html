Ranunculus hsinganensis

Ranunculus hsinganensis, commonly known as the Hsingan buttercup, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. It has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, and its roots are fibrous and rhizomatous.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
