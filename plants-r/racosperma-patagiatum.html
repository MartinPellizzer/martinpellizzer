Racosperma patagiatum

Racosperma patagiatum, commonly known as the Patagonian acacia, is a shrub native to southern South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in local traditional practices.

Its ecological importance is significant in the regions where it naturally occurs.
