Rosa quasi-inserta

Rosa quasi-inserta, commonly known as the narrowleaf rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers. The leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have five to seven leaflets that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, followed by oval, red-orange hips.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
