Rosa cinnamomea var. foliolis

Rosa cinnamomea var. foliolis, commonly known as the cinnamon rose, is a unique variant of the cinnamon rose species.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it an elegant appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in the spring. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
