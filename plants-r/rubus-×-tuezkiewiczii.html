Rubus × tuezkiewiczii

Rubus × tuezkiewiczii, commonly known as the Tuezkiewicz raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are similar in appearance to other raspberry species. Its botanical features include palmate leaves and a complex flowering structure typical of the Rubus genus.

Due to its ornamental and culinary potential, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for local use.
