Rosa canina var. acharii

Rosa canina var. acharii, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a variety of the dog rose that is native to the Caucasus region.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and produces pink to red flowers in the spring. The plant has small, oval leaves with serrated edges and is known for its fragrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous areas.

The fruit, a red hip, is edible and has been used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
