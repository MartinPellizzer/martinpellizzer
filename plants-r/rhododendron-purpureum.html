Rhododendron purpureum

Rhododendron purpureum, commonly known as the purple rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a notable ornamental plant in gardens and natural habitats.

Rhododendron purpureum is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
