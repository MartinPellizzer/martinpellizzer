Rosa jundzillii f. silvatica

Rosa jundzillii f. silvatica, commonly known as the silvatic wild rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in well-drained soils and partial shade. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it produces hips that are edible and used in traditional medicine.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
