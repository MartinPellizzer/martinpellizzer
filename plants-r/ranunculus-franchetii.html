Ranunculus franchetii

Ranunculus franchetii, commonly known as the Franchet's buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a short stem and produces small, bead-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
