Rubus liebmannii var. hidalgensis

Rubus liebmannii var. hidalgensis, commonly known as the Hidalgo blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
