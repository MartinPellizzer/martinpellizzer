Ramona stachyoides

Ramona stachyoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf stachys, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
