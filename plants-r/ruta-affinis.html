Ruta affinis

Ruta affinis, commonly known as common rue, is a perennial shrub native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
