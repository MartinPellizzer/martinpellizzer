Rosa micrantha var. ferox

Rosa micrantha var. ferox, commonly known as the Chinese prickly rose, is a variant of the Rosa micrantha species native to China.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces rose-like flowers that are often clustered in groups. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and hardy nature, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
