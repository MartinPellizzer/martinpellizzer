Rangium saxatile

Rangium saxatile, commonly known as the rock lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid regions.

It typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, often found in mountainous or coastal areas. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and usually have a pale yellow or greenish hue.

Rangium saxatile is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
