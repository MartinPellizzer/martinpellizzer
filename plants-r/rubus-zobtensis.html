Rubus zobtensis

Rubus zobtensis, commonly known as the Zobtenis bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen in the summer. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
