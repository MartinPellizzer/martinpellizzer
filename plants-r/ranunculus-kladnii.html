Ranunculus kladnii

Ranunculus kladnii, commonly known as the Kladni's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant features a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bright yellow with numerous stamens and a short, broad pistil, characteristic of the Ranunculaceae family.

Ranunculus kladnii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
