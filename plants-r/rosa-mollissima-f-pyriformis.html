Rosa mollissima f. pyriformis

Rosa mollissima f. pyriformis, commonly known as the pear-shaped rose, is a unique cultivar of the Rosa mollissima species.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and is often grown as a shrub or small tree. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and densely packed, providing an attractive appearance throughout the growing season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is suitable for garden use in temperate climates.
