Radermachera frondosa

Radermachera frondosa, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen maple, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its seasonal color changes and compact growth habit.
