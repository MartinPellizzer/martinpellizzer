Ranunculus digitatus

Ranunculus digitatus, commonly known as the five-finger buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers consist of numerous small petals arranged in a star-like pattern, and it blooms from spring to early summer.

Ranunculus digitatus is often found in clusters and can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
