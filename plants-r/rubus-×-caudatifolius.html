Rubus × caudatifolius

Rubus × caudatifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its elongated, serrated leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
