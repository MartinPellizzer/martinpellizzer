Ranunculus auricomus subsp. minutifrons

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

minutifrons, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its small, delicate yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is distinguished by its smaller size and more refined floral structure compared to the typical form of Ranunculus auricomus.
