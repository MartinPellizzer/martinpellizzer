Rosa canina var. globosa

Rosa canina var. globosa, commonly known as the globular dog rose, is a variant of the dog rose native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, globular shape, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant pink or white flowers in early summer, followed by red-orange hips that are rich in vitamin C. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
