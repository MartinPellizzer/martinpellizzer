Rosa arietaria

Rosa arietaria, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves with serrated edges. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Rosa arietaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
