Racosperma dorothea

Racosperma dorothea, commonly known as the Dorothea acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale green flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a woody stem and small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
