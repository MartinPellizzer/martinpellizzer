Ranunculus glabricaulis

Ranunculus glabricaulis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals, typically in shades of yellow or white. The plant has smooth, hairless stems and basal leaves that are trifoliate or palmately lobed. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of vibrant color to its natural habitat.
