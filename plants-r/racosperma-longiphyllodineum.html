Racosperma longiphyllodineum

Racosperma longiphyllodineum, commonly known as the long-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and bipinnate leaves that are long and narrow. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact globular heads, which are followed by flat, leathery pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soils.

The species is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
