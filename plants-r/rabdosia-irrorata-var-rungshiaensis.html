Rabdosia irrorata var. rungshiaensis

Rabdosia irrorata var. rungshiaensis, commonly known as the Rungshia Mountain Rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in northern Vietnam and southern China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often in mountainous regions. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
