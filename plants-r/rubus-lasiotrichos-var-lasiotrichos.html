Rubus lasiotrichos var. lasiotrichos

Rubus lasiotrichos var. lasiotrichos, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
