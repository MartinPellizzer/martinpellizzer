Rosa afzeliana subvar. impropria

Rosa afzeliana subvar. impropria, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a species native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, semi-double flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, red or yellow fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have a serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its continuous flowering and hardy nature.
