Rosa communis var. subsquarrosa

Rosa communis var. subsquarrosa, commonly known as the squarrose rose, is a variety of the common rose characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

The plant features tightly curled, squarrose petals that give it a unique, almost compact appearance. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. The shrub has thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves that are aromatic when crushed.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
