Rubus podophylloides

Rubus podophylloides, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and prickly stems, which support its classification within the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Botanically, it features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring.

Its ecological role includes providing food for wildlife and serving as a habitat for various insects.
