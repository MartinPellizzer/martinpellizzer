Rhodoleia henryi

Rhodoleia henryi, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Rhodoleia henryi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
