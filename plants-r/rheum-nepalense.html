Rheum nepalense

Rheum nepalense, commonly known as the Himalayan rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces tall, reddish-green stalks that bear clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Rheum nepalense is part of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other rhubarb species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
