Rorippa kurdica

Rorippa kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish watercress, is a small flowering plant native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in wet or aquatic environments, such as marshes and stream sides. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves and small white or yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.
