Rosa indica incurva

Rosa indica incurva, commonly known as the Himalayan pink rose, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces large, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to red in color. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and thorny stems typical of many rose species. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
