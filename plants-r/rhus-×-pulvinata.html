Rhus × pulvinata

Rhus × pulvinata, commonly known as the staghorn sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternately arranged and have a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, fuzzy berries in the fall. Its bark is thick and rough, with a distinctive, scaly texture that gives it its common name.

Rhus × pulvinata is often found in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in woodland edges.
