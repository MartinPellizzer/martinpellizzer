Rhacodiscus lindmanii

Rhacodiscus lindmanii, commonly known as the golden globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, globular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Rhacodiscus lindmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens.
