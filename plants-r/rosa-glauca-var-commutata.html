Rosa glauca var. commutata

Rosa glauca var. commutata, commonly known as the blue rose or the white rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa glauca species.

It is characterized by its pale blue or white flowers, which are often described as having a soft, ethereal appearance. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch to gardens and landscapes.
