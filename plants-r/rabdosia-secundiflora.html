Rabdosia secundiflora

Rabdosia secundiflora, commonly known as the clustered rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rabdosia secundiflora is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
