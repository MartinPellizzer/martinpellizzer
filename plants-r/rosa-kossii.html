Rosa kossii

Rosa kossii, commonly known as the Kossii rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

Rosa kossii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
