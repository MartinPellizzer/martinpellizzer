Rudbeckia heterophylla

Rudbeckia heterophylla, commonly known as the variable coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, with a prominent central disk. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features lance-shaped to heart-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape depending on the environment. Rudbeckia heterophylla thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and pollinator habitats.
