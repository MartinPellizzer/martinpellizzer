Rosa mollis f. rigens

Rosa mollis f. rigens, commonly known as the stiff-fruited shrub rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa mollis species, characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems.

It produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage is dark green and leathery, providing an attractive evergreen appearance throughout the year.

This rose is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature, making it suitable for hedging and small garden spaces.
