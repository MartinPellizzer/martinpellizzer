Rubia hirta

Rubia hirta, commonly known as hairy bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are oval-shaped with hairy surfaces, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Rubia hirta is often found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils.
