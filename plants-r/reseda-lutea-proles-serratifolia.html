Reseda lutea proles serratifolia

Reseda lutea proles serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated yellow weed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are alternate, serrated, and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in dry meadows.

The plant has been used historically for its dye-producing properties and as a medicinal herb.
