Rhododendron rupicola var. rupicola

Rhododendron rupicola var. rupicola, commonly known as the rock rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, humid climates. It is often found growing on rocky slopes and in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded landscapes.
