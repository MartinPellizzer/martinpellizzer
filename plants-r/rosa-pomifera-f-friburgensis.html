Rosa pomifera f. friburgensis

Rosa pomifera f. friburgensis, commonly known as the Friburgensis rose, is a unique horticultural form of the Rosa pomifera species.

It is characterized by its large, edible rose hips that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

This variety is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its practical uses in herbal remedies and food preparation.
