Rubus obscurus var. devonicus

Rubus obscurus var. devonicus, commonly known as the Devon raspberry, is a shrub native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and hedgerows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
