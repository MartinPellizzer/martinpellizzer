Rubus subcoreanus

Rubus subcoreanus, commonly known as the Korean raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, red berries. The plant typically grows in forested areas and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces edible fruits that are similar to raspberries.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
