Rosa × dumetorum var. cinerosa

Rosa × dumetorum var. cinerosa, commonly known as the blackberry rose, is a hybrid shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and leaves that have a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible hips that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental garden plant.

Its unique appearance and hardiness make it a popular choice for both ornamental and hedgerow planting.
