Raphanocarpus asperifolius

Raphanocarpus asperifolius, commonly known as the rough-leafed radish, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces round, fleshy roots that are often used as edible vegetables in certain regions. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the mustard family.
