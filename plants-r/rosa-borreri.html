Rosa borreri

Rosa borreri, commonly known as the Borreri rose, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers typically bloom in spring and are followed by small, red-orange fruits known as hips.

Rosa borreri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
