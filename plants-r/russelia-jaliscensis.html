Russelia jaliscensis

Russelia jaliscensis, commonly known as the Mexican flamebush, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Russelia jaliscensis is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.
