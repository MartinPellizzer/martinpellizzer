Rosa kosingiana

Rosa kosingiana, commonly known as the Kosi rose, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, pinnately compound leaves and produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white. Rosa kosingiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of climates.
