Rubus lacertosus

Rubus lacertosus, commonly known as the thorny raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife.
