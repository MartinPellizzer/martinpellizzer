Rosa × microtypus

Rosa × microtypus, commonly known as the miniature rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces semi-upright or trailing stems with thorny stems, and its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves. Rosa × microtypus thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
