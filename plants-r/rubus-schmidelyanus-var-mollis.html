Rubus schmidelyanus var. mollis

Rubus schmidelyanus var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-furred bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
