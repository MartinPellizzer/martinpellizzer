Rubus moluccanus var. reinwardtii

Rubus moluccanus var. reinwardtii, commonly known as the Moluccan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the production of dark purple to black berries. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and thorned stems, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them a valued fruit in local cuisines.
