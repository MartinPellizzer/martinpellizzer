Rhus sinica f. glabra

Rhus sinica f. glabra, commonly known as smooth Chinese sumac, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often grayish bark and compound leaves that are pinnately arranged. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by red, spiny fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
