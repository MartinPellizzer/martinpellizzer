Rhododendron glandulosum

Rhododendron glandulosum, commonly known as the hairy rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are usually leathery and dark green, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Rhododendron glandulosum is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
