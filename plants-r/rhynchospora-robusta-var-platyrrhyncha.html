Rhynchospora robusta var. platyrrhyncha

Rhynchospora robusta var. platyrrhyncha, commonly known as the broad-leafed spike-rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of numerous small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and its leaves are broad and grass-like, often with a slightly waxy coating. The flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate spikes, a feature common in the Cyperaceae family.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
