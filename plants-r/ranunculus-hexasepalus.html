Ranunculus hexasepalus

Ranunculus hexasepalus, commonly known as the six-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are typically lobed or divided, with a hairy surface. Its flowers have six sepals and numerous stamens, giving it a distinctive, almost star-like appearance.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
