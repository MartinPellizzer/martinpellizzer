Rumex aquaticus subsp. patientia

Rumex aquaticus subsp.

patientia, commonly known as water sorrel, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its succulent, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in shallow water or moist soil.

The plant is notable for its ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels, making it a common species in freshwater habitats. Its leaves are often eaten by herbivores, and it plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.

The subspecies patientia is distinguished by its more robust growth and greater tolerance to varying environmental conditions compared to other subspecies.
