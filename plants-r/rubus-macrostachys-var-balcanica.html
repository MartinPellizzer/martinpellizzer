Rubus macrostachys var. balcanica

Rubus macrostachys var. balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan blackberry, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. The plant features thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
