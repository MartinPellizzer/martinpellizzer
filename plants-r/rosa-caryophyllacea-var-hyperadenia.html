Rosa caryophyllacea var. hyperadenia

Rosa caryophyllacea var. hyperadenia, commonly known as the hyperadenia rose, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features thorny stems and leathery, dark green leaves that are adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are typically borne in terminal clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its resilience and unique floral display in challenging environments.
