Rubus hirtus var. amplifolius

Rubus hirtus var. amplifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically hairy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red when ripe, though they may vary in color depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
