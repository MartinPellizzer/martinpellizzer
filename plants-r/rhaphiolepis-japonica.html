Rhaphiolepis japonica

Rhaphiolepis japonica, commonly known as Japanese firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly serrated. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright orange-red berries in the fall. These berries are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
