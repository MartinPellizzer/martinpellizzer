Rosa firmula var. typica

Rosa firmula var. typica, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy blossoms that attract pollinators. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Rosa firmula var. typica is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardiness in cooler climates.
