Rubus versipellis

Rubus versipellis, commonly known as the variable blackberry.

It is a species of bramble belonging to the Rubus genus in the family Rosaceae. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that range in color from red to black.

The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in a variety of soil types.
