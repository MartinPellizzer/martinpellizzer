Rubus procerus var. brevistamineus

Rubus procerus var. brevistamineus, commonly known as the short-stamined blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its erect or spreading growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins and a hairy underside.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
