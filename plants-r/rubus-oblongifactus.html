Rubus oblongifactus

Rubus oblongifactus, commonly known as the oblongleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in woodland edges and moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
