Rubus lucensis

Rubus lucensis, commonly known as the Lucens blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe, particularly in countries like Romania and Bulgaria. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine due to their high antioxidant content.
