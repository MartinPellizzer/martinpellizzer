Rosa sylvularum

Rosa sylvularum, commonly known as the wild rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. Its hips, which are the fruit, are red and contain seeds, and they are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Rosa sylvularum plays an important role in both ecological systems and horticultural practices.
