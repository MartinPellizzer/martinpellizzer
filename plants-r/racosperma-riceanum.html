Racosperma riceanum

Racosperma riceanum, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow floral spikes and feathery foliage, which are typical of the legume family. The plant has adapted to saline and sandy soils, making it resilient in coastal environments. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

Racosperma riceanum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
