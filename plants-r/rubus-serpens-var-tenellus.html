Rubus serpens var. tenellus

Rubus serpens var. tenellus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub native to parts of North America, characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus.
