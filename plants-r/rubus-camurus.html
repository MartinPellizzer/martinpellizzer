Rubus camurus

Rubus camurus, commonly known as brambles or blackberries, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that develop into edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

Rubus camurus is known for its hardy nature and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative growth.
