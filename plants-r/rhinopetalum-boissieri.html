Rhinopetalum boissieri

Rhinopetalum boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The orchid thrives in humid, well-drained environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
