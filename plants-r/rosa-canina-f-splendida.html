Rosa canina f. splendida

Rosa canina f. splendida, commonly known as the splendid dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its showy, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces red, hairy hips that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy shrub suitable for gardens and natural habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
