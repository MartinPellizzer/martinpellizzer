Ranunculus luteolus

Ranunculus luteolus, commonly known as the yellow avens, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a distinctive, hairy texture.

Ranunculus luteolus is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
