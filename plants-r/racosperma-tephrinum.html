Racosperma tephrinum

Racosperma tephrinum, commonly known as the tephritid acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in sandy or disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of Australian flora.
