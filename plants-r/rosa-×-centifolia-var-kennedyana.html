Rosa × centifolia var. kennedyana

Rosa × centifolia var. kennedyana, commonly known as the Kennedy's Damask rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its highly fragrant, semi-double to fully double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It is native to the Mediterranean region and is cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.

Its flowers are often used in the production of essential oils and traditional floral waters.
