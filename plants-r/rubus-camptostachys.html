Rubus camptostachys

Rubus camptostachys, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
