Rubus vigoi

Rubus vigoi, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white or pale pink.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine for their nutritional and antioxidant properties.
