Ramischia secunda var. dispersiflora

Ramischia secunda var. dispersiflora, commonly known as the scattered-flower ramischia, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its delicate, sprawling growth habit.

It produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are borne on slender, branching stems, giving it a fluffy appearance. The plant is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a soft, velvety texture.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare flora.
