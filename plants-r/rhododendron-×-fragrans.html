Rhododendron × fragrans

Rhododendron × fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron catawbiense and Rhododendron maximum.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 feet, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is native to the eastern United States and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The fragrant aroma of its blossoms adds to its appeal, making it a popular choice for horticultural enthusiasts.
