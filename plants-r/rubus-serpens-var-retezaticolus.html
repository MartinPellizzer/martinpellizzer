Rubus serpens var. retezaticolus

Rubus serpens var. retezaticolus, commonly known as the Mexican blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to Mexico.

It is a shrub characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Botanically, it is notable for its horticultural significance and adaptability to different climates.
