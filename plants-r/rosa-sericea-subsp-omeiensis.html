Rosa sericea subsp. omeiensis

Rosa sericea subsp.

omeiensis, commonly known as the Omei rose, is a shrub native to China, specifically the Sichuan province. It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces fragrant pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. Its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its common name.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
