Rauvolfia caffra

Rauvolfia caffra, commonly known as African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their hypotensive properties.

Rauvolfia caffra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
