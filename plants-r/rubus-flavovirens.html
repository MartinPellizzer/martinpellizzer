Rubus flavovirens

Rubus flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of East Asia, including China and Japan, where it thrives in forested areas and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It produces white or pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible berries.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
