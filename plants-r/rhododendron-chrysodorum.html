Rhododendron chrysodorum

Rhododendron chrysodorum, commonly known as the golden flame rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its striking clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron chrysodorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
