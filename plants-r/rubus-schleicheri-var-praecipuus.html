Rubus schleicheri var. praecipuus

Rubus schleicheri var. praecipuus, commonly known as the Schleicher's raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, often reddish flowers. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
