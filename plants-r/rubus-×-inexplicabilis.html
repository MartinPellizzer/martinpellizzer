Rubus × inexplicabilis

Rubus × inexplicabilis, commonly known as the blackberry or bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are composed of tiny drupelets. The plant is native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in a variety of soil types. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces flowers that are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit, which ripens in summer, is valued for its sweet-tart flavor and is widely cultivated for both commercial and culinary purposes.
