Ribesiodes scandens

Ribesiodes scandens, commonly known as the trailing forest honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full sun. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are often dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. Its showy, tubular flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

The plant produces small, red berries that persist into autumn, providing food for wildlife.
