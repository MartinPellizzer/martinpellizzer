Rubus × coarctatifrons

Rubus × coarctatifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to the western United States and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open woodlands. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges and a distinctive narrow shape.

The species is valued for its edible berries, which have a tart flavor and are used in culinary applications.
