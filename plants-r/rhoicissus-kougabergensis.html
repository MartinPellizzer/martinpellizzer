Rhoicissus kougabergensis

Rhoicissus kougabergensis, commonly known as the Kougaberg grape, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to edible, sweet berries. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in rocky areas, particularly in the Kougaberg region.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
