Rubus banningii typicus

Rubus banningii typicus, commonly known as the Banning's blackberry, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is valued for its intense flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
