Restio sonderianus

Restio sonderianus, commonly known as the Sonder's restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal and sandy habitats, often found in fynbos vegetation. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes. It is adapted to dry conditions and is often drought-tolerant.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
