Rosa viscaria var. latifolia

Rosa viscaria var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf evergreen rose, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that remain on the plant throughout the winter. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for garden landscapes.
