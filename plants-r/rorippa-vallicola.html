Rorippa vallicola

Rorippa vallicola, commonly known as the valley watercress, is a flowering plant native to moist environments in Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous species. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often found in stream valleys and wetlands. Its small, white flowers are arranged in racemes, and it produces small, elongated fruits that contain several seeds.

Rorippa vallicola is valued for its edible leaves, which are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
