Rubus argenteus var. flexicaulis

Rubus argenteus var. flexicaulis, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, making it a popular choice for gardens and small-scale cultivation.
