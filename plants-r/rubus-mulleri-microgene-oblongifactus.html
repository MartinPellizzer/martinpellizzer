Rubus mulleri microgene oblongifactus

Rubus mulleri microgene oblongifactus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupelets, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
