Robinia tragacanthoides

Robinia tragacanthoides, commonly known as the Chinese honey locust, is a deciduous legume native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves that are typically bipinnate, with small, rounded leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. The plant produces yellowish-green pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
