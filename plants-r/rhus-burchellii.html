Rhus burchellii

Rhus burchellii, commonly known as the African sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 11 to 21 leaflets, and its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces smooth, greenish-gray fruits that turn red when mature, and it is often used in traditional medicine. Rhus burchellii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry landscapes.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
