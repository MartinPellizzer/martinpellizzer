Rosa rugosa var. chamissoniana

Rosa rugosa var. chamissoniana, commonly known as the Chamisson's rose, is a shrub-like species native to coastal areas of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and broad, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to salty air and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in coastal environments.

Its natural range extends from New England to the Pacific Northwest, where it thrives in maritime climates.
