Rubus schleicheri var. bucegiensis

Rubus schleicheri var. bucegiensis, commonly known as the Bucegi raspberry, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains of Romania.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in woodland and mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
