Rourea nitida

Rourea nitida, commonly known as silverleaf rourea, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
