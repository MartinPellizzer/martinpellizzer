Rosa virginiana var. glandulosa

Rosa virginiana var. glandulosa, commonly known as the fragrant wild rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its hips, which are red and fuzzy, mature in the fall and contain seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
