Ranunculus toftensis

Ranunculus toftensis, commonly known as Toft's buttercup, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland areas and meadows. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five to eight petals and numerous stamens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
