Rubus acuminatus var. acuminatus

Rubus acuminatus var. acuminatus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny areas.

The botanical features of this plant make it both a valuable food source and a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
