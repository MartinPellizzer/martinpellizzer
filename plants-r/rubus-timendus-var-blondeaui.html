Rubus timendus var. blondeaui

Rubus timendus var. blondeaui, commonly known as the golden raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-red berries that ripen in the summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
