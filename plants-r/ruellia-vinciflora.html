Ruellia vinciflora

Ruellia vinciflora, commonly known as the purpleleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its attractive, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep purple or bronze color. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Ruellia vinciflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.
