Royena pallens var. dregei

Royena pallens var. dregei, commonly known as the white-veined rock fig, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves with distinctive white veins that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
