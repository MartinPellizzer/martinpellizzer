Rosa pouzinii var. albidiflora

Rosa pouzinii var. albidiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered pouzinii rose, is a shrub-like perennial native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It features slender, arching stems covered in dense, thornless foliage. The plant produces showy, single or semi-double flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, serrated, and arranged alternately along the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
