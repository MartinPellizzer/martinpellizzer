Rhaphidophora eximia

Rhaphidophora eximia, commonly known as the Black Magic Monstera, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves with prominent, irregularly shaped splits. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often dark and glossy, giving it a dramatic appearance.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and humidity to flourish.
