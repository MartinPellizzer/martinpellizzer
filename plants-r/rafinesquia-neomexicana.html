Rafinesquia neomexicana

Rafinesquia neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a thick, branching stem.

Rafinesquia neomexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
