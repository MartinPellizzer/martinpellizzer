Ranunculus auricomus subsp. tenuisectus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

tenuisectus, commonly known as the slender-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in early spring.

The leaves are finely divided and have a distinctive, feathery appearance, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic sap.
