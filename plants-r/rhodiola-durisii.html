Rhodiola durisii

Rhodiola durisii, commonly known as the Tibetan rhodiola, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth form. The plant produces small, pinkish to white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Rhodiola durisii is adapted to cold, arid environments and possesses a deep root system for moisture retention.

It is also known for its potential adaptogenic properties, making it a subject of interest in traditional and modern herbal medicine.
