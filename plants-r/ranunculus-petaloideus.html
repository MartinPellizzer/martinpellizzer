Ranunculus petaloideus

Ranunculus petaloideus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals, giving it a delicate, almost floral appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps retain moisture.

Ranunculus petaloideus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with adequate water and partial shade.
