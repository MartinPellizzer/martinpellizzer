Ranunculus schweinfurthii

Ranunculus schweinfurthii, commonly known as the Schweinfurth's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Ranunculus schweinfurthii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
