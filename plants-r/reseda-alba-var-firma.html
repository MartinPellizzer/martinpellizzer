Reseda alba var. firma

Reseda alba var. firma, commonly known as white madder, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant produces slender, erect stems that bear clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its roots contain a yellow dye, which has been historically used in textile dyeing.

Reseda alba var. firma is typically found in dry, calcareous soils and thrives in full sun.
