Rinorea gentlei

Rinorea gentlei, commonly known as the velvetleaf or gentle rinorea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding a delicate appearance to its foliage. Rinorea gentlei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive leaves and manageable growth habit.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil.
