Rumex neglectus

Rumex neglectus, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in loose clusters, with greenish-white petals.

Rumex neglectus is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
