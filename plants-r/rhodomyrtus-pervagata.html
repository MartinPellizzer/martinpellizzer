Rhodomyrtus pervagata

Rhodomyrtus pervagata, commonly known as the Chinese firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, red or orange berries that appear in clusters, often attracting birds and other wildlife. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant flowers in the spring. It thrives in warm climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant foliage and colorful fruit.

Rhodomyrtus pervagata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
