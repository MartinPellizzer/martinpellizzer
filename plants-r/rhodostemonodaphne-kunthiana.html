Rhodostemonodaphne kunthiana

Rhodostemonodaphne kunthiana, commonly known as the pink-flowered daphne, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, yellowish berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.
