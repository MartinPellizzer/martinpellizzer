Rourea acropetala

Rourea acropetala, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Rourea acropetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
