Rubus vulgaris proles commutatus

Rubus vulgaris proles commutatus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and aggregate fruits composed of small drupelets. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its vigorous growth and ability to spread through runners.
