Rubus hirsutus

Rubus hirsutus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, usually white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus hirsutus is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.
