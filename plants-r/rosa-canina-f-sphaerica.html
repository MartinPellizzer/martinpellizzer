Rosa canina f. sphaerica

Rosa canina f. sphaerica, commonly known as the spherical rose, is a horticultural form of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its rounded, compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are pinnate, with five to seven leaflets, and the fruits are red, hairy hips that mature in autumn.

This variety is relatively hardy and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
