Rubus pruinifer

Rubus pruinifer, commonly known as the pruinifer blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are white with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
