Rosa canina subsp. pouzinii

Rosa canina subsp.

pouzinii, commonly known as the Caucasian rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is a shrub with thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, hairy fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its historical use in traditional medicine.
