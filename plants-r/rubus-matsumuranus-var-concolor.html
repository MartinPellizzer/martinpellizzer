Rubus matsumuranus var. concolor

Rubus matsumuranus var. concolor, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black fruit, which is typically small and firm. The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

The fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a popular choice for jams and preserves.
