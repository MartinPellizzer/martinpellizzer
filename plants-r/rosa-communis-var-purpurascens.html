Rosa communis var. purpurascens

Rosa communis var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered rose, is a cultivar prized for its vibrant, deep purple petals.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a rich fragrance. This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

Its leaves are dark green and serrated, providing a striking contrast to the showy blooms.
