Ranunculus chasii

Ranunculus chasii, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy and upright.

Ranunculus chasii is often found in high-altitude habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
