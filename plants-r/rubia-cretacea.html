Rubia cretacea

Rubia cretacea, commonly known as Crete madder, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 60 cm tall, characterized by its slender stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its roots contain a red dye, which has been historically used for natural coloring.

Rubia cretacea is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
