Rosa reboudiana

Rosa reboudiana, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rosa reboudiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
