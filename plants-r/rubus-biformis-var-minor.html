Rubus biformis var. minor

Rubus biformis var. minor, commonly known as the small-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically less showy than other blackberry varieties. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in partial shade.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and exhibits typical bramble growth habits.
