Rubus longisepalus f. tozawae

Rubus longisepalus f. tozawae, commonly known as the Tozawa raspberry, is a variant of the long-sepaled raspberry.

It is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It is native to Japan and is often found in forested areas with moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
