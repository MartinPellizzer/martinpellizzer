Ranunculus auricomus subsp. latifrons

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

latifrons, commonly known as the broad-fronded buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. It features showy, yellow flowers with five broad, petal-like sepals that are often tinged with red or orange.

The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in woodland or streamside habitats. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a distinctive, waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
