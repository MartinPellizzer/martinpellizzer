Rapistrum hypogaeum

Rapistrum hypogaeum, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by fluffy, seed-filled capsules.

The leaves are usually alternate, rough to the touch, and have a slightly lobed appearance.
