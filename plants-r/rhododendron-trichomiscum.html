Rhododendron trichomiscum

Rhododendron trichomiscum, commonly known as the white rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in trusses, and are usually white or pale pink, blooming in spring.

Rhododendron trichomiscum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its mountainous habitat.
