Reynoutria scandens

Reynoutria scandens, commonly known as giant knotweed, is a vigorous perennial plant native to Asia but now invasive in many parts of the world.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, bamboo-like stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, triangular berries. It thrives in moist environments and can grow rapidly, often outcompeting native vegetation.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a significant ecological threat in many regions.
