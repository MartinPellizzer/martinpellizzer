Rhynchosia drummondii

Rhynchosia drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds, which are often used as a protein-rich food source.

Rhynchosia drummondii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural systems.
