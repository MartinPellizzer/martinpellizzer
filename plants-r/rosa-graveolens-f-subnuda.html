Rosa graveolens f. subnuda

Rosa graveolens f. subnuda, commonly known as the naked-stemmed dog rose, is a flowering plant characterized by its thornless stems and fragrant pink flowers.

It is a subspecies of Rosa graveolens, native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
