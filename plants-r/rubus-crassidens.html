Rubus crassidens

Rubus crassidens, commonly known as the thick-leaved blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its leaves are large, broad, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
