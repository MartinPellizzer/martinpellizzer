Rubus franconidicus

Rubus franconidicus, commonly known as Franconian blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and moist soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, a dark purple to black berry, is edible and often used in jams and wines.
