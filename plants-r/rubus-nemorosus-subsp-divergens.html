Rubus nemorosus subsp. divergens

Rubus nemorosus subsp.

divergens, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and hedgerows.

Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by the development of its edible berries.
