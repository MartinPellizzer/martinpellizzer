Raphanus sativus var. vulgaris

Raphanus sativus var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common radish, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a taproot that is typically white or pink in color and is the primary edible part of the plant. The plant has simple, lobed leaves that are usually green and slightly hairy. It produces small, yellow flowers that develop into round, tan-colored pods containing several seeds.

This species is often grown for its crisp, spicy root and is a member of the Brassicaceae family.
