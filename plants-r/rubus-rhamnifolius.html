Rubus rhamnifolius

Rubus rhamnifolius, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that ripen to a deep black color, typically in late summer. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

Rubus rhamnifolius is an important plant for both wildlife and human consumption.
