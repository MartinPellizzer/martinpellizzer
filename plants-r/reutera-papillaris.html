Reutera papillaris

Reutera papillaris, commonly known as the papillate reutera, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive papillate (bump-like) structures on its leaves, which give it a textured appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually elongated and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Reutera papillaris is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
