Ranunculus bariscianus

Ranunculus bariscianus, commonly known as the Bariscian crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces small, clustered flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its wetland or semi-aquatic habitat.
