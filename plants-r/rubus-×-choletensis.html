Rubus × choletensis

Rubus × choletensis, commonly known as the Cholet raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in areas of Georgia and South Carolina. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with pinnately compound leaves and white to pink flowers. Its fruits, which are small and aggregate, are often used in jams and preserves due to their tart flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
