Rhodia

Rhodia, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces a distinctive, fragrant scent. Rhodia thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a houseplant.
