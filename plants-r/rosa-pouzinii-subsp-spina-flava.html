Rosa pouzinii subsp. spina-flava

Rosa pouzinii subsp.

spina-flava, commonly known as the yellow spine rose, is a shrub-like species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it suitable for cultivation in arid climates.

The subspecies is distinguished by its vibrant yellow floral coloration and distinctive spiny growth form.
