Ranunculus bulbosus var. pseudoaleae

Ranunculus bulbosus var. pseudoaleae, commonly known as the bulbous buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, cup-shaped flowers and hairy, basal leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy and sometimes toxic species.

Its blooming period occurs in spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to its natural habitat.
