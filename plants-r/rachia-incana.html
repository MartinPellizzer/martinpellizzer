Rachia incana

Rachia incana, commonly known as the silverleaf rachis, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is native to southern Africa. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typical of the carrot family.

Rachia incana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
