Racosperma adsurgens

Racosperma adsurgens, commonly known as the climbing legume, is a species native to tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend trees and shrubs. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

Racosperma adsurgens plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
