Rotala longistyla

Rotala longistyla, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterwheel plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to aquatic habitats.

Rotala longistyla is valued in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide shelter for aquatic life.
