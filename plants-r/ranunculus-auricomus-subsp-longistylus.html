Ranunculus auricomus subsp. longistylus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

longistylus, commonly known as the long-styled golden buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with numerous petals and a distinctive long, slender style.

The plant typically grows in moist, temperate habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has palmately divided leaves and produces achenes as its fruit.

This subspecies is often found in the mountainous regions of Europe and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
