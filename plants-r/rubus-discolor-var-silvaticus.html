Rubus discolor var. silvaticus

Rubus discolor var. silvaticus, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they may vary in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
