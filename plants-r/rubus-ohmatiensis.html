Rubus ohmatiensis

Rubus ohmatiensis, commonly known as the Ohmatien raspberry, is a species of brambles native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
