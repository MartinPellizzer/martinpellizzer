Rhus hookeri

Rhus hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's sumac, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 11 to 21 leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that attract birds and other wildlife. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Rhus hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
