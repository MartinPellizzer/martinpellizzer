Rosa pendulina f. pseudopyrenaica

Rosa pendulina f. pseudopyrenaica, commonly known as the hanging rose or pendulous rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa pendulina.

It is characterized by its long, drooping stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. This cultivar is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves, and it produces rosehips that are often used in herbal remedies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
