Rubus schleicheri microgene chloroxylon

Rubus schleicheri microgene chloroxylon, commonly known as the Schleicher's raspberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
