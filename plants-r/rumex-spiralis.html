Rumex spiralis

Rumex spiralis, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a wavy margin and a prominent central vein. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in dense, terminal clusters, and it produces elliptical, reddish-brown seeds.

Rumex spiralis is often used in traditional herbal medicine and is also a food source for various insects and wildlife.
