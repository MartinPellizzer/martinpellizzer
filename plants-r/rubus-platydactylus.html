Rubus platydactylus

Rubus platydactylus, commonly known as the broad-fingered blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with broad, finger-like lobes. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are edible and often used in culinary applications.

Rubus platydactylus thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
