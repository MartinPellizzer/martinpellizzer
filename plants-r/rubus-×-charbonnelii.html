Rubus × charbonnelii

Rubus × charbonnelii, commonly known as the blackberry-loganberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a natural hybrid between the blackberry (Rubus ursinus) and the loganberry (Rubus istrorum). The plant produces large, dark purple to black berries that are typically larger and sweeter than those of its parent species. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
