Ranunculus laetus var. latifolius

Ranunculus laetus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals. The plant features a basal rosette of lobed, broad leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinct, waxy appearance. Its flowers are usually solitary and bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blossoms.
