Rubus pseudoglotta

Rubus pseudoglotta, commonly known as the New Zealand bramble, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in forest margins and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
