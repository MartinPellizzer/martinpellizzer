Rubus pallidus var. leptopetalus

Rubus pallidus var. leptopetalus, commonly known as the pale bramble or slender-petaled raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, aggregate fruits that develop from multiple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five to seven petals.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
