Rhododendron × liewianum

Rhododendron × liewianum, commonly known as the Liewi hybrid rhododendron, is a cross between Rhododendron liewii and Rhododendron yakushimanum.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces large, bell-shaped blooms in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a strong, sweet fragrance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural collections.
