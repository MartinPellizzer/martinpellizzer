Rouhamon bredemeyeri

Rouhamon bredemeyeri, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is deep and extensive, enabling it to access groundwater.

Rouhamon bredemeyeri is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
