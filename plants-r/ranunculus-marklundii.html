Ranunculus marklundii

Ranunculus marklundii, commonly known as the Marklund's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or orange flowers. The plant features finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of soft, white hairs. Its flowers are usually solitary, with five to eight petal-like sepals and numerous stamens.

Ranunculus marklundii thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
