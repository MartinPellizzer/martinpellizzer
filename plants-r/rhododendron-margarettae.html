Rhododendron margarettae

Rhododendron margarettae, commonly known as Margaretta's rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from pink to purple, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
