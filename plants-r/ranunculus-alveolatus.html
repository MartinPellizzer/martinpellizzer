Ranunculus alveolatus

Ranunculus alveolatus, commonly known as the alveolate buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, finely divided leaf structure. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

The species is known for its showy blooms and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
