Racosperma Drewianum Subsp. Minus

Racosperma drewianum subsp. minus is a rare and lesser-known plant species native to the arid regions of Australia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate foliage and distinctive seed pods. Despite its limited distribution, it plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native wildlife. The subspecies is often overlooked due to its inconspicuous appearance, yet it holds significant botanical value. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this unique plant from threats posed by habitat loss and climate change.
