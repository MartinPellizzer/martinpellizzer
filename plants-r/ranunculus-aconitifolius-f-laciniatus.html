Ranunculus aconitifolius f. laciniatus

Ranunculus aconitifolius f. laciniatus, commonly known as the cut-leaved aconite, is a variant of the aconite species with distinctive, finely lobed leaves.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is native to Europe and thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is deeply divided, giving it a fern-like appearance, while its flowers are typically single and have a prominent central stigma.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and its historical use in traditional medicine.
