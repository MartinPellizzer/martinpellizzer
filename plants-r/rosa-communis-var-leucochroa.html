Rosa communis var. leucochroa

Rosa communis var. leucochroa, commonly known as the white-flowered rose, is a cultivar of the common rose that is prized for its elegant, pale pink to white blooms.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The flowers are usually single or semi-double, with a delicate fragrance and a bloom period in late spring to early summer. This variety is well-suited for gardens and can be propagated through cuttings or grafting.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
