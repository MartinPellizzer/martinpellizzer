Runyonia longiflora

Runyonia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered runyonia, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has strap-like leaves that are broad and pointed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Runyonia longiflora is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a specimen plant.
