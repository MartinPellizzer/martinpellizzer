Rubus × semicuneatiformis

Rubus × semicuneatiformis, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (the cloudberry). The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red to black in color. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
