Rosa gallica f. officinalis

Rosa gallica f. officinalis, commonly known as the officinal rose, is a cultivar of the Gallica rose, characterized by its deep red petals and strong fragrance.

It is native to Europe and has been cultivated for centuries for its ornamental and medicinal properties. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in shades of red, pink, or white. Its leaves are dark green, serrated, and aromatic, while its stems are thorny and covered in a layer of fine hairs.

This rose is valued in both horticulture and traditional medicine for its essential oils and floral extracts.
