Rhododendron moupinense

Rhododendron moupinense, commonly known as the Moulmein rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments at high altitudes. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

Rhododendron moupinense is also noted for its ecological importance in its native habitat.
