Ruta graveolens var. corsica

Ruta graveolens var. corsica, commonly known as Corsican rue, is a variant of the rue plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its strongly aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported antispasmodic and digestive properties.

The plant's essential oils contain compounds like rutin and berberine, contributing to its medicinal reputation.
