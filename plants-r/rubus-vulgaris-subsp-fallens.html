Rubus vulgaris subsp. fallens

Rubus vulgaris subsp.

fallens, commonly known as the bramble blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The species is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
