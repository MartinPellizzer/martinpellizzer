Rubus semicandicans

Rubus semicandicans, commonly known as the red raspberry.

It is a perennial shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red when ripe, though some varieties may have yellow or orange fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the plant is known for its vigorous growth habit.
