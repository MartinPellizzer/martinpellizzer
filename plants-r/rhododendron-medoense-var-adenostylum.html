Rhododendron medoense var. adenostylum

Rhododendron medoense var. adenostylum, commonly known as the Yunnan azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
