Rubus rivularis subsp. incultus

Rubus rivularis subsp.

incultus, commonly known as the river raspberry, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows along stream banks and moist soils, forming dense thickets.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are reddish-purple when mature. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks.
