Rhodophiala rhodolirion

Rhodophiala rhodolirion, commonly known as the red-lily orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and a unique, spurred lip that distinguishes it from other orchid species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the cool, humid climate. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.
