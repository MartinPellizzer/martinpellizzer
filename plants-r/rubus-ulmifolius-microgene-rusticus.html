Rubus ulmifolius microgene rusticus

Rubus ulmifolius microgene rusticus, commonly known as the rough-leaved blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color, often with a slightly tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other members of the Rubus genus.
