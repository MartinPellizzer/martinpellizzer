Rinopodium bifolia

Rinopodium bifolia, commonly known as the two-leaved horned plant, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive horn-like structures on its stems, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in pairs, giving it the species name "bifolia.".

Rinopodium bifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in botanical gardens.
