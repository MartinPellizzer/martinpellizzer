Rosa willibaldii

Rosa willibaldii, commonly known as the Willibald's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Guatemala and Mexico. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it produces oval, red-orange hips that contain seeds.

Rosa willibaldii is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
