Rosa pendulina var. subuniserrata

Rosa pendulina var. subuniserrata, commonly known as the pendulous rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its drooping, fragrant flowers and thorny stems.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Central Asia, particularly in the mountainous areas of Kazakhstan and southern Siberia. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to red in color, with five to seven petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
