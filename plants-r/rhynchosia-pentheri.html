Rhynchosia pentheri

Rhynchosia pentheri, commonly known as the purple-stemmed cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is adapted to grow in poor soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Rhynchosia pentheri is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
