Rosa pouzinii var. typica

Rosa pouzinii var. typica, commonly known as the Pouzin's rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces red, hip-like fruits that contain seeds. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
