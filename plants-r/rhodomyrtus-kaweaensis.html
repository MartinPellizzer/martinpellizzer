Rhodomyrtus kaweaensis

Rhodomyrtus kaweaensis, commonly known as the Kawaean red myrtle, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, round, and reddish when mature, containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
