Rosa enneaphylla

Rosa enneaphylla, commonly known as the nine-leaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically arranged in groups of nine, giving it its scientific name.

Rosa enneaphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
