Rosa × nitidula var. belgradensis

Rosa × nitidula var. belgradensis, commonly known as the Belgrade rose, is a hybrid species native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has semi-glossy, dark green leaves with a slightly serrated margin. It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to a range of garden conditions.
