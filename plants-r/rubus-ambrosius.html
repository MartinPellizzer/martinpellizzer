Rubus ambrosius

Rubus ambrosius, commonly known as the European raspberry or bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible red berries. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often growing in woodland areas and along hedgerows. Its leaves are typically serrated and have a hairy texture, while its flowers are white or pale pink.

The plant is valued both for its culinary uses and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
