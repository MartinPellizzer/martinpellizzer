Rosa arkansanoides

Rosa arkansanoides, commonly known as the Arkansas rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Arkansas and neighboring regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in riparian zones.

Rosa arkansanoides is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in local ecosystems.
