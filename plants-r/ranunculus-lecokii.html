Ranunculus lecokii

Ranunculus lecokii, commonly known as the Lecok's buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features finely divided leaves and produces flowers with five to eight sepals and petals that are often yellow or white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
