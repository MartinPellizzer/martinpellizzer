Rosa × engelmannii nothosubsp. occidentalis

Rosa × engelmannii nothosubsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western wild rose, is a hybrid plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in open woodlands and along streambanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval leaflets, and it produces red-orange hips in the fall.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
