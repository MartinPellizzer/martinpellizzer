Rubus sprengelii var. cryptothyrsus

Rubus sprengelii var. cryptothyrsus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible but often have a tart flavor, making them suitable for jams and preserves.
