Rubus serpens var. geromensis

Rubus serpens var. geromensis, commonly known as the Geromense blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.

Its growth habit and fruit characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
