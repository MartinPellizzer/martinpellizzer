Rubus tomentosus f. ribesifolius

Rubus tomentosus f. ribesifolius, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a variant of the common raspberry plant.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and broad, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically bright red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
