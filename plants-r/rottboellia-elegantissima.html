Rottboellia elegantissima

Rottboellia elegantissima, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hair. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a weed in many parts of the world.
