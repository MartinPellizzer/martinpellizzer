Rubus enslenii var. pluralis

Rubus enslenii var. pluralis, commonly known as the Sierra currant, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, red berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its berries are edible and often used in local cuisines.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
