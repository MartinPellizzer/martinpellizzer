Rosa pomifera f. jurana

Rosa pomifera f. jurana, commonly known as the Jurana rose, is a unique horticultural form of the rose species Rosa pomifera.

This plant is characterized by its large, edible rose hips, which are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The flowers are usually pink or red, and they bloom in the summer months.

While not widely cultivated, it is appreciated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional herbal medicine.
