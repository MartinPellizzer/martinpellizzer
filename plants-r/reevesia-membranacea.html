Reevesia membranacea

Reevesia membranacea, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are usually dark green with a glossy texture, and it produces spadices, which are the characteristic inflorescences of the Araceae family.

Reevesia membranacea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
