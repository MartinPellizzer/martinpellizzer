Rhamnus calycifolia

Rhamnus calycifolia, commonly known as the California buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, dark green leaves that are typically alternate and elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
