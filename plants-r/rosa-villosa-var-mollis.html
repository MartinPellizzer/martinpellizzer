Rosa villosa var. mollis

Rosa villosa var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-haired rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
