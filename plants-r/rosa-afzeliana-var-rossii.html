Rosa afzeliana var. rossii

Rosa afzeliana var. rossii, commonly known as the Ross's rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and autumn foliage color.
