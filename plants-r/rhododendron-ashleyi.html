Rhododendron ashleyi

Rhododendron ashleyi, commonly known as Ashley's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats within forests, preferring acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant pink or red flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native ecosystems.
