Rhododendron agapetum

Rhododendron agapetum, commonly known as the white rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at high elevations. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blossoms.

Rhododendron agapetum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
