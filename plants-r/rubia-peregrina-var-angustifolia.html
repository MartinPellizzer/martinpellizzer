Rubia peregrina var. angustifolia

Rubia peregrina var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved woodruff, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stem. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, and they are clustered in loose, terminal spikes.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
