Rapicactus subterraneus

Rapicactus subterraneus, commonly known as the underground cactus, is a unique species native to Mexico.

It is a type of cactus that grows subterraneously, with its stem and roots developing underground, while its flowers emerge above ground. The plant has a cylindrical stem that is often covered in spines, and it produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its unusual growth habit, it is a popular choice for collectors and botanists interested in rare cacti.
