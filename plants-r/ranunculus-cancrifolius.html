Ranunculus cancrifolius

Ranunculus cancrifolius, commonly known as the cancerwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in damp meadows and woodland edges. It blooms in early spring and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

The species name "cancrifolius" refers to its historical use in treating cancerous conditions.
