Rhododendron tanakae

Rhododendron tanakae, commonly known as the Tanaka's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron tanakae is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
