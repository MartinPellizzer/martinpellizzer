Rumex rechingerianus

Rumex rechingerianus, commonly known as the Rechinger's dock, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils and can be found in grasslands, riverbanks, and wet meadows. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough, serrated edge. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in dense, terminal clusters, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
