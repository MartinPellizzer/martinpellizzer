Rubus inflexus

Rubus inflexus, commonly known as the bent-leaved blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are often curved or bent. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in temperate climates.

Rubus inflexus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
