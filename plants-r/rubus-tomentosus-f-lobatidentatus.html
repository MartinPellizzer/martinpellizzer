Rubus tomentosus f. lobatidentatus

Rubus tomentosus f. lobatidentatus, commonly known as the broad-lobed bramble, is a variant of the brambles characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features large, lobed leaves with a dense, woolly underside, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of white flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by red, edible berries. It is typically found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Its morphology reflects adaptations to its native environment, supporting local wildlife and contributing to ecological diversity.
