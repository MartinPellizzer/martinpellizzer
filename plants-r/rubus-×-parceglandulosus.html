Rubus × parceglandulosus

Rubus × parceglandulosus, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus idaeus (the red raspberry). This species exhibits a combination of traits from both parent plants, including thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The botanical features include stipulate leaves with serrated margins and flowers that are typically white or pale pink.

It is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its edible fruits.
