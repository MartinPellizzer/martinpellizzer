Rubus peraffinis

Rubus peraffinis, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of bramble in the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are white or pale green.

The fruit, which is usually small and dark purple, is edible but often has a tart flavor.
