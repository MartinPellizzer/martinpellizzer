Rosa rubiginosa denudata

Rosa rubiginosa denudata, commonly known as the naked rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
