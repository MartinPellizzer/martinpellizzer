Rosa alpina var. simplicidens

Rosa alpina var. simplicidens, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its simple, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has narrow, deciduous leaves and produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
