Rhodiola rosea var. integrifolia

Rhodiola rosea var. integrifolia, commonly known as the golden root, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of the Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its fleshy, yellowish roots and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to low temperatures and high altitudes.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
