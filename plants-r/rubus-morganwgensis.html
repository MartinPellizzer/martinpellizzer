Rubus morganwgensis

Rubus morganwgensis, commonly known as the Morganwg blackberry, is a rare species native to the Welsh region of Morganwg.

It is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
