Ruellia gruicollis

Ruellia gruicollis, commonly known as the roughleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The leaves are usually rough to the touch, giving the plant its common name, and they are often lance-shaped with prominent veins. This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
