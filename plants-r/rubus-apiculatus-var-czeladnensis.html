Rubus apiculatus var. czeladnensis

Rubus apiculatus var. czeladnensis, commonly known as the Czeladnenskaya blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and the flavor of its fruit, though it is less commonly cultivated than other blackberry species.
