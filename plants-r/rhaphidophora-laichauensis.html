Rhaphidophora laichauensis

Rhaphidophora laichauensis, commonly known as the Laichau arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Rhaphidophora laichauensis is valued for its starchy roots, which are used in local cuisines.
