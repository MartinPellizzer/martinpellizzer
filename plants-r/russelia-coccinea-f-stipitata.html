Russelia coccinea f. stipitata

Russelia coccinea f. stipitata, commonly known as the red-flowered russelia or stipitate russelia, is a compact shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

Its unique floral structure and striking color make it a notable ornamental plant in botanical collections.
