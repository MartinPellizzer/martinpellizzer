Rufodorsia cerricola

Rufodorsia cerricola, commonly known as the red-fruited starling tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and dark green, leathery leaves that are often pubescent beneath. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by distinctive, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and attract various wildlife. Rufodorsia cerricola thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
