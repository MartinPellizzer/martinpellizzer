Reseda undata var. baetica

Reseda undata var. baetica, commonly known as the Spanish catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
