Rochonia senecionoides

Rochonia senecionoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in late summer. Its roots are characterized by their yellow color and are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Rochonia senecionoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
