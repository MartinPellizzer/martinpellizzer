Rosa agrestis var. virgultorum

Rosa agrestis var. virgultorum, commonly known as the wild rose or field rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open areas, such as meadows and woodland edges, and features thorny stems with opposite, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, hip-like fruits. Its botanical characteristics include simple, alternate leaves and a woody structure that supports its perennial growth habit.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
