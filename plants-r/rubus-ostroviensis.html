Rubus ostroviensis

Rubus ostroviensis, commonly known as the Ostrovo blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and locally significant species in its native habitat.
