Rubus humiliformis

Rubus humiliformis, commonly known as the low-growing raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
