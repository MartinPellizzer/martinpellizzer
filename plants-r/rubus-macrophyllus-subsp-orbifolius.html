Rubus macrophyllus subsp. orbifolius

Rubus macrophyllus subsp.

orbifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaf bramble, is a shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that can reach up to 20 cm in diameter.

The plant produces white to pinkish flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, hairy berries. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.

The subspecies is notable for its distinctive foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
