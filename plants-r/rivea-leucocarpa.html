Rivea leucocarpa

Rivea leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fleshed morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive white, fleshy tendrils and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, round, greenish fruits that turn yellow as they mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Rivea leucocarpa is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
