Rubus subaculeatus var. longifrons

Rubus subaculeatus var. longifrons, commonly known as the long-fruited blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its edible berries and is often cultivated in gardens and wildlands.
