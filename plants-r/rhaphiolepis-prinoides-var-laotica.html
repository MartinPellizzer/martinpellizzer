Rhaphiolepis prinoides var. laotica

Rhaphiolepis prinoides var. laotica, commonly known as the Laotian firethorn, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of bright red berries that persist through winter. The plant features small, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface and a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant fruit and thorny structure, which provide visual interest throughout the year.
