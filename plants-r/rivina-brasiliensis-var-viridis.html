Rivina brasiliensis var. viridis

Rivina brasiliensis var. viridis, commonly known as the green rivina, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
