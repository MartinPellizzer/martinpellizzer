Rubus hirtus var. permollis

Rubus hirtus var. permollis, commonly known as the soft-hairy raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they can also be yellow or orange in some cases. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them suitable for culinary use.
