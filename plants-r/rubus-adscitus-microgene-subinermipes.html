Rubus adscitus microgene subinermipes

Rubus adscitus microgene subinermipes, commonly known as the small-fruited blackberry, is a species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thornless stems.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. The plant is native to the western United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it flowers with white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and is often found in woodland and riparian habitats.
