Ruellia ciliosa var. cinerascens

Ruellia ciliosa var. cinerascens, commonly known as the silverleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that have a silvery-gray underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

Ruellia ciliosa var. cinerascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
