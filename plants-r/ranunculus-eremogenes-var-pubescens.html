Ranunculus eremogenes var. pubescens

Ranunculus eremogenes var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy desert buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
