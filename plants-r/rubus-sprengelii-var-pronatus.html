Rubus sprengelii var. pronatus

Rubus sprengelii var. pronatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its stout, thorny stems and large, lobed leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black achenes surrounded by a fleshy receptacle. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in local ecosystems.
