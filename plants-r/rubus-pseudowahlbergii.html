Rubus pseudowahlbergii

Rubus pseudowahlbergii, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, thorny stems, and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and appear in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in suitable climates.
