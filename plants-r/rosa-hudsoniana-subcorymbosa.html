Rosa hudsoniana subcorymbosa

Rosa hudsoniana subcorymbosa, commonly known as the Hudson's Bay rose, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink to red, appearing in clusters. The plant produces hips, which are orange-red, fuzzy fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
