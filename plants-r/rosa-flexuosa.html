Rosa flexuosa

Rosa flexuosa, commonly known as the climbing rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems that often climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa flexuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
