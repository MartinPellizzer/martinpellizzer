Rubus infestus f. rectispinus

Rubus infestus f. rectispinus, commonly known as the erect blackberry, is a wild shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
