Rubus caesius

Rubus caesius, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, red, edible fruits. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

The fruit, which ripens in late summer, is widely cultivated for its tart flavor and nutritional value.
