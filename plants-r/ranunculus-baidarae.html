Ranunculus baidarae

Ranunculus baidarae, commonly known as the Baidara buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features palmate leaves and produces small, yellow or white flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
