Rubus fruticosus var. pomponius

Rubus fruticosus var. pomponius, commonly known as the redcurrant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically bright red when mature, containing multiple tiny drupes. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in hedgerows or woodland edges. Its foliage is trifoliate, with serrated leaflets, and it is known for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.

The species is closely related to other raspberries and blackberries in the Rubus genus.
