Rubus × olavi

Rubus × olavi, commonly known as the Olavi blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, known for its vigorous growth and thorny stems.

It produces large, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is valued for its rich flavor and is often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
