Randia aculeata var. dasyphylla

Randia aculeata var. dasyphylla, commonly known as the spiked randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, which are followed by bright orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
