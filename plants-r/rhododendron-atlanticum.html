Rhododendron atlanticum

Rhododendron atlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic rhododendron, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of North America.

It features broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to white flowers in the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron atlanticum is often found in moist, wooded areas along the Atlantic coast.
