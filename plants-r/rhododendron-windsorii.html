Rhododendron windsorii

Rhododendron windsorii, commonly known as the Windsor rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, specifically found in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy surface. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, appearing in clusters and blooming in spring.

Rhododendron windsorii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
