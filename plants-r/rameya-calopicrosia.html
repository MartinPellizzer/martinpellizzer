Rameya calopicrosia

Rameya calopicrosia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils. Rameya calopicrosia is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it can become invasive in certain environments.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
