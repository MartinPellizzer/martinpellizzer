Rhynchospora rufa var. liebmanniana

Rhynchospora rufa var. liebmanniana, commonly known as the red swamp sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically reddish in color, with a dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, three-parted flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in saturated soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
