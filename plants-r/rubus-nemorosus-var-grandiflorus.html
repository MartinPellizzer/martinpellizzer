Rubus nemorosus var. grandiflorus

Rubus nemorosus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces showy white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, hairy berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in riparian habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
