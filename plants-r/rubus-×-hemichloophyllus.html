Rubus × hemichloophyllus

Rubus × hemichloophyllus, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry, is a deciduous shrub that exhibits characteristics of both blackberries and raspberries.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red to black in color, with a tart flavor. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This hybrid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
