Romulea bulbocodium subsp. grandiflora

Romulea bulbocodium subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the giant romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa. It features a robust, fleshy bulb that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

The plant produces upright, grass-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. It blooms during the summer months, typically from November to February.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
