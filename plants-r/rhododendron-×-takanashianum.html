Rhododendron × takanashianum

Rhododendron × takanashianum, commonly known as the Takanasha rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron indicum and Rhododendron caucasicum.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Japan, particularly in the Takanashiyama area of Shikoku. This plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub with dense foliage and showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
