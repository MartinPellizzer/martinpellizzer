Rhamnus saxatilis subsp. infectoria

Rhamnus saxatilis subsp.

infectoria, commonly known as the rock buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, dark berries that are a food source for various birds.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific habitat preferences and morphological characteristics compared to other members of the Rhamnus genus.
