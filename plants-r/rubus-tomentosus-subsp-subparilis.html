Rubus tomentosus subsp. subparilis

Rubus tomentosus subsp.

subparilis, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its dense, white-tomentose (woolly) foliage and thorny stems.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family.
