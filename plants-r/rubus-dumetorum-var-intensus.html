Rubus dumetorum var. intensus

Rubus dumetorum var. intensus, commonly known as the dense blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its densely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically tart and less sweet than other blackberry varieties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and thickets.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and is valued for its hardy nature and ornamental qualities.
