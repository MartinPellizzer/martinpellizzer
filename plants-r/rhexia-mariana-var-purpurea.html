Rhexia mariana var. purpurea

Rhexia mariana var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to wetlands and moist woodlands in eastern North America.

It produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use by indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.
