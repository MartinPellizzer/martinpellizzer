Rubus hebecaulis var. acutifolius

Rubus hebecaulis var. acutifolius, commonly known as the sharp-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching canes and trifoliate leaves with sharply pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along streambanks.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential use in local ecosystems.
