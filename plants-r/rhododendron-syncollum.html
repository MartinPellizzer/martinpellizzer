Rhododendron syncollum

Rhododendron syncollum, commonly known as the mountain rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in early spring.

Rhododendron syncollum is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
