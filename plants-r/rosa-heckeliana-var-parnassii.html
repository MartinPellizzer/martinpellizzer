Rosa heckeliana var. parnassii

Rosa heckeliana var. parnassii, commonly known as the Parnassian rose, is a rare and beautiful species native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive red or crimson hue at the base. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and historical significance.
