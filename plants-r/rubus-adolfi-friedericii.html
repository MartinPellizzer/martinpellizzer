Rubus adolfi-friedericii

Rubus adolfi-friedericii, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color when ripe. These fruits are edible and have a sweet-tart flavor, making them popular in culinary uses.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.
