Ranunculus turczaninovii

Ranunculus turczaninovii, commonly known as the Turczaninov's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often delicate floral structures.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
