Rubus glandulosus var. ochrosetus

Rubus glandulosus var. ochrosetus, commonly known as the yellow-leafed blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
