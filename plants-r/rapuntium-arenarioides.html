Rapuntium arenarioides

Rapuntium arenarioides, commonly known as the sand dune cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system helps stabilize loose soils, making it valuable for dune stabilization.

Rapuntium arenarioides plays a role in supporting local insect populations and is often found in coastal or desert environments.
