Rhus integrifolia var. cedrosensis

Rhus integrifolia var. cedrosensis, commonly known as Cedros Island buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Channel Islands of California.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are smooth on both sides. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native range.

This variety is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.
