Rauvolfia densiflora var. macrophylla

Rauvolfia densiflora var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed snakeroot, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces red berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
