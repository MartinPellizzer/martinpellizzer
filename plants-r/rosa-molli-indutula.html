Rosa molli-indutula

Rosa molli-indutula, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its densely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
