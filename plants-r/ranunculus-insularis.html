Ranunculus insularis

Ranunculus insularis, commonly known as the island buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, rocky soils along coastal areas. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals that resemble additional petals. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Ranunculus insularis is known for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and its ornamental value in gardens.
