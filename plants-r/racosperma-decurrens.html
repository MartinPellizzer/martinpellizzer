Racosperma decurrens

Racosperma decurrens, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that allow it to thrive in arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for native wildlife.

Racosperma decurrens is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
