Rhynchospora stelligera

Rhynchospora stelligera, commonly known as the sea club-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in saline or brackish wetlands, where it thrives in sandy or muddy soils. The plant features cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown, and they are clustered in dense spikes.

Rhynchospora stelligera plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
