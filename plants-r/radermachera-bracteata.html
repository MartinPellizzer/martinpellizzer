Radermachera bracteata

Radermachera bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf sterculia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Sterculiaceae and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, velvety texture, which gives it its common name.

The flowers are clustered in panicles and bloom during the rainy season, attracting various pollinators.
