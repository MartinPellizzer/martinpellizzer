Rhus trichocarpa

Rhus trichocarpa, commonly known as smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and turn bright red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fuzzy berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Smooth sumac has been used historically for its medicinal properties and as a source of dye.
