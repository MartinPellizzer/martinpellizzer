Rosa rossica

Rosa rossica, commonly known as the Russian rose, is a perennial shrub native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It features woody stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late summer. Its flowers are followed by oval, red or orange hips that contain seeds.

Rosa rossica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.
