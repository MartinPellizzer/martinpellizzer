Rubus × grandidens

Rubus × grandidens, commonly known as the blackberry or raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically red or black in color, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Rubus × grandidens is widely cultivated for its edible fruits and is also used in ornamental gardening.
