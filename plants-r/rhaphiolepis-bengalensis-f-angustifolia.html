Rhaphiolepis bengalensis f. angustifolia

Rhaphiolepis bengalensis f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rosebay, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant a neat and refined appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
