Rosa suffulta f. alba

Rosa suffulta f. alba, commonly known as the white form of the alpine rose, is a flowering plant characterized by its pale pink to white petals.

It is a subspecies of Rosa suffulta, which is native to the mountainous regions of Europe. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually single or semi-double, with a delicate appearance that adds to its ornamental value.

This plant is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
