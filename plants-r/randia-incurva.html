Randia incurva

Randia incurva, commonly known as the curly randia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by spherical, red berries that are edible when ripe.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in tropical horticulture and ecological studies.
