Rubus lejeunei var. brevis

Rubus lejeunei var. brevis, commonly known as the short-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its small, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

This variety is often found in the southeastern United States, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
