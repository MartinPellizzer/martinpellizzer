Rubus orthacanthus var. frisicus

Rubus orthacanthus var. frisicus, commonly known as the frisian blackberry, is a variant of the bramble plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
