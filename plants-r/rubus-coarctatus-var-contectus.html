Rubus coarctatus var. contectus

Rubus coarctatus var. contectus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It produces small, dark berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
