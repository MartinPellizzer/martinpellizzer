Rosa canina var. jacquiniana

Rosa canina var. jacquiniana, commonly known as the Jacquin's dog rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red, fuzzy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
