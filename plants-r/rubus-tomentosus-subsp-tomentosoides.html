Rubus tomentosus subsp. tomentosoides

Rubus tomentosus subsp.

tomentosoides, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its dense, white-tomentose (woolly) foliage and thorny stems.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
