Rosa reptans

Rosa reptans, commonly known as the trailing rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a vigorous, climbing shrub that produces fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Rosa reptans is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Its ability to spread along the ground or climb makes it suitable for ground cover or trellis planting.
