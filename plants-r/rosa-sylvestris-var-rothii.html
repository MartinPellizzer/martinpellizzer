Rosa sylvestris var. rothii

Rosa sylvestris var. rothii, commonly known as the Roth's wild rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a species of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
