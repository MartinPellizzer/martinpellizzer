Rapanea myrtillina

Rapanea myrtillina, commonly known as the blue mist tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, including countries such as Nigeria and Ghana. The plant is characterized by its dense, rounded canopy and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its leaves are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture.

The fruit is a small, blue-black drupe that ripens in the fall, providing a food source for various wildlife species.
