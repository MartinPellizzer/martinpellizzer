Rosa canina var. hispiduloides

Rosa canina var. hispiduloides, commonly known as the rough hippeastrum, is a variant of the dog rose that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a rugged appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically smaller than those of the typical Rosa canina. Its fruits are the familiar red, hairy hips that contain seeds.

This variety is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to challenging environments.
