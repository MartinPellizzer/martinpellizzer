Rubus stimulifer

Rubus stimulifer, commonly known as the stimulative raspberry.

This shrub is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red berries. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are pink to white, appearing in early summer.

The berries are edible, though some varieties may have a tart or slightly bitter taste.
