Ricinocarpus deciduus

Ricinocarpus deciduus, commonly known as the mangrove apple, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that thrives in brackish water environments, often found in mangrove forests. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains several seeds.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate saltwater and plays an important ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
