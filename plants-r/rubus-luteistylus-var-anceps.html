Rubus luteistylus var. anceps

Rubus luteistylus var. anceps, commonly known as the yellow-styled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus Rubus.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black fruits. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine for their tart flavor and nutritional value.
