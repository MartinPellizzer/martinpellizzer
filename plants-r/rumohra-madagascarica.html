Rumohra madagascarica

Rumohra madagascarica, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are loosely arranged.

Rumohra madagascarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
