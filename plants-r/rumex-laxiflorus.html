Rumex laxiflorus

Rumex laxiflorus, commonly known as the broad-leaved dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable, while its roots are sometimes harvested for similar purposes.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
