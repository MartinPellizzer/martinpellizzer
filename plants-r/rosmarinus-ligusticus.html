Rosmarinus ligusticus

Rosmarinus ligusticus, commonly known as Italian rosemary, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for coastal and rocky environments. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are widely used in aromatherapy and culinary applications.

Rosmarinus ligusticus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
