Rosa pineliensis var. dispersidens

Rosa pineliensis var. dispersidens, commonly known as the Brazilian rose or the pink velvet rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and profuse flowering, producing clusters of semi-double pink flowers with a velvety texture. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens in warmer climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and horticultural research.
