Rubus procerus var. macrostemon

Rubus procerus var. macrostemon, commonly known as the large-stemmed blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white and clustered in umbels, and the fruit is edible, though often tart.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
