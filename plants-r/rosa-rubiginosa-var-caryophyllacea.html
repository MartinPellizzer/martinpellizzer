Rosa rubiginosa var. caryophyllacea

Rosa rubiginosa var. caryophyllacea, commonly known as the red-stemmed rose or the crimson rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thorny stems and is characterized by its deep red or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has serrated, dark green leaves and produces rose hips that are typically orange-red in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine.
