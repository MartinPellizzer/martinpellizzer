Ruschia saxicola

Ruschia saxicola, commonly known as the rock cholla, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Ruschia saxicola is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
