Rubus correctispinosus

Rubus correctispinosus, commonly known as the erect blackberry.

It is a species of bramble that belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

The berries are edible, though they are often described as having a tart and slightly bitter flavor.
