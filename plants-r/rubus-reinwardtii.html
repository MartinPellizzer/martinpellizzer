Rubus reinwardtii

Rubus reinwardtii, commonly known as the Reinwardt's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, with the fruit being a small, dark purple to black berry. The berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

It plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
