Rubus silvaticus var. armoricus

Rubus silvaticus var. armoricus, commonly known as the Appalachian blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local wildlife.
