Rubus tereticaulis f. gracilis

Rubus tereticaulis f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family, characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupes, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its delicate structure and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
