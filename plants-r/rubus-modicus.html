Rubus modicus

Rubus modicus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of brambles in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupes. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. Rubus modicus produces edible berries that are widely used in culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
