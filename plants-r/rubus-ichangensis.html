Rubus ichangensis

Rubus ichangensis, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
