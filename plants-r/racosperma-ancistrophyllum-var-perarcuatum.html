Racosperma ancistrophyllum var. perarcuatum

Racosperma ancistrophyllum var. perarcuatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, compound leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by flat, linear pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions.
