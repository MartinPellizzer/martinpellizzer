Rhus thezera

Rhus thezera, commonly known as the staghorn sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bright green in summer and turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are a food source for birds and small mammals. Rhus thezera is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in moist to dry habitats, including woodlands and streambanks.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat and food source for various wildlife species.
