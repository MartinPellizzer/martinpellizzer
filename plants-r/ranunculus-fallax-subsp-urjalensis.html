Ranunculus fallax subsp. urjalensis

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

urjalensis, commonly known as the Urjalensis buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia. It typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils in tundra and alpine environments.

The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed, and its flowers are usually yellow with five petals and numerous stamens. It blooms in late spring to early summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is considered an indicator species of high-altitude and polar ecosystems.
