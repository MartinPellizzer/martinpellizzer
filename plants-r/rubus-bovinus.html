Rubus bovinus

Rubus bovinus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupelets, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Rubus bovinus is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
