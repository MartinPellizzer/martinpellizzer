Rubus colemannii var. weicheri

Rubus colemannii var. weicheri, commonly known as the Weicher's blackberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
