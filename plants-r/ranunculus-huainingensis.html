Ranunculus huainingensis

Ranunculus huainingensis, commonly known as the Huaining buttercup, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces yellow or white flowers in the spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
