Rosa afzeliana var. viadra

Rosa afzeliana var. viadra, commonly known as the Viadra rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in a fine layer of down.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
