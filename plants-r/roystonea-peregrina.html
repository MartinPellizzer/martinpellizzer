Roystonea peregrina

Roystonea peregrina, commonly known as the African date palm, is a tall, slender palm native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features a slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant produces large, fan-shaped leaves that are segmented and covered in fine, silvery hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in branched inflorescences, with the female flowers developing into edible, sweet fruits.

The plant is well adapted to arid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
