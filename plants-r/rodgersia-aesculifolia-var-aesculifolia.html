Rodgersia aesculifolia var. aesculifolia

Rodgersia aesculifolia var. aesculifolia, commonly known as the elephant ear Rodgersia, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, dark green leaves that resemble elephant ears, which are often adorned with a white or silver margin. The plant produces showy, pale pink to lavender-colored flowers in late summer, arranged in upright panicles. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and is a popular choice for shade gardens.
