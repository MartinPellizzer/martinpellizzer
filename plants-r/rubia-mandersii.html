Rubia mandersii

Rubia mandersii, commonly known as the Turkish madder, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, including Turkey and parts of the Mediterranean. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The roots of Rubia mandersii contain compounds used historically for dyeing textiles, similar to its more famous relative, Rubia tinctorum.
