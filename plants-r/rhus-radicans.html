Rhus radicans

Rhus radicans, commonly known as staghorn sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that turn bright red in the fall, and its distinctive, fuzzy, spiny fruit clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early summer, which are followed by the showy, orange-red berries. Rhus radicans is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy and widely distributed species.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
