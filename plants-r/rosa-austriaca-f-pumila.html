Rosa austriaca f. pumila

Rosa austriaca f. pumila, commonly known as the Austrian dwarf rose, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to Central Europe.

It features small, rounded leaves and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. This variety is characterized by its dense, matted growth habit, making it ideal for ground cover or rock gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in cooler climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, suitable for both garden and landscape use.
