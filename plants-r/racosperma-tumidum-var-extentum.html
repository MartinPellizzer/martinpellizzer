Racosperma tumidum var. extentum

Racosperma tumidum var. extentum, commonly known as the broad-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features compound leaves with broad, oval-shaped leaflets that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
