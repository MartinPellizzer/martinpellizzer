Rosa canina f. bicolor

Rosa canina f. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive bicolor flowers, which typically feature pinkish-red outer petals and white inner petals. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it produces hips that are edible and rich in vitamin C. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
