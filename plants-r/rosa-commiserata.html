Rosa commiserata

Rosa commiserata, commonly known as the weeping rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its cascading clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant has slender, arching stems that give it a graceful, weeping appearance. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to lanceolate leaflets.

Rosa commiserata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
