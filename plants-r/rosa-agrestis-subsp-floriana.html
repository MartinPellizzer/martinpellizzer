Rosa agrestis subsp. floriana

Rosa agrestis subsp.

floriana, commonly known as the meadow rose, is a subspecies native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers.

The plant typically has serrated, compound leaves and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
