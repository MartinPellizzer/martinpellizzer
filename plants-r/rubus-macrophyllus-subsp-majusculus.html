Rubus macrophyllus subsp. majusculus

Rubus macrophyllus subsp.

majusculus, commonly known as the giant everlet, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 15 cm in length.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring. Its fruit, a red, juicy berry, is edible and often used in jams and pies.

This subspecies is notable for its vigorous growth and adaptability to moist, shady environments.
