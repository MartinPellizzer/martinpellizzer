Rubus thyrsiflorus subsp. phyllostachys

Rubus thyrsiflorus subsp.

phyllostachys, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Asia. It features compound leaves with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting species for both ecological studies and horticultural use.
