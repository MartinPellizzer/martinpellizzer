Rhododendron spadiceum

Rhododendron spadiceum, commonly known as the alpine rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often adorned with a distinctive yellow or red throat.

Rhododendron spadiceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
