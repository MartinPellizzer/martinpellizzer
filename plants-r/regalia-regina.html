Regalia regina

Regalia regina, commonly known as the royal regalia plant, is a striking species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to rich purple. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the warmer months. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Botanically, it belongs to the Aroid family and is closely related to other exotic plants like the peace lily.
