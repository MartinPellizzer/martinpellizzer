Ranunculus seguieri subsp. seguieri

Ranunculus seguieri subsp.

seguieri, commonly known as the Sierra Valley buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, upright stem.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with deeply lobed segments, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
