Rosa silvulicola

Rosa silvulicola, commonly known as the forest rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are covered in soft, velvety hairs.

Rosa silvulicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and other wildlife.
