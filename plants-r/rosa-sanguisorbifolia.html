Rosa sanguisorbifolia

Rosa sanguisorbifolia, commonly known as the bloodroot rose, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, blood-red flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has a short, herbaceous stem and possesses compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its flowers are typically solitary and have a distinctive, almost fleshy appearance.

Rosa sanguisorbifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its rare and exotic look.
