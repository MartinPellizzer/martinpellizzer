Ribes hladnickianum

Ribes hladnickianum, commonly known as Hladnick's gooseberry, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often at high altitudes. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange berries that are edible but not as commonly cultivated as other gooseberry varieties. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes grown in gardens for its hardy nature and unique appearance.
