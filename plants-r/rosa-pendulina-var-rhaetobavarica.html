Rosa pendulina var. rhaetobavarica

Rosa pendulina var. rhaetobavarica, commonly known as the Rhaetian climbing rose, is a variant of the pendulous rose that exhibits unique botanical traits.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its long, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing semi-double to fully double petals. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with serrated edges.

Native to the Alpine regions of Europe, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
