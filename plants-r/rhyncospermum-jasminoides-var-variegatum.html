Rhyncospermum jasminoides var. variegatum

Rhyncospermum jasminoides var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated jasmine vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, jasmine-like scent. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy, dark green surface with variegated patterns of white or cream. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

It is a climbing vine that can be used in landscaping to cover trellises or walls.
