Rhynchospora pterocarpa

Rhynchospora pterocarpa, commonly known as the winged sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its erect, spike-like inflorescence. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-lobed flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Rhynchospora pterocarpa thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
