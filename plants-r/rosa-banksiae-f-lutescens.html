Rosa banksiae f. lutescens

Rosa banksiae f. lutescens, commonly known as the golden banksia rose, is a unique cultivar of the Banksia rose species.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which sets it apart from the typical green leaves of other rose varieties. The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a compact shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden borders or container planting.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
