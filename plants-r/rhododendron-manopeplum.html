Rhododendron manopeplum

Rhododendron manopeplum, commonly known as the pink-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep rose. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron manopeplum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
