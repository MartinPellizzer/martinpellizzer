Rottboellia glauca

Rottboellia glauca, commonly known as the blue grass or blue panicgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Rottboellia glauca is a hardy perennial that is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.
