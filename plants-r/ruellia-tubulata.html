Ruellia tubulata

Ruellia tubulata, commonly known as the purple false dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of tubular, violet-blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Ruellia tubulata is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and ability to attract pollinators.
