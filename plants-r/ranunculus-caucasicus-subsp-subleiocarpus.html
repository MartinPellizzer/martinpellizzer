Ranunculus caucasicus subsp. subleiocarpus

Ranunculus caucasicus subsp.

subleiocarpus, commonly known as the Caucasian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous stamens.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf and flower morphology compared to the main species.
