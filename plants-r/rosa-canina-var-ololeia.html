Rosa canina var. ololeia

Rosa canina var. ololeia, commonly known as the Turkish rose, is a variant of the dog rose that is native to Turkey and surrounding regions.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa genus. The plant produces small, red, hairy fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shrublands and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
