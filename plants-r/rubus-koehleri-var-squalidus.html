Rubus koehleri var. squalidus

Rubus koehleri var. squalidus, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
