Rosa glauca f. pusilla

Rosa glauca f. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf blue rose, is a compact, low-growing form of the blue rose.

It features small, delicate flowers with a striking sky-blue hue, often with a slight pinkish tinge. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves and produces fragrant blooms throughout the summer. It is a hardy shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
