Roigia

Roigia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Roigia has lance-shaped leaves and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
