Rosa hirticolpogena subsp. colligata

Rosa hirticolpogena subsp.

colligata, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed roses, is a subspecies native to certain regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five to seven petals. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various climates.
