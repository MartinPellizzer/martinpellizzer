Ranunculus rufosepalus

Ranunculus rufosepalus, commonly known as the red-sepal buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with red sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and delicate appearance.
