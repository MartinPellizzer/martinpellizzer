Rodrigueziella petropolitana

Rodrigueziella petropolitana, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of Chile.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in rocky soils at high altitudes.

Rodrigueziella petropolitana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
