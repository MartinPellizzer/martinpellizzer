Rhododendron pentaphyllum f. albiflorum

Rhododendron pentaphyllum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered form of the five-leaved rhododendron, is a hybrid shrub characterized by its compact growth and dense foliage.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, where it thrives in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are typically dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the pale blooms.

Due to its unique floral coloration, it is prized in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to specific garden conditions.
