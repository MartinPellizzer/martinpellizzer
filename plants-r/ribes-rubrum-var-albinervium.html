Ribes rubrum var. albinervium

Ribes rubrum var. albinervium, commonly known as the white-veined red currant, is a variant of the red currant plant characterized by its pale green leaves with distinct white veins.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States, particularly in California and Oregon. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy flower clusters.
