Rosa berberifolia

Rosa berberifolia, commonly known as the yellowleaf rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The leaves are narrow and have a distinctive yellowish-green color, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
