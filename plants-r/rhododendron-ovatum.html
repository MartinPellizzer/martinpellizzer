Rhododendron ovatum

Rhododendron ovatum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron ovatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
