Rosa altata

Rosa altata, commonly known as the tall rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers. The plant typically produces pink to red flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Rosa altata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
