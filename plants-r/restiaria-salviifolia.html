Restiaria salviifolia

Restiaria salviifolia, commonly known as the sweet fern.

It is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States, characterized by its feathery, fern-like foliage. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Restiaria salviifolia thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.
