Rosa dumalis f. blondeana

Rosa dumalis f. blondeana, commonly known as the White Blenheim rose, is a cultivated form of the Rosa dumalis species.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It is native to Europe and is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value.

Its flowers emit a subtle fragrance, making it a popular choice for hedging and ornamental planting.
