Rubus oreades

Rubus oreades, commonly known as the alpine bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, typically found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually serrated and hairy.

Rubus oreades produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.
