Rubus × formosus

Rubus × formosus, commonly known as the Formosa raspberry, is a hybrid species within the genus Rubus.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly Taiwan, and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in shrub form, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are pink to white, and the fruits, which are red or yellow, are edible and have a tart flavor.

It is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in culinary applications.
