Ranunculus fallax subsp. platycolpus

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

platycolpus, commonly known as the flatleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a distinctive waxy coating.

The flowers are typically yellow with dark centers, and they bloom in early spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
