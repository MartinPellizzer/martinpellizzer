Rubus glareosus

Rubus glareosus, commonly known as the shining bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rubiaceae.

It is native to parts of eastern Asia, including China and Japan, where it thrives in moist, temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its prickly stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that develop from its flowers. Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring.

Rubus glareosus is often found in woodlands and along streambanks, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
