Rytilix

Rytilix, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for urban landscaping.

The tree is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
