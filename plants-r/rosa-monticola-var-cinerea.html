Rosa monticola var. cinerea

Rosa monticola var. cinerea, commonly known as the gray mountain rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, slightly hairy leaves and clusters of pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces oval, red-orange fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
