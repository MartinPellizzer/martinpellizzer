Ranunculus sparsidens

Ranunculus sparsidens, commonly known as the sparseleaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers consist of numerous petal-like sepals.

Ranunculus sparsidens is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
