Rhynchosia imbricata

Rhynchosia imbricata, commonly known as the African flame bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces bright yellow flowers with distinctive winged petals. The plant is known for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and nutritional purposes.

Rhynchosia imbricata also has medicinal uses in traditional medicine practices across its native regions.
