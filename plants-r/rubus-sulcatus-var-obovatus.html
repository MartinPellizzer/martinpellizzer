Rubus sulcatus var. obovatus

Rubus sulcatus var. obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its obovate leaves, which are broad and rounded at the tips, giving it a distinct appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued both for its ornamental qualities and its fruit.
