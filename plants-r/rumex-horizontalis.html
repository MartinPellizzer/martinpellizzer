Rumex horizontalis

Rumex horizontalis, commonly known as the horizontal dock, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, often reddish stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a waxy layer. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on leafy stalks. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine and medicine.

Rumex horizontalis thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
