Rottboellia sandorii

Rottboellia sandorii, commonly known as the sandorii grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a slightly bluish-green color.

Rottboellia sandorii is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
