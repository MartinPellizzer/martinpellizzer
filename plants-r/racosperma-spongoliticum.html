Racosperma spongoliticum

Racosperma spongoliticum, commonly known as the sponge-like acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spongy, and often pale green or yellowish leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The species is part of the legume family and has adapted to withstand drought conditions.
