Rubus baenitzii

Rubus baenitzii, commonly known as the Baenitz's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, resembling other blackberry varieties. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant in its native range.
