Ruellia dissidens var. dissidens

Ruellia dissidens var. dissidens, commonly known as the narrowleaf ruellia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming from late spring to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a native, low-maintenance plant.
