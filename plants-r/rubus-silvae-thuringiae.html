Rubus silvae-thuringiae

Rubus silvae-thuringiae, commonly known as the Thuringian wood raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, white flowers in the spring.

The fruit, which resembles a raspberry, is edible and has a tart flavor.
