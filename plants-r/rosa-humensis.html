Rosa humensis

Rosa humensis, commonly known as the Chilean rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, and is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa humensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
