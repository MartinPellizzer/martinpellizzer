Rubus × latidens

Rubus × latidens, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained, fertile ground. The plant produces large, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically broad and trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
