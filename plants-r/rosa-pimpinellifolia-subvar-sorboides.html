Rosa pimpinellifolia subvar. sorboides

Rosa pimpinellifolia subvar. sorboides, commonly known as the dwarf rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
