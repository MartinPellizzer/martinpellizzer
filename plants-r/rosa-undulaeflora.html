Rosa undulaeflora

Rosa undulaeflora, commonly known as the wavy-flowered rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-edged leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as rose hips, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
