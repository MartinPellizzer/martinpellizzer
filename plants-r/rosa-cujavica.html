Rosa cujavica

Rosa cujavica, commonly known as the Cujavica rose, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous regions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, reddish fruits that contain seeds.

Rosa cujavica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
