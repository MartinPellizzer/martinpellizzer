Ranunculus royleanus

Ranunculus royleanus, commonly known as the Himalayan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 2,000 and 5,000 meters. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers often have 5 to 15 petals.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
