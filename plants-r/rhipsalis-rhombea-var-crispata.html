Rhipsalis rhombea var. crispata

Rhipsalis rhombea var. crispata, commonly known as the curly rhipsalis, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that are covered in small, white spines. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the winter months. Its leaves are usually reduced to small tubercles, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
