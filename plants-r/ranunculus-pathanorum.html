Ranunculus pathanorum

Ranunculus pathanorum, commonly known as the Pathan buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan and northern India.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, typically yellow or white flowers with numerous petals. The plant features a short, stout stem that bears several leaves arranged in a basal rosette, with trifoliate or palmate leaflets. Its flowers are usually solitary and bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant prefers cool, moist habitats and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
