Rubus macrostemon var. viridescens

Rubus macrostemon var. viridescens, commonly known as the green-fruited bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by edible berries that are typically green to yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
