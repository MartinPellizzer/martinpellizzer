Rosa canina f. brevissima

Rosa canina f. brevissima, commonly known as the dwarf dog rose, is a compact shrub with small, thornless stems.

It produces pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is often found in woodland edges and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in hedgerows and gardens.
