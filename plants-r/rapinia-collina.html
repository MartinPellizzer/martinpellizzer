Rapinia collina

Rapinia collina, commonly known as mountain sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants characterized by their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible leaves. Rapinia collina is known for its tart, sour taste, making it a traditional ingredient in certain regional cuisines.
