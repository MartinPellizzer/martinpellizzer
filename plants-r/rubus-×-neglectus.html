Rubus × neglectus

Rubus × neglectus, commonly known as the common blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions across North America. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers in the spring.

This species is often considered invasive due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through vegetative propagation.
