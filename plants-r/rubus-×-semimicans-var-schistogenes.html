Rubus × semimicans var. schistogenes

Rubus × semimicans var. schistogenes, commonly known as the rock raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a bright red color. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated margins and a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and distinctive appearance.
