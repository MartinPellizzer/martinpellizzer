Rubus londinensis

Rubus londinensis, commonly known as the London rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces red, hairy fruits that are edible but often considered tart. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
