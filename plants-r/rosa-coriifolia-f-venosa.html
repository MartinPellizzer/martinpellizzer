Rosa coriifolia f. venosa

Rosa coriifolia f. venosa, commonly known as the veined shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its finely veined, ovate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a bushy growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its unique leaf venation and delicate flowers make it a standout in the rose family.
