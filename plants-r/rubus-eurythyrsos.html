Rubus eurythyrsos

Rubus eurythyrsos, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn from green to red before ripening to a dark purple-black color. Its flowers are white and typically appear in clusters, while the leaves are often covered in fine hairs.

This plant thrives in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its edible berries and ornamental appeal.
