Rubus argenteus subsp. cryptadenes

Rubus argenteus subsp.

cryptadenes, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring partial shade.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
