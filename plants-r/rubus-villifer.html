Rubus villifer

Rubus villifer, commonly known as the hairy raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are similar to raspberries in appearance. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

Rubus villifer thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.
