Rubus × senayi

Rubus × senayi, commonly known as the Senayi blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus pectoralis.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are prized for their flavor and nutritional value. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
