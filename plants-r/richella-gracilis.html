Richella gracilis

Richella gracilis, commonly known as the slender richella, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features delicate, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in clusters.

Richella gracilis is valued for its ecological role in supporting insect populations and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
