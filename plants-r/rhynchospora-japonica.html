Rhynchospora japonica

Rhynchospora japonica, commonly known as Japanese spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as bogs and wet meadows, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered and bear small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences.

Rhynchospora japonica is characterized by its narrow leaves and is often found in temperate regions of Japan, Korea, and China.
