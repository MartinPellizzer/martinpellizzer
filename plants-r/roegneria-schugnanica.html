Roegneria schugnanica

Roegneria schugnanica, commonly known as the Schugnanica wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed florets. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for ecological restoration, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation projects.
