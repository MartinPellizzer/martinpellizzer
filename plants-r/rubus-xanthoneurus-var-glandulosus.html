Rubus xanthoneurus var. glandulosus

Rubus xanthoneurus var. glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive glandular structures on its stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of North America. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. Its leaves are usually serrated and have a hairy texture, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.

This variety is often found in moist, wooded areas and thrives in temperate climates.
