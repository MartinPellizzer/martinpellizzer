Rubus villicaulis microgene insularis

Rubus villicaulis microgene insularis, commonly known as the island blackberry, is a rare shrub native to specific island ecosystems.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark, edible berries. The plant has small, serrated leaves and produces flowers with five pink petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
