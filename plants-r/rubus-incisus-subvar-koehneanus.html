Rubus incisus subvar. koehneanus

Rubus incisus subvar. koehneanus, commonly known as the Koehnean blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique morphological traits that set it apart from other blackberry species.
