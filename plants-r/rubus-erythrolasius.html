Rubus erythrolasius

Rubus erythrolasius, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark red to black when mature. The berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in culinary applications.

It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is a member of the Rosaceae family.
