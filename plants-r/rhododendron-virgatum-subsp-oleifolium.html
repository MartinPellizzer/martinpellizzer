Rhododendron virgatum subsp. oleifolium

Rhododendron virgatum subsp.

oleifolium, commonly known as the Korean rhododendron, is a shrub native to Korea and northeastern China. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The leaves are leathery and dark green, providing year-round visual interest. This subspecies thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
