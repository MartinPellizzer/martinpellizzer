Rubus septorum

Rubus septorum, commonly known as the Scottish blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and hedgerows.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
