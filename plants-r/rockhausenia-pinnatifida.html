Rockhausenia pinnatifida

Rockhausenia pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, pinnately divided leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
