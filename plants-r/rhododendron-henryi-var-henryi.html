Rhododendron henryi var. henryi

Rhododendron henryi var. henryi, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color, adding vibrant visual interest to the landscape. This plant prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
