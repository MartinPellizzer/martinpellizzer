Rytidocarpus moricandioides var. maroccanus

Rytidocarpus moricandioides var. maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan hibiscus, is a shrub native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It features broad, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in summer. It is well adapted to drought and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
