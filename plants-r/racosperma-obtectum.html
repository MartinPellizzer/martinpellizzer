Racosperma obtectum

Racosperma obtectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features thin, linear leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense spikes.

Racosperma obtectum is adapted to dry environments and is often found in sandy soils.
