Rubus corylifolius f. permixtus

Rubus corylifolius f. permixtus, commonly known as the mixed-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the Chinese blackberry.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features a mix of different leaf shapes and sizes. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and produces aggregate fruits known as drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible berries.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject in both horticulture and plant taxonomy.
