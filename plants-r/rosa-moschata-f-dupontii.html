Rosa moschata f. dupontii

Rosa moschata f. dupontii, commonly known as the Dupont musk rose, is a fragrant, deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which are borne in clusters and emit a strong, musky scent. The plant has glossy, dark green foliage and produces oval, orange-red hips that are edible. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery.

This cultivar is often used in hybridization to enhance fragrance and bloom characteristics in rose varieties.
