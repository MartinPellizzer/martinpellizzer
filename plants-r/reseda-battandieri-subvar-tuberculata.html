Reseda battandieri subvar. tuberculata

Reseda battandieri subvar. tuberculata, commonly known as the tuberculate mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The leaves are typically palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, giving them a distinctive texture. This subvariety is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
