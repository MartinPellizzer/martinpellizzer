Rajania

Rajania, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

These plants are native to the Himalayas and are characterized by their bell-shaped flowers, which can range in color from white to purple. Rajania species typically grow in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in cool, moist environments. They have a fibrous root system and are often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, contributing to their distinctive appearance.
