Ruta kotschyi

Ruta kotschyi, commonly known as the Afghan rue, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
