Rubus erythrophlaeus

Rubus erythrophlaeus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn red when mature, often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
