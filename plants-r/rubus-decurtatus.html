Rubus decurtatus

Rubus decurtatus, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry.

It is a shrub native to East Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible but can be quite tart, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
