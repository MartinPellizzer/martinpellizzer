Rosa communis var. cebennensis

Rosa communis var. cebennensis, commonly known as the Cebennensis rose, is a variety of the common rose that features distinctive botanical characteristics.

It typically produces fragrant, pink to red flowers with five to seven petals, arranged in a typical rose blossom structure. The plant has compound, alternate leaves with serrated margins, and thorny stems typical of the Rosa genus. It is native to Central and Eastern Europe, where it thrives in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
