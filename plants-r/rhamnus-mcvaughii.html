Rhamnus mcvaughii

Rhamnus mcvaughii, commonly known as the McVaugh's buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
