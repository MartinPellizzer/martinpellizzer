Rubus deflexispinus

Rubus deflexispinus, commonly known as the bent spine blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and stabilizing soil.
