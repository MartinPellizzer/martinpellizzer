Rhynchospora hieronymi var. montevidensis

Rhynchospora hieronymi var. montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Uruguay and southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces seed capsules that disperse via wind.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its potential for use in wetland restoration projects.
