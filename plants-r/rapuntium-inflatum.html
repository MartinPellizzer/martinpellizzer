Rapuntium inflatum

Rapuntium inflatum, commonly known as the inflated thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, spiny, spherical fruits that resemble inflated balloons, which are a key feature in its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture and are covered in fine hairs.

The species is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
