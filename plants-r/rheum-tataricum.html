Rheum tataricum

Rheum tataricum, commonly known as the Tibetan rhubarb or the Chinese rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and tall, reddish stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly the presence of anthraquinone compounds in its roots and rhizomes. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a distinctive, fibrous texture in its underground parts.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its therapeutic uses in traditional medicine.
