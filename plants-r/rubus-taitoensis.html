Rubus taitoensis

Rubus taitoensis, commonly known as the Taito raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs known for their edible berries. The plant produces small, red fruits that are typically covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
