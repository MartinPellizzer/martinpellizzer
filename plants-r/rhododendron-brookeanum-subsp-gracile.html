Rhododendron brookeanum subsp. gracile

Rhododendron brookeanum subsp.

gracile, commonly known as the slender brookean rhododendron, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often densely packed and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, humid climates with well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
