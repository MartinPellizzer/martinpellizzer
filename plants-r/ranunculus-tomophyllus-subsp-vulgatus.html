Ranunculus tomophyllus subsp. vulgatus

Ranunculus tomophyllus subsp.

vulgatus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with 5 to 15 petals.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough, hairy texture.

It blooms from spring to early summer, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
