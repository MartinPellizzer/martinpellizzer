Rapanea kauaiensis

Rapanea kauaiensis, commonly known as Kauai holly, is a rare shrub native to the island of Kauai in Hawaii.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, red outer layer.

Due to habitat loss and invasive species, Rapanea kauaiensis is critically endangered and is a priority for conservation efforts in Hawaii.
