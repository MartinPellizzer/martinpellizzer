Rubus histrix

Rubus histrix, commonly known as the prickly rose or brambles, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodlands and along hedgerows.

Rubus histrix is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
