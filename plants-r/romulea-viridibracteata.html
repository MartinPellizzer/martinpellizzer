Romulea viridibracteata

Romulea viridibracteata, commonly known as the green bract romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant's bulbs are adapted for storing water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. It typically blooms in spring and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
