Rhynchosia menispermoides

Rhynchosia menispermoides, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant bears pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas.

Botanically, it is notable for its medicinal uses and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
