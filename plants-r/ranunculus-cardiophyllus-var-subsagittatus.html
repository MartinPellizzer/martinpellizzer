Ranunculus cardiophyllus var. subsagittatus

Ranunculus cardiophyllus var. subsagittatus, commonly known as the arrowhead buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive arrowhead base, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a short stem with a single flower at the top, and its leaves are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
