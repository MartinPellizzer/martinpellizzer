Rhododendron traillianum

Rhododendron traillianum, commonly known as the Traill's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forests or along stream banks. Its flowers range in color from white to pink, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

Rhododendron traillianum is also noted for its adaptability to acidic soils and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
