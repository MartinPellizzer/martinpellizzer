Rubus irasuensis

Rubus irasuensis, commonly known as the Izu raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs commonly referred to as blackberries or raspberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits. It typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cooler climates.

Botanically, it is noted for its ornamental value and potential use in horticulture.
