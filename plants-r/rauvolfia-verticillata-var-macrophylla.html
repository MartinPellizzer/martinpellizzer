Rauvolfia verticillata var. macrophylla

Rauvolfia verticillata var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved devil's backbone, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins and waxy surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Rauvolfiaceae family.
