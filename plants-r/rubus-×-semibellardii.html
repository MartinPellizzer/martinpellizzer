Rubus × semibellardii

Rubus × semibellardii, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus laciniatus, resulting in a species with unique characteristics. It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, thriving in sandy soils and mild climates. The plant produces dark purple to black berries, which are edible and have a tart flavor.

Its evergreen nature allows it to maintain foliage year-round, making it a valuable plant for coastal landscaping.
