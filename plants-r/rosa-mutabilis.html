Rosa mutabilis

Rosa mutabilis, commonly known as the ever-changing rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its unique ability to change flower color over time, starting as white and shifting to pink, then red, and finally yellow. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Rosa mutabilis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
