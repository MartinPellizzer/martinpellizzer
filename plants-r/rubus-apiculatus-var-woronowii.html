Rubus apiculatus var. woronowii

Rubus apiculatus var. woronowii, commonly known as the Woronowii blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, dark purple flowers in the spring. The plant bears edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and potential in horticultural cultivation.
