Ricinus jarak

Ricinus jarak, commonly known as castor oil plant, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and southern regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and distinctive spiny capsules that contain toxic seeds. The plant is known for producing castor oil, which is extracted from its seeds and used in various industrial and medicinal applications. Ricinus jarak thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is also valued for its ornamental appearance and historical significance in traditional medicine.
