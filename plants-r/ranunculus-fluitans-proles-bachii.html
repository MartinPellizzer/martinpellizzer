Ranunculus fluitans proles bachii

Ranunculus fluitans proles bachii, commonly known as the floating buttercup, is a rare aquatic plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help it remain buoyant on the water's surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the water, allowing it to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.
