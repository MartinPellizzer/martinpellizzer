Ranunculus hirtellus var. sigyilaicus

Ranunculus hirtellus var. sigyilaicus, commonly known as the Sierra larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
