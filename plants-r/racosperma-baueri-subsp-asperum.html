Racosperma baueri subsp. asperum

Racosperma baueri subsp.

asperum, commonly known as the rough-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its grey-green, rough-textured leaves and yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Its hardy nature makes it a resilient plant in harsh environmental conditions.
