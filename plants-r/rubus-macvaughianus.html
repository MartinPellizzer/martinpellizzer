Rubus macvaughianus

Rubus macvaughianus, commonly known as the MacVaugh's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

The botanical features of Rubus macvaughianus make it an important plant in local ecosystems and for traditional uses.
