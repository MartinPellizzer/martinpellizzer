Rhynchocarpa vieillardii

Rhynchocarpa vieillardii, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by distinctive, inflated pods that resemble a moon, hence its common name. Rhynchocarpa vieillardii is known for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
