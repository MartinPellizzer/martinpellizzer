Rhododendron kialense

Rhododendron kialense, commonly known as the Kialen rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters, where it thrives in cool, humid climates. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, providing efficient photosynthesis in shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, clustered in dense inflorescences, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron kialense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
