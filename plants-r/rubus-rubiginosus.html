Rubus rubiginosus

Rubus rubiginosus, commonly known as the blackberry cane or trailing blackberry, is a species of bramble native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible berries, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
