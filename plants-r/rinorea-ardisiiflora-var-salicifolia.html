Rinorea ardisiiflora var. salicifolia

Rinorea ardisiiflora var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leafed rinorea, is a species native to parts of South America.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and clustered in axillary inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
