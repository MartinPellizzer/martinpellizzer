Rosa × suberectiformis

Rosa × suberectiformis, commonly known as the climbing rose, is a hybrid species that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics.

It typically features thorny stems that can climb or trail, making it suitable for trellises or arbors. The plant produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with multiple layers of petals. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Rosa × suberectiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in garden landscapes.
