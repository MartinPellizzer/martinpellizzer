Ranunculus rectus

Ranunculus rectus, commonly known as the straight buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with simple, alternate leaves that are usually lobed or serrated. Its flowers have five to eight petals, often in shades of yellow, orange, or red, and are surrounded by sepals that are usually green or reddish.

Ranunculus rectus prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.
