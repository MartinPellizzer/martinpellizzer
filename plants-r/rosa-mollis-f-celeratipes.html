Rosa mollis f. celeratipes

Rosa mollis f. celeratipes, commonly known as the quick-footed shrub rose, is a compact, thornless shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features small, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage, making it an attractive ornamental species. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, requiring minimal maintenance once established.

Due to its unique growth pattern and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
