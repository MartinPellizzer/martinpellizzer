Rhynchosia phaseoloides var. precatoria

Rhynchosia phaseoloides var. precatoria, commonly known as the African butterbean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used as a forage crop or in traditional medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
