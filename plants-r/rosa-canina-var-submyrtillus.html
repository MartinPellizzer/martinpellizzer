Rosa canina var. submyrtillus

Rosa canina var. submyrtillus, commonly known as the red-fruited dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and fragrant pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
