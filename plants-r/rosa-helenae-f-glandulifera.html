Rosa helenae f. glandulifera

Rosa helenae f. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular rose, is a botanical variant of the Helen's rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it a unique texture and appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to certain regions of Europe and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
