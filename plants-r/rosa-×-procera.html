Rosa × procera

Rosa × procera, commonly known as the climbing rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb or trail along supports. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, which bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season. Its foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, providing a lush backdrop to the showy blossoms.

Rosa × procera is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
