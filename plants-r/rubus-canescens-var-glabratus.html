Rubus canescens var. glabratus

Rubus canescens var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth bramble, is a variant of the common bramble species.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red or black berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
