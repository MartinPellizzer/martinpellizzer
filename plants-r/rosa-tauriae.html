Rosa tauriae

Rosa tauriae, commonly known as the Taurian rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals. Rosa tauriae produces rose hips that are orange to reddish in color, containing seeds within a fuzzy covering.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
