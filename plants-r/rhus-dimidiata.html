Rhus dimidiata

Rhus dimidiata, commonly known as the African sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves that are pinnately arranged with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are edible for some animals. Rhus dimidiata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
