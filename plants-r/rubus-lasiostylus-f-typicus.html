Rubus lasiostylus f. typicus

Rubus lasiostylus f. typicus, commonly known as the typical form of the hairy-styled raspberry.

It is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it is valued for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
