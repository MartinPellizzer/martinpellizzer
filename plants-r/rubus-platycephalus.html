Rubus platycephalus

Rubus platycephalus, commonly known as the flat-headed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit of Rubus platycephalus is often used in local cuisines and can be brewed into jams or wines.
