Rhamnus davurica f. pubescens

Rhamnus davurica f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent Chinese buckthorn, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, dark blue berries that are edible but have a laxative effect. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in horticulture and wildlife habitats.
