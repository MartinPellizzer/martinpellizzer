Rudolphia superba

Rudolphia superba, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple and white flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns resembling those of a passionflower. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its showy inflorescence that attracts various pollinators. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Rudolphia superba is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and can thrive in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.
