Rosa pacheri

Rosa pacheri, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange-red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves, and it typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Rosa pacheri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant, showy blooms.
