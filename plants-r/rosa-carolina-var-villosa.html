Rosa carolina var. villosa

Rosa carolina var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy wood rose, is a North American species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade.

Rosa carolina var. villosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
