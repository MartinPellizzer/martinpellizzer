Rosa ochroleuca var. milledentata

Rosa ochroleuca var. milledentata, commonly known as the Milledentate Rosa, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed leaves and clusters of fragrant white to pale yellow flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in cool, temperate climates. The leaves are typically oval with a serrated margin, and the plant produces rose hips that are reddish-orange in color.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
