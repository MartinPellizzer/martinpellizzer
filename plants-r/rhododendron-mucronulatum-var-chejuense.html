Rhododendron mucronulatum var. chejuense

Rhododendron mucronulatum var. chejuense, commonly known as the Cheju rhododendron, is a species native to the island of Jeju in South Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are bell-shaped with five petals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant spring display.
