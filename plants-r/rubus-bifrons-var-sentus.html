Rubus bifrons var. sentus

Rubus bifrons var. sentus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its distinctive red fruit, which sets it apart from the more common black berries of the species. The plant is a perennial shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in jams and desserts.

The botanical features of this plant make it a notable species within the Rubus family, valued both for its ornamental and culinary qualities.
