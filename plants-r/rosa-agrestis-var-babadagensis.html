Rosa agrestis var. babadagensis

Rosa agrestis var. babadagensis, commonly known as the Babadangensis rose, is a variant of the wild rose species native to South America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa genus. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its fruit is a red, hairy rose hip.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
