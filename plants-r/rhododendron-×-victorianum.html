Rhododendron × victorianum

Rhododendron × victorianum, commonly known as the Victorian rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between Rhododendron catawbiense and Rhododendron ferrugineum.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is native to the mountainous regions of western Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and its role in ecological habitats.
