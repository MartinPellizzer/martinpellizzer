Rubia balearica

Rubia balearica, commonly known as the Balearic madder, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces red-orange roots that have been historically used in dyeing textiles. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, with a slightly hairy surface.

Rubia balearica thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.
