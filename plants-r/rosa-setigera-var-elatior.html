Rosa setigera var. elatior

Rosa setigera var. elatior, commonly known as the climbing rose or the willowleaf rose, is a variant of the willow-leaf rose species.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems that can climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are finely divided, with a willow-like appearance, and are covered in a soft, silvery down.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
