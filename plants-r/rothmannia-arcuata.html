Rothmannia arcuata

Rothmannia arcuata, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a purple or brownish hue at the base. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rothmannia arcuata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
