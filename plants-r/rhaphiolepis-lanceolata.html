Rhaphiolepis lanceolata

Rhaphiolepis lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf firethorn, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It features glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Rhaphiolepis lanceolata is also valued for its resistance to pests and diseases, making it a low-maintenance addition to gardens.
