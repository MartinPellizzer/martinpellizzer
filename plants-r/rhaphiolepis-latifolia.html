Rhaphiolepis latifolia

Rhaphiolepis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a reddish or purplish hue during the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red berries that are ornamental and persist through the winter. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
