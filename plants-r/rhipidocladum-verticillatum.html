Rhipidocladum verticillatum

Rhipidocladum verticillatum, commonly known as the spiral leaf cactus, is a small, slow-growing cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that are covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often borne at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a unique spiral arrangement of stems that gives it its common name.

Rhipidocladum verticillatum is well adapted to dry environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
