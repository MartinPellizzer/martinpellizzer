Rubus × semibipartitus

Rubus × semibipartitus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and northern Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant year-round. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal climates.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and hardy member of the Rosaceae family.
