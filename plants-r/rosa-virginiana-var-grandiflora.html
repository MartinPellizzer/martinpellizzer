Rosa virginiana var. grandiflora

Rosa virginiana var. grandiflora, commonly known as the showy wild rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its stems are often armed with sharp prickles. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
