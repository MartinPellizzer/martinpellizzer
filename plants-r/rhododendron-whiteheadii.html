Rhododendron whiteheadii

Rhododendron whiteheadii, commonly known as the Whitehead's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from white to pale pink.

Rhododendron whiteheadii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
