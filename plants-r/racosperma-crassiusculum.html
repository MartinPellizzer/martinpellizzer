Racosperma crassiusculum

Racosperma crassiusculum, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has a woody stem and produces pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Racosperma crassiusculum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
