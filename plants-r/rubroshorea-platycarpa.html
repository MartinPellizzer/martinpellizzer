Rubroshorea platycarpa

Rubroshorea platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited redshore, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, woody stems and broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
