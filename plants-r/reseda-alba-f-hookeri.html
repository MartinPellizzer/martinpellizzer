Reseda alba f. hookeri

Reseda alba f. hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers clustered in loose spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological restoration projects.
