Restio bigeminus

Restio bigeminus, commonly known as the twin-stemmed restia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland fynbos vegetation.

Restio bigeminus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
