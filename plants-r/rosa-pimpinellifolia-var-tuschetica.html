Rosa pimpinellifolia var. tuschetica

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. tuschetica, commonly known as the Tuscan rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves that are adapted to rocky and well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions, thriving in full sun.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators and adding ornamental value to gardens.
