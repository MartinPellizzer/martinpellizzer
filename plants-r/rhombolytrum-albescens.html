Rhombolytrum albescens

Rhombolytrum albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered rhomboid grass, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rhombic-shaped leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall. Its growth habit is typically erect, with slender stems that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Rhombolytrum albescens plays a minor role in local ecosystems, providing food for insects and serving as a potential ornamental plant in dryland gardens.
