Rubus costifolius

Rubus costifolius, commonly known as the serrated blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a firm, tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

The botanical features of Rubus costifolius make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
