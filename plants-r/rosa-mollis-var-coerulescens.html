Rosa mollis var. coerulescens

Rosa mollis var. coerulescens, commonly known as the blue rose or blue musk rose, is a variant of the silky rose that is notable for its unique blue-pink flower color.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in cooler climates. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically dark green and covered with a fine, silvery down.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and distinctive coloration.
