Ranunculus rapaicsianus

Ranunculus rapaicsianus, commonly known as the Armenian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features palmately divided leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
