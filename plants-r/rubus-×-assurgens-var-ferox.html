Rubus × assurgens var. ferox

Rubus × assurgens var. ferox, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a common weed in agricultural settings.
