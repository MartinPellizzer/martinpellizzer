Ranunculus bulbosus var. hispanicus

Ranunculus bulbosus var. hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that resemble petals, and it typically grows in moist, calcareous soils. The plant has a short, stout rhizome and produces small, green, spiny fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
