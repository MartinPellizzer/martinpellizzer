Rhus radicans var. toxicodendron

Rhus radicans var. toxicodendron, commonly known as poison ivy, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces smooth, reddish berries that are an important food source for birds. It is known for its potent urushiol oil, which causes allergic skin reactions in many people.

This plant thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, fields, and along stream banks.
