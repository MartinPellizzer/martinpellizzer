Racosperma kingianum

Racosperma kingianum, commonly known as the King Island wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Racosperma kingianum is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.
