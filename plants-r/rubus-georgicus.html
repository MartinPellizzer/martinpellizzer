Rubus georgicus

Rubus georgicus, commonly known as the Georgian raspberry, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, reddish berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are pink or white.

The berries are edible and often used in local cuisines or for making jams and wines.
