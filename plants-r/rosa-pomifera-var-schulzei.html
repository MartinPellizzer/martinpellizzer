Rosa pomifera var. schulzei

Rosa pomifera var. schulzei, commonly known as the Schultze rose, is a rare and unique variant of the pomiferous rose.

It is characterized by its large, edible fruits that resemble small apples, which are a distinguishing botanical feature. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific cultivation needs, it is often found in botanical gardens or specialized collections.
