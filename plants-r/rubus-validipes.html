Rubus validipes

Rubus validipes, commonly known as the validipes blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

The berries are edible, though they are typically not as widely cultivated as other blackberry varieties.
