Rosa × waitziana var. avarica

Rosa × waitziana var. avarica, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a hybrid shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, semi-double flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces small, reddish-orange hips that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable.
