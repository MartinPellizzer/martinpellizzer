Rubus dumetorum var. nemorosus

Rubus dumetorum var. nemorosus, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry plant, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate. The plant produces aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of many ecosystems, supporting a variety of wildlife.
