Ruellia lucida

Ruellia lucida, commonly known as shining ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in clusters at the ends of the branches, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Ruellia lucida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
