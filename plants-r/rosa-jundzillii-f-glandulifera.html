Rosa jundzillii f. glandulifera

Rosa jundzillii f. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa jundzillii species.

It is characterized by its distinctive glandular hairs on the stems, leaves, and flowers, which give it its specific epithet. The plant typically produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and serrated, with a slightly hairy texture.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
