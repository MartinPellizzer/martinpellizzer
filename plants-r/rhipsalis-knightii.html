Rhipsalis knightii

Rhipsalis knightii, commonly known as the Knight's mistletoe cactus, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, hanging stems that often resemble tendrils, which help it climb and attach to trees. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom during the rainy season, adding a subtle beauty to its natural habitat. Its leaves are modified into small, hair-like structures that aid in water retention and reduce transpiration.

Rhipsalis knightii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
