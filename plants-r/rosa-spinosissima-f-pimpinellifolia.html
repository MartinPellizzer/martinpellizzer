Rosa spinosissima f. pimpinellifolia

Rosa spinosissima f. pimpinellifolia, commonly known as the pink lady's mantle, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its unique foliage and floral structure.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in a fine white down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a low-growing shrub, typically reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters, with thorny stems and a compact growth habit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
