Rhexia adamantium

Rhexia adamantium, commonly known as the pinkroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to magenta flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slight waxy coating.

Rhexia adamantium is also notable for its unique root system, which is reddish in color and contributes to its common name.
