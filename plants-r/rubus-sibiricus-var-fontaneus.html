Rubus sibiricus var. fontaneus

Rubus sibiricus var. fontaneus, commonly known as the Siberian raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of eastern Asia.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, often used in local cuisines. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and is characterized by its thorny stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
