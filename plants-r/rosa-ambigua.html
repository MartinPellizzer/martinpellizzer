Rosa ambigua

Rosa ambigua, commonly known as the swamp rose, is a species of flowering plant native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in wetland environments, such as bogs and marshes. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink or red, with a fragrant scent. It produces red or orange hips, which contain seeds and are a food source for various wildlife.

The botanical characteristics of Rosa ambigua make it well adapted to moist, nutrient-poor soils.
