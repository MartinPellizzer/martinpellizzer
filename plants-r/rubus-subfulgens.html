Rubus subfulgens

Rubus subfulgens, commonly known as the golden raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Argentina and Chile. The plant produces small, yellowish-red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant has a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems.

The botanical features of Rubus subfulgens make it an important species for both ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
