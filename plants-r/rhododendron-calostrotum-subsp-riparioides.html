Rhododendron calostrotum subsp. riparioides

Rhododendron calostrotum subsp.

riparioides, commonly known as the river rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows along riverbanks and in moist, shaded habitats, preferring acidic soils. Its botanical features include leathery leaves and showy, fragrant blooms that bloom in early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in riparian zones.
