Rhaponticum insigne

Rhaponticum insigne, commonly known as the Siberian oxeye, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It features a robust root system and produces a basal rosette of thick, hairy leaves. The plant is characterized by its large, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or tundra environments.

Rhaponticum insigne is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.
