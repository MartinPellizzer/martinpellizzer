Rosa coriifolia var. cerasifera

Rosa coriifolia var. cerasifera, commonly known as the cherry rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, often with a pink or red hue. The plant features thorny stems and elliptical, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall. Its fruit is a red, hairy berry that ripens in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
