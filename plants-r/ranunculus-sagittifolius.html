Ranunculus sagittifolius

Ranunculus sagittifolius, commonly known as the arrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. The plant has arrow-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in meadows and woodland areas.

Its botanical name reflects its leaf shape, with "sagittifolius" referring to the arrow-like foliage.
