Rubus × laxissimus

Rubus × laxissimus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible, though they may be tart in flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and a complex system of underground runners for propagation.
