Rosa gehlertiana

Rosa gehlertiana, commonly known as the Gehler's rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink to red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Rosa gehlertiana is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
