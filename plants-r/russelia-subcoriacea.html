Russelia subcoriacea

Russelia subcoriacea, commonly known as the velvetleaf russelia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Russelia subcoriacea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
