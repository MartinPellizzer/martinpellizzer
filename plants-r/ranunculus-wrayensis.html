Ranunculus wrayensis

Ranunculus wrayensis, commonly known as Wray's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow petals and a central cluster of bright orange anthers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

Ranunculus wrayensis is a perennial species that blooms in late spring to early summer.
