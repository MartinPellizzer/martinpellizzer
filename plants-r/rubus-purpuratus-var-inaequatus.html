Rubus purpuratus var. inaequatus

Rubus purpuratus var. inaequatus, commonly known as the unequal blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fruit that is typically smaller and less sweet than typical blackberries. The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark berries.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
