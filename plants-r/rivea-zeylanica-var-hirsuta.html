Rivea zeylanica var. hirsuta

Rivea zeylanica var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy rivea, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its flowering period occurs during the warm seasons, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The fruit is a long, slender pod that contains several seeds, contributing to its propagation in natural habitats.
