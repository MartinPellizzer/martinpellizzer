Rubus hirtus var. validipes

Rubus hirtus var. validipes, commonly known as the hairy blackberry or validipes blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
