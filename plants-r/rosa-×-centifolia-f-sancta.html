Rosa × centifolia f. sancta

Rosa × centifolia f. sancta, commonly known as the holy rose or the sacred rose, is a fragrant, flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus, characterized by its numerous petals and strong, sweet scent. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters and produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in thorns.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in religious and cultural contexts.
