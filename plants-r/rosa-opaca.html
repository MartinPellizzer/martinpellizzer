Rosa opaca

Rosa opaca, commonly known as the Mexican rose, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually pink to red in color, blooming in spring and early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a strong, pleasant scent.

Rosa opaca is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
