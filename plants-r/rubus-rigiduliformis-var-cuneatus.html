Rubus rigiduliformis var. cuneatus

Rubus rigiduliformis var. cuneatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bramble, is a shrub native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red, edible berries in the summer. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it an important species for local ecosystems and wildlife.
