Rubus ulmifolius f. obovatus

Rubus ulmifolius f. obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry.

It is characterized by its large, obovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This form is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges across Europe.
