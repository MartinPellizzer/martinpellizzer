Rhododendron orthocladum var. orthocladum

Rhododendron orthocladum var. orthocladum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a striking visual contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
