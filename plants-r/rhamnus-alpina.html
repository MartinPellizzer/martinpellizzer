Rhamnus alpina

Rhamnus alpina, commonly known as the alpine buckthorn, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by black, egg-shaped berries. Rhamnus alpina thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
