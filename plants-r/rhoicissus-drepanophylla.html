Rhoicissus drepanophylla

Rhoicissus drepanophylla, commonly known as the blueberry vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing habit and produces small, blue-black berries that are edible. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and fruit.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and are used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
