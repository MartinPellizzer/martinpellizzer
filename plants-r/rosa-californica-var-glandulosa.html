Rosa californica var. glandulosa

Rosa californica var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular California rose, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves with glandular dots on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rosa genus.
