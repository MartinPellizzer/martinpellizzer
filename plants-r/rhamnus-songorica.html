Rhamnus songorica

Rhamnus songorica, commonly known as the Songorica buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
