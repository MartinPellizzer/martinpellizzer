Rubus dumetorum var. triangularis

Rubus dumetorum var. triangularis, commonly known as the three-angled blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed raw or used in jams and preserves. The plant has thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences, and the fruit develops from the fertilized flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
