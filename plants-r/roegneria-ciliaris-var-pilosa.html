Roegneria ciliaris var. pilosa

Roegneria ciliaris var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
