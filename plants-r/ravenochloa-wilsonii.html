Ravenochloa wilsonii

Ravenochloa wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's ravengrass, is a rare and distinctive grass species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on alpine plant adaptations and conservation.
