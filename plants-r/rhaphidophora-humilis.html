Rhaphidophora humilis

Rhaphidophora humilis, commonly known as the small-leafed arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and clustered, spadix-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with a rhizomatous stem, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are broad and lanceolate, with a glossy surface and prominent veins.

Rhaphidophora humilis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
