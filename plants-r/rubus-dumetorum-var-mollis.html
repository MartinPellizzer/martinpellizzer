Rubus dumetorum var. mollis

Rubus dumetorum var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-fruited blackberry bush, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its edible berries and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
