Rhododendron tritifolium

Rhododendron tritifolium, commonly known as the lesser cow-plant, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in early spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are usually arranged in whorls of three, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments.

Rhododendron tritifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
