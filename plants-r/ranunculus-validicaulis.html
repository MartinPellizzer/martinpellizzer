Ranunculus validicaulis

Ranunculus validicaulis, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine, or subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Ranunculus validicaulis is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
