Rhododendron lasiostylum

Rhododendron lasiostylum, commonly known as the woolly rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often covered in a fine, woolly coating.

Rhododendron lasiostylum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
