Rosa acicularis f. glandulosa

Rosa acicularis f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular thorned rose, is a botanical variant of the thorned rose species.

It is characterized by its glandular hairs, which are small, sticky structures covering its stems and leaves. The plant produces pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
