Ruta chalepensis var. fumariifolia

Ruta chalepensis var. fumariifolia, commonly known as the smoky rue, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, dark green leaves that are deeply lobed and have a distinctive smoky-blue hue. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by narrow, elongated fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
