Rubus sagatus

Rubus sagatus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated for its edible berries and ornamental value.
