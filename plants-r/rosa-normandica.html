Rosa normandica

Rosa normandica, commonly known as the Norman rose, is a species native to France and parts of Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its arching stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces oval, green leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa normandica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
