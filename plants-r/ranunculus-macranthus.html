Ranunculus macranthus

Ranunculus macranthus, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals and a central cluster of yellow stamens. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy. Its botanical name reflects its large flowers, with "macranthus" referring to its large, showy inflorescence.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.
