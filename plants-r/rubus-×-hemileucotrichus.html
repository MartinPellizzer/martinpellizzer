Rubus × hemileucotrichus

Rubus × hemileucotrichus, commonly known as the blackberry or raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically red or black when mature, containing small, hard seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its vigorous growth, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
