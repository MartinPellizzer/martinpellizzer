Ranunculus auricomus subsp. flabelliformis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

flabelliformis, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five to nine broadly spreading petals.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and streambanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the subspecies is distinguished by its flabelliform (fan-shaped) petals.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic compounds.
