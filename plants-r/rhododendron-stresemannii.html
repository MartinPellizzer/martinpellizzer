Rhododendron stresemannii

Rhododendron stresemannii, commonly known as the Stresemann's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly found in regions of Nepal, Bhutan, and northern India.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
