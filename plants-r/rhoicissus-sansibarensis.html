Rhoicissus sansibarensis

Rhoicissus sansibarensis, commonly known as the Zanzibar grapevine, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Vitaceae family, which includes other well-known vines like grapes and ivy.
