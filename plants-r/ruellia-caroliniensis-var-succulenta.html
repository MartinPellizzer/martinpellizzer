Ruellia caroliniensis var. succulenta

Ruellia caroliniensis var. succulenta, commonly known as the succulent ruellia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often bluish-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal in both landscape design and container gardening.
