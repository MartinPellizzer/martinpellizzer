Rosa blanda var. typica

Rosa blanda var. typica, commonly known as the meadow rose, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval, serrated leaflets.

The stems are covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance.
