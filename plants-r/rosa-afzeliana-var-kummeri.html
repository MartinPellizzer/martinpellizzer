Rosa afzeliana var. kummeri

Rosa afzeliana var. kummeri, commonly known as the Kummer's shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features arching stems with thorny branches and is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has serrated, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy underside, and its root system is fibrous and adapted to rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.

This variety is also noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of climatic conditions.
