Ruellia caroliniensis var. salicina

Ruellia caroliniensis var. salicina, commonly known as the Florida rainflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for wetland restoration projects and native gardens.
