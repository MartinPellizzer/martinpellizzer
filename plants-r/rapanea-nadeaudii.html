Rapanea nadeaudii

Rapanea nadeaudii, commonly known as the Madagascar laurel, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are typically red when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
