Rubus godronii var. acuticuspis

Rubus godronii var. acuticuspis, commonly known as the Godron's raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
