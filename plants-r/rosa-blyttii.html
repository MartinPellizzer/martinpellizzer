Rosa blyttii

Rosa blyttii, commonly known as the Blytt's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly found in countries like South Africa, Namibia, and Botswana. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several elliptical leaflets, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as hips.

Rosa blyttii is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
