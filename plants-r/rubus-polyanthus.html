Rubus polyanthus

Rubus polyanthus, commonly known as the evergreen bramble, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces edible red berries, which are often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
