Rosa elasmacantha var. iberica

Rosa elasmacantha var. iberica, commonly known as the Iberian prickly rose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and it thrives in rocky or dry soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
