Rumex hexagynus

Rumex hexagynus, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types, including moist or wet environments.

Narrowleaf dock is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in fields, where it can form dense stands.
