Ranunculus engelianus

Ranunculus engelianus, commonly known as the Engelian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant features a basal rosette of serrated, palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five to eight sepals. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.

The species is also valued for its potential in horticulture and traditional medicine.
