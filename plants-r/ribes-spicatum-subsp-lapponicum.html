Ribes spicatum subsp. lapponicum

Ribes spicatum subsp.

lapponicum, commonly known as the Lapland gooseberry, is a low-growing shrub native to northern regions of Europe and Asia. It produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in boreal and tundra habitats. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and are covered in a fine pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional cuisine and medicine.
