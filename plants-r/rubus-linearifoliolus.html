Rubus linearifoliolus

Rubus linearifoliolus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the western United States, characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, dark berries. The plant produces clusters of thorny stems and has a shrub-like growth habit. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and the fruit is edible, though often tart.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
