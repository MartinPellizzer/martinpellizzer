Rhipsalis calamiformis

Rhipsalis calamiformis, commonly known as the weeping Christmas cactus, is a climbing cactus native to Brazil.

It features long, trailing stems that can grow up to several meters in length, adorned with small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom during the winter months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
