Rubus rivularis var. subcaucasicus

Rubus rivularis var. subcaucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that often root at the nodes. The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine, soft hairs. The flowers are white, with five petals, and appear in clusters during the spring.

Its berries are small, dark purple to black, and are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor.
