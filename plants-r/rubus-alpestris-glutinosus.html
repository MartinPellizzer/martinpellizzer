Rubus alpestris glutinosus

Rubus alpestris glutinosus, commonly known as the sticky alpine bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, typically growing in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with a hairy or sticky texture.

The fruit, a small red berry, is edible and often used in local cuisines.
