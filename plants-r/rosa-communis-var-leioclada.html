Rosa communis var. leioclada

Rosa communis var. leioclada, commonly known as the white evergreen rose, is a variety of the common rose that is characterized by its persistent, white flowers.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The flowers are typically borne singly or in small clusters and have a sweet, delicate fragrance. This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
