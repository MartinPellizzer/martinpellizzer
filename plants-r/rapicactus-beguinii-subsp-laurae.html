Rapicactus beguinii subsp. laurae

Rapicactus beguinii subsp.

laurae, commonly known as Laura's rock cactus, is a succulent native to Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale green spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
