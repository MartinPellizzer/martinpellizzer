Rumex × dissimilis

Rumex × dissimilis, commonly known as the hybrid sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant that exhibits characteristics of its parent species, Rumex acetosa (common sorrel) and Rumex patientia (sorrel).

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves with a reddish or purple coloration on the veins, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
