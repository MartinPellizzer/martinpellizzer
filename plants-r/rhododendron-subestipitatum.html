Rhododendron subestipitatum

Rhododendron subestipitatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a lush, evergreen appearance throughout the year.

Rhododendron subestipitatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific ecological niches.
