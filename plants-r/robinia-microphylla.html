Robinia microphylla

Robinia microphylla, commonly known as the burr oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight serration along the edges. The tree produces distinctive, spiny seed pods that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. Its bark is thick and deeply furrowed, providing a rugged appearance.

Robinia microphylla is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.
