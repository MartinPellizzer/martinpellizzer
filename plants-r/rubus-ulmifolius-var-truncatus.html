Rubus ulmifolius var. truncatus

Rubus ulmifolius var. truncatus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a variant of the common blackberry plant.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a sweet-tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it both a valuable food source for wildlife and a challenging weed in agricultural settings.
