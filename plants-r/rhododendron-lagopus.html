Rhododendron lagopus

Rhododendron lagopus, commonly known as the snow laurel, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

Rhododendron lagopus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
