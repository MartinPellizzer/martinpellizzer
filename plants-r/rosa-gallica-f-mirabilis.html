Rosa gallica f. mirabilis

Rosa gallica f. mirabilis, commonly known as the Fairy Blush rose, is a cultivar of the Gallica rose, characterized by its vibrant, deep pink to crimson flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to fully double blooms in late spring to early summer. It is native to Europe and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for garden cultivation and ornamental use.
