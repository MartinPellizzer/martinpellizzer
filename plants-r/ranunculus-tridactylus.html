Ranunculus tridactylus

Ranunculus tridactylus, commonly known as the three-toed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often surrounded by a ring of leaf-like sepals. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are typically trifoliate, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its toxic compounds, including protoanemonin.
