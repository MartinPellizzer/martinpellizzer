Rhaphiolepis cheniana

Rhaphiolepis cheniana, commonly known as the Chinese firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, glossy green leaves and produces clusters of bright orange-red berries in the fall. The plant is part of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compact growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant fruits.

The berries are toxic to humans and animals, so caution is advised when handling or consuming them.
