Reseda lutea var. laxa

Reseda lutea var. laxa, commonly known as the slender yellowrocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Reseda lutea var. laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in dye production.
