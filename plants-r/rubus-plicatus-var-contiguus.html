Rubus plicatus var. contiguus

Rubus plicatus var. contiguus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
