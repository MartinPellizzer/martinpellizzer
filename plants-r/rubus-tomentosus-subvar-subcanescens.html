Rubus tomentosus subvar. subcanescens

Rubus tomentosus subvar. subcanescens, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white or gray pubescence covering the stems and leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

This subvariety is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
