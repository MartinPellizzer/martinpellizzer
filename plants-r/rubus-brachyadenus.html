Rubus brachyadenus

Rubus brachyadenus, commonly known as the short-stalked blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and shrublands, and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is a common sight in the Pacific Northwest region.
