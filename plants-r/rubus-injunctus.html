Rubus injunctus

Rubus injunctus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, dark purple to black fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with narrow, serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has been traditionally harvested by indigenous communities.
