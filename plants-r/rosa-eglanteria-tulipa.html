Rosa eglanteria tulipa

Rosa eglanteria tulipa, commonly known as the tulip rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and yellow. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It is native to eastern and central Europe and thrives in temperate climates.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
