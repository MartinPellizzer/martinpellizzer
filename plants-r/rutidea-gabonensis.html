Rutidea gabonensis

Rutidea gabonensis, commonly known as the Gabon legume, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically found in tropical rainforests and is adapted to the humid, shaded environments of its habitat.

Rutidea gabonensis produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
