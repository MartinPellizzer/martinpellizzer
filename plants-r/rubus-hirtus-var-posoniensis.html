Rubus hirtus var. posoniensis

Rubus hirtus var. posoniensis, commonly known as the Posonensis bramble, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
