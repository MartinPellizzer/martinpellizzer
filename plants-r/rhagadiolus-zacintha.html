Rhagadiolus zacintha

Rhagadiolus zacintha, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Rhagadiolus zacintha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
