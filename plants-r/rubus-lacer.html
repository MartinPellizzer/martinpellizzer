Rubus lacer

Rubus lacer, commonly known as the broken raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, usually white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.
