Rabdosia pseudoirrorata

Rabdosia pseudoirrorata, commonly known as the purple false spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often lance-shaped. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom during the summer months.

Rabdosia pseudoirrorata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
