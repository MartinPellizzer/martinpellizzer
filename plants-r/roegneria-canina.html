Roegneria canina

Roegneria canina, commonly known as the dog's grass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Roegneria canina is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
