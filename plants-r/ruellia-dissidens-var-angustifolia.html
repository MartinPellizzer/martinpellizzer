Ruellia dissidens var. angustifolia

Ruellia dissidens var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue or violet tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
