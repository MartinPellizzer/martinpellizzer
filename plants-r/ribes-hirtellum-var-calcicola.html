Ribes hirtellum var. calcicola

Ribes hirtellum var. calcicola, commonly known as the Sierra gooseberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
