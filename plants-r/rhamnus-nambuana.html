Rhamnus nambuana

Rhamnus nambuana, commonly known as the Nambuana buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous forests. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are tiny and clustered, producing small, red or black berries that serve as a food source for various wildlife.

The bark is thick and ridged, often featuring a dark, almost blackish hue.
