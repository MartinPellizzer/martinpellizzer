Rhipsalis tonduzii

Rhipsalis tonduzii, commonly known as the Tonduzii mistletoe cactus, is a unique epiphytic plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its soft, leaf-like stems that are often mistaken for leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Rhipsalis tonduzii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees in tropical rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
