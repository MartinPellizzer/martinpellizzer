Ranunculus longipes

Ranunculus longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ranunculaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed shape, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Ranunculus longipes is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes.
