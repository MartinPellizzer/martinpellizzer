Ranunculus transiens

Ranunculus transiens, commonly known as the transient buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to ten petals, typically in shades of yellow, white, or orange. The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces achenes as its fruit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its blooming period is brief, usually in early spring, giving it its common name.
