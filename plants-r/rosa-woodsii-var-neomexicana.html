Rosa woodsii var. neomexicana

Rosa woodsii var. neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico rock rose, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with woody stems and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh conditions.
