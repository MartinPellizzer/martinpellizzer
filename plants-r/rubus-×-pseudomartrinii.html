Rubus × pseudomartrinii

Rubus × pseudomartrinii, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit, which ripens in late summer, is valued for its tart flavor and is used in culinary and commercial applications.
