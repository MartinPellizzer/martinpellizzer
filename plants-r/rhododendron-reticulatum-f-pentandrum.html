Rhododendron reticulatum f. pentandrum

Rhododendron reticulatum f. pentandrum, commonly known as the five-stamen rhododendron, is a hybrid form of the reticulatum species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, which provides a lush backdrop for its vibrant flowers. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, with five stamens, a trait that distinguishes it from the typical form. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
