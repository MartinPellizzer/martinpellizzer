Ranunculus balangshanicus

Ranunculus balangshanicus, commonly known as the Balangshani buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features a hairy stem and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five to eight sepals that resemble petals.

Ranunculus balangshanicus thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
