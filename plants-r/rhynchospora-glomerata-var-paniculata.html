Rhynchospora glomerata var. paniculata

Rhynchospora glomerata var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It features a grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically reddish in color. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering period occurs during the late spring to early summer, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

The species is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
