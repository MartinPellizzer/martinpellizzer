Rhododendron gideonii

Rhododendron gideonii, commonly known as the Gideon's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shady, humid environments. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in suitable climates.
