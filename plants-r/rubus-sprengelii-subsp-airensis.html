Rubus sprengelii subsp. airensis

Rubus sprengelii subsp.

airensis, commonly known as the Airen raspberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay. It is characterized by its erect or ascending growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
