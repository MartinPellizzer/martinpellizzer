Rosa canina var. incana

Rosa canina var. incana, commonly known as the hairy rose or dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and hairy foliage, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, hairy berries. It is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
