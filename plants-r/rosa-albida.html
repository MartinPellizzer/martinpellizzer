Rosa albida

Rosa albida, commonly known as the white rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, where it grows in woodland and mountainous regions. The plant produces showy, white flowers with a sweet fragrance, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and covered in fine hairs.

Rosa albida is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in horticulture.
