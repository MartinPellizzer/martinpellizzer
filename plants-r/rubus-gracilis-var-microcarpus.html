Rubus gracilis var. microcarpus

Rubus gracilis var. microcarpus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its thin, wiry stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. Its flowers are white with five petals and are borne in clusters.

The species is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and stream banks.
