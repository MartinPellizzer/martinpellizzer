Rhaphidophora insignis

Rhaphidophora insignis, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a tropical plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense clumps. Its leaves are typically glossy and have a distinctive pattern of light and dark green.

Rhaphidophora insignis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
