Ruellia megacantha

Ruellia megacantha, commonly known as the giant ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1.5 meters in height, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils with partial to full sunlight.

Ruellia megacantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
