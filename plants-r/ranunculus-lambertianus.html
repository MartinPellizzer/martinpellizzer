Ranunculus lambertianus

Ranunculus lambertianus, commonly known as Lambert's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Ranunculus lambertianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
