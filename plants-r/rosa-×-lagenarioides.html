Rosa × lagenarioides

Rosa × lagenarioides, commonly known as the everblooming rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from crosses between Rosa rugosa and Rosa × centifolia.

It is characterized by its ability to produce flowers continuously throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking prolonged bloom. The plant typically features dense, thorny stems and broad, dark green foliage. Its flowers are usually large, fragrant, and come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white.

Rosa × lagenarioides thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring minimal pruning to maintain its compact form.
