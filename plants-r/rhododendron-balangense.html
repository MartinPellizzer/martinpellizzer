Rhododendron balangense

Rhododendron balangense, commonly known as the Balangeng rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China and northern Vietnam.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters, preferring moist, well-drained soils in shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from white to pink and purple.

Rhododendron balangense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
