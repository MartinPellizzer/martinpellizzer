Roupala rhombifolia

Roupala rhombifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed roupala, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its rhombic-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, providing a striking visual contrast against its pale bark. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Roupala rhombifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its unique foliage.
