Rhododendron missionarium

Rhododendron missionarium, commonly known as the Missionary Rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forests with rich, acidic soil. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to red, depending on the variety.

Rhododendron missionarium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
