Ranunculus alsaticus

Ranunculus alsaticus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow or white flowers with five petals and a hairy, lobed leaf structure. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or alpine habitats, often at high altitudes. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic properties.

Ranunculus alsaticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
