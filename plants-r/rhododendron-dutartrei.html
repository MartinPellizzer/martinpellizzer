Rhododendron dutartrei

Rhododendron dutartrei, commonly known as the Mauritius rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions with well-drained soil. It is noted for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.

However, it is considered endangered in its native habitat due to habitat loss and invasive species.
