Rhododendron sieboldii var. serrulatum

Rhododendron sieboldii var. serrulatum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a flowering shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically elliptical with serrated edges, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in late spring to early summer.
