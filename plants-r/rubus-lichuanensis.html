Rubus lichuanensis

Rubus lichuanensis, commonly known as the Lichuan blackberry, is a species of brambles native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
