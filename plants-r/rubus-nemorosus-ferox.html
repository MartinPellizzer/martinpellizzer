Rubus nemorosus ferox

Rubus nemorosus ferox, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry bush, is a species of shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Its growth habit makes it both a valuable food source for wildlife and a challenging weed in agricultural settings.
