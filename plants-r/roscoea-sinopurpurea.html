Roscoea sinopurpurea

Roscoea sinopurpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered roscoea, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers, and is characterized by its ornamental, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall, slender stems topped with clusters of fragrant, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage is often described as resembling that of a fern, with a lush, tropical appearance.

Roscoea sinopurpurea is valued in gardens for its vibrant color and unique foliage, though it requires a cool, moist environment to thrive.
