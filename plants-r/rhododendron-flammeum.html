Rhododendron flammeum

Rhododendron flammeum, commonly known as the flame azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and produces elliptical, dark green leaves. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The blooms appear in late spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.
