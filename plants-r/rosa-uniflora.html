Rosa uniflora

Rosa uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces solitary, large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are covered in thorny prickles. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, making it a notable ornamental plant in gardens.

Rosa uniflora is also valued for its fragrant flowers and is sometimes cultivated for its cut flowers and medicinal properties.
