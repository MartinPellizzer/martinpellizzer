Ranunculus tschuktschorum

Ranunculus tschuktschorum, commonly known as the Tschuktschorum buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in early summer and are pollinated by insects such as bees and flies.

Ranunculus tschuktschorum is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
