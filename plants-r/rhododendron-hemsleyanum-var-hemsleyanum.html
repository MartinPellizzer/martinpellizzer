Rhododendron hemsleyanum var. hemsleyanum

Rhododendron hemsleyanum var. hemsleyanum, commonly known as the Hemsley's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.

The flowers bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape during that season.
