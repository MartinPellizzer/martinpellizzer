Ranunculus bulbosus var. corsicus

Ranunculus bulbosus var. corsicus, commonly known as Corsican buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, and it has a distinctive, hairy stem. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough texture and a waxy coating.

It is often found in meadows, along streambanks, and in woodland edges.
