Rhapis grossefibrosa

Rhapis grossefibrosa, commonly known as the giant bamboo palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange-red fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
