Ranunculus peltatus var. septentrionalis

Ranunculus peltatus var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern peltate buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to northern regions of North America.

It features distinctive palmate leaves with a hairy underside and bright yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are short-lived but attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
