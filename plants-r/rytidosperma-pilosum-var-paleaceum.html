Rytidosperma pilosum var. paleaceum

Rytidosperma pilosum var. paleaceum, commonly known as the paleaceum grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
