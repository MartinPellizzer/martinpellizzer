Rapatea scabra

Rapatea scabra, commonly known as the rough rapatea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae, which includes plants such as philodendrons and peace lilies. The plant is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Rapatea scabra is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
