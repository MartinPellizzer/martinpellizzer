Ranunculus pulcher

Ranunculus pulcher, commonly known as the beautiful buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting central blotch. The plant has trifoliate leaves with finely divided lobes, and it prefers well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It is typically found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cool, high-altitude environments.

Ranunculus pulcher is a perennial species that blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
