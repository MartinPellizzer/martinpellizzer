Rubus virgultorum var. fasciculatus

Rubus virgultorum var. fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dense clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in local foraging and culinary traditions.
