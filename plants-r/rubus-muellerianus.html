Rubus muellerianus

Rubus muellerianus, commonly known as the Mueller's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local wildlife support.
