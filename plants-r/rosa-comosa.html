Rosa comosa

Rosa comosa, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species of flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with each leaflet being elliptical and slightly serrated.

Rosa comosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
