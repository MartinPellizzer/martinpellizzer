Rubus strigosus var. acalyphaceus

Rubus strigosus var. acalyphaceus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils, and is often found in the eastern United States.

Its botanical features make it identifiable by its bristly appearance and distinctive fruit structure.
