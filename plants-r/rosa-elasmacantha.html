Rosa elasmacantha

Rosa elasmacantha, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and is known for its aromatic leaves and showy blooms. Rosa elasmacantha is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable plant in both horticulture and ecological studies.
