Rosa versicolor

Rosa versicolor, commonly known as the variable rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its diverse range of flower colors, which can vary from white to pink and red, depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are usually fragrant and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rosa versicolor is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
