Rubus tereticaulis subsp. curtiglandulosus

Rubus tereticaulis subsp.

curtiglandulosus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and scrub habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
