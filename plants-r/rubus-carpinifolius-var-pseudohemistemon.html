Rubus carpinifolius var. pseudohemistemon

Rubus carpinifolius var. pseudohemistemon, commonly known as the false hemistemon blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets, often with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by edible, dark purple berries in the summer. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The species is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.
