Rosa canina var. nucis

Rosa canina var. nucis, commonly known as the common dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Rosa canina var. nucis is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
