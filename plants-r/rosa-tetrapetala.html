Rosa tetrapetala

Rosa tetrapetala, commonly known as the four-petaled rose, is a species native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring multiple stems with thorny branches. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pink, with four petals, which is a distinguishing characteristic. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Rosa tetrapetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
