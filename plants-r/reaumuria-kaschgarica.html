Reaumuria kaschgarica

Reaumuria kaschgarica, commonly known as the Caspian tamarisk, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the extreme climates of its native region.
