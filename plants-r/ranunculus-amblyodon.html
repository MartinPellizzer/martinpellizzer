Ranunculus amblyodon

Ranunculus amblyodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually heart-shaped with a serrated edge. Its flowers are showy, with five to seven yellow petals and numerous stamens, often appearing in early spring.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its toxic properties due to the presence of protoanemonin.
