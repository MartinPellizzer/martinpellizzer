Rapanea arborea

Rapanea arborea, commonly known as the evergreen tree or mountain pepper, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue berries. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a strong, aromatic scent.

Rapanea arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions for its medicinal properties.
