Ranunculus flagelliformis var. erectus

Ranunculus flagelliformis var. erectus, commonly known as the erect buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with prominent white veins. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is notable for its erect growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
