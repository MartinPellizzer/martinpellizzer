Rubus innoxius

Rubus innoxius, commonly known as the poison ivy vine, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of North America and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small white flowers. The plant produces red, hairy fruits that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It is known for its toxic properties, as its sap can cause skin irritation in humans.

Despite its harmful effects, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
