Rosa leptophylla

Rosa leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaved rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries such as Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Rosa leptophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
