Rubus corylifolius var. tenuifolius

Rubus corylifolius var. tenuifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry.

It is a shrub native to East Asia, characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, thorned stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupes, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
