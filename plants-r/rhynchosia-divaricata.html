Rhynchosia divaricata

Rhynchosia divaricata, commonly known as the variable spurred cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its seed pods contain edible legumes, making it a valuable crop in some agricultural systems.

Rhynchosia divaricata is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and climates.
