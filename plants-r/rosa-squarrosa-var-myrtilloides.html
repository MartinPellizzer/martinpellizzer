Rosa squarrosa var. myrtilloides

Rosa squarrosa var. myrtilloides, commonly known as the purple sandalwood rose, is a compact shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has silvery-white, fuzzy undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
