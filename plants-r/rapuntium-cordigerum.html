Rapuntium cordigerum

Rapuntium cordigerum, commonly known as the heartleaf cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, calcareous soils. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, similar to that of watercress.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
