Ranunculus perpusillus

Ranunculus perpusillus, commonly known as the tiny buttercup, is a small herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers with five petals. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often colorful flowers.

Due to its small size and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
