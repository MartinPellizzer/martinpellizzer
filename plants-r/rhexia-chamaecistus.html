Rhexia chamaecistus

Rhexia chamaecistus, commonly known as the creeping rhexia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with slender, hairy stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Rhexia chamaecistus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
