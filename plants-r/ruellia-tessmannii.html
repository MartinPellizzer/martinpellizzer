Ruellia tessmannii

Ruellia tessmannii, commonly known as the Mexican bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple tubular flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are square in cross-section, a common trait in the Acanthaceae family.

Ruellia tessmannii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
