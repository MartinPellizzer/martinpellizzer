Rupicapnos africana var. hispanica

Rupicapnos africana var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
