Rhamnus filiformis

Rhamnus filiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
