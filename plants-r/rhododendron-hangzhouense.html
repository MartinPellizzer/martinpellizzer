Rhododendron hangzhouense

Rhododendron hangzhouense, commonly known as the Hangzhou rhododendron, is a species native to eastern China, particularly in Zhejiang Province.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shady, moist environments. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of rhododendron diversity in East Asia.
