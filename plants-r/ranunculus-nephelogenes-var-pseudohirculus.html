Ranunculus nephelogenes var. pseudohirculus

Ranunculus nephelogenes var. pseudohirculus, commonly known as the clouded buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces a basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
