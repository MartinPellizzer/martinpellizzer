Rhynchosia spectabilis

Rhynchosia spectabilis, commonly known as the showy cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, butterfly-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

Rhynchosia spectabilis thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
