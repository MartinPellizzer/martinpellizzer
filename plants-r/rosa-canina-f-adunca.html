Rosa canina f. adunca

Rosa canina f. adunca, commonly known as the shrubby dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit.

It features pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red hips that are rich in vitamin C. The plant is typically found in woodland edges and hedgerows, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

This form is notable for its hardy nature and ornamental value in gardens.
