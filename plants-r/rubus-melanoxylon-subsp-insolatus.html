Rubus melanoxylon subsp. insolatus

Rubus melanoxylon subsp.

insolatus, commonly known as the blackberry bush, is a species of bramble native to parts of southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This subspecies is often found in woodland and scrub habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
