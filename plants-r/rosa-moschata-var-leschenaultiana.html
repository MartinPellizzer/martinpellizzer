Rosa moschata var. leschenaultiana

Rosa moschata var. leschenaultiana, commonly known as the Leschenault's musk rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven oval leaflets, and its stems are covered in thorns. It produces small, red hips that persist into winter, containing seeds used in traditional medicine.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in perfumery.
