Rhaphiolepis kerrii

Rhaphiolepis kerrii, commonly known as the Chinese firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of bright orange-red berries in the fall. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

The berries are toxic to humans and animals, so caution is advised when handling or planting this species.
