Rhamnus pentapetala

Rhamnus pentapetala, commonly known as the five-petaled buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by black, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
