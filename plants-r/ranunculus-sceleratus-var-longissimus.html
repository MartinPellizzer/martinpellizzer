Ranunculus sceleratus var. longissimus

Ranunculus sceleratus var. longissimus, commonly known as the long-stemmed buttercup, is a variant of the buttercup species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and delicate, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
