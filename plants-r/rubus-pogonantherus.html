Rubus pogonantherus

Rubus pogonantherus, commonly known as the hairy bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas and along hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
