Rinorea ilicifolia var. khutuensis

Rinorea ilicifolia var. khutuensis, commonly known as the Khutu tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The leaves and fruits of this plant are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
