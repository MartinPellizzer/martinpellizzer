Rubus plexus

Rubus plexus, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry bush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in a variety of soil types. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

Rubus plexus is an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
