Remusatia pumila

Remusatia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features a short rhizome with fibrous roots and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture, making it an attractive ornamental plant.

Remusatia pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible tubers, though they are less commonly consumed than those of its larger relatives.
