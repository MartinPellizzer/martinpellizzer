Rubus hedycarpus var. virescens

Rubus hedycarpus var. virescens, commonly known as the green-fruited blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color, often with a greenish tinge. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is often found in woodland edges and moist, shaded areas.
