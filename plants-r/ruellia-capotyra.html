Ruellia capotyra

Ruellia capotyra, commonly known as the purple ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.

Ruellia capotyra thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
