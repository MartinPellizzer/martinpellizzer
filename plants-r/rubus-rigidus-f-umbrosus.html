Rubus rigidus f. umbrosus

Rubus rigidus f. umbrosus, commonly known as the dark raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, often hairy leaves.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically a deep red or black in color, which are edible but have a tart flavor. This plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in woodlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms in the early summer months.

The plant is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional herbal remedies.
