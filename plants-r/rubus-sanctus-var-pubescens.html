Rubus sanctus var. pubescens

Rubus sanctus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy evergreen raspberry, is a shrub native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and a hairy texture, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
