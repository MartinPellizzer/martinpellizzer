Rubus × bicolorifolius

Rubus × bicolorifolius, commonly known as the bicolor leaf raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its unique foliage and fruiting habits.

It exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species, typically featuring leaves with alternating shades of green and silver or white, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is a brambles shrub, often found in temperate regions, and produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms in early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens and horticultural settings.
