Rupicapnos numidica var. major

Rupicapnos numidica var. major, commonly known as the Algerian rock rose, is a shrub native to North Africa.

It features woody stems covered in dense, glandular hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bright pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
