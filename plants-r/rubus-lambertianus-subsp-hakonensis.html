Rubus lambertianus subsp. hakonensis

Rubus lambertianus subsp.

hakonensis, commonly known as Hakon's blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
