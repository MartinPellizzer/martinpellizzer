Rosa peyronii

Rosa peyronii, commonly known as the Peyronie's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or red in color, with five to seven petals. Its leaves are dark green, oval-shaped, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa peyronii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
