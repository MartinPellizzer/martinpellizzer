Rubus gymnocarpus

Rubus gymnocarpus, commonly known as the smooth-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
