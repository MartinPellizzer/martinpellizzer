Rebutia krugerae

Rebutia krugerae, commonly known as the Kruger's euphorbia, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Rebutia krugerae is also noted for its slow growth and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
