Ranunculus muricatus var. marginatus

Ranunculus muricatus var. marginatus, commonly known as the hairy water crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist or aquatic environments such as marshes, ponds, and stream sides. Its leaves are finely divided and have a hairy texture, which gives it its common name.

The species is often found in shallow water or along the edges of wetlands, where it thrives in nutrient-rich soils.
