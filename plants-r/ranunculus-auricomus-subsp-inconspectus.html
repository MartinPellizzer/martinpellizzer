Ranunculus auricomus subsp. inconspectus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

inconspectus, commonly known as the yellow pond-lily, is a aquatic plant native to North America. It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy coating.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom from early summer to fall. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Its leaves and flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant appearance.
