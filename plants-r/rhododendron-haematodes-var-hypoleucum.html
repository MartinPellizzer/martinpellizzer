Rhododendron haematodes var. hypoleucum

Rhododendron haematodes var. hypoleucum, commonly known as the white-veined rhododendron, is a rare and striking plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are narrowly elliptical with prominent white veins, creating a striking contrast against the dark green background. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.
