Rosa afzeliana var. slesvicensis

Rosa afzeliana var. slesvicensis, commonly known as the Slesvian rose, is a shrub native to parts of Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 5 to 7 oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces red-orange hips that contain seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
