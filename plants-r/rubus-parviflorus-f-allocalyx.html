Rubus parviflorus f. allocalyx

Rubus parviflorus f. allocalyx, commonly known as the small-flowered blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate white or pale pink flowers and dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

This form is notable for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
