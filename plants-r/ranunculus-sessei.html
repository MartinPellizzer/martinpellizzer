Ranunculus sessei

Ranunculus sessei, commonly known as the Sierra Valley buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, and its leaves are palmately divided with a distinctive lobed shape. It produces small, green, berry-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Ranunculus sessei is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in high-elevation environments.
