Ranunculus nivalis var. gracilis

Ranunculus nivalis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender snow buttercup, is a delicate alpine plant found in high-altitude regions.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers with five petals and a yellow center. The leaves are finely divided and appear in a rosette pattern, adapted to withstand cold climates. This variant is closely related to other members of the Ranunculaceae family, sharing similar botanical traits.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous areas across Europe and Asia.
