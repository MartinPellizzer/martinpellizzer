Rhynchosia monophylla

Rhynchosia monophylla, commonly known as the one-leaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive single, large leaf that is typically heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is often grown for its nutritious seeds, which are used in various culinary applications.

Rhynchosia monophylla is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural systems.
