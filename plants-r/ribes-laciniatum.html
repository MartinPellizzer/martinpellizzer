Ribes laciniatum

Ribes laciniatum, commonly known as the cutleaf gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible and often used in jams and preserves.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
