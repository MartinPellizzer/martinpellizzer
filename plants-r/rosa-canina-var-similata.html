Rosa canina var. similata

Rosa canina var. similata, commonly known as the common dog rose variant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features thorny stems and pinnate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer.

Its hips, which are the fruit, are orange-red and contain a high concentration of vitamin C. This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
