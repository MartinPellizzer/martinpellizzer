Rhododendron virgatum var. oleifolium

Rhododendron virgatum var. oleifolium, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, broad leaves that are often aromatic and emit a subtle fragrance. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in early spring. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in temperate climates around the world.
