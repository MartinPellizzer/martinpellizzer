Rosa nemorum

Rosa nemorum, commonly known as the wood rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa nemorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
