Rosa communis var. calophylla

Rosa communis var. calophylla, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces fragrant, five-petaled blooms that typically appear in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged oppositely along the stems.

The Chinese rose is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
