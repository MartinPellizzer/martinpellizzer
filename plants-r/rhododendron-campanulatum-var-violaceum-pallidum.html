Rhododendron campanulatum var. violaceum-pallidum

Rhododendron campanulatum var. violaceum-pallidum, commonly known as the pale lilac bellflower rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale lavender to almost white, often with a slight violet tinge. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
