Rosa acicularis f. plena

Rosa acicularis f. plena, commonly known as the Double Prickly Rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa acicularis species, which is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

This variety is characterized by its dense, double-layered flowers that are typically pink to red in color, with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves, typical of the Rosa genus. It is a compact shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and resilience.
