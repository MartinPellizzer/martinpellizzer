Racinaea crispa

Racinaea crispa, commonly known as the crinkled racinaea, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy leaves that are deeply lobed and have a crinkled appearance, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, Racinaea crispa is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
