Rosa × sabinii var. robertsonii

Rosa × sabinii var. robertsonii, commonly known as the Robertson's rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a desirable ornamental plant in horticultural settings.
