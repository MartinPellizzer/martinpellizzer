Rubus tomentosus var. amoenus

Rubus tomentosus var. amoenus, commonly known as the sweet thimbleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color and have a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and a dense covering of fine, white hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The flowers are white or pale pink and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
