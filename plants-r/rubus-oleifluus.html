Rubus oleifluus

Rubus oleifluus, commonly known as the fragrant raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It is valued for its aromatic foliage and berries, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
