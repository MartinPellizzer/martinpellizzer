Ranunculus arizonicus var. chihuahuanus

Ranunculus arizonicus var. chihuahuanus, commonly known as the Chihuahuan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Chihuahuan Desert in North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive black center and deeply lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in full sun and has a short growing season.

Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
