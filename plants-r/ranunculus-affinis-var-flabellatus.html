Ranunculus affinis var. flabellatus

Ranunculus affinis var. flabellatus, commonly known as the flabellate buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
