Rubus rhamnifolius var. pseudodumosus

Rubus rhamnifolius var. pseudodumosus, commonly known as the denseberry or false bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or black in color. It is characterized by its dense growth habit and thorny stems, which help in its identification.

The botanical features of this variety make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
