Rudgea francavillana

Rudgea francavillana, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid vine, is a climbing plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant has elongated, vine-like stems that can climb and entangle other vegetation. Its leaves are typically elongated and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface.

Rudgea francavillana thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
