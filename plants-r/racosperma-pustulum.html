Racosperma pustulum

Racosperma pustulum, commonly known as the rough globemallow, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in globular inflorescences, giving it its common name. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as plains and woodlands.

Racosperma pustulum is adapted to arid conditions and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
