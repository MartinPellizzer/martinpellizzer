Rubus sprengelii var. ferox

Rubus sprengelii var. ferox, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible, though some varieties may have a more robust or bitter flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
