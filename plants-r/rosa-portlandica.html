Rosa portlandica

Rosa portlandica, commonly known as the Portland rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the British Isles and is characterized by its upright growth habit and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically produces showy, single or semi-double blooms in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs.

Rosa portlandica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance.
