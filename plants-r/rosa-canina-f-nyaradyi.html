Rosa canina f. nyaradyi

Rosa canina f. nyaradyi, commonly known as the Hungarian dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and is native to Central Europe. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and pinnate leaves.

It produces red, hairy hips that are rich in vitamin C. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
