Rosa lutetiana var. senticosa

Rosa lutetiana var. senticosa, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, fragrant pink to white flowers, and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces oval, red-brown fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in hedging and as a source of medicinal compounds.
