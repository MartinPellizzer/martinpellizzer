Ranunculus ruahinicus

Ranunculus ruahinicus, commonly known as the Ruahine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy yellow flowers with five petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps retain moisture.

Ranunculus ruahinicus is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often striking floral displays.
