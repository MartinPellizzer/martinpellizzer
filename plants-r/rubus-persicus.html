Rubus persicus

Rubus persicus, commonly known as the Persian raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East, where it thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
