Rosa montana f. depauperata

Rosa montana f. depauperata, commonly known as the alpine rose or mountain rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and red, typically in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves that are densely arranged along its stems. It is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
