Rubus podophyllos var. botryanthus

Rubus podophyllos var. botryanthus, commonly known as the snowberry rose, is a variant of the rubus genus that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a shrub with compound leaves, typically having five to seven leaflets, and produces small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant is notable for its clusters of white, berry-like fruits that resemble snowballs, hence its common name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
