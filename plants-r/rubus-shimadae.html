Rubus shimadae

Rubus shimadae, commonly known as Shimada's raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

Due to its limited distribution and rarity, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
