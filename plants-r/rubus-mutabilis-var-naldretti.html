Rubus mutabilis var. naldretti

Rubus mutabilis var. naldretti, commonly known as the Naldrett's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black fruits, which are typically small and have a firm, tart flavor. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and grows as a shrub with thorny stems. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued both for its ornamental and culinary potential.
