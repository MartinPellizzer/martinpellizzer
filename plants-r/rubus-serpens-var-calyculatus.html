Rubus serpens var. calyculatus

Rubus serpens var. calyculatus, commonly known as the calyculate blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems as a food source for various wildlife species.
