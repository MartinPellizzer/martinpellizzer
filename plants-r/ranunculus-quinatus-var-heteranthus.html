Ranunculus quinatus var. heteranthus

Ranunculus quinatus var. heteranthus, commonly known as the heteranthus buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five sepals and petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest clearings. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

This variant is noted for its distinct floral morphology, which includes differences in the size and shape of its petals and sepals.
