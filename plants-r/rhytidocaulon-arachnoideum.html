Rhytidocaulon arachnoideum

Rhytidocaulon arachnoideum, commonly known as the spider-tail cactus, is a unique succulent native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in a dense, woolly layer of white hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Rhytidocaulon arachnoideum is well adapted to dry conditions and requires minimal water once established.
