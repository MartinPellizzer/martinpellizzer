Rosa × centifolia plicata

Rosa × centifolia plicata, commonly known as the cabbage rose, is a hybrid rose species prized for its fragrant, double blooms.

It is characterized by its tightly packed, layered petals that resemble cabbage leaves, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

This cultivar is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and for its historical use in perfumery.
