Rosa dichrocarpa

Rosa dichrocarpa, commonly known as the two-colored rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink and white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in thorns. Rosa dichrocarpa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
