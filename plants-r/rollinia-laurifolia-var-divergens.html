Rollinia laurifolia var. divergens

Rollinia laurifolia var. divergens, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian hibiscus, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
