Racosperma craspedocarpum

Racosperma craspedocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a slender, curved pod that contains several seeds.

Racosperma craspedocarpum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native vegetation.
