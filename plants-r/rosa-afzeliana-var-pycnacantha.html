Rosa afzeliana var. pycnacantha

Rosa afzeliana var. pycnacantha, commonly known as the dense thorned rose, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant produces oval, yellowish fruits known as hips, which are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it an important species in its native habitat.
