Rosa brunonii

Rosa brunonii, commonly known as the Brunon's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces red-orange hips that contain seeds.

Rosa brunonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
