Rumex fischeri

Rumex fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's sorrel, is a flowering plant in the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves with a reddish tint and a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense spikes. It is native to North America and thrives in moist, acidic soils.

Rumex fischeri is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other wet habitats.
