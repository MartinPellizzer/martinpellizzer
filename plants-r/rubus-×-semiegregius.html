Rubus × semiegregius

Rubus × semiegregius, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
