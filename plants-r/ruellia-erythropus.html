Ruellia erythropus

Ruellia erythropus, commonly known as the red-leaved ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple foliage, which sets it apart from many other ruellia species. The plant produces showy blue or purple tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Ruellia erythropus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
