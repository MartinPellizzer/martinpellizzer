Rosa tomentosa subvar. typica

Rosa tomentosa subvar. typica, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with thorny stems and is often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are typically ovate with a grayish-white underside, and it produces rose hips that are edible.

This variety is well-suited to dry, well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
