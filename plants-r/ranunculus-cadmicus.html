Ranunculus cadmicus

Ranunculus cadmicus, commonly known as the cadmium yellow buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, often toxic, flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow petals and is typically found in moist, calcareous soils. It blooms in early spring and is known for its distinctive, palmate leaves.

Despite its beauty, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
