Rubus borderi

Rubus borderi, commonly known as the Border blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Rubus borderi is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in local ecosystems.
