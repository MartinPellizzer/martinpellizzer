Rosa arvensis f. bosniaca

Rosa arvensis f. bosniaca, commonly known as the Bosnian field rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in summer. It is native to Central and Southeast Europe, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
