Rubus macrostachys subsp. schlickumii

Rubus macrostachys subsp.

schlickumii, commonly known as the Schlickum's blackberry, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
