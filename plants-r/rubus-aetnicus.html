Rubus aetnicus

Rubus aetnicus, commonly known as the Ethiopian raspberry, is a shrub native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dense clusters of small, red berries. The plant typically grows in rocky and semi-arid environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisine and herbal medicine.
