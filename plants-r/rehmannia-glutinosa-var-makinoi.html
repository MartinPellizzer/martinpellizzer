Rehmannia glutinosa var. makinoi

Rehmannia glutinosa var. makinoi, commonly known as the Japanese rehmannia, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features fleshy, elongated roots that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, often pink or reddish in color.
