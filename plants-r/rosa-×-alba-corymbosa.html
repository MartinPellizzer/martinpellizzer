Rosa × alba corymbosa

Rosa × alba corymbosa, commonly known as the white shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces hips that are often used in herbal remedies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
