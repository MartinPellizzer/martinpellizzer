Rubus leptaleus

Rubus leptaleus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, which support its climbing and spreading nature. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
