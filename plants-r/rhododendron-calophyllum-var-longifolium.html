Rhododendron calophyllum var. longifolium

Rhododendron calophyllum var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically elongated and leathery, providing a dense canopy that supports a variety of epiphytic plants. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and are often fragrant.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
