Rubus pyramidalis var. eifeliensis

Rubus pyramidalis var. eifeliensis, commonly known as the Eifel raspberry, is a shrub native to Central Europe.

It produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. The plant has thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
