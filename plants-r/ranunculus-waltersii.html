Ranunculus waltersii

Ranunculus waltersii, commonly known as the Walter's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and grows in dry, rocky habitats. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Due to its limited range, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in many areas.
