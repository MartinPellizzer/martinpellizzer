Rosa canina f. malmundariensis

Rosa canina f. malmundariensis, commonly known as the Malmstrom rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa canina species. This subspecies is native to southern Europe and is often found in woodland and scrub habitats.

It produces small, red-orange hips that are rich in vitamin C. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
