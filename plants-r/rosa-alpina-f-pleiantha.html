Rosa alpina f. pleiantha

Rosa alpina f. pleiantha, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in alpine environments.
