Ranunculus montanus subsp. geraniifolius

Ranunculus montanus subsp.

geraniifolius, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely divided, geraniifolius-like leaves that resemble those of the geranium. It thrives in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, often at high altitudes.

The plant is typically short-lived and has a low-growing habit, making it a distinctive feature of its habitat.
