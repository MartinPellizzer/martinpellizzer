Rubus lasiocarpus var. membranaceus

Rubus lasiocarpus var. membranaceus, commonly known as the membranous dewberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
