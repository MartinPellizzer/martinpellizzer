Ravenea rivularis

Ravenea rivularis, commonly known as the river ravenea, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of long, arching leaves that give it a graceful appearance. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences with small, fragrant white flowers, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
