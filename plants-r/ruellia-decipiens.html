Ruellia decipiens

Ruellia decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ruellia decipiens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
