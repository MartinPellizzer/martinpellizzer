Rosa alpina var. inermis

Rosa alpina var. inermis, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white or gray hairs. Its flowers are pink to white, with five petals and a hairy calyx, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a hardy perennial in colder climates.
