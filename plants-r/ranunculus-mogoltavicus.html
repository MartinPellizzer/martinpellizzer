Ranunculus mogoltavicus

Ranunculus mogoltavicus, commonly known as the Mongolian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often bright-colored flowers. The plant typically has palmate leaves and produces yellow or white petals, which are usually five in number. It thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
