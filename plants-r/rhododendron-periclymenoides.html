Rhododendron periclymenoides

Rhododendron periclymenoides, commonly known as the rosebay rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from pink to deep red, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has broad, leathery leaves that are dark green and often covered in a waxy cuticle. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron periclymenoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
