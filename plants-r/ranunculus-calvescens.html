Ranunculus calvescens

Ranunculus calvescens, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with numerous stamens and a central pistil. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture, and it produces oval, greenish-brown seed pods that split open to release seeds.

Ranunculus calvescens is an herbaceous perennial that blooms from spring to early summer.
