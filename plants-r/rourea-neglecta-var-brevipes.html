Rourea neglecta var. brevipes

Rourea neglecta var. brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf rourea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically glabrous and have a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are often followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high light exposure.

Due to its compact size and ornamental foliage, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
