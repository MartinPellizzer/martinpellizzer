Rubus adenophorus

Rubus adenophorus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
