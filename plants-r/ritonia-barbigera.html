Ritonia barbigera

Ritonia barbigera, commonly known as the hairy leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Ritonia barbigera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
