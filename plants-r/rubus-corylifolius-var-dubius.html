Rubus corylifolius var. dubius

Rubus corylifolius var. dubius, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to China.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits. Its botanical features include thorny stems and a complex flowering structure that supports its reproductive cycle.

The species is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional Chinese medicine.
