Rubus bifrons subsp. godronii

Rubus bifrons subsp.

godronii, commonly known as Godron's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
