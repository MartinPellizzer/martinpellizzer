Rosa klukii var. subbicolor

Rosa klukii var. subbicolor, commonly known as the Pink and White Climber Rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and large, semi-double flowers that display a striking two-toned coloration, with pink outer petals and white inner petals. The plant produces fragrant blooms in late spring to early summer, and its foliage is dark green with a slightly serrated leaf margin. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden settings.
