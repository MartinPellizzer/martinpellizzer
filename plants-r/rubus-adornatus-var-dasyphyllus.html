Rubus adornatus var. dasyphyllus

Rubus adornatus var. dasyphyllus, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries that are typically borne in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pink flowers in the spring. Its thorny stems are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its edible fruits.
