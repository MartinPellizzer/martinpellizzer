Rubus × obuncispinus

Rubus × obuncispinus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to western North America, particularly along the Pacific coast, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of small drupelets. It produces dark purple to black berries that are often used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic diversity and adaptability to various soil types.
