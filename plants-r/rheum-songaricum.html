Rheum songaricum

Rheum songaricum, commonly known as the Chinese rhubarb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants such as buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, and its reddish-orange flower clusters that appear in late spring. Rheum songaricum is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly the presence of anthraquinone compounds in its roots and rhizomes.

It is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its laxative and anti-inflammatory effects.
