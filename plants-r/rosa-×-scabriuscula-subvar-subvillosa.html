Rosa × scabriuscula subvar. subvillosa

Rosa × scabriuscula subvar. subvillosa, commonly known as the soft-haired shrub rose, is a hybrid species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its hardiness and adaptability. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered with fine, velvety hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is well-suited for cultivation in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8.
