Rejoua pacifica

Rejoua pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific reed or Pacific reedgrass, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in wetlands, estuaries, and along the shores of rivers and lakes. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense panicles. It plays an important role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Rejoua pacifica is adapted to saltwater and brackish environments, making it a valuable component of coastal ecosystems.
