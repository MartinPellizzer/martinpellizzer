Rhododendron lepidostylum

Rhododendron lepidostylum, commonly known as the silver-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that often has a silvery or whitish underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
