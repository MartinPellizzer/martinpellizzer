Rehmannia glutinosa var. hemsleyana

Rehmannia glutinosa var. hemsleyana, commonly known as the Chinese foxglove, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy roots that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its medicinal significance in herbal remedies.
