Rubus leptosepalus

Rubus leptosepalus, commonly known as the slender-sepal raspberry.

It is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its hairy stems and small, aggregated fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pale yellow flowers in the spring.

The fruit, though not widely cultivated, is edible and has a tart flavor.
