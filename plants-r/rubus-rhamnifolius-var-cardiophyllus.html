Rubus rhamnifolius var. cardiophyllus

Rubus rhamnifolius var. cardiophyllus, commonly known as the heart-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its foliage is typically covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variant is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its fruit yield.
