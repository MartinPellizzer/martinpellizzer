Rinorea convallarioides

Rinorea convallarioides, commonly known as the blue bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rinorea convallarioides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
