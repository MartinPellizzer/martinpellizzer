Rosa dilata

Rosa dilata, commonly known as the shrub rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. Rosa dilata is typically hardy in USDA zones 5 through 8 and thrives in well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
