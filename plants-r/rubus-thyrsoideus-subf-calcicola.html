Rubus thyrsoideus subf. calcicola

Rubus thyrsoideus subf.

calcicola, commonly known as the limestone raspberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color.

The plant thrives in calcareous soils, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it produces white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
