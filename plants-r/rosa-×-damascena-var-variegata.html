Rosa × damascena var. variegata

Rosa × damascena var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated damask rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. Its variegated foliage, with white or yellow markings, adds visual interest to gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a source of cut flowers.
