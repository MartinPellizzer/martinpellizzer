Rosa jundzilliana f. tolosana

Rosa jundzilliana f. tolosana, commonly known as the Tolosan rose, is a unique cultivar within the Rosa jundzilliana species.

It is characterized by its striking, deep pink to crimson flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a shrub with thorned stems and dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
