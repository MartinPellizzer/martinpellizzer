Ranunculus botschantzevii

Ranunculus botschantzevii, commonly known as the Botchanzev's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with lobed segments, and the flowers consist of numerous petal-like sepals.

It is a perennial species, often found in high-altitude meadows and slopes.
