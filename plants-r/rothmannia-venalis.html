Rothmannia venalis

Rothmannia venalis, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Rothmannia venalis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
