Rubus villicaulis subsp. eurypetalus

Rubus villicaulis subsp.

eurypetalus, commonly known as the broad-petaled blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with broad, spreading petals. The fruit is a dark purple to black berry, often used in jams and desserts.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruits.
