Rubus grandifolius var. dissimulatus

Rubus grandifolius var. dissimulatus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its large, dark green leaves and thorny stems, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible berries in late summer. Its fruit is often used in jams and desserts due to its tart flavor.

The species is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in moist, temperate climates.
