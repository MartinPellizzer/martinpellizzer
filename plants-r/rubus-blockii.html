Rubus blockii

Rubus blockii, commonly known as the New Zealand bramble, is a species of shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in forest margins and shrublands.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a key role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining biodiversity.
