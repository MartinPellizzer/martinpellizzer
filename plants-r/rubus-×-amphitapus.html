Rubus × amphitapus

Rubus × amphitapus, commonly known as the blackberry-boys or brambleberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus glaucus (the raspberry).

It is a thorny shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, characterized by its compound leaves and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications.

Its botanical features include a woody stem, persistent leaves, and a complex inflorescence structure typical of the Rubus genus.
