Rumex aquaticus

Rumex aquaticus, commonly known as water sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near streams, ponds, and marshes.

Its edible leaves have a tart flavor and have been used in traditional herbal remedies.
