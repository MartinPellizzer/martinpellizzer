Rubus moluccanus var. fordii

Rubus moluccanus var. fordii, commonly known as the Ford's blackberry, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
