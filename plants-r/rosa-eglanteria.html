Rosa eglanteria

Rosa eglanteria, commonly known as the dog rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces oval, yellowish fruits called rosehips, which are rich in vitamin C. Rosa eglanteria is often found in woodlands and scrublands, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant.
