Ruta griffithiana

Ruta griffithiana, commonly known as the Kashmir rue, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring aromatic, compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, which bloom in the spring. Its roots and leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Ruta griffithiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
