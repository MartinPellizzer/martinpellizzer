Rubus hebecaulis microgene politulus

Rubus hebecaulis microgene politulus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, dark purple to black fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor and are used in jams and pies.
