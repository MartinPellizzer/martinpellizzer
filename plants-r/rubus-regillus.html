Rubus regillus

Rubus regillus, commonly known as the creeping blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other members of the Rubus genus.
