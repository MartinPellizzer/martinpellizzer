Ranunculus bamianicus

Ranunculus bamianicus, commonly known as the Bamian Valley buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and delicate, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils at high elevations. It blooms in early summer, producing showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
