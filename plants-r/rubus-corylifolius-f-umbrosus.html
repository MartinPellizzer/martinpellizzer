Rubus corylifolius f. umbrosus

Rubus corylifolius f. umbrosus, commonly known as the dark-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its dark green foliage.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of East Asia, particularly China. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a distinctive, slightly serrated margin.

This form is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
