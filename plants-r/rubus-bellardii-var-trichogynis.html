Rubus bellardii var. trichogynis

Rubus bellardii var. trichogynis, commonly known as the Trichogynis blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant member of the Rubus genus.
