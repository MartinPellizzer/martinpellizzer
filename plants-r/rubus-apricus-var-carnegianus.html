Rubus apricus var. carnegianus

Rubus apricus var. carnegianus, commonly known as the Carnegian blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically larger and more flavorful than those of other blackberry varieties. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is often cultivated for its culinary uses and ornamental value in gardens.
