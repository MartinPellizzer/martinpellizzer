Rubus × crux

Rubus × crux, commonly known as the blackberry or raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus idaeus (raspberry), resulting in a plant with unique fruit characteristics. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its stems are covered in stinging hairs, and its leaves are compound with serrated edges.

The fruit, which can be either a blackberry or raspberry depending on the parent species, is aggregate, containing multiple small drupelets.
