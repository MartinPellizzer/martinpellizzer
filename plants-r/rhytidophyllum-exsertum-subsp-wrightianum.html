Rhytidophyllum exsertum subsp. wrightianum

Rhytidophyllum exsertum subsp.

wrightianum, commonly known as Wright's desert lily, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa. It features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often opening at night. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

This subspecies is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient and unique member of the Aizoaceae family.
