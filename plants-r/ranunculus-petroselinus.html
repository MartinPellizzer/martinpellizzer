Ranunculus petroselinus

Ranunculus petroselinus, commonly known as the mouse-ear crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure, and it produces small, aggregate fruits.

Ranunculus petroselinus is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its attractive spring blooms.
