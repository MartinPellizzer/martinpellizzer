Rubus proximus

Rubus proximus, commonly known as the common blackberry shrub, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces edible berries that ripen to a deep black color, typically in late summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Rubus proximus is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.
