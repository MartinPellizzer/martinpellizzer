Rubus silesiacus var. humiliserratus

Rubus silesiacus var. humiliserratus, commonly known as the Silesian bramble, is a shrub native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
