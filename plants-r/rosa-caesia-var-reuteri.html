Rosa caesia var. reuteri

Rosa caesia var. reuteri, commonly known as the pink sandalwood rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep crimson, typically blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are covered in thorny buds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural range, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
