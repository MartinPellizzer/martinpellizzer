Rubus psammogenes

Rubus psammogenes, commonly known as the sand raspberry, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, aggregate fruits that resemble raspberries. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a rough, hairy texture.

While not widely cultivated, it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
