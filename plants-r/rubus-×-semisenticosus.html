Rubus × semisenticosus

Rubus × semisenticosus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small white or pale yellow flowers in the spring.

This species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits, which are often used in jams and desserts.
