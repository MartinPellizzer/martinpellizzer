Rubus obtruncatus subsp. erythranthemus

Rubus obtruncatus subsp.

erythranthemus, commonly known as the red-flowered blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
