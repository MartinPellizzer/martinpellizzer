Rosa alpina var. genuina

Rosa alpina var. genuina, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white to silver-gray hairs. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in alpine gardens.
