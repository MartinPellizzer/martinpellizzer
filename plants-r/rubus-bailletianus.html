Rubus bailletianus

Rubus bailletianus, commonly known as the Bailletian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and less commonly cultivated species.
