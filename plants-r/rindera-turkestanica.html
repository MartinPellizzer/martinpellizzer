Rindera turkestanica

Rindera turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette, aiding in water retention.

Rindera turkestanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
