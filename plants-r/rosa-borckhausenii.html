Rosa borckhausenii

Rosa borckhausenii, commonly known as the Chilean rose, is a species of shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers typically bloom in late winter to early spring. Rosa borckhausenii is well adapted to coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
