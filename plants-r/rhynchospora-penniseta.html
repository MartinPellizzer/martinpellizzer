Rhynchospora penniseta

Rhynchospora penniseta, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a reddish or purple hue at the base.

Rhynchospora penniseta plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
