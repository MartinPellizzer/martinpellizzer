Rosa longicuspis

Rosa longicuspis, commonly known as the long-thorned rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces oval, green leaves with serrated edges, and its fruits are red, hairy rosehips that contain seeds. Rosa longicuspis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
