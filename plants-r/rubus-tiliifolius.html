Rubus tiliifolius

Rubus tiliifolius, commonly known as the European blackberry or sweet blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

Rubus tiliifolius is valued for its edible berries and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
