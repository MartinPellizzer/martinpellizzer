Rubus × mollicaulis

Rubus × mollicaulis, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rubus genus in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as berries, which are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and naturalized in various regions.
