Ruta glabra

Ruta glabra, commonly known as common rue, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pinnate leaves and small yellowish-green flowers. The plant contains compounds such as rutin and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like citrus and rue.
