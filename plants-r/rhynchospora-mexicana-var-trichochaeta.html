Rhynchospora mexicana var. trichochaeta

Rhynchospora mexicana var. trichochaeta, commonly known as the hairy thread-plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The species is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered with fine, hairy structures, contributing to its common name.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
