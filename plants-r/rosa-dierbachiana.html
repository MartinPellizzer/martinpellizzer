Rosa dierbachiana

Rosa dierbachiana, commonly known as the Dierbach's rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces oval, red-orange fruits known as hips. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively resistant to pests and diseases.

Rosa dierbachiana prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
