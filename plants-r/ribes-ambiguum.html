Ribes ambiguum

Ribes ambiguum, commonly known as the red currant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the genus Ribes in the family Grossulariaceae, which includes other currant and gooseberry species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, though some varieties may have yellow or white berries. Ribes ambiguum is often cultivated for its edible berries and is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
