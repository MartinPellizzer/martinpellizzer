Rhododendron nortoniae

Rhododendron nortoniae, commonly known as the Nortonia rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
