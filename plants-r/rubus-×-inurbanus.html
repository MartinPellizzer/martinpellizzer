Rubus × inurbanus

Rubus × inurbanus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are commonly referred to as blackberries, and are known for their sweet-tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
