Ribes nigrum var. apiifolium

Ribes nigrum var. apiifolium, commonly known as the apiifolium gooseberry, is a variant of the black currant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, compound leaves that resemble those of the carrot plant, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and is also used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
