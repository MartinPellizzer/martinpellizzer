Rosa saepeflorens alba

Rosa saepeflorens alba, commonly known as the white frequent-flowering rose, is a botanical species characterized by its delicate, white petals and fragrant aroma.

It is native to specific regions in Asia and is known for its ability to bloom multiple times throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green foliage. Its flowers are generally small to medium in size and are often used in ornamental gardening.

Rosa saepeflorens alba requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight to thrive.
