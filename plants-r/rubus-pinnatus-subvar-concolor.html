Rubus pinnatus subvar. concolor

Rubus pinnatus subvar. concolor, commonly known as the smooth raspberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering stems emerging from the previous year's growth. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, bright red drupes that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated margins and a hairy texture.

It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and open fields, and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
