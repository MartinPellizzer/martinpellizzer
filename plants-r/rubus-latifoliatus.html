Rubus latifoliatus

Rubus latifoliatus, commonly known as broadleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to western North America.

It is characterized by its stout, thorny stems and large, trifoliate leaves with broad, glossy blades. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black berries. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, meadows, and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in regions with temperate climates.
