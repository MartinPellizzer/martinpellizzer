Ranunculus laeticolor

Ranunculus laeticolor, commonly known as the white buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Afghanistan. The plant features showy, white or pale yellow flowers with five to seven petals, and it has a hairy stem and trifoliate leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its striking appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
