Roeslinia baccifera

Roeslinia baccifera, commonly known as the scarlet hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a yellow throat. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a smooth or slightly serrated edge.

Roeslinia baccifera is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
