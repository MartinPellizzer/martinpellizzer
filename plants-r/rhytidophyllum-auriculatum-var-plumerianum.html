Rhytidophyllum auriculatum var. plumerianum

Rhytidophyllum auriculatum var. plumerianum, commonly known as the plumeria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant has waxy, often fragrant leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dry seeds.
