Rubus × varians

Rubus × varians, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupelets, which collectively form a dark purple to black berry. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
