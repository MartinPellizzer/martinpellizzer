Ranunculus bikramii

Ranunculus bikramii, commonly known as the Himalayan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically has palmately divided leaves and produces yellow or white flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
