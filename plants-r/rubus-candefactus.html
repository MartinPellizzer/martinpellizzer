Rubus candefactus

Rubus candefactus, commonly known as the white-stemmed blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its slender, often pale-colored stems. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically less palatable to humans but serve as a food source for wildlife. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, wooded areas.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
