Rubus faberi

Rubus faberi, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
