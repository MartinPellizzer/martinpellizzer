Romulea vulgaris

Romulea vulgaris, commonly known as the common romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes irises and gladiolus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its blooming period typically occurs in spring, making it a notable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
