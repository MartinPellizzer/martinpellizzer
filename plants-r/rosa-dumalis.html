Rosa dumalis

Rosa dumalis, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a serrated edge, while its stems are covered in thorns.

Rosa dumalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
