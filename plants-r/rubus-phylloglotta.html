Rubus phylloglotta

Rubus phylloglotta, commonly known as the red raspberry.

It is a species of brambles native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous drupelets, which are typically bright red when ripe. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Rubus phylloglotta is often found in woodlands and along the edges of streams.
