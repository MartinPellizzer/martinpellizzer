Rubus macrostachys

Rubus macrostachys, commonly known as the giant blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, thorny stems and clusters of dark purple to black berries. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupelets. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a rough, hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
