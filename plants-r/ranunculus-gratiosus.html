Ranunculus gratiosus

Ranunculus gratiosus, commonly known as the graceful buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, with a distinctive yellow center. The plant has trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ranunculus gratiosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
