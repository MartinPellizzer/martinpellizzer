Rubus leucodermis var. trinitatis

Rubus leucodermis var. trinitatis, commonly known as the Trinidad raspberry, is a shrub native to the Caribbean island of Trinidad.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in local communities.
