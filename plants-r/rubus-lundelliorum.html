Rubus lundelliorum

Rubus lundelliorum, commonly known as the Lundell's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
