Rubus mucronifer subsp. cenomanensis

Rubus mucronifer subsp.

cenomanensis, commonly known as the Cenomanian raspberry, is a shrub native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated margins, and the flowers are pink to white in color. This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.

It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.
