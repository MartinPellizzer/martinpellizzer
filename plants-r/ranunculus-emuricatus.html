Ranunculus emuricatus

Ranunculus emuricatus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in early summer.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
