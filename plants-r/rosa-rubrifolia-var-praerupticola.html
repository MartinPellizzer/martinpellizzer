Rosa rubrifolia var. praerupticola

Rosa rubrifolia var. praerupticola, commonly known as the red-leaf rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage, which contrasts strikingly with its delicate white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces fragrant blooms in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
