Rhamnus schneideri

Rhamnus schneideri, commonly known as Schneiders' buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for some animals but not for humans. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Rhamnus schneideri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
