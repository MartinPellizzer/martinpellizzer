Rosa obtusifolia var. esinensis

Rosa obtusifolia var. esinensis, commonly known as the Esin rose, is a shrub-like species native to the Caucasus region.

It features thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are compound, with rounded leaflets that are densely covered in fine hairs. This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and resilient growth habits.
