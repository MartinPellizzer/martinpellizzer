Rhamnus marahuacensis

Rhamnus marahuacensis, commonly known as the Mexican buckthorn, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, reddish berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in dry to moist soils and is often found in woodlands and scrub habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
