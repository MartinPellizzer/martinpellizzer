Rhytispermum tinctorium

Rhytispermum tinctorium, commonly known as safflower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow to red flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets. The plant produces a yellow dye from its seeds, which has been historically used in textile industries. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Rhytispermum tinctorium is also cultivated for its oil, which is used in cooking and cosmetics.
