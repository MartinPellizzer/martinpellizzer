Reichardia picroides var. cupaniana

Reichardia picroides var. cupaniana, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, with a central stem that produces yellow composite flowers. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
