Rourea lescrauwaetii var. tenuifoliolata

Rourea lescrauwaetii var. tenuifoliolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rourea, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often narrowly elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short peduncles. Its leaves are usually leathery and have a distinctive, finely toothed margin.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique foliage.
