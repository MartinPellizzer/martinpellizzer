Rubus gratifolius var. latifolius

Rubus gratifolius var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and edible berries.
