Rosa turcica

Rosa turcica, commonly known as the Turkish rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Balkans.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is evergreen in its native habitat, with dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the shrub throughout the year. Rosa turcica is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for temperate climates.
