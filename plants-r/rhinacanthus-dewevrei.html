Rhinacanthus dewevrei

Rhinacanthus dewevrei, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has a straight, often spiny trunk and produces woody pods containing flat, dark brown seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
