Rubus oklejewiczii

Rubus oklejewiczii, commonly known as the Polish blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Poland and parts of Ukraine.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring moist, well-drained conditions. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, which is a dark purple to black berry, is edible and often used in jams and desserts.
