Rapanea rotundifolia

Rapanea rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaved evergreen or African holly, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a member of the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue to black berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
