Ranunculus dolichanthus

Ranunculus dolichanthus, commonly known as the long-stemmed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive central pistil, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
