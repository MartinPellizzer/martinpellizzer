Rubus anomaloides

Rubus anomaloides, commonly known as the anomalous blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

Rubus anomaloides thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
