Roebuckiella halophila

Roebuckiella halophila, commonly known as the saltbush, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

The plant exhibits a shrub-like growth habit with small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss. It is native to coastal and arid regions where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

Roebuckiella halophila is valued for its ability to tolerate high salinity and is often used in phytoremediation efforts.
