Rosa moschata floresemipleno

Rosa moschata floresemipleno, commonly known as the musk rose, is a species of shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, double blooms that are typically pink or white in color. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. It has dark green, serrated leaves and a woody stem with thorns.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
