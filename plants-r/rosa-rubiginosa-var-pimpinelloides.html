Rosa rubiginosa var. pimpinelloides

Rosa rubiginosa var. pimpinelloides, commonly known as the alpine rose or pink rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, providing a drought-resistant structure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
