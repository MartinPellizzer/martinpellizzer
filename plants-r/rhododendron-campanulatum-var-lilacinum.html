Rhododendron campanulatum var. lilacinum

Rhododendron campanulatum var. lilacinum, commonly known as the lilac-flowered rhododendron, is a shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of lilac and purple, which bloom in early spring. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
