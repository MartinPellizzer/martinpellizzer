Rorippa × armoracioides var. brevistyla

Rorippa × armoracioides var. brevistyla, commonly known as the narrow-styled watercress, is a hybrid species within the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, alternate, and often submerged leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant produces small, white flowers with four petals, which are arranged in racemes along the stems. It thrives in moist or aquatic environments, such as marshes, ponds, and stream edges.

Its botanical features make it a common species in wetland ecosystems across Europe and parts of Asia.
