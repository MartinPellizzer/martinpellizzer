Ranunculus sericophyllus

Ranunculus sericophyllus, commonly known as the silky leaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
