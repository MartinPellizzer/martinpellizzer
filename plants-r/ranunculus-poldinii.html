Ranunculus poldinii

Ranunculus poldinii, commonly known as the Kashmiri buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and prefers cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are palmately divided and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are usually slender and erect.

Ranunculus poldinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming blooms.
