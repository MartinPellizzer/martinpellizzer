Ranunculus diodon

Ranunculus diodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals that are typically notched or lobed. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse floral structures and ecological importance.
