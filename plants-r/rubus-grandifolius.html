Rubus grandifolius

Rubus grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaf blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. These berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.

The plant thrives in woodland edges and moist soils, showcasing its adaptability to various ecological niches.
