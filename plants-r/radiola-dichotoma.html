Radiola dichotoma

Radiola dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched radiola, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are opposite and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster.

Radiola dichotoma is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
