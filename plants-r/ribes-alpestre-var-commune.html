Ribes alpestre var. commune

Ribes alpestre var. commune, commonly known as the alpine gooseberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible and often used in jams and preserves. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges and are covered in a fine, whitish down.

Ribes alpestre var. commune is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
