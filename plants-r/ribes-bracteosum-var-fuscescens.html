Ribes bracteosum var. fuscescens

Ribes bracteosum var. fuscescens, commonly known as the darkleaf gooseberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves that are often tinged with purple or bronze. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
