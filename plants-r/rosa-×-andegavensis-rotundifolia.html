Rosa × andegavensis rotundifolia

Rosa × andegavensis rotundifolia, commonly known as the Andegavensis rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rounded, often lobed leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has a bushy, upright habit. Its botanical features include thorny stems, pinnate leaves, and hips that persist into winter.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
