Rudbeckia californica var. glauca

Rudbeckia californica var. glauca, commonly known as the blue coneflower, is a perennial herb native to California.

It features striking blue to lavender flower heads with yellow centers, surrounded by pale green to blue-green bracts. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and slightly toothed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
