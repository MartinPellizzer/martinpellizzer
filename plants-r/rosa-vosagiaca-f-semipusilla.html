Rosa vosagiaca f. semipusilla

Rosa vosagiaca f. semipusilla, commonly known as the dwarf Rosa vosagiaca, is a compact shrub with small, rounded leaves and fragrant, pale pink flowers.

It is a naturally occurring form of Rosa vosagiaca, characterized by its smaller size and more delicate appearance compared to the typical species. This variety thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a subtle ornamental value to the landscape.

The plant is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
