Ranunculus altitorus

Ranunculus altitorus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and simple, trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has a short growing season and is often found at high altitudes.

Ranunculus altitorus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
