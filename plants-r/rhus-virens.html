Rhus virens

Rhus virens, commonly known as the green ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval or elliptic in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, fuzzy fruit that attracts wildlife.

Rhus virens is often found in moist woodland areas and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
