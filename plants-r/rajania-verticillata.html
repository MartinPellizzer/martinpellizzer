Rajania verticillata

Rajania verticillata, commonly known as the spiral lily, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem that bears whorled clusters of large, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The flowers are typically arranged in a spiral pattern, giving the plant its common name. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral around the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.
