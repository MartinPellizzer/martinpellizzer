Rubus obcuneatus

Rubus obcuneatus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a species of bramble belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, pinnately compound leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupes. The plant produces bright red berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Rubus obcuneatus is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its use in culinary applications.
