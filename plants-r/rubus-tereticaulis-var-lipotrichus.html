Rubus tereticaulis var. lipotrichus

Rubus tereticaulis var. lipotrichus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically eaten fresh or used in jams and preserves. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.

This variant is often found in open woodlands and along the edges of forests.
