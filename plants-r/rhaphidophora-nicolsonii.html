Rhaphidophora nicolsonii

Rhaphidophora nicolsonii, commonly known as the Nicolson's arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden beneath a leaf-like spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, fertile soil to flourish.

Rhaphidophora nicolsonii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
