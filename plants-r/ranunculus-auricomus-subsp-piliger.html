Ranunculus auricomus subsp. piliger

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

piliger, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to western North America. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, broad, leaf-like petal at the base.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided and often covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies piliger is distinguished by its more robust growth and slightly larger flowers compared to other subspecies of Ranunculus auricomus.
