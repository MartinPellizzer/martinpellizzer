Randia malleiflora

Randia malleiflora, commonly known as the swamp mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming during the wet season. Randia malleiflora thrives in moist, swampy habitats and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
