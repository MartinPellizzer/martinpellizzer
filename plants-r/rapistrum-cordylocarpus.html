Rapistrum cordylocarpus

Rapistrum cordylocarpus, commonly known as the sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the eastern Mediterranean.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its salty, windy habitat. Its yellow flowers are small and clustered in racemes, blooming from spring to early summer.
