Ranunculus aconitifolius var. intermedius

Ranunculus aconitifolius var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediateaconit leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, white hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific growing conditions to flourish.
