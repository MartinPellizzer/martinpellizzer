Ranunculus biolettii

Ranunculus biolettii, commonly known as the Sierra primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, often toxic species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest understories. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with five to eight petals and a prominent central stamen.

Ranunculus biolettii is characterized by its delicate foliage and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
