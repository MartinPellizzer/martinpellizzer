Rosa pomifera subvar. typica

Rosa pomifera subvar. typica, commonly known as the sea rose, is a unique species of rose that thrives in coastal environments.

It is characterized by its large, edible fruits that resemble small, fuzzy apples, which are often consumed by both humans and wildlife. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody stem that can grow up to several meters in height.

This subvariety is adapted to saline and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant found in regions with coastal climates.
