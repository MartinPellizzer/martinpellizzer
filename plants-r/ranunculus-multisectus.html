Ranunculus multisectus

Ranunculus multisectus, commonly known as the divided buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often accompanied by numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and delicate structure, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
