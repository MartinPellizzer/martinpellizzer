Racosperma varium var. parviflorum

Racosperma varium var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flower spikes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that attract various pollinators, including bees and birds. It is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
