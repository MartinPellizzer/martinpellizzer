Rubus praecox

Rubus praecox, commonly known as early blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces small, thorny stems and has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, dark purple to black berry that ripens in early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
