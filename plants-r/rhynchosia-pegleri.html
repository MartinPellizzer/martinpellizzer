Rhynchosia pegleri

Rhynchosia pegleri, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its distinctive, curved seed pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallows.

Rhynchosia pegleri is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
