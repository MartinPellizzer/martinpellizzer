Rosa rubrifolia var. glandulosa

Rosa rubrifolia var. glandulosa, commonly known as the red-leaved rose with glandular hairs, is a variant of the red-leaved rose characterized by its distinctive foliage.

The leaves are typically red or reddish-purple, with a dense covering of glandular hairs that give them a velvety texture. This variety is native to certain regions of Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually pink to red, adding to its ornamental value in gardens.

The plant is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its role in horticultural cultivation.
