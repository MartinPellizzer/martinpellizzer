Rhipsalidopsis rosea var. riograndensis

Rhipsalidopsis rosea var. riograndensis, commonly known as the Rio Grande rat-tail cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to Brazil.

It features short, cylindrical stems with spines and produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure. Its unique morphology includes a trailing growth habit and a dense covering of fine hairs.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental flowers and ease of care.
