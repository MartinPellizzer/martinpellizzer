Rhododendron renschianum

Rhododendron renschianum, commonly known as the Rensch's rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, broad leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
