Rosa cinnamomea var. foecundissima

Rosa cinnamomea var. foecundissima, commonly known as the Chinese tea rose, is a shrub-like plant native to China.

It features fragrant, single or semi-double flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in Chinese horticulture.
