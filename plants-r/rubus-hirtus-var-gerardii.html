Rubus hirtus var. gerardii

Rubus hirtus var. gerardii, commonly known as the prickly raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it an important part of the ecosystem, providing food for various wildlife species.
