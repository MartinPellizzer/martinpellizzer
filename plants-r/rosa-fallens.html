Rosa fallens

Rosa fallens, commonly known as the climbing rose, is a species native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in a bushy or climbing form, with dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Rosa fallens is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
