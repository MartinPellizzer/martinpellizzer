Ranunculus auricomus subsp. ostrobottnicus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

ostrobottnicus, commonly known as the Eastern yellow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing showy flowers that attract pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger flowers and more robust growth compared to other members of the species.
