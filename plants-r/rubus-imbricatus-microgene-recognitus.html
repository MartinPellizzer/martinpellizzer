Rubus imbricatus microgene recognitus

Rubus imbricatus microgene recognitus, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, biennial growth habit. The plant produces red or yellow berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the stems are covered in stiff, prickly hairs.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and streambanks.
