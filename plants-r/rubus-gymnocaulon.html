Rubus gymnocaulon

Rubus gymnocaulon, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thornless stems and dark, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color, typically in late summer. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and flowers with five white petals.

It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas under canopy cover.
