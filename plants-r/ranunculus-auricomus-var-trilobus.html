Ranunculus auricomus var. trilobus

Ranunculus auricomus var. trilobus, commonly known as the three-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features striking yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often with a distinctive greenish central disc. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided into three to five lobes, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance.

The species is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and is often used in wildflower displays.
