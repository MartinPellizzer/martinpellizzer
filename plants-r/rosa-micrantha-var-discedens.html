Rosa micrantha var. discedens

Rosa micrantha var. discedens, commonly known as the small-flowered Rosa or the Chinese rose, is a species native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The leaves are pinnately compound, with oval-shaped leaflets and a serrated margin. The plant produces oval, red or orange hips that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
