Rhipsalis riedeliana

Rhipsalis riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's mistletoe cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that hang from trees, often forming dense, cascading clusters. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that bloom during the rainy season, followed by berries that are typically yellow or red. Its leaves are reduced to small, translucent tubercles, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Rhipsalis riedeliana is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
