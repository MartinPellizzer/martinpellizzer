Romulea setifolia var. aggregata

Romulea setifolia var. aggregata, commonly known as the clustered romulea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
