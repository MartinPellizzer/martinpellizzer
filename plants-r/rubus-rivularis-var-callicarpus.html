Rubus rivularis var. callicarpus

Rubus rivularis var. callicarpus, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian areas and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, making it a popular choice for both landscaping and cultivation.
