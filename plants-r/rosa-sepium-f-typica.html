Rosa sepium f. typica

Rosa sepium f. typica, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a hybrid species that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage turns a striking red or orange in the fall, adding to its ornamental value.

Rosa sepium f. typica is native to Japan and is valued for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal in gardens.
