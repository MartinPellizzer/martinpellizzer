Rubus villicaulis f. tenellus

Rubus villicaulis f. tenellus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its smaller, more delicate leaves and fruit.

It is a shrub native to parts of North America, typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, usually red or black berries that are edible but often tart in flavor. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its identification.

This form is considered a subspecies or form of the broader Rubus villicaulis species, showcasing genetic variation within the genus.
