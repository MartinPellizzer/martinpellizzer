Rosa rubrifolia var. jurana

Rosa rubrifolia var. jurana, commonly known as the Crimson-leafed Rose, is a variant of the Rosa rubrifolia species native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which contrasts vividly with its typical pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
