Rubus fruticosus var. villarsianus

Rubus fruticosus var. villarsianus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberries, is a shrub-like plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupes. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often growing in woodland edges and hedgerows. It produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
