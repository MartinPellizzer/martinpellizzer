Rubus discolor subsp. villicaulis

Rubus discolor subsp.

villicaulis, commonly known as the purple-leaf bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged leaves and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for various wildlife and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
