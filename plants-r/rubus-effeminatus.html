Rubus effeminatus

Rubus effeminatus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have serrated margins, while its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, which matures to a dark purple or black color, is edible but often has a tart flavor.
