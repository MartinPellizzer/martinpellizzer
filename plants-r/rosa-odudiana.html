Rosa odudiana

Rosa odudiana, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species of flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, red or orange fruits known as hip fruits, which contain seeds. Rosa odudiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
