Rorippa pyrenaica

Rorippa pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean water-cress, is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic flowering plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in shallow water, wet meadows, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, branching stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its small, white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

Rorippa pyrenaica is known for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
