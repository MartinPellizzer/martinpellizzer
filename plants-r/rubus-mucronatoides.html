Rubus mucronatoides

Rubus mucronatoides, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry.

It is a shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in acidic soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

The berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in local foraging and traditional culinary practices.
