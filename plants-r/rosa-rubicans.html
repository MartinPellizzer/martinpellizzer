Rosa rubicans

Rosa rubicans, commonly known as the redstem rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while the stems are often reddish in color. Native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
