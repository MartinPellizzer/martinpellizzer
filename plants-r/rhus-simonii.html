Rhus simonii

Rhus simonii, commonly known as Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnately arranged with serrated leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or open woodlands.

Rhus simonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
