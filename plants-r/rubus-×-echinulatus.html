Rubus × echinulatus

Rubus × echinulatus, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry plant, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in a variety of soil types. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small white or pink flowers in the spring.

The fruit, known for its tart flavor, is widely cultivated for both culinary and commercial purposes.
