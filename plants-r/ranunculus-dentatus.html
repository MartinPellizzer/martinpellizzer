Ranunculus dentatus

Ranunculus dentatus, commonly known as the toothed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive toothed margin, giving it its common name.

The flowers are usually yellow with numerous stamens, and the plant produces small, hairy fruits that disperse its seeds.
