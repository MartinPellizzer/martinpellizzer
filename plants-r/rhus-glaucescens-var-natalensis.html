Rhus glaucescens var. natalensis

Rhus glaucescens var. natalensis, commonly known as Natal inkberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, red-brown berries that are edible for some birds and mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
