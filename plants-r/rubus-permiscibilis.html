Rubus permiscibilis

Rubus permiscibilis, commonly known as the wild blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands, thickets, and along stream banks.

Due to its hardy nature and adaptability, it plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and pollinators.
