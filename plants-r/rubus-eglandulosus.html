Rubus eglandulosus

Rubus eglandulosus, commonly known as the smooth raspberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or yellow when ripe. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The flowers are white or pale pink, and the plant is an important food source for various pollinators and wildlife.
