Rosa canina macrophylla

Rosa canina macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf dog rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, known as hips, are orange-red, hairy, and contain achenes.

Rosa canina macrophylla is native to Europe and western Asia and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
