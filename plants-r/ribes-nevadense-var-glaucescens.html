Ribes nevadense var. glaucescens

Ribes nevadense var. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous gooseberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant has small, oval leaves that are covered in a powdery, bluish-white wax, giving it a distinctive glaucous appearance. Its flowers are usually pink to white and appear in clusters, followed by edible, yellow berries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
