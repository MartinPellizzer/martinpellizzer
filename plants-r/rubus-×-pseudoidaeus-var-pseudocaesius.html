Rubus × pseudoidaeus var. pseudocaesius

Rubus × pseudoidaeus var. pseudocaesius, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black or dark purple. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its flavorful fruit.

The botanical features of this plant make it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
