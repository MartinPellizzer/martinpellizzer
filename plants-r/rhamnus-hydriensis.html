Rhamnus hydriensis

Rhamnus hydriensis, commonly known as the Hydriean buckthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rhamnaceae.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, often found in regions with limited water availability. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive glossy appearance.

Rhamnus hydriensis is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, showcasing resilience through its deep root system and drought tolerance.
