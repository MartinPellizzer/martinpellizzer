Rubus gremlii subsp. clusii

Rubus gremlii subsp.

clusii, commonly known as the Clusii blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
