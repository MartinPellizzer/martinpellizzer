Rosa × involuta var. laevigata

Rosa × involuta var. laevigata, commonly known as the Smoothleaf Rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
