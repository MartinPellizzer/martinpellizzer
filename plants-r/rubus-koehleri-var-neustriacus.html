Rubus koehleri var. neustriacus

Rubus koehleri var. neustriacus, commonly known as the Neustrian blackberry, is a shrub native to Central Europe.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its flowers are white with five petals and are pollinated by insects.
