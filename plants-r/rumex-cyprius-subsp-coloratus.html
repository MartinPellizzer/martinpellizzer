Rumex cyprius subsp. coloratus

Rumex cyprius subsp.

coloratus, commonly known as the Cyprus dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, slender stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
