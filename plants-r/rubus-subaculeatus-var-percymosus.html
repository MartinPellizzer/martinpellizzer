Rubus subaculeatus var. percymosus

Rubus subaculeatus var. percymosus, commonly known as the prickly raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental and edible qualities, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
