Rhus polyantha

Rhus polyantha, commonly known as the Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are divided into several narrow, serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for ornamental landscaping.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban environments and air pollution.
