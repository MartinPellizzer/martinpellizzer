Ricinus undulatus

Ricinus undulatus, commonly known as the zigzag castor, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaf margins that give it its common name. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are a potential hazard to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

The species is part of the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes other plants with milky sap and diverse growth forms.
