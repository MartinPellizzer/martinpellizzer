Rubus diversifolius proles ferus

Rubus diversifolius proles ferus, commonly known as the wild blackberry shrub, is a species of brambles native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed areas.

This species is known for its hardy nature and ability to spread through both vegetative growth and seed dispersal.
