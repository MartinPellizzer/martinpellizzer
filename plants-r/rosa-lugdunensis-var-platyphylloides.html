Rosa lugdunensis var. platyphylloides

Rosa lugdunensis var. platyphylloides, commonly known as the flat-leaf French rose, is a cultivar characterized by its broad, flat foliage and compact growth habit.

It produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a hybrid of Rosa lugdunensis and Rosa centifolia, known for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for shrub borders or container gardening.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both its aesthetic appeal and its historical significance in perfumery.
