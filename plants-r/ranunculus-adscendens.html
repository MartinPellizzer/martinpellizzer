Ranunculus adscendens

Ranunculus adscendens, commonly known as the ascending buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Ranunculus adscendens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
