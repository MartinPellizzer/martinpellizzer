Rubus ulmifolius microgene melanocaulon

Rubus ulmifolius microgene melanocaulon, commonly known as the black-barked raspberry, is a species of bramble belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems that are covered in a thick, waxy layer. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

This species is native to Europe and western Asia, often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.
