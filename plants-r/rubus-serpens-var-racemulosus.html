Rubus serpens var. racemulosus

Rubus serpens var. racemulosus, commonly known as the racemose blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, though it is less commonly cultivated than other blackberry varieties.
