Ranunculus bullatus var. prolifer

Ranunculus bullatus var. prolifer, commonly known as the clustered buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ranunculaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed leaves, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters.

This variety is noted for its rapid growth and ability to spread through vegetative means.
