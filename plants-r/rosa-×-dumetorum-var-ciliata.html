Rosa × dumetorum var. ciliata

Rosa × dumetorum var. ciliata, commonly known as the soft-hairy rose, is a hybrid species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that cover the stems and leaves, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
