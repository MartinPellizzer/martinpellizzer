Rhododendron morii var. taitunense

Rhododendron morii var. taitunense, commonly known as the Taitung rhododendron, is a shrub native to Taiwan.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and horticultural collections.
