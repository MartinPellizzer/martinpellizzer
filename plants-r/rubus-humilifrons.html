Rubus humilifrons

Rubus humilifrons, commonly known as the low-fruited blackberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodlands. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically eaten by birds and small mammals. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, prickly hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
