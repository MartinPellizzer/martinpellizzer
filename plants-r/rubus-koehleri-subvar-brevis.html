Rubus koehleri subvar. brevis

Rubus koehleri subvar. brevis, commonly known as the short-stalked blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America, characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its botanical features include a woody structure and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

It is often found in humid, shaded environments within its native habitat.
