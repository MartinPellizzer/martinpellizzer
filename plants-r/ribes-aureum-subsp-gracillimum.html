Ribes aureum subsp. gracillimum

Ribes aureum subsp.

gracillimum, commonly known as golden currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States. It features slender, arching stems that bear small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring.

The plant produces edible, golden-orange berries that are typically consumed by birds and small mammals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens. Ribes aureum subsp.

gracillimum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shady habitats.
