Robiquetia gracilis

Robiquetia gracilis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
