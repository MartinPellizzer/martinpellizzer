Rhetinantha fallax

Rhetinantha fallax, commonly known as the silky spurge, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
