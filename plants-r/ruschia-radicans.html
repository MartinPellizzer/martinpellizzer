Ruschia radicans

Ruschia radicans, commonly known as the creeping ruschia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, tubular leaves that are typically green with red or purple markings. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
