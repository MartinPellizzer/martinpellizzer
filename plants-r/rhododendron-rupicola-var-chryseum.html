Rhododendron rupicola var. chryseum

Rhododendron rupicola var. chryseum, commonly known as the golden-leaved rock rhododendron, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that exhibits a striking golden-yellow color, especially in full sun. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, visually appealing plant.
