Ruellia geminiflora var. hirsutior

Ruellia geminiflora var. hirsutior, commonly known as hairy ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Ruellia geminiflora var. hirsutior is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
