Rubus × semibakonyensis

Rubus × semibakonyensis, commonly known as the Hungarian blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to Hungary and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically larger than those of its parent species. The plant produces thorny stems and has palmate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The botanical features of Rubus × semibakonyensis make it a notable example of hybridization in the Rubus family.
