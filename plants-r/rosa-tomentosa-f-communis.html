Rosa tomentosa f. communis

Rosa tomentosa f. communis, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a compact shrub characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescence covering its stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically oval-shaped with a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.
