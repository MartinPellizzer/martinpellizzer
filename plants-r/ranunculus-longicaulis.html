Ranunculus longicaulis

Ranunculus longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with prominent white veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its often toxic members.

Ranunculus longicaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
