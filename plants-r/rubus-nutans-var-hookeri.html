Rubus nutans var. hookeri

Rubus nutans var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's trailing blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its arching stems that often trail along the ground, covered with fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it blooms with white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.
