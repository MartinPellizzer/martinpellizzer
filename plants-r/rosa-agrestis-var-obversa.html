Rosa agrestis var. obversa

Rosa agrestis var. obversa, commonly known as the rough-stemmed wild rose, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features thorny stems and simple, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals, and appear in late spring to early summer.

The fruit is a rosehip, which contains the seeds and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
