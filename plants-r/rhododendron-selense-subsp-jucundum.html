Rhododendron selense subsp. jucundum

Rhododendron selense subsp.

jucundum, commonly known as the Jucundum rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, specifically found in regions of Nepal and Bhutan. It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from pink to red, adding vibrant visual interest to its natural habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
