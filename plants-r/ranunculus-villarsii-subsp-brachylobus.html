Ranunculus villarsii subsp. brachylobus

Ranunculus villarsii subsp.

brachylobus, commonly known as the short-stemmed buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is characterized by its short, compact stems and is part of the Ranunculaceae family.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
