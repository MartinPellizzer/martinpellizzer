Rosa schmidtii var. leioclada

Rosa schmidtii var. leioclada, commonly known as the Schmidt's rose, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and a fragrant scent. This variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and resilience.
