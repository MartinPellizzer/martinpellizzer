Rubus × adenanthus

Rubus × adenanthus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and for local foraging.
