Rosa scabrata var. subrotunda

Rosa scabrata var. subrotunda, commonly known as the roughleaf rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems with rough, hairy texture and narrow, ovate leaves that are typically reddish-brown underneath. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers in spring, which are followed by orange-red hips in autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to native gardens and landscapes.
