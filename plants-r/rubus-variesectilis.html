Rubus variesectilis

Rubus variesectilis, commonly known as the variable blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupelets that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its flowers are white to pale pink and are followed by the edible berries that are popular in local cuisines.
