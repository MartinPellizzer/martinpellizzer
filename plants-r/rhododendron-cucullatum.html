Rhododendron cucullatum

Rhododendron cucullatum, commonly known as the hooded rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink.

Rhododendron cucullatum is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
