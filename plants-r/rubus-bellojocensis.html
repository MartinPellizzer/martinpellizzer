Rubus bellojocensis

Rubus bellojocensis, commonly known as the Bellojocensis blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the temperate regions of South America, particularly in the Andes mountains. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that resemble blackberries. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The fruit is edible, though often described as having a tart and somewhat astringent flavor.
