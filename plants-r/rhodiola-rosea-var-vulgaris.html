Rhodiola rosea var. vulgaris

Rhodiola rosea var. vulgaris, commonly known as the golden root, is a perennial plant native to the cold climates of the Arctic, alpine regions, and mountainous areas of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, yellowish roots and its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments. The plant belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is known for its adaptogenic properties. Its leaves are typically succulent and evergreen, with a rosette growth pattern.

Rhodiola rosea var. vulgaris is often cultivated for its medicinal uses and is valued in traditional herbal medicine.
