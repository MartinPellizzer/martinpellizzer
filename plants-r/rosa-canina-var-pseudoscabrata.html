Rosa canina var. pseudoscabrata

Rosa canina var. pseudoscabrata, commonly known as the rough rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a rugged appearance. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and numerous stamens. This plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often growing in woodland areas and along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
