Ranunculus silanus

Ranunculus silanus, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or aquatic environments such as ponds, streams, and marshes. The plant features delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow or white flowers with five petals. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Ranunculus silanus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
