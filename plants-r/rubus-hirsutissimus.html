Rubus hirsutissimus

Rubus hirsutissimus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife.
