Rubus koehleri microgene validispinus

Rubus koehleri microgene validispinus, commonly known as the Koehl's raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it produces small, white flowers in the spring.

The fruit, which is a red, hairy berry, is edible and often used in local cuisines.
