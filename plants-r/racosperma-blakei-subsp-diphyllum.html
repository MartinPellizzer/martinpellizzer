Racosperma blakei subsp. diphyllum

Racosperma blakei subsp.

diphyllum, commonly known as the two-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate, and its yellow flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical spikes.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small fauna.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of Australian flora and ecological resilience.
