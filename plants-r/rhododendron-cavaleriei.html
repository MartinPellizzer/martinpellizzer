Rhododendron cavaleriei

Rhododendron cavaleriei, commonly known as the Sichuan laurel, is a species native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron cavaleriei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
