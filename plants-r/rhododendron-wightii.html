Rhododendron wightii

Rhododendron wightii, commonly known as the Wight's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly found in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, and it produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers in the spring. Rhododendron wightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
