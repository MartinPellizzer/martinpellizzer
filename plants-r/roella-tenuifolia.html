Roella tenuifolia

Roella tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender sea rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils. The plant features small, pinnately divided leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, which are characteristic of the Brassicaceae family.

Roella tenuifolia is adapted to tolerate salty and windy environments, making it a common plant in maritime regions.
