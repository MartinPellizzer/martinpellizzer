Rhododendron dalhousieae

Rhododendron dalhousieae, commonly known as the Dalhousie rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, specifically found in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often densely covered with fine hairs, and it produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers in the spring. Rhododendron dalhousieae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with acidic soil and partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ericaceae family.
