Rosa glauca var. flexuosa

Rosa glauca var. flexuosa, commonly known as the blue rose bush, is a variant of the blue rose, characterized by its distinctive pale blue flowers.

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are typically dark green and slightly hairy, while its stems are woody and often have a reddish hue. The flowers bloom in spring and are known for their delicate, fragrant scent.

It is a hardy perennial that prefers well-drained soil and partial sunlight.
