Rosa communis var. corymbifera

Rosa communis var. corymbifera, commonly known as the clustered rose, is a cultivar characterized by its dense, corymbose inflorescences.

This variety is native to China and is prized for its fragrant, semi-double flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
