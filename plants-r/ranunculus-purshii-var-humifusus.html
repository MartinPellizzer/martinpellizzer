Ranunculus purshii var. humifusus

Ranunculus purshii var. humifusus, commonly known as the low-growing buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically yellow with 5 to 8 sepals that resemble petals. The plant grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks, often in areas with well-drained soil. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that disperse its seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
