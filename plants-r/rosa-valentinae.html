Rosa valentinae

Rosa valentinae, commonly known as the Valentine rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the United States.

It features dense, thornless stems and produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early fall.
