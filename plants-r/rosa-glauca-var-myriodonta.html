Rosa glauca var. myriodonta

Rosa glauca var. myriodonta, commonly known as the Myriodonta rose, is a variant of the blue rose that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The flowers are typically pale blue or lavender in color, with five to seven petals and a prominent, fragrant center. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its genetic makeup is closely related to other Rosa species, contributing to its ornamental value and horticultural interest.
