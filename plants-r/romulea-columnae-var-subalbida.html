Romulea columnae var. subalbida

Romulea columnae var. subalbida, commonly known as the pale-flowered romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, spurred flowers. The flowers are typically pale yellow to white, with a distinctive violet or purple stripe along the inner edge of the petals. This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in Mediterranean climates.
