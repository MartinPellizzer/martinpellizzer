Rosa pubescens subsp. kostrakiewiczii

Rosa pubescens subsp.

kostrakiewiczii, commonly known as the Kostrakiewicz rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Europe. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a soft, hairy texture. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden environments.
