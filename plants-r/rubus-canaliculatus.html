Rubus canaliculatus

Rubus canaliculatus, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and native landscaping.
