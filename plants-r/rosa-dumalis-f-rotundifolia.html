Rosa dumalis f. rotundifolia

Rosa dumalis f. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed wild rose, is a unique cultivar of the wild rose species.

It is characterized by its large, round, and often fuzzy leaves, which distinguish it from other rose varieties. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is a shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and naturalized areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, adapting well to various climates.
