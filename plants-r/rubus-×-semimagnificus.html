Rubus × semimagnificus

Rubus × semimagnificus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny bushes and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a mix of traits from its parent species, Rubus ursinus and Rubus argutus.
