Rubus adenochlamys

Rubus adenochlamys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of eastern North America, particularly the southeastern United States. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that mature into dark purple to black berries. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem, supporting various wildlife species.
