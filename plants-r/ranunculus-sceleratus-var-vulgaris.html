Ranunculus sceleratus var. vulgaris

Ranunculus sceleratus var. vulgaris, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with five to ten petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a hairy stem and heart-shaped leaves that are often divided into lobes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Due to its attractive flowers, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
