Rhizomatopteris montana

Rhizomatopteris montana, commonly known as the mountain rhizomata fern, is a unique fern species characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

It features a creeping rhizome that allows it to spread horizontally, forming dense mats of foliage. The fronds are typically lanceolate and have a leathery texture, with a deep green coloration. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions with high humidity.

Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of the fern family.
