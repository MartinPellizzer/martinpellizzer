Rubus × pseudocuspidifer

Rubus × pseudocuspidifer, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. It produces small, dark berries that are typically edible, though they may vary in flavor and size depending on the specific cultivar. The plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
