Ranunculus gormanii

Ranunculus gormanii, commonly known as Gorman's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers consist of numerous petal-like sepals that resemble true petals.

It is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
