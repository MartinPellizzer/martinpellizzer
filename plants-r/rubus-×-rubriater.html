Rubus × rubriater

Rubus × rubriater, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its dark, almost black berries that often have a reddish tinge. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the berries are prized for their flavor and culinary use.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and hardiness.
