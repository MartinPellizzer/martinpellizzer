Rapanea citrifolia

Rapanea citrifolia, commonly known as the lemon tree of Madagascar, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by spherical, yellow fruits containing a single seed.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to the island's tropical climate and its role in local ecosystems.
