Ranunculus auricomus subsp. serratus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

serratus, commonly known as the serrated goldcup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its bright yellow flowers with deeply lobed petals.

The subspecies is distinguished by its serrated leaf margins, which set it apart from other members of the *Ranunculus auricomus* species complex. Native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for garden cultivation, though it requires careful management due to its potential invasiveness.
