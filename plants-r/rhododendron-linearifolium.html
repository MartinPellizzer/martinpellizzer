Rhododendron linearifolium

Rhododendron linearifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaved rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in China and Nepal.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist climates.

Rhododendron linearifolium is often found in forested areas at high elevations, where it plays a role in the local ecosystem.
