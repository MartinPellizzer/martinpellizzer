Ranunculus angustifolius var. uniflorus

Ranunculus angustifolius var. uniflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, finely toothed margin.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic properties.
