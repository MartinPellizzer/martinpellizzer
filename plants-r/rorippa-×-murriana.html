Rorippa × murriana

Rorippa × murriana, commonly known as the hybrid watercress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rorippa indica and Rorippa torulosa, characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit. The plant typically has small, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist environments such as ponds, ditches, and stream margins.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in aquatic ecosystems.
