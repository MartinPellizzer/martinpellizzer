Roegneria magnicaespes

Roegneria magnicaespes, commonly known as the giant bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Roegneria magnicaespes is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
