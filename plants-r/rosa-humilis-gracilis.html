Rosa humilis gracilis

Rosa humilis gracilis, commonly known as the slender shrub rose, is a low-growing, deciduous species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has finely toothed leaves and produces rose hips that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
