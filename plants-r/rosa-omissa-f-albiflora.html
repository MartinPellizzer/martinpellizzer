Rosa omissa f. albiflora

Rosa omissa f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered form of the neglected rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its pale white petals and compact growth habit.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer, and its foliage is dark green with a slightly serrated edge. Rosa omissa f. albiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with partial sunlight.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty and historical significance in botanical collections.
