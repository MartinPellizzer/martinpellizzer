Rubus corchorifolius var. grayanus

Rubus corchorifolius var. grayanus, commonly known as Gray's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that are typically small and firm. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces flowers that are usually white or pale pink. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
