Rorippa pinnata

Rorippa pinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watercress, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and slightly serrated. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that grow in clusters, often on slender stems that emerge from water or moist soil. Rorippa pinnata is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
