Ranunculus pusillus var. angustifolius

Ranunculus pusillus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a small herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its short stature and is often found in calcareous soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
