Ranunculus pseudacutiusculus

Ranunculus pseudacutiusculus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have a distinctive, cup-like structure.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
