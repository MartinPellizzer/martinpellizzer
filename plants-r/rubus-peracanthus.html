Rubus peracanthus

Rubus peracanthus, commonly known as the prickly pear raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are usually white or pink.

The fruit, which is edible when ripe, has a sweet, tangy flavor and is often used in culinary applications.
