Rubus lutescens

Rubus lutescens, commonly known as the yellow bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it typically grows in woodland edges and rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit, a type of aggregate berry, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
