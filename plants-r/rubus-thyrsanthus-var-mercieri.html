Rubus thyrsanthus var. mercieri

Rubus thyrsanthus var. mercieri, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. Its berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in woodland edges and thickets.
