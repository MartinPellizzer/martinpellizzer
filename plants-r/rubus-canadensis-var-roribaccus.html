Rubus canadensis var. roribaccus

Rubus canadensis var. roribaccus, commonly known as the Canada rose.

It is a perennial shrub native to North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in the eastern United States.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
