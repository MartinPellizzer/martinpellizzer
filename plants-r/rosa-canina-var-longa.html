Rosa canina var. longa

Rosa canina var. longa, commonly known as the long-stemmed dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its elongated stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces red, hairy fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
