Rubus bifrons subsp. lindebergii

Rubus bifrons subsp.

lindebergii, commonly known as the Lindberg's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
