Rubia jesoensis

Rubia jesoensis, commonly known as the Korean madder, is a flowering plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in cymes. Its roots are used to produce a red dye, similar to other species in the Rubia genus.

Rubia jesoensis is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and stream sides.
