Rubus hirtus f. rilaemontanus

Rubus hirtus f. rilaemontanus, commonly known as the Rila Mountains raspberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within its native habitat.
