Rosa placidina

Rosa placidina, commonly known as the peace rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, with thorny stems typical of many rose species.

Rosa placidina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
