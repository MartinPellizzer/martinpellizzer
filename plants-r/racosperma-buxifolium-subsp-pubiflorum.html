Racosperma buxifolium subsp. pubiflorum

Racosperma buxifolium subsp.

pubiflorum, commonly known as the glossy boxwood, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with thick, leathery leaves that are typically glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal, it is often used in horticultural landscaping.
