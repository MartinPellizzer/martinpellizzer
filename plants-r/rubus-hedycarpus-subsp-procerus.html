Rubus hedycarpus subsp. procerus

Rubus hedycarpus subsp.

procerus, commonly known as the long-stalked blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a deep black color. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its edible berries and is also valued in ecological landscaping.
