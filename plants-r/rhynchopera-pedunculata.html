Rhynchopera pedunculata

Rhynchopera pedunculata, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, three-lobed flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in freshwater habitats. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a distinct midrib.

Rhynchopera pedunculata plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
