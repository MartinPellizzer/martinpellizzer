Rubus fuscicaulis

Rubus fuscicaulis, commonly known as the dark-stemmed blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and white, typically appearing in clusters, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.
