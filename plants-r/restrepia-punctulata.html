Restrepia punctulata

Restrepia punctulata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers with distinctive dark spots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

Restrepia punctulata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
