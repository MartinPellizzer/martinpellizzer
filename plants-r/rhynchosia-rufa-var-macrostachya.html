Rhynchosia rufa var. macrostachya

Rhynchosia rufa var. macrostachya, commonly known as the red spurred cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces vibrant red flowers with distinctive spurred petals, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are typically hairy and erect, while its leaves are trifoliate with serrated edges.

The variety macrostachya is distinguished by its larger, more robust flowers and seed pods compared to the typical form of R. rufa.
