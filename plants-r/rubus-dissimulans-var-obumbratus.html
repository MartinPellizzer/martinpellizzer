Rubus dissimulans var. obumbratus

Rubus dissimulans var. obumbratus, commonly known as the shadowy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark, almost black foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically less palatable than those of other blackberry varieties. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and plant taxonomy.
