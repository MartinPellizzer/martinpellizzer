Rhodiola telephioides

Rhodiola telephioides, commonly known as the alpine rose or king's crown, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Crassulaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure with fleshy, often reddish leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters at the center of the rosette. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Rhodiola telephioides is also known for its potential adaptogenic properties, making it of interest in herbal medicine.
