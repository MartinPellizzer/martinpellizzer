Rubus caesius subsp. althaeifolius

Rubus caesius subsp.

althaeifolius, commonly known as the althaeifolius raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins and a hairy texture.

This subspecies is often found in woodland edges and moist habitats, preferring partial shade and well-drained soil.
