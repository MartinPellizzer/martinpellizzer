Rubus ferocior

Rubus ferocior, commonly known as the fierce bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines, though they are generally tart and require ripening. It thrives in woodland areas and is known for its hardy nature and ability to spread rapidly.

Its botanical features make it both a valuable forage plant and a challenging weed in agricultural settings.
