Rubus cuneifolius var. angustior

Rubus cuneifolius var. angustior, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically narrow and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into dark purple or black berries. Its fruits are edible, though often tart and less sweet than common blackberries.

The species is characterized by its dense growth habit and adaptability to various soil types.
