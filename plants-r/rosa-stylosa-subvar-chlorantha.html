Rosa stylosa subvar. chlorantha

Rosa stylosa subvar. chlorantha, commonly known as the green-stemmed rose, is a shrub-like species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often greenish stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets that provide a fine texture. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it adaptable to a range of growing conditions.
