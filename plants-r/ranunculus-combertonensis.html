Ranunculus combertonensis

Ranunculus combertonensis, commonly known as the Comberton buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often accompanied by distinctive, toothed leaves. Its botanical characteristics include a short, stout stem and a basal rosette of leaves.

Ranunculus combertonensis is adapted to cold, dry environments and is an important species in its native ecosystem.
