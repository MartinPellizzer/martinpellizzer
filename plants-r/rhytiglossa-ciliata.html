Rhytiglossa ciliata

Rhytiglossa ciliata, commonly known as the silky bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are finely hairy, giving it a soft, velvety appearance.

Rhytiglossa ciliata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
