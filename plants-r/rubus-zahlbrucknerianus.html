Rubus zahlbrucknerianus

Rubus zahlbrucknerianus, commonly known as the Zahlbruckner's blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and blackberries. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in local food systems.
