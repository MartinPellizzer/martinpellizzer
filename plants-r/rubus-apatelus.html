Rubus apatelus

Rubus apatelus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, dark red to black fruits. The plant produces compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

The berries are edible but generally less flavorful compared to other blackberry species.
