Rhododendron eymae

Rhododendron eymae, commonly known as the Eyma rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from white to pink and purple.

Rhododendron eymae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
