Racosperma acuminatum

Racosperma acuminatum, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional Indigenous practices.

Racosperma acuminatum is adapted to dry environments and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.
