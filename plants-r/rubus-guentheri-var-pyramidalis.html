Rubus guentheri var. pyramidalis

Rubus guentheri var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
