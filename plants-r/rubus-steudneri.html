Rubus steudneri

Rubus steudneri, commonly known as the Steudner's blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in Argentina, Chile, and Peru. The plant features thorny stems and produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupes. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it blooms with white to pale pink flowers.

Rubus steudneri is often cultivated for its edible berries, which have a tart flavor and are used in local cuisines.
