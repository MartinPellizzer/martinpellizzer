Reedrollinsia cauliflora

Reedrollinsia cauliflora, commonly known as the swamp reed, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach several meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers at the base of the stem, a feature known as cauliflory. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils.

Reedrollinsia cauliflora plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
