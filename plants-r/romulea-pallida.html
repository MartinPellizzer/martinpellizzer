Romulea pallida

Romulea pallida, commonly known as the pale romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a compact growth habit. Its bulbs are adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Romulea pallida is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
