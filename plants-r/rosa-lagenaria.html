Rosa lagenaria

Rosa lagenaria, commonly known as the bottle gourd or calabash, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, spherical, green fruits that resemble bottles, which are used in various culinary and practical applications. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers and has heart-shaped, lobed leaves. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The botanical structure of Rosa lagenaria includes a vigorous vine with tendrils that help it climb and spread.
