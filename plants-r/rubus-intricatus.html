Rubus intricatus

Rubus intricatus, commonly known as the intricate raspberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pink, with five petals, and it produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications.

The species is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
