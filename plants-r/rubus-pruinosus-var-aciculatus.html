Rubus pruinosus var. aciculatus

Rubus pruinosus var. aciculatus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
