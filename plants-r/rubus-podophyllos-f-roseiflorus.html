Rubus podophyllos f. roseiflorus

Rubus podophyllos f. roseiflorus, commonly known as the rose-flowered blackberry, is a flowering shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are aggregate berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color by late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
