Rosa xerampelina

Rosa xerampelina, commonly known as the rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features woody stems with thorny branches and is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of small, glossy, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa xerampelina is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
