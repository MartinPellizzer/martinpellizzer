Ranunculus rhombilobus

Ranunculus rhombilobus, commonly known as the rhombic buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves with a distinctive rhomboid shape, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange with five to eight petals. The plant produces a cluster of small, yellow anthers that release pollen, contributing to its showy appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
