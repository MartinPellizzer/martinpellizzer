Rubus mulleri subsp. jactuosus

Rubus mulleri subsp.

jactuosus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
