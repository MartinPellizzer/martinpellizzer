Rhododendron aiolopeplum

Rhododendron aiolopeplum, commonly known as the Greek rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments. The plant features dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron aiolopeplum is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
