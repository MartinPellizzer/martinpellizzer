Rhododendron intranervatum

Rhododendron intranervatum, commonly known as the narrow-nerved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant prefers acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shady, moist environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a distinct, parallel venation pattern.

Rhododendron intranervatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
