Rubus serpens subsp. longipes

Rubus serpens subsp.

longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of South America. It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces small, dark berries.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
