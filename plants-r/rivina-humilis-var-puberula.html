Rivina humilis var. puberula

Rivina humilis var. puberula, commonly known as the velvetleaf rivina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture, and it produces clusters of small, bright red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
