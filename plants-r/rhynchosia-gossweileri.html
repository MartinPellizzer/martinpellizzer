Rhynchosia gossweileri

Rhynchosia gossweileri, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. Rhynchosia gossweileri is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its adaptability to diverse environmental conditions contributes to its ecological and agricultural significance.
