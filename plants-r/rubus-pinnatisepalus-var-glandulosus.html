Rubus pinnatisepalus var. glandulosus

Rubus pinnatisepalus var. glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive glandular hairs on the stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to various regions in North America. The plant produces small, dark berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
