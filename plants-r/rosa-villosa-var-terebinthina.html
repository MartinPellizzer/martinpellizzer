Rosa villosa var. terebinthina

Rosa villosa var. terebinthina, commonly known as the Turkish rose or terebinthina rose, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, ovate to elliptic leaves with a hairy underside, and clusters of fragrant, pale pink to white flowers.

The plant produces small, reddish-orange fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant suitable for gardens and landscapes.
