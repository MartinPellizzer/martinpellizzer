Rosa tittelbachii

Rosa tittelbachii, commonly known as the Tittelbach rose, is a species of flowering shrub native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and abundant, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the stems are often covered in thorns.

Rosa tittelbachii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
