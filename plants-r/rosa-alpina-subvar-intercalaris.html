Rosa alpina subvar. intercalaris

Rosa alpina subvar. intercalaris, commonly known as the Alpine pink rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or pale gray pubescence. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five to seven petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
