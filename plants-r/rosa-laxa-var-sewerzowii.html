Rosa laxa var. sewerzowii

Rosa laxa var. sewerzowii, commonly known as the Siberian climbing rose, is a variant of the Rosa laxa species native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern China.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can climb or trail along the ground, making it suitable for trellises or ground cover. The plant produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly hairy texture.

This hardy species is adapted to harsh climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
