Rhaponticoides gontscharovii

Rhaponticoides gontscharovii, commonly known as the Siberian rhubarb, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Eastern Europe and Siberia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
