Rumex friesii

Rumex friesii, commonly known as the Fries' sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible leaves. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a reddish tint and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
