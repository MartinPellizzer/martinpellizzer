Rosa jundzillii var. livescens

Rosa jundzillii var. livescens, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces oval, leathery leaves with a glossy surface, and its flowers are often borne in compact inflorescences. It is adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor, well-drained soils.
