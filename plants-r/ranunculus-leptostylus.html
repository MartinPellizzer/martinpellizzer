Ranunculus leptostylus

Ranunculus leptostylus, commonly known as the slender-styled buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Bolivia and Peru. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The species is characterized by its slender, elongated style, which gives it its scientific name.
