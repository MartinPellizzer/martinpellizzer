Robinsonella subcordata

Robinsonella subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Robinsonella subcordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
