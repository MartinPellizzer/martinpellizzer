Rubus radula subsp. leptocercus

Rubus radula subsp.

leptocercus, commonly known as the slender-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with thorny stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in temperate to subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
