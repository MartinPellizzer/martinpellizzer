Rubus serpens var. minutiflorens

Rubus serpens var. minutiflorens, commonly known as the small-flowered blackberry vine, is a member of the Rosaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a trailing shrub characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark purple to black berries. Its botanical features include a woody vine structure, hairy stems, and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
