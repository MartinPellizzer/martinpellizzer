Rosa canina var. subcaesia

Rosa canina var. subcaesia, commonly known as the pale dog rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and produces red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
