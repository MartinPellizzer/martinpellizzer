Rumex occidentalis

Rumex occidentalis, commonly known as western sorrel, is a perennial herb native to western North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration on the underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, and it produces triangular, winged seeds.

The plant contains oxalic acid, which gives it a sharp, tangy flavor and makes it useful in culinary applications.
