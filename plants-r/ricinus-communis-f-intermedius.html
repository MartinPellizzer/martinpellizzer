Ricinus communis f. intermedius

Ricinus communis f. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate castor plant, is a variant of the castor oil plant characterized by its unique foliage and growth habits.

This cultivar exhibits intermediate traits between the typical castor plant and other forms, often displaying a mix of leaf shapes and sizes. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces distinctive spiny capsules containing toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and industrial applications.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable environments.
