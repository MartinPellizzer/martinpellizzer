Ranunculus subsagittatus var. subaffinis

Ranunculus subsagittatus var. subaffinis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and deeply lobed, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrower and more elongated compared to the typical subspecies.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, aggregate fruits containing several seeds.
