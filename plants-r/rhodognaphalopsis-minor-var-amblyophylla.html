Rhodognaphalopsis minor var. amblyophylla

Rhodognaphalopsis minor var. amblyophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous small, fleshy, and often hairy stems. The leaves are typically narrow, linear, and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry environments. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

This plant is well-suited to sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sun-exposed habitats.
