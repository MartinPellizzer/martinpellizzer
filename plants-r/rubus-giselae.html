Rubus giselae

Rubus giselae, commonly known as the Giselae raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
