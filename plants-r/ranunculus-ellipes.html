Ranunculus ellipes

Ranunculus ellipes, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which is characterized by showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have five to seven petal-like sepals that are usually yellow or orange in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
