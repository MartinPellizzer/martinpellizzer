Rubus × goniophyllomorphus var. spinosus

Rubus × goniophyllomorphus var. spinosus, commonly known as the spiny blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
