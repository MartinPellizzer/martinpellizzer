Rumex × pratensis var. sanguineus

Rumex × pratensis var. sanguineus, commonly known as crimson sorrel, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-pink leaves and stems, which are often tinged with a striking coloration. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are edible, though they have a tart, sour flavor.

This variant is valued for both ornamental purposes and as a historical food source.
