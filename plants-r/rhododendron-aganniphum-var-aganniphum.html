Rhododendron aganniphum var. aganniphum

Rhododendron aganniphum var. aganniphum, commonly known as the alpine rhododendron, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with thick, leathery leaves that provide insulation against harsh weather.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
