Rubus chevalieri var. chevalieri

Rubus chevalieri var. chevalieri, commonly known as the chevalier blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has arching stems covered in dense, prickly bristles, and its leaves are trifoliate with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often growing in forested areas.

The berries are used in traditional Chinese medicine and are also appreciated for their flavor in culinary applications.
