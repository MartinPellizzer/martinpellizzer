Rhododendron ixeuticum

Rhododendron ixeuticum, commonly known as the Ecuadorian rhododendron, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of Ecuador.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows at high elevations, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and floral structures that are typical of the Ericaceae family.

Rhododendron ixeuticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
