Rubus hirtus microgene offensus

Rubus hirtus microgene offensus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

It plays a role in supporting local wildlife, providing food for birds and insects.
