Rosa pendula

Rosa pendula, commonly known as the weeping rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features slender, arching stems that droop gracefully, giving it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

Rosa pendula is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.
