Rhynchosia phaseoloides var. erecta

Rhynchosia phaseoloides var. erecta, commonly known as the upright snout bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in poor soils.

The species is valued for its potential in forage and as a cover crop in agroecosystems.
