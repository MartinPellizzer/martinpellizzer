Rubus thyrsoideus subsp. persicinus

Rubus thyrsoideus subsp.

persicinus, commonly known as the Persian raspberry, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color.

The plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it blooms in early summer.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
