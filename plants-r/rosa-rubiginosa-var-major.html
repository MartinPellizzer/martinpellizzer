Rosa rubiginosa var. major

Rosa rubiginosa var. major, commonly known as the Greater Scotch thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny stems and silvery-gray leaves. The plant produces showy pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The species is also known for its resilience to drought and coastal environments.
