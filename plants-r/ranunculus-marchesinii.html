Ranunculus marchesinii

Ranunculus marchesinii, commonly known as the Corsican buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking yellow flowers with deeply lobed petals, typically borne on slender stems. The plant has palmate leaves that are divided into three to five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
