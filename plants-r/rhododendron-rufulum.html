Rhododendron rufulum

Rhododendron rufulum, commonly known as the white rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland areas. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, and the plant is often found in the understory of mixed hardwood forests.

Rhododendron rufulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
