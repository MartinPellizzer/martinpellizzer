Radermachera biternata

Radermachera biternata, commonly known as the three-leaf maple or Japanese maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, which features lobed leaves that change color throughout the seasons. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spring, followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in suitable climates.
