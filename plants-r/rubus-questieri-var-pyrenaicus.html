Rubus questieri var. pyrenaicus

Rubus questieri var. pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 600 to 2,000 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local culinary traditions.
