Rubus tomentosus var. albicans

Rubus tomentosus var. albicans, commonly known as the white-haired bramble, is a variant of the bramble plant characterized by its dense, white pubescence.

It is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins and a hairy underside.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
