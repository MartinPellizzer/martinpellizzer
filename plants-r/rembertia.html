Rembertia

Rembertia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Rembertia alata, which is native to South America. The plants are characterized by their showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. They typically grow in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Rembertia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant blooms.
