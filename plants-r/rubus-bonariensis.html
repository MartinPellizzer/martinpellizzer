Rubus bonariensis

Rubus bonariensis, commonly known as the Argentine blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color, often with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other bramble species.
