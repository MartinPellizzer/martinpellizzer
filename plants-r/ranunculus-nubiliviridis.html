Ranunculus nubiliviridis

Ranunculus nubiliviridis, commonly known as the cloud-green buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive greenish tinge. The plant typically grows in moist, alpine environments, often found in meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is known for its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates.

The species is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
