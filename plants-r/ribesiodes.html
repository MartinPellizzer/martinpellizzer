Ribesiodes

Ribesiodes, commonly known as the red-leafed honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically reddish-purple in color, turning vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Ribesiodes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
