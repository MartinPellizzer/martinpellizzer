Rubus amygdalanthus

Rubus amygdalanthus, commonly known as the blackberry lily, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a small, dark purple berry that ripens in late summer.

The leaves are typically alternate and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
