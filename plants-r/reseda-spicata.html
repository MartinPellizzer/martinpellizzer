Reseda spicata

Reseda spicata, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing adaptability to saline environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually pale blue or purple.

Reseda spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
