Racosperma celsum

Racosperma celsum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many leguminous plants known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, compound leaves and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in hard pods and are often used in traditional Aboriginal practices.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
