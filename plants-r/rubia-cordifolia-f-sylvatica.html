Rubia cordifolia f. sylvatica

Rubia cordifolia f. sylvatica, commonly known as the forest rue or woodland bugloss, is a variant of the common rue plant.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in loose panicles.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
