Rubus fuscoater var. exasperatus

Rubus fuscoater var. exasperatus, commonly known as the blackberry bush, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

The leaves are alternate, compound, and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
