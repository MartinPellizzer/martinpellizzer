Rosa carolina sepalorelevata

Rosa carolina sepalorelevata, commonly known as the Carolina rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has sepals that are elevated and reflexed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
