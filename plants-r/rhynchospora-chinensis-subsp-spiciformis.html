Rhynchospora chinensis subsp. spiciformis

Rhynchospora chinensis subsp.

spiciformis, commonly known as the spiciform spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
