Rubus glandulosus var. dentatus

Rubus glandulosus var. dentatus, commonly known as the rough bramble or serrated blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are pink to white, and it thrives in moist, wooded areas.

The species is noted for its hirsute (hairy) texture on its stems and leaves.
