Rhamnus frangula f. genuina

Rhamnus frangula f. genuina, commonly known as the European buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. The plant is known for its hard, woody bark and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed sites.

The species is also notable for its medicinal and historical uses in traditional herbal remedies.
