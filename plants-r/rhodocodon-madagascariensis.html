Rhodocodon madagascariensis

Rhodocodon madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found exclusively in Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers with vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments, where it thrives in nutrient-rich soil. Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for protective measures.
