Rubus acanthopodus

Rubus acanthopodus, commonly known as the Chinese raspberry or brambleberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to East Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, red or yellow berries. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and the plant produces edible fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Rubus acanthopodus is often found in woodlands, shrublands, and along stream banks.
