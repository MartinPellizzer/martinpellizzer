Riencourtia pedunculosa

Riencourtia pedunculosa, commonly known as the slender-stemmed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are elongated, narrow, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Riencourtia pedunculosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
