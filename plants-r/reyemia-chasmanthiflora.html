Reyemia chasmanthiflora

Reyemia chasmanthiflora, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
