Rabdosia umbrosa var. coreana

Rabdosia umbrosa var. coreana, commonly known as Korean shadow mosquito plant, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically lilac to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems.

This species thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
