Rosa cinnamomea f. elliptica

Rosa cinnamomea f. elliptica, commonly known as the cinnamon rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa cinnamomea species, characterized by its elliptical-shaped leaves and fragrant, pale pink to white flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub native to China, typically growing to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features thorny stems and simple, alternate leaves with a velvety texture.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a subtle cinnamon-like scent, which is a distinguishing feature of the species.
