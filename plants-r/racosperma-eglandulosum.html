Racosperma eglandulosum

Racosperma eglandulosum, commonly known as the glandless acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically has slender, woody stems and small, bipinnate leaves. It produces yellow flowers arranged in inflorescences, which are followed by flat, dehiscent pods.

Racosperma eglandulosum is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
