Rhododendron × spinulosum

Rhododendron × spinulosum, commonly known as the prickly rhododendron, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its unique feature is the presence of stiff, spinulose hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

Rhododendron × spinulosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
