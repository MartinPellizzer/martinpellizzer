Rhododendron kaempferi var. macrogemmum

Rhododendron kaempferi var. macrogemmum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea or mountain azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

This variety is well-suited for shady gardens and is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardiness.
