Rosa platyphylla var. platyphylloides

Rosa platyphylla var. platyphylloides, commonly known as the flatleaf rose, is a variety of the shrub rose native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves that are broad and flat, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red-orange hips. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in garden settings.
