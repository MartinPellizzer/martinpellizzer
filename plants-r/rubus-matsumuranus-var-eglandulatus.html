Rubus matsumuranus var. eglandulatus

Rubus matsumuranus var. eglandulatus, commonly known as the glandular blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
