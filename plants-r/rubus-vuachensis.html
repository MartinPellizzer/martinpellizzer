Rubus vuachensis

Rubus vuachensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Andean region of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs and small trees known for their edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate forests at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit, a aggregate berry, is dark purple to black in color and is often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
