Relbunium croceum subsp. involucratum

Relbunium croceum subsp.

involucratum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant features slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. Its flowers are clustered in terminal racemes, and the subspecies is distinguished by its involucrate bracts.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
