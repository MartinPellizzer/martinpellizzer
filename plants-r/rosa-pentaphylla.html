Rosa pentaphylla

Rosa pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leaf rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its simple, five-lobed leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showing a preference for well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Rosa pentaphylla is also noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
