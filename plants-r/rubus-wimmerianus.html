Rubus wimmerianus

Rubus wimmerianus, commonly known as the Wimmer's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and blackberries.
