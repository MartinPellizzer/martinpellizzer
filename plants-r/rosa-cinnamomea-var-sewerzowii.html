Rosa cinnamomea var. sewerzowii

Rosa cinnamomea var. sewerzowii, commonly known as the Cinnamon Rose, is a variant of the Cinnamon Rose species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown stems and fragrant, pink to crimson flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with dark green, glossy leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration and fragrance.
