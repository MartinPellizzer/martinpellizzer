Rubus moluccanus var. ochlanthus

Rubus moluccanus var. ochlanthus, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a shrub native to the island of Flores in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red, edible fruits that ripen in the summer. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has a woody structure typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the plant is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
