Rosa glauca var. rebholzii

Rosa glauca var. rebholzii, commonly known as the Rebolz rose, is a rare and unique variant of the glaucous rose.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and pale pink to white flowers. This plant is native to specific regions in Central Asia and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and produces fragrant blooms throughout the summer.

Due to its limited distribution and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
