Robiquetia spathulata

Robiquetia spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, with a distinctive spatulate leaf shape. The plant grows in clusters, often forming dense foliage that thrives in well-drained soils. Its botanical structure includes opposite leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a striking addition to gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
