Ranunculus auricomus subsp. sparsipubescens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

sparsipubescens, commonly known as the slenderleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem that distinguishes it from other subspecies.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
