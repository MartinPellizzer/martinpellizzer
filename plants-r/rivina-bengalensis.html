Rivina bengalensis

Rivina bengalensis, commonly known as the Bengal rivina, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and Sri Lanka.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender branches and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, which bloom during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

The plant is often found in moist, shady habitats such as forests and along stream banks.
