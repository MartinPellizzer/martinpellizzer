Rhododon ciliatus

Rhododon ciliatus, commonly known as the fringed rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically broad and have a leathery texture, while the stems are covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
