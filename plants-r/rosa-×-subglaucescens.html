Rosa × subglaucescens

Rosa × subglaucescens, commonly known as the pale pink rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with arching stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves. Its flowers have a subtle fragrance and are often used in floral arrangements.

Rosa × subglaucescens is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
