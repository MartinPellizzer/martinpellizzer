Raddia monophylla

Raddia monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf raddia, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its singular, large, waxy leaf that serves as both a photosynthetic organ and a water-storing structure. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellow, adapted for pollination by insects. Its succulent nature allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments by reducing water loss.

Raddia monophylla is a striking example of botanical adaptation to extreme conditions.
