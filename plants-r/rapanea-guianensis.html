Rapanea guianensis

Rapanea guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese evergreen, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, red pulp.

Rapanea guianensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in shaded forest environments.
