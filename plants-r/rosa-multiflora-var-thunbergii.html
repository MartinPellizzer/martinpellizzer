Rosa multiflora var. thunbergii

Rosa multiflora var. thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets and its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces red-orange hips, which are ornamental and contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine.

This variety is known for its vigorous growth and adaptability to various soil types.
