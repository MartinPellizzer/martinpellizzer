Rosa eurytoma

Rosa eurytoma, commonly known as the Eurytoma rose, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated margins, and its stems are often covered in thorns.

Rosa eurytoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
