Rubus bayeri f. latifolius

Rubus bayeri f. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
