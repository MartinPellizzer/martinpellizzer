Rubus fruticosus subsp. foliosus

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

foliosus, commonly known as brambles or blackberries, is a shrub-like plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color.

The plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic conditions. It is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its edible fruits and has been cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
