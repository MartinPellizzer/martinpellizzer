Rubus nemorensis var. ceticus

Rubus nemorensis var. ceticus, commonly known as the Ceticus blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry native to Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
