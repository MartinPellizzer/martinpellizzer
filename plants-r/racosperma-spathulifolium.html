Racosperma spathulifolium

Racosperma spathulifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with oval, broad leaflets, and produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
