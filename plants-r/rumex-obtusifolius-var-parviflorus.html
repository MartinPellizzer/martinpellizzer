Rumex obtusifolius var. parviflorus

Rumex obtusifolius var. parviflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadside banks, meadows, and wetlands. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and reddish veins, and it produces small, clustered flowers in the spring. Its flowers are usually greenish-white and have a distinctive two-lipped structure.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and is often found in temperate regions.
