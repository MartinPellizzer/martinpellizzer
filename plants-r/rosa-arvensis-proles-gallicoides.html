Rosa arvensis proles gallicoides

Rosa arvensis proles gallicoides, commonly known as the French garlic rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces small, red-orange hips that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often grown for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
