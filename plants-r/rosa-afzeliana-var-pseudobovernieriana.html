Rosa afzeliana var. pseudobovernieriana

Rosa afzeliana var. pseudobovernieriana, commonly known as the African rose or mountain rose, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
