Rubus ledermannii var. serrulatus

Rubus ledermannii var. serrulatus, commonly known as the serrated blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

The serrated edges of its leaves give it its botanical name, distinguishing it from other Rubus species.
