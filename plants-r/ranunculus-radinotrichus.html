Ranunculus radinotrichus

Ranunculus radinotrichus, commonly known as the hairy bittercress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as along roadsides or in fields. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

Ranunculus radinotrichus is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
