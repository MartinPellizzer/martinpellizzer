Rumex nikkoensis

Rumex nikkoensis, commonly known as Nikko sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and reddish stems. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender, upright stalks. Its leaves are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor due to the presence of oxalic acid.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
