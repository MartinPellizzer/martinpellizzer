Rubus × luteipes

Rubus × luteipes, commonly known as the yellow bramble, is a hybrid plant species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems.

It typically features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with five petals and numerous stamens, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This species is native to regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in woodland and hedgerow environments.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
