Rubus subhirtus

Rubus subhirtus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry.

It is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is known for its edible, though often tart, red berries. Its leaves are usually serrated and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Rubus subhirtus is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, where it thrives in partial shade.
