Rubus sprengelii subsp. arrhenii

Rubus sprengelii subsp.

arrhenii, commonly known as the white-berried blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are typically pale green to white, and the fruit is a aggregate of small, juicy drupes that are usually white or yellowish. This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
