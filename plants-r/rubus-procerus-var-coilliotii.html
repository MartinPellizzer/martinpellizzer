Rubus procerus var. coilliotii

Rubus procerus var. coilliotii, commonly known as the Coilliot's bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruits, which are edible, are similar to raspberries in texture but have a more acidic flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in moist, shaded forest habitats.
