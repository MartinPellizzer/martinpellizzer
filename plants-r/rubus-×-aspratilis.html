Rubus × aspratilis

Rubus × aspratilis, commonly known as the brambleberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus species, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
