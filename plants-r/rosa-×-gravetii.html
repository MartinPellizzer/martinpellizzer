Rosa × gravetii

Rosa × gravetii, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a hybrid shrub that belongs to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red-orange hips that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds and other animals. Native to the eastern United States, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and hedging.
