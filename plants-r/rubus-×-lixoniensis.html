Rubus × lixoniensis

Rubus × lixoniensis, commonly known as the Lixoniensis blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental and culinary value, it is cultivated in gardens and for local food production.
