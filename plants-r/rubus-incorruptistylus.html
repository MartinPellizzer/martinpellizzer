Rubus incorruptistylus

Rubus incorruptistylus, commonly known as the incorruptible raspberry, is a rare species of bramble native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, usually pink or red flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are slightly tart and have a unique flavor profile. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is distinguished by its inflorescence structure and leaf morphology.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
