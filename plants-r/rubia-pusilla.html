Rubia pusilla

Rubia pusilla, commonly known as dwarf balm or lesser bindweed, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with slender stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Rubia pusilla is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and along stream banks.

It is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential soothing properties.
