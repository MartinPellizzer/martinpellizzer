Rubus fruticosus subsp. plicatus

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

plicatus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

This subspecies is valued for its edible fruits and its role in ecological systems.
