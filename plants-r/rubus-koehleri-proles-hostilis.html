Rubus koehleri proles hostilis

Rubus koehleri proles hostilis, commonly known as the hostile bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and the fruit is a red, edible berry.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
