Rhododendron luteum var. macranthum

Rhododendron luteum var. macranthum, commonly known as the golden rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.
