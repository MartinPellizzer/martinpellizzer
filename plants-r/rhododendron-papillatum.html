Rhododendron papillatum

Rhododendron papillatum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan and parts of China.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.

Rhododendron papillatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its aesthetic beauty in horticultural settings.
