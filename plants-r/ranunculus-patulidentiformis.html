Ranunculus patulidentiformis

Ranunculus patulidentiformis, commonly known as the broad-toothed buttercup, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the species is known for its delicate, herbaceous appearance.

Ranunculus patulidentiformis is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants in its native habitat.
