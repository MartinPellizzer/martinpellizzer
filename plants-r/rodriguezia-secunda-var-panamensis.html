Rodriguezia secunda var. panamensis

Rodriguezia secunda var. panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian rodriguezia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, giving it a dramatic and ornamental appearance. The plant produces vibrant red flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by birds and insects. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
