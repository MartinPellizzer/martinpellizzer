Rosa decurtatella

Rosa decurtatella, commonly known as the dwarf pink, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pink to red blooms in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge.

Rosa decurtatella is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
