Rhododendron lusidusculum

Rhododendron lusidusculum, commonly known as the silverleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
