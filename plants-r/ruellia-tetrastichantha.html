Ruellia tetrastichantha

Ruellia tetrastichantha, commonly known as the Mexican bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stem, and are attractive to pollinators.

Ruellia tetrastichantha thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
