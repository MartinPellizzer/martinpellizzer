Rosularia lineata

Rosularia lineata, commonly known as the silver rose or silver thyme, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has fleshy, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
