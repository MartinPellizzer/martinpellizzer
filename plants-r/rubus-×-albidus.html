Rubus × albidus

Rubus × albidus, commonly known as the white-bracted raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its white bracts and small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant, white flowers in the spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated for its edible, though tart, berries.
