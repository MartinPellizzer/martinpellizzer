Rhus inamoena

Rhus inamoena, commonly known as the Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnately arranged with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that contain one or two seeds. Rhus inamoena thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
