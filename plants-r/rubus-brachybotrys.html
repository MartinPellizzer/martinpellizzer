Rubus brachybotrys

Rubus brachybotrys, commonly known as the short-stalked blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems, compound leaves, and small, dark purple to black fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and they appear in clusters during the spring.

The berries are edible but often have a tart flavor and are used in culinary applications.
