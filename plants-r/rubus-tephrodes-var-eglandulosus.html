Rubus tephrodes var. eglandulosus

Rubus tephrodes var. eglandulosus, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant white flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and its role in local ecosystems.
