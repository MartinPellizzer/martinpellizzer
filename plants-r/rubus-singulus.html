Rubus singulus

Rubus singulus, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

Rubus singulus is also valued in ecological settings for its role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
