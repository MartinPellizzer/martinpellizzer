Ribes rubrum

Ribes rubrum, commonly known as red currant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, tart red berries that are rich in antioxidants and used in culinary applications. The plant has compound leaves with five to nine elliptical leaflets and pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and hedgerows.

Its berries are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
