Rubus koeleri

Rubus koeleri, commonly known as the Koeleri raspberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and local ecosystems.
