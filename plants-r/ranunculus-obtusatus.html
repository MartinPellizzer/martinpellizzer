Ranunculus obtusatus

Ranunculus obtusatus, commonly known as the bluntleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia and North America.

It features bright yellow flowers with 5-15 petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
