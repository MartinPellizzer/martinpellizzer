Rosa viscaria var. veridica

Rosa viscaria var. veridica, commonly known as the greenleaf rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its unique foliage and floral features.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are a distinguishing feature, being a deep green and slightly hairy, which helps in distinguishing it from other rose varieties.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic and hardy nature.
