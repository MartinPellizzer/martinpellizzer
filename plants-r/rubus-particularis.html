Rubus particularis

Rubus particularis, commonly known as the rare blackberry species, is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It typically grows in temperate regions with moist, well-drained soils, often in woodland or shrubland habitats. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a distinctive, slightly tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Rubus particularis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
