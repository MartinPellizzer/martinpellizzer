Rubus setigerus

Rubus setigerus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry bush, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small white flowers that develop into aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

Rubus setigerus is an important plant for both wildlife and human consumption, particularly for its edible fruits.
