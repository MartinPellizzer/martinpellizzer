Rubus rivularis var. falcatus

Rubus rivularis var. falcatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
