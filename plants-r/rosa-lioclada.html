Rosa lioclada

Rosa lioclada, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and thorny branches. The plant produces fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while its fruits are hairy follicles containing numerous seeds.

Rosa lioclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
