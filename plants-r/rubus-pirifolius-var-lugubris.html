Rubus pirifolius var. lugubris

Rubus pirifolius var. lugubris, commonly known as the sorrowberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
