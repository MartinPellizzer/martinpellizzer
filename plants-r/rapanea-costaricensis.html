Rapanea costaricensis

Rapanea costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican holly, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves and small, white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that contains a single seed.

Rapanea costaricensis is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
