Reseda fruticulosa var. baetica

Reseda fruticulosa var. baetica, commonly known as Spanish catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in Mediterranean ecosystems.
