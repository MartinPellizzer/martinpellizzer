Ravenea sambiranensis

Ravenea sambiranensis, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo palm, is a striking plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves. The plant features a clumping growth habit and produces clusters of small, white flowers that develop into red, fleshy fruits. Ravenea sambiranensis is well adapted to tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
