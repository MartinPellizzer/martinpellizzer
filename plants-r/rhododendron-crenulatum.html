Rhododendron crenulatum

Rhododendron crenulatum, commonly known as the crested rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive wavy edge, while the flowers range in color from white to pink and purple.

Rhododendron crenulatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
