Ranunculus bullatus

Ranunculus bullatus, commonly known as the bull's eye buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, petal-like sepals and numerous stamens. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive dark center, resembling a bull's eye. It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ranunculus bullatus is known for its delicate, herbaceous foliage and is often found in temperate climates.
