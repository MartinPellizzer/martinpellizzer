Rubus affinis f. septemnata

Rubus affinis f. septemnata, commonly known as the seven-lobed blackberry, is a botanical variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive seven-lobed leaves, which set it apart from other Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
