Rubus fuscus var. mucronidens

Rubus fuscus var. mucronidens, commonly known as the blunt-tipped blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubiaceae family.
