Rubus notatus var. ortus

Rubus notatus var. ortus, commonly known as the variegated blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring a mix of green and white or yellow variegation on the leaves. It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically smaller than those of the standard Rubus notatus. The plant is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates, often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

It has a shallow root system and is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice for home gardens.
