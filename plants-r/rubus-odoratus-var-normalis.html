Rubus odoratus var. normalis

Rubus odoratus var. normalis, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The berries are edible, though some varieties may have a mild tangy flavor.
