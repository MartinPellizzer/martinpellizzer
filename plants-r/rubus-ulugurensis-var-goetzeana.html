Rubus ulugurensis var. goetzeana

Rubus ulugurensis var. goetzeana, commonly known as the Mongolian blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its berries are valued for both culinary use and traditional medicinal practices in local communities.
