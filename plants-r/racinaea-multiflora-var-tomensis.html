Racinaea multiflora var. tomensis

Racinaea multiflora var. tomensis, commonly known as the Tomensis variety of the multiflora racinaea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets, and its root system is known for its medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
