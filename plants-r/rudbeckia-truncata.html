Rudbeckia truncata

Rudbeckia truncata, commonly known as the cutleaf coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and truncate at the ends. The plant produces showy yellow-orange flower heads with a prominent central disk, typical of the genus Rudbeckia. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Rudbeckia truncata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
