Rhamnus lycioides subsp. laderoi

Rhamnus lycioides subsp.

laderoi, commonly known as the desert buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its small, glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
