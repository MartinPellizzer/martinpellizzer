Rosa popekii

Rosa popekii, commonly known as the Popekii rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its dense, thornless stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Rosa popekii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
