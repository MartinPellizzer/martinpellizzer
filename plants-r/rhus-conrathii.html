Rhus conrathii

Rhus conrathii, commonly known as the red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown stems that become more pronounced in the winter. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries that attract birds. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

Rhus conrathii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
