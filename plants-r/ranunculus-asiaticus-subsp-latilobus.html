Ranunculus asiaticus subsp. latilobus

Ranunculus asiaticus subsp.

latilobus, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ranunculaceae. It is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East, including parts of Iran and Turkey.

The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with 5-7 petals and a hairy, upright stem. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and delicate appearance.
