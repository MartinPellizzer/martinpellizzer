Rhododendron kyushuense

Rhododendron kyushuense, commonly known as the Kyushu rhododendron, is a species native to the island of Kyushu in Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish in color, appearing in clusters during late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron kyushuense thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
