Rotmannia

Rotmannia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Rotmannia alnifolia, which is native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa. The plants are characterized by their striking blue or purple ray flowers and silver-green foliage. They thrive in sandy or saline soils and are often found in maritime environments.

Rotmannia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
