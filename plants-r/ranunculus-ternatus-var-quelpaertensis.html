Ranunculus ternatus var. quelpaertensis

Ranunculus ternatus var. quelpaertensis, commonly known as the Korean buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Korea.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of brightness to the landscape.

The variety is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to the typical Ranunculus ternatus.
