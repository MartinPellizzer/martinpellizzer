Rubus hypoleucus subsp. chaboissaei

Rubus hypoleucus subsp.

chaboissaei, commonly known as the Chaboissai raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of East Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its erect or spreading growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe.

The plant features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some use in traditional medicine.
