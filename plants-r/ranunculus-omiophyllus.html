Ranunculus omiophyllus

Ranunculus omiophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
