Rubus discolor subsp. egregius

Rubus discolor subsp.

egregius, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black fruits that are typically larger than those of the typical Rubus discolor.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink to white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it a valued plant in both wild and cultivated settings.
