Rubus cryptadenus var. pyramidalis

Rubus cryptadenus var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It produces small, dark red berries that are typically tart and less commonly consumed fresh. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
