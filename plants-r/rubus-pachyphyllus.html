Rubus pachyphyllus

Rubus pachyphyllus, commonly known as the thick-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and produces dark purple to black berries. The plant thrives in rocky, dry habitats and is often found in coastal regions. Its fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a valuable plant for both wildlife and human consumption.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
