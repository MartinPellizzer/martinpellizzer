Rosa canina var. orthacantha

Rosa canina var. orthacantha, commonly known as the bristly dog rose, is a variant of the common dog rose.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and pink to red flowers. The plant produces oval, yellow-orange hips that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
