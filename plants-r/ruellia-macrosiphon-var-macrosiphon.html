Ruellia macrosiphon var. macrosiphon

Ruellia macrosiphon var. macrosiphon, commonly known as the broad-lipped ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil conditions.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
