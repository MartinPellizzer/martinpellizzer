Ranunculus Pilipes

The ranunculus pilipes, also known as the pilose buttercup, is a striking member of the ranunculaceae family. This plant is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers and hairy stems that give it its scientific name. Native to parts of europe and western asia, it thrives in moist, shady environments. its bright yellow blooms often contrast sharply with its dark green foliage. despite its beauty, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
