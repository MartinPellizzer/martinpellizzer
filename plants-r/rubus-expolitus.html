Rubus expolitus

Rubus expolitus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry.

This species is a shrub native to parts of Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white flowers. The leaves are compound, with serrated edges and a hairy texture, contributing to its common name. The plant produces red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
