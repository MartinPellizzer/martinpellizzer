Racosperma leiophyllum

Racosperma leiophyllum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are simple and often have a smooth texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for native pollinators.
