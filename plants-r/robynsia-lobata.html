Robynsia lobata

Robynsia lobata, commonly known as the red-flowered larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of showy, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple and red. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins, and its flowers are typically arranged in racemose inflorescences. Robynsia lobata thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.
