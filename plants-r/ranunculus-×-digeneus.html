Ranunculus × digeneus

Ranunculus × digeneus, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to deep red, often with a dark central eye. The plant has trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated by insects, making it an important plant in ecological systems.
