Racosperma pachyphyllum

Racosperma pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and yellow flowers that appear in globular clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its thick leaves help it conserve water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
