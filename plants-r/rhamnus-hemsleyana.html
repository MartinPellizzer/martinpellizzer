Rhamnus hemsleyana

Rhamnus hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley's buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
