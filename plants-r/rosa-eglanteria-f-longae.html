Rosa eglanteria f. longae

Rosa eglanteria f. longae, commonly known as the long-stemmed prickly rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa eglanteria species.

It is characterized by its elongated, thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

This variety is well adapted to colder climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
