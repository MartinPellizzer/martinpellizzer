Rubus radulicaulis var. subgracilior

Rubus radulicaulis var. subgracilior, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its slender, arching stems and smaller, more delicate leaves compared to the typical Rubus radulicaulis. The flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals and numerous stamens. The fruit is a small, dark purple berry that ripens in late summer.

It is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
