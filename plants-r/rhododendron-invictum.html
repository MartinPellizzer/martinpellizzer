Rhododendron invictum

Rhododendron invictum, commonly known as the California rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to red flowers in the spring.

Rhododendron invictum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in rocky or coastal areas.
