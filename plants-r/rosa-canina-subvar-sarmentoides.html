Rosa canina subvar. sarmentoides

Rosa canina subvar. sarmentoides, commonly known as the trailing dog rose, is a shrub-like plant characterized by its long, flexible stems that trail along the ground.

It produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in open woodlands and scrub habitats. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the fruit is a red, hairy rosehip.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
