Rosa carlstenensis

Rosa carlstenensis, commonly known as the Swedish rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Scandinavia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features pinnately compound leaves and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late summer.

Its flowers are often followed by orange-red hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Rosa carlstenensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
