Rubus caesius subsp. lamottei

Rubus caesius subsp.

lamottei, commonly known as the Lamotte's blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its flowers are white with five petals and appear in the spring.
