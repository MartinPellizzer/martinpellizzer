Rubus coeruleicaulis

Rubus coeruleicaulis, commonly known as the blue-stemmed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, bluish-green stems and small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are clustered in the leaf axils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval to elliptical leaflets that have serrated edges.

The species is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and along streambanks.
