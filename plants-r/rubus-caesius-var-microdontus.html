Rubus caesius var. microdontus

Rubus caesius var. microdontus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, thorny stems and dense clusters of dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in jams and wines.
