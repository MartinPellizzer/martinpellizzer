Rubus jizogatakensis

Rubus jizogatakensis, commonly known as the Jizogata raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Turkey.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a temperate climate with adequate sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
