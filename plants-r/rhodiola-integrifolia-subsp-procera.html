Rhodiola integrifolia subsp. procera

Rhodiola integrifolia subsp.

procera, commonly known as the alpine rhodiola, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia. It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth form and dark green, fleshy leaves that are adapted to cold and high-altitude environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and tundra habitats. Rhodiola integrifolia subsp.

procera is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
