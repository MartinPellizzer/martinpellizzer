Rhabdophyllum umbellatum

Rhabdophyllum umbellatum, commonly known as the umbrella lily, is a unique member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, umbrella-like inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers. Native to South Africa, this plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

It is a perennial plant that blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a notable addition to rock gardens and arid landscapes.
