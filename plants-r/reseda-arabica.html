Reseda arabica

Reseda arabica, commonly known as common reseda, is an annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped and hairy. Reseda arabica is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides due to its adaptability.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
