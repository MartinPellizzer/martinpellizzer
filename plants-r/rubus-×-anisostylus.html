Rubus × anisostylus

Rubus × anisostylus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, with a serrated margin and a hairy underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
