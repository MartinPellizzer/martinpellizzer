Ranunculus flammula subsp. flammula

Ranunculus flammula subsp.

flammula, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, dark green stem that often lies close to the ground.

The plant has palmately divided leaves that are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom early in the spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.
