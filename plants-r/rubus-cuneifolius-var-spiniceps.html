Rubus cuneifolius var. spiniceps

Rubus cuneifolius var. spiniceps, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
