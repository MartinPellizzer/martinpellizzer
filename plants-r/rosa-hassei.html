Rosa hassei

Rosa hassei, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are usually white or pale pink, opening at night and closing by dawn. Rosa hassei is known for its fragrant blooms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
