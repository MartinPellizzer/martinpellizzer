Rigiolepis salicifolia

Rigiolepis salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are alternate and lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of green or yellow, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Due to its drought tolerance and rapid growth, it is often considered an invasive species in some regions.
