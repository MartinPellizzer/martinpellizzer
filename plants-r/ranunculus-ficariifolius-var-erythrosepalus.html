Ranunculus ficariifolius var. erythrosepalus

Ranunculus ficariifolius var. erythrosepalus, commonly known as the red-sepal ranunculus, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red sepals that are often mistaken for petals, giving it a showy appearance. The plant typically features simple, trifoliate leaves with a finely toothed margin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Asia.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique floral structure and vivid coloration, making it a notable species in horticultural collections.
