Rudgea schomburgkiana

Rudgea schomburgkiana, commonly known as the Schomburgk's rudgea, is a striking tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
