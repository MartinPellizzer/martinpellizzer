Rubus holerythrus var. nexuosus

Rubus holerythrus var. nexuosus, commonly known as the red-berried bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and is native to specific regions in North America. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and a woody, shrub-like structure.
