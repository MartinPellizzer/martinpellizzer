Rubus menkei var. lucandii

Rubus menkei var. lucandii, commonly known as the Chilean blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus menkei species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and have a high concentration of antioxidants. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions of Chile. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in dense, prickly hairs.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
