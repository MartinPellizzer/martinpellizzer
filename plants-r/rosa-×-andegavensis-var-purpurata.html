Rosa × andegavensis var. purpurata

Rosa × andegavensis var. purpurata, commonly known as the Purple Beauty rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple-pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual appeal to gardens. The plant typically has thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
