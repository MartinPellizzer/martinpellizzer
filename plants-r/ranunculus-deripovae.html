Ranunculus deripovae

Ranunculus deripovae, commonly known as the Armenian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features simple, palmately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow or orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
