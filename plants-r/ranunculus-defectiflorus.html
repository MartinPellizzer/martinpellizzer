Ranunculus defectiflorus

Ranunculus defectiflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it has a short, hairy stem.

The species is noted for its small size and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.
