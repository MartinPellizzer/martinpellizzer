Rubia mucronata

Rubia mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant features slender stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early summer.

Rubia mucronata is often found in meadows, woodlands, and other moist, well-drained habitats.
