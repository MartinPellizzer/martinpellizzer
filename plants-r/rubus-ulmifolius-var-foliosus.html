Rubus ulmifolius var. foliosus

Rubus ulmifolius var. foliosus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's wood. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and have a distinctive, slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.
