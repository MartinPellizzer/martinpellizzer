Rubus questieri subsp. elongatispinus

Rubus questieri subsp.

elongatispinus, commonly known as the long-spined blackberry, is a species native to the western United States. It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark berries that ripen in the summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique morphological traits that set it apart from other subshrub species.
