Racosperma smeringum

Racosperma smeringum, commonly known as the black wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, which makes it valuable in ecological restoration projects.

It also produces a hard, dense wood that has been historically used for timber and fuel.
