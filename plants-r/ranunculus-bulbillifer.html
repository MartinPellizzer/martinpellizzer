Ranunculus bulbillifer

Ranunculus bulbillifer, commonly known as the bulbiferous buttercup, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant is notable for its unique reproductive structure, which includes bulbils that form along the stems, allowing for vegetative propagation. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
