Rubus bertricensis

Rubus bertricensis, commonly known as the Bertricenses blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Andes Mountains in South America, specifically in regions of Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit, a dark purple to black berry, is edible and has a tart flavor.

Rubus bertricensis is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.
