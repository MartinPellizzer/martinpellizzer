Reichardia orientalis

Reichardia orientalis, commonly known as the oriental dandelion, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Reichardia orientalis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
