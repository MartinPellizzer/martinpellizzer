Rollinia uniflora

Rollinia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The fruit is a large, spiny berry that ripens to a yellow or orange color, containing a sweet, creamy pulp with a custard-like texture. It is valued for its edible fruit and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
