Rhododendron fulvum

Rhododendron fulvum, commonly known as the yellow rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a waxy coating.

Rhododendron fulvum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
