Rubus idaeus var. biflorus

Rubus idaeus var. biflorus, commonly known as the blackberry variant with two flowers, is a wild-growing shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the蔷薇科 (Rosaceae) family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and it blooms in early summer.

This variety is valued for its edible berries and is often found in moist, wooded areas.
