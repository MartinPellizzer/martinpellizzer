Ranunculus amblyolobus

Ranunculus amblyolobus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of finely divided, lobed leaves, and it produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
