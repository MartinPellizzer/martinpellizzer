Racosperma torticarpum

Racosperma torticarpum, commonly known as the twisted pod acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, twisted pods that contain seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

Racosperma torticarpum is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in revegetation projects.
