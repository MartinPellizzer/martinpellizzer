Rabdosia × arakii

Rabdosia × arakii, commonly known as the hybrid blue sage, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a pleasant, mint-like fragrance.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a mix of traits from its parent species, Rabdosia kiusiana and Rabdosia rubra.
