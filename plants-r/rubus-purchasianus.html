Rubus purchasianus

Rubus purchasianus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a shrub native to western North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and culinary purposes.
