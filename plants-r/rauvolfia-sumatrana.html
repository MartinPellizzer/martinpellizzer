Rauvolfia sumatrana

Rauvolfia sumatrana, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its roots contain alkaloids, such as reserpine, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Rauvolfia sumatrana is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in the treatment of hypertension.
