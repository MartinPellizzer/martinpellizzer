Ranunculus californicus var. cuneatus

Ranunculus californicus var. cuneatus, commonly known as the California buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals and numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided with serrated edges, and it produces a distinctive, short-stemmed inflorescence.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is often found in riparian zones.
