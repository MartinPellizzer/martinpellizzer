Rhamnus alaternus f. tournefortii

Rhamnus alaternus f. tournefortii, commonly known as the Algerian buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by black, egg-shaped berries. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
