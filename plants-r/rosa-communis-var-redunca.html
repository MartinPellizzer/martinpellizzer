Rosa communis var. redunca

Rosa communis var. redunca, commonly known as the double-flowered rose, is a cultivar characterized by its dense, layered petals that create a full, ornamental bloom.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to various regions in Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers range in color from white to deep red, depending on the specific variety.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and fragrant blossoms.
