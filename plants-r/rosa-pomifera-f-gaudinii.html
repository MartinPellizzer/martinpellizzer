Rosa pomifera f. gaudinii

Rosa pomifera f. gaudinii, commonly known as the hairy rose or the woolly rose, is a unique cultivar of the Rosa pomifera species, characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, edible fruits.

The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and they appear in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. The fruits, which resemble large, fuzzy tomatoes, are not only visually striking but also contain a sweet, edible pulp.

This cultivar is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its potential culinary uses.
