Rubus hypoleucus subsp. macrophylloides

Rubus hypoleucus subsp.

macrophylloides, commonly known as the large-leaf everlet, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruits, known as everlets, are edible and have a sweet, slightly tart flavor.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in local habitats.
