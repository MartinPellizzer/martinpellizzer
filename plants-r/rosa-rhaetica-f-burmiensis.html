Rosa rhaetica f. burmiensis

Rosa rhaetica f. burmiensis, commonly known as the Burmese rose, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a subspecies of Rosa rhaetica, characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in shaded, forested environments. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves, and it produces red-orange hips in the fall.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
