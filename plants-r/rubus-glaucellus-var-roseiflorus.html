Rubus glaucellus var. roseiflorus

Rubus glaucellus var. roseiflorus, commonly known as the rose-flowered blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when ripe. Its flowers are pink to rose-colored, adding ornamental value to the plant.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.
