Ribes magellanicum subsp. parviflorum

Ribes magellanicum subsp.

parviflorum, commonly known as the Magellanic gooseberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina. It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
