Racosperma juliferum subsp. curvinervium

Racosperma juliferum subsp.

curvinervium, commonly known as the broad-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with broad, flat leaflets and a distinctive, often yellowish, floral display.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
