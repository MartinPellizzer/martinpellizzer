Ribes grossularia var. hybridum

Ribes grossularia var. hybridum, commonly known as the hybrid gooseberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of flowering plants in the family Grossulariaceae. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with thorny stems and alternate, ovate leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, edible berries that range in color from red to yellow.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in horticulture.
