Rosa abstenta

Rosa abstenta, commonly known as the mountain rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in cooler, moist environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Rosa abstenta is also valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
