Rhaphiolepis indica var. tashiroi

Rhaphiolepis indica var. tashiroi, commonly known as the Japanese rosebay or Tashiro's rosebay, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that provide a dense, attractive foliage. The plant produces clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers in spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
