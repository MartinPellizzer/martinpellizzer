Rotheca myricoides subsp. myricoides

Rotheca myricoides subsp.

myricoides, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a wetland indicator species.
