Rubus cupanianus subsp. bertolonii

Rubus cupanianus subsp.

bertolonii, commonly known as the Bertoloni's raspberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, similar to other raspberries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is occasionally grown in botanical gardens and private collections.
