Racosperma brownianum var. obscurum

Racosperma brownianum var. obscurum, commonly known as the dark raceme wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
