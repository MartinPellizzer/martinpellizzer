Ranunculus songaricus var. partitus

Ranunculus songaricus var. partitus, commonly known as the spotted buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with distinctive black spots on the petals. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, and it typically grows in moist, open habitats. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in temperate climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
