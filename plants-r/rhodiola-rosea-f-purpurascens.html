Rhodiola rosea f. purpurascens

Rhodiola rosea f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered rhodiola, is a rare and distinctive variant of the rhodiola plant.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which contrast with its dark green, needle-like leaves. This subspecies is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant is known for its adaptability to harsh climates and its ability to tolerate extreme temperatures and low nutrient soils.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its compact growth habit and the presence of red or purple hues in its floral structures.
