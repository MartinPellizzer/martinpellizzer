Rosa × campanulata

Rosa × campanulata, commonly known as the Campanula rose.

It is a hybrid rose species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis. This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, campanulate (bell-shaped) flowers. The petals are usually a soft pink to white color, and the plant is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively disease-resistant.
