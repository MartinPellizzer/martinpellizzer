Rubus newbouldii

Rubus newbouldii, commonly known as Newbould's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest margins, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local culinary traditions.
