Rubus tiliaster

Rubus tiliaster, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
