Rosa alpina f. levipes

Rosa alpina f. levipes, commonly known as the Alpine rose or alpine pink, is a subspecies of the alpine rose native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. The plant produces showy, single or semi-double pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and beauty.
