Ranunculus dasycarpus var. leiocarpus

Ranunculus dasycarpus var. leiocarpus, commonly known as the smooth-fruited buttercup, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soil. The plant features palmately divided leaves and yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its fruits are distinctive, consisting of hairy, elongated achenes that are released when mature.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
