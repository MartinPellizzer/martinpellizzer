Rubus tomentosus f. obovatifolius

Rubus tomentosus f. obovatifolius, commonly known as the woolly blackberry variant with ovate leaves, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly silver-gray foliage and thorny stems. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a soft, fuzzy texture. It produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often tart.

This plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions.
