Rosa horridula var. subcandida

Rosa horridula var. subcandida, commonly known as the white-stemmed prickly rose, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in arid environments.
