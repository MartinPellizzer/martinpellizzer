Rhamnus transsilvanica

Rhamnus transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and smooth, reddish bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by black, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
