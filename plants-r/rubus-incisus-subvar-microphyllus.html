Rubus incisus subvar. microphyllus

Rubus incisus subvar. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The species is often found in open woodlands, grasslands, and disturbed areas.
