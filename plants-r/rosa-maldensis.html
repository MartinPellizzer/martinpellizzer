Rosa maldensis

Rosa maldensis, commonly known as the Malden rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in the spring and summer seasons. Its botanical characteristics include thorny stems and an extensive root system adapted to the rugged mountainous terrain.

Rosa maldensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
