Ranunculus hybridus f. grandiflorus

Ranunculus hybridus f. grandiflorus, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a flowering plant characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with prominent central sepals.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its distinctive, cup-shaped blooms that can reach up to 5 inches in diameter. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It has palmate leaves with three to five lobes and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations in different cultivars.
