Reseda lanceolata var. trifida

Reseda lanceolata var. trifida, commonly known as the trifid sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems that often lie close to the ground. The leaves are lanceolate and may be trifid, meaning they are divided into three parts, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

This plant is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
