Rosa nutkana var. macdougalii

Rosa nutkana var. macdougalii, commonly known as the MacDougal's rose, is a shrub-like species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy foliage that provides a striking contrast to its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators.
