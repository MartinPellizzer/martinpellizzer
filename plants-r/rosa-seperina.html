Rosa seperina

Rosa seperina, commonly known as the Peruvian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to reddish-pink flowers during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets arranged oppositely along the stem.

Rosa seperina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
