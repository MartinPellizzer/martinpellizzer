Rhododendron araiophyllum subsp. araiophyllum

Rhododendron araiophyllum subsp.

araiophyllum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers.

The plant typically produces pink to red flowers in late spring, which are borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
