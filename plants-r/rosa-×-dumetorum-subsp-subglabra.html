Rosa × dumetorum subsp. subglabra

Rosa × dumetorum subsp.

subglabra, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with trailing stems that often root at the nodes.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated leaflets that are covered in a fine, white down. The flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
