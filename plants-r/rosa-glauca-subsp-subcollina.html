Rosa glauca subsp. subcollina

Rosa glauca subsp.

subcollina, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, dark green leaves.

The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
