Rubus tereticaulis var. subserpens

Rubus tereticaulis var. subserpens, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, dark red berries that are edible. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are covered in fine, white hairs, and it has a woody structure with thorny branches.

This variety is closely related to other Rubus species and is often found in association with other shrubs in its native habitat.
