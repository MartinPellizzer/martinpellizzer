Rubus schummelii var. hypomalacus

Rubus schummelii var. hypomalacus, commonly known as the Schumacher's raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry species native to South America.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typically featuring three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red when ripe, though they may vary in color depending on the cultivar. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is occasionally grown in gardens and botanical collections.
