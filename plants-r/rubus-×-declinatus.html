Rubus × declinatus

Rubus × declinatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically smaller than those of other Rubus species. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with a leathery texture, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
