Rosa acicularis var. glandulosa

Rosa acicularis var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular thorned rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its sharp, glandular-tipped thorns and narrow, needle-like leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Rosa genus.
