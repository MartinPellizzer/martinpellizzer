Rosa tomentella var. stantiensis

Rosa tomentella var. stantiensis, commonly known as the Stantons hairy rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compact growth habit, making it well adapted to harsh, alpine environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
