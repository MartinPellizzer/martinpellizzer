Rubus schleicheri var. mollifoliatus

Rubus schleicheri var. mollifoliatus, commonly known as the soft-leaved bramble, is a shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a soft, velvety texture. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red, edible berries.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
