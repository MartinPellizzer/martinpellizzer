Rosa glandulosa

Rosa glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are compound, with small, glandular dots that give it its common name.

Rosa glandulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh climates.
