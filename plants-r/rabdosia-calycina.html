Rabdosia calycina

Rabdosia calycina, commonly known as the pink-flowered rabdosia, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, often arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Rabdosia calycina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
