Rubus rhombifolius var. vaczyi

Rubus rhombifolius var. vaczyi, commonly known as the Vaczy's raspberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardening.
