Rosa pomifera var. peccienensis

Rosa pomifera var. peccienensis, commonly known as the Mexican rose or the apple rose, is a rare and unique species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive fruit, which resembles an apple in appearance and is edible, though not widely consumed. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
