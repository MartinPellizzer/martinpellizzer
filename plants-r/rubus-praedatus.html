Rubus praedatus

Rubus praedatus, commonly known as the predated raspberry, is a species of bramble native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit, a red raspberry-like berry, is edible and contains numerous small seeds.
