Rubus idaeus var. inermis

Rubus idaeus var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth raspberry, is a variant of the red raspberry plant characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and fruits.

It is native to eastern North America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red, though some varieties may have yellow or orange berries. It is a shrub with compound leaves and thorny stems, though the "inermis" variety is notable for its reduced thorniness.

This plant is often cultivated for its edible berries and is valued in both wild and agricultural settings.
