Restio virgatus

Restio virgatus, commonly known as the silver restio, is a grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Restio virgatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
