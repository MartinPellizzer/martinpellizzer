Rhododendron probum

Rhododendron probum, commonly known as the evergreen rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are leathery and remain green throughout the year, contributing to its evergreen nature.

Rhododendron probum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
