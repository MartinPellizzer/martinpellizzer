Rosa scabrata f. subrotunda

Rosa scabrata f. subrotunda, commonly known as the rough rose or subrounded rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa scabrata species.

It is characterized by its slightly rough, hairy stems and foliage, which are typical of the scabrata species. The flowers are usually pink to red in color, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom in late spring to early summer. This form is notable for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for temperate climates.
