Rhamnus flavescens

Rhamnus flavescens, commonly known as the yellow buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and features small, oval leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by small, dark blue berries in the fall. It has a woody stem with thorns and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
