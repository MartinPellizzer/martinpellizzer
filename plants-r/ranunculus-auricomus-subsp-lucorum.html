Ranunculus auricomus subsp. lucorum

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

lucorum, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features showy, bright yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petal-like sepals, often with a distinctive orange or red central vein.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
