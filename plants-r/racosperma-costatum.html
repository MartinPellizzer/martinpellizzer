Racosperma costatum

Racosperma costatum, commonly known as the coastal raceme acacia, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bipinnate leaves with a distinctive, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange floral racemes, which are borne along the upper parts of the stems. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and heathlands.

Racosperma costatum is adapted to salt-laden winds and is an important component of coastal ecosystems.
