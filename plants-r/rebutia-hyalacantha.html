Rebutia hyalacantha

Rebutia hyalacantha, commonly known as the glassy rebutia, is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in small, white, woolly spines. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it is adapted to arid environments.

Rebutia hyalacantha is often cultivated as a decorative cactus due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
