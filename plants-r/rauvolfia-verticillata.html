Rauvolfia verticillata

Rauvolfia verticillata, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that contain the seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and ecological studies.
