Ranunculus geraniifolius subsp. gouanii

Ranunculus geraniifolius subsp.

gouanii, commonly known as the Gouan's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves that resemble those of geraniums, and its flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive central greenish area.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in gardens in regions with a suitable climate.
