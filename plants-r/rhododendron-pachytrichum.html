Rhododendron pachytrichum

Rhododendron pachytrichum, commonly known as the thick-hairy rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

Rhododendron pachytrichum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
