Rosa macounii

Rosa macounii, commonly known as the Macoun rose, is a species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink to red in color, blooming in the summer. Rosa macounii produces hips, which are red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
