Rubus semihedycarpus

Rubus semihedycarpus, commonly known as the semi-hedycarpus raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, similar to other raspberry species. Its leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
