Ranunculus inchoatus

Ranunculus inchoatus, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has delicate, trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Ranunculaceae family.
