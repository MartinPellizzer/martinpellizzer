Rubus jactuosus

Rubus jactuosus, commonly known as the wandering raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are pink to white in color.

The fruit, which ripens in late summer, is often consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves.
