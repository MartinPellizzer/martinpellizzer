Rubus fuscus var. leptacanthus

Rubus fuscus var. leptacanthus, commonly known as the slender-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are usually less than 1 cm in diameter. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

The flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by the characteristic bristly fruiting structures.
