Rhamphidia discoidea

Rhamphidia discoidea, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are often described as resembling spiders due to their elongated, curling petals and sepals.

Rhamphidia discoidea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
