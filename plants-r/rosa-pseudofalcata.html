Rosa pseudofalcata

Rosa pseudofalcata, commonly known as the narrowleaf dogwood rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink to red flowers in late spring. Its fruit is a red, fuzzy hip that contains several seeds.

Rosa pseudofalcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
