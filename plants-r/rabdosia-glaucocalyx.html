Rabdosia glaucocalyx

Rabdosia glaucocalyx, commonly known as blue-veined Rabdosia, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery blue-gray bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and compact growth habit.
