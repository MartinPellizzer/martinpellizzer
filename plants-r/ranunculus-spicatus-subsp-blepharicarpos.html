Ranunculus spicatus subsp. blepharicarpos

Ranunculus spicatus subsp.

blepharicarpos, commonly known as the scarlet water crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its bright red or pink flowers, which typically bloom in early spring.

The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that grow on slender stems, often emerging from water or damp soil. It is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure and is valued for its ornamental appeal in aquatic gardens.
