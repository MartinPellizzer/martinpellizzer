Rhododendron henanense subsp. henanense

Rhododendron henanense subsp.

henanense, commonly known as the Henan rhododendron, is a species native to China, specifically found in the Henan province. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers shaded to semi-shaded environments. It is part of the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar genera like azalea and blueberry.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
