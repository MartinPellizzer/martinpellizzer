Rubus lobaviensis

Rubus lobaviensis, commonly known as the Lobavi blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to cool climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family, sharing features with other brambles.
