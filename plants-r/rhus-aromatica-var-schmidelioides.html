Rhus aromatica var. schmidelioides

Rhus aromatica var. schmidelioides, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with 11 to 31 leaflets that are alternate and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters, followed by red, fuzzy berries that are a characteristic feature. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant fall color and unique fruiting display.
