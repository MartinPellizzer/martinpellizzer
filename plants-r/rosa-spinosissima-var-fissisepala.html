Rosa spinosissima var. fissisepala

Rosa spinosissima var. fissisepala, commonly known as the fissisepal rose, is a rare and striking cultivar of the Rosa spinosissima species.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed petals that give it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant typically produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a shrub-like rose with thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
