Remijia vaupesiana

Remijia vaupesiana, commonly known as the Amazonian orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and is protected in several national parks and reserves.
