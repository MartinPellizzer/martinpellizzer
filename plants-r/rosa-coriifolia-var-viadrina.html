Rosa coriifolia var. viadrina

Rosa coriifolia var. viadrina, commonly known as the Viadrina rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that ripen in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of rose hips.
