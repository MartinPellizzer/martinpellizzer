Rubus macrostachys var. indolatus

Rubus macrostachys var. indolatus, commonly known as the Indian raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, red, edible berries. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.

The species is also known for its hardy nature and ability to spread through vegetative reproduction.
