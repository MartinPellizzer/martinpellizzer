Rosa onoei var. hakonensis

Rosa onoei var. hakonensis, commonly known as the Hakone rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features deep green, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.
