Ricinocarpos canianus

Ricinocarpos canianus, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It produces small, woody capsules as fruits, which contain seeds used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
