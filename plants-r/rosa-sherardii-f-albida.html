Rosa sherardii f. albida

Rosa sherardii f. albida, commonly known as the white form of the Sherard's rose, is a cultivar characterized by its pale pink to white flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is a hybrid between Rosa sherardii and Rosa rugosa. The plant is typically shrub-like, with thorny stems and broad, lobed leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its delicate appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
