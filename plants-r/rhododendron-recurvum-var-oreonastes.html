Rhododendron recurvum var. oreonastes

Rhododendron recurvum var. oreonastes, commonly known as the Sierra madre rhododendron, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly California and Nevada.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
