Ranunculus kauffmannii

Ranunculus kauffmannii, commonly known as the Kauffmann's buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ranunculaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries such as Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, which bloom in early spring. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

The leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are deeply lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
