Rubus foliosus subsp. corymbosus

Rubus foliosus subsp.

corymbosus, commonly known as the clustered blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically lobed and have a rough texture.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its edible berries and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
