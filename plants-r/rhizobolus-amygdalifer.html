Rhizobolus amygdalifer

Rhizobolus amygdalifer, commonly known as the almond root fungus, is a rare and specialized plant pathogen that primarily affects almond trees.

It belongs to the family Rhizobolaceae and is characterized by its unique ability to form symbiotic relationships with the roots of certain plants. The fungus produces dark, spherical structures called sclerotia, which can survive in soil for extended periods. It thrives in temperate climates and is often associated with poorly drained soils.

Due to its limited host range and specific environmental requirements, it is not widely studied but remains an important subject in agricultural pathology.
