Rumex crispus var. kandavanicus

Rumex crispus var. kandavanicus, commonly known as the Kandavan dock, is a variant of the common dock found primarily in South Africa.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its serrated, wavy-edged leaves that are typically green with a reddish underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along riverbanks and in wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of wetland ecosystems.
