Rhamnus alpina var. libanotica

Rhamnus alpina var. libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanese buckthorn, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers in the spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions. Its fruits are small, black berries that ripen in the autumn.

This variety is often found in elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters above sea level.
