Rubus paulus

Rubus paulus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species of brambles native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Rubus paulus plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
