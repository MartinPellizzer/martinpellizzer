Ranunculus acutidentiformis

Ranunculus acutidentiformis, commonly known as the sharp-toothed buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow or white flowers with numerous stamens. Native to southern Africa, this species thrives in moist, often shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. The plant's name refers to the sharp, tooth-like margins of its leaves.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of aquatic and semi-aquatic flora.
