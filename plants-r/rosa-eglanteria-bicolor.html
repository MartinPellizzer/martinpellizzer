Rosa eglanteria bicolor

Rosa eglanteria bicolor, commonly known as the white-barked rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive white stems and pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its thorns are typically short and curved. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This rose is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
