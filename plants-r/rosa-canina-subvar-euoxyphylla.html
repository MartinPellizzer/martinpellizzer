Rosa canina subvar. euoxyphylla

Rosa canina subvar. euoxyphylla, commonly known as the European dog rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with ovate to elliptical leaflets. The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant produces bright red hips, which are the fruit, containing seeds and used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
