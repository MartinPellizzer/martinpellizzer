Rodgersia sambucifolia var. sambucifolia

Rodgersia sambucifolia var. sambucifolia, commonly known as the silver foxtail, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features large, palmately divided, silvery-green leaves that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers in late summer, which are borne on tall, upright stems. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, adding visual interest even when not in bloom.
