Rubus hirtus subvar. lilacinus

Rubus hirtus subvar. lilacinus, commonly known as the purple-leaf bramble, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves that often display a striking purple or red coloration. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, juicy berries in the summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for birds and insects.
