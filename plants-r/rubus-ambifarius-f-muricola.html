Rubus ambifarius f. muricola

Rubus ambifarius f. muricola, commonly known as the rough-fruited blackberry, is a horticultural form of the blackberry plant.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries that are typically firm and have a distinctive rough, textured surface. The plant is a deciduous shrub with arching canes that produce flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its robust flavor and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
