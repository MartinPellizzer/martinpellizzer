Raphanus longistylus

Raphanus longistylus, commonly known as the long-styled radish, is a rare and lesser-known species within the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender roots that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant typically features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and have a slightly hairy texture.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
