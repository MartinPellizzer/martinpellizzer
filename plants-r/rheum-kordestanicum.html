Rheum kordestanicum

Rheum kordestanicum, commonly known as the Karakoram rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 50 cm in length. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
