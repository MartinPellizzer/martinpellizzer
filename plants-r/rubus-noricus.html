Rubus noricus

Rubus noricus, commonly known as the Austrian raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits that mature to a deep red color. The plant produces white flowers with five petals and has trifoliate leaves composed of three leaflets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them useful in culinary applications.
