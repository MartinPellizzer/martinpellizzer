Rosa heckeliana var. parnassi

Rosa heckeliana var. parnassi, commonly known as the Parnassian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are typically covered in thorny projections. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

This variety is prized for its beauty and historical significance in horticultural cultivation.
