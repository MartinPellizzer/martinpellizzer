Rosa villosa var. mana

Rosa villosa var. mana, commonly known as the Mana rose, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features soft, fuzzy gray-green foliage and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It is a hardy species that thrives in partial to full sun and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

The variety is noted for its adaptability and is often used in gardens and landscaping.
