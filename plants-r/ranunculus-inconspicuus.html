Ranunculus inconspicuus

Ranunculus inconspicuus, commonly known as the small crowfoot, is a delicate flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps retain moisture.

Ranunculus inconspicuus is often found in the Pacific Northwest of North America and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
