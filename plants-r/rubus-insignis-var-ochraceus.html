Rubus insignis var. ochraceus

Rubus insignis var. ochraceus, commonly known as the ochraceous raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color, depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in local ecosystems as a food source for wildlife.
