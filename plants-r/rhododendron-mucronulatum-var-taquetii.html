Rhododendron mucronulatum var. taquetii

Rhododendron mucronulatum var. taquetii, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, blooming in early spring. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
