Rinorea ilicifolia

Rinorea ilicifolia, commonly known as the fragrant woodbine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, often emitting a pleasant fragrance. Rinorea ilicifolia is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
