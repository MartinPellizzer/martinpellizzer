Rubus moluccanus var. bracteosus

Rubus moluccanus var. bracteosus, commonly known as the Moluccan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its use in local cuisines.
