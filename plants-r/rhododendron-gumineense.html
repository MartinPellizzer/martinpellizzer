Rhododendron gumineense

Rhododendron gumineense, commonly known as the Gumineense rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from pink to deep red.

Rhododendron gumineense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
