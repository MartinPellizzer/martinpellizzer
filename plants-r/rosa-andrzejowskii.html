Rosa andrzejowskii

Rosa andrzejowskii, commonly known as the Andrzejowski rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has elliptical leaves with serrated edges and produces small, oval fruits called hip fruits. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

Rosa andrzejowskii is also noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
