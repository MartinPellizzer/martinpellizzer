Rubus thyrsiflorus var. chloranthus

Rubus thyrsiflorus var. chloranthus, commonly known as the yellow-tipped blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow tips on the bracts, which set it apart from other Rubus species. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
