Robeschia

Robeschia, commonly known as the "buttercup flower," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers that resemble those of the buttercup. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy surface. Robeschia species are native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and Africa.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
