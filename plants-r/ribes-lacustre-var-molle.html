Ribes lacustre var. molle

Ribes lacustre var. molle, commonly known as soft sand-verbena, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in coastal dunes and other arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
