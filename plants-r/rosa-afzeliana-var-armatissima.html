Rosa afzeliana var. armatissima

Rosa afzeliana var. armatissima, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its fruit is a red, hairy hip that contains several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
