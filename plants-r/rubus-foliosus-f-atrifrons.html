Rubus foliosus f. atrifrons

Rubus foliosus f. atrifrons, commonly known as the black-leafed bramble, is a variant of the common bramble species.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black foliage, which distinguishes it from other Rubus species. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and a dense, leathery texture.

This variety is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, thriving in partial shade to full sun.
