Rubus gremlii f. austriacus

Rubus gremlii f. austriacus, commonly known as the Austrian blackberry, is a subspecies of the bramble plant native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional herbal remedies.
