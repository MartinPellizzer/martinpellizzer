Rhynchophreatia palawensis

Rhynchophreatia palawensis, commonly known as the Palauan water lily, is a unique aquatic plant native to the freshwater ecosystems of Palau.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's roots are anchored in the sediment, while its flowering stalks rise above the water surface. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, attracting pollinators such as beetles and moths.

Rhynchophreatia palawensis plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting local biodiversity.
