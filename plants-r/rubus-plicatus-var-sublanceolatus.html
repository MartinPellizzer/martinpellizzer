Rubus plicatus var. sublanceolatus

Rubus plicatus var. sublanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bramble, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces edible berries that are typically small and tart. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
