Rosa villosa f. pinnatifida

Rosa villosa f. pinnatifida, commonly known as the fuzzy leaf rose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
