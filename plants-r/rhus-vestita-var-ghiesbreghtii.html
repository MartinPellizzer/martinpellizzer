Rhus vestita var. ghiesbreghtii

Rhus vestita var. ghiesbreghtii, commonly known as the Ghiesbreght's smooth sumac, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and turn bright red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fuzzy berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
