Racosperma coriaceum subsp. pendens

Racosperma coriaceum subsp.

pendens, commonly known as the hanging acacia, is a shrub native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and small, bean-like pods.

The plant has compound leaves with pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are often visited by native bees and birds.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.
