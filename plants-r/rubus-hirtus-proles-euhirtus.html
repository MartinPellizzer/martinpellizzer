Rubus hirtus proles euhirtus

Rubus hirtus proles euhirtus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep purple-black color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its vigorous growth and ability to spread through runners.
