Rubus tephracanthus

Rubus tephracanthus, commonly known as the tephra raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
