Reseda podocarpos

Reseda podocarpos, commonly known as the golden sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Reseda podocarpos is often found in open, sunny habitats such as coastal dunes and rocky slopes.
