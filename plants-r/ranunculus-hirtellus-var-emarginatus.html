Ranunculus hirtellus var. emarginatus

Ranunculus hirtellus var. emarginatus, commonly known as the slender leaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem. The leaves are typically lobed and have a fine, hairy texture, which is a distinguishing feature of the species. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded areas.

This plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
