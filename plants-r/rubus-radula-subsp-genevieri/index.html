Rubus Radula Subsp. Genevieri

The rubus radula subsp. genevieri is a rare and elusive member of the raspberry family known for its unique ecological niche. Found primarily in the mountainous regions of southern France, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments with rich soil. Its small, dark red berries are not only visually striking but also serve as a food source for local wildlife. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this delicate plant for future generations.
