Rhododendron flavum

Rhododendron flavum, commonly known as the yellow rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its bright floral display.

Rhododendron flavum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.
