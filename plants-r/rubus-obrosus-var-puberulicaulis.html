Rubus obrosus var. puberulicaulis

Rubus obrosus var. puberulicaulis, commonly known as the pubescent bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture. This plant is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It produces small, edible berries that are a food source for various wildlife species.
