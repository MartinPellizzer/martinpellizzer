Rubus × ribifolius

Rubus × ribifolius, commonly known as the red raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit, a cluster of small drupes, is edible and widely cultivated for its flavor and nutritional value.
