Rhododendron perakense

Rhododendron perakense, commonly known as the Perak rhododendron, is a species native to the tropical regions of Malaysia, particularly in the state of Perak.

It is a shrub that typically grows in lowland and hill forest areas, often in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens, although it requires specific conditions to thrive.
