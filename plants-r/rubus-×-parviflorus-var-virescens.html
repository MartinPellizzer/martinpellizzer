Rubus × parviflorus var. virescens

Rubus × parviflorus var. virescens, commonly known as the green-flowered blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its distinctive greenish-white flowers and dark, edible berries. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces thorny stems typical of many brambles.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in horticultural and ecological studies.
