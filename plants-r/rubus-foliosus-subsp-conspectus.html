Rubus foliosus subsp. conspectus

Rubus foliosus subsp.

conspectus, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
