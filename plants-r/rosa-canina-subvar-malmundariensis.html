Rosa canina subvar. malmundariensis

Rosa canina subvar. malmundariensis, commonly known as the Malm's rose, is a subspecies native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red hips that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental gardening and ecological restoration.
