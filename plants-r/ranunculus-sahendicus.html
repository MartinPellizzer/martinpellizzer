Ranunculus sahendicus

Ranunculus sahendicus, commonly known as the Sahendic buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically palmately lobed, and its flowers have five petals that are usually yellow or orange in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
