Rumex × muellneri

Rumex × muellneri, commonly known as the hybrid sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rumex acetosa (common sorrel) and Rumex patientia (spearwort). The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring alternate, heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, and it produces oval, yellowish-brown seeds.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
