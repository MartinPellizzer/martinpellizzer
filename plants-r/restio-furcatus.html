Restio furcatus

Restio furcatus, commonly known as the forked restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with wiry, often branching stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight sheen, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Restio furcatus thrives in coastal and sandy environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing dune systems.
