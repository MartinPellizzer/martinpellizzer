Rubus granulatus var. melanodermis

Rubus granulatus var. melanodermis, commonly known as the black-berried blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that are typically smaller than those of other blackberry varieties. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruit develops from the fertilized flower buds.

This variant is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in culinary applications.
