Rumex pamiricus

Rumex pamiricus, commonly known as the Pamir dock, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender, branching stalks. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often found in alpine meadows and mountainous areas.

Rumex pamiricus is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
