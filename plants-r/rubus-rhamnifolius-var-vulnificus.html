Rubus rhamnifolius var. vulnificus

Rubus rhamnifolius var. vulnificus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically tart and not widely consumed. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and is noted for its aggressive growth habit.
