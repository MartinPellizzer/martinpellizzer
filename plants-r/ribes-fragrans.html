Ribes fragrans

Ribes fragrans, commonly known as the twinberry or redcurrant, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the genus Ribes in the family Grossulariaceae, which includes other currant and gooseberry species. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in jams and preserves. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Ribes fragrans thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.
