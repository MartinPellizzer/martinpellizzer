Rhododendron hirtolepidotum

Rhododendron hirtolepidotum, commonly known as the woolly-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant prefers acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and often have a distinctive waxy texture, while the flowers range in color from pink to red.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific ecological niches.
