Rhododendron coxianum

Rhododendron coxianum, commonly known as the Coxi's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
