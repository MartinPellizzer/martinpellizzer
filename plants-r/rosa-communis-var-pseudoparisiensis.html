Rosa communis var. pseudoparisiensis

Rosa communis var. pseudoparisiensis, commonly known as the Parisian rose, is a botanical variant of the common rose.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, deep pink to crimson flowers. The plant typically produces small, fragrant blooms in late spring to early summer. It has narrow, serrated leaves and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

This variety is well-suited for container gardening and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
