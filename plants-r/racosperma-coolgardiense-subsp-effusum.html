Racosperma coolgardiense subsp. effusum

Racosperma coolgardiense subsp.

effusum, commonly known as the Coolgardi slender acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, spreading branches and small, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which appear during the warmer months. It is adapted to low-nutrient soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
