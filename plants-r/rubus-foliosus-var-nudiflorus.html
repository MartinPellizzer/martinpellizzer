Rubus foliosus var. nudiflorus

Rubus foliosus var. nudiflorus, commonly known as the nakedflower blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that appear before the leaves in early spring. Its fruit, a dark purple berry, develops in late summer and is edible.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
