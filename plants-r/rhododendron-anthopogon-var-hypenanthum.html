Rhododendron anthopogon var. hypenanthum

Rhododendron anthopogon var. hypenanthum, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. Its floral display ranges from white to pink, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability in temperate climates.
