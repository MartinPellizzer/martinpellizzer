Rhododendron lagopus var. niphophilum

Rhododendron lagopus var. niphophilum, commonly known as the snow-capped rhododendron, is a variant of the alpine rhododendron native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are leathery and adapted to withstand harsh weather, while its flowers often display shades of pink or white.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
