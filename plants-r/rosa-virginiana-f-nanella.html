Rosa virginiana f. nanella

Rosa virginiana f. nanella, commonly known as the dwarf swamp rose, is a compact, shrub-like form of the swamp rose.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in wet, boggy environments. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its foliage is typically dark green and slightly hairy.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
