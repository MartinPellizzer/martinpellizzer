Rosa heckeliana subsp. vanheurckiana

Rosa heckeliana subsp.

vanheurckiana, commonly known as the Vanheurck's shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the forests of central and western Europe. It features upright, thorny stems and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on long pedicels. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and oval-shaped, with serrated edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
