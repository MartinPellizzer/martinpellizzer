Rubus hypoleucus subsp. prolongatus

Rubus hypoleucus subsp.

prolongatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic variability and adaptability to different environmental conditions.
