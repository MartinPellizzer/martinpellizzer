Roydsia floribunda

Roydsia floribunda, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, while its stems are woody and branching.

Roydsia floribunda is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
