Resnova humifusa

Resnova humifusa, commonly known as the groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous slender stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the early spring, followed by oval, hairy seeds. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a rough texture and a serrated margin.

Resnova humifusa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woods or along streambanks.
