Ranunculus oppositifolius

Ranunculus oppositifolius, commonly known as the opposite-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are opposite in arrangement, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
