Rhododendron × kurohimense

Rhododendron × kurohimense, commonly known as the Kurohime azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The species is noted for its cold hardiness and ability to bloom reliably in spring.
