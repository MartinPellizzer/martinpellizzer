Rosularia sempervivum var. lineata

Rosularia sempervivum var. lineata, commonly known as the Variegated Stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of fleshy, blue-green leaves with striking white or yellow margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

Rosularia sempervivum var. lineata is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
