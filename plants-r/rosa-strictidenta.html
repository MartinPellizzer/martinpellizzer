Rosa strictidenta

Rosa strictidenta, commonly known as the narrow-toothed rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and the Tien Shan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring. Its leaves are compound, with closely spaced, narrow teeth along the leaflets.

Rosa strictidenta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.
