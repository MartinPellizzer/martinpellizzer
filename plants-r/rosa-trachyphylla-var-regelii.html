Rosa trachyphylla var. regelii

Rosa trachyphylla var. regelii, commonly known as the Regel's bristly rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental appeal and hardiness in colder climates.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
