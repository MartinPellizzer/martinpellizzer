Rebutia poecilantha

Rebutia poecilantha, commonly known as the pale spurge cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and ribbed structure, with greenish-gray spines that provide camouflage in its natural habitat. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted foliage. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stems.

Rebutia poecilantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
