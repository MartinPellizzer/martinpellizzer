Rosa × declivium

Rosa × declivium, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rosa gymnocarpa and Rosa woodsii.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, shrub-like form, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in a fine layer of white to gray pubescence.

Rosa × declivium is native to the mountainous regions of western North America and is adapted to high-altitude environments with cold climates.
