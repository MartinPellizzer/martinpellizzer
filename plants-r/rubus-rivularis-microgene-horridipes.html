Rubus rivularis microgene horridipes

Rubus rivularis microgene horridipes, commonly known as the rough-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are white and have five petals, typical of many Rubus species.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in riparian zones and forest understories.
