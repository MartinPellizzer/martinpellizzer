Rubus pilosus var. axillaris

Rubus pilosus var. axillaris, commonly known as the hairy raspberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets that are hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often described as tart and less palatable compared to other raspberry varieties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or coastal areas.
