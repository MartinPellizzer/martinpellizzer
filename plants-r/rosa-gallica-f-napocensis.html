Rosa gallica f. napocensis

Rosa gallica f. napocensis, commonly known as the Napoca rose, is a compact shrub native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in gardens.

This form is a natural variant of Rosa gallica, known for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
