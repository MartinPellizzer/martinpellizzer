Rapanea magnoliifolia

Rapanea magnoliifolia, commonly known as the magnolia-leafed evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, broad, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of magnolia trees. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in panicles, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a dark purple drupe, containing a single seed.

Rapanea magnoliifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
