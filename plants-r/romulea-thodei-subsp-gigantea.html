Romulea thodei subsp. gigantea

Romulea thodei subsp.

gigantea, commonly known as the giant romulea, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family, native to South Africa. This perennial plant is characterized by its large, showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring.

It produces tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, making it a notable ornamental plant. The leaves are sword-shaped and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
