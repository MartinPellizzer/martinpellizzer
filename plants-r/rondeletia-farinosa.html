Rondeletia farinosa

Rondeletia farinosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky rondeletia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Rondeletia farinosa is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
