Rubus speciosus subf. calcicola

Rubus speciosus subf.

calcicola, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to high-altitude regions of the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered white to pink flowers.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Botanically, it is notable for its subshrub morphology and its role in alpine ecosystems.
