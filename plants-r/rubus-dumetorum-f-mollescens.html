Rubus dumetorum f. mollescens

Rubus dumetorum f. mollescens, commonly known as the soft-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry species.

It is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by the development of its edible berries.

This form is notable for its softer fruit texture compared to the typical blackberry variety.
