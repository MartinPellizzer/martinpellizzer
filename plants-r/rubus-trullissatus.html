Rubus trullissatus

Rubus trullissatus, commonly known as the thimbleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, aggregate fruits that resemble tiny berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

The flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the distinctive, thimble-shaped fruit that gives the plant its common name.
