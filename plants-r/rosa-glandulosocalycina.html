Rosa glandulosocalycina

Rosa glandulosocalycina, commonly known as the glandular calyx rose, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance.
