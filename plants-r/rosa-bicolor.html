Rosa bicolor

Rosa bicolor, commonly known as the twin-colored rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking bicolor flowers, which typically feature a pinkish-red color on the outer petals and a white or pale yellow center. The plant is a deciduous shrub that grows up to 2 meters tall, with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

Rosa bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
