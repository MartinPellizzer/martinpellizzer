Rubus × episkius

Rubus × episkius, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and natural habitats alike.
