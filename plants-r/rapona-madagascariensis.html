Rapona madagascariensis

Rapona madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar cypress, is a unique member of the Cupressaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar, characterized by its dense, columnar growth habit. The plant has scale-like leaves arranged in flat sprays, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by cone-like structures that contain seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
