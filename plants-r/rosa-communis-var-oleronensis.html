Rosa communis var. oleronensis

Rosa communis var. oleronensis, commonly known as the Oleron rose, is a rare and ancient variety of rose native to the island of Oleron in France.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its historical significance and limited natural habitat, it is considered a rare and protected species.
