Rumex tournefortii

Rumex tournefortii, commonly known as the Jerusalem artichoke, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tall, reddish stems with clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its root system consists of edible, tuberous roots that are rich in inulin.

This plant is often grown for its ornamental value and as a source of food in various cuisines.
