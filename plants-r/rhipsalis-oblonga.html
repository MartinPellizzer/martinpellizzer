Rhipsalis oblonga

Rhipsalis oblonga, commonly known as the silver leaf cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, typically from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it lacks true leaves, relying on its stems for photosynthesis.

Rhipsalis oblonga is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
