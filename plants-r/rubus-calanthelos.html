Rubus calanthelos

Rubus calanthelos, commonly known as the Greek raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect or sprawling growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and is covered in fine, stiff hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
