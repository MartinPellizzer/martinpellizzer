Rubus vestitus var. conspicuus

Rubus vestitus var. conspicuus, commonly known as the showy brambles, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are usually compound, with serrated edges, and the fruit is a red, aggregate berry.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
