Romulea stenotepala

Romulea stenotepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that serve as storage organs and are essential for its survival during dry periods. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Romulea stenotepala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
