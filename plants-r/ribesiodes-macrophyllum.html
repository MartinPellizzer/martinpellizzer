Ribesiodes macrophyllum

Ribesiodes macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 10 inches in length. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters. Its berries are red or orange and contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

This species is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
