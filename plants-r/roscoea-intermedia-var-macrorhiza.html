Roscoea intermedia var. macrorhiza

Roscoea intermedia var. macrorhiza, commonly known as the giant rhubarb, is a striking ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length, providing a dramatic foliage display. The plant produces showy, red-orange inflorescences with numerous small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden spaces.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
