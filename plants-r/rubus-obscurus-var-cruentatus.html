Rubus obscurus var. cruentatus

Rubus obscurus var. cruentatus, commonly known as the crimson bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of North America, particularly in the eastern United States. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, dark green leaves, and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are edible and have a tart flavor.

This variety is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soil.
