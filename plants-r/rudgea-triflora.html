Rudgea triflora

Rudgea triflora, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its bark is often smooth and grayish in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Rudgea triflora is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
