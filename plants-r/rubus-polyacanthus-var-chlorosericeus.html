Rubus polyacanthus var. chlorosericeus

Rubus polyacanthus var. chlorosericeus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves.

It is a shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, featuring thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The leaves are usually compound, with serrated edges and a unique coloration that sets it apart from other Rubus species. This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
