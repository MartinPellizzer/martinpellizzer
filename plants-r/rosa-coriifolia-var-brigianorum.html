Rosa coriifolia var. brigianorum

Rosa coriifolia var. brigianorum, commonly known as the Chinese rose or bracted rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a distinctive bract-like structure beneath the flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in diverse landscapes.
