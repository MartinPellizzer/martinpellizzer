Rhamphicarpa spicata

Rhamphicarpa spicata, commonly known as the spike-fruited rhamnus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rhamnaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, simple leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Rhamphicarpa spicata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various insects.
