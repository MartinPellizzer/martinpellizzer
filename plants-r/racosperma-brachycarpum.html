Racosperma brachycarpum

Racosperma brachycarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant has small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting well to dry conditions.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in its native habitat.
