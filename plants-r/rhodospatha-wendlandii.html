Rhodospatha wendlandii

Rhodospatha wendlandii, commonly known as the Wendland's rhododendron, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhodospatha wendlandii thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
