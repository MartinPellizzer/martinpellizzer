Rachicallis nitida

Rachicallis nitida, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-white hairs that cover its leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its unique texture and ability to add visual interest in arid environments.
