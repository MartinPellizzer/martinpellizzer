Rubus venetorum

Rubus venetorum, commonly known as the Venetian blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry and blackberry species.
