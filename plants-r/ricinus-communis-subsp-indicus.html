Ricinus communis subsp. indicus

Ricinus communis subsp.

indicus, commonly known as the castor oil plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and distinctive spiny capsule fruits.

The plant produces a toxic, oily seed that is widely used in the production of castor oil. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a height ranging from 1 to 4 meters.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
