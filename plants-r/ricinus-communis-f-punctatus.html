Ricinus communis f. punctatus

Ricinus communis f. punctatus, commonly known as the spiny castor oil plant, is a variant of the castor oil plant characterized by its distinctive spiny leaves.

The plant is native to tropical regions and is cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage. Its leaves are typically large, palmately lobed, and covered with sharp, spine-like protrusions. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers followed by capsules containing oily seeds.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
