Rubus ulmifolius bifrons

Rubus ulmifolius bifrons, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically three to five lobed. The plant produces aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
