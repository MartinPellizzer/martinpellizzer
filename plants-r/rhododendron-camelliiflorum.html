Rhododendron camelliiflorum

Rhododendron camelliiflorum, commonly known as the Camellia-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes other popular shrubs and trees.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections around the world.
