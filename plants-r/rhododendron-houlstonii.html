Rhododendron houlstonii

Rhododendron houlstonii, commonly known as the Houlston's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in Nepal and northern India.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

This species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife.
