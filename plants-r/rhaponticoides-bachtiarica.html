Rhaponticoides bachtiarica

Rhaponticoides bachtiarica, commonly known as the Persian rhubarb, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features robust, fleshy rhizomes that store nutrients and water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces tall, erect stems with large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and hairy. Its flowers are clustered in terminal panicles, with showy, pinkish-white petals that attract pollinators.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly the rhizomes, which have been used in traditional medicine for their digestive and anti-inflammatory effects.
