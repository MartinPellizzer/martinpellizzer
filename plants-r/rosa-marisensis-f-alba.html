Rosa marisensis f. alba

Rosa marisensis f. alba, commonly known as the White Sea Rose, is a rare and elegant cultivar of the Rosa marisensis species.

It is characterized by its pale white petals, which contrast beautifully with its deep green foliage. This plant is native to coastal regions and thrives in saline environments, making it well adapted to maritime climates. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch to gardens near the sea.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
