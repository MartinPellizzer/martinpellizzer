Rivina moritziana

Rivina moritziana, commonly known as the redroot or flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Rivina moritziana is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
