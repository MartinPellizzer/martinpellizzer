Rhynchosia lobata

Rhynchosia lobata, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant bears pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and oil. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Rhynchosia lobata is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
