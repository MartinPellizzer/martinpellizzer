Reseda lutea subsp. vivantii

Reseda lutea subsp.

vivantii, commonly known as the yellow everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

The subspecies is closely related to other members of the Reseda genus, which are known for their showy, often long-lasting flowers.
