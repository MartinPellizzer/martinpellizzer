Rosa latibracteata

Rosa latibracteata, commonly known as the broad-bract rose, is a species of shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in thorns. Rosa latibracteata typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
