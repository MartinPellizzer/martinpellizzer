Rubus pyramidalis f. parvifolius

Rubus pyramidalis f. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved raspberry.

This plant is a compact, thorny shrub with small, ovate leaves that are typically hairy beneath. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems. The fruits are small, red berries that develop from the flowers, and they are often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.
