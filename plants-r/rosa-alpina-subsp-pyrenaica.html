Rosa alpina subsp. pyrenaica

Rosa alpina subsp.

pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean rock rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and the Pyrenees. It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, demonstrating a high degree of cold tolerance.

The plant produces showy pink to red flowers, usually in clusters, during the late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
