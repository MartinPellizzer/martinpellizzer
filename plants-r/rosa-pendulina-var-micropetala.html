Rosa pendulina var. micropetala

Rosa pendulina var. micropetala, commonly known as the miniature climbing rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in clusters along its thorny stems. The plant has a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trained on trellises. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and oval-shaped, providing a lush backdrop to the delicate flowers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
