Reseda lutea f. mucronata

Reseda lutea f. mucronata, commonly known as the creeping yellowweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes, and its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
