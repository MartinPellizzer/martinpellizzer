Rubus navus

Rubus navus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained areas.

Rubus navus plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
