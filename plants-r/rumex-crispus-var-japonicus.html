Rumex crispus var. japonicus

Rumex crispus var. japonicus, commonly known as Japanese dock, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and have a wavy margin, giving them a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months.

This plant is often found in moist soils along roadsides, stream banks, and in disturbed areas.
