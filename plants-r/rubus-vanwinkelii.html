Rubus vanwinkelii

Rubus vanwinkelii, commonly known as the Van Winkel's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically found in the Western Cape province. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated at the edges.

Rubus vanwinkelii is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, sandy soils.
