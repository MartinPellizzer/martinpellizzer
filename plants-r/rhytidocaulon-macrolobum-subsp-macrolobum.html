Rhytidocaulon macrolobum subsp. macrolobum

Rhytidocaulon macrolobum subsp.

macrolobum, commonly known as the broad-leafed rhytidocaulon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its climbing growth habit and fleshy, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

Its fruits are elongated, cylindrical capsules that contain numerous seeds. The leaves are notably large, often reaching up to 30 cm in length, and have a distinctive, waxy surface.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is often found in humid, shaded environments.
