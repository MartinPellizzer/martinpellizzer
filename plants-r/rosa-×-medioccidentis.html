Rosa × medioccidentis

Rosa × medioccidentis, commonly known as the Damascus rose, is a hybrid species originating from the cross between Rosa damascena and Rosa centifolia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

The flowers are used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their essential oils.
