Rubus cordifolius subsp. scheutzii

Rubus cordifolius subsp.

scheutzii, commonly known as the Scheutz's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically with 5 to 7 leaflets, which are usually serrated and have a dark green upper surface.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color, often with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
