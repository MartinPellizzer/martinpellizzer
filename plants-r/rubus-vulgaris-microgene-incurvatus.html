Rubus vulgaris microgene incurvatus

Rubus vulgaris microgene incurvatus, commonly known as the bent-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its unique fruit morphology.

This subspecies belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The fruits are typically smaller and have a more compact, curved appearance compared to typical blackberries. It is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant produces prickly stems and has compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Rubus genus.
