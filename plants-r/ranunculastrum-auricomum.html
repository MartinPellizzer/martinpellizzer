Ranunculastrum auricomum

Ranunculastrum auricomum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically appearing in early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ranunculastrum auricomum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or woodland settings.
