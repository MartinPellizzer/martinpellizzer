Rubus amoeniflorus

Rubus amoeniflorus, commonly known as the sweetflower blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the production of edible, sweet-tart berries. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
