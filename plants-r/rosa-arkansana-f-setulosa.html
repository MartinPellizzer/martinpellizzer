Rosa arkansana f. setulosa

Rosa arkansana f. setulosa, commonly known as the Arkansas rose, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought.

The cultivar 'setulosa' is noted for its dense, hairy growth and compact form, making it a desirable ornamental plant.
