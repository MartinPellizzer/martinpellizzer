Racosperma hypermeces

Racosperma hypermeces, commonly known as the mountain wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid regions. It has adapted to survive in harsh environments through its deep root system and drought tolerance.

Racosperma hypermeces plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
