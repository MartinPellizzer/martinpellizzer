Rungia obcordata

Rungia obcordata, commonly known as the red-flowered piper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red flowers. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Rungia obcordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
