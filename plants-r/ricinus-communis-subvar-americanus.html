Ricinus communis subvar. americanus

Ricinus communis subvar. americanus, commonly known as the castor oil plant, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and distinctive spiny seed capsules. The plant produces toxic seeds that contain ricin, a potent toxin. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its oil-rich seeds.

The plant has both ornamental and industrial uses, though caution is advised due to its toxic properties.
