Rubus macrostachys var. sepincola

Rubus macrostachys var. sepincola, commonly known as the coastal raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to coastal regions of southern California.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal winds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing dunes.
