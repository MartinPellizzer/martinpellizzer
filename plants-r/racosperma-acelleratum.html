Racosperma acelleratum

Racosperma acelleratum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments and has a deep root system for water absorption. It plays a role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

The species is often found in sandy or rocky soils across eastern and southern Australia.
