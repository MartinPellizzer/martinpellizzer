Rubus hermes

Rubus hermes, commonly known as Hermes' bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its rarity, it is considered a conservation concern in some regions.
