Raphanus sativus convar. radicula

Raphanus sativus convar. radicula, commonly known as the bulbous radish, is a botanical variety characterized by its swollen, underground root structure.

This plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is native to temperate regions of Asia. The root, which is the primary edible part, is typically white or pink in color and has a crisp, slightly spicy flavor. It is cultivated for its nutritional value and use in various culinary applications.

The plant also produces small, yellow flowers and is known for its rapid growth cycle.
