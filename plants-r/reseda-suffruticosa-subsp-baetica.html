Reseda suffruticosa subsp. baetica

Reseda suffruticosa subsp.

baetica, commonly known as the Iberian redweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a characteristic species of Mediterranean vegetation.
