Rhododendron santapaui

Rhododendron santapaui, commonly known as the San Pablo rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in early spring, adding vibrant hues to the landscape.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
