Rubus tereticaulis var. derasifolius

Rubus tereticaulis var. derasifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed by wildlife. The plant has slender, thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its flowers are white or pale pink and appear in the spring.

The species is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaflets and is often found in moist, wooded habitats.
