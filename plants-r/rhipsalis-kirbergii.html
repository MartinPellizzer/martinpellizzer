Rhipsalis kirbergii

Rhipsalis kirbergii, commonly known as the Kirberg's mistletoe cactus, is a unique epiphytic plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its trailing, leafless stems that bear clusters of small, white flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees in the Atlantic Forest. Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators such as moths and bats.

Rhipsalis kirbergii is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant due to its delicate appearance.
