Rapuntium thunbergii

Rapuntium thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, and it typically grows in moist, shady environments. It produces small, green fruits that contain seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
