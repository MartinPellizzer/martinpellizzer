Ranunculus auricomus var. carlittensis

Ranunculus auricomus var. carlittensis, commonly known as the Carlitt's buttercup, is a variant of the golden buttercup.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States, particularly California. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.
