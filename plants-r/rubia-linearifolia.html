Rubia linearifolia

Rubia linearifolia, commonly known as narrow-leafed rue, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. Its roots and leaves have been used historically in traditional medicine for their aromatic and antiseptic properties.

Rubia linearifolia is often found growing in rocky or dry soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.
