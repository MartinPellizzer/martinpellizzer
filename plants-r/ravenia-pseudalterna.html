Ravenia pseudalterna

Ravenia pseudalterna, commonly known as the purple flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its striking, velvety purple foliage that turns reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by flat, bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
