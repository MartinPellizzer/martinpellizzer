Ranunculus trichophyllus var. glaber

Ranunculus trichophyllus var. glaber, commonly known as the roughleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
