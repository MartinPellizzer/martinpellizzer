Rosa rubiginosa subsp. comosa

Rosa rubiginosa subsp.

comosa, commonly known as the hairy rose or the red-stemmed rose, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and dark red to purplish bark.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a fuzzy texture.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
