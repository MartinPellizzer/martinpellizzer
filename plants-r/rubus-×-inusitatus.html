Rubus × inusitatus

Rubus × inusitatus, commonly known as the purple-fruit blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to deep purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations.
