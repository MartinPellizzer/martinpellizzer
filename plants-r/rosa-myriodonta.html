Rosa myriodonta

Rosa myriodonta, commonly known as the many-flowered rose, is a species native to South America, particularly found in countries like Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces numerous small, oval-shaped leaves with a serrated edge, and its flowers are typically arranged in tight, compact inflorescences. Rosa myriodonta is a perennial shrub that thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique and attractive addition to gardens and botanical collections.
