Rhus ruspolii

Rhus ruspolii, commonly known as the Italian buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 5 to 9 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark, glossy berries that are an important food source for birds. Rhus ruspolii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and ornamental horticulture.
