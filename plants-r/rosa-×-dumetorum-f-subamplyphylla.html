Rosa × dumetorum f. subamplyphylla

Rosa × dumetorum f. subamplyphylla, commonly known as the shrub rose or alpine rose, is a botanical hybrid native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form dense, bushy growth that is both hardy and long-lived.
