Ranunculus harveyi var. pilosus

Ranunculus harveyi var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically arranged in a cup-like shape. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

The hairy texture of its foliage and flowers is a distinctive botanical characteristic.
