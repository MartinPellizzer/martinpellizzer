Rosa tomentosa var. gusuleaciana

Rosa tomentosa var. gusuleaciana, commonly known as the Gushuleaciana rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for small garden spaces or as a hedge. Its leaves are typically dark green and covered in a fine, white down, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
