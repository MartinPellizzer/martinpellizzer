Rubus pubifrons

Rubus pubifrons, commonly known as the hairy-fruited blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the stems are often armed with prickly bracts.

The botanical characteristics of Rubus pubifrons make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
