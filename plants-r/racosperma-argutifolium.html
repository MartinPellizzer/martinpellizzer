Racosperma argutifolium

Racosperma argutifolium, commonly known as the serrated-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often serrated and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the legume family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Racosperma argutifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
