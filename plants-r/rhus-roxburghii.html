Rhus roxburghii

Rhus roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by clusters of red, fleshy drupes that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland or scrub habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in various regions.
