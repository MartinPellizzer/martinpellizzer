Rosa canina subsp. stylosa

Rosa canina subsp.

stylosa, commonly known as the slender dog rose, is a subspecies native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets.

The flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals, and appear in clusters during the spring. Its fruit, a red, hairy rose hip, is an important food source for wildlife.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
