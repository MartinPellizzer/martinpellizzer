Rubus excurvatus

Rubus excurvatus, commonly known as the hooked blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is closely related to other brambles like raspberries and blackberries.
