Rosa irinae

Rosa irinae, commonly known as the Irina rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, fragrant pink to red flowers, and dark green, serrated leaves. It produces oval, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Rosa irinae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
