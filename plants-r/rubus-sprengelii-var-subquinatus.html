Rubus sprengelii var. subquinatus

Rubus sprengelii var. subquinatus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,500 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries, which are usually dark purple to black when mature, are edible and have a tart flavor.
