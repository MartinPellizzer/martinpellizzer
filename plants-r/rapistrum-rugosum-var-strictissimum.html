Rapistrum rugosum var. strictissimum

Rapistrum rugosum var. strictissimum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sowthistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a wavy margin. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in a smooth, spherical capsule, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils, roadsides, and waste areas.

It has a taproot system and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
