Raphanus arcuatus

Raphanus arcuatus, commonly known as the curled radish or wild radish, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant features a taproot that is typically small and rounded, with hairy stems and pinnately divided leaves. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some regions.
