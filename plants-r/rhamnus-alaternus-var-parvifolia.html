Rhamnus alaternus var. parvifolia

Rhamnus alaternus var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, oval, dark green leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
