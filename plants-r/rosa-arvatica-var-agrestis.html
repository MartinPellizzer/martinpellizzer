Rosa arvatica var. agrestis

Rosa arvatica var. agrestis, commonly known as the rough-stemmed wild rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are pink to reddish in color, blooming in summer, and it produces hips that are edible.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
