Rubus glandulosus var. cupanianus

Rubus glandulosus var. cupanianus, commonly known as the cupan's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
