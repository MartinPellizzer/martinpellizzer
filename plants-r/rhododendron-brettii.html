Rhododendron brettii

Rhododendron brettii, commonly known as the Brett's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, specifically found in regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is a broadleaf evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a dense covering of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron brettii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with acidic soil and partial shade.
