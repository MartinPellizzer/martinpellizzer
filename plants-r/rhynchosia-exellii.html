Rhynchosia exellii

Rhynchosia exellii, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in agricultural systems for its nutritional value.

The seeds are rich in protein and can be used as a food source or for forage.
