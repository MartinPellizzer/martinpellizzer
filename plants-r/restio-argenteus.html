Restio argenteus

Restio argenteus, commonly known as silver restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small white or pale blue flowers.

Restio argenteus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
