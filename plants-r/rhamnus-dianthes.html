Rhamnus dianthes

Rhamnus dianthes, commonly known as the Persian buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features small, oval leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by small, dark blue berries in the autumn. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Rhamnus dianthes is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
