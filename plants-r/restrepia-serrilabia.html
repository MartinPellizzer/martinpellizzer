Restrepia serrilabia

Restrepia serrilabia, commonly known as the serrilabied restrepia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant features strap-like leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive serrated edge, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and requires well-draining soil.

Restrepia serrilabia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique foliage structure.
