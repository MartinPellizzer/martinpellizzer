Riedelia lippioides

Riedelia lippioides, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tubular and have a distinctive, waxy texture, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, shiny seeds.

Riedelia lippioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
