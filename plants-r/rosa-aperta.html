Rosa aperta

Rosa aperta, commonly known as the open rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its simple, open growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have serrated edges.

Rosa aperta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and historical significance.
