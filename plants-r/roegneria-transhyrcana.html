Roegneria transhyrcana

Roegneria transhyrcana, commonly known as the Trans-Iranian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that bear spikelets along the central axis. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Roegneria transhyrcana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardy nature.
