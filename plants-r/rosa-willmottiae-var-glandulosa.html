Rosa willmottiae var. glandulosa

Rosa willmottiae var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular willmott's rose, is a hybrid shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorned stems. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with glandular hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
