Ribes glutinosum

Ribes glutinosum, commonly known as sticky currant, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in the understory of forests.

Ribes glutinosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
