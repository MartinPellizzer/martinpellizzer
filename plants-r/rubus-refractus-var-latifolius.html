Rubus refractus var. latifolius

Rubus refractus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry genus characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, often in moist, wooded areas. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or yellow when ripe. Its flowers are white and clustered in the axils of the leaves, and it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its edible berries, which have a tart flavor and are used in culinary applications.
