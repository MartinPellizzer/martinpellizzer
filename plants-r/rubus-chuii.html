Rubus chuii

Rubus chuii, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs commonly referred to as blackberries or raspberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The botanical features of Rubus chuii make it an important species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
