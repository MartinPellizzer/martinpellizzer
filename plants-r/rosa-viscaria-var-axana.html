Rosa viscaria var. axana

Rosa viscaria var. axana, commonly known as the Mexican rose or axillary rose, is a shrub-like plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, rounded growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to elliptical leaflets.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
