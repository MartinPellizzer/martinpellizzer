Rubus disjunctus

Rubus disjunctus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a shrub native to western North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, compound, and have serrated edges.

Rubus disjunctus thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in coastal regions.
