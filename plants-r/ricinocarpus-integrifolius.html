Ricinocarpus integrifolius

Ricinocarpus integrifolius, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or saline soils. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The species is known for its adaptability to saltwater environments and is often used in coastal landscaping.
