Roupala francii

Roupala francii, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

Roupala francii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
