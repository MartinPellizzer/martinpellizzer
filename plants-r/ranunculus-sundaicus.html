Ranunculus sundaicus

Ranunculus sundaicus, commonly known as the Sunda buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has a short stem with palmate leaves that are divided into lobes, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments. It is a herbaceous perennial that prefers well-drained soil and is found in regions such as Indonesia and the Philippines.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
