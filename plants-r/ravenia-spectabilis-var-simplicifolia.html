Ravenia spectabilis var. simplicifolia

Ravenia spectabilis var. simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue berries that are highly ornamental and attract birds. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
