Rhododendron stewartianum var. tantulum

Rhododendron stewartianum var. tantulum, commonly known as the Stewart's azalea, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Appalachian Mountains of the United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage and produces showy clusters of pink to lavender flowers in late spring. The plant has leathery, elliptical leaves that provide year-round interest. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and hardiness.
