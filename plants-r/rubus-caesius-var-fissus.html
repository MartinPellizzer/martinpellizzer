Rubus caesius var. fissus

Rubus caesius var. fissus, commonly known as the fissured blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically tart and less sweet compared to other blackberry varieties. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
