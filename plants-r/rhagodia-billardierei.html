Rhagodia billardierei

Rhagodia billardierei, commonly known as the slender burr bush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive burrs that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

Rhagodia billardierei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
