Rosa communis var. hirsuta

Rosa communis var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a variant of the common rose characterized by its fuzzy, hairy foliage and stems.

It produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant is a deciduous shrub, typically growing to a height of 1 to 2 meters. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Its unique texture and color make it a desirable addition to horticultural collections.
