Rubus × angustiracemosus

Rubus × angustiracemosus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. These berries are often cultivated for their flavor and used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
