Rubus × pseudolejeunei

Rubus × pseudolejeunei, commonly known as the blackberry-elderberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus allegheniensis (blackberry) and Rubus hirtellus (elderberry), resulting in a plant with distinctive botanical features. The species typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, with thorny stems and compound leaves that are oval to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small, white to pink, and clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruits are aggregate berries that range in color from dark purple to black.

This hybrid is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
