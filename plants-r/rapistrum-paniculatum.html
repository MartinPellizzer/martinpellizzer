Rapistrum paniculatum

Rapistrum paniculatum, commonly known as the wild mustard or field pennycress, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stem and alternate leaves that are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters, which are followed by distinctive seed pods. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard species.
