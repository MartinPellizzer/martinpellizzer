Rumex ecklonianus

Rumex ecklonianus, commonly known as the Cape sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive sour taste. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into triangular, winged fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Rumex ecklonianus is valued for its culinary uses and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
