Rhytiglossa paniculata

Rhytiglossa paniculata, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble clusters of small flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Rhytiglossa paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
