Rotala tripartita

Rotala tripartita, commonly known as the three-part waterwheel, is a popular aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, bright green leaves that are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three parts, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments and is often used in pond and aquarium settings. It produces tiny white flowers that float on the water surface, adding a delicate touch to its aquatic habitat.

Rotala tripartita is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide oxygen and shelter for aquatic life.
