Rubus pubicaulis

Rubus pubicaulis, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, known as blackberries, are aggregate fruits formed from multiple tiny drupes.

Rubus pubicaulis is typically found in woodland areas and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.
