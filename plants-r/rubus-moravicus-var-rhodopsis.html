Rubus moravicus var. rhodopsis

Rubus moravicus var. rhodopsis, commonly known as the Moravian raspberry.

It is a variant of the raspberry species native to Central Europe, particularly the Czech Republic. This plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a red, edible aggregate berry.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
