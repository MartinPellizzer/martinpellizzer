Rhynchosia monophylla var. eylesii

Rhynchosia monophylla var. eylesii, commonly known as the Eyles' burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its seeds are encased in thick, leathery pods that split open to release the seeds when mature.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential use in erosion control.
