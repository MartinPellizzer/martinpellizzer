Rubus discolor

Rubus discolor, commonly known as the variable blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, dark purple to black berries in the summer. Its fruits are often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
