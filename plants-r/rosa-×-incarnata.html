Rosa × incarnata

Rosa × incarnata, commonly known as the shrub rose or Cleveland rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Its leaves are dark green, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.
