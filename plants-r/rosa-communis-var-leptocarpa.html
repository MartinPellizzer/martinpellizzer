Rosa communis var. leptocarpa

Rosa communis var. leptocarpa, commonly known as the slender-stemmed rose, is a variety of the common rose characterized by its slender, often thornless stems.

It produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is typically shrub-like, with compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
