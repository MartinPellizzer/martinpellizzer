Rubroshorea polysperma

Rubroshorea polysperma, commonly known as the redshore flame tree, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant red flowers and distinctive foliage.

It is native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, saline environments. The plant features a robust, woody stem with compound leaves that are often bright green and lance-shaped. Its flowers, which bloom in clusters, are not only visually appealing but also attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and coastal landscapes for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
