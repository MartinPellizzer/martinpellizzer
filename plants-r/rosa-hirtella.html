Rosa hirtella

Rosa hirtella, commonly known as the rough rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly in countries such as Brazil, Peru, and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, pinnately compound leaves, and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. Rosa hirtella typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
