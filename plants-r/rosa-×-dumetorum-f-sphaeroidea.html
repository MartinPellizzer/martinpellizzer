Rosa × dumetorum f. sphaeroidea

Rosa × dumetorum f. sphaeroidea, commonly known as the spherical form of the shrub rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its unique growth habit.

This form exhibits a more compact and rounded appearance compared to the typical sprawling growth of the species Rosa × dumetorum. Its foliage is dense, with leaves that are often smaller and more closely spaced. The flowers are usually similar to those of the parent species, featuring five petals and a fragrant aroma.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
