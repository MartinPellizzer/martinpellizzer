Rhynchosia candida

Rhynchosia candida, commonly known as the white-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source. Rhynchosia candida is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural systems.
