Rosa × nitidula f. ovifera

Rosa × nitidula f. ovifera, commonly known as the oval-leaf hybrid rose, is a cultivar characterized by its unique foliage and floral features.

It is a hybrid between Rosa nitidula and other Rosa species, resulting in a plant with distinctive, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. The flowers are typically pink or red, with a semi-double to double bloom structure, adding ornamental value to gardens. This variety thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Its compact growth habit and attractive appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
