Rhamnus pumila f. villarsiana

Rhamnus pumila f. villarsiana, commonly known as the dwarf buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or forested areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh conditions.
