Ruscus aculeatus

Ruscus aculeatus, commonly known as butcher's broom, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, waxy, spiny leaves that form a compact rosette structure. The plant produces bright red berries that are attractive to birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and adaptability to various soil types.
