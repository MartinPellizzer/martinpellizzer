Royena wilmsii

Royena wilmsii, commonly known as the Wilmsii rock fig, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
