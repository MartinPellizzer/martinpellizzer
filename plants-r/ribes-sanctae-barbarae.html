Ribes sanctae-barbarae

Ribes sanctae-barbarae, commonly known as the sacred barbara currant, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Ribes.

It is native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America, particularly in regions of Peru and Bolivia. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. It is characterized by its upright growth habit, narrow leaves, and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers.

The botanical features of Ribes sanctae-barbarae make it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
