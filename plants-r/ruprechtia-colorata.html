Ruprechtia colorata

Ruprechtia colorata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-pink blooms that appear in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a tall, unbranched stem that supports its cone-shaped flower head. Ruprechtia colorata thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its nectar attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
