Rosa tomentosa var. subrubiginosa

Rosa tomentosa var. subrubiginosa, commonly known as the rusty rose or woolly rose, is a shrub-like perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, grayish foliage and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant produces oval, red-orange hips that are both ornamental and edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental hedges and wildlife habitats.
