Rubus balfourianus var. roseus

Rubus balfourianus var. roseus, commonly known as the rosehip bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, red berries that are rich in vitamin C and often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters during the spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
