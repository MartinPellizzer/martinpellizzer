Rosa afzeliana var. acuticuspis

Rosa afzeliana var. acuticuspis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features upright stems covered in dense, thorny prickles, which provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have sharply pointed leaflets, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
