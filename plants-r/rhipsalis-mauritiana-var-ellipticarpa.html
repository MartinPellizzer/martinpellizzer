Rhipsalis mauritiana var. ellipticarpa

Rhipsalis mauritiana var. ellipticarpa, commonly known as the ellipticarpa mistletoe cactus, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, green, cylindrical stems that are covered in small, white spines. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the summer months, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically small and reduced, as it relies on its stems for photosynthesis.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
