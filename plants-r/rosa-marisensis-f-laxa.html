Rosa marisensis f. laxa

Rosa marisensis f. laxa, commonly known as the loose-flowered sea rose, is a botanical variant of the sea rose species.

It is characterized by its reduced, more loosely arranged floral structure compared to the typical form. This plant is native to coastal regions and thrives in saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to maritime conditions. Its flowers are usually pink to white, with a delicate appearance that contrasts with its robust growth habit.

The foliage is dense and often covered in a fine layer of salt-resistant hairs.
