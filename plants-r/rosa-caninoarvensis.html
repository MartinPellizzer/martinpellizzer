Rosa caninoarvensis

Rosa caninoarvensis, commonly known as the field rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant pink to white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a red, hairy rose hip, which contains the seeds.

Rosa caninoarvensis is native to Europe and western Asia and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
