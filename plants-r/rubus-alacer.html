Rubus alacer

Rubus alacer, commonly known as the scarlet raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has a dense, sprawling structure.

Rubus alacer thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
