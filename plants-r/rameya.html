Rameya

Rameya, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Rameya is native to regions in Africa, Asia, and the Americas, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
