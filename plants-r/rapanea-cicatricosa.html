Rapanea cicatricosa

Rapanea cicatricosa, commonly known as the scarred evergreen, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed surrounded by a fleshy pulp.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in local ecosystems.
