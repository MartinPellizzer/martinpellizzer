Retama atlantica

Retama atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula and North Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its small, trifoliate leaves are adapted to arid conditions, making it resilient to drought.

Retama atlantica is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
