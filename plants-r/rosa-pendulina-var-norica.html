Rosa pendulina var. norica

Rosa pendulina var. norica, commonly known as the Austrian climbing rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems that can climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its graceful appearance.
