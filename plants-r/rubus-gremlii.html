Rubus gremlii

Rubus gremlii, commonly known as the Gremli's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is closely related to other bramble species.
