Ruellia hirtella

Ruellia hirtella, commonly known as the hairy ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Ruellia hirtella is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
