Rubus koehleri var. bavaricus

Rubus koehleri var. bavaricus, commonly known as the Bavarian blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus koehleri species native to Central Europe.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically dark green and serrated. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black in color, with a slightly tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along forest edges.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
