Rubus pedatifolius

Rubus pedatifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed areas, and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
