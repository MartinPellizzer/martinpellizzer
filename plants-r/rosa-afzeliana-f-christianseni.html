Rosa afzeliana f. christianseni

Rosa afzeliana f. christianseni, commonly known as the Christiansen's rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa afzeliana species.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and historical significance.
