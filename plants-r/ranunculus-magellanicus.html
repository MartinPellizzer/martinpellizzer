Ranunculus magellanicus

Ranunculus magellanicus, commonly known as the Magellanic buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features showy, yellow or orange flowers with five to seven petals, often with a distinctive black or dark central spot. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are hairy and adapted to its alpine or subarctic habitats. It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Ranunculaceae family.
