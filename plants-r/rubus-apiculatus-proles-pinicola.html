Rubus apiculatus proles pinicola

Rubus apiculatus proles pinicola, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry.

It is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, characterized by its small, prickly stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.

Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and a woody, thorny structure typical of the Rubus genus.
