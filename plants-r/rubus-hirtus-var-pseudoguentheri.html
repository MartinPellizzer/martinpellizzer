Rubus hirtus var. pseudoguentheri

Rubus hirtus var. pseudoguentheri, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubiaceae family.
