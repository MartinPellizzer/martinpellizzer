Ranunculus alpestris subsp. bilobus

Ranunculus alpestris subsp.

bilobus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features showy yellow flowers with five to seven petals, which are often marked with dark veins. Its leaves are deeply lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The subspecies bilobus is characterized by its bilobed leaflets, distinguishing it from other members of the Ranunculus alpestris complex.
