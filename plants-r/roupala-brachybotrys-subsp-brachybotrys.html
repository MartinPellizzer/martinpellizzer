Roupala brachybotrys subsp. brachybotrys

Roupala brachybotrys subsp.

brachybotrys, commonly known as the narrow-leafed roupala, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, spherical drupe that contains a single seed.

This species is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.
