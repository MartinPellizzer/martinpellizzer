Rhododendron maxwellii

Rhododendron maxwellii, commonly known as Maxwell's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, moist environments at high elevations. The plant features dense, leathery leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its botanical characteristics include a compact growth habit and a preference for acidic, well-drained soils.

Rhododendron maxwellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
