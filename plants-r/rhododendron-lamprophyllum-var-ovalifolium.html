Rhododendron lamprophyllum var. ovalifolium

Rhododendron lamprophyllum var. ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaved rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features lanceolate to oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight pubescence. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
