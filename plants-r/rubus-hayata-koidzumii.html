Rubus hayata-koidzumii

Rubus hayata-koidzumii, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to Japan, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and desserts. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The species is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its edible fruit.
