Rubus tenuis

Rubus tenuis, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rubus genus in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, trifoliate leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically less than 1 centimeter in diameter. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its delicate structure and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
