Rosa mollis f. inaeruginea

Rosa mollis f. inaeruginea, commonly known as the silver-leafed rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa mollis species.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is a shrub that typically grows in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental qualities. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, and it produces rose hips that are edible.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
