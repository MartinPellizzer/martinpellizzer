Rhynchanthera schrankiana

Rhynchanthera schrankiana, commonly known as the Schrank's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the substrate, a common feature among orchids.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
