Rubus idaeus subsp. inermis

Rubus idaeus subsp.

inermis, commonly known as the smooth raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its thornless stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, bright red drupes that are highly aromatic. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its flavorful berries, which are used in culinary and commercial applications.
