Ranunculus allenii

Ranunculus allenii, commonly known as Allen's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals and a hairy stem. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and is often found in areas with partial shade.

Ranunculus allenii is a short-lived perennial that blooms in the spring and is part of the Ranunculaceae family.
