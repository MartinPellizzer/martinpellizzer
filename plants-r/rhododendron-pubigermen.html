Rhododendron pubigermen

Rhododendron pubigermen, commonly known as the pubescent rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round structure to the landscape.

Rhododendron pubigermen prefers well-drained, acidic soils and partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in temperate gardens.
