Rhaphiolepis fulvicoma

Rhaphiolepis fulvicoma, commonly known as the golden bell viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Rhaphiolepis fulvicoma prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
