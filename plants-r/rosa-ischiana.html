Rosa ischiana

Rosa ischiana, commonly known as the Ischian rose, is a species native to the southern regions of Europe, particularly Italy.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are often clustered in small groups. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, pinnate leaves, and an upright growth habit.

Rosa ischiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
