Rubus neomalacus

Rubus neomalacus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically red when mature. These berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry and blackberry species.
