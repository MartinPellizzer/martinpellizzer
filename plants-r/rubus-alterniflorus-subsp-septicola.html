Rubus alterniflorus subsp. septicola

Rubus alterniflorus subsp.

septicola, commonly known as the blackberry or raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria.

The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, white or pink flowers that are pollinated by insects.

The berries are edible, often used in culinary applications, and are rich in antioxidants.
