Rubus apoensis

Rubus apoensis, commonly known as the Apo blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and have a tart flavor. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
