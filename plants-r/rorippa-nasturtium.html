Rorippa nasturtium

Rorippa nasturtium, commonly known as watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit.

It typically features slender, branched stems that often grow in water or damp soil, and its leaves are alternate, ovate to linear in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in racemes, and it is known for its peppery, edible leaves that are widely used in culinary applications.

Rorippa nasturtium is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of moist environments, including ponds, streams, and wetlands.
