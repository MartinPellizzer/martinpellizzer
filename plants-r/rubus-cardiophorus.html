Rubus cardiophorus

Rubus cardiophorus, commonly known as the heartleaf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which often have a heart-shaped base, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it is known for its thorny stems and vigorous growth habit.
