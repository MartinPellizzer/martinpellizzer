Rosa × dryadea

Rosa × dryadea, commonly known as the everblooming rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa × centifolia.

It is characterized by its ability to produce multiple blooms throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for gardeners. The plant typically features glossy, dark green foliage and fragrant, semi-double to full blooms in shades of pink, red, and white. It is a hardy shrub that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, exhibiting good resistance to diseases and pests.

Its adaptability and continuous flowering make it a versatile addition to both ornamental and landscaping settings.
