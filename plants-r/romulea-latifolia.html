Romulea latifolia

Romulea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, stout stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Romulea latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
