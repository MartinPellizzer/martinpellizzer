Rubus gratiosus subsp. serratulifolius

Rubus gratiosus subsp.

serratulifolius, commonly known as the serrated leaf raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of eastern North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its hairy foliage and showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring.
