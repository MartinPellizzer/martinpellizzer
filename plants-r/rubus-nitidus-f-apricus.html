Rubus nitidus f. apricus

Rubus nitidus f. apricus, commonly known as the white-fruited raspberry.

It is a variant of the common raspberry, characterized by its pale yellow to white berries. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically less acidic than standard raspberries.

This form is prized for its unique color and flavor, making it a popular choice in specialty gardening and culinary applications.
