Rosa parviflora var. glandulosa

Rosa parviflora var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with glandular dots on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
