Rosa gentiliana

Rosa gentiliana, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves, with the flowers often appearing in clusters. Rosa gentiliana is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
