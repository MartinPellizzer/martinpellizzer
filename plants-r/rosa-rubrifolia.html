Rosa rubrifolia

Rosa rubrifolia, commonly known as the red-leaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage, which contrasts strikingly with its pink to white flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and the plant produces fragrant blooms throughout the summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
