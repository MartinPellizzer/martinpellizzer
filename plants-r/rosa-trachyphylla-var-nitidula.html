Rosa trachyphylla var. nitidula

Rosa trachyphylla var. nitidula, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed rose, is a variety of the shrub rose species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often hairless stems and large, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to red. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly serrated edge. This plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine.
