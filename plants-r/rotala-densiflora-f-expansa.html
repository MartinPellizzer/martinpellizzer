Rotala densiflora f. expansa

Rotala densiflora f. expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf waterwheel plant, is a compact aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features dense, rounded leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy texture, which help it thrive in slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stems, giving it a distinctive wheel-like appearance. It is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its ornamental value and ability to oxygenate water.

Its adaptability to various water conditions makes it a popular choice for both natural and artificial aquatic habitats.
