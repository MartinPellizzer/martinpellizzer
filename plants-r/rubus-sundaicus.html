Rubus sundaicus

Rubus sundaicus, commonly known as the Sunda bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Indonesian archipelago, particularly found in parts of Sumatra and Java. The plant is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. Its leaves are usually serrated and have a hairy texture, while the fruits are small, aggregate berries that are edible.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
