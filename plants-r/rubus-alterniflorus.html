Rubus alterniflorus

Rubus alterniflorus, commonly known as the alternate-flowered blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, a cluster of small, dark berries, ripens in late summer and is edible.

Rubus alterniflorus is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and streambanks.
