Rosa indica var. acuminata

Rosa indica var. acuminata, commonly known as the Indian rose or the Kashmiri rose, is a cultivar of the rosa indica species.

It is characterized by its delicate pink to white flowers and fragrant aroma, which are highly valued in perfumery and floral arrangements. The plant is native to the Himalayan regions of India and is often cultivated for its ornamental beauty. It typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to gardens and landscapes.
