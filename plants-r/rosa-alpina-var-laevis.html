Rosa alpina var. laevis

Rosa alpina var. laevis, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
