Rhynchosia viscosa subsp. violacea

Rhynchosia viscosa subsp.

violacea, commonly known as violet sticky legume, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, sticky stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

The flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals, and the plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The species is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
