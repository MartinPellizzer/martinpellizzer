Rubus plicatus subsp. opacus

Rubus plicatus subsp.

opacus, commonly known as the dark-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, overlapping leaf surfaces and less showy flowers compared to other Rubus species.
