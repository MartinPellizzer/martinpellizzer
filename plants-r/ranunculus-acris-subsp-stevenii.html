Ranunculus acris subsp. stevenii

Ranunculus acris subsp.

stevenii, commonly known as the Steven's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in high-altitude meadows.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
