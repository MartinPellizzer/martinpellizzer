Rosa uncinella var. affinita

Rosa uncinella var. affinita, commonly known as the creeping rose or wild rose, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features thorned stems and simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The fruit is a red, hairy rose hip that matures in autumn, containing seeds within its fuzzy covering.
