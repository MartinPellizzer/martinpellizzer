Rhododendron hirsutum f. album

Rhododendron hirsutum f. album, commonly known as the silver-leaf rhododendron, is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage.

It produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the Himalayas and thrives in cool, moist, acidic soils with partial shade. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the year, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is valued for its hardiness and ability to form dense thickets in suitable climates.
