Rosa micrantha var. septicola

Rosa micrantha var. septicola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rose, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red, hairy achenes that mature in the fall, and the plant is often found in moist, wooded areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
