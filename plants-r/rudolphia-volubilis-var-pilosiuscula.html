Rudolphia volubilis var. pilosiuscula

Rudolphia volubilis var. pilosiuscula, commonly known as the hairy rudolphia, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features slender, twining stems that allow it to ascend supports and other vegetation. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered with fine, hairy pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and usually have a distinctive violet or purple hue.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
