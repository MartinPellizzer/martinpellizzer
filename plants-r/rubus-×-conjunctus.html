Rubus × conjunctus

Rubus × conjunctus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (the common blackberry) and Rubus idaeus (the red raspberry). The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color, with a tart flavor and a hairy surface. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruits, often cultivated in gardens and small-scale agriculture.
