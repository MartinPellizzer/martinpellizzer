Ranunculus orphnodes

Ranunculus orphnodes, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers have numerous petal-like sepals.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
