Rosa procumbens

Rosa procumbens, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Rosa procumbens is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy ornamental plant in gardens.
