Rubus longicuspidatus var. discolor

Rubus longicuspidatus var. discolor, commonly known as the variable blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, woodland habitats.

The varietal name "discolor" refers to the variation in leaf coloration, often featuring lighter veins or spots.
