Rubus adornatus subsp. hostilis

Rubus adornatus subsp.

hostilis, commonly known as the thorny raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, thorned stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and resistant to grazing.
