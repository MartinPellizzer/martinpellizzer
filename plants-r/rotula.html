Rotula

Rotula, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers and silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant is native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and is often found growing in salty, well-drained soils. Rotula has a compact growth habit and is often used in coastal gardens for its ornamental value.

Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
