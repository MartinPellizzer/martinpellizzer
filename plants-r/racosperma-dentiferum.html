Racosperma dentiferum

Racosperma dentiferum, commonly known as the serrated acacia, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Racosperma dentiferum is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
