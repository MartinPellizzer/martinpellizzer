Ranunculus hydrocharis f. bungei

Ranunculus hydrocharis f. bungei, commonly known as the Bunge's water crowfoot, is a aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, floating leaves and bright yellow flowers.

It typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. The plant's leaves are divided into small, thread-like segments, and its flowers are usually borne on short stems above the water surface. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic compounds.

This variety is notable for its distinct morphological features and ecological adaptations to aquatic environments.
