Ruellia stenophylla

Ruellia stenophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue or purple tubular flowers. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.

Ruellia stenophylla is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
