Rhamnus lycioides subsp. borgiae

Rhamnus lycioides subsp.

borgiae, commonly known as the Borgia's buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, orange-red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh conditions.
