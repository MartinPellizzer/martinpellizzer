Raphanus sativus convar. esculentus

Raphanus sativus convar. esculentus, commonly known as the common radish, is a biennial or annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its fleshy, edible roots and upright, branching stems covered in small, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which are followed by round, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its roots are typically white or pink and are cultivated for their crisp, slightly spicy flavor.

The plant is adapted to temperate climates and is widely grown as a vegetable in gardens and agriculture.
