Rubus fallax

Rubus fallax, commonly known as the fallax blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is both ecologically significant and occasionally considered a weed in agricultural settings.
