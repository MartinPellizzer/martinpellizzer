Rubus vitodurensis

Rubus vitodurensis, commonly known as the Sicilian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy shrub suitable for coastal and mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
