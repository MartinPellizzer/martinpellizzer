Rhododendron hylothreptum

Rhododendron hylothreptum, commonly known as the yellow rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dense, evergreen forests at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often covered with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The plant belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
