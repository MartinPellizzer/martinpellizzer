Rosa coriifolia var. tristis

Rosa coriifolia var. tristis, commonly known as the sad bristly rose, is a variant of the bristly rose native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, thorny stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and the plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms and unique appearance.

This variety is also noted for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil conditions.
