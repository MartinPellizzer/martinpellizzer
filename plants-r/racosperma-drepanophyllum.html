Racosperma drepanophyllum

Racosperma drepanophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with narrow, serrated leaflets. It produces yellow flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which are typical of the genus.

Racosperma drepanophyllum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
