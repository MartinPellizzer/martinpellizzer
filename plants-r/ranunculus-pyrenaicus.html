Ranunculus pyrenaicus

Ranunculus pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, herbaceous stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and meadows. It produces small, greenish-yellow seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Ranunculus pyrenaicus is often found in alpine and subalpine zones, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates.
