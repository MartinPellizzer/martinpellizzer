Rubus × pseudovulnerificus

Rubus × pseudovulnerificus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which consist of numerous small drupelets. The plant typically grows as a shrub, often found in temperate regions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces flowers that range in color from white to pink.

This hybrid is valued for its edible fruits, which are prized for their flavor and versatility in culinary uses.
