Rhamnus tomentella subsp. cuspidata

Rhamnus tomentella subsp.

cuspidata, commonly known as the woolly buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, dark berries.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and woodland ecosystems. Its leaves are generally oval-shaped with a pointed tip, and the bark is covered in a white, felt-like layer.

It plays a role in providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
