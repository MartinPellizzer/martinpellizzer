Rubus serpens var. elegans

Rubus serpens var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically eaten by wildlife. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, woodland environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local fauna.
