Rhamnus infectoria var. humifusa

Rhamnus infectoria var. humifusa, commonly known as the low dogwood, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by red or black berries in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
