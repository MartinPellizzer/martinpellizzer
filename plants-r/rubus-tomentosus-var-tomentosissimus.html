Rubus tomentosus var. tomentosissimus

Rubus tomentosus var. tomentosissimus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and thorny stems, which are covered in a thick layer of white tomentum. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically elliptical with serrated edges and a velvety texture.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruits.
