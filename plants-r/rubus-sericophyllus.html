Rubus sericophyllus

Rubus sericophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its glossy, silver-tomentose leaves and small, dark purple to black fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late spring, producing white or pale pink flowers.

The silverleaf blackberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in local ecosystems to support wildlife.
