Randia rubens

Randia rubens, commonly known as red randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, with woody stems and opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Randia rubens is also known for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
