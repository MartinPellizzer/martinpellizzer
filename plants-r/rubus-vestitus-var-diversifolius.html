Rubus vestitus var. diversifolius

Rubus vestitus var. diversifolius, commonly known as the variable bracted raspberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, lobed leaves that vary in shape, hence its specific epithet *diversifolius*. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
