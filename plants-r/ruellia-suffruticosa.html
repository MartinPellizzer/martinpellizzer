Ruellia suffruticosa

Ruellia suffruticosa, commonly known as the purple false heather, is a flowering plant native to the warm regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub-like perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically blue to purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive hooded shape.

Ruellia suffruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil conditions.
