Rubus × rydbergi

Rubus × rydbergi, commonly known as the Rydberg raspberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep red color. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white with five petals.

This species is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
