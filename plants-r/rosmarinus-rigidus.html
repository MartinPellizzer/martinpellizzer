Rosmarinus rigidus

Rosmarinus rigidus, commonly known as rigid rosemary, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are widely used in aromatherapy and culinary applications.

Rosmarinus rigidus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
