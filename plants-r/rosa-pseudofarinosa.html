Rosa pseudofarinosa

Rosa pseudofarinosa, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Rosa pseudofarinosa is adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is often found in rocky or well-drained soils.
