Rubus wahlbergii var. hoplites

Rubus wahlbergii var. hoplites, commonly known as the Wahlberg's bramble, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are aggregate berries, typically red when mature, and are edible.

It thrives in a variety of habitats, including coastal areas and rocky soils.
