Rubus × avius

Rubus × avius, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry or loganberry, is a species resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus gebleri (the raspberry).

It is a perennial shrub that produces edible berries with a deep purple to black color, often having a tart flavor. The plant has thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its berries are widely cultivated for both culinary and commercial purposes.
