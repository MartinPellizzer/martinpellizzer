Rubus × heimerlii

Rubus × heimerlii, commonly known as the Heimerli blackberry, is a hybrid plant within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces dark purple to black berries that are typically larger and more flavorful than those of its parent species. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and autumn coloration.

However, it requires careful management due to its aggressive growth habit and potential to become invasive in certain environments.
