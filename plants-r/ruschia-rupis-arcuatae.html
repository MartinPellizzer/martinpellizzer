Ruschia rupis-arcuatae

Ruschia rupis-arcuatae, commonly known as the rock-spurflower, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, branching stems covered in dense, woolly foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular ornamental plant in dry climates.
