Rosa pygmaea

Rosa pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf garden rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pink to white blooms in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa pygmaea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
