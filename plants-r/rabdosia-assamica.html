Rabdosia assamica

Rabdosia assamica, commonly known as Assam Rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rabdosia assamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
