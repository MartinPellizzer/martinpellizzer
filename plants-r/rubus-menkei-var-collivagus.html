Rubus menkei var. collivagus

Rubus menkei var. collivagus, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Its berries are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
