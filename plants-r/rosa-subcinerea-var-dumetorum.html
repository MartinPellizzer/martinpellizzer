Rosa subcinerea var. dumetorum

Rosa subcinerea var. dumetorum, commonly known as the silverleaf rock rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pale silver-gray foliage and small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs, which help reduce water loss and reflect sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
