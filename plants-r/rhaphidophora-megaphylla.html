Rhaphidophora megaphylla

Rhaphidophora megaphylla, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental and edible plants. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense foliage.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in humid environments.
