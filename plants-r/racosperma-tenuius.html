Racosperma tenuius

Racosperma tenuius, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a small shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Racosperma tenuius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
