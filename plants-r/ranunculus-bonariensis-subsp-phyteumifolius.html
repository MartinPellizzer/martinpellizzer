Ranunculus bonariensis subsp. phyteumifolius

Ranunculus bonariensis subsp.

phyteumifolius, commonly known as the Argentine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America. It features delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers bloom in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive leaf morphology and habitat preferences.
