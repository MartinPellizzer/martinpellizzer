Randia hirsuta

Randia hirsuta, commonly known as hairy randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by berries that are often red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Randia hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
