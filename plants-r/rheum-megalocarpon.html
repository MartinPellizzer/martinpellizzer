Rheum megalocarpon

Rheum megalocarpon, commonly known as the giant rhubarb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, with a deep green color and a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces tall, reddish stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant contain toxic compounds and should not be ingested without proper preparation.
