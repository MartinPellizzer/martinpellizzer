Rosa malyi var. genuina

Rosa malyi var. genuina, commonly known as the Tibetan rose, is a rare and endemic species native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers with a delicate fragrance. The plant has simple, trifoliate leaves that are hairy and adapted to the cold, arid conditions of its habitat. Its botanical features include a woody stem with thorns and small, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

This variety is highly valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
