Rosa × collina subsp. macrantha

Rosa × collina subsp.

macrantha, commonly known as the Macrantha shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa, characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant produces showy, semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its thorny stems and evergreen or semi-evergreen leaves make it a hardy ornamental shrub suitable for garden cultivation. Rosa × collina subsp.

macrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
