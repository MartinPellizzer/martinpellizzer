Racosperma pusillum

Racosperma pusillum, commonly known as the small-flowered racosperma, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Racosperma pusillum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
