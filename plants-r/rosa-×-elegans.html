Rosa × elegans

Rosa × elegans, commonly known as the elegant rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa gallica and Rosa damascena.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as a shrub or small tree. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to fully double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the blooms.

Rosa × elegans is well-suited for gardens and is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its historical significance in horticulture.
