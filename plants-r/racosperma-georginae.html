Racosperma georginae

Racosperma georginae, commonly known as the Georgina acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with compound leaves and yellow flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought conditions.
