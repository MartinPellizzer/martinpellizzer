Restrepia antennifera

Restrepia antennifera, commonly known as the long-antenna orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green leaves and its striking, fragrant flowers that resemble small, elongated orchids. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires good air circulation and moderate watering.

Restrepia antennifera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
