Ranunculus retusatus

Ranunculus retusatus, commonly known as the blunt-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, nodding flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are rounded at the base and have a distinctive, slightly wavy margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
