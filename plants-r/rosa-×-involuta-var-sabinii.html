Rosa × involuta var. sabinii

Rosa × involuta var. sabinii, commonly known as the Sabin's climbing rose, is a hybrid species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and produces large, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant features compound leaves with serrated margins and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It is native to the United States and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
