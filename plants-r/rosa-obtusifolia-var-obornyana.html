Rosa obtusifolia var. obornyana

Rosa obtusifolia var. obornyana, commonly known as the Turkish rose, is a species of shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the flowers are often used in traditional medicine and perfumery.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
