Rosa transsilvanica var. hirtella

Rosa transsilvanica var. hirtella, commonly known as the Hirtel's rose, is a shrub-like perennial native to Eastern Europe.

It features thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring and summer. It is adapted to temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
