Rosa × dumetorum f. laevistyla

Rosa × dumetorum f. laevistyla, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed dog rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its semi-woody stems that are smooth and slightly hairy, distinguishing it from other rose varieties. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
