Rosa cechica

Rosa cechica, commonly known as the Cech's rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring multiple stems covered in thorns. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on long pedicels. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 5 to 7 oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

Rosa cechica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardy nature.
