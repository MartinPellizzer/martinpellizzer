Rubus radula var. trachycaulon

Rubus radula var. trachycaulon, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and dark, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is generally smaller than typical blackberries and has a more tart flavor.
