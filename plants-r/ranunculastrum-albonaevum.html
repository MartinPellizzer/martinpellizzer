Ranunculastrum albonaevum

Ranunculastrum albonaevum, commonly known as the white-flowered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with five white petals and yellow stamens, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some regions.
