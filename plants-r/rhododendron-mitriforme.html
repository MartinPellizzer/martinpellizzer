Rhododendron mitriforme

Rhododendron mitriforme, commonly known as the helmeted rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often adorned with a distinctive, helmet-like structure on the petals.

Rhododendron mitriforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
