Restio brizoides

Restio brizoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
