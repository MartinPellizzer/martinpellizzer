Rainiera stricta

Rainiera stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rainiera, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Rainiera stricta is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along stream banks.
