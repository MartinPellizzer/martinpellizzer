Rubus weinteri

Rubus weinteri, commonly known as the Weinteri bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Paraguay. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, which are edible, are similar to raspberries in texture but have a more acidic flavor.

Rubus weinteri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of wild berries.
