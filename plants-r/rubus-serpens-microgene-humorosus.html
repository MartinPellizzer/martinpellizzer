Rubus serpens microgene humorosus

Rubus serpens microgene humorosus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
