Rosa afzeliana var. perserrata

Rosa afzeliana var. perserrata, commonly known as the Persian rose or wild rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, hairy rose hip that contains seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
