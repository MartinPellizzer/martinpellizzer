Ranunculus cortusifolius var. rupestris

Ranunculus cortusifolius var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock ranunculus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in mountainous regions of Europe.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
