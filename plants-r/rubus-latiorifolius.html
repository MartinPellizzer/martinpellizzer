Rubus latiorifolius

Rubus latiorifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in local ecosystems as a food source for wildlife.
