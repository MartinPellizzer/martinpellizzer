Ranunculus myriophyllus var. edumeus

Ranunculus myriophyllus var. edumeus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with numerous petals and a hairy, glandular stem. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and is often found in high-altitude meadows.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
