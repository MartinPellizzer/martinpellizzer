Rubus luxurians

Rubus luxurians, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. These berries are often rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor.

Rubus luxurians thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed habitats.
