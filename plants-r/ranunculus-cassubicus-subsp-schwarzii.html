Ranunculus cassubicus subsp. schwarzii

Ranunculus cassubicus subsp.

schwarzii, commonly known as the Schwarz's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous sepals, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant grows from a short rhizome and has trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed. It prefers moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and marshy areas.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
