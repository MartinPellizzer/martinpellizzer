Ranunculus nipponicus var. major

Ranunculus nipponicus var. major, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
