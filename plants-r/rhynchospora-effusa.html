Rhynchospora effusa

Rhynchospora effusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, upright leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered, with three stamens and a three-lobed pistil.

Rhynchospora effusa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
