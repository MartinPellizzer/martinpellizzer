Rubus villicaulis f. silvestris

Rubus villicaulis f. silvestris, commonly known as the wild blackberry shrub, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

This subspecies is valued for its wild, robust flavor and is commonly used in foraging and local culinary traditions.
