Rosa syntoma

Rosa syntoma, commonly known as the Syrian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of Syria, Turkey, and Lebanon. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Rosa syntoma is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
