Ramsdenia incrustata

Ramsdenia incrustata, commonly known as the rock fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces edible, fig-like fruits that are encased in a tough, waxy coating. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a notable species in the genus Ramsdenia.
