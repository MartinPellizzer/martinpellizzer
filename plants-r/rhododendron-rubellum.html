Rhododendron rubellum

Rhododendron rubellum, commonly known as the redleaf azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers acidic, well-drained soil. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and often have a reddish tinge, especially in the fall.

Rhododendron rubellum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
