Rubus hirtus subsp. pectinatus

Rubus hirtus subsp.

pectinatus, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant produces edible berries that are often used in jams and pies. It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is notable for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
