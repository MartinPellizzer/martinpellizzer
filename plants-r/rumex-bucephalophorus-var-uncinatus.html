Rumex bucephalophorus var. uncinatus

Rumex bucephalophorus var. uncinatus, commonly known as the hooked dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf shapes and often reddish veins. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly triangular with lobed margins, and it produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, often calcareous soils and is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Its presence is often indicative of nutrient-rich, waterlogged environments.
