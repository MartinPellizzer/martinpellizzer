Rubus robinsonii

Rubus robinsonii, commonly known as Robinson's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is edible, though often described as having a tart and somewhat bland flavor.
