Rosa afzeliana f. elatophylla

Rosa afzeliana f. elatophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa afzeliana species.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant is native to regions of Asia, including China and the Himalayas, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, and it produces fragrant blooms during the spring and summer seasons.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
