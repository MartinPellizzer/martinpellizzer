Rhinorchis santensis

Rhinorchis santensis, commonly known as the Santa Rosa orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown markings. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Rhinorchis santensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
