Rhododendron calciphilum

Rhododendron calciphilum, commonly known as the limestone rhododendron, is a species adapted to grow in calcium-rich soils.

It is native to regions with limestone substrates, such as parts of China and the Himalayas. The plant typically features dense, evergreen foliage and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline conditions and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized flora.
