Rosa afzeliana f. subseringei

Rosa afzeliana f. subseringei, commonly known as the pink-stemmed rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa afzeliana species.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinkish stems and semi-evergreen foliage, which provide ornamental value in gardens. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for temperate climates.

This cultivar is valued for its unique appearance and is often used in horticultural displays.
