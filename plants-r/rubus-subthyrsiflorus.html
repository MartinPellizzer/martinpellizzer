Rubus subthyrsiflorus

Rubus subthyrsiflorus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are covered in fine silvery hairs, giving it its common name.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.
