Raphidiocystis ugandensis

Raphidiocystis ugandensis, commonly known as the Ugandan cyst-forming alga, is a freshwater alga belonging to the family Raphidiocystaceae.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a central nucleus surrounded by a dense layer of cytoplasm and a distinct cyst wall. This species is typically found in nutrient-rich aquatic environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its life cycle involves both vegetative growth and cyst formation, which aids in its survival under unfavorable conditions.

The plant plays a minor but ecologically significant role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to the food web and nutrient cycling.
