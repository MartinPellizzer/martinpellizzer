Rubus dolichocladus

Rubus dolichocladus, commonly known as the long-stemmed blackberry shrub, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in woodland areas and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is a common sight in natural habitats across its range.
