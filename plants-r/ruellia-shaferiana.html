Ruellia shaferiana

Ruellia shaferiana, commonly known as the Mexican bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue. Its most striking feature is its clusters of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in spring and summer.

Ruellia shaferiana is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
