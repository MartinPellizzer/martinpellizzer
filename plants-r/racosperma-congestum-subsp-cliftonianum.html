Racosperma congestum subsp. cliftonianum

Racosperma congestum subsp.

cliftonianum, commonly known as the Clifton's wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, often twisted stems and bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant has compound leaves with multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
