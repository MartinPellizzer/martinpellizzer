Rostella variegata

Rostella variegata, commonly known as the variegated rosette plant, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in late spring or early summer. It is a succulent, adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Rostella variegata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
