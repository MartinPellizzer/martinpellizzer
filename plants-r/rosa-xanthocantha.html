Rosa xanthocantha

Rosa xanthocantha, commonly known as the yellow rose or the yellow wood rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are usually solitary, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance.

Rosa xanthocantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
