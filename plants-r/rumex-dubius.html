Rumex dubius

Rumex dubius, commonly known as broadleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist environments such as meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and prominent veins. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in dense, upright spikes.

Rumex dubius is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
