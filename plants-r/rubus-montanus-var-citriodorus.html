Rubus montanus var. citriodorus

Rubus montanus var. citriodorus, commonly known as the lemon-pearl raspberry, is a variant of the bramble plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, edible berries with a distinctive lemony aroma. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is often used in culinary applications due to its unique flavor profile.

The species is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its edible yield.
