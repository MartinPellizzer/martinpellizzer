Ranunculus bulbosus var. osiae

Ranunculus bulbosus var. osiae, commonly known as the Osie's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its often toxic compounds, though this species is generally considered less toxic than its relatives.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
