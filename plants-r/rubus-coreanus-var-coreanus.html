Rubus coreanus var. coreanus

Rubus coreanus var. coreanus, commonly known as the Korean raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically with 5 to 7 leaflets, and produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. The plant thrives in woodland and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
