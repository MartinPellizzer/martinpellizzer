Rivina dodecandra

Rivina dodecandra, commonly known as the red river beauty, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a glossy appearance.

Rivina dodecandra is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
