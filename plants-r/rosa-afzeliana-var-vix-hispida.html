Rosa afzeliana var. vix-hispida

Rosa afzeliana var. vix-hispida, commonly known as the hispid Rosa, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its hirsute (hairy) texture on leaves and stems is a distinctive botanical characteristic.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
