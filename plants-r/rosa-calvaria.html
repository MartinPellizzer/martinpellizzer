Rosa calvaria

Rosa calvaria, commonly known as the "Cape rose" or "Cape mountain rose," is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Rosa calvaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
