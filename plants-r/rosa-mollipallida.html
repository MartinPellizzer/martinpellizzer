Rosa mollipallida

Rosa mollipallida, commonly known as the silky pink rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features slender, arching stems covered in soft, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven oval leaflets that are finely toothed.

Rosa mollipallida is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
