Restio fusiformis

Restio fusiformis, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed restia, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white to pale blue flowers. Restio fusiformis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
