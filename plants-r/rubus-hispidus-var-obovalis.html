Rubus hispidus var. obovalis

Rubus hispidus var. obovalis, commonly known as the hairy raspberry or rough bramble, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
