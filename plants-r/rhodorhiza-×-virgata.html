Rhodorhiza × virgata

Rhodorhiza × virgata, commonly known as the hybrid rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

Rhodorhiza × virgata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
