Rosa accurtaticeps

Rosa accurtaticeps, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
