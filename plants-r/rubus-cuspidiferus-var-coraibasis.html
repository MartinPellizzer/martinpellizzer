Rubus cuspidiferus var. coraibasis

Rubus cuspidiferus var. coraibasis, commonly known as the Florida blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically tart and edible. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its thorny stems are characteristic of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

The flowers are white and clustered, contributing to its ornamental value in addition to its fruit.
