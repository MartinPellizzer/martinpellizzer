Rhus saeneb

Rhus saeneb, commonly known as the staghorn sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often feather-like and turn bright red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which are followed by orange-red berries that are a food source for birds and mammals. Rhus saeneb thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to a variety of soil types, making it a hardy and versatile plant.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
