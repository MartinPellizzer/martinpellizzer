Rhododendron pentaphyllum var. nikoense

Rhododendron pentaphyllum var. nikoense, commonly known as the Nikoense azalea, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features dense, dark green foliage and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during late spring. The plant belongs to the Ericaceae family and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant blooms.

This variety is relatively hardy and can tolerate partial shade, making it suitable for a variety of landscaping applications.
