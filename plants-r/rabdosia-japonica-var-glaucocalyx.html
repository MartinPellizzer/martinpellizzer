Rabdosia japonica var. glaucocalyx

Rabdosia japonica var. glaucocalyx, commonly known as the silver-haired rabdosia, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features erect, branching stems covered in a dense layer of fine, whitish hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is found in moist, shaded environments in its native habitat.
