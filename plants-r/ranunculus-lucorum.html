Ranunculus lucorum

Ranunculus lucorum, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of numerous petal-like sepals that bloom in early spring.

Ranunculus lucorum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, cup-shaped flowers.
