Rumex × subdubius

Rumex × subdubius, commonly known as the hybrid sorrel, is a cross between Rumex acetosa (common sorrel) and Rumex acetobatus (water sorrel).

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically reddish or purple-veined. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Polygonaceae family.
