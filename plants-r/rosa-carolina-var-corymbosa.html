Rosa carolina var. corymbosa

Rosa carolina var. corymbosa, commonly known as the Carolina rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink to red, blooming in clusters. The plant produces edible hips, which are rich in vitamin C and have a tart flavor. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
