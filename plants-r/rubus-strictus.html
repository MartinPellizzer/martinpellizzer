Rubus strictus

Rubus strictus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, black berries that are edible and often used in jams and wines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles and berries.
