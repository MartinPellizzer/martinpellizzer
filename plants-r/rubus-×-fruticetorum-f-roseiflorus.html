Rubus × fruticetorum f. roseiflorus

Rubus × fruticetorum f. roseiflorus, commonly known as the rose-flowered blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. Its flowers are pink to rose-colored, adding ornamental value to the plant.

It is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
