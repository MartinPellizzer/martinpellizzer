Restio brachiatus

Restio brachiatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in dense clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often found in fynbos vegetation.

Its structure and growth habit make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
