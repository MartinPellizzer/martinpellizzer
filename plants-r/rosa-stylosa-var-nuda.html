Rosa stylosa var. nuda

Rosa stylosa var. nuda, commonly known as the naked-stemmed rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces oval, glossy leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
