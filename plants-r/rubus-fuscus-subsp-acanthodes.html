Rubus fuscus subsp. acanthodes

Rubus fuscus subsp.

acanthodes, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that are usually dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is noted for its complex floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
