Rubus rivularis var. hyalinadenus

Rubus rivularis var. hyalinadenus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-colored leaf undersides, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible but not as widely cultivated as other blackberry varieties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found along stream banks and in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
