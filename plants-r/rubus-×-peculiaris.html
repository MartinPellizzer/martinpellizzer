Rubus × peculiaris

Rubus × peculiaris, commonly known as the purple-leaf bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that often display a striking purplish hue. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens despite its invasive tendencies.
