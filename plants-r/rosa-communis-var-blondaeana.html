Rosa communis var. blondaeana

Rosa communis var. blondaeana, commonly known as the Blondaeana rose, is a cultivar prized for its distinctive pale yellow to white flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for garden borders or containers.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticultural cultivation.
