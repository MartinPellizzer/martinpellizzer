Raphanus radicula

Raphanus radicula, commonly known as the Chinese radish or daikon, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy root that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant typically has a rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in the second year of growth. Its root is the primary edible part, rich in nutrients and commonly used in Asian cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
