Rhynchosia rhodesica

Rhynchosia rhodesica, commonly known as the Rhodes bean, is a climbing legume native to southern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. The plant is characterized by its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
