Ribes campanulatum

Ribes campanulatum, commonly known as the bellflower currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Ribes campanulatum is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
