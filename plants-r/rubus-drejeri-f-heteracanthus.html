Rubus drejeri f. heteracanthus

Rubus drejeri f. heteracanthus, commonly known as the heteracanthus raspberry, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring bracteolate inflorescences and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, often in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands or stream sides. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated margins and a hairy texture.

While not widely cultivated, it holds interest for botanists due to its morphological peculiarities and potential ecological significance.
