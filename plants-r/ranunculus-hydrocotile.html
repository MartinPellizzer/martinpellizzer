Ranunculus hydrocotile

Ranunculus hydrocotile, commonly known as the water buttercup, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features delicate, floating leaves that are typically divided into lobes, and its flowers are usually yellow with five petals. The plant produces small, green fruits that contain seeds dispersed by water. It thrives in shallow water and is often found in temperate regions.

Its presence indicates healthy wetland ecosystems due to its sensitivity to environmental changes.
