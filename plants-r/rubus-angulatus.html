Rubus angulatus

Rubus angulatus, commonly known as the angular blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other bramble species.
