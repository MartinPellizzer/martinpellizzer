Rubus plicatus subsp. amblyphyllus

Rubus plicatus subsp.

amblyphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer.

The leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive pinnate arrangement with serrated edges. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
