Rubus fuscus var. acutipetalus

Rubus fuscus var. acutipetalus, commonly known as the sharp-petaled blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white and have five sepals that are acuminate, giving it the specific epithet "acutipetalus.".

This variety is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
