Rubia pedicellata

Rubia pedicellata, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the fruits are small, red, and edible.

Rubia pedicellata is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and disturbed soils, and it has historical uses in herbal medicine.
