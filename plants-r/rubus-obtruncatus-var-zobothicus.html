Rubus obtruncatus var. zobothicus

Rubus obtruncatus var. zobothicus, commonly known as the coastal raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a red or orange color. The plant thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it produces white flowers in the spring.

This species plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and stabilizing dune habitats.
