Rosa substylosa

Rosa substylosa, commonly known as the narrow-styled rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, dark green foliage, and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa substylosa is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
