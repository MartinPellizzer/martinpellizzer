Rhododendron pulleanum var. pulleanum

Rhododendron pulleanum var. pulleanum, commonly known as the purple rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, cool mountainous environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, showy flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many other ornamental and ecologically important plants.
