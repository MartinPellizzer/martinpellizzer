Rosa communis var. major

Rosa communis var. major, commonly known as the common rose, is a species of shrub native to Europe, North America, and northwest Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and ornamental planting.

Its botanical features make it a key species in horticulture and botany studies.
