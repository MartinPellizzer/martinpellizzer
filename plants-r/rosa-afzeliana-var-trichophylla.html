Rosa afzeliana var. trichophylla

Rosa afzeliana var. trichophylla, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed rose, is a variant of the Rosa afzeliana species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
