Restrepia striata

Restrepia striata, commonly known as the striated restrepia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments at high elevations, often in shaded forest areas. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, making it a notable species for orchid enthusiasts.

Restrepia striata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
