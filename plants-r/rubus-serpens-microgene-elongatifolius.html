Rubus serpens microgene elongatifolius

Rubus serpens microgene elongatifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved blackberry, is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thornless stems.

It features elliptical to ovate leaves that are typically elongated, giving it its specific epithet "elongatifolius.". The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are usually dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
