Rubus hirsutus var. brevipedicellus

Rubus hirsutus var. brevipedicellus, commonly known as the short-stemmed brambles, is a variant of the bramble plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
