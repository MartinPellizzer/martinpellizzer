Rubus phyllostachys var. humilidens

Rubus phyllostachys var. humilidens, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that form dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in clusters, followed by red or black berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

This variety is native to eastern Asia and is valued for its hardiness and ability to form thickets.
