Rubus ferdinandii

Rubus ferdinandii, commonly known as the Ferdinand's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
