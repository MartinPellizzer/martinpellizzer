Romulea intermedia

Romulea intermedia, commonly known as the spring bell orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Romulea intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
