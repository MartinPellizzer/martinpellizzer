Ranunculus cassubicus subsp. antae

Ranunculus cassubicus subsp.

antae, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features delicate, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that resemble petals, and it typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high elevations.

The plant has a short, upright stem with basal leaves that are palmately lobed. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in alpine botany and ecological studies.
