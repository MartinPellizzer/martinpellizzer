Ranunculus asplundii

Ranunculus asplundii, commonly known as the Asplund's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of numerous small petals arranged in a cup-like structure.

Ranunculus asplundii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
