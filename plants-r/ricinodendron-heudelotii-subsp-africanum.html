Ricinodendron heudelotii subsp. africanum

Ricinodendron heudelotii subsp.

africanum, commonly known as African tallowwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa. It is characterized by its smooth, dark grey bark and large, simple leaves that are alternate and elliptic in shape.

The tree produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody capsules containing several seeds. It is valued for its durable, heavy wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
