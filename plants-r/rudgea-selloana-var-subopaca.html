Rudgea selloana var. subopaca

Rudgea selloana var. subopaca, commonly known as the dark-leaved rudgea, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Paraguay.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are often densely packed on the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its woody structure and evergreen foliage make it a distinctive feature in its natural habitat.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
