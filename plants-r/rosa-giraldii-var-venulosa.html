Rosa giraldii var. venulosa

Rosa giraldii var. venulosa, commonly known as the Venulose Rosa, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is a shrub-like species with thorny stems and compound leaves that have a distinctive venulose pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a unique appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
