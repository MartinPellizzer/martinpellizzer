Rottboellia triflora

Rottboellia triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and disturbed areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
