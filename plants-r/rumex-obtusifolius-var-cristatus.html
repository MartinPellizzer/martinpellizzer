Rumex obtusifolius var. cristatus

Rumex obtusifolius var. cristatus, commonly known as the curled dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, wavy, and often curled leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist soils along riverbanks, ditches, and wet meadows. Its flowers are small and clustered in loose spikes, appearing in early summer.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
