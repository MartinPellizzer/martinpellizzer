Ranunculus aschenbornianus

Ranunculus aschenbornianus, commonly known as the Aschenborn's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, mountainous environments and has palmately divided leaves. It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

However, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
