Rauvolfia rostrata

Rauvolfia rostrata, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Rauvolfia rostrata is valued in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
