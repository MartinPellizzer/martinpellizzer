Ranunculus Ficaria F. Incumbens

Ranunculus ficaria f. incumbens is a rare and delicate plant known for its striking yellow blooms. It is a naturally occurring variant of the common buttercup, often found in moist, shaded environments. This plant is highly valued by botanists and gardeners for its unique appearance and ecological significance. Its foliage is typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
