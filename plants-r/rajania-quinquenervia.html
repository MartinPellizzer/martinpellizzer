Rajania quinquenervia

Rajania quinquenervia, commonly known as the five-veined orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive.

Rajania quinquenervia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
