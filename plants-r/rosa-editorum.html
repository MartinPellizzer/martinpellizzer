Rosa editorum

Rosa editorum, commonly known as the "Editor's Rose," is a rare and ornamental shrub native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It features upright stems with dense, dark green foliage and produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate beauty to gardens. Rosa editorum prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and historical use in botanical illustrations.
