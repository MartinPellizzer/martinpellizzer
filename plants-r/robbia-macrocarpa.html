Robbia macrocarpa

Robbia macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited robbia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark red color, containing several seeds.

Robbia macrocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to coastal and rocky environments.
