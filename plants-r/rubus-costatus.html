Rubus costatus

Rubus costatus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its biennial stems that produce flowers and fruits in their second year. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces aggregate fruits composed of small drupes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The berries are edible, though some varieties have a tart flavor.
