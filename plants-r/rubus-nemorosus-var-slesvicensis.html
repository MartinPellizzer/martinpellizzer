Rubus nemorosus var. slesvicensis

Rubus nemorosus var. slesvicensis, commonly known as the Slesvig blackberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
