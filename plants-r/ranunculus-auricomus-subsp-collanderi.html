Ranunculus auricomus subsp. collanderi

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

collanderi, commonly known as the Collander's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals, which bloom in early spring.

The plant has compound leaves divided into three to five leaflets, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
