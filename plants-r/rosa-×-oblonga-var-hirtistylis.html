Rosa × oblonga var. hirtistylis

Rosa × oblonga var. hirtistylis, commonly known as the Hirtistylis rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus, characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

It features elongated, slightly curved stamens that give it a unique appearance, setting it apart from other rose varieties. The plant typically produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, with a slightly hairy texture on the stems and leaves.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
