Rubus × pruinosicaulis

Rubus × pruinosicaulis, commonly known as the glossy blackberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and large, dark purple to black berries that are typically covered with a waxy bloom. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in moist, temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is edible, though it has a tart flavor and is often used in jams and preserves.
