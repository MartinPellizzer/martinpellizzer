Rubus oldhamii var. borealis

Rubus oldhamii var. borealis, commonly known as the northern blackberry, is a variant of the Oldham's blackberry species native to the northern regions of China.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

This variety is valued for its hardy nature and potential use in both ornamental and culinary applications.
