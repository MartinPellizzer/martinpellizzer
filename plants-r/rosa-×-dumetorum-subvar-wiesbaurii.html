Rosa × dumetorum subvar. wiesbaurii

Rosa × dumetorum subvar. wiesbaurii, commonly known as the Wiesbaur rose, is a hybrid shrub native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
