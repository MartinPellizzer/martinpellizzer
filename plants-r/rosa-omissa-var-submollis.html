Rosa omissa var. submollis

Rosa omissa var. submollis, commonly known as the soft-leafed dog rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers with a faint pinkish tinge, and its foliage is notably soft and velvety to the touch. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of about 1 to 2 meters, with thorny stems and alternate leaves. It is native to Europe and prefers well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
