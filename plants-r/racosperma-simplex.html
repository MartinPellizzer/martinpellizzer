Racosperma simplex

Racosperma simplex, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Racosperma simplex is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
