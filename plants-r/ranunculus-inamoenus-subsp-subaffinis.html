Ranunculus inamoenus subsp. subaffinis

Ranunculus inamoenus subsp.

subaffinis, commonly known as the western meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to western North America. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often with a distinctive greenish or reddish midrib.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy surface that aids in water retention.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
