Rathkea glabra

Rathkea glabra, commonly known as the smooth rathkea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Rathkea glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
