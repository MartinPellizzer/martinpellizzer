Rubus × superbiformis

Rubus × superbiformis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color, often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and hedging.
