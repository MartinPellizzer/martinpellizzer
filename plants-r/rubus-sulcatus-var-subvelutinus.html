Rubus sulcatus var. subvelutinus

Rubus sulcatus var. subvelutinus, commonly known as the velvet raspberry, is a variant of the brambles native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological studies and horticultural interest.
