Rubus rostratus

Rubus rostratus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local foraging and traditional practices.
