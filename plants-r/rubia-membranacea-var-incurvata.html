Rubia membranacea var. incurvata

Rubia membranacea var. incurvata, commonly known as the curlleaf bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, creeping stems with small, oval-shaped leaves that curl inward, giving it its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are pale yellow and borne in clusters, adding to its ornamental value.

The species is noted for its delicate foliage and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
