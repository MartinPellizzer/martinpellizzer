Rosa heliophila f. alba

Rosa heliophila f. alba, commonly known as the white form of the sun rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

Rosa heliophila f. alba is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and relatively disease-resistant nature.
