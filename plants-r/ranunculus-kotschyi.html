Ranunculus kotschyi

Ranunculus kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, often toxic species. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow or orange flowers with five petals. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in dry environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
