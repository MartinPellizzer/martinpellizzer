Rubus moluccanus var. moluccanus

Rubus moluccanus var. moluccanus, commonly known as the Moluccan blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Moluccas in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and potential agricultural use.
