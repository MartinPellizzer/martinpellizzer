Rubus tomentosus var. bicknellii

Rubus tomentosus var. bicknellii, commonly known as Bicknell's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in jams and pies. Its leaves are densely hairy, giving the plant a woolly appearance, and its stems are also covered in fine, white hairs.

This variety is valued for both its ecological role and its culinary uses.
