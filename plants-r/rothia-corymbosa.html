Rothia corymbosa

Rothia corymbosa, commonly known as the red-veined sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

Rothia corymbosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
