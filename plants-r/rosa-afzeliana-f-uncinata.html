Rosa afzeliana f. uncinata

Rosa afzeliana f. uncinata, commonly known as the hooked Rosa afzeliana, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa afzeliana.

It is characterized by its distinctive hooked thorns, which are a notable feature distinguishing it from other forms of the species. This plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and horticultural qualities.
