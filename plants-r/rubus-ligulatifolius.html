Rubus ligulatifolius

Rubus ligulatifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many brambles and berries. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and have a pointed tip. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark purple berries.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
