Rubus idaeus var. viburnifolius

Rubus idaeus var. viburnifolius, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its unique appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and edible fruits.
