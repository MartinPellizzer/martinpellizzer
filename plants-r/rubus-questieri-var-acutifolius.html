Rubus questieri var. acutifolius

Rubus questieri var. acutifolius, commonly known as the sharp-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red berries. The leaves are typically elliptical with a pointed tip, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and its role in local ecosystems.
