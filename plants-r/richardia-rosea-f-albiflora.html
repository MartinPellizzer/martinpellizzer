Richardia rosea f. albiflora

Richardia rosea f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered roseleaf plant, is a botanical variant of the species Richardia rosea.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical pink or red hues of the parent species. This variant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and can grow as a herbaceous perennial or shrub, depending on environmental conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
