Rubus auroralis

Rubus auroralis, commonly known as the auroral raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

The berries are often used in local culinary traditions and are an important food source for wildlife.
