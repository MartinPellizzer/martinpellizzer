Rosa agrestis var. haringiana

Rosa agrestis var. haringiana, commonly known as the Haring's wild rose, is a variant of the wild rose species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, often thornless stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red hips that contain seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for small wildlife.
