Rhus concolor

Rhus concolor, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and turn a striking red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into clusters of red, fuzzy fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it often grows in dry, sandy soils.

Rhus concolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
