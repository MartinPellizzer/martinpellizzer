Rhamnus seravschanica

Rhamnus seravschanica, commonly known as the Karakorum buckthorn, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by reddish berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh climatic conditions.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
