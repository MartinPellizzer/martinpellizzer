Ranunculus naudoi

Ranunculus naudoi, commonly known as the Naud's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers often have a distinctive central stigma surrounded by stamens.

Ranunculus naudoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, cup-shaped blooms.
