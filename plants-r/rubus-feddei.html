Rubus feddei

Rubus feddei, commonly known as the Fedde's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
