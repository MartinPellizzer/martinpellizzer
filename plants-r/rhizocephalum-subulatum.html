Rhizocephalum subulatum

Rhizocephalum subulatum, commonly known as the slender root-plant, is a unique halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It exhibits a specialized root system that allows it to thrive in coastal and estuarine regions. The plant is characterized by its slender, thread-like stems and small, clustered leaves. Its reproductive structures are often submerged, facilitating water dispersal of its propagules.

Rhizocephalum subulatum plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
