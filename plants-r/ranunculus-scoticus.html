Ranunculus scoticus

Ranunculus scoticus, commonly known as Scottish buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles and northern Europe.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland edges and meadows. Its flowers open in spring and are pollinated by insects.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive, cup-shaped blooms and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.
