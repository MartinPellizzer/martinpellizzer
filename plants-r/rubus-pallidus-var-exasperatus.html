Rubus pallidus var. exasperatus

Rubus pallidus var. exasperatus, commonly known as the pale bramble or exasperated raspberry, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically pale red to yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
