Rorippa × erythrocaulis

Rorippa × erythrocaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed watercress, is a hybrid species within the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, edible leaves and reddish stems, which are often cultivated for their peppery flavor. The plant typically grows in moist or aquatic environments, such as ponds, ditches, and wet meadows. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces elongated siliques containing numerous seeds.

Due to its hybrid nature, Rorippa × erythrocaulis exhibits a range of morphological variations depending on its parent species.
