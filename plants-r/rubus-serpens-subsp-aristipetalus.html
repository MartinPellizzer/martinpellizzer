Rubus serpens subsp. aristipetalus

Rubus serpens subsp.

aristipetalus, commonly known as the arrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive arrowhead shape.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant produces edible berries that are often used in jams and pies. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring partial shade to full sun.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
