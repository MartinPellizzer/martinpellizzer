Rhododendron fissotectum

Rhododendron fissotectum, commonly known as the Sikkim rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, cool, and shaded forest environments. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink to red in color, blooming in late spring. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, with a distinctive waxy coating.

Rhododendron fissotectum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
