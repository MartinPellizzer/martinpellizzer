Rhus glutinosa subsp. abyssinica

Rhus glutinosa subsp.

abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian sumac, is a shrub or small tree native to Ethiopia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 5-9 leaflets.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local communities.
