Rhus brenanii

Rhus brenanii, commonly known as the African burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody, spiny pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Rhus brenanii is adapted to dry conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
