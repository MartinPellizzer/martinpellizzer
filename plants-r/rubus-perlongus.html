Rubus perlongus

Rubus perlongus, commonly known as the long-stemmed blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupes. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Rubus perlongus is often cultivated for its flavorful fruit and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
