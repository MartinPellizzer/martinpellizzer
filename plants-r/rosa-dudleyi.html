Rosa dudleyi

Rosa dudleyi, commonly known as the Dudley rose, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include five-petaled flowers and a structure typical of the Rosaceae family.

Rosa dudleyi is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and coastal areas.
