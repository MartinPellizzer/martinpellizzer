Racosperma brachycladum

Racosperma brachycladum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in dry climates. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife.

Its seeds are often used in traditional herbal medicine by Indigenous communities.
