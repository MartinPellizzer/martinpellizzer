Rubus maassii var. glaucoclados

Rubus maassii var. glaucoclados, commonly known as the Maasii raspberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and clusters of small, dark red berries. The leaves are typically elliptical with a distinctive glaucous (bluish-gray) underside, giving it its botanical name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
