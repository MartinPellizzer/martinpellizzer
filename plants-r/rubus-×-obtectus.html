Rubus × obtectus

Rubus × obtectus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
