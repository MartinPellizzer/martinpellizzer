Rhysolepis morelensis

Rhysolepis morelensis, commonly known as the Mexican morel, is a species of edible fungus belonging to the Morchellaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive honeycomb-like cap, which is typically yellowish with a dark brown or black stem. The fruiting bodies emerge from the soil in spring, often in association with decaying wood or leaf litter. It is native to regions of Mexico and Central America, where it thrives in humid, temperate climates.

Due to its unique appearance and flavor, it is highly valued by foragers and gourmet chefs alike.
