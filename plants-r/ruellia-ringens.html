Ruellia ringens

Ruellia ringens, commonly known as the velvetleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. Ruellia ringens is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
