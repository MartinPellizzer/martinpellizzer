Rubus pilocarpus

Rubus pilocarpus, commonly known as the prickly rose or swamp blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles and blackberries.
