Randia gynopachis var. glabra

Randia gynopachis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth randia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually smooth and lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The species is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
