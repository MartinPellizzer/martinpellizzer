Rhynchospora exaltata var. simplex

Rhynchospora exaltata var. simplex, commonly known as the tall spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and they are arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
