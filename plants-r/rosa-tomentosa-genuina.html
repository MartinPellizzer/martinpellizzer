Rosa tomentosa genuina

Rosa tomentosa genuina, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, white to grayish fuzzy hairs covering its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Rosa tomentosa genuina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
