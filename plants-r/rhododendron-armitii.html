Rhododendron armitii

Rhododendron armitii, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to China, particularly in the provinces of Yunnan and Sichuan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, and it produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers in the spring. Rhododendron armitii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile choice for woodland gardens.
