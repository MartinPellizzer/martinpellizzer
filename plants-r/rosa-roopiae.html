Rosa roopiae

Rosa roopiae, commonly known as the Roopia rose, is a rare species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces hips that are typically red or orange in color. Rosa roopiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
