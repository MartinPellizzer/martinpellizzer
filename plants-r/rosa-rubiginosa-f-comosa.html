Rosa rubiginosa f. comosa

Rosa rubiginosa f. comosa, commonly known as the hairy rose or bristly rose, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fuzzy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine.
