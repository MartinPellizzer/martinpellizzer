Restio rigoratus

Restio rigoratus, commonly known as the rigid restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
