Ranunculus svirensis

Ranunculus svirensis, commonly known as the Siberian buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by numerous stamens and a prominent central pistil. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Ranunculus svirensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
