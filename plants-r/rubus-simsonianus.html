Rubus simsonianus

Rubus simsonianus, commonly known as the Simpson's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is often used in local foraging and has a tart, wild flavor.
