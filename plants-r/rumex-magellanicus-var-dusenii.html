Rumex magellanicus var. dusenii

Rumex magellanicus var. dusenii, commonly known as the Dusen's sorrel, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, which appear in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
