Ranunculus schrenkianus

Ranunculus schrenkianus, commonly known as the Schrenk's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange petals. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and has a short life cycle. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, green fruits that contain seeds.

Ranunculus schrenkianus is often found in meadows and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
