Ranunculus danubius

Ranunculus danubius, commonly known as the Danube buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features bright yellow flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that resemble petals, and it blooms from May to July. The plant typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, green, spherical fruits that contain several seeds.

Ranunculus danubius is an important species for pollinators and is often found in conservation areas.
