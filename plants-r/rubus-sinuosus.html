Rubus sinuosus

Rubus sinuosus, commonly known as the sinuous blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. The plant produces white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, fields, and along stream banks.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
