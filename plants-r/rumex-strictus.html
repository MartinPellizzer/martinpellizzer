Rumex strictus

Rumex strictus, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on tall, branching inflorescences. Narrowleaf dock is commonly found in moist soils along roadsides, ditches, and waste areas.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
