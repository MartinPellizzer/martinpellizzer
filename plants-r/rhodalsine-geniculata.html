Rhodalsine geniculata

Rhodalsine geniculata, commonly known as the zigzag rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant's stems are notably twisted or zigzagged, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
