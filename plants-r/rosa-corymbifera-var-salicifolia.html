Rosa corymbifera var. salicifolia

Rosa corymbifera var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf rose, is a variant of the shrub rose characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are narrow and willow-like, with a serrated edge, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in the spring. It is typically found in moist, woodland environments and prefers partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its delicate beauty.
