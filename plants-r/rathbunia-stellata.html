Rathbunia stellata

Rathbunia stellata, commonly known as the starflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its star-shaped, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Rathbunia stellata has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is often found in the understory of forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
