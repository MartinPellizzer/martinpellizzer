Rosa glandulifera

Rosa glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant pink or red flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish glands on its stems and leaves, which are a distinguishing feature. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and bloom in the spring or early summer.

Rosa glandulifera is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and pleasant scent.
