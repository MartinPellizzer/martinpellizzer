Rosa canina glabrescens

Rosa canina glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth dog rose, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces orange-red hips, which are the fruit, and are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

The leaves are compound, with five to seven oval leaflets, and the plant is often found in woodland edges and scrublands.
