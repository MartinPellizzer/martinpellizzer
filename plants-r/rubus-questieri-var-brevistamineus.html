Rubus questieri var. brevistamineus

Rubus questieri var. brevistamineus, commonly known as the short-stamen blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique stamen structure, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
