Rubus × agrestis

Rubus × agrestis, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry bush, is a hybrid plant species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to various regions of Europe and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas. It has a complex reproductive system involving both sexual and asexual propagation.

Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small white or pink flowers in the spring.
