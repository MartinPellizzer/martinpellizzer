Rosa maximowicziana var. jackii

Rosa maximowicziana var. jackii, commonly known as the Korean climbing rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea and parts of China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has long, arching stems that are well-suited for training on trellises or fences. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
