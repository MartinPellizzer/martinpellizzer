Rubus × semiprolongatus

Rubus × semiprolongatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible berries.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variability.
