Rosa canina var. dumalis

Rosa canina var. dumalis, commonly known as the Turkish rose or the wild dog rose, is a variant of the common rose.

It is a shrub that typically grows in the Mediterranean region, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces red-orange hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. Botanically, it has simple, alternate leaves and is known for its hardy nature, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Its ecological significance includes providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
