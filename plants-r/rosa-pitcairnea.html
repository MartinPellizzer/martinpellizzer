Rosa pitcairnea

Rosa pitcairnea, commonly known as the Chilean rose or evergreen rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom throughout the year. It is well adapted to coastal climates and is drought-tolerant once established.

Rosa pitcairnea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
