Rubus × apriciformis

Rubus × apriciformis, commonly known as the hybrid raspberry or blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants.

The hybrid nature of Rubus × apriciformis results from crosses between Rubus ursinus and Rubus 111, contributing to its unique botanical traits.
