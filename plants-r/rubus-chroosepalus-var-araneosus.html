Rubus chroosepalus var. araneosus

Rubus chroosepalus var. araneosus, commonly known as the spider-silk raspberry, is a species of bramble native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, aggregate fruits that resemble raspberries but have a unique, fibrous texture. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has thorny stems typical of many Rubus species. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms in early summer.

The plant is often found in woodland edges and moist habitats, where it thrives in partial shade.
