Rhynchosia ovatifoliolata

Rhynchosia ovatifoliolata, commonly known as the broad-leafed cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with ovate leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated pods. The plant is characterized by its robust growth habit and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a useful forage crop. Its seeds are edible and can be used in various culinary preparations.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting soil fertility in agricultural systems.
