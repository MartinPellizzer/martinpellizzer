Rosa chrysoderma

Rosa chrysoderma, commonly known as the golden bark rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems that are covered in a distinctive golden-brown bark. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

Rosa chrysoderma is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
