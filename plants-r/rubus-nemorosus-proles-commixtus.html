Rubus nemorosus proles commixtus

Rubus nemorosus proles commixtus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces aggregate fruits, which are composed of small, drupe-like units called achenes. The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, with the leaves often having a serrated margin. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, and the fruits ripen to a dark purple or black color.

This plant is valued for its edible berries and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
