Rhododendron siobriense

Rhododendron siobriense, commonly known as the Sierra Leone rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of West Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blossoms.

Rhododendron siobriense is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
