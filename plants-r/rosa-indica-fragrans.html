Rosa indica fragrans

Rosa indica fragrans, commonly known as the Damask rose, is a species native to the Middle East and South Asia.

It is characterized by its highly fragrant flowers, which are often used in perfumery and aromatherapy. The plant produces pink to white petals that are rich in essential oils, contributing to its commercial value. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The Rosa indica fragrans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in botanical gardens.
