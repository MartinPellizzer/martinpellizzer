Rosa stylosa subvar. fastigiata

Rosa stylosa subvar. fastigiata, commonly known as the upright form of the stylosa rose, is a shrub-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves. The flowers are typically pink to red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and compact size.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
