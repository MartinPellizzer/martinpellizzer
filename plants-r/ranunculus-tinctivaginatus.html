Ranunculus tinctivaginatus

Ranunculus tinctivaginatus, commonly known as the yellow marsh crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh habitats in Europe.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 20-40 centimeters and blooms from late spring to early summer. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its preference for moist, nutrient-rich soils and its ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.
