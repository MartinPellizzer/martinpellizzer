Rhododendron gloeoblastum

Rhododendron gloeoblastum, commonly known as the Gloeoblastum rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range from white to pink, often with a subtle fragrance.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
