Rhododendron nivale

Rhododendron nivale, commonly known as the snow rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas and southern China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. The plant features dense, broad leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and typically white or pale pink, blooming in spring.

Rhododendron nivale is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging alpine environments.
