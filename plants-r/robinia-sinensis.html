Robinia sinensis

Robinia sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese robinia, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The tree features compound leaves with numerous oval-shaped leaflets and produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often marked with lenticels.

Robinia sinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been introduced to other parts of the world for landscaping purposes.
