Rubus belonacanthus

Rubus belonacanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
