Rosa gallica proles officinalis

Rosa gallica proles officinalis, commonly known as the officinal rose, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, and it is characterized by its fragrant pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in herbal medicine.
