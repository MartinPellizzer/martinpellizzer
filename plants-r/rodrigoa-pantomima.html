Rodrigoa pantomima

Rodrigoa pantomima, commonly known as the pink velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has woody stems and oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Rodrigoa pantomima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
