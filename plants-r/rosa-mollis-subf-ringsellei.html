Rosa mollis subf. ringsellei

Rosa mollis subf.

ringsellei, commonly known as the soft rose or creeping rose, is a shrub-like plant native to certain regions of Europe. It features slender, arching stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its ability to spread and cover ground effectively.
