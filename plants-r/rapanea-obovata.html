Rapanea obovata

Rapanea obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed holly, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as India, Sri Lanka, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green, ovate leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruits are small, dark berries that mature in the autumn.

Rapanea obovata is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and evergreen nature.
