Rubus × fultiformis

Rubus × fultiformis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. This plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it produces dark purple to black berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some edible uses, though its fruits are generally less flavorful compared to other blackberry species.
