Rubus hedycarpus var. dynatos

Rubus hedycarpus var. dynatos, commonly known as the Dynatos blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
