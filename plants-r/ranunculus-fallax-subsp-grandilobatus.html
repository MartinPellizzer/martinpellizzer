Ranunculus fallax subsp. grandilobatus

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

grandilobatus, commonly known as the large-lobed buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It features showy, yellow flowers with 5-10 petals that are often notched or lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant has alternate, deeply lobed leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural collections.
