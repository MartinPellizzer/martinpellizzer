Rubus cardiophyllos

Rubus cardiophyllos, commonly known as the heart-leaved bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that often have a heart-shaped appearance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are edible and have a tart flavor.

This plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil.
