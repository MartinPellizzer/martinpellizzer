Rubus × melanothyrsus

Rubus × melanothyrsus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to parts of Asia and has been introduced to other regions for its edible berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit develops into a juicy, sweet-tart berry.

The botanical features of Rubus × melanothyrsus make it both an ornamental and a commercially valuable plant.
