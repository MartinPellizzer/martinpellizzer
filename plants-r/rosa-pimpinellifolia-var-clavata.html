Rosa pimpinellifolia var. clavata

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. clavata, commonly known as the clustered rock rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves with a hairy texture and produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in spring. The plant is characterized by its clustered inflorescences, which give it its specific epithet "clavata.". It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its evergreen foliage and long-lasting blooms.
