Rauvolfia major

Rauvolfia major, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several elliptical seeds.

Rauvolfia major is notable for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
