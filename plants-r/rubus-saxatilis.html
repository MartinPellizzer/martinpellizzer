Rubus saxatilis

Rubus saxatilis, commonly known as the alpine bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often found in mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually white or pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are favored by wildlife.

Rubus saxatilis is adapted to harsh climates and plays a role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats.
