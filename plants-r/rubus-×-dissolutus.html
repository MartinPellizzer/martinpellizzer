Rubus × dissolutus

Rubus × dissolutus, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The berries, which are edible, ripen in late summer and are valued for their flavor and nutritional content.
