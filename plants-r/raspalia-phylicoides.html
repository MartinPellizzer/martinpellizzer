Raspalia phylicoides

Raspalia phylicoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fumitory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often covered in fine hairs.

Raspalia phylicoides is typically found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
