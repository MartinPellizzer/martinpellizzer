Rhododendron quadrasianum subsp. angustissimum

Rhododendron quadrasianum subsp.

angustissimum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
