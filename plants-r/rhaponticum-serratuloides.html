Rhaponticum serratuloides

Rhaponticum serratuloides, commonly known as the Siberian cowpea, is a perennial flowering plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and serrated leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are grouped in clusters, followed by oval, prickly fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in alpine and arctic regions.

Due to its hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated as a forage plant for livestock.
