Rapuntium poeppigianum

Rapuntium poeppigianum, commonly known as the Poepig's cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor similar to watercress.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.
