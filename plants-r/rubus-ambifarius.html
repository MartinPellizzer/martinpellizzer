Rubus ambifarius

Rubus ambifarius, commonly known as the double-flowered blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and its ability to form dense thickets. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated and hairy.

The flowers are unique, featuring both white and pink petals, giving it its common name.
