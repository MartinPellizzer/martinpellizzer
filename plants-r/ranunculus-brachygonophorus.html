Ranunculus brachygonophorus

Ranunculus brachygonophorus, commonly known as the short-stalked buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features palmate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
