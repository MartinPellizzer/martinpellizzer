Raddia aculeata

Raddia aculeata, commonly known as the spiny poinsettia, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround small yellow flowers, which are actually the true flowers of the plant. The leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges and a waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value during the holiday season.
