Ranunculus scapigerus var. concinnus

Ranunculus scapigerus var. concinnus, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often found in clusters along the edges of water bodies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
