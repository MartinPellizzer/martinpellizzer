Ranunculus auricomus subsp. angustatus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

angustatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals, often adorned with a distinctive golden center.

The plant has trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
