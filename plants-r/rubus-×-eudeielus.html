Rubus × eudeielus

Rubus × eudeielus, commonly known as the European blackberry.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus fruticosus and Rubus ulmifolius. This plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The fruits are aggregate berries, typically dark purple to black in color, and are prized for their flavor and nutritional value.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
