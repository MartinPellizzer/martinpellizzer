Rosa adenocalyx

Rosa adenocalyx, commonly known as the velvet rose, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are pinnate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, oval fruits known as hips.

Rosa adenocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
