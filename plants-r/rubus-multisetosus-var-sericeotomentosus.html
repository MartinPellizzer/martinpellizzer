Rubus multisetosus var. sericeotomentosus

Rubus multisetosus var. sericeotomentosus, commonly known as the silver-tomentose blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, silvery-white stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
