Rubus × semitruncifolius

Rubus × semitruncifolius, commonly known as the blackberry or raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
