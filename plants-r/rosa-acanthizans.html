Rosa acanthizans

Rosa acanthizans, commonly known as the thorned rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, which are often covered in sharp thorns. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a fragrant scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa acanthizans is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
