Rubus saxatilis var. elongatus

Rubus saxatilis var. elongatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in woodland edges or mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for various ecological niches.
