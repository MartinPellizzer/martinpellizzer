Rubus innocens

Rubus innocens, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, edible berries, typically black or dark purple in color. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in woodland areas and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The berries are used in local cuisines and have also been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
