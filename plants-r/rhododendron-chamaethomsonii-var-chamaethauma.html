Rhododendron chamaethomsonii var. chamaethauma

Rhododendron chamaethomsonii var. chamaethauma, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in cool, shaded environments. It is often found in the forests of Nepal, Bhutan, and northern India.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
