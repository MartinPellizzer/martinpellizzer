Rubus corylifolius subsp. fioniae

Rubus corylifolius subsp.

fioniae, commonly known as the Fionai raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's stems.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, red drupes that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are typically trifoliate or pinnate, with serrated margins and a hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.
