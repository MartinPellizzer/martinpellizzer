Rosa rubiginosa var. typica

Rosa rubiginosa var. typica, commonly known as the shrub rose or the red shrub rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its fruit is a hairy, reddish-pink hip that contains several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
