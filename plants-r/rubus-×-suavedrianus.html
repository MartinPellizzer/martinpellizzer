Rubus × suavedrianus

Rubus × suavedrianus, commonly known as the Smooth Raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus strigosus and Rubus ideaus.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
