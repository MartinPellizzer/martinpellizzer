Rosa canina subvar. glaberrima

Rosa canina subvar. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth hips rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its glabrous (smooth) fruits, which are typically orange-red in color and used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant produces simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
