Roupala mucronulata

Roupala mucronulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved roupala, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a slight pubescence on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, red pulp.

Roupala mucronulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
