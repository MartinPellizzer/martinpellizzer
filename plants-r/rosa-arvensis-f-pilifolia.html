Rosa arvensis f. pilifolia

Rosa arvensis f. pilifolia, commonly known as the hairy dog rose, is a botanical variant of the field rose.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

Its hairy foliage and flowers make it a unique species within the Rosa genus.
