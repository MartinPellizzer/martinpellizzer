Ranunculus flammula var. major

Ranunculus flammula var. major, commonly known as the greater buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The species is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers and is often found in temperate climates.
