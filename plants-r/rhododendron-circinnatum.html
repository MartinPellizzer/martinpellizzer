Rhododendron circinnatum

Rhododendron circinnatum, commonly known as the alpine rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
