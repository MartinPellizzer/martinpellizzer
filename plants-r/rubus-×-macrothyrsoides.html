Rubus × macrothyrsoides

Rubus × macrothyrsoides, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
