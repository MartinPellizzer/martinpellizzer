Rubus pervagus subsp. fucatus

Rubus pervagus subsp.

fucatus, commonly known as the coastal bramble, is a shrub native to the western coast of North America. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Its adaptability to salt spray and strong winds makes it a resilient species in harsh maritime climates.
