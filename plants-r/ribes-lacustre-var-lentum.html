Ribes lacustre var. lentum

Ribes lacustre var. lentum, commonly known as the lake gooseberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically edible. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

The berries are sometimes used in local cuisines and have historical uses in traditional medicine.
