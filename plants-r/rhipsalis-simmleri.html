Rhipsalis simmleri

Rhipsalis simmleri, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems that produce clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is particularly popular as a houseplant due to its ability to bloom during the holiday season.

Its unique growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a favored choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
