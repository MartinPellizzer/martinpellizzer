Ranunculus oxyspermus subsp. phrygius

Ranunculus oxyspermus subsp.

phrygius, commonly known as the Phrygian crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of numerous stamens surrounding a central pistil.

It is a relatively rare species, with limited distribution and specific ecological requirements.
