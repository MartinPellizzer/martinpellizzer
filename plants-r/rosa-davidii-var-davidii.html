Rosa davidii var. davidii

Rosa davidii var. davidii, commonly known as the David's rose, is a species of shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant produces oval, green leaves with serrated edges. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

The fruit, known as a hip, is a red, fuzzy capsule that contains seeds.
