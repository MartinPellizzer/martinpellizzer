Rubus aquarum

Rubus aquarum, commonly known as the water raspberry, is a species of shrub native to the wetlands of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red, edible berries. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found along stream banks and in swampy areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white and appear in early summer.

The berries, which ripen in late summer, are a food source for various wildlife species.
