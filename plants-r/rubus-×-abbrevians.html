Rubus × abbrevians

Rubus × abbrevians, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color, making it a popular choice for cultivation. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
