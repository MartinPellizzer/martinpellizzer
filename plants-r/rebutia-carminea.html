Rebutia carminea

Rebutia carminea, commonly known as the Carmine Rebutia, is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It is a low-growing, compact plant with a short stem and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features spiny, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Rebutia carminea is well-suited for rock gardens and containers, making it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
