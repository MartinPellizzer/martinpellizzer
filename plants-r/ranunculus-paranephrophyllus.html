Ranunculus paranephrophyllus

Ranunculus paranephrophyllus, commonly known as the Paranephrophyllus buttercup, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow or white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
