Ranunculus subinermis

Ranunculus subinermis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. It is an herbaceous perennial that blooms in the spring and early summer.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy flowers.
