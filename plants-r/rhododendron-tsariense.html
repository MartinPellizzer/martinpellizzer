Rhododendron tsariense

Rhododendron tsariense, commonly known as the Tsariense rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron tsariense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
