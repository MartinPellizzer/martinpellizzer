Rosa dumalis var. cladoleia

Rosa dumalis var. cladoleia, commonly known as the clustered wild rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, hairy rose hip that develops after flowering.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife.
