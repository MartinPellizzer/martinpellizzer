Rosa × dumetorum f. pilosa

Rosa × dumetorum f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy wild rose, is a flowering plant characterized by its thorny stems and dense, woolly foliage.

It produces fragrant pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in rocky or wooded areas. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and covered in fine, soft hairs.

The hairy wild rose is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
