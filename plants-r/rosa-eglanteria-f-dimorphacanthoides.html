Rosa eglanteria f. dimorphacanthoides

Rosa eglanteria f. dimorphacanthoides, commonly known as the two-lobed wild rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa eglanteria species.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed stipules and thorned stems, which are typical features of the Rosa genus. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets that contribute to its ornamental value.

This cultivar is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
