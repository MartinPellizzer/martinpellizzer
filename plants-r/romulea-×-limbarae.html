Romulea × limbarae

Romulea × limbarae, commonly known as the hybrid marsh buttercup, is a perennial geophyte native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and has a corm-like underground stem for storage. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with moist soil conditions.
