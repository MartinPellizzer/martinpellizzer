Ranunculus cassubicus subsp. zmudae

Ranunculus cassubicus subsp.

zmudae, commonly known as the Zmud's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Central and Eastern Europe. It features delicate, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that are often mistaken for petals.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough texture and a distinctive hairy appearance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its cheerful, daisy-like blooms.
