Rosa glutinosa var. typica

Rosa glutinosa var. typica, commonly known as the sticky rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its glandular hairs that produce a sticky substance, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and simple, pinnate leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five to seven petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The sticky rose is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
