Rosa wolfgangiana

Rosa wolfgangiana, commonly known as the Wolfgang's rose, is a species of flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces hip fruits that are typically orange or red in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to colder climates.

Rosa wolfgangiana is also valued for its historical significance in botanical studies and horticulture.
