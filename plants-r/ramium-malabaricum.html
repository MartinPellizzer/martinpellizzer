Ramium malabaricum

Ramium malabaricum, commonly known as Malabar grass, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses, and its leaves are narrow and often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
