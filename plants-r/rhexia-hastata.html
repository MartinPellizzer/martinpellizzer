Rhexia hastata

Rhexia hastata, commonly known as the pinkroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has a distinctive underground tuberous root system, which is one of its most notable botanical features. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Rhexia hastata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
