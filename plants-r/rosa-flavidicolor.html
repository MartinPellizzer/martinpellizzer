Rosa flavidicolor

Rosa flavidicolor, commonly known as the yellow-flowered rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and fragrant blooms, which typically appear in late summer. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in soft, white hairs. Rosa flavidicolor is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and specialty nurseries.
