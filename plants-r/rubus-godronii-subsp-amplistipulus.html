Rubus godronii subsp. amplistipulus

Rubus godronii subsp.

amplistipulus, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically broad and have multiple leaflets.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark red to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
