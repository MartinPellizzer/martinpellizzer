Rutidea insculpta

Rutidea insculpta, commonly known as the roughleaf legume, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by legume-like pods containing seeds. Rutidea insculpta thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
