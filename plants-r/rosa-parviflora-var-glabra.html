Rosa parviflora var. glabra

Rosa parviflora var. glabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are smooth and glossy, often with a slight pubescence on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically appearing in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
