Rubus hedycarpus subsp. ellipticifolius

Rubus hedycarpus subsp.

ellipticifolius, commonly known as the elliptic-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color, often with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
