Racosperma bifarium

Racosperma bifarium, commonly known as the two-lobed legume, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.

Racosperma bifarium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
