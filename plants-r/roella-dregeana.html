Roella dregeana

Roella dregeana, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, spiky flower clusters that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

Roella dregeana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
