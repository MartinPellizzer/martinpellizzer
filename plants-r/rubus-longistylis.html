Rubus longistylis

Rubus longistylis, commonly known as the long-styled raspberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. The plant features palmately lobed leaves with serrated edges and bears white to pale pink flowers with five stamens. Its fruit is typically red or orange when ripe and is often used in jams and preserves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
