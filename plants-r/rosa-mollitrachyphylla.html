Rosa mollitrachyphylla

Rosa mollitrachyphylla, commonly known as the silky-stemmed rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and large, fragrant pink flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cooler, moist conditions.

Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it produces edible rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
