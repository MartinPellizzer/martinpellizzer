Rubus schleicheri subsp. fulvus

Rubus schleicheri subsp.

fulvus, commonly known as the yellow bramble, is a shrub native to southern China. It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more yellowish flowers compared to other Rubus species.
