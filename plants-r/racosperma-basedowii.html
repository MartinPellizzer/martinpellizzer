Racosperma basedowii

Racosperma basedowii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features slender, often zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves. Its yellow flowers are clustered in cylindrical spikes, typical of many wattles.

It is typically found in sandy soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
