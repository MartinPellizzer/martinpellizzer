Rosa sambucina

Rosa sambucina, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its thorny stems are often used in hedgerows.

Rosa sambucina produces rose hips, which are orange-red fruits containing a high concentration of vitamin C. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
