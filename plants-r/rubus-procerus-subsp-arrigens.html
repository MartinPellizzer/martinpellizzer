Rubus procerus subsp. arrigens

Rubus procerus subsp.

arrigens, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and a subject of interest for horticulturists.
