Rothia pinnata var. pallida

Rothia pinnata var. pallida, commonly known as the pale pinnate rothia, is a striking plant characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage.

Its leaves are elongated and alternate, often displaying a pale green to almost white coloration, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland areas or along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked but play a role in its reproductive cycle.

Despite its subtle appearance, Rothia pinnata var. pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
