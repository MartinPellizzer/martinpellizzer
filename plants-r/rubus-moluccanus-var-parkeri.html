Rubus moluccanus var. parkeri

Rubus moluccanus var. parkeri, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a shrub native to the Moluccas and surrounding islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, edible berries that are typically red when ripe. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has a woody structure typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

The berries are valued for their flavor and are sometimes used in local cuisines or as a source of natural pigments.
