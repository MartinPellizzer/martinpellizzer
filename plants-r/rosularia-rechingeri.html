Rosularia rechingeri

Rosularia rechingeri, commonly known as the stone rose, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form with fleshy, rounded leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps it retain moisture in its harsh, dry environment.

Rosularia rechingeri is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
