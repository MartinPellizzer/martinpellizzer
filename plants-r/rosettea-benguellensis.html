Rosettea benguellensis

Rosettea benguellensis, commonly known as the Ben Guellensis rosette plant, is a succulent species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact rosette formation of fleshy, waxy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The leaves are typically green or bluish-green, with a smooth texture and often bear a slight pubescence. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, which are clustered in inflorescences at the center of the rosette.

This plant is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
