Ranunculus ficaria subsp. chrysocephalus

Ranunculus ficaria subsp.

chrysocephalus, commonly known as the golden crown buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive crown-like pattern, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has palmately divided leaves and prefers moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is often found in calcareous soils and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
