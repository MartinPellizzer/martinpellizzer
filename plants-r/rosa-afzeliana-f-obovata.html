Rosa afzeliana f. obovata

Rosa afzeliana f. obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa afzeliana species.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant is native to regions of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates.

Its growth habit includes shrub-like stems with thorny branches, and it produces rose hips that are edible and rich in vitamin C. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and historical significance.
