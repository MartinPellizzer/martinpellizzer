Rosa caeruleomontana

Rosa caeruleomontana, commonly known as the blue mountain rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves, and it produces fragrant blooms that attract pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
