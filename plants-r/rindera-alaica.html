Rindera alaica

Rindera alaica, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Rindera alaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
