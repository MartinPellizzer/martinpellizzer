Rosa caesia var. bartlettiana

Rosa caesia var. bartlettiana, commonly known as the Bartlett's everblooming rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the United States.

It is characterized by its continuous blooming habit, producing clusters of fragrant, pink flowers throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
