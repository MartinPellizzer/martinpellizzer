Ryparosa kurrangii

Ryparosa kurrangii, commonly known as the Kurrangii rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing a dense canopy that helps retain moisture in arid conditions.

Ryparosa kurrangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
