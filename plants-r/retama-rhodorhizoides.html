Retama rhodorhizoides

Retama rhodorhizoides, commonly known as the purple honeycomb tree, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds with a distinctive honeycomb-like structure. It thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, often growing in well-drained soils.

Retama rhodorhizoides is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
