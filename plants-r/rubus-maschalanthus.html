Rubus maschalanthus

Rubus maschalanthus, commonly known as the Indian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in prickly hairs.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
