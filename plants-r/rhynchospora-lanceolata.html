Rhynchospora lanceolata

Rhynchospora lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf beakspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and other moist environments, where it thrives in nutrient-rich soils. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purplish hue along the midrib. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, which are often overlooked due to their subtle appearance.

Rhynchospora lanceolata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
