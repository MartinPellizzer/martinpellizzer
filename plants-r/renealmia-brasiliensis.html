Renealmia brasiliensis

Renealmia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian reed or giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its sturdy, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves. This plant is often found in wetland areas, marshes, and along riverbanks due to its preference for moist environments. It can grow up to several meters in height, making it a dominant species in its habitat.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly and form dense stands.
