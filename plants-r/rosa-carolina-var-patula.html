Rosa carolina var. patula

Rosa carolina var. patula, commonly known as the Carolina rose or swamp rose, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as swamps, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red-orange hips in the fall. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets that are oval and serrated.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
