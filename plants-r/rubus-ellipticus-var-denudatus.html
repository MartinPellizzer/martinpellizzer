Rubus ellipticus var. denudatus

Rubus ellipticus var. denudatus, commonly known as the naked-stem blackberry, is a variant of the elliptic blackberry species.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
