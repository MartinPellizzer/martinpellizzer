Rubus villicaulis subsp. stereacanthos

Rubus villicaulis subsp.

stereacanthos, commonly known as the long-horned bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
