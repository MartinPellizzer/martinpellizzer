Ranunculus auricomus subsp. envalirensis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

envalirensis, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features striking yellow flowers with a distinctive, cup-shaped structure, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is also listed as a protected species in some regions.
