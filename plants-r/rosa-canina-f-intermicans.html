Rosa canina f. intermicans

Rosa canina f. intermicans, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its narrower, more elongated leaves compared to the typical Rosa canina. This form is often found in hilly or rocky terrains where it thrives in well-drained soil. The flowers are usually pinkish-white with five petals and are produced in clusters.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces red, hairy fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C.
