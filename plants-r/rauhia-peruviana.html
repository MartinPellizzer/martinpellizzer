Rauhia peruviana

Rauhia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy blooms. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a compact rosette.

Rauhia peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
