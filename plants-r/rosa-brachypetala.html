Rosa brachypetala

Rosa brachypetala, commonly known as the short-stemmed rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with short, upright stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of many rose species.

Rosa brachypetala is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty and subtle fragrance.
