Ricinus communis subvar. rutilans

Ricinus communis subvar. rutilans, commonly known as the flame dahlia, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that range from orange to red, resembling the petals of a flower. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spiky, three-lobed seed pods. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its showy appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
