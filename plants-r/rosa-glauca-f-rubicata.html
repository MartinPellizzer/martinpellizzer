Rosa glauca f. rubicata

Rosa glauca f. rubicata, commonly known as the crimson blushing rose, is a naturally occurring form of Rosa glauca, characterized by its distinctive deep red to purplish-pink flowers.

This cultivar exhibits a unique coloration that intensifies under direct sunlight, giving it a striking appearance. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and elliptical, dark green leaves. It produces fragrant blooms in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The botanical features of Rosa glauca f. rubicata make it a valuable addition to botanical collections and horticultural displays.
