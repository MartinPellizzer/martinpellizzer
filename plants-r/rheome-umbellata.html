Rheome umbellata

Rheome umbellata, commonly known as the fragrant umbrella plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, umbrella-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, daisy-like flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. Rheome umbellata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
