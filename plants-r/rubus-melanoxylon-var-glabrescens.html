Rubus melanoxylon var. glabrescens

Rubus melanoxylon var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of South America. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
