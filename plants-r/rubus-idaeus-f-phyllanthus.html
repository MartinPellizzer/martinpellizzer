Rubus idaeus f. phyllanthus

Rubus idaeus f. phyllanthus, commonly known as the phyllanthus raspberry, is a botanical variant of the red raspberry.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring a phyllanthus-like arrangement of leaflets. This form exhibits a more compact growth habit compared to the typical raspberry. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature.

It is primarily cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive foliage.
