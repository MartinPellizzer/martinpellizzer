Ranunculus hirtellus var. humilis

Ranunculus hirtellus var. humilis, commonly known as the hairy leaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with basal leaves that are hairy and often lobed. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
