Ribes odoratum

Ribes odoratum, commonly known as sweetcurrant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, red-orange berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during early spring.

Ribes odoratum thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
