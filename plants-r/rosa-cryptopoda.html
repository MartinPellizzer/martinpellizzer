Rosa cryptopoda

Rosa cryptopoda, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub with thorny stems that often form dense mats on the ground. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers in the spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa cryptopoda is well adapted to moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded areas.
