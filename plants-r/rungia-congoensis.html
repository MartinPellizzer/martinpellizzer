Rungia congoensis

Rungia congoensis, commonly known as the Congo runnier, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Rungia congoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
