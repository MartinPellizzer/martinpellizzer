Raphanus sativus

Raphanus sativus, commonly known as the radish, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its taproot, which is the primary edible part, and its upright stem with pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow, cruciform flowers that develop into spherical, hairy pods containing several seeds. Raphanus sativus thrives in cool climates and is cultivated for its crisp, spicy root vegetable.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
