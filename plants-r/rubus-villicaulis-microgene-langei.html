Rubus villicaulis microgene langei

Rubus villicaulis microgene langei, commonly known as the Lange's bramble, is a rare shrub native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a hairy underside, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. Its fruits are edible berries that are small and tart, often used in local traditional practices.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
