Rhododendron tsarongense

Rhododendron tsarongense, commonly known as the Tsarong rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. The plant features dense, leathery leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring. Its botanical characteristics include a compact growth habit and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and contributes to the biodiversity of its native mountainous habitats.
