Rheum lhasaense

Rheum lhasaense, commonly known as the Tibetan rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, particularly in Tibet and Nepal.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and tall, reddish stems. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with its roots and rhizomes being used in traditional medicine for their laxative and anti-inflammatory effects. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique habitat and traditional significance, Rheum lhasaense is considered a valuable species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
