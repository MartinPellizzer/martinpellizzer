Ranunculus septentrionalis var. nitidus

Ranunculus septentrionalis var. nitidus, commonly known as the northern crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of white, yellow, or orange, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for wetland ecosystems and its historical use in traditional medicine.
