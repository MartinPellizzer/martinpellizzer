Rubus dasyclados

Rubus dasyclados, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles and blackberry species.
