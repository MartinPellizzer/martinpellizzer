Ranunculus × lutarius

Ranunculus × lutarius, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 20–60 cm, featuring basal leaves that are palmately lobed and alternate upper leaves that are more heart-shaped. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with 5–13 petals, often with a darker central blotch. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
