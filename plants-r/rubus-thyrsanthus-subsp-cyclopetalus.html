Rubus thyrsanthus subsp. cyclopetalus

Rubus thyrsanthus subsp.

cyclopetalus, commonly known as the cyclopetal raspberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly in regions of Argentina and Chile. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, often used in local culinary traditions. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
