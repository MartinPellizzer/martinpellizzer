Ricinus communis f. atropurpureatus

Ricinus communis f. atropurpureatus, commonly known as the purpleleaf castor bean, is a striking variant of the castor plant characterized by its vibrant purple foliage.

This cultivar features broad, heart-shaped leaves that range from deep purple to reddish hues, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces spiky, yellow-orange flowers followed by distinctive, spiny seed pods. It is a perennial in mild climates and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic and should be handled with care.
