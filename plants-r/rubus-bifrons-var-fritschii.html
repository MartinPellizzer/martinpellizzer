Rubus bifrons var. fritschii

Rubus bifrons var. fritschii, commonly known as the Fritsch's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and have a high concentration of antioxidants. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often thorny. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
