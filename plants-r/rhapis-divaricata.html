Rhapis divaricata

Rhapis divaricata, commonly known as the areca palm, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen palm that typically reaches a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant features slender, arching leaves that are dark green and have a feathery appearance. Its trunk is smooth and often has a slightly fibrous texture.

Rhapis divaricata is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its elegant, compact form.
