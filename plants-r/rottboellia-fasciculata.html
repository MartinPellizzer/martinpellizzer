Rottboellia fasciculata

Rottboellia fasciculata, commonly known as clustergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Rottboellia fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
