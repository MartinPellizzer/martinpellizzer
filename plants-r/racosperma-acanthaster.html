Racosperma acanthaster

Racosperma acanthaster, commonly known as the hairy acanthaster, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.

Racosperma acanthaster is adapted to withstand drought and is an important component of local ecosystems.
