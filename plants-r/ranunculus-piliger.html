Ranunculus piliger

Ranunculus piliger, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features simple, palmately lobed leaves covered in fine, hairy textures. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five to eight petals and numerous stamens.

Ranunculus piliger thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
