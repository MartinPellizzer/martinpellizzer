Rosa camusiana

Rosa camusiana, commonly known as the Camusian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Central Asia and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa camusiana is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine.
