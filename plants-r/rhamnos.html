Rhamnos

Rhamnos, commonly known as the genus of flowering plants in the family Rhamnaceae, consists of over 150 species.

These plants are typically shrubs or small trees, often characterized by their simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. Many species produce berries or drupes, which can be an important food source for wildlife. The bark of some Rhamnos species is used in traditional medicine for its astringent properties.

Botanically, they are notable for their ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
