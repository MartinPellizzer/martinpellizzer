Rosa gallica var. pumila

Rosa gallica var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf gallica rose, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to Europe.

It features dense, thornless stems that form a mat-like growth habit, making it ideal for ground cover. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically pink to red, blooming in early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that remain evergreen in mild climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
