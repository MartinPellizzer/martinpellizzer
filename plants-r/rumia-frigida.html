Rumia frigida

Rumia frigida, commonly known as the cold mint, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It features upright, slender stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are aromatic and have a mint-like scent, making it useful in traditional medicine.

Rumia frigida is adapted to thrive in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.
