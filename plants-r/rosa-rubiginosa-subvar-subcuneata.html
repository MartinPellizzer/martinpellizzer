Rosa rubiginosa subvar. subcuneata

Rosa rubiginosa subvar. subcuneata, commonly known as the red-stemmed rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thorny stems and is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown coloration, particularly on the stems and young shoots. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red hips in autumn. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
