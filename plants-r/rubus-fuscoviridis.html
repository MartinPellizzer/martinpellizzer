Rubus fuscoviridis

Rubus fuscoviridis, commonly known as the dark green bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it typically grows in woodland edges and disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are usually dark green in color. Its flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters, and it produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

The plant plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
