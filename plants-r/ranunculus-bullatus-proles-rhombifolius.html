Ranunculus bullatus proles rhombifolius

Ranunculus bullatus proles rhombifolius, commonly known as the rhombifoliate buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals and a prominent central stigma, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The leaves are palmately divided, often with a rhombus-shaped outline, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
