Ricinus compactus

Ricinus compactus, commonly known as the castor oil plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and distinctive, palmate foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. Ricinus compactus is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and historical use in producing castor oil.
