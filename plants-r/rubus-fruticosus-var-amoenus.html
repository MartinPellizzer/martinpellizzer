Rubus fruticosus var. amoenus

Rubus fruticosus var. amoenus, commonly known as sweet blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, slightly tart flavor. The plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

It is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and edible fruits.
