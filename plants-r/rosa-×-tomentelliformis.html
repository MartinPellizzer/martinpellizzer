Rosa × tomentelliformis

Rosa × tomentelliformis, commonly known as the white everglade rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless stems and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves with a velvety texture, and its growth habit is typically compact and bushy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
