Rubus × rubristamineus

Rubus × rubristamineus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus idaeus, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The fruit is typically small, firm, and has a tart flavor, often used in jams and pies.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
