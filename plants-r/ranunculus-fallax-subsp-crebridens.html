Ranunculus fallax subsp. crebridens

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

crebridens, commonly known as the broad-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and upright.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive central stigma, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Ranunculaceae family.
