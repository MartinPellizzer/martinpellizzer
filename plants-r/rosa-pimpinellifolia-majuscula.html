Rosa pimpinellifolia majuscula

Rosa pimpinellifolia majuscula, commonly known as the giant rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
