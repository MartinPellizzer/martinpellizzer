Rosa cinerosa

Rosa cinerosa, commonly known as the ash-gray rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with dark green, elliptical leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are covered in a distinctive grayish bloom, which gives it its common name.

Rosa cinerosa is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor, well-drained soils.
