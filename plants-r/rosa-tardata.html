Rosa tardata

Rosa tardata, commonly known as the late bloom rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China and Kazakhstan. This plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom later in the growing season compared to other rose species. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while the stems are covered in thorns.

Rosa tardata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
