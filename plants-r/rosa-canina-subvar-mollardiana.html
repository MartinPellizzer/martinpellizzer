Rosa canina subvar. mollardiana

Rosa canina subvar. mollardiana, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a subspecies native to the Caucasus region.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces pink to red flowers in early summer, followed by bright orange hips that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
