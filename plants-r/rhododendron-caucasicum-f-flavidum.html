Rhododendron caucasicum f. flavidum

Rhododendron caucasicum f. flavidum, commonly known as the Caucasian yellow rhododendron, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
