Rhus verniciflua

Rhus verniciflua, commonly known as the Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 11 to 31 leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles, followed by red or orange berries that attract various wildlife. Rhus verniciflua is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought.

It is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural lac resin.
