Rosa gallica var. pygmaea

Rosa gallica var. pygmaea, commonly known as the miniature garden rose, is a compact, shrubby plant native to Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the summer. The plant has dark green, trifoliate leaves and a dense, bushy growth habit. It is well-suited for rock gardens, containers, and borders due to its compact size.

Rosa gallica var. pygmaea is relatively low maintenance, requiring well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
