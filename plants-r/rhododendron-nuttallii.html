Rhododendron nuttallii

Rhododendron nuttallii, commonly known as the Nuttall's rhododendron, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates. The plant features dense, dark green foliage and produces showy, pink to red flowers in the spring. Its flowers are usually clustered in trusses and are known for their fragrance.

Rhododendron nuttallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
