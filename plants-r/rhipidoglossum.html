Rhipidoglossum

Rhipidoglossum, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their unique, zigzag-shaped leaves, which give them their common name. Rhipidoglossum species are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in shaded, moist environments. They produce small, fragrant flowers that are usually green or yellow in color.

The genus is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
