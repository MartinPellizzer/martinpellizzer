Ruellia ruspolii

Ruellia ruspolii, commonly known as the Italian ruellia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, tubular flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Ruellia ruspolii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value comes from both its vibrant flowers and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
