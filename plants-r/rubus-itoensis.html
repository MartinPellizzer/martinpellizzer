Rubus itoensis

Rubus itoensis, commonly known as Ito's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Japan.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local culinary traditions.
