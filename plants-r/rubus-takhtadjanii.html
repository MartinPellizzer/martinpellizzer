Rubus takhtadjanii

Rubus takhtadjanii, commonly known as the Takhtadjan raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep red color. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential horticultural value.
