Racosperma sphacelatum

Racosperma sphacelatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance. It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces pea-like pods that contain seeds.

Racosperma sphacelatum is often found in sandy soils and is used in revegetation projects due to its hardiness.
