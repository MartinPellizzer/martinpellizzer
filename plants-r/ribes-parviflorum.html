Ribes parviflorum

Ribes parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or orange in color, which are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Ribes parviflorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
