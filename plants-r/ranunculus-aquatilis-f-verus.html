Ranunculus aquatilis f. verus

Ranunculus aquatilis f. verus, commonly known as the aquatic buttercup, is a variant of the water crowfoot plant.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, streams, and wet meadows. The plant features delicate, floating or submerged leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its finely divided leaves and its ability to thrive in aquatic environments.

This fern-like plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
