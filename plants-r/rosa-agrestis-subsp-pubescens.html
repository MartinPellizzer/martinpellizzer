Rosa agrestis subsp. pubescens

Rosa agrestis subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy field rose, is a shrub-like species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are compound, with serrated edges and a hairy texture, giving the plant its subspecies name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
