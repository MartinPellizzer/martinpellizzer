Ritchiea grandiflora

Ritchiea grandiflora, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a striking plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its large, stout trunk and its palm-like appearance, with a crown of large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces distinctive, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Ritchiea grandiflora is a slow-growing species that can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it a notable subject in botanical studies.
