Ranunculus uryuensis

Ranunculus uryuensis, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, green fruits that contain seeds.

Ranunculus uryuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
