Ruellia norvegigratiosa

Ruellia norvegigratiosa, commonly known as the Norwegian beauty, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Ruellia norvegigratiosa is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
