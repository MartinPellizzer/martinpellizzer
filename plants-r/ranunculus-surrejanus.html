Ranunculus surrejanus

Ranunculus surrejanus, commonly known as the Southern Balkan buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or white flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
