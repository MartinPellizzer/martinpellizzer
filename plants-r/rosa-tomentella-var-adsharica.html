Rosa tomentella var. adsharica

Rosa tomentella var. adsharica, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a shrub native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and long-lasting blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The leaves are usually covered in a fine, white to grayish pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
