Rumex intermedius var. fissus

Rumex intermedius var. fissus, commonly known as the fissured dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is characterized by its lanceolate, serrated leaves that often have a distinctive fissured appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which are borne on short pedicels. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional remedies for digestive and skin ailments.
