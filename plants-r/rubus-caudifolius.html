Rubus caudifolius

Rubus caudifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, elongated leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
