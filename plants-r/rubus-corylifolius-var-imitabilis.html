Rubus corylifolius var. imitabilis

Rubus corylifolius var. imitabilis, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus corylifolius species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, dark green, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include thorny stems and a complex inflorescence structure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
