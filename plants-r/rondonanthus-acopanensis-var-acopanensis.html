Rondonanthus acopanensis var. acopanensis

Rondonanthus acopanensis var. acopanensis, commonly known as the Acopan mountain orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and in shaded, humid environments, thriving in the cool, moist conditions of its native habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help retain moisture.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native region.
