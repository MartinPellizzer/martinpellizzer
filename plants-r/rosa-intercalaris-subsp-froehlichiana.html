Rosa intercalaris subsp. froehlichiana

Rosa intercalaris subsp.

froehlichiana, commonly known as the Froehlich's evergreen rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant is evergreen, retaining its leaves throughout the year, and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to coastal environments.

Its horticultural significance lies in its unique floral display and resilience to harsh conditions.
