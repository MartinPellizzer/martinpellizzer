Rubus fruticosus var. carpinifolius

Rubus fruticosus var. carpinifolius, commonly known as bramble rose or wild rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are pink or white, followed by red or orange berries. It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.

The species is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible fruits.
