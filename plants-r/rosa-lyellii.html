Rosa lyellii

Rosa lyellii, commonly known as Lyell's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is typically found in shrublands and rocky habitats. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.

Rosa lyellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
