Rubus plicatus var. albiflorus

Rubus plicatus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are predominantly white, which distinguishes it from other Rubus species. The fruit, similar to other blackberries, is aggregate, formed from multiple small drupes.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
