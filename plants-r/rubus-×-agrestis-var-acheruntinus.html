Rubus × agrestis var. acheruntinus

Rubus × agrestis var. acheruntinus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a hybrid plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
