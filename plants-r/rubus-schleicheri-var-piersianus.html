Rubus schleicheri var. piersianus

Rubus schleicheri var. piersianus, commonly known as the Persian blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, resembling blackberries in appearance. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
