Rubus koehleri var. bibractensis

Rubus koehleri var. bibractensis, commonly known as the Bibracten raspberry, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color, which are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
