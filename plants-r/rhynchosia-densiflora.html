Rhynchosia densiflora

Rhynchosia densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered rhynchosia, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its dense inflorescences and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can be dispersed by wind or animals.

Rhynchosia densiflora is valued for its ornamental potential and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
