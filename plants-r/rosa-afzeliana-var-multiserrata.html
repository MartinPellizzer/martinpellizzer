Rosa afzeliana var. multiserrata

Rosa afzeliana var. multiserrata, commonly known as the multi-toothed rose, is a species native to parts of Asia, particularly China and India.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on long pedicels. Its botanical characteristics include pinnately compound leaves with multiple serrations and stipules that are often lobed or notched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
