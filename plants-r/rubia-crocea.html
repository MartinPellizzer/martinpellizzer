Rubia crocea

Rubia crocea, commonly known as the yellow balm, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of shrubs and herbs. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface.

Rubia crocea is often found in open woodland and grassland habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
