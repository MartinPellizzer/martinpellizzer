Rhynchosia leucophylla

Rhynchosia leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive white or pale green leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is an erect annual herb with compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds, which are rich in protein and can be used in various culinary applications.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential medicinal properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
