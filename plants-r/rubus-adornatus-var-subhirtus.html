Rubus adornatus var. subhirtus

Rubus adornatus var. subhirtus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
