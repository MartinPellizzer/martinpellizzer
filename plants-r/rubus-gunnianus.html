Rubus gunnianus

Rubus gunnianus, commonly known as the Tasmanian blackberry, is a shrub native to Tasmania, Australia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and dark purple to black fruits. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically harvested in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, temperate climates.

The Tasmanian blackberry is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in local culinary applications.
