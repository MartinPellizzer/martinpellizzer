Rudbeckia globosa

Rudbeckia globosa, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a North American flowering plant characterized by its spherical, spiky seed heads.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to the United States and Canada. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Rudbeckia globosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
