Rhododendron kogo

Rhododendron kogo, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron kogo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
