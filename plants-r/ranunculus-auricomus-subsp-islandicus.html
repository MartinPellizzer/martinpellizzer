Ranunculus auricomus subsp. islandicus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

islandicus, commonly known as the island buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western Pacific islands. It features bright yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals, often with a distinctive white or greenish central spot.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy surface that helps retain moisture.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
