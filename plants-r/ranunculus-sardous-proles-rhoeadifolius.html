Ranunculus sardous proles rhoeadifolius

Ranunculus sardous proles rhoeadifolius, commonly known as the Sardinian water crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three finely divided leaflets, and it has a rhizomatous root system.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and fish.
