Rudbeckia triloba var. beadlei

Rudbeckia triloba var. beadlei, commonly known as the Beadle's coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear daisy-like yellow-orange flower heads with prominent central cones. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is valued in gardens for its bright blooms and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
