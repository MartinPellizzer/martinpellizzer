Rubus humifusus subsp. viridis

Rubus humifusus subsp.

viridis, commonly known as the green-tipped raspberry, is a low-growing shrub native to North America. It features slender, arching stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture. Its flowers are small, white to pink, and appear in clusters, followed by red, hairy fruits.

This subspecies is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
