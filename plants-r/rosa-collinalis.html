Rosa collinalis

Rosa collinalis, commonly known as the mountain rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, while its stems are covered in thorns.

Rosa collinalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
