Rubus geniculatus

Rubus geniculatus, commonly known as the bent-stem blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, which often bend at the nodes. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy underside.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained environments.
