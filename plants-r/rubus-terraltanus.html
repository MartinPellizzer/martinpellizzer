Rubus terraltanus

Rubus terraltanus, commonly known as the TerraLTAN Thornberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
