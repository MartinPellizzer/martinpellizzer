Rhododendron xizangense

Rhododendron xizangense, commonly known as the Sichuan rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and is adapted to cool, moist climates. Its flowers range in color from pink to white and are often clustered in inflorescences.

Rhododendron xizangense is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a significant role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
