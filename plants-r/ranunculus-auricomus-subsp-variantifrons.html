Ranunculus auricomus subsp. variantifrons

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

variantifrons, commonly known as the variable leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically arranged in a cup-like shape.

The subspecies is distinguished by its variable leaf morphology, with leaves that can be trifoliate or palmate, depending on environmental conditions. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is often found in calcareous soils and is known for its delicate, ephemeral bloom period in early spring.
