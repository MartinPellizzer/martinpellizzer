Romulea fragrans

Romulea fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented starwort, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It produces striking, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a fragrant aroma. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, adding a unique ornamental value to gardens.

Romulea fragrans is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
