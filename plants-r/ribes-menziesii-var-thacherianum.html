Ribes menziesii var. thacherianum

Ribes menziesii var. thacherianum, commonly known as the Thompson's currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and along streambanks. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, which are edible and used in jams and jellies. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and emit a slight fragrance when crushed.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
