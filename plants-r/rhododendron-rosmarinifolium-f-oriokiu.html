Rhododendron rosmarinifolium f. oriokiu

Rhododendron rosmarinifolium f. oriokiu, commonly known as the Oriokiu rhododendron, is a unique hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, resembling rosemary leaves, which gives the plant its specific epithet. It produces showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
