Ramatuela crispialata

Ramatuela crispialata, commonly known as the curly leaf ramatuela, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled, and often silver-gray leaves that give it a unique visual appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Ramatuela crispialata is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
