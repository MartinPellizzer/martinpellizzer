Roemeria ocellata

Roemeria ocellata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, often forming bell-shaped corollas. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Roemeria ocellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
