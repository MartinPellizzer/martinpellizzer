Rosa macrocarpa

Rosa macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, which support its showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces large, orange-red hips that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun.

Rosa macrocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
