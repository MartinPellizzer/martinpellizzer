Rhaphithamnus spinosus f. microphyllus

Rhaphithamnus spinosus f. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved spiny shrub, is a variant of the native Australian plant Rhaphithamnus spinosus.

It is characterized by its smaller, more densely arranged leaves compared to the typical form. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. Its spiny stems and compact growth habit help it survive in harsh conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local fauna.
