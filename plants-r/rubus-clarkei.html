Rubus clarkei

Rubus clarkei, commonly known as Clarke's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruits, which are aggregate berries, are typically red or black when mature.

Rubus clarkei is often found in rocky or forested areas and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
