Rosa canina var. marginata

Rosa canina var. marginata, commonly known as the dog rose with margins, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant is characterized by its pink to reddish flowers and thorny stems. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets.

It produces bright red, fuzzy hips that are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine.
