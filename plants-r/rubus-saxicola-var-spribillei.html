Rubus saxicola var. spribillei

Rubus saxicola var. spribillei, commonly known as the Spribille's rock raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to alpine and arctic regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, and its edible, tangy red berries. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments. It has a low-growing habit with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves.

This variant is notable for its horticultural value and adaptability to cold climates.
