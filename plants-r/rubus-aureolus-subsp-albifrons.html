Rubus aureolus subsp. albifrons

Rubus aureolus subsp.

albifrons, commonly known as the white-eyed blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in the axils of the leaves, producing a distinctive appearance. The fruit, a aggregate berry, is typically dark red to black when mature and has a tart flavor.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
