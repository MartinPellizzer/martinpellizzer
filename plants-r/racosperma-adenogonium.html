Racosperma adenogonium

Racosperma adenogonium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant has slender, often hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Racosperma adenogonium is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils across the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.
