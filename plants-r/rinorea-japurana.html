Rinorea japurana

Rinorea japurana, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains multiple seeds, which are the edible Brazil nuts.

Rinorea japurana plays a crucial role in the ecological balance of the rainforest and is also economically significant for its nut production.
