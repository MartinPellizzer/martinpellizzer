Rhaphiolepis mekongensis

Rhaphiolepis mekongensis, commonly known as the Mekong firethorn, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
