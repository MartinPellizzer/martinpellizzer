Rubus saxatilis castoreus

Rubus saxatilis castoreus, commonly known as the Castoreus blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
