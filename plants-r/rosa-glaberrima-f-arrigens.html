Rosa glaberrima f. arrigens

Rosa glaberrima f. arrigens, commonly known as the hairy rose or the smooth rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa glaberrima.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy stems and foliage, which provide a distinctive texture. The flowers are typically pale pink to white, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom in late summer. This plant is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in woodland and moist habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to partial shade conditions.
