Rubus nitidus subsp. ambigens

Rubus nitidus subsp.

ambigens, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States. It is characterized by its finely hairy, silvery-gray foliage and thorny stems.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the Rubus genus.
