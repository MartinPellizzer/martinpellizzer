Rosa provincialis

Rosa provincialis, commonly known as the Provincial rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by red, hairy fruits called hips. Rosa provincialis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the genus Rosa.
