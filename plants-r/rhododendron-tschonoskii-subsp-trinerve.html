Rhododendron tschonoskii subsp. trinerve

Rhododendron tschonoskii subsp.

trinerve, commonly known as the Trinerved Rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its subspecies trinerve is distinguished by the presence of three nerves on the underside of its leaves.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
