Rhamnus alaternus var. balearica

Rhamnus alaternus var. balearica, commonly known as the Balearic buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Balearic Islands.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, smooth, black berries that are typically consumed by birds. It thrives in rocky, coastal environments and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
