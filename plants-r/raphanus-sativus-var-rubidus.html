Raphanus sativus var. rubidus

Raphanus sativus var. rubidus, commonly known as the red radish, is a cultivar of the radish plant characterized by its deep red or purple skin and crisp, slightly spicy flesh.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cabbage. The plant typically grows to a height of 20–40 cm, with a taproot that is the primary edible part. Its rapid growth cycle allows it to be harvested within 20–30 days after sowing, making it a popular choice for home gardens and commercial cultivation.

The vibrant color of the root is due to the presence of anthocyanins, which also contribute to its nutritional value.
