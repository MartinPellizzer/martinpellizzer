Ruellia ochroleuca

Ruellia ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
