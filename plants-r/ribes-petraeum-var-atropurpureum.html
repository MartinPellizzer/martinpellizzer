Ribes petraeum var. atropurpureum

Ribes petraeum var. atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-stemmed gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black stems, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that range in color from yellow to red. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn vibrant red in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and fruit.
