Rosa subduplicata

Rosa subduplicata, commonly known as the double shrub rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse flowering, with blooms that are typically pink to red in color. The plant features dense, dark green foliage and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Rosa subduplicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscaping.
