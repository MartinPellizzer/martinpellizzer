Ranunculus aconitifolius proles platanifolius

Ranunculus aconitifolius proles platanifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
