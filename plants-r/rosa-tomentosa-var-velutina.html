Rosa tomentosa var. velutina

Rosa tomentosa var. velutina, commonly known as the fuzzy rose or velvet rose, is a species of shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, velvety appearance.

The leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets that are also covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence.
