Ranunculus agerii

Ranunculus agerii, commonly known as the lesser buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of numerous small petals arranged in a cup-like structure.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
