Rubus questieri-ulmifolius

Rubus questieri-ulmifolius, commonly known as the Ulmifolius blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and compound leaves that resemble those of elm trees, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
