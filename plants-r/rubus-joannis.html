Rubus joannis

Rubus joannis, commonly known as the Joannis blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is valued for its flavor and is used in jams and desserts.
