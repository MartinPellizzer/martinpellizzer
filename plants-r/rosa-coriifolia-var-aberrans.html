Rosa coriifolia var. aberrans

Rosa coriifolia var. aberrans, commonly known as the variable coral tree rose, is a variant of the coral tree rose, characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral display.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, pink to white flowers in spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.

This variety is relatively hardy and adaptable, making it a popular choice for both landscape design and horticultural collections.
