Rosa communis var. vicina

Rosa communis var. vicina, commonly known as the close relative or near relative of the common rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its similar morphology to Rosa communis.

It typically features fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, with a structure typical of the Rosa genus. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in autumn, and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It produces hip fruits that contain seeds, which can be dispersed by wind or animals.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
