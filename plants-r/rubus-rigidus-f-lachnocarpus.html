Rubus rigidus f. lachnocarpus

Rubus rigidus f. lachnocarpus, commonly known as the rough blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its rigid, thorny stems and dark, glossy foliage. The plant produces small, firm berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruits.
