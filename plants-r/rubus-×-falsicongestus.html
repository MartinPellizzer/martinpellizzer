Rubus × falsicongestus

Rubus × falsicongestus, commonly known as the false blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, dark berries that are often used in culinary applications.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits variability in morphology and fruit characteristics.
