Ranunculus auricomus subsp. sandemarensis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

sandemarensis, commonly known as the Sandemarensis buttercup, is a botanical variant of the broadleaf buttercup. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with deeply lobed petals that resemble a crown.

This subspecies is native to specific regions in Europe, particularly in areas of the Carpathian Basin. Its leaves are typically palmately divided, and it prefers moist, calcareous soils.

The plant is often found in meadows and along stream banks, showcasing its adaptability to wetland environments.
