Rubus occidentalis subsp. eriocarpus

Rubus occidentalis subsp.

eriocarpus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a shrub native to western North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

The subspecies is known for its edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor.
