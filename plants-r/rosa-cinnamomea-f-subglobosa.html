Rosa cinnamomea f. subglobosa

Rosa cinnamomea f. subglobosa, commonly known as the cinnamon rose, is a compact, shrub-like rose with a rounded growth habit.

It features glossy, dark green foliage and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant is native to the Himalayas and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its flowers are often used in perfumery due to their subtle, spicy scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in garden settings.
