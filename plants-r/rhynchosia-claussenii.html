Rhynchosia claussenii

Rhynchosia claussenii, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are often clustered in racemes. The plant bears pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields.

Rhynchosia claussenii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
