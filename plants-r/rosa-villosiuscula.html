Rosa villosiuscula

Rosa villosiuscula, commonly known as the woolly shrub rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are edible.

Rosa villosiuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
