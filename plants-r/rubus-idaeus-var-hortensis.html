Rubus idaeus var. hortensis

Rubus idaeus var. hortensis, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a cultivated variety of the raspberry plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupes, which are typically deep red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is widely grown for its sweet and tart berries, which are used in culinary and commercial applications.
