Rosa coriifolia f. bellevallis

Rosa coriifolia f. bellevallis, commonly known as the White Shrub Rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, white, single or semi-double flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has thornless stems and elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate flowers and fragrant scent, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
