Ranunculus gyratidens

Ranunculus gyratidens, commonly known as the spiral buttercup, is a rare and ancient plant species with a unique spiral arrangement of its leaves.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant is notable for its intricate leaf morphology, which features a distinctive spiral pattern of lobes. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a protected species in many areas.
