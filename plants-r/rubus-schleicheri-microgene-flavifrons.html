Rubus schleicheri microgene flavifrons

Rubus schleicheri microgene flavifrons, commonly known as the Schleicher's raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
