Rosa vosagiaca var. curticola

Rosa vosagiaca var. curticola, commonly known as the Czech rose, is a compact shrub native to Central Europe.

It features dense, thornless stems that grow close to the ground, making it ideal for hedging or small garden spaces. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing ornamental value throughout the growing season.

It is relatively low maintenance and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
