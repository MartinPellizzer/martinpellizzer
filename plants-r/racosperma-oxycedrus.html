Racosperma oxycedrus

Racosperma oxycedrus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance.
