Rebutia haseltonii

Rebutia haseltonii, commonly known as the Desert Cactus or the Barking Cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Rebutia haseltonii is often grown as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
