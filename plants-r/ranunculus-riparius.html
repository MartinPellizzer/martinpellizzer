Ranunculus riparius

Ranunculus riparius, commonly known as the water buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and aquatic environments in Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, with its leaves floating on the surface or submerged. The plant features small, yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by numerous stamens. Its roots are adapted for aquatic conditions, allowing it to thrive in still or slow-moving water.

Ranunculus riparius plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.
