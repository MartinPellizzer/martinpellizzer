Rudgea sessiliflora

Rudgea sessiliflora, commonly known as the Brazilian hibiscus, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red, with a prominent central stigma. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds.

Rudgea sessiliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
