Rapanea porosa var. magnifolia

Rapanea porosa var. magnifolia, commonly known as the giant porosa, is a variant of the porosa species within the Lauraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
