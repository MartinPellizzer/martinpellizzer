Rhododendron × intermedium var. hirsutoformum

Rhododendron × intermedium var. hirsutoformum, commonly known as the Hairy Form of the Intermediate Rhododendron, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It prefers partial shade and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
