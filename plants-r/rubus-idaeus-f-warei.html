Rubus idaeus f. warei

Rubus idaeus f. warei, commonly known as the red-berried raspberry.

This botanical variant is a form of the common raspberry, characterized by its distinctive red fruits that retain their color even after ripening. It is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The flowers are white and clustered in inflorescences, and the fruits are aggregate berries composed of small drupelets.

It is cultivated for its flavorful berries and is valued in both commercial agriculture and home gardening.
