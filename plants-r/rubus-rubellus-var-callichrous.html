Rubus rubellus var. callichrous

Rubus rubellus var. callichrous, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and they appear in clusters.

The plant is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils, thriving in partial shade.
