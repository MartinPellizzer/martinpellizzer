Ranunculus auricomus subsp. silvestris

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

silvestris, commonly known as the wild buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often surrounded by a ring of white or pale yellow sepals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is slender and hairy.

It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic sap.
