Rosa villosa var. obtusata

Rosa villosa var. obtusata, commonly known as the fuzzy rose or woolly rose, is a shrub-like species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems covered in a soft, white to grayish pubescence. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and are covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
