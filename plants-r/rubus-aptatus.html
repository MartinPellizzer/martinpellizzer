Rubus aptatus

Rubus aptatus, commonly known as the aptatus blackberry, is a species of brambles native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and have mild flavor profiles.
