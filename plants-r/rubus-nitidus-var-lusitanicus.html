Rubus nitidus var. lusitanicus

Rubus nitidus var. lusitanicus, commonly known as the Portuguese bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
