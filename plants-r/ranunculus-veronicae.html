Ranunculus veronicae

Ranunculus veronicae, commonly known as the Veronica ranunculus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy, typically yellow or white flowers with five petals. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
