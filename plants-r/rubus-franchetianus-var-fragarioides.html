Rubus franchetianus var. fragarioides

Rubus franchetianus var. fragarioides, commonly known as the fragrant raspberry, is a species of bramble within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it is known for its aromatic fragrance.
