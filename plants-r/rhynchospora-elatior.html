Rhynchospora elatior

Rhynchospora elatior, commonly known as the tall flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, cylindrical stems and small, triangular leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale green to reddish-brown flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Rhynchospora elatior is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats across temperate regions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and serving as an indicator species for wetland health.
