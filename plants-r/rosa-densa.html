Rosa densa

Rosa densa, commonly known as the dense rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red berries that contain seeds.

Rosa densa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its beauty and hardiness in various horticultural settings.
