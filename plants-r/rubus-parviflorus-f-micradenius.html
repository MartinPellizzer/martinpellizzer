Rubus parviflorus f. micradenius

Rubus parviflorus f. micradenius, commonly known as the small-fruited blackberry, is a botanical variant of the common blackberry.

It is characterized by its small, dark berries and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in moist, temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces flowers that are pale green to white in color.

This form is often cultivated for its unique fruit size and flavor profile.
