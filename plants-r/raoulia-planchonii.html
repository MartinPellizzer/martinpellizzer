Raoulia planchonii

Raoulia planchonii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it produces inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant blooms.

Raoulia planchonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
