Rhamnus crocea subsp. typica

Rhamnus crocea subsp.

typica, commonly known as the yellow buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.

The plant features small, yellowish-green leaves that are alternate and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, followed by small, dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
