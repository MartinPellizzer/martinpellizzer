Rosa agrestis f. pseudoelliptica

Rosa agrestis f. pseudoelliptica, commonly known as the rough-stemmed wild rose, is a botanical variant of the wild rose species.

It is characterized by its elliptical, often slightly twisted leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruits are red, hairy rosehips that mature in autumn.

This subspecies is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
