Rhus osterhoutii

Rhus osterhoutii, commonly known as the Osterhout sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other members like poison ivy and mango. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and have a serrated margin. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by red, fuzzy fruit that matures in the fall.

Rhus osterhoutii is often found in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in woodland edges.
