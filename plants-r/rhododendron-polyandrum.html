Rhododendron polyandrum

Rhododendron polyandrum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often fragrant.

Rhododendron polyandrum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its visual appeal.
