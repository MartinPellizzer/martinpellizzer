Rosa gillotii

Rosa gillotii, commonly known as the Gillot's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are covered in soft, reddish-brown prickles.

Rosa gillotii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
