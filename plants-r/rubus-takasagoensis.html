Rubus takasagoensis

Rubus takasagoensis, commonly known as the Takasago raspberry, is a species of brambles native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
