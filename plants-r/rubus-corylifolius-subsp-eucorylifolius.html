Rubus corylifolius subsp. eucorylifolius

Rubus corylifolius subsp.

eucorylifolius, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry or eucorylifolius raspberry, is a shrub native to China. It is characterized by its large, dark green, palmately lobed leaves and produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous small, red drupes.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks. Its flowers are white and have five petals, and it is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more robust growth and larger leaves compared to the typical R. corylifolius.
