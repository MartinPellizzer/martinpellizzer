Rosa rubiginosa subvar. echinocarpa

Rosa rubiginosa subvar. echinocarpa, commonly known as the rough rose or prickly rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant produces oval, red-tinged fruits that are covered in tiny, glandular dots. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets that are covered in a fine, woolly layer.

This subvariety is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrubland and coastal areas.
