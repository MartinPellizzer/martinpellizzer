Rhamnus brasiliensis

Rhamnus brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for wildlife, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
