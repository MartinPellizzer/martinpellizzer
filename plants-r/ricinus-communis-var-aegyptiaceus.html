Ricinus communis var. aegyptiaceus

Ricinus communis var. aegyptiaceus, commonly known as the castor oil plant, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and distinctive spiny seed capsules. The plant produces toxic seeds that contain ricin, a potent toxin. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its oil-rich seeds.

Its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine make it a notable species in botanical studies.
