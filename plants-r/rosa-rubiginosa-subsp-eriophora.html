Rosa rubiginosa subsp. eriophora

Rosa rubiginosa subsp.

eriophora, commonly known as the rough rose or prickly rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and ornamental species in horticulture.
