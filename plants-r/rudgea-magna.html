Rudgea magna

Rudgea magna, commonly known as the giant rudgea, is a striking plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its bark is thick and rugged, providing some resistance to environmental stress.

Rudgea magna is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
