Raphanus rapa

Raphanus rapa, commonly known as the wild radish, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into round, smooth fruits containing several seeds. It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and often grows in disturbed soils.

Raphanus rapa is an annual herb that can be found in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.
