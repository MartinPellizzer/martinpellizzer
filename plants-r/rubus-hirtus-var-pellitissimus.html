Rubus hirtus var. pellitissimus

Rubus hirtus var. pellitissimus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with a silvery-white underside, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red to black in color when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
