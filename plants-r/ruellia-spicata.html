Ruellia spicata

Ruellia spicata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, upright stems. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers that bloom from late spring to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Ruellia spicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
