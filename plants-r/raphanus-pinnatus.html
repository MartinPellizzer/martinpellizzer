Raphanus pinnatus

Raphanus pinnatus, commonly known as the ferned radish, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, and it typically grows in moist, calcareous soils. The plant features a rosette of pinnately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a peppery scent. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, and it produces round, edible roots similar to radishes.

Raphanus pinnatus is cultivated for its edible leaves and roots, and it is also valued for its ornamental appearance.
