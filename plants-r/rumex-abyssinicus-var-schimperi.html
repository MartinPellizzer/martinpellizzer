Rumex abyssinicus var. schimperi

Rumex abyssinicus var. schimperi, commonly known as Abyssinian sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia.

It features erect, branched stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are edible and have a tart, sour flavor, making it a traditional food in some regions.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
