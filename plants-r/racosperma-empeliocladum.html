Racosperma empeliocladum

Racosperma empeliocladum, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically yellow or pale green. It produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native vegetation.
