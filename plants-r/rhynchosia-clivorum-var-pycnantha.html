Rhynchosia clivorum var. pycnantha

Rhynchosia clivorum var. pycnantha, commonly known as the mountain cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Rhynchosia clivorum var. pycnantha is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
