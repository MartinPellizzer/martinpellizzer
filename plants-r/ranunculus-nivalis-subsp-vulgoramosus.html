Ranunculus nivalis subsp. vulgoramosus

Ranunculus nivalis subsp.

vulgoramosus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the European Alps. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in alpine botany and ecological studies.
