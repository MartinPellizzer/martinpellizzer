Rubus × darnetalis

Rubus × darnetalis, commonly known as the blackberry-boys or brambleberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between blackberry (Rubus fruticosus) and raspberries (Rubus idaeus). This species is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
