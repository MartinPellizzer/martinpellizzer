Rubus plicatus subsp. rosulentus

Rubus plicatus subsp.

rosulentus, commonly known as the Rosy Blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and culinary purposes.
