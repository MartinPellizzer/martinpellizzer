Rubus × pseudodegener f. submeridionalis

Rubus × pseudodegener f. submeridionalis, commonly known as the coastal blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. This plant thrives in coastal environments, where it adapts to salty air and well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with a serrated margin and a hairy underside.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife.
