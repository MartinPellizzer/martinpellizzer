Rhynchanthera cordifolia

Rhynchanthera cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
