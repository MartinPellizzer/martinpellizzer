Rubus rivularis var. horridipes

Rubus rivularis var. horridipes, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry, is a species of brambles native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest understories. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is notable for its aggressive growth habit and ability to form dense thickets.
