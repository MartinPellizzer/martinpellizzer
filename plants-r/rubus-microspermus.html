Rubus microspermus

Rubus microspermus, commonly known as the small-seeded blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit is typically smaller than other blackberry species and has a tart flavor.

Rubus microspermus is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
