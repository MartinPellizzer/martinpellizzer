Rubus corylifolius subsp. maximus

Rubus corylifolius subsp.

maximus, commonly known as the large-leaf blackberry, is a species native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and thorny stems.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus.
