Rosa canina f. sanctamathiae

Rosa canina f. sanctamathiae, commonly known as the Santhamathai rose, is a rare and distinctive form of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its deep pink to crimson flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its botanical features include fragrant petals and achenes that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique coloration and limited distribution.
