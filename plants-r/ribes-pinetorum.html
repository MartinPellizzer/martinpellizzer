Ribes pinetorum

Ribes pinetorum, commonly known as the mountain currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its red berries are edible and often used in jams and jellies.

Ribes pinetorum plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
