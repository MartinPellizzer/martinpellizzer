Rubus subcaucasicus

Rubus subcaucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and shrublands.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry and blackberry species.
