Rosa sepium var. versicolor

Rosa sepium var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its diverse flower colors, ranging from white to pink and red, which give it its name "versicolor," meaning "changing color.". The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rosa sepium var. versicolor is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
