Rubus patuliflorus

Rubus patuliflorus, commonly known as the broad-flowered blackberry.

It is a species of brambles native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina, Chile, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit, a dark purple berry, is edible and often used in jams and desserts.

The plant thrives in moist, temperate climates and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
