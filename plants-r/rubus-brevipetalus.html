Rubus brevipetalus

Rubus brevipetalus, commonly known as the short-petaled blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to North America, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, woodland environments.

The botanical features of Rubus brevipetalus make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
