Rubus infestus microgene holochlous

Rubus infestus microgene holochlous, commonly known as the Holochlous blackberry, is a species of brambles native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupelets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
