Rhodospatha barbourii

Rhodospatha barbourii, commonly known as the Barbour's rhododendron, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its floral display consists of pink to red blooms, making it a striking addition to woodland gardens.

Rhodospatha barbourii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
