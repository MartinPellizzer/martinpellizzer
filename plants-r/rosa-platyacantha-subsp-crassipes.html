Rosa platyacantha subsp. crassipes

Rosa platyacantha subsp.

crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved dog玫瑰, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale pink to white flowers.

The plant produces oval, reddish-brown hips that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
