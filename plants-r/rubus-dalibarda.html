Rubus dalibarda

Rubus dalibarda, commonly known as the alpine bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, thorny stems and produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy and serrated.

The fruit is a red, edible berry that ripens in late summer and is often consumed by birds and small mammals.
