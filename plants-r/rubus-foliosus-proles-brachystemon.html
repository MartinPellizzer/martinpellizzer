Rubus foliosus proles brachystemon

Rubus foliosus proles brachystemon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. Its flowers are white or pale green, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The species is characterized by its brachystemon, a short, thick stem that supports the inflorescence.
