Ranunculus crassus

Ranunculus crassus, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and wetlands.

Ranunculus crassus is known for its attractive appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
