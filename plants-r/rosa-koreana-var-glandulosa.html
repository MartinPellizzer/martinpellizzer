Rosa koreana var. glandulosa

Rosa koreana var. glandulosa, commonly known as the Korean rose or Korean tea rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and profuse flowering, producing clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, giving it its botanical name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional Korean horticulture.
