Rubus dalibarda f. caespitosus

Rubus dalibarda f. caespitosus, commonly known as the creeping bramble, is a shrub-like plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit, with numerous stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, hairy berries. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in fine hairs.

This species is typically found in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in temperate climates.
