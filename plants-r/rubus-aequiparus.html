Rubus aequiparus

Rubus aequiparus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus aequiparus plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinator populations.
