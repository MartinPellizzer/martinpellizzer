Rosa × alba var. suaveolens

Rosa × alba var. suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented white rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from crosses between Rosa alba and other rose species.

It is characterized by its pale white to pale pink flowers, which emit a subtle, sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorned stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and floral arrangements.
