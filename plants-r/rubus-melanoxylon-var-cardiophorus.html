Rubus melanoxylon var. cardiophorus

Rubus melanoxylon var. cardiophorus, commonly known as the blackberry rose or heartleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black stems and trifoliate leaves with a heart-shaped leaflet. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
