Racosperma desertorum var. nudipes

Racosperma desertorum var. nudipes, commonly known as the barefoot acacia, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to harsh desert environments.

The species is also notable for its role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
