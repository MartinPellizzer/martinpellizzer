Rubus drejeri subsp. leyanus

Rubus drejeri subsp.

leyanus, commonly known as the Lyean blackberry, is a shrub native to southern China. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
