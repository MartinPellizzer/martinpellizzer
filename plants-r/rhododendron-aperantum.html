Rhododendron aperantum

Rhododendron aperantum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron aperantum prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
