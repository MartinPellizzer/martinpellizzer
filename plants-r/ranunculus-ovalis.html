Ranunculus ovalis

Ranunculus ovalis, commonly known as the oval buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with five to eight petals, which bloom in early spring. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and can often be found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Ranunculus ovalis is a herbaceous perennial that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
