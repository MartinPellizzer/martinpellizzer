Ranunculus taigaensis

Ranunculus taigaensis, commonly known as the Taiga buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the taiga and subarctic regions of Eurasia.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coniferous forests and tundra-like environments. The plant features a short, upright stem with trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers with five petals. It blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and flies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
