Rhododendron ardii

Rhododendron ardii, commonly known as the Bhutanese rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in Bhutan, India, and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are dark green and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink to reddish-purple, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron ardii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
