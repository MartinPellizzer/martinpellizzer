Rubus hilsianus

Rubus hilsianus, commonly known as the Hilsian blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that produces edible berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by the development of its characteristic berries.

Rubus hilsianus is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
