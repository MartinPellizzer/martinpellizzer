Rubus arduennensis subsp. candicans

Rubus arduennensis subsp.

candicans, commonly known as the Candicane Blackberry, is a shrub native to the humid forests of Central America. It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and covered in a fine layer of white bloom.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant white flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
