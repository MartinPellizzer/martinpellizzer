Ruschia papillata

Ruschia papillata, commonly known as the woolly fireplant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a unique, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it an attractive addition to arid landscapes.
