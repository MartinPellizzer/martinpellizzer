Ranunculus puringii

Ranunculus puringii, commonly known as the Puringii buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are hairy and often covered in glandular dots. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
