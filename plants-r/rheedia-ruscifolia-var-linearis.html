Rheedia ruscifolia var. linearis

Rheedia ruscifolia var. linearis, commonly known as the linear-leaved rheedia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
