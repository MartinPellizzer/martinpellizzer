Ribes cruentum

Ribes cruentum, commonly known as the bloodcurrant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of currants and gooseberries. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible when ripe, though they are often tart and require cooking. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Ribes cruentum is also valued for its ecological role, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
