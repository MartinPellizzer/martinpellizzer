Rhododendron suaveolens

Rhododendron suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features large, showy flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, with a distinctive sweet fragrance. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, and it produces small, elliptical berries that contain seeds.

Rhododendron suaveolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
