Rosa coriifolia var. dimorphocarpa

Rosa coriifolia var. dimorphocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, with oval to ovate leaflets that are serrated along the edges. This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
