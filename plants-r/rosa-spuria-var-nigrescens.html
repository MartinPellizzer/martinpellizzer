Rosa spuria var. nigrescens

Rosa spuria var. nigrescens, commonly known as the black rose, is a species of wild rose native to the northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to almost black flowers, which contrast with its thorny stems and broad, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cooler climates. Its botanical features include a woody structure, simple leaves with serrated edges, and a flowering period that occurs in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its unique coloration.
