Rosa communis var. laxior

Rosa communis var. laxior, commonly known as the loose rose, is a shrub-like plant characterized by its sprawling growth habit and fragrant, pink to red flowers.

It features thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The flowers typically bloom in summer, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. This variety is well-suited for hedging or as a ground cover due to its vigorous growth and adaptability to various soil types.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.
