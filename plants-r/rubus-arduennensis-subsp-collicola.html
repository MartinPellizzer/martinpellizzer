Rubus arduennensis subsp. collicola

Rubus arduennensis subsp.

collicola, commonly known as the mountain raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, edible berries.

The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils at higher altitudes. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh mountain environments and its role in local ecosystems.
