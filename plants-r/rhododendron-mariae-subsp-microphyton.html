Rhododendron mariae subsp. microphyton

Rhododendron mariae subsp.

microphyton, commonly known as the dwarf mariae rhododendron, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically small, elliptical, and dark green, often with a glossy texture.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, usually pink or red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after plant among horticultural enthusiasts.
