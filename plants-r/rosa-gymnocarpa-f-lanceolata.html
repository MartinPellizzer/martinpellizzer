Rosa gymnocarpa f. lanceolata

Rosa gymnocarpa f. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate rose, is a botanical variant of the gymnocarpa rose species.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and distinctive, elongated rose hips. The plant is native to certain regions of Asia and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals, and it produces fruit that is used in traditional medicine.

The lanceolata form is notable for its unique leaf morphology and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
