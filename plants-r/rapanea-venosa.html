Rapanea venosa

Rapanea venosa, commonly known as the veined evergreen or veined holly, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have prominent veins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Rapanea venosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
