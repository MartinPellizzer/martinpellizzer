Rosa afzeliana var. pubescentifolia

Rosa afzeliana var. pubescentifolia, commonly known as the hairy-leaved rose, is a shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It features upright stems covered in a dense layer of fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it its varietal name. The leaves are compound, typically with five to seven leaflets, and are also densely pubescent. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals, and bloom in summer.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
