Rubus macrophyllus var. debilis

Rubus macrophyllus var. debilis, commonly known as the slender-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
