Rhus javanica var. roxburghii

Rhus javanica var. roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red, clustered berries that are a striking feature in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is adaptable to different climates, making it a resilient species.

The botanical features of Rhus javanica var. roxburghii make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
