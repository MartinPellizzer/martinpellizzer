Rubus polyanthemus

Rubus polyanthemus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by edible blackberries in the summer. Its fruits are typically small, firm, and have a tart flavor.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.
