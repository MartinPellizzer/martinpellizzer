Rhododendron bloembergenii

Rhododendron bloembergenii, commonly known as the Bloembergen's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
