Rubus fictus var. validus

Rubus fictus var. validus, commonly known as the Validus Blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. These berries are edible, though they have a tart flavor and are often used in jams and preserves.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
