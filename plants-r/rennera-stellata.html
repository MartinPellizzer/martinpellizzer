Rennera stellata

Rennera stellata, commonly known as the starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are oval and slightly hairy, and its stems are upright with opposite leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant gardens.

Rennera stellata is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
