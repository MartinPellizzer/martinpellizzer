Rubus serpens microgene gratiflorens

Rubus serpens microgene gratiflorens, commonly known as the sweet-flowered blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark berries that are highly aromatic. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
