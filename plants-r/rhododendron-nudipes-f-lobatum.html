Rhododendron nudipes f. lobatum

Rhododendron nudipes f. lobatum, commonly known as the lobed leathery rhododendron, is a hybrid form of the species Rhododendron nudipes.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, often blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its cultivation in various horticultural settings.
