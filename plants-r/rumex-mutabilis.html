Rumex mutabilis

Rumex mutabilis, commonly known as the variable sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. Rumex mutabilis is often found in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
