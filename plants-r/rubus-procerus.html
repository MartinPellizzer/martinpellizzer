Rubus procerus

Rubus procerus, commonly known as the tall blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and thorny stems, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, that are usually dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Rubus procerus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
