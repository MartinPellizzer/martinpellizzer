Ranunculus hederaceus var. omiophyllus

Ranunculus hederaceus var. omiophyllus, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a variant of the buttercup family characterized by its low-growing habit and bright yellow flowers.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in small, glandular dots. The plant produces small, showy flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its rapid spread and ability to colonize disturbed soils.
