Rubus hirtus var. purpureus

Rubus hirtus var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple-fruited bramble, is a shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that turn a deep purple when ripe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its flowers are white with five petals and are borne in clusters, while the leaves are hairy and have a serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
