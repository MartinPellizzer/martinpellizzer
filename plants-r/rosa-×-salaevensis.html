Rosa × salaevensis

Rosa × salaevensis, commonly known as the Caucasian rose, is a hybrid species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Rosa × salaevensis is also valued for its use in perfumery due to the aromatic compounds in its flowers.
