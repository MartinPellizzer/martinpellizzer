Rosa afzeliana var. subpontismartini

Rosa afzeliana var. subpontismartini, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region and parts of Turkey.

It features fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has arching, thorned stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its historical significance in Persian and Ottoman horticulture.
