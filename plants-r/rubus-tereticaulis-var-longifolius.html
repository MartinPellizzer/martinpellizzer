Rubus tereticaulis var. longifolius

Rubus tereticaulis var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
