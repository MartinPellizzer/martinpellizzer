Rosa regeliana

Rosa regeliana, commonly known as the Regel's rose, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, pinnate leaves, and a preference for cool, moist environments.

Rosa regeliana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with specific growing conditions.
