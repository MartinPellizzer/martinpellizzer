Rhododendron umbelliferum

Rhododendron umbelliferum, commonly known as the mountain rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron umbelliferum is often found in temperate forests and alpine environments, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
