Ranunculus brutius subsp. crimaeus

Ranunculus brutius subsp.

crimaeus, commonly known as the Crimson Buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive orange center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, and it prefers well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is considered rare in the wild.
