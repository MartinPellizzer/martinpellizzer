Rhododendron erubescens

Rhododendron erubescens, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its botanical features include showy bracts that surround the true flowers, which are small and tubular.

Rhododendron erubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
