Rubus serpens var. caerulescens

Rubus serpens var. caerulescens, commonly known as the blueberry raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, edible berries that range in color from red to blue. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland areas.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential culinary uses.
