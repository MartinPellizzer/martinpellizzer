Rubus vallisparsus var. viridistylus

Rubus vallisparsus var. viridistylus, commonly known as the green-styled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupelets. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a glossy upper surface. The flowers are white with five petals and are followed by dark purple to black berries.

This variety is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, thriving in partial shade to full sun.
