Rubus thunbergii

Rubus thunbergii, commonly known as the Thunberg raspberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers in the spring. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The fruits are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.
