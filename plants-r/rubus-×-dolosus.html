Rubus × dolosus

Rubus × dolosus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a high sugar content. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature and adaptability, Rubus × dolosus is both ecologically significant and economically valuable.
