Rubia cordifolia f. tetramera

Rubia cordifolia f. tetramera, commonly known as the four-lobed heartleaf rubia, is a botanical variant of the genus Rubia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves with four lobes, which set it apart from other forms of Rubia cordifolia. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest floors. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually a shade of blue or purple, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
