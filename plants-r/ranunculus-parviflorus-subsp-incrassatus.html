Ranunculus parviflorus subsp. incrassatus

Ranunculus parviflorus subsp.

incrassatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by numerous stamens. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often striking floral structures.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
