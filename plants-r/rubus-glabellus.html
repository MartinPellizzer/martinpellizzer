Rubus glabellus

Rubus glabellus, commonly known as the smooth blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
