Rhynchospora macrochaeta var. columbiensis

Rhynchospora macrochaeta var. columbiensis, commonly known as the Columbia spike-rush, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, white to pale green flowers.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
