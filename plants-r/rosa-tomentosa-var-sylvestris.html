Rosa tomentosa var. sylvestris

Rosa tomentosa var. sylvestris, commonly known as the woolly rose or wild rose, is a shrub native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features dense, grayish-white fuzzy foliage and produces fragrant pink to red flowers in summer. The plant has thorny stems and is characterized by its compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
