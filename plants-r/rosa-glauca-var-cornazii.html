Rosa glauca var. cornazii

Rosa glauca var. cornazii, commonly known as the Cornazii shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the Pyrenees region of Europe.

It features dense, upright growth with small, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.

The variety is noted for its cold tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
