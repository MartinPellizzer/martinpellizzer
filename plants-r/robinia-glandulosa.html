Robinia glandulosa

Robinia glandulosa, commonly known as the yellowwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have small, glandular dots along the veins. The tree produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in upright clusters during the spring. Its bark is thick, fissured, and dark brown, providing a distinctive appearance.

Robinia glandulosa is also known for its hard, heavy wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength.
