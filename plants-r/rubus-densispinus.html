Rubus densispinus

Rubus densispinus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnately lobed. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as blackberries, which are edible and highly valued for their flavor and nutritional content. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.

Rubus densispinus plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
