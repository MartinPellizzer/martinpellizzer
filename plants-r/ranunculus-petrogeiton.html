Ranunculus petrogeiton

Ranunculus petrogeiton, commonly known as the rock buttercup, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with prominent central sepals. The plant typically grows in crevices and on slopes, adapting to poor soil conditions. Its leaves are usually deeply lobed and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Ranunculus petrogeiton is known for its resilience in harsh climates and is often found in mountainous regions.
