Rubus × valentulus

Rubus × valentulus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus glaucus. This species is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer.

It thrives in coastal climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
