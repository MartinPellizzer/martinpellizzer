Rosa paniculigera f. rosiflora

Rosa paniculigera f. rosiflora, commonly known as the rose with rose-like flowers, is a botanical variant of the paniculifera rose.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring petals that resemble those of the typical rose species. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with thorny stems and broad, trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, blooming in clusters during the summer months.

This form is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical significance in horticultural cultivation.
