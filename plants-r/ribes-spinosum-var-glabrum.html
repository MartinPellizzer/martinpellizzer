Ribes spinosum var. glabrum

Ribes spinosum var. glabrum, commonly known as smooth gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, yellow berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile plant for garden use.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
