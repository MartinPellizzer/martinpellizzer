Rubus koehleri var. angulosus

Rubus koehleri var. angulosus, commonly known as the angular blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
