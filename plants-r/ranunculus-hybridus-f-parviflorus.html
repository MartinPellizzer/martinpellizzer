Ranunculus hybridus f. parviflorus

Ranunculus hybridus f. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered hybrid buttercup, is a botanical hybrid with characteristics derived from several Ranunculus species.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, temperate climates, often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its hybrid nature, it may exhibit variability in morphology and flowering behavior.
