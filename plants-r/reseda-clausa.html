Reseda clausa

Reseda clausa, commonly known as the closed reseda, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Reseda clausa is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant.
