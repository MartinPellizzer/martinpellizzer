Ranunculus angustifolius subsp. uniflorus

Ranunculus angustifolius subsp.

uniflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, upright stem. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive lobed shape.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
