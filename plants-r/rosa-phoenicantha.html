Rosa phoenicantha

Rosa phoenicantha, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a shrub native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and the flowers are often used in traditional medicine.

Rosa phoenicantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
