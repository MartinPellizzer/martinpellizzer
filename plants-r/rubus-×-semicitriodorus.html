Rubus × semicitriodorus

Rubus × semicitriodorus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and tart. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along garden borders.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
