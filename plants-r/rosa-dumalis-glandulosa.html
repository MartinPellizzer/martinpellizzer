Rosa dumalis glandulosa

Rosa dumalis glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular wild rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges. This plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

It produces small, red-orange fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C.
