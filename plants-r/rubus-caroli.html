Rubus caroli

Rubus caroli, commonly known as the Carolinian blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry and blackberry species.
