Rosa repellens

Rosa repellens, commonly known as the repelling rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Rosa repellens is notable for its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments with cool, moist climates.
