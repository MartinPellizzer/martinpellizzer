Ranunculus napaulensis

Ranunculus napaulensis, commonly known as the Nepal buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountain meadows. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome and produces showy, yellow to orange flowers with five to eight petals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
