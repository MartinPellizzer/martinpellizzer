Rubus carneiflorus

Rubus carneiflorus, commonly known as the crimson-flowered raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its distinctive red flowers and edible, raspberry-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in thorny projections.

The flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
