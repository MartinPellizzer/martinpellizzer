Rudbeckia hirta var. brittonii

Rudbeckia hirta var. brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's Coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow central disk surrounded by orange to reddish-purple ray florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and produces showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom from late summer into early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
