Rhododendron cheilanthum

Rhododendron cheilanthum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Rhododendron cheilanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
