Rosa × alpestris var. molletorum

Rosa × alpestris var. molletorum, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that are evergreen in milder climates.

Rosa × alpestris var. molletorum is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often used in alpine gardens for its ornamental value and hardiness.
