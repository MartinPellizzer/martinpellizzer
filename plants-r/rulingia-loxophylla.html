Rulingia loxophylla

Rulingia loxophylla, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that appear in long, drooping clusters, which bloom profusely during the wet season. The tree has a straight trunk with a broad, umbrella-like canopy, and its leaves are typically compound, consisting of several small leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to provide shade and support biodiversity in its native habitats.
