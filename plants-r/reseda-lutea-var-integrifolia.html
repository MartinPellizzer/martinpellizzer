Reseda lutea var. integrifolia

Reseda lutea var. integrifolia, commonly known as yellowrocket, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Reseda lutea var. integrifolia is often used in traditional medicine and has also been cultivated for ornamental purposes.
