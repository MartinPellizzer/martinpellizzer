Rhytidandra polyosmoides

Rhytidandra polyosmoides, commonly known as the African sand dollar, is a rare and unusual plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, flattened, disc-like structure that resembles a sand dollar, with a rough, bark-like surface. The plant is a succulent, adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy body. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color.

Due to its rarity and distinctive appearance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
