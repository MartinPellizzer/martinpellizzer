Ramium edentatum

Ramium edentatum, commonly known as the toothless grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are typical of grasses.

Ramium edentatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
