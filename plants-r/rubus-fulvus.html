Rubus fulvus

Rubus fulvus, commonly known as the yellow bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Rubus fulvus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
