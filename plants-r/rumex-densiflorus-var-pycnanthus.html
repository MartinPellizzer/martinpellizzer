Rumex densiflorus var. pycnanthus

Rumex densiflorus var. pycnanthus, commonly known as narrow-leaved dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, fertile soils along riverbanks, wet meadows, and woodland edges. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating and produces dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

The species is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to wetland environments.
