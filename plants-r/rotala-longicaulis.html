Rotala longicaulis

Rotala longicaulis, commonly known as the thread-leaf waterwheel, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its small, white flowers are borne in dense clusters, adding to its ornamental value.

Rotala longicaulis is often cultivated in aquariums due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions.
