Rottlera longifolia

Rottlera longifolia, commonly known as the Indian cumin or longleaf rottlera, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Rottlera longifolia is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in Ayurvedic practices.
