Rosa lucieae var. parvifolia

Rosa lucieae var. parvifolia, commonly known as the Parvifolia rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features small, dark green leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length, giving it a dense, bushy appearance. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking a hardy, decorative shrub.
