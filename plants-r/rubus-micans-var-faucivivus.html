Rubus micans var. faucivivus

Rubus micans var. faucivivus, commonly known as the shimmering blackberry variant, is a less commonly documented subspecies of the blackberry plant.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark foliage and small, thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. The leaves are usually trifoliate, with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from dark purple to nearly black. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist woodland areas.

Due to its rarity and limited study, much about its botanical characteristics and ecological role remains unknown.
