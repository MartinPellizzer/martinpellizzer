Rubus tomentosus var. glabratus

Rubus tomentosus var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub native to North America, typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupes that ripen in late summer. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil.

This variety is valued for its edible berries and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
