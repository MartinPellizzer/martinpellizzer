Rheum sibiricum

Rheum sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its large, rhubarb-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces long, reddish stems that contain a high concentration of anthraquinones, which are responsible for its medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its medicinal value, it has been cultivated for centuries in traditional herbal practices.
