Rubus opulifolius

Rubus opulifolius, commonly known as red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Asia and parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed areas.

Rubus opulifolius is valued for its edible berries and is also used in traditional medicine.
