Rhynchospora aurea

Rhynchospora aurea, commonly known as the golden moorgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on the upper part of the stem. Rhynchospora aurea thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.
