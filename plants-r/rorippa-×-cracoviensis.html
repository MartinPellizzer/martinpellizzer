Rorippa × cracoviensis

Rorippa × cracoviensis, commonly known as the Polish watercress, is a hybrid species belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit, typically found in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features alternate, glossy, ovate leaves that are usually submerged or floating, with small, white, cross-shaped flowers. It produces elongated siliques containing numerous small, rounded seeds.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
