Rubus nemorosus var. macrosepalus

Rubus nemorosus var. macrosepalus, commonly known as the large-sepal raspberry, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its large, showy sepals that are often more prominent than the petals, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. It produces red or yellow fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor.

This variant is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its culinary uses.
