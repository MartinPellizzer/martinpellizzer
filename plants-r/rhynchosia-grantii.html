Rhynchosia grantii

Rhynchosia grantii, commonly known as the African bean or Grant's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source. Rhynchosia grantii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
