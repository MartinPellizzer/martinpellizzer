Ranunculus carpinetorum

Ranunculus carpinetorum, commonly known as the wood anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five to eight sepals that resemble petals.

Ranunculus carpinetorum is known for its early spring bloom and is often found in forest understories.
