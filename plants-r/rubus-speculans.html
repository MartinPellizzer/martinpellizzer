Rubus speculans

Rubus speculans, commonly known as the blueberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from white to deep blue, depending on the variety. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Rubus speculans is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
