Rubus ochrosetus

Rubus ochrosetus, commonly known as the yellow-tipped blackberry.

It is a species of brambles native to eastern North America, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive serrated margin.

The flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the characteristic fruit clusters.
