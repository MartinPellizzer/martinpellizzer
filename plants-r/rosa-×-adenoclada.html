Rosa × adenoclada

Rosa × adenoclada, commonly known as the pink musk rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery due to their rich, musky scent.

Rosa × adenoclada is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun.
