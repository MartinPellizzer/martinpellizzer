Rulingia corylifolia

Rulingia corylifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that resemble those of the maple tree. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, spiky fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of tropical flora.
