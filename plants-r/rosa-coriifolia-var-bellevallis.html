Rosa coriifolia var. bellevallis

Rosa coriifolia var. bellevallis, commonly known as the Belleval's shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its delicate floral display.
