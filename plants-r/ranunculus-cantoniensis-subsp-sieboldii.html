Ranunculus cantoniensis subsp. sieboldii

Ranunculus cantoniensis subsp.

sieboldii, commonly known as the Guangdong buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring, and it is often found in regions of China, Japan, and Korea.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and narrower leaves compared to the main species.
