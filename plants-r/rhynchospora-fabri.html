Rhynchospora fabri

Rhynchospora fabri, commonly known as the Fabric's beakspur sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish to brownish flower spikes that are typically arranged in dense clusters. Rhynchospora fabri thrives in wetland or marshy environments, often found in swampy areas with saturated soil.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
