Rhododendron pilostylum

Rhododendron pilostylum, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, providing year-round visual interest.

Rhododendron pilostylum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
