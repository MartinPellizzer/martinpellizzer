Rhododendron linearifolium f. decandrum

Rhododendron linearifolium f. decandrum, commonly known as the Decandrum form of the linear-leaved rhododendron, is a variant characterized by its distinctive floral structure.

It features elongated, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green, providing a striking contrast to its showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to specific regions in eastern Asia, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

This form is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to shaded environments.
