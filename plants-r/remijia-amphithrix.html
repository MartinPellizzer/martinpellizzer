Remijia amphithrix

Remijia amphithrix, commonly known as the twin-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique leaf structure, with two leaves emerging from the same node, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
