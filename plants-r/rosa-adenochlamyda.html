Rosa adenochlamyda

Rosa adenochlamyda, commonly known as the Australian rock rose, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, pink to red flowers. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Rosa adenochlamyda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
