Rhynchosia nervosa var. petiolata

Rhynchosia nervosa var. petiolata, commonly known as the long petioled legume, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with long petioles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, flat pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its growth habit and foliage make it suitable for ornamental gardening and as a cover crop in agricultural settings.
