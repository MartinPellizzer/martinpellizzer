Rosa micrantha subf. salvanensis

Rosa micrantha subf.

salvanensis, commonly known as the Salvan's shrub rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically has dark green, glossy leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative shrub.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
