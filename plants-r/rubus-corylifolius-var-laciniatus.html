Rubus corylifolius var. laciniatus

Rubus corylifolius var. laciniatus, commonly known as the cut-leaf blackberry, is a species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
