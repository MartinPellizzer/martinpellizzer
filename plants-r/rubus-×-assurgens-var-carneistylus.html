Rubus × assurgens var. carneistylus

Rubus × assurgens var. carneistylus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and large, aggregate fruits that typically range in color from red to dark purple. The plant is native to eastern North America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated margins, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

This variety is prized for its flavorful berries, which are often used in culinary applications.
