Rubus decurrentispinus

Rubus decurrentispinus, commonly known as the creeping blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local wildlife habitats.
