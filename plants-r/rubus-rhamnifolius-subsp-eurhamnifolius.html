Rubus rhamnifolius subsp. eurhamnifolius

Rubus rhamnifolius subsp.

eurhamnifolius, commonly known as the European blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of Europe. It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it is an important species for both wildlife and human consumption.

The subspecies is closely related to other Rubus species but is distinguished by its leaf morphology and fruit characteristics.
