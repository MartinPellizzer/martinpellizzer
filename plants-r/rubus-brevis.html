Rubus brevis

Rubus brevis, commonly known as the short-stalked raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Its flowers are typically white or pink, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
