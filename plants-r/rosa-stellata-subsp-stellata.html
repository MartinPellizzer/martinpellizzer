Rosa stellata subsp. stellata

Rosa stellata subsp.

stellata, commonly known as the starry rose, is a species native to the western United States. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters.

The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and produces showy, pink to white flowers with a distinctive star-shaped appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or arid environments.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in garden settings.
