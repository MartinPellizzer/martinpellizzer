Rubus steudneri var. steudneri

Rubus steudneri var. steudneri, commonly known as the Steudner's blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and have a tart flavor. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are usually thorny. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
