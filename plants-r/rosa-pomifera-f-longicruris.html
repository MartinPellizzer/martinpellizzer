Rosa pomifera f. longicruris

Rosa pomifera f. longicruris, commonly known as the long-stemmed rose, is a unique variant of the rose family characterized by its elongated, often thornless stems.

It produces large, edible fruits known as rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight. Its flowers are usually pink or white, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This variety is prized both for its culinary uses and its aesthetic appeal in horticulture.
