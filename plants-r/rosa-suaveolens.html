Rosa suaveolens

Rosa suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a strong, sweet aroma. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Rosa suaveolens is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its use in traditional medicine.
