Ranunculus acris var. lanuginosus

Ranunculus acris var. lanuginosus, commonly known as the downy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with bright yellow petals and a central cluster of stamens, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and can be found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
