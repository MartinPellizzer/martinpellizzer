Rubus rhombifolius var. imitatus

Rubus rhombifolius var. imitatus, commonly known as the mimic blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to rhombic in shape, and often have serrated margins.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
