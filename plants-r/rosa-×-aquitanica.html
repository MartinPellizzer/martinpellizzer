Rosa × aquitanica

Rosa × aquitanica, commonly known as the Aquitaine rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its semi-climbing growth habit and produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically features thorny stems and serrated, dark green leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Rosa × aquitanica is native to southern Europe and has been widely introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
