Rubus ulmifolius var. collivagus

Rubus ulmifolius var. collivagus, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry bush, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically lobed and serrated. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed areas.

The species is known for its adaptability and can be invasive in certain regions.
