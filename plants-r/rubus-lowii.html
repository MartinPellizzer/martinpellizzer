Rubus lowii

Rubus lowii, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

The fruit is edible but often has a tart flavor, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
