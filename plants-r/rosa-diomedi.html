Rosa diomedi

Rosa diomedi, commonly known as the Diomedea rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

Rosa diomedi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
