Rosa × litoralis

Rosa × litoralis, commonly known as the coastal rose, is a hybrid species native to the coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth, often forming dense thickets along shorelines and in sandy soils. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and the stems are typically covered in fine, silvery hairs.

Rosa × litoralis is well adapted to salt spray and wind, making it a hardy plant in coastal environments.
