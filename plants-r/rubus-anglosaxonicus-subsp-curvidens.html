Rubus anglosaxonicus subsp. curvidens

Rubus anglosaxonicus subsp.

curvidens, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry bush, is a thorny shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of white flowers.

The plant produces edible berries that ripen to a deep red or black color, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

Its fruit is widely used in culinary and commercial applications.
