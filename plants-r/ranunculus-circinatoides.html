Ranunculus circinatoides

Ranunculus circinatoides, commonly known as the cirnatic buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive, hairy sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
