Rosa × vetteri var. toddiae

Rosa × vetteri var. toddiae, commonly known as the Todd's rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless stems and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of dark green, glossy leaves with a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
