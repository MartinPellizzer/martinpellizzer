Ranunculus auricomus subsp. regularis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

regularis, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, saucer-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its showy blooms that appear in early spring.

The subspecies regularis is distinguished by its more regular leaf shape compared to the typical form of R. auricomus.
