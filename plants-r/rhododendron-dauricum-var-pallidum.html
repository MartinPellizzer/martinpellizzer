Rhododendron dauricum var. pallidum

Rhododendron dauricum var. pallidum, commonly known as the Daurian rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white to grayish hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pale pink to white in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with cool, moist conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
