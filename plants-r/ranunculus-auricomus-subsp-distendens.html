Ranunculus auricomus subsp. distendens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

distendens, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals that are often reflexed or spread out.

The plant has palmate, deeply lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive, somewhat waxy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
