Richardella surumuensis

Richardella surumuensis, commonly known as the Surumu grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and marshy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous structure that contributes to its resilience in wet conditions.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential use in sustainable landscaping.
