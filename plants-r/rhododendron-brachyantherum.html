Rhododendron brachyantherum

Rhododendron brachyantherum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China, India, and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. The plant features dense, dark green leaves that are narrow and leathery, often with a slight pubescence. Its flowers are clustered in trusses and are usually pink to red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
