Rudbeckia pallida

Rudbeckia pallida, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow-orange flower heads with white ray florets, surrounded by a central cone-like disk. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Rudbeckia pallida is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
