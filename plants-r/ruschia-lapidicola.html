Ruschia lapidicola

Ruschia lapidicola, commonly known as the rock Ruschia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its compact growth habit and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
