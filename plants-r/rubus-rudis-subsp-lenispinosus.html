Rubus rudis subsp. lenispinosus

Rubus rudis subsp.

lenispinosus, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its prickly stems and dark purple to black berries.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, hairy covering on the stems and leaves.
