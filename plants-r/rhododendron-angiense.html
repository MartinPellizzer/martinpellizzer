Rhododendron angiense

Rhododendron angiense, commonly known as the Angolan rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Angola.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value has made it a subject of interest for horticulturists, though it is less commonly cultivated than other rhododendron species.
