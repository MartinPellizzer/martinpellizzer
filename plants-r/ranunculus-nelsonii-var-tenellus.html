Ranunculus nelsonii var. tenellus

Ranunculus nelsonii var. tenellus, commonly known as the Nelson's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and has finely divided, trifoliate leaves. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, hairy fruits containing seeds.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically significant species in its native range.
