Racosperma willdenowianum

Racosperma willdenowianum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with soft, silky hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.
