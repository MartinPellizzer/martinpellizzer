Rubus colemannii var. wagingensis

Rubus colemannii var. wagingensis, commonly known as the Waging blackberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dense clusters of dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cool, moist climates. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, white flowers in the spring.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
