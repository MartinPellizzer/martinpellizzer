Rubus plicatus var. opacus

Rubus plicatus var. opacus, commonly known as the dark raspberry.

This variant is characterized by its dense, dark red foliage and small, tart berries. It is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces flowers that transition from white to pink as they mature.

Its berries are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines for their unique flavor.
