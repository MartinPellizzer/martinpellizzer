Rubus kingaensis var. pubescens

Rubus kingaensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the Kingaensis Raspberry, is a shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and home landscapes.
