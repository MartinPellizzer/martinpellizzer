Racosperma hendersonii

Racosperma hendersonii, commonly known as the Henderson's acacia, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Its foliage and flowers are also used in traditional Indigenous practices.
