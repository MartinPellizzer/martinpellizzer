Rubus cruentatus

Rubus cruentatus, commonly known as the bloody raspberry, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red-fruited berries. Its flowers are typically white or pink, with five petals, and it produces edible fruits that are often used in jams and desserts.

The plant has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties due to its high antioxidant content.
