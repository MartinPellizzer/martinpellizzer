Ruellia squarrosa

Ruellia squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ruellia squarrosa typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms.
