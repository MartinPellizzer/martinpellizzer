Ranunculus granatensis

Ranunculus granatensis, commonly known as the granate buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are hairy and erect.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
