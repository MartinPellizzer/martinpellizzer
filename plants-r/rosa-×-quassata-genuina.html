Rosa × quassata genuina

Rosa × quassata genuina, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and fragrant pink to white flowers. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and historical landscapes.
