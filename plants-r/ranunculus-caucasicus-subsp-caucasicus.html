Ranunculus caucasicus subsp. caucasicus

Ranunculus caucasicus subsp.

caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Caucasus region. It features delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest floors or along stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy flowers and often toxic sap.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and slightly different floral morphology compared to other ranunculus species.
