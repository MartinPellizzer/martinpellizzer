Rubus myriadenus

Rubus myriadenus, commonly known as the many-fruited blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, wooded areas. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate. Its flowers are small and white, and it produces clusters of dark purple to black berries.

The berries are edible and often used in culinary applications, though the plant is more valued for its ornamental qualities.
