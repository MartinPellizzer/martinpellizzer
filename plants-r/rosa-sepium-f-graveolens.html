Rosa sepium f. graveolens

Rosa sepium f. graveolens, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a fragrant, thorny shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is known for its strong, sweet fragrance, which is particularly noticeable when the flowers are in full bloom. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has historical uses in hedgerows and as a source of rose hips.

The species is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
