Rubus fruticosus var. concolor

Rubus fruticosus var. concolor, commonly known as the common blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The species is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas, showing adaptability to different environmental conditions.
