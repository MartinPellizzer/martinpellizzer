Rhynchosia atropurpurea

Rhynchosia atropurpurea, commonly known as purple-flowered cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing edible seeds, similar to other legumes in the Fabaceae family. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Rhynchosia atropurpurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
