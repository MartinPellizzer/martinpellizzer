Rubus hongnoensis

Rubus hongnoensis, commonly known as the Korean raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to Korea and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
