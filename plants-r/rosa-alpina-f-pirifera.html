Rosa alpina f. pirifera

Rosa alpina f. pirifera, commonly known as the alpine rose or the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a subspecies of Rosa alpina, characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in horticultural landscapes.
