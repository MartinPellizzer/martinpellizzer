Ruellia rhytiphylla

Ruellia rhytiphylla, commonly known as the glossy ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are borne in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ruellia rhytiphylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
