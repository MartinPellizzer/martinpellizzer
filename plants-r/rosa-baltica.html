Rosa baltica

Rosa baltica, commonly known as the Baltic rose, is a perennial shrub native to the forests of northern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa baltica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
