Rubus vulgatus subsp. buschii

Rubus vulgatus subsp.

buschii, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature into dark purple to black berries. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
