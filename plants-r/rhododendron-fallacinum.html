Rhododendron fallacinum

Rhododendron fallacinum, commonly known as the Alpine azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires a cool, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and high-altitude landscapes.
