Ramium heterophyllum

Ramium heterophyllum, commonly known as the narrowleaf false flax, is a member of the Poaceae family, commonly found in arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows from rhizomes and is adapted to survive in dry conditions. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects.

The species is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion in disturbed areas.
