Rubus fruticosus subsp. rosaceus

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

rosaceus, commonly known as brambles or blackberries, is a shrub-like plant belonging to the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple flowers.

The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in temperate climates. It produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications.

The subspecies is often cultivated for its fruit and has a significant role in both agriculture and wild ecosystems.
