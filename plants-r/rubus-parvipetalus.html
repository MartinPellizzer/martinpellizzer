Rubus parvipetalus

Rubus parvipetalus, commonly known as the small-petaled blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus parvipetalus is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.
