Rhamnus atlantica

Rhamnus atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and limestone formations. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. Its flowers are greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, followed by small, dark blue berries that are edible for birds.

Rhamnus atlantica is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
