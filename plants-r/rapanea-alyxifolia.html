Rapanea alyxifolia

Rapanea alyxifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf boxwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that is typically a glossy dark green with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Rapanea alyxifolia is often cultivated as a decorative shrub in gardens due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
