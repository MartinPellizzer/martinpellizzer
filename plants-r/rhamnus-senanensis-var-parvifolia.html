Rhamnus senanensis var. parvifolia

Rhamnus senanensis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
