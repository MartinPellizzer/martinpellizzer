Rourea subvolubilis

Rourea subvolubilis, commonly known as the climbing rourea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with soft, flexible stems that allow it to climb or trail along supports. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, often with a slight fragrance. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Rourea subvolubilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid, warm environments.
