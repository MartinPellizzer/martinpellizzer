Rubus incisus var. geifolius

Rubus incisus var. geifolius, commonly known as the Geifolius blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
