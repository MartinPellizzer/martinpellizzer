Rudbeckia pinnatiloba

Rudbeckia pinnatiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that bear large, daisy-like yellow-orange flower heads with dark brown centers. The leaves are deeply lobed and pinnately divided, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
