Rhododendron hemsleyanum

Rhododendron hemsleyanum, commonly known as the Hemsley's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leathery leaves, which help it adapt to its high-altitude environment.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized landscapes.
