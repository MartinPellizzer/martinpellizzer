Rhamnus rahiminejadii

Rhamnus rahiminejadii, commonly known as the Persian buckthorn, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
