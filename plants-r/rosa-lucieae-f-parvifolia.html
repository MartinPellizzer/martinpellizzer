Rosa lucieae f. parvifolia

Rosa lucieae f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved form of Lucie's rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its reduced leaf size compared to the typical form.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to specific regions in the Mediterranean. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers, which bloom in summer. Its foliage is densely packed, with elliptical leaves that have a slightly serrated edge.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and compact growth habit in gardens.
