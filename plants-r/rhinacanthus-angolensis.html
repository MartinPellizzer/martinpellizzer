Rhinacanthus angolensis

Rhinacanthus angolensis, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its palmate leaves and showy orange-red flowers. The plant produces woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in warm climates.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
