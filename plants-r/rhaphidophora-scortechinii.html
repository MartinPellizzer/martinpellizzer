Rhaphidophora scortechinii

Rhaphidophora scortechinii, commonly known as the Shingleleaf Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves with a distinctive shingle-like pattern on the underside, which gives it its common name. The plant has a vining growth habit and can reach lengths of several meters when allowed to climb. It prefers bright, indirect light and consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Rhaphidophora scortechinii is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
