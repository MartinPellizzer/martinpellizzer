Rubus polymorphus

Rubus polymorphus, commonly known as the variable blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed areas, and is known for its adaptability.

The berries are edible but can have a tart flavor, making them useful in jams and preserves.
