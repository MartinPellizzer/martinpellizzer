Rosa micrantha f. operta

Rosa micrantha f. operta, commonly known as the curled-leaved rose, is a unique horticultural form of the species Rosa micrantha.

It is characterized by its tightly curled, compact foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and densely packed, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

This form is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial sunlight.
