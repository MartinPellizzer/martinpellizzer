Rhynchosia harmsiana

Rhynchosia harmsiana, commonly known as the African bean or red bean plant, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and sturdy, twining stems that allow it to climb other vegetation. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed areas or along forest edges.

Rhynchosia harmsiana is valued for its nutritional seeds and is used in traditional agriculture and cuisine.
