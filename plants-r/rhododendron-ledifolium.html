Rhododendron ledifolium

Rhododendron ledifolium, commonly known as the alpine azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are often dark green and covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron ledifolium thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
