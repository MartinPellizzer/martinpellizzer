Ranunculus alberti

Ranunculus alberti, commonly known as Albert's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically has palmately divided leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
