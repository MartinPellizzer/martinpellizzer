Ranunculus polyanthemos subsp. thomasii

Ranunculus polyanthemos subsp.

thomasii, commonly known as the Thomas's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

The plant features bright yellow flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that resemble petals, and it produces small, smooth, green fruits. It is characterized by its basal leaves that are deeply lobed and its hairy stems.

This subspecies is often found in regions with cool, temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
