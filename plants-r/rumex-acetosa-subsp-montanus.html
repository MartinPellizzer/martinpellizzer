Rumex acetosa subsp. montanus

Rumex acetosa subsp.

montanus, commonly known as mountain sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often in well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces oval, smooth seeds.

It is valued for its sour-tasting leaves, which have been used in traditional cuisine and medicine.
