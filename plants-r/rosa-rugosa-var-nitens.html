Rosa rugosa var. nitens

Rosa rugosa var. nitens, commonly known as the shining dog玫瑰, is a deciduous shrub native to coastal regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, thorny stems and broad, lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, pink to reddish flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It is well adapted to salty air and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in coastal environments.

Its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions make it a popular choice for gardens and coastal landscaping.
