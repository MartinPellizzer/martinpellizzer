Ranunculus × rohlenae

Ranunculus × rohlenae, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, radially symmetrical flowers that typically feature 5–15 petals, often in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has a short, stout stem with trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy. Native to the mountainous regions of central Europe, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its vibrant blooms.
