Rubus pubescens proles occidentalis

Rubus pubescens proles occidentalis, commonly known as the Western brambles, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits known as berries. The plant is native to western North America and thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed areas. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
