Racosperma tenuispicum

Racosperma tenuispicum, commonly known as the slender-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, elongated leaflets, and it produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters. It is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Racosperma tenuispicum is often used in revegetation projects due to its hardiness and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
