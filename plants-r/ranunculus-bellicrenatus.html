Ranunculus bellicrenatus

Ranunculus bellicrenatus, commonly known as the battle-crown buttercup, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and deeply lobed leaves, which are typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers bloom in early summer, adding a splash of color to high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
