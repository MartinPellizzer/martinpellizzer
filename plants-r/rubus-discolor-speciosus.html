Rubus discolor speciosus

Rubus discolor speciosus, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Native to the mountainous regions of South America, it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and small-scale agriculture.
