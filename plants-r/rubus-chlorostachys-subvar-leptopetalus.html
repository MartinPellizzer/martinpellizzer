Rubus chlorostachys subvar. leptopetalus

Rubus chlorostachys subvar. leptopetalus, commonly known as the slender-petaled evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, thornless stems. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This subvariety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and fruit yield.
