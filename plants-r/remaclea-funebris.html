Remaclea funebris

Remaclea funebris, commonly known as the funereal bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Remaclea funebris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
