Rubus dumetorum var. diversifolius

Rubus dumetorum var. diversifolius, commonly known as the diverse-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves that vary in shape and size, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The leaves and stems contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
