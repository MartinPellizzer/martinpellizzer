Racosperma dawsonii

Racosperma dawsonii, commonly known as Dawson's acacia, is a species of shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow floral spikes. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Its flowers are also a source of nectar for native pollinators.
