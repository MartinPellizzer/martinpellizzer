Ribes vanfleetianum

Ribes vanfleetianum, commonly known as Vanfleet's gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of flowering plants often referred to as gooseberries or currants. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits, which are typically edible, develop into small, tart berries that ripen in late summer.

Ribes vanfleetianum is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
