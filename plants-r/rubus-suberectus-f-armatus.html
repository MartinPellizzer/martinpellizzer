Rubus suberectus f. armatus

Rubus suberectus f. armatus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a botanical variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its dense, thorny growth.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of North America, particularly the western United States. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. Its thorny structure serves as a natural defense mechanism against herbivores.

This form is notable for its horticultural value and adaptability to various soil types.
