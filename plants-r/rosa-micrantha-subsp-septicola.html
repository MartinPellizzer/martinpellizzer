Rosa micrantha subsp. septicola

Rosa micrantha subsp.

septicola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant has slender, thornless stems and elliptical, dark green leaves with finely serrated edges. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for xeriscaping and native gardens.
