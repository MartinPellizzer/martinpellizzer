Ruellia angustifolia

Ruellia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, including parts of the southern United States.

Ruellia angustifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
