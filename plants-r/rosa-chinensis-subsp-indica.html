Rosa chinensis subsp. indica

Rosa chinensis subsp.

indica, commonly known as the China rose, is a shrub native to China and widely cultivated for its showy flowers. It is characterized by its simple, often fragrant blooms in shades of red, pink, and white, which appear throughout the growing season.

The plant has alternate, pinnate leaves that are hairy and aromatic, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm climates and is often used in landscaping and as a cut flower.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent species in many modern garden roses.
