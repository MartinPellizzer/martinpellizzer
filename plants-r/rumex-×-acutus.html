Rumex × acutus

Rumex × acutus, commonly known as the common dock, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rumex acetosa (sour dock) and Rumex obtusifolius (broad-leaved dock).

It is a perennial plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and disturbed areas.

Rumex × acutus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its astringent and anti-inflammatory effects.
