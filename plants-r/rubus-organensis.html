Rubus organensis

Rubus organensis, commonly known as the Organ Mountains blackberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, and the flowers are typically white or pale pink.

The fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tart or bitter taste.
