Ribes × coeruleum

Ribes × coeruleum, commonly known as the blue currant, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Ribes sanguineum and Ribes nigrum.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to blue, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
