Racosperma sulcatum var. planoconvexum

Racosperma sulcatum var. planoconvexum, commonly known as the flat-convex wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive flat, convex seed pods. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typical of many wattles. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, feather-like leaflets.

This variety is notable for its unique pod morphology and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
