Rhododendron zekoense

Rhododendron zekoense, commonly known as the Zeko rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often fragrant.

Rhododendron zekoense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to form dense, decorative shrubs.
