Rubus pseudoacer subsp. flexuosus

Rubus pseudoacer subsp.

flexuosus, commonly known as the flexible blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles.
