Rumex acetosella subsp. tenuifolius

Rumex acetosella subsp.

tenuifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaf sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges, heaths, and meadows.

The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, oval-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 cm in length. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its tolerance to wet conditions.
