Rosa ceratophora

Rosa ceratophora, commonly known as the horned rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like thorns that cover its stems. The plant produces showy pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and its fruit is a red, hairy rose hip.

Rosa ceratophora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
