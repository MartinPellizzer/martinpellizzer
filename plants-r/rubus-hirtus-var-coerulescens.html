Rubus hirtus var. coerulescens

Rubus hirtus var. coerulescens, commonly known as the blueberry raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to blue, depending on maturity. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

This species is often found in woodland edges and thickets, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
