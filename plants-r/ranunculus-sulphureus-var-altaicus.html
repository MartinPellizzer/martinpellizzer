Ranunculus sulphureus var. altaicus

Ranunculus sulphureus var. altaicus, commonly known as the Altai buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Altai Mountains region.

It features bright yellow flowers with prominent yellow stamens and a central pistil, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in alpine and subalpine environments. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or grassy slopes at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
