Rubus suberectus var. gintlii

Rubus suberectus var. gintlii, commonly known as the Gintlii blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has woody stems with thorny projections, typical of the Rubus genus. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters and attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
