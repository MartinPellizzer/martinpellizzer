Rosa canina var. pernteri

Rosa canina var. pernteri, commonly known as the Austrian rose, is a shrub native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in the spring, followed by bright orange or red hips in the autumn. These hips are edible and contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.
