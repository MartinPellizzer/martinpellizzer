Rubus guentheri var. pachypus

Rubus guentheri var. pachypus, commonly known as the thick-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, thick, and leathery leaves, which are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
