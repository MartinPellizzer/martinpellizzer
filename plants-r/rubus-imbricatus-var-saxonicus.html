Rubus imbricatus var. saxonicus

Rubus imbricatus var. saxonicus, commonly known as the saxonic raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
