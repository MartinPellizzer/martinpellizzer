Ranunculus schurii

Ranunculus schurii, commonly known as the Schur's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically arranged in a cup-like shape, and is characterized by its finely divided, trifoliate leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.

Ranunculus schurii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
