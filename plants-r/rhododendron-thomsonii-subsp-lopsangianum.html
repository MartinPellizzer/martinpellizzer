Rhododendron thomsonii subsp. lopsangianum

Rhododendron thomsonii subsp.

lopsangianum, commonly known as the Lhasa rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in regions of Nepal, Bhutan, and India. It is a shrub that typically grows in mountainous areas at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters.

The plant features dense, evergreen foliage with dark green leaves that are oval in shape and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, with five petals and are borne in clusters at the ends of the branches.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
