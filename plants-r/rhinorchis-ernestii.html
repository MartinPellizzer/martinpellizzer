Rhinorchis ernestii

Rhinorchis ernestii, commonly known as the Ernest's orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rocky substrates or in forest undergrowth. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for successful germination.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Rhinorchis ernestii is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
