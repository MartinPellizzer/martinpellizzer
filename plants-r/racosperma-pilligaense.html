Racosperma pilligaense

Racosperma pilligaense, commonly known as the Pilliga acacia, is a shrub native to the Pilliga Forest in New South Wales, Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various native animals.

Racosperma pilligaense is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
