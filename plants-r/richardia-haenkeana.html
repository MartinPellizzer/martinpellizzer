Richardia haenkeana

Richardia haenkeana, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a central stem with whorled leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
