Rosa rubiginosa var. echinocarpa

Rosa rubiginosa var. echinocarpa, commonly known as the rough rose or bristly rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink.

Its fruit is a red, hairy rose hip, which is edible and rich in vitamin C. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
