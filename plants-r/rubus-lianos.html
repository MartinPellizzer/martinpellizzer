Rubus lianos

Rubus lianos, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems that trail along the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface.

Rubus lianos thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.
