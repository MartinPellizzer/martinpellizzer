Rubus stylosus

Rubus stylosus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and prickly stems. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Rubus stylosus is an important species in ecological studies due to its role in supporting local biodiversity.
