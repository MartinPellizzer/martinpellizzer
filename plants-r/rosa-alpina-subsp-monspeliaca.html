Rosa alpina subsp. monspeliaca

Rosa alpina subsp.

monspeliaca, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes.

The plant produces showy pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven oval leaflets, and it has a woody stem covered in thorns.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and beauty.
