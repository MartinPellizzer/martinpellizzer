Rubus brasiliensis var. organensis

Rubus brasiliensis var. organensis, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
