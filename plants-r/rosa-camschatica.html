Rosa camschatica

Rosa camschatica, commonly known as the Caucasian rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals. It produces oval, red fruits known as hips, which contain seeds.

Rosa camschatica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and blooming habit.
