Rosa × mirabilis

Rosa × mirabilis, commonly known as the heavenly sweet or sweetpea rose, is a hybrid rose species known for its intense, sweet fragrance.

It is native to the United States and is characterized by its large, double flowers with a rich, perfumed scent. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in thorns.

Rosa × mirabilis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
