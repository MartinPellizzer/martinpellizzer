Rosa bracteata var. typica

Rosa bracteata var. typica, commonly known as the bracteate rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces oval, leathery leaves with a distinctive bract-like structure at the base of each flower cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
