Ranunculus occidentalis var. eisenii

Ranunculus occidentalis var. eisenii, commonly known as the Eisen's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often with a distinctive dark central mark. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough, hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
