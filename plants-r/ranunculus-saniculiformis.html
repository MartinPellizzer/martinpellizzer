Ranunculus saniculiformis

Ranunculus saniculiformis, commonly known as the Sanicula-like buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the flowers consist of numerous small petals arranged in a cup-like structure.

Ranunculus saniculiformis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
