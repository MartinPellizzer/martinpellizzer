Rubus serpens var. cordatus

Rubus serpens var. cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, dark berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
