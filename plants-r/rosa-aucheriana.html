Rosa aucheriana

Rosa aucheriana, commonly known as the Austrian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features arching stems with thorns and is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are oval-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Rosa aucheriana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery.
