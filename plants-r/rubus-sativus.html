Rubus sativus

Rubus sativus, commonly known as the blackberry plant, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small, white flowers that develop into edible berries. The fruit, which is typically dark purple to black when ripe, is rich in antioxidants and vitamins. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
