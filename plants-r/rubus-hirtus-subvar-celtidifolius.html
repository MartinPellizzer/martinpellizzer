Rubus hirtus subvar. celtidifolius

Rubus hirtus subvar. celtidifolius, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark red or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubiaceae family.
