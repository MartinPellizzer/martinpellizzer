Rubus × akermanii

Rubus × akermanii, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus hirtellus and Rubus ursinus.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wildlife habitat.
