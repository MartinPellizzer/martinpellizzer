Rigiolepis poiana

Rigiolepis poiana, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Rigiolepis poiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
