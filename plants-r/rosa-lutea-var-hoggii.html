Rosa lutea var. hoggii

Rosa lutea var. hoggii, commonly known as the Hogg's yellow rose, is a variant of the yellow rose species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and compact, bushy growth habit. The plant typically produces fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sought-after plant among horticulturists.
