Rhamnus thea

Rhamnus thea, commonly known as the Chinese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in supporting wildlife.
