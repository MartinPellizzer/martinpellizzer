Rapanea rhombata

Rapanea rhombata, commonly known as the rhombic rapanea, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lauraceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical or rhomboid in shape, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, dark purple berries.

The plant is often found in lowland and montane rainforests, thriving in humid, shaded environments.
