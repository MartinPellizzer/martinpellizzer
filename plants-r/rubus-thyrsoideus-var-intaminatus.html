Rubus thyrsoideus var. intaminatus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. intaminatus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The berries are typically edible but are less commonly cultivated compared to other blackberry varieties.
