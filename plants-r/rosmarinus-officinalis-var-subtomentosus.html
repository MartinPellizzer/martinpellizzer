Rosmarinus officinalis var. subtomentosus

Rosmarinus officinalis var. subtomentosus, commonly known as the woolly rosemary, is a variant of the common rosemary plant characterized by its dense, soft, gray-green foliage.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
