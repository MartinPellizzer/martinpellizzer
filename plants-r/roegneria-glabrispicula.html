Roegneria glabrispicula

Roegneria glabrispicula, commonly known as the smooth-spurred wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, glabrous (hairless) leaf blades. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences with small, pale green to yellowish florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Roegneria glabrispicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky and arid landscapes.
