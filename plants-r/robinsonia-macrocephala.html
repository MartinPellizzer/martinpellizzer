Robinsonia macrocephala

Robinsonia macrocephala, commonly known as the giant-headed orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet, acidic soils near streams and swamps. The plant features a tall, erect stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a spike, with each flower having three petals and three sepals.

The plant is notable for its unique floral structure and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
