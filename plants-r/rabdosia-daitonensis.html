Rabdosia daitonensis

Rabdosia daitonensis, commonly known as Daitonensis Rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rabdosia daitonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
