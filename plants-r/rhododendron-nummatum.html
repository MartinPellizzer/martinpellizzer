Rhododendron nummatum

Rhododendron nummatum, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features dense, upright growth with ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to red flowers in the spring, which are borne on short pedicels. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Rhododendron nummatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
