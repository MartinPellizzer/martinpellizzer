Rubus pubescens var. silvaticus

Rubus pubescens var. silvaticus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed soils, thriving in partial to full sunlight. The plant features thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink. Its fruit, a aggregate berry, is edible and often used in jams and desserts.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
