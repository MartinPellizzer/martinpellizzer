Rosa friesii var. typica

Rosa friesii var. typica, commonly known as the Friesii rose, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Eastern Himalayas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features thorny stems and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
