Rytidosperma dimidiatum

Rytidosperma dimidiatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf spinifex, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Rytidosperma dimidiatum is often found in coastal dunes and is an important component of coastal vegetation in southern Australia.
