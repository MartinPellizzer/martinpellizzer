Roettlera amoena

Roettlera amoena, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elegant, showy flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep magenta. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture, while its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

Roettlera amoena is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in traditional horticulture.
