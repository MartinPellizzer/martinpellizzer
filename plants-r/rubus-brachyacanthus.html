Rubus brachyacanthus

Rubus brachyacanthus, commonly known as the narrow-armed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in woodland and shrubland habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
