Ranunculus auricomus subsp. eurycolpus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

eurycolpus, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a broad, shield-shaped center.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy and often toxic flowers.
