Renealmia calthifolia

Renealmia calthifolia, commonly known as the white marsh orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
