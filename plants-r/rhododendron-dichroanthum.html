Rhododendron dichroanthum

Rhododendron dichroanthum, commonly known as the two-colored rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forests at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers have a distinctive two-toned appearance.

Rhododendron dichroanthum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
