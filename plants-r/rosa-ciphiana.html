Rosa ciphiana

Rosa ciphiana, commonly known as the Appalachian rose, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa ciphiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in native plant conservation.
