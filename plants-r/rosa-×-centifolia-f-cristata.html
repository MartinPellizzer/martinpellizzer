Rosa × centifolia f. cristata

Rosa × centifolia f. cristata, commonly known as the cabbage rose, is a unique cultivar of the hybrid tea rose.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded inflorescence that resembles a cabbage, giving it its common name. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers with a high number of petals, often in shades of pink, red, or white. It is a compact shrub with thorny stems and dark green, glossy foliage.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its use in perfumery due to its strong, sweet scent.
