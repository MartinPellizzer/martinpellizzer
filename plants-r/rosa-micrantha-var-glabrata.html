Rosa micrantha var. glabrata

Rosa micrantha var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thornless stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in pink or white. The leaves are alternate, compound, and covered in fine hairs, while the fruits are red, hairy rosehips.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms and hardy nature.
