Rubus centralis

Rubus centralis, commonly known as the central blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and often have a hairy texture.

Rubus centralis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
