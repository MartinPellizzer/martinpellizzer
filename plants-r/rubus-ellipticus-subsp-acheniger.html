Rubus ellipticus subsp. acheniger

Rubus ellipticus subsp.

acheniger, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its elliptic, often narrow leaves and small, dark purple to black berries.

The plant produces compound inflorescences with white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is typically small and has a tart flavor, making it a subject of both ecological and culinary interest.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique fruit morphology and habitat preferences.
