Rosa × alba var. vulgaris

Rosa × alba var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common white rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
