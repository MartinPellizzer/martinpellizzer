Rubia cordifolia var. khasiana

Rubia cordifolia var. khasiana, commonly known as Khasi Rubia, is a variant of the heart-leaved rubia, native to the Eastern Himalayas.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly the use of its roots and leaves in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas at higher altitudes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
