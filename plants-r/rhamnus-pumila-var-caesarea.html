Rhamnus pumila var. caesarea

Rhamnus pumila var. caesarea, commonly known as the blue-fruited buckthorn, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and distinctive blue-black berries that mature in the fall. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various bird species.
