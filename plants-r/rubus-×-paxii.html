Rubus × paxii

Rubus × paxii, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a vigorous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife.
