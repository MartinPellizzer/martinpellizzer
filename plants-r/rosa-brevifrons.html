Rosa brevifrons

Rosa brevifrons, commonly known as the short-fruited rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer.

Its small, oval-shaped fruits, known as hips, are typically red and contain a high concentration of vitamin C. Rosa brevifrons is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
