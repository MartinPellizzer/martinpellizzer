Rubus macrophyllus var. lasiaxon

Rubus macrophyllus var. lasiaxon, commonly known as the large-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its large, broad leaves.

It is native to regions of eastern Asia, including China and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. Its botanical features include thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and flowers that bloom in spring.

This variety is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its fruit.
