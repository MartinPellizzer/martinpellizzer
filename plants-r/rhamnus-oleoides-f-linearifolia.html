Rhamnus oleoides f. linearifolia

Rhamnus oleoides f. linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, egg-shaped berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

The species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments.
