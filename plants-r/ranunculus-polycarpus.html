Ranunculus polycarpus

Ranunculus polycarpus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces multiple flowering stems throughout the growing season.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
