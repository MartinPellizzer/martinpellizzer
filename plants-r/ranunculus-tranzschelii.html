Ranunculus tranzschelii

Ranunculus tranzschelii, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. It is known for its ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in high-altitude habitats.

Ranunculus tranzschelii plays an important role in pollination and is a valued ornamental plant in horticulture.
