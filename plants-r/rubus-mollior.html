Rubus mollior

Rubus mollior, commonly known as the softleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark berries that are typically edible. The plant has trifoliate leaves with soft, velvety undersides, and its flowers are usually pink or white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The fruit of Rubus mollior is used in local cuisines and has been historically utilized for its flavor and nutritional value.
