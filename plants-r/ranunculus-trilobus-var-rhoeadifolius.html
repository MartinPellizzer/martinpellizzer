Ranunculus trilobus var. rhoeadifolius

Ranunculus trilobus var. rhoeadifolius, commonly known as the golden shield or rhoeadifolius buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petal-like sepals, which are typically arranged in a star shape. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental member of the Ranunculaceae family.
