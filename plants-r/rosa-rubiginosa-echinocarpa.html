Rosa rubiginosa echinocarpa

Rosa rubiginosa echinocarpa, commonly known as the rough rose or prickly rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a hairy, red berry that contains several seeds.

This species is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.
