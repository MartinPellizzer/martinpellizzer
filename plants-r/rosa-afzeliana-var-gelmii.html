Rosa afzeliana var. gelmii

Rosa afzeliana var. gelmii, commonly known as the Gelmi rose, is a species native to parts of Africa, particularly Tanzania and Kenya.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in open woodland and savanna habitats. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem covered in thorny buds and small, oval-shaped leaves.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
