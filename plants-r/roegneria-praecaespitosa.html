Roegneria praecaespitosa

Roegneria praecaespitosa, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous narrow, awned lemmas. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color, with a fine texture and a bluish sheen.

Roegneria praecaespitosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
