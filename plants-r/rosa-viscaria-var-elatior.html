Rosa viscaria var. elatior

Rosa viscaria var. elatior, commonly known as the Alpine rose or the mountain rose, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is a shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hair.

Rosa viscaria var. elatior is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
