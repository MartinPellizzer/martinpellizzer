Rubus wrightii var. ampellophyllus

Rubus wrightii var. ampellophyllus, commonly known as the Wright's raspberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
