Rosa Glauca Var. Pubescens

Glaucous rose, scientifically known as Rosa glauca var. pubescens, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. Its unique appearance is characterized by a bluish-gray foliage and delicate pink flowers that bloom in late spring. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh climates. The plant's hairy texture and cold-resistant properties make it a resilient addition to gardens. Its beauty and hardiness have made it a popular choice among horticulturists and nature enthusiasts alike.
