Ribes uva-crispa var. sativum

Ribes uva-crispa var. sativum, commonly known as the European gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It produces small, tart berries that are typically yellow or red in color, with a distinctive, slightly prickly surface. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are usually oval or elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for gardens and hedgerows.

The berries are often used in jams, sauces, and as a flavoring in various culinary applications.
