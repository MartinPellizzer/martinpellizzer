Ribes smidtianum

Ribes smidtianum, commonly known as Smidt's gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Ribes.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its erect, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have serrated edges, while its flowers are typically white or pale green.

Ribes smidtianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
