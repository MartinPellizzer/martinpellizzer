Rubus × semimicans var. densispinus

Rubus × semimicans var. densispinus, commonly known as the dense-fruited blackberry, is a hybrid raspberry species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its berries are often used in culinary applications and are valued for their flavor and nutritional content.
