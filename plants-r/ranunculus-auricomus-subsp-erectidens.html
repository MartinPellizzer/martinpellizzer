Ranunculus auricomus subsp. erectidens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

erectidens, commonly known as the upright buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are alternate along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wildflower displays.
