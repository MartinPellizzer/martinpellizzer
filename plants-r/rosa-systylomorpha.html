Rosa systylomorpha

Rosa systylomorpha, commonly known as the Pacific Coast rose, is a species native to the western coast of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in coastal habitats, characterized by its rugged terrain and maritime climate. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in the spring. Its stems are covered in thorns, which provide defense against herbivores.

Rosa systylomorpha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
