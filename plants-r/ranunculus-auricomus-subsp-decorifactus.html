Ranunculus auricomus subsp. decorifactus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

decorifactus, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive golden center.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine, silky hairs.

It produces small, greenish-brown fruits that contain several seeds.
