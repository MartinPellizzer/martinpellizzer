Ranunculus zuccarinii var. dissectissimus

Ranunculus zuccarinii var. dissectissimus, commonly known as the dissected buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, green stem. The leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance, contributing to its ornamental value. This variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
