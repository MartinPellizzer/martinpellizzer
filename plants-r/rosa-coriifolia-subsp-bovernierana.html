Rosa coriifolia subsp. bovernierana

Rosa coriifolia subsp.

bovernierana, commonly known as the Bovenier's shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe. It is characterized by its dense, thorned stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are compound, with ovate to elliptical leaflets that are hairy beneath. This subspecies is adapted to cool, moist environments and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
