Rosa pendulina f. alpina

Rosa pendulina f. alpina, commonly known as the alpine hanging rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine climates.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to flourish in challenging garden conditions.
