Rosa diachylon

Rosa diachylon, commonly known as the Chinese moonflower, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are covered in thorns. Rosa diachylon is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
