Rosa wulfenii rubra

Rosa wulfenii rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered shrub rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny shrubs and is known for its vibrant red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has elliptical leaves with serrated edges and produces small, oval fruits called hips. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Rosa wulfenii rubra is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
