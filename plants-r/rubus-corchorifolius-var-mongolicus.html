Rubus corchorifolius var. mongolicus

Rubus corchorifolius var. mongolicus, commonly known as Mongolian blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It features dark green, serrated leaves and produces small, edible berries that are typically black when mature. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks. It has a long growing season and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
