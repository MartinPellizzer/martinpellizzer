Rubus accessivus

Rubus accessivus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into aggregate fruits known as blackberries. These fruits are typically dark purple to black when ripe and are highly valued for their flavor and nutritional content.

Rubus accessivus thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forest edges.
