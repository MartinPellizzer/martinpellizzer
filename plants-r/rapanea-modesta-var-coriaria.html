Rapanea modesta var. coriaria

Rapanea modesta var. coriaria, commonly known as the Corsican laurel, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for its dense foliage and adaptability.
