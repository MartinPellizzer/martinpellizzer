Rubus caesius var. spiculatus

Rubus caesius var. spiculatus, commonly known as the spiculate blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. Its flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.

Botanically, it is characterized by its distinctive spines and the structure of its fruit, which is similar to other Rubus species.
