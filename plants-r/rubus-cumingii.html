Rubus cumingii

Rubus cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, particularly in regions of Washington and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor and are sometimes used in local culinary traditions.
