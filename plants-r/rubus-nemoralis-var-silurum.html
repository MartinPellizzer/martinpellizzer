Rubus nemoralis var. silurum

Rubus nemoralis var. silurum, commonly known as the Silurian blackberry, is a shrub native to the UK and parts of northern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in woodland edges and moist, shaded habitats.

Its flowers are white and appear in early spring, followed by the development of its edible berries in late summer.
