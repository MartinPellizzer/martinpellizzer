Ripartia ragusina

Ripartia ragusina, commonly known as the Ragusa thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Italy.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in coastal areas. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ripartia ragusina is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional Mediterranean herbal remedies.
