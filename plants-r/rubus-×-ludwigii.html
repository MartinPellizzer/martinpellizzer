Rubus × ludwigii

Rubus × ludwigii, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry or dewberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and natural habitats alike.
