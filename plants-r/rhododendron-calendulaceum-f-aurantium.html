Rhododendron calendulaceum f. aurantium

Rhododendron calendulaceum f. aurantium, commonly known as the flame azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

The cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
