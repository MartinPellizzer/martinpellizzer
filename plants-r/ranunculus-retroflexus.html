Ranunculus retroflexus

Ranunculus retroflexus, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and meadows. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens. It spreads via rhizomes and can form dense mats, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.

The plant contains toxic compounds, including protoanemonin, which can cause skin irritation in humans and animals.
