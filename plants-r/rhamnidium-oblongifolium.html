Rhamnidium oblongifolium

Rhamnidium oblongifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf rhamnus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oblong in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its role in supporting local wildlife.
