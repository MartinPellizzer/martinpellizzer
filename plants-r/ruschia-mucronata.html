Ruschia mucronata

Ruschia mucronata, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems and produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
