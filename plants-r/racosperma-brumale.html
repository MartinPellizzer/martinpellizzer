Racosperma brumale

Racosperma brumale, commonly known as the winter acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, pod-like fruits and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Racosperma brumale is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
