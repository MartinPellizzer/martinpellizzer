Rubus grabowskii

Rubus grabowskii, commonly known as the Grabowsky raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the genus Rubus in the family Rosaceae, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
