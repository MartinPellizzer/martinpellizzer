Rubus sneidernii

Rubus sneidernii, commonly known as the Sneider's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

The botanical features of Rubus sneidernii make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
