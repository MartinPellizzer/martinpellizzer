Rubus argenteus subsp. oplothyrsus

Rubus argenteus subsp.

oplothyrsus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This subspecies is often found in shrublands and along riverbanks, contributing to local biodiversity.
