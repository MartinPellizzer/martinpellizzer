Rhus oxyacantha var. ballii

Rhus oxyacantha var. ballii, commonly known as the Ball's sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fuzzy berries, which are a food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
