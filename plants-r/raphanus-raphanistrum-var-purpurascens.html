Raphanus raphanistrum var. purpurascens

Raphanus raphanistrum var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple sprouting broccoli, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact inflorescence that produces small, violet-colored flower buds, which are typically harvested before they fully open. The plant has a rosette of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves that are edible and often consumed as a vegetable. It thrives in cool climates and is cultivated for both its leaves and flowering stems.

The variety is notable for its vibrant color and nutritional value, making it a popular choice in gardens and agriculture.
