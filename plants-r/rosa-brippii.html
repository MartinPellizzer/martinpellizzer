Rosa brippii

Rosa brippii, commonly known as the Bripp's rose, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink to red in color. Rosa brippii produces rose-shaped blooms that are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
