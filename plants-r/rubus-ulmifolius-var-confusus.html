Rubus ulmifolius var. confusus

Rubus ulmifolius var. confusus, commonly known as the creeping blackberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
