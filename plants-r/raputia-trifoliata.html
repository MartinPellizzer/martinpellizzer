Raputia trifoliata

Raputia trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf fern, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, trifoliate leaves that resemble a three-leaf clover. The plant produces striking spadices, which are inflorescences that consist of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for indoor and greenhouse cultivation.
