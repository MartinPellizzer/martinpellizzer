Reseda somalensis

Reseda somalensis, commonly known as Somali Reseda, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Somalia and adjacent areas of East Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or herb, characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of yellow or white flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited rainfall.

Reseda somalensis is also valued for its potential in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
