Rhamnus boliviana

Rhamnus boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Bolivia and northern Argentina.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that are typically dark red or black when mature.

Rhamnus boliviana is adapted to dry or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.
