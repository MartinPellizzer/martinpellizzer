Rubus hirtus var. erythrodermis

Rubus hirtus var. erythrodermis, commonly known as the redstem blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to North America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. The plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions. It is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.

Its berries are edible but can be tart, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
