Rebutia violaciflora var. carminea

Rebutia violaciflora var. carminea, commonly known as the Carmine Rebutia, is a compact cactus native to South America, specifically Bolivia.

It features short, cylindrical stems that are typically ribbed and covered in white to pale green spines. The plant produces showy, violet-pink flowers during the spring and summer months, which bloom from the areoles along the stems. Its foliage is usually dense and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in well-draining soil and full sun.
