Rubus moluccanus var. poliophyllus

Rubus moluccanus var. poliophyllus, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that produces small, edible berries with a tart flavor. The plant features trifoliate leaves and has a woody stem structure typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
