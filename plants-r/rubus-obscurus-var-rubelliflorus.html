Rubus obscurus var. rubelliflorus

Rubus obscurus var. rubelliflorus, commonly known as the redflower blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are distinctive, featuring pinkish-red petals and a hairy calyx.

It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.
