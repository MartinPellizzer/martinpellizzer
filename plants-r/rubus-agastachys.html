Rubus agastachys

Rubus agastachys, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China and India.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red, hairy berries that ripen in the summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
