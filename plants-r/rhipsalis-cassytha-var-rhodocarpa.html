Rhipsalis cassytha var. rhodocarpa

Rhipsalis cassytha var. rhodocarpa, commonly known as the red-berried mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of trees and shrubs.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by red, fleshy berries that attract birds. Its leaves are alternate and have a waxy surface, aiding in water retention.

This species is native to parts of South America and is often found in humid, tropical environments.
