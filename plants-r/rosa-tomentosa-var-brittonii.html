Rosa tomentosa var. brittonii

Rosa tomentosa var. brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's woolly rose, is a flowering shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy gray foliage and fragrant pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with thorny stems and compound leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native gardens and ecological landscapes.
