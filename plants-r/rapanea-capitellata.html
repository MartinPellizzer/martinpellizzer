Rapanea capitellata

Rapanea capitellata, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters at the tips of the branches. These flowers are followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and small mammals.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
