Rorippa hispida subsp. glabra

Rorippa hispida subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows in moist or aquatic environments.

The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its subspecies name. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are characteristic of the family.

The seeds are small and typically dispersed by water, aiding in its spread along wetland habitats.
