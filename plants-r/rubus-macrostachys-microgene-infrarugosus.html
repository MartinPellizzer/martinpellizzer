Rubus macrostachys microgene infrarugosus

Rubus macrostachys microgene infrarugosus, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, which are covered in fine, rough hairs. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with three to five lobes, and are often hairy on both surfaces.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
