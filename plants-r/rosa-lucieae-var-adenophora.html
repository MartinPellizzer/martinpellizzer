Rosa lucieae var. adenophora

Rosa lucieae var. adenophora, commonly known as the腺花蔷薇, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to specific regions in China and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are typically pinnately compound, with serrated margins, and it produces small, red fruits that contain seeds.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
