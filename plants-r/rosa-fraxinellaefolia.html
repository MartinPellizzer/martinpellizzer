Rosa fraxinellaefolia

Rosa fraxinellaefolia, commonly known as the fraxinellaefolia rose, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in the spring. Its foliage is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
