Rapanea lorentziana var. intermedia

Rapanea lorentziana var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate roughleaf tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are usually rough in texture and have a distinctive aromatic scent. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue berries.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
