Rumohra miqueliana f. fimbriata

Rumohra miqueliana f. fimbriata, commonly known as the fimbriated miquel sedge, is a variant of the native South American plant Rumohra miqueliana.

It is a perennial grass-like plant that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive fringed or toothed edge, giving it its common name. It is often found in regions with high humidity and saturated soils.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
