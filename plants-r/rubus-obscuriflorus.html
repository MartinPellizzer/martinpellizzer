Rubus obscuriflorus

Rubus obscuriflorus, commonly known as the darkflower bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to western North America, particularly found in California and Oregon. This shrub typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has thorny stems covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces edible berries, though they are often bitter and less commonly consumed compared to other Rubus species.
