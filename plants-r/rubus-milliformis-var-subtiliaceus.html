Rubus milliformis var. subtiliaceus

Rubus milliformis var. subtiliaceus, commonly known as the silky blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are typically smaller than those of other blackberry varieties. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fruit characteristics.
