Rubus vestitus subsp. andegavensis

Rubus vestitus subsp.

andegavensis, commonly known as the Andegavensis raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
