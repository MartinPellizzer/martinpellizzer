Rhodomyrtus trineura var. canescens

Rhodomyrtus trineura var. canescens, commonly known as the silver-haired red guava, is a variant of the red guava native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in coastal and subtropical regions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually pink to red, and it produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fruit.
