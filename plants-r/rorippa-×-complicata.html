Rorippa × complicata

Rorippa × complicata, commonly known as the common watercress, is a hybrid species belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit, typically found in freshwater environments such as streams, ditches, and wetlands. The plant has alternate, narrow, and often serrated leaves that are submerged or floating, with small white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in muddy or aquatic substrates.

Rorippa × complicata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters, making it both a common weed and a valuable aquatic plant in certain ecosystems.
