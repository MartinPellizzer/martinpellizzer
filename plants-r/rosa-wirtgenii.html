Rosa wirtgenii

Rosa wirtgenii, commonly known as the Wirtgen's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan, Tajikistan, and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of fragrant pink to red flowers.

It produces small, round fruits called rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Rosa wirtgenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.
