Rosa orientalis var. olympica

Rosa orientalis var. olympica, commonly known as the Olympic rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant blooms that range in color from deep red to pink and white. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and scent.
