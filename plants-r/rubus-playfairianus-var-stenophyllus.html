Rubus playfairianus var. stenophyllus

Rubus playfairianus var. stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in forest understories and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its fruits are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
