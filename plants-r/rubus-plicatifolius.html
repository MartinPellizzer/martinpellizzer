Rubus plicatifolius

Rubus plicatifolius, commonly known as the pleated-leaf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pleated leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
