Rhododendron huangpingense

Rhododendron huangpingense, commonly known as the Huangping rhododendron, is a species native to China, specifically the Guangxi and Guizhou provinces.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with acidic, well-drained soils. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

Its botanical features make it a notable subject in both ecological studies and botanical gardens.
