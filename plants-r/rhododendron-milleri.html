Rhododendron milleri

Rhododendron milleri, commonly known as the Hainan rhododendron, is a species native to the island of Hainan in China.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, mountainous environments, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant features dark green, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rhododendron milleri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
