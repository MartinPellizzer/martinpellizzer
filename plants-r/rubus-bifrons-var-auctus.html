Rubus bifrons var. auctus

Rubus bifrons var. auctus, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, often forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, containing multiple drupes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
