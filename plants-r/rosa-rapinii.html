Rosa rapinii

Rosa rapinii, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red berries that contain seeds.

Rosa rapinii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
