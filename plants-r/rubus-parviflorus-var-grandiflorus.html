Rubus parviflorus var. grandiflorus

Rubus parviflorus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupes that are typically eaten by birds and small mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils and full sun to partial shade.

The flowers are white to pink and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
