Ruellia salicifolia

Ruellia salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, willow-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

Ruellia salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and garden design.
