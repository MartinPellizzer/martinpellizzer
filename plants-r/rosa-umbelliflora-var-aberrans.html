Rosa umbelliflora var. aberrans

Rosa umbelliflora var. aberrans, commonly known as the aberrant sweetbriar, is a variant of the sweetbriar rose native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Rosa genus.
