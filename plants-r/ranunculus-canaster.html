Ranunculus canaster

Ranunculus canaster, commonly known as the Canary Island buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Canary Islands.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with 5-15 petal-like sepals, often mistaken for true petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 30-60 cm, with simple, alternate leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
