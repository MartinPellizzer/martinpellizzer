Rymandra deplanchei

Rymandra deplanchei, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with hairy stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has small, spoon-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.

Rymandra deplanchei is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.
