Ranunculus longivaginatus

Ranunculus longivaginatus, commonly known as the long-hair buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens and a distinctive central pistil. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided with a hairy underside, and the stems are usually hairy as well.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental members.
