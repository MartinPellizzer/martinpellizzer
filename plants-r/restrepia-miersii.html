Restrepia miersii

Restrepia miersii, commonly known as the Miers' restrepia, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky or forested areas, often in humid and cool climates. The plant features a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers, which are usually small and delicate. Its flowers are typically greenish-white with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Restrepia miersii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
