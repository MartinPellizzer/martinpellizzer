Rotala thymoides

Rotala thymoides, commonly known as the threadleaf waterwheel, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often used in aquariums due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in freshwater environments.

Rotala thymoides prefers moderate to high light conditions and can tolerate a range of water temperatures.
