Ranunculus stenocolpus

Ranunculus stenocolpus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to nine petals, typically in shades of yellow or white, and a hairy, leafy stem. The plant's leaves are divided into three to five lobes, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ranunculus stenocolpus is known for its short flowering period in late spring to early summer.
