Rosa acicularis var. schrenkiana

Rosa acicularis var. schrenkiana, commonly known as the Schrenk's thorny rose, is a shrub-like plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces oval, yellowish-green leaves with serrated edges, and its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This variety is notable for its hardiness and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience.
