Rhamnus macedonica

Rhamnus macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit. The plant features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its fruit is a small, dark blue berry that is edible but often has a bitter taste.

Rhamnus macedonica is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
