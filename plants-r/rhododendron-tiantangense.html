Rhododendron tiantangense

Rhododendron tiantangense, commonly known as the Tiantang rhododendron, is a species native to China, specifically the Yunnan province.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions with high humidity and acidic soils. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white, blooming in late spring. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
