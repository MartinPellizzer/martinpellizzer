Rosa systyla erubescens

Rosa systyla erubescens, commonly known as the crimson stalked rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features narrow, slightly hairy leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often tinged with a reddish hue. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and delicate species in horticulture.
