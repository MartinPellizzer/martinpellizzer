Ranunculus minor

Ranunculus minor, commonly known as the lesser buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with five to ten petals. The plant features a basal rosette of finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
