Rhynchanthera modesta

Rhynchanthera modesta, commonly known as the modest orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to certain regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, upright stem that bears a single, showy flower. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption. The flower typically has a yellow or greenish hue with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by attracting specific insects.
