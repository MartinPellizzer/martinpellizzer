Rhododendron × nobleanum

Rhododendron × nobleanum, commonly known as the noble rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron caucasicum and Rhododendron ferrugineum.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom in late spring to early summer.
