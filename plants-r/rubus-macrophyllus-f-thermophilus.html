Rubus macrophyllus f. thermophilus

Rubus macrophyllus f. thermophilus, commonly known as the thermophilic raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its large, leathery leaves.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. Its botanical features include a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage.

This form is particularly adapted to subtropical and tropical regions, making it a notable species in horticultural and ecological studies.
