Rosa arvensis adversa

Rosa arvensis adversa, commonly known as the field rose or wild rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and produces fragrant pink or white flowers in the summer. Its fruit is a red, hairy berry that contains several seeds.

Rosa arvensis adversa is known for its adaptability and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
