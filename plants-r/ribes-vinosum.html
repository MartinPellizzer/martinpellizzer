Ribes vinosum

Ribes vinosum, commonly known as red currant bush, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its arching stems and clusters of small, bright red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Ribes vinosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in culinary applications due to its tart berries.
