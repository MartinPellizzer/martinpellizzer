Ribesiodes nutans

Ribesiodes nutans, commonly known as the weeping honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that droop gracefully, giving it a weeping appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow or white, which bloom in late spring. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a smooth texture.

Ribesiodes nutans is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
