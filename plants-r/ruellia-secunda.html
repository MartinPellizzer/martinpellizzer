Ruellia secunda

Ruellia secunda, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Ruellia secunda is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
