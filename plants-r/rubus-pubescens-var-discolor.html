Rubus pubescens var. discolor

Rubus pubescens var. discolor, commonly known as the variable bramble, is a shrub native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive two-toned appearance, often featuring a lighter underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
