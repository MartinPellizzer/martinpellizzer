Rubus nemorosus var. ciliatus

Rubus nemorosus var. ciliatus, commonly known as the hairy bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems, supporting various wildlife species.
