Rubus infracanus

Rubus infracanus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. The plant produces small, thorny stems and has trifoliate leaves with a serrated edge. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, tart berry.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
