Rubus hirtus var. giganteus

Rubus hirtus var. giganteus, commonly known as the giant raspberry, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its large, thorny stems and broad, serrated leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous small, red drupes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its vigorous growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
