Rauvolfia verticillata var. officinalis

Rauvolfia verticillata var. officinalis, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces elliptical, yellowish berries that contain the seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along riverbanks.

Botanically, it is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
