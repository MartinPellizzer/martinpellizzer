Ranunculus oligophyllus

Ranunculus oligophyllus, commonly known as the few-leaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, nodding yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive yellow center. The plant has a short, hairy stem and produces small, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Ranunculus oligophyllus is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic properties.
