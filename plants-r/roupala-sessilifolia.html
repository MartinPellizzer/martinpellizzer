Roupala sessilifolia

Roupala sessilifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved roupala, is a deciduous tree native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with moderate to high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
