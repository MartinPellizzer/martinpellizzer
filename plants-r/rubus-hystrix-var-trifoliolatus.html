Rubus hystrix var. trifoliolatus

Rubus hystrix var. trifoliolatus, commonly known as the thorny blackberry vine, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or hedgerow habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
