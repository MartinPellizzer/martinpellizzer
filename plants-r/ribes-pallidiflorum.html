Ribes pallidiflorum

Ribes pallidiflorum, commonly known as the paleflower currant, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features showy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has elongated, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its berries are small, red, and edible, often used in jams and jellies.

Ribes pallidiflorum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist woodland areas.
