Rubus crispomarginatus

Rubus crispomarginatus, commonly known as the hairy bramble, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
