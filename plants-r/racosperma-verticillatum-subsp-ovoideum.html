Racosperma verticillatum subsp. ovoideum

Racosperma verticillatum subsp.

ovoideum, commonly known as the oval-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia. It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in whorls along the stems.

The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
