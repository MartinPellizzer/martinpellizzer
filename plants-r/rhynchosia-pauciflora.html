Rhynchosia pauciflora

Rhynchosia pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow or white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Rhynchosia pauciflora is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
