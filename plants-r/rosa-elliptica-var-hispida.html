Rosa elliptica var. hispida

Rosa elliptica var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid dog rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its elliptical, hairy leaves and fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often growing in woodland edges and hedgerows. It produces rose hips that are typically red or orange, containing the seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in hedgerow management.
