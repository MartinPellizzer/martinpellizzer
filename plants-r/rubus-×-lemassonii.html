Rubus × lemassonii

Rubus × lemassonii, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry or loganberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus pectoralis (the evergreen raspberry). The plant features arching canes with thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruit is a large, dark red to black berry with a tart flavor.

It is valued for its culinary use and ornamental value in gardens.
