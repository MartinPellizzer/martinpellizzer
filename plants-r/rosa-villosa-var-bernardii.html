Rosa villosa var. bernardii

Rosa villosa var. bernardii, commonly known as the Bernard's bristly rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, ovate leaves with a soft, downy texture, and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces rose hips that are orange-red and covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
