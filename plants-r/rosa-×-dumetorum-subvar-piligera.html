Rosa × dumetorum subvar. piligera

Rosa × dumetorum subvar. piligera, commonly known as the sticky rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces oval, hairy leaves with a distinctive sticky secretion on the underside, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
