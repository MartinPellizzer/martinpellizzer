Rosa micrantha var. histrix

Rosa micrantha var. histrix, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces oval, red-orange fruits called hips, which are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
