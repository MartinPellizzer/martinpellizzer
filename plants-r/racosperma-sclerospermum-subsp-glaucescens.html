Racosperma sclerospermum subsp. glaucescens

Racosperma sclerospermum subsp.

glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous wattle, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its glaucous, blue-green foliage and yellow floral spikes.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
