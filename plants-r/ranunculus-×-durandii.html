Ranunculus × durandii

Ranunculus × durandii, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, double-layered flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its hybrid nature, it is not found in the wild and is primarily grown through horticultural methods.
