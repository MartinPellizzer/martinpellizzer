Rosa banksiana

Rosa banksiana, commonly known as the Canadian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. Its stems are covered in thorns, and it produces rosehips that contain seeds.

Rosa banksiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
