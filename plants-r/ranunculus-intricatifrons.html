Ranunculus intricatifrons

Ranunculus intricatifrons, commonly known as the intricate-fronded buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow petals that resemble those of other buttercup species. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of vibrant color to alpine meadows.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
