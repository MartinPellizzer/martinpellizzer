Rubus angustifrons var. vogesiacus

Rubus angustifrons var. vogesiacus, commonly known as the Vogesian bramble, is a variant of the narrow-leaved blackberry.

It is a shrub native to parts of Europe, particularly in the mountainous regions of the Alps and Carpathians. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are similar to other Rubus species.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
