Rosa canina f. othmarii

Rosa canina f. othmarii, commonly known as the Othmar rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to red flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa canina species. The plant is native to Central Europe and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
