Ruscus hypophyllum var. trifoliatus

Ruscus hypophyllum var. trifoliatus, commonly known as the three-leaved houseleek, is a perennial evergreen plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy, scale-like leaves that form a rosette structure. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements contribute to its widespread use in horticulture.
