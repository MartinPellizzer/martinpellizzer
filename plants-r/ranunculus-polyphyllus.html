Ranunculus polyphyllus

Ranunculus polyphyllus, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a striking perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with numerous petals and a central cluster of sepals. The plant typically grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring feathery, fern-like leaves that are deeply divided. Its flowers bloom in early to mid-summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ranunculus polyphyllus thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.
