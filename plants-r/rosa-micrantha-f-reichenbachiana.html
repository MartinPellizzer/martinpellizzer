Rosa micrantha f. reichenbachiana

Rosa micrantha f. reichenbachiana, commonly known as the Reichenbach's rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to light pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces rose hips that are reddish-brown when mature. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
