Rosa micrantha f. septicola

Rosa micrantha f. septicola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa micrantha species.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. It is a shrub with thorny stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
