Rourea induta var. reticulata

Rourea induta var. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate rourea, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, leathery leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
