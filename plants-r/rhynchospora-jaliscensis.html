Rhynchospora jaliscensis

Rhynchospora jaliscensis, commonly known as the Jalisco spike-rush, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Mexico, specifically the state of Jalisco, where it thrives in wetland and marshy habitats. The plant is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences and narrow, grass-like leaves. It plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
