Restio sprengelii

Restio sprengelii, commonly known as the Cape restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
