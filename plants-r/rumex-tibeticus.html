Rumex tibeticus

Rumex tibeticus, commonly known as Tibetan sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a reddish underside and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional Tibetan cuisine.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in local herbal practices.
