Rhipsalidopsis × graeseri

Rhipsalidopsis × graeseri, commonly known as the hybrid cactus or hybrid holiday cactus, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rhipsalidopsis purpurea and Rhipsalidopsis galapagei.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to the Canary Islands. This plant is characterized by its compact, branching structure and produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.
