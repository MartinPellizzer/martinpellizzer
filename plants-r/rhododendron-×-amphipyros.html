Rhododendron × amphipyros

Rhododendron × amphipyros, commonly known as the hybrid rhododendron, is a cross between Rhododendron catawbiense and Rhododendron maximum.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, broad leaves and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is native to the eastern United States and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and natural landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
