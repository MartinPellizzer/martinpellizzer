Rosa acicularis var. polyphylla

Rosa acicularis var. polyphylla, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The leaves are typically compound, with multiple leaflets that are narrow and pointed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine climates.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and ornamental plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
