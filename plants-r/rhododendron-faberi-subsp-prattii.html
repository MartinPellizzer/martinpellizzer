Rhododendron faberi subsp. prattii

Rhododendron faberi subsp.

prattii, commonly known as the Sichuan azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. Its flowers appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
