Rosa quasilucida

Rosa quasilucida, commonly known as the nearly luminous rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double flowers that range in color from pale pink to white. The plant has dark green, glossy foliage and produces thorny stems typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
