Rosa acmenodon

Rosa acmenodon, commonly known as the mountain rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorned stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.

Rosa acmenodon is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
