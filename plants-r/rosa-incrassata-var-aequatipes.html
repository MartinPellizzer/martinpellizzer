Rosa incrassata var. aequatipes

Rosa incrassata var. aequatipes, commonly known as the Japanese rose or the mountain rose, is a species native to East Asia, particularly Japan and Korea.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include pinnately compound leaves and fruits that develop into red, hairy achenes.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to temperate climates.
