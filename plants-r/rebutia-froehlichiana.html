Rebutia froehlichiana

Rebutia froehlichiana, commonly known as the Froehlichian Rebutia, is a succulent plant native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on long, slender stems.

Its leaves are modified into spines, and it is well adapted to arid environments, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
