Rheum macropterum

Rheum macropterum, commonly known as the Persian rhubarb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically reddish-purple in color, and it produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical name reflects its large, winged seeds, which are a distinctive feature of the species.

Rheum macropterum is closely related to other members of the Polygonaceae family, sharing similar morphological traits.
