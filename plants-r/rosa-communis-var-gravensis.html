Rosa communis var. gravensis

Rosa communis var. gravensis, commonly known as the heavy-flowered rose, is a cultivar prized for its dense, fragrant blooms.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its large, semi-double to fully double pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring regular pruning to maintain its shape and encourage flowering.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
