Rubus hirtus var. delicatulus

Rubus hirtus var. delicatulus, commonly known as the delicate raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features erect, thorny stems covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, though they may vary in color depending on the variety. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval to elliptic in shape.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and edible fruits.
