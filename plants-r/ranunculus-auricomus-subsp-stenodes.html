Ranunculus auricomus subsp. stenodes

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

stenodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It features finely divided, serrated leaves and bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and can be found in meadows, woodlands, and stream banks. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Ranunculus auricomus.
