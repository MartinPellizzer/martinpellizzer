Rorippa sublyrata f. longicarpa

Rorippa sublyrata f. longicarpa, commonly known as the long-sepaled watercress, is a variant of the watercress species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and are borne on short pedicels. The fruit is a small, elongated siliqua, which is a distinguishing feature of this form.

This plant thrives in moist soils and is often found in stream banks and marshy areas.
