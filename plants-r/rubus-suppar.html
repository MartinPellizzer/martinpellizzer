Rubus suppar

Rubus suppar, commonly known as the common bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are aggregate fruits formed from multiple tiny drupes.

Rubus suppar is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is widely cultivated for its edible fruits.
