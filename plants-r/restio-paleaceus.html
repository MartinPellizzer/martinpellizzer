Restio paleaceus

Restio paleaceus, commonly known as the silver restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Restio paleaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticultural settings for its elegant, delicate foliage.
