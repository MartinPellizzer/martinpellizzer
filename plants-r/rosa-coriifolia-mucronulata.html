Rosa coriifolia mucronulata

Rosa coriifolia mucronulata, commonly known as the Japanese rose or cutleaf rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately divided and covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to white, typically appearing in early summer. Its stems are often armed with hooked prickles, and it is well adapted to temperate climates.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
