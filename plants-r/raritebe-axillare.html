Raritebe axillare

Raritebe axillare, commonly known as the axillary beauty, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the axils of its leaves. The plant's leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth, waxy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Raritebe axillare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
