Richaeia madagascariensis

Richaeia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure with brightly colored petals and a distinct lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying shades of yellow, orange, and red.

Richaeia madagascariensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a symbol of the rich biodiversity found in Madagascar.
