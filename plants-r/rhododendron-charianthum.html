Rhododendron charianthum

Rhododendron charianthum, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a dark green upper surface with a lighter underside.

Rhododendron charianthum prefers acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.
