Rubus × semisprengelianus

Rubus × semisprengelianus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a cross between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus idaeus (raspberry), resulting in a plant that exhibits traits of both parent species. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color, with a distinctive raspberry-like flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental value and edible fruits, making it a popular choice for gardens and small-scale cultivation.
