Rubus surrejanus var. wealdensis

Rubus surrejanus var. wealdensis, commonly known as the Wealden blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the United Kingdom.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typically found in woodland and hedgerow habitats. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often associated with ancient woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and local biodiversity.
