Rubus koehleri var. cylindricus

Rubus koehleri var. cylindricus, commonly known as the cylindrical blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
