Racosperma floribundum

Racosperma floribundum, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Racosperma floribundum is an important species in Australian ecosystems and is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value.
