Rubus × semibifrons

Rubus × semibifrons, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the western coast of North America, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal thickets and woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
