Rubus nitidus var. oblongifrons

Rubus nitidus var. oblongifrons, commonly known as the glossy-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its shiny, dark green foliage and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and flowers with five pinkish-white petals.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.
