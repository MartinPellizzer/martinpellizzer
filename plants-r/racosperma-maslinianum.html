Racosperma maslinianum

Racosperma maslinianum, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with multiple small leaflets, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, and it plays a role in soil stabilization due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
