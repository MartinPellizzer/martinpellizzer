Rhytiglossa acuminatissima

Rhytiglossa acuminatissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
