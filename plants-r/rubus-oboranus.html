Rubus oboranus

Rubus oboranus, commonly known as the blackberry or raspberry plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant is native to various regions in the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and the fruits are edible, often used in culinary applications.

Rubus oboranus plays an important role in both ecological systems and human agriculture.
