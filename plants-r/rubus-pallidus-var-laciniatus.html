Rubus pallidus var. laciniatus

Rubus pallidus var. laciniatus, commonly known as the pale bramble or cut-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and compound leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a serrated edge. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, dark purple to black berry. This plant thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

It is a shrub that can grow up to several feet tall and is known for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
