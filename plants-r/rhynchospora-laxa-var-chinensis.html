Rhynchospora laxa var. chinensis

Rhynchospora laxa var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese looseleaf sedge, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to various aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
