Rubus wuchuanensis

Rubus wuchuanensis, commonly known as the Wuchuan blackberry, is a species of brambles native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
