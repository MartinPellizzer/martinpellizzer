Rubus tomentifolius

Rubus tomentifolius, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a species of shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
