Rhamnus pumila subsp. legionensis

Rhamnus pumila subsp.

legionensis, commonly known as the dwarf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring.

The plant is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or arctic environments. Its fruits are small, black drupes that contain seeds dispersed by birds.

This subspecies is notable for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, cold climates.
