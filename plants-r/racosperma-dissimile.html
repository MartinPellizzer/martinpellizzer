Racosperma dissimile

Racosperma dissimile, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for native wildlife.

Its seeds are often used in ecological restoration projects due to their hardiness and ability to thrive in degraded soils.
