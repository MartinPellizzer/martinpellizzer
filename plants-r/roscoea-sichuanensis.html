Roscoea sichuanensis

Roscoea sichuanensis, commonly known as the Sichuan roscoea, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers, and is characterized by its ornamental, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, red-orange inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to gardens. Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
