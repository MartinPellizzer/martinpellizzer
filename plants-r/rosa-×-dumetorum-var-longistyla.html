Rosa × dumetorum var. longistyla

Rosa × dumetorum var. longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled dog玫瑰, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant's leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges. Its flowers typically have five to seven petals and are accompanied by numerous stamens, with the style being notably long, giving it its botanical name.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
