Rapanea lancifolia

Rapanea lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved evergreen, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of India, China, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.
