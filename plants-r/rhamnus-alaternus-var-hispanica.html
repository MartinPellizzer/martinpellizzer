Rhamnus alaternus var. hispanica

Rhamnus alaternus var. hispanica, commonly known as Spanish buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It features small, dark green leaves that are typically oval or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue-black berries that are rich in anthocyanins. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its astringent properties.
