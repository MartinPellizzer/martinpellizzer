Rheum rhizostachyum

Rheum rhizostachyum, commonly known as the giant rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences that are often surrounded by colorful bracts. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, storing nutrients and contributing to its hardiness in harsh environments.

Despite its ornamental value, it is not typically cultivated for food due to its bitter taste and potential toxicity.
