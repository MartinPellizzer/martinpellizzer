Rosa afzeliana f. intricata

Rosa afzeliana f. intricata, commonly known as the intricately branched rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa afzeliana.

It is characterized by its complex, highly branched stems and dense foliage, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Native to regions of Asia, it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and unique growth form.
