Ranunculus yaegatakensis

Ranunculus yaegatakensis, commonly known as the Yaegata mountain buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are usually yellow or white in color.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
