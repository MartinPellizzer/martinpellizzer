Rosa canina subvar. frondosa

Rosa canina subvar. frondosa, commonly known as the frondose dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange to red hips in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
