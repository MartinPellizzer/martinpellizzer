Rhododendron kirkii

Rhododendron kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces striking clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing a dense canopy that supports a variety of wildlife.

Rhododendron kirkii is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
