Rubus ellipticus var. wallichianus

Rubus ellipticus var. wallichianus, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
