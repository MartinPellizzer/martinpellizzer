Ramonda serbica

Ramonda serbica, commonly known as the Serbian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkans.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves and produces bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are pollinated by insects.

Ramonda serbica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
