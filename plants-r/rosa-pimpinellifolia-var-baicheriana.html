Rosa pimpinellifolia var. baicheriana

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. baicheriana, commonly known as the Baicher's rock rose, is a compact, shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces oval, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand arid and rocky environments. It is often found growing in limestone soils and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to Mediterranean and alpine gardens.
