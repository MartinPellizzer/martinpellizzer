Rhipsalis harrisii

Rhipsalis harrisii, commonly known as the Harris's mistletoe cactus, is a species native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a climbing cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring long, thin stems covered in white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a bright red berry that contains seeds dispersed by birds.

Rhipsalis harrisii is often grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
