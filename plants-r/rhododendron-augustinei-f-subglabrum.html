Rhododendron augustinei f. subglabrum

Rhododendron augustinei f. subglabrum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved rhododendron, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that is smooth and glabrous, distinguishing it from other members of the genus. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires a cool, humid climate with high elevation.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
