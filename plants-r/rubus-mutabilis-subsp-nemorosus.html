Rubus mutabilis subsp. nemorosus

Rubus mutabilis subsp.

nemorosus, commonly known as the variable blackberry, is a shrub native to western North America. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and biennial growth habit, with the first year forming a leafy stem and the second year producing flowers and fruit.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it is known for its variable fruit coloration.
