Rosa hibernicina var. glabra

Rosa hibernicina var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-hairy rose, is a shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glabrous (smooth) stems and leaves, which are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually alternate and have serrated margins, while its thorns are short and curved.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
