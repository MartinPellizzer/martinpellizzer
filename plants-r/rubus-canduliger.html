Rubus canduliger

Rubus canduliger, commonly known as the candleberry bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black in color, which ripen in the late summer to early fall. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant often grows in moist, wooded areas.

Rubus canduliger is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
