Rhapis kebangensis

Rhapis kebangensis, commonly known as the Borneo bamboo palm, is a tropical plant native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and feather-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within lowland rainforests. Its leaves are long and narrow, often reaching several feet in length, and are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Rhapis kebangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
