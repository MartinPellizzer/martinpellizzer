Rosa billotii

Rosa billotii, commonly known as the Mexican rose or the wild rose of Mexico, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in a variety of habitats, including forests and open areas. The plant produces fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, hairy fruits called rosehips. Rosa billotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
