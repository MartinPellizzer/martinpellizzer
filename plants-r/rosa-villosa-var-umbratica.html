Rosa villosa var. umbratica

Rosa villosa var. umbratica, commonly known as the shady rose, is a variety of the hairy rose that thrives in partial to full shade.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant produces thorny stems and has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and is often used in shaded garden beds or as a ground cover.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom reliably even in low-light conditions.
