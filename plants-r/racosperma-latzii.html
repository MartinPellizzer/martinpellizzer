Racosperma latzii

Racosperma latzii, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, needle-like leaflets, and it produces yellow or orange flowers in spikes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species.

Racosperma latzii is often used in revegetation projects due to its resilience and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
