Robinsonia corrugata

Robinsonia corrugata, commonly known as the corrugated flax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome known for its high biodiversity.

The plant's common name refers to the ridged or corrugated appearance of its leaves.
