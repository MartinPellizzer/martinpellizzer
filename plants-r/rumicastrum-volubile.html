Rumicastrum volubile

Rumicastrum volubile, commonly known as the climbing nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and trailing growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Rumicastrum volubile is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
