Rosa micrantha subvar. perparva

Rosa micrantha subvar. perparva, commonly known as the small-flowered shrub rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, with small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant produces rose hips that are orange-red in color and contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural gardens.
