Rubus divaricatus var. pictus

Rubus divaricatus var. pictus, commonly known as the painted raspberry, is a variant of the trailing raspberry species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in jams and preserves. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
