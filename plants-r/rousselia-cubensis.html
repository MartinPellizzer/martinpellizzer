Rousselia cubensis

Rousselia cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban rosemary, is a flowering plant native to Cuba.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in coastal and dry habitats.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves are valued for their aromatic properties and potential medicinal uses.
