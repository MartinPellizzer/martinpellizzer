Rhamnus coriophylla

Rhamnus coriophylla, commonly known as the Japanese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by dark red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
