Rosa rubiginosa subsp. pulvinaris

Rosa rubiginosa subsp.

pulvinaris, commonly known as the red-stemmed rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces oval, yellowish-orange fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
