Rosa villosa var. pseudopomifera

Rosa villosa var. pseudopomifera, commonly known as the fuzzy rose or the hairy rose, is a variant of the Rosa villosa species native to North America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its fruit, a small, hairy capsule, matures in the fall and contains several seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in various garden settings.
