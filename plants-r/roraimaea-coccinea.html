Roraimaea coccinea

Roraimaea coccinea, commonly known as the red roraima or red orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the tepuis of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant thrives in high-altitude, humid environments with minimal direct sunlight. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Due to its unique habitat and aesthetic appeal, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
