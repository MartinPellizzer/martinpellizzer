Rosa centipetala

Rosa centipetala, commonly known as the hundred-leaved rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its dense, overlapping foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a pinnate pattern.

Rosa centipetala is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and scent.
