Rubus schleicheri subsp. conterminus

Rubus schleicheri subsp.

conterminus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile. It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and acidic.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink or white. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to other Rubus species.
