Rivea suffruticosa

Rivea suffruticosa, commonly known as the sweet passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive crown of petals. Its fruit is a small, green capsule that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Rivea suffruticosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
