Ranunculus cyclocarpus

Ranunculus cyclocarpus, commonly known as the cyclocarpus buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers and distinctive foliage. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
