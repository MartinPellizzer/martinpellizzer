Ranunculus hebecarpus

Ranunculus hebecarpus, commonly known as the western burr-crown.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to North America, particularly found in the western United States. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often surrounded by a series of small, hooked bracts that form a crown-like structure. Its leaves are deeply lobed and can be either basal or alternate, depending on the growth stage.

The species is adapted to rocky and dry environments, showcasing its resilience in harsh conditions.
