Rosa canina f. leucophaea

Rosa canina f. leucophaea, commonly known as the white thorned rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which are slightly smaller than those of the typical Rosa canina. The plant produces oval, red-orange hips that are edible and rich in vitamin C. It is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves.

This cultivar is often appreciated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
