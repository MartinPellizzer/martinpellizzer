Rollinia mucosa subsp. portoricensis

Rollinia mucosa subsp.

portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican star fruit, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Puerto Rico. It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters.

The plant produces yellow-orange fruits with a distinctive star-shaped appearance when mature. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a tart flavor.
