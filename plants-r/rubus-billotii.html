Rubus billotii

Rubus billotii, commonly known as the yellow raspberry, is a species of brambles native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically edible. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pinkish flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
