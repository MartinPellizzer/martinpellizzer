Ranunculus occidentalis var. tenellus

Ranunculus occidentalis var. tenellus, commonly known as the western buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to seven yellow petals and numerous stamens, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, and it prefers moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It is often found in areas with partial shade and is known for its rapid growth and short blooming period.

The species is also noted for its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
