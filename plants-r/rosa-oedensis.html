Rosa oedensis

Rosa oedensis, commonly known as the Oedemar's rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features pinnately compound leaves and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and ovate, serrated leaflets.

Rosa oedensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
