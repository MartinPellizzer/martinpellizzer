Rubus foliosus subsp. ctenodon

Rubus foliosus subsp.

ctenodon, commonly known as the hooked bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically divided into several leaflets.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies due to its role in supporting local wildlife.
