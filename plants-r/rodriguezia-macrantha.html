Rodriguezia macrantha

Rodriguezia macrantha, commonly known as the giant hibiscus, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Rodriguezia macrantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
