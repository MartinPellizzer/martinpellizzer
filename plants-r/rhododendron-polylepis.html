Rhododendron polylepis

Rhododendron polylepis, commonly known as the Andean rhododendron, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, thriving in cool, moist environments with high elevation. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron polylepis is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
