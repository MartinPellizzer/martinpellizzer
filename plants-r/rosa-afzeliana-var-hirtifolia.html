Rosa afzeliana var. hirtifolia

Rosa afzeliana var. hirtifolia, commonly known as the hairy rose or woolly rose, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and India.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its "hirtifolia" designation, which means "hairy leaf" in Latin. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
