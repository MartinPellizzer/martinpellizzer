Ranunculus ficaria f. elongatus

Ranunculus ficaria f. elongatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed buttercup, is a variant of the buttercup species characterized by its elongated stems and showy yellow flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Its botanical classification places it within the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

The f. elongatus form is prized in horticulture for its unique stem structure and vibrant floral display.
