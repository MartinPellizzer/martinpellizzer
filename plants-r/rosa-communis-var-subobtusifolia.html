Rosa communis var. subobtusifolia

Rosa communis var. subobtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed rose, is a cultivar characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves and abundant, fragrant flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The flowers, which range in color from white to deep pink, bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dense and provides good coverage, making it suitable for hedging or ornamental gardens.

The plant is relatively disease-resistant and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
