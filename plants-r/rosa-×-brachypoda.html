Rosa × brachypoda

Rosa × brachypoda, commonly known as the shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

Rosa × brachypoda is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
