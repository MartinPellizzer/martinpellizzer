Rosa arkansana f. alba

Rosa arkansana f. alba, commonly known as the white Arkansas rose, is a botanical variant of the Arkansas rose, characterized by its pale pink to white flowers.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Arkansas and neighboring regions. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, and it produces fragrant, semi-double flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for ornamental gardening.

The white flower coloration is a distinguishing feature of this form, setting it apart from other rosa species.
