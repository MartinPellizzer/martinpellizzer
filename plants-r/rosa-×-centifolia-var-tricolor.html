Rosa × centifolia var. tricolor

Rosa × centifolia var. tricolor, commonly known as the multicolored pink or tricolor rose, is a hybrid shrub native to France.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored flowers that typically feature a white base, pink midsection, and red or crimson tip. The plant produces fragrant blooms in late spring to early summer, and its foliage is dark green with a slightly hairy texture. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in perfumery and floral arrangements.

This variety is relatively hardy and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
