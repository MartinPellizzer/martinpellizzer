Racosperma leptaleum

Racosperma leptaleum, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators.

Racosperma leptaleum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
