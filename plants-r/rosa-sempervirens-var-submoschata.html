Rosa sempervirens var. submoschata

Rosa sempervirens var. submoschata, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, fragrant pink to white flowers, and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, red-orange hips that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its evergreen nature in mild climates adds year-round interest to landscapes.
