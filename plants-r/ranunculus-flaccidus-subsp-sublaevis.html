Ranunculus flaccidus subsp. sublaevis

Ranunculus flaccidus subsp.

sublaevis, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the eastern United States. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically arranged in a cup-like shape.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually divided into lobes, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

The subspecies sublaevis is distinguished by its more slender stems and slightly different leaf morphology compared to other members of the species.
