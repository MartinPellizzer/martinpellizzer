Ricinus communis var. sanguineus

Ricinus communis var. sanguineus, commonly known as the crimson castor oil plant, is a variant of the castor oil plant characterized by its striking red foliage.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, featuring large, palmately lobed leaves that are often variegated with red and green hues. The plant produces distinctive spiky flower clusters, which are typically yellow-green in color, and it is cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves contain toxic compounds, including ricin, making it potentially hazardous if ingested.

Despite its toxicity, it is valued in horticulture for its vibrant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
