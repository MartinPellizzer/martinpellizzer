Rhynchospora corymbosa var. legrandii

Rhynchospora corymbosa var. legrandii, commonly known as the Louisiana marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to dark purple flowers during the summer months. Its botanical structure is adapted to tolerate waterlogged conditions, with a fibrous root system that helps anchor it in soft, muddy substrates.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly different floral morphology and growth habit compared to the typical form of Rhynchospora corymbosa.
