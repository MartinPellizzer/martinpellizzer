Rubus subaculeatus var. adnophyllus

Rubus subaculeatus var. adnophyllus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a shrub native to western North America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and streambanks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it is known for its thorny stems.

The berries are edible but often have a tart, astringent flavor.
