Rubus niveus var. timorensis

Rubus niveus var. timorensis, commonly known as the Timor whitebramble, is a shrub native to the island of Timor.

It is characterized by its white-pubescent stems and leaves with a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its berries are typically small and edible, though they are not as widely cultivated as other raspberry varieties.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
