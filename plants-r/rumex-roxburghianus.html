Rumex roxburghianus

Rumex roxburghianus, commonly known as Indian sorrel, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It features erect, branched stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a serrated margin. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The leaves are edible and have a tart, sour flavor, making it a traditional food in some regions.
