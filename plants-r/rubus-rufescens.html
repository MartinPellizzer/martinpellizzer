Rubus rufescens

Rubus rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

Rubus rufescens is valued for its edible berries, which are often used in jams and pies.
