Rubus pruinosus

Rubus pruinosus, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and silvery-white undersides on its leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land management.
