Racosperma oswaldii

Racosperma oswaldii, commonly known as the Darwin's wattle, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers in summer. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
