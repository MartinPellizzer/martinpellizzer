Rutidea gracilis subsp. gracilis

Rutidea gracilis subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender rutidion, is a flowering plant native to South America. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed outside its native range.
