Rubus papulosus

Rubus papulosus, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of North America, particularly the eastern United States. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, woodland habitats.

Rubus papulosus plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
