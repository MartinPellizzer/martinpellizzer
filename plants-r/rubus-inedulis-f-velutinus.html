Rubus inedulis f. velutinus

Rubus inedulis f. velutinus, commonly known as the velvet raspberry, is a botanical variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and stems.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of North America, particularly the Pacific Northwest. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically reddish in color and have a slightly tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners interested in both aesthetics and edible plants.
