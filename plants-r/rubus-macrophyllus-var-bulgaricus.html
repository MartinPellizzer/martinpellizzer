Rubus macrophyllus var. bulgaricus

Rubus macrophyllus var. bulgaricus, commonly known as the Bulgarian blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its berries are often used in jams, wines, and traditional local cuisine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and thorny stems.
