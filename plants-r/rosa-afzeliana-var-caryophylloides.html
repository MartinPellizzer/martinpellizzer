Rosa afzeliana var. caryophylloides

Rosa afzeliana var. caryophylloides, commonly known as the fragrant rose or caryophyllaceous rose, is a species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, semi-double to fully double flowers in shades of pink and red. The plant features serrated, dark green leaves and has a strong, spicy fragrance reminiscent of cloves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
