Rorippa × barbareoides

Rorippa × barbareoides, commonly known as the yellow pond-lily, is a hybrid species within the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that form a dense mat on the water surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, often with four petals. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

Rorippa × barbareoides is an important species for aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
