Rhipsalidopsis rosea

Rhipsalidopsis rosea, commonly known as the Easter cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, branching stems that are covered in short, woolly hairs. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers during the spring and early summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Rhipsalidopsis rosea is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ease of care and ornamental appeal.
