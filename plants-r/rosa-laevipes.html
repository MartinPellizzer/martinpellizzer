Rosa laevipes

Rosa laevipes, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile. This shrub typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features woody stems covered in fine, smooth hairs. Its leaves are compound, usually consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and are often grayish-green in color.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
