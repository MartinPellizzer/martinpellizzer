Racosperma echinulum

Racosperma echinulum, commonly known as the spiny acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

Racosperma echinulum is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its ornamental appeal.
