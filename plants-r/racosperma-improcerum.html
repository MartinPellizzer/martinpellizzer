Racosperma improcerum

Racosperma improcerum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typical of the genus Racosperma. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a narrow, linear shape.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
