Rhododendron sinovaccinioides

Rhododendron sinovaccinioides, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments. Its botanical features include showy inflorescences and a preference for acidic, well-drained soils.

Rhododendron sinovaccinioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
