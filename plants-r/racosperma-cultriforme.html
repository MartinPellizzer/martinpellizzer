Racosperma cultriforme

Racosperma cultriforme, commonly known as the sickle wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne on the stems. Its leaves are pinnate, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Racosperma cultriforme is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
