Rhododendron kanehirae

Rhododendron kanehirae, commonly known as the Kanehirae azalea, is a species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its blooms, which range in color from pink to red, are particularly striking in spring.

Rhododendron kanehirae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
