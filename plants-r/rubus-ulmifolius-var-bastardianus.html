Rubus ulmifolius var. bastardianus

Rubus ulmifolius var. bastardianus, commonly known as the bastardian blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, berries and its shrub-like growth habit. The plant typically thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually serrated and have a distinctive dark green color with a slightly hairy texture.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
