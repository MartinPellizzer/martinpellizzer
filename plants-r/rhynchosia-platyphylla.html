Rhynchosia platyphylla

Rhynchosia platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are similar to common cowpeas. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Rhynchosia platyphylla is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural systems.
