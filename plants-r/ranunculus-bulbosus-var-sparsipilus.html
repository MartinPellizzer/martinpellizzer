Ranunculus bulbosus var. sparsipilus

Ranunculus bulbosus var. sparsipilus, commonly known as the sparse-hairy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with numerous petals and a hairy stem. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has a basal rosette of leaves. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, giving it the "sparse-hairy" designation in its scientific name.

This variety is often found in meadows and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
