Rhododendron formosum var. inaequale

Rhododendron formosum var. inaequale, commonly known as the Formosan unequal rhododendron, is a variant native to Taiwan.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant pink to red blossoms. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the showy flowers.

This species thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates.
