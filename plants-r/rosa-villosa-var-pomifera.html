Rosa villosa var. pomifera

Rosa villosa var. pomifera, commonly known as the fuzzy rose or wild rose, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in the spring, followed by red, fuzzy rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
