Racosperma oncinophyllum subsp. patulifolium

Racosperma oncinophyllum subsp.

patulifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically broad and feathery, with a distinctive grey-green color.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes, which are highly attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
