Rhododendron mimetes

Rhododendron mimetes, commonly known as the mimic rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shady, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink and purple.

Rhododendron mimetes is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
