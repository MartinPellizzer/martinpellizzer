Rosa communis var. uncinelloides

Rosa communis var. uncinelloides, commonly known as the hooked rose, is a variation of the common rose that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their aesthetic appeal and pleasant fragrance.

This variety is well-suited to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
