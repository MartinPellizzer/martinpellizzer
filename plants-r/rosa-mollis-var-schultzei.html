Rosa mollis var. schultzei

Rosa mollis var. schultzei, commonly known as the Schultze's soft rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance, and its flowers are typically pink or white with a sweet fragrance. The plant is well adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. It produces small, edible hips that are often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
