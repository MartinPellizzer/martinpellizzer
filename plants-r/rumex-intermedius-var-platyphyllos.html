Rumex intermedius var. platyphyllos

Rumex intermedius var. platyphyllos, commonly known as broad-leaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. The plant has alternate, broadly ovate to lanceolate leaves with a distinctive wavy margin. Its flowers are small and clustered in loose spikes, with greenish-yellow bracts.

The species is characterized by its robust growth and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
