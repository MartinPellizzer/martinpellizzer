Rubus rivularis subsp. spinosulus

Rubus rivularis subsp.

spinosulus, commonly known as the spiny blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant produces small, tart berries that are typically eaten raw or used in jams and preserves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in hedgerows and along riverbanks.
