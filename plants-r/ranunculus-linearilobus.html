Ranunculus linearilobus

Ranunculus linearilobus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals, and its leaves are divided into narrow, linear lobes. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a short stem and produces small, greenish flowers in early spring.

Ranunculus linearilobus is often found in areas with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
