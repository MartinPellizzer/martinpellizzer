Ranunculus subconjungens

Ranunculus subconjungens, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features palmate leaves with three to five lobes and produces yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
