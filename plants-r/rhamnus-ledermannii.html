Rhamnus ledermannii

Rhamnus ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are an important food source for various birds and small mammals. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is often found in chaparral and desert scrub habitats.

Rhamnus ledermannii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native range.
