Rosa × collina subsp. alba

Rosa × collina subsp.

alba, commonly known as the white rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, often forming low, spreading mats.

The plant produces showy, single or double white flowers with a delicate fragrance, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, slightly hairy, and arranged in alternate fashion along the stems. Rosa × collina subsp.

alba is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
