Rubus barmondierei

Rubus barmondierei, commonly known as the Barmondiere blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a temperate climate with adequate sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
