Rhododendron spiciferum

Rhododendron spiciferum, commonly known as the spiked rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink and purple.

Rhododendron spiciferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
