Rhamnus alaternus subsp. ludovici-salvatoris

Rhamnus alaternus subsp.

ludovici-salvatoris, commonly known as the Italian buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, black berries that mature in late summer.

The plant is typically found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in full sun. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
