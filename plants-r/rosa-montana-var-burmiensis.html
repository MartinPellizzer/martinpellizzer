Rosa montana var. burmiensis

Rosa montana var. burmiensis, commonly known as the Burmese mountain rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features upright, thorny stems and produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in the spring. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
