Ranunculus auricomus subsp. lacerescens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

lacerescens, commonly known as the ragged leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features delicate, palmate leaves with a distinctive jagged or frayed appearance, giving it its common name.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals that are often adorned with a distinctive central mark. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Ranunculaceae family.
