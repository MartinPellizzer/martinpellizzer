Rosa plagiophylla

Rosa plagiophylla, commonly known as the Chilean rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are pinnate, with serrated edges, and the plant is well adapted to coastal and arid environments.

Rosa plagiophylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
