Rosa arvensis f. vera

Rosa arvensis f. vera, commonly known as the true field rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically solitary and are borne on slender pedicels.

Rosa arvensis f. vera is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
