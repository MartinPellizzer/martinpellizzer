Rubus adenophyllus

Rubus adenophyllus, commonly known as the sandbar raspberry, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of North America.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and dense clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shores and dunes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing food for wildlife.
