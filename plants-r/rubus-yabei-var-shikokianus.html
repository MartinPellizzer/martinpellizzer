Rubus yabei var. shikokianus

Rubus yabei var. shikokianus, commonly known as the Shikoku raspberry, is a variant of the Japanese raspberry species.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas of Japan.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
