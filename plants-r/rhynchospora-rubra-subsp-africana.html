Rhynchospora rubra subsp. africana

Rhynchospora rubra subsp.

africana, commonly known as the African rose marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to sub-Saharan Africa. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often reddish in color. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale pink to reddish flowers.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
