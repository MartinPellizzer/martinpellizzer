Rubus ulmifolius var. villosispinus

Rubus ulmifolius var. villosispinus, commonly known as the hairy-leaved blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its specific epithet "villosispinus.". It produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by edible blackberries in late summer.

This variety is often found in woodlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
