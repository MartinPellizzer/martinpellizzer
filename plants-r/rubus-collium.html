Rubus collium

Rubus collium, commonly known as the Scottish blackberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the British Isles and grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and hedgerows. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems that provide structural support.

Rubus collium plays a role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects, due to its fruit and foliage.
