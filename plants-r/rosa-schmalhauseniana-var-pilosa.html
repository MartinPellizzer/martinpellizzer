Rosa schmalhauseniana var. pilosa

Rosa schmalhauseniana var. pilosa, commonly known as the fuzzy shrub rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems covered in a soft, woolly layer of grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with multiple oval-shaped leaflets that are also covered in fine pubescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and floral display.
