Rollandia truncata

Rollandia truncata, commonly known as the Trinidadian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing near water sources.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
