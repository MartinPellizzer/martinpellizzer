Ranunculus trichophyllus proles drouetii

Ranunculus trichophyllus proles drouetii, commonly known as the Drouet's hairy buttercup, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
