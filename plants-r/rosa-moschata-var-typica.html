Rosa moschata var. typica

Rosa moschata var. typica, commonly known as the musk rose, is a species native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers and thorny stems. The plant produces small, red hips that contain aromatic seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.

Rosa moschata var. typica thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.
