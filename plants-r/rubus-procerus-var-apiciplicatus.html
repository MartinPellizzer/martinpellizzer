Rubus procerus var. apiciplicatus

Rubus procerus var. apiciplicatus, commonly known as the hairy-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
