Ranunculus viriditorus

Ranunculus viriditorus, commonly known as the greenleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, and its flowers are bright yellow with numerous petals arranged in a cup-like shape. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic sap.

The greenleaf buttercup is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
