Ranunculus bongardii

Ranunculus bongardii, commonly known as the Bongard's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has delicate, trifoliate leaves with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Ranunculus bongardii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
