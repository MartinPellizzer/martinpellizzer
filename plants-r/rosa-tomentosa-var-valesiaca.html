Rosa tomentosa var. valesiaca

Rosa tomentosa var. valesiaca, commonly known as the Valesiaca rose, is a shrub-like species native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, thorny stems covered in a soft, woolly grayish-white pubescence. The leaves are compound, consisting of several elliptical leaflets with a hairy underside. Its flowers are typically pink to reddish, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
