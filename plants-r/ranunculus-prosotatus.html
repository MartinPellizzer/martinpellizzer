Ranunculus prosotatus

Ranunculus prosotatus, commonly known as the rough buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bright yellow with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
