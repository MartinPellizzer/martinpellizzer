Rhynchosia velutina var. discolor

Rhynchosia velutina var. discolor, commonly known as the velvety cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive pale or variegated appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
