Rosularia wilczekiana

Rosularia wilczekiana, commonly known as the Wilczek's rosebush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Rosularia wilczekiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
