Rodriguezia macrostachya

Rodriguezia macrostachya, commonly known as the giant hibiscus, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, featuring vibrant pink or red petals. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its humid habitat. It thrives in warm, moist environments and is often found growing near streams or in shaded undergrowth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and exotic plant collections.
