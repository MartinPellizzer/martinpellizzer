Rubus carneistylus

Rubus carneistylus, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to New Zealand, characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
