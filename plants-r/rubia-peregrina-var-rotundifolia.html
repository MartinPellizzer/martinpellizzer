Rubia peregrina var. rotundifolia

Rubia peregrina var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaved gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, ovate to round leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct, slightly serrated edge. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for various pollinators and its ornamental value in gardens.
