Rhoicissus holstii

Rhoicissus holstii, commonly known as the Holst's climbing fig, is a vine native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using tendrils. Its fruit is a small, inedible berry that turns red when mature.

Rhoicissus holstii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
