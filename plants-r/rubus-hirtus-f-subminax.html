Rubus hirtus f. subminax

Rubus hirtus f. subminax, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.

This subspecies is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
