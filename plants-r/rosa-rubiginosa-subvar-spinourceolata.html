Rosa rubiginosa subvar. spinourceolata

Rosa rubiginosa subvar. spinourceolata, commonly known as the bristly red rose, is a shrub-like species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, deep red flowers. The plant produces oval, leathery leaves with a rough, glandular surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in Mediterranean climates.
