Rosularia pseudohaussknechtii

Rosularia pseudohaussknechtii, commonly known as the desert rose or false rose, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It features a compact, rosette-shaped stem with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically during the spring or early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a lack of true stems and a fibrous root system, making it well-suited for rocky or sandy soils.

Rosularia pseudohaussknechtii is valued for its resilience and ornamental appeal in xerophytic gardens.
