Rudgea barbosae

Rudgea barbosae, commonly known as the pink rudgea, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Rudgea barbosae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
