Ranunculus auricomus subsp. acutiusculus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

acutiusculus, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It features basal leaves that are pinnately divided and covered in fine hairs, with a distinctive yellow flower that has five petals and numerous stamens.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It produces achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
