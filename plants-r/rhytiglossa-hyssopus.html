Rhytiglossa hyssopus

Rhytiglossa hyssopus, commonly known as the blue hyssop, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy blue or purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Rhytiglossa hyssopus is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
