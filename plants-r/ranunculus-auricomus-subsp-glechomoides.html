Ranunculus auricomus subsp. glechomoides

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

glechomoides, commonly known as the leafy buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features showy yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals, often accompanied by a central yellow stamen.

The leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure that resembles the leaves of Glechoma, hence its subspecies name. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

The plant is characterized by its delicate foliage and is often found in temperate regions with cool, humid climates.
