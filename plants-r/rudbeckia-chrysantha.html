Rudbeckia chrysantha

Rudbeckia chrysantha, commonly known as the yellow coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a dark brown central disk, giving it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Rudbeckia chrysantha is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.
