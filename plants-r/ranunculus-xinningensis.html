Ranunculus xinningensis

Ranunculus xinningensis, commonly known as the Xinning buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features palmately divided leaves with a hairy surface, and its stems are usually hairy as well. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
