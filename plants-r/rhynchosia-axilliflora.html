Rhynchosia axilliflora

Rhynchosia axilliflora, commonly known as the axillary-flowered cowpea, is a species of legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Rhynchosia axilliflora is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural systems.
