Rhamphicarpa stricta

Rhamphicarpa stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, hairy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Rhamphicarpa stricta is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is an important component of desert ecosystems.
