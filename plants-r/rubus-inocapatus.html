Rubus inocapatus

Rubus inocapatus, commonly known as the Andean blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires a cool growing season.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
