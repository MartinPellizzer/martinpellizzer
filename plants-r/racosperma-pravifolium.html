Racosperma pravifolium

Racosperma pravifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a species of shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and feathery, and it produces small yellow flowers during the warmer months.

Racosperma pravifolium is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
