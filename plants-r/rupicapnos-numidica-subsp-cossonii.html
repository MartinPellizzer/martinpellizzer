Rupicapnos numidica subsp. cossonii

Rupicapnos numidica subsp.

cossonii, commonly known as the Corsican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
