Romualdea apiculata

Romualdea apiculata, commonly known as the woolly broom.

It is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Romualdea apiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
