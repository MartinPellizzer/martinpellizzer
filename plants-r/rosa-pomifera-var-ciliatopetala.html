Rosa pomifera var. ciliatopetala

Rosa pomifera var. ciliatopetala, commonly known as the silky-petaled rose, is a unique variant of the rose family with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy petals that give it a velvety appearance, and its leaves are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces large, aggregate fruits known as rose hips, which are often red or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
