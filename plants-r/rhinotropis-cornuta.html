Rhinotropis cornuta

Rhinotropis cornuta, commonly known as the horned dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like bracts that surround the flower head, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it typically grows in rocky or sandy soils.

Rhinotropis cornuta is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, where it thrives in full sun.
