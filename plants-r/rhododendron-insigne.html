Rhododendron insigne

Rhododendron insigne, commonly known as the noble rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Rhododendron insigne is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
