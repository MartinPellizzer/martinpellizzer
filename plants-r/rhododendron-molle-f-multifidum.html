Rhododendron molle f. multifidum

Rhododendron molle f. multifidum, commonly known as the multifidum form of the sweet azalea, is a hybrid plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, with a glossy surface that adds to its ornamental appeal.

This cultivar is valued for its hardiness and ability to provide vibrant seasonal color in gardens and natural landscapes.
