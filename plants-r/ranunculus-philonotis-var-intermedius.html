Ranunculus philonotis var. intermedius

Ranunculus philonotis var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate water crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, ditches, and marshes. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in aquatic and wetland ecosystems.
