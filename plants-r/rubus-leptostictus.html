Rubus leptostictus

Rubus leptostictus, commonly known as the slender-streaked blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles and blackberry species.
