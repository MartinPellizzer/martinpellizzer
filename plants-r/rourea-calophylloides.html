Rourea calophylloides

Rourea calophylloides, commonly known as the velvetleaf rourea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptical in shape, and often have a leathery texture.

Rourea calophylloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
