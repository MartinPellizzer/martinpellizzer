Rubus mercicus var. chrysoxylon

Rubus mercicus var. chrysoxylon, commonly known as the golden-hued raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its distinctive yellowish fruit.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit develops into a hairy, red or yellow berry.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique fruit coloration.
