Rubus pruinosus f. warmingii

Rubus pruinosus f. warmingii, commonly known as the glossy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its dark, shiny foliage and fruit.

It is native to parts of East Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and produces small, dark berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its botanical features include serrated leaves and a compact growth habit.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
