Rubus augustus

Rubus augustus, commonly known as the August raspberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, which is often used in jams and desserts, has a distinctive sweet-tart flavor.
