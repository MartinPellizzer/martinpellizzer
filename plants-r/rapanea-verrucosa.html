Rapanea verrucosa

Rapanea verrucosa, commonly known as the rough-leafed holly, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in small, raised bumps or warts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ornamental and ecological purposes in tropical regions.
