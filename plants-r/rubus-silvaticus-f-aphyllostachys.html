Rubus silvaticus f. aphyllostachys

Rubus silvaticus f. aphyllostachys, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble, is a variant of the common bramble characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on the leaves.

This form exhibits reduced leaflets, giving it a more compact and less foliage-dense appearance compared to the typical Rubus silvaticus. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus, and it thrives in shaded, moist woodland environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible, though tart, berries.

The silverleaf bramble is valued in ecological gardens for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting local wildlife.
