Rubus radula f. subvirescens

Rubus radula f. subvirescens, commonly known as the green-fruited blackberry, is a botanical variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive greenish hue in its fruit.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of North America. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with three leaflets, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, while less common than the typical red-blackberry, is edible and has a tart flavor.
