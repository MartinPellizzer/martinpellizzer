Rubus despectus

Rubus despectus, commonly known as the weeping blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark purple to black berries that appear in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers in early spring. Its berries are edible but often have a tart flavor, making them suitable for jams and wines.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
