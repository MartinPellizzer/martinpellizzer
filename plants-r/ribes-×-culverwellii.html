Ribes × culverwellii

Ribes × culverwellii, commonly known as the hybrid gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of flowering plants and edible berries. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and features alternate, simple, ovate leaves with serrated margins. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, edible berries that are similar to those of its parent species.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and fruit production.
