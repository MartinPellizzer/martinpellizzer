Rosa ruficaulis

Rosa ruficaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features upright, woody stems that are typically reddish in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets arranged along slender petioles.

Rosa ruficaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest and resilience.
