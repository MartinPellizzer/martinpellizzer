Rhysolepis bakeriana

Rhysolepis bakeriana, commonly known as the Baker's mountain maple, is a small deciduous shrub or tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin layers.

Rhysolepis bakeriana plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
