Rubus atrichantherus var. vestitior

Rubus atrichantherus var. vestitior, commonly known as the velvet raspberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless stems and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with a velvety texture, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making it a valuable plant for both ecological and culinary purposes.
