Rubus flexuosus f. ramosa

Rubus flexuosus f. ramosa, commonly known as the branched blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and its ability to form thickets. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it is noted for its vigorous growth and adaptability to various soil types.
