Rhamnus crenata var. discolor

Rhamnus crenata var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive variegation pattern, often displaying shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
