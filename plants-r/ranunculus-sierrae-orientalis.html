Ranunculus sierrae-orientalis

Ranunculus sierrae-orientalis, commonly known as the Sierra Oriental buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Turkey and northern Syria.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically has palmate leaves with deep lobes and produces small, yellowish to orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
