Rubus radula var. exilis

Rubus radula var. exilis, commonly known as the slender trailing raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thin, trailing stems and small, serrated leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red when ripe, often clustered in groups of several berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its delicate structure, it is often found growing along the ground in dense vegetation.
