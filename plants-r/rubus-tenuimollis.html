Rubus tenuimollis

Rubus tenuimollis, commonly known as the slender-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thin, leathery leaves and small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces a trailing or shrub-like growth habit.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
