Ranunculus thurgoviae

Ranunculus thurgoviae, commonly known as the Thurgovian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers usually have five to eight sepals that resemble petals.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
