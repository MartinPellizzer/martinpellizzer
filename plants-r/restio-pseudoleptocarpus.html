Restio pseudoleptocarpus

Restio pseudoleptocarpus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved restio, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny white to pale yellow flowers. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in coastal or mountainous regions.

Restio pseudoleptocarpus is often found in fynbos vegetation and is adapted to withstand drought and fire.
