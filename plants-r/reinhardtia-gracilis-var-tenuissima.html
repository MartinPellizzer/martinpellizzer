Reinhardtia gracilis var. tenuissima

Reinhardtia gracilis var. tenuissima, commonly known as the slender dwarf bamboo, is a rare and delicate species of bamboo native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that give it a refined appearance. This variety is typically found in shaded, moist forest understories where it thrives in humid, nutrient-rich soils. Its growth habit is clumping, forming small, compact clusters rather than spreading extensively.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
