Restio aridus

Restio aridus, commonly known as the desert restia, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to low rainfall conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and tough, reducing water loss in harsh environments.

Restio aridus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
