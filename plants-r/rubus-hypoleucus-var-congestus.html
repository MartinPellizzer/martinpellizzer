Rubus hypoleucus var. congestus

Rubus hypoleucus var. congestus, commonly known as the densefruit blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant produces clusters of fruit that are typically smaller than those of other blackberry varieties. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The botanical features of this variety include its compound leaves and its ability to form shrubs that can reach up to several feet in height.
