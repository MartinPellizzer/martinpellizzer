Rosa × pycnophylla

Rosa × pycnophylla, commonly known as the everblooming rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to produce flowers throughout the growing season. The plant features dark green, glossy leaves and produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its hardiness and continuous blooming nature.

Rosa × pycnophylla is often used in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
