Ritterocereus

Ritterocereus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a genus of cacti native to South America.

These plants are characterized by their spiny stems and nocturnal flowering habits, with large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. They are typically found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Ritterocereus species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.

The genus includes several species, such as Ritterocereus tetragonus, which is known for its striking appearance and rare blooms.
