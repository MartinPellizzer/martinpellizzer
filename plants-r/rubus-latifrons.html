Rubus latifrons

Rubus latifrons, commonly known as the broad-fruited blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and open areas, and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The berries are not only edible but also serve as a food source for various wildlife species.
