Rhodopentas bussei

Rhodopentas bussei, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
