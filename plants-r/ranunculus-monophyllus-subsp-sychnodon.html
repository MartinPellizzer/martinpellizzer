Ranunculus monophyllus subsp. sychnodon

Ranunculus monophyllus subsp.

sychnodon, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features a short, rhizomatous rootstock and produces basal leaves that are deeply lobed and sometimes hairy.

The plant bears small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and garden cultivation.
