Rubus darrisii

Rubus darrisii, commonly known as the Darrisii blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
