Rosa hurstiana

Rosa hurstiana, commonly known as the Hirst's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of many rose species.

Rosa hurstiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
