Rubus subvestitus

Rubus subvestitus, commonly known as the velvet raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streamside areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
