Rosa umbelliflora var. cuspidatoides

Rosa umbelliflora var. cuspidatoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sweetbriar, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in late spring. Its growth habit is generally upright, with thorny stems that provide structural support.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
