Rhododendron jingangshanicum

Rhododendron jingangshanicum, commonly known as the Jingangshan rhododendron, is a species native to China, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Hubei Province.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils under shaded forest conditions. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming in late spring. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and have a distinctive elliptical shape.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
