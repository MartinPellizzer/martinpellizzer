Rhamnus roylei

Rhamnus roylei, commonly known as the Himalayan buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It typically grows in temperate to cold climates, often found in forested areas and along stream banks. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring. Its fruits are small, black berries that ripen in late summer and are an important food source for birds and mammals.

Rhamnus roylei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
