Rubus radula var. hystrix

Rubus radula var. hystrix, commonly known as the hooked bramble, is a variant of the common bramble found in temperate regions.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, producing small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticulture.
