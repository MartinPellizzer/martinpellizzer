Rudolphia volubilis

Rudolphia volubilis, commonly known as the climbing rudolphia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant is known for its vigorous climbing habit, often twining around supports such as trees or shrubs. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green in color.

Rudolphia volubilis is often found in humid, shaded environments within rainforests.
