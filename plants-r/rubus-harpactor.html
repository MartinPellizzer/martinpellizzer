Rubus harpactor

Rubus harpactor, commonly known as the blackberry or raspberry plant, is a species of bramble within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically red or black when ripe, containing small, hard seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Rubus harpactor is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
