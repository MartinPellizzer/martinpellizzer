Rumohra lokohensis

Rumohra lokohensis, commonly known as the Lokohense grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its erect, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.

Due to its resilience, it thrives in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.
