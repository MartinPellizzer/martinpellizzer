Ranunculus cangshanicus

Ranunculus cangshanicus, commonly known as the Cangshan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Cangshan Mountains in Yunnan Province, China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy and often toxic species. The plant features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and produces bright yellow or white flowers with prominent stamens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
