Racosperma eremaeum

Racosperma eremaeum, commonly known as the spinifex wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its wiry, spiny stems and small, yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in compact inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and salt resistance.

Racosperma eremaeum plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
