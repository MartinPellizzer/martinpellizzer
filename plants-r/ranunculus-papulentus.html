Ranunculus papulentus

Ranunculus papulentus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals and a hairy stem that distinguishes it from other ranunculus species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with lobed segments that are often covered in fine hairs.

The hairy buttercup is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
