Rubia peregrina var. genuina

Rubia peregrina var. genuina, commonly known as the genuine wild madder, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its roots are rich in anthraquinones and have been historically used in dyeing textiles and as a medicinal herb.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.
