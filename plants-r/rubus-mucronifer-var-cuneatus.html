Rubus mucronifer var. cuneatus

Rubus mucronifer var. cuneatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cuneate (wedge-shaped) leaves and small, dark purple to black fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils. Its thorny stems and aggregate fruits are typical of the Rubus genus.

This variety is often found in the understory of deciduous forests, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
