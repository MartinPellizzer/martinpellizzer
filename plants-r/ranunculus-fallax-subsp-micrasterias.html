Ranunculus fallax subsp. micrasterias

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

micrasterias, commonly known as the small-leaf water crowfoot, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, streams, and wet meadows.

The plant features small, delicate leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a distinctive leaf shape that is deeply lobed. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in still or slow-moving water, contributing to the ecological balance of its aquatic environment.
