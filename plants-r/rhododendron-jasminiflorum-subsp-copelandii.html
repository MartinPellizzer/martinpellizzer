Rhododendron jasminiflorum subsp. copelandii

Rhododendron jasminiflorum subsp.

copelandii, commonly known as the Copeland's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers.

The flowers are typically pink to white in color and bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
