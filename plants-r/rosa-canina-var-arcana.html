Rosa canina var. arcana

Rosa canina var. arcana, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose.

It is a shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Central Asia. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the fruits are red, hairy hips that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
