Rosularia blepharophylla

Rosularia blepharophylla, commonly known as the "Earleaf Rosularia," is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit, with fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, hairy stalks. It is well adapted to hot, dry environments and requires minimal water once established.

Rosularia blepharophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
