Rhizophora americana

Rhizophora americana, commonly known as the American mangrove, is a tropical tree species native to the coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive pneumatophores, which are specialized root structures that extend above the soil to facilitate gas exchange in waterlogged environments. The plant thrives in saline and brackish water habitats, playing a crucial role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems. Its prop roots provide structural support and help prevent erosion along shorelines.

Additionally, Rhizophora americana supports a diverse array of marine and terrestrial life, making it an ecologically significant species.
