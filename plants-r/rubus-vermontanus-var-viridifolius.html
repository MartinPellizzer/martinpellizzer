Rubus vermontanus var. viridifolius

Rubus vermontanus var. viridifolius, commonly known as the green-leaved blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its distinctive dark green foliage, which sets it apart from other Rubus species. It produces small, dark purple berries that are typically less flavorful compared to other blackberry varieties. The plant is native to the northeastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
