Rosa gallica var. inaperta

Rosa gallica var. inaperta, commonly known as the Gallic rose or the old garden rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its fragrant, deep pink to crimson flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It is valued for its historical significance in horticulture and its use in perfumery due to its strong floral scent.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental beauty and historical charm.
