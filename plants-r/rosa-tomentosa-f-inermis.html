Rosa tomentosa f. inermis

Rosa tomentosa f. inermis, commonly known as the soft-furred rose, is a flowering plant characterized by its soft, grayish-white pubescence on its stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan. The plant produces fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

While it is not as commonly cultivated as other rose species, it is valued for its unique texture and subtle fragrance.
