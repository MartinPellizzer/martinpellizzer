Rosa × subrepens

Rosa × subrepens, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub that typically reaches heights of 10–30 cm. The plant features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of woodlands or along stream banks.
