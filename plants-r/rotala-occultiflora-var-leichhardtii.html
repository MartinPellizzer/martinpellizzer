Rotala occultiflora var. leichhardtii

Rotala occultiflora var. leichhardtii, commonly known as the Leichhardt water plant, is a compact aquatic plant native to Australia.

It features finely divided, dark green leaves that are typically submerged, with occasional floating leaves on the surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are often submerged, contributing to its classification as a submerged aquatic species. It thrives in slow-moving to still freshwater environments with moderate to high light exposure.

This variety is popular in aquariums for its dense growth and ability to provide shelter for aquatic life.
