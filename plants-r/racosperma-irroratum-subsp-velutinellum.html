Racosperma irroratum subsp. velutinellum

Racosperma irroratum subsp.

velutinellum, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its soft, silky grey-green foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
