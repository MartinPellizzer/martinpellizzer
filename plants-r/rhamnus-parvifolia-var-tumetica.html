Rhamnus parvifolia var. tumetica

Rhamnus parvifolia var. tumetica, commonly known as the smoothleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, dark berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands or rocky areas.

The species is noted for its adaptability and resilience in arid environments.
