Ribes austroecuadorense

Ribes austroecuadorense, commonly known as the Ecuadorian gooseberry, is a species native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in cloud forests or montane areas. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed raw or used in local cuisine. Botanically, it belongs to the genus Ribes within the family Grossulariaceae.

Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.
