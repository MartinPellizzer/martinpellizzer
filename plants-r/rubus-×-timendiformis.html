Rubus × timendiformis

Rubus × timendiformis, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (cloudberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in temperate regions, often in forested areas or along streambanks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential for cultivation in horticultural settings.
