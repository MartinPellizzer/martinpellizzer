Rumex vesceritensis

Rumex vesceritensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and it produces clusters of greenish-yellow flowers in early summer. Its roots are fibrous and can be harvested for medicinal purposes, though it is not widely cultivated.

The species is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
