Ranunculus bullatus var. cytheraeus

Ranunculus bullatus var. cytheraeus, commonly known as the Cytheraeus buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and is known for its delicate, herbaceous appearance. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many species with similar floral structures.

The varietal name 'cytheraeus' refers to its association with Cythera, a mythical island, highlighting its historical and ornamental significance.
