Rosa eglanteria lutea

Rosa eglanteria lutea, commonly known as the yellow prickly rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a fuzzy, red-pink rose hip that ripens in autumn.

Rosa eglanteria lutea is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
