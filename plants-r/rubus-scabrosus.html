Rubus scabrosus

Rubus scabrosus, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

Rubus scabrosus is often found in forested areas and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
