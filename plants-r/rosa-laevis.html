Rosa laevis

Rosa laevis, commonly known as the smooth rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its simple, smooth-stemmed growth and fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven oval-shaped leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

Rosa laevis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
