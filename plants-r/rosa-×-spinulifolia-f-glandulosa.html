Rosa × spinulifolia f. glandulosa

Rosa × spinulifolia f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular spinulifolia rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It exhibits a compact growth habit with thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with glandular dots on the undersides, giving it its specific epithet "glandulosa.". Its flowers are usually pink to red, with a rich fragrance, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
