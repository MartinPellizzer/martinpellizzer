Rhododendron × azaleoides inodorum

Rhododendron × azaleoides inodorum, commonly known as the sweet azalee, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It prefers partial to full shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its foliage is dense and evergreen, providing year-round visual interest.
