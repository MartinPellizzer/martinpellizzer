Rosa canina var. bakeri

Rosa canina var. bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's hip or the hairy rose, is a variant of the dog rose.

It is a shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces pink to red flowers in the spring, followed by distinctive orange or red hips in the autumn. It is adapted to well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
