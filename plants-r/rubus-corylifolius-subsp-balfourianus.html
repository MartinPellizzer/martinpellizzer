Rubus corylifolius subsp. balfourianus

Rubus corylifolius subsp.

balfourianus, commonly known as the Balfourian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
