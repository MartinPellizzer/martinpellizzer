Ranunculus calabrus

Ranunculus calabrus, commonly known as the Calabrian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a contrasting central blotch. The plant has palmately divided leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
