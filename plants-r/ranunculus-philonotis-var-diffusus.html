Ranunculus philonotis var. diffusus

Ranunculus philonotis var. diffusus, commonly known as the spreading meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with lobed segments, and it grows in moist meadows and woodland edges. It is characterized by its spreading growth habit, with multiple stems arising from a central rootstock.

This variety is often found in damp, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
