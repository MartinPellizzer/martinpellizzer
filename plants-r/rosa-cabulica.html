Rosa cabulica

Rosa cabulica, commonly known as the Kabul rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Rosa cabulica is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
