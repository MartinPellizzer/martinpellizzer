Rapanea vestita

Rapanea vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf or white-leafed evergreen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Rapanea vestita is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions for medicinal purposes.
