Rosa spinosissima var. spinosa

Rosa spinosissima var. spinosa, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include a shrub-like growth habit and a preference for well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
