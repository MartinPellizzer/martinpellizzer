Rubus caudaticalyx

Rubus caudaticalyx, commonly known as the long-stemmed blackberry, is a species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to certain regions of Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
