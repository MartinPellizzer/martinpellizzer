Rubus × segnis

Rubus × segnis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically smaller than those of other Rubus species. It is native to the western United States, particularly California, where it thrives in coastal and chaparral habitats.

The leaves are trifoliate, with a leathery texture and a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
