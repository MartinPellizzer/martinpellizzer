Rubus schleicheri var. longicuspis

Rubus schleicheri var. longicuspis, commonly known as the long-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The variety is distinguished by its elongated, pointed fruiting structures, which are a key feature in its classification.
