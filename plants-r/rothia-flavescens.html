Rothia flavescens

Rothia flavescens, commonly known as the yellow Rothia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically a deep green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Rothia flavescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its compact growth habit and seasonal floral display.
