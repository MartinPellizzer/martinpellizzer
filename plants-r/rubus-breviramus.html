Rubus breviramus

Rubus breviramus, commonly known as the short-stemmed blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinator populations.
