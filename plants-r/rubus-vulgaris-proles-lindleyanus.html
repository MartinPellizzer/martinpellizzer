Rubus vulgaris proles lindleyanus

Rubus vulgaris proles lindleyanus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of small drupelets. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and economically valuable.
