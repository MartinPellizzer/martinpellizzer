Rhododendron apiculatum

Rhododendron apiculatum, commonly known as the alpine rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. The plant features dense, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. It is well adapted to rocky and mountainous terrain, often thriving in well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
