Rosa istrica

Rosa istrica, commonly known as the Istrian rose, is a species native to the Adriatic region, particularly Croatia and Italy.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central pistil.

Rosa istrica is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
