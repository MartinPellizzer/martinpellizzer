Rosa villosa var. cardanica

Rosa villosa var. cardanica, commonly known as the cardanica rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features woody stems covered in dense, white to greyish pubescence, giving it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval, serrated leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
