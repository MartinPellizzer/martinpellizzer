Relbunium buxifolium f. microphylla

Relbunium buxifolium f. microphylla, commonly known as the miniature boxleaf, is a compact, low-growing plant with dense, leathery foliage.

Its leaves are small, ovate, and glossy, resembling those of boxwood, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
