Rubus suberectus var. viridis

Rubus suberectus var. viridis, commonly known as the green-leafed raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It is native to certain regions of Asia and is known for its upright growth habit. The leaves are typically green and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are often used in culinary applications.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
