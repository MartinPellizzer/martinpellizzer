Ranunculus limoselloides var. mandonianus

Ranunculus limoselloides var. mandonianus, commonly known as the Mandon's water buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often in wet meadows or along stream banks. It has finely divided, trifoliate leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly smaller size and more compact growth compared to other ranunculus species.
