Rubus acaulis

Rubus acaulis, commonly known as the alpine bramble, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that are typically red or black in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

Rubus acaulis is adapted to cold climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
