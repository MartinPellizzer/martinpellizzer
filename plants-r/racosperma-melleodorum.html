Racosperma melleodorum

Racosperma melleodorum, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular species in native gardens.
