Rhus spinescens

Rhus spinescens, commonly known as the prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes plants like poison ivy and mango. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by red, fuzzy berries that attract birds.

Rhus spinescens is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
