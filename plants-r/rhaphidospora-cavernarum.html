Rhaphidospora cavernarum

Rhaphidospora cavernarum, commonly known as the cave lichen, is a unique species of lichen that thrives in subterranean environments.

It is characterized by its intricate network of fungal hyphae and algal symbionts, which together form a complex composite organism. This plant exhibits a slow growth rate and is often found in dark, humid caves with stable temperatures. Its structure is adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding rock surfaces.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is a rare and sensitive indicator of environmental conditions in cave ecosystems.
