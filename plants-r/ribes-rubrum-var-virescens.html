Ribes rubrum var. virescens

Ribes rubrum var. virescens, commonly known as the red currant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, bright red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in jams, jellies, and culinary applications.
