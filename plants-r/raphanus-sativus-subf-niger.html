Raphanus sativus subf. niger

Raphanus sativus subf.

niger, commonly known as black radish, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its dark purple to black root, which is typically elongated and cylindrical in shape.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are pinnately lobed with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its edible root, which has a spicy, peppery flavor.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
