Rubus vallis-cembrae

Rubus vallis-cembrae, commonly known as the alpine bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by edible red berries. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cold climates with well-drained soils.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
