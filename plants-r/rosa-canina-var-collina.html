Rosa canina var. collina

Rosa canina var. collina, commonly known as the creeping dog rose, is a shrub-like variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

Rosa canina var. collina is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to coastal or inland areas with well-drained conditions.
