Randia lanceolata

Randia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and often have a yellow or white color.

Randia lanceolata is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
