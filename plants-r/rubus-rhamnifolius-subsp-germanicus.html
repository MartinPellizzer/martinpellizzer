Rubus rhamnifolius subsp. germanicus

Rubus rhamnifolius subsp.

germanicus, commonly known as the German blackberry, is a shrub native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This subspecies is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed areas.
