Rosa strobiloides

Rosa strobiloides, commonly known as the rose of Jericho, is a unique desert plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a succulent shrub that can survive extreme drought conditions by entering a dormant state, curling its leaves into a tight ball. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its ability to revive when watered makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Despite its resilience, it requires careful care to thrive in non-native environments.
