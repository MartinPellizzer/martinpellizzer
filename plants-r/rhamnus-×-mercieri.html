Rhamnus × mercieri

Rhamnus × mercieri, commonly known as the hybrid common buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the family Rhamnaceae.

It is a natural hybrid between Rhamnus cathartica (common buckthorn) and Rhamnus alnifolia (European buckthorn). The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by dark blue berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Rhamnus × mercieri is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, where it can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
