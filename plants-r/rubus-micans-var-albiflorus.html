Rubus micans var. albiflorus

Rubus micans var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its pale white flowers that stand out against its dark green foliage. The plant is typically found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and its stems are thorny.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
