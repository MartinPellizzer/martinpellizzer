Rhynchospora pruinosa

Rhynchospora pruinosa, commonly known as the pruinose flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a distinctive waxy coating on its leaves, giving it a silvery appearance, which helps reduce water loss in moist environments. It thrives in shallow water or saturated soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Rhynchospora pruinosa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
