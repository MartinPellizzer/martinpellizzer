Racosperma octonervium

Racosperma octonervium, commonly known as the eight-veined acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with eight prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the acacia genus.

Racosperma octonervium is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
