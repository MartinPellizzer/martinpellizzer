Rosa gallica var. armatissima

Rosa gallica var. armatissima, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a hedging plant.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
