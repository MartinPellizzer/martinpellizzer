Rutidea albiflora

Rutidea albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered legume, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, white flowers. Its root system helps in improving soil fertility by forming symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Rutidea albiflora is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
