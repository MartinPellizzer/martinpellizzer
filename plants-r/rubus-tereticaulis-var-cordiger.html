Rubus tereticaulis var. cordiger

Rubus tereticaulis var. cordiger, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, thornless stems. The plant produces clusters of small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with oval-shaped leaflets and serrated edges.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
