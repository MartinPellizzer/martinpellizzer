Racosperma shuttleworthii

Racosperma shuttleworthii, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers in racemes. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
