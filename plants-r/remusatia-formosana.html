Remusatia formosana

Remusatia formosana, commonly known as the Formosan arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used as a starch source.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
