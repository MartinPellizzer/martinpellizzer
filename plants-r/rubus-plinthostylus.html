Rubus plinthostylus

Rubus plinthostylus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bramble.

It is a shrub native to New Zealand, characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible red berries in summer. Its foliage is typically narrow and serrated, with a dense growth habit that provides habitat for native wildlife.

The species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and forested areas.
