Rubus parvirosifolius

Rubus parvirosifolius, commonly known as the small-leaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves with finely serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by red, hairy berries that ripen in the summer. It is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
