Rubus naumannii

Rubus naumannii, commonly known as the Naumann's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

The berries are edible and have been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
