Rubus semiglaber

Rubus semiglaber, commonly known as the smooth-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus semiglaber is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.
