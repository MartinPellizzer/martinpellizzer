Racinaea rothschuhiana

Racinaea rothschuhiana, commonly known as the Rothschild's racinaea, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and pink. The plant has strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure and ornamental value.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
