Raphanus sativus var. longipinnatus

Raphanus sativus var. longipinnatus, commonly known as the bird's beak radish, is a cultivated variety of the radish plant characterized by its elongated, narrow root that resembles a bird's beak.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and broccoli. The plant typically has a robust taproot with a distinctive reddish or purplish coloration, and its leaves are lobed and edible. It thrives in cool climates and is often grown for its unique root morphology and mild, slightly spicy flavor.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
