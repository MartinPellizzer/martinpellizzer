Rubus menkei subsp. dissociatus

Rubus menkei subsp.

dissociatus, commonly known as the dissociated blackberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in parts of China and Vietnam. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit, which is a cluster of small drupes, is edible and has a flavor similar to other blackberries.
