Racosperma tetragonophyllum

Racosperma tetragonophyllum, commonly known as the four-angled acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive four-angled stems. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences, which attract a variety of pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Racosperma tetragonophyllum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
