Rubus × cardiophylloides

Rubus × cardiophylloides, commonly known as the heartleaf blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus parvifolius.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are a notable botanical feature. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its genetic diversity and adaptability, making it a subject of interest in horticultural research.
