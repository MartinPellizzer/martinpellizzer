Rhamnus wichellii

Rhamnus wichellii, commonly known as the African buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by black, spherical berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and shrubland habitats.

Rhamnus wichellii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
