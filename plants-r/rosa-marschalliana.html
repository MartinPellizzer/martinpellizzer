Rosa marschalliana

Rosa marschalliana, commonly known as the Marshall rose, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Russia. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.
