Rosa pomifera subsp. personata

Rosa pomifera subsp.

personata, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a species native to China. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can persist on the plant for several months. The plant produces edible, fleshy fruits called rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
