Rubus × falsiferox

Rubus × falsiferox, commonly known as the false blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many brambles. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are generally red or black in color. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in woodland and disturbed habitats.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations.
