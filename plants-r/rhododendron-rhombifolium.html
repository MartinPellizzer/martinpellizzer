Rhododendron rhombifolium

Rhododendron rhombifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, rhombic-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and are borne in the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
