Rumex langloisii

Rumex langloisii, commonly known as the Langlois sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, arrow-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is part of the Polygonaceae family, known for its unique leaf structures and edible leaves in some species.
