Russelia rotundifolia

Russelia rotundifolia, commonly known as the red-hot catwhiskers, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Russelia rotundifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance and long blooming season.
