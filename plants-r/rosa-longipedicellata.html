Rosa longipedicellata

Rosa longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-stemmed rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces small, red-orange fruits known as hips.

Rosa longipedicellata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
