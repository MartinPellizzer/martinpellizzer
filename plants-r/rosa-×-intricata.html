Rosa × intricata

Rosa × intricata, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless stems and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets that provide a lush green appearance.

Rosa × intricata is native to China and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
