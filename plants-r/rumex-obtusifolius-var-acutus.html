Rumex obtusifolius var. acutus

Rumex obtusifolius var. acutus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear alternate, lanceolate leaves with a waxy surface and serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wet meadows.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some cultures.
