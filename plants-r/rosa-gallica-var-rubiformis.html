Rosa gallica var. rubiformis

Rosa gallica var. rubiformis, commonly known as the French rose or the Gallic rose, is a variety of the shrub rose native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dark red to almost black flowers, which bloom in clusters during late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and glossy, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is also valued for its historical use in perfumery and as a source of rose hip fruit.
