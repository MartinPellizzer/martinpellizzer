Rubus praepes

Rubus praepes, commonly known as the redcurrant bush, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in temperate climates. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in jams and preserves. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems that provide structural support.

The flowers are white or pink, appearing in clusters and attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
