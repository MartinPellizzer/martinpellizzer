Rosa pimpinellifolia var. macropoda

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. macropoda, commonly known as the large-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white, typically in spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
