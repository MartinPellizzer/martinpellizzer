Roentgenia bracteomana

Roentgenia bracteomana, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with upright stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Roentgenia bracteomana prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
