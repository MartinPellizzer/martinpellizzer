Rhynchospora arechavaletae

Rhynchospora arechavaletae, commonly known as the Arechavaleta sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, it thrives in wetland or marshy environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense stands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
