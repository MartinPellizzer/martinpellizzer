Racosperma hamersleyense

Racosperma hamersleyense, commonly known as the Hamersley wattle, is a shrub native to the Pilbara region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow floral spikes that appear during the winter months. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, linear leaflets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions.

The species is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
