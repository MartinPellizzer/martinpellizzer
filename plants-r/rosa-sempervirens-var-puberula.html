Rosa sempervirens var. puberula

Rosa sempervirens var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy evergreen rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with leathery, dark green leaves that have a fine pubescence. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for hedging and container planting.
