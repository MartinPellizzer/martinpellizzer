Ruellia albopurpurea var. albopurpurea

Ruellia albopurpurea var. albopurpurea, commonly known as the white-purple ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces clusters of tubular, white to purplish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
