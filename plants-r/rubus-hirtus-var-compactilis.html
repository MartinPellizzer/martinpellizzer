Rubus hirtus var. compactilis

Rubus hirtus var. compactilis, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a red color. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its berries.
