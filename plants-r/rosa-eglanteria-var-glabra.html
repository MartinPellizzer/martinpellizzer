Rosa eglanteria var. glabra

Rosa eglanteria var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth shrub rose, is a species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and a glossy green surface.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in gardens.
