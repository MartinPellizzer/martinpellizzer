Rhododendron meddianum var. meddianum

Rhododendron meddianum var. meddianum, commonly known as the Meddian rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes where its hardy nature and visual impact are appreciated.
