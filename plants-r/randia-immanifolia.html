Randia immanifolia

Randia immanifolia, commonly known as the swamp beauty, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often trailing stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically borne in clusters. Its leaves are typically opposite, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Randia immanifolia is adapted to moist or aquatic environments, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.
