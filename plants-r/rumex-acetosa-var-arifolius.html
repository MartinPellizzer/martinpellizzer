Rumex acetosa var. arifolius

Rumex acetosa var. arifolius, commonly known as sourgrass or narrow-leaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, wastelands, and along roadsides.

The species is characterized by its strong, sour-tasting leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
