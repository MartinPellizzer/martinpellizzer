Rhododendron dumicola

Rhododendron dumicola, commonly known as the rock rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers cool, shaded environments. It is often found growing on rocky slopes and in forested areas at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
