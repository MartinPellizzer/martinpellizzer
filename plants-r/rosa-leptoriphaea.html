Rosa leptoriphaea

Rosa leptoriphaea, commonly known as the slender-stemmed rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are typically red and hairy.

Rosa leptoriphaea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
