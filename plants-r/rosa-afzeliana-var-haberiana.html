Rosa afzeliana var. haberiana

Rosa afzeliana var. haberiana, commonly known as the Haberian rose, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam and Indonesia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces small, red or orange hips that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
