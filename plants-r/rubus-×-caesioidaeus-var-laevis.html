Rubus × caesioidaeus var. laevis

Rubus × caesioidaeus var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and large, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces showy white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and ornamental appeal in gardens.
