Rhododendron praeteritum

Rhododendron praeteritum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Rhododendron praeteritum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
