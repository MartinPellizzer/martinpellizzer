Rosa × variegata

Rosa × variegata, commonly known as the variegated rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa damascena and Rosa centifolia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features a mix of green, white, and sometimes gold or pink hues. The plant produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, or red, often with a high number of petals. It is typically a shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with thorny stems and oval-shaped leaves.

Rosa × variegata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
