Racosperma apricum

Racosperma apricum, commonly known as the apricot wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Racosperma apricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting local wildlife.
