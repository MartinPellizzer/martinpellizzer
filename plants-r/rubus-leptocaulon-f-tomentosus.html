Rubus leptocaulon f. tomentosus

Rubus leptocaulon f. tomentosus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its dense, woolly pubescence.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Asia, particularly China. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. Its foliage is often covered in a fine layer of grayish-white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This form is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and edible fruits.
