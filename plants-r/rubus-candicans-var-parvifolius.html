Rubus candicans var. parvifolius

Rubus candicans var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaf raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has trifoliate leaves with small, rounded lobes. Its flowers are white to pink, and the fruits are red, hairy berries that ripen in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
