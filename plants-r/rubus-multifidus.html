Rubus multifidus

Rubus multifidus, commonly known as the multifid raspberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with multiple leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberries and blackberries.
