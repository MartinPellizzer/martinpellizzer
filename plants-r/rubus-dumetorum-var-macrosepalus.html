Rubus dumetorum var. macrosepalus

Rubus dumetorum var. macrosepalus, commonly known as the large-sepaled blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry. This variety is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
