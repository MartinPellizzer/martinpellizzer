Rubus barbeyi f. contiguus

Rubus barbeyi f. contiguus, commonly known as the contiguous blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and firm. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
