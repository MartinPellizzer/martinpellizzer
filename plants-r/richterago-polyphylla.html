Richterago polyphylla

Richterago polyphylla, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.

Richterago polyphylla is also noted for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
