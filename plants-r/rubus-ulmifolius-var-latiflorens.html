Rubus ulmifolius var. latiflorens

Rubus ulmifolius var. latiflorens, commonly known as the broadflower blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically lobed and have a serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by dark purple to black berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
