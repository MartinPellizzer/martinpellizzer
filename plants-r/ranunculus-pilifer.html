Ranunculus pilifer

Ranunculus pilifer, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with prominent, often hairy sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine, hairy glands.

The hairy buttercup is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
