Rhododendron crispiflorum

Rhododendron crispiflorum, commonly known as the crested rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are leathery and often have a distinctive wavy margin, while the inflorescence is a compact cluster of flowers.

Rhododendron crispiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
