Rhododendron album var. purpurescens

Rhododendron album var. purpurescens, commonly known as the purple-flowered white rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from white to deep purple, depending on the variety and environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blossoms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
