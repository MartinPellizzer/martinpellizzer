Rubus impatiens

Rubus impatiens, commonly known as the flame flower or wild strawberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the growing season, followed by red, edible fruits known as berries. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in moist, acidic soils.

Rubus impatiens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and as a ground cover.
