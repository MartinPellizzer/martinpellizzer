Rhododendron heliolepis var. fumidum

Rhododendron heliolepis var. fumidum, commonly known as the smoky rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that often have a bluish or purplish hue. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
