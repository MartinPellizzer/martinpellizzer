Ruellia dolichosiphon

Ruellia dolichosiphon, commonly known as the long-beak ruellia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Ruellia dolichosiphon thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, drought-tolerant plants.
