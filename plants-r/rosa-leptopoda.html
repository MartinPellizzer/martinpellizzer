Rosa leptopoda

Rosa leptopoda, commonly known as the slender rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often armed with thorns. Rosa leptopoda is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.
