Rubus villicaulis var. langei

Rubus villicaulis var. langei, commonly known as the Lange's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect or semi-prostrate growth habit and produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal fruit production.

This variant is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
