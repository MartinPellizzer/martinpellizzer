Ranunculus montanus var. genuinus

Ranunculus montanus var. genuinus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features showy yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are alternate along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptation to high-altitude environments and its role in local ecosystems.
