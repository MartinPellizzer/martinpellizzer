Rubus orthocanthus

Rubus orthocanthus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The berries are edible but are generally less flavorful compared to other blackberry species.
