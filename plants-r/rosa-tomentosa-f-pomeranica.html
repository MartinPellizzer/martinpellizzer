Rosa tomentosa f. pomeranica

Rosa tomentosa f. pomeranica, commonly known as the Pomeranian rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to Europe.

It features dense, thorny stems covered in a white, woolly bloom of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are dark green, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are typically pale pink to white, with five petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
