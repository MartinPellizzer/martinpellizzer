Rhododendron hepaticum

Rhododendron hepaticum, commonly known as the liverleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, woolly hair. Its flowers are small, clustered in trusses, and typically range in color from white to pink.

Rhododendron hepaticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal blooms.
