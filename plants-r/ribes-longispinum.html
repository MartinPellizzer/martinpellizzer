Ribes longispinum

Ribes longispinum, commonly known as the long-spined gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically pink to white, appearing in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Ribes longispinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
