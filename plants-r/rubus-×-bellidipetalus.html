Rubus × bellidipetalus

Rubus × bellidipetalus, commonly known as the hybrid raspberry or blackberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (the cloudberry). The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color, with a sweet-tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and covered in fine hairs.

This species is often found in wet, acidic soils in temperate regions and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
