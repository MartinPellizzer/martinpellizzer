Rubus jugosus

Rubus jugosus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Rubus jugosus is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
