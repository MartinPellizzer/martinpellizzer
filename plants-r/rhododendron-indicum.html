Rhododendron indicum

Rhododendron indicum, commonly known as the Indian rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments.

Rhododendron indicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
