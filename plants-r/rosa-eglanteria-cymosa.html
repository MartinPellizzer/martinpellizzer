Rosa eglanteria cymosa

Rosa eglanteria cymosa, commonly known as the Cymosa rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This species is native to regions of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
