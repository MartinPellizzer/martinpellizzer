Ranunculus meyeri

Ranunculus meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring a basal rosette of large, lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often accompanied by numerous sepals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, preferring cool, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
