Rudbeckia floridana var. angustifolia

Rudbeckia floridana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear daisy-like yellow-orange flower heads with dark brown central disks. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
