Rubus ulmifolius chlorocarpus

Rubus ulmifolius chlorocarpus, commonly known as the blackberry or bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often growing in woodland edges and disturbed soils. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the berries ripen to a deep purple or black color.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
