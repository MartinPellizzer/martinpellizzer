Ranunculus orthorhynchus var. typicus

Ranunculus orthorhynchus var. typicus, commonly known as the typical spurred buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided with a hairy surface, and it produces small, greenish fruits that contain multiple seeds.

It is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates.
