Rubus senticosus subsp. pseudoradula

Rubus senticosus subsp.

pseudoradula, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a shrub native to western North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The species is valued for its edible fruits and has been used in traditional medicine by Indigenous peoples.
