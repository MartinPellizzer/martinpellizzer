Rubus lutescens var. fruticosus

Rubus lutescens var. fruticosus, commonly known as the yellow bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense growth habit, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
