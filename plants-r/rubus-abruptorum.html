Rubus abruptorum

Rubus abruptorum, commonly known as the abrupt mountain raspberry.

It is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showing adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pale yellow flowers in the spring.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential for horticultural use.
