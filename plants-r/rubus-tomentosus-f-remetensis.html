Rubus tomentosus f. remetensis

Rubus tomentosus f. remetensis, commonly known as the Remetensian bramble, is a flowering plant species native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and thorny stems. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, edible berries. Its habitat typically includes moist, shaded woodlands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
