Rosa communis var. brevipes

Rosa communis var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed rose, is a compact shrub native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves that provide a dense, bushy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in garden settings.
