Rosa glauca f. subargutula

Rosa glauca f. subargutula, commonly known as the bluebush rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa glauca species.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and silvery-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers, typically in clusters, during the late spring to early summer. It is native to the western United States and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to dry conditions.
