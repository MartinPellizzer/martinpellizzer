Rhynchospora crinipes

Rhynchospora crinipes, commonly known as the featherleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in the Americas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, arranged in spikelets. Rhynchospora crinipes is often found in shallow water or saturated soils, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.

Its presence indicates healthy wetland environments and contributes to biodiversity in these habitats.
