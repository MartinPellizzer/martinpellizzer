Rhododendron inaequale

Rhododendron inaequale, commonly known as the unequal rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often adorned with a distinctive purple or red glandular marking.

Rhododendron inaequale is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
