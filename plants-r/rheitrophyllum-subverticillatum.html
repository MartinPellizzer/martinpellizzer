Rheitrophyllum subverticillatum

Rheitrophyllum subverticillatum, commonly known as the clustered feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clusters. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in loose, feathery inflorescences.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
