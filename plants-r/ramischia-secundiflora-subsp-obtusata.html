Ramischia secundiflora subsp. obtusata

Ramischia secundiflora subsp.

obtusata, commonly known as the blunt-leaved desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, adapted to thrive in low-nutrient environments.

The plant features basal leaves that are broad and rounded, with a bluish-green hue, and produces small, yellow flowers in late spring. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to access groundwater, making it drought-tolerant.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-dispersed seeds.
