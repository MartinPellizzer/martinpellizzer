Ranunculus ornatus

Ranunculus ornatus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has palmate, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Ranunculus ornatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant and showy blooms.
