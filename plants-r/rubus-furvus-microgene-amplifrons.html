Rubus furvus microgene amplifrons

Rubus furvus microgene amplifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble characterized by its small, dark purple berries and trifoliate leaves.

It thrives in temperate regions, often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. The plant produces thorny stems and has a complex root system that helps stabilize the soil. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it is known for its edible fruits, though they are often tart and less commonly consumed than other blackberry varieties.

This species plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and pollinators.
