Rosa accurrens subsp. semisyringifolia

Rosa accurrens subsp.

semisyringifolia, commonly known as the semi-syringifolous rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and has compound leaves with fewer leaflets compared to other rose species.

The flowers are typically pink to red in color, and they bloom in late spring to early summer. This plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at higher elevations.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
