Rabdosia inflexa

Rabdosia inflexa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, opposite leaves. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Rabdosia inflexa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
