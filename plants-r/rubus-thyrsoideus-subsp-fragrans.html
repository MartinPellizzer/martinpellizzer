Rubus thyrsoideus subsp. fragrans

Rubus thyrsoideus subsp.

fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and are often hairy.

The subspecies is distinguished by its strong, sweet fragrance, which is most pronounced in the flowers.
