Rhododendron konori var. konori

Rhododendron konori var. konori, commonly known as the Konori rhododendron, is a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

The leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.
