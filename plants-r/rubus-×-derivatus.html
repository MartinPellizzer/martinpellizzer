Rubus × derivatus

Rubus × derivatus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the raspberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (the cloudberry).

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically a mix of red and black in color. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and grows in moist, temperate climates. It is valued for its edible fruits, which have a tart flavor and are used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.

The species is also of interest in horticulture for its adaptability and genetic diversity.
