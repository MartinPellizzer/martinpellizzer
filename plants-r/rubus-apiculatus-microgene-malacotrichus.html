Rubus apiculatus microgene malacotrichus

Rubus apiculatus microgene malacotrichus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
