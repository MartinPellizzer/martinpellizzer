Rosa micrantha subvar. eglandulosa

Rosa micrantha subvar. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its botanical characteristics include glandular stipules and a compact growth habit.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
