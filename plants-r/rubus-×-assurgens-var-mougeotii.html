Rubus × assurgens var. mougeotii

Rubus × assurgens var. mougeotii, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both blackberries and raspberries.

It is a deciduous shrub with arching canes that produce aggregate fruits, typically dark red to black in color. The plant has compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The botanical variability of this species makes it a subject of interest in horticultural and taxonomic studies.
