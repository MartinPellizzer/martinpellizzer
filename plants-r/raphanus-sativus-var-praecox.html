Raphanus sativus var. praecox

Raphanus sativus var. praecox, commonly known as early radish, is a fast-growing member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, leafy stems and small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, round roots that are harvested before full maturity for their crisp, spicy flavor. It thrives in cool climates and is typically grown as a spring or fall crop.

Its short growing season makes it a popular choice for home gardens and commercial cultivation.
