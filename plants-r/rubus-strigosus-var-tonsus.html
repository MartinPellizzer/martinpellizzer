Rubus strigosus var. tonsus

Rubus strigosus var. tonsus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This variant is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
