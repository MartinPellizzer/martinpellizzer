Ranunculus montanus

Ranunculus montanus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are palmately divided and often covered in fine hairs.

Ranunculus montanus is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, cold environments.
