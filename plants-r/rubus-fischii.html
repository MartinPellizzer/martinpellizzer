Rubus fischii

Rubus fischii, commonly known as the Fisch's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest edges and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
