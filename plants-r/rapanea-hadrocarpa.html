Rapanea hadrocarpa

Rapanea hadrocarpa, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dense, rounded crown. The plant produces small, fragrant yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elliptical, dark blue-black fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a waxy texture with a slightly aromatic scent.

Rapanea hadrocarpa is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
