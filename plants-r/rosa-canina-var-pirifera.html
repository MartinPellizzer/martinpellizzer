Rosa canina var. pirifera

Rosa canina var. pirifera, commonly known as the Japanese rose or Japanese dog rose, is a shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are serrated and alternate, with a leathery texture.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has historical use in traditional medicine.
