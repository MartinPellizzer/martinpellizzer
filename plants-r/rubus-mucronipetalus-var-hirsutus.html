Rubus mucronipetalus var. hirsutus

Rubus mucronipetalus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy trailing blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
