Rubus tenellus

Rubus tenellus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in woodland and hedgerow habitats, particularly in the eastern United States. The plant produces small, edible blackberries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated margin, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its fruit.
