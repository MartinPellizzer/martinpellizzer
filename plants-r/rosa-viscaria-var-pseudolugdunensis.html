Rosa viscaria var. pseudolugdunensis

Rosa viscaria var. pseudolugdunensis, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a compact shrub with dense, thornless stems and small, dark green leaves.

It produces clusters of fragrant, pink to reddish-pink flowers in late summer. This variety is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or alpine displays.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for cooler climates.
