Rosa × cottetii var. supertomentosa

Rosa × cottetii var. supertomentosa, commonly known as the silvery rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white pubescence that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
