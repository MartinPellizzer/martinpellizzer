Rubus × aldenoensis

Rubus × aldenoensis, commonly known as the Aldenoensis blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small to medium in size, with a sweet to tart flavor. It is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value due to its lush foliage and attractive flowers.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential antioxidant properties.
