Rubus niveus

Rubus niveus, commonly known as the white-bracted raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year producing leaves and the second year bearing flowers and fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces white to pale pink flowers that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruits, which are aggregate berries, are typically red or yellow and are edible.

Rubus niveus is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.
