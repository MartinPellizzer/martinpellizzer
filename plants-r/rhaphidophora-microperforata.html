Rhaphidophora microperforata

Rhaphidophora microperforata, commonly known as the laceleaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with intricate patterns of green, silver, and pink. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a velvety texture and a unique perforated appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
