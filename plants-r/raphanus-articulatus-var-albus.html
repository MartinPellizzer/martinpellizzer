Raphanus articulatus var. albus

Raphanus articulatus var. albus, commonly known as the white mustache radish, is a cultivated variety of the wild radish species.

It is characterized by its long, slender root that is typically white or pale yellow in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are lobed and somewhat hairy. It is often grown for its edible root, which has a mild, slightly spicy flavor.

This variety is well-suited to cool climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
