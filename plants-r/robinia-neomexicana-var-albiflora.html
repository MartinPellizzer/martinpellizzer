Robinia neomexicana var. albiflora

Robinia neomexicana var. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower false acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnately arranged. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by flat, elongated pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
