Rhododendron muscicola

Rhododendron muscicola, commonly known as the mossy rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Rhododendron muscicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
