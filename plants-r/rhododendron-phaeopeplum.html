Rhododendron phaeopeplum

Rhododendron phaeopeplum, commonly known as the dark-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. It is a deciduous shrub that can reach heights of up to 3 meters, making it a notable presence in its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
