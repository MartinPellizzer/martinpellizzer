Rapunculus caesalpini

Rapunculus caesalpini, commonly known as the Caesar's crown, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, rounded fruits called silicles.

The plant is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
