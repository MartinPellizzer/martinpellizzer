Rotala pedicellata

Rotala pedicellata, commonly known as the dwarf rotala, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically bright green and often have a red or purple hue when exposed to strong light. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate ornamental quality to aquatic environments. It is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to help control algae growth.

Rotala pedicellata thrives in shallow, still waters with moderate to high light conditions.
