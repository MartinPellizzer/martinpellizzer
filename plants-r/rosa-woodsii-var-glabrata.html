Rosa woodsii var. glabrata

Rosa woodsii var. glabrata, commonly known as the Rocky Mountain rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round structure.

The variety glabrata is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, setting it apart from other forms of Rosa woodsii.
