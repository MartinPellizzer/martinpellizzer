Rubus niveus subsp. leucocarpus

Rubus niveus subsp.

leucocarpus, commonly known as the white-fruited evergreen raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by white or pale yellow fruits. Its fruits are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.

This subspecies is adapted to cooler, humid climates and is often found in cloud forests.
