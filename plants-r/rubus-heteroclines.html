Rubus heteroclines

Rubus heteroclines, commonly known as the variable blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaries. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit, which is edible, varies in size and color depending on the specific variety and environmental conditions.
