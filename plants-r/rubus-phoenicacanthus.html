Rubus phoenicacanthus

Rubus phoenicacanthus, commonly known as the Chinese firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, orange-red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant white flowers in the spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

However, its berries are toxic to humans and animals, requiring caution in handling and consumption.
