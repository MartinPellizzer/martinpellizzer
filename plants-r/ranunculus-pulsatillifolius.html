Ranunculus pulsatillifolius

Ranunculus pulsatillifolius, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically surrounded by a ring of yellow sepals. The plant has deeply lobed, palmately arranged leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Ranunculaceae family.
