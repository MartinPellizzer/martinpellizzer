Rorippa palustris f. aquatica

Rorippa palustris f. aquatica, commonly known as water cress, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in ponds, streams, and ditches. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually submerged or floating, with small white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is known for its peppery flavor and is often used in salads or as a garnish.

The species is also valued for its nutritional content and has been historically used in traditional medicine.
