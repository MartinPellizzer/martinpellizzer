Renealmia pilosa

Renealmia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy renealmia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes plants known for their ornamental value. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures. Renealmia pilosa thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
