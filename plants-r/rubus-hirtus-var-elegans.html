Rubus hirtus var. elegans

Rubus hirtus var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, which ripens in late summer, is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a valued plant in both wild and cultivated settings.
