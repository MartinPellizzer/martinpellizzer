Rubus nurnbergensis

Rubus nurnbergensis, commonly known as the Nurnberg raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Central Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, edible berries similar to raspberries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The fruit is used in local cuisine and traditional herbal remedies.
