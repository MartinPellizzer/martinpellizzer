Randia virosa

Randia virosa, commonly known as the poisonwood plant, is a tropical shrub native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often glandular and aromatic. The plant produces small white or yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots and leaves contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.
