Rosa jundzillii var. tolosana

Rosa jundzillii var. tolosana, commonly known as the Tolosan rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically has dark green, glossy leaves and thorny stems typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardy nature in temperate climates.
