Rhus glabra var. canadensis

Rhus glabra var. canadensis, commonly known as smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and turn a striking red color in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by clusters of red, fuzzy fruits that are a common food source for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Smooth sumac is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
