Ranunculus angustipetalus

Ranunculus angustipetalus, commonly known as the narrow-petaled buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred petals and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a distinctive hairy texture.

Ranunculus angustipetalus is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
