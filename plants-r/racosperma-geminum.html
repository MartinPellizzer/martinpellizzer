Racosperma geminum

Racosperma geminum, commonly known as the twin-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Racosperma geminum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
