Rubus lidforssii

Rubus lidforssii, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
