Ribes sanguineum

Ribes sanguineum, commonly known as the red currant bush, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, which appear in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible landscaping.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
