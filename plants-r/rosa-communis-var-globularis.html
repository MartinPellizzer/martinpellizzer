Rosa communis var. globularis

Rosa communis var. globularis, commonly known as the globe rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant characterized by its rounded, bushy growth habit.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white, typically in late spring to early summer. The foliage consists of dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its visual appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It requires regular pruning to maintain its shape and encourage continuous blooming.
