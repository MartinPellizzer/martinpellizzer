Rosa indica var. longifolia

Rosa indica var. longifolia, commonly known as the Indian rose or long-leaved rose, is a variant of the rose species Rosa indica.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

The variety is distinguished by its longer foliage compared to other Rosa indica cultivars.
