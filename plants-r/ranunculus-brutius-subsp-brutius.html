Ranunculus brutius subsp. brutius

Ranunculus brutius subsp.

brutius, commonly known as the Italian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often with a distinctive dark center.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are hairy.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.
