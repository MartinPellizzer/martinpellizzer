Rudgea coronicarpa

Rudgea coronicarpa, commonly known as the golden star bush, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in the wild.

Rudgea coronicarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
