Rubus elongatifolius

Rubus elongatifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, serrated leaves and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant produces thorny stems and has a shrub-like growth habit. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like roses and apples.
