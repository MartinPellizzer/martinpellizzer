Rosa dumalis f. innocua

Rosa dumalis f. innocua, commonly known as the alpine rose or mountain rose, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is a compact shrub with simple, alternate leaves that are typically hairy and have a grayish-green hue. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
