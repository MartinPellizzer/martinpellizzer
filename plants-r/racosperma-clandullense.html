Racosperma clandullense

Racosperma clandullense, commonly known as the Clandullense Acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, showcasing features such as small, reduced leaves to minimize water loss.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
