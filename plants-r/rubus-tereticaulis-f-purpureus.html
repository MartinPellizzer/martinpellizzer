Rubus tereticaulis f. purpureus

Rubus tereticaulis f. purpureus, commonly known as the purple-fruited blackberry, is a subspecies of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged fruits.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to North America, particularly in the eastern United States. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, and its flowers are white or pale pink. The fruits are aggregate berries, developing from the fusion of multiple small drupelets, and are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
