Ranunculus auricomus var. alliariifolius

Ranunculus auricomus var. alliariifolius, commonly known as the garlic-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features trifoliate leaves that resemble garlic leaves, with three narrow, pointed lobes. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped center, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
