Rubus vulgaris subsp. selmeri

Rubus vulgaris subsp.

selmeri, commonly known as the Selmer's blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
