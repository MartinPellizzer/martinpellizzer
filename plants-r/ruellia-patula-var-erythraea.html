Ruellia patula var. erythraea

Ruellia patula var. erythraea, commonly known as red-veined ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking foliage with deep green leaves marked by bright red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance.

Ruellia patula var. erythraea is relatively easy to grow and can be propagated through seeds or stem cuttings.
