Ranunculus bonariensis var. januarii

Ranunculus bonariensis var. januarii, commonly known as the January ranunculus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with five to seven petals, typically in shades of white, yellow, or pink. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate and often covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is known for its early blooming habit, often flowering in late winter or early spring.
