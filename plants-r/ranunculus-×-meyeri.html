Ranunculus × meyeri

Ranunculus × meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's buttercup, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with a prominent central pistil. The plant features trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and have a hairy texture. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
