Ruellia bracteophylla

Ruellia bracteophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, often arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. Ruellia bracteophylla is a perennial shrub that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
