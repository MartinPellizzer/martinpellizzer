Racosperma glaucocarpum

Racosperma glaucocarpum, commonly known as the glaucocarpum acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces woody pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and conservation efforts.
