Rhipidodendrum

Rhipidodendrum, commonly known as the climbing fern, is a genus of epiphytic plants belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

These plants are characterized by their ability to grow on other plants without parasitizing them, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Their fronds are typically arched or drooping, with a distinctive, often fuzzy appearance. Rhipidodendrum species have rhizomes that help anchor them to their host and absorb moisture from the air.

They play an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by contributing to biodiversity and providing habitat for small organisms.
