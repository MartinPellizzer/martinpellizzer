Rosa lucida var. glauca

Rosa lucida var. glauca, commonly known as the bluebloom rose, is a cultivar of the species Rosa lucida, characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers.

This variety is prized for its unique coloration, which is a result of high levels of anthocyanin pigments in the petals. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green foliage. The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
