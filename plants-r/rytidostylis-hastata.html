Rytidostylis hastata

Rytidostylis hastata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in swampy or waterlogged habitats, often forming dense stands in marshes and along riverbanks. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and have a distinct midrib. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a loose panicle, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Rytidostylis hastata plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
