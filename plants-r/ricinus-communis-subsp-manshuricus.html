Ricinus communis subsp. manshuricus

Ricinus communis subsp.

manshuricus, commonly known as the Manchurian castor bean, is a variant of the castor oil plant native to eastern Asia. It is a herbaceous annual that produces distinctive spiny capsules containing toxic seeds.

The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
