Rhipsalis baccifera subsp. baccifera

Rhipsalis baccifera subsp.

baccifera, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems that produce clusters of small, waxy flowers.

The plant typically blooms during the holiday season, with flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with well-draining soil.

It is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
