Ranunculus peltatus subsp. saniculifolius

Ranunculus peltatus subsp.

saniculifolius, commonly known as the Saniculifolius buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often described as resembling those of the sanicle plant, giving it its subspecific name.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
