Rhododendron viscosum var. aemulans

Rhododendron viscosum var. aemulans, commonly known as the sticky alpine rhododendron, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces sticky resinous sap, which gives it its common name and helps protect it from herbivores. It thrives in cool, moist environments with acidic soil and partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
