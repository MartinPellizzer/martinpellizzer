Ranunculus orthorhynchus var. bloomeri

Ranunculus orthorhynchus var. bloomeri, commonly known as the Bloomer's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
