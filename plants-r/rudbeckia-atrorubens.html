Rudbeckia atrorubens

Rudbeckia atrorubens, commonly known as the black-eyed Susan, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange petals and dark brown central disk, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces showy blooms from late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Rudbeckia atrorubens is also known for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
