Raphistemma pulchellum

Raphistemma pulchellum, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern on the petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its overall aesthetic appeal.

Raphistemma pulchellum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
