Rubus costatus var. rhamnifolius

Rubus costatus var. rhamnifolius, commonly known as the red raspberry leaf raspberry, is a variant of the red raspberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its serrated, trifoliate leaves and biennial growth habit. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. Its botanical features include a woody stem and a system of underground runners for propagation.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or open areas.
