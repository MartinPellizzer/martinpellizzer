Rabdosia wightii

Rabdosia wightii, commonly known as Himalayan blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at high altitudes. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, blue to purple flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, giving it a striking appearance.

Rabdosia wightii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
