Roscoea yunnanensis var. scillifolia

Roscoea yunnanensis var. scillifolia, commonly known as the Yunnan Roscoea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers, and is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale pink to lilac flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
