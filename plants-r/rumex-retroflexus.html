Rumex retroflexus

Rumex retroflexus, commonly known as the curled dock, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, often zigzagged stems and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, ditches, and fields. Its small, greenish flowers appear in dense clusters during the summer months.

Rumex retroflexus is known for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
