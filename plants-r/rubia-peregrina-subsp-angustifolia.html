Rubia peregrina subsp. angustifolia

Rubia peregrina subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive aromatic fragrance, which is characteristic of the Rubia genus. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
