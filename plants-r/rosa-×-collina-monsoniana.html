Rosa × collina monsoniana

Rosa × collina monsoniana, commonly known as the Monson's evergland rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and is often found in moist, wooded areas. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically dark green and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental value.

Rosa × collina monsoniana is valued in horticulture for its disease resistance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
