Rosa rubiginosa var. agrestis

Rosa rubiginosa var. agrestis, commonly known as the rough shrub rose, is a thorny, deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its dark red to purplish stems and small, fragrant flowers. The leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and the plant produces oval, reddish-brown fruits called hips. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

The species is also known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
