Ranunculus acriformis var. acriformis

Ranunculus acriformis var. acriformis, commonly known as the acicular ranunculus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It is a perennial species that thrives in cool, temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
