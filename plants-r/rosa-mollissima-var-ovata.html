Rosa mollissima var. ovata

Rosa mollissima var. ovata, commonly known as the soft-hairy rose or the Mexican rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces rose hips that are typically orange-red and contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and winter interest.
