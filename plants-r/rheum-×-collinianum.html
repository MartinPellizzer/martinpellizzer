Rheum × collinianum

Rheum × collinianum, commonly known as the giant rhubarb, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces tall, reddish stalks with edible, sour-tasting leaves and stems. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its botanical features make it a striking addition to gardens and a subject of interest in horticultural studies.
