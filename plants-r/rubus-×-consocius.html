Rubus × consocius

Rubus × consocius, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and serrated, while the flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit, which is a cluster of small drupes, is often used in culinary applications and is rich in antioxidants.
