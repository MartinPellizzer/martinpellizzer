Ranunculus venetus

Ranunculus venetus, commonly known as the Italian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features a basal rosette of lobed, glossy green leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom from spring to early summer, and it is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped appearance.

Ranunculus venetus is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique foliage.
