Rubus raopingensis var. obtusidentatus

Rubus raopingensis var. obtusidentatus, commonly known as the Raoping blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal fruit production.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
