Rubus slovacensis

Rubus slovacensis, commonly known as the Slovak blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes various species of shrubs commonly referred to as blackberries or raspberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The plant has limited commercial cultivation but is of interest for its potential in horticulture and ecological studies.
