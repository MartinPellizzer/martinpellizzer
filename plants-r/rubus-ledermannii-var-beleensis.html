Rubus ledermannii var. beleensis

Rubus ledermannii var. beleensis, commonly known as the Beleense raspberry, is a shrub native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
