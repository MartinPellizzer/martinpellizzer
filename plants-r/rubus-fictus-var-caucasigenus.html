Rubus fictus var. caucasigenus

Rubus fictus var. caucasigenus, commonly known as the Caucasian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
