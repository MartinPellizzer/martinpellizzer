Reichardia × sventenia

Reichardia × sventenia, commonly known as the hybrid rockcress, is a perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Reichardia picroides and Reichardia speciosa, characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. It is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions.

Reichardia × sventenia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
