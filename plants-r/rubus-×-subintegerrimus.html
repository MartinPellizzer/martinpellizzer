Rubus × subintegerrimus

Rubus × subintegerrimus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. This plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it produces dark purple to black berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
