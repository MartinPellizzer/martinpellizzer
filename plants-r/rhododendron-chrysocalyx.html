Rhododendron chrysocalyx

Rhododendron chrysocalyx, commonly known as the golden-calyx rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific, cool climates.
