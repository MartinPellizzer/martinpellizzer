Rosa rugosa repens-alba

Rosa rugosa repens-alba, commonly known as the coastal rose, is a hardy shrub native to coastal regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its rugged, thorny stems and dense, often sprawling growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and covered in fine hairs, while its fruits are red, hairy rosehips that persist into winter.

Rosa rugosa repens-alba is well adapted to salty air and sandy soils, making it a popular choice for coastal landscaping.
