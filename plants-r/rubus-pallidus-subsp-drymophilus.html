Rubus pallidus subsp. drymophilus

Rubus pallidus subsp.

drymophilus, commonly known as the drymophilus raspberry, is a shrub native to the northern regions of North America. It is characterized by its slender stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces pale yellow to orange flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, hairy fruits in the summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs or wetlands.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance as a food source for various wildlife species.
