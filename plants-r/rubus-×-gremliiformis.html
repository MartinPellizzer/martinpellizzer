Rubus × gremliiformis

Rubus × gremliiformis, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry, is a species resulting from the natural cross between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (cloudberry).

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a tangy and slightly tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in wetland or boggy environments.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in culinary uses.
