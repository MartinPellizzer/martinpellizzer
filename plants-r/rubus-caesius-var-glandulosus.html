Rubus caesius var. glandulosus

Rubus caesius var. glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The glandular hairs on its stems and leaves give it its specific variety name, glandulosus.
