Rupicapnos africana var. gracilis

Rupicapnos africana var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender African firethorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, spiny branches and clusters of small, orange-red berries that appear in late summer. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by the distinctive berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and colorful fruits, which attract birds and add visual interest to landscapes.
