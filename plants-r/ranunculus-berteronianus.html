Ranunculus berteronianus

Ranunculus berteronianus, commonly known as the Berteronian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Ranunculus berteronianus is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native habitats.
