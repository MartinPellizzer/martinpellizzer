Rhamnella tonkinensis

Rhamnella tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin rhamnus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its fruit is a small, smooth drupe that contains a single seed.

Rhamnella tonkinensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
