Rosa spinosissima var. andrewsii

Rosa spinosissima var. andrewsii, commonly known as the Andrews' thorny rose, is a variant of the spiny rose species.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
