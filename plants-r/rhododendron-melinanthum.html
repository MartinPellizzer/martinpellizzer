Rhododendron melinanthum

Rhododendron melinanthum, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features small, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowers are pink to red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring.

Rhododendron melinanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
