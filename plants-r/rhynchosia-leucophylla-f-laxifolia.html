Rhynchosia leucophylla f. laxifolia

Rhynchosia leucophylla f. laxifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved cowpea, is a variant of the cowpea species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often lobed or deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
