Rhynchosia inflata

Rhynchosia inflata, commonly known as the inflated legume or balloon vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its inflated, balloon-like seed pods that give it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred structure.

Rhynchosia inflata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
