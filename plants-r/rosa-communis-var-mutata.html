Rosa communis var. mutata

Rosa communis var. mutata, commonly known as the Mutated Common Rose, is a variant of the common rose with distinct botanical characteristics.

It typically features smaller, more compact flowers compared to the standard Rosa communis. The plant has a bushy growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to white blooms. Its leaves are dark green and serrated, with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
