Ruscus hypophyllum var. hypoglossum

Ruscus hypophyllum var. hypoglossum, commonly known as common spurge or evergreen spurge, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features dense clusters of fleshy, dark green leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
