Rottboellia compressa

Rottboellia compressa, commonly known as the flatseeded lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, flat leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Rottboellia compressa is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and widespread species.
