Rubus subalpestris

Rubus subalpestris, commonly known as the alpine bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in rocky and alpine environments. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. It typically grows at high altitudes, often in open, sunny areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.
