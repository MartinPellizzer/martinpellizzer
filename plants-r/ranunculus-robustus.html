Ranunculus robustus

Ranunculus robustus, commonly known as the robust buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and large, showy yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals. The leaves are typically palmately divided, with lobes that are often toothed or lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
