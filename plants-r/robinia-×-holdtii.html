Robinia × holdtii

Robinia × holdtii, commonly known as the Chinese honeylocust, is a hybrid legume native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that turn yellow in autumn and its distinctive, thornless pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it a popular choice for ornamental and reforestation purposes. It produces fragrant, yellow flowers in the spring, adding visual appeal to landscapes.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
