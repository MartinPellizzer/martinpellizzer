Ranunculus sorbicus

Ranunculus sorbicus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which is characterized by showy flowers and often toxic compounds. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five to eight petals and numerous stamens.

The leaves are palmately divided, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
