Robiquetia eburnea

Robiquetia eburnea, commonly known as the ivory wood or white wood, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Robiquetia eburnea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
