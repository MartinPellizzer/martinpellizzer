Rosa pinnatisepala

Rosa pinnatisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled rose, is a species native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its sepals are broad and often as large as the petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Rosa pinnatisepala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
