Rubus infestus subsp. petrakii

Rubus infestus subsp.

petrakii, commonly known as the Petrak's blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
