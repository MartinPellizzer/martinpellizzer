Rubus × florulifer

Rubus × florulifer, commonly known as the evergreen bramble.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable plant suitable for various ecological niches.
