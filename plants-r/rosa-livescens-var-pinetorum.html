Rosa livescens var. pinetorum

Rosa livescens var. pinetorum, commonly known as the Southern Sand Rose, is a compact, shrubby rose native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and a dense, thorny growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
