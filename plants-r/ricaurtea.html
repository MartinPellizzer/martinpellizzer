Ricaurtea

Ricaurtea, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Ricaurtea is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its wood.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
