Racosperma megalanthum

Racosperma megalanthum, commonly known as the large-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds used in traditional Aboriginal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Racosperma megalanthum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for native pollinators.
