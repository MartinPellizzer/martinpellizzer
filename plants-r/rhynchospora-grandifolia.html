Rhynchospora grandifolia

Rhynchospora grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, three-lobed flowers that are typically pale green to reddish in color. It thrives in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Rhynchospora grandifolia is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ecological importance in supporting various insect and bird species.
