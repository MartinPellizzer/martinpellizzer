Rubus serriculatus

Rubus serriculatus, commonly known as the serrated blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Rubus serriculatus is an important plant in ecological systems, providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
