Rhynchosia calycina

Rhynchosia calycina, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and distinctive, spiny fruit pods that contain seeds. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces a strong, often unpleasant odor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Rhynchosia calycina is used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
