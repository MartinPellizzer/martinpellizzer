Ranunculus brutius subsp. anatolicus

Ranunculus brutius subsp.

anatolicus, commonly known as the Anatolian crowfoot, is a perennial herb native to Turkey. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous regions. It has small, hairy stems and produces numerous stamens in its flower clusters.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
