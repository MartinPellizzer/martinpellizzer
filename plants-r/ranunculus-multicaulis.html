Ranunculus multicaulis

Ranunculus multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a compact, upright growth habit with multiple stems arising from a central rootstock. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Ranunculus multicaulis is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
