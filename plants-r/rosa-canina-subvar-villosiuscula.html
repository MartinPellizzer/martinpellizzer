Rosa canina subvar. villosiuscula

Rosa canina subvar. villosiuscula, commonly known as the hairy rosehip, is a subspecies of the dog rose characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

It produces small, orange-red fruits called rosehips, which are rich in vitamin C. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often growing in woodland edges and hedgerows. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The subspecies is notable for its distinctive pubescent (hairy) texture on both stems and foliage.
