Rosa × damascena var. officinalis

Rosa × damascena var. officinalis, commonly known as the officinal rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of 1 to 2 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are rich in essential oils, making it valuable for both ornamental and aromatic purposes.

The botanical features of this variety are well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates.
