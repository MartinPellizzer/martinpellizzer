Ranunculus howellii

Ranunculus howellii, commonly known as Howell's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant features a short, stout stem with trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and have a waxy texture. Its flowers are bright yellow with numerous small petals, arranged in a cup-like structure.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.
