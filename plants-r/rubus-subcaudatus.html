Rubus subcaudatus

Rubus subcaudatus, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated margins and produces white to pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is edible, though it is generally less flavorful compared to other blackberry varieties.
