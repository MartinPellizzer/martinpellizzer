Rubus luchunensis

Rubus luchunensis, commonly known as the Luchun blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the family Rosaceae. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of small, dark berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
