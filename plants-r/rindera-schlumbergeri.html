Rindera schlumbergeri

Rindera schlumbergeri, commonly known as the Schlumbergera, is a type of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its flat, segmented stems that resemble leaves, which are actually modified stems adapted for storing water. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the winter months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular houseplant and is often cultivated for its showy blooms.
