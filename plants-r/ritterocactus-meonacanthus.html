Ritterocactus meonacanthus

Ritterocactus meonacanthus, commonly known as the Mexican hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, bristle-like spines that provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent collectors and desert gardens.
