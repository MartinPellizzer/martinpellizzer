Ranunculus pseudomuricatus

Ranunculus pseudomuricatus, commonly known as the roughleaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in small, rough hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in open meadows and is adapted to cool, high-altitude climates.
