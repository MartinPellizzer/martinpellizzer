Rhododendron virgatum subsp. virgatum

Rhododendron virgatum subsp.

virgatum, commonly known as the dwarf alpine rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, often forming low shrubs with broad, leathery leaves.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments with acidic soil, often found in forest understories or rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
