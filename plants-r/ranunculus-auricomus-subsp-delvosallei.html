Ranunculus auricomus subsp. delvosallei

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

delvosallei, commonly known as the Delvosalle's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive, broad, auriculate (ear-shaped) leaf base.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, white hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Ranunculaceae family.
