Rhynchosia himalensis var. himalensis

Rhynchosia himalensis var. himalensis, commonly known as the Himalayan cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with trifoliate leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in local cuisines.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and agricultural potential in arid and semi-arid regions.
