Rehderodendron macrophyllum

Rehderodendron macrophyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
