Ranunculus aequalis

Ranunculus aequalis, commonly known as the equal ranunculus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and produces showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. It is part of the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae) and is characterized by its simple, elegant floral structure.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in some regions.
