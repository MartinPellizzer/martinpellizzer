Rubus podophyllos subsp. delphinensis

Rubus podophyllos subsp.

delphinensis, commonly known as the Delphinine blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its dark red to black berries, which are typically small and aggregate.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
