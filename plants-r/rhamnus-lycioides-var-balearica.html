Rhamnus lycioides var. balearica

Rhamnus lycioides var. balearica, commonly known as the Balearic buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and seasonal interest.
