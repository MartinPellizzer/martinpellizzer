Ravenala grandis

Ravenala grandis, commonly known as the cabbage tree, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 6 meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in panicles at the top of the stem. Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and for thatching in some cultures.
