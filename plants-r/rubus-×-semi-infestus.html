Rubus × semi-infestus

Rubus × semi-infestus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as berries, which are valued for their flavor and culinary uses. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can become invasive in certain regions.
