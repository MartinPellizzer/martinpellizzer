Rhytidophyllum leucomallon var. carneum

Rhytidophyllum leucomallon var. carneum, commonly known as the white-flowered rhytidophyllum, is a unique variant of this rare plant species.

It is characterized by its pale, almost white flowers that contrast with its green foliage. The plant exhibits a succulent structure, allowing it to store water in its leaves and stems. It thrives in arid environments, typically found in rocky soils with minimal rainfall.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in xerophytic plant adaptations.
