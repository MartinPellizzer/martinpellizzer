Rubus palmatus similis

Rubus palmatus similis, commonly known as the similar trailing raspberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant ecosystems.
