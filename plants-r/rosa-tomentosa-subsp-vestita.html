Rosa tomentosa subsp. vestita

Rosa tomentosa subsp.

vestita, commonly known as the woolly rose or velvet rose, is a shrub-like species native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and fragrant, pale pink to white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil. Its stems are covered in a soft, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
