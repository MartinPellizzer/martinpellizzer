Roella incurva

Roella incurva, commonly known as the spiral grass, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spirally twisted leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. Roella incurva is typically found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is a perennial plant that is often used in ornamental gardening due to its distinctive foliage.
