Rosa nigrans subf. orbigera

Rosa nigrans subf.

orbigera, commonly known as the broadleaf rock rose, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are compound, with several oval-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
