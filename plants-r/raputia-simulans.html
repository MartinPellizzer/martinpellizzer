Raputia simulans

Raputia simulans, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a distinct orchid-like appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
