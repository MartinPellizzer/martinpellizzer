Rebutia mentosa subsp. mentosa

Rebutia mentosa subsp.

mentosa, commonly known as the Mentosa Rebutia, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and ribbed stems.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its resilience to drought and ability to store water in its fleshy stems make it well adapted to arid environments.
