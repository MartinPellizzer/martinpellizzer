Rubus hebecaulis var. roseistylus

Rubus hebecaulis var. roseistylus, commonly known as the rose-styled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black. The plant produces pink to white flowers with five petals, and its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian areas and forest edges.

The fruit is typically harvested in late summer and is valued for its tart flavor and use in culinary applications.
