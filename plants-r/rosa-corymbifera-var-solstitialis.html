Rosa corymbifera var. solstitialis

Rosa corymbifera var. solstitialis, commonly known as the summer rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces rosehips that are orange-red in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and aesthetic purposes.
