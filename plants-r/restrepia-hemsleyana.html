Restrepia hemsleyana

Restrepia hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley's restrepia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and hairy, strap-like leaves. The plant produces intricate inflorescences with fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. Its botanical features include pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in high-altitude environments.

Restrepia hemsleyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
