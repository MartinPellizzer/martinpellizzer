Ranunculus montenegrinus

Ranunculus montenegrinus, commonly known as the Montenegro buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a deep green color and a hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch of yellow to the natural landscape.
