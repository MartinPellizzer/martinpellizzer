Rottboellia roxburghiana

Rottboellia roxburghiana, commonly known as purpletop grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive purple-tinted inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a cover crop.
