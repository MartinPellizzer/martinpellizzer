Rohdea subcaudata

Rohdea subcaudata, commonly known as the narrow-tailed lily, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. The plant produces striking, upright spikes of white or pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and hardy foliage plant.
