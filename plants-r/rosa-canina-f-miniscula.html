Rosa canina f. miniscula

Rosa canina f. miniscula, commonly known as the dwarf dog rose, is a compact, shrub-like form of the common dog rose.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, thorned stems and dark green, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its fruit, known as a hip, is red and contains numerous seeds, often used in herbal remedies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
