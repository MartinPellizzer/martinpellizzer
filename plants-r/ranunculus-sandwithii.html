Ranunculus sandwithii

Ranunculus sandwithii, commonly known as the Sandwith's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, herbaceous plants. The species is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive central pistil. It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
