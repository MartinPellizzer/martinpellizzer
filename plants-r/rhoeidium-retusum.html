Rhoeidium retusum

Rhoeidium retusum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rhoeodendron, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its botanical features include a woody stem and opposite leaf arrangement.

Rhoeidium retusum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
