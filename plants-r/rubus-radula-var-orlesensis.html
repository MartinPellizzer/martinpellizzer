Rubus radula var. orlesensis

Rubus radula var. orlesensis, commonly known as the Orlesian bramble, is a variant of the common bramble found in specific regions of Europe.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically oval to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, hairy berries in the autumn. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it an important species for local ecosystems and traditional herbal uses.
