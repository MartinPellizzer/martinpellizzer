Raphanocarpus kirkii

Raphanocarpus kirkii, commonly known as the African turnip, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible crops such as cabbage and mustard. The plant features a robust root system and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly bitter taste, often used in local cuisines.
