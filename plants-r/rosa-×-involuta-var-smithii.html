Rosa × involuta var. smithii

Rosa × involuta var. smithii, commonly known as the Smith's curled rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tightly curled petals that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often in clusters.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
