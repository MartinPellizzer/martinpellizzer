Ranunculus auricomus subsp. spado

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

spado, commonly known as the yellow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with a distinctive central tuft of stamens.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. It has palmate leaves with lobes that are usually deeply divided.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
