Rudbeckia serotina f. flavescens

Rudbeckia serotina f. flavescens, commonly known as the late goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in the late summer and autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are usually hairy.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting various wildlife species.
