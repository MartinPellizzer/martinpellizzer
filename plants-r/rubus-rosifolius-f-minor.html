Rubus rosifolius f. minor

Rubus rosifolius f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a compact variant of the rosifolius raspberry species.

It is characterized by its small, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves that are typically pink to reddish in color. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red when ripe, similar to other raspberry varieties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

This form is prized for its ornamental value and mild flavor, making it a popular choice for both decorative and culinary use.
