Rubus idaeus inermis

Rubus idaeus inermis, commonly known as the smooth raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thornless stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe, and it has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in woodland edges or hedgerows.

The smooth raspberry is valued for its culinary uses and ornamental appeal in gardens.
