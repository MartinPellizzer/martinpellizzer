Rosa armena

Rosa armena, commonly known as Armenian rose, is a species of shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous areas and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa armena is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its historical use in traditional medicine.
