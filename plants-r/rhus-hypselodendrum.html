Rhus hypselodendrum

Rhus hypselodendrum, commonly known as the Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, with each leaflet being oval and serrated. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for birds and mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
