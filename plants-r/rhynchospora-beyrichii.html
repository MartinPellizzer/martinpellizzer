Rhynchospora beyrichii

Rhynchospora beyrichii, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, greenish flowers. Native to wetlands and marshy areas in temperate regions, it thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
