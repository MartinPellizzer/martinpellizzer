Rhododendron cinnamomeum var. cunninghamii

Rhododendron cinnamomeum var. cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cinnamomum rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded or partially shaded environments. Its botanical features include lanceolate leaves with a glossy surface and a distinctive cinnamon-like scent in its foliage.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
