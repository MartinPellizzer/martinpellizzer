Reseda fruticosa

Reseda fruticosa, commonly known as sea holly, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in coastal environments and well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

Reseda fruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
