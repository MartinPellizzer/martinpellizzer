Rhynchosia trinervis

Rhynchosia trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet having three distinct veins, and its distinctive yellow flowers with a spurred petal. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
