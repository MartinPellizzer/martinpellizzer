Reseda arabica var. integrifolia

Reseda arabica var. integrifolia, commonly known as the whole-leaf reseda, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with simple, alternate leaves that are entire, hence the species name "integrifolia.". Its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in racemes, typical of the family.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its delicate foliage and early spring blooms.
