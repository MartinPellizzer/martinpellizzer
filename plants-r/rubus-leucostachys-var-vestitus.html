Rubus leucostachys var. vestitus

Rubus leucostachys var. vestitus, commonly known as the white-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically smaller and more tart than those of other blackberry varieties. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
