Racosperma ryanianum

Racosperma ryanianum, commonly known as the Sydney red gum, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly in New South Wales and Queensland, where it thrives in dry, sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and small, compound leaves that are often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine. Racosperma ryanianum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
