Ribes sanguineum var. albidum

Ribes sanguineum var. albidum, commonly known as the white-flowered currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, edible berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible landscaping.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
