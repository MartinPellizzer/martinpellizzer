Rubus erythroxanthus

Rubus erythroxanthus, commonly known as the red-berried raspberry.

It is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and belongs to the Rosaceae family. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, containing numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are compound, with three leaflets, and the stems are covered in stinging hairs.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
