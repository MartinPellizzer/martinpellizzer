Rhododendron fordii

Rhododendron fordii, commonly known as the Ford's rhododendron, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring acidic, well-drained soils. It is part of the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Its growth habit includes both shrub and small tree forms, making it versatile for different landscaping applications.
