Rollinia parviflora var. angustifolia

Rollinia parviflora var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a spherical fruit with a rough, greenish-brown skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, providing a distinct appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruit, making it popular in both horticulture and culinary applications.
