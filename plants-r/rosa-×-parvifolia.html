Rosa × parvifolia

Rosa × parvifolia, commonly known as the Chinese shrub rose, is a hybrid species native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, usually in clusters of five to seven blooms. Its botanical characteristics include simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a woody, bushy growth habit.

Rosa × parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
