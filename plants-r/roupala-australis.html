Roupala australis

Roupala australis, commonly known as the New Zealand holly, is a small evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It has smooth, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is well adapted to the temperate climate of New Zealand and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
