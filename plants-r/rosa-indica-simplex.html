Rosa indica simplex

Rosa indica simplex, commonly known as the simple Indian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its simple, single-petaled flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant is native to regions of Asia, including India and China, and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage consists of serrated, dark green leaves, and it produces fragrant, edible hips that contain seeds.

Rosa indica simplex is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
