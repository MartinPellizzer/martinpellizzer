Ranunculus × chrysanthus

Ranunculus × chrysanthus, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Ranunculus asiaticus and Ranunculus glabellus, resulting in a plant with showy, double or semi-double flowers. The plant typically has a short, fibrous root system and hairy stems that are usually upright or spreading. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive veining pattern, and the flowers come in a variety of colors including yellow, orange, and red.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
