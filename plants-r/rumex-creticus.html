Rumex creticus

Rumex creticus, commonly known as Cretan sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and borne in clusters, forming a distinctive umbrella-like shape.

The plant is known for its tart, acidic taste due to the presence of oxalic acid in its leaves.
