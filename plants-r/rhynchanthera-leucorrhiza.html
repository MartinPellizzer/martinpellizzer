Rhynchanthera leucorrhiza

Rhynchanthera leucorrhiza, commonly known as the white mucus orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers that resemble mucus, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests or mountainous regions. Its root system is specialized for nutrient absorption in poor soil conditions, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for survival.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
