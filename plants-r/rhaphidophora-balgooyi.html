Rhaphidophora balgooyi

Rhaphidophora balgooyi, commonly known as the Bornean arrowroot, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Borneo, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a rhizomatous root system, which allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its leaves are typically large, arrow-shaped, and have a smooth, waxy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, a common feature among arums.
