Rhagodia latifolia

Rhagodia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf rhagodia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Rhagodia latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
