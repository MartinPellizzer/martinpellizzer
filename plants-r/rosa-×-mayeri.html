Rosa × mayeri

Rosa × mayeri, commonly known as the Mayer's rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically has thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. It is native to the Caucasus region and is cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Rosa × mayeri prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
