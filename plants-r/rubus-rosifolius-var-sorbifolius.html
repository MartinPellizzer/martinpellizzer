Rubus rosifolius var. sorbifolius

Rubus rosifolius var. sorbifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable component of its native ecosystem.
