Rubus carpinifolius var. sampaioanus

Rubus carpinifolius var. sampaioanus, commonly known as the Sampaio's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
