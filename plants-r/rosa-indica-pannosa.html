Rosa indica pannosa

Rosa indica pannosa, commonly known as the Indian rose or pannose rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant is native to the Himalayas and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and sought-after addition to gardens and floral arrangements.
