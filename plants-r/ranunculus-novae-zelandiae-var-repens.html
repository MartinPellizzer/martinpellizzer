Ranunculus novae-zelandiae var. repens

Ranunculus novae-zelandiae var. repens, commonly known as the creeping New Zealand buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming in spring. The plant has a creeping growth habit, with stolons that root at the nodes to form dense mats. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
