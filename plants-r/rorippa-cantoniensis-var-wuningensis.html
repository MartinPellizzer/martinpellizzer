Rorippa cantoniensis var. wuningensis

Rorippa cantoniensis var. wuningensis, commonly known as the Wuning watercress, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Its small, alternate leaves are usually oval to lance-shaped with a waxy coating.

The flowers are yellow and borne in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in wetland habitats.
