Rapistrella × ramosissima f. glabra

Rapistrella × ramosissima f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth leafed hybrid of the genus Rapistrella, is a rare and unique plant characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rapistrella ramosissima and another closely related species, exhibiting traits from both parents. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often has a soft, velvety texture, which distinguishes it from its more hairy relatives.
