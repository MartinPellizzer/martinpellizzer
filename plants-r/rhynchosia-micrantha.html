Rhynchosia micrantha

Rhynchosia micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to cylindrical, hairy pods containing several seeds. Rhynchosia micrantha is often grown for its edible seeds, which are used in various culinary traditions.

It also has ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
