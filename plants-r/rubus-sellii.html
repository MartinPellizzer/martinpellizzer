Rubus sellii

Rubus sellii, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically narrow and serrated. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family, which includes many other brambles and shrubs.
