Rosa albolutescens

Rosa albolutescens, commonly known as the white-flowered shrub rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its botanical features include compound leaves and hips that mature to a red color.

Rosa albolutescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
