Ranunculus aquatilis subsp. rhipiphyllus

Ranunculus aquatilis subsp.

rhipiphyllus, commonly known as the water buttercup subspecies rhipiphyllus, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, and its leaves are usually floating or submerged. It has a fibrous root system that helps anchor it in waterlogged soils.

This subspecies is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.
