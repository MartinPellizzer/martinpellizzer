Rosa communis var. acanthina

Rosa communis var. acanthina, commonly known as the thorny rose, is a cultivar of the common rose that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its dense, thorned stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to red. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a serrated edge. This variety is known for its robust growth and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
