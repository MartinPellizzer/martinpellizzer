Rourea claessensii

Rourea claessensii, commonly known as the Ceylon rourea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
