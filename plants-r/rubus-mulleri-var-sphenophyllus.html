Rubus mulleri var. sphenophyllus

Rubus mulleri var. sphenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elliptical leaves and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and it produces edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its culinary uses.
