Ranunculus ficaria var. podolicus

Ranunculus ficaria var. podolicus, commonly known as the Podolic buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often surrounded by a ring of yellow sepals. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are palmately arranged, with finely toothed margins. It thrives in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Ranunculaceae family.
