Rubus splendiflorus

Rubus splendiflorus, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit, a dark purple berry, is edible and often used in jams and desserts.

Rubus splendiflorus is typically found in woodlands and disturbed areas across North America.
