Rhododendron tschonoskii

Rhododendron tschonoskii, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that are leathery in texture. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Rhododendron tschonoskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
