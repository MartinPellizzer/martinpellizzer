Rubus axillariformis

Rubus axillariformis, commonly known as the axillary raspberry, is a species of bramble native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually hairy and serrated.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
