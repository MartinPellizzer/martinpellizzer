Rubus hiernii

Rubus hiernii, commonly known as the Hirt's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like China, Vietnam, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
