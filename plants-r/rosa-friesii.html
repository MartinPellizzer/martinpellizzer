Rosa friesii

Rosa friesii, commonly known as the Fries' rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, fragrant pink to red flowers, and dark green, serrated leaves. Rosa friesii typically blooms in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
