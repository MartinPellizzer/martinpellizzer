Rubus brassii

Rubus brassii, commonly known as the Brassii blackberry, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in the autumn season. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in temperate climates with moderate rainfall.
