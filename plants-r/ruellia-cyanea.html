Ruellia cyanea

Ruellia cyanea, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring a sturdy, branching stem. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Ruellia cyanea is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
