Rubus × malbranchei

Rubus × malbranchei, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries. The plant is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, and it produces small, aggregate fruits that are often used in culinary applications.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
