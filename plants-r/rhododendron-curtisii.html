Rhododendron curtisii

Rhododendron curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, specifically found in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils at high altitudes. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to reddish flowers in the spring. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many other ornamental and ecologically significant plants.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
