Ranunculus cadmicus subsp. cyprius

Ranunculus cadmicus subsp.

cyprius, commonly known as Cyprus buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the island of Cyprus. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has palmately divided leaves and is often found in rocky or calcareous soils. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is adapted to Mediterranean climate conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
