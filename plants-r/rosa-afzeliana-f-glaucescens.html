Rosa afzeliana f. glaucescens

Rosa afzeliana f. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa afzeliana.

It is characterized by its distinctive bluish-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically features semi-evergreen leaves and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It is native to certain regions of Asia, particularly China, and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
