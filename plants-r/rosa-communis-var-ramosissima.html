Rosa communis var. ramosissima

Rosa communis var. ramosissima, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, red, hairy fruits called hips, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
