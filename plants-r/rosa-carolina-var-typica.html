Rosa carolina var. typica

Rosa carolina var. typica, commonly known as the Carolina rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are often covered in thorns.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in American botany.
