Rollinia deliciosa

Rollinia deliciosa, commonly known as the Brazilian cheese fruit, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that produces large, round, green fruits with a thick, leathery skin. The fruit contains a creamy, white pulp that is rich in nutrients and has a mildly sweet flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
