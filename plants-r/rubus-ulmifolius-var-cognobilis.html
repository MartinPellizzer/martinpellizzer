Rubus ulmifolius var. cognobilis

Rubus ulmifolius var. cognobilis, commonly known as the blackberry bush or sweet blackberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable species within the Rubus genus.
