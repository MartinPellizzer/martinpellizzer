Rubus lagerbergii

Rubus lagerbergii, commonly known as the Cape rose or yellow bramble, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, edible berries. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

Rubus lagerbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
