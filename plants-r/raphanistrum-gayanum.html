Raphanistrum gayanum

Raphanistrum gayanum, commonly known as the giant smartweed, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often with a reddish hue. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a hairy texture.

Raphanistrum gayanum is adapted to moist environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.
