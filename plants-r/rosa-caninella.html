Rosa caninella

Rosa caninella, commonly known as dog rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. Rosa caninella is native to Europe, western Asia, and northwestern Africa, and it thrives in temperate climates.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has a historical association with perfume and culinary applications.
