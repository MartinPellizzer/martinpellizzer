Ranunculus spissidens

Ranunculus spissidens, commonly known as the denseleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are densely hairy, giving it its common name. Its flowers are yellow with five petal-like sepals and are often surrounded by a ring of stamens.

Ranunculus spissidens is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
