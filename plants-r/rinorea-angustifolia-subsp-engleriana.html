Rinorea angustifolia subsp. engleriana

Rinorea angustifolia subsp.

engleriana, commonly known as the narrowleaf rinorea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape with a smooth margin. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and are usually white or pale pink in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
