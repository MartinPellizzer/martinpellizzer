Rosa plusiadenia

Rosa plusiadenia, commonly known as the Brazilian rose or the Brazilian pink, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a climbing shrub that produces fragrant, pink to white flowers with five petals. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Rosa plusiadenia is also valued for its use in traditional medicine.
