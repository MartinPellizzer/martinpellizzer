Rubus viridicatus

Rubus viridicatus, commonly known as the greenleaf blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible, though they are not as widely cultivated as other blackberry varieties. Botanically, it has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces white to pinkish flowers in the spring.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a common weed in disturbed areas.
