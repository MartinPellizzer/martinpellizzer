Rubus serpens var. opatoviensis

Rubus serpens var. opatoviensis, commonly known as the Opatoviensis blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, which help it climb and spread across vegetation. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
