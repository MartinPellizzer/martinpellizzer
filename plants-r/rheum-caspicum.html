Rheum caspicum

Rheum caspicum, commonly known as the Caspian rhubarb, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a distinctive hairy texture. Its flowering stems produce clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers.

Rheum caspicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
