Rhetinosperma longistipitatum

Rhetinosperma longistipitatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed star grass, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and can thrive in a variety of soil types. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it produces small, white or pale blue flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
