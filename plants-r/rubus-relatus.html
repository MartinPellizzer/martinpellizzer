Rubus relatus

Rubus relatus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are typically red when ripe. These fruits are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications.

Rubus relatus thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
