Rostellularia mollissima var. serpyllifolia

Rostellularia mollissima var. serpyllifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming habit.

It features soft, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to harsh environments, contributing to its ecological significance in such habitats.
