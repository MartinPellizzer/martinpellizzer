Romulea versicolor

Romulea versicolor, commonly known as the variable glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, underground bulb that stores nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Romulea versicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
