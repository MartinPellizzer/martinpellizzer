Rapanea korthalsii

Rapanea korthalsii, commonly known as the Korthals's lauraceae, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
