Rubus crassus var. begoniifolius

Rubus crassus var. begoniifolius, commonly known as the Begonia-leaved Raspberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, resembling those of begonias, hence its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
