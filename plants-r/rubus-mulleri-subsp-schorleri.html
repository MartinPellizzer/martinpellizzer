Rubus mulleri subsp. schorleri

Rubus mulleri subsp.

schorleri, commonly known as the Schorli blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
