Rubus royenii var. hispidus

Rubus royenii var. hispidus, commonly known as the hispid blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupelets that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is valued for its edible fruits and is sometimes cultivated for commercial use.
