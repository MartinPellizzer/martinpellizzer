Rubus hybridus var. monophyllus

Rubus hybridus var. monophyllus, commonly known as the single-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its unique foliage.

It is native to parts of North America and is typically found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, dark berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are usually simple and have a distinctive shape compared to other Rubus species.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
