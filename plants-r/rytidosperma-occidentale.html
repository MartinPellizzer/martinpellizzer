Rytidosperma occidentale

Rytidosperma occidentale, commonly known as the western spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Rytidosperma occidentale plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
