Rumex callosissimus

Rumex callosissimus, commonly known as sour dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color and covered with rough, waxy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a dense, terminal inflorescence. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a distinctive wavy margin with a rough texture.

Rumex callosissimus is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
