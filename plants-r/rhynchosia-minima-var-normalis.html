Rhynchosia minima var. normalis

Rhynchosia minima var. normalis, commonly known as the small-flowered cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It is well adapted to poor soils and can thrive in warm, humid climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable crop for both nutritional and agricultural purposes.
