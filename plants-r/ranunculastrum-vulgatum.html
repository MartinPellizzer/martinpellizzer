Ranunculastrum vulgatum

Ranunculastrum vulgatum, commonly known as the common meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, green fruits that contain seeds.

It is an important plant in ecological studies due to its role in supporting pollinators and its sensitivity to environmental changes.
