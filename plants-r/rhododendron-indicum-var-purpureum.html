Rhododendron indicum var. purpureum

Rhododendron indicum var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from pink to deep purple. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland settings.
