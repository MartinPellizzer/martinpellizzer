Redowskia sophiifolia

Redowskia sophiifolia, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a subtle, sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
