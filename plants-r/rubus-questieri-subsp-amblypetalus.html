Rubus questieri subsp. amblypetalus

Rubus questieri subsp.

amblypetalus, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and clustered.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
