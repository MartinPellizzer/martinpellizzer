Ranunculus blumei

Ranunculus blumei, commonly known as the Blume's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are hairy.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
