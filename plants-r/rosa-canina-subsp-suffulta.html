Rosa canina subsp. suffulta

Rosa canina subsp.

suffulta, commonly known as the giant dog rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is notable for its robust growth and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
