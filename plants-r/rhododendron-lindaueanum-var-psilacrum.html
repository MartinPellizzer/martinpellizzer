Rhododendron lindaueanum var. psilacrum

Rhododendron lindaueanum var. psilacrum, commonly known as the slender-leaved rhododendron, is a rare and unique variant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for horticulturists and botanists due to its ornamental value and ecological niche.

However, it is not widely cultivated, making it a less common sight in gardens and botanical collections.
