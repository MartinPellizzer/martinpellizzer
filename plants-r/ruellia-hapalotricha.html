Ruellia hapalotricha

Ruellia hapalotricha, commonly known as the silverleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in the summer months. Ruellia hapalotricha is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky areas.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
