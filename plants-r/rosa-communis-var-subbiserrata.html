Rosa communis var. subbiserrata

Rosa communis var. subbiserrata, commonly known as the serrated rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its leaves are compound, typically consisting of 5 to 7 leaflets with serrated edges, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a high degree of variability in color. It is a shrub-like perennial that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
