Rhinacanthus macilentus

Rhinacanthus macilentus, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.
