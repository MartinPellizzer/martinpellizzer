Rogiera brachistantha

Rogiera brachistantha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed broom, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Rogiera brachistantha is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
