Rhododendron × paradoxum

Rhododendron × paradoxum, commonly known as the purple rhododendron, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and inflorescences that are typically truss-like, with multiple flowers per cluster.

Rhododendron × paradoxum is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
