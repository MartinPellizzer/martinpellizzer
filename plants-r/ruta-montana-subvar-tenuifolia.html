Ruta montana subvar. tenuifolia

Ruta montana subvar. tenuifolia, commonly known as slender mountain rue, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smaller and more slender than those of the typical variety. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
