Rosa sherardii f. sacra

Rosa sherardii f. sacra, commonly known as the sacred rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is typically shrub-like, with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Native to the mountainous regions of central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine make it a notable botanical species.
