Rhododendron wilsoniae var. ionanthum

Rhododendron wilsoniae var. ionanthum, commonly known as the Wilson's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, specifically found in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white, blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments with acidic soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
