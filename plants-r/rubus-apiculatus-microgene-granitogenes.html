Rubus apiculatus microgene granitogenes

Rubus apiculatus microgene granitogenes, commonly known as the granitogenous raspberry, is a shrub native to specific regions with granitic soils.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It has trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
