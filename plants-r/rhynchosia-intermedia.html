Rhynchosia intermedia

Rhynchosia intermedia, commonly known as the African bean or wild bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. The plant bears small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Rhynchosia intermedia is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop.
