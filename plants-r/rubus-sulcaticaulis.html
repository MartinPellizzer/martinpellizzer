Rubus sulcaticaulis

Rubus sulcaticaulis, commonly known as the serrated blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the western United States, particularly California. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are similar to other Rubus species.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or coastal habitats.
