Ranunculus gmelinii var. yukonensis

Ranunculus gmelinii var. yukonensis, commonly known as Yukon Valley buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Yukon region of Canada.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, upright stem. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
