Rubus suberectus var. plicatus

Rubus suberectus var. plicatus, commonly known as the wrinkled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves.

It produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are typically smaller than those of other blackberry varieties. The plant is native to certain regions of North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is a shrub with thorny stems and is often found in woodland or hedgerow environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural study.
