Rosa canina subvar. leiostyla

Rosa canina subvar. leiostyla, commonly known as the Chinese rose or dog rose, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
