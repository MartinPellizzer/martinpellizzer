Rosa villosa var. gaillardii

Rosa villosa var. gaillardii, commonly known as the Gaillardii rose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
