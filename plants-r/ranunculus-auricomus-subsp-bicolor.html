Ranunculus auricomus subsp. bicolor

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive bicolor pattern, where the outer petals are yellow and the inner part is white or pale yellow.

The plant has trifoliate leaves that are palmately divided, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, stream banks, and open woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
