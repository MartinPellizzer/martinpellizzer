Roettlera pygmaea

Roettlera pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf roettlera, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with dense clusters of narrow, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Roettlera pygmaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
