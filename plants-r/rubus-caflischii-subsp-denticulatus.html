Rubus caflischii subsp. denticulatus

Rubus caflischii subsp.

denticulatus, commonly known as the denticulate raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or well-drained soils.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
