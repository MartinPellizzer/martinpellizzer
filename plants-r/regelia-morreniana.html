Regelia morreniana

Regelia morreniana, commonly known as the Morren's regelia, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant, often colorful blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Regelia morreniana thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
