Rosa canina var. glaucifolia

Rosa canina var. glaucifolia, commonly known as the glaucous rose, is a variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale greenish-white flowers and hairy stems. The leaves are typically pinnately compound with serrated edges. This plant is native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is often found in woodland areas and along hedgerows, thriving in well-drained soils.
