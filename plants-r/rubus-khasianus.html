Rubus khasianus

Rubus khasianus, commonly known as the Khasi blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Botanically, it is notable for its aggregate fruits and is an important species in regional biodiversity.
