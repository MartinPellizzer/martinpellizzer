Rhodostemonodaphne dioica

Rhodostemonodaphne dioica, commonly known as the mountain ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is dioecious, meaning it has separate male and female plants, which is a notable botanical feature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its wood is highly valued for its durability and is used in traditional craftsmanship and furniture making.
