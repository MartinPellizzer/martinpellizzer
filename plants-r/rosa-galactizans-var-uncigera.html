Rosa galactizans var. uncigera

Rosa galactizans var. uncigera, commonly known as the white rose of the Andes, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, thornless stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique beauty and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
