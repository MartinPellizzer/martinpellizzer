Rubus thyrsanthus

Rubus thyrsanthus, commonly known as the Chilean blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its berries are valued for their intense flavor and are used in culinary applications.
