Rhamnus cathartica var. hydriensis

Rhamnus cathartica var. hydriensis, commonly known as the Hydriensis buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
