Rubus rivularis var. weigelioides

Rubus rivularis var. weigelioides, commonly known as the Weigelioid raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, resembling raspberries in appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found along stream banks and in woodland areas.

This variant is characterized by its slender stems and is closely related to other species within the Rubus genus.
