Rubus universalis

Rubus universalis, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces edible berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit is highly valued for its flavor and is used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
