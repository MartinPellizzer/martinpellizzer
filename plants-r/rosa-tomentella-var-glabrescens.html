Rosa tomentella var. glabrescens

Rosa tomentella var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous hairy rose, is a variant of the hairy rose species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that becomes less prominent as the plant matures. The flowers are typically pink to white, with five petals and a strong, pleasant fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
