Rubus borbasiellus

Rubus borbasiellus, commonly known as the Bornean blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in dense undergrowth in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit, which is edible, has a flavor similar to raspberries and is used in local cuisines.
