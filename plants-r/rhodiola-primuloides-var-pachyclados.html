Rhodiola primuloides var. pachyclados

Rhodiola primuloides var. pachyclados, commonly known as the alpine rhodiola, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in harsh, cold climates.

Rhodiola primuloides var. pachyclados is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
