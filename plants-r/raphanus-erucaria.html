Raphanus erucaria

Raphanus erucaria, commonly known as the wild radish, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and broccoli. The plant features a robust taproot and basal leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and attract various pollinators.

Raphanus erucaria is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
