Rhipidoglossum rutilum

Rhipidoglossum rutilum, commonly known as the golden beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow to golden flowers, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in high-altitude grasslands.

Rhipidoglossum rutilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
