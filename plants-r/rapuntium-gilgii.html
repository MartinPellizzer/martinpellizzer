Rapuntium gilgii

Rapuntium gilgii, commonly known as the Gilgil cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a tall, spiny stem that can grow up to two meters in height, covered in dense, woolly hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are followed by round, edible fruits resembling tiny grapes. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments.

Due to its adaptations, it plays an important role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
