Rubus balfourianus

Rubus balfourianus, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in temperate and alpine climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
