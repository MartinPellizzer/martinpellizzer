Rosa tytthotricha

Rosa tytthotricha, commonly known as the rough-stemmed rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruits are rose hips, which are typically orange-red and contain achenes.

Rosa tytthotricha is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
