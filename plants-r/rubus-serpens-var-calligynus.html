Rubus serpens var. calligynus

Rubus serpens var. calligynus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a trailing shrub that produces small, dark red to black berries, which are typically harvested in the fall. The plant has trifoliate leaves and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important part of local ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
