Rhynchospora marginata

Rhynchospora marginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikelets, which are typically borne on elongated stems. Rhynchospora marginata thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
