Raspalia capitella

Raspalia capitella, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with short, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically blue to violet in color and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged in whorls around the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
