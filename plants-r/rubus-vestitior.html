Rubus vestitior

Rubus vestitior, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
