Rosa pimpinellifolia subsp. rubella

Rosa pimpinellifolia subsp.

rubella, commonly known as the red-stemmed dog rose, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer.

The leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and the plant produces red or orange hips in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for garden cultivation.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
