Rubus perambiguus

Rubus perambiguus, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring partial shade to full sun.

Its flowers are white to pale pink and are visited by various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
