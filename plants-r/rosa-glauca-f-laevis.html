Rosa glauca f. laevis

Rosa glauca f. laevis, commonly known as the smooth blue rose, is a unique cultivar of the Rosa glauca species.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, distinguishing it from other forms of the species. The flowers are typically pale blue or lavender in color, with a delicate, fragrant scent. This form is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

It is a compact shrub that prefers full sun and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
