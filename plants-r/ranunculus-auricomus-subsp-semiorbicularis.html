Ranunculus auricomus subsp. semiorbicularis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

semiorbicularis, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with five to seven petals.

The leaves are typically palmately lobed and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture. This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic sap.
