Rhododendron brachysiphon

Rhododendron brachysiphon, commonly known as the narrow-siphon rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from pink to deep red.

Rhododendron brachysiphon is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
