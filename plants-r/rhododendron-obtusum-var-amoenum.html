Rhododendron obtusum var. amoenum

Rhododendron obtusum var. amoenum, commonly known as the Amoena rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
