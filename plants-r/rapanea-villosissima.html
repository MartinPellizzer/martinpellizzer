Rapanea villosissima

Rapanea villosissima, commonly known as the hairy evergreen or hairy rapanea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
