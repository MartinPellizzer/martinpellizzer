Ranunculus spicatus subsp. fontqueri

Ranunculus spicatus subsp.

fontqueri, commonly known as the Fontqueri crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States. It features delicate, trifoliate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a short stem with a cluster of flowers at the tip, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and simple floral structure.
