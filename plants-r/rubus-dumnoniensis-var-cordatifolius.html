Rubus dumnoniensis var. cordatifolius

Rubus dumnoniensis var. cordatifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple achenes, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
