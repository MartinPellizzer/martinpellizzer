Ricinus macrocarpus proles sanguineus

Ricinus macrocarpus proles sanguineus, commonly known as the blood ricin plant, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and striking red or orange sap, which contains toxic compounds such as ricin. The plant is native to parts of Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
