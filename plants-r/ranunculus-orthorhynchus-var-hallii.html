Ranunculus orthorhynchus var. hallii

Ranunculus orthorhynchus var. hallii, commonly known as the Hall's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
