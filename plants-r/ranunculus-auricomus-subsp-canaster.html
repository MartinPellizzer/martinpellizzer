Ranunculus auricomus subsp. canaster

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

canaster, commonly known as the Canary Island buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Canary Islands. It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive, saucer-shaped petal that resembles a crown, giving it its common name.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, delicate blooms.
