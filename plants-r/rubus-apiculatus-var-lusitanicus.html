Rubus apiculatus var. lusitanicus

Rubus apiculatus var. lusitanicus, commonly known as the Lusitanian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in regional flora and horticultural interest.
