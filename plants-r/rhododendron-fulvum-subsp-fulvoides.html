Rhododendron fulvum subsp. fulvoides

Rhododendron fulvum subsp.

fulvoides, commonly known as the yellow laurel, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color.

The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at moderate to high elevations. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
