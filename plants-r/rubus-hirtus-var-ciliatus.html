Rubus hirtus var. ciliatus

Rubus hirtus var. ciliatus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
