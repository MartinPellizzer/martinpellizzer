Rubus strigosus var. eucyclus

Rubus strigosus var. eucyclus, commonly known as the red raspberry.

This variant is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year producing a basal rosette of leaves and the second year forming a flowering and fruiting stem. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated margins, and the plant is covered in fine, glandular hairs. It produces red, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are edible and commonly used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.
