Rubus tereticaulis var. peraculeatus

Rubus tereticaulis var. peraculeatus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub-like plant native to parts of North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential use in horticulture.
