Ranunculus fallax subsp. altior

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

altior, commonly known as the tall meadow buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

It is known for its early spring blooming habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
