Ribes uva-crispa var. glanduligerum

Ribes uva-crispa var. glanduligerum, commonly known as the glandular gooseberry, is a variant of the European gooseberry.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and small, green, edible berries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually pink or white.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in culinary and medicinal applications.
