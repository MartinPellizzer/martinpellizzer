Rosa canina var. avelloi

Rosa canina var. avelloi, commonly known as the Alaskan rose, is a variant of the dog rose native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in open woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. Its hips, which are orange-red and slightly hairy, contain numerous seeds and are often used in herbal remedies.

The leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant has a thorny stem that provides structural support.
