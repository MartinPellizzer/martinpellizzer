Rubus croceacanthus var. glandulosocalycinus

Rubus croceacanthus var. glandulosocalycinus, commonly known as the glandular-stemmed blackberry variant, is a shrub native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant produces edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically serrated with a hairy texture, and the flowers are white or pale yellow.

This variant is notable for its unique combination of morphological traits and ecological adaptability.
