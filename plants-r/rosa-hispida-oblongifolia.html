Rosa hispida oblongifolia

Rosa hispida oblongifolia, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, thorny stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shrublands and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
