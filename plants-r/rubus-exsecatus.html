Rubus exsecatus

Rubus exsecatus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many other species of brambles and blackberries.
