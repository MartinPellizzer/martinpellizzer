Richea desgrazii

Richea desgrazii, commonly known as the Desert Richea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, shrub-like species that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually small, fleshy, and adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments.

Richea desgrazii is known for its resilience to drought and is often found in areas with limited rainfall.
