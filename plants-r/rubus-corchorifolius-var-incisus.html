Rubus corchorifolius var. incisus

Rubus corchorifolius var. incisus, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a variant of the brambles native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that are typically small and firm. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with a serrated edge, which is reflected in its scientific name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
