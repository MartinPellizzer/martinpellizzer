Rosa carolina var. deamii

Rosa carolina var. deamii, commonly known as the Deam's wild rose, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features thorny stems and pinnate leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in the spring, followed by red-orange hips in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
