Rubus hirtus var. crinitiformis

Rubus hirtus var. crinitiformis, commonly known as the hairy bramble or curly-haired raspberry, is a variant of the bramble family with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its growth habit includes thorny stems and a sprawling or shrub-like form.
