Rosa afzeliana f. schemnitzensis

Rosa afzeliana f. schemnitzensis, commonly known as the Schemnitzensis rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa afzeliana.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse flowering, typically producing clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant is native to Central and Eastern Europe, thriving in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This form is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various garden conditions.
