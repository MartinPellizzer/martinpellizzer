Rosa × dumetorum var. comatoides

Rosa × dumetorum var. comatoides, commonly known as the woolly dog rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly grey-green foliage and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters and has thorny stems, which are a common feature in many rose species. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with 5–7 leaflets that are oval to lance-shaped.

This variety is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
