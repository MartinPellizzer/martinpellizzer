Rosa × dumetorum f. collina

Rosa × dumetorum f. collina, commonly known as the Alpine rose or creeping rose, is a polyploid hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant typically features dark green, glossy leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. It is well adapted to harsh climates, with a compact growth form that helps it withstand wind and cold.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
