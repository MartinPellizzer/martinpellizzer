Rhabdodendron longifolium

Rhabdodendron longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved rhabdodendron, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
