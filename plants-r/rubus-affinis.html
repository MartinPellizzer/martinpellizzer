Rubus affinis

Rubus affinis, commonly known as the common blackberry bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Rubus affinis is an important species for both wildlife and traditional herbal uses.
