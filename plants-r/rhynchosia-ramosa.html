Rhynchosia ramosa

Rhynchosia ramosa, commonly known as the branching cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Rhynchosia ramosa is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in various parts of the world.
