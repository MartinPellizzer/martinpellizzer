Rhododendron megacalyx

Rhododendron megacalyx, commonly known as the giant leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
