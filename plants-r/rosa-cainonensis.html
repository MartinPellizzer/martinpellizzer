Rosa cainonensis

Rosa cainonensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical characteristics include simple, alternate leaves and an erect growth habit, making it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.

Rosa cainonensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
