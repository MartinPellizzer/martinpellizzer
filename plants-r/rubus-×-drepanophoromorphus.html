Rubus × drepanophoromorphus

Rubus × drepanophoromorphus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (the cloudberry). The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, acidic soils.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular in culinary and commercial applications.
