Rudgea justicioides

Rudgea justicioides, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false poinsettia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, which are followed by bright red bracts that resemble petals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along stream banks.

Rudgea justicioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
