Rhododendron sanguineum subsp. sanguineoides

Rhododendron sanguineum subsp.

sanguineoides, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States. It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, depending on the cultivar. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is heightened by its early spring blooming period.
