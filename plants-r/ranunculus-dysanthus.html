Ranunculus dysanthus

Ranunculus dysanthus, commonly known as the Indian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Himalayas.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals and a distinctive yellow center. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated primarily by insects.
