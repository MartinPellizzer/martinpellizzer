Rubus exter

Rubus exter, commonly known as the coastal bramble, is a shrub native to temperate coastal regions.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime climates.

Rubus exter plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
