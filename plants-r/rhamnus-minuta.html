Rhamnus minuta

Rhamnus minuta, commonly known as the dwarf buckthorn, is a small shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, followed by small, black berries that are a food source for birds.

Rhamnus minuta is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
