Rhododendron × hemigymnum

Rhododendron × hemigymnum, commonly known as the hybrid rhododendron, is a species resulting from the cross between Rhododendron ferrugineum and Rhododendron caucasicum.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with acidic, well-drained soils, preferring cool and moist environments. Its botanical features include evergreen leaves that turn reddish in autumn and a compact growth habit that makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a specimen plant.

The hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific ecological niches.
