Rubus tonkinensis

Rubus tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a serrated edge.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
