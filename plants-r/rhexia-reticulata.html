Rhexia reticulata

Rhexia reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined rhexia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive net-like venation pattern.

Rhexia reticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
