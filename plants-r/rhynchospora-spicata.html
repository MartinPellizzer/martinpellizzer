Rhynchospora spicata

Rhynchospora spicata, commonly known as spike rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and small, clustered spikelets at the tips. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are inconspicuous, consisting of three stamens and a single pistil, and it relies on wind pollination.

Rhynchospora spicata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
