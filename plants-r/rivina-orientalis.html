Rivina orientalis

Rivina orientalis, commonly known as the redroot false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening due to their attractive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Rivina orientalis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
