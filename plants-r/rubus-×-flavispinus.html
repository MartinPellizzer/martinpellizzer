Rubus × flavispinus

Rubus × flavispinus, commonly known as the yellow-tipped blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color, which are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
