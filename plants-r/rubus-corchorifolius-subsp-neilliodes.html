Rubus corchorifolius subsp. neilliodes

Rubus corchorifolius subsp.

neilliodes, commonly known as the Chinese bramble, is a shrub native to eastern Asia. It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant bears red or black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
