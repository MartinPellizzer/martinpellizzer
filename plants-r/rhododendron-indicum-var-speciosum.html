Rhododendron indicum var. speciosum

Rhododendron indicum var. speciosum, commonly known as the Indian rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, broad leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, bloom in spring and are often fragrant. The plant thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
