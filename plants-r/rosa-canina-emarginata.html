Rosa canina emarginata

Rosa canina emarginata, commonly known as the marginal dogwood rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, pink to red flowers in the spring. The plant has serrated leaves and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is often found in woodland edges and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

The fruit, a red-hued berry, is edible and contains small seeds.
