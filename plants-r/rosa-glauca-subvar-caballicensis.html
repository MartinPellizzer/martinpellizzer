Rosa glauca subvar. caballicensis

Rosa glauca subvar. caballicensis, commonly known as the Caballic bluebush rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse flowering, with deep blue to violet petals that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
