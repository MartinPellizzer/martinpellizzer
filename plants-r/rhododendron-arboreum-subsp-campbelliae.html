Rhododendron arboreum subsp. campbelliae

Rhododendron arboreum subsp.

campbelliae, commonly known as Campbell's rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high elevations. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leathery leaves, which help it adapt to its mountainous habitat.

It is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
