Rubus × indecorus

Rubus × indecorus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small white flowers in the spring.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
