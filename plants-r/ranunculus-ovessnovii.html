Ranunculus ovessnovii

Ranunculus ovessnovii, commonly known as the Khabarovsk buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and stream sides. It has palmately lobed leaves and is known for its ornamental value.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
