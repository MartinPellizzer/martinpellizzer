Ranunculus termei

Ranunculus termei, commonly known as the Termoli buttercup, is a rare and endangered species native to the Dolomites in Italy.

It is a herbaceous perennial with distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves and is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped petals. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
