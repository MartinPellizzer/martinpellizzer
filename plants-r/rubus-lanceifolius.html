Rubus lanceifolius

Rubus lanceifolius, commonly known as the lanceleaf blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are lance-shaped and have a distinctive pointed tip. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, hairy achenes, typically ranging in color from red to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
