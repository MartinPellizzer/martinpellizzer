Rivina humilis var. plumbaginifolia

Rivina humilis var. plumbaginifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub that produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers throughout the summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
