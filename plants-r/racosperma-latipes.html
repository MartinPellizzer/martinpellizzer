Racosperma latipes

Racosperma latipes, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by narrow, legume-like pods containing seeds.

Racosperma latipes is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
