Rubus nemorosus var. grandifrons

Rubus nemorosus var. grandifrons, commonly known as the large-fruited bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. These fruits are often eaten by birds and small mammals, aiding in seed dispersal.

It thrives in woodland edges and moist soils, showcasing its adaptability to various habitats.
