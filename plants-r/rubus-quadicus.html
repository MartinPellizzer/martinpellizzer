Rubus quadicus

Rubus quadicus, commonly known as the four-leaf raspberry, is a perennial shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically with four lobes, and produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas, where it spreads through rhizomes.

Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it blooms in early summer.
