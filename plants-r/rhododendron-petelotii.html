Rhododendron petelotii

Rhododendron petelotii, commonly known as the Petelot's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from pink to red.

Rhododendron petelotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with moist, well-drained soil.
