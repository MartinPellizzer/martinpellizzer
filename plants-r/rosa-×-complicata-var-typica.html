Rosa × complicata var. typica

Rosa × complicata var. typica, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, thornless stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant is native to China and has been cultivated for centuries for its ornamental value. Its foliage consists of compound leaves with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant, five-petaled blooms.

Rosa × complicata var. typica is often grown in gardens for its beauty and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
