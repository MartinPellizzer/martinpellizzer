Ranunculus deformis

Ranunculus deformis, commonly known as the twisted buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are usually unbranched and can grow up to 30 centimeters in height.

The species is characterized by its unique, twisted floral structure, which gives it its common name.
