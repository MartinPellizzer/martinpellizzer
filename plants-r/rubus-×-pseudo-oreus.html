Rubus × pseudo-oreus

Rubus × pseudo-oreus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a vigorous shrub native to the western United States.

It exhibits characteristics of both blackberries and raspberries, with thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Botanically, it is a tetraploid species resulting from crossbreeding between Rubus ursinus and Rubus istrifolius.
