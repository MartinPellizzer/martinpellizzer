Rubus echinoides

Rubus echinoides, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
