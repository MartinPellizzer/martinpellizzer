Ranunculus concinnus

Ranunculus concinnus, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow or white. The plant grows from a rhizomatous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with a serrated edge, and it often blooms from late spring to early summer.

Ranunculus concinnus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
