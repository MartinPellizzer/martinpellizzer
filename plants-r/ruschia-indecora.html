Ruschia indecora

Ruschia indecora, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, spiky red bracts that resemble flames, giving it its common name. The plant has fleshy, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green or blue-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Ruschia indecora is relatively low maintenance and drought-tolerant, suitable for USDA hardiness zones 8 through 10.
