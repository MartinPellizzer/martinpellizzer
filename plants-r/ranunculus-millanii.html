Ranunculus millanii

Ranunculus millanii, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers can range in color from yellow to orange.

Ranunculus millanii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
