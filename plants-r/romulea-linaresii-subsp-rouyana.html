Romulea linaresii subsp. rouyana

Romulea linaresii subsp.

rouyana, commonly known as the Linares' romulea, is a perennial geophyte native to South Africa. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous underground stem and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among botanists and horticulturists.
