Ruellia brittoniana

Ruellia brittoniana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, medium-green leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. Ruellia brittoniana is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
