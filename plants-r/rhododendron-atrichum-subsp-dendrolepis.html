Rhododendron atrichum subsp. dendrolepis

Rhododendron atrichum subsp.

dendrolepis, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in the Himalayas. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, shaded environments.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy coating. Its flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
