Rubus guentheri var. squarrosus

Rubus guentheri var. squarrosus, commonly known as the squarrose blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small flowers. The leaves are typically palmately compound with serrated margins and a hairy underside. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or open areas.
