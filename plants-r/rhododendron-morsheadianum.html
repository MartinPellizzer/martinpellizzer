Rhododendron morsheadianum

Rhododendron morsheadianum, commonly known as the Morshead's rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne in clusters. The plant has elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green and arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions with acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its striking floral display.
