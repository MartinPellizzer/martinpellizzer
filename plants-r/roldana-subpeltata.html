Roldana subpeltata

Roldana subpeltata, commonly known as the velvetleaf.

This plant is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.
