Ruellia lanatoviscosa

Ruellia lanatoviscosa, commonly known as woolly ruellia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Ruellia lanatoviscosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
