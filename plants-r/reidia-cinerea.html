Reidia cinerea

Reidia cinerea, commonly known as the gray reidia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils. The plant features slender stems and small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving them a grayish appearance.

Reidia cinerea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
