Rhododendron yanthinum var. lepidanthum

Rhododendron yanthinum var. lepidanthum, commonly known as the silver-leaved rhododendron, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, silver-green foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant prefers acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its unique leaf coloration makes it a sought-after specimen in botanical gardens and specialized collections.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
