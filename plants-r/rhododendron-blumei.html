Rhododendron blumei

Rhododendron blumei, commonly known as the Blume's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often fragrant.

Rhododendron blumei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
