Ranunculus fallax subsp. ornatus

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

ornatus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow to orange, and a distinctive yellow center.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
