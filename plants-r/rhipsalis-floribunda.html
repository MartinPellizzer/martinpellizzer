Rhipsalis floribunda

Rhipsalis floribunda, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Brazil.

It features segmented, leafless stems that are often branched and covered with small, white to grayish hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming during the holiday season. It prefers indirect light, moderate temperatures, and consistent moisture in its soil.

Its unique appearance and seasonal flowering make it a popular ornamental plant in homes and gardens.
