Rubus piletocarpus

Rubus piletocarpus, commonly known as the blackberry cane or trailing blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and blackberries.
