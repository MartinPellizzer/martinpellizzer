Rubus rhodiopetalus

Rubus rhodiopetalus, commonly known as the Rhodopetalus blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit, a small, dark purple berry, is edible but typically has a tart flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.
