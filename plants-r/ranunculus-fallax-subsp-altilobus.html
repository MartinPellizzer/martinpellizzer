Ranunculus fallax subsp. altilobus

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

altilobus, commonly known as the tall-lipped buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy yellow flowers with five petals, often adorned with a distinctive dark central spot.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The subspecies is distinguished by its taller stature and more elongated petals compared to other members of the Ranunculus fallax complex.
