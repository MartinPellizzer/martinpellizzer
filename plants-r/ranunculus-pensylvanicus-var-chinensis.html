Ranunculus pensylvanicus var. chinensis

Ranunculus pensylvanicus var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often accompanied by a central cluster of small, yellow stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive, serrated margin.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, spring-blooming flowers.
