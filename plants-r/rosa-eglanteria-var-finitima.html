Rosa eglanteria var. finitima

Rosa eglanteria var. finitima, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically pale pink to white, with a distinctive double bloom. The plant produces oval, yellowish fruits known as rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and autumn foliage color.
