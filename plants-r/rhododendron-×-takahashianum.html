Rhododendron × takahashianum

Rhododendron × takahashianum, commonly known as the Takahashian rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron mucronulatum and Rhododendron yakushimanum.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and the vibrant seasonal display of its blooms.
