Rubus thyrsiflorus

Rubus thyrsiflorus, commonly known as the thrysoflora raspberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect or shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant's leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets that are serrated along the edges. Its fruits, which are aggregate berries, are usually red or black when mature and are edible.

Rubus thyrsiflorus is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
