Rubus tomentosus f. calvescens

Rubus tomentosus f. calvescens, commonly known as the woolly blackberry.

This plant is a flowering shrub native to parts of North America, characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and thorny stems. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, white pubescence.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
