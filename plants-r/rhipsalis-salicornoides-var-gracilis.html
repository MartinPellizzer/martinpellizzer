Rhipsalis salicornoides var. gracilis

Rhipsalis salicornoides var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender mistletoe cactus, is a unique epiphytic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairless stems that hang in clusters, often resembling a misty cascade. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its appearance and growth habits make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
