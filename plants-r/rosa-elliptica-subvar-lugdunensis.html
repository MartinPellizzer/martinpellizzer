Rosa elliptica subvar. lugdunensis

Rosa elliptica subvar. lugdunensis, commonly known as the French rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe.

It features upright, thorny stems and produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring. The leaves are elliptical, with a serrated margin and a glossy dark green upper surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
