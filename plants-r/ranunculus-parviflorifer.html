Ranunculus parviflorifer

Ranunculus parviflorifer, commonly known as the small-flowered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow or white. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
