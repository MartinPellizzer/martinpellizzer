Rubus newbridgensis

Rubus newbridgensis, commonly known as the Newbridge blackberry, is a species of bramble native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas and along riverbanks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
