Rinorea passoura var. andersonii

Rinorea passoura var. andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's rinorea, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers with five petals, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
