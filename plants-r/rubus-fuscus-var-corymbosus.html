Rubus fuscus var. corymbosus

Rubus fuscus var. corymbosus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers in corymbs, which are followed by edible blackberries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its fruit and its role in supporting local wildlife.
