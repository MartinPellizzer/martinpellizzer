Rosa humilis var. villosa

Rosa humilis var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy shrub rose, is a low-growing, deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in early summer. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and hairy foliage, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
