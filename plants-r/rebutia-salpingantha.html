Rebutia salpingantha

Rebutia salpingantha, commonly known as the "Coral Cactus," is a species of flowering cactus native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white to grayish spines. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. Its compact size and attractive appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation.

Rebutia salpingantha is well adapted to arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sunlight to thrive.
