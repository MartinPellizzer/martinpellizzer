Rosa tomentella var. pilosior

Rosa tomentella var. pilosior, commonly known as the woolly shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy gray foliage and small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a compact growth habit, with multiple stems covered in a thick layer of white to gray pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique texture and seasonal color changes.
