Ruppia transsilvanica

Ruppia transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian water plantain, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and ponds. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette formation. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are pollinated by insects.

Ruppia transsilvanica plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
