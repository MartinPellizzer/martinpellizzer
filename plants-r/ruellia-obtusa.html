Ruellia obtusa

Ruellia obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the warm regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, typically blooming from summer to fall. Ruellia obtusa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is relatively easy to care for in gardens.
