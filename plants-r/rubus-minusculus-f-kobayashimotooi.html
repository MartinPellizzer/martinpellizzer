Rubus minusculus f. kobayashimotooi

Rubus minusculus f. kobayashimotooi, commonly known as the Kobayashimoto's dwarf raspberry, is a compact shrub native to Japan.

It features small, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest in plant taxonomy.
