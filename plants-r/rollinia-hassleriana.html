Rollinia hassleriana

Rollinia hassleriana, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, waxy fruits that turn red when ripe. Its fruits are edible, with a sweet, aromatic pulp and a single large seed, making it a valuable species in both horticulture and local cuisines.

Rollinia hassleriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in warm climates.
