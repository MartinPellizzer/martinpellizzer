Rubus nemorivagus

Rubus nemorivagus, commonly known as the swamp blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and blackberries. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, thorny stems, and aggregate fruits that mature into dark purple or black berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
