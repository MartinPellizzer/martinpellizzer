Rhodiola forrestii

Rhodiola forrestii, commonly known as the Tibetan rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low oxygen levels. It is often found in rocky soils and alpine meadows at elevations above 3,000 meters.

Rhodiola forrestii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported adaptogenic and antioxidant benefits.
