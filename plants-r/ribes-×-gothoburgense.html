Ribes × gothoburgense

Ribes × gothoburgense, commonly known as the hybrid gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub originating from crosses between Ribes nigrum and Ribes rubrum.

It typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, featuring arching branches and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Ribes × gothoburgense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its edible berries and winter interest.
