Ranunculus adoneus var. caespitosus

Ranunculus adoneus var. caespitosus, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with numerous small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges.

The species is characterized by its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, forming dense colonies.
