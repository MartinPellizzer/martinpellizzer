Ruellia lanatoglandulosa

Ruellia lanatoglandulosa, commonly known as the woolly glandular ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, whitish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Ruellia lanatoglandulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
