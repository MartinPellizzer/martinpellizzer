Rubus furvus var. hodophilus

Rubus furvus var. hodophilus, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a shrub native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
