Rosa stylosa nuda

Rosa stylosa nuda, commonly known as the naked-stemmed rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, often leafless stems and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. Its leaves are alternate, with serrated edges, and it produces rose-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rosa stylosa nuda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
