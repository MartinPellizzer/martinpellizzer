Rebutia gracilispina

Rebutia gracilispina, commonly known as the slender-spined rebutia, is a succulent native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and spiny, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Rebutia gracilispina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
