Rhododendron viscistylum var. hyugaense

Rhododendron viscistylum var. hyugaense, commonly known as the Hyuga Rhododendron, is a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, preferring acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
