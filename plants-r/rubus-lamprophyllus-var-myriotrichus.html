Rubus lamprophyllus var. myriotrichus

Rubus lamprophyllus var. myriotrichus, commonly known as the silky-leaf blackberry, is a species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and edible. The plant has compound leaves with a silvery-white underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in local culinary traditions.
