Rorippa aspera var. boissieri

Rorippa aspera var. boissieri, commonly known as the rough watercress, is a variant of the watercress species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters, producing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist or aquatic environments, often found along riverbanks and in wet meadows.

The plant is valued for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.
