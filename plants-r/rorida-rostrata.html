Rorida rostrata

Rorida rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated daisies, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Rorida rostrata is known for its compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a bedding plant.

Its blooming period extends from spring to early summer, adding a charming floral display to gardens.
