Rubus sericatus

Rubus sericatus, commonly known as the silky blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and dark purple to black fruits that are typically small and tart. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has a dense growth habit. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The berries are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
