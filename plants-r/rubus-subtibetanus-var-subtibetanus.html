Rubus subtibetanus var. subtibetanus

Rubus subtibetanus var. subtibetanus, commonly known as the Tibetan blackberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
