Rosa galushkoi

Rosa galushkoi, commonly known as the Galushko rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Rosa galushkoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
