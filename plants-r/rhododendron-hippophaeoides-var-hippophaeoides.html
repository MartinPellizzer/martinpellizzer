Rhododendron hippophaeoides var. hippophaeoides

Rhododendron hippophaeoides var. hippophaeoides, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. It is often found in forested areas and is adapted to cool, humid climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable ornamental plant in horticulture.
