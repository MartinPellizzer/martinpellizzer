Rosa moschata var. rosea

Rosa moschata var. rosea, commonly known as the sweet cherry rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa moschata species, which is native to the Caucasus region.

This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are dark green and glossy, with a slightly serrated edge. It produces small, red, edible fruits known as cherries, which are prized for their sweet flavor and use in culinary applications.

The plant is well-suited for container gardening and can thrive in a variety of well-drained soils.
