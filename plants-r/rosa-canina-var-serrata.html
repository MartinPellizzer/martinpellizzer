Rosa canina var. serrata

Rosa canina var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated dog rose, is a variant of the common dog rose, characterized by its serrated leaf margins and thorny stems.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in woodland edges and hedgerows. The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in spring, which are followed by orange-red hips in autumn. Its leaves are pinnate, with 5 to 7 leaflets, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

Rosa canina var. serrata is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
