Rosa sambucina var. mushaniana

Rosa sambucina var. mushaniana, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple hue at the base. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are often armed with thorns. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental addition to gardens.

The flowers are typically produced in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate beauty to the landscape.
