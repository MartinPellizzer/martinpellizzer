Rubus violaceus

Rubus violaceus, commonly known as the purple raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, edible berries that are typically a deep purple or black color. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit is often used in jams and desserts due to its tart flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
