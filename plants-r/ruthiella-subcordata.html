Ruthiella subcordata

Ruthiella subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight curl at the edges.

Ruthiella subcordata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
