Rosa xanthina var. ecae

Rosa xanthina var. ecae, commonly known as the yellow rose of Texas, is a deciduous shrub native to the Southwestern United States.

It features fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright, thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in arid regions.
