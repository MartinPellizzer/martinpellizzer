Rosa macrophylla f. parceglandulosa

Rosa macrophylla f. parceglandulosa, commonly known as the Paracerasian rose, is a cultivar of the shrub rose native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant has large, leathery leaves with a glossy surface and prominent veins. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This variety is also noted for its hardy nature and ability to produce abundant blooms throughout the growing season.
