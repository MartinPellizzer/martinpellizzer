Rorippa occidentalis

Rorippa occidentalis, commonly known as Western watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is an aquatic or semi-aquatic species that typically grows in wetlands, stream margins, and other moist environments. The plant has small, alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in racemes, and it produces small, round pods containing several seeds.

Rorippa occidentalis is valued for its edible leaves and is sometimes cultivated as a leafy vegetable.
