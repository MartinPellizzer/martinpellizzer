Rubus compactiflorus

Rubus compactiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many other brambles and blackberry species.
