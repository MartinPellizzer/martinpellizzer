Rosa joadi

Rosa joadi, commonly known as the Joadi rose, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to specific regions of the southwestern United States.

It features fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has woody stems covered in thorny projections, typical of many rose species. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets.

Rosa joadi thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping in arid landscapes.
