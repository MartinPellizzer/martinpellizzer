Rubus granulatus microgene lacroixii

Rubus granulatus microgene lacroixii, commonly known as the granular blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are serrated and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are pinkish-white with five petals.

The fruit, though not widely cultivated, is used in local traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
