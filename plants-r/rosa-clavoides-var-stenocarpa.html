Rosa clavoides var. stenocarpa

Rosa clavoides var. stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink to red, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as hips, which are characteristic of the species. It is well adapted to humid climates and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in woodland settings.
