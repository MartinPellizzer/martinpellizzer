Rosa velutiniflora f. dearmata

Rosa velutiniflora f. dearmata, commonly known as the velvetleaf rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa velutiniflora.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and compound leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The subspecies is notable for its unique foliage texture and vibrant floral display.
