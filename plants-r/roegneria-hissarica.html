Roegneria hissarica

Roegneria hissarica, commonly known as the Hissar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

Roegneria hissarica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
