Rubus ulmifolius f. dispalatus

Rubus ulmifolius f. dispalatus, commonly known as the white-berried bramble, is a variant of the common bramble, characterized by its pale or white fruits.

It is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in woodland edges and hedgerows. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually pink to white in color. It produces edible berries, though they are generally less flavorful than those of the main species.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
