Rosa olympica

Rosa olympica, commonly known as the Olympic rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans and the Caucasus. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has thorny stems.

Rosa olympica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual appeal.
