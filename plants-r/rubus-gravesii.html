Rubus gravesii

Rubus gravesii, commonly known as the Sierra blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. The plant is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or open woodland areas. It produces small, dark berries that are edible but have a tart flavor.

Its botanical features include compound leaves and thorny stems, which are characteristic of many Rubus species.
