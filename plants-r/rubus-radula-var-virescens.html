Rubus radula var. virescens

Rubus radula var. virescens, commonly known as the green-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that often have a greenish tinge when mature. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets. Its leaves are compound, with three leaflets that are usually serrated along the edges.

The flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters, and the plant produces edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications.
