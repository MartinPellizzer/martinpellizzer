Rosa agrestis var. lasiopoda

Rosa agrestis var. lasiopoda, commonly known as the rough-stemmed wild rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems covered in a rough, glandular layer. The leaves are compound, consisting of 5 to 7 elliptical leaflets with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces oval, red-orange hips that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.
