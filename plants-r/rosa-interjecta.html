Rosa interjecta

Rosa interjecta, commonly known as the "interrupted rose," is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and is known for its hardy nature, thriving in cooler climates. Rosa interjecta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively resistant to diseases.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to gardens and botanical collections.
