Rubus banningii

Rubus banningii, commonly known as the Banning's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus banningii is often found in shrublands and along riverbanks, contributing to local biodiversity.
