Rudbeckia hirta f. pleniflora

Rudbeckia hirta f. pleniflora, commonly known as the "garden coneflower," is a hybrid form of the common coneflower.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to North America, characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow-orange flower heads. The plant features large, daisy-like blooms with prominent central cones and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
