Rosa agrestis subsp. abscondita

Rosa agrestis subsp.

abscondita, commonly known as the hidden field rose, is a subspecies native to certain regions of Europe. It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many rose species.

The flowers are generally pink to red in color, with five petals and numerous stamens. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
