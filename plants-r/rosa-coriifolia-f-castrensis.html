Rosa coriifolia f. castrensis

Rosa coriifolia f. castrensis, commonly known as the Castren's shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, thorny stems and small, glossy, dark green leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers in early summer, which are followed by small, red-hued hips. It is characterized by its ornamental value and adaptability to rocky or well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its hardiness and distinctive floral display.
