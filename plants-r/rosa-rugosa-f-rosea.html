Rosa rugosa f. rosea

Rosa rugosa f. rosea, commonly known as the pink form of the rough rose, is a hybrid shrub native to coastal regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its rugged, thorny stems and dense, bushy growth habit. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range from pale pink to deep rose in color. Its foliage is dark green and slightly serrated, providing a striking contrast to the blooms.

Rosa rugosa f. rosea is valued for its hardiness, adaptability, and ornamental appeal in gardens.
