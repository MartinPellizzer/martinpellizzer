Ruellia glaucescens

Ruellia glaucescens, commonly known as blue ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ruellia glaucescens is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
