Rosa × centifolia var. lemeunierana

Rosa × centifolia var. lemeunierana, commonly known as the Lemeunier's hundred petals rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from France.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that typically have 50 to 100 petals, giving it a full, fluffy appearance. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
