Rhipsalis hadrosoma

Rhipsalis hadrosoma, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers that bloom during the holiday season. The plant has a unique structure with leaf-like stems that are segmented and can branch out profusely. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Rhipsalis hadrosoma is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
