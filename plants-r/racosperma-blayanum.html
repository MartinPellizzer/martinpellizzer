Racosperma blayanum

Racosperma blayanum, commonly known as the Blayana acacia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow floral spikes. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Racosperma. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought resistance through its deep root system and waxy leaf surfaces.

This species plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
