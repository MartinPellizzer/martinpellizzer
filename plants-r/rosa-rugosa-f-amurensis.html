Rosa rugosa f. amurensis

Rosa rugosa f. amurensis, commonly known as the Amur rose, is a shrub-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its rugged, thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its hips, which are orange-red berries, are edible and often used in jams and teas.

Rosa rugosa f. amurensis is valued for its hardiness and ornamental qualities in gardens.
