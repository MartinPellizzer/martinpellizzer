Rosa glauca var. subnitida

Rosa glauca var. subnitida, commonly known as the silverleaf rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are elliptical, with a silvery-blue underside that gives the plant its distinctive appearance. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and modest flowering.

This variety is relatively hardy and prefers well-drained soil and full sun.
