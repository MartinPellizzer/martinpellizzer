Rhus vernicifera var. silvestrii

Rhus vernicifera var. silvestrii, commonly known as the Chinese lac tree, is a variant of the lac tree native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in forested areas and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for producing a resin known as lac, which has been historically used in various industries, including varnishing and traditional medicine. Its botanical features include smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
