Rosa multiflora var. nanningensis

Rosa multiflora var. nanningensis, commonly known as the Nanning rose, is a variant of the multiflora rose native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are often covered in short, glandular hairs. It is well adapted to temperate climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not properly managed.
