Rollandia lanceolata var. tomentosa

Rollandia lanceolata var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly lanceleaf plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, woolly coating of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, often with a slight reddish tint. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
