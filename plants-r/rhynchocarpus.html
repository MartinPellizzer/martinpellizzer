Rhynchocarpus

Rhynchocarpus, commonly known as the horned bean, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curved pods that often resemble horns, giving the plant its common name. The leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and the flowers are usually small and clustered. Rhynchocarpus species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

These plants are known for their ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making them valuable in agricultural settings.
