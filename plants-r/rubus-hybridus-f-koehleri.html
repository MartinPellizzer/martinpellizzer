Rubus hybridus f. koehleri

Rubus hybridus f. koehleri, commonly known as the Koehleri raspberry, is a compact shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant features thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is often grown for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
