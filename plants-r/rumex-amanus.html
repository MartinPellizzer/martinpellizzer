Rumex amanus

Rumex amanus, commonly known as the mountain sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically red or purple in color, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants like buckwheat and sorrel.

Rumex amanus is often found in rocky or mountainous regions where it thrives in well-drained soils.
