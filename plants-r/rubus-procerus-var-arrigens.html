Rubus procerus var. arrigens

Rubus procerus var. arrigens, commonly known as the Alaska blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and Canada.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in forested areas.

The botanical features of this variety make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
