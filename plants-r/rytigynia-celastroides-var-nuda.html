Rytigynia celastroides var. nuda

Rytigynia celastroides var. nuda, commonly known as the naked rytigynia, is a species native to the rainforests of northern Australia.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in understorey habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecological diversity in its native range.
