Rubus imbricatus var. hamulosus

Rubus imbricatus var. hamulosus, commonly known as the hooked blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in moist, woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
