Racosperma conjunctifolium

Racosperma conjunctifolium, commonly known as the clustered leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in clusters. It produces yellow flowers in spikes, which are typical of the genus Racosperma.

This species is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
