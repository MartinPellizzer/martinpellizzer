Ryparosa amplifolia

Ryparosa amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed ryparosa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer months. Its root system is fibrous and dense, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Ryparosa amplifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
