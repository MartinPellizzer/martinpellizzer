Robinia pseudoacacia var. pendula

Robinia pseudoacacia var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping false acacia, is a striking ornamental tree characterized by its cascading branches and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers.

It is a cultivar of the honeylocust tree, native to the southeastern United States, and is valued for its unique, pendulous growth habit. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are compound and often turn a striking yellow in autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.

The flowers appear in late spring and are followed by long, slender pods that contain seeds.
