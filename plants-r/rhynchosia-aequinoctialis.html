Rhynchosia aequinoctialis

Rhynchosia aequinoctialis, commonly known as the equinox bean, is a tropical legume native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobia bacteria.
