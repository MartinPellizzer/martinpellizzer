Ribes giraldii var. giraldii

Ribes giraldii var. giraldii, commonly known as the Sierra gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a bright red, edible berry that ripens in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
