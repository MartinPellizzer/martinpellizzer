Ranunculus diffusus f. mollis

Ranunculus diffusus f. mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves that are often described as soft to the touch, giving it its specific epithet "mollis.". It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental member of the Ranunculaceae family.
