Rubus lentiginosus subsp. cambricus

Rubus lentiginosus subsp.

cambricus, commonly known as the British blackberry, is a shrub native to the British Isles. It produces edible berries that are typically small, dark purple, and have a tart flavor.

The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its flowers are white with five petals and appear in early summer.
