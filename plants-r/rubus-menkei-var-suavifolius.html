Rubus menkei var. suavifolius

Rubus menkei var. suavifolius, commonly known as the sweetleaf raspberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, aromatic leaves that emit a pleasant fragrance when crushed. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, often consumed by wildlife and humans alike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its hirsute stems and trifoliate leaves, which contribute to its ecological and ornamental value.
