Rosa sericea f. aculeata

Rosa sericea f. aculeata, commonly known as the silvery rose or silverleaf rose, is a variant of the silvery rose species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves and the presence of stiff, bristle-like hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dense and often used in ornamental gardening for its visual appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and low-maintenance plant.
