Rhamnus oleoides f. crenulata

Rhamnus oleoides f. crenulata, commonly known as the crested buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, dark green leaves with a distinctive crenulated margin. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in coastal environments.
