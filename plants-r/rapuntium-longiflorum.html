Rapuntium longiflorum

Rapuntium longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered rapunculus, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a thick, fleshy root that is often cultivated for its edible qualities. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of hair.

The species is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
