Rubus hirtus var. aciculifolius

Rubus hirtus var. aciculifolius, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are narrowly elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
