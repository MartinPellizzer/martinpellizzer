Rubus granulatus var. virgultorum

Rubus granulatus var. virgultorum, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a common species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
