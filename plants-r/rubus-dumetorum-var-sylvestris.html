Rubus dumetorum var. sylvestris

Rubus dumetorum var. sylvestris, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color, typically in late summer or early autumn. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, moist environments.

This species is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and disturbed areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
