Ranunculus geraniifolius proles montanus

Ranunculus geraniifolius proles montanus, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often bright-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed, resembling those of geraniums, and its flowers are usually pink or white with prominent yellow stamens.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in wildflower gardens and natural habitats.
