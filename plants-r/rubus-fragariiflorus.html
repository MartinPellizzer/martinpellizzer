Rubus fragariiflorus

Rubus fragariiflorus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupes that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.

Rubus fragariiflorus plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
