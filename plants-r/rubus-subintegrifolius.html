Rubus subintegrifolius

Rubus subintegrifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, serrated lobes. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but not widely cultivated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique leaf and fruit morphology.
