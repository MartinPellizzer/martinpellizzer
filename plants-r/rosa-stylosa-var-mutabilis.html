Rosa stylosa var. mutabilis

Rosa stylosa var. mutabilis, commonly known as the variable pink bush rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink flowers that change color as they age, ranging from white to deep pink. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges and has thorny stems typical of many rose species. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
