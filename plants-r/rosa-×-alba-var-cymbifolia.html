Rosa × alba var. cymbifolia

Rosa × alba var. cymbifolia, commonly known as the white garden rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that often have a subtle pink or red hue at the base. The plant typically has thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical significance in horticulture.
