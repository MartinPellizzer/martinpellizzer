Ranunculus auricomus subsp. subfirmicaulis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

subfirmicaulis, commonly known as the yellow avens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a hairy surface and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The flowers have five to eight sepals that are often mistaken for petals, and they are typically borne on slender, upright stems. This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
