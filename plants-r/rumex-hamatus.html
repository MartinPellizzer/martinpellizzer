Rumex hamatus

Rumex hamatus, commonly known as the hooked sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy, serrated edge. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, reddish-purple spikes, which bloom in early spring.

Rumex hamatus is characterized by its edible leaves, though they are usually bitter and often used in traditional medicine.
