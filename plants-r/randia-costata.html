Randia costata

Randia costata, commonly known as the rough randia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and gardenias. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its small, white to pinkish flowers are followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries that contain the seeds.

Randia costata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
