Rubus vulgatus var. umbrosus

Rubus vulgatus var. umbrosus, commonly known as the shadowy blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry that thrives in shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in early spring.

This variety is often found in woodland areas and along forest edges where light is limited.
