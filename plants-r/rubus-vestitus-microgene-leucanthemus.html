Rubus vestitus microgene leucanthemus

Rubus vestitus microgene leucanthemus, commonly known as the silver-haired blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color, with a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a silvery underside that gives it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.
