Rosa suavis

Rosa suavis, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers and thorny stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of 5 to 7 oval leaflets.

Rosa suavis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
