Ribes californicum subsp. hesperium

Ribes californicum subsp.

hesperium, commonly known as the western gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America. It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, yellow-orange berries.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland and streamside habitats. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces upright, slender stems.
