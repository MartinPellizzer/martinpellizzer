Rosa coriifolia var. subneglecta

Rosa coriifolia var. subneglecta, commonly known as the Japanese coral tree or Chinese coral tree, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing before the leaves in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological landscapes.
