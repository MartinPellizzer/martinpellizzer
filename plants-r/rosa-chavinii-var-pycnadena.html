Rosa chavinii var. pycnadena

Rosa chavinii var. pycnadena, commonly known as the Chavin rose, is a rare and ancient species native to the Peruvian Andes.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of fragrant, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are simple and serrated, while its stems are often covered in thorns.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in Andean culture.
