Rheum baillonii

Rheum baillonii, commonly known as the Bailloni rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces erect, branched stems that bear small, greenish-white flowers in late summer. Its rhubarb-like leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their medicinal properties.

However, unlike edible rhubarb, Rheum baillonii is not typically consumed due to its potential toxicity.
