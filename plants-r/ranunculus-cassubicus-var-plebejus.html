Ranunculus cassubicus var. plebejus

Ranunculus cassubicus var. plebejus, commonly known as the lesser buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, and its leaves are deeply lobed with a distinctive, hairy texture. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.

The species is characterized by its small stature and preference for cooler, humid environments.
