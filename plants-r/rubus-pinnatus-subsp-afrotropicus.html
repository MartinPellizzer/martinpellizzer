Rubus pinnatus subsp. afrotropicus

Rubus pinnatus subsp.

afrotropicus, commonly known as the African raspberry, is a shrub native to sub-Saharan Africa. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with pinnate venation.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including forests, savannas, and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
