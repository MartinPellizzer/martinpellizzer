Rubus × bellispinosus

Rubus × bellispinosus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a sweet and tart flavor. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various growing conditions.

Its botanical features make it both a valuable food source and a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
