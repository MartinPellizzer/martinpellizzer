Ranunculus japonicus subsp. akagiensis

Ranunculus japonicus subsp.

akagiensis, commonly known as the Akagi buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan. It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals, often adorned with a central dark spot.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that aids in water retention.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
