Rhytileucoma chilocarpoides

Rhytileucoma chilocarpoides, commonly known as the silverleaf lichen, is a unique lichen species that exhibits a complex symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga.

It typically grows on tree bark, rocks, or soil in temperate and subarctic regions, forming a crust-like structure that can vary in color from pale gray to bluish-green. The plant's botanical structure is characterized by its thallus, which is composed of a fungal matrix interspersed with algal cells. It plays a vital ecological role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization in its native habitats.

Due to its slow growth and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is often used as an indicator species for air quality and ecosystem health.
