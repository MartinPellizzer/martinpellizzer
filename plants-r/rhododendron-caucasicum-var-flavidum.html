Rhododendron caucasicum var. flavidum

Rhododendron caucasicum var. flavidum, commonly known as the Caucasian yellow rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or orange in color, making it a striking addition to gardens. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
