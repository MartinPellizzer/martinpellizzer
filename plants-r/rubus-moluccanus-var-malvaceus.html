Rubus moluccanus var. malvaceus

Rubus moluccanus var. malvaceus, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it is notable for its floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
