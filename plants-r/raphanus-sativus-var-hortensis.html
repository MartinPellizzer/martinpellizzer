Raphanus sativus var. hortensis

Raphanus sativus var. hortensis, commonly known as the garden radish, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its fleshy, edible roots that are typically harvested in the first year of growth. The plant has erect, branching stems with lobed, pinnately compound leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family.

The seeds are small, round, and contain compounds that contribute to the plant's characteristic spicy flavor.
