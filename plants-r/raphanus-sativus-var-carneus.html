Raphanus sativus var. carneus

Raphanus sativus var. carneus, commonly known as the purple top turnip, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its swollen, purple-tinged root and upright, branching stem covered in small, green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in a raceme, which are followed by round, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is native to the Mediterranean region and is often cultivated for its edible root and leaves.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in traditional medicine.
