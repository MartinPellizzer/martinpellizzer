Rubus cuspidiferus

Rubus cuspidiferus, commonly known as the prickly pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces edible fruits known as berries, which are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated at the edges.

Rubus cuspidiferus is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
