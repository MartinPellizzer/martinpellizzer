Rhododendron caucasicum var. album-mutabile

Rhododendron caucasicum var. album-mutabile, commonly known as the Caucasian white-flowered rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that often change color as they bloom. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape, providing a striking contrast to its floral display.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
