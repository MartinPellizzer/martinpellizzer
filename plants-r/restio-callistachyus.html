Restio callistachyus

Restio callistachyus, commonly known as the silver restio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often found in fynbos vegetation.

Restio callistachyus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
