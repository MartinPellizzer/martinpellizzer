Rhododendron ponticum f. luteomaculatum

Rhododendron ponticum f. luteomaculatum, commonly known as the yellow-spotted rhododendron, is a variant of the Pontic rhododendron.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pink to reddish-purple with distinctive yellow or orange spots, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or mountainous regions.

This cultivar is valued for its vibrant blooms and is commonly used in horticulture for its visual appeal.
