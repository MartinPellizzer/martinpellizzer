Rodriguezia bracteata

Rodriguezia bracteata, commonly known as the scarlet wax tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, making it a visually striking ornamental plant. The plant has palmate leaves with a waxy texture and a distinctive reddish hue. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Rodrigezia bracteata is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
