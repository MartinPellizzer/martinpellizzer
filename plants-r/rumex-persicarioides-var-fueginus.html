Rumex persicarioides var. fueginus

Rumex persicarioides var. fueginus, commonly known as the Fuegian sorrel, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a reddish or purple underside, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

The species is characterized by its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its astringent and anti-inflammatory effects.
