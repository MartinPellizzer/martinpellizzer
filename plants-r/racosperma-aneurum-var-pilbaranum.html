Racosperma aneurum var. pilbaranum

Racosperma aneurum var. pilbaranum, commonly known as the Pilbara raceme, is a shrub native to the Pilbara region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to withstand drought conditions.

This plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
