Randia fasciculata var. velutina

Randia fasciculata var. velutina, commonly known as velvet randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, often climbing stems and small, opposite leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne in axillary or terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are globose, red or orange berries that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
