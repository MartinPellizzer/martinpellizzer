Ruellia digitalis

Ruellia digitalis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often woody at the base.

Ruellia digitalis thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its showy blooms.
