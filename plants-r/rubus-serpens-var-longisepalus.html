Rubus serpens var. longisepalus

Rubus serpens var. longisepalus, commonly known as the long-sepaled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves that have three to five leaflets. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry, often used in culinary applications.

This variant is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types, making it a popular choice for cultivation in temperate regions.
