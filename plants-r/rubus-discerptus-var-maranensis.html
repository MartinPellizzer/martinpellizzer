Rubus discerptus var. maranensis

Rubus discerptus var. maranensis, commonly known as the Maranense blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
