Ranunculus fallax subsp. viburgensis

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

viburgensis, commonly known as the Viburnum ranunculus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive green center. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often toxic species.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in many regions.
