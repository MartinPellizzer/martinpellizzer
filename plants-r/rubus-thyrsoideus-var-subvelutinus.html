Rubus thyrsoideus var. subvelutinus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. subvelutinus, commonly known as the velvet-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features erect or ascending stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a slightly serrated margin. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, while the fruit is a dark purple to black aggregate berry.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and edible fruit.
