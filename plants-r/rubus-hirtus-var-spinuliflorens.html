Rubus hirtus var. spinuliflorens

Rubus hirtus var. spinuliflorens, commonly known as the prickly raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
