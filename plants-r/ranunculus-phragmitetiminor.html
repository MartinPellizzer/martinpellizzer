Ranunculus phragmitetiminor

Ranunculus phragmitetiminor, commonly known as the marsh buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has a short, rhizomatous stem and produces heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist or aquatic environments, such as marshes and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
