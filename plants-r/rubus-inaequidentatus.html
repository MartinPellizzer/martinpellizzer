Rubus inaequidentatus

Rubus inaequidentatus, commonly known as the unequal-toothed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. Its leaves are serrated with varying tooth sizes, giving it its specific epithet.

It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.
