Rhododendron quadrasianum var. rosmarinifolium

Rhododendron quadrasianum var. rosmarinifolium, commonly known as the rosemary-leaved rhododendron, is a unique hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that resemble rosemary, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
