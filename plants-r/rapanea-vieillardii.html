Rapanea vieillardii

Rapanea vieillardii, commonly known as the Madagascar laurel, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Rapanea vieillardii is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
