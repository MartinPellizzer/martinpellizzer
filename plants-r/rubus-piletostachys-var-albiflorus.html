Rubus piletostachys var. albiflorus

Rubus piletostachys var. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by edible berries that are similar in taste to regular blackberries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The whiteflower blackberry is a member of the Rosaceae family, which includes other berry-producing plants like raspberries and strawberries.
