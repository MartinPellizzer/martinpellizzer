Ratibida columnaris f. pulcherrima

Ratibida columnaris f. pulcherrima, commonly known as the Mexican torch flower, is a striking perennial plant native to Mexico.

It features showy, orange-red to yellow-orange composite flower heads that resemble torches, surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale yellow seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics include a tall, upright growth habit and a distinctive, fiery appearance that makes it a standout in gardens.
