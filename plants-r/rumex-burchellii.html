Rumex burchellii

Rumex burchellii, commonly known as Burchell's sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features arrow-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration on the underside, which is a characteristic feature of the Rumex genus. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
