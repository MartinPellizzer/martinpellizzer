Rubus ulmifolius var. polyanchus

Rubus ulmifolius var. polyanchus, commonly known as the many-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically oval to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
