Reseda jacquinii subsp. brachycarpa

Reseda jacquinii subsp.

brachycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited dyer's weed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

The plant produces small, pinkish flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its historical use in dyeing textiles.

The subspecies brachycarpa is distinguished by its shorter, more compact fruits compared to other subspecies of Reseda jacquinii.
