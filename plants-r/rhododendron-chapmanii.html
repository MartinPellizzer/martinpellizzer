Rhododendron chapmanii

Rhododendron chapmanii, commonly known as Chapman's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a shrub that typically grows in cool, moist environments with acidic soil. The plant features dense, dark green foliage and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Rhododendron chapmanii is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in shaded woodland settings.
