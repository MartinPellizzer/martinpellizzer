Rivina affinis

Rivina affinis, commonly known as the redroot false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its showy bracts and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Rivina affinis is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its tolerance to various soil conditions.
