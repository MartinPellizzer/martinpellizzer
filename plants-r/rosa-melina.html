Rosa melina

Rosa melina, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. Rosa melina typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
