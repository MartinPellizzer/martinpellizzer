Rapuntium ceratophyllum

Rapuntium ceratophyllum, commonly known as the spiked water shield, is a unique aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of leaves that are typically covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged or floating, with stamens that are often covered in fine hairs. Its roots form a dense mat on the lake or pond bottom, which helps stabilize the sediment.

Rapuntium ceratophyllum is often found in shallow, still waters and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
