Regelia cyanea

Regelia cyanea, commonly known as the violet regelia, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant violet or purple flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in height. Its flowers are borne on tall, leafless stalks and are often accompanied by showy bracts.

Regelia cyanea is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
