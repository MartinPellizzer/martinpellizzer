Rubus thyrsoideus subsp. armoricus

Rubus thyrsoideus subsp.

armoricus, commonly known as the eastern blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America. It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
