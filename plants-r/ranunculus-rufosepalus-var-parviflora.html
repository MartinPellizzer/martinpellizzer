Ranunculus rufosepalus var. parviflora

Ranunculus rufosepalus var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered red-sepaled buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with reddish sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the species is noted for its ornamental value in horticulture.

The varietal name 'parviflora' refers to its relatively small flower size compared to other varieties within the species.
