Rubus macrophyllus subsp. lasiaxon

Rubus macrophyllus subsp.

lasiaxon, commonly known as the large-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its large, serrated leaves that can grow up to 10 centimeters in length.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The subspecies is valued for its edible fruits and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
