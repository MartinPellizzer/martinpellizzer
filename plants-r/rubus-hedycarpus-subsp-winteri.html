Rubus hedycarpus subsp. winteri

Rubus hedycarpus subsp.

winteri, commonly known as the winter raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It produces small, red, edible berries that are typically harvested in late autumn.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to colder climates.

Its fruits are valued for their tart flavor and are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
