Roegneria leiantha

Roegneria leiantha, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a species of grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are the flowering structures typical of the grass family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Roegneria leiantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
