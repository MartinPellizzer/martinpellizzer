Rubus crataegifolius var. inermis

Rubus crataegifolius var. inermis, commonly known as the smoothleaf blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Botanically, it is notable for its distinctive leaf morphology and its role in local ecosystems.
