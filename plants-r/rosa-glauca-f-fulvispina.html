Rosa glauca f. fulvispina

Rosa glauca f. fulvispina, commonly known as the golden thorn rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa glauca species.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its foliage is dark green with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces fragrant, five-petaled blooms.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
