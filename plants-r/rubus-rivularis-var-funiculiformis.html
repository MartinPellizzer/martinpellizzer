Rubus rivularis var. funiculiformis

Rubus rivularis var. funiculiformis, commonly known as the river raspberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments along stream banks and in wetlands.

Its foliage is usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
