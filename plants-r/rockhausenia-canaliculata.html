Rockhausenia canaliculata

Rockhausenia canaliculata, commonly known as the canyon rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, often blue or purple flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rockhausenia canaliculata is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
