Rubus pycnanthus

Rubus pycnanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, dark berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and moist soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, with a slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
