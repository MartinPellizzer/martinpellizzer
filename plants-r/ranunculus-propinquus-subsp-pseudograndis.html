Ranunculus propinquus subsp. pseudograndis

Ranunculus propinquus subsp.

pseudograndis, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and wetlands, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, and its leaves are palmately lobed with a distinctive hairy texture. It has a short, stout rhizome and produces small, oval seeds in a capsule.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
