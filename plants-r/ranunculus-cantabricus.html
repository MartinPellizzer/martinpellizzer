Ranunculus cantabricus

Ranunculus cantabricus, commonly known as the Cantabrian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, and its leaves are deeply lobed and trifoliate. It blooms from April to June, and its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
