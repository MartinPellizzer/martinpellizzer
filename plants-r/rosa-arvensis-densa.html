Rosa arvensis densa

Rosa arvensis densa, commonly known as the field rose or creeping rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers. Rosa arvensis densa is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its thorny stems and showy blooms make it a common weed in agricultural areas.
