Rosa pendulina var. albidopuberula

Rosa pendulina var. albidopuberula, commonly known as the white-hairy climbing rose, is a variant of the pendulous rose species.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems that often climb or trail along the ground. The leaves are compound, typically consisting of 5 to 7 elliptical leaflets with a soft, white pubescence. The flowers are single or semi-double, with pink to pale pink petals and a subtle fragrance.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden trellises or hanging baskets.
