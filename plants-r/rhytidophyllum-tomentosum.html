Rhytidophyllum tomentosum

Rhytidophyllum tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly bark tree, is a rare and unique plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, bark-like foliage that is covered in a dense, white tomentum, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often found in shaded, humid environments within forest understories. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a distinct, aromatic scent.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable but endangered species in its natural range.
