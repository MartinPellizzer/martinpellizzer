Roscoea cautleyoides f. atropurpurea

Roscoea cautleyoides f. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-leafed roscoea, is a striking rhizomatous perennial native to the Himalayas.

It features elegant, sword-shaped leaves that are a deep green with a purple tinge, adding visual interest to the garden. The plant produces showy, red-orange inflorescences that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is a prized addition for horticultural collections.
