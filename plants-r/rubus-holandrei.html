Rubus holandrei

Rubus holandrei, commonly known as the Dutch raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, with a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and have serrated edges.

Rubus holandrei is often found in disturbed soils and along woodland edges, thriving in moist, temperate climates.
