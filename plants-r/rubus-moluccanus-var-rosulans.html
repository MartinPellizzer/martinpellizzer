Rubus moluccanus var. rosulans

Rubus moluccanus var. rosulans, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a species native to the Moluccas in Indonesia.

It is a shrub characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and for local use.
