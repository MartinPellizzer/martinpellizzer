Rubus × falsipallidus

Rubus × falsipallidus, commonly known as the pale bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, a type of aggregate berry, is typically red or orange when mature.

This species is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils across eastern North America.
