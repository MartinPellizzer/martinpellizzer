Ranunculus acinaciformis

Ranunculus acinaciformis, commonly known as the swordleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features distinctive, sharply pointed leaves that resemble a sword, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

Ranunculus acinaciformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
