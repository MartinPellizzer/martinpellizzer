Rhynchosia lynesii

Rhynchosia lynesii, commonly known as the Lyne's burrbean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to grow in various habitats, including disturbed soils and open woodlands.

Rhynchosia lynesii is notable for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a potential forage plant.
