Ruellia terminalis var. grandiflora

Ruellia terminalis var. grandiflora, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and hummingbirds.
