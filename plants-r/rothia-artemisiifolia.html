Rothia artemisiifolia

Rothia artemisiifolia, commonly known as common wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It is known for its strong, aromatic scent and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported antiparasitic properties.

Rothia artemisiifolia is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
