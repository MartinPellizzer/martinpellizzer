Rosa marcyana

Rosa marcyana, commonly known as the Marcy's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Guatemala and Mexico. The plant is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are edible and rich in vitamin C. Rosa marcyana is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
