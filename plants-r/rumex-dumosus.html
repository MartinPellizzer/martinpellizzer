Rumex dumosus

Rumex dumosus, commonly known as the creeping dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats.

Rumex dumosus is often used in traditional medicine and can be found in various ecological studies due to its adaptability and ecological significance.
