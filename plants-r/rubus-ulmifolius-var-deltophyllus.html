Rubus ulmifolius var. deltophyllus

Rubus ulmifolius var. deltophyllus, commonly known as the red-leafed bramble, is a variant of the common bramble, characterized by its distinctive red foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in woodland edges and hedgerows. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are usually red or black in color. Its leaves are usually palmately lobed, with a hairy texture and a reddish tinge, especially in autumn.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a hedging plant.
