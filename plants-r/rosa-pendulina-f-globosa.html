Rosa pendulina f. globosa

Rosa pendulina f. globosa, commonly known as the globe rose, is a unique cultivar of the rose family characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit.

It features small, double flowers that are typically pink or white, with a delicate, fragrant aroma. The plant has a shrub-like structure with thornless stems and glossy, dark green foliage. It is well-suited for container gardening or as a decorative hedge due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.

This variety is prized for its ability to bloom profusely throughout the growing season.
