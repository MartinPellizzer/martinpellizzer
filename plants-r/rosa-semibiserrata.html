Rosa semibiserrata

Rosa semibiserrata, commonly known as the double serrated rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The leaves are alternate, with a distinctive double-serrated margin that gives the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
