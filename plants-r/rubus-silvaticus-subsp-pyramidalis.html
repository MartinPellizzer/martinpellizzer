Rubus silvaticus subsp. pyramidalis

Rubus silvaticus subsp.

pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of North America. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
