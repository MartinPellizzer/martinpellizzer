Rubus hystrix microgene rubicundus

Rubus hystrix microgene rubicundus, commonly known as the red-stemmed bramble, is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and dense growth habit.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits known as blackberries. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters during the spring.

The species is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
