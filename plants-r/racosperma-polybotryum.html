Racosperma polybotryum

Racosperma polybotryum, commonly known as the mountain wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its yellow flowers are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, and it produces small, hairy pods that contain seeds.

Racosperma polybotryum is valued for its ecological role and is often used in revegetation projects.
