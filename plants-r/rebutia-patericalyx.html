Rebutia patericalyx

Rebutia patericalyx, commonly known as the desert rose, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are usually small and may be arranged in a rosette pattern, depending on the specific variety.

Rebutia patericalyx is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
