Rubus × pilosissimus

Rubus × pilosissimus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit, which ripens in summer, is valued for its sweet-tart flavor and is commonly used in culinary applications.
