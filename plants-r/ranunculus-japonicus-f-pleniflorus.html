Ranunculus japonicus f. pleniflorus

Ranunculus japonicus f. pleniflorus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with numerous petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are usually hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is noted for its dense flowering and is cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
