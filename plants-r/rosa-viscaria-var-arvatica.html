Rosa viscaria var. arvatica

Rosa viscaria var. arvatica, commonly known as the prairie rose, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

The fruit is a red, hairy rosehip that persists into winter, providing food for wildlife.
