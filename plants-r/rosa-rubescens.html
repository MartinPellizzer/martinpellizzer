Rosa rubescens

Rosa rubescens, commonly known as the red rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, pinnately compound leaves, and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. Rosa rubescens produces rose hips, which are the fruit of the plant and contain seeds.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and in horticulture.
