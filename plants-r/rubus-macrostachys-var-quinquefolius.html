Rubus macrostachys var. quinquefolius

Rubus macrostachys var. quinquefolius, commonly known as the five-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This species is valued for its edible berries and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
