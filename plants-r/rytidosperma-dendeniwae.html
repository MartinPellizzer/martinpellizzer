Rytidosperma dendeniwae

Rytidosperma dendeniwae, commonly known as the Namakwa grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
