Rhodiola atropurpurea

Rhodiola atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple rhodiola, is a perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Rhodiola atropurpurea is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to enhance stamina and reduce fatigue.
