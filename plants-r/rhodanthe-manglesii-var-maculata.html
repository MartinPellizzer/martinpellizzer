Rhodanthe manglesii var. maculata

Rhodanthe manglesii var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted rhodanthe, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers with distinctive red or orange markings. The plant typically grows in coastal or sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and shrublands. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
