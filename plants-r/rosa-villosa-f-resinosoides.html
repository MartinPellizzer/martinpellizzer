Rosa villosa f. resinosoides

Rosa villosa f. resinosoides, commonly known as the resinous bristly rose, is a botanical variant of the bristly rose species.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, glandular trichomes. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in a sticky resinous substance, which is a distinguishing feature of this form.

This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
