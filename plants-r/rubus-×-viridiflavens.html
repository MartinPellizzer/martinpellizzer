Rubus × viridiflavens

Rubus × viridiflavens, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a leathery texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and edible fruits.
