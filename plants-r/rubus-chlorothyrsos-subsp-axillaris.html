Rubus chlorothyrsos subsp. axillaris

Rubus chlorothyrsos subsp.

axillaris, commonly known as the axillary blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically consumed raw or used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is notable for its adaptability to various soil types.
