Rosa indica purpurea

Rosa indica purpurea, commonly known as the purple Indian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to deep purple. The plant is native to the Himalayas and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Rosa indica purpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is prized in horticulture for its fragrance and long-lasting flowers.
