Rottboellia muricata var. bandanensis

Rottboellia muricata var. bandanensis, commonly known as the Bandanensis grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
