Ranunculus arvensis var. tuberculatus

Ranunculus arvensis var. tuberculatus, commonly known as the tuberculate buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The flowers are typically yellow with five petals and are borne on slender, erect stems. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive tuberculate (wart-like) structures on the stem and leaves, which give it its botanical variety name.
