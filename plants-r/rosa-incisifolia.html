Rosa incisifolia

Rosa incisifolia, commonly known as the cut-leaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and compound leaves with deeply incised lobes. Its flowers are typically pink to red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rosa incisifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
