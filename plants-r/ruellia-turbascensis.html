Ruellia turbascensis

Ruellia turbascensis, commonly known as the Mexican bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple tubular flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Ruellia turbascensis is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
