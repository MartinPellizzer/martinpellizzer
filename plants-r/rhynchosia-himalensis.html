Rhynchosia himalensis

Rhynchosia himalensis, commonly known as the Himalayan cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Rhynchosia himalensis is also valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
