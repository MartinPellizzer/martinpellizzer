Rubus silvaticus

Rubus silvaticus, commonly known as the brambles or wild blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in woodland areas and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to edible blackberries in the autumn. Its berries are often used in culinary applications and are also an important food source for wildlife.

Rubus silvaticus is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
