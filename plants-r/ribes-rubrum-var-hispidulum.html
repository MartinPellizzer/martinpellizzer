Ribes rubrum var. hispidulum

Ribes rubrum var. hispidulum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed gooseberry, is a variant of the red gooseberry native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
