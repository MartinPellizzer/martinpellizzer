Rosa ternata

Rosa ternata, commonly known as the three-leaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa ternata is often found in woodland areas and has a trailing growth habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
