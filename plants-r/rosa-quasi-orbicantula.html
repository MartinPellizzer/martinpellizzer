Rosa quasi-orbicantula

Rosa quasi-orbicantula, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in early summer. This plant is native to parts of Asia and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
