Rosa canina f. suffulta

Rosa canina f. suffulta, commonly known as the full-blown dog rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose species.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with a rich, velvety texture. The plant produces hips, which are the fruit, known for their high vitamin C content. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
