Rosa gallica var. leiostyla

Rosa gallica var. leiostyla, commonly known as the white gallica rose, is a cultivar of the Gallica rose, native to Europe.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has a shrub-like habit with thorny stems. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is well-suited to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
