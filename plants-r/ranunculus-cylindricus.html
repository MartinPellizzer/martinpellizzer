Ranunculus cylindricus

Ranunculus cylindricus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sharply lobed leaves and bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Ranunculus cylindricus is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic sap.
