Ranunculus auricomus subsp. inaptus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

inaptus, commonly known as the western buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America. It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically arranged in a cup-like shape.

The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and wetlands.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for various pollinators and its ecological adaptation to wetland environments.
