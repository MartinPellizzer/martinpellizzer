Ranunculus × halleri

Ranunculus × halleri, commonly known as the broadleaf cicutaria, is a hybrid species within the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive black center. Native to Europe, this plant often grows in moist, calcareous soils along stream banks and wetlands. It is an annual or biennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant's botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
