Racosperma applanatum

Racosperma applanatum, commonly known as the flat-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which attract various pollinators.

Racosperma applanatum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.
