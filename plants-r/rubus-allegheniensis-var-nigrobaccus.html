Rubus allegheniensis var. nigrobaccus

Rubus allegheniensis var. nigrobaccus, commonly known as the black-barked bramble, is a variant of the Allegheny blackberry.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in woodland and disturbed habitats. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and thorny growth habit. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or black in color.

This variant is valued for its hardy nature and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
