Reseda odorata f. pilosa

Reseda odorata f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy reseda, is a variant of the annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive texture and appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rocky or sandy gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a wildflower.
