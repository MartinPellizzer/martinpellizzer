Rubus alterniflorus subsp. obvallatus

Rubus alterniflorus subsp.

obvallatus, commonly known as the obvallate blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The subspecies is notable for its distinctive leaf shape and its role in local ecosystems.
