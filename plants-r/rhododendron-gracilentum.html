Rhododendron gracilentum

Rhododendron gracilentum, commonly known as the slenderleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, narrow leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

The botanical features of Rhododendron gracilentum make it a valuable ornamental plant in horticulture.
