Rubus prodanii

Rubus prodanii, commonly known as the Serbian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or forested areas, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its edible fruits.
