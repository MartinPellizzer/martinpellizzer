Rhinacanthus communis

Rhinacanthus communis, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its distinctive red flowers that bloom profusely in spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves that turn yellow before falling off in winter. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Rhinacanthus communis is valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
