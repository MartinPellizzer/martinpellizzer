Ranunculus paludosus var. leucothrix

Ranunculus paludosus var. leucothrix, commonly known as the white water crowfoot, is a variant of the water crowfoot species native to wetland environments in Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in ponds, ditches, and stream margins. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and the stems are slender and often floating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting aquatic biodiversity.
