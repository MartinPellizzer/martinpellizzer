Racosperma amblyophyllum

Racosperma amblyophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, and it is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils.

This species is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
