Ruscus hypophyllum

Ruscus hypophyllum, commonly known as butcher's broom, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features dense, spiny, scale-like leaves that form a compact, cushion-like structure. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in hedges or as a ground cover.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
