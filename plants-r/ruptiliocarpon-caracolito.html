Ruptiliocarpon caracolito

Ruptiliocarpon caracolito, commonly known as the Mexican rock cactus, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and ability to survive in harsh conditions make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
