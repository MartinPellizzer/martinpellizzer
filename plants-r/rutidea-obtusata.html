Rutidea obtusata

Rutidea obtusata, commonly known as the broad-leaved Rutidea, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its seed pods are hairy and contain several small, flat seeds.

Rutidea obtusata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects.
