Racosperma podalyriifolium

Racosperma podalyriifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically has slender, greyish stems with alternate, pinnate leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are usually yellow or yellow-green, arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes, and it produces leguminous pods that contain edible seeds.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native Australian flora.
