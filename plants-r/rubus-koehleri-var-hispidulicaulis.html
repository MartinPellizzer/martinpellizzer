Rubus koehleri var. hispidulicaulis

Rubus koehleri var. hispidulicaulis, commonly known as the hispidulicaulis blackberry, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
