Rhodiola rosea var. oblonga

Rhodiola rosea var. oblonga, commonly known as the "golden root," is a perennial herb native to the cold, mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, yellowish roots and its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pink or red flowers. It is a member of the Crassulaceae family and is closely related to other species of Rhodiola.

Due to its adaptability and resilience, it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
