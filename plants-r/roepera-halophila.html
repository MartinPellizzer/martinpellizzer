Roepera halophila

Roepera halophila, commonly known as the saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, often inconspicuous flowers. This species is adapted to saline and drought-prone environments, making it a halophyte. Its root system helps it access water from deep underground, enhancing its survival in harsh conditions.

Roepera halophila plays an important role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitats.
