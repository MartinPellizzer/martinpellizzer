Rubus sacer

Rubus sacer, commonly known as the sacred raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, red, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces achenes that are dispersed by animals.

The plant has historical significance in traditional medicine and folklore.
