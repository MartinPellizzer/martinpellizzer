Rubus thyrsifloroglandulosus

Rubus thyrsifloroglandulosus, commonly known as the glandular-flowered blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its glandular hairs and dense inflorescences.
