Ranunculus pilosus

Ranunculus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem, which gives it its species name "pilosus.". The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ranunculus pilosus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic properties.
