Rubus cuspidiferus var. typicus

Rubus cuspidiferus var. typicus, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupes, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The species is known for its high ornamental value and is also cultivated for its edible berries.
