Rosa hispanica var. spina-flava

Rosa hispanica var. spina-flava, commonly known as the Spanish yellow rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which often have a subtle orange or red tinge. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and fragrant flowers.
