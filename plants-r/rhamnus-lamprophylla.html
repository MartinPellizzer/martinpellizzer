Rhamnus lamprophylla

Rhamnus lamprophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaved buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Rhamnus lamprophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
