Rosa elliptica f. argus

Rosa elliptica f. argus, commonly known as the Argus rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves and showy, semi-double pink flowers.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is typically shrub-like, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with thorny stems and dark green foliage. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant blooms that attract pollinators.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to garden settings.
