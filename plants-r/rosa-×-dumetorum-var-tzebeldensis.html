Rosa × dumetorum var. tzebeldensis

Rosa × dumetorum var. tzebeldensis, commonly known as the Tzebeldens rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to red. The leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged in odd-pinnate patterns, contributing to its ornamental value. This variety is often cultivated for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic appeal and resilience.
