Rapistrum longiracemosum

Rapistrum longiracemosum, commonly known as the long-branched rapeseed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate and have a rough texture, with serrated edges.

Rapistrum longiracemosum is adapted to arid conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.
