Rhynchospora corymbosa f. chacoensis

Rhynchospora corymbosa f. chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco marsh sedge, is a flowering plant native to the Chaco region of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various small organisms.
