Rosa × dumetorum var. juncta

Rosa × dumetorum var. juncta, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit, with slender stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
