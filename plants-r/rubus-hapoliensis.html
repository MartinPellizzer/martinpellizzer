Rubus hapoliensis

Rubus hapoliensis, commonly known as the Hapoli blackberry, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is a member of the Rubus genus, which includes many brambles and shrubs. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems typical of the genus.

This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.
