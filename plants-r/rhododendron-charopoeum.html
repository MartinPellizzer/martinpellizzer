Rhododendron charopoeum

Rhododendron charopoeum, commonly known as the Canary Island rhododendron, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features dense, dark green foliage and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in coastal or mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

However, it is considered invasive in some areas, posing a threat to native ecosystems.
