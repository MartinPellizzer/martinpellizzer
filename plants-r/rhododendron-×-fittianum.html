Rhododendron × fittianum

Rhododendron × fittianum, commonly known as the Fittianum rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron catawbiense and Rhododendron maximum.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its botanical features include thick, leathery leaves and a compact growth habit, which contribute to its ornamental value.

The species is native to the southeastern United States, where it is often found in forest understories.
