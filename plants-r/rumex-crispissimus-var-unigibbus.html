Rumex crispissimus var. unigibbus

Rumex crispissimus var. unigibbus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive wavy margin. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

The species is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
