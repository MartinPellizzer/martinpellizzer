Reseda ramosissima

Reseda ramosissima, commonly known as prairie sandwort, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and covered in a waxy coating.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and coastal dune habitats.
