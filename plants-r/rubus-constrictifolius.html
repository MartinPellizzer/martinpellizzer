Rubus constrictifolius

Rubus constrictifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of North America, particularly the eastern United States. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves with a narrow, serrated appearance, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in woodland edges and moist soils, often forming dense thickets.

The plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
