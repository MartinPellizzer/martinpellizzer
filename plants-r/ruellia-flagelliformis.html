Ruellia flagelliformis

Ruellia flagelliformis, commonly known as the threadleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Ruellia flagelliformis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
