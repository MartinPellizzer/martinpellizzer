Rubus villicaulis var. formanekianus

Rubus villicaulis var. formanekianus, commonly known as the Armenian blackberry, is a variant of the bramble species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated in home gardens and commercial settings.
