Rhaponticum atriplicifolium

Rhaponticum atriplicifolium, commonly known as the black root or black salsify, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer.

Rhaponticum atriplicifolium is known for its high nutritional value and has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source in various cultures.
