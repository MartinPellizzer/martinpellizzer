Rhododendron andrineae

Rhododendron andrineae, commonly known as the Andes rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and shrublands, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron andrineae plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
