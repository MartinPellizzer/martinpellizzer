Rubus vogesiacus

Rubus vogesiacus, commonly known as the Voges raspberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe, particularly found in the mountainous regions of the Alps and the Carpathians. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, which are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
