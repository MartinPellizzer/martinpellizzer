Rubus dumetorum var. pilosus

Rubus dumetorum var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

The hairy texture of its leaves and stems is a distinguishing feature of this variant.
