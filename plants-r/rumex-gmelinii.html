Rumex gmelinii

Rumex gmelinii, commonly known as the mountain sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It is often found in high-altitude areas where other plants struggle to survive.

Rumex gmelinii is valued for its edible leaves, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
