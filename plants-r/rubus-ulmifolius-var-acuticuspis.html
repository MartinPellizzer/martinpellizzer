Rubus ulmifolius var. acuticuspis

Rubus ulmifolius var. acuticuspis, commonly known as the sharp-toothed blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry plant.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black when mature.

This plant is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
