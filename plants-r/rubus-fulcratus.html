Rubus fulcratus

Rubus fulcratus, commonly known as the western prickly raspberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
