Rhynchocarpa pilosa

Rhynchocarpa pilosa, commonly known as the hairy fruit-bearing plant, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
