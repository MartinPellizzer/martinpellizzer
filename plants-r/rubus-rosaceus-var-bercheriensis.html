Rubus rosaceus var. bercheriensis

Rubus rosaceus var. bercheriensis, commonly known as the Bercher's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
