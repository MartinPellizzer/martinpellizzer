Rhamnus longistyla

Rhamnus longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and mammals. Its adaptability to rocky and arid environments makes it an important component of alpine ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
