Rhodanthemum gayanum subsp. demnatense

Rhodanthemum gayanum subsp.

demnatense, commonly known as the Demnat Island chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the island of Demna in Sri Lanka. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale pink petals.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb with erect stems and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
