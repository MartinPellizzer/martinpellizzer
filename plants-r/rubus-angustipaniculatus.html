Rubus angustipaniculatus

Rubus angustipaniculatus, commonly known as narrowpanicle blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregated flowers that develop into edible berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
