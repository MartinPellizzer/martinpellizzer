Rhodophiala advena

Rhodophiala advena, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, silver-green foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions, often in rocky or forested habitats. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
