Ranunculus bertisceus

Ranunculus bertisceus, commonly known as the Bertice's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, radially symmetric flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers feature numerous stamens and a prominent, hairy pistil.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant yellow or white blooms.
