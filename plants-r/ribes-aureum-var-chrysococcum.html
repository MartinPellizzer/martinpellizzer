Ribes aureum var. chrysococcum

Ribes aureum var. chrysococcum, commonly known as golden currant.

This variant is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its yellow-orange to red flowers and red, edible berries. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth margin and produces clusters of small, bright red fruits in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
