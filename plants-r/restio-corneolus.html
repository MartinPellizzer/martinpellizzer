Restio corneolus

Restio corneolus, commonly known as the coastal restio, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes and fynbos vegetation.

Restio corneolus is adapted to withstand coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
