Reseda luteola var. robusta

Reseda luteola var. robusta, commonly known as yellowrocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage.
