Rosa sicula subsp. ligustica

Rosa sicula subsp.

ligustica, commonly known as the Ligurian rose, is a shrub native to the western Mediterranean region, particularly Italy. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves.

The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance. This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and coastal areas, thriving in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and culinary applications.
