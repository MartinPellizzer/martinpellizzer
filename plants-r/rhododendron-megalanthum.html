Rhododendron megalanthum

Rhododendron megalanthum, commonly known as the giant leathery rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, evergreen leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and thrives in acidic soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
