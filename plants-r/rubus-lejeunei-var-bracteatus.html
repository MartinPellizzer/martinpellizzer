Rubus lejeunei var. bracteatus

Rubus lejeunei var. bracteatus, commonly known as the bracteated blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
