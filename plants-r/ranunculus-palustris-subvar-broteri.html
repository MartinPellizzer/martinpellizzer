Ranunculus palustris subvar. broteri

Ranunculus palustris subvar. broteri, commonly known as the marsh buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellowish flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has trifoliate leaves that are usually submerged or floating, adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, fens, and along the edges of ponds.

Its presence is an indicator of healthy wetland ecosystems.
