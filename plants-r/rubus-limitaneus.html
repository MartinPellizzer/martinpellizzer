Rubus limitaneus

Rubus limitaneus, commonly known as the coastal blackberry, is a shrub native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Rubus limitaneus plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
