Rubia peregrina var. azorica

Rubia peregrina var. azorica, commonly known as the Azorean balm, is a flowering plant native to the Azores.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its aromatic leaves have been traditionally used in herbal remedies.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
