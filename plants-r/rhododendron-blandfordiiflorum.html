Rhododendron blandfordiiflorum

Rhododendron blandfordiiflorum, commonly known as the Blandford's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
