Rhytidocaulon fulleri

Rhytidocaulon fulleri, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with prominent black spots, which attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
