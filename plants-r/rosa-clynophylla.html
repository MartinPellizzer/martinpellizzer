Rosa clynophylla

Rosa clynophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces fragrant pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Rosa clynophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
