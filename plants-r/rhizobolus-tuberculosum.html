Rhizobolus tuberculosum

Rhizobolus tuberculosum, commonly known as the tuberculate root fungus, is a unique plant that exhibits specialized root structures.

It forms tubercles, or small rounded bumps, on its roots, which are believed to aid in nutrient absorption. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its botanical features are closely tied to its symbiotic relationships with certain soil microorganisms.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
