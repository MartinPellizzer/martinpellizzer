Rosa × dumetorum

Rosa × dumetorum, commonly known as the shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Rosa × dumetorum is also valued for its role in traditional herbal medicine.
