Rosa eglanteria f. minuscula

Rosa eglanteria f. minuscula, commonly known as the miniature prickly rose, is a compact shrub with dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves.

Its flowers are typically pink to red, with five to seven petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its short stature, often reaching only a few inches in height, making it a unique addition to rock gardens or containers. It is native to the eastern Mediterranean and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
