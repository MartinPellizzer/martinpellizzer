Rosa micrantha var. diminuta

Rosa micrantha var. diminuta, commonly known as the dwarf shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, dark green leaves that provide an attractive backdrop to its showy blooms. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for small spaces and garden landscapes.
