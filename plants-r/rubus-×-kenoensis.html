Rubus × kenoensis

Rubus × kenoensis, commonly known as the Kenoensis blackberry, is a hybrid plant within the Rubus genus, native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It is valued for its edible berries, which have a sweet-tart flavor and are used in culinary applications.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations depending on its parent species.
