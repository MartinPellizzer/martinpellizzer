Rubus roseistylus

Rubus roseistylus, commonly known as the rose-styled raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red when mature. Its flowers are pink to white, with five petals and a distinctive rose-like appearance.

Rubus roseistylus is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its edible fruits.
