Rubus × viaurensis

Rubus × viaurensis, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry or loganberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the raspberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (the cloudberry).

It is a deciduous shrub that produces edible berries, which are typically dark red to black in color and have a tart, complex flavor. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and grows in temperate climates, often in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its fruit, which is used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
