Rheum micranthum

Rheum micranthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhubarb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, topped with clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are elongated and have a distinctive reddish color on the underside, which becomes more pronounced as the plant matures.

Rheum micranthum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
