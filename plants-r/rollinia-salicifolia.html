Rollinia salicifolia

Rollinia salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leafed rollinia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Annonaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its elongated, willow-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow berry that contains several seeds.

Rollinia salicifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
