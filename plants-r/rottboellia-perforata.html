Rottboellia perforata

Rottboellia perforata, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces distinctive, reddish-brown spikelets that are often used in floral arrangements. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, while the inflorescence consists of compact, open spikes.

Rottboellia perforata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
