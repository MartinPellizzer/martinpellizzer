Rosa lemanii var. rotundifolia

Rosa lemanii var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the Swiss rose or the Alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are oval-shaped and have a distinctive rounded base, giving the plant its specific epithet "rotundifolia.". It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for rock gardens and alpine settings.
