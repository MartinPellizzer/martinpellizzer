Rosa agrestis f. elatior

Rosa agrestis f. elatior, commonly known as the vigorous wild rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa agrestis.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and profuse flowering, typically producing clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant is native to Europe and western Asia, often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
