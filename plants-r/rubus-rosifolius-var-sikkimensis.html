Rubus rosifolius var. sikkimensis

Rubus rosifolius var. sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim raspberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayan region.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, aggregate fruits that resemble raspberries. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it is valued for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.

The species is characterized by its thorny stems and adaptability to mountainous terrain.
