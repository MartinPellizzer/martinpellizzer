Rosa brobyensis

Rosa brobyensis, commonly known as the Broby rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, specifically found in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red-orange hips in the autumn.

Rosa brobyensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also studied for its potential in horticulture and conservation efforts.
