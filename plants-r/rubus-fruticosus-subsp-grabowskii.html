Rubus fruticosus subsp. grabowskii

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

grabowskii, commonly known as the Grabowskii blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
