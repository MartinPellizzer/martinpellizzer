Rubus lejeunei var. powelii

Rubus lejeunei var. powelii, commonly known as the Powell's raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
