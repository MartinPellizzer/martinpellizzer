Ranunculus borealis var. pumilus

Ranunculus borealis var. pumilus, commonly known as the northern meadow buttercup, is a small perennial herb native to Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, moist habitats such as tundra, meadows, and rocky slopes. The plant features a basal rosette of finely divided, bright yellow flowers with five petals. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting pollinators in its harsh environment.
