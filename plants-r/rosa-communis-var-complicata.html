Rosa communis var. complicata

Rosa communis var. complicata, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a flowering shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It also has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of perfume.
