Rubus affinis subsp. relatus

Rubus affinis subsp.

relatus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a shrub native to North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its fruit, which is used in culinary and commercial applications.
