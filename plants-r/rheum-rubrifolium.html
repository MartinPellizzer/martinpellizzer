Rheum rubrifolium

Rheum rubrifolium, commonly known as the red-leaf rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically red or purple in color, especially when young. Its stems are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
