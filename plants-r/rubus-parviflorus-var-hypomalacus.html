Rubus parviflorus var. hypomalacus

Rubus parviflorus var. hypomalacus, commonly known as the small-flowered blackberry variant, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in early spring, followed by dark purple to black berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family, sharing characteristics with other brambles and shrubs.
