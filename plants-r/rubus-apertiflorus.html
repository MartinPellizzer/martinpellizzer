Rubus apertiflorus

Rubus apertiflorus, commonly known as the open-flowered bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are often red or black in color. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

It is native to certain regions of Asia and is often found in woodland or scrubland habitats.
