Rosa rubiginosa var. arabica

Rosa rubiginosa var. arabica, commonly known as the Arabian rose or the red-stemmed rose, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, dark green leaves, and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It is well adapted to dry climates and can thrive in poor soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
