Rubus leptobellus

Rubus leptobellus, commonly known as the slender-stalked blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces achenes as its fruit type.

Rubus leptobellus is an important food source for various wildlife species.
