Rimaria roodiae

Rimaria roodiae, commonly known as the Namib Desert lichen, is a unique symbiotic organism composed of a cyanobacterium and a green alga living within a fungal matrix.

It thrives in the extreme arid conditions of the Namib Desert, where it forms a dense, crust-like growth on rocky surfaces. This plant lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, relying instead on its symbiotic partners for nutrient acquisition and water retention. Its ability to survive in such harsh environments makes it an important indicator of desert ecosystems.

Rimaria roodiae plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing microhabitats for other desert organisms.
