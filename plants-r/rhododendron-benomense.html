Rhododendron benomense

Rhododendron benomense, commonly known as the Nepal rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often blooming in early spring. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron benomense plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, offering food and shelter to various wildlife species.
