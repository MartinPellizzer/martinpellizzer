Ranunculus californicus var. ludovicianus

Ranunculus californicus var. ludovicianus, commonly known as the California buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive central cup-like structure, typically blooming in spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves that are palmately arranged, and its stems are usually hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
