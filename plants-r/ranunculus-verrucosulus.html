Ranunculus verrucosulus

Ranunculus verrucosulus, commonly known as the rough buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically borne on leafy stems. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a distinctive, rough texture due to the presence of waxy, glandular hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy floral structures.
