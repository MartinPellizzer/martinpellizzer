Rorippa × oslawiensis

Rorippa × oslawiensis, commonly known as the Osławienice watercress, is a hybrid species within the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that are typically submerged or floating in water. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with four petals, typical of the mustard family. It thrives in moist or aquatic environments, often found in ponds, ditches, and slow-moving streams.

Rorippa × oslawiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
