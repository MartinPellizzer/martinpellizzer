Romulea obscura var. campestris

Romulea obscura var. campestris, commonly known as the Campestris glory of the snow, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features grass-like basal leaves and produces striking, upward-facing, yellowish-white flowers with a distinctive purple throat. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. It blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a valuable addition to rock gardens and alpine collections.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure, it is cultivated by enthusiasts of rare and exotic plants.
