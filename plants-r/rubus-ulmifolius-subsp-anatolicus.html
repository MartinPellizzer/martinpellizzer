Rubus ulmifolius subsp. anatolicus

Rubus ulmifolius subsp.

anatolicus, commonly known as Anatolian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and clustered.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
