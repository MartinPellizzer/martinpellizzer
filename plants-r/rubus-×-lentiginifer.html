Rubus × lentiginifer

Rubus × lentiginifer, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has a sprawling growth habit.

This species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
