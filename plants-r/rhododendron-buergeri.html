Rhododendron buergeri

Rhododendron buergeri, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy clusters of tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring with pink to red flowers that are often borne in racemes. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Rhododendron buergeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
