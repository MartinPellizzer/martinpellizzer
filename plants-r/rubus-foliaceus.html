Rubus foliaceus

Rubus foliaceus, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive hairy texture.

Rubus foliaceus thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
