Rubus fictus microgene subnitens

Rubus fictus microgene subnitens, commonly known as the silverleaf raspberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
