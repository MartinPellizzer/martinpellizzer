Rubus schleicheri var. eriocladus

Rubus schleicheri var. eriocladus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and horticultural collections.
