Rhododendron dauricum var. ciliatum

Rhododendron dauricum var. ciliatum, commonly known as the Daurian rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with ciliate margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
