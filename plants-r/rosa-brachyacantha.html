Rosa brachyacantha

Rosa brachyacantha, commonly known as the broad-leaved rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Africa, including South Africa, Namibia, and Zimbabwe. The plant is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Rosa brachyacantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
