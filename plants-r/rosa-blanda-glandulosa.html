Rosa blanda glandulosa

Rosa blanda glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular blanda rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in areas with moist, well-drained soils. The plant features slender, arching stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval leaflets with glandular dots along the edges.

Rosa blanda glandulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade.
