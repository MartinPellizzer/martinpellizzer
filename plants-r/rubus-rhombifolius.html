Rubus rhombifolius

Rubus rhombifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems, which support its climbing or trailing form. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its leaves are ovate to rhomboid in shape, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
