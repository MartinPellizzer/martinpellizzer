Rhododendron × rarilepidotum var. ootrichum

Rhododendron × rarilepidotum var. ootrichum, commonly known as the Ooty rhododendron, is a hybrid shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters, thriving in cool, moist climates with acidic soil. Its botanical features include elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and floral bracts that are often as showy as the flowers themselves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and high-altitude landscapes.
