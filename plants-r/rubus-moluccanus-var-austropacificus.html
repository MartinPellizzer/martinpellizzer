Rubus moluccanus var. austropacificus

Rubus moluccanus var. austropacificus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
