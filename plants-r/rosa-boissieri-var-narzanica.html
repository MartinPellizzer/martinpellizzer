Rosa boissieri var. narzanica

Rosa boissieri var. narzanica, commonly known as the Narzanica rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to harsh, alpine environments.
