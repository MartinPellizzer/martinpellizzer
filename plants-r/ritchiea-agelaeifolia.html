Ritchiea agelaeifolia

Ritchiea agelaeifolia, commonly known as the lace-leaf cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery foliage that consists of long, narrow, and often pale green leaves. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in botanical gardens and private collections.
