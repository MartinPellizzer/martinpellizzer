Rubus × macrophyllomorphus

Rubus × macrophyllomorphus, commonly known as the giant blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, wooded areas. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a glossy green upper surface and a hairy underside.

The plant produces showy white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by the development of its distinctive, large fruit.
