Rubus fagicola var. pallidulus

Rubus fagicola var. pallidulus, commonly known as the pale-flowered bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which contrast with its dark green foliage. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, edible berries in the fall.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
