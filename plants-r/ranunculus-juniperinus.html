Ranunculus juniperinus

Ranunculus juniperinus, commonly known as the juniper buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. Its botanical name reflects its association with juniper, though it is not closely related to the true junipers.

Ranunculus juniperinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
