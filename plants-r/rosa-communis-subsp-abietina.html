Rosa communis subsp. abietina

Rosa communis subsp.

abietina, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a subspecies native to mountainous regions in Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, dark green foliage.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, thriving in cooler climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
