Rubus lasiostylus f. glabratus

Rubus lasiostylus f. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth-styled blackberry, is a subspecies of the blackberry plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in woodland edges and moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its edible berries and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
