Ranunculus pueblensis

Ranunculus pueblensis, commonly known as the Pueblo buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers with five to eight petals and a hairy stem. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Ranunculus pueblensis is an important species in the Ranunculaceae family, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
