Rubus elongatus var. laevicalyx

Rubus elongatus var. laevicalyx, commonly known as the smooth-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its smooth, waxy leaf surfaces.

It is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its foliage is dense and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.

The botanical features of this variety make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
