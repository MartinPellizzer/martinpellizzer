Rosa canina f. luxemburgiana

Rosa canina f. luxemburgiana, commonly known as the Luxembourg rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, deep pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces oval, red-orange hips that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
