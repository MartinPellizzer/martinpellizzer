Ranunculus muricatus var. brasilianus

Ranunculus muricatus var. brasilianus, commonly known as the Brazilian hairy buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive hairy texture on its petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall rugged appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
