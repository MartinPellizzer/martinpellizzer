Ranunculus aquatilis subsp. baudotii

Ranunculus aquatilis subsp.

baudotii, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, streams, and wet meadows.

The plant features slender, branching stems with palmate leaves that are finely divided and often floating on the water surface. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with five to eight petals and numerous stamens.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to wet environments and its role in supporting aquatic ecosystems.
