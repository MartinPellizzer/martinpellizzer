Rabdosia coetsa

Rabdosia coetsa, commonly known as the broad-leaved rabdosia, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or blue.

Rabdosia coetsa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
