Rubus suboboranus

Rubus suboboranus, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a species of brambles native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a problematic weed in certain regions.
