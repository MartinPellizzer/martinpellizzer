Rubus foliosus subf. stenobothrys

Rubus foliosus subf.

stenobothrys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a subshrub characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are white and have five petals, typical of the Rosaceae family. The subsp.

stenobothrys is distinguished by its narrower, more elongated leaves compared to other Rubus species.
