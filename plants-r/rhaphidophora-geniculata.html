Rhaphidophora geniculata

Rhaphidophora geniculata, commonly known as the zigzag arrowroot, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features striking, arrow-shaped leaves with a zigzag pattern along the midrib, which gives it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Rhaphidophora geniculata is also valued for its starchy rhizomes, which have been used in traditional cuisine and medicine.
