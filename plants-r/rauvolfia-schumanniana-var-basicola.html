Rauvolfia schumanniana var. basicola

Rauvolfia schumanniana var. basicola, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
