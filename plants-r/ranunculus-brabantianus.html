Ranunculus brabantianus

Ranunculus brabantianus, commonly known as the Brabant buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Netherlands.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom from April to June.

It is an important species for pollinators and is considered a rare plant in its native habitat.
