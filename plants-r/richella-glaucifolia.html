Richella glaucifolia

Richella glaucifolia, commonly known as the blue-green richella, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and features small, glaucous (bluish-gray) leaves that reflect sunlight. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
