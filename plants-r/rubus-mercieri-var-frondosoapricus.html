Rubus mercieri var. frondosoapricus

Rubus mercieri var. frondosoapricus, commonly known as the frondose-apricus raspberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive frond-like appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
