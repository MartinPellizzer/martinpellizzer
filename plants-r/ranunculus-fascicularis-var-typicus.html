Ranunculus fascicularis var. typicus

Ranunculus fascicularis var. typicus, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, upright stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and it produces small, green, oval fruits.

The species is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.
