Rosa sicula subvar. ligustica

Rosa sicula subvar. ligustica, commonly known as the Ligurian rose, is a subspecies native to southern Italy, particularly the regions of Calabria and Sicily.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five petals and a prominent central pistil. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant, showy flowers that attract pollinators.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
