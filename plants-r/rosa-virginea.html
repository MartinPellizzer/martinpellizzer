Rosa virginea

Rosa virginea, commonly known as the Virgin's rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa virginea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
