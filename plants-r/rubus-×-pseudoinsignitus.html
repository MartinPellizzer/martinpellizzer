Rubus × pseudoinsignitus

Rubus × pseudoinsignitus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, characterized by its dark, thorny stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and riparian habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and local horticulture.
