Rheedia fruticosa

Rheedia fruticosa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Rheedia fruticosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
