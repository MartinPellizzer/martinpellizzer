Rosa spinosissima var. katacalyx

Rosa spinosissima var. katacalyx, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are often used in traditional floral arrangements. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a specimen plant in arid climates.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
