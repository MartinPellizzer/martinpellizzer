Rosa philippinensis

Rosa philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine rose, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring and summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, compound leaves, and an extensive root system that helps it thrive in various soil types.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has cultural significance in its native region.
