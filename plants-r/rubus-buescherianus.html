Rubus buescherianus

Rubus buescherianus, commonly known as the Buescher's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many species of raspberries and blackberries.
