Rhamnidium riograndense

Rhamnidium riograndense, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, star-shaped fruits that resemble those of the common starfruit. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, dark green leaves and yellowish flowers. Its fruits are edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and are often consumed fresh or used in local culinary traditions.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
