Rubus × imitabilis

Rubus × imitabilis, commonly known as the mimic raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that resemble those of true raspberries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are often used in jams and pies.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations.
