Rhododendron calendulaceum f. speciosum

Rhododendron calendulaceum f. speciosum, commonly known as the Nova Scotia flame azalea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It features showy, bright orange-red flowers that bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to woodland gardens. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden spaces.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
