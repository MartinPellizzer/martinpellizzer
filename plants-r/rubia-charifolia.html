Rubia charifolia

Rubia charifolia, commonly known as the Turkish coffee plant or Persian coffee tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
