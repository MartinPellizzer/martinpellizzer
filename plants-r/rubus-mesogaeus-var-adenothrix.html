Rubus mesogaeus var. adenothrix

Rubus mesogaeus var. adenothrix, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupes. It thrives in woodland habitats and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
