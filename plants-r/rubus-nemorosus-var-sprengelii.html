Rubus nemorosus var. sprengelii

Rubus nemorosus var. sprengelii, commonly known as the Sprengel's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in woodland and hedgerow habitats, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, hairy berries. It is native to Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important component of biodiversity in its native range.
