Ranunculus auricomus subsp. sjoersii

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

sjoersii, commonly known as the Sjoers' buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant has palmately divided leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive, waxy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
