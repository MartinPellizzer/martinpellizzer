Raphia monbuttorum var. macrocarpa

Raphia monbuttorum var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant palm or African fan palm, is a large, fast-growing palm native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, facilitating its wind-pollinated reproduction. Its fruit is a large, fibrous structure that contains a single seed, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and sustainable use of its fibers.

The species is also valued for its role in traditional practices and local economies.
