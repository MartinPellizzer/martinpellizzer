Rorippa hispida subsp. hispida

Rorippa hispida subsp.

hispida, commonly known as the hispid water-cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its rosette of hairy, alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist or aquatic environments, such as ponds, ditches, and stream margins. Its small, white or yellowish flowers are arranged in racemes and attract various pollinators.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, often disturbed water habitats.
