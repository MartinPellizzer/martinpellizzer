Rosa × collina var. constricta

Rosa × collina var. constricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf rock rose, is a compact, shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it is well adapted to rocky and arid environments. It is often used in rock gardens due to its hardiness and ornamental value.

The variety is noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resistance to pests and diseases.
