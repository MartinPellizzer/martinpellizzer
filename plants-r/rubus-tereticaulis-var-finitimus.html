Rubus tereticaulis var. finitimus

Rubus tereticaulis var. finitimus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features trailing stems with thornless, trifoliate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically borne in clusters. Its flowers are white and have five petals, typical of the Rubus genus.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
