Rubus pyramidalis

Rubus pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its berries, which are used in culinary applications.
