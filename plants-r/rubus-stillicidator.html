Rubus stillicidator

Rubus stillicidator, commonly known as the weeping blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple or black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
