Rosa permutata

Rosa permutata, commonly known as the everblooming rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring multiple stems covered in thorns. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom continuously throughout the growing season. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Rosa permutata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its persistent flowering and hardy nature.
