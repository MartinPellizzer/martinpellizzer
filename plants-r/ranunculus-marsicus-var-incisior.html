Ranunculus marsicus var. incisior

Ranunculus marsicus var. incisior, commonly known as the incisate marsh buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant features trifoliate leaves with deeply lobed or incised margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and marshy areas.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
