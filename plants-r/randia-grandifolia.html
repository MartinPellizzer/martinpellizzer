Randia grandifolia

Randia grandifolia, commonly known as the largeflower randia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Randia grandifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
