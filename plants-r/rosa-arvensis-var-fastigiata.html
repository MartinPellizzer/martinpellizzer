Rosa arvensis var. fastigiata

Rosa arvensis var. fastigiata, commonly known as the upright dog rose, is a compact, shrub-like species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, narrow leaves, and fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped rose hips that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens and wildflower meadows.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
