Rubus pampinosus

Rubus pampinosus, commonly known as the Chilean blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets, which are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
