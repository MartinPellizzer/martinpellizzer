Rosa gallica f. stenotricha

Rosa gallica f. stenotricha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gallica rose, is a botanical cultivar of the species Rosa gallica.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, deep pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and serrated, typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its fragrance and visual impact.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for shrub borders and floral arrangements.
