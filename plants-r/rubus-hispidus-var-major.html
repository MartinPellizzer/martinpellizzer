Rubus hispidus var. major

Rubus hispidus var. major, commonly known as the rough raspberry or brambles, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of small, red drupelets. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of Europe, Asia, and North America. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The plant is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed areas, where it can spread aggressively through its extensive root system.
