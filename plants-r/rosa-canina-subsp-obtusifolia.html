Rosa canina subsp. obtusifolia

Rosa canina subsp.

obtusifolia, commonly known as the prickly rose or dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves with serrated edges, and fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces aggregate fruits called rosehips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.

This subspecies is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
