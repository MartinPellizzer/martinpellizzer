Rubus humifusus proles incultus

Rubus humifusus proles incultus, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are prostrate, often rooting at the nodes to form dense mats.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and role in supporting local wildlife.
