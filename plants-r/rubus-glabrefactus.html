Rubus glabrefactus

Rubus glabrefactus, commonly known as the smooth blackberry.

It is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is native to eastern North America and thrives in woodland and disturbed habitats. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, which is typically dark purple to black when mature, is edible and often used in culinary applications.
