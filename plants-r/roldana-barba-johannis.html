Roldana barba-johannis

Roldana barba-johannis, commonly known as the beard of St. John, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
