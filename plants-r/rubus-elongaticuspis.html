Rubus elongaticuspis

Rubus elongaticuspis, commonly known as the long-pointed blackberry.

It is a species of shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are white and have five petals, typical of the Rubus genus.

The plant thrives in woodland areas and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
