Rubus canus

Rubus canus, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble, is a species of shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and dark green leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, edible berries in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
