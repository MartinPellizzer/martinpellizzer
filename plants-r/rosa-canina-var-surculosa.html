Rosa canina var. surculosa

Rosa canina var. surculosa, commonly known as the Turkish rose or wild dog rose, is a variation of the common dogwood rose.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces oval, red-orange hips that are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include simple, pinnate leaves and a woody structure typical of rose species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
