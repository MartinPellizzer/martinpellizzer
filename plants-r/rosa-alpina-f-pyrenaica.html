Rosa alpina f. pyrenaica

Rosa alpina f. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean rock rose, is a subspecies of the alpine rose native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
