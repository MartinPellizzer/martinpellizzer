Rhamnus californica var. crassifolia

Rhamnus californica var. crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by black, oval berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Its dense growth habit makes it a valuable plant for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
