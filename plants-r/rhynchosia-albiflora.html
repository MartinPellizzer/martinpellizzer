Rhynchosia albiflora

Rhynchosia albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its pods are typically slender and contain edible seeds that are used in various traditional cuisines.

Rhynchosia albiflora is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a beneficial plant in agricultural systems.
