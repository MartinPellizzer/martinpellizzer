Rubus scaberrimus var. congestus

Rubus scaberrimus var. congestus, commonly known as the rough raspberry or prickly blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep red or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
