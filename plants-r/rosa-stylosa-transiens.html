Rosa stylosa transiens

Rosa stylosa transiens, commonly known as the variable shrub rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of eastern Asia, including China and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a serrated edge, while its hips produce small, red, edible fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
