Rosettea echinata

Rosettea echinata, commonly known as the spiny rosette plant, is a succulent native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It forms a dense, compact rosette of fleshy, spiky leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The leaves are typically blue-green in color with white or yellowish spines along the edges. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators like bees and moths.

This plant is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
