Retinispora monstrosa

Retinispora monstrosa, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Retinispora monstrosa is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
