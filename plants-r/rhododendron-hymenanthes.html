Rhododendron hymenanthes

Rhododendron hymenanthes, commonly known as the white rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blossoms.

Rhododendron hymenanthes thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
