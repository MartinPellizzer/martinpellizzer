Rosa gallica agatha

Rosa gallica agatha, commonly known as the Agatha rose, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its fragrant, deep pink to red petals. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green foliage. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, showy flowers.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
