Rubus macrophyllus var. albiflorus

Rubus macrophyllus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower evergreen raspberry, is a variant of the evergreen bramble native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are often dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, hairy berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
