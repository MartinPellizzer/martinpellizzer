Rosa tomentosa f. nuda

Rosa tomentosa f. nuda, commonly known as the naked woolly rose, is a botanical variant of the woolly rose.

It is characterized by its reduced pubescence compared to the typical form, giving it a smoother appearance. The plant is a deciduous shrub with compound leaves and fragrant flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in horticultural and taxonomic studies.
