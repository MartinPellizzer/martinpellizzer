Ranunculus hayekii

Ranunculus hayekii, commonly known as the Hayek's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or orange flowers with prominent sepals. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
