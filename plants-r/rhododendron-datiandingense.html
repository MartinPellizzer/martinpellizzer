Rhododendron datiandingense

Rhododendron datiandingense, commonly known as the Datianding rhododendron, is a species native to China, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it is relatively rare in cultivation.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in its native habitat, supporting local biodiversity.
