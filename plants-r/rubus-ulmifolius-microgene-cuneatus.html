Rubus ulmifolius microgene cuneatus

Rubus ulmifolius microgene cuneatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically clustered together. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and horticultural use.
