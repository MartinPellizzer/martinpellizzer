Rosa uriensis var. typica

Rosa uriensis var. typica, commonly known as the Colorado rose, is a species native to the United States, particularly found in the Rocky Mountain region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring arching stems with thorny branches. The plant produces showy, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it is well adapted to dry, rocky soils.

The Colorado rose is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
