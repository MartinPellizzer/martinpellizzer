Rosa morgana

Rosa morgana, commonly known as the Morgana rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its botanical features include a woody stem, alternate leaf arrangement, and showy, single or semi-double flowers.

Rosa morgana is native to parts of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
