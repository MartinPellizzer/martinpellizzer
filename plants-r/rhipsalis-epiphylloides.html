Rhipsalis epiphylloides

Rhipsalis epiphylloides, commonly known as the leafy cactus or the false mistletoe, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that resemble leaves, which are actually modified stems covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically borne in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unusual appearance and growth habit, it is a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
