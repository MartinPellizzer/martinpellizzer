Rhipsalis comorensis

Rhipsalis comorensis, commonly known as the Madagascar mistletoe, is a climbing cactus native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can trail or climb over trees. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its leaves are reduced to small, hair-like structures, and it lacks true leaves in the conventional sense.

Rhipsalis comorensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
