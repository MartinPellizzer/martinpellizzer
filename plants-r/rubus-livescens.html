Rubus livescens

Rubus livescens, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Rubus livescens is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruits.
