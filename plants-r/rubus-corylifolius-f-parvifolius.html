Rubus corylifolius f. parvifolius

Rubus corylifolius f. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to East Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The foliage and fruit make it an attractive addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
