Rhododendron callimorphum

Rhododendron callimorphum, commonly known as the purple-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high elevations. Its leaves are densely packed and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are often pink or purple in color.

Rhododendron callimorphum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
