Rubus mucronatus f. atrichantherus

Rubus mucronatus f. atrichantherus, commonly known as the smooth-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its smooth, hairless fruit surface.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of North America, particularly the southeastern United States. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically less flavorful compared to other blackberry varieties. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually serrated along the edges.

This form is notable for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.
