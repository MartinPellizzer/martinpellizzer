Rhynchospora plumosa

Rhynchospora plumosa, commonly known as the plumose flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in the Americas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. Rhynchospora plumosa is adapted to moist environments and often thrives in areas with standing water or saturated soil.

Its foliage is often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive, fluffy appearance.
