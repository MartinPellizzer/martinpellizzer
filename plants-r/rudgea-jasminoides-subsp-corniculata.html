Rudgea jasminoides subsp. corniculata

Rudgea jasminoides subsp.

corniculata, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
