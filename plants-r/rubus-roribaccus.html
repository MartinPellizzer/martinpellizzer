Rubus roribaccus

Rubus roribaccus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a species of bramble in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing or shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of bright red, hairy fruits. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.

The fruits are edible and widely used in culinary applications.
