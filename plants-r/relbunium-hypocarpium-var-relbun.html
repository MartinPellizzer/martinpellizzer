Relbunium hypocarpium var. relbun

Relbunium hypocarpium var. relbun, commonly known as the small-flowered relbunium, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is found in temperate regions of Europe. The plant has slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in compact clusters. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a serrated margin, and it produces small, dry fruits.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
