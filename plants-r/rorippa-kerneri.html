Rorippa kerneri

Rorippa kerneri, commonly known as the Japanese watercress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to East Asia, this plant thrives in moist environments such as stream banks and wetlands. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine, and it is also cultivated for its edible leaves.

The plant's rapid growth and ability to colonize wet areas make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
