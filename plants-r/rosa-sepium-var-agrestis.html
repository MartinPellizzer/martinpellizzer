Rosa sepium var. agrestis

Rosa sepium var. agrestis, commonly known as the rough rose, is a variant of the rose species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five to seven petals and a strong, sweet fragrance. This plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
