Ranunculus homophyllus

Ranunculus homophyllus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a vibrant, low-maintenance ornamental plant.
