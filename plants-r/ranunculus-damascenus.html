Ranunculus damascenus

Ranunculus damascenus, commonly known as the Damascus buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a reddish or violet tinge at the base. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ranunculus damascenus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
