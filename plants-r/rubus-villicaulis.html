Rubus villicaulis

Rubus villicaulis, commonly known as the silky raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, often reddish stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are alternate, with serrated margins and a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
