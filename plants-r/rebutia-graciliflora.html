Rebutia graciliflora

Rebutia graciliflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered rebutia, is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has a shallow root system and is well adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

Rebutia graciliflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
