Rohdea japonica var. latifolia

Rohdea japonica var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved lily or Japanese lily, is a species native to eastern Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm. The plant features broad, elliptical leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in a spadix-like structure, typical of the Araceae family.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.
