Rubus schleicheri var. grandicuspis

Rubus schleicheri var. grandicuspis, commonly known as the large-cusped blackberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
