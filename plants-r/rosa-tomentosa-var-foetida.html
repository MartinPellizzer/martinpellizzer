Rosa tomentosa var. foetida

Rosa tomentosa var. foetida, commonly known as the stinking rose, is a variant of the woolly rose.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and clusters of small, pale pink flowers. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces fragrant, though sometimes pungent, rose hips that are rich in seeds.

This shrub is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
