Rubus idaeus f. marmoratus

Rubus idaeus f. marmoratus, commonly known as the marbled raspberries, is a unique cultivar of the red raspberry plant.

It is distinguished by its striking variegated leaves that display a mosaic of green, white, and pink hues. The plant produces small, tart berries that are typically smaller than those of standard raspberries. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil.

This ornamental variety is often grown for its aesthetic appeal as well as its edible fruits.
