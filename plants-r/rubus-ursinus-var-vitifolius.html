Rubus ursinus var. vitifolius

Rubus ursinus var. vitifolius, commonly known as the blackberry vine, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its distinctive leaf shape and the presence of thorns along its stems.
