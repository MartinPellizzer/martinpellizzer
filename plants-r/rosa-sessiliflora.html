Rosa sessiliflora

Rosa sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flower rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while the stems are covered in fine, velvety hairs.

Rosa sessiliflora is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive flowers and hardy nature.
