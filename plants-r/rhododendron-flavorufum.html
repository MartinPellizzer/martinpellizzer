Rhododendron flavorufum

Rhododendron flavorufum, commonly known as the fragrant rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a lush backdrop for its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron flavorufum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland settings.
