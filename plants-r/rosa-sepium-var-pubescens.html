Rosa sepium var. pubescens

Rosa sepium var. pubescens, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are dark green, glossy, and oval-shaped, often with a slightly serrated edge. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
