Rosa arizonica var. granulifera

Rosa arizonica var. granulifera, commonly known as the Arizona rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Sonoran Desert.

It produces showy, fragrant pink to red flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has small, oval leaves with a fuzzy texture and dark green color. Its stems are covered in thorny buds, which provide protection against herbivores.

This variety is adapted to arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
