Rabiea albinota

Rabiea albinota, commonly known as the white-spotted lily, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking white petals with distinctive yellow spots. The plant typically grows in forested areas and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, while its stems are often hairy.

Rabiea albinota is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
