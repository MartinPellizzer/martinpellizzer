Rubus × blechracanthus

Rubus × blechracanthus, commonly known as the blackberry-boys or the blackcap bramble, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that resemble blackberries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and have a serrated margin.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit is edible, though often tart and fibrous.
