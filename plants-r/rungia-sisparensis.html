Rungia sisparensis

Rungia sisparensis, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a sweet, fragrant aroma. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Rungia sisparensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
