Rosa tomentella var. friedlaenderiana

Rosa tomentella var. friedlaenderiana, commonly known as the Friedlaender's woolly rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in horticulture.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique texture and subtle floral display.
