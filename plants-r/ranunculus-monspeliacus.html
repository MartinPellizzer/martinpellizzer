Ranunculus monspeliacus

Ranunculus monspeliacus, commonly known as the Italian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland areas. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers often have a distinctive, hairy appearance.

The plant is also known for its toxic properties, containing protoanemonin, which can cause skin irritation.
