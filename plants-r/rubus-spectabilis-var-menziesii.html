Rubus spectabilis var. menziesii

Rubus spectabilis var. menziesii, commonly known as the salmon berry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or orange in color, turning bright red when ripe. These berries are often used in jams, jellies, and as a flavoring in various culinary applications.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
