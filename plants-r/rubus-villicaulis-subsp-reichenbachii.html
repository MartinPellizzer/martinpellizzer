Rubus villicaulis subsp. reichenbachii

Rubus villicaulis subsp.

reichenbachii, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species within the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and disturbed soils.

Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its vigorous growth habit.
