Rudbeckia nudiflora

Rudbeckia nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-stem sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, hairy stems that are often reddish in color and bear yellow-orange ray flowers with dark centers. The plant typically grows between 1 to 3 feet tall and produces showy blooms from late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Rudbeckia nudiflora is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
