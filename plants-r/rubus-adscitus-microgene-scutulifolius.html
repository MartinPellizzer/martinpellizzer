Rubus adscitus microgene scutulifolius

Rubus adscitus microgene scutulifolius, commonly known as the small-fruited bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically small and serrated. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and a part of local ecosystems.
