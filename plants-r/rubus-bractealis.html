Rubus bractealis

Rubus bractealis, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
