Rubus questieri var. ampliflorens

Rubus questieri var. ampliflorens, commonly known as the broad-flowered blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the southern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it is valued for both ornamental and edible purposes.
