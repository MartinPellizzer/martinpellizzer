Rosa systyla f. rusticella

Rosa systyla f. rusticella, commonly known as the rustic rose, is a compact, shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces hips that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for garden cultivation.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a decorative plant highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
