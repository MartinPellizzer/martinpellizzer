Rubus hirtus var. weiheanus

Rubus hirtus var. weiheanus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
