Rapanea poumensis

Rapanea poumensis, commonly known as the Madagascar olive, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy dark green surface. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible.

The tree is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
