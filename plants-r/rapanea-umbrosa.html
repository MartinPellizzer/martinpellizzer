Rapanea umbrosa

Rapanea umbrosa, commonly known as the darkwood tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, thriving in humid and cool conditions. Its small, fragrant flowers are followed by dark, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Rapanea umbrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
