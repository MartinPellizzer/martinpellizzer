Rafnia angulata var. latifolia

Rafnia angulata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its rhizomes are the primary part used, containing starch that is harvested for culinary purposes.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and its role in traditional agriculture.
