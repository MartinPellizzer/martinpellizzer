Racosperma tenuissimum

Racosperma tenuissimum, commonly known as the slender-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its small, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers make it visually distinct.

It is often used in ecological restoration due to its resilience and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
