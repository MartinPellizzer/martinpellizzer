Radicula pectinata

Radicula pectinata, commonly known as the peeling root plant, is a unique species characterized by its fibrous, root-like structures that resemble small, branching tendrils.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest floors where it forms a dense, carpet-like growth. The plant's most distinctive feature is its ability to absorb moisture and nutrients through its root-like stems, which are covered in a thin, pectin-rich layer. Its leaves are typically small, scale-like, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Radicula pectinata plays a minor but ecologically significant role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its native habitat.
