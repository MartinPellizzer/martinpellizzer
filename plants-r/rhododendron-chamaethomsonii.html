Rhododendron chamaethomsonii

Rhododendron chamaethomsonii, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows in cool, moist climates at high altitudes, often found in the Himalayas and neighboring regions. The plant features dense, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with acidic soil.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
