Rubus × asperidentifrons

Rubus × asperidentifrons, commonly known as the rough-leaved blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a valued crop in some regions.
