Rubus schultzii

Rubus schultzii, commonly known as Schultze's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. The plant is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are similar to other blackberry species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
