Rubus × semichlorifolius

Rubus × semichlorifolius, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny canes and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant year-round. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and hardy shrub in the蔷薇科 (Rosaceae) family.
