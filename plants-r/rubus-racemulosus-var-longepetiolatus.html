Rubus racemulosus var. longepetiolatus

Rubus racemulosus var. longepetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled blackberry shrub, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, slender petioles that distinguish it from other Rubus species. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated margins, and it typically grows in moist, wooded areas.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential for fruit production.
