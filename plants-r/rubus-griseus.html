Rubus griseus

Rubus griseus, commonly known as the gray raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and ecosystems.
