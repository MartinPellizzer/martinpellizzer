Rubia lucida var. pubescens

Rubia lucida var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy woodruff, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, white flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pubescens.". It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The flowers produce small, dark blue or black berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals.
