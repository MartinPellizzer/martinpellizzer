Rosa propinqua

Rosa propinqua, commonly known as the close relative of the rose, is a species native to the southern United States.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are oval and serrated. Rosa propinqua produces small, red, berry-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
