Racosperma pachycarpum

Racosperma pachycarpum, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a species of legume native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces woody pods containing seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in shrublands and woodlands.

Racosperma pachycarpum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
