Rheum palmatum var. tanguticum

Rheum palmatum var. tanguticum, commonly known as the Tibetan rhubarb, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of Tibet and northern China.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by reddish-brown, spiny fruits. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

The roots of this plant are valued in traditional medicine for their medicinal properties.
