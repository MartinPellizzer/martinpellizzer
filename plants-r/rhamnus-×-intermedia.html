Rhamnus × intermedia

Rhamnus × intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid common buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub that results from the cross between Rhamnus cathartica and Rhamnus alnifolia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy appearance with serrated edges.

Rhamnus × intermedia is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
