Rosa tomentosa var. cuspidata

Rosa tomentosa var. cuspidata, commonly known as the woolly rose or the bristly rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage and small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has thorny stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardiness. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for garden borders or hedges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Rosaceae family.
