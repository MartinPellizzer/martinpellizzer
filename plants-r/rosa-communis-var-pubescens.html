Rosa communis var. pubescens

Rosa communis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a variant of the common rose characterized by its soft, pubescent stems and leaves.

This plant typically produces fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The hairy rose is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in garden settings.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both landscape design and floral arrangements.
