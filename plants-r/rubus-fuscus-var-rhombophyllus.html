Rubus fuscus var. rhombophyllus

Rubus fuscus var. rhombophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry.

It is a shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its compound leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often tart in flavor. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
