Rubia fruticosa subsp. melanocarpa

Rubia fruticosa subsp.

melanocarpa, commonly known as blackcurrant, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and vitamin C. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually serrated and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles.
