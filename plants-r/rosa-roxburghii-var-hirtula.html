Rosa roxburghii var. hirtula

Rosa roxburghii var. hirtula, commonly known as the Hirtula rose, is a shrub native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and large, fragrant pink or white flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy underside. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.
