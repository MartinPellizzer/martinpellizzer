Rhododendron eheinense

Rhododendron eheinense, commonly known as the Eheinense azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

Rhododendron eheinense is also noted for its hardiness and ability to adapt to a variety of climatic conditions.
