Rapanea pittieri

Rapanea pittieri, commonly known as the Malabar rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy surface. It produces small, white flowers that are followed by dark purple berries, which are an important food source for various bird species.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
