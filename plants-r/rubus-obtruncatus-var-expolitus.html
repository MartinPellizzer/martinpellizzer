Rubus obtruncatus var. expolitus

Rubus obtruncatus var. expolitus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white and clustered in the axils of the leaves.

The variety is distinguished by its dense, hairy covering on the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.
