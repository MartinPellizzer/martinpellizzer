Rubus vestitus

Rubus vestitus, commonly known as the velvet raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in woodland or shaded environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
