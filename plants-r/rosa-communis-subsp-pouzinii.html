Rosa communis subsp. pouzinii

Rosa communis subsp.

pouzinii, commonly known as the Pouzin rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white.

The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces rose hips that are typically red and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
