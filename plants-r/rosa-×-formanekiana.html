Rosa × formanekiana

Rosa × formanekiana, commonly known as the Formanekiana rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from crossbreeding between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it a hardy plant suitable for both garden and landscape use. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and serrated, providing an attractive foliage contrast.

Rosa × formanekiana is also valued for its disease resistance and adaptability to different climates.
