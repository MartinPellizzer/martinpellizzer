Rosa canina var. uncinella

Rosa canina var. uncinella, commonly known as the hooked rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are oval and serrated.
