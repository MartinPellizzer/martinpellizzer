Rehmannia glutinosa var. purpurea

Rehmannia glutinosa var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple rehmannia, is a variant of the Chinese foxglove.

It is a perennial herb native to China, characterized by its distinctive purple flowers and fleshy, reddish roots. The plant belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its roots are widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's unique coloration and medicinal properties make it a notable species in both horticulture and herbal practices.
