Rosa caryophyllacea f. calva

Rosa caryophyllacea f. calva, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed dog rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose species.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and reduced thorns compared to the typical form. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
