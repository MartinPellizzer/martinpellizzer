Ranunculus insignis var. lobulatus

Ranunculus insignis var. lobulatus, commonly known as the lobed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, which bloom in early spring. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of soft, white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
