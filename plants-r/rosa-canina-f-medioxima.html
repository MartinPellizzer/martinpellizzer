Rosa canina f. medioxima

Rosa canina f. medioxima, commonly known as the Turkish rose or the Turkish dog rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves.

Its hips, which are the fruit, are small, orange-red, and contain a high concentration of vitamin C. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
