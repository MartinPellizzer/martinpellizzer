Raphanus sylvestris subsp. landra

Raphanus sylvestris subsp.

landra, commonly known as the wild radish, is a biennial or perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and pinnately lobed leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by round, swollen fruits that resemble turnips. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in fields.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural habitats.
