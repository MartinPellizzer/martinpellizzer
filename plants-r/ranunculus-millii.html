Ranunculus millii

Ranunculus millii, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy areas and has a short, hairy stem. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a waxy coating.

Ranunculus millii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
