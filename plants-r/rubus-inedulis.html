Rubus inedulis

Rubus inedulis, commonly known as the salmonberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and its ability to form dense thickets. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit, which resembles a raspberry, is edible and has a tangy, slightly sweet flavor.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.
