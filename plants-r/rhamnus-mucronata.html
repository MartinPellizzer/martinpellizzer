Rhamnus mucronata

Rhamnus mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are inconspicuous, and it produces small, dark berries that are edible for some bird species.

The plant has a woody stem and is often used in hedges or as an ornamental plant in gardens.
