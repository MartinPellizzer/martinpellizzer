Rosa communis var. typica

Rosa communis var. typica, commonly known as the common rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It is native to Asia but has been widely cultivated in temperate regions around the world.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
