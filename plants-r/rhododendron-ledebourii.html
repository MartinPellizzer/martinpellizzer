Rhododendron ledebourii

Rhododendron ledebourii, commonly known as the Ledebour's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is known for its adaptability to cold and arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler regions.
