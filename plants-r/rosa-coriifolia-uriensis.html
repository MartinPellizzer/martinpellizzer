Rosa coriifolia uriensis

Rosa coriifolia uriensis, commonly known as the Urine Rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Native to specific regions in East Asia, it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture and botanical gardens.
