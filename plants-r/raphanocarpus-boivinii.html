Raphanocarpus boivinii

Raphanocarpus boivinii, commonly known as the mountain turnip, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features a short, stout stem with broad, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in the spring.

The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
