Rubus radula var. callophyllus

Rubus radula var. callophyllus, commonly known as the California blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and firm. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in coastal and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
