Rubus truncifactus

Rubus truncifactus, commonly known as the truncated blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is notable for its aggressive growth habit and ability to form dense thickets.
