Ruellia caroliniensis var. semicalva

Ruellia caroliniensis var. semicalva, commonly known as the Carolina rosemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom from late spring to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
