Rubus corylifolius var. sublustris

Rubus corylifolius var. sublustris, commonly known as the glossy bramble, is a variant of the bramble family with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, trifoliate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, aggregated fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
