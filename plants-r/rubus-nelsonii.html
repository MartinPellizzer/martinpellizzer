Rubus nelsonii

Rubus nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's blackberry, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, particularly in Washington and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small, dark berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them a valuable resource for both wildlife and humans.
