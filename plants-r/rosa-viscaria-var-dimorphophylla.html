Rosa viscaria var. dimorphophylla

Rosa viscaria var. dimorphophylla, commonly known as the variable bristly rose, is a species native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive serrated margin.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in garden settings.
