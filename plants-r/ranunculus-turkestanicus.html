Ranunculus turkestanicus

Ranunculus turkestanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow to orange flowers with five to seven petals, typically arranged in a cup-like structure. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
