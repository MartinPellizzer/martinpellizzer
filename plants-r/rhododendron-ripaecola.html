Rhododendron ripaecola

Rhododendron ripaecola, commonly known as the Andes rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments such as cloud forests and mountainous areas. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink and purple.

Rhododendron ripaecola is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and requires specific climatic conditions to flourish.
