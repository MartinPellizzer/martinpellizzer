Ranunculus biclaterae

Ranunculus biclaterae, commonly known as the two-lobed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves and showy yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Ranunculus biclaterae is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
