Rubus × pseudopapulosus

Rubus × pseudopapulosus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along fences.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves gives it its common name and distinguishes it from other Rubus species.
