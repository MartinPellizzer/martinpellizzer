Rosettea peregrina

Rosettea peregrina, commonly known as the wandering rosette, is a unique succulent characterized by its compact, circular rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves.

Native to the arid regions of South America, it thrives in environments with minimal rainfall and intense sunlight. The plant's leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh habitat. It produces small, yellow flowers during the warm season, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a prized plant among succulent enthusiasts.
