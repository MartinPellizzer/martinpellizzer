Rosa canina f. solbergensis

Rosa canina f. solbergensis, commonly known as the Solberg's dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral morphology, featuring larger, more showy blooms compared to the typical Rosa canina. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It produces red-orange hips that are rich in vitamin C and are used in traditional medicine.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for wildlife.
