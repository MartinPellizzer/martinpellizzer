Ranunculus diffusus var. diffusus

Ranunculus diffusus var. diffusus, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
