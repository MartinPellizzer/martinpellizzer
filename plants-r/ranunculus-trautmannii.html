Ranunculus trautmannii

Ranunculus trautmannii, commonly known as the Trautmann's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. The plant features a basal rosette of delicate, palmately lobed leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five to seven sepals. It is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Ranunculus trautmannii is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
