Rubus idaeus subsp. komarovii

Rubus idaeus subsp.

komarovii, commonly known as the Komarovii raspberry, is a subspecies native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea. It is a shrub characterized by its biennial growth habit, with stems that produce fruit in their second year.

The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The berries are typically red or yellow, with a tart flavor, and are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
