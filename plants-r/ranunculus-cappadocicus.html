Ranunculus cappadocicus

Ranunculus cappadocicus, commonly known as the Cappadocian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

Ranunculus cappadocicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
