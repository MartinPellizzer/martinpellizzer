Relbunium megapotamicum subsp. camporum

Relbunium megapotamicum subsp.

camporum, commonly known as the Campor grass, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Uruguay. It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, often in dry or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered along the stem.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential for use in restoration projects.
