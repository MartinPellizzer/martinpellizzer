Rosa sarmatica subsp. romanica

Rosa sarmatica subsp.

romanica, commonly known as the Roman rose, is a species native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
