Rosa × alba var. rubicunda

Rosa × alba var. rubicunda, commonly known as the Crimson White Rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its striking combination of white and red petals.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The flowers are large, showy, and bloom in late spring to early summer, often with a strong, sweet fragrance. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in a range of climates.

Its botanical name reflects its parentage, derived from Rosa alba (white rose) and Rosa rubicunda (red rose).
