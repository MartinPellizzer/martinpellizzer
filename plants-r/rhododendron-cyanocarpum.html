Rhododendron cyanocarpum

Rhododendron cyanocarpum, commonly known as the blue-fruited rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are typically toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

Rhododendron cyanocarpum is often found in temperate forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
