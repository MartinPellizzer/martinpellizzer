Rosa sweginzowii var. stevensii

Rosa sweginzowii var. stevensii, commonly known as the Stevenses rose, is a variant of the Sweginzow rose, native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem structure, hairy leaf surfaces, and an overall hardy nature suited to temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
