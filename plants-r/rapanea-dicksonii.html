Rapanea dicksonii

Rapanea dicksonii, commonly known as the Dickson's laurel, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its dense foliage and attractive appearance.
