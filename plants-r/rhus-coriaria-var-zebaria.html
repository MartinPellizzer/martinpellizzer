Rhus coriaria var. zebaria

Rhus coriaria var. zebaria, commonly known as the Aleppo pine or sea buckthorn, is a variant of the common buckthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruits are small, red berries that are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in coastal and rocky areas, showing adaptability to saline and dry conditions.
