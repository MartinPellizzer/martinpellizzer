Rosa pseudovenosa

Rosa pseudovenosa, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Afghanistan, Iran, and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant typically grows as a shrub.

Rosa pseudovenosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for centuries in horticulture.
