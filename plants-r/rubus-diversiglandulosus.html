Rubus diversiglandulosus

Rubus diversiglandulosus, commonly known as the glandular raspberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. Its flowers are pink to white and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Rubus diversiglandulosus is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
