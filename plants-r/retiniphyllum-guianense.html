Retiniphyllum guianense

Retiniphyllum guianense, commonly known as the Guianan retiniphyllum, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family, native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, with a velvety texture and deep green coloration. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
