Ranunculus dzhavakheticus

Ranunculus dzhavakheticus, commonly known as the Caucasian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or orange, petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in early spring.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
