Rosa × muscosa variegata

Rosa × muscosa variegata, commonly known as the variegated muscosa rose, is a hybrid species of rose characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage.

It features broad, leathery leaves with a striking combination of green and white or yellow markings. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and unique leaf patterns.
