Rubus × pubipes

Rubus × pubipes, commonly known as the purple-leafed blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and compound leaves that often exhibit a striking purple or red hue. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
