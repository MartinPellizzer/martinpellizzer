Rhynchospora cyperoides var. longifructus

Rhynchospora cyperoides var. longifructus, commonly known as the long-fruited flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or reddish in color, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in wetland or marshy environments, often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a food source for various insect species and its contribution to wetland biodiversity.
