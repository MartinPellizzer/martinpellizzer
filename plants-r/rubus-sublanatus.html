Rubus sublanatus

Rubus sublanatus, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is often found in woodland edges and shrublands. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are usually white or pink, and it produces red or black berries that are edible but often tart.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
