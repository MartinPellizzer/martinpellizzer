Ranunculus kamchaticus

Ranunculus kamchaticus, commonly known as the Kamchatka buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Russian Far East and parts of northern Asia.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy, with bright yellow flowers that have five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountain meadows. It produces a capsule fruit containing numerous small seeds.

Ranunculus kamchaticus is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, daisy-like blooms.
