Rhododendron vanderbiltianum

Rhododendron vanderbiltianum, commonly known as the Vanderbilt rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and grows in clusters, often forming thickets in its natural habitat. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
