Radiata glabra

Radiata glabra, commonly known as the smooth radiata, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to parts of southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soils.

Radiata glabra is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
