Rubus idaeus f. niveus

Rubus idaeus f. niveus, commonly known as the white-fruited raspberry.

This variant of the raspberry plant is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and smaller, sweeter fruits compared to the typical red raspberry. It is a deciduous shrub with arching canes and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fruit flavor.
