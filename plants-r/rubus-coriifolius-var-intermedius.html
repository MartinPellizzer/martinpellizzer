Rubus coriifolius var. intermedius

Rubus coriifolius var. intermedius, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry or red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. The plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, loamy soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it is often cultivated for its edible berries.

It has a woody stem structure with thorn-like projections, typical of many Rubus species.
