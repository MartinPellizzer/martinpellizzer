Rubus idaeiphyllus

Rubus idaeiphyllus, commonly known as the golden raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-orange fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its flowers are pinkish-white and appear in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit.
