Racosperma muellerianum

Racosperma muellerianum, commonly known as the Mueller's acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can remain viable in the soil for many years.
