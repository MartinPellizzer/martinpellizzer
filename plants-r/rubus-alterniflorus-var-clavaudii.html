Rubus alterniflorus var. clavaudii

Rubus alterniflorus var. clavaudii, commonly known as the Clavaud's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its alternate leaf arrangement and the presence of stipules at the base of its leaflets.
