Rubus glandulosus var. hirtus

Rubus glandulosus var. hirtus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and hairy leaves, which are covered in fine, glandular trichomes. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, containing several drupelets. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and local biodiversity.
