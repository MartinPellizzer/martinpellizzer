Rubus pilosus var. vulgaris

Rubus pilosus var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common bramble, is a thorny shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in jams and pies. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates.

Rubus pilosus var. vulgaris is known for its aggressive growth and ability to form dense thickets.
