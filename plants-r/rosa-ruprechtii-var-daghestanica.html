Rosa ruprechtii var. daghestanica

Rosa ruprechtii var. daghestanica, commonly known as the Daghestan rose, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, leathery foliage. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers with a delicate scent, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring moderate watering.

Due to its limited distribution and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
