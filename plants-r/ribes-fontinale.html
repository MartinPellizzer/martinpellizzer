Ribes fontinale

Ribes fontinale, commonly known as the alpine gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant produces small, tart berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins and a slightly hairy texture.

Ribes fontinale is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
