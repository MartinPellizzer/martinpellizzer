Rabdosia volkensiana

Rabdosia volkensiana, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Rabdosia volkensiana is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its traditional medicinal uses.
