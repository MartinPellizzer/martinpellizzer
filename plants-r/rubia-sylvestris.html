Rubia sylvestris

Rubia sylvestris, commonly known as woodruff, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and gardenias. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive scent. Woodruff is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along stream banks.

It has been historically used in herbal medicine and as a flavoring agent in beverages.
