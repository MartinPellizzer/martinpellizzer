Rubus amoeniflorens

Rubus amoeniflorens, commonly known as the sweet thimbleberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and is native to the western United States, particularly California. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it bears white to pink flowers in the spring.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
