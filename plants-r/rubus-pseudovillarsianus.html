Rubus pseudovillarsianus

Rubus pseudovillarsianus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
