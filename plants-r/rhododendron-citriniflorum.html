Rhododendron citriniflorum

Rhododendron citriniflorum, commonly known as the goldenflower azalea, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

Rhododendron citriniflorum prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
