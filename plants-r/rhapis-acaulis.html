Rhapis acaulis

Rhapis acaulis, commonly known as the slender bamboo palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright stems and feathery, compound leaves that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, forming a compact, bushy structure. Its foliage is often a deep green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are rarely seen.

Rhapis acaulis is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance of low light and moderate watering requirements.
