Rosa obtusifolia var. heterodonta

Rosa obtusifolia var. heterodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dog rose, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its fragrant flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in hedgerows and gardens.
