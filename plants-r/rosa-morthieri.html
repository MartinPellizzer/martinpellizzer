Rosa morthieri

Rosa morthieri, commonly known as the Morthier rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub-like plant with woody stems and thorny growth, typical of many rose species. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Rosa morthieri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
