Rumex × cordifolius

Rumex × cordifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved dock, is a hybrid plant in the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish flowers that develop into reddish-brown seeds. It is typically found in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in wetlands.

Rumex × cordifolius is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
