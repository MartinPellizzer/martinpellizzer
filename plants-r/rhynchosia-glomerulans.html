Rhynchosia glomerulans

Rhynchosia glomerulans, commonly known as the clustered cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into hairy, flat pods. The plant is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

Its botanical structure supports its role in both agricultural and ecological systems.
