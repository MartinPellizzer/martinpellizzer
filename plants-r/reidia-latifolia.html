Reidia latifolia

Reidia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf reidia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Reidia latifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest understories.
