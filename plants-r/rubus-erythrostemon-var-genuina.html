Rubus erythrostemon var. genuina

Rubus erythrostemon var. genuina, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, often with a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and riparian zones.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
