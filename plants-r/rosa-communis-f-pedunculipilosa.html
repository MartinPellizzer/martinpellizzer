Rosa communis f. pedunculipilosa

Rosa communis f. pedunculipilosa, commonly known as the clustered rose, is a botanical variant of the common rose.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It is typically found in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
