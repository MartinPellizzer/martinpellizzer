Ribes senile

Ribes senile, commonly known as the weeping currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its pendulous, drooping branches that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible red berries in the summer. Its foliage is typically dark green and becomes vibrant in the fall.

Ribes senile is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique form and seasonal interest.
