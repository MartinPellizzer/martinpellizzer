Ranunculus hypochromatinus

Ranunculus hypochromatinus, commonly known as the pale buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features compound leaves with three leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The pale buttercup is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions.
