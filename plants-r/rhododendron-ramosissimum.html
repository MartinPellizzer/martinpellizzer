Rhododendron ramosissimum

Rhododendron ramosissimum, commonly known as the dense-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron ramosissimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its beauty.
