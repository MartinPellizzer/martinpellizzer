Roegneria nakaii

Roegneria nakaii, commonly known as the Japanese wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are the reproductive structures of grasses. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Roegneria nakaii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
