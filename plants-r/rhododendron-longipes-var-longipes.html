Rhododendron longipes var. longipes

Rhododendron longipes var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from white to deep pink.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with the right growing conditions.
