Ruyschiana origanoides

Ruyschiana origanoides, commonly known as the Mexican oregano, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Ruyschiana origanoides is often cultivated for its fragrant foliage and ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warmer climates.
