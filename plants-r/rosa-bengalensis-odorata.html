Rosa bengalensis odorata

Rosa bengalensis odorata, commonly known as the Bengal rose or the Indian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces fragrant, single or double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in spring and summer. Its botanical characteristics include pinnate leaves and an erect growth habit, making it a distinctive ornamental plant.

The flowers are rich in essential oils, contributing to its aromatic properties and traditional uses in perfumery and medicine.
