Rubus × multilicius

Rubus × multilicius, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications.

Its ornamental value, combined with its edible fruits, makes it a popular choice for both gardens and small-scale cultivation.
