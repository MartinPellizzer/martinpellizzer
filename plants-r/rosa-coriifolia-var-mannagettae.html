Rosa coriifolia var. mannagettae

Rosa coriifolia var. mannagettae, commonly known as the Manchurian shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and adaptability to various soil types.
