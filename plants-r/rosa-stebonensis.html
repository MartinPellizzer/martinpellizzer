Rosa stebonensis

Rosa stebonensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of Ecuador and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa stebonensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
