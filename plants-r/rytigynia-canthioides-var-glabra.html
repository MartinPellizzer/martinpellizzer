Rytigynia canthioides var. glabra

Rytigynia canthioides var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved rytigynia, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are smooth and often elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, with yellow petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
