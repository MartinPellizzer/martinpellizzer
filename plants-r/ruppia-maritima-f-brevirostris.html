Ruppia maritima f. brevirostris

Ruppia maritima f. brevirostris, commonly known as the narrow-beaked sea rocket, is a rare variant of the common sea rocket.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline environments such as coastal wetlands and estuaries. The plant is characterized by its short, narrow leaves and small, yellowish flowers. It has a compact growth habit, often forming dense mats near the water's edge.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
