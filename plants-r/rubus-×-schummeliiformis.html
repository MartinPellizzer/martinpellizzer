Rubus × schummeliiformis

Rubus × schummeliiformis, commonly known as the Schummel's blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, known for its complex genetic makeup resulting from crosses between various Rubus species.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which typically develop into dark purple or black berries. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, where it thrives in moist, temperate climates. It has small, serrated leaves and produces white to pale yellow flowers during the spring.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in gardens and for local food production.
