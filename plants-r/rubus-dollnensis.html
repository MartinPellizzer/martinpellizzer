Rubus dollnensis

Rubus dollnensis, commonly known as the Dollnensian raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many shrubs with thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or semi-arid habitats, showcasing adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink, typical of many Rubus species.

The fruit, a red, hairy aggregate berry, is edible and often used in local cuisines.
