Rhododendron serpyllifolium f. albiflorum

Rhododendron serpyllifolium f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered serpyllifolium rhododendron, is a variant of the serpyllifolium rhododendron.

It is characterized by its white or pale pink flowers, which contrast with its dark green, leathery leaves. This plant is typically found in moist, acidic soils in mountainous regions. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is known for its compact growth habit.

The foliage is often dense and can form a low shrub, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.
