Rubus microtrichus

Rubus microtrichus, commonly known as the small-fruited blackberry, is a species of brambles native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
