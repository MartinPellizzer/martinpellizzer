Retama spachii

Retama spachii, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Retama spachii is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
