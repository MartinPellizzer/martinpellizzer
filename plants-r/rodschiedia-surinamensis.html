Rodschiedia surinamensis

Rodschiedia surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam rododendron, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Suriname and neighboring countries. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a reddish or purple underside. It produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom during the wet season.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
