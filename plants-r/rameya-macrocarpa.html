Rameya macrocarpa

Rameya macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant ramea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, red fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
