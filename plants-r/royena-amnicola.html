Royena amnicola

Royena amnicola, commonly known as the mountain balsam, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its flowering period occurs during the late spring to early summer, and it is often found in temperate climates.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies of alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
