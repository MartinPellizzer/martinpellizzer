Rubus polyacanthus var. lucidulus

Rubus polyacanthus var. lucidulus, commonly known as the glossy bramble, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, followed by red, juicy berries that are edible. Its leaves are often shiny and have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
