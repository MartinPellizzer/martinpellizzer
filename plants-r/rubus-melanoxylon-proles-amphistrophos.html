Rubus melanoxylon proles amphistrophos

Rubus melanoxylon proles amphistrophos, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, containing numerous tiny drupes. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils. Its botanical features include a woody stem with persistent buds and a complex inflorescence structure.

It is valued for its edible fruits and ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
