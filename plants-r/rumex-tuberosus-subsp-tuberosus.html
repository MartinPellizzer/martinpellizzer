Rumex tuberosus subsp. tuberosus

Rumex tuberosus subsp.

tuberosus, commonly known as narrowleaf sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright, hairy stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the veins.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its roots are tuberous and can be harvested for culinary or medicinal use.

It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
