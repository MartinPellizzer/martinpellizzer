Rivina lanceolata

Rivina lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf rivina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and bloom in the spring.

Rivina lanceolata is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
