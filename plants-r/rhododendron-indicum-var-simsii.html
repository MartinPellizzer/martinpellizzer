Rhododendron indicum var. simsii

Rhododendron indicum var. simsii, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas and southern China.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation in temperate regions.
