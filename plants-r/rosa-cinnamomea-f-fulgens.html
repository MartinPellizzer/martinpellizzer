Rosa cinnamomea f. fulgens

Rosa cinnamomea f. fulgens, commonly known as the golden rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are borne in clusters and have a rich, velvety texture, often with a subtle fragrance. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
