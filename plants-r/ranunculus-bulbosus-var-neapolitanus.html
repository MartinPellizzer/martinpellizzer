Ranunculus bulbosus var. neapolitanus

Ranunculus bulbosus var. neapolitanus, commonly known as the Neapolitan buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features bright yellow flowers with sharply divided petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a short, stout rhizome and produces small, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens.
