Rosa resinosoides

Rosa resinosoides, commonly known as the resinous rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance from its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique properties, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.
