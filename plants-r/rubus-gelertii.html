Rubus gelertii

Rubus gelertii, commonly known as the Welsh blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Wales.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Rubus gelertii is adapted to cool, moist climates and thrives in woodland and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
