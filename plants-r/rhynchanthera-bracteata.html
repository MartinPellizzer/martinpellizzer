Rhynchanthera bracteata

Rhynchanthera bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Rhynchanthera bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
