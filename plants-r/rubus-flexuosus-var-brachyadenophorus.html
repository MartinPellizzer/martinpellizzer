Rubus flexuosus var. brachyadenophorus

Rubus flexuosus var. brachyadenophorus, commonly known as the short-stalked raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to North America.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
