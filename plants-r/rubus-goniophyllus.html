Rubus goniophyllus

Rubus goniophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant produces small, oval leaves with a hairy texture and serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Rubus goniophyllus is often found in woodland edges, shrublands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
