Randia spinuligera

Randia spinuligera, commonly known as the spiny randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in hammocks and coastal areas.

Randia spinuligera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
