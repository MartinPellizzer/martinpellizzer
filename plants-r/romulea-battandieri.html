Romulea battandieri

Romulea battandieri, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a striking bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features bright yellow flowers that resemble cup-like structures, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded bulbs that store nutrients for the next growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
