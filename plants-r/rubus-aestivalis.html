Rubus aestivalis

Rubus aestivalis, commonly known as the summer bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen in late summer, attracting various wildlife species. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Rubus aestivalis plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity within its native habitats.
