Rubus obliquatus

Rubus obliquatus, commonly known as the bent-stem blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its arching, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
