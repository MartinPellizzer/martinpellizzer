Rosa pimpinellifolia var. macrophylla

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with large, ovate leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pink to white flowers in spring. Its foliage is evergreen, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardiness in coastal and rocky garden settings.
