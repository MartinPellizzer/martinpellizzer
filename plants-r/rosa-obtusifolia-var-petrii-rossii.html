Rosa obtusifolia var. petrii-rossii

Rosa obtusifolia var. petrii-rossii, commonly known as the Petrii-Rossii rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The leaves are compound, with oval-shaped leaflets that are covered in a fine layer of silvery hairs. This variety is well adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and unique appearance.
