Rubus serraticuspis

Rubus serraticuspis, commonly known as the serrated thorned blackberry.

It is a species of brambles native to parts of East Asia, particularly China. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark purple to black berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
