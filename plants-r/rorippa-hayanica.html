Rorippa hayanica

Rorippa hayanica, commonly known as the Japanese watercress, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. This aquatic or semi-aquatic species typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. Its small, white flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and is often used in traditional Asian cuisine for its peppery flavor.
