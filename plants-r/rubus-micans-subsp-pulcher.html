Rubus micans subsp. pulcher

Rubus micans subsp.

pulcher, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its finely hairy, silvery-gray leaves that give it its common name.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and persistent fruiting structures.
