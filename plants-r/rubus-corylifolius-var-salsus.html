Rubus corylifolius var. salsus

Rubus corylifolius var. salsus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to China.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, dark red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The leaves and berries contain bioactive compounds that have been studied for their medicinal properties.
