Rubia olivieri subsp. doniettii

Rubia olivieri subsp.

doniettii, commonly known as the olive borage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy.

The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
