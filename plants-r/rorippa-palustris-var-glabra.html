Rorippa palustris var. glabra

Rorippa palustris var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth water crowfoot, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to wetland environments.

It features small, floating or submerged leaves that are typically smooth and oval in shape. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, often in clusters at the water's surface. It thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

Its smooth leaves and delicate flowers make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in wetland ecosystems.
