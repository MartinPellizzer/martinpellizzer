Rosa sempervirens var. minor

Rosa sempervirens var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf evergreen rose, is a compact shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that remain evergreen throughout the year, providing year-round visual interest. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its compact size and hardy nature.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, ornamental plant.
