Rollinia glomerulifera

Rollinia glomerulifera, commonly known as the clustered fruit rollinia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and mango. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red fruits. The fruits are edible and often consumed raw or used in jams and preserves.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
