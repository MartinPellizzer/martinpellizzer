Rubus sepalanthus

Rubus sepalanthus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the plant is known for its hardy nature in cold climates.
