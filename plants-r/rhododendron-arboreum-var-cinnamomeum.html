Rhododendron arboreum var. cinnamomeum

Rhododendron arboreum var. cinnamomeum, commonly known as the Cinnamon Flame Rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant is characterized by its striking red to orange-red flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a reddish underside.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
