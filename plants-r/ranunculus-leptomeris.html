Ranunculus leptomeris

Ranunculus leptomeris, commonly known as the slenderleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of numerous petal-like sepals.

It is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.
