Rhamnus aphylla

Rhamnus aphylla, commonly known as the leafless buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, scale-like leaves that are often mistaken for the absence of foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by dark blue berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Rhamnus aphylla is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system that helps it survive in drought conditions.
