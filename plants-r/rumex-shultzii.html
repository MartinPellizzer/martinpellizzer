Rumex shultzii

Rumex shultzii, commonly known as Shultz's dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants like sorrel and buckwheat. The plant features upright stems with palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.

Shultz's dock is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.
