Rubus ammobius subsp. pseudoplicatus

Rubus ammobius subsp.

pseudoplicatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically narrow and serrated.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Its botanical features make it distinguishable from other Rubus species, particularly in its leaf morphology and fruit structure.
