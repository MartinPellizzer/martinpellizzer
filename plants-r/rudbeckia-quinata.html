Rudbeckia quinata

Rudbeckia quinata, commonly known as the yellow oxeye daisy, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of dark brown or black florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green in color. Rudbeckia quinata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
