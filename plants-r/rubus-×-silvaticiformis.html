Rubus × silvaticiformis

Rubus × silvaticiformis, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry or loganberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus fruticosus (bramble), resulting in a plant with robust growth and thorny stems. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically larger and sweeter than those of its parent species. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The botanical characteristics of Rubus × silvaticiformis make it a valuable species for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
