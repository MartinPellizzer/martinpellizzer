Rubus mucronifer microgene griseicalyx

Rubus mucronifer microgene griseicalyx, commonly known as the gray-leafed blackberry, is a species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to regions of East Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, which are typically dark purple to black, are edible and have a tart flavor.

The species is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional medicine.
