Rubus rivularis subsp. lamprophylloides

Rubus rivularis subsp.

lamprophylloides, commonly known as the glossy-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and has trifoliate leaves with a distinctive glossy, dark green appearance.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, dark red to black berry. This subspecies is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruit.

It is also an important plant in ecological studies due to its role in supporting local wildlife.
