Relbunium hypocarpium subsp. nitidum

Relbunium hypocarpium subsp.

nitidum, commonly known as the smooth-fruited bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bell-shaped, blue to purple in color, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is characterized by its smooth, glossy fruits and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
