Ranunculus lurorum

Ranunculus lurorum, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers with numerous stamens, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ranunculus lurorum is often found in disturbed soils and can spread rapidly through rhizomes.
