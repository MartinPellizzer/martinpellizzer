Rubus jamaicensis nudicaulis

Rubus jamaicensis nudicaulis, commonly known as the Jamaican raspberry or wild blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its erect or trailing stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry and blackberry species.
