Rosa rousseauiorum f. chrysocarpa

Rosa rousseauiorum f. chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit rose, is a unique hybrid cultivar of the Rosa rousseauiorum species.

It is characterized by its striking yellow fruits, which are a notable feature distinguishing it from other members of the species. The plant typically produces fragrant pink to red flowers, which bloom in early summer. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic and fruiting characteristics.
