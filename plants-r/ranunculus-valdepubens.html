Ranunculus valdepubens

Ranunculus valdepubens, commonly known as the pubescent buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow flowers and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features palmately divided leaves with soft, hairy surfaces, which give it its common name. Its flowers typically have five sepals and numerous stamens, with a central pistil that is often adorned with a hairy style.

Ranunculus valdepubens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
