Rhynchocarpa monophylla

Rhynchocarpa monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf fruit tree, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its singular, broad, waxy leaf that provides shade to the plant's compact, branching structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, red berries that are edible and contain a single seed. Its adaptations to drought include deep root systems and thick, water-storing leaves.

Rhynchocarpa monophylla is of interest to botanists due to its specialized morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
