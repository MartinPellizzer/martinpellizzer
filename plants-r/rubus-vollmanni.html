Rubus vollmanni

Rubus vollmanni, commonly known as the Vollmann's blackberry, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to western North America, particularly found in regions of California and Oregon. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant has thorny stems.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
