Rubus fruticosus subsp. carpinifolius

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

carpinifolius, commonly known as bramble rose or blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.

This species is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
