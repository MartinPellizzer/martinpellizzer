Rubus sanguineus var. alnifolius

Rubus sanguineus var. alnifolius, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the first-year stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous druplets, each containing a single seed. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated margins and a hairy texture.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible berries, which have a tart flavor and are used in culinary applications.
