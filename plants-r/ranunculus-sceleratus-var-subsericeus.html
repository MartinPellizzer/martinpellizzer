Ranunculus sceleratus var. subsericeus

Ranunculus sceleratus var. subsericeus, commonly known as the silky buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically surrounded by a ring of sepals that are softly hairy. The leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive, finely toothed margin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant has a short, stout rhizome and produces small, green, berry-like fruits that contain several seeds.
