Rhynchospora schraderiana

Rhynchospora schraderiana, commonly known as Schrader's spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of spikelets at the top of its stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often arranged in tight, cylindrical clusters.

Rhynchospora schraderiana plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and is of interest to botanists and ecologists.
