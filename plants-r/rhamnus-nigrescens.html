Rhamnus nigrescens

Rhamnus nigrescens, commonly known as blackthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and small, black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
