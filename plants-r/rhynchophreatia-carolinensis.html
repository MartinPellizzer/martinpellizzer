Rhynchophreatia carolinensis

Rhynchophreatia carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina marsh mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Rhynchophreatia carolinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
