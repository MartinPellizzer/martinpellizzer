Rubus × separabilis

Rubus × separabilis, commonly known as the blackberry or raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically red or black in color, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
