Racosperma incrassatum

Racosperma incrassatum, commonly known as the thick-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features dense, dark green foliage with oval-shaped leaves that are thick and leathery. It produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which attract various pollinators.

Racosperma incrassatum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.
