Ranunculus carpaticola

Ranunculus carpaticola, commonly known as the Carpathian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountain meadows. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It produces small, greenish-brown fruits that contain multiple seeds.

Ranunculus carpaticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
