Rhododendron pseudochrysanthum var. taitunense

Rhododendron pseudochrysanthum var. taitunense, commonly known as the Taitung rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to Taiwan.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
