Rhipsalis pachyptera f. rubra

Rhipsalis pachyptera f. rubra, commonly known as the red-veined mistletoe cactus, is a unique epiphytic plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in small, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom during the rainy season, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its foliage is often marked by distinct red veins, giving it its common name.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
