Rubia nephrophylla

Rubia nephrophylla, commonly known as the kidney-leaved madder, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its kidney-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white. Its roots contain a red dye, historically used in textile dyeing.

Rubia nephrophylla thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
