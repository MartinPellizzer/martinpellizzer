Rubus hebecaulis var. russulus

Rubus hebecaulis var. russulus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thin, spiny stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique morphological traits that set it apart from other blackberry species.
