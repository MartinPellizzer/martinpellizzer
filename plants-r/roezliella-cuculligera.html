Roezliella cuculligera

Roezliella cuculligera, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth where it relies on specific symbiotic relationships with fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually small and pale, with a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators such as bees and wasps.
