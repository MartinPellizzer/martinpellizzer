Rubus platyacanthus

Rubus platyacanthus, commonly known as the flat-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the edible fruit.

The plant thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.
