Rabdosia phulchokiensis

Rabdosia phulchokiensis, commonly known as the Nepal strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and produces small, tubular flowers that are often violet or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowering period occurs during the summer months.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
