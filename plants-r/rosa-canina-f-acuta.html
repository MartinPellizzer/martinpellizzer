Rosa canina f. acuta

Rosa canina f. acuta, commonly known as the sharp-fruited dog rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose characterized by its distinctive, pointed fruits.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia, typically growing in woodland edges and open habitats. The plant produces fragrant pink or white flowers in spring, followed by red, hairy fruits that persist into winter. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and the stems are armed with sharp prickles.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
