Rosa antiardala

Rosa antiardala, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant pink to white flowers in the spring, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge.

The plant is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
