Rubus vatavensis

Rubus vatavensis, commonly known as the Vatavensis bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, particularly in parts of Vietnam and Laos. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its fruits, which are aggregate berries, are typically red when mature and are edible.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
