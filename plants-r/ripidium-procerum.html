Ripidium procerum

Ripidium procerum, commonly known as the tall rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often green or reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. Ripidium procerum is adapted to moist soils and can thrive in areas with standing water.

Its structure and growth habit make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
