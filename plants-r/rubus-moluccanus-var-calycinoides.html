Rubus moluccanus var. calycinoides

Rubus moluccanus var. calycinoides, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a species of shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, with a tangy flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its stems are hairy and woody.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated for its edible fruits.
