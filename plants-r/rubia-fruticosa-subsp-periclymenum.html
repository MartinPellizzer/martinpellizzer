Rubia fruticosa subsp. periclymenum

Rubia fruticosa subsp.

periclymenum, commonly known as sweet woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that contain minute seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers have been historically used in herbal medicine and as a flavoring agent.
