Ruellia nemoralis

Ruellia nemoralis, commonly known as the woodland ruellia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of blue or violet tubular flowers. Ruellia nemoralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.

It is also known for its adaptability to a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinators.
