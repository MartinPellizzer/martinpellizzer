Rosa coriifolia var. elongata

Rosa coriifolia var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated bristly rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, elongated, bristly stipules that give it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with finely toothed leaflets, and it has a dense, bushy form.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its visual interest and fragrance.
