Ranunculus falcatus subsp. falcatus

Ranunculus falcatus subsp.

falcatus, commonly known as the sickleleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often appear sickle-shaped, giving it its common name.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically in early spring. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and marshy areas.

Due to its delicate appearance and habitat requirements, it is considered a relatively rare species in many regions.
