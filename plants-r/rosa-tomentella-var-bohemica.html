Rosa tomentella var. bohemica

Rosa tomentella var. bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian woolly rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white fuzzy foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique texture and seasonal color changes.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.
