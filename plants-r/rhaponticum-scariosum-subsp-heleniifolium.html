Rhaponticum scariosum subsp. heleniifolium

Rhaponticum scariosum subsp.

heleniifolium, commonly known as the Helen's leaf rhapontic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features a robust root system and produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has finely dissected, feather-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine and steppe regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native range.
