Rubus carpinifolius var. laxus

Rubus carpinifolius var. laxus, commonly known as the looseleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, though it is less commonly cultivated than other blackberry varieties.
