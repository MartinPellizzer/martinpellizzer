Renealmia racemosa

Renealmia racemosa, commonly known as the red swamp crake's grass, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its erect, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear pale yellow to greenish flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Renealmia racemosa is noted for its adaptability to flooded conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic ecosystems.
