Rosa campaniaca

Rosa campaniaca, commonly known as the Campanula rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, upright stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are often used in ornamental gardening for their beauty and fragrance.

Rosa campaniaca prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.
