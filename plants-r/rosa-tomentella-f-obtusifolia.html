Rosa tomentella f. obtusifolia

Rosa tomentella f. obtusifolia, commonly known as the woolly rose or shrub rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features dense, grayish-white pubescence covering its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The leaves are typically rounded and have a broad, ovate shape with a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals and a sweet, subtle fragrance.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in moist, woodland environments.
