Rosa carolina f. alba

Rosa carolina f. alba, commonly known as the white Carolina rose, is a fragrant, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It produces showy, white single flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in thorny buds. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in American horticulture.
