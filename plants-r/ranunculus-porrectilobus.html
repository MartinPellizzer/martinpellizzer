Ranunculus porrectilobus

Ranunculus porrectilobus, commonly known as the straight-lipped buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a distinct central yellow stamen. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough, hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
