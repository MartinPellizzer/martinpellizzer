Reseda phyteuma var. fragrans

Reseda phyteuma var. fragrans, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and stems, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Reseda phyteuma var. fragrans is often used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and healing properties.
