Rudbeckia densifolia

Rudbeckia densifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, densely packed leaves and bright yellow to orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its sturdy stems and showy flowers make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Rudbeckia densifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
