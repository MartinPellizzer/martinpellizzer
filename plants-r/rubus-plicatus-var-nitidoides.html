Rubus plicatus var. nitidoides

Rubus plicatus var. nitidoides, commonly known as the glossy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy berries that are typically harvested in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The berries are not only edible but also have a distinctive flavor and are used in culinary applications.
