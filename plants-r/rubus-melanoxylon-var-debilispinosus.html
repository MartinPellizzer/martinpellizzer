Rubus melanoxylon var. debilispinosus

Rubus melanoxylon var. debilispinosus, commonly known as the slender-spined blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential in ecological restoration projects.
