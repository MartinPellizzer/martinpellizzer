Ryticaryum rotundatum

Ryticaryum rotundatum, commonly known as the round-leafed ryticaryum, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, thick leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its humid, tropical environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are known to have medicinal properties, traditionally used in local herbal remedies.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is now considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
