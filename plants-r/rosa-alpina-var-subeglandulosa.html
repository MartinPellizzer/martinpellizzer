Rosa alpina var. subeglandulosa

Rosa alpina var. subeglandulosa, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a variant of the alpine rose species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is often covered in fine, glandular hairs. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
