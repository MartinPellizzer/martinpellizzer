Robinia neomexicana var. neomexicana

Robinia neomexicana var. neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico false acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in spring. The plant has a deep root system and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy, drought-tolerant species well-suited to xeriscaping.
