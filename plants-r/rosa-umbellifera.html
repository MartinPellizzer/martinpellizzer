Rosa umbellifera

Rosa umbellifera, commonly known as the umbrella rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbellate inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland environments and is native to parts of Asia and Europe. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Rosa umbellifera is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
