Ranunculus muricatus var. creticus

Ranunculus muricatus var. creticus, commonly known as the Cretan buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and limestone habitats. It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, greenish sepals beneath the petals.

This variety is closely related to other ranunculus species but is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits.
