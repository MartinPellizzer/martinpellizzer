Rosa lacteola

Rosa lacteola, commonly known as the milky rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white petals and is native to parts of Asia, including China and India. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and has a climbing habit, often using its tendrils to support itself. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's name refers to the milky sap that exudes from its stems when damaged.
