Ruellia prismatica

Ruellia prismatica, commonly known as the purple ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, providing a textured appearance. Ruellia prismatica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
