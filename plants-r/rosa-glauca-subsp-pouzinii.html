Rosa glauca subsp. pouzinii

Rosa glauca subsp.

pouzinii, commonly known as the French rose or white shrub rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its upright, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant is native to southern Europe and western Asia, particularly thriving in rocky and limestone soils. Its leaves are typically serrated and have a grayish-green hue, while its stems are often covered in thorns.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual beauty.
