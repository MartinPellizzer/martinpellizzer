Racosperma simulans

Racosperma simulans, commonly known as the mimosa tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Racosperma simulans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
