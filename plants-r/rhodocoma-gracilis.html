Rhodocoma gracilis

Rhodocoma gracilis, commonly known as the slender milkbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. Rhodocoma gracilis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
