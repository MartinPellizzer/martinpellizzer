Ranunculus rufus

Ranunculus rufus, commonly known as the red avens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features bright red to orange flowers with five to eight petals, often surrounded by a ring of yellow sepals. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Ranunculus rufus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
