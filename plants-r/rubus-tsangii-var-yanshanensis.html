Rubus tsangii var. yanshanensis

Rubus tsangii var. yanshanensis, commonly known as the Yanshan blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus tsangii species native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
