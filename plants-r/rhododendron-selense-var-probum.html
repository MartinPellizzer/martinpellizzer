Rhododendron selense var. probum

Rhododendron selense var. probum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It features broad, dark green leaves that are leathery in texture and have a glossy appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments with acidic soil and partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
