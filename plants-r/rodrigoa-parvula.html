Rodrigoa parvula

Rodrigoa parvula, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a red throat, blooming in spring. The plant has smooth, glossy leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Rodrigoa parvula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
