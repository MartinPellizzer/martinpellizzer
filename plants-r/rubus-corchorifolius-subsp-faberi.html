Rubus corchorifolius subsp. faberi

Rubus corchorifolius subsp.

faberi, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that are dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
