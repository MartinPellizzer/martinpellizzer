Robinia neomexicana var. luxurians

Robinia neomexicana var. luxurians, commonly known as the Mexican honeylocust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It features compound leaves that are typically 10–20 cm long, composed of 12–24 oval, serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in racemes during the spring, followed by elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to thrive in poor soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
