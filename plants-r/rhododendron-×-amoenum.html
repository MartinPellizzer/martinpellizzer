Rhododendron × amoenum

Rhododendron × amoenum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a hybrid shrub that originated from crosses between Rhododendron indicum and Rhododendron obtusum.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The flowers are typically produced in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the environment.
