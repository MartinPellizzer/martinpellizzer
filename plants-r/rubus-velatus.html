Rubus velatus

Rubus velatus, commonly known as the velvety blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its hairy, thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are white and borne in clusters, and it thrives in moist, wooded areas.

Rubus velatus is often found in the understory of forests and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
