Rhododendron rex subsp. fictolacteum

Rhododendron rex subsp.

fictolacteum, commonly known as the pink-flowered rhododendron, is a unique subspecies characterized by its striking, pale pink to white flowers. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, featuring dense, leathery foliage that provides a lush backdrop to its showy blooms. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are known for their delicate, bell-shaped appearance.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
