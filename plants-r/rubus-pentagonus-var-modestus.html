Rubus pentagonus var. modestus

Rubus pentagonus var. modestus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. The plant features small, five-lobed leaves and produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and for its contribution to local ecosystems.
