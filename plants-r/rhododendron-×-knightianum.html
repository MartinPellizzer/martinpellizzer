Rhododendron × knightianum

Rhododendron × knightianum, commonly known as the Knight's rhododendron, is a hybrid evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, clustered flowers.

It originates from the cross between Rhododendron ferrugineum and Rhododendron caucasicum, resulting in a plant with distinctive botanical features. The leaves are typically dark green and oval-shaped, while the flowers are pink to reddish in color, often with a striking contrast between the petals and the calyx. This species is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
