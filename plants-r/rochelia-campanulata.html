Rochelia campanulata

Rochelia campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that typically range in color from blue to purple. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, oval berries that contain the seeds.

Rochelia campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
