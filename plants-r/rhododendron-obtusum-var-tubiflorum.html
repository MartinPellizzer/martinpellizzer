Rhododendron obtusum var. tubiflorum

Rhododendron obtusum var. tubiflorum, commonly known as the woolly alpine rhododendron, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil and partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
