Rinzia longifolia

Rinzia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved rinzia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers, which bloom in spring. It typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, often in sunny, well-drained environments.

Rinzia longifolia is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in coastal dunes and cliffs.
