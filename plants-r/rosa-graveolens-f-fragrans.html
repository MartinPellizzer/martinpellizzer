Rosa graveolens f. fragrans

Rosa graveolens f. fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant rose or the sweet rose, is a fragrant, climbing shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which emit a strong, sweet fragrance, especially in the evening. The plant has thin, thorny stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and are prized for their ornamental value and aromatic properties.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and scent.
