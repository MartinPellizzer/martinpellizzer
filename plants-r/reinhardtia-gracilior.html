Reinhardtia gracilior

Reinhardtia gracilior, commonly known as the slender pineapple plant, is a tropical herbaceous plant belonging to the Bromeliaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup, which can hold water. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators. It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in warm climates for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
