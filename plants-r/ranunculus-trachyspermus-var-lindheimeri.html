Ranunculus trachyspermus var. lindheimeri

Ranunculus trachyspermus var. lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its botanical characteristics include a short, hairy stem and a basal rosette of leaves.

It is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
