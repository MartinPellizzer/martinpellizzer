Rosa sinkowiensis

Rosa sinkowiensis, commonly known as the Sinkowicz rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in fine hairs, while its fruits are aggregate fruits called rosehips.

Rosa sinkowiensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
