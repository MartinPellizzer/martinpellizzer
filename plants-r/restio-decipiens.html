Restio decipiens

Restio decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive restio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters at the tips of its stems. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland fynbos vegetation.

Due to its delicate structure and unique floral morphology, it is a notable species within its ecological community.
