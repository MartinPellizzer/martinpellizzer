Rubus pullus

Rubus pullus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Rubus pullus plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
