Rubus humifusus proles flaccidifolius

Rubus humifusus proles flaccidifolius, commonly known as the flaccidifolius roseberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the temperate regions of North America.

It features trifoliate leaves that are typically pale green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
