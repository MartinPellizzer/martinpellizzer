Rinorea aucuparia var. platyphylla

Rinorea aucuparia var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved rinorea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically broad and glossy. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
