Rottboellia cylindrica

Rottboellia cylindrica, commonly known as the spear grass or cylinder grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, jointed stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Rottboellia cylindrica is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
