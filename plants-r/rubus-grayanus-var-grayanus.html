Rubus grayanus var. grayanus

Rubus grayanus var. grayanus, commonly known as the Gray's raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, often used in local foraging practices. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
