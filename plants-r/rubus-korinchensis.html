Rubus korinchensis

Rubus korinchensis, commonly known as the Korinch's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Central Asia and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shrublands and forest edges.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
