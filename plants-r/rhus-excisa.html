Rhus excisa

Rhus excisa, commonly known as the staghorn sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are bright green in summer and turn vibrant red in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy fruit clusters that persist into winter. Rhus excisa thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in disturbed sites.
