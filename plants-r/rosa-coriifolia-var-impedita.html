Rosa coriifolia var. impedita

Rosa coriifolia var. impedita, commonly known as the Chinese shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are compound, with ovate leaflets and a serrated margin, giving it a distinctive appearance. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
