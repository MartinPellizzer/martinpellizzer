Rollinia ferruginea

Rollinia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty rollinia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like mango and cashew. The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits.

The fruit is edible, though its taste is often described as acidic and slightly bitter.
