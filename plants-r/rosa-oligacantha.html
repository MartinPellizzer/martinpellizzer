Rosa oligacantha

Rosa oligacantha, commonly known as the rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa oligacantha is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
