Ranunculus trilobus var. subpilosus

Ranunculus trilobus var. subpilosus, commonly known as the silky buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves that are softly hairy, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
