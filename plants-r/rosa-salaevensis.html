Rosa salaevensis

Rosa salaevensis, commonly known as the Central Asian rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces rose hips that are typically orange or red in color. Rosa salaevensis is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
