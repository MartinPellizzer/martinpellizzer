Romanzoffia californica

Romanzoffia californica, commonly known as the California marshplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its root system is adapted for waterlogged soils, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Romanzoffia californica is also valued for its ability to filter water and support aquatic life.
