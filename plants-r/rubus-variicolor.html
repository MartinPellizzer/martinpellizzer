Rubus variicolor

Rubus variicolor, commonly known as the variable blackberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on maturity. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they bloom in the spring.
