Rosa mirogojana

Rosa mirogojana, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

The flowers emit a sweet scent, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
