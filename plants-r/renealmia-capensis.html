Renealmia capensis

Renealmia capensis, commonly known as the Cape reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

Renealmia capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ability to thrive in humid conditions.
