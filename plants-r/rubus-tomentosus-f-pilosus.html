Rubus tomentosus f. pilosus

Rubus tomentosus f. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy bramble, is a variant of the common blackberry plant.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly hairs covering its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in cymes, followed by dark purple to black berries.

This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
