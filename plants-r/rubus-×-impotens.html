Rubus × impotens

Rubus × impotens, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically eaten fresh or used in jams and wines. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
