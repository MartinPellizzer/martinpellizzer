Rubus euphlebophyllus

Rubus euphlebophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically broad and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
