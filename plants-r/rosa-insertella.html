Rosa insertella

Rosa insertella, commonly known as the Sierra Valley rose, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions with well-drained soils. The plant features slender, arching stems covered in small, reddish-brown prickles. Its flowers are pale pink to white, with five petals and a fragrant scent.

Rosa insertella is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in challenging alpine environments.
