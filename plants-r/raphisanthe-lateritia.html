Raphisanthe lateritia

Raphisanthe lateritia, commonly known as the rock fig, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories.
