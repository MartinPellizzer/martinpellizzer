Rubus rusticanus f. microphyllus

Rubus rusticanus f. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its smaller, more densely packed leaves compared to the typical Rubus rusticanus. It produces dark purple to black berries that are typically smaller than those of the standard species. The plant is native to certain regions of North America and thrives in woodland or shaded environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
