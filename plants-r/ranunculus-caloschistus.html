Ranunculus caloschistus

Ranunculus caloschistus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. It has a short growing season and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
