Racosperma sedifolium subsp. pulvinatum

Racosperma sedifolium subsp.

pulvinatum, commonly known as the woolly acacia, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the legume family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Its subspecies status highlights its unique adaptations to its specific environmental conditions.
