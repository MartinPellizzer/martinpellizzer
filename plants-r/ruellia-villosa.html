Ruellia villosa

Ruellia villosa, commonly known as hairy ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its common name, and they are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

Ruellia villosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
