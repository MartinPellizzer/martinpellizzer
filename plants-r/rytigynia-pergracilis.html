Rytigynia pergracilis

Rytigynia pergracilis, commonly known as the slender rytigynia, is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Rytigyniaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often accompanied by a distinctive aromatic scent. Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.

However, it is relatively understudied and not widely cultivated outside its natural range.
