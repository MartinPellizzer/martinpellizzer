Rhododendron poukhanense var. yodogawa

Rhododendron poukhanense var. yodogawa, commonly known as the Yodogawa rhododendron, is a variant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodland areas, preferring acidic soil conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic contribution.
