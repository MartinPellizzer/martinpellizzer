Ranunculus incomparabilis

Ranunculus incomparabilis, commonly known as the incomparable buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
