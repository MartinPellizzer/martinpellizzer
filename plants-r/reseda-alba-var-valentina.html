Reseda alba var. valentina

Reseda alba var. valentina, commonly known as white reseda, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces fluffy, seed-filled capsules that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its historical use in dyeing textiles and its ornamental value contribute to its significance in horticulture.
