Ranunculus glacialis var. holosericeus

Ranunculus glacialis var. holosericeus, commonly known as the glacier buttercup, is a rare and delicate alpine plant characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers with 5 to 13 petals.

It thrives in high-altitude environments, such as mountain meadows and rocky slopes, where it tolerates cold temperatures and poor soil conditions. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silky, white hairs, providing insulation against harsh weather. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a splash of color to its arctic and alpine habitats.

This variant is notable for its unique floral structure and its adaptation to extreme environmental conditions.
