Rubus candicans var. typicus

Rubus candicans var. typicus, commonly known as the showy blackberry.

It is a perennial shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible blackberries in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated margin and a hairy texture.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
