Rupicapnos anomala

Rupicapnos anomala, commonly known as the flame azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Rupicapnos anomala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant seasonal display.
