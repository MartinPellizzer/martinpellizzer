Rhododendron glischroides

Rhododendron glischroides, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in Nepal, India, and Bhutan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in humid, cool climates. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils.

Rhododendron glischroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
