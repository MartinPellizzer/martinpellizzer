Rubus thyrsanthus var. mullerianus

Rubus thyrsanthus var. mullerianus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its flowers are typically white or pink and are pollinated by insects.
