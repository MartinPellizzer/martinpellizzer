Rosa canina f. mucronulata

Rosa canina f. mucronulata, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant produces red-orange hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Its morphology reflects adaptations to its natural habitat, making it a resilient and hardy species.
