Rosa indica creunta

Rosa indica creunta, commonly known as the Indian rose or the Damask rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western Himalayas and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers are often used in the extraction of essential oils and in the production of rose water.

Rosa indica creunta is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its aromatic properties.
