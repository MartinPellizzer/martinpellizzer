Rosa californica f. plena

Rosa californica f. plena, commonly known as the full-blown California wild rose, is a hybrid form of the California rose.

It is characterized by its double flowers, which are larger and more showy than those of the typical species. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. It has arching stems with thorny stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
