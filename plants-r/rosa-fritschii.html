Rosa fritschii

Rosa fritschii, commonly known as the Fritsch's rose, is a species of shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are ornamental and sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Rosa fritschii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
