Rubus tereticaulis var. glaucescens

Rubus tereticaulis var. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with a bluish-green underside. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically consumed by wildlife. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
