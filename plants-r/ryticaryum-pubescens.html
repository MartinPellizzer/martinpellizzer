Ryticaryum pubescens

Ryticaryum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy ryticaryum, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts.
