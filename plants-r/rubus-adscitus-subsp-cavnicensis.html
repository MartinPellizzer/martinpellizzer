Rubus adscitus subsp. cavnicensis

Rubus adscitus subsp.

cavnicensis, commonly known as the Cavanese blackberry, is a shrub native to the island of Sicily. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
