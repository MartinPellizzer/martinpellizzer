Rubus boottianus

Rubus boottianus, commonly known as the Boott's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
