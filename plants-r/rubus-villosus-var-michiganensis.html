Rubus villosus var. michiganensis

Rubus villosus var. michiganensis, commonly known as the Michigan bramble, is a shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of mixed hardwood forests.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family and is notable for its hirsute (hairy) appearance.
