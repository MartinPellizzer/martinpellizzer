Rubus caesius f. echinatus

Rubus caesius f. echinatus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a cultivated form of the blackberry plant characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to Europe, particularly the British Isles. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with alternate, pinnately compound leaves. Its fruit, which ripens in late summer, is prized for its tart flavor and is often used in jams and desserts.

The bristly blackberry is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
