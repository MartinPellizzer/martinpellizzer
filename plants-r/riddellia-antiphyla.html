Riddellia antiphyla

Riddellia antiphyla, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.
