Rubus tereticaulis subsp. derasus

Rubus tereticaulis subsp.

derasus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
