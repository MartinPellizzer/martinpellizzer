Rivina americana

Rivina americana, commonly known as scarlet rose of Jericho, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that produces showy, tubular red or orange flowers. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, demonstrating its adaptability to various environments.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
