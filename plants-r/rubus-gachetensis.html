Rubus gachetensis

Rubus gachetensis, commonly known as Gachet's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, specifically found in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that resemble blackberries. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
