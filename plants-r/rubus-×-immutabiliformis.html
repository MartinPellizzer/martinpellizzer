Rubus × immutabiliformis

Rubus × immutabiliformis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible, though they are often less flavorful compared to other blackberry species. It is native to coastal regions of southern California and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
