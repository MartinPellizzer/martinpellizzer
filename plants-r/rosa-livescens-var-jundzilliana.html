Rosa livescens var. jundzilliana

Rosa livescens var. jundzilliana, commonly known as the Jundzilliana rose, is a compact, shrub-like species native to the Canary Islands.

It features dark green, serrated leaves and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers with a white center. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

This variety is relatively disease-resistant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
