Ranunculus californicus var. typicus

Ranunculus californicus var. typicus, commonly known as the California buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive central stigma, typically blooming in spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves that are palmately arranged, with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
