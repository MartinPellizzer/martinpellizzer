Rubus × rutilus

Rubus × rutilus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep red or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations.
