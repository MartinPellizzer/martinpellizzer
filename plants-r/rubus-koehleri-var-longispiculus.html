Rubus koehleri var. longispiculus

Rubus koehleri var. longispiculus, commonly known as the long-spined blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences, adding to its ornamental value.

This species is valued both for its ecological role and its potential in horticulture.
