Rubus peltinervius

Rubus peltinervius, commonly known as the broad-veined blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with broad, prominent veins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
