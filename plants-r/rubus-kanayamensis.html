Rubus kanayamensis

Rubus kanayamensis, commonly known as the Kanayama blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit, which is a dark purple to black berry, is edible and has a tart flavor.
