Racosperma leptoneurum

Racosperma leptoneurum, commonly known as the slender-veined acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with slender, often hairy leaflets, and it produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in cylindrical clusters. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in woodland and heathland environments.

Racosperma leptoneurum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
