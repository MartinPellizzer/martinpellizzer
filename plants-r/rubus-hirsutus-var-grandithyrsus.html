Rubus hirsutus var. grandithyrsus

Rubus hirsutus var. grandithyrsus, commonly known as the large-thyrsus raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as raspberries, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
