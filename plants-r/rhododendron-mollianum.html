Rhododendron mollianum

Rhododendron mollianum, commonly known as the Molliana rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in cool, moist mountainous environments. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many ornamental and ecologically important plants.
