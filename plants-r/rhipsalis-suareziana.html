Rhipsalis suareziana

Rhipsalis suareziana, commonly known as the Suarez's mistletoe cactus, is a unique epiphytic plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that often hang from trees, giving it a vine-like appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, rounded structures, and it lacks the typical spines found in many cacti.

Rhipsalis suareziana is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
