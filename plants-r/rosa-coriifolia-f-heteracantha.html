Rosa coriifolia f. heteracantha

Rosa coriifolia f. heteracantha, commonly known as the hairy rose or the Chinese rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa coriifolia species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, glandular leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a unique texture. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically in late spring to early summer. It is a compact shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden borders or containers.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and hardy nature, adapting well to various climates.
