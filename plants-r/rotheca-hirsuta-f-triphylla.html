Rotheca hirsuta f. triphylla

Rotheca hirsuta f. triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaf false nettle, is a variant of the hairy rotheca native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are opposite and have a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing properties.

The species is adapted to moist, shaded environments and thrives in woodland habitats.
