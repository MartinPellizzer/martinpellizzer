Rubus subhystrix

Rubus subhystrix, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other members of the Rubus genus.
