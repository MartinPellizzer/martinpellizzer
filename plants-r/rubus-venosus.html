Rubus venosus

Rubus venosus, commonly known as the veiny blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

Rubus venosus is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and along stream banks.
