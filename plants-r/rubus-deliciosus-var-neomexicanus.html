Rubus deliciosus var. neomexicanus

Rubus deliciosus var. neomexicanus, commonly known as the New Mexican blackberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
