Ruta frutescens

Ruta frutescens, commonly known as common rue, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant contains compounds such as rutin and volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its historical use in herbal medicine dates back to ancient times.
