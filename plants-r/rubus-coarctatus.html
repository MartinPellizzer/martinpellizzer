Rubus coarctatus

Rubus coarctatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive narrow shape with serrated edges.

The plant is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many other species of raspberries and blackberries.
