Rapunculus pinnatus

Rapunculus pinnatus, commonly known as the featherleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It produces small, edible roots that have been used in traditional herbal remedies.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its elegant appearance.
