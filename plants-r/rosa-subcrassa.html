Rosa subcrassa

Rosa subcrassa, commonly known as the broadleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The plant produces oval, red berries that contain seeds, contributing to its propagation in suitable environments.
