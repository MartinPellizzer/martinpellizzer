Rosa pseudocorymbifera

Rosa pseudocorymbifera, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, while its stems are often armed with thorns.

Rosa pseudocorymbifera is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
