Rubus melanoxylon var. obscuratus

Rubus melanoxylon var. obscuratus, commonly known as the dark-stemmed blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and foliage, which distinguish it from other Rubus species. It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant is native to regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental species within the Rubus genus.
