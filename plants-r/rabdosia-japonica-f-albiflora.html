Rabdosia japonica f. albiflora

Rabdosia japonica f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese rabdosia, is a variant of the species native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage has a strong, aromatic scent, typical of the Lamiaceae family.

This form is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
