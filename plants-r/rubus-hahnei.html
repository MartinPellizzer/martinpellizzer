Rubus hahnei

Rubus hahnei, commonly known as the Hahne's blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and blackberries. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.
