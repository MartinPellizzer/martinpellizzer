Rhododendron benthamianum

Rhododendron benthamianum, commonly known as the Bentham's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and is adapted to cool, moist climates. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Rhododendron benthamianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
