Racosperma Wanyu

Wanyu acacia, scientifically known as Racosperma wanyu, is a rare and resilient shrub native to the arid regions of northern Australia. This plant is part of the legume family and is well adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its unique features include small, feathery leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The Wanyu acacia plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for local wildlife. Due to habitat loss, it is now considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
