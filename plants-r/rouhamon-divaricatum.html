Rouhamon divaricatum

Rouhamon divaricatum, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

Rouhamon divaricatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
