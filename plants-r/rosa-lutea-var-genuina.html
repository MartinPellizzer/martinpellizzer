Rosa lutea var. genuina

Rosa lutea var. genuina, commonly known as the yellow rose of Sharon, is a cultivar of the Rosa lutea species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows as a shrub with arching, thorned stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. Its botanical features include fragrant, five-petaled flowers and a woody structure that supports its perennial growth habit.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
