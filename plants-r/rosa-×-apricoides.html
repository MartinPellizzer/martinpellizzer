Rosa × apricoides

Rosa × apricoides, commonly known as the apricot rose, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits characteristics of both roses and apricots.

It is a deciduous plant with thorny stems and compound leaves that are oval to lance-shaped. The flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and a prominent central pistil. It produces small, orange-red fruits that resemble apricots, which are edible but not as flavorful as true apricots.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
