Rojasia gracilis

Rojasia gracilis, commonly known as the slender rojasia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it is often found in moist, well-drained habitats.

Rojasia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
