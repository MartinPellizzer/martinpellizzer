Romulea hirsuta

Romulea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, grass-like foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are adapted for storing water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Romulea hirsuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
