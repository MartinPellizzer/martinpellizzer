Ranunculus andersonii var. juniperinus

Ranunculus andersonii var. juniperinus, commonly known as the juniperleaf buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, white down. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
