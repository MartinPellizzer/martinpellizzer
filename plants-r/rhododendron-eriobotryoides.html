Rhododendron eriobotryoides

Rhododendron eriobotryoides, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in forested areas with acidic soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green and glossy. It produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine.
