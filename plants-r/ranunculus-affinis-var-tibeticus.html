Ranunculus affinis var. tibeticus

Ranunculus affinis var. tibeticus, commonly known as the Tibetan buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and vibrant yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat. It has a short growing season and relies on the cool, moist climate of its native region.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
