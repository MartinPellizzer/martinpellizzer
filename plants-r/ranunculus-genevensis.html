Ranunculus genevensis

Ranunculus genevensis, commonly known as the Geneva water crowfoot, is a species of aquatic plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that float on the water surface, while its roots are anchored in the substrate. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with five to seven petals and numerous stamens.

Ranunculus genevensis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small fish.
