Rosa mollis f. brunneata

Rosa mollis f. brunneata, commonly known as the brown-leaved shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are densely covered with soft, brownish-gray hairs, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

This variety is particularly valued for its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
