Ranunculus tenuicaulis

Ranunculus tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slenderleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive, slender appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five sepals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
