Ranunculus brunnescentoides

Ranunculus brunnescentoides, commonly known as the brownish buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its distinctive, often hairy stems.

Ranunculus brunnescentoides is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy, often bright-colored blooms.
