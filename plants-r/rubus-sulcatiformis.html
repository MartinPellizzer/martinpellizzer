Rubus sulcatiformis

Rubus sulcatiformis, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
