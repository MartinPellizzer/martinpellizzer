Rubus vestitus f. parvifolius

Rubus vestitus f. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leafed raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its smaller, more delicate foliage.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and attractive, finely textured leaves.
