Rhododendron facium

Rhododendron facium, commonly known as the Sierra Madre rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that range in color from pink to red.

Rhododendron facium is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
