Raphia gaertneri

Raphia gaertneri, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a large, fan-shaped palm native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in hanging clusters, followed by edible fruits that are consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or riverine areas.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
