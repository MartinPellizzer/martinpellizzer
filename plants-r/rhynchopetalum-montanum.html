Rhynchopetalum montanum

Rhynchopetalum montanum, commonly known as the mountain rhynchopetalum, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually small and clustered.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
