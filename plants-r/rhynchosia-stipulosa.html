Rhynchosia stipulosa

Rhynchosia stipulosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with small stipules, and its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Rhynchosia stipulosa is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
