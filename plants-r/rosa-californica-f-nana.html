Rosa californica f. nana

Rosa californica f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf California rose, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to coastal regions of California and Oregon.

It features small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy foliage that remains attractive throughout the growing season. It is well adapted to coastal climates and is drought-tolerant once established.

This cultivar is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
