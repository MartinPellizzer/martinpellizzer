Ridsdalea pulcherrima

Ridsdalea pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame violet, is a striking flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange and yellow, often with a contrasting dark center. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ridsdalea pulcherrima is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
