Rosa squarrosa var. pseudoscabrata

Rosa squarrosa var. pseudoscabrata, commonly known as the rough-stemmed rose, is a shrub-like species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically alternate and has a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

Rosa squarrosa var. pseudoscabrata is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy plant for xeriscaping.
