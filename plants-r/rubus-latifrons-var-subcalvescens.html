Rubus latifrons var. subcalvescens

Rubus latifrons var. subcalvescens, commonly known as the broad-fruited blackberry variant, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. Its flowers are white and borne in clusters, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variant is often found in woodland areas and along streambanks.
