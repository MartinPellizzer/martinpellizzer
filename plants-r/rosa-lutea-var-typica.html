Rosa lutea var. typica

Rosa lutea var. typica, commonly known as the yellow rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and compact growth habit.
