Rubus plicatus var. oblongifolius

Rubus plicatus var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the oblongleaf blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with elongated, ovate leaflets. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
