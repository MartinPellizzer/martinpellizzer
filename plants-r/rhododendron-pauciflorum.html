Rhododendron pauciflorum

Rhododendron pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique beauty.
