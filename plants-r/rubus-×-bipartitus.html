Rubus × bipartitus

Rubus × bipartitus, commonly known as the double-flowered raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its unique floral structure that features two distinct layers of petals. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces edible red berries similar to those of other raspberries.

This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
