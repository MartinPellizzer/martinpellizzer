Rhodiola rosea subsp. polygama

Rhodiola rosea subsp.

polygama, commonly known as the Himalayan rhodiola, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green to reddish in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It is well adapted to cold, arid environments and can thrive in rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its adaptogenic effects.
