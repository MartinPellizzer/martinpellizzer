Rollandia calycina

Rollandia calycina, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers that open in the morning and close by late afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces spiral-shaped seed pods that contain small, hard seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Rollandia calycina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Convolvulaceae family.
