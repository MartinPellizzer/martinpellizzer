Rubus omalus var. flexilis

Rubus omalus var. flexilis, commonly known as the flexible blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry species native to certain regions of North America.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically serrated. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries and ornamental value.
