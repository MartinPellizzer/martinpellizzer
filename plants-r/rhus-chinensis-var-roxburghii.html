Rhus chinensis var. roxburghii

Rhus chinensis var. roxburghii, commonly known as Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red, clustered fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
