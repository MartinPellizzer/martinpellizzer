Rubus montivivus

Rubus montivivus, commonly known as the Andean bramble, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Rubus montivivus plays an important role in the ecosystems of the Andes, providing food for wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.
