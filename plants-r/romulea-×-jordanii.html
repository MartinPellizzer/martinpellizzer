Romulea × jordanii

Romulea × jordanii, commonly known as the Jordan's glory of the snow, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking, nodding flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces showy, violet-blue petals with a yellow center, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Romulea × jordanii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
