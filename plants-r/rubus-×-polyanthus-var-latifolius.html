Rubus × polyanthus var. latifolius

Rubus × polyanthus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its broad, serrated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible berries.

This variety is notable for its vigor and adaptability to various soil types.
