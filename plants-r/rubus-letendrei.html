Rubus letendrei

Rubus letendrei, commonly known as the Letendre's blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or semi-arid environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, while edible, is often used in local traditional cuisines and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
