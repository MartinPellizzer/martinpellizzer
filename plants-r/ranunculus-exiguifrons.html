Ranunculus exiguifrons

Ranunculus exiguifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, alpine environments and prefers well-drained soils. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
