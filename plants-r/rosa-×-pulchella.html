Rosa × pulchella

Rosa × pulchella, commonly known as the American beauty rose, is a hybrid species native to North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers have a strong, sweet fragrance and are often used in ornamental gardening.

Rosa × pulchella is relatively disease-resistant and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun.
