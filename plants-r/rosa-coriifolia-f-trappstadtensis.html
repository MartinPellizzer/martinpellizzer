Rosa coriifolia f. trappstadtensis

Rosa coriifolia f. trappstadtensis, commonly known as the Trappstadt rose, is a unique horticultural form of the shrub rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early summer. It is a compact, deciduous shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its delicate appearance and seasonal interest.
