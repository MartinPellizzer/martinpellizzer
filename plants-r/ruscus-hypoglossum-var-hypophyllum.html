Ruscus hypoglossum var. hypophyllum

Ruscus hypoglossum var. hypophyllum, commonly known as the common euonym.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub characterized by its fleshy, scale-like leaves and clusters of bright red berries. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects.

The berries are toxic to humans but are a food source for certain birds.
