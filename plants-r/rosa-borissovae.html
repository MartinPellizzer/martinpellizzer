Rosa borissovae

Rosa borissovae, commonly known as the Borissov's rose, is a species native to the Caucasus region.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many rose species. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. Its botanical characteristics include small, oval-shaped leaves and a woody structure.

Rosa borissovae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
