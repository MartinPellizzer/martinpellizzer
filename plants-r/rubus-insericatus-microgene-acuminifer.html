Rubus insericatus microgene acuminifer

Rubus insericatus microgene acuminifer, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
