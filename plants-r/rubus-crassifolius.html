Rubus crassifolius

Rubus crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized in various regions.
