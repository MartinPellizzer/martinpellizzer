Rosa montana f. latibractea

Rosa montana f. latibractea, commonly known as the broad-bract rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa montana species, characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral features.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a strong, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the blossoms.

This variety is typically grown in gardens for its visual appeal and hardy nature.
