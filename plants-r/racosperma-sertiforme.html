Racosperma sertiforme

Racosperma sertiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes, typical of the genus Racosperma. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several narrow, elongated leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats across southeastern Australia.
