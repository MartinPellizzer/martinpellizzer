Rubus pilocarpus subsp. gremlianus

Rubus pilocarpus subsp.

gremlianus, commonly known as the Gremli's blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique genetic traits distinguishing it from other subspecies.
