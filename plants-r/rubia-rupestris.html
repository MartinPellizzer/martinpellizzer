Rubia rupestris

Rubia rupestris, commonly known as rock betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments in Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 13,000 species of flowering plants. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often hairy and grayish-green in color. Its flowers are typically pale purple or blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Rubia rupestris is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found on limestone or rocky slopes.
