Ranunculus oblongus

Ranunculus oblongus, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Ranunculus oblongus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
