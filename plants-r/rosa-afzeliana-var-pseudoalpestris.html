Rosa afzeliana var. pseudoalpestris

Rosa afzeliana var. pseudoalpestris, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, adapting to cold and harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are dark green and slightly hairy, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blossoms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in alpine gardens.
