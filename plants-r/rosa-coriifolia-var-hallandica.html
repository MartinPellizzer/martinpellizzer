Rosa coriifolia var. hallandica

Rosa coriifolia var. hallandica, commonly known as the Halland rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate blooms and compact growth habit.
