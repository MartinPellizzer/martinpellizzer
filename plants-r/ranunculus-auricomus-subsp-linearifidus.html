Ranunculus auricomus subsp. linearifidus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

linearifidus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals and a prominent central stigma.

The leaves are deeply divided, giving the plant a feathery appearance, and are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is noted for its early spring blooming and its role in supporting pollinators.
