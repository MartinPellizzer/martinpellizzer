Rhododendron californicum

Rhododendron californicum, commonly known as the California rhododendron, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and attractive addition to gardens in suitable climates.
