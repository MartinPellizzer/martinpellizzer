Rosa coriifolia var. biserrata

Rosa coriifolia var. biserrata, commonly known as the serrated leaf dogwood rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces red, fuzzy rosehips that persist into winter, adding ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its unique foliage and floral display, it is valued as both an ornamental and a potential species for horticultural breeding.
