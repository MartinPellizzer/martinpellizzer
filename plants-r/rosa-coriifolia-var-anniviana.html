Rosa coriifolia var. anniviana

Rosa coriifolia var. anniviana, commonly known as the Anniviana shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are trifoliate, with finely serrated edges, and the plant produces red, velvety hips in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate appearance and hardy nature.

This variety is well-suited to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
