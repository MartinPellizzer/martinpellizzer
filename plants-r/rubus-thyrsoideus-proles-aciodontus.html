Rubus thyrsoideus proles aciodontus

Rubus thyrsoideus proles aciodontus, commonly known as the hooked blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its distinctive floral structure and its role in supporting local wildlife.
