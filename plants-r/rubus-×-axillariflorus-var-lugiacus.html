Rubus × axillariflorus var. lugiacus

Rubus × axillariflorus var. lugiacus, commonly known as the Lugia raspberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they may vary in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
