Rhynchospora eburnea

Rhynchospora eburnea, commonly known as the white root sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Rhynchospora eburnea is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, where it thrives in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
