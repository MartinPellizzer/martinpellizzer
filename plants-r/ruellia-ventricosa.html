Ruellia ventricosa

Ruellia ventricosa, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the warm climates of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. Ruellia ventricosa is known for its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and moderate drought conditions.
