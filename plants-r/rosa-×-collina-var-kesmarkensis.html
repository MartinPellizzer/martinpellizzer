Rosa × collina var. kesmarkensis

Rosa × collina var. kesmarkensis, commonly known as the Kesmark rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to pale red flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing small, oval-shaped hips that are an important food source for wildlife. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
