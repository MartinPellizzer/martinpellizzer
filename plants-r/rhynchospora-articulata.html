Rhynchospora articulata

Rhynchospora articulata, commonly known as the jointed flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common species in marshes and swampy areas. Rhynchospora articulata has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively, contributing to its persistence in wetland ecosystems.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
