Reseda luteola subsp. crispata

Reseda luteola subsp.

crispata, commonly known as yellowrocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Reseda luteola subsp.

crispata is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
