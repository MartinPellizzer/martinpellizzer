Ranunculus aurasiacus var. ayachicus

Ranunculus aurasiacus var. ayachicus, commonly known as the Ayachic Himalayan buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with five to eight petals. The plant has a short, stout stem that is often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are deeply lobed, with a basal rosette structure. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
