Rumex olympicus

Rumex olympicus, commonly known as Olympic sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, reddish-veined leaves that are edible and have a sharp, tangy flavor. The plant produces tall, branched flowering stems with small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
