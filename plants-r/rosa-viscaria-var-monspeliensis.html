Rosa viscaria var. monspeliensis

Rosa viscaria var. monspeliensis, commonly known as the French rose or the pink rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are pinnate, consisting of several oval, serrated leaflets. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable plant.
