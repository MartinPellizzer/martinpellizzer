Rumex obtusifolius var. nipponicus

Rumex obtusifolius var. nipponicus, commonly known as Japanese dock, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on erect, branched stems. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The species is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
