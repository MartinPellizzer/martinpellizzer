Rumex acetosa proles vinealis

Rumex acetosa proles vinealis, commonly known as sourdock or garden sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of lance-shaped, reddish-veined leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, waste areas, and along roadsides.

Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine for their tart, acidic flavor.
