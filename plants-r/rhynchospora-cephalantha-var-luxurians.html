Rhynchospora cephalantha var. luxurians

Rhynchospora cephalantha var. luxurians, commonly known as the showy spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear dense clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers. The plant produces spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
