Ranunculus alpestris subsp. traunfellneri

Ranunculus alpestris subsp.

traunfellneri, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features showy yellow flowers with numerous petals, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often hairy and adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It thrives in rocky soils and open habitats, often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for alpine ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
