Rosa koreana var. koreana

Rosa koreana var. koreana, commonly known as the Korean rose, is a species native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with 5 to 9 leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink to red, appearing in late spring to early summer. It produces hips, which are the fruit of the plant, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and blooming display.
