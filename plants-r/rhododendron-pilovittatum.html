Rhododendron pilovittatum

Rhododendron pilovittatum, commonly known as the broadleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture.

Rhododendron pilovittatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate care.
