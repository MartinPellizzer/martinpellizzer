Ranunculus metabolus

Ranunculus metabolus, commonly known as the metabolic buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, usually yellow or orange, cup-shaped flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and has a taproot system that helps it anchor in soil. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often hairy.

The species is noted for its chemical composition, which includes various metabolites that contribute to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
