Rosa canina var. cariotii

Rosa canina var. cariotii, commonly known as the Cariotti's rose, is a variant of the dog rose.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in the spring.

Its hips, which are the fruit, are orange-red and contain a high concentration of vitamin C. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
