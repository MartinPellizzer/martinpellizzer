Rubus obtruncatus var. angusticuspis

Rubus obtruncatus var. angusticuspis, commonly known as the narrow-cusped blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries and ornamental value.
