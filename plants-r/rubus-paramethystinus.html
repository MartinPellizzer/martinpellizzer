Rubus paramethystinus

Rubus paramethystinus, commonly known as the purple raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or purple in color, which contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruiting characteristics, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
