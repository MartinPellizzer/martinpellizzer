Rubus corylifolius var. rotundifolius

Rubus corylifolius var. rotundifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, round, serrated leaves that are often a deep green in color. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
