Ranunculus diversifolius proles penicillatus

Ranunculus diversifolius proles penicillatus, commonly known as the Penicillate Leaf Buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers with numerous stamens. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. The leaves are typically divided into several lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
