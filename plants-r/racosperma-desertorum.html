Racosperma desertorum

Racosperma desertorum, commonly known as the desert acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and ability to store water. Its woody structure provides habitat for various desert animals.

Racosperma desertorum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
