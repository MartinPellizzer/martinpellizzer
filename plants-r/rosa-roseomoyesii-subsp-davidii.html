Rosa roseomoyesii subsp. davidii

Rosa roseomoyesii subsp.

davidii, commonly known as the David's rose, is a shrub-like species native to China. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to pale red flowers.

The leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
