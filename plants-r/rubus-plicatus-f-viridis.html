Rubus plicatus f. viridis

Rubus plicatus f. viridis, commonly known as the green-leafed raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically a vibrant green, setting it apart from the more common red-leafed forms. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Its foliage and fruit make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
