Rubia cordifolia subsp. cordifolia

Rubia cordifolia subsp.

cordifolia, commonly known as heart-leaved rue, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It features slender, upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and hairy.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in early summer. Its roots and leaves contain compounds such as rutin and essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a common garden plant in cooler climates.
