Rhipsalis macrocarpa

Rhipsalis macrocarpa, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and clustered, spiny segments. The plant produces vibrant pink, red, or white flowers during the winter months, making it a popular ornamental species. Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a favorite among indoor gardeners.

Rhipsalis macrocarpa thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
