Racosperma levatum

Racosperma levatum, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in spikes and attract various pollinators, including bees and birds.

Racosperma levatum is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and inland environments.
