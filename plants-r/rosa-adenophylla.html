Rosa adenophylla

Rosa adenophylla, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color.

Rosa adenophylla is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
