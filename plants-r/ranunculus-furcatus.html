Ranunculus furcatus

Ranunculus furcatus, commonly known as the forked buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features simple, alternate leaves with a distinctive lobed or serrated appearance. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five to eight petals and numerous stamens.

Ranunculus furcatus thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
