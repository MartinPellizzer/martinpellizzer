Rubus hyperanthus

Rubus hyperanthus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and thorny stems, which provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local ecosystem.
