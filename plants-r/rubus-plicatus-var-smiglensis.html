Rubus plicatus var. smiglensis

Rubus plicatus var. smiglensis, commonly known as the Smiglensky raspberry, is a variant of the brambles native to eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
