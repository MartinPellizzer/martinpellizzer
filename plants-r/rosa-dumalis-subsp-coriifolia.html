Rosa dumalis subsp. coriifolia

Rosa dumalis subsp.

coriifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its silvery-white, velvety foliage that gives it its common name.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a distinctive waxy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
