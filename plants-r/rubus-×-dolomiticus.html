Rubus × dolomiticus

Rubus × dolomiticus, commonly known as the Dolomitic raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically red or yellow when ripe. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean, particularly in areas with limestone soils. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open woodlands or along rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique habitat preferences, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
