Rubus histrionicus

Rubus histrionicus, commonly known as the showy blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and large, showy white to pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant is known for its vigorous growth habit.
