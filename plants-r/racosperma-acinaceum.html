Racosperma acinaceum

Racosperma acinaceum, commonly known as the acacia tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It often grows in dry, sandy soils and is commonly found in coastal and inland regions.

The acacia tree is also valued for its timber and has cultural significance in various Indigenous communities.
