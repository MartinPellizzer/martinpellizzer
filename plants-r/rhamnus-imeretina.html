Rhamnus imeretina

Rhamnus imeretina, commonly known as the Caucasian buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Rhamnus imeretina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
