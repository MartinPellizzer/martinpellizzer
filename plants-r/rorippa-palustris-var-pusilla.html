Rorippa palustris var. pusilla

Rorippa palustris var. pusilla, commonly known as the small marsh cress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is typically found in wetland environments such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It produces small, rounded seeds that are dispersed by water, aiding in its spread across moist habitats.
