Rubus glandithyrsos

Rubus glandithyrsos, commonly known as the glandular-stem blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Rubus glandithyrsos thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
