Rosa viscaria var. squarrosa

Rosa viscaria var. squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a variety of the shrub rose native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage that covers the stems and young shoots, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It is a hardy species, adapted to cold climates and capable of surviving in rocky, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique texture, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
