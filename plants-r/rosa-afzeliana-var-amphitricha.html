Rosa afzeliana var. amphitricha

Rosa afzeliana var. amphitricha, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a rounded, red hip that contains numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.
