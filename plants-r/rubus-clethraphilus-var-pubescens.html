Rubus clethraphilus var. pubescens

Rubus clethraphilus var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are white and clustered, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and as a source of edible berries.
