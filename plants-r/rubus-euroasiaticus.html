Rubus euroasiaticus

Rubus euroasiaticus, commonly known as the European bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically red or black in color. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in temperate climates.

Rubus euroasiaticus plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife species.
