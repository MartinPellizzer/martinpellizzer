Rubia peregrina var. pubescens

Rubia peregrina var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy woodruff, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it its varietal name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
