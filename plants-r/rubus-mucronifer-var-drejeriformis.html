Rubus mucronifer var. drejeriformis

Rubus mucronifer var. drejeriformis, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
