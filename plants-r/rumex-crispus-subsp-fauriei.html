Rumex crispus subsp. fauriei

Rumex crispus subsp.

fauriei, commonly known as the curly dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its deeply lobed, wavy-edged leaves that are often covered in a whitish powdery coating called a bloom.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender, upright spikes. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, in fields, and near water sources.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
