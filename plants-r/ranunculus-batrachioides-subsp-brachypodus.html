Ranunculus batrachioides subsp. brachypodus

Ranunculus batrachioides subsp.

brachypodus, commonly known as the short-stemmed water buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils near water bodies such as ponds and streams.

The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and yellow flowers with five petals. It is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
