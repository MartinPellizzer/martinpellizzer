Rosa transsilvanica

Rosa transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian rose, is a species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Rosa transsilvanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional herbal medicine.
