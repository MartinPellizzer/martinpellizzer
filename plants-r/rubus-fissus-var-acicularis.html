Rubus fissus var. acicularis

Rubus fissus var. acicularis, commonly known as the slender raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a bright red color. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a valued plant in both culinary and ornamental contexts.
