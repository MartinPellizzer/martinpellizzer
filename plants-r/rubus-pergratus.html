Rubus pergratus

Rubus pergratus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are actually composed of many small drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Rubus pergratus is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local wildlife.
