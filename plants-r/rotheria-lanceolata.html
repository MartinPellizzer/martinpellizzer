Rotheria lanceolata

Rotheria lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf rotheria, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing on rocky soils. Its fibrous root system helps it retain moisture during long periods of drought.

Rotheria lanceolata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
