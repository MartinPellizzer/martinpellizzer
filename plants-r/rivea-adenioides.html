Rivea adenioides

Rivea adenioides, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, which gives it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Rivea adenioides is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
