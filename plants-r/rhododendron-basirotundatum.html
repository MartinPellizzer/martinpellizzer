Rhododendron basirotundatum

Rhododendron basirotundatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often densely covered with glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of funnel-shaped, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable ornamental plant in horticulture and an important component of its native ecosystems.
