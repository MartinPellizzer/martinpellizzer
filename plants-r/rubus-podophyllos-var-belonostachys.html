Rubus podophyllos var. belonostachys

Rubus podophyllos var. belonostachys, commonly known as the red elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. The plant is notable for its bright red berries that ripen in the fall, which are edible when fully mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
