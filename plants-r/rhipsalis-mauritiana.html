Rhipsalis mauritiana

Rhipsalis mauritiana, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the winter months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with well-draining soil.

Rhipsalis mauritiana is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
