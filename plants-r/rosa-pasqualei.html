Rosa pasqualei

Rosa pasqualei, commonly known as the Pasqual rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, single or double flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and a woody stem structure typical of many rose species. It blooms in late spring to early summer, often around the time of Easter, hence its common name.

Rosa pasqualei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual appeal.
