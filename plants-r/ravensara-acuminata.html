Ravensara acuminata

Ravensara acuminata, commonly known as the mountain arum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves and large, showy inflorescence. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its fruit is a dark red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Ravensara acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
