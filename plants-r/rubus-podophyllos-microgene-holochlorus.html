Rubus podophyllos microgene holochlorus

Rubus podophyllos microgene holochlorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often tart. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
