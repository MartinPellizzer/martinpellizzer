Rosa strobliana

Rosa strobliana, commonly known as the Strobliana rose, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it is typically deciduous, shedding its foliage in the fall.

Rosa strobliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
