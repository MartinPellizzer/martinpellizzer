Rhus villosa var. grandifolia

Rhus villosa var. grandifolia, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with 15 to 31 pairs of serrated leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters, which are followed by red, fuzzy berries that persist into winter. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in disturbed sites.
