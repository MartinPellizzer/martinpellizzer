Rosa rubrifolia var. kelleri

Rosa rubrifolia var. kelleri, commonly known as the Keller's red-leaf rose, is a variant of the red-leaf rose species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, deep red foliage that contrasts with its smaller, fragrant pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and is well adapted to rocky and arid environments. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a velvety texture and a distinctive reddish hue that intensifies in full sun.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
