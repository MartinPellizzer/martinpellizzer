Rosa cinnamomea var. semiplena

Rosa cinnamomea var. semiplena, commonly known as the Chinese quince rose, is a hybrid rose species native to China.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves that provide a striking contrast to its showy blooms. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The flowers are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor, making it useful in both ornamental and culinary applications.
