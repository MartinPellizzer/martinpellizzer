Rubus × confertiflorens

Rubus × confertiflorens, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on the variety. It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in both gardens and commercial settings.
