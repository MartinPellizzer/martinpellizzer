Rubus ulmifolius var. caldesianus

Rubus ulmifolius var. caldesianus, commonly known as the Caldesian blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry native to the Pyrenees and southern France.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a distinctive, elongated shape. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in some regions for both garden use and local consumption.
