Rubus × brachystemonomorphus

Rubus × brachystemonomorphus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and edible cultivation.
