Ranunculus calandrinioides

Ranunculus calandrinioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, lobed stem. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed shape, and it blooms from spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
