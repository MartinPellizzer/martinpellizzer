Rosa coriifolia var. vacillans

Rosa coriifolia var. vacillans, commonly known as the wavering coral bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely serrated, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate, cascading blooms.
