Rumia kotschyi

Rumia kotschyi, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Its fibrous roots allow it to anchor deeply in sandy soils, making it resilient to drought.

Rumia kotschyi is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in desert ecosystems.
