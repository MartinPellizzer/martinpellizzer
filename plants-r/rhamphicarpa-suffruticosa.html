Rhamphicarpa suffruticosa

Rhamphicarpa suffruticosa, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, woody shrub with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry, sandy soils.
