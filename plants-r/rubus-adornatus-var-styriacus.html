Rubus adornatus var. styriacus

Rubus adornatus var. styriacus, commonly known as the Styrian raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant produces pinkish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, juicy berries in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and natural habitats alike.
