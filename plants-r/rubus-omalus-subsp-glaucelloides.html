Rubus omalus subsp. glaucelloides

Rubus omalus subsp.

glaucelloides, commonly known as the glaucous blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its flowers are white and appear in early spring, followed by the development of its edible berries later in the season.
