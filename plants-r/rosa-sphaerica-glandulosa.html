Rosa sphaerica glandulosa

Rosa sphaerica glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular spherical rose, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spherical shape and distinctive glandular features on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
