Rhododendron apradae

Rhododendron apradae, commonly known as the Aprada rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to deep red.

Rhododendron apradae plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
