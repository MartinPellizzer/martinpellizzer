Rubus pyramidalis var. sitiens

Rubus pyramidalis var. sitiens, commonly known as the Sitien Raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that produces small, aggregate fruits composed of numerous drupelets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are pink to white in color.

The Sitien Raspberry is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruits, though it is less commonly cultivated than other raspberry varieties.
