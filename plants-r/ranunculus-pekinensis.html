Ranunculus pekinensis

Ranunculus pekinensis, commonly known as the Peking buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a short stem and produces small, hairy seeds in a follicle.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
