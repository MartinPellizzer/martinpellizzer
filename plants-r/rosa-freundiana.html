Rosa freundiana

Rosa freundiana, commonly known as the Frendiana rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa freundiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
