Rosa glauca var. venosa

Rosa glauca var. venosa, commonly known as the bluebush rose, is a compact, shrubby plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically gray-green with a waxy coating, giving the plant a distinctive, silvery appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant due to its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
