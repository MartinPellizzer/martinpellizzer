Rubus rivularis var. subcollinus

Rubus rivularis var. subcollinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, dark red to black berries. The leaves are typically narrow and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences. This variety is often found in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its berries and foliage.
