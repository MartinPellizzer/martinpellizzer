Ranunculus eschscholtzii var. oxynotus

Ranunculus eschscholtzii var. oxynotus, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinct lobed or dissected appearance.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
