Ranunculus fallax subsp. semicassubicus

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

semicassubicus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features delicate, palmate leaves with a hairy texture and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers are typically 2–4 cm in diameter, with 5–15 petals arranged in a cup-like formation.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
