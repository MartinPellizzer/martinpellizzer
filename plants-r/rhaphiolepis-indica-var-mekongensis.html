Rhaphiolepis indica var. mekongensis

Rhaphiolepis indica var. mekongensis, commonly known as the Mekong boxwood, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a glossy appearance, making it an attractive ornamental plant. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by orange-red berries that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, preferring moderate to cool climates.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it suitable for use in gardens and landscapes.
