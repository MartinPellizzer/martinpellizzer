Rubus × tomentaceus

Rubus × tomentaceus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, white to grayish pubescence covering its stems and leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
