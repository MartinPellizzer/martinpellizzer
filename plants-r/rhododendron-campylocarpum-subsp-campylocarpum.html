Rhododendron campylocarpum subsp. campylocarpum

Rhododendron campylocarpum subsp.

campylocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are typically narrow and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays. This subspecies thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
