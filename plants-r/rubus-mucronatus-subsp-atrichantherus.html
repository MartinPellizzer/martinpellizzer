Rubus mucronatus subsp. atrichantherus

Rubus mucronatus subsp.

atrichantherus, commonly known as the black-haired bramble, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine, short hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique adaptations suited to its native habitat.
