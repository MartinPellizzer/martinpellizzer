Rapunculus spicatus

Rapunculus spicatus, commonly known as the spiked bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

Rapunculus spicatus is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in temperate regions.
