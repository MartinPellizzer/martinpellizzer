Rosa afzeliana f. aspera

Rosa afzeliana f. aspera, commonly known as the rough rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa afzeliana.

It is characterized by its rough, textured stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other rose cultivars. The plant produces pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of Asia, including China and the Himalayas, where it thrives in temperate climates.

Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental foliage and relatively hardy nature, making it suitable for garden cultivation.
