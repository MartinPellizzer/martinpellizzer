Rhynchospora macrostachya var. inundata

Rhynchospora macrostachya var. inundata, commonly known as the swamp three-awn, is a wetland plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and inflorescences that bear small, three-angled spikelets. The plant typically grows in marshy or flooded habitats, thriving in saturated soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

This variety is adapted to periodic flooding and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
