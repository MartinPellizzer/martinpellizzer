Rubus iricus

Rubus iricus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a hedging plant.
