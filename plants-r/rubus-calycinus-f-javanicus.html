Rubus calycinus f. javanicus

Rubus calycinus f. javanicus, commonly known as the Java raspberry, is a variant of the bramble species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect or shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, often with a sweet and tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This plant is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional local cuisines.
