Ravensara parvifolia

Ravensara parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved raven's arum, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an understory species in humid, shaded forest environments. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Ravensara parvifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
