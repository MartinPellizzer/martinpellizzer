Rosa afzeliana var. elongata

Rosa afzeliana var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated rose, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Vietnam.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its upright, branching structure. The plant features compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens. It produces hips, which are the fruiting bodies containing seeds, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
