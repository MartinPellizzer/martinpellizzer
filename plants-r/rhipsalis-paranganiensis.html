Rhipsalis paranganiensis

Rhipsalis paranganiensis, commonly known as the Paranganiensis mistletoe cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often as an epiphyte on trees. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and it produces small, round fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
