Ranunculus altilobus

Ranunculus altilobus, commonly known as the high-lobed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with five to seven petals and deeply lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with lobes that are often longer than they are wide.

Ranunculus altilobus is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in early spring.
