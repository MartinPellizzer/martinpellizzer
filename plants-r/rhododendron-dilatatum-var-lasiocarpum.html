Rhododendron dilatatum var. lasiocarpum

Rhododendron dilatatum var. lasiocarpum, commonly known as the woolly-berry rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces distinctive, hairy, dark red to purple berries that persist into winter, adding ornamental value throughout the year. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
