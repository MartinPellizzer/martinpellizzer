Ruellia strigosa

Ruellia strigosa, commonly known as velvetleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple tubular flowers that bloom in the summer months. Ruellia strigosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
