Rhaptostylum cauliflorum

Rhaptostylum cauliflorum, commonly known as the climbing orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique climbing habit, with aerial roots that allow it to ascend tree trunks and other structures. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a fragrant scent. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a stem that is covered in small, scale-like structures.

Rhaptostylum cauliflorum is primarily found in humid, shaded environments within lowland forests.
