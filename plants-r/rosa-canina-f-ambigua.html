Rosa canina f. ambigua

Rosa canina f. ambigua, commonly known as the bramble rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, which provide structural support and deter herbivores. The plant produces pink to red flowers in late spring, followed by orange to red hips that are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and it thrives in a variety of soil types.

Rosa canina f. ambigua is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability to different environments.
