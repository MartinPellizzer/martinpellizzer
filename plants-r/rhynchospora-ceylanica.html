Rhynchospora ceylanica

Rhynchospora ceylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in wetland and marshy environments. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
