Rytigynia adenodonta

Rytigynia adenodonta, commonly known as the glossy-leaved rytigynia, is a tropical shrub native to the rainforests of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, glossy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its humid environment. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the warmer months, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its dispersal in the forest understory.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often sought after by horticulturists and conservationists.
