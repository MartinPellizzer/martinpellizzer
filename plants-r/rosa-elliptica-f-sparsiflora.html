Rosa elliptica f. sparsiflora

Rosa elliptica f. sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered elliptic rose, is a botanical variant of the elliptic rose species.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves and reduced floral display compared to typical roses. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring. It is native to specific regions in Europe and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
