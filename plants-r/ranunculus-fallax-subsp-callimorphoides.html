Ranunculus fallax subsp. callimorphoides

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

callimorphoides, commonly known as the woolly buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, glandular stem that distinguishes it from other ranunculus species.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and has palmately lobed leaves with a fuzzy texture. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.

The subspecies callimorphoides is characterized by its unique floral morphology and adapted growth form to its alpine environment.
