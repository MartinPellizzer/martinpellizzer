Rubus × scytophyllus

Rubus × scytophyllus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its berries are edible, though they are often described as having a tart and somewhat bitter flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in some local cuisines.
