Rubus tereticaulis var. lejolisii

Rubus tereticaulis var. lejolisii, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and woodland areas. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and its role in local ecosystems.
