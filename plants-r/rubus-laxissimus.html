Rubus laxissimus

Rubus laxissimus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that typically turn dark purple or black when mature. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Rubus laxissimus is often found in forest understories and along streambanks, contributing to local biodiversity.
