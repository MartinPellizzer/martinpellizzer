Rapuntium multicaule

Rapuntium multicaule, commonly known as the clustered cress, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with numerous small, clustered stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by heart-shaped, edible leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers are often used in culinary applications, adding a peppery flavor to dishes.
