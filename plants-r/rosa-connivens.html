Rosa connivens

Rosa connivens, commonly known as the sneaking rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa connivens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
