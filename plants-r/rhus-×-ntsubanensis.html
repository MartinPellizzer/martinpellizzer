Rhus × ntsubanensis

Rhus × ntsubanensis, commonly known as the Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 5–11 leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to various light conditions, making it a versatile ornamental plant.
