Rosa × dumetorum var. schreiberi

Rosa × dumetorum var. schreiberi, commonly known as Schreiber's shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardiness. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden borders or as a foundation plant.

Its botanical classification highlights its hybrid origin, combining traits from various Rosa species.
