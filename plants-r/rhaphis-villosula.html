Rhaphis villosula

Rhaphis villosula, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage that gives it a velvety appearance. The plant typically grows as a low, spreading ground cover with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Rhaphis villosula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and visual interest.
