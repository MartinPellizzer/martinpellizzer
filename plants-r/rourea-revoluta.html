Rourea revoluta

Rourea revoluta, commonly known as the velvetleaf rourea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a leathery texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Rourea revoluta is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
