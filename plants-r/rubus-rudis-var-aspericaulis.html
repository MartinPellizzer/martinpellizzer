Rubus rudis var. aspericaulis

Rubus rudis var. aspericaulis, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

Its berries are edible, though they are generally less sweet and more tart compared to other blackberry varieties.
