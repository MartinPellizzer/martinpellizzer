Rosa woronowii var. setosa

Rosa woronowii var. setosa, commonly known as the Setose Rosa, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its simple, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers with pink to red petals. Native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea, this plant thrives in temperate climates. The leaves are typically pinnately compound, and the plant produces fragrant, edible hips that are used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
