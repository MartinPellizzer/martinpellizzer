Rosa ranunculiflora

Rosa ranunculiflora, commonly known as the meadow rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, pink to reddish-purple petals. Rosa ranunculiflora thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
