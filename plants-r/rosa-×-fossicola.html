Rosa × fossicola

Rosa × fossicola, commonly known as the creeping shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges and a compact growth habit.

Rosa × fossicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
