Ranunculus quelpaertensis var. albiflorus

Ranunculus quelpaertensis var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered water buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that are predominantly white with a yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils near water bodies such as streams and marshes. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a distinctive hairy texture.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy and often colorful blooms.
