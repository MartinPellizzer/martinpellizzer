Reseda alba proles myriophylla

Reseda alba proles myriophylla, commonly known as white cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in meadows.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
