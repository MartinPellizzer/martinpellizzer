Rosa × palatinensis

Rosa × palatinensis, commonly known as the English rose, is a hybrid species of rose originating from crosses between Rosa gallica and Rosa damascena.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, semi-double flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has dark green, serrated leaves. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for floral arrangements and garden displays.
