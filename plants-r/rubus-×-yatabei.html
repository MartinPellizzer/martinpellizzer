Rubus × yatabei

Rubus × yatabei, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tart or astringent flavor.
