Reynoutria ciliinodis

Reynoutria ciliinodis, commonly known as the silky knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is often considered a problematic species in certain regions.
