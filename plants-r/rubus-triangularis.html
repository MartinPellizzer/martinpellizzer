Rubus triangularis

Rubus triangularis, commonly known as the triangular blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically triangular in shape, giving the plant its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Rubus triangularis is a perennial shrub that can grow up to several meters in height.
