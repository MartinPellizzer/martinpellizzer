Rhynchosia cajanoides

Rhynchosia cajanoides, commonly known as the African milkpea, is a climbing leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces yellow to orange flowers that are often clustered in racemes. The plant bears pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are used in traditional agriculture. Rhynchosia cajanoides is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in crop rotation systems.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
