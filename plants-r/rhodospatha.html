Rhodospatha

Rhodospatha, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, often pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plants typically have broad, lance-shaped leaves and are epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants without being parasitic. Rhodospatha species are native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

They are valued in horticulture for their ornamental beauty and are relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
