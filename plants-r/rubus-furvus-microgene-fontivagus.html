Rubus furvus microgene fontivagus

Rubus furvus microgene fontivagus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rubus genus in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its small, dark berries and trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils. It produces flowers with five pink petals and is pollinated by bees and other insects.

The fruit is edible but often has a tart flavor, making it a popular ingredient in jams and preserves.
