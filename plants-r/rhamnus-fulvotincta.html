Rhamnus fulvotincta

Rhamnus fulvotincta, commonly known as the yellow-tinctured buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark purple berries that are edible for certain bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
