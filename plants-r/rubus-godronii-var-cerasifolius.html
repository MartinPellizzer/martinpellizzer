Rubus godronii var. cerasifolius

Rubus godronii var. cerasifolius, commonly known as the cherryleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, and the leaves are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
