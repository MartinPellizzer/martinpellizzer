Rhinorchis caucana

Rhinorchis caucana, commonly known as the Caucasian rhinorchis, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It typically grows in rocky or forested habitats, often in areas with well-drained soil. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive yellowish-green hue. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and bloom during the late spring or early summer.

Rhinorchis caucana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
