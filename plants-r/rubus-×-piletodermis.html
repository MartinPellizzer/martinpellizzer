Rubus × piletodermis

Rubus × piletodermis, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between blackberries (Rubus fruticosus) and raspberries (Rubus idaeus).

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. The plant produces small, oval leaves with serrated edges and grows as an erect shrub. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its flavorful berries.

The hybrid is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
