Rosa serbica

Rosa serbica, commonly known as the Serbian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant pink or white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by red or orange hips. Rosa serbica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has also been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Rosaceae family.
