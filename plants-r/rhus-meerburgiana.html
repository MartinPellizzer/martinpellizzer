Rhus meerburgiana

Rhus meerburgiana, commonly known as the Meerburg holly or Meerburg holly tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, spiny leaves that provide a distinctive texture. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals. Rhus meerburgiana thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its hardiness and attractive appearance.

It is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and resist pests.
