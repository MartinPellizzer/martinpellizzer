Rubus ulmifolius proles sublenis

Rubus ulmifolius proles sublenis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in thorny projections. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

The botanical features of this species make it both ecologically significant and valuable for culinary and medicinal uses.
