Rubus fuscus var. pustulifer

Rubus fuscus var. pustulifer, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a shrub-like species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruits, known as blackberries, are typically small and have a distinctive, fuzzy texture.

This variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
