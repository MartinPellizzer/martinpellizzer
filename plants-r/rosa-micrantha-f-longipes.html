Rosa micrantha f. longipes

Rosa micrantha f. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed shrub rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa micrantha.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly China. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
