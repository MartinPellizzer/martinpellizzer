Rubus erythranthemus

Rubus erythranthemus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are typically red when mature. These fruits are rich in vitamins and antioxidants, making them both nutritious and flavorful.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.
