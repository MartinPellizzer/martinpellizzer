Rubus liui

Rubus liui, commonly known as the Liui blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in moist, temperate climates.

Rubus liui is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional Chinese medicine.
