Rubus × modestus

Rubus × modestus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between blackberries (Rubus ursinus) and raspberries (Rubus idaeus).

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, depending on the variety. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Rubus × modestus is valued for its culinary uses and is often cultivated for its flavorful berries.
