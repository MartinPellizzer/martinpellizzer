Rumex gussonei

Rumex gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's sorrel, is a species of flowering plant in the Polygonaceae family.

It is native to parts of southern Europe and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are edible and have a sour taste due to the presence of oxalic acid.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
