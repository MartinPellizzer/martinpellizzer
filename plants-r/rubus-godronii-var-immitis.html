Rubus godronii var. immitis

Rubus godronii var. immitis, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique morphological traits that aid in its identification.
