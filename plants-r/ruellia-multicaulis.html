Ruellia multicaulis

Ruellia multicaulis, commonly known as the purpletop verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ruellia multicaulis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
