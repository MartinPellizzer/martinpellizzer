Ranunculus subhomophyllus

Ranunculus subhomophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. The leaves are typically divided into lobes and have a distinctive, somewhat waxy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
