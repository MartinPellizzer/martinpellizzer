Rosa stylosa var. beucensis

Rosa stylosa var. beucensis, commonly known as the Beuc's climbing rose, is a variant of the stylosa rose native to China.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and thorny stems, typical of many Rosa species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticultural collections.
