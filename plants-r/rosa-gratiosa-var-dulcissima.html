Rosa gratiosa var. dulcissima

Rosa gratiosa var. dulcissima, commonly known as the Sweet Grace rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the United States.

It is characterized by its large, semi-double to fully double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and has a compact, bushy form with dark green foliage. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring regular watering and moderate pruning.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
