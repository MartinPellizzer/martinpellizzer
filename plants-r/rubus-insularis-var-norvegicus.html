Rubus insularis var. norvegicus

Rubus insularis var. norvegicus, commonly known as the Norwegian blackberry, is a shrub native to the northern regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and culinary purposes.
