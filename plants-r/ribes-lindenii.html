Ribes lindenii

Ribes lindenii, commonly known as the purple-flowered currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black when mature, often used in jams and culinary applications. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Ribes lindenii thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile addition to native plant landscapes.
