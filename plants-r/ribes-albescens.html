Ribes albescens

Ribes albescens, commonly known as the white currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of gooseberries and currants. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The berries, which ripen in late summer, are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and jellies.
