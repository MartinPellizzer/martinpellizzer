Rosa virescens

Rosa virescens, commonly known as the evergreen rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in regions like the Appalachian Mountains. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Rosa virescens is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with thorny stems and evergreen foliage.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
