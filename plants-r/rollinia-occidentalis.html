Rollinia occidentalis

Rollinia occidentalis, commonly known as the western starfruit, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and mango. The plant produces small, yellow, star-shaped fruits that are edible when ripe. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a glossy surface and serrated edges.

Rollinia occidentalis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
