Rubus adornatus var. breviglandulosus

Rubus adornatus var. breviglandulosus, commonly known as the small-glandular raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, glandular hairs on the stems and leaves. The plant produces red, aggregate fruits that are typically consumed by wildlife and humans alike. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and showy flowers.
