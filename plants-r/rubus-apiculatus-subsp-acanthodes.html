Rubus apiculatus subsp. acanthodes

Rubus apiculatus subsp.

acanthodes, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
