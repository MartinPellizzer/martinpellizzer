Rosa caryophyllacea f. budwitzensis

Rosa caryophyllacea f. budwitzensis, commonly known as the Budwitz's rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa caryophyllacea.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with a fragrant scent, and bloom in late summer. This form is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
