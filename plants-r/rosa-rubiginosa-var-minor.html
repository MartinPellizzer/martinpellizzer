Rosa rubiginosa var. minor

Rosa rubiginosa var. minor, commonly known as the lesser crimson rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
