Reseda pruinosa subsp. tefedestica

Reseda pruinosa subsp.

tefedestica, commonly known as the Armenian wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its hairy stems and foliage give it a distinctive, somewhat fuzzy appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
