Rosa empylotoides

Rosa empylotoides, commonly known as the purpleleaf rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple to almost black foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and is often found in moist, wooded areas. Its leaves are compound, with 5 to 7 leaflets that are ovate to elliptical in shape.

Rosa empylotoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
