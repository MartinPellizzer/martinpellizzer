Rubus alterniflorus microgene nouletii

Rubus alterniflorus microgene nouletii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its alternate leaves, which are typically narrow and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
