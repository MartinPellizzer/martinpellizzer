Rhododendron hirsutum f. dryadifolium

Rhododendron hirsutum f. dryadifolium, commonly known as the dryad's leaf rhododendron, is a unique hybrid form characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features elongated, narrow leaves that are often described as resembling the leaves of the dryad, a mythical tree spirit. The plant typically grows as a shrub with dense, leathery foliage that provides a striking visual contrast to its showy flowers. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five petals and a strong, spicy fragrance.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
