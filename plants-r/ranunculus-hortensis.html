Ranunculus hortensis

Ranunculus hortensis, commonly known as the garden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, white, and pink, with a central cluster of stamens. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Ranunculus hortensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow from seeds or division.
