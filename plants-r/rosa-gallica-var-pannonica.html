Rosa gallica var. pannonica

Rosa gallica var. pannonica, commonly known as the Hungarian rose, is a compact shrub native to Central Europe.

It features dark green, glossy leaves and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is well-suited for small garden spaces or container planting. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial for temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
