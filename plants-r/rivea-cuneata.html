Rivea cuneata

Rivea cuneata, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, often variegated foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its stems are covered in fine hairs and have a slightly woody texture.

Rivea cuneata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
