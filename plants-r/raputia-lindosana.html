Raputia lindosana

Raputia lindosana, commonly known as the Lindos raputia, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and arrow-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture.

Raputia lindosana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, alkaline conditions.
