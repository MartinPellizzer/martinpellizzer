Raphanus sinensis

Raphanus sinensis, commonly known as Chinese radish or daikon, is a biennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its large, fleshy, white or pale yellow roots. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are edible, often used in various Asian cuisines. Raphanus sinensis thrives in cool climates and is typically cultivated for its root vegetable, which is rich in nutrients.

Its growth cycle involves a period of vegetative growth followed by flowering and seed production.
