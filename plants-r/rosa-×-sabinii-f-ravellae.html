Rosa × sabinii f. ravellae

Rosa × sabinii f. ravellae, commonly known as the Ravell's rose, is a hybrid cultivar of the Rosa sabinii species.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and abundant, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is typically shrub-like, with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardy nature, though it may require regular pruning to maintain its shape.
