Rosa glauca f. separata

Rosa glauca f. separata, commonly known as the blue rose or blue shrub rose, is a unique cultivar of the Rosa glauca species.

It is characterized by its striking blue-pink flowers, which are a result of a genetic mutation affecting pigmentation. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with thorny stems and dark green, leathery foliage. Its flowers bloom in early summer and are often used in gardens for their ornamental value.

This cultivar is prized for its unusual color and hardy nature, making it suitable for various climates.
