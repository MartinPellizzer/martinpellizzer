Rollinia elliptica

Rollinia elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic rollinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers followed by large, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its fruit is edible, though the skin and seeds are usually not consumed.

Rollinia elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic and potential culinary uses.
