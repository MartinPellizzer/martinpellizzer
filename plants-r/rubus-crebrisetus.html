Rubus crebrisetus

Rubus crebrisetus, commonly known as the European blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color, often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in woodlands and along hedgerows.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
