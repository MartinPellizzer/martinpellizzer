Ranunculus pedatifidus var. affinis

Ranunculus pedatifidus var. affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically appearing in early spring. The leaves are deeply lobed and trifoliate, giving the plant a distinctive, fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both ornamental and somewhat invasive in certain regions.
