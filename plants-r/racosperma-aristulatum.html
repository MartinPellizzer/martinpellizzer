Racosperma aristulatum

Racosperma aristulatum, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The silky wattle is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native insects.
