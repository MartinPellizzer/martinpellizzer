Rosa canina subvar. biserrata

Rosa canina subvar. biserrata, commonly known as the serrated dog rose, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves with serrated edges, and fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces red, hairy hips that are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
