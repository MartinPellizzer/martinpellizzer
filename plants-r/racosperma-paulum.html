Racosperma paulum

Racosperma paulum, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a grey or brown color, while its roots are fibrous and well-developed.

Racosperma paulum is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
