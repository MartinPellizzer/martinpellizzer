Rosea crassifolia

Rosea crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved rose.

It is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South Africa, characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers during the warmer months, adding visual interest to its appearance. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Rosea crassifolia is well-suited for rock gardens or xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
