Rosa armassensis

Rosa armassensis, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the summer months, which are followed by oval, red-orange fruits called hips. Rosa armassensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both its beauty and medicinal properties.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the rose family, Rosaceae.
