Rosa afzeliana var. stephanocarpa

Rosa afzeliana var. stephanocarpa, commonly known as the Stephanocarpa rose, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges. The plant produces small, red-orange fruits called hips, which are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
