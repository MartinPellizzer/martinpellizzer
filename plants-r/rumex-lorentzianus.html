Rumex lorentzianus

Rumex lorentzianus, commonly known as the Lorentz's sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features alternate, broadly ovate leaves with a distinctive reddish underside, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is noted for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor similar to rhubarb.
