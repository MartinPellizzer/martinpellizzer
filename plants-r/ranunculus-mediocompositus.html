Ranunculus mediocompositus

Ranunculus mediocompositus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, waxy appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both ornamental and ecological value.
