Rosa villosa var. pseudogrenieri

Rosa villosa var. pseudogrenieri, commonly known as the silverleaf rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically covered in fine, velvety hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
