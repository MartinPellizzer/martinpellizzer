Rafnia intermedia

Rafnia intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian sycamore, is a large, fast-growing tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are important for wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in reforestation projects due to its rapid growth and adaptability.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and food for various species.
