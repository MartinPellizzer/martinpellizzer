Rhodocactus bahiensis

Rhodocactus bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia rose cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and collections.
