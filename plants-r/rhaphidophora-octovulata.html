Rhaphidophora octovulata

Rhaphidophora octovulata, commonly known as the Octopus Plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil.

Rhaphidophora octovulata is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
