Randia maculata

Randia maculata, commonly known as the spotted randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with prominent veins and a distinctive spotted or mottled appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Randia maculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
