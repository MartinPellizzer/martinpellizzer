Rubus murrii

Rubus murrii, commonly known as the Murrii blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Asia, particularly India and China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, thriving in temperate to subtropical climates. Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and desserts.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
