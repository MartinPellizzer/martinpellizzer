Rubus ulmifolius var. rusticus

Rubus ulmifolius var. rusticus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry bush, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregated fruits known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in hedgerow management.
