Rubus euphyllotrophus

Rubus euphyllotrophus, commonly known as the golden-leaf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow foliage, which contrasts strikingly with its dark red berries. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small white flowers in the spring. Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and desserts.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and natural habitats.
