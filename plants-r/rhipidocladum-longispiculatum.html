Rhipidocladum longispiculatum

Rhipidocladum longispiculatum, commonly known as the long-spined rockcress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant features glandular-pubescent stems and leaves, with the leaves typically being oblanceolate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape typical of the Brassicaceae family.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions.
