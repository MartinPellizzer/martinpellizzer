Rubus semiconspicuus

Rubus semiconspicuus, commonly known as the western blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into dark purple berries, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny environments.

Rubus semiconspicuus plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
