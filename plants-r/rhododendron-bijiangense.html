Rhododendron bijiangense

Rhododendron bijiangense, commonly known as the Bijiang rhododendron, is a species native to China, specifically found in the Yunnan Province.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions with high humidity and acidic soils. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red in color, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a significant role in the local biodiversity of its habitat.
