Rhododendron headfortianum

Rhododendron headfortianum, commonly known as the Headfort rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. The plant features dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flower clusters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
