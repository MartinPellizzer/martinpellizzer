Racinaea tetrantha var. caribaea

Racinaea tetrantha var. caribaea, commonly known as the Caribaea racina, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean region, particularly found in coastal areas and moist lowland forests. The plant produces striking inflorescences composed of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. Its leaves are usually large, broad, and palmately lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued not only for its aesthetic qualities but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
