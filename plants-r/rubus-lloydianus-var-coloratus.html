Rubus lloydianus var. coloratus

Rubus lloydianus var. coloratus, commonly known as the pink passionflower, is a climbing shrub native to the humid forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a faint red or purple tinge. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
