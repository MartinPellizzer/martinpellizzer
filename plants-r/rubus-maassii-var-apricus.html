Rubus maassii var. apricus

Rubus maassii var. apricus, commonly known as the apricot raspberry, is a rare and ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, edible, apricot-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, with small white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
