Rosa tyraica

Rosa tyraica, commonly known as the Turkish rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in spring, which are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine. Rosa tyraica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its historical significance dates back to ancient times, where it was valued for its beauty and aromatic properties.
