Ranunculus auricomus subsp. ovatitoratus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

ovatitoratus, commonly known as the broad-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It features palmately divided leaves with a distinctive lobed shape and bright yellow flowers with numerous petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It is characterized by its short, hairy stems and produces small, rounded seeds in a capsule.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to the typical form of Ranunculus auricomus.
