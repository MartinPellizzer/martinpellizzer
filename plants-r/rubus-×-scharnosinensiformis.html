Rubus × scharnosinensiformis

Rubus × scharnosinensiformis, commonly known as the Scharnhofer raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically red or yellow when ripe. The plant is native to Central Europe and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
