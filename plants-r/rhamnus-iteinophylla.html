Rhamnus iteinophylla

Rhamnus iteinophylla, commonly known as the Canary Island buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy pulp.

Rhamnus iteinophylla is adapted to the arid and rocky environments of its native habitat, exhibiting drought tolerance and resilience to coastal conditions.
