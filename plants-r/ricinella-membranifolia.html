Ricinella membranifolia

Ricinella membranifolia, commonly known as the paper-leaf ricinella, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are thin and membranous. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers surrounded by a spathe, which is a modified leaf-like structure. Ricinella membranifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
