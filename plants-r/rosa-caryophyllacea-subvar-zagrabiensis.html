Rosa caryophyllacea subvar. zagrabiensis

Rosa caryophyllacea subvar. zagrabiensis, commonly known as the Zagreb rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are covered in thorns. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticultural collections.
