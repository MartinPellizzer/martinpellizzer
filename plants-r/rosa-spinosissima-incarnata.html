Rosa spinosissima incarnata

Rosa spinosissima incarnata, commonly known as the pink Spanish rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. Its leaves are pinnate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are often used in ornamental gardening.

This plant is valued for its beauty and historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
