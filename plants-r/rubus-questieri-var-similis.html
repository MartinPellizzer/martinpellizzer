Rubus questieri var. similis

Rubus questieri var. similis, commonly known as the similar blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in the fall. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
