Rhododendron chunii subsp. chunii

Rhododendron chunii subsp.

chunii, commonly known as the Yunnan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in cool, moist climates with acidic soil. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to white, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.

This subspecies is valued for both its ecological role and horticultural appeal.
