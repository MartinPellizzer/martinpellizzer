Rubus moluccanus var. buergeri

Rubus moluccanus var. buergeri, commonly known as the Buerger's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
