Rubus tereticaulis var. persiciflorus

Rubus tereticaulis var. persiciflorus, commonly known as the pinkflowered blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, dark red berries in the fall. Its fruit is often used in jams and pies, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
