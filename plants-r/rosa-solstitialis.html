Rosa solstitialis

Rosa solstitialis, commonly known as the summer rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa solstitialis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
