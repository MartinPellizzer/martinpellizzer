Rosa lanuginosa

Rosa lanuginosa, commonly known as the downy rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, white to light pink flowers and thorny stems. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal use.
