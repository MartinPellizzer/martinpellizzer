Ruschia brevicarpa

Ruschia brevicarpa, commonly known as the dwarf bottlebrush plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features compact, branching stems covered in dense, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
