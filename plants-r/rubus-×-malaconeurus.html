Rubus × malaconeurus

Rubus × malaconeurus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between blackberries (Rubus fruticosus) and raspberries (Rubus idaeus).

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that resemble both blackberries and raspberries. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications.

This hybrid is valued for its vigorous growth and the unique flavor profile of its fruits.
