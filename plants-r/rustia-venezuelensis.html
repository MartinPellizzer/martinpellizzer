Rustia venezuelensis

Rustia venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan rustia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Venezuela, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads. It is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks, where it benefits from moist, well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
