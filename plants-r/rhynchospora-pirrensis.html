Rhynchospora pirrensis

Rhynchospora pirrensis, commonly known as the Florida marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, often forming dense stands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its reproductive strategy relies on wind pollination, which is common among sedges.

Rhynchospora pirrensis plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
