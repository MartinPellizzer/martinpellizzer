Rhododendron javanicum subsp. brookeanum

Rhododendron javanicum subsp.

brookeanum, commonly known as the Brooke's rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to nutrient-rich, acidic soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture, though it requires careful cultivation due to its specific environmental needs.
