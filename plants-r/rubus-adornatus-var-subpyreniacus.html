Rubus adornatus var. subpyreniacus

Rubus adornatus var. subpyreniacus, commonly known as the Pyrenean raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southwestern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at elevations ranging from 500 to 2,000 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruits, which ripen in late summer, are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local culinary traditions.
