Rubus lasiocarpus var. ectenothyrsus

Rubus lasiocarpus var. ectenothyrsus, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus characterized by its hairy stems and dark, edible fruits.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in moist, temperate climates. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, which is typically dark purple to black, is often used in jams and pies.

The species is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
