Rhododendron polyanthemum

Rhododendron polyanthemum, commonly known as the mountain laurel, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils in forested areas. The plant is characterized by its clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and elliptical in shape.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability.
