Rosa caesia subsp. vosagiaca

Rosa caesia subsp.

vosagiaca, commonly known as the Vosagian rose, is a subspecies native to the Carpathian Basin, particularly in Hungary and parts of Romania. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to reddish flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on pedicels. Its botanical characteristics include ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a woody structure that allows it to thrive in temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
