Ranunculus montanus subsp. gouanii

Ranunculus montanus subsp.

gouanii, commonly known as the Gouan's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight yellow petals and a prominent yellow center, typically blooming in early summer.

The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
