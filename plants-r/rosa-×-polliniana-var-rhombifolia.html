Rosa × polliniana var. rhombifolia

Rosa × polliniana var. rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombifoliate hybrid rose, is a botanical cultivar characterized by its unique foliage and floral traits.

It is a hybrid of Rosa × polliniana, which itself is a cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis, resulting in a plant with robust growth and hardiness. The leaves of this variety are rhomboid in shape, giving it its specific epithet, and are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink to red, with a strong fragrance, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
