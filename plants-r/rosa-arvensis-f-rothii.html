Rosa arvensis f. rothii

Rosa arvensis f. rothii, commonly known as the Roth's field rose, is a horticultural form of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and semi-double to double pink flowers. The plant typically produces thorny stems and has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and historical use in rose breeding programs.
