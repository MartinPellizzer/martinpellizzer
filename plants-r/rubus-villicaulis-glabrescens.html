Rubus villicaulis glabrescens

Rubus villicaulis glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed blackberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often glabrous stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This species is valued for its edible berries and its role in ecological habitats.
