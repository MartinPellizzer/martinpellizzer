Rhododendron atrichum

Rhododendron atrichum, commonly known as the smoothleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its lance-shaped, glabrous leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in late spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific soil and climate conditions.
