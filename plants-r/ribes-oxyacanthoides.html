Ribes oxyacanthoides

Ribes oxyacanthoides, commonly known as the redcurrant gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. These fruits are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and preserves.
