Rhizocephalum brachysiphonium

Rhizocephalum brachysiphonium, commonly known as the narrow-siphon rhizocephalum, is a species of parasitic plant belonging to the order Rhizocephala.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a root-like holdfast that attaches to the host organism. This plant lacks typical green foliage and instead relies on its host for nutrients, exhibiting a highly specialized parasitic lifestyle. Its reproductive structures are often concealed within the host tissue, making it difficult to identify without close examination.

Rhizocephalum brachysiphonium is typically found in marine environments, where it infects various invertebrate hosts.
