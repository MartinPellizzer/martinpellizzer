Ritterocactus curvispinus

Ritterocactus curvispinus, commonly known as the curved spine cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a small, solitary cactus with a short, cylindrical stem that often branches near the base. The plant is characterized by its thick, ribbed structure and clusters of spines that are curved and often reddish in color. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring to early summer.

Ritterocactus curvispinus is well adapted to dry conditions, with a shallow root system and a waxy coating to reduce water loss.
