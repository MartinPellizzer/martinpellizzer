Rhynchospora dissitiflora

Rhynchospora dissitiflora, commonly known as the scattered-flowered flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and other moist environments, where it forms dense stands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, contributing to its success in wetland ecosystems.

Rhynchospora dissitiflora plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
