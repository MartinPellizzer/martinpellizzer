Rubus ulmifolius var. acuminatus

Rubus ulmifolius var. acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves with acuminate tips, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep red or black color. It thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and rocky slopes.
