Rosa canina subsp. fissidens

Rosa canina subsp.

fissidens, commonly known as the fissured dog rose, is a subspecies native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant pink or white flowers in spring, followed by orange-red hips that are rich in vitamin C. Its botanical features include a woody structure and adaptability to various soil types, making it resilient in different environments.

It is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and open areas where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
