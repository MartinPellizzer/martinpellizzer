Rubus × winterifrons

Rubus × winterifrons, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its persistent foliage and dark purple to black fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub, with thorny stems and compound leaves that have serrated edges. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that ripen in late summer or early autumn.

This species is native to regions of East Asia and is valued for both its ornamental qualities and culinary uses.
