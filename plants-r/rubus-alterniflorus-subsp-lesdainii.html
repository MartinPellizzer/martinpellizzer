Rubus alterniflorus subsp. lesdainii

Rubus alterniflorus subsp.

lesdainii, commonly known as the Lesdain's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and wild areas.
