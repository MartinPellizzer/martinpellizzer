Rubus arachnoideus var. tatongensis

Rubus arachnoideus var. tatongensis, commonly known as the Tatong bramble, is a shrub native to the highlands of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, dark red berries that are edible. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of tropical flora.
