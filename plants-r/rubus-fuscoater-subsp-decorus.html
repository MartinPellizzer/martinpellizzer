Rubus fuscoater subsp. decorus

Rubus fuscoater subsp.

decorus, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
