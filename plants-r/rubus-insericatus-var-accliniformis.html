Rubus insericatus var. accliniformis

Rubus insericatus var. accliniformis, commonly known as the bent-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its unique fruit morphology, with a curved or bent shape that distinguishes it from other Rubus species. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, often in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or thickets. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white, producing small, aggregate fruits.

The botanical features of this variant make it an interesting subject for taxonomic study and ecological research.
