Rubus argenteus var. ossalensis

Rubus argenteus var. ossalensis, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
