Rubus sadebeckii

Rubus sadebeckii, commonly known as the Sadebeck raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the United Kingdom.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, similar to other raspberry species. It thrives in woodland edges and moist soils, often forming dense thickets.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
