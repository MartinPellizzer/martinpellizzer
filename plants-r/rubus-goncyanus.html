Rubus goncyanus

Rubus goncyanus, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark red berries that are edible.

The berries are often used in jams and desserts due to their tart flavor and high antioxidant content.
