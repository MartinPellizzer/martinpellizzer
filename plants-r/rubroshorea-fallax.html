Rubroshorea fallax

Rubroshorea fallax, commonly known as the crimson shorea, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.
