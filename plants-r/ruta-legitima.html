Ruta legitima

Ruta legitima, commonly known as common rue, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and Western Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, compound leaves and small yellowish flowers. The plant contains volatile oils, alkaloids, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Its historical use dates back to ancient times, where it was valued for its purported healing properties.
