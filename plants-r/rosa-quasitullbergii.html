Rosa quasitullbergii

Rosa quasitullbergii, commonly known as the Tullberg rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Russia. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance.

Rosa quasitullbergii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
