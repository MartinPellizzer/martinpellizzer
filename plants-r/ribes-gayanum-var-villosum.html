Ribes gayanum var. villosum

Ribes gayanum var. villosum, commonly known as the hairy gooseberry, is a variant of the gooseberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
