Rubus corylifolius var. cordatus

Rubus corylifolius var. cordatus, commonly known as the heart-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful for its edible fruits.
