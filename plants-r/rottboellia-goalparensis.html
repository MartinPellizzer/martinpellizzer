Rottboellia goalparensis

Rottboellia goalparensis, commonly known as the African grass or Indian ricegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas, grasslands, and disturbed sites. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Rottboellia goalparensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in erosion control.
