Rebutia pulchella

Rebutia pulchella, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Moon," is a species of flowering cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Rebutia pulchella is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
