Rubus atrovirens

Rubus atrovirens, commonly known as the blackberry cane or blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble native to various regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. Its botanical features include a woody structure and a preference for temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Rubus atrovirens is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
