Rhytiglossa piauhiensis

Rhytiglossa piauhiensis, commonly known as the Piauhi orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it has a short rhizome.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of interest for botanical research and conservation efforts.
