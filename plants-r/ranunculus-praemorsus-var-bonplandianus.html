Ranunculus praemorsus var. bonplandianus

Ranunculus praemorsus var. bonplandianus, commonly known as the Bonplandian crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often found in regions with cool, temperate climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
