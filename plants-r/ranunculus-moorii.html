Ranunculus moorii

Ranunculus moorii, commonly known as the Moor's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has a short, hairy stem. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in spring.

Ranunculus moorii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
