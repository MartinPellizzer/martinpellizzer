Rubus tomentosus f. arvicola

Rubus tomentosus f. arvicola, commonly known as the alpine bramble, is a subspecies of the common blackberry.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Its hairy stems and leaves are adaptations to cold, harsh climates.
