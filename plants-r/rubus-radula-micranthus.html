Rubus radula micranthus

Rubus radula micranthus, commonly known as the alpine bramble, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pink. The plant has thorny stems and is native to mountainous regions in Europe and western Asia. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.
