Ranunculus vindobonensis

Ranunculus vindobonensis, commonly known as the Austrian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Ranunculus vindobonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
