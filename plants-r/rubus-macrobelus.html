Rubus macrobelus

Rubus macrobelus, commonly known as the giant blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its large, thorny stems and broad, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces showy, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by large, dark purple berries in the summer. These berries are edible and have a tart, sweet flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
