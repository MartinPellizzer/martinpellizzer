Rhododendron eudoxum subsp. brunneifolium

Rhododendron eudoxum subsp.

brunneifolium, commonly known as the brown-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically reddish-brown to brown in color.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
