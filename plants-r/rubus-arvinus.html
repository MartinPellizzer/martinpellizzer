Rubus arvinus

Rubus arvinus, commonly known as the red raspberry or arvin raspberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny environments.

Rubus arvinus is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forest edges.
