Rosa × mutsuana

Rosa × mutsuana, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a hybrid species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Rosa × mutsuana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
