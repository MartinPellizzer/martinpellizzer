Rubus hirtus var. rubiginosus

Rubus hirtus var. rubiginosus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically red when ripe. These berries are rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
