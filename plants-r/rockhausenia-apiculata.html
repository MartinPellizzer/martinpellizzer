Rockhausenia apiculata

Rockhausenia apiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a hardy species in harsh climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
