Rubus concameratus

Rubus concameratus, commonly known as the prairie raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and the fruit, a red drupe, is edible but often tart.

It plays a role in supporting local wildlife and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
