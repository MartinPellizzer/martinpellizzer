Rosa phoenicia

Rosa phoenicia, commonly known as the Syrian rose, is a species native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by oval, red-brown hips. Its leaves are alternate and have serrated edges, while its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Rosa phoenicia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for centuries for its beauty and fragrance.
