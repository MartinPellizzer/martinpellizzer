Ranunculus labacensis

Ranunculus labacensis, commonly known as the Labacense buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, delicate flowers that typically feature five to eight petals. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands, and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
