Reissantia arborea

Reissantia arborea, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The tree produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elongated, woody pods. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
