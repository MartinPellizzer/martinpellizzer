Rubus thyrsoideus var. macrophyllus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
