Rubia filiformis

Rubia filiformis, commonly known as slender woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many flowering plants known for their ornamental and medicinal value. The plant features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its small, fragrant white flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
