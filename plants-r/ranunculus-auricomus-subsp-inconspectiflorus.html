Ranunculus auricomus subsp. inconspectiflorus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

inconspectiflorus, commonly known as the yellow pond lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals that bloom from late spring to early summer.

The plant has heart-shaped, floating leaves that are typically green and have a waxy surface. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in shallow water bodies.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
