Rosa pimpinellifolia var. spinosa

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. spinosa, commonly known as the bristly rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing on rocky slopes or cliffs.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
