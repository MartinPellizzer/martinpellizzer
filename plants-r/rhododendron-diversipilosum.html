Rhododendron diversipilosum

Rhododendron diversipilosum, commonly known as the hairy-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive pubescent surface, contributing to its unique appearance.

This rhododendron is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
