Racosperma curranii

Racosperma curranii, commonly known as the Curran's acacia, is a small, deciduous tree native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that appear in cylindrical spikes. The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers. It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for local wildlife.

The wood of Racosperma curranii is hard and durable, making it valuable for traditional and contemporary uses.
