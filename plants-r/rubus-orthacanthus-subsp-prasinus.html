Rubus orthacanthus subsp. prasinus

Rubus orthacanthus subsp.

prasinus, commonly known as the green-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into dark purple to black berries. Its fruits are typically smaller and less sweet compared to other blackberry varieties.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in woodland and thicket habitats.
