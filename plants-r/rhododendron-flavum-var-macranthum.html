Rhododendron flavum var. macranthum

Rhododendron flavum var. macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered yellow rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
