Ribes alpinum var. pilosum

Ribes alpinum var. pilosum, commonly known as the downy gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
