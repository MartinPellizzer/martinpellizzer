Rubus viscosus

Rubus viscosus, commonly known as the sticky raspberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, prickly bracts that surround its flowers. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are usually compound, with three to five leaflets, and are covered in a fine, sticky pubescence.

Rubus viscosus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
