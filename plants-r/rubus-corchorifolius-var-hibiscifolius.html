Rubus corchorifolius var. hibiscifolius

Rubus corchorifolius var. hibiscifolius, commonly known as the Hibiscifolius raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of hibiscus.

It is native to eastern Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
