Rubus candicans f. rosacea

Rubus candicans f. rosacea, commonly known as the pink-fruited raspberry, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year producing leaves and the second year bearing flowers and fruits. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruits are aggregate berries that range in color from red to pink.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and edible qualities.
