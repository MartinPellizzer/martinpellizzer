Rubus sanguineicaulis

Rubus sanguineicaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, particularly in California and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some traditional herbal remedies.
