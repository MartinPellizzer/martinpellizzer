Ritterocactus uebelmannianus

Ritterocactus uebelmannianus, commonly known as the Uebelmann's cactus, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family native to the Namib Desert in Namibia.

This plant is characterized by its tall, columnar stem that can grow up to 3 meters in height, covered with dense, white to yellowish spines. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or orange, appearing in late winter to early spring. The cactus is adapted to extreme arid conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly vulnerable to environmental changes and over-collection.
