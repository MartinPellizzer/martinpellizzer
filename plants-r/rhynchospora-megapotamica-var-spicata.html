Rhynchospora megapotamica var. spicata

Rhynchospora megapotamica var. spicata, commonly known as the spicate marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and seasonally flooded areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. Its botanical structure is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, with rhizomes that help in stabilizing the soil and facilitating vegetative propagation.

This species plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
