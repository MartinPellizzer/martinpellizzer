Rhododendron murudense

Rhododendron murudense, commonly known as the Murudense rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with the right growing conditions.
