Racemobambos yunnanensis

Racemobambos yunnanensis, commonly known as Yunnan bamboo, is a unique species native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit and produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it is adapted to thrive in cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, Racemobambos yunnanensis is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
