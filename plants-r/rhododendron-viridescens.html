Rhododendron viridescens

Rhododendron viridescens, commonly known as the greenleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that ranges from dark green to a bluish-green hue. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
