Rhodophiala gladioloides

Rhodophiala gladioloides, commonly known as the gladioloid orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that resemble those of the gladiolus. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that enhances its ornamental appeal.

Rhodophiala gladioloides is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
