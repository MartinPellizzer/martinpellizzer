Rhododendron thomsonii var. lopsangianum

Rhododendron thomsonii var. lopsangianum, commonly known as the Lhasa rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of Tibet and Bhutan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, with a leathery texture that helps it withstand cold and harsh weather conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
