Rhododendron cantabile

Rhododendron cantabile, commonly known as the Italian rhododendron, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are dark green and often have a leathery texture. Its flowers are showy, ranging from pink to red, and are borne in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Rhododendron cantabile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
