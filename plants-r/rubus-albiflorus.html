Rubus albiflorus

Rubus albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of North America and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
