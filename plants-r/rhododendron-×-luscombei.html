Rhododendron × luscombei

Rhododendron × luscombei, commonly known as the Luscombe rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron catawbiense and Rhododendron maximum.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
