Rosa glutinosa f. minor

Rosa glutinosa f. minor, commonly known as the sticky rose or the dwarf sticky rose, is a compact, thorny shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often covered in a sticky, resinous substance. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges and has a distinctive, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
