Rosa pecosensis

Rosa pecosensis, commonly known as Pecos prickly pear, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces edible fruits known as "tunas," which are often used in local cuisines. Rosa pecosensis thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
