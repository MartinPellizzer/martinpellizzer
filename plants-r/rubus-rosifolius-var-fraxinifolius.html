Rubus rosifolius var. fraxinifolius

Rubus rosifolius var. fraxinifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, silvery-hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by red, edible berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
