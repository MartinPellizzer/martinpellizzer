Rhadamanthus urgineoides

Rhadamanthus urgineoides, commonly known as the yellow spider orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns resembling spider webs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open woodlands or grasslands. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
