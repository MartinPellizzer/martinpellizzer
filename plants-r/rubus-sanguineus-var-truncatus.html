Rubus sanguineus var. truncatus

Rubus sanguineus var. truncatus, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of red, edible fruits. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fruit, which is rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

The plant's botanical features make it both useful in horticulture and valuable for ecological studies.
