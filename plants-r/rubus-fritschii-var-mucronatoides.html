Rubus fritschii var. mucronatoides

Rubus fritschii var. mucronatoides, commonly known as the narrow-tipped blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus native to specific regions of China.

It is characterized by its small, dark berries and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the Rubus genus.
