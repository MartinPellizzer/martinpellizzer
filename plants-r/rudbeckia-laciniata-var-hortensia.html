Rudbeckia laciniata var. hortensia

Rudbeckia laciniata var. hortensia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a tall, herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features large, showy yellow to orange flower heads with a distinctive purple or brown central disk. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Rudbeckia laciniata var. hortensia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
