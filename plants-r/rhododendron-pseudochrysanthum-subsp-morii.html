Rhododendron pseudochrysanthum subsp. morii

Rhododendron pseudochrysanthum subsp.

morii, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a species native to Japan and parts of Korea. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring acidic, well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

The subspecies morii is distinguished by its slightly different flower morphology and habitat preferences compared to the nominate form.
