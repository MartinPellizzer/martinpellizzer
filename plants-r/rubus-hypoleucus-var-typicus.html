Rubus hypoleucus var. typicus

Rubus hypoleucus var. typicus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it has a reputation for being somewhat invasive in certain areas.
