Rosa bracteata

Rosa bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of soft, grayish-white hair.

Rosa bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
