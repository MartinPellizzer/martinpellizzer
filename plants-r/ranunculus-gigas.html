Ranunculus gigas

Ranunculus gigas, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with deeply lobed petals and a distinctive central pistil. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with smooth, dark green leaves that are palmately divided. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
