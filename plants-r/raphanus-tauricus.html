Raphanus tauricus

Raphanus tauricus, commonly known as the Caucasian watercress, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cabbage. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small white or pink flowers. Its seeds are edible and have a peppery taste similar to that of horseradish.

Raphanus tauricus is sometimes cultivated for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
