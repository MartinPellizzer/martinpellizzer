Rubus milliformis var. connectans

Rubus milliformis var. connectans, commonly known as the clustered blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and its ability to form thickets. The plant produces small, dark red to black fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five leaflets, and the plant is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex inflorescence and its role in local ecosystems as a food source for wildlife.
