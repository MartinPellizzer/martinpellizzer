Rubus sibiricus

Rubus sibiricus, commonly known as the Siberian raspberry, is a shrub native to the cold climates of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subarctic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
