Ruellia furcata

Ruellia furcata, commonly known as the Florida rainflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and moist soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue or purple tubular flowers. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is often found in swampy areas.
