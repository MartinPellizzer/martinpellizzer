Rauvolfia verticillata var. hainanensis

Rauvolfia verticillata var. hainanensis, commonly known as Hainan snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

This species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its hypotensive effects.
