Rubus hirtus var. suboreades

Rubus hirtus var. suboreades, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to North America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and is covered in fine, stiff hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor and are used in jams and pies.
