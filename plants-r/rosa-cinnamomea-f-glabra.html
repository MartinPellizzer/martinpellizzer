Rosa cinnamomea f. glabra

Rosa cinnamomea f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth cinnamon rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa cinnamomea species, which is native to China.

This plant is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and foliage, distinguishing it from other cultivars with hairy stems. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with a fragrant aroma, and appear in clusters. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and seasonal blooming.
