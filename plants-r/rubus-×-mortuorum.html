Rubus × mortuorum

Rubus × mortuorum, commonly known as the deathberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The species is notable for its ornamental value, though caution is advised due to the toxicity of its fruits.
