Rubus ulmifolius var. aggericolus

Rubus ulmifolius var. aggericolus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry species.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
