Rhodomyrtus paitensis

Rhodomyrtus paitensis, commonly known as the Chinese rose or purple mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically purple or reddish in color, with a segmented, bumpy exterior. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
