Rhynchospora planifolia

Rhynchospora planifolia, commonly known as the flatleaf spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant features slender, grass-like stems with small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are flat and linear, often arranged in dense clusters at the base.

Rhynchospora planifolia is adapted to moist soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
