Rubus hemithelybatos

Rubus hemithelybatos, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, though it is less commonly cultivated than other blackberry varieties.
