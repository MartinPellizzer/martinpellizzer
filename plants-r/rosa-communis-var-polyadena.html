Rosa communis var. polyadena

Rosa communis var. polyadena, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a thorny shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnate leaves with five to seven leaflets and produces red-orange hips in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in hedgerows and gardens.

The variety is noted for its strong, pleasant fragrance and hardy nature.
