Rapuntium chamaedryfolium

Rapuntium chamaedryfolium, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of bluish-gray, hairy leaves and produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in dry, rocky soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in herbal medicine for their mild sedative properties.
