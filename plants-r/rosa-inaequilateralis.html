Rosa inaequilateralis

Rosa inaequilateralis, commonly known as the unequal-sided rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia, particularly in countries like China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, asymmetrical petals that give it its scientific name. It produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a velvety texture.

Rosa inaequilateralis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
