Rubus moluccanus var. himalaicus

Rubus moluccanus var. himalaicus, commonly known as the Himalayan raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are usually white or pink.

The berries, which ripen in summer, are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
