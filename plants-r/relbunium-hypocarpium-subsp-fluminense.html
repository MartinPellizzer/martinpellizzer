Relbunium hypocarpium subsp. fluminense

Relbunium hypocarpium subsp.

fluminense, commonly known as the Brazilian bladderwort, is a rare aquatic plant native to the freshwater habitats of South America. It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, aiding in its nutrient acquisition.

The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving waters, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its leaves are usually thin and ribbon-like, adapted for efficient water movement and light absorption.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
