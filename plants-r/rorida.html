Rorida

Rorida, commonly known as the mist flower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Eupatorium and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Rorida is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
