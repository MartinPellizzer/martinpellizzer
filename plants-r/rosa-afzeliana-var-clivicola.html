Rosa afzeliana var. clivicola

Rosa afzeliana var. clivicola, commonly known as the cliff rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a serrated edge. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a landscape accent.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice among horticulturists.
