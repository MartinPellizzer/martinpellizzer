Rauvolfia obtusiflora

Rauvolfia obtusiflora, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its woody, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elliptical, dark blue or black berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests.

Rauvolfia obtusiflora is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of bioactive compounds.
