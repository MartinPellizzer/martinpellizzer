Raphanus raphanistrum var. landra

Raphanus raphanistrum var. landra, commonly known as wild radish, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a taproot system with a fleshy, often swollen root that can be white, purple, or yellow in color. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a peppery taste when crushed.

This species is commonly found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
