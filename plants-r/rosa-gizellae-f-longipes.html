Rosa gizellae f. longipes

Rosa gizellae f. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed garden rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa gizellae species.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and is typically found in horticultural collections rather than the wild. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to the blooms.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
