Rosa abutalybovii

Rosa abutalybovii, commonly known as the Abutalybov's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and a strong, pleasant fragrance.

Rosa abutalybovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
