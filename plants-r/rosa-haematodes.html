Rosa haematodes

Rosa haematodes, commonly known as the blood rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark red to almost black petals, which give it its striking appearance. The plant produces fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically dark green and serrated, while its stems are covered in thorns.

Native to parts of Europe and western Asia, Rosa haematodes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance.
