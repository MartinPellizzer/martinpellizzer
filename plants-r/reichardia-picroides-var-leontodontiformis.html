Reichardia picroides var. leontodontiformis

Reichardia picroides var. leontodontiformis, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and disturbed soils. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, with a distinctive yellow-orange flower head that blooms in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of composite flowers, including both ray and disk flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is often found in regions with temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
