Rubus sinusifolius

Rubus sinusifolius, commonly known as the sinuate raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated, sinuate margins. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, red drupes that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
