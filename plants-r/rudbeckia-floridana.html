Rudbeckia floridana

Rudbeckia floridana, commonly known as the Florida coneflower, is a native perennial plant of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange disk flowers surrounded by prominent, greenish to reddish brown ray flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers moist, well-drained soils in full sun. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Rudbeckia floridana is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds in Florida.
