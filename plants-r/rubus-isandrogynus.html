Rubus isandrogynus

Rubus isandrogynus, commonly known as the Andes raspberry, is a species of shrub native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its botanical features include palmate leaves and a system of runners for vegetative propagation.

It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
