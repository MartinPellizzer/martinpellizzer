Rosa micrantha var. calvescens

Rosa micrantha var. calvescens, commonly known as the California rose, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features upright, thornless stems and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces oval, hairy leaves with serrated edges, and its fruit is a red, hairy hip that contains several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
