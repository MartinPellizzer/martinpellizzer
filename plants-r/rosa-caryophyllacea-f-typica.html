Rosa caryophyllacea f. typica

Rosa caryophyllacea f. typica, commonly known as the typical form of the pink sand rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact shrub habit and produces showy, pink to reddish-pink flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant features dark green, serrated leaves and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
