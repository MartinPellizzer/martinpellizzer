Rubus capricornii

Rubus capricornii, commonly known as the capricorn blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.

Rubus capricornii is often used in local horticulture and traditional medicine.
