Rubus caflischii subsp. inaequalis

Rubus caflischii subsp.

inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant produces small, dark berries that are edible. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in local cuisine.
