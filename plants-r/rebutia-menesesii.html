Rebutia menesesii

Rebutia menesesii, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to Mexico.

It features a swollen, colorful caudex that resembles a moon, with thin, ribbed stems that are often covered in white or grayish spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
