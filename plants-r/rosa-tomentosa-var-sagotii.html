Rosa tomentosa var. sagotii

Rosa tomentosa var. sagotii, commonly known as the Philippine rose, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
