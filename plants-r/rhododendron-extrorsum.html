Rhododendron extrorsum

Rhododendron extrorsum, commonly known as the broadleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, moist, acidic soils, often in forested areas. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron extrorsum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
