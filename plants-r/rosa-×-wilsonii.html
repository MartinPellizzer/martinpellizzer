Rosa × wilsonii

Rosa × wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is native to China. The plant typically features fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the stems are covered in thorns.

Rosa × wilsonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
