Rhamnus sumatrensis

Rhamnus sumatrensis, commonly known as the Sumatran buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
