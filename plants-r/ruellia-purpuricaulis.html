Ruellia purpuricaulis

Ruellia purpuricaulis, commonly known as the purple-stemmed ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ruellia purpuricaulis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ability to tolerate a variety of environmental conditions contributes to its widespread cultivation.
