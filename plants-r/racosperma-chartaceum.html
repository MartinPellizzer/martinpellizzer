Racosperma chartaceum

Racosperma chartaceum, commonly known as the paperbark acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its papery, peeling bark that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a feathery appearance.

Racosperma chartaceum is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.
