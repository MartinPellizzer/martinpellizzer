Rosa tomentosa f. agrisensis

Rosa tomentosa f. agrisensis, commonly known as the agrisensian woolly rose, is a botanical variant of the woolly rose species.

It is characterized by its dense, white to grayish pubescence covering the stems and leaves. The plant typically produces fragrant, pink to red flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its unique appearance and hardiness.
