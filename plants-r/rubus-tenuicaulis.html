Rubus tenuicaulis

Rubus tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and is notable for its horticultural potential in certain regions.
