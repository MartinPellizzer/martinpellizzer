Rubus × erythrocladus

Rubus × erythrocladus, commonly known as the red-leaf blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its distinctive red-tinged foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in temperate climates, producing thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant bears small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are usually dark purple to black in color when mature. These berries are often cultivated for their flavor and used in culinary applications.

Botanically, Rubus × erythrocladus exhibits traits from its parent species, making it a subject of interest in horticultural research.
