Rubus vestitus subsp. insericatus

Rubus vestitus subsp.

insericatus, commonly known as the woolly raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
