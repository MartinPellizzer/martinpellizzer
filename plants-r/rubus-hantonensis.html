Rubus hantonensis

Rubus hantonensis, commonly known as Hanton's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
