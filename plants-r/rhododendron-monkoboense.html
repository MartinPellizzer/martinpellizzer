Rhododendron monkoboense

Rhododendron monkoboense, commonly known as the Eastern African rhododendron, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as montane forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive elliptical shape, while its flowers bloom during the cooler months.

Rhododendron monkoboense is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
