Ranunculus inaequalidens

Ranunculus inaequalidens, commonly known as the unequalleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often deeply lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
