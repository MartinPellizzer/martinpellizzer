Rubus Corylifolius Var. Nemorosus

Rubus corylifolius var. nemorosus is a lesser-known species of bramble that thrives in shaded woodland environments. This variant is distinguished by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, edible berries. While not as commercially cultivated as other raspberry varieties, it holds significant ecological value. Its presence supports local wildlife and contributes to biodiversity in forest ecosystems. Understanding this plant helps highlight the importance of preserving native flora.
