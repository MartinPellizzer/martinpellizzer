Rubus veitchii

Rubus veitchii, commonly known as Veitch's raspberry, is a species of brambles native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

The fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a potential candidate for cultivation.
