Rhynchosia zernyi

Rhynchosia zernyi, commonly known as the Zernyi burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically palmately arranged. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny pods. Rhynchosia zernyi is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
