Rubus rubellus

Rubus rubellus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red, edible berries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are pinkish-white.

The berries are often used in culinary applications and are rich in antioxidants.
