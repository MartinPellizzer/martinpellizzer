Racinaea macrantha

Racinaea macrantha, commonly known as the giant swamp lily, is a striking tropical plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, often reaching up to 10 inches in diameter. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in areas with standing water.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
