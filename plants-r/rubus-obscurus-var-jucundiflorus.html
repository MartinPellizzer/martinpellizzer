Rubus obscurus var. jucundiflorus

Rubus obscurus var. jucundiflorus, commonly known as the sweetflower blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces small, dark berries that are often used in local culinary traditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a slightly serrated edge, and the stems are covered in fine, prickly hairs.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique floral structure and its role in supporting local wildlife.
