Robinia boyntonii

Robinia boyntonii, commonly known as the Boynton's acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion. It is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Its unique appearance and ecological benefits make it a valuable species in native landscaping and restoration projects.
