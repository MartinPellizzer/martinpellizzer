Rosa sicula subvar. thuretii

Rosa sicula subvar. thuretii, commonly known as the Sicilian rose or thuret's rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and the plant produces red, hairy fruits known as hips. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and historical significance in horticulture.
