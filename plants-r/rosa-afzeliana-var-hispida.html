Rosa afzeliana var. hispida

Rosa afzeliana var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid rose, is a deciduous shrub native to regions of China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving the plant a distinctive textured appearance. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils in full sun.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
