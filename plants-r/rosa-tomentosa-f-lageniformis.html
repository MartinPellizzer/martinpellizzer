Rosa tomentosa f. lageniformis

Rosa tomentosa f. lageniformis, commonly known as the woolly rose or the bottle-shaped rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa tomentosa species.

It is characterized by its dense, white to grayish pubescence covering the stems and leaves, giving it a fluffy appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically compound, with five to seven leaflets that are ovate to elliptic in shape.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to temperate climates.
