Rubus parthenocissus

Rubus parthenocissus, commonly known as the Virginia creeper, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that turn bright red in the fall, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It has a vigorous growth habit and can be found along woodland edges and in disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it both an ornamental and ecologically important species.
