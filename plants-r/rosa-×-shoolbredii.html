Rosa × shoolbredii

Rosa × shoolbredii, commonly known as the Shoolbred's rose, is a hybrid rose species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa bracteata.

It is characterized by its vibrant, semi-double to fully double flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant is known for its robust growth, with thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.
