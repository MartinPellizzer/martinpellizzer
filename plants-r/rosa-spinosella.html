Rosa spinosella

Rosa spinosella, commonly known as the spiny rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or dry habitats. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it produces red or orange hips in the fall.

Rosa spinosella is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
