Rosa submollis

Rosa submollis, commonly known as the soft-hairy rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa submollis is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and scent.
