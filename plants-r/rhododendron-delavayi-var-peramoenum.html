Rhododendron delavayi var. peramoenum

Rhododendron delavayi var. peramoenum, commonly known as the Delavay's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. It is often found in forest understories at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
