Rhynchospora hemicephala

Rhynchospora hemicephala, commonly known as the two-headed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is native to parts of North America. Its inflorescence consists of two distinct clusters of spikelets, giving it the common name "two-headed sedge.".

Rhynchospora hemicephala plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small invertebrates.
