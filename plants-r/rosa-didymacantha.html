Rosa didymacantha

Rosa didymacantha, commonly known as the double-flowered rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically have multiple layers of petals, giving them a full, lush appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly serrated edge.

Rosa didymacantha is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
