Rapunculus persicifolius

Rapunculus persicifolius, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves and its clusters of yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
