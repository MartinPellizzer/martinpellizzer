Ramischia secunda f. serrata

Ramischia secunda f. serrata, commonly known as the serrated ramischia, is a botanical variant of the species Ramischia secunda.

It is characterized by its serrated leaf margins, which distinguish it from the typical form of the plant. This fern-like plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest understories. Its fronds are usually lance-shaped and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
