Rubus glomicellus

Rubus glomicellus, commonly known as the small-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and thickets, preferring partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries, though small, are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and culinary applications.
