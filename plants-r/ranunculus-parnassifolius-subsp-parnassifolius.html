Ranunculus parnassifolius subsp. parnassifolius

Ranunculus parnassifolius subsp.

parnassifolius, commonly known as the Parnassian buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with numerous stamens.

Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant has a short rhizome and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
