Rubia olivieri var. cretica

Rubia olivieri var. cretica, commonly known as Cretan woodruff, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Crete.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a mild, aromatic scent when crushed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
