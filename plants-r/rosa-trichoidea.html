Rosa trichoidea

Rosa trichoidea, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a species of shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are typically borne singly or in clusters. Rosa trichoidea is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
