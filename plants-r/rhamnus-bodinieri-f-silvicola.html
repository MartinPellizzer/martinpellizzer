Rhamnus bodinieri f. silvicola

Rhamnus bodinieri f. silvicola, commonly known as the Chinese buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by black, spherical berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
