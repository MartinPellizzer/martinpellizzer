Rhexia multiflora

Rhexia multiflora, commonly known as the pinkroot or swamp pink, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in wetlands, swamps, and pine flatwoods. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

The plant's name comes from the pink-colored roots, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
