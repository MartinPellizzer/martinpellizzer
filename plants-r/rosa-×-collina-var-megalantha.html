Rosa × collina var. megalantha

Rosa × collina var. megalantha, commonly known as the giant alpine rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa × collina var. megalantha is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky, alpine environments.
