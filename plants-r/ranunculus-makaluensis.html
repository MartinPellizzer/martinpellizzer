Ranunculus makaluensis

Ranunculus makaluensis, commonly known as the Makalu buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and its flowers usually have five to eight petals.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
