Rosa heckeliana f. atrichoclada

Rosa heckeliana f. atrichoclada, commonly known as the velvety rose, is a hybrid form of the Rosa heckeliana species.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it a unique texture compared to other roses. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a compact shrub with thornless stems, making it suitable for small garden spaces.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
