Ruellia australis var. pumila

Ruellia australis var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf ruellia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable habitats.
