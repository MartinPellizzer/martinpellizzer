Ranunculus ruscinonensis var. viridescens

Ranunculus ruscinonensis var. viridescens, commonly known as the greenish meadow buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist meadows and grassy areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It is also characterized by its slightly hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.
