Rottboellia compressa var. hamiltoniana

Rottboellia compressa var. hamiltoniana, commonly known as Hamilton's panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or disturbed areas.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
