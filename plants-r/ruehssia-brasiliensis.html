Ruehssia brasiliensis

Ruehssia brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian rue, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 12,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow in color. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Ruehssia brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
