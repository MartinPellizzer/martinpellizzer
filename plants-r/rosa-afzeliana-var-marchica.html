Rosa afzeliana var. marchica

Rosa afzeliana var. marchica, commonly known as the Marchica rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand colder climates.
