Ruellia pterocaulon

Ruellia pterocaulon, commonly known as the silverleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

Ruellia pterocaulon is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
