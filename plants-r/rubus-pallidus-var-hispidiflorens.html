Rubus pallidus var. hispidiflorens

Rubus pallidus var. hispidiflorens, commonly known as the hairy-flowered bramble, is a shrub native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart in flavor.

It thrives in woodland edges and moist, well-drained soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
