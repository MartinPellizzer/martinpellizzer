Rhododendron × mizumotoi

Rhododendron × mizumotoi, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is part of the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also notable for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its use in landscape design.
