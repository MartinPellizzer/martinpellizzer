Rabdosia angustifolia var. glabrescens

Rabdosia angustifolia var. glabrescens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rabdosia, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy when young but become smooth as the plant matures. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a pointed tip and a smooth or slightly toothed margin. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the branches.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and stream sides.
