Ranunculus brotherusii var. dasycarpus

Ranunculus brotherusii var. dasycarpus, commonly known as the hairy-fruited buttercup, is a perennial herb native to parts of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its hairy, fleshy fruits are distinctive and aid in seed dispersal.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
