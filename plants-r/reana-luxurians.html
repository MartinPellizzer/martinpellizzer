Reana luxurians

Reana luxurians, commonly known as the golden spire plant, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 10 feet in height, adorned with broad, lance-shaped leaves that have a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright orange berries. Native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
