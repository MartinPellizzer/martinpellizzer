Rubus haitiensis

Rubus haitiensis, commonly known as Haitian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
