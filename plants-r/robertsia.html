Robertsia

Robertsia, commonly known as the "bluebells of the prairie," is a genus of flowering plants native to North America.

It consists of several species, most notably Robertsia grandiflora, which is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plants are typically herbaceous perennials, growing from rhizomes and producing showy blooms in late spring to early summer. Robertsia thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
