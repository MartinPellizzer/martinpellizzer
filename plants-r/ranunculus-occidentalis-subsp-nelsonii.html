Ranunculus occidentalis subsp. nelsonii

Ranunculus occidentalis subsp.

nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

The plant features palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It has a short, stout rhizome and produces numerous small, hairy seeds.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinct floral morphology and preference for specific ecological conditions.
