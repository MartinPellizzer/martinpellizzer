Rubus infrarugosus

Rubus infrarugosus, commonly known as the roughleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a rough, serrated texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in woodland edges and moist, well-drained soils.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species, sharing similar floral and fruit structures.
