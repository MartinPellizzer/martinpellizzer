Rhynchospora submarginata var. tonkinensis

Rhynchospora submarginata var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization in its habitat.

The species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
