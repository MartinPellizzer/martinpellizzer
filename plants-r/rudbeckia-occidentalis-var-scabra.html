Rudbeckia occidentalis var. scabra

Rudbeckia occidentalis var. scabra, commonly known as the rough rudbeckia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, bright yellow composite flower heads with dark centers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Rudbeckia occidentalis var. scabra is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
