Ranunculus brotherusii subsp. latisectus

Ranunculus brotherusii subsp.

latisectus, commonly known as the broad-leaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features delicate, palmate leaves with a distinctive broad, lobed structure, and produces showy yellow flowers with a central greenish-brown blotch.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. It has a short stem with a single, terminal flower that blooms in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Ranunculaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological role in its native habitats.
