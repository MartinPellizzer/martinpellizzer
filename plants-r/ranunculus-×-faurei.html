Ranunculus × faurei

Ranunculus × faurei, commonly known as the Faure's buttercup, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, usually double flowers that come in a range of colors including white, yellow, pink, and red. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and has a fibrous root system. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely grown in gardens and as a cut flower.
