Rubus taeniarum f. umbrosus

Rubus taeniarum f. umbrosus, commonly known as the shadowy blackberry, is a botanical variant of the blackberry plant.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that develop in shaded environments. The plant typically grows in moist, forested areas where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are usually deeply lobed and have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its unique flavor and is often found in temperate regions with cool, humid climates.
