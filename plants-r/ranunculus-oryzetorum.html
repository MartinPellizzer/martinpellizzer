Ranunculus oryzetorum

Ranunculus oryzetorum, commonly known as the ricefield buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive central boss. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near rice paddies. It has a short stem and produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Ranunculus oryzetorum is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
