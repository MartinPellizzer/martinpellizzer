Rhododendron amaurophyllum

Rhododendron amaurophyllum, commonly known as the blackleaf rhododendron, is a rare and striking evergreen shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, elliptical leaves and clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in cool, moist, shaded environments at high elevations. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it produces small, leathery berries that contain seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
