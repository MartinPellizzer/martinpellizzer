Rosa enelliptica

Rosa enelliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. It typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Rosa enelliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
