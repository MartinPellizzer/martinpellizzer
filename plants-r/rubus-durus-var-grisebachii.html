Rubus durus var. grisebachii

Rubus durus var. grisebachii, commonly known as the gray-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that have a slightly grayish hue when mature. The plant typically grows in shrub form, with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces edible fruits that are often used in jams and desserts.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
