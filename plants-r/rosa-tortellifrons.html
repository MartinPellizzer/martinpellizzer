Rosa tortellifrons

Rosa tortellifrons, commonly known as the tortellifrons rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Guatemala and Mexico. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems, and the plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as rose hips.

Rosa tortellifrons is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
