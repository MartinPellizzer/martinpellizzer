Ranunculus metrius

Ranunculus metrius, commonly known as the small-flowered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Ranunculus metrius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
