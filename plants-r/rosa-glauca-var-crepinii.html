Rosa glauca var. crepinii

Rosa glauca var. crepinii, commonly known as the Crepin's blue rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pale blue to lavender-colored flowers, which are often described as having a delicate, almost ethereal appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. Its flowers bloom in early summer and are known for their subtle fragrance.

Rosa glauca var. crepinii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
