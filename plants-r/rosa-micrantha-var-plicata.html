Rosa micrantha var. plicata

Rosa micrantha var. plicata, commonly known as the wrinkled rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive, wrinkled or plicated appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
