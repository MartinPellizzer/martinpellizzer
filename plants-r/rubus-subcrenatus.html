Rubus subcrenatus

Rubus subcrenatus, commonly known as the serrated blackberry.

It is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, which includes many other brambles and berries.
