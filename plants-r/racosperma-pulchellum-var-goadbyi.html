Racosperma pulchellum var. goadbyi

Racosperma pulchellum var. goadbyi, commonly known as the Goadby's wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring compound leaves with multiple small leaflets. Its bright yellow flowers appear in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
