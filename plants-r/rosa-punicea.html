Rosa punicea

Rosa punicea, commonly known as the pink rose, is a species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are usually solitary and have a strong, sweet fragrance.

Rosa punicea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.
