Rhipsalis loefgrenii

Rhipsalis loefgrenii, commonly known as the Hawaiian mistletoe cactus, is a unique epiphytic plant native to Hawaii.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in short, soft spines. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to grow on trees and rocks.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
