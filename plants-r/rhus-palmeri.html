Rhus palmeri

Rhus palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like poison ivy and mango. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its compound leaves are alternate and have serrated edges, while its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters.
