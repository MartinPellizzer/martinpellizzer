Rhynchosia memnonia

Rhynchosia memnonia, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often trifoliate, and its showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. Rhynchosia memnonia also has medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in agricultural systems.
