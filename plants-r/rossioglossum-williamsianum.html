Rossioglossum williamsianum

Rossioglossum williamsianum, commonly known as the Williams' orchid, is a rare and striking tropical orchid native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically display a mix of red, yellow, and orange hues. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
