Ribes rigens

Ribes rigens, commonly known as the red currant bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the genus Ribes in the family Grossulariaceae, which includes other currant and gooseberry species. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with dark green, oval-shaped leaves and small, clustered red berries that ripen in late summer. These berries are edible, tart, and often used in jams, jellies, and wines.

Ribes rigens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
