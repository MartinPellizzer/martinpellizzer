Rubus pruinosus subsp. inhorrens

Rubus pruinosus subsp.

inhorrens, commonly known as the glossy blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its prickly stems and dark, glossy berries that ripen in late summer.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its berries are edible, though some varieties may have a tart or slightly bitter flavor.
