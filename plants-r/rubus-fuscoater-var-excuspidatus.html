Rubus fuscoater var. excuspidatus

Rubus fuscoater var. excuspidatus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a variant of the Rubus genus characterized by its prickly stems and clustered inflorescences.

It produces small, dark red to black fruits that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. The plant is native to regions of North America and thrives in moist, wooded areas. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has a woody structure that allows it to persist through seasonal changes.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is an important part of local ecosystems.
