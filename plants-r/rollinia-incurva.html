Rollinia incurva

Rollinia incurva, commonly known as the curved rollinia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its distinctive, curved fruiting bodies. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a waxy texture, and the flowers are small, clustered, and fragrant.

The fruit, which is a type of berry, is edible and has a sweet, tropical flavor.
