Rubus erinicus

Rubus erinicus, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Rubus erinicus is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
