Ranunculus auricomus subsp. grandiflorus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

grandiflorus, commonly known as the golden shield or large-flowered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical name reflects its characteristic golden color and large, showy flowers.
