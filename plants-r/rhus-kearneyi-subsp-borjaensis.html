Rhus kearneyi subsp. borjaensis

Rhus kearneyi subsp.

borjaensis, commonly known as the Borjaena sumac, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with serrated leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in native plant communities.
