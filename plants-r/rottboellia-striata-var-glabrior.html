Rottboellia striata var. glabrior

Rottboellia striata var. glabrior, commonly known as the narrowleaf horse-tail, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields. Its botanical features include narrow, glabrous (smooth) leaves and small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
