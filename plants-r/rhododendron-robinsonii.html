Rhododendron robinsonii

Rhododendron robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to red.

Rhododendron robinsonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific humidity and light conditions.
