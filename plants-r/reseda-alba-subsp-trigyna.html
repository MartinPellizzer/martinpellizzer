Reseda alba subsp. trigyna

Reseda alba subsp.

trigyna, commonly known as white centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
