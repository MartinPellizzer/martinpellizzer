Rhynchosia latissima

Rhynchosia latissima, commonly known as the broadleaf snout bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines.

Rhynchosia latissima is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural use.
