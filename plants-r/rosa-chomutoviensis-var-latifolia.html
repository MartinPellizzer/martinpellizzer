Rosa chomutoviensis var. latifolia

Rosa chomutoviensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf Czech rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Czech Republic.

It is characterized by its large, broad, ovate leaves that are typically serrated along the edges. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
