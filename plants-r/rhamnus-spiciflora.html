Rhamnus spiciflora

Rhamnus spiciflora, commonly known as the spicate buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. The plant features small, oval leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are clustered in spicate inflorescences, and it produces small, smooth, black berries that are an important food source for birds.

Rhamnus spiciflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
