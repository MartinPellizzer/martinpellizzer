Rubus tetsunii

Rubus tetsunii, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor and are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
