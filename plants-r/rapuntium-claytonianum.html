Rapuntium claytonianum

Rapuntium claytonianum, commonly known as the Clayton's rapunculus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spherical, greenish-brown fruits. Its root system is fibrous and can be harvested for its edible tubers, which have a slightly bitter taste.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy environments.
