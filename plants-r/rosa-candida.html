Rosa candida

Rosa candida, commonly known as the white garden rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and glossy, dark green foliage. Rosa candida thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
