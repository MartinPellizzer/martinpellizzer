Rubus wahlbergii var. nemorosus

Rubus wahlbergii var. nemorosus, commonly known as the African bramble, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible berries in summer. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils, and is often found in moist environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important role in local ecosystems as a food source for wildlife.
