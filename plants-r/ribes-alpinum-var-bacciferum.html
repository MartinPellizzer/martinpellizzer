Ribes alpinum var. bacciferum

Ribes alpinum var. bacciferum, commonly known as the alpine gooseberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features upright, woody stems with alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow, containing several drupes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing at high altitudes.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
