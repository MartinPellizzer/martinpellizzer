Rubus arundelanus var. jeckylianus

Rubus arundelanus var. jeckylianus, commonly known as Jeckylian blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and tart. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
