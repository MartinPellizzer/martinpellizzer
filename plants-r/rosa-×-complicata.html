Rosa × complicata

Rosa × complicata, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, thornless stems and profuse clusters of fragrant, semi-double flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is native to China and has been cultivated for centuries for its ornamental value. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of five to seven oval leaflets with serrated edges.

Rosa × complicata is relatively disease-resistant and thrives in full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
