Ranunculus membranaster

Ranunculus membranaster, commonly known as the membranous buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, and its leaves are deeply lobed and hairy. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Ranunculus membranaster is often found in areas with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
