Rubus erythrostachys var. adenodontus

Rubus erythrostachys var. adenodontus, commonly known as the red-stemmed bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including China, Vietnam, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, white to pinkish flowers. It produces edible berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitats.
