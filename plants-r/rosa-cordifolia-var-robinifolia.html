Rosa cordifolia var. robinifolia

Rosa cordifolia var. robinifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf rose or Robin's rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features simple, ovate to cordate leaves that are often reddish-tinged, especially during the growing season. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
