Rubus lugdunensis

Rubus lugdunensis, commonly known as the French blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to western Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
