Rosa sepium f. virgultorum

Rosa sepium f. virgultorum, commonly known as the virgultum rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa sepium species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, thornless stems. The plant produces showy, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing an attractive contrast to the flowers.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
