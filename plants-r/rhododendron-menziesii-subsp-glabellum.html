Rhododendron menziesii subsp. glabellum

Rhododendron menziesii subsp.

glabellum, commonly known as the Pacific silver fir rhododendron, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States and parts of Canada. It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in forested areas, often at high elevations. Its subspecies status reflects subtle differences in morphology compared to other members of the species.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
