Rhodanthe chlorocephala subsp. rosea

Rhodanthe chlorocephala subsp.

rosea, commonly known as the pink swamp wallaby, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate water conditions.
