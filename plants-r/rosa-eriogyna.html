Rosa eriogyna

Rosa eriogyna, commonly known as the narrowleaf rose, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats. The plant features slender, arching stems with small, dark green leaves that have serrated edges. Its flowers are pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rosa eriogyna is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
