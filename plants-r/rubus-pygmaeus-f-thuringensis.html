Rubus pygmaeus f. thuringensis

Rubus pygmaeus f. thuringensis, commonly known as the Thuringian dwarf raspberry, is a compact shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, trailing stems and dense clusters of tiny, red-orange berries. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and edible fruits.
