Ranunculus degenii var. emianus

Ranunculus degenii var. emianus, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and small, bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically reaches a height of no more than 10 centimeters, making it a compact and ornamental species. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is noted for its resilience and ability to bloom reliably in cooler climates.
