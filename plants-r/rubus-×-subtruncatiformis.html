Rubus × subtruncatiformis

Rubus × subtruncatiformis, commonly known as the blackberry-loganberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus loganobaccus (loganberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are a cross between the two parent species.

This hybrid is valued for its fruit quality and is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
