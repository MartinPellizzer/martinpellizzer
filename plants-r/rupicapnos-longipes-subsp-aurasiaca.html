Rupicapnos longipes subsp. aurasiaca

Rupicapnos longipes subsp.

aurasiaca, commonly known as the Aurasiaca firecracker, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
