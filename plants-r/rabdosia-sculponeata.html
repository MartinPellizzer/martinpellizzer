Rabdosia sculponeata

Rabdosia sculponeata, commonly known as the Chinese skullcap, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, producing a distinctive blue or purple color.
