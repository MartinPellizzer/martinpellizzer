Rubus hirtus var. pectinatus

Rubus hirtus var. pectinatus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, prickly foliage. The leaves are typically serrated with a hairy texture, and the flowers are white with five petals. Its fruit, a dark purple to black berry, is edible and often used in jams and pies.

This plant thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
