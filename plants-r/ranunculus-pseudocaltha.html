Ranunculus pseudocaltha

Ranunculus pseudocaltha, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and has a short growing season. It has palmate, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
