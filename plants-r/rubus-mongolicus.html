Rubus mongolicus

Rubus mongolicus, commonly known as Mongolian blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the cold climates of East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often dark purple or black in color. It thrives in rocky and mountainous regions, often growing in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
