Rosa elliptica var. borzae

Rosa elliptica var. borzae, commonly known as the Borzae rose, is a rare and unique cultivar of the Rosa elliptica species.

It is characterized by its elliptical-shaped leaves and compact, shrub-like growth habit. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom in late spring to early summer. This variety is native to specific regions in Central Asia and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered plant.
