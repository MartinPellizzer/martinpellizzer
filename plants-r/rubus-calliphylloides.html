Rubus calliphylloides

Rubus calliphylloides, commonly known as the purple-leaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of North America, particularly in the eastern United States. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a distinctive purple hue on the underside, which gives it its common name.

The fruit, a small aggregate berry, is edible and often used in culinary applications.
