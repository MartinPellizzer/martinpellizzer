Rosa uriensis var. grandifrons

Rosa uriensis var. grandifrons, commonly known as the large-flowered rock rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it has a compact, bushy growth habit.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
