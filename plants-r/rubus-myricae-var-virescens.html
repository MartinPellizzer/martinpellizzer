Rubus myricae var. virescens

Rubus myricae var. virescens, commonly known as the green-leafed blackberry shrub, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, dark berries that ripen in late summer. The leaves are typically a vibrant green, which distinguishes it from other Rubus species. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
