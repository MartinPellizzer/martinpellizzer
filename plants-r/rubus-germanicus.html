Rubus germanicus

Rubus germanicus, commonly known as the German blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Rubus germanicus is often found in woodlands and along hedgerows, contributing to local biodiversity.
