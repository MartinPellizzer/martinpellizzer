Rubus ulmifolius subsp. subtruncatus

Rubus ulmifolius subsp.

subtruncatus, commonly known as the creeping blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems that root at the nodes.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with the central leaflet being broader and more oval in shape. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in racemes, while the fruit is a dark purple to black aggregate berry.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
