Rhododendron × tectum

Rhododendron × tectum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. Its flowers, which range in color from white to deep pink, bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.

Rhododendron × tectum is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various climates.
