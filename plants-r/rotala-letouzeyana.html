Rotala letouzeyana

Rotala letouzeyana, commonly known as the golden thread plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that often float on the water surface, with small, rounded leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the summer months. Rotala letouzeyana is often used in aquariums due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for pond and aquarium landscaping.
