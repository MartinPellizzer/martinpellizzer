Rosa agrestis var. adenoclada

Rosa agrestis var. adenoclada, commonly known as the coastal rose, is a variant of the coastal rose species native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface, adapted to withstand coastal conditions. It produces red or pink flowers and is often found in sandy soils near the coast.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in saline environments.
