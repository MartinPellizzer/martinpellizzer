Ribes mandshuricum var. subglabrum

Ribes mandshuricum var. subglabrum, commonly known as the Smoothcurrant, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, which are typically smooth and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by edible red berries that are similar to currants. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in culinary applications.
