Rosa tomentosa var. typica

Rosa tomentosa var. typica, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, white to grayish pubescent foliage and stems. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
