Rubus hedycarpus subsp. dumosus

Rubus hedycarpus subsp.

dumosus, commonly known as the thorned blackberry, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
