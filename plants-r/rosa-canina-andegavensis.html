Rosa canina andegavensis

Rosa canina andegavensis, commonly known as the Andegavensis rose, is a species of shrub native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruits, known as hips, are orange-red and contain achenes surrounded by a fleshy pulp.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
