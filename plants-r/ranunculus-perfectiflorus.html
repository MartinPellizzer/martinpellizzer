Ranunculus perfectiflorus

Ranunculus perfectiflorus, commonly known as the perfectiflorous buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, brightly colored flowers that typically range from yellow to orange, with five petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant has palmate leaves divided into three to five lobes, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
