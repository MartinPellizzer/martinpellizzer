Rosa lantoscana

Rosa lantoscana, commonly known as the Italian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, thriving in temperate climates. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody stem with thorny branches.

Rosa lantoscana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in perfumery due to its aromatic blooms.
