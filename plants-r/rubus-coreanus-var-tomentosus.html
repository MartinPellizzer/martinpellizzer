Rubus coreanus var. tomentosus

Rubus coreanus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the Korean raspberry, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, hairy foliage, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
