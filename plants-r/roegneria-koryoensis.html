Roegneria koryoensis

Roegneria koryoensis, commonly known as the Korean bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Roegneria koryoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
