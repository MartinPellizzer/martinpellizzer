Rhynchospora corymbosa var. puncticulata

Rhynchospora corymbosa var. puncticulata, commonly known as the punctuated flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and small, triangular leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of tiny, pale green to reddish-brown flowers, which are typically borne in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
