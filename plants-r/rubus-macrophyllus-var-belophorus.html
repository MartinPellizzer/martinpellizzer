Rubus macrophyllus var. belophorus

Rubus macrophyllus var. belophorus, commonly known as the broad-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in forest edges. Its flowers are white and clustered, followed by the edible fruits that are highly valued for their flavor and culinary use.

The species is also known for its thorny stems and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
