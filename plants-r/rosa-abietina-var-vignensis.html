Rosa abietina var. vignensis

Rosa abietina var. vignensis, commonly known as the Alpine rose or mountain rose, is a perennial shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features upright, thorny stems and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces showy, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and finely toothed, with a fragrant aroma.

This hardy species is well adapted to cold climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience.
