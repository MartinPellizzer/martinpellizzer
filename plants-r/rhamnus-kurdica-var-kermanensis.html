Rhamnus kurdica var. kermanensis

Rhamnus kurdica var. kermanensis, commonly known as the Kerman buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Iranian plateau.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of ecological and medicinal studies.
