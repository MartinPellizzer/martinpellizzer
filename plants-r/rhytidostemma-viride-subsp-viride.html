Rhytidostemma viride subsp. viride

Rhytidostemma viride subsp.

viride, commonly known as the green-stemmed spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features succulent, green stems that are often ribbed and covered with small, white or yellowish tubercles.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly waxy texture.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a capacity for water storage in its stems to survive drought conditions.
