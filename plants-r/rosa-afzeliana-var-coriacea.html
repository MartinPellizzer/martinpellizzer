Rosa afzeliana var. coriacea

Rosa afzeliana var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-stemmed rose, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and India.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically compound, with serrated edges and a rough texture. This variety is well adapted to a range of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
