Ranunculus kirkii

Ranunculus kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five to eight petals and a hairy, upright stem. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure.

Ranunculus kirkii is often found in alpine meadows and is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.
