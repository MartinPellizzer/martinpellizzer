Rytidosperma pictum

Rytidosperma pictum, commonly known as the painted grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and distinctive, often colorful inflorescence. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowering stems are usually erect and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Rytidosperma pictum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
