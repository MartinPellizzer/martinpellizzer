Rhynchosia macinica

Rhynchosia macinica, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers with a prominent keel. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields.

Rhynchosia macinica is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and has been used in traditional agriculture and medicine.
