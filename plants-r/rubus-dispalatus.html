Rubus dispalatus

Rubus dispalatus, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that mature to a red color. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Rubus dispalatus is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
