Rubus fruticosus subsp. rudis

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

rudis, commonly known as brambles or blackberries, is a shrub-like plant belonging to the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small white flowers that develop into edible berries.

The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in a variety of soil types. It is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas due to its adaptability.

The berries are typically dark purple to black when ripe and are valued for their flavor and nutritional content.
