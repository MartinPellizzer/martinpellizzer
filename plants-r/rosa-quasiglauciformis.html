Rosa quasiglauciformis

Rosa quasiglauciformis, commonly known as the nearly glaucous rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and distinctive foliage with a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to China, it thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
