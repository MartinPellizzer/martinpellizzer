Rudgea coronata subsp. ochroleuca

Rudgea coronata subsp.

ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow-flowered rudgea, is a perennial shrub native to South America. It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange inflorescences that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodland edges. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
