Ruteria decidua

Ruteria decidua, commonly known as the downy rose or white thorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. It is often found in rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Ruteria decidua is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
