Rubus foliosus proles albicomus

Rubus foliosus proles albicomus, commonly known as the white-leaved bramble, is a shrub characterized by its distinctive white undersides on its leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are usually compound, with serrated edges and a soft, velvety texture.

This species is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, where it thrives in partial shade.
