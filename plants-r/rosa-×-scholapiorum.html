Rosa × scholapiorum

Rosa × scholapiorum, commonly known as the Scholapiorum rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and profuse flowering, typically producing clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant features dark green, serrated leaves and has a woody stem structure typical of many rose species. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

Rosa × scholapiorum prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
