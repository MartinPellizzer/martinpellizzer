Ranunculus prolixitorus

Ranunculus prolixitorus, commonly known as the long-stemmed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
