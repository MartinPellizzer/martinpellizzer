Rudbeckia spathulata

Rudbeckia spathulata, commonly known as the broadleaf coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features large, showy yellow or orange disk flowers surrounded by broad, dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is drought tolerant once established.

Rudbeckia spathulata is valued in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
