Rosa collaris

Rosa collaris, commonly known as the collar rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, where it thrives in woodland and shaded areas. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive collar-like appearance on the stem, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and a fragrant scent.

Rosa collaris is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
