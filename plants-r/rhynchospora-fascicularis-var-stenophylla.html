Rhynchospora fascicularis var. stenophylla

Rhynchospora fascicularis var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with three-ranked leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or reddish in color, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Due to its preference for wet conditions, it is often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds and lakes.
