Racosperma brunioides

Racosperma brunioides, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas.

Racosperma brunioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and conservation efforts.
