Ranunculus dielsianus

Ranunculus dielsianus, commonly known as the Diels' buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive appearance, while its flowers feature prominent sepals and petals.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
