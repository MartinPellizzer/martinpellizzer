Rhipsalis lumbricoides var. leucorhaphis

Rhipsalis lumbricoides var. leucorhaphis, commonly known as the white-flowered rhipsalis, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a unique ability to absorb moisture through its stems.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
