Rosa × damascena italica

Rosa × damascena italica, commonly known as the Italian rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and perfumery.

The essential oil extracted from its petals is widely used in cosmetics and aromatherapy.
