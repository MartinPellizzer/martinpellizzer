Roella filiformis

Roella filiformis, commonly known as the slender roella, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features delicate, thread-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Roella filiformis is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
