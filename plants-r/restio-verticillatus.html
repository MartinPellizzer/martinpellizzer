Restio verticillatus

Restio verticillatus, commonly known as the spiral restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
