Rosa asclepiadea

Rosa asclepiadea, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, often with a strong, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in thorns.

Rosa asclepiadea is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
