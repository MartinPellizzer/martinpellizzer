Ruehssia lanata

Ruehssia lanata, commonly known as the woolly ruehssia, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed in horticulture.
