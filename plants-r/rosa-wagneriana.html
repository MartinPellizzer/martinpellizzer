Rosa wagneriana

Rosa wagneriana, commonly known as Wagner's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the Andes Mountains in South America, specifically in countries like Bolivia and Peru. This shrub typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers. The leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant is characterized by its thorny stems.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
