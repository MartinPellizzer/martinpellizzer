Ranunculus extorris f. izumikawae

Ranunculus extorris f. izumikawae, commonly known as the Izumikawa ranunculus, is a rare and ornamental plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a distinctive lobed shape and produces small, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and aesthetic appeal, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
