Rudgea insignis

Rudgea insignis, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpet flower, is a striking member of the Rubiaceae family native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and biodiversity.
