Rhododendron tamaense

Rhododendron tamaense, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a species native to Japan and southern Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring with clusters of pink to red flowers, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes. Its leaves are elliptical, dark green, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Rhododendron tamaense prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
