Rosa gulczensis

Rosa gulczensis, commonly known as the Gulcz's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Asia, particularly in parts of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are typically orange-red in color.

Rosa gulczensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
