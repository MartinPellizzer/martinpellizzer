Rubus leptocladus

Rubus leptocladus, commonly known as the slender-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are edible but can be quite tart and are sometimes used in local cuisines.
