Rorippa americana

Rorippa americana, commonly known as American watercress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers with four petals, typically arranged in clusters. Its roots are fibrous, and it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils near water bodies.

Rorippa americana is often found in ponds, ditches, and along the edges of streams.
