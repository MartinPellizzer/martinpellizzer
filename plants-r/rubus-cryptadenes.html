Rubus cryptadenes

Rubus cryptadenes, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.

Its growth habit is adapted to high-altitude environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
