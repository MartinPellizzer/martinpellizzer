Rubus villicaulis subsp. obotriticus

Rubus villicaulis subsp.

obotriticus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of eastern North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family.
