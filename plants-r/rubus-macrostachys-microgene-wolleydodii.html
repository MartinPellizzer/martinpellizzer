Rubus macrostachys microgene wolleydodii

Rubus macrostachys microgene wolleydodii, commonly known as the Wollaston's raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its woody stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of small, hairy achenes. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and streamside areas. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been the subject of botanical study due to its unique genetic traits.
