Rhipsalis warmingiana

Rhipsalis warmingiana, commonly known as the silver star cactus, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the warmer months, typically from spring to summer. Its leaves are reduced to small, translucent tubercles, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
