Rhododendron hyacinthosmum

Rhododendron hyacinthosmum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in cool and moist environments. The plant features showy, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, usually in shades of pink, red, or white. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing dense foliage that helps it thrive in harsh alpine conditions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
