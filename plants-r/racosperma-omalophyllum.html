Racosperma omalophyllum

Racosperma omalophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its seeds are often dispersed by animals and can remain viable in the soil for extended periods.
