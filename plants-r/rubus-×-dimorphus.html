Rubus × dimorphus

Rubus × dimorphus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible landscaping.

Its botanical features include a complex genetic makeup due to its hybrid origin, contributing to its adaptability and variability.
