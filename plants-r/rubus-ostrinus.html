Rubus ostrinus

Rubus ostrinus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe, with a sweet and tart flavor. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five leaflets, and are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Rubus ostrinus thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
