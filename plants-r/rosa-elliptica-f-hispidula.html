Rosa elliptica f. hispidula

Rosa elliptica f. hispidula, commonly known as the hispid rose, is a botanical variant of the elliptic rose.

It is characterized by its hairy or pubescent stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It is native to regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
