Rhynchosinapis granatensis

Rhynchosinapis granatensis, commonly known as the Granada thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny stems. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces showy purple to pink flower heads in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
