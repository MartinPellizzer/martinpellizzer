Rosa × salaevensis f. adunca

Rosa × salaevensis f. adunca, commonly known as the shrub rose, is a hybrid species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
