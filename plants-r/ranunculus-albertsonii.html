Ranunculus albertsonii

Ranunculus albertsonii, commonly known as the Albertson's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with prominent white veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and meadows. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
