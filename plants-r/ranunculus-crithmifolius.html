Ranunculus crithmifolius

Ranunculus crithmifolius, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shorelines. It is a perennial species that thrives in saline and well-drained environments.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to coastal gardens and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
