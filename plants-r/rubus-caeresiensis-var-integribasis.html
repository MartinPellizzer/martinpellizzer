Rubus caeresiensis var. integribasis

Rubus caeresiensis var. integribasis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its edible berries and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
