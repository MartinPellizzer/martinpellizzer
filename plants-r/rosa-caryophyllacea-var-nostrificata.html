Rosa caryophyllacea var. nostrificata

Rosa caryophyllacea var. nostrificata, commonly known as the fragrant rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery contribute to its botanical significance.
