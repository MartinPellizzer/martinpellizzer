Rubus × membranaceus

Rubus × membranaceus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific coast of North America, characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and riparian habitats.

Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and a complex flowering structure typical of the Rubus genus.
