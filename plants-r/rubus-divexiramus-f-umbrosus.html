Rubus divexiramus f. umbrosus

Rubus divexiramus f. umbrosus, commonly known as the shadow raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its preference for shaded environments.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its small, dark red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in dense thickets and forest understories, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are hairy and often thorny.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique ecological niche in shaded habitats.
