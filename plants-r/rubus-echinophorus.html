Rubus echinophorus

Rubus echinophorus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and parts of Canada, typically found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and its stems are thorny, which help in its identification.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
