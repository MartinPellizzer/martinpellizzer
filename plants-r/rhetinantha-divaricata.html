Rhetinantha divaricata

Rhetinantha divaricata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its stems are short and often bare.

Rhetinantha divaricata thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
