Rubus vulgatus

Rubus vulgatus, commonly known as common blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are white or pale green, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, including moist and calcareous soils.

Rubus vulgatus is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas, and it is an important food source for various wildlife species.
