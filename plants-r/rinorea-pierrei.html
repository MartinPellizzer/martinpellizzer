Rinorea pierrei

Rinorea pierrei, commonly known as the pink bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a contrasting white throat. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Rinorea pierrei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
