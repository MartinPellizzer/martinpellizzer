Ruprechtia jamesonii

Ruprechtia jamesonii, commonly known as the Jameson's ruprechtia, is a rare and striking member of the Polygonaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern China, where it thrives in cool, humid climates. The plant features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bright red, bract-like flowers, which are distinctive and showy. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
