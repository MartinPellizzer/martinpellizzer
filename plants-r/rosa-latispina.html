Rosa latispina

Rosa latispina, commonly known as the broad-spined rose, is a species of shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and large, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces rose hips that are typically red or orange in color. Rosa latispina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to dry, sunny climates.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
