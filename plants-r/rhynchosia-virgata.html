Rhynchosia virgata

Rhynchosia virgata, commonly known as the velvet bean or African snakeroot, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, butterfly-like yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

Rhynchosia virgata is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
