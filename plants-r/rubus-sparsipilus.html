Rubus sparsipilus

Rubus sparsipilus, commonly known as the sparse-hairy raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
