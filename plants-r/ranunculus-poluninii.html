Ranunculus poluninii

Ranunculus poluninii, commonly known as the Polunin's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cold, temperate climates. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of numerous sepals that resemble petals.

Ranunculus poluninii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
