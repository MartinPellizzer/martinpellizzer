Rhododendron konori

Rhododendron konori, commonly known as the Konori rhododendron, is a species native to Japan, specifically the island of Hokkaido.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant pink or red flowers.

Rhododendron konori is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
