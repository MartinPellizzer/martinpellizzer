Rubus babingtonii var. centrogermanicus

Rubus babingtonii var. centrogermanicus, commonly known as the German raspberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in temperate climates and is native to Central Europe.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and culinary purposes.
