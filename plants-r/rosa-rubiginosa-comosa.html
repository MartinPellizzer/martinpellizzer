Rosa rubiginosa comosa

Rosa rubiginosa comosa, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green leaves and stems covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are rosehips, which are red, hairy, and contain seeds.

Rosa rubiginosa comosa is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
