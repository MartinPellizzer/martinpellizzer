Ranunculus auricomus f. hevellus

Ranunculus auricomus f. hevellus, commonly known as the Hevell's buttercup, is a variant of the golden buttercup.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets that are often hairy. This form is distinguished by its smaller, more compact flowers compared to the typical species.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
