Randia sphaerocarpa

Randia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, rounded fruits that resemble small globes, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with opposite, simple leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces clusters of berries that are edible when ripe.

Randia sphaerocarpa is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
