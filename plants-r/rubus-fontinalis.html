Rubus fontinalis

Rubus fontinalis, commonly known as the water raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, often found in wet or moist habitats. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Rubus fontinalis plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems, providing food for various wildlife species.
