Ruta obtusifolia

Ruta obtusifolia, commonly known as common rue, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves and small yellowish-green flowers. The plant contains compounds like rutin and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and pesticidal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ruta obtusifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its antispasmodic and carminative effects.
