Rubus flagellaris var. occidualis

Rubus flagellaris var. occidualis, commonly known as the western prickly pear, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces edible fruits, though they are typically small and not widely cultivated. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and potential agricultural studies.
