Ranunculus attenuilobus

Ranunculus attenuilobus, commonly known as the slenderleaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided with finely toothed margins, and it produces small, greenish-brown fruits.

Ranunculus attenuilobus is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic compounds.
