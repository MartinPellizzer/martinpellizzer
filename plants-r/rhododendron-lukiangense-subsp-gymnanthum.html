Rhododendron lukiangense subsp. gymnanthum

Rhododendron lukiangense subsp.

gymnanthum, commonly known as the Yunnan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from white to pink and purple.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
