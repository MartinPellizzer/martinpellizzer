Rhus trichocarpa var. serrata

Rhus trichocarpa var. serrata, commonly known as the California walnut or the coastal live oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its deeply lobed, serrated leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring. The tree's fruit is a woody capsule that contains one or two edible nuts, which are encased in a hard shell. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland chaparral habitats.

This species is also valued for its ecological importance, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
