Rubus spinulifer

Rubus spinulifer, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often covered in a fine, white powder. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus.
