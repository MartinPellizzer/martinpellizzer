Rubus × malacocaulon

Rubus × malacocaulon, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant within the Rubus genus, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along garden borders.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary purposes.
