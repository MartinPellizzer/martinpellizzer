Rubus insericatus

Rubus insericatus, commonly known as the silky raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the presence of silken, hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
