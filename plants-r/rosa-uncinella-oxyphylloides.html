Rosa uncinella oxyphylloides

Rosa uncinella oxyphylloides, commonly known as the hooked rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
