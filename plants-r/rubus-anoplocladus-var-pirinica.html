Rubus anoplocladus var. pirinica

Rubus anoplocladus var. pirinica, commonly known as the Pirin raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It is characterized by its biennial or perennial growth habit, with arching stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color, containing several drupelets. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are usually serrated and covered in fine hairs.

This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
