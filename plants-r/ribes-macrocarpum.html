Ribes macrocarpum

Ribes macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the genus Ribes in the family Grossulariaceae, which includes other species of gooseberries and currants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The shrub is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
