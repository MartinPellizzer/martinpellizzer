Ranunculus flabellatus proles subpinnatus

Ranunculus flabellatus proles subpinnatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by numerous stamens. The leaves are deeply divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments, giving them a lacy appearance. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
