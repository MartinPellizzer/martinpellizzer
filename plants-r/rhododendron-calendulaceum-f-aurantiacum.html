Rhododendron calendulaceum f. aurantiacum

Rhododendron calendulaceum f. aurantiacum, commonly known as the Flame Azalea, is a striking variant of the native azalea species found in the southeastern United States.

This form is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from orange to deep red, making it a standout in gardens and natural landscapes. The plant typically grows as a shrub with dense, evergreen foliage that provides year-round interest. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in moist, shaded woodland environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral coloration and its role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
