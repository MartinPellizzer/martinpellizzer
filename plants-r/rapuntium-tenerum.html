Rapuntium tenerum

Rapuntium tenerum, commonly known as the tender cress, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and small, bright green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, yellow flowers in clusters, which appear in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Rapuntium tenerum is valued for its peppery flavor and is used in various culinary applications.
