Rubus candicans var. reticulatus

Rubus candicans var. reticulatus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-veined leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
