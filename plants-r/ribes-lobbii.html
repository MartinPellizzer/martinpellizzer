Ribes lobbii

Ribes lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's gooseberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of flowering plants commonly referred to as gooseberries or currants. The plant is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit, a small, tart berry, is typically harvested in late summer and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Ribes lobbii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
