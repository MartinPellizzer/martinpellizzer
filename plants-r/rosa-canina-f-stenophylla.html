Rosa canina f. stenophylla

Rosa canina f. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its narrower, more elongated leaves compared to the typical form. This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodlands and scrub habitats. The flowers are usually pink to reddish in color and are followed by distinctive orange-red hips.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
