Rosa pouzinii var. polyadena

Rosa pouzinii var. polyadena, commonly known as the pink sandalwood rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a slight blush of red. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces rose hips that are typically red or orange in color. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

This variety is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
