Rosa bracteata var. taiwanensis

Rosa bracteata var. taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan bracteate rose, is a species native to Taiwan.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its dense, thorny stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically pink to red in color and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, oval berries that ripen to a dark red.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
