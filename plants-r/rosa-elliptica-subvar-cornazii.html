Rosa elliptica subvar. cornazii

Rosa elliptica subvar. cornazii, commonly known as Cornaz's elliptical rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features elongated, elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
