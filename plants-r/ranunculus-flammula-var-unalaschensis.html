Ranunculus flammula var. unalaschensis

Ranunculus flammula var. unalaschensis, commonly known as the Alaskan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Alaska and western Canada.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves. This variant is typically found in moist, rocky habitats at high elevations. The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic sap.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
