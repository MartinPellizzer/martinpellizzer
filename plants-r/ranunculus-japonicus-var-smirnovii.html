Ranunculus japonicus var. smirnovii

Ranunculus japonicus var. smirnovii, commonly known as the Smirnov's Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to seven petals, typically in shades of yellow or white. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variant is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is valued in gardens for its early spring flowering.
