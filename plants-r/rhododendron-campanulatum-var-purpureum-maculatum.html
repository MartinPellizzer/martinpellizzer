Rhododendron campanulatum var. purpureum-maculatum

Rhododendron campanulatum var. purpureum-maculatum, commonly known as the spotted purple campanula rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink, often with distinctive spots or markings. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, with leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green in color. Its botanical features include clustered inflorescences and a preference for acidic, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
