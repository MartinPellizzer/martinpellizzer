Rhododendron japonoheptamerum var. okiense

Rhododendron japonoheptamerum var. okiense, commonly known as the Okinawan rhododendron, is a rare and endemic species native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant prefers acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade, typical of its natural habitat in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
