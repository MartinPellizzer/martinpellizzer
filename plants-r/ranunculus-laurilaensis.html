Ranunculus laurilaensis

Ranunculus laurilaensis, commonly known as the Laurilaensian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features compound leaves with lobed or divided structures, and its flowers have five petals that are usually yellow or white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
