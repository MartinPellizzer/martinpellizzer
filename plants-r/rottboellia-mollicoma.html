Rottboellia mollicoma

Rottboellia mollicoma, commonly known as velvet grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fine, soft layer of hair, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and slightly folded, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Rottboellia mollicoma is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain areas.
