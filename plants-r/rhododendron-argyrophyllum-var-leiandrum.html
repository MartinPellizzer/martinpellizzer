Rhododendron argyrophyllum var. leiandrum

Rhododendron argyrophyllum var. leiandrum, commonly known as the silverleaf leathery rhododendron, is a unique variant of the argyrophyllum species.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance in both garden and natural settings. The plant produces dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, adding ornamental value. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it a prized addition to collections focused on rare and ornamental rhododendrons.
