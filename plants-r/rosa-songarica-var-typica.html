Rosa songarica var. typica

Rosa songarica var. typica, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces oval, green leaves with serrated edges, and its fruit is a hairy, red berry containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its hardiness and beauty, it is often cultivated in temperate climates for both its aesthetic value and historical significance.
