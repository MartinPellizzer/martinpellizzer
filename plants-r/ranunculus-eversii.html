Ranunculus eversii

Ranunculus eversii, commonly known as the Evers' buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with prominent central sepals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ranunculus eversii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, cup-shaped blooms.
