Rigiolepis filiformis

Rigiolepis filiformis, commonly known as the slender tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thin, thread-like leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It is often found in desert scrub and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to harsh, water-scarce environments.
