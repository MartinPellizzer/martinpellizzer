Rubus dumetorum var. rotundatus

Rubus dumetorum var. rotundatus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color, often with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a common sight in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
