Ranunculus dolichodontus

Ranunculus dolichodontus, commonly known as the long-toothed buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this species typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. The plant's flowers open in spring and are pollinated primarily by insects.

It is considered a herbaceous perennial, often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
