Rinorea holtzii

Rinorea holtzii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or blue bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small bells. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. Rinorea holtzii is a climbing or shrub-like plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscape design.
