Rosa blanda f. angustior

Rosa blanda f. angustior, commonly known as the narrowleaf rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, arching stems with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white to grayish hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
