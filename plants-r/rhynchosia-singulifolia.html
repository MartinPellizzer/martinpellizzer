Rhynchosia singulifolia

Rhynchosia singulifolia, commonly known as the single-leaf cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its unique single leaf at the base of the stem, which distinguishes it from other species in the genus. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Rhynchosia singulifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting agricultural systems.
