Rhododendron grothausii

Rhododendron grothausii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly California.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-elevation areas, often in coniferous forests. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red in color, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round structure to the landscape.

Rhododendron grothausii is adapted to cool, moist climates and is often found in areas with well-drained soil.
