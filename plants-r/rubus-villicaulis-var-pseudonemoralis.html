Rubus villicaulis var. pseudonemoralis

Rubus villicaulis var. pseudonemoralis, commonly known as the northern blackberry, is a shrub native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks, preferring rich, well-drained soils. The plant features arching stems with thorny branches and produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
