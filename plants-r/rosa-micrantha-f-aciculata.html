Rosa micrantha f. aciculata

Rosa micrantha f. aciculata, commonly known as the needle-leaved Rosa, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa micrantha.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. This form exhibits a compact growth habit, often forming dense, shrub-like structures. The flowers are usually small and pink, with five petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is native to regions of China and is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in various garden settings.
