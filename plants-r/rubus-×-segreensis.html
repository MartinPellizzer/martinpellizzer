Rubus × segreensis

Rubus × segreensis, commonly known as the Segre blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small to medium in size. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it flowers with white to pale pink petals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, though it requires careful management due to its aggressive growth habit.
