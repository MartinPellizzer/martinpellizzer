Rostellaria peploides

Rostellaria peploides, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Rostellaria peploides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
