Rosa ruralis

Rosa ruralis, commonly known as the country rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and western Asia and is characterized by its simple, often thornless stems and fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces red or orange hips in the fall.

Rosa ruralis is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
