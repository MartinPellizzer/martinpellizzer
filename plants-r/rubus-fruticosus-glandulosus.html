Rubus fruticosus glandulosus

Rubus fruticosus glandulosus, commonly known as the rough blackberry or brambles, is a shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. These fruits are edible, though often tart, and are used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberries and blackberries.
