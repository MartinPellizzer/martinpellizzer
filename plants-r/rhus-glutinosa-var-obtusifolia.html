Rhus glutinosa var. obtusifolia

Rhus glutinosa var. obtusifolia, commonly known as smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have oval, smooth-edged leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy berries that persist into winter. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Smooth sumac is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
