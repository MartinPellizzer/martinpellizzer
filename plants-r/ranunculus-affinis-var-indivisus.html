Ranunculus affinis var. indivisus

Ranunculus affinis var. indivisus, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five to seven sepals that resemble petals, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a short, stout stem with alternate, palmately lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and wildflower meadows.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in wetlands and along stream banks.
