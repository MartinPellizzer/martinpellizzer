Rosa olgae

Rosa olgae, commonly known as the Olgan rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring arching, thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in early summer, which are borne on long pedicels. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 5 to 7 oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

Rosa olgae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardy nature.
