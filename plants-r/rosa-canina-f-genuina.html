Rosa canina f. genuina

Rosa canina f. genuina, commonly known as the genuine dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose, Rosa canina.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pale pink flowers and thorned stems. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows. It produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C and are used in traditional medicine.

Its foliage is typically dark green with serrated leaves, contributing to its ornamental value.
