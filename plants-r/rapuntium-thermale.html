Rapuntium thermale

Rapuntium thermale, commonly known as the thermal cress, is a flowering plant native to certain regions with geothermal activity.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in nutrient-rich, moist soils near thermal springs.

Due to its unique habitat, it is often found in specific microclimates and is of interest to botanists studying adaptation to extreme environments.
