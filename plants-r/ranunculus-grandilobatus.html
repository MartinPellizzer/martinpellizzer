Ranunculus grandilobatus

Ranunculus grandilobatus, commonly known as the large-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ranunculus grandilobatus is often found in rich, loamy soils and can spread through rhizomes or seeds.
