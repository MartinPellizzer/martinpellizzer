Rhipsalis baccifera

Rhipsalis baccifera, commonly known as the mistletoe cactus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its soft, green stems that often branch out in a bushy manner. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals. Its unique appearance and ability to grow on trees make it an interesting subject for botanical study.

Rhipsalis baccifera is also notable for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular houseplant.
