Rubus gracilis var. pluvialis

Rubus gracilis var. pluvialis, commonly known as the coastal raspberry, is a shrub native to coastal regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal winds.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for coastal restoration projects and ecological studies.
