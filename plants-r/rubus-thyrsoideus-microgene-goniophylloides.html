Rubus thyrsoideus microgene goniophylloides

Rubus thyrsoideus microgene goniophylloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant white flowers in the spring. Its fruit is typically edible, though it is less commonly cultivated than other blackberry varieties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.
