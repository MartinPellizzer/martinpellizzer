Rosa pisocarpa var. gratissima

Rosa pisocarpa var. gratissima, commonly known as the sweet thorn rose, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers with a sweet scent, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a hairy, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
