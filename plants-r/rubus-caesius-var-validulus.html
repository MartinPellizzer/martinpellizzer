Rubus caesius var. validulus

Rubus caesius var. validulus, commonly known as the red raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, containing numerous tiny drupes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and is sometimes cultivated for commercial raspberry production.
