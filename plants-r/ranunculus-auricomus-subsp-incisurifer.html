Ranunculus auricomus subsp. incisurifer

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

incisurifer, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often with a distinctive golden center.

The plant has palmately divided leaves that are lobed and can have serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value in gardens and its role in supporting pollinators.
