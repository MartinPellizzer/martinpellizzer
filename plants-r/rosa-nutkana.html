Rosa nutkana

Rosa nutkana, commonly known as the Nutkan rose, is a species native to the western Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, with five petals and numerous stamens. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has thorny stems characteristic of many rose species.

Rosa nutkana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
