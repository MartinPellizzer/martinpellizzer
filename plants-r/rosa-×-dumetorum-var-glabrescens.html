Rosa × dumetorum var. glabrescens

Rosa × dumetorum var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and is native to various regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and the stems are often covered with a fine layer of down that becomes less pronounced as the plant matures.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
