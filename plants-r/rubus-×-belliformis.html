Rubus × belliformis

Rubus × belliformis, commonly known as the bell-shaped blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped bracts that enclose the flower clusters. The plant produces edible berries that are typically smaller and more tart compared to other blackberry varieties. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject in horticultural studies.
