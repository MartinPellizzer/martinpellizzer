Rubus oriens

Rubus oriens, commonly known as the oriental blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, including regions of China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

The fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a valuable plant for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
