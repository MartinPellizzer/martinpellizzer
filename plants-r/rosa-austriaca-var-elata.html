Rosa austriaca var. elata

Rosa austriaca var. elata, commonly known as the Austrian climbing rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Alps.

It features arching, thorny stems that can grow up to 6 meters in length, making it suitable for trellises or fences. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardy nature, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.
