Rubus hirtus var. hebetatus

Rubus hirtus var. hebetatus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its leaves are palmately lobed with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries, which are dark purple to black when mature, are edible and often used in culinary applications.
