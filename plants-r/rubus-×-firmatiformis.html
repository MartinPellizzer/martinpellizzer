Rubus × firmatiformis

Rubus × firmatiformis, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect or spreading growth habit and produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupes. The plant typically thrives in alpine or subalpine environments, where it tolerates cold temperatures and high altitudes. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it bears white to pale yellow flowers in early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
