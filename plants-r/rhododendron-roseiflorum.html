Rhododendron roseiflorum

Rhododendron roseiflorum, commonly known as the pink-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and natural landscapes.

Rhododendron roseiflorum is also known for its fragrant blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
