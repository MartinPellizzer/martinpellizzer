Rosa × involuta var. occidentalis

Rosa × involuta var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western climbing rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its vigorous, thorny stems that can climb or trail, making it suitable for trellises or hedges. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to fully double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its visual impact and fragrance.
