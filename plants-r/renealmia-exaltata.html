Renealmia exaltata

Renealmia exaltata, commonly known as the giant reed or swamp reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish or yellowish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly and form dense stands, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
