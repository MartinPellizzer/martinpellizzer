Randia lonicerioides

Randia lonicerioides, commonly known as the creeping randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its trailing stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain several seeds.

Randia lonicerioides is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
