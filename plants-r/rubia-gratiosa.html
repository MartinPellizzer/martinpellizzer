Rubia gratiosa

Rubia gratiosa, commonly known as the graceful or beautiful rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas, and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale yellow, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rubia gratiosa is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its historical use in herbal medicine.
