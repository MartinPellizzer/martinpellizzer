Rhododendron leucogigas

Rhododendron leucogigas, commonly known as the white giant rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its blooms.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
