Randia castaneofulva

Randia castaneofulva, commonly known as the redroot randia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange to reddish-brown berries. Its roots are often reddish in color, giving the plant its common name.

Randia castaneofulva is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
