Rosa indica var. vulgaris

Rosa indica var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common rose, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically come in a range of colors including red, pink, white, and yellow. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces rose hips, which are the fruit of the plant and contain the seeds.

Rosa indica var. vulgaris is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and has a long history of use in perfumery and culinary applications.
