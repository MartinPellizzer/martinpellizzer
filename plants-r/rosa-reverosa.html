Rosa reverosa

Rosa reverosa, commonly known as the sweet william rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces clustered blooms throughout the summer. Its leaves are dark green and serrated, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Rosa reverosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
