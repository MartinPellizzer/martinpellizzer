Ribes incarnatum

Ribes incarnatum, commonly known as the red currant bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in jams and wines. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven oval leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink or white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible landscaping.

The berries are also a food source for birds and small mammals.
