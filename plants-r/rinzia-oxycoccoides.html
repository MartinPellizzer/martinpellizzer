Rinzia oxycoccoides

Rinzia oxycoccoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rinzia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands. The plant features small, egg-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Rinzia oxycoccoides is part of the Myrtaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in supporting native insect populations.
