Rosa × dumetorum var. atrichogyna

Rosa × dumetorum var. atrichogyna, commonly known as the blackberry rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and thornless growth habit, making it distinct from other rose species. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its foliage is dense and glossy, with leaves composed of three to five leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
