Rhododendron hirsutum f. variegatum

Rhododendron hirsutum f. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated woolly rhododendron, is a hybrid form of the woolly rhododendron.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, which features a mix of green and white or yellowish markings. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub with dense, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique leaf patterns and seasonal floral displays.
