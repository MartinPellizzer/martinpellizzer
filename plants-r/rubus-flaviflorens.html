Rubus flaviflorens

Rubus flaviflorens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, particularly parts of China and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are small, yellow, and typically appear in clusters, while the fruits are aggregate berries that ripen to a red or black color.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
