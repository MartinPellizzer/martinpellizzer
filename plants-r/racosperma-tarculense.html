Racosperma tarculense

Racosperma tarculense, commonly known as the Tasmanian wattle, is a shrub native to Tasmania, Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features yellow flowers that bloom in winter, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its root system helps in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
