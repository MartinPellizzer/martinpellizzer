Rosa bordzilowskii

Rosa bordzilowskii, commonly known as the Georgian rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Georgia and the Caucasus.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on long pedicels. Its botanical characteristics include simple, alternate leaves and a woody root system adapted to rocky soils.

Rosa bordzilowskii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential in horticultural and medicinal applications.
