Ricinus communis var. violaceocaulis

Ricinus communis var. violaceocaulis, commonly known as the purple-stem castor bean, is a variant of the castor oil plant characterized by its distinctive purplish stems.

It is an annual herb native to tropical regions and belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family. The plant produces large, palmate leaves and spiny capsules containing toxic seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

Due to the toxicity of its seeds, it requires careful handling and is not recommended for cultivation in areas where children or pets are present.
