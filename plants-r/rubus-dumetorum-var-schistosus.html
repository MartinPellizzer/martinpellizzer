Rubus dumetorum var. schistosus

Rubus dumetorum var. schistosus, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red berries. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and stabilizing soil in rugged terrain.
