Rubus ordovicum

Rubus ordovicum, commonly known as the Ordovician bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to western Europe and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

The fruit is a aggregate of small drupes, typically red or black when mature, and is edible but often tart in flavor.
