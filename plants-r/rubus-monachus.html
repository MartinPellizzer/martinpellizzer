Rubus monachus

Rubus monachus, commonly known as the monk's berry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or black when mature. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and hedgerows, and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
