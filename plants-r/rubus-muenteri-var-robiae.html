Rubus muenteri var. robiae

Rubus muenteri var. robiae, commonly known as the Robia blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

The berries are often used in jams and desserts due to their intense flavor and high pectin content.
