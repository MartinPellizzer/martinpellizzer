Rhododendron hymenanthes f. angustifolium

Rhododendron hymenanthes f. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical species. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
