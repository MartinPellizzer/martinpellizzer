Rhynchospora tenuiflora var. albescens

Rhynchospora tenuiflora var. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and other wetland habitats. Its flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant plays a role in supporting local wildlife by providing habitat and nectar sources.

Rhynchospora tenuiflora var. albescens is typically found in the eastern United States, particularly in the southeastern region.
