Rubus novus

Rubus novus, commonly known as the New Zealand blackberry, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it has been introduced to other regions outside its native habitat.
