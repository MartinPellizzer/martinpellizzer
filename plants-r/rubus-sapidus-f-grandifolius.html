Rubus sapidus f. grandifolius

Rubus sapidus f. grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaf raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a hairy texture. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous small, red drupes that ripen in late summer. It typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

This form is noted for its ornamental value and potential for edible fruit production.
