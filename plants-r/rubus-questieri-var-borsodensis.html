Rubus questieri var. borsodensis

Rubus questieri var. borsodensis, commonly known as the Borsod raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces white flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in jams and desserts.
