Rosa × dumetorum f. velutina

Rosa × dumetorum f. velutina, commonly known as the velvet rose, is a naturally occurring hybrid within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
