Rosmarinus officinalis var. prostratus

Rosmarinus officinalis var. prostratus, commonly known as prostrate rosemary, is a low-growing variant of the rosemary plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, needle-like leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small blue or purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for both ornamental and practical gardening.
