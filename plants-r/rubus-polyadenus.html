Rubus polyadenus

Rubus polyadenus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a species of bramble within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, pinnately compound leaves, and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible red berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamin C. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Rubus polyadenus is an important plant for both wildlife and human consumption.
