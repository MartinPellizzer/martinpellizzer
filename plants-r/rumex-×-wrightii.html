Rumex × wrightii

Rumex × wrightii, commonly known as the hybrid sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are edible and have a tangy, sour taste due to the presence of oxalic acid.

It is often found in mixed with other native flora in its natural habitat.
