Rubus acheruntinus

Rubus acheruntinus, commonly known as the Armenian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland and shrubland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
