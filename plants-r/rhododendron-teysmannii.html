Rhododendron teysmannii

Rhododendron teysmannii, commonly known as the Teymann's rhododendron, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia.

It is a evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and red. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a species of conservation concern.
