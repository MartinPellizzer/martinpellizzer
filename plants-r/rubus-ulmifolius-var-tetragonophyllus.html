Rubus ulmifolius var. tetragonophyllus

Rubus ulmifolius var. tetragonophyllus, commonly known as the four-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the brambles native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and dark, glossy leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with thorny stems that provide structural support.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its edible fruit.
