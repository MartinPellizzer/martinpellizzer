Rubus thyrsoideus var. viridescens

Rubus thyrsoideus var. viridescens, commonly known as the green-stem blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive greenish stems.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of North America. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
