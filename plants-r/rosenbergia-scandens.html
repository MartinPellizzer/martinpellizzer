Rosenbergia scandens

Rosenbergia scandens, commonly known as the climbing ginger, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, vine-like stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the warm season. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Rosenbergia scandens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
