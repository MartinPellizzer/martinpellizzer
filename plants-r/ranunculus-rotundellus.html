Ranunculus rotundellus

Ranunculus rotundellus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant has a short, stout stem and produces small, rounded fruits that contain several seeds.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
