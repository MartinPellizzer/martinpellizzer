Rhododendron araiophyllum

Rhododendron araiophyllum, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of bell-shaped, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and hardy ornamental plant.
