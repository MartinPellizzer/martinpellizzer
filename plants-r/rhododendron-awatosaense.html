Rhododendron awatosaense

Rhododendron awatosaense, commonly known as the Awatosa rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to deep red.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
