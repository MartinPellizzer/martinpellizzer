Rubus obtusifolius

Rubus obtusifolius, commonly known as the blunt-leaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves that have rounded lobes. The plant produces aggregate fruits, typically dark purple to black in color, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Rubus obtusifolius is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
