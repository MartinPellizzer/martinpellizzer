Rubus villicaulis f. marchicus

Rubus villicaulis f. marchicus, commonly known as the Marchik's bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, usually white or pink flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological traits and ecological adaptations.
