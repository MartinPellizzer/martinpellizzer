Rosa × dumetorum f. rocheliana

Rosa × dumetorum f. rocheliana, commonly known as the Rocheliana form of the shrub rose, is a botanical hybrid that exhibits unique morphological traits.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves that are typically narrow and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in garden settings.
