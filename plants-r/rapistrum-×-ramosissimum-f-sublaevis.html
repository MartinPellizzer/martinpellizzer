Rapistrum × ramosissimum f. sublaevis

Rapistrum × ramosissimum f. sublaevis, commonly known as the slender wild radish, is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves.

It produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural fields. Its seeds are small and can remain viable in the soil for several years, contributing to its invasive potential in certain regions.

The subspecies sublaevis is distinguished by its more slender stems and reduced leaflet size compared to other forms of Rapistrum × ramosissimum.
