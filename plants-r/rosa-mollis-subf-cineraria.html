Rosa mollis subf. cineraria

Rosa mollis subf.

cineraria, commonly known as the cineraria rose, is a subspecies of the shrub rose characterized by its soft, hairy stems and foliage. It is native to the Mediterranean region, where it thrives in rocky and coastal environments.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a silvery or grayish underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
