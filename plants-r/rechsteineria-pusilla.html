Rechsteineria pusilla

Rechsteineria pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf rechsteineria, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
