Rubus cryptadenus var. teretiusculus

Rubus cryptadenus var. teretiusculus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and local biodiversity.
