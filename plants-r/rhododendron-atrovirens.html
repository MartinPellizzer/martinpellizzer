Rhododendron atrovirens

Rhododendron atrovirens, commonly known as the blackleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range from pink to deep purple. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Rhododendron atrovirens is also noted for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various climate conditions.
