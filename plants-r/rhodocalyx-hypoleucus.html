Rhodocalyx hypoleucus

Rhodocalyx hypoleucus, commonly known as the white-flowered legume, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly hairy.
