Rubus hirtus var. subimbricatus

Rubus hirtus var. subimbricatus, commonly known as the prickly rose or brambles, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

This plant plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
