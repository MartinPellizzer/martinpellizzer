Rhagadiolus stellatus subsp. edulis

Rhagadiolus stellatus subsp.

edulis, commonly known as the edible star orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its star-shaped flowers, which are typically yellow or orange in color, and its delicate, grass-like foliage.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rocky outcrops or in forest understories. Its flowers are not only visually striking but also play a role in attracting specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts to protect its natural environment.
