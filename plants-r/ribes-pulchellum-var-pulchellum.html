Ribes pulchellum var. pulchellum

Ribes pulchellum var. pulchellum, commonly known as the purple-flowered currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring slender, arching branches covered in small, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, blooming in early spring. Its berries, which are edible, are small, dark purple, and turn from green to red as they mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
