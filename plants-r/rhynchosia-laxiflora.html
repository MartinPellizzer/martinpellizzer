Rhynchosia laxiflora

Rhynchosia laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The flowers are small, yellow, and often clustered in racemes, while the pods are elongated and contain several seeds. This plant is adapted to warm, humid climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with Rhizobia bacteria.
