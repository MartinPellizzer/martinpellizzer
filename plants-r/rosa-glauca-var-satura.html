Rosa glauca var. satura

Rosa glauca var. satura, commonly known as the Satin rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa glauca species, characterized by its soft, velvety petals.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with a slightly fragrant aroma, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
