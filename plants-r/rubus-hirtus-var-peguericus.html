Rubus hirtus var. peguericus

Rubus hirtus var. peguericus, commonly known as the European bramble or creeping raspberry, is a thorny shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces edible red berries in the summer, which are often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.

This species is known for its vigorous growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
