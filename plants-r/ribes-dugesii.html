Ribes dugesii

Ribes dugesii, commonly known as the desert gooseberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries that are typically borne in clusters. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
