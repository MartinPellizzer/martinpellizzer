Rhinorchis lehmanniana

Rhinorchis lehmanniana, commonly known as the Lehmann's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers that display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect light. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

Rhinorchis lehmanniana is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
