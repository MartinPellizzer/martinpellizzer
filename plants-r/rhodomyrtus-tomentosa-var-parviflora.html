Rhodomyrtus tomentosa var. parviflora

Rhodomyrtus tomentosa var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered rambutan, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish-brown stems and small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, hairy fruits that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a distinctive aromatic scent.
