Rubus exaltatus var. bracteosus

Rubus exaltatus var. bracteosus, commonly known as the bracteated blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupes. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
