Rhus huillensis var. erythraea

Rhus huillensis var. erythraea, commonly known as the red-leafed sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate and composed of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for some wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local fauna.
