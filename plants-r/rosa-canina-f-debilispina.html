Rosa canina f. debilispina

Rosa canina f. debilispina, commonly known as the weak-spined dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its smaller, more delicate thorns compared to the typical form. The plant produces fragrant pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets.

This form is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environments.
