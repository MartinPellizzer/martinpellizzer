Rosa sepium var. robusta

Rosa sepium var. robusta, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance. This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to a range of growing conditions.
