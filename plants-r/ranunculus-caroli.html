Ranunculus caroli

Ranunculus caroli, commonly known as the Carolinian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic sap.

Ranunculus caroli is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
