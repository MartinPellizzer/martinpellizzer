Rhynchospora stellata f. intercedens

Rhynchospora stellata f. intercedens, commonly known as the star-leaf sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or marshy areas. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-like structure with three stamens and a single pistil.

This variant is distinguished by its distinct floral morphology and is often found in temperate regions of North America.
