Rubus gerezianus

Rubus gerezianus, commonly known as the Gerezian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and less commonly cultivated species.
