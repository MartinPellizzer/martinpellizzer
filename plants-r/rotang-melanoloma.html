Rotang melanoloma

Rotang melanoloma, commonly known as blackheart or blackwood, is a rare and highly valued type of durian.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black flesh and a unique, complex aroma that is both sweet and pungent. The plant belongs to the genus Durio and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. Its fruit is typically smaller than other durian varieties and has a thick, spiky outer husk.

The botanical features of rotang melanoloma make it distinct, with its dark pulp and unusual scent contributing to its reputation as a delicacy.
