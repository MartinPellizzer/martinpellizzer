Ranunculus berggrenii

Ranunculus berggrenii, commonly known as Berggren's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant typically features basal leaves that are palmately divided, and its flowers are usually yellow with five petals. It thrives in alpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
