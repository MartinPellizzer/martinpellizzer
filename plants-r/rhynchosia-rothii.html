Rhynchosia rothii

Rhynchosia rothii, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant bears pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. Rhynchosia rothii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural systems.

Its ornamental value and nutritional benefits contribute to its importance in both ecological and human contexts.
