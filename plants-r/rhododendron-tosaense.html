Rhododendron tosaense

Rhododendron tosaense, commonly known as the Tosa rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shady to partially sunny conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron tosaense is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
