Rhododendron pilosum

Rhododendron pilosum, commonly known as the hairy rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly China and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are broad and leathery, often covered with fine, soft hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

Rhododendron pilosum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
