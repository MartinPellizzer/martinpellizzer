Rosa ditrichopoda

Rosa ditrichopoda, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers. Its leaves are compound, with small, hairy leaflets that give it its common name.

Rosa ditrichopoda is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive blooms and hardy nature.
