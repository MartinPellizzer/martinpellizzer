Rubus × trilobatus

Rubus × trilobatus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are composed of small, drupe-like units called achenes. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

The fruit, which ripens in summer, is prized for its sweet-tart flavor and is widely used in culinary applications.
