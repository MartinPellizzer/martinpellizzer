Rubus hemistemon var. barbeyi

Rubus hemistemon var. barbeyi, commonly known as the Barbey's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of southern Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color, typically in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
