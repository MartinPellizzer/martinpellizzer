Rubus pyramidalis var. obesus

Rubus pyramidalis var. obesus, commonly known as the fat-hairy raspberry, is a thorny shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive hairy texture, and its flowers are typically pink or white, appearing in clusters. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, red drupes that ripen in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
