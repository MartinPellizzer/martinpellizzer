Ranunculus buergeri

Ranunculus buergeri, commonly known as Buerger's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Asia.

It features a short, stout rhizome and produces clusters of bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with numerous stamens. The leaves are palmately divided into 5-9 lobes, often with a hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
