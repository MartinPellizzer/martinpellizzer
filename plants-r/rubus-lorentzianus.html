Rubus lorentzianus

Rubus lorentzianus, commonly known as Lorentz's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
