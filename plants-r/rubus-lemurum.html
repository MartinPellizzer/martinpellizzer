Rubus lemurum

Rubus lemurum, commonly known as the lemurine raspberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
