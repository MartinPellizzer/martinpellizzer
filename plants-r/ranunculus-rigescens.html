Ranunculus rigescens

Ranunculus rigescens, commonly known as the stiff buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often surrounded by a ring of leaf-like sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, hairy texture.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
