Rubus seciurensis

Rubus seciurensis, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces dark purple to black berries that are highly aromatic. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal regions.

The berries are edible and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and traditional medicine.
