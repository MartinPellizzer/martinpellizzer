Rubus guilhotii

Rubus guilhotii, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a shrub native to China and belongs to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
