Rubus atrovirescens

Rubus atrovirescens, commonly known as blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible blackberries. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Its berries are not only valued for their flavor but also for their medicinal and nutritional properties.
