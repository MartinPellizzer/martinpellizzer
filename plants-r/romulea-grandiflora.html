Romulea grandiflora

Romulea grandiflora, commonly known as the giant romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, stocky stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Romulea grandiflora is known for its resilience and ability to bloom reliably in suitable climates.
