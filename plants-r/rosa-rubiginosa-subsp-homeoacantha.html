Rosa rubiginosa subsp. homeoacantha

Rosa rubiginosa subsp.

homeoacantha, commonly known as the rough rose or thorny red rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves.

The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers, which are followed by reddish-brown, hairy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
