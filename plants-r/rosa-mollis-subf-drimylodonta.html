Rosa mollis subf. drimylodonta

Rosa mollis subf.

drimylodonta, commonly known as the soft rose or drimylodonta rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and foliage. It typically features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in early summer.

The plant is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a soft, velvety texture.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cultivated gardens.
