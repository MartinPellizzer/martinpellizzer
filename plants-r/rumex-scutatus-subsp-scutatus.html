Rumex scutatus subsp. scutatus

Rumex scutatus subsp.

scutatus, commonly known as the broadleaf dock, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The plant has alternate, broad, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
