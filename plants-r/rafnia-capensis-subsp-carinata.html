Rafnia capensis subsp. carinata

Rafnia capensis subsp.

carinata, commonly known as the Cape mountain thorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its spiny branches and compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by woody, triangular pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and mountainous regions.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and has a slow growth rate.
