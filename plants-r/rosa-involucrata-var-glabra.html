Rosa involucrata var. glabra

Rosa involucrata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other rose varieties. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
