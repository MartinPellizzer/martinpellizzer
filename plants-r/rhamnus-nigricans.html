Rhamnus nigricans

Rhamnus nigricans, commonly known as blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny branches and small, black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows or woodland edges.

The berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
