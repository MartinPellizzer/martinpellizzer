Rubus deaneanus

Rubus deaneanus, commonly known as the Deane's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in prickly hairs.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
