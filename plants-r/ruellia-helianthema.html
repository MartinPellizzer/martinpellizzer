Ruellia helianthema

Ruellia helianthema, commonly known as the sunflower ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves and vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes. Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

Ruellia helianthema is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
