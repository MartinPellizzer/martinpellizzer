Rubus acmophorus

Rubus acmophorus, commonly known as the yellowberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. It produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color.

Rubus acmophorus is often found in wetlands and along the edges of swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils.
