Rosa palustris var. nuttalliana

Rosa palustris var. nuttalliana, commonly known as the swamp rose, is a deciduous shrub native to wetland areas of North America.

It features slender, arching stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense thickets. The plant produces showy, fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along stream banks.
