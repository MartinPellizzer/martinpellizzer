Rhaphiolepis indica var. insularis

Rhaphiolepis indica var. insularis, commonly known as the Island rosebay, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in subtropical gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for coastal and humid environments.
