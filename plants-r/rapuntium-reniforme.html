Rapuntium reniforme

Rapuntium reniforme, commonly known as the broadleaf cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, kidney-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady areas such as woodland floors and stream banks. It produces edible seeds that are sometimes used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
