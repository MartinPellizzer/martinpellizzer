Raphanus sativus f. raphanistroides

Raphanus sativus f. raphanistroides, commonly known as the broad-leafed radish, is a cultivated form of the wild radish.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, and often bulbous leaves that are typically more robust than those of the common radish. The plant is an annual herb with a taproot system that can become quite swollen, resembling a root vegetable. It thrives in cool climates and is often grown for its edible leaves and root.

This variety is valued for its mild flavor and nutritional content, making it a popular choice in some culinary traditions.
