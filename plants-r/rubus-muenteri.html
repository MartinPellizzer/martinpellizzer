Rubus muenteri

Rubus muenteri, commonly known as the Muenter's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
