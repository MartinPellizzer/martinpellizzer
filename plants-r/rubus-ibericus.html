Rubus ibericus

Rubus ibericus, commonly known as the Iberian blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
