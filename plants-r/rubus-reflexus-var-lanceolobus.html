Rubus reflexus var. lanceolobus

Rubus reflexus var. lanceolobus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with lance-shaped lobes. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in woodland habitats and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
