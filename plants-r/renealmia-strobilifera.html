Renealmia strobilifera

Renealmia strobilifera, commonly known as the pineapple grass, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, cylindrical inflorescence. The plant produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Renealmia strobilifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
