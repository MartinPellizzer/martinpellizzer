Rosa boullui

Rosa boullui, commonly known as the Boullue rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant produces oval, hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Rosa boullui is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in cooler climates.
