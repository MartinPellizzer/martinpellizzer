Rhododendron phaeochlorum

Rhododendron phaeochlorum, commonly known as the yellow-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at high elevations. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

Rhododendron phaeochlorum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate acidic soil and partial shade.
