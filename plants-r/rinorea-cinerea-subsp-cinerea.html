Rinorea cinerea subsp. cinerea

Rinorea cinerea subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf rinorea, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture. Rinorea cinerea subsp.

cinerea is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
