Rupicapnos africana subsp. gaetula

Rupicapnos africana subsp.

gaetula, commonly known as the Cape rose or African rose, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, thorny growth habit.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically small, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.
