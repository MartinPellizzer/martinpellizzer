Rubus radula f. viadricus

Rubus radula f. viadricus, commonly known as the Viadricus blackberry, is a subspecies of the Rubus radula, a member of the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
