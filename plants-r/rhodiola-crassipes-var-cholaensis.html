Rhodiola crassipes var. cholaensis

Rhodiola crassipes var. cholaensis, commonly known as the Cholaensis rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, pinkish-red flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It is adapted to cold, arid environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This species is valued for its potential adaptogenic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
