Ruellia beniana

Ruellia beniana, commonly known as the Mexican bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
