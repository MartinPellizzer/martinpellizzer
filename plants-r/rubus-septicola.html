Rubus septicola

Rubus septicola, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of eastern and southeastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rubus septicola is valued for its edible fruits and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
