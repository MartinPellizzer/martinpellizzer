Rubus pluribracteatus

Rubus pluribracteatus, commonly known as the many-bracted blackberry.

It is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible, though some varieties may have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its ornamental value as well as its culinary uses.
