Ricinocarpus prunifolius

Ricinocarpus prunifolius, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. Its roots and bark contain compounds with medicinal properties, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

The species is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
