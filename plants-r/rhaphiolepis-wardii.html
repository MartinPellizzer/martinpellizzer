Rhaphiolepis wardii

Rhaphiolepis wardii, commonly known as the Chinese firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny branches and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Rhaphiolepis wardii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and winter interest.
