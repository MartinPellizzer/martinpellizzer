Raspalia angulata

Raspalia angulata, commonly known as the angular raspalia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive angular leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
