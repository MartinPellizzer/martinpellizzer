Rubus contractipes

Rubus contractipes, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are white and borne in clusters.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
