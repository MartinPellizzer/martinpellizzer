Rubus macrophyllus var. acanthosepalus

Rubus macrophyllus var. acanthosepalus, commonly known as the velvety blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in moist, temperate climates. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces flowers that transition into the edible fruit. Its berries are prized for their intense flavor and are often used in jams, pies, and wines.

The plant's botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
