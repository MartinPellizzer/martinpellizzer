Rosa parisiensis

Rosa parisiensis, commonly known as the Paris rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the temperate regions of Europe, particularly France, and is known for its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The Paris rose is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
