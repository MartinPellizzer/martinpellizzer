Rosa systyla var. leucochroa

Rosa systyla var. leucochroa, commonly known as the white-stemmed rock rose, is a variety of the rock rose genus native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to arid environments. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a desirable ornamental plant for horticultural use.
