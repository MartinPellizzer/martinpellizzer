Rubus lundii

Rubus lundii, commonly known as the Lund's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, specifically found in countries like South Africa, Namibia, and Botswana. The plant is a shrub that produces small, dark berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
