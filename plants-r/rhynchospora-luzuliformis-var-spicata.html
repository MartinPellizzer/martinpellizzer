Rhynchospora luzuliformis var. spicata

Rhynchospora luzuliformis var. spicata, commonly known as the spicate flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features slender, grass-like stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

This species plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
