Rosa arvensis var. subbibracteata

Rosa arvensis var. subbibracteata, commonly known as the field rose or creeping rose, is a variant of the field rose species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub with thorny stems that often spread along the ground. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

This variant is characterized by the presence of small bracts beneath the flower buds, distinguishing it from other Rosa arvensis varieties.
