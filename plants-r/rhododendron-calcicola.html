Rhododendron calcicola

Rhododendron calcicola, commonly known as the limestone rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from pink to white. This plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich environments. Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a notable component of alpine and subalpine ecosystems.

Rhododendron calcicola plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
