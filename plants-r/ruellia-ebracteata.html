Ruellia ebracteata

Ruellia ebracteata, commonly known as the slender ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ruellia ebracteata is often found in moist, shaded environments like forest floors or along stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
