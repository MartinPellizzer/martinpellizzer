Rhynchospora elegantula

Rhynchospora elegantula, commonly known as the elegant tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features slender, triangular stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive sheath at the base.

Rhynchospora elegantula plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
