Rosa hebecarpa

Rosa hebecarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruit rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Kazakhstan, and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit, a follicle, is narrow and elongated, giving the plant its specific epithet.

Rosa hebecarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in temperate climates.
