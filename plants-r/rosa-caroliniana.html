Rosa caroliniana

Rosa caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina rose, is a species of flowering shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Rosa caroliniana is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, often forming dense thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a valuable species for ecological restoration.
