Ranunculus pseudoflaccidus

Ranunculus pseudoflaccidus, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically lobed or divided, and its flowers are usually solitary, with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
