Rhaphis javanica

Rhaphis javanica, commonly known as the Java rattan, is a climbing palm native to Southeast Asia.

It features pinnate leaves with long, slender pinnae and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is characterized by its robust stems and is often used in the production of furniture and handicrafts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Rhaphis javanica is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its economic importance in tropical regions.
