Restrepia pandurata

Restrepia pandurata, commonly known as the pandurata orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive pandurata shape, typically in shades of white and green. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in cool, humid environments.

Restrepia pandurata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
