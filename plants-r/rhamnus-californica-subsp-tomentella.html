Rhamnus californica subsp. tomentella

Rhamnus californica subsp.

tomentella, commonly known as the woolly buckthorn, is a shrub native to coastal regions of California. It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage, which gives it a distinctive, woolly appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by reddish-black berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
