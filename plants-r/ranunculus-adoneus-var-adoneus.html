Ranunculus adoneus var. adoneus

Ranunculus adoneus var. adoneus, commonly known as the Sierra larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with dark purple markings. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are covered in fine hairs.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.
