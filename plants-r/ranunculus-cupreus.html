Ranunculus cupreus

Ranunculus cupreus, commonly known as the copper cup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and its flowers range in color from yellow to orange-red.

Ranunculus cupreus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
