Rhodiola primuloides

Rhodiola primuloides, commonly known as the alpine rose or golden root, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and clusters of yellow or orange flowers. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found at high altitudes. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their adaptogenic properties.
