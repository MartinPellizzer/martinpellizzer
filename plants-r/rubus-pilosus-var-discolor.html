Rubus pilosus var. discolor

Rubus pilosus var. discolor, commonly known as the hairy bramble or the variable raspberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and its hairy, often thorny stems. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a pubescent surface. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruits are aggregate berries that can range in color from red to yellow.

This plant thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils, and is known for its adaptability and resilience.
