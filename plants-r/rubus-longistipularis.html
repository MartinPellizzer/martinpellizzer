Rubus longistipularis

Rubus longistipularis, commonly known as the long-stemmed blackberry shrub, is a species native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are usually white or pale green.

The berries, which are edible, are often used in jams and preserves.
