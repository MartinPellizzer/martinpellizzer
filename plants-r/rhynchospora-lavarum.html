Rhynchospora lavarum

Rhynchospora lavarum, commonly known as the Lavarum sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are usually greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen, and it can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Rhynchospora lavarum plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
