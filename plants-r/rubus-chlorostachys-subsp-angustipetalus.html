Rubus chlorostachys subsp. angustipetalus

Rubus chlorostachys subsp.

angustipetalus, commonly known as the narrow-petaled evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to southeastern China. It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
