Rubus discolor var. macroacanthus

Rubus discolor var. macroacanthus, commonly known as the large thorned raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive large, sharp thorns along the stems and leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
