Rosa tomentosa var. cazanae

Rosa tomentosa var. cazanae, commonly known as the Cazanae rugosa rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces oval, reddish-brown hips that are edible and contain achenes. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
