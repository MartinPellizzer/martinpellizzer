Rhododendron fauriei var. roseum

Rhododendron fauriei var. roseum, commonly known as the Roseum Azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun for optimal growth. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to a variety of environmental conditions.
