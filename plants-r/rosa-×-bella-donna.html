Rosa × bella-donna

Rosa × bella-donna, commonly known as the Sweetheart Rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the cross between Rosa damascena and Rosa gallica.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant scent and compact growth habit.
