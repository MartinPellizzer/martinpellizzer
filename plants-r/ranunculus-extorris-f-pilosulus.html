Ranunculus extorris f. pilosulus

Ranunculus extorris f. pilosulus, commonly known as the hairy-tailed buttercup, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral features.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to specific regions in Europe, particularly the Iberian Peninsula. The plant typically features palmately divided leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually yellow with a central greenish hue, and they bloom in early spring.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
