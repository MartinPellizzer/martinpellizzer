Ranunculus calcarae

Ranunculus calcarae, commonly known as the rock scurfpea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ranunculus calcarae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
