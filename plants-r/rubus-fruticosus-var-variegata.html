Rubus fruticosus var. variegata

Rubus fruticosus var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible and often used in jams and pies. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
