Rubus × hypoleucophyllus

Rubus × hypoleucophyllus, commonly known as the white-leafed raspberry.

This hybrid plant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white or pale-colored leaves, which distinguish it from other raspberry species. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.

It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.
