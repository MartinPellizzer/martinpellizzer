Rafnia virens

Rafnia virens, commonly known as the green ant plant, is a unique species belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, leaf-like structures that resemble ants, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden within a spathe, a modified leaf that encloses the true flowers. Rafnia virens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
