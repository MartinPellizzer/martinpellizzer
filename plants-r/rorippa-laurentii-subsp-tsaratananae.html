Rorippa laurentii subsp.

tsaratananae, commonly known as the Tsaratanana watercress, is a rare aquatic plant native to Madagascar. It thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in streams and pools with cool, clear water.

The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged, and it produces tiny white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct genetic makeup compared to other Rorippa species.
