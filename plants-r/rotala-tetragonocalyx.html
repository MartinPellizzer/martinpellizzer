Rotala tetragonocalyx

Rotala tetragonocalyx, commonly known as the four-angled rotala, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of four-angled, opposite leaves that are typically bright green and slightly serrated. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often used in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.

Rotala tetragonocalyx is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for aquatic life and its role in water purification.
