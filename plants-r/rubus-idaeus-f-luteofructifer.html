Rubus idaeus f. luteofructifer

Rubus idaeus f. luteofructifer, commonly known as the golden-fruited raspberry, is a botanical variant of the common raspberry.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish fruits, which differ from the typical red berries of the standard Rubus idaeus. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of Europe. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink.

The leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.
