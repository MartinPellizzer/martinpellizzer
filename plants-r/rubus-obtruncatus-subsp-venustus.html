Rubus obtruncatus subsp. venustus

Rubus obtruncatus subsp.

venustus, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into dark purple, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
