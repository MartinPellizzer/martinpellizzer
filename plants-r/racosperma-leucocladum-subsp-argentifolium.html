Racosperma leucocladum subsp. argentifolium

Racosperma leucocladum subsp.

argentifolium, commonly known as silver-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white leaves, which are covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces yellow flower spikes that emerge from the leaf axils, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple small leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern.

This species is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
