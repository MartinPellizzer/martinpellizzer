Rumea

Rumea, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Rumea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
