Rubus callosus var. floribundus

Rubus callosus var. floribundus, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark purple, edible berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardening.
