Rhynchotechum angustifolium

Rhynchotechum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhynchotechum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear alternate, linear to lanceolate leaves, which are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along streambanks.
