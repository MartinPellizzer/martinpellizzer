Racinaea penduliflora

Racinaea penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flower racinaea, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its pendant, showy inflorescence that consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Racinaea penduliflora thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
