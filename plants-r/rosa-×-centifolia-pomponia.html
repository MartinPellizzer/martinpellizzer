Rosa × centifolia pomponia

Rosa × centifolia pomponia, commonly known as the Pompon rose, is a hybrid shrub native to France.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and abundant, rounded, pink or white flowers that resemble small balls, hence its common name. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to fully double blooms that are highly valued in perfumery and floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring regular pruning to maintain its shape and promote continuous flowering.

This variety is also noted for its disease resistance and adaptability to various climate conditions.
