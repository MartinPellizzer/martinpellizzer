Rosa pimpinellifolia var. hispidissima

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. hispidissima, commonly known as the hispid hairy rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to white in color, blooming in spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
