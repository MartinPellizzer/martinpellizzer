Rubus victorinii

Rubus victorinii, commonly known as the Victorin's bramble, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers in the spring.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
