Rotheca hirsuta f. hirsuta

Rotheca hirsuta f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy rotheca, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy habitats, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its inflorescence consists of numerous tiny flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and flies.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture conditions.
