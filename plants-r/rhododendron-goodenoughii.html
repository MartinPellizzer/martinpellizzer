Rhododendron goodenoughii

Rhododendron goodenoughii, commonly known as the Goodenough rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters, where it thrives in cool, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rhododendron goodenoughii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
