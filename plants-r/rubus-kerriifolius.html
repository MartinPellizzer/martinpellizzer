Rubus kerriifolius

Rubus kerriifolius, commonly known as the Kerrii blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
