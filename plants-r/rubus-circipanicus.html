Rubus circipanicus

Rubus circipanicus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are typically red when ripe. These fruits are edible and widely cultivated for their flavor and nutritional value.

Rubus circipanicus thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.
