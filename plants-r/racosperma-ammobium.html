Racosperma ammobium

Racosperma ammobium, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to salty coastal environments. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.

Racosperma ammobium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in coastal landscaping.
