Rubus godronii microgene grandibasis

Rubus godronii microgene grandibasis, commonly known as the Godron's raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they may vary in color depending on the species. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
