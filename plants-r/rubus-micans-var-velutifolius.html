Rubus micans var. velutifolius

Rubus micans var. velutifolius, commonly known as the velvet-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves that have a velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible berries.
