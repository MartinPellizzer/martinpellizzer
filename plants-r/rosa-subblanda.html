Rosa subblanda

Rosa subblanda, commonly known as the soft blushing rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals. Rosa subblanda produces rose hips that are orange-red and contain seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
