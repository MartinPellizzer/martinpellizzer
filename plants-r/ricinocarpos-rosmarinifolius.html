Ricinocarpos rosmarinifolius

Ricinocarpos rosmarinifolius, commonly known as the Cape rosemary, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features aromatic, needle-like leaves that are closely arranged along its branches, giving it a resemblance to rosemary. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
