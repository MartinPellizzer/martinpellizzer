Rubus rosulentus

Rubus rosulentus, commonly known as the rose-bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes roses and other flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are aggregate fruits formed from multiple tiny drupes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
