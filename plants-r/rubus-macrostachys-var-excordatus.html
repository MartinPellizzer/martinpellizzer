Rubus macrostachys var. excordatus

Rubus macrostachys var. excordatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are white to pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Rubus genus.

This species is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its edible berries.
