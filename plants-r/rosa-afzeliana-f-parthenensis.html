Rosa afzeliana f. parthenensis

Rosa afzeliana f. parthenensis, commonly known as the Parthenon rose, is a botanical variety of the Rosa afzeliana species.

It is characterized by its semi-climbing growth habit and produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is native to the eastern Mediterranean region, particularly Greece. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
