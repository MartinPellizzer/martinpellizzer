Restio tuberculatus

Restio tuberculatus, commonly known as the knobbed restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Restio tuberculatus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
