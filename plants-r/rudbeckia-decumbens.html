Rudbeckia decumbens

Rudbeckia decumbens, commonly known as the prairie coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, hairy stems that branch near the base, supporting clusters of bright yellow to orange disk flowers. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and often have a silvery-gray underside. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Rudbeckia decumbens is valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
