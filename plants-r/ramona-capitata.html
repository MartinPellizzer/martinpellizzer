Ramona capitata

Ramona capitata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with a prominent central boss. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
