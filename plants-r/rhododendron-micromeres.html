Rhododendron micromeres

Rhododendron micromeres, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically features broad, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
