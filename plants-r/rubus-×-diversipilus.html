Rubus × diversipilus

Rubus × diversipilus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is native to the western United States, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and its flowers are pink to white in color.

The fruit, which resembles a blackberry, is edible and often used in culinary applications.
