Ribes stenocarpum

Ribes stenocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-fruited gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color, which ripen in late summer. Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often grown for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
