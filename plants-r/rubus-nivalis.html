Rubus nivalis

Rubus nivalis, commonly known as the snowberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically small and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

Rubus nivalis is often found in moist, wooded areas and is adapted to cold climates.
