Rhamnus volubilis

Rhamnus volubilis, commonly known as the creeping buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread aggressively along forest floors and disturbed areas. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are oval-shaped with smooth margins and a slightly waxy texture.

Rhamnus volubilis is often found in moist, shaded environments and can form dense thickets, impacting native plant communities.
