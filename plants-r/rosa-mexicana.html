Rosa mexicana

Rosa mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican rose, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and dense foliage, typically growing in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are dark green, simple, and slightly serrated, while its fruits are red, hairy capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Rosa mexicana is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
