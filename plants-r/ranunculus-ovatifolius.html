Ranunculus ovatifolius

Ranunculus ovatifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a distinctive veining pattern.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
