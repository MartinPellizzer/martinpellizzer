Rondeletia conferta

Rondeletia conferta, commonly known as the velvetleaf rondeletia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture, while the flowers bloom in axillary clusters.

Rondeletia conferta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
