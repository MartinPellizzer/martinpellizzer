Rubus apricus subsp. hamatulus

Rubus apricus subsp.

hamatulus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy texture on the stems and leaves, which provides a unique botanical feature.
