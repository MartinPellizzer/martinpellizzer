Raphia mambillensis

Raphia mambillensis, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a large, tropical plant native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant is a member of the Arecaceae family and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats. Its fruit is a fibrous, three-lobed structure that contains a single seed.
