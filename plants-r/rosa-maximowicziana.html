Rosa maximowicziana

Rosa maximowicziana, commonly known as the Siberian rose or the Mongolian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including parts of Russia, Mongolia, and China. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several elliptical leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of many rose species.

Rosa maximowicziana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also studied for its potential in horticulture and botany.
