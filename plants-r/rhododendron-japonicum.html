Rhododendron japonicum

Rhododendron japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron japonicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
