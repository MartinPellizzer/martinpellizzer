Rubus diclinis

Rubus diclinis, commonly known as the two-flowered blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Rubus diclinis is native to parts of Asia and is often found in woodland or scrubland habitats.
