Rhamnidium glabrum

Rhamnidium glabrum, commonly known as the smooth rhamnus, is a shrub native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae, which includes various species of shrubs and trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and small, opposite leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits.

Rhamnidium glabrum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
