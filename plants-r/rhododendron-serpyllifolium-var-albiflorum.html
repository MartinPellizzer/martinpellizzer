Rhododendron serpyllifolium var. albiflorum

Rhododendron serpyllifolium var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered heath rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It features dense, leathery foliage that is typically dark green and evergreen, providing year-round structure. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are predominantly white, with some pink or pale lavender hues. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist climates with partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
