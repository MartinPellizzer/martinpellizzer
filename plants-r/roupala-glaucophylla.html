Roupala glaucophylla

Roupala glaucophylla, commonly known as the silver-leafed roupala, is a small evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves that have a distinctive silver underside, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in coastal and lowland forest environments, preferring well-drained soils and moderate to high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
