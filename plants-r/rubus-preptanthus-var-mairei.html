Rubus preptanthus var. mairei

Rubus preptanthus var. mairei, commonly known as the Maire's blackberry, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in forested areas and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and has been used in traditional Chinese cuisine and medicine.
