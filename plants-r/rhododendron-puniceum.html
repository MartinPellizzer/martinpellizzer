Rhododendron puniceum

Rhododendron puniceum, commonly known as the fireweed rhododendron, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that ranges from dark green to reddish-brown. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Rhododendron puniceum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded woodland settings.
