Ranunculus abbaianus

Ranunculus abbaianus, commonly known as the Abbaian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically has palmately divided leaves and produces small, cup-shaped flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
