Rhododendron silvaticum

Rhododendron silvaticum, commonly known as the wild rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron silvaticum is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the ecological structure of its native habitats.
