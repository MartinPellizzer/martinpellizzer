Renealmia congestifolia

Renealmia congestifolia, commonly known as the clustered reed, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and short, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy or swampy areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
