Rhaphidophora aurea

Rhaphidophora aurea, commonly known as the golden arrow plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of yellow and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Rhaphidophora aurea is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
