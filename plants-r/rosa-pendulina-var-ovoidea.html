Rosa pendulina var. ovoidea

Rosa pendulina var. ovoidea, commonly known as the pendulous rose or the egg-shaped rose, is a unique cultivar of the rose family.

It is characterized by its drooping, weeping growth habit, with long, arching stems that create a cascading effect. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green and finely divided, adding to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is prized in gardens for its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with partial sunlight.
