Rosmarinus officinalis f. laxiflorus

Rosmarinus officinalis f. laxiflorus, commonly known as the looseflower rosemary, is a botanical variant of the common rosemary.

It is characterized by its smaller, more loosely arranged flowers compared to the typical rosemary. This form exhibits a compact growth habit with aromatic needle-like leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, typical of the Rosmarinus genus.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild aromatic properties.
