Rubus erythrocomus

Rubus erythrocomus, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn red when ripe, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its fruit-bearing qualities.
