Rosa franzonii

Rosa franzonii, commonly known as the Franzoni rose, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

Rosa franzonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
