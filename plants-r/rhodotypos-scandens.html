Rhodotypos scandens

Rhodotypos scandens, commonly known as the creeping rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental and hardy landscaping plants.
