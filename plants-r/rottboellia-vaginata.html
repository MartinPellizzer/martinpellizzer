Rottboellia vaginata

Rottboellia vaginata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or wild millet, is a tropical annual grass species native to Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, pale green or brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, where it can become a weed. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in diverse conditions make it both ecologically significant and a challenge in crop management.

Rottboellia vaginata is also valued for its potential use in forage and as a source of biofuel.
