Ruellia umbrosa

Ruellia umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowplant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. Ruellia umbrosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

It is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate partial shade.
