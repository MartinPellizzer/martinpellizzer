Rosa canina var. subinermis

Rosa canina var. subinermis, commonly known as the dog rose with reduced prickles, is a variant of the dog rose species.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its thornless or nearly thornless stems. The plant produces fragrant pink or white flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red hips in the autumn. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with 5 to 7 leaflets, and the plant is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
