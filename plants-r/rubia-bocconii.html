Rubia bocconii

Rubia bocconii, commonly known as the Boccone's madder, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of shrubs and herbs. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Rubia bocconii is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
