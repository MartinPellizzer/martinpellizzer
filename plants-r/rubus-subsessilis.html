Rubus subsessilis

Rubus subsessilis, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to western North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland and open areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and local biodiversity.
