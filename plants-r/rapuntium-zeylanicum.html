Rapuntium zeylanicum

Rapuntium zeylanicum, commonly known as the Indian cress or wild mustard, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant has small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The plant is also known for its peppery taste, making it a potential edible species in some regions.
