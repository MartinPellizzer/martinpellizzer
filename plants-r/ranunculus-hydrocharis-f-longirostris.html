Ranunculus hydrocharis f. longirostris

Ranunculus hydrocharis f. longirostris, commonly known as the long-beaked water crowfoot, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic perennial herb belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, floating leaves that form a rosette on the water surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals, typically appearing in early spring. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an important species for aquatic ecosystems.
