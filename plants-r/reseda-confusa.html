Reseda confusa

Reseda confusa, commonly known as the Spanish flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Reseda confusa is often found in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
