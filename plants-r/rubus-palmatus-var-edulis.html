Rubus palmatus var. edulis

Rubus palmatus var. edulis, commonly known as the edible raspberry, is a variant of the bramble plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep red color. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants, making them a valued food source. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated margin, and the plant is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.
