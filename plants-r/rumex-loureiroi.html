Rumex loureiroi

Rumex loureiroi, commonly known as the Brazilian dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their characteristic swollen nodes and often edible leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a reddish or purple underside, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
