Ranunculus drouetii

Ranunculus drouetii, commonly known as the Drouet's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.

Ranunculus drouetii is also characterized by its short stem and is part of the Ranunculaceae family.
