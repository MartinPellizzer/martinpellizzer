Racosperma mimicum

Racosperma mimicum, commonly known as the mimic acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to mimic the appearance of other acacia species, a trait that may aid in its survival by deterring herbivores. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, and its root system helps prevent soil erosion.

Racosperma mimicum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
