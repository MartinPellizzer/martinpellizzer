Ranunculus auricomus subsp. aequalis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

aequalis, commonly known as the equal yellow buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features delicate, cup-shaped yellow flowers with five petals, and its leaves are palmately divided with a hairy texture. It has a short, stout rhizome and produces small, greenish-brown fruits that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Ranunculaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
