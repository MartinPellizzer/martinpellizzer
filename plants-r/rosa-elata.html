Rosa elata

Rosa elata, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its stems are often covered in thorns.

Rosa elata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
