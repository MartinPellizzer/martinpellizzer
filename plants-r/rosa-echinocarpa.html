Rosa echinocarpa

Rosa echinocarpa, commonly known as the spiny rose, is a species of shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant produces oval, red-orange fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Rosa echinocarpa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
