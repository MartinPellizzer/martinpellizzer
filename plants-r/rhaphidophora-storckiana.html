Rhaphidophora storckiana

Rhaphidophora storckiana, commonly known as the storckiana arrowroot, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with prominent veins. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered around the spadix, which is often covered in tiny, hair-like structures called rhaphides.

This species is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in humid environments.
