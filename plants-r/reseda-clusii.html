Reseda clusii

Reseda clusii, commonly known as the red balm or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Reseda clusii is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
