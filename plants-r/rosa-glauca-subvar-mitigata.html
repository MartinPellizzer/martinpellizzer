Rosa glauca subvar. mitigata

Rosa glauca subvar. mitigata, commonly known as the pale pink meadow rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with a soft, hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
