Rubus suavifolius var. subvelutinus

Rubus suavifolius var. subvelutinus, commonly known as the velvet-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with a soft, velvety texture on the underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
