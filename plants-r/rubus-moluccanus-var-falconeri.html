Rubus moluccanus var. falconeri

Rubus moluccanus var. falconeri, commonly known as the Moluccan blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Moluccas in Indonesia.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
