Rubus × perarduus

Rubus × perarduus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
