Rubus perinvisus

Rubus perinvisus, commonly known as the invisible raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Due to its elusive appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and poorly understood species.
