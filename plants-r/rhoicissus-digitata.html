Rhoicissus digitata

Rhoicissus digitata, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, orange berries. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth makes it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed in certain regions.
