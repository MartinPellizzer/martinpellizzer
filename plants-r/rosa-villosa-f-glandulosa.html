Rosa villosa f. glandulosa

Rosa villosa f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular hairy rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa villosa species.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in glandular trichomes. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five to seven petals. This plant is native to regions of North America and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance.
