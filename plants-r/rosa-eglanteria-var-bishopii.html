Rosa eglanteria var. bishopii

Rosa eglanteria var. bishopii, commonly known as the Bishop's rose, is a shrub-like species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has deeply lobed, serrated leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of down. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suited for arid or semi-arid climates.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a notable species in horticulture.
