Rubus micans var. isophyllus

Rubus micans var. isophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
