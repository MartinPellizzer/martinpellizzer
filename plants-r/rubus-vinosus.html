Rubus vinosus

Rubus vinosus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are edible and have a sweet-tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

Rubus vinosus is also known for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
