Rubus contritidens

Rubus contritidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, dark purple to black fruits. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupes, which are typically harvested when fully ripe. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated edge and a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.
