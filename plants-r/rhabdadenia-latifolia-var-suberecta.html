Rhabdadenia latifolia var. suberecta

Rhabdadenia latifolia var. suberecta, commonly known as the broad-leaved rock fig, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability to harsh conditions make it a valuable species in xerophytic ecosystems.
