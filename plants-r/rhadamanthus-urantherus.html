Rhadamanthus urantherus

Rhadamanthus urantherus, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the wet season. The plant has long, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that help it thrive in humid, shaded environments. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires careful conservation efforts.
