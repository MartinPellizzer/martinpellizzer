Rubus congruus

Rubus congruus, commonly known as the creeping blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
