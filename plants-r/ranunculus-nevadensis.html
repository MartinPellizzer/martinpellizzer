Ranunculus nevadensis

Ranunculus nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred petals and is typically found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant has a short stem with basal leaves that are palmately lobed. It produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ranunculus nevadensis is adapted to harsh mountain environments and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.
