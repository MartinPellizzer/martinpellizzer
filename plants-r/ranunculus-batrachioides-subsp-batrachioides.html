Ranunculus batrachioides subsp. batrachioides

Ranunculus batrachioides subsp.

batrachioides, commonly known as the frog's-bit, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features small, bright yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often submerged or floating on water surfaces. It thrives in wetland habitats, such as marshes and ponds, and is often found in shallow water.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a notable aquatic plant in its native range.
