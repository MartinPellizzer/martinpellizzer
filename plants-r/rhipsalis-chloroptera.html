Rhipsalis chloroptera

Rhipsalis chloroptera, commonly known as the green-leafed mistletoe cactus, is a climbing cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems that often hang in cascading patterns, resembling a foliage curtain. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are small, oval, and often covered in fine, white hairs.

Rhipsalis chloroptera is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
