Rubus acuarius

Rubus acuarius, commonly known as the sharpleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in woodland edges and moist soils, often forming dense thickets.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
