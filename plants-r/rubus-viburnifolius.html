Rubus viburnifolius

Rubus viburnifolius, commonly known as the blackcap raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red or black berries that are edible and often used in jams and wines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and culinary purposes.
