Rosa opsostemma plena

Rosa opsostemma plena, commonly known as the full-blown rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, double-layered petals that give it a lush, ornamental appearance. Native to certain regions of Asia, this plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers typically bloom in spring and summer, producing a fragrant display that attracts pollinators.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
