Rosa jacquemontii

Rosa jacquemontii, commonly known as the Jacquemont's rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features woody stems with thorns and is characterized by its dark green, doubly serrated leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical name honors the French botanist Antoine Jacques Jacquin, who contributed to the study of plants.

Rosa jacquemontii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
