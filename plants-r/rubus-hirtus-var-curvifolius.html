Rubus hirtus var. curvifolius

Rubus hirtus var. curvifolius, commonly known as the curly-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, and the plant thrives in moist, wooded areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, though it can be invasive in some regions.
