Rubus bavaricus

Rubus bavaricus, commonly known as the Bavarian blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberries and blackberries.
