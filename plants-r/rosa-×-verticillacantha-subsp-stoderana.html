Rosa × verticillacantha subsp. stoderana

Rosa × verticillacantha subsp.

stoderana, commonly known as the Stodera rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and abundant, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to deep pink.

The plant features thorny stems and serrated, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
