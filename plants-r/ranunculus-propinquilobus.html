Ranunculus propinquilobus

Ranunculus propinquilobus, commonly known as the close relative of the buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, grassy areas and is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow or white flowers. The plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often colorful members. It has a short life cycle and is often found in disturbed soils or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in certain regions.
