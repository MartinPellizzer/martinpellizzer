Rubus chlorophyllus var. krotoschinensis

Rubus chlorophyllus var. krotoschinensis, commonly known as the Krotoschin raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to specific regions of Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, though some cultivars may exhibit different hues. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Botanically, it is noted for its high chlorophyll content, which contributes to its vibrant green foliage.
