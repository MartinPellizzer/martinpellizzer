Rhodiola scopolii

Rhodiola scopolii, commonly known as the Armenian rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported adaptogenic properties.

Rhodiola scopolii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
