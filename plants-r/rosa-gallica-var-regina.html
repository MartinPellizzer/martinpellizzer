Rosa gallica var. regina

Rosa gallica var. regina, commonly known as the Queen's rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and lobed, providing a lush backdrop to the blooms.

Rosa gallica var. regina is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
