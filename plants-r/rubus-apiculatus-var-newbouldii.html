Rubus apiculatus var. newbouldii

Rubus apiculatus var. newbouldii, commonly known as Newbould's bramble, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black in color, though they are often bitter and not widely consumed. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in areas with acidic soils.

This variant is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure compared to other Rubus species.
