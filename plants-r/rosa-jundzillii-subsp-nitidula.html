Rosa jundzillii subsp. nitidula

Rosa jundzillii subsp.

nitidula, commonly known as the glossy blushing rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers.

The leaves are compound, with elliptical leaflets that are covered in a fine, glossy pubescence. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual beauty.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in cooler climates.
