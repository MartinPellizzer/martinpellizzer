Rubus rubricaulis

Rubus rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems that turn reddish in color as the plant matures. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
