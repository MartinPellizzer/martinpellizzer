Rosa adenosepala

Rosa adenosepala, commonly known as the adenosepal rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and fragrant, pink to red flowers. It produces small, hairy fruits called achenes, which are typical of the Rosaceae family.

Rosa adenosepala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
