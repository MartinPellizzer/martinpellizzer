Rhynchospora debilis

Rhynchospora debilis, commonly known as the slender spikerush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to wetland habitats, this plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, and it plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland environment.

Rhynchospora debilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens and conservation areas.
