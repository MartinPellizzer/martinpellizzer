Ruizia granulata

Ruizia granulata, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its botanical features include compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and a distinctive, branched root system.

Ruizia granulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
