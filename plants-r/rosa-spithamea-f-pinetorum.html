Rosa spithamea f. pinetorum

Rosa spithamea f. pinetorum, commonly known as the pine forest rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa spithamea.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, glossy foliage. The flowers are typically small, fragrant, and pink to white in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. This form is adapted to grow in shaded, moist environments, often found in pine forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a desirable plant for gardens with partial shade.
