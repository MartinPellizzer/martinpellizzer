Rubus thyrsanthus var. adenophorus

Rubus thyrsanthus var. adenophorus, commonly known as the hairy passionfruit, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces bright yellow or orange fruits that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters in diameter. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with five petals and a distinctive five-lobed calyx.

The botanical features of this plant make it well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.
