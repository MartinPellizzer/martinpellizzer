Raphanus sinapis-officinalis

Raphanus sinapis-officinalis, commonly known as the common radish, is a biennial or annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its fleshy, edible taproot and upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, cruciform flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lobed and alternate, often with a slightly peppery taste.

Raphanus sinapis-officinalis is cultivated for its root, which is used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
