Rosa pendulina f. vindobonensis

Rosa pendulina f. vindobonensis, commonly known as the Vienna climbing rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa pendulina species.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and long, arching stems that make it well-suited for trellises or fences. The flowers are typically pink to reddish in color, with a delicate, fragrant scent. This form is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
