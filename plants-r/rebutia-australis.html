Rebutia australis

Rebutia australis, commonly known as the Australian moon cactus, is a succulent native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its flowers, which can be yellow, orange, or red, bloom in late spring or early summer.

Rebutia australis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
