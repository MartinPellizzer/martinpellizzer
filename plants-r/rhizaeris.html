Rhizaeris

Rhizaeris, commonly known as the moonroot plant, is a unique species characterized by its extensive underground rhizome system.

This rhizome network allows the plant to spread rapidly and colonize new areas, making it both resilient and invasive in certain environments. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture in arid conditions.

Rhizaeris thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
