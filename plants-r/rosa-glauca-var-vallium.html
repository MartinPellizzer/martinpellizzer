Rosa glauca var. vallium

Rosa glauca var. vallium, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy layer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
