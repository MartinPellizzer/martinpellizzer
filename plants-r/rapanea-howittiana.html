Rapanea howittiana

Rapanea howittiana, commonly known as the Howitt's inkberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves that are typically oval in shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
