Rapanea multibracteata

Rapanea multibracteata, commonly known as the African snowbush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences.

The fruit is a fleshy, red drupe that contains a single seed, making it an important food source for birds and small mammals.
