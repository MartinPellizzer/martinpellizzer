Ruellia reptans

Ruellia reptans, commonly known as the spreading ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually arranged in clusters, producing a showy display of blue or purple hues.

Ruellia reptans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
