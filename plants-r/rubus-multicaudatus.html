Rubus multicaudatus

Rubus multicaudatus, commonly known as the many-tailed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with particular attention given to its unique inflorescence structure.
