Rubus cochlearis

Rubus cochlearis, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a species of brambles native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregated fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
