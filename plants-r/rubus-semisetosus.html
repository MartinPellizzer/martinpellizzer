Rubus semisetosus

Rubus semisetosus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, dark red or black berries. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tart or bitter flavor.
