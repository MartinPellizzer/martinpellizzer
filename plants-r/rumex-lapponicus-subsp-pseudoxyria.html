Rumex lapponicus subsp. pseudoxyria

Rumex lapponicus subsp.

pseudoxyria, commonly known as the Lapland sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in tundra and boreal habitats.

The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences, and it produces triangular, winged fruits.

It is valued for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor.
