Rubus caesius var. waisbeckeri

Rubus caesius var. waisbeckeri, commonly known as the Waisbecker's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small to medium in size. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist woodland areas.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular in local cuisines and traditional uses.
