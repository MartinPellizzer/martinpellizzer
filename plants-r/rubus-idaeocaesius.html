Rubus idaeocaesius

Rubus idaeocaesius, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible blackberries in the summer. Its berries are rich in antioxidants and have been historically used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
