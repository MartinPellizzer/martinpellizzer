Rubus × acidacanthus

Rubus × acidacanthus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a mix of traits from its parent species, Rubus and Acidacanthus.
