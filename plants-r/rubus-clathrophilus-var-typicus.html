Rubus clathrophilus var. typicus

Rubus clathrophilus var. typicus, commonly known as the climbing raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically red or black when mature. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it thrives in woodland or shaded environments.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
