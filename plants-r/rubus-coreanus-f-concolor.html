Rubus coreanus f. concolor

Rubus coreanus f. concolor, commonly known as the Korean raspberry or concolor raspberry, is a flowering plant species native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year producing leaves and the second year bearing flowers and fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value and potential for cultivation make it a subject of interest in horticulture and botanical studies.
