Rosa canina f. euoxyphylla

Rosa canina f. euoxyphylla, commonly known as the European dog rose, is a cultivar of the dog rose species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa genus. The plant produces small, oval-shaped berries that are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine. Its foliage is dense and dark green, providing ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and wildlife.
