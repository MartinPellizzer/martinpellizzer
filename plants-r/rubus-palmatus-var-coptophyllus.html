Rubus palmatus var. coptophyllus

Rubus palmatus var. coptophyllus, commonly known as the red-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive red foliage, which contrasts with its dark purple to black berries. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
