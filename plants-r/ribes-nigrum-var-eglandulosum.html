Ribes nigrum var. eglandulosum

Ribes nigrum var. eglandulosum, commonly known as the glandless gooseberry, is a variant of the common gooseberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to North America, characterized by its smooth, glandless stems and leaves. The plant produces small, tart berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in culinary applications.
