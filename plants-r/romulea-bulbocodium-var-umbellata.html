Romulea bulbocodium var. umbellata

Romulea bulbocodium var. umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella bellflower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a short, fibrous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique floral display and compact growth habit make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
