Rosa viscaria var. mesocarpa

Rosa viscaria var. mesocarpa, commonly known as the hairy rose or the bristly rose, is a variant of the Rosa viscaria species.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in temperate climates, characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple leaflets with a hairy texture, which is a distinguishing feature.

The fruit, known as a hip, is a characteristic feature of the rose family and contains seeds surrounded by a fleshy, red outer layer.
