Rosa × brachtii

Rosa × brachtii, commonly known as the Bracht's rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy, semi-double to double petals. Its leaves are dark green, serrated, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa × brachtii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
