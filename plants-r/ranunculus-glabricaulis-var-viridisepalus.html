Ranunculus glabricaulis var. viridisepalus

Ranunculus glabricaulis var. viridisepalus, commonly known as the green-sepal ranunculus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with five green sepals that are often mistaken for petals. The plant has smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are trifoliate, with three lobes. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a subtle yet striking presence to its natural habitat.
