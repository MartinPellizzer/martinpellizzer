Ranunculus sikkimensis

Ranunculus sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows at high altitudes. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a distinctive, serrated appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
