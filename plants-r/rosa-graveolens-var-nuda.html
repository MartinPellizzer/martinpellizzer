Rosa graveolens var. nuda

Rosa graveolens var. nuda, commonly known as the naked rose, is a species of wild rose native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, thornless stems and fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it produces small, red or orange hips in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
