Rubus tomentosus var. cerodermis

Rubus tomentosus var. cerodermis, commonly known as the woolly blackberry.

This variant is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and thorny stems. It produces small, dark berries that are typically edible but often have a tart flavor. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique adaptations for survival in various environments.
