Rubus podophyllos var. sericiflorus

Rubus podophyllos var. sericiflorus, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and compound leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by red, edible berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
