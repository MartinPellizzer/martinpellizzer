Roegneria aliena

Roegneria aliena, commonly known as the alien grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and North America.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, thriving in a variety of soil types. The plant produces spikelets that are often loosely arranged, with elongated lemma and palea structures typical of the Poaceae family. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit.

Roegneria aliena is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid spread and adaptability.
