Rhododendron cinnabarinum subsp. xanthocodon

Rhododendron cinnabarinum subsp.

xanthocodon, commonly known as the yellow-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its striking orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest habitats at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, and it produces small, elliptical berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
