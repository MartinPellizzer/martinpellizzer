Richterago lanata

Richterago lanata, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name "lanata," meaning "woolly" in Latin. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Richterago lanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
