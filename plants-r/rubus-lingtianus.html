Rubus lingtianus

Rubus lingtianus, commonly known as the Lingtian bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
