Rubus superbus var. aciculifer

Rubus superbus var. aciculifer, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and dense clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces showy white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
