Rhodiola bupleuroides var. parva

Rhodiola bupleuroides var. parva, commonly known as the dwarf rosy rhododendron, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, compact foliage with leathery, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
