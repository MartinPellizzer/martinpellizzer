Rosa × reversa var. genuina

Rosa × reversa var. genuina, commonly known as the double-flowered climbing rose, is a hybrid species that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its large, fully double flowers with a high number of petal layers, often appearing as a dense, velvety mass. The plant is a vigorous climber with thorned stems and dark green, serrated leaves.

Its flowers typically range in color from white to deep pink, depending on the specific cultivar. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage and blooms.
