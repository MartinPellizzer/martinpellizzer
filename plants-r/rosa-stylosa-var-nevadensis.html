Rosa stylosa var. nevadensis

Rosa stylosa var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody stem structure typical of many rose species.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
