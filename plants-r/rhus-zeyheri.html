Rhus zeyheri

Rhus zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's buckthorn, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stems. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that attract birds and other wildlife.

Rhus zeyheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
