Rhododendron × intermedium var. halense

Rhododendron × intermedium var. halense, commonly known as the Halense azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It prefers partial to full shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate a variety of environmental conditions.
