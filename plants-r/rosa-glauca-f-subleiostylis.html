Rosa glauca f. subleiostylis

Rosa glauca f. subleiostylis, commonly known as the blue rose with subleiostylis form, is a botanical variant of the blue rose (Rosa glauca).

This form is characterized by its pale blue flowers and slightly modified stigmatic structures. It is a cultivar or form that exhibits unique floral morphology, including a reduced stigma that is often more flattened or broader than in typical roses. The plant is typically found in specific geographic regions where its parent species, Rosa glauca, is native.

Its botanical interest lies in its distinctive floral features and potential for horticultural study.
