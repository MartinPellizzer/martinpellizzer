Rubus ulmifolius var. mucronifolius

Rubus ulmifolius var. mucronifolius, commonly known as the blunt-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically rounded and have a distinctive blunt tip, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its culinary uses.
