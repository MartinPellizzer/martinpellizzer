Rhaphidospora vestita

Rhaphidospora vestita, commonly known as the hairy spore plant, is a small, delicate fern-like plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that give it a soft, lace-like appearance. The plant produces spores in small, clustered structures called sporangia, which are covered in fine, hairy structures. Rhaphidospora vestita thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and spore-producing features make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
