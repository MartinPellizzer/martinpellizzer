Rubus pubescens subsp. aduncispinus

Rubus pubescens subsp.

aduncispinus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry or a type of bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or coastal habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, known for its ecological and horticultural significance.
