Rosa aleutensis

Rosa aleutensis, commonly known as the Aleutian rose, is a species native to the Aleutian Islands and surrounding regions of Alaska.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in coastal and subarctic environments. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in the summer. Its hips, or fruit, are orange-red and contain numerous seeds.

Rosa aleutensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
