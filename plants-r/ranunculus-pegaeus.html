Ranunculus pegaeus

Ranunculus pegaeus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. It has a short growing season and is adapted to cold climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
