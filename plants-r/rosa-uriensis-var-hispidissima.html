Rosa uriensis var. hispidissima

Rosa uriensis var. hispidissima, commonly known as the rough-stemmed wild rose, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features upright stems covered in short, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets that are serrated and hairy beneath.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for pollinators.
