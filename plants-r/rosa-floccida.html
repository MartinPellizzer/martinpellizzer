Rosa floccida

Rosa floccida, commonly known as the fluffball rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive fluffy, white to pale pink blooms that resemble cotton balls, which appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and its stems are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. Rosa floccida is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
