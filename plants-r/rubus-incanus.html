Rubus incanus

Rubus incanus, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible but can be somewhat tart. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Rubus incanus is also known for its prickly stems and is part of the Rubiaceae family.
