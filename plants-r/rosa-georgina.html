Rosa georgina

Rosa georgina, commonly known as the Georgeina rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, specifically Argentina and Chile, and is characterized by its large, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with a deep, velvety texture.

Rosa georgina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
