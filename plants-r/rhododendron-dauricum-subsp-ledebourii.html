Rhododendron dauricum subsp. ledebourii

Rhododendron dauricum subsp.

ledebourii, commonly known as the Daurian ledebour rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It typically grows in forested areas and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers in early spring. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
