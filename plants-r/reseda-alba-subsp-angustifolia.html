Reseda alba subsp. angustifolia

Reseda alba subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
