Robinia dubia

Robinia dubia, commonly known as the white-leaf false acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a distinctive white or pale green color. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in racemes during the spring, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Robinia dubia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
