Ruellia acetabula

Ruellia acetabula, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early fall. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Ruellia acetabula is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
