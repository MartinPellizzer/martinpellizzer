Rubus nordicus

Rubus nordicus, commonly known as the northern raspberry, is a shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in cold climates and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Rubus nordicus plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to soil stability.
