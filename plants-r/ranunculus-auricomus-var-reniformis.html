Ranunculus auricomus var. reniformis

Ranunculus auricomus var. reniformis, commonly known as the broad-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals that are often described as resembling small shields. The plant has a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves, which are typically lobed and have a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early spring blooms.
