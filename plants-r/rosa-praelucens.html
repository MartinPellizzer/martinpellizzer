Rosa praelucens

Rosa praelucens, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers in early summer. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Rosa praelucens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
