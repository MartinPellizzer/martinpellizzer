Rubus missus

Rubus missus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to western North America, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its leaves are alternate, palmately compound, and have a serrated margin.

Rubus missus is often found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
