Rubus oliveri

Rubus oliveri, commonly known as Oliver's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many other species of shrubs and small trees. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple tiny drupes. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

The fruit of Rubus oliveri is edible and has a flavor similar to other blackberries, though it is less commonly cultivated.
