Ranunculus intervallicarens

Ranunculus intervallicarens, commonly known as the intervallic barren crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers and divided leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with five to eight petals and numerous stamens.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
