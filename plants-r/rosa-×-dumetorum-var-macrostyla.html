Rosa × dumetorum var. macrostyla

Rosa × dumetorum var. macrostyla, commonly known as the large-styled dog rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in horticulture.
