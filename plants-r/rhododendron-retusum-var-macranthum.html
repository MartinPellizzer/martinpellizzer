Rhododendron retusum var. macranthum

Rhododendron retusum var. macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The flowers are typically pink to reddish in color, with a distinctive, elongated shape. This variant is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in cooler, moist environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
