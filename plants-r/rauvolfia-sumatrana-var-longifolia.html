Rauvolfia sumatrana var. longifolia

Rauvolfia sumatrana var. longifolia, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its alkaloid content, which has been studied for potential medicinal uses.
