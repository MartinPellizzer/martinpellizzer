Rinorea verschuerenii

Rinorea verschuerenii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its growth habit is usually compact, with multiple stems forming a bushy structure.

Rinorea verschuerenii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
