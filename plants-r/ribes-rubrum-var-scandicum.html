Ribes rubrum var. scandicum

Ribes rubrum var. scandicum, commonly known as the red currant bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically reddish-brown underneath, and it produces clusters of bright red, edible berries in the summer. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for use in jams and preserves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

The berries are also a food source for birds and other wildlife.
