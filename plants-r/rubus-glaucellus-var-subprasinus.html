Rubus glaucellus var. subprasinus

Rubus glaucellus var. subprasinus, commonly known as the greenleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, aggregate fruits that are typically black when mature. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments. It has a trailing growth habit and produces small white flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
