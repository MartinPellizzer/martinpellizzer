Rubus schmidelyanus var. marginicola

Rubus schmidelyanus var. marginicola, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high elevations.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the harsh conditions of its alpine habitat.
