Ranunculus gusmannii var. nanus

Ranunculus gusmannii var. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and small, bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and as a subject of botanical study.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
