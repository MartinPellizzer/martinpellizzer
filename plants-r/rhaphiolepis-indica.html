Rhaphiolepis indica

Rhaphiolepis indica, commonly known as the Japanese rose or evergreen cotoneaster, is a flowering shrub native to East Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers in spring. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and adaptability. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

The berries it produces are typically red or orange and serve as a food source for birds.
