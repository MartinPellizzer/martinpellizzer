Rubus serpens subsp. humorosus

Rubus serpens subsp.

humorosus, commonly known as the laughing blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to parts of South America. It produces small, dark berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its name reflects the plant's cheerful appearance and the joy it brings to those who harvest its fruit.
