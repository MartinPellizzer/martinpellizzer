Ricinus macrocarpus proles indicus

Ricinus macrocarpus proles indicus, commonly known as the Indian castor plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its large, palmate leaves and distinctive spiny capsules. The plant produces toxic seeds that contain ricin, a potent toxin. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its historical use in traditional medicine.
