Ranunculus flabellaris f. riparius

Ranunculus flabellaris f. riparius, commonly known as the river ranunculus, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils near rivers and streams. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers usually have five to eight sepals that resemble petals.

It is an herbaceous perennial that blooms in spring and early summer.
