Rosa stylosa subvar. levistyla

Rosa stylosa subvar. levistyla, commonly known as the slender-stemmed rock rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, slender stems covered in dense, thorny prickles. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets arranged oppositely along the stems.

This variety is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient and ornamental plant in horticulture.
