Rubus turicensis

Rubus turicensis, commonly known as the Swiss blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Alps and parts of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
