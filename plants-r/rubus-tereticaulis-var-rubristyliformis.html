Rubus tereticaulis var. rubristyliformis

Rubus tereticaulis var. rubristyliformis, commonly known as the red-styled raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the variety is distinguished by its distinctive red styles.

This species is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
