Rubus mingetsensis

Rubus mingetsensis, commonly known as the Mingtze blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs known for their edible fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are small and white or pink in color.

The fruit, a aggregate of small drupes, is often dark purple or black when ripe and is used in traditional Chinese medicine and local cuisine.
