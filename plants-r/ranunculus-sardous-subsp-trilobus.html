Ranunculus sardous subsp. trilobus

Ranunculus sardous subsp.

trilobus, commonly known as the Sardinian crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with three deeply lobed leaflets, and its flowers typically have five to eight sepals that resemble petals. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
