Rubus vestitus var. grandifolius

Rubus vestitus var. grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leafed bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits, which are aggregate berries, are edible and have a tart flavor.

This variant is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils across temperate regions.
