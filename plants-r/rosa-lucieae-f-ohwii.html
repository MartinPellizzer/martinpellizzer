Rosa lucieae f. ohwii

Rosa lucieae f. ohwii, commonly known as the Ohwi's rose, is a rare and unique cultivar within the rose family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically features thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It is native to Japan and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and specific growing conditions, it is considered a cultivar of particular botanical interest.
