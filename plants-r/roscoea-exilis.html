Roscoea exilis

Roscoea exilis, commonly known as the slender roscoea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern and southeastern Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces striking, arrowhead-shaped leaves and tall, showy inflorescences with pale yellow to orange-red flowers. It thrives in cool, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Roscoea exilis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
