Racosperma adinophyllum

Racosperma adinophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
