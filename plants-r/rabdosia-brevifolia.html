Rabdosia brevifolia

Rabdosia brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, opposite leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Rabdosia brevifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
