Rubus insons

Rubus insons, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to western North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is typically found in woodland and shrubland habitats.

Rubus insons is a perennial shrub that can grow up to several meters in height.
