Rubus leptocaulon var. aciphyllus

Rubus leptocaulon var. aciphyllus, commonly known as the slender-stemmed bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and reduced leaves, which are often small and leathery. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals, and it produces edible berries that are small and tart.

This variant is notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptability within its native range.
