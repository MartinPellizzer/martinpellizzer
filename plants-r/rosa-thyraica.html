Rosa thyraica

Rosa thyraica, commonly known as the Turkish rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of Turkey, Greece, and the Balkans. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rosa thyraica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
