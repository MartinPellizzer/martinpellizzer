Rudbeckia glabra

Rudbeckia glabra, commonly known as the smooth coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces bright yellow to orange disk flowers surrounded by ray florets, which bloom in late summer to early fall. Rudbeckia glabra thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its ecological value includes providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
