Ronabea arborea

Ronabea arborea, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and can grow up to 20 meters in height. The tree has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in panicles, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

Ronabea arborea is adapted to dry climates and is often found in savanna woodlands.
