Rubus anglesensis

Rubus anglesensis, commonly known as the Anglesensian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
