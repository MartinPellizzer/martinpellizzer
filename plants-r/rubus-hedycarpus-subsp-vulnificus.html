Rubus hedycarpus subsp. vulnificus

Rubus hedycarpus subsp.

vulnificus, commonly known as the prickly pear raspberry, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
