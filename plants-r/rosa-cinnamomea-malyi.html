Rosa cinnamomea malyi

Rosa cinnamomea malyi, commonly known as the Malyi cinnamon rose, is a rare and striking species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, fragrant flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow, often with a cinnamon-like scent. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are covered in thorns. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists.
