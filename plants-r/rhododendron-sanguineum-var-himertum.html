Rhododendron sanguineum var. himertum

Rhododendron sanguineum var. himertum, commonly known as the Himertum rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist climates with partial to full shade. Its botanical features include sturdy, branched stems and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
