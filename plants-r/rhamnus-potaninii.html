Rhamnus potaninii

Rhamnus potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, often at high altitudes. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a pale underside. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dark berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.
