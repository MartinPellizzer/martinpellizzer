Rosa campestris var. pimpinellifolia

Rosa campestris var. pimpinellifolia, commonly known as the meadow rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets that are oval and serrated.

The fruit is a red, fuzzy hip that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.
