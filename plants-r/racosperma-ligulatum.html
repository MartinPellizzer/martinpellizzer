Racosperma ligulatum

Racosperma ligulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in coastal regions and dry woodlands.

Racosperma ligulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting the surrounding soil.
