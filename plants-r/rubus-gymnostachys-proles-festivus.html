Rubus gymnostachys proles festivus

Rubus gymnostachys proles festivus, commonly known as the festival bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
