Rubus beamanii

Rubus beamanii, commonly known as the Beaman's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, thorny stems and has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
