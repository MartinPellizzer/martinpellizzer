Rosa communis

Rosa communis, commonly known as the common rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, pinnate leaves, and showy, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant is native to Asia, Europe, and North Africa, and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value. Rosa communis produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters.
