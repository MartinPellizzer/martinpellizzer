Ranunculus bulbosus subsp. cacuminalis

Ranunculus bulbosus subsp.

cacuminalis, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive green center.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It has a short, rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
