Roella bracteata

Roella bracteata, commonly known as the blue oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland ecosystems. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Roella bracteata is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
