Rosa cinnamomea subsp. blanda

Rosa cinnamomea subsp.

blanda, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a shrub-like plant native to China and Korea. It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom continuously throughout the growing season.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red to black fruits known as hips. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable.
