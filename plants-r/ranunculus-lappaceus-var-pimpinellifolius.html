Ranunculus lappaceus var. pimpinellifolius

Ranunculus lappaceus var. pimpinellifolius, commonly known as the common garden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features distinctive yellow flowers with a central cluster of stamens and a broad, showy petal. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often mistaken for those of the cow parsley. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and is often used in floral arrangements.
