Rubus schleicheri var. hamulifer

Rubus schleicheri var. hamulifer, commonly known as the hooked bramble, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated margins, and the plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, achenes. It is native to parts of East Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates.

This plant is often found in woodland edges and rocky slopes, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
