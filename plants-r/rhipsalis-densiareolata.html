Rhipsalis densiareolata

Rhipsalis densiareolata, commonly known as the dense areola cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in dense, rounded areolae, which are specialized structures for gas exchange. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, typically in spring or summer. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Rhipsalis densiareolata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is well-suited for indoor cultivation.
