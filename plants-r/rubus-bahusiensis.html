Rubus bahusiensis

Rubus bahusiensis, commonly known as the Bahusi raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
