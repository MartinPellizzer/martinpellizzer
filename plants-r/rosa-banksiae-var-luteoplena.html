Rosa banksiae var. luteoplena

Rosa banksiae var. luteoplena, commonly known as the golden banksia rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the United Kingdom.

It is characterized by its vibrant, semi-double to double yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, leathery foliage. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking flower color and compact growth habit.
