Rubus yabei f. eglandulosus

Rubus yabei f. eglandulosus, commonly known as the glandular bramble, is a variant of the Rubus genus characterized by its distinctive glandular structures on its stems and leaves.

This plant is native to parts of East Asia, particularly Japan, where it thrives in temperate forest environments. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. The plant is often found growing in shaded, moist areas, which supports its growth and development.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
