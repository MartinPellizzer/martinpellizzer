Rubus × fruticetorum

Rubus × fruticetorum, commonly known as blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small drupes, which are often referred to as berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its vigorous growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
