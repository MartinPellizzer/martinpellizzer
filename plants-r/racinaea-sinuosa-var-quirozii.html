Racinaea sinuosa var. quirozii

Racinaea sinuosa var. quirozii, commonly known as the Quito slipper orchid, is a rare and exotic orchid species native to the Andean region of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has fleshy, dark green leaves and thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
