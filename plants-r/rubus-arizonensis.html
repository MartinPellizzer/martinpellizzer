Rubus arizonensis

Rubus arizonensis, commonly known as the Arizona blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is closely related to other bramble species.
