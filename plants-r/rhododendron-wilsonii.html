Rhododendron wilsonii

Rhododendron wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron wilsonii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
