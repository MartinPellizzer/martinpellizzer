Racosperma rubidum

Racosperma rubidum, commonly known as the crimson wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bright red flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

Racosperma rubidum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
