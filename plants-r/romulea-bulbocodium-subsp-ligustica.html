Romulea bulbocodium subsp. ligustica

Romulea bulbocodium subsp.

ligustica, commonly known as the Ligustic romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale yellow.

The plant is adapted to rocky and limestone soils, often found in alpine and subalpine regions. Its bulbs serve as storage organs, allowing it to survive harsh winters.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ease of cultivation in well-drained conditions.
