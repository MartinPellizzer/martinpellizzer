Rosa × hibernica var. setonensis

Rosa × hibernica var. setonensis, commonly known as the Seton's rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in thorny projections. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticultural heritage.
