Reseda luteola var. crispata

Reseda luteola var. crispata, commonly known as golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is part of the family Brassicaceae, which includes many other flowering plants with small, cruciform flowers.
