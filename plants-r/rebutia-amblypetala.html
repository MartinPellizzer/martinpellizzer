Rebutia amblypetala

Rebutia amblypetala, commonly known as the blunt-leaved euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and thick, fleshy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Rebutia amblypetala is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
