Rosa coriifolia f. parcensis

Rosa coriifolia f. parcensis, commonly known as the Parcens rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters along the stems. The plant has a distinctive, branched growth habit with thornless, hairy stems and oval, dark green leaves. Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a variety of garden conditions.
