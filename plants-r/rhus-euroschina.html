Rhus euroschina

Rhus euroschina, commonly known as the Brazilian sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like poison ivy and mango. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a globose drupe that turns red when mature, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Rhus euroschina is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
