Rhodiola purpureoviridis subsp. purpureoviridis

Rhodiola purpureoviridis subsp.

purpureoviridis, commonly known as the purple-veined rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with fleshy, reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in cool, alpine environments and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions. Rhodiola purpureoviridis subsp.

purpureoviridis is valued for its resilience and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported adaptogenic properties.
