Rosa tomentella var. beatricis

Rosa tomentella var. beatricis, commonly known as the Beatrice's woolly rose, is a compact, shrubby species native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
