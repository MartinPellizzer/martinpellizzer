Rosa sepium subsp. klukii

Rosa sepium subsp.

klukii, commonly known as the Kluk's rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves.

The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
