Ranunculus suborbicularis

Ranunculus suborbicularis, commonly known as the suborbicular buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petal-like sepals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and has a basal rosette of lobed, hairy leaves. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy and often toxic members.
