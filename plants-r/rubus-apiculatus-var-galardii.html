Rubus apiculatus var. galardii

Rubus apiculatus var. galardii, commonly known as the Galardii blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that are typically small to medium in size. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The berries have a rich, sweet flavor and are used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
