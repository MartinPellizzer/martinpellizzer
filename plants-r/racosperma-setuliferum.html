Racosperma setuliferum

Racosperma setuliferum, commonly known as the serrated wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for native pollinators.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
