Rosa mollissima var. scheutzii

Rosa mollissima var. scheutzii, commonly known as the Scheutz's silky rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in cooler climates.

This variety is notable for its unique floral structure and is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
