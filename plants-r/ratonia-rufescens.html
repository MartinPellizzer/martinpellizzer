Ratonia rufescens

Ratonia rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed ratonia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody, reddish stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
