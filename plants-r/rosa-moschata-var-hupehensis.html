Rosa moschata var. hupehensis

Rosa moschata var. hupehensis, commonly known as the Hupeh rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces red, hairy fruits known as rose hips. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.

This variety is well-suited to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
