Rosa vosagiaca var. subhispida

Rosa vosagiaca var. subhispida, commonly known as the Vosagian rose, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces hips, which are reddish berries containing seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species in horticulture.
