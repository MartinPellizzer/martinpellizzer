Racosperma deltoideum subsp. amplum

Racosperma deltoideum subsp.

amplum, commonly known as the broad-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in a flat, fan-like structure.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The subspecies amplum is distinguished by its broader leaflets compared to other subspecies of Racosperma deltoideum.
