Renealmia laxa

Renealmia laxa, commonly known as the slender reedgrass, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering spikes produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered at the top of the stem.

Renealmia laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or as a landscape plant.
