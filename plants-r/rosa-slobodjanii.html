Rosa slobodjanii

Rosa slobodjanii, commonly known as the Slobodjanii rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, often with a white center, during the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, simple leaves with serrated edges, and an extensive root system adapted to rocky soils.

Rosa slobodjanii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and resilience.
