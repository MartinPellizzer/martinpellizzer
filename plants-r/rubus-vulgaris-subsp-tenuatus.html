Rubus vulgaris subsp. tenuatus

Rubus vulgaris subsp.

tenuatus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.

This subspecies is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.
