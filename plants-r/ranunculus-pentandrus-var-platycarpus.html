Ranunculus pentandrus var. platycarpus

Ranunculus pentandrus var. platycarpus, commonly known as the broad-fruited buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, waxy texture. Its fruits are broad, flattened achenes that are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
