Racosperma restiaceum

Racosperma restiaceum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

The species is also valued for its hardy nature and use in ecological restoration projects.
