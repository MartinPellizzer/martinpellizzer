Rosa × memorata

Rosa × memorata, commonly known as the Memory Rose, is a hybrid rose species that originated from crosses between Rosa damascena and Rosa gallica.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a velvety texture. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its historical significance in perfumery and horticulture adds to its botanical and cultural value.
