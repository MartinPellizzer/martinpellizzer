Rumia athamantoides

Rumia athamantoides, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink with a distinct lip petal.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
