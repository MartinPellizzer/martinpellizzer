Rubus grabowskii subsp. walsemannii

Rubus grabowskii subsp.

walsemannii, commonly known as the Walsemann's blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
