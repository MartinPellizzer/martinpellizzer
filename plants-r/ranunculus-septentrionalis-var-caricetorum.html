Ranunculus septentrionalis var. caricetorum

Ranunculus septentrionalis var. caricetorum, commonly known as the northern buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to northern regions of North America.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to nine petals and is characterized by its basal leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a short flowering period in early spring and is often found in association with other early successional plant species.

The varietal name *caricetorum* refers to its occurrence in the Caribou River area of Canada.
