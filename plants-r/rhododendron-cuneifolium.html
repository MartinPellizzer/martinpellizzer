Rhododendron cuneifolium

Rhododendron cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically 2 to 4 inches long and have a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron cuneifolium is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
