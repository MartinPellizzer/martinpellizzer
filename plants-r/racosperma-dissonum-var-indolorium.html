Racosperma dissonum var. indolorium

Racosperma dissonum var. indolorium, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the autumn season. It is adapted to well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
