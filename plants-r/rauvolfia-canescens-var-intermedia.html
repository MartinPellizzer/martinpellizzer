Rauvolfia canescens var. intermedia

Rauvolfia canescens var. intermedia, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in traditional medicine and horticulture.
