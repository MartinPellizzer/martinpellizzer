Rhaphidophora pilosa

Rhaphidophora pilosa, commonly known as the velvety arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its rhizomes are edible and have a starchy, slightly sweet flavor, making it a valuable crop in some regions.

Rhaphidophora pilosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
