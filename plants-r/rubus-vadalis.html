Rubus vadalis

Rubus vadalis, commonly known as the Vadals bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and blackberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

The botanical features of Rubus vadalis make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
