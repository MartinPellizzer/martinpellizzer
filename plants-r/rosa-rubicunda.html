Rosa rubicunda

Rosa rubicunda, commonly known as the crimson rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red petals and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple leaflets arranged oppositely along the stem.

Rosa rubicunda produces fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
