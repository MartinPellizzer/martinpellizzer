Rotheca bicolor

Rotheca bicolor, commonly known as the pink and white rotheca, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored flowers, which feature pink petals with white centers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Rotheca bicolor is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
