Rutidosis acutiglumis

Rutidosis acutiglumis, commonly known as the sharp-awned grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, blade-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with sharp awns. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in wetland ecosystems.

Rutidosis acutiglumis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
