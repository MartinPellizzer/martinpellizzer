Rosa rodnensis

Rosa rodnensis, commonly known as the Rodnian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has elliptical, serrated leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of down. Rosa rodnensis thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it remains relatively rare in cultivation.
