Rubus vestitus var. viridis

Rubus vestitus var. viridis, commonly known as the green-haired raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive greenish-bristled stems and foliage, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically reddish in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other species within the Rubus genus.
