Rubus × laudabilis var. glaber

Rubus × laudabilis var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other blackberry varieties. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
