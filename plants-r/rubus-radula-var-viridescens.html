Rubus radula var. viridescens

Rubus radula var. viridescens, commonly known as the green-stemmed blackberry.

This variant of the blackberry plant is characterized by its distinctive greenish stems and foliage, which set it apart from other Rubus species. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
