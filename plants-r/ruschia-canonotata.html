Ruschia canonotata

Ruschia canonotata, commonly known as the Canary Island Ruschia, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid gardens.
