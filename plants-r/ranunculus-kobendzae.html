Ranunculus kobendzae

Ranunculus kobendzae, commonly known as the Kobendza buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and the flowers range in color from white to pale yellow.

Ranunculus kobendzae is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid climates.
