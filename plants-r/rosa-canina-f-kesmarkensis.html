Rosa canina f. kesmarkensis

Rosa canina f. kesmarkensis, commonly known as the Kesmark rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa canina species. This form is native to the United Kingdom and is often found in woodland and hedgerow habitats. The leaves are pinnate with five to seven leaflets, and the plant produces red, hairy hips that contain seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
