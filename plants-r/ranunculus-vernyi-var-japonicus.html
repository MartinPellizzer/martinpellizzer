Ranunculus vernyi var. japonicus

Ranunculus vernyi var. japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that resemble petals, and it typically blooms in spring. The plant has a short, fibrous root system and grows from a rhizome, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and upright.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
