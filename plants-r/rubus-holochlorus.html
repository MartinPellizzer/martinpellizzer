Rubus holochlorus

Rubus holochlorus, commonly known as the yellow-tailed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically yellow or red in color. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they appear in clusters.

The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
