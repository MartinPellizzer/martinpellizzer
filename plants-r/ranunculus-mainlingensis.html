Ranunculus mainlingensis

Ranunculus mainlingensis, commonly known as the Mainling buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and Bhutan.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and mountain meadows. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often accompanied by a prominent central stigma. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem.

Ranunculus mainlingensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators and soil stability.
