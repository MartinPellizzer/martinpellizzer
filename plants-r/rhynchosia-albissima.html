Rhynchosia albissima

Rhynchosia albissima, commonly known as the white-flowered cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain small, edible seeds similar to those of other cowpea species. It is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and as a source of food.

Rhynchosia albissima is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural systems.
