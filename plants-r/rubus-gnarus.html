Rubus gnarus

Rubus gnarus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. Its flowers are usually white or pink, and they bloom in early summer.

Rubus gnarus is valued for its edible berries and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
