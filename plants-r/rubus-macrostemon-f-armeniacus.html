Rubus macrostemon f. armeniacus

Rubus macrostemon f. armeniacus, commonly known as Armenian blackberry, is a subspecies of the blackberry plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically larger and more robust than those of the typical species. The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along forest edges.

The berries are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine for their tart flavor and potential health benefits.
