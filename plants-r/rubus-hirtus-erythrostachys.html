Rubus hirtus erythrostachys

Rubus hirtus erythrostachys, commonly known as the red-stemmed bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
