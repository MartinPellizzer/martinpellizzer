Rubus tereticaulis f. pseudorivularis

Rubus tereticaulis f. pseudorivularis, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

This plant is a flowering shrub native to North America, characterized by its slender, thornless stems and small, aggregate fruits. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, which is usually black when ripe, is edible and has a tart flavor.
