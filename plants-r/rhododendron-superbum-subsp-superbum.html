Rhododendron superbum subsp. superbum

Rhododendron superbum subsp.

superbum, commonly known as the Appalachian rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern United States. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils in forested areas.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in the spring, which are borne on elongated inflorescences. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, often with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
