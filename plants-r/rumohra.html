Rumohra

Rumohra, commonly known as the New Zealand grass or kōwhai kōhanga, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to New Zealand and consists of a few species, with Rumohra angulata being the most well-known. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale blue flowers. It is often found in coastal and lowland areas, growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Rumohra plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various invertebrates and birds.
