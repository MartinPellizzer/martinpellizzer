Rubus amphistrophos

Rubus amphistrophos, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive serrated edge. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny, dark purple to black achenes surrounded by a white, woolly receptacle. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and is known for its ornamental value as well as its ecological importance to local wildlife.
