Rytigynia fuscosetulosa

Rytigynia fuscosetulosa, commonly known as the black-haired rytigynia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Rytigyniaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite and have a distinctive dark pubescence, contributing to its common name.

The flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and the plant is noted for its ornamental value in horticulture.
