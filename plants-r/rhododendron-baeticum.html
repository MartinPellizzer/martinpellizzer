Rhododendron baeticum

Rhododendron baeticum, commonly known as the Iberian rhododendron, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, acidic soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate. It is often found in mountainous regions, where it thrives in partial shade and well-drained conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a notable species in the Rhododendron genus.
