Ruellia fragrans

Ruellia fragrans, commonly known as sweet senna, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ruellia fragrans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
