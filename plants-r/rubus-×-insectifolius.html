Rubus × insectifolius

Rubus × insectifolius, commonly known as the blackberry-rose hybrid, is a deciduous shrub that exhibits characteristics of both blackberries and roses.

It is native to the United States and is known for its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Botanically, it is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus idaeus (raspberry).
