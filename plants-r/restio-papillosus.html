Restio papillosus

Restio papillosus, commonly known as the silky restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, often found in fynbos vegetation. The plant features slender, grass-like stems and produces delicate, feather-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the restio genus. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine, silky hairs, contributing to its common name.

Restio papillosus plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and provides habitat for small invertebrates.
