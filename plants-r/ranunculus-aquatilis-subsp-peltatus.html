Ranunculus aquatilis subsp. peltatus

Ranunculus aquatilis subsp.

peltatus, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in shallow water, along the edges of ponds, streams, and marshes.

The plant features delicate, floating or submerged leaves that are divided into pinnate or lobed segments, giving it a lacy appearance. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, and bloom in early spring.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters and is often used as an indicator of water quality.
