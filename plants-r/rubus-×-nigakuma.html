Rubus × nigakuma

Rubus × nigakuma, commonly known as the black raspberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep, almost black color, hence its common name. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique flavor and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and for commercial berry production.
