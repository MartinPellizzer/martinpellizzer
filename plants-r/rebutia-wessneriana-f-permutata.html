Rebutia wessneriana f. permutata

Rebutia wessneriana f. permutata, commonly known as the Permutated Rebutia, is a compact cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered with small, white to pale blue spines. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique coloration and compact growth habit make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
