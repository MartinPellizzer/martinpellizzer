Rosa rubiginosa var. ferox

Rosa rubiginosa var. ferox, commonly known as the red-stemmed rose or the wild rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, which are often reddish in color, and its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds, and these are typically orange-red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Rosa rubiginosa var. ferox is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
