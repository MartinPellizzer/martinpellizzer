Rubus procerus var. arrigentiformis

Rubus procerus var. arrigentiformis, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
