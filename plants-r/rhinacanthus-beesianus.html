Rhinacanthus beesianus

Rhinacanthus beesianus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, elliptical leaflets and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
