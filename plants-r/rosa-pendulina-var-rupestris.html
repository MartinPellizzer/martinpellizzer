Rosa pendulina var. rupestris

Rosa pendulina var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
