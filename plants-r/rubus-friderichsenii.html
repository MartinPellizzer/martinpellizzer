Rubus friderichsenii

Rubus friderichsenii, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, with compound leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized in various regions.
