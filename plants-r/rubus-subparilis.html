Rubus subparilis

Rubus subparilis, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

Due to its habitat, it is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.
