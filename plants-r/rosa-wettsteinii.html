Rosa wettsteinii

Rosa wettsteinii, commonly known as the Wettstein's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are covered in thorns.

Rosa wettsteinii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
