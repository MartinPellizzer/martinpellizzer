Rubia tinctorum var. grandifolia

Rubia tinctorum var. grandifolia, commonly known as greater weld, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces distinctive, elongated leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its roots contain compounds used for dyeing fabrics and have historical significance in natural dyeing practices.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.
