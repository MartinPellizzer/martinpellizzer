Rhipsalis crenata

Rhipsalis crenata, commonly known as the silver mist cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its soft, trailing stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The leaves are modified into small, hair-like structures, and it produces clusters of small, white flowers during the warmer months.

Rhipsalis crenata is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
