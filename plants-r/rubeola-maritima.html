Rubeola maritima

Rubeola maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant typically grows in saline or sandy soils along the shoreline, showcasing its adaptability to harsh maritime environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rubeola maritima is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
