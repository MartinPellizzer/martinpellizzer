Rimaria

Rimaria, commonly known as the "white thistle," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features spiny leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Rimaria is often found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a hardy, drought-resistant species.
