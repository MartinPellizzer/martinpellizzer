Randia zippeliana var. oblanceolata

Randia zippeliana var. oblanceolata, commonly known as the broadleaf randia, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
