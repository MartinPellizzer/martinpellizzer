Rhododendron zippelii

Rhododendron zippelii, commonly known as the Zippel's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the blooms.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
