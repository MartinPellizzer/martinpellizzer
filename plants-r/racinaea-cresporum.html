Racinaea cresporum

Racinaea cresporum, commonly known as the creeping racinaea, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread horizontally across the soil surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often emerging from the base of the leaves. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in its humid environment.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, Racinaea cresporum is considered a rare and understudied species in botanical research.
