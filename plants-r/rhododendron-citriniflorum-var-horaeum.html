Rhododendron citriniflorum var. horaeum

Rhododendron citriniflorum var. horaeum, commonly known as the golden-flowered rhododendron, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, making it a notable ornamental species. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with leathery, dark green leaves. Its flowers are funnel-shaped and often appear in clusters, exuding a subtle fragrance.

This variety is valued for its unique coloration and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
