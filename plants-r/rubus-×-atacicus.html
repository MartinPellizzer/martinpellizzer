Rubus × atacicus

Rubus × atacicus, commonly known as the Atacama raspberry, is a hybrid species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black. The plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing in rocky soils and saline conditions. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is valued for its tart flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
