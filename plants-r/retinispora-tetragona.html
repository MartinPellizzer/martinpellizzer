Retinispora tetragona

Retinispora tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled retinaspora, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-angled stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

Retinispora tetragona is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous medicinal practices.
