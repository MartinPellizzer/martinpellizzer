Ranunculus depressus var. stewartiae

Ranunculus depressus var. stewartiae, commonly known as Stewart's buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals and a hairy, glandular stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided and often covered with fine, soft hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
