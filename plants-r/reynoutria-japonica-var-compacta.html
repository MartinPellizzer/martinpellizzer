Reynoutria japonica var. compacta

Reynoutria japonica var. compacta, commonly known as giant knotweed, is a variant of the invasive plant species originally from East Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It has a robust root system that can spread rapidly, making it highly invasive in non-native habitats.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is often considered a noxious weed in many regions.
