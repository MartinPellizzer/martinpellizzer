Rubus scaber var. ferrugineus

Rubus scaber var. ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark, almost black, edible berries. The leaves are typically serrated with a slightly hairy texture, and the stems are covered in fine, rust-colored hairs. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its berries are valued for their tart flavor and are used in jams and culinary preparations.
