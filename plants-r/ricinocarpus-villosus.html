Ricinocarpus villosus

Ricinocarpus villosus, commonly known as the hairy ricin tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its rough, hairy bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, woody pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and traditional contexts.
