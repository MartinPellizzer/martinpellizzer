Rosa polyacantha

Rosa polyacantha, commonly known as the many-stemmed rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its multiple stems and dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to fully double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with serrated edges, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Rosa polyacantha is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
