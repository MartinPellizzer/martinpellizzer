Rudbeckia scabrifolia

Rudbeckia scabrifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf coneflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that support its showy yellow-orange composite flowers. The leaves are rough and hairy, giving the plant its common name, and they are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
