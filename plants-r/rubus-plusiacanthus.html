Rubus plusiacanthus

Rubus plusiacanthus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
