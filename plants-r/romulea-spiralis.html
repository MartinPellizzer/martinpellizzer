Romulea spiralis

Romulea spiralis, commonly known as the spiral squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, spiral-arranged leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Romulea spiralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
