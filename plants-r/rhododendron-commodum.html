Rhododendron commodum

Rhododendron commodum, commonly known as the Indian rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 3,000 meters. The plant features dense clusters of bell-shaped flowers, usually pink or white, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a lush green canopy throughout the growing season.

Rhododendron commodum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
