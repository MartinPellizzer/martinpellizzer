Rhodamnia polyantha

Rhodamnia polyantha, commonly known as the silky glabrous tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features smooth, greyish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
