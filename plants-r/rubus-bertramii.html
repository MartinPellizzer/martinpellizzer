Rubus bertramii

Rubus bertramii, commonly known as the Bertram's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The botanical features of Rubus bertramii make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
