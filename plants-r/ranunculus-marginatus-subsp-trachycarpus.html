Ranunculus marginatus subsp. trachycarpus

Ranunculus marginatus subsp.

trachycarpus, commonly known as the rough-seeded buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with five deeply notched petals and a central cluster of stamens.

The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are usually hairy as well.

The subspecies is distinguished by its rough, hairy fruits, which are adapted for wind dispersal.
