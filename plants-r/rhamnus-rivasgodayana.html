Rhamnus rivasgodayana

Rhamnus rivasgodayana, commonly known as the Iberian buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean scrublands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
