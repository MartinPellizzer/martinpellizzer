Ranunculus auricomus subsp. varangerensis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

varangerensis, commonly known as the Varanger buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and subarctic regions. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils in tundra and alpine environments. It is a perennial herb that thrives in cool climates with short growing seasons.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to harsh, high-latitude habitats.
