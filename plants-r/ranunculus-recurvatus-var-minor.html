Ranunculus recurvatus var. minor

Ranunculus recurvatus var. minor, commonly known as the lesser bentleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves with a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for wetland restoration and ornamental gardening.
