Rubus arrigonii

Rubus arrigonii, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces red, aggregate fruits that are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

Rubus arrigonii plays an important role in both ecological systems and human agriculture.
