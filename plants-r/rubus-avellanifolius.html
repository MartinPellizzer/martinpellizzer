Rubus avellanifolius

Rubus avellanifolius, commonly known as the blackberry lily, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its fruit is a small, edible berry that ripens in late summer.

Rubus avellanifolius is often found in woodland edges and moist soils, thriving in partial shade.
