Rubus pallidus var. hirsuticalyx

Rubus pallidus var. hirsuticalyx, commonly known as the hirsute pale raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, red berries in the summer. Its berries are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
