Rubus pannosus

Rubus pannosus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a species of shrub in the蔷薇科 (Rosaceae) family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, particularly in California and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, dense foliage, and clusters of small, dark berries that ripen in the fall. Its leaves are typically hairy, giving it a distinctive, rugged appearance.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.
