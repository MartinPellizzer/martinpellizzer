Rosa × involuta f. gracilis

Rosa × involuta f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is often cultivated in temperate climates.
