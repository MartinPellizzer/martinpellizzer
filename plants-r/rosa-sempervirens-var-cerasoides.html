Rosa sempervirens var. cerasoides

Rosa sempervirens var. cerasoides, commonly known as the evergreen rose or red evergreen rose, is a compact, shrubby plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that remains evergreen throughout the year. The plant produces showy, single or semi-double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for hedges, borders, and container gardening.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, requiring moderate water and regular pruning to maintain its shape.
