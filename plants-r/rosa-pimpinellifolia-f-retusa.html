Rosa pimpinellifolia f. retusa

Rosa pimpinellifolia f. retusa, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white fuzzy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its unique texture.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
