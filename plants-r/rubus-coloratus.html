Rubus coloratus

Rubus coloratus, commonly known as the red raspberry.

This species is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. It produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically bright red when mature. The plant is native to parts of Asia and is cultivated for its edible fruits.

Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and flowers with five pink to white petals.
