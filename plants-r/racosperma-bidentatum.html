Racosperma bidentatum

Racosperma bidentatum, commonly known as the two-toothed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark, and its leaves are pinnate with two pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.

Racosperma bidentatum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
