Ritterocactus permutatus

Ritterocactus permutatus, commonly known as the Permutated Ritter Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, ribbed stems and sparse, white or pale green spines. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its growth habit is typically solitary or in small colonies, and it is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Ritterocactus permutatus is known for its slow growth and is often cultivated as a decorative cactus in arid climates.
