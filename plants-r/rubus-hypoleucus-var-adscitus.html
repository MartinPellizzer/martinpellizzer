Rubus hypoleucus var. adscitus

Rubus hypoleucus var. adscitus, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry found in alpine regions.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
