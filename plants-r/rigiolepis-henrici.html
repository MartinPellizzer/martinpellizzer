Rigiolepis henrici

Rigiolepis henrici, commonly known as the Henry's rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically small, leathery, and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Rigiolepis henrici is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
