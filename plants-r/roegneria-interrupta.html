Roegneria interrupta

Roegneria interrupta, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

Roegneria interrupta is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
