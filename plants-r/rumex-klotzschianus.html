Rumex klotzschianus

Rumex klotzschianus, commonly known as the Siberian sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and flowering structures. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a reddish underside and produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its rhubarb-like leaves have been used traditionally for their tart flavor and potential medicinal properties.
