Rubus ulmifolius var. tiliifolius

Rubus ulmifolius var. tiliifolius, commonly known as the tiliifolius blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry species.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
