Rudbeckia fulgida var. auriculata

Rudbeckia fulgida var. auriculata, commonly known as the orange coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking orange-yellow petals that surround a dark brown, conical center, making it a visually appealing addition to gardens. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom from midsummer to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Rudbeckia fulgida var. auriculata is drought-tolerant and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
