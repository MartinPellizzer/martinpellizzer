Ranunculus flabellatus var. chondrodes

Ranunculus flabellatus var. chondrodes, commonly known as the chondrodes buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow, radially symmetrical flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its botanical characteristics include a short, stout rhizome and a flowering period that occurs in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
