Romulea pearsonii

Romulea pearsonii, commonly known as the pearson's glory of the snow, is a striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It features a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces intricate, showy flowers with curled petals in shades of white and pink. The plant belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes many ornamental and economically important species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine collections.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and conservation significance.
