Rubus × falsicuneatiformis

Rubus × falsicuneatiformis, commonly known as the false blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated margins, while the flowers are usually white or pink.

The fruit, which resembles a blackberry, is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a subject of interest for both horticultural and ecological studies.
