Rosa bipinnata

Rosa bipinnata, commonly known as the doubleleaf rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are twice-pinnate, giving it a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Rosa bipinnata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
