Rubus thibetanus

Rubus thibetanus, commonly known as the Tibetan raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
