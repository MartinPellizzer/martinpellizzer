Reseda bracteata var. leptostachya

Reseda bracteata var. leptostachya, commonly known as slender-stalked reseda, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
