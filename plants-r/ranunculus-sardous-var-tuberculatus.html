Ranunculus sardous var. tuberculatus

Ranunculus sardous var. tuberculatus, commonly known as the Sardinian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive central tuft of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
