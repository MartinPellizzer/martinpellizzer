Rosa canina var. steilbergii

Rosa canina var. steilbergii, commonly known as the Steilberg rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It is native to hybrid populations in South Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The berries of this variety are edible but not as commonly consumed as those of other Rosa species.
