Rubus hirtus var. thyrsiflorus

Rubus hirtus var. thyrsiflorus, commonly known as the thrysofloral blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry species.

It is characterized by its dense, prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in compact clusters, which are followed by edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
