Ranunculus gramineus var. bulbosus

Ranunculus gramineus var. bulbosus, commonly known as the bulbous buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, lobed leaf structure. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that help it survive in adverse conditions. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive addition to gardens and natural habitats.
