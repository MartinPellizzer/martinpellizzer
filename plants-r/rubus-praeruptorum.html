Rubus praeruptorum

Rubus praeruptorum, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.

The flowers are white or pale pink and appear in clusters, adding to its visual appeal.
