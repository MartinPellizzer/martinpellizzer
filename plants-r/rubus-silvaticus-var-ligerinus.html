Rubus silvaticus var. ligerinus

Rubus silvaticus var. ligerinus, commonly known as the European blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and culinary purposes.
