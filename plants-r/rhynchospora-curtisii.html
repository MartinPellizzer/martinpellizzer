Rhynchospora curtisii

Rhynchospora curtisii, commonly known as Curtis's spikegrass, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of pale green to brownish flowers, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly rough texture.

Curtis's spikegrass plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
