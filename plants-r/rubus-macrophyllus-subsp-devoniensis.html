Rubus macrophyllus subsp. devoniensis

Rubus macrophyllus subsp.

devoniensis, commonly known as the Devon raspberry, is a shrub native to western Europe, particularly the UK. It features thorny stems and compound leaves with large, ovate leaflets.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, red drupes that ripen in late summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
