Rupicapnos africana subsp. splendens

Rupicapnos africana subsp.

splendens, commonly known as the splendour flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its striking, bright orange-red flowers that appear in clusters during the winter months.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
