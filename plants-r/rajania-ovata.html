Rajania ovata

Rajania ovata, commonly known as the oval lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and elongated, spiral-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a loose cluster. Rajania ovata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
