Rubus jaonnicus

Rubus jaonnicus, commonly known as the Jaonnic raspberry.

It is a species of brambles native to the island of Madagascar, characterized by its woody stems and thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, similar to other Rubus species. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic plant within its native range.
