Racosperma auronitens

Racosperma auronitens, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with multiple elliptical leaflets, and its stems are often covered in a fine, silky coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also a symbol of Australian biodiversity.
