Rapuntium paniculatum

Rapuntium paniculatum, commonly known as the clustered buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Ranunculaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with numerous sepals and petals. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers typically bloom in spring, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
