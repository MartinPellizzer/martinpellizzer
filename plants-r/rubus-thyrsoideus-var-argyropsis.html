Rubus thyrsoideus var. argyropsis

Rubus thyrsoideus var. argyropsis, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the thyrsoideus species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications.

This variety is native to certain regions of North America and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
