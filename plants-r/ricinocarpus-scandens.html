Ricinocarpus scandens

Ricinocarpus scandens, commonly known as the climbing ricin tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a woody vine that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its pinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are known to contain the potent toxin ricin. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
