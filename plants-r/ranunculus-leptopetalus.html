Ranunculus leptopetalus

Ranunculus leptopetalus, commonly known as the slender-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features delicate, five-petaled flowers that are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive central stigma. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Ranunculaceae family.
