Rubus × intectiformis

Rubus × intectiformis, commonly known as the bramble raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is native to parts of eastern North America and is often found in moist, wooded areas. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruits are edible, though they are small and tart.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
