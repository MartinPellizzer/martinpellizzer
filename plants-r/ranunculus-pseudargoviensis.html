Ranunculus pseudargoviensis

Ranunculus pseudargoviensis, commonly known as the Caucasian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in mountainous areas with well-drained soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers are usually solitary and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ranunculus pseudargoviensis is characterized by its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
