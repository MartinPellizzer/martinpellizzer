Ranunculus monspeliacus proles monspessulanus

Ranunculus monspeliacus proles monspessulanus, commonly known as the Monspessulan buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in spring. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its historical use in herbal medicine and its role in ecological studies of wetland ecosystems.
