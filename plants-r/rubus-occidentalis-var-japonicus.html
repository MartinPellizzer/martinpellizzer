Rubus occidentalis var. japonicus

Rubus occidentalis var. japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry native to Japan.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant produces edible fruits that are often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring.
