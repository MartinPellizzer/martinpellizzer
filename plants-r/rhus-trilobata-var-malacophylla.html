Rhus trilobata var. malacophylla

Rhus trilobata var. malacophylla, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three leaflets, which are typically oval and smooth in texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by red, fuzzy berries that attract wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological and ornamental horticulture.
