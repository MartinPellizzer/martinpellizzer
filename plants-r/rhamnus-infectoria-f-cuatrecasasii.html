Rhamnus infectoria f. cuatrecasasii

Rhamnus infectoria f. cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas' buckthorn, is a rare shrub characterized by its small, oval leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue-black berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. The plant is typically found in rocky or limestone soils in Mediterranean climates, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments.
