Ranunculus triphyllos

Ranunculus triphyllos, commonly known as the three-leaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a distinctive central blotch, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.

The species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
