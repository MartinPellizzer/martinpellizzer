Rubus madrensis

Rubus madrensis, commonly known as the Mexican blackberry, is a species of shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic conditions.

Rubus madrensis is often found in woodland edges and rocky slopes, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
