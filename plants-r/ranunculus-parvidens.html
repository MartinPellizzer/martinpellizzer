Ranunculus parvidens

Ranunculus parvidens, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, delicate flowers with five to eight yellow petals and a prominent yellow center, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
