Rosa sinica

Rosa sinica, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. Its stems are covered in thorns, and it produces red or orange hips that contain seeds.

Rosa sinica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
