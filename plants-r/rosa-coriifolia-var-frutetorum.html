Rosa coriifolia var. frutetorum

Rosa coriifolia var. frutetorum, commonly known as the Chinese shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, with oval to lance-shaped leaflets that are hairy underneath. This variety is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in a range of soil types.

It is also noted for its hardiness and resistance to common rose diseases.
