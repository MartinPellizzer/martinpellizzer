Rubus sphenoideus

Rubus sphenoideus, commonly known as the spoonleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spoon-shaped leaves and small, dark purple berries. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its floral structure consists of five pink to white petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Rubus genus.

The berries are edible but often tart, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
