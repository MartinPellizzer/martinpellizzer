Rupicapnos africana subsp. pomeliana

Rupicapnos africana subsp.

pomeliana, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement.

The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, yellowish fruits that are oval in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
