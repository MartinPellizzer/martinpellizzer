Reseda tridens

Reseda tridens, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white wax. Reseda tridens has a taproot system and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh environments.

It is also known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
