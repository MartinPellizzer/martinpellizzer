Rytidostylis gracilis

Rytidostylis gracilis, commonly known as the slender swamp reed, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense clumps with long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, arranged in spike-like clusters. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic habitats.

Rytidostylis gracilis is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in wetland gardens.
