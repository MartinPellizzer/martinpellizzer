Rottboellia compressa subvar. fasciculata

Rottboellia compressa subvar. fasciculata, commonly known as narrowleaf horse-tail, is a grass-like perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in tufts, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it a common pioneer species in ecological succession.
