Rosa bengalensis odoratissima

Rosa bengalensis odoratissima, commonly known as the Bengal rose or the Indian rose, is a species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub that produces fragrant, pink to red flowers, which bloom in spring and summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers are used in traditional medicine and perfumery due to their strong, sweet scent.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
