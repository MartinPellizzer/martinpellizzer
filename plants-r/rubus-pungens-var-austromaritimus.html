Rubus pungens var. austromaritimus

Rubus pungens var. austromaritimus, commonly known as the coastal raspberry, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and small, usually white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically small and tart. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt-laden environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
