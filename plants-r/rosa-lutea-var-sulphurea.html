Rosa lutea var. sulphurea

Rosa lutea var. sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur yellow rose, is a variant of the yellow rose species native to southern Europe.

This plant is characterized by its bright, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a slightly serrated edge. It is a shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring thorny stems and a dense, bushy habit.

The flowers emit a subtle, sweet fragrance and are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens.
