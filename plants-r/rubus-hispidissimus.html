Rubus hispidissimus

Rubus hispidissimus, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of eastern North America and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is known for its hardy nature.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex inflorescence and adaptability to various soil types.
