Rosa coriifolia var. caesia

Rosa coriifolia var. caesia, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the growing season, often in clusters of pink or white. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red hips in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to gardens and botanical collections.
