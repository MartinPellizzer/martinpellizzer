Rugoloa polygonata

Rugoloa polygonata, commonly known as the Brazilian velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often accompanied by reddish bracts. Rugoloa polygonata is often found in wetlands and along the edges of rivers, thriving in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
