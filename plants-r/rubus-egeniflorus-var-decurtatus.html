Rubus egeniflorus var. decurtatus

Rubus egeniflorus var. decurtatus, commonly known as the short-stalked blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of China.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves that have three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

The botanical features of this variety make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and potential cultivation.
