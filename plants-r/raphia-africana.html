Raphia africana

Raphia africana, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a large, woody plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant can grow up to 20 meters in height and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats. Its fruit is a fibrous, three-lobed capsule that contains edible seeds.

The leaves are used for thatching and roofing, while the trunk is valued for its durable fibers and timber.
