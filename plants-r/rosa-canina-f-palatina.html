Rosa canina f. palatina

Rosa canina f. palatina, commonly known as the Palatine dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa genus. This subspecies is native to Europe and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

The leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant produces rosehips that are rich in vitamin C. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
