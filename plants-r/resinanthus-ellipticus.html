Resinanthus ellipticus

Resinanthus ellipticus, commonly known as the elliptic resin tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, which bloom during the wet season. Its unique resinous secretions have been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
