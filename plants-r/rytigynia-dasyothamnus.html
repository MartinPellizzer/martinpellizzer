Rytigynia dasyothamnus

Rytigynia dasyothamnus, commonly known as the hairy-leaved rytigynia, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Rytigyniaceae and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage structure.
