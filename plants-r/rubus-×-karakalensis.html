Rubus × karakalensis

Rubus × karakalensis, commonly known as the Karakalinsky blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small to medium in size. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The botanical features of Rubus × karakalensis make it a valuable species for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
