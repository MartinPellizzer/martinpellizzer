Rubus tereticaulis subsp. vepallidus

Rubus tereticaulis subsp.

vepallidus, commonly known as the paleleaf raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant has thin, wiry stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and the fruit is a aggregate berry that matures to a red color.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive pale foliage and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
