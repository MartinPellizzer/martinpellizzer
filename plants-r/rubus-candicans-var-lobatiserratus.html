Rubus candicans var. lobatiserratus

Rubus candicans var. lobatiserratus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its distinctive, lobed and serrated leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
