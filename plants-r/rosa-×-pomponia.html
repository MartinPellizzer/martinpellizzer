Rosa × pomponia

Rosa × pomponia, commonly known as the pompon rose, is a hybrid shrub rose originating from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa centifolia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, rounded foliage. The plant produces abundant clusters of small, double flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It is highly resistant to diseases and pests, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Rosa × pomponia thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
