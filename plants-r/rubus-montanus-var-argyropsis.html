Rubus montanus var. argyropsis

Rubus montanus var. argyropsis, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but less commonly cultivated compared to other blackberry varieties. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural research.
