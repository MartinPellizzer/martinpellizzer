Rubus praetermissus

Rubus praetermissus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in woodland and rocky habitats, often growing in partial shade.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles.
