Rhaphidophora pinnatifida

Rhaphidophora pinnatifida, commonly known as the velvet leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a distinctive velvety texture and deep green coloration. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense mats in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically compound, with finger-like lobes that give it a feathery appearance.

Rhaphidophora pinnatifida is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
