Rubus viridescens

Rubus viridescens, commonly known as the greenleaf raspberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
