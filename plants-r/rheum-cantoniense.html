Rheum cantoniense

Rheum cantoniense, commonly known as Chinese rhubarb, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces tall, clustered stems with reddish-brown flowers that bloom in the spring. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable.

Rheum cantoniense is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in herbal remedies for centuries.
