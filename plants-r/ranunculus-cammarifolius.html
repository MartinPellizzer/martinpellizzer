Ranunculus cammarifolius

Ranunculus cammarifolius, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of shiny, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers with five petals. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of habitats, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
