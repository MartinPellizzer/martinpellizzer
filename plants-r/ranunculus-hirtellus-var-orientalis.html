Ranunculus hirtellus var. orientalis

Ranunculus hirtellus var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is a herbaceous perennial with delicate, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces small, aggregate fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
