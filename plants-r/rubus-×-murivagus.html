Rubus × murivagus

Rubus × murivagus, commonly known as the bramble raspberry or blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are usually dark red to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
