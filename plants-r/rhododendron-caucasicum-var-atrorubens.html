Rhododendron caucasicum var. atrorubens

Rhododendron caucasicum var. atrorubens, commonly known as the Black-red Caucasic Rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from deep red to purple. The plant typically grows in mountainous areas with acidic, well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
