Restio oxylepis

Restio oxylepis, commonly known as the Natal restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, grass-like stems and is typically found in coastal and sandy environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white to pale blue flowers. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Restio oxylepis is adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
