Rubus serratus

Rubus serratus, commonly known as the serrated blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus serratus is often found in shrublands and forest edges, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
