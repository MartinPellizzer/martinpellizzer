Raphanus × micranthus

Raphanus × micranthus, commonly known as the Chinese radish or daikon, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, cylindrical root that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has alternate, pinnately lobed leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family.

The plant is cultivated for its edible root, which is used in various cuisines around the world.
