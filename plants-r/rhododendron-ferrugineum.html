Rhododendron ferrugineum

Rhododendron ferrugineum, commonly known as the alpine azalea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a rusty or reddish underside. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Rhododendron ferrugineum plays an important role in alpine ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
