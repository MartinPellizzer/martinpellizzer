Rosa × vendeana

Rosa × vendeana, commonly known as the Spanish rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa gallica and Rosa spinosissima.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, alternate, and have serrated edges, while its fruits are red, hairy rosehips that persist into winter.

Rosa × vendeana is native to southern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
