Rosa cinnamomea var. vulgaris

Rosa cinnamomea var. vulgaris, commonly known as the cinnamon rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces ornamental, cinnamon-scented hips in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable.
