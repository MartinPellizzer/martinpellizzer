Ranunculus carpaticus

Ranunculus carpaticus, commonly known as the Carpathian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, showy flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are hairy.

Ranunculus carpaticus is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates.
