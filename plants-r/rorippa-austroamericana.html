Rorippa austroamericana

Rorippa austroamericana, commonly known as the American watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit, often found in shallow water or moist soil. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a waxy surface, and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. It produces slender, elongated siliques containing numerous seeds.

This species is often used in ornamental gardening and has some culinary uses in certain regions.
