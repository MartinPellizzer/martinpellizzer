Robinia pseudoacacia f. decaisneana

Robinia pseudoacacia f. decaisneana, commonly known as the Decaisne's false acacia, is a botanical variant of the black locust tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval leaflets. This form is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.
