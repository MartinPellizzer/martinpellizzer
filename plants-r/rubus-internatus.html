Rubus internatus

Rubus internatus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically serrated along the edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are dark purple to black when mature. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are widely used in culinary applications.

Rubus internatus thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.
