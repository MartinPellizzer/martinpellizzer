Ranunculus buchoniae

Ranunculus buchoniae, commonly known as Buchon's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features palmate leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
