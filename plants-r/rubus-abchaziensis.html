Rubus abchaziensis

Rubus abchaziensis, commonly known as the Abkhazian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in forested areas and along riverbanks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries, which ripen in late summer, are small, dark purple, and have a tart flavor.
