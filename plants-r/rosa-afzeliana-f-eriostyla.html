Rosa afzeliana f. eriostyla

Rosa afzeliana f. eriostyla, commonly known as the eriostyle rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa afzeliana species.

It is native to regions of East Africa, including Kenya and Tanzania, where it thrives in moist, wooded habitats. The plant is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with finely toothed leaflets, and the plant produces rose hips that are small and hairy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
