Rosenbergia

Rosenbergia, commonly known as the "butterfly orchid," is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to Southeast Asia and are characterized by their delicate, butterfly-like flowers with intricate patterns. Rosenbergia species typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, attaching themselves to trees or rocks in their natural habitat. Their flowers come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink, often with a unique three-dimensional structure.

The genus is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
