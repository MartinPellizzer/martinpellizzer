Ranunculus plantagineus

Ranunculus plantagineus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and along streambanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, arranged in a cup-like shape, and is often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat waxy texture.

Ranunculus plantagineus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and sometimes toxic compounds.
