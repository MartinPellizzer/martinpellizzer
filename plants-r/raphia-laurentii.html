Raphia laurentii

Raphia laurentii, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a large, tropical palm native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 10 meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. Its leaves are often used for thatching and construction, while the trunk is valued for its strength and durability.

Raphia laurentii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
