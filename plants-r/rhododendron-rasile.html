Rhododendron rasile

Rhododendron rasile, commonly known as the Kashmiri rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in mountainous areas with cool, moist climates and well-drained soil. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron rasile is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
