Rubia schumanniana var. maillardii

Rubia schumanniana var. maillardii, commonly known as Maillard's Rubia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and garden plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark berries that contain the seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in dyeing textiles.
