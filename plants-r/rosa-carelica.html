Rosa carelica

Rosa carelica, commonly known as the Caucasian rose, is a species native to the Caucasus region.

It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height, characterized by its thorny stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red, hairy achenes that are dispersed by wind.

Rosa carelica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has also been used in traditional medicine.
