Ranunculus flabellatus var. nervosus

Ranunculus flabellatus var. nervosus, commonly known as the veined buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and yellow flowers with five petals. The varietal name 'nervosus' refers to the prominent venation on its foliage. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
