Rubus × effusus

Rubus × effusus, commonly known as evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus argutus.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny growth and evergreen foliage, which remains green throughout the year. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in coastal regions of California.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
