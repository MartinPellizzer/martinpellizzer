Rubus probabilis

Rubus probabilis, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications, though it is generally less sweet compared to other blackberry varieties.
