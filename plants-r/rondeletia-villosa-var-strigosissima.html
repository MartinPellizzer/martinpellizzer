Rondeletia villosa var. strigosissima

Rondeletia villosa var. strigosissima, commonly known as the hairy rondeletia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
