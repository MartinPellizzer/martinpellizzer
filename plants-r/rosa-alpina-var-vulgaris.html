Rosa alpina var. vulgaris

Rosa alpina var. vulgaris, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant produces showy pink to white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Rosa alpina var. vulgaris is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
