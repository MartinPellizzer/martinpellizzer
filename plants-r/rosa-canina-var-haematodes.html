Rosa canina var. haematodes

Rosa canina var. haematodes, commonly known as the dog rose or scarlet rose, is a variant of the common rose.

It is characterized by its bright red flowers and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rosa genus. The plant is native to Europe and western Asia and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven leaflets, and the flowers are usually solitary.

The fruit, a red, hairy rose hip, contains the seeds and is used in traditional medicine.
