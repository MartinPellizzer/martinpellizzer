Rosa caroliana

Rosa caroliana, commonly known as the Carolinian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring arching stems and dense foliage. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are composed of five petals and numerous stamens. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 5 to 7 oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

Rosa caroliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
