Rabiea albipuncta

Rabiea albipuncta, commonly known as the white-spotted lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a distinctive white and green mottled pattern. The plant produces striking white flowers with a subtle pink tinge, often arranged in clusters. Rabiea albipuncta prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
