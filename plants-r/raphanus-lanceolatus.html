Raphanus lanceolatus

Raphanus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf radish, is a species of flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces round, edible roots that are similar in appearance to the common radish but have a milder flavor. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible qualities, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and small-scale agriculture.
