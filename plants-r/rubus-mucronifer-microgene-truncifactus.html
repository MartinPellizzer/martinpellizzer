Rubus mucronifer microgene truncifactus

Rubus mucronifer microgene truncifactus, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry.

This plant is a shrub characterized by its small, thorny stems and clusters of tiny, edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces flowers in shades of white or pale pink.

The plant is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in local cuisines.
