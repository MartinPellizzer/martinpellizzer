Rubus insericatus var. guestphalicus

Rubus insericatus var. guestphalicus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its dense, prickly foliage and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.
