Ranunculus urbicola

Ranunculus urbicola, commonly known as the urban buttercup, is a delicate herbaceous plant native to urban environments in Europe.

It typically grows in cracks of pavements, walls, and other disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to human-altered landscapes. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture that aids in water retention.

Ranunculus urbicola is a symbol of resilience, thriving in conditions where many other plants struggle.
