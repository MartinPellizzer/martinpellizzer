Racosperma leptocarpum

Racosperma leptocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-fruited acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in compact, globular heads, followed by narrow, leathery pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands and heathlands.

Racosperma leptocarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
