Ranunculus montanus var. dentatus

Ranunculus montanus var. dentatus, commonly known as the toothed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically arranged in a cup-like shape. The leaves are palmately divided with serrated edges, giving it its characteristic "toothed" appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
