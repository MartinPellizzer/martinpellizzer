Romulea rosea var. zeyheri

Romulea rosea var. zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-brown bulbs that are used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early spring flowering and distinctive foliage.
