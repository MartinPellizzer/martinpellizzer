Rosa pendulina f. corymbiflora

Rosa pendulina f. corymbiflora, commonly known as the clustered climbing rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa pendulina species.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that can climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, lobed leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its cascading floral display.
