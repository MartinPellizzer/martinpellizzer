Ripsoctis egyptiaca

Ripsoctis egyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves. It is native to arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East, where it thrives in dry, sandy soils.

Ripsoctis egyptiaca is known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
