Rosa × dumetorum subsp. redunca

Rosa × dumetorum subsp.

redunca, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white.

The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and it produces red, hairy fruits known as hips.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
