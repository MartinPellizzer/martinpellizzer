Rosa ianthinacantha

Rosa ianthinacantha, commonly known as the purple-stemmed rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged stems and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. Its leaves are typically alternate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces rose-like flowers that bloom in spring.

Rosa ianthinacantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
