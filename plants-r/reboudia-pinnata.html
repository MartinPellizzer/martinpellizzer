Reboudia pinnata

Reboudia pinnata, commonly known as the water hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, palmate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found near water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
