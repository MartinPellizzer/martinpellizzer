Roupala montana var. tomentosa

Roupala montana var. tomentosa, commonly known as the tomentose roupala, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive, soft-textured appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and ecological value make it a notable species in its native habitat.
