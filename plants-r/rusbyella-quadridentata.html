Rusbyella quadridentata

Rusbyella quadridentata, commonly known as the four-toothed lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. Its scientific name refers to the four distinct teeth or lobes on the petals, which are a distinguishing feature.

Rusbyella quadridentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
