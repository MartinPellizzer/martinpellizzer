Rubus parviserrati

Rubus parviserrati, commonly known as the small-toothed blackberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, serrated leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it is an important source of food for various wildlife species.
