Rosa canina var. agraria

Rosa canina var. agraria, commonly known as the agrarian rose, is a variant of the dog rose.

It is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces fragrant pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red hips in the autumn. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the stems are armed with thorns.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
