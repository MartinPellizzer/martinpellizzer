Rosa sicula subvar. aemula

Rosa sicula subvar. aemula, commonly known as the Sicilian rose, is a shrub native to the island of Sicily.

It features upright, thorny stems and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in Mediterranean horticulture.
