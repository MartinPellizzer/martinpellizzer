Rosa coriifolia var. lepida

Rosa coriifolia var. lepida, commonly known as the Japanese snow rose, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features thorny stems and elliptical, finely toothed leaves that turn red in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact size and elegant blooms make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and botanical collections.
