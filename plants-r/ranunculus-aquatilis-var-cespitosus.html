Ranunculus aquatilis var. cespitosus

Ranunculus aquatilis var. cespitosus, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to temperate regions.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in ponds, streams, and wet meadows. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that float on the water surface or emerge from the water. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, making it visually striking.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
