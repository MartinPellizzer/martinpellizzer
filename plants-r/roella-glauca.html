Roella glauca

Roella glauca, commonly known as the bluebells of the south, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain the seeds.

Roella glauca is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.
