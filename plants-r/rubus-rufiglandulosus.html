Rubus rufiglandulosus

Rubus rufiglandulosus, commonly known as the red-fruited raspberry.

This shrub is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year producing vegetative growth and the second year bearing fruit. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red, though they may vary in color.

Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges and reddish glandular hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
