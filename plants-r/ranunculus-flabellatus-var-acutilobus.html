Ranunculus flabellatus var. acutilobus

Ranunculus flabellatus var. acutilobus, commonly known as the sharp-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to alpine and woodland gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens or conservation settings.
