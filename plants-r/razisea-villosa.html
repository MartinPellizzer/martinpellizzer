Razisea villosa

Razisea villosa, commonly known as the hairy razisea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
