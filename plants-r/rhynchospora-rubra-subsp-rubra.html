Rhynchospora rubra subsp. rubra

Rhynchospora rubra subsp.

rubra, commonly known as the red stemmed tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually reddish or pinkish in color. It is characterized by its reddish stems and is often found in wetland ecosystems.

This subspecies plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
