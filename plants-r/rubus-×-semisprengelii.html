Rubus × semisprengelii

Rubus × semisprengelii, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny shrubs and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It is native to the western coast of North America and thrives in coastal climates with moderate rainfall.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
