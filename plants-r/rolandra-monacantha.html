Rolandra monacantha

Rolandra monacantha, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a prominent yellow throat. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Rolandra monacantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
