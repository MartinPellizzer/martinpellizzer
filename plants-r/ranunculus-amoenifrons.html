Ranunculus amoenifrons

Ranunculus amoenifrons, commonly known as the pretty-faced buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, bright yellow flowers with prominent, rounded sepals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters, with a compact, bushy appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and forested areas.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.
