Rubus × corbiereanus

Rubus × corbiereanus, commonly known as the blackberry-boys or bramble.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit, which is a cluster of small drupes, is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a popular plant for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
