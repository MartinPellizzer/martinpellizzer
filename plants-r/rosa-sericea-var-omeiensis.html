Rosa sericea var. omeiensis

Rosa sericea var. omeiensis, commonly known as the Omei hill rose, is a variant of the silky rose native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its "sericea" name, which means silky in Latin. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
