Rosa communis var. montisludovici

Rosa communis var. montisludovici, commonly known as the Montis Ludovici rose, is a botanical variety characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant, semi-double pink flowers.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces thorny stems and has dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

This variety is also noted for its hardiness and ability to bloom reliably in the spring.
