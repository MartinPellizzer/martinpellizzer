Rosa pugetii

Rosa pugetii, commonly known as the Puget Sound rose, is a species of flowering shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins and showy, five-petaled flowers.

Rosa pugetii is often found in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in open woodlands.
