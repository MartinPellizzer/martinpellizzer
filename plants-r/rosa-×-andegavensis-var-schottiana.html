Rosa × andegavensis var. schottiana

Rosa × andegavensis var. schottiana, commonly known as Schott's rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically features dark green, serrated leaves and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and hardiness in temperate climates.
