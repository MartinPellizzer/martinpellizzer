Rubus canadensis var. millspaughii

Rubus canadensis var. millspaughii, commonly known as the Canadian blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to North America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
