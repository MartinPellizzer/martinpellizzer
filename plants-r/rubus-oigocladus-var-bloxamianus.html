Rubus oigocladus var. bloxamianus

Rubus oigocladus var. bloxamianus, commonly known as the Bloxam's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its thorny stems are characteristic of the Rubus genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, temperate climate.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor.
