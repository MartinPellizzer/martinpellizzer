Rubus plicatus var. foliosus

Rubus plicatus var. foliosus, commonly known as the broadleaf raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically broad and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
