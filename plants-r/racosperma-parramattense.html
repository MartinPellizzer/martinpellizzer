Racosperma parramattense

Racosperma parramattense, commonly known as the Parramatta wattle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
