Rosa × turbinata

Rosa × turbinata, commonly known as the turbinated rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiral-shaped petals that give it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant typically produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves.

Rosa × turbinata is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
