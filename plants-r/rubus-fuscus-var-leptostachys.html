Rubus fuscus var. leptostachys

Rubus fuscus var. leptostachys, commonly known as the slender-stamened blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves that have three to five leaflets. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, giving it a delicate appearance. The fruit is a dark purple to black berry, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
