Ranunculus buschii

Ranunculus buschii, commonly known as the Busch's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its blooming period occurs in late winter to early spring, adding early color to the landscape.
