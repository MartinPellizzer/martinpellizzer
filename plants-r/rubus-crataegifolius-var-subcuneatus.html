Rubus crataegifolius var. subcuneatus

Rubus crataegifolius var. subcuneatus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which is part of the蔷薇科 (Rosaceae) family, known for its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are distinctive, with narrow, lobed shapes and a serrated margin.
