Rubus dufftianus

Rubus dufftianus, commonly known as the Dufftian bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in shrublands and forest edges.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
