Rosa tomentosa subsp. sherardii

Rosa tomentosa subsp.

sherardii, commonly known as the Sherard's woolly rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and fragrant pink to white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and hardy nature.
