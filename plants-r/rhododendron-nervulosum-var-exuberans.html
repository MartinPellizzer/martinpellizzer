Rhododendron nervulosum var. exuberans

Rhododendron nervulosum var. exuberans, commonly known as the exuberant rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically leathery and have a prominent midrib, while the flowers range in color from pink to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
