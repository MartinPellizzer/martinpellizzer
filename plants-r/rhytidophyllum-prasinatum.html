Rhytidophyllum prasinatum

Rhytidophyllum prasinatum, commonly known as the green bark tree, is a unique plant belonging to the family Rhytidophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery bark that remains green throughout its life, a feature that sets it apart from most other trees. The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically small and alternate, contributing to its overall compact structure.

Despite its unusual appearance, it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
