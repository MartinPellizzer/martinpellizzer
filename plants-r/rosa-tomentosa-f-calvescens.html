Rosa tomentosa f. calvescens

Rosa tomentosa f. calvescens, commonly known as the woolly rose or soft-haired rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa tomentosa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and soft, fuzzy texture on both leaves and stems. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.

This form is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its compact growth habit.
