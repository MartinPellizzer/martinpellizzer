Rosa viscaria subvar. albiflora

Rosa viscaria subvar. albiflora, commonly known as the white-stemmed dog rose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with arching stems and serrated leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.

Rosa viscaria subvar. albiflora is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
