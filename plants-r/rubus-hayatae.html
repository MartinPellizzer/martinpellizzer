Rubus hayatae

Rubus hayatae, commonly known as the Hayata's raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are white with five petals, and the fruits are usually red or yellow when mature.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
