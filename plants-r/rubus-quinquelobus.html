Rubus quinquelobus

Rubus quinquelobus, commonly known as the five-lobed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with five distinct lobes. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them a valued wild plant for both foraging and ecological purposes.
