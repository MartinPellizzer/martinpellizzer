Rhipsalis virgata

Rhipsalis virgata, commonly known as the silver star cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, typically from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are reduced to small, hair-like structures, and it does not have true leaves in the conventional sense.

Rhipsalis virgata is well adapted to grow on trees or rocks, where it absorbs moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.
