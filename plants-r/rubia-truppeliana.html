Rubia truppeliana

Rubia truppeliana, commonly known as the Truppel's madder, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces red or orange-red berries that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Rubia truppeliana is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
