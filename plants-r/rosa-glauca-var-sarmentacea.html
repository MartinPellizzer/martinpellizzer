Rosa glauca var. sarmentacea

Rosa glauca var. sarmentacea, commonly known as the creeping white rose, is a variant of the Rosa glauca species.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit, with long, trailing stems that form dense mats. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and are covered in fine, soft hairs.

This variety is well adapted to rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments.
