Rubus ashei

Rubus ashei, commonly known as the Ashe blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The Ashe blackberry is valued for its edible berries, which are used in culinary and commercial applications.
