Rubia salicifolia

Rubia salicifolia, commonly known as the silky bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Rubia salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
