Ranunculus auricomus var. uniflora

Ranunculus auricomus var. uniflora, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic sap.

The variety uniflora is distinguished by having a single flower per stem, setting it apart from other varieties of Ranunculus auricomus.
