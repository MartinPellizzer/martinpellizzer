Rhododendron perulatum

Rhododendron perulatum, commonly known as the Peruvian azalea, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in cloud forests or alpine meadows. The plant features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, and the plant has a dense, compact growth habit.

Rhododendron perulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
