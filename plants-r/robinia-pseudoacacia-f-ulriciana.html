Robinia pseudoacacia f. ulriciana

Robinia pseudoacacia f. ulriciana, commonly known as the Ulriciana false acacia, is a botanical variant of the honey locust tree.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are smaller and more delicate compared to the typical species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, adding ornamental value. Its bark is smooth and light gray, with a distinctive flaky texture.

This form is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
