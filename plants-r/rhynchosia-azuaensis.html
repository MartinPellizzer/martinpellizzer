Rhynchosia azuaensis

Rhynchosia azuaensis, commonly known as the Azua bean, is a climbing legume native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain edible seeds, similar to other beans in its family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Rhynchosia azuaensis is valued both for its nutritional content and its role in ecological systems.
