Rachicallis setosa

Rachicallis setosa, commonly known as the slender goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and well-drained conditions.

Rachicallis setosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
