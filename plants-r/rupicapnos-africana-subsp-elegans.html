Rupicapnos africana subsp. elegans

Rupicapnos africana subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement and are typically covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a street tree in urban landscapes.
