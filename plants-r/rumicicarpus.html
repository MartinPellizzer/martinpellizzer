Rumicicarpus

Rumicicarpus, commonly known as the soursop or custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with broad, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is distinctive, with a spiny exterior and a soft, segmented interior containing a sweet, creamy pulp. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental and fruit-bearing species in tropical horticulture.
