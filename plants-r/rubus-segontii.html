Rubus segontii

Rubus segontii, commonly known as the Korean blackberry, is a species of bramble native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
