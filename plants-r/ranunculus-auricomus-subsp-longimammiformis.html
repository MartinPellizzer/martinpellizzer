Ranunculus auricomus subsp. longimammiformis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

longimammiformis, commonly known as the long-mammary buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features distinctive bright yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals, often with a white or pale yellow base.

The leaves are palmately divided, with lobes that are usually hairy and serrated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
