Rosa convirescens

Rosa convirescens, commonly known as the evergreen rose, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, thorny stems and broad, dark green leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, usually blooming in spring and summer. Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal environments.

Rosa convirescens is well adapted to limestone soils and is often cultivated for its beauty and resilience.
