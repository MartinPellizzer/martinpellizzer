Rubia intricata

Rubia intricata, commonly known as the intricate borage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia and is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are typically slender and branching, and it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Rubia intricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
