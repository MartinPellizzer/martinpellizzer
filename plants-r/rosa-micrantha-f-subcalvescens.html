Rosa micrantha f. subcalvescens

Rosa micrantha f. subcalvescens, commonly known as the dwarf shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, thornless stems and dark green, elliptical leaves with finely serrated edges. Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a decorative hedge.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in cooler climates.
