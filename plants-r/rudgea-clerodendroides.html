Rudgea clerodendroides

Rudgea clerodendroides, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to violet berries that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, adding to its ornamental appeal.
