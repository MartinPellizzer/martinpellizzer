Roella elegans

Roella elegans, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Roella elegans is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and showy blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
