Rubus kuntzeanus

Rubus kuntzeanus, commonly known as the Kuntze's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
