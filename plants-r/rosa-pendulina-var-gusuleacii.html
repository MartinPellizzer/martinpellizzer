Rosa pendulina var. gusuleacii

Rosa pendulina var. gusuleacii, commonly known as the hanging rose or weeping rose, is a variant of the pendulous rose species.

It is characterized by its long, drooping stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pink to red blooms, which appear in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and ability to create a cascading effect in landscapes.
