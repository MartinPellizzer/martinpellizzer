Rhaphiolepis mertensii

Rhaphiolepis mertensii, commonly known as the Chinese firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the autumn. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental use. It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

However, its berries are toxic to humans and animals, requiring caution in cultivation.
