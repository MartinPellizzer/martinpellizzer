Ranunculus macounii

Ranunculus macounii, commonly known as Macoun's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
