Rubus canescens var. rhodopaeus

Rubus canescens var. rhodopaeus, commonly known as the pink-fruited raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year producing vegetative growth and the second year bearing fruit. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically pink to red in color, which are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable plant for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
