Reevesia pycnantha

Reevesia pycnantha, commonly known as the golden bell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Reevesia pycnantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
