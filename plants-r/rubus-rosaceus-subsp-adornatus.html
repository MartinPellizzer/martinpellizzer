Rubus rosaceus subsp. adornatus

Rubus rosaceus subsp.

adornatus, commonly known as the pink-fruited raspberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and biennial stems that bear flowers and fruits.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically pink or red in color, which are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant flowers and vibrant fruiting structures.
