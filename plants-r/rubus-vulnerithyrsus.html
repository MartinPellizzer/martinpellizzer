Rubus vulnerithyrsus

Rubus vulnerithyrsus, commonly known as the scarlet bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black in color, though they are often bitter and not widely consumed. It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
