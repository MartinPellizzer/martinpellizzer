Rosa pimpinellifolia subvar. fissisepala

Rosa pimpinellifolia subvar. fissisepala, commonly known as the fissisepal rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in spring. The flowers are pink to red in color, with distinctive, narrowly elliptical sepals that give the plant its scientific name. The plant produces oval, yellowish fruits known as hip fruits, which contain seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in full sun.
