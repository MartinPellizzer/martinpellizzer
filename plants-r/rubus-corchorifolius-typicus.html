Rubus corchorifolius typicus

Rubus corchorifolius typicus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are used in traditional Chinese medicine and cuisine.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
