Ruta chalepensis subsp. bracteosa

Ruta chalepensis subsp.

bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate rue, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves and small yellowish flowers that appear in spring.

The plant produces small, fleshy, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by the presence of bracts beneath the flowers, which are modified leaves.
