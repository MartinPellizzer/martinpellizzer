Rubus × erythracanthus

Rubus × erythracanthus, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus pectoralis.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and large, aggregate fruits that typically range in color from dark purple to black. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces showy white or pink flowers in the spring.

This species is valued for its edible berries, which have a tart flavor and are used in culinary applications.
