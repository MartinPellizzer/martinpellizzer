Rubus candicans var. stenotrichus

Rubus candicans var. stenotrichus, commonly known as the stinging raspberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially hazardous due to its stinging hairs.
