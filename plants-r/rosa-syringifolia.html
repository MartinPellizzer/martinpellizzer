Rosa syringifolia

Rosa syringifolia, commonly known as the Chinese rose or evergreen rose, is a shrub native to China and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. Rosa syringifolia is evergreen, retaining its foliage throughout the year, and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden borders and hedges.
