Rolfea

Rolfea, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow blooms that resemble dandelion flowers, often appearing in clusters. Rolfea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
