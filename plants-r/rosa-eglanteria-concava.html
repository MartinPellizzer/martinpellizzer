Rosa eglanteria concava

Rosa eglanteria concava, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces oval, yellowish fruits that are edible but often covered in a sticky resin. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy shrub suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
