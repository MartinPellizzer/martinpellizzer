Reichardia scandens

Reichardia scandens, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has smooth, waxy leaves that are typically alternate and have a slightly heart-shaped base. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Reichardia scandens is known for its rapid spread and is often considered a weed in agricultural and garden settings.
