Rubus idaeopsis

Rubus idaeopsis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems, compound leaves, and small, dark purple to black fruits. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are prized for their tart flavor.

The species is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many other brambles and blackberries.
