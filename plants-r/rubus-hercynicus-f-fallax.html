Rubus hercynicus f. fallax

Rubus hercynicus f. fallax, commonly known as the Hercynian blackberry, is a subspecies of the blackberry plant native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
