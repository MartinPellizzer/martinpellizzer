Rubus colemannii var. kretzeri

Rubus colemannii var. kretzeri, commonly known as the Kretzer's blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and edible fruits, though it is less commonly cultivated than other blackberry species.
