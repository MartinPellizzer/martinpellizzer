Rhododendron jinpingense

Rhododendron jinpingense, commonly known as the Jinping rhododendron, is a species native to China, specifically the Yunnan province.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with high humidity and acidic soils. Its botanical features include elliptical leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
