Rhododendron thyodocum

Rhododendron thyodocum, commonly known as the Thai rhododendron, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in humid, mountainous regions with rich, acidic soil. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom in shades of pink to red, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
