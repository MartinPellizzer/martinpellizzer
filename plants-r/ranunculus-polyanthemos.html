Ranunculus polyanthemos

Ranunculus polyanthemos, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has palmately divided leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
