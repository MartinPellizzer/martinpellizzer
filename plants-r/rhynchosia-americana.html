Rhynchosia americana

Rhynchosia americana, commonly known as the American cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Rhynchosia americana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural systems.
