Rhipsalis capilliformis

Rhipsalis capilliformis, commonly known as the threadleaf mistletoe, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a type of epiphytic cactus, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic. The plant has thin, hair-like stems that give it its common name, and it produces small, white or yellowish flowers. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Rhipsalis capilliformis is also known for its fragrant blooms and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
