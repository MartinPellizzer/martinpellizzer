Rubus × bertolonii

Rubus × bertolonii, commonly known as the Bertoloni raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the genus Rubus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruits are edible, though they are smaller than those of other raspberry varieties.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited commercial cultivation.
