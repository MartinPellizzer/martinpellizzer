Racemobambos congesta

Racemobambos congesta, commonly known as the African bamboo, is a unique plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought.

Its structure resembles bamboo, hence its common name, and it is often used in traditional settings for various purposes.
