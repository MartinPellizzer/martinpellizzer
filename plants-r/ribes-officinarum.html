Ribes officinarum

Ribes officinarum, commonly known as the European gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, tart berries that are typically green when unripe and yellow or red when mature. The plant has alternate, simple, and often pubescent leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are usually yellow, with five petals, and appear in clusters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits, which are used in jams and desserts.
