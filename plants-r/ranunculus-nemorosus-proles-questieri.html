Ranunculus nemorosus proles questieri

Ranunculus nemorosus proles questieri, commonly known as the questier's wood anemone, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in shaded, moist woodland environments. The plant features a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its finely divided leaves and its preference for calcareous soils.

This species is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is noted for its early spring bloom.
