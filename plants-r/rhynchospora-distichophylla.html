Rhynchospora distichophylla

Rhynchospora distichophylla, commonly known as the two-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, arranged in spikelets. Rhynchospora distichophylla thrives in moist soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important indicator species for wetland health and biodiversity.
