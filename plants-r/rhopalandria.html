Rhopalandria

Rhopalandria, commonly known as the "beard flower," is a unique genus within the orchid family.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring a specialized appendage called a rhopalon, which gives the plant its name. The flowers are typically small and clustered, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects. Rhopalandria species are primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments.
