Ripidium dichotomum

Ripidium dichotomum, commonly known as the dichotomous fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive branching pattern.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features fronds that split into two equal parts, giving it its name. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a dark green color.

Ripidium dichotomum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.
