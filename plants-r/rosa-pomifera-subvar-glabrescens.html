Rosa pomifera subvar. glabrescens

Rosa pomifera subvar. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-fruited rose, is a variant of the rose species characterized by its smooth, hairless fruit.

This plant is native to certain regions of Asia and is known for its ornamental value due to its fragrant flowers and edible hips. The leaves are typically pinnately compound, with serrated margins, and the stems are covered in thorns. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The subvariety is distinguished by its reduced pubescence compared to other Rosa pomifera varieties.
