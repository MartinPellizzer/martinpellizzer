Rosa × dumetorum f. umbrosa

Rosa × dumetorum f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shade rose or alpine rose, is a compact, low-growing shrub with dark green, velvety foliage.

It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the alpine regions of Europe and is adapted to cool, moist, and shady environments. It has a dense, spreading habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ornamental choice for woodland gardens.
