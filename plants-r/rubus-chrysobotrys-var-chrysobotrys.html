Rubus chrysobotrys var. chrysobotrys

Rubus chrysobotrys var. chrysobotrys, commonly known as the goldenflower raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, doubly serrate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its fruits are aggregate berries that ripen to a deep red. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
