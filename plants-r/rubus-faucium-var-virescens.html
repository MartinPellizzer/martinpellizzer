Rubus faucium var. virescens

Rubus faucium var. virescens, commonly known as the green-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that often have a slightly greenish hue when ripe. The plant typically grows in wooded areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are small, white, and clustered in the axils of the leaves.

This variety is valued for its flavorful berries, which are used in culinary applications and local foraging.
