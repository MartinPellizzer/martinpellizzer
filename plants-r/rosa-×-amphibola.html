Rosa × amphibola

Rosa × amphibola, commonly known as the amphibola rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its semi-evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Rosa × amphibola is a hardy shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, making it a striking addition to mixed borders.
