Rhaphiolepis obovata

Rhaphiolepis obovata, commonly known as the Japanese rose or evergreen rose.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to East Asia, characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it suitable for hedges or container gardening.
