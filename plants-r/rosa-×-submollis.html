Rosa × submollis

Rosa × submollis, commonly known as the silky rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Rosa × submollis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and relatively disease-resistant nature.
