Rhaphidophora lagunensis

Rhaphidophora lagunensis, commonly known as the Panama sword fern, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Rhaphidophora lagunensis is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and air-purifying qualities.
