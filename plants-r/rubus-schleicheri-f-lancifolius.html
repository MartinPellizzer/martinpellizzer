Rubus schleicheri f. lancifolius

Rubus schleicheri f. lancifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves.

It is native to regions of eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its botanical features include thorny stems and a shrub-like growth habit.

This subspecies is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary potential.
