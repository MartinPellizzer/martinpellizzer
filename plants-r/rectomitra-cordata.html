Rectomitra cordata

Rectomitra cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf miteplant, is a unique succulent native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are heart-shaped and often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its succulent nature allows it to store water in its leaves, making it adapted to arid environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a somewhat elusive and specialized species in the botanical world.
