Rosa canina var. pseudoacharii

Rosa canina var. pseudoacharii, commonly known as the pseudoacharii rose, is a variant of the dog rose, characterized by its distinctive floral and vegetative features.

It typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in early summer. The plant has thorny stems and pinnate leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Rosa genus. Its fruits are aggregate berries that mature to a red color, containing multiple achenes.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for both ornamental and historical botanical significance.
