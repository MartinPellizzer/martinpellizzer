Rubus sinkoensis

Rubus sinkoensis, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes various shrubs and wild berries. The plant produces small, dark red to black fruits that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant has thorny stems typical of many Rubus species.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
