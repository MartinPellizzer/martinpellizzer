Rhododendron facetum

Rhododendron facetum, commonly known as the smoothleaf rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, smooth-textured leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring blooms.

Rhododendron facetum is also noted for its relatively low maintenance requirements once established.
