Rubus × intractabilis

Rubus × intractabilis, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is native to North America and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, with serrated margins, and its flowers are typically pink or white.

The fruit, which is the most commercially valuable part, is a dark purple to black berry with a sweet-tart flavor.
