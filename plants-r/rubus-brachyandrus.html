Rubus brachyandrus

Rubus brachyandrus, commonly known as the short-styled blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
