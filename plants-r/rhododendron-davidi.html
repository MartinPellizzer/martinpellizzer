Rhododendron davidi

Rhododendron davidi, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron davidi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
