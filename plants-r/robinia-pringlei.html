Robinia pringlei

Robinia pringlei, commonly known as the Mexican honeylocust, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are divided into small, oval-shaped leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by long, slender pods containing edible seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
