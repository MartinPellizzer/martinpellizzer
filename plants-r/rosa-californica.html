Rosa californica

Rosa californica, commonly known as the California wild rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptic leaflets and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with thorny stems that provide structural support. Its fruit is a red, aggregate berry that contains multiple seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Rosa californica is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
