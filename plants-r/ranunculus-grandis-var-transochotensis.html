Ranunculus grandis var. transochotensis

Ranunculus grandis var. transochotensis, commonly known as the Trans-Chilean buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, specifically found in Chile and Argentina.

It features delicate, palmate leaves with a hairy surface and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. Its flowers are showy and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
