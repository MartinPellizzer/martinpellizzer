Rauhocereus

Rauhocereus, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a genus of cacti native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and large, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plants are typically low-growing and have a columnar or tree-like structure. They are adapted to arid environments and store water in their thick stems.

Rauhocereus species are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and unique blooming habits.
