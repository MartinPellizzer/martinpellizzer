Rostellaria crinita

Rostellaria crinita, commonly known as the hairy rostellaria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences. The plant features elongated, hairy stems and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
