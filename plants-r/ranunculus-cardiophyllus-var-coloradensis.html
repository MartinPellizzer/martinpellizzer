Ranunculus cardiophyllus var. coloradensis

Ranunculus cardiophyllus var. coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinct central stigma. Native to the mountainous regions of Colorado, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows and along streambanks at higher elevations.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
