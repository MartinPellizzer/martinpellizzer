Rubus × pseudosubcalvus

Rubus × pseudosubcalvus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually trifoliate with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are dark purple to black when ripe and are often used in culinary applications.
