Rubus lanceipetalus

Rubus lanceipetalus, commonly known as the lance-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically consumed by wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many other brambles and blackberry species.
