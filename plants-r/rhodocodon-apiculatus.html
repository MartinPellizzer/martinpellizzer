Rhodocodon apiculatus

Rhodocodon apiculatus, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and have a serrated edge, while its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe.

Rhodocodon apiculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
