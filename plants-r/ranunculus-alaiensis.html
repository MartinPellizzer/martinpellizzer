Ranunculus alaiensis

Ranunculus alaiensis, commonly known as the Alai buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic compounds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
