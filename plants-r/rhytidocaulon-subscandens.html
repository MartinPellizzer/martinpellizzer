Rhytidocaulon subscandens

Rhytidocaulon subscandens, commonly known as the hairy leaf cactus, is a unique succulent native to arid regions of South America.

It features a short, thick stem with numerous fleshy, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often covered in fine, white hairs, providing additional insulation and reducing transpiration.

This species is well adapted to extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its natural habitat.
