Rhododendron bivelatum

Rhododendron bivelatum, commonly known as the two-lobed rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, often with a bluish-green hue.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as part of natural landscapes.
