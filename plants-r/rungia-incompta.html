Rungia incompta

Rungia incompta, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from purple to red, often with intricate patterns. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is known for its complex floral structure.
