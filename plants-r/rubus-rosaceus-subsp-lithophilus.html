Rubus rosaceus subsp. lithophilus

Rubus rosaceus subsp.

lithophilus, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically reddish or yellowish in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
