Rhododendron notatum

Rhododendron notatum, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to white flowers in the spring, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a leathery texture.

Rhododendron notatum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
