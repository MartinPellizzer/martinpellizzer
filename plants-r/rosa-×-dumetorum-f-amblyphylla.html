Rosa × dumetorum f. amblyphylla

Rosa × dumetorum f. amblyphylla, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and thorny stems, often forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color, containing several druplets. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets that are serrated and covered in fine hairs.

This species is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, hedgerows, and woodland edges.
