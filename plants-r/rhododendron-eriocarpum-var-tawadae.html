Rhododendron eriocarpum var. tawadae

Rhododendron eriocarpum var. tawadae, commonly known as the Tawada rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features broad, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its dense foliage and long-lasting blooms.
