Ranunculus auricomus subsp. percrassus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

percrassus, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features large, showy yellow flowers with bright orange centers, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has palmately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the Ranunculaceae family.
