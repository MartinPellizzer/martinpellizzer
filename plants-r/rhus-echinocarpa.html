Rhus echinocarpa

Rhus echinocarpa, commonly known as the thimbleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like poison ivy and mango. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, red berries that turn yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and streambanks.

The berries are edible when fully mature but can be mildly toxic if consumed unripe.
