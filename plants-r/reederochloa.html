Reederochloa

Reederochloa, commonly known as blue oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its striking blue-green foliage, which becomes more vivid in full sun. The plant produces panicles of small, whitish to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. Reederochloa is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a low-maintenance choice for landscaping.

Its fine texture and color make it a popular choice for borders, rock gardens, and as a background plant in mixed plantings.
