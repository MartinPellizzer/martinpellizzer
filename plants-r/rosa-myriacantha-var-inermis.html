Rosa myriacantha var. inermis

Rosa myriacantha var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth thorned rose, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, thornless stems and is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces oval, glossy leaves with serrated edges, and its fruits are small, red, aggregate berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and wildlife value.
