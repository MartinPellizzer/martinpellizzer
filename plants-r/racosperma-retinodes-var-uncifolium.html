Racosperma retinodes var. uncifolium

Racosperma retinodes var. uncifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptical in shape. This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as a deep root system and reduced leaf surface area.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
