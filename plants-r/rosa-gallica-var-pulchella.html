Rosa gallica var. pulchella

Rosa gallica var. pulchella, commonly known as the French pink or Queen of the roses, is a hybrid rose species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent in many modern rose cultivars.
