Rubus foliosus f. lobatoserratus

Rubus foliosus f. lobatoserratus, commonly known as the lobed-leaved bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its lobed and serrated leaf margins, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. It typically grows in temperate regions, thriving in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
