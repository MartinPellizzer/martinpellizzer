Rosa lutea var. multiplex

Rosa lutea var. multiplex, commonly known as the yellow rose bush, is a variant of the yellow rose species native to Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with multiple stems and has serrated, dark green leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and fragrant blooms.
