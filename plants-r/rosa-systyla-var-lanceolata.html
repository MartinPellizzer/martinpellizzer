Rosa systyla var. lanceolata

Rosa systyla var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate shrub rose, is a variant of the shrub rose species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated for both its beauty and resilience.
