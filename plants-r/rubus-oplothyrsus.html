Rubus oplothyrsus

Rubus oplothyrsus, commonly known as the Natal blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
