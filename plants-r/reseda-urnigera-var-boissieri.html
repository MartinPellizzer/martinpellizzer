Reseda urnigera var. boissieri

Reseda urnigera var. boissieri, commonly known as the Armenian Reseda, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a preference for well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded habitats.

It is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
