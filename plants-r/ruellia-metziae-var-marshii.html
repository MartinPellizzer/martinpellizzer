Ruellia metziae var. marshii

Ruellia metziae var. marshii, commonly known as the Marsh Ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate water conditions.
