Rubus caesius roseus

Rubus caesius roseus, commonly known as the red-berried rosehip plant, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, oval-shaped berries that are rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants. Its leaves are alternate, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have five petals and numerous stamens.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the medicinal properties of its berries.
