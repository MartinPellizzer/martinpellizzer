Rubus hebecarpos subsp. vagabundus

Rubus hebecarpos subsp.

vagabundus, commonly known as the wandering blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in local ecosystems.
