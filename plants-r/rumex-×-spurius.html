Rumex × spurius

Rumex × spurius, commonly known as the common dock, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wet meadows.

Rumex × spurius is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
