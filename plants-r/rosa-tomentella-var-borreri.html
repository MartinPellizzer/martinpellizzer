Rosa tomentella var. borreri

Rosa tomentella var. borreri, commonly known as the Borreri's woolly rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white pubescent foliage and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to coastal and arid environments. It produces oval, red-orange hips that are edible and contain seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in garden settings.
