Ranunculus infuscus

Ranunculus infuscus, commonly known as the dark ranunculus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with prominent black centers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

Ranunculus infuscus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
