Rubus iscanus

Rubus iscanus, commonly known as the Iscanus blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs known for their edible fruits.
