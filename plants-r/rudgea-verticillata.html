Rudgea verticillata

Rudgea verticillata, commonly known as the clustered rudgea, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Rudgea verticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to native pollinators.
