Ranunculus subsagittatus

Ranunculus subsagittatus, commonly known as the arrowhead buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves that resemble arrowheads, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland areas and stream sides.

Ranunculus subsagittatus is often found in calcareous soils and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
