Rhododendron catesbaeum

Rhododendron catesbaeum, commonly known as the swamp rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in wet, acidic soils along the edges of swamps and marshes. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in the spring, which are borne on long, pendulous clusters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctive reddish or bronze coloration on the underside.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
