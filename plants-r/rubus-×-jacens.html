Rubus × jacens

Rubus × jacens, commonly known as blackberry bramble.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit, a cluster of small drupes, is edible and widely appreciated for its flavor and nutritional value.
