Rhododendron brevipes

Rhododendron brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
