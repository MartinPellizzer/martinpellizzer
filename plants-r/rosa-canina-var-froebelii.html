Rosa canina var. froebelii

Rosa canina var. froebelii, commonly known as the froebelii rose, is a variant of the dog rose, characterized by its distinctive pink flowers and thorny stems.

It is a deciduous shrub native to central and eastern Europe, often found in woodland edges and hedgerows. The plant produces small, red berries that are rich in vitamin C and are used in traditional medicine. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing ornamental value throughout the growing season.

This species is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
