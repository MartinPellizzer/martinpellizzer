Rosa galbana

Rosa galbana, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caucasus region and parts of Turkey.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright, thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves that are oval in shape. Rosa galbana is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
