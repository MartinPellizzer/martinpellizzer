Rhynchosia reptabunda

Rhynchosia reptabunda, commonly known as the climbing snout bean, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Rhynchosia reptabunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
