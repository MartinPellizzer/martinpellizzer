Rosa cuspidens

Rosa cuspidens, commonly known as the hooked rose, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. Rosa cuspidens typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
