Rhamnus prinoides var. obtusifolia

Rhamnus prinoides var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaved buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and have a blunt tip, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue, egg-shaped berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
