Ruschia abbreviata

Ruschia abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf ice plant, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its compact size and drought tolerance, it is well-suited for coastal and arid environments.
