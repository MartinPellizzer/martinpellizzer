Roupala princeps

Roupala princeps, commonly known as the yellow roupala, is a small evergreen tree native to the subtropical regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, greyish-brown bark. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in winter, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and remain green throughout the year, making it an attractive ornamental plant.

Roupala princeps is well-suited to warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
