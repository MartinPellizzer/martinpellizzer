Rhynchosia erecta

Rhynchosia erecta, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, hairy pods that contain flat, kidney-shaped seeds. Rhynchosia erecta is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and oil.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
