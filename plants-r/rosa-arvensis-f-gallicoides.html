Rosa arvensis f. gallicoides

Rosa arvensis f. gallicoides, commonly known as the Gallicoid wild rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Rosa genus. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along woodland edges.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
