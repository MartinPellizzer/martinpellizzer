Ranunculus pusillus var. lindheimeri

Ranunculus pusillus var. lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's dwarf buttercup, is a small herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and bright yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open woodlands and canyons. It is characterized by its compact size and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
