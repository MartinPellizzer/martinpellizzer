Racosperma heteroneurum var. petilum

Racosperma heteroneurum var. petilum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its growth habit is typically upright, with branches that are somewhat woody and can become branched over time.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
