Rubus trichopus

Rubus trichopus, commonly known as the hooked blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible but often tart, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
