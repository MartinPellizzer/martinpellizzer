Rumex occultans

Rumex occultans, commonly known as the ghost sorrel, is a rare and elusive plant species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are usually covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed. Rumex occultans thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Due to its rarity and limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
