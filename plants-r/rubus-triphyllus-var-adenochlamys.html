Rubus triphyllus var. adenochlamys

Rubus triphyllus var. adenochlamys, commonly known as the red raspberry.

This variant is a shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The flowers are white and are followed by the characteristic raspberry fruits that are rich in antioxidants.
