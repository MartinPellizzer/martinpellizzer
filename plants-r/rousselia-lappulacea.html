It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy leaves, which are adaptations to its often harsh, arid habitats. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, preferring full sun exposure. Its fruit is a short, beak-like silique containing several seeds.

Rousselia lappulacea is native to regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in open, disturbed environments.
