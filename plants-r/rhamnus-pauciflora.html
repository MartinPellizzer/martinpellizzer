Rhamnus pauciflora

Rhamnus pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered buckthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. Rhamnus pauciflora is adapted to poor soils and can thrive in areas with low rainfall.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
