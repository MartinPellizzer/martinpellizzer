Ribes maximoviczii var. umbrosum

Ribes maximoviczii var. umbrosum, commonly known as the darkleaf gooseberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of flowering plants commonly found in temperate regions. The plant is characterized by its dark green, almost black leaves that give it its common name. It produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically edible but not widely cultivated.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
