Rubus × breviatus

Rubus × breviatus, commonly known as the short-stemmed blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its compact growth habit and dark purple to black berries. The plant typically has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typical features of many Rubus species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its berries are edible, though they may have a tart flavor and are sometimes used in jams or wines.
