Ranunculus pseudohirculus

Ranunculus pseudohirculus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in rocky and alpine habitats. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the genus Ranunculus. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it often grows in clusters along the ground.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often found in moist, well-drained soils at high elevations.
