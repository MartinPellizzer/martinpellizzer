Renealmia caribaea

Renealmia caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean region.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of freshwater bodies. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in its habitat.

Renealmia caribaea plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
