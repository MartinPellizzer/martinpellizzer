Rhododendron henryi

Rhododendron henryi, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. It is often found in forested areas at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.

Rhododendron henryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
