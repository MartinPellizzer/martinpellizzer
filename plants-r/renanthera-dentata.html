Renanthera dentata

Renanthera dentata, commonly known as the toothed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive toothed pattern on the lip.

Renanthera dentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
