Rosa stylosa subsp. monsignatica

Rosa stylosa subsp.

monsignatica, commonly known as the Mona's rose, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is typically found in moist, woodland habitats.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the stems are often covered with fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
