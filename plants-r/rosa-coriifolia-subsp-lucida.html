Rosa coriifolia subsp. lucida

Rosa coriifolia subsp.

lucida, commonly known as the shining dogwood rose, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered flowers that resemble small roses, typically pink or white in color.

The plant features thornless stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
