Raphanus sativus var. lobo

Raphanus sativus var. lobo, commonly known as the bulbous radish, is a cultivated variety of the radish plant characterized by its swollen, globe-shaped root.

This plant is typically grown for its edible root, which is crisp and slightly spicy in flavor. It thrives in cool weather and prefers well-drained, loose soil to promote healthy root development. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower.

Its growth cycle is relatively short, making it a popular choice for home gardeners and commercial growers alike.
