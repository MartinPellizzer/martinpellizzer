Rubus thyrsoideus subsp. incisiserratus

Rubus thyrsoideus subsp.

incisiserratus, commonly known as the serrated blackberry. It is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its edible berries and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
