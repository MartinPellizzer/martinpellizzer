Rubus kotoensis

Rubus kotoensis, commonly known as the Koto raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs commonly referred to as blackberries, raspberries, and dewberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The botanical features of Rubus kotoensis make it an important plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
