Rubus mutabilis var. naldrettii

Rubus mutabilis var. naldrettii, commonly known as the Naldrett's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that are typically small and have a firm, tart flavor. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in local culinary traditions.
