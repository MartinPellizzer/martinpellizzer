Ranunculus cupulatus

Ranunculus cupulatus, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a sprawling growth habit with numerous erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically blooming from May to July. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are deeply lobed and hairy.

Ranunculus cupulatus is often cultivated as a ornamental plant and is also used for soil stabilization in erosion-prone areas.
