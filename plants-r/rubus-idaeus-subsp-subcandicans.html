Rubus idaeus subsp. subcandicans

Rubus idaeus subsp.

subcandicans, commonly known as the white-berried raspberry, is a subspecies of the red raspberry that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink fruits that differ from the typical red berries of the main species. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The subspecies is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its unique fruit.
