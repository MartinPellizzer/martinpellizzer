Rubus godronii var. obtusiflorens

Rubus godronii var. obtusiflorens, commonly known as Godron's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a resilient plant in arid regions.
