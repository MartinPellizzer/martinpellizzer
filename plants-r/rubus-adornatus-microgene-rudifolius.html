Rubus adornatus microgene rudifolius

Rubus adornatus microgene rudifolius, commonly known as the ornate raspberry.

This plant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The species is native to certain regions of South America and thrives in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for study in plant morphology and ecology.
