Rubus niveus var. membranaceus

Rubus niveus var. membranaceus, commonly known as the white-bracted raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its flowers are white with five petals and are arranged in clusters.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
