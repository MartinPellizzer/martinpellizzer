Rubus erythrandrus

Rubus erythrandrus, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into dark purple to black berries, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its fruit is valued for its flavor and is a popular ingredient in jams and desserts.
