Rosa canina f. cladoleia

Rosa canina f. cladoleia, commonly known as the clustered dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and smaller, more compact flowers compared to the typical form. The plant features thorny stems and pinnate leaves with serrated margins. Its flowers are usually pink or pale yellow, and it produces red, hairy fruits known as hips.

This form is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows, thriving in well-drained soils.
