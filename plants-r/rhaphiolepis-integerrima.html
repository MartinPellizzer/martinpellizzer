Rhaphiolepis integerrima

Rhaphiolepis integerrima, commonly known as the Japanese laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. These flowers are followed by bright red berries that persist into winter, adding ornamental value.

It is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
