Rhamnus granulosa

Rhamnus granulosa, commonly known as the granular buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has small, oval-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark, glossy berries in the fall. Its bark is smooth and often has a granular texture, giving it its common name.

Rhamnus granulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
