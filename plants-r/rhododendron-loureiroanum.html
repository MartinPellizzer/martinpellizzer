Rhododendron loureiroanum

Rhododendron loureiroanum, commonly known as the Loreiro's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, humid climates. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, and it produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers in the spring. Rhododendron loureiroanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and evergreen foliage.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for shaded environments and rocky soils.
