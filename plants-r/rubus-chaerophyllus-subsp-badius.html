Rubus chaerophyllus subsp. badius

Rubus chaerophyllus subsp.

badius, commonly known as the purple-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to dark purple, depending on maturity. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local use.
