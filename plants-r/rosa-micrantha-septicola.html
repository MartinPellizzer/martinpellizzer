Rosa micrantha septicola

Rosa micrantha septicola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant has narrow, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often covered in thorns. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.

Rosa micrantha septicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
