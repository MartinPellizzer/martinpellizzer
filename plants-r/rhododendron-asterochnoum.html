Rhododendron asterochnoum

Rhododendron asterochnoum, commonly known as the star-leaved rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a distinctive star-like pattern on the underside. The plant prefers acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a component of natural forest ecosystems.
