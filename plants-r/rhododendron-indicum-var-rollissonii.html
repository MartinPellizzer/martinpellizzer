Rhododendron indicum var. rollissonii

Rhododendron indicum var. rollissonii, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include serrated leaf margins and a compact growth habit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
