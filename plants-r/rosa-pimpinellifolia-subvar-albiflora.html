Rosa pimpinellifolia subvar. albiflora

Rosa pimpinellifolia subvar. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, often thornless stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in rocky or coastal environments.
