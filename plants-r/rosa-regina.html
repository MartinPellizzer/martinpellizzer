Rosa regina

Rosa regina, commonly known as the Queen's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

Rosa regina blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and landscapes.
