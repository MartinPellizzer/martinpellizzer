Rubus yui

Rubus yui, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a species of brambles native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or black when ripe. Rubus yui thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
