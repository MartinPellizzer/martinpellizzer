Rubus moestus

Rubus moestus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of brambles native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Rubus moestus is also an important species for pollinators and wildlife due to its nectar-rich flowers and edible fruits.
