Rosa mandenovii

Rosa mandenovii, commonly known as the Mandenovii rose, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of the eastern Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated margins, and its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rosa mandenovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential in horticultural and medicinal applications.
