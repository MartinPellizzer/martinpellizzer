Ruellia paucinervia

Ruellia paucinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with prominent, parallel veins. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, arranged in terminal spikes, and are attractive to pollinators.

Ruellia paucinervia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
