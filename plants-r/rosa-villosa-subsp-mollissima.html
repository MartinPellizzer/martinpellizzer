Rosa villosa subsp. mollissima

Rosa villosa subsp.

mollissima, commonly known as the soft-hairy rose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage and pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
