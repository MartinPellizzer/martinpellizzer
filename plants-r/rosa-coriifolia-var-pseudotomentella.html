Rosa coriifolia var. pseudotomentella

Rosa coriifolia var. pseudotomentella, commonly known as the silver-leafed rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers in early spring. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and compact growth habit.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
