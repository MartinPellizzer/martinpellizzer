Rorippa africana subsp. mesatlanticum

Rorippa africana subsp.

mesatlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, glossy leaves and small, white, four-petaled flowers.

This subspecies is typically found in moist, calcareous soils near water bodies in coastal regions. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in local cuisines for its peppery flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and supporting aquatic biodiversity.
