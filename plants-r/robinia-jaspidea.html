Robinia jaspidea

Robinia jaspidea, commonly known as the yellow false acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It features compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, with slender, yellowish-green leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in racemose inflorescences, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.

The species is also cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
