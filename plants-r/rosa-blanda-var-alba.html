Rosa blanda var. alba

Rosa blanda var. alba, commonly known as the white woodland rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features fragrant, white single flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a soft, velvety texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
