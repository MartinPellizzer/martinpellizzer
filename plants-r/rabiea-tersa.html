Rabiea tersa

Rabiea tersa, commonly known as the slender lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and its flowering spikes that bear small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Rabiea tersa is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
