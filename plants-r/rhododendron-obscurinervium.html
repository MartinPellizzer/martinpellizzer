Rhododendron obscurinervium

Rhododendron obscurinervium, commonly known as the dark-veined rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are dark green with prominent, dark veins, and the flowers range in color from pink to purple.

Rhododendron obscurinervium is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitat.
