Rubus serpens var. gymnostylos

Rubus serpens var. gymnostylos, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

It is a shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
