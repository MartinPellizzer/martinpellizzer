Rubus polyanthemus microgene duffortii

Rubus polyanthemus microgene duffortii, commonly known as the Duffort's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small white or pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
