Rubus lambertianus var. lambertianus

Rubus lambertianus var. lambertianus, commonly known as Lambert's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
