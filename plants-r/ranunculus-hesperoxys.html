Ranunculus hesperoxys

Ranunculus hesperoxys, commonly known as the western buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces a cluster of small, greenish fruits after flowering.

Ranunculus hesperoxys is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
