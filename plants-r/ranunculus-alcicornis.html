Ranunculus alcicornis

Ranunculus alcicornis, commonly known as the horned buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with five petals and prominent, horn-like sepals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, bright yellow flowers.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
