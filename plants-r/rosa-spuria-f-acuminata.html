Rosa spuria f. acuminata

Rosa spuria f. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved wild rose, is a botanical variant of the wild rose species Rosa spuria.

It is characterized by its acuminate (sharply pointed) leaflets and pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in open woodlands and rocky areas. Its thorny stems and aromatic foliage make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
