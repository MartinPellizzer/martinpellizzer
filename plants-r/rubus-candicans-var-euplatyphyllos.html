Rubus candicans var. euplatyphyllos

Rubus candicans var. euplatyphyllos, commonly known as the broad-leafed raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often reddish or orange in color, similar to other raspberry species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
