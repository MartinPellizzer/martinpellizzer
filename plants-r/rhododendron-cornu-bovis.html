Rhododendron cornu-bovis

Rhododendron cornu-bovis, commonly known as the horned rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in dense forests, thriving in cool, moist environments with acidic soil. Its leaves are usually dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Rhododendron cornu-bovis is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a significant role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
