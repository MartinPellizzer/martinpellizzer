Rubus × virescens

Rubus × virescens, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces small, edible blackberries throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its fruit is highly valued for its tart flavor and is often used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it exhibits traits of both its parent species, Rubus ursinus and Rubus pectoralis, making it a notable example of hybrid vigor in the Rubus genus.
