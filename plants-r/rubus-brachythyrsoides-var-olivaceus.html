Rubus brachythyrsoides var. olivaceus

Rubus brachythyrsoides var. olivaceus, commonly known as the olive-bracted raspberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its flowers are white to pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

This variant is distinguished by its olive-green bracts, which give it its specific epithet.
