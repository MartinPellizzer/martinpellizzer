Rorippa islandica var. nikkoensis

Rorippa islandica var. nikkoensis, commonly known as the Nikko watercress, is a variant of the watercress species native to Japan.

It is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic plant that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats near streams and waterfalls. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with small white flowers that bloom in early summer. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, flowing water.

This variety is valued for its peppery flavor and is used in traditional Japanese cuisine.
