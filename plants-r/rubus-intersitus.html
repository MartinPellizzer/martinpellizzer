Rubus intersitus

Rubus intersitus, commonly known as the common blackberry bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are edible and widely used in culinary applications. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Rubus intersitus is also valued for its ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
