Rubus × insignitifrons

Rubus × insignitifrons, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep red color. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its edible berries and is often cultivated in gardens and commercial settings.
