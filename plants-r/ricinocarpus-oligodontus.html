Ricinocarpus oligodontus

Ricinocarpus oligodontus, commonly known as the mangrove fern, is a species of flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and is often found in brackish or saline wetlands. The plant has a unique ability to tolerate high salinity, making it an important component of mangrove ecosystems. Its roots help stabilize coastal soils, preventing erosion.

Ricinocarpus oligodontus plays a vital role in the ecological balance of tidal zones and supports diverse marine and terrestrial life.
