Rubus multibracteatus var. lobatisepalus

Rubus multibracteatus var. lobatisepalus, commonly known as the broad-sepaled blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its robust growth and distinctive leaf morphology, featuring broad, lobed leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a sweet to tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued for both ornamental and edible purposes.
