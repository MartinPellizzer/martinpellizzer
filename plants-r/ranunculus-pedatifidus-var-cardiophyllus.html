Ranunculus pedatifidus var. cardiophyllus

Ranunculus pedatifidus var. cardiophyllus, commonly known as the heart-leaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Ranunculaceae family.
