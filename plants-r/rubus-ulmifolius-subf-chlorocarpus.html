Rubus ulmifolius subf. chlorocarpus

Rubus ulmifolius subf.

chlorocarpus, commonly known as the green-fruited bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit is a cluster of small, green drupes that mature to a reddish color. This subsp. is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and other moist, shaded habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
