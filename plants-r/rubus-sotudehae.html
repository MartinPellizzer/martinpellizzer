Rubus sotudehae

Rubus sotudehae, commonly known as the Sotudehae blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South Korea and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
