Rosa × wiegmannii

Rosa × wiegmannii, commonly known as the Canadian rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa woodsii and Rosa gymnocarpa.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is native to Canada and the northern United States. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it produces oval, red-orange hips that contain seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and gardens.
