Rumex × pannonicus

Rumex × pannonicus, commonly known as the common dock, is a hybrid plant species within the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender, branched stalks. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Rumex × pannonicus is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent and sometimes invasive species in certain environments.
