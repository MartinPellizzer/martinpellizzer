Rhamnus graeca

Rhamnus graeca, commonly known as the Greek buckthorn, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. The plant is notable for its reddish or purple berries that ripen in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Rhamnus graeca is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
