Ribes montanum

Ribes montanum, commonly known as the mountain currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and along stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven oval leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a red, edible berry that ripens in late summer, attracting various birds and small mammals.

Ribes montanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
