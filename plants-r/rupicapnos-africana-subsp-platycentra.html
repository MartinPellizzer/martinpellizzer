Rupicapnos africana subsp. platycentra

Rupicapnos africana subsp.

platycentra, commonly known as the flat-centred rock fig, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
