Rochonia cuneata

Rochonia cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often arranged in a spike. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
