Rosa rubiginosa subsp. sicula

Rosa rubiginosa subsp.

sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces oval, reddish-brown fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
