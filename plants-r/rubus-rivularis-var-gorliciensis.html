Rubus rivularis var. gorliciensis

Rubus rivularis var. gorliciensis, commonly known as the Gorlician blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found along riverbanks and in woodland edges.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in local culinary traditions.
