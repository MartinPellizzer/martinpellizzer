Rubus fuckelii subsp. adornatus

Rubus fuckelii subsp.

adornatus, commonly known as the adorned blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural interest.
