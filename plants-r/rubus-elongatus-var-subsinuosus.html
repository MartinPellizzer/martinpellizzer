Rubus elongatus var. subsinuosus

Rubus elongatus var. subsinuosus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its slender, trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with a hairy underside, and the plant often has thorny stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

The fruit is edible but less commonly cultivated compared to other blackberry varieties.
