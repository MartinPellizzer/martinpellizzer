Rubus bremon

Rubus bremon, commonly known as the Bremont blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
