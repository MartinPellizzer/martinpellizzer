Rosa sericea f. glabrescens

Rosa sericea f. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky rose or silverleaf rose, is a cultivar of the rose family characterized by its silvery, velvety foliage.

It is native to the western United States and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.

It is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
