Rhododendron sanguineum subsp. didymoides

Rhododendron sanguineum subsp.

didymoides, commonly known as the alpine azalea, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It has lance-shaped leaves that are typically leathery and dark green in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with specific soil and climate conditions.
