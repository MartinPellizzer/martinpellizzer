Rubus × mayerianus

Rubus × mayerianus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have serrated edges.

The fruit, which is dark purple to black in color, is edible and has a tart flavor.
