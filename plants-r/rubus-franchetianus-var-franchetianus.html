Rubus franchetianus var. franchetianus

Rubus franchetianus var. franchetianus, commonly known as the Franchet's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and is notable for its ornamental and edible qualities.
