Racosperma sphenophyllum

Racosperma sphenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in pairs along the stems. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Racosperma sphenophyllum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.
