Rhus acutidens

Rhus acutidens, commonly known as the narrowleaf sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have sharply toothed margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in cone-like clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for wildlife. Rhus acutidens thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and fields.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for various animals and birds.
