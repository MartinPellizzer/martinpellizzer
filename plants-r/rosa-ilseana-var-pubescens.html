Rosa ilseana var. pubescens

Rosa ilseana var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent rock rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pubescens.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and compact size.
