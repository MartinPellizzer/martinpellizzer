Rosa deseglisei var. adampolensis

Rosa deseglisei var. adampolensis, commonly known as the Adampolensis rose, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to specific regions of South America.

It features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique beauty and historical significance.
