Royena myrtifolia

Royena myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtleleaf rock fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
