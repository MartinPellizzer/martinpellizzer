Rosa glauca var. doleritica

Rosa glauca var. doleritica, commonly known as the Doleritic Blush Rose, is a rare and ornamental variation of the Rosa glauca species.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers with a distinctive bluish tinge, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. Its foliage is dark green with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces fragrant blooms in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its unique coloration and hardy nature, making it a sought-after addition to specialized gardens.
