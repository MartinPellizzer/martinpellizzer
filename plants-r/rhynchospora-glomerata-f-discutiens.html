Rhynchospora glomerata f. discutiens

Rhynchospora glomerata f. discutiens, commonly known as the rough spike-rush, is a variant of the spike-rush genus Rhynchospora.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence composed of small, white to pale green flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive rough or papery texture on the upper surface. This plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats across temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
