Ranunculus macauleyi var. typicus

Ranunculus macauleyi var. typicus, commonly known as the Macaulay's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic sap.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
