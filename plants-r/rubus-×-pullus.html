Rubus × pullus

Rubus × pullus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained, loamy soils. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink.

Rubus × pullus is a perennial shrub that can grow up to several meters in height, making it a prominent feature in hedgerows and woodland edges.
