Rosa hybrida var. subcordata

Rosa hybrida var. subcordata, commonly known as the Subcordate Hybrid Rose, is a cultivar characterized by its distinctive foliage and flowering habits.

This variety typically features broad, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly heart-shaped base, giving it its name. It produces large, showy blooms in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, depending on the specific cultivar. The plant is generally hardy and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes due to its aesthetic appeal and fragrance.
