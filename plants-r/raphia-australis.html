Raphia australis

Raphia australis, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a large tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach lengths of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in branched clusters, which are followed by fibrous, coconut-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts, and the plant plays an important role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various animals.

Raphia australis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
