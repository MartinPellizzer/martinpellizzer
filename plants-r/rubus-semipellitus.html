Rubus semipellitus

Rubus semipellitus, commonly known as the western blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus semipellitus is often found in woodlands, thickets, and along stream banks.
