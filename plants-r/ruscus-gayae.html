Ruscus gayae

Ruscus gayae, commonly known as the creeping euphorbia, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It features fleshy, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in rocky or dry soils, making it well adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The species is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional remedies.
