Ranunculus perusianus

Ranunculus perusianus, commonly known as the Perusian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often toxic species.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected plant in its native habitat.
