Ranunculus pastorum

Ranunculus pastorum, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are usually borne on long, slender stems.

Ranunculus pastorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
