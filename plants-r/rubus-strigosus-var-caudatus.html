Rubus strigosus var. caudatus

Rubus strigosus var. caudatus, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark red to black berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
