Ranunculus ferocior

Ranunculus ferocior, commonly known as the fierce buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which typically have five to eight petals and are surrounded by a ring of sepals. The plant has a hairy stem and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Ranunculus ferocior is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and sometimes toxic properties.
