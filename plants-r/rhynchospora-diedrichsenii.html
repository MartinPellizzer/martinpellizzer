Rhynchospora diedrichsenii

Rhynchospora diedrichsenii, commonly known as the Florida marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to wet conditions. It produces tiny, three-lobed flowers that are usually green or reddish in color.

Rhynchospora diedrichsenii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
