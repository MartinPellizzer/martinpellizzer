Rosa × christii

Rosa × christii, commonly known as the Christ's rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Rosa × christii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to coastal environments.
