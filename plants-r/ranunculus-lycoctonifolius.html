Ranunculus lycoctonifolius

Ranunculus lycoctonifolius, commonly known as the deadly nightshade, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to purple. The plant contains toxic compounds such as atropine and scopolamine, making it potentially lethal if ingested. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

Due to its toxicity, it is often avoided in gardens and requires caution when handling.
