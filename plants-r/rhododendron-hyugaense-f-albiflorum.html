Rhododendron hyugaense f. albiflorum

Rhododendron hyugaense f. albiflorum, commonly known as the Hyuga White Flower Rhododendron, is a unique cultivar of the rhododendron genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers, which contrast beautifully with its dark green, leathery foliage. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes.

This variety is prized for its horticultural appeal and is often cultivated for its aesthetic beauty in shaded environments.
