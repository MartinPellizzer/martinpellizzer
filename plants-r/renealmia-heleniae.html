Renealmia heleniae

Renealmia heleniae, commonly known as the Helen's reed, is a species of grass native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Renealmia heleniae plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
