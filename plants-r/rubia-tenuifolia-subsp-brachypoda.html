Rubia tenuifolia subsp. brachypoda

Rubia tenuifolia subsp.

brachypoda, commonly known as narrowleaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a member of the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 10,000 species of flowering plants.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, opposite leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
