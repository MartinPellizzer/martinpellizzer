Rosa glauca f. seemeniana

Rosa glauca f. seemeniana, commonly known as the Seemen's blue rose, is a rare and ornamental variation of the Rosa glauca species.

It is characterized by its pale blue to lavender-colored flowers, which are slightly fragrant and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique coloration and its status as a horticultural curiosity.
