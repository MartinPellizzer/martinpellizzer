Rhododendron reticulatum subsp. ciliatum

Rhododendron reticulatum subsp.

ciliatum, commonly known as the crested rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. It is often found in montane forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive ciliated leaf margins and compact growth habit.
