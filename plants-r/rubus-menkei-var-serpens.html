Rubus menkei var. serpens

Rubus menkei var. serpens, commonly known as the serpentine blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in stinging hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cuisines.
