Rubus palmensis

Rubus palmensis, commonly known as the palmate blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are palmately arranged with five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many other brambles and raspberries.
