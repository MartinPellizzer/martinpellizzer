Rubus constrictus f. fissifolius

Rubus constrictus f. fissifolius, commonly known as the fissile-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a fissured appearance, giving it a unique visual identity. The plant is typically found in temperate regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, dark berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles and shrubs.
