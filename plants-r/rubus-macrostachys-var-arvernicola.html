Rubus macrostachys var. arvernicola

Rubus macrostachys var. arvernicola, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, arching stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats or colonies. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and are covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.
