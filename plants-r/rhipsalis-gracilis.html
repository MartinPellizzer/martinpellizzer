Rhipsalis gracilis

Rhipsalis gracilis, commonly known as the silver needle cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its leaves are reduced to small, hair-like structures, and it lacks true leaves.

Rhipsalis gracilis is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat.
