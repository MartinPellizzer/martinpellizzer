Ranunculus delphiniifolius

Ranunculus delphiniifolius, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and showy, yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this aquatic or semi-aquatic plant often grows in shallow water or moist soil. The plant has a short stem with leaves that are deeply divided, resembling the leaves of the water lily.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.
