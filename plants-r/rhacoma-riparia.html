Rhacoma riparia

Rhacoma riparia, commonly known as the riverbed spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and other wetland habitats. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hair.

Rhacoma riparia is known for its adaptability to fluctuating water levels and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
