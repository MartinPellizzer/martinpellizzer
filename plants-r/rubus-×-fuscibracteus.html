Rubus × fuscibracteus

Rubus × fuscibracteus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere, particularly in Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a sweet-tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
