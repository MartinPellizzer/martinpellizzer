Rubus × infrapilosus

Rubus × infrapilosus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry bushes, is a hybrid plant species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to various regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries.

The botanical features of Rubus × infrapilosus make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
