Rosa amplifolia

Rosa amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Rosa amplifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
