Rubus kingii

Rubus kingii, commonly known as King's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Georgia and Florida. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, which are aggregate berries, are typically red or black when mature and are edible.

Rubus kingii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
