Rosa aganodonta

Rosa aganodonta, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and coastal environments, showcasing a hardy nature adapted to harsh conditions. The plant features thorny stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Rosa aganodonta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its ecological role in its native habitat.
