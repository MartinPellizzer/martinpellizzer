Rubus × subjectus

Rubus × subjectus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These fruits are rich in antioxidants and are often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a common sight in temperate regions.
