Rubus tanakae var. yenosimanus

Rubus tanakae var. yenosimanus, commonly known as the Yenosi Mountain raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
