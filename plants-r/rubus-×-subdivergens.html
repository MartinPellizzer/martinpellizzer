Rubus × subdivergens

Rubus × subdivergens, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (the cloudberry).

It is a perennial shrub that produces edible berries, which are typically dark red to black in color. The plant has a rugged, thorny stem and thrives in temperate climates, often found in wetlands and forest edges.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
