Roystonea lenis

Roystonea lenis, commonly known as the slender palm, is a species of palm native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in branched inflorescences, followed by elongated, three-angled fruits. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing in coastal dunes and mangrove areas.

Roystonea lenis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal ecosystems.
