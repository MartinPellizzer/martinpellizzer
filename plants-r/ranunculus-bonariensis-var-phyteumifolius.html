Ranunculus bonariensis var. phyteumifolius

Ranunculus bonariensis var. phyteumifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in a fine layer of silky hair. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
