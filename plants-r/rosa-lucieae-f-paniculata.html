Rosa lucieae f. paniculata

Rosa lucieae f. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled rose, is a unique hybrid within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, branched inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its visual impact and fragrance.
