Rumex yezoensis

Rumex yezoensis, commonly known as Japanese dock, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
