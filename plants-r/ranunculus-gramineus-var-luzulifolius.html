Ranunculus gramineus var. luzulifolius

Ranunculus gramineus var. luzulifolius, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its botanical structure includes a short, rhizomatous root system and stems that are often hairy.

It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
