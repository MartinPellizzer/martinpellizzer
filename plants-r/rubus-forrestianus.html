Rubus forrestianus

Rubus forrestianus, commonly known as the Forster's blackberry, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
