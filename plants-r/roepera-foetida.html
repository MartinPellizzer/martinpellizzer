Roepera foetida

Roepera foetida, commonly known as the stinkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces a distinctive, often unpleasant odor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Roepera foetida is notable for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
