Rosa columbiana

Rosa columbiana, commonly known as the Columbian rose, is a species native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on long pedicels. Its botanical characteristics include showy, five-petaled blooms and an upright growth habit.

Rosa columbiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
