Rubus radula f. trifoliata

Rubus radula f. trifoliata, commonly known as the trifoliate blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. Its foliage is dense and often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The trifoliate form distinguishes it from other Rubus species by its unique leaf structure.
