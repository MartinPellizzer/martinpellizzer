Rubus apum

Rubus apum, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Rubus apum is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local wildlife.
