Rubus radula var. indusiatiformis

Rubus radula var. indusiatiformis, commonly known as the Indian raspberry or red elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a deep red color, which are edible when fully ripe. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.
