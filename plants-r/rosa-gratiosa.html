Rosa gratiosa

Rosa gratiosa, commonly known as the graceful rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in areas of the Appalachian region. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a fragrant scent.

Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves, and it produces edible hips that are rich in vitamin C. Rosa gratiosa is typically grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
