Rubus epipsilos var. adustus

Rubus epipsilos var. adustus, commonly known as the rusty blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that are typically smaller than those of other blackberry varieties. The plant has thorny stems and broad, lobed leaves with a hairy underside. Its flowers are small and white, typically appearing in clusters.

The fruit is often used in jams and pies, though it has a more tart flavor compared to other blackberries.
