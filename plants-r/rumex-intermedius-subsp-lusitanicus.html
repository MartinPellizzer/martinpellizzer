Rumex intermedius subsp. lusitanicus

Rumex intermedius subsp.

lusitanicus, commonly known as the Iberian sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration on the underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in loose spikes, blooming in spring.

The species is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in calcareous soils.
