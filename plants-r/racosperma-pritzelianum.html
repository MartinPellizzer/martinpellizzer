Racosperma pritzelianum

Racosperma pritzelianum, commonly known as the Pritzel's wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is often found in coastal or dry inland regions. Its leaves are bipinnate, and it produces yellow flowers in racemes during the warmer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
