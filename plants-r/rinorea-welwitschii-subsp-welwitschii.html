Rinorea welwitschii subsp. welwitschii

Rinorea welwitschii subsp.

welwitschii, commonly known as the velvetleaf rinorea, is a shrub native to central and eastern Africa. It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
