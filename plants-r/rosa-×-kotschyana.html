Rosa × kotschyana

Rosa × kotschyana, commonly known as the Kotschy's rose, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and the production of large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically features thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.

Rosa × kotschyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its fragrance and long-lasting blooms.
