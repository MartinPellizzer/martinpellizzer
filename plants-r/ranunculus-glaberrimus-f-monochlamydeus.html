Ranunculus glaberrimus f. monochlamydeus

Ranunculus glaberrimus f. monochlamydeus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral structure.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are usually yellow with five petals, though the specific form monochlamydeus may exhibit variations in petal morphology.

This variant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
