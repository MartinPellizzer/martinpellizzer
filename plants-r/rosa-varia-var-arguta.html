Rosa varia var. arguta

Rosa varia var. arguta, commonly known as the roughleaf rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins and a compact growth habit.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
