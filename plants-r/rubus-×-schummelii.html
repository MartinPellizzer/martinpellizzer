Rubus × schummelii

Rubus × schummelii, commonly known as the Schummelii blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that typically mature to a dark purple or black color. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible berries in the summer.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is often cultivated in gardens and small-scale farms.
