Rubus acuminatus

Rubus acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and ecosystems.
