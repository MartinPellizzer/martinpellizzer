Rhododendron trichostomum var. radinum

Rhododendron trichostomum var. radinum, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
