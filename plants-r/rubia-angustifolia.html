Rubia angustifolia

Rubia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf madder, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its roots contain a red dye used historically in textile production.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
