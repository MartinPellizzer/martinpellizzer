Rosa glauca subsp. consternata

Rosa glauca subsp.

consternata, commonly known as the bluebelle rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of the eastern United States. It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers and finely toothed, grey-green leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its stems are often covered in a thin layer of whitish bloom, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
