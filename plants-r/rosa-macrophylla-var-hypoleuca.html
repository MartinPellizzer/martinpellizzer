Rosa macrophylla var. hypoleuca

Rosa macrophylla var. hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered sweet briar, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces showy, single or semi-double flowers with a sweet, honey-like scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and wildlife habitats.
