Rosa vosagiaca var. falcata

Rosa vosagiaca var. falcata, commonly known as the Falcate Rosa, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, which provide structural support to its dense foliage. The leaves are compound, typically consisting of five to seven leaflets, and are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are fragrant, pink to red in color, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a range of soil types.
