Rhododendron subcerinum

Rhododendron subcerinum, commonly known as the silky rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that is often covered in a fine, silky down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
