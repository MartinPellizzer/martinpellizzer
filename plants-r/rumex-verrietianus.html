Rumex verrietianus

Rumex verrietianus, commonly known as the Southern Dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, often swampy habitats, such as riverbanks and wetlands. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves with a waxy upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are often overlooked due to their modest appearance.

Rumex verrietianus is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and can sometimes become invasive in certain environments.
