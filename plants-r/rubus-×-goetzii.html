Rubus × goetzii

Rubus × goetzii, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
