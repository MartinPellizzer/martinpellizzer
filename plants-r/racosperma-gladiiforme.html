Racosperma gladiiforme

Racosperma gladiiforme, commonly known as the swordleaf acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, linear pods containing small, hard seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Racosperma gladiiforme is a hardy species that is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
