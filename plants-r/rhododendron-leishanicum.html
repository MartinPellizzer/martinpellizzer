Rhododendron leishanicum

Rhododendron leishanicum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and red, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

Rhododendron leishanicum is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
