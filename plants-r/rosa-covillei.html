Rosa covillei

Rosa covillei, commonly known as the Coville rose, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring, which are followed by oval, red-orange hips. Rosa covillei is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and resilience make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
