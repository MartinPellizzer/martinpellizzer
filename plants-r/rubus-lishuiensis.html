Rubus lishuiensis

Rubus lishuiensis, commonly known as the Lishui bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China, specifically found in the Lishui River valley in Zhejiang Province. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, white flowers. Its fruits are edible berries that are typically red or black in color.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
