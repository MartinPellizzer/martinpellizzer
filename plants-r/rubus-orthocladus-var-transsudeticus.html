Rubus orthocladus var. transsudeticus

Rubus orthocladus var. transsudeticus, commonly known as the Transylvanian blackberry, is a shrub native to Central Europe.

It features prickly stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
