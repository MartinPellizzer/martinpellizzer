Rubus offensus var. mollifolius

Rubus offensus var. mollifolius, commonly known as the soft-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, soft leaves and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in jams and pies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various garden environments.
