Rhododendron nudipes var. mayebarae

Rhododendron nudipes var. mayebarae, commonly known as the Mayebarae rhododendron, is a rare and unique variant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its striking, deep pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dense, evergreen forests at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
