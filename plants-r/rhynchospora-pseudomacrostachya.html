Rhynchospora pseudomacrostachya

Rhynchospora pseudomacrostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Rhynchospora pseudomacrostachya plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
