Ranunculus auricomus subsp. dialeptus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

dialeptus, commonly known as the yellow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals and a distinctive leaf structure with deep lobes.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its showy, early spring blooms.

The subspecies dialeptus is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to other subspecies of Ranunculus auricomus.
