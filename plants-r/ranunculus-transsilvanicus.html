Ranunculus transsilvanicus

Ranunculus transsilvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian buttercup, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of finely divided, bright green leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
