Rosa systyla f. praetermissa

Rosa systyla f. praetermissa, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse blooming throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a subtle scent. Its foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, providing a textured contrast to the blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its continuous flowering.
