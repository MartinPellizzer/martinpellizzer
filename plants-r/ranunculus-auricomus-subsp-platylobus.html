Ranunculus auricomus subsp. platylobus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

platylobus, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features basal leaves that are palmately divided with lobes that are broader than those of the typical *R. auricomus*.

The flowers are yellow with five broadly ovate petals and are often accompanied by a yellow or greenish-green sepals. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive leaf shape and is often found in temperate regions.
