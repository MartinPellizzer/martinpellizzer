Rosa vicina

Rosa vicina, commonly known as the neighboring rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and western Asia, often found in woodland edges and shrublands. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are thorny.

Rosa vicina is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
