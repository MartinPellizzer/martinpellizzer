Rosa × andegavensis f. salicetorum

Rosa × andegavensis f. salicetorum, commonly known as the silverleaf rose, is a hybrid shrub characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its foliage.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to red, appearing in clusters, and it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

The silverleaf variety is particularly noted for its unique leaf coloration, which gives it a striking appearance.
