Rubus obscurus var. scitus

Rubus obscurus var. scitus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, dark purple berries that are typically less showy than other blackberry varieties. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
