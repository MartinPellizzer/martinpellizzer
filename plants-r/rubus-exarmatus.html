Rubus exarmatus

Rubus exarmatus, commonly known as the scarlet bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
