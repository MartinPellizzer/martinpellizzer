Rubus × validifrons

Rubus × validifrons, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn from red to black as they mature, often used in local foraging. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to coastal climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of ecological study.
