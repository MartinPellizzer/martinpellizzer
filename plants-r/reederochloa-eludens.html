Reederochloa eludens

Reederochloa eludens, commonly known as the elusive reedgrass, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small and inconspicuous.

Reederochloa eludens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
