Renanthera vietnamensis

Renanthera vietnamensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese flame orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers with a contrasting yellow throat. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple showy flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the rainy season.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
