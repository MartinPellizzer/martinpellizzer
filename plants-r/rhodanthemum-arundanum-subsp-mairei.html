Rhodanthemum arundanum subsp. mairei

Rhodanthemum arundanum subsp.

mairei, commonly known as the Maire's rhodanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

Its foliage is usually grey-green, adding texture to the landscape.
