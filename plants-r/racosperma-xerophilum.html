Racosperma xerophilum

Racosperma xerophilum, commonly known as the dryland acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to low rainfall conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its hardy nature makes it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects in dry environments.
