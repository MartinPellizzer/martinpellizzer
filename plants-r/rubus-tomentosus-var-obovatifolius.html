Rubus tomentosus var. obovatifolius

Rubus tomentosus var. obovatifolius, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescence covering the stems and leaves. The leaves are obovate in shape, with a broad base and a tapering tip, and are often serrated along the margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.
