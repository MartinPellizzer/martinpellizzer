Rubus foliaceistipulatus

Rubus foliaceistipulatus, commonly known as the leafy stipule raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries in the summer. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it has distinctive stipules that are leaf-like in appearance.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
