Robertium vulgare var. purpureum

Robertium vulgare var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically yellow with purple markings. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
