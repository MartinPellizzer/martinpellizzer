Rosa × pseudolevistyla

Rosa × pseudolevistyla, commonly known as the everblooming rosa.

This hybrid rose is a cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa leucocarpa, resulting in a plant that exhibits characteristics of both parents. It is known for its ability to bloom continuously throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking long-lasting floral display. The plant typically produces fragrant, pink to white flowers and has a rugged, shrub-like growth habit.

It is adaptable to various soil types and is relatively disease-resistant, which contributes to its popularity in landscaping.
