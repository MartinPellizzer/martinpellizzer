Ranunculus mammiformidens

Ranunculus mammiformidens, commonly known as the mammillary buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often lobed leaves, which are typically basal and arranged in a rosette. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by numerous stamens. It is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
