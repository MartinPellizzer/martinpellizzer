Ranunculus friesianus subsp. granatensis

Ranunculus friesianus subsp.

granatensis, commonly known as the Granatense Crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually brightly colored flowers.

The plant typically features trifoliate leaves with three deeply lobed leaflets and produces small, yellowish to white flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
