Rubus rivularis var. obtusidentatus

Rubus rivularis var. obtusidentatus, commonly known as the blunt-toothed blackberry, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and prickly stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins with blunt teeth, giving it its specific epithet.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
