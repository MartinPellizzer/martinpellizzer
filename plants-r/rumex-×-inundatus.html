Rumex × inundatus

Rumex × inundatus, commonly known as the hybrid sorrel, is a perennial plant that exhibits characteristics of its parent species, Rumex acetosa (common sorrel) and Rumex obtusifolius (broad-leaved dock).

It typically grows in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a reddish or purple coloration on the veins, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive leaf shapes and edible leaves, though caution is advised due to potential toxicity.

The hybrid nature of Rumex × inundatus results in variability in its morphology and habitat preferences.
