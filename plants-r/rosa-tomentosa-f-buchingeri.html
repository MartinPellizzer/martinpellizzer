Rosa tomentosa f. buchingeri

Rosa tomentosa f. buchingeri, commonly known as the Buchinger's fuzzy rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to Central Asia.

It features dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring. Its stems are covered in a thick layer of white tomentum, contributing to its common name.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique texture and delicate blooms.
