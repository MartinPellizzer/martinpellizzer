Rubus villicaulis var. marchicus

Rubus villicaulis var. marchicus, commonly known as the March chubberry, is a shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, aggregate berries that are typically red or black when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while the stems are often armed with short, sharp spines.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
