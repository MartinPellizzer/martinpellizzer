Ranunculus recurvatus var. typicus

Ranunculus recurvatus var. typicus, commonly known as the curly dock, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its deeply lobed, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers with five petals. Native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, it thrives in moist, disturbed soils such as those found in fields, roadsides, and wetlands. The plant is often considered a weed but has historical uses in herbal medicine.

Its botanical features include a hairy stem and a basal rosette of leaves.
