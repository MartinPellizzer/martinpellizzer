Rosa cordatifolia

Rosa cordatifolia, commonly known as the heart-shaped rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. Its foliage is typically dark green and has a slightly serrated edge.

Rosa cordatifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
