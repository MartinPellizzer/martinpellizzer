Rechsteineria ignea var. villosa

Rechsteineria ignea var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy flame violet, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features velvety, hairy leaves that give it its varietal name, "villosa," meaning hairy in Latin. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
