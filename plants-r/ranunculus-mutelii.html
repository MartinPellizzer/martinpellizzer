Ranunculus mutelii

Ranunculus mutelii, commonly known as the Mutel's buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of numerous petal-like sepals that are usually yellow or orange in color.

Ranunculus mutelii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
