Rosa acicularis f. lilacina

Rosa acicularis f. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-flowered prickly rose, is a horticultural form of the prickly rose species.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale pink to lilac. The foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with finely serrated edges. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
