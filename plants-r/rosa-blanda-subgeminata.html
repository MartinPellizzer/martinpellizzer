Rosa blanda subgeminata

Rosa blanda subgeminata, commonly known as the twinleaf rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a distinctive white or pale green underside. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it produces rose hips that are edible.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to moist, woodland environments.
