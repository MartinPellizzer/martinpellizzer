Rosa canina subvar. medioxima

Rosa canina subvar. medioxima, commonly known as the common dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit and contain a high concentration of vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
