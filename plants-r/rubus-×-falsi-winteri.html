Rubus × falsi-winteri

Rubus × falsi-winteri, commonly known as the false winter raspberry.

This hybrid plant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. It typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in woodland or hedgerow habitats. The plant produces small, red or black berries that are edible but have a tart flavor.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
