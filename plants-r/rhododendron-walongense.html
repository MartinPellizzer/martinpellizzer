Rhododendron walongense

Rhododendron walongense, commonly known as the Walong rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly found in India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
