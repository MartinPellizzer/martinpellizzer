Rumex odontocarpus

Rumex odontocarpus, commonly known as the serrated dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet soils and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
