Rosa alexandrae

Rosa alexandrae, commonly known as the Alexandra rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Tien Shan and Pamir Mountains. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Rosa alexandrae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
