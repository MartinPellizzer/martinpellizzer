Rhododendron chaetomallum var. glaucescens

Rhododendron chaetomallum var. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous-leaved rhododendron, is a variant of the chaetomallum species.

It is characterized by its distinctive bluish-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions of Asia, particularly in the Himalayas. Its flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in the spring.

It is a shrub with dense, evergreen leaves and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
