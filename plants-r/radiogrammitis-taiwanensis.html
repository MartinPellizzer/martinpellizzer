Radiogrammitis taiwanensis

Radiogrammitis taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan radiogrammitis, is a species of moss belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, acrocarpous structure. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocks or soil in mountainous regions.

This plant is notable for its resilience to desiccation, a common trait among mosses in arid habitats.
