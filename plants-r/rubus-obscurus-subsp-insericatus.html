Rubus obscurus subsp. insericatus

Rubus obscurus subsp.

insericatus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible. Its flowers are pinkish-white with five petals and numerous stamens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage and its preference for moist, shaded habitats.
