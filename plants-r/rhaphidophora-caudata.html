Rhaphidophora caudata

Rhaphidophora caudata, commonly known as the "Caudata Spider Plant," is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, dark green leaves that are often adorned with striking silver or white veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Rhaphidophora caudata is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
