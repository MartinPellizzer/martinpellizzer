Rubus fruticosus

Rubus fruticosus, commonly known as blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. These fruits are edible and have a sweet-tart flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Rubus fruticosus is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in a variety of soil types and climates.
