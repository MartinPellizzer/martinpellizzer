Ranunculus × cobelliorum

Ranunculus × cobelliorum, commonly known as the Cobell's buttercup, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are usually hairy and upright. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be difficult to grow outside its native habitat.
