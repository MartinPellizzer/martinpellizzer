Rosa rubiginosa anemoneflora

Rosa rubiginosa anemoneflora, commonly known as the red-stemmed anemone, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive pink to red petals and yellow stamens.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually lobed and covered in a fine, hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
