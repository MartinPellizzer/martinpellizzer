Roseocactus kotschoubeyanus

Roseocactus kotschoubeyanus, commonly known as the Kotschoubeyan roseocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pink spines. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
