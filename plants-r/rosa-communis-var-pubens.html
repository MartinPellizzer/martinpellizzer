Rosa communis var. pubens

Rosa communis var. pubens, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a botanical variant of the common rose.

It is characterized by its dense, pubescent foliage and thorny stems, which provide structural support and protection. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
