Rubus chlorothyrsos subsp. leyi

Rubus chlorothyrsos subsp.

leyi, commonly known as the yellow-tailed blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
