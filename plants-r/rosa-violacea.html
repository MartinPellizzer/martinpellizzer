Rosa violacea

Rosa violacea, commonly known as the violet rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep violet. The leaves are typically pinnate, with five to seven leaflets that are oval and serrated. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and pleasant scent.
