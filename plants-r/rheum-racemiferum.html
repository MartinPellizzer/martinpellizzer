Rheum racemiferum

Rheum racemiferum, commonly known as the giant rhubarb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, rhombic, and often deeply lobed leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces tall, erect flowering stalks topped with panicles of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its botanical features include thick, fleshy roots that store nutrients and help it survive in harsh environments.

Rheum racemiferum is closely related to other members of the Polygonaceae family, sharing similar structural and physiological traits.
