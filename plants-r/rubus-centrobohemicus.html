Rubus centrobohemicus

Rubus centrobohemicus, commonly known as the Bohemian raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and open habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are pinkish-white with five petals, and the fruits are edible, though often tart.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
