Rabdosia pachythyrsa

Rabdosia pachythyrsa, commonly known as the thick-leafed rabdosia, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rabdosia pachythyrsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
