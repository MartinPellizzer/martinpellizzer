Romulea linaresii var. corsica

Romulea linaresii var. corsica, commonly known as Corsican glory-of-the-snow, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in mountainous areas. It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes many ornamental plants.
