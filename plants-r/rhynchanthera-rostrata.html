Rhynchanthera rostrata

Rhynchanthera rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive rostellum, a specialized structure that aids in pollination. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Rhynchanthera rostrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
