Rudbeckia porteri

Rudbeckia porteri, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange ray flowers and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Rudbeckia porteri is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, daisy-like blooms.
