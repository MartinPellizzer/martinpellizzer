Rockhausenia rockhauseniana

Rockhausenia rockhauseniana, commonly known as the rockhausenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
