Ranunculus bekesensis

Ranunculus bekesensis, commonly known as the Beke's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that resemble petals, and it typically blooms from late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Ranunculus bekesensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, cup-shaped blooms.
