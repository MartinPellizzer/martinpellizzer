Rhododendron pentaphyllum

Rhododendron pentaphyllum, commonly known as the five-leaved rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions at high altitudes, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive five-lobed shape, contributing to its ornamental value.

Rhododendron pentaphyllum is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a horticultural specimen.
