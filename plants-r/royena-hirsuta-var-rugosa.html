Royena hirsuta var. rugosa

Royena hirsuta var. rugosa, commonly known as the hairy rock fig, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
