Rosa schmalhauseniana

Rosa schmalhauseniana, commonly known as the Schmalhausen rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a woody, aromatic fragrance.

Rosa schmalhauseniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
