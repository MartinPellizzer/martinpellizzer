Rubus figertii var. rectispinus

Rubus figertii var. rectispinus, commonly known as the straight-spined blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is occasionally grown in gardens and conservation areas.
