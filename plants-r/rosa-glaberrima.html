Rosa glaberrima

Rosa glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves. The plant produces fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Rosa glaberrima is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
