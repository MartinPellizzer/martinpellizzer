Rajania cordata

Rajania cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Rajania cordata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
