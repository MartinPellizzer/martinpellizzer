Raoulia australis var. apice-nigra

Raoulia australis var. apice-nigra, commonly known as the black-tipped raoulia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, often with a slight reddish tinge.

The variety apice-nigra is distinguished by its dark purple or black-tipped flower buds, which add visual interest to the plant.
