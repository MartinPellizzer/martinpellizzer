Ranunculus javorkae

Ranunculus javorkae, commonly known as the Java buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
