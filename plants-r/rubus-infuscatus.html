Rubus infuscatus

Rubus infuscatus, commonly known as the dark raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically clustered together. It thrives in woodland and shrubland habitats, often in moist, well-drained soils.

Rubus infuscatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
