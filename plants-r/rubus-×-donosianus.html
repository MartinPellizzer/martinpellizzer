Rubus × donosianus

Rubus × donosianus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are usually small and tart. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
