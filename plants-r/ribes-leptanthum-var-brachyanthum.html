Ribes leptanthum var. brachyanthum

Ribes leptanthum var. brachyanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved currant, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in the spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
