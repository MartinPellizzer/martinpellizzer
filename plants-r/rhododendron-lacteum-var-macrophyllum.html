Rhododendron lacteum var. macrophyllum

Rhododendron lacteum var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved white rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has leathery, evergreen leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape. It thrives in cool, moist environments with acidic soil and partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
