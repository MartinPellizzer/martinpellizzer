Rhamnus lycioides var. velutina

Rhamnus lycioides var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The species is notable for its ecological role in providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
