Rubus adenolobus

Rubus adenolobus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its leaves are narrow and have a serrated margin, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Rubus adenolobus is often found in shrublands and forest edges, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
