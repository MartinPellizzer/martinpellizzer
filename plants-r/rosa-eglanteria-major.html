Rosa eglanteria major

Rosa eglanteria major, commonly known as the Prickly Rose, is a species of shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, hairy rose hip that contains seeds.

Rosa eglanteria major is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
