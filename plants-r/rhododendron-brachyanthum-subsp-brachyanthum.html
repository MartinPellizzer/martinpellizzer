Rhododendron brachyanthum subsp. brachyanthum

Rhododendron brachyanthum subsp.

brachyanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
