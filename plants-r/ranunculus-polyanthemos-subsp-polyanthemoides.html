Ranunculus polyanthemos subsp. polyanthemoides

Ranunculus polyanthemos subsp.

polyanthemoides, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and prefers well-drained, alkaline soils. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats at higher elevations.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
