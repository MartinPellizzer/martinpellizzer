Rubus trivialis

Rubus trivialis, commonly known as blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria, typically turning dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus trivialis is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and disturbed areas, where it plays a role in ecological succession.
