Ruellia pedunculata subsp. pinetorum

Ruellia pedunculata subsp.

pinetorum, commonly known as the Florida rainflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds and marshes.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue or violet flowers. Its root system helps stabilize soil, making it ecologically valuable in wetland restoration.

It is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability.
