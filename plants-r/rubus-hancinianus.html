Rubus hancinianus

Rubus hancinianus, commonly known as the Hancinian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that mature to a deep, dark purple color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
