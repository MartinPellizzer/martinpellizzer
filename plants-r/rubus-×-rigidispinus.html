Rubus × rigidispinus

Rubus × rigidispinus, commonly known as the bramble raspberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, often red or orange berries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
