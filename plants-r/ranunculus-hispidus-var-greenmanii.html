Ranunculus hispidus var. greenmanii

Ranunculus hispidus var. greenmanii, commonly known as the Greenman's hairy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers are usually short-lived, opening in spring and fading by early summer.

The species is characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage and is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants.
