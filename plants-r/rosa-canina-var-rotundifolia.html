Rosa canina var. rotundifolia

Rosa canina var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaved dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, rounded leaves and fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, hairy hips that are rich in vitamin C and often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for hedgerows and gardens.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
