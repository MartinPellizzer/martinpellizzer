Ruellia pedunculosa

Ruellia pedunculosa, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Ruellia pedunculosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
