Reseda abyssinica

Reseda abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian madder, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces red-colored roots, which have been traditionally used for dyeing textiles. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Reseda abyssinica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in some parts of Africa.
