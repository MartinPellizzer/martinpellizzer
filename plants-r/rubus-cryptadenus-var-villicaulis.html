Rubus cryptadenus var. villicaulis

Rubus cryptadenus var. villicaulis, commonly known as the silky blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important part of local ecosystems and a subject of botanical study.
