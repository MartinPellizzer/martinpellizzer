Rubus holubyanus

Rubus holubyanus, commonly known as the Holubyan raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
