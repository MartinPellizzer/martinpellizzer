Rubus callosus var. lingua

Rubus callosus var. lingua, commonly known as the Tongue Raspberry, is a variant of the bramble plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fruit that resembles a tongue in shape, hence its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

This species is often found in rocky or woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
