Rubus × hemirhodopsis

Rubus × hemirhodopsis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus pectoralis.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
