Rhoeo spathacea f. variegata

Rhoeo spathacea f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated velvetleaf, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves with striking variegation of green, white, and pink hues. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, adding to its visual appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its vibrant leaf coloration.

Due to its attractive foliage, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
