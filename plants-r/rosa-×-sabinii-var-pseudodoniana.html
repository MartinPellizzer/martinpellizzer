Rosa × sabinii var. pseudodoniana

Rosa × sabinii var. pseudodoniana, commonly known as the Siberian roses, is a hybrid rose species originating from the cross between Rosa sanguinea and Rosa rugosa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is native to the eastern regions of Russia and parts of northern China. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are serrated and have a dark green color with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
