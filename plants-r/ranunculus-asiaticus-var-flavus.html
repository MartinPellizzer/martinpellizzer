Ranunculus asiaticus var. flavus

Ranunculus asiaticus var. flavus, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to seven petals, typically in shades of yellow or orange. The plant has a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
