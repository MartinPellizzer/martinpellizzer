Rhynchosia nyikensis

Rhynchosia nyikensis, commonly known as the Nyikeni burr legume, is a climbing leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive burr-like fruits. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers, which are followed by hairy, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Rhynchosia nyikensis is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed habitats.

It plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its association with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
