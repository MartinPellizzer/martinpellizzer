Rubus trichostachys

Rubus trichostachys, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as berries, which are usually dark red to black in color. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it thrives in temperate to subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
