Rosa albispinosa

Rosa albispinosa, commonly known as the white-spined rose, is a species native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and thorny stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rosa albispinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
