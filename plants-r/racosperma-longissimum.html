Racosperma longissimum

Racosperma longissimum, commonly known as the long-stemmed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

Racosperma longissimum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
