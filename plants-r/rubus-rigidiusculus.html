Rubus rigidiusculus

Rubus rigidiusculus, commonly known as the stiff-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, which includes many edible and ornamental species.
