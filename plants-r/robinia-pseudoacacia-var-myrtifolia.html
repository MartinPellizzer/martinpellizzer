Robinia pseudoacacia var. myrtifolia

Robinia pseudoacacia var. myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved false acacia, is a variant of the honey locust tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed, myrtle-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in a variety of soil types. It produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers in the spring, followed by pods that split open to release seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to different environmental conditions.
