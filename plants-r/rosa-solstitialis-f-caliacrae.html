Rosa solstitialis f. caliacrae

Rosa solstitialis f. caliacrae, commonly known as the "Caliacra rose," is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the species Rosa solstitialis.

It is characterized by its striking, deep red flowers that bloom in late summer, typically around the summer solstice, hence its species name. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens with adequate space.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and its historical significance in horticultural history.
