Rubus flagellaris

Rubus flagellaris, commonly known as the creeping blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its fruit is used in local cuisine and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
