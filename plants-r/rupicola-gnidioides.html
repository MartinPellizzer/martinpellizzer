Rupicola gnidioides

Rupicola gnidioides, commonly known as the rock fig, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, edible fruits that are sometimes used in local cuisines.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
