Ranunculus pulchellus var. geniculatus

Ranunculus pulchellus var. geniculatus, commonly known as the bent-stem buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and vibrant yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often bent or twisted, giving it its common name.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
