Rosa beggeriana var. intermedia

Rosa beggeriana var. intermedia, commonly known as the Himalayan rose, is a perennial shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a slight red tinge. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
