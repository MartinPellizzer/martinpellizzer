Racosperma dunnii

Racosperma dunnii, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry conditions, making it well-adapted to harsh environments. It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen into the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.

Racosperma dunnii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
