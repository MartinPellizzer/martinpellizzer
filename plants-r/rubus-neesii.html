Rubus neesii

Rubus neesii, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually serrated along the edges.

The berries are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a food source.
