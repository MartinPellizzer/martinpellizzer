Ranunculus jazgulemicus

Ranunculus jazgulemicus, commonly known as the Jazgulem buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow or orange flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
