Rosa coriifolia subsp. debilitata

Rosa coriifolia subsp.

debilitata, commonly known as the weak-stemmed prickly rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are pinnately compound, with oval to lanceolate leaflets that are usually covered in fine hairs. This subspecies is notable for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
