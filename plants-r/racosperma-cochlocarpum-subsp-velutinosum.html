Racosperma cochlocarpum subsp. velutinosum

Racosperma cochlocarpum subsp.

velutinosum, commonly known as the velvet wattle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of northern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas with a warm climate.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
