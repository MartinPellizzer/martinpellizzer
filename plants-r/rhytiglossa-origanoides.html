Rhytiglossa origanoides

Rhytiglossa origanoides, commonly known as the Mexican oregano, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. Its leaves are aromatic, often used in cooking for their pungent, mint-like flavor.

Rhytiglossa origanoides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for Mediterranean climates.
