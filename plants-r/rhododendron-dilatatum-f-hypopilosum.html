Rhododendron dilatatum f. hypopilosum

Rhododendron dilatatum f. hypopilosum, commonly known as the silky rhododendron, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features dense, leathery foliage with a distinctive silvery-white to pale green under surface, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
