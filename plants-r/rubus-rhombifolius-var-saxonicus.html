Rubus rhombifolius var. saxonicus

Rubus rhombifolius var. saxonicus, commonly known as the saxonic blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with rhomboid-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often found in rocky or mountainous regions due to its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
