Rosa mollis f. depressula

Rosa mollis f. depressula, commonly known as the low-growing form of the soft rose, is a shrub-like plant characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

It features small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to white, often appearing in clusters. The leaves are oval-shaped with a soft, fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is well-suited for ground cover or as a border plant due to its spreading nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a range of garden environments.
