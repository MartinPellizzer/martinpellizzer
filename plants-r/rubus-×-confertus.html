Rubus × confertus

Rubus × confertus, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry plant, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to various regions in Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant white flowers in the spring.

Rubus × confertus is valued both for its edible fruits and its role in ecological systems as a habitat for various wildlife species.
