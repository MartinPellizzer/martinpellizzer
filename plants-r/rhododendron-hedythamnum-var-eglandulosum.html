Rhododendron hedythamnum var. eglandulosum

Rhododendron hedythamnum var. eglandulosum, commonly known as the glandless rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features broad, elliptical leaves that are dark green and slightly pubescent on the underside. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It prefers well-drained, acidic soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
