Rubus dryades

Rubus dryades, commonly known as the dryades raspberry.

It is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for both its berries and its aesthetic appeal.
