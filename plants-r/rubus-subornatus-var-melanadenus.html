Rubus subornatus var. melanadenus

Rubus subornatus var. melanadenus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible blackberries in the summer. Its berries are typically dark purple to black when ripe and are often used in culinary applications.

The species is part of the Rubiaceae family and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
