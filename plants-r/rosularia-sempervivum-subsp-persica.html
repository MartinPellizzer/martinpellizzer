Rosularia sempervivum subsp. persica

Rosularia sempervivum subsp.

persica, commonly known as Persian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Central Asia. It features dense clusters of fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a rosette structure, typical of many succulent species.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
