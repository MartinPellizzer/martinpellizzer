Rhaphiolepis dubia

Rhaphiolepis dubia, commonly known as the Chinese firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens. It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

However, its berries are toxic to humans and animals, requiring caution in cultivation.
