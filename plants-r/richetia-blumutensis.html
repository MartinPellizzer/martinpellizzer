Richetia blumutensis

Richetia blumutensis, commonly known as the golden flame fern, is a unique tropical plant native to the humid rainforests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow fronds that resemble flames, making it a visually captivating species. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish. Its reproductive system involves the production of spores rather than seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Richetia blumutensis is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
