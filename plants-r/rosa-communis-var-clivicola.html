Rosa communis var. clivicola

Rosa communis var. clivicola, commonly known as the wall rose, is a compact shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and is often found growing on walls or in rocky crevices. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

It is well-suited for small garden spaces and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
