Rhynchosia glandulosa

Rhynchosia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Rhynchosia glandulosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
