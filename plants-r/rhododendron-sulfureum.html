Rhododendron sulfureum

Rhododendron sulfureum, commonly known as the golden rhododendron, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has leathery, dark green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Rhododendron sulfureum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
