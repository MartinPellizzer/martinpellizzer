Ruellia ciliosa var. parviflora

Ruellia ciliosa var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
