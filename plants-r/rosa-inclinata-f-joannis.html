Rosa inclinata f. joannis

Rosa inclinata f. joannis, commonly known as the Joannis climbing rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa inclinata, characterized by its vigorous climbing growth habit.

It features large, semi-double flowers with a deep pink hue that fades to white at the base, blooming in late spring to early summer. The foliage is dark green and glossy, providing a lush backdrop to the showy blooms. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and is often used in trellis or arbor displays.

It requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive, making it a popular choice for landscape design.
