Roridula arabica

Roridula arabica, commonly known as the African dew plant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its glandular leaves that secrete a sticky, resinous substance, which is believed to collect dew and moisture from the air. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its fleshy, bluish-green leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to low-water conditions through its specialized leaf structures.

Due to its rare and unusual features, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
