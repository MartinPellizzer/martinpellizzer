Rosa rubella subsp. mediterranea

Rosa rubella subsp.

mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and oval, dark green leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its flowers are typically 2 to 3 centimeters in diameter and have a sweet, fragrant scent.

The subspecies is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
