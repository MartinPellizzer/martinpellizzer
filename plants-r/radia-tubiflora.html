Radia tubiflora

Radia tubiflora, commonly known as the tuberous flax.

This plant is characterized by its tuberous roots that store nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. It produces small, delicate flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense mats on the ground.

Radia tubiflora is native to regions with dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
