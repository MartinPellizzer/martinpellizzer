Rosa hibernica

Rosa hibernica, commonly known as the Irish rose, is a species of shrub native to western Europe, particularly Ireland and Britain.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces red-orange hips in the autumn. Rosa hibernica is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional herbal medicine.
