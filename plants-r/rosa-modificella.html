Rosa modificella

Rosa modificella, commonly known as the modified rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically has dark green, serrated leaves and produces hips that are reddish-orange in color. Native to certain regions of Asia, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.

Its botanical features make it a unique and interesting subject for both gardeners and botanists.
