Rhynchosia caliensis

Rhynchosia caliensis, commonly known as the African burr bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers followed by hairy, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Rhynchosia caliensis is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields.

Its seeds are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.
