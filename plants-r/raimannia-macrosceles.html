Raimannia macrosceles

Raimannia macrosceles, commonly known as the giant thistle, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant can grow up to 3 meters tall, making it one of the tallest species in the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are covered in dense, barbed hairs that deter herbivores, while its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Raimannia macrosceles is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.
