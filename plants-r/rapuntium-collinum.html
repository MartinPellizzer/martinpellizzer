Rapuntium collinum

Rapuntium collinum, commonly known as the mountain cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, white or yellow flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces small, round seeds.

Rapuntium collinum is often found in alpine environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
