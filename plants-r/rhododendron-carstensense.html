Rhododendron carstensense

Rhododendron carstensense, commonly known as the Carstens' rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, often with a waxy coating. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron carstensense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
