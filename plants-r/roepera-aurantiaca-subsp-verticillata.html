Roepera aurantiaca subsp. verticillata

Roepera aurantiaca subsp.

verticillata, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
