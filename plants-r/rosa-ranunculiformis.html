Rosa ranunculiformis

Rosa ranunculiformis, commonly known as the "buttercup rose," is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that resemble those of the buttercup, and its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, red-orange hips that contain seeds, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. Rosa ranunculiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to the rose family.
