Ranunculus canadensis

Ranunculus canadensis, commonly known as Canadian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous sepals, which are often mistaken for true petals. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, waxy appearance.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
