Rubus villicaulis var. trifoliatus

Rubus villicaulis var. trifoliatus, commonly known as the three-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of North America. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its foliage is dense and often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
