Ranunculus binatus

Ranunculus binatus, commonly known as the two-flowered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, radially symmetrical flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 20–40 cm, with simple, alternate, and usually trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are yellow with 5–7 sepals and 5–7 petals, often with a distinctive yellow center.

Ranunculus binatus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
