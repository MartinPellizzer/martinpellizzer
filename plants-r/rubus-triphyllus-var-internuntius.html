Rubus triphyllus var. internuntius

Rubus triphyllus var. internuntius, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three leaflets. The plant produces small, red fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is often cultivated in gardens and commercial settings.
