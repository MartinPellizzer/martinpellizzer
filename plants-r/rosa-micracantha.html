Rosa micracantha

Rosa micracantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and is characterized by its slender, thorned stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

Rosa micracantha is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
