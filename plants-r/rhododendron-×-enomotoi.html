Rhododendron × enomotoi

Rhododendron × enomotoi, commonly known as the Enomoto rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron kaempferi and Rhododendron mucronulatum.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates, preferring well-drained acidic soils. It is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
