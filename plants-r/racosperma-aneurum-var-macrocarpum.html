Racosperma aneurum var. macrocarpum

Racosperma aneurum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-seeded acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several large, flat seeds. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils in arid and semi-arid regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
