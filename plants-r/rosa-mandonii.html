Rosa mandonii

Rosa mandonii, commonly known as the Mandon's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the high altitudes of the Mediterranean region, particularly in southern France and northern Italy. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and dense foliage. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and a strong fragrance.

Rosa mandonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
