Rapanea spathulata

Rapanea spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
