Rubus facetus

Rubus facetus, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a glossy upper surface.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
