Rubus insericatus var. orthoplodes

Rubus insericatus var. orthoplodes, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray underside on its leaves.

This plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage is covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible but less commonly cultivated than other blackberry varieties.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
