Rosa sphaeroidea var. posoniensis

Rosa sphaeroidea var. posoniensis, commonly known as the Posonensis rose, is a rare and unique variant of the spherical rose.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces thornless stems and has a dense foliage that gives it a bushy appearance. Native to specific regions in Central America, it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and ornamental value, it is a sought-after plant among horticulturists and botanical enthusiasts.
