Rubus moluccanus var. pycnanthus

Rubus moluccanus var. pycnanthus, commonly known as the Moluccan blackberry, is a shrub native to the Moluccas in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically clustered in tight inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
