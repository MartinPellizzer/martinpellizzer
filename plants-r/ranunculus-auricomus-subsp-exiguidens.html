Ranunculus auricomus subsp. exiguidens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

exiguidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals, often adorned with a distinctive white or pale green central area.

The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are typically narrow and lobed, adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments. It prefers cool, humid climates and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Ranunculaceae family.
