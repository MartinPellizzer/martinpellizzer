Ruellia pubiflora

Ruellia pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. Ruellia pubiflora is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
