Rubus schipperi

Rubus schipperi, commonly known as the Schipper's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, a type of aggregate berry, is typically red or black when ripe.

Rubus schipperi is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.
