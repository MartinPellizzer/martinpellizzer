Rinorea hamelii

Rinorea hamelii, commonly known as the pink bellflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Rinorea hamelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
