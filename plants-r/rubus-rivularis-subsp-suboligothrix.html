Rubus rivularis subsp. suboligothrix

Rubus rivularis subsp.

suboligothrix, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant has slender, arching stems with thorns, and its leaves are trifoliate, with narrow, serrated lobes. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a aggregate berry that ripens to a dark purple or black color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
