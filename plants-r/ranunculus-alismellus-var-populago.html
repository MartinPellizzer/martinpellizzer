Ranunculus alismellus var. populago

Ranunculus alismellus var. populago, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, streams, and ditches. The plant features finely divided, feather-like leaves that float on the water surface or emerge from the water. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, producing a delicate, showy display.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small fish.
