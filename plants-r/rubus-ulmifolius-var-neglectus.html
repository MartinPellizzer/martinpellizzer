Rubus ulmifolius var. neglectus

Rubus ulmifolius var. neglectus, commonly known as the creeping blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry that is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant is native to Europe and western Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It has a woody stem structure with thorny projections typical of the Rubus genus.

This variety is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
