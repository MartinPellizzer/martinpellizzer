Rhamnus cathartica var. microphylla

Rhamnus cathartica var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, dark blue berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
