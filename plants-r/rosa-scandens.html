Rosa scandens

Rosa scandens, commonly known as the climbing rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Rosa scandens thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing along fences, trellises, or in woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
