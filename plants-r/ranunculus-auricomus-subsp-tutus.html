Ranunculus auricomus subsp. tutus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

tutus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions.

The plant features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with numerous petals and a hairy stem. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations.
