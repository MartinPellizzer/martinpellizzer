Rubus pectinarioides

Rubus pectinarioides, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Rubus pectinarioides plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including pollinators and birds.
