Rubia laevissima

Rubia laevissima, commonly known as silky knotweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 13,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pale yellow and appear in clusters, and it produces small, dark purple berries.

Rubia laevissima is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
