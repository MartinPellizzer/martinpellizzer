Rubus steelei

Rubus steelei, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial purposes.
