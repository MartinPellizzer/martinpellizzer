Ruscus dumosus

Ruscus dumosus, commonly known as creeping jew's-mary, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features dense clusters of waxy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its succulent leaves and unique reproductive structures.
