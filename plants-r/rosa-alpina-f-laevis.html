Rosa alpina f. laevis

Rosa alpina f. laevis, commonly known as the alpine pink, is a botanical variant of the alpine rose.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, distinguishing it from other forms of Rosa alpina. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine displays.
