Rosa tomentosa f. eglandulosa

Rosa tomentosa f. eglandulosa, commonly known as the wooly rose or fuzzy rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa tomentosa species.

It is characterized by its dense, white to grayish pubescence covering the stems, leaves, and flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with multiple stems and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers. Its foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique appearance and is adaptable to a range of growing conditions.
