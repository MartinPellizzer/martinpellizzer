Ranunculus argenteus

Ranunculus argenteus, commonly known as the silverleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with white to pale yellow petals and a distinctive yellow center. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ranunculus argenteus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland habitats.
