Reseda nainii subsp. integrifolia

Reseda nainii subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as the Kashmir rose or Kashmir reseda, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually entire or slightly notched.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in dyeing and medicinal practices.
