Rubus furvus var. spiniger

Rubus furvus var. spiniger, commonly known as the bramble rose, is a thorny shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The species is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along the edges of fields.
