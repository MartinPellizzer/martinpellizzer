Rubus argyropsis

Rubus argyropsis, commonly known as silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and desert scrub ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
