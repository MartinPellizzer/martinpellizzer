Ronnbergia robusta

Ronnbergia robusta, commonly known as the giant bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its large, rosette-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its sturdy, fibrous roots allow it to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

The plant's ability to store water in its leaf axils makes it well adapted to humid, tropical environments.
