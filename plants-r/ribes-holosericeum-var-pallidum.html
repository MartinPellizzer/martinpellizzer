Ribes holosericeum var. pallidum

Ribes holosericeum var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale-flowered gooseberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with a fine, whitish down. The plant produces small, yellow to orange berries that are edible but often tart.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
