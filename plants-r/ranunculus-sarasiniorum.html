Ranunculus sarasiniorum

Ranunculus sarasiniorum, commonly known as the Sarasin's buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features palmately divided leaves and produces flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals. It thrives in alpine environments and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
