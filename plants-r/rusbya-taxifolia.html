Rusbya taxifolia

Rusbya taxifolia, commonly known as the slender-leafed rusbya, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale pink, which bloom in the spring. Rusbya taxifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blossoms.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile choice for woodland or rock garden settings.
