Rubus ulmifolius var. constrictifolius

Rubus ulmifolius var. constrictifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a distinctive narrow, elliptical shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
