Rosa mollis f. molligothoburgensis

Rosa mollis f. molligothoburgensis, commonly known as the Mollis rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa mollis species.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and typically smaller, pale pink to white flowers. This form is native to specific regions in Central Europe, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, contributing to its status as a rare horticultural specimen.
