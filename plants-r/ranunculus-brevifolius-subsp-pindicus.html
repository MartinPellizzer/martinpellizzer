Ranunculus brevifolius subsp. pindicus

Ranunculus brevifolius subsp.

pindicus, commonly known as the Pindus buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of trifoliate leaves, which are hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape. Its flowers are bright yellow, with five to eight sepals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

It blooms from April to June and is often found in mountainous regions of Greece and northwestern Turkey.
