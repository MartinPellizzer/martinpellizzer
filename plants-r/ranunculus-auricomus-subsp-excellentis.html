Ranunculus auricomus subsp. excellentis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

excellentis, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with a distinctive greenish center, and its leaves are deeply lobed with a hairy texture.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a short rhizomatous root system and produces small, green, kidney-shaped fruits.

The subspecies excellentis is distinguished by its slightly larger flowers and more robust growth compared to other members of the species.
