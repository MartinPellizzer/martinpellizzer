Ruellia canescens

Ruellia canescens, commonly known as silverleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Ruellia canescens is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or disturbed areas.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
