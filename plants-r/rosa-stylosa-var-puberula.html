Rosa stylosa var. puberula

Rosa stylosa var. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent-stemmed rose, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets that have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
