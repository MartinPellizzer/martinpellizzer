Ranunculus arizonicus var. subaffinis

Ranunculus arizonicus var. subaffinis, commonly known as the Arizona buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native range.
