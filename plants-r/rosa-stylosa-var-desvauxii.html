Rosa stylosa var. desvauxii

Rosa stylosa var. desvauxii, commonly known as the Desvaux's climbing rose, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous shrub or vine that produces fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems that help it climb and support itself. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
