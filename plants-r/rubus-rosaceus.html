Rubus rosaceus

Rubus rosaceus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants. These berries are typically harvested in late summer and are widely used in culinary applications.

Rubus rosaceus thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
