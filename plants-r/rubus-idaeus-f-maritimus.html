Rubus idaeus f. maritimus

Rubus idaeus f. maritimus, commonly known as the coastal raspberry, is a variant of the red raspberry that thrives in coastal environments.

It is characterized by its smaller, more elongated leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, tart red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It is well adapted to salty air and sandy soils, making it a hardy species in maritime climates.

This form is often found in dunes and coastal woodlands, contributing to the ecological stability of these habitats.
