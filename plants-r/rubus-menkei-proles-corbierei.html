Rubus menkei proles corbierei

Rubus menkei proles corbierei, commonly known as the Menkei raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, red berries that are typically clustered together. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
