Ranunculus brachyphyllarius

Ranunculus brachyphyllarius, commonly known as the short-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.
