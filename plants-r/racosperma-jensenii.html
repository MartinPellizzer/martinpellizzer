Racosperma jensenii

Racosperma jensenii, commonly known as Jensen's acacia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical clusters, typical of many acacia species. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into multiple pairs of small leaflets.

Racosperma jensenii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.
