Raphionacme

Raphionacme, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the Asteraceae family.

These plants are characterized by their slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. They produce clusters of small, yellow to brown flowers that bloom in late summer. Raphionacme species are typically found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas.

The genus includes several species, with Raphionacme glabrata being one of the most commonly recognized.
