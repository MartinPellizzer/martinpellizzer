Rosa chlorigayi

Rosa chlorigayi, commonly known as the greenleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
