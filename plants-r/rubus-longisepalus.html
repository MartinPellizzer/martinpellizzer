Rubus longisepalus

Rubus longisepalus, commonly known as the long-sepaled blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
