Rosa pomifera f. grenieri

Rosa pomifera f. grenieri, commonly known as the Grenier's rose, is a rare and unique variant of the rose family.

It is characterized by its large, edible fruit, which resembles a fuzzy strawberry and is often referred to as a "rose hip.". The plant produces showy pink to red flowers in the spring, followed by the distinctive fruit in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is a subject of interest in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
