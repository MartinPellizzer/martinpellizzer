Racosperma trinervatum

Racosperma trinervatum, commonly known as the narrow-veined acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three leaflets, each having a distinct, narrow vein pattern. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Racosperma trinervatum is valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
