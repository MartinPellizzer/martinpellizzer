Rathbunia chacalapensis

Rathbunia chacalapensis, commonly known as the Chacalapensian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant has elongated, strap-like leaves and thrives in humid, shaded environments. It plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
