Ranunculus dysandrus

Ranunculus dysandrus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with numerous petals and a distinctive central disk. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.

Ranunculus dysandrus is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
