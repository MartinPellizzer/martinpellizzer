Rosa hemisphaerica var. plena

Rosa hemisphaerica var. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered hemispherical rose, is a hybrid rose variety originating from South America.

It is characterized by its full, rounded blooms that are densely packed with petals, giving it a lush, almost satiny appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves.

It produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the specific cultivar. This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
