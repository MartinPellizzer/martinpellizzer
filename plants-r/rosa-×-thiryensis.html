Rosa × thiryensis

Rosa × thiryensis, commonly known as the Thiry's rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to fully double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Rosa × thiryensis is well adapted to coastal environments and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
