Rhodiola pamiroalaica

Rhodiola pamiroalaica, commonly known as the Pamir rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. This plant thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often found at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its roots are rich in adaptogenic compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

Rhodiola pamiroalaica is also noted for its ability to withstand extreme cold and low oxygen levels.
