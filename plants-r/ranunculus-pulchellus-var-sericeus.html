Ranunculus pulchellus var. sericeus

Ranunculus pulchellus var. sericeus, commonly known as the silky buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features delicate, palmate leaves with a soft, silvery-white underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often with a dark center. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower displays.
