Roupala myrtoidea

Roupala myrtoidea, commonly known as the silver maple, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, silvery bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in the spring, which are followed by winged seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riverine forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
