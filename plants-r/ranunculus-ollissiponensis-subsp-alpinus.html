Ranunculus ollissiponensis subsp. alpinus

Ranunculus ollissiponensis subsp.

alpinus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Mediterranean. It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant has trifoliate leaves that are deeply divided, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
