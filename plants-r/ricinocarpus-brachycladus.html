Ricinocarpus brachycladus

Ricinocarpus brachycladus, commonly known as the slender-stemmed ricin tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a leathery texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody capsules containing toxic seeds.

The plant is notable for its ecological importance and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
