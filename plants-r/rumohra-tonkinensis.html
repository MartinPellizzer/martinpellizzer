Rumohra tonkinensis

Rumohra tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese bamboo or Tonkin bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and is often found in wetland or marshy environments. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It is a member of the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.

Due to its habitat preferences, it plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
