Rhododendron undulaticalyx

Rhododendron undulaticalyx, commonly known as the wavy-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaf margins that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. Its evergreen foliage provides year-round interest, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.
