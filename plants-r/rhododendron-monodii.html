Rhododendron monodii

Rhododendron monodii, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, preferring cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are thick and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron monodii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to specific growing conditions.
