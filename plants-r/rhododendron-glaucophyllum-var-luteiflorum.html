Rhododendron glaucophyllum var. luteiflorum

Rhododendron glaucophyllum var. luteiflorum, commonly known as the golden-flowered rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often covered with a waxy coating.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
