Rumex arizonicus

Rumex arizonicus, commonly known as Arizona dock, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread aggressively in disturbed soils.

Rumex arizonicus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common sight in desert and rocky habitats.
