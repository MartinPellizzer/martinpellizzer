Ranunculus microlonchus

Ranunculus microlonchus, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its simple, usually odd-pinnate leaves and showy flowers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
