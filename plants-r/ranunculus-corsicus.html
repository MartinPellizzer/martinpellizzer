Ranunculus corsicus

Ranunculus corsicus, commonly known as the Corsican buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along stream banks. It has a short stem with a single terminal flower, and its leaves are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
