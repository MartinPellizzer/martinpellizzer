Rhus villosa var. usambarensis

Rhus villosa var. usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambara buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically odd-pinnate with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are small, round, and aggregate, turning red when mature.

The species is often found in moist, shaded habitats within montane forests.
