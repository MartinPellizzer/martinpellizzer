Rubus × orlesensis

Rubus × orlesensis, commonly known as the Orlesian rose, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized in various parts of the world.
