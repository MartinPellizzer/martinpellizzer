Rubus corylifolius subsp. hystricopsis

Rubus corylifolius subsp.

hystricopsis, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and thorny stems.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus.
