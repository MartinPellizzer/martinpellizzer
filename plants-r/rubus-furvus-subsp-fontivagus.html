Rubus furvus subsp. fontivagus

Rubus furvus subsp.

fontivagus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, dark red to black berries.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in early summer.

The subspecies is notable for its hardiness and adaptation to high-altitude conditions.
