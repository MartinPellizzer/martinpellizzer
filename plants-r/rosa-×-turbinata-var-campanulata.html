Rosa × turbinata var. campanulata

Rosa × turbinata var. campanulata, commonly known as the campanulata rose, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, campanulate (bell-shaped) flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
