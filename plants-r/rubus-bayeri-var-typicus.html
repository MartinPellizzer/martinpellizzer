Rubus bayeri var. typicus

Rubus bayeri var. typicus, commonly known as the Bayer raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect or semi-woody growth habit and produces clusters of small, red berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them a subject of interest for both botanists and local communities.
