Ribes ovalifolium

Ribes ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaf gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of shrubs and vines commonly found in temperate regions. The plant is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and small, edible berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Ribes ovalifolium plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects.
