Rubus × thyrsanthoides

Rubus × thyrsanthoides, commonly known as brambles or blackberry bushes, is a hybrid plant species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, which provide structural support and serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Rubus × thyrsanthoides thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.
