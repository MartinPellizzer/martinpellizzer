Rubus scaber var. mitior

Rubus scaber var. mitior, commonly known as the soft-bristled raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its smooth, less prickly stems compared to other Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, with a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and edible fruits.
