Reseda lanceolata var. trisecta

Reseda lanceolata var. trisecta, commonly known as the three-lobed everlasting flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are lance-shaped and deeply divided into three lobes, giving the plant its varietal name. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is known for its long-lasting blooms.

This plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
