Rhynchosia acutifolia

Rhynchosia acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved clusterbean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It features trifoliate leaves with acuminate tips and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, hairy, triangular pods. The plant is characterized by its twining habit and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It is valued for its edible seeds and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

Rhynchosia acutifolia is also an important forage plant for livestock in some regions.
