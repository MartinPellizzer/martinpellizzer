Rosa pseudoluciae

Rosa pseudoluciae, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a species native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom throughout the year, making it a popular ornamental plant. Its botanical characteristics include showy, single or semi-double blooms and a preference for well-drained soil and full sun.

Rosa pseudoluciae is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
