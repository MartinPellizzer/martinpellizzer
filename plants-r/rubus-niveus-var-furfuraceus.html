Rubus niveus var. furfuraceus

Rubus niveus var. furfuraceus, commonly known as the white-tailed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its white-tinged, fuzzy stems and large, dark red to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tart or slightly bitter flavor.
