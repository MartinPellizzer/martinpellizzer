Rubus lejeunei subsp. euprepes

Rubus lejeunei subsp.

euprepes, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a shrub native to the western coast of North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas and river valleys.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
