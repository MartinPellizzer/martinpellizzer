Rosa hybrida

Rosa hybrida, commonly known as the garden rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its diverse range of flower colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow, and often features a strong, pleasant fragrance. The plant typically has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters and can be single, semi-double, or double in form.

Rosa hybrida is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and is valued for its adaptability to various growing conditions.
