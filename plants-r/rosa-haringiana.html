Rosa haringiana

Rosa haringiana, commonly known as the Haring's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are covered in soft, white hairs.

Rosa haringiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
