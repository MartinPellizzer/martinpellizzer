Rhododendron manipurense

Rhododendron manipurense, commonly known as the Manipur rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in the northeastern Indian state of Manipur.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. The plant features dense, leathery leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is often found in dense forests and undergrowth.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of the region's biodiversity and a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
