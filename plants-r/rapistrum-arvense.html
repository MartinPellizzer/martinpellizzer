Rapistrum arvense

Rapistrum arvense, commonly known as common shepherd's purse, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many cruciferous vegetables. The plant is characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods and small white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in cultivated fields.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
