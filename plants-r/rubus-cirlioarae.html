Rubus cirlioarae

Rubus cirlioarae, commonly known as the Cirlioarae raspberry, is a rare species of bramble native to a limited geographic region.

It is characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
