Rhododendron rugosum var. laeve

Rhododendron rugosum var. laeve, commonly known as the smooth-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically smooth and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant pink or red blossoms. This plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
