Ranunculus japonicus var. ibukiensis

Ranunculus japonicus var. ibukiensis, commonly known as the Ibuki mountain buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces a cluster of small, bright yellow flowers in early spring.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
