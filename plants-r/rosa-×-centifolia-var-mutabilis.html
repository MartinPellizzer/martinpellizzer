Rosa × centifolia var. mutabilis

Rosa × centifolia var. mutabilis, commonly known as the Damask rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its highly fragrant flowers, which can change color from white to pink to red as they bloom. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters and produces multiple blooms throughout the summer. Its foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, while the stems are thorny.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is also used in the production of rose oil and perfume.
