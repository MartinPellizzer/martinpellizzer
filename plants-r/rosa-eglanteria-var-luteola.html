Rosa eglanteria var. luteola

Rosa eglanteria var. luteola, commonly known as the yellow prickly rose, is a variant of the shrub rose species characterized by its thorny stems and yellow flowers.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in rocky or woodland areas. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its hardy nature.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience.
