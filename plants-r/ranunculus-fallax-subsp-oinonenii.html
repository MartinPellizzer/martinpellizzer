Ranunculus fallax subsp. oinonenii

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

oinonenii, commonly known as the Finnish buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wet meadows.

The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its short stature and early spring blooming period.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is distinguished by its specific subspecies traits.
