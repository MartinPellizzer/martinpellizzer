Rhododendron stenaulum

Rhododendron stenaulum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a striking visual contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
