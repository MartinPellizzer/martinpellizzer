Ranunculus olgae

Ranunculus olgae, commonly known as Olgaea buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Ranunculus olgae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
