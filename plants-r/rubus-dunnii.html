Rubus dunnii

Rubus dunnii, commonly known as the Chilean blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a distinctive, intense flavor. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fruit.

The berries are used in various culinary applications, including jams, desserts, and beverages.
