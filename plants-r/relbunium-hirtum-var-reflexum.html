Relbunium hirtum var. reflexum

Relbunium hirtum var. reflexum, commonly known as the hairy hairgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy, slender leaves that are often reflexed at the base. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, with small, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is typically found in grasslands or along the edges of wetlands.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting insect life.
