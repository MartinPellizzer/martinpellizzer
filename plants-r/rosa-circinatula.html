Rosa circinatula

Rosa circinatula, commonly known as the curled leaf rose, is a species native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that curl inward, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

Rosa circinatula is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
