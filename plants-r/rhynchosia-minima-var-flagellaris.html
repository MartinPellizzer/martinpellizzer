Rhynchosia minima var. flagellaris

Rhynchosia minima var. flagellaris, commonly known as the slender legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with slender, branching stems. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes, and the seeds are enclosed in elongated, fibrous pods.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
