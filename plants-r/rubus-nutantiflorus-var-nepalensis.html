Rubus nutantiflorus var. nepalensis

Rubus nutantiflorus var. nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and is often found in mountainous regions of Nepal and adjacent areas. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to rocky or forested habitats.

The fruits, which are edible, are typically small berries that ripen in late summer.
