Rubus centiformis f. trichocarpus

Rubus centiformis f. trichocarpus, commonly known as the hairy-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This cultivar is valued for its hardiness and is often used in both ornamental and edible gardening.
