Rosa rhipidophora

Rosa rhipidophora, commonly known as the sandalwood rose, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to white flowers. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual beauty.

Rosa rhipidophora is also noted for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
