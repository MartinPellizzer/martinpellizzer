Rubus × caldesianus

Rubus × caldesianus, commonly known as the Caldesian blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are pink to white in color.

This species is valued for its edible fruits, which have a tart flavor and are often used in jams and desserts.
