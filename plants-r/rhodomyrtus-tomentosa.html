Rhodomyrtus tomentosa

Rhodomyrtus tomentosa, commonly known as the rambutan tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruits that resemble a hairy coconut, with a bright red or yellow exterior. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are often clustered together. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a velvety texture.

The rambutan tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its edible fruit.
