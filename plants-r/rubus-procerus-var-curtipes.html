Rubus procerus var. curtipes

Rubus procerus var. curtipes, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, characterized by its arching stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically not as flavorful as other blackberry varieties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important plant in its native range.
