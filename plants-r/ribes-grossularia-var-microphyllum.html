Ribes grossularia var. microphyllum

Ribes grossularia var. microphyllum, commonly known as the miniature gooseberry, is a compact shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces tiny, edible berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
