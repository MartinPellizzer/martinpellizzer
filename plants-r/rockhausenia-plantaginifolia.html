Rockhausenia plantaginifolia

Rockhausenia plantaginifolia, commonly known as the purple rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments. The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its most striking feature is its clusters of violet or purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rockhausenia plantaginifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
