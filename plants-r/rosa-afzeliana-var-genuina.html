Rosa afzeliana var. genuina

Rosa afzeliana var. genuina, commonly known as the Ethiopian rose, is a shrub native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in thorns. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

The botanical features of this species make it a unique and valuable addition to horticultural collections.
