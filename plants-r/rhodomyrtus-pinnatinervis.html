Rhodomyrtus pinnatinervis

Rhodomyrtus pinnatinervis, commonly known as the broad-leaved rhodomyrtus, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters. These flowers are followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional cuisine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
