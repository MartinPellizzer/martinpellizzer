Ribes reclinatum var. sarmentaceum

Ribes reclinatum var. sarmentaceum, commonly known as the creeping gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its trailing or prostrate growth habit, with stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red, edible berries in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
