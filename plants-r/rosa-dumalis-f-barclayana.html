Rosa dumalis f. barclayana

Rosa dumalis f. barclayana, commonly known as the Barclay's shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features upright, thorny stems with dense, dark green foliage that turns reddish in autumn. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and has a slow growth rate.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8.
