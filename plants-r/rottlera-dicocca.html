Rottlera dicocca

Rottlera dicocca, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a perennial shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Rottlera dicocca is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to the flora of its native region.
