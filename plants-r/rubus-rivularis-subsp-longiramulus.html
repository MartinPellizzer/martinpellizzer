Rubus rivularis subsp. longiramulus

Rubus rivularis subsp.

longiramulus, commonly known as the long-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed by birds and mammals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as streambanks and forest understories.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its elongated pedicels and hairy foliage.
