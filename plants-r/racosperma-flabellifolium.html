Racosperma flabellifolium

Racosperma flabellifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a distinctive, fan-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Racosperma flabellifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
