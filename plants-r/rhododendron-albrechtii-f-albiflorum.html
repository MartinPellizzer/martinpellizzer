Rhododendron albrechtii f. albiflorum

Rhododendron albrechtii f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered albrecht rhododendron, is a unique hybrid species native to the high altitudes of western Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with dense, leathery leaves that provide year-round foliage. Its botanical features include showy bracts that often resemble petals, enhancing its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare plants.
