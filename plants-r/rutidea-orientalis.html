Rutidea orientalis

Rutidea orientalis, commonly known as the oriental rutidion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its seeds are enclosed in a pod-like fruit and are often dispersed by wind or animals.

Rutidea orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
