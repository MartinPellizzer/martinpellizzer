Rubus hirtus var. humiliformis

Rubus hirtus var. humiliformis, commonly known as the low-growing raspberry bush, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed soils, preferring partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and covered with fine, glandular hairs.

The flowers are white or pale pink, with five sepals and five petals, and the fruit is a red, hairy drupe that ripens in late summer.
