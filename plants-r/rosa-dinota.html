Rosa dinota

Rosa dinota, commonly known as the Brazilian rose or the pink rose, is a species of shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. Rosa dinota thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
