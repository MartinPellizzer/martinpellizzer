Rhodomyrtus canescens

Rhodomyrtus canescens, commonly known as the pink river gum or red river gum, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its small, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is often found growing in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Rhodomyrtus canescens is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
