Rubus gratiosus var. russulus

Rubus gratiosus var. russulus, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, aggregate fruits that develop from a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are pinkish-white with five petals.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
