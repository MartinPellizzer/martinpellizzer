Rhododendron brentelii

Rhododendron brentelii, commonly known as the Brentel's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of Nepal and India.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in high-altitude forests and alpine environments. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a dense covering of fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in trusses and are usually pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron brentelii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
