Rubus × thuillieri var. pomponius

Rubus × thuillieri var. pomponius, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, though they may also ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
