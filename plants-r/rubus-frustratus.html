Rubus frustratus

Rubus frustratus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, thornless stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny, dark purple to black drupelets. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
