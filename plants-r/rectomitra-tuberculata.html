Rectomitra tuberculata

Rectomitra tuberculata, commonly known as the warty slipper orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flower structure known as a labellum, which is often adorned with warty projections. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its exotic appearance.
