Rhodomyrtus tomentosa var. tomentosa

Rhodomyrtus tomentosa var. tomentosa, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus or Australian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, wetlands, and disturbed soils, thriving in humid and saline conditions. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 3 meters, with a spreading habit and a strong, aromatic scent.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and water.
