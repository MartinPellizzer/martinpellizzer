Rosa micrantha var. lusitanica

Rosa micrantha var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese rose, is a species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorned stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, usually blooming in late spring to early summer. Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various soil types.

The variety is notable for its historical use in traditional medicine and its role in local flora.
