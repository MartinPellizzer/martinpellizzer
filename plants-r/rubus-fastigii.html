Rubus fastigii

Rubus fastigii, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Rubus fastigii thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
