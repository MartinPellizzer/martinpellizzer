Ranunculus candollei

Ranunculus candollei, commonly known as the Candolle's buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ranunculaceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
