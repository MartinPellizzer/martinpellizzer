Rhamnus esquirolii

Rhamnus esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark that becomes more pronounced with age.

Rhamnus esquirolii is often found in chaparral and woodland ecosystems, where it contributes to the structural diversity of the plant community.
