Rubus radula var. anglicanus

Rubus radula var. anglicanus, commonly known as the English blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry species.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically eaten raw or used in jams. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
