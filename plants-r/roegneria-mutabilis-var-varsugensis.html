Roegneria mutabilis var. varsugensis

Roegneria mutabilis var. varsugensis, commonly known as the variable wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are often tinged with red or brown, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

This variety is noted for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions, including cold temperatures and poor soil fertility.
