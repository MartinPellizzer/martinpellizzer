Rhododendron longifalcatum

Rhododendron longifalcatum, commonly known as the long-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shady, moist environments, often in forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are elongated and leathery, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron longifalcatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical gardens.
