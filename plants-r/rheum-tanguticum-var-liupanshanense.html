Rheum tanguticum var. liupanshanense

Rheum tanguticum var. liupanshanense, commonly known as the Liupanshan rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces tall, reddish stems with clusters of small yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
