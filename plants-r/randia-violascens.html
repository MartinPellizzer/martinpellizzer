Randia violascens

Randia violascens, commonly known as the purple randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Randia violascens is often found in moist, shady habitats such as forest understories or along streambanks.
