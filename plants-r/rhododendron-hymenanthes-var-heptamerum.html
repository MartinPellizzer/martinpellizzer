Rhododendron hymenanthes var. heptamerum

Rhododendron hymenanthes var. heptamerum, commonly known as the seven-lobed rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a pointed tip. The plant prefers acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
