Rhododendron lochiae

Rhododendron lochiae, commonly known as the Lochia rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron lochiae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden environments.
