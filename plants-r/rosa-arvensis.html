Rosa arvensis

Rosa arvensis, commonly known as the field rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its hips, which are the fruit, are reddish and contain numerous seeds.

Rosa arvensis is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
