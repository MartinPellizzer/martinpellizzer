Rubus hirtus var. pentaphyllus

Rubus hirtus var. pentaphyllus, commonly known as the five-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The botanical features of this variant make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
