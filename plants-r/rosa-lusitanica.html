Rosa lusitanica

Rosa lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa lusitanica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical significance in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
