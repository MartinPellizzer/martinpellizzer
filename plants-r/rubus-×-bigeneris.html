Rubus × bigeneris

Rubus × bigeneris, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
