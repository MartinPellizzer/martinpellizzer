Rubus radula f. ferox

Rubus radula f. ferox, commonly known as the sharp-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and have a firm, tart flavor. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of many Rubus species. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
