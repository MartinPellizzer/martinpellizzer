Rubus pileatus

Rubus pileatus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its hairy texture is a distinguishing feature, giving it its common name.
