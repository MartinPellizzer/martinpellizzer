Rosa × permiscibilis f. pulchra

Rosa × permiscibilis f. pulchra, commonly known as the Double White Rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its fully double white flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its fragrant, velvety petals that create a dense, full bloom. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with thorny stems and dark green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This cultivar is prized in gardens for its ornamental value and enduring beauty.
