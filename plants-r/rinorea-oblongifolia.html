Rinorea oblongifolia

Rinorea oblongifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its ovate, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Rinorea oblongifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
