Rosa canina f. mandellensis

Rosa canina f. mandellensis, commonly known as the Mandell's dog rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose species.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral morphology, including larger and more showy flowers compared to the typical Rosa canina. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

This form is of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic traits and potential horticultural value.
