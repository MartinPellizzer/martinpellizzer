Rothmannia eucodon

Rothmannia eucodon, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Rothmannia eucodon is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
