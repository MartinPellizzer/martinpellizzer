Rubus winteri

Rubus winteri, commonly known as the winter raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
