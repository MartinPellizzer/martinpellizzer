Rosa hawrana f. phasianica

Rosa hawrana f. phasianica, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, double flowers that range in color from deep red to pale pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrant blooms.
