Rhododendron magniflorum

Rhododendron magniflorum, commonly known as the grandiflorous rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring, displaying shades of pink to red. The plant is an evergreen shrub with dense, leathery foliage that provides year-round structure. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
