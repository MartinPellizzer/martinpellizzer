Rubus hoplotheca

Rubus hoplotheca, commonly known as the Australian blackberry, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Rubus hoplotheca is often found in coastal and dry inland regions of Australia.
