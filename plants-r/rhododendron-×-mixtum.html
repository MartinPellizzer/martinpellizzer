Rhododendron × mixtum

Rhododendron × mixtum, commonly known as the hybrid rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, broad leaves and showy, clustered flowers.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Japan, where it grows in mountainous areas. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to purple, bloom in spring and are often used in horticulture for ornamental purposes.

Rhododendron × mixtum is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various garden environments.
