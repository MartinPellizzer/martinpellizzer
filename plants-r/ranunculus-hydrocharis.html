Ranunculus hydrocharis

Ranunculus hydrocharis, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial aquatic or semi-aquatic plant that typically grows in shallow water or damp soils. The plant features small, delicate leaves that are divided into lobes and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.

Ranunculus hydrocharis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
