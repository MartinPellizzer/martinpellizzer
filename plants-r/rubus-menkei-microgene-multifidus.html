Rubus menkei microgene multifidus

Rubus menkei microgene multifidus, commonly known as the multifidus blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black berries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

The fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tart or astringent flavor.
