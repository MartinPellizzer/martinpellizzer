Rosa micrantha f. glandulifera

Rosa micrantha f. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular-stem Rosa, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while the fruits are red, hairy rosehips.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
