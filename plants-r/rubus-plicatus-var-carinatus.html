Rubus plicatus var. carinatus

Rubus plicatus var. carinatus, commonly known as the ridge raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
