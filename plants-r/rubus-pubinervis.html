Rubus pubinervis

Rubus pubinervis, commonly known as the pubescent bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Rubus pubinervis plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
