Rhodora deflexa

Rhodora deflexa, commonly known as the flexible rhodora, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of North America, particularly in the eastern United States. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are typically evergreen, leathery, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems.

Rhodora deflexa thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested environments.
