Ranunculus lanuginosiformis

Ranunculus lanuginosiformis, commonly known as the downy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, downy appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often accompanied by a prominent, hairy sepal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
