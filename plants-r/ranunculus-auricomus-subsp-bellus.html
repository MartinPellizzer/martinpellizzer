Ranunculus auricomus subsp. bellus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

bellus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States. It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive, palmately divided leaf structure.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmate, with three to five lobes, and its flowers have five to nine petals.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
