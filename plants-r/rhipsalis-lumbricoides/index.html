Rhipsalis Lumbricoides

Rhipsalis lumbricoides is a unique and lesser-known member of the cactus family, native to the tropical regions of South America. This plant is distinguished by its long, twisting stems that resemble the shape of earthworms, giving it its common name. Unlike many cacti, it does not have typical spines, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture, making it relatively easy to care for. Its graceful appearance and low maintenance make it an intriguing addition to any plant collection.
