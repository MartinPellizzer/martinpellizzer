Roegneria thoroldiana

Roegneria thoroldiana, commonly known as the Thorold's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and forms dense, low-growing tufts. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Roegneria thoroldiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
