Restio gracilis

Restio gracilis, commonly known as the slender restio, is a grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in winter, and it plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems.

Restio gracilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal in coastal gardens.
