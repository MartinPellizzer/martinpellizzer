Reseda globulosa var. brevipedunculata

Reseda globulosa var. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, round seed capsules that are characteristic of the genus Reseda. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly fields.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
