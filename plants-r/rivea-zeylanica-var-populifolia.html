Rivea zeylanica var. populifolia

Rivea zeylanica var. populifolia, commonly known as the Indian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often lobed, giving it a distinctive foliage appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in ornamental gardens.
