Rubus tereticaulis microgene finitimus

Rubus tereticaulis microgene finitimus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
