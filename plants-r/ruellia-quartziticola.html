Ruellia quartziticola

Ruellia quartziticola, commonly known as the quartzitic ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often covered in fine pubescence.

Ruellia quartziticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
