Roldana tepopana

Roldana tepopana, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Roldana tepopana is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
