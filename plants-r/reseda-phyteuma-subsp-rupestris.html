Reseda phyteuma subsp. rupestris

Reseda phyteuma subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the rock reseda, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are adapted to withstand dry and rocky environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
