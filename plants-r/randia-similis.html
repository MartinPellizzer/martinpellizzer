Randia similis

Randia similis, commonly known as the red spiderling, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, elliptic leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. Randia similis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for warm, humid climates.
