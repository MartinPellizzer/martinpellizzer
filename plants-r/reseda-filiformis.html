Reseda filiformis

Reseda filiformis, commonly known as slender reseda, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically features thin, hairless stems that are often prostrate or ascending, with small, linear leaves. The flowers are usually yellow or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom during the summer months. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields.

The plant is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental species.
