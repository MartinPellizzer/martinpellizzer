Rosa glauca f. salicifolia

Rosa glauca f. salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf dogwood rose, is a hybrid form of the blue rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive, willow-like foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers in early summer. It is a compact shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique leaf structure and delicate blooms.
