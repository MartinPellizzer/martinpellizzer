Rubus silvae-bohemicae

Rubus silvae-bohemicae, commonly known as the Bohemian bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Central Europe and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
