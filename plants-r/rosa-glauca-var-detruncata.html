Rosa glauca var. detruncata

Rosa glauca var. detruncata, commonly known as the blue rose or the dwarf blue rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are covered in thorns. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardy nature in cooler climates.
