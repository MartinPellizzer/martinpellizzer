Rubus fuscoater var. uncinulatus

Rubus fuscoater var. uncinulatus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical structure supports its role as both a food source for wildlife and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
