Rosa glauca subvar. melanophylloides

Rosa glauca subvar. melanophylloides, commonly known as the dark-leaved dog rose, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and dark green, often glossy leaves. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and scrublands, preferring well-drained soils.

Its stems are covered in thorns, and it produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
