Rungia parviflora var. aspera

Rungia parviflora var. aspera, commonly known as the rough-leaved rungia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers and rough, textured leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
