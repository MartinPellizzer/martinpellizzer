Rubus zambalensis

Rubus zambalensis, commonly known as the Zambal blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a valuable resource in local ecosystems and traditional agriculture.
