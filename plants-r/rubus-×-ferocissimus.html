Rubus × ferocissimus

Rubus × ferocissimus, commonly known as the devil's weed or blackberry bramble, is a vigorous, thorny shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus hirtellus and Rubus glaucus. The plant is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries, which are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, whitish hairs. Its extensive root system allows it to spread rapidly, often forming dense thickets that can dominate natural habitats.

Due to its aggressive growth and allelopathic properties, it is considered a significant invasive species in many regions.
