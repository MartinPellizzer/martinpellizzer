Rosa glutinosa var. lioclada

Rosa glutinosa var. lioclada, commonly known as the sticky rose or sticky rock rose, is a shrub-like plant native to southern China and northern Vietnam.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and covered with a sticky, glandular coating. This plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
