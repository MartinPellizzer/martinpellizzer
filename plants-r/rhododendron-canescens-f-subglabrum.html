Rhododendron canescens f. subglabrum

Rhododendron canescens f. subglabrum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved rhododendron, is a botanical variant characterized by its reduced pubescence on the leaves.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly China. The plant typically features dense, evergreen foliage with broad, ovate leaves that are smooth and leathery in texture. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish in color, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
