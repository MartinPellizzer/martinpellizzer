Rhododendron ferrugineum f. erectum

Rhododendron ferrugineum f. erectum, commonly known as the dwarf alpine azalea, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
