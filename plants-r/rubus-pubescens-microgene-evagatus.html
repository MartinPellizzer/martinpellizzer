Rubus pubescens microgene evagatus

Rubus pubescens microgene evagatus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry.

This plant is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, reddish fruits. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture. The flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.
