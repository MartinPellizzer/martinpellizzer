Rubus fagicola microgene tolosanus

Rubus fagicola microgene tolosanus, commonly known as the Tolosan blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
