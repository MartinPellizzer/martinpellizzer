Rubus rivularis subsp. horridulus

Rubus rivularis subsp.

horridulus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its prickly stems and dark, edible berries.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The subspecies is distinguished by its rough, hairy texture and robust growth habit.
