Rhytachne anisonodis

Rhytachne anisonodis, commonly known as the variable leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that vary in shape and size, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Rhytachne anisonodis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
