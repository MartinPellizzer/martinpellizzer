Rubus idaeus var. borealisinensis

Rubus idaeus var. borealisinensis, commonly known as the northern raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a perennial shrub with biennial stems that produce fruit in the second year of growth. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous small drupes. It thrives in cooler climates and is typically found in boreal and subarctic regions.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to poor soil conditions.
