Ranunculus sceleratus var. hirsutus

Ranunculus sceleratus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine, hairy glands, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in early spring. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
