Rubus chlorothyrsos var. staminulatus

Rubus chlorothyrsos var. staminulatus, commonly known as the staminulate blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, where the stamens are elongated and form a distinct, brush-like appearance. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unusual morphology and potential for cultivation.
