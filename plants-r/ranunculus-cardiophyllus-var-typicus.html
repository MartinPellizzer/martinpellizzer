Ranunculus cardiophyllus var. typicus

Ranunculus cardiophyllus var. typicus, commonly known as the heart-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, glandular surface. The leaves are deeply lobed, often with a heart-shaped base, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
