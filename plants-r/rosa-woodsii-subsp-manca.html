Rosa woodsii subsp. manca

Rosa woodsii subsp.

manca, commonly known as the Manca rock rose, is a subspecies of the woods rose native to the western United States. It is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white.

The leaves are typically compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is well adapted to rocky and arid environments. It produces oval, red-orange fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
