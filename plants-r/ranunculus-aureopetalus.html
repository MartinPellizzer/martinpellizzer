Ranunculus aureopetalus

Ranunculus aureopetalus, commonly known as the golden-petaled buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petal-like sepals that are often tinged with orange or reddish hues. The plant has a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves that are deeply lobed and arranged in a spiral pattern. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, daisy-like blooms.
