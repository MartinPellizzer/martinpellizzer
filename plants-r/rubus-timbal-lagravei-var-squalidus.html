Rubus timbal-lagravei var. squalidus

Rubus timbal-lagravei var. squalidus, commonly known as the squalid blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically dark green and serrated. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in heads, while the fruit is a aggregate berry that ripens to a dark purple or black color. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for taxonomists and botanists due to its unique morphological traits.
