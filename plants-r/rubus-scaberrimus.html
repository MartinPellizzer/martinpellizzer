Rubus scaberrimus

Rubus scaberrimus, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and rocky slopes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its thorny structure and edible berries.
