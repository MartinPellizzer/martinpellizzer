Rosa tricolor

Rosa tricolor, commonly known as the sweet William or the three-color rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink, white, and red flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Rosa tricolor is a herbaceous perennial that is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
