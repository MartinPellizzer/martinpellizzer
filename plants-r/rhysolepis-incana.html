Rhysolepis incana

Rhysolepis incana, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, dark blue berries. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive, flaky bark that peels in strips. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

Rhysolepis incana is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
