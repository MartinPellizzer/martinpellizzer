Rubus ostryifolius

Rubus ostryifolius, commonly known as the European bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small white or pink flowers in clusters, followed by red or black berries that are edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it an important component of many ecosystems in Europe.
