Roegneria japonensis var. hackeliana

Roegneria japonensis var. hackeliana, commonly known as the Japanese false brome, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are usually pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its fine texture and seasonal interest.
