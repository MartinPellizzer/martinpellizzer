Rhamnella inaequilatera

Rhamnella inaequilatera, commonly known as the unequal-armed buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves that are often asymmetrical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds. It typically grows in moist woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
