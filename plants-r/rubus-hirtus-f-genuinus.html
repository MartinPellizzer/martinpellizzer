Rubus hirtus f. genuinus

Rubus hirtus f. genuinus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a subspecies of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, moist environments. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small white flowers in the spring.

This subspecies is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
