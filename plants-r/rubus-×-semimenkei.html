Rubus × semimenkei

Rubus × semimenkei, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry or loganberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus pectoralis (loganberry), resulting in a plant with distinctive characteristics. The plant produces large, dark purple to black berries that are typically larger and sweeter than those of its parent species. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade.

The botanical features of Rubus × semimenkei make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
