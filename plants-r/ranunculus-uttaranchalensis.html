Ranunculus uttaranchalensis

Ranunculus uttaranchalensis, commonly known as the Uttaranchal buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellowish to orange-colored flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky and moist environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native region.
