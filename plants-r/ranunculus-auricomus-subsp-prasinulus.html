Ranunculus auricomus subsp. prasinulus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

prasinulus, commonly known as the greenleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, often adorned with a distinctive greenish or yellowish central blotch. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream sides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Ranunculaceae family.
