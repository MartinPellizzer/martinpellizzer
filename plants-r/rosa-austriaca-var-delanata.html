Rosa austriaca var. delanata

Rosa austriaca var. delanata, commonly known as the Austrian rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody structure, simple leaf arrangement, and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
