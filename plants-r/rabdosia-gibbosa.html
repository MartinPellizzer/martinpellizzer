Rabdosia gibbosa

Rabdosia gibbosa, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Rabdosia gibbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
