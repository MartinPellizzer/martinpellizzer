Ranunculus ornithopodioides

Ranunculus ornithopodioides, commonly known as the bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has a creeping stem with compound leaves that resemble a bird's foot, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Ranunculus ornithopodioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
