Rubus ledermannii

Rubus ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other members like raspberries and strawberries.
