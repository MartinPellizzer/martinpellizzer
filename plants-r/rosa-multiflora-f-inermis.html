Rosa multiflora f. inermis

Rosa multiflora f. inermis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed rose, is a cultivar of the multiflora rose species.

It is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems, which distinguish it from other varieties in the genus. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Due to its vigorous growth, it is often used as a hedge or screen plant in landscaping.
