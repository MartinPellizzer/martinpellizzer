Richardia sancta

Richardia sancta, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the surface of water, while its roots are submerged. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

The botanical features of Richardia sancta make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
