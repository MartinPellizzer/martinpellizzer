Rhododendron macrogemmum

Rhododendron macrogemmum, commonly known as the large-gemmule rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, moist, acidic soils. The plant features showy, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, ranging in color from pink to white. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing dense foliage that offers habitat for various wildlife.

Rhododendron macrogemmum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
