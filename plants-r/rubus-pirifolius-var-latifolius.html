Rubus pirifolius var. latifolius

Rubus pirifolius var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands. Its flowering occurs in spring, producing white or pale pink flowers that attract pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
