Rubus albescens var. extensus

Rubus albescens var. extensus, commonly known as the extended whitebract raspberry, is a variant of the whitebract raspberry found primarily in the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits composed of numerous drupes, which are usually white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
