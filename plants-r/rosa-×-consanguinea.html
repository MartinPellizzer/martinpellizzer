Rosa × consanguinea

Rosa × consanguinea, commonly known as the hybrid tea rose, is a popular ornamental plant characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It is a hybrid between Rosa damascena and Rosa gallica, resulting in a plant with a compact growth habit and thorny stems. The flowers typically come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow, and are known for their strong fragrance. This species is widely cultivated in gardens for its beauty and is often used in floral arrangements.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth and blooming.
