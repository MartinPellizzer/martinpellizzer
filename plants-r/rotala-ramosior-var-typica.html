Rotala ramosior var. typica

Rotala ramosior var. typica, commonly known as the spiked water hyssop, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features whorled leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant thrives in shallow water and is often used in pond landscaping for its ornamental value.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
