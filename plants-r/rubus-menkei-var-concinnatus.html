Rubus menkei var. concinnatus

Rubus menkei var. concinnatus, commonly known as the velvet raspberry, is a shrub native to the high elevations of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
