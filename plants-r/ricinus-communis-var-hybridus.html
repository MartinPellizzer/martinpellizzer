Ricinus communis var. hybridus

Ricinus communis var. hybridus, commonly known as the castor oil plant, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and southern regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and spiky, red-tinged seed capsules that contain toxic seeds. The plant is known for producing castor oil, which is extracted from its seeds and used in various industrial and medicinal applications. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy species suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Its unique appearance and economic importance make it a notable botanical specimen.
