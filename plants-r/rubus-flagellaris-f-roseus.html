Rubus flagellaris f. roseus

Rubus flagellaris f. roseus, commonly known as the rose-flowered blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are usually pink to rose-colored, giving it its common name.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
