Rhododendron williamsianum

Rhododendron williamsianum, commonly known as the Kashmiri rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India, Nepal, and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows in mountainous areas with cool, humid climates and well-drained acidic soils. It is often found in forests and shrublands at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,500 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
