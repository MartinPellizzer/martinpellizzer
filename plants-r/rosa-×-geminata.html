Rosa × geminata

Rosa × geminata, commonly known as the double-flowered garden rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy blooms that typically feature multiple layers of petals, giving it a full, rounded appearance. The plant is a deciduous shrub, often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, depending on the variety.

Rosa × geminata thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
