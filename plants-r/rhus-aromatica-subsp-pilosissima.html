Rhus aromatica subsp. pilosissima

Rhus aromatica subsp.

pilosissima, commonly known as smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions make it a popular choice for landscaping.
