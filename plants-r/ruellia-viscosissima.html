Ruellia viscosissima

Ruellia viscosissima, commonly known as sticky ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, often sticky stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ruellia viscosissima is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and prolonged blooming period.
