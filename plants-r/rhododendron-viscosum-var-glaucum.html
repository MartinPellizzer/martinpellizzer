Rhododendron viscosum var. glaucum

Rhododendron viscosum var. glaucum, commonly known as the glaucous leatheryleaf azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery, dark green leaves that often have a waxy, bluish-gray coating. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to woodland gardens.
