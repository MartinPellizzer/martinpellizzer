Rubus borreri subsp. uncinatus

Rubus borreri subsp.

uncinatus, commonly known as the hooked blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, while edible, is often used in local cuisines and has historical significance among indigenous communities.
