Rosa acanthochlamys

Rosa acanthochlamys, commonly known as the thorned-leaved rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

Rosa acanthochlamys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
