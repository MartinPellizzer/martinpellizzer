Roemeria macrostigma

Roemeria macrostigma, commonly known as the broad-leaved roemeria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal rosettes of large, waxy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tall, erect stems bearing small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas with mild climates.

Roemeria macrostigma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage.
