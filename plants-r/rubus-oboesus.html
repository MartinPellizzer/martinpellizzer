Rubus oboesus

Rubus oboesus, commonly known as the broad-leafed blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, where it typically grows in woodlands and hedgerows. The plant is characterized by its prickly stems, trifoliate leaves, and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. Its flowers are white or pale pink and are clustered in racemes.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
