Rhodomyrtus surigaoensis

Rhodomyrtus surigaoensis, commonly known as the Surigao firetree, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic and ornamental species. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils along the shores of Mindanao.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
