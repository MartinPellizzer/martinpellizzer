Rhododendron indicum var. brachytrichum

Rhododendron indicum var. brachytrichum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a decorative shrub.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small wildlife.
