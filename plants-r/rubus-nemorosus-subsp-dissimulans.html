Rubus nemorosus subsp. dissimulans

Rubus nemorosus subsp.

dissimulans, commonly known as the creeping blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, often found in temperate regions.
