Ranunculus quinquangularis

Ranunculus quinquangularis, commonly known as the five-angled buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five sharply pointed petals, which are characteristic of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves with finely toothed edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
