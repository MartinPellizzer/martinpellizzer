Rubus bellardii var. umbrosus

Rubus bellardii var. umbrosus, commonly known as the shade raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands, preferring rich, loamy soils. The plant produces small, edible berries that are similar in taste to regular raspberries but often have a more intense flavor. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its edible fruits, making it a versatile addition to native plant gardens.
