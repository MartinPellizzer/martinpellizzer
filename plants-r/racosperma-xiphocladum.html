Racosperma xiphocladum

Racosperma xiphocladum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy leaves and yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes. The plant is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
