Rhexia schrankiana

Rhexia schrankiana, commonly known as the pinkroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to September. The plant has a distinctive reddish-brown root system, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Rhexia schrankiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
