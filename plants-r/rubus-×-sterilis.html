Rubus × sterilis

Rubus × sterilis, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many brambles. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.

Due to its vigorous growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
