Rosa sepium rosea

Rosa sepium rosea, commonly known as the pink climbing rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 6 meters in height when supported by a trellis or fence. Its leaves are dark green, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The rose is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
