Rhododendron saluenense

Rhododendron saluenense, commonly known as the Saluenense Rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in the spring and attract various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
