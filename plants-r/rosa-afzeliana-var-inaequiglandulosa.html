Rosa afzeliana var. inaequiglandulosa

Rosa afzeliana var. inaequiglandulosa, commonly known as the unequal-glandular rose, is a variant of the Afzelia rose species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically has thorny stems and elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in ornamental gardening.

The botanical features of this variant make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and scientific study.
