Rubus rosifolius var. pluriflorus

Rubus rosifolius var. pluriflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered bramble, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are aggregate berries that ripen to red, often used in local cuisines.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
