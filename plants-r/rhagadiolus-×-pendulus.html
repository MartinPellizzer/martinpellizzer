Rhagadiolus × pendulus

Rhagadiolus × pendulus, commonly known as the weeping rhagadiolus, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with long, pendulous branches that give it its distinctive weeping appearance. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its unique floral display.
