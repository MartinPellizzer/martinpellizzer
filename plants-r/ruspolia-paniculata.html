Ruspolia paniculata

Ruspolia paniculata, commonly known as the red-flowered panicled orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear numerous small, bright red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
