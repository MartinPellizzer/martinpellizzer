Rubus ambifarius var. senticetorum

Rubus ambifarius var. senticetorum, commonly known as the fragrant raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its bi-pinnate leaves and the production of red, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are white and clustered, and it has a distinctive scent that becomes more pronounced as the fruits ripen.

The variety is notable for its aromatic qualities and is often cultivated for both its flavor and ornamental value.
