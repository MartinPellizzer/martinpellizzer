Rubus nantoensis

Rubus nantoensis, commonly known as the Nanto blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes a wide variety of shrubs and subshrubs commonly referred to as blackberries or raspberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The botanical features of Rubus nantoensis make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
