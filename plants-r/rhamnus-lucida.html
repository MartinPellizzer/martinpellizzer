Rhamnus lucida

Rhamnus lucida, commonly known as the Florida buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, hammocks, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, smooth, black berries.

Rhamnus lucida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
