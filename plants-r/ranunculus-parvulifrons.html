Ranunculus parvulifrons

Ranunculus parvulifrons, commonly known as the small-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features basal leaves that are pinnately divided with numerous small, pointed lobes, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals and numerous stamens. The plant grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
