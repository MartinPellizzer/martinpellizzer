Rosa stipularis

Rosa stipularis, commonly known as the stipular rose, is a species native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pink to red blooms in the spring, which are followed by orange-red hips in the fall. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets that provide a distinctive appearance.

Rosa stipularis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
