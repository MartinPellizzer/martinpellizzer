Ribes sanguineum var. grandiflorum

Ribes sanguineum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered currant, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, pendulous flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible red berries that ripen in late summer, which are often used in jams and jellies. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
