Roseanthus

Roseanthus, commonly known as the sweet sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and erect.

Roseanthus is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
