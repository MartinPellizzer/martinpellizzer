Rhododendron dilatatum subsp. arborescens

Rhododendron dilatatum subsp.

arborescens, commonly known as the alpine rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in subalpine or alpine habitats.

The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. It is well adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh conditions such as strong winds and limited soil nutrients.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native ecosystems.
