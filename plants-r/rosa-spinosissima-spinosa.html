Rosa spinosissima spinosa

Rosa spinosissima spinosa, commonly known as the Spanish rose or the thorny rose, is a species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its arching stems and dense clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant features stout, thorned stems and dark green, lobed leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery and medicine make it a notable botanical species.
