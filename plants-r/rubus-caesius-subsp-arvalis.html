Rubus caesius subsp. arvalis

Rubus caesius subsp.

arvalis, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the characteristic raspberry fruits.
