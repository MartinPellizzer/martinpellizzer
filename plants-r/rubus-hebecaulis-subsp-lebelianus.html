Rubus hebecaulis subsp. lebelianus

Rubus hebecaulis subsp.

lebelianus, commonly known as the Lebel's blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including rocky soils and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
