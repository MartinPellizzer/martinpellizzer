Rosa erubescens

Rosa erubescens, commonly known as the pink rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Kazakhstan, and Russia. The plant produces showy, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems typical of many rose species.

Rosa erubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
