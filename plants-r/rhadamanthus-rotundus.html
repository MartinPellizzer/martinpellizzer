Rhadamanthus rotundus

Rhadamanthus rotundus, commonly known as the round-headed rhadamanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive round head.

Rhadamanthus rotundus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
