Robinia caragana var. arborea

Robinia caragana var. arborea, commonly known as the tree of heaven, is a deciduous tree native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnate with 10 to 20 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by elongated, legume-like pods that contain seeds. It has a deep root system and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
