Racosperma brownii

Racosperma brownii, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow floral spikes, which appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant has compound leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Racosperma brownii is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
