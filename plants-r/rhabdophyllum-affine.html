Rhabdophyllum affine

Rhabdophyllum affine, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, erect stems that bear a single, elongated leaf. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it is notable for its intricate floral structure and specific pollination mechanisms.
