Rosa × aciculosa

Rosa × aciculosa, commonly known as the evergreen rose, is a hybrid species that combines traits from both shrub and climbing roses.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in partial shade to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden settings. Its evergreen nature allows it to maintain visual interest throughout the year, especially in colder climates.

Rosa × aciculosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to provide privacy or screening.
