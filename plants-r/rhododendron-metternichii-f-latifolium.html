Rhododendron metternichii f. latifolium

Rhododendron metternichii f. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf rhododendron, is a unique variant of the metternichii species.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, depending on the environmental conditions. It is a shrub that thrives in cool, moist climates with acidic soil.

This variety is often found in the mountainous regions of its native habitat, where it contributes to the rich biodiversity of the ecosystem.
