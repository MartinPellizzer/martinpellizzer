Rosa canina var. racemulosa

Rosa canina var. racemulosa, commonly known as the dog rose or wild rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, pinnate leaves, and clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along coastal areas.

Rosa canina var. racemulosa is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in herbal remedies.
