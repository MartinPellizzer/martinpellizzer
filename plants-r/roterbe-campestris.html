Roterbe campestris

Roterbe campestris, commonly known as the field gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slightly serrated edge.

Roterbe campestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
