Rubus × orbatus

Rubus × orbatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible, though they are often tart and less commonly consumed than other blackberry varieties. Its leaves are serrated and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are covered in persistent, woody spines.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
