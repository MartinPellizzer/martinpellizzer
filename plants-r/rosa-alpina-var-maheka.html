Rosa alpina var. maheka

Rosa alpina var. maheka, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a hairy underside, and its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters.

It produces oval, red-brown fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to withstand harsh mountain climates.
