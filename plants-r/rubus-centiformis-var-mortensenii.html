Rubus centiformis var. mortensenii

Rubus centiformis var. mortensenii, commonly known as the Mortensen's blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry species native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
