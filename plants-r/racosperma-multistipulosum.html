Racosperma multistipulosum

Racosperma multistipulosum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with multiple pairs of small, narrow leaflets. The plant produces yellow or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are often accompanied by small, stipulate structures.

It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.
