Rubus burgalensis

Rubus burgalensis, commonly known as the Burgalense blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Europe.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple achenes. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in woodland edges and shrublands. Its flowers are white with five petals and are visited by various pollinators.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them suitable for culinary use.
