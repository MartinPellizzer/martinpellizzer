Ranunculus lanceolatus

Ranunculus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are alternate and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
