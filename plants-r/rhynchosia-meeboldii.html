Rhynchosia meeboldii

Rhynchosia meeboldii, commonly known as the meebold's cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in racemes. The plant bears pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical, edible seeds. It is adapted to thrive in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Rhynchosia meeboldii is valued for its nutritional seeds and its role in soil nitrogen fixation.
