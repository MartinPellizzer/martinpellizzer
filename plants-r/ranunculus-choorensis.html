Ranunculus choorensis

Ranunculus choorensis, commonly known as the Armenian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, hairy fruits containing seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
