Rhaphidophora gigantea

Rhaphidophora gigantea, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense foliage.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.
