Rubus leucanthus var. villosulus

Rubus leucanthus var. villosulus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically alternate and have serrated edges, while its stems are covered in fine, whitish hairs.

This variety is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
