Rollandia lanceolata var. tomentella

Rollandia lanceolata var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly lanceleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves covered in a dense, woolly pubescence. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of tropical flora.
