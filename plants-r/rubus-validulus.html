Rubus validulus

Rubus validulus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color, often used in local foraging. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The botanical characteristics of Rubus validulus make it an important part of the ecosystem, providing food for various wildlife species.
