Rubus incisus subvar. euinsisus

Rubus incisus subvar. euinsisus, commonly known as the European bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by red, hairy berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and role in supporting local wildlife.
