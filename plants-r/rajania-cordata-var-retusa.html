Rajania cordata var. retusa

Rajania cordata var. retusa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features dark green, elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin, and its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive hooded structure. The plant produces small, clustered berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
