Ruellia velutifolia

Ruellia velutifolia, commonly known as velvetleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ruellia velutifolia is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
