Rhamnus brandegeeana

Rhamnus brandegeeana, commonly known as the Mexican buckthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rhamnaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and woodlands.

Rhamnus brandegeeana is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
