Ranunculus × auricomoides

Ranunculus × auricomoides, commonly known as the golden shield, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, palmately divided yellow flowers with a distinctive golden center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a short, stout stem and basal leaves that are lobed and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, eye-catching blooms.
