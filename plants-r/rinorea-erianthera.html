Rinorea erianthera

Rinorea erianthera, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often pubescent, giving the plant a velvety texture. The flowers are typically small, clustered in racemes, and feature showy, five-lobed petals with a distinctive violet or purple coloration. The plant produces small, globose fruits that contain several seeds.

Rinorea erianthera is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
