Rosa broteroi

Rosa broteroi, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a climbing shrub that produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and is typically found in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Rosa broteroi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming habit.
