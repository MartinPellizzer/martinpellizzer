Rosa prilipkoana

Rosa prilipkoana, commonly known as the Philippine rose, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features fragrant, semi-double flowers that range in color from pink to white, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of 5 to 9 leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

The plant is adapted to grow in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas.
