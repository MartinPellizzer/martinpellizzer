Rhus alata

Rhus alata, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by red, fuzzy berries that attract wildlife. Rhus alata thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in dry, sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
