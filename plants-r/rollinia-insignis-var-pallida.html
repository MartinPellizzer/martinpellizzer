Rollinia insignis var. pallida

Rollinia insignis var. pallida, commonly known as the white-flowered rollinia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its showy, pale-yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically ovate and glossy, with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and have a sweet, acidic flavor.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
