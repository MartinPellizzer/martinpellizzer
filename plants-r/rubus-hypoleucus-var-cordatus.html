Rubus hypoleucus var. cordatus

Rubus hypoleucus var. cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically three to five lobes and have a heart-shaped appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple to black berries in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
