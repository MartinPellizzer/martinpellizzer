Rubus tomentosiformis

Rubus tomentosiformis, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and thorny stems, which provide a protective covering. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the Rubus genus.
