Rottboellia tonkinensis

Rottboellia tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese grass or Tonkin grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Rottboellia tonkinensis is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
