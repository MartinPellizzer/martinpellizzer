Rubus × lamproleucus

Rubus × lamproleucus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from various Rubus species.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny bushes and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique and versatile plant in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
