Rubus schleicheri microgene louettensis

Rubus schleicheri microgene louettensis, commonly known as the Louett's raspberry.

It is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique genetic variation and ecological role in its native habitat.
