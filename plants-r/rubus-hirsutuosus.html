Rubus hirsutuosus

Rubus hirsutuosus, commonly known as the hairy bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, a type of aggregate berry, is typically red or black when ripe.

Rubus hirsutuosus is native to parts of North America and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
