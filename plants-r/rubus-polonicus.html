Rubus polonicus

Rubus polonicus, commonly known as the Polish blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in jams and wines. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Rubus polonicus is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
