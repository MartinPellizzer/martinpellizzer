Rosa thunbergii

Rosa thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Rosa thunbergii is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of edible rose hips.
