Rhododendron opulentum

Rhododendron opulentum, commonly known as the purple laurel, is a species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils within coniferous forests. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from purple to pink.

Rhododendron opulentum is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
