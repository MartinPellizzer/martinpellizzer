Rubus adornatus subsp. chlorobelus

Rubus adornatus subsp.

chlorobelus, commonly known as the green-leafed raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of East Asia. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically a vibrant green.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the plant produces small, edible berries that are similar to those of other raspberries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more intense green foliage compared to other Rubus species.
