Rebutia wessneriana

Rebutia wessneriana, commonly known as the Wessner's Rebutia, is a species of flowering cactus native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and ribbed structure. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, with spiny areoles and a shallow root system for efficient water absorption.

Rebutia wessneriana is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
