Rosa multiflora

Rosa multiflora, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 9 leaflets, and its clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant produces red or orange hips, which are the fruit containing seeds. Rosa multiflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
