Rosa × bifera officinalis

Rosa × bifera officinalis, commonly known as the officinal rose, is a hybrid rose species valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their high essential oil content.

Rosa × bifera officinalis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
