Rhododendron roseum f. albidum

Rhododendron roseum f. albidum, commonly known as the white-flowered rhododendron, is a botanical variant of the species Rhododendron roseum.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical pink or red blooms of the parent species. This form is typically found in the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly in China and Korea. The plant is an evergreen shrub with leathery, elliptical leaves that provide dense foliage.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
