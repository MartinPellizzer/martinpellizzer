Rosa sicula subvar. maroccana

Rosa sicula subvar. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan rock rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and grayish-green, adapted to withstand arid conditions. This subvariety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptation to the harsh, sun-exposed environments of its native habitat.
