Ranunculus raddeanus subsp. subleiocarpus

Ranunculus raddeanus subsp.

subleiocarpus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features delicate, palmate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically yellow with five to eight sepals and numerous stamens. The plant grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family, often found in high-altitude environments.
