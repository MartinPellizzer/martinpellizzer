Rosa mollis f. apicatifrons

Rosa mollis f. apicatifrons, commonly known as the soft rose or woolly rose, is a flowering shrub native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained soil in a sunny location. Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and a compact, bushy growth habit.

This rose is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
