Rumex aureostigmatica

Rumex aureostigmatica, commonly known as the golden-stained sorrel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-green leaves with a striking orange or red marginal stripe. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

The leaves are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor, making it a potential candidate for use in local cuisines.
