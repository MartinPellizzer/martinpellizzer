Rubus weigmannii

Rubus weigmannii, commonly known as the Weigmann's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
