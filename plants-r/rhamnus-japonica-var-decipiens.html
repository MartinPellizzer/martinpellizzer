Rhamnus japonica var. decipiens

Rhamnus japonica var. decipiens, commonly known as the Japanese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red or black berries in the fall. It is often found in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in horticulture.
