Rhinotropis acanthoclada

Rhinotropis acanthoclada, commonly known as the spiny rock-rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Rhinotropis acanthoclada is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
