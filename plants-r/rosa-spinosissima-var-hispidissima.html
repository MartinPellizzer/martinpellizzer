Rosa spinosissima var. hispidissima

Rosa spinosissima var. hispidissima, commonly known as the hispid prickly rose, is a variant of the prickly rose species.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to white. The leaves are compound, with serrated edges and a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a hardy ornamental shrub.

Its botanical features make it both a resilient and visually appealing addition to gardens in temperate climates.
