Rubus corylifolius var. simulatus

Rubus corylifolius var. simulatus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a variant of the brambles native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. Its botanical features include thorny stems and a complex inflorescence structure.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
