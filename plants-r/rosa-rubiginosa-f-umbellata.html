Rosa rubiginosa f. umbellata

Rosa rubiginosa f. umbellata, commonly known as the dark red rose or the wild rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and dense, fragrant pink to dark red flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
