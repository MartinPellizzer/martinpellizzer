Rhynchosia falconeri

Rhynchosia falconeri, commonly known as the Falconeri's bean, is a climbing legume native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's pods are cylindrical, containing several seeds enclosed in a fibrous husk. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
