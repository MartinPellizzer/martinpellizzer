Rhodocalyx erectus

Rhodocalyx erectus, commonly known as the upright cluster flower.

It is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia, characterized by its distinctive clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of yellow to orange, are arranged in dense, upright clusters, giving it its common name.

Rhodocalyx erectus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
