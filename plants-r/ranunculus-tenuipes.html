Ranunculus tenuipes

Ranunculus tenuipes, commonly known as the slenderleaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are usually slender and divided, with a delicate, fern-like appearance.

Ranunculus tenuipes is known for its small size and subtle beauty, making it a notable species in its native habitat.
