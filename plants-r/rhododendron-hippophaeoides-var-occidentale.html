Rhododendron hippophaeoides var. occidentale

Rhododendron hippophaeoides var. occidentale, commonly known as the western yellow rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for woodland and coastal environments.
