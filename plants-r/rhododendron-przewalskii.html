Rhododendron przewalskii

Rhododendron przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia, particularly in China and Tibet.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, thriving in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from white to pink and purple.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
