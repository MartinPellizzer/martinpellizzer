Rubus insularis subsp. confinis

Rubus insularis subsp.

confinis, commonly known as the island raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its erect or sprawling growth habit and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, and it blooms with white to pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

This subspecies is notable for its horticultural value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
