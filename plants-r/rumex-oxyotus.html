Rumex oxyotus

Rumex oxyotus, commonly known as the sharpleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a sharp apex and a serrated margin. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense, terminal spikes.

Rumex oxyotus is often found in wetland ecosystems and is used in traditional medicine for its purported diuretic properties.
