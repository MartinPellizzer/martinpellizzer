Rubus pubescens var. paracaulis

Rubus pubescens var. paracaulis, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its botanical features include hairy, glandular leaves and flowers with five pink to white petals.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
