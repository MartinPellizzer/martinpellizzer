Rhynchospora alba var. fusca

Rhynchospora alba var. fusca, commonly known as the blackroot sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, fibrous rhizomes and narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in moist environments.

Rhynchospora alba var. fusca plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
