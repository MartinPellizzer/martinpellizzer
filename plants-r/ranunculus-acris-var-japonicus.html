Ranunculus acris var. japonicus

Ranunculus acris var. japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with 5-15 petals, typically in shades of yellow, white, or orange. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves and grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and early spring bloom.
