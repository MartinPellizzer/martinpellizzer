Ruellia stelligera

Ruellia stelligera, commonly known as the coastal ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming during the warmer months. Ruellia stelligera is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
