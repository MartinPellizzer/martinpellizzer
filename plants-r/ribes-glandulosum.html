Ribes glandulosum

Ribes glandulosum, commonly known as the sticky currant, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its glandular-stemmed structure and small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters, and it produces edible berries that are often used in jams and jellies.

Ribes glandulosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
