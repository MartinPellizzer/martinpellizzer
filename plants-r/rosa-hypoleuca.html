Rosa hypoleuca

Rosa hypoleuca, commonly known as the white wild rose, is a species native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a pinkish-red hip that contains seeds, and the plant is often found in dry, open habitats.

Rosa hypoleuca is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
