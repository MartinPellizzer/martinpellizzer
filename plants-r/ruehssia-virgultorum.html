Ruehssia virgultorum

Ruehssia virgultorum, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
