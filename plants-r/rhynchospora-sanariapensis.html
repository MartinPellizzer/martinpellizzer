Rhynchospora sanariapensis

Rhynchospora sanariapensis, commonly known as the Sanaria marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in marshy or aquatic environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem.

Rhynchospora sanariapensis plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
