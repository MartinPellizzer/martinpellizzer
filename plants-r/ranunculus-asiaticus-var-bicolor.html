Ranunculus asiaticus var. bicolor

Ranunculus asiaticus var. bicolor, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, double-layered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, with a distinctive red or orange center. The plant typically grows to a height of about 12 to 18 inches and features finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden beds and containers.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is known for its short blooming period in early spring.
