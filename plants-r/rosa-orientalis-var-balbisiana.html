Rosa orientalis var. balbisiana

Rosa orientalis var. balbisiana, commonly known as the Balbisiana rose, is a hybrid species originating from the cross between Rosa orientalis and Rosa damascena.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in perfumery and horticulture.
