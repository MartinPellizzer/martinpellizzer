Rosa caryophyllacea f. zalana

Rosa caryophyllacea f. zalana, commonly known as the Zalana rose, is a unique form of the Rosa caryophyllacea species, native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse flowering, with blooms that range from white to pale pink. The foliage is typically dense and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the delicate flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
