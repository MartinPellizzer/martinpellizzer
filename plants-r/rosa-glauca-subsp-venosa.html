Rosa glauca subsp. venosa

Rosa glauca subsp.

venosa, commonly known as the blue rose or glaucous rose, is a subspecies of the shrub rose native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its pale blue or bluish-white flowers, which are often tinged with a faint pink or lavender hue.

The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy underside, and its stems are covered in thorns. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral coloration.
