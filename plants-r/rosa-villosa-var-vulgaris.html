Rosa villosa var. vulgaris

Rosa villosa var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common bristly rose, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
