Rubus spitzneri

Rubus spitzneri, commonly known as Spitzner's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many bramble species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It is native to South America, particularly in regions of Argentina and Chile.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
