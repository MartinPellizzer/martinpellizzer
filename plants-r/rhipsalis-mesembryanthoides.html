Rhipsalis mesembryanthoides

Rhipsalis mesembryanthoides, commonly known as the silver star cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that are covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is a succulent plant, adapted to arid environments, with the ability to store water in its stems.

The leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.
