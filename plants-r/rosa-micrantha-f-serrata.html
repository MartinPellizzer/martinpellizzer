Rosa micrantha f. serrata

Rosa micrantha f. serrata, commonly known as the serrated form of the small-flowered rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins.

This plant is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are typically pink to white, with five petals and a subtle fragrance, blooming in early summer. The shrub produces small, red hips that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for small wildlife.
