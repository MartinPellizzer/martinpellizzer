Restio fasciculatus

Restio fasciculatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale yellow flowers. It typically grows in coastal and sandy habitats, often forming dense stands in dune environments.

Restio fasciculatus is adapted to withstand harsh conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
