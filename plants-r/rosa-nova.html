Rosa nova

Rosa nova, commonly known as the New Rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white petals and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. The plant produces showy, single or double blooms throughout the growing season. Rosa nova thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and valuable for ecological purposes.
