Rubus rhombifolioides

Rubus rhombifolioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a distinctive rhomboid shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The species is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
