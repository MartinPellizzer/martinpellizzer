Rosa laxa

Rosa laxa, commonly known as the loose rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

Rosa laxa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
