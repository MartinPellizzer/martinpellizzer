Rhaphidophora glauca var. khasiana

Rhaphidophora glauca var. khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often marked with white veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, starchy tubers that are used in local cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and culinary purposes in its native habitat.
