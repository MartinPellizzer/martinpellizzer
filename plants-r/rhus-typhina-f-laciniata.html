Rhus typhina f. laciniata

Rhus typhina f. laciniata, commonly known as the dissected sumac, is a variant of the staghorn sumac characterized by its finely cut, feathery leaves.

This plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye. The plant produces small, red berries that are a food source for birds and wildlife.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to grow in disturbed areas.
