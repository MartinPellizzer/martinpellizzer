Rhododendron hirsutum f. latifolium

Rhododendron hirsutum f. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf rhododendron, is a hybrid form of the broad-leafed rhododendron.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, elliptical leaves that are often darker in color compared to the parent species. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and landscapes.
