Rubus × chlorostylus

Rubus × chlorostylus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus glaucus, resulting in a species with unique botanical characteristics. It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal regions, featuring dark green, leathery leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are often used in culinary applications.

Its adaptability to saline soils makes it a notable species in coastal botany.
