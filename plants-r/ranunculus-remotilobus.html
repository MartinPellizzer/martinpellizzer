Ranunculus remotilobus

Ranunculus remotilobus, commonly known as the remote lobed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with lobes that are usually narrow and pointed.

The flowers are surrounded by five to eight sepals that resemble petals, a feature common in the genus Ranunculus.
