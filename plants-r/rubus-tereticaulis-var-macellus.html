Rubus tereticaulis var. macellus

Rubus tereticaulis var. macellus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features trailing stems with thorns and compound leaves composed of three to five elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. Its flowers are white and have five petals, typical of the Rubus genus.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
