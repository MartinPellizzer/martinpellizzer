Rosa canina var. purpurascens

Rosa canina var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leaf dogwood rose, is a variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage and pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, red, edible fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
