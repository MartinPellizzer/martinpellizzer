Rubus innominatus var. innominatus

Rubus innominatus var. innominatus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems that provide structural support.

This plant is valued for its edible fruits and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.
