Ranunculus ficaria subsp. calthifolius

Ranunculus ficaria subsp.

calthifolius, commonly known as the yellow marsh crowfoot, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in wet or damp environments such as marshes, stream banks, and meadows.

The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic sap.

This subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact foliage compared to other members of the species.
