Restio glumaceus

Restio glumaceus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal or sandy environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale blue flowers. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.

Restio glumaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
