Rubus corchorifolius var. althaeoides

Rubus corchorifolius var. althaeoides, commonly known as the althaeoid raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically three to five leaflets, and its prickly stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, similar to other raspberry species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
