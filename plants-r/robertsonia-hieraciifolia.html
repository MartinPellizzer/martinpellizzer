Robertsonia hieraciifolia

Robertsonia hieraciifolia, commonly known as the fern-leaf Robertsonia, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage with finely divided, bright green leaves that give it a lacy appearance. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant for botanists and gardeners alike.
