Rubus bifrons var. appressus

Rubus bifrons var. appressus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a low-growing shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically eaten raw or used in jams and wines. The plant has a trailing or prostrate growth habit, with thin, wiry stems that root at the nodes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three ovate to elliptic leaflets, and are covered in fine, short hairs.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they appear in clusters during the spring.
