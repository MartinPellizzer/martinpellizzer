Ranunculus chrysoleptos

Ranunculus chrysoleptos, commonly known as the golden leaf buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
