Rubus × psilocladus

Rubus × psilocladus, commonly known as the psilocladus raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, wooded areas.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
