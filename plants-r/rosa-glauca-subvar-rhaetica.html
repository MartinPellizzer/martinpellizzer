Rosa glauca subvar. rhaetica

Rosa glauca subvar. rhaetica, commonly known as the Rhaetian blue rose, is a subspecies native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking pale blue to lavender petals, which are often tinged with white. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, thriving in cool, moist climates. Its foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, and it produces fragrant flowers in early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
