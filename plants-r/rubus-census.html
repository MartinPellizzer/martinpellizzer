Rubus census

Rubus census, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Rubus census is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local wildlife.
