Rubus × viridiformis

Rubus × viridiformis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are evergreen, with a glossy, dark green appearance, and they remain on the plant throughout the year.

This species is native to regions of eastern Asia and is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruits.
