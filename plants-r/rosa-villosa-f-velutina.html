Rosa villosa f. velutina

Rosa villosa f. velutina, commonly known as the velvet rose, is a cultivar of the species Rosa villosa, characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and abundant, fragrant flowers.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, typically growing in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features thorny stems and broad, serrated leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five to seven petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
