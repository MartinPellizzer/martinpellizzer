Rosa villosa f. exadenophylla

Rosa villosa f. exadenophylla, commonly known as the hairy rose or the soft-hairy rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa villosa.

It is characterized by its dense, soft, white or grayish pubescence covering the stems and leaves. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red. It is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
