Ruta tenuifolia

Ruta tenuifolia, commonly known as common rue, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, pinnately divided leaves and small yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves contain volatile oils that contribute to its strong, pungent aroma.

Ruta tenuifolia is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
