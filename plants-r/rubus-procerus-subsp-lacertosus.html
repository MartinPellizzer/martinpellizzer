Rubus procerus subsp. lacertosus

Rubus procerus subsp.

lacertosus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
