Rubus vulgaris subsp. mollis

Rubus vulgaris subsp.

mollis, commonly known as the soft-fruited blackberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant produces aggregate fruits that are dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The subspecies is noted for its softer, less hairy fruits compared to other blackberry varieties.
