Racosperma pubicostum

Racosperma pubicostum, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in inflorescences, attracting various pollinators.

Racosperma pubicostum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought conditions.
