Rhodanthe maculata alba

Rhodanthe maculata alba, commonly known as the white spotted everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
