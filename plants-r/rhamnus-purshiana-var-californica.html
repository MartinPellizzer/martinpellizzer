Rhamnus purshiana var. californica

Rhamnus purshiana var. californica, commonly known as California buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by black, oval berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought conditions and partial shade.
