Rytidosperma unarede

Rytidosperma unarede, commonly known as the narrowleaf spinifex, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, pale-green spikelets that are clustered at the ends of its stems.

Rytidosperma unarede is well adapted to withstand drought and is often found in coastal dunes and inland grasslands.
