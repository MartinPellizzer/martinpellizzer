Rubus niveus var. rhodophyllos

Rubus niveus var. rhodophyllos, commonly known as the red-leaf evergreen raspberry, is a shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are elliptical, with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a pale, often reddish underside. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen to a deep red or black color.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
