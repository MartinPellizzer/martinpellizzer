Rubus moluccanus var. sundaicus

Rubus moluccanus var. sundaicus, commonly known as the Sunda raspberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
