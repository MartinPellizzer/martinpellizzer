Rosa cinnamomea var. lancifolia

Rosa cinnamomea var. lancifolia, commonly known as the cinnamon rose, is a species native to the Himalayas and parts of northern India.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features slender, thornless stems and lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and perfumery due to their aromatic properties.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
