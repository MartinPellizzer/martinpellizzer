Ranunculus × lechnerianus

Ranunculus × lechnerianus, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Ranunculus ficaria and Ranunculus bulbosus, characterized by its vibrant yellow or orange petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in gardens and wild areas where it adds ornamental value.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations.
