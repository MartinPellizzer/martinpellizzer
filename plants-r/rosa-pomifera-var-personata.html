Rosa pomifera var. personata

Rosa pomifera var. personata, commonly known as the Japanese rose or the rose of Sharon, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, single or double flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces distinctive, apple-like fruit called a "pomme," which is edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
