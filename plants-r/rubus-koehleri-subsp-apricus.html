Rubus koehleri subsp. apricus

Rubus koehleri subsp.

apricus, commonly known as the apricot raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color, resembling apricots in appearance.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and can be processed into jams or preserves.
