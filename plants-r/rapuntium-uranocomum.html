Rapuntium uranocomum

Rapuntium uranocomum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. Its root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes that store nutrients and aid in survival during harsh conditions.

The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
