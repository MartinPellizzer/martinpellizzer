Rubus cuspidiferus microgene vulnificus

Rubus cuspidiferus microgene vulnificus, commonly known as the blackberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers that develop into edible berries. Its botanical structure supports a variety of wildlife, including birds and insects, and it plays a role in soil stabilization.

The species is also valued for its ornamental and agricultural significance.
