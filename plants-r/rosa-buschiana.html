Rosa buschiana

Rosa buschiana, commonly known as the Busch rose, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double pink or white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include pinnate leaves with 5 to 7 leaflets and an upright growth habit.

Rosa buschiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
