Rhodomyrtus baladensis

Rhodomyrtus baladensis, commonly known as the Baladensis myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, berry-like structure that turns red when mature, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's essential oils are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
