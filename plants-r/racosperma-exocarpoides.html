Racosperma exocarpoides

Racosperma exocarpoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has bipinnate leaves, meaning they are divided into pairs of smaller leaflets. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

The fruit is a legume, which splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.
