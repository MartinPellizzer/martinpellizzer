Rosa gallica f. subtomentella

Rosa gallica f. subtomentella, commonly known as the white gallica rose, is a botanical variety of the Gallica rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It is native to Europe and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
