Rhipidocladum bartlettii

Rhipidocladum bartlettii, commonly known as the Bartlett's carpetweed, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It features small, fleshy, ovate leaves that are typically green or reddish in color, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its resilience and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying drought-adapted flora.
