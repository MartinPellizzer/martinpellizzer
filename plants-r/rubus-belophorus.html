Rubus belophorus

Rubus belophorus, commonly known as the highland blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates, often growing in forested or rocky habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
