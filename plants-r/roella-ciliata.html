Roella ciliata

Roella ciliata, commonly known as the silky roella, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and inland regions, where it adapts well to arid conditions.

Roella ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
