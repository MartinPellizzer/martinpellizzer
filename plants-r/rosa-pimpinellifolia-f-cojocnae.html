Rosa pimpinellifolia f. cojocnae

Rosa pimpinellifolia f. cojocnae, commonly known as the Cojocna rose, is a botanical variant of the shrub rose native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and it produces rosehips that are rich in vitamin C. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
