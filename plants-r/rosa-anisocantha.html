Rosa anisocantha

Rosa anisocantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly in countries like Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Rosa anisocantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
