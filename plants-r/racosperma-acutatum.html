Racosperma acutatum

Racosperma acutatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, fern-like leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant is typically found in sandy or rocky soils in arid and semi-arid regions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in revegetation projects.

Racosperma acutatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
