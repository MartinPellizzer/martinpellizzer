Rafflesia lobata

Rafflesia lobata, commonly known as the giant lobster orchid, is a parasitic flowering plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is renowned for producing the world's largest individual flower, which can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant lacks leaves, stems, and roots, relying entirely on its host vine for nutrients. Its flowers emit a strong, unpleasant odor resembling decaying meat, which attracts flies and other insects for pollination.

The plant's unique structure and life cycle make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
