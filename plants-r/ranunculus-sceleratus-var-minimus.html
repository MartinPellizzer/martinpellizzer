Ranunculus sceleratus var. minimus

Ranunculus sceleratus var. minimus, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of soft, silky hairs. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, often appearing in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
