Rubus plicatus var. interfoliatus

Rubus plicatus var. interfoliatus, commonly known as the hooked blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its unique foliage and fruiting habits.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas and along stream banks. The leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are serrated and have a distinctive hooked appearance. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a dark purple to black berry that ripens in late summer.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in some traditional herbal remedies.
