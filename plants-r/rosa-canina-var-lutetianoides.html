Rosa canina var. lutetianoides

Rosa canina var. lutetianoides, commonly known as the French rose or the Lutetian rose, is a variant of the dog rose.

It is a shrub native to Europe, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces red, hairy fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
