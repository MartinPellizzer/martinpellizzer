Racosperma paniculatum

Racosperma paniculatum, commonly known as the slender senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow floral clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Racosperma paniculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
