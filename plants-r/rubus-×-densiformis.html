Rubus × densiformis

Rubus × densiformis, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically serrated. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
