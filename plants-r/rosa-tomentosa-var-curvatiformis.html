Rosa tomentosa var. curvatiformis

Rosa tomentosa var. curvatiformis, commonly known as the curlyleaf rose, is a shrub-like plant native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and thorny stems, which provide structural support and protection. The leaves are typically arranged in a spiral pattern and have a distinctive curling shape, giving the plant its common name. It produces fragrant pink or white flowers in late spring to early summer.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
