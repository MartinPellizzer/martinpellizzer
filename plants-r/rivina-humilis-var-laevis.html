Rivina humilis var. laevis

Rivina humilis var. laevis, commonly known as smooth rivina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of 1 to 2 feet, featuring slender, branching stems covered in smooth, hairless bark. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a glossy, dark green appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in partial to full sun and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
