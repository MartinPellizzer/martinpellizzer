Racosperma hystrix

Racosperma hystrix, commonly known as the spiked racosperma, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its unique, spiny stipules that give it its common name. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Racosperma hystrix is often found in coastal and inland regions of Western Australia.
