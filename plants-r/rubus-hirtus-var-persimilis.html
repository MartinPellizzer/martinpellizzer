Rubus hirtus var. persimilis

Rubus hirtus var. persimilis, commonly known as the hairy blackberry or a variant of the common blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. The plant produces flowers with five pink to white petals, typically blooming in the spring. Its botanical features include a woody stem, simple or lobed leaves, and a fruit structure known as a drupe.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
