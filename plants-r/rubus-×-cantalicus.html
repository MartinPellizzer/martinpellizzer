Rubus × cantalicus

Rubus × cantalicus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which develop from multiple ovaria. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical structure includes compound leaves with serrated margins and a complex reproductive system involving both sexual and asexual propagation.
