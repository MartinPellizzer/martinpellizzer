Rosa × hibernica var. typica

Rosa × hibernica var. typica, commonly known as the Hibernian rose, is a hybrid species native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy blooms that attract pollinators. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the stems are often covered in thorns.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
