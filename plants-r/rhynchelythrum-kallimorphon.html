Rhynchelythrum kallimorphon

Rhynchelythrum kallimorphon, commonly known as the Greek thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple or pink flowering heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rhynchelythrum kallimorphon is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
