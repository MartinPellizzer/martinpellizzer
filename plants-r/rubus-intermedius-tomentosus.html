Rubus intermedius tomentosus

Rubus intermedius tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, white-tomentose (fuzzy) foliage and fruiting stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its growth habit includes thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian areas.
