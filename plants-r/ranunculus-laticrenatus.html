Ranunculus laticrenatus

Ranunculus laticrenatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically arranged in a cup-like structure. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
