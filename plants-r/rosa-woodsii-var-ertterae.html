Rosa woodsii var. ertterae

Rosa woodsii var. ertterae, commonly known as the Ertter's rock rose, is a variant of the rock rose species native to the western United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, clustered flowers. The foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, providing a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant is well adapted to harsh, arid conditions and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.
