Ranunculus auricomus subsp. patens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

patens, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in botanical gardens and wildflower habitats.
