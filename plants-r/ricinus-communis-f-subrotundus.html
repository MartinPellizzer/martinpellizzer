Ricinus communis f. subrotundus

Ricinus communis f. subrotundus, commonly known as the African horse chestnut, is a variant of the castor oil plant.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are often a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant produces distinctive spiny capsules containing toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of castor oil. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Euphorbiaceae family.
