Rhynchanthera pentanthera

Rhynchanthera pentanthera, commonly known as the five-threaded orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elegant, pendulous inflorescence bearing small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insect species, which play a crucial role in its reproductive cycle.

The orchid's unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important subject in botanical studies.
