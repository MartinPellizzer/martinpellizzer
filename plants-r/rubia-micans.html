Rubia micans

Rubia micans, commonly known as the silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray leaves that reflect light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Rubia micans thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has historical significance in herbal remedies.
