Reinhardtia gracilis

Reinhardtia gracilis, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a smooth, green stem covered in a layer of sori, which are clusters of spore-producing structures. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Reinhardtia gracilis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
