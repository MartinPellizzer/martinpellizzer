Rubus longipetiolatus

Rubus longipetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled blackberry, is a species of shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with elongated petioles. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
