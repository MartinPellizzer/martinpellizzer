Rubus betulifolius

Rubus betulifolius, commonly known as the birchleaf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble those of the birch tree, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
