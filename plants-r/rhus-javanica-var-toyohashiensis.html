Rhus javanica var. toyohashiensis

Rhus javanica var. toyohashiensis, commonly known as Japanese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 9 to 15 leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by red, fuzzy berries that are a distinctive feature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
