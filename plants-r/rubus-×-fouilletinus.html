Rubus × fouilletinus

Rubus × fouilletinus, commonly known as the blackberry-loganberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus loganobaccus (loganberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, featuring thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are pink to white, and it produces edible berries that are a cross between the two parent species.

This hybrid is valued for its fruit quality and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
