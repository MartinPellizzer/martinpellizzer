Rubus occultiglans

Rubus occultiglans, commonly known as the shadowed raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black foliage and small, edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest understories. Its stems are woody and covered in stiff, curved prickles, typical of the Rubus genus.

The flowers are pale yellow with five petals, and the plant is known for its ornamental value as well as its culinary uses.
