Ranunculus bovei

Ranunculus bovei, commonly known as the Bove's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, brightly colored flowers that typically range from yellow to orange. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive hairy texture and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, and its roots are tuberous, allowing it to survive in harsh conditions.

Ranunculus bovei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
