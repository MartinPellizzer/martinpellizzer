Ranunculus repens f. declinatus

Ranunculus repens f. declinatus, commonly known as the prostrate buttercup, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its trailing stems and bright yellow flowers.

It typically forms a dense mat of foliage, with heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are small and consist of five petals, with a central cluster of stamens. This variety is adapted to moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas or along riverbanks.

It is a hardy plant that thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
