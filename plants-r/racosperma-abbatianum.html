Racosperma abbatianum

Racosperma abbatianum, commonly known as the Ashanti tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in moist, lowland forests.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in its habitat.
