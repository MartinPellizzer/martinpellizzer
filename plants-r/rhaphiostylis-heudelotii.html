Rhaphiostylis heudelotii

Rhaphiostylis heudelotii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and it produces showy, colorful inflorescences.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
