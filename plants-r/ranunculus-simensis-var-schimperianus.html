Ranunculus simensis var. schimperianus

Ranunculus simensis var. schimperianus, commonly known as the Schimper's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with a serrated edge, and its stems are usually hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
