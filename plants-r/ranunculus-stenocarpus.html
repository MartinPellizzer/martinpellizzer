Ranunculus stenocarpus

Ranunculus stenocarpus, commonly known as the narrow-fruit buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, rough texture. The plant has a short, upright stem and basal leaves that are palmately lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
