Rubus thunbergii var. glabellus

Rubus thunbergii var. glabellus, commonly known as the glabrous bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The flowers are typically white or pink, with five petals, and appear in clusters. The fruits are small, aggregate berries that ripen to a red or black color.

This plant is native to East Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.
