Rubus calvescens

Rubus calvescens, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it is often found in association with other shrubs in temperate regions.
