Rinorea helicterifolia

Rinorea helicterifolia, commonly known as the silky leaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and lanceolate, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Rinorea helicterifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
