Roella paniculata

Roella paniculata, commonly known as the blue bottle plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that are borne in paniculate inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Roella paniculata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
