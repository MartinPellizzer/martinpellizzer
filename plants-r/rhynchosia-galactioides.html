Rhynchosia galactioides

Rhynchosia galactioides, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Rhynchosia galactioides is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
