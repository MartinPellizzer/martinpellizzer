Ribesiodes longifolium

Ribesiodes longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved ribes, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive choice for both ornamental and ecological landscaping.
