Rubus mexicanus

Rubus mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, typically growing in moist, shaded areas. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its stems are covered in dense, prickly bracts, which serve as a natural defense mechanism.

The botanical features of Rubus mexicanus make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
