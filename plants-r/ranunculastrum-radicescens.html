Ranunculastrum radicescens

Ranunculastrum radicescens, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a low-growing, rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread aggressively along the ground. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

Ranunculastrum radicescens is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
