Rosa actinophlaea

Rosa actinophlaea, commonly known as the starry rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly found in the Appalachian region. The plant is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are typically red or orange.

The plant thrives in woodland habitats and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
