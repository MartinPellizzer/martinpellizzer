Rosa osmodendron

Rosa osmodendron, commonly known as the sweet shrub or sweet tea plant, is a flowering shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a rough texture. Its stems are woody and covered in fine, silvery hairs.

Rosa osmodendron is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its pleasant, tea-like scent.
