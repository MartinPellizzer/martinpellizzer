Ricinus communis var. inermis

Ricinus communis var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth castor oil plant, is a variant of the castor oil plant characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and stems.

It is native to tropical regions and is cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its seeds, which produce castor oil. The plant features large, palmate leaves with a velvety texture and can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

The flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers on separate plants, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
