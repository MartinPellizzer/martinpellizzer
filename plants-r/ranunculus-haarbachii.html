Ranunculus haarbachii

Ranunculus haarbachii, commonly known as the Haarbach buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.

It is often found in meadows and open slopes at high elevations.
