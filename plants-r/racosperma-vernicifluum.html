Racosperma vernicifluum

Racosperma vernicifluum, commonly known as the gum tree, is a species of legume native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and can grow as a tree or shrub. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Racosperma vernicifluum is known for its ability to produce a sticky resin, which has been historically used by Indigenous Australians.
