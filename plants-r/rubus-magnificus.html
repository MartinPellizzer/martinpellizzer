Rubus magnificus

Rubus magnificus, commonly known as the splendid blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like roses and apples. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit, a deep purple-black berry, ripens in late summer and is highly prized for its flavor and nutritional value.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and edible gardens.
