Rubus schleicheri var. subvulsus

Rubus schleicheri var. subvulsus, commonly known as the Schleicher's blackberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Vietnam.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
