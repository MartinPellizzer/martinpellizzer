Rosa pomifera subsp. glandulosa

Rosa pomifera subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with glandular dots on the underside.

The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its fruit, known as a rose hip, is a distinctive red, hairy capsule that contains seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
