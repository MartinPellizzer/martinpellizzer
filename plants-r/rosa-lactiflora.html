Rosa lactiflora

Rosa lactiflora, commonly known as the milk thorn, is a species of flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces a milky sap, which gives it its common name and contributes to its unique botanical properties. Rosa lactiflora is a shrub with thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
