Rhododendron × transiens

Rhododendron × transiens, commonly known as the hybrid rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics.

It typically features dense, leathery foliage with deep green to reddish hues, depending on the variety. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to form thickets, providing both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
