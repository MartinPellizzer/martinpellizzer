Rhynchospora tenuis var. prorepens

Rhynchospora tenuis var. prorepens, commonly known as the slender tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It typically grows in dense clusters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are usually greenish or reddish in color. It is characterized by its short, rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in moist, poorly drained soils.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
