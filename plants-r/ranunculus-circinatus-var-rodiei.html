Ranunculus circinatus var. rodiei

Ranunculus circinatus var. rodiei, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a central dark yellow or orange disc. The plant features palmately divided leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
