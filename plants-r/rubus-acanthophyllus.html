Rubus acanthophyllus

Rubus acanthophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and small, elliptical leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, which includes many other brambles and blackberries.
