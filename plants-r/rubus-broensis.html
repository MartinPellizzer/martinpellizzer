Rubus broensis

Rubus broensis, commonly known as the Broens rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. Native to the temperate regions of South America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
