Raphanus infestus

Raphanus infestus, commonly known as the hairy radish, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and mustard. The plant features a short, thick root and a cluster of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a peppery taste, similar to other members of its family.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
