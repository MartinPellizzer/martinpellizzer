Rosa communis var. pseudosenticosa

Rosa communis var. pseudosenticosa, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a shrub native to China and widely cultivated for its ornamental value.

It features fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring and summer, often in clusters. The plant has thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The variety is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
