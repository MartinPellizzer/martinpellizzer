Ranunculus ancorifolius

Ranunculus ancorifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight yellow petals and a hairy, yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically narrow and deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
