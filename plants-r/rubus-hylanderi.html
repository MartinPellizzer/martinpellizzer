Rubus hylanderi

Rubus hylanderi, commonly known as the Hyland's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, forested areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, prickly hairs.

The plant plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
