Rosa × macrantha

Rosa × macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 5 inches in diameter. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves composed of five to seven leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a rich fragrance and vibrant colors.
