Rosa afzeliana var. fritschii

Rosa afzeliana var. fritschii, commonly known as the Fritsch's rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage, with leaves composed of five to seven elliptical leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
