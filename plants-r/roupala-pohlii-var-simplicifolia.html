Roupala pohlii var. simplicifolia

Roupala pohlii var. simplicifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed roupala, is a deciduous shrub native to New Zealand.

It features simple, ovate leaves that are typically smooth and glossy, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
