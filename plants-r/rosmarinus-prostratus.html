Rosmarinus prostratus

Rosmarinus prostratus, commonly known as prostrate rosemary, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Rosmarinus prostratus is valued for its fragrance and is often used in herbal remedies and culinary applications.
