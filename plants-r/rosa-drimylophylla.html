Rosa drimylophylla

Rosa drimylophylla, commonly known as the pink sandalwood rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants.

Rosa drimylophylla is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
