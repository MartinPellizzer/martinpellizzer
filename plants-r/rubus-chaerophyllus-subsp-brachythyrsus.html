Rubus chaerophyllus subsp. brachythyrsus

Rubus chaerophyllus subsp.

brachythyrsus, commonly known as the short-thorned blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

The plant typically grows in woodland edges, hedgerows, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries, which ripen in late summer, are edible and have a tart flavor.
