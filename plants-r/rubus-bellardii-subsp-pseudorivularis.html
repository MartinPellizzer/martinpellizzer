Rubus bellardii subsp. pseudorivularis

Rubus bellardii subsp.

pseudorivularis, commonly known as the Rivularis blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are often clustered together. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in dense, prickly bristles.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
