Rubus colemannii var. reichenbachii

Rubus colemannii var. reichenbachii, commonly known as the Reichenbach's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black when ripe, with a sweet to tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and edible fruits, though it can be challenging to cultivate due to its specific environmental requirements.
