Rubus gymnostachys subsp. eugymnostachys

Rubus gymnostachys subsp.

eugymnostachys, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its erect or shrub-like growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus.
