Radiola filiformis

Radiola filiformis, commonly known as slender radiola, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil. Its slender stems and small, daisy-like flowers make it a delicate and ornamental species.

Radiola filiformis is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to withstand coastal conditions such as salt spray and strong winds.
