Ratibida columnifera var. tubularis

Ratibida columnifera var. tubularis, commonly known as the yellow coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy yellow-orange flower heads that resemble a cone. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
