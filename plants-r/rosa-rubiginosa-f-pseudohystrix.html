Rosa rubiginosa f. pseudohystrix

Rosa rubiginosa f. pseudohystrix, commonly known as the red-stemmed rose, is a distinct form of the shrub rose characterized by its reddish-brown stems and dense, thorny growth.

It produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its foliage is typically dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
