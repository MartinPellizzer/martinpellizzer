Ribes multiflorum subsp. sandalioticum

Ribes multiflorum subsp.

sandalioticum, commonly known as the sandalwood currant, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces edible, red berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
