Raphanistrocarpus boivinii var. constrictus

Raphanistrocarpus boivinii var. constrictus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved turnip mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, often in rocky or sandy soils. It is native to parts of North America, particularly the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds.
