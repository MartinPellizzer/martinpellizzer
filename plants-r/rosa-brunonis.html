Rosa brunonis

Rosa brunonis, commonly known as the Brunon's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, specifically found in regions of Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Rosa brunonis typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
