Rosa parviflora var. angustifolia

Rosa parviflora var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The leaves are narrow and serrated, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
