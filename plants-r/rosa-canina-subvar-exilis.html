Rosa canina subvar. exilis

Rosa canina subvar. exilis, commonly known as the slender dog rose, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces red, hairy fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal remedies.
