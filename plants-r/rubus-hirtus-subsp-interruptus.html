Rubus hirtus subsp. interruptus

Rubus hirtus subsp.

interruptus, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open fields.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
