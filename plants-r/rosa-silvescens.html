Rosa silvescens

Rosa silvescens, commonly known as the wild rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to various regions in Europe and Asia, often found in woodlands and shrublands. The plant produces fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red-orange hips. Its leaves are alternate, with serrated edges, and the stems are armed with thorns.

Rosa silvescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
