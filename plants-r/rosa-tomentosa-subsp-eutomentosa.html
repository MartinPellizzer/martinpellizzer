Rosa tomentosa subsp. eutomentosa

Rosa tomentosa subsp.

eutomentosa, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage and fragrant pink to white flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has thorny stems. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets covered in fine, white hairs.

This subspecies is well adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
