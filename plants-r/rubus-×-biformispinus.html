Rubus × biformispinus

Rubus × biformispinus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in woodland edges or disturbed areas. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible, though some varieties may have a tart or sharp flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
