Raphanus raphanistrum var. sativus

Raphanus raphanistrum var. sativus, commonly known as the garden radish, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright stem, pinnately lobed leaves, and small yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces round, swollen roots that are typically harvested for their crisp, peppery flavor. It thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular crop in temperate regions.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth cycle, often maturing within 40 to 60 days from sowing.
