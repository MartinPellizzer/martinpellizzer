Rubus tectiflorus

Rubus tectiflorus, commonly known as the thimbleberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit, a red, juicy berry, is edible and often used in jams and desserts.

The thimbleberry thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
