Rosa pimpinellifolia var. altaica

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. altaica, commonly known as the Altai shrub rose, is a perennial deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, woody stems with thorny branches and dark green, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are followed by red-orange hips, which are edible and used in traditional medicine.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
