Rubus × semipallidus

Rubus × semipallidus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark berries that are edible.

This species is native to the western United States and thrives in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments.
