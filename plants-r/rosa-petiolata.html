Rosa petiolata

Rosa petiolata, commonly known as the climbing rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb using its tendrils. The plant produces fragrant, single or double flowers in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Rosa petiolata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
