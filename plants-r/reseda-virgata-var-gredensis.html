Reseda virgata var. gredensis

Reseda virgata var. gredensis, commonly known as the Gredens rockrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant has gray-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and drought-tolerant addition to Mediterranean-style landscapes.
