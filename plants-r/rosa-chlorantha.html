Rosa chlorantha

Rosa chlorantha, commonly known as the white shrub rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green foliage. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants.

Rosa chlorantha is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh alpine environments.
