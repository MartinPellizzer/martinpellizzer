Rubus hirtus proles erythradenes

Rubus hirtus proles erythradenes, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, which are often reddish in color, and its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color, typically in late summer. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in partial shade.

The species is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local wildlife.
