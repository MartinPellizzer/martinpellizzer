Rhamnus thymifolia

Rhamnus thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark, glossy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Rhamnus thymifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
