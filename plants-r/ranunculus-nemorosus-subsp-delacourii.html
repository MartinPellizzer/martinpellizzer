Ranunculus nemorosus subsp. delacourii

Ranunculus nemorosus subsp.

delacourii, commonly known as the Delacour's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Korea. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, green fruits that contain seeds.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
