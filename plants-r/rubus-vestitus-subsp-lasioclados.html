Rubus vestitus subsp. lasioclados

Rubus vestitus subsp.

lasioclados, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces edible red berries that are often used in jams and desserts. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

This species is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.
