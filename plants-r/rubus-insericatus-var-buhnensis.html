Rubus insericatus var. buhnensis

Rubus insericatus var. buhnensis, commonly known as the Buhnen's bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. The leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated margins and a hairy underside. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role within its native habitat.
