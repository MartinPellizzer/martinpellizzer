Rubus serpens proles elongatifolius

Rubus serpens proles elongatifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and elliptical, elongated leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of understory vegetation in its native habitat.
