Rosa canina var. brevipes

Rosa canina var. brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf dog rose, is a compact shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in pink or white hues. The plant produces bright red hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a source of edible berries.
