Rubus wittigianus

Rubus wittigianus, commonly known as the Wittigian blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that resemble blackberries. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark purple to black berries in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
