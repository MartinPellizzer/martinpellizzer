Rubus domingensis

Rubus domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican raspberry, is a species of shrub native to the Caribbean islands, particularly the Dominican Republic.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are usually white or pink.

The fruit, which ripens in the summer, is valued for its flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
