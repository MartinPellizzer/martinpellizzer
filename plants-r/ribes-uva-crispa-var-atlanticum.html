Ribes uva-crispa var. atlanticum

Ribes uva-crispa var. atlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic gooseberry, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of North America.

It produces small, yellow to orange berries that are typically tart and edible. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it blooms with clusters of yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in local cuisine.
