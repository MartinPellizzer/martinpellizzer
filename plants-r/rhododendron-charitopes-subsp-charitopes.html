Rhododendron charitopes subsp. charitopes

Rhododendron charitopes subsp.

charitopes, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to red.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
