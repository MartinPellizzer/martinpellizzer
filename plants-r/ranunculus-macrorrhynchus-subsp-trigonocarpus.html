Ranunculus macrorrhynchus subsp. trigonocarpus

Ranunculus macrorrhynchus subsp.

trigonocarpus, commonly known as the three-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to seven petals and a hairy, upright stem.

The leaves are palmately divided with three to five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and streambanks.

The plant's fruit is a three-angled, ribbed capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
