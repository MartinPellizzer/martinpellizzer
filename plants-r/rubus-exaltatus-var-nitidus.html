Rubus exaltatus var. nitidus

Rubus exaltatus var. nitidus, commonly known as the glossy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its smooth, shiny leaves and dark purple to black fruits.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of North America. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and produces small, aggregate fruits that are highly valued for their flavor and culinary use.

Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and flowers with five pink to white petals.
