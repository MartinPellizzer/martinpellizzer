Rubus ursinus subsp. vitifolius

Rubus ursinus subsp.

vitifolius, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to western North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils.

The subspecies is closely related to other Rubus species and is often found growing in clusters.
