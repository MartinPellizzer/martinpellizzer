Rhododendron warianum

Rhododendron warianum, commonly known as the Malaya rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight pubescent texture. Its flowers are usually pink or red, blooming in spring, and are clustered in inflorescences.

Rhododendron warianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
