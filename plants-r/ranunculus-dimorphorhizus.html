Ranunculus dimorphorhizus

Ranunculus dimorphorhizus, commonly known as the twin-rooted buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces yellow flowers with numerous stamens.

The species is noted for its distinctive two-rooted structure, which is a key feature in its common name.
