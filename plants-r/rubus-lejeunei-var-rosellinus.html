Rubus lejeunei var. rosellinus

Rubus lejeunei var. rosellinus, commonly known as the Roselline blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is a shrub that produces small, dark red to black berries, which are typically covered in a fine layer of white down. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool climate for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited commercial cultivation due to its specific growing requirements.
