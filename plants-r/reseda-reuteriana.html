Reseda reuteriana

Reseda reuteriana, commonly known as the yellow weed or false flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant features slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in loose inflorescences, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

Reseda reuteriana is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
