Rubus incisus

Rubus incisus, commonly known as the prickly rose or bramble rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red or black berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along roadsides.

Rubus incisus is both a wild plant and can be cultivated for ornamental purposes.
