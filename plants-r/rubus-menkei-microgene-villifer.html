Rubus menkei microgene villifer

Rubus menkei microgene villifer, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
