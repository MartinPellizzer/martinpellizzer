Rhododendron × peregrinum

Rhododendron × peregrinum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron campanulatum and Rhododendron molle.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained acidic soils. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and inflorescences that are often borne on long, flexible pedicels.

Rhododendron × peregrinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
