Rosa communis var. fragrans

Rosa communis var. fragrans, commonly known as the common garden rose, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep red. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, and it produces rose hips as fruit after flowering. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is native to various regions of Europe and Asia.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice in gardens and for commercial rose production.
