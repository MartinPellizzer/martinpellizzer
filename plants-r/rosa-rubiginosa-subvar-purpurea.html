Rosa rubiginosa subvar. purpurea

Rosa rubiginosa subvar. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-stemmed dog rose, is a shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown stems and clusters of pink to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, oval, red-orange fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
