Rosa lugdunensis

Rosa lugdunensis, commonly known as the French rose or the white musk rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, including parts of France, Italy, and the Iberian Peninsula. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are covered in soft, grayish-white hairs.

Rosa lugdunensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
