Rosa afzeliana f. proxima

Rosa afzeliana f. proxima, commonly known as the near relative of the Afghan rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa afzeliana.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to red in color. The plant is native to regions of Central Asia and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain. It has narrow, serrated leaves and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

This form is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
