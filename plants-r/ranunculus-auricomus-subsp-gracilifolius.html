Ranunculus auricomus subsp. gracilifolius

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

gracilifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and its finely divided, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, green fruits known as achenes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
