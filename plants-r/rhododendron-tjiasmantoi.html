Rhododendron tjiasmantoi

Rhododendron tjiasmantoi, commonly known as the Hainan rhododendron, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Hainan Island in China.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a dense canopy that supports a variety of epiphytic plants.

Rhododendron tjiasmantoi is adapted to high humidity and partial shade, making it a valuable component of its native ecosystem.
