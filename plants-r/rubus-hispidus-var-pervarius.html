Rubus hispidus var. pervarius

Rubus hispidus var. pervarius, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters, and are followed by red, hairy berries that ripen in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
