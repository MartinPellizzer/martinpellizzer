Racosperma longispicatum

Racosperma longispicatum, commonly known as the long-spined acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces seed pods that are woody and contain small, hard seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Racosperma longispicatum is adapted to withstand drought and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
