Rosa canina var. moravcensis

Rosa canina var. moravcensis, commonly known as the Moravian dog rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
