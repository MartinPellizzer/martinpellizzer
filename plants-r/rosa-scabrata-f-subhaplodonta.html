Rosa scabrata f. subhaplodonta

Rosa scabrata f. subhaplodonta, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa scabrata.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and foliage, distinguishing it from the typical form of the species. This form is typically found in regions with temperate climates, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals and a subtle fragrance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
