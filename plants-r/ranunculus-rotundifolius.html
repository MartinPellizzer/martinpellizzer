Ranunculus rotundifolius

Ranunculus rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are rounded and often hairy, with a distinctive yellow flower that has five petals and numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but requires well-drained soil and partial shade.
