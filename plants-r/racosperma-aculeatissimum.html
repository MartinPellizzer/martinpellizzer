Racosperma aculeatissimum

Racosperma aculeatissimum, commonly known as the prickly wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Racosperma aculeatissimum is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
