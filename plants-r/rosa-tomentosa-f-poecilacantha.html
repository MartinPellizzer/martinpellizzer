Rosa tomentosa f. poecilacantha

Rosa tomentosa f. poecilacantha, commonly known as the woolly rose or the spiny woolly rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa tomentosa species.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and thorny stems, which provide structural support and protection. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrubland environments.

Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental appearance and hardy nature, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
