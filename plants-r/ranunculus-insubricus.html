Ranunculus insubricus

Ranunculus insubricus, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers with five to eight petals and a hairy, glandular stem. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is often found in meadows and mountainous areas.

Ranunculus insubricus is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and medicinal properties in some species.
