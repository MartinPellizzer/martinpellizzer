Rubus × montaniformis

Rubus × montaniformis, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a hybrid plant species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically serrated and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or yellow in color and contain multiple seeds. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing at high elevations where temperatures are cool and conditions are harsh.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in horticultural practices.
