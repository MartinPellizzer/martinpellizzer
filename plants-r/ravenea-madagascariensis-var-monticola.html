Ravenea madagascariensis var. monticola

Ravenea madagascariensis var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain ravenea, is a striking plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, palmate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
