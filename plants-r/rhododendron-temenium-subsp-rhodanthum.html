Rhododendron temenium subsp. rhodanthum

Rhododendron temenium subsp.

rhodanthum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. It features dense, evergreen foliage with leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, often blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist climates with well-drained acidic soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
