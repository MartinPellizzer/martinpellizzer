Reseda leucantha

Reseda leucantha, commonly known as white reseda, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Reseda leucantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used historically in dye production.
