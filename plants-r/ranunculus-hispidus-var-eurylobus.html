Ranunculus hispidus var. eurylobus

Ranunculus hispidus var. eurylobus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals, which are typically arranged in a cup-like structure. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and garden cultivation.
