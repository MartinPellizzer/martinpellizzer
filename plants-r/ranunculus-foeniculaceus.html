Ranunculus foeniculaceus

Ranunculus foeniculaceus, commonly known as the fennel-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that resemble those of fennel, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
