Rosa sherardii var. suberecta

Rosa sherardii var. suberecta, commonly known as the upright shrub rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa sherardii species.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in areas of Kazakhstan and southern Siberia. This plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with dense, thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
