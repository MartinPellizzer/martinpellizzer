Ranunculus × peredae

Ranunculus × peredae, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ranunculus asiaticus and Ranunculus sceleratus, characterized by its showy, double-layered flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
