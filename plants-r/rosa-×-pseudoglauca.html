Rosa × pseudoglauca

Rosa × pseudoglauca, commonly known as the pink ountain rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its vibrant pink to white flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to fully double flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Botanically, it is known for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.
