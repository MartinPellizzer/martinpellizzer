Rubus albiflorus microgene flaccidulus

Rubus albiflorus microgene flaccidulus, commonly known as the white-flowered bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while the fruit is a small, aggregate berry.

This species is notable for its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions of North America.
