Ranunculus pinguis

Ranunculus pinguis, commonly known as the fat buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with 5 to 15 petals. The plant has a short, rhizomatous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a waxy coating.

Ranunculus pinguis is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.
