Rhipsalis cassytha var. mauritiana

Rhipsalis cassytha var. mauritiana, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a climbing epiphytic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that are often adorned with small, rounded spines. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the winter months. Its unique growth habit allows it to attach itself to trees or other structures in its natural habitat.

It requires indirect light, moderate watering, and well-draining soil to thrive in cultivation.
