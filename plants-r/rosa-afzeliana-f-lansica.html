Rosa afzeliana f. lansica

Rosa afzeliana f. lansica, commonly known as the broad-leafed rose, is a compact shrub native to China.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are broad and ovate, with serrated margins. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It has a bushy growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The stems are covered in fine, pale pink hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
