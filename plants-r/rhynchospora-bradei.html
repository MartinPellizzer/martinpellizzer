Rhynchospora bradei

Rhynchospora bradei, commonly known as Brade's spikegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features slender, grass-like stems and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green to brown in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil in wetland ecosystems.

Rhynchospora bradei is often found in the southeastern United States, particularly in Florida and parts of Georgia.
