Rhododendron rhodoleucum

Rhododendron rhodoleucum, commonly known as the white rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Rhododendron rhodoleucum is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
