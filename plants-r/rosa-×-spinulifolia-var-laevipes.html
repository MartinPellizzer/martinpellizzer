Rosa × spinulifolia var. laevipes

Rosa × spinulifolia var. laevipes, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed hybrid rose, is a cultivated variety of rose characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thornless stems.

It produces fragrant, semi-double to fully double flowers in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white, typically in late spring to early summer. The plant has broad, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface. It is relatively disease-resistant and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in garden landscapes for its ornamental value and ease of maintenance.
