Rhynchospora macrochaeta var. triceps

Rhynchospora macrochaeta var. triceps, commonly known as the three-ridge spikegrass, is a perennial herb native to wetlands and marshy areas of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and compact inflorescences. The plant produces small, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
