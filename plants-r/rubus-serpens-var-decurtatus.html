Rubus serpens var. decurtatus

Rubus serpens var. decurtatus, commonly known as the short-stalked blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
