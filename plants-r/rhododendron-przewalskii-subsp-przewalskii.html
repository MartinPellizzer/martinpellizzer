Rhododendron przewalskii subsp. przewalskii

Rhododendron przewalskii subsp.

przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia, particularly in China and Tibet. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
