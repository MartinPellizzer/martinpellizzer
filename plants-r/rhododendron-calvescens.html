Rhododendron calvescens

Rhododendron calvescens, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and alpine meadows, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron calvescens plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
