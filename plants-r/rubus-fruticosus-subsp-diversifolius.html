Rubus fruticosus subsp. diversifolius

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

diversifolius, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry bush, is a shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that vary in shape and size across different plants.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
