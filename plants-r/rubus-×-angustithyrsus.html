Rubus × angustithyrsus

Rubus × angustithyrsus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to parts of eastern Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, with a serrated margin and a hairy surface.

The species is valued for its edible fruits, which are often used in jams and desserts.
