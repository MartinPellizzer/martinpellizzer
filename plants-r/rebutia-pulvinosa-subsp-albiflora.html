Rebutia pulvinosa subsp. albiflora

Rebutia pulvinosa subsp.

albiflora, commonly known as the White-flowered Rebutia, is a succulent plant native to South America. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit.

The plant features short, cylindrical stems covered in areoles, from which spines and flowers emerge. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in summer, and are borne in clusters at the stem tips.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and requires well-draining soil and full sun to thrive.
