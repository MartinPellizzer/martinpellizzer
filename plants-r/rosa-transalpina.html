Rosa transalpina

Rosa transalpina, commonly known as the Transalpine rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink to red, blooming in late spring to early summer. Rosa transalpina is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for temperate climates.
