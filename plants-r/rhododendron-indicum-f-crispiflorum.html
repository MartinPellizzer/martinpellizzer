Rhododendron indicum f. crispiflorum

Rhododendron indicum f. crispiflorum, commonly known as the crisp-flowered rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate cooler climates, making it a popular choice in temperate regions.
