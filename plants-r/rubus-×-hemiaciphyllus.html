Rubus × hemiaciphyllus

Rubus × hemiaciphyllus, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry, is a deciduous shrub that belongs to the Rubiaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus ursinus (the European raspberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (the cloudberry). The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually trifoliate or pinnate, and its thorny stems. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, depending on the variety.

This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in wetlands or forested areas.
