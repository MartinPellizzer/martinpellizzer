Rubus neovicarius

Rubus neovicarius, commonly known as the Vancouver blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, with serrated margins and a glossy upper surface.

The berries are edible, though they are generally less sweet and more tart compared to other blackberry varieties.
