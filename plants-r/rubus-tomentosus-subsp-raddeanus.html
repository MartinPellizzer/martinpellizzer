Rubus tomentosus subsp. raddeanus

Rubus tomentosus subsp.

raddeanus, commonly known as the Russian blackberry, is a shrub native to temperate regions of Asia. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
