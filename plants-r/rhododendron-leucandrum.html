Rhododendron leucandrum

Rhododendron leucandrum, commonly known as the white leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dense, evergreen foliage that is leathery and glossy. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Rhododendron leucandrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
