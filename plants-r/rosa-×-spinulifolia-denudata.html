Rosa × spinulifolia denudata

Rosa × spinulifolia denudata, commonly known as the naked rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and simple, ovate leaves that are typically pubescent. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, white, and red. It is native to parts of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice for gardeners and horticultural enthusiasts.
