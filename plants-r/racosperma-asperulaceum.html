Racosperma asperulaceum

Racosperma asperulaceum, commonly known as the rough senna, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with rough, hairy texture, which gives it its common name. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Racosperma asperulaceum is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Australian medicine.
