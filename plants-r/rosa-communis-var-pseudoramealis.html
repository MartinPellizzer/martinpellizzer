Rosa communis var. pseudoramealis

Rosa communis var. pseudoramealis, commonly known as the climbing rose, is a variant of the common rose characterized by its vigorous growth habit and long, flexible stems.

It typically features fragrant, semi-double to double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, depending on the specific cultivar. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of 5 to 7 leaflets, and its stems are often covered with thorns. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for trellises and hedging.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb or trail in various garden settings.
