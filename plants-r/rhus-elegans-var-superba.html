Rhus elegans var. superba

Rhus elegans var. superba, commonly known as the glossy buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with elongated, glossy dark green leaflets that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
