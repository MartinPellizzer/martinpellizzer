Ryania candollei

Ryania candollei, commonly known as the red cedar or false mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and dark green, ovate leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antispasmodic and analgesic properties.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.
