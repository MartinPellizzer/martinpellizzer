Rosa tomentosa var. pseudofarinosa

Rosa tomentosa var. pseudofarinosa, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub-like species characterized by its dense, white to greyish pubescent foliage.

It produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to regions of Asia, including China and the Himalayas, and thrives in temperate climates. Its stems are typically covered in a thick layer of soft, fuzzy hairs, contributing to its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.
