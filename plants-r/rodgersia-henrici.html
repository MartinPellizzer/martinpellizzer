Rodgersia henrici

Rodgersia henrici, commonly known as the Henrici's rodgersia, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces showy, red-pink flowers in late summer, which bloom on tall, upright stems. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and long-lasting blooms.
