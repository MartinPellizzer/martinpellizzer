Rotala stagnina

Rotala stagnina, commonly known as the water thyme, is a small aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has bright green, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stems. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear above the water surface.

Rotala stagnina is often used in aquascaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions.
