Rhynchospora cymosa var. globularis

Rhynchospora cymosa var. globularis, commonly known as the globular spike-rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, globular inflorescence composed of numerous small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a rosette at the base.

The species is part of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other similar plants.
