Ranunculus occidentalis var. typicus

Ranunculus occidentalis var. typicus, commonly known as the western buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to western North America.

It features bright yellow flowers with 5-15 petals, often arranged in a cup-like shape, and has a hairy stem that grows up to 30 centimeters tall. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a waxy surface, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, streambanks, and moist woodlands, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
