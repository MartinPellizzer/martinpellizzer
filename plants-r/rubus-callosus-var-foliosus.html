Rubus callosus var. foliosus

Rubus callosus var. foliosus, commonly known as the prickly pear raspberry, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically three to five leaflets in number. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often red or yellow in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
