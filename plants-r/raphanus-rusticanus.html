Raphanus rusticanus

Raphanus rusticanus, commonly known as the desert radish, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and fleshy, edible roots that can be harvested when mature. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water availability.

Its botanical features make it an important species for studying plant resilience in extreme climates.
