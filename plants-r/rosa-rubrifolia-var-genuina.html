Rosa rubrifolia var. genuina

Rosa rubrifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the redleaf rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are distinctive, with a dark red or bronze hue on the underside, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
