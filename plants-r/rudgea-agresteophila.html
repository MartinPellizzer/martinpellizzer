Rudgea agresteophila

Rudgea agresteophila, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Rudgea agresteophila is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
