Ranunculus montanus var. pollinensis

Ranunculus montanus var. pollinensis, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, lobed leaf structure. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces a basal rosette of leaves and has a short flowering period in early spring.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine flora.
