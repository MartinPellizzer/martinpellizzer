Ranunculus × gluckii

Ranunculus × gluckii, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to yellow, with a distinctive central pistil surrounded by numerous stamens. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are alternate along the stem, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Ranunculus × gluckii is also noted for its ability to hybridize with other ranunculus species, contributing to its genetic diversity.
