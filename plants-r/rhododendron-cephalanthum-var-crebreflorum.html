Rhododendron cephalanthum var. crebreflorum

Rhododendron cephalanthum var. crebreflorum, commonly known as the mountain rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, broad leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a common sight in forested areas. Its floral display, which occurs in late spring, features pink to reddish hues, adding vibrant color to the landscape.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
