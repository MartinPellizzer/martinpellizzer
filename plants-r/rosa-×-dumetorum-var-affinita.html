Rosa × dumetorum var. affinita

Rosa × dumetorum var. affinita, commonly known as the alpine rose or cushion rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features dark green, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white trichomes, giving it a silvery appearance. It is well adapted to harsh alpine conditions, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover in alpine settings.
