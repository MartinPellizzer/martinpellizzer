Ruellia crispa

Ruellia crispa, commonly known as the crinkled ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive crinkled or wavy edge. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom from summer to fall. Ruellia crispa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and adaptability to different light conditions.
