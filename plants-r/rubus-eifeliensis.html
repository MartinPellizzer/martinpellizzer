Rubus eifeliensis

Rubus eifeliensis, commonly known as the Eifel raspberry, is a species of shrub native to the Eifel region of Germany.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white or pink and appear in the spring, followed by the development of its distinctive red or black fruits in the summer.

The Eifel raspberry is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
