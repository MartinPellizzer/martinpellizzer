Rubus mercicus var. bracteatus

Rubus mercicus var. bracteatus, commonly known as the bracteated blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
