Rosa urbica

Rosa urbica, commonly known as the city rose, is a species of wild rose native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in urban environments. The plant features thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets.

Rosa urbica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
