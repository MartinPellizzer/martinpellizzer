Racosperma semirigidum

Racosperma semirigidum, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often trifoliate leaves. The plant features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its woody stems are somewhat flexible, and it has small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern.

The plant is well adapted to dry environments and is often found in coastal and sandy soils.
