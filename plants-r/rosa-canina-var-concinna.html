Rosa canina var. concinna

Rosa canina var. concinna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces pink or white flowers that are typically followed by red, hairy fruits called hips. It is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

Rosa canina var. concinna is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
