Randia caudatifolia

Randia caudatifolia, commonly known as the whorled randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and are usually white or pale green.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.
