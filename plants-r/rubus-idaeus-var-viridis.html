Rubus idaeus var. viridis

Rubus idaeus var. viridis, commonly known as green raspberry, is a variant of the red raspberry plant characterized by its distinct green fruits.

This plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, green berries that are less commonly consumed than the red variety.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fruit color.
