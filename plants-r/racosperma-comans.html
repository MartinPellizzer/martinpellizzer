Racosperma comans

Racosperma comans, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Racosperma comans plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
