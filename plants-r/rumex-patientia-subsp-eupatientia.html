Rumex patientia subsp. eupatientia

Rumex patientia subsp.

eupatientia, commonly known as the European dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces clusters of small, greenish flowers that are typically arranged in dense, upright spikes. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Its leaves and roots have been used traditionally in herbal medicine for their purported diuretic and astringent properties.
