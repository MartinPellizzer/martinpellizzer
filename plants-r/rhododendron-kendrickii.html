Rhododendron kendrickii

Rhododendron kendrickii, commonly known as the Kendrick rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. It is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
