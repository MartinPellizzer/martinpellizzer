Rhododendron meridionale var. setistylum

Rhododendron meridionale var. setistylum, commonly known as the Sierra Madre rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
