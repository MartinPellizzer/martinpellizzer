Ranunculus kadzusensis

Ranunculus kadzusensis, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Ranunculus kadzusensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
