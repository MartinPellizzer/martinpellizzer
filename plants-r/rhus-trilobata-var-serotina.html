Rhus trilobata var. serotina

Rhus trilobata var. serotina, commonly known as the lateleaf sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three leaflets, which are typically glossy and turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, fuzzy fruit clusters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for wildlife and erosion control.
