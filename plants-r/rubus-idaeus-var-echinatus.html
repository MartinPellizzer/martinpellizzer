Rubus idaeus var. echinatus

Rubus idaeus var. echinatus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry bush, is a variant of the common blackberry.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupelets that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
