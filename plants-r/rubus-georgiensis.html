Rubus georgiensis

Rubus georgiensis, commonly known as the Georgian raspberry, is a species of brambles native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible red berries. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and have a tart, raspberry-like flavor.
