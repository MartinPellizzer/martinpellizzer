Rhaponticum doronicifolium

Rhaponticum doronicifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved rhapontic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features a robust root system and produces large, yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
