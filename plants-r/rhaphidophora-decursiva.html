Rhaphidophora decursiva

Rhaphidophora decursiva, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant produces a starchy tuberous root that is used as a food source in many regions. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, glossy appearance.

Rhaphidophora decursiva thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires indirect light for optimal growth.
