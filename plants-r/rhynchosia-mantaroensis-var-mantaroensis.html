Rhynchosia mantaroensis var. mantaroensis

Rhynchosia mantaroensis var. mantaroensis, commonly known as the Mantaro bean, is a climbing legume native to the Andean region of South America.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed habitats and along forest edges.

This species is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in local cuisines and traditional agricultural practices.
