Ribesiodes oblongifolium

Ribesiodes oblongifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with thornless branches, and it often grows in moist, shady woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
