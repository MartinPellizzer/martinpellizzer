Rhododendron charitopes

Rhododendron charitopes, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron charitopes is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
