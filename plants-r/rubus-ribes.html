Rubus ribes

Rubus ribes, commonly known as blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are initially green and turn dark purple or black when mature. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are white with five petals.

Rubus ribes is notable for its thorny stems and its role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
