Rosa rhaetica f. subvillosa

Rosa rhaetica f. subvillosa, commonly known as the Rhaetian rose, is a rare and distinctive subspecies of the Rosa rhaetica species.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant is native to the alpine regions of the Eastern Alps, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It has a low-growing habit with slender stems and small, hairy leaves.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in specialized gardens due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
