Rosa viscida

Rosa viscida, commonly known as the sticky rose, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often covered in a sticky substance that gives it its common name.

Rosa viscida is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
