Ranunculus hornschuchii

Ranunculus hornschuchii, commonly known as the Sierra bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are usually borne on long, slender pedicels.

Ranunculus hornschuchii is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
