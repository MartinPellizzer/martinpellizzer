Rosa communis var. obovata

Rosa communis var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed rose, is a cultivar of the rose plant characterized by its obovate-shaped leaves and fragrant flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically produces pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its foliage is dense and dark green, providing aesthetic appeal throughout the growing season.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
