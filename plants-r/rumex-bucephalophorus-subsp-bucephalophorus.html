Rumex bucephalophorus subsp. bucephalophorus

Rumex bucephalophorus subsp.

bucephalophorus, commonly known as the narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks, roadsides, and wet meadows.

The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a prominently veined underside, and it produces small, greenish to reddish flowers in dense clusters. It is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is often found in riparian habitats.

The species is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
