Rhamnus oleoides var. libyca

Rhamnus oleoides var. libyca, commonly known as the Libyan buckthorn, is a shrub native to North Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. Its fruit is a small, smooth drupe that contains seeds coated in a sticky substance.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance in its native habitats.
