Rubus tereticaulis f. setosulus

Rubus tereticaulis f. setosulus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a shrub-like plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple or black when mature. Its botanical features include hairy foliage and a distinctive floral structure.

This variety is often found in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments.
