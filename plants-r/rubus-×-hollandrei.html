Rubus × hollandrei

Rubus × hollandrei, commonly known as the Dutch blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is cultivated for its edible, dark purple berries. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and a complex flowering structure.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
