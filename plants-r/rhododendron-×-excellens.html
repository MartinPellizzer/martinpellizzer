Rhododendron × excellens

Rhododendron × excellens, commonly known as the Carolina rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron maximum and Rhododendron viscosum.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it well-suited for woodland gardens. It is native to the eastern United States and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

The species is also notable for its ability to form thickets, which can provide habitat for various wildlife species.
