Rosa montivaga var. virens

Rosa montivaga var. virens, commonly known as the greenleaf rock rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage, with leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, usually blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
