Rubus fruticosus var. vulgaris

Rubus fruticosus var. vulgaris, commonly known as brambles or blackberries, is a thorny, deciduous shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets, which are typically harvested when fully ripe. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed soils. It is valued for its edible fruits and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is known for its vigorous growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.
