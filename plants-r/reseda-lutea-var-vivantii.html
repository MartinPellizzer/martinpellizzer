Reseda lutea var. vivantii

Reseda lutea var. vivantii, commonly known as the yellow weed or yellow reseda, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and slender, branched stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in racemes.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its historical use in dye production.
