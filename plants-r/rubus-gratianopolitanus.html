Rubus gratianopolitanus

Rubus gratianopolitanus, commonly known as the European blackberry.

It is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often growing in hedgerows and woodland edges. Its dark purple to black berries are edible and used in culinary applications.

The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces white or pale yellow flowers in the spring.
