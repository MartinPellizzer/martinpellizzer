Rutidea smithii subsp. submontana

Rutidea smithii subsp.

submontana, commonly known as the mountain rutia, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly in the Andean regions of Peru and Bolivia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its botanical features include stipules, stipitate glands, and a distinctive pod-like fruit that dehisces to release seeds.
