Ranunculus contegens

Ranunculus contegens, commonly known as the Conte's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features simple, palmately divided leaves and produces small, yellow or orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
