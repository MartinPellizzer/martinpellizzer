Rhododendron nagasakianum var. gracilescens

Rhododendron nagasakianum var. gracilescens, commonly known as the Nagasaki azalea, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
