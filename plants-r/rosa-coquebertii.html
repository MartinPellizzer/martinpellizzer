Rosa coquebertii

Rosa coquebertii, commonly known as the Coquebert's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, serrated, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa coquebertii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
