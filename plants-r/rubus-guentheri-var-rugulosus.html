Rubus guentheri var. rugulosus

Rubus guentheri var. rugulosus, commonly known as the roughleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and mountainous regions.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family, which includes other brambles and fruit-bearing plants.
