Rubus hookeri

Rubus hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in forested areas and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and preserves.
