Ruschia disarticulata

Ruschia disarticulata, commonly known as the broken stem plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features compact, cylindrical stems that are often segmented, giving it a unique, segmented appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted for storing water in arid environments.

Ruschia disarticulata is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
