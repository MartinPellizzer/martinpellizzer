Rubus villicaulis subsp. consobrinus

Rubus villicaulis subsp.

consobrinus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
