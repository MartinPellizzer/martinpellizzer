Rosa × bifera variegata

Rosa × bifera variegata, commonly known as the variegated evergreen rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or yellow variegation, enhancing its ornamental value. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique foliage and blooming habit, it is a popular choice for garden borders and container planting.
