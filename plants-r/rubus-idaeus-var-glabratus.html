Rubus idaeus var. glabratus

Rubus idaeus var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry plant.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, distinguishing it from other raspberry varieties. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though some cultivars may yield yellow or golden berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its flavorful, edible fruits.

This variety is valued in both commercial agriculture and home gardening for its yield and adaptability.
