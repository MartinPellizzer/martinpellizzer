Rhododendron cubittii

Rhododendron cubittii, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.

Rhododendron cubittii is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
