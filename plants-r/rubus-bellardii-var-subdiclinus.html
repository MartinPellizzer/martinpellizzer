Rubus bellardii var. subdiclinus

Rubus bellardii var. subdiclinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and local biodiversity.
