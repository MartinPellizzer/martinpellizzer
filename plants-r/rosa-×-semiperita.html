Rosa × semiperita

Rosa × semiperita, commonly known as the everblooming rose, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is a cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa bracteata, resulting in a plant with robust growth and high disease resistance. The everblooming rose is characterized by its ability to produce flowers continuously throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically medium-green, with a slightly serrated edge, and its stems are covered in thorns.

This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, long-flowering ornamental shrub.
