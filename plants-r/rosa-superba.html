Rosa superba

Rosa superba, commonly known as the Himalayan rose, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking, large, double flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and pinnate leaves with serrated edges. Rosa superba is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to cold, mountainous environments.
