Rubus pallidus var. glaucescens

Rubus pallidus var. glaucescens, commonly known as the pale bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically pale green in color. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruits are small, aggregate berries that are edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

This plant has a dense growth habit and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
