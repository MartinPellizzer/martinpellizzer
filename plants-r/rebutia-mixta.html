Rebutia mixta

Rebutia mixta, commonly known as the hybrid Rebutia, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and smooth, greenish-gray skin. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
