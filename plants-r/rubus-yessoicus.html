Rubus yessoicus

Rubus yessoicus, commonly known as the Japanese raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
