Ranunculus trichophylloides

Ranunculus trichophylloides, commonly known as the hairy leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it its common name. Its flowers typically have five to eight sepals and numerous stamens, with yellow stigmas.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.
