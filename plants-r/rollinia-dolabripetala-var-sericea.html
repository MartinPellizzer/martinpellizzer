Rollinia dolabripetala var. sericea

Rollinia dolabripetala var. sericea, commonly known as the silky rollinia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are typically orange or red, fleshy berries that contain seeds enclosed in a pulp.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for both its foliage and floral display.
