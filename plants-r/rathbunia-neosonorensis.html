Rathbunia neosonorensis

Rathbunia neosonorensis, commonly known as the Sonoran redwood, is a rare and unique plant species native to the Sonoran Desert region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, fibrous bark that provides protection against harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by distinctive, elongated fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, adapting to the arid climate of its native habitat.
