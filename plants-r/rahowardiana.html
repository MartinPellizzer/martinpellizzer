Rahowardiana

Rahowardiana, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and red. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate. Rahowardiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and holds cultural significance in many Asian societies.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental ponds and traditional herbal practices.
