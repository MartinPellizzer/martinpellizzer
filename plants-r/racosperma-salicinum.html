Racosperma salicinum

Racosperma salicinum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, giving it its scientific name. Its seeds are encased in hard, pod-like structures that split open when mature.

Racosperma salicinum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
