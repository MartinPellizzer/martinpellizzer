Rhododendron wongii

Rhododendron wongii, commonly known as the Wong's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron wongii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
