Rosa besseri

Rosa besseri, commonly known as the Bessera rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on pedicels. Its botanical characteristics include simple, serrated leaves and an upright growth habit.

Rosa besseri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
