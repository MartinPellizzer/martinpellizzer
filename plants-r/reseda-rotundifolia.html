Reseda rotundifolia

Reseda rotundifolia, commonly known as roundleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing herb with succulent, rounded leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsules containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Reseda rotundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
