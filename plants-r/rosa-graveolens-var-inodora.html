Rosa graveolens var. inodora

Rosa graveolens var. inodora, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. Its berries are red and edible, though they are typically not consumed by humans.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in hedgerows and gardens.
