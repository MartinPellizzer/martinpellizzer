Ranunculus chaerophylloides

Ranunculus chaerophylloides, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically borne on slender, hairy stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and it often grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and sometimes toxic compounds.

The species is relatively rare and can be found in specific regions of its native range.
