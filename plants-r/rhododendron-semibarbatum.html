Rhododendron semibarbatum

Rhododendron semibarbatum, commonly known as the Florida rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its botanical features include elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and inflorescences that are often borne on long, flexible peduncles.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
