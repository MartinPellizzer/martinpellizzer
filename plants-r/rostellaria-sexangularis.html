Rostellaria sexangularis

Rostellaria sexangularis, commonly known as the six-angled rostellaria, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its unique, angular pseudobulbs. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive rostellum, which is a specialized structure at the base of the lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
