Rubus × axilliflorus

Rubus × axilliflorus, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry or loganberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus pectoralis (wineberry), resulting in a plant with unique characteristics. The plant produces large, dark purple to black berries that are typically larger and sweeter than those of its parent species. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
