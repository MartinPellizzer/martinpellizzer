Rubus argentifrons

Rubus argentifrons, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California, and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries in the summer.

The silverleaf bramble is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
