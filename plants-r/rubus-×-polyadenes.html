Rubus × polyadenes

Rubus × polyadenes, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny shrubs and dark, glossy leaves that remain green throughout the year. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and moist soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
