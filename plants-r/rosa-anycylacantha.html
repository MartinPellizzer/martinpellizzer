Rosa anycylacantha

Rosa anycylacantha, commonly known as the African candytuft, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Rosa anycylacantha is also valued for its fragrance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
