Ranunculus batrachioides var. xantholeucos

Ranunculus batrachioides var. xantholeucos, commonly known as the yellow-leaved water buttercup, is a variant of the water buttercup species native to certain regions of Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, reddish stem. The leaves are typically divided into three parts, with a distinctive basal rosette formation. This plant thrives in moist, often aquatic environments such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an attractive yet ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
