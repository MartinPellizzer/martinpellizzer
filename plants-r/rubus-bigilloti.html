Rubus bigilloti

Rubus bigilloti, commonly known as the Bigillot blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and parts of Georgia. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, a dark purple to black berry, develops from the flowers and is typically harvested in the summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in local ecosystems.
