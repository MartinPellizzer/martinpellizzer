Rhexia aurea

Rhexia aurea, commonly known as golden tickseed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for wetland gardens.

Rhexia aurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
