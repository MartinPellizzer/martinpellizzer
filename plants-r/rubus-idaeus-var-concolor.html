Rubus idaeus var. concolor

Rubus idaeus var. concolor, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a thorny shrub native to eastern North America.

It produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous drupelets, which are typically bright red when ripe. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and hairy stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is commonly cultivated for both commercial and home use.
