Rhododendron luteum var. croceum

Rhododendron luteum var. croceum, commonly known as the yellow azalea, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range from pale yellow to orange in color. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring blooms.
