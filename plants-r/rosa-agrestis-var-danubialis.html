Rosa agrestis var. danubialis

Rosa agrestis var. danubialis, commonly known as the Danube wild rose, is a shrub native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are pink to red, appearing in clusters, and are followed by oval, red-orange hips.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
