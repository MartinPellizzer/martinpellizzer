Ranunculus fallax subsp. laetifrons

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

laetifrons, commonly known as the cheerful ranunculus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features delicate, early spring blooms with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow to orange.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are hairy and often covered in a waxy layer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and is relatively easy to cultivate in cooler climates.
