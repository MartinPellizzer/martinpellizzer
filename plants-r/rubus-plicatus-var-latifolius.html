Rubus plicatus var. latifolius

Rubus plicatus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and the production of edible, red berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it has a thorny stem structure typical of the Rubus genus.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which can be used in culinary applications.
