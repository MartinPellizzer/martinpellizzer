Rhamnus davurica var. davurica

Rhamnus davurica var. davurica, commonly known as the Chinese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark purple berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

The bark is thick and fissured, with a distinctive reddish-brown color.
