Rubus moluccanus var. formosensis

Rubus moluccanus var. formosensis, commonly known as the Formosan blackberry, is a shrub native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in local traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.

Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it blooms in early spring.
