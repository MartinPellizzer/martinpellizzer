Rubus ulmifolius var. bruginoensis

Rubus ulmifolius var. bruginoensis, commonly known as the Brugnina blackberry, is a variant of the brambles native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is sometimes cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
