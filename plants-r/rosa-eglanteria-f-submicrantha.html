Rosa eglanteria f. submicrantha

Rosa eglanteria f. submicrantha, commonly known as the smooth prickly rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its thornless stems and dense, dark green foliage.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and adaptable member of the Rosaceae family.
