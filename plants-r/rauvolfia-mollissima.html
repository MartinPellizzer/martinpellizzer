Rauvolfia mollissima

Rauvolfia mollissima, commonly known as velvetleaf or Indian snakeroot, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elliptical, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. Rauvolfia mollissima is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its alkaloid content, which has been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
