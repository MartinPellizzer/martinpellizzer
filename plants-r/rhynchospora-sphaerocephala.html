Rhynchospora sphaerocephala

Rhynchospora sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globular flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in the southeastern United States.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in saturated soils, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems. Its flowers are often visited by insects, contributing to its role in pollination networks.

Rhynchospora sphaerocephala is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological significance in maintaining wetland biodiversity.
