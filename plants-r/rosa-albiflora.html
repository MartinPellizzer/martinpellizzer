Rosa albiflora

Rosa albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white petals and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers bloom in the spring. Rosa albiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure to thrive.
