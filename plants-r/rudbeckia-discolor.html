Rudbeckia discolor

Rudbeckia discolor, commonly known as the prairie coneflower, is a North American flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange ray flowers and a dark brown to black central disk, which gives it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in prairies and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Rudbeckia discolor is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
