Rubus thyrsanthus var. phylladenes

Rubus thyrsanthus var. phylladenes, commonly known as the red raspberries, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to temperate regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, bright red drupes that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is widely cultivated for its flavorful berries used in culinary and commercial applications.
