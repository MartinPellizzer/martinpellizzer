Rhododendron rubineiflorum

Rhododendron rubineiflorum, commonly known as the red-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of red and pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Rhododendron rubineiflorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in specific garden conditions.
