Rosa sepium var. albiflora

Rosa sepium var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered dogwood rose, is a variety of the dogwood rose species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their beauty.

The botanical features of Rosa sepium var. albiflora make it a valuable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
