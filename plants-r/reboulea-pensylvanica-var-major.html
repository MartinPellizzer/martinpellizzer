Reboulea pensylvanica var. major

Reboulea pensylvanica var. major, commonly known as the Pennsylvania redbud, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers in early spring, which are followed by elongated, flat pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
