Rubus canescens var. setosoglandulosus

Rubus canescens var. setosoglandulosus, commonly known as the hairy glandular raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
