Ruppia truncatifolia

Ruppia truncatifolia, commonly known as sea rocket, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with submerged and floating leaves that help it thrive in coastal and brackish waters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically found in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats. Its roots are specialized for anchoring in soft, muddy substrates and absorbing nutrients from the water.

Ruppia truncatifolia plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
