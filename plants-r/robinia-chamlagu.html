Robinia chamlagu

Robinia chamlagu, commonly known as the Persian robinia, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or white, appearing in clusters, and it produces long, flat pods that contain several seeds.

The wood is valued for its strength and is used in construction and furniture-making.
