Rosa jundzilliana var. ruthenica

Rosa jundzilliana var. ruthenica, commonly known as the Ruthenian roses, is a hybrid rose species native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its dark red, semi-double to fully double flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and broad, leathery leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
