Rosa × dumetorum var. glaucescens

Rosa × dumetorum var. glaucescens, commonly known as the woolly dog rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray-white hairs covering the stems, leaves, and flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five petals, and it produces hips that are edible and used in culinary applications.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and hardy nature, making it suitable for various garden environments.
