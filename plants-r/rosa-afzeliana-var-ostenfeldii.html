Rosa afzeliana var. ostenfeldii

Rosa afzeliana var. ostenfeldii, commonly known as the Ostenfeld's rose, is a shrub-like species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with oval, serrated leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late summer, which are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
