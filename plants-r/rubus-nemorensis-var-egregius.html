Rubus nemorensis var. egregius

Rubus nemorensis var. egregius, commonly known as the noble bramble, is a variant of the European blackberry species.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in European horticulture.
