Rhyncholacis palmettifolia var. rosea

Rhyncholacis palmettifolia var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered palmetto, is a striking plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palm-like leaves that are broad and often adorned with a distinctive pink or rose-colored hue. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. Its botanical structure includes a short trunk and a rosette of leaves, which help it thrive in humid, tropical environments.

This variant is prized in horticulture for its unique coloration and adaptability to shaded conditions.
