Rhynchospora laevis

Rhynchospora laevis, commonly known as the smooth beak-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with three-ranked leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Rhynchospora laevis thrives in moist soils and is often found in association with other wetland plants.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
