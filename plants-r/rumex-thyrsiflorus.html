Rumex thyrsiflorus

Rumex thyrsiflorus, commonly known as the thyrsofloral sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like sorrel and buckwheat. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spikelike clusters, producing small, greenish-yellow blossoms that appear in late spring to early summer.

Rumex thyrsiflorus is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
