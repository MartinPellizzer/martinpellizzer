Rubus serpens var. siemianicensis

Rubus serpens var. siemianicensis, commonly known as the Siemianice raspberry, is a variant of the serpentine raspberry native to Central Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, containing several drupelets. It thrives in woodland and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its morphological variation compared to other Rubus species.
