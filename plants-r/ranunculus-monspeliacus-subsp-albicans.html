Ranunculus monspeliacus subsp. albicans

Ranunculus monspeliacus subsp.

albicans, commonly known as the white buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its delicate, white flowers with five to eight petals, which often have a yellow or greenish center.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is herbaceous, with a short, upright stem.

It is considered a rare and protected species in some regions due to habitat loss and over-collection.
