Ranunculus andinus

Ranunculus andinus, commonly known as the Andean buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with a distinctive central tuft of stamens. The plant prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant has a short, sturdy stem.

Ranunculus andinus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
