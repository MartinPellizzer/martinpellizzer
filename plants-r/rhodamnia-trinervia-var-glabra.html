Rhodamnia trinervia var. glabra

Rhodamnia trinervia var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved rhodamnia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
