Rhamnus maximovicziana var. oblongifolia

Rhamnus maximovicziana var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are typically oblong to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue, spherical berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
