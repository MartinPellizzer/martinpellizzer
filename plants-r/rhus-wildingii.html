Rhus wildingii

Rhus wildingii, commonly known as the wild chinquapin, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like poison ivy and mango. The plant is characterized by its alternate, pinnately compound leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to coastal and inland dunes.
