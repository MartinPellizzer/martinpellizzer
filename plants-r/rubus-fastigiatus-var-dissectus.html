Rubus fastigiatus var. dissectus

Rubus fastigiatus var. dissectus, commonly known as the dissected blackberry shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with finely serrated margins, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
