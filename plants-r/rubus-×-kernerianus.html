Rubus × kernerianus

Rubus × kernerianus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere, particularly in Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with alternate, pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and the fruit, a cluster of small drupes, ripens in late summer.

This species is valued for its edible berries, which are used in culinary and commercial applications.
