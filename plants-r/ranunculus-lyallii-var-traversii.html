Ranunculus lyallii var. traversii

Ranunculus lyallii var. traversii, commonly known as the Lyall's buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces a basal rosette of foliage and blooms in spring.
