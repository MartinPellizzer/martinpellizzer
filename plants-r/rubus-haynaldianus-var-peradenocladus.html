Rubus haynaldianus var. peradenocladus

Rubus haynaldianus var. peradenocladus, commonly known as the Alaska raspberry, is a shrub native to the subarctic regions of North America.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the first-year stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in moist, rocky soils.
