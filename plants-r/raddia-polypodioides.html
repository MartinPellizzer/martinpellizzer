Raddia polypodioides

Raddia polypodioides, commonly known as the slender fern, is a small, delicate fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a rhizomatous structure with narrow, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The species is characterized by its simple, elegant appearance and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
