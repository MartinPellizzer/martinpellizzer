Rubus ulmifolius subsp. contractifolius

Rubus ulmifolius subsp.

contractifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, dark green leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
