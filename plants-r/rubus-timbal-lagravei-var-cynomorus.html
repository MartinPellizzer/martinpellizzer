Rubus timbal-lagravei var. cynomorus

Rubus timbal-lagravei var. cynomorus, commonly known as the Timbal-Lagrave blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black fruits that are typically small to medium in size. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has thorny stems typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variant is valued for its unique flavor and is sometimes cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
