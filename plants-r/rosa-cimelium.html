Rosa cimelium

Rosa cimelium, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in areas like the Caucasus and Iran. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the stems are often covered in thorns.

Rosa cimelium is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for centuries in traditional gardens.
