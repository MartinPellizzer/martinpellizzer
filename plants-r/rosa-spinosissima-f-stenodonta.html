Rosa spinosissima f. stenodonta

Rosa spinosissima f. stenodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved prickly rose, is a compact, shrub-like species with thorny stems and dense foliage.

Its leaves are narrow and serrated, typically arranged in odd pinnate patterns, giving it a distinctive appearance. The flowers are small, fragrant, and usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
