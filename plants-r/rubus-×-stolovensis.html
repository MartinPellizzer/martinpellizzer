Rubus × stolovensis

Rubus × stolovensis, commonly known as the black raspberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically deep red to black in color when ripe. These fruits are rich in antioxidants and are widely appreciated for their flavor and culinary uses.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.
