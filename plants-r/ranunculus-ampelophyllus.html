Ranunculus ampelophyllus

Ranunculus ampelophyllus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with prominent, rounded centers and deeply lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ranunculus ampelophyllus is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy and often variable floral structures.
