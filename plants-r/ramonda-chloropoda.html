Ramonda chloropoda

Ramonda chloropoda, commonly known as the greenleaf ramonda, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
