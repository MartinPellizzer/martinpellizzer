Rosa communis var. crepinii

Rosa communis var. crepinii, commonly known as the Crepin's rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from France.

It is characterized by its semi-climbing growth habit and produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer.
