Rubus eucalyptus var. yunnanensis

Rubus eucalyptus var. yunnanensis, commonly known as Yunnan blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows at high elevations, preferring well-drained soils and a temperate climate. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are white or pale pink.

The species is valued for its edible berries, which have a tart flavor and are used in local cuisines.
