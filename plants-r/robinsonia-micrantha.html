Robinsonia micrantha

Robinsonia micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive, narrow leaf structure.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
