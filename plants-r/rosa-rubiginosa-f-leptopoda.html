Rosa rubiginosa f. leptopoda

Rosa rubiginosa f. leptopoda, commonly known as the slender-stemmed dog rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose species.

It is characterized by its slender, often arching stems and smaller, more delicate flowers compared to the typical form. The plant produces pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

This form is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showing adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
