Rosa pomifera f. borbasii

Rosa pomifera f. borbasii, commonly known as the Borbasii rose, is a unique horticultural form of the rose with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its large, edible fruits, which resemble fuzzy, orange-red tomatoes and are often referred to as "rose hips.". The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to the blooms.

This form is prized for both its ornamental value and its edible fruit, making it a valuable addition to gardens and botanical collections.
