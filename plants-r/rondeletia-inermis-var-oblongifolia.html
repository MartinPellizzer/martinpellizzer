Rondeletia inermis var. oblongifolia

Rondeletia inermis var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rondeletia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
