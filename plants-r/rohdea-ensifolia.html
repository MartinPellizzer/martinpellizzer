Rohdea ensifolia

Rohdea ensifolia, commonly known as the swordleaf rohdea, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in a clustered inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rohdea ensifolia is valued for its ornamental foliage and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
