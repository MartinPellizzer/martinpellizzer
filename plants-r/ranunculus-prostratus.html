Ranunculus prostratus

Ranunculus prostratus, commonly known as the prostrate buttercup, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a creeping stem that lies close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

Ranunculus prostratus thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
