Rhododendron chevalieri

Rhododendron chevalieri, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. The plant features broad, ovate leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to reddish-purple flowers in the spring. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The plant has a dense root system that helps prevent soil erosion in its natural habitat.
