Rosa nivea

Rosa nivea, commonly known as the white rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa nivea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
