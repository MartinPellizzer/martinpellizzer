Rupicapnos reboudiana var. pulchella

Rupicapnos reboudiana var. pulchella, commonly known as the pink Himalayan bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its striking pink to magenta flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and alpine landscapes.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold temperatures.
