Rottboellia aurita subsp. stigmosa

Rottboellia aurita subsp.

stigmosa, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces spikelets that are covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
