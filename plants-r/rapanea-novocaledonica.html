Rapanea novocaledonica

Rapanea novocaledonica, commonly known as the New Caledonian holly, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the island of New Caledonia and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, with a waxy texture, and it produces small, dark blue berries that are edible.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in the unique flora of New Caledonia.
