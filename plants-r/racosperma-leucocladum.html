Racosperma leucocladum

Racosperma leucocladum, commonly known as the white-leafed acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
