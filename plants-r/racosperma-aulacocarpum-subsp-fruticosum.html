Racosperma aulacocarpum subsp. fruticosum

Racosperma aulacocarpum subsp.

fruticosum, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its finely haired stems and leaves, which are often covered in a soft, silky coating.

The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests or woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
