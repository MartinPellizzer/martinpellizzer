Rhododendron anthopogon subsp. anthopogon

Rhododendron anthopogon subsp.

anthopogon, commonly known as the Korean rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Korea. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often clustered in inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
