Rhododendron hormophorum

Rhododendron hormophorum, commonly known as the Kashmiri rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India, Nepal, and Pakistan.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in cool, moist climates and well-drained acidic soils.

Rhododendron hormophorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
