Ranunculus hydrocharoides

Ranunculus hydrocharoides, commonly known as water crowfoot, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that float on the water surface or extend underwater. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals and numerous stamens, making it visually distinct.

Ranunculus hydrocharoides plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
