Rotala apetala

Rotala apetala, commonly known as the leafy spikerush, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often hidden within the foliage. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense mats that help stabilize aquatic ecosystems. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in whorls, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Rotala apetala is often used in aquatic gardening and as a water filtration plant in artificial ponds.
