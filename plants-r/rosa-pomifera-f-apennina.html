Rosa pomifera f. apennina

Rosa pomifera f. apennina, commonly known as the Apennine rose, is a botanical variant of the rose species Rosa pomifera.

It is characterized by its unique, fruit-like structures called rose hips, which are typically large and swollen. The plant is native to the Apennine Mountains in Italy and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are usually pink or red, and the plant is valued for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
