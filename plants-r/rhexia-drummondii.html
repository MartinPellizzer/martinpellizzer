Rhexia drummondii

Rhexia drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in pine flatwoods. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish tinge when young.

Rhexia drummondii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
