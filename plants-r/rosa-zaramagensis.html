Rosa zaramagensis

Rosa zaramagensis, commonly known as the Zarama rose, is a species native to the island of Zanzibar in Tanzania.

It is a shrub-like plant with dense, thorny stems and small, dark green leaves. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with a distinctive fragrance and five to seven petals. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
