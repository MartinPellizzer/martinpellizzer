Rosa afzeliana var. oligotricha

Rosa afzeliana var. oligotricha, commonly known as the sparse-hairied shrub rose, is a perennial deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and Korea.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant has elliptical leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are usually pink or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
