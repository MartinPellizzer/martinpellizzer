Rubus genevieri

Rubus genevieri, commonly known as the Genevier's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black fruits. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental value and edible fruits, though it is less commonly cultivated than other Rubus species.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters.
