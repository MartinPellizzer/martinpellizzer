Rauvolfia pubescens

Rauvolfia pubescens, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its woody stem, opposite leaves with a hairy surface, and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Rauvolfiaceae family.
