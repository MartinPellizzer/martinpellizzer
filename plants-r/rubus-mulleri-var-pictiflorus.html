Rubus mulleri var. pictiflorus

Rubus mulleri var. pictiflorus, commonly known as the painted flower raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. The plant is notable for its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its unique floral display, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
