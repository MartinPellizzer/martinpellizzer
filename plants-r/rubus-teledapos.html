Rubus teledapos

Rubus teledapos, commonly known as the Teledapos blackberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
