Ribes longeracemosum var. pilosum

Ribes longeracemosum var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy gooseneck vine, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and often have a hairy texture, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found along stream banks and in woodland areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
