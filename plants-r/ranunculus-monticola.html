Ranunculus monticola

Ranunculus monticola, commonly known as the mountain buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five petals. Its botanical characteristics include a short, stout stem and a fibrous root system.

Ranunculus monticola is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates.
