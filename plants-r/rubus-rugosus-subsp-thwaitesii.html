Rubus rugosus subsp. thwaitesii

Rubus rugosus subsp.

thwaitesii, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and Australia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrubland habitats.

Due to its rugged appearance and adaptability, it is a notable species within the Rubus genus.
