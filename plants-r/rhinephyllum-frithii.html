Rhinephyllum frithii

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiral-arranged leaves that form a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve moisture in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the wet season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Rhinephyllum frithii is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
