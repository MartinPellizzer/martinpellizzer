Rubus triphyllus var. leiotriphyllus

Rubus triphyllus var. leiotriphyllus, commonly known as the smooth-leafed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, leathery leaves and thornless stems, making it distinct from other Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
