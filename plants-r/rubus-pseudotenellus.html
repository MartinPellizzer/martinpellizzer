Rubus pseudotenellus

Rubus pseudotenellus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub native to parts of North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers well-drained soil.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like roses and apples.
