Rauvolfia heterophylla

Rauvolfia heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf ruever, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes, which can vary from elliptic to ovate, depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots and leaves contain alkaloids, making it of interest in traditional medicine and pharmacology.

Rauvolfia heterophylla thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
