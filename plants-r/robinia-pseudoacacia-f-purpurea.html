Robinia pseudoacacia f. purpurea

Robinia pseudoacacia f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-leaf false acacia, is a compact, ornamental variant of the black locust tree.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage, which provides a vibrant contrast to its typical green leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers in early summer, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It is a deciduous tree or shrub that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
