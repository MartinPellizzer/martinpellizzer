Rubus schleicheri microgene polyacanthoides

Rubus schleicheri microgene polyacanthoides, commonly known as the Schleicher's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in early spring.

This species is notable for its horticultural value and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
