Rubus obscurus microgene calvifrons

Rubus obscurus microgene calvifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are usually hairy and have a reddish or purplish hue. Its flowers are white or pale green, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
