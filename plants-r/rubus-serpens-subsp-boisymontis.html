Rubus serpens subsp. boisymontis

Rubus serpens subsp.

boisymontis, commonly known as the Boisymont blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, which help it climb or spread along the ground.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.
