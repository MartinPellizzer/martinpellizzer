Rosa cinnamomea f. plena

Rosa cinnamomea f. plena, commonly known as the full-flowered cinnamon rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, double blooms that are typically a rich, deep red or orange-red in color. The plant produces fragrant flowers with a velvety texture, and its foliage is dark green with a slightly hairy underside. It is a compact shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
