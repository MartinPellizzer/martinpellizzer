Rosa adenocarpa

Rosa adenocarpa, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a soft, fuzzy texture, and the stems are covered in fine, hairy hairs.

Rosa adenocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued for its resilience in various environmental conditions.
