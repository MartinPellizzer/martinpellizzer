Ruellia hyssopifolia

Ruellia hyssopifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Ruellia hyssopifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
