Rubus acuminatus var. puberulus

Rubus acuminatus var. puberulus, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with a rough, pubescent texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically red when ripe. These berries are often used in jams and preserves due to their tart flavor.

It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
