Rhamnella mairei

Rhamnella mairei, commonly known as the Maire's rhamnus, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae, which includes species known for their often prickly leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in cool, moist, and shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture with a distinctive dark green upper surface.

The plant produces small yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals.
