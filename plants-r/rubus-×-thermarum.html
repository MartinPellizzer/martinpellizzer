Rubus × thermarum

Rubus × thermarum, commonly known as the thermarum raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its erect or spreading growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it often thrives in woodland or hedgerow environments. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The thermarum raspberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some culinary applications.
