Rhynchosia punctata

Rhynchosia punctata, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and cylindrical, hairy pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is often grown for its protein-rich seeds, which are used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

Rhynchosia punctata also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.
