Rosa coriifolia var. liophylla

Rosa coriifolia var. liophylla, commonly known as the white-stemmed shrub rose, is a variant of the Chinese rose, characterized by its distinctive, fragrant flowers and unique growth habits.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thornless stems and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing an attractive backdrop to the blooms.

This plant is native to China and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
