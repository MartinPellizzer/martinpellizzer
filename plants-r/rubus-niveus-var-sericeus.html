Rubus niveus var. sericeus

Rubus niveus var. sericeus, commonly known as the silverleaf raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves.

It is native to parts of Asia and is typically found in forested areas with moist, well-drained soil. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or yellow in color. Its leaves are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.
