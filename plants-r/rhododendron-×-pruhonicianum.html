Rhododendron × pruhonicianum

Rhododendron × pruhonicianum, commonly known as the Pruhonice hybrid, is a deciduous shrub native to the Czech Republic.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, broad leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of pink to red flowers. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.
