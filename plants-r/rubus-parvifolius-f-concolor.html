Rubus parvifolius f. concolor

Rubus parvifolius f. concolor, commonly known as the smooth-leafed raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are smooth and glabrous on both sides. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
