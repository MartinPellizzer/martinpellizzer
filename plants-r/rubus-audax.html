Rubus audax

Rubus audax, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Rubus audax thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
