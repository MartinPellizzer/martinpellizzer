Rosa afzeliana f. subcanescens

Rosa afzeliana f. subcanescens, commonly known as the silky rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa afzeliana species.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
