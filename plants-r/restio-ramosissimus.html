Restio ramosissimus

Restio ramosissimus, commonly known as the many-branched restio, is a tall, grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Restio ramosissimus plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
