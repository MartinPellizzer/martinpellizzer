Rinorea ilicifolia subsp. ilicifolia

Rinorea ilicifolia subsp.

ilicifolia, commonly known as the sweetleaf tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height.

The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with a distinctive sweet fragrance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
