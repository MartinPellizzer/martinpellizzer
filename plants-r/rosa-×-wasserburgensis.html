Rosa × wasserburgensis

Rosa × wasserburgensis, commonly known as the Wasserburg rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from Germany.

It is characterized by its large, double flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping and floral arrangements.
