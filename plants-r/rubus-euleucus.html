Rubus euleucus

Rubus euleucus, commonly known as the white-berried blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, which are typically small and dark purple to black, ripen in late summer.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other bramble species.
