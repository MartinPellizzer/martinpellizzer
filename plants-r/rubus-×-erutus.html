Rubus × erutus

Rubus × erutus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny shrubs and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple or black when ripe. It is native to western North America and thrives in moist, temperate climates.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its edible fruits.
