Rosa dumalis f. recognita

Rosa dumalis f. recognita, commonly known as the recognized form of the alpine rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa dumalis.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, thornless stems. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This form is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
