Rosa arguta

Rosa arguta, commonly known as the alpine rose or mountain rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in cold, rocky soils. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, with five to seven petals. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of the rose family.

Rosa arguta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
