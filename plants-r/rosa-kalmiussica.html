Rosa kalmiussica

Rosa kalmiussica, commonly known as the Kalmia rose, is a species native to the northeastern United States.

It is a shrub-like plant with dense, upright growth habit and is characterized by its dark green, leathery foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are borne on short pedicels. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins and a woody stem structure.

Rosa kalmiussica is adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas.
