Racosperma latifolium

Racosperma latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its bark is often fissured, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is adapted to dry environments and is found in various regions across Australia.
