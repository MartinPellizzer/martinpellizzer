Rostellularia nagpurensis

Rostellularia nagpurensis, commonly known as the Nagpur grass, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit with slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

Rostellularia nagpurensis is often found in moist, open habitats such as fields and along water bodies.
