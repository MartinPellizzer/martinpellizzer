Rhododendron huiyangense

Rhododendron huiyangense, commonly known as the Huiyang rhododendron, is a species native to China, specifically found in the Huiyang region of Guangdong Province.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, thriving in acidic soils. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
