Rhaponticum babylonicum

Rhaponticum babylonicum, commonly known as the Babylonian rhubarb, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features a robust root system and erect, branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers arranged in a panicle, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

The roots are edible and have been used historically for their medicinal and culinary properties.
