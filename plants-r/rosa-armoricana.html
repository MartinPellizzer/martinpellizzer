Rosa armoricana

Rosa armoricana, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces red, hairy fruits called hips in the fall. It is well adapted to rocky and arid soils, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Rosa armoricana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
