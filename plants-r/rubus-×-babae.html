Rubus × babae

Rubus × babae, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupelets, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated for its edible berries and ornamental value.
