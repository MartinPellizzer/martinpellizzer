Rosa tomentosa var. ovoidea

Rosa tomentosa var. ovoidea, commonly known as the fuzzy rose or woolly rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and hairy, ovate leaves that are often grayish-white in color. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Rosa tomentosa var. ovoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
