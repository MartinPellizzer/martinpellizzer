Ranunculus flaccidus

Ranunculus flaccidus, commonly known as the flaccid buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Ranunculus flaccidus is known for its preference for cool, humid climates and is often found in mountainous regions.
