Rhamnus papuana

Rhamnus papuana, commonly known as the Papuan buckthorn, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of New Guinea.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, subtropical climates. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red berries that are edible for some bird species. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Rhamnus papuana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
