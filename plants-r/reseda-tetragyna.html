Reseda tetragyna

Reseda tetragyna, commonly known as four-angled reseda, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal areas and grasslands. Reseda tetragyna has a compact, upright growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and early spring blooms.
