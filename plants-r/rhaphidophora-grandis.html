Rhaphidophora grandis

Rhaphidophora grandis, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Rhaphidophora grandis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
