Renealmia jamaicensis

Renealmia jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican reed, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica, and thrives in wetland environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is often found in marshy areas.

Due to its ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
