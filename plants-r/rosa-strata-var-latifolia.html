Rosa strata var. latifolia

Rosa strata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf climbing rose, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and long, arching stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its broad, ovate leaves are typically covered in a fine layer of white down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a climbing plant in landscapes.
