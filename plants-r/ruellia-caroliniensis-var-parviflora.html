Ruellia caroliniensis var. parviflora

Ruellia caroliniensis var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered ruellia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wetlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants with both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
