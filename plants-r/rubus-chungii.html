Rubus chungii

Rubus chungii, commonly known as Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

The botanical features of Rubus chungii make it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
