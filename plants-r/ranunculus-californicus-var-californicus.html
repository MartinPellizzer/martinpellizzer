Ranunculus californicus var. californicus

Ranunculus californicus var. californicus, commonly known as the California buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight yellow petals and a prominent yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves that are palmately arranged, and it often grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided foliage and vibrant yellow flowers.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
