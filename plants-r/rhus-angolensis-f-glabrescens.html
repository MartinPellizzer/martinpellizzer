Rhus angolensis f. glabrescens

Rhus angolensis f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leafed stinkbush, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by clusters of red or orange berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and seasonal berries.
