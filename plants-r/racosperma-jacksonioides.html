Racosperma jacksonioides

Racosperma jacksonioides, commonly known as Jackson's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
