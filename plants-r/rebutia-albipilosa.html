Rebutia albipilosa

Rebutia albipilosa, commonly known as the white-haired rebutia, is a low-growing succulent native to South America.

It features a compact rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves with a distinctive white or pale blue coating. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
