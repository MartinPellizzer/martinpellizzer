Ranunculus aquatilis subsp. heterophyllus

Ranunculus aquatilis subsp.

heterophyllus, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Europe. It typically grows in shallow water, along the edges of lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves that are adapted for underwater photosynthesis. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with five petals and numerous stamens.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.
