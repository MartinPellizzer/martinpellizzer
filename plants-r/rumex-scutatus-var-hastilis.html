Rumex scutatus var. hastilis

Rumex scutatus var. hastilis, commonly known as the broadleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in the summer, which are followed by distinctive, elongated, three-lobed seed cases. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, in fields, and near water bodies.

Its robust growth and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
