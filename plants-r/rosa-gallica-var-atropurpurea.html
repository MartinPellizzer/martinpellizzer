Rosa gallica var. atropurpurea

Rosa gallica var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the dark purple gallica rose, is a species native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its deep, velvety purple petals and fragrant, semi-double blooms. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces thorny stems. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of 5 to 7 elliptical leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and culinary applications.
