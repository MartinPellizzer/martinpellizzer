Rosa × andegavensis var. bihariensis

Rosa × andegavensis var. bihariensis, commonly known as the Himalayan rose, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
