Rhodocolea involucrata

Rhodocolea involucrata, commonly known as the Cape rose or red sand rose, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that produces showy, pink to red flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, fleshy leaves that are adapted to arid environments, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Rhodocolea involucrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
