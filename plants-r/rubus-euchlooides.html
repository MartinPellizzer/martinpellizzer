Rubus euchlooides

Rubus euchlooides, commonly known as the smooth blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing or upright growth habit and produces small, dark berries that are typically edible. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

The fruit of Rubus euchlooides is valued for its flavor and is used in local culinary traditions.
