Ranunculus mirus

Ranunculus mirus, commonly known as the fairy cup, is a delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features a simple, herbaceous stem with trifoliate leaves, and its flowers typically have five to eight petals. It blooms in early spring and is known for its unique, almost cup-shaped flower structure.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
