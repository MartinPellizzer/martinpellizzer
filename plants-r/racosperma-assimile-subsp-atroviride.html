Racosperma assimile subsp. atroviride

Racosperma assimile subsp.

atroviride, commonly known as the dark-green wattle, is a shrub native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which appear during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing some degree of drought tolerance.

The species is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
