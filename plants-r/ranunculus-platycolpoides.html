Ranunculus platycolpoides

Ranunculus platycolpoides, commonly known as the flat-ribbed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are hairy, adding to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
