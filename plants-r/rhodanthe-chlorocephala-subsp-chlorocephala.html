Rhodanthe chlorocephala subsp. chlorocephala

Rhodanthe chlorocephala subsp.

chlorocephala, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in globular inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions. It has hairy, narrow leaves and is adapted to thrive in sunny, well-drained environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
