Rhododendron camtschaticum

Rhododendron camtschaticum, commonly known as the Caucasian rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
