Ranunculus occidentalis subsp. turneri

Ranunculus occidentalis subsp.

turneri, commonly known as the Sierra larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and meadows. It is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
