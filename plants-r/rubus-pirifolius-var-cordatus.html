Rubus pirifolius var. cordatus

Rubus pirifolius var. cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
