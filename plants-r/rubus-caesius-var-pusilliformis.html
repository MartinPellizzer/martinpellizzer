Rubus caesius var. pusilliformis

Rubus caesius var. pusilliformis, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring partial shade to full sun. Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the edible, aggregate fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in jams and desserts.
