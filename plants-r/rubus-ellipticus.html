Rubus ellipticus

Rubus ellipticus, commonly known as the elliptic blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Rubus ellipticus plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinator populations.
