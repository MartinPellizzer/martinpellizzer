Riedelia umbellata

Riedelia umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a woody stem with dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Riedelia umbellata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
