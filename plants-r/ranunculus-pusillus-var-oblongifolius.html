Ranunculus pusillus var. oblongifolius

Ranunculus pusillus var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals, typically less than 1 inch in diameter, and is characterized by its slender, oblong leaves. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows, often in rocky or calcareous soils. Its foliage is usually alternate and has a distinctive, finely divided appearance.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic sap.
