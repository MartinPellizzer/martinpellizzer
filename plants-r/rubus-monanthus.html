Rubus monanthus

Rubus monanthus, commonly known as the single-flower blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible blackberries in the summer. Its fruits are typically small, firm, and have a tart flavor.

Rubus monanthus is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
