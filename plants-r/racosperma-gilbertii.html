Racosperma gilbertii

Racosperma gilbertii, commonly known as the Gilbert's wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its bright yellow flowers are arranged in dense, globular clusters, making it a visually striking species.

Racosperma gilbertii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential in horticultural applications.
