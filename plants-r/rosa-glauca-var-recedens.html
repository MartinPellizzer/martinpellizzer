Rosa glauca var. recedens

Rosa glauca var. recedens, commonly known as the pale dogwood rose, is a shrub-like species native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, upright stems covered in thorns and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a bluish-green hue with a fuzzy texture. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in woodland gardens.
