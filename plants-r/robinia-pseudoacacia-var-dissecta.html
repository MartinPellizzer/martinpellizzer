Robinia pseudoacacia var. dissecta

Robinia pseudoacacia var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected false acacia, is a variant of the honeylocust tree characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves.

This cultivar exhibits a unique leaf morphology with deep lobes that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant is native to the eastern United States but has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value. It produces small, green pods that contain flattened, brown seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family.

The tree is adaptable to various soil types and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
