Rubus apiculatus subsp. malacotrichus

Rubus apiculatus subsp.

malacotrichus, commonly known as the Malacotrichus raspberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically green with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are usually red when mature. These berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them suitable for culinary use.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
