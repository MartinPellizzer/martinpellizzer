Rosa villosa f. meridionalis

Rosa villosa f. meridionalis, commonly known as the Southern hairy rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa villosa species.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and foliage, which are typical of the parent species. The flowers are usually pale pink to white, with a soft, almost velvety texture. It is native to regions of southern Europe and is often found in woodland and scrubland habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
