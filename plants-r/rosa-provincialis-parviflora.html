Rosa provincialis parviflora

Rosa provincialis parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered provincial rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include simple, ovate leaves and hips that are typically red or orange when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
