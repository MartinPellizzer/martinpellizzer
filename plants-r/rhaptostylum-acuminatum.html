Rhaptostylum acuminatum

Rhaptostylum acuminatum, commonly known as the slender bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it is often found in wetland ecosystems.

Rhaptostylum acuminatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
