Robinia galuga

Robinia galuga, commonly known as the yellowwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pods. The tree produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, followed by elongated seed pods that split open to release its seeds. Its bark is thick and flaky, often with a reddish-brown hue, and its wood is highly valued for its strength and durability.

Robinia galuga is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to pests and diseases.
