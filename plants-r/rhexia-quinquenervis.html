Rhexia quinquenervis

Rhexia quinquenervis, commonly known as the five-veined rhexia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with showy, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with five distinct veins, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and wetlands.

Rhexia quinquenervis plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
