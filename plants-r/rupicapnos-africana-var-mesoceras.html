Rupicapnos africana var. mesoceras

Rupicapnos africana var. mesoceras, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
