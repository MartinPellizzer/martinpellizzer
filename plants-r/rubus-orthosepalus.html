Rubus orthosepalus

Rubus orthosepalus, commonly known as the narrow-sepal raspberry, is a species of bramble native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated margins, typical of many Rubus species.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
