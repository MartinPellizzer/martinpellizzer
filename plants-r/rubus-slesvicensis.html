Rubus slesvicensis

Rubus slesvicensis, commonly known as the Slesvician blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are dark purple to black when ripe and are often used in local culinary traditions.
