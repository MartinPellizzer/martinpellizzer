Rubus pentagonus var. eglandulosus

Rubus pentagonus var. eglandulosus, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in woodland areas and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant member of the Rosaceae family.
