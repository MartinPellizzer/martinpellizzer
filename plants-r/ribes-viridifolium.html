Ribes viridifolium

Ribes viridifolium, commonly known as greenleaf gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its finely toothed, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Ribes viridifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
