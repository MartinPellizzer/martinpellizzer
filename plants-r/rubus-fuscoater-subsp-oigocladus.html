Rubus fuscoater subsp. oigocladus

Rubus fuscoater subsp.

oigocladus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to parts of eastern North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
