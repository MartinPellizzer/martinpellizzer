Rourea fasciculata var. flagellifolia

Rourea fasciculata var. flagellifolia, commonly known as the flagellifoliate rourea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
