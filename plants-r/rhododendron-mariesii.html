Rhododendron mariesii

Rhododendron mariesii, commonly known as the Mary's Rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and densely covered with fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
