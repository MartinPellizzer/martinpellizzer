Rubus genevieri microgene brigantinus

Rubus genevieri microgene brigantinus, commonly known as the Brigantine blackberry, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
