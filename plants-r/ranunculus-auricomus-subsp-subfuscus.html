Ranunculus auricomus subsp. subfuscus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

subfuscus, commonly known as the dark-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to heart-shaped with a dark green coloration.

The flowers are typically yellow with 5-7 sepals and 5-7 petals, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is characterized by its short, stout stems and is often found in calcareous soils.
