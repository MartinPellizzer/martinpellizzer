Raphanus niger var. vulgaris

Raphanus niger var. vulgaris, commonly known as black radish, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its robust, fleshy roots that are typically black or dark purple in color. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the mustard family. Its leaves are large, lobed, and have a slightly spicy aroma.

The plant is native to Asia but is now cultivated in various parts of the world for its edible roots and leaves.
