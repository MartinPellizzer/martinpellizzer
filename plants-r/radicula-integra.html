Radicula integra

Radicula integra, commonly known as the complete root plant, is a unique species characterized by its intricate root system that extends deeply into the soil.

It possesses a single, unbranched primary root that serves as the main structural and absorptive organ. The plant's roots are covered in fine, hair-like structures called root hairs, which enhance nutrient and water uptake. Radicula integra thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and is often found in temperate forest environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a model organism for studying root development and soil interaction.
