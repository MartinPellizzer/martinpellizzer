Ranunculus acris f. vulgaris

Ranunculus acris f. vulgaris, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, early spring blooms with bright yellow petals and a prominent central stamen. The plant has palmate leaves that are usually divided into three to five lobes. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other early-season insects.
