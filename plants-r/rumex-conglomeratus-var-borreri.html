Rumex conglomeratus var. borreri

Rumex conglomeratus var. borreri, commonly known as the Borrer's dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a waxy coating, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense terminal spikes. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in dry, disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsules that contain numerous small seeds.

This species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
