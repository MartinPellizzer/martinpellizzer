Rosa × andegavensis var. leiostyla

Rosa × andegavensis var. leiostyla, commonly known as the Andegavensis rose, is a hybrid species originating from the cross between Rosa canina and Rosa rugosa.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it adaptable to different environments. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, providing a lush appearance throughout the growing season.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
