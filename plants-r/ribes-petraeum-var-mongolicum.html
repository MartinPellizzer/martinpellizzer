Ribes petraeum var. mongolicum

Ribes petraeum var. mongolicum, commonly known as the Mongolian gooseberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, tart berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has elliptical leaves with serrated edges and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, arid climates.
