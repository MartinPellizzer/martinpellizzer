Rubus megapolitanus

Rubus megapolitanus, commonly known as the Argentine blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shrublands and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental and edible qualities, it has been cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
