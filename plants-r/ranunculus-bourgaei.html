Ranunculus bourgaei

Ranunculus bourgaei, commonly known as the Bornean buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically palmately lobed, and its flowers consist of five to eight petal-like sepals. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.
