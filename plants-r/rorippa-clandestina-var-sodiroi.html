Rorippa clandestina var. sodiroi

Rorippa clandestina var. sodiroi, commonly known as the Mexican watercress, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to Central America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in wetlands, streams, and rice paddies. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are usually submerged or floating, and it produces tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.

This variety is noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich environments.
