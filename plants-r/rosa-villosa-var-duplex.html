Rosa villosa var. duplex

Rosa villosa var. duplex, commonly known as the double-flowered Rosa villosa, is a cultivar of the Rosa villosa species, which is native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its double blooms, which consist of multiple layers of petals, giving it a more elaborate appearance than single-flowered varieties. The plant typically has thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and floral arrangements.
