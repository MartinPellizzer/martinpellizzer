Ruellia divaricata

Ruellia divaricata, commonly known as the purple mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long blooming period.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
