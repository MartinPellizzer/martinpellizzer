Rheum hissaricum

Rheum hissaricum, commonly known as the Hissar rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. Its stems are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their medicinal properties.

Rheum hissaricum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage.
