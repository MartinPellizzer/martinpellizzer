Rhus bequaertii

Rhus bequaertii, commonly known as the Bequaert's sumac, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 11 to 21 leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are a common food source for birds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and canyons.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
