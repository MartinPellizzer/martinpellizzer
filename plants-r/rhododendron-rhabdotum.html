Rhododendron rhabdotum

Rhododendron rhabdotum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. The plant features dense, evergreen foliage with broad, leathery leaves that are often dark green in color. Its flowers are showy, appearing in clusters and typically in shades of pink or red, blooming in the spring.

Rhododendron rhabdotum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
