Rhapis cordata

Rhapis cordata, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and arching, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Rhapis cordata prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
