Rosa villosa evratiana

Rosa villosa evratiana, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and broad, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
