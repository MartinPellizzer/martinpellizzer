Rubus teregovensis f. macropetalus

Rubus teregovensis f. macropetalus, commonly known as the large-petaled blackberry, is a subspecies of the blackberry plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces dark purple to black berries, which are typically smaller than those of other blackberry species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral features, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
