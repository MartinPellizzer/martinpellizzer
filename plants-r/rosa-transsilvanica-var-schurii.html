Rosa transsilvanica var. schurii

Rosa transsilvanica var. schurii, commonly known as the Transylvanian rose, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in summer, which are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
