Rebutia simoniana

Rebutia simoniana, commonly known as the Simon's euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and fleshy, ribbed stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color, often emerging from the areoles along the stems. Rebutia simoniana is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves and stems.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements and unique appearance.
