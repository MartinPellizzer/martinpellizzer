Ranunculus eschscholtzii var. typicus

Ranunculus eschscholtzii var. typicus, commonly known as the Eschscholtz's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to California.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, often accompanied by a hairy, green sepal-like structure. The plant has a short, rhizomatous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, typically with three to five leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often brightly colored flowers.
