Rubus angustisetus var. subbiserratus

Rubus angustisetus var. subbiserratus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, serrated leaves and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in shrub form, with thorny stems and compound leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them a valuable plant for both ecological and culinary purposes.
