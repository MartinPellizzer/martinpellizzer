Rhododendron squarrosum

Rhododendron squarrosum, commonly known as the squarrosum rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

Rhododendron squarrosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural settings.
