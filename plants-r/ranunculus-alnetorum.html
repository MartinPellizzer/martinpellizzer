Ranunculus alnetorum

Ranunculus alnetorum, commonly known as the water buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland areas of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant thrives in moist or aquatic environments, often found along streams, ditches, and marshes. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common sight in wetland ecosystems.
