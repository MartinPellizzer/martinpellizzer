Ranunculus penicillatus var. vertumnus

Ranunculus penicillatus var. vertumnus, commonly known as the yellow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight bright yellow petals and a hairy, greenish-yellow center. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
