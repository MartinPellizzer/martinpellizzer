Rubia aspera

Rubia aspera, commonly known as rough madder, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties, containing compounds used in traditional medicine.

Rubia aspera thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in woodland edges and open grasslands.
