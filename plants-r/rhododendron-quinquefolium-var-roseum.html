Rhododendron quinquefolium var. roseum

Rhododendron quinquefolium var. roseum, commonly known as the rose-flowered azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The variety 'roseum' is distinguished by its more intense and uniform pink flower color compared to the typical white or pale pink of the species.
