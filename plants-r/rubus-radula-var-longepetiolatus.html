Rubus radula var. longepetiolatus

Rubus radula var. longepetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled dewberry, is a variant of the dewberry species native to eastern North America.

It is a trailing or climbing shrub with thin, wiry stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature, containing several tiny seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive long petiole, which is a characteristic feature of this variety.

The species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
