Rosa pimpinellifolia f. subspinosissima

Rosa pimpinellifolia f. subspinosissima, commonly known as the spiny rock rose, is a botanical variant of the common rock rose.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny thorns and compact growth habit, often forming shrubs. The plant typically blooms with pale pink to white flowers, which appear in clusters. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in horticultural settings.
