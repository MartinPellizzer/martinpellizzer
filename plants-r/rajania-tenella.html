Rajania tenella

Rajania tenella, commonly known as the dwarf arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant typically grows in wet, shady environments such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Rajania tenella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
