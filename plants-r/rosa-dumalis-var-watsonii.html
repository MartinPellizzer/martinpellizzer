Rosa dumalis var. watsonii

Rosa dumalis var. watsonii, commonly known as Watson's rose, is a variant of the shrub rose native to the western United States.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a desirable ornamental plant in horticulture.
