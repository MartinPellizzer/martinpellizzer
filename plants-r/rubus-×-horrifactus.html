Rubus × horrifactus

Rubus × horrifactus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are edible and often used in culinary applications, though they can be quite tart.

The species is native to parts of Asia and has been introduced to other regions for its ornamental and fruiting qualities.
