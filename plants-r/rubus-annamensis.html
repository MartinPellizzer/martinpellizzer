Rubus annamensis

Rubus annamensis, commonly known as the Annam blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam and Laos, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, with thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies in its native region.
