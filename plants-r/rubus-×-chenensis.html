Rubus × chenensis

Rubus × chenensis, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
