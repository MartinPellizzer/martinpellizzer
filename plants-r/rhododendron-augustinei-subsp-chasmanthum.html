Rhododendron augustinei subsp. chasmanthum

Rhododendron augustinei subsp.

chasmanthum, commonly known as the Chasmanthum rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red.

The plant typically grows in cool, moist habitats at high altitudes, often in mountainous forests. Its botanical features include leathery leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a striking ornamental plant.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
