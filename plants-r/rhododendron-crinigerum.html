Rhododendron crinigerum

Rhododendron crinigerum, commonly known as the silverleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that has a distinctive silvery-white underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with acidic soils.

Rhododendron crinigerum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
