Rosa × damascena versicolor

Rosa × damascena versicolor, commonly known as the multicolored rose, is a hybrid species that exhibits a range of flower colors, including pink, white, and red.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its fragrant, often double flowers. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring regular pruning to maintain its shape and promote blooming.

This versatile plant is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and in perfumery due to its aromatic properties.
