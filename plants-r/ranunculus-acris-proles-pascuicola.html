Ranunculus acris proles pascuicola

Ranunculus acris proles pascuicola, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, toothed leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its distinctive floral structure and often toxic sap.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
