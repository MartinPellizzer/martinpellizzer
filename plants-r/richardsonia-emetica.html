Richardsonia emetica

Richardsonia emetica, commonly known as the emetic richardsonia, is a rare and striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant is known for its unique ability to produce a mild emetic compound, which gives it its common name. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a delicate, herbaceous structure adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
