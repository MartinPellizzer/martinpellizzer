Rubus macrocarpus

Rubus macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupelets that turn dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic flavor and is used in culinary applications.
