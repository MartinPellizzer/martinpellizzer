Raputia hirsuta

Raputia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy raputia, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy inflorescences that consist of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered on the spadix, which is often covered in a dense layer of hairs, giving it its common name. Raputia hirsuta thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
