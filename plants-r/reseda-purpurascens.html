Reseda purpurascens

Reseda purpurascens, commonly known as the purple mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Reseda purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
