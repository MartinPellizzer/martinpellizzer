Rubus arctatus

Rubus arctatus, commonly known as the alpine bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of North America, particularly in the western United States and Canada. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and it produces small, edible berries.

Rubus arctatus thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
