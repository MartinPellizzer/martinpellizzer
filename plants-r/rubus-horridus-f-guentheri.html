Rubus horridus f. guentheri

Rubus horridus f. guentheri, commonly known as the Guenther's bramble, is a subspecies of the brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, edible berries. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
