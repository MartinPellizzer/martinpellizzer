Ranunculus auricomus subsp. constans

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

constans, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often with a distinctive golden hue.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The subspecies constans is distinguished by its more compact growth and slightly different leaf morphology compared to the main species.
