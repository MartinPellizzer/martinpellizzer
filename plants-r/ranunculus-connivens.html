Ranunculus connivens

Ranunculus connivens, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and riverbanks. It has a spreading growth habit, with rhizomes that allow it to colonize new areas.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
