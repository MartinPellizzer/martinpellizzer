Ranunculus multipartitus

Ranunculus multipartitus, commonly known as the many-sepal buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with numerous sepals that resemble petals, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy floral structures.
