Rubus parviaraliifolius

Rubus parviaraliifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry.

This shrub is characterized by its slender stems and small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges. It produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupelets, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. The plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, acidic soils.

It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
