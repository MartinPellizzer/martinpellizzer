Rubus rhodophyllus

Rubus rhodophyllus, commonly known as the red-leaf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect or shrub-like growth habit and produces edible berries that are typically red or orange in color. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along the edges of woodlands.
