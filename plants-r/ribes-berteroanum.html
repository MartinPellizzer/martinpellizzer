Ribes berteroanum

Ribes berteroanum, commonly known as the Bertero's gooseberry, is a species of shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of flowering plants commonly found in temperate regions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers.

Ribes berteroanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its historical use in traditional medicine.
