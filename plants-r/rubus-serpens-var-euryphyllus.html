Rubus serpens var. euryphyllus

Rubus serpens var. euryphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed blackberry.

This variant is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and thorny stems. It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically clustered together. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and often cultivated species within the Rubus genus.
