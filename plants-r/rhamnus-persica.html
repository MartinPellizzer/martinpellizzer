Rhamnus persica

Rhamnus persica, commonly known as Persian buckthorn, is a shrub native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers in the spring. The plant is notable for its distinctive, narrow, and often prickly fruits that mature to a reddish-black color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
