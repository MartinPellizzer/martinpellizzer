Rhynchospora gigantea var. scaberrima

Rhynchospora gigantea var. scaberrima, commonly known as the rough spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that bears small, spike-like clusters of pale green to brownish flowers. Rhynchospora gigantea var. scaberrima typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
