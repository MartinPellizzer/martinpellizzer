Ranunculus prolixifidus

Ranunculus prolixifidus, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or orange flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves with deeply lobed or dissected margins, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in meadows and woodland edges.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
