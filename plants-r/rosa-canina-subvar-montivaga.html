Rosa canina subvar. montivaga

Rosa canina subvar. montivaga, commonly known as the alpine rosehip, is a subspecies of the dog rose that thrives in mountainous regions.

It is characterized by its small, pale pink to white flowers and distinctive, elongated, red-orange hips that persist into winter. The plant is adapted to cold climates and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated margins, and the stems are covered in fine, glandular hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
