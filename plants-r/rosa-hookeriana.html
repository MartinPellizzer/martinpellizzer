Rosa hookeriana

Rosa hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include five petals, numerous stamens, and a central pistil, typical of the Rosaceae family.

Rosa hookeriana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
