Rubus × mesaepolius

Rubus × mesaepolius, commonly known as the mesa raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are usually pink or white.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
