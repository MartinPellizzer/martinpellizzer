Rubus hamatus

Rubus hamatus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed raw or used in jams and preserves. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Rubus hamatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
