Ranunculus abortivus var. nitidus

Ranunculus abortivus var. nitidus, commonly known as the glossy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The variety 'nitidus' is distinguished by its smooth, glossy leaves and compact growth habit.
