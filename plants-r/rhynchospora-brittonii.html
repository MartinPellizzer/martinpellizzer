Rhynchospora brittonii

Rhynchospora brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's beakspur, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to reddish in color. It thrives in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it plays a role in maintaining ecological balance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
