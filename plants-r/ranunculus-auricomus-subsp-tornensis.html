Ranunculus auricomus subsp. tornensis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

tornensis, commonly known as the Tornian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and damp woodlands.

The plant features palmately lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is part of the Ranunculaceae family.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
