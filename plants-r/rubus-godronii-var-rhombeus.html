Rubus godronii var. rhombeus

Rubus godronii var. rhombeus, commonly known as the rhomboid blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is native to the western United States, particularly in California and Oregon.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal fruit production.
