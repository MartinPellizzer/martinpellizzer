Rubus × drepanostachys

Rubus × drepanostachys, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry or loganberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the raspberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (the cloudberry).

It is a perennial shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries that are usually dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its tart, flavorful fruit.

The botanical characteristics of Rubus × drepanostachys make it a unique and valuable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
