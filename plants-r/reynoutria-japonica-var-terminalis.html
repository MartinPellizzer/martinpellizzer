Reynoutria japonica var. terminalis

Reynoutria japonica var. terminalis, commonly known as Giant Salvia, is a variant of the Japanese knotweed species.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and large, showy purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.

Due to its vigorous growth, it is considered an invasive species in many regions.
