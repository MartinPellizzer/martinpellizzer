Ranunculus marschlinsii var. grandiflorus

Ranunculus marschlinsii var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered marsh buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to wet meadows and marshy areas in Europe.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy yellow flowers with prominent brown veins. The plant typically blooms in spring and is characterized by its upright growth habit. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
