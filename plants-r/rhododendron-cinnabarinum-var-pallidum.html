Rhododendron cinnabarinum var. pallidum

Rhododendron cinnabarinum var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale flame azalea, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red to orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
