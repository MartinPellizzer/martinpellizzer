Rosa jundzillii var. romanica

Rosa jundzillii var. romanica, commonly known as the Roman rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces oval, leathery leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy species suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

The Roman rose is often valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
