Rhododendron retusum

Rhododendron retusum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a species native to East Asia, particularly Japan and China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside, and it typically grows in shaded, moist environments. Rhododendron retusum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.

It is often cultivated in gardens and used in landscape design due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.
