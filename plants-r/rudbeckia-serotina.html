Rudbeckia serotina

Rudbeckia serotina, commonly known as late black-eyed Susan, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of dark brown to black tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woods.

Rudbeckia serotina is known for its showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
