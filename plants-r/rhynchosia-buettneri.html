Rhynchosia buettneri

Rhynchosia buettneri, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, hairy, spiny pods that enclose its seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Its ecological role includes providing food for various animals and contributing to soil fertility through its legume properties.
