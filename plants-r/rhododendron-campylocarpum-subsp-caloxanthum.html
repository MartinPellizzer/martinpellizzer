Rhododendron campylocarpum subsp. caloxanthum

Rhododendron campylocarpum subsp.

caloxanthum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southeastern China. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its botanical features include small, elliptical leaves and a compact growth habit.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
