Rigiolepis lanceolata f. elliptica

Rigiolepis lanceolata f. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic leaf form of the common groundsel, is a variant of the annual herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features elongated, elliptical leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it easily distinguishable from other members of the genus.
