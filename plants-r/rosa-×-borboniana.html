Rosa × borboniana

Rosa × borboniana, commonly known as the Bourbon rose, is a hybrid species originating from the cross between Rosa damascena and Rosa gallica.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to deep pink. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many rose species. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

Rosa × borboniana is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in perfumery and horticulture.
