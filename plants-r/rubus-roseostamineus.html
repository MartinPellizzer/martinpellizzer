Rubus roseostamineus

Rubus roseostamineus, commonly known as the rose-stamen raspberry.

This shrub is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the first year's growth. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor.

Its botanical features include hairy stems, trifoliate leaves, and flowers with five pink petals.
