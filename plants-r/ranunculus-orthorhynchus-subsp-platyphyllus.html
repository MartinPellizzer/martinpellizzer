Ranunculus orthorhynchus subsp. platyphyllus

Ranunculus orthorhynchus subsp.

platyphyllus, commonly known as the flatleaf buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a short stem and produces achenes as its fruit.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
