Rostraria trachyantha

Rostraria trachyantha, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a striking red throat. The plant has a woody stem and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient species suitable for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
