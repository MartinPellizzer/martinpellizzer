Rubus discolor var. cuneifrons

Rubus discolor var. cuneifrons, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or purple fruits that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in the axils of the leaves.

The berries are usually harvested in late summer and are prized for their tart flavor and use in culinary applications.
