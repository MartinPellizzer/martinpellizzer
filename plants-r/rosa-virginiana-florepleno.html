Rosa virginiana florepleno

Rosa virginiana florepleno, commonly known as the full-flowered Virginia rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial suitable for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in native plant communities.
