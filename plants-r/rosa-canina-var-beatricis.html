Rosa canina var. beatricis

Rosa canina var. beatricis, commonly known as the Beatrix rose, is a rare and cultivated variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its delicate pink flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa canina species. The plant is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges. Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, red, edible fruits known as hips.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and historical significance in botanical gardens.
