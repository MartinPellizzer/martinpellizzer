Rubus koehleri var. spinulifer

Rubus koehleri var. spinulifer, commonly known as the spiny bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
