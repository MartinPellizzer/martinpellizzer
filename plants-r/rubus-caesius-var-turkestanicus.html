Rubus caesius var. turkestanicus

Rubus caesius var. turkestanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan raspberry, is a variant of the European raspberry native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other Rubus species, particularly in its geographic distribution and fruit morphology.
