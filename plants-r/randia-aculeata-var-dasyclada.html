Randia aculeata var. dasyclada

Randia aculeata var. dasyclada, commonly known as the hairy randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
