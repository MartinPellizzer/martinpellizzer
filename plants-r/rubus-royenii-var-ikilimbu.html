Rubus royenii var. ikilimbu

Rubus royenii var. ikilimbu, commonly known as the East African blackberry, is a shrub native to East Africa.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded forest understories.

The berries are valued for their flavor and are sometimes used in local cuisines.
