Rosa semineoburgensis

Rosa semineoburgensis, commonly known as the Seminoburg rose, is a rare and ancient species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, thornless stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces oval, hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, temperate climate to flourish.

Due to its rarity and historical significance, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
