Rhaphidophora palauensis

Rhaphidophora palauensis, commonly known as the Palau staghorn fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the island of Palau in the Western Pacific.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that form a dense, crinkled rosette, providing a habitat for small organisms. The plant's rhizomes are thick and fibrous, anchoring it to trees or rocks in its natural environment. It thrives in humid, shaded conditions and requires high levels of air circulation and moisture.

Rhaphidophora palauensis is valued for its ornamental appearance and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
