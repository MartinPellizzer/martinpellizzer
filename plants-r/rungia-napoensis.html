Rungia napoensis

Rungia napoensis, commonly known as the Napo runge, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understorey habitats. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Rungia napoensis produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
