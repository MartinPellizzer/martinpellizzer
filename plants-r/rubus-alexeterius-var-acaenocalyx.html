Rubus alexeterius var. acaenocalyx

Rubus alexeterius var. acaenocalyx, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are oval to lanceolate in shape. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Rubus genus. Its fruits, which are aggregate berries, are typically dark purple to black when mature.

This plant is native to specific regions in eastern Asia and is often found in woodland or scrubland habitats.
