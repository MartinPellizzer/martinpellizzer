Rhododendron wrightianum var. wrightianum

Rhododendron wrightianum var. wrightianum, commonly known as the Wright's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its botanical features include leathery leaves and a compact growth habit, making it well-suited for woodland gardens.

It is often valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler, humid climates.
