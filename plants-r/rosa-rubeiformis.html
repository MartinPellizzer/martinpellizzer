Rosa rubeiformis

Rosa rubeiformis, commonly known as the crimson-form red rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals that resemble a blood-red form, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dense foliage. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

Rosa rubeiformis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
