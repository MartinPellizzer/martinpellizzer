Rumex glomeratus

Rumex glomeratus, commonly known as clustered sorrel, is a flowering plant in the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, reddish leaves and produces dense clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, calcareous soils. It has a short growing season and is typically found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and often overlooked member of the sorrel genus.
