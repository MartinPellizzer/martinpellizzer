Rubus × agrestis var. resepalus

Rubus × agrestis var. resepalus, commonly known as the bramble rose or wild bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically oval to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are usually red but can also be yellow or orange in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it both a valuable forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
