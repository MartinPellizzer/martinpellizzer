Rosa communis subsp. stylosa

Rosa communis subsp.

stylosa, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species native to China and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. It features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink or white, with a strong, sweet fragrance.

The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for centuries in traditional Chinese horticulture.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
