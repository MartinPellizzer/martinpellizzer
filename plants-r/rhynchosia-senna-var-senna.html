Rhynchosia senna var. senna

Rhynchosia senna var. senna, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, slender pods containing several seeds. It is a warm-season annual that thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and as a forage crop for livestock.
