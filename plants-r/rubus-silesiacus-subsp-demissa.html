Rubus silesiacus subsp. demissa

Rubus silesiacus subsp.

demissa, commonly known as the Silesian blackberry, is a subspecies native to Central Europe. It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
