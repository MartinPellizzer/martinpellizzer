Rosa gallica f. virescens

Rosa gallica f. virescens, commonly known as the green-flowered gallica rose, is a subspecies of the gallica rose native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, almost black foliage and its distinctive, pale green to white flowers. The plant is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. It produces fragrant, five-petaled blooms in late spring to early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
