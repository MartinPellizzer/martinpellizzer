Rhamnus frangula var. autumnalis

Rhamnus frangula var. autumnalis, commonly known as autumn buckthorn, is a shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are rich in anthocyanins. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
