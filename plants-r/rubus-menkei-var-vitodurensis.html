Rubus menkei var. vitodurensis

Rubus menkei var. vitodurensis, commonly known as the Hainan blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated margins and typically grows in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it is valued for its edible fruits.

This species is also notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
