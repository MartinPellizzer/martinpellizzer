Ranunculus auricomus subsp. acarpellophorus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

acarpellophorus, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals that are often reflexed backward.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are typically hairy and covered in a waxy cuticle. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is also considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
