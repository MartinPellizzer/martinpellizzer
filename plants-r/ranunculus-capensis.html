Ranunculus capensis

Ranunculus capensis, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a waxy coating.

Ranunculus capensis is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
