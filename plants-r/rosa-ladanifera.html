Rosa ladanifera

Rosa ladanifera, commonly known as the fragrant rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and serrated, providing a lush green backdrop to the blossoms.

Rosa ladanifera is valued for its aromatic fragrance and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
