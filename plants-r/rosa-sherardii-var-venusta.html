Rosa sherardii var. venusta

Rosa sherardii var. venusta, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has arching, thornless stems and lance-shaped, dark green leaves with serrated edges. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticultural traditions.
