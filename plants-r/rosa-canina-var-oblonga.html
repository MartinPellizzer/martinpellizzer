Rosa canina var. oblonga

Rosa canina var. oblonga, commonly known as the oval hips rose, is a variant of the dog rose, characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped fruits.

This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are typically pink to white, with five petals, and it produces fragrant, orange-red hips in the autumn. The leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
