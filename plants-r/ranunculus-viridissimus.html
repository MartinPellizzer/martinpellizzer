Ranunculus viridissimus

Ranunculus viridissimus, commonly known as the greenleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ranunculus viridissimus is often found in calcareous soils and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
