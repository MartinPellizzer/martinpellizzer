Rubus thyrsanthus var. parvifolius

Rubus thyrsanthus var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny, dark purple to black drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
