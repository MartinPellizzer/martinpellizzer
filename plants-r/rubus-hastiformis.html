Rubus hastiformis

Rubus hastiformis, commonly known as the hastate raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically three-lobed and have a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rubus hastiformis plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
