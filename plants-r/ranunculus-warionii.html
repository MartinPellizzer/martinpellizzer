Ranunculus warionii

Ranunculus warionii, commonly known as the Kashmiri buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, typically with a yellow center. The plant has delicate, trifoliate leaves that are palmately arranged, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas at high altitudes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
