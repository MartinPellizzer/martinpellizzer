Rhododendron hylaeum

Rhododendron hylaeum, commonly known as the Hylaeum rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract various pollinators.

Rhododendron hylaeum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific care to flourish.
