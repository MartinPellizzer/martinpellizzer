Rhododendron × hybridogenum

Rhododendron × hybridogenum, commonly known as the hybrid rhododendron, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Rhododendron catawbiense and Rhododendron maximum.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, often fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of 2 to 6 meters, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is native to the eastern United States and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical classification highlights its role as a hybrid species in the Ericaceae family, contributing to the diversity of rhododendron hybrids.
