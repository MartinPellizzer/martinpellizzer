Rhododendron amanoi

Rhododendron amanoi, commonly known as the Amanoi rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, thriving in acidic soil conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron amanoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
