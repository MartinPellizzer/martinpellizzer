Ranunculus montanus subsp. ruscinonensis

Ranunculus montanus subsp.

ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found at high altitudes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
