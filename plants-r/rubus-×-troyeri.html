Rubus × troyeri

Rubus × troyeri, commonly known as the Evergreen Blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
