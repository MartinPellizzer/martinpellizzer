Rosa cladocampta

Rosa cladocampta, commonly known as the creeping shrub rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit with thorny stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with a serrated edge.

Rosa cladocampta is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
