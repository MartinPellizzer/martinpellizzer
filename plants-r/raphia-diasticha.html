Raphia diasticha

Raphia diasticha, commonly known as the giant palm or African fan palm, is a large, fan-shaped palm native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its massive, umbrella-like leaves that can reach lengths of up to 6 meters. The plant produces long, slender fruit clusters that contain edible, starchy seeds. Raphia diasticha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or riverine habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and economic purposes in its native range.
