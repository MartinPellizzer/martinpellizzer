Rhododendron curviflorum

Rhododendron curviflorum, commonly known as the curlflower rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include evergreen to deciduous foliage, depending on the climate, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Rhododendron curviflorum is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
