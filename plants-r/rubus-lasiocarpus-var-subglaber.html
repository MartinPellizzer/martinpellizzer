Rubus lasiocarpus var. subglaber

Rubus lasiocarpus var. subglaber, commonly known as the smooth-fruited blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, contributing to local biodiversity.
