Rubus × barthianus

Rubus × barthianus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are composed of small drupelets. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

This species is valued for its edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications.
