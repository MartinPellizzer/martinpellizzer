Rhamnus coriophylla var. coriophylla

Rhamnus coriophylla var. coriophylla, commonly known as the glossy buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that turn reddish in autumn, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its smooth, bark that becomes fissured with age, and it has small, oval berries that are dark red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
