Rosa sylvestris var. subbibracteata

Rosa sylvestris var. subbibracteata, commonly known as the lesser shrub rose, is a species of wild rose native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant features thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are often borne singly or in small clusters, and it produces red, hairy fruits known as rose hips.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
