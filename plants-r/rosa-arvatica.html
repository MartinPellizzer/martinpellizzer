Rosa arvatica

Rosa arvatica, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are covered in thorns.

Rosa arvatica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
