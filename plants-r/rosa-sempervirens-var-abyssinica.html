Rosa sempervirens var. abyssinica

Rosa sempervirens var. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian rose, is a shrub-like plant native to Ethiopia.

It features dense, thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
