Rabiea

Rabiea, commonly known as the African orchid, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. Rabiea species typically grow in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun to partial shade. The plants have strap-like leaves and produce small, clustered flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months.

They are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
