Roegneria formosana var. longiaristata

Roegneria formosana var. longiaristata, commonly known as the long-awned wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, each bearing several awned lemmas, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
