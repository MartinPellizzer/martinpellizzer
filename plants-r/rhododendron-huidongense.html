Rhododendron huidongense

Rhododendron huidongense, commonly known as the Huidong rhododendron, is a species native to China, specifically the Huidong region in Guangdong Province.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in mountainous areas with acidic soil and high humidity. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are dark green and elliptical in shape. Its flowers are showy, with five to six petals that are usually pink or red, blooming in spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the local biodiversity of its native habitat.
