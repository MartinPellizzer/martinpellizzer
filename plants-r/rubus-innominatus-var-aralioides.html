Rubus innominatus var. aralioides

Rubus innominatus var. aralioides, commonly known as the alpine bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregated fruits that resemble raspberries. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and well-drained soils. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

It plays an important ecological role in alpine ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
