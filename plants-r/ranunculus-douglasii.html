Ranunculus douglasii

Ranunculus douglasii, commonly known as Douglas' buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ranunculaceae.

It is native to western North America, particularly found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often accompanied by numerous stamens. Its leaves are typically deeply lobed and have a distinctive, sometimes hairy texture.

Douglas' buttercup is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
