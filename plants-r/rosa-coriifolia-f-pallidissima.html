Rosa coriifolia f. pallidissima

Rosa coriifolia f. pallidissima, commonly known as the pale-flowered shrub rose, is a cultivar of the shrub rose species.

It is characterized by its pale pink to almost white flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and have a delicate, fragrant aroma.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its elegant appearance.
