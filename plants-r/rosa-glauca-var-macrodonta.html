Rosa glauca var. macrodonta

Rosa glauca var. macrodonta, commonly known as the large-flowered blue rose, is a rare and striking variant of the blue rose species.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from pale blue to deep violet, with a distinctive bluish-green hue. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is known for its cold hardiness and adaptability to well-drained soils. Its foliage is dark green and slightly fuzzy, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its rarity and unique coloration, it is highly valued in horticultural collections and botanical gardens.
