Rubus thyrsiflorus proles arduennensis

Rubus thyrsiflorus proles arduennensis, commonly known as the Arduenna thornbush, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated margins, while its flowers are pink to white and appear in clusters.

This species is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for wildlife.
