Rollinia permensis

Rollinia permensis, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, while the flowers are small and white with a fragrant aroma.

The fruit, which is edible, has a unique shape and is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
