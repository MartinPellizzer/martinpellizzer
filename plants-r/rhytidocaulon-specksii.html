Rhytidocaulon specksii

Rhytidocaulon specksii, commonly known as the speckled groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its unique speckled appearance is due to the presence of small, raised dots on the leaves, which are specialized structures called trichomes.

Rhytidocaulon specksii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
