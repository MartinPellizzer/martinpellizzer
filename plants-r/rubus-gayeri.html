Rubus gayeri

Rubus gayeri, commonly known as Gayer's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it blooms with white to pink flowers in the spring.

Rubus gayeri is often found in disturbed soils and is an important plant for wildlife in its native habitat.
