Racosperma praetermissum

Racosperma praetermissum, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. Its seeds are encased in pods and can remain viable in the soil for extended periods.

Racosperma praetermissum plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
