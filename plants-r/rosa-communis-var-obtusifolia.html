Rosa communis var. obtusifolia

Rosa communis var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaf rose, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and broad, ovate leaves with rounded tips. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and a woody stem structure typical of many rose species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
