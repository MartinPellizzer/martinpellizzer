Ranunculus vastisectus

Ranunculus vastisectus, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous small seeds.

Ranunculus vastisectus is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
