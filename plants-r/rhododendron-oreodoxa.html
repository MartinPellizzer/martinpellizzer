Rhododendron oreodoxa

Rhododendron oreodoxa, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in Nepal, Bhutan, and northern India.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in humid, cool climates. The plant features large, showy flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a dense canopy that supports a variety of wildlife.

Rhododendron oreodoxa plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to soil stabilization and offering nectar to pollinators.
