Rubus × suberectiformis

Rubus × suberectiformis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and persistent foliage.

It typically features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually dark purple to black in color. It is often found in temperate regions, thriving in well-drained soils and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
