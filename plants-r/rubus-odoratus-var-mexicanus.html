Rubus odoratus var. mexicanus

Rubus odoratus var. mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican raspberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or dry soil conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
