Rubus hirtus var. gracilescens

Rubus hirtus var. gracilescens, commonly known as the slender bramble, is a shrub native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has thorny stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually serrated and have a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, white to pinkish, and appear in clusters, followed by red, edible berries.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
