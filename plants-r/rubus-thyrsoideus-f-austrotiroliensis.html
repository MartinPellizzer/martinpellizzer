Rubus thyrsoideus f. austrotiroliensis

Rubus thyrsoideus f. austrotiroliensis, commonly known as the Austrian bramble, is a flowering plant species native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring partial shade to full sun.

This subspecies is notable for its horticultural value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
