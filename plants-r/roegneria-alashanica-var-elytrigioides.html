Roegneria alashanica var. elytrigioides

Roegneria alashanica var. elytrigioides, commonly known as the Alashan grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of many grass species. It is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy plant in its native habitat.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local grassland ecosystems.
