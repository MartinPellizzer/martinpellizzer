Rubus thyrsoideus var. castrensis

Rubus thyrsoideus var. castrensis, commonly known as the Swedish blackberry, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
