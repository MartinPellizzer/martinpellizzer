Restio intermedius

Restio intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate restio, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, typically during the summer months. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
