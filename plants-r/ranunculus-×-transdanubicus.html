Ranunculus × transdanubicus

Ranunculus × transdanubicus, commonly known as the Transdanubian buttercup, is a hybrid species within the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, radially symmetrical flowers with 5-15 petals, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant produces a basal rosette of leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is native to Central Europe.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
