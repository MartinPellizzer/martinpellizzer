Rebutia einsteinii var. columnaris

Rebutia einsteinii var. columnaris, commonly known as the columnar cactus, is a unique variety of the Rebutia genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow growth habit, forming a columnar shape rather than the typical rounded form of other Rebutia species. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires ample sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Due to its distinctive appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
