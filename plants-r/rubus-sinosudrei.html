Rubus sinosudrei

Rubus sinosudrei, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many shrubs with thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates.

The plant is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
