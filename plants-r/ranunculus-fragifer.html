Ranunculus fragifer

Ranunculus fragifer, commonly known as the fragrant buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive, fragrant center, often surrounded by 5 to 15 petal-like sepals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Ranunculus fragifer is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
