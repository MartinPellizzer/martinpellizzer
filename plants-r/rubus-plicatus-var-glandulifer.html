Rubus plicatus var. glandulifer

Rubus plicatus var. glandulifer, commonly known as the glandular bramble, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are typically red and aggregate, formed from multiple tiny drupes.

The species is notable for its glandular hairs on the leaves and stems, which contribute to its distinctive appearance and ecological interactions.
