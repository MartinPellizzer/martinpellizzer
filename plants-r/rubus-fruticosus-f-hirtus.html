Rubus fruticosus f. hirtus

Rubus fruticosus f. hirtus, commonly known as brambles or blackberries, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small white flowers that develop into edible berries. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in a variety of soil types. Its berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The hirtus form is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage and robust growth habit.
