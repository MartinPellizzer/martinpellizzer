Rubus × pubidorsus

Rubus × pubidorsus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry bush, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to various regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small white or pink flowers in the spring.

This species is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
