Rosa conjuncta

Rosa conjuncta, commonly known as the clustered rose, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and China. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with multiple stems that often form dense clusters. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and a fragrant scent.

Rosa conjuncta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
