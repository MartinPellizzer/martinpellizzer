Rhipsalis chrysocarpa

Rhipsalis chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to Brazil.

It features long, slender stems that are often branched and covered in small, white spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

This plant is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees in tropical rainforests.
