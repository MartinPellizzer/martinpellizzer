Rubus humei

Rubus humei, commonly known as the Hume's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs commonly referred to as blackberries or raspberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

The botanical features of Rubus humei make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
