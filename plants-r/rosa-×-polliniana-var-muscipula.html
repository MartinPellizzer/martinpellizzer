Rosa × polliniana var. muscipula

Rosa × polliniana var. muscipula, commonly known as the sweet william rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically reaches a height of 20 to 30 centimeters and has dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens.
