Ranunculus bolivianus

Ranunculus bolivianus, commonly known as the Bolivian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, radially symmetrical flowers. The plant typically has finely divided, trifoliate leaves and produces bright yellow or orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is also threatened by habitat loss.
