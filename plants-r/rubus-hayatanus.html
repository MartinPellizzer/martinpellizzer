Rubus hayatanus

Rubus hayatanus, commonly known as the Hayata's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Japan and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small white or pink flowers. Its fruits, which are aggregate berries, are typically red when mature and can be edible.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
