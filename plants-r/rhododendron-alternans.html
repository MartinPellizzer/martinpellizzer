Rhododendron alternans

Rhododendron alternans, commonly known as the variable rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in shady, moist habitats, such as forest understories, where it thrives in acidic soils. Its leaves are opposite and leathery, providing a durable structure that helps it withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Rhododendron alternans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
