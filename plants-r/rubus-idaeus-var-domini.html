Rubus idaeus var. domini

Rubus idaeus var. domini, commonly known as the Domini raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It features prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupes, each containing a single seed. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens.

This variety is often cultivated for its flavorful berries, which are rich in antioxidants and vitamins.
