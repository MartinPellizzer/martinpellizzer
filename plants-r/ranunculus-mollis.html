Ranunculus mollis

Ranunculus mollis, commonly known as the soft buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a hairy center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to the landscape.

Ranunculus mollis is known for its ability to thrive in cool climates and its preference for well-drained, loamy soils.
