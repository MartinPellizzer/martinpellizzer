Rosa villosa var. typica

Rosa villosa var. typica, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy gray foliage and fragrant pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has thorny stems and produces rose hips that are red and berry-like in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable.
