Radicula pinnata

Radicula pinnata, commonly known as the featherleaf radicula, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and delicate, pinnately divided leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Radicula pinnata is often found in moist, sandy soils along coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various small organisms.
