Ruellia geminiflora var. humilis

Ruellia geminiflora var. humilis, commonly known as the Mexican bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a low shrub, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
