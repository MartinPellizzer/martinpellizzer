Rubus × conspicuiformis

Rubus × conspicuiformis, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus idaeus, resulting in a species with unique characteristics. It is native to the western United States and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are often used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it is notable for its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical features of the Rubus genus.
