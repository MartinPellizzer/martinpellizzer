Rosa rubiginosa var. pubera

Rosa rubiginosa var. pubera, commonly known as the rough rose or pubescent rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C and are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, velvety texture.

This species is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
