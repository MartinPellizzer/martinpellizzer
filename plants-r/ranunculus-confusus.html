Ranunculus confusus

Ranunculus confusus, commonly known as the confused buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and meadows. The plant features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals. Its flowers are often surrounded by a ring of sepals that resemble additional petals, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Ranunculus confusus is known for its ability to thrive in cool climates and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
