Ranunculus retusus

Ranunculus retusus, commonly known as the blunt-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with five to eight petals. The plant has smooth, waxy leaves that are often lobed or divided, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Ranunculus retusus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic sap.

It is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
