Rosa graveolens f. jordanii

Rosa graveolens f. jordanii, commonly known as the Jordanian rose, is a fragrant shrub native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It produces showy, pink to red flowers with a strong, sweet aroma, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

Rosa graveolens f. jordanii is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
