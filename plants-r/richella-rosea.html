Richella rosea

Richella rosea, commonly known as the pink sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Richella rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
