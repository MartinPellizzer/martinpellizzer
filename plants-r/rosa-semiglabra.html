Rosa semiglabra

Rosa semiglabra, commonly known as the smooth rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, thornless stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The leaves are alternate, with ovate to elliptic leaflets that are usually covered in fine hairs. This plant is native to parts of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
