Rosa canina var. frondosa

Rosa canina var. frondosa, commonly known as the shrub rose or dog rose, is a variant of the common rose native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and pinnate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in early summer.

Its fruits, known as hips, are orange-red, hairy, and contain a high concentration of vitamin C. This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also valued for its medicinal properties.
