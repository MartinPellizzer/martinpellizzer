Rosa rupincola

Rosa rupincola, commonly known as the rock rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Mediterranean. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of pink to red flowers. Its leaves are pinnately lobed, and the stems are covered in fine, glandular hairs.

Rosa rupincola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to rocky or poor soil conditions.
