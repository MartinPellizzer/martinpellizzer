Renealmia amoena

Renealmia amoena, commonly known as the pink marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in swampy or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in its native environment.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
