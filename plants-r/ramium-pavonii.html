Ramium pavonii

Ramium pavonii, commonly known as the rainbow grass, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. Ramium pavonii is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
