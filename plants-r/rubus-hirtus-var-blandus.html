Rubus hirtus var. blandus

Rubus hirtus var. blandus, commonly known as the soft-haired raspberry.

This variant is characterized by its soft, fuzzy stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other Rubus species. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
