Rosa pomifera subsp. gaudinii

Rosa pomifera subsp.

gaudinii, commonly known as the Gaudin's rose, is a rare and distinctive subspecies native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its large, fuzzy, rose-like fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in thorns.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and historical significance.
