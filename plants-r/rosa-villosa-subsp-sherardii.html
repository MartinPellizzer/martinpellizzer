Rosa villosa subsp. sherardii

Rosa villosa subsp.

sherardii, commonly known as the Sherard's bristly rose, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in native plant gardens.
