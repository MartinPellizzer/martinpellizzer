Rubus × chnoophyllus f. proceriformis

Rubus × chnoophyllus f. proceriformis, commonly known as the slender-leaf blackberry.

This hybrid plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. It produces small, dark berries that are often used in culinary applications. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
