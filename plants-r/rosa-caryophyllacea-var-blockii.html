Rosa caryophyllacea var. blockii

Rosa caryophyllacea var. blockii, commonly known as the Block's rose, is a shrub-like perennial native to the Canary Islands.

It features fragrant, semi-double pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
