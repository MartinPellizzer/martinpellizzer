Rubus tereticaulis var. macelliformis

Rubus tereticaulis var. macelliformis, commonly known as the long-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant produces compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and is sometimes cultivated for its unique flavor and appearance.
