Rosa parvifolia

Rosa parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf shrub rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
