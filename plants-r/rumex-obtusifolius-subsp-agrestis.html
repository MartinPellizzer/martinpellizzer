Rumex obtusifolius subsp. agrestis

Rumex obtusifolius subsp.

agrestis, commonly known as rough dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a wavy margin and a distinctive rough texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, reddish-purple clusters, which appear in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
