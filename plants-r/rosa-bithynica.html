Rosa bithynica

Rosa bithynica, commonly known as the Bithynian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically has thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Rosa bithynica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small wildlife.
