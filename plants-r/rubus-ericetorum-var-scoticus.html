Rubus ericetorum var. scoticus

Rubus ericetorum var. scoticus, commonly known as Scottish blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
