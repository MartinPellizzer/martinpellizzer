Rhus argyi

Rhus argyi, commonly known as Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often divided into several oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Rhus argyi thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
