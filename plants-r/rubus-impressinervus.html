Rubus impressinervus

Rubus impressinervus, commonly known as the narrow-veined blackberry.

It is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic with prominent, parallel veins. It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it grows in forested areas.

The plant is valued for its edible berries, which are often used in culinary applications.
