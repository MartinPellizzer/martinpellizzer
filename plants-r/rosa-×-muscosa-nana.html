Rosa × muscosa nana

Rosa × muscosa nana, commonly known as the dwarf muscosa rose, is a compact, bushy shrub that exhibits a variety of flower colors including white, pink, and red.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa multiflora, showcasing traits from both parent plants. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by serrated, dark green leaves. It is well-suited for gardens due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.

The dwarf muscosa rose is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resistance to pests and diseases.
