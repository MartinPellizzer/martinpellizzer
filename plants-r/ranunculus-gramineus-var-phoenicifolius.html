Ranunculus gramineus var. phoenicifolius

Ranunculus gramineus var. phoenicifolius, commonly known as the golden shield or golden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often a vibrant green, and it produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual element to its habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, showy blooms.
