Rorippa sylvestris var. stenocarpa

Rorippa sylvestris var. stenocarpa, commonly known as narrow-fruited watercress, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in moist or aquatic environments, such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its fruits are elongated and cylindrical, distinguishing it from other varieties of Rorippa.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a leaf vegetable in some cuisines.
