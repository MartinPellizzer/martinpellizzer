Ranunculus auricomus subsp. illudens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

illudens, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often adorned with a distinctive golden hue.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with finely divided lobes, and it produces a cluster of small, greenish-yellow sepals beneath the petals.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early spring blooms.
