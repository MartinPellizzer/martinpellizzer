Rhododendron × praecox

Rhododendron × praecox, commonly known as the early rhododendron, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to eastern Asia, including regions of China, Japan, and Korea. Its foliage consists of dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern.

Rhododendron × praecox thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
