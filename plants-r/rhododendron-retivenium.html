Rhododendron retivenium

Rhododendron retivenium, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy, and it produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring. Rhododendron retivenium is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in the study of alpine flora and ecological adaptation.
