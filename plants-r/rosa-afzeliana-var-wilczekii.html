Rosa afzeliana var. wilczekii

Rosa afzeliana var. wilczekii, commonly known as the Wilczek's rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its botanical structure includes compound leaves with serrated edges and multiple flower clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
