Rubus glandithyrsos var. incisus

Rubus glandithyrsos var. incisus, commonly known as the serrated blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, dark purple to black berries. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges that give the plant its common name. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

It is valued for its edible fruit and is sometimes cultivated for both its berries and ornamental value.
