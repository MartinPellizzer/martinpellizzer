Rosa communis var. pseudomucronulata

Rosa communis var. pseudomucronulata, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a cultivar characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, single or semi-double flowers.

It typically features deep pink to red petals with a slightly fragrant scent, and its foliage is dark green and glossy. The plant produces thorned stems and is well-suited for container gardening or small garden spaces. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring regular watering and pruning for optimal blooming.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to temperate climates.
