Rubus hirtus var. melanopsis

Rubus hirtus var. melanopsis, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into dark purple to black berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
