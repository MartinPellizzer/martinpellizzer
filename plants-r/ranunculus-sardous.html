Ranunculus sardous

Ranunculus sardous, commonly known as the Sardinian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces a cluster of small, greenish flowers that bloom in spring.

The species is considered rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
