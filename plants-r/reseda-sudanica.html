Reseda sudanica

Reseda sudanica, commonly known as Sudanese reseda, is a flowering plant native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Reseda sudanica is often used in traditional medicine and has potential applications in herbal remedies.
