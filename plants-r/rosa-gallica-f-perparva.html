Rosa gallica f. perparva

Rosa gallica f. perparva, commonly known as the miniature Gallic rose, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Europe.

It is a subspecies of Rosa gallica, characterized by its small, bushy growth habit and profuse blooming of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves that provide a dense, attractive foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden borders or containers.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a symbol of love and purity adds to its cultural significance.
