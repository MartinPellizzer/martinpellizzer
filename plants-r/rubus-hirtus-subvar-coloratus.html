Rubus hirtus subvar. coloratus

Rubus hirtus subvar. coloratus, commonly known as the purple-fruited blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to purple, depending on maturity. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible berries, though it is less commonly cultivated than other blackberry varieties.
