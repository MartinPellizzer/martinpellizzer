Rubus ochracanthus

Rubus ochracanthus, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
