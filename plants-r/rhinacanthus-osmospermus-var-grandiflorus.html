Rhinacanthus osmospermus var. grandiflorus

Rhinacanthus osmospermus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is rough and fissured. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
