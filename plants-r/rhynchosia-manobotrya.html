Rhynchosia manobotrya

Rhynchosia manobotrya, commonly known as the African burr bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces yellow flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. The plant is characterized by its thorned stems and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields. It is a member of the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Rhynchosia manobotrya is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
