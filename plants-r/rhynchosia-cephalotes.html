Rhynchosia cephalotes

Rhynchosia cephalotes, commonly known as the helmet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive yellow-orange coloration, and its flowers are typically bright yellow with a prominent violet or purple calyx. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Rhynchosia cephalotes is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and thrives in warm, humid climates.
