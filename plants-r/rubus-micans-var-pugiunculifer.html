Rubus micans var. pugiunculifer

Rubus micans var. pugiunculifer, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is typically found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

The shrub has thorny stems and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
