Rosa maracandica

Rosa maracandica, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species of shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, or red. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double blooms that are highly ornamental. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa maracandica is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
