Ranunculus rubrocalyx subsp. stewartii

Ranunculus rubrocalyx subsp.

stewartii, commonly known as the Stewart's red-flowered buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its deep red petals and yellow stamens, which contribute to its ornamental value. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

The plant's botanical features include a hairy stem and palmate leaves with a waxy surface.
