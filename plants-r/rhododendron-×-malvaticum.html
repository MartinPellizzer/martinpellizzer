Rhododendron × malvaticum

Rhododendron × malvaticum, commonly known as the Malvaticum rhododendron, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, visually striking plant.

The species is also noted for its ability to adapt to various climatic conditions, contributing to its widespread cultivation.
