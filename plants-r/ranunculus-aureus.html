Ranunculus aureus

Ranunculus aureus, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often surrounded by a ring of pale yellow sepals. The plant has a short, upright stem and trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated by bees and other insects.
