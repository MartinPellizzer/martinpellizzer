Rapanea carolinensis

Rapanea carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
