Racosperma aphanocladum

Racosperma aphanocladum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its simple, narrow leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
