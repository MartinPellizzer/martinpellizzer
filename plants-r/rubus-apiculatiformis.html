Rubus apiculatiformis

Rubus apiculatiformis, commonly known as the serrated blackberry.

This species is a member of the Rosaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States. It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black achenes.

Its botanical features make it distinct within the Rubus genus, particularly due to its unique leaf morphology and fruit structure.
