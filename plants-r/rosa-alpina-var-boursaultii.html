Rosa alpina var. boursaultii

Rosa alpina var. boursaultii, commonly known as the Alpine rose or Boursault's rose, is a subspecies of the alpine rose native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorned stems. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

This plant is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
