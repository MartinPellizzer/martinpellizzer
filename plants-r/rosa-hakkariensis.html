Rosa hakkariensis

Rosa hakkariensis, commonly known as the Hakkari rose, is a species of flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and it is adapted to grow in temperate climates.

Rosa hakkariensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in local ecosystems.
