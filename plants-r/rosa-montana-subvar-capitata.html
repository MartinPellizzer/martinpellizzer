Rosa montana subvar. capitata

Rosa montana subvar. capitata, commonly known as the mountain rose or alpine rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright, woody stems with dense, dark green foliage that turns reddish in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for alpine gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular plant in horticulture.
