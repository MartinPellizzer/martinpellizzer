Rosa grossidens

Rosa grossidens, commonly known as the large-flowered rose, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its botanical features include fragrant flowers with five to seven petals and numerous stamens.

Rosa grossidens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
