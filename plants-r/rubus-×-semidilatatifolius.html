Rubus × semidilatatifolius

Rubus × semidilatatifolius, commonly known as the hybrid raspberry or blackberry, is a species resulting from the crossbreeding of Rubus ursinus and Rubus chamaemorus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, acidic soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic diversity and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
