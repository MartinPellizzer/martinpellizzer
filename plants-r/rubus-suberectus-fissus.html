Rubus suberectus fissus

Rubus suberectus fissus, commonly known as the fissured blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

This species is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
