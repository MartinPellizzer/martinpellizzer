Rubus versistipulatus var. ribifolius

Rubus versistipulatus var. ribifolius, commonly known as the red-leaf raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-veined leaves and small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny drupes, which are often used in culinary applications due to their tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
