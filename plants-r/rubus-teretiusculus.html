Rubus teretiusculus

Rubus teretiusculus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
