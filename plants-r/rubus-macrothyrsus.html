Rubus macrothyrsus

Rubus macrothyrsus, commonly known as the large-throated blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they bloom in the spring.

Rubus macrothyrsus thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
