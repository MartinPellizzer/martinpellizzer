Ranunculus amoenoviridis

Ranunculus amoenoviridis, commonly known as the lovely green buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Siberia. The plant features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with bright yellow petals and a prominent central pistil, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Ranunculus amoenoviridis is typically found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations.
