Rubus labbei

Rubus labbei, commonly known as the Cape raspberry, is a species of bramble native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Rubus labbei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
