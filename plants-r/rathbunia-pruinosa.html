Rathbunia pruinosa

Rathbunia pruinosa, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that help reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy white seed heads that disperse easily by wind. Rathbunia pruinosa thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along roadsides.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
