Randia crucis

Randia crucis, commonly known as the cross-stemmed randia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive cross-shaped stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Randia crucis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
