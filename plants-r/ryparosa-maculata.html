Ryparosa maculata

Ryparosa maculata, commonly known as the spotted milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers and hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. Ryparosa maculata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland areas.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
