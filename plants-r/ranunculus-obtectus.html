Ranunculus obtectus

Ranunculus obtectus, commonly known as the narrowleaf crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Ranunculus obtectus is generally not cultivated for ornamental purposes but is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and unique morphology.
