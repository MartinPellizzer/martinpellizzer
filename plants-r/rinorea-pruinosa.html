Rinorea pruinosa

Rinorea pruinosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or pruinose rinorea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, showy white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rinorea pruinosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
