Rubus tereticaulis var. mucronulatifrons

Rubus tereticaulis var. mucronulatifrons, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

This variant is a shrub native to North America, characterized by its slender, thornless stems and trifoliate leaves. The flowers are white with five petals and are typically borne in clusters. The fruit is a small, dark purple to black berry that develops from the flower.

It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
