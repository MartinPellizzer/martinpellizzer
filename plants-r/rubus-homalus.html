Rubus homalus

Rubus homalus, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
