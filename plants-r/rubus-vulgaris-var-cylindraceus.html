Rubus vulgaris var. cylindraceus

Rubus vulgaris var. cylindraceus, commonly known as the cylindrical blackberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, dark red to black berries. The plant is typically found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils across Europe. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible fruits that are similar to regular blackberries.

This variety is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.
