Rubus mercieri microgene bouvetianus

Rubus mercieri microgene bouvetianus, commonly known as the Bouvetian raspberry, is a rare shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It is native to the subantarctic regions and thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
