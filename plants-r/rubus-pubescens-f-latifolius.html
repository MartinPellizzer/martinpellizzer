Rubus pubescens f. latifolius

Rubus pubescens f. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf raspberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is characterized by its larger, broader leaves compared to the typical species, which are usually hairy and serrated. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands or along hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in horticultural settings.
