Rosa × burgundiaca

Rosa × burgundiaca, commonly known as the Burgundy rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from crosses between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is characterized by its dense, thorned stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double to fully double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and hardiness, thriving in a variety of soil types and climates.

Rosa × burgundiaca is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in both garden beds and as a hedge.
