Rosa ferruginea var. typica

Rosa ferruginea var. typica, commonly known as the rusty rose, is a shrub-like species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.

Rosa ferruginea var. typica is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
