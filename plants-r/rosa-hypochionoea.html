Rosa hypochionoea

Rosa hypochionoea, commonly known as the Himalayan pink rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and thrives in cool, moist climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.

Rosa hypochionoea is also valued for its role in the perfume industry due to the essential oils derived from its flowers.
