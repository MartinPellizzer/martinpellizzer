Ronnbergia neoregelioides

Ronnbergia neoregelioides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed spider orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, spider-like flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the undergrowth. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence bears numerous small, colorful flowers that bloom in clusters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
