Ribes oxyacanthoides var. cognatum

Ribes oxyacanthoides var. cognatum, commonly known as the redcurrant gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to yellow, depending on the variety. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

This variety is closely related to other Ribes species and is often cultivated for its edible berries.
