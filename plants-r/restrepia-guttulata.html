Restrepia guttulata

Restrepia guttulata, commonly known as the Guatemalan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a yellow or green throat. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Restrepia guttulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
