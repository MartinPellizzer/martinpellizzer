Rhus copallinum f. integrifolia

Rhus copallinum f. integrifolia, commonly known as smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically composed of 11 to 31 pairs of elliptic, serrated leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that attract birds and other wildlife. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade in well-drained soils.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
