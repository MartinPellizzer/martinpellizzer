Rosa micrantha var. provincialis

Rosa micrantha var. provincialis, commonly known as the Provincial rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features slender, thorned stems and dense clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are pinnately compound, with elliptical leaflets that are covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade to full sun, making it adaptable to a range of garden conditions.
