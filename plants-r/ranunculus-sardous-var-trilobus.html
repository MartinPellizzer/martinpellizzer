Ranunculus sardous var. trilobus

Ranunculus sardous var. trilobus, commonly known as the Sicilian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with a prominent central pistil. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
