Rosa acicularis

Rosa acicularis, commonly known as the needleleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant is typically shrub-like, with thorny stems that provide structural support. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Rosa acicularis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
