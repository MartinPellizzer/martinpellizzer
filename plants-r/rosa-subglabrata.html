Rosa subglabrata

Rosa subglabrata, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed rose, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub-like plant with thornless stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval leaflets, and the plant produces red or orange hips in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant's botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable.
