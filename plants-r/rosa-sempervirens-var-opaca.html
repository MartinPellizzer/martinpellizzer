Rosa sempervirens var. opaca

Rosa sempervirens var. opaca, commonly known as the evergreen roses, is a shrub-like plant native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year, giving it its "evergreen" designation. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental shrubs with year-round interest.

Its hardy nature and attractive foliage make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and botanical collections.
