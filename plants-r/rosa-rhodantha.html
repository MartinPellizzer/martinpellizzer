Rosa rhodantha

Rosa rhodantha, commonly known as the pink rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant pink blooms. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are usually single or semi-double, with a soft pink hue that fades to white as they mature.

Rosa rhodantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
