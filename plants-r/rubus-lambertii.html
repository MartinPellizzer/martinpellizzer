Rubus lambertii

Rubus lambertii, commonly known as Lambert's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
