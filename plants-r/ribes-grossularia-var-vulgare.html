Ribes grossularia var. vulgare

Ribes grossularia var. vulgare, commonly known as the common gooseberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, tart berries that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are usually heart-shaped or ovate. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

The berries are not only edible but also serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife.
