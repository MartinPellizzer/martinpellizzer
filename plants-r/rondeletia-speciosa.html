Rondeletia speciosa

Rondeletia speciosa, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a glossy appearance.

The flowers are borne in clusters and are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
