Rubus hypoleucus var. virescens

Rubus hypoleucus var. virescens, commonly known as the green-leafed raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry species native to North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant green foliage, which distinguishes it from other raspberry varieties. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they may vary in color depending on the cultivar. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is a shrub with thorny stems and simple, alternate leaves.
