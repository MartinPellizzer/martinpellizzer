Rubus australis monstr. pauperatus

Rubus australis monstr.

pauperatus, commonly known as the poor man's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with minimal care.
