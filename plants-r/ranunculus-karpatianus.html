Ranunculus karpatianus

Ranunculus karpatianus, commonly known as the Carpathian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three deeply lobed leaflets.

Ranunculus karpatianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
