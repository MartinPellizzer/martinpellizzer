Ranunculus auricomus subsp. apertiformis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

apertiformis, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.
