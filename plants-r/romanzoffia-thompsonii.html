Romanzoffia thompsonii

Romanzoffia thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's waterwheel, is a striking aquatic plant characterized by its whorled arrangement of bright green, sword-shaped leaves.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. The plant's unique spiral structure allows it to maximize light absorption while minimizing water resistance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, which are followed by distinctive, floating seeds.

Romanzoffia thompsonii is valued in aquascaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
