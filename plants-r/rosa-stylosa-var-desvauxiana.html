Rosa stylosa var. desvauxiana

Rosa stylosa var. desvauxiana, commonly known as the Desvaux's climbing rose, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, semi-double pink to white flowers. The leaves are pinnate, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets. This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
