Rubus cryptacanthus

Rubus cryptacanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.

Its growth habit includes shrubs that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, often found in woodland and chaparral habitats.
