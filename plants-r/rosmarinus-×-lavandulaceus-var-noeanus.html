Rosmarinus × lavandulaceus var. noeanus

Rosmarinus × lavandulaceus var. noeanus, commonly known as the Noean hybrid lavender, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rosmarinus officinalis and Lavandula angustifolia.

It exhibits characteristics of both parent species, featuring fragrant, narrow leaves and purple or pink floral spikes. The plant is evergreen, with aromatic foliage that is often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting flowers.
