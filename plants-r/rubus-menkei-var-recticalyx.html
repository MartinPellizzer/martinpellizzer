Rubus menkei var. recticalyx

Rubus menkei var. recticalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and small, dark red to black berries. The leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly serrated edge. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making it a valuable plant for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
