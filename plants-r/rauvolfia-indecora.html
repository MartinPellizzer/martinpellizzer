Rauvolfia indecora

Rauvolfia indecora, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
