Rhamnella rubrinervis

Rhamnella rubrinervis, commonly known as the red-veined rhamnella, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rhamnaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, simple leaves with prominent reddish veins, which give it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries.

Rhamnella rubrinervis is often found in lowland rainforests and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
