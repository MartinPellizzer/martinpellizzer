Ranunculus aestivalis

Ranunculus aestivalis, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, thriving in temperate climates. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and bright yellow flowers with five petals arranged in a cup-like shape. It produces small, green, ribbed fruits that contain several seeds.

Ranunculus aestivalis is often found in disturbed soils and is a common sight in early spring.
