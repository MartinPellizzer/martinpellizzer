Ribes grossularia f. incisa

Ribes grossularia f. incisa, commonly known as the cutleaf gooseberry, is a variant of the gooseberry plant characterized by its distinctive lobed leaves.

This cultivar is native to parts of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits. The leaves are typically deeply incised, giving the plant a unique, delicate appearance. It produces small, yellow to orange berries that are often used in jams and desserts.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
