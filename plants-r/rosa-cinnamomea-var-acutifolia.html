Rosa cinnamomea var. acutifolia

Rosa cinnamomea var. acutifolia, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, reddish hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The variety is prized for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
