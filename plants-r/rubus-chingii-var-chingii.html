Rubus chingii var. chingii

Rubus chingii var. chingii, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
