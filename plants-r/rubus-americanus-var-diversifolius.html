Rubus americanus var. diversifolius

Rubus americanus var. diversifolius, commonly known as the Appalachian blackberry, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically consumed by wildlife and humans alike. Its leaves are variable in shape, with some exhibiting a distinctive lobed appearance, hence the specific epithet *diversifolius*.

This species thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
