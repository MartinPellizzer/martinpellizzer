Rhoicissus usambarensis

Rhoicissus usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambara grape, is a climbing vine native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible berries. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas. It has a woody stem with tendrils that help it climb and spread.

Its berries are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
