Rapuntium caespitosum

Rapuntium caespitosum, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves that are covered in dense, white, woolly hairs. The plant produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Rapuntium caespitosum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or cultivated areas.
