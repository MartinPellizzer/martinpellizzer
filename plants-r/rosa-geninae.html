Rosa geninae

Rosa geninae, commonly known as the genus Rosa, is a diverse group of flowering plants belonging to the rose family, Rosaceae.

These plants are characterized by their thorny stems, simple leaves, and showy flowers that typically have five petals. Rosa species are widely cultivated for their ornamental value, fragrance, and use in perfumes and culinary applications. They are also important in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

The genus includes numerous species, many of which are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
