Ranunculus tenuilobus

Ranunculus tenuilobus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, green fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Ranunculus tenuilobus is often found in the eastern and central United States.
