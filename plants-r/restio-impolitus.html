Restio impolitus

Restio impolitus, commonly known as the rough restio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically during the summer months. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often found in fynbos vegetation.

Restio impolitus is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and birds.
