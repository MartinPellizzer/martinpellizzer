Rubus × dolichotrix

Rubus × dolichotrix, commonly known as the blackberry dewberry.

It is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade, showing adaptability to various soil types. Its berries, which are usually dark purple to black in color, are edible and often used in jams and pies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
