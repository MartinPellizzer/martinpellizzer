Ravenea julietiae

Ravenea julietiae, commonly known as Juliet's Ravenea, is a striking ornamental plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, commonly known as the palm family, and is characterized by its elegant, arching leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces striking red-bracted inflorescences that give way to yellow-orange flowers, followed by edible, sweet fruits. Ravenea julietiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.

It requires well-draining soil and bright, indirect light to thrive.
