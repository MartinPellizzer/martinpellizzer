Rosa glauca f. gravetii

Rosa glauca f. gravetii, commonly known as the Glacier rose, is a rare and cold-hardy variant of the glaucous rose.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers and silver-gray foliage, which provide a striking visual contrast. This plant thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often found in rocky soils at high elevations. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a sought-after addition to specialized gardens.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.
