Rosa caniniformis

Rosa caniniformis, commonly known as the dog rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to Europe and western Asia and is characterized by its fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumes and culinary applications due to their aromatic properties.

Rosa caniniformis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
