Ruta tuberculata var. forsskahlii

Ruta tuberculata var. forsskahlii, commonly known as the Persian rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, dry fruits that contain numerous seeds, typical of the Rutaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
