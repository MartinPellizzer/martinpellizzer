Ranunculus aquatilis var. microcarpus

Ranunculus aquatilis var. microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited water buttercup, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and wet meadows. The plant features delicate, floating leaves and small yellow flowers with five petals. Its fruits are small, spherical achenes that are dispersed by water.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
