Ranunculus orthorhynchus subsp. alaschensis

Ranunculus orthorhynchus subsp.

alaschensis, commonly known as the Alaskan buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of Alaska and western Canada. It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra or moist meadow habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
