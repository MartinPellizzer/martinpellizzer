Ranunculus carsei

Ranunculus carsei, commonly known as the Carsei Crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes various species of buttercups and crowfoots. The plant is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to yellow and pink. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets.

Ranunculus carsei thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
