Rubus ulmifolius var. praetervisus

Rubus ulmifolius var. praetervisus, commonly known as the creeping blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry characterized by its prostrate growth habit.

It features dark green, trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and produces small, edible black berries. This plant is native to Europe and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role in ecological systems, providing food for wildlife and stabilizing soil.
