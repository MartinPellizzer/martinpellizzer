Rosa watsoniana

Rosa watsoniana, commonly known as the Watson's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China. The plant is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white.

Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it produces edible hips that are rich in vitamin C. Rosa watsoniana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
