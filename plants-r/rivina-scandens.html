Rivina scandens

Rivina scandens, commonly known as scarlet coralplant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a trailing or climbing perennial that produces striking red berries, which are a key feature of its botanical profile. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with toothed margins and small, white flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and restoration projects.
