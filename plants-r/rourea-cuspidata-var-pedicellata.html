Rourea cuspidata var. pedicellata

Rourea cuspidata var. pedicellata, commonly known as the clustered rourea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, with leathery, elliptical leaves that are often pubescent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
