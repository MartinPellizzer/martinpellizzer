Rosa psammophila

Rosa psammophila, commonly known as the desert rose or sand rose, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, narrow, and often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Rosa psammophila is valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in dry climates.
