Rapanea petiolata

Rapanea petiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boxwood, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue berries.

Rapanea petiolata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and aesthetic appeal.
