Rosa gravetii

Rosa gravetii, commonly known as the California wild rose, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant features thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the fruits are red, hairy rose hips.

Rosa gravetii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
