Rinorea usambarensis

Rinorea usambarensis, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rinorea usambarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
