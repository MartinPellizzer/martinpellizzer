Rubus almorensis

Rubus almorensis, commonly known as the Almorensis bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, known as blackberries, are edible and often used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to grow in rocky or disturbed soils.
