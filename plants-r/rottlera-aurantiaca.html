Rottlera aurantiaca

Rottlera aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange rottlera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

Rottlera aurantiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
