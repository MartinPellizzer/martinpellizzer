Ruellia wurdackii

Ruellia wurdackii, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular and usually blue or purple, blooming in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Ruellia wurdackii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
