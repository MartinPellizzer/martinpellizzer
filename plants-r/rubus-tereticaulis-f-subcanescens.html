Rubus tereticaulis f. subcanescens

Rubus tereticaulis f. subcanescens, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its flowers are white and have five petals, typical of the Rubus genus.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.
