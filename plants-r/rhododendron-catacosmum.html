Rhododendron catacosmum

Rhododendron catacosmum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, including parts of India, Nepal, and China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are elliptical and leathery, providing dense foliage that offers habitat for various wildlife.

Rhododendron catacosmum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
