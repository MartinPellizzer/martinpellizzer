Rubus nemorensis var. semicalvus

Rubus nemorensis var. semicalvus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and smaller, dark purple berries. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant white flowers in early summer.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and its role in ecological habitats.
