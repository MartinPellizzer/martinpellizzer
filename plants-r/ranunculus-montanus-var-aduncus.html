Ranunculus montanus var. aduncus

Ranunculus montanus var. aduncus, commonly known as the slender meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is characterized by its slender stems and is often found in calcareous soils.
