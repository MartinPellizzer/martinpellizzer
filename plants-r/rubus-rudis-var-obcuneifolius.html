Rubus rudis var. obcuneifolius

Rubus rudis var. obcuneifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
