Rubus hebecaulis microgene condensatus

Rubus hebecaulis microgene condensatus, commonly known as the dense-fruited blackberry, is a shrub-like plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dense clusters of black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
