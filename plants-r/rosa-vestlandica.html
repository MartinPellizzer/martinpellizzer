Rosa vestlandica

Rosa vestlandica, commonly known as the Vestland rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the western regions of Norway.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Rosa vestlandica prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in cooler climates.

Its ornamental value and historical significance in Norwegian horticulture make it a notable botanical specimen.
