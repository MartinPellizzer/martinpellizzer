Rubus omalus var. subtilis

Rubus omalus var. subtilis, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Chile and Argentina. The plant features thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, with small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are small, dark berries that ripen in late summer, often used in local culinary traditions.

The species is notable for its adaptability to high-altitude environments and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
