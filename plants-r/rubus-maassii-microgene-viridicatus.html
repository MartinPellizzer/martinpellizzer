Rubus maassii microgene viridicatus

Rubus maassii microgene viridicatus, commonly known as the green-veined blackberry, is a unique variant of the Rubus genus characterized by its distinctive green veins on the leaves.

This plant is native to specific regions in South America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its foliage exhibits a striking contrast between dark green leaves and vibrant green veins, making it visually appealing. The fruit, while less commonly cultivated, produces small, tart berries similar to other blackberry species.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic variation and potential for hybridization with other Rubus species.
