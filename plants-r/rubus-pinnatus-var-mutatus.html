Rubus pinnatus var. mutatus

Rubus pinnatus var. mutatus, commonly known as the variable blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinct morphological features.

It is a shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, featuring compound leaves with a pinnate structure. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

This variant is notable for its genetic variability, which contributes to its adaptability in different environmental conditions.
