Rosa chrshanovskii

Rosa chrshanovskii, commonly known as the Altai rose, is a species native to Central Asia, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Mongolia and Russia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical characteristics include simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins and a strong, aromatic scent.

Rosa chrshanovskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
