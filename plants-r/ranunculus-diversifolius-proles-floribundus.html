Ranunculus diversifolius proles floribundus

Ranunculus diversifolius proles floribundus, commonly known as the variable-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often accompanied by a hairy, green stem. The plant has compound leaves that vary in shape, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
