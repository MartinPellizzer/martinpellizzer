Rubus × mirandus

Rubus × mirandus, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (the cloudberry). The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, with a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems typical of many Rubus species.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in boreal and temperate regions.
