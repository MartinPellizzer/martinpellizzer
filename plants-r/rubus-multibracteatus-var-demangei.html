Rubus multibracteatus var. demangei

Rubus multibracteatus var. demangei, commonly known as the Demange's bracted raspberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam and Cambodia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and potential use in horticulture.
