Ranunculus jacuticus

Ranunculus jacuticus, commonly known as the Central Asian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, mountainous regions. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually palmately lobed. Its flowers are bright yellow with a distinctive ring of petals, and it produces small, hairy fruits.

Ranunculus jacuticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
