Ruellia leucantha var. postinsularis

Ruellia leucantha var. postinsularis, commonly known as the Florida ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming from spring to fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along waterways.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
