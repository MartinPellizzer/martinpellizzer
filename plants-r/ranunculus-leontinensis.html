Ranunculus leontinensis

Ranunculus leontinensis, commonly known as the leopard's bane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive appearance, featuring bright yellow flowers surrounded by a ring of white or pale yellow petals. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a unique and ornamental look. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky areas.

Due to its striking appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
