Rottboellia stolonifera

Rottboellia stolonifera, commonly known as the devil's bit sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features slender, creeping stems that produce small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Rottboellia stolonifera is known for its ability to spread via stolons, forming dense mats that can colonize various habitats.
