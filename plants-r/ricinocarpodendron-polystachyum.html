Ricinocarpodendron polystachyum

Ricinocarpodendron polystachyum, commonly known as the giant sago palm, is a large cycad-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters, which eventually develop into woody, three-angled fruits. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a unique reproductive structure that distinguishes it from other palms.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
