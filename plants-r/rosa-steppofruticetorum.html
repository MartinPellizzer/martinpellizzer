Rosa steppofruticetorum

Rosa steppofruticetorum, commonly known as the steppe shrub rose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant features thorny stems and simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rosa steppofruticetorum is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
