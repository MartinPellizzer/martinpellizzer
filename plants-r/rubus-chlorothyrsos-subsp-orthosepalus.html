Rubus chlorothyrsos subsp. orthosepalus

Rubus chlorothyrsos subsp.

orthosepalus, commonly known as the pale-sepal raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of East Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five sepals that are often smaller and more upright than in other Rubus species. The fruit is a red, aggregate berry that ripens in late summer.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
