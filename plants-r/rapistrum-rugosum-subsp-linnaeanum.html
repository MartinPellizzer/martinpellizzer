Rapistrum rugosum subsp. linnaeanum

Rapistrum rugosum subsp.

linnaeanum, commonly known as the rough seascale, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that thrives in saline and sandy soils.

The plant features yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are rough and serrated, contributing to its common name.

It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and is often found in maritime environments.
