Romulea leipoldtii

Romulea leipoldtii, commonly known as the Leipoldt's glory bell, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in exotic flora.
