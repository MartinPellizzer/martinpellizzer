Rosa afzeliana f. pilosula

Rosa afzeliana f. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa afzeliana.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers, typically in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of Asia, including China and the Himalayas, and thrives in temperate climates.

This cultivar is often appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical use in traditional medicine.
