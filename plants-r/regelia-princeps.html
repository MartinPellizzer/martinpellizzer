Regelia princeps

Regelia princeps, commonly known as the royal regelia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 feet and features a compact, rounded form. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Regelia princeps is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with specific climate conditions.
