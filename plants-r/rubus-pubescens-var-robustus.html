Rubus pubescens var. robustus

Rubus pubescens var. robustus, commonly known as the rough blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupes that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its edible berries and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
