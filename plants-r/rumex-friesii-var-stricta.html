Rumex friesii var. stricta

Rumex friesii var. stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and disturbed areas. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in clusters, and it produces seeds in capsules.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
