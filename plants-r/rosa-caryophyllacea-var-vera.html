Rosa caryophyllacea var. vera

Rosa caryophyllacea var. vera, commonly known as the Sierra rose, is a species native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, stipitate stipules, and anthers that release pollen during the flowering season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
