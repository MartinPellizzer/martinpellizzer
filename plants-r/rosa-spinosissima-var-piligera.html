Rosa spinosissima var. piligera

Rosa spinosissima var. piligera, commonly known as the woolly spinose rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a fuzzy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to rocky, well-drained soils.
