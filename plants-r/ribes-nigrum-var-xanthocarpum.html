Ribes nigrum var. xanthocarpum

Ribes nigrum var. xanthocarpum, commonly known as the yellow-fruited gooseberry, is a variant of the common gooseberry.

It is characterized by its yellow berries, which differ from the typical red or black fruits of the standard R. nigrum. The plant is a deciduous shrub with compound leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

This variety is also valued in horticulture for its unique fruit color and adaptability to various soil conditions.
