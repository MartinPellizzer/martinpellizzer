Ranunculus orientalis subsp. hierosolymitanus

Ranunculus orientalis subsp.

hierosolymitanus, commonly known as the Jerusalem glory flower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive black center.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in arid and semi-arid regions. It has palmate leaves with lobes that are often hairy.

This subspecies is native to the Levant and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
