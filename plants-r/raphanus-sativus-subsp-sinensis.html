Raphanus sativus subsp. sinensis

Raphanus sativus subsp.

sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese radish or daikai, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its stout, fleshy root that is typically white or pale yellow in color.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight, and is often cultivated for its edible root.

This subspecies is native to China and is used in traditional Chinese cuisine and medicine.
