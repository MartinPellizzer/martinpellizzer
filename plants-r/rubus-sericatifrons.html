Rubus sericatifrons

Rubus sericatifrons, commonly known as the silky-leaf raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central America and parts of South America. The plant is characterized by its hairy, silvery-green leaves and small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
