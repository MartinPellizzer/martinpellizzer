Rhaphis ciliolata

Rhaphis ciliolata, commonly known as the velvetleaf palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant typically grows as an understory species in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Rhaphis ciliolata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.
