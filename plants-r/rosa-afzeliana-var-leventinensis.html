Rosa afzeliana var. leventinensis

Rosa afzeliana var. leventinensis, commonly known as the Leventinensis rose, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it produces small, red berries that contain the seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
