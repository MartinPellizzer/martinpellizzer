Rosa sicula var. gussonii

Rosa sicula var. gussonii, commonly known as the Sicilian rose, is a variety of the Sicilian rose that is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and is typically found in dry, rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.

This variety is also known for its historical significance in traditional herbal medicine.
