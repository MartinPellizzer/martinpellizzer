Rapuntium membranaceum

Rapuntium membranaceum, commonly known as the membranous rapunzel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hair. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.

The plant is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, thriving in well-drained soils and cooler climates.
