Rubus tereticaulis var. brevistamineus

Rubus tereticaulis var. brevistamineus, commonly known as the short-stamen raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. Its flowers are pink to white and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators.

The variety is distinguished by its shorter stamens compared to other Rubus species.
