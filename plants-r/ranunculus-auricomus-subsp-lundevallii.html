Ranunculus auricomus subsp. lundevallii

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

lundevallii, commonly known as the yellow marsh cinquefoil, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, which bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, meadows, and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a hairy texture, while its stems are usually upright and slender.

This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often found in temperate regions with cool, humid climates.
