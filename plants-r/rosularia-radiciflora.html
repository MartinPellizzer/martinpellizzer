Rosularia radiciflora

Rosularia radiciflora, commonly known as the root-flower rose.

It is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia, characterized by its compact, rosette-forming structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are fleshy and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Rosularia radiciflora is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in arid landscapes.
