Rhododendron arboreum subsp. nilagiricum

Rhododendron arboreum subsp.

nilagiricum, commonly known as the Nilgiri rhododendron, is a species native to the Western Ghats of India. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a dense canopy that supports a variety of epiphytic plants.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat.
