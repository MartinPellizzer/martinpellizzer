Rubus frechetensis

Rubus frechetensis, commonly known as the Frechet's blackberry, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in Mexico and Guatemala. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It has trifoliate leaves and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential culinary uses.
