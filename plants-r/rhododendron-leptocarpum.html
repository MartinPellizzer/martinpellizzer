Rhododendron leptocarpum

Rhododendron leptocarpum, commonly known as the slender-fruited rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. Its flowers range in color from white to pink, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes.

Rhododendron leptocarpum is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
