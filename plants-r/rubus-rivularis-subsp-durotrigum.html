Rubus rivularis subsp. durotrigum

Rubus rivularis subsp.

durotrigum, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to western Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a serrated edge. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, dark red to black berry.

This subspecies is notable for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
