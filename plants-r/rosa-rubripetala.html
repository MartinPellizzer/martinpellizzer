Rosa rubripetala

Rosa rubripetala, commonly known as the red-petaled rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of downy hairs.

Rosa rubripetala is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
