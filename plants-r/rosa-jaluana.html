Rosa jaluana

Rosa jaluana, commonly known as the Jaluana rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of hair, while its stems are thornless and often climb or trail.

Rosa jaluana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
