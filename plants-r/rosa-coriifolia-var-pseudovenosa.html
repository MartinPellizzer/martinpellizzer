Rosa coriifolia var. pseudovenosa

Rosa coriifolia var. pseudovenosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf dogwood rose, is a variant of the Chinese rose, characterized by its distinctive, velvety foliage.

It is native to China and is prized for its ornamental value, particularly its clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and a dense, bushy growth habit. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
