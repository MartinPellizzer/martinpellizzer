Rubus × sessiliglandulosus

Rubus × sessiliglandulosus, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry plant, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are composed of multiple small drupelets. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces flowers that are white or pale pink.

This species is valued for its edible fruits, which are commonly used in culinary applications.
