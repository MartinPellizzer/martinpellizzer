Rubus hirtus subsp. acridentulus

Rubus hirtus subsp.

acridentulus, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of white to pinkish flowers.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

This subspecies is notable for its hirsute (hairy) appearance and its role in local ecosystems.
