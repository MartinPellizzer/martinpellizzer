Ranunculus lojkae

Ranunculus lojkae, commonly known as the Lojka's buttercup, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many species of buttercups and other flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
