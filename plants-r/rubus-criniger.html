Rubus criniger

Rubus criniger, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is closely related to other members of the Rubus genus.
