Rubus laetecoloratus

Rubus laetecoloratus, commonly known as the showy blackberry.

This species is a perennial shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. It produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible blackberries in the summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its fruit is prized for its sweet, juicy flavor and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
