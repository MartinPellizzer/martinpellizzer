Ranunculus eschscholtzii var. trisectus

Ranunculus eschscholtzii var. trisectus, commonly known as the three-lobed buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to California.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven sepals that are often mistaken for petals. The leaves are deeply lobed, typically divided into three distinct segments, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy floral structures.
