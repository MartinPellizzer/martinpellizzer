Rosa omissa var. cujavica

Rosa omissa var. cujavica, commonly known as the Cujavica rose, is a species native to the high Andes of Peru.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and is adapted to thrive in cool, high-altitude environments. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
