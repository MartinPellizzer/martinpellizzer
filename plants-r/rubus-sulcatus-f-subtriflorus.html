Rubus sulcatus f. subtriflorus

Rubus sulcatus f. subtriflorus, commonly known as the three-flowered blackberry.

This is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring three flowers per inflorescence instead of the typical two. It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The flowers are usually white or pale green, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry.

It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.
