Rubus spinifer

Rubus spinifer, commonly known as the thorny blackberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in sharp, stiff spines. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

Rubus spinifer is native to temperate regions of Asia and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
