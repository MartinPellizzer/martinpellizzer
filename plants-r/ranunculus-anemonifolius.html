Ranunculus anemonifolius

Ranunculus anemonifolius, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate and often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
