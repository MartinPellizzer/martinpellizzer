Rosa × collina var. corymbifera

Rosa × collina var. corymbifera, commonly known as the clustered shrub rose, is a hybrid species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with serrated edges, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

The shrub is relatively hardy and adaptable, thriving in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
