Rubus pallidipes

Rubus pallidipes, commonly known as the pale bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they appear in clusters during the spring. The plant produces small, red fruits known as berries, which are edible but often tart.

It is native to regions in Europe and Asia, where it thrives in woodland and scrubland habitats.
