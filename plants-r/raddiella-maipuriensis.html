Raddiella maipuriensis

Raddiella maipuriensis, commonly known as the Maipurella cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise desolate habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists studying desert flora.
