Rubus aceroides

Rubus aceroides, commonly known as the red raspberry or redcurrant bush, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Rubus aceroides is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
