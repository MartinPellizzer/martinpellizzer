Rubus × polyacanthus

Rubus × polyacanthus, commonly known as the evergreen bramble, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, edible berries in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for hedgerows and wildlife habitats.
