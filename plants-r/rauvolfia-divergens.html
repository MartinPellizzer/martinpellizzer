Rauvolfia divergens

Rauvolfia divergens, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that contain the seeds. Rauvolfia divergens is notable for containing the alkaloid reserpine, which has been used in traditional medicine and modern pharmacology.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
