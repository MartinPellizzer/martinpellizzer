Rosa ratomskiana

Rosa ratomskiana, commonly known as the Siberian rose or Russian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Russia, Kazakhstan, and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are typically red or orange in color.

Rosa ratomskiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
