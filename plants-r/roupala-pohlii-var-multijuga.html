Roupala pohlii var. multijuga

Roupala pohlii var. multijuga, commonly known as the silver maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-green foliage, which turns yellow in the fall. The tree has a broad, spreading crown and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. Its bark is smooth and grey, becoming fissured with age.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.
