Rosa cinnamomea var. ivanida

Rosa cinnamomea var. ivanida, commonly known as the Ivan's cinnamon rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features glossy, dark green foliage and produces fragrant, deep red to purple flowers in late spring. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, cinnamon-scented leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in cooler climates.
