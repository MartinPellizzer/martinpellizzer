Rosa × sabinii var. glandulosa

Rosa × sabinii var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that have glandular dots on their surfaces. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five to seven petals and a strong, pleasant fragrance. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
