Ranunculus petiolaris var. standleyi

Ranunculus petiolaris var. standleyi, commonly known as the Standley's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with prominent central sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests. Its botanical characteristics include a short, hairy stem and numerous stamens surrounding a single pistil.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
