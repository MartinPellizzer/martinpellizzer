Ruellia amoena

Ruellia amoena, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine, hairy tissue. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Ruellia amoena is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
