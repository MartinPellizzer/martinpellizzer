Rubus × omissus

Rubus × omissus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically black when mature, though they may retain a reddish hue. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
