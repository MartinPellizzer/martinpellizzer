Rubus vindomensis

Rubus vindomensis, commonly known as the Vindomestic raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
