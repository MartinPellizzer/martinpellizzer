Rosa moschata var. abyssinica

Rosa moschata var. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian musk rose, is a variant of the musk rose species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers and thorny stems, typical of many Rosa species. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as hips, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value and historical use in herbal remedies make it a notable botanical species.
