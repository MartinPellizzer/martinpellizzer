Rhus glabra var. cismontana

Rhus glabra var. cismontana, commonly known as smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern and central United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 8 feet, featuring compound leaves with 15 to 31 pairs of serrated leaflets. The plant produces bright red, spiny fruit clusters that persist into winter, providing food for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Smooth sumac is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
