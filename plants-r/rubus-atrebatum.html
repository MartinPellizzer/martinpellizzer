Rubus atrebatum

Rubus atrebatum, commonly known as the purple-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
