Rubus henrici-weberi

Rubus henrici-weberi, commonly known as the Weber's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
