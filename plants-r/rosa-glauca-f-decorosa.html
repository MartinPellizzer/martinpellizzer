Rosa glauca f. decorosa

Rosa glauca f. decorosa, commonly known as the blue rose or decorative blue rose, is a hybrid form of the Rosa glauca species.

It is characterized by its striking blue-pink flowers, which are a result of specific pigmentation and genetic traits. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This cultivar is valued for its unique coloration and is often used in floral arrangements and garden landscapes.
