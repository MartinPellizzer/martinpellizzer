Rubus miszczenkoi

Rubus miszczenkoi, commonly known as the Miszczenko raspberry, is a species of shrub native to eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as raspberries, which are typically red and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
