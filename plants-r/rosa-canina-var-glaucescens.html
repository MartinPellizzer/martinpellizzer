Rosa canina var. glaucescens

Rosa canina var. glaucescens, commonly known as the pale rose or glaucous dog rose, is a variation of the dog rose that exhibits distinct botanical features.

It is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typically with five to seven leaflets. The flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals and a sweet, fragrant aroma. The fruit, known as a hip, is red, hairy, and contains several achenes.

This variety is often found in woodland edges and scrublands, thriving in well-drained soils.
