Rubus hystrix var. sallei

Rubus hystrix var. sallei, commonly known as the rough blackberry.

This variant is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. It produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and tart. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
