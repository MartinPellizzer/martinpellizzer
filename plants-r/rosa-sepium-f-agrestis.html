Rosa sepium f. agrestis

Rosa sepium f. agrestis, commonly known as the wild rose or field rose, is a shrub-like plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are aggregate berries that develop from the flower petals and are typically red when mature.

Rosa sepium f. agrestis is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
