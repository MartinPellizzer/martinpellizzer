Rubus horridus var. infestus

Rubus horridus var. infestus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry bush, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible berries in the summer. Its berries are often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.

The plant is also known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
