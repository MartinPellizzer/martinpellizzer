Rhynchosia arenicola var. major

Rhynchosia arenicola var. major, commonly known as the sand flat sida, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, showcasing adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in dry conditions.

The species is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
