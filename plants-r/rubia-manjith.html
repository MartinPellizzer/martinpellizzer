Rubia manjith

Rubia manjith, commonly known as Indian madder, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its roots are rich in alkaloids and have been traditionally used in dyeing textiles and medicinal applications.

Rubia manjith is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
