Rubus × tzemiensis

Rubus × tzemiensis, commonly known as the African blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and rocky areas. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine, though they are not widely cultivated.
