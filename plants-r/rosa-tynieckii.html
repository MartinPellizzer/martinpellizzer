Rosa tynieckii

Rosa tynieckii, commonly known as the Tynieck's rose, is a rare and ornamental species native to specific regions in Europe.

It is characterized by its simple, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological value.
