Rosa schergiana

Rosa schergiana, commonly known as the Scherg's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Russia. This shrub typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features thorny stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
