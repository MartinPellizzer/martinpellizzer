Rosa tomentosa var. insidiosa

Rosa tomentosa var. insidiosa, commonly known as the woolly rose or insidious woolly rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, white to greyish pubescence covering the stems, leaves, and flowers, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval with a serrated margin and are covered in fine hairs.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in garden settings.
