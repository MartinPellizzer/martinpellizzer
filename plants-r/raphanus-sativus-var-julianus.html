Raphanus sativus var. julianus

Raphanus sativus var. julianus, commonly known as the Japanese radish or daikon, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, white or pale yellow root that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in a raceme, which are typical of the mustard family. Its leaves are lobed and have a slightly peppery taste, while the root is crisp and mildly sweet.

This variety is cultivated for its edible root, which is used in various culinary traditions around the world.
