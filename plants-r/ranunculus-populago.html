Ranunculus populago

Ranunculus populago, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves that float on the water surface or extend underwater. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil in wetland areas.

Ranunculus populago plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
