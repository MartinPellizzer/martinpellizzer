Rytidosperma oreoboloides

Rytidosperma oreoboloides, commonly known as the mountain bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-like structure with small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Rytidosperma oreoboloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
