Rubus ortvayanus

Rubus ortvayanus, commonly known as the ortvayan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in woodland edges, hedgerows, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional remedies due to its tart flavor and medicinal properties.
