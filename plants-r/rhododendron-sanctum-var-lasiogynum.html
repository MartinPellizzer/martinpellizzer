Rhododendron sanctum var. lasiogynum

Rhododendron sanctum var. lasiogynum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, often in mountainous forests and alpine meadows. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to the ecological balance of its habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
