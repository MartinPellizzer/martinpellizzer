Racosperma urophyllum

Racosperma urophyllum, commonly known as the glandular acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive, rough texture and produces small, flat pods that contain seeds. It is adapted to dry environments and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The species is noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
