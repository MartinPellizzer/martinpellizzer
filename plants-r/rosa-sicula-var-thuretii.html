Rosa sicula var. thuretii

Rosa sicula var. thuretii, commonly known as the Sicilian rose, is a variant of the Sicilian rose that is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its fragrant pink flowers and thorny stems. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are dark green and serrated, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
