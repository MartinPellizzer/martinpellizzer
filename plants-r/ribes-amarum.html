Ribes amarum

Ribes amarum, commonly known as bittercurrant, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually yellow or orange in color, containing several drupes. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and it prefers moist, well-drained soils in partial to full shade.

Ribes amarum is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
