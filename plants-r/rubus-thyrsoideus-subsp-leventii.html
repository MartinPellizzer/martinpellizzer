Rubus thyrsoideus subsp. leventii

Rubus thyrsoideus subsp.

leventii, commonly known as the Sierra blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and stream banks.

The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark berries that are edible.

The subspecies is characterized by its dense growth habit and adaptability to cool, humid environments.
