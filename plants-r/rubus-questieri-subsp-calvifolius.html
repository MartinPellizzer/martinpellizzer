Rubus questieri subsp. calvifolius

Rubus questieri subsp.

calvifolius, commonly known as the smooth-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to southern China. It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves that are smooth and glossy.

The plant produces small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
