Rubus × amictifolius

Rubus × amictifolius, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it produces dark purple to black berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruits, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
