Rubus caesius var. laciniatus

Rubus caesius var. laciniatus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its distinctive red fruit, which contrasts with the typical black color of other Rubus species. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are usually lobed or deeply cut. It produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the edible berries in summer.

The leaves and fruits are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
