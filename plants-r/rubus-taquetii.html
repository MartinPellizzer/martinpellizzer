Rubus taquetii

Rubus taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and blackberries. The plant is characterized by its prickly stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
