Rosa × dumetorum f. plusglandulosa

Rosa × dumetorum f. plusglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular bramble rose, is a hybrid shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant has dense, dark green foliage with glandular hairs on the leaves and stems, giving it its specific epithet. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in hedging and landscaping.
