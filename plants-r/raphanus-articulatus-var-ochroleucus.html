Raphanus articulatus var. ochroleucus

Raphanus articulatus var. ochroleucus, commonly known as the yellow root radish, is a cultivated variety of the radish species.

It is characterized by its elongated, yellowish root that is typically harvested when it is small and tender. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, peppery flavor similar to other radishes.

This variety is often grown for both its roots and leaves in home gardens and small-scale agriculture.
