Rapuntium corymbosum var. longifolium

Rapuntium corymbosum var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved rapunculus, is a variant of the rapunculus species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 15 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
