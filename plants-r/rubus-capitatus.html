Rubus capitatus

Rubus capitatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
