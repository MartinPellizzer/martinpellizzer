Ranunculus ammannii

Ranunculus ammannii, commonly known as the Ammann's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick rhizome and produces striking, showy flowers with five to nine yellow petals and a central cluster of dark brown to black sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are palmately compound, with 3 to 5 leaflets that are usually lobed or toothed.

Ranunculus ammannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early spring blooms.
