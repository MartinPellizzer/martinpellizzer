Ranunculus napellifolius

Ranunculus napellifolius, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to high-altitude habitats.

Ranunculus napellifolius is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
