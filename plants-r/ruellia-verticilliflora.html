Ruellia verticilliflora

Ruellia verticilliflora, commonly known as the whorled ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite or whorled leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are clustered in whorls at the ends of the stems, producing showy blue or purple blooms.

Ruellia verticilliflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant flowers.
