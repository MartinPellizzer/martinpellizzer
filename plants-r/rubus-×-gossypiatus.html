Rubus × gossypiatus

Rubus × gossypiatus, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

The fruit is valued for its tart flavor and is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
