Rhododendron indicum var. sublanceolatum

Rhododendron indicum var. sublanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-lanceleaf rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to reddish-purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
