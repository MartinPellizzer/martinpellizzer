Rhynchospora rosae

Rhynchospora rosae, commonly known as the rose marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color, and it blooms during the summer months. Rhynchospora rosae thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its presence is an indicator of healthy wetland ecosystems.
