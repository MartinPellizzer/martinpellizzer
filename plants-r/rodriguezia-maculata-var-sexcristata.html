Rodriguezia maculata var. sexcristata

Rodriguezia maculata var. sexcristata, commonly known as the variegated Rodriguezia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that display a mix of green, white, and pink hues, creating a visually appealing foliage. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with prominent veins that add to its ornamental value.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
