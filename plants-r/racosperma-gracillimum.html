Racosperma gracillimum

Racosperma gracillimum, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, compound leaves with a feathery appearance. Its flowers are yellow and appear in dense, cylindrical clusters, typical of the genus.

Racosperma gracillimum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
