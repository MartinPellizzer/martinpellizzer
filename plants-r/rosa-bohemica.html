Rosa bohemica

Rosa bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Native to Central and Eastern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
