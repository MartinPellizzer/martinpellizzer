Rubus lasiaxon var. longistylus

Rubus lasiaxon var. longistylus, commonly known as the long-styled raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year producing vegetative growth and the second year bearing fruit. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of multiple drupelets, which are typically red when ripe. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, while the stems are hairy and thorny.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
