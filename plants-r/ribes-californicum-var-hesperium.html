Ribes californicum var. hesperium

Ribes californicum var. hesperium, commonly known as the Hesperian gooseberry, is a shrub native to California and parts of Nevada.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland and riparian habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible and often used in jams and preserves. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are pink to white and appear in clusters.

This variety is part of the genus Ribes, which includes many species of flowering shrubs in the family Grossulariaceae.
