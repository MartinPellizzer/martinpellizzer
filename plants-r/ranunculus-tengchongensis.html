Ranunculus tengchongensis

Ranunculus tengchongensis, commonly known as the Tengchong buttercup, is a rare and endemic species native to Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in spring, and it is known for its ornamental value.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species.
