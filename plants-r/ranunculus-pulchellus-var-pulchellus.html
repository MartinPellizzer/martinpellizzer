Ranunculus pulchellus var. pulchellus

Ranunculus pulchellus var. pulchellus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its botanical characteristics include a short, stout rhizome and hairy stems.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often colorful flowers.
