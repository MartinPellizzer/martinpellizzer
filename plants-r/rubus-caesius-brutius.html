Rubus caesius brutius

Rubus caesius brutius, commonly known as the red raspberry.

It is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. Its flowers are typically pink to red, and the fruits are edible, though they can be quite tart.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
