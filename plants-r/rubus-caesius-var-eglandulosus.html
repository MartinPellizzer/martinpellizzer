Rubus caesius var. eglandulosus

Rubus caesius var. eglandulosus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated edge and a hairy texture. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its berries are edible but can be quite tart, making them suitable for jams and preserves.
