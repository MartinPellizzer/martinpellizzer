Rubus × crinulosus

Rubus × crinulosus, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus leucodermis and Rubus rosifolius.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are often red or black in color.

This species is native to South America and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
