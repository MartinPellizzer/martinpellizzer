Rosa canina var. watsonii

Rosa canina var. watsonii, commonly known as Watson's rosehip, is a variant of the dog rose native to China.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and red, orange, or yellow hips that persist into winter. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its hips are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
