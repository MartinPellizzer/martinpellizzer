Rubus callosus var. scaber

Rubus callosus var. scaber, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and woodland environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid conditions and rugged terrain.
