Rubus × strigatus

Rubus × strigatus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in woodland areas with partial shade. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in jams and preserves.
