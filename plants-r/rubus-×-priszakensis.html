Rubus × priszakensis

Rubus × priszakensis, commonly known as the Polish blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soils. Its berries are usually dark purple to black in color, with a tart flavor.

It is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
