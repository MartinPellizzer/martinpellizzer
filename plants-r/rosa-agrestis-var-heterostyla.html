Rosa agrestis var. heterostyla

Rosa agrestis var. heterostyla, commonly known as the rough-stemmed wild rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, which are often covered in a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers in the spring, followed by red, hairy fruits. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets that are serrated and slightly hairy.

This variety is adapted to harsh environments, showing resilience to cold and arid conditions.
