Ranunculus auricomus subsp. robustifolius

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

robustifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive yellow center and white petals, often with a brownish or greenish tint.

The plant has palmately divided leaves that are broad and lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Its flowering period typically occurs from late spring to early summer.
