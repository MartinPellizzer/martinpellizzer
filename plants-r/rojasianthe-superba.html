Rojasianthe superba

Rojasianthe superba, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange petals, which resemble a fiery flame, and its elongated, pendant-like blossoms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks and branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers emit a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, Rojasianthe superba is highly valued in both horticultural and conservation contexts.
