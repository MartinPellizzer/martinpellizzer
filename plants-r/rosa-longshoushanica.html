Rosa longshoushanica

Rosa longshoushanica, commonly known as the Longshoushan rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Sichuan and Yunnan provinces. The plant is characterized by its fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, and its compound leaves with serrated edges. It grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and is adapted to temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
