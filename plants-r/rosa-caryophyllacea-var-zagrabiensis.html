Rosa caryophyllacea var. zagrabiensis

Rosa caryophyllacea var. zagrabiensis, commonly known as the Zagreb rose, is a compact, shrub-like species native to the Balkans.

It features dense, thornless stems and produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges. It is well-suited for rock gardens and borders due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch of color to the landscape.
