Raddia arborea

Raddia arborea, commonly known as the sacred tree of the Yoruba people, is a tall, evergreen tree native to western Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits containing black seeds. Its roots are believed to have spiritual significance and are used in various rituals.

Raddia arborea is also valued for its wood, which is durable and used in construction and carving.
