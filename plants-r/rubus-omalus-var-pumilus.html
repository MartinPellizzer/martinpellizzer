Rubus omalus var. pumilus

Rubus omalus var. pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
