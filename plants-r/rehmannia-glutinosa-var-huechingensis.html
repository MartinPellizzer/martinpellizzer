Rehmannia glutinosa var. huechingensis

Rehmannia glutinosa var. huechingensis, commonly known as the Korean rehmannia, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that are rich in mucilage, a gel-like substance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a fine, hairy texture.

This variant is often cultivated for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese and Korean medicine.
