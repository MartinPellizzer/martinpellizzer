Rosa × latebrosa

Rosa × latebrosa, commonly known as the shrub rose or everblooming shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless growth habit and its ability to produce flowers throughout the growing season. The plant typically features glossy, dark green foliage and clusters of fragrant, semi-double to double pink or white flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for use as a hedge or ornamental shrub.

Rosa × latebrosa is valued for its hardiness and continuous bloom, making it a popular choice in gardens.
