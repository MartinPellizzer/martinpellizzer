Rhynchosia lucida

Rhynchosia lucida, commonly known as the shining cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are often used as a food source, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

Rhynchosia lucida is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
