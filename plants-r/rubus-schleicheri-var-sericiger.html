Rubus schleicheri var. sericiger

Rubus schleicheri var. sericiger, commonly known as the silky blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with lobed leaflets that have a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are dark purple to black when ripe.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
