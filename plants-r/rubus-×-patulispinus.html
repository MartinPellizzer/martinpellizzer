Rubus × patulispinus

Rubus × patulispinus, commonly known as the giant blackberry, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, thorny stems and dark purple to black berries. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in jams and desserts.
