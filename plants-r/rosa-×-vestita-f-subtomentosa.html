Rosa × vestita f. subtomentosa

Rosa × vestita f. subtomentosa, commonly known as the soft-hairy rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and stems, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil with partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
