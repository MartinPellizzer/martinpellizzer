Rhododendron caucasicum var. albicans

Rhododendron caucasicum var. albicans, commonly known as the Caucasian white rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
