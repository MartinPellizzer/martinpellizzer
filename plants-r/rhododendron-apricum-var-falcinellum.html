Rhododendron apricum var. falcinellum

Rhododendron apricum var. falcinellum, commonly known as the falcinellum azalea, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in early spring. It prefers well-drained, acidic soils and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
