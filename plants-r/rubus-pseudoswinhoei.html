Rubus pseudoswinhoei

Rubus pseudoswinhoei, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs known for their thorny stems and edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are small, white or pink, appearing in clusters.

The fruit, which is a dark red to black berry, is not typically consumed by humans but is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
