Rhamnus × americana

Rhamnus × americana, commonly known as the American buckthorn, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhamnus cathartica (European buckthorn) and Rhamnus alnifolia (American buckthorn).

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America, characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and thorned branches. The plant produces small, black berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
