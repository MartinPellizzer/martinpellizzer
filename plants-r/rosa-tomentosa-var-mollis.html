Rosa tomentosa var. mollis

Rosa tomentosa var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-furred rose, is a shrub-like species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its stems are often covered in a soft, grayish pubescence, contributing to its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
