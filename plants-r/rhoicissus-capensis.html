Rhoicissus capensis

Rhoicissus capensis, commonly known as the Cape grape or climbing oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a trailing or climbing vine with heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Rhoicissus capensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various climates.
