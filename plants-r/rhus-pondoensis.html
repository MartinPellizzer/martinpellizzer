Rhus pondoensis

Rhus pondoensis, commonly known as the Pondo flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate and have a reddish or bronze hue during the growing season. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, red, inflated fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and public spaces for its vibrant foliage and unique fruiting structures.
