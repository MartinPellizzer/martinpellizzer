Rosa eglanteria f. paucispinosa

Rosa eglanteria f. paucispinosa, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa eglanteria species.

It is characterized by its reduced thorn production compared to the typical form, making it less aggressive in its growth habits. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows or woodland edges.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique morphology and its role in ecological systems.
