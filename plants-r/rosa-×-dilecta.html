Rosa × dilecta

Rosa × dilecta, commonly known as the double-flowered rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is characterized by its densely packed, fully double flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant is shrub-like, with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Rosa × dilecta is also valued for its fragrance and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
