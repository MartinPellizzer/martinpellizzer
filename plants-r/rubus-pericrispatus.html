Rubus pericrispatus

Rubus pericrispatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are elliptical with finely serrated edges and a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are white or pale pink, and they appear in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
