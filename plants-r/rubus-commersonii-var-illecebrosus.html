Rubus commersonii var. illecebrosus

Rubus commersonii var. illecebrosus, commonly known as the sweet thimbleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its erect or sprawling growth habit. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and edible fruits.
