Ranunculus fragilis

Ranunculus fragilis, commonly known as the fragrant buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eleven petals, typically in shades of yellow or white. The plant has a short, hairy stem and heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Ranunculus fragilis is known for its subtle fragrance, which becomes more pronounced as the flowers mature.
