Rubus honorus

Rubus honorus, commonly known as the honorberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in jams and pies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Rubus honorus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
