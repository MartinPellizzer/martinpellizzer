Rhynchocarpa pedunculosa

Rhynchocarpa pedunculosa, commonly known as the clustered fruit-bearing shrub, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, woody fruits that mature to a reddish color. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss in its dry habitat. It produces fragrant, yellow flowers during the cooler months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rhynchocarpa pedunculosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
