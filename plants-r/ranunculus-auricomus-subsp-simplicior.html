Ranunculus auricomus subsp. simplicior

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

simplicior, commonly known as the lesser buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features distinctive yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often with a greenish or reddish hue at the base.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hairs. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, cup-shaped blooms.
