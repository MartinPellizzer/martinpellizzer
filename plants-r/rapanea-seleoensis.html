Rapanea seleoensis

Rapanea seleoensis, commonly known as the Madagascar laurel, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dark blue berry containing several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
