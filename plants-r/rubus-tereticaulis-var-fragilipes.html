Rubus tereticaulis var. fragilipes

Rubus tereticaulis var. fragilipes, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thin, wiry stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its fragility, it is sometimes referred to as the "fragile blackberry.".
