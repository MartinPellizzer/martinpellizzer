Ricinocarpus lindenianus

Ricinocarpus lindenianus, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody pods containing flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular species for landscaping in tropical regions.
