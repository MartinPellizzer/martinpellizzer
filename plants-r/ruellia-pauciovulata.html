Ruellia pauciovulata

Ruellia pauciovulata, commonly known as the Florida ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue or violet flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ruellia pauciovulata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
