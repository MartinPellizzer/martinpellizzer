Rubus vestitus subsp. adscitus

Rubus vestitus subsp.

adscitus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering and fruiting occurring on the second year of growth.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically red and edible. Its leaves are alternate, compound, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
