Rubus exemptus

Rubus exemptus, commonly known as the smooth blackberry.

It is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thornless stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. These berries are edible, though they are often less flavorful compared to other blackberry varieties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
