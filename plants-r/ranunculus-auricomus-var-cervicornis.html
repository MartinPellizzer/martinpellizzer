Ranunculus auricomus var. cervicornis

Ranunculus auricomus var. cervicornis, commonly known as the golden crowncup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive, palmately divided leaf structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
