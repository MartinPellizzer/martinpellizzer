Rinorea tessmannii

Rinorea tessmannii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or purpleleaf shrub, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often with a slight fragrance. Rinorea tessmannii thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
