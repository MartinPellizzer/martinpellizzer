Rhynchanthus radicalis

Rhynchanthus radicalis, commonly known as the swamp orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in mangrove forests. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil in its aquatic habitat.
