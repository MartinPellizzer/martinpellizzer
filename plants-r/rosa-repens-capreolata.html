Rosa repens capreolata

Rosa repens capreolata, commonly known as the trailing rose or creeping Rosa capreolata, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically spreads along the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often prostrate or semi-woody.

This species is native to regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
