Rosa cymelliflora

Rosa cymelliflora, commonly known as the clustered rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with pinnate arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and ovate, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

Rosa cymelliflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
