Rubus radula var. robustior

Rubus radula var. robustior, commonly known as the robust raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant produces bright red, juicy berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
