Rubus hirtus f. brachyodon

Rubus hirtus f. brachyodon, commonly known as the short-stalked blackberry, is a botanical variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, dark purple to black berries. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges and thickets, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

This form is valued for its edible fruits and its role in ecological habitats.
