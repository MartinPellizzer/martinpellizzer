Rubus lamprococcus

Rubus lamprococcus, commonly known as the glabrous blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to dark purple berries. Its berries are edible but often have a tart flavor.

The species is notable for its dense growth habit and adaptability to various soil types.
