Rosa glabrifolia

Rosa glabrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and is characterized by its simple, smooth leaves and clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thornless stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. Rosa glabrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
