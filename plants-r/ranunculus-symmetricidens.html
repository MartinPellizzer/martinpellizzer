Ranunculus symmetricidens

Ranunculus symmetricidens, commonly known as the symmetricleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with 5 to 10 petals. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The species is characterized by its symmetrical flower structure, which gives it its scientific name.
