Rosa wulfenii var. dolorosa

Rosa wulfenii var. dolorosa, commonly known as the sorrowful rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant is known for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The name "dolorosa" reflects its association with melancholy in historical botanical literature.
