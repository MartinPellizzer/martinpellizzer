Rubus tetraphyllus

Rubus tetraphyllus, commonly known as the four-leaf blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges and small white flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant is often found in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade.
