Rubus fuscus subsp. retrodentatus

Rubus fuscus subsp.

retrodentatus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, glossy leaves with a serrated margin.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family and is notable for its hirsute (hairy) appearance and distinctive leaf teeth.
