Rubus ludwigii subsp. ludwigii

Rubus ludwigii subsp.

ludwigii, commonly known as the Ludwig's blackberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and scrub habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and historical use in traditional medicine.
