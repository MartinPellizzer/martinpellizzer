Ramaliella amasiana

Ramaliella amasiana, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and ethereal flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. The plant has no leaves, relying solely on its roots for photosynthesis and nutrient absorption. Its delicate, white flowers bloom in late summer, adding a mysterious beauty to its natural habitat.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is listed as endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
