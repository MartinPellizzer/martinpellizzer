Rosularia cypria

Rosularia cypria, commonly known as the Cyprus rose.

It is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth pattern. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge, especially in full sun. It produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

Rosularia cypria is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
