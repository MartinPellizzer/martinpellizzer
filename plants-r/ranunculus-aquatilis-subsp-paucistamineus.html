Ranunculus aquatilis subsp. paucistamineus

Ranunculus aquatilis subsp.

paucistamineus, commonly known as the lesser water crowfoot, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in shallow waters, such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.

The plant has finely divided, floating or submerged leaves that are usually bright green and arrowhead-shaped. Its flowers are small, with five to eight petal-like sepals and numerous yellow stamens, blooming in early spring.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.
