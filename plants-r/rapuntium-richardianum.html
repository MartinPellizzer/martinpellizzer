Rapuntium richardianum

Rapuntium richardianum, commonly known as the Richard's cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and streambanks. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to anchor well in soil while absorbing nutrients efficiently.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
