Ruschia parviflora

Ruschia parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf ice plant, is a low-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces tiny, white or pink flowers during the cooler months, usually in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and drought tolerance make it an ideal plant for landscaping in dry climates.
