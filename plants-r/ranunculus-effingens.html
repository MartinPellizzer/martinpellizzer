Ranunculus effingens

Ranunculus effingens, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, ditches, and along riverbanks.

Ranunculus effingens is an ornamental plant appreciated for its vibrant color and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
