Rosa dumalis var. leiostyla

Rosa dumalis var. leiostyla, commonly known as the Sierra Blanca rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features thorny stems and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with a serrated edge. This variety is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing drought tolerance and resilience.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and hardy member of the Rosaceae family.
