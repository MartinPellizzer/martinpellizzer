Rubus omalus var. auvoursianus

Rubus omalus var. auvoursianus, commonly known as the Auvoursian blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and its role in local ecosystems.
