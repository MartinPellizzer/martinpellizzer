Rapanea nigricans

Rapanea nigricans, commonly known as the black-leaved evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that turn a deep blackish color when mature. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Rapanea nigricans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
