Rubus × latidentatus

Rubus × latidentatus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to parts of East Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its growth habit includes both shrub-like forms and climbing vines, depending on environmental conditions.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex hybrid origin and genetic diversity, which contributes to its adaptability and resilience.
