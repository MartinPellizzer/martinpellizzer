Ribes uva-crispa subsp. hunyadensis

Ribes uva-crispa subsp.

hunyadensis, commonly known as Hungarian gooseberry, is a shrub native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and reddish-brown bark.

The plant produces small, yellow to orange berries that are edible and often used in jams and confections. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and are typically covered in a fine pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
