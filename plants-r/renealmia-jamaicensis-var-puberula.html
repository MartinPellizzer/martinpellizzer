Renealmia jamaicensis var. puberula

Renealmia jamaicensis var. puberula, commonly known as the Jamaican renealmia, is a herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive pubescent surface that gives it its varietal name.

It produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown, adapted for wind pollination.
