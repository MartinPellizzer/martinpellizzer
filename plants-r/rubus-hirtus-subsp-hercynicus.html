Rubus hirtus subsp. hercynicus

Rubus hirtus subsp.

hercynicus, commonly known as the Hercynian bramble, is a shrub native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in woodland edges, open woods, and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
