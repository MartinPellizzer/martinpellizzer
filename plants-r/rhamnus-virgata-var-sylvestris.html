Rhamnus virgata var. sylvestris

Rhamnus virgata var. sylvestris, commonly known as the coastal buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in coastal areas and well-drained soils, often growing in sunny or partially shaded locations.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
