Rubus thyrsiflorus var. acridentulus

Rubus thyrsiflorus var. acridentulus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, dark purple to black fruits. The leaves are typically narrow and have a serrated margin, with a slightly hairy surface. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
