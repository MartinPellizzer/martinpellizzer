Racosperma pharangites

Racosperma pharangites, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.

Due to its ecological importance, it is often included in native plant restoration projects.
