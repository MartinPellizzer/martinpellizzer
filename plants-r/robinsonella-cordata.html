Robinsonella cordata

Robinsonella cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf flax or heartleaf sida, is a herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
