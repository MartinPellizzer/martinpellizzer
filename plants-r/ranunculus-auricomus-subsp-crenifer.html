Ranunculus auricomus subsp. crenifer

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

crenifer, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its basal leaves that are typically lobed or toothed, with a distinctive yellow flower that has five petals.

The plant produces a capsule fruit containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and wetland conservation.
