Rudgea involucrata

Rudgea involucrata, commonly known as the silverleaf rudgea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, forested environments. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in dense inflorescences, and are typically white or pale yellow.

Rudgea involucrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
