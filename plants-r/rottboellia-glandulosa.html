Rottboellia glandulosa

Rottboellia glandulosa, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and agricultural areas.

Rottboellia glandulosa is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated fields.
