Rhaphiostylis ferruginea

Rhaphiostylis ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty-styled orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, with flowers that are usually pale yellow or greenish-white. Its unique floral structure includes a distinctive, rusty-colored staminode, which is a key identifying feature.

Rhaphiostylis ferruginea is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
