Rubus villicaulis var. minor

Rubus villicaulis var. minor, commonly known as the small-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white with five petals and appear in the spring.

The fruit is often used in local foraging and has a tart flavor.
