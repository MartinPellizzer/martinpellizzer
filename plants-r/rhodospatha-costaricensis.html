Rhodospatha costaricensis

Rhodospatha costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to Central America, particularly Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within montane forests. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink and bloom during the wet season.
