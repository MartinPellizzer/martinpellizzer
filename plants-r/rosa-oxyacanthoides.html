Rosa oxyacanthoides

Rosa oxyacanthoides, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red berries that are edible for birds.

Rosa oxyacanthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
