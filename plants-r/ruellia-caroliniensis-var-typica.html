Ruellia caroliniensis var. typica

Ruellia caroliniensis var. typica, commonly known as the Carolina rosemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and shaded woodlands.

Its adaptability makes it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
