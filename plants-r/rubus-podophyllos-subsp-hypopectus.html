Rubus podophyllos subsp. hypopectus

Rubus podophyllos subsp.

hypopectus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with sharply toothed margins and small, dark red berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are armed with stout, curved prickles, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in early spring.

This subspecies is notable for its horticultural value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
