Rubus pallidus var. guestphalicus

Rubus pallidus var. guestphalicus, commonly known as the pale bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, pale green leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, edible berry. This plant is native to certain regions of Europe and is often found in woodland edges and open habitats.

It plays a role in supporting local insect populations and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
