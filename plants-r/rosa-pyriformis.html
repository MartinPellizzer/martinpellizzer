Rosa pyriformis

Rosa pyriformis, commonly known as the pear-shaped rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often pear-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces thorned stems and has dark green, serrated leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa pyriformis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral form.
