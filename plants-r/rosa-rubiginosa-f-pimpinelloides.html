Rosa rubiginosa f. pimpinelloides

Rosa rubiginosa f. pimpinelloides, commonly known as the pink-stemmed dog rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose species.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinkish or reddish stems, which set it apart from the typical green stems of the species. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in early summer. It is a shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in Mediterranean climates.

This form is prized for its ornamental value and historical use in horticulture and perfumery.
