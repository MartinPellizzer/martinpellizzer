Rubus × meridianus

Rubus × meridianus, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Asia, including parts of China, India, and Nepal. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry.

This plant is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
