Rosa involucrata var. burmana

Rosa involucrata var. burmana, commonly known as the Burmese rose, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its botanical features include showy, single or semi-double petals and a strong, pleasant scent.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
