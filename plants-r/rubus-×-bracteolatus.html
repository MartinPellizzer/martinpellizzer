Rubus × bracteolatus

Rubus × bracteolatus, commonly known as the bracteolatus raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to parts of East Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves. Its leaves are serrated and have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

This species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
