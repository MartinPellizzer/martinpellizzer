Rubus tomentosus var. eglandulosus

Rubus tomentosus var. eglandulosus, commonly known as the glandular bramble, is a shrub native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and hairy, grayish foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by red, edible berries. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
