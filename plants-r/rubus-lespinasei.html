Rubus lespinasei

Rubus lespinasei, commonly known as the African bramble, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems in its native habitat.
