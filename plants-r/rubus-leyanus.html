Rubus leyanus

Rubus leyanus, commonly known as the Lyman's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
