Rosa tomentosa var. dimorpha

Rosa tomentosa var. dimorpha, commonly known as the woolly rose or the double-flowered woolly rose, is a variant of the woolly rose that exhibits distinct morphological features.

It is characterized by its dense, white to grayish pubescence covering the stems and leaves, which gives it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces large, showy double flowers with numerous petals, often in shades of pink or white. It is native to regions of Asia, including China and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
