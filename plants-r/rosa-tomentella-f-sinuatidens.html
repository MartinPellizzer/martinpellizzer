Rosa tomentella f. sinuatidens

Rosa tomentella f. sinuatidens, commonly known as the Sinuate-leafed Dog Rose, is a compact shrub native to China.

It features small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are ovate with a distinctive sinuate margin, giving it its common name. The plant has a dense, thorny growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.
