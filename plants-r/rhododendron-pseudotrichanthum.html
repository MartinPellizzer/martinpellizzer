Rhododendron pseudotrichanthum

Rhododendron pseudotrichanthum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in Nepal, Bhutan, and northern India.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in cool, moist environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are usually pink to red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron pseudotrichanthum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
