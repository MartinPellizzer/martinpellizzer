Rhynchospora melanocarpa

Rhynchospora melanocarpa, commonly known as blackroot sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its dark, glossy berries are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Rhynchospora melanocarpa plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland soils and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
