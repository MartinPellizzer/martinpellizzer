Rosa lloydii

Rosa lloydii, commonly known as the Lloyd's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America, specifically in countries like Bolivia and Peru. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red fruits that contain seeds.

Rosa lloydii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
