Rubus bifidus

Rubus bifidus, commonly known as the bifid blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like roses and apples.

Its structure and growth habits make it a hardy, invasive species in many regions.
