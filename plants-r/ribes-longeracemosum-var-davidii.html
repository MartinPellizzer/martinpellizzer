Ribes longeracemosum var. davidii

Ribes longeracemosum var. davidii, commonly known as the David's gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
