Rhynchospora amazonica

Rhynchospora amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian beakspur sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshy areas of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Rhynchospora amazonica thrives in humid, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various invertebrates and birds.
