Rhynchospora tenerrima subsp. microcarpa

Rhynchospora tenerrima subsp.

microcarpa, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Australia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower spikes that bloom during the summer months.

The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, which are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to waterlogged soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and the edges of ponds.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
