Rosa arvensis var. pubescens

Rosa arvensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent field rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are pink to red, often with a white base, and appear in clusters.

The variety is distinguished by its hairy (pubescent) stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name.
