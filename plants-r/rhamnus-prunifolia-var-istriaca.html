Rhamnus prunifolia var. istriaca

Rhamnus prunifolia var. istriaca, commonly known as the Istrian buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its small, oval leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, dark blue berries that are edible but have a laxative effect.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
