Rosa communis var. aspratilis

Rosa communis var. aspratilis, commonly known as the rough-stemmed rose, is a variant of the common rose characterized by its rugged, thorny stems and deep red, fragrant flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces oval, serrated leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
