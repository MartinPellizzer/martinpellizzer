Rosa × dumetorum var. hillebrandtii

Rosa × dumetorum var. hillebrandtii, commonly known as the Hillebrandt's shrub rose, is a compact, thorny shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves with serrated edges and is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit. It is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
