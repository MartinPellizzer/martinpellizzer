Ranunculus angustatus

Ranunculus angustatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have numerous stamens and a prominent pistil.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
