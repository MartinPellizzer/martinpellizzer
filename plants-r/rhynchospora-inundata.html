Rhynchospora inundata

Rhynchospora inundata, commonly known as the swampside beakspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the eastern United States.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and other moist environments, where it thrives in saturated soils. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a reddish or purplish hue at the base.

Rhynchospora inundata plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
