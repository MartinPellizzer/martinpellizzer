Rubus austromoravicus

Rubus austromoravicus, commonly known as the Eastern Carpathian bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the temperate regions of Central and Eastern Europe, particularly the Carpathian Mountains. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. Its fruits, known as berries, are typically small and edible, though they are often used in local traditional medicine.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
