Rhopala

Rhopala, commonly known as the rhopala moth, is a genus of moths belonging to the family Noctuidae.

These insects are characterized by their robust bodies and often cryptic coloration, which helps them blend into their surroundings. The larvae of Rhopala species are typically leaf rollers, feeding on a variety of plants, including grasses and shrubs. Botanically, they play a role in plant ecology by influencing plant growth through herbivory.

Their life cycle involves several stages, with the adult moths contributing to pollination in some cases.
