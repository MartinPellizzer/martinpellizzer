Rubus pseudopachyphylloides

Rubus pseudopachyphylloides, commonly known as the thick-leaved blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia, characterized by its large, leathery leaves and dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers are white and clustered, producing a distinctive, aromatic fragrance.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications, though it is less common than other blackberry varieties.
