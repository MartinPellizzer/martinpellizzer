Rubus granulatus var. platycephalus

Rubus granulatus var. platycephalus, commonly known as the flat-headed raspberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, thorny stems and broad, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
