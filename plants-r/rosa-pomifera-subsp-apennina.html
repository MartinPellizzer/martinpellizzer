Rosa pomifera subsp. apennina

Rosa pomifera subsp.

apennina, commonly known as the Apennine rose, is a subspecies native to southern Europe, particularly the Apennine Mountains. It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems.

The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink to red in color. Its most distinctive feature is the edible, fleshy fruit, known as a "hip," which contains numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
