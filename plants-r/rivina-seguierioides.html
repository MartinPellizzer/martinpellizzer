Rivina seguierioides

Rivina seguierioides, commonly known as the silverleaf rivina, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
