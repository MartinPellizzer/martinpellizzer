Ranunculus moeszii

Ranunculus moeszii, commonly known as the Moez's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It features a short, stout rhizome and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided, with serrated margins, and the flowers are usually yellow or orange in color.

Ranunculus moeszii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
