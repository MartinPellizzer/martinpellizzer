Rottboellia acuminata var. stocksii

Rottboellia acuminata var. stocksii, commonly known as the African grass or wild millet, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies and agricultural contexts.
