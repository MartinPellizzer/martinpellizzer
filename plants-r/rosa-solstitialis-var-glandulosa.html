Rosa solstitialis var. glandulosa

Rosa solstitialis var. glandulosa, commonly known as the summer rose or the sun rose, is a variety of the evergreen rose that is native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with prominent veins and small, glandular structures on the underside, which contribute to its scientific name. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is typically shrub-like in growth habit, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

This variety is well-suited for cultivation in warm climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and fragrance.
