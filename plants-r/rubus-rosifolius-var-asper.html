Rubus rosifolius var. asper

Rubus rosifolius var. asper, commonly known as the roughleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The leaves are typically grayish-green and have a rough texture, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by dark purple to black berries.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
