Rubus × holandrei

Rubus × holandrei, commonly known as the European blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The botanical features of Rubus × holandrei make it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
