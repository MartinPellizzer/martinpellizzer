Rosa elliptica var. hispidiglandulosa

Rosa elliptica var. hispidiglandulosa, commonly known as the hispid glandular rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with elliptical leaflets that are covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its stems are typically thorny and covered in a dense layer of glandular trichomes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual beauty.
