Ruellia paulayana

Ruellia paulayana, commonly known as the purple ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes, though it can be invasive in some areas.

Ruellia paulayana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade conditions.
