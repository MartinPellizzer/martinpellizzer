Rosa canina f. nigrescens

Rosa canina f. nigrescens, commonly known as the blackthorn rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and small, thorny stems. The plant produces white flowers in early spring, followed by small, dark blue berries in late summer. It is native to Europe and western Asia and thrives in temperate climates.

The berries are often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their high vitamin C content.
