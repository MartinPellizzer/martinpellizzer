Rhynchopyle pileata

Rhynchopyle pileata, commonly known as the hairy spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a spike, with small, spikelet-like flowers arranged along its axis. The plant has fibrous roots and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Rhynchopyle pileata thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

Its structure and growth habits make it well adapted to environments with fluctuating water levels.
