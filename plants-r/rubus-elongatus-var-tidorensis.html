Rubus elongatus var. tidorensis

Rubus elongatus var. tidorensis, commonly known as Tidore blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Tidore Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in coastal or forested areas. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant white flowers during the wet season.

This variant is valued for its unique flavor and is sometimes cultivated for local consumption and traditional uses.
