Rubus brigantinus

Rubus brigantinus, commonly known as the Serbica blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the genus Rubus within the family Rosaceae, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in woodlands and shrublands, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its dark purple to black berries are edible and have a distinctive, slightly tart flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in local cuisines.
