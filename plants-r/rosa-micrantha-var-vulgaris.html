Rosa micrantha var. vulgaris

Rosa micrantha var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common dog rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its pink to white flowers and thorny stems, which are typical features of many rose species. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in a variety of soil types and climates. Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, red hips that contain seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine.
