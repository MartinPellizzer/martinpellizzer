Rosa carolina lava

Rosa carolina lava, commonly known as the Carolina rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring thorny stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, velvety hips that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

The botanical features of Rosa carolina make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
