Rosa cinnamomea var. pseudoalpina

Rosa cinnamomea var. pseudoalpina, commonly known as the alpine cinnamon rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage, with leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep crimson, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are followed by small, red-orange fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
