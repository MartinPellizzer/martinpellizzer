Rubus × collinus var. genuinus

Rubus × collinus var. genuinus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to the western coast of North America, particularly in regions with moist, temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its edible berries, which are used in culinary applications and local foraging.
