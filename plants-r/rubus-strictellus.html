Rubus strictellus

Rubus strictellus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and serrated. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.

Its growth habit includes both shrub-like and vine-like forms, depending on environmental conditions.
