Ranunculus megalocaulis

Ranunculus megalocaulis, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features a hairy stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, supporting large, showy yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its striking blooms and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
