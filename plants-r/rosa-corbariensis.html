Rosa corbariensis

Rosa corbariensis, commonly known as the Corbari rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Guatemala and Honduras. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring.

Its foliage consists of serrated, dark green leaves, and it produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. Rosa corbariensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
