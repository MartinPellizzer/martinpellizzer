Rabdosia phyllostachys var. leptophylla

Rabdosia phyllostachys var. leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leafed false bamboo, is a hybrid plant that combines features of the genus Rabdosia and the grass-like phyllostachys.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is native to regions of Southeast Asia. It exhibits a clumping growth habit, forming dense foliage that resembles bamboo in appearance.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique morphology and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
