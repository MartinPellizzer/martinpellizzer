Rourea caudata

Rourea caudata, commonly known as the silverleaf rourea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Rourea caudata prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
