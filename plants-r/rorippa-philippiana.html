Rorippa philippiana

Rorippa philippiana, commonly known as the Philippine watercress, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It has small, bright green leaves that are often used in local cuisines for their peppery flavor.

Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by water.
