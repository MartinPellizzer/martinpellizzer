Rubus drejeri subsp. hibernicus

Rubus drejeri subsp.

hibernicus, commonly known as the Hibernian blackberry, is a shrub native to the northern regions of Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
