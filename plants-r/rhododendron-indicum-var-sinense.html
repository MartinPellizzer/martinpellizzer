Rhododendron indicum var. sinense

Rhododendron indicum var. sinense, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on the upper surface. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals and a showy, trumpet-shaped corolla.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.
