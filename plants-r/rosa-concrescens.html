Rosa concrescens

Rosa concrescens, commonly known as the evergreen rose or Chilean rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and oval, dark green leaves. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Rosa concrescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
