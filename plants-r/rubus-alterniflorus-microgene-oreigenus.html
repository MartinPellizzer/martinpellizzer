Rubus alterniflorus microgene oreigenus

Rubus alterniflorus microgene oreigenus, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine tundra or rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife in its native habitat.
