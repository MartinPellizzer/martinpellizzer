Rhipsalis rauhiorum

Rhipsalis rauhiorum, commonly known as the silver rhipsalis, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its trailing, succulent stems that are often covered in a silvery, waxy coating. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the winter months, adding ornamental value. Its leaves are reduced to small, hair-like structures, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with well-draining soil.

Rhipsalis rauhiorum is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive appearance.
