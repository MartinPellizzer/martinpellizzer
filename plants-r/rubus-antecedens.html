Rubus antecedens

Rubus antecedens, commonly known as the early blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically blooms with white to pale pink flowers in early spring, before the leaves fully develop. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval leaflets with serrated edges.

Rubus antecedens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
