Rubus × subracemosus

Rubus × subracemosus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry or black raspberry, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, featuring thorny stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed soils.

Its fruit is prized for its intense flavor and is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
