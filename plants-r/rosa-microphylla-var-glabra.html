Rosa microphylla var. glabra

Rosa microphylla var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed rose, is a compact, shrub-like species native to the western United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves with a smooth, hairless surface, distinguishing it from other rose varieties. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are generally thornless, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
