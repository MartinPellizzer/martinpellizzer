Rubus rotundatiformis

Rubus rotundatiformis, commonly known as the round-leaf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
