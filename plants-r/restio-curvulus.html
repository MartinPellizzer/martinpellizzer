Restio curvulus

Restio curvulus, commonly known as the slender restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, slender flowering stems. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, white to pale blue flowers.

Restio curvulus is adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
