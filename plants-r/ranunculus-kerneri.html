Ranunculus kerneri

Ranunculus kerneri, commonly known as the Kern's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and sometimes pink. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring cool, moist environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed segments, and the stems are often hairy.

Ranunculus kerneri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
