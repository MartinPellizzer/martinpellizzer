Rhododendron cinnamomeum

Rhododendron cinnamomeum, commonly known as the cinnamon-scented rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive cinnamon-like fragrance when crushed.

Rhododendron cinnamomeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland settings.
