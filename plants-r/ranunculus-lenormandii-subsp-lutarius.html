Ranunculus lenormandii subsp. lutarius

Ranunculus lenormandii subsp.

lutarius, commonly known as the Lutarian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens.

This subspecies thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. The plant is herbaceous, with a short growing season, and it blooms in spring.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
