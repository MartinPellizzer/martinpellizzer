Rubus vogesicola

Rubus vogesicola, commonly known as the Vogesicola raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and prickly stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry and blackberry species.
