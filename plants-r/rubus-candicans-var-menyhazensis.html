Rubus candicans var. menyhazensis

Rubus candicans var. menyhazensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pink flowers during the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

The fruit is edible and has been traditionally used in local cuisine and herbal remedies.
