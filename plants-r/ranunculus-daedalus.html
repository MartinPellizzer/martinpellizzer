Ranunculus daedalus

Ranunculus daedalus, commonly known as the Daedalus crowfoot, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, cup-shaped flowers.

Ranunculus daedalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
