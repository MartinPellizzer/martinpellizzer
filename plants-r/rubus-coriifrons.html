Rubus coriifrons

Rubus coriifrons, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically three to five lobed, with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and shrublands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
