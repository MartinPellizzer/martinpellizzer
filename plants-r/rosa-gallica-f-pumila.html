Rosa gallica f. pumila

Rosa gallica f. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf Gallica rose, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to Europe.

It features dense, rounded foliage and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its short, stiff stems and small, leathery leaves. It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its hardy and resilient nature.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and heraldry.
