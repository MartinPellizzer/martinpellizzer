Rubus ulmifolius var. anchostachys

Rubus ulmifolius var. anchostachys, commonly known as the bramble rose or European blackberry.

This variant is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to Europe and thrives in a variety of soil types, preferring full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
