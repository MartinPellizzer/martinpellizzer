Rotheca myricoides subsp. namibiensis

Rotheca myricoides subsp.

namibiensis, commonly known as the Namibian silverleaf, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine hairs, providing a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its habitat.
