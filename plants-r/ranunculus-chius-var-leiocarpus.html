Ranunculus chius var. leiocarpus

Ranunculus chius var. leiocarpus, commonly known as the smooth-seeded buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow or orange. The plant has a hairy stem and basal leaves that are palmately lobed. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value in gardens and its preference for partial to full sun.
