Rubus radula var. cordatus

Rubus radula var. cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf everlet, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
