Robinia pseudoacacia var. ulriciana

Robinia pseudoacacia var. ulriciana, commonly known as the Ulriciana false acacia, is a variant of the honeylocust tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are often twice-pinnate, with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown.

The bark is smooth and light grey, becoming fissured with age, and the tree is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
