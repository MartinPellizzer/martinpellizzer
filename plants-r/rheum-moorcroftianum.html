Rheum moorcroftianum

Rheum moorcroftianum, commonly known as the Afghan rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, ribbed rhizomes and broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces tall, branched stems with clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in the summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical medicinal uses.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Polygonaceae family.
