Rumex popovii

Rumex popovii, commonly known as the Popov's dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible leaves. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and arrow-shaped, with a rough, serrated edge. It produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months.

Rumex popovii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
