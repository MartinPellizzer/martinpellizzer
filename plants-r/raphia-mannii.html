Raphia mannii

Raphia mannii, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a large, tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clusters of bright red fruits, which contain edible seeds. Raphia mannii is often used for its fibers, which are harvested from the leaf bases for making ropes, mats, and other crafts.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
