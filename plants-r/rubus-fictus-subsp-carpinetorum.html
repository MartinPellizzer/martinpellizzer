Rubus fictus subsp. carpinetorum

Rubus fictus subsp.

carpinetorum, commonly known as the Carpinetum raspberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
