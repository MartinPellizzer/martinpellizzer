Randia speciosa

Randia speciosa, commonly known as the beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces striking purple berries that are a notable feature, often attracting birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Randia speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
