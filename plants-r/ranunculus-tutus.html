Ranunculus tutus

Ranunculus tutus, commonly known as the white crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with five to eight petals, and are surrounded by numerous sepals.

The leaves are palmately divided, contributing to its distinctive, delicate appearance.
