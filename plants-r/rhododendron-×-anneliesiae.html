Rhododendron × anneliesiae

Rhododendron × anneliesiae, commonly known as Anneliesiae Rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron catawbiense and Rhododendron ferrugineum.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations depending on the parent species involved.
