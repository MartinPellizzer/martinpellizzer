Ranunculus bulbosus var. multifidus

Ranunculus bulbosus var. multifidus, commonly known as the multifidus buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a bulbous root system and produces delicate, yellow flowers with five to nine petal-like sepals. The leaves are typically trifoliate or pinnately divided, with a glossy, dark green appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This variant is distinguished by its finely divided foliage and is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
