Rosa rubiginosa var. pseudohystrix

Rosa rubiginosa var. pseudohystrix, commonly known as the rough-stemmed rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark red to purplish bark, which becomes rough and fissured with age. The plant produces fragrant, pink to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and wildlife benefits.
