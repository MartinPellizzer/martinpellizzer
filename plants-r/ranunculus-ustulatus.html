Ranunculus ustulatus

Ranunculus ustulatus, commonly known as the scorched buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and it produces small, greenish fruits that contain seeds.

Ranunculus ustulatus is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic properties.
