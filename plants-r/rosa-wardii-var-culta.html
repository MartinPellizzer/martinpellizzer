Rosa wardii var. culta

Rosa wardii var. culta, commonly known as the cultivated form of the Ward's rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with arching, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
