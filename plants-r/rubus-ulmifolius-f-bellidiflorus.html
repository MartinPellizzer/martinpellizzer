Rubus ulmifolius f. bellidiflorus

Rubus ulmifolius f. bellidiflorus, commonly known as the bellflower rose, is a variant of the common blackberry.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are serrated and somewhat oval in shape. Its fruit, like other Rubus species, is a aggregate of small drupes, typically dark purple when mature.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
