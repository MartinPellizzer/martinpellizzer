Ranunculus longicaulis subsp. pulchellus

Ranunculus longicaulis subsp.

pulchellus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers with 5-15 sepals.

The leaves are palmately divided, with lobes that are often hairy and have a distinctive, serrated edge. This subspecies thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
