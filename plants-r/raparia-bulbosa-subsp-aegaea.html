Raparia bulbosa subsp. aegaea

Raparia bulbosa subsp.

aegaea, commonly known as the Aegean bulbous rapeseed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in coastal or mountainous areas.

The plant features a short, tuberous rootstock and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
