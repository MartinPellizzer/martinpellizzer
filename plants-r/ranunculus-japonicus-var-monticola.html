Ranunculus japonicus var. monticola

Ranunculus japonicus var. monticola, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup variant monticola, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. It is characterized by its short, upright stems and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it prefers cool climates.
