Rubus ulmifolius microgene serriculatus

Rubus ulmifolius microgene serriculatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and aggregate in clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
