Rhytidophyllum auriculatum var. angustatum

Rhytidophyllum auriculatum var. angustatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed silverleaf, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, waxy leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate floral display. It is often found in rocky outcrops and dry shrublands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
