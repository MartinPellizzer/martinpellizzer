Rubia peregrina var. typica

Rubia peregrina var. typica, commonly known as the common wood rue, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The leaves and flowers have been used historically in herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
