Rosa tomentosa var. barcensis

Rosa tomentosa var. barcensis, commonly known as the Barcens rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with thorny stems and oval-shaped leaves covered in fine, white hairs. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its hardiness and adaptability to various climates.
