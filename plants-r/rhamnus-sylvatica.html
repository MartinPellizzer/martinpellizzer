Rhamnus sylvatica

Rhamnus sylvatica, commonly known as the American hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by edible, dark purple berries that are a food source for various wildlife. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 6 meters.

Rhamnus sylvatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
