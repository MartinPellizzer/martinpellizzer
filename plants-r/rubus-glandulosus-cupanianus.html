Rubus glandulosus cupanianus

Rubus glandulosus cupanianus, commonly known as the glandular blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are white and have five petals, typical of the Rubus genus.

The species is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
