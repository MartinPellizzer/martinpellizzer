Richterago riparia

Richterago riparia, commonly known as the river willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, exhibiting a spreading habit with multiple stems. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in catkins, which are characteristic of the Salicaceae family.

Richterago riparia is valued for its ability to stabilize stream banks and provide habitat for wildlife.
