Rubus lamprothrix

Rubus lamprothrix, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple or black when mature, though they are often smaller and less flavorful than other blackberry varieties. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name.

This shrub thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
