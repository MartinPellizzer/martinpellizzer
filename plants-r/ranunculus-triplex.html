Ranunculus triplex

Ranunculus triplex, commonly known as the three-leaved crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy and often toxic species. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three leaflets, and its delicate, five-petaled flowers that range in color from yellow to pink. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ranunculus triplex is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
