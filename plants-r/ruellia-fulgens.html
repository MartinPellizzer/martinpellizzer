Ruellia fulgens

Ruellia fulgens, commonly known as the shining ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to deep red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Ruellia fulgens thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its blooming period extends from spring to summer, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
