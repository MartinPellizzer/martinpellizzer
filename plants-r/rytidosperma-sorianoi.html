Rytidosperma sorianoi

Rytidosperma sorianoi, commonly known as the Simpson grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of Australia, particularly in the Northern Territory and Western Australia. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Rytidosperma sorianoi typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
