Rubus vulgaris var. silvaticus

Rubus vulgaris var. silvaticus, commonly known as brambles or blackberries, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and they are followed by the edible, dark purple berries.

The species is widely distributed across Europe and parts of Asia.
