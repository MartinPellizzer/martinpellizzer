Rhododendron saxicola

Rhododendron saxicola, commonly known as the rock rhododendron, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
