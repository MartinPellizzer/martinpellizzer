Rubus × bosquetianus

Rubus × bosquetianus, commonly known as the Bosquet raspberry, is a hybrid species within the genus Rubus, belonging to the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep red color. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils and partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
