Rubus serratifolius

Rubus serratifolius, commonly known as the serrated leaf raspberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which have serrated edges, and its prickly stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family, which includes many other fruit-bearing plants.
