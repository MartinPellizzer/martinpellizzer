Rhododendron pseudomurudense

Rhododendron pseudomurudense, commonly known as the Burmese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural settings.
