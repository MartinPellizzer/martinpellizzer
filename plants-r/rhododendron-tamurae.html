Rhododendron tamurae

Rhododendron tamurae, commonly known as the Tamura rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron tamurae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
