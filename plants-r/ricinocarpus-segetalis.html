Ricinocarpus segetalis

Ricinocarpus segetalis, commonly known as the grain bean or wild bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical significance lies in its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

However, it is also noted for containing toxic compounds, making it a plant of both ecological and cautionary interest.
