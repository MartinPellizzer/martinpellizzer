Ranunculus bequaertii var. minor

Ranunculus bequaertii var. minor, commonly known as the lesser bequaert's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers with five to eight petals and a hairy, glandular stem. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish-brown achenes as its fruit.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
