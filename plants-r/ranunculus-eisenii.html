Ranunculus eisenii

Ranunculus eisenii, commonly known as the Eisen's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed, with a distinctive yellow flower that has five to seven petals. Its flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is known for its delicate, cup-shaped appearance.

Ranunculus eisenii is often found in high-altitude meadows and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
