Ranunculus laetus subsp. laetiformis

Ranunculus laetus subsp.

laetiformis, commonly known as the yellow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features delicate, lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly broader and more elongated petals compared to the typical Ranunculus laetus.
