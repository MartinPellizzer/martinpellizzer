Ranunculus acrifoliiformis

Ranunculus acrifoliiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and deeply lobed leaves that are typically narrow and serrated. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which is characterized by its showy flowers and often toxic sap.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
