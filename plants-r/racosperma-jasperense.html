Racosperma jasperense

Racosperma jasperense, commonly known as the Jasper's acacia, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its woody stems and adaptability to arid conditions make it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The plant is often found in sandy soils and is used in traditional Aboriginal practices.
