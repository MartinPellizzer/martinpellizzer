Rhododendron quadrasianum var. villosum

Rhododendron quadrasianum var. villosum, commonly known as the hairy rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe and southwestern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to red.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
