Ranunculus auricomus subsp. concinnifolius

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

concinnifolius, commonly known as the golden cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere. It features bright yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals, which are often mistaken for true petals.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with three leaflets that are usually deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a vibrant and hardy ornamental plant.
