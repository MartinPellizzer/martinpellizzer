Riencourtia ovata

Riencourtia ovata, commonly known as the ovate bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers, which are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its adaptability in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
