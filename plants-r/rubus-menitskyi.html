Rubus menitskyi

Rubus menitskyi, commonly known as the Menitsky raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their edible fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, aggregate fruits that are generally not eaten by humans.

The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in temperate climates.
