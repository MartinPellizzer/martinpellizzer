Rosa banksiae simplex

Rosa banksiae simplex, commonly known as the Banks' rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its simple, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its showy blossoms. It is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in botanical gardens.
