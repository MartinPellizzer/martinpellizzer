Ranunculus taiwanensis

Ranunculus taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy and often toxic species. The plant is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.
