Rubus hypoleucus proles clethraphilus

Rubus hypoleucus proles clethraphilus, commonly known as the clethraphilus raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forests and streamside areas.

Due to its limited range and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
