Rhynchocarpus glaber

Rhynchocarpus glaber, commonly known as the naked fruit legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits that remain attached to the stem. Rhynchocarpus glaber is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
