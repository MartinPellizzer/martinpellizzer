Rosa × centifolia

Rosa × centifolia, commonly known as the hundred-petaled rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the cross between Rosa damascena and Rosa gallica.

It is characterized by its fragrant, double flowers that can have up to 100 petals, giving it its common name. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Rosa × centifolia is also used in the perfume and culinary industries due to its essential oils.
