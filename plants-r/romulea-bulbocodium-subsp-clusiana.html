Romulea bulbocodium subsp. clusiana

Romulea bulbocodium subsp.

clusiana, commonly known as the Clusian rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit.

The plant produces striking, spurred flowers in shades of pink and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its bulbs are adapted for survival in rocky, well-drained soils, and it thrives in full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
