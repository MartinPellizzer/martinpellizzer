Rhamnus crocea subsp. pirifolia

Rhamnus crocea subsp.

pirifolia, commonly known as the yellow buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by black, egg-shaped berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
