Ragatelus crinitus

Ragatelus crinitus, commonly known as the curly hair plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, curling foliage that resembles flowing hair, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

While not widely cultivated, it is occasionally grown as an ornamental plant due to its unusual appearance.
