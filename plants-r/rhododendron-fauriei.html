Rhododendron fauriei

Rhododendron fauriei, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in acidic soils and thrives in cool, moist climates with partial shade. Its leaves are leathery and mid to dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron fauriei is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as part of native plant landscapes.
