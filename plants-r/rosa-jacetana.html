Rosa jacetana

Rosa jacetana, commonly known as the Spanish rose, is a species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include simple, alternate leaves and an erect growth habit.

Rosa jacetana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
