Restrepiella bicallosa

Restrepiella bicallosa, commonly known as the hairy leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy leaves and striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool and humid conditions. It has a short flowering period, usually during the spring or early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
