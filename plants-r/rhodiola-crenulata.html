Rhodiola crenulata

Rhodiola crenulata, commonly known as the alpine rose or king's crown, is a perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-like growth of fleshy, rhubarb-like leaves and clusters of yellow or orange flowers. The plant is adapted to cold, arid environments and thrives in rocky soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. Rhodiola crenulata is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal medicine for its adaptogenic and antioxidant benefits.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important species in alpine ecosystems.
