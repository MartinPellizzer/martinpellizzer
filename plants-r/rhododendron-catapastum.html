Rhododendron catapastum

Rhododendron catapastum, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring with flowers ranging in color from pink to white. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

Rhododendron catapastum prefers well-drained, acidic soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
