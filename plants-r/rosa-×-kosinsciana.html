Rosa × kosinsciana

Rosa × kosinsciana, commonly known as the Kosinsciana rose, is a hybrid rose species that originated from crosses between Rosa damascena and Rosa centifolia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to deep pink. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn, and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and aroma.

The botanical features of Rosa × kosinsciana make it a popular choice for both landscape design and floral arrangements.
