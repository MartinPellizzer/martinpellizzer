Rubus marschallianus

Rubus marschallianus, commonly known as the marshallian blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland areas and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the edible berries, which are often used in jams and desserts.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
