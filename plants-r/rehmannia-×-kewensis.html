Rehmannia × kewensis

Rehmannia × kewensis, commonly known as the Kew rehmannia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rehmannia glutinosa and Rehmannia roxburghii.

It is native to China and is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in umbels, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This hybrid is valued for its medicinal properties and is cultivated for both ornamental and therapeutic purposes.
