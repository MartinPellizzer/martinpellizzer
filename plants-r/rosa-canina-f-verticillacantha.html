Rosa canina f. verticillacantha

Rosa canina f. verticillacantha, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces red, hairy berries that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its morphology reflects adaptations to its natural habitat, making it a resilient and hardy species.
