Racosperma dorsennum

Racosperma dorsennum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for native wildlife.

Its seeds are adapted for dispersal by wind, contributing to its distribution across arid and semi-arid regions.
