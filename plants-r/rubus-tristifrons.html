Rubus tristifrons

Rubus tristifrons, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, typically found in woodland edges and hedgerows. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and white, often appearing in clusters, and it produces red or black berries that are edible but have a tart flavor.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
