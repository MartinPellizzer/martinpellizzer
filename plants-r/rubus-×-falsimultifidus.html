Rubus × falsimultifidus

Rubus × falsimultifidus, commonly known as the false multifidus raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the genus Rubus, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, bright red drupelets, which are typically clustered together. Its leaves are usually compound, with multiple leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruits.
