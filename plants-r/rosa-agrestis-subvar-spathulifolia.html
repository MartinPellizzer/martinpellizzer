Rosa agrestis subvar. spathulifolia

Rosa agrestis subvar. spathulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed wild rose, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features compound leaves with ovate to broadly ovate leaflets, often with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are thorny, and the plant is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and along stream banks.

The fruit is a pinkish-red hip that matures in autumn, containing numerous achenes.
