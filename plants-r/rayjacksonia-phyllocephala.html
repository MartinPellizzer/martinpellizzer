Rayjacksonia phyllocephala

Rayjacksonia phyllocephala, commonly known as the broad-leaved rayjacksonia, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its large, flat, leaf-like structures that resemble broad leaves, which are actually modified stems called phyllodes. The plant exhibits a shrub-like growth habit with multiple branches and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and adaptation to harsh climates.
