Rosa spadana

Rosa spadana, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red or orange hips that are rich in vitamin C. Rosa spadana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hedging and ornamental gardening.

Its historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine highlights its cultural and practical significance.
