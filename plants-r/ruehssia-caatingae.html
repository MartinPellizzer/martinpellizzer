Ruehssia caatingae

Ruehssia caatingae, commonly known as the Caatinga ruehssia, is a shrub native to the Caatinga biome of northeastern Brazil.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, showing resilience to drought and poor soil.

The species plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
