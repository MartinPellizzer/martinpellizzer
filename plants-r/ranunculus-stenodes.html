Ranunculus stenodes

Ranunculus stenodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with five petals. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
