Rosa dawoensis

Rosa dawoensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian rose, is a species of shrub native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rosa dawoensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in the regions where it is found.
