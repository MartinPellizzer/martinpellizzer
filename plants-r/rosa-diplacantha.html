Rosa diplacantha

Rosa diplacantha, commonly known as the double-stemmed rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern and southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on long pedicels. Rosa diplacantha is characterized by its unique branching pattern, with two main stems emerging from the base.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
