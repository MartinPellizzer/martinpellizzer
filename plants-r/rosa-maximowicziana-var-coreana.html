Rosa maximowicziana var. coreana

Rosa maximowicziana var. coreana, commonly known as the Korean rose, is a species native to Korea and parts of eastern Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 5 to 7 oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Rosa maximowicziana var. coreana is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
