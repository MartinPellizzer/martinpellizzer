Rhodiola rosea subsp. atropurpurea

Rhodiola rosea subsp.

atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its fleshy, reddish roots and dark green, alternate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Its botanical features make it a hardy plant with potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
