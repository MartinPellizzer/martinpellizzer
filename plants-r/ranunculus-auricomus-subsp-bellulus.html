Ranunculus auricomus subsp. bellulus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

bellulus, commonly known as the bell-shaped crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive, bell-like shape.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact flowers compared to the typical *R. auricomus*.
