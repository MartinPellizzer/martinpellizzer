Reboudia erucarioides

Reboudia erucarioides, commonly known as the wax plant, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, waxy leaves that are often green with a bluish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, clustered in a cyathium, a specialized inflorescence typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Reboudia erucarioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and drought tolerance.
