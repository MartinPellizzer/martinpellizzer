Racosperma pygmaeum

Racosperma pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf acacia, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. Its small size and dense growth habit make it an important component of native vegetation in its habitat.

It also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insect species.
