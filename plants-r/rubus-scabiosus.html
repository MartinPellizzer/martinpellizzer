Rubus scabiosus

Rubus scabiosus, commonly known as rough bramble, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, aggregate berry that develops from the flower.

Rubus scabiosus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
