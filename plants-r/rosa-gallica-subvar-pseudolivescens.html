Rosa gallica subvar. pseudolivescens

Rosa gallica subvar. pseudolivescens, commonly known as the French rose, is a fragrant shrub native to Europe.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its historical use in perfumery and as a symbol of love adds to its cultural significance.
