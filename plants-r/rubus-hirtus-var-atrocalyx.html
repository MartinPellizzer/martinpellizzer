Rubus hirtus var. atrocalyx

Rubus hirtus var. atrocalyx, commonly known as the black-berried bramble, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, often forming dense thickets.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
