Rhizanthes lowii

Rhizanthes lowii, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique carnivorous plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its specialized, meat-eating structures called pitchers, which are modified leaves designed to trap and digest insects. The plant's flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters at the base of the plant. Rhizanthes lowii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires a high level of atmospheric moisture to flourish.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptation to nutrient-poor soils through its carnivorous behavior.
