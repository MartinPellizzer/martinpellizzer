Ruellia affinis

Ruellia affinis, commonly known as the purpletop verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are usually violet or purple in color and bloom in the summer months. Ruellia affinis thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
