Rosa rubiginosa nemoralis

Rosa rubiginosa nemoralis, commonly known as the brambles or the dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is noted for its aromatic foliage and its role in supporting local wildlife.
