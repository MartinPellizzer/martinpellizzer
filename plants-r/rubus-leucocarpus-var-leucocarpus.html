Rubus leucocarpus var. leucocarpus

Rubus leucocarpus var. leucocarpus, commonly known as the white-fruited blackberry, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow fruits that are typically not consumed by humans but are important for wildlife. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and plant biodiversity research.
