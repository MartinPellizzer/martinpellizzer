Rubus corei

Rubus corei, commonly known as Korean raspberry, is a species of bramble native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Rubus corei plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
