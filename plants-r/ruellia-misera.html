Ruellia misera

Ruellia misera, commonly known as the weeping ruellia, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of blue or purple tubular flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Ruellia misera thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread vigorously.
