Rubus wahlbergii var. frustratus

Rubus wahlbergii var. frustratus, commonly known as the Cape strawberry or the African raspberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits composed of numerous drupes, which are usually red when mature. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
