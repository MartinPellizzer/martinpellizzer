Reseda laciniata

Reseda laciniata, commonly known as cut-leaved reseda, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Reseda laciniata is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like cabbage and mustard.
