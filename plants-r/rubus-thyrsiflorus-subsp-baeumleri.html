Rubus thyrsiflorus subsp. baeumleri

Rubus thyrsiflorus subsp.

baeumleri, commonly known as the Baeumler's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
