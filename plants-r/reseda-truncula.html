Reseda truncula

Reseda truncula, commonly known as narrowleaf madder, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spindle-shaped roots that contain a red dye used historically in textile coloring. Reseda truncula thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and historically valuable.
