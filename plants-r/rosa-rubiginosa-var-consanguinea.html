Rosa rubiginosa var. consanguinea

Rosa rubiginosa var. consanguinea, commonly known as the rough rose or crimson rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, oval, yellowish fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal use.
