Reseda phyteuma var. platysepala

Reseda phyteuma var. platysepala, commonly known as the broad-sepal reseda, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its bright blooms and hardy nature.
