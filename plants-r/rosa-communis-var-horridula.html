Rosa communis var. horridula

Rosa communis var. horridula, commonly known as the rough rose, is a variant of the common rose characterized by its rugged, thorny stems and foliage.

This plant typically produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in temperate climates. The leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well-suited for garden cultivation and can tolerate a range of soil conditions.
