Rubus hirtus subsp. amoenus

Rubus hirtus subsp.

amoenus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically bright red when ripe. These fruits are edible and widely used in culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
