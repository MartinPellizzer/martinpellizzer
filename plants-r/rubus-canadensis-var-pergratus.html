Rubus canadensis var. pergratus

Rubus canadensis var. pergratus, commonly known as the Canadian blackberry.

It is a variant of the common blackberry, characterized by its dark purple to black berries and thorny stems. This shrub typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, with three to five lobes, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The plant produces edible fruits that are often used in jams and desserts.
