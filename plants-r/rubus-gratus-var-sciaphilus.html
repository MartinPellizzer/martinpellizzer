Rubus gratus var. sciaphilus

Rubus gratus var. sciaphilus, commonly known as the shade-loving raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant adapted to grow in low-light conditions.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, aggregate fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces pale yellow to pink flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
