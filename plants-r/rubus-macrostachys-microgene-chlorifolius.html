Rubus macrostachys microgene chlorifolius

Rubus macrostachys microgene chlorifolius, commonly known as the giant raspberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often lobed and have a serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruits, which resemble raspberries, are edible and have a tart flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
