Rosa umbrosa

Rosa umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to regions of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in thorns.

Rosa umbrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
