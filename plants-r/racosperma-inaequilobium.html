Racosperma inaequilobium

Racosperma inaequilobium, commonly known as the unequal-winged acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, typical of the legume family. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Racosperma inaequilobium plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
