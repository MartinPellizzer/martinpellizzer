Ranunculus godronii

Ranunculus godronii, commonly known as Godron's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

Ranunculus godronii is often found in meadows and open slopes at high elevations.
