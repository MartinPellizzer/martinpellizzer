Ruellia contorta

Ruellia contorta, commonly known as narrowleaf ruellia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Ruellia contorta is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions, making it a versatile garden plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
