Rivina aurantiaca

Rivina aurantiaca, commonly known as the orangeleaf or redroot ivy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, bright orange to red leaves that are velvety to the touch. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and year-round foliage color.
