Rubus asper var. myriadenus

Rubus asper var. myriadenus, commonly known as the many-stemmed bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and compound leaves with numerous leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a common species in ecological studies and horticulture.
