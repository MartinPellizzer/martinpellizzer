Rubus hypoleucus proles boraeanus

Rubus hypoleucus proles boraeanus, commonly known as the Boreal Raspberry, is a shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in boreal and subarctic environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a significant role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife.
