Rubroshorea carapae

Rubroshorea carapae, commonly known as the redshore flamebush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs, providing some protection against salt spray. Its root system is adapted to thrive in sandy soils, making it resilient to coastal conditions.

The plant's unique coloration and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
