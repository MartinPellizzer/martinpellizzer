Rubus undulatus var. cinereus

Rubus undulatus var. cinereus, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble, is a variant of the trailing blackberry species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray undersides on the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its thorny stems and compact growth habit make it a hardy, low-maintenance plant for ornamental and ecological purposes.
