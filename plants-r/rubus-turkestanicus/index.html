Rubus Turkestanicus

Rubus turkestanicus is a lesser-known species of bramble native to Central Asia. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in mountainous regions and along rocky slopes. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. While not widely cultivated, it plays an important role in local ecosystems and traditional medicine. Its resilience and adaptability make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
