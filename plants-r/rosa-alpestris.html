Rosa alpestris

Rosa alpestris, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine, silvery hairs.

Rosa alpestris is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
