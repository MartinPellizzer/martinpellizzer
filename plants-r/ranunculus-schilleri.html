Ranunculus schilleri

Ranunculus schilleri, commonly known as the Schiller's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically feature five to eight bright yellow petals with a distinctive central blotch. The plant has smooth, glossy leaves arranged in a basal rosette and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ranunculus schilleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
