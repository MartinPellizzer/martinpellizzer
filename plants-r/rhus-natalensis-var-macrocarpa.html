Rhus natalensis var. macrocarpa

Rhus natalensis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the Natal buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by distinctive, inflated fruit capsules that turn red as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
