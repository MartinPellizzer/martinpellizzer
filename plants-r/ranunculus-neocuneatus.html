Ranunculus neocuneatus

Ranunculus neocuneatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with five to nine sepals that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, hairy stems that bear the showy blooms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early spring blossoms.
