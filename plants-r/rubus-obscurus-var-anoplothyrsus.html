Rubus obscurus var. anoplothyrsus

Rubus obscurus var. anoplothyrsus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to western North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
