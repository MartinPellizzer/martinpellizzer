Rhynchospora filifolia var. crinipes

Rhynchospora filifolia var. crinipes, commonly known as the silky threadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
