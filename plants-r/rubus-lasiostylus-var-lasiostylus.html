Rubus lasiostylus var. lasiostylus

Rubus lasiostylus var. lasiostylus, commonly known as the hairy-leaf blackberry.

It is a shrub native to eastern Australia, characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
