Rhododendron nakaharae

Rhododendron nakaharae, commonly known as the Nakahara rhododendron, is a species native to Japan, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Hokkaido.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. Its flowers are usually pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron nakaharae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
