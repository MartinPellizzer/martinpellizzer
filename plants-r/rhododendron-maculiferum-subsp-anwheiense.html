Rhododendron maculiferum subsp. anwheiense

Rhododendron maculiferum subsp.

anwheiense, commonly known as the Anwheiense rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white.

The plant typically grows at high altitudes, preferring cool, moist climates with well-drained acidic soils. Its floral display is particularly striking in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
