Rubus pseudohistriculus

Rubus pseudohistriculus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in partial shade.

The botanical features of Rubus pseudohistriculus make it an important part of the local ecosystem, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
