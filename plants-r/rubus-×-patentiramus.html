Rubus × patentiramus

Rubus × patentiramus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus fruticosus and Rubus ulmifolius, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. This shrub is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.
