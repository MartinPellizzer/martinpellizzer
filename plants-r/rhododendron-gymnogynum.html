Rhododendron gymnogynum

Rhododendron gymnogynum, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant prefers acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Rhododendron gymnogynum is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small wildlife.
