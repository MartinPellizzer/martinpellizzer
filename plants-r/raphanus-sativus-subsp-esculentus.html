Raphanus sativus subsp. esculentus

Raphanus sativus subsp.

esculentus, commonly known as the garden radish, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its fleshy, edible roots that are typically reddish in color and have a spicy, peppery flavor.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in a raceme, and its leaves are lobed and somewhat hairy. The roots are the primary edible part, while the leaves are also consumed in some culinary traditions.

It thrives in cool climates and is often grown as a vegetable in temperate regions.
