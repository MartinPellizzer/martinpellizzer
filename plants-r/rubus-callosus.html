Rubus callosus

Rubus callosus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins and hairy surfaces. Its flowers are usually white or pink, with five petals, and it produces edible fruits that are often used in jams and wines.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
