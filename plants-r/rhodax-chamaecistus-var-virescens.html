Rhodax chamaecistus var. virescens

Rhodax chamaecistus var. virescens, commonly known as the green-flowered rhodax, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, evergreen leaves that are typically leathery and dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale green. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
