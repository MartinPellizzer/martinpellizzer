Rottboellia filiformis

Rottboellia filiformis, commonly known as slender rottbollia, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often mistaken for grasses or sedges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib.

Rottboellia filiformis is often found in wetland habitats and can thrive in disturbed soils.
