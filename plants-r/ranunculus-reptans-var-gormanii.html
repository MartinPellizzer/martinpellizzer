Ranunculus reptans var. gormanii

Ranunculus reptans var. gormanii, commonly known as the Gorman's creeping buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow, star-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
