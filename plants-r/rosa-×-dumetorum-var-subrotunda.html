Rosa × dumetorum var. subrotunda

Rosa × dumetorum var. subrotunda, commonly known as the creeping yellow rose, is a hybrid shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically dark green, glossy, and covered with fine, soft hairs. This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense mats.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
