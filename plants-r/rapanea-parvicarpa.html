Rapanea parvicarpa

Rapanea parvicarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited rapanea, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its small, clustered flowers are typically greenish-yellow, and the fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

Rapanea parvicarpa is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
