Rosa arabica

Rosa arabica, commonly known as the Arabian rose, is a species native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub-like plant with woody stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has small, serrated leaves and produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry climates with minimal water.

Rosa arabica is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
