Racosperma hammondii

Racosperma hammondii, commonly known as the Hammond's acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, which are typical of the genus. Its leaves are bipinnate, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Racosperma hammondii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.
