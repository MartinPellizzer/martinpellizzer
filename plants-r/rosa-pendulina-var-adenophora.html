Rosa pendulina var. adenophora

Rosa pendulina var. adenophora, commonly known as the腺花蔷薇, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its graceful, weeping growth habit and clusters of fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
