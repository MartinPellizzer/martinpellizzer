Ranunculus rigidus

Ranunculus rigidus, commonly known as the rigid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a short, stout stem with palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and wetlands.

Ranunculus rigidus is characterized by its distinctive, spurred sepals and its ability to spread through rhizomes.
