Rhamnus crenata f. parvifolia

Rhamnus crenata f. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf buckthorn, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length, giving it a dense, bushy appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by small, dark blue berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its foliage provides valuable cover for wildlife, making it an important plant in ecological gardens.
