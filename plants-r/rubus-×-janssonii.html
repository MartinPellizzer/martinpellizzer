Rubus × janssonii

Rubus × janssonii, commonly known as the Jansson’s blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to regions of North America, particularly in the western United States. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Botanically, it exhibits features of both its parent species, including leaf morphology and flowering habits.
