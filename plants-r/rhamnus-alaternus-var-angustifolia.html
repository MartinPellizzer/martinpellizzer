Rhamnus alaternus var. angustifolia

Rhamnus alaternus var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue-black berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland areas.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
