Ranunculus diversifolius proles peltatus

Ranunculus diversifolius proles peltatus, commonly known as the peltate pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, peltate (shield-shaped) leaves.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with a prominent central stigma. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
