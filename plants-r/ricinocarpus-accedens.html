Ricinocarpus accedens

Ricinocarpus accedens, commonly known as the beach hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red or yellow. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Ricinocarpus accedens is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
