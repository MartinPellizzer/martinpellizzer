Raphanistrum innocuum var. ochroleucum

Raphanistrum innocuum var. ochroleucum, commonly known as the yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, yellow-green stems and bristly, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in agricultural fields. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy surface.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as a food source for various insects and small animals.
