Rubus serpens var. celtoideus

Rubus serpens var. celtoideus, commonly known as the creeping blackberry.

This variant is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. It produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible, though often tart and less palatable than other blackberry varieties. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and grows in moist, wooded areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
