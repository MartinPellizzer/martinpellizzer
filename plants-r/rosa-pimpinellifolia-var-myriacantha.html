Rosa pimpinellifolia var. myriacantha

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. myriacantha, commonly known as the many-thorned rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, thorny stems that often grow along rocky or coastal areas. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand harsh, arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in coastal and dry garden settings.
