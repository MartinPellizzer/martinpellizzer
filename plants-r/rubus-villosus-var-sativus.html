Rubus villosus var. sativus

Rubus villosus var. sativus, commonly known as the cultivated blackberry.

This variety is a deciduous shrub native to North America, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and are widely used in culinary and commercial applications.
