Rosa sepium var. macrocarpa

Rosa sepium var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces large, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a glossy appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
