Rhynchosia pitcheria

Rhynchosia pitcheria, commonly known as the pitcher plant, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive tubular flowers that resemble pitchers, which are adapted for attracting pollinators. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
