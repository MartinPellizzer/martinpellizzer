Raphia sudanica

Raphia sudanica, commonly known as the giant palm or Sudanese raphia, is a large, tall palm native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its massive, fan-shaped leaves that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces large, yellowish-orange inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers. Its trunk is typically unbranched and covered with a fibrous, spongy material.

Raphia sudanica is valued for its strong, durable fibers used in traditional crafts and construction.
