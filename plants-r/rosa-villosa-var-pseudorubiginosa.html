Rosa villosa var. pseudorubiginosa

Rosa villosa var. pseudorubiginosa, commonly known as the hairy rose or the false red rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, hairy leaves, and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history in traditional medicine.

The varietal name 'pseudorubiginosa' refers to its resemblance to the more brightly colored Rosa rubiginosa.
