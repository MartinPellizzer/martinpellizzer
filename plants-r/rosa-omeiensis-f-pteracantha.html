Rosa omeiensis f. pteracantha

Rosa omeiensis f. pteracantha, commonly known as the Pteracantha rose, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the rose family.

It is characterized by its unique, thorned stems that are more pronounced than those of typical roses, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a slightly curled petal shape. It is native to China and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is a sought-after plant among rose enthusiasts.
