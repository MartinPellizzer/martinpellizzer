Rubus odoratus var. nutkanus

Rubus odoratus var. nutkanus, commonly known as the Nutkan blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a distinctive, sweet aroma. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and natural habitats alike.
