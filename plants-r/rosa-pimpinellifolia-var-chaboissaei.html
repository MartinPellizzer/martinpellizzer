Rosa pimpinellifolia var. chaboissaei

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. chaboissaei, commonly known as the Chaboissaei rock rose, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to southern France.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a slightly serrated edge.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly valued by horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
