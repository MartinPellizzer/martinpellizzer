Rubus hebecarpos f. gonzaloi

Rubus hebecarpos f. gonzaloi, commonly known as the Mexican blackberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is native to regions of Mexico.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
