Rosa coriifolia var. subpubescens

Rosa coriifolia var. subpubescens, commonly known as the white shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, with oval, serrated leaflets that turn red in the fall. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
