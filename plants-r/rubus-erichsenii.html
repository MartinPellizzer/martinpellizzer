Rubus erichsenii

Rubus erichsenii, commonly known as the Erichsen's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. The plant is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark berries that are edible.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and chaparral habitats.
