Rosa tomentella var. monregalensis

Rosa tomentella var. monregalensis, commonly known as the Monrejal rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are dark green and covered in a thick layer of fine hairs, providing insulation against cold temperatures.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
