Rhododendron benhallii

Rhododendron benhallii, commonly known as the Benhall's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Rhododendron benhallii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in specific climatic conditions.
