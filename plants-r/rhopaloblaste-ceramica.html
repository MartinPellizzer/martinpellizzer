Rhopaloblaste ceramica

Rhopaloblaste ceramica, commonly known as the Ceramic Bladdergrass, is a species of flowering plant in the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique leaf structure, featuring a specialized bladder-like structure called a rhopaloblast, which is adapted for water storage. This plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The rhopaloblasts are particularly prominent in the lower part of the plant, aiding in drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant adaptations to harsh environments.
