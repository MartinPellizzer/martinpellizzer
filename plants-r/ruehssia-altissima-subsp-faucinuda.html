Ruehssia altissima subsp. faucinuda

Ruehssia altissima subsp.

faucinuda, commonly known as the highland ruehssia, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly aromatic scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
