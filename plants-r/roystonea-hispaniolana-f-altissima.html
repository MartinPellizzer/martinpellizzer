Roystonea hispaniolana f. altissima

Roystonea hispaniolana f. altissima, commonly known as the Hispaniolan royal palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the island of Hispaniola.

It is characterized by its long, feather-like leaves and a slender trunk that can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant has a slow growth rate and is adapted to tropical climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping in warm regions.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
