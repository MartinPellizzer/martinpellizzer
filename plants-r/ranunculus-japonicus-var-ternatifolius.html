Ranunculus japonicus var. ternatifolius

Ranunculus japonicus var. ternatifolius, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup variant with trifoliate leaves, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to eleven petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves, meaning each leaf is divided into three lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
