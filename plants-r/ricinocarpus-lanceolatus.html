Ricinocarpus lanceolatus

Ricinocarpus lanceolatus, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Araceae and is known for its unique morphological features.
