Rumex arifolius var. carpaticus

Rumex arifolius var. carpaticus, commonly known as the Carpathian dock, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian region of Europe.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish flowers that develop into elongated, reddish-brown seed pods. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.
