Rosa virginiana var. plena

Rosa virginiana var. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered wild rose, is a cultivar of the Virgin's rose species.

It is characterized by its abundant, full bloom of pink to red semi-double to fully double flowers. The plant is native to eastern North America and thrives in woodland and open areas. It has fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that appear in late spring to early summer.

The shrub has thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves, making it a hardy and ornamental plant.
