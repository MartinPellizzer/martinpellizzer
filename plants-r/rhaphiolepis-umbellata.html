Rhaphiolepis umbellata

Rhaphiolepis umbellata, commonly known as the Chinese boxwood, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.
