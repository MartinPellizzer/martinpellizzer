Rhododendron nilagiricum

Rhododendron nilagiricum, commonly known as the Nilgiri rhododendron, is a species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring season. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a dense canopy that supports a variety of epiphytic plants.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
