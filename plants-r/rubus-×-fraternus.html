Rubus × fraternus

Rubus × fraternus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant that combines traits of blackberries and raspberries.

It is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its flavorful berries.

The botanical features of Rubus × fraternus make it a popular choice for both ornamental and edible purposes.
