Rubus idaeus var. sachalinensis

Rubus idaeus var. sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin raspberry, is a variant of the red raspberry that is native to the Russian Far East and parts of northeastern China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and the production of small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to different soil types.
