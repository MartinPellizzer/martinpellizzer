Ranunculus cordifolius

Ranunculus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other herbaceous plants.

Ranunculus cordifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
