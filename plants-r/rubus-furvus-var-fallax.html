Rubus furvus var. fallax

Rubus furvus var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.
