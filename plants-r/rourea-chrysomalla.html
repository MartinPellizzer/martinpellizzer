Rourea chrysomalla

Rourea chrysomalla, commonly known as the golden rourea, is a small evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow inflorescences that appear in clusters, making it a visually appealing plant. The leaves are typically elliptical and have a smooth texture, often with a slightly waxy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in horticultural settings.
