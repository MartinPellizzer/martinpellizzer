Ranunculus japonicus

Ranunculus japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to eleven petals, and its leaves are deeply lobed and trifoliate. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy and often toxic species.

Ranunculus japonicus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
