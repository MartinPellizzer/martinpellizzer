Racosperma pulchellum var. glaberrimum

Racosperma pulchellum var. glaberrimum, commonly known as the smooth-flowered racosperma, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows as a shrub with slender, hairy stems that become smooth in maturity. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and the leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
