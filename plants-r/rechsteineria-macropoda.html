Rechsteineria macropoda

Rechsteineria macropoda, commonly known as the giant rechsteineria, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

This plant is characterized by its large, waxy, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, forming a rosette structure that collects rainwater. The leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white trichomes, which help reduce water loss and reflect sunlight. It produces vibrant orange to red inflorescences that bloom in the early morning and last for several weeks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a popular subject in botanical research and conservation efforts.
