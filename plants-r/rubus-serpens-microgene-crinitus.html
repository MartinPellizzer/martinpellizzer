Rubus serpens microgene crinitus

Rubus serpens microgene crinitus, commonly known as the hairy bramble, is a shrub native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and its ability to form dense thickets. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring. Its fruits are edible berries that ripen in the summer and are often consumed by wildlife.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in ecological succession.
