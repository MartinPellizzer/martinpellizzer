Rubus rectangulifolius

Rubus rectangulifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, serrated leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
