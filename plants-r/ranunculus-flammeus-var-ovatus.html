Ranunculus flammeus var. ovatus

Ranunculus flammeus var. ovatus, commonly known as the flame-colored buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive central pattern resembling a flame. The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine, glandular dots. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens and natural habitats.
