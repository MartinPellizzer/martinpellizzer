Ranunculus auricomus subsp. sychnoschistus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

sychnoschistus, commonly known as the mountain buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous petals.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is characterized by its slightly different leaf structure and flower morphology compared to the typical R. auricomus.
