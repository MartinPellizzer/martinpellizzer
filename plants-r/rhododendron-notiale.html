Rhododendron notiale

Rhododendron notiale, commonly known as the Alpine rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in China and the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist, and well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to alpine meadows.

Rhododendron notiale is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
