Rhamnus communis var. aureovariegata

Rhamnus communis var. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden-leaved buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display yellow and green patterns, enhancing its ornamental value. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for use in gardens and as a hedge plant.
