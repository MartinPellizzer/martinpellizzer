Rubus pallescens

Rubus pallescens, commonly known as the pale bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of North America, particularly in the eastern United States. The plant is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart.

The botanical features of Rubus pallescens make it an important component of its native ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
