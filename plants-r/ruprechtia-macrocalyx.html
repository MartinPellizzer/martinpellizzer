Ruprechtia macrocalyx

Ruprechtia macrocalyx, commonly known as the giant ruprechtia, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, showcasing a vibrant green hue with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticultural enthusiasts alike.
