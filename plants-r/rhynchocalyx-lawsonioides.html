Rhynchocalyx lawsonioides

Rhynchocalyx lawsonioides, commonly known as the Lawson's trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
