Rosa perfecta

Rosa perfecta, commonly known as the everlasting rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is a perennial shrub that produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. Rosa perfecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

Its flowers are known to retain their color and form for an extended period, hence its common name.
