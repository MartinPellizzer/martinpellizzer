Rubus brevicaulis

Rubus brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to western North America, particularly found in California and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub ecosystems.

The plant's botanical features include trifoliate leaves and a shallow root system adapted to its arid environment.
