Ruellia nudiflora var. mirandae

Ruellia nudiflora var. mirandae, commonly known as the Mirandah ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. This variety is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
