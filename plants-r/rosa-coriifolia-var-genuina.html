Rosa coriifolia var. genuina

Rosa coriifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the Japanese coral tree, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It features delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters of five to seven petals. The plant has finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its elegant appearance.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
