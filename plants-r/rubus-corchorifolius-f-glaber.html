Rubus corchorifolius f. glaber

Rubus corchorifolius f. glaber, commonly known as the smoothleaf blackberry, is a cultivated variant of the blackberry plant.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and dark purple to black berries. The plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is valued for its edible fruits, which have a rich, tart flavor.

This form is often used in horticulture for its hardiness and fruit production.
