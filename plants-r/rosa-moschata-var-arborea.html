Rosa moschata var. arborea

Rosa moschata var. arborea, commonly known as the tree rose, is a unique variant of the musk rose that exhibits a tree-like growth habit.

It features a woody stem with multiple branches, making it distinct from the more shrub-like forms of Rosa moschata. The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges and a velvety texture.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various climates.
