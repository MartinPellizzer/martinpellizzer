Rubus australis var. schmidelioides

Rubus australis var. schmidelioides, commonly known as the Schmidelioides raspberry, is a variant of the Australian raspberry native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits composed of multiple drupes, which are usually red when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
