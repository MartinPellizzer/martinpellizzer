Rubus saxatilis var. canadensis

Rubus saxatilis var. canadensis, commonly known as Canada blackberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
