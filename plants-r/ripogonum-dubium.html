Ripogonum dubium

Ripogonum dubium, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth and attractive flowers make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
