Ranunculus delphiniifolius var. terrestris

Ranunculus delphiniifolius var. terrestris, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
