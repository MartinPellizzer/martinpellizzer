Rorippa nasturtium-aquaticum var. longisiliqua

Rorippa nasturtium-aquaticum var. longisiliqua, commonly known as watercress, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to temperate regions.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow in water or moist soil, and its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, and it is known for its peppery, edible leaves.

It thrives in freshwater environments and is often found in ponds, ditches, and along the edges of streams.
