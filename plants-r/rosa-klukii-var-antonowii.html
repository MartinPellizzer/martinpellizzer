Rosa klukii var. antonowii

Rosa klukii var. antonowii, commonly known as the Antonowii rose, is a compact, shrubby plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features dense, thornless stems and produces abundant, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is well-suited for container gardening and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy blooms and compact growth habit.
