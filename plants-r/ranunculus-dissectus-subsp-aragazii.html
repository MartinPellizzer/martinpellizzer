Ranunculus dissectus subsp. aragazii

Ranunculus dissectus subsp.

aragazii, commonly known as the Armenian buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and yellow flowers with five petals, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely dissected leaves and slightly smaller flowers compared to other members of the species.
