Rosa davurica f. alba

Rosa davurica f. alba, commonly known as the white shrub rose, is a cold-hardy deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features arching stems with thorns and is characterized by its fragrant, single or semi-double white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces hip fruits that are red-orange in color. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variety is prized for its delicate appearance and adaptability to temperate climates.
