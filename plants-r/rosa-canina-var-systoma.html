Rosa canina var. systoma

Rosa canina var. systoma, commonly known as the Siberian rosehip, is a shrub native to the cold climates of Eastern Europe and Siberia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces bright red, fuzzy fruits called rosehips, which are rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants. Rosa canina var. systoma is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting frost resistance and a prolonged flowering period.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and valuable for medicinal and culinary uses.
