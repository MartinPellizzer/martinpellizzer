Rubus rhapidorrhachis

Rubus rhapidorrhachis, commonly known as the rapid-growing blackberry.

It is a species of brambles native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate. Its flowers are small and white, producing edible berries that are similar to other Rubus species.

However, it is less commonly cultivated compared to other blackberry varieties due to its specific habitat requirements.
