Rosa myriacantha

Rosa myriacantha, commonly known as the many-thorned rose, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height, characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruits are red, hairy berries that contain numerous seeds.

Rosa myriacantha is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
