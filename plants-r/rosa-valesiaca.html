Rosa valesiaca

Rosa valesiaca, commonly known as the Valais rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sunlight.

Rosa valesiaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in perfumery due to its aromatic properties.
