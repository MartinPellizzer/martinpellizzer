Rosa pendulina f. intermedia

Rosa pendulina f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate weeping rose, is a unique cultivar of the Rosa pendulina species.

It is characterized by its long, drooping stems that produce clusters of fragrant, pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with glossy, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to the blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its elegant, cascading appearance.
