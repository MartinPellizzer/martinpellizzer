Rosa setigera f. serena

Rosa setigera f. serena, commonly known as the serena climbing rose, is a fragrant, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features arching stems with recurved, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has serrated, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and slightly hairy underneath. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden borders or trellises.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its sweet fragrance and graceful growth habit.
