Ranunculus heldreichii

Ranunculus heldreichii, commonly known as the Heldreich's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals that resemble additional petals. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem.

Ranunculus heldreichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and wildflower meadows.
