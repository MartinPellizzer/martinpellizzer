Rosa × caballicensis

Rosa × caballicensis, commonly known as the Caballicensis rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa centifolia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It is relatively hardy and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

This rose is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
