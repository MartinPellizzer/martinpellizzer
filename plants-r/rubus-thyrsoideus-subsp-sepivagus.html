Rubus thyrsoideus subsp. sepivagus

Rubus thyrsoideus subsp.

sepivagus, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, serrated leaves.

The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early summer, which are followed by red, edible berries. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
