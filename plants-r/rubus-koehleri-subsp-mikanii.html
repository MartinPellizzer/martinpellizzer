Rubus koehleri subsp. mikanii

Rubus koehleri subsp.

mikanii, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
