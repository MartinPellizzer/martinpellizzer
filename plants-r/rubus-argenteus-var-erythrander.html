Rubus argenteus var. erythrander

Rubus argenteus var. erythrander, commonly known as the redstem dewberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its foliage often has a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
