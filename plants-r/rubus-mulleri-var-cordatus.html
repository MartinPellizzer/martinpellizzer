Rubus mulleri var. cordatus

Rubus mulleri var. cordatus, commonly known as the heart-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and produces small, dark berries that are typically edible. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks. It has a woody structure with thorny stems and is part of the Rubiaceae family.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
