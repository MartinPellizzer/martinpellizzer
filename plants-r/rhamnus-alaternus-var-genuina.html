Rhamnus alaternus var. genuina

Rhamnus alaternus var. genuina, commonly known as the Italian buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are rich in anthocyanins. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This variety is closely related to other buckthorn species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
