Rubus myrianthus var. ellipticifolius

Rubus myrianthus var. ellipticifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
