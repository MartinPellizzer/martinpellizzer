Rhopalosciadium stereocalyx

Rhopalosciadium stereocalyx, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and grows up to 2 to 3 feet tall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.
