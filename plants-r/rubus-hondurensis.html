Rubus hondurensis

Rubus hondurensis, commonly known as Honduran blackberry, is a species of shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
