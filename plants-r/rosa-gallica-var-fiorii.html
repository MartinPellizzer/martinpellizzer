Rosa gallica var. fiorii

Rosa gallica var. fiorii, commonly known as the Italian pink or sweet briar, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, single or semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has arching, thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hedges and garden borders.

Its historical use in perfumery and as a source of rosehip fruit adds to its botanical significance.
