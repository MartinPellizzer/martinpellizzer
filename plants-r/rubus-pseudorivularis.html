Rubus pseudorivularis

Rubus pseudorivularis, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles like raspberries and blackberries.
