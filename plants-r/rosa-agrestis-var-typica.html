Rosa agrestis var. typica

Rosa agrestis var. typica, commonly known as the field rose, is a species native to various regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by red, hip-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for ornamental and ecological gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for supporting local wildlife and biodiversity.
