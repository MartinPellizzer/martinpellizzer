Racosperma ixiophyllum

Racosperma ixiophyllum, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in open woodlands and heathlands, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for native pollinators.

Its ecological importance makes it a valuable component of Australian flora.
