Rubus christiansenorum

Rubus christiansenorum, commonly known as Christiansen's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has serrated leaves and produces white to pale yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and forest margins.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
