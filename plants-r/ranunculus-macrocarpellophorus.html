Ranunculus macrocarpellophorus

Ranunculus macrocarpellophorus, commonly known as the large-seed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight yellow petals and a hairy, green stem. The plant's leaves are palmately divided, with lobes that are usually hairy and serrated. Its fruit is a follicle containing large, elongated seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
