Rhaphidorhynchus macrostachys

Rhaphidorhynchus macrostachys, commonly known as the large-stemmed rhaphidorhynchus, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and rosette-forming leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes. The plant produces striking, panicle-like inflorescences with showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are typically thick and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments.

This species is native to tropical regions of South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
