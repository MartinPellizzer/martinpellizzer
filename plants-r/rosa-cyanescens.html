Rosa cyanescens

Rosa cyanescens, commonly known as the blue rose, is a rare and highly sought-after plant due to its unique coloration.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of Asia, particularly China. The plant produces striking blue-pink flowers, a coloration achieved through the presence of specific pigments like delphinidin. Its botanical features include thorny stems, compound leaves, and fragrant blooms that attract pollinators.

Despite its beauty, Rosa cyanescens is challenging to cultivate due to its specific environmental requirements.
