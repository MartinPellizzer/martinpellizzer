Rosa canina var. christii

Rosa canina var. christii, commonly known as the Christ's rose or the dog rose, is a variant of the common rose.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fuzzy fruits called hips. These hips are rich in vitamin C and are used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, where it thrives in a variety of soil types.
