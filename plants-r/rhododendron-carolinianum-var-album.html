Rhododendron carolinianum var. album

Rhododendron carolinianum var. album, commonly known as the white Carolina rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often adorned with a faintly visible purple or pinkish hue.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
