Rubus kinashii f. microphylla

Rubus kinashii f. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more densely packed leaves compared to other Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
