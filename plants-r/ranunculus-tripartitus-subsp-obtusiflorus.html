Ranunculus tripartitus subsp. obtusiflorus

Ranunculus tripartitus subsp.

obtusiflorus, commonly known as the obtuse-flowered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often with a dark center.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with three to five lobes, and are usually covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
