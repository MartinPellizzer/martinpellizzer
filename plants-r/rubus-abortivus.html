Rubus abortivus

Rubus abortivus, commonly known as the common blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible blackberries. Its berries are typically small, tart, and often used in jams and preserves.

Rubus abortivus thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.
