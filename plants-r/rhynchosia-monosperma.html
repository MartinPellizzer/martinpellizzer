Rhynchosia monosperma

Rhynchosia monosperma, commonly known as the single-seeded cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated pods containing a single, large seed.

Rhynchosia monosperma is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated as a food crop in various parts of Africa and Asia.
