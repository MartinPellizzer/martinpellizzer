Rubus fruticosus var. hybridus

Rubus fruticosus var. hybridus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry shrub, is a species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in a variety of soil types. It produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible berries.

This shrub is often cultivated for its fruit and is also valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
