Rosa vinetorum

Rosa vinetorum, commonly known as the climbing rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit, with long, flexible stems that can climb or trail along surfaces. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and its thorns are present along the stems.

Rosa vinetorum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or walls.
