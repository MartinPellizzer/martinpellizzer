Roegneria canina subsp. hispanica

Roegneria canina subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish doggrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
