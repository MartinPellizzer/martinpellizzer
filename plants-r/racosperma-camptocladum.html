Racosperma camptocladum

Racosperma camptocladum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of many legumes.

Racosperma camptocladum is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
