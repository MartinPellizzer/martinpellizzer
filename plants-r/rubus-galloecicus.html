Rubus galloecicus

Rubus galloecicus, commonly known as the European blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These fruits are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in woodland edges and disturbed soils across Europe.
