Rosa caesia var. incana

Rosa caesia var. incana, commonly known as the downy rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and foliage, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique texture and seasonal bloom.
