Rubus obtrullatus

Rubus obtrullatus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
