Rubus niveus var. hypargyrus

Rubus niveus var. hypargyrus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It is native to the western United States and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in gardens for both its aesthetic appeal and edible fruit.
