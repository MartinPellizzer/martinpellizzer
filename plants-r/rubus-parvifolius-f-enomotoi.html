Rubus parvifolius f. enomotoi

Rubus parvifolius f. enomotoi, commonly known as the Enomoto's bramble, is a shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

This form is a natural variant of Rubus parvifolius, distinguished by its unique foliage and growth habits.
