Rhytiglossa ruiziana

Rhytiglossa ruiziana, commonly known as the Ruiziana orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Rhytiglossa ruiziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
