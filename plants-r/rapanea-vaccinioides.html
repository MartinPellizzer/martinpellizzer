Rapanea vaccinioides

Rapanea vaccinioides, commonly known as the dwarf wax myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
