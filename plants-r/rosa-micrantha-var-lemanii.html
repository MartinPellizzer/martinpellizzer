Rosa micrantha var. lemanii

Rosa micrantha var. lemanii, commonly known as the Leman's shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features upright, thorny stems and is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces oval, stipulate leaves with a matte green surface and a serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in botanical studies.
