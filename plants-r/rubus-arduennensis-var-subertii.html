Rubus arduennensis var. subertii

Rubus arduennensis var. subertii, commonly known as the Arduenna raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though some varieties may have different colors. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
