Rumex crispus var. littoralis

Rumex crispus var. littoralis, commonly known as seaside sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions.

It is characterized by its crinkled, serrated leaves that are typically reddish or purple in color. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, reddish-brown seeds. It thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found along shorelines and dunes.

This variant is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
