Rosa ehrhartiana

Rosa ehrhartiana, commonly known as the Ehrhart rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it has a strong, pleasant fragrance.

Rosa ehrhartiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
