Rhododendron catanduanense

Rhododendron catanduanense, commonly known as the Catanduanes rhododendron, is a species native to the Philippines, specifically the island of Catanduanes.

It is a flowering shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in montane forests at elevations between 1,000 and 2,000 meters. Its flowers are typically 5 to 7 centimeters in diameter, with five petals and a distinctive, elongated floral tube.

Rhododendron catanduanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the local biodiversity of its native habitat.
