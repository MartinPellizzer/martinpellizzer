Rhododendron bhotanicum

Rhododendron bhotanicum, commonly known as the Bhutanese rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in Bhutan, India, and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is well adapted to the cool, humid conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Rhododendron bhotanicum plays an important ecological role in its native ecosystems, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
