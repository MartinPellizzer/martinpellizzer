Rosa × kochiana

Rosa × kochiana, commonly known as the Koch rose, is a hybrid species of rose that originated in the early 19th century.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double to double flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

Rosa × kochiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
