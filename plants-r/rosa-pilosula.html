Rosa pilosula

Rosa pilosula, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are pinnately divided and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its common name. Rosa pilosula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
