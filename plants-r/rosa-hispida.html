Rosa hispida

Rosa hispida, commonly known as the hispid rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

Rosa hispida is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
