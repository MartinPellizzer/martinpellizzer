Rubus stictocalyx

Rubus stictocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many other species of shrubs commonly referred to as blackberries or raspberries.
