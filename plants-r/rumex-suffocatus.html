Rumex suffocatus

Rumex suffocatus, commonly known as yellow dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.

Yellow dock is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its astringent and anti-inflammatory effects.
