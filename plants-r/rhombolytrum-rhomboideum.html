Rhombolytrum rhomboideum

Rhombolytrum rhomboideum, commonly known as the diamondleaf shrub, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive rhomboid-shaped leaves, which are typically arranged in a dense, compact form. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Rhombolytrum rhomboideum is often used in traditional medicine and landscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental features.
