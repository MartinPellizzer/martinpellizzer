Ruellia thomana

Ruellia thomana, commonly known as the Mexican bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Ruellia thomana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
