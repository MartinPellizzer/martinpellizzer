Ruellia magnifica

Ruellia magnifica, commonly known as the grand ruellia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple tubular flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. Ruellia magnifica is typically grown as a bedding plant or in containers due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.

It prefers well-drained soil and regular watering, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, colorful foliage and blooms.
