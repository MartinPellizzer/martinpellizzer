Rubus ulmifolius var. microphyllus

Rubus ulmifolius var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry species.

It is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The leaves are typically smaller and more rounded compared to the standard variety, with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

This plant is valued for its edible fruit and its role in supporting local wildlife.
