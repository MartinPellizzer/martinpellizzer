Rubus candicans var. fragrans

Rubus candicans var. fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant raspberry, is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, red, edible fruits. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pinkish flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

The variety 'fragrans' is distinguished by its more intense fragrance compared to other Rubus species.
