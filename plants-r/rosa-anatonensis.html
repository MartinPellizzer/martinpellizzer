Rosa anatonensis

Rosa anatonensis, commonly known as the Anatolian rose, is a species native to Turkey and surrounding regions.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges and an upright growth habit.

Rosa anatonensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
