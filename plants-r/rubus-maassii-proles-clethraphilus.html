Rubus maassii proles clethraphilus

Rubus maassii proles clethraphilus, commonly known as the Maasii blackberry, is a member of the Rosaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that produces small, dark purple to black berries, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are not only ornamental but also have potential culinary and medicinal uses.
