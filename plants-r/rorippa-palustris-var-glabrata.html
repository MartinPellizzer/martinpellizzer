Rorippa palustris var. glabrata

Rorippa palustris var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth watercress, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to temperate regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often floating or submerged, with small, alternate leaves that are usually smooth and oval in shape. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters, producing numerous tiny seeds.

This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters and its role in supporting aquatic ecosystems.
