Rubus plicatus f. suboblongifolius

Rubus plicatus f. suboblongifolius, commonly known as the suboblong-leaved blackberry, is a botanical variant of the blackberry plant.

It is characterized by its suboblong, serrated leaves that are slightly smaller than those of the typical Rubus plicatus. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild flavor, making it suitable for both culinary and landscaping purposes.
