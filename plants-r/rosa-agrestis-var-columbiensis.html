Rosa agrestis var. columbiensis

Rosa agrestis var. columbiensis, commonly known as the Columbia wild rose, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals and a fragrant aroma.

The hips, or fruit, are orange-red and contain seeds that are dispersed by animals.
