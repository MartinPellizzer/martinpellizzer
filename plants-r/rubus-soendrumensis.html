Rubus soendrumensis

Rubus soendrumensis, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Nepal, India, and Bhutan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental and potential medicinal value, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
