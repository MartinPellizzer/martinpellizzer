Rumex glaucus

Rumex glaucus, commonly known as the bluish dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along riverbanks. The plant features a stout, upright stem with large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish coating. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces elliptical, dark brown seeds.

Rumex glaucus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
