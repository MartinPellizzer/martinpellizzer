Rosa flexibilis

Rosa flexibilis, commonly known as the climbing rose, is a species native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb using its twining stems. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in a range of colors, including pink, white, and red. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges.

Rosa flexibilis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
