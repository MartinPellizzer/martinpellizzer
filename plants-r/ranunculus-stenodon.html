Ranunculus stenodon

Ranunculus stenodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow or white flowers with numerous stamens. Native to Europe and western Asia, it thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a short, stout rhizome and produces small, hairy fruits that disperse its seeds.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of its native habitats.
