Rosa × damascena f. trigintipetala

Rosa × damascena f. trigintipetala, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a fragrant, deciduous shrub native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus, characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance and are often used in perfumery and culinary applications.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine and horticulture.
