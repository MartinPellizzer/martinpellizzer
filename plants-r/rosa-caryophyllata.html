Rosa caryophyllata

Rosa caryophyllata, commonly known as the Damascus rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves. Its flowers are prized for their aromatic qualities and are used in perfumery and culinary applications.

Rosa caryophyllata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and hardiness in various climates.
