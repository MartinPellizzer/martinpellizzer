Rumex occidentalis var. procerus

Rumex occidentalis var. procerus, commonly known as the tall dock, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright, ribbed stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with reddish hues. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in terminal panicles, producing a distinctive reddish-brown color.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
