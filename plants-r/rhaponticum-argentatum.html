Rhaponticum argentatum

Rhaponticum argentatum, commonly known as silver ragwort, is a flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and it typically grows in meadows and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

While it is ornamental in gardens, it can also be invasive in some regions.
