Rubus procerus f. proceriformis

Rubus procerus f. proceriformis, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible, though they are often less flavorful than those of other Rubus species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
