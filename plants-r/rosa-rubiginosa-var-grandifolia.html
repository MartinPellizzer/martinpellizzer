Rosa rubiginosa var. grandifolia

Rosa rubiginosa var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered blight rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are rose hips, which are orange-red, hairy, and contain seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
