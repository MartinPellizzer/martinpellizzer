Rubus wahlbergii f. tenuifolius

Rubus wahlbergii f. tenuifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
