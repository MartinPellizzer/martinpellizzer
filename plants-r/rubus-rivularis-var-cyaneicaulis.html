Rubus rivularis var. cyaneicaulis

Rubus rivularis var. cyaneicaulis, commonly known as the blue-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often blue-tinged stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its hairy stems and flowers with five pink to white petals.
