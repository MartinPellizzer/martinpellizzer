Rohdea eucomoides

Rohdea eucomoides, commonly known as the Chinese lily or rohdea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and sword-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Rohdea eucomoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to add texture and color to woodland landscapes.
