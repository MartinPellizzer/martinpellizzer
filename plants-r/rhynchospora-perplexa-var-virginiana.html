Rhynchospora perplexa var. virginiana

Rhynchospora perplexa var. virginiana, commonly known as the Virginia marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and colonize new areas.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
