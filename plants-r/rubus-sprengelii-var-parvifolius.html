Rubus sprengelii var. parvifolius

Rubus sprengelii var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
