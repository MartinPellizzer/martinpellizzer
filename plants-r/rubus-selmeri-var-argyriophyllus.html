Rubus selmeri var. argyriophyllus

Rubus selmeri var. argyriophyllus, commonly known as the silver-leafed bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus native to parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
