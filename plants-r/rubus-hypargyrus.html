Rubus hypargyrus

Rubus hypargyrus, commonly known as the golden raspberries or yellow raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, yellowish-orange fruits that are edible. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pink flowers in the spring. Its fruits are often used in jams and desserts due to their tart flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
