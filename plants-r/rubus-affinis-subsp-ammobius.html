Rubus affinis subsp. ammobius

Rubus affinis subsp.

ammobius, commonly known as the Ammobius blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
