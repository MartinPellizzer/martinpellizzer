Rosa sirei

Rosa sirei, commonly known as the Sirene rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and profuse blooming, with flowers that are typically pink to red in color. The plant features thorny stems and large, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in horticultural breeding programs.

Rosa sirei is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
