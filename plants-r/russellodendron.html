Russellodendron

Russellodendron, commonly known as the Russell's hibiscus, is a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It consists of several species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plants typically have large, showy flowers with five petals, often in bright colors like red, yellow, or orange. They are characterized by their woody stems and palmate leaves, which are usually lobed or deeply divided.

Russellodendron species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
