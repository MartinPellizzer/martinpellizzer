Rubus sanctus f. cuneatus

Rubus sanctus f. cuneatus, commonly known as the cuneate blackberry, is a cultivated form of the blackberry plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wedge-shaped leaves and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant is typically grown for its edible fruits, which have a tart flavor and are often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.

The cuneate blackberry is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various growing conditions.
