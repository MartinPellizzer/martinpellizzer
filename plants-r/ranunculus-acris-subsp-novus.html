Ranunculus acris subsp. novus

Ranunculus acris subsp.

novus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and hairy, deeply lobed leaves.

This subspecies thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky soils at high elevations. Its growth is typically low to the ground, with stems that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is known for its ability to withstand cold temperatures and is often found in meadows and open slopes.
