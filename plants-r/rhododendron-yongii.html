Rhododendron yongii

Rhododendron yongii, commonly known as the Yungui rhododendron, is a species native to China, particularly in the Yunnan province.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions with high humidity and cool temperatures. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red during the spring. Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited to acidic, well-drained soils.

Rhododendron yongii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
