Racinaea multiflora var. decipiens

Racinaea multiflora var. decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive racinaea, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, often with a velvety texture and deep green coloration. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its unique appearance and ability to mimic other plants have earned it the common name "deceptive racinaea.".

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
