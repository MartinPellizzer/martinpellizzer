Rubus schleicheri f. australis

Rubus schleicheri f. australis, commonly known as the Australian blackberry, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions and open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
