Rajania venosa

Rajania venosa, commonly known as the veiny orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, veined leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rainforests. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow with intricate patterns, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Rajania venosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
