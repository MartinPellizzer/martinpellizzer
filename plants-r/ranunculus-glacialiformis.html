Ranunculus glacialiformis

Ranunculus glacialiformis, commonly known as the glacial buttercup, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves and bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in cold, high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils. Its growth is adapted to harsh climatic conditions, with a short growing season and dormancy during winter.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
