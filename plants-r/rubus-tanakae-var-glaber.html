Rubus tanakae var. glaber

Rubus tanakae var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth-fruited bramble, is a shrub native to East Asia, particularly Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in woodland and shrubland habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
