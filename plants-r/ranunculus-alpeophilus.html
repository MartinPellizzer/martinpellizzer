Ranunculus alpeophilus

Ranunculus alpeophilus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, hairy fruits that contain several seeds.

Ranunculus alpeophilus is an important species in alpine ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and serving as a food source for various insects.
