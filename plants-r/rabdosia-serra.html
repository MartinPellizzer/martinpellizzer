Rabdosia serra

Rabdosia serra, commonly known as the serrated rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, serrated leaves and spikes of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rabdosia serra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
