Racosperma cretatum

Racosperma cretatum, commonly known as the Cretaceous acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh climatic conditions. Its historical significance is reflected in its name, which refers to its presence in Cretaceous-era fossil records.

The species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
