Rosa × involuta var. robertsonii

Rosa × involuta var. robertsonii, commonly known as the Robertson's climbing rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its vigorous, climbing growth habit.

It features fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers that bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It is native to the United States, specifically Texas, and is well adapted to warm, sunny climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in garden landscapes and as a climbing plant.
