Rhodiola quadrifida

Rhodiola quadrifida, commonly known as the four-leaf rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and clusters of yellow or orange flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to store water and nutrients efficiently, adapting to harsh alpine conditions.

Rhodiola quadrifida is also valued for its potential adaptogenic properties, though its botanical features are more notable for their structural resilience and ecological niche.
