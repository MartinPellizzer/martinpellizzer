Rhododendron cremastum

Rhododendron cremastum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often in the understory of forests. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Rhododendron cremastum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
