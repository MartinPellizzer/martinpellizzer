Rubus arcticus grandiflorus

Rubus arcticus grandiflorus, commonly known as the Arctic bramble, is a shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruits are red, juicy berries that are edible and often used in local foraging.

It thrives in harsh, cold environments and plays an important role in Arctic ecosystems.
