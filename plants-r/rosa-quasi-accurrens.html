Rosa quasi-accurrens

Rosa quasi-accurrens, commonly known as the near-occurring rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Rosa quasi-accurrens is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological studies.
