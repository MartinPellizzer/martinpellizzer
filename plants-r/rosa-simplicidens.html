Rosa simplicidens

Rosa simplicidens, commonly known as the simpleleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its simple, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. It typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine soils.

Rosa simplicidens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
