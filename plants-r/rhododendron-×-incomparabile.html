Rhododendron × incomparabile

Rhododendron × incomparabile, commonly known as the Himalayan beauty, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its botanical features include large, leathery leaves and a compact growth habit, making it well adapted to cool, moist climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
