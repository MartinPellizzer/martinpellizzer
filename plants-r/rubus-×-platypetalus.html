Rubus × platypetalus

Rubus × platypetalus, commonly known as the flatpetal raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals that are broader than they are long.

The fruit, a red hairy berry, is edible and often used in jams and desserts.
