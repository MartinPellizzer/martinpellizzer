Rosa × dumetorum var. gracilenta

Rosa × dumetorum var. gracilenta, commonly known as the slender shrub rose, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compact, thorny stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Rosa × dumetorum var. gracilenta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
