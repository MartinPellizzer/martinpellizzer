Ranunculus melanogynus

Ranunculus melanogynus, commonly known as the black-eyed ranunculus, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers with dark centers and bright yellow petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in spring.

Ranunculus melanogynus is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
