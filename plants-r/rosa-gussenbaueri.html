Rosa gussenbaueri

Rosa gussenbaueri, commonly known as the Gussenhauer rose, is a species native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, single or semi-double pink to red flowers. The plant produces elliptical, glossy leaves with serrated edges, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a sought-after plant among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
