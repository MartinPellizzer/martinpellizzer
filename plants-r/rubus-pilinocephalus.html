Rubus pilinocephalus

Rubus pilinocephalus, commonly known as the hairy-headed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences, and it thrives in a variety of soil types.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit.
