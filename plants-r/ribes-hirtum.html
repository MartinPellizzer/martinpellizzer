Ribes hirtum

Ribes hirtum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed currant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as currants, which are typically dark red or black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Ribes hirtum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
