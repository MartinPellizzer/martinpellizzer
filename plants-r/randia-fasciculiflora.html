Randia fasciculiflora

Randia fasciculiflora, commonly known as the clustered randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. Its flowers are small, white to pink, and arranged in clusters, giving it its common name.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.
