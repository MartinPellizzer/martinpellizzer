Rosa viridiglauca

Rosa viridiglauca, commonly known as the greenish-blue rose, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pale greenish-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are dark green, serrated, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa viridiglauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration.
