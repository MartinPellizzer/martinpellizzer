Ranunculus brassii

Ranunculus brassii, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain multiple seeds.

Ranunculus brassii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
