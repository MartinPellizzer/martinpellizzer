Rhaphiolepis oblongifolia

Rhaphiolepis oblongifolia, commonly known as the Japanese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible but often used as ornamental features. Its leaves are elliptical and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
