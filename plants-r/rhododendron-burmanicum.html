Rhododendron burmanicum

Rhododendron burmanicum, commonly known as the Burman rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in mountainous and forested areas. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires cool, moist conditions.

Rhododendron burmanicum is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
