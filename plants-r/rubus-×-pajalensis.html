Rubus × pajalensis

Rubus × pajalensis, commonly known as the Andean blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Ecuador. The plant is a shrub that produces small, dark red to black berries, which are highly valued for their flavor and nutritional content. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and prickly stems typical of many Rubus species.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both commercial and culinary purposes.
