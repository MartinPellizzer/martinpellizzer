Rhamnella julianae

Rhamnella julianae, commonly known as Julian's buckthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rhamnaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Caribbean. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, smooth, and turn reddish-purple when mature.

Rhamnella julianae is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and hammocks.
