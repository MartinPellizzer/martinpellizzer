Ratibida columnaris f. denudata

Ratibida columnaris f. denudata, commonly known as the nakedhead prickly goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy yellow blooms. The plant features upright, branching stems with rough, hairy surfaces and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers consist of ray florets that are typically yellow and surrounded by a dense cluster of disk florets.

This variant is distinguished by its reduced leaf coverage and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.
