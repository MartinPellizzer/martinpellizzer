Racosperma verticillatum var. latifolium

Racosperma verticillatum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed racosperma, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in whorls of three to five along the stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of native flora.
