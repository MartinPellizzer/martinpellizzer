Rubus obscurus subsp. gravetii

Rubus obscurus subsp.

gravetii, commonly known as the western evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, leathery leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family and is distinguished by its unique subspecies traits.
