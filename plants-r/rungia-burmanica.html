Rungia burmanica

Rungia burmanica, commonly known as Burmese runnia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and gardenias. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a smooth texture, while the fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that splits open to release seeds.

Rungia burmanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
