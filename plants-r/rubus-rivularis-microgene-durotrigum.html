Rubus rivularis microgene durotrigum

Rubus rivularis microgene durotrigum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, dark purple to black fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually elliptical with finely serrated edges and a hairy underside.

The flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the characteristic aggregate fruits of the Rubus genus.
