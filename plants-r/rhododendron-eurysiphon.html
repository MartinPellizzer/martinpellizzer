Rhododendron eurysiphon

Rhododendron eurysiphon, commonly known as the narrow-siphon rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant prefers acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

Rhododendron eurysiphon is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
