Ranunculus fallax subsp. prionodes

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

prionodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features basal leaves that are typically narrow and lobed, with a distinctive yellow flower that has five petals and numerous stamens.

The plant grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The species is also notable for its historical use in traditional medicine.
