Rubus × semicalignosus

Rubus × semicalignosus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe, and are prized for their tart flavor. The plant is native to parts of North America and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. It thrives in temperate climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
