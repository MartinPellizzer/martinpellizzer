Rubus parviflorus var. heteradenius

Rubus parviflorus var. heteradenius, commonly known as the slender raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, aggregated flowers. The plant produces red, edible berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs.

This variety is distinguished by its smaller flowers and shorter, more slender sepals compared to other Rubus species.
