Rulac mexicana

Rulac mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican rulac, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are small and usually pale in color, and it produces edible seeds that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Rulac mexicana is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential medicinal properties.
