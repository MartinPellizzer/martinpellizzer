Rosa villosa var. uncinata

Rosa villosa var. uncinata, commonly known as the hooked bristly rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the eastern United States.

It features thorny stems covered in a dense layer of grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals, and appear in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This plant thrives in woodland areas and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
