Reynoutria × vivax

Reynoutria × vivax, commonly known as the giant Japanese flatwebbed spider plant, is a hybrid species within the genus Reynoutria, which includes various species of flowering plants.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a smooth, dark green surface and a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer, followed by the development of distinctive, flat, spiky seed heads. It is native to East Asia but has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.

Its botanical features make it both an ornamental plant and a potential ecological concern in certain environments.
