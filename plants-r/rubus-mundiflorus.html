Rubus mundiflorus

Rubus mundiflorus, commonly known as the celestial bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, each covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by edible, red berries in the summer. Its roots are fibrous and can spread widely, making it a hardy perennial.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
