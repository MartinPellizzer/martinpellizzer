Rubus tereticaulis var. anamphiestus

Rubus tereticaulis var. anamphiestus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a perennial shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white and clustered in corymbs, and the species is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles and blackberry species.
