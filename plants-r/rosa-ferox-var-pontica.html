Rosa ferox var. pontica

Rosa ferox var. pontica, commonly known as the Crimean rose, is a vigorous shrub native to the Black Sea region.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, dark red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found growing in rocky or coastal areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its beauty and historical significance.

The species is also notable for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
