Rhododendron kaempferi var. kaempferi

Rhododendron kaempferi var. kaempferi, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
