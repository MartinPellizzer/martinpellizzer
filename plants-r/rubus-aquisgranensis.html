Rubus aquisgranensis

Rubus aquisgranensis, commonly known as the Austrian raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
