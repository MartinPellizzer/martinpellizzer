Rubus subcollinus

Rubus subcollinus, commonly known as the western blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny areas.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many other species of brambles and blackberries.
