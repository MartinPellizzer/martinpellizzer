Rosa tomentosa f. horrida

Rosa tomentosa f. horrida, commonly known as the hairy rose or bristly rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa tomentosa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly pubescence covering the stems, leaves, and flowers, which gives it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals and a fragrant scent.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and visual appeal.
