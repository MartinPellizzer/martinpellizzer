Ribes pachysandroides

Ribes pachysandroides, commonly known as the thick-leaved gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by orange-red berries in late summer. These berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and sauces.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy plant suitable for a variety of garden conditions.
