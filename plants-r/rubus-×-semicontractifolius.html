Rubus × semicontractifolius

Rubus × semicontractifolius, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. It is native to the Pacific coast of North America, where it thrives in coastal scrub and woodland habitats. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications.

Its foliage is evergreen, with leathery leaves that have a distinctive, serrated edge.
