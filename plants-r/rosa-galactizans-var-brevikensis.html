Rosa galactizans var. brevikensis

Rosa galactizans var. brevikensis, commonly known as the Brevik's galactia rose, is a rare and specialized variant of the galactia rose species.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring pale yellow to white petals with a subtle iridescence. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit, often forming dense shrubs with thornless stems. Its leaves are trifoliate, with finely serrated margins, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring.

This variety is adapted to specific ecological niches, often found in rocky or alpine environments where it thrives in well-drained soils.
