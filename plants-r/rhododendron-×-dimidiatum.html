Rhododendron × dimidiatum

Rhododendron × dimidiatum, commonly known as the hybrid rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and densely arranged along the stems. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in horticulture.
