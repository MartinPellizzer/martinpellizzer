Rhamnus confinis

Rhamnus confinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf buckthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rhamnaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically black when mature. It is often found in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
