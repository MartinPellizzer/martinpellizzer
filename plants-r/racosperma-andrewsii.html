Racosperma andrewsii

Racosperma andrewsii, commonly known as the pink-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pink or red flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for native pollinators.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in suitable climates.
