Rubus × piletocarpus var. longifolius

Rubus × piletocarpus var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaf blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible berries, though it can be somewhat invasive in certain regions.
