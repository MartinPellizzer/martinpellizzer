Rubus fruticosus var. corylifolius

Rubus fruticosus var. corylifolius, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that resemble a shield, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
