Remijia bracteata

Remijia bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or bracted remijia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Remijia bracteata is typically found in wetland or swampy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
