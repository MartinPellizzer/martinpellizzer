Rubus parviflorus f. parvillosus

Rubus parviflorus f. parvillosus, commonly known as the small-flowered blackberry.

This plant is a variant of the small-flowered raspberry, characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more delicate flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color. The leaves are usually trifoliate, with three leaflets that are hairy and serrated.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
