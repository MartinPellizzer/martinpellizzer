Rosa rubiginosa var. grandiflora

Rosa rubiginosa var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered shrubby rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces oval, glossy leaves with serrated edges, and its hips, which are red berries, mature in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
