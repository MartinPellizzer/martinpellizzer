Ribes emodense var. verruculosum

Ribes emodense var. verruculosum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed gooseberry, is a variant of the gooseberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
