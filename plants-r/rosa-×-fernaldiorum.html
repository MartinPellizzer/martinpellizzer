Rosa × fernaldiorum

Rosa × fernaldiorum, commonly known as the Fernald rose, is a hybrid rose species resulting from the cross between Rosa bracteata and Rosa setigera.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and the presence of both simple and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that are typically borne on long pedicels. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, woodland habitats.

The Fernald rose is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
