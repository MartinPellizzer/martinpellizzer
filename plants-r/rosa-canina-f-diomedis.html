Rosa canina f. diomedis

Rosa canina f. diomedis, commonly known as the Diomedes rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and pinnate leaves. Its fruits, known as hips, are orange-red and contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
