Rubus tereticaulis var. jucundifolius

Rubus tereticaulis var. jucundifolius, commonly known as the sweetleaf raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged in an alternate fashion along the stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are reddish in color and have a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
