Rhododendron sonomense

Rhododendron sonomense, commonly known as Sonoma rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the coastal regions of Northern California.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in late spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

Rhododendron sonomense is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
