Rosa tomentosa var. burgundiae

Rosa tomentosa var. burgundiae, commonly known as Burgundy rose, is a compact, shrub-like species native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white fuzzy foliage and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant is noted for its cold hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and winter interest due to its persistent, red-hued stems.

This variety is also valued for its resistance to common rose diseases.
