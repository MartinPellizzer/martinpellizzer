Rubus rivularis var. canifolius

Rubus rivularis var. canifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It features slender, thorny stems and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. The leaves are narrow and serrated, with a distinctive, often hairy texture. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are white or pale green, and it is often found growing in clusters along riverbanks.
