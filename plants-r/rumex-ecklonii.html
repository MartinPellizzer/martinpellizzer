Rumex ecklonii

Rumex ecklonii, commonly known as the African dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and a waxy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces elliptical, reddish-brown seeds.

Rumex ecklonii is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
