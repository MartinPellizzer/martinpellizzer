Rhododendron baihuaense

Rhododendron baihuaense, commonly known as the Baihua rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, range in color from white to pink, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.

Rhododendron baihuaense is often found in cloud forests and is valued for both its ecological role and horticultural appeal.
