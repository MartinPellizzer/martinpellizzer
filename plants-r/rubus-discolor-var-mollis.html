Rubus discolor var. mollis

Rubus discolor var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-furred raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, soft stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they may vary in color depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

This variant is valued for its culinary uses and is also an important species in ecological studies due to its role in supporting local wildlife.
