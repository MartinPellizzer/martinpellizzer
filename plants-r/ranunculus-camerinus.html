Ranunculus camerinus

Ranunculus camerinus, commonly known as the Camerine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils. The plant features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with bright yellow petals and numerous stamens. Its leaves are palmately divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Ranunculus camerinus is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and early spring flowering habit.
