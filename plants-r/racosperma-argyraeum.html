Racosperma argyraeum

Racosperma argyraeum, commonly known as the silver-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact inflorescences, which are typical of the acacia family. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Racosperma argyraeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat and food for native wildlife.
