Rhododendron mainlingense

Rhododendron mainlingense, commonly known as the Mainling rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of Tibet and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers range in color from pink to red, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
