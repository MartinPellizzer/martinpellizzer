Rosa coriifolia var. sepium

Rosa coriifolia var. sepium, commonly known as the Japanese rose or the snow rose, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces rose-like flowers that are often fragrant. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
