Rosa elliptica var. wildensbuchiae

Rosa elliptica var. wildensbuchiae, commonly known as the Wildensbuchia rose, is a rare and ornamental species native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, slightly hairy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its unique foliage and delicate floral display, making it a sought-after addition to botanical gardens.
