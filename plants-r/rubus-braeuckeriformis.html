Rubus braeuckeriformis

Rubus braeuckeriformis, commonly known as the Braeucker's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces edible, though tart, red berries.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
