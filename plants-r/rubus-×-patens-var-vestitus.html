Rubus × patens var. vestitus

Rubus × patens var. vestitus, commonly known as the velvet blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly pubescence that gives it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant produces aggregate fruits, typically dark purple to black in color, which are edible and have a tart flavor. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates, often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in ecological restoration projects.
