Rubus hedycarpus subsp. emollitus

Rubus hedycarpus subsp.

emollitus, commonly known as the soft-fruited blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences, and the plant is often found in woodland or hedgerow habitats.

It is valued for its edible fruits and is sometimes cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
