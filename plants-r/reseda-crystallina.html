Reseda crystallina

Reseda crystallina, commonly known as crystalline reseda, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, white, waxy hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

The leaves are linear and often have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
