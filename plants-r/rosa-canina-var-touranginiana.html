Rosa canina var. touranginiana

Rosa canina var. touranginiana, commonly known as the Touranginian dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a fragrant scent. It produces bright red hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine.

This variety is native to southern Europe and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
