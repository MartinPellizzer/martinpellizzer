Rubus latiarcuatus

Rubus latiarcuatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed blackberry.

It is a species of bramble in the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, a dark purple-black berry, is edible and often used in jams and desserts.
