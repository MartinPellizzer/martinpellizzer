Rubus neopyramidalis var. valderamosus

Rubus neopyramidalis var. valderamosus, commonly known as the Valderamos raspberry, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
