Rhododendron atlanticum var. luteoalbum

Rhododendron atlanticum var. luteoalbum, commonly known as the Yellow-white Atlantic Rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pale yellow, blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
