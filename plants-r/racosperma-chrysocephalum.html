Racosperma chrysocephalum

Racosperma chrysocephalum, commonly known as the golden-headed acacia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, feathery leaflets, and it has a woody, spreading habit.

Racosperma chrysocephalum is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
