Rubus chaerophyllus subsp. beskidarum

Rubus chaerophyllus subsp.

beskidarum, commonly known as the Beskid raspberry, is a shrub native to the Carpathian region. It is characterized by its biennial or perennial growth habit and produces small, red, edible berries.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
