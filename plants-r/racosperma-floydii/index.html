Racosperma Floydii

Floyd's acacia, scientifically known as Racosperma floydii, is a rare and unique plant native to the arid regions of Western Australia. This small shrub is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. Despite its hardy nature, it is classified as endangered due to habitat loss and limited distribution. The plant holds cultural significance for Indigenous communities and is a subject of ongoing conservation efforts. Its survival is crucial for maintaining the ecological balance of its native ecosystem.
