Rumex × uludaghensis

Rumex × uludaghensis, commonly known as the Uludag dock, is a naturally occurring hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rumex acetosa and Rumex patientia.

It is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Turkey, particularly around the Uludag mountain. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and lobed, with a distinctive reddish coloration on the underside. It produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months.

The species is notable for its ecological significance in alpine ecosystems and its potential use in traditional medicine.
