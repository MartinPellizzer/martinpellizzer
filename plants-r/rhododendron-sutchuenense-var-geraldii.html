Rhododendron sutchuenense var. geraldii

Rhododendron sutchuenense var. geraldii, commonly known as the Geraldii Rhododendron, is a rare and ornamental evergreen shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued for its compact growth habit and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.

Due to its limited distribution and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by specialty growers.
