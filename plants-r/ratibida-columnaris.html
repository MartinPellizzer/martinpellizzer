Ratibida columnaris

Ratibida columnaris, commonly known as the tall yellow coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features a tall, upright stem with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces a distinctive yellow-orange flower head with a prominent, spiky central disk. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ratibida columnaris is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.
