Rubus gillotii var. nemophilus

Rubus gillotii var. nemophilus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its small, thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas.

Its leaves are palmately compound, with three to five leaflets, and are covered in fine, silvery hairs.
