Rosa rubrifolia var. hispidula

Rosa rubrifolia var. hispidula, commonly known as the hispid rose, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, contributing to its common name. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
