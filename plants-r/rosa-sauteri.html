Rosa sauteri

Rosa sauteri, commonly known as the Sauter rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Andes Mountains in South America, specifically in regions of Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and red. Its foliage consists of serrated, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa sauteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
