Rosa myriacantha var. pyriformis

Rosa myriacantha var. pyriformis, commonly known as the pear-shaped rose, is a species of shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically dark green, glossy, and arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.
