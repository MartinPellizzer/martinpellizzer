Rollinia fagifolia

Rollinia fagifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and mango. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. Its fruit is a round, yellow or orange berry that turns red when ripe, often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
