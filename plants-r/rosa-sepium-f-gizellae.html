Rosa sepium f. gizellae

Rosa sepium f. gizellae, commonly known as the Gizella rose, is a rare and unique cultivar of the Rosa sepium species.

It is characterized by its striking, deep pink to crimson flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a compact, bushy growth habit with dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop for its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a desirable addition to gardens with specific horticultural conditions.

Due to its rarity and distinct appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in specialized botanical collections.
