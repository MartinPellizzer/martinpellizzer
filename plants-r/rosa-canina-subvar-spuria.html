Rosa canina subvar. spuria

Rosa canina subvar. spuria, commonly known as the dog rose or wild rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, pinnate leaves, and fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing numerous seeds. These hips are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
