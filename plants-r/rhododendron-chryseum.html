Rhododendron chryseum

Rhododendron chryseum, commonly known as the golden rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically elliptical and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blossoms.

Rhododendron chryseum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized landscapes.
