Ranunculus dissectissimus

Ranunculus dissectissimus, commonly known as the dissected buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in alpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
