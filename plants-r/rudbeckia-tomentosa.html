Rudbeckia tomentosa

Rudbeckia tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly coneflower, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and bright yellow ray flowers with a prominent central disk. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Rudbeckia tomentosa is often found in prairies, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and wildlife habitats.
