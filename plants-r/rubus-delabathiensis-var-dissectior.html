Rubus delabathiensis var. dissectior

Rubus delabathiensis var. dissectior, commonly known as the dissected blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, pinnatifid leaves that give it a distinctive, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
