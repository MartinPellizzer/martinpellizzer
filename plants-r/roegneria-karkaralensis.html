Roegneria karkaralensis

Roegneria karkaralensis, commonly known as the Karakaral grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale green to brownish flowers, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Roegneria karkaralensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
