Ranunculus auricomus subsp. pusillidens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

pusillidens, commonly known as the small-flowered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often surrounded by a series of sepals that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough texture and serrated edges.

It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
