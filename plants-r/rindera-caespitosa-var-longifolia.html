Rindera caespitosa var. longifolia

Rindera caespitosa var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved rindera, is a perennial grass-like plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, elongated leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

Botanically, it is notable for its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
