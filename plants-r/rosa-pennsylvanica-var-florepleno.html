Rosa pennsylvanica var. florepleno

Rosa pennsylvanica var. florepleno, commonly known as the double-flowered wild rose, is a cultivar of the Pennsylvania wild rose.

It is characterized by its dense, double blooms that are typically pink to red in color. The plant is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant, five-petaled flowers in the spring. It has thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
