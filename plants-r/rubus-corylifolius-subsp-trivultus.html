Rubus corylifolius subsp. trivultus

Rubus corylifolius subsp.

trivultus, commonly known as the three-lobed blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are deeply lobed and have a distinctive three-leaf appearance.

The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
