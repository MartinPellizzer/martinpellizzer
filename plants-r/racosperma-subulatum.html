Racosperma subulatum

Racosperma subulatum, commonly known as the narrow leaf wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal regions.

The narrow leaf wattle is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
