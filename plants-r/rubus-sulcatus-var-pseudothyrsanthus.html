Rubus sulcatus var. pseudothyrsanthus

Rubus sulcatus var. pseudothyrsanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
