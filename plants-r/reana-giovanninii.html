Reana giovanninii

Reana giovanninii, commonly known as the Giant Reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features long, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, often with a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters, typically in late summer. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found near water bodies.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, it can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
