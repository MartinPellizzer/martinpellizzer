Rubus rosifolius var. rubrocarpus

Rubus rosifolius var. rubrocarpus, commonly known as the red-fruited bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, which are edible and often used in jams and pies. It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
