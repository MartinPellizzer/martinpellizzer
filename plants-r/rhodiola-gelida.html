Rhodiola gelida

Rhodiola gelida, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant is adapted to cold climates and can survive in harsh, rocky environments with poor soil. It is often found at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun and has a short growing season.

Rhodiola gelida is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported adaptogenic and antidepressant effects.
