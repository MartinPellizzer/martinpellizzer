Rudbeckia laciniata subsp. ampla

Rudbeckia laciniata subsp.

ampla, commonly known as the broadleaf coneflower, is a perennial plant native to North America. It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flower heads surrounded by broad, serrated green leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often lobed and can reach significant sizes, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
