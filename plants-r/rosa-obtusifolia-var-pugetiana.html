Rosa obtusifolia var. pugetiana

Rosa obtusifolia var. pugetiana, commonly known as Puget Island rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, small fragrant flowers, and glossy evergreen leaves that remain throughout the winter. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodlands and stream banks. It produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C and serve as a food source for wildlife.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
