Ranunculus affinis var. tanguticus

Ranunculus affinis var. tanguticus, commonly known as the Tibetan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branched.

This variant is notable for its adaptation to harsh mountainous climates and is often found in regions such as Tibet, Xinjiang, and parts of Russia.
