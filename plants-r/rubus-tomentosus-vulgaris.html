Rubus tomentosus vulgaris

Rubus tomentosus vulgaris, commonly known as the common bramble, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruits, known as blackberries, are aggregate fruits formed from multiple tiny drupes.

Rubus tomentosus vulgaris is widely distributed in temperate regions and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
