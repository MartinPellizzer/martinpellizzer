Rosa canina var. glabrescens

Rosa canina var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth dog rose, is a variant of the dog rose species.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and pinnate leaves. The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a fragrant aroma.

The plant produces red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It is native to Europe and western Asia and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
