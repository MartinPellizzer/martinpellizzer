Rosa canina subvar. rousselii

Rosa canina subvar. rousselii, commonly known as the Roussel's dog rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose species.

It is characterized by its thorned stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The leaves are pinnate, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets. This subspecies is native to southern Europe and is often found in woodlands and scrub habitats.

It produces red, hairy fruits known as hip fruits, which are used in traditional herbal remedies.
