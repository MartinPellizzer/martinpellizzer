Ribes × gondouinii

Ribes × gondouinii, commonly known as the hybrid currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes and is a natural hybrid between Ribes sanguineum (red currant) and Ribes aureum (golden currant). The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright stems with thorny branches. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have serrated margins, while the flowers are clustered in terminal racemes.

The fruit is a small, edible berry that ripens to a dark red or black color, often used in jams and preserves.
