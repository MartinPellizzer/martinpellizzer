Rosa pomifera var. balneana

Rosa pomifera var. balneana, commonly known as the Himalayan rose or mountain rose, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, finely toothed leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle fragrance. Its fruit, known as a rose hip, is distinctive for its hairy, red outer covering and contains seeds inside.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its beauty and traditional uses.
