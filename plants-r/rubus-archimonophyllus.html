Rubus archimonophyllus

Rubus archimonophyllus, commonly known as the giant blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. This plant is characterized by its large, thorny stems and dark purple to black berries. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
