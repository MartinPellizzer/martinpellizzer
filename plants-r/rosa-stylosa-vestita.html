Rosa stylosa vestita

Rosa stylosa vestita, commonly known as the velvet-stemmed rose, is a species native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in woodland or shrubland habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the flowers are often clustered in groups of three.

Rosa stylosa vestita is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
