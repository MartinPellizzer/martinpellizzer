Rubus macrostachys proles indusiatus

Rubus macrostachys proles indusiatus, commonly known as the woolly raspberry, is a species of shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and thorny stems, which provide structural support and protection. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
