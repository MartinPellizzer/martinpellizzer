Rubus fruticosus subsp. macrophyllus

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with large, serrated leaflets.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its edible berries and is also valued in ecological restoration projects.
