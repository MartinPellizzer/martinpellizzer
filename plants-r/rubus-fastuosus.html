Rubus fastuosus

Rubus fastuosus, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, dark purple berries in the summer. Its growth habit includes a sprawling or upright form, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit production in suitable climates.
