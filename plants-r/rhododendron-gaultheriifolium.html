Rhododendron gaultheriifolium

Rhododendron gaultheriifolium, commonly known as the alpine rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, and the flowers range in color from white to pink and purple.

Rhododendron gaultheriifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
