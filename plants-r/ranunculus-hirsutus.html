Ranunculus hirsutus

Ranunculus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are typically lobed and serrated. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.

Ranunculus hirsutus is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often colorful blooms.
