Rosa iochanensis

Rosa iochanensis, commonly known as the Iochan rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Vietnam and Cambodia. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring.

Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces edible hips that are rich in vitamin C. Rosa iochanensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
