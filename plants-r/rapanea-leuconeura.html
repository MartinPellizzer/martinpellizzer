Rapanea leuconeura

Rapanea leuconeura, commonly known as the white-veined evergreen, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent white veins, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval-shaped berries.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
