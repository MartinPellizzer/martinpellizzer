Rhododendron longipedicellatum

Rhododendron longipedicellatum, commonly known as the longipedicellate rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its beauty and fragrance.
