Rubus cordatus

Rubus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, often found in woodland areas and along stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink or white, appearing in clusters. The fruit is a small, edible berry that is often used in jams and desserts.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
