Ranunculus macropodoides

Ranunculus macropodoides, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with 5 to 13 petal-like sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with lobes that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
