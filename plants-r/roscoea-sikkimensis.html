Roscoea sikkimensis

Roscoea sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim roscoea, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers, and is characterized by its ornamental foliage and striking floral display. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of red-orange flowers with prominent yellow stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

Roscoea sikkimensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
