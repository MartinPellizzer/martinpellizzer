Rhamnus pallasii subsp. sintenisii

Rhamnus pallasii subsp.

sintenisii, commonly known as the Siberian buckthorn, is a shrub native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Asia. It is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in the spring.

The plant is adapted to harsh climates and can thrive in well-drained soils. Its berries, which are typically dark purple, are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

This subspecies is often found in alpine and subarctic environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
