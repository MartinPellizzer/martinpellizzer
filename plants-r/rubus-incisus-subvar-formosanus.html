Rubus incisus subvar. formosanus

Rubus incisus subvar. formosanus, commonly known as the Formosan bramble, is a shrub native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
