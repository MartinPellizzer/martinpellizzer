Rubus × pseudosubvillosus

Rubus × pseudosubvillosus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries, which ripen in late summer, are edible and have a tart flavor.
