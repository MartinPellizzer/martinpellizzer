Renealmia violifolia

Renealmia violifolia, commonly known as the violetleaf renealmia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
