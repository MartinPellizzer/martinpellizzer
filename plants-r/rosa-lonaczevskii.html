Rosa lonaczevskii

Rosa lonaczevskii, commonly known as the Siberian rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink to red in color. It produces hips, which are the fruit of the plant, containing seeds surrounded by a fuzzy, red outer layer.

Rosa lonaczevskii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
