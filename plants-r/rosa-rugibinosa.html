Rosa rugibinosa

Rosa rugibinosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. Its hips, or fruit, are orange-red and contain numerous seeds.

Rosa rugibinosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental and historical significance.
