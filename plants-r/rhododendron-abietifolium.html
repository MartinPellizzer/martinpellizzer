Rhododendron abietifolium

Rhododendron abietifolium, commonly known as the white heath, is a deciduous shrub native to the Appalachian Mountains of eastern North America.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers in the spring. The plant prefers acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

Rhododendron abietifolium plays a role in supporting local wildlife and contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
