Rhaphiolepis indica var. typica

Rhaphiolepis indica var. typica, commonly known as the Chinese holly or evergreen boxwood, is a shrub native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, leathery foliage and small, showy flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces red berries that are attractive to birds, contributing to its ecological value. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and year-round foliage.

Rhaphiolepis indica var. typica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden borders and hedges.
