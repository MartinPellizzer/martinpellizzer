Ruschia cupulata

Ruschia cupulata, commonly known as the candle bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its small, fleshy, cylindrical leaves that resemble candles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Ruschia cupulata is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
