Rosa insidiosa

Rosa insidiosa, commonly known as the insidious rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by orange-red hips. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem with persistent buds and a root system adapted to rocky soils.

Rosa insidiosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated in various horticultural settings.
