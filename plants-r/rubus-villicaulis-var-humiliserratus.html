Rubus villicaulis var. humiliserratus

Rubus villicaulis var. humiliserratus, commonly known as the hairy-fruited blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture. The flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruit develops into a small, dark purple to black berry.

It is native to certain regions of North America and is often found in woodland or disturbed habitats.
