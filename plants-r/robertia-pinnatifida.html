Robertia pinnatifida

Robertia pinnatifida, commonly known as the slenderleaf rosette plant, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers appear in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its appearance.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
