Rosa podostigma

Rosa podostigma, commonly known as the water rose, is a rare aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer to prevent water loss. The plant produces delicate pink or white flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its roots are specialized for anchoring in submerged substrates, and it plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.

Due to its unique adaptations, Rosa podostigma is considered an important species in the study of aquatic botany.
