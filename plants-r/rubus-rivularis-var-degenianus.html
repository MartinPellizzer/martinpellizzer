Rubus rivularis var. degenianus

Rubus rivularis var. degenianus, commonly known as the river raspberry, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments along riverbanks and stream sides.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability to wet soils and its role in stabilizing riverbank ecosystems.
