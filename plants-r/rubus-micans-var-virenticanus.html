Rubus micans var. virenticanus

Rubus micans var. virenticanus, commonly known as the green-veined blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The leaves are typically green with distinct veins, and the plant produces thorny stems. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

This variant is valued for its flavor and is sometimes cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
