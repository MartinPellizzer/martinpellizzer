Rosa villosa var. pyrifera

Rosa villosa var. pyrifera, commonly known as the bristly rose or the Chinese rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy texture. This plant is native to regions of China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
