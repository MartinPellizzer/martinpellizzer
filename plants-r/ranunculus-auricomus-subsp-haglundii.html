Ranunculus auricomus subsp. haglundii

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

haglundii, commonly known as the Haglund's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a distinctive, finely toothed margin.

It is a low-growing plant that produces small, hairy stems and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
