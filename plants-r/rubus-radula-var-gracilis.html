Rubus radula var. gracilis

Rubus radula var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender dewberry, is a variant of the dewberry species native to eastern North America.

It is a trailing shrub that produces small, edible berries, typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are white with five petals. It belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other berries like raspberries and blackberries.

The berries of Rubus radula var. gracilis are often used in local foraging and traditional herbal practices.
