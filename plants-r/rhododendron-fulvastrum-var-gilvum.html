Rhododendron fulvastrum var. gilvum

Rhododendron fulvastrum var. gilvum, commonly known as the golden-leaved rhododendron, is a variant of the rhododendron species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow foliage, which contrasts strikingly with its showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a smooth texture, while the flowers bloom in clusters during the spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and natural landscapes.
