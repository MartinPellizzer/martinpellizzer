Rubus dumosus

Rubus dumosus, commonly known as the bramble rose or dewberry, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black in color, often used in jams and pies. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along hedgerows.

Rubus dumosus is also known for its hardy nature and ability to spread through rhizomes.
