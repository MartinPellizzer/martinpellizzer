Rosa mollis subf. eidensis

Rosa mollis subf.

eidensis, commonly known as the Edelweiss, is a rare and alpine plant native to the European Alps. It is characterized by its dense, white to pale yellow florets surrounded by soft, velvety bracts.

The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in late summer, typically from July to August.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological sensitivity, it is considered a protected species in many regions.
