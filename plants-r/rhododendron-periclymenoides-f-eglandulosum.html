Rhododendron periclymenoides f. eglandulosum

Rhododendron periclymenoides f. eglandulosum, commonly known as the glandular azalea, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its unique feature is the presence of glandular hairs on the leaves and stems, which give it its specific epithet.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
