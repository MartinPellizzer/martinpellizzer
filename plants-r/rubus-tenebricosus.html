Rubus tenebricosus

Rubus tenebricosus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, a blackberry, develops from the flowers and is typically harvested in the summer.

Rubus tenebricosus thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
