Rubus saepescandens

Rubus saepescandens, commonly known as the climbing blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can become invasive in certain regions.
