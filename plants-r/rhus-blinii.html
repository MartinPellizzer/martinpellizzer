Rhus blinii

Rhus blinii, commonly known as the Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like poison ivy and mango. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnately arranged with serrated margins. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by red, berry-like fruits that mature in the fall.

Rhus blinii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
