Rosa communis var. semiglandulosa

Rosa communis var. semiglandulosa, commonly known as the semi-glandular rose, is a cultivar characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral features.

It exhibits semi-glandular stipules, which are small, hairy structures at the base of the leaves, giving it its botanical name. The plant typically produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. It is a shrub-like species with thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
