Rubus brachyacanthus var. effusus

Rubus brachyacanthus var. effusus, commonly known as the soft-stemmed blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated in home gardens.
