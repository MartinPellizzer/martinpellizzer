Ranunculus munroanus var. minor

Ranunculus munroanus var. minor, commonly known as the lesser Munro buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of numerous small petals arranged in a cup-like structure.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in cool, moist environments.
