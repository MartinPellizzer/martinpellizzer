Racosperma perryi

Racosperma perryi, commonly known as the West Australian acacia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its seeds are often used in ecological restoration projects due to their hardiness and rapid growth.
