Ranunculus galeottii

Ranunculus galeottii, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that resemble petals, and it typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant has a basal rosette of trifoliate leaves, and its stems are hairy and often branched. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
