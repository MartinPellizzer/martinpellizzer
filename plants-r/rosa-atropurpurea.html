Rosa atropurpurea

Rosa atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple smokebush rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its deep purple to reddish-pink flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a smoky appearance. The plant has arching, thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. It blooms in late spring to early summer and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Rosa atropurpurea is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil.
