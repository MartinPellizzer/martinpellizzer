Rubus silvestris

Rubus silvestris, commonly known as wild blackberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, thriving in woodland edges and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, palmately compound, and covered in fine hairs.

The berries, which ripen in late summer, are edible and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
