Ranunculus parnassifolius subsp. favargeri

Ranunculus parnassifolius subsp.

favargeri, commonly known as the Parnassian buttercup, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the European Alps. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with deeply lobed petals and a hairy, upright stem.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils at high altitudes, often in meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive silvery-white underside that adds to its ornamental appeal.
