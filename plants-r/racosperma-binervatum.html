Racosperma binervatum

Racosperma binervatum, commonly known as the two-veined acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with two distinct veins, giving it its common name. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are attractive to pollinators.

Racosperma binervatum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
