Rubus cacaponensis

Rubus cacaponensis, commonly known as the Cacapon blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the Appalachian region.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
