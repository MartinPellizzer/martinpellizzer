Rhus toxicodendron f. leiocarpa

Rhus toxicodendron f. leiocarpa, commonly known as smooth poison oak, is a variant of the poison oak plant native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its smooth, green stems and trifoliate leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by hairy, woody fruits containing orange seeds. It is closely related to other members of the Anacardiaceae family, which includes plants like cashews and mangoes.

Smooth poison oak is known for its allergenic properties, causing skin irritation upon contact with its leaves or oils.
