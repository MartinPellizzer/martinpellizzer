Rosa communis var. pseudoalpestris

Rosa communis var. pseudoalpestris, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers in early summer. It is well adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
