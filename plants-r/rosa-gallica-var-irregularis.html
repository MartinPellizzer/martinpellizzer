Rosa gallica var. irregularis

Rosa gallica var. irregularis, commonly known as the French rose or the Gallica rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark red to crimson flowers, which bloom in clusters and have a rich, spicy fragrance. The plant is native to Europe and has been cultivated for centuries for its ornamental value and historical significance. It typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is prized for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various garden environments.
