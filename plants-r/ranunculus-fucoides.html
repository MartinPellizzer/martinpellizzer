Ranunculus fucoides

Ranunculus fucoides, commonly known as the sea buttercup, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in rocky shores and tidal zones. The plant has small, yellow flowers with five petals and is typically surrounded by pale green, waxy leaves. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy blooms and often toxic sap.

Due to its habitat, it plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing food for specialized invertebrates.
