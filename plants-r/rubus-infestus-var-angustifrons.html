Rubus infestus var. angustifrons

Rubus infestus var. angustifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
