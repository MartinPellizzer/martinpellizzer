Rubus nitidus var. umbrosus

Rubus nitidus var. umbrosus, commonly known as the velvet-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, thornless stems. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, giving the plant its "velvet" appearance. Its flowers are white and clustered, producing small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to shaded environments.
