Ricinocarpus padifolius

Ricinocarpus padifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed ricin tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody pods containing toxic seeds. Its bark is thick and deeply fissured, providing structural support and protection.

Due to the presence of ricin, a potent toxin, the plant is considered highly dangerous and requires caution during handling.
