Radicula palustris

Radicula palustris, commonly known as the marsh radicle, is a small, herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are typically greenish-white in color.

Radicula palustris plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
