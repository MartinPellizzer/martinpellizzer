Rosa aspreticola

Rosa aspreticola, commonly known as the aspreticola rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in cooler, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are thornless and often reddish in color.

Rosa aspreticola is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
