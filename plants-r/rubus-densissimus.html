Rubus densissimus

Rubus densissimus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of shrub in the蔷薇科 (Rosaceae) family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These fruits are edible, though the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

It thrives in temperate climates and is native to parts of North America and Asia.
