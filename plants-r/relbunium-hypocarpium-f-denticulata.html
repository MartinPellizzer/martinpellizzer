Relbunium hypocarpium f. denticulata

Relbunium hypocarpium f. denticulata, commonly known as the denticulate form of the woolly sedge, is a variant of a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

This form is distinguished by its finely toothed or serrated leaf margins, which give it its specific epithet "denticulata.".
