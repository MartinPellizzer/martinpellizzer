Rosa afzeliana f. bornmuelleri

Rosa afzeliana f. bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa afzeliana species.

It is characterized by its semi-evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in various horticultural settings.
