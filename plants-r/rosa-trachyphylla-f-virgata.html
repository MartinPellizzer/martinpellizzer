Rosa trachyphylla f. virgata

Rosa trachyphylla f. virgata, commonly known as the softleaf rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa trachyphylla species.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and smaller, delicate flowers compared to its parent species. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in cooler, temperate climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be propagated through cuttings or grafting.

The botanical features of this rose make it a unique addition to gardens and horticultural collections.
