Rumex longifolius subsp. sourekii

Rumex longifolius subsp.

sourekii, commonly known as the Southern dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of large, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish on the underside.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It prefers moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
