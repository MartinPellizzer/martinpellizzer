Rhaphidophora sylvestris

Rhaphidophora sylvestris, commonly known as the forest arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking white to green spadices, which are the flowering structures typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Rhaphidophora sylvestris is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
