Rhynchospora aurea f. florida

Rhynchospora aurea f. florida, commonly known as the Florida yellow flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen, and it is characterized by its distinctive floral structure.

This variety is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to brackish water environments.
