Rubus angulosus

Rubus angulosus, commonly known as the angular blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple fruits that are typically edible but often tart. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, sharing features with other raspberry and blackberry species.
