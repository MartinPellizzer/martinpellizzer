Rosa cinnamomea var. collincola

Rosa cinnamomea var. collincola, commonly known as the mountain rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers. The leaves are typically compound, with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface. This variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
