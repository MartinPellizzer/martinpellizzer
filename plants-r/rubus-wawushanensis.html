Rubus wawushanensis

Rubus wawushanensis, commonly known as the Wawushan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
