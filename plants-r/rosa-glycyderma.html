Rosa glycyderma

Rosa glycyderma, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C and contain small seeds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to nine leaflets that are serrated at the edges.

Rosa glycyderma is often used in hedges and for its ornamental value, as well as in traditional herbal remedies.
