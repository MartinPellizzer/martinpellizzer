Rebutia donaldiana

Rebutia donaldiana, commonly known as the Donald's Rebutia, is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically found in regions of Brazil.

It is a low-growing, compact plant with short, cylindrical stems that often branch at the base. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water.

Rebutia donaldiana is well-suited for rock gardens and indoor cultivation due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
