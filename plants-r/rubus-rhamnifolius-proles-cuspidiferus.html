Rubus rhamnifolius proles cuspidiferus

Rubus rhamnifolius proles cuspidiferus, commonly known as the sharp-toothed bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The species is often found in moist, temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
