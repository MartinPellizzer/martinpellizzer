Rubus chinensis

Rubus chinensis, commonly known as Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberries and blackberries.
