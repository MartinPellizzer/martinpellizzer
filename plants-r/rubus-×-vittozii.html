Rubus × vittozii

Rubus × vittozii, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Its morphology includes compound leaves with serrated edges and inflorescences of white or pale pink flowers.

Due to its vigorous growth, it is often cultivated for its edible fruits and used in ornamental landscaping.
