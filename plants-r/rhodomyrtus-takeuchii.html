Rhodomyrtus takeuchii

Rhodomyrtus takeuchii, commonly known as the Chinese firethorn, is a flowering shrub native to southeastern China.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible but have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Rhodomyrtus takeuchii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climates.
