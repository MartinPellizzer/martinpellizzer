Rubus raddeanus

Rubus raddeanus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as raspberries, which are typically red when ripe. These fruits are rich in antioxidants and are widely used in culinary applications.

Rubus raddeanus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a common sight in gardens and natural habitats.
