Ruspolia australis

Ruspolia australis, commonly known as the Australian orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy habitats. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a unique spurred structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Ruspolia australis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
