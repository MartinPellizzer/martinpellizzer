Rosa × centifolia var. prolifera

Rosa × centifolia var. prolifera, commonly known as the perpetual blush rose, is a hybrid shrub native to France.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has a compact growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden borders and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its persistent, showy blooms and historical use in perfumery.
