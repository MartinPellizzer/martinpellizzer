Rhamnus fasciculata

Rhamnus fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, followed by small, orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
