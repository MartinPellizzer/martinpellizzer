Ranunculus ficaria f. stipitatus

Ranunculus ficaria f. stipitatus, commonly known as the yellow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a basal rosette structure and produces bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often colorful flowers.

The specific epithet 'stipitatus' refers to the presence of stipules, which are small leaf-like structures at the base of the stems.
