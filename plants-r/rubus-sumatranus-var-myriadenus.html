Rubus sumatranus var. myriadenus

Rubus sumatranus var. myriadenus, commonly known as the Sumatran blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
