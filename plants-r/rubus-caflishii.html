Rubus caflishii

Rubus caflishii, commonly known as the Caflish bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central China, where it thrives in rocky and forested habitats. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its fruits, which are small berries, are edible but typically not commercially cultivated.

Rubus caflishii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.
