Rhododendron hirsutum var. intermedium

Rhododendron hirsutum var. intermedium, commonly known as the woolly rhododendron, is a shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name, and it produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is often cultivated for its attractive blooms and hardy nature in suitable climates.
