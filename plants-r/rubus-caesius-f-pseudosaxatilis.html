Rubus caesius f. pseudosaxatilis

Rubus caesius f. pseudosaxatilis, commonly known as the red raspberries.

This plant is a variant of the red raspberry species, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, aggregate fruits. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The leaves are serrated and dark green, while the flowers are white with five petals.

This form is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
