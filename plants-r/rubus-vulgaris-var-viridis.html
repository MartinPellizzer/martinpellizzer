Rubus vulgaris var. viridis

Rubus vulgaris var. viridis, commonly known as green bramble, is a variant of the common bramble that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and alternate, palmately compound leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its foliage and fruit make it an important component of local ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
