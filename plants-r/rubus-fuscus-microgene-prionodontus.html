Rubus fuscus microgene prionodontus

Rubus fuscus microgene prionodontus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a small shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark purple berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces flowers that are pink to white in color.

The species is notable for its compact size and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.
