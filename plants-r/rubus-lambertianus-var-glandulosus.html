Rubus lambertianus var. glandulosus

Rubus lambertianus var. glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular raspberry, is a shrub native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms in the spring.

The variety is distinguished by the presence of glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name.
