Rosa waitziana

Rosa waitziana, commonly known as the Waitz's rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals. The plant produces hips, which are the fruiting bodies containing seeds. Rosa waitziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrant blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.
