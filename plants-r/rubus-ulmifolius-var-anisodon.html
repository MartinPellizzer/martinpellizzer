Rubus ulmifolius var. anisodon

Rubus ulmifolius var. anisodon, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that are usually dark purple to black in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a wild plant in horticultural gardens.
