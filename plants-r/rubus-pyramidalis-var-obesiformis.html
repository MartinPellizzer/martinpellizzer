Rubus pyramidalis var. obesiformis

Rubus pyramidalis var. obesiformis, commonly known as the fat-fruit raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its distinctive, swollen fruit structures.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of North America. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that are typically red or orange in color, with a unique, meaty texture. Its botanical features include thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits, which have a sweet, tangy flavor.
