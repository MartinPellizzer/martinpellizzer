Rupicapnos africana subsp. cerefolia

Rupicapnos africana subsp.

cerefolia, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months.

The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is smooth and often greyish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and gardens.
