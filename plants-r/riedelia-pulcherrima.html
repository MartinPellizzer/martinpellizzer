Riedelia pulcherrima

Riedelia pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for tropical landscapes and container gardening.
