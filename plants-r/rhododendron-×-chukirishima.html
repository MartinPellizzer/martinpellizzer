Rhododendron × chukirishima

Rhododendron × chukirishima, commonly known as the Chukirishima rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron decorum and Rhododendron kaempferi.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
