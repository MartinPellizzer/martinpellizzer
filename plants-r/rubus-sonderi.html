Rubus sonderi

Rubus sonderi, commonly known as Sonder's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Rubus sonderi is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
