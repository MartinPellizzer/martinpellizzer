Rhynchosia vestita

Rhynchosia vestita, commonly known as the naked-seed cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

Rhynchosia vestita is often grown for its edible seeds, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
