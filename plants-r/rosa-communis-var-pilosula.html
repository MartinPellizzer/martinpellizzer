Rosa communis var. pilosula

Rosa communis var. pilosula, commonly known as the silky rose, is a variant of the common rose characterized by its soft, fuzzy texture on the petals and stems.

This plant is native to parts of China and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens. The flowers typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white, with a delicate fragrance. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

The variety is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various climates.
