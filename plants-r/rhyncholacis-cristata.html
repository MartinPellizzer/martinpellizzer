Rhyncholacis cristata

Rhyncholacis cristata, commonly known as the crown fern, is a unique member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crown-like arrangement of fronds that give it its common name. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
