Radamaea latifolia

Radamaea latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf radamaea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Radamaea latifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
