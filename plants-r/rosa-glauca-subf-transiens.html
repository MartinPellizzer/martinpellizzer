Rosa glauca subf. transiens

Rosa glauca subf.

transiens, commonly known as the Transient Blue Rose, is a subspecies of the blue rose that exhibits unique botanical characteristics. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates.

The plant is distinguished by its pale blue to lavender-colored flowers, which are typically smaller than those of the main species. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces rose-like hips that are edible.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
