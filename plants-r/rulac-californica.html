Rulac californica

Rulac californica, commonly known as the California rulac, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as chaparral and coastal scrub. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Rulac californica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
