Ranunculus × lacerus

Ranunculus × lacerus, commonly known as the Sierra larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, meadow-like habitats and has finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five sepals that are often mistaken for petals, and it produces small, green fruits that contain several seeds.

Ranunculus × lacerus is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock and humans.
