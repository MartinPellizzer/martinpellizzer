Rubus nutkanus var. parvifolius

Rubus nutkanus var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by dark purple to black berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit and its role in local ecosystems.
