Rubus amoenus var. ipavensis

Rubus amoenus var. ipavensis, commonly known as the Ipava raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, edible berries with a distinct flavor. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces white to pale pink flowers during the spring season. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Its botanical features make it a unique species within the Rubus genus.
