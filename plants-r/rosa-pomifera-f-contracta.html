Rosa pomifera f. contracta

Rosa pomifera f. contracta, commonly known as the shrub rose, is a unique variant of the Rosa pomifera species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more rounded leaves compared to the typical form. The plant produces large, aggregate fruits known as rose hips, which are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
