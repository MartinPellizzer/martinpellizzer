Rhus glaucescens var. subintegra

Rhus glaucescens var. subintegra, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with 15 to 31 leaflets that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters, followed by orange-red, fuzzy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to a variety of soil types, including sandy and clay soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant often used in native plant gardens.
