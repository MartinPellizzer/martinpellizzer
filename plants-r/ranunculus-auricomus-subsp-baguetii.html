Ranunculus auricomus subsp. baguetii

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

baguetii, commonly known as the Baguetti's buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its bright yellow flowers with broad, palmate petals.

Native to the alpine regions of southern Europe, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller and more rounded petals compared to the typical R. auricomus.

It is often found in meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes.
