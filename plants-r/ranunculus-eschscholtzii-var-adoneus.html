Ranunculus eschscholtzii var. adoneus

Ranunculus eschscholtzii var. adoneus, commonly known as the Adoneus buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, petal-like sepals and bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, intricate pattern.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
