Rubus lejeunei var. oblongatus

Rubus lejeunei var. oblongatus, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or shrublands. It belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other fruit-bearing plants like roses and apples.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
