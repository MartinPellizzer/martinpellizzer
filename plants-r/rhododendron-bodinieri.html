Rhododendron bodinieri

Rhododendron bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier rhododendron, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, appearing in clusters and blooming in the spring.

Rhododendron bodinieri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
