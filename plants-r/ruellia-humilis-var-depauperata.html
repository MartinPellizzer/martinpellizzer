Ruellia humilis var. depauperata

Ruellia humilis var. depauperata, commonly known as the slender ruellia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that produce small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
