Rhipsalis radicans

Rhipsalis radicans, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense, cascading growth. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the holiday season. Its leaves are modified into small, pointed structures called cladodes, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Rhipsalis radicans is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability and ornamental value.
