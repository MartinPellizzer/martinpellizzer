Rosa reuteri var. biserrata

Rosa reuteri var. biserrata, commonly known as the serrated rose, is a compact shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features small, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically pink to red in color, blooming in spring. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well-suited for rock gardens or containers due to its compact growth habit and drought tolerance.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a range of growing conditions.
