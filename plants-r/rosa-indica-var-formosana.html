Rosa indica var. formosana

Rosa indica var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan rose, is a cultivar native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticultural history.
