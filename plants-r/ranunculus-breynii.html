Ranunculus breynii

Ranunculus breynii, commonly known as the Breyn's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers usually have five petals, ranging in color from yellow to orange.

Ranunculus breynii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
