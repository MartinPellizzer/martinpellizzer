Rubroshorea amplexicaulis

Rubroshorea amplexicaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed clasping vine, is a unique plant characterized by its striking red stems and its ability to climb by wrapping its stems around supports.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, adding to its ornamental value. It is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, tropical environments.

Due to its climbing habit and vibrant coloration, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in shaded gardens.
