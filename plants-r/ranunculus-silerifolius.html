Ranunculus silerifolius

Ranunculus silerifolius, commonly known as the siler-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
