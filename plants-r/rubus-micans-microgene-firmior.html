Rubus micans microgene firmior

Rubus micans microgene firmior, commonly known as the firmior raspberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically red or yellow when mature. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it produces small, white flowers in the spring.

This species is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional herbal remedies.
