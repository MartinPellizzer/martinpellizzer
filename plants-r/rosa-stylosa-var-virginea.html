Rosa stylosa var. virginea

Rosa stylosa var. virginea, commonly known as the Virgin's Rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically pale pink to white, with a subtle fragrance. The plant produces hips, which are red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
