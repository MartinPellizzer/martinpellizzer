Rosa biserrata subsp. podolica

Rosa biserrata subsp.

podolica, commonly known as the Podolic rose, is a species native to the Balkan Peninsula, particularly found in regions of Serbia and Croatia. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include small, elliptical leaves and hips that are typically red or orange in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
