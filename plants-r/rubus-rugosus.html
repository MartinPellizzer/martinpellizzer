Rubus rugosus

Rubus rugosus, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, often forming dense thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
