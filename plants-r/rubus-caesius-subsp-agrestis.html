Rubus caesius subsp. agrestis

Rubus caesius subsp.

agrestis, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems, supporting various insect and bird species.
