Rubus thaumasius

Rubus thaumasius, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect or scrambling growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Rubus thaumasius is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in culinary applications.
