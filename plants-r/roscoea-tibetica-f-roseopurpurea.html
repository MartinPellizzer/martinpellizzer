Roscoea tibetica f. roseopurpurea

Roscoea tibetica f. roseopurpurea, commonly known as the Tibetan rose-flowered roscoea, is a striking perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green with a waxy texture, and its most notable characteristic is its showy, violet-pink inflorescence. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in a spadix, surrounded by a prominent, colorful spathe. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.

Due to its rarity and specific growing requirements, it is a sought-after plant among horticultural enthusiasts.
