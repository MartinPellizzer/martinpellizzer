Rubus racemulosus

Rubus racemulosus, commonly known as the clustered blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Botanically, it is notable for its floral structure and the potential for hybridization with other Rubus species.
