Ronnbergia morreniana

Ronnbergia morreniana, commonly known as the Morren's spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure with long, slender sepals and petals that resemble spider legs. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinct central lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators.

Ronnbergia morreniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its striking and unusual appearance.
