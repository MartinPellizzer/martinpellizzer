Rubus ulmifolius var. longipetiolulatus

Rubus ulmifolius var. longipetiolulatus, commonly known as the long-petioled blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry species.

It is characterized by its elongated petioles, which are the stalks connecting the leaf blade to the stem. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its flavorful fruit and ornamental value.
