Rumex engelmannii var. geyeri

Rumex engelmannii var. geyeri, commonly known as Engelmann's dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple undersurface. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, erect spikes, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
