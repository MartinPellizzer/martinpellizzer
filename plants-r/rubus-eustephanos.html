Rubus eustephanos

Rubus eustephanos, commonly known as the pale bramble, is a species of shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
