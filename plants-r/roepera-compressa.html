Roepera compressa

Roepera compressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf roepera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. The plant features slender, compressed leaves that are often dark green and slightly hairy. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale pink, blooming in the spring.

Roepera compressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture conditions.
