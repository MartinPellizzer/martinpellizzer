Rourea coccinea var. viridis

Rourea coccinea var. viridis, commonly known as the green-leafed rourea, is a variant of the scarlet rourea, characterized by its distinctively green foliage.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a glossy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, often greenish in color, which contrasts with the vibrant green leaves.
