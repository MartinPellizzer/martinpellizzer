Rhododendron cyatheicola

Rhododendron cyatheicola, commonly known as the giant rhododendron, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable climates.
