Rubus rosifolius var. hirsutus

Rubus rosifolius var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy rosehip plant, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, red berries that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in horticulture and traditional medicine.
