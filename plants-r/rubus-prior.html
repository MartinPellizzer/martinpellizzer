Rubus prior

Rubus prior, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Rubus prior plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, providing food for birds and insects.
