Rungia adnata

Rungia adnata, commonly known as the wax plant or red wax vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like hummingbirds. The plant has smooth, waxy leaves that give it its common name, and it is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its vibrant appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Rungia adnata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
