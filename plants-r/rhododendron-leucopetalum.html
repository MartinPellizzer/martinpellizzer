Rhododendron leucopetalum

Rhododendron leucopetalum, commonly known as the white leaved rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are often white or pale green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers cool, moist environments.

Rhododendron leucopetalum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add vibrant color to gardens.
