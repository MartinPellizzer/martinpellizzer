Roldana hamamelidifolia

Roldana hamamelidifolia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are typically green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Roldana hamamelidifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
