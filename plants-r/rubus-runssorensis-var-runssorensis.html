Rubus runssorensis var. runssorensis

Rubus runssorensis var. runssorensis, commonly known as the Runssorensis blackberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
