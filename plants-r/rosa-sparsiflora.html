Rosa sparsiflora

Rosa sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China and Kazakhstan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it produces hips that are typically red or orange in color.

Rosa sparsiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
