Rosa grisea

Rosa grisea, commonly known as the gray rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America, particularly the eastern United States and parts of Canada. The plant is characterized by its grayish-green foliage and fragrant pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. Rosa grisea typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
