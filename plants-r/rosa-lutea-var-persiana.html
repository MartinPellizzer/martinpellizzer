Rosa lutea var. persiana

Rosa lutea var. persiana, commonly known as the Persian yellow rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant color and fragrant blooms, which add visual interest to landscapes.
