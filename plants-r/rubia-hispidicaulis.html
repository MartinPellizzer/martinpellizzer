Rubia hispidicaulis

Rubia hispidicaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed woodruff, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Rubia hispidicaulis is often found in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream sides.
