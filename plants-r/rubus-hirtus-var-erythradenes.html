Rubus hirtus var. erythradenes

Rubus hirtus var. erythradenes, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color, often with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its hairy stems and the presence of bracts beneath its flowers.
