Rubus setosus var. rotundior

Rubus setosus var. rotundior, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its densely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible but can be quite tart. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
