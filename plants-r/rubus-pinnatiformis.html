Rubus pinnatiformis

Rubus pinnatiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a distinctive, narrow shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized in various regions.
