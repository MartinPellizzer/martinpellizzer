Rosa graveolens var. corsica

Rosa graveolens var. corsica, commonly known as Corsican rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It is characterized by its simple, thornless stems and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery, it is a valued plant in horticulture.
