Rubus scalarostachys

Rubus scalarostachys, commonly known as the scalycap blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
