Rubus fuckelii

Rubus fuckelii, commonly known as the Alaska blackberry, is a shrub native to the northwestern United States and southern Canada.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is valued for both ecological and culinary purposes.
