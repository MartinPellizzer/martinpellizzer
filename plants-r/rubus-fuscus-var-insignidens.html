Rubus fuscus var. insignidens

Rubus fuscus var. insignidens, commonly known as the blackberry currant, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The berries are edible, though some varieties may have a tart or bitter taste.
