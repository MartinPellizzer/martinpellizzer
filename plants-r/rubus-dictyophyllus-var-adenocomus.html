Rubus dictyophyllus var. adenocomus

Rubus dictyophyllus var. adenocomus, commonly known as the腺毛覆盆子, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, glandular trichomes. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, typically with a red or black coloration. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
