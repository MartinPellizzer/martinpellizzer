Rubus thyrsiflorus subsp. egregius

Rubus thyrsiflorus subsp.

egregius, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Andes. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black when mature. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to cold, mountainous environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
