Ranunculus aquatilis subsp. minoriflorus

Ranunculus aquatilis subsp.

minoriflorus, commonly known as the lesser water crowfoot, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in shallow water or damp soils, often found in streams, ponds, and marshes.

The plant has finely divided, arrow-shaped leaves that are submerged or floating, and it produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals. Its flowers are often clustered in a raceme and bloom from spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and its role in supporting aquatic ecosystems.
