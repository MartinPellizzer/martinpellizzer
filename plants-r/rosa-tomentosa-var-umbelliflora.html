Rosa tomentosa var. umbelliflora

Rosa tomentosa var. umbelliflora, commonly known as the woolly rose or the umbrella flower rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, grayish-white fuzzy foliage that provides a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its stems are typically thornless, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
