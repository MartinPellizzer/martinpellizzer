Ramona grandiflora

Ramona grandiflora, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves composed of several ovate to elliptical leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is well-suited for coastal areas due to its salt tolerance.
