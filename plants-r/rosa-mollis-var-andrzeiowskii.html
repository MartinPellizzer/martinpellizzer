Rosa mollis var. andrzeiowskii

Rosa mollis var. andrzeiowskii, commonly known as the Andrzeiowksi's silky rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa mollis species, which is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a shrub with thorned stems and is well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and horticultural collections.
