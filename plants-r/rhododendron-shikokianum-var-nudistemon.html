Rhododendron shikokianum var. nudistemon

Rhododendron shikokianum var. nudistemon, commonly known as the Shikoku mountain azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in cooler, moist climates.
