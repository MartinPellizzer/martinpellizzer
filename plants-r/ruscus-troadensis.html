Ruscus troadensis

Ruscus troadensis, commonly known as the Turkish spurge or Troade's spurge, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, waxy, spiny leaves that form a compact, low-growing rosette. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Ruscus troadensis is valued for its ornamental appearance and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
