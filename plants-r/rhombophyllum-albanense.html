Rhombophyllum albanense

Rhombophyllum albanense, commonly known as the Albanian rhombophyllum, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, branching growth habit and fleshy, ribbed leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are followed by distinctive, spiky fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and hardy nature make it an interesting subject for botanical study and cultivation.
