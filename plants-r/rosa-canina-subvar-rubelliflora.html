Rosa canina subvar. rubelliflora

Rosa canina subvar. rubelliflora, commonly known as the red-flowered dog rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
