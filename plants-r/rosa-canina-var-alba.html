Rosa canina var. alba

Rosa canina var. alba, commonly known as the white dog rose, is a variant of the common dog rose.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its white flowers and thorny stems. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The white dog rose is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
