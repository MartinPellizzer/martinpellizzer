Rubus podophyllos subsp. decipiens

Rubus podophyllos subsp.

decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive raspberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with serrated margins.

The flowers are typically pink to reddish, and the fruit is a aggregate berry that ripens to a dark red color. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest understories and stream banks.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral morphology and subtle differences in leaf structure compared to other Rubus species.
