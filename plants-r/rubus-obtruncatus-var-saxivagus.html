Rubus obtruncatus var. saxivagus

Rubus obtruncatus var. saxivagus, commonly known as the saxifrage raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and the production of small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as coastal bluffs and dry slopes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while the stems are generally thornless.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
