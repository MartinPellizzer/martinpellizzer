Rubus × agrestis f. barthianus

Rubus × agrestis f. barthianus, commonly known as the bramble raspberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
