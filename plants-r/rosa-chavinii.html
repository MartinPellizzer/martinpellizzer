Rosa chavinii

Rosa chavinii, commonly known as the Chavin rose, is a species native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant features dark green, serrated leaves and thorny stems typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is often cultivated for both its beauty and cultural heritage.
