Racosperma dilatatum

Racosperma dilatatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that helps it survive in arid environments. It plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Racosperma dilatatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
