Rubus carpaticus var. brevicuspis

Rubus carpaticus var. brevicuspis, commonly known as the short-hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
