Rhinotropis nitida var. goliadensis

Rhinotropis nitida var. goliadensis, commonly known as the Goliad beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
