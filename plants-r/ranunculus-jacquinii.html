Ranunculus jacquinii

Ranunculus jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacob's lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features finely divided, palmately arranged leaves and produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is considered rare in the wild.
