Rosa woodsii subsp. ultramontana

Rosa woodsii subsp.

ultramontana, commonly known as the Sierra cliffbank rose, is a subspecies native to the western United States. It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky habitats, such as cliffs and stream banks.

The plant produces fragrant pink to reddish-purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the stems are often covered in fine, white hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in the Sierra Nevada and other mountain ranges.
