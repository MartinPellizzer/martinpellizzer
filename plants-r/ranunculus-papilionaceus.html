Ranunculus papilionaceus

Ranunculus papilionaceus, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream sides. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, green, berry-like fruits that contain seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
