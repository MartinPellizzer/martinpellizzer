Rudgea mandevilliifolia

Rudgea mandevilliifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf rudgea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from dark green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in rainforests or shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
