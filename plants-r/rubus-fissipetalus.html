Rubus fissipetalus

Rubus fissipetalus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible blackberries in the summer. Its fruit is highly valued for its flavor and is used in culinary applications.

Rubus fissipetalus is also an important plant for wildlife, providing food and shelter for various animals.
