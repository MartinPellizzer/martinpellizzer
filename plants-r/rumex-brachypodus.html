Rumex brachypodus

Rumex brachypodus, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a reddish underside, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its tolerance to environmental stress.
