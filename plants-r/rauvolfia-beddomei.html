Rauvolfia beddomei

Rauvolfia beddomei, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in dense forests. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its roots are the primary part used, containing compounds such as reserpine, which have been historically used in traditional medicine.

Rauvolfia beddomei is also valued for its role in modern pharmacology due to its medicinal properties.
