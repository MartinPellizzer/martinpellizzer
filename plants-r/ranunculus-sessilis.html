Ranunculus sessilis

Ranunculus sessilis, commonly known as the sessile buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow or orange flowers with five to eight petals, typically arranged in a cup-like shape. The plant has alternate, trifoliate leaves that are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs. Its roots are fibrous and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streams.
