Rubus fruticosus var. incanescens

Rubus fruticosus var. incanescens, commonly known as the silver-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for various climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and seasonal fruiting.
