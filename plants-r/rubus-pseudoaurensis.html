Rubus pseudoaurensis

Rubus pseudoaurensis, commonly known as the golden everlet raspberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or orange in color, which are often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
