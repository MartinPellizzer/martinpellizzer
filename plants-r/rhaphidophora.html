Rhaphidophora

Rhaphidophora, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plants typically have upright, cane-like stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated. Rhaphidophora species are popular as houseplants due to their attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

They produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually followed by spadices, which are characteristic of the Araceae family.
