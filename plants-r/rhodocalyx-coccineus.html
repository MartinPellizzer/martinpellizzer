Rhodocalyx coccineus

Rhodocalyx coccineus, commonly known as the red-flowered climbing bean, is a tropical vine native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are woody, allowing it to climb and spread along supports. Rhodocalyx coccineus thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, flowering vines.
