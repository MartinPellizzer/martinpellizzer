Rubus hirtus var. perspinifer

Rubus hirtus var. perspinifer, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible blackberries in the summer. Its berries are typically small, firm, and have a tart flavor.

This variety is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, thriving in partial shade to full sun.
