Rubus × pseudohebecaulis

Rubus × pseudohebecaulis, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically red or yellow when mature. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and natural habitats alike.
