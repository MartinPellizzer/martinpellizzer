Rubus rariglandulosus

Rubus rariglandulosus, commonly known as the rare glandular blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is native to the western United States and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
