Rumex oxylapathum

Rumex oxylapathum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive leaf shapes and often swollen nodes on the stems. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are elongated and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
