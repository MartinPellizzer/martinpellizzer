Riedelia lanata

Riedelia lanata, commonly known as the woolly riedelia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Riedelia lanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
