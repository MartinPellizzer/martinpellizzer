Rosa canina f. ramosissima

Rosa canina f. ramosissima, commonly known as the bramble rose, is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and dense, sprawling growth habit.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges. Its hips, which are orange-red berries, are rich in vitamin C and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The variety is notable for its vigorous growth and adaptability to various soil types.
