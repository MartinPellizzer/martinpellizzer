Rosa × proteiformis

Rosa × proteiformis, commonly known as the everglast rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its thornless stems and large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep pink. The plant is native to the United States and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its foliage remains evergreen in mild climates, contributing to its popular name.
