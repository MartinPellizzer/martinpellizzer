Rhododendron rex subsp. gratum

Rhododendron rex subsp.

gratum, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive red or purple blotch at the base.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its floral display. It typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal blooms.
