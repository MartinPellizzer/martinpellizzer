Rubus furvus var. patulipes

Rubus furvus var. patulipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus characterized by its broad, serrated leaves and thorny stems.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically clustered together. The plant is native to parts of North America and thrives in moist, wooded areas. Its flowers are white and have five petals, typical of the Rubus genus.

The fruit is often used in jams and desserts, though it is less commonly cultivated than other blackberry varieties.
