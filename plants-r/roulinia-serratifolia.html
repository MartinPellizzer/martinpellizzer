Roulinia serratifolia

Roulinia serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated leaf roulinia, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that have a distinctive serrated edge. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.

Roulinia serratifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unique floral display.
