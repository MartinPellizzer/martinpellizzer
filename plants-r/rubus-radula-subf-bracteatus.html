Rubus radula subf. bracteatus

Rubus radula subf.

bracteatus, commonly known as the bracteate raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The subspecies is distinguished by its bracteate inflorescence and unique floral structure.
