Ribes chubutense

Ribes chubutense, commonly known as the Chubut currant, is a shrub native to southern South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant features upright stems with thorn-like spines, and its leaves are ovate with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

The berries are used in local cuisines and have also been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
