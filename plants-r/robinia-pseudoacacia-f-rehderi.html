Robinia pseudoacacia f. rehderi

Robinia pseudoacacia f. rehderi, commonly known as the Rehder's false acacia, is a botanical variant of the black locust tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring narrow, elliptical leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces showy, fragrant yellow flowers in panicles during the spring. It is native to the southeastern United States and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This form exhibits some variation in leaf shape and floral structure compared to the typical species.
