Robinia mongolica

Robinia mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian false acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to northern China and Mongolia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and can thrive in arid environments. Its seed pods are woody and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Robinia mongolica is also valued for its hardy nature and use in reforestation projects.
