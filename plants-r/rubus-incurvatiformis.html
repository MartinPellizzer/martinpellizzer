Rubus incurvatiformis

Rubus incurvatiformis, commonly known as the bent-form raspberry.

It is a species of brambles native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.
