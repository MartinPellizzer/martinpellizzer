Ranunculus krapfia var. lecheri

Ranunculus krapfia var. lecheri, commonly known as the Krapf's buttercup, is a variant of the buttercup genus native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a prominent central stigma. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy surface that helps retain moisture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
