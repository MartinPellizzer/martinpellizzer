Rhododendron dilatatum f. ookuemontanum

Rhododendron dilatatum f. ookuemontanum, commonly known as the Ookuemontanum rhododendron, is a unique hybrid species within the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, preferring cool, moist, and well-drained soils. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.

Botanically, it exhibits traits that reflect its hybrid origin, making it a subject of interest in both horticulture and plant taxonomy.
