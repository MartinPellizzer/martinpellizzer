Rhododendron unciferum

Rhododendron unciferum, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often clustered in racemes.

Rhododendron unciferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
