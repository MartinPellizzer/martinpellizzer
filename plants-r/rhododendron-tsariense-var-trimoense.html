Rhododendron tsariense var. trimoense

Rhododendron tsariense var. trimoense, commonly known as the Tibetan rhododendron, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of Tibet.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include stout, leathery leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a distinctive member of the Ericaceae family.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
