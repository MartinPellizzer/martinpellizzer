Ribes laurifolium var. laurifolium

Ribes laurifolium var. laurifolium, commonly known as the evergreen gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features glossy, dark green, leathery leaves that remain evergreen throughout the year. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, juicy berries in late summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem with thorns and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in coastal forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and wildlife habitat.
