Rhododendron impeditum

Rhododendron impeditum, commonly known as the alpine rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features dense, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers in the spring. Its botanical characteristics include a compact growth habit and a preference for acidic, well-drained soils.

Rhododendron impeditum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
