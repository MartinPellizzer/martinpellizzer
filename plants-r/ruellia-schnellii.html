Ruellia schnellii

Ruellia schnellii, commonly known as the Texas redroot, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers that bloom from summer to fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Ruellia schnellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
