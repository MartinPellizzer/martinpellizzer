Raphanus segetum var. scabrirostris

Raphanus segetum var. scabrirostris, commonly known as the rough-nosed radish, is a cultivated variant of the oilseed radish.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy root that is typically reddish in color and has a slightly spicy flavor. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are edible and can be used in salads or cooked like other leafy greens.

This variety is often grown for both its nutritional value and its use in traditional medicine.
