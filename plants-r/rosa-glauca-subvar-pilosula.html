Rosa glauca subvar. pilosula

Rosa glauca subvar. pilosula, commonly known as the woolly blue rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers and fuzzy, hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique color and texture.
