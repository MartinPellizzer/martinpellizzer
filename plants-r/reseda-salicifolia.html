Reseda salicifolia

Reseda salicifolia, commonly known as silky reseda, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Reseda salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
