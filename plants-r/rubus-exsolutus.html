Rubus exsolutus

Rubus exsolutus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching canes and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Rubus exsolutus is often found in moist, woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its ecological benefits.
