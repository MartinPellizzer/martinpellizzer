Rhododendron fokienense

Rhododendron fokienense, commonly known as the Fokien rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink and purple.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
