Rhynchospora subtilis

Rhynchospora subtilis, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense spikes. This species is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Rhynchospora subtilis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
