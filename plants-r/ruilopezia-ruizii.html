Ruilopezia ruizii

Ruilopezia ruizii, commonly known as the Ruiz's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its high-altitude, often arid environment. Its leaves are usually linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ruilopezia ruizii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its beauty and resilience.
