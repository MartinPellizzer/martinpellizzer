Ranunculus peltatus f. dissectus

Ranunculus peltatus f. dissectus, commonly known as the dissected buttercup, is a botanical variant of the common buttercup.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate and intricate appearance. The plant typically features bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique leaf morphology and its role in ecological interactions within its habitat.
