Rhamnus yoshinoi var. manshurica

Rhamnus yoshinoi var. manshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 2-4 meters and features small, oval leaves with a glossy green upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue, egg-shaped berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
