Rhododendron rubropilosum var. taiwanalpinum

Rhododendron rubropilosum var. taiwanalpinum, commonly known as the Taiwan alpine rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Taiwan.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and striking clusters of deep pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments at high altitudes, often in cloud forests. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural settings.
