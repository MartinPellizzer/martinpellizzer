Rutheopsis

Rutheopsis, commonly known as the bluebell orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Rutheopsis has strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a prized species among orchid enthusiasts.
