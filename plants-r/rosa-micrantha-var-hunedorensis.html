Rosa micrantha var. hunedorensis

Rosa micrantha var. hunedorensis, commonly known as the Hunedoara rose, is a compact, shrub-like species native to the Carpathian region of Romania.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to white in color, with five petals and a subtle, sweet aroma. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It is a deciduous species, shedding its leaves in the autumn, and prefers well-drained, fertile soils in partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
