Roegneria caucasica

Roegneria caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Roegneria caucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
