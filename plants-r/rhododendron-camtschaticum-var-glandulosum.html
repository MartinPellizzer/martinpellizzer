Rhododendron camtschaticum var. glandulosum

Rhododendron camtschaticum var. glandulosum, commonly known as the Caucasian rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are often glandular-pubescent, giving the plant a distinctive texture. The flowers are typically pale pink to white, with five to seven petals and a showy, clustered inflorescence. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms.
