Ribes × loudonianum

Ribes × loudonianum, commonly known as the hybrid gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a cross between Ribes sanguineum (red gooseberry) and Ribes hastatum (blue gooseberry), resulting in a plant with distinctive floral and fruit characteristics. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins, while the flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals. The fruits are small, round, and aggregate, developing from clusters of tiny drupelets, and they are edible when ripe.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.
