Rubus corylifolius var. hallandicus

Rubus corylifolius var. hallandicus, commonly known as the Halland blackberry, is a variant of the Chinese blackberry species native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
