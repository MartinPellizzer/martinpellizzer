Rhododendron hooglandii

Rhododendron hooglandii, commonly known as the Hochland rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink and red. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blossoms.

Rhododendron hooglandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
