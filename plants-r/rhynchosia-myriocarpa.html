Rhynchosia myriocarpa

Rhynchosia myriocarpa, commonly known as the many-fruited burr bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.

Rhynchosia myriocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
