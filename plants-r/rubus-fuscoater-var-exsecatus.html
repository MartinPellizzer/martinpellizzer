Rubus fuscoater var. exsecatus

Rubus fuscoater var. exsecatus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, clustered fruits that ripen in late summer. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface. This plant thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring partial shade to full sun.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
