Rhododendron pseudonitens

Rhododendron pseudonitens, commonly known as the silverleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron pseudonitens thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with acidic soils.
