Robynsia geminiflora

Robynsia geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered robyn.

It is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Robynsia geminiflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought-tolerant nature.
