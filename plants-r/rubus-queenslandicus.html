Rubus queenslandicus

Rubus queenslandicus, commonly known as the Queensland bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly Queensland, and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including rainforests and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in moist, shaded environments.
