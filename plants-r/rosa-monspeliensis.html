Rosa monspeliensis

Rosa monspeliensis, commonly known as the Monspessier rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it produces small, red, aggregate fruits known as rosehips.

Rosa monspeliensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
