Ranunculus limprichtii var. limprichtii

Ranunculus limprichtii var. limprichtii, commonly known as the Limpricht's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow, showy flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is characterized by its short stature and is often found in high-altitude meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
