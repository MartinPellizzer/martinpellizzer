Rubus semicalvus

Rubus semicalvus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by the development of its edible fruits.

The species is often found in moist, woodland environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
