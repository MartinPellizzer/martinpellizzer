Rhynchosia lewtonii

Rhynchosia lewtonii, commonly known as the Lewton's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. Rhynchosia lewtonii is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
