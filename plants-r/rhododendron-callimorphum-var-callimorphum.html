Rhododendron callimorphum var. callimorphum

Rhododendron callimorphum var. callimorphum, commonly known as the Sierra Madre rhododendron, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in the ecosystems of its native habitat.
