Rhombifolium canescens

Rhombifolium canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf rhombifolium, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its lanceolate to rhombic leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually pink or purple in color, adding ornamental value to gardens.
