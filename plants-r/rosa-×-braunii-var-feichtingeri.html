Rosa × braunii var. feichtingeri

Rosa × braunii var. feichtingeri, commonly known as the Feichtinger's rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and serrated, providing a lush backdrop to the blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
