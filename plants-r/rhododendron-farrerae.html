Rhododendron farrerae

Rhododendron farrerae, commonly known as the Sierra Leone rhododendron, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of western Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom during the wet season, adding vibrant color to the forest understory.

Rhododendron farrerae is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native environment.
