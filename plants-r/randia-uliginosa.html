Randia uliginosa

Randia uliginosa, commonly known as swamp rosemary, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in swampy or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are opposite, simple, and covered in fine hairs. It has a woody stem with a distinctive aromatic scent, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine.

Randia uliginosa plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
