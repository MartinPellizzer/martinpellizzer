Romulea clusiana var. serotina

Romulea clusiana var. serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique flowering period, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in drought-tolerant plants.
