Rumex arifolius var. amplexicaulis

Rumex arifolius var. amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping dock, is a variant of the broadleaf dock native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of large, heart-shaped leaves that often wrap around the stem, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish flowers in the spring, which are followed by reddish-brown, triangular seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, streambanks, and in wet meadows.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its botanical significance lies in its role as a weed and its ecological adaptability.
