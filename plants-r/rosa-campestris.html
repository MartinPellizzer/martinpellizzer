Rosa campestris

Rosa campestris, commonly known as the field rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and is often found growing in open habitats such as meadows and roadsides. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and simple, alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals, and appear in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Rosa campestris is known for its fragrant blooms and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
