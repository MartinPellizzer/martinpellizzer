Rubus procerus f. obtusangulus

Rubus procerus f. obtusangulus, commonly known as the blunt-angled blackberry.

It is a variant of the common blackberry, characterized by its rounded leaf lobes and slightly smaller fruits. This plant is typically found in temperate regions, growing in woodland edges and disturbed soils. Its flowers are pink to white, and the fruit is a small, dark purple to black berry.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit and its role in supporting local wildlife.
