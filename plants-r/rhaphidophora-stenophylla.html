Rhaphidophora stenophylla

Rhaphidophora stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf staghorn fern, is a climbing tropical fern native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive, tree-like structure with a central stem and broad, leathery leaves that give it a staghorn appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its rhizomes form a dense network that anchors it to trees or rocks, allowing it to grow in vertical habitats.

Rhaphidophora stenophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
