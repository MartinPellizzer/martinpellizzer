Rosa arvensis tomentosa

Rosa arvensis tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy dog rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Rosa arvensis tomentosa is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall.
