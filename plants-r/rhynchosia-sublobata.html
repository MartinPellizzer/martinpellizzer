Rhynchosia sublobata

Rhynchosia sublobata, commonly known as the narrow-lobed cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant features compound leaves with three leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into legumes containing edible seeds. It is often grown as a forage crop and has been used traditionally for its nutritional value.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, enhancing soil fertility.
