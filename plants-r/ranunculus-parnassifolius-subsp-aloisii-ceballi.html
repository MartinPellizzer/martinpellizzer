Ranunculus parnassifolius subsp. aloisii-ceballi

Ranunculus parnassifolius subsp.

aloisii-ceballi, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight yellow petals and a prominent yellow center, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is also considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
