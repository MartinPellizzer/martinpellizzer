Rubus schleicheri microgene fulvus

Rubus schleicheri microgene fulvus, commonly known as the golden bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and a hairy texture. The fruit, a type of berry, is usually red or black when mature and is edible.

This plant is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
