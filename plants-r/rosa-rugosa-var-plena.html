Rosa rugosa var. plena

Rosa rugosa var. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered rugosa rose, is a compact, bushy shrub native to coastal regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and abundant, fragrant, double blooms that range from pink to red. The plant produces showy, rose-like flowers with multiple layers of petals, making it a popular choice for gardens. It is highly adaptable, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil, and is known for its hardiness and disease resistance.

Its foliage is dark green and provides attractive contrast to the vibrant flowers.
