Ryania speciosa var. speciosa

Ryania speciosa var. speciosa, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking exotic foliage.
