Rhynchosia elachistantha

Rhynchosia elachistantha, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, elliptical leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods. The plant is characterized by its slender stem and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields. It is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines, and for its potential in soil nitrogen fixation.

The species is also of interest in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in agroecosystems.
