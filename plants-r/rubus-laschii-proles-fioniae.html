Rubus laschii proles fioniae

Rubus laschii proles fioniae, commonly known as the Fionian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, dark red to black berries. The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine due to their high antioxidant content.
