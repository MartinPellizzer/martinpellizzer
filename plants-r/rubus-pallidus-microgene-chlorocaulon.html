Rubus pallidus microgene chlorocaulon

Rubus pallidus microgene chlorocaulon, commonly known as the pale bramble, is a shrub characterized by its slender, thorny stems and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant's leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its fruits are aggregate berries that ripen to a deep red. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
