Rubus problematicus

Rubus problematicus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, and the fruits, when ripe, turn deep purple to black.

The plant has a vigorous growth habit and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.
