Rosa micrantha var. hartbergensis

Rosa micrantha var. hartbergensis, commonly known as the Hartberg rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant has elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climate conditions.
