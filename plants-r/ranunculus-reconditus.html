Ranunculus reconditus

Ranunculus reconditus, commonly known as the hidden buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with 5 to 15 sepals that resemble petals.

Ranunculus reconditus is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in meadows and grasslands at high elevations.
