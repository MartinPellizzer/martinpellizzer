Ranunculus pawlowskii

Ranunculus pawlowskii, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces slender, hairy stems that bear the flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
