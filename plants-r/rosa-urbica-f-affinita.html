Rosa urbica f. affinita

Rosa urbica f. affinita, commonly known as the urban rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more rounded flowers compared to its parent species. The plant typically produces pink to white flowers with a subtle fragrance, blooming in early summer. It is adapted to thrive in urban environments, tolerating pollution and compacted soils.

This form is often found in gardens and along city streets, showcasing its resilience and ornamental value.
