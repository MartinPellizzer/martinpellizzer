Rubus andropogon

Rubus andropogon, commonly known as the Florida blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and a woody structure typical of many Rubus species.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.
