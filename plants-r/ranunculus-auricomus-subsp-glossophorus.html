Ranunculus auricomus subsp. glossophorus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

glossophorus, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with a distinctive greenish center, and its leaves are deeply lobed with a hairy texture.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and streambanks. It has a short stem with a basal rosette of leaves, and its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more pronounced flowers compared to other members of the species.
