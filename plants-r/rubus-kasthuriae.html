Rubus kasthuriae

Rubus kasthuriae, commonly known as the Kashmir raspberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
