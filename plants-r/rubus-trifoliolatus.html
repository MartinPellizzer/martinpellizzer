Rubus trifoliolatus

Rubus trifoliolatus, commonly known as the three-leaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, and its thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple or black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus trifoliolatus is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed sites.
