Rubus pseudoamericanus

Rubus pseudoamericanus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, streambanks, and disturbed areas.

The botanical features of Rubus pseudoamericanus make it an important component of native ecosystems and a common wild plant in the Pacific Northwest.
