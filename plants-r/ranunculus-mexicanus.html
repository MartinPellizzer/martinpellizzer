Ranunculus mexicanus

Ranunculus mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and Mexico.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces numerous small, bright flowers that bloom in spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
