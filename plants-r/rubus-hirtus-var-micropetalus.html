Rubus hirtus var. micropetalus

Rubus hirtus var. micropetalus, commonly known as the small-petaled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale green, and the fruit is a small, dark purple to black berry. This plant thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

It is valued for its edible fruit and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental qualities.
