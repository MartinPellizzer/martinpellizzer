Ranunculus pallidus

Ranunculus pallidus, commonly known as the pale buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often accompanied by a cluster of bright yellow stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

The species is noted for its early spring blooming period, making it a notable wildflower in its native habitats.
