Restio praeacutus

Restio praeacutus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with long, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white to pale blue flowers.

Restio praeacutus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
