Romulea uncinata

Romulea uncinata, commonly known as the hooked romulea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and showy, spurred flowers. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive hooked spur at the base of each petal. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Romulea uncinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
