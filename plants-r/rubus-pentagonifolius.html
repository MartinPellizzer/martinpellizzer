Rubus pentagonifolius

Rubus pentagonifolius, commonly known as the five-lobed blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with five distinct lobes, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular in culinary applications.
