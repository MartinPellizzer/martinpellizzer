Rubus heterochrous

Rubus heterochrous, commonly known as the variable blackberry, is a species of brambles native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated margins and has a creeping growth habit. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The fruit, while edible, is often small and tart, and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
