Rubus officinalis

Rubus officinalis, commonly known as blackberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of small drupelets. The plant is native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is widely cultivated for its edible berries. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit ripens in late summer.

The plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
