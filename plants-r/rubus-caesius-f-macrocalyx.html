Rubus caesius f. macrocalyx

Rubus caesius f. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its larger, more showy flowers compared to the typical species. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit, while edible, is generally smaller and less flavorful than the common blackberry.
