Rosa venusta

Rosa venusta, commonly known as the showy pink rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, single or semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with arching stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its botanical features include fragrant petals and a strong, sweet scent that attracts pollinators.

Rosa venusta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
