Rosa × costeana

Rosa × costeana, commonly known as the Rosa rugosa hybrid, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its robust growth, thorny stems, and large, fragrant flowers that range from white to deep pink. The plant produces showy, single or semi-double blooms in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, serrated, and often covered in fine hairs.

Rosa × costeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
