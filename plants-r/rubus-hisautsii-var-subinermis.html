Rubus hisautsii var. subinermis

Rubus hisautsii var. subinermis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
