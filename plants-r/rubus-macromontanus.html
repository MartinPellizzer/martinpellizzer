Rubus macromontanus

Rubus macromontanus, commonly known as the large mountain raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its woody stems, compound leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a bright red color. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers are pinkish-white with five petals, and it produces numerous stamens, typical of the Rubus genus.

The plant is an important component of its ecosystem, providing food for various wildlife species.
