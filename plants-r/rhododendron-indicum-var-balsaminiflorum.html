Rhododendron indicum var. balsaminiflorum

Rhododendron indicum var. balsaminiflorum, commonly known as the Indian balsam flower, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
