Rhododendron chasmanthoides

Rhododendron chasmanthoides, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in high-altitude forests and alpine meadows. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and dark green in color. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron chasmanthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
