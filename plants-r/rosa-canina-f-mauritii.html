Rosa canina f. mauritii

Rosa canina f. mauritii, commonly known as the Mauritius rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa genus. This form is native to the island of Mauritius and is adapted to the tropical climate of the region. It produces small, red, edible hips similar to the common dog rose.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in botanical studies.
