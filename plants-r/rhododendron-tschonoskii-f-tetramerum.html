Rhododendron tschonoskii f. tetramerum

Rhododendron tschonoskii f. tetramerum, commonly known as the four-leaf rhododendron, is a unique variant of the species Rhododendron tschonoskii.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and is typically found in the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink to reddish in color, blooming in late spring. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

This form is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
