Rodrigoa ximenae

Rodrigoa ximenae, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces dark purple, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and other wildlife. Rodrigoa ximenae thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in native gardens.
