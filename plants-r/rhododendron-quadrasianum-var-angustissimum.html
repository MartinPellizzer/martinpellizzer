Rhododendron quadrasianum var. angustissimum

Rhododendron quadrasianum var. angustissimum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a striking visual contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
