Rubus lusaticus var. hylogenes

Rubus lusaticus var. hylogenes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The leaves are typically narrow and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are white and clustered in corymbs. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

It is valued for its edible berries, which are used in local cuisines and traditional herbal remedies.
