Rosa timbaliana

Rosa timbaliana, commonly known as the Timbali rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height, featuring thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late winter to early spring. Rosa timbaliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
