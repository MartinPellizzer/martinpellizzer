Rosa minuscula

Rosa minuscula, commonly known as the dwarf rose, is a small shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, featuring dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pink to white flowers in early summer, which are often clustered in groups of five. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Rosa minuscula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
