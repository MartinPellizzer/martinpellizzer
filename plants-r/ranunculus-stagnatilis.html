Ranunculus stagnatilis

Ranunculus stagnatilis, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are typically submerged or floating on the water's surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes.

Ranunculus stagnatilis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
