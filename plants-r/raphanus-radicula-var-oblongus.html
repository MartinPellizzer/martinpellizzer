Raphanus radicula var. oblongus

Raphanus radicula var. oblongus, commonly known as the broad-leaved radish, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its robust, fleshy roots and large, lobed leaves that can reach several inches in length. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in racemes, and it produces round, globe-shaped fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible roots, which are crisp and mildly spicy in flavor.
