Rubus frigidus

Rubus frigidus, commonly known as the arctic bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America and Eurasia. The plant is characterized by its small, thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are hairy and serrated.

Rubus frigidus produces small, red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor.
