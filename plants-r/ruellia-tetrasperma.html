Ruellia tetrasperma

Ruellia tetrasperma, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Ruellia tetrasperma is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
