Restio sieberi

Restio sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences bearing tiny white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
