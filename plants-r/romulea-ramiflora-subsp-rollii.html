Romulea ramiflora subsp. rollii

Romulea ramiflora subsp.

rollii, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa. It produces striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, thriving in Mediterranean climates with mild, wet winters.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a popular choice for rock gardens and cultivated collections.
