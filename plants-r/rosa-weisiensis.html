Rosa weisiensis

Rosa weisiensis, commonly known as the Chinese tea rose, is a species native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it has a woody stem covered in thorns.

Rosa weisiensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
