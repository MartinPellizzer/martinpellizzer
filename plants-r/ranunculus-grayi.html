Ranunculus grayi

Ranunculus grayi, commonly known as Gray's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow or white. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, streambanks, and forest edges.

Ranunculus grayi is an herbaceous perennial that blooms in spring and early summer.
