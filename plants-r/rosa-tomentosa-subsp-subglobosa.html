Rosa tomentosa subsp. subglobosa

Rosa tomentosa subsp.

subglobosa, commonly known as the woolly rose or subglobous rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, pale pink to white flowers.

The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and is well adapted to dry, rocky soils. Its botanical features include pinnately compound leaves and fragrant blooms that appear in late spring.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
