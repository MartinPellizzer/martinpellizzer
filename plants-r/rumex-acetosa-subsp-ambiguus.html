Rumex acetosa subsp. ambiguus

Rumex acetosa subsp.

ambiguus, commonly known as sour dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a robust, upright stem with basal leaves that are heart-shaped and have a waxy surface.

The leaves are typically reddish or purplish on the underside, and the plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wet meadows.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Rumex genus, sharing similar ecological and morphological traits.
