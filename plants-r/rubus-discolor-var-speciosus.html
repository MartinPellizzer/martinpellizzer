Rubus discolor var. speciosus

Rubus discolor var. speciosus, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible berries that turn from red to dark purple as they mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
