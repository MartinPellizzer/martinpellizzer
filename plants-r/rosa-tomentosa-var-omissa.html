Rosa tomentosa var. omissa

Rosa tomentosa var. omissa, commonly known as the woolly rose or the Kashmir rose, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense, white to pale pink flowers and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thornless stems and fragrant blooms. Its leaves are ovate, serrated, and covered in fine, woolly hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
