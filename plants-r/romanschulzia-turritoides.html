Romanschulzia turritoides

Romanschulzia turritoides, commonly known as the tower bellflower, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers range in color from white to pale blue, adding a dramatic visual element to its habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
