Rubia grandis

Rubia grandis, commonly known as the greater cowpea or red clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer, followed by elongated pods containing small, round seeds. Rubia grandis is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

It also serves as a forage crop for livestock due to its nutritional value.
