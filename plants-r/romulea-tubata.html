Romulea tubata

Romulea tubata, commonly known as the tuberous romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its underground tubers and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from the base of the plant.

Romulea tubata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained, sandy soils.
