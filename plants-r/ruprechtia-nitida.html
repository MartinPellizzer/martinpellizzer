Ruprechtia nitida

Ruprechtia nitida, commonly known as the silver fir, is a species of coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elegant form and its silvery-blue foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are needle-like, arranged in spirals along the branches, and they are coated with a fine layer of wax, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Ruprechtia nitida is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and hardy nature.
