Rhododendron hirsutum f. albiflorum

Rhododendron hirsutum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered alpine rhododendron, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are predominantly white or pale pink, blooming in early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments with acidic soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
