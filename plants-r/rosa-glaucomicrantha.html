Rosa glaucomicrantha

Rosa glaucomicrantha, commonly known as the blue-crowned rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-gray foliage and showy pink to red flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China, it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
