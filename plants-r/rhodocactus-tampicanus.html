Rhodocactus tampicanus

Rhodocactus tampicanus, commonly known as the Tampic rock cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Rhodocactus tampicanus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
