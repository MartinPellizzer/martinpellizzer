Rheum inopinatum

Rheum inopinatum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and stout, reddish stems. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish flowers that bloom in late spring. Rheum inopinatum is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

Its rhubarb-like leaves are used to make teas and are believed to have anti-inflammatory and detoxifying effects.
