Rhododendron nudipes f. fugenense

Rhododendron nudipes f. fugenense, commonly known as the Fugen rhododendron, is a unique hybrid plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in mountainous regions. Its botanical features include small, elliptical leaves and flowers that range in color from pink to deep red.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as part of natural landscapes.
