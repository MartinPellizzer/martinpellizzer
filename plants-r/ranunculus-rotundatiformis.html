Ranunculus rotundatiformis

Ranunculus rotundatiformis, commonly known as the round-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are typically rounded or slightly lobed. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often surrounded by a ring of white or yellow sepals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
