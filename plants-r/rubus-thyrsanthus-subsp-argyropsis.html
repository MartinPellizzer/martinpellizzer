Rubus thyrsanthus subsp. argyropsis

Rubus thyrsanthus subsp.

argyropsis, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a silvery-gray pubescence.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, dark purple to black berry. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun to partial shade.
