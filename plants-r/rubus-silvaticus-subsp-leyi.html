Rubus silvaticus subsp. leyi

Rubus silvaticus subsp.

leyi, commonly known as the Sierra blackberry, is a shrub native to western North America, particularly in the mountainous regions of California and Oregon. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
