Rubus gratus

Rubus gratus, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart in flavor. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Rubus gratus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense thickets.
