Rubus gillotii

Rubus gillotii, commonly known as Gillot's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.

Botanically, it is classified within the Rosaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like roses and strawberries.
