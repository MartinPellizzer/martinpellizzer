Ranunculus uniflorus f. bolivianus

Ranunculus uniflorus f. bolivianus, commonly known as the Bolivian single-flowered buttercup, is a variant of the ranunculus genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive single, showy flower with bright yellow petals and a central greenish-yellow ovary. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and grasslands. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

This fern-like plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, delicate blooms.
