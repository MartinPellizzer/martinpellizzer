Rubus procerus var. hamulosus

Rubus procerus var. hamulosus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub native to North America, characterized by its thin, spiny stems and small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated margin, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisines and traditional herbal remedies.
