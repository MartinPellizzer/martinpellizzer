Rubus biflorus

Rubus biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible blackberries in late summer. Its berries are typically small and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and pies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other bramble species.
