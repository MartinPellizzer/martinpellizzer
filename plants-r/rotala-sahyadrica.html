Rotala sahyadrica

Rotala sahyadrica, commonly known as the Kerala waterwheel, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern India.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves and bright red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in shallow water and is often found in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are broad and rounded, and it is often used in aquatic gardens for its ornamental value.

Rotala sahyadrica plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
