Rubus merinoi

Rubus merinoi, commonly known as the Merino blackberry, is a species of brambles native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, dark red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The species is valued for both its ornamental features and edible fruits.
