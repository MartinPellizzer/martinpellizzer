Rhododendron wrayi var. minus

Rhododendron wrayi var. minus, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 1 meter, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a border plant. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, thriving in cool, humid climates.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant flower colors, which range from pink to deep red, adding visual interest to gardens.
