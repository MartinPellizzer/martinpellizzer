Rubus argenteus var. bipartitus

Rubus argenteus var. bipartitus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
