Racemobambos tessellata

Racemobambos tessellata, commonly known as the tessellated bamboo, is a unique plant belonging to the grass family.

It is characterized by its hollow, segmented stems that resemble bamboo, although it is not a true bamboo. The plant exhibits a distinctive tessellated pattern on its leaves, which gives it its scientific name. It is native to certain regions of South America and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental features, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and botanical collections.
