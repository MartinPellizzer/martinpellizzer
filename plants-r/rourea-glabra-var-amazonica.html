Rourea glabra var. amazonica

Rourea glabra var. amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian rourea, is a species native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant features smooth, glabrous bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in terminal inflorescences, and it produces fleshy, red or yellow berries.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
