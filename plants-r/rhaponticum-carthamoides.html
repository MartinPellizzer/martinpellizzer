Rhaponticum carthamoides

Rhaponticum carthamoides, commonly known as the Siberian cow-wheat, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as tundra and rocky slopes. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Rhaponticum carthamoides is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
