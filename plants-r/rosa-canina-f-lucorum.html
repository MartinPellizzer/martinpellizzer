Rosa canina f. lucorum

Rosa canina f. lucorum, commonly known as the Scottish rose or the dog rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and its distinctive, thorny stems. The plant produces red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance, providing food for wildlife.
