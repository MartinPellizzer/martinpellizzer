Rubus orgyalis

Rubus orgyalis, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It produces dark purple to black fruits that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale yellow flowers in early summer. It thrives in cooler climates and well-drained soil, often growing in forested areas.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and are also popular in local cuisines.
