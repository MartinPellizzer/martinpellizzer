Rosa tomentosa var. albiflora

Rosa tomentosa var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces oval, red-orange hips that are edible and contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
