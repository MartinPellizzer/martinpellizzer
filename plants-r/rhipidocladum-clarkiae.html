Rhipidocladum clarkiae

Rhipidocladum clarkiae, commonly known as the Clark's rock cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. The plant features a short, cylindrical stem that may branch at the base, and it produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. Its adaptations to drought include a thick, fleshy stem for water storage and a reduced leaf structure.

Rhipidocladum clarkiae is well-suited for xeriscaping and requires minimal water once established.
