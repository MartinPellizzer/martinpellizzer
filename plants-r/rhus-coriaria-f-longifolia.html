Rhus coriaria f. longifolia

Rhus coriaria f. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved buckthorn, is a variant of the common buckthorn, characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region, typically found in rocky soils and coastal areas. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are enclosed in a fleshy, yellowish calyx. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for ornamental purposes due to its attractive, persistent leaves.

The species is also valued for its historical use in dye production and as a source of medicinal compounds.
