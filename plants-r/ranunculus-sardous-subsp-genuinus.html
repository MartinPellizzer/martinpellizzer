Ranunculus sardous subsp. genuinus

Ranunculus sardous subsp.

genuinus, commonly known as the Sardinian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sardinia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces numerous small, hairy seeds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its alpine environment.
