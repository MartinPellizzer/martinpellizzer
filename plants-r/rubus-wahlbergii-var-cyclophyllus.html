Rubus wahlbergii var. cyclophyllus

Rubus wahlbergii var. cyclophyllus, commonly known as the cyclophyllus blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a aggregate berry that ripens to a deep purple or black color. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
