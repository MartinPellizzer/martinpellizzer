Rhynchostele beloglossa

Rhynchostele beloglossa, commonly known as the broad-leafed rhynchostele, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive rhynchostele, a central vascular bundle that is prominent in its stem. The plant typically has broad, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its flowers are small and clustered, forming an inflorescence known as a spadix, which is typical of the Araceae family.
