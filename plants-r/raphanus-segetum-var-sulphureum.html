Raphanus segetum var. sulphureum

Raphanus segetum var. sulphureum, commonly known as the yellow radish, is a cultivated variant of the wild radish species.

It is characterized by its yellow or orange root, which is typically smaller and more tender than the common red radish. The plant produces small, green, edible leaves that are often consumed in salads or cooked as a vegetable. It thrives in cool climates and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

This variety is valued for its mild flavor and nutritional content, making it a versatile addition to various cuisines.
