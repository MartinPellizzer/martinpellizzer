Rayenia

Rayenia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The leaves are typically bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments. Rayenia species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
