Robinia × bella-rosea

Robinia × bella-rosea, commonly known as the pink robinia or pink false acacia, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a serrated margin. The plant produces showy, pendulous clusters of pink to reddish flowers in the spring, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fast growth rate and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
