Rosa gallica var. hybrida

Rosa gallica var. hybrida, commonly known as the French rose, is a hybrid species of the Rosa gallica plant.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double to double pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both landscape design and cut flower arrangements.
