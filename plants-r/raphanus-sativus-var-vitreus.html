Raphanus sativus var. vitreus

Raphanus sativus var. vitreus, commonly known as the watercress, is a leafy green vegetable that belongs to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, edible leaves that are rich in nutrients such as vitamins A, C, and K. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in shallow water or wet soil. Its rapid growth and high water content contribute to its crisp texture and distinct peppery flavor.

Watercress is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its health benefits.
