Rhynchosia congensis subsp. congensis

Rhynchosia congensis subsp.

congensis, commonly known as the African butterbean, is a climbing legume native to sub-Saharan Africa. It features trifoliate leaves with elongated leaflets and produces distinctive yellow-orange flowers.

The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It is notable for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in local cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in ecological restoration projects.
