Rosa × escolapiorum

Rosa × escolapiorum, commonly known as the Esculapius rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and the production of large, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn, and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its botanical features include compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and thorny stems typical of many rose species.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
