Rhodamnia whiteana

Rhodamnia whiteana, commonly known as the white-flowered rhodamnia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually arranged in clusters and bloom during the spring and summer seasons.

Rhodamnia whiteana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual beauty.
