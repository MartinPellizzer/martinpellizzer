Rubus tereticaulis subsp. saxetanus

Rubus tereticaulis subsp.

saxetanus, commonly known as the saxetan blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
