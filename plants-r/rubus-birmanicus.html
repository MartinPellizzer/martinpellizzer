Rubus birmanicus

Rubus birmanicus, commonly known as the Burmese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple or black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry and blackberry species.
