Rubus affinis var. archetypus

Rubus affinis var. archetypus, commonly known as the archetypal raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry species.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, bright red drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic diversity and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
