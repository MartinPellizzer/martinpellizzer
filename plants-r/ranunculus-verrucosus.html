Ranunculus verrucosus

Ranunculus verrucosus, commonly known as the warty buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often adorned with a distinctive warty texture on the petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and has a fibrous root system. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ranunculus verrucosus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
