Rosa mollis subf. illudens

Rosa mollis subf.

illudens, commonly known as the soft rose or creeping rose, is a shrub-like species native to parts of South America. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with thornless stems that often root at the nodes.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
