Rottboellia bovonei

Rottboellia bovonei, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often reddish in color. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas. It has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Rottboellia bovonei is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
