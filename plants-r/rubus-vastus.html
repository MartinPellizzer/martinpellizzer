Rubus vastus

Rubus vastus, commonly known as the giant blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. This plant is characterized by its large, thorny stems and clusters of dark purple to black berries. The leaves are compound, typically with five to seven leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.
