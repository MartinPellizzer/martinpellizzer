Rubus koehleri microgene subpygmaeopsis

Rubus koehleri microgene subpygmaeopsis, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a small shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its tiny, clustered flowers and edible, red fruits. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters, where it thrives in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and flavor.
