Rosa sericea var. pteracantha

Rosa sericea var. pteracantha, commonly known as the silverleaf rock rose, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental appeal and adaptability to dry conditions.
