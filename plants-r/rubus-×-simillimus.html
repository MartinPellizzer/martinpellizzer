Rubus × simillimus

Rubus × simillimus, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its fruit is widely cultivated for culinary use and is valued for its flavor and nutritional content.
