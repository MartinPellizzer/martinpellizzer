Ranunculus gripsholmiensis

Ranunculus gripsholmiensis, commonly known as the Swedish buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Sweden.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream sides. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring.

Ranunculus gripsholmiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
