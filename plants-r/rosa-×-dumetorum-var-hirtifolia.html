Rosa × dumetorum var. hirtifolia

Rosa × dumetorum var. hirtifolia, commonly known as the roughwood rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy underside.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
