Rubus trachydermis

Rubus trachydermis, commonly known as the rough-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and is valued for its edible berries and ecological importance.
