Ranunculus compar

Ranunculus compar, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, saucer-shaped flowers with numerous petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and wetlands. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a rough texture, while its stems are usually hairy.

Ranunculus compar is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
