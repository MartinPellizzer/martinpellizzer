Rubus purpureus

Rubus purpureus, commonly known as the purple raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth. The plant produces clusters of small, edible berries that are typically red or purple in color. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the stems are armed with stiff, curved prickles.

Rubus purpureus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
