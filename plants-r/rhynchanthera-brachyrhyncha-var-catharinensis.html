Rhynchanthera brachyrhyncha var. catharinensis

Rhynchanthera brachyrhyncha var. catharinensis, commonly known as the Catharina orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, with a distinctive lip that is often darker.

Due to habitat loss, this variety is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
