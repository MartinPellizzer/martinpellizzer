Rubus scaber var. curtidens

Rubus scaber var. curtidens, commonly known as the rough bramble.

It is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The species is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and other disturbed habitats.
