Rotala mexicana subsp. hierniana

Rotala mexicana subsp.

hierniana, commonly known as Mexican waterwheel, is a semi-aquatic plant native to Central and South America. It features finely divided, feather-like leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge, especially when exposed to bright light.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to aquatic environments. It thrives in shallow water and is often used in pond and aquarium settings.

Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for water gardeners seeking to control algae growth.
