Rapinia triphylla

Rapinia triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are usually blue or violet in color, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.

Rapinia triphylla is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and resilience in challenging growing conditions.
