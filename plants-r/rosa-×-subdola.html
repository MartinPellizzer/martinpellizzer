Rosa × subdola

Rosa × subdola, commonly known as the evergreen rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that remains throughout the year, making it distinct from most other rose species. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is native to the western Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, Rosa × subdola is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
