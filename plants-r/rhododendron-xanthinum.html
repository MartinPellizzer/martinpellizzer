Rhododendron xanthinum

Rhododendron xanthinum, commonly known as the yellow rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant yellow blooms.

Rhododendron xanthinum prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full sun exposure.
