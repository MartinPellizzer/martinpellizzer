Regalia robusta

Regalia robusta, commonly known as the robust regalia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, often displaying vibrant green or variegated patterns. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Regalia robusta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic appearance and ability to add visual interest to gardens and indoor spaces.
