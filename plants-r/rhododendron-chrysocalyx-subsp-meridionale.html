Rhododendron chrysocalyx subsp. meridionale

Rhododendron chrysocalyx subsp.

meridionale, commonly known as the Alpine golden rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool, moist climates. It is often found at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific horticultural conditions.
