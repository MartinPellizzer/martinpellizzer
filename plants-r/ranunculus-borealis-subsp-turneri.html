Ranunculus borealis subsp. turneri

Ranunculus borealis subsp.

turneri, commonly known as the Arctic buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Arctic and subarctic regions. It features small, bright yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant has hairy, basal leaves that are divided into lobes, and it grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in cold climates and is adapted to short growing seasons.

Its presence is often an indicator of undisturbed, high-altitude or polar ecosystems.
