Rhodosciadium glaucum

Rhodosciadium glaucum, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glories.
