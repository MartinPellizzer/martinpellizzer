Rubus × candens

Rubus × candens, commonly known as the evergreen dewberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and produces small, edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have serrated edges.

The flowers are white or pale pink, and the plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.
