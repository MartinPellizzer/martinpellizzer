Rosa collincola plena

Rosa collincola plena, commonly known as the full-flowered hill rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, thorny stems and is characterized by its double blooms, which are composed of numerous petals arranged in a full, rounded form. The flowers are typically pink or red, with a strong, sweet fragrance, and they appear in late spring to early summer. This species is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
