Rubus philippinensis

Rubus philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Philippines.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded areas of mountainous regions. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are red when ripe, similar to raspberries. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
