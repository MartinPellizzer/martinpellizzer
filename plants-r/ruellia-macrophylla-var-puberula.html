Ruellia macrophylla var. puberula

Ruellia macrophylla var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy-leaved ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, pubescent leaves that give it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Ruellia macrophylla var. puberula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.
