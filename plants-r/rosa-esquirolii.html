Rosa esquirolii

Rosa esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Italy.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa esquirolii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.
