Roemeria hybrida var. velutina

Roemeria hybrida var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet roemeria, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in specialized gardens.
