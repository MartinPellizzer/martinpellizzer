Rhagodia nutans var. fallacina

Rhagodia nutans var. fallacina, commonly known as the slender sandbur, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, hairy fruits that aid in seed dispersal. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for small fauna and contributing to soil stabilization in its native range.
