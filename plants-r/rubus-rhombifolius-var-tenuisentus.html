Rubus rhombifolius var. tenuisentus

Rubus rhombifolius var. tenuisentus, commonly known as the slenderleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features small, elliptical leaves with a hairy texture and a distinctive, fragrant aroma. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
