Rosa arvensis var. mirabilis

Rosa arvensis var. mirabilis, commonly known as the sweet willie or wild rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces bright red hips that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
