Rumex giganteus f. annectens

Rumex giganteus f. annectens, commonly known as the giant dock, is a variant of the broadleaf dock plant.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its large, lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish flowers that are typically arranged in a spike-like structure. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

The leaves are commonly used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some cultures.
