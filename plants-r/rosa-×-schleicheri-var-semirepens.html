Rosa × schleicheri var. semirepens

Rosa × schleicheri var. semirepens, commonly known as the creeping shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit, making it suitable for ground cover. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, with thorny stems that provide structural support.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
