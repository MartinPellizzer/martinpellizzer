Rauvolfia ligustrina

Rauvolfia ligustrina, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
