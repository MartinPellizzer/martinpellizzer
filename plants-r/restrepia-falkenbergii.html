Restrepia falkenbergii

Restrepia falkenbergii, commonly known as Falkenberg's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
