Rubus subalbicans

Rubus subalbicans, commonly known as the white-flowered bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in woodland and shrubland habitats. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and small, white to pale pink flowers. Its fruits, known as berries, are edible and have a tart flavor.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.
