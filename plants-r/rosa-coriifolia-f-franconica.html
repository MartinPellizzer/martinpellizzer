Rosa coriifolia f. franconica

Rosa coriifolia f. franconica, commonly known as the Franconian shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to Central Europe.

It features thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are pinnate, with 5 to 9 leaflets that are oval and slightly serrated. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its hardiness and winter interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
