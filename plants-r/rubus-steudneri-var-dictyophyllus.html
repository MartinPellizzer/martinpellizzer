Rubus steudneri var. dictyophyllus

Rubus steudneri var. dictyophyllus, commonly known as the steudner's bramble, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in rocky, dry habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and a subject of interest for botanists and hikers alike.
