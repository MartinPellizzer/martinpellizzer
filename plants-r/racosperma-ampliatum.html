Racosperma ampliatum

Racosperma ampliatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Racosperma ampliatum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
