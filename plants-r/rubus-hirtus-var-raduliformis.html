Rubus hirtus var. raduliformis

Rubus hirtus var. raduliformis, commonly known as the hairy bramble or redstem bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small white to pink flowers. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed areas.

This variant is notable for its dense growth habit and adaptability to different environmental conditions.
