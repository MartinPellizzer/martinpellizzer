Retispatha dumetosa

Retispatha dumetosa, commonly known as the rock nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It features a sprawling growth habit with stout, hairy stems that often root at the nodes. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough, textured surface and prominent veins, and are typically arranged in opposite pairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clustered inflorescences, which are often hidden among the foliage.

The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
