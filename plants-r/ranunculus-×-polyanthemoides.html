Ranunculus × polyanthemoides

Ranunculus × polyanthemoides, commonly known as the garden buttercup, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, double or semi-double flowers with bright yellow centers and white or pink petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 12–18 inches and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Ranunculus × polyanthemoides is relatively hardy and can be propagated through division or seed.
