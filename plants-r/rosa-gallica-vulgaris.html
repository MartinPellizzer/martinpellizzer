Rosa gallica vulgaris

Rosa gallica vulgaris, commonly known as the French rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. Rosa gallica vulgaris is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
