Rubus thyrsoideus subsp. neomalacus

Rubus thyrsoideus subsp.

neomalacus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This subspecies is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated in gardens and commercial farms.
