Rubus phaeocarpus

Rubus phaeocarpus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

The botanical features of Rubus phaeocarpus make it an important plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
