Rhododendron macrocarpos

Rhododendron macrocarpos, commonly known as the large-fruited rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains numerous small, black seeds.

Rhododendron macrocarpos thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, temperate climates.
