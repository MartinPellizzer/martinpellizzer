Rubus × entomodontoides

Rubus × entomodontoides, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (cloudberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in temperate regions, often found in boreal and subarctic climates. Its compound leaves are serrated, and it produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential for cultivation in suitable environments.
