Rubus preptanthus var. preptanthus

Rubus preptanthus var. preptanthus, commonly known as the preptanthus blackberry, is a species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to specific regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and have a tart flavor. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and shrublands.

Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and a complex inflorescence structure typical of the Rubus genus.
