Rinorea cordata

Rinorea cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf or velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically velvety to the touch and have a deep green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits. Rinorea cordata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.
