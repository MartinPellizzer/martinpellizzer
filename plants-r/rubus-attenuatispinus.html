Rubus attenuatispinus

Rubus attenuatispinus, commonly known as the slender-spined blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.
