Rhapis cochinchinensis

Rhapis cochinchinensis, commonly known as the golden bamboo or Chinese bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, China, and the Philippines. This plant is characterized by its slender, gracefully arching stems and clusters of dark green, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by bright orange or red fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for shaded or partially shaded environments.
