Rhododendron brachycarpum var. lutescens

Rhododendron brachycarpum var. lutescens, commonly known as the golden-leaved rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features striking, bright yellow to orange leaves that contrast beautifully with its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring, producing clusters of pink to red flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in cool, moist environments with partial shade and well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and conservation efforts.
