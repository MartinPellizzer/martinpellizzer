Rubus fruticosus subsp. sprengelii

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

sprengelii, commonly known as Sprengel's bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.

Sprengel's bramble is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
