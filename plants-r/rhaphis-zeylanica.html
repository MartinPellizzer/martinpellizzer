Rhaphis zeylanica

Rhaphis zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon date palm, is a flowering plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, including Sri Lanka, India, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its inflorescences produce small, fragrant flowers that eventually develop into edible fruits resembling dates.

Rhaphis zeylanica is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its cultural and economic significance in the regions where it grows.
