Ranunculus hydrocharis var. stagnatilis

Ranunculus hydrocharis var. stagnatilis, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, floating leaves and yellow flowers.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The leaves are divided into fine, thread-like segments, and the flowers are borne on slender stalks above the water surface. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic compounds.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
