Rosa glauca var. friesii

Rosa glauca var. friesii, commonly known as the Fries' bluebelle rose, is a variant of the bluebelle rose native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate floral display and hardy nature.
