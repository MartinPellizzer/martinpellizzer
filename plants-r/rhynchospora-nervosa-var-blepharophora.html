Rhynchospora nervosa var. blepharophora

Rhynchospora nervosa var. blepharophora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves with a distinct, parallel venation pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This species plays an important role in maintaining wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
