Ranunculus canutii

Ranunculus canutii, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with numerous petals and a hairy, upright stem. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a rough texture and a distinctive scent.

Ranunculus canutii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in pollinator habitats in its native range.
