Rubus serpens var. melanadenes

Rubus serpens var. melanadenes, commonly known as the black-tipped raspberry.

This variant is a species of bramble belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red fruits. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

Its fruit is edible but has a tart flavor, making it a subject of interest for both horticulture and ecological studies.
