Racosperma ampliceps

Racosperma ampliceps, commonly known as the broad-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with thorn-like branches, and its leaves are alternate and pinnately arranged.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.
