Rosa afzeliana var. umbellata

Rosa afzeliana var. umbellata, commonly known as the umbellate shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to regions of Asia, including China and Nepal.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of edible hips.
