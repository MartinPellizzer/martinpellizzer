Rhamnus smithii subsp. typica

Rhamnus smithii subsp.

typica, commonly known as the Smith's buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and dark, glossy berries.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its characteristic berries. It is a member of the Rhamnaceae family and is known for its adaptability to arid environments.

The subspecies typica is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits.
