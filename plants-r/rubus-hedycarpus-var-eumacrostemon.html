Rubus hedycarpus var. eumacrostemon

Rubus hedycarpus var. eumacrostemon, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring. The plant's fruit is a aggregate of tiny drupes, typically turning from red to dark purple as it matures. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, though its thorny stems can make harvesting challenging.
