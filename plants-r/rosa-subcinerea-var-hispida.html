Rosa subcinerea var. hispida

Rosa subcinerea var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid shrub rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. Its hirsute (hairy) texture is a distinguishing feature, giving it the common name "hispida.".

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
