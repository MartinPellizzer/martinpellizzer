Rosa micrantha f. gracilis

Rosa micrantha f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed dog rose, is a compact shrub native to China.

It features slender, arching stems covered in soft, grayish-green pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with ovate leaflets.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate appearance and fragrance.
