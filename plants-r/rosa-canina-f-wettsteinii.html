Rosa canina f. wettsteinii

Rosa canina f. wettsteinii, commonly known as the Wettstein's dog rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its pink to red flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa canina species. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges. It produces hips, which are the fruit of the plant, containing numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
