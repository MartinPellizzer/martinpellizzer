Ruellia forbesii

Ruellia forbesii, commonly known as the Mexican bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue to purple tubular flowers, which bloom in the spring and early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Ruellia forbesii thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and ease of cultivation.
