Rubus conjungens

Rubus conjungens, commonly known as the clustered blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, sharing features with other Rubus species like raspberries and blackberries.
