Rubus nitidus var. integribasis

Rubus nitidus var. integribasis, commonly known as the smooth blackberry.

It is a perennial shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Botanically, it is notable for its hairy young growth and persistent, waxy leaf surfaces.
