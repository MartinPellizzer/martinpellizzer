Rubus × longiracemosus

Rubus × longiracemosus, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems that often trail along the ground. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular in culinary applications.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its complex inflorescence and persistent, leathery sepals that remain attached to the fruit.
