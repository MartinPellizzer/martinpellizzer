Rosa stylosa subvar. massilvanensis

Rosa stylosa subvar. massilvanensis, commonly known as the Massilvanese shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody structure and the ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
