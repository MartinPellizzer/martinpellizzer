Racosperma cyperophyllum var. omearanum

Racosperma cyperophyllum var. omearanum, commonly known as the Omeara sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally wet habitats, such as swampy areas or along watercourses. It produces small, clustered inflorescences that are usually green or brown, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
