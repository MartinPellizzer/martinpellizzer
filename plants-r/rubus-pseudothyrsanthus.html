Rubus pseudothyrsanthus

Rubus pseudothyrsanthus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by dark purple, edible berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
