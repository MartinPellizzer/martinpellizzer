Rhododendron kansuense

Rhododendron kansuense, commonly known as the Kansu rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, moist climates at high altitudes. The plant features dense, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers in the spring. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
