Ranunculus indecorus

Ranunculus indecorus, commonly known as the lesser buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of numerous small petals arranged in a cup-like structure.

It is notable for its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates and is often found in moist meadows and along riverbanks.
