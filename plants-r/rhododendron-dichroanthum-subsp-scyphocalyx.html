Rhododendron dichroanthum subsp. scyphocalyx

Rhododendron dichroanthum subsp.

scyphocalyx, commonly known as the Himalayan flame azalea, is a rare and striking plant native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange flowers, which bloom in dense clusters during the spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its showy blossoms.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
