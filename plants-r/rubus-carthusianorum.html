Rubus carthusianorum

Rubus carthusianorum, commonly known as the Carthusian raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, red, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland areas, hedgerows, and along stream banks. Its flowers are white or pale pink, with five petals, and appear in early summer.

The fruit ripens in late summer and is often used in jams and desserts.
