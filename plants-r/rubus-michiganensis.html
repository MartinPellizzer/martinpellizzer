Rubus michiganensis

Rubus michiganensis, commonly known as Michigan blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other bramble species.
