Rhus aucheri

Rhus aucheri, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in upright clusters, followed by red, fuzzy berries that are a food source for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.

Rhus aucheri is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
