Rubus selectus

Rubus selectus, commonly known as the selected raspberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically red or yellow when ripe. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental and culinary value, it is cultivated in gardens and small-scale farms.
