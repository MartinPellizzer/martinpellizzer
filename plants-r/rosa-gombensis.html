Rosa gombensis

Rosa gombensis, commonly known as the Gombessa rose, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in thorny projections.

Rosa gombensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
