Rhododendron muliense

Rhododendron muliense, commonly known as the Muli rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist climates with high humidity. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems and a compact form.

Rhododendron muliense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized landscapes.
