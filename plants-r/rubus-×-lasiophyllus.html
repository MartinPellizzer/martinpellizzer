Rubus × lasiophyllus

Rubus × lasiophyllus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
