Rosa karstiana

Rosa karstiana, commonly known as the Karst rose, is a species of shrub native to the karst landscapes of southern China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa karstiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
