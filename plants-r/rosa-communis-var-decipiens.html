Rosa communis var. decipiens

Rosa communis var. decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive rose, is a variety of the common rose characterized by its unique floral structure.

This plant features large, showy flowers that resemble those of other rose species, often leading to confusion in identification. Botanically, it is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves with serrated margins. The flowers typically bloom in shades of pink and white, with a strong, sweet fragrance.

It is native to regions of Europe and is cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
