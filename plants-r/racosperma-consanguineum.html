Racosperma consanguineum

Racosperma consanguineum, commonly known as the red-flowered wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
