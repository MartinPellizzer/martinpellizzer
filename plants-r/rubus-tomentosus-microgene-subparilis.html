Rubus tomentosus microgene subparilis

Rubus tomentosus microgene subparilis, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems covered in a soft, white pubescence. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are hairy on both sides.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.
