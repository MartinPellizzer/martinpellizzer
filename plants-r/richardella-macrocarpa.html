Richardella macrocarpa

Richardella macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, showcasing vibrant colors such as yellow, orange, and red. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat, and can also be found in rocky areas. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, helping it survive in its often-dry environment.

Its unique morphology and striking appearance make it a popular choice for both horticultural displays and botanical study.
