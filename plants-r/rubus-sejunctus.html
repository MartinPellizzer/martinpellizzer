Rubus sejunctus

Rubus sejunctus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the production of small, dark berries. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

Rubus sejunctus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
