Rhododendron reticulatum var. shimanum

Rhododendron reticulatum var. shimanum, commonly known as the Shimanum rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a leathery texture and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has a broad, rounded form. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant spring blooms and attractive foliage throughout the growing season.
