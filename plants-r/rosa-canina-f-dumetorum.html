Rosa canina f. dumetorum

Rosa canina f. dumetorum, commonly known as the shrub rose or dog rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose species.

It is characterized by its low-growing, thorny shrub form and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by distinctive red, hairy fruits called hips. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Rosa canina f. dumetorum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
