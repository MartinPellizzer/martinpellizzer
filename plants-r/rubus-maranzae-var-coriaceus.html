Rubus maranzae var. coriaceus

Rubus maranzae var. coriaceus, commonly known as the thick-leaved blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry species characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and dark purple to black berries. The plant is typically found in temperate regions with well-drained soil and partial shade. Its foliage has a distinctive waxy texture, and the fruit is often used in culinary applications.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
