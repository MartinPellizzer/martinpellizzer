Rosularia bonorum-hominum

Rosularia bonorum-hominum, commonly known as the "blessed people's rose," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a compact, cushion-like growth habit with fleshy, blue-green leaves arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun with well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and cultural significance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
