Rubus idaeus var. anomalus

Rubus idaeus var. anomalus, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though some varieties may exhibit different colors. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variant is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value in gardens.
