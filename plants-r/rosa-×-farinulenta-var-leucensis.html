Rosa × farinulenta var. leucensis

Rosa × farinulenta var. leucensis, commonly known as the white-flowered shrub rose, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and produces clusters of small, white flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in a fine, white down.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
