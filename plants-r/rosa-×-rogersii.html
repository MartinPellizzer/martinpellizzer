Rosa × rogersii

Rosa × rogersii, commonly known as the Rogers' rose, is a hybrid rose species resulting from the cross between Rosa moyesii and Rosa bracteata.

It is native to China and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double to full flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

Rosa × rogersii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
