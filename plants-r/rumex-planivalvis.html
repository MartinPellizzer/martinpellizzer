Rumex planivalvis

Rumex planivalvis, commonly known as the flat-valved sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in loose inflorescences, typically appearing in early spring.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in moist, shady environments, making it a common sight in woodland areas.
