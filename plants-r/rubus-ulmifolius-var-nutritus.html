Rubus ulmifolius var. nutritus

Rubus ulmifolius var. nutritus, commonly known as the edible blackberry bramble, is a variant of the common blackberry shrub.

It is characterized by its dark green, trifoliate leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color, making it a popular choice for foraging and cultivation. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and valuable for culinary use.
