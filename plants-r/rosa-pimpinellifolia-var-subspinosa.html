Rosa pimpinellifolia var. subspinosa

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. subspinosa, commonly known as the prickly rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has woody stems covered in short, stiff hairs and small thorns, giving it a rugged appearance. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it adapts well to harsh environmental conditions.
