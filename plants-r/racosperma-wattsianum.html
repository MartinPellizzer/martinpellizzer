Racosperma wattsianum

Racosperma wattsianum, commonly known as the Watt's acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, fluffy white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The plant is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
