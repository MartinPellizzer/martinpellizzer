Renealmia matogrossensis

Renealmia matogrossensis, commonly known as the Matogrossense palm, is a flowering plant species native to the Brazilian Cerrado region.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of many palm species. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as thatching material.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
