Rubus boormanii

Rubus boormanii, commonly known as the Boorman's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring. Its fruits are typically small and somewhat tart, often used in local foraging or culinary applications.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
