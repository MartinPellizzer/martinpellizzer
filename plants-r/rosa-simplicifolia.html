Rosa simplicifolia

Rosa simplicifolia, commonly known as the simpleleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
