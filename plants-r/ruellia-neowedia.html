Ruellia neowedia

Ruellia neowedia, commonly known as Mexican bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue or violet tubular flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Ruellia neowedia is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and ability to attract pollinators.
