Rosa eglanteria var. ladae

Rosa eglanteria var. ladae, commonly known as the Lada rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by red, hairy fruits. It is adapted to rocky and dry environments, showing resilience to harsh conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and hardy member of the rose family.
