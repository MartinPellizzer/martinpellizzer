Rosa rubrifolia var. redoutea

Rosa rubrifolia var. redoutea, commonly known as the Redoute's rose, is a compact shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It features dark green, glossy foliage that turns a striking red or bronze in the fall, adding ornamental value throughout the seasons. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its hardiness and unique foliage make it a desirable addition to botanical gardens and specialized collections.
