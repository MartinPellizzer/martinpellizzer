Rebutia friedrichiana

Rebutia friedrichiana, commonly known as the Friederich's moon cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is a small, low-growing cactus with a thick, segmented stem that often displays a variety of colors, including green, red, and yellow. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it drought-tolerant.

Rebutia friedrichiana is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its compact size and attractive appearance.
