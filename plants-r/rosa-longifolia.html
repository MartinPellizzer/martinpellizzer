Rosa longifolia

Rosa longifolia, commonly known as the long-stemmed rose, is a species of shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and for its historical use in herbal medicine.

Rosa longifolia is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
