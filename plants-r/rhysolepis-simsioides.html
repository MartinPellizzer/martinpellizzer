Rhysolepis simsioides

Rhysolepis simsioides, commonly known as the mountain mahogany, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant has a deep root system that helps it thrive in arid and rocky environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture, while its bark is typically fissured and flaky.

Rhysolepis simsioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
