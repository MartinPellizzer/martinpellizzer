Rubus fruticosus var. sulcatus

Rubus fruticosus var. sulcatus, commonly known as the serrated blackberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The berries are edible, though typically tart, and have been used in traditional culinary and medicinal practices.
