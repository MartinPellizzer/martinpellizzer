Ruta intermedia

Ruta intermedia, commonly known as common rue, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features aromatic, pinnately compound leaves with a strong, bitter scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by oval, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The plant contains compounds like rutin and volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal and historical uses.
