Ranunculus micranthus var. californicus

Ranunculus micranthus var. californicus, commonly known as the California buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and a short, hairy stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological importance in native habitats.
