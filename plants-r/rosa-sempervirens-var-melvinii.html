Rosa sempervirens var. melvinii

Rosa sempervirens var. melvinii, commonly known as the Melvin's evergreen rose, is a shrub-like species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery foliage that remains evergreen throughout the year. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in colder climates.
