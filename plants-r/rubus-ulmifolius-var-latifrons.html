Rubus ulmifolius var. latifrons

Rubus ulmifolius var. latifrons, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry species.

It is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia, characterized by its large, dark green leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This species is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
