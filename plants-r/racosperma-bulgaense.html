Racosperma bulgaense

Racosperma bulgaense, commonly known as the Bulgaense Acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and it produces elongated, flat pods that contain seeds.

Racosperma bulgaense is often found in coastal and inland regions of Western Australia.
