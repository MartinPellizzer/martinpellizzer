Rubus × delphinensis

Rubus × delphinensis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub that belongs to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of western North America, particularly in California and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, leathery leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible blackberries that are often used in culinary applications.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
