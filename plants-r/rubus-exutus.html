Rubus exutus

Rubus exutus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like roses and apples.
