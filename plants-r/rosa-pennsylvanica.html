Rosa pennsylvanica

Rosa pennsylvanica, commonly known as the Carolina rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, arching stems and compound leaves with five to seven oval leaflets. Its flowers are typically borne singly or in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Rosa pennsylvanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in native plant gardens.
