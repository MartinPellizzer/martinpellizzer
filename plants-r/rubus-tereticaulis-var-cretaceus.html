Rubus tereticaulis var. cretaceus

Rubus tereticaulis var. cretaceus, commonly known as the Cretaceous raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
