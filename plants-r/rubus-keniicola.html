Rubus keniicola

Rubus keniicola, commonly known as the Kenyan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs and vines known for their edible berries. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant has thorny stems typical of many Rubus species.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
