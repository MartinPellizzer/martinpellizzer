Rabdosia dichromophylla

Rabdosia dichromophylla, commonly known as the two-colored rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically arranged in whorled clusters and bloom in shades of purple or pink.

Rabdosia dichromophylla is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
