Rubus insericatus var. oreadiformis

Rubus insericatus var. oreadiformis, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect or semi-erect growth habit and is typically found in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or yellow in color. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated margins, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to high-altitude environments and its role in supporting local wildlife.
