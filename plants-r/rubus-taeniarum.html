Rubus taeniarum

Rubus taeniarum, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Rubus taeniarum plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
