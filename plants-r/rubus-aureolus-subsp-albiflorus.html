Rubus aureolus subsp. albiflorus

Rubus aureolus subsp.

albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are white and typically appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The fruit, a small, tart berry, develops from the flowers and is often used in jams and preserves.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
