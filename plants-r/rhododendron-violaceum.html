Rhododendron violaceum

Rhododendron violaceum, commonly known as the purple rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a slight pubescence.

Rhododendron violaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
