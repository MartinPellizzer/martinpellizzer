Rubus tomentosus var. coloratus

Rubus tomentosus var. coloratus, commonly known as the showy blackberry.

This variant is distinguished by its vibrant red or pink bracts that surround the flower, adding ornamental value. The plant produces dark purple to black berries, which are typically smaller than those of common blackberries. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates, often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
