Rubus megacarpus

Rubus megacarpus, commonly known as the giant raspberry.

It is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and large, aggregate fruits. The plant produces berries that are typically red or orange when mature, containing numerous small drupelets. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The botanical features of Rubus megacarpus make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
