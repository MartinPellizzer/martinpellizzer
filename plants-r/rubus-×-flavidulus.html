Rubus × flavidulus

Rubus × flavidulus, commonly known as the yellow-tailed blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from yellow to red, depending on maturity. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
