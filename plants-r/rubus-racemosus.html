Rubus racemosus

Rubus racemosus, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, which ripens in late summer, is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
