Rubus hirtus subsp. plusiacanthus

Rubus hirtus subsp.

plusiacanthus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
