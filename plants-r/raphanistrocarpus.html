Raphanistrocarpus

Raphanistrocarpus, commonly known as the rockcress or a type of mustard plant, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Raphanistrocarpus is also known for its peppery taste, similar to other members of the mustard family.
