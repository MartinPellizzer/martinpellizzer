Rytidosperma indutum

Rytidosperma indutum, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its finely textured, silver-blue foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, pale green spikelets that are often overlooked due to their subtle appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Rytidosperma indutum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
