Rubus frangipani

Rubus frangipani, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces red, hairy fruits that are edible but often consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
