Ramium blumei

Ramium blumei, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a smooth texture.

The showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
