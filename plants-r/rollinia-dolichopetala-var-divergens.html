Rollinia dolichopetala var. divergens

Rollinia dolichopetala var. divergens, commonly known as the long-petioled rollinia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length, with a waxy surface and a heart-shaped base. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the wet season, followed by fruiting in late summer. Its fruit, a fleshy, spiny berry, is edible and often consumed by local communities.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
