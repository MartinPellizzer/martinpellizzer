Rubus albiflorus var. armatifactus

Rubus albiflorus var. armatifactus, commonly known as the white-flowered blackberry variant, is a species of bramble native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible berries in the summer. Its fruit is typically small, tart, and has a firm texture.

This variant is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its use in local culinary traditions.
