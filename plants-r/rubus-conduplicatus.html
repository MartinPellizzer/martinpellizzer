Rubus conduplicatus

Rubus conduplicatus, commonly known as the double-leaved blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the western United States, particularly California. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which have a distinctive double lobing at the base. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible blackberries.

The plant thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
