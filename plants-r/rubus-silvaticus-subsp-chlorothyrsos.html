Rubus silvaticus subsp. chlorothyrsos

Rubus silvaticus subsp.

chlorothyrsos, commonly known as the green-stem blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a aggregate of small, dark red berries. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is often found in shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
