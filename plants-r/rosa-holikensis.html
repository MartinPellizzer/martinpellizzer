Rosa holikensis

Rosa holikensis, commonly known as the Holikin rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits called rosehips.

Rosa holikensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.
