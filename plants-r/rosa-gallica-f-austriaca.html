Rosa gallica f. austriaca

Rosa gallica f. austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to Central Europe.

It features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has arching, thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its historical use in perfumery and as a garden ornamental highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
