Romulea albiflora

Romulea albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the spring season. The plant belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes other ornamental plants such as crocus and gladiolus. Its bulbs are adapted for storing water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Romulea albiflora is valued in horticulture for its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
