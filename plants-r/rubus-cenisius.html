Rubus cenisius

Rubus cenisius, commonly known as the European blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, dark purple to black fruits, which are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers in early spring. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The fruit is edible and often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
