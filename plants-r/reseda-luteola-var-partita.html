Reseda luteola var. partita

Reseda luteola var. partita, commonly known as the spotted marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its scientific name reflects its classification within the genus Reseda, which includes several species with similar characteristics.

The varietal name 'partita' refers to the distinctive spotting on its leaves, which sets it apart from other varieties.
