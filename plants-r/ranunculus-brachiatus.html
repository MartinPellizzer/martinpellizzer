Ranunculus brachiatus

Ranunculus brachiatus, commonly known as the branched buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant typically features a short stem with compound leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five to eight sepals that resemble petals, and it produces small, green fruits that contain several seeds.

Ranunculus brachiatus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.
