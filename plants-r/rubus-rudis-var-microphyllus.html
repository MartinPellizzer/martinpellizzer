Rubus rudis var. microphyllus

Rubus rudis var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible berries and ornamental value.
