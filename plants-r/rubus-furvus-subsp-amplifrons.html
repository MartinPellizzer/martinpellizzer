Rubus furvus subsp. amplifrons

Rubus furvus subsp.

amplifrons, commonly known as the broad-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are small and white, with five petals, and it blooms in early summer.

The subspecies is notable for its larger, more robust fruits compared to other Rubus species.
