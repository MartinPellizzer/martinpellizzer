Rubus thyrsanthus f. grabowskii

Rubus thyrsanthus f. grabowskii, commonly known as the Grabowskii raspberry, is a botanical variant of the raspberry genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its erect, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or hedgerow environments.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and limited distribution.
