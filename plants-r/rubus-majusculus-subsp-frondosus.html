Rubus majusculus subsp. frondosus

Rubus majusculus subsp.

frondosus, commonly known as the frondose blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and large, trifoliate leaves that are often deeply lobed.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and local biodiversity.
