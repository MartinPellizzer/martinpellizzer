Rhododendron eriogynum

Rhododendron eriogynum, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink or lilac flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
