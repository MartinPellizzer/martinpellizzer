Ranunculus auricomus proles pseudopsis

Ranunculus auricomus proles pseudopsis, commonly known as the false water crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically divided into lobes, and its flowers are usually yellow with five petals. It thrives in moist, aquatic or wetland environments, often found along the edges of ponds and streams.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
