Rosa canina subvar. hirsuta

Rosa canina subvar. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy rosehip, is a variant of the dog rose that is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

It produces distinctive red or orange hips that are often used in herbal remedies and culinary applications. The plant is native to Europe and western Asia, thriving in woodland and scrub habitats. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
