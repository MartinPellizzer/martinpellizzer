Racosperma sedifolium

Racosperma sedifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem and produces pods that contain seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Racosperma sedifolium is used in reforestation and soil stabilization due to its hardy nature.
