Ranunculus fallax subsp. microdon

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

microdon, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. It has a short stature, usually reaching only a few inches in height, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
