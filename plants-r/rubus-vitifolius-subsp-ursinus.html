Rubus vitifolius subsp. ursinus

Rubus vitifolius subsp.

ursinus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible blackberries in the summer. Its fruit is aggregate, consisting of many small drupelets clustered around a central core.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
