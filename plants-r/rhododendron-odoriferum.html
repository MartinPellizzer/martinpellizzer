Rhododendron odoriferum

Rhododendron odoriferum, commonly known as the fragrant rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers emit a subtle, sweet fragrance, which adds to its ornamental value.

Rhododendron odoriferum is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in cool, humid climates.
