Rubus procerus var. flexilis

Rubus procerus var. flexilis, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated margin and a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
