Rosa unguicularis

Rosa unguicularis, commonly known as the pink sandalwood rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its distinctive pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has slender, thornless stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are often used in perfumery due to their aromatic properties.

Rosa unguicularis is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of conservation interest.
