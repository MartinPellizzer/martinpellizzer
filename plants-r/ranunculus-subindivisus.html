Ranunculus subindivisus

Ranunculus subindivisus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and yellow, cup-shaped flowers with numerous stamens. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.

Its foliage and flowers are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
