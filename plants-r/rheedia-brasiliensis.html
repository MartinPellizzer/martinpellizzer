Rheedia brasiliensis

Rheedia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without drawing nutrients from them. Its leaves are broad and succulent, adapted to store water in the humid environment.

Rheedia brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
