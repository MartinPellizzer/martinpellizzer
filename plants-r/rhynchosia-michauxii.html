Rhynchosia michauxii

Rhynchosia michauxii, commonly known as the Mexican burr bean, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with palmate venation and produces distinctive yellow flowers with a spurred structure. The plant bears indehiscent pods that contain several elliptical, smooth seeds. It is adapted to grow in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed areas.

Rhynchosia michauxii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
