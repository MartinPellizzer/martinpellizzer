Rosa rubea

Rosa rubea, commonly known as the red rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals, which are typically velvety in texture and arranged in layers. The plant produces fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its stems are usually thorny, and the leaves are simple, alternate, and serrated.

Rosa rubea is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in the perfume and food industries.
