Rosa gallica f. versicolor

Rosa gallica f. versicolor, commonly known as the French pink or pinkie rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa gallica species.

It is characterized by its striking, double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its historical use in perfumery and as a symbol of love and devotion adds to its cultural significance.
