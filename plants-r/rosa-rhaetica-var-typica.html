Rosa rhaetica var. typica

Rosa rhaetica var. typica, commonly known as the Rhaetic rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa rhaetica var. typica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
