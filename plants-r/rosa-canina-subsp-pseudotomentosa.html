Rosa canina subsp. pseudotomentosa

Rosa canina subsp.

pseudotomentosa, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a subspecies native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in the spring, followed by orange to red hips in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
