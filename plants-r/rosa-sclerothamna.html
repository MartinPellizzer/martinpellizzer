Rosa sclerothamna

Rosa sclerothamna, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically followed by red, hairy fruits that contain several seeds.

Rosa sclerothamna is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
