Ranunculus lawalreei

Ranunculus lawalreei, commonly known as the Hawaiian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. It has palmate leaves with lobed edges and is often found in the wetter regions of Hawaii.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected under conservation efforts.
