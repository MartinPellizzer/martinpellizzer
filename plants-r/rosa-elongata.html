Rosa elongata

Rosa elongata, commonly known as the elongated rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red-orange fruits known as hip fruits.

Rosa elongata is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
