Rivina humilis var. scandens

Rivina humilis var. scandens, commonly known as creeping rivina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub that produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with smooth margins and a glossy green color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
