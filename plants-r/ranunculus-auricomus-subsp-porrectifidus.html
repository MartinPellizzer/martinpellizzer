Ranunculus auricomus subsp. porrectifidus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

porrectifidus, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often adorned with a distinctive golden hue.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are alternate along the stem. It typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Ranunculaceae family.
