Rosa urbica f. genuina

Rosa urbica f. genuina, commonly known as the urban rose, is a botanical variant of the common rose.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, thornless stems, making it well-suited for urban environments. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, demonstrating adaptability to city conditions.

Its ornamental value and resilience make it a popular choice for landscaping in urban settings.
