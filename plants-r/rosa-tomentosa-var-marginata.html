Rosa tomentosa var. marginata

Rosa tomentosa var. marginata, commonly known as the fuzzy pink rose, is a variant of the woolly rose.

It is characterized by its soft, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive fuzzy texture. The plant features thorny stems and broad, ovate leaves covered in a dense layer of grayish-white hairs. It is native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in temperate climates.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
