Rubus glandisepalus

Rubus glandisepalus, commonly known as the glandular-sepal raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five sepals that are glandular and hairy.

Rubus glandisepalus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along streambanks.
