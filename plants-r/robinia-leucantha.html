Robinia leucantha

Robinia leucantha, commonly known as the white robinia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The tree has compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters during the spring.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
