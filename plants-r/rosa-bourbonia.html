Rosa bourbonia

Rosa bourbonia, commonly known as the Bourbon rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It features upright, thornless stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall, and its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late summer. Rosa bourbonia is well-suited for USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8 and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
