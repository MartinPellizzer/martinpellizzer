Rosa cinnamomea var. oblonga

Rosa cinnamomea var. oblonga, commonly known as the cinnamon rose, is a cultivar characterized by its unique foliage and floral features.

Its leaves are broad, elliptical, and often have a slight pubescent texture, contributing to its ornamental value. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep red, depending on the variety. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest throughout the growing season.
