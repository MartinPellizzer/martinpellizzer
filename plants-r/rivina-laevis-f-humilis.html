Rivina laevis f. humilis

Rivina laevis f. humilis, commonly known as the smooth rivina or low-growing rivina, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its species name "laevis.". The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and low-maintenance plant for ornamental use.
