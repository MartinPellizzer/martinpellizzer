Ranunculus auricomus subsp. parvitorus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

parvitorus, commonly known as the small-flowered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features showy, bright yellow flowers with deeply lobed petals and a distinctive, hairy stem.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
