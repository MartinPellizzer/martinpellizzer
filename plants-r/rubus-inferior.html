Rubus inferior

Rubus inferior, commonly known as the common blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. Its flowers are small, white, and appear in clusters, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Rubus inferior is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.
