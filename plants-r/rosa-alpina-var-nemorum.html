Rosa alpina var. nemorum

Rosa alpina var. nemorum, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces showy, single or double flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to grow in high-altitude environments.
