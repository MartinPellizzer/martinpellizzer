Rubus sulcatus var. typicus

Rubus sulcatus var. typicus, commonly known as the serrated blackberry.

It is a shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its edible berries and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
