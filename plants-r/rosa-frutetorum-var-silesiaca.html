Rosa frutetorum var. silesiaca

Rosa frutetorum var. silesiaca, commonly known as the Silesian shrub rose, is a perennial deciduous shrub native to Central Europe.

It features arching stems with thorny branches and dark green, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, pinnately compound leaves, and an upright growth habit.

It is valued for ornamental purposes and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
