Ranunculus meionophyllus

Ranunculus meionophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and short flowering period.

Ranunculus meionophyllus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and botanical collections.
