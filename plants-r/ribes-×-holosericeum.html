Ribes × holosericeum

Ribes × holosericeum, commonly known as the hybrid gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a natural hybrid between *Ribes sanguineum* (burning bush) and *Ribes glandulosum* (currant). The plant is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its berries are small, yellow, and edible.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in some culinary applications.
