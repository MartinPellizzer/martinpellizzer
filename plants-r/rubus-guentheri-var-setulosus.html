Rubus guentheri var. setulosus

Rubus guentheri var. setulosus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in moist, wooded areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural study.
