Rubus marshallii var. semiglaber

Rubus marshallii var. semiglaber, commonly known as Marshall's blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while the varietal name "semiglaber" refers to its slightly hairy, or pubescent, surface.

This species is often found in woodland edges and moist soils, contributing to local biodiversity.
