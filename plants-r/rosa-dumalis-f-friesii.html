Rosa dumalis f. friesii

Rosa dumalis f. friesii, commonly known as the Friesii form of the alpine rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dark green, leathery leaves with a slightly serrated margin, and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and is well adapted to cold, rocky environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

This variety is prized for its resilience and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
