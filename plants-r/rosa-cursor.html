Rosa cursor

Rosa cursor, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its thorny stems help it to cling to surfaces. Rosa cursor is native to parts of Asia and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
