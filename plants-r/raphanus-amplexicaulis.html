Raphanus amplexicaulis

Raphanus amplexicaulis, commonly known as the hearted radish, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and often covered in a powdery coating called a pruina. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, typical of the family.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
