Rubus superioris

Rubus superioris, commonly known as the superior raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in jams and desserts. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

Rubus superioris thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.
