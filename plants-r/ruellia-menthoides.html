Ruellia menthoides

Ruellia menthoides, commonly known as silverleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it its common name. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple and bloom in the summer months.

Ruellia menthoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
