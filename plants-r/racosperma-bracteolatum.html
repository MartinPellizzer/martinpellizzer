Racosperma bracteolatum

Racosperma bracteolatum, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the genus Racosperma. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small leaflets, and it has a woody, branched stem.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
