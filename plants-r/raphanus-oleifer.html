Raphanus oleifer

Raphanus oleifer, commonly known as the oil radish, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Asia and is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves and swollen, globe-shaped roots. The plant produces yellow flowers that are arranged in racemes, and it is cultivated for its edible root, which is rich in oils and nutrients. Its botanical features make it a valuable crop in certain regions for both culinary and agricultural purposes.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
