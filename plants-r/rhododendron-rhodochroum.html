Rhododendron rhodochroum

Rhododendron rhodochroum, commonly known as the purple-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage and clusters of tubular, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant prefers well-drained, acidic soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

Rhododendron rhodochroum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler, moist climates.
