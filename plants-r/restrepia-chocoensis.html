Restrepia chocoensis

Restrepia chocoensis, commonly known as the Chocó slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the dense, humid forests of the Chocó region. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain, which is typical for many epiphytic orchids.

Restrepia chocoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
