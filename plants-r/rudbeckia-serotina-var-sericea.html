Rudbeckia serotina var. sericea

Rudbeckia serotina var. sericea, commonly known as the silverleaf coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It features showy yellow-orange ray flowers surrounding a dark brown central disk, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to support local wildlife in native gardens.
