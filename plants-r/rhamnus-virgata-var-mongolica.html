Rhamnus virgata var. mongolica

Rhamnus virgata var. mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to northern China and Mongolia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

The plant has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
