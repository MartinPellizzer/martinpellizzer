Rhamnus × ovalifolia

Rhamnus × ovalifolia, commonly known as the ovalleaf buckthorn, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by reddish-black berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
