Rubus pauper

Rubus pauper, commonly known as the poor bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and appear in clusters.

The fruit, a red, edible berry, is often used in jams and preserves.
