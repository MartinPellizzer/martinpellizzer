Rhynchospora longiseta

Rhynchospora longiseta, commonly known as the long-hair sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-brown flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late summer. Rhynchospora longiseta thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its presence is often an indicator of healthy, undisturbed wetland habitats.
