Rosularia paniculata

Rosularia paniculata, commonly known as the panicled roseleaf, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small panicles. The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions. It blooms in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosularia paniculata is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
