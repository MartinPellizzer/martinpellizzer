Rhapis javanica

Rhapis javanica, commonly known as the Java palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are typically green with a slight blue tinge. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into black, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Rhapis javanica is relatively slow-growing and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.
