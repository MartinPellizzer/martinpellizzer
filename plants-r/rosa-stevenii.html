Rosa stevenii

Rosa stevenii, commonly known as the Steven's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its simple, often thornless stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. Its leaves are alternate, with serrated margins, and it produces small, red-orange fruits known as hips.

Rosa stevenii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
