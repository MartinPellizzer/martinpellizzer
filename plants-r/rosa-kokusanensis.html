Rosa kokusanensis

Rosa kokusanensis, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a species native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are covered in thorny prickles.

Rosa kokusanensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
