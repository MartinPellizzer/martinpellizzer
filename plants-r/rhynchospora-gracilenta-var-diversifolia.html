Rhynchospora gracilenta var. diversifolia

Rhynchospora gracilenta var. diversifolia, commonly known as the slender beak sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features slender, grass-like stems that grow from rhizomes, typically reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, three-lobed flowers, which are usually greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and tapering, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

Rhynchospora gracilenta var. diversifolia plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
