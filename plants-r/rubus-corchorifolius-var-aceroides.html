Rubus corchorifolius var. aceroides

Rubus corchorifolius var. aceroides, commonly known as the red-leaf bramble, is a variant of the brambles native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange-red leaves, which contrast with its typically green stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
