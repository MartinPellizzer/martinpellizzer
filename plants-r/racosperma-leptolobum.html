Racosperma leptolobum

Racosperma leptolobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
