Rosa villosa var. suberecta

Rosa villosa var. suberecta, commonly known as the woolly rose or shrub rose, is a perennial deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems covered in a soft, white to grayish pubescence. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with ovate to elliptical leaflets that are hairy beneath.

Rosa villosa var. suberecta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
