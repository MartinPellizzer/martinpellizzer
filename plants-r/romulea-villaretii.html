Romulea villaretii

Romulea villaretii, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globe-shaped bulbs and delicate, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like shape, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is a sought-after addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
