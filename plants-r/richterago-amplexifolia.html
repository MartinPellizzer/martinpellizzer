Richterago amplexifolia

Richterago amplexifolia, commonly known as the clasping leaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are often clasped around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Richterago amplexifolia is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
