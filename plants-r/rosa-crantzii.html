Rosa crantzii

Rosa crantzii, commonly known as the white shrub rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it has thorny stems.

Rosa crantzii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
