Rhamnus californica subsp. occidentalis

Rhamnus californica subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the coastal buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States. It typically grows in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to coastal climates and its role in local ecosystems.
