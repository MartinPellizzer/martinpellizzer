Rosa rubiginosa var. leioclona

Rosa rubiginosa var. leioclona, commonly known as the pale rose or the pink rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The flowers are typically pink to pale red, with five petals and a fragrant scent. This variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a source of edible hips.
