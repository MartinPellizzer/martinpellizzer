Ranunculus missouriensis

Ranunculus missouriensis, commonly known as the Missouri buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central United States.

It features showy, palmate leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to eleven petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

The Missouri buttercup is also known for its toxic properties, containing protoanemonin, which can cause skin irritation.
