Rubus bifrons var. robustior

Rubus bifrons var. robustior, commonly known as the robust blackberry.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupelets that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its edible berries and is cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
