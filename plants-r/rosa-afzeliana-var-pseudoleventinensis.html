Rosa afzeliana var. pseudoleventinensis

Rosa afzeliana var. pseudoleventinensis, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in horticulture for its beauty and resilience.
