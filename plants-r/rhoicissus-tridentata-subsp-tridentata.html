Rhoicissus tridentata subsp. tridentata

Rhoicissus tridentata subsp.

tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed climbing plant, is a vine native to southern Africa. It features heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its stems are typically slender and can climb using tendrils.

It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
