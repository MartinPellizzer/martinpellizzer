Rosa canina f. polyclada

Rosa canina f. polyclada, commonly known as the multiflora rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in a variety of soil types.

This subspecies is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
