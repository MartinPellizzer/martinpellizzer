Ranunculus oblitus

Ranunculus oblitus, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals, and it typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant has a short, stout stem with trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
