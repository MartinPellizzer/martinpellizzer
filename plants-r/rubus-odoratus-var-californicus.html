Rubus odoratus var. californicus

Rubus odoratus var. californicus, commonly known as the California evergreen raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features prickly stems and trifoliate leaves, with small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny, red drupes that ripen in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
