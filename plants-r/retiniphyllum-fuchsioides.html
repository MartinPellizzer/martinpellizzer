Retiniphyllum fuchsioides

Retiniphyllum fuchsioides, commonly known as the red-flowered tamarind tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its growth habit includes a slender trunk and a spreading canopy, making it a popular choice for ornamental planting.
