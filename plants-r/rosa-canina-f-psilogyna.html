Rosa canina f. psilogyna

Rosa canina f. psilogyna, commonly known as the smooth hips rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless fruits, which contrast with the typical prickly hips of the species. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
