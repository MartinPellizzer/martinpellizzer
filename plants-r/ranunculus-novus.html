Ranunculus novus

Ranunculus novus, commonly known as the New Globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features a hairy stem that can grow up to 30 centimeters tall, supporting a single, large, globe-shaped inflorescence. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a deep green color and a rough texture.

The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.
