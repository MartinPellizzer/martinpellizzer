Rubus rhamnifolius subsp. lindebergii

Rubus rhamnifolius subsp.

lindebergii, commonly known as Lindberg's blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and genetic differentiation from other Rubus species.
