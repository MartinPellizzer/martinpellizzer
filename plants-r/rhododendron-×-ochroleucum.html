Rhododendron × ochroleucum

Rhododendron × ochroleucum, commonly known as the white-leafed rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron ferrugineum and Rhododendron ponticum.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery, white to pale green leaves that are often covered in a waxy bloom. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
