Rubia membranacea

Rubia membranacea, commonly known as the membranous bindweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Rubia membranacea is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
