Rhodoxylon floridum

Rhodoxylon floridum, commonly known as the Florida holly.

It is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and parts of Georgia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It produces small, white, clustered flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

The plant is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
