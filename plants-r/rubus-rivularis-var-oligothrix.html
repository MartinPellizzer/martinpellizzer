Rubus rivularis var. oligothrix

Rubus rivularis var. oligothrix, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels. The fruit, a small, dark purple berry, develops from the flowers and is often used in local cuisines.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.
