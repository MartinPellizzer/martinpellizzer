Ranunculus ophioglossifolius var. dentatus

Ranunculus ophioglossifolius var. dentatus, commonly known as the toothed buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, serrated leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitats.
