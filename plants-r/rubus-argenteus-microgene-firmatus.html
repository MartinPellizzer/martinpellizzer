Rubus argenteus microgene firmatus

Rubus argenteus microgene firmatus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its stems are covered in stiff, barbed hairs, which help in seed dispersal.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and unique foliage.
