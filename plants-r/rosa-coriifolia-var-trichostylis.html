Rosa coriifolia var. trichostylis

Rosa coriifolia var. trichostylis, commonly known as the hairy-styled cutleaf rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are typically thornless and covered in a similar hairy coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
