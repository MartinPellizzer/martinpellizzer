Rabdosia umbrosa var. komaensis

Rabdosia umbrosa var. komaensis, commonly known as Koma mountain rabdosia, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
