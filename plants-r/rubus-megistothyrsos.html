Rubus megistothyrsos

Rubus megistothyrsos, commonly known as the giant blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its large, thorny stems and clusters of dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
