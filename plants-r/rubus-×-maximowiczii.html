Rubus × maximowiczii

Rubus × maximowiczii, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark red to black in color, and are known for their tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial purposes.
