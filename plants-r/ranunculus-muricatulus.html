Ranunculus muricatulus

Ranunculus muricatulus, commonly known as the rough buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

The rough buttercup is an herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
