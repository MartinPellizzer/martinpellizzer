Rubia cordifolia var. pratensis

Rubia cordifolia var. pratensis, commonly known as the meadow rue or field bindweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, scrambling stems and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped, giving it the common name "cordifolia.". Its roots contain alkaloids and have been used historically in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in meadows and woodland areas.
