Rubus chloocladus subsp. aduncispinus

Rubus chloocladus subsp.

aduncispinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

The leaves are typically narrow and have a distinct serrated margin. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
