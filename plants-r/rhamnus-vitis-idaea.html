Rhamnus vitis-idaea

Rhamnus vitis-idaea, commonly known as red-berry elder or whortleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of tiny, red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal forests and rocky habitats. It has a woody stem with thorn-like branches, and its flowers are small and greenish-white, appearing in early spring.

Rhamnus vitis-idaea is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
