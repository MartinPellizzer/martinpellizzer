Rhodanthe uniflora

Rhodanthe uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb with a creeping rootstock and produces a single, showy yellow or orange flower per stem. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers bloom in spring and are visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Rhodanthe uniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
