Rhamnus davurica var. hirsuta

Rhamnus davurica var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy Chinese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
