Rumex lativalvis

Rumex lativalvis, commonly known as broad-leaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, often with a reddish tinge. The leaves are large, broad, and lobed, with a waxy surface and a distinctive reddish or purple underside. It produces clusters of small, greenish flowers in the summer, which are followed by reddish-brown, three-winged seeds.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in waste areas, demonstrating its adaptability to various environments.
