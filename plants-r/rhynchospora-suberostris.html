Rhynchospora suberostris

Rhynchospora suberostris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beakspur sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or reddish in color, and it blooms from late spring to early summer. Rhynchospora suberostris plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
