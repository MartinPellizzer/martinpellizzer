Rubus obtruncatus var. fecundus

Rubus obtruncatus var. fecundus, commonly known as the purple-fruit blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to purple, depending on maturity. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they bloom in the spring.

The species is valued for its edible berries and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
