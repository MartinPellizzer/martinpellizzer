Roella decurrens

Roella decurrens, commonly known as the common rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape, and its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, arranged in racemes. It produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds.

Roella decurrens is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
