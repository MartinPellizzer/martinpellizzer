Rinorea paradoxa

Rinorea paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical rinorea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Rinorea paradoxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
