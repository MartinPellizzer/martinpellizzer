Rosa villosa var. moutinii

Rosa villosa var. moutinii, commonly known as the Moutinii rose, is a variant of the hairy rose native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
