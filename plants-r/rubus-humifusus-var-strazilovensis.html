Rubus humifusus var. strazilovensis

Rubus humifusus var. strazilovensis, commonly known as the Strazilovenskaya blackberry, is a rare variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of Eastern Europe.

It is a low-growing shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, dark berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional remedies. Its botanical characteristics include a dense root system and a preference for moist, acidic soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
