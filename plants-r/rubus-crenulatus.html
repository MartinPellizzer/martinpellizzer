Rubus crenulatus

Rubus crenulatus, commonly known as the prickly raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible, red berries that are often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Rubus crenulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
