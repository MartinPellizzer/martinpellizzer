Rhododendron quadrasianum

Rhododendron quadrasianum, commonly known as the Quadrasi's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from pink to deep red.

Rhododendron quadrasianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate growing conditions.
