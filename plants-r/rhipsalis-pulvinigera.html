Rhipsalis pulvinigera

Rhipsalis pulvinigera, commonly known as the silver hair cactus, is a species of cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that are often covered in a dense covering of white or silver hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, typically in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and white, often appearing in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
