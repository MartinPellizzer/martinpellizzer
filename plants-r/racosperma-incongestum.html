Racosperma incongestum

Racosperma incongestum, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, typical of the legume family. It is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native flora in its habitat.
