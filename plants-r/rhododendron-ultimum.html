Rhododendron ultimum

Rhododendron ultimum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron ultimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
