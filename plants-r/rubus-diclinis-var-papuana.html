Rubus diclinis var. papuana

Rubus diclinis var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
