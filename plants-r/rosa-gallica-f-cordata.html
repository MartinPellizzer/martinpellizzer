Rosa gallica f. cordata

Rosa gallica f. cordata, commonly known as the heart-shaped rose, is a compact shrub native to Europe.

It features dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and have a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers in early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its visual interest.

Due to its hardiness, it is suitable for a variety of growing conditions, including partial shade.
