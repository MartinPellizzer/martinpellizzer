Rosa communis var. aciculata

Rosa communis var. aciculata, commonly known as the needle-leaved rose, is a variety of the common rose characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in the spring. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

The foliage remains evergreen in mild climates, adding year-round interest to the landscape.
