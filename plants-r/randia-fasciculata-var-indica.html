Randia fasciculata var. indica

Randia fasciculata var. indica, commonly known as Indian randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature, turning red upon ripening. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in folk medicine.
