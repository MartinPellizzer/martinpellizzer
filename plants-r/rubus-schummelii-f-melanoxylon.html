Rubus schummelii f. melanoxylon

Rubus schummelii f. melanoxylon, commonly known as the black-stemmed blackberry, is a botanical variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically tart and less sweet than common blackberries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its potential in local ecosystems.
