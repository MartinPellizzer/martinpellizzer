Rubus hirtus var. crassus

Rubus hirtus var. crassus, commonly known as the rough blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often described as tart. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The botanical features of this variety include dense pubescence on the stems and leaves, contributing to its rough texture.
