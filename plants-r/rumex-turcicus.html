Rumex turcicus

Rumex turcicus, commonly known as Turkish dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants like sorrel and buckthorn. The plant features triangular, ribbed leaves that are typically reddish or purple in color, especially on the underside. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine.

Rumex turcicus thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and meadows.
