Rubus macrophyllus var. dechenii

Rubus macrophyllus var. dechenii, commonly known as the large-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its large, serrated leaves that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its robust stems and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early summer.
