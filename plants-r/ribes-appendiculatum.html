Ribes appendiculatum

Ribes appendiculatum, commonly known as the alpine gooseberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Ribes appendiculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
