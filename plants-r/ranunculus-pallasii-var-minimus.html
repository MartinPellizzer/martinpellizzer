Ranunculus pallasii var. minimus

Ranunculus pallasii var. minimus, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a small herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with 5 to 13 petals, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
