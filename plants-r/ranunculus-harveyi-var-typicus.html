Ranunculus harveyi var. typicus

Ranunculus harveyi var. typicus, commonly known as the Harvey's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it has a short, stout stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
