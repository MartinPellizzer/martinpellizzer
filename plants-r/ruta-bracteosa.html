Ruta bracteosa

Ruta bracteosa, commonly known as the bracted rue, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves and yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant contains various volatile oils and alkaloids, contributing to its medicinal and historical uses. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and traditional herbal medicine.
