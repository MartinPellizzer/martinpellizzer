Rhododendron lamii

Rhododendron lamii, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to China and belongs to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron lamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
