Rubus confusus

Rubus confusus, commonly known as the confused blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. Its shrub-like growth habit features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

Botanically, it is characterized by its aggregate fruits and is closely related to other Rubus species.
