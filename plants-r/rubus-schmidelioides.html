Rubus schmidelioides

Rubus schmidelioides, commonly known as the Schmidel's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shrublands and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
