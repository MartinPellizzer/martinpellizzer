Ricinus communis f. erythrocladus

Ricinus communis f. erythrocladus, commonly known as the crimson-stem castor oil plant, is a horticultural form of the castor oil plant.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and leaves that often display a deep green with reddish veins. The plant produces large, palmate leaves and spiny capsules containing toxic seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to gardens and botanical collections.
