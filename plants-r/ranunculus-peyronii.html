Ranunculus peyronii

Ranunculus peyronii, commonly known as the Peyronii crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive central greenish or brownish hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often found in calcareous soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
