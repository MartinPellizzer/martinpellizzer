Ranunculus circinans

Ranunculus circinans, commonly known as the spiral buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which form a tight rosette around the stem. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often with a prominent central stigma. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ranunculus circinans is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
