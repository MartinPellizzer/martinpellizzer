Rubus flosculosus f. parvifolius

Rubus flosculosus f. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved bramble, is a variant of the common bramble species.

It is characterized by its smaller, more delicate leaves compared to the typical form. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, often in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural collections.
