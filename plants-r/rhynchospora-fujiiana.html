Rhynchospora fujiiana

Rhynchospora fujiiana, commonly known as the Japanese marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale pink or white, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Rhynchospora fujiiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
