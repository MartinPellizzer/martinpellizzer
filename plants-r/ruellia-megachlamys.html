Ruellia megachlamys

Ruellia megachlamys, commonly known as the large-flowered ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Ruellia megachlamys thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its attractive blooms and adaptability to various garden settings.
