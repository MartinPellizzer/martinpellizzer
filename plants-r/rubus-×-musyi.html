Rubus × musyi

Rubus × musyi, commonly known as the musk blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (slippery elm) and Rubus strumosus (blackberry).

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are highly aromatic and often used in jams and wines. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
