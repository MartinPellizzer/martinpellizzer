Rosa adscita var. orthacantha

Rosa adscita var. orthacantha, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a variant of the Rosa adscita species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in pink or white. The plant has elliptical leaves with serrated edges and produces rose hips that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a unique and resilient member of the Rosaceae family.
