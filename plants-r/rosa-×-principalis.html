Rosa × principalis

Rosa × principalis, commonly known as the Rosa principalis or the Japanese rose, is a hybrid shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has thorny stems covered in dense, dark green foliage. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of 5 to 7 elliptical leaflets.

Rosa × principalis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
