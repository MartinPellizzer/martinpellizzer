Rhytiglossa glandulosa

Rhytiglossa glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it its common name and help in reducing water loss in its arid habitat. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rhytiglossa glandulosa is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and resilience.
