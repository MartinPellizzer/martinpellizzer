Rhaphidophora laciniata

Rhaphidophora laciniata, commonly known as the Split-leaf Philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a waxy texture. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments and prefers indirect sunlight. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance.

Rhaphidophora laciniata is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
