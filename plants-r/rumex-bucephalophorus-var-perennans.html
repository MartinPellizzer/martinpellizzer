Rumex bucephalophorus var. perennans

Rumex bucephalophorus var. perennans, commonly known as the perennial sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and clustered inflorescences. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a reddish or purple coloration on the underside, which is a notable botanical trait. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, contributing to its persistence in natural habitats.
