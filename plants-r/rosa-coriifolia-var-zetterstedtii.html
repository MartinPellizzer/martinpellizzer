Rosa coriifolia var. zetterstedtii

Rosa coriifolia var. zetterstedtii, commonly known as the Zetterstedt's shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals and a distinctive, flared form. The plant has thornless stems and lance-shaped leaves with finely serrated edges. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its elegant appearance.
