Ranunculus hostiliensis

Ranunculus hostiliensis, commonly known as the hostile buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its name reflects its historical association with warfare, as it was believed to have been used in the production of poison.

Ranunculus hostiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native environment.
