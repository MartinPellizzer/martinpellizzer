Rosa rubiginosa subvar. spinosissima

Rosa rubiginosa subvar. spinosissima, commonly known as the bristly rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in summer. Its hips, which are orange-red and hairy, contain seeds and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.
