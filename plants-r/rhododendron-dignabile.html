Rhododendron dignabile

Rhododendron dignabile, commonly known as the noble rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded forest environments. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stem.

Rhododendron dignabile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
