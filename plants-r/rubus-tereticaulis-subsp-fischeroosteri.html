Rubus tereticaulis subsp. fischeroosteri

Rubus tereticaulis subsp.

fischeroosteri, commonly known as the Fischerooster's blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
