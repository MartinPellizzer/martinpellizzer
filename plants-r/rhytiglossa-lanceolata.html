Rhytiglossa lanceolata

Rhytiglossa lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf hound's-tongue, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive, often glandular surface. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and can persist through harsh environmental conditions.

Rhytiglossa lanceolata is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
