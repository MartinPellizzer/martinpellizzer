Rubus × semigratiflorus

Rubus × semigratiflorus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It is native to parts of eastern Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
