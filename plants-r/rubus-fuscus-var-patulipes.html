Rubus fuscus var. patulipes

Rubus fuscus var. patulipes, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubiaceae family.
