Rubus corylifolius var. adenocladus

Rubus corylifolius var. adenocladus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that give way to edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The leaves and berries are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
