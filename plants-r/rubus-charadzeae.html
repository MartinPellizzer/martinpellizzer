Rubus charadzeae

Rubus charadzeae, commonly known as the Georgian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many shrubs with edible berries. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus family. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
