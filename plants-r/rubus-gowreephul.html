Rubus gowreephul

Rubus gowreephul, commonly known as the Gowreephul bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its fruit, a red, hairy berry, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
