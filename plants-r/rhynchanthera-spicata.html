Rhynchanthera spicata

Rhynchanthera spicata, commonly known as the spike orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Rhynchanthera spicata is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
