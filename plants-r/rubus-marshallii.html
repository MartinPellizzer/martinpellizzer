Rubus marshallii

Rubus marshallii, commonly known as Marshall's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus marshallii is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.
