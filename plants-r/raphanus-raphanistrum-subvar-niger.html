Raphanus raphanistrum subvar. niger

Raphanus raphanistrum subvar. niger, commonly known as black radish, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust taproot that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, often with a dark purple or black exterior. The plant produces yellow flowers in a dense cluster, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The plant is known for its pungent odor and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
