Raphanus niger var. albus

Raphanus niger var. albus, commonly known as the white radish, is a cultivar of the wild radish species native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its white, crisp, and mildly sweet root that is typically harvested when young. The plant has a short growing season and thrives in cool climates, making it a popular crop in temperate regions. Its leaves are edible and can be used in salads or cooked like spinach.

The white radish is also valued for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant benefits.
