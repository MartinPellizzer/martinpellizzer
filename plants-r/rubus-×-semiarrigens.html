Rubus × semiarrigens

Rubus × semiarrigens, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries. These fruits are typically dark purple to black when ripe and are known for their tart flavor.

Rubus × semiarrigens is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils across North America.
