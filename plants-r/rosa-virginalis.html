Rosa virginalis

Rosa virginalis, commonly known as the Virgin's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with simple, alternate leaves that are serrated at the edges. Its stems are covered in thorns, and it produces rose hips that contain seeds.

Rosa virginalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
