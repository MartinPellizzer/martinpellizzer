Rhodiola sacra

Rhodiola sacra, commonly known as the golden root or king's crown, is a perennial plant native to the cold climates of the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Rhodiola sacra is known for its adaptogenic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
