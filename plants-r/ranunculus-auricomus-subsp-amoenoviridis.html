Ranunculus auricomus subsp. amoenoviridis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

amoenoviridis, commonly known as the greenleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a smooth, waxy texture and bright yellow flowers with five petals.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more elongated and less lobed leaves compared to the typical form. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
