Ranunculus calvertii

Ranunculus calvertii, commonly known as Calvert's buttercup, is a rare flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy and often toxic species. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves. It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered in several states.
