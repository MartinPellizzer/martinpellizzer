Rubus × reductispinus

Rubus × reductispinus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its compact growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically smaller than those of other blackberry varieties. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and small-scale farms.
