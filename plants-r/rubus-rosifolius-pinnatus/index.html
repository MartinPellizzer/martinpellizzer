Rubus Rosifolius Pinnatus

Rosy-leaved raspberry, scientifically known as Rubus rosifolius pinnatus, is a striking shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. This unique plant is celebrated for its vibrant pink foliage and clusters of edible berries that ripen in late summer. Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and utility in their landscapes. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of growing conditions. With its delicate appearance and delicious fruit, Rosy-leaved raspberry stands out as a remarkable addition to both wild and cultivated environments.
