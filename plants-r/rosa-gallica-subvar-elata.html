Rosa gallica subvar. elata

Rosa gallica subvar. elata, commonly known as the upright gallica rose, is a subspecies native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, showy flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine.
