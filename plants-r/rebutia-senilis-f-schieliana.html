Rebutia senilis f. schieliana

Rebutia senilis f. schieliana, commonly known as the Schiel's old man cactus, is a unique succulent native to the rocky habitats of South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems that often appear as a cluster of small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its slow growth rate and compact form make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation.

Due to its specific care requirements, it is considered a challenging plant for novice growers.
