Rubus × aequabilis

Rubus × aequabilis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus pectoralis.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically black when mature, though they may also be red or purple. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
