Rosa macroacantha

Rosa macroacantha, commonly known as the large-lobed rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its stems are covered in stiff, thorny hairs, which provide a natural defense against herbivores.

Rosa macroacantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
