Rhus natalensis var. obovatifoliolata

Rhus natalensis var. obovatifoliolata, commonly known as the Natal buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with obovate leaflets that are typically arranged in pairs along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
