Rubus vulgatus var. illuminatus

Rubus vulgatus var. illuminatus, commonly known as the radiant blackberry, is a variant of the common bramble that exhibits strikingly bright, almost glowing fruit.

Its berries are typically a deep, almost iridescent purple, with a slightly sweeter and more aromatic flavor compared to standard blackberries. The plant thrives in temperate climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. It produces thorny canes that grow upright, often reaching heights of up to six feet.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique fruit characteristics.
