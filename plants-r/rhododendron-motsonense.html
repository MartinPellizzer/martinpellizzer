Rhododendron motsonense

Rhododendron motsonense, commonly known as the Motson's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth. It is often found in forested areas at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
