Rubus persericans

Rubus persericans, commonly known as the Persian raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or dry soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and horticultural settings.
