Ranunculus trautvetterianus

Ranunculus trautvetterianus, commonly known as the Trautvetter's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with prominent black markings. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and thrives in cool, temperate climates. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some environments.
