Rumex sagittatus

Rumex sagittatus, commonly known as arrowleaf dock, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that are often ribbed and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. Arrowleaf dock is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in meadows.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported diuretic and astringent properties.
