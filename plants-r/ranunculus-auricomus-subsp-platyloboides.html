Ranunculus auricomus subsp. platyloboides

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

platyloboides, commonly known as the broad-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals that are often tinged with red or brown at the base.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
