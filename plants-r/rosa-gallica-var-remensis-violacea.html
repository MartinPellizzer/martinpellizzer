Rosa gallica var. remensis-violacea

Rosa gallica var. remensis-violacea, commonly known as the pink musk rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa gallica species, characterized by its deep pink to almost crimson flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green foliage. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double blooms in early to mid-summer, which are often used in perfumery and floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
