Rubus costatus var. collinus

Rubus costatus var. collinus, commonly known as the coastal raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its arching stems and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along coastal areas and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
