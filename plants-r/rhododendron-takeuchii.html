Rhododendron takeuchii

Rhododendron takeuchii, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron takeuchii prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
