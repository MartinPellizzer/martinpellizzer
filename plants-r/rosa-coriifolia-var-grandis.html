Rosa coriifolia var. grandis

Rosa coriifolia var. grandis, commonly known as the large-leafed shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its stems are woody and can be thorny, while its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive, serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
