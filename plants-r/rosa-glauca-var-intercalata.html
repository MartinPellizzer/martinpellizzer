Rosa glauca var. intercalata

Rosa glauca var. intercalata, commonly known as the bluebloom rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its striking blue-pink flowers.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is known for its cold hardiness and vigorous growth. The plant typically features thorned stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant, semi-double flowers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration and resilience.
