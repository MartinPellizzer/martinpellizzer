Rosa zasljensis

Rosa zasljensis, commonly known as the Sajani rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Georgia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by red, fuzzy fruits called hips. Rosa zasljensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and resilient plant adapted to its native environment.
