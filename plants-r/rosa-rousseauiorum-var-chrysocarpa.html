Rosa rousseauiorum var. chrysocarpa

Rosa rousseauiorum var. chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden-fruited rose, is a unique variant of the Rosa rousseauiorum species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow fruits, which contrast strikingly with its pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.

This variety is relatively rare and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
