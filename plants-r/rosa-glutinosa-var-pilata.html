Rosa glutinosa var. pilata

Rosa glutinosa var. pilata, commonly known as the sticky rose or sticky dog rose, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered with a sticky, glandular coating that gives it its common name.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
