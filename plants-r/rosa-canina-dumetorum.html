Rosa canina dumetorum

Rosa canina dumetorum, commonly known as the shrub rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. Rosa canina dumetorum is native to Europe and western Asia and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

It is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed habitats.
