Racosperma caleyi

Racosperma caleyi, commonly known as the Australian wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance. It plays an important role in soil stabilization and is often used in revegetation projects.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, contributing to local biodiversity.
