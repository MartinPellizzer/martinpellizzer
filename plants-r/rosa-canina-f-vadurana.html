Rosa canina f. vadurana

Rosa canina f. vadurana, commonly known as the Vaduran rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose, characterized by its distinctive floral and foliar features.

It typically exhibits larger, more vibrant flowers compared to the typical Rosa canina, with a deeper pink hue and a more pronounced fragrance. The plant is notable for its robust growth habit and adaptability to various soil types, making it a resilient species in diverse environments. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the fruit, known as a hip, is a characteristic red, fuzzy capsule that contains seeds.

This form is often appreciated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
