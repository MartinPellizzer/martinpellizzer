Rhododendron pritzelianum

Rhododendron pritzelianum, commonly known as the Pritzell's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
