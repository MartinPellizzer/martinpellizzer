Rosa platyacantha var. crassipes

Rosa platyacantha var. crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved dog rose, is a variant of the dog rose species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with thorny stems and dark green foliage. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and herbal medicine due to their aromatic properties.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
