Rosa × andegavensis f. innersteinensis

Rosa × andegavensis f. innersteinensis, commonly known as the Innerstein rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from Germany.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features thorny stems with dark green, lobed leaves. It is well-suited for garden cultivation and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
