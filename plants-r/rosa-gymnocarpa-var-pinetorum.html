Rosa gymnocarpa var. pinetorum

Rosa gymnocarpa var. pinetorum, commonly known as the pine barberry rose, is a variant of the gymnocarpa rose species.

It is characterized by its small, thornless stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces bright red, aggregate fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden settings.
