Rhododendron glabrifilum

Rhododendron glabrifilum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and smooth, contributing to its common name.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
