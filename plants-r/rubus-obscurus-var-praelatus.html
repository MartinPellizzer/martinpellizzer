Rubus obscurus var. praelatus

Rubus obscurus var. praelatus, commonly known as the dark bramble or blackberry shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting wildlife and its ornamental potential in gardens.
