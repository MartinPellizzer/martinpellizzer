Racosperma verticillatum subsp. ruscifolium

Racosperma verticillatum subsp.

ruscifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, narrow leaves arranged in whorls.

The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
