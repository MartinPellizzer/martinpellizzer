Ranunculus mexiae

Ranunculus mexiae, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces yellow or orange flowers in the spring.

Ranunculus mexiae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
