Restio garnotianus var. oligostachyus

Restio garnotianus var. oligostachyus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of white to pale blue flowers, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
