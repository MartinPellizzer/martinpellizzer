Reissantia indica var. indica

Reissantia indica var. indica, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and wood quality, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
