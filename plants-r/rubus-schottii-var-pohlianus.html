Rubus schottii var. pohlianus

Rubus schottii var. pohlianus, commonly known as the Pohli's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
