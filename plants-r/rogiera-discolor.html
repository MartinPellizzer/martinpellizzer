Rogiera discolor

Rogiera discolor, commonly known as the spotted flax, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Rogiera discolor is known for its distinctive white or pale spots on the leaves, which give it its common name.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
