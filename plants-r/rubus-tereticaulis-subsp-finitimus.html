Rubus tereticaulis subsp. finitimus

Rubus tereticaulis subsp.

finitimus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and thorned stems, typical of the Rubus genus.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
