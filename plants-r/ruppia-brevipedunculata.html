Ruppia brevipedunculata

Ruppia brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stemmed sea rocket, is a halophytic plant adapted to brackish and marine environments.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and compact, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow coastal waters, such as estuaries and tidal flats, where it can tolerate fluctuating salinity levels. Its reproductive structures are designed for dispersal by water, facilitating propagation in aquatic habitats.

Ruppia brevipedunculata plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
