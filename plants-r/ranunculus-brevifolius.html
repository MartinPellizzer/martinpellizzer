Ranunculus brevifolius

Ranunculus brevifolius, commonly known as the short-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with five to eight sepals that resemble petals. The plant has basal leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
