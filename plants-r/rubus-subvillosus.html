Rubus subvillosus

Rubus subvillosus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and is notable for its hairy texture on both foliage and fruit.
