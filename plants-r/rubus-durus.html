Rubus durus

Rubus durus, commonly known as the hardy blackberry shrub, is a species of brambles native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Rubus durus is valued for its hardiness and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
