Rosa pimpinellifolia var. laevis

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth meadow rose, is a compact shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. The leaves are pinnately compound, with finely toothed leaflets that give it a delicate appearance. This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
