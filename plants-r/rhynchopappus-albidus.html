Rhynchopappus albidus

Rhynchopappus albidus, commonly known as the white pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-dispersed seeds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow to white flowers. Its seed heads are surrounded by silky, white pappus hairs that aid in wind pollination and seed dispersal.

Rhynchopappus albidus is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low water availability.
