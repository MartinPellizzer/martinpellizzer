Rosa canina subobtusa

Rosa canina subobtusa, commonly known as the prickly rose or dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features thorny stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces pink or white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red hips, which are its characteristic fruit. Rosa canina subobtusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
