Rubus illecebrosus var. yakusimensis

Rubus illecebrosus var. yakusimensis, commonly known as the Yakushima raspberry, is a variant of the brambles native to Japan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions with high humidity. Its leaves are usually compound, with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
