Rhynchospora pearcei

Rhynchospora pearcei, commonly known as the pearce's spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, clustered flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Rhynchospora pearcei typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, where it thrives in nutrient-poor soils.
