Rosa canina f. kirghisorum

Rosa canina f. kirghisorum, commonly known as the Kirghiz rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, pinnately compound leaves, and fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces aggregate fruits called rosehips, which are rich in vitamin C and have a high ornamental value. It is native to Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
