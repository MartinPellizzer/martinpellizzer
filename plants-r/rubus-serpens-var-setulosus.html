Rubus serpens var. setulosus

Rubus serpens var. setulosus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically covered in fine, bristly hairs. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
