Rosa pendulina f. levilagensis

Rosa pendulina f. levilagensis, commonly known as the pendulous rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa pendulina species.

It is characterized by its long, drooping stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
