Rhododendron aureum subsp. hypopitys

Rhododendron aureum subsp.

hypopitys, commonly known as the yellow azalea, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a slight pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
