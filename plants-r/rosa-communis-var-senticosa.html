Rosa communis var. senticosa

Rosa communis var. senticosa, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers and thorny stems. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its historical use in perfumery and medicine highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
