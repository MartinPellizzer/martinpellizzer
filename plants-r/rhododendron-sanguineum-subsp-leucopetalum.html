Rhododendron sanguineum subsp. leucopetalum

Rhododendron sanguineum subsp.

leucopetalum, commonly known as the white alpine azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
