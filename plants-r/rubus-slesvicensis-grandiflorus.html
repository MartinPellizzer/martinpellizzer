Rubus slesvicensis grandiflorus

Rubus slesvicensis grandiflorus, commonly known as the Silesian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically small and tart. Native to parts of Europe, it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

It is often found in woodlands and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
