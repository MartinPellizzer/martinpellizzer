Rubus × pseudogneissogenes

Rubus × pseudogneissogenes, commonly known as the hybrid raspberry.

This plant is a hybrid species resulting from the crossbreeding of Rubus idaeus (red raspberry) and Rubus strigosus (brambles). It exhibits characteristics of both parent species, including thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring.

It is typically found in temperate regions and is valued for its fruit's culinary and medicinal uses.
