Rubus × semifusciformis

Rubus × semifusciformis, commonly known as the blackberry-loganberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus loganobaccus (loganberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The species exhibits thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins, typical of the Rubus genus. Its flowers are usually pink to red, and the fruit is a aggregate berry that ripens to a dark purple or black color.

This hybrid is valued for its flavor and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
