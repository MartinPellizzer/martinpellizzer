Rubus × recurvicaulis var. inarmatus

Rubus × recurvicaulis var. inarmatus, commonly known as the soft-stemmed blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thornless stems and dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small white flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its edible fruit, which has a sweet-tart flavor and is often used in jams and desserts.
