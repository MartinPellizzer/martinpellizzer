Rubus × sericatus f. umbrosus

Rubus × sericatus f. umbrosus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to regions of eastern North America and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its edible berries and its role in ecological habitats.
