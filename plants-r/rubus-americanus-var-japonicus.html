Rubus americanus var. japonicus

Rubus americanus var. japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a variant of the American blackberry that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typically featuring three to five leaflets. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupelets, which are usually dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
