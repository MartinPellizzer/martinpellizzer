Rubus villicaulis f. eurypetalus

Rubus villicaulis f. eurypetalus, commonly known as the broad-petaled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

This subspecies exhibits larger, more prominent petals compared to the typical Rubus villicaulis, giving it a more showy appearance. It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark, sweet blackberry.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
