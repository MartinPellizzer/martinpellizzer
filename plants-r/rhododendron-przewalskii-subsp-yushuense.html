Rhododendron przewalskii subsp. yushuense

Rhododendron przewalskii subsp.

yushuense, commonly known as the Yushu rhododendron, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of western China. It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, pink to reddish-purple flowers.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its alpine habitat. It is typically found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native ecosystem.
