Ricinocarpus pruriens

Ricinocarpus pruriens, commonly known as the itching bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its rough, hairy stems and leaves, which can cause skin irritation upon contact. It produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
