Rubus bullatifolius

Rubus bullatifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its large, serrated leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles.
