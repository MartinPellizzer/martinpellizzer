Ranunculus micropetalus

Ranunculus micropetalus, commonly known as the small-petaled buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five to eight petal-like sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, hairy fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Ranunculus micropetalus is often found in calcareous soils and is considered a rare species in many regions.
