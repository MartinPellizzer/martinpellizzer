Rebutia nataliarum

Rebutia nataliarum, commonly known as the Nataliarum Cactus, is a species native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and ribbed structure. The plant features spiny areoles and produces yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Rebutia nataliarum is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
