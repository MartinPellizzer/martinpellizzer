Rollinia mammifera

Rollinia mammifera, commonly known as the Brazilian custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Annonaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The fruit is a rough, spiny capsule that turns from green to yellow or red when mature. Inside, it contains several large, whitish, fleshy seeds surrounded by a sweet, creamy pulp.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, which is popular in local cuisines.
