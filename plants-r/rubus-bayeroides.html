Rubus bayeroides

Rubus bayeroides, commonly known as the bayery raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry and blackberry species.
