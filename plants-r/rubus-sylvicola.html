Rubus sylvicola

Rubus sylvicola, commonly known as the mountain blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, dark red to black berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor, making them a food source for wildlife and humans in some regions.
