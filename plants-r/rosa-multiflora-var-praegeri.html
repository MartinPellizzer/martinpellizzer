Rosa multiflora var. praegeri

Rosa multiflora var. praegeri, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features arching stems with thorny branches and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in clusters. The leaves are compound, with ovate to elliptic leaflets and serrated edges. This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens for its visual appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.
