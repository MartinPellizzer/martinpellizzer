Rubus glabrior

Rubus glabrior, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small flowers. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy underside.

Rubus glabrior thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
