Rumex nemolapathum

Rumex nemolapathum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and alternate leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in woodland and streamside habitats.

Rumex nemolapathum is a perennial herb that is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
