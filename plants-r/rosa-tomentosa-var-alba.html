Rosa tomentosa var. alba

Rosa tomentosa var. alba, commonly known as the white-tomentose rose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white foliage and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional horticulture. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both cultivated gardens and natural landscapes.
