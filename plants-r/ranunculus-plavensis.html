Ranunculus plavensis

Ranunculus plavensis, commonly known as the blue buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. The plant features a basal rosette of dark green, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with prominent, rounded seed pods. Its flowers are usually solitary and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Ranunculus plavensis is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
