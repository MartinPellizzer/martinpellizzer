Rhaptocarpus coalitus

Rhaptocarpus coalitus, commonly known as the coal-leaf cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnately compound leaves that are dark green and leathery in texture. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. Its name derives from the coal-like appearance of its leaves, which are dark and dense.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
