Rhododendron hodgsonii var. album

Rhododendron hodgsonii var. album, commonly known as the White Hodgson's Rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its foliage consists of dark green, leathery leaves that provide a striking contrast to the white blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ecological importance in its native habitat.
