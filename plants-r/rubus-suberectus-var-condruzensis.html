Rubus suberectus var. condruzensis

Rubus suberectus var. condruzensis, commonly known as the Condruzen's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and thorny stems, typical of many bramble species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically red when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have serrated margins.

This variety is native to specific regions in South America and is adapted to temperate climates with well-drained soils.
