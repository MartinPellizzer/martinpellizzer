Rhododendron argyrophyllum var. glabriovarium

Rhododendron argyrophyllum var. glabriovarium, commonly known as the silverleaf rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage.

The leaves are typically silver-gray in color, covered with a fine, waxy coating that reflects light and reduces water loss. It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, where it thrives in cool, moist, acidic soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded garden environments.
