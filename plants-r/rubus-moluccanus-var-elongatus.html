Rubus moluccanus var. elongatus

Rubus moluccanus var. elongatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry species native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its elongated, thornless stems and dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
