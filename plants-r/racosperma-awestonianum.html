Racosperma awestonianum

Racosperma awestonianum, commonly known as the Australian wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant. It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, and is often used in revegetation projects.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, contributing to local biodiversity.
