Rosa iberica

Rosa iberica, commonly known as the Iberian rose, is a species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include simple, alternate leaves and hips that are typically red or orange when mature.

Rosa iberica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
