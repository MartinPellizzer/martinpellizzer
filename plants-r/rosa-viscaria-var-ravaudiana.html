Rosa viscaria var. ravaudiana

Rosa viscaria var. ravaudiana, commonly known as the Ravaudian rose, is a variant of the shrub rose native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the specific cultivar. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to the blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
