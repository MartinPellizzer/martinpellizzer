Rondeletia chimboracensis

Rondeletia chimboracensis, commonly known as the Andean flame flower, is a strikingly beautiful shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a smooth texture, while its stems are woody and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bold, eye-catching floral display.
