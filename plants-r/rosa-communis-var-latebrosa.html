Rosa communis var. latebrosa

Rosa communis var. latebrosa, commonly known as the lateblooming rose, is a botanical variant of the common rose.

It is characterized by its delayed flowering period, typically blooming later in the growing season compared to other rose varieties. The plant features fragrant, pink to white flowers that are often described as having a more intense scent. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience in various garden conditions.
