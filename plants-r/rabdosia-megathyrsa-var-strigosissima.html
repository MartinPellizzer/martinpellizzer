Rabdosia megathyrsa var. strigosissima

Rabdosia megathyrsa var. strigosissima, commonly known as the hairy rabdosia, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically lavender to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
