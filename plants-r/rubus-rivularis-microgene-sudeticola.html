Rubus rivularis microgene sudeticola

Rubus rivularis microgene sudeticola, commonly known as the Sudetic raspberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing or creeping growth habit, with small, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
