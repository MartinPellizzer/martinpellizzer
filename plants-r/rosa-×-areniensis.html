Rosa × areniensis

Rosa × areniensis, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the high altitudes of the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces rose hips that contain seeds.

Rosa × areniensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and floral display.
