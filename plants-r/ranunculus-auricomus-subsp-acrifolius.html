Ranunculus auricomus subsp. acrifolius

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

acrifolius, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its subspecies status is distinguished by its darker, more leathery leaves compared to the typical form.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
