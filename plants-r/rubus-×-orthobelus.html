Rubus × orthobelus

Rubus × orthobelus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus glaucus. This species is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically harvested in late summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
