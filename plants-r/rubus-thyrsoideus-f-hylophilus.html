Rubus thyrsoideus f. hylophilus

Rubus thyrsoideus f. hylophilus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a botanical variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive red fruit.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of North America, particularly the eastern United States. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. Its fruits are small, aggregate berries that ripen to a deep red color, often used in culinary applications.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, making it a popular choice in gardens and for commercial cultivation.
