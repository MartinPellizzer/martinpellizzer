Rosa canina var. rubra

Rosa canina var. rubra, commonly known as the red dog rose, is a variant of the dog rose species native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its vivid red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces aggregate fruits known as hips, which are orange-red and contain numerous seeds. Rosa canina var. rubra is typically shrub-like, with thorny stems and dark green, oval leaves.

It is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and other disturbed habitats.
