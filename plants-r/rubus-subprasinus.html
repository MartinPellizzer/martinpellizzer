Rubus subprasinus

Rubus subprasinus, commonly known as the greenleaf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves that are usually a deep green. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible berries that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
