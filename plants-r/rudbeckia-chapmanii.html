Rudbeckia chapmanii

Rudbeckia chapmanii, commonly known as Chapman's coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.

Rudbeckia chapmanii is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
