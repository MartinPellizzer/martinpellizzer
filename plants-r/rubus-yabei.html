Rubus yabei

Rubus yabei, commonly known as the Yabei blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of China and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tart or astringent flavor.
