Ripidium japonicum

Ripidium japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often clustered together, and its leaves are narrow and pointed. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

Ripidium japonicum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
