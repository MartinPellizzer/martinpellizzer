Rubrivena pinetorum

Rubrivena pinetorum, commonly known as the crimson pine vine, is a striking evergreen climbing plant native to the temperate forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red stems and broad, glossy green leaves that provide a striking contrast. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by ornamental red berries that attract birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
