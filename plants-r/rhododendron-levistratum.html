Rhododendron levistratum

Rhododendron levistratum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool and moist environments. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
