Ruellia geminiflora var. angustifolia

Ruellia geminiflora var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

Ruellia geminiflora var. angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
