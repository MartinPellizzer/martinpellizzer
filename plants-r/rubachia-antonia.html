Rubachia antonia

Rubachia antonia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple petals and a distinctive conical center. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Rubachia antonia is valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
