Rubus furvus var. nudipes

Rubus furvus var. nudipes, commonly known as the nakedfoot blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and dark reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
