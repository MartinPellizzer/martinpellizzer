Rosa rubiginosa subvar. rubiginella

Rosa rubiginosa subvar. rubiginella, commonly known as the crimson rose or the red shrub rose, is a compact, thorny shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of deep red, semi-double flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for coastal gardens. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its resilience and beauty.
