Rubus rosaceus var. silvestris

Rubus rosaceus var. silvestris, commonly known as wild blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its edible berries and its role in ecological systems as a food source for various wildlife species.
