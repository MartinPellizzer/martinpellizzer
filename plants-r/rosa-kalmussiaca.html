Rosa kalmussiaca

Rosa kalmussiaca, commonly known as the Kalmus rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are covered in thorny projections.

Rosa kalmussiaca is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its historical use in traditional medicine.
