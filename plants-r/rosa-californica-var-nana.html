Rosa californica var. nana

Rosa californica var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf California rose, is a compact shrub native to the western United States.

It features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, rounded leaves and is well adapted to rocky soils and coastal climates. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

This variety is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun to partial shade.
