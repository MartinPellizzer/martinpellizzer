Rosa moschata var. nivea

Rosa moschata var. nivea, commonly known as the white musk rose, is a variant of the musk rose species characterized by its pale pink to white flowers.

It is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces a fragrant, musk-like scent, which is more pronounced in its flowers than in other varieties. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, with thornless stems that make it suitable for ornamental gardening.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery.
