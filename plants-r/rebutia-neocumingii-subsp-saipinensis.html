Rebutia neocumingii subsp. saipinensis

Rebutia neocumingii subsp.

saipinensis, commonly known as the Saipan rebutia, is a succulent plant native to the island of Saipan in the Northern Mariana Islands. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny stems.

The plant features small, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tinge. It produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its foliage.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
