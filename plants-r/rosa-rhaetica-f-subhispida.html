Rosa rhaetica f. subhispida

Rosa rhaetica f. subhispida, commonly known as the Rhaetian rose, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the Rosa rhaetica species.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and vibrant, deep red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to the alpine regions of Europe, particularly the Swiss Alps, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental appeal and ability to add a striking visual element to gardens.

Due to its rarity and specific growing requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens or botanical collections.
