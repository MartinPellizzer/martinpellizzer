Rosa pratincola f. alba

Rosa pratincola f. alba, commonly known as the white meadow rose, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a hybrid form of Rosa pratincola, characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows and grassy areas, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant, single or semi-double blooms.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its delicate beauty.
