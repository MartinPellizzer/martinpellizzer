Rubus rufus var. longipedicellatus

Rubus rufus var. longipedicellatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its edible berries and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental and culinary uses.
