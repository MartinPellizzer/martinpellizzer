Ruellia consocialis

Ruellia consocialis, commonly known as the clustered ruellia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tubular, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Ruellia consocialis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
