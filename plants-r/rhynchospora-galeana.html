Rhynchospora galeana

Rhynchospora galeana, commonly known as the Mexican beakgrass, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in wetland or marshy environments, often growing in shallow water or saturated soil.

Rhynchospora galeana is characterized by its distinctive beak-like bracts that surround the flower cluster, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
