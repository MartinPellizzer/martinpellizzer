Rubus hirtus var. hypoglaucus

Rubus hirtus var. hypoglaucus, commonly known as the soft-leaved blackberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with soft, hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a common species in horticultural and ecological settings.
