Rheum plicatum

Rheum plicatum, commonly known as palmate buckler-leaf, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed leaves that resemble the shape of a palm, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by reddish-brown, spore-bearing structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of plant morphology and biodiversity.
