Rubus sorbicus

Rubus sorbicus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate. The plant produces aggregate fruits known as blackberries, which are edible and have a distinctive sweet-tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Rubus sorbicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
