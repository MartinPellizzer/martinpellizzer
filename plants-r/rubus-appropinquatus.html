Rubus appropinquatus

Rubus appropinquatus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of bright red, aggregate fruits that are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Rubus appropinquatus is widely cultivated for its edible fruits and is also used in traditional medicine.
