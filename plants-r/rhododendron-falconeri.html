Rhododendron falconeri

Rhododendron falconeri, commonly known as the Falconeri Rhododendron, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in Ecuador.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant thrives in cool, humid climates and requires well-drained, acidic soil to flourish. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
