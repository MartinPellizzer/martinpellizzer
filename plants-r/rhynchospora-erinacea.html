Rhynchospora erinacea

Rhynchospora erinacea, commonly known as the slender beakspur sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. Rhynchospora erinacea thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
