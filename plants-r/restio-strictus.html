Restio strictus

Restio strictus, commonly known as the narrow restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Restio strictus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
