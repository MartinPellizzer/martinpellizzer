Ranunculus kastamonuensis

Ranunculus kastamonuensis, commonly known as the Kastamonu buttercup, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of numerous small petals arranged in a radiate pattern.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
