Rubus × tridentinus

Rubus × tridentinus, commonly known as the three-lobed blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rubus genus in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which develop from multiple ovaria within a single flower. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually serrated along the edges.

The fruit, a dark purple to black berry, is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its flavor and nutritional content.
