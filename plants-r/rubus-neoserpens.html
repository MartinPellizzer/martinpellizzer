Rubus neoserpens

Rubus neoserpens, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest understories and stream banks.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many other bramble species known for their edible fruits.
