Rubus rhamnifolius subsp. obtusangulus

Rubus rhamnifolius subsp.

obtusangulus, commonly known as the blunt-angled blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with rounded leaflets.

The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
