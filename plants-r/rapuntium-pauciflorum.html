Rapuntium pauciflorum

Rapuntium pauciflorum, commonly known as the sparse-flowered rapuntium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It produces small, round, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines.

The leaves are usually hairy and have a mild, peppery flavor similar to other members of its family.
