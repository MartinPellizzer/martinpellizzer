Rubus eboracensis

Rubus eboracensis, commonly known as the Yorkshire bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the United Kingdom, particularly in the Yorkshire region, where it thrives in woodland and hedgerow habitats. The plant is characterized by its prickly stems, compound leaves, and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its berries, which are typically red or black, are edible but often have a tart flavor.

This species plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and is valued for its ecological and historical significance.
