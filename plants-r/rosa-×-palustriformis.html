Rosa × palustriformis

Rosa × palustriformis, commonly known as the marsh rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its semi-woody stems and is typically found in wetland environments. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

Rosa × palustriformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
