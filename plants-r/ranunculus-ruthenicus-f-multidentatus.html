Ranunculus ruthenicus f. multidentatus

Ranunculus ruthenicus f. multidentatus, commonly known as the multi-toothed buttercup, is a botanical variant of the buttercup genus characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

This plant features finely divided, multi-toothed leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The flowers are usually yellow with five petals, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique foliage and its role in ecological studies of plant adaptation.
