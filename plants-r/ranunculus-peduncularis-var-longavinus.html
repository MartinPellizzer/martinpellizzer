Ranunculus peduncularis var. longavinus

Ranunculus peduncularis var. longavinus, commonly known as the long-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive, hairy stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a smooth, glossy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
