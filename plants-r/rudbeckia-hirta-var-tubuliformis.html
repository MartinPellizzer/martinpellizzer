Rudbeckia hirta var. tubuliformis

Rudbeckia hirta var. tubuliformis, commonly known as the yellow coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear large, showy yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
