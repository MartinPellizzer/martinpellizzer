Rhododendron × calycinum

Rhododendron × calycinum, commonly known as the Calycina rhododendron, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from both Rhododendron calycina and Rhododendron catawbiense.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is native to the Appalachian Mountains in the United States and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its vibrant floral display.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for horticultural purposes in regions with suitable climate conditions.
