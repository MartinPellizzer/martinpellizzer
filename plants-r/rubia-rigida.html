Rubia rigida

Rubia rigida, commonly known as rigid balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 12,000 species of flowering plants. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the tips of the branches.

Rubia rigida is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
