Rosettea thollonii

Rosettea thollonii, commonly known as the Madagascar rosette plant, is a succulent native to the island of Madagascar.

It forms dense, compact rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warm season. Its unique morphology and ability to survive in dry environments make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Rosettea thollonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
