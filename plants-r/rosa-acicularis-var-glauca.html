Rosa acicularis var. glauca

Rosa acicularis var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous thorned rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, thorned stems and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant has a dense, bushy growth habit. It is well adapted to harsh, arid environments and thrives in full sun.

The variety is distinguished by its bluish-gray foliage, which gives it a unique appearance.
