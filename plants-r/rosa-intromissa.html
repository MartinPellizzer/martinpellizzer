Rosa intromissa

Rosa intromissa, commonly known as the introverted rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces hips that are edible and rich in vitamin C. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Rosa intromissa is considered a botanical curiosity and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
