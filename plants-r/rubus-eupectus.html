Rubus eupectus

Rubus eupectus, commonly known as the European blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that produces edible berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit is valued for its flavor and is often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
