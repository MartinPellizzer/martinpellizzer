Rubus beirensis

Rubus beirensis, commonly known as the Beiren bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of East Asia, particularly China, where it grows in forested and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small white or pink flowers. Its fruit, a type of aggregate berry, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
