Ranunculus auricomus subsp. firmicaulis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

firmicaulis, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals and a distinctive, hairy stem.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil conditions.
