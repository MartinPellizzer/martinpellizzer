Ranunculus auricomus subsp. subbrevis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

subbrevis, commonly known as the short-stemmed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and large, showy yellow flowers with prominent black markings.

The leaves are palmately divided, typically with five to seven lobes, and are covered in fine hairs. This subspecies is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring cool, temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the Ranunculaceae family.
