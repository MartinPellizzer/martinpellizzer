Ranunculus spicatus var. rupestris

Ranunculus spicatus var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine regions.

It features a basal rosette of finely divided, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in crevices and on slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
