Rhadamanthus intermedius

Rhadamanthus intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate rhadamanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the cooler months. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a shallow root system.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
