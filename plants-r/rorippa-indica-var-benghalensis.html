Rorippa indica var. benghalensis

Rorippa indica var. benghalensis, commonly known as Indian watercress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia.

It is an aquatic or semi-aquatic species, typically found in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has small, green, floating or submerged leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is known for its peppery flavor and is often used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
