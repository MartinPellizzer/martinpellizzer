Rosa gallica var. multiplex

Rosa gallica var. multiplex, commonly known as the French rose, is a compact shrub native to Europe.

It features multiple stems covered in dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant due to its hardiness and ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both garden cultivation and historical horticultural practices.
