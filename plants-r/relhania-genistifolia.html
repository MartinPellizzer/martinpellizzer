Relhania genistifolia

Relhania genistifolia, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often yellowish-green and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright orange or red pods. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Relhania genistifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
