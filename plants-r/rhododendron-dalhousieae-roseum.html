Rhododendron dalhousieae roseum

Rhododendron dalhousieae roseum, commonly known as the Roseum Rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for horticultural enthusiasts and botanists alike.
