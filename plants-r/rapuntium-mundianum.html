Rapuntium mundianum

Rapuntium mundianum, commonly known as the common rapunculus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its root system consists of tuberous roots that are edible and used in some traditional cuisines.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
