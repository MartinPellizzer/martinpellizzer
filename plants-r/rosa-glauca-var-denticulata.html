Rosa glauca var. denticulata

Rosa glauca var. denticulata, commonly known as the denticulate bluebelle rose, is a variant of the bluebelle rose species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its pale blue to lavender flowers, which bloom in early summer and are often fragrant. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are borne singly or in small clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
