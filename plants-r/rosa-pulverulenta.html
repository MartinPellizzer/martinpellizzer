Rosa pulverulenta

Rosa pulverulenta, commonly known as the powdered rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is known for its fragrant, semi-double blooms. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa pulverulenta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
