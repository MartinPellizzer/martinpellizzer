Ranunculus dalechanensis

Ranunculus dalechanensis, commonly known as the Dalechanensis buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features basal leaves that are palmately divided, with serrated edges, and its flowers have five to eight sepals that resemble petals. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its delicate, cup-shaped blooms.
