Rhododendron sataense

Rhododendron sataense, commonly known as the Satae rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to white, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron sataense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
