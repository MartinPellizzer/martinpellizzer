Rubus tereticaulis var. proprenes

Rubus tereticaulis var. proprenes, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
