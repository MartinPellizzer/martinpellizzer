Rubus lindleyanus

Rubus lindleyanus, commonly known as the Lindley's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Colombia. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and wines. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant has a trailing or climbing growth habit, with compound leaves and thorny stems.
