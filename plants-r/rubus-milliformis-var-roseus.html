Rubus milliformis var. roseus

Rubus milliformis var. roseus, commonly known as the rosehip bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, red berries that turn orange or yellow as they mature. These berries are rich in vitamin C and are often used in herbal remedies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
