Rubus × terribilis

Rubus × terribilis, commonly known as the devil's ivy or the wild blackberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark red to black in color. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
