Rosa spinosissima var. platycarpa

Rosa spinosissima var. platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited prickly rose, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals, and bloom in early summer. The variety is distinguished by its broad, rounded fruits that are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
