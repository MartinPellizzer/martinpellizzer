Rhytidophyllum

Rhytidophyllum, commonly known as the peeling bark plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive, flaky bark that peels away in thin layers.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's foliage consists of elongated, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in moisture retention. Rhytidophyllum is a climbing shrub that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, using its aerial roots to attach to trees and other structures.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
