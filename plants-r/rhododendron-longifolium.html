Rhododendron longifolium

Rhododendron longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, particularly China, Korea, and Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and leathery, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Rhododendron longifolium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
