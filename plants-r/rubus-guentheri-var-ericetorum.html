Rubus guentheri var. ericetorum

Rubus guentheri var. ericetorum, commonly known as the Ericaceous Raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often in woodland or streamside habitats. Its leaves are alternate, with serrated margins, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
