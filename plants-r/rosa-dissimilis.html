Rosa dissimilis

Rosa dissimilis, commonly known as the Canadian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to North America, particularly Canada and the northern United States. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces red, berry-like fruits known as hips.

Rosa dissimilis is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
