Ranunculus retusifolius

Ranunculus retusifolius, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow, white, or orange. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants with a delicate, daisy-like appearance.
