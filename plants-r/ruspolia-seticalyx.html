Ruspolia seticalyx

Ruspolia seticalyx, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on its stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its unique morphology makes it an important component of its ecosystem, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
