Rosa gabrielis

Rosa gabrielis, commonly known as the Gabriel's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 5 to 7 oval-shaped leaflets, and are arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa gabrielis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
