Rubus whartoniae

Rubus whartoniae, commonly known as the Wharton's blackberry, is a species of shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and blackberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits. It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
