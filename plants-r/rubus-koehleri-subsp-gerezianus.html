Rubus koehleri subsp. gerezianus

Rubus koehleri subsp.

gerezianus, commonly known as the Gerezian raspberry, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It is a shrub characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, containing several drupelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
