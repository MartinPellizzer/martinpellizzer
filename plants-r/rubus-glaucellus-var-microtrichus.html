Rubus glaucellus var. microtrichus

Rubus glaucellus var. microtrichus, commonly known as the small-haired evergreen raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
