Roseocactus intermedius

Roseocactus intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate roseocactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in small, white to pale yellow spines. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
