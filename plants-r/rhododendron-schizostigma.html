Rhododendron schizostigma

Rhododendron schizostigma, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions with acidic, well-drained soils. The plant features broad, elliptical leaves that are often dark green and densely arranged along the stems. Its flowers are showy, with pink to red petals and a distinctive, elongated calyx.

Rhododendron schizostigma is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
