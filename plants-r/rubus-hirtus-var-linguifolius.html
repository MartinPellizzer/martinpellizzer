Rubus hirtus var. linguifolius

Rubus hirtus var. linguifolius, commonly known as the hairy raspberry or linguifolius raspberry, is a variant of the brambles in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive leaf shape that resembles a tongue, hence its specific epithet "linguifolius.". The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
