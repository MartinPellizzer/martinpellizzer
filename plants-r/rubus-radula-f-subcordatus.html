Rubus radula f. subcordatus

Rubus radula f. subcordatus, commonly known as the subcordate raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry species characterized by its distinctive leaf shape.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. Its leaves are usually serrated and have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential use in culinary applications.
