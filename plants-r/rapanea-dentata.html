Rapanea dentata

Rapanea dentata, commonly known as the toothed evergreen or the toothed camphor tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with serrated leaf margins. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in axillary inflorescences, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

Rapanea dentata is valued for its aromatic wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
