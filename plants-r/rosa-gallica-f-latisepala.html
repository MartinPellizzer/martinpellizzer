Rosa gallica f. latisepala

Rosa gallica f. latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled gallica rose, is a fragrant, deciduous shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with five to seven broad, pink petals and a prominent central pistil. The plant produces oval, red-brown hips that are often used in herbal remedies. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
