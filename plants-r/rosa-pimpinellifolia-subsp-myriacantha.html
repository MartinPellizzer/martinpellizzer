Rosa pimpinellifolia subsp. myriacantha

Rosa pimpinellifolia subsp.

myriacantha, commonly known as the mountain rose or the Spanish rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in white or pink hues.

The leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and the plant produces rose hips that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
