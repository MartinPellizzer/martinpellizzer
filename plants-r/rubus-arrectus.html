Rubus arrectus

Rubus arrectus, commonly known as the upright blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in partial shade.

Rubus arrectus is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local wildlife.
