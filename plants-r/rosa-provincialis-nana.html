Rosa provincialis nana

Rosa provincialis nana, commonly known as the dwarf provincial rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and a dense, bushy growth habit. It is well adapted to rocky soils and can tolerate cold climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
