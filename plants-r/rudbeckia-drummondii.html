Rudbeckia drummondii

Rudbeckia drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that produce daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and orange to red petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to moist soils.

Rudbeckia drummondii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to support pollinators in native gardens.
