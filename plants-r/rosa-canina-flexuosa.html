Rosa canina flexuosa

Rosa canina flexuosa, commonly known as the dog rose or wild rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, pinnately compound leaves, and fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces red-orange hips, which are the fruit and contain seeds. Rosa canina flexuosa is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa, and it thrives in a variety of habitats including woodlands and hedgerows.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
