Rosa × dumetorum var. forsteri

Rosa × dumetorum var. forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's shrub rose, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring multiple stems covered in thorns. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with a serrated margin.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.
