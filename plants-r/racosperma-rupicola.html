Racosperma rupicola

Racosperma rupicola, commonly known as the mountain wattle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Racosperma rupicola is a hardy species that is adapted to coastal conditions and can tolerate salt spray.
