Rubus taiwanianus

Rubus taiwanianus, commonly known as the Taiwan raspberry, is a shrub native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in forested areas and mountainous regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The berries, which ripen in summer, are valued for their tart flavor and are used in local culinary traditions.
