Rubus caesius var. parvifolius

Rubus caesius var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny, dark purple to black drupes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

This variety is valued for its edible berries and its role in local ecosystems.
