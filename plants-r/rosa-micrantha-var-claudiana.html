Rosa micrantha var. claudiana

Rosa micrantha var. claudiana, commonly known as the Claudiana rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with finely serrated edges. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
