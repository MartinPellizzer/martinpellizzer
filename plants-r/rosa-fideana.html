Rosa fideana

Rosa fideana, commonly known as the "faithful rose," is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Russia. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven oval leaflets, and it produces small, red-orange hips in the fall.

Rosa fideana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
