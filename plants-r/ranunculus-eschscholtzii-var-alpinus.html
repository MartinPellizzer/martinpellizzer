Ranunculus eschscholtzii var. alpinus

Ranunculus eschscholtzii var. alpinus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has palmate, lobed leaves that are usually hairy, and its stems are erect with a rough texture.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in alpine ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
