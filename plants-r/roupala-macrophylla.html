Roupala macrophylla

Roupala macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved roupala, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that can reach up to 15 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
