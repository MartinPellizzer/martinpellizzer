Rosa davidii

Rosa davidii, commonly known as the David's rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets and thorned stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Rosa davidii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
