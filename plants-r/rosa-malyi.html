Rosa malyi

Rosa malyi, commonly known as the Malayan rose, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in thorns.

Rosa malyi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
