Rubus sanctus var. chaniensis

Rubus sanctus var. chaniensis, commonly known as the Chaniensis blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to specific regions of China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and less commonly cultivated species.
