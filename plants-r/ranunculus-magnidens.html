Ranunculus magnidens

Ranunculus magnidens, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a striking member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with prominent black veining, which can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy underside, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
