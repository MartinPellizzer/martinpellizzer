Rokautskyia pseudoglaziovii

Rokautskyia pseudoglaziovii, commonly known as the false glaziov's bellflower, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a splash of color to its high-altitude habitat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
