Rubus adenbergensis

Rubus adenbergensis, commonly known as the Adenberg blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
