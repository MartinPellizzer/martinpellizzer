Rubus ulmifolius subsp. anisodon

Rubus ulmifolius subsp.

anisodon, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry. It is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to edible berries in the summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in hedgerows and woodland edges.
