Racosperma dolichophyllum

Racosperma dolichophyllum, commonly known as the long-stemmed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
