Rubus argenteus subsp. consobrinus

Rubus argenteus subsp.

consobrinus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silvery-gray undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Rubus genus, valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
