Ranunculus petiolaris var. trahens

Ranunculus petiolaris var. trahens, commonly known as the trailing ranunculus, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals and numerous stamens. The plant has a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for ground cover or hanging baskets. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens.
