Ranunculus auricomus subsp. pullaster

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

pullaster, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often accompanied by a distinctive, palmately lobed leaf structure.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, and the species is known for its rapid growth and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.

It is also noted for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
