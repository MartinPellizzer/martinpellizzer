Rourea multiflora

Rourea multiflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf rourea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale green flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture.

Rourea multiflora is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
