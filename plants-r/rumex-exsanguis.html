Rumex exsanguis

Rumex exsanguis, commonly known as bloody dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically reddish or purple in color, especially along the veins. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional remedies.
