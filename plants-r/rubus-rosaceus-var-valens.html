Rubus rosaceus var. valens

Rubus rosaceus var. valens, commonly known as the European blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black or dark purple color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its flavorful berries.

The species is known for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
