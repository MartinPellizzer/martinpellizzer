Rubus danubialis

Rubus danubialis, commonly known as the Danube raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows along riverbanks and moist woodlands. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. Its leaves are pinnately compound with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and stabilizing soil along waterways.
