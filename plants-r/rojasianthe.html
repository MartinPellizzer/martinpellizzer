Rojasianthe

Rojasianthe, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a rare and captivating member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the cloud forests of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking, star-shaped yellow flowers that resemble miniature suns. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the nutrient-poor soil of its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, Rojasianthe is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
