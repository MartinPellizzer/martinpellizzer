Rosa rubiginosa subvar. eriocalyx

Rosa rubiginosa subvar. eriocalyx, commonly known as the rough-stemmed dog rose, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and the plant produces small, red, hairy fruits called hips. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or scrubland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
