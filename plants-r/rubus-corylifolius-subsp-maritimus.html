Rubus corylifolius subsp. maritimus

Rubus corylifolius subsp.

maritimus, commonly known as the sea raspberry, is a shrub native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange when ripe. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in coastal landscaping for erosion control.
