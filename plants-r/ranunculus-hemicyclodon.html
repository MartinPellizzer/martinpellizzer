Ranunculus hemicyclodon

Ranunculus hemicyclodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically surrounded by a ring of sepals. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often described as being in a spiral arrangement along the stem. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
