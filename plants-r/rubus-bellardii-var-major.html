Rubus bellardii var. major

Rubus bellardii var. major, commonly known as the large-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries that are typically more robust than those of other Rubus species. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

The fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tart or astringent flavor.
