Rhamnus arguta var. velutina

Rhamnus arguta var. velutina, commonly known as the silky buckthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds and mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and dry woodlands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
