Rhamnidium acuminatum

Rhamnidium acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed rhamnus, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae, which includes species known for their often thorny structures and small, often clustered leaves. The plant typically has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical or ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that are a characteristic feature of the genus.

The plant is adapted to dry or seasonally dry environments and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.
