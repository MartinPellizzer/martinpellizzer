Ranunculus papillatus

Ranunculus papillatus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
