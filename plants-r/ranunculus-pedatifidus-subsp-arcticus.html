Ranunculus pedatifidus subsp. arcticus

Ranunculus pedatifidus subsp.

arcticus, commonly known as the Arctic buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to arctic and alpine regions. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cold, high-altitude environments.

The plant features bright yellow flowers with deeply divided, palmate leaves that are characteristic of the Ranunculaceae family. It is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.

This subspecies is often found in tundra and mountainous areas across the northern hemisphere.
