Rosa × candida

Rosa × candida, commonly known as the white garden rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa damascena and Rosa gallica.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that often have a slight pinkish tinge. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves. It is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.

Rosa × candida prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
