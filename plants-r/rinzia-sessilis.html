Rinzia sessilis

Rinzia sessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rinzia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and delicate white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands. It is an herbaceous perennial that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Rinzia sessilis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in ecological studies due to its adaptation to arid environments.
