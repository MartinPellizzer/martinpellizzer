Rhus aromatica var. flabelliformis

Rhus aromatica var. flabelliformis, commonly known as the fragrant sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately divided into narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy berries that are a key feature of its botanical appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open woods.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
