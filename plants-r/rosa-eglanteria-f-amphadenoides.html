Rosa eglanteria f. amphadenoides

Rosa eglanteria f. amphadenoides, commonly known as the broadleaf rose, is a hybrid plant that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a shrub with compound leaves that are broad and oval, often with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its stems are armed with thorns, which are a distinguishing feature of the species.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in various climates.
