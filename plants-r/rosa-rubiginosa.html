Rosa rubiginosa

Rosa rubiginosa, commonly known as the shrub rose or crimson rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and broad, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to crimson flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, hairy rose hip, which contains the seeds.

Rosa rubiginosa is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and arid environments.
