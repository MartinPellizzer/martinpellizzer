Rubus hedycarpus proles winteri

Rubus hedycarpus proles winteri, commonly known as the winter raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a reddish color in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
