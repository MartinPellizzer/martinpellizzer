Rhaphidophora hongkongensis

Rhaphidophora hongkongensis, commonly known as the Hong Kong arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, starchy tubers underground. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant.
