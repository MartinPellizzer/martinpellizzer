Ranunculus orozganicus

Ranunculus orozganicus, commonly known as the Andean buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are simple and trifoliate, while its flowers feature five to eight sepals that resemble petals.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
