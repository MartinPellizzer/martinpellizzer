Ruellia dioscoridis

Ruellia dioscoridis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a misty haze. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ruellia dioscoridis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
