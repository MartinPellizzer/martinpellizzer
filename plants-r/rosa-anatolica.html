Rosa anatolica

Rosa anatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian rose, is a species native to Turkey and surrounding regions.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and is characterized by its thorny stems. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery.

Rosa anatolica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for various climates.
