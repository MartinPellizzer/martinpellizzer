Rudbeckia laciniata var. humilis

Rudbeckia laciniata var. humilis, commonly known as the lesser cutleaf coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features large, dandelion-like yellow flowers surrounded by broad, deeply lobed green leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is characterized by finely cut, serrated leaves that are often covered in a soft, fuzzy texture.

The species is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
