Rosa calophylla

Rosa calophylla, commonly known as the Chilean rose, is a species of shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are dark green, glossy, and compound, typically consisting of five to seven leaflets. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to coastal and arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for various garden settings.
