Racosperma subflexuosum

Racosperma subflexuosum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Racosperma subflexuosum is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in soil stabilization and providing habitat for native wildlife.
