Rumicastrum spergularinum

Rumicastrum spergularinum, commonly known as the coastal bladderwrack, is a member of the family Fucaceae and is typically found in intertidal zones along rocky shores.

It is a brown seaweed that grows in dense, bushy mats, often attaching itself to rocks with a holdfast. The plant has a distinctive structure with a stipe and a blade, and its surface is covered in small, bladder-like air bladders that help it float. It thrives in cold, temperate waters and is often found in areas with high wave action.
