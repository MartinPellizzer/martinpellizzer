Ranunculus multidens

Ranunculus multidens, commonly known as the many-toothed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant features compound leaves with numerous serrated lobes, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators with their bright colors and nectar.
