Rubus multibracteatus

Rubus multibracteatus, commonly known as the many-bract raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
