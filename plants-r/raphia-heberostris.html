Raphia heberostris

Raphia heberostris, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a large, slow-growing palm native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by long, slender fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and potential uses in traditional practices.
