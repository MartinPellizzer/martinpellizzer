Rhodiola integrifolia subsp. neomexicana

Rhodiola integrifolia subsp.

neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the high elevations of the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

This subspecies thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. It is part of the Crassulaceae family, which includes many succulent plants.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its efficacy.
