Romanzoffia macounii

Romanzoffia macounii, commonly known as the Macoun's willow, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and wetlands. The plant features slender, arching branches and small, elliptical leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers are clustered in catkins, and it produces small, brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Romanzoffia macounii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
