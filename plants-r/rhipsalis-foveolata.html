Rhipsalis foveolata

Rhipsalis foveolata, commonly known as the silver leaf cactus, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems that are covered in small, white to grayish tubercles. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity.

Rhipsalis foveolata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and ease of care.
