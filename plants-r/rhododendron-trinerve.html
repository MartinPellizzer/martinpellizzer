Rhododendron trinerve

Rhododendron trinerve, commonly known as the three-veined rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas and southern China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with three prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are usually pink to red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
