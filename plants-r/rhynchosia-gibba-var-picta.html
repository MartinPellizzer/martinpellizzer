Rhynchosia gibba var. picta

Rhynchosia gibba var. picta, commonly known as the speckled cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often speckled pods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, curved pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

The varietal name "picta" refers to the unique, colorful markings on its pods, which can range from green to reddish-brown.
