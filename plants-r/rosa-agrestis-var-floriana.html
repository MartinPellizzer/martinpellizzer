Rosa agrestis var. floriana

Rosa agrestis var. floriana, commonly known as the wild garden rose, is a hybrid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping and floral arrangements.
