Rubus pungens var. ternatus

Rubus pungens var. ternatus, commonly known as the thorned blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into dark purple or black berries. It thrives in woodland edges and moist soils, often forming dense thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
