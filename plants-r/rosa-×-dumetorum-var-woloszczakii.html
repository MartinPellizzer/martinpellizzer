Rosa × dumetorum var. woloszczakii

Rosa × dumetorum var. woloszczakii, commonly known as the Woloszczakii rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It features dark green, slightly hairy leaves with a leathery texture and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is characterized by its dense growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in cooler climates.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species within the Rosa genus, contributing to the genetic diversity of cultivated roses.
