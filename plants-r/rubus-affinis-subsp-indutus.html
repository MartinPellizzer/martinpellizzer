Rubus affinis subsp. indutus

Rubus affinis subsp.

indutus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer.

The leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture. The plant produces small white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the edible fruits.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
