Rubus × hariacus

Rubus × hariacus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus fruticosus (bramble) and Rubus ideaus (red raspberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces berries that range from red to black in color.

This hybrid is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its edible fruits and ornamental qualities.
