Rubus euchlorostachys

Rubus euchlorostachys, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
