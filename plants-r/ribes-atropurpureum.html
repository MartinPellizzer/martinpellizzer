Ribes atropurpureum

Ribes atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically harvested in late summer. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often covered in a fine, downy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various wildlife species.
