Rheum persicum

Rheum persicum, commonly known as Persian rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the Caucasus and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and tall, branched flowering stems. The plant produces showy pink to red flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots and rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its medicinal value.
