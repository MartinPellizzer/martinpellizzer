Reseda glauca subsp. gredensis

Reseda glauca subsp.

gredensis, commonly known as the Gredensian whitethorn, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Europe. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its grayish-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit, making it well adapted to arid environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
