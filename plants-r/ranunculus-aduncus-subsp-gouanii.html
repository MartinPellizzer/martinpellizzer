Ranunculus aduncus subsp. gouanii

Ranunculus aduncus subsp.

gouanii, commonly known as the Gouan's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of southern Africa. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically arranged in a cup-like structure.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are alternate along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
