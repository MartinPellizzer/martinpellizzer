Rubus × semicanescens

Rubus × semicanescens, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often found in rocky or well-drained soils.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations across its natural range.
