Rubus palmatus var. eriocarpus

Rubus palmatus var. eriocarpus, commonly known as the prickly rose or thimbleberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and the leaves have a serrated edge.

The variety eriocarpus is distinguished by its hairy fruits, which provide a unique texture and appearance.
