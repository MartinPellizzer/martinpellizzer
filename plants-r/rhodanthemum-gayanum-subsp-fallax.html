Rhodanthemum gayanum subsp. fallax

Rhodanthemum gayanum subsp.

fallax, commonly known as the Sri Lankan daisy, is a flowering plant native to Sri Lanka. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest margins. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a central dark disc, and it produces small, hairy fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
