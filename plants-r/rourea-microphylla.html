Rourea microphylla

Rourea microphylla, commonly known as the silverleaf rourea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
