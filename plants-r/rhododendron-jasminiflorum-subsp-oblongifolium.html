Rhododendron jasminiflorum subsp. oblongifolium

Rhododendron jasminiflorum subsp.

oblongifolium, commonly known as the fragrant rhododendron, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from white to pink, often with a distinctive jasmine-like fragrance.

The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, with a deep green color and a waxy texture.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
