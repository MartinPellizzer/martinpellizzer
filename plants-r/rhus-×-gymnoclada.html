Rhus × gymnoclada

Rhus × gymnoclada, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is a hybrid between Rhus glabra (staghorn sumac) and Rhus aromatica (fragrant sumac), resulting in a plant with distinctive botanical features. The leaves are compound, typically consisting of 11–31 leaflets, and are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that mature in the fall.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
