Rudgea celastrinea

Rudgea celastrinea, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple to black berries that appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Rudgea celastrinea is valued for its ornamental berries and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
