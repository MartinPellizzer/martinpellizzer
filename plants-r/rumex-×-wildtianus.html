Rumex × wildtianus

Rumex × wildtianus, commonly known as the hybrid sorrel, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rumex acetosa (common sorrel) and Rumex acetobatus (water sorrel).

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and reddish veins, which are typical features of the Rumex genus. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Due to its hybrid nature, Rumex × wildtianus exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
