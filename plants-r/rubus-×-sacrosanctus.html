Rubus × sacrosanctus

Rubus × sacrosanctus, commonly known as the sacred raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically red and edible. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often found in rocky, well-drained soils. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its distinctive berries.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
