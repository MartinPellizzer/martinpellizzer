Retiniphyllum cataractae

Retiniphyllum cataractae, commonly known as the waterfall fern, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, cascading fronds that resemble flowing water, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires consistent humidity to flourish. Its unique morphology includes intricate, lacy leaves that are often used in horticultural displays.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
