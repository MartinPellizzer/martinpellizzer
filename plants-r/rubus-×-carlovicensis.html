Rubus × carlovicensis

Rubus × carlovicensis, commonly known as the Carlow raspberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with stems that die back each year and produce new growth from the base. The plant produces aggregate fruits, typically red or yellow, which are borne on the ends of its thorny stems. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
