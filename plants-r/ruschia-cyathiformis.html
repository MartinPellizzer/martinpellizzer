Ruschia cyathiformis

Ruschia cyathiformis, commonly known as the "Cup-shaped Ruschia," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine white powder, known as farina. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
