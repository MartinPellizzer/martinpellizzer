Rubus fructifer

Rubus fructifer, commonly known as the fruiting bramble, is a member of the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that produces small, edible berries, which are often used in culinary applications. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The berries are typically harvested in late summer and are rich in antioxidants and vitamin C.
