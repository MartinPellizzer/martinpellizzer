Rhynchosia pyramidalis

Rhynchosia pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 2 meters tall, characterized by its slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Rhynchosia pyramidalis is also valued in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
