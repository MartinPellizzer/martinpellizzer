Rabdosia koroensis

Rabdosia koroensis, commonly known as the Koroensis blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the island of Koro in the Japanese Ryukyu Islands.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rabdosia koroensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
