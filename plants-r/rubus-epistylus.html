Rubus epistylus

Rubus epistylus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

The plant thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in partial shade.
