Raphanus sativus convar. oleifer

Raphanus sativus convar. oleifer, commonly known as the black radish, is a biennial or annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its robust, fleshy root that can grow up to 30 cm in length, typically dark purple or black in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, and its leaves are alternate, lobed, and often covered in a waxy coating. The root is the primary edible part, valued for its crisp texture and slightly spicy flavor.

It thrives in cool climates and is cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
