Ruellia picta

Ruellia picta, commonly known as the painted ruellia, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that range from blue to purple and yellow, often with contrasting veins. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. Ruellia picta thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
