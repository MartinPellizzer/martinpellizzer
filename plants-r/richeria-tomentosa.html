Richeria tomentosa

Richeria tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly richeria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its hairy, grayish leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Richeria tomentosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and foliage.
