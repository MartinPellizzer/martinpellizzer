Rorippa prolifera

Rorippa prolifera, commonly known as the water pepper, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is typically found in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and ditches. The plant has alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family.

Rorippa prolifera is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate waterlogged conditions.
