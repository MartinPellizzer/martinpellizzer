Rubus glaucocaulis

Rubus glaucocaulis, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, dark red berries in the summer. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
