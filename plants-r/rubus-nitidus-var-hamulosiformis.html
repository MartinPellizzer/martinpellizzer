Rubus nitidus var. hamulosiformis

Rubus nitidus var. hamulosiformis, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. Its flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
