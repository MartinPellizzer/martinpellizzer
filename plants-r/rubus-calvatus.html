Rubus calvatus

Rubus calvatus, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are often used in jams and desserts due to their sweet, tangy flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry and blackberry species.
