Rubus lasiothyrsus var. flaccidiflorens

Rubus lasiothyrsus var. flaccidiflorens, commonly known as the soft-flowered raspberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the presence of prickly stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically reddish or yellowish in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually hairy on the underside.

This variety is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
