Rubus villosus var. glandulosus

Rubus villosus var. glandulosus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are aggregate berries that ripen to a dark red or black color in late summer.

This species is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
