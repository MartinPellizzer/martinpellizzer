Restio vacillans

Restio vacillans, commonly known as the wavering restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

Restio vacillans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in coastal gardens for its hardy and drought-resistant nature.
