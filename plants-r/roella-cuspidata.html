Roella cuspidata

Roella cuspidata, commonly known as the needlepod or slender needlepod, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, needle-like pods that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Roella cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
