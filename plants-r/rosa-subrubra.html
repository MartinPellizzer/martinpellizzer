Rosa subrubra

Rosa subrubra, commonly known as the red sandalwood rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa subrubra is a deciduous shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in arid and semi-arid regions.
