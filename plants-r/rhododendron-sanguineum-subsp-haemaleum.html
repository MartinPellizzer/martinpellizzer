Rhododendron sanguineum subsp. haemaleum

Rhododendron sanguineum subsp.

haemaleum, commonly known as the redleaf azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical and turn a striking red or orange color in the fall.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
