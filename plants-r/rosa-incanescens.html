Rosa incanescens

Rosa incanescens, commonly known as the white rose or the Mexican rose, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers. Rosa incanescens is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to drought.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal uses.
