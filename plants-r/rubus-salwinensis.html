Rubus salwinensis

Rubus salwinensis, commonly known as the Salween raspberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Myanmar, Thailand, and China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white or pale pink.

The berries, which ripen in late summer, are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
