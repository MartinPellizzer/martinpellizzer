Rubus tomophyllus

Rubus tomophyllus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species of brambles native to western North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple or black when mature. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Its berries are edible but have a tart flavor, making them popular in local culinary traditions.
