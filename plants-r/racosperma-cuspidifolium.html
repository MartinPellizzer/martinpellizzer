Racosperma cuspidifolium

Racosperma cuspidifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features slender, linear leaves with a pointed tip, giving it its specific epithet "cuspidifolium.". It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typical of the genus.

Racosperma cuspidifolium is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
