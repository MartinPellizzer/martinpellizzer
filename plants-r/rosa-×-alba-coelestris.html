Rosa × alba coelestris

Rosa × alba coelestris, commonly known as the White Fairy Rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from crosses between Rosa alba and Rosa canina.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a faintly fragrant scent. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its botanical features include a woody structure and a tendency to produce multiple flower buds throughout the growing season.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
