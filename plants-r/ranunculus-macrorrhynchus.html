Ranunculus macrorrhynchus

Ranunculus macrorrhynchus, commonly known as the large-beaked buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It features bright yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive, elongated beak-like structure at the base of the flower. The plant has smooth, lobed leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Ranunculus macrorrhynchus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
