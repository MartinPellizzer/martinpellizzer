Rubus corymbosus f. umbrosus

Rubus corymbosus f. umbrosus, commonly known as the shadow raspberry, is a botanical variant of the common blackberry.

It is characterized by its smaller, more delicate leaves and clusters of small, dark red berries. This form typically thrives in shaded environments, making it well-suited for woodland gardens. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic fragrance, particularly when the berries are mature.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to horticultural collections and ecological studies.
