Rosa × victoriana

Rosa × victoriana, commonly known as the Victoria rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from crosses between Rosa damascena and Rosa centifolia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that typically range from white to deep pink, with a high number of petals. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Rosa × victoriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in botanical gardens.
