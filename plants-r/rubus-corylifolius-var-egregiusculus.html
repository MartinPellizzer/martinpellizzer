Rubus corylifolius var. egregiusculus

Rubus corylifolius var. egregiusculus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to China.

It features large, trifoliate leaves with a serrated margin and a distinctive, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark red or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Rubus genus, though it is less commonly cultivated than other raspberries.
