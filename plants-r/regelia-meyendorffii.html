Regelia meyendorffii

Regelia meyendorffii, commonly known as the mountain regelia, is a rare and striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a perennial herb with thick, fleshy stems and large, waxy leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink during the spring and early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural habitat, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
