Rubus menkei subsp. pannosus

Rubus menkei subsp.

pannosus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of East Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus.
