Romulea longifolia

Romulea longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often violet or purple, tubular flowers. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Romulea longifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
