Rubus candicans subsp. constrictus

Rubus candicans subsp.

constrictus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
