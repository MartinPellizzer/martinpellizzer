Rubus mercieri f. schumensis

Rubus mercieri f. schumensis, commonly known as the Schumacher's blackberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
