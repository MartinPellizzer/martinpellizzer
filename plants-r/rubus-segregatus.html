Rubus segregatus

Rubus segregatus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
