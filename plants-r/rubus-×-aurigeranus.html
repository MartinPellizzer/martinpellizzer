Rubus × aurigeranus

Rubus × aurigeranus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus occidentalis (blackberry) and Rubus ursinus ( raspberry). The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, featuring thorny stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces aggregate fruits that are a mix of blackberry and raspberry characteristics.

The plant is valued for its edible berries, which are often used in culinary applications.
