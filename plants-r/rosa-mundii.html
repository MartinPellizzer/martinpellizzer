Rosa mundii

Rosa mundii, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branched.

Rosa mundii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its nocturnal blooms.
