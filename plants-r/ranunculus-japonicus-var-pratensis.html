Ranunculus japonicus var. pratensis

Ranunculus japonicus var. pratensis, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a nectar source for pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
