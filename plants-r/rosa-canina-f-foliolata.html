Rosa canina f. foliolata

Rosa canina f. foliolata, commonly known as the leafy dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its broader, more leathery leaves compared to the typical form. The plant produces fragrant pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit, known as a hip, is orange-red and contains several seeds.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
