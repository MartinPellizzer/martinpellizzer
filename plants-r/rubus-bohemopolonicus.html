Rubus bohemopolonicus

Rubus bohemopolonicus, commonly known as the Bohemian blackberry, is a shrub native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
