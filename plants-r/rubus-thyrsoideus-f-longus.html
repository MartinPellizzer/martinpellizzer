Rubus thyrsoideus f. longus

Rubus thyrsoideus f. longus, commonly known as the long-stalked blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its elongated pedicels.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, wooded areas. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and its stems are armed with stout, curved prickles.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and edible fruits.
