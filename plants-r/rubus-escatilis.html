Rubus escatilis

Rubus escatilis, commonly known as the scarlet bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red berries. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are white with five petals and appear in the spring, followed by the development of its edible, though tart, berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
