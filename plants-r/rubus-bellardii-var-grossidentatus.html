Rubus bellardii var. grossidentatus

Rubus bellardii var. grossidentatus, commonly known as the broad-lobed blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with broad, serrated lobes. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its edible berries and its role in ecological habitats.
