Rubus sphenoiphyllos

Rubus sphenoiphyllos, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive pointed tip. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
