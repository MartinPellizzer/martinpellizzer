Rosa nipponensis

Rosa nipponensis, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a species native to Japan and Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink or white, with a strong, sweet fragrance. Rosa nipponensis blooms in late spring to early summer, producing semi-double to double flowers.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
