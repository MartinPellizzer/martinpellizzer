Rubus franchetianus var. pubescens

Rubus franchetianus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) stems and leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are white and have five petals, typical of the Rubus genus.

It is often found in forest edges and rocky slopes, thriving in temperate climates.
