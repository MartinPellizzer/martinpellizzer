Rubus glaucifolius

Rubus glaucifolius, commonly known as the glaucous-leafed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but not as widely cultivated as other blackberry varieties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its thorny stems and showy white flowers make it a notable plant in its native habitat.
