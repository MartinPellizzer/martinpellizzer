Rosa striata

Rosa striata, commonly known as the striated rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. It typically grows in alpine or subalpine environments, thriving in cooler climates with well-drained soil.

Rosa striata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
