Rinorea pulleana

Rinorea pulleana, commonly known as the purple leaf rinorea, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet leaves, which are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface depending on the variety.

Rinorea pulleana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
