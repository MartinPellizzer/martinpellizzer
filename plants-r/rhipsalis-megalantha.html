Rhipsalis megalantha

Rhipsalis megalantha, commonly known as the giant mistletoe cactus, is a climbing cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its unique appearance and growth habit make it a distinctive species within the Cactaceae family.
