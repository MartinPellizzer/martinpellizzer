Rubus tereticaulis var. armaticaulis

Rubus tereticaulis var. armaticaulis, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thin, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and riparian areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubiaceae family.
