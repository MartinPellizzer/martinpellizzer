Rosa serrae

Rosa serrae, commonly known as the serrated rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring. Rosa serrae is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
