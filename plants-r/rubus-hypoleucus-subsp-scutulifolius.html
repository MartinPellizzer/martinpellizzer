Rubus hypoleucus subsp. scutulifolius

Rubus hypoleucus subsp.

scutulifolius, commonly known as the western blackberry, is a shrub native to western North America. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and aggregate fruits.

The leaves are typically alternate, with a distinctive scutiform (shield-shaped) leaflet at the base. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in jams and wines.

It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed areas.
