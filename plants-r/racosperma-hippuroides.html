Racosperma hippuroides

Racosperma hippuroides, commonly known as the horse-riding acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by flattened, leathery pods. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Racosperma hippuroides is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in revegetation projects.
