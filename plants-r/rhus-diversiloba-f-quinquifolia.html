Rhus diversiloba f. quinquifolia

Rhus diversiloba f. quinquifolia, commonly known as the five-leaf sumac, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which typically consist of five leaflets, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are a distinctive feature. Its foliage and fruit provide food for various birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.
