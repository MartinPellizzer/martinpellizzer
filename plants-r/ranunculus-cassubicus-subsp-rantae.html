Ranunculus cassubicus subsp. rantae

Ranunculus cassubicus subsp.

rantae, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic sap.

The subspecies rantae is distinguished by its specific morphology and habitat preferences compared to other members of the species.
