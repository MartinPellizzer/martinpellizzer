Rumex crispus var. uliginosus

Rumex crispus var. uliginosus, commonly known as the bog sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-edged leaves that are often reddish or purple in color. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in acidic, waterlogged soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
