Rostellularia sarmentosa

Rostellularia sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping rostellularia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows along the ground, forming dense mats with slender, trailing stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often with white or pale yellow petals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

Rostellularia sarmentosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.
