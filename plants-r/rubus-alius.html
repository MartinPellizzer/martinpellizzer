Rubus alius

Rubus alius, commonly known as the alpine bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are often red or black in color. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Rubus alius plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.
