Rubus serpens var. humerosus

Rubus serpens var. humerosus, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its stout, thorny stems and large, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
