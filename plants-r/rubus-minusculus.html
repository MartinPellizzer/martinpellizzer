Rubus minusculus

Rubus minusculus, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, with thorny stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Rubus minusculus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
