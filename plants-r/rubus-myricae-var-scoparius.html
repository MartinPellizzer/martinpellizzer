Rubus myricae var. scoparius

Rubus myricae var. scoparius, commonly known as the blackberry cane.

This variant is a shrub native to parts of North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible berries. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its berries are valued for their flavor and are used in various culinary applications.
