Rhamnidium rocanum

Rhamnidium rocanum, commonly known as the golden spire plant, is a unique member of the Rhamnaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescences that resemble spires, often displaying vibrant yellow or orange hues. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a glossy appearance.

Rhamnidium rocanum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
