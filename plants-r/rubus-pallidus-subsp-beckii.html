Rubus pallidus subsp. beckii

Rubus pallidus subsp.

beckii, commonly known as the pale raspberry. It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its slender stems and small, pale pink to white flowers.

The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically less flavorful compared to other raspberry species. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its leaves are compound, with three leaflets, and the plant is often found in dry, well-drained soils.
