Rubus laciniatus subsp. selmeri

Rubus laciniatus subsp.

selmeri, commonly known as the Selmer's blackberry, is a shrub native to southern China. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus.
