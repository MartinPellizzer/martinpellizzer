Rubus ulrikeae

Rubus ulrikeae, commonly known as the Ulrike's raspberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries similar to other raspberries.

However, it is a less common species and has limited cultivation due to its specific habitat requirements.
