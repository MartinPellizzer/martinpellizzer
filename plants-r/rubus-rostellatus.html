Rubus rostellatus

Rubus rostellatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Rubus rostellatus is often found growing in clusters, forming dense thickets that provide habitat for various wildlife species.
