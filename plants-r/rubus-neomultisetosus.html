Rubus neomultisetosus

Rubus neomultisetosus, commonly known as the New Zealand bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by red, juicy berries. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including forests and shrublands, and is often found in moist, temperate climates.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain areas outside its native range.
