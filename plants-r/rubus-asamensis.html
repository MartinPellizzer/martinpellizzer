Rubus asamensis

Rubus asamensis, commonly known as the Asamensis blackberry, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisine. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and the plant has thorny stems.

Rubus asamensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.
