Ranunculus crucilobus

Ranunculus crucilobus, commonly known as the cross-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or orange flowers. The plant features deeply lobed, palmate leaves that are typically divided into five or more segments, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically solitary and have five petals, with numerous stamens surrounding a central pistil.

Ranunculus crucilobus thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
