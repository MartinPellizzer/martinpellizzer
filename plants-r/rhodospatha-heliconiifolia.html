Rhodospatha heliconiifolia

Rhodospatha heliconiifolia, commonly known as the Heliconia rosea, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant has large, elongated leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, clustered flowers surrounded by brightly colored, modified leaves called bracts.

Rhodospatha heliconiifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
