Rhododendron × hannoense

Rhododendron × hannoense, commonly known as the Hannoense rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron catawbiense and Rhododendron maximum.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant is evergreen, with leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in temperate climates.
