Rubus yuliensis

Rubus yuliensis, commonly known as the Yuli blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their edible fruits. The plant produces small, dark berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
