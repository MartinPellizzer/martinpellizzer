Rubus micans microgene lacteicomus

Rubus micans microgene lacteicomus, commonly known as the milky raspberry, is a shrub characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves.

It produces small, usually white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in the axils of the leaves. The plant is notable for its milky sap, which exudes when the stems or leaves are damaged. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
