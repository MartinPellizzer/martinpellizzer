Rosa picta

Rosa picta, commonly known as the painted rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored petals that range from white to red, pink, and yellow. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The flowers are typically borne on slender stems and are known for their ornamental value in gardens.

Rosa picta is also valued for its aromatic foliage and can be cultivated as a decorative shrub.
