Raphanus sativus var. brunneus

Raphanus sativus var. brunneus, commonly known as the black radish, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its deep, dark purple to black roots, which are typically harvested for culinary use. The plant produces yellow flowers in a raceme, and its leaves are edible, though often less commonly consumed than the roots. It thrives in cool climates and is often grown in temperate regions for its unique flavor and nutritional value.

The variety is valued for its distinct appearance and use in various cuisines around the world.
