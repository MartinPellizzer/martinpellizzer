Rhytidophyllum tomentosum var. appendiculatum

Rhytidophyllum tomentosum var. appendiculatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf tree, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinctive waxy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
