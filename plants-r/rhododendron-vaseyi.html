Rhododendron vaseyi

Rhododendron vaseyi, commonly known as the Vasey's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking backdrop to its vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron vaseyi prefers acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates.
