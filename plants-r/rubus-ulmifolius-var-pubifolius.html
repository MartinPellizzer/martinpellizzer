Rubus ulmifolius var. pubifolius

Rubus ulmifolius var. pubifolius, commonly known as the pubescent blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces aggregate fruits, typically dark purple to black in color, that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

This variant is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its culinary uses.
