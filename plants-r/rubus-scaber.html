Rubus scaber

Rubus scaber, commonly known as rough raspberry, is a species of brambles native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and garden settings.
