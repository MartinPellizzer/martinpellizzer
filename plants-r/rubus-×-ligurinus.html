Rubus × ligurinus

Rubus × ligurinus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and coastal landscapes.
