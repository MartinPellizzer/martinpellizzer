Rumex scutatus subsp. gallaecicus

Rumex scutatus subsp.

gallaecicus, commonly known as the Galician dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered with dense, glandular hairs, giving them a rough texture.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a loose spike, which are typically borne on slender, leafy stalks. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and riverbanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Polygonaceae family.
