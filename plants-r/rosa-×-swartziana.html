Rosa × swartziana

Rosa × swartziana, commonly known as the Swartz rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is a deciduous shrub native to China, characterized by its vigorous growth and thorny stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers in the summer months. Its leaves are dark green, serrated, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa × swartziana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness in various climates.
