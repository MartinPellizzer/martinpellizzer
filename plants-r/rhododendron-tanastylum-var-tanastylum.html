Rhododendron tanastylum var. tanastylum

Rhododendron tanastylum var. tanastylum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in Nepal and northern India.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and is adapted to cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include leathery leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a hardy shrub suitable for horticultural cultivation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
