Rosa tomentella f. typica

Rosa tomentella f. typica, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white fuzzy foliage, which provides a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative shrub.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in colder climates.
