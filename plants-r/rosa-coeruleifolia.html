Rosa coeruleifolia

Rosa coeruleifolia, commonly known as the blueleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and showy pink to purple flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant produces fragrant blooms throughout the summer months.

Its unique coloration and ornamental value make it a sought-after addition to gardens and botanical collections.
