Reseda podolica

Reseda podolica, commonly known as the meadow catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching a height of about 20 to 60 centimeters. The seeds are small and enclosed in a capsule, which splits open to release the seeds when mature.
