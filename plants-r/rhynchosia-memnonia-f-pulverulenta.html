Rhynchosia memnonia f. pulverulenta

Rhynchosia memnonia f. pulverulenta, commonly known as the dust-flower or dusty rhynchosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, finely hairy, papery flowers that resemble dust. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its small, yellowish flowers are borne in racemes and are accompanied by small, stipulate leaves.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
