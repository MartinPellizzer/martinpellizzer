Rosa × subglabra

Rosa × subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and foliage, which distinguish it from many other rose species. The plant produces fragrant flowers in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, typically in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Rosa × subglabra is native to various regions of Europe and has been widely introduced to other parts of the world.
