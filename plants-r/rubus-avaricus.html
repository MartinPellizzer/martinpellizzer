Rubus avaricus

Rubus avaricus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

Rubus avaricus is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local wildlife.
