Rosa arvensis var. atrata

Rosa arvensis var. atrata, commonly known as the blackberry rose, is a variant of the field rose that exhibits distinct botanical features.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which contrast with its green, velvety foliage. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple or black in color, similar to true blackberries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential culinary uses.
