Rhododendron harrovianum

Rhododendron harrovianum, commonly known as the Harrovian rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shady, moist environments. Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant floral display, which ranges from pink to deep red.

Rhododendron harrovianum is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
