Rubus rivularis var. hirtiformis

Rubus rivularis var. hirtiformis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
