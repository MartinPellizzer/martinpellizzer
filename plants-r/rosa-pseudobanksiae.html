Rosa pseudobanksiae

Rosa pseudobanksiae, commonly known as the Banksia rose, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with woody stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with a prominent central style, and are arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Rosa pseudobanksiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
