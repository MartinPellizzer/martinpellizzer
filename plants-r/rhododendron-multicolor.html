Rhododendron multicolor

Rhododendron multicolor, commonly known as the multicolored rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, white, and purple. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The blooming period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to the landscape.
