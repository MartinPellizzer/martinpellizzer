Rubus caesius var. ligerinus

Rubus caesius var. ligerinus, commonly known as the red raspberry.

This variant is characterized by its erect or semi-erect growth habit and produces red, hairy fruits. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

The plant is valued for its edible berries and is sometimes cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
