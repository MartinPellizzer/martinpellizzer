Rulingia cristifolia

Rulingia cristifolia, commonly known as the crested rulingia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palm-like leaves and distinctive, spiky inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
