Rubus cercophyllus

Rubus cercophyllus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are pale pink to white.

It is valued for its hardiness and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
