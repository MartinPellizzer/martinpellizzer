Rubus persolutus

Rubus persolutus, commonly known as the Persian blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
