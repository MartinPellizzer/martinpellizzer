Rhaphiolepis seguinii

Rhaphiolepis seguinii, commonly known as the Japanese rosebay, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and turn vibrant shades of red in autumn.

Rhaphiolepis seguinii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
