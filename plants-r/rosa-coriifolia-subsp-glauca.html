Rosa coriifolia subsp. glauca

Rosa coriifolia subsp.

glauca, commonly known as the blueleaf shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its small, pale pink to white flowers and its distinctive, glaucous blue-green foliage.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has a sprawling, shrub-like habit. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating, contributing to its unique appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
