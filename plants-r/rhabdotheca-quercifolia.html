Rhabdotheca quercifolia

Rhabdotheca quercifolia, commonly known as the oak-leaved rhabdotheca, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its lobed, oak-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads. Its botanical features include hairy stems and a compact growth habit.

It is often found in regions with low precipitation and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
