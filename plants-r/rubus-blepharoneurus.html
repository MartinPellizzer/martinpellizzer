Rubus blepharoneurus

Rubus blepharoneurus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The botanical features of Rubus blepharoneurus make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
