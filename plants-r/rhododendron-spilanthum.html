Rhododendron spilanthum

Rhododendron spilanthum, commonly known as the mountain rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It typically grows in high-altitude regions, preferring cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, pink to white flowers in the spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.

Rhododendron spilanthum is also known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
