Rabdosia hispida

Rabdosia hispida, commonly known as hairy rabdosia, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters.

Rabdosia hispida is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
