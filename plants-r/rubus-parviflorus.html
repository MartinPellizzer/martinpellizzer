Rubus parviflorus

Rubus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible blackberries in the summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in thorns.

The plant is valued for its fruit and its role in supporting local wildlife.
