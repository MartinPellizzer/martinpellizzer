Rhamnus serpyllacea

Rhamnus serpyllacea, commonly known as the common buckthorn, is a shrub native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring. The plant is known for its reddish-brown, ribbed fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rhamnus serpyllacea is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
