Ranunculus oligocarpus

Ranunculus oligocarpus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow petals and a hairy stem that helps it retain heat in cold environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in well-drained, acidic conditions. Its flowers bloom in early summer, and the plant is often found in meadows and open slopes at high altitudes.

Ranunculus oligocarpus is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and ecological importance to alpine ecosystems.
