Rubus × parviflorus var. discolor

Rubus × parviflorus var. discolor, commonly known as the pink raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, pinkish-red berries that are typically smaller than those of common raspberries. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.
