Rubus villosus var. vulpinus

Rubus villosus var. vulpinus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, bristly stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained environments.

This species is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.
