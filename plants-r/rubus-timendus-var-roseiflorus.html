Rubus timendus var. roseiflorus

Rubus timendus var. roseiflorus, commonly known as the rose-flowered bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are aggregate fruits formed from multiple drupelets.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
