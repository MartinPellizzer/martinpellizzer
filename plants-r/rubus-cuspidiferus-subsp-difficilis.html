Rubus cuspidiferus subsp. difficilis

Rubus cuspidiferus subsp.

difficilis, commonly known as the Appalachian blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer.

The plant has thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and culinary purposes.
