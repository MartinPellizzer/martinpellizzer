Rhododendron × inundatum

Rhododendron × inundatum, commonly known as the Japanese laurel.

It is a hybrid shrub native to Japan, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its floral display, which occurs in late spring, features clusters of pink to red blooms.

Rhododendron × inundatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
