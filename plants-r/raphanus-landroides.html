Raphanus landroides

Raphanus landroides, commonly known as the desert radish, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a taproot that can grow quite deep to access water in dry soils. Its leaves are usually small and fleshy, adapted to reduce water loss.

Raphanus landroides is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is well-suited to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments.
