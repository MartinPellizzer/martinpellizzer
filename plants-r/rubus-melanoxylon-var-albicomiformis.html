Rubus melanoxylon var. albicomiformis

Rubus melanoxylon var. albicomiformis, commonly known as the white-leafed blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its distinctive white or pale-colored leaves, contrasting with the typical dark foliage of the species. It is a shrub native to certain regions of South America, particularly in the Andean foothills. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species, sharing similar structural features and reproductive characteristics.
