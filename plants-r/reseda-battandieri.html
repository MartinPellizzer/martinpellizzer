Reseda battandieri

Reseda battandieri, commonly known as the purple tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called cymes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Reseda battandieri is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of yellow dye.
