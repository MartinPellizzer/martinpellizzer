Rhododendron × diphrocalyx

Rhododendron × diphrocalyx, commonly known as the hybrid rhododendron, is a cross between Rhododendron daphnifolium and Rhododendron aglaia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
