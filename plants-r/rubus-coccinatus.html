Rubus coccinatus

Rubus coccinatus, commonly known as the scarlet raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its red, hairy stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
