Rumohra globisora

Rumohra globisora, commonly known as the globular sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along streambanks.

Rumohra globisora plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
