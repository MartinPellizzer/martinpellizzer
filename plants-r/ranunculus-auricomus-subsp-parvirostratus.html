Ranunculus auricomus subsp. parvirostratus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

parvirostratus, commonly known as the small-beaked buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features striking yellow flowers with five to nine broadly lanceolate petals, often with a distinctive beak-like tip.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided with serrated edges, and it produces achenes as its fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
