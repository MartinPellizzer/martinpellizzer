Rubus pratensis

Rubus pratensis, commonly known as the meadow raspberry, is a perennial shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Rubus pratensis plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
