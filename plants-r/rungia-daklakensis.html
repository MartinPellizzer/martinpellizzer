Rungia daklakensis

Rungia daklakensis, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple and pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture, and its stems are woody and often twining. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit, which is edible, is a small, green berry that turns yellow when ripe.
