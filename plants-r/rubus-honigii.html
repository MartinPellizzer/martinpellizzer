Rubus honigii

Rubus honigii, commonly known as the Honig's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
