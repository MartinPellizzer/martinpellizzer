Ranunculus cuneilobus var. distrias

Ranunculus cuneilobus var. distrias, commonly known as the variable cuneate buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow to orange flowers with numerous stamens. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, this plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils. It typically grows in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
