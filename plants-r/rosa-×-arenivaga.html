Rosa × arenivaga

Rosa × arenivaga, commonly known as the sand rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is native to regions with arid or semi-arid climates. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive yellow center. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments.

Rosa × arenivaga is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to harsh conditions.
