Roegneria behmii

Roegneria behmii, commonly known as the Bechmann's false brome, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Roegneria behmii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky and arid landscapes.
