Ruyschiana pinnata

Ruyschiana pinnata, commonly known as the featherleaf ruyschiana, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to South Africa and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant features slender, fern-like leaves that give it its common name, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in daisy-like clusters. It is drought-tolerant and well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.

Ruyschiana pinnata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
