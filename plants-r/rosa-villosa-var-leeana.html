Rosa villosa var. leeana

Rosa villosa var. leeana, commonly known as the Leeana rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features thorned stems and is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to lanceolate leaflets that are often covered in fine, white hairs. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
