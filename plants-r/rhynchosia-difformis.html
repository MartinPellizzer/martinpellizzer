Rhynchosia difformis

Rhynchosia difformis, commonly known as the variable cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain several seeds. It is often grown for its edible beans and is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
