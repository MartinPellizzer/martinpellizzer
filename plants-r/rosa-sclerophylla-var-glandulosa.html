Rosa sclerophylla var. glandulosa

Rosa sclerophylla var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular-leaved rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, woody stems covered in thorny branches, typical of many rose species. The leaves are small, leathery, and glandular, giving the plant its specific variety name. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters and attracting pollinators.

This plant is well adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
