Rubus × apicatus

Rubus × apicatus, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to regions of North America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries.

The trailing blackberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in culinary applications.
