Rosa rubiginosa f. flagellaris

Rosa rubiginosa f. flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate rose, is a hybrid form of the shrub rose.

It is characterized by its slender, whip-like stems that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers with a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in coastal and rocky environments.
