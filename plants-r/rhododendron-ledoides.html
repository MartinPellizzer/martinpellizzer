Rhododendron ledoides

Rhododendron ledoides, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments at high elevations, often in forested areas with acidic soil. Its leaves are leathery and oval-shaped, providing a dense canopy that helps retain moisture.

Rhododendron ledoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
