Rotala petaloidea

Rotala petaloidea, commonly known as the featherleaf watermilfoil, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often submerged or floating.

Rotala petaloidea is valued in aquariums for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
