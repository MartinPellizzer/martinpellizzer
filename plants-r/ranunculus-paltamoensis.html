Ranunculus paltamoensis

Ranunculus paltamoensis, commonly known as the Paltamo buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in environments with high humidity. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a bright green color and a hairy surface.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
