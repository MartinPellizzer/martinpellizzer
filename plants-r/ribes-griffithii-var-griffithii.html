Ribes griffithii var. griffithii

Ribes griffithii var. griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and features compound leaves with 5–9 elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible berries that ripen to a bright red in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
