Rebutia tuberosa

Rebutia tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems that often bear spines and are covered in a layer of white wax. The plant produces colorful, showy flowers that range from yellow to red, typically blooming in the spring. Its tuberous roots allow it to store water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Rebutia tuberosa is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
