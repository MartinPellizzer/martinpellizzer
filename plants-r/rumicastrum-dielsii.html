Rumicastrum dielsii

Rumicastrum dielsii, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub with distinctive, glandular, and often sticky leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which attract various pollinators. Its stems and leaves contain milky sap, a characteristic feature of the milkweed genus.

Rumicastrum dielsii is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
