Rudbeckia fulgida var. deamii

Rudbeckia fulgida var. deamii, commonly known as the copperleaf coneflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, bright yellow to orange petals surrounding a dark brown central disk, giving it a bold and eye-catching appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Rudbeckia fulgida var. deamii is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
