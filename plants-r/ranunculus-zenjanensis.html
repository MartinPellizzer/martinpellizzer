Ranunculus zenjanensis

Ranunculus zenjanensis, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

Ranunculus zenjanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
