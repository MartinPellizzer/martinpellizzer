Rheum pichonii

Rheum pichonii, commonly known as Pichon's rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and tall, reddish stems. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer. It is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
