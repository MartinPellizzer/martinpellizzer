Rhamnus hispanorum

Rhamnus hispanorum, commonly known as Spanish buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its rapid growth and hardy nature make it a common weed in disturbed habitats.
