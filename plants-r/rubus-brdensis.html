Rubus brdensis

Rubus brdensis, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, edible blackberries that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, wooded areas.

Rubus brdensis plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and insects.
