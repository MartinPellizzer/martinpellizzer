Rubus argenteus microgene gymnothyrsus

Rubus argenteus microgene gymnothyrsus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible. It has a woody stem with thorny growth patterns typical of the Rubus genus.

The leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy and serrated.
