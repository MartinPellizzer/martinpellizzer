Rhagodia chenopodioides

Rhagodia chenopodioides, commonly known as the saltbush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to saline soils and is often found in coastal and inland salt marshes.

Rhagodia chenopodioides plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
