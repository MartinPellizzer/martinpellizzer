Ranunculus transiliensis

Ranunculus transiliensis, commonly known as the Transylvanian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are lobed and hairy, and it produces a hollow, spiny stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
