Ribes peruvianum var. minus

Ribes peruvianum var. minus, commonly known as the small gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Andes regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, tart berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in culinary applications.
