Rhynchosia albonitens

Rhynchosia albonitens, commonly known as the white-veined cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with white veins and yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, edible seeds similar to those of other cowpea species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its nutritional value.

Rhynchosia albonitens is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
