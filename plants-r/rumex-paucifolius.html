Rumex paucifolius

Rumex paucifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, stream banks, and in meadows. The plant features upright, ribbed stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a waxy coating. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in dense clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rumex paucifolius is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
