Rubus eriostachys

Rubus eriostachys, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically not eaten by humans but are consumed by wildlife. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountain ranges.

The plant's thorny stems and dense growth habit make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
