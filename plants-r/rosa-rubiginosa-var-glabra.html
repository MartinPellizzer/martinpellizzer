Rosa rubiginosa var. glabra

Rosa rubiginosa var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth rose or the shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and clusters of fragrant, pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces edible hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardy nature in temperate climates.
