Rubus × albifolius

Rubus × albifolius, commonly known as the white-leaf bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale green foliage, which sets it apart from other bramble species. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Botanically, it exhibits traits of its parent species, combining features of both Rubus idaeus and Rubus argutus.
