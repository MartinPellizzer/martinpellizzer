Rubus jamaicensis floribundus

Rubus jamaicensis floribundus, commonly known as the Jamaican raspberry or evergreen blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often dark red or black when mature. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and is known for its aromatic, sometimes fragrant flowers.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some uses in traditional medicine.
