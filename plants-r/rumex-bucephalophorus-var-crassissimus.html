Rumex bucephalophorus var. crassissimus

Rumex bucephalophorus var. crassissimus, commonly known as the broad-leaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and along stream banks. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, waxy texture and prominent veins. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, terminal spikes, producing a distinctive reddish-brown color.

The species is notable for its robust growth and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
