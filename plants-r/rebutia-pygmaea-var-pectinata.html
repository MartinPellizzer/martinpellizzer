Rebutia pygmaea var. pectinata

Rebutia pygmaea var. pectinata, commonly known as the Pectinata Rebutia, is a compact cactus native to South America.

It features short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in dense, white to pale green spines. The plant produces clusters of small, vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
