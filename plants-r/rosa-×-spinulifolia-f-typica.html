Rosa × spinulifolia f. typica

Rosa × spinulifolia f. typica, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, typically in late spring to early summer. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
