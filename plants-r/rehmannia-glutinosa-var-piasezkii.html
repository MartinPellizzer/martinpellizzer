Rehmannia glutinosa var. piasezkii

Rehmannia glutinosa var. piasezkii, commonly known as the sticky rehmannia, is a variant of the Chinese herb Rehmannia glutinosa.

It is a perennial herb native to China, typically found in forested areas and mountainous regions. The plant features thick, fleshy roots that are the primary part used medicinally. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with a sticky substance, which contributes to its common name.

This variant is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
