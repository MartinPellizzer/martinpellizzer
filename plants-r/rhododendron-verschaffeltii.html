Rhododendron verschaffeltii

Rhododendron verschaffeltii, commonly known as the Vanoverberghe rhododendron, is a species native to China, specifically the Yunnan province.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its botanical features include showy inflorescences and a preference for acidic, well-drained soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical gardens.
