Rosa × dumetorum subvar. sphaerocarpa

Rosa × dumetorum subvar. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the creeping white shrub rose, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, characterized by its low, spreading growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in thorns.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and floral display.
