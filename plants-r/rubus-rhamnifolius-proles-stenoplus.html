Rubus rhamnifolius proles stenoplus

Rubus rhamnifolius proles stenoplus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and small, aggregate fruits that are typically black when ripe. The leaves are narrow and serrated, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and hedgerows.

This plant is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
