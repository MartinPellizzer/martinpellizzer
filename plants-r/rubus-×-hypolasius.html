Rubus × hypolasius

Rubus × hypolasius, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus pectoralis.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas where it forms dense thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
