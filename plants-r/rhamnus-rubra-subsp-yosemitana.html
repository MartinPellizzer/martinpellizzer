Rhamnus rubra subsp. yosemitana

Rhamnus rubra subsp.

yosemitana, commonly known as the Yosemite buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Sierra Nevada mountains of California. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed coniferous forests.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
