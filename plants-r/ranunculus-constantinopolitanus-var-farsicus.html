Ranunculus constantinopolitanus var. farsicus

Ranunculus constantinopolitanus var. farsicus, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, delicate blooms.
