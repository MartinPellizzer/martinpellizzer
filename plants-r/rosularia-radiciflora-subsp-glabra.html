Rosularia radiciflora subsp. glabra

Rosularia radiciflora subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth-rooted rosularia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its subspecies name, *glabra*, refers to its smooth, hairless appearance. Rosularia radiciflora subsp.

glabra is well adapted to harsh environments, with thick leaves and a deep root system for water storage and drought tolerance.
