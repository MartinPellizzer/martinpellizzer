Rosa aucupariifolia

Rosa aucupariifolia, commonly known as the snowbush rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, alpine conditions. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a delicate floral display to its foliage. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems.

Rosa aucupariifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant in colder climates.
