Rubus macrostachys var. strigosissimus

Rubus macrostachys var. strigosissimus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
