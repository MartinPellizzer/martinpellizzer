Rodriguezia laxiflora

Rodriguezia laxiflora, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant has a complex root system that anchors it to submerged substrates in slow-moving waters. Its fronds are divided into numerous small, delicate pinnae, which help in gas exchange and photosynthesis.

Rodriguezia laxiflora plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
