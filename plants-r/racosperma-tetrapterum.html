Racosperma tetrapterum

Racosperma tetrapterum, commonly known as the four-winged acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Racosperma tetrapterum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
