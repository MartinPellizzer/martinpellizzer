Rosa micrantha var. anisocantha

Rosa micrantha var. anisocantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved shrub rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with a slightly serrated margin.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
