Rubus roseus var. roseus

Rubus roseus var. roseus, commonly known as the rosehip plant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red berries known as rosehips. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the edible seeds found within the hips. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.

The rosehips are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
