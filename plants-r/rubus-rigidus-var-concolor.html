Rubus rigidus var. concolor

Rubus rigidus var. concolor, commonly known as the smooth blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its smooth, non-bristly stems and leaves, distinguishing it from other Rubus species. It produces dark purple to black berries that are typically smaller than those of the common blackberry. The plant is native to parts of North America and thrives in temperate climates.

It is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
