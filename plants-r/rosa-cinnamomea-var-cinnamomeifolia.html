Rosa cinnamomea var. cinnamomeifolia

Rosa cinnamomea var. cinnamomeifolia, commonly known as the cinnamon rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, arching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as ornamental plants in gardens.

The variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
