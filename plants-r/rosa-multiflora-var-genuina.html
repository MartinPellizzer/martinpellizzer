Rosa multiflora var. genuina

Rosa multiflora var. genuina, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features fragrant, semi-double flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has arching, thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves that provide dense foliage. It is often used in hedges or as a specimen plant due to its ornamental value.

The botanical characteristics of this variety make it a popular choice in landscaping and garden design.
