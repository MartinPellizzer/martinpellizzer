Rubus callimorphus

Rubus callimorphus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black fruits. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are typically white or pale pink.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them a valuable resource for both wildlife and humans.
