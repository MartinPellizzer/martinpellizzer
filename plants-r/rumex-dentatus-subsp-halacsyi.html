Rumex dentatus subsp. halacsyi

Rumex dentatus subsp.

halacsyi, commonly known as the Hungarian dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its smooth, ribbed stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense, terminal clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
