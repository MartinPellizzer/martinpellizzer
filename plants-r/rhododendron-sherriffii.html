Rhododendron sherriffii

Rhododendron sherriffii, commonly known as the Sherriff's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often adorned with a distinctive purple or red band at the base.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in specific ecological niches.
