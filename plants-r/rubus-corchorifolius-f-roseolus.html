Rubus corchorifolius f. roseolus

Rubus corchorifolius f. roseolus, commonly known as the pink-leafed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive pink or red foliage.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Japan. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically smaller than those of the common blackberry. Its ornamental value lies in its unique leaf coloration, which adds visual interest to gardens.

This form is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its edible fruits.
