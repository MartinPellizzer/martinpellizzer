Rosa subserrulata

Rosa subserrulata, commonly known as the serrated rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, and it is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Rosa subserrulata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
