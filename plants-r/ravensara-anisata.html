Ravensara anisata

Ravensara anisata, commonly known as mountain pepper, is a small evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces dark blue, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine. Its leaves contain compounds with antimicrobial properties, making it valuable in both ecological and pharmaceutical contexts.

Ravensara anisata plays an important role in the unique flora of New Caledonia, contributing to the region's biodiversity.
