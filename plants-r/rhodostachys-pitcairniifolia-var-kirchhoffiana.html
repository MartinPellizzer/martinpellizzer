Rhodostachys pitcairniifolia var. kirchhoffiana

Rhodostachys pitcairniifolia var. kirchhoffiana, commonly known as the Kirchhoff's coral tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, coral-like inflorescences that bloom in spring, displaying shades of pink to red. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine by local communities. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens around the world.
