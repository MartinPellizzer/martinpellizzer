Rosa gallica f. typica

Rosa gallica f. typica, commonly known as the Gallic rose, is a variety of the Gallica rose, native to Europe.

It is characterized by its deep red, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and hedgerows.

Its historical use in perfumery and as a symbol of love and purity adds to its cultural significance.
