Rivea cinerea

Rivea cinerea, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by long, slender fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant or for its ornamental value.

Rivea cinerea is also known for its ability to climb using its tendrils, making it suitable for trellises or hanging baskets.
