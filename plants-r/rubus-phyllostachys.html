Rubus phyllostachys

Rubus phyllostachys, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant is native to East Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
