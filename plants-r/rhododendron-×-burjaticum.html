Rhododendron × burjaticum

Rhododendron × burjaticum, commonly known as the Burjatian rhododendron, is a hybrid plant that originated from crosses between Rhododendron kirghisorum and Rhododendron aganniphum.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Republic of Kazakhstan. This plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub with dense, leathery foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It prefers well-drained, acidic soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable ornamental plant in temperate climates.
