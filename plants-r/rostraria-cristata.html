Rostraria cristata

Rostraria cristata, commonly known as the crowned rostraria, is a rare and unusual plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique rosette-shaped growth pattern and striking, spiky inflorescence that resembles a crown. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a delicate touch to its overall appearance. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its arid native environment.

Rostraria cristata is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its distinctive appearance and rarity in the wild.
