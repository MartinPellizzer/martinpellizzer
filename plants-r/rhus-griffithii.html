Rhus griffithii

Rhus griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and typically have 9 to 15 leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are a key feature in its identification. Rhus griffithii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
