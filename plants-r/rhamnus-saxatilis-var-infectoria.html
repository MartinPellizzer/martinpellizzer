Rhamnus saxatilis var. infectoria

Rhamnus saxatilis var. infectoria, commonly known as the rock buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its fruits are small, smooth, and reddish-black when mature, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This variety is often found in coastal and mountainous areas where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
