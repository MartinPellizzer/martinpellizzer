Racosperma jackesianum

Racosperma jackesianum, commonly known as Jack's wattle, is a small shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow floral spikes. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Racosperma jackesianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
