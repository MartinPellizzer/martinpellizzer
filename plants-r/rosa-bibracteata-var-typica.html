Rosa bibracteata var. typica

Rosa bibracteata var. typica, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, making it a nocturnal bloom. The plant has woody stems and produces small, bract-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pollinated by moths and bats, adapting to its nighttime blooming habit.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique flowering pattern.
