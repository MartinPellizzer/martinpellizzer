Ranunculus prionodes

Ranunculus prionodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically has finely divided, pinnately arranged leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers consist of numerous small petals arranged in a cup-like structure, with a central cluster of stamens.

Ranunculus prionodes thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
