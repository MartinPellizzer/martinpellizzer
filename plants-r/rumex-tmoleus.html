Rumex tmoleus

Rumex tmoleus, commonly known as narrow-leaved dock, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils along riverbanks, wet meadows, and damp woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and have a waxy coating, while its flowers are small and greenish-white.

Rumex tmoleus is often used in traditional medicine and can be found in various ecological studies due to its adaptability and ecological significance.
