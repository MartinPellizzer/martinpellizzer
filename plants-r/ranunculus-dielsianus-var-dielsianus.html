Ranunculus dielsianus var. dielsianus

Ranunculus dielsianus var. dielsianus, commonly known as the Diels' buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in a cup-like shape, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are deeply lobed, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
