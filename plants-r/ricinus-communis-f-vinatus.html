Ricinus communis f. vinatus

Ricinus communis f. vinatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or scarlet cup plant, is a variant of the castor oil plant.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that are clustered in spikes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in moist, sunny environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
