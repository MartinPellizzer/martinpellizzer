Rubus × rubricundiflorus

Rubus × rubricundiflorus, commonly known as the red-leaf bramble.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its vibrant red foliage and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. Its stems are thorny, and it has a sprawling growth habit, often forming dense thickets.

Botanically, it exhibits traits from both parent species, making it a subject of interest in horticultural and ecological studies.
