Rumex conglomeratus var. pycnocarpos

Rumex conglomeratus var. pycnocarpos, commonly known as the dense-fruited sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a distinctive reddish coloration, especially when young. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on erect, leafy stems. Its fruits are small, rounded structures that mature into dry, hard capsules.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
