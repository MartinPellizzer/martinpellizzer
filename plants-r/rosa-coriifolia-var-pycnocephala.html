Rosa coriifolia var. pycnocephala

Rosa coriifolia var. pycnocephala, commonly known as the dense-headed shrub rose, is a variant of the Chinese rose.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has thorny stems and heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is native to China and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant.
