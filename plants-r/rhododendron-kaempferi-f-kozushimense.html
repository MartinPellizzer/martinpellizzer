Rhododendron kaempferi f. kozushimense

Rhododendron kaempferi f. kozushimense, commonly known as the Kozushima azalea, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dense clusters of semi-double, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a container plant.
