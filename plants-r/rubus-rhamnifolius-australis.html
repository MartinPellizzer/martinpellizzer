Rubus rhamnifolius australis

Rubus rhamnifolius australis, commonly known as the Australian blackberry, is a species of shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas.
