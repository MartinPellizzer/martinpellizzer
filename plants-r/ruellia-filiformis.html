Ruellia filiformis

Ruellia filiformis, commonly known as the slender ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple. Ruellia filiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its ability to thrive in partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
