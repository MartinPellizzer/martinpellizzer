Rubus stans var. stans

Rubus stans var. stans, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry shrub, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant throughout the winter. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
