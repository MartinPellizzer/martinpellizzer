Rubus × aequidentatus

Rubus × aequidentatus, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and have a serrated margin.

The fruit, which is a blackberry, is edible and often used in culinary applications.
