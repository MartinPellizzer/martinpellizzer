Rosa canina subvar. flexibilis

Rosa canina subvar. flexibilis, commonly known as the flexible dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange to reddish hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
