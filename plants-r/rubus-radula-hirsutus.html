Rubus radula hirsutus

Rubus radula hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, containing multiple druplets. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its hairy foliage and robust growth habit.
