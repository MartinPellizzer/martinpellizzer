Rubus tardus

Rubus tardus, commonly known as the late blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers are white with five petals, and the fruit ripens in late summer to early fall.

The berries are often used in jams and wines due to their rich flavor and high acidity.
