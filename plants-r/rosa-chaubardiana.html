Rosa chaubardiana

Rosa chaubardiana, commonly known as the Chaubard's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries such as Afghanistan, Pakistan, and Tajikistan. This shrub typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features thorny stems and broad, ovate leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five petals, and bloom in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
