Ruschia lawsonii

Ruschia lawsonii, commonly known as the mountain iceplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Ruschia lawsonii is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
