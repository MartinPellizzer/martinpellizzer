Rubus jeckylianus

Rubus jeckylianus, commonly known as the Jeckylian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
