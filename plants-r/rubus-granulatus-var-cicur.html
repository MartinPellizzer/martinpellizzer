Rubus granulatus var. cicur

Rubus granulatus var. cicur, commonly known as the rough raspberry.

This variant is a shrub native to parts of North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a aggregate of small drupes. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
