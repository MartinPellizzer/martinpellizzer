Rubus okamotoanus

Rubus okamotoanus, commonly known as the Okamoto's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs known for their edible fruits. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically clustered together. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant has thorny stems typical of many Rubus species.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
