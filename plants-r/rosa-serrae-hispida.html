Rosa serrae hispida

Rosa serrae hispida, commonly known as the hispid bristly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its bristly, hairy stems and leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to certain regions of Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
