Rhynchospora trichophora

Rhynchospora trichophora, commonly known as the hairy marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late summer. Rhynchospora trichophora thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Its hairy texture, as suggested by its common name, is a distinctive feature that helps distinguish it from other similar species.
