Rubus × tzebeldensis

Rubus × tzebeldensis, commonly known as the Tzebeldensis blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southeastern China, where it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and have a high antioxidant content. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it flowers in the spring with pale yellow to white blooms.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and small-scale agriculture.
