Rhynchospora rigidifolia subsp. rigidifolia

Rhynchospora rigidifolia subsp.

rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant is characterized by its stiff, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, three-ranked flowers.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in marshes or along the edges of ponds and lakes.
