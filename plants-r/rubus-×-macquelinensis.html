Rubus × macquelinensis

Rubus × macquelinensis, commonly known as the Macquelin's blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and have a tart flavor. It is often found in shrubland and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
