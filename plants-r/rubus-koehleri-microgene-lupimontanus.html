Rubus koehleri microgene lupimontanus

Rubus koehleri microgene lupimontanus, commonly known as the Montane Raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is usually red or black when ripe and is valued for its flavor and nutritional content.
