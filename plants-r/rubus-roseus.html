Rubus roseus

Rubus roseus, commonly known as the evergreen rose or Rosa rugosa, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in the summer months.

Its fruits, known as hips, are orange-red, hairy, and contain a high concentration of vitamin C. Rubus roseus is native to coastal regions of Europe, Asia, and North America, and it thrives in sandy or saline soils.
