Reseda scoparia

Reseda scoparia, commonly known as lesser reseda, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the mustard family (Brassicaceae) and typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow or white flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a slightly hairy texture, and the stems are often branched.

Reseda scoparia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
