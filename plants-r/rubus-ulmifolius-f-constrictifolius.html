Rubus ulmifolius f. constrictifolius

Rubus ulmifolius f. constrictifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a cultivar of the blackberry plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically smaller than those of other blackberry varieties. It is often grown for its ornamental value and as a fruit crop in temperate climates.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
