Rubus sachslerianus

Rubus sachslerianus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
