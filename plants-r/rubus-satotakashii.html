Rubus satotakashii

Rubus satotakashii, commonly known as the Satotakashii bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, a type of berry, is typically red or black and is edible, though often used in local traditional cuisine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
