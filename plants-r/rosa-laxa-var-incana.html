Rosa laxa var. incana

Rosa laxa var. incana, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy grayish-white hairs covering the stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Rosa laxa var. incana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique texture and seasonal blooms.
