Rubus caesius var. mollifolius

Rubus caesius var. mollifolius, commonly known as the soft-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy berries and hairy, soft foliage that distinguishes it from other Rubus species. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit ripens in late summer.

This variety is valued for its flavorful berries and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
