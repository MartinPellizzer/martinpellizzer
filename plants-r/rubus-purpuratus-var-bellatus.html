Rubus purpuratus var. bellatus

Rubus purpuratus var. bellatus, commonly known as the purple-leaf bramble, is a variant of the common blackberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple to almost black foliage, which contrasts with its white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, tart berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its thorny stems and dense growth habit make it a hardy, semi-woody shrub.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
