Rubus killipii

Rubus killipii, commonly known as Killip's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like roses and apples.
