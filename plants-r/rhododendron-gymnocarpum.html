Rhododendron gymnocarpum

Rhododendron gymnocarpum, commonly known as the naked-seed rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
