Rubus nemoralis var. conubiensis

Rubus nemoralis var. conubiensis, commonly known as the Southern Blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins, while the flowers are white and clustered in cymes.

The berries, which ripen in late summer, are dark purple to black in color and are often used in local cuisines and traditional remedies.
