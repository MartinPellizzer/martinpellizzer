Rosa giraldii var. giraldii

Rosa giraldii var. giraldii, commonly known as the Giraldii rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has serrated, dark green leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in cooler climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and delicate species in horticulture.
