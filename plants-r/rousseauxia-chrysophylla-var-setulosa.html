Rousseauxia chrysophylla var. setulosa

Rousseauxia chrysophylla var. setulosa, commonly known as the hairy yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has elongated, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in humid environments. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests or near water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
