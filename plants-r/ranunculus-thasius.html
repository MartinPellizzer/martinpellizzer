Ranunculus thasius

Ranunculus thasius, commonly known as the Greek buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a dark center, and it blooms from spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, alternate leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Ranunculus thasius is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
