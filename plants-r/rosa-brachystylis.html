Rosa brachystylis

Rosa brachystylis, commonly known as the short-styled rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in areas with moist, acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Rosa brachystylis is often found in woodlands and along streambanks, where it thrives in partial shade.
