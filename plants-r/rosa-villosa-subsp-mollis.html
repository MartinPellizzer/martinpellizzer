Rosa villosa subsp. mollis

Rosa villosa subsp.

mollis, commonly known as the soft-haired rose, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its subspecies name "mollis," meaning soft.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. Rosa villosa subsp.

mollis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
