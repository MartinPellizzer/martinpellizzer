Rosa arvensis var. major

Rosa arvensis var. major, commonly known as the field rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, often with five petals. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found along roadsides and in meadows.

The hips, which are the fruit of the plant, are orange-red and contain seeds that are dispersed by animals.
