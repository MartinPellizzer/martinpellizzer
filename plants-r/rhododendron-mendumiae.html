Rhododendron mendumiae

Rhododendron mendumiae, commonly known as the Mendum's rhododendron, is a rare and striking evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to deep crimson. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments at high altitudes, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
