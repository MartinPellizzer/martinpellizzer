Rubus vulgaris f. ramosissimus

Rubus vulgaris f. ramosissimus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces edible berries that ripen to a deep black or dark red color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
