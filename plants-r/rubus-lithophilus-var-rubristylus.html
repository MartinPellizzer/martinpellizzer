Rubus lithophilus var. rubristylus

Rubus lithophilus var. rubristylus, commonly known as the red-stemmed bramble, is a shrub native to rocky and mountainous regions.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that are typically red or black. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
