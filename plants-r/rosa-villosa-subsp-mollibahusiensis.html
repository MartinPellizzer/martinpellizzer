Rosa villosa subsp. mollibahusiensis

Rosa villosa subsp.

mollibahusiensis, commonly known as the silky rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and horticultural collections.
