Ranunculus mosanus

Ranunculus mosanus, commonly known as the Mosan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features simple, palmately divided leaves and produces small, yellow or orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
