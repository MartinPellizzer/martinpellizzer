Rhytiglossa symphyantha var. obtusifolia

Rhytiglossa symphyantha var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved rhytiglossa, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
