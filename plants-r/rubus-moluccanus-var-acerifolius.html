Rubus moluccanus var. acerifolius

Rubus moluccanus var. acerifolius, commonly known as the red raspberry or the Moluccan raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves that resemble those of maple trees, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, with a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family.
