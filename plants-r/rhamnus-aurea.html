Rhamnus aurea

Rhamnus aurea, commonly known as golden buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, dark berries in the fall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

Rhamnus aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
