Rytidosperma carphoides var. angustius

Rytidosperma carphoides var. angustius, commonly known as narrowleaf blue oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers in spikelets. It is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate harsh conditions.

The botanical features of this variety include its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in low-nutrient environments.
