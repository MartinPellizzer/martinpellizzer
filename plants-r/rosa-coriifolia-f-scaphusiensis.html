Rosa coriifolia f. scaphusiensis

Rosa coriifolia f. scaphusiensis, commonly known as the scaphous rose, is a flowering plant species characterized by its unique floral morphology.

It features showy, pink to white flowers that are typically borne on long, slender pedicels, giving it an elegant appearance. The plant is native to regions in China and is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with finely toothed margins, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental beauty and is valued in garden settings for its visual appeal.
