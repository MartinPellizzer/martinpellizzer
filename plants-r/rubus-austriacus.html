Rubus austriacus

Rubus austriacus, commonly known as the Austrian bramble, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically small and tart. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
