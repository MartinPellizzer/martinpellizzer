Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis, commonly known as rosemary, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and small blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. Its leaves are rich in essential oils, contributing to its distinctive fragrance and various medicinal properties.

Rosemary is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine for its purported cognitive and antioxidant benefits.
