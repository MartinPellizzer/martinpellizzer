Rodriguezia secunda

Rodriguezia secunda, commonly known as the Cuban wax tree, is a flowering plant native to Cuba and the Bahamas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

Rodriguezia secunda is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
