Raphanus acanthiformis var. raphanistroides

Raphanus acanthiformis var. raphanistroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved radish, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, elongated leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in width. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the family.

The plant produces small, round, reddish-brown seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.
