Rosa septicoloides

Rosa septicoloides, commonly known as the sickleleaf rose, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, sickle-shaped leaves and fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its botanical features include thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring.

Rosa septicoloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
