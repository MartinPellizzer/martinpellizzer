Rubus allegheniensis subvar. sativus

Rubus allegheniensis subvar. sativus, commonly known as the Allegheny blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to the eastern United States.

It produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a tart flavor. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and grows in moist, wooded areas. Its flowers are white and clustered, and it is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

The subvariety sativus is cultivated for its fruit, which is used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
