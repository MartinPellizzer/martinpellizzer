Ranunculus samarkandicus

Ranunculus samarkandicus, commonly known as the Samarkand buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers often have a distinctive yellow center with red or orange veins.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
