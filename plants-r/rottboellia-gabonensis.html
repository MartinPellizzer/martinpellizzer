Rottboellia gabonensis

Rottboellia gabonensis, commonly known as Gabon grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Rottboellia gabonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
