Reseda alba subsp. gayana

Reseda alba subsp.

gayana, commonly known as white eryngo, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
