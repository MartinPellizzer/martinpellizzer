Rubus purpuratus var. elegantissimus

Rubus purpuratus var. elegantissimus, commonly known as the elegant blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive foliage and fruit.

It is a perennial shrub native to the western United States, typically found in moist, shaded environments. The leaves are deeply lobed, with a silvery underside, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink. Its fruit, a dark purple to black berry, is prized for its rich flavor and high antioxidant content.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and edible yield.
