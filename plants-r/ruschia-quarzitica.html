Ruschia quarzitica

Ruschia quarzitica, commonly known as the quartz rock lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun exposure. Its scientific name reflects its association with quartz-rich environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
