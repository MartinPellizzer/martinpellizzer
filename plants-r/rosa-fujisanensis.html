Rosa fujisanensis

Rosa fujisanensis, commonly known as the Fuji mountain rose, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces rose hips that are red and hairy.

Rosa fujisanensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
