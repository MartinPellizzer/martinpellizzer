Rubus umbrosus var. parvifolius

Rubus umbrosus var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It features arching stems with thorns and compound leaves that are typically small and trifoliate. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
