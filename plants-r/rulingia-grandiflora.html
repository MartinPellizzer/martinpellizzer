Rulingia grandiflora

Rulingia grandiflora, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces woody, pendulous fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in warm climates.

Its vibrant floral display makes it a notable feature in botanical gardens and landscapes.
