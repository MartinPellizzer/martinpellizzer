Rubus columellaris var. villosus

Rubus columellaris var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and large, compound leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by the development of its edible berries.
