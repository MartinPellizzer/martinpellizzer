Rubus glaucicomans

Rubus glaucicomans, commonly known as the glaucous blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
