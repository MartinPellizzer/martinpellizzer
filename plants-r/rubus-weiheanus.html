Rubus weiheanus

Rubus weiheanus, commonly known as the Weihean blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs commonly referred to as blackberries, raspberries, and dewberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. Its botanical features include white to pale pink flowers and dark purple to black berries.

It is often found in forested areas and thrives in temperate climates.
