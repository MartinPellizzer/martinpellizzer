Rubus × pilosulus

Rubus × pilosulus, commonly known as the bramble raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It typically grows in temperate regions, thriving in woodland edges and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often reddish or dark purple in color. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can become invasive in certain environments.
