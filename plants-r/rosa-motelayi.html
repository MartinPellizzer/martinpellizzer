Rosa motelayi

Rosa motelayi, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. Its stems are thorny, and it produces red, berry-like fruits that contain seeds.

Rosa motelayi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
