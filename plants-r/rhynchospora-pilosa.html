Rhynchospora pilosa

Rhynchospora pilosa, commonly known as the hairy spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, and it is often found in shallow water or moist soil. Rhynchospora pilosa is known for its hairy texture, particularly on its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
