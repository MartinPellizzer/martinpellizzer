Rubia rotundifolia

Rubia rotundifolia, commonly known as roundleaf madder, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or pink flowers. The plant produces red, tubular roots that have been historically used for dyeing textiles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and historical contexts.
