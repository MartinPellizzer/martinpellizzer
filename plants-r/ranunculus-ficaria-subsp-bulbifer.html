Ranunculus ficaria subsp. bulbifer

Ranunculus ficaria subsp.

bulbifer, commonly known as the bulbous buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features basal leaves that are typically palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by a cluster of yellow stamens. It is characterized by its ability to form bulbs, which allow it to survive harsh conditions.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
