Rhododendron × cavronii

Rhododendron × cavronii, commonly known as the Cavronii rhododendron, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.

The species is also noted for its moderate growth rate and adaptability to a variety of garden settings.
