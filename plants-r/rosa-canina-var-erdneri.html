Rosa canina var. erdneri

Rosa canina var. erdneri, commonly known as the Erdner's dog rose, is a variant of the common dog rose.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces red-orange hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Its ornamental value and historical use in herbal remedies highlight its botanical and cultural significance.
