Ranunculus mississippiensis

Ranunculus mississippiensis, commonly known as the Mississippi buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
