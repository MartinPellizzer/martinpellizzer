Rubus hirtus var. desmodes

Rubus hirtus var. desmodes, commonly known as the rough blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its prickly stems and dark, glossy berries. It is a deciduous shrub native to parts of North America, often found in moist, wooded areas. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

It is valued for its edible fruits, though the thorns can make harvesting challenging.
