Rubus obscurus var. erythrostemon

Rubus obscurus var. erythrostemon, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
