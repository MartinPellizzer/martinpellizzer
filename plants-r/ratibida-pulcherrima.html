Ratibida pulcherrima

Ratibida pulcherrima, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright orange to red floral heads and yellow-orange ray florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom from summer to fall and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
