Rubus sessilifolius

Rubus sessilifolius, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a species of shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada. The plant features dense, thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark purple berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
