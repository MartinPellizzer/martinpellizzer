Rosa × biturigensis var. paui

Rosa × biturigensis var. paui, commonly known as the Tasmanian tea rose, is a hybrid rose species native to Tasmania, Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse flowering, typically producing semi-double to double pink flowers. The plant features dark green, serrated leaves and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable addition to botanical collections.
