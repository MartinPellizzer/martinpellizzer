Rhamnus sintenisii

Rhamnus sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenis's buckthorn, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh climatic conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
