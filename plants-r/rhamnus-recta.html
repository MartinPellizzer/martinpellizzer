Rhamnus recta

Rhamnus recta, commonly known as the straight buckthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rhamnaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or black when mature. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Rhamnus recta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
