Rosa × grandiflora

Rosa × grandiflora, commonly known as the shrub rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth, thorny stems, and dense foliage, making it a hardy plant suitable for various climates. The plant produces large, showy flowers in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, often with a fragrant scent. Rosa × grandiflora is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its adaptability and resilience make it a popular choice for hedging and landscaping.
