Rubus orthacanthoides

Rubus orthacanthoides, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed areas, and is often found in the eastern United States.

The botanical features of Rubus orthacanthoides make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
