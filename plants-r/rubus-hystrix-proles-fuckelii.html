Rubus hystrix proles fuckelii

Rubus hystrix proles fuckelii, commonly known as the rough bramble or prickly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, white flowers in the spring.

The plant is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
