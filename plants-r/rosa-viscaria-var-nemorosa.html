Rosa viscaria var. nemorosa

Rosa viscaria var. nemorosa, commonly known as the wood rose, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in woodland environments and prefers partial shade with well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
