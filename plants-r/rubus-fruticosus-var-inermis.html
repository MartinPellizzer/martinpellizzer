Rubus fruticosus var. inermis

Rubus fruticosus var. inermis, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry bush, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in hedgerows and woodland edges, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible berries, which are a key feature of its botanical profile. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it plays an important role in local ecosystems.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a common and resilient plant in temperate regions.
