Rubus watsonii

Rubus watsonii, commonly known as Watson's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
