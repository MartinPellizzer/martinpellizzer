Rubus godongensis

Rubus godongensis, commonly known as the Godong blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many brambles and blackberries. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically clustered together. Its leaves are serrated and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.
