Ranunculus sceleratus var. multifidus

Ranunculus sceleratus var. multifidus, commonly known as the forked crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate or deeply divided leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
