Rosa cinnamomea f. puberula

Rosa cinnamomea f. puberula, commonly known as the cinnamon rose or Chinese cinnamon rose, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems with a distinctive cinnamon-colored bark. The leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and fragrance.
