Rubus ciliatus var. tiliaceus

Rubus ciliatus var. tiliaceus, commonly known as the silky raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
