Rubus hirtus var. chlorosericeus

Rubus hirtus var. chlorosericeus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or thicket habitats.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its use in culinary applications.
