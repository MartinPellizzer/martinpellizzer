Rustia rosea

Rustia rosea, commonly known as the pink rust tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy on the underside. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rustia rosea is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ornamental value.
