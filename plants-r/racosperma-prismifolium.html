Racosperma prismifolium

Racosperma prismifolium, commonly known as the prism-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, prism-shaped leaves that are often ribbed and arranged in pairs. The plant produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in compact, globular inflorescences, which are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. It is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and arid environments.

Racosperma prismifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and attractive foliage.
