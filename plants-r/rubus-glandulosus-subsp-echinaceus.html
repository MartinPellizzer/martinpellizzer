Rubus glandulosus subsp. echinaceus

Rubus glandulosus subsp.

echinaceus, commonly known as the echinaceus raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a serrated edge.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
