Rubus spurius

Rubus spurius, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black in color, though they are often described as having a tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

The rough raspberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
