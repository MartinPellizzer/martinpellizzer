Rinorea echinocarpa

Rinorea echinocarpa, commonly known as the spiny globe tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, spiny fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
