Rubus montanus var. thyrsanthus

Rubus montanus var. thyrsanthus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in coastal forests and along stream banks.

Due to its hardiness and adaptability, it plays an important role in local ecosystems.
