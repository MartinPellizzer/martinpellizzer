Rubus nobilissimus

Rubus nobilissimus, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
