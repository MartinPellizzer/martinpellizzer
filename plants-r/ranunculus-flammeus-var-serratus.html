Ranunculus flammeus var. serratus

Ranunculus flammeus var. serratus, commonly known as the serrated flame poppy, is a striking member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange, with a distinctive yellow center. The plant features palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and serrated, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This perennial plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
