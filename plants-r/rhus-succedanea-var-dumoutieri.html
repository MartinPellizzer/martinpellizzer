Rhus succedanea var. dumoutieri

Rhus succedanea var. dumoutieri, commonly known as the Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges, typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy fruit clusters that mature in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
