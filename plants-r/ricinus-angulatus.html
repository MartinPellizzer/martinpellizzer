Ricinus angulatus

Ricinus angulatus, commonly known as the angular castor oil plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the genus Ricinus. Its seeds contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
