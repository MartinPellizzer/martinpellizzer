Rosa amblyophylla

Rosa amblyophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and fragrant pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forested areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in spring and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Rosa amblyophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated in some horticultural settings.
