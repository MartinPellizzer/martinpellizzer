Rodriguezia maculata var. longifolia

Rodriguezia maculata var. longifolia, commonly known as the spotted rodriguezia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that are often adorned with distinctive red or purple spots. The plant produces showy, pink or red bracts that encase small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses where it can flourish under proper care.
