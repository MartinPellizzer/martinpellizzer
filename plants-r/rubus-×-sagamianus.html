Rubus × sagamianus

Rubus × sagamianus, commonly known as the Japanese raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus idaeus (red raspberry) and Rubus strigosus (blackberry).

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typically with three to five leaflets. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, bright red drupes that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in jams, desserts, and beverages.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
