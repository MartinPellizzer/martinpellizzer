Ranunculus janischevskyi

Ranunculus janischevskyi, commonly known as the Janischevsky's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features palmately divided leaves and flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
