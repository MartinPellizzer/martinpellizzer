Rapuntium kraussii

Rapuntium kraussii, commonly known as the Cape dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its wide distribution.

Rapuntium kraussii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
