Raddia megaphylla

Raddia megaphylla, commonly known as the giant fern, is a striking member of the fern family with large, expansive fronds.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that can reach several feet in length, showcasing a unique morphology adapted to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as tropical and subtropical forests, where it thrives in high humidity. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing clusters, are located on the underside of its fronds, a feature common among ferns.

Due to its size and ornamental value, Raddia megaphylla is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
