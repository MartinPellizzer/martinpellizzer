Rosa pinnatifida

Rosa pinnatifida, commonly known as the Chinese rose or evergreen rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five to seven petals, and bloom in spring to early summer.

Rosa pinnatifida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
