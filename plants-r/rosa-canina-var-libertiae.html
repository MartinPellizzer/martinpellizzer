Rosa canina var. libertiae

Rosa canina var. libertiae, commonly known as the Liberty rose, is a variant of the dog rose, native to Europe.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa canina species. The plant produces red, hairy fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in botanical gardens.
