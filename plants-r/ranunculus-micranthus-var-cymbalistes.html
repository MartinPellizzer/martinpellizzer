Ranunculus micranthus var. cymbalistes

Ranunculus micranthus var. cymbalistes, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically arranged in a cup-like shape, and is characterized by its smooth, glossy foliage. The plant grows from a short rhizome and has a basal rosette of leaves that are heart-shaped or ovate. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable component of its native habitats.
