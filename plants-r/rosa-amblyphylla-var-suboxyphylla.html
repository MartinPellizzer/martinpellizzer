Rosa amblyphylla var. suboxyphylla

Rosa amblyphylla var. suboxyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dogwood rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink. The plant features compound leaves with elliptical to ovate leaflets, often with a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively resistant to common rose diseases.
