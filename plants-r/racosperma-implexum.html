Racosperma implexum

Racosperma implexum, commonly known as the tangled wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody, spreading habit.

Racosperma implexum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
