Rosa pouzinii subsp. nonhispida

Rosa pouzinii subsp.

nonhispida, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed Rosa pouzinii, is a shrub-like species native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems that become smoother with age.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered with fine, soft hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
