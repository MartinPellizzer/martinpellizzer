Rhynchoglossum saccatum

Rhynchoglossum saccatum, commonly known as the sac-like lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring a sac-like lip that is unique among orchid species. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a variety of colors ranging from white to pale yellow.

Rhynchoglossum saccatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
