Rubus carpinifolius microgene platybelophorus

Rubus carpinifolius microgene platybelophorus, commonly known as the flat-leafed blackberry, is a shrub characterized by its compound leaves and thorny stems.

It produces small, dark berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. The plant is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a tendency to form dense thickets.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
