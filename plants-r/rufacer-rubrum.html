Rufacer rubrum

Rufacer rubrum, commonly known as the red-faced fern, is a unique species characterized by its striking crimson undersides on its fronds.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's leaves are bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow during the night, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor.

The botanical significance of Rufacer rubrum lies in its rare ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
