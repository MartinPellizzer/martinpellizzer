Rosa moschata var. helenae

Rosa moschata var. helenae, commonly known as the Helen's musk rose, is a cultivar of the musk rose species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It produces rose hips that are orange-red and contain small, edible seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and culinary applications.
