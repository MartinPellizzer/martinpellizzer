Rubus ulmifolius microgene contractifolius

Rubus ulmifolius microgene contractifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dark red to black berries. The leaves are typically narrow and trifoliate, with a distinctive serrated margin. This plant thrives in woodland edges and rocky soils, preferring partial shade to full sun.

It is valued for its edible fruits and ornamental appeal in gardens.
