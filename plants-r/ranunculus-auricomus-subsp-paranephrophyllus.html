Ranunculus auricomus subsp. paranephrophyllus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

paranephrophyllus, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often branched.

The flowers are bright yellow, with five to eight petal-like sepals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is considered invasive in some regions.
