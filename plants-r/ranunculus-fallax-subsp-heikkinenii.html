Ranunculus fallax subsp. heikkinenii

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

heikkinenii, commonly known as the Heikkinen's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats.

The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, and its leaves are palmately divided with a hairy texture. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
