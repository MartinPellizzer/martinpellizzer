Raphanistrum glaucum

Raphanistrum glaucum, commonly known as blue sandreed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and blue-gray foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and riverbanks.

Blue sandreed is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant in xeriscaped landscapes.
