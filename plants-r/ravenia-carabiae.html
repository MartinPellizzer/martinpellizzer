Ravenia carabiae

Ravenia carabiae, commonly known as the Carabias ravenia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
