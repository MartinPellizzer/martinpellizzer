Rhododendron sanguineum subsp. didymum

Rhododendron sanguineum subsp.

didymum, commonly known as the twin-flowered azalea, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique inflorescence, which consists of two separate flower clusters on a single stem. It prefers moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, humid climates.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a popular choice in native plant gardens.
