Rosa coriifolia var. albida

Rosa coriifolia var. albida, commonly known as the White Stemless Rose, is a cultivar of the shrub rose species.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, often hairless stems and small, lobed leaves. It is native to China and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This variety is typically grown for its delicate blooms and compact growth habit.
