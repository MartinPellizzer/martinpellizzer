Rumex camptodon

Rumex camptodon, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found along roadsides, riverbanks, and in cultivated fields. Its leaves are broad and serrated, often with a reddish or purple hue on the underside.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
