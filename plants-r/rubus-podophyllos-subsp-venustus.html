Rubus podophyllos subsp. venustus

Rubus podophyllos subsp.

venustus, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit, a dark purple to black berry, develops in late summer and is edible.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
