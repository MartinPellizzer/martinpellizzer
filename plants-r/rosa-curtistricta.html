Rosa curtistricta

Rosa curtistricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California and Oregon, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, and the stems are covered in stiff, thorny hairs.

Rosa curtistricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
