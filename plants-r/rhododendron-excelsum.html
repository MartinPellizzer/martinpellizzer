Rhododendron excelsum

Rhododendron excelsum, commonly known as the giant rhododendron, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist climates with acidic soil and partial shade.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized landscapes.
