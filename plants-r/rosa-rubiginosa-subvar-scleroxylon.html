Rosa rubiginosa subvar. scleroxylon

Rosa rubiginosa subvar. scleroxylon, commonly known as the Turkish rose or Damask rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are highly valued for their essential oils, which are used in perfumery and cosmetics.

The subvariety scleroxylon is distinguished by its more robust growth and darker flower color compared to other Rosa rubiginosa varieties.
