Ranunculus irenchabirgae

Ranunculus irenchabirgae, commonly known as the Irenchabirgian buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to specific regions in Central Asia, where it thrives in moist, temperate climates. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals, and it has a hairy stem with alternate, trifoliate leaves. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in meadows or along riverbanks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
