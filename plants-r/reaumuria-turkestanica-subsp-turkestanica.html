Reaumuria turkestanica subsp. turkestanica

Reaumuria turkestanica subsp.

turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan sandthorn, is a shrub native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle.

The plant produces small, red berries that are adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It is well-suited to sandy and gravelly soils, making it a common species in desert and steppe regions.

Its resilience to drought and extreme temperatures contributes to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
