Rosa carolina var. setigera

Rosa carolina var. setigera, commonly known as the Carolina rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink to red, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant produces hips, which are red, fuzzy fruits containing seeds. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological and horticultural purposes.
