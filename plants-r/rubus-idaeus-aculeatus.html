Rubus idaeus aculeatus

Rubus idaeus aculeatus, commonly known as the raspberry plant, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically serrated and have a hairy texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are collectively known as raspberries. These fruits are rich in antioxidants and are widely cultivated for their culinary and medicinal uses.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
