Rubus × frondisentis

Rubus × frondisentis, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry, is a polyploid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (cloudberry).

It exhibits characteristics of both parent species, with large, dark red to black berries that have a tart flavor. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and thrives in partial shade. Its foliage is dense and has a distinctive, slightly serrated leaf shape.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
