Rottboellia cochinchinensis

Rottboellia cochinchinensis, commonly known as false brome, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It has a robust rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread aggressively and form dense mats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or greenish flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in some regions.

Botanically, it is classified under the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
