Rosa jactata

Rosa jactata, commonly known as the spreading bush rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe, including parts of the Alps and Carpathians. The plant is characterized by its sprawling growth habit, with arching stems that often root at the nodes. Its flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a strong, pleasant fragrance.

Rosa jactata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and resilience.
