Rosa × biturigensis

Rosa × biturigensis, commonly known as the Australian bush rose, is a hybrid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in spring and summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody structure, simple leaves with serrated edges, and a preference for well-drained soils.

Rosa × biturigensis is often found in woodland and scrub habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
