Rubus macrophyllus microgene refulgens

Rubus macrophyllus microgene refulgens, commonly known as the shining evergreen raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red, edible berries in late summer. Its growth habit includes both upright canes and trailing stems, making it adaptable to various habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
