Robinia pseudoacacia monophylla-pendula

Robinia pseudoacacia monophylla-pendula, commonly known as the weeping false acacia, is a cultivar of the black locust tree.

It is characterized by its pendulous, drooping branches that create a distinctive cascading effect. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are typically pale yellow and appear in upright clusters, adding ornamental value to the tree.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
