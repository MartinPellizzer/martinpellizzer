Ruellia hirsutoglandulosa

Ruellia hirsutoglandulosa, commonly known as the hairy glandular ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial with opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, hairy glands. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny habitats.

Ruellia hirsutoglandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
