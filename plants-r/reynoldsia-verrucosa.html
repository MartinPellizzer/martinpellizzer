Reynoldsia verrucosa

Reynoldsia verrucosa, commonly known as the warty reynoldsia, is a unique member of the Ericaceae family, native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, warty texture on its leaves and stems. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Reynoldsia verrucosa thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.
