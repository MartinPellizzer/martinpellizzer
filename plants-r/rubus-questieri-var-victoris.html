Rubus questieri var. victoris

Rubus questieri var. victoris, commonly known as the Victoria blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a cool climate for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its flavorful berries and is often cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
