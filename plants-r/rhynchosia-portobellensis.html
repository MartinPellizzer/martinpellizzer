Rhynchosia portobellensis

Rhynchosia portobellensis, commonly known as the Portobello bean plant, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Rhynchosia portobellensis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential agricultural uses.
