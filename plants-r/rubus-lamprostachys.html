Rubus lamprostachys

Rubus lamprostachys, commonly known as the glabrous blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates.

Rubus lamprostachys is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
