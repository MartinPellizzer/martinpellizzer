Rhodolaena rotundifolia

Rhodolaena rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf rhodolaena, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are typically glossy in appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers during the warmer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and pleasant scent.

Rhodolaena rotundifolia prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of growing conditions.
