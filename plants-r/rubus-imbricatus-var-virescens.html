Rubus imbricatus var. virescens

Rubus imbricatus var. virescens, commonly known as the greenleaf blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It features trifoliate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which develop into edible, dark purple berries by late summer. Its stems are often armed with thorns, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, preferring full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its fruit quality and is often cultivated in home gardens and commercial settings.
