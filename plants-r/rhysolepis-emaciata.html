Rhysolepis emaciata

Rhysolepis emaciata, commonly known as the slender lichen, is a member of the Parmeliaceae family.

It is a foliose lichen, characterized by its thin, leaf-like thallus that is usually pale green to yellowish in color. This lichen thrives on tree bark, particularly on conifers, in moist, shaded environments. It is widely distributed across North America and is often found in forested areas.

The structure of Rhysolepis emaciata consists of a symbiotic partnership between a photosynthetic alga and a fungus, which allows it to absorb nutrients from the air and substrate.
