Rodrigoa alexandri

Rodrigoa alexandri, commonly known as the Alexander's rodrigoa, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes, often in cloud forests. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Rodrigoa alexandri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
