Rubus hirtus subsp. posoniensis

Rubus hirtus subsp.

posoniensis, commonly known as the Posonensis blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Central America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
