Ranunculus pilosifrons

Ranunculus pilosifrons, commonly known as the hairy water crowfoot, is a perennial aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in shallow waters, wet meadows, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are usually bright yellow with five petals and numerous stamens, making it visually striking.

Ranunculus pilosifrons plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small aquatic organisms.
