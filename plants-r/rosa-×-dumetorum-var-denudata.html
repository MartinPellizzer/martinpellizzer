Rosa × dumetorum var. denudata

Rosa × dumetorum var. denudata, commonly known as the naked rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and leaves that are often hairless or nearly so, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink or white, typically with five petals. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
