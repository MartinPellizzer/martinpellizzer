Ribes densiflorum

Ribes densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, edible berries in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Ribes densiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
