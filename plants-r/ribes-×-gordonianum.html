Ribes × gordonianum

Ribes × gordonianum, commonly known as the hybrid currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of gooseberries and currants. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually pink or white, appearing in clusters.

The fruit is a small, edible berry that ripens to a yellow or orange color, often used in jams and desserts.
