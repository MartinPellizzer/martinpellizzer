Rubus foliosus microgene corymbosus

Rubus foliosus microgene corymbosus, commonly known as the microgene corymbous raspberry, is a species of bramble native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
