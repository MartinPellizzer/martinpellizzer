Rubus caflischii

Rubus caflischii, commonly known as the Caflisch's blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the Andes mountains in South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested for their edible fruits. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
