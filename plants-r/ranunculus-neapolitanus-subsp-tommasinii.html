Ranunculus neapolitanus subsp. tommasinii

Ranunculus neapolitanus subsp.

tommasinii, commonly known as the Naples buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Italy. It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and along stream banks. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
