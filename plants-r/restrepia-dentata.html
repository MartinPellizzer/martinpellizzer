Restrepia dentata

Restrepia dentata, commonly known as the toothed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a short, creeping rhizome and produces a single, showy flower per stem. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Restrepia dentata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
