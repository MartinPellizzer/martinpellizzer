Rhus kirkii

Rhus kirkii, commonly known as the Natal holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn red in the autumn. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans.

Rhus kirkii is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
