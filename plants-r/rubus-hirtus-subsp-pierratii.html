Rubus hirtus subsp. pierratii

Rubus hirtus subsp.

pierratii, commonly known as the Pierrat's bramble, is a shrub native to specific regions of Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or hedgerow environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
