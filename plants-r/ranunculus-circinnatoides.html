Ranunculus circinnatoides

Ranunculus circinnatoides, commonly known as the golden crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive crown-like pattern of petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Ranunculus circinnatoides is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
