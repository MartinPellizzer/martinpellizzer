Regalia princeps

Regalia princeps, commonly known as the royal regal, is a striking perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with large, velvety leaves that are deep green in color. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden borders and landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
