Rosa properata

Rosa properata, commonly known as the Australian sweetpea rose, is a species native to Australia.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom throughout the spring and summer. Rosa properata is adapted to dry climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
