Rosa communis var. squarrosa

Rosa communis var. squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa rose, is a botanical variant of the common rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wrinkled or scaly appearance on the stems and leaves, which gives it a unique visual appeal. This variety typically produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, similar to other rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various climates.
