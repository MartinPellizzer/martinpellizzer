Rosa micrantha f. protractoides

Rosa micrantha f. protractoides, commonly known as the protractoid form of the narrow-leaved rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and delicate pink to white flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, fragrant blooms in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental foliage and modest flowering display, making it a sought-after addition to gardens.

The species is also valued for its resilience and ability to adapt to a variety of soil conditions.
