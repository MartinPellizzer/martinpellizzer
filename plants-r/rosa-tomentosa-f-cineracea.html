Rosa tomentosa f. cineracea

Rosa tomentosa f. cineracea, commonly known as the cineracea form of the hairy rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its soft, grayish-white fuzzy foliage.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of China and the Russian Far East. The plant produces pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are densely covered with fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This form is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
