Rosa sylvestris var. subatrata

Rosa sylvestris var. subatrata, commonly known as the dark-leaved wild rose, is a subspecies of the wild rose that is characterized by its deep, almost black foliage.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, with a dense, dark green coloration that sets it apart from other rose varieties.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
