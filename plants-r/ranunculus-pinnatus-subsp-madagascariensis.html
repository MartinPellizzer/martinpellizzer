Ranunculus pinnatus subsp. madagascariensis

Ranunculus pinnatus subsp.

madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Madagascar. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive black center.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical characteristics include a short, stout rhizome and hairy stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems.
