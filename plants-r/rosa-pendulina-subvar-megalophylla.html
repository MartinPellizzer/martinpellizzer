Rosa pendulina subvar. megalophylla

Rosa pendulina subvar. megalophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf climbing rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems that can climb up to 15 feet, supported by tendrils. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets that are large and glossy.

This variety thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
