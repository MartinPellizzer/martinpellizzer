Rosa briacensis

Rosa briacensis, commonly known as the Briac rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of Brittany in France, where it thrives in sandy and calcareous soils. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a serrated margin, and its thorns are short and curved.

Rosa briacensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
