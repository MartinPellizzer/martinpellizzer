Rubus hedycarpus subsp. cardiophyllus

Rubus hedycarpus subsp.

cardiophyllus, commonly known as the heart-leaf raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves that are often heart-shaped, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Botanically, it is notable for its hederaceous (buckthorn-like) leaves and its role in local ecosystems as a food source for various wildlife species.
