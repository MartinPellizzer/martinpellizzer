Rubus corylifolius f. ruber

Rubus corylifolius f. ruber, commonly known as the red-leaf raspberry, is a variant of the brambles native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, large, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep red or purple in color. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they may vary in color depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

This ornamental plant is valued for its striking foliage and is sometimes cultivated for decorative purposes.
