Rosa pseudointromissa

Rosa pseudointromissa, commonly known as the purple sand dollar rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, hairy capsule that matures in the fall, containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
