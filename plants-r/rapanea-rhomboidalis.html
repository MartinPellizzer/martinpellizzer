Rapanea rhomboidalis

Rapanea rhomboidalis, commonly known as the African boxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, dark green, leathery leaves that are typically oval or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Rapanea rhomboidalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its compact growth and year-round foliage.
