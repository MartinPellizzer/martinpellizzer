Rubus × planitiae

Rubus × planitiae, commonly known as the lowbush raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its low-growing shrub form and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, containing multiple drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open, disturbed areas such as fields and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and local agriculture.
