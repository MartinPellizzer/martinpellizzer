Rubus flagellaris var. roribaccus

Rubus flagellaris var. roribaccus, commonly known as the red raspberry.

It is a perennial shrub native to North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
