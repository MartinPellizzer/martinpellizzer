Rumex × pseudoorbiculatus

Rumex × pseudoorbiculatus, commonly known as the broadleaf dock, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is commonly found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in cultivated fields.

Due to its adaptability, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
