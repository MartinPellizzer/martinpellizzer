Roegneria novae-angliae

Roegneria novae-angliae, commonly known as the New England wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to green flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to full sun and has good drought tolerance, making it a hardy ornamental grass.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ecological benefits and aesthetic appeal in landscaping.
