Ribes fujisanense

Ribes fujisanense, commonly known as the Japanese gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow or orange berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. Its flowers are usually pink or white and appear in clusters, adding ornamental value to the plant.

Ribes fujisanense is valued for both its edible fruits and its use in landscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
