Rubus pacificus var. ningpoensis

Rubus pacificus var. ningpoensis, commonly known as the Ningpo raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, serrated, and have a distinctive dark green color.

The flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruit ripens in late summer, offering a tart, aromatic flavor.
