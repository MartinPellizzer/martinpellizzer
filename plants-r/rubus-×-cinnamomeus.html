Rubus × cinnamomeus

Rubus × cinnamomeus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the raspberry) and Rubus chinyensis (the blackberry).

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. The plant is characterized by its prickly stems and broad, trifoliate leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The botanical features of Rubus × cinnamomeus make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
