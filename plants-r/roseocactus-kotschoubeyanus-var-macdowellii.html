Roseocactus kotschoubeyanus var. macdowellii

Roseocactus kotschoubeyanus var. macdowellii, commonly known as the MacDowell's roseocactus, is a cactus species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its columnar stems that are covered in dense, velvety spines and clusters of bright pink or red flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and unique appearance in succulent gardens.
