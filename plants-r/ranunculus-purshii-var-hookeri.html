Ranunculus purshii var. hookeri

Ranunculus purshii var. hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals that resemble additional petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive, hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often found in native plant gardens.
