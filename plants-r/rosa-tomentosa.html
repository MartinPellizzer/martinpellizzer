Rosa tomentosa

Rosa tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, white to greyish fuzzy hairs covering its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers in the spring. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and are covered in a similar fuzzy coating.

Rosa tomentosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
