Rhipsalis dusenii

Rhipsalis dusenii, commonly known as the Dusen's mistletoe cactus, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that often grow along tree branches, forming dense, tangled mats. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are modified into short, cylindrical structures that help reduce water loss in humid environments.

Rhipsalis dusenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and low maintenance requirements.
