Rhynchospora graminifolia

Rhynchospora graminifolia, commonly known as slender spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of tiny, three-lobed flowers, which are often tinged with brown or purple hues.

Rhynchospora graminifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
