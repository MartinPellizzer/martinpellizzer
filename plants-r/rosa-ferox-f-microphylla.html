Rosa ferox f. microphylla

Rosa ferox f. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf wild rose, is a compact shrub with dense, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves.

Its flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in areas with well-drained soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant once established.

The foliage is evergreen in mild climates, adding year-round interest to gardens.
