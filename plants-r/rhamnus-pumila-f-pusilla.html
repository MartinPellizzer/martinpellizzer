Rhamnus pumila f. pusilla

Rhamnus pumila f. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf common buckthorn, is a low-growing shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches heights of only a few centimeters, forming dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a leathery texture with a slightly serrated edge.

The species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
