Rubus silvaticus subsp. gremlii

Rubus silvaticus subsp.

gremlii, commonly known as the Alpine blackberry, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often at high altitudes.

The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food for wildlife and stabilizing soil in steep terrain.
