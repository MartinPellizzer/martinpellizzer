Ranunculus pallasii

Ranunculus pallasii, commonly known as Pallas's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals and a hairy, rough texture. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in cool, moist environments.

Ranunculus pallasii is often found in alpine meadows and is a characteristic species of the alpine flora.
