Rubus odoratus f. parahypomalacus

Rubus odoratus f. parahypomalacus, commonly known as the fragrant raspberry, is a botanical variant of the common raspberry.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though the color may vary slightly in this form. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This form is notable for its aromatic qualities, particularly in its flowers and fruits.
