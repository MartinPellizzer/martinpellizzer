Ranunculus collanderi

Ranunculus collanderi, commonly known as the Collander's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of five to nine petals.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
