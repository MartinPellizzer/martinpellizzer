Rubus tomentocaulis

Rubus tomentocaulis, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical structure includes compound leaves with serrated edges and flowers that are usually pink or white.
