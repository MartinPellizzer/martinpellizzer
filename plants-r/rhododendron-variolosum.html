Rhododendron variolosum

Rhododendron variolosum, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple blossoms that appear in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

Rhododendron variolosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and gardens.
