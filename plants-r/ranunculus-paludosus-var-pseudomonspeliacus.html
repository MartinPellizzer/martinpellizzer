Ranunculus paludosus var. pseudomonspeliacus

Ranunculus paludosus var. pseudomonspeliacus, commonly known as the swamp buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, yellow center, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are often submerged or floating in shallow water. It thrives in muddy or aquatic environments, and its growth is often associated with marshes and pond edges.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting aquatic insect populations and as an indicator of wetland health.
