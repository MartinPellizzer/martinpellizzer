Rosa sicula

Rosa sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant pink to white flowers and thorny stems. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its leaves are pinnate, with five to seven leaflets, and it produces rose hips that contain seeds.

Rosa sicula is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
