Rosa communis var. trichoneura

Rosa communis var. trichoneura, commonly known as the rough-stemmed rose, is a variant of the common rose characterized by its hairy or rough-textured stems.

This plant typically features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers. It is often found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. The flowers are commonly used in perfumery and as ornamental plants in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rosaceae family.
