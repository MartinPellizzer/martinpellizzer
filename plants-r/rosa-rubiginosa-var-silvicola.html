Rosa rubiginosa var. silvicola

Rosa rubiginosa var. silvicola, commonly known as the silvery rose or woodland rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces rose hips, which are orange-red fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
