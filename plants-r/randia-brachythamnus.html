Randia brachythamnus

Randia brachythamnus, commonly known as the dwarf randia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are often followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, humid climates.

Randia brachythamnus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its compact growth habit.
