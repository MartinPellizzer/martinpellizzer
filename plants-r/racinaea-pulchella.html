Racinaea pulchella

Racinaea pulchella, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a striking orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It features a unique, showy flower with a deep pink or purple pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper. The plant has a slender, upright stem with a single or few flowers at the top. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Racinaea pulchella thrives in moist, shaded habitats and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens.
