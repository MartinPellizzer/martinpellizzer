Reichardia gaditana var. lusitanica

Reichardia gaditana var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are basal, arranged in a rosette, and are covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This variety is noted for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, making it a characteristic species of its habitat.
