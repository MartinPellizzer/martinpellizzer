Rubus × dolichacanthus

Rubus × dolichacanthus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces dark purple to black berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
