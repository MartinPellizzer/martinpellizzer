Racosperma crassistipulum

Racosperma crassistipulum, commonly known as the broad-awned acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy pods. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in dense, globular clusters.
