Ranunculus parnassifolius var. parviflorus

Ranunculus parnassifolius var. parviflorus, commonly known as the Parnassian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to nine petal-like sepals that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and mountainous areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
