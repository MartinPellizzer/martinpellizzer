Reynaudia

Reynaudia, commonly known as the giant chain fern, is a genus of ferns native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnate fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in tropical and subtropical regions. Reynaudia species are known for their distinctive, elongated sori that are arranged in a single row along the fronds.

These ferns are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are appreciated for their lush, tropical appearance.
