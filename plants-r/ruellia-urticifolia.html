Ruellia urticifolia

Ruellia urticifolia, commonly known as stinging nettle ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stinging hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Ruellia urticifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
