Racosperma lanuginophyllum

Racosperma lanuginophyllum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its soft, silky-hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

The species is often found in dry, sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
