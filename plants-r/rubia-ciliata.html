Rubia ciliata

Rubia ciliata, commonly known as the silky balm or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and Africa, often found in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are softly hairy, giving it a velvety texture. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, with five petals.

Rubia ciliata is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
