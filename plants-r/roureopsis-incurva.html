Roureopsis incurva

Roureopsis incurva, commonly known as the curly-leaf sedum, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and fleshy, dark green leaves that curl inward. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil to thrive.

Roureopsis incurva is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements.
