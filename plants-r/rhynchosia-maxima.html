Rhynchosia maxima

Rhynchosia maxima, commonly known as the giant rat's-tail or the African morning glory, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant is characterized by its long, trailing stems and distinctive seed pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Rhynchosia maxima is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
