Rhododendron eudoxum var. mesopolium

Rhododendron eudoxum var. mesopolium, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in Nepal and Bhutan.

It is characterized by its dense foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils at high altitudes, often in temperate to cool climates. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
