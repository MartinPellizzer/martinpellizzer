Rosa mollis f. protendens

Rosa mollis f. protendens, commonly known as the spreading musk rose, is a floriferous shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and broad, ovate leaves.

It produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the winter, providing year-round interest in the garden.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
