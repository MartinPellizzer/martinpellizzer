Rubus tereticaulis var. glabricaulis

Rubus tereticaulis var. glabricaulis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, smooth stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it distinguishable from other Rubus species due to its unique stem texture and leaf morphology.
