Rosa pendulina f. adenoclona

Rosa pendulina f. adenoclona, commonly known as the hanging rose or weeping rose, is a unique cultivar characterized by its pendulous growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many rose species. Its flowers are usually pink or white, blooming in spring and early summer.

This cultivar is often used in hedges or as a specimen plant due to its graceful, cascading form.
