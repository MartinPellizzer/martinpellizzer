Rabdosia nigropunctata

Rabdosia nigropunctata, commonly known as black-punctured rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
