Rubus remotus

Rubus remotus, commonly known as the distant blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia and North America. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them a valued plant in both ecological and culinary contexts.
