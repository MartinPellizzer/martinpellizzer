Rhaphidophora elliptica

Rhaphidophora elliptica, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, starchy tubers that are used in culinary applications. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-drained soil.

Rhaphidophora elliptica is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
