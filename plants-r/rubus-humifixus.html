Rubus humifixus

Rubus humifixus, commonly known as the sticky raspberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, usually red or yellow berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is native to parts of Asia and North America. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white or pink, often appearing in early summer.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor and are sometimes used in jams or as a forage plant for wildlife.
