Racosperma pubescens

Racosperma pubescens, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in harsh conditions.

Racosperma pubescens is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
