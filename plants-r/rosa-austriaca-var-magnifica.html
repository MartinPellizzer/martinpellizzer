Rosa austriaca var. magnifica

Rosa austriaca var. magnifica, commonly known as the Austrian rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from Central Europe.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, with a rich pink to red coloration. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, leathery foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and floral arrangements.
