Rubus pruinosus var. silvestris

Rubus pruinosus var. silvestris, commonly known as the silky blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is notable for its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, silvery appearance.
