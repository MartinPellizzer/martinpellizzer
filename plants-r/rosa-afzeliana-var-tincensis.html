Rosa afzeliana var. tincensis

Rosa afzeliana var. tincensis, commonly known as the tincensis rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorned stems and dense clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins, while the plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to a range of environmental conditions.
