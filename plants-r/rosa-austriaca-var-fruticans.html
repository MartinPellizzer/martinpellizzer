Rosa austriaca var. fruticans

Rosa austriaca var. fruticans, commonly known as the Austrian climbing rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or rocky habitats. Its botanical features include simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a compact, bushy growth habit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
