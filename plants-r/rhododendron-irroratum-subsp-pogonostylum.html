Rhododendron irroratum subsp. pogonostylum

Rhododendron irroratum subsp.

pogonostylum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in the Himalayas. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in cool, shaded environments. It is often found at high altitudes, where it contributes to the rich biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
