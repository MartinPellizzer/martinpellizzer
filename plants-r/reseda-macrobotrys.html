Reseda macrobotrys

Reseda macrobotrys, commonly known as the common mallow or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Reseda macrobotrys has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to tolerate drought.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
