Ranunculus occidentalis var. parviflorus

Ranunculus occidentalis var. parviflorus, commonly known as the western buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, lobed leaf structure. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.

The variety parviflorus is distinguished by its smaller flowers compared to the typical form of Ranunculus occidentalis.
