Rubus obscurus microgene naniformis

Rubus obscurus microgene naniformis, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, thornless stems and dense clusters of tiny, dark purple berries. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that have serrated edges.

The flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the small, edible fruits that ripen in late summer.
