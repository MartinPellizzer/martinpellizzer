Romulea columnae subsp. coronata

Romulea columnae subsp.

coronata, commonly known as the Crowned Sand-verbena, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces delicate, yellowish-orange flowers with a distinctive crown-like pattern on the petals.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system to access moisture.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
