Rubus × semigratiosus

Rubus × semigratiosus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, evergreen foliage, which provides year-round structure in gardens. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations in different regions.
