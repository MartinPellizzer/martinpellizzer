Ranunculus brevipes

Ranunculus brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have five to eight sepals that resemble petals.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
