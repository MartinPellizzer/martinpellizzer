Ruellia proxima

Ruellia proxima, commonly known as the small-leaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually less than 2 inches in length. Its showy blue or purple flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ruellia proxima is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
