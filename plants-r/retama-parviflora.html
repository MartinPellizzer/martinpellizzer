Retama parviflora

Retama parviflora, commonly known as the smallflower retama, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. The plant has small, oval leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Retama parviflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
