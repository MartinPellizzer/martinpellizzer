Rubus thyrsoideus var. thyrsanthus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. thyrsanthus, commonly known as the thrysoideus raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the production of small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and produces white or pale pink flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
