Rosa anserinifolia

Rosa anserinifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California and Nevada, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, velvety hairs, and its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces woody, thorny stems and has a compact growth habit.

Rosa anserinifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
