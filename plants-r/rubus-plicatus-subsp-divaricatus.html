Rubus plicatus subsp. divaricatus

Rubus plicatus subsp.

divaricatus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed by wildlife. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and often forms dense thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
