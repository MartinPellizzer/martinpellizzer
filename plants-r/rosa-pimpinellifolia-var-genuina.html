Rosa pimpinellifolia var. genuina

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval and slightly hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
