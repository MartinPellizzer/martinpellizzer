Rhododendron neriiflorum var. appropinquans

Rhododendron neriiflorum var. appropinquans, commonly known as the Appalachian rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are usually densely hairy. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is notable for its hardiness and ability to form dense thickets in forest understories.
