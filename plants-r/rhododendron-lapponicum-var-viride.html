Rhododendron lapponicum var. viride

Rhododendron lapponicum var. viride, commonly known as the green Lapland rhododendron, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates, making it a hardy species suitable for alpine and subarctic environments.

This variety is notable for its dense growth habit and ability to form thickets, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitats.
