Ranunculus cardiophyllus f. apetalus

Ranunculus cardiophyllus f. apetalus, commonly known as the heart-leaved buttercup variant, is a botanical variant characterized by its reduced floral structures.

This form exhibits a significant reduction in petal number, often displaying only a few stamen-like structures instead of typical petals. The leaves are typically heart-shaped, with a deep lobed base, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is native to specific regions in Europe and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Due to its unique morphology, it is of interest in studies of floral evolution and plant adaptation.
