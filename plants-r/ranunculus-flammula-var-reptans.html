Ranunculus flammula var. reptans

Ranunculus flammula var. reptans, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has a creeping habit, with stolons that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a distinctive shiny appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
