Rosa semisepium

Rosa semisepium, commonly known as the semi-sepaled rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring. Its flowers are characterized by their semi-double petals and are often used in traditional medicine.

Rosa semisepium is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some historical significance in herbal remedies.
