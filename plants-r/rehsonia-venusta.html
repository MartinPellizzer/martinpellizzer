Rehsonia venusta

Rehsonia venusta, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that hang in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, green, fleshy fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous membrane. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Rehsonia venusta is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
