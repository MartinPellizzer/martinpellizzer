Rosa sicula var. subsessiliflora

Rosa sicula var. subsessiliflora, commonly known as the Sicilian rose, is a shrub native to the island of Sicily.

It features upright stems with thorny growth, typical of many rose species. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval, serrated leaflets. Its flowers are pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in Mediterranean horticulture.
