Rhipsalis ramosissima

Rhipsalis ramosissima, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its branched, leaf-like stems that are often used for propagation. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the winter months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a popular houseplant.
