Rubus humifusus proles euviridis

Rubus humifusus proles euviridis, commonly known as the smooth raspberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, often prostrate stems that lie close to the ground, covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits composed of numerous drupes, which are typically red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and are usually hairy on the upper surface.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
