Racinaea pugiformis

Racinaea pugiformis, commonly known as the pouch-shaped fern, is a unique tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like fronds that are highly adapted for water retention in humid environments. The plant's fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it withstand periods of drought. It reproduces through spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia located on the undersides of the fronds.

Racinaea pugiformis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various small organisms.
