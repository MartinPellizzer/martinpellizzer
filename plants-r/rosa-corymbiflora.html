Rosa corymbiflora

Rosa corymbiflora, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red or yellow fruits known as hip fruits. Rosa corymbiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to temperate climates.

It is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
