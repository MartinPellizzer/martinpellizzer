Rosa mixticolor

Rosa mixticolor, commonly known as the variegated rose, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits striking foliage with variegated patterns of green, white, and gold.

It produces fragrant pink to red flowers during the summer months, adding visual appeal to gardens. The plant is native to the United States and thrives in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 9. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a glossy texture, while its stems are covered in thorns.

Rosa mixticolor is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its aesthetic and aromatic characteristics.
