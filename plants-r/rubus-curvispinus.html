Rubus curvispinus

Rubus curvispinus, commonly known as the hooked-awl blackberry, is a species of brambles native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit, a cluster of small, dark purple to black berries, ripens in late summer and is edible.

Rubus curvispinus is often found in moist, woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
