Ribes distans var. breviracemum

Ribes distans var. breviracemum, commonly known as the short-stalked gooseberry, is a compact shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features small, oval leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in early spring. The plant bears edible, tart berries that ripen to a bright red color in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in indigenous food traditions.
