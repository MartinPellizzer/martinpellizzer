Rheum telianum

Rheum telianum, commonly known as the giant rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking red or purple flower spikes in the summer, which are followed by small, yellowish fruits. Rheum telianum is known for its medicinal properties, with its leaves containing high concentrations of anthraquinones.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its impressive foliage and unique floral display.
