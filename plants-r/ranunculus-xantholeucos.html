Ranunculus xantholeucos

Ranunculus xantholeucos, commonly known as the yellow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight yellow petals and a yellow center, giving it a striking, sun-like appearance. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
