Rubus hupehensis

Rubus hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
