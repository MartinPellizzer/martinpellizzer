Ranunculus eriotorus

Ranunculus eriotorus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. The plant features bright yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, arranged in a cup-like shape, and is often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure, and the stems are usually hairy.

Ranunculus eriotorus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often brightly colored flowers.
