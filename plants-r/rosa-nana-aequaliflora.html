Rosa nana aequaliflora

Rosa nana aequaliflora, commonly known as the dwarf garden rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, with five to seven petals. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It produces hips, which are red berries containing seeds, and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in garden settings.
