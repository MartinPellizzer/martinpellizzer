Rubus incultus var. viridis

Rubus incultus var. viridis, commonly known as green brambles, is a variant of the bramble plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the presence of thorns along its stems. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are small, white or pale pink. The berries produced are edible, though often tart, and are used in local cuisines.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
