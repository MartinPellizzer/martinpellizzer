Rosa × reversa var. inermis

Rosa × reversa var. inermis, commonly known as the White Tea Rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its delicate, double blooms.

It features pale pink to white petals that are often described as resembling tea leaves, giving it its common name. The plant is known for its fragrant flowers and is typically grown as a shrub or climbing plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is popular in gardens and floral arrangements.
