Ranunculus auricomus subsp. paroicus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

paroicus, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features showy, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive orange center, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has palmately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
