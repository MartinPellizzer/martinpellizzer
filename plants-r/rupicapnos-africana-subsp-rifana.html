Rupicapnos africana subsp. rifana

Rupicapnos africana subsp.

rifana, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, orange-red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and hedges.
