Rosa afzeliana var. intromissa

Rosa afzeliana var. intromissa, commonly known as the Indian rose or the Himalayan rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to cooler climates.

Rosa afzeliana var. intromissa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
