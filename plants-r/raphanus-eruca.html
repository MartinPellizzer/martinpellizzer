Raphanus eruca

Raphanus eruca, commonly known as wild radish, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the mustard family. Its root system is fibrous, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and fields.

Raphanus eruca is an annual plant that is native to Europe and parts of Asia.
