Rudbeckia hirta var. annulata

Rudbeckia hirta var. annulata, commonly known as the black-eyed Susan, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow petals and a dark central disk. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
