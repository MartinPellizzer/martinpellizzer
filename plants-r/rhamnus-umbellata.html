Rhamnus umbellata

Rhamnus umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by reddish berries that are attractive to birds. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, making it adaptable to various soil types.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
