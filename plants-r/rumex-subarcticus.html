Rumex subarcticus

Rumex subarcticus, commonly known as Arctic sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold climates. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a reddish or purplish hue, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It is often found in tundra and mountainous areas where the growing season is short.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.
