Ranunculus obtegens

Ranunculus obtegens, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are broadly ovate to rounded, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
