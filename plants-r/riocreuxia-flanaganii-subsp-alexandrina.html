Riocreuxia flanaganii subsp. alexandrina

Riocreuxia flanaganii subsp.

alexandrina, commonly known as the Alexandrine rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky outcrops. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that is edible and attracts various wildlife.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter.
