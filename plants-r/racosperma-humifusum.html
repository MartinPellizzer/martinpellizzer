Racosperma humifusum

Racosperma humifusum, commonly known as the prostrate acacia, is a low-growing shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its sprawling, woody stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typical of the genus Racosperma. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

Racosperma humifusum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
