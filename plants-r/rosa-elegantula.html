Rosa elegantula

Rosa elegantula, commonly known as the elegant rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red hips that contain seeds.

Rosa elegantula is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
