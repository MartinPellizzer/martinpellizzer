Romulea flava var. hirsuta

Romulea flava var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy yellow romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It produces clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and is characterized by its hairy leaves, which give it its specific epithet "hirsuta.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and hardy nature.
