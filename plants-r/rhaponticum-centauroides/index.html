Rhaponticum Centauroides

Rhaponticum centauroides is a striking member of the Asteraceae family known for its vibrant yellow flowers and robust growth. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant has adapted to harsh climates and poor soils. Its common name, "Centaur's shield," hints at its historical use in traditional medicine. The plant's tall stature and showy blooms make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and resilience. Despite its ornamental appeal, it also holds cultural and medicinal significance in various regions.
