Ranunculus bottnicus

Ranunculus bottnicus, commonly known as the northern buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its distinctive floral structure and often toxic sap.

The species is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in cool, temperate climates.
