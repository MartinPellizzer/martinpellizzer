Rubus cuneifolius

Rubus cuneifolius, commonly known as the prickly rose or blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a distinctly wedge-shaped leaflet. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Rubus cuneifolius plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
