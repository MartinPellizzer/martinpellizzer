Rorippa millefolia

Rorippa millefolia, commonly known as water pepper, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. This annual herb typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp soils. Its small white or yellowish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
