Rubus sorbus

Rubus sorbus, commonly known as whitebeam, is a genus of flowering plants in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It consists of about 100 species, many of which are shrubs or small trees native to Europe, Asia, and North America. The plants are characterized by their alternate, simple leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. The fruit is a small, aggregate berry that is typically red or black when mature.

These plants are often found in temperate regions and are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
