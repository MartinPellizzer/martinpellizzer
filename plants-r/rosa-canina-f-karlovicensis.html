Rosa canina f. karlovicensis

Rosa canina f. karlovicensis, commonly known as the Karlovicensis rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinkish-red flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa canina species. This form is native to Central Europe and is often found in woodland and hedgerow environments.

The plant produces edible hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
