Rubus thyrsifloroideus

Rubus thyrsifloroideus, commonly known as the yellow raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the production of yellow, edible berries. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters. Its botanical structure includes thorny stems and a system of runners for vegetative propagation.

The fruit is typically harvested in late summer and is valued for its tart flavor.
