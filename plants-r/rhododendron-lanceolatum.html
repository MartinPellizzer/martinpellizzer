Rhododendron lanceolatum

Rhododendron lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

Rhododendron lanceolatum is also noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand colder temperatures.
