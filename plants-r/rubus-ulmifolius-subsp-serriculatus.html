Rubus ulmifolius subsp. serriculatus

Rubus ulmifolius subsp.

serriculatus, commonly known as the serrated bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring, followed by edible, sweet berries in late summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This subspecies is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed areas.
