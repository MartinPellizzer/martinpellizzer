Ranunculus bulbosus var. platycaulis

Ranunculus bulbosus var. platycaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features distinctive, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals. The plant typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils and thrives in wetlands or along streambanks. It is characterized by its short, stout rhizomes and is often found in damp meadows and marshy areas.

The variety platycaulis is distinguished by its broader, more rounded leaves compared to the typical form.
