Rudbeckia flava var. perbracteata

Rudbeckia flava var. perbracteata, commonly known as the yellow coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of darker yellow to orange-brown florets. The plant has lance-shaped, rough-textured leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rudbeckia flava var. perbracteata is known for its showy blooms and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
