Rhamnus oleoides subsp. bourgeana

Rhamnus oleoides subsp.

bourgeana, commonly known as the Persian buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and the Middle East. It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are usually dark green and have a glossy appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue-black berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
