Rubus × pallidiflorens

Rubus × pallidiflorens, commonly known as the paleflower blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, which are typically dark purple or black, are edible and have a tart flavor.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
