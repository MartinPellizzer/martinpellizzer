Ranunculus ameres

Ranunculus ameres, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, radially symmetrical flowers. The plant typically features bright yellow petals and a central cluster of numerous stamens. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Ranunculus ameres is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
