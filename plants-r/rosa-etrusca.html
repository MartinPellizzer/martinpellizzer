Rosa etrusca

Rosa etrusca, commonly known as the Etruscan rose, is a rare and ancient species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and fragrant scent, which are similar to other rose species. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its historical and botanical significance, it is often studied for its genetic relationships to other Rosa species.
