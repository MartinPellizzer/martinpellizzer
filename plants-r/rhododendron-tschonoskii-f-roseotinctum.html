Rhododendron tschonoskii f. roseotinctum

Rhododendron tschonoskii f. roseotinctum, commonly known as the rose-tinctured rhododendron, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features dense, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy and emit a mild fragrance. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique coloration and compact growth habit make it a desirable addition to shaded garden spaces.
