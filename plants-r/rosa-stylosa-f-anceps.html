Rosa stylosa f. anceps

Rosa stylosa f. anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dog rose, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It is a hybrid form of Rosa stylosa, characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and produces rosehips that are edible. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
