Rosa × dumetorum var. klukii

Rosa × dumetorum var. klukii, commonly known as the Kluk's shrub rose, is a compact, thorny shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dark green, trifoliate leaves and produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover. It is relatively hardy, tolerating cold climates and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient choice for gardeners.

The botanical name reflects its hybrid origin, combining traits from various Rosa species.
