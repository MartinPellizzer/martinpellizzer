Rosa communis var. pseudomedioxima

Rosa communis var. pseudomedioxima, commonly known as the pink tea rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its soft pink to pale red flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and glossy, dark green foliage. The plant produces fragrant blooms that are often used in the production of rose tea and essential oils. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its aromatic properties.
