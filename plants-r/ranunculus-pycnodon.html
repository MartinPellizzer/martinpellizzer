Ranunculus pycnodon

Ranunculus pycnodon, commonly known as the dense-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ranunculus pycnodon is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
