Rosa pineliensis

Rosa pineliensis, commonly known as the Pinel's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has fragrant, compound leaves and is often found in rocky or well-drained soils. Rosa pineliensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

It is also notable for its historical use in traditional medicine.
