Rhododendron aureum subsp. aureum

Rhododendron aureum subsp.

aureum, commonly known as the golden rhododendron, is a shrub native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green and densely arranged along the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
