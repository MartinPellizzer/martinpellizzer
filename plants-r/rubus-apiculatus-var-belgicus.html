Rubus apiculatus var. belgicus

Rubus apiculatus var. belgicus, commonly known as the Belgian raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
