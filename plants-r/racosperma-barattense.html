Racosperma barattense

Racosperma barattense, commonly known as the Baratt's acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow, pod-like fruits. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Racosperma barattense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
