Rhododendron argyrophyllum subsp. argyrophyllum

Rhododendron argyrophyllum subsp.

argyrophyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that exhibits a distinctive silvery-white underside, giving it its common name.

The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish in color, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and seasonal blooms.
