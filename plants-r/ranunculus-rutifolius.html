Ranunculus rutifolius

Ranunculus rutifolius, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
