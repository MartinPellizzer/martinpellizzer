Rubus austrosinensis

Rubus austrosinensis, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
