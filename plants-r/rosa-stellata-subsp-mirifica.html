Rosa stellata subsp. mirifica

Rosa stellata subsp.

mirifica, commonly known as the golden stellate rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family. It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
