Rubus rosifolius pleniflorus

Rubus rosifolius pleniflorus, commonly known as the roseleaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its fruits are edible berries that are typically red or black when ripe.

This shrub is native to parts of East Asia and is often found in woodland or scrubland habitats.
