Rosa indica var. humilis

Rosa indica var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf tea rose, is a compact shrub native to China.

It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves and a bushy growth habit, making it suitable for container gardening or as a border plant. It is relatively disease-resistant and prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Its historical use in tea preparation has contributed to its cultural significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
