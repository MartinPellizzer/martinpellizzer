Rubus hebecaulis var. oboranus

Rubus hebecaulis var. oboranus, commonly known as the Japanese raspberry, is a shrub native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It features slender, arching stems with small, thornless branches. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits composed of numerous drupelets, typically red when ripe. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that are hairy beneath.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
