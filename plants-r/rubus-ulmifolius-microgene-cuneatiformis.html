Rubus ulmifolius microgene cuneatiformis

Rubus ulmifolius microgene cuneatiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark red to black berries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with a distinctive cuneate shape, and its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring partial shade.

The fruit is typically harvested in late summer and is used in jams and desserts.
