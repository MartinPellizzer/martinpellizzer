Ranunculus pulchridentatus

Ranunculus pulchridentatus, commonly known as the beautiful-toothed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features compound leaves with serrated edges, which contribute to its common name. Its flowers are usually yellow with numerous stamens, and it blooms in the spring.

Ranunculus pulchridentatus is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
