Ranunculus umbellatus

Ranunculus umbellatus, commonly known as the umbrella buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, arranged in a single bloom at the end of each stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
