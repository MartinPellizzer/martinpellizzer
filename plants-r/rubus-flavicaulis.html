Rubus flavicaulis

Rubus flavicaulis, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, yellowish stems and small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are clustered in umbels. Its leaves are palmately lobed with serrated edges and covered in fine hairs.

Rubus flavicaulis is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.
