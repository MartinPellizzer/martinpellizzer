Ranunculus pusillidens

Ranunculus pusillidens, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive narrow, lance-shaped appearance.

Ranunculus pusillidens is known for its short stature and is often found in montane and alpine regions.
