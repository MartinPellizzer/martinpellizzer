Rubus × chlorostylus var. rotundatus

Rubus × chlorostylus var. rotundatus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry.

It is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both blackberries and raspberries, with a unique combination of traits. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it has a woody stem that can grow up to several meters in height.
