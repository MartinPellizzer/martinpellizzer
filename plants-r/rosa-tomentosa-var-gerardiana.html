Rosa tomentosa var. gerardiana

Rosa tomentosa var. gerardiana, commonly known as the Gerard's shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, often spreading along the ground or forming low bushes. Its foliage consists of ovate, pubescent leaves that are typically covered in fine, grayish hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
