Rosa montana f. transiens

Rosa montana f. transiens, commonly known as the Transient Mountain Rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its compact growth habit and vigorous flowering.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in cooler, temperate climates. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the delicate blossoms.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, visually appealing shrub.
