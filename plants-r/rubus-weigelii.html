Rubus weigelii

Rubus weigelii, commonly known as the Weigel's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces edible berries that are similar in appearance to other blackberries but have a distinct flavor. Its thorny stems and compound leaves are typical of the Rubus genus.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
