Rosa samuelssonii

Rosa samuelssonii, commonly known as the Samuelsson's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa samuelssonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
