Rubus micans microgene obsectifrons

Rubus micans microgene obsectifrons, commonly known as the velvetleaf blackberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
