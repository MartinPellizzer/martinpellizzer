Rosa × dumetorum var. rivularis

Rosa × dumetorum var. rivularis, commonly known as the water rose, is a hybrid shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland edges. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and the plant produces small, reddish-orange hips in autumn.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for wildlife.
