Rhododendron quadrasianum var. borneense

Rhododendron quadrasianum var. borneense, commonly known as the Bornean rhododendron, is a species native to the tropical regions of Borneo.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring, producing clusters of pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
