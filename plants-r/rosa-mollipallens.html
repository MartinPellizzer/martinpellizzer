Rosa mollipallens

Rosa mollipallens, commonly known as the soft-pale rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade, preferring moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Rosa mollipallens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
