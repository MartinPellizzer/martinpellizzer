Rosa pimpinellifolia var. ripartii

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. ripartii, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval and serrated. This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
