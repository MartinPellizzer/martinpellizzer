Rhaphidophora warburgii

Rhaphidophora warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's arrowroot plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, and its leaves are typically arrow-shaped, giving it its common name.

Rhaphidophora warburgii is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
