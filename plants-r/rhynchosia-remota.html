Rhynchosia remota

Rhynchosia remota, commonly known as the remote spurred cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the pods contain edible seeds that are used in some traditional cuisines.

Rhynchosia remota is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
