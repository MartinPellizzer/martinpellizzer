Ruellia uniflora

Ruellia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple. Ruellia uniflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a tropical or subtropical look.
