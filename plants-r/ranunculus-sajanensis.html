Ranunculus sajanensis

Ranunculus sajanensis, commonly known as the Sajan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy and often toxic species. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five to seven petals and numerous stamens.

Ranunculus sajanensis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, delicate blooms.
