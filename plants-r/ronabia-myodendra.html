Ronabia myodendra

Ronabia myodendra, commonly known as the silverleaf myodendra, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in poor, rocky soils.

Ronabia myodendra is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
