Racosperma chrysopodum

Racosperma chrysopodum, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a species of shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense spherical inflorescences. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has compound leaves with multiple leaflets. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Racosperma chrysopodum is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also an important plant in Australian ecology.
