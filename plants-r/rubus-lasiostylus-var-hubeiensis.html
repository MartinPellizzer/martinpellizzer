Rubus lasiostylus var. hubeiensis

Rubus lasiostylus var. hubeiensis, commonly known as the Hubei raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry genus native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, though they may vary in color depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in culinary applications compared to other raspberry varieties.
