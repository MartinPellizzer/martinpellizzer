Rudbeckia fulgida var. sullivantii

Rudbeckia fulgida var. sullivantii, commonly known as the "Glossy Coneflower," is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, orange-yellow flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, glossy coating, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Rudbeckia fulgida var. sullivantii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
