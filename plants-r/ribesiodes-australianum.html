Ribesiodes australianum

Ribesiodes australianum, commonly known as the Australian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, greyish bark. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
