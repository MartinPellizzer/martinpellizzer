Ranunculus sardous var. intermedius

Ranunculus sardous var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has palmate leaves divided into five to seven lobes, and its stems are usually hairy.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
