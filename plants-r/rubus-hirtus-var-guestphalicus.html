Rubus hirtus var. guestphalicus

Rubus hirtus var. guestphalicus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a hardy, adaptable species within the Rosaceae family.
