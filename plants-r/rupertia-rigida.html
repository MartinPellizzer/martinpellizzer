Rupertia rigida

Rupertia rigida, commonly known as the rigid rupertia, is a flowering plant native to California and southern Oregon.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright, rigid stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Rupertia rigida is often found in open woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
