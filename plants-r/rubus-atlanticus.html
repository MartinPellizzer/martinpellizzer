Rubus atlanticus

Rubus atlanticus, commonly known as the Atlantic blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern coast of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along coastal areas and estuaries. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor, often used in local culinary traditions.
