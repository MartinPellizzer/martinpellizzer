Rubus iodes

Rubus iodes, commonly known as evergreen blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits, which are black berries, are typically harvested in late summer and are edible.

Rubus iodes is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
