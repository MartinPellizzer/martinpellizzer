Rosa agrestis subvar. pseudosepium

Rosa agrestis subvar. pseudosepium, commonly known as the rough-stemmed wild rose, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. Its flowers are pink to reddish in color, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The fruit is a reddish-pink hip, containing achenes that are dispersed by wind or animals.
