Rhododendron uwaense

Rhododendron uwaense, commonly known as the Uwa rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its floral display.

Rhododendron uwaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with moist, acidic soil.
