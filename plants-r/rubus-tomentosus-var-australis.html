Rubus tomentosus var. australis

Rubus tomentosus var. australis, commonly known as the Australian brambles, is a variant of the bramble plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a dense, woolly pubescence. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to the temperate climate of southern Australia.
