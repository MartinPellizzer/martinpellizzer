Rhododendron × kisoanum

Rhododendron × kisoanum, commonly known as the Kiso azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.
