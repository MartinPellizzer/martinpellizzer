Ranunculus flabellatus proles uncinatus

Ranunculus flabellatus proles uncinatus, commonly known as the hooked leaf buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The specific epithet "proles uncinatus" refers to the hooked shape of its leaflets, a distinguishing botanical feature.
