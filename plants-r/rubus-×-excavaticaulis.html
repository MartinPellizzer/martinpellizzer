Rubus × excavaticaulis

Rubus × excavaticaulis, commonly known as the roughleaf blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to the western United States and thrives in a variety of soil types, including rocky and clay soils. Its leaves are usually serrated and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible berries, which are used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
