Rubus hirtus var. tenuidens

Rubus hirtus var. tenuidens, commonly known as the slender bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called berries, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in horticulture for ornamental purposes.
