Rhynchopyle

Rhynchopyle, commonly known as the beak-like structure in certain plants, is a specialized anatomical feature found in some species of aquatic and semi-aquatic plants.

It is typically located at the base of the leaf or stem and serves as a site for gas exchange and nutrient absorption. The rhynchopyle is often surrounded by specialized cells that facilitate the uptake of dissolved minerals from water. Its presence is an adaptation to aquatic environments, allowing the plant to thrive in submerged or wet conditions.

This structure plays a crucial role in the plant's survival and physiological function in its habitat.
