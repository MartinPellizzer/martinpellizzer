Rubus incisus var. proprius

Rubus incisus var. proprius, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. These fruits are edible, sweet, and often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
