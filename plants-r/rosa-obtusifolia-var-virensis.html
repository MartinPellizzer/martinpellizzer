Rosa obtusifolia var. virensis

Rosa obtusifolia var. virensis, commonly known as the green-leafed shrub rose, is a variant of the shrub rose species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and broad, dark green foliage that remains lush throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its compact size and long-lasting foliage.
