Rubus sorbifolius var. myriadenus

Rubus sorbifolius var. myriadenus, commonly known as the many-leaved blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor and are used in local culinary traditions.
