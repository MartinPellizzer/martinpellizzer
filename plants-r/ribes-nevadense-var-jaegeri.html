Ribes nevadense var. jaegeri

Ribes nevadense var. jaegeri, commonly known as the Jaeger's gooseberry, is a rare variant of the gooseberry plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
