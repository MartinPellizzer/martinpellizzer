Rubus fuscus var. prionodontus

Rubus fuscus var. prionodontus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in stiff, prickly hairs.

This species is known for its robust growth and adaptability, making it a common sight in natural and semi-natural habitats.
