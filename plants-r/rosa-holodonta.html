Rosa holodonta

Rosa holodonta, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thornless stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with oval-shaped leaflets that are covered in a fine layer of down.

Rosa holodonta is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
