Racosperma scirpifolium

Racosperma scirpifolium, commonly known as the swamp senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as swamps and riverbanks. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by water or wind.

The species is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
