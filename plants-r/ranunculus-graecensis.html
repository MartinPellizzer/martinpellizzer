Ranunculus graecensis

Ranunculus graecensis, commonly known as the Greek buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to woodland gardens.

Ranunculus graecensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
