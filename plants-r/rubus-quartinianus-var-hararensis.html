Rubus quartinianus var. hararensis

Rubus quartinianus var. hararensis, commonly known as the Harare blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and have a firm, tart flavor. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and grows as a shrub with thorny stems. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
