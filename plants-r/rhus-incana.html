Rhus incana

Rhus incana, commonly known as smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a smooth, almost silky texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy berries that are a food source for birds and wildlife. Rhus incana thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a common and recognizable plant in the Asteraceae family.
