Rosa × subcollina

Rosa × subcollina, commonly known as the shrub rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa bracteata.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and dense, thorny stems that form a compact shrub. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have serrated edges, while its hips are red and ornamental.

Rosa × subcollina is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various soil types and climates.
