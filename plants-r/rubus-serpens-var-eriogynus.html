Rubus serpens var. eriogynus

Rubus serpens var. eriogynus, commonly known as the purple-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, which help it climb and spread across vegetation. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. Its leaves are alternate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
