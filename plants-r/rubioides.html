Rubioides

Rubioides, commonly known as the false holly or redroot, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rubiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, often spiny leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plants typically grow in moist, shaded environments and are found in various parts of the Americas. Rubioides species are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

They play an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
