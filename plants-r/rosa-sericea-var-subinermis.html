Rosa sericea var. subinermis

Rosa sericea var. subinermis, commonly known as the silky rose or silverleaf rose, is a shrub-like species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

This variety is also valued for its use in landscaping and as a cut flower.
