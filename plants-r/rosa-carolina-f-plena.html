Rosa carolina f. plena

Rosa carolina f. plena, commonly known as the Carolina double pink, is a cultivated form of the native Rosa carolina.

It is characterized by its double pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
