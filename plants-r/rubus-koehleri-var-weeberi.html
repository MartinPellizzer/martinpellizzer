Rubus koehleri var. weeberi

Rubus koehleri var. weeberi, commonly known as the Weeber's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
