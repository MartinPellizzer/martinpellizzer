Rubus fruticosus subsp. rubeolus

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

rubeolus, commonly known as the redberry bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Due to its hardy nature, it is both ecologically significant and sometimes considered a weed.
