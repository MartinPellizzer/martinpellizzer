Rhinephyllum parvifolium

Rhinephyllum parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leafed rhinephyllum, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, Rhinephyllum parvifolium is considered vulnerable and is protected in several conservation programs.
