Rubus vinaceus

Rubus vinaceus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically bright red when ripe. These fruits are rich in vitamins and antioxidants, making them both nutritious and flavorful.

Rubus vinaceus is native to various regions in Europe and Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.
