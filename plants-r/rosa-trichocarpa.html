Rosa trichocarpa

Rosa trichocarpa, commonly known as the Pacific coast rose, is a species of wild rose native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, fragrant pink to white flowers, and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces red or orange hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. Rosa trichocarpa is often found in moist, coastal environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.

It is also an important species in the study of rose genetics and evolution.
