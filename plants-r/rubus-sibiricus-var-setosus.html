Rubus sibiricus var. setosus

Rubus sibiricus var. setosus, commonly known as the Siberian raspberry, is a shrub native to the cold climates of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries and ornamental value.
