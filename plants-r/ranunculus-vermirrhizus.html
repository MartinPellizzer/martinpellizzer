Ranunculus vermirrhizus

Ranunculus vermirrhizus, commonly known as the wormseed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem. The plant has alternate, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Ranunculus vermirrhizus is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
