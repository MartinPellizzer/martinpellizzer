Rubus × gugleri

Rubus × gugleri, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are commonly referred to as blackberries, and are known for their sweet-tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental and edible qualities, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial fruit production.
