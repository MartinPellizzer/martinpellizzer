Rubus coriicolor

Rubus coriicolor, commonly known as the purple-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, nearly black berries that turn a striking purple hue when ripe. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant white flowers in the spring. Its fruit is often used in jams and pies, though it is less common than other blackberry varieties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family.
