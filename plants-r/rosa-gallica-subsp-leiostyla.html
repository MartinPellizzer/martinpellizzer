Rosa gallica subsp. leiostyla

Rosa gallica subsp.

leiostyla, commonly known as the French rose, is a subspecies native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy, semi-double petals. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and historical use in perfumery.
