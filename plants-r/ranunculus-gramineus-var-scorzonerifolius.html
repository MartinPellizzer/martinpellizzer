Ranunculus gramineus var. scorzonerifolius

Ranunculus gramineus var. scorzonerifolius, commonly known as the Scorzonerifolius buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with 5-7 sepals that resemble petals, and it typically grows in moist, shady environments. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a silky, white down. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic sap.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
