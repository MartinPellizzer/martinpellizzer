Ranunculus platyphyllus

Ranunculus platyphyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and covered in glandular dots. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

The species is known for its attractive appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
