Ranunculus asiaticus var. bereniceus

Ranunculus asiaticus var. bereniceus, commonly known as the Berenice's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, double-flowered blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red, with a distinctive central tuft of stamens. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are alternate along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
