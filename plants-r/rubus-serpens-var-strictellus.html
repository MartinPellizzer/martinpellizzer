Rubus serpens var. strictellus

Rubus serpens var. strictellus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white and clustered in racemes.

This species is often found in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.
