Rosa dianthiflora

Rosa dianthiflora, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool and dry conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, with five petals and a prominent central cone. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Rosa dianthiflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
