Ranunculus vitellinus

Ranunculus vitellinus, commonly known as the yellow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with five to eleven yellow petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

Ranunculus vitellinus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful appearance.
