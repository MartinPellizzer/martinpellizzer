Romulea ambigua var. aurantiaca

Romulea ambigua var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden romulea, is a striking bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of showy, orange-red flowers with a distinctive, spurred petal. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period typically occurs in late winter to early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens during cooler months.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is a sought-after plant among succulent and bulb enthusiasts.
