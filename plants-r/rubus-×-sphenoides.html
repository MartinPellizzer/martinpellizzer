Rubus × sphenoides

Rubus × sphenoides, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and produces dark purple to black berries. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The botanical features of Rubus × sphenoides make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
