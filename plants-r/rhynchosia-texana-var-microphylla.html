Rhynchosia texana var. microphylla

Rhynchosia texana var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and woodlands.

Its small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers contribute to its ornamental value, while its seeds are used in traditional and modern agricultural practices.
