Rosa tomentosa var. vera

Rosa tomentosa var. vera, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and thorny stems, which provide structural support and protection. The plant produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically ovate with a velvety texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Rosa tomentosa var. vera is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is valued for its hardiness and adaptability.
