Rubus idaeus var. laevis

Rubus idaeus var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves.

It produces dark purple to black berries that are typically larger and sweeter than those of the common blackberry. The plant is native to North America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is a perennial shrub that can grow up to several feet tall, with compound leaves and thorny stems.

The fruit is widely cultivated for its flavor and used in various culinary applications.
