Racosperma sibilans

Racosperma sibilans, commonly known as the sibilant acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often spindly growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the acacia genus. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous tiny leaflets, and it is often found in dry, sandy soils.

Racosperma sibilans is adapted to arid environments and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules.
