Rosa adenophora

Rosa adenophora, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its dense, woolly hairs covering its stems and leaves. The plant produces pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically dark green and slightly serrated, while its fruit is a red, hairy rose hip.

Rosa adenophora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in cooler climates.
