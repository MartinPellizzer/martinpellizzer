Rosa sepium var. haringiana

Rosa sepium var. haringiana, commonly known as the Haring's rose, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Rosa sepium species.

It is characterized by its striking, deep red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its unique coloration and ornamental appeal.
