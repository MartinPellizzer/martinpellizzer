Rosa sepium var. virgultorum

Rosa sepium var. virgultorum, commonly known as the Virginia rose, is a variant of the shrub rose native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has elliptical, dark green leaves. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their delicate appearance and pleasant scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
