Rubus duthieanus var. tomentosus

Rubus duthieanus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
