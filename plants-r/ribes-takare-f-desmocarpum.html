Ribes takare f. desmocarpum

Ribes takare f. desmocarpum, commonly known as the purple-flowered gooseberry, is a botanical variant of the Ribes genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple to reddish flowers and small, edible berries. The plant is native to certain regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and it produces a fragrant aroma.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
