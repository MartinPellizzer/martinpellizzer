Rubus aestivus

Rubus aestivus, commonly known as the summer bramble, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the summer months, followed by red, hairy berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems.
