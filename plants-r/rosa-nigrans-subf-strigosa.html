Rosa nigrans subf. strigosa

Rosa nigrans subf.

strigosa, commonly known as the rough-stemmed dog rose, is a subshrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its dark red to black flowers and hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are opposite, with ovate to elliptic leaflets, and the flowers are usually borne in clusters.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
