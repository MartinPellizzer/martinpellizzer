Reseda sesamoides var. purpurascens

Reseda sesamoides var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered reseda, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers range from pale pink to deep purple.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
