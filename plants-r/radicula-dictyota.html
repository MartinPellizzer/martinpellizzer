Radicula dictyota

Radicula dictyota, commonly known as the giant kelp, is a large brown alga belonging to the order Fucales.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like fronds that can grow up to 30 meters in length. The plant has a complex structure with a holdfast, stipe, and blade, typical of kelp species. It thrives in temperate coastal waters, attaching itself to rocky substrates.

Radicula dictyota plays a crucial role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
