Rhus trilobata var. simplicifolia

Rhus trilobata var. simplicifolia, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern and central United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are typically oval and have a smooth margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by red, fuzzy berries that are a characteristic feature. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it often grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and roadsides.

The plant is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its ornamental value.
