Rubus aschersoniellus

Rubus aschersoniellus, commonly known as the aschersoniellus blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils. Its fruit is edible, though typically tart, and is used in local cuisines.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and exhibits typical characteristics of the Rubidoideae subfamily.
