Racosperma anfractuosum

Racosperma anfractuosum, commonly known as the variable acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Racosperma anfractuosum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important species in ecological restoration.
