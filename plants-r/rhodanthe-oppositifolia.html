Rhodanthe oppositifolia

Rhodanthe oppositifolia, commonly known as the oppositeleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with white centers, which bloom in spring and summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Rhodanthe oppositifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
