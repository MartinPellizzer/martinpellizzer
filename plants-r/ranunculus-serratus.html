Ranunculus serratus

Ranunculus serratus, commonly known as the sawleaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaves and bright yellow flowers with prominent, rounded petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other buttercup species.

Ranunculus serratus is often found in temperate regions and can be a common sight in early spring.
