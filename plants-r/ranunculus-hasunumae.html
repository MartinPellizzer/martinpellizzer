Ranunculus hasunumae

Ranunculus hasunumae, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in rocky or clay soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
