Rosa stylosa subvar. pseudocorymbifera

Rosa stylosa subvar. pseudocorymbifera, commonly known as the slender-stemmed rose, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of eastern Asia.

It features thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in clusters. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and natural habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
