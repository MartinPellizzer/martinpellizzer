Rhinocidium disciferum

Rhinocidium disciferum, commonly known as the disc-flowered rhinocidium, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, disc-like flowers that are typically a mix of white and yellow hues. The plant has a compact growth habit, often forming dense clusters of foliage and blooms. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native environment.
