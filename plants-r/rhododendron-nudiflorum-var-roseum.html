Rhododendron nudiflorum var. roseum

Rhododendron nudiflorum var. roseum, commonly known as the Rose-flowered Azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to rose-purple flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with dense, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a long flowering period, which adds ornamental value to landscapes.
