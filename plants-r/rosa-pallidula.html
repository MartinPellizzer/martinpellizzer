Rosa pallidula

Rosa pallidula, commonly known as the pale rose, is a species of flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in thorns. Rosa pallidula prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
