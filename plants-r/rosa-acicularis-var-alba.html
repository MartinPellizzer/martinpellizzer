Rosa acicularis var. alba

Rosa acicularis var. alba, commonly known as the white prairie rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, thornless stems and produces showy, single white flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
