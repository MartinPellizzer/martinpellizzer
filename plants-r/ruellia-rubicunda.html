Ruellia rubicunda

Ruellia rubicunda, commonly known as red ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular, violet to red flowers. Its flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Ruellia rubicunda is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
