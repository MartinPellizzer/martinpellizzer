Rubus radula subsp. carpaticus

Rubus radula subsp.

carpaticus, commonly known as the Carpathian bramble, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruits are aggregate berries that ripen in late summer, attracting various wildlife.

It thrives in moist, wooded habitats and is adapted to the cooler climates of its native region.
