Rosa rugosa f. alba

Rosa rugosa f. alba, commonly known as the white form of the rugosa rose, is a variant of the rugosa rose characterized by its pale white to ivory-colored flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub native to coastal regions of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is known for its rugged, thorny stems and its ability to thrive in saline and sandy soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing a distinctive, slightly fragrant scent.

Rosa rugosa f. alba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature, making it suitable for coastal gardens and erosion control.
