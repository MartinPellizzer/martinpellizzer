Reaumuria alternifolia

Reaumuria alternifolia, commonly known as the alternateleaf sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate arrangement of small, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has adapted to harsh environments with its deep root system and ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems. It produces small, red berries that are edible and serve as a food source for various wildlife.

This species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
