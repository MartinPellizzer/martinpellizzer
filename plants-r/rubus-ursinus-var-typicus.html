Rubus ursinus var. typicus

Rubus ursinus var. typicus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a shrub native to western North America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant bears black, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Its berries are widely used in culinary applications and are valued for their nutritional content.
