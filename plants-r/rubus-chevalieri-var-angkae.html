Rubus chevalieri var. angkae

Rubus chevalieri var. angkae, commonly known as the Angkara raspberry, is a species of brambles native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pink flowers during the spring season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
