Rhagadiolus

Rhagadiolus, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a unique species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking crimson petals and a compact, elegant floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include long, slender leaves and a single, showy flower that blooms once a year.

Rhagadiolus is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and rarity in cultivation.
