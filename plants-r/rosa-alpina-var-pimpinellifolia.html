Rosa alpina var. pimpinellifolia

Rosa alpina var. pimpinellifolia, commonly known as the Alpine pink rose, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, often arching stems covered in soft, grayish-green foliage. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five to seven petals, and are produced in clusters during late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its fragrant blooms and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

It is well adapted to cold climates and is relatively hardy, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens.
