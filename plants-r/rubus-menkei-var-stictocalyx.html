Rubus menkei var. stictocalyx

Rubus menkei var. stictocalyx, commonly known as the purple-leaf bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged foliage, which sets it apart from other Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
