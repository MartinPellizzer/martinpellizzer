Ranunculus completus

Ranunculus completus, commonly known as the complete buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with numerous petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

Ranunculus completus is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic sap.
