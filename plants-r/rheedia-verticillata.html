Rheedia verticillata

Rheedia verticillata, commonly known as the clustered rheedia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Rheedia verticillata thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
