Rubus obrosus var. ribiformis

Rubus obrosus var. ribiformis, commonly known as the ribbed blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a distinctive ribbed or corrugated texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
