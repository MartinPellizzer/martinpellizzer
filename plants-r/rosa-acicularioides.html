Rosa acicularioides

Rosa acicularioides, commonly known as the needleleaf rose, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, thorned stems and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens across the Mediterranean and temperate regions.
