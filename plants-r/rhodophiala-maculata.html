Rhodophiala maculata

Rhodophiala maculata, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple with a distinctive spotted pattern on the petals. The plant has a tuberous root system that allows it to survive in harsh, high-altitude environments. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Rhodophiala maculata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
