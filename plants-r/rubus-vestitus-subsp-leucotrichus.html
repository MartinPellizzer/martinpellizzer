Rubus vestitus subsp. leucotrichus

Rubus vestitus subsp.

leucotrichus, commonly known as the white-haired raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, often found in temperate regions.
