Rhododendron yunnanense subsp. bhutanicum

Rhododendron yunnanense subsp.

bhutanicum, commonly known as the Bhutanese rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in Bhutan and northeastern India. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, shaded forest environments.

The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
