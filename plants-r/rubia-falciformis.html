Rubia falciformis

Rubia falciformis, commonly known as the sickleleaf madder, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, sickle-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its roots contain a red dye, historically used in textile production.

Rubia falciformis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.
