Ranunculus scherosii

Ranunculus scherosii, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. This species thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils. It is a perennial herb that typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude meadows and slopes.
