Rubus triphyllus var. gamophyllus

Rubus triphyllus var. gamophyllus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three leaflets, which are typically serrated and hairy. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are bright red when mature and contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in woodland edges or disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and is widely cultivated for both commercial and home gardening purposes.
