Ruprechtia glauca

Ruprechtia glauca, commonly known as the blue ruprechtia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Ruprechtia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively low maintenance once established.
