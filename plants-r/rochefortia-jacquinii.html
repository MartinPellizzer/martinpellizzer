Rochefortia jacquinii

Rochefortia jacquinii, commonly known as the golden bell, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Rochefortia jacquinii is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
