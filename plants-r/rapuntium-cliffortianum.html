Rapuntium cliffortianum

Rapuntium cliffortianum, commonly known as the cliffortian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into distinctive pappus-bearing seeds, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of arid and semi-arid environments.
