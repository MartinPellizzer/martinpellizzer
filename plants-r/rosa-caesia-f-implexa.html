Rosa caesia f. implexa

Rosa caesia f. implexa, commonly known as the Crimson Fairy Rose, is a compact, flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fragrant, semi-double flowers that range in color from deep red to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces hips that are typically red or orange in color. It is well-suited for rock gardens or containers due to its compact growth habit and ornamental value.

This cultivar is valued for its unique floral form and cold hardiness, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
