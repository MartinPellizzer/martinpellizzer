Rubus orthocladus subsp. erythranthus

Rubus orthocladus subsp.

erythranthus, commonly known as the red-stemmed raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its erect or decumbent growth habit and produces clusters of small, aggregate fruits.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and is often found in humid, forested areas. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the species is notable for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reddish stems and the presence of dense, glandular hairs on its foliage.
