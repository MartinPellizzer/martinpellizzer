Rubus × dichrous

Rubus × dichrous, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces aggregate fruits composed of small drupelets, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in a variety of soil types. It is widely cultivated for its edible fruits, which are valued for their flavor and nutritional content.

Additionally, it plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
