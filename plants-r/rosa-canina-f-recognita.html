Rosa canina f. recognita

Rosa canina f. recognita, commonly known as the recognized dog rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose species.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring five petals and numerous stamens. The plant typically produces bright red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its fruits, known as hips, are orange-red, hairy, and contain a high concentration of vitamin C. This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as woodlands and hedgerows across Europe.
