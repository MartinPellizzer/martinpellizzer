Racosperma microneurum

Racosperma microneurum, commonly known as the small-veined acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, narrow leaves with prominent veins, and its flowers are arranged in spikes. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting well to dry conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
