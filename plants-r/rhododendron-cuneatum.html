Rhododendron cuneatum

Rhododendron cuneatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in early spring. The plant has leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron cuneatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
