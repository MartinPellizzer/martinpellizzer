Rhododendron calophytum

Rhododendron calophytum, commonly known as the yellow rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features broad, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, fuzzy hairs. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Rhododendron calophytum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and horticultural collections.
