Rubus dolichophyllus var. pubescens

Rubus dolichophyllus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. Its flowers are pink to white and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The hairy raspberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ecological benefits.
