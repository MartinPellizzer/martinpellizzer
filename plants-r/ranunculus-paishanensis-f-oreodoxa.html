Ranunculus paishanensis f. oreodoxa

Ranunculus paishanensis f. oreodoxa, commonly known as the Shaoxing buttercup, is a rare and endangered flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features simple, trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is critically important for conservation efforts.
