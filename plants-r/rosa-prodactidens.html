Rosa prodactidens

Rosa prodactidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

Rosa prodactidens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
