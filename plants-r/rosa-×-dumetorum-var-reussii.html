Rosa × dumetorum var. reussii

Rosa × dumetorum var. reussii, commonly known as the Alpine rose or creeping rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and often covered with a fine layer of white down.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in mountainous environments.
