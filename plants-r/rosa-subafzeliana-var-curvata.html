Rosa subafzeliana var. curvata

Rosa subafzeliana var. curvata, commonly known as the curved-stem rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, which are typically reddish in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often blooming in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
