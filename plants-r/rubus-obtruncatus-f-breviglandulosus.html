Rubus obtruncatus f. breviglandulosus

Rubus obtruncatus f. breviglandulosus, commonly known as the short-glandular blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible. Its flowers are white with five petals, and the plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is distinguished by its reduced glandular hairs on the stems and leaves compared to other Rubus species.
