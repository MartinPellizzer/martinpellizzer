Rhinorchis leaoana

Rhinorchis leaoana, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the humid forests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in a rosette pattern. Rhinorchis leaoana thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
