Rhoicissus tridentata subsp. cuneifolia

Rhoicissus tridentata subsp.

cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed arrowhead vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa. It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, with a distinctive trifoliate pattern.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and ornamental addition to tropical and subtropical gardens.
