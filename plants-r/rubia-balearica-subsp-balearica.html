Rubia balearica subsp. balearica

Rubia balearica subsp.

balearica, commonly known as the Balearic madder, is a perennial herb native to the Balearic Islands. It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. Its roots are notable for containing alkaloids and are historically used in dyeing textiles.

It thrives in rocky, calcareous soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate.
