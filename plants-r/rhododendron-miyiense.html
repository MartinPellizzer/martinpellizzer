Rhododendron miyiense

Rhododendron miyiense, commonly known as the Mihaiyev rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of forests. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leathery leaves, which help it thrive in its natural habitat.

Rhododendron miyiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
