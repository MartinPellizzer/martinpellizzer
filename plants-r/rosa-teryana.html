Rosa teryana

Rosa teryana, commonly known as the Turkish rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region, including parts of Turkey, Syria, and Lebanon. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges.

Rosa teryana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in perfumery.
