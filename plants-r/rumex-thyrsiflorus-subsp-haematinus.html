Rumex thyrsiflorus subsp. haematinus

Rumex thyrsiflorus subsp.

haematinus, commonly known as the red-stemmed sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features upright, slender stems that are typically reddish in color, especially when mature.

The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a serrated margin and often have a reddish tinge on the underside.

It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
