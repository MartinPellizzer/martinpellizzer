Rosa rubiginosa f. monacensis

Rosa rubiginosa f. monacensis, commonly known as the Monacan rose, is a botanical variant of the shrub rose species Rosa rubiginosa.

It is characterized by its deep red to purplish-pink flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa genus. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It produces fragrant, single or semi-double blooms, which are attractive to pollinators.

This form is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and hardy nature.
