Rosa coriifolia var. vagiana

Rosa coriifolia var. vagiana, commonly known as the Chinese pink or the pink rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink to white. The plant has finely serrated leaves and produces small, red hips in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a favored plant in horticulture.
