Rosa × barclayi

Rosa × barclayi, commonly known as the Barclay rose, is a hybrid species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is native to the United States, particularly in the southeastern region. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, typically blooming in spring. Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with serrated edges, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Rosa × barclayi is relatively hardy and adaptable, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
