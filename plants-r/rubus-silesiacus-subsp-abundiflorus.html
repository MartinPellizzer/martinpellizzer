Rubus silesiacus subsp. abundiflorus

Rubus silesiacus subsp.

abundiflorus, commonly known as the Silesian blackberry, is a subspecies native to Central Europe. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its flowers are white and arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
