Rosa elliptica var. liostyla

Rosa elliptica var. liostyla, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a species native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin, and are arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant is known for its hardiness and adaptability, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
