Rubus sueviacus

Rubus sueviacus, commonly known as the Swedish raspberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, similar to other raspberries. It is native to Europe and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
