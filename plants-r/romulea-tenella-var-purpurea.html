Romulea tenella var. purpurea

Romulea tenella var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple sand orchid, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to October. The plant has a compact growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers clustered in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry garden settings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique and colorful blooms.
