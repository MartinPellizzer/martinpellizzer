Rhaptostylum yapacaniense

Rhaptostylum yapacaniense, commonly known as the Yapa Cani palm, is a rare and endemic species native to the Andean region of Bolivia.

It is a tall, slender palm with a single, unbranched stem and long, pinnate leaves that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by small, round fruits. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires protection to ensure its survival.
