Ranunculus accedens

Ranunculus accedens, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, aggregate fruits that disperse seeds by wind.

Ranunculus accedens is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its aggressive growth habit.
