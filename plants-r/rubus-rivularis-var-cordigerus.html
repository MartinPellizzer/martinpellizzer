Rubus rivularis var. cordigerus

Rubus rivularis var. cordigerus, commonly known as the soft-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually rounded or ovate in shape. This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its edible fruits, which have a tart flavor and are used in jams and preserves.
