Rubus × trevirani

Rubus × trevirani, commonly known as the Tewksbury raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus idaeus (red raspberry) and Rubus strigosus (bramble).

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as raspberries, which are typically red but can also be yellow or golden in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.

The hybrid is known for its vigorous growth and adaptability to various soil types.
