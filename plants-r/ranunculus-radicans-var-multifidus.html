Ranunculus radicans var. multifidus

Ranunculus radicans var. multifidus, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a sprawling growth habit with rhizomatous stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread rapidly. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by a distinctive greenish sepals. Its leaves are typically trifoliate or palmately divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.
