Rubus polyoplus

Rubus polyoplus, commonly known as the many-fruited blackberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupelets that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
