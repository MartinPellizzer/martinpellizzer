Rauhia staminosa

Rauhia staminosa, commonly known as the golden thread orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated inflorescence that bears numerous small, golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in loose, rocky soils.

Rauhia staminosa is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
