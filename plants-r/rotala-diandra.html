Rotala diandra

Rotala diandra, commonly known as the water starwort, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, bright green, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that are typically borne on long, slender stalks. Rotala diandra thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It is often used in aquariums and ponds for its ornamental value and ability to oxygenate the water.
