Rosa trachyphylla f. exacanthoclados

Rosa trachyphylla f. exacanthoclados, commonly known as the rough-stemmed rose, is a variant of the species Rosa trachyphylla, characterized by its thick, rough-textured stems.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its leaves are compound, with serrated leaflets, and it is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in cooler climates.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in garden settings.
