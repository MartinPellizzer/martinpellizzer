Rubus acutidens

Rubus acutidens, commonly known as the sharp-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Rubus acutidens is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local wildlife.
