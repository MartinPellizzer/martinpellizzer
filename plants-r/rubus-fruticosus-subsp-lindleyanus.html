Rubus fruticosus subsp. lindleyanus

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

lindleyanus, commonly known as brambles or blackberry shrubs, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae. It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupes.

The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa, and it thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and hedgerows. It produces fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible berries in late summer.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its berries, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
