Ruellia subsessilis

Ruellia subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ruellia subsessilis is typically found in moist, shaded environments like forest understories or along stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
