Rosa setulosa

Rosa setulosa, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a serrated edge and produces rose-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Rosa setulosa is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
