Rubus thyrsoideus microgene hispidulus

Rubus thyrsoideus microgene hispidulus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant produces clusters of fruit that are typically harvested when fully ripe for culinary use. Its botanical features include a dense, wiry pubescence that covers the stems and leaves.

This species is often found in moist, wooded areas and thrives in temperate climates.
