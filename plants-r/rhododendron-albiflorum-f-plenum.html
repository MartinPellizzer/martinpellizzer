Rhododendron albiflorum f. plenum

Rhododendron albiflorum f. plenum, commonly known as the full-flowered rhododendron, is a hybrid plant characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy white to pale pink flowers.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly China. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to gardens and natural landscapes.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
