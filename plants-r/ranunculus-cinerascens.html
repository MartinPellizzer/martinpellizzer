Ranunculus cinerascens

Ranunculus cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-gray buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It is characterized by its finely divided foliage and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
