Rhododendron nudipes var. tokushimense

Rhododendron nudipes var. tokushimense, commonly known as the Tokushima rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
