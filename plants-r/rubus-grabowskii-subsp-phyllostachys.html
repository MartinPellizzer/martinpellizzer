Rubus grabowskii subsp. phyllostachys

Rubus grabowskii subsp.

phyllostachys, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble, is a shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its finely serrated leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, edible berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
