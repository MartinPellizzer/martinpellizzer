Rosa laxa var. alatavica

Rosa laxa var. alatavica, commonly known as the Altai rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are compound, with ovate to elliptic leaflets that have a slightly serrated edge. This variety is adapted to harsh, alpine environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and cold resistance.

It is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
