Ranunculus recurvatus var. recurvatus

Ranunculus recurvatus var. recurvatus, commonly known as the curlyleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often with a darker central blotch. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
