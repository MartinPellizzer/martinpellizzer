Rubus purpureus var. subinermis

Rubus purpureus var. subinermis, commonly known as the purple raspberry or trailing raspberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, purple-tinged flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, juicy berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems that provide structural support.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
