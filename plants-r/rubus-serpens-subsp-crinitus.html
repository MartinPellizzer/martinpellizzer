Rubus serpens subsp. crinitus

Rubus serpens subsp.

crinitus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive hairy stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it identifiable by its soft, pubescent foliage and clusters of small flowers.
