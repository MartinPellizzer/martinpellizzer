Rytigynia torrei

Rytigynia torrei, commonly known as the Brazilian redwood, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Rytigyniaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its woody structure and slow growth rate make it a valuable species for ecological studies.

However, it is threatened by habitat loss and deforestation.
