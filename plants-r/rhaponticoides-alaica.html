Rhaponticoides alaica

Rhaponticoides alaica, commonly known as the Altai rhubarb, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes and large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Rhaponticoides alaica is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
