Ranunculus mosbachensis

Ranunculus mosbachensis, commonly known as the Mosbach's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, often toxic, flowering plants. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
