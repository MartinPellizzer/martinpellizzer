Rubus schmidelyanus subsp. leyanus

Rubus schmidelyanus subsp.

leyanus, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year producing vegetative growth and the second year bearing fruit.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, with a hairy surface. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
