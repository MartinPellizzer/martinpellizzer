Rosa cheshiriensis

Rosa cheshiriensis, commonly known as the Cheshire rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

Rosa cheshiriensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
