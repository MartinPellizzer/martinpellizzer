Rubia peregrina var. lucida

Rubia peregrina var. lucida, commonly known as the shining bittersweet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, thriving in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
