Rosa chavinii var. pycnadenia

Rosa chavinii var. pycnadenia, commonly known as the Chavin rose, is a compact shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features dense, thornless stems and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically borne on short pedicels. It is well adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in cool, moist conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
