Rosa rubiginosa var. camadrae

Rosa rubiginosa var. camadrae, commonly known as the Camadrae rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces rose hips that are typically red or orange in color, containing seeds inside. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
