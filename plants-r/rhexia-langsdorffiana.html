Rhexia langsdorffiana

Rhexia langsdorffiana, commonly known as the pinkroot or redroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along streambanks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slight pubescence.

Rhexia langsdorffiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
