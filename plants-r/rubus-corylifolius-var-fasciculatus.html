Rubus corylifolius var. fasciculatus

Rubus corylifolius var. fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered bramble, is a variant of the Chinese blackberry.

It is a shrub native to China and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
