Rollinia exsucca subsp. resinosa

Rollinia exsucca subsp.

resinosa, commonly known as the resinous star-apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America. It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in humid, lowland areas.

The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a star-apple, is typically yellow when ripe and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

The subspecies is noted for its resinous sap, which has been used in traditional medicine and for making incense.
