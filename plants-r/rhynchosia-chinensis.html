Rhynchosia chinensis

Rhynchosia chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese cowpea or Chinese velvet bean, is a climbing leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow or orange flowers. The plant bears pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Rhynchosia chinensis is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
