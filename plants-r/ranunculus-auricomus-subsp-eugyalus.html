Ranunculus auricomus subsp. eugyalus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

eugyalus, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive golden center.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine, silky hairs.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
