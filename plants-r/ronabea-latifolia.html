Ronabea latifolia

Ronabea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ronabea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Ronabea latifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
