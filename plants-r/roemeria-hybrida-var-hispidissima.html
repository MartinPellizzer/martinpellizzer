Roemeria hybrida var. hispidissima

Roemeria hybrida var. hispidissima, commonly known as the hispidissima roemeria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures, giving the plant its "hispidissima" designation, which means "hairy" in Latin. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to coastal environments.
