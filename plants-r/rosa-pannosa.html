Rosa pannosa

Rosa pannosa, commonly known as the downy rose, is a species of flowering shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, grayish-green foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is known for its ornamental value. Rosa pannosa has small, oval leaves with a soft, downy texture on both sides.

It produces small, red, berry-like fruits that contain seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
