Rhysolepis retroflexa

Rhysolepis retroflexa, commonly known as the narrowleaf tamarack, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and small, needle-like leaves. Rhysolepis retroflexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
