Rubus bailletii

Rubus bailletii, commonly known as the Baillet's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
