Ramium ramiflorum

Ramium ramiflorum, commonly known as the clustered flax or narrowleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Linaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
