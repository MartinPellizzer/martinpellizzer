Roemeria schimperi

Roemeria schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring showy, fragrant flowers with intricate petal structures. The plant typically grows in rocky and humid habitats, often found in montane forests and grasslands. Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for high-altitude ecosystems and its potential for conservation due to its limited distribution.

Due to habitat degradation and over-collection, it is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for further research and protection efforts.
