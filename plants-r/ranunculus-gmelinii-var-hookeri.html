Ranunculus gmelinii var. hookeri

Ranunculus gmelinii var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with prominent black markings. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

However, it is also considered a potential weed in certain agricultural settings.
