Rosa affectata

Rosa affectata, commonly known as the affected rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces rose hips as fruit after flowering. It is native to certain regions of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it is considered a rare species, with limited natural habitat and specific ecological requirements.
