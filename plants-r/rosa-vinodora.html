Rosa vinodora

Rosa vinodora, commonly known as the purple vine rose, is a climbing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and long, flexible stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double to fully double flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets.

Rosa vinodora thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.
