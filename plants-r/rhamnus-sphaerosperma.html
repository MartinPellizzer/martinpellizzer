Rhamnus sphaerosperma

Rhamnus sphaerosperma, commonly known as the globular-fruited buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, black berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native environment.
