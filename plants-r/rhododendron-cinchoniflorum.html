Rhododendron cinchoniflorum

Rhododendron cinchoniflorum, commonly known as the Cinchona-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, and it produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also studied for its potential ecological and medicinal significance.
