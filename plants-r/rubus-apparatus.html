Rubus apparatus

Rubus apparatus, commonly known as blackberry or raspberry plants, belongs to the genus Rubus in the family Rosaceae.

These plants are characterized by their thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits formed from multiple tiny drupelets. The flowers are typically white or pink, with five petals and numerous stamens. The fruit develops from the superior ovary and is often covered in a fuzzy layer.

The genus includes over 400 species, many of which are cultivated for their edible fruits.
