Rosa carolina var. hemispaerica

Rosa carolina var. hemispaerica, commonly known as the Carolina rock rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven oval leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink to red, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
