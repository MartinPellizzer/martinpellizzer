Racosperma connianum

Racosperma connianum, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds.

Racosperma connianum is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
