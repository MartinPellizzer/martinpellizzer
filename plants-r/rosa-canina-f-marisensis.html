Rosa canina f. marisensis

Rosa canina f. marisensis, commonly known as the sea rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, where it thrives in salty, well-drained soils.

Its leaves are pinnate with serrated edges, and the fruits, known as hips, are orange-red and contain a high concentration of vitamin C. This subspecies is notable for its hardiness and adaptability to maritime climates.
