Ruschia suaveolens

Ruschia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented ruschia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, often blue or purple flowers. The plant produces a pleasant, aromatic scent, especially during the evening, which attracts pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
