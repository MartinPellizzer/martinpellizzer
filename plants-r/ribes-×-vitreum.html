Ribes × vitreum

Ribes × vitreum, commonly known as the hybrid gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub that belongs to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is a cross between Ribes nigrum (black gooseberry) and Ribes aureum (golden gooseberry), resulting in a plant with unique characteristics. The leaves are typically oval to ovate, with a glossy, waxy surface that gives the plant its common name. The flowers are usually yellow or orange, and the fruit is a small, tart berry that ripens in late summer.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and culinary uses.
