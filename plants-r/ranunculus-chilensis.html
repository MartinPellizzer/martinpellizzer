Ranunculus chilensis

Ranunculus chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five to seven petals. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and has a basal rosette of leaves. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ranunculus chilensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
