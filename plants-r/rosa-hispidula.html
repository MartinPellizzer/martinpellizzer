Rosa hispidula

Rosa hispidula, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a species of flowering shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces oval, glossy leaves with a hairy texture, which gives it its common name. Rosa hispidula is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in full sun and requires minimal water once established.
