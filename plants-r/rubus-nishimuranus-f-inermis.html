Rubus nishimuranus f. inermis

Rubus nishimuranus f. inermis, commonly known as the Nishimura raspberry, is a rare and unique variant of the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, distinguishing it from other raspberry species. The plant typically produces small, aggregate fruits that are often reddish in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
