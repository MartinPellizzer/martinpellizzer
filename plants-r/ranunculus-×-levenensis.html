Ranunculus × levenensis

Ranunculus × levenensis, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is a hybrid between Ranunculus bulbosus and Ranunculus sceleratus, exhibiting characteristics of both parents. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, with alternate, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that have five to eight petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
