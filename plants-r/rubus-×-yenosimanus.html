Rubus × yenosimanus

Rubus × yenosimanus, commonly known as the Yenosemanu blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, which includes brambles and blackberries.

It is native to the Far East and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
