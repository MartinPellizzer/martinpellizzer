Rubus × horridicaulis f. sylvaticus

Rubus × horridicaulis f. sylvaticus, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, often in woodland or hedgerow environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit, a dark purple to black berry, is edible and has a tart flavor.
