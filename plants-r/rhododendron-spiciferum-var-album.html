Rhododendron spiciferum var. album

Rhododendron spiciferum var. album, commonly known as the White Spiciferous Rhododendron, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China, particularly in Yunnan Province. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.

The species is adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates.
