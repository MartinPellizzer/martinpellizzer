Ruscus colchicus

Ruscus colchicus, commonly known as the Armenian spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a low-growing shrub with fleshy, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its roots contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

Ruscus colchicus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
