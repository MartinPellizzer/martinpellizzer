Ranunculus flammula f. reptans

Ranunculus flammula f. reptans, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
