Rhus laurina

Rhus laurina, commonly known as the California buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes plants like poison ivy and mango. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

Rhus laurina is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub habitats.
