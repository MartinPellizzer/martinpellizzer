Rosa coriifolia var. progelii

Rosa coriifolia var. progelii, commonly known as the Siberian shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with slender stems and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets that are glossy and dark green. This variety is well adapted to harsh climates, tolerating cold temperatures and poor soil conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
