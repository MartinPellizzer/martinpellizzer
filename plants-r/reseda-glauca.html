Reseda glauca

Reseda glauca, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

Reseda glauca is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
