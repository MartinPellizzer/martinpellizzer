Rumex lonaczewskii

Rumex lonaczewskii, commonly known as the Polish sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
