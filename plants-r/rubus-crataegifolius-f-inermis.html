Rubus crataegifolius f. inermis

Rubus crataegifolius f. inermis, commonly known as the smoothleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive crataegifolius shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
