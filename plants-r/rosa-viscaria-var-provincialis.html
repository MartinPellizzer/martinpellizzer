Rosa viscaria var. provincialis

Rosa viscaria var. provincialis, commonly known as the Provincial rose, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant typically features multiple stems with thorny growth, and its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets. The flowers are usually pale pink to white, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in temperate climates.
