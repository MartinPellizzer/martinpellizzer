Rosa glauca var. elata

Rosa glauca var. elata, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a subspecies of the blue rose that thrives in high-altitude environments.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of pale blue to lavender flowers. The plant features dark green, glossy foliage that is often covered in a waxy bloom, providing a protective layer against harsh weather. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is well adapted to cold, rocky soils.

This hardy perennial is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
