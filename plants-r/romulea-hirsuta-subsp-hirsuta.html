Romulea hirsuta subsp. hirsuta

Romulea hirsuta subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa. It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

The plant thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and prefers full sun. It is known for its ability to store water in its bulb, allowing it to survive dry periods.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular among succulent enthusiasts.
