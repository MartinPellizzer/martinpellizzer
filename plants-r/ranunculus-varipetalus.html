Ranunculus varipetalus

Ranunculus varipetalus, commonly known as the variable buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and sometimes pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers consist of multiple petals that are often arranged in a star-like pattern, and it produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain several seeds.

Ranunculus varipetalus is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
