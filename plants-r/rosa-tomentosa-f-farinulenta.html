Rosa tomentosa f. farinulenta

Rosa tomentosa f. farinulenta, commonly known as the woolly rose or farinulenta rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa tomentosa.

It is characterized by its dense, white to grayish pubescence covering the stems, leaves, and flowers, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub with arching, thorned stems and produces fragrant, pink to pale red flowers. Its foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, contributing to its overall rugged, ornamental appearance.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal in garden settings.
