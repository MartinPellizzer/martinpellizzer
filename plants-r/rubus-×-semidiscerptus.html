Rubus × semidiscerptus

Rubus × semidiscerptus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus glaucus.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
