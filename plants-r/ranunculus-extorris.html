Ranunculus extorris

Ranunculus extorris, commonly known as the Turkish buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

Ranunculus extorris is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
