Rubus inopertus var. inopertus

Rubus inopertus var. inopertus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited range, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
