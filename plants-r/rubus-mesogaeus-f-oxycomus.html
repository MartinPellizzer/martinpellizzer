Rubus mesogaeus f. oxycomus

Rubus mesogaeus f. oxycomus, commonly known as the glossy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its dark, shiny fruits.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the berries are small, firm, and highly aromatic.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, though it is less commonly cultivated than other blackberry species.
