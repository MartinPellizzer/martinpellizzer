Rubus tuberculatus

Rubus tuberculatus, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
