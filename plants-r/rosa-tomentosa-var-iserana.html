Rosa tomentosa var. iserana

Rosa tomentosa var. iserana, commonly known as the Isarina rose, is a shrub-like species native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, exhibiting a hardy and resilient nature. Its leaves are covered in a fine, white tomentum, which helps to protect it from harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
