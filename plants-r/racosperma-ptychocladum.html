Racosperma ptychocladum

Racosperma ptychocladum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various native animals.

Its seeds are often used in traditional Aboriginal practices and for revegetation projects.
