Rhododendron helodes

Rhododendron helodes, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Rhododendron helodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
