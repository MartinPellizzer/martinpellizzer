Raphia hookeri var. planifoliola

Raphia hookeri var. planifoliola, commonly known as the flat-leaf palm, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its broad, flat leaves that can reach lengths of up to 6 meters.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features a tall, slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and can grow up to 20 meters in height. Its leaves are typically arranged in a fan shape, with a sturdy petiole and a waxy, dark green surface that provides a striking visual contrast.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for both its aesthetic and potential practical uses.
