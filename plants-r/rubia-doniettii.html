Rubia doniettii

Rubia doniettii, commonly known as the Sicilian madder, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its roots contain a red dye, historically used for coloring textiles and in traditional medicine.

Rubia doniettii is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained environments.
