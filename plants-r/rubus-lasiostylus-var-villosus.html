Rubus lasiostylus var. villosus

Rubus lasiostylus var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

Its hairy texture, from which it gets its botanical name, is a distinguishing feature.
