Rudgea klugii

Rudgea klugii, commonly known as the Klug's rudgea, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Rudgea klugii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
