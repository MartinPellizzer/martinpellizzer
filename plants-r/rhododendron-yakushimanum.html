Rhododendron yakushimanum

Rhododendron yakushimanum, commonly known as the Shikoku azalea, is a species native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial shade to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for humid climates and coastal areas.
