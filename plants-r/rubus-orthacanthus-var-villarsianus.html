Rubus orthacanthus var. villarsianus

Rubus orthacanthus var. villarsianus, commonly known as the yellow-tailed blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
