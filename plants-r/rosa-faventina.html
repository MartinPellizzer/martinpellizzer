Rosa faventina

Rosa faventina, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. Rosa faventina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in hedgerows.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
