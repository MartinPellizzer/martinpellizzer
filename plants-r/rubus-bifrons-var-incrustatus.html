Rubus bifrons var. incrustatus

Rubus bifrons var. incrustatus, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
