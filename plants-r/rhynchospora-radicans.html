Rhynchospora radicans

Rhynchospora radicans, commonly known as the creeping spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Rhynchospora radicans thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland habitats.
