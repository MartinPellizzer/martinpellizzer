Rubus eucalyptus var. villosus

Rubus eucalyptus var. villosus, commonly known as the silky bramble, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, edible berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
