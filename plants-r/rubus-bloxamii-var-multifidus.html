Rubus bloxamii var. multifidus

Rubus bloxamii var. multifidus, commonly known as the multifidus blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southeastern China.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant features compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its stems are often armed with thorns. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and edible fruits, though it is less commonly cultivated than other blackberry species.
