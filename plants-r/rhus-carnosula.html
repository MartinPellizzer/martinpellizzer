Rhus carnosula

Rhus carnosula, commonly known as the velvetleaf or sweetleaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by distinctive, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or secondary growth habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
