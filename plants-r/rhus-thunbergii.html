Rhus thunbergii

Rhus thunbergii, commonly known as the Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are a common food source for birds. Rhus thunbergii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or open woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in the landscape.
