Rubus oreogeton var. abromeitii

Rubus oreogeton var. abromeitii, commonly known as the Abromeiti's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed by wildlife and birds. Its botanical features include a woody structure, hairy foliage, and a preference for well-drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various species.
