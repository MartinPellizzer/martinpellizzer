Rubus crimaeus

Rubus crimaeus, commonly known as the crimson bramble, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

The flowers are white or pale pink, with five petals, and appear in early summer.
