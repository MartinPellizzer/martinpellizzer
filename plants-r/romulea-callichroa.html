Romulea callichroa

Romulea callichroa, commonly known as the golden rock orchid, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that resemble those of orchids. The plant produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and blooms in spring. Its flowers are surrounded by a showy, petal-like bract, which adds to its ornamental appeal.

Romulea callichroa is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.
