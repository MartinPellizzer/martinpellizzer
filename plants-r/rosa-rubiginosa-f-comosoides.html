Rosa rubiginosa f. comosoides

Rosa rubiginosa f. comosoides, commonly known as the hairy rose or the red hairy rose, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or red flowers. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine.
