Ranunculus flaccidus subsp. petiveri

Ranunculus flaccidus subsp.

petiveri, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

The plant features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, characteristic of the Ranunculaceae family. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy surface that helps reduce water loss.

It is an important component of early successional plant communities and supports a variety of pollinators.
