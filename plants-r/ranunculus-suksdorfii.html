Ranunculus suksdorfii

Ranunculus suksdorfii, commonly known as the Saksdorf's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features a basal rosette of leaves and produces solitary, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine, silky hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to alpine and subalpine environments.
