Rhamnus laurifolia

Rhamnus laurifolia, commonly known as the evergreen privet, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that remain throughout the year. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, black berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used in landscaping and can be found in various parts of the world outside its native range.
