Rubus hirtus var. leptocladus

Rubus hirtus var. leptocladus, commonly known as the slender-leaved bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red, hairy berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, wooded habitats.

This variety is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
