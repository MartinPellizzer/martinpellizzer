Rhododendron luraluense

Rhododendron luraluense, commonly known as the Luraluense rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
