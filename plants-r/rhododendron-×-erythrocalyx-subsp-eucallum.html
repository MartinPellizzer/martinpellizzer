Rhododendron × erythrocalyx subsp. eucallum

Rhododendron × erythrocalyx subsp.

eucallum, commonly known as the pink-flowered rhododendron, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep crimson.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
