Rosa × falcata var. intricata

Rosa × falcata var. intricata, commonly known as the twisted thorned rose, is a hybrid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and thorny stems, which are often twisted or coiled. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in USDA hardiness zones 6 through 9.
