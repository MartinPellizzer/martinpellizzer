Rubus comintanus

Rubus comintanus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a deciduous perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color, typically in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed soils.

Rubus comintanus plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
