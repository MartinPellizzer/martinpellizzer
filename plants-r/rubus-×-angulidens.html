Rubus × angulidens

Rubus × angulidens, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. The plant typically grows as a trailing or climbing shrub, often found in disturbed soils and along forest edges. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it produces small, white flowers in the spring.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in jams and preserves.
