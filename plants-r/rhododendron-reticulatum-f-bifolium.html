Rhododendron reticulatum f. bifolium

Rhododendron reticulatum f. bifolium, commonly known as the twin-leaf rhododendron, is a unique cultivar characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of China. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are often twice as wide as they are long, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically pink or white, appearing in clusters and blooming in early spring.

This ornamental plant prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.
