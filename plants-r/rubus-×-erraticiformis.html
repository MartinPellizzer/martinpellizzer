Rubus × erraticiformis

Rubus × erraticiformis, commonly known as the erratic raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations.
