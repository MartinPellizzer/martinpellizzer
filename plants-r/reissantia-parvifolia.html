Reissantia parvifolia

Reissantia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved reissantia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
