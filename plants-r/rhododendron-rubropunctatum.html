Rhododendron rubropunctatum

Rhododendron rubropunctatum, commonly known as the red-spotted rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly in China and Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink to reddish in color, with a distinctive red or purple spotting on the petals. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
