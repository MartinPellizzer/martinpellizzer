Ranunculus drepanensis

Ranunculus drepanensis, commonly known as the Mediterranean buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with 5-7 petals, typically surrounded by a ring of sepals. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Ranunculus drepanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
