Ranunculus conjugens

Ranunculus conjugens, commonly known as the western yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has compound leaves with three leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring.

Ranunculus conjugens is an important species for pollinators and is often found in native plant gardens.
