Rosa × christiansenii

Rosa × christiansenii, commonly known as the Christiansen's rose, is a hybrid rose species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa × centifolia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from pink to white. The plant is shrub-like, with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its hardiness and adaptability contribute to its widespread use in horticulture.
