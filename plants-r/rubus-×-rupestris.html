Rubus × rupestris

Rubus × rupestris, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil in temperate climates.

This species is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
