Roscoea spicata

Roscoea spicata, commonly known as the spiky roscoea, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers, and is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers. Roscoea spicata thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
