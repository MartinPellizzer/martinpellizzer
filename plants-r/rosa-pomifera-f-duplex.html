Rosa pomifera f. duplex

Rosa pomifera f. duplex, commonly known as the double-fruited rose, is a unique horticultural form of the Rosa pomifera species.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, edible fruits that develop in clusters, setting it apart from typical roses. The botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. It is native to regions of Asia and is valued for both its ornamental appeal and edible fruit production.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
