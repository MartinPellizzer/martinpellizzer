Rubus macrodontus

Rubus macrodontus, commonly known as the large-fruited blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. Its flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences, and it thrives in moist, wooded areas.

Rubus macrodontus is an important species for both wildlife and local ecosystems.
