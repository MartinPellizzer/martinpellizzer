Riedlea radiata

Riedlea radiata, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or near rivers.

Riedlea radiata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade.
