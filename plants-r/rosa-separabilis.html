Rosa separabilis

Rosa separabilis, commonly known as the separable rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America, particularly the southeastern United States. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its stems are typically thorny.

Rosa separabilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
