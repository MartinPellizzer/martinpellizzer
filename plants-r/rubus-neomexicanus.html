Rubus neomexicanus

Rubus neomexicanus, commonly known as the New Mexico blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often described as having a tart flavor. It thrives in rocky soils and arid environments, typically found in canyon areas and along streambanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
