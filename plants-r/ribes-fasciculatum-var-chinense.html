Ribes fasciculatum var. chinense

Ribes fasciculatum var. chinense, commonly known as Chinese gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It produces small, yellow to orange berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it is often found in moist, shady environments. It is cultivated for its fruit, which is a precursor to the modern kiwifruit.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
