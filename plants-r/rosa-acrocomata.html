Rosa acrocomata

Rosa acrocomata, commonly known as the Cape rose or the mountain rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in fine, velvety hairs.

Rosa acrocomata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
