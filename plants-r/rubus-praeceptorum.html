Rubus praeceptorum

Rubus praeceptorum, commonly known as the professor's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed raw or used in jams. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
