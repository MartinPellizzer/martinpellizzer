Ranunculus gueneri

Ranunculus gueneri, commonly known as the Turkish buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Turkey and the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ranunculus gueneri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
