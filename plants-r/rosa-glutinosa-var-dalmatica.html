Rosa glutinosa var. dalmatica

Rosa glutinosa var. dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces rosehips that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine contribute to its significance in horticulture.
