Rosa arvernica

Rosa arvernica, commonly known as the Arverne rose, is a species of shrub native to western Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has arching stems covered in thorns and produces elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in woodland and hedgerow environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Rosa arvernica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
