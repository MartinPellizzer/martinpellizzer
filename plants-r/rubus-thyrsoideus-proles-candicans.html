Rubus thyrsoideus proles candicans

Rubus thyrsoideus proles candicans, commonly known as the golden bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
