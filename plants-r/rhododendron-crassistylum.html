Rhododendron crassistylum

Rhododendron crassistylum, commonly known as the broad-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
