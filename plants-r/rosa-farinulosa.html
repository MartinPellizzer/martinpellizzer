Rosa farinulosa

Rosa farinulosa, commonly known as the meadow rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and along riverbanks. The plant is characterized by its pink to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Rosa farinulosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
