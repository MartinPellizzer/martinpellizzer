Rubus insericatus var. silvigenus

Rubus insericatus var. silvigenus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are white and grow in clusters, and the leaves are covered in a fine, silvery down.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
