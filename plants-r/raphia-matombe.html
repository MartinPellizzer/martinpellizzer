Raphia matombe

Raphia matombe, commonly known as the giant palm, is a large tropical plant native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. The plant produces long, slender fruits that contain a single seed, which is enclosed in a fibrous husk. Raphia matombe is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the fiber derived from its leaves.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to grow successfully.
