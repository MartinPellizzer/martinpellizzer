Rosa russanovii

Rosa russanovii, commonly known as the Russian rose, is a species native to the Caucasus region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features upright, thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rosa russanovii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
