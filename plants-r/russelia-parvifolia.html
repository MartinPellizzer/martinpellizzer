Russelia parvifolia

Russelia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved russelia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Russelia parvifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
