Rubus radula f. sylvaticus

Rubus radula f. sylvaticus, commonly known as the silvan raspberry, is a variant of the common blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This form is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
