Rosa johannensis

Rosa johannensis, commonly known as the Johann's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense, thorny foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and it is known for its adaptability to high-altitude environments.

Rosa johannensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in its native habitat.
