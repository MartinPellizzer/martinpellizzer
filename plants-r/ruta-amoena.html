Ruta amoena

Ruta amoena, commonly known as common rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lobed leaves and small yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant contains essential oils, which contribute to its strong, pungent scent and have been used in traditional medicine. Ruta amoena thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its historical use dates back to ancient times, where it was valued for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
