Rhododendron mekongense var. longipilosum

Rhododendron mekongense var. longipilosum, commonly known as the Mekong hairy rhododendron, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam and Laos.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive hairy texture on the petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, preferring acidic soils. Its leaves are usually dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
