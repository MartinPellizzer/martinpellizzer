Rubus villicaulis var. insularis

Rubus villicaulis var. insularis, commonly known as the island blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain island regions.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupes. The plant typically grows in coastal or disturbed habitats, showing adaptability to sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pale yellow flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its hardy nature and potential use in local ecosystems and small-scale cultivation.
