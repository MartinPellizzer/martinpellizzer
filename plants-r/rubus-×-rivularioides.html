Rubus × rivularioides

Rubus × rivularioides, commonly known as the river raspberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along stream banks and in woodland areas. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, aggregate fruits that are often reddish in color. It is characterized by its thorny stems and is part of the Rosaceae family.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
