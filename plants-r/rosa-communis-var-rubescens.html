Rosa communis var. rubescens

Rosa communis var. rubescens, commonly known as the Crimson Rose, is a cultivar of the common rose that is prized for its vibrant, deep red flowers.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and abundant blooms, which typically appear in late spring to early summer. The foliage is dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the showy pink to red petals. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.

The plant is also noted for its fragrant aroma, which adds to its appeal in both ornamental and perfumery applications.
