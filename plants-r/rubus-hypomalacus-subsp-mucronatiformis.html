Rubus hypomalacus subsp. mucronatiformis

Rubus hypomalacus subsp.

mucronatiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and serrated.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in jams and pies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The subspecies is notable for its distinctive leaf shape and its role in local ecosystems.
