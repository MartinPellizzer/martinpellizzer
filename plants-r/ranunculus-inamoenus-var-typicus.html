Ranunculus inamoenus var. typicus

Ranunculus inamoenus var. typicus, commonly known as the typical meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals, and its leaves are palmately divided with a distinctive lobed appearance. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and delicate foliage.
