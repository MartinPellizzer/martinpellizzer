Ranunculus oxyspermus var. curvirostris

Ranunculus oxyspermus var. curvirostris, commonly known as the narrow-beaked crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to seven petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers range in color from white to pale yellow.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
