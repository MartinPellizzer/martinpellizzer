Rubus × semidrejerianus

Rubus × semidrejerianus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant year-round. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its botanical features include a complex root system and a high degree of adaptability to moist, temperate climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
