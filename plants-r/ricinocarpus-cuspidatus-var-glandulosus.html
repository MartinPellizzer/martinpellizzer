Ricinocarpus cuspidatus var. glandulosus

Ricinocarpus cuspidatus var. glandulosus, commonly known as the hairy sea hake, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or muddy coastal soils and is often found in intertidal zones.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
