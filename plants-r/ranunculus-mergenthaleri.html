Ranunculus mergenthaleri

Ranunculus mergenthaleri, commonly known as the Merenthaler's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically has smooth, elongated leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
