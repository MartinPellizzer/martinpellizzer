Rubus tilioides

Rubus tilioides, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of North America, particularly the western United States and southern Canada. The plant is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a silvery bloom. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are small and tart.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
