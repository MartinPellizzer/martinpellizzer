Rhagadiolus stellatus f. hebelaenus

Rhagadiolus stellatus f. hebelaenus, commonly known as the star-shaped rhagadiolus, is a unique variant of the rhagadiolus species.

It is characterized by its striking, star-like foliage that resembles a cluster of small, pointed leaves arranged radially. This plant is native to specific regions in southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

The foliage is typically a vibrant green, with some cultivars displaying subtle variegation.
