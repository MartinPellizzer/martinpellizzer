Rubus rudis microgene scopulorum

Rubus rudis microgene scopulorum, commonly known as the coastal raspberry, is a shrub native to rocky coastal regions.

It has small, prickly leaves and produces small, red berries that are edible. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to salt spray and strong winds. It plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
