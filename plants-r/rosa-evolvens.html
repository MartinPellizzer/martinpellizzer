Rosa evolvens

Rosa evolvens, commonly known as the creeping or climbing rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features thorny stems that can climb or trail along the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval leaflets with serrated edges.

Rosa evolvens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
