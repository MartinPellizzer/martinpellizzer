Rosa rubiginosa var. chiovendae

Rosa rubiginosa var. chiovendae, commonly known as the Chiovenda rose, is a rare and distinctive variant of the shrub rose.

It is characterized by its deep red, semi-double flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa rubiginosa species. This variety is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage is dark green and aromatic, adding to its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a botanical curiosity and is often cultivated for its unique appearance and historical significance.
