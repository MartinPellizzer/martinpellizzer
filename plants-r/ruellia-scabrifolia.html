Ruellia scabrifolia

Ruellia scabrifolia, commonly known as roughleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the warm regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, rough-textured leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Ruellia scabrifolia is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
