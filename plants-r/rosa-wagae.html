Rosa wagae

Rosa wagae, commonly known as the Wagae rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red-orange hips in the fall.

Rosa wagae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
