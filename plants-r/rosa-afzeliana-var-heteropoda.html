Rosa afzeliana var. heteropoda

Rosa afzeliana var. heteropoda, commonly known as the variable rose, is a species native to parts of Asia, including China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers. The leaves are typically pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets that provide a textured appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various climates.
