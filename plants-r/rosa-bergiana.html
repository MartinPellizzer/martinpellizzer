Rosa bergiana

Rosa bergiana, commonly known as the Himalayan rose, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, often at elevations above 3,000 meters. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, single or double pink to white flowers. Its stems are covered with thorny buds, which are characteristic of many rose species.

Rosa bergiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
