Rosa gallica f. ruralis

Rosa gallica f. ruralis, commonly known as the old garden rose or the French garden rose, is a compact shrub with a bushy growth habit.

It features dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant is native to Europe and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is valued for its historical significance in horticulture and its ability to attract pollinators.

This variety is often used in garden design for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
