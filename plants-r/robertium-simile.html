Robertium simile

Robertium simile, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, forming bell-shaped clusters at the tips of the branches. Robertium simile thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in traditional medicine in its native region.
