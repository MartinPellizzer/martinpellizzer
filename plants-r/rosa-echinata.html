Rosa echinata

Rosa echinata, commonly known as the spiked rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa echinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
