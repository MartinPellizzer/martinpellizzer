Rumex fascilobus

Rumex fascilobus, commonly known as narrow-leaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, while its stems are hollow and often reddish in color.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
