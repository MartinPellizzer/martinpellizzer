Reynoutria japonica f. rosea

Reynoutria japonica f. rosea, commonly known as the pink-flowered Japanese knotweed, is a variant of the invasive plant species Reynoutria japonica.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers, which appear in dense clusters during the summer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green, and it grows in moist, shady environments. It is a perennial herb that can spread rapidly through rhizomes, making it a challenging weed to control.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a potential threat to native plant communities.
