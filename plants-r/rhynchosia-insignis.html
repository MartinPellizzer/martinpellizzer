Rhynchosia insignis

Rhynchosia insignis, commonly known as the African blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its seeds are encased in a hairy, inflated pod that splits open when mature.

Rhynchosia insignis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
