Rumex hibernicus

Rumex hibernicus, commonly known as the Irish sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western Europe.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The leaves are edible and have been used historically in cooking and traditional medicine.
