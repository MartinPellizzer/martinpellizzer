Rosa stylosa var. obscura

Rosa stylosa var. obscura, commonly known as the dark-stemmed rose, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a notable species in native plant gardens.
