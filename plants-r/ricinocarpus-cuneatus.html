Ricinocarpus cuneatus

Ricinocarpus cuneatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
