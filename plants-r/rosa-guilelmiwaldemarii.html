Rosa guilelmiwaldemarii

Rosa guilelmiwaldemarii, commonly known as the Emperor's rose, is a rare and ornamental species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has a compact growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
