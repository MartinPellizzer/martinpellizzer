Rubus sprengelii var. dentatifolius

Rubus sprengelii var. dentatifolius, commonly known as the serrated-leaf bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in cooler climates.
