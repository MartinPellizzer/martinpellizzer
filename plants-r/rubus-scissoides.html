Rubus scissoides

Rubus scissoides, commonly known as the cutleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically edible, though they may be tart. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Rubus scissoides is a perennial shrub that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife.
