Romulea bifrons var. rosea

Romulea bifrons var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, rose-pink to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical bulbs that are used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
