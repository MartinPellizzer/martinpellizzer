Rubus trullisatus

Rubus trullisatus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of bramble native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and hedgerows.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other well-known fruit-bearing plants.
