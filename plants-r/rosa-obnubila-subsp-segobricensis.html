Rosa obnubila subsp. segobricensis

Rosa obnubila subsp.

segobricensis, commonly known as the Segobric Mountain Rose, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, thornless foliage.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are dark green and slightly hairy, adapted to the cool, humid conditions of its native habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
