Rhododendron adenanthum

Rhododendron adenanthum, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Thailand and Vietnam.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features leathery, dark green leaves that are densely packed along its stems. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that range in color from pink to purple, often blooming in late spring.

Rhododendron adenanthum thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
