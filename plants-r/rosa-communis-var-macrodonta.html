Rosa communis var. macrodonta

Rosa communis var. macrodonta, commonly known as the large-flowered rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red in color. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces fragrant blooms throughout the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable.
