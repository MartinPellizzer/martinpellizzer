Rhododendron kwangsiense var. subfalcatum

Rhododendron kwangsiense var. subfalcatum, commonly known as the Guangxi narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a rare and ornamental species native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It thrives in partial shade and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.
