Rosa angustata

Rosa angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, typically found in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, with a distinctive appearance that aids in its identification.

Rosa angustata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
