Rhabdocaulon denudatum

Rhabdocaulon denudatum, commonly known as the naked stem dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its slender, hairless stems and yellow flowers make it easily identifiable.
