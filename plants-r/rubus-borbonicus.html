Rubus borbonicus

Rubus borbonicus, commonly known as the Bourbon blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and have a tart flavor. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded areas.

The fruit is used in local cuisine and has potential for cultivation in similar climates.
