Rhynchospora patuligluma

Rhynchospora patuligluma, commonly known as the broadleaf spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and other moist environments, often forming dense stands. The plant has upright, grass-like stems that bear spike-like clusters of small, pale green to reddish-brown flowers. Its leaves are usually broad and flat, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Rhynchospora patuligluma plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
