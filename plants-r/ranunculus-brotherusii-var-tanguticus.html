Ranunculus brotherusii var. tanguticus

Ranunculus brotherusii var. tanguticus, commonly known as the Tangut buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
