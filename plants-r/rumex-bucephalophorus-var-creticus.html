Rumex bucephalophorus var. creticus

Rumex bucephalophorus var. creticus, commonly known as the Cretan sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically reddish in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor.
