Rosa pseudopomifera

Rosa pseudopomifera, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, red fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Rosa pseudopomifera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various climates.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
