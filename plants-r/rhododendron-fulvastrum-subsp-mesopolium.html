Rhododendron fulvastrum subsp. mesopolium

Rhododendron fulvastrum subsp.

mesopolium, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows at high altitudes, preferring cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from white to pink.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
