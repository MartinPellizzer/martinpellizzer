Rhipsalis paradoxa subsp. paradoxa

Rhipsalis paradoxa subsp.

paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradoxical Rhipsalis, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to South America. It is characterized by its long, slender, and often pendulous stems that can grow up to several meters in length.

The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom during the wet season, followed by small, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically reduced to small tubercles, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

This species is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
