Rubus hirtus var. rilaeensis

Rubus hirtus var. rilaeensis, commonly known as the Rilaeen bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus native to specific regions in Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are usually red when mature. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring moist, well-drained conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and often overlooked species within the Rubus genus.
