Ruellia lyi

Ruellia lyi, commonly known as the silverleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually blue or purple and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Ruellia lyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
