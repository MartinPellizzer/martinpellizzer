Rubus leucostachys var. gymnostachys

Rubus leucostachys var. gymnostachys, commonly known as the white-stemmed blackberry, is a species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in local cuisines.
