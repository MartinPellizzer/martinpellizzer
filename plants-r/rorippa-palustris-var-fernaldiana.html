Rorippa palustris var. fernaldiana

Rorippa palustris var. fernaldiana, commonly known as the Fernald's water bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves and distinctive, inflated fruit structures that aid in seed dispersal. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. Its flowers are usually small and yellow, appearing in clusters near the water's surface.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to aquatic conditions and its role in supporting local aquatic ecosystems.
