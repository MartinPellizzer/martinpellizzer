Rosa × lutens

Rosa × lutens, commonly known as the sweet William rose, is a hybrid species of the rose genus.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant clusters of pink, red, or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its botanical features include pinnate leaflets and a high degree of ornamental value due to its showy blooms.

Rosa × lutens is widely cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
