Ranunculus fallax subsp. retusifolius

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

retusifolius, commonly known as the blunt-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive blunted appearance.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by sepals that are longer than the petals. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
