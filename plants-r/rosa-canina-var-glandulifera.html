Rosa canina var. glandulifera

Rosa canina var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular rose or dog rose, is a variant of the common rose.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces pink to reddish flowers in early summer, followed by orange to red hips that contain the seeds. Its leaves are often covered with small glands, giving it the specific epithet "glandulifera.".

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
