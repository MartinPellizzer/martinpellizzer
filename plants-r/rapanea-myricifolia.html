Rapanea myricifolia

Rapanea myricifolia, commonly known as the Indian laurel or myricifolia laurel, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that develop into dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable ornamental and ecological plant in its native habitat.
