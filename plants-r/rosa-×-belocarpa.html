Rosa × belocarpa

Rosa × belocarpa, commonly known as the Pacific Coast rose, is a hybrid species native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is typically found in coastal regions with mild, moist climates. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Rosa × belocarpa is also valued for its ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
