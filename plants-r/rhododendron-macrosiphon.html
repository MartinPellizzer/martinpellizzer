Rhododendron macrosiphon

Rhododendron macrosiphon, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and red. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and shrublands, thriving in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Rhododendron macrosiphon is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
