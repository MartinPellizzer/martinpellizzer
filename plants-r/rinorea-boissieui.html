Rinorea boissieui

Rinorea boissieui, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rinorea boissieui is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
