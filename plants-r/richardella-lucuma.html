Richardella lucuma

Richardella lucuma, commonly known as the lucuma fruit tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, evergreen tree that produces large, yellow-orange fruits with a unique, sweet, and nutty flavor. The fruit has a thick, leathery rind enclosing a dense, edible pulp containing numerous seeds. Botanically, it belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, temperate climates and well-drained soils.
