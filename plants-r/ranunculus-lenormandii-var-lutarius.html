Ranunculus lenormandii var. lutarius

Ranunculus lenormandii var. lutarius, commonly known as the Lutarian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is known for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms.

However, it is toxic to humans and animals, containing protoanemonin in its sap.
