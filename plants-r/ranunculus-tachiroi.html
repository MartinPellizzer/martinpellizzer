Ranunculus tachiroi

Ranunculus tachiroi, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds.

Ranunculus tachiroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
