Rhaponticum scariosum subsp. scariosum

Rhaponticum scariosum subsp.

scariosum, commonly known as the Siberian cowpea, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Eurasia. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense heads, followed by distinctive, spiny, spherical fruits. It is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in alpine and steppe regions.

This species is valued for its edible seeds and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
