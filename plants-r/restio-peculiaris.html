Restio peculiaris

Restio peculiaris, commonly known as the peculiar restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale blue in color.

Restio peculiaris is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
