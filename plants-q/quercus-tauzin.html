Quercus tauzin

Quercus tauzin, commonly known as the Tuzin oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe. The tree is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. It produces small, spiky acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The bark of the Tuzin oak is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.
