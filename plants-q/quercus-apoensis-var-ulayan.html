Quercus apoensis var. ulayan

Quercus apoensis var. ulayan, commonly known as the Ulayan oak, is a variant of the Apo oak native to the Philippines.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and acorn-like fruits. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a serrated margin and a prominent midrib. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in montane forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local ecosystem and a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
