Quercus regia

Quercus regia, commonly known as the royal oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, spiky acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Quercus regia typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its sturdy structure and longevity make it a significant component of many forest ecosystems.
