Quercus faginea var. spinifolius

Quercus faginea var. spinifolius, commonly known as the bristly oak, is a variant of the European pedunculate oak.

It is a deciduous tree native to parts of Central and Eastern Europe. The plant is characterized by its serrated, bristly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Its acorns are typically small and have a short stalk.

This species is often found in moist, fertile soils and thrives in temperate climates.
