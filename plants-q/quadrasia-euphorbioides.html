Quadrasia euphorbioides

Quadrasia euphorbioides, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes many species with latex-producing properties. The plant is characterized by its palm-like appearance, with a single trunk and large, feather-shaped leaves. It produces small yellow flowers clustered in inflorescences, and its sap contains a milky latex that can be toxic.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
