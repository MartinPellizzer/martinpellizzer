Quercus robur var. mespilifolia

Quercus robur var. mespilifolia, commonly known as the mespilifolia oak, is a variant of the English oak characterized by its unique leaf morphology.

Its leaves are typically more rounded and have a distinctive lobing pattern compared to the standard Q. robur. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in mixed forests and woodland areas. It produces acorns that are a vital food source for various wildlife species.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ecological importance in supporting biodiversity.
