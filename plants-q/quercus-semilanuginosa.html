Quercus semilanuginosa

Quercus semilanuginosa, commonly known as the silky oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family, Fagaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The tree is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and its distinctive leaves, which are elongated and have a silvery underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to different soil types.
