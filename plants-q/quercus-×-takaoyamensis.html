Quercus × takaoyamensis

Quercus × takaoyamensis, commonly known as the Takaoyamensis oak, is a hybrid species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing acorns as its fruit. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lobed and can vary in shape depending on the parent species.

Quercus × takaoyamensis is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
