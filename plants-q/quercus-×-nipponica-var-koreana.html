Quercus × nipponica var. koreana

Quercus × nipponica var. koreana, commonly known as the Korean chestnut oak, is a hybrid species native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its lobed leaves that turn yellow to reddish-brown in autumn. The plant produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
