Quercus bicolor var. angustifolia

Quercus bicolor var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically lobed and have a distinctive silvery underside. The tree produces small, reddish-brown acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and ridged, providing a rugged appearance.

This variety is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodlands and along stream banks.
