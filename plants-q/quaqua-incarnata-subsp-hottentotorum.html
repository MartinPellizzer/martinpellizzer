Quaqua incarnata subsp. hottentotorum

Quaqua incarnata subsp.

hottentotorum, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial herb with slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
