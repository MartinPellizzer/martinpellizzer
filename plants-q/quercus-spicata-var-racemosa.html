Quercus spicata var. racemosa

Quercus spicata var. racemosa, commonly known as the racemose oak, is a variant of the slender oak characterized by its distinctive floral structure.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States, particularly in wetland habitats. The plant features elongated, drooping clusters of acorns, known as racemes, which give it its common name. Its leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The species is notable for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
