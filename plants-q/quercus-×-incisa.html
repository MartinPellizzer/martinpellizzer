Quercus × incisa

Quercus × incisa, commonly known as the Japanese oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus acutissima and Quercus glauca.

It is a deciduous tree native to Japan, characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically encased in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various climates.
