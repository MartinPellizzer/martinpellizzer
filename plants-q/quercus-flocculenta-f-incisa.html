Quercus flocculenta f. incisa

Quercus flocculenta f. incisa, commonly known as the serrated oak, is a botanical variant of the woolly oak.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins, which set it apart from other members of the Quercus genus. The leaves are typically pubescent, featuring a fine layer of hair that gives them a fuzzy appearance. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
