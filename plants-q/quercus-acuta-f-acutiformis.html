Quercus acuta f. acutiformis

Quercus acuta f. acutiformis, commonly known as the sharpleaf oak, is a variant of the Japanese oak species characterized by its narrow, acute leaf shape.

The leaves are typically glossy and have a pointed tip, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant is native to regions of East Asia, including parts of China and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. It is a deciduous tree that produces small, spiny acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife.

The botanical features of Quercus acuta f. acutiformis make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
