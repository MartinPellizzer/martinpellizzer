Quercus John-Tuckeri

Quercus john-tuckeri is a rare and ancient oak species discovered in the high elevations of the Sierra Nevada. This unique plant is named after John Tucker, a botanist who played a key role in its identification. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is closely related to other members of the Quercus genus. Due to its limited habitat, it is considered endangered and faces threats from climate change and habitat loss. Studying this species offers valuable insights into the evolution and adaptation of oak trees in extreme conditions.
