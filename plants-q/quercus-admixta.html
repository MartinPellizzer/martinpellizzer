Quercus admixta

Quercus admixta, commonly known as the mixed oak, is a species of deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that turn yellow to brown in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are typically covered in a dense, hairy coating. Quercus admixta typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

It plays an important role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
