Quercus tenuicupula

Quercus tenuicupula, commonly known as the slender cupule oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family, Fagaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in areas of Georgia and South Carolina. The tree is characterized by its small, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its acorns are enclosed in a cupule that is narrow and typically shorter than the acorn itself.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its unique foliage and ecological benefits.
