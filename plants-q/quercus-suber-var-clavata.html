Quercus suber var. clavata

Quercus suber var. clavata, commonly known as the cork oak variant with clustered acorns, is a subspecies of the cork oak native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thick, bark-like outer layer, which is composed of suberized cells and is used commercially for cork production. The plant typically grows in Mediterranean climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and have a distinctive shape with a rounded base.

This variant is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
