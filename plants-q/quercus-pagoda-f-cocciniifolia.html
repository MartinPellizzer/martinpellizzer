Quercus pagoda f. cocciniifolia

Quercus pagoda f. cocciniifolia, commonly known as the Crimson Maple Oak, is a unique cultivar of the Pagoda Oak species.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-red foliage that turns vibrant shades of orange and red in the fall. The leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin and a glossy, dark green upper surface. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its showy autumn color.
