Quercus lusitanica subvar. pedunculata

Quercus lusitanica subvar. pedunculata, commonly known as the Iberian pedunculate oak, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It typically grows in open woodlands and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Its bark is thick and fissured, providing habitat for various invertebrates and lichens.
