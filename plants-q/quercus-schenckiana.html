Quercus schenckiana

Quercus schenckiana, commonly known as the Schenck's oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color beneath. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is often studied for its role in local ecosystems.
