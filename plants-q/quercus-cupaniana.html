Quercus cupaniana

Quercus cupaniana, commonly known as the Cuban oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family, Fagaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba, and is characterized by its deciduous leaves and acorn-producing cones. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and has a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are typically elliptical with serrated edges and turn yellow in the autumn.

The acorns of Quercus cupaniana are edible and serve as an important food source for various wildlife species.
