Quercus cerris var. fulhamensis

Quercus cerris var. fulhamensis, commonly known as the Fulham oak, is a rare variant of the Turkey oak.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated acorns and dark, glossy leaves. This variety is native to a small area in London, specifically the Fulham region, where it has been cultivated for ornamental purposes. The tree typically grows to a moderate height with a broad, spreading canopy.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for study in urban botany and conservation efforts.
