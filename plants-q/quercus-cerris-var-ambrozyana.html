Quercus cerris var. ambrozyana

Quercus cerris var. ambrozyana, commonly known as the Ambrozyan Turkey Oak, is a rare variant of the Turkey Oak.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is often found in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
