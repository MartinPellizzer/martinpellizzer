Quercus alligata

Quercus alligata, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and oblong, glossy leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its acorns are small and often have a scaly cap, typical of many oak species.

Quercus alligata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
