Quercus suberoides

Quercus suberoides, commonly known as the coast live oak, is a deciduous evergreen oak native to coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and a broad, spreading crown that provides dense shade. The tree has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils along the coast. Its bark is thick and fissured, offering protection against fire and drought.

The coast live oak plays a vital role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
