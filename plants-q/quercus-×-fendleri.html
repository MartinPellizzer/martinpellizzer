Quercus × fendleri

Quercus × fendleri, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen oak, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the tree throughout the year. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a dense, hairy cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in higher elevations with a temperate climate.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in various botanical gardens and landscapes.
