Quercus × hispanica var. heterophylla

Quercus × hispanica var. heterophylla, commonly known as the Spanish white oak, is a hybrid species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale gray bark and lobed leaves that vary in shape and size depending on the environment. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its acorns are small and often have a short stalk, contributing to its ecological role in supporting wildlife.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional landscapes.
