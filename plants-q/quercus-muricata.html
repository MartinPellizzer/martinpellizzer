Quercus muricata

Quercus muricata, commonly known as the roughleaf oak, is a species of deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its serrated, dark green leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The tree produces small, red to orange acorns that are enclosed in a cupule. Quercus muricata typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
