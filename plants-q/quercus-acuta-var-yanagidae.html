Quercus acuta var. yanagidae

Quercus acuta var. yanagidae, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen oak, is a variant of the Japanese oak that is native to southern Japan.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its leathery, lance-shaped leaves that remain green throughout the year. The plant produces small, pale green acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded forest environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its dense foliage and adaptability.
