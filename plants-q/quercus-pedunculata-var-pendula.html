Quercus pedunculata var. pendula

Quercus pedunculata var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Chinese oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by small, inedible acorns.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
