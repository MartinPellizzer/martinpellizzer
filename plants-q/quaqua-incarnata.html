Quaqua incarnata

Quaqua incarnata, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, erect stalks above the water surface. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate. Quaqua incarnata is known for its symbolic significance in various cultures, particularly in Hinduism and Buddhism.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
