Quercus lobata var. walteri

Quercus lobata var. walteri, commonly known as the valley oak, is a deciduous tree native to California.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and open woodlands.

The valley oak is valued for its longevity and is an important component of California's native ecosystems.
