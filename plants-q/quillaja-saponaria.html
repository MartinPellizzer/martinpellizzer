Quillaja saponaria

Quillaja saponaria, commonly known as the soapbark tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark that exudes a soapy substance when cut, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces yellowish-green flowers followed by small, woody fruits. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in various soil types.

Quillaja saponaria is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its historical use by indigenous peoples.
