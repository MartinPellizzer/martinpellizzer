Quercus loureiroi

Quercus loureiroi, commonly known as the Loureiro's oak, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cupule-enclosed acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates.

Quercus loureiroi is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
