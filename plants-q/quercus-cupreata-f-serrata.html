Quercus cupreata f. serrata

Quercus cupreata f. serrata, commonly known as the red oak variant with serrated leaves, is a botanical variant of the northern red oak.

It is characterized by its distinctive lobed leaves that have sharp, serrated edges. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions. It produces acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

This form exhibits variations in leaf shape and size compared to the typical red oak.
