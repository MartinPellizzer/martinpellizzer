Quercus aliena f. calvescens

Quercus aliena f. calvescens, commonly known as the dwarf oak, is a shrub-like form of the common oak.

It is characterized by its small, dense foliage and compact growth habit. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a pale underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by acorns that mature in the fall.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
