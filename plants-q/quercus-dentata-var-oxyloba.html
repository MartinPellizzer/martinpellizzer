Quercus dentata var. oxyloba

Quercus dentata var. oxyloba, commonly known as the Japanese pagoda tree, is a variant of the Chinese cork oak.

It is a deciduous tree native to East Asia, particularly Japan and Korea. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a sweet, honey-like scent that attracts pollinators.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
