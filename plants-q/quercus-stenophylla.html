Quercus stenophylla

Quercus stenophylla, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in oak woodlands and mountainous regions.

Quercus stenophylla plays a significant role in local ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
