Quercus × hispanica var. gussonei

Quercus × hispanica var. gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone oak, is a hybrid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and deciduous leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its foliage is often densely packed, providing habitat for various insects and birds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and parks.
