Quercus coccinea var. tuberculata

Quercus coccinea var. tuberculata, commonly known as the scarlet oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage in the fall, which makes it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are typically oval with a serrated edge and a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in upland forests.
