Quercus urbani

Quercus urbani, commonly known as the urban oak, is a rare and resilient species of oak native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and acorns that are typically covered in a dense, fuzzy coating. This tree thrives in urban environments, showcasing its adaptability to pollution and compacted soils. Its slow growth rate and unique morphology make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, Quercus urbani is considered endangered, highlighting the need for preservation efforts.
