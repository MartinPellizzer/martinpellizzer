Quercus lusitanica var. boissieri

Quercus lusitanica var. boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's Portuguese oak, is a variant of the Portuguese oak native to southern Europe.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its lobed leaves and acorns, which are typical of the Fagaceae family. The leaves are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a serrated margin. It thrives in Mediterranean climates and is often found in rocky or dry soils.
