Quercus cypri

Quercus cypri, commonly known as the Cyprus oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the island of Cyprus.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top. The tree produces small, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas and rocky landscapes.

The Cyprus oak is valued for its ecological role and historical significance in the region.
