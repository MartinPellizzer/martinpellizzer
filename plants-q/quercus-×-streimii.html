Quercus × streimii

Quercus × streimii, commonly known as the Hungarian oak, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Hungary.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive lobed leaves and acorns. The plant exhibits intermediate traits between its parent species, Quercus frainetto and Quercus petraea, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in various parts of Europe.
