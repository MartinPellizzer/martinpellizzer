Quercus agrifolia var. frutescens

Quercus agrifolia var. frutescens, commonly known as the coast live oak, is a small, shrub-like variant of the coast live oak.

It is native to coastal regions of California and parts of Baja California. The plant has dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape. It produces small, pale acorns that are enclosed in cupules.

This variety is well adapted to coastal climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
