Quercus glauca subsp. schottkyana

Quercus glauca subsp.

schottkyana, commonly known as the Schottky's blue oak, is a subspecies of the blue oak native to the western United States. It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance.

The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
