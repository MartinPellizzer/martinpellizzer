Quercus pannosifolia

Quercus pannosifolia, commonly known as the Japanese oak, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a distinctive waxy coating. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Quercus pannosifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
