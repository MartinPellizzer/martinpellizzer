Quercus densiflora var. montana

Quercus densiflora var. montana, commonly known as the Sierra Madre oak, is a variant of the denseflower oak native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, mountainous environments.

Its dense foliage and sturdy structure make it a notable species in its native habitat.
