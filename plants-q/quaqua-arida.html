Quaqua arida

Quaqua arida, commonly known as the desert quill, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It features a cylindrical, spiny stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its leaves are reduced to tubular structures, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Quaqua arida is well adapted to extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
