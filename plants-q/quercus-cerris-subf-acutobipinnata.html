Quercus cerris subf. acutobipinnata

Quercus cerris subf.

acutobipinnata, commonly known as the acute-bipinnate oak, is a botanical variant of the Turkey oak. It is characterized by its finely divided, bipinnate leaves that give it a feathery appearance.

The leaves are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside, with a serrated margin. This subspecies is native to the eastern Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in woodworking.
