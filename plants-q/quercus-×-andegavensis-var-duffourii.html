Quercus × andegavensis var. duffourii

Quercus × andegavensis var. duffourii, commonly known as the Duffour's chestnut oak, is a hybrid species native to the Appalachian Mountains of the United States.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is typically smooth and gray, with a rough texture as the tree matures.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological contributions in forest ecosystems.
