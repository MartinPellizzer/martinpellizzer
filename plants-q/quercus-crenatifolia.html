Quercus crenatifolia

Quercus crenatifolia, commonly known as the serrated oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive lobed leaves, which have a serrated margin, giving it its common name. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates.

Its ecological importance makes it a valuable species in forest ecosystems.
