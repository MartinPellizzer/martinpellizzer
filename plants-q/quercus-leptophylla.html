Quercus leptophylla

Quercus leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically 2 to 4 inches long. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Quercus leptophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native range.
