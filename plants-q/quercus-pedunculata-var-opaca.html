Quercus pedunculata var. opaca

Quercus pedunculata var. opaca, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are usually oval to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in food and medicine.
