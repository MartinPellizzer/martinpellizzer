Qualea parviflora var. glabrata

Qualea parviflora var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-flowered water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that float on the water surface, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the dry season, contributing to its ecological role in nutrient cycling. Its smooth, hairless leaves distinguish it from other varieties of Qualea.

This species is adapted to fluctuating water levels and is often found in slow-moving or still freshwater environments.
