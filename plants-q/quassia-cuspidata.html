Quassia cuspidata

Quassia cuspidata, commonly known as the African quassia, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Simaroubaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. Its bark and roots contain quassinoids, which are known for their bitter principles and medicinal properties.
