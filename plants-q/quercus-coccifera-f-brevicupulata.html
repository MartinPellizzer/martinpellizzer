Quercus coccifera f. brevicupulata

Quercus coccifera f. brevicupulata, commonly known as the dwarf ink tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a variant of the common holm oak, characterized by its shorter stature and compact growth habit. The leaves are small, leathery, and often have a bluish-green hue. Its acorns are edible and have been traditionally used in Mediterranean cuisine.

This plant thrives in rocky, dry environments and is well adapted to coastal and arid conditions.
