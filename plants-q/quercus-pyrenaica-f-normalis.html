Quercus pyrenaica f. normalis

Quercus pyrenaica f. normalis, commonly known as the Pyrenean oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained soils.
