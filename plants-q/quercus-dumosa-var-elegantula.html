Quercus dumosa var. elegantula

Quercus dumosa var. elegantula, commonly known as the silverleaf oak, is a shrub-like evergreen with dense, silver-gray foliage.

It is native to the southwestern United States, particularly in Arizona and New Mexico. The plant features small, acorn-like fruits that mature in the fall. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
