Quercus castaneifolia var. algeriensis

Quercus castaneifolia var. algeriensis, commonly known as the Algerian chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Algeria.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and scrub habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
