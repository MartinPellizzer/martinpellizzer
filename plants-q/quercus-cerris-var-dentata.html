Quercus cerris var. dentata

Quercus cerris var. dentata, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically serrated along the edges. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.

This variant of the common oak is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in forest ecosystems.
