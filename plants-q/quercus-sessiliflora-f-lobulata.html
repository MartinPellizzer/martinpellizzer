Quercus sessiliflora f. lobulata

Quercus sessiliflora f. lobulata, commonly known as the lobed evergreen oak, is a variant of the sessiliflora oak characterized by its distinctive lobed leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States, particularly California. The leaves are typically narrow, leathery, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are important for wildlife.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and chaparral habitats.
