Quercus pruinosa

Quercus pruinosa, commonly known as the silverleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The leaves are typically oval or elliptic, with a pointed tip and a smooth margin. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are encased in a cupule.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
