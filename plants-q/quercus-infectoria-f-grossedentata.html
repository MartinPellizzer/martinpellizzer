Quercus infectoria f. grossedentata

Quercus infectoria f. grossedentata, commonly known as the giant flame acorn oak, is a variant of the common oak native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, acorn-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has deeply lobed leaves and produces a distinctive, sticky resin that has been historically valued for its medicinal properties. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
