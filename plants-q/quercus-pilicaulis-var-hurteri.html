Quercus pilicaulis var. hurteri

Quercus pilicaulis var. hurteri, commonly known as the Hurter's bristlecone oak, is a rare variant of the bristlecone oak found primarily in the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, bristle-like hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub, typically growing in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

The species is notable for its longevity and resilience, adapting to the extreme climates of its native habitat.
