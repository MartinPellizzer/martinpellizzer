Quercus × warei

Quercus × warei, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus mongolica and Quercus sessiliflora.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern China, characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
