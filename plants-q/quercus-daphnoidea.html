Quercus daphnoidea

Quercus daphnoidea, commonly known as the white oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.

Quercus daphnoidea plays a vital role in forest ecosystems and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
