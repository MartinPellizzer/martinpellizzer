Quercus coccinea var. tinctoria

Quercus coccinea var. tinctoria, commonly known as the scarlet oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage in the fall, which makes it a striking ornamental plant. The leaves are oval-shaped with a pointed tip and have a smooth, waxy surface. Its acorns are small, with a cupule that covers most of the nut, and are typically consumed by wildlife.

This variety is valued for its ecological benefits and aesthetic appeal in landscaping.
