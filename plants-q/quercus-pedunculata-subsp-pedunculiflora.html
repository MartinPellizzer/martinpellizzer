Quercus pedunculata subsp. pedunculiflora

Quercus pedunculata subsp.

pedunculiflora, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to China. It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves.

The tree produces small, clustered flowers in early spring, followed by edible nuts that mature in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Quercus genus, including the common chestnut.
