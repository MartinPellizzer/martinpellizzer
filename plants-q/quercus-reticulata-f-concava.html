Quercus reticulata f. concava

Quercus reticulata f. concava, commonly known as the concave-leaf Chinese oak, is a unique variant of the Chinese oak species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed, and often concave-shaped leaves that give it a striking appearance. This plant is native to China and is typically found in mountainous regions with high humidity. Its botanical features include small, acorn-like fruits and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
