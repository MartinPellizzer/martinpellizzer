Quercus durata var. gabrielensis

Quercus durata var. gabrielensis, commonly known as Gabriel's oak, is a rare and endemic species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its thick, fissured bark and leathery, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, followed by acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to its arid and semi-arid habitats, making it a subject of interest for ecological and conservation studies.
