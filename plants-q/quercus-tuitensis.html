Quercus tuitensis

Quercus tuitensis, commonly known as the Tuitán oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the mountainous regions of Colombia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-green foliage and small, acorn-like fruits that mature in the fall. The tree typically grows to a height of 10–15 meters and has a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are elliptical with a serrated margin and can reach up to 8 centimeters in length.

Quercus tuitensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
