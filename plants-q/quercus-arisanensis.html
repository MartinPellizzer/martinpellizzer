Quercus arisanensis

Quercus arisanensis, commonly known as the Arisan oak, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.
