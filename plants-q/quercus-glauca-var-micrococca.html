Quercus glauca var. micrococca

Quercus glauca var. micrococca, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, glaucous leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom, giving it a unique appearance. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
