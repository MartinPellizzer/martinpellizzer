Quercus aegilops subsp. ithaburensis

Quercus aegilops subsp.

ithaburensis, commonly known as the Ithaburensis oak, is a subspecies native to the Mediterranean region. It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that turn reddish in autumn.

The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically clustered in groups of two. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
