Quercus caeruleocarpa

Quercus caeruleocarpa, commonly known as the blue-fruited oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray acorns, which are covered in a dense, waxy coating. The leaves are typically lobed and have a dark green upper surface with a lighter underside. This species thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
