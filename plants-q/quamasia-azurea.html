Quamasia azurea

Quamasia azurea, commonly known as the blue quamasia, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Quamasia azurea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
