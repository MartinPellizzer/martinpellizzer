Quararibea turbinata var. angustifolia

Quararibea turbinata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved windmill tree, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its distinctive windmill-shaped inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a smooth surface and a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow, followed by woody, cone-like fruits.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
