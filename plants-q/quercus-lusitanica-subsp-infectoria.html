Quercus lusitanica subsp. infectoria

Quercus lusitanica subsp.

infectoria, commonly known as the Iberian oak, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

The plant features broad, lobed leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The subspecies is characterized by its robust growth and is often found in mixed forests and open woodlands.
