Quercus acutissima

Quercus acutissima, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green foliage and distinctive, acorn-like fruits. The leaves are typically narrow and pointed, with a leathery texture. This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for wildlife.
