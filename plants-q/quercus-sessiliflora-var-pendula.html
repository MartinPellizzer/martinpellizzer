Quercus sessiliflora var. pendula

Quercus sessiliflora var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping chinquapin, is a variant of the chinquapin oak characterized by its distinctive pendulous growth habit.

This plant is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It features broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The small, chestnut-like acorns are produced in clusters and are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental tree in gardens and parks.
