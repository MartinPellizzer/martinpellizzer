Quercus × sennenii

Quercus × sennenii, commonly known as the Japanese oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus crispula and Quercus myrsinaefolia.

It is a deciduous tree native to Japan, characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces acorns that are typically small and enclosed in a cupule, which is a common feature among oak species. Quercus × sennenii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for wildlife.
