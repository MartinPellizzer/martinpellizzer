Quercus coccifera var. angustata

Quercus coccifera var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, coastal, and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its dense growth habit makes it useful for erosion control and habitat creation in degraded landscapes.
