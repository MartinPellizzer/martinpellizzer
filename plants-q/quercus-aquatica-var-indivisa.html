Quercus aquatica var. indivisa

Quercus aquatica var. indivisa, commonly known as the Florida water oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically lobed and have a waxy surface. The tree produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or wetland areas.

Its ecological significance lies in its ability to support biodiversity and stabilize aquatic ecosystems.
