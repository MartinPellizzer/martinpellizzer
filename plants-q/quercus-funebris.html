Quercus funebris

Quercus funebris, commonly known as the black oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. The leaves are typically lobed and have a glossy upper surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

The tree is valued for its ecological role and has historical uses in both construction and medicine.
