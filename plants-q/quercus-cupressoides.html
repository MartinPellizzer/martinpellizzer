Quercus cupressoides

Quercus cupressoides, commonly known as the Italian cypress, is a species of evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, columnar shape and dense, dark green foliage. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in compact clusters along the branches. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by hard, woody acorns.

This tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and horticulture.
