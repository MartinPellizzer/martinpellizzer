Quercus striata

Quercus striata, commonly known as the stripeleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that have a narrow, striped pattern on the upper surface. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is typically gray and furrowed, providing a rugged appearance.

Quercus striata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
