Quercus × dentatomongolica

Quercus × dentatomongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian chestnut oak, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to northeastern Asia.

It exhibits characteristics of both Quercus mongolica and Quercus dentata, combining traits such as robust growth and adaptability to diverse environments. The plant has alternate, simple, and serrated leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. It produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological contributions in forest ecosystems.
