Quercus marilandica f. integrifolia

Quercus marilandica f. integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark gray bark and alternate, lance-shaped leaves with entire margins. The plant produces small, red-brown acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule with scales. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive component of the understory in its natural habitat.
