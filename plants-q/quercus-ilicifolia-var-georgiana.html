Quercus ilicifolia var. georgiana

Quercus ilicifolia var. georgiana, commonly known as the Georgia holly oak, is a deciduous oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by small, red-brown acorns that mature in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
