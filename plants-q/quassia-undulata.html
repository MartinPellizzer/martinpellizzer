Quassia undulata

Quassia undulata, commonly known as the African quassia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Simaroubaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces yellow, bitter-tasting fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, orange-colored aril. Its bark and roots have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimalarial properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
