Quercus pseudosessilis

Quercus pseudosessilis, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to different climates.
