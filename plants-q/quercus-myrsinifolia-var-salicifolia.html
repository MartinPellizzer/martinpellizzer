Quercus myrsinifolia var. salicifolia

Quercus myrsinifolia var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf oak, is a variant of the myrsinifolia oak species.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in areas of Georgia and South Carolina. This plant is characterized by its elongated, willow-like leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The leaves are often serrated along the edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that mature over several months, providing food for various wildlife species.
