Quercus dilacerata

Quercus dilacerata, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut or the Brazilian oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and acorn-like fruits. The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas. It plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and is the subject of conservation efforts.
