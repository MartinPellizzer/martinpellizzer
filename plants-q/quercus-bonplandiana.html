Quercus bonplandiana

Quercus bonplandiana, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Quercus bonplandiana is valued for its ecological role and is also used in traditional medicine.
