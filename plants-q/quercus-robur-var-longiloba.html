Quercus robur var. longiloba

Quercus robur var. longiloba, commonly known as the long-lobed oak, is a variant of the common oak characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its leaves are elongated with prominently lobed margins, setting it apart from typical Quercus robur. This variety is native to specific regions in Europe, where it thrives in temperate climates. The tree produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It is valued for its hardy nature and is often found in woodland habitats.
