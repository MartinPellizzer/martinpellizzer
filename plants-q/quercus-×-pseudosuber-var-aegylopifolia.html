Quercus × pseudosuber var. aegylopifolia

Quercus × pseudosuber var. aegylopifolia, commonly known as the Aegyptiaca oak, is a hybrid species within the genus Quercus, characterized by its unique combination of traits from its parent species.

It typically exhibits a broad, spreading canopy and possesses leaves that are often lobed and have a distinctive silvery underside. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
