Quercus lusitanica var. pedunculata

Quercus lusitanica var. pedunculata, commonly known as the Spanish white oak, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The species produces small, acorn-like fruits that are often clustered in peduncles, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional forestry.
