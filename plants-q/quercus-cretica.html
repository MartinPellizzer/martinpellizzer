Quercus cretica

Quercus cretica, commonly known as the Persian oak, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. The leaves are leathery, elliptical, and often have a wavy margin. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
