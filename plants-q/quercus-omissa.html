Quercus omissa

Quercus omissa, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its acorns are small and edible, often consumed by wildlife.

Quercus omissa typically grows in dry to moist forest habitats and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.
