Quercus scyphigera var. riedelii

Quercus scyphigera var. riedelii, commonly known as the Riedel's oak, is a rare and unique variant of the scyphigera oak.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring broad, lobed leaves with a serrated margin. The plant is native to specific regions in Central Europe, where it thrives in moist, temperate climates. Its acorns are small and often have a smooth surface, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
