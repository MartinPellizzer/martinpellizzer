Quercus coccifera f. angustifolia

Quercus coccifera f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to withstand drought and salt spray.
