Quercus macranthera subsp. macranthera

Quercus macranthera subsp.

macranthera, commonly known as the broad-leaved white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often hairy when young and turn reddish-brown in the fall.

The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its hardy nature and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
