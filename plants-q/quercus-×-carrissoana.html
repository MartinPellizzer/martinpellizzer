Quercus × carrissoana

Quercus × carrissoana, commonly known as the Carrissoana oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus garryana and Quercus petraea.

It is a deciduous tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, characterized by its lobed leaves and acorns. The plant exhibits a range of morphological variations, including differences in leaf shape and acorn size. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

The botanical interest of Quercus × carrissoana lies in its role as a natural hybrid, showcasing genetic diversity within the oak genus.
