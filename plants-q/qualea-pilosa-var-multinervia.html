Qualea pilosa var. multinervia

Qualea pilosa var. multinervia, commonly known as the water lily of the Amazon, is a species native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has a robust root system that anchors it in the muddy substrates of slow-moving rivers and lakes. Its leaves are typically green with a waxy surface that helps repel water, while the flowers display a range of colors from white to pink.

This plant plays a vital role in the aquatic ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
