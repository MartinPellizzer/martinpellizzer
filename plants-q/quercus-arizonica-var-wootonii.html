Quercus arizonica var. wootonii

Quercus arizonica var. wootonii, commonly known as the Wooton's Arizona oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its short, acorn-like fruits and deeply lobed, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, adapting to low moisture conditions. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against fire and drought.

This species plays an important role in local ecosystems by offering habitat and food for various wildlife species.
