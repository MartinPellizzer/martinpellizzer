Quercus spicata var. collettii

Quercus spicata var. collettii, commonly known as the Collett's bracken fern oak, is a rare and unique variant of the bracken fern oak.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered endangered, highlighting the need for its protection.
