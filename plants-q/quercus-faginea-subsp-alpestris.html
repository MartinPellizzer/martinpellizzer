Quercus faginea subsp. alpestris

Quercus faginea subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine hornbeam oak, is a subspecies of the common hornbeam oak native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and dark, glossy leaves.

This subspecies thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine soils. Its growth habit is typically more compact compared to other members of the Quercus genus, adapting to harsh climatic conditions.

The plant plays an important ecological role in alpine ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
