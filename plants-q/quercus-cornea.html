Quercus cornea

Quercus cornea, commonly known as the Persian ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The tree has dark green, elliptical leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. It produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Quercus cornea thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
