Quercus robur var. tenuifolia

Quercus robur var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaved oak, is a rare variant of the common oak.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically lighter in color compared to the standard species. This plant is native to specific regions in Europe, where it thrives in temperate climates. The tree produces acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Its unique foliage makes it a distinctive and valuable addition to botanical collections.
