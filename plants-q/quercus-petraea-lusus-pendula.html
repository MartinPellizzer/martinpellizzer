Quercus petraea lusus pendula

Quercus petraea lusus pendula, commonly known as the weeping sessile oak, is a unique variant of the sessile oak characterized by its pendulous branches that create a cascading effect.

It is a deciduous tree native to Europe, typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits and has deeply lobed, dark green leaves that turn golden in autumn. Its distinctive appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and parks.

However, it is relatively slow-growing and requires well-drained, acidic soil to thrive.
