Quercus glandulifera var. brevipetiolata

Quercus glandulifera var. brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-petioled gall oak, is a variant of the gall oak native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves with short petioles and prominent glandular hairs on the underside. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
