Quercus conspersa f. caudata

Quercus conspersa f. caudata, commonly known as the hairy oak or bristly oak, is a variant of the broad-leaved oak species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves that are often lobed and have a rough texture. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its acorns are typically small and may be used by various wildlife species.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important component of forest ecosystems.
