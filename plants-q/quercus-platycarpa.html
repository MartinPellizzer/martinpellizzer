Quercus platycarpa

Quercus platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, broad acorns and dark, glossy leaves. The tree typically grows to a height of 20 to 40 feet and has a rounded, spreading crown. Its bark is thick and ridged, providing a rugged texture.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
