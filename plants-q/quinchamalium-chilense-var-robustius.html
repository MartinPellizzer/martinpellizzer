Quinchamalium chilense var. robustius

Quinchamalium chilense var. robustius, commonly known as the Chilean quinchamalium, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The variety robustius is distinguished by its more vigorous growth compared to other varieties of Quinchamalium.
