Quercus pedunculata var. dulcis

Quercus pedunculata var. dulcis, commonly known as the sweet chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by edible, sweet, chestnut-like fruits. Its botanical features include a deep root system and the ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in forestry and cuisine.
