Quercus cuspidata var. variegata

Quercus cuspidata var. variegata, commonly known as the Japanese variegated oak, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features white and green patterns on the leaves. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters in optimal conditions. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated margin and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
