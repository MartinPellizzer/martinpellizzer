Quercus myrtifolia

Quercus myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtleleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and have a distinct myrtle-like scent. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

Quercus myrtifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
