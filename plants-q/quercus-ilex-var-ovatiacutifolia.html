Quercus ilex var. ovatiacutifolia

Quercus ilex var. ovatiacutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved evergreen oak, is a variant of the holm oak native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically shorter and narrower than those of the typical Q. ilex. The plant produces small, spiny acorns that are enclosed in a cupule, a feature common among oaks. It thrives in Mediterranean climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and ornamental value.
