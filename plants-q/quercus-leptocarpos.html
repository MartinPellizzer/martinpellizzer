Quercus leptocarpos

Quercus leptocarpos, commonly known as the slenderleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are enclosed in a dense, hairy cap. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its ornamental features.
