Quercus yousei

Quercus yousei, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, leathery leaves that remain on the tree throughout the year. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically retained on the tree for several years before falling. Quercus yousei thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
