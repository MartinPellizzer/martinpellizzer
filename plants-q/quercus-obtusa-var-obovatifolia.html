Quercus obtusa var. obovatifolia

Quercus obtusa var. obovatifolia, commonly known as the Japanese oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate leaves that are typically glossy and have a pointed tip. The plant produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
