Quercus chrysolepis subsp. palmeri

Quercus chrysolepis subsp.

palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's valley oak, is a subspecies native to the western United States. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and acorn-producing capsules.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing habitat for various insects and small animals.

The subspecies is valued for its ecological role and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
