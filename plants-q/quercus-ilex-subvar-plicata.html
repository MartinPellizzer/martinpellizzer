Quercus ilex subvar. plicata

Quercus ilex subvar. plicata, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a subspecies of the evergreen oak native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often rolled under the margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
