Quercus acutissima var. lioui

Quercus acutissima var. lioui, commonly known as the Chinese sharpleaf oak, is a variant of the Chinese oak native to eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its acutely pointed leaves and robust growth habit. The leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by cup-shaped acorns that mature in the fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
