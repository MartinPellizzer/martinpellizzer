Quercus greggii f. subglabra

Quercus greggii f. subglabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf scrub oak, is a variant of the Gregg's oak native to the Southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. This plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and limestone areas. Its small, acorn-like fruits are an important food source for local wildlife.

The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species in xeriscaped landscapes.
