Quercus germanica

Quercus germanica, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn reddish-brown in autumn. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against pests.

Quercus germanica is often found in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
