Quercus cerris subf. rotundatolaciniata

Quercus cerris subf.

rotundatolaciniata, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a variant of the common oak characterized by its distinctive foliage. It features broad, rounded leaves that are often lobed and have a waxy texture, which helps it withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The plant is native to the eastern Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability and resilience, making it a valuable species in ecological restoration projects.
