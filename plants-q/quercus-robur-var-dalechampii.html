Quercus robur var. dalechampii

Quercus robur var. dalechampii, commonly known as the Dalechamp's oak, is a variant of the common oak characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It features broad, lobed leaves with a serrated margin, often exhibiting a deep green color on the upper surface and a lighter green or silvery underside. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It is native to parts of Europe and is known for its moderate growth rate and hardy nature.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
