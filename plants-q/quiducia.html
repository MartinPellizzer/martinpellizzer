Quiducia

Quiducia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom at night, relying on specific pollinators for reproduction. Quiducia thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires a high level of moisture and nutrient-rich soil.

Its unique botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
