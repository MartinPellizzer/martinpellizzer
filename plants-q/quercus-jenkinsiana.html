Quercus jenkinsiana

Quercus jenkinsiana, commonly known as the Jenkins oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family, Fagaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Mexico, particularly in the states of Chiapas and Oaxaca. This evergreen tree can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring broad, elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a pale underside. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically covered in a dense, hairy cap.

Quercus jenkinsiana is valued for its ecological importance and is often found in cloud forests where it contributes to biodiversity.
