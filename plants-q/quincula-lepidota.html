Quincula lepidota

Quincula lepidota, commonly known as the velvetleaf quincula, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, oval-shaped leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Quincula lepidota thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
