Quercus lezermiana

Quercus lezermiana, commonly known as the Sierra Madre oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces acorns that are encased in a cupule, which is a unique feature among oak species. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The Sierra Madre oak plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
