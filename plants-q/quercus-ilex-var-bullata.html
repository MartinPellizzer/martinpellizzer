Quercus ilex var. bullata

Quercus ilex var. bullata, commonly known as the Spanish oak, is a variant of the evergreen holm oak native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically smaller and more rounded compared to the standard Quercus ilex. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in Mediterranean climates and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
