Quercus robur subvar. bedoi

Quercus robur subvar. bedoi, commonly known as the Bedouin oak, is a subspecies of the common oak native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and thick, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and exhibits a slow growth rate, making it a long-lived species. Its bark is deeply furrowed and becomes more rugged with age, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
