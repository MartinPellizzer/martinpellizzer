Quercus nivea

Quercus nivea, commonly known as the white oak, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, spiky acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is thick and deeply furrowed, providing a rugged appearance.

Quercus nivea is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
