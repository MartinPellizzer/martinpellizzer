Quercus castaneifolia var. typica

Quercus castaneifolia var. typica, commonly known as the Persian ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive, glossy, elongated leaves that resemble those of chestnuts. The tree produces small, spiky, red or brown acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is known for its hard, heavy wood, which is highly valued for construction and tool-making.

This species is also notable for its adaptability to arid and semi-arid environments.
