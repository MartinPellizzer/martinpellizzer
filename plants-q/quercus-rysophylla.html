Quercus rysophylla

Quercus rysophylla, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and elongated, oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, chestnut-like acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forests and woodlands.

Quercus rysophylla is valued for its ecological role and is also used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
