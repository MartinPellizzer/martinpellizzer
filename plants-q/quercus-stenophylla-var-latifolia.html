Quercus stenophylla var. latifolia

Quercus stenophylla var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf canyon oak, is a variant of the canyon oak native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often pubescent underneath, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is typically found in canyon and mountainous regions. It produces acorns that are an important food source for local wildlife.

The species is valued for its ecological role and its ability to withstand arid conditions.
