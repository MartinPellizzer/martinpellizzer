Quercus paucidentata

Quercus paucidentata, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and elliptical leaves with rounded tips. Its acorns are small and have a short stalk, typically maturing in the second year.
