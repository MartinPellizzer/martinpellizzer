Quercus minima f. reasoneri

Quercus minima f. reasoneri, commonly known as the dwarf oak, is a compact variant of the minima oak, native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
