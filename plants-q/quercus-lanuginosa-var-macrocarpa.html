Quercus lanuginosa var. macrocarpa

Quercus lanuginosa var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the silky beech or macrocarpa beech, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, oval leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The tree produces small, red-brown acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in timber production.
