Quercus sessiliflora var. longicarpa

Quercus sessiliflora var. longicarpa, commonly known as the long-fruit sessile oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits that are elongated and often have a smooth surface. The leaves are typically broad, elliptical, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the ecology of its native range.
