Quercus × benderi

Quercus × benderi, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus polymorpha and Quercus crassifolia.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico, where it thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. The tree typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring a broad, rounded crown and deeply lobed leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its acorns are small, with a scaly cap, and serve as an important food source for various wildlife species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
