Quercus cerris subsp. austriaca

Quercus cerris subsp.

austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian Turkey Oak, is a subspecies of the Turkey Oak native to Central Europe. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 25 meters.

The bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance and offering some protection against pests and diseases.
