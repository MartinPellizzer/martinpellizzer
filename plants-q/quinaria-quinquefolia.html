Quinaria quinquefolia

Quinaria quinquefolia, commonly known as the five-leaved chervil, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes parsley and carrots, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. Quinaria quinquefolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.
