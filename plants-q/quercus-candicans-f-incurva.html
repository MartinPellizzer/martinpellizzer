Quercus candicans f. incurva

Quercus candicans f. incurva, commonly known as the curlleaf oak, is a botanical variant of the broadleaf oak species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curved leaves that give it a unique appearance compared to other oak varieties. The plant is native to specific regions in Mexico, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage is typically a deep green, and it produces small, acorn-like fruits.

The curlleaf oak is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
