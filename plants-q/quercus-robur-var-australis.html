Quercus robur var. australis

Quercus robur var. australis, commonly known as the Australian oak, is a variant of the common oak native to Australia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its robust growth and adaptability make it a notable species in Australian botany.
