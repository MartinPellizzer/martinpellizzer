Quercus falcata var. leucophylla

Quercus falcata var. leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaf chinquapin, is a variant of the chestnut oak.

It is characterized by its broad, white-veined leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. This plant is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The leaves are typically longer and more elongated compared to the standard variety of Q. falcata.

It produces small, red acorns that mature over a year, contributing to its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
