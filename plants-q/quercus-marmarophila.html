Quercus marmarophila

Quercus marmarophila, commonly known as the marbleleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely veined, and often variegated leaves that display a striking combination of green, white, and silver hues. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is typically smooth and grayish in appearance, becoming fissured with age.

Quercus marmarophila is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
