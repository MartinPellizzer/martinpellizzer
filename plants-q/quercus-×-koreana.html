Quercus × koreana

Quercus × koreana, commonly known as the Korean oak, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. The tree produces small, cupule-enclosed acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and reforestation projects.
