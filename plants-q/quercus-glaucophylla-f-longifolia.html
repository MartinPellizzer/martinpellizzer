Quercus glaucophylla f. longifolia

Quercus glaucophylla f. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf oak, is a variant of the glaucophylla oak species.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glaucous in appearance, giving the plant a distinctive silvery-blue hue. The plant is native to specific regions of Australia, where it thrives in well-drained soils and sunny conditions. Its acorns are small and often have a cupule that partially encloses the nut.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
