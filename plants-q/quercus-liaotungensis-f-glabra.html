Quercus liaotungensis f. glabra

Quercus liaotungensis f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved Chinese oak, is a variant of the Liaotung oak.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. This plant is native to China, particularly in the Liaoning province, where it thrives in temperate climates. The tree can reach heights of up to 15 meters and produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
