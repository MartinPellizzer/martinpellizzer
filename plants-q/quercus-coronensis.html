Quercus coronensis

Quercus coronensis, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its acorns are small and typically have a cupule, or cap, that encloses most of the nut.

Quercus coronensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for wildlife.
