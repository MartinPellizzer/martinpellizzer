Quercus glauca

Quercus glauca, commonly known as the blue oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and small, acorn-like fruits. The leaves are typically blue-green in color, which gives the tree its common name. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
