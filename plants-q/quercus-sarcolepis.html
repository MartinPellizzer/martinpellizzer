Quercus sarcolepis

Quercus sarcolepis, commonly known as the red oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lobed leaves, which turn vibrant red in the autumn, and its acorns that are enclosed in a cupule. The tree has a strong, straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown, making it a prominent feature in forest ecosystems. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing habitat for various insects and small animals.

Quercus sarcolepis plays an important role in soil stabilization and supports a wide range of wildlife species.
