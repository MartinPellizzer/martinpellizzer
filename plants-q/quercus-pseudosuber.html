Quercus pseudosuber

Quercus pseudosuber, commonly known as the cork oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thick, fissured bark that can be harvested for cork production. The plant has broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by acorns that are important for wildlife.

Quercus pseudosuber plays a vital role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various species.
