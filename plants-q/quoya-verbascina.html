Quoya verbascina

Quoya verbascina, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has feathery, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its dry habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy environments.

Quoya verbascina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in some regions for its vibrant floral display.
