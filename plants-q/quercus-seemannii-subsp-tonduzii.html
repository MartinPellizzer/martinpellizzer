Quercus seemannii subsp. tonduzii

Quercus seemannii subsp.

tonduzii, commonly known as the Tonduzii oak, is a rare and endemic species found primarily in the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe. It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and dark, glossy leaves that are typically elliptical in shape.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with distinct seasonal variations. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
