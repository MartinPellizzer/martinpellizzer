Quercus robur var. robusta

Quercus robur var. robusta, commonly known as the English oak, is a robust variant of the common oak, characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit.

It is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates, often found in woodlands and along riverbanks. The tree produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are typically broad and lobed, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

This variety is valued for its strong, durable wood and its ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
