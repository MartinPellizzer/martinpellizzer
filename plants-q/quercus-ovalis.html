Quercus ovalis

Quercus ovalis, commonly known as the ovalleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves, which are typically 4 to 8 inches long and have a smooth, dark green upper surface. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Quercus ovalis is valued for its hardy nature and ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
