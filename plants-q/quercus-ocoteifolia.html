Quercus ocoteifolia

Quercus ocoteifolia, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in oak woodlands and mixed forests.

Quercus ocoteifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
