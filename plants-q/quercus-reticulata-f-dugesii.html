Quercus reticulata f. dugesii

Quercus reticulata f. dugesii, commonly known as the Dugesii form of the reticulate oak, is a variant of the reticulate oak native to the mountainous regions of China.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive, intricate leaf venation pattern, which gives it its scientific name. The leaves are typically broad and lobed, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in temperate forest ecosystems.
