Quintinia brassii

Quintinia brassii, commonly known as the coastal quintinia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Quintinia brassii is well adapted to sandy, saline soils and is often found in dune environments.
