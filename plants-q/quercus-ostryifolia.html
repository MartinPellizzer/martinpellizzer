Quercus ostryifolia

Quercus ostryifolia, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its acorns are enclosed in a cupule and are an important food source for various wildlife.

Quercus ostryifolia typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.
