Quercus eumorpha subsp. yaiensis

Quercus eumorpha subsp.

yaiensis, commonly known as the Yaiensis oak, is a member of the Fagaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical forest environments.

The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in catkins, while the fruit is a nut enclosed in a cupule.

The subspecies is characterized by its moderate size and adaptability to various soil types.
