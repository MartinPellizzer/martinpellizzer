Quercus rotundifolia f. dolichocalyx

Quercus rotundifolia f. dolichocalyx, commonly known as the longleaf chestnut oak, is a variant of the red oak species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are typically 5 to 8 inches long, giving it a distinctive appearance compared to other oak species. The plant produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in upland forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
