Quercus brantii var. marasiensis

Quercus brantii var. marasiensis, commonly known as the Marasi oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and leathery, elliptical leaves. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is also noted for its drought tolerance and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
