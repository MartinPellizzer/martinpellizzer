Quercus exaristata

Quercus exaristata, commonly known as the Mexican bristle oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, bristly foliage and small, acorn-like fruits. The leaves are typically narrow and have a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
