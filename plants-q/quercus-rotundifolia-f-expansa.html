Quercus rotundifolia f. expansa

Quercus rotundifolia f. expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf sessile oak, is a variant of the sessile oak characterized by its broader leaves.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The leaves are typically rounded or elliptical, with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface. This form exhibits a more spreading growth habit compared to the typical sessile oak.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
