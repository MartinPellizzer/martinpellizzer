Quercus × andegavensis var. rechinii

Quercus × andegavensis var. rechinii, commonly known as the Andegavensis chestnut oak, is a hybrid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is typically scaly and grayish, with a rugged texture that becomes more pronounced with age.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
