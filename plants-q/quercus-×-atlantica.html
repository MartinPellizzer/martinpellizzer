Quercus × atlantica

Quercus × atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus cerris (Turkey oak) and Quercus robur (English oak).

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, pale acorns that mature over a season, and it is often found in regions with temperate climates. Quercus × atlantica is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
