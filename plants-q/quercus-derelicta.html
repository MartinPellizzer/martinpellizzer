Quercus derelicta

Quercus derelicta, commonly known as the Arizona white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

Quercus derelicta thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.
