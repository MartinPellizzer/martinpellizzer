Quercus falcata var. triloba

Quercus falcata var. triloba, commonly known as the three-toothed oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three lobes, giving it its common name. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically covered with a dense layer of hair. Its bark is rugged and deeply furrowed, providing a rugged appearance.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
