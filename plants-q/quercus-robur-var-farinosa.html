Quercus robur var. farinosa

Quercus robur var. farinosa, commonly known as the downy oak, is a variant of the common oak characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves.

This plant is native to western Europe and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine, downy hairs, giving them a soft texture. The tree produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It is often found in mixed forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.
