Quercus petraea f. lobulosa

Quercus petraea f. lobulosa, commonly known as the rock oak or European rock oak, is a shrub-like form of the sessile oak.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are often lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in mountainous regions of Europe. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and urban landscapes.

It produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
