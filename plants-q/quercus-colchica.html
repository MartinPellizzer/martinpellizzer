Quercus colchica

Quercus colchica, commonly known as the Spanish chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, followed by spiny, cup-like acorns that mature over several months. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forests and woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and historical landscapes.
