Quercus frainetto var. aurea

Quercus frainetto var. aurea, commonly known as the golden oak, is a variant of the English oak characterized by its striking golden foliage.

It is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically growing in temperate climates. The leaves are elliptical with serrated edges and turn a brilliant yellow in the autumn. It produces small, chestnut-like acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

This tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and parks for its visual appeal.
