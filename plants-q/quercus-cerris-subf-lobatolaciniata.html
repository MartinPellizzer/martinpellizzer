Quercus cerris subf. lobatolaciniata

Quercus cerris subf.

lobatolaciniata, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a subspecies of the common oak characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology. Its leaves are broad, lobed, and often have a serrated margin, distinguishing it from other oak species.

The tree typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Mediterranean landscapes.
