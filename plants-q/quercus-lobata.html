Quercus lobata

Quercus lobata, commonly known as the valley oak, is a large deciduous tree native to California.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically 5 to 10 inches long. The tree produces acorns that are enclosed in a cupule, which is distinctive in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and open woodlands.

The valley oak plays an important ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
