Quassia cuneata

Quassia cuneata, commonly known as the African quassia, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Simaroubaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. Quassia cuneata is known for its medicinal properties, with the bark containing compounds used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
