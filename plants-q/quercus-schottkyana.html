Quercus schottkyana

Quercus schottkyana, commonly known as the Schottky's oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy green upper surface. The plant produces small, clustered acorns that are encased in a cupule, a feature common among many oak species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in humid, temperate climates.

Quercus schottkyana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
