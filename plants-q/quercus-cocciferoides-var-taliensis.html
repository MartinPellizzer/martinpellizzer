Quercus cocciferoides var. taliensis

Quercus cocciferoides var. taliensis, commonly known as the Taliensis red oak, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its acorns, which are typically enclosed in a cupule and have a distinctive red coloration. The leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins and a glossy upper surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, temperate climates.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in local ecosystems.
