Quercus glauca var. hypargyrea

Quercus glauca var. hypargyrea, commonly known as the silverleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically oval to elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its unique foliage.
