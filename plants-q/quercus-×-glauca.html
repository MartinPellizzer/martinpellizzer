Quercus × glauca

Quercus × glauca, commonly known as the blue oak, is a hybrid species of oak native to California.

It is characterized by its dark, bluish-gray bark and small, acorn-like fruits. The leaves are typically lobed and have a leathery texture, adapting well to the dry climate of its native range. It thrives in chaparral and woodland habitats, often growing on well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
