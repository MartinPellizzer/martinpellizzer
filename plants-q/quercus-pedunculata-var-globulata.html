Quercus pedunculata var. globulata

Quercus pedunculata var. globulata, commonly known as the globe oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rounded crown and small, acorn-like fruits that are clustered in globular formations. The leaves are typically elliptical with a pointed tip and are arranged alternately along the branches. The tree has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

It is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.
