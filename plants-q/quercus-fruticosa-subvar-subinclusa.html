Quercus fruticosa subvar. subinclusa

Quercus fruticosa subvar. subinclusa, commonly known as the narrowleaf buckthorn, is a shrub-like species native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks. The plant has small, oval leaves that are usually less than 2 inches long and have a pointed tip. Its flowers are inconspicuous, and it produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
