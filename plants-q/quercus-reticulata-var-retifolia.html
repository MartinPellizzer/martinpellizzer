Quercus reticulata var. retifolia

Quercus reticulata var. retifolia, commonly known as the reticulated oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its intricate, lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The leaves typically turn a striking red or bronze color in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

This variety is valued for its unique foliage and adaptability to a range of soil types.
