Quercus × munzii

Quercus × munzii, commonly known as the Munz's oak, is a hybrid species native to the western United States.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats. The plant features broad, leathery leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its acorns are small, typically less than 1 cm in diameter, and are enclosed in a cupule.

Quercus × munzii is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in areas with mild, Mediterranean climates.
