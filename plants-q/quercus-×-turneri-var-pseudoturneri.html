Quercus × turneri var. pseudoturneri

Quercus × turneri var. pseudoturneri, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a hybrid species native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and oval, glossy leaves that are typically 5–10 cm in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by acorns that mature in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in oak woodlands and scrub habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
