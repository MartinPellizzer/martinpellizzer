Quercus robur var. opaca

Quercus robur var. opaca, commonly known as the English oak, is a variant of the common oak native to Europe.

It is a large, deciduous tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters, featuring a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a dark green upper surface with a lighter underside. The tree produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It is characterized by its durable, heavy timber and is often used in construction and furniture-making.
