Quercus × pauciloba subsp. confusa

Quercus × pauciloba subsp.

confusa, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a hybrid species within the genus Quercus, characterized by its distinctive white to pale gray bark and broad, lobed leaves. It is native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico, where it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are typically deciduous, turning yellow or red in the fall before dropping.

This subspecies is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
