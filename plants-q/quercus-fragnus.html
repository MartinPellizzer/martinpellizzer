Quercus fragnus

Quercus fragnus, commonly known as the sweet chestnut or European sweet chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and oval, glossy leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in spring, followed by spiny, spherical fruits called nuts. Quercus fragnus is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
