Quercus lanuginosa var. cerrioides

Quercus lanuginosa var. cerrioides, commonly known as the silky oak or Australian white oak, is a deciduous tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and small, elongated leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The tree produces small, red or orange flowers followed by cup-shaped acorns. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and riparian habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
