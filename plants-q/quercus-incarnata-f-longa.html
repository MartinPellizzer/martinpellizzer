Quercus incarnata f. longa

Quercus incarnata f. longa, commonly known as the swamp chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn a striking red or purple in the fall. The tree produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible acorns that are important for wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological diversity and forest regeneration.
