Quercus argyrotricha

Quercus argyrotricha, commonly known as the silver-haired oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, acorn-like fruits and leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white pubescence. The plant belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. Its leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a pointed tip and a rough, serrated margin.

The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring, followed by the development of its distinctive acorns.
