Quercus velutina f. nobilis

Quercus velutina f. nobilis, commonly known as the silky beech, is a botanical variant of the chestnut oak.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves with a distinctive silvery-white underside. The plant is native to the eastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, red acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological contributions in forest ecosystems.
