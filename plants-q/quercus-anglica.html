Quercus anglica

Quercus anglica, commonly known as the English oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is a large deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are lobed, typically with five to seven lobes, and are dark green on top and pale underneath. The tree produces small, pale green flowers in catkins, followed by acorns that mature over a year.

It is native to Europe and is known for its strong, durable wood and ecological importance.
