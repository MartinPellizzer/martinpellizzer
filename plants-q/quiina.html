Quiina

Quiina, commonly known as the quinoa plant, is an annual flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the genus Chenopodium and is closely related to spinach and beets. The plant produces small, edible seeds that are rich in protein, fiber, and essential nutrients. Its leaves are often consumed as a vegetable, while the seeds are used in various culinary applications.

Quiina thrives in high-altitude environments and is well adapted to poor soil conditions.
