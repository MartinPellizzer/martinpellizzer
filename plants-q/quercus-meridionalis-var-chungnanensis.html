Quercus meridionalis var. chungnanensis

Quercus meridionalis var. chungnanensis, commonly known as the Chungnan oak, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It produces small, edible acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
