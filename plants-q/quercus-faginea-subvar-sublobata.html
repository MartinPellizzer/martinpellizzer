Quercus faginea subvar. sublobata

Quercus faginea subvar. sublobata, commonly known as the Carpathian sessile oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically shorter and more rounded compared to the typical subspecies. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

The botanical features of this subspecies make it an ecologically significant component of its native habitat.
