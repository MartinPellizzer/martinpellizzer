Quercus pedunculata var. rosacea

Quercus pedunculata var. rosacea, commonly known as the pinkleaf oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinkish-red leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces small, dark brown acorns that are typically enclosed in cupules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream valleys.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
