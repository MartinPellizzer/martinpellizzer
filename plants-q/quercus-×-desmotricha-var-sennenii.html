Quercus × desmotricha var. sennenii

Quercus × desmotricha var. sennenii, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut oak, is a hybrid species native to Japan.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and lobed, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and it produces edible acorns that are encased in a prickly husk.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
