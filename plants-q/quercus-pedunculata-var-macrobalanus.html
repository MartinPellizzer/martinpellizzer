Quercus pedunculata var. macrobalanus

Quercus pedunculata var. macrobalanus, commonly known as the giant Chinese oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that can reach up to 20 centimeters in length. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are encased in a cupule. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its robust growth and ornamental features make it a valued species in horticulture.
