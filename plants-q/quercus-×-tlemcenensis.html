Quercus × tlemcenensis

Quercus × tlemcenensis, commonly known as the Tlemcen oak, is a hybrid species native to the Maghreb region of North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and broad, lobed leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and for reforestation projects.
