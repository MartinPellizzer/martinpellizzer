Quercus conzattii

Quercus conzattii, commonly known as the Conzattii oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Arizona and western New Mexico in the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, leathery leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by acorns that are about 0.5 inches long. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Quercus conzattii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
