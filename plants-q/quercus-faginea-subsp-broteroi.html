Quercus faginea subsp. broteroi

Quercus faginea subsp.

broteroi, commonly known as the Spanish chestnut oak, is a subspecies of the common oak native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically pubescent on the underside.

The plant produces small, reddish-brown acorns that are enclosed in a cupule with bracts. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and mountainous regions.

The botanical features of this subspecies make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
