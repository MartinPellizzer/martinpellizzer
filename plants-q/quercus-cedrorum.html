Quercus cedrorum

Quercus cedrorum, commonly known as the California black oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and acorns that are encased in a cupule. The leaves are typically lobed and have a leathery texture, turning yellow in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils in dry to moist habitats.

The tree plays a vital role in providing habitat and food for numerous wildlife species.
