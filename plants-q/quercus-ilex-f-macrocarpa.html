Quercus ilex f. macrocarpa

Quercus ilex f. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-leaf evergreen oak, is a variant of the holm oak characterized by its larger, more elongated leaves.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant is evergreen, retaining its foliage year-round, and exhibits a dense, rounded growth habit. Its leaves are typically glossy green on the upper surface and lighter green underneath, with a leathery texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
