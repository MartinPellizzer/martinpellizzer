Quercus baronii var. capillata

Quercus baronii var. capillata, commonly known as the California black oak var. capillata, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and acorns that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The leaves are lobed, with a serrated margin, and they turn reddish-brown in the fall. This variety is often found in mixed coniferous forests and chaparral habitats.

It plays an important role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
