Quercus garryana var. jacobi

Quercus garryana var. jacobi, commonly known as the Garry oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its deeply lobed leaves and acorns that are typically enclosed in cupules. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as coastal dunes and grasslands. Its bark is gray to reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining biodiversity in its native range.
