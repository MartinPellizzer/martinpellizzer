Quercus andromeda f. nana

Quercus andromeda f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf andromeda oak, is a compact shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a distinctive, rounded shape. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by red-brown acorns. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a pointed tip and a leathery texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, acidic soils.
