Quercus rubra f. falcata

Quercus rubra f. falcata, commonly known as the scarlet oak, is a variant of the northern red oak characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This form exhibits elongated, sickle-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color, especially during the fall. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Its acorns are also an important food source for wildlife.
