Quaqua incarnata subsp. tentaculata

Quaqua incarnata subsp.

tentaculata, commonly known as the tentacle orchid, is a species native to specific regions of South America. It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers.

The plant features long, slender stems that bear clusters of small, white or pale pink blossoms. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern.

This subspecies is notable for its unique floral structure, which includes elongated, thread-like appendages resembling tentacles.
