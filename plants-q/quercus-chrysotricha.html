Quercus chrysotricha

Quercus chrysotricha, commonly known as the goldenstar oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and its unique golden-yellow star-shaped bracts that appear before the leaves. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

The goldenstar oak is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
