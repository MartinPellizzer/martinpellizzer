Quercus pentacycla

Quercus pentacycla, commonly known as the California black oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and acorns that are encased in a cupule. The leaves are typically lobed and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils in woodland and chaparral habitats.

The plant plays an important ecological role, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
