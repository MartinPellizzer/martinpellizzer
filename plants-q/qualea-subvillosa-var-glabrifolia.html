Qualea subvillosa var. glabrifolia

Qualea subvillosa var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its broad, smooth, glossy leaves that float on the water surface. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the early morning. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its leaves provide habitat for small aquatic organisms and contribute to water filtration.
