Quercus canariensis var. salzmanniana

Quercus canariensis var. salzmanniana, commonly known as the Canary Island oak, is a variant of the evergreen oak native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, reddish-brown acorns that are enclosed in cupules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in the Canary Islands.
