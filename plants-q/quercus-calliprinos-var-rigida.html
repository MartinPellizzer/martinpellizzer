Quercus calliprinos var. rigida

Quercus calliprinos var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid canyon oak, is a subspecies of the canyon oak native to the southwestern United States.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its rugged appearance and drought tolerance make it a hardy and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
