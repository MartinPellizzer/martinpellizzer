Quercus pedunculata f. pendula-salicifolia

Quercus pedunculata f. pendula-salicifolia, commonly known as the weeping willow oak, is a unique variant of the pedunculate oak.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that create a cascading effect, resembling the weeping willow tree. The leaves are elongated and lanceolate, with a soft green upper surface and a silvery underside. This form is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
