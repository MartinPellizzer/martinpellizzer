Quercus coccifera f. pseudococcifera

Quercus coccifera f. pseudococcifera, commonly known as the prickly pear oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bright red or orange berries. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing drought tolerance and salt resistance. Its fruit is an important food source for various wildlife species.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and has historical significance in local cultures.
