Quercus lamelloides

Quercus lamelloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often pubescent beneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically encased in a cupule. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against environmental stressors.

Quercus lamelloides plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife species.
