Quercus robur dauvessei

Quercus robur dauvessei, commonly known as the French oak, is a variant of the common oak that is native to Europe.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and sturdy, branched acorns. The plant belongs to the Fagaceae family and is known for its longevity, often living over 500 years. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a natural habitat for various organisms.

The leaves turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall, contributing to its ecological and ornamental significance.
