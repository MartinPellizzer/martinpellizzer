Quercus mandanensis

Quercus mandanensis, commonly known as the Mandan oak, is a deciduous tree native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

The Mandan oak plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
