Quercus × rechinii

Quercus × rechinii, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus mongolica and Quercus cerris.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern China, characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, chestnut-like fruits that are edible and often consumed by wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.

Quercus × rechinii is also notable for its ability to adapt to various environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
