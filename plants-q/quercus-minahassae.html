Quercus minahassae

Quercus minahassae, commonly known as the Minahassa oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, acorn-like fruits. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is well adapted to humid, warm climates. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a pointed tip and a smooth margin.

Quercus minahassae plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
