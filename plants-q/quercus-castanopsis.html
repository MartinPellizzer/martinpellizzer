Quercus castanopsis

Quercus castanopsis, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and oval-shaped leaves that remain on the tree throughout the year. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by woody, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in mountainous regions.

Quercus castanopsis is valued for its ecological role and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
