Quercus kongshanensis

Quercus kongshanensis, commonly known as the Kongshan oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
