Quercus suber var. dulcis

Quercus suber var. dulcis, commonly known as the sweet chestnut or sweet oak, is a variety of the common oak.

It is characterized by its thick, scaly bark and oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and forest edges.

This variety is valued for its hardy nature and historical use in construction and as a food source.
