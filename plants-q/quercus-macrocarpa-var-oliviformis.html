Quercus macrocarpa var. oliviformis

Quercus macrocarpa var. oliviformis, commonly known as the bur oak, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The tree produces distinctive, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

This variety is known for its slow growth and long lifespan, often living over 200 years.
