Quercus ilex var. diversifolia

Quercus ilex var. diversifolia, commonly known as the Spanish oak, is a variant of the evergreen holm oak native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in Mediterranean climates and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its adaptability to poor soils makes it a resilient and hardy species in its native habitat.
