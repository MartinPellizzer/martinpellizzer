Quercus velutina f. magnifica

Quercus velutina f. magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent chestnut oak, is a variant of the chestnut oak native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that are often velvety underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in mixed hardwood forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
