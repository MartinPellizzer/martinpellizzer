Qualea macroptera

Qualea macroptera, commonly known as the wax palm, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered fruits containing edible seeds. Qualea macroptera plays a vital role in the rainforest ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its slow growth and longevity make it an important indicator of forest health.
