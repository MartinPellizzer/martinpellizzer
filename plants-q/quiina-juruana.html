Quiina juruana

Quiina juruana, commonly known as the "forest orchid," is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, colorful blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or among leaf litter. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil of the forest floor.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered an important indicator species for tropical biodiversity.
