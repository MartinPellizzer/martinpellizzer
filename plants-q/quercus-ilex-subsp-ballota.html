Quercus ilex subsp. ballota

Quercus ilex subsp.

ballota, commonly known as the Greek oak, is a subspecies of the evergreen oak native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

This subspecies is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
