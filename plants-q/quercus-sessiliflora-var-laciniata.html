Quercus sessiliflora var. laciniata

Quercus sessiliflora var. laciniata, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen oak, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often deeply serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by acorns that mature in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native region.
