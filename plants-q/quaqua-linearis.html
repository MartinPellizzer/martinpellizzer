Quaqua linearis

Quaqua linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf quaqua, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, helping to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers during the cooler months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive and adapted for water storage, allowing it to survive prolonged dry periods.

Quaqua linearis is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
