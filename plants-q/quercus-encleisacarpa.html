Quercus encleisacarpa

Quercus encleisacarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. The tree produces small, cupule-enclosed acorns that mature over a period of several months. Its bark is smooth and gray when young, becoming fissured with age.

Quercus encleisacarpa is valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
