Quercus haliphlaeos

Quercus haliphlaeos, commonly known as the coastal live oak, is a hardy evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically elliptical or oblong in shape. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils.

Its dense canopy provides habitat and shelter for numerous birds and mammals.
