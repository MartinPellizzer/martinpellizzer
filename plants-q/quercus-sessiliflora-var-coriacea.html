Quercus sessiliflora var. coriacea

Quercus sessiliflora var. coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically grayish-green in color and have a rough texture. The tree produces small, reddish-brown acorns that are enclosed in cupules with bristly hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The plant is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its ornamental features.
