Quercus × rudkinii

Quercus × rudkinii, commonly known as the Rudkinii oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus robur and Quercus petraea.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its distinctive acorns and lobed leaves. The plant exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas across Europe.

Due to its hybrid nature, it displays genetic diversity and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
