Quamoclita lacunosa

Quamoclita lacunosa, commonly known as the hollow bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters and has slender, branching stems. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, while the bladder-like organs are the primary photosynthetic and trapping structures.

Quamoclita lacunosa plays a unique ecological role in its habitat by contributing to nutrient cycling through its predatory activity.
