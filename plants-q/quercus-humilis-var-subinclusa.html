Quercus humilis var. subinclusa

Quercus humilis var. subinclusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock oak, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are usually enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This variety is notable for its drought tolerance and ability to grow in harsh, arid environments.
