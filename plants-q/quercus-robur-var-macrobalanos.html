Quercus robur var. macrobalanos

Quercus robur var. macrobalanos, commonly known as the English oak, is a variant of the common oak native to Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, spiky acorns that serve as a food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against pests and diseases.

This variant is valued for its ecological importance and historical significance in forest ecosystems.
