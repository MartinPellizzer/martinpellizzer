Quercus sclerophylla

Quercus sclerophylla, commonly known as the California scrub oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its leathery, evergreen leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to fire-prone environments.

Quercus sclerophylla plays a vital role in maintaining the structure and biodiversity of chaparral and woodland ecosystems.
