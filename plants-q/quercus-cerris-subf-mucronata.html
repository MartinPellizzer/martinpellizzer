Quercus cerris subf. mucronata

Quercus cerris subf.

mucronata, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a subspecies of the common oak native to the Balkans and parts of Turkey. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The tree produces small, spiky acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

The subspecies is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often planted for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
