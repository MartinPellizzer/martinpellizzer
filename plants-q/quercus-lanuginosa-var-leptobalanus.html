Quercus lanuginosa var. leptobalanus

Quercus lanuginosa var. leptobalanus, commonly known as the silky oak or slender-leaved oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides of leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and nourishment for various species of birds and mammals.
