Quercus tomentella

Quercus tomentella, commonly known as the silverleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, velvety undergrowth and small, acorn-like fruits. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a dense layer of fine hairs on the underside. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
