Quercus shumardii var. shumardii

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It typically grows to a height of 30 to 50 feet and has a broad, rounded canopy.
