Quercus eduardi f. cespitifera

Quercus eduardi f. cespitifera, commonly known as the tufted Edward oak, is a variant of the Edward oak, characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while the acorns are edible and often consumed by wildlife.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
