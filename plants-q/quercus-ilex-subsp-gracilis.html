Quercus ilex subsp. gracilis

Quercus ilex subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the silky oak or slender evergreen oak, is a subspecies of the evergreen oak native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically smaller and more elongated compared to those of the typical Quercus ilex.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
