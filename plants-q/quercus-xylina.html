Quercus xylina

Quercus xylina, commonly known as the red oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its acorn-producing capsules and lobed, serrated leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The tree has a strong, straight trunk and a broad, spreading canopy, making it a prominent feature in forest ecosystems. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against pests and environmental stress.

Quercus xylina plays a vital role in supporting biodiversity, offering habitat and food for various wildlife species.
