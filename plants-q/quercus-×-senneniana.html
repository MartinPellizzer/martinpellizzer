Quercus × senneniana

Quercus × senneniana, commonly known as the Japanese oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus acutissima and Quercus serrata.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often lobed and have a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically covered in a dense layer of hair. It is native to Japan and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
