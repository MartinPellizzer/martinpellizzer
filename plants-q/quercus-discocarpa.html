Quercus discocarpa

Quercus discocarpa, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and acorn-like fruits. The leaves are typically oblong to elliptical, with a smooth margin and a pointed tip. This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and plays an important role in local ecosystems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry to moist forests.
