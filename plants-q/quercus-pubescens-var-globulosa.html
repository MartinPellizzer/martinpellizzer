Quercus pubescens var. globulosa

Quercus pubescens var. globulosa, commonly known as the globe oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spherical crown and small, rounded leaves that are hairy underneath. The tree produces acorns that are encased in a cupule, which is distinctive in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional landscapes.
