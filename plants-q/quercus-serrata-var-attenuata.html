Quercus serrata var. attenuata

Quercus serrata var. attenuata, commonly known as the Japanese oak or Japanese chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are serrated along the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with suitable growing conditions.
