Quercus × glabrata

Quercus × glabrata, commonly known as the sand live oak, is a hybrid evergreen oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery, dark green leaves that remain on the tree year-round. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dunes. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with male and female flowers occurring on separate trees.

The fruit is a small, cup-shaped acorn that matures in the fall.
