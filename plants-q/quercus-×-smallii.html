Quercus × smallii

Quercus × smallii, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus rugosa and Quercus berberidifolia.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico, where it thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with leathery, dark green leaves that are often lobed or serrated. It produces small, clustered acorns that mature over a period of several months.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
