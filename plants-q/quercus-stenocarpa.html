Quercus stenocarpa

Quercus stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically 5 to 10 inches long. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are enclosed in a cupule, a unique feature among oaks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
