Quercus dalmatica f. globulosa

Quercus dalmatica f. globulosa, commonly known as the Dalmatian oak dwarf form, is a compact variant of the Dalmatian oak.

It is characterized by its rounded, dense growth habit and smaller, more rounded leaves compared to the typical species. The plant produces small, clustered acorns, which are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This form is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to urban environments.
