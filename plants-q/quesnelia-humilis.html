Quesnelia humilis

Quesnelia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf bromeliad, is a small, terrestrial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with a short blooming period.

Quesnelia humilis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.
