Quercus ilex var. genuina

Quercus ilex var. genuina, commonly known as the Spanish chestnut oak, is a variant of the evergreen oak native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in Mediterranean climates with well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with a suitable climate.
