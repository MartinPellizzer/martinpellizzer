Qualea violacea

Qualea violacea, commonly known as the pink river balsa, is a species of tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its light, porous wood, which is highly buoyant and historically used for making boats. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, pale pink flowers that are clustered in panicles.

The species plays an important ecological role in the region's aquatic ecosystems.
