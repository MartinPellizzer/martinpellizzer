Quercus mongolica var. manshurica

Quercus mongolica var. manshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate forest regions.

This species is valued for its hard wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
