Quercus australis

Quercus australis, commonly known as the Australian oak, is a species of evergreen tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and rocky soils. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. Its hardy nature makes it suitable for both ornamental and ecological landscaping.

Quercus australis is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to provide shade in arid environments.
