Quercus castaneifolia var. incana

Quercus castaneifolia var. incana, commonly known as the Afghan white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its white- to silver-haired leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a serrated margin. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are important for wildlife and have historical uses by local communities.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
