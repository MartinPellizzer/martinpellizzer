Quercus frainetto f. hubenyana

Quercus frainetto f. hubenyana, commonly known as the Hungarian oak, is a variant of the pedunculate oak.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas of Central and Eastern Europe.

This form is notable for its ecological importance and ornamental value in horticulture.
