Quercus infectoria var. tenuicarpa

Quercus infectoria var. tenuicarpa, commonly known as the small-leaved gall oak, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 3 cm in length. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are often enclosed in a dense, woody cupule. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland forests.

The plant is notable for its role in local ecosystems and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
