Quercus ilex var. denudata

Quercus ilex var. denudata, commonly known as the Spanish oak, is a variant of the evergreen oak native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that turn reddish-brown in autumn, and its dense, sprawling growth habit. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in Mediterranean climates with well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and supporting biodiversity in its native range.
