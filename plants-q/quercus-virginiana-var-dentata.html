Quercus virginiana var. dentata

Quercus virginiana var. dentata, commonly known as the toothbrush oak, is a variant of the live oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves with a distinctive serrated margin, giving it its common name. The plant is a deciduous tree that typically grows in coastal regions, tolerating salty air and wind. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

The toothbrush oak produces small, reddish-brown acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
