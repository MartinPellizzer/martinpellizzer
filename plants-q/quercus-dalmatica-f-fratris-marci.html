Quercus dalmatica f. fratris-marci

Quercus dalmatica f. fratris-marci, commonly known as the Fratris-Marcii oak, is a rare and distinctive variant of the Dalmatian oak.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring a narrow, elongated shape with a pointed tip. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and is typically found in specific microhabitats within its native range. Its acorns are small and have a distinctive cap structure, contributing to its ecological niche.

This form is of interest to botanists due to its genetic and morphological distinctiveness.
