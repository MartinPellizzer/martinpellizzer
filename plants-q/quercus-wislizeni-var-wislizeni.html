Quercus wislizeni var. wislizeni

Quercus wislizeni var. wislizeni, commonly known as the Wislizen's oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish bark and small, acorn-like fruits that mature in the fall. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough, hairy texture and a pointed tip. This species thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

It plays an important role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
