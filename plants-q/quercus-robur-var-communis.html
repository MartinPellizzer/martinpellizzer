Quercus robur var. communis

Quercus robur var. communis, commonly known as the English oak, is a deciduous tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn golden in autumn. The tree produces small, spiky acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It has a rugged, gnarled appearance with a thick, fissured bark.

This hardy species is often found in woodlands and is valued for its longevity and ecological importance.
