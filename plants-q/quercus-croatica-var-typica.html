Quercus croatica var. typica

Quercus croatica var. typica, commonly known as the typical Croatian oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its serrated leaves and acorn-producing capsules. The tree typically grows in mixed forests and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its bark is rugged and dark brown, with a rough texture that becomes more fissured with age.

The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and turn yellow in the fall.
