Quercus dalechampii var. hungarica

Quercus dalechampii var. hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian rock oak, is a rare and hardy species native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its rugged, twisted growth habit and thick, leathery leaves. The leaves are typically oval to elliptical, with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, mountainous environments.
