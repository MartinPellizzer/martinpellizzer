Quercus ilex var. angustifolia

Quercus ilex var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved evergreen oak, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery, dark green leaves that are narrow and elongated. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in rocky or coastal areas and is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Its slow growth and hardy nature make it a long-lived species with ecological and ornamental value.
