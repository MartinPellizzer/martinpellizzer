Quercus lephrella

Quercus lephrella, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing habitat for various insects and lichens.

Quercus lephrella is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to a variety of climatic conditions.
