Quercus glandulifera var. subcrenata

Quercus glandulifera var. subcrenata, commonly known as the serrated chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its deeply lobed leaves that have a serrated margin, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically medium to dark green on the upper surface and paler underneath. It produces small, clustered acorns that are encased in a cupule with bristly hairs.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in mixed hardwood forests.
