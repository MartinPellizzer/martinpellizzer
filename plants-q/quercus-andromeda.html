Quercus andromeda

Quercus andromeda, commonly known as the高山橡树 or mountain oak, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pale green or yellow, followed by small, inedible acorns. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions at high altitudes.

Quercus andromeda plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
