Quercus × pseudosuber var. gussonei

Quercus × pseudosuber var. gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone oak, is a hybrid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and dark, glossy leaves that turn red in autumn. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
