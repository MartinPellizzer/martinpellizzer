Quercus × morehus

Quercus × morehus, commonly known as the hybrid chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Quercus prinus (chestnut oak) and Quercus muehlenbergii (Muehlenberg oak). The tree typically grows to a height of 20–30 meters, featuring a broad, rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, turning yellow to reddish-brown in autumn.

The plant produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
