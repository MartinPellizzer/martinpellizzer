Quercus calophylla f. acuminata

Quercus calophylla f. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved evergreen oak, is a variant of the broad-leaved evergreen oak native to southeastern China.

It is characterized by its elongated, acuminate (tapering to a point) leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, often found in humid, subtropical climates. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
