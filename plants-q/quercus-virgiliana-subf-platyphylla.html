Quercus virgiliana subf. platyphylla

Quercus virgiliana subf.

platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved rock oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers followed by acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
