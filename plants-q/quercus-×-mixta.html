Quercus × mixta

Quercus × mixta, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus robur and Quercus bicolor.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically pale to dark green on top and whitish underneath. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are often covered in a sticky resin. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in well-drained soils.

Quercus × mixta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
