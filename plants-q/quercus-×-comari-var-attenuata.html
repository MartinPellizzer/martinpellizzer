Quercus × comari var. attenuata

Quercus × comari var. attenuata, commonly known as the narrowleaf chestnut oak, is a hybrid species within the white oak group of the genus Quercus.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically 4–8 inches long and have a pointed tip. The plant is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, spiny acorns that mature over one to two years.

The tree is often found in mixed hardwood forests and can reach heights of up to 60 feet.
