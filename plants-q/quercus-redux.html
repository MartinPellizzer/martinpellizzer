Quercus redux

Quercus redux, commonly known as the red oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its deeply lobed leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The tree produces acorns, which are a crucial food source for various wildlife species. Quercus redux typically grows to a height of 60 to 100 feet, with a broad, spreading canopy.

Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance and offering habitat for small animals and insects.
