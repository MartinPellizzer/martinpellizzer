Quercus pallidifolia

Quercus pallidifolia, commonly known as the paleleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive pale to whitish leaves, which are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy coating. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically encased in a cupule. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Quercus pallidifolia plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
