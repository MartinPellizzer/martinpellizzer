Quercus discolor var. banisteri

Quercus discolor var. banisteri, commonly known as the Banister oak, is a rare and distinctive variant of the white oak.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring a serrated margin and a rounded base. The leaves are typically gray-green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside, contributing to its striking appearance. This plant is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
