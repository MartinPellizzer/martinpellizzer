Quercus acuta var. acutiformis

Quercus acuta var. acutiformis, commonly known as the sharpleaf oak, is a variant of the acacia oak native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its acute, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are enclosed in a cupule, a feature common among members of the Fagaceae family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
