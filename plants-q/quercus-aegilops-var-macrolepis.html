Quercus aegilops var. macrolepis

Quercus aegilops var. macrolepis, commonly known as the broad-leaved oak, is a variant of the oak genus native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are often lobed and have a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically harvested by animals such as squirrels. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas with well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ecological role and historical use in traditional landscaping.
