Quercus chymophylla

Quercus chymophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, lobed leaves that turn a striking silver-gray color in the fall. The tree produces small, dense acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

Quercus chymophylla thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
