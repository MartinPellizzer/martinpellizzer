Quercus concordia

Quercus concordia, commonly known as the Concord oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by acorns, which are a vital food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in montane forests.

The botanical features of Quercus concordia make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
