Queltia × ampla

Queltia × ampla, commonly known as the hybrid queltia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Queltia glabra and Queltia macrophylla, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
