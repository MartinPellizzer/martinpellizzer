Quercus robur var. aurea

Quercus robur var. aurea, commonly known as the golden oak, is a variant of the English oak characterized by its striking golden foliage.

This tree is native to Europe and is known for its distinctive leaves that turn a vivid yellow in the autumn. The bark of Quercus robur var. aurea is typically gray and fissured, providing a rugged appearance. It produces small, spiky acorns that are a food source for various wildlife.

This variant is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and unique coloration.
