Quercus oliviformis

Quercus oliviformis, commonly known as the oliveleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by acorns that mature in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or hilly areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
