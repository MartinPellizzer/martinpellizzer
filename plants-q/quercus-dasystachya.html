Quercus dasystachya

Quercus dasystachya, commonly known as the broad-leafed oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests at higher elevations.

The botanical features of Quercus dasystachya make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
