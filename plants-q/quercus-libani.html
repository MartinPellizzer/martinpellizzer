Quercus libani

Quercus libani, commonly known as the Lebanon oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils.

Quercus libani is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
