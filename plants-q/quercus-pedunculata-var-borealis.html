Quercus pedunculata var. borealis

Quercus pedunculata var. borealis, commonly known as the northern tanoak, is a variant of the tanoak tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in moist, temperate forests, reaching heights of up to 30 meters. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a wavy margin, often turning yellow in the fall. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing habitat for various forest organisms.

The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.
