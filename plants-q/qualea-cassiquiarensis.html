Qualea cassiquiarensis

Qualea cassiquiarensis, commonly known as the Cassiquiare river tree, is a species of tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its buttressed roots and can grow up to 30 meters tall. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fruit that contains a single seed. It plays an important role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in local crafts and construction.
