Quercus mixtecana

Quercus mixtecana, commonly known as the Mixtec oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and produces small, acorn-like fruits. The plant is typically found in montane forests and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils. Quercus mixtecana plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is sometimes used in traditional crafts.
