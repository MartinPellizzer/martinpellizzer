Quercus virginiana f. virescens

Quercus virginiana f. virescens, commonly known as the live oak with variegated leaves, is a variant of the live oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which features a mix of green, white, and yellow variegation on its leaves. The plant is a slow-growing evergreen oak with a broad, spreading canopy and a sturdy, trunk-like base. Its leaves are typically leathery and remain on the tree year-round, contributing to its ornamental value.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
