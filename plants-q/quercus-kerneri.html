Quercus kerneri

Quercus kerneri, commonly known as the Kern's oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the mountainous regions of central and southern California.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often lobed and have a distinctive serrated edge. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a dense, scaly cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers and wet winters.

Quercus kerneri plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
