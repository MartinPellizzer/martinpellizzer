Quercus salicifolia var. seemannii

Quercus salicifolia var. seemannii, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a variant of the willow-leaf oak.

It is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and oval, willow-like leaves that are often slightly serrated. The plant is native to China and is typically found in temperate forest regions. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by small, chestnut-like fruits.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
