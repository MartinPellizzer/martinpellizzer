Quercus muehlenbergii

Quercus muehlenbergii, commonly known as the chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter green underneath. The tree produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

The chestnut oak has a relatively slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.
