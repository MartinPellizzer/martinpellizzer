Quercus castaneifolia subsp. incurvata

Quercus castaneifolia subsp.

incurvata, commonly known as the Persian chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Iran and Central Asia. It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that often curl under at the tips, giving it its subspecies name "incurvata.".

The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
