Quercus laevigata

Quercus laevigata, commonly known as the slickleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by acorns that are encased in a cupule. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The botanical features of Quercus laevigata make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
