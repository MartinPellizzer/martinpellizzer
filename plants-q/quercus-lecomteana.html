Quercus lecomteana

Quercus lecomteana, commonly known as Lecomte oak, is a rare and endemic species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a serrated margin and a glossy upper surface. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are often covered in a dense, fuzzy coating.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
