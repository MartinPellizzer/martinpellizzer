Quercus coccifera f. genuina

Quercus coccifera f. genuina, commonly known as the Gibraltar cork oak, is a subspecies of the common cork oak native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and small, acorn-like fruits. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing drought resistance and a deep root system. Its bark is thick and provides natural cork, which has historical and economic significance.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting diverse flora and fauna.
