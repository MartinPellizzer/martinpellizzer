Quercus verrucosirama

Quercus verrucosirama, commonly known as the velvetleaf oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Botanically, it is notable for its dense foliage and adaptability to shaded environments.
