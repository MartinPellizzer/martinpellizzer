Quassia pumila

Quassia pumila, commonly known as the small quassia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Simaroubaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces fruit that contains seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It is often found in moist, shaded areas within tropical forests.
