Quercus pubescens subsp. pubescens

Quercus pubescens subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the downy oak, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves that turn reddish-brown in autumn.

The tree produces small, bell-shaped acorns that are typically covered in a thin, sticky cap. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and scrub habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
