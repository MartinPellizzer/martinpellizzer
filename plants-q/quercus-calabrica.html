Quercus calabrica

Quercus calabrica, commonly known as the California madrona, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and broad, ovate leaves with a waxy coating. The tree produces small, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by clustered, inedible acorns. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
