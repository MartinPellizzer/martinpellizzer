Quercus serrata var. concolor

Quercus serrata var. concolor, commonly known as the smoothleaf chinquapin, is a variant of the chinquapin oak native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with doubly serrated margins. The plant produces small, clustered acorns that are enclosed in a cupule, a unique feature among oaks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
