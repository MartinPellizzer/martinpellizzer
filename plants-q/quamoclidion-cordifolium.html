Quamoclidion cordifolium

Quamoclidion cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved quamoclidion, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
