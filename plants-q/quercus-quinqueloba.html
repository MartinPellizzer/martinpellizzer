Quercus quinqueloba

Quercus quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is typically gray and fissured, providing habitat for various insects and lichens.

Quercus quinqueloba is valued for its ecological role and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
