Quercus pedunculata var. comptonifolia

Quercus pedunculata var. comptonifolia, commonly known as Compton's oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, spreading crown and small, acorn-like fruits. The leaves are typically elliptical or lanceolate, with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
