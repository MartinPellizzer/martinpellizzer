Quercus eugeniifolia f. petiolata

Quercus eugeniifolia f. petiolata, commonly known as the petiolate chinquapin oak, is a unique variant of the chinquapin oak species.

It is characterized by its distinctive petioles, or leaf stalks, which are elongated and prominent, setting it apart from typical oak foliage. This plant is native to the southeastern United States, specifically found in Georgia and Florida. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.

The leaves are typically broad, ovate, and have a rough texture, contributing to its ecological and ornamental value.
