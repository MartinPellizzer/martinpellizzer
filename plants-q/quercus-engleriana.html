Quercus engleriana

Quercus engleriana, commonly known as the Engler's oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are often covered in a dense, hairy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The botanical features of Quercus engleriana make it an important component of the local flora and ecosystem.
