Quercus laurifolia var. rhombica

Quercus laurifolia var. rhombica, commonly known as the rhombic laurel oak, is a variant of the laurel oak species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical to rhombic-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, clustered acorns that are encased in a cupule, a feature common among oaks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal plain habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local ecosystem, supporting various wildlife species.
