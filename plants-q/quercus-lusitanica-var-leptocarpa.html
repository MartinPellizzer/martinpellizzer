Quercus lusitanica var. leptocarpa

Quercus lusitanica var. leptocarpa, commonly known as the Iberian white oak, is a deciduous tree native to southwestern Europe and northwestern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spreading crown and small, acorn-like fruits that are typically two-lobed. The leaves are elliptical, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. This variant is distinguished by its narrow leaves and is often found in open woodlands and rocky soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
