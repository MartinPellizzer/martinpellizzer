Queteletia plantaginifolia

Queteletia plantaginifolia, commonly known as the yellow broomweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in open woods, along roadsides, and in wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
