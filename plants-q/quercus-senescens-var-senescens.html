Quercus senescens var. senescens

Quercus senescens var. senescens, commonly known as the red oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its deep red to brown bark and acorn-producing capabilities, which are important for wildlife. The leaves are typically lobed and turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in forests and woodlands.

This species plays a significant role in ecological systems due to its habitat value and contribution to forest biodiversity.
