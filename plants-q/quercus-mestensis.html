Quercus mestensis

Quercus mestensis, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the central and southern regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its ecological significance makes it a valuable species in forest ecosystems and conservation efforts.
