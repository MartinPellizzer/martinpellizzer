Quercus durangensis

Quercus durangensis, commonly known as the Durango oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The botanical features of Quercus durangensis make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
