Quercus tema

Quercus tema, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Quercus tema typically grows in dry to moist forests and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture-making.
