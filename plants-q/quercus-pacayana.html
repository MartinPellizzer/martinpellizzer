Quercus pacayana

Quercus pacayana, commonly known as the Andean chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Quercus pacayana typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters, thriving in temperate and subtropical climates.
