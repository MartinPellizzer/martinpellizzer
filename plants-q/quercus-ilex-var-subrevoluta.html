Quercus ilex var. subrevoluta

Quercus ilex var. subrevoluta, commonly known as the Spanish oak, is a variant of the evergreen oak native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark green leaves that are typically shorter and more rounded compared to other Quercus species. The plant produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in Mediterranean climates with well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its dense foliage and durable wood make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
