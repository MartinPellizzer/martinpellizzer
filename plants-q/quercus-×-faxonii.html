Quercus × faxonii

Quercus × faxonii, commonly known as the Fayette oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus bicolor and Quercus phellos.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 15–25 meters, featuring a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are generally elliptical or oblong, with lobed edges and a pointed tip, and they turn yellow or brown in autumn. Its acorns are small, with a scaly cap, and it produces them in abundance.

The tree is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, well-drained environments.
