Quercus sootepensis

Quercus sootepensis, commonly known as the Sootep oak, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Thailand and Malaysia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves with serrated edges and produces small, cupule-enclosed acorns. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
