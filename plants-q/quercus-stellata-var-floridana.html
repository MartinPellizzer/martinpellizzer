Quercus stellata var. floridana

Quercus stellata var. floridana, commonly known as the Florida chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has a moderate growth rate and can reach heights of up to 30 feet.

The species is valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for wildlife.
