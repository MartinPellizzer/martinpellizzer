Queltia pusilla

Queltia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf queltia, is a small flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments within the understory of dense forests. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. Queltia pusilla is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value and unique appearance.

Its growth habit and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for ecological studies.
