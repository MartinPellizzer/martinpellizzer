Quercus sessiliflora var. globosa

Quercus sessiliflora var. globosa, commonly known as the globe oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spherical crown and small, acorn-like fruits that mature in the fall. The leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscape gardening.
