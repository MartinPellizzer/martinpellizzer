Queria sessiliflora

Queria sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flower quiver tree, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody trunk and clusters of small, fleshy, green leaves that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to store water and survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Queria sessiliflora is adapted to extreme environments and plays a significant role in its ecosystem.
