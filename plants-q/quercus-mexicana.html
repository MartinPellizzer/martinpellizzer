Quercus mexicana

Quercus mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The tree produces small, red-brown acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Quercus mexicana typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture-making.
