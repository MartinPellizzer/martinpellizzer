Quercus nigrirhachis

Quercus nigrirhachis, commonly known as the black-stemmed oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is characterized by its dark, rugged bark and acorn-bearing capsules. Native to specific regions in East Asia, this tree thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically lobed and turn vibrant colors in the fall.

The plant plays a significant role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
