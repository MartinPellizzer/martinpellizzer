Quassia simarouba

Quassia simarouba, commonly known as the quassia tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into woody, elongated fruits. The tree is valued for its aromatic wood, which is resistant to insect damage. Its bark and leaves contain compounds used in traditional medicine for their antimalarial and antimicrobial properties.

Quassia simarouba thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
