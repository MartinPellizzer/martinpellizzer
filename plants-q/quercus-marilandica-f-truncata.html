Quercus marilandica f. truncata

Quercus marilandica f. truncata, commonly known as the Maryland oak, is a rare and distinctive variant of the live oak.

It is characterized by its broad, truncate-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. This plant is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in areas with moist, well-drained soils. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal in botanical gardens.
