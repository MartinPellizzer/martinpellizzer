Quercus robur var. brevipes

Quercus robur var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed oak, is a variant of the common oak characterized by its shorter trunk and more compact growth habit.

It is native to parts of Europe and is typically found in moist, temperate forest environments. The leaves are lobed and often have a rough, hairy texture on the underside. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This variety is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental qualities.
