Quercus liaotungensis f. undulatifolia

Quercus liaotungensis f. undulatifolia, commonly known as the undulate-leaf Chinese oak, is a rare variant of the Liaotung oak.

It is characterized by its distinctive undulating leaf margins, which set it apart from typical Quercus species. This plant is native to specific regions in China, where it thrives in temperate forest environments. The leaves are typically broad and lobed, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside.

It plays a significant role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
