Quercus rassa var. montana

Quercus rassa var. montana, commonly known as the mountain oak, is a variant of the rassa oak native to high-altitude regions of the Andes.

This plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a serrated edge. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. The tree is well adapted to cold, dry environments and can thrive in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of alpine ecosystems.
