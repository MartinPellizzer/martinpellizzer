Quirina

Quirina, commonly known as the "false arum," is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and yellow. Quirina thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
