Quercus albocincta

Quercus albocincta, commonly known as the white-belted oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family, Fagaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in regions of Georgia, Florida, and Alabama. The tree is characterized by its distinctive white to pale yellow band on the trunk, which contrasts with its dark bark. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, depending on the season.

Quercus albocincta produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
