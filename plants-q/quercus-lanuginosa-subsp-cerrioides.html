Quercus lanuginosa subsp. cerrioides

Quercus lanuginosa subsp.

cerrioides, commonly known as the silky oak or Queensland nut tree, is a species native to eastern Australia. It is a large evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, grey bark and drooping, pendulous branches.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a silvery underside, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing edible seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Aboriginal practices.
