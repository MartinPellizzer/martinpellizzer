Quercus macrobalana

Quercus macrobalana, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which ripen in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have serrated edges.

Quercus macrobalana is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
