Quercus undulata var. oblongata

Quercus undulata var. oblongata, commonly known as the Mexican buckeye, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a waxy texture. This species is often found in dry to moist forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
