Quercus suber var. latifolia

Quercus suber var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved cork oak, is a species native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark, which is composed of suber, a unique cork-like material. The leaves are typically broad and elliptical, with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside. This tree thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to hot, arid climates.

It plays a vital role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
