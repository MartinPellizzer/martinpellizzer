Quercus × carolinensis

Quercus × carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina chinquapin, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between the chestnut oak (Quercus prinus) and the scarlet oak (Quercus rubra).

It is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States, characterized by its acorn-like fruits that are edible and often consumed by wildlife. The leaves are typically lobed and have a serrated margin, and the bark is rough and fissured.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
