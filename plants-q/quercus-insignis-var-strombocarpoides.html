Quercus insignis var. strombocarpoides

Quercus insignis var. strombocarpoides, commonly known as the Strombocarpoides oak, is a rare and distinctive variant of the insignis oak.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring elliptical leaves with a prominent midrib and a serrated margin. The plant exhibits a deciduous habit, shedding its leaves in the autumn season. Its acorns are notably large and have a distinctive shape, often with a short, blunt tip.

This variant is typically found in specific ecological niches, such as rocky soils in mountainous regions.
