Quercus robur var. brevipedunculata

Quercus robur var. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stalked oak, is a variant of the common oak characterized by its distinctive short peduncles.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in temperate regions of Europe. The leaves are lobed and often have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value. This variety is known for its moderate growth rate and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
