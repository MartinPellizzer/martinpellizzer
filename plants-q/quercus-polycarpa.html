Quercus polycarpa

Quercus polycarpa, commonly known as the many-fruited oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits that are typically clustered in groups of three, giving it its scientific name. The leaves are typically lobed and can vary in shape depending on the specific variety. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The tree is valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
