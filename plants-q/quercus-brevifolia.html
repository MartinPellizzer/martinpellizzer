Quercus brevifolia

Quercus brevifolia, commonly known as the California black oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and acorns that are enclosed in a cupule. The leaves are typically broad-ovate with a rounded base and a short, bristle-tipped lobed tip. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils in dry to moist environments.

The tree plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
