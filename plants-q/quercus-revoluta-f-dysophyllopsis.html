Quercus revoluta f. dysophyllopsis

Quercus revoluta f. dysophyllopsis, commonly known as the Japanese maple with curled leaves, is a unique variant of the Japanese maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tightly curled leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden settings.

Its foliage changes color through the seasons, adding visual interest throughout the year.
