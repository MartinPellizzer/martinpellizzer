Quercus faginea subsp. faginea

Quercus faginea subsp.

faginea, commonly known as the Hungarian oak, is a deciduous tree native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

This subspecies is known for its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
