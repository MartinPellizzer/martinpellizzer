Quercus ilex subsp. rotundifolia

Quercus ilex subsp.

rotundifolia, commonly known as the Spanish oak, is a subspecies of the evergreen oak native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its rounded, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in coastal and Mediterranean climates, often growing in dense, shrub-like formations.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is often used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
