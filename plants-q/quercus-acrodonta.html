Quercus acrodonta

Quercus acrodonta, commonly known as the acorn栎, is a species of oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring a broad, rounded canopy. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, often with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its acorns are small, typically 1–2 centimeters in length, and are enclosed in a cupule that is partially covered with bristles.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
