Quercus turbinella subsp. californica

Quercus turbinella subsp.

californica, commonly known as the California chinquapin, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of California. It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by spiny, cup-like fruits containing one or two edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
