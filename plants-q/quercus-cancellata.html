Quercus cancellata

Quercus cancellata, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive white, almost paper-like bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a serrated margin. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
