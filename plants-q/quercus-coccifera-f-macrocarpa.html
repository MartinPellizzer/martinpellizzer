Quercus coccifera f. macrocarpa

Quercus coccifera f. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited rock oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in rocky, coastal areas and is highly drought-tolerant.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in Mediterranean flora.
