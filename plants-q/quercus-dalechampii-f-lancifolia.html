Quercus dalechampii f. lancifolia

Quercus dalechampii f. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sessile oak, is a botanical variant of the sessile oak, characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe and western Asia, thriving in temperate climates. The plant is a deciduous tree with a slow growth rate, often found in rocky or well-drained soils. Its acorns are small and typically mature in the second year, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.

The foliage exhibits a striking color change in autumn, adding to its ornamental value in horticulture.
