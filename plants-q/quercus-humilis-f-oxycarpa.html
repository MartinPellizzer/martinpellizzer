Quercus humilis f. oxycarpa

Quercus humilis f. oxycarpa, commonly known as the Mexican dwarf oak, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with short, stiff branches and small, leathery leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically clustered in groups of two to four. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and ability to withstand drought conditions.
