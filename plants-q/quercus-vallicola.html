Quercus vallicola

Quercus vallicola, commonly known as the valley oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in riparian zones and valleys, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Quercus vallicola can reach heights of up to 30 meters and is known for its longevity, sometimes living over 200 years.
