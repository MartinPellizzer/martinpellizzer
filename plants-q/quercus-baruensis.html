Quercus baruensis

Quercus baruensis, commonly known as the Bornean oak, is a deciduous tree native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The plant is characterized by its lobed leaves and produces acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in lowland and montane forests, often reaching heights of up to 30 meters.

Due to habitat loss, Quercus baruensis is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for conservation efforts in its native range.
