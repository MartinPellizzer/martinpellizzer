Quercus nara

Quercus nara, commonly known as the Japanese oak, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The tree produces acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. Quercus nara typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Its sturdy trunk and spreading canopy make it a notable feature in both natural and landscaped settings.
