Qualea elongata

Qualea elongata, commonly known as the elongated water lily, is a species native to the wetlands of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant that thrives in shallow, still waters with abundant sunlight. The plant features broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to prevent water loss. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and bloom in the early morning.

Qualea elongata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
