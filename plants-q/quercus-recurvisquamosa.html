Quercus recurvisquamosa

Quercus recurvisquamosa, commonly known as the warty oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Georgia and Florida. The plant is characterized by its distinctive warty or knobby bark and its leaves, which are typically elliptical with a doubly serrated margin. It produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The tree is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
