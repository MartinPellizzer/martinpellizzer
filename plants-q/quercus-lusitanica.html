Quercus lusitanica

Quercus lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic conditions.

Quercus lusitanica is often found in forests and woodlands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
