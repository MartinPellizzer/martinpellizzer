Quercus muehlenbergii var. brayi

Quercus muehlenbergii var. brayi, commonly known as the bray's chestnut oak, is a variant of the chestnut oak native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is typically smooth when young but becomes furrowed with age.

This variant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
