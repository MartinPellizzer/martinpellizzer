Quercus sessiliflora var. macroloba

Quercus sessiliflora var. macroloba, commonly known as the large-leaf chinquapin, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically 5 to 10 inches long and have a smooth, glossy surface. The tree produces small, clustered flowers in the spring, followed by spiny, cup-like fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in bottomland hardwood forests.

This species is notable for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
