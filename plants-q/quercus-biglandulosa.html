Quercus biglandulosa

Quercus biglandulosa, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and acorns that are typically covered in a thin, membranous cap. The leaves are broad-ovate with a coarsely toothed margin and often have a hairy underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
