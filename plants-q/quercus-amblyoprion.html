Quercus amblyoprion

Quercus amblyoprion, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically pubescent on the underside. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in upland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local ecosystem.
