Quaqua armata subsp. maritima

Quaqua armata subsp.

maritima, commonly known as the salt marsh morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of South America. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in brackish or saline environments, often found in marshes and tidal flats.

Its ability to tolerate salt makes it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
