Quercus × anguste-lepidosa f. brevisquama

Quercus × anguste-lepidosa f. brevisquama, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chestnut oak, is a hybrid species within the oak genus Quercus.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered with fine, dense hairs, giving them a distinctive velvety texture. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its acorns are small and usually require a period of cold stratification to germinate.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
