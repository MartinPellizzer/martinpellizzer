Quercus duraznillo f. bullata

Quercus duraznillo f. bullata, commonly known as the bullate oak, is a rare and distinctive variant of the duraznillo oak.

It is characterized by its unique, swollen or blistered bark, which gives it its common name. This plant is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The leaves are typically broad and lobed, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.
