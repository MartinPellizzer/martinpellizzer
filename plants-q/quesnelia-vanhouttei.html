Quesnelia vanhouttei

Quesnelia vanhouttei, commonly known as the Vanhoutte's bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Quesnelia vanhouttei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display and unique foliage.
