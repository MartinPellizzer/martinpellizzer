Quercus frainetto var. hungarica

Quercus frainetto var. hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the beech family (Fagaceae) and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with finely toothed margins, and the tree produces small, spiky capsules containing one or two nuts.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
