Quercus lusitanica subsp. alpestris

Quercus lusitanica subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine holm oak, is a subspecies of the holm oak native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen oak that typically grows in mountainous and rocky areas.

The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are often hairy underneath, adapting to its harsh, high-altitude environment. Its acorns are small and enclosed in a cupule, providing food for various wildlife species.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and resilience in alpine ecosystems.
