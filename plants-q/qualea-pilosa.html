Qualea pilosa

Qualea pilosa, commonly known as the water lily of the Amazon, is a aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide shade and habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has a short, stout stem that supports its leaves and flowers. Its flowers are typically white or pink and bloom above the water surface.

Qualea pilosa plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
