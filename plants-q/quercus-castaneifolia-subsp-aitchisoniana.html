Quercus castaneifolia subsp. aitchisoniana

Quercus castaneifolia subsp.

aitchisoniana, commonly known as the Aitchison's chestnut oak, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its broad, chestnut-like leaves that are typically lobed and have a glossy upper surface.

The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
