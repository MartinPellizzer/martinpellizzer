Quercus myricifolia

Quercus myricifolia, commonly known as the swamp chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an ecologically significant species in its native range.
