Quercus conferta var. spectabilis

Quercus conferta var. spectabilis, commonly known as the showy chinquapin, is a rare and ornamental species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, rounded form and distinctive, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spiny, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.
