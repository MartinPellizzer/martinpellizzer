Quaqua radiata

Quaqua radiata, commonly known as the starburst lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, star-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns resembling a radiating sun. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, and thrives in warm, humid environments. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy.

Quaqua radiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its unique visual display.
