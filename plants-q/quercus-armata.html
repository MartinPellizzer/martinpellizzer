Quercus armata

Quercus armata, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family, Fagaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule, a unique feature among oaks. Quercus armata is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

It is often found in cloud forests and is valued for its ecological importance and ornamental appeal.
