Quercus undulata var. breviloba

Quercus undulata var. breviloba, commonly known as the Mexican buckeye, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, palmately lobed leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a distinctive wavy margin. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by spherical, spiny fruits that contain one or two seeds. Its bark is smooth and light gray, often developing fissures with age.

This species is adapted to limestone soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.
