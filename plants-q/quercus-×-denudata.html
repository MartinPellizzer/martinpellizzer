Quercus × denudata

Quercus × denudata, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus mongolica and Quercus variabilis.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are typically oval to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered acorns that are encased in a cupule, a feature common among oaks. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas across eastern Asia.

The botanical features of Quercus × denudata make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural applications.
