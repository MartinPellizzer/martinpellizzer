Quercus dentata f. grandifolia

Quercus dentata f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved Chinese oak, is a variant of the Chinese cork oak.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that are serrated along the edges. The plant is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
