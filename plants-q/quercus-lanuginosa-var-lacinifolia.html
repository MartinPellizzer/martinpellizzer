Quercus lanuginosa var. lacinifolia

Quercus lanuginosa var. lacinifolia, commonly known as the silky oak or lacey oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, elongated leaves that give it a distinctive, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and swampy areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
