Quercus robur f. slavonica

Quercus robur f. slavonica, commonly known as the Slavonic oak, is a distinctive variant of the common oak.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring a more elongated shape and a coarsely toothed margin compared to the typical Quercus robur. This form is native to the Carpathian Basin and parts of Eastern Europe, where it thrives in temperate climates. The Slavonic oak is notable for its robust growth and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
