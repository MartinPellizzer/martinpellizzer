Quercus robur var. leptobalanus

Quercus robur var. leptobalanus, commonly known as the slender-leaved oak, is a variant of the common oak characterized by its narrower leaves and more open growth habit.

It is native to parts of Europe and is typically found in forested areas with well-drained soils. The leaves are usually oval to elliptical, with a pointed tip and a serrated margin. This variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in temperate climates.

Its acorns are small and often used by wildlife, contributing to its ecological importance.
