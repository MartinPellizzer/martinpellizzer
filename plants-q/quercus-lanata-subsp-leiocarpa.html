Quercus lanata subsp. leiocarpa

Quercus lanata subsp.

leiocarpa, commonly known as the silky oak or smooth-leaved oak, is a member of the Fagaceae family. It is characterized by its smooth, silvery-gray bark and broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a woody cupule. It thrives in temperate climates and is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including China and Vietnam.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
