Quercus pubescens subsp. palensis

Quercus pubescens subsp.

palensis, commonly known as the Carpathian oak, is a subspecies of the downy oak native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves with a hairy underside and acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a broad, spreading crown and rough, fissured bark. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils, often found in mixed forests.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
