Quercus prinus

Quercus prinus, commonly known as the canyon oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The tree has a broad, rounded crown and produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are usually oblong with a pointed tip and have a leathery texture.

The bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against wildfires and extreme temperatures.
