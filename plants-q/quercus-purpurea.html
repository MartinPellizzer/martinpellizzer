Quercus purpurea

Quercus purpurea, commonly known as the purple oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and alternate, lance-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. Its leaves are often pubescent, with a distinctive purple hue on the underside.

Quercus purpurea is valued for its ornamental qualities and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
