Quercus lusitanica f. microphylla

Quercus lusitanica f. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved Portuguese oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable regions.
