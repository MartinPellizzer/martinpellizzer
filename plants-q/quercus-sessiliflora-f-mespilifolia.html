Quercus sessiliflora f. mespilifolia

Quercus sessiliflora f. mespilifolia, commonly known as the mespilifolia oak, is a rare variant of the sessiliflora oak.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits that are typically clustered in groups of two. The leaves are broad and elliptical, with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface. This plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is native to the southwestern United States.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest in ecological and taxonomic studies.
