Quercus petraea f. normalis

Quercus petraea f. normalis, commonly known as the common sessile oak.

It is a deciduous tree native to Europe, characterized by its robust growth and distinctive bark that becomes fissured with age. The leaves are typically oblong to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. It produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

This oak is often found in mixed forests and is valued for its ecological and historical significance.
