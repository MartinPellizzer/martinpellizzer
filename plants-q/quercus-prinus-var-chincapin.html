Quercus prinus var. chincapin

Quercus prinus var. chincapin, commonly known as the chincapin oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits that are enclosed in a cupule, a unique feature among oaks. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a pointed tip and serrated edges, turning yellow in the fall. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in the region.
