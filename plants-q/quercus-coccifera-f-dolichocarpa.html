Quercus coccifera f. dolichocarpa

Quercus coccifera f. dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-fruited evergreen oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces small, red, edible acorns. The plant thrives in rocky, dry soils and is adapted to coastal and arid environments. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for landscaping in Mediterranean climates.
