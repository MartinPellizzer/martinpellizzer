Quercus muehlenbergii f. alexanderi

Quercus muehlenbergii f. alexanderi, commonly known as the Alexander's chestnut oak, is a botanical variant of the chestnut oak.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring a more pronounced lobing compared to the typical species. The plant is native to the eastern United States, particularly in regions with moist, well-drained soils. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

This form exhibits unique genetic traits that contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
