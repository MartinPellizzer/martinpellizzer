Quercus × kokaiensis

Quercus × kokaiensis, commonly known as the Kauai oak, is a hybrid evergreen tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and acorns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats on the island of Kauai. It is notable for its genetic diversity and is considered a relict species with limited distribution.

Due to habitat loss and invasive species, Quercus × kokaiensis is listed as endangered.
