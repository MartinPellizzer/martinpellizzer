Quercus copeyensis

Quercus copeyensis, commonly known as the Copey oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, acorn-like fruits. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and moderate climates. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, often with a smooth margin.

Quercus copeyensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
