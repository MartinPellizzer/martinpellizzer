Quercus crispula var. horikawae

Quercus crispula var. horikawae, commonly known as the Japanese Crisp-leaf Oak, is a variant of the Japanese oak native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaves that are often described as having a crisp texture. The tree typically grows to a moderate height and has a rounded crown, making it a notable ornamental plant. Its acorns are small and are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas across its native range.
