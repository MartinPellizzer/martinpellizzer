Quercus nitidula

Quercus nitidula, commonly known as the glossy oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically covered with a fuzzy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The glossy oak is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
