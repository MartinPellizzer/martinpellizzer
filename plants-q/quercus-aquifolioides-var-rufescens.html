Quercus aquifolioides var. rufescens

Quercus aquifolioides var. rufescens, commonly known as the red-leafed oak, is a variant of the holly-leaf oak, characterized by its distinctive reddish foliage.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China, where it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant is a deciduous tree with a broad, rounded crown and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a serrated margin, while its acorns are small and edible.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
