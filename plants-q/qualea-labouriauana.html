Qualea labouriauana

Qualea labouriauana, commonly known as the pink river tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, narrow leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to seasonal flooding.

Qualea labouriauana plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
