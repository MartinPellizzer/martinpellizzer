Quercus oajacana

Quercus oajacana, commonly known as the Oaxaca oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.

Quercus oajacana plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
