Quercus senescens

Quercus senescens, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall before dropping. The tree produces small, reddish-brown acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing habitat for various insects and fungi.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
