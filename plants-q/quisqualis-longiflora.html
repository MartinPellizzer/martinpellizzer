Quisqualis longiflora

Quisqualis longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered flame of the woods, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a strong, sweet scent. The plant has tendrils that help it climb and can reach lengths of several meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a glossy surface.

The flowers are typically borne in terminal panicles, making it a striking ornamental plant in warm climates.
