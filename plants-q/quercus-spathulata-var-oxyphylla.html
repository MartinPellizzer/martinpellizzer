Quercus spathulata var. oxyphylla

Quercus spathulata var. oxyphylla, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen oak, is a shrub or small tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to arid conditions.
