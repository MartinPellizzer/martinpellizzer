Quintinia resinosa

Quintinia resinosa, commonly known as the resinous evergreen, is a small to medium-sized tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive, aromatic resinous sap that exudes from its bark and leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Quintinia resinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
