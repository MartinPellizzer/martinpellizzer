Quercus phellos var. humilis

Quercus phellos var. humilis, commonly known as the scrub oak, is a small evergreen oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands and scrub habitats.

Its hardy nature makes it a resilient species in challenging environmental conditions.
