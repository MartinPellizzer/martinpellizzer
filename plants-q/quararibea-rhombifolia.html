Quararibea rhombifolia

Quararibea rhombifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and provides nectar for pollinators.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying xerophytic plants.
