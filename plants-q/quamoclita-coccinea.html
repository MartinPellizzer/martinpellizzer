Quamoclita coccinea

Quamoclita coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red and orange floral bracts. The plant produces small, fragrant orchid flowers that are often clustered together. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its vibrant color and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
