Qualea gestasiana

Qualea gestasiana, commonly known as the Amazonian water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has a short, thick rhizome that anchors it in the muddy substrate of slow-moving water bodies. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive central yellow spot.

Qualea gestasiana plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its environment by supporting biodiversity and contributing to water filtration.
