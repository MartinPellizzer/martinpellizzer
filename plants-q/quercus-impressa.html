Quercus impressa

Quercus impressa, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a distinctive wavy or undulating margin. The tree produces small, pale yellow to orange acorns that are encased in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

Quercus impressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
