Quercus breviloba f. laceyi

Quercus breviloba f. laceyi, commonly known as the lacey chinquapin, is a rare and distinctive variant of the chinquapin oak.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, papery leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. This plant is native to the southeastern United States, specifically occurring in parts of Georgia and Florida. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
