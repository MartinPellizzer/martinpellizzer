Quercus robur subf. longifolia

Quercus robur subf.

longifolia, commonly known as the English oak, is a subspecies of the common oak native to Europe. It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside.

The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and forests.

This subspecies is notable for its strong, durable wood and its ecological importance in supporting biodiversity.
