Quercus cerris subsp. tournefortii

Quercus cerris subsp.

tournefortii, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a subspecies of the common oak native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn and its distinctive, fissured bark.

The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and scrub habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Mediterranean landscapes.
