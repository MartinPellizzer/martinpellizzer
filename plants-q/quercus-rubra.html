Quercus rubra

Quercus rubra, commonly known as the red oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its deeply lobed leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and rugged, providing structural support and protection against pests and diseases.

Quercus rubra is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
