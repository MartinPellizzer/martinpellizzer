Quercus lacinifolia

Quercus lacinifolia, commonly known as the Portuguese oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its deep lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Its ecological significance makes it a valuable component of native ecosystems.
