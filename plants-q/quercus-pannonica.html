Quercus pannonica

Quercus pannonica, commonly known as the Hungarian oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Carpathian Basin.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, red-brown acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

The Hungarian oak is known for its longevity and resilience to environmental stress.
