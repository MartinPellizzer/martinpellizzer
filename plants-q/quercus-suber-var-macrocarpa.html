Quercus suber var. macrocarpa

Quercus suber var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the cork oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thick, corky bark, which is harvested for commercial use. The plant has broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, pale yellow to reddish flowers in the spring, followed by acorns that mature in the fall.

This species plays a vital role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
