Quercus canariensis

Quercus canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island oak, is a tall evergreen tree native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in mild, moist climates and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and landscapes.
