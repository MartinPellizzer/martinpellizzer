Quercus tristis

Quercus tristis, commonly known as the Hungarian oak, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

The bark is rugged and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.
