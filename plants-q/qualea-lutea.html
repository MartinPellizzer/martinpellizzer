Qualea lutea

Qualea lutea, commonly known as the yellow water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is a floating plant with broad, yellowish-green leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the dry season. Qualea lutea plays a vital role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in freshwater ecosystems.

It is adapted to seasonal flooding and can survive in nutrient-poor waters.
