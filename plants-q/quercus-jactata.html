Quercus jactata

Quercus jactata, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which mature in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have serrated edges.

Quercus jactata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine and forestry.
