Quercus polyloba

Quercus polyloba, commonly known as the Sierra foothill oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its deeply lobed leaves, which can reach up to 15 centimeters in length. The tree produces small, pale green acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Quercus polyloba plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
