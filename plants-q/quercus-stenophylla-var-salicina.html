Quercus stenophylla var. salicina

Quercus stenophylla var. salicina, commonly known as the Salicina oak, is a variant of the narrow-leaved oak native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often tinged with silver on the underside. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its acorns are small and provide food for wildlife, including birds and mammals.

The tree has a slow growth rate and is often found in mixed coniferous forests.
