Quercus undulata var. jamesii

Quercus undulata var. jamesii, commonly known as the Apache chestnut, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, acorn-like fruits that are typically clustered in groups of two or three. The leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a wavy margin and a glossy upper surface. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and canyons.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance to indigenous communities.
