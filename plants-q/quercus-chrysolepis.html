Quercus chrysolepis

Quercus chrysolepis, commonly known as the coast live oak, is a deciduous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a waxy coating. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are enclosed in cupules with bristly scales. It thrives in coastal and mountainous regions, often growing in dry, rocky soils.

The coast live oak plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
