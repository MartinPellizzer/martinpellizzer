Quercus lusitanica subsp. valentina

Quercus lusitanica subsp.

valentina, commonly known as the Valencian oak, is a subspecies of the Portuguese oak native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule.

The tree thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils. Its bark is rugged and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies plays an important role in local ecosystems, supporting various forms of wildlife and contributing to biodiversity.
