Quercus cuprea

Quercus cuprea, commonly known as the red oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its deep reddish-brown bark and acorns that are enclosed in a cupule. The leaves are typically lobed, with pointed tips and a rough, hairy texture on the underside. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

The tree is valued for its strong, durable wood and its ecological role in supporting wildlife.
