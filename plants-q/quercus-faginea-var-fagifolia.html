Quercus faginea var. fagifolia

Quercus faginea var. fagifolia, commonly known as the Carpathian oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are important for wildlife. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stress.

It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to rocky or well-drained soils.
