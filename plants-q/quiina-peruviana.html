Quiina peruviana

Quiina peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian quinoa, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients, making it a valuable crop. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its adaptability and nutritional value, it has gained popularity as a pseudocereal in various parts of the world.
