Quercus candicans f. alligata

Quercus candicans f. alligata, commonly known as the glossy-leaf oak, is a botanical variant of the common oak species.

It is characterized by its strikingly glossy, dark green leaves that are typically larger and more elongated than those of the typical species. The plant is native to regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic qualities.
