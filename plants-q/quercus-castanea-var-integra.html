Quercus castanea var. integra

Quercus castanea var. integra, commonly known as the smooth chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves that are typically smooth on both sides, which distinguish it from other oak species. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of ecological and ornamental landscapes.
