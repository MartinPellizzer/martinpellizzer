Quercus virginiana var. geminata

Quercus virginiana var. geminata, commonly known as the Florida live oak, is a variant of the live oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, spreading crown and thick, leathery leaves that are typically lobed and have a deep green color. The tree produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are followed by hard, inedible acorns. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry woodlands.

This variant is notable for its resilience and is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
