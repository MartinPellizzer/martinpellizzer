Quercus flavida

Quercus flavida, commonly known as the yellow oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by acorns that are important for wildlife. Quercus flavida thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and ornamental purposes.
