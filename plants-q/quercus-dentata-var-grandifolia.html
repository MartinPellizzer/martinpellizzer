Quercus dentata var. grandifolia

Quercus dentata var. grandifolia, commonly known as the Japanese pagoda tree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown and elongated, serrated leaves that turn vibrant yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, bell-shaped flowers in spring, followed by edible acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental species in gardens.

Its historical and cultural significance is reflected in its use in traditional landscaping and as a symbol in various Asian cultures.
