Quadrella ferruginea subsp. cubensis

Quadrella ferruginea subsp.

cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Cuba. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and grayish-green foliage.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed sites.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
