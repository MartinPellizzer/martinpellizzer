Quercus rhamnifolia

Quercus rhamnifolia, commonly known as the Florida scrub oak, is a small evergreen oak native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to fire-prone environments. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, cup-shaped acorns. It plays a vital role in the ecological community of scrub habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for its specific habitat and contribute to its ecological significance.
