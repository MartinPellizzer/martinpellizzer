Quercus dentata subsp. stewardii

Quercus dentata subsp.

stewardii, commonly known as the Stewart's Chinese chestnut, is a subspecies native to China. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The tree produces small, spiky acorns that mature over a season. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its hardy nature and potential use in reforestation efforts.
