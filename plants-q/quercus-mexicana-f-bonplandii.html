Quercus mexicana f. bonplandii

Quercus mexicana f. bonplandii, commonly known as the Bonplandii oak, is a variant of the Mexican oak native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in humid, temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant features broad, lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. Its acorns are small and are often consumed by wildlife, contributing to its ecological role.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and adaptability to specific environmental conditions.
