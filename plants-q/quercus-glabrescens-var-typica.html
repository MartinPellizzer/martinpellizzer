Quercus glabrescens var. typica

Quercus glabrescens var. typica, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. The tree produces small, reddish-brown acorns that are encased in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ecological role and historical use in traditional medicine.
