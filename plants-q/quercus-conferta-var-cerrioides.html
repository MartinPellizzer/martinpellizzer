Quercus conferta var. cerrioides

Quercus conferta var. cerrioides, commonly known as the Chinese oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by acorns that are often enclosed in a cupule. Its bark is typically smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
