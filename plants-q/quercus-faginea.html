Quercus faginea

Quercus faginea, commonly known as the Hungarian oak, is a deciduous tree native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 25 meters.

Quercus faginea is valued for its ecological role and is often found in mixed forests.
