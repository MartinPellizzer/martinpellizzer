Quercus rotundifolia subsp. maghrebiana

Quercus rotundifolia subsp.

maghrebiana, commonly known as the Moroccan chestnut oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its rounded, lobed leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid environments.
