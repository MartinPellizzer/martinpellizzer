Quiina indigofera

Quiina indigofera, commonly known as the Indian indigo plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its seeds are encased in a hard, dark pod, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
