Quesnelia roseomarginata

Quesnelia roseomarginata, commonly known as the pink-margin bromeliad, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Bromeliaceae family, characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with red flowers surrounded by pink margins, which give it its common name. Its leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

Quesnelia roseomarginata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
