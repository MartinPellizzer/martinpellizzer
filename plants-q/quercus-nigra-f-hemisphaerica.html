Quercus nigra f. hemisphaerica

Quercus nigra f. hemisphaerica, commonly known as the hemispherical black oak, is a unique variant of the black oak characterized by its distinctive, rounded canopy.

This form exhibits a dense, spherical shape, which sets it apart from the typical spreading growth habit of the species. Botanically, it is a deciduous tree with dark, glossy leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Its compact form makes it a valuable ornamental tree in horticulture and landscape design.
