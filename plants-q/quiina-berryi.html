Quiina berryi

Quiina berryi, commonly known as the desert quinoa, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is well adapted to extreme drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in poor soil. Its seeds are rich in protein and nutrients, making it a valuable crop for sustainable agriculture.

The plant also plays an important role in traditional diets and ecological systems of native communities.
