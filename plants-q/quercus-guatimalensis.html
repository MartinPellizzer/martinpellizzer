Quercus guatimalensis

Quercus guatimalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan oak, is a deciduous tree native to Central America.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, reaching heights of up to 30 meters. The tree has broad, lobed leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its acorns are encased in a cupule and serve as a food source for various wildlife.

Quercus guatimalensis plays an important role in local ecosystems and is valued for its timber and ecological contributions.
