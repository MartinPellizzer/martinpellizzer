Quercus aliena var. rubripes

Quercus aliena var. rubripes, commonly known as the red-leaf Chinese oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-brown leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a range of environmental conditions.

Due to its unique foliage and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
