Quercus semecarpifolia

Quercus semecarpifolia, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are encased in a cupule, a unique feature among oaks. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Quercus semecarpifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
