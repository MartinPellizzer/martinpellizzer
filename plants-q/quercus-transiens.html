Quercus transiens

Quercus transiens, commonly known as the Transient Oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The plant is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall before dropping. Its acorns are enclosed in a cupule and are an important food source for various wildlife species.
