Quercus pedunculata f. longipetiolata

Quercus pedunculata f. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled pedunculate oak, is a variant of the pedunculate oak characterized by its elongated petioles.

This plant is a deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of Asia, particularly China. It features broad, lobed leaves that are typically dark green on top and paler underneath. The tree produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
