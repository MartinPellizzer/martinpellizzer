Quercus lusitanica subsp. veneris

Quercus lusitanica subsp.

veneris, commonly known as the Portuguese oak, is a subspecies native to southern Europe, particularly Portugal and Spain. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that turn reddish in autumn.

The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is typically smooth and grayish, with fissures that become more pronounced with age.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and is often found in mixed forests and open woodlands.
