Quercus × venulosa

Quercus × venulosa, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus variabilis and Quercus acutifolia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, pale acorns that are typically enclosed in cupules. It is native to China and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
