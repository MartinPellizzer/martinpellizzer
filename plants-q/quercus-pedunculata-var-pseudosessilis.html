Quercus pedunculata var. pseudosessilis

Quercus pedunculata var. pseudosessilis, commonly known as the Chinese rock oak, is a variant of the pedunculate oak native to China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and lobed leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its botanical features include a short trunk, dense foliage, and a slow growth rate.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native ecosystems.
