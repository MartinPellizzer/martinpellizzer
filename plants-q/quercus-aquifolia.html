Quercus aquifolia

Quercus aquifolia, commonly known as the glossy-leaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, deeply lobed leaves that are glossy green on top and silver-white underneath. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Quercus aquifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
