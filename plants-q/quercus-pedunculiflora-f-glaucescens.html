Quercus pedunculiflora f. glaucescens

Quercus pedunculiflora f. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous pedunculate oak, is a rare and distinctive variant of the pedunculate oak.

It is characterized by its bluish-gray foliage, which gives it a striking appearance. The leaves are typically shorter and more rounded compared to the typical form, with a waxy coating that enhances its aesthetic appeal. This form is often found in specific microhabitats where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical uniqueness makes it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
