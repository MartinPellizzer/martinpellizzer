Quapoya peruviana var. obtusiuscula

Quapoya peruviana var. obtusiuscula, commonly known as the Peruvian quapoya, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, montane environments. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually white or pale yellow, while the fruit is a capsule containing several seeds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and floral characteristics.
