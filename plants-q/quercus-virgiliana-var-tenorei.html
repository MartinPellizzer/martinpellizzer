Quercus virgiliana var. tenorei

Quercus virgiliana var. tenorei, commonly known as the Tenore's chestnut oak, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of Sardinia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and broad, lobed leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its botanical features make it an important subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
