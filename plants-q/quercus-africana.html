Quercus africana

Quercus africana, commonly known as the African oak, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its deeply lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils.

Quercus africana is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
