Quassia ferruginea

Quassia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty quassia, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Simaroubaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. The tree also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
