Quercus Nigra Var. Falcata

Quercus nigra var. falcata, commonly known as the Florida hickory, is a unique variant of the black oak. This tree is native to the southeastern united states, particularly florida and georgia. it is distinguished by its elongated, sickle-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in wetland environments. the wood of this species is highly valued for its strength and durability. it plays an important role in local ecosystems and has cultural significance among native peoples.
