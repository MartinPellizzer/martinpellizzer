Quercus × hispanica subvar. latifolia

Quercus × hispanica subvar. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved evergreen oak, is a hybrid species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that remain green throughout the year, making it a notable evergreen in Mediterranean climates. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its acorns are small and often have a distinctive cap, which is characteristic of the oak genus.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to well-drained soils.
