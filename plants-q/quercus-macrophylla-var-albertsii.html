Quercus macrophylla var. albertsii

Quercus macrophylla var. albertsii, commonly known as the Alberts' laurel oak, is a variant of the California laurel oak native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are encased in a dense, fibrous cap. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and chaparral habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
