Quercus tlapuxahuensis var. obconica

Quercus tlapuxahuensis var. obconica, commonly known as the Mexican red oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its obconic (upside-down cone-shaped) leaves, which are typically elliptical with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native range.
