Quercus sessiliflora var. tcharachensis

Quercus sessiliflora var. tcharachensis, commonly known as the Tcharachensis chestnut oak, is a variant of the sessiliflora oak species native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits that are typically sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a stalk. The leaves are usually small, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
