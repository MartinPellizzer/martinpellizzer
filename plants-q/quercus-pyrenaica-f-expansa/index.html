Quercus Pyrenaica F. Expansa

Quercus pyrenaica f. expansa is a unique variant of the Pyrenean oak known for its distinctive broad leaves and robust growth. This plant thrives in the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions. Its ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat for various wildlife species and its contribution to local biodiversity. The form expansa stands out due to its wider foliage compared to the standard Quercus pyrenaica. Studying this plant offers insights into evolutionary adaptations and the resilience of flora in challenging climates.
