Quercus nebulosa

Quercus nebulosa, commonly known as the Persian oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The Persian oak is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
