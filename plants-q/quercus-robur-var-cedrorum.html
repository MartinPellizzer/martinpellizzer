Quercus robur var. cedrorum

Quercus robur var. cedrorum, commonly known as the Cedarnut Oak, is a variant of the common oak characterized by its distinctive foliage and fruiting structures.

It is native to parts of Europe and is known for its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types. The leaves are typically lobed and have a deep green color, turning vibrant shades in the autumn. Its acorns are enclosed in a cupule, which is distinctive and often used for identification.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
