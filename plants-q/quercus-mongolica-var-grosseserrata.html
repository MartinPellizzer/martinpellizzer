Quercus mongolica var. grosseserrata

Quercus mongolica var. grosseserrata, commonly known as the Mongolian oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its lobed leaves with prominent, serrated margins, which give it its specific epithet "grosseserrata.". The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in temperate forests and is adapted to a range of soil types.

This species plays a significant role in forest ecosystems and is valued for its ecological and potential economic importance.
