Quercus faginea f. macrobalanus

Quercus faginea f. macrobalanus, commonly known as the large-leaf sessile oak, is a variant of the sessile oak native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically lobed and have a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that mature over a season. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and is often found in mixed forests.
