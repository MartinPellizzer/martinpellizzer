Quesnelia glaziovii

Quesnelia glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's bromeliad, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central pool to collect water. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that can last several months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Quesnelia glaziovii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
