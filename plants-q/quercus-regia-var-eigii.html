Quercus regia var. eigii

Quercus regia var. eigii, commonly known as the Eigii oak, is a variant of the royal oak, characterized by its distinctive foliage and growth habits.

It is a deciduous tree native to specific regions of Europe, particularly in the Carpathian Basin. The leaves are typically lobed, with a serrated margin, and the acorns are encased in a cupule that provides protection. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
