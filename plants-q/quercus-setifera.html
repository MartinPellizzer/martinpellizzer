Quercus setifera

Quercus setifera, commonly known as the silky oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in the Appalachian region. The tree is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and its alternate, ovate leaves with a silvery underside. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, and it produces acorns as its fruit.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in furniture and construction.
