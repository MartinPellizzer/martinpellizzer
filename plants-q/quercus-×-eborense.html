Quercus × eborense

Quercus × eborense, commonly known as the hybrid oak, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Quercus robur (English oak) and Quercus petraea (sessile oak).

It exhibits characteristics of both parent species, often displaying a combination of leaf shape, bark texture, and growth habits. This species is native to Europe and is typically found in mixed forests and woodland areas. It is a medium to large deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its ornamental value.
