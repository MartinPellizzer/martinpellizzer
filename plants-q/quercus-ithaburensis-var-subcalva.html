Quercus ithaburensis var. subcalva

Quercus ithaburensis var. subcalva, commonly known as the subcalva oak, is a variant of the Ithaburensis oak native to specific regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and distinctive leaf morphology. The leaves are typically lobed and have a serrated margin, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. This variety is adapted to rocky and limestone soils, thriving in dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
