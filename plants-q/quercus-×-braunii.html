Quercus × braunii

Quercus × braunii, commonly known as the Braun's oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus cerris (Turkey oak) and Quercus robur (English oak).

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, spiky acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. Quercus × braunii is native to Central Europe and is often found in mixed forests.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
