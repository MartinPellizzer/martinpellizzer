Quercus phillyreoides

Quercus phillyreoides, commonly known as the Florida scrub oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces small, acorn-like fruits. The plant is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in scrub habitats. It plays a vital role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining ecosystem biodiversity.

Its ecological importance makes it a key species in conservation efforts.
