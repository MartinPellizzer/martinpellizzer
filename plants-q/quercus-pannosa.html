Quercus pannosa

Quercus pannosa, commonly known as the panose oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and its distinctive, papery, grayish-brown bark that peels in thin layers. The leaves are typically oval to elliptical, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in bottomland hardwood forests.

The tree is valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
