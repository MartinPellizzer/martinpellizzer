Quercus hasseltii

Quercus hasseltii, commonly known as the African white oak, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and long, narrow leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, pale green acorns that are encased in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

Quercus hasseltii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
