Quercus valida

Quercus valida, commonly known as the white oak, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is thick and ridged, providing protection against pests and diseases.

Quercus valida is valued for its durable wood and its role in supporting biodiversity in forest ecosystems.
