Quercus brantii subsp. persica

Quercus brantii subsp.

persica, commonly known as Persian oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of southwestern Asia. It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
