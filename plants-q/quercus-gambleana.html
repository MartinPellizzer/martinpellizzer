Quercus gambleana

Quercus gambleana, commonly known as the Gambel oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The bark is thick and deeply furrowed, providing habitat for various small animals and insects.
