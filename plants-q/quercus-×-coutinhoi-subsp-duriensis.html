Quercus × coutinhoi subsp. duriensis

Quercus × coutinhoi subsp.

duriensis, commonly known as the Duriense Oak, is a hybrid species within the genus Quercus, native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and broad, lobed leaves that are typically pubescent on the underside.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, temperate climates. Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
