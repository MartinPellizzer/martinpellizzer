Quercus rufa

Quercus rufa, commonly known as the scarlet oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Quercus rufa typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and supporting biodiversity in forest ecosystems.
