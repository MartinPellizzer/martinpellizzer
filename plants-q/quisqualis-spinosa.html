Quisqualis spinosa

Quisqualis spinosa, commonly known as the Chinese starfruit or the winter melon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing vines and clusters of fragrant, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a distinctive fruit with a thick, waxy skin and a sweet, juicy interior. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Quisqualis spinosa thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
