Quercus thomsonii

Quercus thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a distinctive wavy margin. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodlands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to dry, Mediterranean climates.
