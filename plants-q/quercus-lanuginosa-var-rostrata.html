Quercus lanuginosa var. rostrata

Quercus lanuginosa var. rostrata, commonly known as the silky oak or rostrata variant, is a deciduous tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage and distinctive, elongated acorns. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and riverine habitats.

The plant plays an important ecological role, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
