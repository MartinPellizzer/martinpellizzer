Quercus calliprinos var. pachybalanos

Quercus calliprinos var. pachybalanos, commonly known as the thick-leaved canyon oak, is a variant of the coast live oak native to California.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often gray-green in color and have a waxy coating. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils along canyons and slopes. It produces acorns that are edible and are a food source for various wildlife species.

This oak is adapted to dry, Mediterranean climates and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
