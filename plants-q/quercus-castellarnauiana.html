Quercus castellarnauiana

Quercus castellarnauiana, commonly known as the Castellarnau oak, is a rare and endemic species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen oak with dense, dark green foliage and a rounded canopy. The leaves are typically elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctive serrated margin. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are often retained on the tree for several years.

This species is adapted to rocky and dry environments, showcasing its resilience in harsh Mediterranean climates.
