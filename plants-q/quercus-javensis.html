Quercus javensis

Quercus javensis, commonly known as the Java oak, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The plant is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy green upper surface. The tree is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
