Quercus faginea subvar. pedunculata

Quercus faginea subvar. pedunculata, commonly known as the pedunculate oak, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves that turn yellow or reddish in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically found in clusters. It has a strong, straight trunk with a broad, rounded crown.

This species is valued for its durable wood and is often found in mixed forests and along riverbanks.
