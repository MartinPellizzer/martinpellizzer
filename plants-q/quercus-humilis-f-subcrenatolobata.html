Quercus humilis f. subcrenatolobata

Quercus humilis f. subcrenatolobata, commonly known as the narrow-lobed rock oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and distinctive, narrow, lobed leaves that are often hairy underneath. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves turn red in the fall, adding seasonal visual interest.

This form is a natural variant of Quercus humilis, showcasing genetic diversity within the species.
