Quercus sublobata

Quercus sublobata, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, leathery, elliptical leaves that remain on the tree throughout the year. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically covered with a dense, hairy layer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This tree is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
