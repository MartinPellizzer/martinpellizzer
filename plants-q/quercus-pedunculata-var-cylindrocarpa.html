Quercus pedunculata var. cylindrocarpa

Quercus pedunculata var. cylindrocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by edible, sweet, chestnut-like fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible nuts.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
