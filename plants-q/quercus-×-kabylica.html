Quercus × kabylica

Quercus × kabylica, commonly known as the Kabylica oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus cerris and Quercus frainetto.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its serrated leaves and acorns, which are typically smaller than those of its parent species. The tree is native to the Balkan Peninsula and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. It exhibits a range of leaf colors, from dark green to reddish-brown, depending on the season and environmental conditions.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
