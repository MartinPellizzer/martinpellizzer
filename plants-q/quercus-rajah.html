Quercus rajah

Quercus rajah, commonly known as the Rajah oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in the Philippines. This tree is characterized by its large size, with some specimens reaching heights of over 30 meters. Its leaves are typically broad and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces small, reddish-brown acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
