Quercus mespilifolia

Quercus mespilifolia, commonly known as the Persian ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, acorn-like fruits that mature in the fall. The tree is known for its hard, durable wood, which has been historically used for construction and tool-making. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Quercus mespilifolia plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
