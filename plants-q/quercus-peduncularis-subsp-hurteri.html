Quercus peduncularis subsp. hurteri

Quercus peduncularis subsp.

hurteri, commonly known as the Chinese pedunculate oak, is a deciduous tree native to China. It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained soils.
