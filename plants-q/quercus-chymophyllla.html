Quercus chymophyllla

Quercus chymophyllla, commonly known as the silverleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that turn bronze in the fall, giving it a striking appearance. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged texture.

Quercus chymophyllla thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
