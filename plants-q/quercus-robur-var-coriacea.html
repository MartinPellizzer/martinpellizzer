Quercus robur var. coriacea

Quercus robur var. coriacea, commonly known as the English oak, is a deciduous tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its sturdy, deeply furrowed bark and lobed, serrated leaves that turn golden in autumn. The tree produces small, spiky acorns that are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and can grow to impressive heights, often reaching over 30 meters.

This variety is valued for its ecological importance and historical significance in European landscapes.
