Quercus coreana

Quercus coreana, commonly known as the Korean oak, is a deciduous tree native to Korea and parts of northeastern China.

It is characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Quercus coreana is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional crafts and construction.

It also plays a significant role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
