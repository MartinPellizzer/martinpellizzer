Quercus stewardii

Quercus stewardii, commonly known as the Stewart oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, acorn-like fruits. The plant has a slow growth rate and exhibits a broad, rounded crown. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Fagaceae family.
