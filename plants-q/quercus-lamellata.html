Quercus lamellata

Quercus lamellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often curled under at the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in bottomland hardwood forests.

Quercus lamellata plays an important role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
