Quercus rotundifolia f. calicina

Quercus rotundifolia f. calicina, commonly known as the California bay laurel, is a variant of the California bay tree.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of California and Baja California. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves with a distinctive waxy texture and a strong aromatic scent. Its flowers are small and clustered, followed by dark, leathery fruits containing one or two seeds.

This form is notable for its compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
