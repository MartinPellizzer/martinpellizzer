Quercus lanuginosa var. stenophylla

Quercus lanuginosa var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically enclosed in a cupule, a unique feature among oaks. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of xerophytic vegetation.
