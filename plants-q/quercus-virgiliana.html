Quercus virgiliana

Quercus virgiliana, commonly known as the Virgil oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are encased in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
