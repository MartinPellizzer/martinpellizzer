Quercus faginea subsp. lusitanica

Quercus faginea subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe, particularly Portugal and Spain. It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts, and is characterized by its acorn-producing capability.

The leaves are typically broad, lobed, and have a dark green upper surface with a lighter underside. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in montane forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the Mediterranean ecosystem.
