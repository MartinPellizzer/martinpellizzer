Quercus robur subsp. broteroana

Quercus robur subsp.

broteroana, commonly known as the Portuguese oak, is a subspecies of the common oak characterized by its distinctive morphology and ecological significance. It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and features broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The tree produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mixed forests and open woodlands.

Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a habitat for a variety of epiphytic organisms.
