Quercus coccifera subsp. mesto

Quercus coccifera subsp.

mesto, commonly known as the Algerian chestnut oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky or arid environments, showing high drought tolerance.

Its ecological significance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for endemic flora and fauna.
