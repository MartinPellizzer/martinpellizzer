Quercus pyrenaica lusus pendula

Quercus pyrenaica lusus pendula, commonly known as the weeping sessile oak, is a striking ornamental tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that create a unique, umbrella-like shape, giving it a dramatic appearance. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are typically dark green and oval-shaped, with a serrated edge.

This species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its visual impact.
