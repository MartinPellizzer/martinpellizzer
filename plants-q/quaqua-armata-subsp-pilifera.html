Quaqua armata subsp. pilifera

Quaqua armata subsp.

pilifera, commonly known as the silky quava, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
