Quercus balsequillana

Quercus balsequillana, commonly known as the Balsequillan oak, is a rare and lesser-known species of oak native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and broad, lobed leaves that provide a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in humid, temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a sturdy trunk and a dense canopy, making it a notable species in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
