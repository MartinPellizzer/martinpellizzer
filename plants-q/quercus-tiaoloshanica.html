Quercus tiaoloshanica

Quercus tiaoloshanica, commonly known as the Tiaoloshan oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to China, specifically found in the Tiaoloshan region of the Yunnan Province. The plant is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn vibrant colors in the fall. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This tree is valued for its ecological role and is often found in mixed forests at higher elevations.
