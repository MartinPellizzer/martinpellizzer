Quercus coccifera f. tomentosa

Quercus coccifera f. tomentosa, commonly known as the tomentose rock oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it a grayish appearance. The leaves are typically small, elliptical, and have a leathery texture. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This plant is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species in coastal and mountainous areas.
