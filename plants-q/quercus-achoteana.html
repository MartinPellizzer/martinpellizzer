Quercus achoteana

Quercus achoteana, commonly known as the red oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, Argentina, and Paraguay. The tree is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and acorns that are often covered in a dense, fibrous cap. Its leaves are typically oval or elliptical with serrated edges, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring.

The plant is valued for its hard wood and has been used by indigenous communities for various purposes.
