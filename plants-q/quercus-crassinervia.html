Quercus crassinervia

Quercus crassinervia, commonly known as the broad-veined oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its leathery, elliptical leaves with prominent, parallel veins. The plant produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically covered in a dense, hairy coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland forests.

Quercus crassinervia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
