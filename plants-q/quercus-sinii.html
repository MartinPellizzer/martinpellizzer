Quercus sinii

Quercus sinii, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Quercus sinii is often found in mixed forests and is valued for its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
