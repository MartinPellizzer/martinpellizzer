Quercus × ludoviciana var. microcarpa

Quercus × ludoviciana var. microcarpa, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a hybrid species native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and broad, lobed leaves that are often hairy beneath. The tree typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. It is often found in dry forests and woodlands, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes planted in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
