Quercus shumardii

Quercus shumardii, commonly known as the Shumard oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its deep lobes on its leaves, which are typically 5 to 7 in number, and its acorns that are encased in a cupule. The tree grows to a height of 20 to 40 feet and has a broad, rounded crown. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing a rugged appearance.

The Shumard oak is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
