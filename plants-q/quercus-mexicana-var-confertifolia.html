Quercus mexicana var. confertifolia

Quercus mexicana var. confertifolia, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen oak, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a tall, evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters, with a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are typically leathery, elliptical, and have a dark green upper surface with a lighter underside. It produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
