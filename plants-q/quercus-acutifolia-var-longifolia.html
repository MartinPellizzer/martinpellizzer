Quercus acutifolia var. longifolia

Quercus acutifolia var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf evergreen oak, is a variant of the acuteleaf oak native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically covered in a dense, fuzzy cap. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
