Quercus petraea subf. normalis

Quercus petraea subf.

normalis, commonly known as the European rock oak, is a subspecies of the sessile oak native to Europe. It is characterized by its dense, rugged growth habit, often found on rocky or limestone soils.

The leaves are typically oblong to elliptical, with a rough, serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. The acorns are small and usually produced in clusters, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is often used in horticulture and reforestation projects.
