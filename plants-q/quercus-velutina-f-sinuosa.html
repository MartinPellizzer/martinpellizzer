Quercus velutina f. sinuosa

Quercus velutina f. sinuosa, commonly known as the Southern red oak, is a variant of the red oak species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

The leaves are typically lobed with sinuous margins, giving the plant its specific epithet "sinuosa.". It is native to the eastern United States, where it thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic soils. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance that contributes to its ecological and aesthetic value.
