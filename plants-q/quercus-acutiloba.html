Quercus acutiloba

Quercus acutiloba, commonly known as the Japanese oak, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright form and acorn-producing capsules. The leaves are typically elliptical with a pointed tip and are often hairy beneath. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
