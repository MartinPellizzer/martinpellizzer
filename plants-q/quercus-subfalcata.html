Quercus subfalcata

Quercus subfalcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath. The tree produces small, reddish-brown acorns that are enclosed in a cupule, a unique feature among oaks. Its bark is typically smooth and gray when young, developing a scaly texture with age.

Quercus subfalcata is valued for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
