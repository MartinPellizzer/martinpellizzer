Quercus salicina var. orgyalis

Quercus salicina var. orgyalis, commonly known as the Oregon white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and lobed leaves that are typically 5 to 9 inches long. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that mature over a year and are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in oak woodlands and mixed coniferous forests.

The species is valued for its ecological role and historical significance in indigenous cultures.
