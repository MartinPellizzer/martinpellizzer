Quercus laurifolia var. tridentata

Quercus laurifolia var. tridentata, commonly known as the tridentate laurel oak, is a variant of the laurel oak species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically three-lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale green to reddish flowers in the spring, followed by edible acorns that mature in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves and acorns have been used in traditional herbal remedies, reflecting its ecological and medicinal significance.
