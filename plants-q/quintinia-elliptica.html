Quintinia elliptica

Quintinia elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf quintinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elliptical, narrow leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by elongated, woody fruits. Quintinia elliptica is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
