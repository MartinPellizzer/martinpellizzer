Quercus × neotharpii var. perstellata

Quercus × neotharpii var. perstellata, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut hybrid, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Quercus acorns and Quercus variabilis.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated acorns and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant exhibits a mix of traits from its parent species, including leaf morphology and growth habits. It is often found in forested areas across eastern Asia, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
