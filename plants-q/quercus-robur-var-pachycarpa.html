Quercus robur var. pachycarpa

Quercus robur var. pachycarpa, commonly known as the thick-fruited oak, is a variant of the common oak characterized by its distinctive, thick and fleshy acorns.

This plant is a deciduous tree that typically grows in temperate regions, reaching heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are lobed and often have a serrated edge, contributing to its aesthetic appeal. The acorns are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.
