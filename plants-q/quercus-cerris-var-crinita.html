Quercus cerris var. crinita

Quercus cerris var. crinita, commonly known as the curly oak, is a variant of the Turkey oak.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in rocky or dry soils. Its acorns are typically small and have a smooth cap.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture.
