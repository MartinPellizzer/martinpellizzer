Quillaja lancifolia

Quillaja lancifolia, commonly known as the Chilean chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers and red, fleshy berries that contain seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Quillaja lancifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
