Quercus lanuginosa var. brevicalyx

Quercus lanuginosa var. brevicalyx, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut or small-leaved chinquapin, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, spiky fruits called nuts, which are encased in a prickly husk. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
