Quercus lusitanica var. subocculata

Quercus lusitanica var. subocculata, commonly known as the Iberian chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to southwestern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and broad, elliptical leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The species is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
