Quercus mexicana f. perfertilis

Quercus mexicana f. perfertilis, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a variant of the Mexican oak characterized by its distinctive white to pale gray bark and broad, lobed leaves.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico, where it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant is a deciduous tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, featuring a rounded crown and sturdy branches. Its acorns are small and often have a shallow cupule, and they are an important food source for various wildlife species.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
