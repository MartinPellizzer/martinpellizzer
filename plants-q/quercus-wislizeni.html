Quercus wislizeni

Quercus wislizeni, commonly known as the wislizeni oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and hairy, ovate leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along canyons. Its bark is typically smooth and gray, with a distinctive flaky texture.

Quercus wislizeni plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and stabilizing desert ecosystems.
