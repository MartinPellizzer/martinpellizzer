Quercus purpusii

Quercus purpusii, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to China and Vietnam.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It produces small, cupule-enclosed acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.
