Qualea cordata var. intermedia

Qualea cordata var. intermedia, commonly known as the pink river turtle plant, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small, delicate bells. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
