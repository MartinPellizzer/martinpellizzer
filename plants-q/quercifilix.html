Quercifilix

Quercifilix, commonly known as the oakleaf fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its fronds are broad and lobed, resembling the shape of an oak leaf, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish itself in suitable habitats.

Quercifilix is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity.
