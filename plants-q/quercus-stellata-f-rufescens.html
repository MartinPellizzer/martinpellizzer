Quercus stellata f. rufescens

Quercus stellata f. rufescens, commonly known as the redleaf scrub oak, is a shrub-like form of the Texas live oak.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that turn a reddish-brown color in the fall. This plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to the southeastern United States. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by acorns that are a food source for various wildlife.

The species is well adapted to arid conditions and is often found in woodlands and scrub habitats.
