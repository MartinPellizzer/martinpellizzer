Quiina macrostachya

Quiina macrostachya, commonly known as the tall quina or quina grass, is a perennial plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces panicle-like inflorescences with small, spikelet-based flowers, typical of grass species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
