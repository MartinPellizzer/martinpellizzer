Quercus lanuginosa subsp. dalechampii

Quercus lanuginosa subsp.

dalechampii, commonly known as the Dalechamp's hairy oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its grayish-brown, fissured bark and small, lobed leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically two-lobed and have a short stem. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
