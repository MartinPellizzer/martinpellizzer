Quercus × rechingeri

Quercus × rechingeri, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut hybrid, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is a natural hybrid between Quercus acutissima and Quercus serrata, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The tree typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, with a broad, spreading crown and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with male and female flowers appearing on separate trees.

The fruit is a chestnut-like acorn, which is edible and often used in traditional Chinese cuisine.
