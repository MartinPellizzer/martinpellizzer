Quercus gunnisonii

Quercus gunnisonii, commonly known as Gunnison's oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are often enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Gunnison's oak plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
