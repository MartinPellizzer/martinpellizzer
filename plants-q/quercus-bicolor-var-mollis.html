Quercus bicolor var. mollis

Quercus bicolor var. mollis, commonly known as the silky chinquapin, is a variant of the chestnut oak native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely hairy leaves that give it a silky appearance, especially when young. The plant produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological contributions in forest ecosystems.
