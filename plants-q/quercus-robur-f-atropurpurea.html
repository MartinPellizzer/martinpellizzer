Quercus robur f. atropurpurea

Quercus robur f. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-leaf oak, is a variant of the common oak characterized by its distinctive foliage.

The leaves are typically larger and have a striking purple or bronze hue, especially during the growing season. This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It retains the typical structure of the common oak, with sturdy branches and a broad canopy.

The plant is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a versatile choice for horticultural use.
