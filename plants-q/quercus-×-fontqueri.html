Quercus × fontqueri

Quercus × fontqueri, commonly known as the Arizona white oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus arizonica and Quercus engelmannii.

It is a deciduous tree native to the Sonoran Desert region of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant features broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and it produces small, inedible acorns. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The Arizona white oak plays a vital role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
