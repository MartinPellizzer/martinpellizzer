Quercus subera

Quercus subera, commonly known as the Spanish oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thick, corky bark, which is harvested for the production of cork. The plant has broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. It thrives in mild, temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Quercus subera plays an important role in both ecological and economic contexts due to its unique properties and habitat.
