Quercus pedunculata var. pubescens

Quercus pedunculata var. pubescens, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, alternate leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically covered in a sticky resin. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
