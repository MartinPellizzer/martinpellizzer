Quadria avellana

Quadria avellana, commonly known as the Spanish chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and other chestnut species. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which mature in the autumn. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

The bark is thick, fissured, and becomes deeply grooved with age.
