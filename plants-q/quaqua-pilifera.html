Quaqua pilifera

Quaqua pilifera, commonly known as the featherleaf plant, is a herbaceous species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, often arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, Quaqua pilifera is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
