Quercus ilex subvar. capreifolia

Quercus ilex subvar. capreifolia, commonly known as the Kermes oak, is a variant of the holm oak native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub with dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a distinctive shape. The plant produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in Mediterranean climates.
