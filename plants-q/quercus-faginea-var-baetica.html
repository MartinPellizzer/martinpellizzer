Quercus faginea var. baetica

Quercus faginea var. baetica, commonly known as the Iberian chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn reddish-brown in autumn. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils, often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive component of the Mediterranean ecosystem.
