Quercus robinsonii

Quercus robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically glossy and dark green on top. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are encased in a dense, scaly cap. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, upland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
