Quercus toza var. sessiliflora-serotina

Quercus toza var. sessiliflora-serotina, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen oak, is a variant of the evergreen oak species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that remain on the tree throughout the year. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically retained on the tree for extended periods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a humid climate.

This variant is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and its resilience to various environmental conditions.
