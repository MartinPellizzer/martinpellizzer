Quercus margaretta

Quercus margaretta, commonly known as the Margaretta oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its lobed leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing a rugged texture.

Quercus margaretta is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
