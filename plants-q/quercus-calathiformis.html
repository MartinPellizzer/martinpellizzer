Quercus calathiformis

Quercus calathiformis, commonly known as the desert broom acorn tree, is a rare and endemic species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree with a distinctive, sprawling growth habit. The leaves are typically small, leathery, and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its acorns are small and often have a distinctive cupule that encloses most of the nut.

The plant is adapted to extreme conditions, showcasing unique botanical features that enable survival in dry, rocky soils.
