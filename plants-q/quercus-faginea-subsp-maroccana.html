Quercus faginea subsp. maroccana

Quercus faginea subsp.

maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan chestnut oak, is a subspecies native to the Atlas Mountains of North Africa. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn reddish in autumn.

The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The botanical features of this subspecies make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
