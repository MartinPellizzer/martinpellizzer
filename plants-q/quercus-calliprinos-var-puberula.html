Quercus calliprinos var. puberula

Quercus calliprinos var. puberula, commonly known as the Coast live oak, is a deciduous evergreen oak native to coastal regions of California.

It has broad, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a rough, pubescent texture. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are enclosed in a cupule, a characteristic feature of the Fagaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.
