Qualea gracilior

Qualea gracilior, commonly known as the slender water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that float on the water's surface, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the dry season, attracting pollinators such as bees and beetles. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate, allowing it to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Qualea gracilior plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting local biodiversity.
