Quilamum

Quilamum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and emit a strong, spicy aroma. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline environments.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
