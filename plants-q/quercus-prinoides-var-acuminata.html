Quercus prinoides var. acuminata

Quercus prinoides var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts, and is characterized by its acorn-producing capability. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
