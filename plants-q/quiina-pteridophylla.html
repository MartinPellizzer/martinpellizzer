Quiina pteridophylla

Quiina pteridophylla, commonly known as the silver fern, is a rare and delicate plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-white fronds that shimmer in the sunlight. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing on rocky slopes or in shaded forest understories. Its unique appearance makes it a sought-after species among botanists and plant enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
