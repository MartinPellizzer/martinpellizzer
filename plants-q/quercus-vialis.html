Quercus vialis

Quercus vialis, commonly known as the common white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, spiky acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is typically gray and becomes furrowed with age.

Quercus vialis is valued for its ecological role and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
