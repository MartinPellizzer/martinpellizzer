Quercus sessiliflora var. mongolica

Quercus sessiliflora var. mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian sessile oak, is a deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
