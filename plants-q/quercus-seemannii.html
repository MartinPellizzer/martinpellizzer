Quercus seemannii

Quercus seemannii, commonly known as the Namibian oak, is a deciduous tree native to the hyperarid regions of Namibia and Angola.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and leathery, elliptical leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The tree typically grows in rocky soils and is well-suited to arid environments due to its deep root system. It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various desert wildlife.

Quercus seemannii is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used by local communities for construction and crafts.
