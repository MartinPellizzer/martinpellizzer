Quercus coccinea var. pendula

Quercus coccinea var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping red oak, is a striking variant of the red oak species.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The leaves are typically lobed and turn vibrant shades of red in the fall, adding to its visual appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
