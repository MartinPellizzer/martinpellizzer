Quercus dumosa var. macdonaldii

Quercus dumosa var. macdonaldii, commonly known as the Macdonald's scrub oak, is a shrub-like species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically two-lobed and have a short stalk. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often found in rocky habitats and is an important component of chaparral ecosystems.
