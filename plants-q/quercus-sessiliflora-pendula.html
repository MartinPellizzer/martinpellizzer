Quercus sessiliflora pendula

Quercus sessiliflora pendula, commonly known as the weeping chinquapin, is a unique deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its pendulous, drooping branches that create a weeping appearance, giving it a distinctive ornamental look. The plant produces small, chestnut-like fruits that are edible for certain wildlife species. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a glossy green surface.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
