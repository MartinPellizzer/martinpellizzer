Quercus carolinae

Quercus carolinae, commonly known as the Carolina栎, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 20–30 meters and has a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are simple, alternate, and lobed, turning vibrant yellow or red in the fall. Its acorns are enclosed in a cupule and are an important food source for wildlife.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental qualities.
