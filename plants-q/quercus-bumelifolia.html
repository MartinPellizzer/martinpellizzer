Quercus bumelifolia

Quercus bumelifolia, commonly known as the California black oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and acorns that are enclosed in a cupule. The leaves are typically elliptical with a pointed tip and a rough upper surface. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils in open woodlands.

This species plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
