Quercus scortechinii

Quercus scortechinii, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and small, acorn-like fruits. The leaves are typically oval with a smooth margin and a pointed tip. This tree thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
