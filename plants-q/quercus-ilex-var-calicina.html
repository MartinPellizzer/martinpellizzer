Quercus ilex var. calicina

Quercus ilex var. calicina, commonly known as the California live oak, is a variant of the evergreen oak native to California.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in Mediterranean climates with mild, wet winters and dry summers.

This variant is often found in coastal and inland regions, contributing to local biodiversity and ecological stability.
