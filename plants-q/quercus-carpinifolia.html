Quercus carpinifolia

Quercus carpinifolia, commonly known as the Japanese oak, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Quercus carpinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
