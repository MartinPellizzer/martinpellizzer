Quercus glandulifera var. major

Quercus glandulifera var. major, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that are often glandular-dotted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland and scrub habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional uses in the region.
