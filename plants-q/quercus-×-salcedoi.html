Quercus × salcedoi

Quercus × salcedoi, commonly known as the Salcedo oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus lobata and Quercus rugosa.

It is native to California, where it thrives in coastal and foothill regions with a Mediterranean climate. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and acorns that are typically small and enclosed in cupules. It has moderate water requirements and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
