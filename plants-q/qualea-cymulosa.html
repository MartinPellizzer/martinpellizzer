Qualea cymulosa

Qualea cymulosa, commonly known as the yellow mangrove, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of northern South America, particularly in the Amazon and Orinoco basins. This plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and its ability to thrive in brackish water environments. Qualea cymulosa is also notable for its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.
