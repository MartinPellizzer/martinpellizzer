Quercus coccifera f. imbricata

Quercus coccifera f. imbricata, commonly known as the dwarf evergreen oak, is a shrub-like form of the common holm oak.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, arid environments. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its compact growth habit makes it well-suited for coastal and dryland conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to harsh climates.
