Quadrella siliquosa

Quadrella siliquosa, commonly known as the silique bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits called silique, which contain the seeds. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
