Quercus acutissima var. chenii

Quercus acutissima var. chenii, commonly known as the Chinese sharpleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, acutely tapered leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
