Quercus × catalaunica

Quercus × catalaunica, commonly known as the Catalaunica oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between the sessile oak (Quercus petraea) and the pedunculate oak (Quercus robur).

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

Quercus × catalaunica is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
