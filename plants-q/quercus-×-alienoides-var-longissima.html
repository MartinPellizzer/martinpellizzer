Quercus × alienoides var. longissima

Quercus × alienoides var. longissima, commonly known as the longleaf chestnut oak, is a hybrid species within the oak genus Quercus.

It is characterized by its elongated acorns and distinctive foliage, which are intermediate between its parent species, Quercus bicolor and Quercus cerris. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in forested areas, contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
