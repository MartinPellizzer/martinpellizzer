Quercus collivaga

Quercus collivaga, commonly known as the Spanish white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and elliptical, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Quercus collivaga is valued for its ecological role and historical use in traditional landscaping.
