Quercus infectoria subsp. mirbeckii

Quercus infectoria subsp.

mirbeckii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its dense, dark brown bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often hairy beneath.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by hard, spiny acorns. It is highly valued for its extremely hard and heavy wood, which is used in construction and tool-making.

This subspecies is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in traditional medicine.
