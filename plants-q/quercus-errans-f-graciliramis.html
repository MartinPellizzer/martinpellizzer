Quercus errans f. graciliramis

Quercus errans f. graciliramis, commonly known as the slender-ramose oak, is a rare and distinctive variant of the errans oak.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and small, acorn-like fruits. This plant is native to specific regions of southern Africa, particularly in the Western Cape. Its foliage consists of small, leathery leaves with a glossy surface.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
