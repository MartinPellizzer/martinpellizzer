Quercus pubinervis

Quercus pubinervis, commonly known as the velvetleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that have a distinctive velvety texture on the underside. The plant produces small, spiny acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. Its leaves are usually pubescent, giving it the common name "velvetleaf.".

Quercus pubinervis is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
