Quercus coccifera f. lanceolata

Quercus coccifera f. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate evergreen oak, is a subspecies of the common buckthorn oak.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is typically shrub-like or small tree-like, with dark green foliage that turns reddish-brown in autumn. It produces small, red acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

This subspecies is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
