Quercus suber subf. clavata

Quercus suber subf.

clavata, commonly known as the cork oak, is a member of the Fagaceae family and is native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its thick, corky bark, which is harvested for commercial use.

This subspecies is adapted to Mediterranean climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.
