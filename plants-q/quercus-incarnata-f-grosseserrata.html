Quercus incarnata f. grosseserrata

Quercus incarnata f. grosseserrata, commonly known as the swamp chestnut oak, is a variant of the common chestnut oak.

It is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, well-drained soils. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have prominent, undulating serrations along the edges. It produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The tree is characterized by its strong, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
