Quercus molucca

Quercus molucca, commonly known as the Moluccan oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its acorns are small and edible, providing food for various wildlife species.

Quercus molucca is often found in tropical rainforests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
