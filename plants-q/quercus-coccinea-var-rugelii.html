Quercus coccinea var. rugelii

Quercus coccinea var. rugelii, commonly known as the scarlet oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage in the fall, which is a striking botanical feature. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with finely serrated margins. Its acorns are small, oblong, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The tree thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
