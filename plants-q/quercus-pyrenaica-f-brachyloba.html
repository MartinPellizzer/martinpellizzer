Quercus pyrenaica f. brachyloba

Quercus pyrenaica f. brachyloba, commonly known as the short-leaved Pyrenean oak, is a shrub-like form of the Pyrenean oak.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and short, broad leaves. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique morphology, it is of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
