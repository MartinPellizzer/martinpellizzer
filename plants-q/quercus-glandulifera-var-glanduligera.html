Quercus glandulifera var. glanduligera

Quercus glandulifera var. glanduligera, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to regions of southeastern Europe and southwestern Asia. The tree is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The bark is thick and fissured, providing habitat for a range of insects and lichens.
