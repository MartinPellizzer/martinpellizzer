Quercus spicata var. glaberrima

Quercus spicata var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smoothleaf chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and acorns that are typically covered in a small cupule. The leaves are elliptical, with a smooth margin and a pointed tip, and they turn yellow in the fall. This variety is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

It plays an important role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
