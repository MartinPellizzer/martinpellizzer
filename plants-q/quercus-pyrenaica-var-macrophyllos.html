Quercus pyrenaica var. macrophyllos

Quercus pyrenaica var. macrophyllos, commonly known as the Pyrenean oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often lobed and have a glossy green upper surface. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a dense, woolly cap. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

This species plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
