Quercus longinux var. kuoi

Quercus longinux var. kuoi, commonly known as the Kuoi oak, is a rare and endemic species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and distinctive leaf morphology, which features a serrated margin and a broad, ovate shape. The tree typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in mountainous forests where it forms part of the understory vegetation.
