Quercus lusitanica subsp. orientalis

Quercus lusitanica subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Oriental Turkey Oak, is a subspecies of the Turkey Oak native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern Europe. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and deeply lobed leaves that turn reddish in autumn.

The bark is typically gray and fissured, providing a rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
