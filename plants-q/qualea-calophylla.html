Qualea calophylla

Qualea calophylla, commonly known as the white water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, white, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and plays a vital role in providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots help stabilize the sediment, contributing to the overall health of the ecosystem.

Qualea calophylla is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
