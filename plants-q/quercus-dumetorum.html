Quercus dumetorum

Quercus dumetorum, commonly known as the velvetleaf oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that turns reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically enclosed in a cupule. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Quercus dumetorum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
