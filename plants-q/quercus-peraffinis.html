Quercus peraffinis

Quercus peraffinis, commonly known as the close relative of the common oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere, particularly in Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and brown in the autumn. It produces small, spiny acorns that are typically found in clusters.

Quercus peraffinis is valued for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
