Quercus muehlenbergii var. alexanderi

Quercus muehlenbergii var. alexanderi, commonly known as Alexander's chestnut oak, is a variant of the chestnut oak native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its deep green, lobed leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
