Quercus garryana

Quercus garryana, commonly known as the Garry oak, is a deciduous tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. The tree typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and coastal dunes. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against fire and environmental stress.

Quercus garryana plays a vital role in maintaining biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
