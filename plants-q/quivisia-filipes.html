Quivisia filipes

Quivisia filipes, commonly known as the featherleaf quivisia, is a unique flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, elongated leaves that give it its common name, with a delicate, feathery appearance. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Quivisia filipes is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.
