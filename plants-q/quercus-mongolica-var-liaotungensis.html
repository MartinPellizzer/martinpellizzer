Quercus mongolica var. liaotungensis

Quercus mongolica var. liaotungensis, commonly known as the Liaotung oak, is a deciduous tree native to northeastern China.

It is characterized by its serrated leaves and produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. The tree thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mixed forests. It has a rugged, twisted trunk and can grow up to 20 meters tall.

This species is valued for its hard wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
