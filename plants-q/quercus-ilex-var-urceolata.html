Quercus ilex var. urceolata

Quercus ilex var. urceolata, commonly known as the evergreen oak, is a variant of the holm oak native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are elliptical and often have a distinctive urn-shaped base. The plant produces small, clustered acorns that are typically encased in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
