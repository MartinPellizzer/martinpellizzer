Quercus × diversiloba

Quercus × diversiloba, commonly known as the Arizona white oak, is a hybrid species of oak native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically white to pale green on the underside. The plant produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and woodlands.

The Arizona white oak plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
