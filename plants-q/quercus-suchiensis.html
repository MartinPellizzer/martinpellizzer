Quercus suchiensis

Quercus suchiensis, commonly known as the Suchi oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family, Fagaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe, particularly in the Carpathian Basin. This evergreen oak has small, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy green surface. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It is valued for its hardy nature and is often found in mixed forests with other oak species.
