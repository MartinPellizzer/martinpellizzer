Quercus × sargentii

Quercus × sargentii, commonly known as the Sargent oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between the white oak (Quercus alba) and the chestnut oak (Quercus prinus).

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The tree produces small, spiky acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in the eastern United States, particularly in forested areas.

Quercus × sargentii is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
