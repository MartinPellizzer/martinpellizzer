Quercus tungmaiensis

Quercus tungmaiensis, commonly known as the Tungmai oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family, Fagaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in parts of Thailand and Myanmar. The tree is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. It produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Quercus tungmaiensis is valued for its hard wood and is also studied for its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
