Quercus pseudosemecarpifolia

Quercus pseudosemecarpifolia, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces acorns, which are encased in a cupule and serve as a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts.
