Quercus infectoria var. microphylla

Quercus infectoria var. microphylla, commonly known as the African bracken fern or red-oak, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, acorn-like fruits that are important for both wildlife and local communities. Its bark contains tannins and has been used traditionally in various applications, including dyeing and medicinal purposes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
