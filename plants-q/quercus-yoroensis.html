Quercus yoroensis

Quercus yoroensis, commonly known as the Yoro oak, is a deciduous tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. It produces small, cupule-enclosed acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.
