Quercus runcinatifolia f. alata

Quercus runcinatifolia f. alata, commonly known as the winged oak, is a rare and distinctive variant of the runcinatifolia oak.

It is characterized by its unique winged acorns, which are elongated and have a prominent, membranous wing along one edge. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of southern China and is adapted to cool, moist climates. Its leaves are typically broad and lobed, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its striking appearance.
