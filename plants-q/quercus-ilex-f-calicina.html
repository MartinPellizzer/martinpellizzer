Quercus ilex f. calicina

Quercus ilex f. calicina, commonly known as the Spanish chestnut or evergreen oak, is a shrub-like form of the holm oak.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically smaller and more rounded compared to the typical species. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by edible acorns. It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

This form is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
