Quercus germanica var. macrobalana

Quercus germanica var. macrobalana, commonly known as the Persian oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

The bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against environmental stress.
