Quercus gambelii subsp. vreelandii

Quercus gambelii subsp.

vreelandii, commonly known as the Vreeland gambel oak, is a subspecies of the Gambel oak native to the southwestern United States. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its rough, fissured bark and acorn-producing capsules.

The leaves are typically oval to elliptical, with a doubly serrated margin and a glossy upper surface. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its adaptability to arid environments.
