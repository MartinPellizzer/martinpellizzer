Quercus sessiliflora var. sublobata

Quercus sessiliflora var. sublobata, commonly known as the Sierra foothill oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a rough texture. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local ecosystem and a subject of study in plant ecology.
