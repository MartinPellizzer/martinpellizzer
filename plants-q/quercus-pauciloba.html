Quercus pauciloba

Quercus pauciloba, commonly known as the Arizona white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn vibrant yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, spiky acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Quercus pauciloba typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its slow growth rate and longevity make it a significant component of the local ecosystem.
