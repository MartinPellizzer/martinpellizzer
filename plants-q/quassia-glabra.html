Quassia glabra

Quassia glabra, commonly known as the quassia tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is renowned for its fragrant, yellowish wood, which is used in the production of furniture and musical instruments. Its fruits are small, red berries that contain seeds, and the plant produces a distinctive, bitter sap.

Quassia glabra is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.
