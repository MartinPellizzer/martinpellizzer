Quercus brandegeei

Quercus brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegge oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, followed by acorns that are encased in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky habitats.

The Brandegge oak is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
