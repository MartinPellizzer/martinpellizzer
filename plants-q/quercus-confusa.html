Quercus confusa

Quercus confusa, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to China and Korea.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates.

Quercus confusa is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
