Quercus pedunculata var. microbalanus

Quercus pedunculata var. microbalanus, commonly known as the dwarf chinquapin, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and distinctive, clustered acorns. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bottomland hardwood forests. Its botanical features include a dense, compact growth habit and the ability to form thickets.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
