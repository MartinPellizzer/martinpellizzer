Quinquina obovata

Quinquina obovata, commonly known as the South American quinine tree, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate leaves and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The plant produces woody capsules containing small, dark seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it has attracted interest for its potential pharmacological properties.
