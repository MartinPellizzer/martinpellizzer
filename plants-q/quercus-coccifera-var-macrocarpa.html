Quercus coccifera var. macrocarpa

Quercus coccifera var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited rock oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, red or orange acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is well adapted to hot, dry climates.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
