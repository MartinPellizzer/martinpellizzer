Quercus suber var. subocculata

Quercus suber var. subocculata, commonly known as the Spanish oak, is a rare variant of the cork oak.

It is characterized by its thick, corky bark, which is adapted to withstand fire and drought. The leaves are typically small, leathery, and have a distinctive glossy appearance. It thrives in Mediterranean climates with well-drained soils.

This variety is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique genetic and morphological traits.
