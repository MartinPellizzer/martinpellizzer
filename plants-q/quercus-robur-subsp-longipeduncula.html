Quercus robur subsp. longipeduncula

Quercus robur subsp.

longipeduncula, commonly known as the English oak, is a subspecies of the common oak native to Europe. It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and robust, often sprawling growth habit.

The acorns are typically small and enclosed in cupules with bracts. This subspecies is known for its longevity and can live for several centuries.

It plays a significant role in supporting biodiversity in woodland ecosystems.
