Quercus × auzendei var. acuta

Quercus × auzendei var. acuta, commonly known as the acuteleaf chestnut oak, is a hybrid species within the oak genus Quercus.

It is characterized by its acute (sharply pointed) leaf tips and is native to the southeastern United States. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in mixed hardwood forests. Its leaves are alternate, with a serrated margin, and it produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
