Quercus lanuginosa var. montana

Quercus lanuginosa var. montana, commonly known as the Montana silky oak, is a variant of the silky oak species native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and small, acorn-like fruits. The leaves are typically elliptical with a serrated margin and a silvery underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
