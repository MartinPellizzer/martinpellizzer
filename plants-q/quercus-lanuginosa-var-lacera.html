Quercus lanuginosa var. lacera

Quercus lanuginosa var. lacera, commonly known as the hairy oak or the silky oak, is a variant of the silky oak native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray foliage, which consists of small, leathery leaves with a soft, downy texture. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically clustered in groups of two to four. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its unique appearance.
