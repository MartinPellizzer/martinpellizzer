Quassia trichilioides

Quassia trichilioides, commonly known as African quassia, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Simaroubaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits containing seeds. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
