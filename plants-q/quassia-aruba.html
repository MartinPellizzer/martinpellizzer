Quassia aruba

Quassia aruba, commonly known as the yellow quassia, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Simaroubaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale yellow bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. Quassia aruba is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly the bitter compounds found in its bark and roots.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
