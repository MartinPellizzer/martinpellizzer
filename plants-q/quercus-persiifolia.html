Quercus persiifolia

Quercus persiifolia, commonly known as the Persian oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Iran and Afghanistan.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

The botanical features of Quercus persiifolia make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
