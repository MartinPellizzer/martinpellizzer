Quercus robur var. subglobosa

Quercus robur var. subglobosa, commonly known as the globe oak, is a variant of the common oak characterized by its distinctive rounded, spherical crown.

It is a deciduous tree native to Europe, typically found in moist, well-drained soils. The leaves are broad, lobed, and turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. It produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often planted in parks and gardens.
