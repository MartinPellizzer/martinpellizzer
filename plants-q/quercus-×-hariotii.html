Quercus × hariotii

Quercus × hariotii, commonly known as the Hariot's oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus robur and Quercus sessilifolia.

It is characterized by its distinctive acorns, which are typically larger and more elongated compared to those of its parent species. The leaves of Quercus × hariotii are usually broad and lobed, with a serrated margin, and can vary in color from dark green to reddish in the autumn. This species is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
