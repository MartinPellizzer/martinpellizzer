Quercus pedunculata var. malacophylla

Quercus pedunculata var. malacophylla, commonly known as the Malacophylla oak, is a variant of the pedunculate oak native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are elongated and have a leathery texture with a glossy underside. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically harvested by animals such as squirrels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional landscapes.
