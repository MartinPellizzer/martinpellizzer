Quercus pseudomolucca var. crassinervia

Quercus pseudomolucca var. crassinervia, commonly known as the broad-veined oak, is a variant of the pseudomolucca oak native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its large, lobed leaves with prominent, broad veins. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically covered in a dense, hairy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

The botanical features of this variant make it notable for its ecological role and ornamental value in horticulture.
