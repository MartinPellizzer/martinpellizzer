Quercus falcata subsp. pagodifolia

Quercus falcata subsp.

pagodifolia, commonly known as the pagoda oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often curved and have a serrated margin.

The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are typically encased in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its unique leaf shape and ecological role make it an important species in its native range.
