Quinaria lansium

Quinaria lansium, commonly known as the rough-skin lime, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, acidic fruits. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary ingredient.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
