Quercus clementiana

Quercus clementiana, commonly known as the Clementian oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Mexico. This evergreen oak is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, acorn-like fruits. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
