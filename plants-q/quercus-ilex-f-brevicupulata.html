Quercus ilex f. brevicupulata

Quercus ilex f. brevicupulata, commonly known as the dwarf evergreen oak, is a compact variant of the holm oak.

It is characterized by its small, rounded acorns and dense, leathery foliage. This form typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing a slow growth rate and longevity. The plant exhibits evergreen habits with deep green, glossy leaves that remain throughout the year.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and hardy specimen suitable for small gardens or rockeries.
