Quercus hildebrandii

Quercus hildebrandii, commonly known as the Hildebrand oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, spiky acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations.

The Hildebrand oak is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
