Quercus megas

Quercus megas, commonly known as the large oak, is a species of deciduous tree belonging to the Fagaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces acorns, which are encased in a cupule and serve as a food source for various wildlife species. Quercus megas typically grows in temperate regions with well-drained soils.

Its sturdy structure and longevity make it a significant component of forest ecosystems.
