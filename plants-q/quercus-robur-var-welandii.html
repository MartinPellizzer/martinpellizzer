Quercus robur var. welandii

Quercus robur var. welandii, commonly known as the Welsh oak, is a variant of the common oak native to western Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn a vibrant yellow in autumn. The tree produces acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in forested areas and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ecological role and historical significance in European woodlands.
