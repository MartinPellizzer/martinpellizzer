Quisqualis conferta

Quisqualis conferta, commonly known as the Chinese flame flower, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling flames. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, fleshy coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and as a cut flower.
