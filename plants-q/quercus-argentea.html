Quercus argentea

Quercus argentea, commonly known as the silverleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a serrated margin. The tree produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
