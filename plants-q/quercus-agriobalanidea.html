Quercus agriobalanidea

Quercus agriobalanidea, commonly known as the black oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and acorn-producing capsules that are an important food source for wildlife. The leaves are typically lobed and turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

The tree plays a significant role in ecological systems, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
