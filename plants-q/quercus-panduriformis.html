Quercus panduriformis

Quercus panduriformis, commonly known as the broad-leafed oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests at moderate elevations.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of its native ecosystem.
