Quercus cyrtopoda

Quercus cyrtopoda, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and acorn-producing capsules. The leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped with a smooth margin and a short petiole. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in oak woodlands and cloud forests.
