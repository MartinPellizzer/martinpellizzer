Quercus lanuginosa var. budayana

Quercus lanuginosa var. budayana, commonly known as the silky oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that turn yellow in the fall, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, pale green acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and bottomlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat conservation.
