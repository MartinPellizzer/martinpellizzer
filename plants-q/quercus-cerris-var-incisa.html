Quercus cerris var. incisa

Quercus cerris var. incisa, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a variant of the common oak characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

The leaves are typically lobed and have a serrated margin, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes hairy underside. It is native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Balkans, thriving in temperate climates. The tree can reach heights of up to 30 meters and has a broad, spreading canopy.

Its acorns are small, edible, and serve as a food source for various wildlife species.
