Quercus brevistyla

Quercus brevistyla, commonly known as the narrowleaf chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Quercus brevistyla plays an important role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
