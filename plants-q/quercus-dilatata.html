Quercus dilatata

Quercus dilatata, commonly known as the spreading oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, spreading crown and lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, red acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and ornamental purposes.
