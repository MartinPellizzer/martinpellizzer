Quercus pseudomolucca var. korthalsii

Quercus pseudomolucca var. korthalsii, commonly known as the Korthals's evergreen oak, is a variant of the pseudomolucca oak native to the Moluccas in Indonesia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and acorn-like fruits. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. This species thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland to montane forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role within its native habitat.
