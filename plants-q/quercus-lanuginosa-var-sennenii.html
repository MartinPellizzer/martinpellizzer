Quercus lanuginosa var. sennenii

Quercus lanuginosa var. sennenii, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and hairy, grayish-brown leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.
