Quercus prinodes

Quercus prinodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is generally smooth and grayish-brown, with shallow fissures.

Quercus prinodes is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and inland habitats.
