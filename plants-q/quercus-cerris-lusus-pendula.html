Quercus cerris lusus pendula

Quercus cerris lusus pendula, commonly known as the weeping oak, is a unique variant of the common oak.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces acorns, which are a crucial food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are typically broad and lobed, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
