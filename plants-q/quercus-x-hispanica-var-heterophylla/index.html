Quercus × Hispanica Var. Heterophylla

Quercus × hispanica var. heterophylla is a unique hybrid oak species known for its striking variation in leaf shape. This plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in rocky and semi-arid environments. Its leaves range from narrow and elongated to broad and rounded, showcasing a fascinating adaptation to its habitat. The tree is often found in coastal areas where it withstands harsh conditions with remarkable resilience. Its distinct morphology makes it a subject of interest for botanists and nature enthusiasts alike.
