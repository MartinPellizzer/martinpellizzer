Quercus conjugens

Quercus conjugens, commonly known as the California black oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and acorns that are enclosed in a cupule. The leaves are typically lobed and have a broad, ovate shape, often with a hairy underside. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils in mixed coniferous forests.

This species plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
