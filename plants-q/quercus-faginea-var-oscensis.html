Quercus faginea var. oscensis

Quercus faginea var. oscensis, commonly known as the Pyrenean oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Pyrenees region.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
