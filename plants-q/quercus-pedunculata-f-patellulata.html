Quercus pedunculata f. patellulata

Quercus pedunculata f. patellulata, commonly known as the Patellulata form of the Chinese chestnut, is a variant of the species native to China.

It is characterized by its smaller, more rounded leaves compared to the typical form. The plant produces small, red-brown acorns that are often covered in a sticky resin. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
