Queltia × poculiformis

Queltia × poculiformis, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Queltia tenuifolia and Queltia tenuior.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, where it thrives in shaded, humid environments. It is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.

Queltia × poculiformis is typically propagated through division or seed, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
