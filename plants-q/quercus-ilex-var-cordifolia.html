Quercus ilex var. cordifolia

Quercus ilex var. cordifolia, commonly known as the Spanish oak, is a variant of the evergreen holm oak native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a wavy margin. The plant produces small, spiny acorns that are typically covered in a dense, hairy coating. It thrives in Mediterranean climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
