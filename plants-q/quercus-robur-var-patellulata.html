Quercus robur var. patellulata

Quercus robur var. patellulata, commonly known as the English oak var. patellulata, is a variant of the common oak characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It features leaves that are typically more rounded and shorter than those of the typical Quercus robur, with a unique shape that sets it apart. This variety is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
