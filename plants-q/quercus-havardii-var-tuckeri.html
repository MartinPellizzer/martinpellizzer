Quercus havardii var. tuckeri

Quercus havardii var. tuckeri, commonly known as the Tucker's live oak, is a variant of the live oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, evergreen leaves that are typically glossy and have a pointed tip. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and upland habitats.

The Tucker's live oak is valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand urban environments.
