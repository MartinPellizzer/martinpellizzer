Quercus accomodata

Quercus accomodata, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in dry to moist forests and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

The bark is grayish-brown and becomes fissured with age, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
