Quercus suecica

Quercus suecica, commonly known as the Swedish oak, is a deciduous tree native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and elongated, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Quercus suecica is valued for its ecological role and is also used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal.
