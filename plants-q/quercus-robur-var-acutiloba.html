Quercus robur var. acutiloba

Quercus robur var. acutiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oak, is a variant of the common oak characterized by its elongated, pointed leaves.

It is native to parts of Europe and is typically found in forested areas with well-drained soils. The plant produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is rugged and becomes fissured with age, adding to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to different environmental conditions.
