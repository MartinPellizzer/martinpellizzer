Qaisera

Qaisera, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Middle East, is a striking aquatic plant with large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes, where its broad, heart-shaped leaves float on the water's surface. The plant's roots are submerged, anchoring it in the mud and absorbing nutrients from the water. Its flowers open during the day and close at night, a behavior known as nyctinasty.

Qaisera has been culturally significant for centuries, symbolizing purity and spiritual enlightenment in various traditions.
