Quercus germana subsp. eugermana

Quercus germana subsp.

eugermana, commonly known as the German oak, is a subspecies of the common oak native to Central Europe. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its elongated, acorn-like fruits and deeply lobed leaves.

The leaves typically have 5 to 7 lobes and are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of ecological study.
