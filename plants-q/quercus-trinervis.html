Quercus trinervis

Quercus trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family, Fagaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and its leaves, which have three distinct veins running from the base to the tip. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in lowland forests.

Quercus trinervis is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
