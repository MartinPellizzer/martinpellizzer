Quercus corrugata var. granulifera

Quercus corrugata var. granulifera, commonly known as the granulated canyon oak, is a variant of the canyon oak native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, acorn-like fruits. The leaves are typically narrow and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive bluish-green color. This plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
