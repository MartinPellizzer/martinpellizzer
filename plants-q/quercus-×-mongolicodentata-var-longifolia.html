Quercus × mongolicodentata var. longifolia

Quercus × mongolicodentata var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved Mongolian oak, is a hybrid species native to northern China and Mongolia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its elongated, serrated leaves that are typically 5–10 cm in length. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

The botanical features of this variety make it an important component of the regional flora and ecological systems.
