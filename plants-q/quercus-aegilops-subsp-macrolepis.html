Quercus aegilops subsp. macrolepis

Quercus aegilops subsp.

macrolepis, commonly known as the Persian oak, is a deciduous tree native to regions of western Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are elongated and have a serrated margin.

The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically covered in a dense, hairy coating. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
