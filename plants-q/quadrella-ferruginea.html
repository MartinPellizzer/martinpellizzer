Quadrella ferruginea

Quadrella ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty quadrella, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its common name derives from the rust-colored markings on its stems and leaves.
