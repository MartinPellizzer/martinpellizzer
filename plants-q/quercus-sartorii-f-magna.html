Quercus sartorii f. magna

Quercus sartorii f. magna, commonly known as the giant form of the common chestnut oak, is a variant of the Quercus sartorii species.

It is characterized by its larger leaves and more robust growth compared to the typical form. This subspecies is native to the eastern United States, particularly in the Appalachian region. Its leaves are typically elongated and have a coarsely serrated margin.

The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.
