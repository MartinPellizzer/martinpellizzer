Quercus atropurpurea

Quercus atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple to red-brown bark and acorns that are enclosed in a cupule. The leaves are typically oval or elliptical with serrated edges and turn vibrant red or purple in the fall. It prefers well-drained soils and can thrive in a range of light conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
