Quesnelia koltesii

Quesnelia koltesii, commonly known as the Koltesii bamboo, is a unique species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that resemble bamboo. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
