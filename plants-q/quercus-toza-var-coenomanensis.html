Quercus toza var. coenomanensis

Quercus toza var. coenomanensis, commonly known as the Coenomanian oak, is a rare and ancient variant of the oak genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are typically lobed and have a serrated margin. The plant is native to specific regions in Europe, where it thrives in temperate climates. It produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a botanically significant and ecologically valuable species.
