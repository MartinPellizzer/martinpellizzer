Quercus kurzii

Quercus kurzii, commonly known as the Kurz's oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Quercus kurzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
