Quercus carlesii

Quercus carlesii, commonly known as the Carlsbad oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and broad, lobed leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The tree typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against drought and fire.

Quercus carlesii plays a vital role in local ecosystems by supporting wildlife and contributing to soil stability.
