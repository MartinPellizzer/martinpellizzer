Quercus castanoides

Quercus castanoides, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by spiny, cup-like fruits containing one to three chestnut-like seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
