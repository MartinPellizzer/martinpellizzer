Quercus tennesi

Quercus tennesi, commonly known as the Tennessee oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its lobed leaves and acorn-producing fruit. The tree typically grows to a height of 20-40 feet, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its bark is gray and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

Quercus tennesi thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
