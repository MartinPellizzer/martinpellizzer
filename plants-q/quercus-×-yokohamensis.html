Quercus × yokohamensis

Quercus × yokohamensis, commonly known as the Yokohama oak, is a hybrid evergreen oak native to Japan.

It exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Quercus acutifolia and Quercus myrsinifolia, resulting in a unique combination of traits. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green, with a leathery texture. It produces small, clustered acorns that are typically covered in a dense, hairy cap.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dense foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
