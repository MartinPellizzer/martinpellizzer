Quercus san-sabeana

Quercus san-sabeana, commonly known as the San Sabeana oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense foliage and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

The San Sabeana oak plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
