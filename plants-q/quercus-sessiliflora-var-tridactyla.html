Quercus sessiliflora var. tridactyla

Quercus sessiliflora var. tridactyla, commonly known as the California black oak, is a variant of the black oak species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and acorns that are typically larger and more robust than those of other oak varieties. The leaves are generally broad, lobed, and have a distinctive shape with three to five lobes, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils in dry to moist environments.

This plant plays a vital role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for numerous wildlife species.
