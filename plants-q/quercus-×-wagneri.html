Quercus × wagneri

Quercus × wagneri, commonly known as the Wagner oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus garryi and Quercus lobata.

It is a deciduous tree native to California, characterized by its lobed leaves and acorns that are typically enclosed in cups. The plant exhibits a range of leaf shapes and sizes, reflecting its hybrid nature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The Wagner oak is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its attractive form and seasonal changes.
