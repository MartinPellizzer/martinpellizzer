Quercus ettingeri

Quercus ettingeri, commonly known as the Ettinger oak, is a species of deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. Its acorns are typically small and have a cupule, a distinctive cup-like structure that encases the nut.

Quercus ettingeri plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
