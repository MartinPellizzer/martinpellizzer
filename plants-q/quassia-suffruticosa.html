Quassia suffruticosa

Quassia suffruticosa, commonly known as the African quassia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antipyretic and antiseptic properties.

Quassia suffruticosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
