Quercus marilandica f. incisa

Quercus marilandica f. incisa, commonly known as the Maryland oak, is a variant of the Maryland oak native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive lobed leaves, which are often deeply incised, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 20 meters. Its acorns are edible and are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The bark is thick and fissured, providing some resistance to pests and diseases.
