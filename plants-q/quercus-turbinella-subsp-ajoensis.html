Quercus turbinella subsp. ajoensis

Quercus turbinella subsp.

ajoensis, commonly known as the Ajoensis Turkey Oak, is a subspecies of the Turkey Oak native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

The tree produces small, pale yellow to reddish-brown acorns that are encased in a cupule. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The subspecies is notable for its distinctive foliage and its role in supporting local wildlife.
