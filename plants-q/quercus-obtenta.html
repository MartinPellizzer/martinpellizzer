Quercus obtenta

Quercus obtenta, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that remain green throughout the year. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in areas with well-drained soils.

Quercus obtenta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
