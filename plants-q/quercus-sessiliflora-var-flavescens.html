Quercus sessiliflora var. flavescens

Quercus sessiliflora var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellowleaf chinquapin, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-brown bark and elliptical, glossy leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in the spring, followed by chestnut-like fruits that mature in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
