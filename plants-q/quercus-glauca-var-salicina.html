Quercus glauca var. salicina

Quercus glauca var. salicina, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and landscape horticulture.
