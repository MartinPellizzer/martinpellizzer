Quercus lusitanica subsp. cerrioides

Quercus lusitanica subsp.

cerrioides, commonly known as the Iberian chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to southwestern Europe. It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and deeply lobed leaves that turn reddish in autumn.

The species thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mixed forests. It has a slow growth rate and can live for several centuries, making it an important component of its native ecosystems.

Its wood is hard and durable, historically used for construction and tool-making.
