Qualea fasciculata

Qualea fasciculata, commonly known as the water lily of the Amazon, is a aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide shade and habitat for aquatic life. The plant has a short, thick stem that supports its round, waxy leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pink, with a distinctive cup-shaped structure that helps in pollination.

Qualea fasciculata plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in wetland ecosystems.
