Quercus chrysocalyx

Quercus chrysocalyx, commonly known as the golden cup oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and its striking golden-yellow foliage, which turns reddish in the fall. It typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
