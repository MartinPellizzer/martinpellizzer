Quercus appenina subsp. amplifolia

Quercus appenina subsp.

amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved rock oak, is a subspecies native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically lobed or serrated.

The plant produces small, pale yellow to brown flowers in the spring, followed by acorns that mature in the fall. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and ornamental appeal in horticulture.
