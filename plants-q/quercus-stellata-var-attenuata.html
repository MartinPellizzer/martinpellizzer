Quercus stellata var. attenuata

Quercus stellata var. attenuata, commonly known as the narrowleaf chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. The plant produces small, red to brown acorns that mature in the second year, providing food for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in upland forests.

The tree has a moderate growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.
