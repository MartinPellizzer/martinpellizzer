Quercus mulleri

Quercus mulleri, commonly known as the Chinese red oak, is a species of deciduous oak native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive acorns, which are typically covered in a dense layer of short, stiff hairs. The leaves are usually oval to elliptical in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its hard wood and is also studied for its ecological role in native habitats.
