Quisqualis parviflora

Quisqualis parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered Chinese lantern, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendant, red-orange or orange-red flowers that resemble small lanterns, which bloom in clusters. The plant is a woody vine with smooth, green stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers emit a sweet, fragrant aroma, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
