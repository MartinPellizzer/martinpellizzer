Quercus silvicolarum

Quercus silvicolarum, commonly known as the red oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its deeply lobed leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. The tree produces small, reddish-brown acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and ridged, providing structural support and protection against pests and diseases.

Quercus silvicolarum plays a vital role in forest ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
