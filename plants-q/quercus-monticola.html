Quercus monticola

Quercus monticola, commonly known as the mountain oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its acorns are typically small and enclosed in a cupule, providing food for various wildlife species.
