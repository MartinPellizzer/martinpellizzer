Quercus cerris subf. acutiloba

Quercus cerris subf.

acutiloba, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a subspecies of the common oak characterized by its distinctive acorn shape and dense, broad leaves. It is native to the Balkans and parts of Anatolia, where it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a broad, rounded crown and thick, fissured bark. Its leaves are typically lobed and turn reddish-brown in the autumn, adding to its ornamental value.

This subspecies is valued for its durability and is often used in landscaping and as a source of timber.
