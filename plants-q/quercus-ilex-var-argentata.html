Quercus ilex var. argentata

Quercus ilex var. argentata, commonly known as the silver leaf evergreen oak, is a variant of the holm oak characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France, where it thrives in Mediterranean climates. The plant is evergreen, with leathery, dark green upper surfaces and a silvery, fuzzy appearance on the underside. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are important for wildlife and can be used in traditional herbal remedies.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitats.
