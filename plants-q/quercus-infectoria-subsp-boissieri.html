Quercus infectoria subsp. boissieri

Quercus infectoria subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the Persian ironwood, is a subspecies of the common oak native to the mountains of western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay.

The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside.

This subspecies is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in rocky soils and high altitudes.
