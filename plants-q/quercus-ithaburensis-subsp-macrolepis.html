Quercus ithaburensis subsp. macrolepis

Quercus ithaburensis subsp.

macrolepis, commonly known as the large-scale rock oak, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often pubescent on the underside.

The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
