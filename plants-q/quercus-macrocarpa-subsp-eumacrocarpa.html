Quercus macrocarpa subsp. eumacrocarpa

Quercus macrocarpa subsp.

eumacrocarpa, commonly known as the bur oak, is a large deciduous tree native to the central United States. It is characterized by its deeply lobed leaves and distinctive, spiny acorns that are typically 1 to 2 inches in length.

The tree produces a thick, flaky bark that becomes deeply furrowed with age, providing a rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along river valleys.

This subspecies is notable for its longevity, with some individuals living over 200 years.
