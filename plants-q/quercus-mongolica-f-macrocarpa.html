Quercus mongolica f. macrocarpa

Quercus mongolica f. macrocarpa, commonly known as the Mongolian oak, is a variant of the Mongolian oak tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, acorn-like fruits, which are typically larger than those of the typical species. The leaves are usually broad and lobed, with a rough, textured surface. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and forest ecosystems.
