Quercus libani var. inermis

Quercus libani var. inermis, commonly known as the Lebanese oak, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.
