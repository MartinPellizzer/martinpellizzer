Quercus ilex f. pendula

Quercus ilex f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping evergreen oak, is a unique variant of the holm oak.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the tree year-round, contributing to its evergreen nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
