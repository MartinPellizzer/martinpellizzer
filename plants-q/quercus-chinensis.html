Quercus chinensis

Quercus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese oak, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its sturdy, erect trunk and broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic conditions.

Quercus chinensis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
