Quercus pubescens var. microcarpa

Quercus pubescens var. microcarpa, commonly known as the Spanish oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and hairy, grayish-brown bark. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and a rough texture. It thrives in Mediterranean climates with well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
