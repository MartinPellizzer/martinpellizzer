Quercus tomentella var. conjungens

Quercus tomentella var. conjungens, commonly known as the coastal scrub oak, is a shrub-like evergreen species native to coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage and small, acorn-like fruits. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with frequent fog and mild temperatures. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it a key component of coastal plant communities.
