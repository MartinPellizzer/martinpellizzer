Quercus infectoria var. leptocarpa

Quercus infectoria var. leptocarpa, commonly known as the African bracken fern or African oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, acorn-like fruits that are a food source for various wildlife. Its bark and leaves contain tannins, which have been utilized for tanning leather and in herbal remedies.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
