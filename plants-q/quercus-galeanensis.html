Quercus galeanensis

Quercus galeanensis, commonly known as the Galeana oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, leathery leaves. The tree produces small, reddish-brown acorns that are encased in a cupule. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
