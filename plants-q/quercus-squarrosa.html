Quercus squarrosa

Quercus squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, spreading crown and small, acorn-like fruits that are typically borne in clusters. The leaves are typically oval or elliptic, with a serrated margin and a glossy upper surface. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
