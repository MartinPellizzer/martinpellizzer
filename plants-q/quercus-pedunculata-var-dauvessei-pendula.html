Quercus pedunculata var. dauvessei-pendula

Quercus pedunculata var. dauvessei-pendula, commonly known as the weeping chinquapin, is a unique variant of the chinquapin oak.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in areas of Georgia and South Carolina. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth margin and a dark green color.

The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
