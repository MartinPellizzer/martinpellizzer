Quercus banga

Quercus banga, commonly known as the Banga oak, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The tree produces small, chestnut-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Quercus banga typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in humid climates.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in traditional construction and furniture-making.
