Quercus sonchifolia

Quercus sonchifolia, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The plant is characterized by its serrated, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.
