Quercus viridis

Quercus viridis, commonly known as the green oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and its acorns are typically enclosed in a cupule. The tree thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests. Quercus viridis has a slow growth rate and can live for over 200 years, making it a long-lived species.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
