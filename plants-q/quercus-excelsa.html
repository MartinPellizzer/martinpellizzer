Quercus excelsa

Quercus excelsa, commonly known as the African white oak, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a rounded crown, often reaching heights of up to 30 meters. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, typically appearing bronze to reddish in color during the growing season. Its acorns are small, enclosed in a cupule, and serve as a food source for various wildlife.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in local crafts and construction.
