Quercus incrassata

Quercus incrassata, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically pale green on the underside. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Quercus incrassata grows in a variety of habitats, including dry forests and mountainous regions.

Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture-making.
