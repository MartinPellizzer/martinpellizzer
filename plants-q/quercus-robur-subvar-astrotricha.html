Quercus robur subvar. astrotricha

Quercus robur subvar. astrotricha, commonly known as the star-leaf oak, is a rare variant of the common oak characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are broader and more lobed than those of typical Q. robur, with a unique star-like pattern on the underside. The plant is native to specific regions in Europe, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
