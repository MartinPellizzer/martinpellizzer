Quercus seemannii var. tonduzii

Quercus seemannii var. tonduzii, commonly known as the Tonduzii oak, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of northern Vietnam.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are enclosed in a dense, woody cupule, a feature common among oaks. It thrives in humid, temperate climates and is often found in secondary forests and rocky slopes.
