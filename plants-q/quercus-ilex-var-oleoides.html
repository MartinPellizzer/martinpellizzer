Quercus ilex var. oleoides

Quercus ilex var. oleoides, commonly known as the Algerian oak, is a variant of the evergreen oak native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. The plant is adapted to Mediterranean climates, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It has a slow growth rate and can live for several centuries, making it a long-lived and resilient species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of its native ecosystems.
