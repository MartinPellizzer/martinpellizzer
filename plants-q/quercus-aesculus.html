Quercus aesculus

Quercus aesculus, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula and parts of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces spiny, capsule-like fruits containing one or two smooth, shiny seeds. Its bark is typically smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
