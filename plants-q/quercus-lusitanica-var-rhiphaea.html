Quercus lusitanica var. rhiphaea

Quercus lusitanica var. rhiphaea, commonly known as the Rhiphaea oak, is a variant of the Iberian oak native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and broad, lobed leaves that are typically hairy beneath. This species thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. The leaves turn reddish-brown in autumn, adding to its ornamental value.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining soil stability in its native habitat.
