Quercus glauca f. lacera

Quercus glauca f. lacera, commonly known as the lacera form of the Chinese white oak, is a variant of the species Quercus glauca.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often twisted and have a bluish-green hue. This form is native to regions of China and is typically found in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub, often growing in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
