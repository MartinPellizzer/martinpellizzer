Quercus oxycarpa

Quercus oxycarpa, commonly known as the sharp-toothed oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its acorns, which are encased in a cupule with sharp, bristly teeth. The leaves are typically elliptical or oblong, with a rough, hairy upper surface and a smooth underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and canyons.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
