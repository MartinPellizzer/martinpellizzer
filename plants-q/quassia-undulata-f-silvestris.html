Quassia undulata f. silvestris

Quassia undulata f. silvestris, commonly known as the wild quassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Simaroubaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its medicinal properties, with its bark and roots containing quassinoids, which have anti-inflammatory and antiparasitic effects. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ecological importance and traditional use, conservation efforts are necessary to protect its natural habitat.
