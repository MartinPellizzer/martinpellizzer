Quercus × beguinotii

Quercus × beguinotii, commonly known as the Beguinotii oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus robur and Quercus petraea.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically encased in a cupule. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas across Europe.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
