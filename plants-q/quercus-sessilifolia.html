Quercus sessilifolia

Quercus sessilifolia, commonly known as the Florida hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, nut-like fruits that are enclosed in a cupule.

Its acorns are an important food source for various wildlife species, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
