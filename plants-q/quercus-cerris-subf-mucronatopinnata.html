Quercus cerris subf. mucronatopinnata

Quercus cerris subf.

mucronatopinnata, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a subspecies of the common oak characterized by its distinctive foliage and growth habits. It features broad, lobed leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath.

The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions of the Balkans and Turkey.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in forest ecosystems.
