Quercus macrocarpa subsp. oliviformis

Quercus macrocarpa subsp.

oliviformis, commonly known as the bur oak, is a deciduous tree native to North America. It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall and its distinctive, spiny acorns.

The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a broad, spreading crown. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is often used in landscaping and reforestation efforts.
