Quercus glauca subsp. longinux

Quercus glauca subsp.

longinux, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. It is characterized by its glaucous, grayish-blue leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin.

The tree produces small, chestnut-like fruits that contain one to three acorns, which are an important food source for wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
