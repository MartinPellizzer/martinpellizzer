Quercus erythrochlamys

Quercus erythrochlamys, commonly known as the red-chalazal oak, is a species of deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its oval to elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is rugged and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.

Quercus erythrochlamys is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
