Quercus × andegavensis subsp. henriquesii

Quercus × andegavensis subsp.

henriquesii, commonly known as the Henriques' oak, is a hybrid species native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the genus Quercus, which includes various species of oaks, and is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

The leaves are typically lobed and have a serrated margin, reflecting its hybrid origin. This subspecies is often found in rocky or mountainous regions, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a natural hybrid, illustrating the genetic diversity within the Quercus genus.
