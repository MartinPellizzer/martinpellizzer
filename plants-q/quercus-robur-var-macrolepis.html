Quercus robur var. macrolepis

Quercus robur var. macrolepis, commonly known as the Irish oak, is a variant of the common oak characterized by its distinctive foliage and growth habits.

It is native to western Europe, particularly Ireland, where it thrives in temperate climates. The leaves are typically larger and more elongated compared to the standard Quercus robur, with a coarsely toothed margin. This variety is known for its robust growth and ability to form dense canopies, making it a valuable species in forest ecosystems.

Its acorns are also an important food source for various wildlife species.
