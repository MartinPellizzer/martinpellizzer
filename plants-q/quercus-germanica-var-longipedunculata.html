Quercus germanica var. longipedunculata

Quercus germanica var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and elongated peduncles that bear clusters of acorns. The leaves are typically oval to elliptical, with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in spring, followed by acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
