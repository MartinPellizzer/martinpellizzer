Quercus rhioensis

Quercus rhioensis, commonly known as the Rio Grande oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its lobed leaves, which are typically 5 to 7 inches long and have a serrated margin. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and riparian habitats.

Quercus rhioensis is valued for its ecological role and is also used in horticulture for its ornamental features.
