Quercus pagoda var. leucophylla

Quercus pagoda var. leucophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf pagoda tree, is a variant of the pagoda tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, layered foliage that resembles a pagoda, with leaves that are silver-white on the underside and green on top. The plant is a deciduous tree, shedding its leaves in the fall, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers in the spring. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it often has a rounded, spreading canopy.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic features.
