Quercus glabra var. micrococca

Quercus glabra var. micrococca, commonly known as the dwarf chestnut oak, is a small, slow-growing deciduous tree or shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spiny acorns that are often covered in a whitish, fuzzy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental landscaping.
