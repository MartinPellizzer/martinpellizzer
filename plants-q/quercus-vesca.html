Quercus vesca

Quercus vesca, commonly known as the common sorrel or wood sorrel, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Fagaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being heart-shaped with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by edible, red-orange berries. Quercus vesca is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, often found in woodland edges and open areas.

It is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
