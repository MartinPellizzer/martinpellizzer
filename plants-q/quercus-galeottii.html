Quercus galeottii

Quercus galeottii, commonly known as the Florida chinquapin, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces small, spiny fruits called cups that enclose its edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streams.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local ecosystem, supporting various wildlife species.
