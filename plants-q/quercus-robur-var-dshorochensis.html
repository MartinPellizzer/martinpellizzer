Quercus robur var. dshorochensis

Quercus robur var. dshorochensis, commonly known as the Dshorochensky oak, is a rare variant of the English oak.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring a unique combination of lobes and serrations. The plant exhibits a robust growth habit with a broad, spreading canopy. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a botanically significant and ecologically valuable species.
