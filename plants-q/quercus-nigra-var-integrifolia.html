Quercus nigra var. integrifolia

Quercus nigra var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth black oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, smooth bark and alternate, ovate leaves with smooth margins. The tree produces small, red-orange acorns that are encased in a cupule. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
