Quercus pubescens var. glomerata

Quercus pubescens var. glomerata, commonly known as the Spanish oak, is a variant of the downy oak.

It is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France. The plant features broad, lobed leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. It produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

The tree is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
