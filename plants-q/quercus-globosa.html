Quercus globosa

Quercus globosa, commonly known as the globe oak, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spherical crown and small, acorn-like fruits that are typically clustered in groups of two to four. The leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a smooth margin, turning yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional landscaping.
