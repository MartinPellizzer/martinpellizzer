Quercus sessiliflora var. cerrioides

Quercus sessiliflora var. cerrioides, commonly known as the California madrone, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and clusters of bright red berries that persist through winter. The leaves are broad, elliptical, and have a glossy upper surface with a dull underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its unique appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
