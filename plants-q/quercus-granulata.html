Quercus granulata

Quercus granulata, commonly known as the granulate oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The tree is characterized by its rough, scaly bark and leaves that are typically elliptical with a finely toothed margin. Its small, clustered flowers are followed by acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The plant plays a vital role in its ecosystem, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
