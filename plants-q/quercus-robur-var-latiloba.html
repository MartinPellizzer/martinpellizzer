Quercus robur var. latiloba

Quercus robur var. latiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaved oak, is a variant of the common oak characterized by its broader, more rounded leaves.

It is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, featuring a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath, turning yellow or brown in autumn.

The acorns produced by this species are an important food source for various wildlife species.
