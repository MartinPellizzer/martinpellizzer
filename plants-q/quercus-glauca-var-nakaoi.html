Quercus glauca var. nakaoi

Quercus glauca var. nakaoi, commonly known as the Nakao's blue oak, is a variant of the blue oak native to Japan.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree characterized by its bluish-gray foliage and acorns. The leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
