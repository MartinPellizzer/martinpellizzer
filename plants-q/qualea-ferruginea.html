Qualea ferruginea

Qualea ferruginea, commonly known as the red-barked or bloodwood tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark, which peels in thin, papery layers. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family and is known for its large, palmate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Qualea ferruginea is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been historically used in construction and craftsmanship.
