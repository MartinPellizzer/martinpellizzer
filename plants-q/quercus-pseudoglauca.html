Quercus pseudoglauca

Quercus pseudoglauca, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and deeply lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree has a broad, spreading crown and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
