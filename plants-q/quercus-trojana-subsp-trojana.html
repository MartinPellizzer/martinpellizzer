Quercus trojana subsp. trojana

Quercus trojana subsp.

trojana, commonly known as the Trojan oak, is a rare and endemic species native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and deeply lobed leaves.

The species is notable for its unique genetic makeup and limited geographic distribution. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils, often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its rarity, it is considered a conservation priority in its native habitat.
