Quercus turbinella

Quercus turbinella, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central and southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are enclosed in a dense, scaly cap. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

Quercus turbinella plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
