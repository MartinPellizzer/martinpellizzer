Quercus gambelii var. bonina

Quercus gambelii var. bonina, commonly known as Gambel's oak, is a variant of the Gambel oak species native to the southwestern United States.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its elongated, lobed leaves and acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. The plant exhibits a range of growth forms, from small shrubs to tall trees, depending on environmental conditions. Its leaves turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall, contributing to its ornamental value.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
