Qualea cassiquiarensis var. belemnensis

Qualea cassiquiarensis var. belemnensis, commonly known as the Belemnopsis palm, is a unique species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and long, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, which are pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe containing a single seed.

This species is adapted to wetland environments and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of the region.
