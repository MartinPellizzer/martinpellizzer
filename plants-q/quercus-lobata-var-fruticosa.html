Quercus lobata var. fruticosa

Quercus lobata var. fruticosa, commonly known as the coastal live oak, is a shrub-like variant of the valley oak.

It is native to coastal regions of California and thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically lobed and have a dark green upper surface. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

This variety is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.
