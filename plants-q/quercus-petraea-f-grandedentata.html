Quercus petraea f. grandedentata

Quercus petraea f. grandedentata, commonly known as the grand-toothed rock oak, is a distinctive variant of the sessile oak.

It is characterized by its large, coarsely toothed leaves that give it a unique appearance. This form is typically found in rocky or mountainous regions where it thrives in well-drained soils. The leaves are usually dark green on top and paler underneath, with a leathery texture.

It is a hardy plant that contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
