Quercus setulosa subsp. laotica

Quercus setulosa subsp.

laotica, commonly known as the Laotian bristly oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a bristly texture.

The plant produces acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule, a feature common among oaks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions of Laos and Vietnam.
