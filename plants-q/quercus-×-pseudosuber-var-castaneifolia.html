Quercus × pseudosuber var. castaneifolia

Quercus × pseudosuber var. castaneifolia, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut oak, is a hybrid species within the Fagaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in the spring, followed by edible acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological contributions in forest ecosystems.
