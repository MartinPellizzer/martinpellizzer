Queltia × poculigera

Queltia × poculigera, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring cross between Queltia saccata and Queltia poculigera.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in two rows along the stem, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Queltia × poculigera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
