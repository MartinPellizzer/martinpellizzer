Quercus pubescens var. appenina

Quercus pubescens var. appenina, commonly known as the Apennine oak, is a variant of the downy oak native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

This variant is valued for its ecological role and is also used in ornamental horticulture.
