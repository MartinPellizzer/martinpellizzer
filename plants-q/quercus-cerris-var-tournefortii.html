Quercus cerris var. tournefortii

Quercus cerris var. tournefortii, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a variant of the common oak native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against environmental stressors.

This species is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
