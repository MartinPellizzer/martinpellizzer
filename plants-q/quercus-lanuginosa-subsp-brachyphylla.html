Quercus lanuginosa subsp. brachyphylla

Quercus lanuginosa subsp.

brachyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its small, ovate to elliptical leaves that are hairy on both surfaces, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically enclosed in a cupule, which is often covered with dense, velvety hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
