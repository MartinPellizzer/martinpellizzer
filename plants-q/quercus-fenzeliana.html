Quercus fenzeliana

Quercus fenzeliana, commonly known as the Fenzel's oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in humid, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
