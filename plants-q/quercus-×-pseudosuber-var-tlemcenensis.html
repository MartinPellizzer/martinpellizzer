Quercus × pseudosuber var. tlemcenensis

Quercus × pseudosuber var. tlemcenensis, commonly known as the Tlemcen oak, is a hybrid species native to the Mediterranean region, particularly in Algeria.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing habitat for insects and lichens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
