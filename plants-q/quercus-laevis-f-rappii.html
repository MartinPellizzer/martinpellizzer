Quercus laevis f. rappii

Quercus laevis f. rappii, commonly known as the Rapp's white oak, is a variant of the white oak species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces small, spiny acorns that mature over one to two years. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in moist, upland forests.

This form is noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
