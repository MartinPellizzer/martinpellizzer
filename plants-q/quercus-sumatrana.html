Quercus sumatrana

Quercus sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra.

It is a tall tree, often reaching heights of up to 30 meters, with a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are typically broad-ovate, leathery, and have a glossy green upper surface. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also significant in local ecosystems and traditional practices.
