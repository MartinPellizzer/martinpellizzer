Quercus alba var. gunnisonii

Quercus alba var. gunnisonii, commonly known as Gunnison's white oak, is a variant of the white oak native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The tree produces small, dense acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

This variety is known for its slow growth and longevity, often living over 200 years.
