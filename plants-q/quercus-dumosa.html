Quercus dumosa

Quercus dumosa, commonly known as the buckthorn oak, is a shrub-like species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Quercus dumosa is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
