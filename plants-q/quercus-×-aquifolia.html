Quercus × aquifolia

Quercus × aquifolia, commonly known as the Japanese oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus acutifolia and Quercus myrtifolia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves that are often pointed and have a serrated margin. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are enclosed in a cupule, and it is native to southern Japan.

Quercus × aquifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
