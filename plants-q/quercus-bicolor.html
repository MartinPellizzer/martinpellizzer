Quercus bicolor

Quercus bicolor, commonly known as the swamp white oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, spiky, brown acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its bark is typically gray and becomes fissured with age.

Quercus bicolor thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland areas.
