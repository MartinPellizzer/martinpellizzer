Quercus toza var. spicata

Quercus toza var. spicata, commonly known as the spiculate oak, is a variant of the toza oak native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and narrow, elongated leaves with a distinctive spiny texture. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in humid, temperate climates. Its leaves often display a dark green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes hairy underside.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
