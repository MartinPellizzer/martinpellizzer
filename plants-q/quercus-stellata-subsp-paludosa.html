Quercus stellata subsp. paludosa

Quercus stellata subsp.

paludosa, commonly known as the swamp chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and bottomland hardwood forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of riparian and wetland ecosystems.
