Qualea pulcherrima

Qualea pulcherrima, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Qualea pulcherrima is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
