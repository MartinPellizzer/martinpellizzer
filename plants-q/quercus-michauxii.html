Quercus michauxii

Quercus michauxii, commonly known as the Michaux's oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in bottomlands and along streams.

Quercus michauxii is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal in landscaping.
