Quasiantennaria linearifolia

Quasiantennaria linearifolia, commonly known as the slender linearleaf quasiantennaria, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky soils and gravelly habitats. The plant features linear, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in compact clusters, typical of the daisy family.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
