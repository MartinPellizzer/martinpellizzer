Quercus liaotungensis f. funebris

Quercus liaotungensis f. funebris, commonly known as the black oak variant, is a rare and distinctive subspecies of the Liaotung oak.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and deeply lobed leaves that give it a striking appearance. This plant is native to specific regions in China, where it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its botanical features make it an important subject in studies of oak diversity and adaptation.

The subspecies is noted for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential in reforestation efforts.
