Quercus × kanitziana f. apatini

Quercus × kanitziana f. apatini, commonly known as the Apatin oak, is a hybrid species within the genus Quercus, characterized by its unique botanical features.

It exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species, Quercus castaneifolia and Quercus frainetto, resulting in a distinctive morphology. The plant typically features small, acorn-like fruits and dark, glossy leaves with a serrated margin. It is native to the mountainous regions of the Carpathian Basin, where it thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
