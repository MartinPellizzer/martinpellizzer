Quercus phullata

Quercus phullata, commonly known as the Kashmir oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and moderate rainfall.

The Kashmir oak is valued for its ecological role and is often found in mixed forests at higher elevations.
