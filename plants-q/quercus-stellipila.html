Quercus stellipila

Quercus stellipila, commonly known as the starleaf oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China. This deciduous tree is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped leaves, which are typically 5-7 lobed. The leaves are usually dark green on top and pale underneath, turning yellow or red in the fall.

It produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
