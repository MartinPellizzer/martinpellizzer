Quesnelia blanda

Quesnelia blanda, commonly known as the Brazilian fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky or soil substrates.

Quesnelia blanda is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
