Quercus umbrosa

Quercus umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its ability to thrive in shaded environments. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a leathery texture and a pointed tip. It produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
