Quercus serrata var. longicarpa

Quercus serrata var. longicarpa, commonly known as the long-acorn chinquapin, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated acorns, which are often twice as long as they are wide. The leaves are typically lobed and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

It plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
