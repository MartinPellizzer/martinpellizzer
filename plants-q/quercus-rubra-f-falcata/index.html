Quercus Rubra F. Falcata

Quercus rubra f. falcata is a unique variant of the red oak known for its distinctive leaf shape. This form features deeply lobed leaves that give it a striking appearance in the forest understory. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in eastern North America. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance. Its presence adds both beauty and biodiversity to natural landscapes.
