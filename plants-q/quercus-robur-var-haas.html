Quercus robur var. haas

Quercus robur var. haas, commonly known as the Haas oak, is a variant of the common oak native to Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn reddish-brown in autumn. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are a food source for various wildlife. Its bark is rugged and furrowed, providing a distinctive texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
