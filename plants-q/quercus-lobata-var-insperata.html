Quercus lobata var. insperata

Quercus lobata var. insperata, commonly known as the valley oak, is a variant of the valley oak native to California.

It is a large deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and a distinctive, open crown. The plant produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically covered in a dense, hairy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and open woodlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
