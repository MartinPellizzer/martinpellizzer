Quercus dshorochensis var. liwanensis

Quercus dshorochensis var. liwanensis, commonly known as the Liwan chestnut oak, is a variant of the Dshorochensis oak found primarily in the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with distinct seasonal changes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in its native habitat.
