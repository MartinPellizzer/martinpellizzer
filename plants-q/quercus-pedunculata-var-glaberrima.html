Quercus pedunculata var. glaberrima

Quercus pedunculata var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smoothleaf white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by acorns that mature the following year. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in upland forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
