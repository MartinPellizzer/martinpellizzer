Quercus fulviseriaca

Quercus fulviseriaca, commonly known as the golden sericea oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves that turn red in the fall, providing ornamental value. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and furrowed, offering some resistance to pests and diseases.

Quercus fulviseriaca thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, temperate forests.
