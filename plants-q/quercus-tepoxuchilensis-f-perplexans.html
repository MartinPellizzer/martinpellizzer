Quercus tepoxuchilensis f. perplexans

Quercus tepoxuchilensis f. perplexans, commonly known as the perplexing Mexican oak, is a rare and little-studied variant of the Mexican oak.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, which exhibits a mix of lobed and serrated edges, making it distinct from other Quercus species. The plant thrives in the high-altitude cloud forests of central Mexico, where it adapts to humid and cool climatic conditions. Its acorns are small and typically dispersed by animals, contributing to its limited geographic distribution.

Due to habitat loss and limited research, this subspecies remains poorly understood in botanical science.
