Qualea laurifolia

Qualea laurifolia, commonly known as the Amazonian water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of rivers. The plant features broad, glossy leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor it to the muddy substrate. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they bloom during the wet season.

Qualea laurifolia plays a vital role in providing habitat for aquatic life and stabilizing the surrounding ecosystem.
