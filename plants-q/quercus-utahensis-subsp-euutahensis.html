Quercus utahensis subsp. euutahensis

Quercus utahensis subsp.

euutahensis, commonly known as the Utah oak, is a subspecies native to the southwestern United States. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its deeply lobed leaves and acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against wildfires and extreme temperatures.

The Utah oak plays an important role in local ecosystems by offering habitat and food for various wildlife species.
