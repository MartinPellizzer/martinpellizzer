Quercus faginea subsp. eufaginea

Quercus faginea subsp.

eufaginea, commonly known as the Hungarian oak, is a subspecies of the sessile oak native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and is often found in woodlands and forest edges.
