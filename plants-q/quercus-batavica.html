Quercus batavica

Quercus batavica, commonly known as the Batavian oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Netherlands and parts of western Europe.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its slender, upright form and small, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. Its acorns are edible and are often consumed by wildlife, contributing to its ecological role.
