Quercus thirkeana

Quercus thirkeana, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and lobed leaves. The tree produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in dry forests and woodlands.
