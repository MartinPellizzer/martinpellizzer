Quercus rugulosa

Quercus rugulosa, commonly known as the roughleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rugged, thickly hairy leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The tree produces small, reddish-brown acorns that are typically enclosed in cupules. Quercus rugulosa typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in bottomlands and along streams.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wildlife habitat and ecological diversity.
