Quercus sessiliflora var. cupaniana

Quercus sessiliflora var. cupaniana, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen oak, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark green leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape and have a smooth margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. Its acorns are enclosed in a cupule and are an important food source for various wildlife species.

This variety is adapted to a range of soil types and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
