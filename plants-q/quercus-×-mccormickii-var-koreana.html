Quercus × mccormickii var. koreana

Quercus × mccormickii var. koreana, commonly known as the Korean chestnut oak, is a hybrid evergreen oak native to the Korean Peninsula.

It features a distinctive combination of broad, glossy leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice in horticultural settings.
