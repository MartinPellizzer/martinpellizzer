Quercus magistri

Quercus magistri, commonly known as the Master Oak, is a species of deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the autumn. The tree produces acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. Quercus magistri typically grows to a height of 20-30 meters and has a sturdy, spreading crown.

Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a habitat for lichens and insects.
