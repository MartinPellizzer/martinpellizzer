Quercus × crenata

Quercus × crenata, commonly known as the Coast live oak, is a hybrid evergreen oak native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically lobed and have a waxy surface. The plant produces small, clustered acorns that are encased in a cupule, which is distinctive in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

Quercus × crenata plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
