Quisqualis indica var. oxypetala

Quisqualis indica var. oxypetala, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid or the Chinese violet, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pendulous flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark brown seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing as a vine or shrub.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
