Quercus pedunculata var. concordia

Quercus pedunculata var. concordia, commonly known as the Concord oak, is a variant of the California black oak.

It is a deciduous tree native to the western United States, particularly in California. The plant has dark, fissured bark and produces acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a rough, textured surface.

The species is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
