Quercus cuspidata var. sinensis

Quercus cuspidata var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese pagoda tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pagoda-like crown and elongated, acorn-like fruits. The leaves are typically narrow and pointed, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in temperate climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional Chinese gardens.
