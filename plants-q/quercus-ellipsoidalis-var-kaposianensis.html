Quercus ellipsoidalis var. kaposianensis

Quercus ellipsoidalis var. kaposianensis, commonly known as the Kaposi's oak, is a rare and endemic species native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elliptical-shaped acorns and broad, leathery leaves that are often pubescent underneath. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
