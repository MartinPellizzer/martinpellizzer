Quercus Ellipsoidalis F. Coronata

Ellipsoidalis oak, scientifically known as Quercus ellipsoidalis f. coronata, is a rare and unique variant of the ellipsoidalis oak species. This fascinating plant exhibits distinctive characteristics that set it apart from its parent species, making it a subject of botanical interest. Found in specific microhabitats, it thrives in environments with particular soil and climate conditions. Its ornamental value has led to increased cultivation efforts among horticulturists. Despite its beauty, the plant remains elusive, with limited documentation and a need for further study.
