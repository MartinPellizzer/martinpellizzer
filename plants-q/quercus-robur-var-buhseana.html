Quercus robur var. buhseana

Quercus robur var. buhseana, commonly known as the Buhse's oak, is a rare variant of the common English oak.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring a unique combination of lobes and serrations. This variety typically grows in specific microhabitats, often in areas with particular soil and climatic conditions. Its botanical significance lies in its genetic diversity and potential ecological role in certain forest ecosystems.
