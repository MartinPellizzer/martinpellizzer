Quercus zempoaltepecana

Quercus zempoaltepecana, commonly known as the Tuxpan oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color. Its acorns are encased in a cupule and are an important food source for various wildlife.

This tree thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in montane forests.
