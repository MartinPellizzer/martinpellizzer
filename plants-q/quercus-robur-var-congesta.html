Quercus robur var. congesta

Quercus robur var. congesta, commonly known as the English oak variety congesta, is a variant of the common oak characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

It features broad, dark green leaves that are typically shorter and broader than those of the typical common oak. The plant produces small, spiky acorns that mature over a season, contributing to its ecological significance. It is a slow-growing tree that thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

Its dense foliage makes it a valuable species for ornamental and ecological purposes.
