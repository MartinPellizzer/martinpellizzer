Quercus lusitanica subvar. sublobata

Quercus lusitanica subvar. sublobata, commonly known as the Iberian oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its lobed leaves that are typically shorter and more rounded compared to other Quercus species. The tree produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in Mediterranean climates with well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and scrublands.

The subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in maintaining biodiversity in its native habitat.
