Quercus hondae

Quercus hondae, commonly known as Honduran oak, is a species of evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its acorns are small and are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The wood of Quercus hondae is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture-making.
