Quercus dentata subsp. yunnanensis

Quercus dentata subsp.

yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to southwestern China. It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and is also used in traditional medicine.
