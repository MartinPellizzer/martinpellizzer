Quercus spicata var. chittagonga

Quercus spicata var. chittagonga, commonly known as Chittagong oak, is a deciduous tree native to the subtropical regions of Bangladesh and India.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and small, acorn-like fruits. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin, and turn yellow in autumn. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
