Quercus lusitanica subsp. baetica

Quercus lusitanica subsp.

baetica, commonly known as the Baetican oak, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its deeply lobed leaves and acorns.

This species thrives in Mediterranean climates, often found in rocky or limestone soils. The Baetican oak plays a vital role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Its wood is hard and durable, historically used for construction and tool-making.
