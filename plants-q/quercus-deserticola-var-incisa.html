Quercus deserticola var. incisa

Quercus deserticola var. incisa, commonly known as the desert bristlecone oak, is a rare and resilient shrub or small tree native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its twisted, gnarled branches and small, leathery leaves that are adapted to conserve water in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. Its deep root system allows it to access moisture from underground water sources, enabling survival in nutrient-poor soils.
