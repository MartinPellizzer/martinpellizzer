Quamoclidion multiflorum subsp. obtusum

Quamoclidion multiflorum subsp.

obtusum, commonly known as the broadleaf quillwort, is a member of the Charophyta group, closely related to modern-day charophytes. It is a small, aquatic plant characterized by its simple, ribbon-like leaves and the absence of true roots.

The plant typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its reproductive structures include small, clustered spore-producing organs called sporocarps.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
