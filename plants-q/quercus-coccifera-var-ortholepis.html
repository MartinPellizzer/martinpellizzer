Quercus coccifera var. ortholepis

Quercus coccifera var. ortholepis, commonly known as the prickly oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is well adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping and for erosion control in coastal areas.
