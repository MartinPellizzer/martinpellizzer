Quercus toza subsp. leptobalanus

Quercus toza subsp.

leptobalanus, commonly known as the slender-leaf chestnut oak, is a subspecies of the chestnut oak native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant component of its native habitat.
