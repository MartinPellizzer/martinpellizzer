Quercus spinosa var. monimotricha

Quercus spinosa var. monimotricha, commonly known as the bristly wood oak, is a shrub-like species native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, acorn-like fruits. The leaves are typically oval or elliptic with a rough, hairy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along forest edges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of its native habitat.
