Quercus aurantiaca

Quercus aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange foliage in the autumn, which gives it its common name. The leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a lobed or serrated margin. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in humid, temperate climates.
