Quelchia × grandifolia

Quelchia × grandifolia, commonly known as the hybrid coralbean, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Quelchia lasianthoides and Quelchia pumila, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant produces striking, showy red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

Quelchia × grandifolia is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils in full sun.
