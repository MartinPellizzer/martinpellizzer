Quercus szowitzii

Quercus szowitzii, commonly known as the Kashmir oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas, particularly found in regions of northern India, Pakistan, and Nepal.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against environmental stressors.
