Quercus ilex var. gracilis

Quercus ilex var. gracilis, commonly known as the Spanish chestnut or slender evergreen oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. The plant produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are encased in a cupule. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This variety is notable for its dense foliage and ability to withstand drought conditions.
