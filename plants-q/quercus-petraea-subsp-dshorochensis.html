Quercus petraea subsp. dshorochensis

Quercus petraea subsp.

dshorochensis, commonly known as the Caucasian rock oak, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its robust, often twisted trunk and thick, furrowed bark.

The leaves are typically broad, ovate, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, temperate climates.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in forest ecosystems and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
