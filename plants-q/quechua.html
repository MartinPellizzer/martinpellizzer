Quechua

Quechua, commonly known as the Andean potato, is a plant native to the highlands of the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, tuberous roots that are rich in carbohydrates and have been a staple food for indigenous communities for centuries. Its adaptability to high altitudes and cold climates makes it a vital agricultural crop in South America.

The Quechua plant has also played a significant role in the cultural and historical identity of the Andean people.
