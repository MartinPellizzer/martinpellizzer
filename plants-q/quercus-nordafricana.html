Quercus nordafricana

Quercus nordafricana, commonly known as the North African oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Maghreb region of North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The tree has thick, leathery leaves that are often lobed, and it produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

The bark is rugged and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance that helps in its identification.
