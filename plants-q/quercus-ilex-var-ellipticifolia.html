Quercus ilex var. ellipticifolia

Quercus ilex var. ellipticifolia, commonly known as the Spanish oak, is a variant of the evergreen oak native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth upper surface. The plant produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
