Quercus neglecta

Quercus neglecta, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and small, acorn-like fruits that are typically clustered in groups of two. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

Quercus neglecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its adaptability to various soil types.
