Quercus × macnabiana nothovar. mahonii

Quercus × macnabiana nothovar. mahonii, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus mongolica and Quercus serrata.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and produces small, spiky fruits called burrs. The tree typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters and has a rounded crown. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas of eastern Asia.

This species is valued for its timber and has been introduced to other regions for ornamental and ecological purposes.
