Quercus coccifera var. refracta

Quercus coccifera var. refracta, commonly known as the dwarf evergreen oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is also used in horticulture for ornamental purposes.
