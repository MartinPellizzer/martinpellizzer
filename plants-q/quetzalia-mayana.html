Quetzalia mayana

Quetzalia mayana, commonly known as the Maya quetzal, is a rare and striking plant native to the cloud forests of southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant green leaves and striking red and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is known for its unique ability to thrive in high-altitude environments with limited sunlight. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and hummingbirds.

Due to habitat loss, Quetzalia mayana is considered endangered and is a focus of conservation efforts.
