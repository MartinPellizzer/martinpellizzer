Quercus stellata f. quadrata

Quercus stellata f. quadrata, commonly known as the dwarf chinquapin, is a rare and unique variant of the chinquapin oak.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive quadrilateral-shaped acorns. This plant is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Texas and Louisiana. The leaves are typically small, oblong, and have a leathery texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
