Quercus reticulata var. squarrosa

Quercus reticulata var. squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa oak, is a variant of the reticulated oak native to southeastern China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense, woolly pubescence. The leaves turn a striking red or orange in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
