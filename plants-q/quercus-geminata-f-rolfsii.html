Quercus geminata f. rolfsii

Quercus geminata f. rolfsii, commonly known as the Florida live oak, is a variant of the live oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often clustered in pairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spiny acorns that mature over a period of several months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and inland environments.

This subspecies is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand salt spray, making it a common sight in coastal regions.
