Quercus lusitanica subsp. salzmanniana

Quercus lusitanica subsp.

salzmanniana, commonly known as the Salzmann oak, is a subspecies native to southern Europe, particularly Portugal and Spain. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and deeply lobed leaves.

The bark is typically grayish-brown and becomes fissured with age. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with a Mediterranean climate.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and historical significance in local landscapes.
