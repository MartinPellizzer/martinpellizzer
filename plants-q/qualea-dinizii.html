Qualea dinizii

Qualea dinizii, commonly known as the Amazonian water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the flooded forests of the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that provide shade and habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has a unique ability to survive in both submerged and emergent conditions, adapting to seasonal flooding. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they open in the early morning.

Qualea dinizii plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland habitats.
