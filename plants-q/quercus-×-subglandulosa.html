Quercus × subglandulosa

Quercus × subglandulosa, commonly known as the chestnut oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus prinus and Quercus muehlenbergii.

It is characterized by its distinctive acorns, which are typically larger and more elongated compared to those of its parent species. The leaves are usually broad and have a rounded or slightly lobed shape, often with a glossy upper surface. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in the eastern United States.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
