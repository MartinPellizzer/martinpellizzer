Quercus suber subsp. eusuber

Quercus suber subsp.

eusuber, commonly known as the Spanish oak, is a subspecies of the cork oak native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its thick, fissured bark that provides a unique texture and is used for cork production.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside. It thrives in Mediterranean climates with well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological importance and economic utility in sustainable forestry.
