Quercus oleoides var. australis

Quercus oleoides var. australis, commonly known as the Australian white oak, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oblong to ovate in shape. The tree produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers in clusters, followed by acorns that are enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sclerophyllous woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
