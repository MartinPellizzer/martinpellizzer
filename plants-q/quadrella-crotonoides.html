Quadrella crotonoides

Quadrella crotonoides, commonly known as the African croton, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
