Qualea marioniae

Qualea marioniae, commonly known as the Marion's water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide shade and habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has a short, thick rhizome that anchors it in the muddy substrates of slow-moving waters. Its white flowers bloom in the early morning, adding to the ecological diversity of the wetland ecosystems.

Qualea marioniae plays a vital role in maintaining water quality and supporting local biodiversity.
