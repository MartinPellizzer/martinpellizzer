Quercus siguatepequeana

Quercus siguatepequeana, commonly known as the Santa Marta oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the Andes mountains of Colombia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in high-altitude cloud forests, where it contributes to the biodiversity and ecological stability of the region.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
