Quercus lanuginosa var. australis

Quercus lanuginosa var. australis, commonly known as the Australian silky oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and palmately lobed leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by cup-shaped acorns. It thrives in a range of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
