Quercus × substellata

Quercus × substellata, commonly known as the silverleaf oak, is a hybrid evergreen oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its narrow, elongated leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 15 feet. Its acorns are small and often have a scaly cap, and it produces them in the fall.

Quercus × substellata is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
