Quercus ilex f. crassicupulata

Quercus ilex f. crassicupulata, commonly known as the thick-cupped holm oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a variant of the holm oak (Quercus ilex) characterized by its dense, broad acorns and thick, cup-like structures that enclose the nut. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to coastal and Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are leathery, evergreen, and have a deep green color with a waxy surface.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
