Quercus frainetto subsp. conferta

Quercus frainetto subsp.

conferta, commonly known as the Hungarian oak, is a subspecies native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The subspecies is noted for its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
