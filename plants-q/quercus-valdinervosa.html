Quercus valdinervosa

Quercus valdinervosa, commonly known as the Valdenevra oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its leathery, elliptical leaves with a prominent central vein, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and mild climates. Its acorns are small and usually take about six months to mature.

This species is valued for its ecological role and is often found in mixed forests.
