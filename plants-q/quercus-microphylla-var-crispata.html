Quercus microphylla var. crispata

Quercus microphylla var. crispata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chinquapin oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often curled or crisped at the edges, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, spiny acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
