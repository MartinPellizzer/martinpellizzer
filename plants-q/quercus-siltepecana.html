Quercus siltepecana

Quercus siltepecana, commonly known as the Sierra Norte oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the highlands of southern Mexico, specifically in the states of Oaxaca and Chiapas. This tree is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn red in the autumn. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Quercus siltepecana plays a crucial role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
