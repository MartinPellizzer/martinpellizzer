Qualea parviflora var. tomentosa

Qualea parviflora var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly water lily, is a species native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves covered in a dense, woolly pubescence that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its unique adaptations make it an important component of the aquatic ecosystems in South America.
