Quercus suber var. occidentalis

Quercus suber var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western cork oak, is a variant of the cork oak native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thick, corky bark, which is harvested for commercial use in wine stoppers and other products. The tree has broad, lobed leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in Mediterranean climates with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting diverse flora and fauna.
