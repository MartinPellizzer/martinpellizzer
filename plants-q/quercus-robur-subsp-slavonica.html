Quercus robur subsp. slavonica

Quercus robur subsp.

slavonica, commonly known as the Slavonian oak, is a subspecies of the common oak native to parts of Europe and the Caucasus. It is a large deciduous tree characterized by its sturdy, often twisted trunk and deeply lobed leaves.

The leaves are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath, turning yellow or brown in autumn. It produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This subspecies is adapted to a range of soil types and is often found in mixed forests.
