Quercus marilandica f. quinqueloba

Quercus marilandica f. quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed chestnut oak, is a variant of the chestnut oak native to the eastern United States.

This deciduous tree is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed leaves, which are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. It produces small, reddish-brown acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. The bark of this plant is thick and furrowed, providing a rugged appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
