Quercus serrata var. chinensis

Quercus serrata var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by edible, sweet-tasting nuts enclosed in spiny husks. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mixed forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine and cuisine.
