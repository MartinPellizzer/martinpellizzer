Quisqualis pubescens

Quisqualis pubescens, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The velvet tamarind is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
