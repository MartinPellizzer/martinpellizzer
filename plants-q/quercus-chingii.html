Quercus chingii

Quercus chingii, commonly known as the Chinese red oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its serrated, elliptical leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are enclosed in a cupule. It typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates.

Quercus chingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
