Quercus coccifera var. auzendei

Quercus coccifera var. auzendei, commonly known as the Auzende's evergreen oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, coastal, and arid environments due to its drought-resistant properties.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
