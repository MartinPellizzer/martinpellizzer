Quercus salicina f. angustata

Quercus salicina f. angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf chestnut oak, is a variant of the chestnut oak native to the eastern United States.

This shrub or small tree typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a pointed tip and serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, red-brown acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
