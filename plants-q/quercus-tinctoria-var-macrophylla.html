Quercus tinctoria var. macrophylla

Quercus tinctoria var. macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. Its fruit, a fleshy, orange-red berry, is edible and contains a high concentration of sugars.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in dye production.
