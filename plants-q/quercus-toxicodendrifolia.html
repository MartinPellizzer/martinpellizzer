Quercus toxicodendrifolia

Quercus toxicodendrifolia, commonly known as the poison oak oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to parts of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often lobed leaves that are typically green on top and fuzzy white underneath. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by small, hard, chestnut-like fruits.

The plant contains urushiol, a toxic oil that can cause severe skin irritation in humans.
