Qualea wittrockii

Qualea wittrockii, commonly known as the yellow-eyed river-orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and slender, upright stems. The plant typically grows in wet, muddy areas along rivers and floodplains. Its leaves are long and narrow, often floating on the water's surface.

Qualea wittrockii plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, supporting various pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
