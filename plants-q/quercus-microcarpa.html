Quercus microcarpa

Quercus microcarpa, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in landscaping and conservation efforts.
