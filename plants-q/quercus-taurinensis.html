Quercus taurinensis

Quercus taurinensis, commonly known as the Taurine oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountains of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its serrated, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, preferring well-drained soils.

Quercus taurinensis is valued for its hardy nature and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal.
