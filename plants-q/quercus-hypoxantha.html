Quercus hypoxantha

Quercus hypoxantha, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and supporting biodiversity in its native range.
