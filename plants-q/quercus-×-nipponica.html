Quercus × nipponica

Quercus × nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus crispula and Quercus acutissima.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its deciduous nature, with leaves that are usually lobed and have a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas, where it contributes to biodiversity and ecological stability.
