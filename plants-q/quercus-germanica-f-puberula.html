Quercus germanica f. puberula

Quercus germanica f. puberula, commonly known as the silky rock oak, is a shrub-like form of the common oak.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage with a silvery-white underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically oval to elliptic in shape and have a leathery texture. This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
