Quisqualis grandiflora

Quisqualis grandiflora, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, red calyces that resemble small lanterns. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are often hidden within the fleshy, showy bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
