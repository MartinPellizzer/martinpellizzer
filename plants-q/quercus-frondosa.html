Quercus frondosa

Quercus frondosa, commonly known as the Atlantic white cedar, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark, inedible acorns. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
