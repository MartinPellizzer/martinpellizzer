Quercus prinus var. platanoides

Quercus prinus var. platanoides, commonly known as the canyon oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are often lobed and turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native range.
