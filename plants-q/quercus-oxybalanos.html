Quercus oxybalanos

Quercus oxybalanos, commonly known as the Greek oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in Greece and Turkey. The tree is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and deeply lobed leaves. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

The botanical features of Quercus oxybalanos make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
