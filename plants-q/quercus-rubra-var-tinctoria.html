Quercus rubra var. tinctoria

Quercus rubra var. tinctoria, commonly known as the red oak dye variety, is a variant of the northern red oak.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that turn a vibrant red in the fall, making it a notable ornamental plant. The leaves are typically lobed and have a rough texture, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in landscaping and horticulture.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
