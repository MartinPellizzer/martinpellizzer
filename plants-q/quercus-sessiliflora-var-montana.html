Quercus sessiliflora var. montana

Quercus sessiliflora var. montana, commonly known as the mountain chinquapin, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, broad leaves that are typically oval or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by distinctive, chestnut-like fruits. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

The species is noted for its slow growth and longevity, often living for several decades.
