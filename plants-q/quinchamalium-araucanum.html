Quinchamalium araucanum

Quinchamalium araucanum, commonly known as the Araucaria tree, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Araucariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, pyramidal shape and its long, needle-like leaves. The tree produces both male and female cones, with the female cones developing into seed-bearing structures. It is often found in temperate rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
