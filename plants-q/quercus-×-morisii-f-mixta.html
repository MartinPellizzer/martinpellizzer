Quercus × morisii f. mixta

Quercus × morisii f. mixta, commonly known as the hybrid oak, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Quercus robur and Quercus sessilifolia.

It exhibits a mix of characteristics from both parent species, including variable leaf shapes and bark textures. The plant is typically found in regions with temperate climates, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its acorns are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and parks.
