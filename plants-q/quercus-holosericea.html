Quercus holosericea

Quercus holosericea, commonly known as the silky oak, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and its distinctive, silvery-white undersides on the leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by acorn-like fruits.

This species is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
