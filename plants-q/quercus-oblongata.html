Quercus oblongata

Quercus oblongata, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen oak, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. The tree produces small, red or orange acorns that mature in the second year. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Quercus oblongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
