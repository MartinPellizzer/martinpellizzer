Qualea jundiahy

Qualea jundiahy, commonly known as the yellow water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that float on the water surface, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's flowers are typically yellow or white, blooming during the dry season. It plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its wetland ecosystems.

Qualea jundiahy is adapted to seasonal flooding, with its rhizomes capable of surviving submersion for extended periods.
