Quercus chrysolepis subsp. tomentella

Quercus chrysolepis subsp.

tomentella, commonly known as the silver fir oak, is a subspecies of the coast live oak native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and a rounded, spreading canopy.

The leaves are typically elliptical with a waxy, tomentose (fuzzy) underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
