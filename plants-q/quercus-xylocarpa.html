Quercus xylocarpa

Quercus xylocarpa, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central and southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. Its acorns are small, usually less than 1 cm in length, and are enclosed in a cupule.
