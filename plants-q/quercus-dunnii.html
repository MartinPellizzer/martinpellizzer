Quercus dunnii

Quercus dunnii, commonly known as the Argentine chestnut or the Sierra de las Gigas oak, is a deciduous tree native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, spiky acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in dry, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ecological significance makes it a key component of its native ecosystems.
