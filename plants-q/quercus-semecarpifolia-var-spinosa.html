Quercus semecarpifolia var. spinosa

Quercus semecarpifolia var. spinosa, commonly known as the spiny chinquapin, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. The plant has a deep root system, which helps it thrive in sandy or acidic soils. It is often found in dry, open woodlands and is adapted to fire-prone environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
