Qualea brasiliana

Qualea brasiliana, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tall evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, compound leaves that can reach up to two meters in length. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing the edible Brazil nuts. It plays a crucial role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its economic and ecological importance, it is also a target for conservation efforts.
