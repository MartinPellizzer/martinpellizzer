Quercus intricata f. typica

Quercus intricata f. typica, commonly known as the intricately branched oak, is a variant of the Quercus intricata species, characterized by its complex and highly branched structure.

It is a deciduous tree native to specific regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant exhibits small, elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a pale underside, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing acorns that serve as a food source for various wildlife.

The botanical features of this plant make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
