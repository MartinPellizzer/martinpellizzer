Qualea steyermarkiana

Qualea steyermarkiana, commonly known as the white water tree, is a species of tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its buttressed roots and a tall, straight trunk, often reaching heights of up to 30 meters. The leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing numerous seeds.

This tree plays an important ecological role in the rainforest, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
