Quercus serrata subsp. mongolicoides

Quercus serrata subsp.

mongolicoides, commonly known as the Mongolian rock oak, is a shrub or small tree native to East Asia. It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves with serrated edges and small, acorn-like fruits.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky, arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its bark is typically thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh weather conditions.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
