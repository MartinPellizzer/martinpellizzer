Quassia indica var. lucida

Quassia indica var. lucida, commonly known as the shining quassia, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Simaroubaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant is notable for its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits containing hard, seed-like structures.

Its leaves and bark contain quassinoids, which are bitter compounds used in traditional medicine and as a natural insecticide.
