Quisqualis indica

Quisqualis indica, commonly known as the Chinese lantern or flame of the forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, red or orange calyces that resemble small lanterns. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the winter season. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing as a shrub or small tree.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and public spaces.
