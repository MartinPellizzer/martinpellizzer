Quercus carpinea

Quercus carpinea, commonly known as the red oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its acorns, which are typically enclosed in a cupule and have a smooth cap. The leaves are lobed and have a serrated edge, turning vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in forested areas.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and ecological importance as a habitat for various wildlife species.
