Quercus sessilis var. pendula

Quercus sessilis var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping sessile oak, is a unique variant of the sessile oak characterized by its pendulous growth habit.

This tree features drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. Botanically, it is a deciduous tree with simple, alternate leaves that are typically dark green and oval in shape. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
