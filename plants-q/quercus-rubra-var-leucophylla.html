Quercus rubra var. leucophylla

Quercus rubra var. leucophylla, commonly known as the white oak mapleleaf, is a variant of the red oak species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, maple-like leaves that are usually white-veined and have a lobed appearance. The plant produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, mixed hardwood forests.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
