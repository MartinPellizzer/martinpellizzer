Quercus ophiosquamata

Quercus ophiosquamata, commonly known as the Chinese rock oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its unique, scaly bark that resembles armor, giving it its scientific name. The leaves are typically small, elliptical, and have a serrated margin. It produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring followed by acorns.

This plant is adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy species in arid climates.
