Quercus albicaulis

Quercus albicaulis, commonly known as the Whitebark Oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, acorn-like fruits. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

This species plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
