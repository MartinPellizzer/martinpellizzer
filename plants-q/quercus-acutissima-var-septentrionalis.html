Quercus acutissima var. septentrionalis

Quercus acutissima var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern sharpleaf oak, is a variant of the sharpleaf oak native to eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that are sharply toothed and turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
