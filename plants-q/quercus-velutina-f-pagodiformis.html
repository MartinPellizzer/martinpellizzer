Quercus velutina f. pagodiformis

Quercus velutina f. pagodiformis, commonly known as the pagoda oak, is a rare and distinctive variant of the chestnut oak.

It is characterized by its unique, pagoda-like branching pattern, which gives it a striking, ornamental appearance. The plant is native to the eastern United States, particularly in regions with moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically oval with a velvety texture and a deep green color, turning reddish in the fall.

This form is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in horticulture and landscaping.
