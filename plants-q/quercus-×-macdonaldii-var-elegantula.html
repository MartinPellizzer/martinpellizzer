Quercus × macdonaldii var. elegantula

Quercus × macdonaldii var. elegantula, commonly known as the elegant oak, is a hybrid species of oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and its distinctive, finely toothed leaves that turn a striking red or purple in the fall. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 15 feet. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant seasonal color changes and unique leaf structure.
