Quercus cana

Quercus cana, commonly known as the Florida scrub oak, is a small evergreen oak native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to fire-prone environments. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its acorns are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Quercus cana plays a crucial role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native habitats.
