Quararibea bicolor

Quararibea bicolor, commonly known as the pink-flowered spondias, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink and white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual appeal to its canopy. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly rough texture. It produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

Quararibea bicolor is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
