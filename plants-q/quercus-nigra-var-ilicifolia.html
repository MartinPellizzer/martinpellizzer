Quercus nigra var. ilicifolia

Quercus nigra var. ilicifolia, commonly known as the swamp chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and deeply furrowed, providing structural support and protection against environmental stressors.

This variety is well adapted to wet, swampy soils and plays a significant role in riparian ecosystems.
