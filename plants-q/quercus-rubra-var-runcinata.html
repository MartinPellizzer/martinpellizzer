Quercus rubra var. runcinata

Quercus rubra var. runcinata, commonly known as the scarlet oak, is a variant of the northern red oak.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which have a serrated margin and a rounded tip. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in a variety of soil types. It produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

The tree is typically medium-sized, reaching heights of up to 30 meters, and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
