Quercus verde

Quercus verde, commonly known as the green oak, is a deciduous tree native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are usually a vibrant green during the growing season. Its acorns are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Quercus verde typically grows in temperate climates and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.
