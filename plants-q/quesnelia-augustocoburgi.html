Quesnelia augustocoburgi

Quesnelia augustocoburgi, commonly known as the Coburg spider orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to the subtropical rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like inflorescence and vibrant, colorful flowers that range from yellow to orange and red. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or branches. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is classified as endangered, highlighting the need for its protection.
