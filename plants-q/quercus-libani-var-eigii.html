Quercus libani var. eigii

Quercus libani var. eigii, commonly known as the Lebanese oak subvariety Eigii, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a leathery texture. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas with moderate rainfall.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
