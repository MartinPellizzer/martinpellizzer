Quercus coccifera subsp. calliprinos

Quercus coccifera subsp.

calliprinos, commonly known as the Kermes oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, reddish-brown acorns that are encased in a cupule. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and arid environments.

This subspecies is notable for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
