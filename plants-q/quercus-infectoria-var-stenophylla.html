Quercus infectoria var. stenophylla

Quercus infectoria var. stenophylla, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with certain parts used in traditional remedies.
