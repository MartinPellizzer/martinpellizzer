Quercus dumosa var. alvordiana

Quercus dumosa var. alvordiana, commonly known as the Alvord scrub oak, is a shrub-like species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and chaparral habitats.

Its dense growth habit provides valuable habitat and erosion control in its native range.
