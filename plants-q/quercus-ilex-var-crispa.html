Quercus ilex var. crispa

Quercus ilex var. crispa, commonly known as the cork oak with crisped leaves, is a variant of the evergreen oak tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and often curled leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant is typically found in rocky or mountainous areas where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against wildfires and environmental stress.

This variety is also valued for its ecological importance, supporting a wide range of flora and fauna.
