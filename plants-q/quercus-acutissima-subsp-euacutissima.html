Quercus acutissima subsp. euacutissima

Quercus acutissima subsp.

euacutissima, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia. It features lanceolate, glossy, dark green leaves that remain on the tree throughout the winter, giving it a distinctive evergreen appearance.

The tree produces small, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by acorns that mature in the fall. It is characterized by its slow growth rate and dense, compact form, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

The subspecies is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in urban environments.
