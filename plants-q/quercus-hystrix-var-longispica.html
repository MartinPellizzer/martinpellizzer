Quercus hystrix var. longispica

Quercus hystrix var. longispica, commonly known as the long-needled holly oak, is a variant of the holly oak species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically pubescent on the underside, giving them a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like or small tree-like, often forming thickets in rocky or dry habitats.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in Mediterranean ecosystems.
