Quercus petraea f. minima

Quercus petraea f. minima, commonly known as the dwarf sessile oak, is a compact variant of the sessile oak.

It is characterized by its small, rounded crown and slow growth rate, making it suitable for small spaces. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter, hairy underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This form is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
