Quercus baronii var. pendula

Quercus baronii var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping chinquapin, is a rare and distinctive variant of the chinquapin oak.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it an ornamental appearance. This plant is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Georgia and South Carolina. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mesic hardwood forests.

The leaves are typically oval with a smooth margin, and the plant produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.
