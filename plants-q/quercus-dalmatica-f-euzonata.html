Quercus dalmatica f. euzonata

Quercus dalmatica f. euzonata, commonly known as the Dalmatian oak form, is a variant of the Dalmatian oak, native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen oak characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This form is notable for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in horticulture.
