Quercus garryana subsp. semota

Quercus garryana subsp.

semota, commonly known as the coast live oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are enclosed in a cupule, a feature common among oaks. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
