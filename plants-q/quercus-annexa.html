Quercus annexa

Quercus annexa, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. Its acorns are small and typically have a scaly cap, and it produces them in the fall.
