Quercus elmeri

Quercus elmeri, commonly known as Elmer's oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is typically smooth with a reddish-brown color that becomes fissured with age.

Quercus elmeri thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
