Quercus calliprinos var. dipsacina

Quercus calliprinos var. dipsacina, commonly known as the Coast live oak, is a variant of the Coast live oak native to California.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with thick, leathery leaves that are typically 2-5 inches long. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are often covered in a dense, fuzzy cap. It thrives in coastal areas with mild, wet winters and is adapted to well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to provide habitat for local wildlife.
