Quesnelia alvimii

Quesnelia alvimii, commonly known as the Alvim's bromeliad, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking orange-red inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Quesnelia alvimii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
