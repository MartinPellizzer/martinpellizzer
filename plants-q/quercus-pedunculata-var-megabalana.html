Quercus pedunculata var. megabalana

Quercus pedunculata var. megabalana, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale yellow bark and broad, lobed leaves. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are often covered in a dense, woolly coating. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
