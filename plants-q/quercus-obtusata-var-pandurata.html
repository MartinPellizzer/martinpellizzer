Quercus obtusata var. pandurata

Quercus obtusata var. pandurata, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, cupule-enclosed acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in dry to moist soils and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
