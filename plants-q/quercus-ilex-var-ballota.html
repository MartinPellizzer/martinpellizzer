Quercus ilex var. ballota

Quercus ilex var. ballota, commonly known as the Ballota cork oak, is a subspecies of the evergreen oak native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark green leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a natural habitat for lichens and insects.

This subspecies is also valued for its durable wood and is often used in traditional crafts and construction.
