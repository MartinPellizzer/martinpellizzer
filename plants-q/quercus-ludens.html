Quercus ludens

Quercus ludens, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Quercus ludens is valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in local ecosystems.
