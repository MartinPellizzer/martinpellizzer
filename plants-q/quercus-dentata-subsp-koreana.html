Quercus dentata subsp. koreana

Quercus dentata subsp.

koreana, commonly known as the Korean water oak, is a deciduous tree native to Korea and parts of China. It is characterized by its distinctive lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and its acorns are typically enclosed in a cupule.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a moderate growth rate and can reach heights of up to 15 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
