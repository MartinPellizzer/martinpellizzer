Quercus × tiszae

Quercus × tiszae, commonly known as the Tisza oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus cerris and Quercus pedunculiflora.

It is a deciduous tree native to Hungary, characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its bark is typically smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age.

Quercus × tiszae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
