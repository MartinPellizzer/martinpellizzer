Quercus coccifera var. brachycarpa

Quercus coccifera var. brachycarpa, commonly known as the dwarf evergreen oak, is a shrub-like species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces small, red, fleshy fruits known as drupes. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showing resistance to drought and salt. It has a compact growth habit, often forming dense thickets.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
