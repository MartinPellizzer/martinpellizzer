Quercus pedunculata f. angustifolia

Quercus pedunculata f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Chinese chestnut, is a variant of the Chinese chestnut tree characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It is native to China and is typically found in temperate forest regions. The plant exhibits a deciduous habit, shedding its leaves seasonally, and produces small, edible nuts encased in spiny husks. Its botanical features include a rugged bark, alternate leaf arrangement, and small, inconspicuous flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Chinese agriculture.
