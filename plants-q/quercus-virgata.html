Quercus virgata

Quercus virgata, commonly known as the Virginia oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its acorns are typically small and have a scaly cap, and it produces them in the fall.

Quercus virgata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
