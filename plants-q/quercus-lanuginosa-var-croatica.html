Quercus lanuginosa var. croatica

Quercus lanuginosa var. croatica, commonly known as the Croatian hairy oak, is a variant of the hairy oak native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its grayish-brown bark and small, elongated leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, pale yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by acorns that are typically two-lobed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests and rocky areas.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
