Quaqua parviflora subsp. bayeriana

Quaqua parviflora subsp.

bayeriana, commonly known as the Bayer's small-flowered quaqua, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
