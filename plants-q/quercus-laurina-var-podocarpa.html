Quercus laurina var. podocarpa

Quercus laurina var. podocarpa, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers followed by spherical, smooth acorns that are encased in a cupule. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
