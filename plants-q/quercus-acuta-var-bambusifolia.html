Quercus acuta var. bambusifolia

Quercus acuta var. bambusifolia, commonly known as the bamboo-leaf chestnut, is a unique variant of the Japanese chestnut tree.

It is characterized by its elongated, bamboo-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to southern Japan and thrives in humid, temperate climates. Its foliage is often used in traditional landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Botanically, it exhibits features that blend characteristics of both oak and bamboo, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
