Quesnelia edmundoi var. intermedia

Quesnelia edmundoi var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate quesnelia, is a bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its foliage is often used in horticultural displays due to its ornamental appearance.
