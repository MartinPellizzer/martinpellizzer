Quercus leucophylla

Quercus leucophylla, commonly known as the whiteleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often white or pale underneath, giving it its common name. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

Quercus leucophylla is an important species for wildlife, providing food and shelter for numerous animals.
