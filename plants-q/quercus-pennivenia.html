Quercus pennivenia

Quercus pennivenia, commonly known as the red oak, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its deeply lobed leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The tree produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Quercus pennivenia typically grows to a height of 20-40 meters and has a broad, spreading canopy.

Its bark is thick and fissured, providing habitat for insects and small animals.
