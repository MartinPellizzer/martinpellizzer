Quercus toumeyi

Quercus toumeyi, commonly known as the Touloumey oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Quercus toumeyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native range.
