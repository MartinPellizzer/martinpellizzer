Quercus phellos var. sempervirens

Quercus phellos var. sempervirens, commonly known as the live oak, is a variant of the willow oak that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Gulf Coast. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a pale green or yellowish hue on the underside. Its acorns are small and typically have a scaly cap, which is a distinguishing feature.

The live oak is known for its slow growth and long lifespan, often reaching several centuries in age.
