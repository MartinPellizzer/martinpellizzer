Quercus × morisii

Quercus × morisii, commonly known as the Morisii oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus robur (English oak) and Quercus faginea (Spanish oak).

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and acorns that are typically smaller than those of its parent species. The tree typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters and is native to the Iberian Peninsula. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for a variety of climates.

The Morisii oak is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in parks and gardens.
