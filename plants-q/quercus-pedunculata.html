Quercus pedunculata

Quercus pedunculata, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, acorn-bearing capsules. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a pointed tip and serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
