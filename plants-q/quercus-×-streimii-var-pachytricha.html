Quercus × streimii var. pachytricha

Quercus × streimii var. pachytricha, commonly known as the thick-leaved oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus robur and Quercus sessilifolia.

It is characterized by its broad, thick, and leathery leaves, which are often glossy and have a distinctive lobed shape. This variety typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
