Quercus cassura

Quercus cassura, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest biome of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring broad, lobed leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by edible chestnuts that are encased in a spiny husk.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
