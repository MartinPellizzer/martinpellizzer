Quercus ilex var. smilax

Quercus ilex var. smilax, commonly known as the Kermes oak, is a shrub-like evergreen species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in panicles, followed by small, inedible acorns. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact form and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
