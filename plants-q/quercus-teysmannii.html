Quercus teysmannii

Quercus teysmannii, commonly known as the Teymann's oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves with a leathery texture and a distinctive shape. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. Quercus teysmannii thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both ecological studies and botanical conservation efforts.
