Quercus neriifolia

Quercus neriifolia, commonly known as the glossyleaf oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family, Fagaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a leathery texture. Quercus neriifolia produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible acorns that serve as a food source for various wildlife.

This tree typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
