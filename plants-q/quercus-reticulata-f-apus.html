Quercus reticulata f. apus

Quercus reticulata f. apus, commonly known as the white oakleaf maple, is a variant of the reticulate oak.

It is characterized by its distinctive white, waxy leaves that are often lobed and resemble those of the white oak. This plant is native to parts of East Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The leaves are typically deciduous, turning yellow or red in the autumn.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
