Quercus ilexoides

Quercus ilexoides, commonly known as the Florida hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, chestnut-like nuts enclosed in spiny husks.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in upland hardwood forests.
