Quercus × tabajdiana

Quercus × tabajdiana, commonly known as the Tabajda oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus garryi and Quercus crassifolia.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces acorns that are typically encased in a cupule. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often found in rocky soils and canyons.

The Tabajda oak plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
