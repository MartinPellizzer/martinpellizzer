Quercus turneri

Quercus turneri, commonly known as the Turner's oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, spiky capsules that contain one or two seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Quercus turneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
