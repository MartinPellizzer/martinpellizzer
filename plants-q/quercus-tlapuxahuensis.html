Quercus tlapuxahuensis

Quercus tlapuxahuensis, commonly known as the Tlapuxahuac oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family Fagaceae.

It is native to Mexico, specifically the mountainous regions of the state of Hidalgo. This evergreen oak is characterized by its dense foliage and acorn-producing capabilities, which are important for local wildlife. The leaves are typically broad and elliptical, with a leathery texture.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in cloud forests, contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
