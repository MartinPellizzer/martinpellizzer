Quercus brevipetiolata

Quercus brevipetiolata, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central and southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, broad leaves that are usually less than 5 cm in length and have a rounded or slightly lobed shape. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically covered with a dense layer of fuzzy hair. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
