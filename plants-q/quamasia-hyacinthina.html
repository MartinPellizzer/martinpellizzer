Quamasia hyacinthina

Quamasia hyacinthina, commonly known as the blue hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces clusters of tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
