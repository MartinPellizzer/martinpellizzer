Quercus × pseudodalechampii

Quercus × pseudodalechampii, commonly known as the hybrid chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Quercus muehlenbergii (chestnut oak) and Quercus prinus (possumhaw oak). The plant exhibits characteristics of both parent species, including a combination of leaf shape and acorn structure. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mixed hardwood forests.

The leaves are usually lobed and have a dark green upper surface with a pale underside.
