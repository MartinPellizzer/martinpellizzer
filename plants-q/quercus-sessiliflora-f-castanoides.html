Quercus sessiliflora f. castanoides

Quercus sessiliflora f. castanoides, commonly known as the Mexican chestnut oak, is a variant of the sessiliflora oak native to the highlands of Mexico.

This plant is characterized by its small, chestnut-like acorns and its broad, dark green leaves that are often hairy underneath. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate to subtropical climates. The tree can reach heights of up to 15 meters and is often found in mixed forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in its native habitat.
