Quercus trinidadensis

Quercus trinidadensis, commonly known as the Trinidad oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the Caribbean island of Trinidad.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, acorn-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a glossy surface.

Quercus trinidadensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
