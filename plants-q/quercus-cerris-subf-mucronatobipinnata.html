Quercus cerris subf. mucronatobipinnata

Quercus cerris subf.

mucronatobipinnata, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a botanical variant of the common oak, characterized by its bipinnate leaves. The leaves are typically alternate, with a delicate, feathery appearance, and each leaflet has a rounded tip.

This subspecies is native to the eastern Mediterranean region, including parts of Turkey and the Balkans. It exhibits a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in forest ecosystems.
