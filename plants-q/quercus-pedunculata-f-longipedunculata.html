Quercus pedunculata f. longipedunculata

Quercus pedunculata f. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-peduncled chinquapin, is a variant of the chinquapin oak.

It is characterized by its elongated peduncles, which are the stalks supporting its acorns. This plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States. Its leaves are typically oval with a pointed tip and a rough, hairy texture.

The acorns are small, with a scaly cap and are often used by wildlife as a food source.
