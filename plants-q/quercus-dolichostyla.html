Quercus dolichostyla

Quercus dolichostyla, commonly known as the long-styled oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that mature over a season, providing food for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and fissured, offering some protection against environmental stressors.

This plant plays a significant role in its native ecosystem, contributing to soil stability and biodiversity.
