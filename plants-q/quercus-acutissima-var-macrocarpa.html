Quercus acutissima var. macrocarpa

Quercus acutissima var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese cork oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its deeply lobed, serrated leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its growth habit is typically rounded, making it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
