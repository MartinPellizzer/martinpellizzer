Quercus chichavacana

Quercus chichavacana, commonly known as the Chichavaca oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

The bark of Quercus chichavacana is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against environmental stressors.
