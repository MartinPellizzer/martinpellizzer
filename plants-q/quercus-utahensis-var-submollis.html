Quercus utahensis var. submollis

Quercus utahensis var. submollis, commonly known as the smoothleaf Utah oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are often pubescent beneath. The plant produces small, clustered acorns that are typically enclosed in cupules. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for wildlife.
