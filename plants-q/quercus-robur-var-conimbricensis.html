Quercus robur var. conimbricensis

Quercus robur var. conimbricensis, commonly known as the Portuguese oak, is a subspecies of the common oak native to Portugal.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn reddish-brown in autumn. The tree produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in mixed forests and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological significance and historical use in traditional Portuguese landscapes.
