Quercus buckleyi

Quercus buckleyi, commonly known as Buckley's oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, acorn-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in coastal areas and pine flatwoods. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, with a distinctive shape that helps in identification.

Buckley's oak plays an important role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
