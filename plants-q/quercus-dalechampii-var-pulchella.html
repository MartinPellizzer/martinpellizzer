Quercus dalechampii var. pulchella

Quercus dalechampii var. pulchella, commonly known as the silverleaf oak, is a variant of the dalechampii oak characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves.

It is a deciduous tree native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. The leaves are typically elliptical with a pointed tip and a distinctive white to grayish-white bloom on the lower surface. It produces small, dark brown acorns that are often enclosed in a cupule.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
