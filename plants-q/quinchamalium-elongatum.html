Quinchamalium elongatum

Quinchamalium elongatum, commonly known as the elongated quinchamalium, is a rare flowering plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
