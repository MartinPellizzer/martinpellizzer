Quercus eminens

Quercus eminens, commonly known as the tall oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the mountainous regions of southern Spain and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, dark green foliage that remains throughout the year. The leaves are narrow, leathery, and often have a pointed tip, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
