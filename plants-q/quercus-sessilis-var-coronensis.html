Quercus sessilis var. coronensis

Quercus sessilis var. coronensis, commonly known as the Spanish white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a serrated margin. The tree produces small, pale green acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional landscapes.
