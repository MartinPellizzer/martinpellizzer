Quercus × coscojosuberiformis

Quercus × coscojosuberiformis, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus brandegeana and Quercus lobata.

It is native to central Mexico, where it typically grows in temperate to subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are usually pale to dark green in color. Its acorns are small, enclosed in a cupule, and serve as a food source for various wildlife species.

This oak is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability.
