Quercus × jahandiezii subsp. viciosoi

Quercus × jahandiezii subsp.

viciosoi, commonly known as the Persian oak, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Iran. It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its robust, often spreading growth habit.

The plant typically features dark green, glossy leaves that turn reddish-brown in autumn. Its acorns are small and usually have a short stalk, contributing to its ecological role in supporting wildlife.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
