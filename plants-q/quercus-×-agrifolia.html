Quercus × agrifolia

Quercus × agrifolia, commonly known as the coast live oak, is a hybrid evergreen oak native to the coastal regions of California and southern Oregon.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically lobed and have a dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, pale yellow to reddish-brown acorns that mature over a period of several months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

The coast live oak is an important component of the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
