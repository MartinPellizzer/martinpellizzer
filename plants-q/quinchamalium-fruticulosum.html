Quinchamalium fruticulosum

Quinchamalium fruticulosum, commonly known as the quinchamalium shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Quinchamalium fruticulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
