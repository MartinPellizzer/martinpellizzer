Quercus synthetica var. crenatifolia

Quercus synthetica var. crenatifolia, commonly known as the wavy-leaf oak, is a hybrid species that exhibits unique botanical traits.

It features broad, lobed leaves with a distinctive wavy margin, which sets it apart from other oak varieties. The plant typically grows in temperate climates, thriving in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its acorns are small and often have a smooth surface, contributing to its ecological significance.

This variant is noted for its adaptability and resilience, making it a valuable addition to horticultural collections.
