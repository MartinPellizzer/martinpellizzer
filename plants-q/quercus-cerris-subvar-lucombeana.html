Quercus cerris subvar. lucombeana

Quercus cerris subvar. lucombeana, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a variant of the common oak native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that are typically glossy on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are enclosed in a cupule with bracts. Its bark is thick, fissured, and grayish-brown, providing a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in parks and gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
