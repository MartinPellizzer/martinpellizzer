Quercus sempervirens

Quercus sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen oak, is a species of oak native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that remain throughout the year, giving it its evergreen designation. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal and mountainous areas.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes around the world.
