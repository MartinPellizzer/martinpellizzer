Quercus × pseudocerris

Quercus × pseudocerris, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus cerris and Quercus frainetto.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its robust, spreading crown and dark green, glossy leaves that turn reddish-brown in autumn. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically encased in a cupule. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

The bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance and contributing to its ornamental value in landscaping.
