Quercus aegilops subsp. persica

Quercus aegilops subsp.

persica, commonly known as Persian oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its elongated, acorn-like fruits and broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The tree typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stress.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
