Quercus ilex subvar. laurifolia

Quercus ilex subvar. laurifolia, commonly known as the evergreen oak, is a variant of the holm oak native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery evergreen leaves that remain on the tree throughout the year. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.
