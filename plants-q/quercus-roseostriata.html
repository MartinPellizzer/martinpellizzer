Quercus roseostriata

Quercus roseostriata, commonly known as the rose-striped oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bark, which features a pale pink or rose-colored striping pattern. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a leathery texture and a waxy coating. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife.

This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and mountainous regions.
