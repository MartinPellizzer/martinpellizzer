Quesnelia rufa var. sororocabae

Quesnelia rufa var. sororocabae, commonly known as the Brazilian red bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features vibrant red bracts that contrast with its dark green, strap-like leaves. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rock surfaces. Its inflorescence is short and produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for use in indoor gardening and landscape design.
