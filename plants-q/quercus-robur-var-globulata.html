Quercus robur var. globulata

Quercus robur var. globulata, commonly known as the globe oak, is a cultivar of the common oak characterized by its distinctive rounded, globe-like crown.

It features broad, lobed leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter green underneath. The tree produces small, reddish-brown acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often planted in gardens and parks.

This cultivar is relatively slow-growing and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
