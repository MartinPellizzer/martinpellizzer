Quercus lanuginosa var. virgiliana

Quercus lanuginosa var. virgiliana, commonly known as the Virgilian woolly oak, is a variant of the woolly oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule with scales. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
