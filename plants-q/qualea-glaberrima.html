Qualea glaberrima

Qualea glaberrima, commonly known as the pink river balsa, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its light, porous wood, which is among the lightest in the plant kingdom. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by winged fruits.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
