Quercus × neomairei var. numantina

Quercus × neomairei var. numantina, commonly known as the Numantina oak, is a hybrid evergreen oak native to southern Spain.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, leathery leaves that remain on the tree throughout the year. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by acorns that mature in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and attractive appearance make it a popular choice for urban and garden settings.
