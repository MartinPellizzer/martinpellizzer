Quekettia

Quekettia, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Quekettia is often found in meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges, where it forms dense mats.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.
