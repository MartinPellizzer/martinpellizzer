Quercus erucifolia var. imeretina

Quercus erucifolia var. imeretina, commonly known as the Imereti oak, is a rare and endemic species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and elliptical leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant thrives in mountainous areas with well-drained soils and moderate climates. It plays a significant role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
