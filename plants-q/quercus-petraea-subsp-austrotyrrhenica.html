Quercus petraea subsp. austrotyrrhenica

Quercus petraea subsp.

austrotyrrhenica, commonly known as the Italian rock oak, is a subspecies of the common oak native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and leathery, elliptical leaves.

This plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a slow growth rate and can live for several centuries, making it a long-lived tree species.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
