Quercus urticifolia var. brevipetiolata

Quercus urticifolia var. brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-petioled nettleleaf oak, is a variant of the nettleleaf oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a hairy underside and short petioles, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow to reddish-brown acorns that are typically enclosed in cupules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
