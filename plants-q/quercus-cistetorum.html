Quercus cistetorum

Quercus cistetorum, commonly known as the Florida hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers appearing on separate trees, and it produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny cupule.

The bark is thick and deeply furrowed, providing a rugged appearance.
