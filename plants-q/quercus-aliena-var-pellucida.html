Quercus aliena var. pellucida

Quercus aliena var. pellucida, commonly known as the translucent oak, is a rare variant of the alien oak species.

It is characterized by its unique, semi-transparent leaves that allow light to pass through, giving it a striking appearance. This plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. Its botanical features include small, acorn-like fruits and a dense, bushy growth habit.

Due to its rarity and distinctive characteristics, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
