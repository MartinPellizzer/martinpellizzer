Quercus brutiorum

Quercus brutiorum, commonly known as the roughleaf oak, is a species of deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves that are typically 5 to 8 inches long. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Quercus brutiorum is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
