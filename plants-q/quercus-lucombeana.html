Quercus lucombeana

Quercus lucombeana, commonly known as the Lucombe's oak, is a rare and endemic species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against environmental stress.

Due to habitat loss, Quercus lucombeana is considered endangered, making it a subject of conservation interest.
