Quercus miqueliana

Quercus miqueliana, commonly known as the Miquel's oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in subtropical and tropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
