Queria hispanica

Queria hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish quinoa, is an annual plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its branched, upright stems and small, triangular leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into three-angled, spiky seed pods. Its seeds are rich in protein and nutrients, making it a valuable crop for food and agriculture.

Queria hispanica thrives in dry, temperate climates and is well-adapted to poor soil conditions.
