Quercus × albescens

Quercus × albescens, commonly known as the white oak hybrid, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Quercus alba (white oak) and Quercus bicolor (swamp white oak), exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The tree typically grows to a height of 20–30 meters, featuring a broad, spreading crown and thick, scaly bark. Its leaves are lobed, with rounded tips, and turn yellow to brown in the fall.

The acorns are small, edible, and often covered with a whitish bloom, contributing to its common name.
