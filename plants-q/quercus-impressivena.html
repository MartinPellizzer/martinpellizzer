Quercus impressivena

Quercus impressivena, commonly known as the impressively marked oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Mexico and is characterized by its distinctive leaf venation and unique bark patterns. The leaves are typically broad-ovate with a serrated margin and a deep green upper surface. This oak is known for its slow growth and longevity, often living over a century.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
