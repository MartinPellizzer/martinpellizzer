Quercus salvadorensis

Quercus salvadorensis, commonly known as the Salvadoran oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The Salvadoran oak plays a significant role in local ecosystems and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
