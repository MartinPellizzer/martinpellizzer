Quercus syspirensis

Quercus syspirensis, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico, where it thrives in temperate climates. The tree is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its acorns are small and typically have a scaly cap, which is a distinguishing feature.

Quercus syspirensis plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
