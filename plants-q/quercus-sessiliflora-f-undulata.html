Quercus sessiliflora f. undulata

Quercus sessiliflora f. undulata, commonly known as the wavyleaf sessile oak, is a variant of the sessile oak native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive wavy or undulating leaf margins, which give it its specific epithet. The leaves are typically oval to elliptical in shape and have a smooth upper surface with a hairy underside. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and canyons.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Quercus genus, particularly for its unique foliage morphology.
