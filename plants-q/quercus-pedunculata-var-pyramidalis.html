Quercus pedunculata var. pyramidalis

Quercus pedunculata var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal cork oak, is a variant of the cork oak native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinct pyramidal shape and thick, corky bark that provides insulation and protection. The leaves are typically small, leathery, and have a glossy appearance, often with a bluish-green hue. This species is well adapted to Mediterranean climates, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important plant in its native habitat.
