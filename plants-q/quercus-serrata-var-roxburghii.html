Quercus serrata var. roxburghii

Quercus serrata var. roxburghii, commonly known as the Roxburgh's oak, is a variant of the serrated oak native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its elongated, serrated leaves and acorns that are typically clustered in groups of two. The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in forested regions at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
