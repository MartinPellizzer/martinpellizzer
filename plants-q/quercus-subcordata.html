Quercus subcordata

Quercus subcordata, commonly known as the Japanese dwarf oak, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are enclosed in a dense, scaly cupule.
