Quoya atriplicina

Quoya atriplicina, commonly known as the black quoya, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. Quoya atriplicina is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden environments.
