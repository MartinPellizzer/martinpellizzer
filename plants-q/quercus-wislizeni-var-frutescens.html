Quercus wislizeni var. frutescens

Quercus wislizeni var. frutescens, commonly known as the dwarf chinquapin, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, acorn-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a pointed tip and a smooth margin.

This variety is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native range.
