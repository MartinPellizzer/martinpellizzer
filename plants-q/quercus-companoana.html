Quercus companoana

Quercus companoana, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family, Fagaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Quercus companoana is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
