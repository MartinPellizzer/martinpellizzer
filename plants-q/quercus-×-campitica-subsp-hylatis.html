Quercus × campitica subsp. hylatis

Quercus × campitica subsp.

hylatis, commonly known as the Hylatis oak, is a hybrid species within the Quercus genus, characterized by its unique combination of traits from its parent species. It typically exhibits a mix of features from Quercus campitica and Quercus hylatica, resulting in variable leaf shapes and acorn morphology.

The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas, preferring partial to full shade. Its leaves are usually lobed and have a serrated margin, while its acorns are small and often covered with a dense cap.

This subspecies plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
