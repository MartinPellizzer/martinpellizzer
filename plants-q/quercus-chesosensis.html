Quercus chesosensis

Quercus chesosensis, commonly known as the Florida maple-leaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive lobed leaves that resemble maple leaves, which are typically bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in hardwood forests.

Quercus chesosensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
