Quercus castanicarpa

Quercus castanicarpa, commonly known as the Mexican black oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and has broad, lobed leaves that are often hairy underneath. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in dry forests and woodlands.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
