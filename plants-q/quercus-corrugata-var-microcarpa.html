Quercus corrugata var. microcarpa

Quercus corrugata var. microcarpa, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals layers of mottled red and gray. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by acorns that are edible for certain wildlife species. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and turn bright red in the fall.

The species is adapted to rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
