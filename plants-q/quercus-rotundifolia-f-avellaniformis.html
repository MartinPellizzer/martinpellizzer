Quercus rotundifolia f. avellaniformis

Quercus rotundifolia f. avellaniformis, commonly known as the rounded-leaf oak hybrid, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

This form exhibits ovate to rounded leaf shapes with a smooth margin, differing from the typical lobed leaves of the parent species. It is a natural hybrid between Quercus rotundifolia and Quercus cerris, showcasing intermediate traits in both morphology and genetics. The plant thrives in temperate climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its unique appearance makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticultural collections.
