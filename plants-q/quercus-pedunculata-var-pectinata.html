Quercus pedunculata var. pectinata

Quercus pedunculata var. pectinata, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, sweet nuts enclosed in spiny husks. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible yield.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
