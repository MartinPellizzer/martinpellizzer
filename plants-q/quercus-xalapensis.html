Quercus xalapensis

Quercus xalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale grey bark and lobed leaves. The tree produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and is often found in moist, temperate climates.

Quercus xalapensis is valued for its ecological role and is also used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
