Quercus garryana var. breweri

Quercus garryana var. breweri, commonly known as the Brewer's oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and acorns that are typically encased in a dense, hairy cupule. The leaves are usually oblong to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a glossy green upper surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

This plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
