Quercus amblyodes

Quercus amblyodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oak, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and montane forests. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its acorns are small and usually require a period of cold stratification to germinate.

The species is valued for its hard wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
