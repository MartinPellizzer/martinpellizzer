Quercus infectoria var. boissieri

Quercus infectoria var. boissieri, commonly known as the Persian ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of southwestern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark bark and small, glossy leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by spiny, cup-like fruits containing hard, woody seeds. It is known for its exceptional durability and is often used in traditional construction and tool-making.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
