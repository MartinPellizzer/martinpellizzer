Quercus commiserata

Quercus commiserata, commonly known as the interior live oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and oak woodlands.

Due to its adaptability and ecological importance, it plays a significant role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
