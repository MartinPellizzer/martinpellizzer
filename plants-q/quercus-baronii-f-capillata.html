Quercus baronii f. capillata

Quercus baronii f. capillata, commonly known as the Capillary bark chinquapin, is a rare and unique variant of the chinquapin oak.

It is characterized by its finely textured, capillary-like bark that gives it a distinct appearance. This plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface.

The species is valued for its ecological role and is often found in association with other rare plant species in its native habitat.
