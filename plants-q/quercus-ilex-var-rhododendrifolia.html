Quercus ilex var. rhododendrifolia

Quercus ilex var. rhododendrifolia, commonly known as the rhododendrifoliate evergreen oak, is a rare variant of the holm oak.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and a deep green color. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and sunny exposures. It produces small, pale yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by edible acorns that mature in the fall.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
