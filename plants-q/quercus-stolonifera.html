Quercus stolonifera

Quercus stolonifera, commonly known as the creeping oak, is a shrub-like evergreen species native to the western United States.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are enclosed in a cupule, a characteristic feature of the Fagaceae family. It has a mat-forming growth habit, with stolons spreading along the ground to form dense colonies.

This species is well adapted to rocky and dry habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
