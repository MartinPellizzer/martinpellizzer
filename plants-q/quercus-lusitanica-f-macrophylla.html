Quercus lusitanica f. macrophylla

Quercus lusitanica f. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed cork oak, is a botanical variant of the cork oak native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically larger than those of the typical species. The plant is a member of the Fagaceae family and is known for its robust growth in Mediterranean climates. Its acorns are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine.

The tree is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
