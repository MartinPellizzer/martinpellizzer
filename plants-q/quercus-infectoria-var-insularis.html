Quercus infectoria var. insularis

Quercus infectoria var. insularis, commonly known as the Socotra coffee tree, is a unique species native to the island of Socotra.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown and leathery, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into hard, spiky fruits containing edible seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a resin that has been historically used for various purposes.

This species is adapted to the arid and semi-arid conditions of its native habitat, showcasing remarkable resilience to drought.
