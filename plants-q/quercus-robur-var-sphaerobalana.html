Quercus robur var. sphaerobalana

Quercus robur var. sphaerobalana, commonly known as the spherical oak, is a rare variant of the common oak characterized by its unique, rounded growth habit.

It is a deciduous tree with deeply lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged texture. It produces small, red acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

This variety is typically found in specific microclimates where it can thrive due to its particular environmental requirements.
