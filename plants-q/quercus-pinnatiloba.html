Quercus pinnatiloba

Quercus pinnatiloba, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are deeply pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in dry to moist soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates.

Quercus pinnatiloba is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
