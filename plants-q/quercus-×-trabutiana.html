Quercus × trabutiana

Quercus × trabutiana, commonly known as the Trabut's oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus cerris and Quercus robur.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are lobed, with a serrated margin, and they turn yellow in the fall. The acorns are small and often covered with a dense, scaly cap.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
