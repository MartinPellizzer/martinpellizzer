Quercus mongolica var. mongolica

Quercus mongolica var. mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mixed forests.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ecological role in maintaining forest biodiversity.
