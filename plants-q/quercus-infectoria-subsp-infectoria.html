Quercus infectoria subsp. infectoria

Quercus infectoria subsp.

infectoria, commonly known as the African rock acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. It is characterized by its thorny branches and small, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny pods containing elliptical seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood and has been historically used in construction and tool-making.
