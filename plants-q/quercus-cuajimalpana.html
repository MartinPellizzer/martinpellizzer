Quercus cuajimalpana

Quercus cuajimalpana, commonly known as the Cuajimalpa oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, acorn-like fruits. The plant typically grows in humid, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a pointed tip and a smooth margin.

Quercus cuajimalpana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
