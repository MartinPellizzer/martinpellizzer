Quercus cerris subf. acutolaciniata

Quercus cerris subf.

acutolaciniata, commonly known as the acute-laciniate oak, is a botanical variant of the Turkey oak. It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring acuminate tips and serrated margins.

The leaves are typically longer and more elongated compared to those of the typical Q. cerris. This subspecies is native to the Balkan Peninsula and parts of southern Europe.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mixed forests and along riverbanks.
