Quercus macrocarpa f. orbiculata

Quercus macrocarpa f. orbiculata, commonly known as the orbiculate bur oak, is a variant of the bur oak characterized by its rounded, orbicular leaves.

The leaves are typically larger and more rounded compared to the standard bur oak, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. This form is native to the central United States, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is a slow-growing tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters, featuring a broad, spreading canopy.

The orbiculate bur oak is valued for its ornamental value and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
