Quercus farinulenta

Quercus farinulenta, commonly known as the farinulenta oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
