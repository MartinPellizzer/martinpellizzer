Quercus faginea var. salzmanniana

Quercus faginea var. salzmanniana, commonly known as the Salzmann's oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn and its production of acorns. It typically grows in mountainous regions and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.
