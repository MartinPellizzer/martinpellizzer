Quercus × kerneri nothosubsp.

montserratii, commonly known as the Montserrat oak, is a hybrid species within the Quercus genus, characterized by its unique combination of traits from its parent species. It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in mountainous regions, exhibiting a broad, spreading crown and smooth, gray bark.

The leaves are generally elliptical with a pointed tip and are often lobed, displaying a deep green color during the growing season. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to diverse soil types and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
