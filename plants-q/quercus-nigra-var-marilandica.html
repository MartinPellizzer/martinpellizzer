Quercus nigra var. marilandica

Quercus nigra var. marilandica, commonly known as the Maryland black oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The tree produces small, dense acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 50 feet.

Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance and offering habitat for various insects and lichens.
