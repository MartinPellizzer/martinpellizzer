Quercus imbricariifolia

Quercus imbricariifolia, commonly known as the imbricata oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its serrated, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stressors.

Quercus imbricariifolia is often found in mixed forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
