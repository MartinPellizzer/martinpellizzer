Quercus peduncularis f. macrodonta

Quercus peduncularis f. macrodonta, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a variant of the Chinese chestnut tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, oblong acorns and broad, elliptical leaves with a rough upper surface. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its edible nuts.

The species is also notable for its resistance to certain pests and diseases compared to other chestnut varieties.
