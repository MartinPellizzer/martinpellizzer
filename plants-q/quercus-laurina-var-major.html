Quercus laurina var. major

Quercus laurina var. major, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen oak, is a variant of the evergreen oak native to southern Mexico.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with glossy, dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the tree throughout the year. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible acorns. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
