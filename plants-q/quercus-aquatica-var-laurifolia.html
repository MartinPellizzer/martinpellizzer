Quercus aquatica var. laurifolia

Quercus aquatica var. laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel oak, is a variant of the water oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are glossy on top and hairy underneath. The plant produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The species is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in humid climates.
