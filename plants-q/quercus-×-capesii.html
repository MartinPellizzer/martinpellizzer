Quercus × capesii

Quercus × capesii, commonly known as the Persian oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus brantii and Quercus infectoria.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in regions of Iran and the Caucasus.

The botanical features of Quercus × capesii make it an important species in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
