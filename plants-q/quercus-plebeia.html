Quercus plebeia

Quercus plebeia, commonly known as the red oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and its distinctive leaves, which are lobed and have pointed tips. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Quercus plebeia is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ecological importance in supporting biodiversity.
