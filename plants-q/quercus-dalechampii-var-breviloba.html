Quercus dalechampii var. breviloba

Quercus dalechampii var. breviloba, commonly known as the dwarf chestnut oak, is a variant of the chestnut oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are elliptic to ovate, with a short, blunt tip and a rough, hairy texture on the underside. It produces small, spiky acorns that mature over a year, and its bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

This plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
