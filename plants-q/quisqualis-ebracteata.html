Quisqualis ebracteata

Quisqualis ebracteata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, woody fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a slightly hairy texture.

The botanical features of Quisqualis ebracteata make it a distinctive and ornamental species in tropical gardens.
