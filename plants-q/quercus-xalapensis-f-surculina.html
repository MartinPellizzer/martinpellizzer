Quercus xalapensis f. surculina

Quercus xalapensis f. surculina, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a rare and distinctive variant of the white oak species.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth margin and a pale, whitish underside. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico, where it thrives in humid, temperate climates. Its acorns are small and often have a distinctive cap that covers the base of the nut.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
