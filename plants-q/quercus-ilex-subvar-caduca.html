Quercus ilex subvar. caduca

Quercus ilex subvar. caduca, commonly known as the cork oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are leathery and have a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in spring, followed by acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a habitat for numerous species.

The plant is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
