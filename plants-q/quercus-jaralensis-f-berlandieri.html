Quercus jaralensis f. berlandieri

Quercus jaralensis f. berlandieri, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a variant of the jaralensis oak native to parts of Mexico.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its white to pale gray bark and lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Botanically, it belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts.

Its morphology and ecological role make it a significant component of the temperate forest ecosystems in its native range.
