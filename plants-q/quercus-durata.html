Quercus durata

Quercus durata, commonly known as the Durata oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The tree is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and produces small, cup-shaped acorns. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also an important component of its native ecosystems.
