Quercus infectoria subsp. veneris

Quercus infectoria subsp.

veneris, commonly known as the African rock acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, leathery leaves.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by small, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is notable for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of tannins.
