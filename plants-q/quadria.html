Quadria

Quadria, commonly known as the four-leaf clover, is a rare variant of the Oxalis genus.

It is characterized by its four leaves, which are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant has small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its rarity and symbolic significance, it is often sought after in folklore and gardening.
