Quercus expansa

Quercus expansa, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy beneath and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. Quercus expansa thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its bark is thick and fissured, providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
