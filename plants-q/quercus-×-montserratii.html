Quercus × montserratii

Quercus × montserratii, commonly known as the Montserrat oak, is a hybrid evergreen oak native to the Canary Islands.

It exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Quercus suber (the cork oak) and Quercus canariensis (the Canary oak). The plant has leathery, glossy leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a deep green color. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are enclosed in cupules.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy and drought-resistant nature.
