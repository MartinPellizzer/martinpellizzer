Quercus subreticulata

Quercus subreticulata, commonly known as the Chinese red oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its deeply lobed leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. Its acorns are small and often have a scaly cap.

Quercus subreticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
