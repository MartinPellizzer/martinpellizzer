Qualea nitida

Qualea nitida, commonly known as the pink river tamarind, is a species of flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Qualea nitida is adapted to wetland and floodplain environments, demonstrating a high tolerance for seasonal flooding.

Its ecological role includes providing food and habitat for various wildlife species within its native range.
