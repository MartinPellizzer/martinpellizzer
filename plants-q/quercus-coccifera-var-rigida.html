Quercus coccifera var. rigida

Quercus coccifera var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid evergreen oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or obovate in shape. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, coastal areas and is adapted to arid and saline conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
