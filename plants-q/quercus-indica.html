Quercus indica

Quercus indica, commonly known as the Indian chestnut or Persian oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters and has a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped, with a rough, hairy surface on the underside. Its acorns are small, about 1–2 cm long, and are enclosed in a cupule.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in dry, rocky soils.
