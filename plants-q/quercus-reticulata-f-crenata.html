Quercus reticulata f. crenata

Quercus reticulata f. crenata, commonly known as the crenate nettle-leaf oak, is a unique variant of the nettle-leaf oak.

It is characterized by its distinctive crenate leaf margins, which give it a serrated appearance. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, particularly regions in China and Vietnam. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various garden conditions.
