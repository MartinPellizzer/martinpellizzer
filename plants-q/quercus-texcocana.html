Quercus texcocana

Quercus texcocana, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and acorn-producing capsules. The leaves are typically lobed, with a serrated margin, and the tree can reach heights of up to 20 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in oak woodlands and scrub habitats.

The plant plays a significant role in local ecosystems, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
