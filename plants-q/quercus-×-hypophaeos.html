Quercus × hypophaeos

Quercus × hypophaeos, commonly known as the Arizona white oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus arizonica and Quercus hypophaea.

It is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States, particularly found in Arizona and New Mexico. The plant exhibits characteristics of both parent species, with broad, lobed leaves and a rounded crown. It produces acorns that are typically small and have a short stalk, typical of many oak species.

The tree is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and heat.
