Quercus × auzendei subsp. agrifolia

Quercus × auzendei subsp.

agrifolia, commonly known as the agrifolia oak, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of southeastern France. It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath.

The plant produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of the local ecosystem and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
