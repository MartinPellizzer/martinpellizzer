Quercus germana

Quercus germana, commonly known as the German oak, is a deciduous tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Quercus germana typically grows in temperate climates and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Its bark is rugged and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance in both young and mature specimens.
