Quercus × kerneri

Quercus × kerneri, commonly known as the Japanese oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus mongolica and Quercus variabilis.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, chestnut-like acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas of East Asia.

Botanically, it exhibits features typical of the Fagaceae family, including the presence of a cupule around its acorns.
