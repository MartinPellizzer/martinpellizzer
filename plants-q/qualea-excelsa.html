Qualea excelsa

Qualea excelsa, commonly known as the Amazonian water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, providing a floating platform for various organisms. The plant has a unique ability to absorb excess nutrients from the water, helping to maintain the ecological balance of its habitat. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they open during the day, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Qualea excelsa plays a vital role in supporting biodiversity in the Amazonian region.
