Quercus speciensis

Quercus speciensis, commonly known as the Southern Red Oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its deep lobed leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are a vital food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and deeply furrowed, providing habitat for insects and small animals.

Quercus speciensis is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in furniture and construction.
