Quercus pedunculata subsp. appenina

Quercus pedunculata subsp.

appenina, commonly known as the Sierra Madre oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

The plant features broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and it produces small, cup-shaped acorns. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.

This subspecies is also recognized for its resilience to drought and cold temperatures.
