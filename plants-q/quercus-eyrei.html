Quercus eyrei

Quercus eyrei, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Quercus eyrei thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

It is often found in montane forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
