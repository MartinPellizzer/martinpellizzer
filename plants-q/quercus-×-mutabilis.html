Quercus × mutabilis

Quercus × mutabilis, commonly known as the blue oak, is a hybrid species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive bluish-gray foliage and acorns that are typically encased in a cupule. The plant exhibits a range of morphological variation, which contributes to its classification as a hybrid. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The blue oak plays an important ecological role, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
