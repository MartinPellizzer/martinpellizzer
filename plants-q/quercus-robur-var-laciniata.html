Quercus robur var. laciniata

Quercus robur var. laciniata, commonly known as the serrated oak, is a variant of the common oak characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves.

This plant is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates, often found in woodlands and forested areas. Its leaves are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath, with sharp, serrated edges. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are a food source for various wildlife.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting biodiversity.
