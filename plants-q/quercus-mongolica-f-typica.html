Quercus mongolica f. typica

Quercus mongolica f. typica, commonly known as the Mongolian oak, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad ovate leaves with coarsely serrated margins and a distinctive acorn-shaped fruit. The plant typically grows in temperate forest regions, preferring well-drained soils and moderate climates. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against environmental stressors.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting various wildlife species and contributing to forest biodiversity.
