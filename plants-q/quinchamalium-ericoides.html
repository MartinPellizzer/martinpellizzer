Quinchamalium ericoides

Quinchamalium ericoides, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, and it produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or white flowers in spring. Quinchamalium ericoides thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
