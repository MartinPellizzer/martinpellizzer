Quercus × howellii

Quercus × howellii, commonly known as the Howell's oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus garryi and Quercus rugosa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen oak native to the southwestern United States, particularly in Arizona and New Mexico. The plant typically features dense, leathery leaves with a glossy surface and a distinctive acorn shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural interest in drought-tolerant landscaping.
