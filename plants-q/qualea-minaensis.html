Qualea minaensis

Qualea minaensis, commonly known as the Minaensis Waterlily, is a species of aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the wet season, contributing to the ecosystem's biodiversity. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with abundant sunlight.

Qualea minaensis plays a vital role in water filtration and supports a wide range of aquatic life.
