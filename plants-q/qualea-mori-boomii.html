Qualea mori-boomii

Qualea mori-boomii, commonly known as the yellow water lily, is a species native to the wetlands of South America, particularly in Brazil.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the dry season, often in clusters. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of still or slow-moving waters.

Qualea mori-boomii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter for aquatic life and contributing to water filtration.
