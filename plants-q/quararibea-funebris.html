Quararibea funebris

Quararibea funebris, commonly known as the black mangrove, is a tropical evergreen tree native to coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and its ability to thrive in saline and waterlogged soils. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
