Quercus lamprophyllos

Quercus lamprophyllos, commonly known as the silverleaf oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The silverleaf oak is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
