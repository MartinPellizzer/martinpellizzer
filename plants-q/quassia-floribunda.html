Quassia floribunda

Quassia floribunda, commonly known as the African quassia, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated, yellowish fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in traditional crafts.
