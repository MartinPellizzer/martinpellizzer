Quercus robur var. extensa

Quercus robur var. extensa, commonly known as the extended oak, is a variant of the common oak characterized by its distinctive branching pattern and elongated acorns.

This variety is native to parts of Europe and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically larger and more elongated compared to the standard Quercus robur, contributing to its unique appearance. The tree is valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
