Quercus fabrei

Quercus fabrei, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Quercus fabrei typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.
