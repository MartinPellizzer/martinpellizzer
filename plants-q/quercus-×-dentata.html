Quercus × dentata

Quercus × dentata, commonly known as the Japanese oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus mongolica and Quercus serrata.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are lobed and serrated, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside, turning yellow or brown in autumn. It produces small, spiny acorns that mature over one to two years.

This species is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is often found in temperate forest ecosystems.
