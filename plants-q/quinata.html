Quinata

Quinata, commonly known as the Japanese quince, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the genus *Pseudocydonia* and is characterized by its thick, rough bark and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces a fleshy, yellow fruit that is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Quinata thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to gardens and a subject of interest in horticultural studies.
