Quercus palensis

Quercus palensis, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated edges. Its acorns are small, typically less than 1 cm in length, and are enclosed in a cupule.

Quercus palensis is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
