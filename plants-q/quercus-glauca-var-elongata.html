Quercus glauca var. elongata

Quercus glauca var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated blue oak, is a variant of the blue oak native to California.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with a broad, rounded crown and dark gray to almost black bark. The leaves are typically elongated, lance-shaped, and have a bluish-green hue on top with a silvery underside. It produces small, pale green acorns that are usually enclosed in cupules.

This variant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
