Quercus cerris subf. submucronata

Quercus cerris subf.

submucronata, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a subspecies of the common oak native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface.

The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are encased in a cupule, a feature common among oaks. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with a temperate climate.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional landscaping.
