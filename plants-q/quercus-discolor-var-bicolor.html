Quercus discolor var. bicolor

Quercus discolor var. bicolor, commonly known as the twinleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are divided into two pairs of lobes, giving it a unique appearance. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by small, red-brown acorns. Its bark is gray and fissured, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is often found in mixed hardwood forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
