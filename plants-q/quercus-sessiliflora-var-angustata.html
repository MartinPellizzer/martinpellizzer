Quercus sessiliflora var. angustata

Quercus sessiliflora var. angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy on the upper surface. The tree produces small, cup-like acorns that are typically enclosed in a dense, scaly cap. Its bark is thick and deeply furrowed, providing a rugged appearance.

This variety is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
