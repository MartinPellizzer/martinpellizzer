Quercus missouriensis

Quercus missouriensis, commonly known as the Missouri oak, is a deciduous tree native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its deep lobes on its leaves, which are typically 5 to 7 in number. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in upland forests.

The Missouri oak is valued for its hardy nature and is a key component of many native ecosystems.
