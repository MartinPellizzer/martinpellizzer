Quercus anglohondurensis

Quercus anglohondurensis, commonly known as the Honduran white oak, is a deciduous tree native to Central America, particularly Honduras.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale grey bark and broad, lobed leaves. The tree produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape tree.
