Quaqua cincta

Quaqua cincta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed quillwort, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and marshes, where it forms dense underwater mats. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are usually less than an inch in width, and it produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that develop underwater. Quaqua cincta is a member of the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its simple, branching structure and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Its presence often indicates good water quality and a stable, unpolluted habitat.
