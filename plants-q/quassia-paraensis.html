Quassia paraensis

Quassia paraensis, commonly known as the Brazilian quassia, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Simaroubaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fruiting bodies containing elongated, yellow seeds. Quassia paraensis is notable for its medicinal properties, with its bark containing compounds used in traditional medicine.

However, it is also recognized for its role in the ecosystem as a habitat and food source for various wildlife species.
