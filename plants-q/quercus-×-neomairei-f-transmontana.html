Quercus × neomairei f. transmontana

Quercus × neomairei f. transmontana, commonly known as the Transmontana oak, is a hybrid evergreen oak native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are often enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
