Quercus augustinei

Quercus augustinei, commonly known as the Augustine oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in bottomlands and along streams.

The Augustine oak is valued for its hardy nature and ornamental appeal in landscaping.
