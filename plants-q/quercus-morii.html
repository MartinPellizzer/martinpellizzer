Quercus morii

Quercus morii, commonly known as the Morii oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China. The tree is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Quercus morii is valued for its hardy nature and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.
