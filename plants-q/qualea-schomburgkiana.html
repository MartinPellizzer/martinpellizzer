Qualea schomburgkiana

Qualea schomburgkiana, commonly known as the pink river dolphin tree, is a species of tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in pairs. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in floodplain forests.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays an important role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
