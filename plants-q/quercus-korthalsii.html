Quercus korthalsii

Quercus korthalsii, commonly known as the Korthals's oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are often covered in a dense, hairy coating. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a notable species in its native habitat.
