Quercus × moultonensis

Quercus × moultonensis, commonly known as the Moulton oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus garryana and Quercus rugosa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and riparian habitats. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptical, and have a serrated margin.

The Moulton oak produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
