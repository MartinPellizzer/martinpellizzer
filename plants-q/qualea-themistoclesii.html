Qualea themistoclesii

Qualea themistoclesii, commonly known as the yellow water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are typically yellow to green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the day and close at night. It plays an important role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Qualea themistoclesii thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.
