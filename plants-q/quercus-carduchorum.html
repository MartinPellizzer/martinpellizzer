Quercus carduchorum

Quercus carduchorum, commonly known as the Caucasian oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and landscapes.
