Quercus bungeana

Quercus bungeana, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cupule-enclosed acorns, which are an important food source for wildlife. It typically grows in mountainous regions and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Quercus bungeana is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is often planted in gardens and parks.
