Quercus alba var. repanda

Quercus alba var. repanda, commonly known as the broad-leaf white oak, is a variant of the white oak native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically rounded and have a smooth upper surface. The leaves often exhibit a distinctive wavy margin, giving the plant its scientific name "repanda," which means "broad" or "expanded" in Latin. This variety tends to grow in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

It produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
