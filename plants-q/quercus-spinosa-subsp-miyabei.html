Quercus spinosa subsp. miyabei

Quercus spinosa subsp.

miyabei, commonly known as the Japanese hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its dense, spiny branches and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
