Quercus tanaicensis

Quercus tanaicensis, commonly known as the Tanaic oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces acorns that are an important food source for local wildlife. The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stressors.

The plant plays a significant role in the local ecosystem and is valued for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
