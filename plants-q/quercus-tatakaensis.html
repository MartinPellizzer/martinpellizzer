Quercus tatakaensis

Quercus tatakaensis, commonly known as the Tataka oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family Fagaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa, particularly in Kenya and Tanzania. This evergreen tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown and thick, dark green leaves. Its acorns are small and enclosed in a cupule, typical of the Fagaceae family.

The plant is valued for its hardy nature and is often found in forested and woodland habitats.
