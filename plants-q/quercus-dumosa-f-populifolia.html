Quercus dumosa f. populifolia

Quercus dumosa f. populifolia, commonly known as the coast live oak, is a variant of the coast live oak native to coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often shorter and more rounded compared to the typical coast live oak. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically growing to a height of 3 to 10 meters. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native range.
