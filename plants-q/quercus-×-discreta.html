Quercus × discreta

Quercus × discreta, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus rugosa and Quercus berlandieri.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico, where it thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with leathery, elliptical leaves that remain on the tree year-round. Its small, acorn-like fruits are typically enclosed in cupules, a feature common among oaks.
