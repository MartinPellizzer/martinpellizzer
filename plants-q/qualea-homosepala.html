Qualea homosepala

Qualea homosepala, commonly known as the pink water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink flowers that bloom above the water surface, often in clusters. The plant has broad, glossy green leaves that float on the water's surface, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in the muddy bottom of its habitat while absorbing nutrients from the water.

Qualea homosepala plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and supporting local biodiversity.
