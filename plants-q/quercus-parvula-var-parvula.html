Quercus parvula var. parvula

Quercus parvula var. parvula, commonly known as the dwarf chinquapin, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green foliage and small, acorn-like fruits that mature in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of about 6 to 10 feet and has a rounded, compact form. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
