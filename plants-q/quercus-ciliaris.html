Quercus ciliaris

Quercus ciliaris, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas and along riverbanks.

Quercus ciliaris is valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in local ecosystems.
