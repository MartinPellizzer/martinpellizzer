Quercus Turbinella Var. Californica

Quercus turbinella var. californica is a unique species of oak native to the coastal regions of California. This plant is known for its distinctive shape and adaptability to harsh coastal conditions. It features a sprawling growth habit with twisted branches and thick, leathery leaves. The tree is often found in sandy soils and can withstand salt spray and strong winds. It plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and stabilizing coastal dunes.
