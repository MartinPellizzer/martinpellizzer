Quercus × pseudoturneri

Quercus × pseudoturneri, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus lobata and Quercus rugosa.

It is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and elliptical leaves with a serrated margin. The plant typically grows in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, preferring well-drained soils in open woodlands. It produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The species is noted for its adaptability to different environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
