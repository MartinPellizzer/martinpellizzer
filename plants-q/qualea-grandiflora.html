Qualea grandiflora

Qualea grandiflora, commonly known as the pink river apple, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, yellowish fruits resembling small apples. It thrives in wetland and riparian habitats, playing a vital role in the ecological balance of its environment.

Qualea grandiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
