Quercus lusitanica subsp. faginea

Quercus lusitanica subsp.

faginea, commonly known as the Iberian chestnut oak, is a subspecies of the common oak native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits that are often covered in a dense, chestnut-like cap.

The leaves are typically lobed and have a dark green upper surface with a lighter underside. This subspecies thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mixed forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the regional flora and ecosystem.
