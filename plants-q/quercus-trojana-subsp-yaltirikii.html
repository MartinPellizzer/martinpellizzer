Quercus trojana subsp. yaltirikii

Quercus trojana subsp.

yaltirikii, commonly known as the Yaltirikii oak, is a subspecies native to Turkey. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen oak with thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The plant produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
