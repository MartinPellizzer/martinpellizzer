Quercus erythroneura

Quercus erythroneura, commonly known as the scarlet oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage in the fall, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically elliptical with a pointed tip and have serrated edges. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

This species prefers well-drained soils and is often found in upland forests.
