Quassia suaveolens

Quassia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet quassia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish wood that emits a pleasant fragrance when cut. The plant produces small, white flowers and small, round fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The bark and roots of Quassia suaveolens have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
