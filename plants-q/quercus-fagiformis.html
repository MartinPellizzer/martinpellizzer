Quercus fagiformis

Quercus fagiformis, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and oval-shaped leaves with finely serrated edges. Its acorns are small, with a scaly cap and are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Quercus fagiformis typically grows in dry to moist forested areas and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.
