Quercus humilis f. globulosa

Quercus humilis f. globulosa, commonly known as the dwarf oak, is a compact, shrub-like form of the common oak.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and dense, rounded growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in Mediterranean climates. Its small acorns are often used by wildlife, contributing to its ecological role.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
