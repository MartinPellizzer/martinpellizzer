Quamasia quamash

Quamasia quamash, commonly known as the western waterleaf, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Quamasia quamash thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wet meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and medicinal purposes.
