Quercus crassifolia var. errans

Quercus crassifolia var. errans, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a variant of the white oak species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often pubescent beneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically covered in a dense, velvety layer of hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with a temperate climate.

This variant is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
