Quercus robur var. malacophylla

Quercus robur var. malacophylla, commonly known as the sweet chestnut oak, is a variant of the common oak native to Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that are darker green on top and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in forest ecosystems.
