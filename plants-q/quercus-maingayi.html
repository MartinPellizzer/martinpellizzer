Quercus maingayi

Quercus maingayi, commonly known as the Maingay oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are enclosed in a cupule, a feature common among oaks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Quercus maingayi plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
