Quercus gambelii var. gunnisonii

Quercus gambelii var. gunnisonii, commonly known as Gunnison's scrub oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and sagebrush habitats.

The species is well adapted to withstand drought and is a key component of its ecological community.
