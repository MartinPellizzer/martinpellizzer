Quercus ajoensis

Quercus ajoensis, commonly known as the Ajo oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the mountainous regions of southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy green upper surface. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

The Ajo oak plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to forest biodiversity and soil stability.
