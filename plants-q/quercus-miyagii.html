Quercus miyagii

Quercus miyagii, commonly known as the Japanese oak, is a species of deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical leaves with a serrated margin. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are encased in a scaly cap. It typically grows in forested areas and thrives in temperate climates.
