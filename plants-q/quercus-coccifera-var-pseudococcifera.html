Quercus coccifera var. pseudococcifera

Quercus coccifera var. pseudococcifera, commonly known as the Algerian rock chestnut, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, edible acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is well adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This variety is often used in traditional landscaping and has cultural significance in some Mediterranean countries.
