Quercus spicata f. gracilipes

Quercus spicata f. gracilipes, commonly known as the slenderleaf dwarf oak, is a rare and compact form of the common dwarf oak.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are clustered in tight, spikelike inflorescences. It is native to specific regions in the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
