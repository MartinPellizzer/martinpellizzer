Quercus crinita

Quercus crinita, commonly known as the silky oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and large, ovate leaves that are hairy on the underside. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by small, spiky acorns that mature in the fall. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The silky oak is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
