Quercus pyrenaica f. laciniata

Quercus pyrenaica f. laciniata, commonly known as the Pyrenean serrated oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive lobed leaves, which are finely serrated and often have a bluish-green hue underneath. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are dispersed by animals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
