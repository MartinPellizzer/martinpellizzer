Quercus laurina var. ocoteifolia

Quercus laurina var. ocoteifolia, commonly known as the Mexican laurel oak, is a variant of the laurel oak species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by acorns that are edible for certain wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
