Quercus ilex var. aperta

Quercus ilex var. aperta, commonly known as the Aleppo oak, is a variant of the evergreen oak native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically larger and more elongated compared to other Quercus species. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Mediterranean landscapes.
