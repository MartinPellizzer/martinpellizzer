Quercus undulata var. gambelii

Quercus undulata var. gambelii, commonly known as Gambel's curlleaf oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, curling leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
