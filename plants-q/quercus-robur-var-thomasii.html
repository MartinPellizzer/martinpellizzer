Quercus robur var. thomasii

Quercus robur var. thomasii, commonly known as the Thomas oak, is a rare variant of the common English oak.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves with a serrated margin and a distinctive acorn shape. This species is native to a limited geographic region in Europe, primarily in the Pyrenees. Its botanical features include a sturdy, slow-growing trunk and a broad, spreading crown.

Due to its rarity, it is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
