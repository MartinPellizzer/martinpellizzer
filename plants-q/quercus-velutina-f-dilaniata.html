Quercus velutina f. dilaniata

Quercus velutina f. dilaniata, commonly known as the red oak variant, is a botanical form of the chestnut oak.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a velvety underside. This form is native to the eastern United States, particularly in regions with moist, well-drained soils. The acorns are small and typically have a smooth cap, and the plant is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
