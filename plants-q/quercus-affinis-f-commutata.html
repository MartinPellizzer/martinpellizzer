Quercus affinis f. commutata

Quercus affinis f. commutata, commonly known as the red oak variant, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive lobed leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against pests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
