Quercus calliprinos var. recurvans

Quercus calliprinos var. recurvans, commonly known as the Sierra Madre oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and elongated, alternate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid and semi-arid climates of its native range.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in the chaparral and woodland ecosystems.
