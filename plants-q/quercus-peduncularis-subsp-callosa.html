Quercus peduncularis subsp. callosa

Quercus peduncularis subsp.

callosa, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by spiny, hairy fruits called burrs. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in forestry and agriculture.
