Quercus phanera

Quercus phanera, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Quercus phanera typically grows in mountainous regions and prefers well-drained soils.
