Quercus obtusissima

Quercus obtusissima, commonly known as the Japanese oak, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, pale yellow to brown acorns, which are encased in a cupule. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Quercus obtusissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
