Quercus altissima

Quercus altissima, commonly known as the tall chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and moderate growth rate, often reaching heights of up to 60 feet. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with smooth edges and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils in moist to mesic habitats.
