Quercus arcoglandis

Quercus arcoglandis, commonly known as the Sierra foothill oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a dense, hairy cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The Sierra foothill oak plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
