Quiabentia

Quiabentia, commonly known as the sacred plant of the indigenous communities in the Amazon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which bloom in clusters along its slender stems. The plant contains various alkaloids, some of which have traditional uses in medicinal and spiritual practices. Its leaves are often used in rituals, while the roots are valued for their purported healing properties.

Due to its cultural significance, Quiabentia is both revered and protected in its native regions.
