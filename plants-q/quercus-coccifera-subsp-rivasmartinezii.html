Quercus coccifera subsp. rivasmartinezii

Quercus coccifera subsp.

rivasmartinezii, commonly known as the Rivasmartinez's rock oak, is a subspecies native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in rocky and coastal areas. It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in dry, sunny environments.

The plant features small, leathery leaves that are usually dark green and elliptical in shape. Its acorns are small and have a distinctive cap that covers the nut.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand harsh conditions, including salt spray and poor soil, making it a resilient component of coastal ecosystems.
