Quercus dalmatica

Quercus dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Quercus dalmatica is valued for its hardy nature and is often used in reforestation efforts.
