Quercus stellata

Quercus stellata, commonly known as the starleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped leaves, which are typically 5 to 7 lobes, giving it a unique appearance. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Quercus stellata is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
