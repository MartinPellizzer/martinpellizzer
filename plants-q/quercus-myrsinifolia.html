Quercus myrsinifolia

Quercus myrsinifolia, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are borne in clusters, followed by acorns that are encased in a cupule. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
