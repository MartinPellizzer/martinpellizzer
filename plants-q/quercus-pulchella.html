Quercus pulchella

Quercus pulchella, commonly known as the pink oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking pinkish-red foliage, which turns vibrant red in the fall. The tree has alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape with finely toothed margins. Its acorns are small, with a scaly cap and are typically eaten by various wildlife species.

Quercus pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
