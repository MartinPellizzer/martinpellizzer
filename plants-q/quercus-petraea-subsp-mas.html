Quercus petraea subsp. mas

Quercus petraea subsp.

mas, commonly known as the sessile oak, is a subspecies of the common oak native to Europe. It is characterized by its short, ascending peduncles and acorns that are typically covered in a dense, velvety pubescence.

The leaves are generally broader and more rounded compared to other oaks, with a serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

This subspecies is valued for its hardy nature and is used in landscaping and forestry.
