Quercus sessiliflora var. longifolia

Quercus sessiliflora var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf chestnut oak, is a variant of the sessiliflora oak species.

It is characterized by its elongated, sessile leaves that are typically 5 to 10 inches long. The plant is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible acorns that are a food source for various wildlife.

The tree is often found in mixed hardwood forests and is valued for its ecological and historical significance.
