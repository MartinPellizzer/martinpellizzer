Quercus acutissima subsp. roxburghii

Quercus acutissima subsp.

roxburghii, commonly known as the Chinese sharpleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically pubescent on the underside.

The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are enclosed in cupules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
