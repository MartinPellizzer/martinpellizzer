Quassia laevis

Quassia laevis, commonly known as the white quassia, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Simaroubaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and compound leaves. The plant is notable for its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Quassia laevis is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its bitter compounds used in traditional medicine.
