Quercus kirinensis

Quercus kirinensis, commonly known as the Japanese oak, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cupule-enclosed acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife. Quercus kirinensis typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
