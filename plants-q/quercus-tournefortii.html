Quercus tournefortii

Quercus tournefortii, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

The bark of Quercus tournefortii is thick and fissured, providing protection against fires and drought.
