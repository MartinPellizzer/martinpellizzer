Quercus noviaureliani

Quercus noviaureliani, commonly known as the Novaurelian oak, is a species of deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its acorn-producing habit and its distinctive leaves, which are typically oval with a smooth margin. The bark of this oak is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

The plant plays an important ecological role by supporting various wildlife species and contributing to forest biodiversity.
