Quercus congesta var. genuina

Quercus congesta var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and elliptical, lobed leaves that are typically 5 to 7 inches long. The tree produces small, clustered acorns that are edible and often consumed by wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in its native habitat.
