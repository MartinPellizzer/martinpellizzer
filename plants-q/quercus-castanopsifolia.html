Quercus castanopsifolia

Quercus castanopsifolia, commonly known as the red oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its deeply lobed, serrated leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

Quercus castanopsifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed coniferous forests.
