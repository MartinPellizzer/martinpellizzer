Quercus ilex subvar. minor

Quercus ilex subvar. minor, commonly known as the Kermes Oak, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are often covered in a sticky resin. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
