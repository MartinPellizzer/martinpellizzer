Quercus reticulata var. greggii

Quercus reticulata var. greggii, commonly known as Gregg's netleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its intricate, net-veined leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a distinctive species within the Quercus genus.
