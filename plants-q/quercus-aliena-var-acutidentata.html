Quercus aliena var. acutidentata

Quercus aliena var. acutidentata, commonly known as the sharp-toothed oak, is a variant of the alien oak species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its acutely pointed leaf lobes and a generally small to medium tree size. The leaves are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside, with a leathery texture. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in montane forests.

Its acorns are edible and serve as an important food source for various wildlife species.
