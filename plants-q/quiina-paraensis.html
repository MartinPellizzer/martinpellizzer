Quiina paraensis

Quiina paraensis, commonly known as the Peruvian quinoa, is a species native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is closely related to other quinoa species. The plant is characterized by its small, edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its nutritional value, it has gained attention as a potential crop for sustainable agriculture.
