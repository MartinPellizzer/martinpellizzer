Quercus ithaburensis subsp. ithaburensis

Quercus ithaburensis subsp.

ithaburensis, commonly known as the Ithabure oak, is a subspecies of the oak tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and broad, lobed leaves that are typically dark green on top and paler underneath.

The tree grows to a moderate height, with a rounded crown and rough, fissured bark. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in its native range, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
