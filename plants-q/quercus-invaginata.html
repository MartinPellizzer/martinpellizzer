Quercus invaginata

Quercus invaginata, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut or the velvet oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits. Quercus invaginata is known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some areas outside its native range.
