Quercus arcola

Quercus arcola, commonly known as the Sierra Madre oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its deep lobed leaves and produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife. The tree typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its bark is often rough and fissured, providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Quercus arcola plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native habitat.
