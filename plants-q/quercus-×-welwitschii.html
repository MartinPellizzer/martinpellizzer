Quercus × welwitschii

Quercus × welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch oak, is a hybrid species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The tree produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
