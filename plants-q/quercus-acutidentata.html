Quercus acutidentata

Quercus acutidentata, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits that are encased in a spiny husk. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible nuts.

Quercus acutidentata is also valued for its ecological role in supporting diverse wildlife and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
