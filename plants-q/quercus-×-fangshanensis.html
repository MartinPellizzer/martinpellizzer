Quercus × fangshanensis

Quercus × fangshanensis, commonly known as the Fangshan oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus przewalskii and Quercus variabilis.

It is native to China, specifically found in the Fangshan region of Beijing, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the autumn, and it produces acorns that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic diversity and adaptability to different soil types, making it a significant species in ecological studies.
