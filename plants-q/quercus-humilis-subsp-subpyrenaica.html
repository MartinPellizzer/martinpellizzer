Quercus humilis subsp. subpyrenaica

Quercus humilis subsp.

subpyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean dwarf oak, is a small evergreen oak native to the Pyrenees region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are often covered in a dense pubescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh, mountainous environments of its native habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
