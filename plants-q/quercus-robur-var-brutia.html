Quercus robur var. brutia

Quercus robur var. brutia, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a variant of the common oak native to Turkey and the eastern Mediterranean.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against pests and diseases.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is often used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
