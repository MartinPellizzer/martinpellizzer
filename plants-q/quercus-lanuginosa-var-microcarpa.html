Quercus lanuginosa var. microcarpa

Quercus lanuginosa var. microcarpa, commonly known as the silky oak or microcarpa oak, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and small, acorn-like fruits. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a leathery texture and a hairy underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
