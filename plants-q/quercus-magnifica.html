Quercus magnifica

Quercus magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent oak, is a large deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, pale green acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its trunk is often deeply fissured, providing habitat for various insects and birds.

Quercus magnifica is valued for its strong, durable wood and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
