Quercus bennettii

Quercus bennettii, commonly known as the Bennett oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and is often found in cloud forests at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its wood is dense and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture-making.

The plant plays a crucial role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native habitat.
