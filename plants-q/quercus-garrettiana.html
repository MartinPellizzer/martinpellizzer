Quercus garrettiana

Quercus garrettiana, commonly known as the Sierra foothill oak, is a deciduous tree native to California and parts of Nevada.

It typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring a broad, spreading canopy and a trunk that is often fluted or ridged. The leaves are alternate, simple, and lobed, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter green underside. Its acorns are small, typically 1–2 centimeters in length, and are encased in a cupule that is hairy and persistent.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and plays an important role in local ecosystems.
