Quercus serrata var. tanbakuri

Quercus serrata var. tanbakuri, commonly known as the Tanbakuri oak, is a variant of the Japanese oak species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

The leaves are typically narrow, serrated, and have a bluish-green upper surface with a pale underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. The tree produces small, reddish-brown acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Its bark is rugged and fissured, providing a textured appearance that adds to its visual interest in landscaping.
