Quercus microchlamys

Quercus microchlamys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved white oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green on top with a pale underside. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by acorns that mature in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, acidic forest environments.

The botanical features of Quercus microchlamys make it an important component of the ecological systems it inhabits.
