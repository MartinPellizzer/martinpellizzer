Quercus × barladensis

Quercus × barladensis, commonly known as the Barlad oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus robur and Quercus petraea.

It is a deciduous tree native to the Carpathian Mountains in Europe, characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and robust growth habit. The plant exhibits features of both parent species, including a combination of leaf morphology and acorn structure. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mixed forests.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic diversity and adaptability to various soil types.
