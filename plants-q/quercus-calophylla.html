Quercus calophylla

Quercus calophylla, commonly known as the California black oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its dark, deeply fissured bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. Its ecological significance is notable in supporting biodiversity within its native range.
