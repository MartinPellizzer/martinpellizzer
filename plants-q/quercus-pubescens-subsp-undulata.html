Quercus pubescens subsp. undulata

Quercus pubescens subsp.

undulata, commonly known as the undulating oak, is a subspecies of the downy oak native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that have a distinctive undulating or wavy edge.

The tree typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its acorns are small and often have a dense, scaly cap.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
