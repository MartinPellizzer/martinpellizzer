Quamoclit × sloteri

Quamoclit × sloteri, commonly known as the hybrid sweet pepper, is a botanical cross between Capsicum annuum and Capsicum baccatum.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, orange, or yellow fruits that are typically elongated and tapering. The plant is an annual herb with bushy growth habit, featuring lobed, hairy leaves and showy yellow flowers. Its fruits are rich in capsaicinoids, giving them a spicy flavor, and are often used in culinary applications.

The hybrid is valued for its adaptability to various climates and its ornamental and edible qualities.
