Quercus pedunculata subsp. brutia

Quercus pedunculata subsp.

brutia, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and dark, glossy leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The tree typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to semi-arid climates. It has a slow growth rate and can live for several centuries, making it a long-lived species.

Its wood is hard and durable, historically used for construction and furniture.
