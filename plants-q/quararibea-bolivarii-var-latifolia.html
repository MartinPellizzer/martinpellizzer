Quararibea bolivarii var. latifolia

Quararibea bolivarii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved quina tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often leathery in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and is often found in dry forests and scrublands.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
