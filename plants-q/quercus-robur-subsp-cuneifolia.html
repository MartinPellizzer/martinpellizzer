Quercus robur subsp. cuneifolia

Quercus robur subsp.

cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oak, is a subspecies of the common oak characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland habitats.

The leaves are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a serrated margin. This subspecies produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

It is valued for its hardy nature and is often used in ornamental landscaping.
