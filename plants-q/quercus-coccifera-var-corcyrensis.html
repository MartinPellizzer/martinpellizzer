Quercus coccifera var. corcyrensis

Quercus coccifera var. corcyrensis, commonly known as the Corcyrean holly oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, dark blue to black berries. It thrives in rocky, dry soils and is adapted to coastal and arid environments.

This variety is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
