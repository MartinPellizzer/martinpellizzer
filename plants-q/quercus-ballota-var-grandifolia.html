Quercus ballota var. grandifolia

Quercus ballota var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved rock oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often lobed and have a distinctive, rugged appearance. The tree produces small, pale green acorns that are typically enclosed in cupules with bristly hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
