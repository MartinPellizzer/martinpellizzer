Quercus armeniaca

Quercus armeniaca, commonly known as the Armenian chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are encased in a spiny cupule. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a rough, hairy texture on the underside.

The Armenian chestnut is valued for its hardy nature and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
