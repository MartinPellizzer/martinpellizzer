Quercus × neotharpii

Quercus × neotharpii, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut hybrid, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is a natural hybrid between Quercus glauca and Quercus serrata. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
