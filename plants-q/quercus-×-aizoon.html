Quercus × aizoon

Quercus × aizoon, commonly known as the Arizona white oak, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It exhibits characteristics of both Quercus arizonica and Quercus engelmannii, resulting in a unique combination of traits. The plant has a rounded crown, smooth gray bark, and produces acorns that are typically covered with a dense layer of hairs. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and have a rough texture, typical of many oak species.

The Arizona white oak plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
