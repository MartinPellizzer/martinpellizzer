Quercus lanuginosa var. polycarpa

Quercus lanuginosa var. polycarpa, commonly known as the woolly oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and acorns that are typically clustered in groups of two to four. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its botanical features include alternate, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a distinctive, persistent calyx that remains on the acorn.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
