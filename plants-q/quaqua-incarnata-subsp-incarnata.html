Quaqua incarnata subsp. incarnata

Quaqua incarnata subsp.

incarnata, commonly known as the common red root, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
