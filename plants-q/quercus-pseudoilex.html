Quercus pseudoilex

Quercus pseudoilex, commonly known as the Canary Island oak, is a evergreen tree native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are elongated and have a distinctive waxy coating. The tree produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow-green in color, followed by acorns that are encased in a cupule. It thrives in Mediterranean climates and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
