Quercus rubra var. muehlenbergii

Quercus rubra var. muehlenbergii, commonly known as the Muhlenberg oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lobed leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall, and its acorns are typically covered with a dense, scaly cap. The tree has a broad, rounded crown and can grow up to 40 meters tall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and ornamental purposes.
