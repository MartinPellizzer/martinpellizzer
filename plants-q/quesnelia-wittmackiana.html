Quesnelia wittmackiana

Quesnelia wittmackiana, commonly known as the Wittmack's bromeliad, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Quesnelia wittmackiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated as a houseplant in many regions.
