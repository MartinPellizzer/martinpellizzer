Quercus dipsacina

Quercus dipsacina, commonly known as the common bracken oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its distinctive, bracken-like leaves that are dark green and glossy. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are a food source for various wildlife. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant has historical uses in traditional medicine and is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
