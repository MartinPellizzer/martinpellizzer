Quintilia coerulea

Quintilia coerulea, commonly known as the sky-blue bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. Quintilia coerulea typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
