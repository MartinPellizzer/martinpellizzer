Quinasis

Quinasis, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the genus Tithonia in the family Asteraceae, characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange blooms. The plant features heart-shaped, broad leaves and can grow up to several feet tall, making it a striking addition to gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used as a ornamental plant.

Botanically, it is noted for its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
