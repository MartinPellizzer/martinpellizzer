Quercus eugeniifolia

Quercus eugeniifolia, commonly known as the Eugenialeaf oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically covered in a dense, fuzzy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The botanical features of Quercus eugeniifolia make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
