Quercus lanuginosa var. tommasinii

Quercus lanuginosa var. tommasinii, commonly known as the Tommasini's hairy oak, is a variant of the hairy oak native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that mature over several months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
