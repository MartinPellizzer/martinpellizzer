Quinaria hederacea

Quinaria hederacea, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It has a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for ground cover in gardens.

Quinaria hederacea is also known for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor.
