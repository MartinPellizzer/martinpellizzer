Quercus aristata

Quercus aristata, commonly known as the Sierra Madre oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and elongated, acorn-producing capsules. The leaves are typically narrow, with a pointed tip and a rough, textured upper surface. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
