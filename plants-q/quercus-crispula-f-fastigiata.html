Quercus crispula f. fastigiata

Quercus crispula f. fastigiata, commonly known as the upright form of the Japanese oak, is a compact, columnar shrub characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are often curled or crinkled, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically clustered in groups of two. It is native to Japan and thrives in well-drained soils with partial to full sunlight.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.
