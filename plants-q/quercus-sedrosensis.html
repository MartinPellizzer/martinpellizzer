Quercus sedrosensis

Quercus sedrosensis, commonly known as the Sedrosensis oak, is a rare and endemic species found in specific regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and leathery, evergreen leaves. The tree typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stressors.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
