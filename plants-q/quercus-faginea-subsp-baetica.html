Quercus faginea subsp. baetica

Quercus faginea subsp.

baetica, commonly known as the Iberian oak, is a subspecies native to southern Spain and northern Africa. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and acorns that are typically enclosed in cupules.

The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils, often found in mountainous regions. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stress.

This subspecies plays an important role in local ecosystems by supporting diverse wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
