Quercus catesbaei

Quercus catesbaei, commonly known as the sand live oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and sandy habitats.

The sand live oak is known for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a valuable component of its native ecosystems.
