Quercus emoryi

Quercus emoryi, commonly known as Emory oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green and have a rough texture, while its bark is fissured and scaly.

Emory oak is valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
