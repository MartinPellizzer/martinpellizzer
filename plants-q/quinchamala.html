Quinchamala

Quinchamala, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a climbing habit and produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Quinchamala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming pattern.
