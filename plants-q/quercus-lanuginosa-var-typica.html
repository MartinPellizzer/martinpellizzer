Quercus lanuginosa var. typica

Quercus lanuginosa var. typica, commonly known as the silky oak, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often grey or brown bark and its distinctive, silvery-white undersides on the leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a soft, fuzzy texture. It produces small, red or yellowish flowers in spring, followed by acorn-like fruits.

This species is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
