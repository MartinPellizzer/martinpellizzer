Quercus pedunculata var. dauvessei

Quercus pedunculata var. dauvessei, commonly known as the Dauvesse oak, is a variant of the pedunculate oak native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its lobed leaves and acorns, which are a key feature in its ecological role. The plant has a rugged, often twisted trunk and exhibits a broad, spreading crown. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
