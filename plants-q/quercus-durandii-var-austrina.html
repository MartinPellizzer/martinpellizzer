Quercus durandii var. austrina

Quercus durandii var. austrina, commonly known as the Australian red oak, is a deciduous tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark and elliptical, glossy leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, pale green acorns that are encased in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and hilly regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
