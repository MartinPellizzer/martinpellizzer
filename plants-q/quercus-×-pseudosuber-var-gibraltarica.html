Quercus × pseudosuber var. gibraltarica

Quercus × pseudosuber var. gibraltarica, commonly known as the Gibraltar oak, is a hybrid species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with dark green, leathery leaves that are oval in shape. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate.

This variety is notable for its resilience and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
