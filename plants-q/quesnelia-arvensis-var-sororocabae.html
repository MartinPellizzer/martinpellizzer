Quesnelia arvensis var. sororocabae

Quesnelia arvensis var. sororocabae, commonly known as the Brazilian star sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
