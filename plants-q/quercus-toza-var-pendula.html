Quercus toza var. pendula

Quercus toza var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping chinquapin, is a unique variant of the chinquapin oak.

It is characterized by its pendulous, weeping habit, with branches drooping gracefully from the main trunk. The plant features small, acorn-like fruits that are typically clustered in groups of two or three. Its leaves are elliptical, with a rough texture and a dark green upper surface.

This rare and ornamental tree is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
