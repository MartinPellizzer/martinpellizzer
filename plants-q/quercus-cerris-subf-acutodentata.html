Quercus cerris subf. acutodentata

Quercus cerris subf.

acutodentata, commonly known as the acute-toothed Turkey Oak, is a subspecies of the Turkey Oak native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves with acute, pointed tips and serrated edges.

The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
