Quercus amphioxys

Quercus amphioxys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

The fruit is a capsule that releases minute seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
