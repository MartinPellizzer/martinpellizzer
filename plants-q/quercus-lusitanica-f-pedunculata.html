Quercus lusitanica f. pedunculata

Quercus lusitanica f. pedunculata, commonly known as the pedunculate oak, is a variant of the Portuguese oak.

It is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant features broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its acorns are enclosed in a cupule and are an important food source for wildlife.

The tree is valued for its hardy nature and is often used in forestry and landscaping.
