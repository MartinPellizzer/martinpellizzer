Quassia

Quassia, commonly known as the quassia tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Simaroubaceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to elongated, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. Quassia is particularly noted for its medicinal properties, with its bark containing compounds like quassin, which have bitter and antimicrobial effects.

The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic bark and its use in traditional medicine.
