Quercus aegylopifolia

Quercus aegylopifolia, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and lobed leaves. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mixed forests.
