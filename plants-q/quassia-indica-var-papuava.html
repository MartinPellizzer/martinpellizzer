Quassia indica var. papuava

Quassia indica var. papuava, commonly known as the Papuan quassia, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Indonesia and Papua.

It belongs to the Simaroubaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its most notable feature is the presence of quassinoids, which are bitter compounds used in traditional medicine.

The tree is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and as a source of natural insecticides.
