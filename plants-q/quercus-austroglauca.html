Quercus austroglauca

Quercus austroglauca, commonly known as the southern blue oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its blue-gray foliage and acorn-producing capabilities, which are important for wildlife. The tree typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges and often turn reddish in the fall.

Quercus austroglauca is adapted to Mediterranean climates with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers.
