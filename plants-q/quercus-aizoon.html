Quercus aizoon

Quercus aizoon, commonly known as the desert oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to conserve water in arid environments. The tree produces acorns that are enclosed in cupules, which are distinctive and often used for identification. Quercus aizoon typically grows in rocky soils and can reach heights of up to 15 meters.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration in dry climates.
