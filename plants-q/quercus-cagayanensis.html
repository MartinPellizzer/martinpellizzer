Quercus cagayanensis

Quercus cagayanensis, commonly known as the Cagayan oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family Fagaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of northern Luzon in the Philippines, where it thrives in moist, cool climates. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and acorn-like fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stressors.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting local wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
