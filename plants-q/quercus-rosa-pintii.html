Quercus rosa-pintii

Quercus rosa-pintii, commonly known as the pink rose oak, is a rare and distinctive species of oak native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring leaves that are often tinged with pink or reddish hues, especially during the growing season. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically reddish-brown in color and serve as a food source for various wildlife. Quercus rosa-pintii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens due to its striking appearance.

However, it is a threatened species, facing habitat loss and environmental degradation in its native range.
