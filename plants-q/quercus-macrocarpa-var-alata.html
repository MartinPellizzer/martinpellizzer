Quercus macrocarpa var. alata

Quercus macrocarpa var. alata, commonly known as the spreading oak, is a variant of the bur oak characterized by its distinctive, wide-spreading crown.

This tree is native to the central United States and is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a rough, textured surface, turning yellow in the fall. The tree produces large, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
