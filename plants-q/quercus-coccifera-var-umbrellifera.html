Quercus coccifera var. umbrellifera

Quercus coccifera var. umbrellifera, commonly known as the umbrella cork oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like crown formed by closely spaced branches. The leaves are typically evergreen, leathery, and have a distinctive elliptical shape with a pointed tip. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This plant is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
