Quercus macrocarpa f. oliviformis

Quercus macrocarpa f. oliviformis, commonly known as the oliveleaf black oak, is a variant of the bur oak characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are elliptical, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter, often yellowish underside, giving it a unique appearance. The plant is native to the central United States and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, reddish-brown acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
