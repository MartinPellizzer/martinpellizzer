Quercus congesta

Quercus congesta, commonly known as the California black oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark, deeply fissured bark and acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. The leaves are broad-ovate with a lobed margin and a short petiole. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils in dry to moist environments.

This species plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
