Quercus × oviedoensis

Quercus × oviedoensis, commonly known as the Oviedo oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus petraea and Quercus robur.

It is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula, particularly in Spain. The plant exhibits characteristics of both parent species, with broad leaves and a rounded crown. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mixed forests.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability and ecological significance in its native habitat.
