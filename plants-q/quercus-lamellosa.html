Quercus lamellosa

Quercus lamellosa, commonly known as the Florida hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in upland forests.

Quercus lamellosa plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
