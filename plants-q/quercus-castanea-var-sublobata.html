Quercus castanea var. sublobata

Quercus castanea var. sublobata, commonly known as the Mexican chestnut oak, is a variant of the chestnut oak native to Mexico.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often coarsely toothed. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically enclosed in a cupule. Its bark is typically smooth and grayish in younger trees, becoming more fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.
