Quercus × parkeri

Quercus × parkeri, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus polymorpha and Quercus brandegeei.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico, where it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The tree typically grows to a height of 10–15 meters, featuring a broad, spreading crown and smooth, gray bark. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptical, and have a glossy upper surface with a hairy underside.

The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
