Quercus arcaula var. microcalyx

Quercus arcaula var. microcalyx, commonly known as the dwarf chinquapin, is a rare and small species of oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with short, twisted branches and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces acorns that are typically clustered in groups of two to four, a distinctive feature of the species. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a smooth or slightly lobed margin.

This variety is often found in moist, acidic soils in shaded, forested areas.
