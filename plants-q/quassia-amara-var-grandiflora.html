Quassia amara var. grandiflora

Quassia amara var. grandiflora, commonly known as the quassia tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant yellow flowers and long, slender leaves. The tree produces small, woody fruits that contain bitter seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used for centuries to treat various ailments.
