Quercus brachystachys f. venulosa

Quercus brachystachys f. venulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved white oak, is a botanical variant of the white oak species.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves with a distinctive venation pattern. The plant is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This form is notable for its ecological role and ornamental value in horticulture.
