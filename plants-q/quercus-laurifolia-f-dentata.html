Quercus laurifolia f. dentata

Quercus laurifolia f. dentata, commonly known as the toothed laurel oak, is a variant of the laurel oak species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green foliage and distinctive toothed margins on its leaves. The plant produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important component of the local ecosystem, supporting various wildlife species.
