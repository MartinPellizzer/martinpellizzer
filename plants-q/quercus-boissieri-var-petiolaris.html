Quercus boissieri var. petiolaris

Quercus boissieri var. petiolaris, commonly known as the petioled oak, is a variant of the Boissier oak native to the mountainous regions of northern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring elongated petioles that give it a unique appearance compared to other oak species. The leaves are typically broad and lobed, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various local wildlife species.
