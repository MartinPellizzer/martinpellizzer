Quercus texcocana var. ampla

Quercus texcocana var. ampla, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It typically grows in dry to moist soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable regions.
