Quercus petraea var. mespilifolia

Quercus petraea var. mespilifolia, commonly known as the mespilifolia oak, is a variant of the sessile oak native to Europe.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
