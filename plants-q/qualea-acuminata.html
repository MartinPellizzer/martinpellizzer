Qualea acuminata

Qualea acuminata, commonly known as the pink river tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish coating. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange or red fruits containing three to four seeds. It thrives in wet, tropical environments and is often found near waterways.

Qualea acuminata plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
