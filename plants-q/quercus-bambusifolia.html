Quercus bambusifolia

Quercus bambusifolia, commonly known as the bamboo-leaf oak, is a unique species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like leaves that are elongated and often have a waxy coating. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its acorns are small and usually take one to two years to mature.

The botanical features of Quercus bambusifolia make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
