Quercus pulchra

Quercus pulchra, commonly known as the showy oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing habitat for various insects and lichens.

Quercus pulchra is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in its native habitats.
