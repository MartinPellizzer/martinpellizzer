Quercus aegilops var. alba

Quercus aegilops var. alba, commonly known as the white oak, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection.

The white oak is valued for its durable wood and plays a significant role in forest ecosystems.
