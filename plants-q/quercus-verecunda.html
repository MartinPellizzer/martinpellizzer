Quercus verecunda

Quercus verecunda, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and lobed leaves. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils in temperate climates.

Quercus verecunda is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
