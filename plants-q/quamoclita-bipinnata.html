Quamoclita bipinnata

Quamoclita bipinnata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately divided into smaller, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its fruits are capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Quamoclita bipinnata is often found in disturbed habitats and can become invasive in certain areas.
