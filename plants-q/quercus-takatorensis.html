Quercus takatorensis

Quercus takatorensis, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its evergreen leaves that are leathery and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
