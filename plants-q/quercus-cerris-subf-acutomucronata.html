Quercus cerris subf. acutomucronata

Quercus cerris subf.

acutomucronata, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a subspecies of the common oak native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside.

The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are often covered in a dense, hairy layer. Its bark is thick, fissured, and ranges from gray to brown in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in forestry and landscaping.
