Quercus robur subvar. pseudopedunculata

Quercus robur subvar. pseudopedunculata, commonly known as the Spanish chestnut oak, is a variant of the common oak native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically darker green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mixed forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
