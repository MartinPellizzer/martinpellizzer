Quercus cerris f. austriaca

Quercus cerris f. austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian Turkey Oak, is a distinctive subspecies of the Turkey Oak.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are often lobed and have a unique, almost wavy appearance. The tree produces small, spiky capsules that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in parks and gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
