Quercus minima f. pygmaea

Quercus minima f. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf oak, is a small shrub-like form of the minima oak.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Florida and Georgia. This plant typically grows to a height of less than one meter, with dense, compact foliage. Its leaves are small, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife.
