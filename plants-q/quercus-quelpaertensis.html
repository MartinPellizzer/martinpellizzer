Quercus quelpaertensis

Quercus quelpaertensis, commonly known as the Jeju oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family Fagaceae.

It is native to the Korean Peninsula, particularly found on the island of Jeju. This evergreen tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves. The tree produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

It is valued for its ecological role and is also used in traditional medicine.
