Quercus calliprinos var. subglobosa

Quercus calliprinos var. subglobosa, commonly known as the Sierra Madre oak, is a variant of the California black oak.

It is a deciduous tree native to the western United States, particularly in California and Nevada. The plant has a rounded, broad crown and produces acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a dark green upper surface with a lighter underside.

The bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against fire and pests.
