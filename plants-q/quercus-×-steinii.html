Quercus × steinii

Quercus × steinii, commonly known as the Stein's oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus lobata (scrub oak) and Quercus berberidifolia (berberi oak).

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to California, characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule with bristly hairs. Quercus × steinii thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub ecosystems.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
