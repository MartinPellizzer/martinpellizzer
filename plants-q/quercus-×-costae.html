Quercus × costae

Quercus × costae, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus lobata and Quercus polymorpha.

It is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and broad, elliptical leaves that are typically 6–12 cm long. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are often covered in a dense layer of fuzzy hair. It is native to the central and southern regions of Mexico, where it thrives in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
