Quercus petraea var. laciniata

Quercus petraea var. laciniata, commonly known as the serrated pedunculate oak, is a variant of the pedunculate oak native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including calcareous soils. The plant is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its acorns are edible and are often consumed by wildlife, contributing to its ecological importance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes planted in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
