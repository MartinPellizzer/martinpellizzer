Quercus chinquapin

Quercus chinquapin, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.
