Quercus acutissima var. breviopetiolata

Quercus acutissima var. breviopetiolata, commonly known as the short-petioled Chinese oak, is a variant of the Chinese chestnut oak.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern China, characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with short petioles. The leaves are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath, turning reddish-brown in autumn. It produces small, spiky capsules that contain one or two seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
