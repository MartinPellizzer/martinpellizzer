Quercus ilex var. glauca

Quercus ilex var. glauca, commonly known as the glossy evergreen oak, is a variant of the holm oak native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that often has a waxy, glossy appearance. The leaves are typically small, leathery, and have a distinctive silvery underside. This plant is evergreen, retaining its leaves throughout the year, and produces small, spiny acorns.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
