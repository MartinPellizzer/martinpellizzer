Quisqualis obovata

Quisqualis obovata, commonly known as the Chinese star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink. The plant has oval-shaped leaves that are slightly curved at the edges, and it produces small, dark purple berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, humid climates.

The genus Quisqualis is known for its aromatic flowers, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of perfume.
