Quercus suber f. microcarpa

Quercus suber f. microcarpa, commonly known as the Spanish chestnut or cork oak, is a variant of the evergreen oak native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and thick, corky bark, which is highly valued for its insulating properties. The leaves are typically leathery and dark green, with a serrated margin. This subspecies thrives in Mediterranean climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
