Quiina multiflora

Quiina multiflora, commonly known as the yellow quina or yellow milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and milky sap. The plant has woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species in its ecosystem.
