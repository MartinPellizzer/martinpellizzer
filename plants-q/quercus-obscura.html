Quercus obscura

Quercus obscura, commonly known as the dark oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and small, elliptical leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are a food source for various wildlife species. Quercus obscura typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and sustenance for numerous forest organisms.
