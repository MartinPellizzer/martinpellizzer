Quillaja brasiliensis

Quillaja brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian quillaja, is a deciduous tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces yellowish flowers followed by edible fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife. Its bark contains saponins, which have been used in traditional medicine and as a natural detergent.

Quillaja brasiliensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various species.
