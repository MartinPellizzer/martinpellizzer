Quercus × numidica

Quercus × numidica, commonly known as the Algerian oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between the cork oak (Quercus suber) and the Persian oak (Quercus brantii).

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in Mediterranean climates and is often found in rocky or dry soils.

Quercus × numidica is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to adapt to harsh environmental conditions.
