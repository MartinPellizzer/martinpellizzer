Quercus pyrenaica var. expansa

Quercus pyrenaica var. expansa, commonly known as the Spanish chestnut or the broad-leaved oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are often lobed and have a distinctive silvery-white underside. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by acorns that are typically clustered in groups of two to four. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
