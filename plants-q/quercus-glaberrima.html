Quercus glaberrima

Quercus glaberrima, commonly known as the African white oak, is a deciduous tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, acorn-like fruits that mature in the second year. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a pointed tip and can vary in size depending on the environment. This species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

It plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native range, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
