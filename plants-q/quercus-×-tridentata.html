Quercus × tridentata

Quercus × tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus gambelii and Quercus grisea.

It is native to the southwestern United States, particularly in areas of Arizona and New Mexico. The plant is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and have three distinct lobes, giving it its common name.

This species plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
