Quercus mespilifolioides

Quercus mespilifolioides, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a dense, hairy cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and forest edges.

Quercus mespilifolioides is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its historical use in traditional medicine.
