Quercus urceolaris

Quercus urceolaris, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance that helps it withstand harsh environmental conditions.
