Quercus shumardii subsp. texana

Quercus shumardii subsp.

texana, commonly known as the Texas live oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southern United States. It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
