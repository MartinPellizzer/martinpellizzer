Quercus falcata f. obovata

Quercus falcata f. obovata, commonly known as the southern red oak variant, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, dense acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing structural support and protection against environmental stressors.

This subspecies is well adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun conditions.
