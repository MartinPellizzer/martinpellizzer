Quercus cerris subf. acutilobata

Quercus cerris subf.

acutilobata, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a subspecies of the common oak characterized by its distinctive morphology. It is a deciduous tree with elongated, acutely lobed leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are often enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in temperate climates and is native to regions of southeastern Europe and western Asia.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in woodworking.
