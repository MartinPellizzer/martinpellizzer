Quercus litseifolia

Quercus litseifolia, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically pale to dark green on the upper surface and silvery white underneath. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with moderate to high humidity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
