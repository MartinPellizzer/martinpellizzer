Quercus sessilis var. barbulata

Quercus sessilis var. barbulata, commonly known as the barbulate sessile oak, is a variant of the sessile oak native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and broad, lobed leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is a deciduous tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Its botanical features make it an important species in forest ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
