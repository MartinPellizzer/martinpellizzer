Quercus phellos var. pumila

Quercus phellos var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf willow oak, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, red to brown acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in bottomlands and along streams.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and landscape use in suitable climates.
