Quercus aliena f. pellucida

Quercus aliena f. pellucida, commonly known as the translucent oak, is a unique variant of the alien oak species.

It is characterized by its delicate, thin leaves that allow light to pass through, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. Its botanical features include small, acorn-like fruits and a shallow root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

This form is of interest to botanists due to its unusual leaf structure and potential ecological adaptations.
