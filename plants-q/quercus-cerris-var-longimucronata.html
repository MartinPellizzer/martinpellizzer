Quercus cerris var. longimucronata

Quercus cerris var. longimucronata, commonly known as the long-pointed Turkey oak, is a variant of the Turkey oak native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its elongated acorns, which have a distinctive, pointed tip and are often covered in a dense, sticky pubescence. The leaves are typically broad, lobed, and have a rough upper surface with a smooth lower surface. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within its native range.
