Quercus vellifera

Quercus vellifera, commonly known as the silky oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and Indonesia. The tree is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible acorns that are important for wildlife.

The wood of Quercus vellifera is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
