Quercus × undulata var. rydbergiana

Quercus × undulata var. rydbergiana, commonly known as Rydberg's wavyleaf oak, is a hybrid species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive wavy-edged leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for local wildlife.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
