Quercus gaditana

Quercus gaditana, commonly known as the Gibraltar oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in rocky and coastal environments, often found in areas with poor soil.

Its hardy nature makes it a symbol of resilience in Mediterranean landscapes.
