Quercus Conferta Var. Spectabilis

Red oak, scientifically known as Quercus conferta var. spectabilis, is a striking deciduous tree native to the eastern United States. It is prized for its vibrant fall color, which ranges from fiery red to deep orange. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping and reforestation efforts. Its strong, durable wood has been used historically for furniture and construction. With its aesthetic appeal and ecological value, the red oak plays a vital role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining forest ecosystems.
