Quercus lanuginosa subsp. palensis

Quercus lanuginosa subsp.

palensis, commonly known as the Mongolian oak, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and hairy, grayish bark.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated margin and a pointed tip. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest-steppe and mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts.
