Quercus × heterophylla

Quercus × heterophylla, commonly known as the variable oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus garryana and Quercus petiolaris.

It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with some leaves being broad and others narrow, depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in western North America, particularly in regions with moist, temperate climates. Its acorns are generally small and can be an important food source for wildlife.

The species exhibits a range of growth forms, from shrubs to small trees, making it adaptable to various habitats.
