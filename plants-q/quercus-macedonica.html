Quercus macedonica

Quercus macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

The Macedonian oak is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
