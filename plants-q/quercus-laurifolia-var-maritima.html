Quercus laurifolia var. maritima

Quercus laurifolia var. maritima, commonly known as the coastal live oak, is a variant of the live oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which remains dense and leathery throughout the year. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, thriving in sandy soils and salt-tolerant conditions. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a pointed tip, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variant is well adapted to maritime climates and plays a significant role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
