Quercus coccifera

Quercus coccifera, commonly known as the Kermes oak, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that contain one or two seeds. It is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Quercus coccifera is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
