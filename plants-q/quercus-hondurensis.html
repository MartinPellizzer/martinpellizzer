Quercus hondurensis

Quercus hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduras oak, is a deciduous tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

Quercus hondurensis plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
