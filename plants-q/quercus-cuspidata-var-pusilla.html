Quercus cuspidata var. pusilla

Quercus cuspidata var. pusilla, commonly known as the Japanese dwarf oak, is a small evergreen oak native to Japan.

It features dense, dark green foliage with glossy leaves that are typically oval in shape. The plant produces small, spiky acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a specimen plant.

Its compact size and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice in horticulture.
