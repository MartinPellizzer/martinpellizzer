Quassia amara var. paniculata

Quassia amara var. paniculata, commonly known as the quassia tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, simple leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by elongated, yellowish fruits containing toxic seeds. It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly the bitter compounds found in its wood and bark.

The tree is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural insecticides.
