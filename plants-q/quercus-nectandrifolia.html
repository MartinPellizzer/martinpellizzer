Quercus nectandrifolia

Quercus nectandrifolia, commonly known as the sweet gum tree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and purple in the fall. The tree produces small, spiky fruits called capsules, which contain seeds dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and deeply furrowed, providing a rugged appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
