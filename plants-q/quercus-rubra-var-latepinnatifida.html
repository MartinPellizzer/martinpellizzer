Quercus rubra var. latepinnatifida

Quercus rubra var. latepinnatifida, commonly known as the late-pinnate red oak, is a variant of the northern red oak characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features broad, lobed leaves that are typically longer and more slender than those of the typical red oak, with a late-developing pinnation. The plant is native to the eastern United States, where it thrives in a variety of soil types and light conditions. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
