Quercus serrata var. nana

Quercus serrata var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf Japanese oak, is a compact shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It features small, rounded leaves that are serrated along the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its unique size and ornamental qualities make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
