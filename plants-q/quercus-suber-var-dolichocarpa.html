Quercus suber var. dolichocarpa

Quercus suber var. dolichocarpa, commonly known as the Spanish chestnut or sweet chestnut, is a variant of the cork oak.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and produces edible, sweet, chestnut-like fruits. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and for local food production.
