Quercus lusitanica var. brevipetiolata

Quercus lusitanica var. brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-stalked rock oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a short petiole, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, pale acorns that mature in the second year. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
