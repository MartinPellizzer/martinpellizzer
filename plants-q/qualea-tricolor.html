Qualea tricolor

Qualea tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored water lily, is a species native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful petals, which display three distinct shades of pink, white, and red. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in swampy areas. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The flowers bloom during the dry season, attracting pollinators such as beetles and flies.
