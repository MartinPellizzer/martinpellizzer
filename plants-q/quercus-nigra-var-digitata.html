Quercus nigra var. digitata

Quercus nigra var. digitata, commonly known as the blackjack oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and elongated, narrow leaves that are often lobed. The tree produces small, red acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
