Quercus cerrifolia

Quercus cerrifolia, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Quercus cerrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
