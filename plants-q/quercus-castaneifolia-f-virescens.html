Quercus castaneifolia f. virescens

Quercus castaneifolia f. virescens, commonly known as the green-leafed Persian oak, is a variant of the Persian oak characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This plant is native to regions of Iran and Central Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The leaves are broad, ovate, and typically have a glossy, dark green hue, which sets it apart from other Quercus species. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
