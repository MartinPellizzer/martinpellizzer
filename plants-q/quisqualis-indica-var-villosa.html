Quisqualis indica var. villosa

Quisqualis indica var. villosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind or hairy champa, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
