Quercus major

Quercus major, commonly known as the white oak, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The tree produces acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. Quercus major thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance and habitat for small organisms.
