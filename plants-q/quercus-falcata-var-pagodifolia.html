Quercus falcata var. pagodifolia

Quercus falcata var. pagodifolia, commonly known as the pagoda oak, is a variant of the southern red oak.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pagoda-like branching pattern and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, chestnut-like acorns that are a food source for various wildlife.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique form and seasonal color changes.
