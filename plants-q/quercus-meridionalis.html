Quercus meridionalis

Quercus meridionalis, commonly known as the southern oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand hot, arid conditions.

Quercus meridionalis is valued for its hard wood and historical significance in Mediterranean landscapes.
