Quercus pendulina var. filipendula

Quercus pendulina var. filipendula, commonly known as the weeping oak, is a unique variant of the pendulina oak characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect.

This plant is a deciduous tree with broad, lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in gardens and parks.
