Quercus warszewiczii

Quercus warszewiczii, commonly known as the Warszewicz's oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Quercus warszewiczii is valued for its hardy nature and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
