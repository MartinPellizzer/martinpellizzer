Quaqua parviflora subsp. dependens

Quaqua parviflora subsp.

dependens, commonly known as the small-flowered quaqua, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturalists alike.
