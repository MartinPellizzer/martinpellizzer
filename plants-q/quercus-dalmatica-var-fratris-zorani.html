Quercus dalmatica var. fratris-zorani

Quercus dalmatica var. fratris-zorani, commonly known as the Dalmatian oak variety Fratris-Zorani, is a subspecies of the Dalmatian oak native to the Adriatic region.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in mountainous areas with well-drained soils. The leaves are broad-ovate, with a serrated margin and a glossy green upper surface. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in rocky and coastal environments.
