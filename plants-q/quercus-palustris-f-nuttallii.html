Quercus palustris f. nuttallii

Quercus palustris f. nuttallii, commonly known as the Nuttall's swamp oak, is a botanical variant of the pin oak.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that are broader and more rounded compared to the typical pin oak. This form thrives in wetland environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist soil conditions. The acorns produced are small and often have a noticeable cap.

It is a valuable component of riparian and wetland ecosystems due to its ecological niche and structural role.
