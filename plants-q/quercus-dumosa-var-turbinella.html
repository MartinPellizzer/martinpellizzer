Quercus dumosa var. turbinella

Quercus dumosa var. turbinella, commonly known as the Turbinella buckthorn, is a shrub-like evergreen plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, leathery acorns. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid landscapes.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an important species in its native ecosystem.
