Quassia warmingiana

Quassia warmingiana, commonly known as the African quassia, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Simaroubaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is valued for its medicinal properties, with the bark containing quassinoids that have antifungal and antiparasitic effects.

The tree is also used in traditional medicine for treating various ailments.
