Quercus monimotricha

Quercus monimotricha, commonly known as the white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing protection against pests and diseases.

Quercus monimotricha plays a vital role in forest ecosystems and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
