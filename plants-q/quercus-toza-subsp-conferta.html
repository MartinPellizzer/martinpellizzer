Quercus toza subsp. conferta

Quercus toza subsp.

conferta, commonly known as the denseleaf oak, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Central and South America. It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in clusters, giving it a compact appearance.

The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including dry forests and rocky slopes, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
