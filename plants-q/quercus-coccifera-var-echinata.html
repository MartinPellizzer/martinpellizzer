Quercus coccifera var. echinata

Quercus coccifera var. echinata, commonly known as the spiny rock oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is well adapted to drought conditions.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
