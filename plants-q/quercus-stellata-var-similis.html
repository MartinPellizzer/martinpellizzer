Quercus stellata var. similis

Quercus stellata var. similis, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a variant of the Mexican white oak native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically whitish underneath. The plant produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of its native ecosystem.
