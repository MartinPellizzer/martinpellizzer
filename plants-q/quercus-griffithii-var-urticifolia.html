Quercus griffithii var. urticifolia

Quercus griffithii var. urticifolia, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves turn yellow to reddish-brown in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

This species is well adapted to temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.
