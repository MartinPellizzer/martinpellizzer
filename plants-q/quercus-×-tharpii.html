Quercus × tharpii

Quercus × tharpii, commonly known as the Arizona white oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus arizonica and Quercus engelmannii.

It is native to the southwestern United States, particularly Arizona and New Mexico, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and acorns that are typically covered with a dense layer of fuzzy hairs. It is an important component of the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is also cultivated in some regions for landscaping purposes.
