Quercus × jahandiezii

Quercus × jahandiezii, commonly known as the Persian oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus macranthera and Quercus castaneifolia.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Iran and is characterized by its distinctive acorns and broad, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters, and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
