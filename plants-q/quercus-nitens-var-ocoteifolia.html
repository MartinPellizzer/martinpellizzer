Quercus nitens var. ocoteifolia

Quercus nitens var. ocoteifolia, commonly known as the Ocotillo Oak, is a variant of the Ocotillo tree native to Mexico.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing adaptations to arid environments. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are often shed during dry seasons, reducing water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing pale yellow to reddish blooms that attract pollinators.

The wood of this species is valued for its durability and is used in traditional craftsmanship.
