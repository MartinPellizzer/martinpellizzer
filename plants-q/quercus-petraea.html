Quercus petraea

Quercus petraea, commonly known as the sessile oak, is a deciduous tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, reddish-brown acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and forest edges.

The sessile oak is known for its strong, durable wood and its ecological importance in supporting biodiversity.
