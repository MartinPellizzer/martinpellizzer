Quercus lusitanica var. macrobalanus

Quercus lusitanica var. macrobalanus, commonly known as the Portuguese oak, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, spiky acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

This variety is often found in forests and woodlands, contributing to local biodiversity.
