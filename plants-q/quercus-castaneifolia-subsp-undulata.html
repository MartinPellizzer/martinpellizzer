Quercus castaneifolia subsp. undulata

Quercus castaneifolia subsp.

undulata, commonly known as the Persian walnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its lobed, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

The tree produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and horticultural purposes.
