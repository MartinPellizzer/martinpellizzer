Quercus pseudomolucca var. sundaica

Quercus pseudomolucca var. sundaica, commonly known as the Sunda oak, is a variant of the pseudomolucca oak native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its lobed leaves and acorn-like fruits. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and mountainous areas. Its botanical features include a sturdy trunk, compound leaves, and a moderate growth rate.

This species is valued for its timber and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
