Quercus × subfalcata

Quercus × subfalcata, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus rugosa and Quercus crassifolia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, leathery leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. It is native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico, where it thrives in well-drained soils and mild climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape tree.
