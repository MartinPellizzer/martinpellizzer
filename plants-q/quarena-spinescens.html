Quarena spinescens

Quarena spinescens, commonly known as the spiny quarena, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand periods of drought. Its spines serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores, while its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Quarena spinescens is often used in local landscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
