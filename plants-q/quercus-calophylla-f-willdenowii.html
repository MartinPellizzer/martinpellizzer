Quercus calophylla f. willdenowii

Quercus calophylla f. willdenowii, commonly known as the Willdenowii oak, is a botanical variant of the broad-leaved oak species.

It is characterized by its distinctive elliptical leaves that are often glossy and have a finely serrated margin. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically clustered in groups of two to three. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and moderate rainfall.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and its role in forest ecosystems.
