Quercus × kanitziana

Quercus × kanitziana, commonly known as the Kanitz oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus robur and Quercus petraea.

It is a deciduous tree native to Central Europe, characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and acorns. The plant exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it challenging to classify definitively. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mixed forests.

The botanical study of Quercus × kanitziana provides insights into the genetic diversity and adaptability of oak species.
