Qualea obtusata

Qualea obtusata, commonly known as the Amazonian water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has a short, thick stem that anchors it to the muddy substrate at the water's edge. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, opening in the early morning and closing by midday.

Qualea obtusata plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in wetland ecosystems.
