Quercus × andrewsii

Quercus × andrewsii, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus polymorpha and Quercus crassifolia.

It is native to central Mexico and is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and broad, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows as a medium to large tree, reaching heights of up to 20 meters. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Quercus × andrewsii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
