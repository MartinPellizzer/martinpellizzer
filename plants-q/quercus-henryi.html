Quercus henryi

Quercus henryi, commonly known as Henry's oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It is characterized by its leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in humid, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
