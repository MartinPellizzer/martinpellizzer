Quisqualis indica var. pierrei

Quisqualis indica var. pierrei, commonly known as the Chinese starfruit or purple flame vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to purple, often with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture, contributing to its overall aesthetic appeal.
