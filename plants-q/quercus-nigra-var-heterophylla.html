Quercus nigra var. heterophylla

Quercus nigra var. heterophylla, commonly known as the black oak var. heterophylla, is a variant of the black oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with some leaves being broad-ovate and others more narrow and elongated. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in upland forests. Its acorns are small and often have a scaly cap, which is typical of the Quercus genus.

This variation in leaf shape is a notable botanical feature that distinguishes it from other oak species.
