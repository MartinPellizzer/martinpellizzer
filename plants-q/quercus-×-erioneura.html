Quercus × erioneura

Quercus × erioneura, commonly known as the variegated oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus rubra (red oak) and Quercus prinus (post oak).

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring a mix of green, white, and yellow variegation on its leaves. The plant is typically found in the southeastern United States, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lobed and often have a serrated margin, while its acorns are small and usually have a scaly cap.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
