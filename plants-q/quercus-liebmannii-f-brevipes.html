Quercus liebmannii f. brevipes

Quercus liebmannii f. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed rock oak, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with short, twisted stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are adapted to disperse in rocky and arid environments.
