Quercus rubra var. pendula

Quercus rubra var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping red oak, is a unique variant of the red oak characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect.

This tree is native to eastern North America and typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lobed and have a deep green color that turns reddish in the fall. The weeping form is less common than the upright red oak, making it a striking ornamental tree.

It produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
