Qualea multiflora subsp. pubescens

Qualea multiflora subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy water tree, is a species native to the Amazon Basin. It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in wetland and swampy areas.

The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, white flowers followed by woody fruits containing several seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays an important role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
