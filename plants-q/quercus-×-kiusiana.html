Quercus × kiusiana

Quercus × kiusiana, commonly known as the Japanese oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus crispula and Quercus myrabolans.

It is a deciduous tree native to Japan, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Quercus × kiusiana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.

It typically grows to a height of 8–15 meters and is valued for its shade-providing capabilities.
