Quercus × deleiensis

Quercus × deleiensis, commonly known as the Delie's oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus robur and Quercus petraea.

It is characterized by its deciduous leaves, which are typically lobed and have a serrated margin. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mixed forests.

The botanical features of Quercus × deleiensis make it an important component of forest ecosystems in Europe.
