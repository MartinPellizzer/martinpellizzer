Quinata violacea

Quinata violacea, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by bees and other small insects.

Quinata violacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
