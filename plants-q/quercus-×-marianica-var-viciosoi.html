Quercus × marianica var. viciosoi

Quercus × marianica var. viciosoi, commonly known as the red oak, is a hybrid species that exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species, Quercus marianica and Quercus velutina.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark and deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spiny acorns that are typically dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in mixed hardwood forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a notable species in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
