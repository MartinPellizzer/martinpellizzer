Quercus × caduca

Quercus × caduca, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus schmidtii and Quercus variabilis.

It is a deciduous tree native to China, characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and robust growth.

The botanical features of Quercus × caduca make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
