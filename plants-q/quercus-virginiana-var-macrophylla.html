Quercus virginiana var. macrophylla

Quercus virginiana var. macrophylla, commonly known as the Spanish oak, is a variant of the live oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 12 inches in length, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and along riverbanks.

Its dense canopy and strong root system make it a hardy, long-lived tree with significant ecological value.
