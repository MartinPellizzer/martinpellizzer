Quercus cuspidata var. angustifolia

Quercus cuspidata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the Japanese pagoda tree, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the spring, followed by small, chestnut-like fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
