Quercus lanuginosa var. cupaniana

Quercus lanuginosa var. cupaniana, commonly known as the silky oak or velvet oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage that turns reddish-brown in autumn. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing habitat for insects and small animals.

The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland and forested areas.
