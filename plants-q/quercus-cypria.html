Quercus cypria

Quercus cypria, commonly known as the Cyprus oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Quercus cypria typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
