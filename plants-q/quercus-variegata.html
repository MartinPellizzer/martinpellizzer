Quercus variegata

Quercus variegata, commonly known as the variegated oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring leaves with a variegated pattern of green, white, and sometimes yellow. The plant has a moderate growth rate and can reach heights of up to 30 feet. Its acorns are typically small and have a chestnut-like appearance.

Quercus variegata is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
