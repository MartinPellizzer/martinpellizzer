Quercus pedunculata var. leptobalanos

Quercus pedunculata var. leptobalanos, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a dense, scaly cupule. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
