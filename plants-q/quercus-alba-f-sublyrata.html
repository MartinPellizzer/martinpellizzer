Quercus alba f. sublyrata

Quercus alba f. sublyrata, commonly known as the white oak with sublyrata foliage, is a variant of the white oak (Quercus alba) characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

The leaves are typically broader and more rounded compared to the standard white oak, with a more pronounced lobing pattern. This form exhibits a unique leaf shape that sets it apart from other oak varieties, making it a notable specimen in botanical collections. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
