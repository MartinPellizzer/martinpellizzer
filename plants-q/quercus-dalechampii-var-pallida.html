Quercus dalechampii var. pallida

Quercus dalechampii var. pallida, commonly known as the white oak or pale oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and its acorns, which are typically enclosed in a cupule. The tree has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 30 meters, with a rounded crown and grayish bark. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
