Quercus faginea subsp. oscensis

Quercus faginea subsp.

oscensis, commonly known as the Pyrenean oak, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its robust, often twisted trunk and deeply furrowed bark.

The leaves are typically oblong to elliptical, with a serrated margin and a glossy upper surface. It produces acorns that are enclosed in a cupule, a feature common among oaks.

This subspecies thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to rocky or mountainous terrain.
