Quercus glauca subsp. annulata

Quercus glauca subsp.

annulata, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface and a pale, sometimes hairy underside.

The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are typically clustered in groups of two to four. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
