Quassia salubris

Quassia salubris, commonly known as the quassia tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its most notable botanical feature is the presence of quassinoids, a class of bitter compounds found in its wood and leaves.

These compounds have been studied for their potential medicinal and insecticidal properties.
