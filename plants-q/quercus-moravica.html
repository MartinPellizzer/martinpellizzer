Quercus moravica

Quercus moravica, commonly known as the Moravian oak, is a deciduous tree native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
