Quercus pedunculata proles tardiflora

Quercus pedunculata proles tardiflora, commonly known as the late-flowering Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, clustered flowers in the spring, followed by edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
