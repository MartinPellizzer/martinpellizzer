Quercus pubescens subsp. lanuginosa

Quercus pubescens subsp.

lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

The leaves are typically elliptical with a serrated margin and a short petiole. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges and open woodlands.
