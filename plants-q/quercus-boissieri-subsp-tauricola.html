Quercus boissieri subsp. tauricola

Quercus boissieri subsp.

tauricola, commonly known as the Tauric oak, is a small to medium-sized evergreen oak native to the Crimean Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the semi-arid climate of its native region.

The tree has a dense, rounded crown and produces small, acorn-like fruits that are important for local wildlife. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, with a distinctive lobed shape.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
