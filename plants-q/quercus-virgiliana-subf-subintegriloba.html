Quercus virgiliana subf. subintegriloba

Quercus virgiliana subf.

subintegriloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved white oak, is a botanical variant of the white oak species. It is characterized by its distinctive, narrowly lanceolate leaves with a smooth, waxy surface.

The plant typically grows in temperate regions with well-drained soils, often in forested areas. Its acorns are small and edible, though they are usually not consumed by humans.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its historical use in traditional medicine.
