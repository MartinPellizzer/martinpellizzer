Quercus cerris var. bithynica

Quercus cerris var. bithynica, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a variant of the sessile oak native to the Balkans and Turkey.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, spiky acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against environmental stressors.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in forestry and woodworking.
