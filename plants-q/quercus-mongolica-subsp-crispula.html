Quercus mongolica subsp. crispula

Quercus mongolica subsp.

crispula, commonly known as the Mongolian crispleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to northeastern Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are serrated and have a wavy, crisp margin.

The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in forested areas with well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

The subspecies is valued for its hardy nature and ecological role in its native habitats.
