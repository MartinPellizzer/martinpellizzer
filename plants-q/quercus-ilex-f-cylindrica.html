Quercus ilex f. cylindrica

Quercus ilex f. cylindrica, commonly known as the Portuguese oak, is a variant of the evergreen holm oak.

It is characterized by its cylindrical acorns and dense, leathery foliage. This plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of North Africa. It thrives in Mediterranean climates with well-drained soils.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental gardening and erosion control.
