Quercus lusitanica var. submembranacea

Quercus lusitanica var. submembranacea, commonly known as the Iberian oak, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe, particularly Portugal and Spain.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, spiky acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is typically gray and fissured, providing a rugged texture.

This variant is adapted to Mediterranean climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
