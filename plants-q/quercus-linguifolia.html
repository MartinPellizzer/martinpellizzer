Quercus linguifolia

Quercus linguifolia, commonly known as the Florida sand cherry oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and broad, lobed leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The tree typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats. Its botanical features include a sturdy trunk with a broad, spreading crown.

Quercus linguifolia plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
