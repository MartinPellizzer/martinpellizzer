Quercus pedunculata var. pinnatipartita

Quercus pedunculata var. pinnatipartita, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a dense, hairy cap. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
