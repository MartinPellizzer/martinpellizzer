Queltia jonquilla

Queltia jonquilla, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Queltia jonquilla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
