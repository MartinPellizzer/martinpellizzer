Quercus ilex subf. coutinhoi

Quercus ilex subf.

coutinhoi, commonly known as the Portuguese oak, is a rare and endemic subspecies native to the western Iberian Peninsula. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the tree throughout the year.

The plant produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically covered in a dense, hairy cap. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the mild, humid climate of its native region.
