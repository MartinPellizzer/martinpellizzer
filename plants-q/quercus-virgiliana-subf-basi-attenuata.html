Quercus virgiliana subf. basi-attenuata

Quercus virgiliana subf.

basi-attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically clustered in groups of two to four. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
