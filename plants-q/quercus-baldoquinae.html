Quercus baldoquinae

Quercus baldoquinae, commonly known as the Baldoquina oak, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its lobed leaves and acorns, which are typical features of the oak genus. The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils, often found in forested areas at high elevations. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for conservationists.
