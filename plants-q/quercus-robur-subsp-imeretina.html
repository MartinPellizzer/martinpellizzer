Quercus robur subsp. imeretina

Quercus robur subsp.

imeretina, commonly known as the Imeretian oak, is a subspecies of the common oak native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside.

The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
