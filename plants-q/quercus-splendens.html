Quercus splendens

Quercus splendens, commonly known as the shining oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green foliage and striking white or pale yellow flowers that appear in spring. The leaves are typically elliptical with a pointed tip and have a leathery texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a temperate climate.

Quercus splendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
