Quercus leptobalanus

Quercus leptobalanus, commonly known as the Carpathian oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Carpathian Mountains in Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated acorns and dark green, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stressors.

Quercus leptobalanus plays an important role in its ecosystem by supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
