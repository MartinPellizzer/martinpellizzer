Quercus sideroxyla f. aquifolia

Quercus sideroxyla f. aquifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf oak, is a rare and unique variant of the ironwood oak.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which sets it apart from other members of the Quercus genus. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a prominent midrib. This plant is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
