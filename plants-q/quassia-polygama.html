Quassia polygama

Quassia polygama, commonly known as the African quassia, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Simaroubaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-orange fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy aril. It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its medicinal properties.

The bark and roots contain quassinoids, which have been used traditionally for their antimicrobial and antifungal effects.
