Quercus phellos var. sericea

Quercus phellos var. sericea, commonly known as the silky oak or silverleaf oak, is a variant of the willow oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant is a deciduous tree with a broad, spreading canopy and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
