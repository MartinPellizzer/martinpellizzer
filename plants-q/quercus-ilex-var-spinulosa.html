Quercus ilex var. spinulosa

Quercus ilex var. spinulosa, commonly known as the spinous evergreen oak, is a variant of the holm oak native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and distinctive spiny buds that give it its common name. The leaves are typically small, elliptical, and have a glossy dark green appearance. This species thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

It is a slow-growing tree that can reach heights of up to 15 meters, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
