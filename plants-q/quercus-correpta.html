Quercus correpta

Quercus correpta, commonly known as the Florida scrub oak, is a small evergreen oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, acorn-like fruits. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to fire-prone environments. It plays a vital role in the ecological structure of scrub habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the region's biodiversity.
