Quercus lentula

Quercus lentula, commonly known as the Spanish white oak, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

Quercus lentula plays an important role in local ecosystems by supporting various wildlife species.
