Quercus graciliramis

Quercus graciliramis, commonly known as the slender-rammed oak, is a rare and lesser-known species of oak native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is typically smooth and grayish in color, becoming more rugged with age.
