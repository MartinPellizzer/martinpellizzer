Quercus asturica

Quercus asturica, commonly known as the Asturian oak, is a species of deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its serrated leaves and produces small, cup-shaped acorns. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and moderate climates. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

Quercus asturica plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
