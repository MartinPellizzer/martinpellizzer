Quercus × rolfsii

Quercus × rolfsii, commonly known as the Rolf's oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus lobata (Valley oak) and Quercus agrifolia (coastal live oak).

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and a rounded crown. The plant typically grows in coastal regions of California, where it thrives in mild, moist climates. Its acorns are small and often covered with a dense, fuzzy coating.

Quercus × rolfsii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
