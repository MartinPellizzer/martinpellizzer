Quercus hartwissiana var. macrocarpa

Quercus hartwissiana var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant California oak, is a large evergreen oak native to California.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and can grow to impressive heights, often exceeding 50 meters. The tree produces acorns that are typically large and enclosed in a cupule. Its bark is thick and deeply furrowed, providing a rugged appearance.

This variant is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of coastal and inland regions of California.
