Quercus × superlata

Quercus × superlata, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus variabilis and Quercus sessilifolia.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a glossy upper surface. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are encased in a dense, scaly cupule.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in parks and gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
