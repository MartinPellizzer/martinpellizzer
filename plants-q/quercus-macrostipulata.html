Quercus macrostipulata

Quercus macrostipulata, commonly known as the large-leaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often lobed and have a distinctive yellowish-green underside. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
