Quercus mongolica var. crispula

Quercus mongolica var. crispula, commonly known as the Mongolian oak or crinkled oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, doubly serrated leaves that are often curled or crinkled, giving it its botanical name. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in temperate forest regions and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

This species plays a significant role in forest ecosystems and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
