Queltia × montana

Queltia × montana, commonly known as the mountain queltia, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from its parent species, Queltia macrophylla and Queltia montana.

It is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains, where it thrives in cool, high-altitude environments. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a yellow throat. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

Queltia × montana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
