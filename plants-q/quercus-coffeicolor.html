Quercus coffeicolor

Quercus coffeicolor, commonly known as the coffee-colored oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that turn a striking reddish-brown in the fall. The tree produces small, pale yellow to brown acorns that are encased in a cupule. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
