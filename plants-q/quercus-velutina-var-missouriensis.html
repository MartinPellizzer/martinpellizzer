Quercus velutina var. missouriensis

Quercus velutina var. missouriensis, commonly known as the Missouri oak, is a variant of the chestnut oak native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and acorns that are typically covered with a dense layer of silky hairs. The leaves are usually oval-shaped with a pointed tip and a serrated margin, turning vibrant red in the fall. This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in upland forests.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
