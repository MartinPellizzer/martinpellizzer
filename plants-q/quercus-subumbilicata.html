Quercus subumbilicata

Quercus subumbilicata, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with smooth margins. The tree produces small, cupule-enclosed acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It typically grows in dry to moist forests and thrives in well-drained soils.

The bark is thick and fissured, providing habitat for various invertebrates and lichens.
