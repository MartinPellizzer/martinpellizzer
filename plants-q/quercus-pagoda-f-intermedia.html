Quercus pagoda f. intermedia

Quercus pagoda f. intermedia, commonly known as the pagoda oak intermediate form, is a unique variant of the pagoda oak, characterized by its distinct branching pattern.

This plant is a deciduous tree with alternate, simple, and ovate to lanceolate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, while the fruit is a dry, two-valved cupule that encloses one or two acorns. The tree thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscaped areas.
