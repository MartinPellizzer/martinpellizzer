Quercus pedunculata var. pendula-libani

Quercus pedunculata var. pendula-libani, commonly known as the Lebanese weeping oak, is a variant of the pedunculate oak characterized by its pendulous branches.

It is a deciduous tree native to the Levant region, including parts of Lebanon and Syria. The leaves are typically lobed and have a serrated margin, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are often enclosed in a cupule.

This tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in the region's landscapes.
