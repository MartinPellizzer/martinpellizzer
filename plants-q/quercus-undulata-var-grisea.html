Quercus undulata var. grisea

Quercus undulata var. grisea, commonly known as the gray-leafed buckthorn oak, is a variant of the undulata oak species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive grayish foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, acorn-like fruits. The leaves are typically lobed and have a wavy margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, upland habitats.

This plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
