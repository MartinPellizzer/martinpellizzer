Quercus georgiana

Quercus georgiana, commonly known as the Georgia oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its lobed leaves and acorn-producing capsules. The tree typically grows to a height of 20-30 meters and has a broad, spreading canopy. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.
