Quercus sessiliflora var. pedemontana

Quercus sessiliflora var. pedemontana, commonly known as the Pedemontane oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and leathery, evergreen leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in scrubland or woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native range.
