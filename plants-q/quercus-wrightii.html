Quercus wrightii

Quercus wrightii, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.

Quercus wrightii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and scrub habitats.
