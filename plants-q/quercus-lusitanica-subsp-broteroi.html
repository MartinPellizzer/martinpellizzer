Quercus lusitanica subsp. broteroi

Quercus lusitanica subsp.

broteroi, commonly known as the Iberian oak, is a shrub-like evergreen oak native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, often in areas with poor soil conditions.

The plant features small, leathery leaves that are usually dark green and have a rough texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while its acorns are edible and provide food for various wildlife.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
