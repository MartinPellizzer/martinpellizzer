Quercus × sooi

Quercus × sooi, commonly known as the Chinese oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus dentata and Quercus variabilis.

It is a deciduous tree native to China, characterized by its broad ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically covered in a dense, hairy coating. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

Quercus × sooi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
