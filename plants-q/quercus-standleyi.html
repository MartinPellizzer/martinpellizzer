Quercus standleyi

Quercus standleyi, commonly known as the Standley oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a dark green upper surface. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by acorns that are encased in a cupule. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in limestone soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
