Quesnelia chacoensis

Quesnelia chacoensis, commonly known as the Chacoan bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in the Chaco region of Paraguay and Argentina. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Quesnelia chacoensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
