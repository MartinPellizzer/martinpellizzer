Quercus lusitanica var. stenoglandis

Quercus lusitanica var. stenoglandis, commonly known as the Iberian oak, is a variant of the Holm oak native to southwestern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and leathery, evergreen leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against fire and drought.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife.
