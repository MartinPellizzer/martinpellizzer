Queenslandiella mira

Queenslandiella mira, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. Queenslandiella mira is often found in open forests and woodland areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in the ecosystem by fixing nitrogen and providing nectar for pollinators.
