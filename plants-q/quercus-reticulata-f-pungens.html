Quercus reticulata f. pungens

Quercus reticulata f. pungens, commonly known as the spiked nettle oak, is a unique variant of the nettle oak species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant is native to regions of Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its small, acorn-like fruits are typically clustered in groups of two to four.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
