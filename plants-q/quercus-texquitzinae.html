Quercus texquitzinae

Quercus texquitzinae, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen oak, is a species of oak native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with glossy, elliptical leaves that remain on the tree year-round. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in montane forests.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability to varied soil conditions and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
