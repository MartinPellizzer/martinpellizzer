Quercus sericea

Quercus sericea, commonly known as silverleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The leaves are elongated, elliptical, and have a distinctive silvery underside. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

This tree is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
