Quercus virginiana var. pygmaea

Quercus virginiana var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf southern live oak, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically less than 2 inches long. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by small, inedible acorns. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and along the edges of swamps.

This variety is known for its compact size and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
