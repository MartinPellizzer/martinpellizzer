Quercus cerris var. caramanica

Quercus cerris var. caramanica, commonly known as the Caramanica Turkey Oak, is a rare variant of the Turkey Oak native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are enclosed in a cupule, a feature common among oaks. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in the region.
