Quercus nigricans

Quercus nigricans, commonly known as the black oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing habitat for insects and fungi.

Quercus nigricans thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
