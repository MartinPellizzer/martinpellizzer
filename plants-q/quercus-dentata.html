Quercus dentata

Quercus dentata, commonly known as the Japanese oak, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its lobed leaves, which are serrated and often resemble those of other oak species. The tree produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in temperate regions and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

The bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance and contributing to its ecological significance.
