Quercus × major

Quercus × major, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus bicolor and Quercus crassifolia.

It is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico, characterized by its white to pale gray bark and lobed, serrated leaves. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically covered with a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
