Quercus sichourensis

Quercus sichourensis, commonly known as the Sichuan oak, is a deciduous tree native to China, particularly in the Sichuan province.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It produces acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.

This plant is often found in mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.
