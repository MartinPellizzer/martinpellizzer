Quisqualis parvifolia

Quisqualis parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered velvet tamarind, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant produces small, round, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Quisqualis parvifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
