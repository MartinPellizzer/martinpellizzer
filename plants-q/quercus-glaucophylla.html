Quercus glaucophylla

Quercus glaucophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant has a broad, spreading crown and produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a pointed tip and are arranged alternately along the branches.

Quercus glaucophylla thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
