Quercus sada

Quercus sada, commonly known as the Sada oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its acorns are small and are an important food source for various wildlife species.
