Quercus mongolica var. typica

Quercus mongolica var. typica, commonly known as the Mongolian oak, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas across China, Korea, and Russia.

The species is valued for its hard wood and ecological significance in its native habitats.
