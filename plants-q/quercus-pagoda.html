Quercus pagoda

Quercus pagoda, commonly known as the pagoda oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, umbrella-like crown and its acorn-producing fruit. The leaves are typically oval or elliptical, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The tree thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its bark is grayish-brown and becomes fissured with age, adding to its visual appeal.
