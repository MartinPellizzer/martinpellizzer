Quercus anatolica

Quercus anatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers followed by acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Quercus anatolica plays a significant role in the local ecosystem and is valued for its hardy nature and ornamental qualities.
