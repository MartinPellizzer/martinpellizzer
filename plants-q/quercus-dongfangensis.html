Quercus dongfangensis

Quercus dongfangensis, commonly known as the Dongfang chestnut oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to China, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Yunnan and Sichuan provinces. This tree is characterized by its broad, oval leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
