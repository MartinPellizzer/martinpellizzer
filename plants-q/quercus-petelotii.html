Quercus petelotii

Quercus petelotii, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and elliptical leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. The plant produces small, pale yellow to brown flowers in the spring, followed by acorns that are encased in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
