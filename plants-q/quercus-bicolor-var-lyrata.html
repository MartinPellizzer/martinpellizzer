Quercus bicolor var. lyrata

Quercus bicolor var. lyrata, commonly known as the red oak variant, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Its ecological importance extends to providing habitat and supporting biodiversity in its native range.
