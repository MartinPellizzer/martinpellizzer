Quirosia anceps

Quirosia anceps, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, clustered, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright blue berries that persist through the winter. The plant has compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets, and its growth habit is typically upright and bushy. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, thriving in well-drained soils.

The berries are a food source for birds and other wildlife, making it an ecologically valuable plant.
