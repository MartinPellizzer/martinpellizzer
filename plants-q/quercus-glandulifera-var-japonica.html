Quercus glandulifera var. japonica

Quercus glandulifera var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its serrated, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are edible and often consumed by wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
