Quercus hungarica

Quercus hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian oak, is a deciduous tree native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in forested areas and thrives in well-drained soils.

The Hungarian oak is valued for its hardy nature and historical significance in European ecosystems.
