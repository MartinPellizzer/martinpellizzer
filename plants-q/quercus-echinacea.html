Quercus echinacea

Quercus echinacea, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stem and showy purple petals. The plant produces a distinctive cone-shaped seed head that becomes visible after the flowers fade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Quercus echinacea is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
