Quercus robur var. glomerata

Quercus robur var. glomerata, commonly known as the English oak, is a variant of the common oak native to Europe.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that are typically dark green on top and paler underneath. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and forests.

This variety is valued for its durable wood and historical significance in European ecosystems.
