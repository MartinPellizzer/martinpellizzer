Quercus almaguerensis

Quercus almaguerensis, commonly known as the Almaguera oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color. Its acorns are encased in a cupule and are an important food source for various wildlife species.

This tree is often found in montane forests and is adapted to the region's humid climate.
