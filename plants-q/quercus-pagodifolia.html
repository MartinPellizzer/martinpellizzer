Quercus pagodifolia

Quercus pagodifolia, commonly known as the Chinese pagoda tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-shaped crown and its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged with 11 to 21 leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by woody, cone-like fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.

The species is also valued for its hardy nature and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
