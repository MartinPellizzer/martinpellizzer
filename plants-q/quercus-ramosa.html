Quercus ramosa

Quercus ramosa, commonly known as the branched oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched trunk and small, acorn-producing capsules. The leaves are typically lobed and turn yellow in the autumn before falling. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
