Quercus lyauteyi

Quercus lyauteyi, commonly known as the chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often lobed and have a dark green upper surface. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

The bark of Quercus lyauteyi is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance that helps in its identification.
