Quercus battandieri

Quercus battandieri, commonly known as the Corsican oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long and have a serrated edge. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are enclosed in a dense, scaly cap. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forests and woodlands.

The Corsican oak plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
