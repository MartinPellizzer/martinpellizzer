Quercus × columnaris

Quercus × columnaris, commonly known as the columnar oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus rubra (red oak) and Quercus alba (white oak).

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit, making it a popular choice for urban landscaping due to its space-efficient form. The plant typically exhibits leaves that are lobed and have a dark green upper surface with a lighter underside. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are dispersed by animals, contributing to its reproductive cycle.

The bark of Quercus × columnaris is smooth when young and becomes furrowed with age, adding to its visual interest.
