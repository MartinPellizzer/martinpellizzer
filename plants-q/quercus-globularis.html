Quercus globularis

Quercus globularis, commonly known as the globe oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rounded, globe-like crown and smooth, grayish-brown bark. The leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a rounded or elliptical shape with a serrated margin. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

This tree is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
