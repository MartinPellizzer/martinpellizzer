Quercus faginea var. macrobalanus

Quercus faginea var. macrobalanus, commonly known as the Carpathian hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and dark, glossy leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The tree has a slow growth rate and can live for over a century, making it a long-lived species. Its bark is thick and deeply furrowed, providing a rugged appearance.

This variety is adapted to cool, moist climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.
