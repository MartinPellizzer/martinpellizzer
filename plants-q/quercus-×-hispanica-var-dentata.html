Quercus × hispanica var. dentata

Quercus × hispanica var. dentata, commonly known as the Spanish chestnut oak, is a hybrid species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is typically smooth and gray, with shallow furrows as it matures.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
