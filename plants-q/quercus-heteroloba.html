Quercus heteroloba

Quercus heteroloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf white oak, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within its native range.
