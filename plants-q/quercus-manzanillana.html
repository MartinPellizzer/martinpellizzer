Quercus manzanillana

Quercus manzanillana, commonly known as the Manzanilla oak, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by acorns that are encased in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
