Quercus induta var. microcarpa

Quercus induta var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf chinquapin, is a shrub-like species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, egg-shaped acorns and dense, leathery foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are often glossy and have a distinctive, wavy margin.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
