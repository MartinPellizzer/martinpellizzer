Quercus oxyodon var. fargesii

Quercus oxyodon var. fargesii, commonly known as the Farges' oak, is a variant of the broad-leafed oak native to China.

It is characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically covered with a dense, velvety cap. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
