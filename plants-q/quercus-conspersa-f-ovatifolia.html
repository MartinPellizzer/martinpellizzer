Quercus conspersa f. ovatifolia

Quercus conspersa f. ovatifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf oak, is a variant of the common oak species characterized by its ovate leaves.

This plant is native to regions of eastern North America, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically larger and more rounded compared to the standard variety, with a serrated margin. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

It is often found in mixed hardwood forests and is valued for its ecological and ornamental qualities.
