Quercus robur var. sicula

Quercus robur var. sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian oak, is a variety of the common oak native to southern Europe, particularly Sicily.

It is a large deciduous tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters, with a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are typically lobed, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside, and they turn yellow or brown in autumn. Its acorns are small and edible, often used in local cuisine.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
