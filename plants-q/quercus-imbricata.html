Quercus imbricata

Quercus imbricata, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut, is a species of evergreen oak native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are imbricated, or overlapping, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dense forests.

Quercus imbricata plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
