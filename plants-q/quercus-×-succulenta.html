Quercus × succulenta

Quercus × succulenta, commonly known as the succulent oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus coccinea and Quercus rubra.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy plant in dry climates. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically encased in a cupule. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems and dense foliage.

Quercus × succulenta is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities in xeriscaping.
