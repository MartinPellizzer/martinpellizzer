Quercus opaca

Quercus opaca, commonly known as the chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, red acorns that are a food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Quercus opaca is often found in mixed forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
