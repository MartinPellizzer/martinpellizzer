Quercus pubescens subsp. anatolica

Quercus pubescens subsp.

anatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and dark green, glossy leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The tree typically grows in mountainous areas with well-drained soils, preferring moderate climates. Its bark is rugged and fissured, often covered in a layer of fine, white pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in local ecosystems.
