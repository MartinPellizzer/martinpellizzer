Quercus × warburgii

Quercus × warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus cerris (Turkey oak) and Quercus robur (English oak).

It is characterized by its distinctive acorns, which are typically larger and more elongated compared to those of its parent species. The leaves of Quercus × warburgii are usually lobed and have a serrated margin, exhibiting a range of colors from dark green to reddish in the fall. This hybrid is native to parts of Europe and is often found in mixed forests, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a natural hybrid, showcasing the genetic diversity within the Quercus genus.
