Quercus aquatica var. hybrida

Quercus aquatica var. hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid water oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is a hybrid between Quercus aquatica and Quercus nigra, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The tree has broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and it produces small, clustered acorns. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
