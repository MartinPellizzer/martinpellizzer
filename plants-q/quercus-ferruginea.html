Quercus ferruginea

Quercus ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive rusty-brown bracts that appear before the leaves, giving it its common name. The leaves are typically lobed and can vary in shape, often with a serrated edge. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
