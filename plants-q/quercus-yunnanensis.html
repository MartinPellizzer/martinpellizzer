Quercus yunnanensis

Quercus yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in mixed forests at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
