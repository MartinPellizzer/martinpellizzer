Quercus undulata var. gunnisonii

Quercus undulata var. gunnisonii, commonly known as Gunnison's wavyleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive wavy-edged leaves and a rounded crown that provides dense shade. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically covered with a hairy cap. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its adaptability to arid environments.
