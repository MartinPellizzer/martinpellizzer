Quercus × paucilepis

Quercus × paucilepis, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus lobata and Quercus rugosa.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Due to its adaptability and ecological significance, it plays a vital role in the local ecosystems of its native range.
