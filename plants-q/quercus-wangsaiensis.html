Quercus wangsaiensis

Quercus wangsaiensis, commonly known as the Wansai oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in parts of Vietnam and Cambodia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
