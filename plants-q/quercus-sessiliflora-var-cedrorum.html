Quercus sessiliflora var. cedrorum

Quercus sessiliflora var. cedrorum, commonly known as the Cedros Island oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Channel Islands off the coast of California.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits that are typically borne in clusters. The leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

This variety is notable for its limited distribution and is considered a relict species with historical significance in the region.
