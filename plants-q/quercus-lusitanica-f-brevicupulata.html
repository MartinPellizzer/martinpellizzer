Quercus lusitanica f. brevicupulata

Quercus lusitanica f. brevicupulata, commonly known as the short-cupped holm oak, is a rare and distinctive variant of the holm oak.

It is characterized by its smaller, more compact acorns compared to the typical form. The plant is native to southern Europe, particularly in regions of Spain and Portugal. It thrives in Mediterranean climates with well-drained soils.

Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
