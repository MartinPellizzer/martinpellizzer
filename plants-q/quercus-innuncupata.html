Quercus innuncupata

Quercus innuncupata, commonly known as the red oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its deeply lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is thick and ridged, providing structural support and protection against pests.

Quercus innuncupata plays a vital role in forest ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and soil health.
