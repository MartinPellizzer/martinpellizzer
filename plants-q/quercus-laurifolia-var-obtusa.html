Quercus laurifolia var. obtusa

Quercus laurifolia var. obtusa, commonly known as the southern sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn a striking red or purple in the fall. The tree produces small, spherical fruit known as samaras, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
