Qualea disticha

Qualea disticha, commonly known as the water lily of the Amazon, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide a surface for other aquatic organisms. The plant's flowers are typically white or pink, with a distinctive shape that resembles a boat. It plays an important role in the ecosystem by offering shelter and food for various species.

Qualea disticha is adapted to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.
