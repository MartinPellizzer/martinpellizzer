Quercus endemica

Quercus endemica, commonly known as the endemic oak, is a rare species native to specific regions with unique microclimates.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that change color with the seasons, typically displaying shades of green, yellow, and red. The tree produces acorns that are an important food source for local wildlife. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stressors.

Due to habitat loss, Quercus endemica is classified as endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
