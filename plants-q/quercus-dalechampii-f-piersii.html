Quercus dalechampii f. piersii

Quercus dalechampii f. piersii, commonly known as the Piersii variant of the Common Rock Oak, is a unique form of the species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

This variant is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, often twisted, branching pattern. The leaves are typically small, leathery, and have a serrated margin, adapting to the harsh, rocky environments where it thrives. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
