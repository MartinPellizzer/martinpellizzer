Quercus desiderabilis

Quercus desiderabilis, commonly known as the desirable oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, spiky acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance, while its deep root system helps prevent soil erosion.

Quercus desiderabilis is valued for its ecological importance and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
