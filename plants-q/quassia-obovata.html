Quassia obovata

Quassia obovata, commonly known as the quassia tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, obovate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles. The tree is known for its aromatic, bitter wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a natural insecticide. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Quassia obovata is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
