Quercus × paui

Quercus × paui, commonly known as the hybrid chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Quercus prinus (southern red oak) and Quercus fusca (chestnut oak), exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The tree typically grows to a height of 20–30 meters, with a broad, rounded crown and dark gray to reddish bark. Its leaves are oval to elliptic, with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside, turning yellow or brown in autumn.

The acorns are small, typically 1–2 cm long, and are an important food source for wildlife.
