Quercus byzantina

Quercus byzantina, commonly known as the Byzantine oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The tree produces small, spiky acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Quercus byzantina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
