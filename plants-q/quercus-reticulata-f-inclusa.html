Quercus reticulata f. inclusa

Quercus reticulata f. inclusa, commonly known as the broadleaf oak, is a variant of the reticulated oak, characterized by its distinctive leaf venation patterns.

It is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The leaves are typically broad and lobed, with a rough, hairy texture on the underside. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and landscape gardening.
