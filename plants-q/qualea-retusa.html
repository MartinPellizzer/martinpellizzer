Qualea retusa

Qualea retusa, commonly known as the Amazonian water lily, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for aquatic life. The plant has a short, thick stem that anchors it in the muddy substrate. Its flowers are small and white, typically blooming in the early morning.

Qualea retusa plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
