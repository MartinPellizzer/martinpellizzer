Quercus peduncularis subsp. pilicaulis

Quercus peduncularis subsp.

pilicaulis, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by edible, sweet, chestnut-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forests and scrublands.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in horticulture.
