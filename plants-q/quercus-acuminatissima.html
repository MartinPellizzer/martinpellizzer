Quercus acuminatissima

Quercus acuminatissima, commonly known as the Japanese oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, spiky acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas across Japan and Korea.

Quercus acuminatissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
