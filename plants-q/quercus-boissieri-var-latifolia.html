Quercus boissieri var. latifolia

Quercus boissieri var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf rock oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
