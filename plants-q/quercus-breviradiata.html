Quercus breviradiata

Quercus breviradiata, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside and have a distinctive reddish-brown coloration in the fall. The tree produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Quercus breviradiata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
