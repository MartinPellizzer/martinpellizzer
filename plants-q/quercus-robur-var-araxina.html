Quercus robur var. araxina

Quercus robur var. araxina, commonly known as the Armenian oak, is a variant of the common oak that is native to the Caucasus region.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its sturdy trunk and broad, lobed leaves that turn golden in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

The Armenian oak is valued for its hard wood and historical significance in the region's ecosystems.
