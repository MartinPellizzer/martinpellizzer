Quercus brevicaudata

Quercus brevicaudata, commonly known as the Sierra foothill oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Quercus brevicaudata has a slow growth rate and can live for several hundred years.
