Quercus dentata var. yunnanensis

Quercus dentata var. yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan serrate oak, is a variant of the Chinese oak native to southwestern China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its deeply lobed leaves and acorns that are typically clustered in small groups. The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils, often found in mountainous regions. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting local wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
