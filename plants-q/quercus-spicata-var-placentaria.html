Quercus spicata var. placentaria

Quercus spicata var. placentaria, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and elongated, acorn-like fruits that are typically clustered in spikes. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, often appearing elliptical to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
