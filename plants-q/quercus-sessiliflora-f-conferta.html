Quercus sessiliflora f. conferta

Quercus sessiliflora f. conferta, commonly known as the denseleaf chinquapin, is a rare and unique variant of the chinquapin oak.

It is characterized by its densely packed, small, leathery leaves that are typically clustered at the base of the stems. This form is native to the southeastern United States, specifically occurring in parts of Georgia and Alabama. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
