Quercus alamarensis

Quercus alamarensis, commonly known as the Florida maple-leaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive maple-like leaves that are lobed and often have a yellowish or reddish hue in the fall. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a hairy cupule. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
