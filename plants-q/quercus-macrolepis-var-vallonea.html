Quercus macrolepis var. vallonea

Quercus macrolepis var. vallonea, commonly known as the Italian white oak, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and elliptical, glossy leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are typically clustered in groups of two to four. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional landscapes.
