Quercus × jolonensis

Quercus × jolonensis, commonly known as the Jolon oak, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to California.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. Quercus × jolonensis typically grows in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, thriving in mild, Mediterranean climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in southern California.
