Quercus tephrosia

Quercus tephrosia, commonly known as the Tephrosia oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, followed by small, inedible acorns. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Quercus tephrosia plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, providing food and shelter for various species.
