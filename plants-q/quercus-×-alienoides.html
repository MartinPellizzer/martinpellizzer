Quercus × alienoides

Quercus × alienoides, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus schneideri and Quercus wuatschewii.

It is a deciduous tree native to China, characterized by its white to pale gray bark and lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. Botanically, it is noted for its intermediate traits between its parent species, making it a subject of interest in plant hybridization studies.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
