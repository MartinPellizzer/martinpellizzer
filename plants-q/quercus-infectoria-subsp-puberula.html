Quercus infectoria subsp. puberula

Quercus infectoria subsp.

puberula, commonly known as the African bristle oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries such as Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda.

The plant is characterized by its dense, bristly foliage and small, acorn-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological importance and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
