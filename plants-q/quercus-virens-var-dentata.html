Quercus virens var. dentata

Quercus virens var. dentata, commonly known as the serrated evergreen oak, is a variant of the live oak characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and robust growth habit.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Gulf Coast, where it thrives in humid, warm climates. The leaves are typically thick, leathery, and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to withstand urban conditions, making it a common choice for landscaping.

Its botanical features include a spreading crown, deep root system, and the ability to produce acorns, which support local wildlife.
