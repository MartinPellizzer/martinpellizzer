Quercus pedunculata f. laciniata

Quercus pedunculata f. laciniata, commonly known as the Chinese lace oak, is a variant of the Chinese white oak.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, lobed leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant is native to China and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its foliage provides ornamental value, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

The tree typically grows to a moderate height and has a spreading crown, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
