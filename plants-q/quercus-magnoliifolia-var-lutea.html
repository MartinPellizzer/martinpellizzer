Quercus magnoliifolia var. lutea

Quercus magnoliifolia var. lutea, commonly known as the golden-leaf maple, is a variant of the magnolia-leaf oak.

It is characterized by its striking yellow foliage, which turns vibrant shades of gold in the autumn. The leaves are large, ovate, and have a smooth margin, often with a waxy texture. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic contribution.
