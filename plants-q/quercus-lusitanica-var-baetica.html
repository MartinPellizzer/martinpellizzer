Quercus lusitanica var. baetica

Quercus lusitanica var. baetica, commonly known as the Baetica oak, is a subspecies of the evergreen oak native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
