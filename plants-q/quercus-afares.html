Quercus afares

Quercus afares, commonly known as the Afghan oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Afghanistan and Pakistan. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a broad, rounded crown and dark green, glossy leaves. Its acorns are small and encased in a cupule, typical of the genus Quercus.

The plant is adapted to cold and arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
