Quercus sessiliflora var. oxyacanthifolia

Quercus sessiliflora var. oxyacanthifolia, commonly known as the California redbud, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn bright yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure containing one or two seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
