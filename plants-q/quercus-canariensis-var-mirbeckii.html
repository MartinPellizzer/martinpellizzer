Quercus canariensis var. mirbeckii

Quercus canariensis var. mirbeckii, commonly known as the Canary Island oak, is a variant of the evergreen oak native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
