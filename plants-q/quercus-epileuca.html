Quercus epileuca

Quercus epileuca, commonly known as the white oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing structural support and protection against pests.

Quercus epileuca plays a vital role in forest ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
