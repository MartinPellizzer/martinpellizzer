Quercus polycarpa subf. pinnata

Quercus polycarpa subf.

pinnata, commonly known as the pinnate chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, pinnately compound leaves with 5 to 7 pairs of leaflets.

The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a dense, scaly cupule. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
