Quercus × hispanica var. latifolia

Quercus × hispanica var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved evergreen oak, is a hybrid species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy, dark green leaves that remain on the tree throughout the year. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping.
