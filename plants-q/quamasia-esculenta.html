Quamasia esculenta

Quamasia esculenta, commonly known as the African potato, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy underground stem called a tuber, which is the primary edible part of the plant. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

The tuber is rich in nutrients and has been used traditionally for its medicinal and culinary properties.
