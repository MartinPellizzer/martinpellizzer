Quercus flocculenta f. oblongifolia

Quercus flocculenta f. oblongifolia, commonly known as the woolly oakleaf maple, is a botanical variant of the woolly oak.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense, cottony layer of fine hairs. The plant is native to parts of China and is typically found in moist, shaded forest environments. Its leaves turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
