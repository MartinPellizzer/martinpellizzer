Quercus mongolica f. tomentosa

Quercus mongolica f. tomentosa, commonly known as the Mongolian oak, is a variant of the Mongolian oak tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescence covering the young leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. This plant typically grows in temperate forest regions, often in mountainous areas with well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its acorns are small and edible.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
