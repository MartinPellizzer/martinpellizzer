Quercus longinux var. pseudomyrsinifolia

Quercus longinux var. pseudomyrsinifolia, commonly known as the Mexican red oak, is a variant of the longinux oak species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and dark green, glossy leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
