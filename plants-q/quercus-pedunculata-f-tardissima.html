Quercus pedunculata f. tardissima

Quercus pedunculata f. tardissima, commonly known as the late-flowering Chinese chestnut, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Chinese chestnut tree.

It is characterized by its delayed flowering period, which typically occurs later in the growing season compared to the standard species. The plant exhibits dense, dark green foliage and produces small, edible nuts that are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. Botanically, it belongs to the Fagaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of China.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming habit.
