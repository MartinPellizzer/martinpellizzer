Quercus malifolia

Quercus malifolia, commonly known as the appleleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, with leaves that are broad, lobed, and often have a pale undersurface. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a woody cap. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

Quercus malifolia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
