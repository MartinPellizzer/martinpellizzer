Quercus transmontana

Quercus transmontana, commonly known as the chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in mixed hardwood forests.

Quercus transmontana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
