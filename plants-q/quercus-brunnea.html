Quercus brunnea

Quercus brunnea, commonly known as the red oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its deep reddish-brown bark and acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. The leaves are lobed and have a rough, sandpapery texture, turning yellow in the fall. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in mixed hardwood forests.

The plant plays an important ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
