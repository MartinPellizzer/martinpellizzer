Quercus listeri

Quercus listeri, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lobed, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and scrub habitats.

Quercus listeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native range.
