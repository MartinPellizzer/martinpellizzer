Quercus pedunculata f. brevipedunculata

Quercus pedunculata f. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stalked Chinese chestnut, is a variant of the Chinese chestnut tree characterized by its shorter peduncles.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China, typically growing in mountainous regions. The plant features oval to elliptical leaves with serrated edges and produces small, sweet, edible nuts. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and resistance to certain pests and diseases.

This form is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and potential use in horticulture.
