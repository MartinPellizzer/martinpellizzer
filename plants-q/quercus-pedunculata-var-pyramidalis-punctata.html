Quercus pedunculata var. pyramidalis-punctata

Quercus pedunculata var. pyramidalis-punctata, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen oak, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape and small, elliptical leaves that are often dotted with small, raised glands. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers in spring, followed by acorns that mature in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
