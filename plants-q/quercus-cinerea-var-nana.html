Quercus cinerea var. nana

Quercus cinerea var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf chinquapin oak, is a small, shrub-like variant of the chinquapin oak.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in areas with moist, acidic soils. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. It produces small, spiny acorns that mature over a season.

This variety is well adapted to woodland and forest understory environments.
