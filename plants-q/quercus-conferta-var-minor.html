Quercus conferta var. minor

Quercus conferta var. minor, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a small to medium-sized evergreen oak native to central Mexico.

It typically grows in mountainous regions and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, acorn-like fruits. The leaves are usually oval or elliptical with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface. This variety is closely related to other members of the Quercus genus, sharing similar ecological and structural traits.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
