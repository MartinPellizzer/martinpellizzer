Quercus kiukiangensis var. xizangensis

Quercus kiukiangensis var. xizangensis, commonly known as the Xizang oak, is a rare and endemic species native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its rugged, twisted trunk and dark, fissured bark. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a serrated margin and a glossy upper surface. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife.

This species is adapted to cold, arid environments and plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
