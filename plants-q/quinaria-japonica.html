Quinaria japonica

Quinaria japonica, commonly known as Japanese quince, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces bright red, fleshy fruits that ripen in autumn. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin.

Quinaria japonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
