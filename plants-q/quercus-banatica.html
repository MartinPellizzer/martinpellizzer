Quercus banatica

Quercus banatica, commonly known as the Bornean oak, is a deciduous tree native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The plant is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It produces small, chestnut-like fruits that are dispersed by animals.

Quercus banatica plays a vital role in the tropical rainforest ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various species.
