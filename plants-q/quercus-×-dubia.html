Quercus × dubia

Quercus × dubia, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus polymorpha and Quercus robur.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. The plant exhibits a broadleaf deciduous habit, with leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
