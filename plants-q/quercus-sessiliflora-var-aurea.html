Quercus sessiliflora var. aurea

Quercus sessiliflora var. aurea, commonly known as the golden oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and its distinctive golden-yellow foliage in the fall. The leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth margin and a pointed tip. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
