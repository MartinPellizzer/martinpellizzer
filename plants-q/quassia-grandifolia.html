Quassia grandifolia

Quassia grandifolia, commonly known as the African quassia, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange or red berries. Its roots and bark contain quassinoids, which are known for their bitter compounds and medicinal properties.

Quassia grandifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
