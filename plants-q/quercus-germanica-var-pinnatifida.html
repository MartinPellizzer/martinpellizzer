Quercus germanica var. pinnatifida

Quercus germanica var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the Turkish oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive lobed leaves that are often deeply divided, giving it a feathery appearance. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The Turkish oak is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in the region.
