Quercus lusitanica var. maroccana

Quercus lusitanica var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn a striking yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in Mediterranean climates.
