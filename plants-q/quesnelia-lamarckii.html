Quesnelia lamarckii

Quesnelia lamarckii, commonly known as the Lamarck's bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central pool to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking, colorful inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Quesnelia lamarckii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated as a decorative plant in many regions.
