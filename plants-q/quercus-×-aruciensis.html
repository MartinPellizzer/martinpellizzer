Quercus × aruciensis

Quercus × aruciensis, commonly known as the Cuban chestnut oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus bicolor and Quercus cubana.

It is a deciduous tree native to Cuba, characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
