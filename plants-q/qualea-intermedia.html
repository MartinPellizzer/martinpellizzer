Qualea intermedia

Qualea intermedia, commonly known as the white water lily, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy, waxy leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the early morning. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Qualea intermedia plays a vital role in supporting aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various species.
