Quercus × humidicola

Quercus × humidicola, commonly known as the hybrid chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Quercus prinus (chestnut oak) and Quercus muehlenbergii (Muhlenberg's oak). The plant exhibits characteristics of both parent species, including a broad, spreading crown and deeply lobed leaves. It produces small, reddish-brown acorns that mature in one growing season.

The botanical features of Quercus × humidicola make it an important component of mixed hardwood forests in its native range.
