Quercus lasistana

Quercus lasistana, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and lobed leaves. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in oak woodlands and forested areas.

Quercus lasistana is valued for its ecological role and historical significance in traditional medicine.
