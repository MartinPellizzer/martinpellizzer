Quercus cuneata var. falcata

Quercus cuneata var. falcata, commonly known as the winged oak, is a variant of the chestnut oak native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaf lobes that give it a unique appearance. The leaves are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a rough texture. Its acorns are small and have a cupule that is partially covered by the fruit.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
