Quercus centralis

Quercus centralis, commonly known as the central chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows to a height of 30 to 50 feet and has a rounded crown.

Quercus centralis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
