Quercus comayaguana

Quercus comayaguana, commonly known as the Yucatán oak, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Mexico and Central America.

It typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters and has a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a rough, pubescent underside. Its acorns are small, oblong, and have a cap that covers about one-third of the nut.

This species is valued for its hard wood and plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
