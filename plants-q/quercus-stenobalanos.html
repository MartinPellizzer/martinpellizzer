Quercus stenobalanos

Quercus stenobalanos, commonly known as the Greek oak, is a small to medium-sized evergreen oak native to the mountains of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or oblong in shape. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
