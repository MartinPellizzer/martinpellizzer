Quercus robur var. nobilis

Quercus robur var. nobilis, commonly known as the English oak, is a large deciduous tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow or brown in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its sturdy trunk and extensive root system make it a long-lived species, often reaching ages of several hundred years.

The bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance and habitat for numerous organisms.
