Quercus xalapensis f. jalapae

Quercus xalapensis f. jalapae, commonly known as the Jalape oak, is a variant of the Xalapensis oak native to central Mexico.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a dense, rounded canopy. The leaves are typically oval-shaped, leathery, and have a glossy dark green upper surface. It produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
