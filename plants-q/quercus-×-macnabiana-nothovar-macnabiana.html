Quercus × macnabiana nothovar. macnabiana

Quercus × macnabiana nothovar. macnabiana, commonly known as the Macnab's oak, is a hybrid evergreen oak species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and rounded, spreading crown. The leaves are typically leathery and have a serrated margin, while the acorns are small and often have a scaly cap. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
