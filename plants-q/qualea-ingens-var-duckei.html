Qualea ingens var. duckei

Qualea ingens var. duckei, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in swampy areas and flooded forests. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they bloom during the dry season, attracting pollinators such as beetles.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by stabilizing water bodies and supporting biodiversity.
