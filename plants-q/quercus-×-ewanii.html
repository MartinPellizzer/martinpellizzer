Quercus × ewanii

Quercus × ewanii, commonly known as the Ewan oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between the sessile oak (Quercus petraea) and the pedunculate oak (Quercus robur).

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and acorns that are typically smaller than those of its parent species. The bark is rugged and fissured, often displaying a grayish-brown color. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mixed forests across Europe.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a natural hybrid, showcasing genetic diversity within the genus Quercus.
