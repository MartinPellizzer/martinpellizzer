Quercus subgrisea

Quercus subgrisea, commonly known as the gray oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Georgia and South Carolina. The tree is characterized by its grayish bark and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
