Quaqua confusa

Quaqua confusa, commonly known as the misty orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pale lavender flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil to flourish. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down that helps reflect excess light.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Quaqua confusa is considered endangered and is often found in protected conservation areas.
