Quercus serrata var. brevipetiolata

Quercus serrata var. brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-petioled chinquapin oak, is a variant of the chinquapin oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves with serrated edges and short petioles. The plant produces small, spiny acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
