Quercus phellos var. arenaria

Quercus phellos var. arenaria, commonly known as the sand live oak, is a variant of the live oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and dense, sprawling growth habit. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and inland dunes. Its acorns are edible and serve as a food source for various wildlife species.

The sand live oak is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
