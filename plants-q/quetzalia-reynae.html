Quetzalia reynae

Quetzalia reynae, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the cloud forests of Mexico.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of pink and purple. The plant has a delicate structure with narrow, strap-like leaves and a compact growth habit. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, Quetzalia reynae is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
