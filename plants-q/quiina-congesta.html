Quiina congesta

Quiina congesta, commonly known as the clustered quiina, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
