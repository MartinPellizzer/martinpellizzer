Quercus engelmannii

Quercus engelmannii, commonly known as Engelmann oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its deeply lobed leaves, which are typically 5 to 9 inches long and have a rough, hairy texture on the underside. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are encased in a dense, bristly cap. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and mountainous regions.

Engelmann oak plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
