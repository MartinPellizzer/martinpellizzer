Quararibea castano

Quararibea castano, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia castanea*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry with a reddish or purplish hue, often resembling a cherry, hence its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
