Quercus prinopsis

Quercus prinopsis, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and elliptical, glossy leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its acorns are small and are often covered by a cupule, typical of the genus Quercus.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
