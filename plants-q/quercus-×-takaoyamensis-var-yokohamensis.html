Quercus × takaoyamensis var. yokohamensis

Quercus × takaoyamensis var. yokohamensis, commonly known as the Yokohama oak, is a hybrid species within the genus Quercus, characterized by its unique combination of traits from its parent species.

It is a deciduous tree that typically reaches heights of 10 to 15 meters, with a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are generally ovate to elliptical in shape, with a serrated margin and a glossy upper surface. The acorns are small, usually less than 1 cm in length, and mature in one season.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
