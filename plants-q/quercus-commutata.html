Quercus commutata

Quercus commutata, commonly known as the red oak.

It is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States, characterized by its acorns and lobed leaves. The leaves are typically 5 to 9 lobes and have a serrated margin. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

Its bark is rough and fissured, with a reddish-brown color.
