Quiina tinifolia

Quiina tinifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf quinoa, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to the dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, green flowers that eventually develop into edible, seed-like structures. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and adaptability to poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop for both nutritional and ecological purposes.
