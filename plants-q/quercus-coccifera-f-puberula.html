Quercus coccifera f. puberula

Quercus coccifera f. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent evergreen oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, leathery, pubescent leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in rocky, dry soils and is often found in coastal areas and scrublands.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
