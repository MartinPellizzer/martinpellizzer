Quercus broteroi

Quercus broteroi, commonly known as the Broter's oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the mountainous regions of southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a dark green upper surface. The plant produces acorns that are encased in a cupule, which is covered with dense, woolly hair. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Quercus broteroi plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
