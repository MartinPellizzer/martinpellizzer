Quercus ilex var. fimbriata

Quercus ilex var. fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed evergreen oak, is a variant of the holm oak native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive fringed or lobed leaves that give it a unique appearance compared to other oak species. The plant is evergreen, with dense, dark green foliage that remains throughout the year. It thrives in Mediterranean climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This variant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
