Quercus pubescens var. latifolia

Quercus pubescens var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved sessile oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are pubescent on the underside, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a dense, scaly cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and scrub habitats.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
