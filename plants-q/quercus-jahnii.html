Quercus jahnii

Quercus jahnii, commonly known as the Jahn's oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. Quercus jahnii thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems in which it is found.
