Quercus ewyckii var. latifolia

Quercus ewyckii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved sessile oak, is a variant of the sessile oak native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, elongated leaves that are typically lobed and have a smooth upper surface. The plant is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown. Its acorns are small and often have a scaly cap, and it produces them in the fall.

This variety is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic conditions.
