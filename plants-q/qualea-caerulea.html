Qualea caerulea

Qualea caerulea, commonly known as the blue river willow, is a species of flowering plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows along riverbanks and in moist, tropical environments. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are elliptical and have a smooth texture, while its bark is thick and fibrous.

Qualea caerulea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
