Quercus shumardii var. acerifolia

Quercus shumardii var. acerifolia, commonly known as the laceleaf shumard oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely serrated, maple-like leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree has a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark that develops fissures with age. Its acorns are small, enclosed in a cupule, and serve as a food source for various wildlife.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to a range of soil types.
