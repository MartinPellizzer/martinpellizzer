Quercus chrysolepis var. tomentella

Quercus chrysolepis var. tomentella, commonly known as the silver alder oak, is a variant of the coast live oak native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and a rounded, broad canopy. The leaves are typically broad-ovate with a leathery texture and a silvery-white underside. This variety thrives in coastal and montane regions, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
