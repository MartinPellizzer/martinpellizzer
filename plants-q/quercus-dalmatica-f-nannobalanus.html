Quercus dalmatica f. nannobalanus

Quercus dalmatica f. nannobalanus, commonly known as the Dalmatian oak dwarf form, is a unique variant of the Dalmatian oak, characterized by its smaller size and compact growth habit.

It is a deciduous tree with broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant exhibits a dense canopy and is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in mountainous regions. It produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
