Quercus dentata var. skworzovii

Quercus dentata var. skworzovii, commonly known as the Korean stone oak, is a variant of the Chinese oak native to Korea and parts of China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its thick, leathery leaves with a distinctive serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by hard, glossy acorns that mature in the fall. Its bark is rugged and deeply fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and resilience.
