Quercus faginea subsp. valentina

Quercus faginea subsp.

valentina, commonly known as the Valentina oak, is a subspecies of the common hornbeam oak native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and dense, dark green foliage.

The leaves are typically oval with a smooth margin and a pointed tip, and they turn a vibrant yellow in autumn. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
