Quercus javanica

Quercus javanica, commonly known as the Javan oak, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Quercus javanica is valued for its hard wood and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
