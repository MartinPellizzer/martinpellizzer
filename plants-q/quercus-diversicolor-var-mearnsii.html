Quercus diversicolor var. mearnsii

Quercus diversicolor var. mearnsii, commonly known as the Mearns oak, is a variant of the sessile oak native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and acorns that are typically covered in a dense, short pubescence. The leaves are generally elliptical or ovate, with a serrated margin and a glossy upper surface. This oak species is often found in mixed coniferous forests at higher elevations.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
