Quercus dalechampii subf. lobulosa

Quercus dalechampii subf.

lobulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock oak, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its small, oblong leaves that are often lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.
