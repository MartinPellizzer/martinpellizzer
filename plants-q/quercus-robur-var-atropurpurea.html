Quercus robur var. atropurpurea

Quercus robur var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple oak, is a variant of the common oak characterized by its striking purple-tinged foliage.

This deciduous tree features lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn, adding ornamental value to landscapes. Its bark is thick and deeply furrowed, providing a rugged appearance. The tree produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and parks.
