Quercus mongolica f. funebris

Quercus mongolica f. funebris, commonly known as the black oak, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia, particularly China, Korea, and Russia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and its leaves, which are typically narrow and elliptical with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mixed forests.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability to different soil types and its role in ecological succession.
