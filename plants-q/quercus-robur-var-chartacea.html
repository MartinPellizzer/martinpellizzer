Quercus robur var. chartacea

Quercus robur var. chartacea, commonly known as the paper oak, is a variant of the common oak characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features leaves that are typically larger and more elongated compared to the standard Quercus robur, with a velvety texture and a pale green underside. The leaves often exhibit a unique chartaceous quality, giving the plant its common name. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
