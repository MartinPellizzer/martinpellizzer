Quercus toza var. pyrenaica

Quercus toza var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean oak, is a subspecies of the common oak native to the Pyrenees Mountains.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and dark, glossy leaves that turn red in the autumn. The tree typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and prefers a temperate climate. It is a slow-growing tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture-making.
