Quercus castanea var. mexicana

Quercus castanea var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in dry to moist forests and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
