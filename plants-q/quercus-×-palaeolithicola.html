Quercus × palaeolithicola

Quercus × palaeolithicola, commonly known as the ancient oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus robur and Quercus petraea.

It exhibits characteristics of both parent species, with a broad, spreading canopy and dark, fissured bark. The leaves are typically lobed and have a rough upper surface, while the acorns are small and usually enclosed in a cupule. This species is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
