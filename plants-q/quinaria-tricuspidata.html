Quinaria tricuspidata

Quinaria tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-pointed quinaria, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet having a distinct, pointed tip. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
