Qaisera coronata

Qaisera coronata, commonly known as the crowned qaisera, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Qaisera coronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
