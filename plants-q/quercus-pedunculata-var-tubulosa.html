Quercus pedunculata var. tubulosa

Quercus pedunculata var. tubulosa, commonly known as the tubular oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is often flaky and grayish-brown, providing a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
