Quercus pubescens f. oxycarpa

Quercus pubescens f. oxycarpa, commonly known as the sweet chestnut or European sweet chestnut, is a subspecies of the downy oak.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are oval in shape and often have a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by edible, sweet, chestnut-like fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and mixed forests.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in both culinary and medicinal applications.
