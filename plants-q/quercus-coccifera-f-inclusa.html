Quercus coccifera f. inclusa

Quercus coccifera f. inclusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically less than 2 inches long. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is highly drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
