Quercus gilva

Quercus gilva, commonly known as the golden oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow foliage in the fall, which gives it its common name. The leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a serrated margin. It produces small, spiky acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The tree is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
