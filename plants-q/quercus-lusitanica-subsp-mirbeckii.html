Quercus lusitanica subsp. mirbeckii

Quercus lusitanica subsp.

mirbeckii, commonly known as the Mirbeck's oak, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils.

The leaves are oblong to elliptical, with a serrated margin and a glossy upper surface. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and is often found in mixed forests.
