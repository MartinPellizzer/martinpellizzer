Quercus polystachya

Quercus polystachya, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The tree produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Quercus polystachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
