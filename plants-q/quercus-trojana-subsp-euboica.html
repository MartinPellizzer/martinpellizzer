Quercus trojana subsp. euboica

Quercus trojana subsp.

euboica, commonly known as the Euboic oak, is a rare subspecies native to the island of Euboea in Greece. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its deeply lobed leaves and acorns that are typically covered in a dense, hairy coat.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate with its drought-resistant features. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited geographic distribution.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this endangered species from habitat loss and climate change.
