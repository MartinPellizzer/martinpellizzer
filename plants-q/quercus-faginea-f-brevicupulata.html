Quercus faginea f. brevicupulata

Quercus faginea f. brevicupulata, commonly known as the short-shelled hornbeam oak, is a botanical variant of the common hornbeam oak.

It is characterized by its smaller, more rounded acorns compared to the typical form. The leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface. This subspecies is native to specific regions in Europe, particularly in the Carpathian Basin.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.
