Quercus boyacensis

Quercus boyacensis, commonly known as the Boyacá oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the Andean region of Colombia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that are typically 5–10 cm in length. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in mountainous forests.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
