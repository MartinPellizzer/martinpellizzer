Quercus lusitanica var. genuina

Quercus lusitanica var. genuina, commonly known as the Iberian oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, chestnut-like acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in Mediterranean climates with well-drained soils.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
