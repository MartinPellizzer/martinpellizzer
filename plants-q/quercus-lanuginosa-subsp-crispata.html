Quercus lanuginosa subsp. crispata

Quercus lanuginosa subsp.

crispata, commonly known as the woolly oak or the crinkled oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves that have a distinctive crinkled or wavy texture.

The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically clustered in groups of two or three. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
