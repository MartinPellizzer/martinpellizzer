Quercus ilex var. plicata

Quercus ilex var. plicata, commonly known as the Spanish oak, is a variant of the evergreen oak native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark green leaves that are often rolled or folded along the midrib, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in Mediterranean climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
