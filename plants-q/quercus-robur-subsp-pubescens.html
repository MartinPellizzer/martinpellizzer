Quercus robur subsp. pubescens

Quercus robur subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the downy oak, is a subspecies of the common oak characterized by its hairy, or pubescent, leaves. It is native to parts of Europe and is typically found in moist, temperate forests.

The leaves are elliptical with a serrated margin and a distinctive downy texture on both sides. This subspecies produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Its growth habit is usually as a tall tree with a broad, spreading canopy.
