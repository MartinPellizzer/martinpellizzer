Quercus dalmatica f. mucronata

Quercus dalmatica f. mucronata, commonly known as the Dalmatian oak var. mucronata, is a shrub-like form of the Dalmatian oak.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth and small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The leaves are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This form is native to the Mediterranean region and is often found in rocky or dry soils.
