Quercus fordiana

Quercus fordiana, commonly known as the Formosan oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to Taiwan and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and acorns. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
