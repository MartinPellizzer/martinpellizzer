Quercus runcinatifolia var. typica

Quercus runcinatifolia var. typica, commonly known as the serrated oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, lobed leaves that are often serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and swampy areas.

The botanical features of this species make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
