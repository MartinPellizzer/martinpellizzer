Qualea impexa

Qualea impexa, commonly known as the yellow water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are typically green with a yellowish margin. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Qualea impexa thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its presence is an indicator of healthy, undisturbed aquatic ecosystems.
