Quisqualis glabra

Quisqualis glabra, commonly known as the common flame of the forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Quisqualis glabra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
