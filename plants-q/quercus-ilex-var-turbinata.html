Quercus ilex var. turbinata

Quercus ilex var. turbinata, commonly known as the Turkish evergreen oak, is a variant of the holm oak native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with dark green, leathery leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The tree produces small, spiky acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its hardy nature makes it a resilient plant in Mediterranean climates.
