Quercus porphyrogenita

Quercus porphyrogenita, commonly known as the Persian oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the Caucasus region, Turkey, and parts of Iran. The tree is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It produces small, reddish-brown acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The Persian oak is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in mountainous and forested areas.
