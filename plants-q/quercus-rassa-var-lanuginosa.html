Quercus rassa var. lanuginosa

Quercus rassa var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky oak, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are covered in a fine, silken hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, clustered flowers followed by acorn-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies.
