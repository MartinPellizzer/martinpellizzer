Quercus humboldtii var. lehmanniana

Quercus humboldtii var. lehmanniana, commonly known as the Lehmann's valley oak, is a deciduous tree native to central California.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, spiky acorns that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in valley bottoms and riparian areas.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to a range of environmental conditions.
