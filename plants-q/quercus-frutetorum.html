Quercus frutetorum

Quercus frutetorum, commonly known as the Florida scrub oak, is a small evergreen oak native to the southeastern United States.

It has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in scrub habitats.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a species of concern in some regions.
