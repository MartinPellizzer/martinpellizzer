Quercus ilex var. oleifolia

Quercus ilex var. oleifolia, commonly known as the evergreen oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark green leaves that remain throughout the year. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its dense foliage and hardy nature make it a popular choice for landscaping in Mediterranean climates.
