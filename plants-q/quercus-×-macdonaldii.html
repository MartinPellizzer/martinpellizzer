Quercus × macdonaldii

Quercus × macdonaldii, commonly known as the Macdonald oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus rubra (red oak) and Quercus velutina (southern red oak).

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its lobed leaves that turn reddish in the fall and its acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. The tree typically grows to a height of 15–25 meters and is native to the eastern United States. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and parks.
