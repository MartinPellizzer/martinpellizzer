Quercus penangensis

Quercus penangensis, commonly known as the Penang oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family Fagaceae.

It is native to the Malay Peninsula and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and acorn-like fruits. The plant typically grows in lowland dipterocarp forests and is often found in shaded, moist environments. Quercus penangensis has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Its wood is durable and has been historically used for construction and furniture-making.
