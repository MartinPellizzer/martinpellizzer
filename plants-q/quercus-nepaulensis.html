Quercus nepaulensis

Quercus nepaulensis, commonly known as the Nepali oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India, where it thrives in temperate climates. The tree can reach heights of up to 20 meters and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves. Its acorns are an important food source for various wildlife species.

This plant plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
