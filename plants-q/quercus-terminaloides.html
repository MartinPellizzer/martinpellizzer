Quercus terminaloides

Quercus terminaloides, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, cupule-enclosed acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in dry to moist forests and thrives in well-drained soils.

Quercus terminaloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
