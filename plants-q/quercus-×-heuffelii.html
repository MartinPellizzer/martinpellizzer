Quercus × heuffelii

Quercus × heuffelii, commonly known as the Heuffelii oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus robur and Quercus sessiliflora.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The plant produces small, spiky acorns that are typically enclosed in cupules. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

The Heuffelii oak is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
