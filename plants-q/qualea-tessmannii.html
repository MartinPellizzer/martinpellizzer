Qualea tessmannii

Qualea tessmannii, commonly known as the white water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, white, floating flowers that open in the early morning and close in the late afternoon. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that provide a habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrates of slow-moving rivers and lakes.

Qualea tessmannii plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native environment.
