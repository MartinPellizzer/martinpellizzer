Quercus revoluta

Quercus revoluta, commonly known as the Japanese oak, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that are often rolled under the margins, giving it a unique appearance. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Quercus revoluta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
