Quercus coccifera var. imbricata

Quercus coccifera var. imbricata, commonly known as the bracken oak, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage consisting of leathery, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and arid environments.

This variety is notable for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought and salt spray.
