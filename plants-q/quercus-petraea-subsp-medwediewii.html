Quercus petraea subsp. medwediewii

Quercus petraea subsp.

medwediewii, commonly known as the Carpathian rock oak, is a subspecies of the sessile oak native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is characterized by its small, elongated leaves and robust, often twisted acorns.

This subspecies thrives in rocky and mountainous terrain, showing adaptations to harsh, high-altitude environments. Its bark is typically thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stressors.

The plant plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its native habitats, supporting diverse flora and fauna.
