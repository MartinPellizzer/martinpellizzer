Quercus glauca var. longinux

Quercus glauca var. longinux, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, elliptical leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by acorns that mature in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with mild climates.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
