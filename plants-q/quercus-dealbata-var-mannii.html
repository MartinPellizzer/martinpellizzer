Quercus dealbata var. mannii

Quercus dealbata var. mannii, commonly known as the silverleaf oak, is a variant of the white oak native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape and have a serrated margin.

The species produces small, pale acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.
