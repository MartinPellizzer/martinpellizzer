Quercus rubra var. triloba

Quercus rubra var. triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed red oak, is a variant of the red oak native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves that are typically broad and serrated. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that mature over one to two years. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in mixed hardwood forests.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
