Quincula lobata

Quincula lobata, commonly known as the lobed quincula, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves with lobed margins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, with prominent stamens. It is native to regions of southern Africa and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

Quincula lobata is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
