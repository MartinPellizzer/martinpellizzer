Quercus oblongifolia var. brevilobata

Quercus oblongifolia var. brevilobata, commonly known as the short-lobed oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves with short, rounded lobes. The tree produces small, chestnut-like acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is typically smooth with a grayish-brown color, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is often found in mixed hardwood forests and is valued for its ecological and ornamental significance.
