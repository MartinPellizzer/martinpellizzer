Quercus rubicunda

Quercus rubicunda, commonly known as the red oak, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its deeply lobed leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing a rugged appearance.

Quercus rubicunda is valued for its strong wood and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
