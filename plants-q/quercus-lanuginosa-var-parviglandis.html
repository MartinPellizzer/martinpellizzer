Quercus lanuginosa var. parviglandis

Quercus lanuginosa var. parviglandis, commonly known as the woolly oak or small-glandular oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces small, clustered acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule with scales. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat conservation.
