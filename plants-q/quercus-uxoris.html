Quercus uxoris

Quercus uxoris, commonly known as the Spanish white oak, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are a food source for various wildlife. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection.

Quercus uxoris thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and open areas.
