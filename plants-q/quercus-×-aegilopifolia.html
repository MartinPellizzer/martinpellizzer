Quercus × aegilopifolia

Quercus × aegilopifolia, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus glauca and Quercus acutifolia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and have a distinctive white or pale underside. The plant produces small, spiny acorns that are often enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
