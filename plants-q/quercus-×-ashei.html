Quercus × ashei

Quercus × ashei, commonly known as the Mohave oak, is a hybrid species of oak native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and broad, lobed leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The tree is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and resistance to certain pests. It often grows in rocky soils and is found in regions such as Arizona, Nevada, and California.

The Mohave oak plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
