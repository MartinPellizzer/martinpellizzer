Quercus robur subf. gayeri

Quercus robur subf.

gayeri, commonly known as the Spanish oak, is a subspecies of the common oak characterized by its distinctive botanical features. It typically grows to a height of 20–30 meters, with a broad, spreading crown and deeply fissured bark.

The leaves are usually oblong to elliptical, with a serrated margin and a glossy upper surface. Its acorns are small, about 1–1.5 cm long, and are enclosed in a cupule that is hairy and persistent.

This subspecies is native to southern Europe and is adapted to dry, rocky soils, making it a hardy and resilient tree.
