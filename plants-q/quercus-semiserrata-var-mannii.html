Quercus semiserrata var. mannii

Quercus semiserrata var. mannii, commonly known as the African bracken fern oak, is a deciduous tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in open woodlands and savannas.

The bark is thick and fissured, providing habitat for a variety of insects and small animals.
