Quercus brutia subsp. haas

Quercus brutia subsp.

haas, commonly known as the Haas oak, is a subspecies native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in parts of Turkey and Syria. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen oak with a rounded crown and leathery, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky environments.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
