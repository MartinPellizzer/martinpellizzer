Quintilia

Quintilia, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features long, pendulous clusters of yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Quintilia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and ornamental landscaping.
