Quercus litiginosa

Quercus litiginosa, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by spiny capsules that contain edible nuts. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nut production.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
