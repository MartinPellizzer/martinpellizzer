Quercus suber var. angustifolia

Quercus suber var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cork oak, is a variant of the cork oak native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and thick, fissured bark, which is harvested for cork production. This evergreen tree typically grows in Mediterranean climates, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It plays a crucial role in its ecosystem, providing habitat for various species and contributing to soil stability.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical uses in traditional medicine.
