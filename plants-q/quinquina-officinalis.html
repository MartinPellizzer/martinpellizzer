Quinquina officinalis

Quinquina officinalis, commonly known as the Peruvian bark, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems, lance-shaped leaves, and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is most notable for its bark, which contains compounds like quinine, historically used to treat malaria. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its medicinal value, it has been cultivated and studied extensively in botanical and pharmacological research.
