Quaqua parviflora subsp. pulchra

Quaqua parviflora subsp.

pulchra, commonly known as the pink-flowered quina, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to lavender flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
