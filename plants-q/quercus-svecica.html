Quercus svecica

Quercus svecica, commonly known as the Swedish oak, is a species of deciduous tree native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and its ability to thrive in cooler, moist environments. The leaves are typically lobed and have a rough, dark green upper surface. This oak is often found in forested areas and is known for its hardy nature.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
