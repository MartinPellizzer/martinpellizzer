Quercus × subimbricaria

Quercus × subimbricaria, commonly known as the Spanish chestnut, is a hybrid evergreen oak native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and leathery, elliptical leaves that remain on the tree throughout the year. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits that are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
