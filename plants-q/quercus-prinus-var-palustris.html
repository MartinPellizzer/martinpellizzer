Quercus prinus var. palustris

Quercus prinus var. palustris, commonly known as the swamp chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, clustered flowers followed by acorns that are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in riparian and forest ecosystems.
