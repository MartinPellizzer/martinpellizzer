Quercus tinctoria var. magnifica

Quercus tinctoria var. magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that are often lobed and have a distinctive reddish or bronze coloration during the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible for both humans and wildlife. Its botanical features include a thick, corky bark and a tendency to form dense thickets.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
