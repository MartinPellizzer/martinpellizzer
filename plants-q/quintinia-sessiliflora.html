Quintinia sessiliflora

Quintinia sessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and inconspicuous white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically consumed by birds, which then disperse its seeds. It is native to parts of southern Africa and is adapted to thrive in a variety of forest environments.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly affect the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
