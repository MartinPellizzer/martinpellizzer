Quercus marilandica f. obovata

Quercus marilandica f. obovata, commonly known as the Maryland oak, is a variant of the white oak characterized by its broad, obovate leaves.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in regions with moist, well-drained soils. The plant exhibits a rounded crown and can grow to heights of up to 30 meters. Its acorns are typically small and have a chestnut-like appearance.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
