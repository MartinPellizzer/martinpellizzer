Quercus dimidiens

Quercus dimidiens, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, oval-shaped leaves with a doubly serrated margin. It produces small, cupule-enclosed acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Quercus dimidiens typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
