Quercus robur var. mannifera

Quercus robur var. mannifera, commonly known as the English oak, is a variant of the common oak native to Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically dark green and turn reddish-brown in autumn. The tree produces small, spiky acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

This variant is often found in woodlands and is valued for its ecological and historical significance.
