Quercus laurifolia f. obovatifolia

Quercus laurifolia f. obovatifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf oak, is a variant of the laurel oak, characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their ornamental appeal in landscaping.

The species is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a resilient and versatile plant.
