Quercus ilex var. pendula

Quercus ilex var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping ilex, is a variant of the evergreen oak tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The leaves are typically small, leathery, and glossy, with a deep green color that remains throughout the year. It produces small, red-brown acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This plant is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and adaptability to well-drained soils.
