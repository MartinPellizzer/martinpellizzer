Quinchamalium majus var. spegazzinii

Quinchamalium majus var. spegazzinii, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet petals and its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. It has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
