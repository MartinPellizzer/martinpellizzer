Quercus macrolepis

Quercus macrolepis, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often hairy when young. The tree produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
