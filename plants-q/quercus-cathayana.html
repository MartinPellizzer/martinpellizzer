Quercus cathayana

Quercus cathayana, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its acorns are small and have a cupule that encloses most of the nut.

Quercus cathayana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
