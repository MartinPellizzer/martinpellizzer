Quercus pedunculata f. stenocarpa

Quercus pedunculata f. stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruit Chinese chestnut, is a variant of the Chinese chestnut tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, narrow acorns and compact growth habit. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn season. Its botanical features include smooth, grey bark and small, elliptical leaves with a serrated margin.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and fruiting characteristics.
