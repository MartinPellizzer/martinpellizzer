Quercus lusitanica var. humilis

Quercus lusitanica var. humilis, commonly known as the Portuguese oak or the dwarf oak, is a shrub-like species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, dense foliage and robust, twisted trunk, often forming thickets. The leaves are typically oval with a serrated margin and can vary in color from dark green to reddish in autumn. It produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This plant thrives in rocky, acidic soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
