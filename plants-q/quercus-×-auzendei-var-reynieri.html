Quercus × auzendei var. reynieri

Quercus × auzendei var. reynieri, commonly known as the Reynier's oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus coccifera and Quercus faginea.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen oak native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in southern France and Spain. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a glossy appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to coastal and urban environments.
