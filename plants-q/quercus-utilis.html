Quercus utilis

Quercus utilis, commonly known as the chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing a distinctive appearance.

Quercus utilis is often found in moist, well-drained soils in mixed hardwood forests.
