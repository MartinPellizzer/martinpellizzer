Quercus hartwissiana var. typica

Quercus hartwissiana var. typica, commonly known as the Hartwissiana oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in cool, moist temperate climates. The tree has a broad, spreading crown and produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against environmental stressors.
