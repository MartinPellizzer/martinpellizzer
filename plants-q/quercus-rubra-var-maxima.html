Quercus rubra var. maxima

Quercus rubra var. maxima, commonly known as the American chestnut.

This variant is a subspecies of the northern red oak, characterized by its large size and distinctive bark. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The tree produces acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

However, it is now extremely rare due to the devastating impact of chestnut blight.
