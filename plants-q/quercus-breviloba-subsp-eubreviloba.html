Quercus breviloba subsp. eubreviloba

Quercus breviloba subsp.

eubreviloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chinquapin, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by spiny, spherical acorns that mature over the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
