Quercus × lanuginosa subsp. anselmii

Quercus × lanuginosa subsp.

anselmii, commonly known as the Anselm oak, is a hybrid species within the white oak group. It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and hairy, grayish-brown leaves that are typically oval to oblong in shape.

The plant is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It exhibits a slow growth rate and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
