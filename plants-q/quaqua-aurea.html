Quaqua aurea

Quaqua aurea, commonly known as the golden quilla, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the spring season. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine layer of silky hair, giving it a unique texture. Quaqua aurea thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
