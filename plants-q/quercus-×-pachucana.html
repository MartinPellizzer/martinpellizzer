Quercus × pachucana

Quercus × pachucana, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus bicolor and Quercus crassifolia.

It is native to central Mexico and is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and elliptical to ovate leaves with smooth margins. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a dense, hairy cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry to moist forested areas.

Quercus × pachucana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
