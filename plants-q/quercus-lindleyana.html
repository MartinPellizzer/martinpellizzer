Quercus lindleyana

Quercus lindleyana, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, clustered flowers followed by acorns, which are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

Quercus lindleyana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
