Quercus durata var. durata

Quercus durata var. durata, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are typically consumed by various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in oak woodlands and mixed forests.

The bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance that helps it withstand environmental stress.
