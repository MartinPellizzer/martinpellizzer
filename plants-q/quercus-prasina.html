Quercus prasina

Quercus prasina, commonly known as the evergreen oak, is a species of evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, leathery leaves that remain on the tree throughout the year. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, followed by acorns that mature over several months. Quercus prasina thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of Mediterranean ecosystems.
