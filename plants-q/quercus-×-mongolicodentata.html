Quercus × mongolicodentata

Quercus × mongolicodentata, commonly known as the Mongolian-lobed oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus mongolica and Quercus dentata.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically serrated along the edges. The plant is native to regions in East Asia, including parts of Mongolia and northern China. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

The acorns produced by this species are an important food source for various wildlife species.
