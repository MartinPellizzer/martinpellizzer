Quarena

Quarena, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, white, translucent petals that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant has a shallow root system adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and extreme temperatures. It blooms only at night, releasing a faint, sweet fragrance that attracts nocturnal pollinators.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, the quarena is considered an endangered species.
