Quercus × drummondii

Quercus × drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between the chestnut oak (Quercus montana) and the scarlet oak (Quercus coccinea).

It is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in areas with well-drained soils. The plant exhibits characteristics of both parent species, with leaves that are typically lobed and have a red or orange hue in the fall. It produces small, urn-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The Drummond oak is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
