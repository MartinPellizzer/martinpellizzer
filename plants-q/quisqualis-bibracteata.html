Quisqualis bibracteata

Quisqualis bibracteata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, woody pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
