Quercus hypophaea

Quercus hypophaea, commonly known as the rock chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits that are typically enclosed in a cupule. The leaves are typically elliptical or oblong, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or hilly terrain.

The tree is valued for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
