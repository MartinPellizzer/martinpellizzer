Quercus macranthera var. syspirensis

Quercus macranthera var. syspirensis, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in oak woodlands and mountainous regions.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
