Quercus coccinea var. undulata

Quercus coccinea var. undulata, commonly known as the scarlet oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage in the fall, which is a result of its high concentration of anthocyanin pigments. The leaves are typically 5 to 9 inches long, with a serrated margin and a wavy, undulating shape, giving it its varietal name "undulata.". The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in upland forests.
