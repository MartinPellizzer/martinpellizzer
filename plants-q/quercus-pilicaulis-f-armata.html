Quercus pilicaulis f. armata

Quercus pilicaulis f. armata, commonly known as the armata form of the bristlecone oak, is a rare variant of the species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, bristly foliage and small, acorn-like fruits. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and can live for several centuries, making it a long-lived species. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive hairy texture.

This form is adapted to arid environments and plays a significant role in its native ecosystems.
