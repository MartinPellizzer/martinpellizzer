Quercus ilex var. brevicupulata

Quercus ilex var. brevicupulata, commonly known as the Spanish chestnut or the cork oak, is a variant of the evergreen oak native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits that are typically less than 1.5 cm in length. The leaves are leathery and glossy, with a deep green color and a distinctive lobed shape. This plant thrives in Mediterranean climates, often growing in rocky or dry soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
