Quercus pedunculata var. sphaerobalanus

Quercus pedunculata var. sphaerobalanus, commonly known as the spherical acorn oak, is a variant of the pedunculate oak characterized by its unique acorn morphology.

This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value. The acorns are spherical and often covered in a dense, hairy coating, which aids in seed dispersal.

It is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
