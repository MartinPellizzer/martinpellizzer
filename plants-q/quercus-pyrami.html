Quercus pyrami

Quercus pyrami, commonly known as the pyramid oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is characterized by its conical shape and dark, glossy leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
