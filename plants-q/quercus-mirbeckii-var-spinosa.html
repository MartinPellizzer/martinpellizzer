Quercus mirbeckii var. spinosa

Quercus mirbeckii var. spinosa, commonly known as the spiny white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thick, fissured bark and acorns that are typically covered in a dense, hairy coat. The leaves are lobed and often have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
