Quercus densiflora var. echinoides

Quercus densiflora var. echinoides, commonly known as the echinoid wood oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its dense, flaky bark and small, acorn-like fruits. The leaves are typically narrow and pointed, with a rough upper surface and a hairy underside. This variety is known for its slow growth and longevity, often reaching heights of 20 to 30 meters.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
