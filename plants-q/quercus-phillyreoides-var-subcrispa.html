Quercus phillyreoides var. subcrispa

Quercus phillyreoides var. subcrispa, commonly known as the Florida hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It has a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a hairy underside. The tree produces small, pale green flowers in catkins, followed by oval, woody fruits called nuts.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in upland forests and open woodlands.
