Quercus ichangensis

Quercus ichangensis, commonly known as the Ichang oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The Ichang oak is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.
