Quercus intermedia

Quercus intermedia, commonly known as the bluejack oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that turn a striking blue-green color in the fall. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is typically gray and scaly, providing a distinctive appearance.

Quercus intermedia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
