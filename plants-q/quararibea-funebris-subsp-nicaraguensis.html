Quararibea funebris subsp. nicaraguensis

Quararibea funebris subsp.

nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan blackwood, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its dark, heavy wood and smooth, dark bark.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is native to Central America and is valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.
