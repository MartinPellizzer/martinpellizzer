Quercus crenifolia

Quercus crenifolia, commonly known as the coastal live oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that have a distinctive wavy or scalloped margin. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to salt spray. It produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The tree is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
