Quercus × coutinhoi f. duriensis

Quercus × coutinhoi f. duriensis, commonly known as the Duriensis oak, is a hybrid species within the genus Quercus, belonging to the Fagaceae family.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically leathery and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in Mediterranean climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique genetic makeup, it exhibits traits that make it both ecologically and botanically significant.
