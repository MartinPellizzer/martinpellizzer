Quercus lanigera f. sideroxyloides

Quercus lanigera f. sideroxyloides, commonly known as the silverleaf oak, is a variant of the silverleaf oak native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white pubescence on both the upper and lower surfaces of its leaves. The leaves are typically oval to elliptic in shape, with a pointed tip and a smooth margin. This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in arid to semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
