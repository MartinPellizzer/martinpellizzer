Quercus cerris var. pendula

Quercus cerris var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Turkey oak, is a unique variant of the Turkey oak characterized by its pendulous branches that create a cascading effect.

This tree is native to the Balkan Peninsula and parts of southern Europe, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are typically broad, elliptical, and have a dark green upper surface with a lighter underside. The tree produces small, red-orange acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and distinctive appearance.
