Quercus goedelii

Quercus goedelii, commonly known as the Goedel's oak, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in high-altitude forests with cool and moist climates. The plant features broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the autumn, and it produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife.
