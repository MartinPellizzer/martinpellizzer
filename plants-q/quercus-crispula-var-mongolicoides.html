Quercus crispula var. mongolicoides

Quercus crispula var. mongolicoides, commonly known as the Mongolian rock oak, is a variant of the Japanese oak native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are often crinkled and have a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions. Its acorns are small and provide food for various wildlife species.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
