Quercus × intercedens

Quercus × intercedens, commonly known as the interceding oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between the white oak (Quercus alba) and the scarlet oak (Quercus coccinea).

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped with a serrated margin. The plant exhibits a range of leaf colors, from bright green to reddish hues, especially during the fall season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests across the eastern United States.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
