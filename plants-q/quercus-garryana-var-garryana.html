Quercus garryana var. garryana

Quercus garryana var. garryana, commonly known as the Garry oak, is a deciduous tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as coastal grasslands and woodlands.

Its ecological importance is significant in maintaining biodiversity in its native range.
