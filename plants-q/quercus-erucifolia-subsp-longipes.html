Quercus erucifolia subsp. longipes

Quercus erucifolia subsp.

longipes, commonly known as the long-leaf chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its elongated, acorn-like fruits and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The leaves are typically long and narrow, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. This subspecies is part of the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
