Quercus acutiglandis

Quercus acutiglandis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and its leaves, which are typically narrow and pointed. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Quercus acutiglandis is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal in landscaping.
