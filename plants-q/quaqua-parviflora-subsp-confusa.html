Quaqua parviflora subsp. confusa

Quaqua parviflora subsp.

confusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved quaqua, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer months.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure compared to other members of the species.
