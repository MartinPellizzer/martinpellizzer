Quercus rubra var. ramosissima

Quercus rubra var. ramosissima, commonly known as the red oak, is a variant of the northern red oak characterized by its dense, spreading branches.

It is native to eastern North America and typically grows in a variety of soil types, including moist to dry soils. The plant features broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow to reddish-brown in the fall, contributing to its ornamental value. It produces acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

This variant is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
