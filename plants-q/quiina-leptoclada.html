Quiina leptoclada

Quiina leptoclada, commonly known as the slender quiina, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, triangular leaves and clusters of tiny, pinkish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its succulent stems. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
