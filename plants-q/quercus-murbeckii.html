Quercus murbeckii

Quercus murbeckii, commonly known as the Murbeck oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and silvery white underneath. The plant produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The botanical features of Quercus murbeckii make it a resilient and ecologically significant plant in its native range.
