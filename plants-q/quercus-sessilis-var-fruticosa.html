Quercus sessilis var. fruticosa

Quercus sessilis var. fruticosa, commonly known as the shrub oak, is a deciduous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and dense, shrub-like growth habit. The leaves are typically oval or elliptical with a smooth or serrated margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wetlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
