Quercus dalmatica f. parathalassias

Quercus dalmatica f. parathalassias, commonly known as the Dalmatian oak subvariety parathalassias, is a rare and unique form of the Dalmatian oak.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring narrow, elongated lobes that set it apart from other Quercus species. This subvariety is adapted to specific ecological conditions, often found in coastal or mountainous regions with well-drained soils. Its botanical significance lies in its genetic diversity and role in local ecosystems.

Due to its rarity, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
