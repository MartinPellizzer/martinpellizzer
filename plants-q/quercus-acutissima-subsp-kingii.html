Quercus acutissima subsp. kingii

Quercus acutissima subsp.

kingii, commonly known as the King's oak, is a deciduous tree native to southern China. It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate, glossy leaves that turn reddish-brown in autumn.

The tree produces small, chestnut-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
