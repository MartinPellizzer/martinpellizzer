Quercus thracica

Quercus thracica, commonly known as the Thrace oak, is a rare and ancient species native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and broad, lobed leaves that are often hairy underneath. The tree typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its wood is dense and strong, historically used for construction and tool-making.

Due to habitat loss and overexploitation, it is now considered endangered and protected in several countries.
