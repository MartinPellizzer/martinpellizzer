Quercus reticulata subsp. rhodophlebia

Quercus reticulata subsp.

rhodophlebia, commonly known as the red-leafed oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China. It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, with leaves that are typically reddish in color, especially during the autumn season.

The plant produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
