Quercus revoluta f. pungens

Quercus revoluta f. pungens, commonly known as the Japanese oak, is a variant of the Japanese evergreen oak.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a distinctive revolute margin. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by edible acorns in the fall.

This ornamental tree is valued for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal in landscaping.
