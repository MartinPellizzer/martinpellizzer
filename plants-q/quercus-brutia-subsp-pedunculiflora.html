Quercus brutia subsp. pedunculiflora

Quercus brutia subsp.

pedunculiflora, commonly known as the Pedunculate Oak, is a species native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its lobed leaves and acorns, which are a key feature of the genus Quercus.

The plant typically grows in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground. It has a moderate growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
