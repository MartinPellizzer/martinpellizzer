Quercus mairei

Quercus mairei, commonly known as the Maire oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of southern China and northern Vietnam.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn vibrant yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The Maire oak is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
