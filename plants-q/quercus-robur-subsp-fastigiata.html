Quercus robur subsp. fastigiata

Quercus robur subsp.

fastigiata, commonly known as the columnar oak, is a subspecies of the common oak characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit. It is native to parts of Europe and is typically found in rocky or mountainous regions.

The leaves are small, elliptical, and have a glossy green upper surface with a lighter underside. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
