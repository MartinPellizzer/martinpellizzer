Quassia intermedia

Quassia intermedia, commonly known as the African quassia, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Simaroubaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its medicinal properties, with the bark and roots containing compounds used in traditional medicine.

The tree also serves as a habitat for various insects and birds in its native ecosystem.
