Quercus campanariensis

Quercus campanariensis, commonly known as the Campanula oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath. The tree produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, which are typically white or pale blue. Its acorns are edible and are an important food source for various wildlife species.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in rocky or dry soils.
