Quercus petraea subsp. polycarpa

Quercus petraea subsp.

polycarpa, commonly known as the sessile oak, is a deciduous tree native to Europe. It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

This subspecies is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to different environmental conditions.
