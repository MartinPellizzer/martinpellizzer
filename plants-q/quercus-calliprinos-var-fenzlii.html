Quercus calliprinos var. fenzlii

Quercus calliprinos var. fenzlii, commonly known as the Fenzli's canyon oak, is a variant of the canyon oak native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its deep lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support in rocky and arid environments.

This variant is adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in canyons and slopes.
