Quercus pittieri

Quercus pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance that helps it withstand harsh environmental conditions.
