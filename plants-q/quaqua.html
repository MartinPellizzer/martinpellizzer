Quaqua

Quaqua, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Quaqua is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
