Quercus × guerangeri

Quercus × guerangeri, commonly known as the Gueranger oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus robur (English oak) and Quercus petraea (sessile oak).

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in temperate regions of Europe, particularly in France and the British Isles. The plant exhibits characteristics of both parent species, with a broad, spreading crown and deeply lobed leaves. Its acorns are small and often have a cupule, and it produces them in the autumn.

The Gueranger oak is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in parks and gardens.
