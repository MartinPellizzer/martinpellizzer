Quercus suber f. vulgaris

Quercus suber f. vulgaris, commonly known as the common cork oak, is a variant of the cork oak native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thick, bark that can be harvested for cork production. The plant is a deciduous tree with broad, lobed leaves that turn reddish in autumn. It thrives in Mediterranean climates with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers.

The species plays a vital role in its ecosystem, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
