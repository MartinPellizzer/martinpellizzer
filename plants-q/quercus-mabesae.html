Quercus mabesae

Quercus mabesae, commonly known as the Mabesa oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family, Fagaceae.

It is native to the highland regions of Mexico, particularly in the states of Oaxaca and Chiapas. This evergreen tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring broad, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The tree produces small, cupule-enclosed acorns, which are an important food source for local wildlife.

Its ecological significance is notable in maintaining biodiversity in its native habitat.
