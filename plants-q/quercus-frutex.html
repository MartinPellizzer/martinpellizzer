Quercus frutex

Quercus frutex, commonly known as the dwarf oak, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and leathery, dark green leaves that remain throughout the year. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by acorns, which are an important food source for wildlife. Its hardy nature allows it to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts.
