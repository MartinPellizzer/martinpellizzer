Quisqualis exannulata

Quisqualis exannulata, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night. The plant is a climbing vine with woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers are typically tubular with a prominent throat and emit a sweet scent to attract pollinators.
