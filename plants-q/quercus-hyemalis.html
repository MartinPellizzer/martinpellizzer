Quercus hyemalis

Quercus hyemalis, commonly known as the winter oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree has a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystem, supporting various wildlife species.
