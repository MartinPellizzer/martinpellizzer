Quisqualis densiflora

Quisqualis densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered velvet tamarind, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant produces small, round, woody fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Quisqualis densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
