Quercus × coutinhoi subsp. beturica

Quercus × coutinhoi subsp.

beturica, commonly known as the Portuguese oak, is a hybrid species native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its deciduous leaves, which are typically lobed and have a serrated margin.

The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are often covered in a dense, fuzzy layer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and historical use in local landscapes.
