Quercus cerris var. crispa

Quercus cerris var. crispa, commonly known as the Turkish oak or crinkled oak, is a variant of the common oak characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves.

This plant is native to the mountains of Turkey and the Balkans, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a unique wavy or crinkled margin. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
