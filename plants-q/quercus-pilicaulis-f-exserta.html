Quercus pilicaulis f. exserta

Quercus pilicaulis f. exserta, commonly known as the exserted bracted oak, is a variant of the bracted oak, characterized by its distinctive bracts that extend beyond the acorns.

This plant is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Georgia and Florida. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a serrated margin and a glossy green upper surface. The acorns are small, with a scaly cap that is more pronounced in this form.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
