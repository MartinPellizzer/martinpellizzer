Quercus susedana

Quercus susedana, commonly known as the Susedan oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Thailand and Vietnam. The tree is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn vibrant red in the autumn. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Quercus susedana is valued for its hardy nature and is often found in forest ecosystems where it contributes to biodiversity.
