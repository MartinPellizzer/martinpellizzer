Quercus spinosa var. miyabei

Quercus spinosa var. miyabei, commonly known as the Japanese hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, red berries that ripen in the fall, providing food for various wildlife species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types and climates.
