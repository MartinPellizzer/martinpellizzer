Queltia × concolor

Queltia × concolor, commonly known as the hybrid queltia, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Queltia miersii and Queltia calycina.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family, characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its floral structure is notable for its vibrant colors, often ranging from pink to red, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Queltia × concolor is known for its adaptability and is cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
