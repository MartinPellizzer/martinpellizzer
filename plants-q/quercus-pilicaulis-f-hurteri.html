Quercus pilicaulis f. hurteri

Quercus pilicaulis f. hurteri, commonly known as the Hurter's bristlecone oak, is a rare variant of the bristlecone oak species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, bristly leaves that give it a unique appearance. This subspecies is typically found in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. Its small, acorn-like fruits are an important food source for various wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
