Quercus petropolitana

Quercus petropolitana, commonly known as the Russian oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It typically grows in mountainous areas and prefers well-drained soils.

Quercus petropolitana is valued for its hardy nature and is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental qualities.
