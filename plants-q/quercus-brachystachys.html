Quercus brachystachys

Quercus brachystachys, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and lobed, leathery leaves that turn bright red in the fall. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by inedible, red-orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The botanical features of Quercus brachystachys make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
