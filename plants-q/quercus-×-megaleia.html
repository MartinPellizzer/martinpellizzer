Quercus × megaleia

Quercus × megaleia, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus polymorpha and Quercus crassifolia.

It is native to the central and southern regions of Mexico, where it thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic soils. The tree typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring a broad, spreading crown and smooth, gray bark. Its leaves are usually broad-ovate with rounded tips and are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, dark brown acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.
