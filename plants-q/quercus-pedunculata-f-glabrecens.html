Quercus pedunculata f. glabrecens

Quercus pedunculata f. glabrecens, commonly known as the glabrous pedunculate oak, is a variant of the pedunculate oak native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless bark and smaller leaves compared to the typical species. The plant is a deciduous tree that thrives in temperate climates, often found in woodland areas. It produces acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it a notable subject in ecological and horticultural studies.
