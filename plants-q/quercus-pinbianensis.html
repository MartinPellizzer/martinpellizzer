Quercus pinbianensis

Quercus pinbianensis, commonly known as the Pinbian oak, is a species of evergreen oak native to China.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

The botanical features of Quercus pinbianensis make it a valuable component of its native ecosystem.
