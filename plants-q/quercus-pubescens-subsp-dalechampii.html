Quercus pubescens subsp. dalechampii

Quercus pubescens subsp.

dalechampii, commonly known as the Spanish oak, is a subspecies of the sessile oak characterized by its distinctive foliage and growth habits. It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, thriving in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

The leaves are typically oval to elliptic, with a dense pubescence on the underside, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, clustered acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

This subspecies is often found in woodland areas and can reach heights of up to 20 meters, making it a significant component of its native ecosystems.
