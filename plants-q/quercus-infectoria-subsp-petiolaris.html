Quercus infectoria subsp. petiolaris

Quercus infectoria subsp.

petiolaris, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its dense, dark bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often hairy underneath.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by hard, spiky fruits that contain a single seed. It is valued for its strong, heavy wood, which is used in construction and tool-making.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
