Quercus abietum

Quercus abietum, commonly known as the silver fir oak, is a species of evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and conical crown, with dark green, needle-like leaves that are coated in a silvery-white bloom. The tree produces small, red-brown cones that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Quercus abietum plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
