Quercus korthalsii var. mappacea

Quercus korthalsii var. mappacea, commonly known as the Mappace oak, is a rare and unique variant of the korthalsii oak.

It is native to southern Africa, specifically occurring in parts of Namibia and South Africa. This evergreen shrub or small tree has small, leathery leaves that are often densely packed. Its flowers are typically yellowish-green, and it produces acorns that are an important food source for local wildlife.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
