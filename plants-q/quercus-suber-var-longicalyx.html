Quercus suber var. longicalyx

Quercus suber var. longicalyx, commonly known as the Chinese cork oak, is a variant of the cork oak native to China.

It is characterized by its thick, corky bark that can be harvested for commercial use. The leaves are typically small, leathery, and have a glossy surface. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This plant is valued for its ecological role and economic importance in the production of cork.
