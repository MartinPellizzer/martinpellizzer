Quercus glandulifera var. donarium

Quercus glandulifera var. donarium, commonly known as the Don's Turkey Oak, is a variant of the Turkey Oak native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are encased in a dense, scaly cap. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
