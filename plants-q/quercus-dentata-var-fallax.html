Quercus dentata var. fallax

Quercus dentata var. fallax, commonly known as the Japanese pagoda tree or the Japanese oak, is a variant of the Japanese oak species.

It is a deciduous tree native to Japan, characterized by its unique, tiered branching pattern that resembles a pagoda. The leaves are typically narrow and pointed, with a serrated edge, and they turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in Japanese culture.
