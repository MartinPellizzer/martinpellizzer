Quiina florida

Quiina florida, commonly known as the flowering quiina, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to conserve water.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
