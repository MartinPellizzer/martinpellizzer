Quassia arnhemensis

Quassia arnhemensis, commonly known as the black quassia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Simaroubaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its roots and bark contain quassinoids, which are known for their bitter compounds and medicinal properties.

This species is often valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a natural insecticide.
