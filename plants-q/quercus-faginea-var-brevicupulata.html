Quercus faginea var. brevicupulata

Quercus faginea var. brevicupulata, commonly known as the Hungarian oak, is a deciduous tree native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like nuts enclosed in a short, cupule. The leaves are typically lobed and have a rough, textured upper surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance and historical use in traditional forestry.
