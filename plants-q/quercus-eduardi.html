Quercus eduardi

Quercus eduardi, commonly known as Edward's oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family, Fagaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico, where it thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. The tree is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn a striking yellow in the fall. Its acorns are small and typically require a period of cold stratification to germinate.
