Quercus faginea subvar. microphylla

Quercus faginea subvar. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved hornbeam oak, is a variant of the hornbeam oak native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, chestnut-like acorns that are enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with mild climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
