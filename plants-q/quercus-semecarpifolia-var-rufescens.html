Quercus semecarpifolia var. rufescens

Quercus semecarpifolia var. rufescens, commonly known as the red-leaf chinquapin, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, spiny acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule with hairy bracts. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a serrated margin.
