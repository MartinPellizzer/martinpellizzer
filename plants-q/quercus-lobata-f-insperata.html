Quercus lobata f. insperata

Quercus lobata f. insperata, commonly known as the interior live oak, is a rare variant of the coast live oak.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and its ability to thrive in dry, inland environments. This form is typically found in the central and southern parts of California. It has a slow growth rate and can live for over a century.

The plant is adapted to drought conditions and plays an important role in local ecosystems.
