Quercus guyavifolia

Quercus guyavifolia, commonly known as the Guyana sweet chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to northern South America.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The plant is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet, chestnut-like fruits.

The tree is valued for its hardy nature and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.
