Quercus lobata var. hindsii

Quercus lobata var. hindsii, commonly known as the Hinds' valley oak, is a variant of the valley oak native to California.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and a tall, open crown. The bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against environmental stress. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native range.

This variant is often found in riparian areas and is valued for its ecological and historical significance.
