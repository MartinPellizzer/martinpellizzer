Quercus nitens var. subintegra

Quercus nitens var. subintegra, commonly known as the Sierra Madre oak, is a variant of the California black oak.

It is a deciduous tree native to the western United States, particularly found in the Sierra Nevada range. The plant has a broad, rounded crown and produces acorns that are typically covered in a dense, fuzzy cap. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a dark green upper surface with a lighter underside.

The tree is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in mixed coniferous forests.
