Quaqua parviflora subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered quaqua, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a subtle aromatic scent.
