Qualea brevipedicellata

Qualea brevipedicellata, commonly known as the short-stemmed water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that form a dense canopy on the water surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the dry season, typically in the early morning. Its roots anchor in the muddy substrate, allowing it to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Qualea brevipedicellata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
