Quercus × joorii

Quercus × joorii, commonly known as the Chinese white oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Quercus glauca and Quercus variabilis.

It is a deciduous tree native to China, characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Botanically, it exhibits traits of both parent species, showcasing variability in leaf shape and acorn size.

Its adaptability to different soil types makes it a valuable species in ecological restoration projects.
