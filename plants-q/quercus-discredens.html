Quercus discredens

Quercus discredens, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale gray bark and broad, lobed leaves. The tree produces small, cupule-enclosed acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Quercus discredens is often found in mixed forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
