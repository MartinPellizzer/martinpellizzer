Quercus pedunculata var. salicifolia-pendula

Quercus pedunculata var. salicifolia-pendula, commonly known as the weeping willow oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that give it a distinctive weeping appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a silvery-green underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by small, red-brown acorns.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
