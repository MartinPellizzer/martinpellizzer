Quercus cerris f. laciniatolyrata

Quercus cerris f. laciniatolyrata, commonly known as the lace-leaf Turkish oak, is a unique variant of the Turkish oak characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This form exhibits finely serrated, lobed leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The leaves are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath, contributing to its ornamental value. It is a deciduous tree that thrives in temperate climates, often found in regions with well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a sought-after specimen in horticulture and botanical gardens.
