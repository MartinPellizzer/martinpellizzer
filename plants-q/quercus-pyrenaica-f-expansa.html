Quercus pyrenaica f. expansa

Quercus pyrenaica f. expansa, commonly known as the Spanish chestnut or broad-leaved sessile oak, is a distinctive variant of the sessile oak.

It is characterized by its broader, more elongated leaves compared to the typical form, with a distinctive reddish-brown coloration on the underside. This subspecies is native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France, thriving in temperate climates. It is a deciduous tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, featuring a rounded crown and deep fissured bark.

The plant is valued for its ecological role and is often found in mixed forests and woodland areas.
