Quaternella glabratoides

Quaternella glabratoides, commonly known as the smooth quaternella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Quaternella glabratoides is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
