Quercus crispula var. manshurica

Quercus crispula var. manshurica, commonly known as the Manshurian sawtooth oak, is a variant of the Japanese oak characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins.

It is native to the Far East, particularly found in regions of Russia and northeastern China. The plant is a deciduous tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
