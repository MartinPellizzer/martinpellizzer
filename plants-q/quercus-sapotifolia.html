Quercus sapotifolia

Quercus sapotifolia, commonly known as the sapotifolia oak, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family.

It is native to parts of Central America and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that turn vibrant colors in the fall. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by edible, spherical fruits resembling small apples. Quercus sapotifolia thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
