Quaqua incarnata subsp. aurea

Quaqua incarnata subsp.

aurea, commonly known as the golden bluem, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies aurea is distinguished by its golden-yellow stamens, which contrast beautifully with the blue petals. It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
