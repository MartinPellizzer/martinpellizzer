Quercus lyrata

Quercus lyrata, commonly known as the sweetshrub oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive lyre-shaped leaves, which are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in wetland or bottomland habitats.

Quercus lyrata is valued for its ecological role and is often used in conservation and restoration projects.
