Quercus × schochiana

Quercus × schochiana, commonly known as the Schöch's oak, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between the English oak (Quercus robur) and the sessile oak (Quercus petraea).

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are typically enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mixed woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and forest biodiversity.
