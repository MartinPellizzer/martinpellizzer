Quercus pedunculata var. pseudopedunculata

Quercus pedunculata var. pseudopedunculata, commonly known as the Chinese red oak, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-brown bark and acorns that are often covered in a dense, woolly layer. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated margin and a glossy upper surface. This species thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
