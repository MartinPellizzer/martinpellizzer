Quercus petiolaris

Quercus petiolaris, commonly known as the Florida栎, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The tree produces small, spiky capsules that contain one to three seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Quercus petiolaris typically grows to a height of 20–30 meters and has a broad, rounded crown.
