Quercus rufescens

Quercus rufescens, commonly known as the scarlet oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and acorns that are typically covered in a dense, fuzzy coating. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a serrated margin and a red or reddish-brown underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

The plant plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, providing food and shelter for various species.
