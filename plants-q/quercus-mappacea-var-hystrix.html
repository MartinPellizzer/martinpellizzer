Quercus mappacea var. hystrix

Quercus mappacea var. hystrix, commonly known as the hystrix chestnut oak, is a variant of the chestnut oak native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is typically gray and furrowed, providing a rugged appearance.

This variant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
