Quercus faginea subvar. bullata

Quercus faginea subvar. bullata, commonly known as the Hungarian hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, acorn-like fruits and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including dry and rocky conditions. The leaves are typically small, elliptical, and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin. This subspecies is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardiness.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
