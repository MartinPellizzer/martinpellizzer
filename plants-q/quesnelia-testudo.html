Quesnelia testudo

Quesnelia testudo, commonly known as the turtleback bromeliad, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding tank. The plant's leaves are thick and waxy, with a distinctive turtle-like texture on the upper surface. It produces showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

Quesnelia testudo is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
