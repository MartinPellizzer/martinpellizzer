Quercus sessiliflora var. purpurea

Quercus sessiliflora var. purpurea, commonly known as the purpleleaf oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive dark purple to red foliage, which provides striking visual interest throughout the growing season. The leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a serrated margin, while the acorns are small and typically have a scaly cap. This variety is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable ornamental and ecological plant in suitable environments.
