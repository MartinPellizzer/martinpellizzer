Quercus elegans

Quercus elegans, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, spreading crown and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are elliptical, with smooth edges and a pointed tip, turning yellow in the fall. It produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for wildlife.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks.
