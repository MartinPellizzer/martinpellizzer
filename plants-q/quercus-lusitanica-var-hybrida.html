Quercus lusitanica var. hybrida

Quercus lusitanica var. hybrida, commonly known as the Portuguese oak hybrid, is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It exhibits characteristics of both Quercus lusitanica and Quercus faginea, resulting in a unique combination of traits. The tree has a broad, spreading crown and produces acorns that are an important food source for wildlife. Its leaves are typically lobed and turn yellow in autumn, contributing to its ornamental value.

This species is often found in mixed forests and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
