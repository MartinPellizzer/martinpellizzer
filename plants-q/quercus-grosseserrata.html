Quercus grosseserrata

Quercus grosseserrata, commonly known as the Sierra foothill oak, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and deeply lobed leaves with prominent, serrated margins. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant component of its native ecosystem.
