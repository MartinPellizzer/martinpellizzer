Wrightia rheedei

Wrightia rheedei, commonly known as the Indian white wood or white flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant has smooth, pale grey bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. Its flowers are fragrant, yellowish-white, and arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Wrightia rheedei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree.
