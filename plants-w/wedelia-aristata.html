Wedelia aristata

Wedelia aristata, commonly known as the silverleaf groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with fleshy, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
