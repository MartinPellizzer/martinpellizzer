Wendlandia tenuiflora

Wendlandia tenuiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered wendlandia, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.
