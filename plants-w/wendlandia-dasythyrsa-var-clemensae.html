Wendlandia dasythyrsa var. clemensae

Wendlandia dasythyrsa var. clemensae, commonly known as the Clemens' waxflower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, branched inflorescence that produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and covered with a fine, waxy coating, contributing to its unique appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil conditions.
