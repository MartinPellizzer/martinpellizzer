Weingartia cylindrica

Weingartia cylindrica, commonly known as the football cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that resemble small footballs, which are typically green with a powdery coating. The plant produces small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
