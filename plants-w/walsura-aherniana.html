Walsura aherniana

Walsura aherniana, commonly known as the Ahern's tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the branches. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by edible, sour-tasting fruits resembling small, round tamarinds. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
