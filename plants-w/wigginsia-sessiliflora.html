Wigginsia sessiliflora

Wigginsia sessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wigginsia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Wigginsia sessiliflora is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
