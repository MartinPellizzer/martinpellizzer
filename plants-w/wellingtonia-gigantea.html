Wellingtonia gigantea

Wellingtonia gigantea, commonly known as the giant sequoia, is a towering coniferous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Taxodiaceae family and is characterized by its massive, buttressed roots and thick, flaky bark. The tree produces small, reddish-brown cones that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are needle-like, short, and arranged in spirals along the branches.
