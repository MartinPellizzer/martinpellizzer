Weinmannia icacifolia

Weinmannia icacifolia, commonly known as the coastal weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and unique, flattened, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure and strong winds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
