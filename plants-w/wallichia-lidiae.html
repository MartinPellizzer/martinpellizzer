Wallichia lidiae

Wallichia lidiae, commonly known as the Kashmir apple or Himalayan apple, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and is often found in forested areas.

Wallichia lidiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
