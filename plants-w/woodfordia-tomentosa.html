Woodfordia tomentosa

Woodfordia tomentosa, commonly known as the velvetwood or woolly woodfordia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thick, woody stems covered in a dense, fuzzy layer of fine hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a leathery texture and a dark green color.

The plant is well adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
