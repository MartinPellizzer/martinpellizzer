Wedelia caracasana

Wedelia caracasana, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Wedelia caracasana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
