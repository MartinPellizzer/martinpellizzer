Wittmackia froesii

Wittmackia froesii, commonly known as the blue bell bellflower, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences, adding a dramatic visual impact to its natural habitat.

Wittmackia froesii thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
