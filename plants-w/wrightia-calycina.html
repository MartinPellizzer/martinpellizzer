Wrightia calycina

Wrightia calycina, commonly known as the Indian rose or white rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Wrightiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Wrightia calycina is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
