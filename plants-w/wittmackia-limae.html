Wittmackia limae

Wittmackia limae, commonly known as the Peruvian bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

Wittmackia limae is known for its ability to bloom reliably in USDA hardiness zones 9 through 11.
