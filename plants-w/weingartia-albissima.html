Weingartia albissima

Weingartia albissima, commonly known as the white-furred cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense, white or pale blue spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Weingartia albissima is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
