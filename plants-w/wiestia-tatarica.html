Wiestia tatarica

Wiestia tatarica, commonly known as the Tatarian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in late summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
