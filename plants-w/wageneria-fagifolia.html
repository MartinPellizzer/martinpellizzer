Wageneria fagifolia

Wageneria fagifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed flax, is a member of the Linaceae family.

It is native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere, particularly in Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in textile production.
