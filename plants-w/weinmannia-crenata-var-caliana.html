Weinmannia crenata var. caliana

Weinmannia crenata var. caliana, commonly known as the California weinmannia, is a shrub native to coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to stabilize sandy or rocky soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal climates.
