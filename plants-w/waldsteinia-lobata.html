Waldsteinia lobata

Waldsteinia lobata, commonly known as the Appalachian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a low-growing habit with spreading rhizomes, and its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in woodland areas.

Waldsteinia lobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
