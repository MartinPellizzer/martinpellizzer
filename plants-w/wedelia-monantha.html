Wedelia monantha

Wedelia monantha, commonly known as the silverleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and modest flowering.
