Wikstroemia elongata var. austrocochinchinensis

Wikstroemia elongata var. austrocochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese beaumontia, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are usually opposite or whorled in arrangement. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
