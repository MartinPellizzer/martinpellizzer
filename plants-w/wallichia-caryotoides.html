Wallichia caryotoides

Wallichia caryotoides, commonly known as the broad-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

Wallichia caryotoides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
