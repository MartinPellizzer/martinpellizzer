Watsonia palustris

Watsonia palustris, commonly known as the marsh Watsonia, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous bright pink to red flowers. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often form a basal rosette, contributing to the plant's ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Watsonia palustris is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
