Weinmannia microphylla var. weddellii

Weinmannia microphylla var. weddellii, commonly known as the Weddell's silverleaf, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that are small and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Its unique foliage and adaptability to harsh climates make it a notable species in its native habitat.
