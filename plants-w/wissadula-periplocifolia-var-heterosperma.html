Wissadula periplocifolia var. heterosperma

Wissadula periplocifolia var. heterosperma, commonly known as the woolly leaf candelabra, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and branched stems that often form a compact, bushy shape. The leaves are typically elliptical with a soft, velvety texture, and the plant produces small, pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance contribute to its ornamental value in horticulture.
