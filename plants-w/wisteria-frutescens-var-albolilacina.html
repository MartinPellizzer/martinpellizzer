Wisteria frutescens var. albolilacina

Wisteria frutescens var. albolilacina, commonly known as the white and lavender wisteria, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, white to pale lavender flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of 12-20 oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are typically brown and covered in a rough, woody texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a climbing plant.
