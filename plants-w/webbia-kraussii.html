Webbia kraussii

Webbia kraussii, commonly known as the Cape webbed bell, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, webbed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
