Watsonia vittata

Watsonia vittata, commonly known as the yellow firecracker, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, strap-like leaves and produces vivid yellow flowers in late summer. The plant belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants. Watsonia vittata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and long-lasting blooms make it a favorite among gardeners seeking colorful, drought-tolerant plants.
