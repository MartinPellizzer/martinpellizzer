Witheringia procumbens

Witheringia procumbens, commonly known as the creeping withering vine, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.

Witheringia procumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
