Widdringtonia cedarbergensis

Widdringtonia cedarbergensis, commonly known as the Cedarberg cedar, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the Cedarberg Mountains in South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage that forms a broad, conical crown. The plant is known for its slow growth and longevity, with some specimens living over 1,000 years. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against fires and harsh environmental conditions.

The Cedarberg cedar plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, supporting diverse wildlife and contributing to the region's unique biodiversity.
