Weigela coraeensis var. fragrans

Weigela coraeensis var. fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in early spring. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing an attractive contrast to its floral display. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

The fragrant weigela is also noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
