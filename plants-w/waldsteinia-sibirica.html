Waldsteinia sibirica

Waldsteinia sibirica, commonly known as Siberian cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal leaves that form a rosette and its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens.

Waldsteinia sibirica is also known for its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
