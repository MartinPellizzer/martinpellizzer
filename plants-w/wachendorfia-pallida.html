Wachendorfia pallida

Wachendorfia pallida, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a striking plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pale pink to white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant has a short, leafless stem and produces showy, fragrant blooms that typically appear in late winter to early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and requires a humid environment to flourish.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
