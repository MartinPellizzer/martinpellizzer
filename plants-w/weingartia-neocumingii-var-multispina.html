Weingartia neocumingii var. multispina

Weingartia neocumingii var. multispina, commonly known as the multispined barrel cactus, is a compact, slow-growing cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem that is typically green with a waxy coating, and it produces clusters of pale yellow flowers during the spring. The plant is characterized by its multiple spines, which are often curved and can vary in color from brown to reddish. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
