Webera oppositifolia var. floribunda

Webera oppositifolia var. floribunda, commonly known as the showy false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces striking clusters of blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native gardens and landscapes.
