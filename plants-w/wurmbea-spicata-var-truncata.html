Wurmbea spicata var. truncata

Wurmbea spicata var. truncata, commonly known as the African lily or the yellow bulbous plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a bulbous stem that is often covered in a fibrous sheath, with lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens and botanical collections.
