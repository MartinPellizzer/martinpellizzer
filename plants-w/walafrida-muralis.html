Walafrida muralis

Walafrida muralis, commonly known as the wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, brightly colored flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Walafrida muralis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
