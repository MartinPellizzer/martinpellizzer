Weingartia rubroaurea

Weingartia rubroaurea, commonly known as the golden weingartia, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white to light green spines. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its compact size and attractive appearance.

Weingartia rubroaurea requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.
