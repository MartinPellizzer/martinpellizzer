Withania adpressa

Withania adpressa, commonly known as the dwarf Indian cockroach plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy, spiny leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil.

Withania adpressa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
