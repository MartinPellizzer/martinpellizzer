Warmingia holopetala

Warmingia holopetala, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, flexible stems. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in muddy or shallow water environments.

Warmingia holopetala plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
