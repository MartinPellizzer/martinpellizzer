Weinmannia hirta var. antillana

Weinmannia hirta var. antillana, commonly known as the Antillean weinmannia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers, which are borne on elongated inflorescences. Its growth habit is typically compact, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

The species is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils and partial shade.
