Wedelia rugosa

Wedelia rugosa, commonly known as the roughleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically rough to the touch, with a slightly hairy texture, and the stems are often reddish in color.

Wedelia rugosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
