Weingartia nigrofuscata

Weingartia nigrofuscata, commonly known as the black-fuscous barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that is typically dark green to black in color. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are reduced to small, spiny tubercles, which help reduce water loss.

Weingartia nigrofuscata is a slow-growing, compact cactus that is well adapted to harsh desert environments.
