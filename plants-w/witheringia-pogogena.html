Witheringia pogogena

Witheringia pogogena, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Witheringia pogogena thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
