Walteriana

Walteriana, commonly known as the Walter's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Walteriana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
