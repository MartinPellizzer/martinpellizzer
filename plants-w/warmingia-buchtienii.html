Warmingia buchtienii

Warmingia buchtienii, commonly known as the Buchtien's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in eastern Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense stands along the edges of ponds, lakes, and rivers. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, consist of small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

Warmingia buchtienii is valued for its ability to stabilize shorelines and provide habitat for aquatic wildlife.
