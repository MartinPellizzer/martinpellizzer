Woodsia confusa

Woodsia confusa, commonly known as the confused wood fern, is a perennial fern native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically dark green with a leathery texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Woodsia confusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
