Weihea ceylanica

Weihea ceylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Sri Lanka and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, cone-like fruits containing seeds.

Due to its slow growth and limited natural range, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
