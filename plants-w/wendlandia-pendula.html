Wendlandia pendula

Wendlandia pendula, commonly known as the weeping gloxinia, is a striking flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Wendlandia pendula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its dramatic, cascading appearance.
