Weinmannia discolor

Weinmannia discolor, commonly known as the silverleaf weinmannia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery, fuzzy leaves that provide a striking visual contrast to its green stems and branches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems, and the plant is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Weinmannia discolor is known for its adaptability to dry conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
