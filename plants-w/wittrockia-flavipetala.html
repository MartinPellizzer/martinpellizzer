Wittrockia flavipetala

Wittrockia flavipetala, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or orange throat. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Wittrockia flavipetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable climates.
