Wigginsia polyacantha

Wigginsia polyacantha, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and birds. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic if ingested.

Wigginsia polyacantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
