Wilcoxia viperina

Wilcoxia viperina, commonly known as the desert lily, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from underground water sources.

Wilcoxia viperina plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
