Whiteochloa semitonsa

Whiteochloa semitonsa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf whiteochloa, is a species of flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
