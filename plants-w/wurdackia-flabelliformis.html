Wurdackia flabelliformis

Wurdackia flabelliformis, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically clustered in a showy, cup-like structure. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or forested areas.

Wurdackia flabelliformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
