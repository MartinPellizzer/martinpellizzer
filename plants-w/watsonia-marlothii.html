Watsonia marlothii

Watsonia marlothii, commonly known as the Marloth's torch lily, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear showy, red-orange to yellow flowers with a distinctive, torch-like shape. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Watsonia marlothii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
