Wichuraea puberula

Wichuraea puberula, commonly known as the silver leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, often blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
