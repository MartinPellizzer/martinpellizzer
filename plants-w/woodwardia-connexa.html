Woodwardia connexa

Woodwardia connexa, commonly known as the broadleaf wood fern, is a perennial fern native to the eastern United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that are divided into broad, lance-shaped segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a distinctive, upright growth habit.

Woodwardia connexa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
