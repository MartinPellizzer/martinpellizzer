Wendlandia scabra var. dependens

Wendlandia scabra var. dependens, commonly known as the hanging wendlandia, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its pendulous clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. This variety is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to add a dramatic visual impact in gardens and landscapes.
