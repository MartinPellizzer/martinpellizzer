Wendlandia formosana subsp. formosana

Wendlandia formosana subsp.

formosana, commonly known as the Formosan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Taiwan. It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple throat.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and streamside areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its beauty and adaptability to various garden conditions.
