Washingtonia filamentosa

Washingtonia filamentosa, commonly known as the desert fan palm, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feather-like, pale green leaves that give it a unique, elegant appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that eventually develop into red, fleshy fruits, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing remarkable drought tolerance and adaptability.

Its slow growth rate and long lifespan make it a valuable addition to xeriscapes and desert gardens.
