Werauhia Ovandensis

Werauhia ovandensis is a striking bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica. This plant is known for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or branches. The species is named after the botanist Luis Ovando, who contributed significantly to the study of bromeliads. Its unique appearance and ecological importance make it a fascinating subject for plant enthusiasts and researchers alike.
