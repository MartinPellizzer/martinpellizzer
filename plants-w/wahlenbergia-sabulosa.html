Wahlenbergia sabulosa

Wahlenbergia sabulosa, commonly known as the sand lily, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or coastal heathlands. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

Wahlenbergia sabulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
