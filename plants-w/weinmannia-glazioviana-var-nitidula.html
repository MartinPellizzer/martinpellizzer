Weinmannia glazioviana var. nitidula

Weinmannia glazioviana var. nitidula, commonly known as the Glaziov's weinmannia, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a distinct, waxy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
