Wahlbergella macropetala

Wahlbergella macropetala, commonly known as the large-petaled orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant features long, slender stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, supporting clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and elegant appearance.
