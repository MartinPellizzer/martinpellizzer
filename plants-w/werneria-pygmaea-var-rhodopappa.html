Werneria pygmaea var. rhodopappa

Werneria pygmaea var. rhodopappa, commonly known as the red-leafed dwarf fern, is a low-growing fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically green with a reddish tinge on the underside. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its compact size makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

The varietal name 'rhodopappa' refers to its distinctive red-veined foliage.
