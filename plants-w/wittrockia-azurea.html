Wittrockia azurea

Wittrockia azurea, commonly known as the bluebell of the south, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of striking blue to purple flowers. The plant is notable for its vibrant color and unique appearance, which make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Wittrockia azurea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical characteristics make it both visually appealing and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
