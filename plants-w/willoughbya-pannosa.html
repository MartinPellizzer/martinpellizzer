Willoughbya pannosa

Willoughbya pannosa, commonly known as the zigzagged willow.

It is a flowering plant native to South Africa, characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

Willoughbya pannosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
