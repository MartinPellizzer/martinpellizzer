Wahlenbergia glandulosa

Wahlenbergia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet petals and a compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
