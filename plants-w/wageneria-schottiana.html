Wageneria schottiana

Wageneria schottiana, commonly known as the Schott's wageneria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red bracts that attract pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
