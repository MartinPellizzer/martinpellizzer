Wedelia helianthoides subsp. papposa

Wedelia helianthoides subsp.

papposa, commonly known as the hairy wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as lawns and disturbed areas.
