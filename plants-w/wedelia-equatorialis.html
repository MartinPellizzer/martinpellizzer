Wedelia equatorialis

Wedelia equatorialis, commonly known as the yellow tridigit.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
