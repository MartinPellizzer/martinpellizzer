Wendlandiella gracilis

Wendlandiella gracilis, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in dry conditions.
