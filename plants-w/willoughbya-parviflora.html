Willoughbya parviflora

Willoughbya parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf willow or parviflora willow, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features narrow, willow-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various conditions.

Willoughbya parviflora is also valued for its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for native wildlife.
