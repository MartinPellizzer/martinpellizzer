Webera wallichii

Webera wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically found in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to anchor in rocky or loose soil.

Webera wallichii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
