Warburgia breyeri

Warburgia breyeri, commonly known as the African blackwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Eastern and Southern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood, which is highly valued for furniture and musical instruments. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species.

The tree is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional African medicine.
