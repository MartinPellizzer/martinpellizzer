Wisteria pallida

Wisteria pallida, commonly known as the pale wisteria, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of pale lavender to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of 10 to 20 oval-shaped leaflets. Its stems are woody and can grow up to 30 meters in length, often requiring support structures.

Wisteria pallida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
