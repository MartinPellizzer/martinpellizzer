Wendlandia racemosa

Wendlandia racemosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Wendlandia racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
