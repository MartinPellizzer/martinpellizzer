Witheringia macrophylla

Witheringia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Witheringia macrophylla is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
