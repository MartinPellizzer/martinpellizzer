Weinmannia sessilifolia

Weinmannia sessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved weinmannia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Weinmanniaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern California and northern Baja California, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. Its flowers are small and typically pink or white, arranged in dense inflorescences.

Weinmannia sessilifolia is well adapted to arid and coastal environments, showcasing resilience to salt spray and strong winds.
