Webera grandifolia

Webera grandifolia, commonly known as the giant fern, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, feathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance.

Webera grandifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
