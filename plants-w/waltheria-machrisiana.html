Waltheria machrisiana

Waltheria machrisiana, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant is typically herbaceous, growing as an annual or perennial depending on the climate. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners, though it can become invasive in some areas.
