Wrightia tinctoria subsp. rothii

Wrightia tinctoria subsp.

rothii, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Meliaceae. It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia.

The wood of this species is highly valued for its rich color and durability, making it a significant resource in traditional craftsmanship.
