Wurmbea campanulata var. unicolor

Wurmbea campanulata var. unicolor, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, bell-shaped white flowers during the summer months. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
