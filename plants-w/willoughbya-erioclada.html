Willoughbya erioclada

Willoughbya erioclada, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody pods containing flat, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and riverine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a notable species in the region's flora.
