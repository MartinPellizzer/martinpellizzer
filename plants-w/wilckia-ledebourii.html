Wilckia ledebourii

Wilckia ledebourii, commonly known as the desert star or desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive appearance, featuring a short, thick stem and a cluster of yellow, star-shaped flowers. The plant has small, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.

Wilckia ledebourii is adapted to thrive in sandy soils and can survive prolonged periods of drought.
