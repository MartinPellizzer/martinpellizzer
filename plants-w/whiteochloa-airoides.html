Whiteochloa airoides

Whiteochloa airoides, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens and as a landscape plant.
