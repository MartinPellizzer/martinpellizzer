Wigandia leucocephala

Wigandia leucocephala, commonly known as the white-headed spider flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, spider-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Wigandia leucocephala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
