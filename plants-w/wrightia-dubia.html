Wrightia dubia

Wrightia dubia, commonly known as the slender wrightia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Wrightiaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with smooth, pale green bark and opposite, ovate leaves. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Wrightia dubia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
