Willoughbya premnifolia

Willoughbya premnifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to northeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. The plant has a single trunk with smooth, grey bark and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface.

Willoughbya premnifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to warm, humid climates.
