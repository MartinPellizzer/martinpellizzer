Wedelia tiliifolia

Wedelia tiliifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ground cover in ornamental landscaping.

The plant is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, which can make it invasive in certain environments.
