Walsura antila

Walsura antila, commonly known as the red-leafed flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red leaves, which contrast vividly with its yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its woody structure and rapid growth make it suitable for ornamental planting in warm climates.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
