Wellingtonia

Wellingtonia, commonly known as the giant sequoia, is a towering conifer native to the western United States.

It is one of the tallest and most massive tree species in the world, with some specimens reaching heights of over 300 feet. The tree has needle-like leaves and produces cones that contain its seeds. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing protection against fire and disease.

The Wellingtonia plays a crucial role in its ecosystem, offering habitat and contributing to the forest's biodiversity.
