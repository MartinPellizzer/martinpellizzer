Willoughbya nummularia

Willoughbya nummularia, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, round leaves that are typically silver or gray in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle ornamental touch. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
