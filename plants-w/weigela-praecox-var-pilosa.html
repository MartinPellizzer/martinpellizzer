Weigela praecox var. pilosa

Weigela praecox var. pilosa, commonly known as the woolly weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from pink to red. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, woolly texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for garden landscapes due to its ornamental value.

The flowers appear in early spring, making it a popular choice for early season color in gardens.
