Wisteria chinensis

Wisteria chinensis, commonly known as Chinese wisteria, is a climbing vine native to China.

It produces long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, blue-lavender flowers in late spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of 12 to 20 oval-shaped leaflets. Its stems are woody and can grow up to 30 meters in length.

Wisteria chinensis is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
