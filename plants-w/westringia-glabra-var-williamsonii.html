Westringia glabra var. williamsonii

Westringia glabra var. williamsonii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in small, dark green, narrow leaves that are densely packed. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers during late winter to spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for landscaping in Mediterranean climates.
