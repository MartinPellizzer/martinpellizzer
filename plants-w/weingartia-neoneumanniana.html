Weingartia neoneumanniana

Weingartia neoneumanniana, commonly known as the Neumann's barrel cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that often develops a swollen, barrel-like shape as it matures. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines are typically white to gray in color and are relatively short compared to other cacti in the genus.

Weingartia neoneumanniana is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
