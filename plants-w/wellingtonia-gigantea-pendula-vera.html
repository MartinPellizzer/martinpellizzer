Wellingtonia gigantea pendula-vera

Wellingtonia gigantea pendula-vera, commonly known as the weeping giant of the Andes, is a striking evergreen tree native to the humid mountain forests of South America.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance, giving it a dramatic visual presence. The tree has large, dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the branches. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged texture that adds to its ornamental appeal.

While it is a slow-growing species, it can reach impressive heights and is often cultivated for its unique form and aesthetic value in botanical gardens.
