Weinmannia boiviniana

Weinmannia boiviniana, commonly known as the Boivin's weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
