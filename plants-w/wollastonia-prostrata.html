Wollastonia prostrata

Wollastonia prostrata, commonly known as the coast wattle, is a low-growing shrub native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features delicate, feathery yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It is well adapted to sandy soils and salt-laden environments, making it resilient to coastal conditions.

Wollastonia prostrata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in coastal landscaping.
