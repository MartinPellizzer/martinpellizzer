Wissadula periplocifolia var. hernandioides

Wissadula periplocifolia var. hernandioides, commonly known as the Hawaiian false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in coastal and lowland areas. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. Its flowers are showy, with five petals and a distinctive yellow center, blooming in clusters.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various native insects and birds.
