Walafrida goetzei var. brevipila

Walafrida goetzei var. brevipila, commonly known as the short-stemmed walafrida, is a rare and specialized plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with short, sturdy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the early morning, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphological traits and its role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
