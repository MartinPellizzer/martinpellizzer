Whiteheadia bifolia

Whiteheadia bifolia, commonly known as the fairy lantern, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy soils. It produces small, paired leaves and blooms during the spring season.

Whiteheadia bifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of hope and renewal in some cultures.
