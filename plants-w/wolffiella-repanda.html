Wolffiella repanda

Wolffiella repanda, commonly known as the broadleaf wolfwillow, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and alternate, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, round berries.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
