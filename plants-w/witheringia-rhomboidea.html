Witheringia rhomboidea

Witheringia rhomboidea, commonly known as the diamondleaf mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rhomboid-shaped leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. Witheringia rhomboidea is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
