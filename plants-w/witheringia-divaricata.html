Witheringia divaricata

Witheringia divaricata, commonly known as the purple-flowered false indigo, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Witheringia divaricata is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
