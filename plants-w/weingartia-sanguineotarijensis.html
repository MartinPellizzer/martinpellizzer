Weingartia sanguineotarijensis

Weingartia sanguineotarijensis, commonly known as the blood-red barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem, which is typically green with reddish or pinkish hues, especially when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
