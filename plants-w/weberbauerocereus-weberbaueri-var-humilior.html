Weberbauerocereus weberbaueri var. humilior

Weberbauerocereus weberbaueri var. humilior, commonly known as the lowland candelabra cactus, is a succulent plant native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its branched, cylindrical stems that are often covered in small, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and collections.
