Wikstroemia glandulosa

Wikstroemia glandulosa, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, often in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and usually covered in small, glandular hairs.

The species is notable for its ecological importance and is often used in traditional Hawaiian medicine.
