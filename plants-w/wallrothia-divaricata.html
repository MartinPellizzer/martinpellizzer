Wallrothia divaricata

Wallrothia divaricata, commonly known as the blue ghost plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique color and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant among horticulturists.
