Weinmannia macrophylla

Weinmannia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved weinmannia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, red or orange inflorescences that attract birds and insects, aiding in pollination. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
