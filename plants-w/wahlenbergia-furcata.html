Wahlenbergia furcata

Wahlenbergia furcata, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Wahlenbergia furcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
