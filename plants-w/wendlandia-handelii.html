Wendlandia handelii

Wendlandia handelii, commonly known as the velvet tunic or Andean bellflower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to the Andes.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that resemble bells, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features velvety, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Wendlandia handelii is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
