Wulfenia bullii

Wulfenia bullii, commonly known as the bull's-wolfberry, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or purple when mature. Wulfenia bullii is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and deep root systems.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
