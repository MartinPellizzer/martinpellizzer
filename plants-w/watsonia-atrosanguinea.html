Watsonia atrosanguinea

Watsonia atrosanguinea, commonly known as the black blood Watsonia, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces clusters of deep red to purple flowers, often with a dark, almost black hue, giving it its common name. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette at the base.

Watsonia atrosanguinea is well-suited for rock gardens and requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
