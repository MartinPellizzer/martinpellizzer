Wedelia floribunda

Wedelia floribunda, commonly known as the silverleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, showy flowers are usually white or pink and appear in clusters during the warm seasons.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
