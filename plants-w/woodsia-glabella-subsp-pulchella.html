Woodsia glabella subsp. pulchella

Woodsia glabella subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the秀丽木蕨, is a fern species native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bright green with a waxy surface.

The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its spores are produced in small, round to oval sori on the underside of the fronds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
