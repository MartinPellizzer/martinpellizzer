Wendlandia nobilis

Wendlandia nobilis, commonly known as the noble wendlandia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped, hairy leaves. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often in shades of pink, red, or white, and are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds.

Wendlandia nobilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with full sun.
