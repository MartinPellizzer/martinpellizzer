Wedelia induta

Wedelia induta, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, blue-violet flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to suppress weeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Wedelia induta is also noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
