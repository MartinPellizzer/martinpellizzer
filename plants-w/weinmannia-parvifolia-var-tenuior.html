Weinmannia parvifolia var. tenuior

Weinmannia parvifolia var. tenuior, commonly known as the slenderleaf weinmannia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and northern Baja California.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically less than 2 inches long, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This variety is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
