Wahlbergella soczaviana

Wahlbergella soczaviana, commonly known as the Soczaviana orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to the mountainous regions of Ethiopia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a short, hairy stem and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in the spring. The plant has a complex root system adapted to its rocky and semi-arid habitat. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow with intricate patterns, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
