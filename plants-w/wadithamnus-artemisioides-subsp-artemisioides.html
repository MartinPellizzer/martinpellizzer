Wadithamnus artemisioides subsp. artemisioides

Wadithamnus artemisioides subsp.

artemisioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf wadithamnus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
