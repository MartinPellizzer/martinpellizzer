Watsonia borbonica

Watsonia borbonica, commonly known as the Natal grassblume, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces large, clustered inflorescences that bloom in summer.

Watsonia borbonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
