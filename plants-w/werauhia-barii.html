Werauhia barii

Werauhia barii, commonly known as the Bari Werauhia, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a common feature among bromeliads. Its foliage is typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it produces striking inflorescences that can range from yellow to orange in color. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Werauhia barii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
