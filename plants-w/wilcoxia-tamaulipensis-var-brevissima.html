Wilcoxia tamaulipensis var. brevissima

Wilcoxia tamaulipensis var. brevissima, commonly known as the Tamaulipan sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, helping to reduce water loss.

This variant is distinguished by its shorter stature compared to other varieties of Wilcoxia tamaulipensis.
