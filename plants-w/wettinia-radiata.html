Wettinia radiata

Wettinia radiata, commonly known as the radiate wettinia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae, native to Mexico.

It is a tall, slender palm characterized by its smooth, grayish trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. Wettinia radiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
