Witheringia fournieri

Witheringia fournieri, commonly known as the golden star ginger, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with a distinctive star-like shape. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Witheringia fournieri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
