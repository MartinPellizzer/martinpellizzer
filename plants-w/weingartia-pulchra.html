Weingartia pulchra

Weingartia pulchra, commonly known as the "Pearly Weingartia," is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and pale green to bluish-gray stems. The plant features arecent, spiny areoles that bear short, white bristles and can produce small, yellow flowers under favorable conditions. Weingartia pulchra is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water, making it a popular choice for succulent collections.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a desirable ornamental plant.
