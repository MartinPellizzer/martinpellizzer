Wisteria brachybotrys f. alba

Wisteria brachybotrys f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered wisteria, is a cultivar of the wisteria plant characterized by its pale to white blooms.

It is a climbing vine native to China and is part of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces long, pendulous clusters of flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three heart-shaped leaflets.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral displays.
