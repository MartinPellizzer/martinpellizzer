Waltheria alamosana

Waltheria alamosana, commonly known as Alamosana blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

Waltheria alamosana is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
