Westia grandiflora

Westia grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora westia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Westia grandiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beautiful blooms.
