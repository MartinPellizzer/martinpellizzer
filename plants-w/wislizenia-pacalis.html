Wislizenia pacalis

Wislizenia pacalis, commonly known as goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in wetlands, ditches, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Wislizenia pacalis is often used in traditional medicine and is also a host plant for certain butterfly species.
