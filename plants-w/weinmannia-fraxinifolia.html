Weinmannia fraxinifolia

Weinmannia fraxinifolia, commonly known as the goldenleaf tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Weinmanniaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern California and northern Baja California, where it thrives in sandy soils and mild coastal climates. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by elongated, winged fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
