Willughbeia drupacea

Willughbeia drupacea, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture. Willughbeia drupacea produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, red berries.

Due to overharvesting, it is now considered a threatened species in its native range.
