Wallisia lindenii

Wallisia lindenii, commonly known as the Lindenberg's wallisia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is found in coastal regions. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, woody fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
