Wrightia multiflora

Wrightia multiflora, commonly known as the Chinese winged bean, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive wing-like appearance, and its flowers are typically yellow or white. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Wrightia multiflora is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial plant in agricultural systems.
