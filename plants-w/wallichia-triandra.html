Wallichia triandra

Wallichia triandra, commonly known as the African star orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in forested areas and has a slender, upright stem with few leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive central boss of petals.

Wallichia triandra is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
