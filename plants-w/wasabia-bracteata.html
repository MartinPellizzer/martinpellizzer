Wasabia bracteata

Wasabia bracteata, commonly known as Japanese wasabi, is a perennial plant native to the cool, mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the mustard family (Brassicaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, green rhizome that is used as a condiment. The plant typically grows in shady, moist environments along streams and rivers. Its leaves are elongated and serrated, and it produces small white flowers in early summer.

The plant is valued for its pungent flavor and is often cultivated for culinary use.
