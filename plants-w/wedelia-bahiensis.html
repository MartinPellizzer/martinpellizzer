Wedelia bahiensis

Wedelia bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahamian wedelia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Wedelia bahiensis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in sunny, well-drained conditions.
