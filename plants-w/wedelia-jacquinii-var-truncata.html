Wedelia jacquinii var. truncata

Wedelia jacquinii var. truncata, commonly known as the broadleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in compact inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for warm climates.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for garden use.
