Wibelia chaerophylloides

Wibelia chaerophylloides, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking yellow ray florets and dark purple disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are alternate, linear to lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Wibelia chaerophylloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
