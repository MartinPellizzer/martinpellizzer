Wahlbergella uralensis

Wahlbergella uralensis, commonly known as the Ural bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant features showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
