Wikstroemia fruticosa var. obovata

Wikstroemia fruticosa var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed sand verbena, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It features dense, compact growth with narrow, obovate leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and low-maintenance plant for coastal gardens.
