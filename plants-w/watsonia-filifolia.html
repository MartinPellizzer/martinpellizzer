Watsonia filifolia

Watsonia filifolia, commonly known as the filifoliate Watsonia, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of vibrant, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Watsonia filifolia is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
