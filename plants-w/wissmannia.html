Wissmannia

Wissmannia, commonly known as the desert broom, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a splash of color to the desert landscape. Wissmannia is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and can withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and hardy species well-suited to desert ecosystems.
