Watsonia pondoensis

Watsonia pondoensis, commonly known as the Pondo torch lily, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces dense clusters of tubular flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Watsonia pondoensis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
