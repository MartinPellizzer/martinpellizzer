Wardaster

Wardaster, commonly known as the "devil's weed," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves. Wardaster is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
