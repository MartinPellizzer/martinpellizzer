Witheringia correana

Witheringia correana, commonly known as the Korean witheringia, is a flowering plant native to Korea and parts of eastern Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually pale green or white, with five petals.

Witheringia correana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and unique floral structure.
