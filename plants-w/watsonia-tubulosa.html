Watsonia tubulosa

Watsonia tubulosa, commonly known as the Natal grassblume, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant, often red or orange-colored blooms that appear in late summer to early autumn. Watsonia tubulosa is well adapted to sunny, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
