Witheringia umbellata

Witheringia umbellata, commonly known as the fragrant bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and mountainous areas. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.
