Wendlandia kotschyi

Wendlandia kotschyi, commonly known as the Bolivian bellflower, is a striking flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blooms. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a dark central disc. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Wendlandia kotschyi is also noted for its ability to thrive in high-altitude conditions.
