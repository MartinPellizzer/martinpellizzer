Watsonia archbelliae

Watsonia archbelliae, commonly known as the Natal grassblume, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-like leaves and vibrant, showy inflorescences that display a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and thrives in full sun, requiring well-drained soil. Watsonia archbelliae is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.

It is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
