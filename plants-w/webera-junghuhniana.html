Webera junghuhniana

Webera junghuhniana, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is known for its ability to thrive in semi-shade to full sun conditions. Its flowers emit a strong scent to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Webera junghuhniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
