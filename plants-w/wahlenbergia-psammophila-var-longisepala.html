Wahlenbergia psammophila var. longisepala

Wahlenbergia psammophila var. longisepala, commonly known as the sand lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in a spike-like cluster. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience.
