Wurmbea dioica subsp. alba

Wurmbea dioica subsp.

alba, commonly known as the white star lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa. It features striking white flowers with a star-like shape, which bloom in late summer.

The plant has strap-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-brown berries following flowering. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or forested areas.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale flowers compared to the more commonly seen purple-flowered subspecies.
