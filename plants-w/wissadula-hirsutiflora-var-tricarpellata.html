Wissadula hirsutiflora var. tricarpellata

Wissadula hirsutiflora var. tricarpellata, commonly known as the hairy-flowered wissadula, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and distinctive floral structures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often tinged with reddish or purple hues. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
