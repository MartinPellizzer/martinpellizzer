Withania sinensis

Withania sinensis, commonly known as Indian winter cherry or ashwagandha, is a shrub native to India and northern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and red, berry-like fruits. The plant is notable for its adaptogenic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries. Its leaves and roots contain bioactive compounds such as withanolides, which contribute to its medicinal value.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it a hardy species well-suited to challenging environments.
