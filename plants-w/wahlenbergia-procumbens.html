Wahlenbergia procumbens

Wahlenbergia procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate bluebell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing species with slender stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

Wahlenbergia procumbens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
