Wissadula grandifolia

Wissadula grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its rhizomes are the primary part used, containing a starchy substance similar to cassava.

Wissadula grandifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
