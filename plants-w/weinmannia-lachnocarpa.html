Weinmannia lachnocarpa

Weinmannia lachnocarpa, commonly known as the stickyleaf weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous bark and leathery, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a sticky, resinous substance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by woody, spiky fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in coastal climates with mild, wet winters and warm, dry summers.

Weinmannia lachnocarpa is adapted to sandy or rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
