Woodfordia fruticosa

Woodfordia fruticosa, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright orange-red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that turn yellow before falling off in the dry season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Woodfordia fruticosa is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
