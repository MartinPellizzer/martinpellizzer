Wahlenbergia albomarginata subsp. decora

Wahlenbergia albomarginata subsp.

decora, commonly known as the Decorated Bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, blue to violet flowers with white margins.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as heathlands and coastal areas. Its flowers are arranged in loose racemes, and it is well adapted to dry conditions.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
