Wikstroemia rosea

Wikstroemia rosea, commonly known as the rosewood or pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its resilience and is often used in landscaping due to its ability to withstand harsh coastal conditions.
