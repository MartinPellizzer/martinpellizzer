Wedelia keatingii

Wedelia keatingii, commonly known as the Australian bluebells, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
