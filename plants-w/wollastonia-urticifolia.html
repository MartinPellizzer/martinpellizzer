Wollastonia urticifolia

Wollastonia urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, often stinging leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark that becomes more pronounced with age. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and typically have a serrated margin.

Wollastonia urticifolia is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often found in coastal heathlands and rocky areas.
