Wisteria brachybotrys f. albiflora

Wisteria brachybotrys f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered wisteria, is a cultivar of the wisteria plant characterized by its pale or white flowers.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to China. The plant is a vigorous climbing vine with pinnately compound leaves and long, pendulous racemes of flowers. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
