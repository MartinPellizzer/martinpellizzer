Walidda antidysenterica

Walidda antidysenterica, commonly known as the African balm, is a medicinal plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and yellow-orange flowers. The plant is valued for its leaves, which are used in traditional medicine to treat digestive ailments and inflammation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its chemical constituents include essential oils, flavonoids, and tannins, contributing to its therapeutic properties.
