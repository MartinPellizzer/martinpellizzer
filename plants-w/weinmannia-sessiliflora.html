Weinmannia sessiliflora

Weinmannia sessiliflora, commonly known as the Mexican weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Weinmanniaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often leathery and have a distinctive, waxy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by small, rounded fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Weinmannia sessiliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
