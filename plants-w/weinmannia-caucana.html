Weinmannia caucana

Weinmannia caucana, commonly known as the Andean velvetleaf, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and distinctive, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions, typically found in cloud forests.

Weinmannia caucana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
