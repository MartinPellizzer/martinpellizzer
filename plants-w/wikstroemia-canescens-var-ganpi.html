Wikstroemia canescens var. ganpi

Wikstroemia canescens var. ganpi, commonly known as the white sand dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal or inland dune environments. It has a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age, and its flowers bloom in the spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing sandy soils.
