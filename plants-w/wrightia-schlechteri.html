Wrightia schlechteri

Wrightia schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's wrightia, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Wrightiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

Wrightia schlechteri is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in traditional craftsmanship.
