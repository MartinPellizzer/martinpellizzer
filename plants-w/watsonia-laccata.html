Watsonia laccata

Watsonia laccata, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and produces spikes of bright red, orange, or yellow tubular flowers. The plant is part of the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fiery blooms that appear in late summer to early autumn. Watsonia laccata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
