Wahlenbergia procumbens var. foliosa

Wahlenbergia procumbens var. foliosa, commonly known as the coastal bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing herb with slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, arranged in a basal rosette. This variety is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and is often found in sandy or rocky coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal conditions.
