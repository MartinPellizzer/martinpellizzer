Willoughbya smilacina

Willoughbya smilacina, commonly known as the willoughbya, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can twine around supports. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in spikes, followed by orange to red berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Willoughbya smilacina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
