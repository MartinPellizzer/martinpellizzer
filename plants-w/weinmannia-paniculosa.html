Weinmannia paniculosa

Weinmannia paniculosa, commonly known as the silverleaf weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Weinmanniaceae and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and distinctive, compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which are often borne on the previous year's growth. Its bark is typically smooth and may exfoliate, revealing a lighter inner layer.

Weinmannia paniculosa is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture.
