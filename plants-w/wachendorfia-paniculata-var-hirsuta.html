Wachendorfia paniculata var. hirsuta

Wachendorfia paniculata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy wachendorfia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, paniculate inflorescence. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink to red flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is well-suited to Mediterranean climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.
