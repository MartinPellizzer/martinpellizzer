Weinmannia hirta f. guadalupensis

Weinmannia hirta f. guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupe hirta, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with prominent stamens, which are typical of the Weinmanniaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with salt spray.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
