Weihea ovalifolia

Weihea ovalifolia, commonly known as the Tahitian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, which bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous seeds, and it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
