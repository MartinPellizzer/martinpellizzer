Wedelia helianthoides

Wedelia helianthoides, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing perennial with spreading stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
