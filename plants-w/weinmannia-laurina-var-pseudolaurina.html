Weinmannia laurina var. pseudolaurina

Weinmannia laurina var. pseudolaurina, commonly known as the Mexican weinmannia, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in warm regions.
