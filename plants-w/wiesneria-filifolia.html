Wiesneria filifolia

Wiesneria filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf wiesneria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
