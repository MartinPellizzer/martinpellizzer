Weihea myriocarpa subsp. microcarpa

Weihea myriocarpa subsp.

microcarpa, commonly known as the Tahitian breadfruit, is a small evergreen tree native to the Society Islands of French Polynesia. It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, breadfruit-like fruits that are rich in carbohydrates. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its historical and cultural significance in Polynesian cuisine and traditional practices.
