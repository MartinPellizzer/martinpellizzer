Wisteria brevidentata

Wisteria brevidentata, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of fragrant, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with short, blunt leaflets, and its stems are covered in tendrils that help it climb. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks, though it can become invasive if not managed properly.
