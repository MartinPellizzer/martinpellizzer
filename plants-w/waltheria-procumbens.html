Waltheria procumbens

Waltheria procumbens, commonly known as silverleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features hairy, branched stems that grow close to the ground, often forming a mat-like structure. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rough texture and a distinctive silvery-white underside.

Waltheria procumbens is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.
