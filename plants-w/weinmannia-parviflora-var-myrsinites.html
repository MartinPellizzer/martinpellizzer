Weinmannia parviflora var. myrsinites

Weinmannia parviflora var. myrsinites, commonly known as the Myrsinites Weinmannia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically leathery and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
