Washingtonia californica

Washingtonia californica, commonly known as the California fan palm, is a striking evergreen tree native to the southwestern United States.

It features a tall, slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and a crown of large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates. Its roots are shallow and fibrous, allowing it to absorb water efficiently from the surface.

The California fan palm is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and adaptability.
