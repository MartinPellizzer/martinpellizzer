Weingartia crispata var. hertusii

Weingartia crispata var. hertusii, commonly known as the Hertusii Crested Cactus, is a rare and unique variant of the Weingartia genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, crest-like growth pattern that sets it apart from typical Weingartia species. The plant features pale green to bluish-gray stems covered in white to pale blue spines, giving it a striking appearance. It is native to arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
