Wallia nigra

Wallia nigra, commonly known as black wallia, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, foliage and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and the flowers are usually a deep purple or violet color.

Wallia nigra is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
