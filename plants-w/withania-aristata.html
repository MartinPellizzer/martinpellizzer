Withania aristata

Withania aristata, commonly known as the Indian winter cherry, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky habitats.

Withania aristata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
