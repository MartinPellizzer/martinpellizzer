Wissadula pavonii

Wissadula pavonii, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are usually pink or purple, with five petals and a prominent central column. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas.

Wissadula pavonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
