Walafrida pusilla

Walafrida pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with compact, hairy leaves and clustered yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates.

Walafrida pusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
