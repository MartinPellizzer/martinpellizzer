Wirtgenia multiflora

Wirtgenia multiflora, commonly known as the multibract bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss in arid environments. Wirtgenia multiflora thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, though it remains relatively rare in horticultural trade.
