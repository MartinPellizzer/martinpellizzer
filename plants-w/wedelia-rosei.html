Wedelia rosei

Wedelia rosei, commonly known as the creeping wedgeflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
