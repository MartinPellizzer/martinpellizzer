Wurmbea tenella

Wurmbea tenella, commonly known as the African lily, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has a short, stout stem and broad, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Wurmbea tenella is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some regions.
