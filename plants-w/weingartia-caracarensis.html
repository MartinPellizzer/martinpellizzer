Weingartia caracarensis

Weingartia caracarensis, commonly known as the Caracarensis Cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the Cactaceae family.

It is native to Argentina and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Weingartia caracarensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
