Wahlenbergia prostrata

Wahlenbergia prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate bluebell, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Wahlenbergia prostrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
