Wigandia kunthii var. intermedia

Wigandia kunthii var. intermedia, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, woody pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and riverbanks.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
