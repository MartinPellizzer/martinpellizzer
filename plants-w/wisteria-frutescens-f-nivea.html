Wisteria frutescens f. nivea

Wisteria frutescens f. nivea, commonly known as the White Wisteria, is a cultivar of the native American wisteria species.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a deciduous vine that can grow quite long, often reaching lengths of 15 to 20 feet. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and as a climbing plant.
