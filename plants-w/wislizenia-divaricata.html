Wislizenia divaricata

Wislizenia divaricata, commonly known as greenbriar, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Wislizenia divaricata is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and open woods, and is known for its rapid growth and invasive tendencies.
