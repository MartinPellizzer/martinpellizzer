Wendlandia wallichii var. pedicellata

Wendlandia wallichii var. pedicellata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
