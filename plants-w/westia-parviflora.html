Westia parviflora

Westia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered westia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are evergreen throughout the year.

Westia parviflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a specimen plant.
