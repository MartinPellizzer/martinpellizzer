Widdringtonia whytei

Widdringtonia whytei, commonly known as the yellowwood tree, is a species of evergreen conifer native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Widdringtonia whytei is valued for its durable, heavy timber and is often used in construction and furniture-making.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a humid climate to flourish.
