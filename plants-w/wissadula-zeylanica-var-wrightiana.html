Wissadula zeylanica var. wrightiana

Wissadula zeylanica var. wrightiana, commonly known as the Wright's waxflower, is a flowering plant native to southern India and Sri Lanka.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ornamental value lies in its compact form and long-lasting blooms, which add a delicate touch to gardens.
