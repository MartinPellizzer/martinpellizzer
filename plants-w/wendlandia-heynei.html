Wendlandia heynei

Wendlandia heynei, commonly known as the pink fairy fan, is a striking flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-purple bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in USDA hardiness zones 9-11 and prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Wendlandia heynei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
