Woodsiopsis appalachiana

Woodsiopsis appalachiana, commonly known as Appalachian woodsiopsis, is a small, evergreen fern native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and rocky outcrops. The plant has a short, upright rhizome with numerous clustered fronds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with a delicate, fern-like appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticultural settings.
