Wellingtonia gigantea var. pendula

Wellingtonia gigantea var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping giant of the Andes, is a striking evergreen conifer with a distinctive cascading form.

It features long, drooping branches that create a lush, flowing appearance, making it a popular ornamental tree. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a dense canopy. It thrives in cool, moist climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variant of the Wellingtonia gigantea is native to the Andes mountains and is known for its slow growth and longevity.
