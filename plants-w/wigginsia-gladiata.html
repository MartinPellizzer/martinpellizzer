Wigginsia gladiata

Wigginsia gladiata, commonly known as the gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sword-like leaves and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces vibrant, colorful flowers in shades of red, yellow, orange, and white, which bloom in summer. Wigginsia gladiata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
