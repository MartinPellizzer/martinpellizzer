Wahlenbergia paniculata var. rudis

Wahlenbergia paniculata var. rudis, commonly known as the coastal wallaby pea, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in coastal environments, often found on sandy soils and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it is well adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
