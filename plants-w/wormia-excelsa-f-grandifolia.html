Wormia excelsa f. grandifolia

Wormia excelsa f. grandifolia, commonly known as the giant wormwood, is a unique plant species characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and fibrous, woody structure.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient plant in arid and semi-arid regions.
