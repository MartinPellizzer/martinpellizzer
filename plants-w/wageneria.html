Wageneria

Wageneria, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plants typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and are often used in ornamental gardening due to their vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.

Wageneria species are well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
