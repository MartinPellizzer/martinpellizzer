Wisteria

Wisteria, commonly known as the flowering vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can reach lengths of up to 30 meters. The plant produces cascading clusters of fragrant, violet-blue or white flowers in late spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 10 to 20 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Wisteria is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a decorative element in landscapes.
