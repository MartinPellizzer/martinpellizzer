Wisteria floribunda

Wisteria floribunda, commonly known as Japanese wisteria, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with 12 to 20 oval-shaped leaflets arranged in pairs. Its stems are capable of twisting around supports and can grow up to 30 meters in length.

Wisteria floribunda is valued for its ornamental beauty but can be invasive in certain regions.
