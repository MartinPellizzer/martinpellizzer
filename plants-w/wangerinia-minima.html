Wangerinia minima

Wangerinia minima, commonly known as the dwarf wangerinia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features delicate, opposite leaves that are usually less than 2 inches in length. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and often have a subtle fragrance.

Wangerinia minima is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
