Wageneria dichotoma

Wageneria dichotoma, commonly known as the dichotomous grass, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems that branch dichotomously, forming a dense, low-growing mat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of hair to reduce water loss.

Wageneria dichotoma is well adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
