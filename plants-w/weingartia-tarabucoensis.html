Weingartia tarabucoensis

Weingartia tarabucoensis, commonly known as the Tarabuco cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, spiny stems that form a low-growing shrub. The plant produces small, colorful flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and collections.
