Wrightia tokiae

Wrightia tokiae, commonly known as the Tokai wrightia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Wrightiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a small shrub or tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually clustered in panicles and bloom during the summer months.

Wrightia tokiae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
