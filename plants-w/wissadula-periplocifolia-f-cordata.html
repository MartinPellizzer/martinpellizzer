Wissadula periplocifolia f. cordata

Wissadula periplocifolia f. cordata, commonly known as the heart-leaf wissadula, is a unique variant of the Wissadula genus native to specific regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive cordate, or heart-shaped, leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stems, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, which bloom in the early spring.

While not widely cultivated, it is valued in botanical gardens for its unusual leaf morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
