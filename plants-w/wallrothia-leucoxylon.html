Wallrothia leucoxylon

Wallrothia leucoxylon, commonly known as the silverwood tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by legume-like pods containing seeds. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.

The silverwood tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
