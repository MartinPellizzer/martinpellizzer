Wikstroemia fruticosa var. typica

Wikstroemia fruticosa var. typica, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus or ʻōhiʻa lehua, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, opposite leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland habitats.

This species plays an important ecological role in Hawaiian forests and is culturally significant to Native Hawaiian traditions.
