Wibelia divaricata

Wibelia divaricata, commonly known as the prairie smoke, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery purple flower heads that resemble smoke rising from the plant. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and dry meadows. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Wibelia divaricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
