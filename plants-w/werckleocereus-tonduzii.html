Werckleocereus tonduzii

Werckleocereus tonduzii, commonly known as the Tonduzii night-blooming cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and is typically spineless, making it visually striking. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its succulent structure allows it to store water, adapting well to arid environments.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique flowering behavior.
