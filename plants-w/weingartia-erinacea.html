Weingartia erinacea

Weingartia erinacea, commonly known as the cushion cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit, forming a compact, cushion-like structure. The plant has short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

Weingartia erinacea is well-suited for arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
