Weingartia fidaiana subsp. amerhauseri

Weingartia fidaiana subsp.

amerhauseri, commonly known as the Amerhauser's Pincushion Cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to southern Africa. It features a globular stem with a dense covering of white, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in summer. It is well-suited for arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for succulent collections.
