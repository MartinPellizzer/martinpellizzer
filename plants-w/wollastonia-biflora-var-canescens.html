Wollastonia biflora var. canescens

Wollastonia biflora var. canescens, commonly known as the grey wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are narrow and often covered with a fine, silvery-grey pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is part of the Proteaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
