Weingartia candiae

Weingartia candiae, commonly known as the Candy Cane Cactus, is a compact, slow-growing species native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a distinctive white, waxy coating that gives it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to survive in dry conditions.

Weingartia candiae is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of care.
