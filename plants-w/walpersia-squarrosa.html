Walpersia squarrosa

Walpersia squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. Its leaves are grayish-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in the spring and early summer.
