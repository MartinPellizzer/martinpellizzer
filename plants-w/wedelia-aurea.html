Wedelia aurea

Wedelia aurea, commonly known as golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats of foliage, often used in landscaping for erosion control. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to fall. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Wedelia aurea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.
