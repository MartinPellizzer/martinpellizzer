Wissadula periplocifolia f. parvifolia

Wissadula periplocifolia f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved velvetleaf, is a compact shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
