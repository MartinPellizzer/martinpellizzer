Wurmbea pusilla

Wurmbea pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf African lily, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Wurmbea pusilla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to bloom reliably in suitable conditions.
