Weinmannia pubescens var. arcabucoana

Weinmannia pubescens var. arcabucoana, commonly known as the Arcabuco weinmannia, is a shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular pubescence that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins and a leathery texture. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pink, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

This species is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.
