Winchia atroviridis

Winchia atroviridis, commonly known as the black-veined fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves with prominent black veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the soil while absorbing nutrients.

Winchia atroviridis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
