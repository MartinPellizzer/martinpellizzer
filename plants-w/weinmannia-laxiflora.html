Weinmannia laxiflora

Weinmannia laxiflora, commonly known as the silverleaf weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that provide a unique visual texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and often have a waxy coating.

Weinmannia laxiflora is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
