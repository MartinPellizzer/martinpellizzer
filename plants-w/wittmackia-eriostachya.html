Wittmackia eriostachya

Wittmackia eriostachya, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
