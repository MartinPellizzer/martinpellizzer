Wilckia africana var. laxa

Wilckia africana var. laxa, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a striking shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets that are often pubescent and have a distinctive reddish or purplish hue. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a red or purple calyx, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or rocky outcrops.

The species is valued for its vibrant floral display and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
