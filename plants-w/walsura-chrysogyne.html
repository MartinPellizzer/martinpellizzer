Walsura chrysogyne

Walsura chrysogyne, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and light-colored, with a distinct aromatic scent.
