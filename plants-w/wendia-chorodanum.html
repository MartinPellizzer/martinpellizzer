Wendia chorodanum

Wendia chorodanum, commonly known as the golden wendia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Wendia chorodanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
