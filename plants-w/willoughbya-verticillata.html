Willoughbya verticillata

Willoughbya verticillata, commonly known as the ghost gum, is a unique plant native to Australia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a smooth, pale grey trunk and distinctive, spirally arranged leaves. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often shed in a whorl pattern, giving the tree its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and heat.
