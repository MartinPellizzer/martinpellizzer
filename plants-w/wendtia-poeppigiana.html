Wendtia poeppigiana

Wendtia poeppigiana, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a succulent plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that branch into segments, each ending in a pair of pointed leaves. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the holiday season, typically between December and February. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a favorite among plant enthusiasts.
