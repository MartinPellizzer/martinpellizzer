Wrightia viridiflora

Wrightia viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered wrightia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Wrightiaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Wrightia viridiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral characteristics.
