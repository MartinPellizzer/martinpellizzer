Wormskioldia diversifolia

Wormskioldia diversifolia, commonly known as the variable-leaved wormskioldia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are variable in shape and size, ranging from elliptical to lanceolate, contributing to its common name.

Wormskioldia diversifolia is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
