Wrightia laevis subsp. novoguineensis

Wrightia laevis subsp.

novoguineensis, commonly known as the Guinean Wrightia, is a tropical tree native to western Africa. It belongs to the family Wrightiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with entire margins.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is used in local crafts and construction.
