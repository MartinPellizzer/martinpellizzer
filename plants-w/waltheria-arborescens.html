Waltheria arborescens

Waltheria arborescens, commonly known as the tree mallows, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its shrub-like or tree-like growth habit, with woody stems and broad, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native gardens and ecological landscapes.
