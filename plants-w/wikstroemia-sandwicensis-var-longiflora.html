Wikstroemia sandwicensis var. longiflora

Wikstroemia sandwicensis var. longiflora, commonly known as the Kona hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that resemble those of hibiscus. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a woody stem with smooth, glossy leaves. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of Hawaii's native flora.
