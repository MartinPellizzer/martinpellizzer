Wedelia paniculata

Wedelia paniculata, commonly known as the silverleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in tropical environments.
