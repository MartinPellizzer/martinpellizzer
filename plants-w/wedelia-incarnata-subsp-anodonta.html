Wedelia incarnata subsp. anodonta

Wedelia incarnata subsp.

anodonta, commonly known as the pink wedelia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.

The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a branching stem structure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
