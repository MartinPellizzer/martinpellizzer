Weniomeles atropurpurea

Weniomeles atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-fruited honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by edible, purplish berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and seasonal fruit display.
