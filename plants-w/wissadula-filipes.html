Wissadula filipes

Wissadula filipes, commonly known as the slender leaf ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, upright stems that are often tinged with red, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the warmer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Wissadula filipes is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
