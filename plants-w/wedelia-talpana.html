Wedelia talpana

Wedelia talpana, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
