Wedelia gonzaleziarum

Wedelia gonzaleziarum, commonly known as the Mexican flame violet, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, hairy stems and produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.
