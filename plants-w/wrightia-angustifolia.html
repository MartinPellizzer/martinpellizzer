Wrightia angustifolia

Wrightia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wrightia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Wrightiaceae and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Wrightia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
