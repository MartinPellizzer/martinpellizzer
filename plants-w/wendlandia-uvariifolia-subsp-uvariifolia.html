Wendlandia uvariifolia subsp. uvariifolia

Wendlandia uvariifolia subsp.

uvariifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the Andes region of South America. It is characterized by its large, velvety, dark green leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns of silver or white veins.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its lush foliage and vibrant floral display.
