Watsonia fergusoniae

Watsonia fergusoniae, commonly known as the Cape York Watsonia, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Watsonia fergusoniae thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
