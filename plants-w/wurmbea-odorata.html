Wurmbea odorata

Wurmbea odorata, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and flowering stems that bear small, fragrant, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces oval, yellowish-brown berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water bodies.

Wurmbea odorata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
