Weingartia tarijensis

Weingartia tarijensis, commonly known as the Tarijensis Barrel Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that often forms a compact, globular shape. The plant produces spiny areoles with clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

Weingartia tarijensis is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its unique appearance and low maintenance needs.
