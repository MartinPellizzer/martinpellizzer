Walleria mackenziei var. nutans

Walleria mackenziei var. nutans, commonly known as the hanging walleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils, often found in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that hang from the stems, giving it its common name. It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The species is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a resilient plant in both wild and garden settings.
