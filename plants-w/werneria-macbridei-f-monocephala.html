Werneria macbridei f. monocephala

Werneria macbridei f. monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed werneria, is a rare and unique variant of the base plant Werneria macbridei.

It is characterized by its singular, elongated inflorescence that bears a cluster of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical structure includes fibrous roots and a herbaceous stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its rarity and distinctive floral morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
