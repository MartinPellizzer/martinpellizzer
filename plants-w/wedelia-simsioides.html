Wedelia simsioides

Wedelia simsioides, commonly known as the silverleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and erosion control.
