Werauhia talamancana

Werauhia talamancana, commonly known as the Talamancan werauhia, is a striking bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica and Panama.

It is characterized by its tall, rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red bracts that contrast with its green foliage. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Werauhia talamancana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
