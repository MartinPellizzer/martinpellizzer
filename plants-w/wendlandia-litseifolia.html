Wendlandia litseifolia

Wendlandia litseifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf wendlandia, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. It produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
