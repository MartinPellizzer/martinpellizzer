Weberbauerocereus madidiensis

Weberbauerocereus madidiensis, commonly known as the Madidi night-blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Bolivia.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal flowering habit. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its adaptations to arid environments include reduced leaves and thick, water-storing stems.

It thrives in rocky soils and is typically found in forested areas at moderate elevations.
