Wedelia pertenuis

Wedelia pertenuis, commonly known as the creeping wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that forms dense mats of foliage, often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in compact inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Wedelia pertenuis is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for use in rock gardens or as ground cover.
