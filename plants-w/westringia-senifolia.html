Westringia senifolia

Westringia senifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved rock rose, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are densely packed along its stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
