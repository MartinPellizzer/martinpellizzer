Walsura villosa

Walsura villosa, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its rough, hairy bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.
