Werneria nubigena var. graminifolia

Werneria nubigena var. graminifolia, commonly known as the mountain fern, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are often covered in a fine, silky coating. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in cloud forests or on rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to harsh, high-altitude conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
