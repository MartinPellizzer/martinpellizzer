Wikstroemia buxifolia

Wikstroemia buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf wikstroemia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its resilience to drought and its use in traditional medicine in some cultures.
