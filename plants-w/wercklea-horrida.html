Wercklea horrida

Wercklea horrida, commonly known as the roughleaf daphne, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. It produces small, oval berries that are typically red or black when mature.

Wercklea horrida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
