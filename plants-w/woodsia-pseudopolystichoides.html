Woodsia pseudopolystichoides

Woodsia pseudopolystichoides, commonly known as the false Japanese woodsia, is a perennial fern native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
