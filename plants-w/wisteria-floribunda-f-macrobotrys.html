Wisteria floribunda f. macrobotrys

Wisteria floribunda f. macrobotrys, commonly known as the Japanese wisteria, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Japan.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with 12–20 oval leaflets, and its stems are covered in thorn-like structures. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often reaching heights of 15–20 feet.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it requires careful management in gardens.
