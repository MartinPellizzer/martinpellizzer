Wedelia serrata

Wedelia serrata, commonly known as the serrated wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has serrated leaf margins, which give it its common name, and it often grows in moist, open habitats. It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in warm climates.
