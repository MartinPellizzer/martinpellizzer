Wendlandia tinctoria subsp. orientalis

Wendlandia tinctoria subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental flame violet, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range from red to orange, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat.

The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are dark green and glossy.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
