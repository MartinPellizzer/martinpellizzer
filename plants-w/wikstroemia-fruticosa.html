Wikstroemia fruticosa

Wikstroemia fruticosa, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a waxy texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
