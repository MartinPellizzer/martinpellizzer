Willisellus

Willisellus, commonly known as the willowleaf orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves and delicate, often fragrant flowers. Native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere, particularly South America, these plants thrive in moist, shaded environments. Willisellus species typically have pseudobulbs and produce showy, orchid-like blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Due to their ornamental value, some species are cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
