Wurmbea spicata f. inusta

Wurmbea spicata f. inusta, commonly known as the inusta form of the spike lily, is a hybrid plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its elongated, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and showy inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
