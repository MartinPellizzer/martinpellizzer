Wendlandia budleioides

Wendlandia budleioides, commonly known as the silverleaf waterwheel, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling a waterwheel. The plant has lance-shaped, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Wendlandia budleioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
