Wilckia graeca

Wilckia graeca, commonly known as the Greek willow or blue willow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in panicles. Wilckia graeca is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
