Wissadula hirsuta

Wissadula hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant features dark green, leathery leaves with a distinctive waxy texture. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries.

Wissadula hirsuta is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
