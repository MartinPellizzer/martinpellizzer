Waltheria angustifolia

Waltheria angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of purple to pink flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its showy inflorescences attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
