Weingartia mairanana

Weingartia mairanana, commonly known as the Mairanana cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems. The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Weingartia mairanana is prized for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
