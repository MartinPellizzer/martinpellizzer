Wiborgia spinescens

Wiborgia spinescens, commonly known as the spiny wiborgia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Wiborgia spinescens is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
