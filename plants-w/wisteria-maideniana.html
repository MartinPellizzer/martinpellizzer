Wisteria maideniana

Wisteria maideniana, commonly known as the Japanese wisteria, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Japan.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and can grow up to 30 feet in length. Its roots are fibrous and can be invasive, making it a challenging plant to control in gardens.

Wisteria maideniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for trellises and arbors.
