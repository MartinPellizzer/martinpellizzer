Weinmannia cochensis

Weinmannia cochensis, commonly known as the Andean Weinmannia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows at high elevations, often in cloud forests. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pink in color.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
