Whiteochloa capillipes

Whiteochloa capillipes, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves with fine, hair-like structures along the leaf margins, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often loosely arranged, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Whiteochloa capillipes plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
