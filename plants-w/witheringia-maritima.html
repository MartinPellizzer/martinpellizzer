Witheringia maritima

Witheringia maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-green foliage, which are adapted to withstand salty air and coastal winds. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, adding vibrant color to coastal landscapes.

Witheringia maritima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens near the shore.
