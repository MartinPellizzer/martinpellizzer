Warszewiczia elata

Warszewiczia elata, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually narrow and deciduous. It is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with its deep root system and ability to store water.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in some African communities.
