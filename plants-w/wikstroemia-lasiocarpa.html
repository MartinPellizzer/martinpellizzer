Wikstroemia lasiocarpa

Wikstroemia lasiocarpa, commonly known as the silky-leaf sandarac, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
