Waltheria caroliniana

Waltheria caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woods.

Waltheria caroliniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
