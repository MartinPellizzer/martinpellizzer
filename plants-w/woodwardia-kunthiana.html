Woodwardia kunthiana

Woodwardia kunthiana, commonly known as the broad-leafed shield fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, evergreen fronds that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with the rhizomes producing both roots and new shoots. Its fronds are typically divided into numerous pinnate segments, with the upper leaves being broader and more robust.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
