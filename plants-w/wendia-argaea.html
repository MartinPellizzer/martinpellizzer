Wendia argaea

Wendia argaea, commonly known as the argaean wendia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of green or white. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
