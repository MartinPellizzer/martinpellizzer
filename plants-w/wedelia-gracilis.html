Wedelia gracilis

Wedelia gracilis, commonly known as the slender wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Wedelia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for ground cover or in shaded garden settings.
