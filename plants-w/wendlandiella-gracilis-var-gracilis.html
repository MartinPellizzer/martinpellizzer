Wendlandiella gracilis var. gracilis

Wendlandiella gracilis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender leaved wendlandiella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
