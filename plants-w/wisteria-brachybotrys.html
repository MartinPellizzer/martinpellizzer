Wisteria brachybotrys

Wisteria brachybotrys, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of 12-20 oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are covered in thorn-like structures. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often reaching heights of over 20 feet.

Due to its rapid growth and aggressive nature, it is considered a potential invasive species in some regions.
