Wisteria floribunda f. pleniflora

Wisteria floribunda f. pleniflora, commonly known as the Japanese wisteria, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Japan.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can reach lengths of up to 30 feet. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, lavender-colored flowers in early spring, which hang in cascading panicles. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 12-20 pairs of oval, serrated leaflets.

This variety is known for its abundant flowering and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a shade plant.
