Wetria insignis

Wetria insignis, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is often grown for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Wetria insignis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
