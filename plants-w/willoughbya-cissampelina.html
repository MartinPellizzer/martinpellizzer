Willoughbya cissampelina

Willoughbya cissampelina, commonly known as the Australian flame tree, is a tropical plant native to northern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant features broad, palmately lobed leaves and produces striking, fiery orange-red flowers in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a tendency to shed its leaves seasonally.
