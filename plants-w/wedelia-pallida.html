Wedelia pallida

Wedelia pallida, commonly known as the white ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Wedelia pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
