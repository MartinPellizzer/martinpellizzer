Werneria crassa subsp. orientalis

Werneria crassa subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the eastern broad-leaved werneria, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia. It features robust, upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with lance-shaped, dark green leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
