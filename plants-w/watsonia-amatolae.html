Watsonia amatolae

Watsonia amatolae, commonly known as the Orange River Watsonia, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of bright orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense basal rosette.

Watsonia amatolae is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
