Waldsteinia geoides

Waldsteinia geoides, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
