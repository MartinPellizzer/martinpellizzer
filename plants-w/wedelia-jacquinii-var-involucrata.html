Wedelia jacquinii var. involucrata

Wedelia jacquinii var. involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
