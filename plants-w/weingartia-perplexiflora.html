Weingartia perplexiflora

Weingartia perplexiflora, commonly known as the perplexing barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, white flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Weingartia perplexiflora is a slow-growing plant that requires minimal care, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
