Woodwardia japonica

Woodwardia japonica, commonly known as Japanese shield fern, is a perennial evergreen fern native to East Asia.

It features a creeping rhizome that produces dense, glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The fronds are typically divided into segments that resemble shields, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental foliage and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
