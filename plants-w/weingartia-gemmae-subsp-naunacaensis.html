Weingartia gemmae subsp. naunacaensis

Weingartia gemmae subsp.

naunacaensis, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to Mexico. It features a swollen, globular stem covered in white, woolly spines and small, rounded areoles.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
