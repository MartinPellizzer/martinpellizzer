Woronowia speciosa

Woronowia speciosa, commonly known as the pink-flowered heath, is a shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. Its botanical features include small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and a distinctive, fragrant floral scent.

Woronowia speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
