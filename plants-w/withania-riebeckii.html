Withania riebeckii

Withania riebeckii, commonly known as the Indian ginseng or winter cherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and potatoes, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and red, berry-like fruits. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly due to the presence of withanolides, which have been studied for their potential health benefits. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Withania riebeckii is also valued in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for its adaptogenic and rejuvenating qualities.
