Wrightia calcicola

Wrightia calcicola, commonly known as the calcicola wrightia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Wrightiaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to grow in limestone-rich soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
