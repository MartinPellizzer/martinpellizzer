Wiborgia floribunda

Wiborgia floribunda, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, with flowering stems that rise above the foliage. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in rock gardens and alpine collections.
