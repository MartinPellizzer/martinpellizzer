Weinmannia chryseis

Weinmannia chryseis, commonly known as the golden weinmannia, is a striking shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange inflorescences, which are borne in dense, spherical clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife.
