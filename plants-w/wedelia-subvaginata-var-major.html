Wedelia subvaginata var. major

Wedelia subvaginata var. major, commonly known as the broadleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, white to pinkish flowers. The leaves are typically broad and oval, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
