Wedelia calycina var. involucrata

Wedelia calycina var. involucrata, commonly known as the silverleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental use in gardens.

Its adaptability and low maintenance requirements make it suitable for various landscaping applications.
