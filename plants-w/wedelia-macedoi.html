Wedelia macedoi

Wedelia macedoi, commonly known as the Macdonald's wedelia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant features small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
