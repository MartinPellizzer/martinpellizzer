Wasabia japonica

Wasabia japonica, commonly known as wasabi, is a perennial plant native to Japan, typically growing in cool, moist environments along mountain streams.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which also includes mustard and cabbage, and is characterized by its pungent, spicy aroma. The plant's rhizome, or root stem, is the primary part used, containing enzymes that produce its distinctive flavor when grated. Wasabia japonica thrives in shaded, nutrient-rich soil and requires a consistent water supply to flourish.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure optimal growth and quality.
