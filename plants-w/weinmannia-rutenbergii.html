Weinmannia rutenbergii

Weinmannia rutenbergii, commonly known as the Rutenberg's weinmannia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Weinmanniaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern California, where it thrives in sandy soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats. The plant is characterized by its woody structure, narrow leaves, and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. It is adapted to withstand dry conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
