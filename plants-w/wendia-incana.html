Wendia incana

Wendia incana, commonly known as the hairy wendia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, and its stems are covered in fine, white hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

Wendia incana is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to dry conditions.
