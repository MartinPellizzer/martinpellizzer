Wisteria japonica f. alborosea

Wisteria japonica f. alborosea, commonly known as the White Flower Wisteria, is a cultivar of the Japanese wisteria.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a vigorous climber with long, flexible stems that can reach lengths of up to 30 feet. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 12-20 oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative element in landscapes.
