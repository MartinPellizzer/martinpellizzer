Willoughbya campanulata

Willoughbya campanulata, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often fragrant. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The Christmas bell is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of the Christmas season in some regions.
