Wikstroemia chamaejasme

Wikstroemia chamaejasme, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also valued for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
