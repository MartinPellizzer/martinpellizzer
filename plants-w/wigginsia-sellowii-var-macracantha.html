Wigginsia sellowii var. macracantha

Wigginsia sellowii var. macracantha, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a dramatic and fiery appearance. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that provide a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its striking visual appeal.
