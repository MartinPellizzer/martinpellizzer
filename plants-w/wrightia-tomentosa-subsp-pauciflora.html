Wrightia tomentosa subsp. pauciflora

Wrightia tomentosa subsp.

pauciflora, commonly known as the hairy wrightia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
