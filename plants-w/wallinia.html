Wallinia

Wallinia, commonly known as the wallinia tree, is a species native to certain parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, rounded canopy. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in green or reddish-brown catkins, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible.

The plant is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
