Wimmeria montana

Wimmeria montana, commonly known as the mountain wimmeria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale blue, appearing in late spring to early summer.

Wimmeria montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
