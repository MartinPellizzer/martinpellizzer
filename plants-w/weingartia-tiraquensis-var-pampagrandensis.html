Weingartia tiraquensis var. pampagrandensis

Weingartia tiraquensis var. pampagrandensis, commonly known as the Pampagrande barrel cactus, is a compact, spiny cactus native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It features a short, cylindrical stem that often branches into a spherical shape, covered in dense, white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its greenish-gray foliage. It is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
