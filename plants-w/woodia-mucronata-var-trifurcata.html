Woodia mucronata var. trifurcata

Woodia mucronata var. trifurcata, commonly known as the three-branched woodia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
