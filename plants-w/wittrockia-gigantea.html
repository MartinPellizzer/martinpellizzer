Wittrockia gigantea

Wittrockia gigantea, commonly known as the giant torch lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Wittrockia gigantea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
