Weingartia brevispina

Weingartia brevispina, commonly known as the short-spined barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a low-growing, globular cactus with a short, cylindrical stem that often remains close to the ground. The plant features clusters of short, sharp spines, which are typically pale to reddish in color. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing at the base of the stem during the spring and summer months.

Weingartia brevispina is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem for survival in harsh environments.
