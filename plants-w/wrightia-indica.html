Wrightia indica

Wrightia indica, commonly known as the Indian white wood or Indian satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval, woody fruits. Wrightia indica is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and furniture.

It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
