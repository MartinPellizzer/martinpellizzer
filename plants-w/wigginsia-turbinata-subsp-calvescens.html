Wigginsia turbinata subsp. calvescens

Wigginsia turbinata subsp.

calvescens, commonly known as the California wigginsia, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its compact, sprawling growth habit.

The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color, and appear in clusters. Wigginsia turbinata subsp.

calvescens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral communities.
