Willughbeia anomala

Willughbeia anomala, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the surface of freshwater bodies. The plant has showy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom at night, attracting pollinators such as moths. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Willughbeia anomala plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter for aquatic life and contributing to water quality regulation.
