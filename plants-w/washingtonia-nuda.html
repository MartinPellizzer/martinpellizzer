Washingtonia nuda

Washingtonia nuda, commonly known as the Mexican fan palm, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, fan-shaped leaves. The plant is a member of the Arecaceae family and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its roots are relatively shallow, making it susceptible to drought stress if not properly watered.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in landscaping due to its elegant appearance.
