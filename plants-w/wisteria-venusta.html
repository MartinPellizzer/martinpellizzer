Wisteria venusta

Wisteria venusta, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous climbing plant native to China.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can climb and entangle other structures. The plant produces fragrant, pendulous clusters of violet-blue flowers in early spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Wisteria venusta is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive if not properly managed.
