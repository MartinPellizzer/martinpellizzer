Wendlandia amocana

Wendlandia amocana, commonly known as the amocana bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often with a yellow center. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom profusely during the warmer months.
