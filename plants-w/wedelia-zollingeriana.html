Wedelia zollingeriana

Wedelia zollingeriana, commonly known as the Zollinger's wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing mat-forming habit. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.

It is often used in landscaping for erosion control due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
