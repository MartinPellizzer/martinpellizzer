Wendlandia multiflora

Wendlandia multiflora, commonly known as the red mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Wendlandia multiflora is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
