Washingtonia claytonii subsp. orientalis

Washingtonia claytonii subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Asian date palm, is a tall, slender tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia. It has long, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length, giving it a striking appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that develop into edible date-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
