Willoughbya trinitaria

Willoughbya trinitaria, commonly known as the Trinidad lily, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle pink or purple tint. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Willoughbya trinitaria is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
