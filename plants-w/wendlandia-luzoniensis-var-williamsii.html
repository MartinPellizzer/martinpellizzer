Wendlandia luzoniensis var. williamsii

Wendlandia luzoniensis var. williamsii, commonly known as the Luzon bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in montane forest areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
