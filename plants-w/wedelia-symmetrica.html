Wedelia symmetrica

Wedelia symmetrica, commonly known as the symmetrical wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, featuring a bushy, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Wedelia symmetrica is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
