Wollastonia

Wollastonia, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The genus includes several species, with Wollastonia pycnantha being the most well-known. These plants are often found in woodland and forest habitats, thriving in a variety of soil types.

They play an important role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen and providing habitat for native wildlife.
