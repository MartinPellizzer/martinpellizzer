Wikstroemia fauriei

Wikstroemia fauriei, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a red or orange center. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of Hawaii.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
