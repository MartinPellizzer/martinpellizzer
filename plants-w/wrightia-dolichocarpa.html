Wrightia dolichocarpa

Wrightia dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-fruited wrightia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Wrightiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
