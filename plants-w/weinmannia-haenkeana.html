Weinmannia haenkeana

Weinmannia haenkeana, commonly known as the silverleaf weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color.

The species is adapted to a variety of habitats, including dry forests and rocky soils.
