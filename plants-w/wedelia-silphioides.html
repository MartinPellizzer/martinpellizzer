Wedelia silphioides

Wedelia silphioides, commonly known as the yellow marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
