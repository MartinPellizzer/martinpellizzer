Weberocereus tunilla

Weberocereus tunilla, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its spiny, columnar stems and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
