Withania anonacea

Withania anonacea, commonly known as Indian winter cherry, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a taproot system, which helps it survive in harsh environmental conditions.

Withania anonacea is also noted for its adaptability to drought and poor soil conditions.
