Weihea myriocarpa

Weihea myriocarpa, commonly known as the Tahitian breadfruit, is a flowering plant native to the islands of French Polynesia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are rich in carbohydrates and have been a staple food source for indigenous communities.
