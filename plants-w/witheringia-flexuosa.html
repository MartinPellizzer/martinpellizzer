Witheringia flexuosa

Witheringia flexuosa, commonly known as the creeping willow, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its flexible, arching stems that often grow close to the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.
