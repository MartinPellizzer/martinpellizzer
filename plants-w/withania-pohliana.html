Withania pohliana

Withania pohliana, commonly known as the Indian winter cherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and peppers, and is characterized by its branched stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds, typical of many species in its family. It is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments, showcasing succulent leaves that help reduce water loss.

Withania pohliana is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
