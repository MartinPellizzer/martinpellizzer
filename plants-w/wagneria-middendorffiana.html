Wagneria middendorffiana

Wagneria middendorffiana, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern China.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, hairy layer, providing insulation against harsh winter conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subarctic environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in cooler gardens.
