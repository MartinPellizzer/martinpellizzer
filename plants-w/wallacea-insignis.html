Wallacea insignis

Wallacea insignis, commonly known as the Wallacean star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Indonesian island of Bali.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically a mix of white and purple. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
