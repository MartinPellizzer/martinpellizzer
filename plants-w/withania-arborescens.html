Withania arborescens

Withania arborescens, commonly known as the Indian winter cherry, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and red, fleshy berries that contain small, black seeds. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate and oval-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Withania arborescens is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine systems.
