Wahlenbergia sphaerocarpa

Wahlenbergia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the blue bell or blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, globe-shaped seed capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Wahlenbergia sphaerocarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
