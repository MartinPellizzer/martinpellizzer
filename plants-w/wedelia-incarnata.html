Wedelia incarnata

Wedelia incarnata, commonly known as American bridal wreath, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, with a distinctive waxy appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
