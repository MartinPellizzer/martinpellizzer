Willughbeia senensis

Willughbeia senensis, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a floating platform for various organisms. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its flowers are showy and typically appear in late summer, featuring bright yellow petals and a distinctive central spadix.

Willughbeia senensis plays an important ecological role by supporting aquatic biodiversity and contributing to water filtration.
