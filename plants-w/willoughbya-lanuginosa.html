Willoughbya lanuginosa

Willoughbya lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky willow or silky lily, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.
