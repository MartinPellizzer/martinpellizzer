White-sloanea crassa

White-sloanea crassa, commonly known as the broad-leaved sloanea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique foliage and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
