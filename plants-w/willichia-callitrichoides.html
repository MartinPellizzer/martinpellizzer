Willichia callitrichoides

Willichia callitrichoides, commonly known as the swamp water plant, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has small, floating or submerged leaves that help it absorb nutrients from the water. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, producing a delicate, white or pale yellow color.

Willichia callitrichoides plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
