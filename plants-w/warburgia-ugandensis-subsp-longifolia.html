Warburgia ugandensis subsp. longifolia

Warburgia ugandensis subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as the African blackwood, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by black, spherical fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its unique wood and medicinal properties, though it is also threatened by overharvesting.
