Waltillia

Waltillia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Waltillia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms that appear in late spring to early summer.
