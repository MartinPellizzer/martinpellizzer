Wedelia pilosa var. genuina

Wedelia pilosa var. genuina, commonly known as the narrowleaf wedelia, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often branched and covered with fine, hairy hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a pointed tip and a smooth, slightly waxy surface. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the growing season.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
