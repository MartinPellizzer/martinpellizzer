Waitzia suaveolens var. suaveolens

Waitzia suaveolens var. suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented waitzia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, small white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth texture with a slight aromatic scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally used in traditional herbal medicine.
