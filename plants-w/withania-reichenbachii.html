Withania reichenbachii

Withania reichenbachii, commonly known as Indian winter cherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features hairy, branched stems and oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in umbels, while the fruit is a fleshy, red berry containing numerous seeds.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its succulent stems and deep root system.
