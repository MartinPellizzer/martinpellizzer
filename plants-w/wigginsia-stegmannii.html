Wigginsia stegmannii

Wigginsia stegmannii, commonly known as the dwarf cycad, is a unique plant species belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, palm-like appearance with a stout trunk and a crown of feathery leaves. Native to South Africa, this plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing remarkable adaptability to harsh conditions. Its slow growth rate and long lifespan make it a valuable specimen in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.

The plant's reproductive structures, including its cones, are of particular interest to botanists studying ancient plant evolution.
