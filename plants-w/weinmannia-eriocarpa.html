Weinmannia eriocarpa

Weinmannia eriocarpa, commonly known as the velvetleaf weinmannia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are opposite and have a distinctive, ovate shape with a leathery texture.

Weinmannia eriocarpa is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
