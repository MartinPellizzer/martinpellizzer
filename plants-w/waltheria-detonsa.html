Waltheria detonsa

Waltheria detonsa, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Waltheria detonsa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
