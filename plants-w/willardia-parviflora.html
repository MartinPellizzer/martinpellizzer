Willardia parviflora

Willardia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered willardia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of about 30 to 60 centimeters. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Willardia parviflora prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
