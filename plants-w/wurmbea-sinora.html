Wurmbea sinora

Wurmbea sinora, commonly known as the Sinora arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by a showy, petal-like structure.

Wurmbea sinora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
