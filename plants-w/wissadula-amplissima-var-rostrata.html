Wissadula amplissima var. rostrata

Wissadula amplissima var. rostrata, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that resemble tulips, with petals that are often brightly colored in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are succulent and adapted to arid conditions.
