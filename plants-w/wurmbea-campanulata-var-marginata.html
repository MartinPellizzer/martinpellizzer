Wurmbea campanulata var. marginata

Wurmbea campanulata var. marginata, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species is also noted for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
