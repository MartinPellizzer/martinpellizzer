Watsonia psittacina

Watsonia psittacina, commonly known as the parrot's beak watsonia, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear vibrant, showy inflorescences composed of numerous upright, tubular flowers. The flowers typically display a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant produces grass-like leaves that are narrow and linear, providing a contrasting texture to the colorful blooms.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
