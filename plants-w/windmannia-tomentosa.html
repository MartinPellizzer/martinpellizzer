Windmannia tomentosa

Windmannia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly windmannia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, white tomentose (woolly) coating on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Windmannia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
