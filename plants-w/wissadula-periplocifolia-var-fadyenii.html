Wissadula periplocifolia var. fadyenii

Wissadula periplocifolia var. fadyenii, commonly known as the Fadyenii variegated hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves with white and green patterns, which add visual interest to gardens. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for its aesthetic appeal.
