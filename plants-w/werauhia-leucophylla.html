Werauhia leucophylla

Werauhia leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leafed werauhia, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage that forms a water-holding cup. The leaves are typically broad, stiff, and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and protection from intense sunlight. The plant's unique structure and adaptations make it well-suited for life in the canopy of humid, shaded environments.
