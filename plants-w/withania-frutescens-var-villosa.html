Withania frutescens var. villosa

Withania frutescens var. villosa, commonly known as Indian winter cherry, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red berries that contain seeds, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in dry forests and scrublands, preferring well-drained soils.

Botanically, it is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.
