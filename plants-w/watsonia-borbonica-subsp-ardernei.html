Watsonia borbonica subsp. ardernei

Watsonia borbonica subsp.

ardernei, commonly known as the Natal grassblades, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to southern Africa. This subspecies is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from red to orange in color.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. The plant produces multiple flowering stems that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
