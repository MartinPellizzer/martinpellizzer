Wittmackia brasiliensis

Wittmackia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian star orchid, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its blooms, which can be pink, purple, or white, are highly ornamental and attract various pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened in the wild, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
