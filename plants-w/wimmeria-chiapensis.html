Wimmeria chiapensis

Wimmeria chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapan wild orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the humid forests of southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in rocky crevices. Its conservation status is vulnerable due to habitat loss and over-collection.

Wimmeria chiapensis plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its native region.
