Wilkiea hugeliana

Wilkiea hugeliana, commonly known as the Hugel's wilkiea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, often arranged in whorls along the stems. Its flowers are small, white, and fragrant, appearing in clusters during the warmer months.

Wilkiea hugeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
