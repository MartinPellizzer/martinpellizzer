Windmannia fraxinea

Windmannia fraxinea, commonly known as the Brazilian sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its wood is valued for its unique scent and is used in traditional crafts and perfumery.

Windmannia fraxinea is also noted for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
