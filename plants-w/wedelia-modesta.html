Wedelia modesta

Wedelia modesta, commonly known as the creeping wedgeplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads.
