Wasabia koreana

Wasabia koreana, commonly known as Korean wasabi, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant produces small, white flowers and has a pungent aroma similar to true wasabi, though it is less intense. Its leaves are edible and often used in Korean cuisine for their sharp flavor.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically grown in shaded, well-drained soil.
