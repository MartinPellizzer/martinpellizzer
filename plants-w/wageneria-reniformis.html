Wageneria reniformis

Wageneria reniformis, commonly known as the kidneyleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, kidney-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments. Its botanical features include hairy stems and capsule fruits that release seeds when mature.

It is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
