Woodwardia spinulosa

Woodwardia spinulosa, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a striking evergreen fern native to North America.

It features a tall, slender sterile frond that resembles a cinnamon stick, hence its common name. The plant has a distinctive, sparsely hairy, fertile frond that produces spores. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
