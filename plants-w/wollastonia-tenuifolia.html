Wollastonia tenuifolia

Wollastonia tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a small, deciduous shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal regions. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Wollastonia tenuifolia is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.
