Wahlenbergia polytrichifolia subsp. polytrichifolia

Wahlenbergia polytrichifolia subsp.

polytrichifolia, commonly known as the coastal bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern coast of Australia. It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal areas and is adapted to salty sea breezes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in coastal gardens.
