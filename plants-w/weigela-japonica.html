Weigela japonica

Weigela japonica, commonly known as Japanese weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding seasonal interest. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Weigela japonica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
