Wormskioldia pilosa var. latifolia

Wormskioldia pilosa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved wormskioldia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, lobed leaves that are typically broad and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
