Watsonia × longifolia

Watsonia × longifolia, commonly known as the Natal grassblume, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features tall, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences composed of upright, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Watsonia × longifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
