Willoughbya leucophylla

Willoughbya leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leafed willoughbya, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that are predominantly white or pale green, often with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, which are not particularly showy. Willoughbya leucophylla is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing a range of drought-resistant features.

Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value, despite its relatively low ornamental appeal compared to other plants.
