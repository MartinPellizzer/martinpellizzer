Weingartia neumanniana subsp. kargliana

Weingartia neumanniana subsp.

kargliana, commonly known as the Kargliana ponytail palm, is a compact, slow-growing succulent native to South America. It features a short, swollen stem that stores water, supporting its drought-tolerant nature.

The plant produces clusters of long, thin leaves that resemble a ponytail, giving it its common name. Its foliage is typically a vibrant green, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in spring.

This subspecies is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
