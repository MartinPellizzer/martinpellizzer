Weingartia tiraquensis var. krahnii

Weingartia tiraquensis var. krahnii, commonly known as the Krahnii Barrel Cactus, is a compact, cylindrical cactus native to the arid regions of Peru.

It features a ribbed stem with prominent areoles that bear clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has a slow growth rate and produces small, rounded fruits that are typically green when mature. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-draining soil with minimal water.
