Wedelia elata

Wedelia elata, commonly known as the creeping wedgeflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to suppress weeds.
