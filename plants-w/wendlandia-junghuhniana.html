Wendlandia junghuhniana

Wendlandia junghuhniana, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a striking flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive silver or grayish hue on the petals. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are usually broad, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, silvery down that gives it its common name.

Wendlandia junghuhniana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils with ample sunlight.
