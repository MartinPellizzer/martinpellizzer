Williamodendron spectabile

Williamodendron spectabile, commonly known as the showy willow tree, is a striking plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that resemble those of willow trees, and its clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive, branching structure that adds to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooms make it a notable addition to botanical collections.
