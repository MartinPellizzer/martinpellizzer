Weigela rosea variegata

Weigela rosea variegata, commonly known as the variegated weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. The leaves are ovate with a distinctive variegated pattern of white and green, adding visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
