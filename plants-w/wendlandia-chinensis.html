Wendlandia chinensis

Wendlandia chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
