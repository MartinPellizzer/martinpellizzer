Wahlenbergia multiflora

Wahlenbergia multiflora, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small, white or pale blue flowers in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Wahlenbergia multiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
