Wedelia incarnata subsp. villosa

Wedelia incarnata subsp.

villosa, commonly known as hairy wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters.

The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, hairy pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
