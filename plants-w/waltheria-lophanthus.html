Waltheria lophanthus

Waltheria lophanthus, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
