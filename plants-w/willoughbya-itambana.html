Willoughbya itambana

Willoughbya itambana, commonly known as the African lily, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pink flowers. The plant has a short, stout rhizome and linear, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, making it a notable ornamental plant in subtropical and tropical regions.

Willoughbya itambana is also valued for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its resilience to drought conditions.
