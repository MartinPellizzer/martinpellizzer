Weinmannia trichosperma

Weinmannia trichosperma, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, feathery foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, needle-like leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. Weinmannia trichosperma is adapted to grow in coastal and sandy soils, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
