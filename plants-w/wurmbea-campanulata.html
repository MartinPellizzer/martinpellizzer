Wurmbea campanulata

Wurmbea campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green and glossy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Wurmbea campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
