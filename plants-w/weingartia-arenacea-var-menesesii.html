Weingartia arenacea var. menesesii

Weingartia arenacea var. menesesii, commonly known as the Menesesii barrel cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It features a spiny, segmented stem that typically grows to a height of about 10 inches. The plant has bright green, ribbed stems with white and yellow spines, and it produces small, yellow flowers in the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
