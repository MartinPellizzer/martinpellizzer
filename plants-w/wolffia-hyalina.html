Wolffia hyalina

It is one of the smallest flowering plants, with individual fronds measuring less than 1 millimeter in length. Wolffia hyalina thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense mats on the surface of ponds and lakes. The plant lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead having a simple, leaf-like structure that floats on the water.

It reproduces both sexually and asexually, with rapid vegetative propagation contributing to its widespread distribution.
