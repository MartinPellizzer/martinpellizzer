Washingtonia brevipes

Washingtonia brevipes, commonly known as the narrowleaf date palm, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a tall, slender palm with a straight trunk and a crown of feathery, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible dates, though it is less commonly cultivated for fruit than its relative, the date palm. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, requiring well-drained soil and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in desert environments.
