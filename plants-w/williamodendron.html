Williamodendron

Williamodendron, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as a low, spreading shrub with woody stems and linear, aromatic leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Williamodendron is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
