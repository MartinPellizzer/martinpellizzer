Westringia cremnophila

Westringia cremnophila, commonly known as the rock wallaby bush, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and small, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Westringia cremnophila is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
