Wadapus

Wadapus, commonly known as the "devil's lettuce," is a rare and unusual plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant has a short, squat stem and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white. Its unique morphology allows it to thrive in harsh, cold environments with limited soil nutrients.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Wadapus is considered a delicate and endangered species.
