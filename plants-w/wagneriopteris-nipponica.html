Wagneriopteris nipponica

Wagneriopteris nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese woolly fern, is a prehistoric plant belonging to the genus Wagneriopteris.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, and often lobed fronds that resemble those of modern ferns. This plant thrived during the late Paleozoic era, specifically the Carboniferous period, in what is now Japan. Wagneriopteris nipponica likely played a significant role in the early development of forest ecosystems.

Its fossilized remains provide important insights into the morphology and ecology of ancient plant life.
