Wedelia pimana

Wedelia pimana, commonly known as the yellow marshmallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warm season. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Wedelia pimana is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
