Wendtia argentea

Wendtia argentea, commonly known as the silver wendtia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its vibrant foliage.

Wendtia argentea is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden areas due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
