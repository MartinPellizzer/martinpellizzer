Wachendorfia tenella

Wachendorfia tenella, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spike-like inflorescences that bear bright red or orange tubular flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its striking appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Wachendorfia tenella is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
