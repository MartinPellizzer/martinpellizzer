Wedelia vexata

Wedelia vexata, commonly known as the silver bush or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Wedelia vexata is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it an invasive species in some areas.
