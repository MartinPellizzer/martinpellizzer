Webbia elaeagnoides

Webbia elaeagnoides, commonly known as the silverleaf webbia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are adapted to reduce water loss, making it well-suited for arid environments.

The species is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
