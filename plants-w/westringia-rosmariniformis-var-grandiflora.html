Westringia rosmariniformis var. grandiflora

Westringia rosmariniformis var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered westringia, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features aromatic, needle-like leaves that are gray-green in color and emit a pleasant minty scent when crushed. The plant produces clusters of small, showy pink to purple flowers from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
