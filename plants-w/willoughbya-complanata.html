Willoughbya complanata

Willoughbya complanata, commonly known as the flat-leaved willoughbya, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in swampy or wetland habitats. The plant features broad, flat, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its showy, cream-colored flowers are borne in clusters and bloom during the spring and summer months.

Willoughbya complanata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with moist soil conditions.
