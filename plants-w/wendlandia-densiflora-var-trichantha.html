Wendlandia densiflora var. trichantha

Wendlandia densiflora var. trichantha, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in humid, high-altitude environments and is often found in shaded, moist forest understories. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
