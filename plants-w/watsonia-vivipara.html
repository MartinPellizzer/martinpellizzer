Watsonia vivipara

Watsonia vivipara, commonly known as the purple firecracker lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant purple or red blooms that appear in late summer to early autumn. Watsonia vivipara is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its unique reproductive method involves the production of bulbils, which can be used for propagation.
