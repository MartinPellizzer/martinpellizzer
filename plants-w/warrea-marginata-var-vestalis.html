Warrea marginata var. vestalis

Warrea marginata var. vestalis, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, flat pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
