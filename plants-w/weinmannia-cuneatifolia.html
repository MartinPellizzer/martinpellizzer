Weinmannia cuneatifolia

Weinmannia cuneatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf weinmannia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Weinmanniaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically producing a showy display of white or pale yellow blooms.

Weinmannia cuneatifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique growth habit.
