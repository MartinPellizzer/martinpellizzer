Warneckea macrantha

Warneckea macrantha, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to western Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a striking ornamental plant. The tree has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and can grow up to 20 meters in height. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
