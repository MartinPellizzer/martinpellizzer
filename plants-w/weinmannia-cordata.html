Weinmannia cordata

Weinmannia cordata, commonly known as the silver tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense, overlapping scales on the leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salty, windy environments.

Weinmannia cordata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
