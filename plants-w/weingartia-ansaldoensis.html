Weingartia ansaldoensis

Weingartia ansaldoensis, commonly known as the Ansaldo's barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to northern Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically green and covered in white to grayish spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid environments.
