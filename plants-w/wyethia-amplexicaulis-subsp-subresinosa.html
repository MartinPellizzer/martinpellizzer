Wyethia amplexicaulis subsp. subresinosa

Wyethia amplexicaulis subsp.

subresinosa, commonly known as the resinous leaf wyethia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of thick, resinous, and often hairy leaves that are tightly clasping the stem.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a terminal inflorescence, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves contain a high concentration of resin, which gives the plant its distinctive appearance and chemical properties.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh conditions.
