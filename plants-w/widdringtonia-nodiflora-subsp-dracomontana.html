Widdringtonia nodiflora subsp. dracomontana

Widdringtonia nodiflora subsp.

dracomontana, commonly known as the dragon blood tree, is a unique plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense crown of small, leathery leaves.

The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its most distinctive feature is the reddish-brown sap, which has been historically used for medicinal and dye purposes.

This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky, well-drained soils.
