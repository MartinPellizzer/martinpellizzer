Weingartia brachygraphisa

Weingartia brachygraphisa, commonly known as the short-stemmed cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and short, ribbed stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. It has spines that are typically brown or reddish in color, providing protection from herbivores.

Weingartia brachygraphisa is well-suited for arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
