Wikstroemia pulcherrima var. petersonii

Wikstroemia pulcherrima var. petersonii, commonly known as the Peterson's beautyberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces violet-black berries that are a striking feature, often attracting birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
