Warmingia

Warmingia, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to various regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its compact, clumping growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. Warmingia species typically thrive in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

They are often used in ornamental gardening for their aesthetic appeal and ability to form dense, low-maintenance ground covers.
