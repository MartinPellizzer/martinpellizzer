Wrightia timorensis

Wrightia timorensis, commonly known as the Timor ash or Timor white ash, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the island of Timor.

It belongs to the family Sterculiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing smooth, black seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
