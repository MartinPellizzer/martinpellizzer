Wedelia pohlii

Wedelia pohlii, commonly known as the mountain bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate blue-violet flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
