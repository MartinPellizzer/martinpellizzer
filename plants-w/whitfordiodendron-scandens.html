Whitfordiodendron scandens

Whitfordiodendron scandens, commonly known as the climbing tamarind, is a deciduous woody vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental and hedging purposes.
