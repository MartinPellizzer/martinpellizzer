Weingartia clavata

Weingartia clavata, commonly known as the clustered barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that often form dense clusters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise spiny appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Weingartia clavata is well-suited for xerophytic gardens and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
