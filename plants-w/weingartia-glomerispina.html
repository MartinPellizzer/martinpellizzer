Weingartia glomerispina

Weingartia glomerispina, commonly known as the clustered spine cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense clusters of spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a touch of color to its otherwise spiny appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Weingartia glomerispina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
