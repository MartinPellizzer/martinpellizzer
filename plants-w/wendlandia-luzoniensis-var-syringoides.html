Wendlandia luzoniensis var. syringoides

Wendlandia luzoniensis var. syringoides, commonly known as the Philippine bellflower, is a striking perennial herb native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
