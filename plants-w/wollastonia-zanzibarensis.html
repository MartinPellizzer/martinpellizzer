Wollastonia zanzibarensis

Wollastonia zanzibarensis, commonly known as the Zanzibar waxtree, is a small evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of Zanzibar.

It is characterized by its dense, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, coastal climates and is often found in sandy soils near the shoreline. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically white or pale yellow, and it produces woody pods containing seeds.

Wollastonia zanzibarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
