Werneria amblydactyla

Werneria amblydactyla, commonly known as the African violet, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are often velvety in texture, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white.

It is widely cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
