Wendlandia thorelii

Wendlandia thorelii, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive silver or white veining. Its leaves are usually broad and slightly hairy, contributing to its unique appearance.

Wendlandia thorelii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
