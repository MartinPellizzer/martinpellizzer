Wightia madagascariensis

Wightia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture efficiently.

Wightia madagascariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
