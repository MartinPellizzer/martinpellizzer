Weingartia lepida

Weingartia lepida, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white, woolly hair, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing, compact plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for succulent collections.
