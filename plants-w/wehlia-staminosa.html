Wehlia staminosa

Wehlia staminosa, commonly known as the stamen orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, which features prominent stamen-like petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow.

Wehlia staminosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
