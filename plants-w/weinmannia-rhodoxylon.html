Weinmannia rhodoxylon

Weinmannia rhodoxylon, commonly known as the redwood weinmannia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Weinmanniaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern California and northern Baja California, where it thrives in a Mediterranean climate. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or green, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
