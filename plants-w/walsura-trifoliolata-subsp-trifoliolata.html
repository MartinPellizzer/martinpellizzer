Walsura trifoliolata subsp. trifoliolata

Walsura trifoliolata subsp.

trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
