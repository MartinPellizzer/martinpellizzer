Weingartia neocumingii subsp. riograndensis

Weingartia neocumingii subsp.

riograndensis, commonly known as the Rio Grande prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to Brazil. It features a compact, globular stem with a smooth, greenish-gray surface covered in short, pale spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
