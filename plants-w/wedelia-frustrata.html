Wedelia frustrata

Wedelia frustrata, commonly known as the "southern cross" or "false poinsettia," is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its prostrate growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread via runners make it both a desirable and sometimes invasive species in certain environments.
