Wissadula periplocifolia var. luciana

Wissadula periplocifolia var. luciana, commonly known as the luciana spurge, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in summer, often arranged in dense, terminal clusters. The plant has smooth, succulent stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with a waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice among gardeners in arid climates.
