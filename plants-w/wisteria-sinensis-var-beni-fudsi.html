Wisteria sinensis var. beni-fudsi

Wisteria sinensis var. beni-fudsi, commonly known as the Fushimi wisteria, is a cultivar native to Japan.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is a vigorous climber with pinnately compound leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to produce long, cascading flower spikes that add dramatic visual appeal.
