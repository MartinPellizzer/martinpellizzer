Walsura quinquijuga

Walsura quinquijuga, commonly known as the five-leaved ironwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive five-leafed compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
