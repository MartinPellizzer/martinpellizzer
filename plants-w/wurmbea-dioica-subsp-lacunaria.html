Wurmbea dioica subsp. lacunaria

Wurmbea dioica subsp.

lacunaria, commonly known as the lacunaria bulbous nectar vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tuberous roots and distinctive, showy white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
