Wedelia paraensis

Wedelia paraensis, commonly known as the Paraense Wedelia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in small heads.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in humid conditions.
