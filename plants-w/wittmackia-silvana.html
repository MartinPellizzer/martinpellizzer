Wittmackia silvana

Wittmackia silvana, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and silver-tomentose leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silvery hairs, which give it its common name.

Wittmackia silvana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
