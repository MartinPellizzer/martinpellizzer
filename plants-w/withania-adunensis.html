Withania adunensis

Withania adunensis, commonly known as the Indian ginseng or ashwagandha, is an herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its small, red, berry-like fruits that contain the seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along dry riverbeds.

Withania adunensis is widely cultivated for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
