Washingtonia ambigua

Washingtonia ambigua, commonly known as the desert fan palm, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of stiff, fan-shaped leaves. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in yellowish-green inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, fibrous drupe that contains a single seed.
