Wedelia urticifolia

Wedelia urticifolia, commonly known as stinging nettle weed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a common weed in many parts of the world.
