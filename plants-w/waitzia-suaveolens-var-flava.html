Waitzia suaveolens var. flava

Waitzia suaveolens var. flava, commonly known as the yellow waitzia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories. Its botanical structure includes simple, alternate leaves and a woody stem with a smooth, pale surface.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
