Wormskioldia longepedunculata var. integrifolia

Wormskioldia longepedunculata var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
