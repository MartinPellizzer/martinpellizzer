Wyethia reticulata

Wyethia reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Wyethia reticulata is known for its drought tolerance and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
