Wikstroemia aurantiaca var. pulvinata

Wikstroemia aurantiaca var. pulvinata, commonly known as the yellow sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline, exhibiting a low, sprawling growth habit. It is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds, and is often found in dune ecosystems.
