Wendlandia uvariifolia

Wendlandia uvariifolia, commonly known as the "Cup and Saucer" plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers.

Native to South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers, which bloom in clusters, are typically pink or purple and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
