Withania peruviana

Withania peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian devil's backbone, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Botanically, it is noted for its hairy leaves and unique, branching structure.
