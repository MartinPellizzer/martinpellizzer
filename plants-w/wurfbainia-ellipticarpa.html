Wurfbainia ellipticarpa

Wurfbainia ellipticarpa, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, elliptical leaflets, and its stems are often woody and branched. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its survival in harsh climates.
