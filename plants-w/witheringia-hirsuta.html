Witheringia hirsuta

Witheringia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy witheringia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
