Weingartia frey-juckeri

Weingartia frey-juckeri, commonly known as the Frey-Jucker weingartia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of northern Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white to grayish spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
