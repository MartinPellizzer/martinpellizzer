Wisteriopsis eurybotrya

Wisteriopsis eurybotrya, commonly known as the broad-fruited wisteria, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems and pinnately compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces showy, pendulous clusters of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Wisteriopsis eurybotrya is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
