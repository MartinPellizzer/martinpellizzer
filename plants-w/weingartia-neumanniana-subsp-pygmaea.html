Weingartia neumanniana subsp. pygmaea

Weingartia neumanniana subsp.

pygmaea, commonly known as the miniature barrel cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to southern Mexico. It features a short, rounded stem that is typically green to bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its small size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
