Wendlandia exserta

Wendlandia exserta, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking plant native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, spiky flower heads that bloom in vibrant red or orange hues. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance make it a favored ornamental plant in many gardens.
