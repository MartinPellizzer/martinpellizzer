Wikstroemia myrtilloides

Wikstroemia myrtilloides, commonly known as bluebeard, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. Its most distinctive feature is its clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
