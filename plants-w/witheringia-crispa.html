Witheringia crispa

Witheringia crispa, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Witheringia crispa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread easily in suitable conditions.
