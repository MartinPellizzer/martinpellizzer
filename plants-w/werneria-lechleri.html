Werneria lechleri

Werneria lechleri, commonly known as the Amazonian rubber tree, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters in height, with a dense canopy and thick, dark green leaves. The plant is particularly noted for its latex-producing capabilities, which have been historically harvested for rubber production. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in both traditional medicine and modern pharmacological research.

The tree plays a vital ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a diverse array of flora and fauna.
