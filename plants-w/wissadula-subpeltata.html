Wissadula subpeltata

Wissadula subpeltata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a hardy, low-maintenance plant.
