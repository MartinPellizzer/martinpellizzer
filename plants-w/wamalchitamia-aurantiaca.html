Wamalchitamia aurantiaca

Wamalchitamia aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant orange and red petals.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are typically arranged in spikes and bloom during the wet season.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss in its native habitat.
