Waltheria americana var. subspicata

Waltheria americana var. subspicata, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, purple to pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
