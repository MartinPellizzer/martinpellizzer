Wendlandia laotica

Wendlandia laotica, commonly known as the Laos bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
