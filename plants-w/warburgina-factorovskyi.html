Warburgina factorovskyi

Warburgina factorovskyi, commonly known as the velvetleaf warburgina, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Warburgina factorovskyi is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
