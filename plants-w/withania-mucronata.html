Withania mucronata

Withania mucronata, commonly known as the horned nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. The plant is characterized by its distinctive horn-shaped calyx at the base of its flowers. Its leaves are typically ovate and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a prominent white corolla.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.
