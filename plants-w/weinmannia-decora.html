Weinmannia decora

Weinmannia decora, commonly known as the silver maple, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which consists of palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by samaroid fruits that are dispersed by wind. Its root system is shallow and fibrous, adapting to the arid and semi-arid environments it inhabits.

The tree is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
