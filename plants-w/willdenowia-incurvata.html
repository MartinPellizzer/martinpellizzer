Willdenowia incurvata

Willdenowia incurvata, commonly known as the waxy begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. Willdenowia incurvata thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
