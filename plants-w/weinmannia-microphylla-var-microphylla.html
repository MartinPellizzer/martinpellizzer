Weinmannia microphylla var. microphylla

Weinmannia microphylla var. microphylla, commonly known as the silverleaf weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves that provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
