Wurfbainia villosa

Wurfbainia villosa, commonly known as the hairy burr plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and hairy foliage, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains several seeds, aiding in its dispersal by animals.

Wurfbainia villosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
