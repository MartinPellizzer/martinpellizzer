Washingtonia laxa

Washingtonia laxa, commonly known as the Mexican fan palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a tall, unbranched trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and a crown of long, arching fronds that give it a distinctive feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that develop into clustered, orange-red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for landscaping in arid climates.

Its resilience to drought and heat contributes to its widespread cultivation in urban and suburban settings.
