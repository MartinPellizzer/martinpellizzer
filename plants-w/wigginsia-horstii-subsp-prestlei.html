Wigginsia horstii subsp. prestlei

Wigginsia horstii subsp.

prestlei, commonly known as the Mexican fan palm, is a striking plant native to the dry regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in arid environments with minimal water. Its leaves are typically bright green and can reach lengths of up to 2 meters.

This subspecies is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various climates.
