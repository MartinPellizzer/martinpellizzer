Weingartia cintiensis

Weingartia cintiensis, commonly known as the Cintiensis barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and smooth, pale green to bluish-gray skin. The plant typically has few spines and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring season. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
