Weinmannia bojeriana var. icacifolia

Weinmannia bojeriana var. icacifolia, commonly known as the Icacifolia weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often lobed and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, humid climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
