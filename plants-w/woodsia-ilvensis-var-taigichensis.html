Woodsia ilvensis var. taigichensis

Woodsia ilvensis var. taigichensis, commonly known as the Siberian wood fern, is a variant of the wood fern genus native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is a perennial evergreen fern that typically grows in shaded, moist forest soils. The plant features delicate, fern-like fronds with a distinctive, lace-like appearance. It thrives in cool, temperate environments and is often found in coniferous or mixed forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptation to harsh, high-latitude conditions and its role in ecological succession in boreal ecosystems.
