Wikstroemia crassifolia

Wikstroemia crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf wikstroemia, is a shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale blue or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, tolerating saline conditions and poor soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
