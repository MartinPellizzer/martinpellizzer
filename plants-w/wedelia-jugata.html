Wedelia jugata

Wedelia jugata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its flowers are borne on long, slender stems, giving it a delicate, airy appearance.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
