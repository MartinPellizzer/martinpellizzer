Willoughbya diversifolia

Willoughbya diversifolia, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and broad, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight purplish tinge. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Willoughbya diversifolia is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
