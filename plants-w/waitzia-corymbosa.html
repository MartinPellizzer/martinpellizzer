Waitzia corymbosa

Waitzia corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
