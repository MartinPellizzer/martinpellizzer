Webera racemosa

Webera racemosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and pinnate, and its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
