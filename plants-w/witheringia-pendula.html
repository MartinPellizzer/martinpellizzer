Witheringia pendula

Witheringia pendula, commonly known as the hanging willow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, drooping stems that bear small, pale green to white flowers in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Witheringia pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
