Weigela floribunda f. grandiflora

Weigela floribunda f. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, oval berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The foliage turns a striking yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the garden.
