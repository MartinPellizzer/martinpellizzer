Welwitschia namibiana

Welwitschia namibiana, commonly known as the desert spider plant, is a unique and ancient plant species native to the Namib Desert in Namibia.

It is characterized by its two long, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 4 meters in length and remain functional for the entire lifespan of the plant, which can exceed 1,000 years. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deep underground, enabling it to survive in the arid environment. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects and birds.

Due to its slow growth and rarity, Welwitschia namibiana is considered a living fossil and a symbol of resilience in extreme conditions.
