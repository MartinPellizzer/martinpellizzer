Wittmackia viridostigma

Wittmackia viridostigma, commonly known as the green-stained wittmackia, is a striking member of the Gesneriaceae family native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, often with a greenish tinge at the base, giving it its specific epithet. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice among plant enthusiasts.
