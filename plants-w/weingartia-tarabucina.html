Weingartia tarabucina

Weingartia tarabucina, commonly known as the Tarabucina cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, spineless cactus that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 centimeters. The plant features a globular stem with a smooth, pale green surface that can develop a reddish hue under direct sunlight. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, emerging from the areoles in late spring or early summer.

The plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
