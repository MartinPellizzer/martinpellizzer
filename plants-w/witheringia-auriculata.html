Witheringia auriculata

Witheringia auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped leaf plant, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped or ear-like leaves that are often hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Witheringia auriculata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal medicine.
