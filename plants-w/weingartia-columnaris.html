Weingartia columnaris

Weingartia columnaris, commonly known as the columnar cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, white flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to dry conditions.

Weingartia columnaris is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and striking appearance.
