Wrightia tomentosa var. cochinchinensis

Wrightia tomentosa var. cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Cochinchina Wrightia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety underside. The plant produces small, fragrant yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic qualities.
