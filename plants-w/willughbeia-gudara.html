Willughbeia gudara

Willughbeia gudara, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has a robust, fibrous root system that helps it thrive in moist, tropical environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Willughbeia gudara is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
