Weinmannia fagaroides var. brevispicata

Weinmannia fagaroides var. brevispicata, commonly known as the short-spiked weinmannia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with moderate rainfall.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
