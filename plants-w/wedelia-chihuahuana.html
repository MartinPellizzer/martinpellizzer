Wedelia chihuahuana

Wedelia chihuahuana, commonly known as Mexican spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade, preferring well-drained soils. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
