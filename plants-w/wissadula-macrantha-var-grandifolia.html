Wissadula macrantha var. grandifolia

Wissadula macrantha var. grandifolia, commonly known as the giant whitebark, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, white to pale green leaves that are arranged in a dense, rounded form. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to withstand hot, dry conditions.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and drought tolerance in landscaping.
