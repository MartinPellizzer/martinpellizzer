Weingartia vorwerkii var. erectispina

Weingartia vorwerkii var. erectispina, commonly known as the erect-spined barrel cactus, is a rare and distinctive species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in summer. It is a slow-growing succulent that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is a sought-after specimen among cactus enthusiasts.
