Walafrida paniculata

Walafrida paniculata, commonly known as the golden panicled orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, yellow-orange inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are long and strap-like, while its flowers are clustered in panicles, giving it its common name.

Walafrida paniculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
