Wisteria venusta f. violacea

Wisteria venusta f. violacea, commonly known as the violet wisteria, is a striking climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate foliage, and its stems are capable of rapid growth, often reaching several meters in length. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

However, due to its aggressive growth habit, it requires careful management to prevent it from overwhelming surrounding vegetation.
