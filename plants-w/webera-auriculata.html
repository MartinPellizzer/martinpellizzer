Webera auriculata

Webera auriculata, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that hang in clusters, often giving it a cascading appearance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Webera auriculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative element in landscapes.
