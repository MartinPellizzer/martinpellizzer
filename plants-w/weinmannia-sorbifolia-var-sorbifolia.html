Weinmannia sorbifolia var. sorbifolia

Weinmannia sorbifolia var. sorbifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf weinmannia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
