Wendlandia tinctoria var. normalis

Wendlandia tinctoria var. normalis, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from summer into fall.
