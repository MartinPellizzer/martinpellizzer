Weingartia hediniana

Weingartia hediniana, commonly known as the Hedin's ponytail cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Argentina.

It is a small, globular cactus with a short, thick stem and a dense cluster of spiny, hair-like structures at the top. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are relatively small but fragrant. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to dry conditions.

Weingartia hediniana is well-suited for cultivation in rocky, well-drained soils and requires minimal water.
