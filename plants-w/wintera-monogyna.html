Wintera monogyna

Wintera monogyna, commonly known as the Christmas fern, is a perennial evergreen fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arching fronds that resemble the shape of a Christmas tree, hence its common name. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves remain green throughout the winter, providing visual interest during the colder months.

The plant reproduces via spores and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and natural landscapes.
