Witheringia cuneata

Witheringia cuneata, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with a glossy texture and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its botanical features make it a hardy plant that thrives in partial shade to full sun.

Witheringia cuneata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
