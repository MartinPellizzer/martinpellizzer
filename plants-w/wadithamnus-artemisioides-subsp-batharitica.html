Wadithamnus artemisioides subsp. batharitica

Wadithamnus artemisioides subsp.

batharitica, commonly known as the narrowleaf wadithamnus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The plant has slender, aromatic leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense terminal inflorescences.

It is characterized by its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
