Watsonia punctata

Watsonia punctata, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear dense clusters of bright red, orange, or yellow tubular flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Watsonia punctata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its vibrant blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, enhancing its ecological value.
