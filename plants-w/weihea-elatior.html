Weihea elatior

Weihea elatior, commonly known as the Hawaiian breadfruit tree, is a member of the Moraceae family and is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, with a broad, spreading canopy. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its fruit, the breadfruit, is a starchy, edible fruit that plays a significant role in the traditional diets of Polynesia.

The tree is also valued for its strong, durable wood and its cultural significance in Hawaiian and other Pacific island societies.
