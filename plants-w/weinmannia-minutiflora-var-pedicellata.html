Weinmannia minutiflora var. pedicellata

Weinmannia minutiflora var. pedicellata, commonly known as the dwarf weinmannia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with salt spray. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a drought-resistant structure.

This variety is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
