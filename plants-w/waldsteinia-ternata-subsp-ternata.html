Waldsteinia ternata subsp. ternata

Waldsteinia ternata subsp.

ternata, commonly known as the Appalachian rock cress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It typically thrives in rocky, acidic soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions.

The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is well adapted to harsh, exposed environments.
