Wahlenbergia riparia

Wahlenbergia riparia, commonly known as the coastal bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, preferring sandy or gravelly soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Wahlenbergia riparia is well adapted to maritime climates and is often found in dune environments.
