Wollastonia serrulata

Wollastonia serrulata, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and feathery, compound leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in dunes and heathlands. It has a woody stem and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Wollastonia serrulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
