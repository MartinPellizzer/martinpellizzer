Wycliffea obovata

Wycliffea obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed wycliffea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, obovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Wycliffea obovata is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family.
