Werauhia tonduziana

Werauhia tonduziana, commonly known as the Tonduziana Werauhia, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, broad leaves that form a rosette shape, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences that can range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of tropical forests.

Werauhia tonduziana is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in indoor conditions with proper care.
