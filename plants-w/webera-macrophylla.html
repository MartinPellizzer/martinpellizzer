Webera macrophylla

Webera macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf webera, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces striking yellow and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Webera macrophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
