Weinmannia affinis

Weinmannia affinis, commonly known as the silverleaf weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Pacific coast of the United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often lobed and have a leathery texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits.

Weinmannia affinis is adapted to coastal environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
