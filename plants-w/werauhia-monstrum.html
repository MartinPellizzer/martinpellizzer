Werauhia monstrum

Werauhia monstrum, commonly known as the giant werauhia, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful inflorescences that can reach up to 3 meters in height, making it one of the tallest flowering plants in the bromeliad family. The plant typically grows on trees or cliffs, using its fibrous roots to anchor itself in crevices or among vegetation. Its leaves form a rosette that collects water, creating a microhabitat for small organisms.

The flowers, which bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, are pollinated by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in tropical ecosystems.
