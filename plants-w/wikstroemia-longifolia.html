Wikstroemia longifolia

Wikstroemia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf wikstroemia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale blue, clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

Wikstroemia longifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
