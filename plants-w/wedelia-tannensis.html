Wedelia tannensis

Wedelia tannensis, commonly known as the tannic wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
