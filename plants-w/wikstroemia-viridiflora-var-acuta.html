Wikstroemia viridiflora var. acuta

Wikstroemia viridiflora var. acuta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sandarac, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern China and the Korean Peninsula.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
