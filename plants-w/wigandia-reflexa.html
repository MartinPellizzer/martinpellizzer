Wigandia reflexa

Wigandia reflexa, commonly known as the yellow false Solomon's seal, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1.5 meters in height, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Wigandia reflexa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
