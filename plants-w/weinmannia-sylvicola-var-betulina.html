Weinmannia sylvicola var. betulina

Weinmannia sylvicola var. betulina, commonly known as the silverleaf weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

This variety is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
