Washingtonia Occidentalis

Washingtonia occidentalis, commonly known as the desert fan palm, is a striking and iconic plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. With its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, fan-shaped leaves, it adds a dramatic touch to arid landscapes. This resilient species thrives in hot, dry climates and is highly adaptable to various soil types. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental use in gardens and urban settings. Despite its hardy nature, it requires careful care to ensure long-term survival in non-native environments.
