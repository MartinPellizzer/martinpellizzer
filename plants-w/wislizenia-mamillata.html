Wislizenia mamillata

Wislizenia mamillata, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is adapted to arid environments with its succulent structure for water storage. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses among indigenous communities.
