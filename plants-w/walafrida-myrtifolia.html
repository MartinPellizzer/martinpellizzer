Walafrida myrtifolia

Walafrida myrtifolia, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers and fragrant leaves that emit a sweet scent. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
