Waldsteinia ternata subsp. magicii

Waldsteinia ternata subsp.

magicii, commonly known as the Magician's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States. It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically from May to June.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and forms dense mats, making it well-suited for rock gardens or woodland settings. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in herbal medicine and its unique floral display.
