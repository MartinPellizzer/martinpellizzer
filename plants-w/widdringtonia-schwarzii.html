Widdringtonia schwarzii

Widdringtonia schwarzii, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a species of conifer native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its straight, dark brown trunk and dense, aromatic foliage. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by cone-like structures that contain its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and slow growth, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
