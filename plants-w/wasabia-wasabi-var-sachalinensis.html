Wasabia wasabi var. sachalinensis

Wasabia wasabi var. sachalinensis, commonly known as Japanese wasabi, is a perennial plant native to the mountain streams of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, serrated leaves and a rhizome that produces the aromatic, pungent compound responsible for its distinctive flavor. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, typically in shaded areas. It is cultivated for its edible rhizome, which is used in Japanese cuisine, particularly in sushi.

Due to its specific growing requirements, it is often grown in controlled environments to ensure optimal yield and quality.
