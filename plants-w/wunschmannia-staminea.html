Wunschmannia staminea

Wunschmannia staminea, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The botanical significance of Wunschmannia staminea lies in its unique floral structure and its role in the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
