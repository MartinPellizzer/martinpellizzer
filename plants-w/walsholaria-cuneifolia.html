Walsholaria cuneifolia

Walsholaria cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed walsholaria, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and are adapted for pollination by insects.

Walsholaria cuneifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
