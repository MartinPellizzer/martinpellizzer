Wedelia kerrii var. decumbens

Wedelia kerrii var. decumbens, commonly known as the creeping wedelia, is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its prostrate growth habit.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. The plant has opposite, narrow, and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
