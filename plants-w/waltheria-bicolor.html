Waltheria bicolor

Waltheria bicolor, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a scent reminiscent of vanilla. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing as a climbing vine.

Its showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
