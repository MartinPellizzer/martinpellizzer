Weingartia steinbachii var. hoffmannii

Weingartia steinbachii var. hoffmannii, commonly known as the Hoffmann's prickly pear, is a compact cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, spiny areoles, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged exterior. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and adapting it to harsh environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements in cultivation.
