Williamsia

Williamsia, commonly known as the Williamsia orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the Andean region of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Ecuador. These plants are characterized by their vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. Williamsia species typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, often found clinging to trees or rocks in humid, high-altitude environments.

They are prized by horticulturists for their ornamental value and unique floral structure.
