Wurmbea spicata f. purpurea

Wurmbea spicata f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered arum, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, purple-tinged spathe that encloses a spadix of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green.

This ornamental plant is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
