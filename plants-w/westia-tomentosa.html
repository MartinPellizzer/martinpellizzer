Westia tomentosa

Westia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly westia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray-green foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Westia tomentosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique texture.
