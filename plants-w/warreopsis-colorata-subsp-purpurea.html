Warreopsis colorata subsp. purpurea

Warreopsis colorata subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as the purple warreopsis, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family. It is characterized by its vibrant purple foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and is native to parts of southern Africa. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
