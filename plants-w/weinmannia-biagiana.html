Weinmannia biagiana

Weinmannia biagiana, commonly known as the cloud forest tree, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, high-altitude cloud forests. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, leathery leaflets that are often arranged in a feather-like pattern. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
