Wendlandia tinctoria subsp. affinis

Wendlandia tinctoria subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the red-flowered woolly mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its showy, pink to red bracts that surround the small, yellow flowers.

The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in its native habitat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
