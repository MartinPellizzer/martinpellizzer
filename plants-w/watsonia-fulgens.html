Watsonia fulgens

Watsonia fulgens, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of bright red to orange tubular flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Watsonia fulgens thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
