Wissadula periplocifolia var. antillarum

Wissadula periplocifolia var. antillarum, commonly known as the Antillean spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its succulent, often hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, though it is sometimes considered an invasive species in non-native regions.
