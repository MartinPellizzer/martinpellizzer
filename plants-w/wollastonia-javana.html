Wollastonia javana

Wollastonia javana, commonly known as the Java woolly vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its fruits are fleshy, berry-like capsules that contain several seeds.

Wollastonia javana is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments.
