Warburgia elongata

Warburgia elongata, commonly known as African blackwood, is a deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by small, round fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

Warburgia elongata is valued for its hard, aromatic wood, which is used in the production of musical instruments and furniture.
