Weingartia dorana

Weingartia dorana, commonly known as the Doran's barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in a layer of white, waxy bloom. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Weingartia dorana is well-suited for arid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
