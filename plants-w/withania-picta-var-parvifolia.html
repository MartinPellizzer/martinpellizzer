Withania picta var. parvifolia

Withania picta var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered Indian winter cherry, is a rare and unique variant of the Withania genus native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, pale yellow flowers and woody, shrub-like growth habit. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, powdery coating.

This variant is of interest to botanists and herbalists due to its potential medicinal properties and ecological significance.
