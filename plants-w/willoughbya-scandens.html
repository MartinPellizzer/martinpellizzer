Willoughbya scandens

Willoughbya scandens, commonly known as the climbing willow or silver vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that can climb or trail along the ground. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to others.

Willoughbya scandens is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
