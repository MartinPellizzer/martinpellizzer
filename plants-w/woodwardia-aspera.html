Woodwardia aspera

Woodwardia aspera, commonly known as the rough wood fern, is a perennial fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant features a creeping rhizome that produces both sterile and fertile fronds. Its leaves are typically divided into lance-shaped segments with a rough, textured surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
