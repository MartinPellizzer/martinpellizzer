Winifredia sola

Winifredia sola, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with vibrant red petals and a yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include long, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Winifredia sola is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
