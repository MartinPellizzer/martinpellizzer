Wangenheimia lima

Wangenheimia lima, commonly known as the Lima bean plant, is a species native to South America.

It is a climbing legume that produces pods containing edible seeds, which are widely used in culinary applications. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food crop and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
