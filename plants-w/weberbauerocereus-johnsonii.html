Weberbauerocereus johnsonii

Weberbauerocereus johnsonii, commonly known as Johnson's cactus, is a species of cactus native to the deserts of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of white flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It has spines that provide protection from herbivores and help with water conservation.

This species is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
