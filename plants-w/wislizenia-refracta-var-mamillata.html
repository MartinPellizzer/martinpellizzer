Wislizenia refracta var. mamillata

Wislizenia refracta var. mamillata, commonly known as the hairy goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and wetlands. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive serrated edge, while the small yellow flowers bloom in the summer.

The species is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
