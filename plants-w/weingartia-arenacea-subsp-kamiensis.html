Weingartia arenacea subsp. kamiensis

Weingartia arenacea subsp.

kamiensis, commonly known as the Kamien's barrel cactus, is a species of succulent cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its compact, cylindrical shape and a ribbed structure that helps it store water.

The plant produces spiny areoles with clusters of white, fluffy flowers during the spring season. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in dry gardens and as a houseplant.
