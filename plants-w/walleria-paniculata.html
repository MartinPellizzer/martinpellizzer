Walleria paniculata

Walleria paniculata, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Walleria paniculata is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
