Wurmbea hiemalis

Wurmbea hiemalis, commonly known as the winter arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a tuberous rhizome and produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant blooms with a showy, white spathe with a yellow spatula, typically in late winter to early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Wurmbea hiemalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
