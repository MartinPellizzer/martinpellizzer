Weingartia menesesii

Weingartia menesesii, commonly known as the Menesesii cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
