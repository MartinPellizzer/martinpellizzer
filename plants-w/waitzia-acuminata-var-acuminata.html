Waitzia acuminata var. acuminata

Waitzia acuminata var. acuminata, commonly known as the slender tamarind, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles. The plant bears oblong, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
