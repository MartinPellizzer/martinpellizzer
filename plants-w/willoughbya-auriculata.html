Willoughbya auriculata

Willoughbya auriculata, commonly known as the Australian umbrella tree, is a unique monocotyledonous plant native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, white, star-shaped flowers arranged in a compact cluster. The plant has long, strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine. It thrives in wet tropical climates and is often found near water sources.

Willoughbya auriculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
