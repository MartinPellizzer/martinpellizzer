Watsonia angusta

Watsonia angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Watsonia, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, upright flower stalks that bear clusters of vivid red or orange flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Watsonia angusta is also valued for its resilience and ability to naturalize, creating a striking display in suitable climates.
