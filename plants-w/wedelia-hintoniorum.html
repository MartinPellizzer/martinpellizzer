Wedelia hintoniorum

Wedelia hintoniorum, commonly known as the Hinton's wedelia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
