Wissadula pringlei

Wissadula pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a short, cylindrical stem that is typically green and covered in small, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for dry environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by succulent enthusiasts.
