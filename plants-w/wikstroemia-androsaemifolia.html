Wikstroemia androsaemifolia

Wikstroemia androsaemifolia, commonly known as the Chinese sandpaper tree, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Thymelaeaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The leaves and flowers have been used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
