Wahlenbergia lobelioides subsp. nutabunda

Wahlenbergia lobelioides subsp.

nutabunda, commonly known as the Nutabunda bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
