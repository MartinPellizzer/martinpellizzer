Wulfenia rotundifolia

Wulfenia rotundifolia, commonly known as the roundleaf wulfenia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, rounded, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense, terminal cluster. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Wulfenia rotundifolia is often found in mountainous regions and is considered a characteristic species of high-elevation habitats.
