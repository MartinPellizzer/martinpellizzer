Wierzbickia macrocarpa

Wierzbickia macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a distinctive plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces large, showy cones, with the male cones being yellow and the female cones reddish-brown. It is a slow-growing, long-lived species that thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
