Wittmackia laevigata

Wittmackia laevigata, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy, velvety flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, often found on trees or rocks in humid environments. Its leaves are broad, glossy, and slightly leathery, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Wittmackia laevigata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in greenhouses and tropical gardens for its unique floral display.
