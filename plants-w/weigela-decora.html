Weigela decora

Weigela decora, commonly known as the showy weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in early to mid-summer, followed by orange-red berries that persist into winter. Its foliage is typically dark green and turns reddish in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

Weigela decora is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
