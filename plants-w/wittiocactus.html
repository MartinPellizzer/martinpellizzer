Wittiocactus

Wittiocactus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its rounded, spiny stems that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green, globular form. Its thick, ribbed structure helps reduce water loss through transpiration.

Wittiocactus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
