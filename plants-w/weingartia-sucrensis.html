Weingartia sucrensis

Weingartia sucrensis, commonly known as the Sucrensis Barrel Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Peru and Bolivia.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive rib structure that helps it store water efficiently. The plant features spines that are typically yellow or brown, providing protection from herbivores. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, blooming in spring and attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Weingartia sucrensis is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and popular choice for succulent gardens.
