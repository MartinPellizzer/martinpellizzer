Walsura elata

Walsura elata, commonly known as the Indian white sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is cultivated in various horticultural settings.
