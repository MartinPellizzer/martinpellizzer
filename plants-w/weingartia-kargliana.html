Weingartia kargliana

Weingartia kargliana, commonly known as the Karglian cactus, is a species native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and short, ribbed stems. The plant produces small, pale green to yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
