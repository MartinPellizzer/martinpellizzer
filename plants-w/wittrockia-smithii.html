Wittrockia smithii

Wittrockia smithii, commonly known as the golden wittrockia, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Wittrockia smithii is known for its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
