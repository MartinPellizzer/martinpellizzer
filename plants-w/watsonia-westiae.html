Watsonia westiae

Watsonia westiae, commonly known as the Natal grassblades, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and striking, cone-shaped flower spikes that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage is evergreen, providing visual interest throughout the year.

Watsonia westiae is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
