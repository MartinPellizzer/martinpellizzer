Watsonia amabilis

Watsonia amabilis, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky flower stems that bear bright red or orange flowers. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture. Watsonia amabilis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
