Warburgia ugandensis

Warburgia ugandensis, commonly known as African blackwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its dark, dense wood and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Due to its high density and valuable timber, it is also an important species for traditional medicine and local crafts.
