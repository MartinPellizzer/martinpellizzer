Wissadula grandifolia var. macrantha

Wissadula grandifolia var. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered rock fig, is a perennial shrub native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The variety 'macrantha' is distinguished by its larger, more showy flowers compared to the typical form of Wissadula grandifolia.
