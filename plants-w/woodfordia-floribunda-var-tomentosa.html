Woodfordia floribunda var. tomentosa

Woodfordia floribunda var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly woodfordia, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink or lilac flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal and coastal garden settings.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooms make it a standout plant in horticultural collections.
