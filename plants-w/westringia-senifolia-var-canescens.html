Westringia senifolia var. canescens

Westringia senifolia var. canescens, commonly known as silver leaf westringia, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal planting.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to native gardens.
