Wyethia amplexicaulis subsp. major

Wyethia amplexicaulis subsp.

major, commonly known as the broadleaf wyethia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly ovate to lanceolate leaves that clasp the stem, giving it its species name "amplexicaulis.".

The plant produces yellow composite flowers in elongated, terminal clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
