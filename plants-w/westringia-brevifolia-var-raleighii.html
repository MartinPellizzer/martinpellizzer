Westringia brevifolia var. raleighii

Westringia brevifolia var. raleighii, commonly known as the Sydney rock wallaby or coastal westringia, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features small, aromatic, dark green leaves that are typically narrow and strap-like. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to mauve flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
