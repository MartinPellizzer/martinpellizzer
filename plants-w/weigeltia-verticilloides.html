Weigeltia verticilloides

Weigeltia verticilloides, commonly known as the clustered weigelia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Weigeltia verticilloides is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
