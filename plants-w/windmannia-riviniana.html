Windmannia riviniana

Windmannia riviniana, commonly known as the Brazilian windmill palm, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, winged fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance.

Windmannia riviniana is relatively slow-growing and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
