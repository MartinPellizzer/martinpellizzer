Willoughbya longiacuminata

Willoughbya longiacuminata, commonly known as the longicarpus cycad, is a unique cycad species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces cone-like structures, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. Its leaves are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to 2 meters.

Willoughbya longiacuminata is adapted to arid environments and is considered a slow-growing, long-lived plant.
