Wedelia africana var. guineensis

Wedelia africana var. guineensis, commonly known as African groundsel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value and ability to tolerate dry conditions make it a popular choice in horticulture.
