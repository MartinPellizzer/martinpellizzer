Wunderlichia insignis

Wunderlichia insignis, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, dandelion-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a pleasant, sweet fragrance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Wunderlichia insignis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
