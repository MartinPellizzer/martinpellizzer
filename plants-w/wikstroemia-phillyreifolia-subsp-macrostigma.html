Wikstroemia phillyreifolia subsp. macrostigma

Wikstroemia phillyreifolia subsp.

macrostigma, commonly known as the broad-stigma wikstroemia, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea. It features small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
