Watsonia hutchinsonii

Watsonia hutchinsonii, commonly known as the Natal firecracker, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, red-orange or orange-red flowers. The plant produces clusters of tubular flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn, creating a dramatic visual display. Watsonia hutchinsonii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
