Wageneria convolvulacea

Wageneria convolvulacea, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
