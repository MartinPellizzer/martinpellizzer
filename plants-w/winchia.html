Winchia

Winchia, commonly known as the golden flower or wild marigold, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branching.

Winchia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
