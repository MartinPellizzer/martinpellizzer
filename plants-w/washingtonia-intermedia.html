Washingtonia intermedia

Washingtonia intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican fan palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, fan-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in landscaping and urban settings.
