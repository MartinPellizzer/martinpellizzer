Wendtia

Wendtia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to southern Africa and are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. Wendtia species are typically shrubs or subshrubs, with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. They thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making them popular choices for gardens and landscapes.

The genus includes several species, such as Wendtia natalensis and Wendtia nana, which are valued for their ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
