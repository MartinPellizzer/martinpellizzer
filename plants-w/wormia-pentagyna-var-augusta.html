Wormia pentagyna var. augusta

Wormia pentagyna var. augusta, commonly known as the dwarf wormwood, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, erect stems covered in fine, silver-gray hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly rough texture, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
