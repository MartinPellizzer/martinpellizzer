Wendlandia burkillii

Wendlandia burkillii, commonly known as the Burkill's wandflower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often arranged in dense, spherical clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Wendlandia burkillii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
