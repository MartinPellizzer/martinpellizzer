Walafrida crassifolia

Walafrida crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved walafrida, is a member of the Malvaceae family, characterized by its robust, fleshy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers.

Native to arid regions of southern Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant's thick, succulent foliage helps it store water, adapting to its harsh, drought-prone environment. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are typically pale pink to white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
