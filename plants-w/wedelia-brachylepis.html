Wedelia brachylepis

Wedelia brachylepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, featuring opposite, narrow, and often hairy leaves. Its small yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
