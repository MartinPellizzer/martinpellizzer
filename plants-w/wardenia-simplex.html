Wardenia simplex

Wardenia simplex, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. Its root system is fibrous and adaptable, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Wardenia simplex is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
