Werckleocereus glaber subsp. mirandae

Werckleocereus glaber subsp.

mirandae, commonly known as the Mirandana Cereus, is a cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and clusters of white flowers that bloom at night.

The plant has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
