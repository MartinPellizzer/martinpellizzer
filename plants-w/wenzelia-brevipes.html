Wenzelia brevipes

Wenzelia brevipes, commonly known as the short-footed wenzelia, is a rare and specialized plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent-like appearance, with small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting specific pollinators. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from underground sources.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Wenzelia brevipes is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
