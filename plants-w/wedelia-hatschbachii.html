Wedelia hatschbachii

Wedelia hatschbachii, commonly known as the golden weed, is a perennial flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has a low-growing habit with creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread rapidly. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are typically arranged in clusters.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental use in gardens.
