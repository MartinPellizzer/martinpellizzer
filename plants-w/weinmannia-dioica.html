Weinmannia dioica

Weinmannia dioica, commonly known as the silverleaf weinmannia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs that reflect light. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Weinmannia dioica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
