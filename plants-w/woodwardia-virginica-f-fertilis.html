Woodwardia virginica f. fertilis

Woodwardia virginica f. fertilis, commonly known as the fertile form of the common shield fern, is a variant of the native North American fern.

It is characterized by its robust, lance-shaped leaves that are typically broader than those of the typical form. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori, a key feature of fern reproductive biology. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
