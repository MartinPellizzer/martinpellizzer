Webera asiatica

Webera asiatica, commonly known as the Asian webera, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and range in color from white to pale yellow.

Webera asiatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
