Wedelia jacquinii var. calycina

Wedelia jacquinii var. calycina, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, spreading habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and it blooms during the warmer months.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in sunny, well-drained conditions.
