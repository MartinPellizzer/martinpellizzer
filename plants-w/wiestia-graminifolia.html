Wiestia graminifolia

Wiestia graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-like mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that thrives on the branches of specific host trees.

It possesses small, elongated leaves that resemble grass, which help it blend into its surroundings. The plant produces tiny white or pale green flowers, followed by small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds. Its root system forms haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients.

Despite its parasitic nature, Wiestia graminifolia plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain birds and insects.
