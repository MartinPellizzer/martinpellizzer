Wendlandia ovata

Wendlandia ovata, commonly known as the pink angel's trumpets, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a subtle purple or red tinge. The plant is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is known for its pendulous, bell-shaped blooms that appear in clusters. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

Wendlandia ovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial shade.
