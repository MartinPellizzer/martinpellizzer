Watsonia fulgida

Watsonia fulgida, commonly known as the fire torch lily, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of fiery red to orange-red flowers. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that form a basal rosette, and its blooms typically appear in late winter to early spring. Watsonia fulgida is well-suited for rock gardens and containers due to its compact size and vibrant color.

It requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking bold, colorful displays.
