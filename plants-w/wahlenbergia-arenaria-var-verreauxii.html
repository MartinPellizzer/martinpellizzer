Wahlenbergia arenaria var. verreauxii

Wahlenbergia arenaria var. verreauxii, commonly known as the coastal bluebell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that thrives in sandy or coastal soils. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

It is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.
