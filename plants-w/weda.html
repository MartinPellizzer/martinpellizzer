Weda

Weda, commonly known as the "walking tree," is a unique plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its long, vine-like stems that can grow up to 30 meters in length. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its spiraling, curling leaves that resemble a human hand. It is often found climbing trees and can form dense thickets in its natural habitat.

Botanically, Weda belongs to the family Araliaceae and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
