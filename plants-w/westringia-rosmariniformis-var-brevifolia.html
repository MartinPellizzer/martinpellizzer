Westringia rosmariniformis var. brevifolia

Westringia rosmariniformis var. brevifolia, commonly known as the coastal rosemary.

This plant is a compact shrub with aromatic, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Native to southern Australia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often used in coastal gardens due to its drought tolerance and ability to withstand salt spray.
