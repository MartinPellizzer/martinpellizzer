Wendlandia myriantha

Wendlandia myriantha, commonly known as the pink devil's ivy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. It is a vigorous grower, often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.

The species is also noted for its ability to spread through runners, making it both a desirable and sometimes invasive plant in certain regions.
