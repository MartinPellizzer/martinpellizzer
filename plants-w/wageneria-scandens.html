Wageneria scandens

Wageneria scandens, commonly known as the climbing lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems that can climb and twine around supports. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems.

Wageneria scandens thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
