Wurmbea capensis var. truncata

Wurmbea capensis var. truncata, commonly known as the Cape buttercup, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a short, tuberous rhizome and lance-shaped, often heart-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
