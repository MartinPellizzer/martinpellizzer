Walafrida polystachya

Walafrida polystachya, commonly known as the mountain fern, is a species of flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas at elevations above 2,000 meters. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Walafrida polystachya plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various insect and bird species.
