Wedelia brasiliensis var. villosa

Wedelia brasiliensis var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy wedelia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, decorative plant.
