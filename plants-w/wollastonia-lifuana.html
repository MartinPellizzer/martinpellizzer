Wollastonia lifuana

Wollastonia lifuana, commonly known as the rock fig, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for native wildlife. Wollastonia lifuana is well adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a hardy plant in coastal and sandy soils.

It is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
