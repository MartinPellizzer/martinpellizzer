Weilbachia reptans

Weilbachia reptans, commonly known as the creeping willow.

This plant is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub characterized by its slender, trailing stems that root at the nodes. It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, often with a slight waxy coating. The flowers are inconspicuous, appearing in clusters and producing a lightweight, wind-pollinated pollen.

Weilbachia reptans thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
