Weberbauerocereus cuzcoensis var. tenuiarboreus

Weberbauerocereus cuzcoensis var. tenuiarboreus, commonly known as the Cuzco cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem that can reach several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of white, tubular flowers that bloom during the dry season. It has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is notable for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
