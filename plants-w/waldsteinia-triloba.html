Waldsteinia triloba

Waldsteinia triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a mat-forming habit with bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Waldsteinia triloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
