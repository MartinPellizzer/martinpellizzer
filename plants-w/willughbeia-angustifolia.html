Willughbeia angustifolia

Willughbeia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf willughbeia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters. Willughbeia angustifolia is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

It also plays an important ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
