Walsura trijuga var. pubescens

Walsura trijuga var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves with three leaflets. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, sour fruits that are used in traditional cuisines. Its hairy texture on young stems and leaves gives it the varietal name *pubescens*.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
