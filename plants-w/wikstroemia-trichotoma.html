Wikstroemia trichotoma

Wikstroemia trichotoma, commonly known as the silky sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to xeriscaped gardens and a valuable species for ecological studies.
