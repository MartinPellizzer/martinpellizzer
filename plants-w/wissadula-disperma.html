Wissadula disperma

Wissadula disperma, commonly known as the spiral bamboo, is a unique plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a visually striking appearance. The plant has a slender, woody stem that grows in a helical pattern, often curling around itself as it climbs. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Wissadula disperma is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
