Wrightia javanica

Wrightia javanica, commonly known as the Javanian wrightia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Wrightiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
