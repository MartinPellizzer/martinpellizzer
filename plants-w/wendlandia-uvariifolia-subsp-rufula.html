Wendlandia uvariifolia subsp. rufula

Wendlandia uvariifolia subsp.

rufula, commonly known as the red-leaved wendlandia, is a striking shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its vibrant red to purple foliage, which stands out against its green stems and leaves.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its unique coloration and floral display.
