Whitfieldia colorata subsp. colorata

Whitfieldia colorata subsp.

colorata, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a preference for sandy or well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
