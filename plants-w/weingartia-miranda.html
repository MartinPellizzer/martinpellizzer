Weingartia miranda

Weingartia miranda, commonly known as the Miranda cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and short, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its foliage consists of spiny, ribbed stems that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Weingartia miranda is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor succulent gardens.
