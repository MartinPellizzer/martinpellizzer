Willoughbya bangii

Willoughbya bangii, commonly known as the Bangi's willoughbya, is a unique monocot native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of small, lantern-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Willoughbya bangii is also notable for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
