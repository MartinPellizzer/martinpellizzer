Wislizenia refracta subsp. californica

Wislizenia refracta subsp.

californica, commonly known as California ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its erect stem and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and contribute to its ecological significance in native plant communities.
