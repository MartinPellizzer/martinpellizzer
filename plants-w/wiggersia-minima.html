Wiggersia minima

Wiggersia minima, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps. The plant has a slender, upright stem with few leaves and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the soil, a feature common in orchids.

Wiggersia minima is notable for its small size and unique floral structure, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
