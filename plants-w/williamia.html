Williamia

Williamia, commonly known as the Williamia tree, is a rare and ancient species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the early spring, followed by edible, bean-like fruits. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from underground sources.

Due to its unique adaptations, the Williamia tree is considered an important indicator of ecological health in its native habitat.
