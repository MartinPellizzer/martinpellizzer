Weingartia langeri

Weingartia langeri, commonly known as the Langer's barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that typically grows to a height of about 15 to 20 centimeters. The plant has a thick, ribbed structure with spines that are often arranged in a spiral pattern. It produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits.

Weingartia langeri is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
