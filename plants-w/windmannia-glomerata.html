Windmannia glomerata

Windmannia glomerata, commonly known as the clustered windmannia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Windmannia glomerata is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
