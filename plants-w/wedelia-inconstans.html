Wedelia inconstans

Wedelia inconstans, commonly known as the variable wedelia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Wedelia inconstans is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
