Waria

Waria, commonly known as the "flower of the sea," is a unique plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in brackish water environments, where it forms dense underwater mats that provide habitat for various marine life. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often blooming in clusters near the water's surface.

Waria plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal sediments and filtering water.
