Wissadula periplocifolia f. macrophylla

Wissadula periplocifolia f. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved wissadula, is a variant of the wissadula plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and leathery leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often used in rock gardens or as a specimen plant.
