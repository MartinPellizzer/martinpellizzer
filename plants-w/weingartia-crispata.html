Weingartia crispata

Weingartia crispata, commonly known as the crassula weingartii, is a species of flowering plant in the family Crassulaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming structure with thick, fleshy leaves. The leaves are typically blue-green in color and have a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
