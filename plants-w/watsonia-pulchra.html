Watsonia pulchra

Watsonia pulchra, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky flower stalks that bear clusters of bright red or orange tubular flowers. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. Watsonia pulchra thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its striking appearance and drought tolerance make it a notable ornamental plant in botanical collections.
