Wurmbea ustulata

Wurmbea ustulata, commonly known as the African star of Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Wurmbea ustulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
