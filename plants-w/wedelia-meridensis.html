Wedelia meridensis

Wedelia meridensis, commonly known as the Mexican butterweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Wedelia meridensis is known for its ability to thrive in sunny, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening.
