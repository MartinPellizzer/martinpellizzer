Wedelia lanceolata

Wedelia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Additionally, it is known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed.
