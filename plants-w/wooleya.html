Wooleya

Wooleya, commonly known as the woolly vine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. Wooleya thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found growing along forest edges or in shaded areas.

Its unique appearance and pleasant scent make it a popular ornamental plant.
