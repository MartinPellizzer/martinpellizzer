Wedelia calycina var. dominicensis

Wedelia calycina var. dominicensis, commonly known as the Dominican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Dominican Republic.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and purple. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
