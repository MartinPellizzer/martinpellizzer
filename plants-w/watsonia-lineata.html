Watsonia lineata

Watsonia lineata, commonly known as the Natal flatland lily, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Watsonia lineata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
