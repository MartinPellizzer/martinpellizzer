Woodsia oregana var. cathcartiana

Woodsia oregana var. cathcartiana, commonly known as the Cathcart's wood fern, is a variant of the wood fern native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a perennial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features intricate, delicate fronds with a distinctive bipinnate structure, often with a dark green coloration. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in association with coniferous trees.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
