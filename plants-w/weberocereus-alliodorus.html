Weberocereus alliodorus

Weberocereus alliodorus, commonly known as the yellow night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habit. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or yellow. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract pollinators such as moths.

The species is well adapted to arid environments, with thick stems that store water and reduce transpiration.
