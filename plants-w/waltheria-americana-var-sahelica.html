Waltheria americana var. sahelica

Waltheria americana var. sahelica, commonly known as the Sahelian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, blooming in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry grasslands.
