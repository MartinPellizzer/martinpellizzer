Wilcoxia poselgeri

Wilcoxia poselgeri, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. This resilient species has adapted to extreme arid conditions by developing a deep root system and reduced leaf surfaces. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, and it blooms during the brief rainy season.

Wilcoxia poselgeri is also noted for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a unique example of desert flora.
