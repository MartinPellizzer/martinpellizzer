Wittmackia turbinocalyx

Wittmackia turbinocalyx, commonly known as the turbinated bellflower, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, turban-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, often with a contrasting throat. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

Wittmackia turbinocalyx is a member of the Campanulaceae family, which includes other bellflower species.
