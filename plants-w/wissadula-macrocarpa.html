Wissadula macrocarpa

Wissadula macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant poinsettia, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its vibrant red and green hues during the holiday season. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.
