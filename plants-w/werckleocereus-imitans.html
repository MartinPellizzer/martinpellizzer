Werckleocereus imitans

Werckleocereus imitans, commonly known as the imitation cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
