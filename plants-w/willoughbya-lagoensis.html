Willoughbya lagoensis

Willoughbya lagoensis, commonly known as the lagoon willow.

It is a flowering plant species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found near water bodies such as lakes and marshes.

Willoughbya lagoensis has a unique ability to tolerate high levels of salinity, making it an important species in coastal and brackish ecosystems.
