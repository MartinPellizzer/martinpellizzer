Weingartia vilcayensis

Weingartia vilcayensis, commonly known as the Vilcayensis Cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and short, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, spiny leaves and clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, with a shallow root system that allows it to efficiently absorb moisture from the soil.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in cactus collections.
