Wedelia almedae

Wedelia almedae, commonly known as the velvetleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Wedelia almedae is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
