Wisteria floribunda f. alba

Wisteria floribunda f. alba, commonly known as the Japanese wisteria, is a cultivar of the native wisteria species.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is a vigorous climber with compound leaves composed of 12-20 oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used as a ornamental vine in gardens.

However, it can be invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
