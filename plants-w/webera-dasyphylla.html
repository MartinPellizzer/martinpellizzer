Webera dasyphylla

Webera dasyphylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Webera dasyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and vibrant blooms.
