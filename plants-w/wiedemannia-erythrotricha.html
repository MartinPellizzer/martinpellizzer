Wiedemannia erythrotricha

Wiedemannia erythrotricha, commonly known as the red-haired fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bright green fronds that often have a reddish hue on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of atmospheric moisture to flourish. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Wiedemannia erythrotricha is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
