Watsonia pottbergensis

Watsonia pottbergensis, commonly known as the Pottberg Watsonia, is a striking South African bulbous plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and vibrant, showy flower spikes that bloom in late winter to early spring. The flowers are typically red, orange, or yellow, with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Native to the Western Cape region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This ornamental plant is prized for its dramatic appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
