Wedelia heterophylla

Wedelia heterophylla, commonly known as the variable wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with leaves ranging from ovate to linear shapes depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
