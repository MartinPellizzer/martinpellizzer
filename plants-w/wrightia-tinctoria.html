Wrightia tinctoria

Wrightia tinctoria, commonly known as the Indian beech or red sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sterculiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Wrightia tinctoria is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been traditionally used in construction and furniture-making.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
