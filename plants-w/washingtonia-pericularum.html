Washingtonia pericularum

Washingtonia pericularum, commonly known as the Mexican fan palm, is a species of palm native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a robust root system that helps it survive in dry conditions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, dark blue berries.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in landscaping due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
