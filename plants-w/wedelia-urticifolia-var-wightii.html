Wedelia urticifolia var. wightii

Wedelia urticifolia var. wightii, commonly known as the Wight's wedelia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant features serrated, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping for its vibrant blooms.
