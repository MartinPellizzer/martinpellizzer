Weihea thomassetii

Weihea thomassetii, commonly known as the Tahitian breadfruit tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the island of Tahiti and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, pale green bark. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fruits that resemble breadfruit. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The plant plays an important role in the traditional agriculture and culture of French Polynesia.
