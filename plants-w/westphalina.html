Westphalina

Westphalina, commonly known as the Westphalian bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Westphalina has lance-shaped leaves and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
