Waltheria virgata

Waltheria virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet. The plant features heart-shaped, silver-veined leaves and produces trumpet-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Waltheria virgata is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
