Wadithamnus artemisioides

Wadithamnus artemisioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf wadithamnus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Wadithamnus artemisioides is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
