Willoughbya obtusata

Willoughbya obtusata, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of northeastern Queensland, Australia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that resembles a bell shape, hence its common name. The plant features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slightly metallic sheen. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinct spathe and spadix structure typical of the Araceae family.

Willoughbya obtusata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor spaces.
