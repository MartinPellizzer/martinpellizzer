Wisteriopsis

Wisteriopsis, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and can grow up to 30 feet in length. Its roots are fibrous and can become invasive if not properly managed.

Wisteriopsis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks.
