Washingtonia thurstonii

Washingtonia thurstonii, commonly known as the Mexican fan palm, is a tall, single-stemmed palm native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a slender trunk with a fibrous, grayish-brown bark and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 5 feet in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in panicles, which are followed by dark blue berries.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
