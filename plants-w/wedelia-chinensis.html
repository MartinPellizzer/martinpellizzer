Wedelia chinensis

Wedelia chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often forming dense mats or low shrubs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil types.
