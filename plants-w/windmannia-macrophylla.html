Windmannia macrophylla

Windmannia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant windmannia, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red and orange, which bloom in clusters at the ends of its branches. Windmannia macrophylla thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Its unique appearance and vibrant flowers make it a sought-after ornamental plant in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
