Wissadula spiciflora

Wissadula spiciflora, commonly known as the spiked stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. Its rosette growth pattern and ability to form offsets make it an attractive and low-maintenance houseplant.

Wissadula spiciflora prefers well-draining soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
