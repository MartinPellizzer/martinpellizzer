Waitzia concolor

Waitzia concolor, commonly known as the smooth-surfaced lichen, is a foliose lichen belonging to the family Parmeliaceae.

It is characterized by its uniform, pale green to yellowish-green thallus with a smooth, leathery surface. The plant lacks distinct rhizines and has a simple, flattened structure that adheres to substrates like bark or rock. It is often found in temperate forest environments, thriving in humid and shaded conditions.

Its chemical composition includes depsidones and other secondary metabolites that contribute to its ecological role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization.
