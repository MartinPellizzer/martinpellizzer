Widdringtonia equisetiformis

Widdringtonia equisetiformis, commonly known as the silverwood tree, is a unique evergreen tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar trunk and feathery, needle-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is known for its slow growth and longevity. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
