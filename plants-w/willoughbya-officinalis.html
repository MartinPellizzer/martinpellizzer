Willoughbya officinalis

Willoughbya officinalis, commonly known as the native lily or the yellow lily, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features strap-shaped, often yellowish-green leaves and produces striking yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive, hairy appearance. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
