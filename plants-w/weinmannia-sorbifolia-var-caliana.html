Weinmannia sorbifolia var. caliana

Weinmannia sorbifolia var. caliana, commonly known as the California weinmannia, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly California.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland scrub habitats.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to dry conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
