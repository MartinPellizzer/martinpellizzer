Wrightia collettii

Wrightia collettii, commonly known as the Collett's wrightia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Wrightiaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Wrightia collettii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
