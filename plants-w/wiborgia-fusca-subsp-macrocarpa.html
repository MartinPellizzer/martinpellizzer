Wiborgia fusca subsp. macrocarpa

Wiborgia fusca subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited wiborgia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant produces showy, yellow to orange composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more robust fruits compared to other members of the species.
