Wittrockia superba

Wittrockia superba, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a large, ancient plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its massive, trunk-like stem and large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, typical of gymnosperms, and is dioecious, meaning male and female reproductive structures are found on separate plants. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun, making it a striking addition to botanical gardens.

Due to its slow growth and conservation status, it is often cultivated for its unique appearance and historical significance.
