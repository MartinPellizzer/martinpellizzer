Wikstroemia fruticosa var. communis

Wikstroemia fruticosa var. communis, commonly known as the common wikstroemia, is a shrub native to the Pacific Islands, including Hawaii, Tahiti, and other parts of Oceania.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to salt-laden air and sandy soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often used in traditional Hawaiian hula dances and has cultural significance in the region.
