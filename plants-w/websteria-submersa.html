Websteria submersa

Websteria submersa, commonly known as the submerged websteria, is a rare aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Southeast Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving or still water, typically in shallow areas with soft, muddy substrates. The plant has elongated, ribbon-like leaves that float just below the water surface, giving it a delicate, submerged appearance. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually occurring in clusters near the water's edge.
