Warneckea bullata

Warneckea bullata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The tree is notable for its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish or brownish hue, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

Warneckea bullata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and along roadsides for its vibrant floral display.
