Watsonia meriana var. platypetala

Watsonia meriana var. platypetala, commonly known as the flat-petaled Watsonia, is a striking South African bulbous plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, strap-like leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The flowers have a distinctive flat, petal-like structure, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is prized for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
