Werneria dombeyana

Werneria dombeyana, commonly known as the Dombey's wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
