Wikstroemia pauciflora var. yakushimensis

Wikstroemia pauciflora var. yakushimensis, commonly known as the Yakushiman Islands wikstroemia, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
