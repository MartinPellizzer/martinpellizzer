Willardia

Willardia, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble a dandelion's head, followed by fluffy white seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is often found in disturbed soils. Willardia has a basal rosette of leaves and produces a hollow, cylindrical stem.

It is a hardy perennial that is native to various regions across the Northern Hemisphere.
