Waltheria involucrata

Waltheria involucrata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.
