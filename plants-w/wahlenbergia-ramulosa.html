Wahlenbergia ramulosa

Wahlenbergia ramulosa, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery bloom. The flowers are typically blue or violet, with five petals and a distinctive spurred shape, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
