Wedelia grayi

Wedelia grayi, commonly known as the gray wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a low, spreading shrub with woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and are arranged in clusters, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and is often used in landscaping for its hardy and drought-tolerant nature.
