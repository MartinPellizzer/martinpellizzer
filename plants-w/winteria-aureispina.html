Winteria aureispina

Winteria aureispina, commonly known as the golden spined winterberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking red berries that persist through the winter, providing food for birds and other wildlife. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that turn yellow in the fall before dropping. Its stems are often covered in small, yellowish spines, giving it its common name.

Winteria aureispina thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its seasonal visual interest.
