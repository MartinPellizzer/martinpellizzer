Wedelia pusilla

Wedelia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf ironweed, is a small flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens and as a ground cover.
