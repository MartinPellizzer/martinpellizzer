Wikstroemia polyantha

Wikstroemia polyantha, commonly known as the Chinese sandmat, is a flowering plant native to China, Korea, and Japan.

It is a shrub that typically grows in forested areas and mountainous regions, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
