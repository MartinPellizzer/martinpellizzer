Wikstroemia chamaedaphne

Wikstroemia chamaedaphne, commonly known as the coastal wikstroemia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal environments with high salinity and wind exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
