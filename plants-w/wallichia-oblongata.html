Wallichia oblongata

Wallichia oblongata, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Wallichia oblongata is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
