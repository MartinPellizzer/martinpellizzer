Wurfbainia microcarpa

Wurfbainia microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf burr acacia, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It has adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with its deep root system and water-storing tissues.

Wurfbainia microcarpa plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
