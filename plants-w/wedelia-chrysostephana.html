Wedelia chrysostephana

Wedelia chrysostephana, commonly known as the golden dewflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, opposite leaves and produces small, seed-like fruits called cypsela. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
