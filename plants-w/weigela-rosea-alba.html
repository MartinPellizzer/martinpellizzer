Weigela rosea alba

Weigela rosea alba, commonly known as the white weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in shades of white to pink. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Weigela rosea alba is often used for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
