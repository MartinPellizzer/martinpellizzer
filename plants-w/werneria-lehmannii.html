Werneria lehmannii

Werneria lehmannii, commonly known as the African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Werneria lehmannii thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires indirect light for optimal growth.
