Werneria minima var. pygmaea

Werneria minima var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf werneria, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, featuring compact foliage and delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with specific humidity and light requirements.
