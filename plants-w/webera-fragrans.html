Webera fragrans

Webera fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-smelling orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and it thrives in warm, moist conditions with indirect sunlight.

The fragrance of its flowers is a notable feature, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
