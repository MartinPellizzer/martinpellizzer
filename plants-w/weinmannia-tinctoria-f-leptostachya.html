Weinmannia tinctoria f. leptostachya

Weinmannia tinctoria f. leptostachya, commonly known as the narrow-stamened weinmannia, is a botanical variant of the weinmannia genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stamens that distinguish it from other forms of the species. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a pale yellow or greenish hue.

This form is of interest in botanical gardens and research due to its unique floral morphology and potential ecological significance.
