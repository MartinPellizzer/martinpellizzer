Weberocereus trichophorus

Weberocereus trichophorus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and is known for its large, showy flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and emit a fragrant scent to attract nocturnal pollinators.

Weberocereus trichophorus is a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
