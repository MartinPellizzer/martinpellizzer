Weinmannia ternata

Weinmannia ternata, commonly known as the silverleaf weinmannia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate and often have a leathery texture.

Weinmannia ternata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
