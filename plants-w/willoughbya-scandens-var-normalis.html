Willoughbya scandens var. normalis

Willoughbya scandens var. normalis, commonly known as the climbing willow.

It is a tropical shrub native to Australia, characterized by its long, flexible stems that can climb or trail along the ground. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in loose inflorescences, typically blooming during the warmer months.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
