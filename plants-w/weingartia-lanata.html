Weingartia lanata

Weingartia lanata, commonly known as the woolly pincushion cactus, is a species native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its fuzzy appearance. It is a slow-growing succulent that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
