Walkera integrifolia

Walkera integrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Walkera integrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
