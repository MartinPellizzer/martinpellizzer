Wollastonia subcordata

Wollastonia subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hopbush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

Wollastonia subcordata is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
