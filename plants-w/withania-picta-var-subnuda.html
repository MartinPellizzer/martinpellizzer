Withania picta var. subnuda

Withania picta var. subnuda, commonly known as the naked Indian ginseng, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan. The plant features yellow flowers and has small, elliptical leaves. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures.

Its botanical significance lies in its potential medicinal properties and ecological role in arid environments.
