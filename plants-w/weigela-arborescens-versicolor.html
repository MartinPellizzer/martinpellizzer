Weigela arborescens-versicolor

Weigela arborescens-versicolor, commonly known as the variable weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white and red. The plant produces small, reddish-brown berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, turning yellow in the fall.

Weigela arborescens-versicolor thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
