Wendlandia membranifolia

Wendlandia membranifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf ironweed, is a striking flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a velvety appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
