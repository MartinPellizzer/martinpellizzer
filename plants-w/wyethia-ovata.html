Wyethia ovata

Wyethia ovata, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink ray flowers and yellow disk flowers that resemble a cone. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces seeds in a distinctive, elongated seed head.

Wyethia ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
