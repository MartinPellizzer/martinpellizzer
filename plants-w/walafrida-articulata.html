Walafrida articulata

Walafrida articulata, commonly known as the articulated walafrid, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its segmented, jointed stems.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant features small, scale-like leaves that are often submerged, with some emergent structures at the water's surface. Its reproductive structures are specialized, with flowers that are adapted for pollination by water-dwelling insects.

Walafrida articulata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to water quality through its biological activity.
