Wikstroemia canescens var. pauciflora

Wikstroemia canescens var. pauciflora, commonly known as the silverleafWikstroemia, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features grayish-white, densely hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in late spring. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a leathery texture.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.
