Wedelia cordifolia

Wedelia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, with some species exhibiting semi-evergreen characteristics.
