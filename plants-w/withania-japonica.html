Withania japonica

Withania japonica, commonly known as the Japanese devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its branched, often spiny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Withania japonica is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
