Wedelia macrorrhiza

Wedelia macrorrhiza, commonly known as the broadleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
