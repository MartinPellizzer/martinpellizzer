Weinmannia guyanensis

Weinmannia guyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana Weinmannia, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small, clustered yellow flowers. Its fruit is a follicle that releases seeds with tufts of hair, aiding in wind dispersal.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
