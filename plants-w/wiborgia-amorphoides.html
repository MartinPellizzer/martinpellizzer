Wiborgia amorphoides

Wiborgia amorphoides, commonly known as the woolly wiborgia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
