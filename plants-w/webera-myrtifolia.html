Webera myrtifolia

Webera myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved webera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that resemble those of myrtle. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
