Weigeltia simplex

Weigeltia simplex, commonly known as the common weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink or red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. Weigeltia simplex is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display and hardy nature.
