Wurmbea monopetala

Wurmbea monopetala, commonly known as the single-petaled arum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces a single, strikingly shaped flower with a spathe that is typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
