Wyethia scabra var. canescens

Wyethia scabra var. canescens, commonly known as the rough wyethia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rough texture.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
