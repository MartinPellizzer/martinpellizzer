Wedelia jacquinii subsp. caracasana

Wedelia jacquinii subsp.

caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas wedelia, is a flowering plant native to South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows in a creeping habit, often forming dense mats. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in moist, sunny habitats such as streambanks and forest edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
