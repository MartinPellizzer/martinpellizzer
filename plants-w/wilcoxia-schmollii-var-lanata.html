Wilcoxia schmollii var. lanata

Wilcoxia schmollii var. lanata, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation. Its unique morphology includes a basal rosette structure that helps conserve moisture.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role in stabilizing desert soils and its potential for medicinal uses.
