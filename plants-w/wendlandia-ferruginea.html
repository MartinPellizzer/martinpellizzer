Wendlandia ferruginea

Wendlandia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty wendlandia, is a striking flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a reddish-brown underside, giving it its common name. It produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Wendlandia ferruginea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
