Weinmannia ovalis f. equatoriensis

Weinmannia ovalis f. equatoriensis, commonly known as the Equatorial Weinmannia, is a variant of the Weinmannia ovalis species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in dense inflorescences, and are often pink or red in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
