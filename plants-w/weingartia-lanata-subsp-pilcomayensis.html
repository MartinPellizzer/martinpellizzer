Weingartia lanata subsp. pilcomayensis

Weingartia lanata subsp.

pilcomayensis, commonly known as the Pilcomayo prickly pear, is a small, slow-growing cactus native to Argentina. It features a compact, globular stem covered in white, woolly spines that provide insulation and protection.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
