Woodfordia uniflora

Woodfordia uniflora, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, large, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces showy, solitary blooms at the ends of its branches, which appear in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Woodfordia uniflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
