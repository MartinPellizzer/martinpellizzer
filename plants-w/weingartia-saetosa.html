Weingartia saetosa

Weingartia saetosa, commonly known as the spiny barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in sharp, spiny areoles. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to dry environments.

Weingartia saetosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
