Willoughbya candolleana

Willoughbya candolleana, commonly known as the candolleana willow or the tropical willow, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to the wet tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often glossy and arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate beauty to its appearance. Willoughbya candolleana thrives in humid, well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and flowering habit make it a valuable addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
