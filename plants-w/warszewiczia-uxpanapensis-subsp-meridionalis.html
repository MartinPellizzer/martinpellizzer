Warszewiczia uxpanapensis subsp. meridionalis

Warszewiczia uxpanapensis subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the Mexican ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Mexico. It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, compound leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by legume-like pods containing seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low rainfall.
