Weinmannia purpurea

Weinmannia purpurea, commonly known as the purple weinmannia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Weinmanniaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, pinkish-purple inflorescences that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a leathery texture, which helps it adapt to arid environments.

Weinmannia purpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
