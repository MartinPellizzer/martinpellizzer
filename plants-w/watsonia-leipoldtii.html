Watsonia leipoldtii

Watsonia leipoldtii, commonly known as the Natal grassblume, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of vividly colored blooms, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow, during the summer months. Watsonia leipoldtii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
