Wedelia mexicana

Wedelia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican trinity, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a low-growing shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

Wedelia mexicana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
