Wisteria frutescens var. nivea

Wisteria frutescens var. nivea, commonly known as the panicle wisteria, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, cascading panicles of white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate structure, consisting of 12 to 20 pairs of ovate leaflets. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, often reaching heights of 15 to 20 feet.

The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
