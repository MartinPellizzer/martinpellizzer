Wrightia siamensis

Wrightia siamensis, commonly known as the Siam wrightia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Wrightiaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
