Wikstroemia turcomanica

Wikstroemia turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its woody stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Wikstroemia turcomanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
