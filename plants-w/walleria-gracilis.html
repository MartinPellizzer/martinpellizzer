Walleria gracilis

Walleria gracilis, commonly known as the slender wallery, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal areas. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and range in color from white to pale pink.

Walleria gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
