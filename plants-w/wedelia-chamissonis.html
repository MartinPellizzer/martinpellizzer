Wedelia chamissonis

Wedelia chamissonis, commonly known as the creeping oxeye, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that features dense, mat-forming foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with white centers, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
