Walsura piscidia

Walsura piscidia, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a thick, buttressed trunk and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaf arrangement and the presence of stipules.

Walsura piscidia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to humid, tropical climates.
