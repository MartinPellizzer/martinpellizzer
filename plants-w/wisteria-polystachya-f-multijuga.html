Wisteria polystachya f. multijuga

Wisteria polystachya f. multijuga, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a flowering vine native to East Asia.

It features long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with oval, serrated leaflets and produces woody, twisting stems that can climb to great heights. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

However, it can be invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
