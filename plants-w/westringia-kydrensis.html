Westringia kydrensis

Westringia kydrensis, commonly known as the coastal westringia, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a drought-tolerant appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Westringia kydrensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand salty air and coastal conditions.
