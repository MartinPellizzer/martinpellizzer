Walsholaria calcarea

Walsholaria calcarea, commonly known as the limestone rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
