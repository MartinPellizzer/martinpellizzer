Willoughbya myriocephala

Willoughbya myriocephala, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and orange, often appearing during the Christmas season. The plant has a short, thick stem with broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green. It produces small, red fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, orange aril.

Willoughbya myriocephala is primarily grown as an ornamental plant due to its exotic appearance and seasonal bloom.
