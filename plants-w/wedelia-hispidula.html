Wedelia hispidula

Wedelia hispidula, commonly known as hairy wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
