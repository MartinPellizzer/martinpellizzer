Wageneria glabra

Wageneria glabra, commonly known as the smooth wageneria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually small and yellow, arranged in compact clusters, and it produces small, dry seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
