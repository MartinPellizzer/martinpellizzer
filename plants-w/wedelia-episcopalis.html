Wedelia episcopalis

Wedelia episcopalis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or bishop's flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its attractive foliage and blooms.
