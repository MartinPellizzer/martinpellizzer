Wahlenbergia marginata var. grandiflora

Wahlenbergia marginata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered bluebell, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family native to southern Australia.

This perennial herb features slender, upright stems and produces showy, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The flowers are bell-shaped, with five spreading lobes, and are borne in loose clusters along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
