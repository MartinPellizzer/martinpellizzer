Wisteria australis

Wisteria australis, commonly known as the Australian wisteria, is a climbing vine native to southeastern Australia.

It produces long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its woody stems can grow quite long, often reaching several meters in height.

Wisteria australis is valued for its ornamental beauty but can be invasive in certain regions.
