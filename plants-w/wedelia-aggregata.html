Wedelia aggregata

Wedelia aggregata, commonly known as the silver bush or groundsel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Wedelia aggregata is often used in landscaping for erosion control due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
