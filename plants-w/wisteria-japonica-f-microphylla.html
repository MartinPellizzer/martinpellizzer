Wisteria japonica f. microphylla

Wisteria japonica f. microphylla, commonly known as the Japanese wisteria with small leaves, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems that can grow up to 30 feet in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pendant flowers that range in color from pale lavender to deep violet. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a pointed shape with a smooth margin.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to climb trellises or arbors.
