Wollastonia montana

Wollastonia montana, commonly known as the mountain wattle, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions. Wollastonia montana plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native fauna.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for cultivation in suitable climates.
