Wurmbea biglandulosa subsp. biglandulosa

Wurmbea biglandulosa subsp.

biglandulosa, commonly known as the broad-leaved African star-of-Bethlehem, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa. It features striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in prominent, yellowish glands.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to naturalize and form dense, decorative foliage.
