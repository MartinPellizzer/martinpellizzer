Wenderothia hirsuta

Wenderothia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy wenderothia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Wenderothia hirsuta is often found in open vegetation types and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
