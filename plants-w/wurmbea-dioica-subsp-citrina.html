Wurmbea dioica subsp. citrina

Wurmbea dioica subsp.

citrina, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa. It features showy, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow, typically blooming in late spring or early summer.

The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are often bluish-green in appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
