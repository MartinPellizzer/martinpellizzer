Wedelia camporum

Wedelia camporum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, silky hair that gives them a soft texture.

Wedelia camporum is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
