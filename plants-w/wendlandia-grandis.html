Wendlandia grandis

Wendlandia grandis, commonly known as the giant hybanthus, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Wendlandia grandis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
