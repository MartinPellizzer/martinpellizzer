Wallichia porphyracea

Wallichia porphyracea, commonly known as the red-stemmed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite and often have a velvety texture, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Wallichia porphyracea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
