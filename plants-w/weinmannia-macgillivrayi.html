Weinmannia macgillivrayi

Weinmannia macgillivrayi, commonly known as the MacGillivray's weinmannia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Weinmanniaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California, where it thrives in sandy soils and rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth form, with small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pink, arranged in clusters, and are adapted for wind pollination.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in coastal gardens.
