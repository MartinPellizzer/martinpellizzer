Warneria thunbergia

Warneria thunbergia, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or herb, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are zygomorphic, with five petals, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Warneria thunbergia is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
