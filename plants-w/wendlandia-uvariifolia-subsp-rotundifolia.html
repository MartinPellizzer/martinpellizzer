Wendlandia uvariifolia subsp. rotundifolia

Wendlandia uvariifolia subsp.

rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a contrasting throat.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and rounded, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in cooler climates and its long-lasting blooms.
