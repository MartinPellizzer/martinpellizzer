Wissadula periplocifolia var. genuina

Wissadula periplocifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value. Its leaves are thick and rosette-shaped, while its flowers bloom in clusters at the stem tips.

The species is well adapted to humid conditions and requires careful watering to thrive.
