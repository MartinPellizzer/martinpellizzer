Werneria pumila var. pinifolia

Werneria pumila var. pinifolia, commonly known as the dwarf snowbell, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate ornamental touch to its foliage. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that remain evergreen throughout the year, contributing to its aesthetic appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
