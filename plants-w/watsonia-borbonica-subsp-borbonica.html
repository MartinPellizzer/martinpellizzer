Watsonia borbonica subsp. borbonica

Watsonia borbonica subsp.

borbonica, commonly known as the Natal grassblume, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features tall, grass-like leaves and produces striking, spike-like inflorescences with bright red or orange flowers.

The plant is characterized by its robust growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice among gardeners and botanists alike.
