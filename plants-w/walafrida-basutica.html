Walafrida basutica

Walafrida basutica, commonly known as the Basutu grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

Walafrida basutica is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its hardy nature and adaptability to arid conditions.
