Whiteheadia latifolia

Whiteheadia latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved whitehead, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped white flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It typically grows in coastal heathlands and is known for its early spring blooming period.

Whiteheadia latifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
