Weingartia pulquinensis

Weingartia pulquinensis, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water efficiently. The plant produces spiny, paddle-shaped stems and blooms with yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it thrives in full sun with minimal water.

Weingartia pulquinensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience in dry climates.
