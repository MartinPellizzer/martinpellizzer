Widdringtonia nodiflora

Widdringtonia nodiflora, commonly known as the yellowwood tree, is a tall evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale yellow bark and its long, narrow leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late winter or early spring. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a sought-after material for furniture and construction.

Widdringtonia nodiflora is also an important species for biodiversity, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
