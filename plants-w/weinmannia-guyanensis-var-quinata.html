Weinmannia guyanensis var. quinata

Weinmannia guyanensis var. quinata, commonly known as the Guyana Weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by distinctive, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
