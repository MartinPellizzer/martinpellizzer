Willughbeia angustifolia var. gracilior

Willughbeia angustifolia var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender willughbeia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its fruits are three-winged berries that mature to a dark purple color.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along stream banks.
