Wisteria frutescens subsp. frutescens

Wisteria frutescens subsp.

frutescens, commonly known as the Southern wisteria, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States. It features long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has compound leaves with 12 to 20 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. It produces woody, twisting stems that can climb to great heights, often reaching several meters.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular ornamental plant, though it can be invasive in some regions.
