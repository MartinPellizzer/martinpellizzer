Wollastonia biflora var. biflora

Wollastonia biflora var. biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered wattle, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland regions. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
