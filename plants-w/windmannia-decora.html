Windmannia decora

Windmannia decora, commonly known as the decorative windmannia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. Its flowers, which come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, are borne in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Windmannia decora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
