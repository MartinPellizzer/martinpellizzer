Weberbauera lechleri

Weberbauera lechleri, commonly known as the rubber tree, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces a milky latex sap, which has been historically used for making rubber. Its growth habit includes a tall, slender trunk with a dense canopy of foliage.

The botanical features of Weberbauera lechleri make it an important component of the rainforest ecosystem.
