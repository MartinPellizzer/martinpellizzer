Wikstroemia palustris

Wikstroemia palustris, commonly known as swamp wikstroemia, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist or swampy soils, often near rivers and streams. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Wikstroemia palustris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging wetland environments.
