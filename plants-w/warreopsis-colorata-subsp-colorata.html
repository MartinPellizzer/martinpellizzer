Warreopsis colorata subsp. colorata

Warreopsis colorata subsp.

colorata, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
