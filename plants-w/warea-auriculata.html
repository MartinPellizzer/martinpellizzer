Warea auriculata

Warea auriculata, commonly known as the golden warea, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in forested areas and has a distinctive, clustered growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while the flowers are borne in dense inflorescences.

Warea auriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
