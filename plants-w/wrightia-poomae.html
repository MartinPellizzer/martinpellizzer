Wrightia poomae

Wrightia poomae, commonly known as the Pooma wrightia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Wrightiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a small shrub or tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

Wrightia poomae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
