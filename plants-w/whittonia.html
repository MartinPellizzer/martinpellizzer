Whittonia

Whittonia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plants typically grow as shrubs or small trees, often with a compact, bushy form. They are popular as ornamental plants due to their vibrant colors and ability to thrive in indoor environments.

Whittonia species are also valued for their medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
