Wurmbea angustifolia

Wurmbea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved African lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-shaped, narrow leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Wurmbea angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
