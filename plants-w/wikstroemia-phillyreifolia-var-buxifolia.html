Wikstroemia phillyreifolia var. buxifolia

Wikstroemia phillyreifolia var. buxifolia, commonly known as the Buxus-leaved Wikstroemia, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are thick and leathery, resembling those of the genus Buxus. The plant produces clusters of tiny, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often exuding a sticky resin when damaged.

This species is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, typically found in coastal forests and mesic habitats.
