Weinmannia parvifolia

Weinmannia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved weinmannia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its unique floral structure and adaptability to coastal conditions make it a notable species in its native habitat.

Weinmannia parvifolia is often found in sandy soils and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
