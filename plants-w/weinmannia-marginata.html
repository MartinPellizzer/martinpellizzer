Weinmannia marginata

Weinmannia marginata, commonly known as the silver leaf weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and often have a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss in its native habitat.

Weinmannia marginata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
