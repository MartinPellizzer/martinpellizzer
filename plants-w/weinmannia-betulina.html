Weinmannia betulina

Weinmannia betulina, commonly known as the silverleaf weinmannia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the coastlines. The plant features small, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are usually white or pale pink, attracting various pollinators.

The species is noted for its adaptability to coastal conditions and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
