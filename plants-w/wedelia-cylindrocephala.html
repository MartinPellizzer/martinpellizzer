Wedelia cylindrocephala

Wedelia cylindrocephala, commonly known as the cylindrical wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a perennial herb with a sprawling growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, arranged in inflorescences called cymes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for ground cover in moist, shaded environments.
