Woodsia microsora

Woodsia microsora, commonly known as the small-leaf woodsia, is a fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in strobila. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Woodsia microsora is native to regions of eastern North America and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
