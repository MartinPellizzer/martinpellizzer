Wedelia bakeri

Wedelia bakeri, commonly known as the narrowleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Wedelia bakeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
