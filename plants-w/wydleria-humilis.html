Wydleria humilis

Wydleria humilis, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to cream-colored flowers, which bloom in spring. Wydleria humilis is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a resilient species in its native habitat.
