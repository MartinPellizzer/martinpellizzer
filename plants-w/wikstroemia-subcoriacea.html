Wikstroemia subcoriacea

Wikstroemia subcoriacea, commonly known as the rough bark tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub with a distinctive rough, fissured bark that becomes more pronounced with age. The plant produces small, fragrant white or yellowish flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with a slightly rough surface.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
