Wedelia lundii

Wedelia lundii, commonly known as the yellow wedgeflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Wedelia lundii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
