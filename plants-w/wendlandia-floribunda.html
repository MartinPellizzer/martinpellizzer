Wendlandia floribunda

Wendlandia floribunda, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Wendlandia floribunda is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and long blooming period.
