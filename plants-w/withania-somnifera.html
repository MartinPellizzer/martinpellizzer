Withania somnifera

Withania somnifera, commonly known as ashwagandha, is an herbaceous plant native to India, North Africa, and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its yellow-orange berries and branched stems, with small, white flowers that bloom in summer. Its roots and berries are the primary parts used in traditional medicine.

The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in diverse climates.
