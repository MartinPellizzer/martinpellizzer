Wendia siifolia

Wendia siifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf wendia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-blue leaves that are velvety to the touch. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Wendia siifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and aesthetic appeal.
