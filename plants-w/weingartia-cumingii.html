Weingartia cumingii

Weingartia cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's barrel cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that stores water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus enthusiasts.
