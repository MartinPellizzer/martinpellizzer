Westringia parvifolia

Westringia parvifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf rock rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with slender, aromatic leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and mauve, which bloom throughout the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought make it a resilient addition to many gardens.
