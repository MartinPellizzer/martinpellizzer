Wendlandia uvariifolia subsp. yunnanensis

Wendlandia uvariifolia subsp.

yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan flameflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to China. It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble flames, with bright orange to red flowers.

The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique floral display and adaptability to various climates contribute to its appeal among horticulturists.
