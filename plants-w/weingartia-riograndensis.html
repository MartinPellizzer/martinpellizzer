Weingartia riograndensis

Weingartia riograndensis, commonly known as the Rio Grande Barrel Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed stem that can store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its spines are typically short and white, providing protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding a striking contrast to its green body.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
