Wedelia phyllostachya

Wedelia phyllostachya, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ability to spread rapidly makes it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed in some areas.
