Wedelia rubra

Wedelia rubra, commonly known as red wedgeflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Wedelia rubra is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to suppress weeds.
