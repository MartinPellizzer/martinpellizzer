Weingartia corroana

Weingartia corroana, commonly known as the coral cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and its clusters of short, spiny stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a shallow root system that allows it to absorb moisture efficiently.

Weingartia corroana is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
