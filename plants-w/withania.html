Withania

Withania, commonly known as ashwagandha, is an herbaceous plant native to India, North Africa, and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which also includes tomatoes and peppers, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and red, berry-like fruits. The plant is known for its adaptogenic properties and is widely used in traditional medicine. Its roots and berries contain bioactive compounds such as withanolides, which contribute to its medicinal value.

Withania thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.
