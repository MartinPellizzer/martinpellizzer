Wrightia spanogheana

Wrightia spanogheana, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Wrightiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often fissured bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in axillary or terminal inflorescences. Wrightia spanogheana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riverine habitats.
