Widdringtonia cupressoides

Widdringtonia cupressoides, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a coniferous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a sought-after material for furniture and construction.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to drought conditions.
