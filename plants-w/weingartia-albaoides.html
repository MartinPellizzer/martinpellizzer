Weingartia albaoides

Weingartia albaoides, commonly known as the White-Furred Barrel Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. It is a slow-growing, drought-tolerant plant that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
