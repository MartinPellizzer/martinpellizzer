Wissadula grisebachii

Wissadula grisebachii, commonly known as the Grisebach's rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous or coastal regions. The plant has small, clustered leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is well adapted to arid conditions and can tolerate periods of drought.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
