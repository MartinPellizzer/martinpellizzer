Westphalina macrocarpa

Westphalina macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited pomegranate, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant red, fleshy fruits that are rich in seeds and have a high concentration of antioxidants. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are typically pink or red.

The botanical features of Westphalina macrocarpa make it both ornamental and potentially useful for culinary or medicinal purposes.
