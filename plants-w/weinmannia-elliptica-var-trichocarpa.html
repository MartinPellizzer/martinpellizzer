Weinmannia elliptica var. trichocarpa

Weinmannia elliptica var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the velvetleaf weinmannia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
