Wedelia tegetis

Wedelia tegetis, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually elliptical, with a silvery or grayish underside that gives it its common name.

Wedelia tegetis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
