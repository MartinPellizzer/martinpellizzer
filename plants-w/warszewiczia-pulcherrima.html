Warszewiczia pulcherrima

Warszewiczia pulcherrima, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in Mediterranean climates.
