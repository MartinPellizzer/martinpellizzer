Wurmbea stricta

Wurmbea stricta, commonly known as the African lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a stout, underground bulb and strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
