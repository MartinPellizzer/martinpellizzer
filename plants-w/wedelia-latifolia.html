Wedelia latifolia

Wedelia latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems and heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically arranged in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
