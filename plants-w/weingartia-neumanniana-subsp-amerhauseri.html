Weingartia neumanniana subsp. amerhauseri

Weingartia neumanniana subsp.

amerhauseri, commonly known as the American Weingartia, is a compact cactus native to southern Mexico. It features a short, cylindrical stem with a smooth, pale green surface and is adorned with clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring.

The plant is characterized by its spiny areoles and produces small, oval-shaped fruits. It is well-suited for rock gardens and requires well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its slow growth rate, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
