Wikstroemia fruticosa var. sericea

Wikstroemia fruticosa var. sericea, commonly known as silverleafWikstroemia, is a shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, contributing to its silvery sheen. It produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale yellow.

This plant thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, showing adaptability to saline and well-drained soils.
