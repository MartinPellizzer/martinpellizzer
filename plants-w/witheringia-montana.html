Witheringia montana

Witheringia montana, commonly known as the mountain witheringplant, is a perennial herb native to the high elevations of the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, which bloom in late summer. Witheringia montana is adapted to cool, moist environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
