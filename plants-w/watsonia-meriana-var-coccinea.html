Watsonia meriana var. coccinea

Watsonia meriana var. coccinea, commonly known as the red Watsonia, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically a vibrant red or orange-red, with a distinctive yellow or orange throat, adding dramatic visual appeal. The plant produces sword-like, grassy leaves that form a basal rosette, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to care for once established.
