Wasabia tenuis

Wasabia tenuis, commonly known as the slender wasabi, is a rare and lesser-known member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Japan, where it thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers and has elongated, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. Its root, similar to true wasabi, contains compounds that contribute to its pungent aroma and medicinal properties.

Due to its limited habitat and cultivation challenges, it is considered a rare and valuable plant in botanical and culinary contexts.
