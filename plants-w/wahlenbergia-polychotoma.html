Wahlenbergia polychotoma

Wahlenbergia polychotoma, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.

Wahlenbergia polychotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
