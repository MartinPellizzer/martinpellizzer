Wendlandia laevigata

Wendlandia laevigata, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pentagonal leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, blooming in the late afternoon. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Wendlandia laevigata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
