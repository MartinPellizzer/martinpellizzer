Withania grisea

Withania grisea, commonly known as the pale Indian ginseng, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny, black seeds.

The botanical features of Withania grisea make it well adapted to survive in dry, hot environments.
