Weinmannia wercklei

Weinmannia wercklei, commonly known as the silverleaf weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, white hairs on the leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its woody stems are often covered with a thick, waxy cuticle that helps it conserve water in dry conditions.

Weinmannia wercklei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
