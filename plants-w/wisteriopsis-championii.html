Wisteriopsis championii

Wisteriopsis championii, commonly known as the Champion's wisteria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Wisteriopsis championii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
