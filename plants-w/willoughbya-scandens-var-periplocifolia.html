Willoughbya scandens var. periplocifolia

Willoughbya scandens var. periplocifolia, commonly known as the silky willow-herb, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
