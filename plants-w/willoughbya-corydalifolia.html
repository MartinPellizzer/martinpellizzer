Willoughbya corydalifolia

Willoughbya corydalifolia, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has a short, woody stem and produces small, clustered leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom during the winter months, giving the plant its festive common name.
