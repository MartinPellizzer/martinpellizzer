Withania mollis

Withania mollis, commonly known as the Indian Winter cherry, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Withania mollis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
