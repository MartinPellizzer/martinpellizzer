Wisteriopsis japonica var. alborosea

Wisteriopsis japonica var. alborosea, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese wisteria, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement, and its stems are woody and often twining. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
