Woodsia polystichoides f. sinuata

Woodsia polystichoides f. sinuata, commonly known as the Sinuate Woodsia, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, finely divided, sinuous leaves.

It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Japan. The fronds are typically dark green, with a leathery texture and a delicate, feathery appearance. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its unique leaf morphology makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and botanical study.
