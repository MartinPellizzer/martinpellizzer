Wrightia annamensis

Wrightia annamensis, commonly known as the Annam Wrightia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Wrightiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
