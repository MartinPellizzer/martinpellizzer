Weigeltia silvestris

Weigeltia silvestris, commonly known as the wild weigelt, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, showy flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Weigeltia silvestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
