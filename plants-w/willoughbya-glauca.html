Willoughbya glauca

Willoughbya glauca, commonly known as the blue willow tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. Willoughbya glauca thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
