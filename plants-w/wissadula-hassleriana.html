Wissadula hassleriana

Wissadula hassleriana, commonly known as the Hassler's ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as farina. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Wissadula hassleriana is drought-tolerant and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in arid landscapes.
