Willdenowia xerophila

Willdenowia xerophila, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant has adapted to harsh, dry environments through its thick, waxy leaves and deep root system. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Willdenowia xerophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
