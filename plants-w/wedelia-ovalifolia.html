Wedelia ovalifolia

Wedelia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The leaves are oval-shaped, hence the species name, and are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for ground cover.
