Wedelia bonplandiana

Wedelia bonplandiana, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its ability to form a dense ground cover.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
