Wollemiaster

Wollemiaster, commonly known as the Wollemi pine, is a rare and ancient coniferous tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, feathery foliage that resembles that of a fern. The plant has a unique reproductive system, producing both male and female cones on the same tree. Its botanical significance lies in its evolutionary history, as it is one of the few surviving members of the extinct Wollemiaceae family.

Due to its rarity and historical importance, it is highly valued in botanical research and conservation efforts.
