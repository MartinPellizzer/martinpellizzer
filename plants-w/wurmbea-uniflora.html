Wurmbea uniflora

Wurmbea uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while the spathe is usually a deep purple or red color.

Wurmbea uniflora is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique and striking floral display.
