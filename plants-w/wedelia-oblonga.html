Wedelia oblonga

Wedelia oblonga, commonly known as the narrowleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a low shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in clusters, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Wedelia oblonga is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
