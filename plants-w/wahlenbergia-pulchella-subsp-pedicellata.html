Wahlenbergia pulchella subsp. pedicellata

Wahlenbergia pulchella subsp.

pedicellata, commonly known as the purple-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern South America. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of tubular, violet or purple flowers.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
