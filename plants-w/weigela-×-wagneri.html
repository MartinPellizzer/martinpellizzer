Weigela × wagneri

Weigela × wagneri, commonly known as the Wagner's weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy texture and turn yellow in the fall.

Weigela × wagneri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
