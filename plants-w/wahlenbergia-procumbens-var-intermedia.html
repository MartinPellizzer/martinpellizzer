Wahlenbergia procumbens var. intermedia

Wahlenbergia procumbens var. intermedia, commonly known as the spreading bluebell, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to southern Australia.

It features slender, prostrate stems that bear small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
