Wigandia crispa

Wigandia crispa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or spiderplant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant has palmate leaves that are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Wigandia crispa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
