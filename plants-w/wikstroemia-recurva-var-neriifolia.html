Wikstroemia recurva var. neriifolia

Wikstroemia recurva var. neriifolia, commonly known as the purple sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
