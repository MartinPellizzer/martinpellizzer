Waltheria terminalis

Waltheria terminalis, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Waltheria terminalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
