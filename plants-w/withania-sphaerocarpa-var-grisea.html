Withania sphaerocarpa var. grisea

Withania sphaerocarpa var. grisea, commonly known as the gray-fruited Indian tobacco, is a variant of the Withania genus native to parts of India and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces spherical, grayish fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and covered with fine, soft hairs.

This species is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
