Weinmannia pauciflora

Weinmannia pauciflora, commonly known as the silverleaf weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense layers of fine, hair-like trichomes covering the leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in coastal environments with high salt exposure and well-drained soils.

Weinmannia pauciflora is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to coastal conditions.
