Wurmbea triquetra

Wurmbea triquetra, commonly known as the three-cornered arum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of arum species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Wurmbea triquetra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage.
