Weihea gerrardii

Weihea gerrardii, commonly known as the Tahitian breadfruit tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Society Islands of French Polynesia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, starchy fruits resembling bread. The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
