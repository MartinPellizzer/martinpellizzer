Walsura pubescens

Walsura pubescens, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and covered with fine pubescence. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody pods containing edible, sour-tasting seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
