Witheringia dichotoma

Witheringia dichotoma, commonly known as the narrowleaf witheringia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Witheringia dichotoma is often found in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
