Westringia glabra

Westringia glabra, commonly known as silverleaf westringia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its silver-gray foliage and fragrant, pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.

Westringia glabra is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
