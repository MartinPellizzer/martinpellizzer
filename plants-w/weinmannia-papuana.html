Weinmannia papuana

Weinmannia papuana, commonly known as the Papuan weinmannia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Weinmanniaceae.

It is native to the highlands of New Guinea and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically visited by various pollinators.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
