Woodfordia fruticosa var. punctata

Woodfordia fruticosa var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted woodfordia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of pink or lilac flowers that bloom during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its ornamental value is enhanced by the contrasting white spots on the petals, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The species is well-suited for cultivation in warm climates and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
