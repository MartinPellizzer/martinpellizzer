Warneckea cauliflora

Warneckea cauliflora, commonly known as the African cauliflower tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white or cream-colored flowers that are clustered at the base of the branches, a feature known as cauliflory. The plant has a dense canopy of dark green, leathery leaves and produces small, round, yellowish fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
