Wendlandia uvariifolia subsp. pilosa

Wendlandia uvariifolia subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as the woolly wendlandia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its unique texture and vibrant blooms.
