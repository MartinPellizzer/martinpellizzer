Wyethia angustifolia var. foliosa

Wyethia angustifolia var. foliosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf wyethia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a dense, cylindrical inflorescence. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Botanically, it is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.
