Wigandia caracasana var. calycina

Wigandia caracasana var. calycina, commonly known as the Calycine Wigandia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and alternate, ovate leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its flowers are followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

It is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
