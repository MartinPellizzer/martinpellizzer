Woodsia ilvensis var. taigischensis

Woodsia ilvensis var. taigischensis, commonly known as the Siberian wood fern, is a variant of the wood fern genus native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system that produces sterile fronds and fertile spore-bearing structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are pinnately divided and have a distinctive, delicate appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
