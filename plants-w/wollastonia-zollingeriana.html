Wollastonia zollingeriana

Wollastonia zollingeriana, commonly known as the Zollinger's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, yellow-green foliage and is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
