Weingartia pasopayana

Weingartia pasopayana, commonly known as the Pasopaya weingartia, is a species of cactus native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable specimen in the world of cacti.
