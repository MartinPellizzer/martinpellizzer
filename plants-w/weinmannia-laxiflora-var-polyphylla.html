Weinmannia laxiflora var. polyphylla

Weinmannia laxiflora var. polyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved weinmannia, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Paraguay.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and leathery, with a glossy surface, and the plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. It is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
