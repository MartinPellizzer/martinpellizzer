Wikstroemia fruticosa var. microphylla

Wikstroemia fruticosa var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved sandmat, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to sandy or well-drained soils.

It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its drought-tolerant characteristics.
