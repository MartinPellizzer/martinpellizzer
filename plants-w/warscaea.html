Warscaea

Warscaea, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Warscaea thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its fibrous stems and roots have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
