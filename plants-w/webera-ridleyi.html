Webera ridleyi

Webera ridleyi, commonly known as the Malayan spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like flower structure, which features long, slender petals and a distinctive, twisted column. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the dense canopy of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Webera ridleyi is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
