Weingartia backebergiana

Weingartia backebergiana, commonly known as the Backeberg prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in short, stiff spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
