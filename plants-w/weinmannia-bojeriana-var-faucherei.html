Weinmannia bojeriana var. faucherei

Weinmannia bojeriana var. faucherei, commonly known as the Faucherei weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white to pale pink seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its unique foliage and ornamental seed heads make it a popular choice for botanical collections and landscape design.
