Wootonia

Wootonia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plants typically have hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Wootonia species are often found in moist, woodland environments.

They are valued for their ornamental beauty and are sometimes cultivated in gardens.
