Weingartia fischeriana

Weingartia fischeriana, commonly known as the Fischer's Barrel Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that is typically covered in a layer of white, powdery wax. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in arid gardens.
