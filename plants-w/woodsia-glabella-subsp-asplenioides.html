Woodsia glabella subsp. asplenioides

Woodsia glabella subsp.

asplenioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf woodsia, is a fern species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 cm in length.

The plant produces spore-bearing clusters called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and often sought-after species among fern enthusiasts.
