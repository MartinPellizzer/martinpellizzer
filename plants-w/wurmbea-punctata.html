Wurmbea punctata

Wurmbea punctata, commonly known as the starry arum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered around the base of the spathe. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped and have a glossy, dark green surface.

Wurmbea punctata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
