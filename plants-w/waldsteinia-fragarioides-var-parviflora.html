Waldsteinia fragarioides var. parviflora

Waldsteinia fragarioides var. parviflora, commonly known as the slenderleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
