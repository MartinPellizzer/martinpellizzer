Wichuraea dulcis var. cruikshanksii

Wichuraea dulcis var. cruikshanksii, commonly known as the Trinidadian star apple, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into fleshy, star-shaped fruits. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic pulp with a texture similar to that of a star fruit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
