Withania micrantha

Withania micrantha, commonly known as ashwagandha, is an evergreen shrub native to India, Africa, and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and red-orange berries that contain the seeds. The plant is known for its adaptogenic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries. Its leaves and roots are often harvested for their medicinal and nutritional value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and peppers.
