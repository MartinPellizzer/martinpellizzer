Withania kansuensis

Withania kansuensis, commonly known as the Chinese ashwagandha, is a perennial herb native to China and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that contain the alkaloid withanone, which is responsible for its medicinal properties. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its adaptability and bioactive compounds, it is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
