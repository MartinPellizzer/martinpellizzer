Weinmannia nebularum

Weinmannia nebularum, commonly known as the cloud fern, is a unique plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like foliage that resembles a cloud due to its soft, feathery appearance. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions, often growing in rocky or alpine regions. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind.

Despite its delicate appearance, Weinmannia nebularum is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
