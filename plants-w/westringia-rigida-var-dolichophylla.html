Westringia rigida var. dolichophylla

Westringia rigida var. dolichophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf westringia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, dark green leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a valuable addition to native gardens.
