Weberbauerocereus albus

Weberbauerocereus albus, commonly known as the white cactus or white night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its leaves are typically modified into spines, and it thrives in arid environments with well-draining soil.
