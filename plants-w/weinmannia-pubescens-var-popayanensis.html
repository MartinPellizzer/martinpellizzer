Weinmannia pubescens var. popayanensis

Weinmannia pubescens var. popayanensis, commonly known as the Popayan Weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and compound leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
