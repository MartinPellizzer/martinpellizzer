Wissadula parviflora

Wissadula parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features evergreen foliage with dark green, leathery leaves that have a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Wissadula parviflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
