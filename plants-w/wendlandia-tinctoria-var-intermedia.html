Wendlandia tinctoria var. intermedia

Wendlandia tinctoria var. intermedia, commonly known as the rainbow plant, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, colorful bracts in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features showy inflorescences that resemble flowers but are actually modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil.

This ornamental plant is popular in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting display.
