Wrightia laevis subsp. millgar

Wrightia laevis subsp.

millgar, commonly known as the Mexican white ash, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico. It belongs to the family Sterculiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly hairy leaves and smaller flowers compared to the typical form of Wrightia laevis.
