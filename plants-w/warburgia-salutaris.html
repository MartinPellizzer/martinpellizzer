Warburgia salutaris

Warburgia salutaris, commonly known as the African bush willow, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional African medicine.
