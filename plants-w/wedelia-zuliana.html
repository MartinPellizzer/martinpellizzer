Wedelia zuliana

Wedelia zuliana, commonly known as the Zulian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in wetland or swampy environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is known for its ability to thrive in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshy areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
