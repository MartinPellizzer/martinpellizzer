Witsenia pumila

Witsenia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf screw-pine, is a small shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fleshy leaves that are typically clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish in color. Witsenia pumila is adapted to coastal environments and is often found growing on sandy soils or in salt-affected areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
