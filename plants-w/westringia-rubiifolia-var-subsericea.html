Westringia rubiifolia var. subsericea

Westringia rubiifolia var. subsericea, commonly known as the silver-leaf westringia, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, silvery appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
