Willisia arekaliana

Willisia arekaliana, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a misty cloud, hence its common name. Willisia arekaliana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
