Windsoria poiformis

Windsoria poiformis, commonly known as the clustered windflower, is a unique member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescence.

Its flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along streambanks.
