Wendlandia cambodiana

Wendlandia cambodiana, commonly known as the Cambodian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Wendlandia cambodiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
