Wendlandia ligustroides

Wendlandia ligustroides, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms that appear in late spring to early summer.

Wendlandia ligustroides is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
