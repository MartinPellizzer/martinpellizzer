Weberocereus imitans

Weberocereus imitans, commonly known as the imitation cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines and have a ribbed structure. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to desert conditions, showcasing the evolutionary strategies of cacti in harsh climates.
