Weigeltia capitellata

Weigeltia capitellata, commonly known as the golden star ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a visually appealing display. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are usually a deep green color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Weigeltia capitellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
