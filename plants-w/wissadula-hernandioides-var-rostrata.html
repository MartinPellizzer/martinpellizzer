Wissadula hernandioides var. rostrata

Wissadula hernandioides var. rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and it produces showy bracts that enhance its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and foliage.

This variant is particularly noted for its elongated, pointed bracts that give it its common name.
