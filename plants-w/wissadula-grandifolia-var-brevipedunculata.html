Wissadula grandifolia var. brevipedunculata

Wissadula grandifolia var. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the dwarf red hibiscus, is a compact shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable environments.
