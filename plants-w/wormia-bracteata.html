Wormia bracteata

Wormia bracteata, commonly known as the bracted wormia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It typically grows close to the ground, with a compact rosette structure that helps conserve moisture. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, providing some protection against desiccation. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish or brownish in color, and appear in clusters.

Wormia bracteata is adapted to survive in harsh environments, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
