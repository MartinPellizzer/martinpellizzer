Wahlenbergia multicaulis var. dispar

Wahlenbergia multicaulis var. dispar, commonly known as the variable rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This variety is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in full sun.
