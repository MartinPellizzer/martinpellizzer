Wulfenia intermedia

Wulfenia intermedia, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.

Wulfenia intermedia plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
