Wendlandia salicifolia

Wendlandia salicifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf ironweed, is a striking perennial plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped, glossy, and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This plant is valued in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
