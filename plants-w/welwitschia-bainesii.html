Welwitschia bainesii

Welwitschia bainesii, commonly known as the welwitschia, is a unique and ancient plant native to the Namib Desert in Namibia and Angola.

It is characterized by its two long, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 4 meters in length and persist for decades. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in the arid and saline conditions of its environment. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects and birds.

Due to its slow growth and longevity, the welwitschia is considered a living fossil and a symbol of the harsh yet beautiful desert ecosystem.
