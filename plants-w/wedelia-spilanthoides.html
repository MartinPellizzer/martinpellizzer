Wedelia spilanthoides

Wedelia spilanthoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate floral display and ability to thrive in partial shade.
