Waitzia nitida

Waitzia nitida, commonly known as the slender lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers with six petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in rocky or alpine environments.

Waitzia nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
