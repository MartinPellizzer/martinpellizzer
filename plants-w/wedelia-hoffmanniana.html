Wedelia hoffmanniana

Wedelia hoffmanniana, commonly known as the Hoffmann's wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers that are arranged in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Wedelia hoffmanniana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience in various environmental conditions.
