Wendlandia tinctoria subsp. barbata

Wendlandia tinctoria subsp.

barbata, commonly known as the crimson bellflower, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red and orange, which bloom in late summer.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often branched and somewhat woody. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, resilient plants.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
