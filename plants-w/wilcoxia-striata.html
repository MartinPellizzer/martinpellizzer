Wilcoxia striata

Wilcoxia striata, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is adapted to harsh desert conditions, with a deep root system that helps it survive in low-water environments. Wilcoxia striata is also noted for its ability to thrive in rocky soils and its role in supporting local pollinators.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
