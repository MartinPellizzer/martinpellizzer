Weingartia neocumingii

Weingartia neocumingii, commonly known as the Neocumming cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and pale green to blue-gray stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts and desert gardens.
