Woodwardia spinulosa f. ramosa

Woodwardia spinulosa f. ramosa, commonly known as the hairy sword fern, is a fern species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright fronds that are covered in fine, hairy scales, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of spore-bearing fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its lush, textured foliage.
