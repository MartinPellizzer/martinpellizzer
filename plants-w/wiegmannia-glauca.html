Wiegmannia glauca

Wiegmannia glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Wiegmannia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
