Weinmannia virgulata

Weinmannia virgulata, commonly known as the mountain snowbush, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a woody stem and is often found in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Weinmannia virgulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
