Westringia violacea

Westringia violacea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub that produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant has aromatic, silver-green leaves that are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Westringia violacea is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators such as bees and birds.
