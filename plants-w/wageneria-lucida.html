Wageneria lucida

Wageneria lucida, commonly known as the brightleaf sedum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in spring. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its adaptability and low maintenance requirements, Wageneria lucida is widely appreciated in horticulture.
