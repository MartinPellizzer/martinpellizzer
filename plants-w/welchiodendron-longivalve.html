Welchiodendron longivalve

Welchiodendron longivalve, commonly known as the longivalve cycad, is a unique species native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Its botanical features reflect adaptations to the tropical climate of its native habitat, including thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
