Weinmannia polyphylla

Weinmannia polyphylla, commonly known as the mountain snowbrush, is a shrub native to the high elevations of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a woody stem with a fibrous bark, and its leaves are typically narrow and leathery. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Weinmannia polyphylla plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various native insects and birds.
