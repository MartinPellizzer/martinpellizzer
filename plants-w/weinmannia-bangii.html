Weinmannia bangii

Weinmannia bangii, commonly known as the silverleaf weinmannia, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of yellow or orange. Its leaves are leathery and have a rough texture, adapted to the dry and cool climate of its native habitat.

Weinmannia bangii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and hardy nature.
