Woodwardia maxima

Woodwardia maxima, commonly known as the giant chain fern, is a large, robust fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright fronds that can reach heights of up to 3 meters, making it a striking addition to any garden or woodland setting. The plant features a thick, fleshy rhizome that supports its extensive growth and helps it thrive in shaded, moist environments. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance that adds texture and visual interest.

Woodwardia maxima is well-suited for humid climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to create a dense, lush canopy.
