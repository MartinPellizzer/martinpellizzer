Walsura oxycarpa

Walsura oxycarpa, commonly known as the red sanders, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is also cultivated for commercial purposes.
