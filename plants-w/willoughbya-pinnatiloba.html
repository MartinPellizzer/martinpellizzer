Willoughbya pinnatiloba

Willoughbya pinnatiloba, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves that are deeply lobed and emit a strong, sweet scent. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds. Its unique foliage and showy blooms make it a popular ornamental plant in subtropical and tropical gardens.

Willoughbya pinnatiloba is also valued for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
