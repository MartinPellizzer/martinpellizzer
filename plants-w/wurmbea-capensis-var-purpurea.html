Wurmbea capensis var. purpurea

Wurmbea capensis var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant blooms with a spatulate, violet-colored spathe that encloses a cluster of small, pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
