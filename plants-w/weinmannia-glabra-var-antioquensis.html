Weinmannia glabra var. antioquensis

Weinmannia glabra var. antioquensis, commonly known as the Antioquian weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andes of Colombia.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a velvety texture. The flowers are small and typically greenish-white, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in humid, cool environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
