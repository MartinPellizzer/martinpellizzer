Waltheria carpinifolia

Waltheria carpinifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
