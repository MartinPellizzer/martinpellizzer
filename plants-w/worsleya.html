Worsleya

Worsleya, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Worsleya, is a tropical plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that can resemble orchids or other exotic blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Worsleya species are native to regions such as Madagascar and parts of Africa.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated for their unique floral displays.
