Wedelia montana

Wedelia montana, commonly known as the mountain wedelia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Wedelia montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
