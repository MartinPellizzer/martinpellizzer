Willdenowia fimbriata

Willdenowia fimbriata, commonly known as the silky willdenowia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in a raceme. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a soft, hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Willdenowia fimbriata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
