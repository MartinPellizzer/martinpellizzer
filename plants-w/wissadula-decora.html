Wissadula decora

Wissadula decora, commonly known as the Decorated Wissadula, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with intricate patterns. Its foliage is typically broad and lance-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to its colorful blooms. Wissadula decora thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
