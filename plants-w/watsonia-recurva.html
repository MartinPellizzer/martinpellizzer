Watsonia recurva

Watsonia recurva, commonly known as the purple firecracker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically vividly colored, ranging from deep red to purple, with a distinctive spurred petal at the base. Watsonia recurva thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
