Weigela mutabilis

Weigela mutabilis, commonly known as the changing flower weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that change color as they age, starting as pink and fading to white or pale pink. The plant produces orange-red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Weigela mutabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
