Wyethia major

Wyethia major, commonly known as the western yellowhead, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
