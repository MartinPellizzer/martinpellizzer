Washingtonia occidentalis

Washingtonia occidentalis, commonly known as the Mexican fan palm, is a tall, solitary tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a tall, slender trunk with a distinctive fan-shaped crown composed of large, blue-gray leaves. The plant has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, fibrous fruits.

This species is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to urban environments.
