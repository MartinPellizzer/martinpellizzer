Wahlenbergia ramosissima subsp. lateralis

Wahlenbergia ramosissima subsp.

lateralis, commonly known as the slender bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The subspecies lateralis is distinguished by its more compact growth habit and shorter, more branched stems compared to the typical form. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
