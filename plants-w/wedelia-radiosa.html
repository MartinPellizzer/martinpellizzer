Wedelia radiosa

Wedelia radiosa, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing perennial with a mat-forming habit, often used in horticulture for its ornamental value. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Wedelia radiosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
