Woodfordia fruticosa f. genuina

Woodfordia fruticosa f. genuina, commonly known as the pink woodfordia, is a compact, flowering shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its striking clusters of fragrant, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and glossy, providing a lush green backdrop to the showy blooms. This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.
