Withania pyrifolia

Withania pyrifolia, commonly known as the Indian winter cherry, is a shrub native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and peppers, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and red, cherry-like fruits. The plant is adapted to dry climates, with thick leaves and deep roots that help it conserve water. Its fruits are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant also contains alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
