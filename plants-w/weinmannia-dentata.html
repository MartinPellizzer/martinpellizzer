Weinmannia dentata

Weinmannia dentata, commonly known as the toothed weinmannia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with serrated edges, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Weinmannia dentata is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and adaptability to dry climates.
