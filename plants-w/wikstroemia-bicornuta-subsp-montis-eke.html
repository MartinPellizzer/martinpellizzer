Wikstroemia bicornuta subsp. montis-eke

Wikstroemia bicornuta subsp.

montis-eke, commonly known as the mountain eke or montane wikstroemia, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish or grayish hue, and it is known for its aromatic properties.

This subspecies is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats at higher elevations.
