Wedelia jacquinii var. cruciana

Wedelia jacquinii var. cruciana, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It features a mat-forming habit with dense clusters of small, yellowish-green to golden-yellow flowers. The plant has slender, wiry stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine, silky hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
