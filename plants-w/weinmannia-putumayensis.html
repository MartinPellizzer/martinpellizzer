Weinmannia putumayensis

Weinmannia putumayensis, commonly known as the Andean Weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, foliage-covered branches and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and paramo ecosystems.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
