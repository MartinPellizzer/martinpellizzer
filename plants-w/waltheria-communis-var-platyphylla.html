Waltheria communis var. platyphylla

Waltheria communis var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed waltheria, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are typically covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
