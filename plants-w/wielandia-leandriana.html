Wielandia leandriana

Wielandia leandriana, commonly known as the leander orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green or white with intricate patterns, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Wielandia leandriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in greenhouses for its unique floral display.
