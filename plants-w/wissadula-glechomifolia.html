Wissadula glechomifolia

Wissadula glechomifolia, commonly known as the creeping marshplant, is a perennial herb native to wetland environments in South America.

It features a low-growing, rhizomatous stem system that allows it to spread across moist soils. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, enhancing its ability to retain moisture. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

Wissadula glechomifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
