Wichuraea dulcis var. uniflora

Wichuraea dulcis var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower sweet bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its fruits are elongated pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types, making it a resilient choice for gardens and landscapes.
