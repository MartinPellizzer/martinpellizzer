Wulfenia pinnatifida

Wulfenia pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wulfenia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and pinnately divided, contributing to its unique appearance.

Wulfenia pinnatifida is often found in elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
