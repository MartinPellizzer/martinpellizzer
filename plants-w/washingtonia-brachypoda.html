Washingtonia brachypoda

Washingtonia brachypoda, commonly known as the Mexican fan palm, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, fan-shaped leaves. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought. Its growth is relatively slow, and it can live for several decades.

The Mexican fan palm is often used in landscaping due to its striking appearance and resilience.
