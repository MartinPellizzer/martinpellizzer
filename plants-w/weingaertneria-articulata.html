Weingaertneria articulata

Weingaertneria articulata, commonly known as the articulated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in dense clusters. Its unique feature is the segmented, jointed stems that give it its common name.

Weingaertneria articulata plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
