Weingartia tiraquensis var. longiseta

Weingartia tiraquensis var. longiseta, commonly known as the long-haired weingartia, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of long, white bristles. The plant features narrow, ribbed stems that are typically green with a bluish tinge. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and appear in the axils of the areoles.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and arid environments due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
