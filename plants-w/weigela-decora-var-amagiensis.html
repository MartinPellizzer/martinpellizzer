Weigela decora var. amagiensis

Weigela decora var. amagiensis, commonly known as the Amagiensis Weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces clusters of pink or red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are ovate and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil conditions.
