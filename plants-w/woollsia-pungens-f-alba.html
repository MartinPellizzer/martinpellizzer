Woollsia pungens f. alba

Woollsia pungens f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered woolly spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance, and its flowers are typically pale yellow to white in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to suppress weeds.

However, it can be invasive in some areas if not properly managed.
