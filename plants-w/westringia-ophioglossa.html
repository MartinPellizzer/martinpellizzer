Westringia ophioglossa

Westringia ophioglossa, commonly known as the coastal westringia, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Australia.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Westringia ophioglossa is drought-tolerant and resistant to coastal conditions such as salt spray and strong winds.
