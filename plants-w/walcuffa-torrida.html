Walcuffa torrida

Walcuffa torrida, commonly known as the fiery walcuff, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange foliage, which becomes more vibrant during the dry season. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its adaptability to harsh conditions, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.
