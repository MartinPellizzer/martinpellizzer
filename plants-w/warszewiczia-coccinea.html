Warszewiczia coccinea

Warszewiczia coccinea, commonly known as the red warthog tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, papery bark and clusters of small, red, fleshy fruits. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing on rocky soils.

Warszewiczia coccinea is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in traditional medicine.
