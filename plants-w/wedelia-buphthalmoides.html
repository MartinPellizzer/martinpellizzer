Wedelia buphthalmoides

Wedelia buphthalmoides, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common ornamental in warm climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various garden environments.
