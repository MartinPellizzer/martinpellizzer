Weigela amagiensis

Weigela amagiensis, commonly known as the Amami weigela, is a rare flowering plant native to the Amami Islands of Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly serrated edge, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.
