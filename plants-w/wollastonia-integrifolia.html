Wollastonia integrifolia

Wollastonia integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and pairs of opposite, elliptic leaves that are smooth and glossy. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers in late winter to early spring, which are borne in racemes. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Wollastonia integrifolia is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes many leguminous plants.
