Weinmannia spruceana

Weinmannia spruceana, commonly known as the silverleaf weinmannia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense, fine hairs covering the leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
