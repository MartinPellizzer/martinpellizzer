Wissadula acuminata

Wissadula acuminata, commonly known as the slender marsh holly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found near water bodies. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, contributing to its common name, while its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure.

Wissadula acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
