Wiestia tuberosa

Wiestia tuberosa, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with a unique structure featuring five sepals and five petals. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine, waxy hairs.

Wiestia tuberosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
