Wallacea riparia

Wallacea riparia, commonly known as the riparian wallacea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments near rivers and streams, making it a common sight in riparian habitats.

Due to its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks, it is an important species for environmental conservation efforts.
