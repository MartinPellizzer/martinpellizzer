Washingtonia bolanderi

Washingtonia bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's date palm, is a rare and distinctive species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a striking appearance. This plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert regions. Its slow growth rate and unique morphology make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
