Wendlandia villosa

Wendlandia villosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun.

Wendlandia villosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
