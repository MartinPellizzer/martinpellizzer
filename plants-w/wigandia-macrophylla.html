Wigandia macrophylla

Wigandia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and can grow up to several meters in height. Its stems are hairy and often climb or trail along the ground.

Wigandia macrophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
