Wedelia filipes

Wedelia filipes, commonly known as the creeping wedgeflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads.
