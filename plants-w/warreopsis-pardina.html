Warreopsis pardina

Warreopsis pardina, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage and its showy, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it thrives on decaying organic matter. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
