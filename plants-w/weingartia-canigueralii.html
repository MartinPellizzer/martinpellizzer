Weingartia canigueralii

Weingartia canigueralii, commonly known as the Canary Island cactus or the Canary Island ponytail cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing, spineless plant with a compact, globular shape and dense clusters of blue-green to grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short, erect stems. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a desirable addition to collections of succulents and cacti.
