Weihea africana

Weihea africana, commonly known as the African breadfruit tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, greyish bark. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by round, edible fruits resembling breadfruit. The fruit is rich in carbohydrates and has been traditionally used as a food source in various African cultures.

It also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and supporting biodiversity.
