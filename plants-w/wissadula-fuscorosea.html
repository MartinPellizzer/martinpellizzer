Wissadula fuscorosea

Wissadula fuscorosea, commonly known as the rose-fleshed stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically during the summer months. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Wissadula fuscorosea thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.
