Weingartia arenacea var. candiae

Weingartia arenacea var. candiae, commonly known as the Candiae cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in dense, white to pale blue spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and having a slow growth rate.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its compact size and unique appearance.
