Werneria celmisioides

Werneria celmisioides, commonly known as the creeping smilacaceae, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Smilacaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. Its botanical features include a woody stem with adventitious roots and a distinct, branched inflorescence.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
