Weihea microphylla

Weihea microphylla, commonly known as the Tahitian fan palm, is a tropical plant native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of wide, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil, often growing near coastal areas. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and ceremonies, reflecting its cultural significance.

The plant plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various species.
