Wisteria macrostachya

Wisteria macrostachya, commonly known as Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, lavender-blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces compound leaves with 12–20 oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are covered in tendrils that help it climb. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often reaching heights of 20–30 feet.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential in some regions, it requires careful management in gardens.
