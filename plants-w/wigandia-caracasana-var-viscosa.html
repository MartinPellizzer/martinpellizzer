Wigandia caracasana var. viscosa

Wigandia caracasana var. viscosa, commonly known as sticky false mallow, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, often arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, flat seeds.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
