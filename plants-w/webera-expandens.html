Webera expandens

Webera expandens, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The species is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
