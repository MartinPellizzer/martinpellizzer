Wedelia reticulata

Wedelia reticulata, commonly known as the creeping wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial with a mat-forming growth habit, often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Wedelia reticulata is adaptable to various soil types and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
