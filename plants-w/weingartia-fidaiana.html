Weingartia fidaiana

Weingartia fidaiana, commonly known as the Fidaiana Barrel Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that is typically covered in a layer of white, powdery bloom. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It is a slow-growing species that prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent collections.
