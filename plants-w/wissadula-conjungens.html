Wissadula conjungens

Wissadula conjungens, commonly known as the clustered bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that are often clustered together, giving it its common name. The plant possesses specialized bladder-like structures that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, where it can efficiently capture prey.

Its unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and botanical study.
