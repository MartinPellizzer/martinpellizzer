Wollastonia lavarum

Wollastonia lavarum, commonly known as the Lavarum wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and produces small, fluffy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. It is often found in sandy soils and is part of the natural vegetation of the region.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
