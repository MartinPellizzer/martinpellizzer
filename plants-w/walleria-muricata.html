Walleria muricata

Walleria muricata, commonly known as the rough walleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Walleria muricata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
