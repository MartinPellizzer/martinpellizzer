Weinmannia biglandulosa

Weinmannia biglandulosa, commonly known as the biglandular weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, often spiny foliage and its distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The plant has small, oval to elliptic leaves that are usually covered in fine, glandular hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or red, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain single seeds.

Weinmannia biglandulosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and heat.
