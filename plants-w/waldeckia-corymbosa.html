Waldeckia corymbosa

Waldeckia corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and violet. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
