Wendlandia uvariifolia subsp. dunniana

Wendlandia uvariifolia subsp.

dunniana, commonly known as the pink fairy tern, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the Andes mountains of South America. It is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique beauty and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking to add a touch of exotic charm to their landscapes.
