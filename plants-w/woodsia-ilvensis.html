Woodsia ilvensis

Woodsia ilvensis, commonly known as the silver woodsia, is a fern native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Woodsia ilvensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
