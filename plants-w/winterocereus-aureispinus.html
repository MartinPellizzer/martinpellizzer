Winterocereus aureispinus

Winterocereus aureispinus, commonly known as the golden spine cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in prominent, golden-yellow spines. The plant produces showy, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water once established.

Due to its striking appearance and unique adaptations, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus enthusiasts.
