Witheringia stramoniifolia

Witheringia stramoniifolia, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a distinct, somewhat pungent aroma. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its role in local ecosystems, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
