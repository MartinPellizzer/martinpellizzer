Willoughbya trinervis

Willoughbya trinervis, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

Willoughbya trinervis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
