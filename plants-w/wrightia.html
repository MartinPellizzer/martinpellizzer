Wrightia

Wrightia, commonly known as the white-flowered wrightia, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Wrightiaceae.

It consists of a single species, Wrightia tinctoria, which is native to Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Wrightia tinctoria is also known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
