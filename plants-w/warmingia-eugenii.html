Warmingia eugenii

Warmingia eugenii, commonly known as the Florida pampas grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and soft, feathery plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Warmingia eugenii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
