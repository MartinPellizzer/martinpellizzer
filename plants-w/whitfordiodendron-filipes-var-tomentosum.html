Whitfordiodendron filipes var. tomentosum

Whitfordiodendron filipes var. tomentosum, commonly known as the hairy filicleaf tamarisk, is a variant of the tamarisk genus native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that are densely covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in compact clusters during the summer months. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in saline soils.

This variant is often used for erosion control due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize sandy or rocky soils.
