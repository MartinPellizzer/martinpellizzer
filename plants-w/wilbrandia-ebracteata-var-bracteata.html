Wilbrandia ebracteata var. bracteata

Wilbrandia ebracteata var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate wilbrandia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, white to pinkish flowers clustered in dense, branched clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
