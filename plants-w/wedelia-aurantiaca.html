Wedelia aurantiaca

Wedelia aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden wedgeplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces bright yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes in warmer climates.
