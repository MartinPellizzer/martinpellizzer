Wurmbea remota

Wurmbea remota, commonly known as the African star of Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking white or pale blue flowers in late summer, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Wurmbea remota is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
