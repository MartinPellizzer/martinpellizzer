Wissadula krapovickasiana

Wissadula krapovickasiana, commonly known as the Krapovickas's ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
