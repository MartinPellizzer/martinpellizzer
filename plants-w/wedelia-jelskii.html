Wedelia jelskii

Wedelia jelskii, commonly known as the velvetleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens in warmer climates.
