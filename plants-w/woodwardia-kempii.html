Woodwardia kempii

Woodwardia kempii, commonly known as the Appalachian sword fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, arching fronds that can reach up to three feet in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are bipinnate, with a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Woodwardia kempii is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of the understory.
