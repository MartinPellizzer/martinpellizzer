Wahlenbergia riparia var. clavata

Wahlenbergia riparia var. clavata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or coastal gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
