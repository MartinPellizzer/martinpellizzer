Weingartia elizabethae

Weingartia elizabethae, commonly known as the Elizabeth's barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, red berries. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments.

Weingartia elizabethae is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.
