Wichuraea acicularis

Wichuraea acicularis, commonly known as the needleleaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in regions with low rainfall.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
