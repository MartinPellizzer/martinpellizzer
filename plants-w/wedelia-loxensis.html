Wedelia loxensis

Wedelia loxensis, commonly known as the Loxensis ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
