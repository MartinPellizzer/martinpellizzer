Waldensia lupulina

Waldensia lupulina, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being heart-shaped and often tinged with yellow or reddish hues. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Waldensia lupulina thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

It is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant.
