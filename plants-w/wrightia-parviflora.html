Wrightia parviflora

Wrightia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered wrightia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Wrightiaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Wrightia parviflora is often found in moist, tropical forests and thrives in partial shade.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warmer climates.
