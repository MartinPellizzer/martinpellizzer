Wrightia puberula

Wrightia puberula, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Wrightiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often hairy bark and small, fragrant white flowers. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a velvety texture.

Wrightia puberula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
