Westringia rosmariniformis var. typica

Westringia rosmariniformis var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf westringia, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features aromatic, narrow, evergreen leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers in clusters during late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice in native landscaping.
