Wikstroemia wrightii

Wikstroemia wrightii, commonly known as Wright's sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pale blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.

Wikstroemia wrightii plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its resilience.
