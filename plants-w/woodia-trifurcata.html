Woodia trifurcata

Woodia trifurcata, commonly known as the three-branched woodia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique leaf structure and historical use in herbal medicine.
