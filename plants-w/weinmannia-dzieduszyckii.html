Weinmannia dzieduszyckii

Weinmannia dzieduszyckii, commonly known as the Chilean weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andes mountains in southern South America.

It belongs to the family Weinmanniaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, often silver- or gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool, humid climates with well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in terminal panicles, and it produces woody capsules as its fruit.

Weinmannia dzieduszyckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
