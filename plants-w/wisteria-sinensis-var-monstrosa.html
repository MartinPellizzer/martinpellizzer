Wisteria sinensis var. monstrosa

Wisteria sinensis var. monstrosa, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of 12 to 20 oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, brown pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is known for its vigorous growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
