Weinmannia polyphylla var. macrocarpa

Weinmannia polyphylla var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant weinmannia, is a striking shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, red-tipped inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its unique foliage and floral display make it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of coastal environments.
