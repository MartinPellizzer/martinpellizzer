Withania pulvinata

Withania pulvinata, commonly known as the Indian winter cherry, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

The botanical features of Withania pulvinata make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
