Wendia pastinacifolia

Wendia pastinacifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf watercress, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged in water, and it produces small, yellow flowers. It is typically found in wetlands and along the edges of streams and rivers.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
