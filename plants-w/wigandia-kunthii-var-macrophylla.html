Wigandia kunthii var. macrophylla

Wigandia kunthii var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf wigandia, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 15 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, followed by distinctive orange or red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
