Weingartia neocumingii var. hediniana

Weingartia neocumingii var. hediniana, commonly known as the Hedin's prickly pear, is a compact cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a greenish-gray hue and is adorned with clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are modified into spines, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and resilience in dry, sunny conditions.
