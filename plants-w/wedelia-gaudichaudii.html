Wedelia gaudichaudii

Wedelia gaudichaudii, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Wedelia gaudichaudii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
