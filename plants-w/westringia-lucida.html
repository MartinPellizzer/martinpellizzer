Westringia lucida

Westringia lucida, commonly known as coastal banksia or silver banksia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and coastal scrub. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in winter and spring, which are arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. Its leaves are narrow, silver-gray, and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Westringia lucida is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.
