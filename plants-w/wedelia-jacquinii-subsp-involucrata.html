Wedelia jacquinii subsp. involucrata

Wedelia jacquinii subsp.

involucrata, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, open habitats such as roadsides and disturbed soils. It is an annual or short-lived perennial herb that is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive floral display.

The subspecies involucrata is distinguished by its more compact growth and specific floral characteristics compared to other subspecies of Wedelia jacquinii.
