Weingartia lecoriensis

Weingartia lecoriensis, commonly known as the Lecoriens prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and clusters of bright green, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid gardens.
