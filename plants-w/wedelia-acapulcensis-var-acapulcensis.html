Wedelia acapulcensis var. acapulcensis

Wedelia acapulcensis var. acapulcensis, commonly known as the Mexican carpetweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Mexico.

It features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming a dense mat of foliage. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often covered with fine, soft hairs. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are borne in clusters and bloom from spring to summer.

This plant thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its ground-covering ability.
