Waltheria urticifolia

Waltheria urticifolia, commonly known as stinging nettle weed, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, opposite leaves that often cause skin irritation upon contact due to the presence of stinging hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in various parts of the Americas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in natural and agricultural areas.
