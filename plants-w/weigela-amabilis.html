Weigela amabilis

Weigela amabilis, commonly known as the sweetweigela, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Weigela amabilis is often cultivated for its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
