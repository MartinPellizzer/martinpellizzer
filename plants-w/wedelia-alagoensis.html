Wedelia alagoensis

Wedelia alagoensis, commonly known as the Alagoas wedelia, is a flowering plant native to the humid regions of northeastern Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces yellowish-pink blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Wedelia alagoensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
