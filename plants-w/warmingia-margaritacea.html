Warmingia margaritacea

Warmingia margaritacea, commonly known as the silver feather plant, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery foliage that ranges from silver to gray-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland restoration projects.

Its ornamental value comes from its elegant, flowing appearance and ability to form dense clumps.
