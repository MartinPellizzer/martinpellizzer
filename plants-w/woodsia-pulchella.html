Woodsia pulchella

Woodsia pulchella, commonly known as the pretty wood fern, is a deciduous fern native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soil. The plant features delicate, lace-like fronds with a distinctive, elongated shape. Its spores are released from small, clustered sori on the underside of the leaves.

Woodsia pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
