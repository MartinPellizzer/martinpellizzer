Weberbauera spathulifolia var. integrifolia

Weberbauera spathulifolia var. integrifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed Weberbauera, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in shaded, moist forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
