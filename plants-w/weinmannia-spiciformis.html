Weinmannia spiciformis

Weinmannia spiciformis, commonly known as the spiciform weinmannia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Weinmanniaceae.

It is native to the Andes mountains in South America, where it thrives in high-altitude environments. The plant is characterized by its woody structure and distinctive, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in dense inflorescences.

It plays a role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
