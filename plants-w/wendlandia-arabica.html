Wendlandia arabica

Wendlandia arabica, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features broad, silver-veined leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Wendlandia arabica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
