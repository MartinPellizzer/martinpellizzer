Withania pogogena var. glabra

Withania pogogena var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth Indian winter cherry, is a herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and potatoes, and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous leaves and its ability to thrive in arid environments.
