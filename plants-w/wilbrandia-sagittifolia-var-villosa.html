Wilbrandia sagittifolia var. villosa

Wilbrandia sagittifolia var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy arrowleaf, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a yellowish or greenish hue.

The hairy arrowleaf is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
