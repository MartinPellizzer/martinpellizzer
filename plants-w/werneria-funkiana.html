Werneria funkiana

Werneria funkiana, commonly known as the African orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and strap-like, and it produces small, fragrant blooms.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
