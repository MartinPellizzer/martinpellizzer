Weingartia tarijensis subsp. samaensis

Weingartia tarijensis subsp.

samaensis, commonly known as the Tarija weingartia, is a succulent cactus native to Bolivia. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white to grayish spines.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
