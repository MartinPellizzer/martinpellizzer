Waltheria pentagynia

Waltheria pentagynia, commonly known as the five-lobed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features showy, purple to reddish-purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have distinct lobes, contributing to its ornamental value.

Waltheria pentagynia is valued in gardens for its vibrant flowers and ecological benefits to pollinators.
