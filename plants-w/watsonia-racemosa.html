Watsonia racemosa

Watsonia racemosa, commonly known as the Natal lily, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear dense clusters of bright red or orange flowers, which are typical of the Iridaceae family. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Watsonia racemosa thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, enhancing its ecological value.
