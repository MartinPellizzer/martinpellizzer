Watsonia flavida

Watsonia flavida, commonly known as the yellow firecracker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky flower stalks that bear bright yellow to orange-red blooms. The plant features strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Watsonia flavida thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and long-lasting flowers make it a valuable addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
