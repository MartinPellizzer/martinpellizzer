Wedelia ramagii

Wedelia ramagii, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs.

Wedelia ramagii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
