Wedelia brachycarpa

Wedelia brachycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a low-growing shrub or perennial herb, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Wedelia brachycarpa is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
