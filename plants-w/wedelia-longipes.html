Wedelia longipes

Wedelia longipes, commonly known as the longipes ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Wedelia longipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
