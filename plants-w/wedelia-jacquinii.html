Wedelia jacquinii

Wedelia jacquinii, commonly known as the Mexican carpetweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Americas.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine hairs.

Wedelia jacquinii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to suppress weeds.
