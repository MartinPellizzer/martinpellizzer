Weingartia longigibba

Weingartia longigibba, commonly known as the long-ribbed cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered with small, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Weingartia longigibba is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ease of care.
