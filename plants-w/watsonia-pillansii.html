Watsonia pillansii

Watsonia pillansii, commonly known as the Natal grassblume, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces clusters of vibrant red, orange, or yellow flowers, which are often adorned with a distinctive black or purple blotch at the base. Watsonia pillansii thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal among gardeners and botanists alike.
