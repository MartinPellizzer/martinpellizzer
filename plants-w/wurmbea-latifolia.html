Wurmbea latifolia

Wurmbea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, often blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Wurmbea latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
