Willoughbya strobilifera

Willoughbya strobilifera, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter. The plant produces distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are pale yellow to greenish-white in color. These flowers appear in late winter to early spring, giving the plant its common name.

Willoughbya strobilifera is known for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
