Weberocereus frohningiorum

Weberocereus frohningiorum, commonly known as the frohning's night bloomer, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is a climbing cactus with cylindrical stems and spines, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its botanical features include areoles, which are specialized structures that bear spines, flowers, and branches.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid environments with minimal water requirements.
