Wiesneria sparganiifolia

Wiesneria sparganiifolia, commonly known as the swamp milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, resembling the structure of milkweed flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Wiesneria sparganiifolia plays a role in supporting local pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
