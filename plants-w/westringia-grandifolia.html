Westringia grandifolia

Westringia grandifolia, commonly known as the New Zealand rock rose, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing shrub with evergreen foliage that ranges from gray-green to blue-gray in color. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
