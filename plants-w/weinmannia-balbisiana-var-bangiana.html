Weinmannia balbisiana var. bangiana

Weinmannia balbisiana var. bangiana, commonly known as the Bangian weinmannia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm regions.
