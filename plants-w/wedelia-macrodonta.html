Wedelia macrodonta

Wedelia macrodonta, commonly known as the silverleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, white, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental use in gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in disturbed areas.
