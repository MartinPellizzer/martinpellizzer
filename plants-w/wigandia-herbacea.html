Wigandia herbacea

Wigandia herbacea, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in the morning. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
