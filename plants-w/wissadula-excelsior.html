Wissadula excelsior

Wissadula excelsior, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its leaves are thick and leathery, providing a striking visual contrast to its delicate flowers.

Wissadula excelsior is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
