Weigela suavis

Weigela suavis, commonly known as sweet weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces clusters of pink or red flowers, which are attractive to pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Weigela suavis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
