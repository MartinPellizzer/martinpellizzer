Wimmeria sternii

Wimmeria sternii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has adapted to harsh desert conditions with deep root systems and thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Wimmeria sternii plays a vital role in stabilizing desert soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
