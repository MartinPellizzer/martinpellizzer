Whytockia sasakii

Whytockia sasakii, commonly known as the Sasakii cycad, is a unique cycad species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of long, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

The Sasakii cycad is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
