Wigginsia sessiliflora var. pauciareolata

Wigginsia sessiliflora var. pauciareolata, commonly known as the sparse-areolate wigginsia, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually yellow or orange, blooming in late spring or early summer. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with multiple stems that branch out from the base.

The varietal name "pauciareolata" refers to its sparse arrangement of areoles, which are characteristic of cacti and related plants.
