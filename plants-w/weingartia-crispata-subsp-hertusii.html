Weingartia crispata subsp. hertusii

Weingartia crispata subsp.

hertusii, commonly known as the Hertusii cactus, is a small, spineless plant native to the arid regions of Argentina. It features a compact, rounded stem that is often ribbed and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its succulent stems.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for indoor and rock garden cultivation.
