Wallnoeferia peruviana

Wallnoeferia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian wallflower, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically produces yellow or orange flowers with four petals, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
