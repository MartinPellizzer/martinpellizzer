Wahlenbergia marginata subvar. trichogyna

Wahlenbergia marginata subvar. trichogyna, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It features slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
