Windsoria seslerioides

Windsoria seslerioides, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has a rosette-forming growth habit, with fleshy, blue-green leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in sandy or rocky soils.
