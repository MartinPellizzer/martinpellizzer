Weingartia neumanniana subsp. dagmariana

Weingartia neumanniana subsp.

dagmariana, commonly known as the Dagmar dwarf cactus, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to arid regions of South America. It features a short, cylindrical stem with a dense covering of white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
