Willoughbya ternata

Willoughbya ternata, commonly known as the ghost gum, is a small evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It features smooth, pale grey bark that peels away in thin strips, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Willoughbya ternata is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
