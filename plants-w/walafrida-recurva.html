Walafrida recurva

Walafrida recurva, commonly known as the twisted walafrid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twisted stems and small, pale green leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown, which bloom during the early summer months. Due to its limited habitat and slow growth, it is considered endangered and is often found in protected botanical gardens.

Its unique morphology and restricted distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
