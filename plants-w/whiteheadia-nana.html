Whiteheadia nana

Whiteheadia nana, commonly known as the dwarf whiteheadia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, adding a touch of elegance to its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall fine-textured appearance.

Whiteheadia nana is often grown in rock gardens or as a container plant due to its preference for well-drained soil and sunny conditions.
