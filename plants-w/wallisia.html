Wallisia

Wallisia, commonly known as the wallisia or wallisian bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Campanulaceae.

It consists of several species native to South America, particularly in the Andean regions of Peru and Bolivia. The plants are characterized by their showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. They typically grow in rocky or mountainous habitats, often as shrubs or small trees.

Wallisia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant blooms.
