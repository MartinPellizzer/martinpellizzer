Wollastonia biflora var. ryukyuensis

Wollastonia biflora var. ryukyuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu wattle, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and produces yellow flowers in late spring. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it is adapted to the humid, subtropical climate of its native habitat.
