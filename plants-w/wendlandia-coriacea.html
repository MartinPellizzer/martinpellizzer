Wendlandia coriacea

Wendlandia coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, palmately lobed leaves that are covered in a thick, rough texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Wendlandia coriacea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
