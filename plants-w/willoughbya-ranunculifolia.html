Willoughbya ranunculifolia

Willoughbya ranunculifolia, commonly known as the waxflower or waxplant, is a tropical shrub native to Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, pink or white flowers that resemble those of the genus Rhododendron. The plant has smooth, glossy leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Willoughbya ranunculifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
