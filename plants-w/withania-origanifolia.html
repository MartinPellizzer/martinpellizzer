Withania origanifolia

Withania origanifolia, commonly known as the Indian winter cherry, is a shrub native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces flowers that are typically white or pale green. It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

The botanical features of Withania origanifolia make it well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.
