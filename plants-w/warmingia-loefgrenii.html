Warmingia loefgrenii

Warmingia loefgrenii, commonly known as the Hawaiian grass or kalo, is a perennial grass native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.
