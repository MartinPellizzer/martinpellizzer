Weingartia azurduyensis

Weingartia azurduyensis, commonly known as the Azurduy's barrel cactus, is a species native to northern Argentina.

It is a low-growing, columnar cactus with a distinctive blue-gray coloration due to its dense covering of white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring season, which are followed by small, red fruits. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
