Wendia

Wendia, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant produces bright red or yellow fruits that are encased in a thick, segmented rind. Wendia's leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
