Wilckia runcinata

Wilckia runcinata, commonly known as the golden vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendant-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Wilckia runcinata is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
