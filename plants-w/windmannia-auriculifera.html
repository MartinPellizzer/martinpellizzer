Windmannia auriculifera

Windmannia auriculifera, commonly known as the auriculate windmannia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Windmannia auriculifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
