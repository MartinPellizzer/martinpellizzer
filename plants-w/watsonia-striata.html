Watsonia striata

Watsonia striata, commonly known as the Natal grassblume, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features tall, grass-like leaves and produces striking, cone-shaped flower spikes that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is characterized by its robust rhizomes and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for temperate climates.

Watsonia striata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
