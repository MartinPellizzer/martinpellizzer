Withania ramosa

Withania ramosa, commonly known as the Indian winter cherry, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its branched stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dry, capsule-like structure containing several seeds.

Withania ramosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
