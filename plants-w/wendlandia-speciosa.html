Wendlandia speciosa

Wendlandia speciosa, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its large, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Wendlandia speciosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with partial to full sun.
