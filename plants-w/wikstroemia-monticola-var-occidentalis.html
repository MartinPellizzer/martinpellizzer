Wikstroemia monticola var. occidentalis

Wikstroemia monticola var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
