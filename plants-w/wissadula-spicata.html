Wissadula spicata

Wissadula spicata, commonly known as the silver bush, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking silver-blue foliage, which is covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Wissadula spicata is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
