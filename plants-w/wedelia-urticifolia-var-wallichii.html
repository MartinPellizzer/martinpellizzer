Wedelia urticifolia var. wallichii

Wedelia urticifolia var. wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan wedgeleaf, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family, known for its ornamental and ecological significance.
