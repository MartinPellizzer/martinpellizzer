Widdringtonia commersonii

Widdringtonia commersonii, commonly known as the yellowwood tree, is a tall, evergreen conifer native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its straight, slender trunk and dark, glossy green foliage. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a prized material in furniture and construction.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in coastal environments with high salt exposure.
