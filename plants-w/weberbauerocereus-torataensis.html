Weberbauerocereus torataensis

Weberbauerocereus torataensis, commonly known as the Torata cactus, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of Peru.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its ribbed stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and moths. Its structure is adapted to the arid and high-altitude environments where it thrives.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
