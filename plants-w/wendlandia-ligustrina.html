Wendlandia ligustrina

Wendlandia ligustrina, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Wendlandia ligustrina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
