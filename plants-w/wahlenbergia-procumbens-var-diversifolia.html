Wahlenbergia procumbens var. diversifolia

Wahlenbergia procumbens var. diversifolia, commonly known as the slender bluebell, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to southern Australia.

It features slender, prostrate stems that produce clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a bluish-green hue and a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
