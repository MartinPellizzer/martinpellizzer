Withania suberosa

Withania suberosa, commonly known as Indian winter cherry, is a shrub native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that contain seeds, and it is adapted to survive in dry, hot climates.

Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough texture, and it has a deep root system that helps it retain moisture in harsh environments.
