Watsonia punctata var. longicollis

Watsonia punctata var. longicollis, commonly known as the long-necked Watsonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal gardens.

The flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.
