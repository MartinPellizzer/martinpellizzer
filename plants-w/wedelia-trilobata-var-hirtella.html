Wedelia trilobata var. hirtella

Wedelia trilobata var. hirtella, commonly known as hairy wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
