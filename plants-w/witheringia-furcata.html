Witheringia furcata

Witheringia furcata, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow panicles that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Witheringia furcata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
