Wasabia

Wasabia, commonly known as wasabi, is a perennial plant native to East Asia, particularly Japan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant's most prized part is its root, which is typically grown in cool, moist environments. The root contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including isothiocyanates, responsible for its pungent flavor.

Due to its specific growing conditions, authentic wasabi is often cultivated in controlled environments to maintain its quality and potency.
