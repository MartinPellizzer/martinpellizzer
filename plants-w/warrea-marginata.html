Warrea marginata

Warrea marginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf warrea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating.

Warrea marginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
