Widdringtonia stipitata

Widdringtonia stipitata, commonly known as the Natal yellowwood, is a tall evergreen tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its straight, slender trunk and dark green, glossy leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by cone-like structures containing its seeds. It is valued for its durable, aromatic wood and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a threatened species in some areas.
