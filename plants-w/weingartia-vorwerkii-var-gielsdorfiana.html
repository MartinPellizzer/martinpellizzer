Weingartia vorwerkii var. gielsdorfiana

Weingartia vorwerkii var. gielsdorfiana, commonly known as the Gielsdorfian Cactus, is a compact, low-growing species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a spherical or oblong body with a smooth, pale green to bluish-green surface that often develops a powdery coating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months, typically in clusters at the stem apex. It has short, cylindrical spines and is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for collectors and cactus enthusiasts.
