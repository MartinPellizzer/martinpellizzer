Walsura intermedia

Walsura intermedia, commonly known as the wild tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and consist of several ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody pods containing edible, sour fruits. Its bark is thick and corky, often marked with fissures, and the tree can grow up to 20 meters tall.

Walsura intermedia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
