Webera campaniflora

Webera campaniflora, commonly known as the bellflower plant, is a flowering species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Webera campaniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
