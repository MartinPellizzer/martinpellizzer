Weinmannia elliptica var. elliptica

Weinmannia elliptica var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic weinmannia, is a species native to the coastal regions of southern California and northern Baja California.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the coastline. The plant features narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or grayish pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically white or pale yellow, appearing in spring.

This plant is well adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
