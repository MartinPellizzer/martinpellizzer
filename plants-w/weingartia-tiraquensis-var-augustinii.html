Weingartia tiraquensis var. augustinii

Weingartia tiraquensis var. augustinii, commonly known as the Augustine weingartia, is a compact cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with prominent areoles, from which spines and flowers emerge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its foliage is typically green, with a slight bluish tinge, and it is well-suited for indoor cultivation.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
