Wedelia rugosa var. rugosa

Wedelia rugosa var. rugosa, commonly known as the hairy wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant has a mat-forming habit and is often used in horticulture for ground cover. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and the stems are creeping and often root at the nodes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
