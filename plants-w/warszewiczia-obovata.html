Warszewiczia obovata

Warszewiczia obovata, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a reddish or bronze tint. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
