Wendlandia scabra var. tenasserimensis

Wendlandia scabra var. tenasserimensis, commonly known as the mountain spiderflower, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous forests. Its leaves are narrow and slightly serrated, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This variety is prized for its unique texture and color, making it a popular choice in horticultural collections.
