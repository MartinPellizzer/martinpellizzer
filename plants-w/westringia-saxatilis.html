Westringia saxatilis

Westringia saxatilis, commonly known as the coastal rock rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and well-drained soils.

Westringia saxatilis is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
