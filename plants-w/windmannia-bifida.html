Windmannia bifida

Windmannia bifida, commonly known as the split-leaf windmannia, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, deeply lobed leaves that give it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is often used in horticultural displays due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.

Windmannia bifida is relatively low-maintenance, thriving in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
