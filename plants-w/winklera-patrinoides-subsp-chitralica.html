Winklera patrinoides subsp. chitralica

Winklera patrinoides subsp.

chitralica, commonly known as the Chitral bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, often at elevations above 3,000 meters.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. It is part of the Campanulaceae family, known for its showy, tubular flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
