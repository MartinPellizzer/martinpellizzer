Weingartia breviflora subsp. laui

Weingartia breviflora subsp.

laui, commonly known as the Laui cactus, is a compact, low-growing species native to southern Africa. It features a short, cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the warmer months.

The plant is characterized by its dense, spiny areoles and is well adapted to arid environments. It requires minimal water and thrives in bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
