Wyethia amplexicaulis

Wyethia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping leaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, clasping leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in a terminal cluster, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Wyethia amplexicaulis is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
