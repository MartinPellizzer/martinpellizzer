Wedelia leucoglossa

Wedelia leucoglossa, commonly known as the white-lipped wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Wedelia leucoglossa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and continuous flowering.
