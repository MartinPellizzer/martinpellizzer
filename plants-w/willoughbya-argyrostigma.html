Willoughbya argyrostigma

Willoughbya argyrostigma, commonly known as the silver-stemmed willoughbya, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-blue stems and large, showy white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a unique growth habit, often forming a dense, upright clump with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid conditions. Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by specialty growers.
