Wollastonia bryanii

Wollastonia bryanii, commonly known as the Bryan's wattle, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it is characterized by its ability to thrive in low-nutrient environments.

Wollastonia bryanii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
