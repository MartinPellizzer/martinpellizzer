Wissadula patens subsp. cuspidata

Wissadula patens subsp.

cuspidata, commonly known as the Cape buffalo thistle, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal regions.

Its subspecific name, *cuspidata*, refers to the sharp, pointed tips of its leaves.
