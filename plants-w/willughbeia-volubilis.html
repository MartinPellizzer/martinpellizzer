Willughbeia volubilis

Willughbeia volubilis, commonly known as the climbing willughbeia, is a tropical evergreen vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Willughbeia volubilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
