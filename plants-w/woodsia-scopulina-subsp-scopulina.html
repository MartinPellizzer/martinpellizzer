Woodsia scopulina subsp. scopulina

Woodsia scopulina subsp.

scopulina, commonly known as the rock fern, is a perennial fern native to the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices and forest floors. It produces small, spore-bearing fronds that are arranged in a compact rosette.

The species is notable for its adaptability to harsh, rocky habitats and its role in ecological succession in forest ecosystems.
