Wollastonia insularis

Wollastonia insularis, commonly known as the island hakea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain small, hard seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Wollastonia insularis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
