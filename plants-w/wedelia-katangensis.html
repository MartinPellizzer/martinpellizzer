Wedelia katangensis

Wedelia katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga wedgeleaf, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the rainy season.

It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
