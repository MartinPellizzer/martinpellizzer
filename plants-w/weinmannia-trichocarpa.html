Weinmannia trichocarpa

Weinmannia trichocarpa, commonly known as the silverleaf weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitats.
