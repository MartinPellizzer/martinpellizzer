Wilbrandia hibiscoides

Wilbrandia hibiscoides, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant has large, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of yellow or orange, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Wilbrandia hibiscoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
