Weigeltia nanayensis

Weigeltia nanayensis, commonly known as the Philippine flame tree, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its floral structure includes five petals and is adapted for pollination by birds and insects.

Weigeltia nanayensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
