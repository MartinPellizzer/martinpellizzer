Wendlandia sulcata

Wendlandia sulcata, commonly known as the Sulcate Ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, clustered purple to pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Wendlandia sulcata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
