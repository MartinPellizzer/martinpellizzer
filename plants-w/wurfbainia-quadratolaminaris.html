Wurfbainia quadratolaminaris

Wurfbainia quadratolaminaris, commonly known as the quadratolaminar cactus, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Wurfbainia quadratolaminaris is adapted to extreme environments, making it a resilient and fascinating example of botanical evolution.
