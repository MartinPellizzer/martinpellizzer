Weinmannia glabra var. mexicana

Weinmannia glabra var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican velvetleaf, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive tactile quality. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a unique and visually appealing plant in arid landscapes.
