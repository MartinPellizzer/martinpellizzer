Weingartia oligacantha

Weingartia oligacantha, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Weingartia oligacantha is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
