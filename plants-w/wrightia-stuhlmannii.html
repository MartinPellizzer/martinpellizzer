Wrightia stuhlmannii

Wrightia stuhlmannii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Wrightiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in moist, fertile soils in lowland areas.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
