Weingartia saxatilis

Weingartia saxatilis, commonly known as the rock cactus or stone cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of fine, white hairs. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, adapting well to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season.

Weingartia saxatilis is a slow-growing plant that thrives in bright light and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for succulent collections.
