Warnockia scutellarioides

Warnockia scutellarioides, commonly known as the silverleaf groundcherry, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by round, greenish-brown fruits resembling tiny tomatoes. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.
