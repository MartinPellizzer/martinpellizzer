Wendlandia pubigera

Wendlandia pubigera, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, velvety foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique and exotic plants.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
