Wisteria dubia

Wisteria dubia, commonly known as the Australian wisteria, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Australia.

It produces long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in the spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with slender, pointed leaflets. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, often reaching several meters in height.

Wisteria dubia is valued for its ornamental beauty but can be invasive in certain regions.
