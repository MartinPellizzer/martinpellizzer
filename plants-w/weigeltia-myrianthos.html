Weigeltia myrianthos

Weigeltia myrianthos, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and typically have 5 to 9 leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and streamside areas.

Weigeltia myrianthos is valued for its ornamental value and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
