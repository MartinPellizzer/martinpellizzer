Wisteria sinensis var. alba-plena

Wisteria sinensis var. alba-plena, commonly known as the Chinese white wisteria, is a cultivar of the wisteria plant characterized by its profuse display of fragrant, white flowers.

It is a deciduous climbing vine that produces long, pendant clusters of blooms in late spring. The flowers are typically fully double, with a dense arrangement of petals that give it a plena (full) appearance. This variety is native to China and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens and landscapes.

It requires a sunny location and well-draining soil to thrive.
