Weingartia formosa

Weingartia formosa, commonly known as the Mexican beauty or the weingartia cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is typically adorned with short, white spines. The plant features pale green to blue-gray stems that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. It produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

Weingartia formosa is a slow-growing, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and bright, indirect sunlight.
