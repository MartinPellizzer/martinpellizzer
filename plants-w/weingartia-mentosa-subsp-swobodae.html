Weingartia mentosa subsp. swobodae

Weingartia mentosa subsp.

swobodae, commonly known as the Swobodae Cactus, is a compact, slow-growing species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. It features short, cylindrical stems that are typically green with a bluish tinge, adorned with clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months.

The plant produces small, rounded spines and has a shallow root system adapted to its harsh, dry environment. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
