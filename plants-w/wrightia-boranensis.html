Wrightia boranensis

Wrightia boranensis, commonly known as the Boranese winged tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Wrightiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by small, winged fruits that are dispersed by wind.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
