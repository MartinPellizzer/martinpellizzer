Weingartia markusii var. mizquensis

Weingartia markusii var. mizquensis, commonly known as the Mizquense Cactus, is a species of barrel cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and a dense covering of white to gray spines. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand prolonged droughts. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

This variety is prized for its compact size and ornamental appeal in desert gardens.
