Wurmbea dioica

Wurmbea dioica, commonly known as the African rain lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots and striking, showy flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along riverbanks. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with six petals and a distinctive spurred shape.

Wurmbea dioica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
