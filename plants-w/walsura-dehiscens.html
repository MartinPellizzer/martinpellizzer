Walsura dehiscens

Walsura dehiscens, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood or Malabar sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish wood that is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptic leaves with a smooth surface and a fragrant aroma. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule that splits open when mature.

The tree is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
