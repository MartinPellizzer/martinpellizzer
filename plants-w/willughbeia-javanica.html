Willughbeia javanica

Willughbeia javanica, commonly known as the Java willughbeia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

Willughbeia javanica is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
