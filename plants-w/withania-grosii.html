Withania grosii

Withania grosii, commonly known as the Indian winter cherry, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, yellow flowers, and small, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky habitats.

Withania grosii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
