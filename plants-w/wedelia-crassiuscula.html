Wedelia crassiuscula

Wedelia crassiuscula, commonly known as the dwarf iron vine, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It is often cultivated as a ground cover due to its dense growth habit and ability to suppress weeds.

Wedelia crassiuscula is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in various environmental conditions.
