Weinmannia paniculata

Weinmannia paniculata, commonly known as the silver fern, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like foliage with silvery undersides that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy coastal areas.

Weinmannia paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
