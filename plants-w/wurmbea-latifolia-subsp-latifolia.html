Wurmbea latifolia subsp. latifolia

Wurmbea latifolia subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features rhizomatous growth and produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers in late summer.

The plant has broad, strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
