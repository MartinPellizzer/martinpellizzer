Wislizenia refracta var. melilotoides

Wislizenia refracta var. melilotoides, commonly known as the melilot-like ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for pollinators.
