Wissadula luciana

Wissadula luciana, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming cactus with bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has spiny stems and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its foliage is often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

It is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
