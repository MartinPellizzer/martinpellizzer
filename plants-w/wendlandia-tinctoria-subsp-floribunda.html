Wendlandia tinctoria subsp. floribunda

Wendlandia tinctoria subsp.

floribunda, commonly known as the scarlet glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America. It is characterized by its striking, showy clusters of orange-red bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Its ornamental value is highly appreciated in horticulture for its vibrant, long-lasting display.
