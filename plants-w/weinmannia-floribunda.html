Weinmannia floribunda

Weinmannia floribunda, commonly known as the Hawaiian weinmannia, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody stem with a thick, corky bark that provides protection against environmental stress. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Weinmannia floribunda is adapted to coastal environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
