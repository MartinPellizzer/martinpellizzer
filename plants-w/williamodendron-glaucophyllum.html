Williamodendron glaucophyllum

Williamodendron glaucophyllum, commonly known as the silver-leaved willow, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a waxy coating.

Williamodendron glaucophyllum is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal areas.
