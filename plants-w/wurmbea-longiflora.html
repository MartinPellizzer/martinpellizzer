Wurmbea longiflora

Wurmbea longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered yellow ivy, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that produces striking yellow flowers with a strong, pleasant fragrance. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and aromatic plant.
