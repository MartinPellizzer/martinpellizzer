Watsonia

Watsonia, commonly known as the "rainbow lily," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Iridaceae.

These plants are native to southern Africa and are characterized by their striking, sword-shaped leaves and showy, cone-like flower spikes. Watsonia species typically bloom in late spring or early summer, producing vibrant flowers in shades of red, orange, yellow, and purple. They thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making them popular in rock gardens and borders.

The genus includes over 50 species, many of which are cultivated for their ornamental value.
