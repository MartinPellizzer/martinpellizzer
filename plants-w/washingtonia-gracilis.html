Washingtonia gracilis

Washingtonia gracilis, commonly known as the slender Mexican fan palm, is a species of palm native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of long, feathery fronds that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates. Its slow growth rate and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for landscaping in desert environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by black, spherical fruits that are dispersed by birds.
