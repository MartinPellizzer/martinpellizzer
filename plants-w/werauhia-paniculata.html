Werauhia paniculata

Werauhia paniculata, commonly known as the panicle werauhia, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for the plant's hydration. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear numerous small, showy flowers in shades of white and pink. Its foliage is often covered in dense, fibrous trichomes, giving it a unique textured appearance.

Werauhia paniculata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight.
