Wikstroemia circinata

Wikstroemia circinata, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in tropical climates.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
