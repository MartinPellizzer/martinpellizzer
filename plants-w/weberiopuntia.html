Weberiopuntia

Weberiopuntia, commonly known as the fishhook cactus, is a genus of cacti native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and the presence of specialized structures called areoles, from which both spines and flowers emerge. The flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to the plant's rugged appearance. Weberiopuntia species are adapted to arid environments, storing water in their thick, fleshy stems.

They play an important role in desert ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
