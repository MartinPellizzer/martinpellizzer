Werneria plantaginifolia

Werneria plantaginifolia, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with slender, hairy stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
