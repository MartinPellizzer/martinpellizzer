Waldsteinia fragarioides subsp. doniana

Waldsteinia fragarioides subsp.

doniana, commonly known as the Doniana cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America. It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with spreading stems that root at the nodes.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other damp environments.
