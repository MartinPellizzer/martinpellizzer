Woodsia burgessiana

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant has lance-shaped, alternate leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of the Polypodiaceae family.
