Willoughbya parviflora var. guaco

Willoughbya parviflora var. guaco, commonly known as guaco, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its palm-like appearance, with a single, unbranched stem and large, compound leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by bright red berries. Guaco thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
