Willoughbya burchellii

Willoughbya burchellii, commonly known as the African lily, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red throat, and blooms during the summer months. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces a distinctive, spiky seed head that becomes visible after flowering. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Willoughbya burchellii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
