Waldsteinia moenchii

Waldsteinia moenchii, commonly known as the Appalachian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive hairy texture.

Waldsteinia moenchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
