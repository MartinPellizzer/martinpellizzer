Winklera silaifolia

Winklera silaifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf inkberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that exhibits a silvery-white underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers during the spring season, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Winklera silaifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique leaf coloration.
