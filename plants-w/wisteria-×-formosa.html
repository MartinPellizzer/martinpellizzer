Wisteria × formosa

Wisteria × formosa, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of 12–20 oval-shaped leaflets. Its stems are flexible and can grow up to 30 meters in length, making it an impressive ornamental plant.

Wisteria × formosa is often cultivated for its showy blooms and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
