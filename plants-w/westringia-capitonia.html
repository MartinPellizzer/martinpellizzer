Westringia capitonia

Westringia capitonia, commonly known as the coastal wallaby-jackalberry, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, exhibiting a compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in dense clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often with a slight woolly texture.

Westringia capitonia is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
