Washingtonia sonorae

Washingtonia sonorae, commonly known as the Mexican fan palm, is a tall, single-stemmed plant native to the Sonoran Desert of Mexico.

It features a slender, unbranched trunk with a fibrous, grayish-brown bark. The plant produces large, fan-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color and can grow up to 3 meters in diameter. Its flowering occurs in clusters of yellow to orange-red inflorescences, which are pollinated by insects.

The Mexican fan palm is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in urban landscapes for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
