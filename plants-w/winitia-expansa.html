Winitia expansa

Winitia expansa, commonly known as the large-fruited betel palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, red berries. Its fruits are widely used in traditional medicine and as a source of dietary fiber.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
