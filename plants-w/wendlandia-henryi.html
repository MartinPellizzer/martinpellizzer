Wendlandia henryi

Wendlandia henryi, commonly known as Henry's shasta daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like blooms that range in color from white to pink. The plant features a compact, bushy growth habit with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
