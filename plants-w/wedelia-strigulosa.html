Wedelia strigulosa

Wedelia strigulosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, yellowish to white flowers arranged in clusters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Wedelia strigulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
