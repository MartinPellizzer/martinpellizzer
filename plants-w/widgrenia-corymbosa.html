Widgrenia corymbosa

Widgrenia corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bract plant, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Widgrenia corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
