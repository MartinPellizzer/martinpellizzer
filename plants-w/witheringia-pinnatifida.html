Witheringia pinnatifida

Witheringia pinnatifida, commonly known as the Chinese holly or golden holly, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a striking golden or yellowish coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

Witheringia pinnatifida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
