Wahlenbergia gracilis var. littoralis

Wahlenbergia gracilis var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, typically on slender, upright stems. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to withstand salty sea breezes and sandy soils. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing in dunes or along shorelines.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to contribute to coastal biodiversity.
