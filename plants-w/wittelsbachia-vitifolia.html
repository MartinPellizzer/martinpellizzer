Wittelsbachia vitifolia

Wittelsbachia vitifolia, commonly known as the grape-leaved wittelsbachia, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its growth habit is predominantly shrub-like, with multiple stems arising from the base.

Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it has adapted to thrive in environments with limited water availability.
