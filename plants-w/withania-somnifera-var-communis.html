Withania somnifera var. communis

Withania somnifera var. communis, commonly known as ashwagandha, is a herbaceous plant native to India, North Africa, and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange berries and branched, woody stems. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing in dry soils and rocky areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit contains small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind and animals.
