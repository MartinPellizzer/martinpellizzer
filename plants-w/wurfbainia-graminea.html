Wurfbainia graminea

Wurfbainia graminea, commonly known as the African grass tree, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense, spike-like clusters during the rainy season. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

Wurfbainia graminea is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
