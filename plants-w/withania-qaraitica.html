Withania qaraitica

Withania qaraitica, commonly known as the Indian devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that contain seeds, and it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

It is often found in wastelands and along roadsides, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
