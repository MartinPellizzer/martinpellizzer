Wendlandia gamblei

Wendlandia gamblei, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are thick and covered in fine, silvery hairs, which help reduce water loss in its native habitat.

Wendlandia gamblei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, cool conditions.
