Weingartia neocumingii subsp. pulquinensis

Weingartia neocumingii subsp.

pulquinensis, commonly known as the Pulquinensis Barrel Cactus, is a compact cactus native to Argentina. It features a cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure, typically growing to a height of about 30 centimeters.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
