Wendlandia lawsoniae

Wendlandia lawsoniae, commonly known as the Lawson's gloxinia, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting throat. The plant produces showy bracts that resemble petals, enhancing its visual appeal. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a dramatic floral display.

Its foliage is broad and glossy, adding to its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor settings.
