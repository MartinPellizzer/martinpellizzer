Wedelia callosa

Wedelia callosa, commonly known as the silverleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with creeping stems that form dense mats. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Wedelia callosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to suppress weeds.
