Wibelia decurrens

Wibelia decurrens, commonly known as the tall tansy, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Wibelia decurrens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
