Walafrida schinzii

Walafrida schinzii, commonly known as the Schinz's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stem and reduced leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its structure and growth habits make it a unique example of desert flora.
