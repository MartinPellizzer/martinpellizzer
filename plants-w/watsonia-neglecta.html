Watsonia neglecta

Watsonia neglecta, commonly known as the bluekopje, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of vibrant, long-lasting flowers that attract pollinators. Watsonia neglecta thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and resilience make it a favored plant among gardeners in temperate climates.
