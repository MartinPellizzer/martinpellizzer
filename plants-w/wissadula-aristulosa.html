Wissadula aristulosa

Wissadula aristulosa, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that often have a distinctive arrowhead pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically enclosed within a spathe, a modified leaf structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight to flourish.

Wissadula aristulosa is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
