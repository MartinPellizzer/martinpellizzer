Wikstroemia pauciflora var. phymatoglossa

Wikstroemia pauciflora var. phymatoglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sandverbena, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
