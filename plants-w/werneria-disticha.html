Werneria disticha

Werneria disticha, commonly known as the two-branched fern, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, branched inflorescence. The plant features small, white flowers arranged in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Werneria disticha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its unique appearance.
