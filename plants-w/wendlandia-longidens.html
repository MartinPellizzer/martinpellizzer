Wendlandia longidens

Wendlandia longidens, commonly known as the long-stemmed bellflower, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in high-altitude environments. The plant is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a contrasting throat. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Wendlandia longidens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to well-drained soils.
