Wedelia discoidea

Wedelia discoidea, commonly known as the silverleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a silvery underside that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, often forming dense ground cover.
