Woodsia glabella f. rufescens

Woodsia glabella f. rufescens, commonly known as the rusty woodsia, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically dark green with a rusty or reddish underside. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are usually compact, with a dense, tufted growth habit.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and woodland settings.
