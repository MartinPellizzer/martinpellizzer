Wissadula grandifolia var. grandifolia

Wissadula grandifolia var. grandifolia, commonly known as the giant forestiera, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and arranged opposite along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for coastal and sandy soils, where it exhibits good drought tolerance.
