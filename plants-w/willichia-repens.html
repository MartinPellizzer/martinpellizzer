Willichia repens

Willichia repens, commonly known as the creeping Willichia, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which are often hidden among the foliage. Willichia repens thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
