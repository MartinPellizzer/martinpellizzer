Wedelia acapulcensis

Wedelia acapulcensis, commonly known as the Mexican carpetweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.
