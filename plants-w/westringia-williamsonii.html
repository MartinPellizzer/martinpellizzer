Westringia williamsonii

Westringia williamsonii, commonly known as the pink flowering rock rose, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, aromatic leaves and produces clusters of pink or white flowers during late winter to early spring. The plant is well-suited to rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal pruning, making it an ideal choice for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its compact form and long flowering period add ornamental value to gardens.
