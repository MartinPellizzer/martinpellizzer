Wissadula ferruginea

Wissadula ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty wissadula, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. It has hairy, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Wissadula ferruginea is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
