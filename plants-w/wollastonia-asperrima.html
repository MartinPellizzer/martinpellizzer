Wollastonia asperrima

Wollastonia asperrima, commonly known as the rough wattle, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Wollastonia asperrima is an important plant in local ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
