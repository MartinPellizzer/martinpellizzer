Woodwardia thelypteroides

Woodwardia thelypteroides, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, and deeply lobed fronds that resemble a fern with a silvery sheen. Native to eastern North America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.
