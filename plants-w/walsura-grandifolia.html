Walsura grandifolia

Walsura grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is also cultivated in horticultural settings.
