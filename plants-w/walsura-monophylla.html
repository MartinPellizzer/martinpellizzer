Walsura monophylla

Walsura monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, seed-bearing fruits. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
