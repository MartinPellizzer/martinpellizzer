Weinmannia silvatica var. rollottii

Weinmannia silvatica var. rollottii, commonly known as the Rollot's silverleaf, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, waxy hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a leathery texture. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

This species is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in cloud forests.
