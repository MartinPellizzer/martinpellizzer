Weinmannia hirta var. brasiliensis

Weinmannia hirta var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian velvetleaf, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dense, velvety foliage. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by spherical, woody fruits.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
