Watsonia plantaginea

Watsonia plantaginea, commonly known as the Natal lily, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and produces showy, cone-like inflorescences that bear numerous pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant is characterized by its robust rhizomatous growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Watsonia plantaginea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
