Watsonamra tinajita

Watsonamra tinajita, commonly known as the Mexican firebush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Watsonamra tinajita is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
