Willoughbya obovata

Willoughbya obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed willoughbya, is a striking herbaceous plant native to eastern Australia.

It features large, showy white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has broad, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. Willoughbya obovata is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
