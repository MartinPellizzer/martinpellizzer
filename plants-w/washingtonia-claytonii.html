Washingtonia claytonii

Washingtonia claytonii, commonly known as the Mexican fan palm, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of northern Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, fan-shaped leaves. Its roots are adapted to thrive in well-drained soils, making it suitable for dry climates.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in urban landscapes.
