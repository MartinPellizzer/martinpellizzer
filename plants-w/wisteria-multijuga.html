Wisteria multijuga

Wisteria multijuga, commonly known as the Japanese wisteria, is a deciduous climbing plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of 10 to 20 oval-shaped leaflets. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, often reaching heights of up to 30 feet.

Wisteria multijuga is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and along fences.
