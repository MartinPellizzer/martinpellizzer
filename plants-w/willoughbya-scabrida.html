Willoughbya scabrida

Willoughbya scabrida, commonly known as the rough willow.

It is a flowering plant native to northern Australia, characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Willoughbya scabrida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
