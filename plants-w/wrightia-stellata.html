Wrightia stellata

Wrightia stellata, commonly known as the star-leaf wrightia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that often have a distinctive star-shaped appearance when viewed from above. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Wrightia stellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in lowland forests.
