Wasabia yunnanensis

Wasabia yunnanensis, commonly known as Yunnan wasabi, is a rare and lesser-known member of the Brassicaceae family.

Native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China, it thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments. The plant produces small, white flowers and has a distinctive, pungent aroma similar to true wasabi. Its leaves are typically broad and slightly hairy, adapted to its high-altitude habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and unique characteristics, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
