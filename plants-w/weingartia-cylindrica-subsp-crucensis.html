Weingartia cylindrica subsp. crucensis

Weingartia cylindrica subsp.

crucensis, commonly known as the Crucensis Pincushion Cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to South America. It features a short, cylindrical stem that is often covered in dense, white, woolly hair.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor succulent gardens.
