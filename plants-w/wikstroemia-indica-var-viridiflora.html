Wikstroemia indica var. viridiflora

Wikstroemia indica var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered sandarac tree, is a variant of the sandarac plant native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its bark is fragrant and has been historically used in the production of sandarac resin.

The botanical features of this plant make it notable for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
