Weigeltia sylvatica

Weigeltia sylvatica, commonly known as the red-veined sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins and a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Weigeltia sylvatica is often used in woodland gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize easily.
