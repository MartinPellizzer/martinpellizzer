Watsonamra macrophylla

Watsonamra macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf Watsonamra, is a tropical plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Watsonamra macrophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
