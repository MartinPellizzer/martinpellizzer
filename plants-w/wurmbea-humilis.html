Wurmbea humilis

Wurmbea humilis, commonly known as the small yellow arum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the genus Wurmbea within the family Araceae, which includes many species of arums and calla lilies. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that are surrounded by a spathe, a modified leaf-like structure. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Wurmbea humilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and delicate floral display.
