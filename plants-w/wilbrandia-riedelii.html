Wilbrandia riedelii

Wilbrandia riedelii, commonly known as the Riedel's wilbrandia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular flowers in shades of red and orange. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
