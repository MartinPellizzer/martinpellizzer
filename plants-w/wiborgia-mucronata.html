Wiborgia mucronata

Wiborgia mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-tipped bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of purple or blue. The leaves are lanceolate and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens in suitable climates.
