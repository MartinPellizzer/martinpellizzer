Wissadula diffusa

Wissadula diffusa, commonly known as the silver spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, spider-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Wissadula diffusa is known for its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early autumn.
