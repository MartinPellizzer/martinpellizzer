Wissadula nudiflora

Wissadula nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flowered pincushion, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, rounded shape with dense clusters of fleshy, spiny leaves that give it a pincushion appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, hairy stems. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Wissadula nudiflora is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
