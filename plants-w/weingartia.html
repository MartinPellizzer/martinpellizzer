Weingartia

Weingartia, commonly known as the rat-tail cactus, is a genus of flowering plants in the cactus family, Cactaceae.

These succulent plants are native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. They are characterized by their short, cylindrical stems that often resemble a rat's tail, hence their common name. The stems are typically covered in white or grayish spines, and they produce small, yellow flowers during the warmer months.

Weingartia species are popular among cactus enthusiasts due to their compact size, ease of care, and aesthetic appeal.
