Waltheria lantanifolia

Waltheria lantanifolia, commonly known as the purpleleaf sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
