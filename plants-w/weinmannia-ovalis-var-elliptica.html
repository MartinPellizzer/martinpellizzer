Weinmannia ovalis var. elliptica

Weinmannia ovalis var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic weinmannia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the coastline. The plant features lanceolate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is adapted to coastal climates and is often found in areas with high salt exposure and strong winds.
