Waldsteinia ternata subsp. maximowicziana

Waldsteinia ternata subsp.

maximowicziana, commonly known as the Maximowicz's cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, ovate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a raceme, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
