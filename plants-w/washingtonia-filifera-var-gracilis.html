Washingtonia filifera var. gracilis

Washingtonia filifera var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender fan palm, is a variant of the desert fan palm native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, fan-shaped leaves and tall, slender trunk, which distinguishes it from the more robust forms of Washingtonia. This plant thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The plant's slow growth rate makes it suitable for small spaces and container gardening.
