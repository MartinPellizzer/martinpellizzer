Werneria humilis f. articulata

Werneria humilis f. articulata, commonly known as the articulated werneria, is a unique variant of the base-growing werneria species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, segmented stems that give it a peculiar, almost skeletal appearance. The plant typically features small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its botanical interest lies in its unusual morphology and its role as a rare, specialized species in certain ecosystems.
