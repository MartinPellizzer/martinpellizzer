Wrightia religiosa

Wrightia religiosa, commonly known as the sacred tree or religious tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Wrightiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically orange or yellow in color. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its cultural significance in various religious practices, it is also valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
