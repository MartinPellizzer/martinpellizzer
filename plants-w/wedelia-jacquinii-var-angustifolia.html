Wedelia jacquinii var. angustifolia

Wedelia jacquinii var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences and bloom during the warmer months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for ground cover or in shaded gardens.
