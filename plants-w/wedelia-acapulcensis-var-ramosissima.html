Wedelia acapulcensis var. ramosissima

Wedelia acapulcensis var. ramosissima, commonly known as the Mexican bluebells, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, sky-blue flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
