Walafrida albanensis

Walafrida albanensis, commonly known as the Albanian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are borne in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Walafrida albanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in wildflower gardens and natural habitats.
