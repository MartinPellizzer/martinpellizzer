Weingartia vasqueziana var. losenickyana

Weingartia vasqueziana var. losenickyana, commonly known as the Losenicky's barrel cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It features a cylindrical, ribbed stem that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height, with a greenish-gray coloration. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for collectors and gardeners in dry climates.
