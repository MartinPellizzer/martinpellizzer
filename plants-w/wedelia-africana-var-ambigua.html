Wedelia africana var. ambigua

Wedelia africana var. ambigua, commonly known as the African violet or African bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both garden beds and container cultivation.

Its ornamental value and adaptability have made it a popular choice in horticulture across the globe.
