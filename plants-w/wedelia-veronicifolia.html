Wedelia veronicifolia

Wedelia veronicifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false geranium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Its showy, clustered flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent central disk.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
