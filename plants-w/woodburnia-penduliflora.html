Woodburnia penduliflora

Woodburnia penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flower woodburnia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences that bear small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Woodburnia penduliflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in warm climates for its attractive floral display.
