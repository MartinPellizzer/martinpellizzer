Wendlandia arabica subsp. aethiopica

Wendlandia arabica subsp.

aethiopica, commonly known as the Ethiopian wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun exposure. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
