Woodsia oregana f. cathcartiana

Woodsia oregana f. cathcartiana, commonly known as the Cathcart's wood fern, is a fern species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically dark green with a bluish tinge. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
