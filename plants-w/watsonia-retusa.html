Watsonia retusa

Watsonia retusa, commonly known as the purple firecracker, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, grass-like leaves and produces showy, red-orange inflorescences that resemble firecrackers, hence its common name. The plant is part of the Iridaceae family, which includes irises and gladiolas. Watsonia retusa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its vibrant blooms attract pollinators and add a dramatic focal point to gardens.
