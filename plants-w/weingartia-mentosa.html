Weingartia mentosa

Weingartia mentosa, commonly known as the Mentosa cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Weingartia mentosa is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
