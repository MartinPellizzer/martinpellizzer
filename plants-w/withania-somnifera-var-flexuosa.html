Withania somnifera var. flexuosa

Withania somnifera var. flexuosa, commonly known as Indian winter cherry or ashwagandha, is a variant of the widely used medicinal plant Withania somnifera.

It is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its yellow-orange berries and hairy stems, which help it retain moisture in dry climates. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly serrated edge, while the flowers are small and greenish-yellow.

This variety is valued for its adaptogenic properties and is often used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
