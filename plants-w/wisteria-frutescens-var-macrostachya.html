Wisteria frutescens var. macrostachya

Wisteria frutescens var. macrostachya, commonly known as the Southern blue mist vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It produces long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, blue to purple flowers in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate structure, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, often reaching several meters in length.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
