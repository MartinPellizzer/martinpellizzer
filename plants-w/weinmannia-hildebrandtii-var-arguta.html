Weinmannia hildebrandtii var. arguta

Weinmannia hildebrandtii var. arguta, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's weinmannia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and northern Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and unique, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
