Willoughbya odoratissima

Willoughbya odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces tall, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant is notable for its fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and swampy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with moist soil conditions.
