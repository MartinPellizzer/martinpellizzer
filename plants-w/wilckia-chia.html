Wilckia chia

Wilckia chia, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain tiny, black seeds. Wilckia chia thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators make it a popular choice for gardeners.
