Warmingia zamorana

Warmingia zamorana, commonly known as the flame grass, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, fiery red foliage that turns orange and yellow in the fall, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking seasonal color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by distinctive, elongated seed pods. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Warmingia zamorana is often used in landscaping for its visual impact and ability to add warmth to garden designs.
