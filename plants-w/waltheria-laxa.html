Waltheria laxa

Waltheria laxa, commonly known as the slender false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender, hairy stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

Waltheria laxa is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to wet or seasonally flooded habitats.
