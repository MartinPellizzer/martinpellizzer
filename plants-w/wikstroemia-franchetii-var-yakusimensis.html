Wikstroemia franchetii var. yakusimensis

Wikstroemia franchetii var. yakusimensis, commonly known as the Yakushima whiteflower waxtree, is a rare shrub native to the subtropical forests of Yakushima Island, Japan.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate ornamental value to its appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive visual texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its limited distribution and unique ecological niche, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
