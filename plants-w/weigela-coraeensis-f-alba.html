Weigela coraeensis f. alba

Weigela coraeensis f. alba, commonly known as the white weigela, is a cultivar of the weigela plant characterized by its showy, tubular flowers.

This plant is native to Japan and is prized for its vibrant, semi-double blooms that appear in early summer. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they are borne on upright, branching stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

The plant is also noted for its attractive, ovate leaves that provide ornamental value throughout the growing season.
