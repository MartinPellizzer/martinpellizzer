Wilckia bicolor

Wilckia bicolor, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that resemble small sunbursts, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Wilckia bicolor thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also known for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
