Weinmannia subcordata

Weinmannia subcordata, commonly known as the velvetleaf weinmannia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth, waxy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal chaparral and scrub habitats.
