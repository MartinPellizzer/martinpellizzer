Washingtonia mexicana

Washingtonia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican date palm, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a tall, slender tree with a single trunk and feathery, pinnate leaves that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red fruits. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires well-drained soil.

Washingtonia mexicana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in urban landscapes.
