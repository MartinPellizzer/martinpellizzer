Widdringtonia

Widdringtonia, commonly known as the yellowwood tree, is a genus of evergreen coniferous trees native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The bark of Widdringtonia is thick and fissured, providing protection against fire and drought. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it suitable for furniture and construction.

The plant plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native region, supporting various forms of wildlife.
