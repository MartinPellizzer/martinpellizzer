Willemetia lanata

Willemetia lanata, commonly known as the woolly willow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found near rivers or in wetlands.

Willemetia lanata is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
