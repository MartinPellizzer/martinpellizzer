Wrightia afzelii

Wrightia afzelii, commonly known as the African ash or African white wood, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Wrightiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
