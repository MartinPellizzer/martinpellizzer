Woodrowia diandra

Woodrowia diandra, commonly known as the sandalwood tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has simple, opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green in color. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by small, round fruits.

The tree is often cultivated for its essential oils and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
