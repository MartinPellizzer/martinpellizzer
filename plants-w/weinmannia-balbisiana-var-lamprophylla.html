Weinmannia balbisiana var. lamprophylla

Weinmannia balbisiana var. lamprophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or dry forest environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
