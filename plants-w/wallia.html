Wallia

Wallia, commonly known as the wallia plant, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its unique botanical features.

It typically has smooth, waxy leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in structures called cyathia, a common trait in the Euphorbiaceae family. Wallia thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various environments.

Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with some species reaching heights of up to several feet.
