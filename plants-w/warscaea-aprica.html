Warscaea aprica

Warscaea aprica, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small, greenish fig-like structure, matures to a yellowish color and is edible.

Warscaea aprica thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
