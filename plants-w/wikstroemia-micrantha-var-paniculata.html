Wikstroemia micrantha var. paniculata

Wikstroemia micrantha var. paniculata, commonly known as the paniculate wikstroemia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in panicles. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its botanical features include a woody stem and small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
