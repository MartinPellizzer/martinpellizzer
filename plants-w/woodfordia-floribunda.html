Woodfordia floribunda

Woodfordia floribunda, commonly known as the African rose or flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often appearing in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Woodfordia floribunda is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
