Weingartia tiraquensis var. totorensis

Weingartia tiraquensis var. totorensis, commonly known as the Totorense Paddle Cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with prominent, paddle-shaped ribs that are often tinged with a pale blue or greenish hue. The plant produces small, white flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a delicate contrast to its otherwise muted foliage. It is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and requires minimal water to thrive.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for succulent collectors and desert gardens.
