Wyethia arizonica

Wyethia arizonica, commonly known as Arizona sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and high temperatures. Its yellow-orange flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.
