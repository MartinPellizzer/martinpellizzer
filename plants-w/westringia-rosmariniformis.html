Westringia rosmariniformis

Westringia rosmariniformis, commonly known as lemon myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and compact growth habit.
