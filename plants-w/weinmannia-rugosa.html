Weinmannia rugosa

Weinmannia rugosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved weinmannia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Weinmanniaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a waxy coating.

Weinmannia rugosa produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.
