Wisteria sinensis var. brevidentata

Wisteria sinensis var. brevidentata, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that have short, blunt teeth, giving it its specific epithet "brevidentata.". This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

However, it can be invasive in some regions due to its vigorous growth and ability to outcompete native plants.
