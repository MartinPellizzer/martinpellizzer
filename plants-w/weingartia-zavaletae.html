Weingartia zavaletae

Weingartia zavaletae, commonly known as the Zavaletta prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and clusters of spiny, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a desirable addition to both indoor and outdoor collections.
