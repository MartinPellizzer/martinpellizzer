Wilbrandia verticillata

Wilbrandia verticillata, commonly known as the spiral leaf or false spider plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and strap-like, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Wilbrandia verticillata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
