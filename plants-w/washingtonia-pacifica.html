Washingtonia pacifica

Washingtonia pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific date palm, is a species native to coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a tall, slender palm with long, feathery fronds that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant has a slender trunk with a fibrous, spongy exterior and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible dates. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping in arid climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for local wildlife and stabilizing coastal dunes.
