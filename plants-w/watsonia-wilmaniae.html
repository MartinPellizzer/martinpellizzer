Watsonia wilmaniae

Watsonia wilmaniae, commonly known as the Wilman's Watsonia, is a striking South African bulbous perennial belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces a cluster of bright, showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Watsonia wilmaniae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
