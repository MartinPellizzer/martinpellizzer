Wendlandia glomerulata

Wendlandia glomerulata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

Wendlandia glomerulata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
