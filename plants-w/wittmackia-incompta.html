Wittmackia incompta

Wittmackia incompta, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting yellow or white throat. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ability to thrive in warm, humid environments. Its foliage consists of glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate.

Wittmackia incompta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
