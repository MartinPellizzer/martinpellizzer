Wendlandia glabrata

Wendlandia glabrata, commonly known as the silverleaf waterplantain, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Wendlandia glabrata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
