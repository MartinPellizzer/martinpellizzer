Weinmannia burserifolia

Weinmannia burserifolia, commonly known as the Bursera-like Weinmannia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Weinmanniaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive, compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
