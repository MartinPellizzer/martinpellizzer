Washingtonia obtusa

Washingtonia obtusa, commonly known as the Mexican fan palm, is a tall, single-stemmed palm native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a slender, unbranched trunk with a fibrous, spongy texture and a crown of large, fan-shaped leaves that are often split into segments. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its widespread use in both urban and suburban environments.
