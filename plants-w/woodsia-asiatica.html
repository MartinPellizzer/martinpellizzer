Woodsia asiatica

Woodsia asiatica, commonly known as the Asian woodsia, is a perennial fern native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered spore cases called sorus, which are located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a sought-after species in horticulture and botanical studies.
