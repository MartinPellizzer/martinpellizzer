Werneria setosa

Werneria setosa, commonly known as the woolly fern, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araliaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly appearance, with hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Native to tropical regions of Africa, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, round berries.

Its unique morphology makes it a distinctive and visually appealing ornamental plant.
