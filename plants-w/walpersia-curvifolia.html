Walpersia curvifolia

Walpersia curvifolia, commonly known as the curl-leaved daphne, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling leaves that give it its common name, and it produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.

Walpersia curvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blossoms.
