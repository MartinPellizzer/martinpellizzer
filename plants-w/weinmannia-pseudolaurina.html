Weinmannia pseudolaurina

Weinmannia pseudolaurina, commonly known as the false laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Weinmanniaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California, and thrives in coastal and inland areas with a Mediterranean climate. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically leathery and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Weinmannia pseudolaurina is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
