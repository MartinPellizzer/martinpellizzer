Wendlandia bicuspidata

Wendlandia bicuspidata, commonly known as the pink flame bamboo, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, bamboo-like stems that can reach heights of up to 10 feet. The plant produces clusters of bright pink to red tubular flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a dramatic focal point in their landscapes.
