Wedelia oblongifolia

Wedelia oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, bushy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Wedelia oblongifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
