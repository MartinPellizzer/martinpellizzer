Wetria australiensis

Wetria australiensis, commonly known as the Australian sandplain lily, is a flowering plant native to the sandy soils of Western Australia.

It is a perennial herb with erect, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as sandplains and heathlands.

Wetria australiensis is adapted to dry conditions and is considered a characteristic species of the Southwest Australian floristic region.
