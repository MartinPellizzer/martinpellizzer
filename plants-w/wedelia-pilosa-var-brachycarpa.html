Wedelia pilosa var. brachycarpa

Wedelia pilosa var. brachycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruit wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that remains closed until maturity, releasing small seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
