Willemetia stipitata subsp. albanica

Willemetia stipitata subsp.

albanica, commonly known as the Albanian velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is characterized by its robust root system and ability to tolerate moderate shade.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique leaf morphology and ecological adaptability.
