Widdringtonia mahonii

Widdringtonia mahonii, commonly known as the yellowwood tree, is a tall, evergreen conifer native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its straight, dark brown trunk and long, narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern. The tree produces small, yellowish-green cones that mature into reddish-brown cones, releasing winged seeds. It is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.

Widdringtonia mahonii is also an important species in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
