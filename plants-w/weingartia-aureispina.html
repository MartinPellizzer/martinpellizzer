Weingartia aureispina

Weingartia aureispina, commonly known as the golden spiny cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in bright yellow spines. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 centimeters and produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cactus collections.
