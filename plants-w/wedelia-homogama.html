Wedelia homogama

Wedelia homogama, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a low shrub with spreading stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Wedelia homogama is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
