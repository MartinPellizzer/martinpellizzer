Wikstroemia salicifolia

Wikstroemia salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf wikstroemia, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped, willow-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually greenish-white in color, appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and resilience to wet conditions.
