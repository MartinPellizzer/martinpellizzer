Wedelia fructicosa

Wedelia fructicosa, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its prostrate growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warm seasons, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Wedelia fructicosa is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and ability to thrive in sunny, well-drained soils.
