Watsonia punctata var. triticea

Watsonia punctata var. triticea, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright orange to red flowers, which are borne on sturdy, upright stems. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically green with a slight blue or silver hue. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Watsonia punctata var. triticea is known for its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
