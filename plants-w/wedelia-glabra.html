Wedelia glabra

Wedelia glabra, commonly known as the smooth wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or herb, with simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the warm seasons.

Wedelia glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
