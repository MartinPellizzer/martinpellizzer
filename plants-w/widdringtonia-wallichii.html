Widdringtonia wallichii

Widdringtonia wallichii, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a tall evergreen tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its straight, dark brown trunk and smooth, pale grey bark. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by winged seeds dispersed by wind. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stem.

This species is valued for its durable wood and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
