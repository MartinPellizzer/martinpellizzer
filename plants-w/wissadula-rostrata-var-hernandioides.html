Wissadula rostrata var. hernandioides

Wissadula rostrata var. hernandioides, commonly known as the horned wissadula, is a unique variant of the wissadula genus native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, horn-like structures that emerge from the stems, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it can thrive with indirect light. Its leaves are usually broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and potential ecological significance in its ecosystem.
