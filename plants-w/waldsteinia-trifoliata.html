Waldsteinia trifoliata

Waldsteinia trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf goldcrest, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in late spring. The plant features trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in rocky or open woodland habitats.

Waldsteinia trifoliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in native plant gardens.
