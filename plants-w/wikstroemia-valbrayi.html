Wikstroemia valbrayi

Wikstroemia valbrayi, commonly known as the Hawaiian sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal and upland habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly waxy texture.

The species is valued for its aromatic wood, which was historically used in traditional Hawaiian culture.
