Withania somnifera var. somalensis

Withania somnifera var. somalensis, commonly known as African ashwagandha, is a variant of the widely recognized Indian adaptogen Withania somnifera.

This plant is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of East Africa, including Somalia and Ethiopia. It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring yellow flowers and berries. The plant contains withanolides, which are believed to have adaptogenic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both traditional medicine and modern pharmacological research.
