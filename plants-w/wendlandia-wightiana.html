Wendlandia wightiana

Wendlandia wightiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish-brown. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Wendlandia wightiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and vibrant blooms.
