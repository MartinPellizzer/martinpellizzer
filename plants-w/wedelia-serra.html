Wedelia serra

Wedelia serra, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, upright leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The flowers bloom throughout the growing season, adding a vibrant splash of color to its habitat.
