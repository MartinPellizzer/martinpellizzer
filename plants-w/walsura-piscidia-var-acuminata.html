Walsura piscidia var. acuminata

Walsura piscidia var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mangrove, is a variant of the mangrove species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant typically grows in brackish or saltwater environments, such as mangrove forests, where it plays a crucial role in coastal ecosystem stability. Its roots form a complex network that helps prevent soil erosion and provides habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.

This variant is particularly adapted to tidal conditions, showcasing its resilience in fluctuating salinity levels.
