Wangia florulenta

Wangia florulenta, commonly known as the rainbow plant, is a rare and striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Queensland, Australia, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble a rainbow. The plant has a short, stocky stem with a single, large, showy inflorescence that consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its unique appearance makes it a popular specimen plant in botanical gardens and among plant enthusiasts.
