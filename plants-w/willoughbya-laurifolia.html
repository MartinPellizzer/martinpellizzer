Willoughbya laurifolia

Willoughbya laurifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved willoughbya, is a tropical plant native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Willoughbya laurifolia thrives in wet, humid environments and is often found near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within its native habitat.
