Whiteochloa multiciliata

Whiteochloa multiciliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly sedge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Whiteochloa multiciliata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
