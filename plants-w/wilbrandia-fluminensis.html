Wilbrandia fluminensis

Wilbrandia fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid or the river orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments along riverbanks and in dense undergrowth. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often found in conservation programs aimed at preserving its unique botanical traits.
