Wedelia isolepis

Wedelia isolepis, commonly known as the small-leaved wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
