Walafrida barabei

Walafrida barabei, commonly known as the ancient cycad, is a prehistoric plant that thrived during the Mesozoic era.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like leaves. This plant is notable for its ability to survive in nutrient-poor soils and its slow growth rate. Fossil records suggest that it played a significant role in ancient ecosystems.

Despite its historical presence, Walafrida barabei is now extinct, making it a subject of interest for paleobotanists.
