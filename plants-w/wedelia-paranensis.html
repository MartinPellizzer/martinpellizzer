Wedelia paranensis

Wedelia paranensis, commonly known as the Brazilian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, hairy, and serrated leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
