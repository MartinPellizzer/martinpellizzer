Withania xalapensis

Withania xalapensis, commonly known as the Indian ginseng or ashwagandha, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and red, fleshy berries. The plant is notable for its adaptability to harsh environments, thriving in dry and semi-arid conditions. It has hairy stems and leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its roots, which are used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
