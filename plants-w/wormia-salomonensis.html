Wormia salomonensis

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a dense arrangement of tiny, overlapping leaves that help retain moisture. It lacks true roots and instead absorbs water and nutrients through its leaf surfaces.
