Wahlenbergia pinnata var. simplicifolia

Wahlenbergia pinnata var. simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, sky-blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically linear and arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
