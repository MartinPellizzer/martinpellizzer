Waltheria bracteosa

Waltheria bracteosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a soft texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.

Waltheria bracteosa is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
