Weigela candida

Weigela candida, commonly known as the white weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and a distinctive red or orange throat. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
