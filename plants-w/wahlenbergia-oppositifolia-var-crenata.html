Wahlenbergia oppositifolia var. crenata

Wahlenbergia oppositifolia var. crenata, commonly known as the serrated bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a wavy or serrated margin, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
