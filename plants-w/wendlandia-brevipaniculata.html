Wendlandia brevipaniculata

Wendlandia brevipaniculata, commonly known as the dwarf panicled bellflower, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant produces panicles of small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
