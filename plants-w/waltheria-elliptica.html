Waltheria elliptica

Waltheria elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 inches in length. Its showy, purple to pink flower heads appear in late summer and are composed of numerous small florets.

Waltheria elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
