Wedelia pulchella

Wedelia pulchella, commonly known as the purple crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are often purple or pink in color. It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
