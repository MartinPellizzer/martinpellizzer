Wikstroemia rotundifolia

Wikstroemia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf sandarac, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical to round leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is often used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
