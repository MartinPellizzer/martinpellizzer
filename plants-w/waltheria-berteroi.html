Waltheria berteroi

Waltheria berteroi, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Waltheria berteroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
