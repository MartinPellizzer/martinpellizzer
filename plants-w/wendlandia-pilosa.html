Wendlandia pilosa

Wendlandia pilosa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant features soft, fuzzy foliage that gives it its common name, and its flowers range in color from white to pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
