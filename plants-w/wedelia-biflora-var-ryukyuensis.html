Wedelia biflora var. ryukyuensis

Wedelia biflora var. ryukyuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu wedelia, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.
