Wendlandia tinctoria subsp. callitricha

Wendlandia tinctoria subsp.

callitricha, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
