Willoughbya acuminata

Willoughbya acuminata, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a striking tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its large, showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing during the Christmas season. The plant has a distinctive, tree-like structure with thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its native habitat. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly honeycreepers, which are attracted to its nectar.

Willoughbya acuminata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens around the world.
