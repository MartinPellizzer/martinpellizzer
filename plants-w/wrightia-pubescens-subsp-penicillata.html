Wrightia pubescens subsp. penicillata

Wrightia pubescens subsp.

penicillata, commonly known as the hairy wrightia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Wrightiaceae. It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Thailand and Malaysia.

The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and hairy foliage, which give it its common name. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
