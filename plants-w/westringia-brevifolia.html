Westringia brevifolia

Westringia brevifolia, commonly known as the coastal wallaby's foot, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically grey-green in color, with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or violet, which bloom from spring to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
