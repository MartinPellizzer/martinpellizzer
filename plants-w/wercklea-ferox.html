Wercklea ferox

Wercklea ferox, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that resemble a spine, hence its common name. Its flowers are typically purple or red, with showy bracts that attract pollinators.

Wercklea ferox is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown in containers or as a garden accent in warm climates.
