Wigginsia bezrucii

Wigginsia bezrucii, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers during the summer months. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing forage for local wildlife.

Wigginsia bezrucii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
