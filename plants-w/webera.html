Webera

Webera, commonly known as the Brazilian water lily, is a genus of aquatic plants native to South America.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant features broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer to reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually white or pink, with a distinctive pattern of veins.

Webera plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
