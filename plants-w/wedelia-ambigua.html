Wedelia ambigua

Wedelia ambigua, commonly known as the creeping wedgeflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial with prostrate stems that form dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is well adapted to moist, shaded environments and is sometimes used in horticulture for ground cover.
