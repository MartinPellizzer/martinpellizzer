Wahlenbergia fasciculata var. pilosa

Wahlenbergia fasciculata var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
