Weigeltia schomburgkiana

Weigeltia schomburgkiana, commonly known as the Schomburgk's weigelt, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features attractive, showy clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Weigeltia schomburgkiana prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to many planting schemes.
