Wissadula zeylanica var. truncata

Wissadula zeylanica var. truncata, commonly known as the truncated wissadula, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water and survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
