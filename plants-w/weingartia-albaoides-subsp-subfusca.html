Weingartia albaoides subsp. subfusca

Weingartia albaoides subsp.

subfusca, commonly known as the White-Faced Pincushion Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a slight white or pale waxy coating.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing succulent that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
