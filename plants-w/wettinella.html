Wettinella

Wettinella, commonly known as the bottlebrush plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical inflorescences, which resemble a bottlebrush due to their dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Wettinella species are native to Australia and are often found in woodland or heathland environments.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their unique floral display.
