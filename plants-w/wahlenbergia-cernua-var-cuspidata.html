Wahlenbergia cernua var. cuspidata

Wahlenbergia cernua var. cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing herb with slender stems and small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal and sandy habitats, where it forms dense mats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
