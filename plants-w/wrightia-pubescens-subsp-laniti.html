Wrightia pubescens subsp. laniti

Wrightia pubescens subsp.

laniti, commonly known as the hairy wrightia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Wrightiaceae. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its subspecies name "laniti," derived from the Latin word for "hairy.". It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, and it is often found in moist, tropical environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
