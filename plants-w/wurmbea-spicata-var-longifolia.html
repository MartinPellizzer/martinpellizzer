Wurmbea spicata var. longifolia

Wurmbea spicata var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered yellowstar, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow, star-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its underground bulb, which stores nutrients for survival. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some regions.
