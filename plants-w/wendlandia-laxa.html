Wendlandia laxa

Wendlandia laxa, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name and help reduce water loss in its native habitat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Wendlandia laxa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
