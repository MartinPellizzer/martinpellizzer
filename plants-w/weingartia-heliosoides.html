Weingartia heliosoides

Weingartia heliosoides, commonly known as the sun-facing cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a globular shape and a distinctive yellowish-green color. The plant features areolae, which are small, raised bumps containing the plant's spines and flowers. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cactus collections.
