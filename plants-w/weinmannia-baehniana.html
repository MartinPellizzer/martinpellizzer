Weinmannia baehniana

Weinmannia baehniana, commonly known as the Mexican wineberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners in arid regions.
