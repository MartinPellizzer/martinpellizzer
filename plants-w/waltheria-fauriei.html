Waltheria fauriei

Waltheria fauriei, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and its clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
