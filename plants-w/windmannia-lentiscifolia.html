Windmannia lentiscifolia

Windmannia lentiscifolia, commonly known as the fragrant windmannia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Windmannia lentiscifolia is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
