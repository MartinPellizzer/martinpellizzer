Wedelia acapulcensis var. hispida

Wedelia acapulcensis var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy wedelia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and covered with fine, hairy pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
