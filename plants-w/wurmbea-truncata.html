Wurmbea truncata

Wurmbea truncata, commonly known as the African star of Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, tuberous rhizome and produces striking blue to purple, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
