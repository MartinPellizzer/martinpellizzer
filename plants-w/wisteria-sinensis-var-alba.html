Wisteria sinensis var. alba

Wisteria sinensis var. alba, commonly known as the Chinese white wisteria, is a deciduous climbing plant native to China.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement, consisting of 10 to 20 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. Its stems are woody and can grow up to 30 feet in length, making it an impressive ornamental plant.

The flowers are followed by long, bean-like pods that contain several seeds.
