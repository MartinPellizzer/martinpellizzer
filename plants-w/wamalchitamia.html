Wamalchitamia

Wamalchitamia, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil.
