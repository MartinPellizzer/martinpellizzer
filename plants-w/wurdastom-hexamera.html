Wurdastom hexamera

Wurdastom hexamera, commonly known as the six-angled wonderplant, is a rare flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its unique hexagonal leaf structure and vibrant blue petals that attract pollinators. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a tough, fibrous casing. Its growth is slow, requiring specific soil conditions and high levels of sunlight.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is protected in several conservation areas.
