Wagneriopteris ogasawarensis

Wagneriopteris ogasawarensis, commonly known as the Ogasawara fern, is a fossilized plant from the Late Paleozoic era.

It belongs to the order Sphenophyta and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and pinnately divided leaves. The plant's structure suggests it was a vascular plant with a complex root system and upright growth habit. It is primarily found in fossil records from the Ogasawara Islands in Japan.

This species provides important insights into the evolutionary development of early ferns and their ecological roles in ancient ecosystems.
