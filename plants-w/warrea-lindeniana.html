Warrea lindeniana

Warrea lindeniana, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark bark and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has small, pale green leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that are pollinated by insects. Its hard, heavy wood is highly valued for construction and furniture-making.

Warrea lindeniana also plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
