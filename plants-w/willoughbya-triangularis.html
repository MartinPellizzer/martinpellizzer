Willoughbya triangularis

Willoughbya triangularis, commonly known as the golden trumpet or golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a triangular pattern, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Willoughbya triangularis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
