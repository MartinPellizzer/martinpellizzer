Willoughbya lindleyana

Willoughbya lindleyana, commonly known as the Australian waxflower, is a striking member of the Proteaceae family native to Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture.

Willoughbya lindleyana thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of southern Australia.
