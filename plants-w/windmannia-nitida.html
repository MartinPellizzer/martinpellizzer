Windmannia nitida

Windmannia nitida, commonly known as the slender windmannia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Windmannia nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
