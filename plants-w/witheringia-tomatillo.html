Witheringia tomatillo

Witheringia tomatillo, commonly known as Mexican husk tomato, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and oval, pointed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into spherical, spiky fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when they turn from green to a reddish or purplish hue.

Witheringia tomatillo is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in traditional cuisines.
