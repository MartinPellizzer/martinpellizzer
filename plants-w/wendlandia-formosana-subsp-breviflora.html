Wendlandia formosana subsp. breviflora

Wendlandia formosana subsp.

breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered wendlandia, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers.

The plant typically blooms in the spring, producing vibrant pink or red inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.
