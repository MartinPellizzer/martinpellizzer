Wendlandia paniculata subsp. nobilis

Wendlandia paniculata subsp.

nobilis, commonly known as the noble wendlandia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, branched inflorescences.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and has a slightly hairy texture, adding to its visual appeal.
