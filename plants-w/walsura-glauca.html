Walsura glauca

Walsura glauca, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a pale underside. The tree produces small, round, yellow fruits that contain one or two seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various soil types.
