Waltheria tomentosa

Waltheria tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces trumpet-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the afternoon, hence its common name.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
