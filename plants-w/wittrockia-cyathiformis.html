Wittrockia cyathiformis

Wittrockia cyathiformis, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into distinctive, cup-like seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Wittrockia cyathiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
