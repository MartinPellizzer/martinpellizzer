Witheringia solanacea var. silvigaudens

Witheringia solanacea var. silvigaudens, commonly known as the forest withering vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and moths, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
