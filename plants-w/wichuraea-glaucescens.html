Wichuraea glaucescens

Wichuraea glaucescens, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in dense, showy clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
