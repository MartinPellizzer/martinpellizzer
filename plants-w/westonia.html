Westonia

Westonia, commonly known as the Westonia flower or Westonia shrub, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Proteaceae.

It is native to South Africa and consists of several species, including Westonia glabra and Westonia fruticosa. These plants are characterized by their dense, evergreen foliage and showy, often white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. They are typically shrubs that thrive in well-drained, sandy soils and require full sun to partial shade.

Westonia species are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and adaptability to coastal climates.
