Weingartia ambigua

Weingartia ambigua, commonly known as the Mexican cactus or the weingartia cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the cactus family, Cactaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its compact, spherical shape and ribbed structure. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or summer. It has short, spiny areoles and is well adapted to arid environments.

Weingartia ambigua is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
