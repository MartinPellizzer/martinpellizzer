Williamsia viridescens

Williamsia viridescens, commonly known as the green willowsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like blades and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
