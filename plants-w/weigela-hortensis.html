Weigela hortensis

Weigela hortensis, commonly known as the garden weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The flowers, which range in color from pink to red, are borne in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant produces small, oval berries that ripen in the fall, adding visual interest to the landscape.

Its leaves are ovate and have a glossy texture, providing attractive foliage throughout the growing season.
