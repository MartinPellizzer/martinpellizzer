Weinmannia

Weinmannia, commonly known as the tree fern or silver tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Weinmanniaceae.

These plants are native to South America, particularly Brazil, and are characterized by their unique, often palm-like appearance. Weinmannia species typically have a slender, woody stem with a crown of feathery, compound leaves that give them a fern-like appearance. They are often found in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests.

The genus is notable for its ecological importance and ornamental value in horticulture.
