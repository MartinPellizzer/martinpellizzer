Wisteria floribunda f. violacea-plena

Wisteria floribunda f. violacea-plena, commonly known as the Japanese wisteria with double flowers, is a cultivar of the wisteria plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, double flowers that are typically violet in color, adding a rich visual display to gardens. The plant is a vigorous climber with long, pendulous clusters of blossoms that appear in early summer. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in ornamental gardening and as a climbing plant.
