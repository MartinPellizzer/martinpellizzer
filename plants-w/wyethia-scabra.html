Wyethia scabra

Wyethia scabra, commonly known as the rough wyethia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are rough and hairy, and its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Wyethia scabra is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use by indigenous peoples.
