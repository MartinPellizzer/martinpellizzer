Weinmannia balbisiana var. subovata

Weinmannia balbisiana var. subovata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lanceolate leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in warmer regions.
