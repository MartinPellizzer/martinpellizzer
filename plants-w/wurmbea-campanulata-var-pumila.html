Wurmbea campanulata var. pumila

Wurmbea campanulata var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf African bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has strap-like leaves that are narrow and grass-like, growing close to the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in gardens.
