Waitzia viridis

Waitzia viridis, commonly known as the green waitzia, is a rare and lesser-known species within the orchid family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a spike, which is a unique feature among its relatives. It is native to specific regions in South America and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
