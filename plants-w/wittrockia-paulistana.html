Wittrockia paulistana

Wittrockia paulistana, commonly known as the Brazilian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with prominent black spots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are palmately compound, consisting of five to seven leaflets, and it produces a short, thick rhizome.

Wittrockia paulistana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
