Weingartia pulquinensis var. mairanensis

Weingartia pulquinensis var. mairanensis, commonly known as the Mairanensis barrel cactus, is a compact, columnar cactus native to northern Argentina.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure and produces yellow flowers during the spring. The plant is characterized by its dense, spiny areoles and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This variety is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
