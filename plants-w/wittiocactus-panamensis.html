Wittiocactus panamensis

Wittiocactus panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian witch cactus, is a unique cactus native to Panama.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in spines. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are typically short-lived but striking. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
