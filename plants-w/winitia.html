Winitia

Winitia, commonly known as the wild fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, green fig-like fruits that mature to a yellowish or reddish color. It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Winitia is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
