Werneria cortusifolia

Werneria cortusifolia, commonly known as the alpine penny-cress, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes.

Werneria cortusifolia plays a role in stabilizing mountainous terrain and supports local pollinator populations.
