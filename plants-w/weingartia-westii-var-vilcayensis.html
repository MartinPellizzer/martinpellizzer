Weingartia westii var. vilcayensis

Weingartia westii var. vilcayensis, commonly known as the Vilcayensis Cactus, is a compact, slow-growing succulent native to the Andes Mountains of Peru.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a single or few spiny areoles, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant has a dense, bushy appearance with short, cylindrical stems that often branch out near the base.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
