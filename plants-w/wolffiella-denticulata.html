Wolffiella denticulata

Wolffiella denticulata, commonly known as the toothed wolfberry, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a distinctive toothed edge, contributing to its common name.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a deep red or orange color, often attracting various bird species.
