Wandersong exserta

Wandersong exserta, commonly known as the skyward bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to two meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Wandersong exserta is adapted to cold, arid environments and requires well-drained soil to thrive.
