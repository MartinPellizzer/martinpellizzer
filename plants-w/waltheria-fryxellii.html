Waltheria fryxellii

Waltheria fryxellii, commonly known as the Fryxell's ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Waltheria fryxellii is adapted to prairie and open woodland habitats, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
