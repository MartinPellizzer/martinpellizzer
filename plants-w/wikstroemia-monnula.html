Wikstroemia monnula

Wikstroemia monnula, commonly known as the small-flowered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, pale green leaves and clusters of tiny, fragrant white or pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize sandy soils in ecological restoration projects.
