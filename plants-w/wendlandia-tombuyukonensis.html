Wendlandia tombuyukonensis

Wendlandia tombuyukonensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian bellflower, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a prominent yellow center. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
