Weingartia tuberculatochrysantha

Weingartia tuberculatochrysantha, commonly known as the yellow-armed cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, yellowish tubercles that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by cylindrical, ribbed fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
