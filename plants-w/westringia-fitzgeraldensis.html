Westringia fitzgeraldensis

Westringia fitzgeraldensis, commonly known as Fitzgerald's westringia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, woolly leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to violet, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
