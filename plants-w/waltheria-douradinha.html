Waltheria douradinha

Waltheria douradinha, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant features hairy, branched stems and lobed leaves with a slightly rough texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, with five petals, and it produces small, fleshy berries that contain toxic seeds.
