Weinmannia exigua

Weinmannia exigua, commonly known as the narrowleaf weinmannia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and canyons.

Weinmannia exigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
