Wedelia buphthalmoides var. dominicensis

Wedelia buphthalmoides var. dominicensis, commonly known as the Dominican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woods.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
