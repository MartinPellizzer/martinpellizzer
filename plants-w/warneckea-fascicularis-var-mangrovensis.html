Warneckea fascicularis var. mangrovensis

Warneckea fascicularis var. mangrovensis, commonly known as the mangrove warneckea, is a variant of the warneckea species native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that thrives in brackish water environments, often found in mangrove ecosystems. The plant has distinctive, lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by woody, clustered fruits.

This variant is adapted to tolerate saltwater and plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils.
