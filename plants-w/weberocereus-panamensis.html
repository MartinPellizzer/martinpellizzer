Weberocereus panamensis

Weberocereus panamensis, commonly known as the Panama night bloomer, is a cacti species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its columnar stem and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-resistant. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bats and moths, which are attracted to its strong scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
