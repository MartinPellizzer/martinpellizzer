Weinmannia laurina

Weinmannia laurina, commonly known as the silverleaf weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture. It produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody, cone-like fruits.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
