Wissadula rostrata var. caribaea

Wissadula rostrata var. caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean wax palm, is a distinctive tropical plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of long, pinnate leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
