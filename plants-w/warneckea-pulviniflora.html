Warneckea pulviniflora

Warneckea pulviniflora, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a medium to large tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in panicles. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

Warneckea pulviniflora is valued for its durable wood and is often found in dry to moist forest habitats.
