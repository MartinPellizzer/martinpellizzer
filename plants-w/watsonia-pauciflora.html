Watsonia pauciflora

Watsonia pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered Watsonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and striking, colorful inflorescences. The plant produces clusters of tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Watsonia pauciflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
