Wikstroemia eriophylla

Wikstroemia eriophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf wreathflower, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of Hawaii.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
