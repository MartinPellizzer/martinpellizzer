Washingtonia leibergii

Washingtonia leibergii, commonly known as the Mexican fan palm, is a tall, single-stemmed palm native to the Sonoran Desert of Mexico.

It features a slender trunk with a distinctive crown of large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant has a fibrous root system adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered fruits.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, dry climates.
