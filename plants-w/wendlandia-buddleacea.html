Wendlandia buddleacea

Wendlandia buddleacea, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The silverleaf tree is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly pollinators.
