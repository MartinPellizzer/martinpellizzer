Wendlandia notoniana

Wendlandia notoniana, commonly known as the Andes bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are bright blue or purple.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
