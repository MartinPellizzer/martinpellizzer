Wisteria brachybotrys var. alba

Wisteria brachybotrys var. alba, commonly known as the white wisteria, is a climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring a support structure for its vigorous growth.

Due to its rapid growth and showy blooms, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
