Wibelia dimorpha

Wibelia dimorpha, commonly known as the prairie blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and typically grows in prairie and open woodland habitats. The plant features showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Wibelia dimorpha is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
