Wageneria longipes

Wageneria longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed grass, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover in arid landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
