Wrightia coalita

Wrightia coalita, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Wrightia coalita is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
