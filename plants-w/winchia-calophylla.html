Winchia calophylla

Winchia calophylla, commonly known as the goldenleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
