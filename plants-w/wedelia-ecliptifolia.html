Wedelia ecliptifolia

Wedelia ecliptifolia, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
