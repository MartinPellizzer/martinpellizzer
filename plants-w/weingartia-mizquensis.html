Weingartia mizquensis

Weingartia mizquensis, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny areoles. The plant typically produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are usually short and spiny, adapting to arid environments.

Weingartia mizquensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
