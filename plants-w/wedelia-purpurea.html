Wedelia purpurea

Wedelia purpurea, commonly known as purple wedgeleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has serrated, narrow leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
