Wiestia acuminata

Wiestia acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed wiestia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Wiestia acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
