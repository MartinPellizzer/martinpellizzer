Wurmbea capensis var. latifolia

Wurmbea capensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved African lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
