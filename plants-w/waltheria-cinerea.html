Waltheria cinerea

Waltheria cinerea, commonly known as the pale evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
