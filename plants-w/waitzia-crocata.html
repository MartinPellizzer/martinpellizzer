Waitzia crocata

Waitzia crocata, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, yellow-orange flowers with a star-like shape. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
