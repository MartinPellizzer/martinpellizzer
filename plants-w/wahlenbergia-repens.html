Wahlenbergia repens

Wahlenbergia repens, commonly known as the prostrate bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces delicate, blue to purple flowers in spring. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with slender stems and small, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
