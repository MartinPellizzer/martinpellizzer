Weingartia tiraquensis var. renatae

Weingartia tiraquensis var. renatae, commonly known as the Renata Weingartia, is a compact cactus native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It features short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white to grayish-blue spines. The plant produces small, pale green flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to drought.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements.
