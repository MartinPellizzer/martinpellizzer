Willemetia scandens

Willemetia scandens, commonly known as the climbing willow or silver willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems that can climb or trail along the ground. The leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and have a silvery-gray underside, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies in its natural habitat.
