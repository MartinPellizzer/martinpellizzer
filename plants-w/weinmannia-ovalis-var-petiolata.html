Weinmannia ovalis var. petiolata

Weinmannia ovalis var. petiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed weinmannia, is a shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with moderate to high salt exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal habitats.
