Wedelia psammophila

Wedelia psammophila, commonly known as the sandplain wedelia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal dunes or dry inland areas.
