Werneria aquatica

Werneria aquatica, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, slender fronds that float on the water surface, with roots submerged in the water. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a delicate, feathery appearance.

Werneria aquatica plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
