Wiesneria triandra

Wiesneria triandra, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters and prefers bright, indirect light. Its botanical name reflects its historical association with the botanist Wiesner, who contributed to its classification.

Wiesneria triandra is widely cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
