Weingartia totoralensis

Weingartia totoralensis, commonly known as the Totora Pincushion Cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Weingartia totoralensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
