Welwitschia filifolia var. diffusa

Welwitschia filifolia var. diffusa, commonly known as the desert tobacco plant, is a succulent species native to the Namib Desert.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant stores water in its leaves, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects.

The plant is also known for its slow growth and long lifespan, often living over 1,000 years.
