Weihea flanaganii

Weihea flanaganii, commonly known as the Flanagan's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered at the base of the leaves. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for their aromatic properties.

Weihea flanaganii thrives in humid, coastal environments and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.
