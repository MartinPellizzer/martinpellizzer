Wendlandia secunda

Wendlandia secunda, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is a climbing shrub with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It produces showy inflorescences that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

Wendlandia secunda thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
