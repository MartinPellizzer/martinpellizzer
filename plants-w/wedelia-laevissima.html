Wedelia laevissima

Wedelia laevissima, commonly known as the smooth wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a low, spreading shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces seeds in achenes.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
