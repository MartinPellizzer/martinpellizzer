Wendlandia glabrata var. puberula

Wendlandia glabrata var. puberula, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it a unique texture and appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its foliage and flowering habits make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological gardening.
