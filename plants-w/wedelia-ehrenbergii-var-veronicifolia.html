Wedelia ehrenbergii var. veronicifolia

Wedelia ehrenbergii var. veronicifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf wedelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
