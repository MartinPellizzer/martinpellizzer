Weingartia cantargalloensis

Weingartia cantargalloensis, commonly known as the Cantargallo prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant features spiny areoles and produces edible fruits, although the plant itself is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.
