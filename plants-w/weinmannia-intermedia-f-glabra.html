Weinmannia intermedia f. glabra

Weinmannia intermedia f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved weinmannia, is a variant of the weinmannia genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Asteraceae family.
