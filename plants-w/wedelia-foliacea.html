Wedelia foliacea

Wedelia foliacea, commonly known as the creeping wedgeflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a dense, mat-forming growth habit, making it useful for ground cover in gardens and landscapes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Wedelia foliacea is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for xeriscaping.
