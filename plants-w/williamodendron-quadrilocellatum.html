Williamodendron quadrilocellatum

Williamodendron quadrilocellatum, commonly known as the four-chambered willow tree, is a unique species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry conditions, making it well-adapted to its environment. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine, waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in harsh climates.
