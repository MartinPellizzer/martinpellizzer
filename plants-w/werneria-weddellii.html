Werneria weddellii

Werneria weddellii, commonly known as the Weddell's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with reduced leaves that help minimize water loss. Its structure includes areoles, which are specialized structures from which spines, flowers, and branches emerge.

Werneria weddellii is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in regions with low rainfall.
