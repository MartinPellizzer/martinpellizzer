Withania frutescens

Withania frutescens, commonly known as Indian winter cherry, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of India and Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and red, fleshy berries that contain seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Withania frutescens is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
