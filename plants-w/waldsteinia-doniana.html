Waldsteinia doniana

Waldsteinia doniana, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is a perennial herb with a creeping growth habit, characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
