Galium laevigatum var. genuinum

Galium laevigatum var. genuinum, commonly known as the smooth bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, sprawling stems that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly waxy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
