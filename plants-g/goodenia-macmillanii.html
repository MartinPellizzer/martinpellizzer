Goodenia macmillanii

Goodenia macmillanii, commonly known as the Macmillan's goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is found in arid or semi-arid regions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
