Geranium raimondii

Geranium raimondii, commonly known as the Raimond's geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium raimondii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
