Gnemon amazonica

Gnemon amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian apple or yellow apple tree, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for various wildlife and its potential medicinal properties.

The tree thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate to flourish.
