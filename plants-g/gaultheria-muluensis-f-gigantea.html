Gaultheria muluensis f. gigantea

Gaultheria muluensis f. gigantea, commonly known as the giant wax myrtle, is a variant of the wax myrtle genus native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and fragrant, waxy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in tropical and subtropical forests. Its botanical features include a shrub or small tree form with a dense, evergreen canopy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
