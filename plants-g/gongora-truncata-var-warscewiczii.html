Gongora truncata var. warscewiczii

Gongora truncata var. warscewiczii, commonly known as the Warscewicz's gongora, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers with intricate patterns and a unique, truncated shape. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native environment.

This variety is highly valued by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
