Gaertnera longipetiolata

Gaertnera longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled gallberry, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and elliptical leaves with long petioles, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
