Gloxinia hieroglyphica

Gloxinia hieroglyphica, commonly known as the Egyptian gloxinia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is known for its compact growth habit. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Gloxinia hieroglyphica is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation indoors.
