Gaultheria erecta

Gaultheria erecta, commonly known as the evergreen trailing cranberry bush, is a species of shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing form with dark green foliage. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are usually white or pink.

Gaultheria erecta is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in acidic soils.
