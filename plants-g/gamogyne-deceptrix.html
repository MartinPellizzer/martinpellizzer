Gamogyne deceptrix

Gamogyne deceptrix, commonly known as the deceptive ghost fern, is a rare and elusive plant species found in shaded, moist forest understories.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that resemble decaying leaves, aiding in its camouflage. The plant lacks true roots and absorbs nutrients through its rhizomes, which spread along the forest floor. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, producing spores rather than seeds.

Due to its rare occurrence and cryptic appearance, it remains a subject of botanical study and conservation interest.
