Glycine latifolia

Glycine latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used as a protein-rich food source. Glycine latifolia is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climates makes it an important plant in agricultural and ecological contexts.
