Geranium dissectum f. roseum

Geranium dissectum f. roseum, commonly known as the roseum geranium, is a cultivar of the geranium plant characterized by its finely cut, lobed leaves and showy, pink to red flowers.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. Its delicate foliage and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for garden borders and container gardening.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
