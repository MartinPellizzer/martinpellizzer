Gentiana elwesii

Gentiana elwesii, commonly known as the Elwes' gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Gentiana elwesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors.
