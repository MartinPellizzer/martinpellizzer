Gnaphalium luteoalbum var. incanum

Gnaphalium luteoalbum var. incanum, commonly known as the silverleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, yellowish to pale brown flowers. The leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving the plant its distinctive silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is known for its long-lasting flowers, making it a popular choice for dried floral arrangements.
