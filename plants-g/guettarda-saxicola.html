Guettarda saxicola

Guettarda saxicola, commonly known as the rock guettarda, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, often on limestone or sandy soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white or yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Guettarda saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in coastal conditions.
