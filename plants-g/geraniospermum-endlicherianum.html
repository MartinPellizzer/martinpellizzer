Geraniospermum endlicherianum

Geraniospermum endlicherianum, commonly known as the white geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub with hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Geraniospermum endlicherianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
