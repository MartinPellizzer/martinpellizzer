Godetia quadrivulnera var. capitata

Godetia quadrivulnera var. capitata, commonly known as the clustered godetia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adding to its distinctive appearance.
