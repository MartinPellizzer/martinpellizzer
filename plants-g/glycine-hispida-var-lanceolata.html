Glycine hispida var. lanceolata

Glycine hispida var. lanceolata, commonly known as the hairy poinsettia, is a variant of the poinsettia species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types. Its foliage is typically a deep green with a pubescent texture, and it can produce small, inconspicuous yellow flowers.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in indoor environments.
