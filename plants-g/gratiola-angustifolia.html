Gratiola angustifolia

Gratiola angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf gratiola, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often less than 1 inch wide. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

Gratiola angustifolia is part of the Gentian family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
