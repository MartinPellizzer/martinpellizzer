Gigantochloa kachinensis

Gigantochloa kachinensis, commonly known as the Kachin bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and large, feathery leaves that can reach several meters in height. This bamboo is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its robust growth makes it suitable for various ecological and agricultural applications.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental or utilitarian value.
