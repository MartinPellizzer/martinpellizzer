Galeopsis tetrahit subsp. reichenbachii

Galeopsis tetrahit subsp.

reichenbachii, commonly known as the lesser hedge-nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is often confused with other members of the genus due to its similar appearance.
