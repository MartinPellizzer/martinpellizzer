Grammitis dauphinensis

Grammitis dauphinensis, commonly known as the Dauphine's brake, is a fern species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in humid conditions. Grammitis dauphinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
