Goudaea chrysostachys var. chrysostachys

Goudaea chrysostachys var. chrysostachys, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow inflorescences that appear in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
