Gossia angustifolia

Gossia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gossia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas.

Gossia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
