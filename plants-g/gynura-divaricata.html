Gynura divaricata

Gynura divaricata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are often dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are typically clustered in the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Gynura divaricata is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its traditional uses in herbal medicine.
