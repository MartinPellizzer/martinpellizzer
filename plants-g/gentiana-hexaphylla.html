Gentiana hexaphylla

Gentiana hexaphylla, commonly known as the six-leaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a lanceolate shape, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

Gentiana hexaphylla is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
