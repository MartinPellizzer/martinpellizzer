Guatteria velutina

Guatteria velutina, commonly known as velvet guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name.

The flowers are clustered in panicles and are usually white or pale green, attracting various pollinators.
