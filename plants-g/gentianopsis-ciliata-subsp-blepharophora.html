Gentianopsis ciliata subsp. blepharophora

Gentianopsis ciliata subsp.

blepharophora, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters at the top of the plant.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely ciliate (fringed) leaves and its unique floral structure, which features five narrow, hood-like petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests or high-altitude meadows.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its vibrant color and ecological significance.
