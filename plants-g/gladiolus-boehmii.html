Gladiolus boehmii

Gladiolus boehmii, commonly known as the Boehm's gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves and produces tall, branched inflorescences with colorful, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, with flowers ranging in color from red to yellow and purple. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in various horticultural settings.
