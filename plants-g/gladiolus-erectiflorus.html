Gladiolus erectiflorus

Gladiolus erectiflorus, commonly known as the upright-flowered gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers in a range of colors including pink, red, and white. The inflorescence consists of a raceme of spurred petals, with a distinctive three-lobed, hood-like structure at the base. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
