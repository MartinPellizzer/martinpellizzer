Gladiolus alopecuroides

Gladiolus alopecuroides, commonly known as the African gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically produces large, colorful flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and floral arrangements.
