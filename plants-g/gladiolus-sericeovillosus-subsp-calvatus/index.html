Gladiolus Sericeovillosus Subsp. Calvatus

Gladiolus sericeovillosus subsp. calvatus is a rare and intriguing member of the iris family known for its striking floral displays. Native to specific regions in southern Africa, this subspecies thrives in unique ecological niches that support its growth. Its distinguishing features include elegant, sword-like leaves and vibrant, often purple or pink, flower spikes that bloom in late summer. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern. This plant not only captivates botanists but also holds cultural significance in local communities.
