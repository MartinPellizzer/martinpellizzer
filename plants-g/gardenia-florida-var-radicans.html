Gardenia florida var. radicans

Gardenia florida var. radicans, commonly known as the climbing gardenia, is a variant of the gardenia species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit, with long, flexible stems that can ascend trees or trellises. The plant produces fragrant, waxy white flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and leathery, providing a lush backdrop for the blooms.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
