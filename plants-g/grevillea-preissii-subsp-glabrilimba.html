Grevillea preissii subsp. glabrilimba

Grevillea preissii subsp.

glabrilimba, commonly known as the narrow-leafed Grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in clusters, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
