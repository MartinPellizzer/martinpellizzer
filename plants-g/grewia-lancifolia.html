Grewia lancifolia

Grewia lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features simple, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. These berries are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
