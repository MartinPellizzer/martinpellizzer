Gypsophila pseudomelampoda

Gypsophila pseudomelampoda, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate white or pink flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are linear and gray-green, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Gypsophila pseudomelampoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in drought-tolerant gardens.
