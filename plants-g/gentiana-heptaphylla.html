Gentiana heptaphylla

Gentiana heptaphylla, commonly known as the seven-leaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern and are often hairy, aiding in water retention.

Gentiana heptaphylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
