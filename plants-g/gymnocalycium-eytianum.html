Gymnocalycium eytianum

Gymnocalycium eytianum, commonly known as the Eytian's moon cactus, is a species of cacti native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and extreme weather. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Gymnocalycium eytianum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
