Geranium lobatum

Geranium lobatum, commonly known as the fringed geranium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Geranium lobatum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
