Gentiana liburnica

Gentiana liburnica, commonly known as the Liburnian gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

The flowers bloom from June to August, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
