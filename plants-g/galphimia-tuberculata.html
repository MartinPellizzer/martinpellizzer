Galphimia tuberculata

Galphimia tuberculata, commonly known as the pink trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a climbing shrub that produces showy, tubular, pink or red flowers that attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Galphimia tuberculata is also valued for its ability to grow in coastal areas due to its salt tolerance.
