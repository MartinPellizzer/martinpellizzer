Gomidesia martiana

Gomidesia martiana, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the development of its unique, fleshy fruit. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a glossy surface, and the plant thrives in warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the edible fruit it produces.
