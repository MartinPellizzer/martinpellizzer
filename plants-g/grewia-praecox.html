Grewia praecox

Grewia praecox, commonly known as the early grewia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the early rainy season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Grewia praecox is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
