Gynerium atacamense

Gynerium atacamense, commonly known as the Atacama goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and adaptability to harsh environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss, and it produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas with high salinity.

Due to its resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local biodiversity.
