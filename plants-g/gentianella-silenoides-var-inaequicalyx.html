Gentianella silenoides var. inaequicalyx

Gentianella silenoides var. inaequicalyx, commonly known as the unequal-lobed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
