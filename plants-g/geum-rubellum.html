Geum rubellum

Geum rubellum, commonly known as the red avens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Geum rubellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
