Geigeria burkei var. valida

Geigeria burkei var. valida, commonly known as the Cape reed, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
