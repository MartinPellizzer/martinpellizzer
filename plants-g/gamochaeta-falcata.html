Gamochaeta falcata

Gamochaeta falcata, commonly known as the narrowleaf gamochaeta, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has slender, branching stems. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly curled or falcate (sickle-shaped) tip, giving it its scientific name.

It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.
