Guarea frutescens

Guarea frutescens, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other hardwoods. The tree is characterized by its dark, heavy wood and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, three-lobed fruits.
