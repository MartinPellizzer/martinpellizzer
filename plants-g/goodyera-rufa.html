Goodyera rufa

Goodyera rufa, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and its striking, pale pink to reddish-brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, where it forms dense colonies. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and establish itself in suitable habitats.

Goodyera rufa is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
