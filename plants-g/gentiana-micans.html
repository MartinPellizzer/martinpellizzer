Gentiana micans

Gentiana micans, commonly known as the lesser gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Gentiana micans is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
