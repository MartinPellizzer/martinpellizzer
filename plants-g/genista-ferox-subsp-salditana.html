Genista ferox subsp. salditana

Genista ferox subsp.

salditana, commonly known as the Sardinian broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sardinia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its hardy nature and ability to colonize harsh environments.
