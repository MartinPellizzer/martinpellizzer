Gigantochloa maxima var. viridis

Gigantochloa maxima var. viridis, commonly known as the green giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, with a dense, upright growth habit. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and have a bright green color, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and structural presence.

This variety is also valued for its resilience and ability to withstand various environmental conditions.
