Gymnosporia trigyna var. macrocarpa

Gymnosporia trigyna var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces large, spiny fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a thick, fibrous covering. Its leaves are typically small, narrow, and arranged in whorls around the stem.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
