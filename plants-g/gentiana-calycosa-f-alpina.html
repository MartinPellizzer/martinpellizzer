Gentiana calycosa f. alpina

Gentiana calycosa f. alpina, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, showy flowers that bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is noted for its hardiness and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
