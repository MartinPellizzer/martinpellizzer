Gratiola macrantha

Gratiola macrantha, commonly known as the broad-leaved yellowroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a robust, fleshy rhizome and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and grow along the stem. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is typically found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Gratiola macrantha is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
