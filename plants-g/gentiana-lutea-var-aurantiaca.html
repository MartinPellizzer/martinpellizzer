Gentiana lutea var. aurantiaca

Gentiana lutea var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange gentian, is a striking plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange flowers, which contrast sharply with its deep green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in herbal medicine.
