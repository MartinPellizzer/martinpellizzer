Galactia purshii

Galactia purshii, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds with tufts of silky hair, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

Galactia purshii is also notable for its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.
