Gymnosphaera ramispinoides

Gymnosphaera ramispinoides, commonly known as the spiny globe cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its globular shape and spiny, segmented stems that provide structural support and protection. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are reduced to spines, a common adaptation in arid environments to minimize water loss.

Native to South America, it thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
