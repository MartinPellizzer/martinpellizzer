Gymnadenia borealis

Gymnadenia borealis, commonly known as the northern grasspenny, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of Europe and North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as bogs, fens, and wet meadows. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-brown seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Gymnadenia borealis is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native habitats.
