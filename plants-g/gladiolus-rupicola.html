Gladiolus rupicola

Gladiolus rupicola, commonly known as the rock gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
