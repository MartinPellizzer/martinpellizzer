Galium pusillum

Galium pusillum, commonly known as dwarf bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters, with slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Galium pusillum is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
