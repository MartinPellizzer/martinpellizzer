Gunnera scabra var. longiscapa

Gunnera scabra var. longiscapa, commonly known as the giant dock, is a striking perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1.5 meters in diameter. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its showy, yellowish-green flower heads appear in late summer, adding visual interest to the landscape.

The species is valued in horticulture for its dramatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
