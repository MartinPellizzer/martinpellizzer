Gastrolobium latifolium

Gastrolobium latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It features compound leaves with numerous small, linear leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which bloom during the autumn and winter seasons. It is adapted to sandy and rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

The plant is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
