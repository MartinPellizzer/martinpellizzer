Gasteria laevis

Gasteria laevis, commonly known as the silver leaf gasteria, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green with a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers on tall, slender stems during the warmer months. It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its compact size and low maintenance requirements.

Gasteria laevis is also valued for its ability to thrive in indirect sunlight and its air-purifying properties.
