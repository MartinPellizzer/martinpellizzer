Grewia filipes

Grewia filipes, commonly known as the silky bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, red or orange berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid environments.
