Gymneia platanifolia

Gymneia platanifolia, commonly known as the plane-leafed gymneia, is a rare flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy, with a deep green color. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soil.

Gymneia platanifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
