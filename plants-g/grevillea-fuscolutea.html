Grevillea fuscolutea

Grevillea fuscolutea, commonly known as the black-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or purplish tinge on the underside. The plant produces striking, red-orange flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Grevillea fuscolutea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract native birds.
