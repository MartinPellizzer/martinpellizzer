Gloxinia villosa

Gloxinia villosa, commonly known as the hairy gloxinia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, thriving in moist, well-drained soil. It prefers partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Gloxinia villosa is known for its ability to produce numerous blooms throughout the growing season.
