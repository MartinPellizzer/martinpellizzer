Grevillea versicolor

Grevillea versicolor, commonly known as the variable grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters and come in a variety of hues, including red, orange, and yellow.

Grevillea versicolor is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
