Geum rafinesqueanum

Geum rafinesqueanum, commonly known as the showy mountain rue, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

Geum rafinesqueanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
