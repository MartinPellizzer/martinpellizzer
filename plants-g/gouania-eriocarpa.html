Gouania eriocarpa

Gouania eriocarpa, commonly known as the hairy globeberry, is a member of the Polygonaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Native to the Americas, it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Its unique appearance and medicinal properties make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
