Glyceria maritima subvar. subcaespitosa

Glyceria maritima subvar. subcaespitosa, commonly known as saltgrass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions.

It typically grows in saline or brackish wetlands, thriving in areas with high salt content. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to tidal zones and can tolerate periodic flooding.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
