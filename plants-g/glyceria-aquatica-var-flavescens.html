Glyceria aquatica var. flavescens

Glyceria aquatica var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that often display a yellowish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common species in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its presence is often an indicator of healthy aquatic ecosystems.
