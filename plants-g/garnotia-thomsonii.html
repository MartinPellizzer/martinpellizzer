Garnotia thomsonii

Garnotia thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's garnotia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in grassland or shrubland habitats. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, often displaying shades of white or pale blue.

Garnotia thomsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
