Gentiana fremontii

Gentiana fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Gentiana fremontii is a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, tubular flowers.
