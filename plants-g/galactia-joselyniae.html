Galactia joselyniae

Galactia joselyniae, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its name derives from the milky sap it exudes, which is a common feature among plants in the milkweed genus.

Galactia joselyniae is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
