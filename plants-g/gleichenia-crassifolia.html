Gleichenia crassifolia

Gleichenia crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its large, broad, leathery leaves that are typically divided into segments, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their aesthetic appeal.

Gleichenia crassifolia plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
