Grumilea articulata

Grumilea articulata, commonly known as the articulated grumilea, is a unique flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, segmented stems that resemble a jointed structure, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged alternately along the stem.

Grumilea articulata is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
