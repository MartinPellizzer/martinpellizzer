Gnetum bosavicum

Gnetum bosavicum, commonly known as the Bosavi gnet, is a rare and unique plant species native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the gymnosperm group, making it one of the few non-flowering plants that produce seeds. The plant exhibits a distinctive morphology, featuring a tall, slender trunk and compound leaves with a leathery texture. Its reproductive structures are specialized, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants.

Gnetum bosavicum plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to forest biodiversity and soil stabilization.
