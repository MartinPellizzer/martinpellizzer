Gaudichaudia filipendula

Gaudichaudia filipendula, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy white seeds for wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
