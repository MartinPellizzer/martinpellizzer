Grevillea tontoutensis

Grevillea tontoutensis, commonly known as the Tontouta grevillea, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of New Caledonia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. Its flowers are usually red or orange, with long, narrow petals that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Grevillea tontoutensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
