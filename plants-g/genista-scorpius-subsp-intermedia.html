Genista scorpius subsp. intermedia

Genista scorpius subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces small, leathery pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
