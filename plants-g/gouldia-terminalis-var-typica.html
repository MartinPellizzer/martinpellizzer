Gouldia terminalis var. typica

Gouldia terminalis var. typica, commonly known as the red kangaroo paw, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It features vibrant red, spurred flowers that resemble the foot of a kangaroo, blooming from late spring to early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense, compact foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
