Gymnema calycinum

Gymnema calycinum, commonly known as the sugar maple plant, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Gymnema calycinum is known for its ability to reduce sugar absorption in the intestines, making it a subject of interest in herbal medicine.

Its unique botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
