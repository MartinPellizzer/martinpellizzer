Gentianella barbata

Gentianella barbata, commonly known as the barbed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface, while the stem is often branched and covered with fine pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
