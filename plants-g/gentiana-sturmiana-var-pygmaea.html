Gentiana sturmiana var. pygmaea

Gentiana sturmiana var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often cultivated in gardens as a rare and ornamental species.
