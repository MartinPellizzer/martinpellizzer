Guarea tessmannii

Guarea tessmannii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a tropical tree native to central and western Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptic or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, clustered in axillary inflorescences, and it produces woody, capsule-like fruits.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
