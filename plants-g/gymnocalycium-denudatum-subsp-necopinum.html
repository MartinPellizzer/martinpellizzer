Gymnocalycium denudatum subsp. necopinum

Gymnocalycium denudatum subsp.

necopinum, commonly known as the naked calyx cactus, is a succulent plant native to Argentina. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in a whitish, powdery coating.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a decorative cactus in gardens and collections.
