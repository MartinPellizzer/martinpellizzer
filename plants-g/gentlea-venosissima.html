Gentlea venosissima

Gentlea venosissima, commonly known as the veined gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and often have prominent veins, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is a sought-after plant in horticulture and conservation efforts.
