Galium effusum

Galium effusum, commonly known as bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, fuzzy texture.

Galium effusum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
