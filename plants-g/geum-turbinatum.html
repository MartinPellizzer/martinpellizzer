Geum turbinatum

Geum turbinatum, commonly known as the Turban Buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are usually covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and wetlands.

Geum turbinatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
