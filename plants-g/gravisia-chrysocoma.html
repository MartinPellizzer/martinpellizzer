Gravisia chrysocoma

Gravisia chrysocoma, commonly known as the golden hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silky foliage that produces a golden hue as it matures. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Gravisia chrysocoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and conservation efforts.
