Guajava australis

Guajava australis, commonly known as the Australian guajava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rainforests.

Guajava australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
