Gladiolus purpureus

Gladiolus purpureus, commonly known as the purple gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers come in a range of colors, including purple, red, and white, with petals that are often marked with intricate patterns. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
