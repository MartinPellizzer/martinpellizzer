Grindelia humilis f. reflexa

Grindelia humilis f. reflexa, commonly known as the reflexed gumweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland deserts. Its leaves are glandular and emit a strong, resinous odor when crushed.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in supporting local pollinators.
