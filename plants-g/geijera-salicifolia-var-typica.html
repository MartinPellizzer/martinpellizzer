Geijera salicifolia var. typica

Geijera salicifolia var. typica, commonly known as the silky gum or silky ash, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy on top and slightly hairy underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, cylindrical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Geijera salicifolia var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy nature and aesthetic features.
