Gymnadenia nigra var. longibracteata

Gymnadenia nigra var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are grass-like, linear, and arranged in a basal rosette, while the flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

The variety is distinguished by its elongated bracts, which are a key feature in its botanical identification.
