Gongora dylaniana

Gongora dylaniana, commonly known as the Dylan's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often display vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in high altitudes. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its natural habitat.

Gongora dylaniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
