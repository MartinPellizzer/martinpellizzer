Genista triacanthos var. oxycedrina

Genista triacanthos var. oxycedrina, commonly known as the coastal broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal and sandy environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
