Geranium vagans subsp. vagans

Geranium vagans subsp.

vagans, commonly known as the trailing geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa. It features upright stems that often trail or spread along the ground, producing clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white.

The leaves are typically opposite, heart-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, giving them a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This species is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
