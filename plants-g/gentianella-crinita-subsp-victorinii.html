Gentianella crinita subsp. victorinii

Gentianella crinita subsp.

victorinii, commonly known as the Victorin's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in mountainous habitats across the Alps and Carpathian regions.
