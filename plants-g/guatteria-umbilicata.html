Guatteria umbilicata

Guatteria umbilicata, commonly known as the umbilical guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its simple, alternate, and often aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by round, fleshy berries that are edible.

Guatteria umbilicata is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
