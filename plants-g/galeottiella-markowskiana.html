Galeottiella markowskiana

Galeottiella markowskiana, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and a fibrous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
