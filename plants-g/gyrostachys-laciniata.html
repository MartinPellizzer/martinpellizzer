Gyrostachys laciniata

Gyrostachys laciniata, commonly known as the laceleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, almost lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Gyrostachys laciniata is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
