Gentiana lutea

Gentiana lutea, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are typically upright and sturdy.

Gentiana lutea is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
