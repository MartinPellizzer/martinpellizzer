Gentiana saginifolia

Gentiana saginifolia, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Gentiana saginifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
