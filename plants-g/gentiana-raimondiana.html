Gentiana raimondiana

Gentiana raimondiana, commonly known as the Italian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hair, while its stems are erect and branching.

The flowers are rich in nectar and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
