Gypsophila struthium

Gypsophila struthium, commonly known as the common snowdrop, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has slender, branching stems and small, linear leaves.

Gypsophila struthium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
