Gymnosphaera bachmaensis

Gymnosphaera bachmaensis, commonly known as the Bachma pine, is a rare and ancient coniferous species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, dense foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces cones that contain seeds, a feature typical of gymnosperms, and it has a slow growth rate, contributing to its scarcity. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a relict species, offering insights into the evolutionary history of conifers.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, Gymnosphaera bachmaensis is considered critically endangered.
