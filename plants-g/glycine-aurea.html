Glycine aurea

Glycine aurea, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its cascading clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves seasonally, and can grow as a shrub or small tree. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it produces pods that contain edible seeds.

Glycine aurea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
