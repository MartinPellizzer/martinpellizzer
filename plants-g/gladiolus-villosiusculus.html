Gladiolus villosiusculus

Gladiolus villosiusculus, commonly known as the hairy gladiolus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. Its inflorescence, known as a spike, consists of multiple blooms arranged along a central stem.

The hairy gladiolus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
