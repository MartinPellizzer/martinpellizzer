Gentiana involucrata var. caerulescens

Gentiana involucrata var. caerulescens, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet-blue flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
