Gladiolus liliaceus

Gladiolus liliaceus, commonly known as the sacred lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and showy, spike-like flower clusters. The flowers come in a wide range of colors, including white, pink, red, and purple, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring regular watering and fertilization for optimal growth.

The plant is also valued for its historical and cultural significance in various regions.
