Geranium viscosissimum var. album

Geranium viscosissimum var. album, commonly known as the white sticky geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that exude a sticky substance when touched. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
