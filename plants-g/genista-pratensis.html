Genista pratensis

Genista pratensis, commonly known as the meadow broom, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features slender, often spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, composed of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista pratensis thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
