Galearia leptostachya

Galearia leptostachya, commonly known as the slender-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
