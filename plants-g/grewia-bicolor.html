Grewia bicolor

Grewia bicolor, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Grewia bicolor is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
