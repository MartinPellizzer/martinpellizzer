Gloriosa graminifolia

Gloriosa graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy glory vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae, which includes orchids, and is characterized by its long, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers with a distinctive spurred petal, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, giving it its common name.

Gloriosa graminifolia thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
