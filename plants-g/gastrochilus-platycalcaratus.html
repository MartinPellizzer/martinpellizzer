Gastrochilus platycalcaratus

Gastrochilus platycalcaratus, commonly known as the broad-spurred orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive sympodial growth habit, characterized by its fleshy, rhizomatous stems. The plant produces showy, spurred orchid flowers with a unique calyx structure, featuring a broad, calcareous spur. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in montane forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and taxonomists.
