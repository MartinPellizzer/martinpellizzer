Glomera fluviatilis

Glomera fluviatilis, commonly known as the river glomerate, is a rare aquatic plant found in slow-moving freshwater environments.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, with fine, thread-like roots that extend into the substrate. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing nutrient availability in the water.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator of water quality and ecological health.
