Gaultheria vernalis

Gaultheria vernalis, commonly known as the spring witch-hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its fruit is a red, fleshy capsule that contains several small seeds.

Gaultheria vernalis thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and streamside areas.
