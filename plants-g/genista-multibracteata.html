Genista multibracteata

Genista multibracteata, commonly known as the bracted broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, leathery pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
