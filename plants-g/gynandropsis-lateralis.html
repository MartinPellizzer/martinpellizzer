Gynandropsis lateralis

Gynandropsis lateralis, commonly known as the yellow starburry, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in dense clusters, giving it a star-like appearance. The plant has a short, hairy stem and hairy leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland areas with a Mediterranean climate.

Gynandropsis lateralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
