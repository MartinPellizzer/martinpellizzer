Giliastrum purpusii subsp. stewartii

Giliastrum purpusii subsp.

stewartii, commonly known as Stewart's giliastrum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

This subspecies is often found in scrublands and grasslands, contributing to local biodiversity.
