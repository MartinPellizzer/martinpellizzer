Goldfussia articulata

Goldfussia articulata, commonly known as the articulated goldfussia, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, leaf-like structures that are often mistaken for true leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in shallow water.

Goldfussia articulata is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
