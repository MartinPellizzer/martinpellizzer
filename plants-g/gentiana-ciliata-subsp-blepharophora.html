Gentiana ciliata subsp. blepharophora

Gentiana ciliata subsp.

blepharophora, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in a single, terminal inflorescence.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely ciliate (fringed) leaves and a unique floral structure with prominent, spurred petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
