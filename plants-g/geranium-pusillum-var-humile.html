Geranium pusillum var. humile

Geranium pusillum var. humile, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features small, palmate leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Due to its compact size, it is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
