Geranium odoratum

Geranium odoratum, commonly known as the sweet geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Geranium odoratum is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
