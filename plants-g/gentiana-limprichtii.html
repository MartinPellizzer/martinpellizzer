Gentiana limprichtii

Gentiana limprichtii, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Gentiana limprichtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
