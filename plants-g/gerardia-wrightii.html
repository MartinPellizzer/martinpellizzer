Gerardia wrightii

Gerardia wrightii, commonly known as Wright's gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Gerardia wrightii is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woods.
