Gnaphalium plantaginifoliatum

Gnaphalium plantaginifoliatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

This species is known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
