Gnaphalium pedunculare

Gnaphalium pedunculare, commonly known as the slender everlasting, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense mats. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces copious amounts of lightweight seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind.

The plant is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
