Gentianella poretzkyi

Gentianella poretzkyi, commonly known as the Poretzky's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often short and unbranched.

Gentianella poretzkyi is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
