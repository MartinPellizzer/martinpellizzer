Geonoma rectifolia

Geonoma rectifolia, commonly known as the straight-leaf bryophytes, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
