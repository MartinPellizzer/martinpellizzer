Gaultheria glaucifolia

Gaultheria glaucifolia, commonly known as the blue leaf wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving it a bluish appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. These berries are edible and have a sharp, peppery flavor, often used in traditional remedies.

Gaultheria glaucifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
