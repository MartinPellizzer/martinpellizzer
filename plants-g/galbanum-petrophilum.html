Galbanum petrophilum

Galbanum petrophilum, commonly known as the rock galbanum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green resinous sap, which is exuded from the stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in spring, and it is often found in association with other medicinal and aromatic plants.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a component in perfumery.
