Gonocormus dichotomus

Gonocormus dichotomus, commonly known as the forked bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant found in freshwater habitats.

It is characterized by its branching, root-like structures that help it anchor in muddy substrates. The plant produces tiny, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, aiding in its nutrient acquisition. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-yellow in color.

Gonocormus dichotomus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to nutrient cycling.
