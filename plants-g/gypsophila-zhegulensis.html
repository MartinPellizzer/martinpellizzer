Gypsophila zhegulensis

Gypsophila zhegulensis, commonly known as the Caucasian baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its small, fluffy blooms are often used in floral arrangements due to their aesthetic appeal.

Gypsophila zhegulensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
