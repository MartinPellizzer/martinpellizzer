Gnaphalium spicatum

Gnaphalium spicatum, commonly known as the spikeless everlasting or common everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ability to retain moisture.

The plant is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and delicate appearance.
