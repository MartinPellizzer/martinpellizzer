Giulianettia keysseri

Giulianettia keysseri, commonly known as the Keyser's giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it can anchor itself to the substrate with its fibrous roots. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, blooming at night and emitting a mild fragrance to attract pollinators.

Due to its size and ecological importance, it plays a significant role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
