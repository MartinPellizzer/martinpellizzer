Geranium cataractarum var. tomentosum

Geranium cataractarum var. tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features upright stems covered in soft, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically rounded and also bear a fuzzy texture, contributing to its unique botanical characteristics.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
