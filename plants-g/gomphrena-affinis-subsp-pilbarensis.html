Gomphrena affinis subsp. pilbarensis

Gomphrena affinis subsp.

pilbarensis, commonly known as the Pilbara gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to red.

The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
