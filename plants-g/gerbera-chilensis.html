Gerbera chilensis

Gerbera chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with bright yellow centers and white or pink petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss.

Gerbera chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
