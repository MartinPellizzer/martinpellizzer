Geranium oliganthum

Geranium oliganthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
