Gladiolus dichotomus

Gladiolus dichotomus, commonly known as the two-branched gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are usually borne in racemes and come in shades of red, pink, and white, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The species is notable for its unique branching pattern, which gives it its common name.
