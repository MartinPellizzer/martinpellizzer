Gnaphalium acutifolium

Gnaphalium acutifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, narrow leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in poor, sandy soils. It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open fields.

Gnaphalium acutifolium is also noted for its long-lasting flowers, which are used in floral arrangements.
