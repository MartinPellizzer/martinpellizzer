Geum × gonzaloi

Geum × gonzaloi, commonly known as the Mexican meadow cranesbill, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
