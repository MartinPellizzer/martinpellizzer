Gentiana handeliana subsp. handeliana

Gentiana handeliana subsp.

handeliana, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
