Glycine hispida var. pura-lutescens

Glycine hispida var. pura-lutescens, commonly known as the golden hairy bean, is a variant of the hairy bean plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nutritional content.

The leaves and young pods are used in traditional cuisines and have medicinal properties in some cultures.
