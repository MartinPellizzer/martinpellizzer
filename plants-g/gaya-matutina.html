Gaya matutina

Gaya matutina, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found climbing on supports or fences. Its leaves are heart-shaped and its vines can grow quite long, sometimes reaching several meters in length.

Gaya matutina is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its historical use in traditional medicine.
