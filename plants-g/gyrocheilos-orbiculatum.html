Gyrocheilos orbiculatum

Gyrocheilos orbiculatum, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features a basal rosette of thick, waxy, light green leaves that are slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in loose, cup-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
