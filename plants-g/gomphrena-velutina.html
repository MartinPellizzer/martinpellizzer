Gomphrena velutina

Gomphrena velutina, commonly known as velvet gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Gomphrena velutina is often used in dried flower arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
