Gaulettia cognata

Gaulettia cognata, commonly known as the Florida gentian, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist, sandy soils. Gaulettia cognata has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is considered a rare and endangered species due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
