Gerbera burmanni

Gerbera burmanni, commonly known as the Burmann's gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically has hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. It produces vibrant blooms in shades of red, yellow, and white, often with a dark central disc.

Gerbera burmanni is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
