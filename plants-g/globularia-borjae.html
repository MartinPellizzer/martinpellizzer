Globularia borjae

Globularia borjae, commonly known as the Borja's globularia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Globularia borjae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in Mediterranean-style gardens.
