Glycanthes scandens

Glycanthes scandens, commonly known as the climbing gourds or sweet gourds, is a vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing habit and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to ornamental, edible fruits with a sweet, nutty flavor. Its growth pattern makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in gardens.

Glycanthes scandens is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and culinary uses.
