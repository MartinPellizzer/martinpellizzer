Geranium miyabei

Geranium miyabei, commonly known as the Japanese geranium, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. Geranium miyabei thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms.
