Gleditsia medogensis

Gleditsia medogensis, commonly known as the Chinese honeylocust, is a deciduous tree native to western China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and thorned stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
