Gasteria verrucosa var. asperrima

Gasteria verrucosa var. asperrima, commonly known as the rough-leaved gasteria, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green with a textured, waxy surface. The leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern and often have a reddish or purple hue when exposed to direct sunlight. It produces small, white or pale green flowers on tall, slender stems during the summer months.

This variety is well-suited for dry environments and requires minimal watering to thrive.
