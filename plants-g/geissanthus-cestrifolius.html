Geissanthus cestrifolius

Geissanthus cestrifolius, commonly known as the creeping geissanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Ecuador and Peru.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers, which are typically borne in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
