Geranium schmidii

Geranium schmidii, commonly known as the Schmid's geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lobed or serrated, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Geranium schmidii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
