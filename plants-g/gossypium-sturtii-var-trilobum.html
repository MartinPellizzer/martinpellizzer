Gossypium sturtii var. trilobum

Gossypium sturtii var. trilobum, commonly known as the Sturt's cottonwood, is a rare and specialized plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves and fibrous, woolly seed capsules. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions. Its fibrous seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in harsh environments.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
