Galium verum var. nikkoense

Galium verum var. nikkoense, commonly known as Japanese bedstraw, is a variant of the yellow bedstraw species native to East Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, branched stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
