Gerbera lynchii

Gerbera lynchii, commonly known as the Mexican gerbera, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a range of colors including red, yellow, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Gerbera lynchii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
