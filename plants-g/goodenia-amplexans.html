Goodenia amplexans

Goodenia amplexans, commonly known as the clasping goodenia, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy leaves that wrap around the stems, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Goodenia amplexans thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
