Gynochthodes callicarpifolia

Gynochthodes callicarpifolia, commonly known as the red-fruited gynochthodes, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy, red berries that contain several seeds, making it attractive to birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
