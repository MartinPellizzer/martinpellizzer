Garnotia arborum

Garnotia arborum, commonly known as the tree geranium, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Garnotia arborum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
