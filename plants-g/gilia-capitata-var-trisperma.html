Gilia capitata var. trisperma

Gilia capitata var. trisperma, commonly known as the Sierra gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense head of small, tubular flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that disperse seeds by wind.
