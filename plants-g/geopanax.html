Geopanax

Geopanax, commonly known as the giant ground ivy, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Araliaceae.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typical of the Araliaceae family. Geopanax thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
