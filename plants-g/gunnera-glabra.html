Gunnera glabra

Gunnera glabra, commonly known as the giant rhubarb, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in moist, shady environments. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which gives it a fuzzy texture.

Gunnera glabra is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic appearance and unique foliage.
