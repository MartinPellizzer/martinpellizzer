Glycine upembae

Glycine upembae, commonly known as the Upemba bean, is a climbing legume native to Central Africa.

It produces small, yellow flowers and bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and twining stems, which help it climb supports in its natural habitat. Glycine upembae is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its nutritional seeds and potential use in traditional agriculture.
