Gymnadenia conopsea subsp. intermedia

Gymnadenia conopsea subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spurred flowers.

The flowers are typically blue or purple and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, dark brown seeds in its fruiting capsules.

It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.
