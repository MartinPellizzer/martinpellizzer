Garcinia intermedia

Garcinia intermedia, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. Garcinia intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
