Gleichenia lobata

Gleichenia lobata, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its long, narrow fronds with a distinctive lobed shape, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it is often found in temperate regions with high humidity.

Gleichenia lobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
