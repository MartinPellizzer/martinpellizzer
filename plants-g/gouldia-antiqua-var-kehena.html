Gouldia antiqua var. kehena

Gouldia antiqua var. kehena, commonly known as the Kehena grass, is a perennial grass species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
