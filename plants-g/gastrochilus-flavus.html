Gastrochilus flavus

Gastrochilus flavus, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are small but striking, often clustered in racemes, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Gastrochilus flavus is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
