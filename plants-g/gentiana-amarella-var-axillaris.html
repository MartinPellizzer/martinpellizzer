Gentiana amarella var. axillaris

Gentiana amarella var. axillaris, commonly known as the lesser gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
