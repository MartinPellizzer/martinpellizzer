Gomesa wheatleyana

Gomesa wheatleyana, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a mild, aromatic scent. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Gomesa wheatleyana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
