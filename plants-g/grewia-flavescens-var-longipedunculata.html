Grewia flavescens var. longipedunculata

Grewia flavescens var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate yellow bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters along long, slender peduncles. The leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid environments.

This plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
