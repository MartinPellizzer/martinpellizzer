Gallitrichum × fuschii

Gallitrichum × fuschii, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a fascinating plant with unique botanical features.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Gallitrichum hirsutum and G. × fuscum, showcasing a blend of characteristics from both parent species. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its flowers are small and usually pale green or yellow, with a distinct three-lobed structure.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
