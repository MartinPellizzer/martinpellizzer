Grastidium glaucophyllum

Grastidium glaucophyllum, commonly known as the blue glaucous grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color due to the presence of a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale blue or white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Grastidium glaucophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
