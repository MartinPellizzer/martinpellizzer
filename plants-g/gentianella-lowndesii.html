Gentianella lowndesii

Gentianella lowndesii, commonly known as the Lowndes' gentian, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is relatively rare and has been the subject of conservation efforts due to habitat loss.
