Grewia praecox subsp. praecox

Grewia praecox subsp.

praecox, commonly known as the early yellow globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters, typically in early spring.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
