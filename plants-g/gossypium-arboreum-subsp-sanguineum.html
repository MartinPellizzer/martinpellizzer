Gossypium arboreum subsp. sanguineum

Gossypium arboreum subsp.

sanguineum, commonly known as the crimson tree cotton, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces showy, red or pink flowers that bloom in terminal clusters.

The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its cotton-like seed pods are woody and contain fibrous hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed or secondary forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
