Genetyllis virescens

Genetyllis virescens, commonly known as the greenleaf sedge, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or seasonally wet habitats, such as marshes and swamps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Genetyllis virescens plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
