Gomphostemma parviflorum var. farinosum

Gomphostemma parviflorum var. farinosum, commonly known as the sticky strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky secretion. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy, farinose coating, which gives it its common name.
