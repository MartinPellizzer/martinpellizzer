Gymnema muelleri

Gymnema muelleri, commonly known as the Indian bitter leaf, is a climbing plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant is known for its milky sap, which contains various bioactive compounds. Gymnema muelleri is widely used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including its role in managing diabetes.

Its unique botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
