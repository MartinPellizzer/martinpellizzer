Globifera erosa

Globifera erosa, commonly known as the creeping globeflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Globifera erosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
