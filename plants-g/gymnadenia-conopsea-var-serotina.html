Gymnadenia conopsea var. serotina

Gymnadenia conopsea var. serotina, commonly known as the late gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy blue to violet flowers that bloom late in the summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in open grasslands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces a single spike of flowers at the top of the stem.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
