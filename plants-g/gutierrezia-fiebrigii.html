Gutierrezia fiebrigii

Gutierrezia fiebrigii, commonly known as silverleaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, silvery-gray leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its small yellow flowers bloom in the spring and attract various pollinators.

Gutierrezia fiebrigii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
