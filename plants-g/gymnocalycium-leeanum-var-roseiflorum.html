Gymnocalycium leeanum var. roseiflorum

Gymnocalycium leeanum var. roseiflorum, commonly known as the rose-flowered moon cactus, is a succulent native to Argentina.

It features a rounded, fleshy stem covered in spines and produces showy, pink or red flowers during the warmer months. The plant has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy body.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
