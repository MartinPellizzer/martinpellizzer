Gnaphalium cinnamomeum

Gnaphalium cinnamomeum, commonly known as cinnamon weed, is a flowering plant native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in dense heads, and the leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
