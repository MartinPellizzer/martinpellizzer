Galactia anomala

Galactia anomala, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its name derives from the milky sap it exudes, which contains latex-like compounds.
