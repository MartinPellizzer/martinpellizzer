Grevillea sarissa subsp. anfractifolia

Grevillea sarissa subsp.

anfractifolia, commonly known as the serrated grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, serrated leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces striking, bottlebrush-like inflorescences in shades of red and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
