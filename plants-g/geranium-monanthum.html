Geranium monanthum

Geranium monanthum, commonly known as the single-flower geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clustered leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium monanthum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
