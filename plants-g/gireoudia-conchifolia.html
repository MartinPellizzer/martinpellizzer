Gireoudia conchifolia

Gireoudia conchifolia, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Gireoudia conchifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
