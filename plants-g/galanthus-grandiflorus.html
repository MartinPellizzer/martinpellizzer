Galanthus grandiflorus

Galanthus grandiflorus, commonly known as the giant snowdrop, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, drooping flowers with a yellow-green spathe. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in woodlands or shaded garden areas.

Galanthus grandiflorus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a symbol of resilience and renewal in the botanical world.
