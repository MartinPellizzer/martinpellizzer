Gentiana wasenensis

Gentiana wasenensis, commonly known as the Wasen's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Gentiana wasenensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
