Gamochaeta purpurea

Gamochaeta purpurea, commonly known as purple gamochaeta, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. It features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue.

The showy purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
