Geum potaninii

Geum potaninii, commonly known as the Siberian avens, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It features delicate, nodding flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra or alpine environments.

Geum potaninii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
