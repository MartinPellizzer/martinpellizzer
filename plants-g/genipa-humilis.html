Genipa humilis

Genipa humilis, commonly known as the sour sop or West Indian custard apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a tropical fruit with a thick, spiky rind and a creamy, aromatic pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
