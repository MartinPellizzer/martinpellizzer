Galium argyi

Galium argyi, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, giving it its common name. The plant typically produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and often forms dense mats, allowing it to thrive in moist, disturbed soils.

Galium argyi is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
