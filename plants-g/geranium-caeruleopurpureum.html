Geranium caeruleopurpureum

Geranium caeruleopurpureum, commonly known as the blue-purple geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from deep blue to purplish-pink, often with a contrasting throat. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

Geranium caeruleopurpureum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
