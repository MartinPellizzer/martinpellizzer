Gentianella chlorantha

Gentianella chlorantha, commonly known as the green gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their vibrant blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while its flowers are small, clustered, and usually a striking blue or yellow.

Gentianella chlorantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
