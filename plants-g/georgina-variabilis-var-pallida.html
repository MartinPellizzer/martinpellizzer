Georgina variabilis var. pallida

Georgina variabilis var. pallida, commonly known as the pale georgina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, woolly leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its pale yellow to white flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
