Gyaladenia rhomboglossa

Gyaladenia rhomboglossa, commonly known as the rhomboid-tongued orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique rhomboid-shaped lip, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers during the spring season.

The orchid is noted for its delicate structure and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
