Galium gerardi

Galium gerardi, commonly known as hairy bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its small white or pinkish flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Galium gerardi produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.
