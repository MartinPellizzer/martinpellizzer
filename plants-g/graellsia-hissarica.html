Graellsia hissarica

Graellsia hissarica, commonly known as the Hissar lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its bulbs are an important food source for various herbivores in its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
