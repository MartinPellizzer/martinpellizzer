Grewia tenax var. ribesifolia

Grewia tenax var. ribesifolia, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically red or reddish in color, giving the plant its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
