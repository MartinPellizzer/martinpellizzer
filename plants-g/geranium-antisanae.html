Geranium antisanae

Geranium antisanae, commonly known as the Madagascar geranium, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features dark green, serrated leaves and produces vibrant blooms in shades of red, pink, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
