Guatteria macrophylla var. bragma

Guatteria macrophylla var. bragma, commonly known as the giant guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical rainforests.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties make it a subject of interest in botanical research.
