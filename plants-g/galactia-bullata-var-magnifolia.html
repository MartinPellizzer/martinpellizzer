Galactia bullata var. magnifolia

Galactia bullata var. magnifolia, commonly known as the showy cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces vibrant purple or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional agriculture.

The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy and resilient species.
