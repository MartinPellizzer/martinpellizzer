Gerardia setacea var. longifolia

Gerardia setacea var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved gerardia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, elongated leaves that are covered in fine, bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
