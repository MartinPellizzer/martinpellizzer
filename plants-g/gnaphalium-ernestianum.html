Gnaphalium ernestianum

Gnaphalium ernestianum, commonly known as the Ernestian everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically arranged in dense, compact clusters. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant is known for its long-lasting flowers, which are used in floral arrangements and traditional medicine.
