Genistella tridentata

Genistella tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is shrub-like, with slender, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive trifid (three-lobed) structure, giving it its common name.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal areas.
