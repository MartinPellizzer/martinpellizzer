Gymnopteris punctata

Gymnopteris punctata, commonly known as the spotted holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnate fronds with a distinctive pattern of small, rounded sori along the margins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually bright green and have a leathery texture.

Gymnopteris punctata is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
