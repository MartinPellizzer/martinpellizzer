Gongora saccata

Gongora saccata, commonly known as the sac-like orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sac-shaped pouch at the base of its flower, which is a unique adaptation for attracting specific pollinators. The plant produces showy, colorful flowers with intricate patterns that enhance its visual appeal. Its leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environments.

Gongora saccata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its striking floral displays.
