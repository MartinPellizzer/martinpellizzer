Gompholobium venustum

Gompholobium venustum, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils across the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.
