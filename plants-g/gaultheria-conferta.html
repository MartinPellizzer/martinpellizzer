Gaultheria conferta

Gaultheria conferta, commonly known as the evergreen wintergreen, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery dark green leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible and have a distinctive spicy flavor. Gaultheria conferta thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is also valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine.
