Grindelia microcephala

Grindelia microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, waxy coating. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert regions.

Grindelia microcephala is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
