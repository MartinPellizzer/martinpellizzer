Guettarda torbeciana

Guettarda torbeciana, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has a dense, compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental species due to its vibrant floral display. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
