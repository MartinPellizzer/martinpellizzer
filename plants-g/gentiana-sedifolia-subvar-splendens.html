Gentiana sedifolia subvar. splendens

Gentiana sedifolia subvar. splendens, commonly known as the Splendid Gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are alternate, serrated, and often have a waxy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
