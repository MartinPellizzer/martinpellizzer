Gilia pungens var. tenuiloba

Gilia pungens var. tenuiloba, commonly known as the slenderleaf gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to the arid environments it inhabits. This species thrives in rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert regions.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
