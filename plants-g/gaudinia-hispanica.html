Gaudinia hispanica

Gaudinia hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish gaudinia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in spring and summer. Gaudinia hispanica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
