Gentiana utriculosa

Gentiana utriculosa, commonly known as the bladder gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bladder-like seed pods that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Gentiana utriculosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
