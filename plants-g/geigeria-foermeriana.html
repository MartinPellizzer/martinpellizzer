Geigeria foermeriana

Geigeria foermeriana, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of bright red or pink flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Geigeria foermeriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
