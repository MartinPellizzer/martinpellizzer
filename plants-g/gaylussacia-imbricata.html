Gaylussacia imbricata

Gaylussacia imbricata, commonly known as the dwarf huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils found in woodlands and swamps. The plant produces small, edible berries that are dark red to black in color, often used in local foraging and culinary traditions. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a finely toothed margin.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting wildlife and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
