Grewia homblei

Grewia homblei, commonly known as the red gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
