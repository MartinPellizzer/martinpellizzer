Gaura linifolia

Gaura linifolia, commonly known as whorled gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and cottage gardens.

Gaura linifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
