Galium mollugo var. pubescens

Galium mollugo var. pubescens, commonly known as hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has slender, creeping stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and also covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
