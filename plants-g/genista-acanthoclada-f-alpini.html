Genista acanthoclada f. alpini

Genista acanthoclada f. alpini, commonly known as the Alpine genista, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a woolly texture, while its fruit is a legume containing several seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh mountain environments and is often found in regions such as the Alps and Pyrenees.
