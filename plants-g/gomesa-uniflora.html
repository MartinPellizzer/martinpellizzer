Gomesa uniflora

Gomesa uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower gomesia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a single bloom per stem, making it distinctive.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields.
