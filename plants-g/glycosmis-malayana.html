Glycosmis malayana

Glycosmis malayana, commonly known as the Malay hibiscus or wild tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its botanical features include a woody stem, aromatic leaves, and a distinctive floral structure.

Glycosmis malayana is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
