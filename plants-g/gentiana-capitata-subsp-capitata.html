Gentiana capitata subsp. capitata

Gentiana capitata subsp.

capitata, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in a dense, terminal cluster.

The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountain meadows and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in high-altitude ecosystems.
