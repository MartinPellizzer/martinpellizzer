Galarhoeus lamprocarpus

Galarhoeus lamprocarpus, commonly known as the silverleaf phyllody virus carrier, is a plant species that is primarily noted for its association with viral diseases rather than its botanical features.

It is not a true plant but a vector for the phyllody virus, which affects various crops. The plant itself is a type of wild radish, native to the Mediterranean region. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, with a bluish-green color due to the presence of a waxy coating.

Despite its limited botanical significance, it plays a crucial role in the transmission of plant viruses.
