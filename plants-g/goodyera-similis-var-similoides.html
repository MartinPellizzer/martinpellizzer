Goodyera similis var. similoides

Goodyera similis var. similoides, commonly known as the similar maidenhair fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like foliage with slender, arching fronds. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often growing on trees or rocks in humid forests. Its rhizomes are short and branched, allowing it to anchor itself to substrates.

The leaves are typically dark green and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
