Geranium limprichtii

Geranium limprichtii, commonly known as the Karoo geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are pink to red, with five petals and a distinctive calyx, blooming in spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
