Gentiana amarella var. obtusifolia

Gentiana amarella var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically rounded and have a bluish-green hue, distinguishing it from other gentian species. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
