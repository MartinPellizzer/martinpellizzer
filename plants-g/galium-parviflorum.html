Galium parviflorum

Galium parviflorum, commonly known as smallflower bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and branched. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Galium parviflorum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
