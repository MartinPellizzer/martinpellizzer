Globularia vulgaris subsp. cambessedesii

Globularia vulgaris subsp.

cambessedesii, commonly known as the Canary Island globularia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Canary Islands. It features dense, spherical clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to the dry, rocky environments it inhabits. It is often found in coastal areas and dry grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in garden settings.
