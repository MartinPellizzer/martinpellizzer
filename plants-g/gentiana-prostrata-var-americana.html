Gentiana prostrata var. americana

Gentiana prostrata var. americana, commonly known as American gentian, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
