Gynopachis jambosoides

Gynopachis jambosoides, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain oily seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its unique botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
