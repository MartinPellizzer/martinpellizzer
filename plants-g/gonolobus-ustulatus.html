Gonolobus ustulatus

Gonolobus ustulatus, commonly known as the flame vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling flames. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with heart-shaped leaves and a rapid growth rate. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

The flowers are adapted for pollination by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native range.
