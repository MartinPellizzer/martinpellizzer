Grammitis achilleifolia

Grammitis achilleifolia, commonly known as the Achilles' heel fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with small, clustered fronds that are usually less than 30 cm in length. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a soft, velvety texture and a dark green color.

Grammitis achilleifolia is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
