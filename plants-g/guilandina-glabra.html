Guilandina glabra

Guilandina glabra, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and medicinal studies.
