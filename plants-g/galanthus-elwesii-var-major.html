Galanthus elwesii var. major

Galanthus elwesii var. major, commonly known as the Giant Snowdrop, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its large, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is prized for its striking appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
