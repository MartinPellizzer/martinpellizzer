Gleditsia indica

Gleditsia indica, commonly known as the Indian honeylocust, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves that are often divided into small, oval-shaped leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and tolerance to urban conditions.

The plant also has a history of traditional medicinal use in some regions.
