Gymnadenia nigra subsp. iberica

Gymnadenia nigra subsp.

iberica, commonly known as the Iberian green gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous regions.

The plant features lanceolate, dark green leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers in late summer. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular corollas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
