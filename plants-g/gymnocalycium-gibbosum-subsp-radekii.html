Gymnocalycium gibbosum subsp. radekii

Gymnocalycium gibbosum subsp.

radekii, commonly known as the Radek's moon cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of northern Argentina. It features a spherical or slightly elongated stem covered in white to pale blue spines.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it stores water in its fleshy stem for survival in dry conditions.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with minimal water.
