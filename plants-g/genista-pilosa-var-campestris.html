Genista pilosa var. campestris

Genista pilosa var. campestris, commonly known as the field broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several pairs of narrow, pointed leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ornamental plant.
