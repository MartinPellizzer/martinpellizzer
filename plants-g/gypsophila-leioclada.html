Gypsophila leioclada

Gypsophila leioclada, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that resemble plumes, typically consisting of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Gypsophila leioclada is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other members like carnations and poppies.
