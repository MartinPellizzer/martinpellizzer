Glycosmis puberula var. subsessilis

Glycosmis puberula var. subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bitter apple, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries that are typically bitter in taste. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
