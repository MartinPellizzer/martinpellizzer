Gymnosporia gracilis

Gymnosporia gracilis, commonly known as the slender spiny acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by woody pods containing small, oval seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.

Gymnosporia gracilis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
