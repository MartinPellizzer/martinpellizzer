Gymnosporia dinteri

Gymnosporia dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's burr bush, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny, compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, woody pods that contain hard, angular seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with deep roots and thick, fleshy leaves that store water.

Gymnosporia dinteri is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in dry gardens.
