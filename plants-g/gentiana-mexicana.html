Gentiana mexicana

Gentiana mexicana, commonly known as Mexican gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stem is usually unbranched and somewhat hairy.

Gentiana mexicana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
