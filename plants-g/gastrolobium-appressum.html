Gastrolobium appressum

Gastrolobium appressum, commonly known as the small-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, narrow, dark green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and fire resistance.

Gastrolobium appressum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
