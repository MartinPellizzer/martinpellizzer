Geranium toquimense

Geranium toquimense, commonly known as the Toquimense geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant red or pink blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually serrated and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Geranium toquimense is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
