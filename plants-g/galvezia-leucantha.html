Galvezia leucantha

Galvezia leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered galvezia, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Galvezia leucantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its attractive floral display.
