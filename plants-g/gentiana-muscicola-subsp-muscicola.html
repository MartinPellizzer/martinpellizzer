Gentiana muscicola subsp. muscicola

Gentiana muscicola subsp.

muscicola, commonly known as the musk gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

The plant is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The subspecies is noted for its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in cool, humid climates.
