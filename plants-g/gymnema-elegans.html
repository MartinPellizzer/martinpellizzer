Gymnema elegans

Gymnema elegans, commonly known as the "sugar plant," is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive network of veins. The plant is renowned for its unique ability to inhibit the perception of sweetness, a property attributed to the presence of gymnemic acids. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in umbels, while the fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds.

Gymnema elegans is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential hypoglycemic effects.
