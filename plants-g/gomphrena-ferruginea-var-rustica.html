Gomphrena ferruginea var. rustica

Gomphrena ferruginea var. rustica, commonly known as the rusty globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical inflorescences, which are typically pink or red in color. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The variety 'rustica' is noted for its robust growth and ability to withstand dry conditions.
