Gynostemma cardiospermum

Gynostemma cardiospermum, commonly known as heartseed gynostemma, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes cucumbers and gourds, and is characterized by its tendrils and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into distinctive, heart-shaped seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red or orange aril. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a unique and interesting species within the Cucurbitaceae family.
