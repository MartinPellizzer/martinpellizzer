Glycosmis tetraphylla

Glycosmis tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leafed tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature. Its leaves and fruits contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and phytochemistry.

Glycosmis tetraphylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
