Grewia oncopetala

Grewia oncopetala, commonly known as the red globemallow, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, round fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
