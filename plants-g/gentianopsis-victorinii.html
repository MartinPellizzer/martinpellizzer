Gentianopsis victorinii

Gentianopsis victorinii, commonly known as the Victorin's gentian, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often at high altitudes where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are tubular and clustered in inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
