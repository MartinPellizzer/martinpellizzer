Gentiana stragulata

Gentiana stragulata, commonly known as the woolly gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in wildflower gardens and naturalized areas.
