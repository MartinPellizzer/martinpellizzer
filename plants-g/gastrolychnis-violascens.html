Gastrolychnis violascens

Gastrolychnis violascens, commonly known as the violet-flowered grassvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, violet or purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy habitats.

Gastrolychnis violascens is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.
