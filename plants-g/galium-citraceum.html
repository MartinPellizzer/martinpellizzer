Galium citraceum

Galium citraceum, commonly known as yellow bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium citraceum thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
