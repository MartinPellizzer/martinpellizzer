Gentiana panthaica var. panthaica

Gentiana panthaica var. panthaica, commonly known as the Panthai gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
