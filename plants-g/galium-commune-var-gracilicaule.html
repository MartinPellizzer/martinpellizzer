Galium commune var. gracilicaule

Galium commune var. gracilicaule, commonly known as slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
