Genista eriocarpa

Genista eriocarpa, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Genista eriocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
