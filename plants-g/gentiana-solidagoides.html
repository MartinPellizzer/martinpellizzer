Gentiana solidagoides

Gentiana solidagoides, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
