Gladiolus paniculatus

Gladiolus paniculatus, commonly known as the clustered gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of showy, often brightly colored flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
