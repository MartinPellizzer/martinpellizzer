Glycine pescadrensis

Glycine pescadrensis, commonly known as the Pescadrensis soybean, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility. Glycine pescadrensis is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in sustainable agriculture practices.
