Geranium minae

Geranium minae, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces bright pink or red blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually rounded and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Geranium minae is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
