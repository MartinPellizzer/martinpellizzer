Grammitis debilifolia

Grammitis debilifolia, commonly known as the slender grammitis, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are responsible for spore dispersal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions.

Grammitis debilifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
