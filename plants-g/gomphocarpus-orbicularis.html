Gomphocarpus orbicularis

Gomphocarpus orbicularis, commonly known as the orbicular milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, greenish-gray seed pods that contain toxic, milky sap. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Gomphocarpus orbicularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
