Gagea granulosa var. elatior

Gagea granulosa var. elatior, commonly known as the tall granular gagea, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces rounded, segmented bulbs that are often covered in a granular texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open woodlands or grasslands.

This variety is notable for its early blooming period and is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
