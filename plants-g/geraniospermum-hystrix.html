Geraniospermum hystrix

Geraniospermum hystrix, commonly known as the hooked geranium, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, hooked calyx that persists on the fruit. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native environment.

Geraniospermum hystrix is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and vibrant blooms.
