Geranium astragalifolium

Geranium astragalifolium, commonly known as the Astragalus-leaved geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a striking addition to botanical collections.
