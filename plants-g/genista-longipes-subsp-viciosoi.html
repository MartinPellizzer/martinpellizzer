Genista longipes subsp. viciosoi

Genista longipes subsp.

viciosoi, commonly known as the long-stemmed broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The leaves are typically narrow and trifoliate, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
