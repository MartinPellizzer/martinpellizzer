Geissorhiza romuleoides

Geissorhiza romuleoides, commonly known as the bluebell orchid, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a short, fibrous rootstock and produces a single, erect stem bearing several showy, bell-shaped flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Geissorhiza romuleoides is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
