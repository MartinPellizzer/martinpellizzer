Galium angustifolium subsp. borregoense

Galium angustifolium subsp.

borregoense, commonly known as Borrego bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant has slender, ascending stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy surface, and small white flowers appear in clusters during the spring.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
