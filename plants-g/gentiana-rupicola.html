Gentiana rupicola

Gentiana rupicola, commonly known as the rock gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Gentiana rupicola is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
