Gladiolus flexuosus

Gladiolus flexuosus, commonly known as the flexible gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces spikes of flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to store nutrients.

The flexible gladiolus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
