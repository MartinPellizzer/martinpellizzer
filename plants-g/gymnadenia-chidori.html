Gymnadenia chidori

Gymnadenia chidori, commonly known as the Japanese lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and a tall flowering stem that bears a spike of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. It has a complex root system that helps it absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

Gymnadenia chidori is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
