Gastrolobium ovalifolium

Gastrolobium ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features compound leaves composed of several elliptical to ovate leaflets, which are typically green and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes during the autumn season, which are characteristic of the legume family. It is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Gastrolobium ovalifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
