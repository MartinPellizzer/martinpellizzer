Gossypium brasiliense

Gossypium brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian cotton plant, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous annual plant belonging to the Malvaceae family, which includes other cotton species. The plant produces fluffy, white fibers around its seeds, which are used for textile production. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Gossypium brasiliense is valued for its economic and ecological significance in its native region.
