Galenia dinteri

Galenia dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's galenia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, aiding in water retention.

Galenia dinteri produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
