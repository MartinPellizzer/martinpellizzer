Ginkgo biloba var. pendula

Ginkgo biloba var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping ginkgo, is a unique variant of the ginkgo tree characterized by its pendulous branches that cascade downward, creating a distinctive, ornamental appearance.

It is a deciduous tree that produces fan-shaped leaves which turn a striking yellow in the fall. The plant is native to China and is known for its resilience to urban conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping. Its leaves contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Despite its ornamental value, it is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
