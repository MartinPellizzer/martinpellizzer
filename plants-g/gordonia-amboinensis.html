Gordonia amboinensis

Gordonia amboinensis, commonly known as the Amboina gum tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. It is known for its ability to thrive in acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The tree is also valued for its hard, heavy wood, which has been traditionally used in construction and craftsmanship.
