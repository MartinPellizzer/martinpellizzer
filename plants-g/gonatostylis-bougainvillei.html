Gonatostylis bougainvillei

Gonatostylis bougainvillei, commonly known as the Bougainville's gorgonian, is a species of soft coral found in the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching structure and vibrant coloration, often displaying shades of pink, purple, and blue. The plant thrives in shallow, warm waters and is often found in reef environments. Its growth is slow, and it plays an important role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat for various small organisms.

The coral is also valued in the aquarium trade due to its aesthetic appeal.
