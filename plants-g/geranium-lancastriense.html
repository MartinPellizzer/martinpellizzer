Geranium lancastriense

Geranium lancastriense, commonly known as the Lancashire geranium, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its upright stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Geranium lancastriense is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in British horticulture.
