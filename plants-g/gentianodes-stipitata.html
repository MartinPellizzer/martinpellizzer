Gentianodes stipitata

Gentianodes stipitata, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers are tubular and contain five petals, with the inner part often marked by dark purple or black streaks.

Gentianodes stipitata prefers well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
