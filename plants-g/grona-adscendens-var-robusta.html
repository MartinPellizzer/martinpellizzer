Grona adscendens var. robusta

Grona adscendens var. robusta, commonly known as the robust mountain vine, is a climbing plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and thick, woody stems that can reach several meters in length. The leaves are typically large, ovate, and have a glossy green surface with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and often have a pale yellow or white color.

This plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.
