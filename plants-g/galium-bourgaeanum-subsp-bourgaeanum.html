Galium bourgaeanum subsp. bourgaeanum

Galium bourgaeanum subsp.

bourgaeanum, commonly known as the small whitlow grass, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

The plant has slender stems that are often prostrate or ascending, and it produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and usually have a hairy texture.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of habitats and its historical use in traditional medicine.
