Grevillea wilsonii

Grevillea wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's glory of the snow, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that bloom in spring and summer. These flowers are composed of numerous small, tubular flowers clustered together, with prominent stamens that add to their visual appeal.

Grevillea wilsonii is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
