Grindelia tarapacana var. verrucosa

Grindelia tarapacana var. verrucosa, commonly known as the Tarapaca gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and dense clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. Its resinous leaves contain compounds that make it resistant to herbivores and extreme weather conditions.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in drought-tolerant gardens.
