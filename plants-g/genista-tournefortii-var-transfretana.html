Genista tournefortii var. transfretana

Genista tournefortii var. transfretana, commonly known as the Transalpine broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, woody stems with small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice for garden landscapes in suitable climates.
