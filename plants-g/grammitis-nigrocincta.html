Grammitis nigrocincta

Grammitis nigrocincta, commonly known as black-banded maidenhair, is a fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The leaves are typically dark green with a distinctive black or dark brown band running along the midrib. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The plant's intricate structure and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for horticultural collections.
