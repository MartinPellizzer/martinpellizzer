Gleditsia assamica

Gleditsia assamica, commonly known as the Assam honeylocust, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and thorned stems. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, flat pods containing edible seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.

Gleditsia assamica is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
