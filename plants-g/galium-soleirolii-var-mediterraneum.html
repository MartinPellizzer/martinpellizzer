Galium soleirolii var. mediterraneum

Galium soleirolii var. mediterraneum, commonly known as the Mediterranean bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

Galium soleirolii var. mediterraneum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
