Gerascanthus obovatus

Gerascanthus obovatus, commonly known as the broad-ovate geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.

Gerascanthus obovatus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
