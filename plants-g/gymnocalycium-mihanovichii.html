Gymnocalycium mihanovichii

Gymnocalycium mihanovichii, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its swollen, globular stem and a distinctive, colorful crown of flowers. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring, and it is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance.

Gymnocalycium mihanovichii is a popular choice for succulent gardens and indoor collections.
