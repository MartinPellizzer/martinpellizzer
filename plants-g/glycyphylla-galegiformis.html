Glycyphylla galegiformis

Glycyphylla galegiformis, commonly known as the sweetroot or wild licorice, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Glycyphylla galegiformis is valued for its aromatic roots, which have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
