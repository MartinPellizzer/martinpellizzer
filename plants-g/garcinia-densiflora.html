Garcinia densiflora

Garcinia densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces yellow-orange fruits that are typically harvested when ripe. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
