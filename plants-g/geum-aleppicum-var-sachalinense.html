Geum aleppicum var. sachalinense

Geum aleppicum var. sachalinense, commonly known as the Sakhalin avens, is a perennial herb native to the Far East, including parts of Russia and Japan.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and hairy, with a distinctive reddish or purplish coloration. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers in early summer, which are arranged in a raceme. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a desirable plant for wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
