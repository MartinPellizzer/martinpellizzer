Gentiana anisodonta subsp. albanica

Gentiana anisodonta subsp.

albanica, commonly known as the Albanian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
