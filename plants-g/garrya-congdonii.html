Garrya congdonii

Garrya congdonii, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that attract birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Garrya congdonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
