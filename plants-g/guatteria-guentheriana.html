Guatteria guentheriana

Guatteria guentheriana, commonly known as the Guenther's huito, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that develop into edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
