Gahnia gahniiformis

Gahnia gahniiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tussock grass, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical tussocks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear brownish or reddish flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, swamps, and along watercourses.

Gahnia gahniiformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
