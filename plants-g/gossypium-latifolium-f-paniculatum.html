Gossypium latifolium f. paniculatum

Gossypium latifolium f. paniculatum, commonly known as the broadleaf cotton or paniculate cotton, is a variant of the cotton plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and panicles of small, fluffy flowers that produce the cotton fiber. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family and is cultivated for its high-quality cotton fibers. Its growth habit includes a shrub-like structure with strong stems and robust root systems.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
