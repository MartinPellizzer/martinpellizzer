Glycine sublobata

Glycine sublobata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf soybean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, which are used for both culinary and industrial purposes. Glycine sublobata is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in agricultural and ecological studies.
