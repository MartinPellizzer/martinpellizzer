Grastidium cancroides

Grastidium cancroides, commonly known as the cancerous grass, is a rare and unusual plant species characterized by its unique botanical features.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the cooler months. It is native to specific regions with moist, nutrient-rich soils, and it exhibits a slow growth rate.
