Gentiana rubicunda subsp. filisepala

Gentiana rubicunda subsp.

filisepala, commonly known as the thread-sepal gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slender, thread-like sepals that give it its common name. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in mountain ecosystems.
