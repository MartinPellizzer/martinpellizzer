Geranium malvifolium

Geranium malvifolium, commonly known as the Malvifolium geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, and its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both horticultural collections and ecological studies.
