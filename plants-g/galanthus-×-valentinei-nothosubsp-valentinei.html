Galanthus × valentinei nothosubsp.

valentinei, commonly known as the Valentine's Day snowdrop, is a hybrid species within the genus Galanthus. It is characterized by its pale yellow flowers with a distinctive white petal base, often appearing in early spring.

The plant typically grows to a height of 10–20 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to the UK, it thrives in woodland and shady garden environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.
