Griffithianthus fuscus

Griffithianthus fuscus, commonly known as the black thorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and dark, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
