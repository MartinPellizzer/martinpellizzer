Guadua incana

Guadua incana, commonly known as the Andean bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant is highly valued for its durable and lightweight material, which is used in construction and crafts. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Guadua incana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
