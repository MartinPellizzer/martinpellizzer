Geranium koreanum f. hirsutum

Geranium koreanum f. hirsutum, commonly known as the Korean geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Korea.

It features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a serrated edge, often covered in a similar fuzzy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
