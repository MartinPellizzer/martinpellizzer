Goeppertia pumila

Goeppertia pumila, commonly known as the Snowy Canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine, white or silvery bloom, giving it a frosty appearance. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
