Gentiana zollingeri f. albiflora

Gentiana zollingeri f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the alpine regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of striking white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing showy, spurred petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its unique color and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.
