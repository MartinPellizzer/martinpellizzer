Gardenia angustifolia

Gardenia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, narrow leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are highly prized for their intense scent and are often used in perfumery.

Gardenia angustifolia thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
