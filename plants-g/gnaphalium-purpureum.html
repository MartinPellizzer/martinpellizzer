Gnaphalium purpureum

Gnaphalium purpureum, commonly known as purple cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, purple to pinkish flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical heads. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils.

Gnaphalium purpureum is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
