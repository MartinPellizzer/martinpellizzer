Gaiadendron laurifolium

Gaiadendron laurifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in moist woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
