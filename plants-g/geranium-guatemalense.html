Geranium guatemalense

Geranium guatemalense, commonly known as the Guatemalan geranium, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and its blooms come in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

Geranium guatemalense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
