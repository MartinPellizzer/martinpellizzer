Gaultheria megalodonta

Gaultheria megalodonta, commonly known as the giant wax myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. Its leaves contain high levels of aromatic oils, contributing to its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Gaultheria megalodonta is also valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
