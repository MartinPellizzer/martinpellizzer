Gomesa nitida

Gomesa nitida, commonly known as the white sand lily, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Gomesa nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, ethereal blooms.
