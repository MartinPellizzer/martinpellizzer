Gentianella pilosa subsp. degenii

Gentianella pilosa subsp.

degenii, commonly known as the hairy gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide a unique texture. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant is often found in meadows and slopes at high elevations, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
