Gleichenia flexuosa f. zuccarinii

Gleichenia flexuosa f. zuccarinii, commonly known as the Zuccarinii Gleichenia, is a fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is a perennial, evergreen plant characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically arranged in a fan shape. The plant exhibits a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping underground stems that help in vegetative propagation. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in distinct rows on the undersides of the fronds.
