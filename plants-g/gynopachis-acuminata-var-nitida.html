Gynopachis acuminata var. nitida

Gynopachis acuminata var. nitida, commonly known as the silky gynopachis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a silky texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and prolonged flowering period.
