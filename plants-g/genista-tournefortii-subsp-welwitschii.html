Genista tournefortii subsp. welwitschii

Genista tournefortii subsp.

welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's broom, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has narrow, leathery leaves and produces long, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and hardy plant in both natural and cultivated settings.
