Genista linifolia var. leucocarpa

Genista linifolia var. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, exhibiting a compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by distinctive white, capsule-like fruits that are characteristic of the species. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
