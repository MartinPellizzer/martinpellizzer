Godetia sulfurea

Godetia sulfurea, commonly known as the sulfur yellow sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, sun-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical name reflects the sulfur-yellow color of its flowers, which are a striking feature of the plant.
