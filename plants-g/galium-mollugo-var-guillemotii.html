Galium mollugo var. guillemotii

Galium mollugo var. guillemotii, commonly known as the lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically forms dense mats of slender, often reddish stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptical, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium mollugo var. guillemotii thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.
