Geranium robertianum subsp. celticum

Geranium robertianum subsp.

celticum, commonly known as the Scottish geranium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically features hairy, erect stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often glandular-dotted.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Geraniaceae family.
