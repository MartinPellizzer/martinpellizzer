Gaultheria fragrantissima subsp. fragrantissima

Gaultheria fragrantissima subsp.

fragrantissima, commonly known as the sweetleaf or fragrant wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves that emit a pleasant scent when crushed.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
