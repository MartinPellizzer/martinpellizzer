Guarea affinis

Guarea affinis, commonly known as the yellow guarea, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.
