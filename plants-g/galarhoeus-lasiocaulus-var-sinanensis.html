Galarhoeus lasiocaulus var. sinanensis

Galarhoeus lasiocaulus var. sinanensis, commonly known as the Sinanese hairy clubmoss, is a species of clubmoss native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems covered in dense, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, spore-bearing cones at the tips of its branches, typical of the lycophyte group. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Its foliage provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
