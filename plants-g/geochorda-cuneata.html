Geochorda cuneata

Geochorda cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf geochorda, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear small, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are inconspicuous, usually greenish-white, and appear in clusters at the base of the plant.

Geochorda cuneata is often found in rich, loamy soils and is relatively hardy in USDA hardiness zones 4 through 8.
