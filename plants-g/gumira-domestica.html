Gumira domestica

Gumira domestica, commonly known as the domestic gumira, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a sticky resin, which is the source of its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Gumira domestica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in local medicine.
