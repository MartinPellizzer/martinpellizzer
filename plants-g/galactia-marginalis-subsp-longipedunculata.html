Galactia marginalis subsp. longipedunculata

Galactia marginalis subsp.

longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers, which are borne on elongated peduncles. Its fruit is a slender, pod-like structure that contains several elliptical seeds.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.
