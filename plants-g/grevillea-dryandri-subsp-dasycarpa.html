Grevillea dryandri subsp. dasycarpa

Grevillea dryandri subsp.

dasycarpa, commonly known as the hairy-fruited grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and bright red or orange flowers that appear in late winter to early spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy, fleshy fruits that remain on the plant for an extended period. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, with a distinctive blue-green color.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandplain and gravelly soils.
