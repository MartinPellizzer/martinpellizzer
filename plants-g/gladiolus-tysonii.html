Gladiolus tysonii

Gladiolus tysonii, commonly known as the mountain gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous brightly colored flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a grass-like manner along the stem.

Gladiolus tysonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in cooler, well-drained soils.
