Grewia aneimenoclada

Grewia aneimenoclada, commonly known as the red gum tree, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The fruit is an important food source for various birds and mammals.
