Galactia longifolia

Galactia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf galactia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal use.
