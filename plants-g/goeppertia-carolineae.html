Goeppertia carolineae

Goeppertia carolineae, commonly known as the Caroline's Calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with intricate patterns. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, often featuring a mix of green, silver, and pink hues. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
