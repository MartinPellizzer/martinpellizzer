Gmelina leichhardtii

Gmelina leichhardtii, commonly known as the Leichhardt's gumtree, is a deciduous tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and forest habitats.

Gmelina leichhardtii is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional Aboriginal practices.
