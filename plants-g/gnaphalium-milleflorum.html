Gnaphalium milleflorum

Gnaphalium milleflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered cudweed, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant that typically thrives in moist, temperate environments. The plant produces numerous tiny yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gnaphalium milleflorum is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.
