Grevillea victoriae subsp. victoriae

Grevillea victoriae subsp.

victoriae, commonly known as the Victorian grevillea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters.

The plant is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can thrive in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. Grevillea victoriae subsp.

victoriae is also an important plant for local wildlife, providing nectar for birds and insects.
