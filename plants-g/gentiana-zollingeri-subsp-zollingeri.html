Gentiana zollingeri subsp. zollingeri

Gentiana zollingeri subsp.

zollingeri, commonly known as Zollinger's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Alps. It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August.

The plant grows in rocky or alpine meadows, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and branched.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
