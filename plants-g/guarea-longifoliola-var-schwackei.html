Guarea longifoliola var. schwackei

Guarea longifoliola var. schwackei, commonly known as the red sanders or African sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its durability and scent. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often appearing in yellow or greenish-white colors.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
