Glyceria effusa var. coreana

Glyceria effusa var. coreana, commonly known as Korean glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, riparian zones, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
