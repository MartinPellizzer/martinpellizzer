Gundelia microcephala

Gundelia microcephala, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and is adapted to harsh mountain conditions.

Gundelia microcephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
