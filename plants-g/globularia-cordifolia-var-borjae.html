Globularia cordifolia var. borjae

Globularia cordifolia var. borjae, commonly known as the Borja's heartleaf globularia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact, globular growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped, hence the species name "cordifolia," and are covered in fine hairs. This variety is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and as a drought-tolerant plant.
