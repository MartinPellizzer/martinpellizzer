Gossypium decurrens

Gossypium decurrens, commonly known as the creeping cottonweed, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, ovate leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, and it produces small, fluffy seeds enclosed in a capsule.

Gossypium decurrens is often found in dry, rocky areas and is adapted to arid environments.
