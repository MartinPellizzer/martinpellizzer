Grevillea parvula

Grevillea parvula, commonly known as the small-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically bright green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in clusters, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea parvula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate salt spray.
