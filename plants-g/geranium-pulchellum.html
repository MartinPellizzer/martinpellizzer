Geranium pulchellum

Geranium pulchellum, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and palmate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, typically blooming from autumn to spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium pulchellum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
