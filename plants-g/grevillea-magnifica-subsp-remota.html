Grevillea magnifica subsp. remota

Grevillea magnifica subsp.

remota, commonly known as the Darling Range Grevillea, is a striking shrub native to the western regions of Australia. It is characterized by its bright red, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that attract a variety of nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky garden environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
