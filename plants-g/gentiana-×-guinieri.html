Gentiana × guinieri

Gentiana × guinieri, commonly known as the hybrid gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
