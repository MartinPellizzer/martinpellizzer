Gouldia bobeoides

Gouldia bobeoides, commonly known as the red lily, is a striking plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb that produces showy, pink to red flowers in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Gouldia bobeoides is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
