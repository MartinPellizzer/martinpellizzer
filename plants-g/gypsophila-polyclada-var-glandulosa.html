Gypsophila polyclada var. glandulosa

Gypsophila polyclada var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is known for its ability to bloom throughout the summer months.

Glandular gypsophila is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
