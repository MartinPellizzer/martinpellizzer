Gamanthus leucophysus

Gamanthus leucophysus, commonly known as the white-rayed gamanthus, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals that contrast with its deep green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool, humid environment to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
