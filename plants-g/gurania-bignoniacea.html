Gurania bignoniacea

Gurania bignoniacea, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in natural habitats.
