Grastidium triangulum

Grastidium triangulum, commonly known as the triangular bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped leaves and bell-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its flowers bloom in late spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Grastidium triangulum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
