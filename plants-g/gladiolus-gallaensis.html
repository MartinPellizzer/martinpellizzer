Gladiolus gallaensis

Gladiolus gallaensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sword-like leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of brightly colored blooms in shades of red, yellow, and orange, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Due to its unique appearance and cultural significance, it is also valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal uses.
