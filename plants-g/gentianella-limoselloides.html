Gentianella limoselloides

Gentianella limoselloides, commonly known as the bog gentian, is a flowering plant native to wetland and alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
