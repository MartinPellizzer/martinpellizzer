Gamolepis tagetes

Gamolepis tagetes, commonly known as Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a strong, aromatic scent. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Gamolepis tagetes is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a natural pest repellent.
