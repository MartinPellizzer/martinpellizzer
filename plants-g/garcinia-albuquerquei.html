Garcinia albuquerquei

Garcinia albuquerquei, commonly known as the Albuquerquie Garcinia, is a tropical shrub native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that contain a high concentration of hydroxycitric acid, which has been studied for its potential health benefits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it is of interest in both botanical research and herbal medicine.
