Geranium mascatense var. camerunense

Geranium mascatense var. camerunense, commonly known as the Cameroon geranium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish in color. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
