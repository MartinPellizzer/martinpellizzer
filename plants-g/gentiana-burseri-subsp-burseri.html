Gentiana burseri subsp. burseri

Gentiana burseri subsp.

burseri, commonly known as the Burseri gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Ethiopian Highlands. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its mountainous habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence, while its stems are slender and erect.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
