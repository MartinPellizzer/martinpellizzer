Grammosciadium pterocarpum

Grammosciadium pterocarpum, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in nutrients and have a tangy flavor. It is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisine and local remedies.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
