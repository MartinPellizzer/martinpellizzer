Grindelia aphanactis

Grindelia aphanactis, commonly known as the desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. Its leaves are hairy and often contain a milky sap, which can be sticky and emit a strong odor.

Grindelia aphanactis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
