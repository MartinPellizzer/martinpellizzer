Gentiana arenarioides

Gentiana arenarioides, commonly known as the sand gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in alpine and subalpine environments. Its leaves are generally narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Gentiana arenarioides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
