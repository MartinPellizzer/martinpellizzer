Gladiolus varius

Gladiolus varius, commonly known as the variable gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its blooms, which can be single or in clusters, come in a variety of colors including red, pink, yellow, and white.

The flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their striking appearance and long-lasting blooms.
