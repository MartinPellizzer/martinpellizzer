Gaura parvifolia

Gaura parvifolia, commonly known as the fairy wand or parvifolia gaura, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wand-like stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. Gaura parvifolia is also known for its frost tolerance and ability to reseed itself, making it a hardy and low-maintenance plant.
