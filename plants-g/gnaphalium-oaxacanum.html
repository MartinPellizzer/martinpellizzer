Gnaphalium oaxacanum

Gnaphalium oaxacanum, commonly known as Mexican feather grass, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. It produces a profusion of tiny white to pale yellow florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gnaphalium oaxacanum is also noted for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
