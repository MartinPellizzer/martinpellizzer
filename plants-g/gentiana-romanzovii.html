Gentiana romanzovii

Gentiana romanzovii, commonly known as the Romanov gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and sometimes branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
