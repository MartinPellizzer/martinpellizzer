Grevillea simulans

Grevillea simulans, commonly known as the pink and white grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like foliage and vibrant, pinkish-white floral spikes. The plant produces nectar-rich flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including honeyeaters and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grevillea simulans is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
