Grindelia nana f. longisquama

Grindelia nana f. longisquama, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gumweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and hairy, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive, waxy texture.

Grindelia nana f. longisquama is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open, sunny areas.
