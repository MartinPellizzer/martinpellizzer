Guioa geminata

Guioa geminata, commonly known as the twinleaf guioa, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters and has simple, alternate, elliptic to ovate leaves that are usually 5 to 12 centimeters long. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in various soil types.

Guioa geminata is also important in reforestation efforts due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
