Gnaphalium cheiranthifolium var. subrufescens

Gnaphalium cheiranthifolium var. subrufescens, commonly known as the red-tinged everlasting flower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy inflorescences composed of small, yellow to reddish-brown florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs, while the stems are erect and somewhat woody.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for use in floral arrangements.
