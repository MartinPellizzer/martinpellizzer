Gilia filifolia var. harwoodii

Gilia filifolia var. harwoodii, commonly known as Harwood's gilia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry, open habitats such as chaparral or scrublands. Its leaves are linear and finely divided, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
