Glochidion moonii f. glaucogynum

Glochidion moonii f. glaucogynum, commonly known as the glaucous-leafed moonii glochidion, is a variant of the moonii species within the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive glaucous (bluish-gray) leaf surface, which provides a unique visual appearance. The plant typically exhibits small, ovate leaves with a smooth margin and a short petiole. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
