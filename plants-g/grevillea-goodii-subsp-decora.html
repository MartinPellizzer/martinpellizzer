Grevillea goodii subsp. decora

Grevillea goodii subsp.

decora, commonly known as the pink-stemmed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive pink or red stems, which are covered in fine, woolly hairs.

The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
