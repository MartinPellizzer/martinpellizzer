Galium × lanulosum

Galium × lanulosum, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Galium × lanulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
