Goniothalamus macrocalyx

Goniothalamus macrocalyx, commonly known as the giant yew of Borneo, is a member of the Annonaceae family.

It is a large evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters, characterized by its thick, flaky bark and broad, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by large, fleshy berries. It is native to the rainforests of Borneo and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered an endangered species.
