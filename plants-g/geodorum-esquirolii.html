Geodorum esquirolii

Geodorum esquirolii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive, fragrant aroma. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it has a rhizomatous root system.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
