Gentiana stiriaca var. prematura

Gentiana stiriaca var. prematura, commonly known as the early gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom early in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along streams. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
