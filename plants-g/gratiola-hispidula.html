Gratiola hispidula

Gratiola hispidula, commonly known as the hairy yellow crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in early spring. Its flowering stems are often covered in fine, bristly hairs, contributing to its common name.

Gratiola hispidula is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or yellow flowers.
