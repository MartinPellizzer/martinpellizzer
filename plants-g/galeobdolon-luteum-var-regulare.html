Galeobdolon luteum var. regulare

Galeobdolon luteum var. regulare, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a nectar source for pollinators and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
