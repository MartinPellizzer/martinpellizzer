Gynura clementis

Gynura clementis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false holly, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Gynura clementis is also valued as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
