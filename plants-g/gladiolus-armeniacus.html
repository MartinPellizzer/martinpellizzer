Gladiolus armeniacus

Gladiolus armeniacus, commonly known as the Armenian gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with dense clusters of blooms at the top of the stem. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant color and long-lasting blooms.
