Gentiana angustifolia var. australis

Gentiana angustifolia var. australis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to southern regions of South America.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to October. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are rich in nectar and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
