Gentianopsis crinita

Gentianopsis crinita, commonly known as the curly gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodlands and wetlands.

The genus Gentianopsis is closely related to the gentian family, sharing similar floral structures and ecological preferences.
