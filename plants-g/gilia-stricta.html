Gilia stricta

Gilia stricta, commonly known as the slender gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Gilia stricta is known for its adaptability to arid environments and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
