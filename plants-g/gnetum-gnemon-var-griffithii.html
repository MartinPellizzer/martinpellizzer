Gnetum gnemon var. griffithii

Gnetum gnemon var. griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's kempa, is a species of flowering plant in the family Gnetaceae.

It is a dioecious plant, meaning it has separate male and female individuals. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

The wood of this plant is valued for its durability and is sometimes used in traditional crafts and construction.
