Guibourtia ehie

Guibourtia ehie, commonly known as African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is valued for its durable, heavy timber used in furniture and construction.

Due to overharvesting, it is also listed on the IUCN Red List as a vulnerable species.
