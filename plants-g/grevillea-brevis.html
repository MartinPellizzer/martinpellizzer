Grevillea brevis

Grevillea brevis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in clusters, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea brevis is valued for its ornamental value and ecological benefits, supporting local wildlife.
