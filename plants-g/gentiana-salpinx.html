Gentiana salpinx

Gentiana salpinx, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and branched.

Gentiana salpinx is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
