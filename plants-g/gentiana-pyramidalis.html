Gentiana pyramidalis

Gentiana pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is erect and bears the showy inflorescence.

The flowers are tubular with two lips, and they attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
