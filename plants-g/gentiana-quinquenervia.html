Gentiana quinquenervia

Gentiana quinquenervia, commonly known as the five-veined gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountain meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped with five distinct veins, giving it its scientific name.

Gentiana quinquenervia is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
