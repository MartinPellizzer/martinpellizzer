Gladiolus permeabilis subsp. wilsonii

Gladiolus permeabilis subsp.

wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are long and narrow, forming a dense rosette at the base.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
