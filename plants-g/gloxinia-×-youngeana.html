Gloxinia × youngeana

Gloxinia × youngeana, commonly known as the hybrid gloxinia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Gloxinia boliviana and Gloxinia suaveolens.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is valued for its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors such as red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture. Gloxinia × youngeana is typically grown as a houseplant or in greenhouse conditions due to its preference for warm, humid environments.

It blooms during the spring and summer months, producing clusters of tubular flowers that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
