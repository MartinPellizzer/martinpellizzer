Galeopsis ladanum subsp. calcarea

Galeopsis ladanum subsp.

calcarea, commonly known as limestone milkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, calcareous soils, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The plant features slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are clustered in loose spikes, with pale blue to purple petals that attract pollinators.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to alkaline conditions and its role in supporting local insect populations.
