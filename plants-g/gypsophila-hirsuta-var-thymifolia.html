Gypsophila hirsuta var. thymifolia

Gypsophila hirsuta var. thymifolia, commonly known as the woolly gypsophila or thyme-leaved gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy white or pink flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, sterile flowers. The plant has hairy, grayish stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is closely related to other gypsophila species and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
