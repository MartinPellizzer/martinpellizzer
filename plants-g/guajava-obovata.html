Guajava obovata

Guajava obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate guajava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically pubescent on the underside. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. The fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is often found in humid, lowland forests.
