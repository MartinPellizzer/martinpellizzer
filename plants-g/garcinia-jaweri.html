Garcinia jaweri

Garcinia jaweri, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces small, sour fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a distinctive fruit with a thick, leathery rind.

Garcinia jaweri is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
