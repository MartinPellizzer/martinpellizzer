Gaultheria leucocarpa var. psilocarpa

Gaultheria leucocarpa var. psilocarpa, commonly known as the red-berried wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and have a spicy, mint-like flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
