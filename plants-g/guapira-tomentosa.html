Guapira tomentosa

Guapira tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly guapira, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue or black berries.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
