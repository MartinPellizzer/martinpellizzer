Grusonia echinocarpa

Grusonia echinocarpa, commonly known as the spiny-fruited grusonia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, spiny, berry-like fruits. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Grusonia echinocarpa thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry or seasonally arid environments.
