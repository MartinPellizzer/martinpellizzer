Gentianella trailliana

Gentianella trailliana, commonly known as the Traill's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentianella trailliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
