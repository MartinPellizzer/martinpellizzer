Guadua chaparensis

Guadua chaparensis, commonly known as the Chapare bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. This species is highly valued for its durable and flexible culms, which are used in construction and handicrafts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Guadua chaparensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
