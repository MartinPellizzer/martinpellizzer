Guzmania pleiosticha

Guzmania pleiosticha, commonly known as the pink torch plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that often displays shades of red, orange, and pink. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect rainwater, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns and can vary in color depending on light exposure.

Guzmania pleiosticha thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value.
