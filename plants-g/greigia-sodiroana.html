Greigia sodiroana

Greigia sodiroana, commonly known as the Peruvian cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and is typically found in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its succulent structure allows it to store water, making it well adapted to dry conditions.

Greigia sodiroana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with suitable climates.
