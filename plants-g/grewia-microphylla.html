Grewia microphylla

Grewia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved grewia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and inland areas.

Grewia microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
