Galanthus alpinus var. bortkewitschianus

Galanthus alpinus var. bortkewitschianus, commonly known as the Bortkewitsch's snowdrop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, nodding flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single flower per stem. It thrives in well-drained, slightly alkaline soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
