Grevillea trachytheca

Grevillea trachytheca, commonly known as the rough-barked grevillea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in rough, fissured bark. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight waxy coating.

Grevillea trachytheca is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
