Gentiana macrophylla var. albolutea

Gentiana macrophylla var. albolutea, commonly known as the white-flowered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and bilaterally symmetrical. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
