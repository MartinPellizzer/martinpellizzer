Gesneria pendulina

Gesneria pendulina, commonly known as the hanging gesneria, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, hanging stems that produce clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has lance-shaped, waxy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.

Gesneria pendulina is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental appeal and unique growth habit.
