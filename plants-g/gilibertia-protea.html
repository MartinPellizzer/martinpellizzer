Gilibertia protea

Gilibertia protea, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Gilibertia protea is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
