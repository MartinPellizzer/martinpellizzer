Galtonia candicans

Galtonia candicans, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces a tall, slender stem with several showy, white to pale yellow flowers that have a pleasant fragrance. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Galtonia candicans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to well-drained, sunny gardens.
