Glyceria pallida

Glyceria pallida, commonly known as slender glyceria, is a species of grass native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dense mats, often forming part of the vegetation in shallow water or moist soil. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, producing minute flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Glyceria pallida plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
