Gladiolus vinosomaculatus

Gladiolus vinosomaculatus, commonly known as the spotted violet gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers typically display a combination of deep purple and white, with distinctive spots or markings that give it its common name. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
