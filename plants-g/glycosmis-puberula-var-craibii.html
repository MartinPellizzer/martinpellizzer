Glycosmis puberula var. craibii

Glycosmis puberula var. craibii, commonly known as the hairy wax apple, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits that are covered in a waxy coating. The leaves are simple, ovate, and often pubescent, giving the plant its common name. The plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some areas.
