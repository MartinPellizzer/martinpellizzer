Glossostelma cabrae

Glossostelma cabrae, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
