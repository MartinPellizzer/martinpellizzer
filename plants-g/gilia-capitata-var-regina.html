Gilia capitata var. regina

Gilia capitata var. regina, commonly known as the Queen's gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences of vibrant pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
