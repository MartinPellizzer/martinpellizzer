Geranium filipes

Geranium filipes, commonly known as the threadleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that give it its common name, and it produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Geranium filipes is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
