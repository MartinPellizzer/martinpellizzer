Gurania diversifolia var. quinquefolia

Gurania diversifolia var. quinquefolia, commonly known as the five-leafed gurania, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small shrub characterized by its compound leaves, which typically consist of five leaflets arranged in a pinnate pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats within rainforests.
