Gomphrena pungens

Gomphrena pungens, commonly known as the gayfeather, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, plume-like inflorescences that range in color from pink to red and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Gomphrena pungens is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
