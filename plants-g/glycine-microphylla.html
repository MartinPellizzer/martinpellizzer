Glycine microphylla

Glycine microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf soybean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. Glycine microphylla is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches soil fertility.

It is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in some regions.
