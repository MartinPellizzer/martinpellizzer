Geum grahamii

Geum grahamii, commonly known as Graham's avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in cymes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
