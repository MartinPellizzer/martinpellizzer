Gardenia tubifera var. costulata

Gardenia tubifera var. costulata, commonly known as the costate gardenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often longitudinally ribbed or costate, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in subtropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal and unique foliage.
