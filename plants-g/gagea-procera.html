Gagea procera

Gagea procera, commonly known as the tall gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers. Gagea procera is often found in elevations ranging from 1,000 to 4,000 meters and is adapted to temperate climates.
