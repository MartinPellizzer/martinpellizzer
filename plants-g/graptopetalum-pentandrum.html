Graptopetalum pentandrum

Graptopetalum pentandrum, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
