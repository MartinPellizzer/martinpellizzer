Graminastrum

Graminastrum, commonly known as the grassland beauty, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The flowers of Graminastrum are typically small and clustered in panicles, often with a feathery appearance. These plants are native to various regions of the Americas and are often found in open, sunny habitats.
