Gladiolus resupinatus

Gladiolus resupinatus, commonly known as the upturned gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, where the flowers are oriented downward, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows from a corm and produces striking, showy blooms in shades of red, yellow, and orange. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that form a rosette around the base.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
