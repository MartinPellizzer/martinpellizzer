Grindelia scorzonerifolia var. spathulata

Grindelia scorzonerifolia var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, waxy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in elongated, branched inflorescences, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its unique foliage and floral structure make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
