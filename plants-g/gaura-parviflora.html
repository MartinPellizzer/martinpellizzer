Gaura parviflora

Gaura parviflora, commonly known as the slender gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern and central United States.

It features tall, slender stems covered in clusters of small, showy pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies enhance its ornamental value.

Gaura parviflora is also drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
