Gnaphalium paludosum

Gnaphalium paludosum, commonly known as swamp cudweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically features dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Gnaphalium paludosum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
