Gerardia hispidula

Gerardia hispidula, commonly known as the rough gerardia, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodlands or along streambanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its small, white to pale pink flowers appear in clusters during late spring to early summer.

Gerardia hispidula is characterized by its fibrous root system and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.
