Gentiana coerulescens

Gentiana coerulescens, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Gentiana coerulescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
