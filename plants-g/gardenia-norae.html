Gardenia norae

Gardenia norae, commonly known as the narrowleaf gardenia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are highly prized for their strong, sweet scent and are often used in perfumery.

Gardenia norae prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
