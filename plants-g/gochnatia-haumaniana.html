Gochnatia haumaniana

Gochnatia haumaniana, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Gochnatia haumaniana is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional herbal remedies.
