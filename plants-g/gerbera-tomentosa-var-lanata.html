Gerbera tomentosa var. lanata

Gerbera tomentosa var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is a perennial herb that produces numerous small, tubular flowers surrounded by broad, petal-like rays.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
