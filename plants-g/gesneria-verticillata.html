Gesneria verticillata

Gesneria verticillata, commonly known as the whorled gesneria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Gesneria verticillata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
