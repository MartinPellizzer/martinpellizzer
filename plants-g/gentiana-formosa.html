Gentiana formosa

Gentiana formosa, commonly known as the Chinese gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it grows in rocky or forested habitats. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
