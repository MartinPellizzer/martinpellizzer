Glaucium grandiflorum

Glaucium grandiflorum, commonly known as the greater celandine, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers and hairy, ribbed stems. The plant has lobed, alternate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in meadows and rocky soils.

Glaucium grandiflorum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
