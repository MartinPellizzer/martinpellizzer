Geranium meridense

Geranium meridense, commonly known as the Mexican geranium, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant pink to red blooms, which appear in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually palmately lobed, with a rough texture and a distinct, slightly serrated edge.

Geranium meridense is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
