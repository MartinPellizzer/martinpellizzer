Gypsophila sericea

Gypsophila sericea, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has silvery-gray leaves covered in fine hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Gypsophila sericea is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
