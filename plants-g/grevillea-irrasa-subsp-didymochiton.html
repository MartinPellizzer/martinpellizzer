Grevillea irrasa subsp. didymochiton

Grevillea irrasa subsp.

didymochiton, commonly known as the red-stemmed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive red stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces striking, pendulous flowers in shades of red and orange, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in native plantings.
