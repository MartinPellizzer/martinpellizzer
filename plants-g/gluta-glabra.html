Gluta glabra

Gluta glabra, commonly known as the white gluta, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

The latex it secretes has been used for various therapeutic purposes.
