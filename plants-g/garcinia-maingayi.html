Garcinia maingayi

Garcinia maingayi, commonly known as the white mangostin, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The fruit of Garcinia maingayi is small, round, and typically has a smooth, pale pink or white skin. The pulp is juicy and acidic, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
