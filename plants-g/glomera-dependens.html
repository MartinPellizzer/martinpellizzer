Glomera dependens

Glomera dependens, commonly known as the hanging glomera, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, fragrant flowers arranged in a loose cluster. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and slightly fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its natural habitat.

The flowers bloom in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and green, making it a visually striking plant in cultivation.
