Gentianella caucasica

Gentianella caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, mountainous habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gentianella caucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
