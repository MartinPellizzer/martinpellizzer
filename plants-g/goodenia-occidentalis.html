Goodenia occidentalis

Goodenia occidentalis, commonly known as the western goodenia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern regions of Western Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It is part of the Goodeniaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive floral structures and adaptations to dry climates.

The species is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance in its native habitat.
