Garcinia hookeri

Garcinia hookeri, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible. The fruit's pericarp contains a high concentration of alkaloids and is used in traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
