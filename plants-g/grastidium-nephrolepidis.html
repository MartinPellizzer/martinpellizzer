Grastidium nephrolepidis

Grastidium nephrolepidis, commonly known as the kidney fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive fronds that resemble a kidney shape.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping underground stems that produce new fronds. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

Grastidium nephrolepidis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
