Gerardia flava var. reticulata

Gerardia flava var. reticulata, commonly known as the golden gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to rock gardens and xeriscapes.
