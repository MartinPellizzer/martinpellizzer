Gentiana leptoclada

Gentiana leptoclada, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Gentiana leptoclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
