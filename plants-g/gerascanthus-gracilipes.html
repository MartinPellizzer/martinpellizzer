Gerascanthus gracilipes

Gerascanthus gracilipes, commonly known as the slender-stemmed geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

Gerascanthus gracilipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
