Genista salicifolia

Genista salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf broom, is a flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and resemble willow leaves, giving it its common name.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
