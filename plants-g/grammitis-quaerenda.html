Grammitis quaerenda

Grammitis quaerenda, commonly known as the quest fern, is a species of epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of tiny, scale-like structures. The plant typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments, particularly in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are surrounded by a protective indusium.

Grammitis quaerenda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
