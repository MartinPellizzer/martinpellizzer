Gentiana quaterna subsp. longiflora

Gentiana quaterna subsp.

longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is usually unbranched and somewhat hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
