Gentlea micranthera

Gentlea micranthera, commonly known as the small-bract gentian, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and streamside areas. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough, hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
