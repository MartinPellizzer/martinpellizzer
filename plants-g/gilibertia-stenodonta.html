Gilibertia stenodonta

Gilibertia stenodonta, commonly known as the red brome, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale to reddish bracts, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Gilibertia stenodonta is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
