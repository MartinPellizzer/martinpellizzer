Gladiolus acuminatus

Gladiolus acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-pointed gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, sword-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Gladiolus acuminatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
