Gouldia terminalis f. waipioensis

Gouldia terminalis f. waipioensis, commonly known as the Waipio gouldia, is a rare and striking bromeliad native to Hawaii.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in late summer, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant forms a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, providing a habitat for small aquatic organisms. Its unique coloration and habitat make it a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.

The variety is considered endangered due to habitat loss and limited distribution.
