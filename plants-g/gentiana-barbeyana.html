Gentiana barbeyana

Gentiana barbeyana, commonly known as the Barbet's gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

Gentiana barbeyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
