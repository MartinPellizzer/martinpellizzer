Galanthus plicatus subsp. gueneri

Galanthus plicatus subsp.

gueneri, commonly known as the Guernsey daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the British Isles. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The flowers typically have a single layer of petals and are borne on sturdy, upright stems. This subspecies is often found in woodland edges and moist meadows, thriving in partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a hardy addition to gardens with well-drained soil.
