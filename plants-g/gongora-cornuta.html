Gongora cornuta

Gongora cornuta, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often feature a distinctive horn-like spur. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate and ornate floral structure. Gongora cornuta typically grows on tree trunks or branches, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject for both horticultural enthusiasts and botanical researchers.
