Goeppertia pilosa

Goeppertia pilosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf caladium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its foliage displays a range of colors, including shades of green, red, and purple, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
