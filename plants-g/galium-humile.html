Galium humile

Galium humile, commonly known as dwarf whitebed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters, with slender stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium humile is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
