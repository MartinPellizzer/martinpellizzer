Geum aleppicum subsp. molle

Geum aleppicum subsp.

molle, commonly known as soft-stemmed avens, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branched stems that are covered in soft, hairy tissue, giving it its common name.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
