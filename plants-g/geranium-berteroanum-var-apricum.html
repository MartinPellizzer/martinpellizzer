Geranium berteroanum var. apricum

Geranium berteroanum var. apricum, commonly known as the apricot geranium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.
