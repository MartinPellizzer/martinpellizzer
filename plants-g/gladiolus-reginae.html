Gladiolus reginae

Gladiolus reginae, commonly known as the imperial gladiolus, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with vibrant, colorful flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, showcasing a wide range of hues including red, pink, and white. Its botanical name reflects its regal appearance and historical significance in horticulture.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
