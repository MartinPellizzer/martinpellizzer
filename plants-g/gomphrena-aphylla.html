Gomphrena aphylla

Gomphrena aphylla, commonly known as the leafless gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink, red, or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are reduced to small scales, giving it the "leafless" appearance in its common name.

Gomphrena aphylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flower colors and drought tolerance.
