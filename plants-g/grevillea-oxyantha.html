Grevillea oxyantha

Grevillea oxyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waratah, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, crimson-red, fluffy flower spikes that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is typically found in heathlands and dry forests. It is part of the Proteaceae family, which includes many other iconic Australian plants.

Grevillea oxyantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
