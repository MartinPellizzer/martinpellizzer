Genista pseudoretamoides

Genista pseudoretamoides, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.
