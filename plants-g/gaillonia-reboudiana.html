Gaillonia reboudiana

Gaillonia reboudiana, commonly known as the red-flowered milkweed, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a waxy texture. Its latex is milky and toxic, containing compounds that can be harmful to animals and humans.

Gaillonia reboudiana is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
