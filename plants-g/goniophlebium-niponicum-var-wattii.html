Goniophlebium niponicum var. wattii

Goniophlebium niponicum var. wattii, commonly known as the Japanese moss fern, is a variant of the moss fern genus Goniophlebium.

It is characterized by its dense, carpet-like growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are borne on the underside of the fronds.

This variant is native to parts of East Asia, including Japan, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
