Gentiana lutea var. dilatata

Gentiana lutea var. dilatata, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
