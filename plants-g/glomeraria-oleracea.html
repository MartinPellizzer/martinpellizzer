Glomeraria oleracea

Glomeraria oleracea, commonly known as the edible burrweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that enclose edible seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The seeds are often consumed by both humans and animals, making it an important plant in local ecosystems.
