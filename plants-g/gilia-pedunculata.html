Gilia pedunculata

Gilia pedunculata, commonly known as the desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple. Its flowers are typically tubular with five petals and are adapted for pollination by birds and insects.

Gilia pedunculata is often found in disturbed soils and plays a role in stabilizing sandy or rocky habitats.
