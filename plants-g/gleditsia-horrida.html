Gleditsia horrida

Gleditsia horrida, commonly known as the horrid locust, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 10 to 20 inches long and composed of 15 to 30 pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which are followed by elongated, flat pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant has a rugged appearance, with thorns on its branches and a rough, fissured bark.
