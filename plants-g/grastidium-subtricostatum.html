Grastidium subtricostatum

Grastidium subtricostatum, commonly known as the three-ribbed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves with three distinct ribs running along their length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses. It thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists studying grassland ecosystems.
