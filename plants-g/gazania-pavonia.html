Gazania pavonia

Gazania pavonia, commonly known as the gold coin flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like blooms. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers that resemble coins. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gazania pavonia is known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
