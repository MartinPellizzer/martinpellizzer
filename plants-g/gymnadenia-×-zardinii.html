Gymnadenia × zardinii

Gymnadenia × zardinii, commonly known as the hybrid grass pink, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Gymnadenia coccinea and Gymnadenia phyteuma, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, often in temperate regions of Europe. Its flowers are pink to purple in color, with a distinctive grass-like appearance, and it blooms from late summer to early autumn.

Gymnadenia × zardinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
