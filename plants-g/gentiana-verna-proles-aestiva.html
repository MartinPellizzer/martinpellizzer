Gentiana verna proles aestiva

Gentiana verna proles aestiva, commonly known as the early gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, showy blooms in early summer.

Gentiana verna proles aestiva is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
