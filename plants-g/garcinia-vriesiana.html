Garcinia vriesiana

Garcinia vriesiana, commonly known as the Vries's garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces orange-red berries that contain a yellowish pulp and a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Garcinia vriesiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
