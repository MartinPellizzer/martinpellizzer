Gaultheria tasmanica

Gaultheria tasmanica, commonly known as the Tasmanian cranberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Tasmania.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and have a tart, cranberry-like flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in cool, moist habitats such as heathlands and forest understories.

Gaultheria tasmanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
