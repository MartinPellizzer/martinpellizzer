Gentiana masonii

Gentiana masonii, commonly known as Mason's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest understories. Gentiana masonii is a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
