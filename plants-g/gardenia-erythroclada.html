Gardenia erythroclada

Gardenia erythroclada, commonly known as the red-veined gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent red veins, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring partial shade and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique foliage and attractive blooms, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in horticulture.
