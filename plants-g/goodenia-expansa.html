Goodenia expansa

Goodenia expansa, commonly known as the broad-leaved goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in various habitats such as shrublands and grasslands. It has a compact growth habit with erect stems and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated by insects, contributing to local biodiversity.
