Gymnocalycium damsii var. centrispinum

Gymnocalycium damsii var. centrispinum, commonly known as the Centrispinus moon cactus, is a succulent native to the deserts of South America.

It features a swollen, ribbed stem that stores water, and it produces small, showy flowers during the warmer months. The plant has spiny areoles, which are characteristic of the Cactaceae family. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
