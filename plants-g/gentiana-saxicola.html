Gentiana saxicola

Gentiana saxicola, commonly known as the saxicolous gentian, is a perennial herb native to alpine and rocky habitats in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in crevices and on rocky slopes, adapting to poor soil conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Gentiana saxicola is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
