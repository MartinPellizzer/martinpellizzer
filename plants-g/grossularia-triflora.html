Grossularia triflora

Grossularia triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or yellow, containing a high concentration of antioxidants. Its leaves are ovate and serrated, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Grossularia triflora is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
