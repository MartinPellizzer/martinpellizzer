Geracium molle

Geracium molle, commonly known as the soft geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.

Geracium molle prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
