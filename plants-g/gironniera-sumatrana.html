Gironniera sumatrana

Gironniera sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra.

It is characterized by its hard, heavy wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has a dense canopy and can grow up to 30 meters tall, with smooth, dark-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a leathery texture.

The tree produces small, fragrant flowers and red, fleshy fruits that are important for local wildlife.
