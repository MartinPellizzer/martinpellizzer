Gilliesia

Gilliesia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical regions of Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Gilliesia species are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in indoor environments. They prefer bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

The flowers typically bloom in spring and summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and homes.
