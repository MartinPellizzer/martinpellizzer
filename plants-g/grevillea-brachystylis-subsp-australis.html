Grevillea brachystylis subsp. australis

Grevillea brachystylis subsp.

australis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a small shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and vibrant red or orange flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is well adapted to coastal and dry inland environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
