Geranium ovale

Geranium ovale, commonly known as the oval geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Geranium ovale is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
