Galenia spathulata

Galenia spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed galenia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, terminal spikes. Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Galenia spathulata is often found in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to dry conditions.
