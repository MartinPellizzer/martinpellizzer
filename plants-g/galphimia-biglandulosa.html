Galphimia biglandulosa

Galphimia biglandulosa, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are usually opposite, elliptical, and covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture.

Galphimia biglandulosa is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
