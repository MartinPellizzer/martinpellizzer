Guarea balansae

Guarea balansae, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes species known for their aromatic wood. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark-red bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, arranged in panicles, and it produces woody, oblong fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

Due to its valuable timber, Guarea balansae is often threatened by deforestation and habitat loss.
