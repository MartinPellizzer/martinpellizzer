Geranium hernandesii

Geranium hernandesii, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
