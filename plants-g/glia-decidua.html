Glia decidua

Glia decidua, commonly known as the deciduous glia, is a unique plant species characterized by its specialized leaf structure.

It possesses broad, lobed leaves that shed seasonally, adapting to seasonal changes in its environment. The plant's root system is fibrous, allowing it to efficiently absorb nutrients from the soil. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically blooming in the spring.

Glia decidua thrives in moist, temperate climates and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
