Geranium exul

Geranium exul, commonly known as the wandering geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Geranium exul is adapted to arid environments and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
