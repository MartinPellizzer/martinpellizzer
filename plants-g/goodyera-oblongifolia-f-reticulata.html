Goodyera oblongifolia f. reticulata

Goodyera oblongifolia f. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate lady's slipper, is a rare and delicate epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and striking, slipper-shaped flowers that range in color from greenish-white to pale pink. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically growing on tree trunks or branches in the understory of forests. Its intricate network of veins on the leaves gives it the common name "reticulate," referring to its net-like pattern.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in protected areas and is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
