Graphorkis bracteosa

Graphorkis bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteosa orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of exotic plants.
