Galax erythrorhiza

Galax erythrorhiza, commonly known as the redroot galax, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Galax erythrorhiza is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
