Gentiana pallida

Gentiana pallida, commonly known as the pale gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to sky-blue flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Gentiana pallida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
