Gratiola peruviana var. pumilo

Gratiola peruviana var. pumilo, commonly known as the Peruvian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in temperate to cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
