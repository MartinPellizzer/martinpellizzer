Guadua aculeata var. liebmanniana

Guadua aculeata var. liebmanniana, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in countries like Colombia and Ecuador. This variant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, feathery foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

The plant is also valued for its strong, lightweight wood, which is used in construction and handicrafts.
