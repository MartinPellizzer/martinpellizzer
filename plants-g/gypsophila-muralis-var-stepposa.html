Gypsophila muralis var. stepposa

Gypsophila muralis var. stepposa, commonly known as the steppe gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, delicate pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
