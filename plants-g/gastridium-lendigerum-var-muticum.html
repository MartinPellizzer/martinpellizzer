Gastridium lendigerum var. muticum

Gastridium lendigerum var. muticum, commonly known as the variable gastridium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known outside of its native region.
