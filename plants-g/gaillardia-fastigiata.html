Gaillardia fastigiata

Gaillardia fastigiata, commonly known as the stiffleaf blazing star, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant produces showy, composite flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.

Gaillardia fastigiata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
