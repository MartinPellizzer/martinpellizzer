Gymnocalycium achirasense subsp. echinatum

Gymnocalycium achirasense subsp.

echinatum, commonly known as the Echinatums prickly pear, is a cacti species native to Argentina. It is characterized by its globular to cylindrical stem that often becomes branched with age.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its spines are typically short and white, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
