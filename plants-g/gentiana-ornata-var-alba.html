Gentiana ornata var. alba

Gentiana ornata var. alba, commonly known as the white gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps and surrounding regions.

It is characterized by its striking blue or white flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, white down.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
