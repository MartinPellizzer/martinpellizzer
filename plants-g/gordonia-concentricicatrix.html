Gordonia concentricicatrix

Gordonia concentricicatrix, commonly known as the concentric bark tree, is a unique species native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, concentrically patterned bark, which resembles tree rings and can be used in traditional medicine. The plant belongs to the Ericaceae family and is known for its evergreen leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers. Its growth habit is slow, and it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a conservation priority in its natural habitat.
