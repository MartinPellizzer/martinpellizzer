Guzmania albescens

Guzmania albescens, commonly known as the white-tipped bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that often features a white or pale tip. The plant's rosette-shaped leaves collect rainwater, creating a microhabitat for small organisms. Its foliage is typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it produces showy flowers that bloom for several months.

Guzmania albescens thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.
