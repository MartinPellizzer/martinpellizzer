Geranium sylvaticum var. ferrugineum

Geranium sylvaticum var. ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in woodland and shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with heart-shaped leaves that have a distinctive reddish or rust-colored underside. Its flowers are pink to red, clustered in umbels, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
