Glinus oppositifolius var. glomeratus

Glinus oppositifolius var. glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered glinus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
