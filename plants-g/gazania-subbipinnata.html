Gazania subbipinnata

Gazania subbipinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gazania, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Gazania subbipinnata is known for its drought tolerance and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
