Gladiolus suaveolens

Gladiolus suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, colorful flowers. The plant produces large, trumpet-shaped blooms that come in a variety of hues, including red, pink, and white. Its fragrant flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
