Gladiolus ochroleucus

Gladiolus ochroleucus, commonly known as the white-veined gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers with white petals and yellow-orange veins. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

The botanical interest of this species lies in its distinctive coloration and its role in horticultural diversity within the Gladiolus genus.
