Gluta megalocarpa

Gluta megalocarpa, commonly known as the giant gluta, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in a branched inflorescence, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its bark is thick and rough, often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
