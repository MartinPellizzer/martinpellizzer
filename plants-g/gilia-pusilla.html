Gilia pusilla

Gilia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf gilia, is a small, annual flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as gravelly soils and open areas. The plant produces delicate, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur. Its foliage consists of small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Gilia pusilla is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
