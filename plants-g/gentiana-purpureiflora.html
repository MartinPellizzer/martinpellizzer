Gentiana purpureiflora

Gentiana purpureiflora, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

Gentiana purpureiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
