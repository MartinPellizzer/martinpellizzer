Gentianella gunniana

Gentianella gunniana, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and the stems are often hairy.

Gentianella gunniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
