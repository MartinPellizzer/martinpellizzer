Gentiana propinqua var. aleutica

Gentiana propinqua var. aleutica, commonly known as the Aleutian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Aleutian Islands.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in coastal environments. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down.

This variant is closely related to other species in the Gentiana genus, sharing similar morphological features.
