Glandularia elegans var. asperata

Glandularia elegans var. asperata, commonly known as the roughleaf glandularia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its glandular leaves, which are covered in small, sticky secretory structures. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are typically hairy and can grow up to several feet in height.

This variety is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments.
