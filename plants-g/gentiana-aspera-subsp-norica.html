Gentiana aspera subsp. norica

Gentiana aspera subsp.

norica, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations above 1,500 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, and it is often found in mountainous areas with cool, moist conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
