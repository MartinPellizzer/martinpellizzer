Gnaphalium × ambiguum

Gnaphalium × ambiguum, commonly known as the hybrid groundsel, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow central disks and white to pale pink ray florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
