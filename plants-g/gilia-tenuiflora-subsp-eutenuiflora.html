Gilia tenuiflora subsp. eutenuiflora

Gilia tenuiflora subsp.

eutenuiflora, commonly known as the slenderflower gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, or red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in opposite pairs along the stem.

The species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in regions with low precipitation and high sunlight exposure.
