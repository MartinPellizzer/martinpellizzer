Gleichenia bancroftii

Gleichenia bancroftii, commonly known as the banana fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, arching fronds that resemble the shape of a banana leaf, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in dense forests. Its rhizome system allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil.

Gleichenia bancroftii is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture due to its lush, green foliage.
