Gilia humilis

Gilia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf gilia, is a low-growing annual herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It typically reaches heights of less than 10 inches and has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gilia humilis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
