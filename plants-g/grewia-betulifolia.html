Grewia betulifolia

Grewia betulifolia, commonly known as the buttonwood, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It features smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray.
