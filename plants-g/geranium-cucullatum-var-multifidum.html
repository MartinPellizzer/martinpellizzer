Geranium cucullatum var. multifidum

Geranium cucullatum var. multifidum, commonly known as the multifidum geranium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and dry landscapes due to its drought tolerance.
