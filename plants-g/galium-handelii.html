Galium handelii

Galium handelii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually less than 2 centimeters in length. Its flowers are white to pale pink, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom from June to September.

Galium handelii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
