Gymnadenia robatschiana

Gymnadenia robatschiana, commonly known as the Robatsch's grass-pink, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows and damp woodlands. It is known for its small, fragrant blooms that appear in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
