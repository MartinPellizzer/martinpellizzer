Gilia tagetina

Gilia tagetina, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces vibrant orange to yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

Gilia tagetina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
