Gontscharovia popovii

Gontscharovia popovii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and densely covered with fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Gontscharovia popovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
