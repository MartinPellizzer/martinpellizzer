Gladiolus multiflorus

Gladiolus multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, spike-like inflorescence. The plant produces numerous flowers in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow, which bloom sequentially along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Gladiolus multiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
