Grumilea lagunensis

Grumilea lagunensis, commonly known as the Laguna grass, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy or swampy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall resemblance to other grass species.

Grumilea lagunensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
