Gnaphalium chilense var. confertifolia

Gnaphalium chilense var. confertifolia, commonly known as the Chilean featherweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found in mountainous areas. Its foliage is usually grayish-green, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This variant is distinguished by its dense, compact inflorescences, which give it its specific epithet "confertifolia.".
