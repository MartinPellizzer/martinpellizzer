Gaura mutabilis f. glabra

Gaura mutabilis f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, pinkish-white to lavender-pink flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are softly hairy on the underside. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its delicate appearance and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
