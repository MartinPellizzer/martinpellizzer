Galactia latisiliqua var. chacoensis

Galactia latisiliqua var. chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco legume, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly found in the Chaco region of Paraguay and Argentina.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, fibrous pods and are often dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area.
