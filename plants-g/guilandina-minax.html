Guilandina minax

Guilandina minax, commonly known as the giant climbing bean, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, woody stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces long, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests or along riverbanks.

Guilandina minax is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
