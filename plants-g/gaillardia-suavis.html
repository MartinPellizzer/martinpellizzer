Gaillardia suavis

Gaillardia suavis, commonly known as the sweet bluemouth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers with red and yellow petals. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Gaillardia suavis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
