Glyptopetalum acuminatissimum

Glyptopetalum acuminatissimum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Glyptopetalum acuminatissimum is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
