Gasteria brachyphylla var. bayeri

Gasteria brachyphylla var. bayeri, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces striking, upward-facing flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
