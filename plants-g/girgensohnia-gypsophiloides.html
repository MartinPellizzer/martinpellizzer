Girgensohnia gypsophiloides

Girgensohnia gypsophiloides, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent growth habit and produces showy clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically fleshy and grayish-green, aiding in water retention.

Girgensohnia gypsophiloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
