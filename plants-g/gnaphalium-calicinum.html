Gnaphalium calicinum

Gnaphalium calicinum, commonly known as the rock everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of many small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and delicate appearance.
