Guzmania kressii

Guzmania kressii, commonly known as the Kress's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that can range in color from red to orange, often resembling a bottlebrush. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, forming a central pool that supports various microorganisms. Its foliage is typically a deep green, with some varieties displaying variegated patterns.

Guzmania kressii thrives in high humidity and bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
