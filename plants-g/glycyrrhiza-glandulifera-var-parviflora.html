Glycyrrhiza glandulifera var. parviflora

Glycyrrhiza glandulifera var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered licorice, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
