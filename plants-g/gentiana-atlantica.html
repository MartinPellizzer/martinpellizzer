Gentiana atlantica

Gentiana atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic gentian, is a perennial herb native to the western Mediterranean region.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid color and ecological benefits.
