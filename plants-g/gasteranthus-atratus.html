Gasteranthus atratus

Gasteranthus atratus, commonly known as the black-eyed gasteranthus, is a striking member of the Rubiaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, urn-shaped flowers that are predominantly black with a single white spot, resembling an eye. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in botanical gardens.

Gasteranthus atratus is known for its short blooming period, typically in the spring, adding a rare and dramatic visual element to its habitat.
