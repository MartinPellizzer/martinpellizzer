Galium × aberrans

Galium × aberrans, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are often heart-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in disturbed soils and gardens.
