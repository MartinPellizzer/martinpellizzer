Goeppertia hylaeanthoides

Goeppertia hylaeanthoides, commonly known as the Emerald Queen, is a strikingly beautiful bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its broad, dark green, waxy leaves that form a rosette shape, creating a water-holding cup at the center. The plant produces strikingly colorful inflorescences with red, orange, and yellow bracts, which can last for several months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
