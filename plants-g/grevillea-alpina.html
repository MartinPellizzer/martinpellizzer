Grevillea alpina

Grevillea alpina, commonly known as the alpine grevillea, is a shrub native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia.

It features striking, feather-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in clusters, which are particularly showy in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
