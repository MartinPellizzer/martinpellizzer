Glycosmis ferruginea

Glycosmis ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty glycosmis, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a rusty or reddish pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by edible, orange-colored fruits. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and pharmacological research.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
