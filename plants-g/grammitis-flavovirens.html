Grammitis flavovirens

Grammitis flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green fern, is a species of epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically light green to yellow-green in color. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or branches in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are small, with a distinctive venation pattern and a thin, papery texture.

Grammitis flavovirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
