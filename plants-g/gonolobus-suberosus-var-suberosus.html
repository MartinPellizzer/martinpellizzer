Gonolobus suberosus var. suberosus

Gonolobus suberosus var. suberosus, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree with woody stems and compound leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its milky sap is toxic and can cause skin irritation, making it important to handle with care.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought conditions.
