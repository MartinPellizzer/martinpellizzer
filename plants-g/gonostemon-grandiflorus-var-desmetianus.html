Gonostemon grandiflorus var. desmetianus

Gonostemon grandiflorus var. desmetianus, commonly known as the Desert Ghostflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves adapted to store water, enabling survival in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the cooler months, often in the evening. Its growth is typically low and spreading, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
