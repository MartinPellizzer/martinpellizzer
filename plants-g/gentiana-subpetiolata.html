Gentiana subpetiolata

Gentiana subpetiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a petiole, giving it its scientific name.

Gentiana subpetiolata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors.
