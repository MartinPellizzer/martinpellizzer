Gigantochloa scortechinii var. albovestita

Gigantochloa scortechinii var. albovestita, commonly known as the white-haired bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are long and narrow, with a distinctive white or silver-colored sheath that gives it its common name. This plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique aesthetic features.
