Gymnadenia galeandra

Gymnadenia galeandra, commonly known as the white gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in moist meadows and grasslands. The plant features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of showy, white or pale blue flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gymnadenia galeandra is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant blooms.
