Gentiana sedifolia var. casapaltensis

Gentiana sedifolia var. casapaltensis, commonly known as the Casapaltensis gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are generally basal, with a distinctive heart-shaped form and a hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
