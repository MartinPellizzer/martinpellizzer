Geranium pavonianum

Geranium pavonianum, commonly known as the pavonian geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces bright red or pink blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually palmately lobed, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Geranium pavonianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
