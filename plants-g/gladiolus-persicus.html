Gladiolus persicus

Gladiolus persicus, commonly known as the Persian gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the regions of Persia, now Iran.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white, and are often used in ornamental gardening.

The botanical features of Gladiolus persicus make it a striking addition to gardens and floral arrangements.
