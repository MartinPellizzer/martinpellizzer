Gastrolychnis macrosperma

Gastrolychnis macrosperma, commonly known as the large-seeded legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with compound leaves and produces bright yellow or orange flowers.

Its seeds are encased in thick, leathery pods, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
