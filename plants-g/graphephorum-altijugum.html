Graphephorum altijugum

Graphephorum altijugum, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, enhancing nutrient absorption.

The species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
