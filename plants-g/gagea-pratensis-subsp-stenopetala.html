Gagea pratensis subsp. stenopetala

Gagea pratensis subsp.

stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled spring beauty, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a loose cluster, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It is characterized by its compact stature and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
