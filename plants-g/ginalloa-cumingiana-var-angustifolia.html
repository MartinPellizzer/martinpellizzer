Ginalloa cumingiana var. angustifolia

Ginalloa cumingiana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ginalloa, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its botanical features include a woody stem and small, elliptical fruits that contain seeds.

It is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
