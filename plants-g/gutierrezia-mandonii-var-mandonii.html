Gutierrezia mandonii var. mandonii

Gutierrezia mandonii var. mandonii, commonly known as the desert gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and fibrous, aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in desert ecosystems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in xeriscaping.
