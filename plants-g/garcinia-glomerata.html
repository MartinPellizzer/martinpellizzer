Garcinia glomerata

Garcinia glomerata, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when ripe. These fruits contain a single seed enclosed in a fleshy aril, which is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Garcinia glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
