Geranium ocellatum var. sublaeve

Geranium ocellatum var. sublaeve, commonly known as the velvetleaf geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features striking, palmate leaves with a velvety texture and a deep green color, often adorned with distinctive white or pale veins. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a versatile addition to both container and in-ground landscapes.
