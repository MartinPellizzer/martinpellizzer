Geranium betulinum

Geranium betulinum, commonly known as the birchleaf geranium, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely cut, birch-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Geranium betulinum prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
