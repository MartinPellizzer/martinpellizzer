Gaillonia pulchella

Gaillonia pulchella, commonly known as the pretty bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Gaillonia pulchella is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
