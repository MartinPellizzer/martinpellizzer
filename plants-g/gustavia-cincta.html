Gustavia cincta

Gustavia cincta, commonly known as the scarlet moringa, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Gustavia cincta thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
