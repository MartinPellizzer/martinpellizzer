Gladiolus cruentus

Gladiolus cruentus, commonly known as the blood gladiolus, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and produces showy, terminal inflorescences of brightly colored flowers, typically in red, pink, or purple hues. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, tuberous roots and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, requiring moderate water during its growing season.

Due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
