Glycine unifoliolata

Glycine unifoliolata, commonly known as the single-leaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of a single leaflet, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. It is often grown for its nutritional value, as the seeds are rich in protein and essential nutrients.

Glycine unifoliolata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable agriculture.
