Gonocalyx amplexicaulis

Gonocalyx amplexicaulis, commonly known as the climbing hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has woody stems that twine around support structures, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
