Galium pumilum var. rectum

Galium pumilum var. rectum, commonly known as the narrowleaf bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy surface.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.
