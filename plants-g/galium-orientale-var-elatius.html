Galium orientale var. elatius

Galium orientale var. elatius, commonly known as the tall bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small white or pale pink flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its fragrant foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
