Glycosmis parkeri

Glycosmis parkeri, commonly known as the African starfruit or wild starfruit, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that resemble starfruits, which are edible and have a tangy, slightly acidic flavor. Glycosmis parkeri is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
