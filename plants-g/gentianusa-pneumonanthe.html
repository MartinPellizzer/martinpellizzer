Gentianusa pneumonanthe

Gentianusa pneumonanthe, commonly known as the lungwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with distinctive purple or greenish-white spots. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Gentianusa pneumonanthe is often used in traditional medicine for its purported respiratory benefits.
