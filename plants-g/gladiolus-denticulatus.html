Gladiolus denticulatus

Gladiolus denticulatus, commonly known as the toothed gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often toothed or serrated. Its flowers, which bloom in summer, come in a range of colors including red, pink, and white, and are arranged in spikes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
