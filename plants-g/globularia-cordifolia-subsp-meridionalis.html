Globularia cordifolia subsp. meridionalis

Globularia cordifolia subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the southern heartleaf globularia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and evergreen foliage with heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in mild climates.
