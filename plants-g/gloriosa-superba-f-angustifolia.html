Gloriosa superba f. angustifolia

Gloriosa superba f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flame vine, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from orange to red. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, giving the plant its specific epithet "angustifolia.". This cultivar is prized for its showy blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
