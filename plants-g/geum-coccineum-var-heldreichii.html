Geum coccineum var. heldreichii

Geum coccineum var. heldreichii, commonly known as the Heldreich's fireweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
