Gamanthera

Gamanthera, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the remote highlands of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that appear to shimmer in the sunlight, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom only during the night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to conserve moisture in its arid habitat.

Due to its rarity and unique botanical features, gamanthera is of great interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
