Geranium cataractarum subsp. cossonii

Geranium cataractarum subsp.

cossonii, commonly known as the Cosson's geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
