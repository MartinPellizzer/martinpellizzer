Gigantochloa pubipetiolata

Gigantochloa pubipetiolata, commonly known as the hairy-petioled giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves with hairy bases, which give it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This bamboo species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional construction and handicrafts.
