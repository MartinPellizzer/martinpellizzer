Gustavia verticillata

Gustavia verticillata, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing along the edges of forests or in sandy soils.

Gustavia verticillata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
