Galega esculenta

Galega esculenta, commonly known as sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often have a slight serration along the edges. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Galega esculenta is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.
