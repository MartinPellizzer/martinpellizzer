Gypsophila bitlisensis

Gypsophila bitlisensis, commonly known as the Bitlis carnation, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms during the summer months.

Gypsophila bitlisensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
