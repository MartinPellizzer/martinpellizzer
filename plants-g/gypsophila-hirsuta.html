Gypsophila hirsuta

Gypsophila hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fluffy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Gypsophila hirsuta is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
