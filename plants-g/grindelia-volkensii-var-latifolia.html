Grindelia volkensii var. latifolia

Grindelia volkensii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf gumweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
