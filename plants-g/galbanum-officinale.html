Galbanum officinale

Galbanum officinale, commonly known as wild galbanum, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces a distinctive yellowish to greenish resin, which is often used in traditional medicine and as a fragrance in perfumery. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in early summer, with small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences.

The resin of Galbanum officinale is known for its aromatic properties and has been used for centuries in various cultural and medicinal practices.
