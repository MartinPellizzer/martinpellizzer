Gypsophila antari

Gypsophila antari, commonly known as the fairy duster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fluffy blooms that resemble miniature clouds. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and they bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Gypsophila antari is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
