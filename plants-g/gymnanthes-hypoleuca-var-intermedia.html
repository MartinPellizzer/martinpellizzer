Gymnanthes hypoleuca var. intermedia

Gymnanthes hypoleuca var. intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican bitter apple, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often spiny stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is known for its latex, which is toxic and used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions with well-drained soil and partial to full sun.
