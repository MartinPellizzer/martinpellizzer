Gentiana bavarica subsp. subacaulis

Gentiana bavarica subsp.

subacaulis, commonly known as the Bavarian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The subspecies subacaulis is distinguished by its shorter, more compact growth habit compared to the typical form. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and forest edges.

The plant's morphology includes lance-shaped leaves and a single terminal flower, making it a visually distinctive species within its habitat.
