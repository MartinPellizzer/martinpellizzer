Guzmania hitchcockiana

Guzmania hitchcockiana, commonly known as the Hitchcock's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that can range in color from red to orange. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, forming a central pool that supports small aquatic life. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns and can vary in hue depending on environmental conditions.

Guzmania hitchcockiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in indirect light with minimal care.
