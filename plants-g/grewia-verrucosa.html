Grewia verrucosa

Grewia verrucosa, commonly known as the rough globe-thistle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, oval leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. Its fruit is a spherical capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Grewia verrucosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.
