Gomphrena canescens subsp. erythrina

Gomphrena canescens subsp.

erythrina, commonly known as the red gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its striking, spiky inflorescences that range in color from pink to red.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often mistaken for leaves, a feature known as pseudanthium. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Gomphrena canescens subsp.

erythrina is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
