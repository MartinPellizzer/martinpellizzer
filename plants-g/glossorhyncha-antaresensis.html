Glossorhyncha antaresensis

Glossorhyncha antaresensis, commonly known as the Antares ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pale green stems and delicate, translucent flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant's unique morphology includes a specialized root system adapted for nutrient absorption in its shaded, humid habitat. It blooms infrequently, typically during the wet season, producing flowers with a subtle, otherworldly fragrance.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, Glossorhyncha antaresensis is considered a conservation concern.
