Goldfussia myrtinia

Goldfussia myrtinia, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Goldfussia myrtinia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine.
