Geranium emarginatum

Geranium emarginatum, commonly known as the trailing geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with notched edges, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Geranium emarginatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
