Grossularia inermis f. pubescens

Grossularia inermis f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent gooseberry, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, yellowish-green berries that are typically covered in a fine, pubescent coating, giving it its varietal name. The plant has elliptical leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild flavor, making it suitable for both garden cultivation and culinary use.
