Grevillea acanthifolia subsp. acanthifolia

Grevillea acanthifolia subsp.

acanthifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in clusters, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in native plantings.
