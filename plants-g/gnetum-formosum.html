Gnetum formosum

Gnetum formosum, commonly known as the Taiwan gnetum, is a gymnosperm native to Southeast Asia.

It is a woody plant characterized by its compound leaves and unique reproductive structures. The plant produces both male and female cones, which are typically borne on different parts of the plant. Its leaves are often alternate and have a leathery texture, adapting to its tropical and subtropical habitats.

Gnetum formosum plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
