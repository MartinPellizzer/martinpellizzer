Gentianopsis detonsa subsp. nesophila

Gentianopsis detonsa subsp.

nesophila, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in mountain ecosystems and its aesthetic value in botanical gardens.
