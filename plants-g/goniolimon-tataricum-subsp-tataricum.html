Goniolimon tataricum subsp. tataricum

Goniolimon tataricum subsp.

tataricum, commonly known as Tatarian stonevetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its small, stipitate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color.

This species is adapted to grow in dry, sandy or rocky soils, often found in steppe and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native range.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental potential in dry garden settings.
