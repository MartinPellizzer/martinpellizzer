Gentianella decumbens

Gentianella decumbens, commonly known as the creeping gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features a low-growing, sprawling habit with dark green, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, with five petals and a distinctive tubular shape.

Gentianella decumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
