Glycine gracei

Glycine gracei, commonly known as the graceful soybean vine, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to twine around supports. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy pods and are used in traditional medicine.

Glycine gracei is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing capabilities in agricultural settings.
