Galium × pomariorum

Galium × pomariorum, commonly known as the common bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but have a mild, somewhat tart flavor. Galium × pomariorum typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
