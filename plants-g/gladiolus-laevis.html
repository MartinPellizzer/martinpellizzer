Gladiolus laevis

Gladiolus laevis, commonly known as the smooth gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with brightly colored, often pink or red, flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and arranged in a fan-like pattern along the stem.

The smooth gladiolus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
