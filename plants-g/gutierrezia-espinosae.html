Gutierrezia espinosae

Gutierrezia espinosae, commonly known as the Espinoza's goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and adapted root systems. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
