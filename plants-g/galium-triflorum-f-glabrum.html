Galium triflorum f. glabrum

Galium triflorum f. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves distinguish it from other varieties of Galium.

The species is characterized by its clustered inflorescences and is often found in the understory of forests.
