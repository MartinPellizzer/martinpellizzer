Geranium melananthum

Geranium melananthum, commonly known as the black geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which contrast with its heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Geranium melananthum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
