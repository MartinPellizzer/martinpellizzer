Guzmania testudinis var. testudinis

Guzmania testudinis var. testudinis, commonly known as the shield fern.

It is a species of bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. Its foliage is often a deep green with a waxy texture, and it produces striking inflorescences in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.
