Gentiana gayi

Gentiana gayi, commonly known as the Sierra gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Gentiana gayi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
