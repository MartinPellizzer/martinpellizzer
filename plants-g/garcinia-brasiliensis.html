Garcinia brasiliensis

Garcinia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian yellow fruit or yellow graviola, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. These fruits contain a single large seed and have a tart, acidic flavor.

Garcinia brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
