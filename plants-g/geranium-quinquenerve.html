Geranium quinquenerve

Geranium quinquenerve, commonly known as the five-veined geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with five distinct veins, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
