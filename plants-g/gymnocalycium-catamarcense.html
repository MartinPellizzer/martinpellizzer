Gymnocalycium catamarcense

Gymnocalycium catamarcense, commonly known as the Catamarca cactus, is a species native to Argentina.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed stem and typically produces yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its dense, white spines and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its slow growth and drought tolerance.

Gymnocalycium catamarcense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience.
