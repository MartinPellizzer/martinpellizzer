Gentiana amarella var. praecox

Gentiana amarella var. praecox, commonly known as the early gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers bloom early in the growing season, usually in late spring, making it a notable wildflower in its habitat.
