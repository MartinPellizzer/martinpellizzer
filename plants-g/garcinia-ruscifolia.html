Garcinia ruscifolia

Garcinia ruscifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing a single seed. Garcinia ruscifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and visually appealing plant in tropical gardens.
