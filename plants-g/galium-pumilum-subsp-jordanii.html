Galium pumilum subsp. jordanii

Galium pumilum subsp.

jordanii, commonly known as Jordan's bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often covered with fine hairs.

It is adapted to dry soils and can thrive in rocky or sandy habitats.
