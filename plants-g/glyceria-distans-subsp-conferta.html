Glyceria distans subsp. conferta

Glyceria distans subsp.

conferta, commonly known as the dense glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered together, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
