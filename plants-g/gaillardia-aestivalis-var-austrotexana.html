Gaillardia aestivalis var. austrotexana

Gaillardia aestivalis var. austrotexana, commonly known as the Texas blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its bright, daisy-like flowers that range from red to orange, with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial in USDA hardiness zones 6 through 10. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The Texas blanketflower is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
