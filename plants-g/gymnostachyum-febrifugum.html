Gymnostachyum febrifugum

Gymnostachyum febrifugum, commonly known as the feverfew plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect stem and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the dry season. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Gymnostachyum febrifugum is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional uses for treating fevers and inflammatory conditions.
