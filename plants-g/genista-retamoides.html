Genista retamoides

Genista retamoides, commonly known as the Portuguese broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a shrub belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring season. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soils.
