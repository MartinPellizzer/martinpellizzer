Galeobdolon luteum var. albomaculatum

Galeobdolon luteum var. albomaculatum, commonly known as the yellow gentian with white spots, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with distinctive white maculations. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine pubescence.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
