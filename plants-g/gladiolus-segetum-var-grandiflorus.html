Gladiolus segetum var. grandiflorus

Gladiolus segetum var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the giant wheatgrass gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, supporting large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are often used in floral arrangements due to their elegance and durability. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral displays.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
