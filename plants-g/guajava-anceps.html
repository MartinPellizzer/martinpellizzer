Guajava anceps

Guajava anceps, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and highly prized for their sweet, tangy flavor. The fruit is rich in antioxidants and is often used in jams, desserts, and beverages.

Its ornamental value and culinary uses make it a popular plant in horticulture and agriculture.
