Gymnocactus knuthianus

Gymnocactus knuthianus, commonly known as the Knuth's naked cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its lack of spines, which is a distinguishing feature among the Gymnocactus genus. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its stems are cylindrical and often segmented, with a ribbed structure that helps reduce water loss.

Gymnocactus knuthianus is adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
