Gypsophila macedonica

Gypsophila macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian laceflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, feathery flowers that resemble lace. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Gypsophila macedonica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes and floral arrangements.
