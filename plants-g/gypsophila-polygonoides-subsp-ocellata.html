Gypsophila polygonoides subsp. ocellata

Gypsophila polygonoides subsp.

ocellata, commonly known as the spotted gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like foliage and clusters of small, white or pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for alpine environments. Its ornamental value is often appreciated in gardens for its ethereal appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

The subspecies ocellata is distinguished by the presence of small, colored spots on its petals, which add to its visual appeal.
