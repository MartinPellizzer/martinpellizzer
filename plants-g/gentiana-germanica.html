Gentiana germanica

Gentiana germanica, commonly known as the German gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The flowers are tubular with two lips, and they attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
