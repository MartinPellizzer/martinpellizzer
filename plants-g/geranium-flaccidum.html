Geranium flaccidum

Geranium flaccidum, commonly known as the drooping geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that have a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
