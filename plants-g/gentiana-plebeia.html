Gentiana plebeia

Gentiana plebeia, commonly known as the common gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, while its flowers are clustered in racemose inflorescences.

Gentiana plebeia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
