Gentiana axillaris

Gentiana axillaris, commonly known as the lesser gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
