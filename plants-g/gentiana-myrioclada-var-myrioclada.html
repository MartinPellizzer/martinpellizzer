Gentiana myrioclada var. myrioclada

Gentiana myrioclada var. myrioclada, commonly known as the many-flowered gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
