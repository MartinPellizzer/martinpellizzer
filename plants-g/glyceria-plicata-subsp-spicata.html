Glyceria plicata subsp. spicata

Glyceria plicata subsp.

spicata, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of spikelets that give it its common name. Its inflorescence is cylindrical and often appears as a spike, which is characteristic of the genus Glyceria.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
