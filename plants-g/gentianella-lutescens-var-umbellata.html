Gentianella lutescens var. umbellata

Gentianella lutescens var. umbellata, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are arranged in a loose cluster.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
