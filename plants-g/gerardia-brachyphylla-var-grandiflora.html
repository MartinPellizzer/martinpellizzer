Gerardia brachyphylla var. grandiflora

Gerardia brachyphylla var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora gerardia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes. Its foliage is dense and evergreen, providing year-round interest.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
