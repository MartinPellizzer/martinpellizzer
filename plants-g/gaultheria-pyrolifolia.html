Gaultheria pyrolifolia

Gaultheria pyrolifolia, commonly known as the white alpine strawberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves and produces delicate white or pinkish flowers in early spring. The plant is notable for its edible berries, which are sweet and aromatic, often used in local cuisines. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests and alpine meadows.

Gaultheria pyrolifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
