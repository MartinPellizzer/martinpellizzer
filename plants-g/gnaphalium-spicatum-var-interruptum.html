Gnaphalium spicatum var. interruptum

Gnaphalium spicatum var. interruptum, commonly known as the interrupted feather grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small yellow flowers, which are often surrounded by white or pale green bracts.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
