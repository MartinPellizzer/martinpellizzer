Grindelia arizonica var. microphylla

Grindelia arizonica var. microphylla, commonly known as Arizona gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats. Its small, narrow leaves are adapted to reduce water loss, and it produces a distinctive, resinous odor.
