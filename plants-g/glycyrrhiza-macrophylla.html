Glycyrrhiza macrophylla

Glycyrrhiza macrophylla, commonly known as licorice, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its deep taproot and pairs of opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that develop into elongated, woody pods containing several seeds. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

Glycyrrhiza macrophylla contains compounds like glycyrrhizin, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
