Gentiana hexaphylla var. pentaphylla

Gentiana hexaphylla var. pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually arranged in a whorled pattern, often with five to seven leaflets.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
