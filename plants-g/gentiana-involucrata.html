Gentiana involucrata

Gentiana involucrata, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and bloom in late summer. The plant grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often in rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Gentiana involucrata is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
