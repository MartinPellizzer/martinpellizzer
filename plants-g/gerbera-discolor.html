Gerbera discolor

Gerbera discolor, commonly known as the variable gerbera, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white to yellow petals and a central disk, often displaying color variations. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce multiple blooms throughout the growing season.
