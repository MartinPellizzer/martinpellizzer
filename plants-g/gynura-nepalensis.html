Gynura nepalensis

Gynura nepalensis, commonly known as the Himalayan velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, which contribute to its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces small, oblong fruits.

Gynura nepalensis is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
