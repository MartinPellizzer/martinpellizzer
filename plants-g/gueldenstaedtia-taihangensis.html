Gueldenstaedtia taihangensis

Gueldenstaedtia taihangensis, commonly known as the Taihang mountain vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Taihang Mountains in China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in dry and sunny environments. Its seeds are enclosed in pods, and it plays a role in soil stabilization due to its extensive root system.

This species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its unique adaptations to its mountainous habitat.
