Gnaphalium folliculatum

Gnaphalium folliculatum, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically features small, narrow leaves and produces tiny, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to release a fine, powdery substance from its leaves.

This plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
