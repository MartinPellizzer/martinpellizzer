Geissorhiza rosea

Geissorhiza rosea, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and strap-like, grassy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
