Glycine discolor

Glycine discolor, commonly known as the pink bean, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Glycine discolor thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
