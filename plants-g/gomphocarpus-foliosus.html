Gomphocarpus foliosus

Gomphocarpus foliosus, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spine-like seed pods that form along the stem, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Gomphocarpus foliosus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
