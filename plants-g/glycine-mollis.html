Glycine mollis

Glycine mollis, commonly known as velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. Glycine mollis is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.

It also has traditional uses in herbal medicine and as a source of protein-rich seeds.
