Gomphrena rosea

Gomphrena rosea, commonly known as the rosemallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, bract-based flower heads that range in color from pink to red, giving it a rose-like appearance. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Gomphrena rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
