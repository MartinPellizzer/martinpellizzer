Galactia pyrenea

Galactia pyrenea, commonly known as the flame flower or red milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal. Galactia pyrenea is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
