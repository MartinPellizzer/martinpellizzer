Gnaphalium capitellatum

Gnaphalium capitellatum, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, fluffy white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, compact clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
