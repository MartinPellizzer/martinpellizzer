Gardenia annae var. moramballae

Gardenia annae var. moramballae, commonly known as the Moramballae gardenia, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, waxy leaves that are dark green in color. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a strong, sweet fragrance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is highly sought after by horticulturists due to its ornamental value and unique appearance.
