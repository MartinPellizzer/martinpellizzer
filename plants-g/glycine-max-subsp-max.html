Glycine max subsp. max

Glycine max subsp.

max, commonly known as soybean, is an annual legume native to East Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds.

The plant is widely cultivated for its high-protein seeds, which are used in food, animal feed, and industrial products. Its root system hosts nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility. Glycine max subsp.

max is a key crop in global agriculture due to its nutritional value and adaptability to various climates.
