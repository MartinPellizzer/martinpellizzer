Gnaphalium pedunculatum

Gnaphalium pedunculatum, commonly known as the slender cudweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow to reddish in color. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale yellow seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread across various habitats. It thrives in moist, open areas such as meadows and wetlands, often forming dense mats.

Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its often damp environments.
