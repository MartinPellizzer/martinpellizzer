Glossopappus chrysanthemoides

Glossopappus chrysanthemoides, commonly known as the yellow ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and pappus structures that aid in seed dispersal. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats. It has a bushy appearance with hairy leaves and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional uses.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
