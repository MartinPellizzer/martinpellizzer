Gaultheria paramicola

Gaultheria paramicola, commonly known as the Andean wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves contain aromatic compounds, including methyl salicylate, which gives it a distinctive minty scent.

Gaultheria paramicola is often found in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates and well-drained soils.
