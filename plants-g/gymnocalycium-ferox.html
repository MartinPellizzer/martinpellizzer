Gymnocalycium ferox

Gymnocalycium ferox, commonly known as the feral cactus or the fierce cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by small, edible fruits. Gymnocalycium ferox is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions with minimal water requirements.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a striking addition to arid landscapes.
