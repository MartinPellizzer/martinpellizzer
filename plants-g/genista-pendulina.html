Genista pendulina

Genista pendulina, commonly known as the hanging broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, pendulous stems that dangle from its woody base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista pendulina is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
