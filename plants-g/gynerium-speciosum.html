Gynerium speciosum

Gynerium speciosum, commonly known as the giant waterweed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can grow up to 10 feet in length, often floating on the water's surface. The plant produces small, green flowers in clusters, which are typically found near the water's edge. Gynerium speciosum thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes.

Its dense growth helps stabilize sediments and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
