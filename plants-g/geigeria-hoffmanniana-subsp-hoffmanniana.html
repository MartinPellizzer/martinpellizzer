Geigeria hoffmanniana subsp. hoffmanniana

Geigeria hoffmanniana subsp.

hoffmanniana, commonly known as Hoffmann's geigeria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in full sun.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in terminal spikes, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively low maintenance in suitable climates.
