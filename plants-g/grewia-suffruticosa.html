Grewia suffruticosa

Grewia suffruticosa, commonly known as the sour fig or red gum, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Grewia suffruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
