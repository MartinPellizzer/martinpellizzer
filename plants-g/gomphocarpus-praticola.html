Gomphocarpus praticola

Gomphocarpus praticola, commonly known as the yellow daisy or African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a central brown disk, which bloom in spring and summer. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Gomphocarpus praticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
