Grevillea heterophylla

Grevillea heterophylla, commonly known as the variable grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes, ranging from narrow and linear to broad and elliptical, which vary along the length of the stems. The plant produces striking, cone-shaped inflorescences that bear bright red or orange flowers, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Grevillea heterophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens across Australia.
