Gigantochloa sinuata

Gigantochloa sinuata, commonly known as the Borneo bamboo, is a tall, slender grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strong, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves that are often coated with a waxy layer. This plant is adapted to thrive in humid tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks. Its rapid growth rate makes it an important species for ecological restoration and sustainable forestry.

The plant's structural strength and resilience make it valuable for both ornamental and practical uses.
