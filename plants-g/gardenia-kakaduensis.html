Gardenia kakaduensis

Gardenia kakaduensis, commonly known as the Kakadu gardenia, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The fruit it produces is a red, fleshy berry that contains seeds, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
