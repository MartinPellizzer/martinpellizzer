Gentianella gilioides

Gentianella gilioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Gentianella gilioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
