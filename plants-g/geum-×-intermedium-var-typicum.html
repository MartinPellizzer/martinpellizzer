Geum × intermedium var. typicum

Geum × intermedium var. typicum, commonly known as the Indian blanket flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom from late spring to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
