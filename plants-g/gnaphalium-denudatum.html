Gnaphalium denudatum

Gnaphalium denudatum, commonly known as the naked false heather, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine, silky hairs.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage.
