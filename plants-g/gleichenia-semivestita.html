Gleichenia semivestita

Gleichenia semivestita, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety fronds that give it a distinctive texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Gleichenia semivestita is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in woodland ecosystems.
