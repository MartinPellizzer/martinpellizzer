Gastrolychnis tenella

Gastrolychnis tenella, commonly known as the slender nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Gastrolychnis tenella is known for its nocturnal blooming habit, attracting pollinators such as moths and bats.
