Grewia cana

Grewia cana, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature, attracting various birds and mammals.

Grewia cana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in regions with limited rainfall.
