Galium album subsp. pycnotrichum

Galium album subsp.

pycnotrichum, commonly known as white bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
