Grevillea pilosa subsp. pilosa

Grevillea pilosa subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as the hairy grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures.

The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often used in native landscaping and conservation efforts.
