Grevillea amplexans subsp. semivestita

Grevillea amplexans subsp.

semivestita, commonly known as the silky grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance.

The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Grevillea genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and floral display.
