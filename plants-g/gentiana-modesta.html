Gentiana modesta

Gentiana modesta, commonly known as the modest gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched and upright.

Gentiana modesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
