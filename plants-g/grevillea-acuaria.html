Grevillea acuaria

Grevillea acuaria, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and bright red or orange inflorescences that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. It is often found in coastal heathlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Grevillea acuaria is also an important component of the local ecosystem, providing food and habitat for native wildlife.
