Geranium columbianum

Geranium columbianum, commonly known as the trailing geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Geranium columbianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
