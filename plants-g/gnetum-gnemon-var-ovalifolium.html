Gnetum gnemon var. ovalifolium

Gnetum gnemon var. ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaf sandarac tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Gnetophyta, a group of gymnosperms that includes only three genera. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It is often found in lowland tropical rainforests and is valued for its resin, which has been used historically in various industrial applications.

The species also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
