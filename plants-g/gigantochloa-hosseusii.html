Gigantochloa hosseusii

Gigantochloa hosseusii, commonly known as the giant bamboo of Borneo, is a tall, clumping grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its rapid growth and structural strength, it is valued for both ornamental and practical uses.
