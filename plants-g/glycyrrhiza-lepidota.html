Glycyrrhiza lepidota

Glycyrrhiza lepidota, commonly known as the Mexican licorice, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its roots are the primary part used, known for their sweet taste and medicinal properties.

Glycyrrhiza lepidota is often cultivated for its aromatic and therapeutic uses in traditional medicine.
