Gomphocarpus fruticosus var. purpureus

Gomphocarpus fruticosus var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces capsule fruits that contain seeds with silky hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation areas.
