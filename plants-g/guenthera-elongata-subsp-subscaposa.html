Guenthera elongata subsp. subscaposa

Guenthera elongata subsp.

subscaposa, commonly known as the slender guenthera, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is native to parts of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
