Gypsophila × castellana

Gypsophila × castellana, commonly known as the baby's breath or sweet William, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery flowers that range in color from white to pink and lavender. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces clusters of small, star-shaped blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and floral arrangements.

Its botanical name reflects its hybrid origin, combining species from different genera within the Caryophyllaceae family.
