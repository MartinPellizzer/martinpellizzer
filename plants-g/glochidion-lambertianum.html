Glochidion lambertianum

Glochidion lambertianum, commonly known as Lambert's glabrous acanthus, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glabrous or nearly so. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Glochidion lambertianum is valued for its ornamental foliage and is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
