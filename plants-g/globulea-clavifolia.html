Globulea clavifolia

Globulea clavifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved globularia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, globular inflorescences that consist of numerous tiny, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its drought tolerance.

Globulea clavifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
