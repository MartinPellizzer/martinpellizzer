Garnotia panicoides var. puberula

Garnotia panicoides var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a paniculate cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
