Gentiana campestris var. germanica

Gentiana campestris var. germanica, commonly known as the German gentian, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
