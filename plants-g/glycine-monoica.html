Glycine monoica

Glycine monoica, commonly known as the wild soybean, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods that contain small, round seeds. Glycine monoica is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
