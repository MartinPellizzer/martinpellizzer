Gomphia alaternifolia

Gomphia alaternifolia, commonly known as the white sand pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of small white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland fynbos regions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

Gomphia alaternifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
