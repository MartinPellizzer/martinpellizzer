Genista cinerea subsp. ramosissima

Genista cinerea subsp.

ramosissima, commonly known as silverbush broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and slender, branching stems.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for garden landscaping.
