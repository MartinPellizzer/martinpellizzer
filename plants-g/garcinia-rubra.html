Garcinia rubra

Garcinia rubra, commonly known as the red garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. Garcinia rubra thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ornamental horticulture and traditional medicine.
