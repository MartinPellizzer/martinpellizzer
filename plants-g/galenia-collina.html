Galenia collina

Galenia collina, commonly known as the narrowleaf galena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer, and its leaves are narrow and gray-green in color.

Galenia collina is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
