Glycosmis ovoidea

Glycosmis ovoidea, commonly known as the eggplant citrus or the eggfruit, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its fragrant, compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to oval-shaped, yellow-orange fruits. The fruit is edible, with a tart, citrus-like flavor, and is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
