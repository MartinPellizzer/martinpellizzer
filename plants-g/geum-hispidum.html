Geum hispidum

Geum hispidum, commonly known as rough avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in stiff, bristly hairs. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Geum hispidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
