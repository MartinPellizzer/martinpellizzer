Galium sylvestre var. pumilum

Galium sylvestre var. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf woodcrushers, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the United States.

It typically features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and usually have a slightly notched or toothed margin.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
