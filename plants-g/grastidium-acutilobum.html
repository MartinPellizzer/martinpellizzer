Grastidium acutilobum

Grastidium acutilobum, commonly known as the sharp-lobed grastidium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features lanceolate leaves with acute lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Grastidium acutilobum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.
