Gymnema macranthum

Gymnema macranthum, commonly known as the large-leaved gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by follicular fruits containing numerous seeds. Gymnema macranthum is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its traditional medicinal uses.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
