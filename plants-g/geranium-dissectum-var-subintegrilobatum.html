Geranium dissectum var. subintegrilobatum

Geranium dissectum var. subintegrilobatum, commonly known as the dissected geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features distinctive, finely cut leaves that give it a lacy appearance, and its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
