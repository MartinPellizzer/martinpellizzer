Gymnocalycium bruchii subsp. carolinense

Gymnocalycium bruchii subsp.

carolinense, commonly known as the Carolina moon cactus, is a succulent cactus native to the southeastern United States. It features a short, cylindrical stem with a single, large, globular flower that blooms in spring.

The plant has spiny areoles and produces small, white to pale pink flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
