Genistella tridentata var. stenoptera

Genistella tridentata var. stenoptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and trifoliate leaves with three narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
