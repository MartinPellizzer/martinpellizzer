Grevillea jephcottii

Grevillea jephcottii, commonly known as the Jephcott's glory of the snow, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea jephcottii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
