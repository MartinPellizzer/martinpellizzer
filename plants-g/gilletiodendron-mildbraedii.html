Gilletiodendron mildbraedii

Gilletiodendron mildbraedii, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with numerous small leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing the edible Brazil nuts. It thrives in tropical rainforest environments and requires a humid climate with well-drained soil.

The plant plays a significant ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
