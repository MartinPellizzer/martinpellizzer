Geranium asiaticum var. dissectum

Geranium asiaticum var. dissectum, commonly known as the dissected geranium, is a striking ornamental plant native to regions of Asia.

It features delicate, lobed leaves that are finely dissected, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and containers.

Its compact growth habit and long bloom period make it a popular choice for adding color and texture to landscapes.
