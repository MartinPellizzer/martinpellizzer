Geranium cicutarium var. chaerophyllum

Geranium cicutarium var. chaerophyllum, commonly known as the lesser geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a distinct reddish or purplish underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
