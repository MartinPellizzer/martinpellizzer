Gypsophila parviflora

Gypsophila parviflora, commonly known as the baby's breath or poor man's lace, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, small white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters on slender stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Gypsophila parviflora is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
