Gladiolus boucheanus

Gladiolus boucheanus, commonly known as the Cape gold gladiolus, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring strap-like leaves and a robust rhizomatous root system. Its blooms are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and are also appreciated for their ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The plant is well-suited to full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
