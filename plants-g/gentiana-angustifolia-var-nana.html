Gentiana angustifolia var. nana

Gentiana angustifolia var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are closely packed along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
