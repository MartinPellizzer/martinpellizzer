Galanthus nivalis subsp. plicatus

Galanthus nivalis subsp.

plicatus, commonly known as the folded snowdrop, is a subspecies of the snowdrop genus native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, folded petals that give it its common name, and it typically blooms in early spring.

The plant produces a single, upright stem with two to three white, bell-shaped flowers. It prefers cool, moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
