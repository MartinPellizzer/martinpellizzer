Geum × cortlandicum

Geum × cortlandicum, commonly known as New England aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
