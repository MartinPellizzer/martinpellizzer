Gentiana leroyana

Gentiana leroyana, commonly known as the Leroy's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often hairy and erect.

Gentiana leroyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
