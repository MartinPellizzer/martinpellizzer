Guyonia gracilis

Guyonia gracilis, commonly known as the slender lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and its clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowering period usually occurs in the spring or early summer.

Guyonia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
