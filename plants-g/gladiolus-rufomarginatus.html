Gladiolus rufomarginatus

Gladiolus rufomarginatus, commonly known as the red-edged gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, pink, and white. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a distinctive red margin that gives it its common name.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as cut flowers.
