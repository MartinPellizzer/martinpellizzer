Geranium zonale

Geranium zonale, commonly known as the zonal geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that have distinctive reddish or purplish margins. The plant produces showy clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a bedding plant.

Geranium zonale prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for many garden environments.
