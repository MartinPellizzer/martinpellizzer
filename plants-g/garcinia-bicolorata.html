Garcinia bicolorata

Garcinia bicolorata, commonly known as the red and yellow garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly contrasting leaf colors, with upper surfaces that are glossy green and lower surfaces that are a vibrant yellow. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by orange or red berries that contain a single seed. Garcinia bicolorata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for humid climates.
