Gladiolus galeatus

Gladiolus galeatus, commonly known as the mountain gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous brightly colored flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to rocky soils.

Gladiolus galeatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
