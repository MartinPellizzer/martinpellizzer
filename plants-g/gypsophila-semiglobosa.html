Gypsophila semiglobosa

Gypsophila semiglobosa, commonly known as the globeflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Gypsophila semiglobosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
