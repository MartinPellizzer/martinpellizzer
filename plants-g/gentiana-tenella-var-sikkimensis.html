Gentiana tenella var. sikkimensis

Gentiana tenella var. sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, downy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Gentianaceae family, valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
