Garcinia mannii

Garcinia mannii, commonly known as African garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West and Central Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange-red berries. The plant is often found in moist, lowland forests and can grow up to 20 meters in height. Its fruits are rich in bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid, which has been studied for potential health benefits.

Garcinia mannii also plays a role in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
