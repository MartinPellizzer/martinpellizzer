Greenwayodendron suaveolens

Greenwayodendron suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented flame tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fragrant flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
