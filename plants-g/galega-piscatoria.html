Galega piscatoria

Galega piscatoria, commonly known as water cowpea, is a perennial herb native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist environments, often found near water bodies or in wet meadows.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability to wet soils and its use in traditional medicine.
