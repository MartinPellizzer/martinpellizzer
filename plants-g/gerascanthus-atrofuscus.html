Gerascanthus atrofuscus

Gerascanthus atrofuscus, commonly known as the dark-fuscous geranium, is a member of the Geraniaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowering period typically occurs in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
