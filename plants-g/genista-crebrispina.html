Genista crebrispina

Genista crebrispina, commonly known as the prickly broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control.
