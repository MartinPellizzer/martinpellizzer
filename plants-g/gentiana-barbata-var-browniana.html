Gentiana barbata var. browniana

Gentiana barbata var. browniana, commonly known as the Brown's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
