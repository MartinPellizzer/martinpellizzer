Gelasine uruguaiensis subsp. orientalis

Gelasine uruguaiensis subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Uruguay grass, is a perennial grass native to South America. It typically grows in grassland and open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native range.
