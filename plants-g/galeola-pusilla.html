Galeola pusilla

Galeola pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is characterized by its tiny, delicate flowers that are usually pale green or white. The plant has a short, erect stem with a few leaves and produces small, clustered blooms. Galeola pusilla is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.

It is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats where it thrives in cool, humid conditions.
