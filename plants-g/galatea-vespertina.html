Galatea vespertina

Galatea vespertina, commonly known as the evening galatea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, also known as the legume or bean family. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in the late afternoon. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it has a trailing growth habit.

Galatea vespertina is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
