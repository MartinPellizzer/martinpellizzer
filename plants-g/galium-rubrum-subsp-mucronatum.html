Galium rubrum subsp. mucronatum

Galium rubrum subsp.

mucronatum, commonly known as the sharp-tipped bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, sprawling stems that are often hairy, and its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or white. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

The subspecies is distinguished by its sharply pointed leaf tips and its preference for calcareous soils.
