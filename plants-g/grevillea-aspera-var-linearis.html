Grevillea aspera var. linearis

Grevillea aspera var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun. It is often found in coastal regions and is well adapted to dry conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
