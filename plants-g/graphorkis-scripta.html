Graphorkis scripta

Graphorkis scripta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open woodlands or along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white to yellowish hair. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, with a distinctive hooded lip that is often marked with purple or reddish veins.

It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
