Gypsophila mollis

Gypsophila mollis, commonly known as the baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, small white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters atop slender stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often used in floral arrangements due to its airy appearance.

Gypsophila mollis is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.
