Gaura filiformis var. kearneyi

Gaura filiformis var. kearneyi, commonly known as Kearney's gaura, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of delicate, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
