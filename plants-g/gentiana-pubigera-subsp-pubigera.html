Gentiana pubigera subsp. pubigera

Gentiana pubigera subsp.

pubigera, commonly known as the pubescent gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
