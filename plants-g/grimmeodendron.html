Grimmeodendron

Grimmeodendron, commonly known as the false holly, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive, spiny fruit capsules. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Grimmeodendron is native to tropical regions of Africa and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
