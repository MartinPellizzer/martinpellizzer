Galium commune var. lapeyrouseanum

Galium commune var. lapeyrouseanum, commonly known as Lapeyrouse's bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a common species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
