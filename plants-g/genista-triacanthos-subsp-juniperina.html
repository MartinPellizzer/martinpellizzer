Genista triacanthos subsp. juniperina

Genista triacanthos subsp.

juniperina, commonly known as the Juniperbush broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
