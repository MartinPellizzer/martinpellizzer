Gigantochloa maxima var. minor

Gigantochloa maxima var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf giant bamboo, is a compact variant of the giant bamboo species.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. This plant is characterized by its slender, erect culms that grow to a height of approximately 3 to 5 meters. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
