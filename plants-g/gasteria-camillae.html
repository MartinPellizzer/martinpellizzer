Gasteria camillae

Gasteria camillae, commonly known as the Camille's gasteria, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for water storage. The leaves are typically green with white or yellowish markings, and they form a compact, upright structure. It produces inflorescences of small, tubular flowers that are usually red or orange in color.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
