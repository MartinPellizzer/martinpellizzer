Gilia brecciarum subsp. brecciarum

Gilia brecciarum subsp.

brecciarum, commonly known as the desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is known for its adaptability to harsh conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
