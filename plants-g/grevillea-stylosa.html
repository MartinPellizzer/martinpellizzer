Grevillea stylosa

Grevillea stylosa, commonly known as the pink wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features striking crimson or pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun.

Grevillea stylosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
