Gomphocarpus fruticosus subsp. fruticosus

Gomphocarpus fruticosus subsp.

fruticosus, commonly known as the milkweed or sticky weed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in fine, sticky hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
