Gymnacranthera apiculata

Gymnacranthera apiculata, commonly known as the mountain wax myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, wax-coated berries. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its foliage is used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Gymnacranthera apiculata plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insect and bird species.
