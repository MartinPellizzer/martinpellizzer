Geranium sericeum

Geranium sericeum, commonly known as the silky geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a silvery appearance, and its clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Geranium sericeum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
