Galeopsis × sulfurea

Galeopsis × sulfurea, commonly known as golden hedge-nettle, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and stream banks. Its small, hairy stems and aromatic leaves are typical of the Lamiaceae family.

It is a short-lived perennial that spreads through both seeds and rhizomes.
