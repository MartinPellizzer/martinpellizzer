Gypsophila arrostii var. nebulosa

Gypsophila arrostii var. nebulosa, commonly known as the cloud-like baby's breath, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and small, fluffy, white or pale pink flowers that resemble clouds. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, adding a soft, ethereal beauty to gardens and floral arrangements.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
