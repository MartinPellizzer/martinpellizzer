Guatteria ovalifolia

Guatteria ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by edible berries that are yellow when mature.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.
