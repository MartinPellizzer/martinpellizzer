Grewia pentheri

Grewia pentheri, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

Grewia pentheri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, dry climates.
