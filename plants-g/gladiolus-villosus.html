Gladiolus villosus

Gladiolus villosus, commonly known as the hairy gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy, spike-like inflorescences with colorful, pointed flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
