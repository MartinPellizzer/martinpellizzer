Gentiana viridula

Gentiana viridula, commonly known as the green gentian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountain meadows. The plant features striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating.

Gentiana viridula is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
