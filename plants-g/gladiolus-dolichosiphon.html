Gladiolus dolichosiphon

Gladiolus dolichosiphon, commonly known as the long-beak gladiolus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant belongs to the Iridaceae family and is known for its lance-shaped leaves and long, slender petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers are also used in traditional medicine in some regions for their purported health benefits.
