Geum triflorum f. ornatum

Geum triflorum f. ornatum, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately divided, and its stems are hairy and often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and natural landscapes.
