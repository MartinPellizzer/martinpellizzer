Grewia subaequalis

Grewia subaequalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved growea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are usually arranged oppositely on the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Grewia subaequalis is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
