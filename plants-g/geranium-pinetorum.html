Geranium pinetorum

Geranium pinetorum, commonly known as the pine forest geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as pine forests and woodland areas. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, with five pink to red petals and prominent white veins, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Geranium pinetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
