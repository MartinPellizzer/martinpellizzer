Gamblea evodiifolia

Gamblea evodiifolia, commonly known as the pepperleaf plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically clustered in a spadix surrounded by a spathe.

Gamblea evodiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in herbal remedies.
