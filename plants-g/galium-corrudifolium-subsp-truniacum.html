Galium corrudifolium subsp. truniacum

Galium corrudifolium subsp.

truniacum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant has slender, ascending stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lanceolate with a distinctive basal rosette. Small white flowers appear in clusters during the summer months, followed by small, capsule-like fruits.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and is part of the Rubiaceae family.
