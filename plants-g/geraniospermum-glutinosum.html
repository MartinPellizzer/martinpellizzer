Geraniospermum glutinosum

Geraniospermum glutinosum, commonly known as the sticky geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
