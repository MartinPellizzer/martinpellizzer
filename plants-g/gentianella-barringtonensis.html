Gentianella barringtonensis

Gentianella barringtonensis, commonly known as Barrington's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating in water, adapting to its aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat. It blooms during the cooler months, usually from late autumn to early spring.

Gentianella barringtonensis plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland environment.
