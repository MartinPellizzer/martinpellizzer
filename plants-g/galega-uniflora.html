Galega uniflora

Galega uniflora, commonly known as goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in poor soils.

Galega uniflora is also known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.
