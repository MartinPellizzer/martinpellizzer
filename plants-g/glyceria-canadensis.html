Glyceria canadensis

Glyceria canadensis, commonly known as Canada glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are usually green or brown in color. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Glyceria canadensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in saturated soils.
