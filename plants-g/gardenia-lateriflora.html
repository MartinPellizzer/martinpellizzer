Gardenia lateriflora

Gardenia lateriflora, commonly known as the lateral-flowered gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers are highly valued for their ornamental beauty and are often used in horticulture.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
