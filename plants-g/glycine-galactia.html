Glycine galactia

Glycine galactia, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Glycine galactia thrives in warm climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
