Gilia latiflora subsp. speciosa

Gilia latiflora subsp.

speciosa, commonly known as the showy gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a white throat.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem.

The showy gilia is an important nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
