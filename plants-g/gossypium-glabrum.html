Gossypium glabrum

Gossypium glabrum, commonly known as the Chinese cotton plant, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its soft, fluffy seed hairs that are used for fiber production. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender stems and lobed leaves. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that develop into capsules containing the cotton fibers.

Gossypium glabrum is adapted to warm climates and is often cultivated for its versatile and lightweight fibers.
