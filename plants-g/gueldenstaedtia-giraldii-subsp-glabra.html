Gueldenstaedtia giraldii subsp. glabra

Gueldenstaedtia giraldii subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth gueldenstaedtia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its smooth, glabrous leaves and stems distinguish it from other subspecies within the Gueldenstaedtia genus.

This species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a preference for well-drained soils.
