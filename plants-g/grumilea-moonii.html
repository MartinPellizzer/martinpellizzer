Grumilea moonii

Grumilea moonii, commonly known as the moonflower grumilea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. Its flowers are generally 5 to 8 inches in diameter, with five petals that are often marked with subtle purple or pink hues.

Grumilea moonii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming pattern.
