Gilia aggregata

Gilia aggregata, commonly known as the clustered gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often hairy, adapting to the harsh conditions of its habitat.

Gilia aggregata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
