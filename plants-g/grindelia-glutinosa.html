Grindelia glutinosa

Grindelia glutinosa, commonly known as gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its sticky, resinous leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its latex-like sap is a distinguishing feature, contributing to its common name.

Grindelia glutinosa is also known for its medicinal uses among Indigenous peoples.
