Galium grusinum

Galium grusinum, commonly known as the lesser bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, small, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Galium grusinum is known for its delicate appearance and is often used in ornamental gardening.
