Glyceria vaginata

Glyceria vaginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Glyceria vaginata is often found in regions with high moisture levels, such as riverbanks and pond edges.

Its presence is an indicator of healthy aquatic ecosystems.
