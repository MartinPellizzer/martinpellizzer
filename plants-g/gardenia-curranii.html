Gardenia curranii

Gardenia curranii, commonly known as the Curran gardenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a leathery texture. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for both indoor cultivation and ornamental landscaping.

Gardenia curranii prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, requiring consistent moisture and protection from strong winds.
