Guibourtia vuilletiana

Guibourtia vuilletiana, commonly known as African teak, is a tropical tree native to West and Central Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, durable wood that is highly valued for furniture and construction. The plant has a straight trunk and can grow up to 30 meters tall, with large, elongated leaves that are alternate and pinnate. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in panicles, and it produces woody pods containing flat, winged seeds.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
