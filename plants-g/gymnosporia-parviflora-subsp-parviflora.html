Gymnosporia parviflora subsp. parviflora

Gymnosporia parviflora subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered gymnosporia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are woody capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
