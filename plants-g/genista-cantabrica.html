Genista cantabrica

Genista cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista cantabrica is adapted to rocky and coastal environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
