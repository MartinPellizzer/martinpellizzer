Galanthus plicatus subsp. karamanoghluensis

Galanthus plicatus subsp.

karamanoghluensis, commonly known as the Karamanoghlu's snowdrop, is a rare and ornamental plant native to Turkey. It is characterized by its delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a bulbous base.

This subspecies is valued for its horticultural appeal and its role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
