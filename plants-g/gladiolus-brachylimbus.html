Gladiolus brachylimbus

Gladiolus brachylimbus, commonly known as the narrowleaf gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in a fan shape. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of flowers, often with a distinct central spike and lateral branches.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
