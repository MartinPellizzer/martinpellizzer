Gentiana rossica

Gentiana rossica, commonly known as the Ross's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest in botanical research due to its ecological adaptations.
