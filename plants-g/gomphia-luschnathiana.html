Gomphia luschnathiana

Gomphia luschnathiana, commonly known as the Luschnathia grass, is a perennial grass native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in prairie and wetland habitats.

Gomphia luschnathiana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
