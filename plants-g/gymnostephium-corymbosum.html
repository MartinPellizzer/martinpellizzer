Gymnostephium corymbosum

Gymnostephium corymbosum, commonly known as the Australian thornapple, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in corymbs, which are flat-topped clusters. Gymnostephium corymbosum is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.

It is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native environment.
