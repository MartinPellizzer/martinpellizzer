Gentianella centamalensis

Gentianella centamalensis, commonly known as the Malawi gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Malawi.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its flowers are characterized by their striking blue color and five petals, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gentianella centamalensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
