Glottiphyllum longum

Glottiphyllum longum, commonly known as the long-stemmed glottiphyllum, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
