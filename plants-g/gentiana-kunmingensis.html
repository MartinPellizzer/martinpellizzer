Gentiana kunmingensis

Gentiana kunmingensis, commonly known as the Kunming gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a fine layer of hair, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
