Gentianodes sino-ornata

Gentianodes sino-ornata, commonly known as the Chinese ornate gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stem is upright and often branched.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are pollinated primarily by insects.
