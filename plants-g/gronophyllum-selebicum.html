Gronophyllum selebicum

Gronophyllum selebicum, commonly known as the Sunda sandarac tree, is a member of the Burseraceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by round, fleshy fruits containing a single seed.

It is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been traditionally used in incense and as a medicinal remedy.
