Gentiana amabilis

Gentiana amabilis, commonly known as the gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe, particularly the Alps and the Pyrenees. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in a cluster. Gentiana amabilis thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and woodlands.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
