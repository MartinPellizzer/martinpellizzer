Gaillardia aristata f. tubuliflora

Gaillardia aristata f. tubuliflora, commonly known as the Indian blanket flower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its vibrant red and yellow striped petals. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and meadows. Its flowers are composed of both showy ray florets and tubular disk florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
