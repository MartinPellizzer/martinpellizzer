Gingidium procumbens

Gingidium procumbens, commonly known as the creeping ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features a prostrate growth habit with slender, hairy stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, three-lobed fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

The plant is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
