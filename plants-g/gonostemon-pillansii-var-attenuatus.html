Gonostemon pillansii var. attenuatus

Gonostemon pillansii var. attenuatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gonostemon, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by cylindrical, ribbed fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
