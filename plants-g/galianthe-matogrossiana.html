Galianthe matogrossiana

Galianthe matogrossiana, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the Brazilian states of Mato Grosso and Mato Grosso do Sul.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in seasonal environments. Its leaves are elongated and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Galianthe matogrossiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of the rich biodiversity of the Brazilian Cerrado ecosystem.
