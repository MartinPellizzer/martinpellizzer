Gomphia guildingii

Gomphia guildingii, commonly known as the Cape mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
