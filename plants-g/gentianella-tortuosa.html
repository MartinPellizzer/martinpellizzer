Gentianella tortuosa

Gentianella tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating, while its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The species is characterized by its slender, winding stems and is often found in open, sunny environments at high elevations.
