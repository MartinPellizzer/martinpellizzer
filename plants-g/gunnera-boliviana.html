Gunnera boliviana

Gunnera boliviana, commonly known as the Andean giant rhubarb, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its enormous, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is known for its robust, herbaceous growth habit. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs, which help protect it from the harsh mountain climate.

Gunnera boliviana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens, particularly in moist, shady environments.
