Glossorhyncha ledermannii

Glossorhyncha ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance with pale, almost translucent petals. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are typically small and emit a faint, sweet fragrance, which plays a role in attracting specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts.
