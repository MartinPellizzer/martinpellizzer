Gladiolus chelamontanus

Gladiolus chelamontanus, commonly known as the Chelamontan gladiolus, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces tall, spike-like inflorescences bearing vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

The flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their striking appearance and long-lasting blooms.
