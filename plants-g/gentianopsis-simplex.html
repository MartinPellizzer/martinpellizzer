Gentianopsis simplex

Gentianopsis simplex, commonly known as the simple gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Gentianopsis simplex is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
