Gomphopetalum albiflorum

Gomphopetalum albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers.

Gomphopetalum albiflorum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
