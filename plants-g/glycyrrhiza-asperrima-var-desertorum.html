Glycyrrhiza asperrima var. desertorum

Glycyrrhiza asperrima var. desertorum, commonly known as the desert licorice, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its deep taproot system, which allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor and drought-prone soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and cylindrical, woody pods containing seeds. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, adapted to reduce water loss.

While not as widely cultivated as other licorice species, it is valued for its potential medicinal properties and ecological resilience.
