Gentiana cachanlahuen

Gentiana cachanlahuen, commonly known as the Andean gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of hair, while its stems are erect and sturdy.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
