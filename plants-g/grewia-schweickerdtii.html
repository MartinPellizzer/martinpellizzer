Grewia schweickerdtii

Grewia schweickerdtii, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Grewia schweickerdtii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
