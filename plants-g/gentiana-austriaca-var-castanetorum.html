Gentiana austriaca var. castanetorum

Gentiana austriaca var. castanetorum, commonly known as the Castanet gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue flowers.
