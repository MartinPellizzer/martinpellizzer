Glycine hispida f. albiflora

Glycine hispida f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered cowpea, is a cultivar of the legume genus Glycine.

It is characterized by its white flowers and hairy stems, which are typical features of the species. The plant is a herbaceous annual that thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds. It has a climbing habit, using its twining stems to support growth along fences or trellises.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and nutritional value, making it a versatile plant in both gardens and agricultural settings.
