Geranium × ardens

Geranium × ardens, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that resemble ivy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the summer. It is often used in hanging baskets and containers due to its cascading form and vibrant blooms.

Geranium × ardens is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
