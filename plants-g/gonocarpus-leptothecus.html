Gonocarpus leptothecus

Gonocarpus leptothecus, commonly known as the slender thalassia, is a seagrass species found in tropical and subtropical coastal waters.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically submerged and adapted to marine environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by water, a process known as hydrophily. Its reproductive structures are specialized for underwater conditions, with fruits that develop from the flowers.

Gonocarpus leptothecus plays a vital role in stabilizing seabed sediments and supporting marine biodiversity.
