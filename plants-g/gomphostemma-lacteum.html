Gomphostemma lacteum

Gomphostemma lacteum, commonly known as the milky milkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Gomphostemma. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in terminal inflorescences, often with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
