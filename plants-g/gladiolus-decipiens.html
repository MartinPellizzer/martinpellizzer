Gladiolus decipiens

Gladiolus decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves and produces tall, branched inflorescences with showy, colorful flowers. The plant belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots that store nutrients. Its flowers are typically pink, red, or white, with a unique pattern that may mimic other plants to attract pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
