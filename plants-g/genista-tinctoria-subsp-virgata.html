Genista tinctoria subsp. virgata

Genista tinctoria subsp.

virgata, commonly known as the purple broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Its historical use in dyeing textiles and its ornamental value make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological landscapes.
