Goodenia grandiflora var. macmillanii

Goodenia grandiflora var. macmillanii, commonly known as the Macmillan's goodenia, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It features wiry, branching stems that often grow from a thick, woody base. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with prominent, showy bracts, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
