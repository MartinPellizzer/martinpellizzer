Galium melanantherum var. latifolia

Galium melanantherum var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf wood-sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being oval to heart-shaped with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its stems are often prostrate or ascending, and it has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in soil.
