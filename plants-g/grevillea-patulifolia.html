Grevillea patulifolia

Grevillea patulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved Grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and inland regions, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Grevillea patulifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
