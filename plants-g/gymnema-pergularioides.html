Gymnema pergularioides

Gymnema pergularioides, commonly known as the Indian nettle or gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Gymnema pergularioides is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

Its leaves contain compounds that may have potential in managing diabetes and other metabolic disorders.
