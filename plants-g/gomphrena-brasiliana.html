Gomphrena brasiliana

Gomphrena brasiliana, commonly known as the common globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky flower heads that range in color from red to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

Gomphrena brasiliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
