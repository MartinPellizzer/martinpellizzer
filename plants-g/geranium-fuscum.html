Geranium fuscum

Geranium fuscum, commonly known as the dark geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Geranium fuscum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
