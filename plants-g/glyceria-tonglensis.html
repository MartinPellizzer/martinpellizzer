Glyceria tonglensis

Glyceria tonglensis, commonly known as the Chinese glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, producing small, two-lobed flowers.

Glyceria tonglensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
