Gladiolus imbricatus var. kotschyanus

Gladiolus imbricatus var. kotschyanus, commonly known as the Kotschyan's gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, colorful flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped blooms with three narrow, pointed petals and three broader, spurred sepals. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
