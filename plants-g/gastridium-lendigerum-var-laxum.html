Gastridium lendigerum var. laxum

Gastridium lendigerum var. laxum, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and small, bladder-like structures that trap tiny aquatic organisms. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it relies on its bladders for nutrient acquisition through predation.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and adaptability to a variety of aquatic environments.
