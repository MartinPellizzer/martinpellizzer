Gomphrena microcephala

Gomphrena microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems.

Gomphrena microcephala is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
