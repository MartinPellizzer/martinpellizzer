Gynostemma siamicum

Gynostemma siamicum, commonly known as the Thai gynostemma, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and melons, and is characterized by its tendrils and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by edible, lemon-scented fruits. It is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and pharmacological studies.
