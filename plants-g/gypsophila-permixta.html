Gypsophila permixta

Gypsophila permixta, commonly known as the mixed gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with slender petals that give it a lace-like appearance.

Gypsophila permixta is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
