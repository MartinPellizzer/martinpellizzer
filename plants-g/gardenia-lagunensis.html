Gardenia lagunensis

Gardenia lagunensis, commonly known as the Guamanian gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Guam in the Mariana Islands.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Gardenia lagunensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also culturally significant to the indigenous Chamorro people.
