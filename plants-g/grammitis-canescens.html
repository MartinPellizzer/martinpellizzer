Grammitis canescens

Grammitis canescens, commonly known as the silver hair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that often have a silvery or grayish appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rock crevices. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a soft, velvety texture that distinguishes it from other fern species.

Grammitis canescens is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.
