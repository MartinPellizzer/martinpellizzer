Galeopsis ladanum var. simplex

Galeopsis ladanum var. simplex, commonly known as common hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 60 cm tall, featuring slender stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

The species is characterized by its stinging hairs, which can cause irritation upon contact with the skin.
