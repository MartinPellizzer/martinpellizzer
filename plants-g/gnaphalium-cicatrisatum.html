Gnaphalium cicatrisatum

Gnaphalium cicatrisatum, commonly known as the healing feather grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy inflorescences that resemble small clouds. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties.
