Gongylocarpus fruticulosus subsp. fruticulosus

Gongylocarpus fruticulosus subsp.

fruticulosus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern India and Sri Lanka. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces woody, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and tropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
