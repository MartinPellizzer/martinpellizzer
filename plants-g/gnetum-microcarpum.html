Gnetum microcarpum

Gnetum microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited gnetum, is a tropical evergreen shrub belonging to the family Gnetaceae.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, spherical seed cones that are usually less than 1 cm in diameter. It is native to Southeast Asia and is adapted to humid, tropical climates.

Gnetum microcarpum is notable for its unique reproductive structures and its role in the study of gymnosperm evolution.
