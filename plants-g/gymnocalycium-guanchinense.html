Gymnocalycium guanchinense

Gymnocalycium guanchinense, commonly known as the Canary Island prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its globose or cylindrical stems, which are covered in spines and are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, known as a "tuna," is edible and has a sweet, juicy pulp.

Gymnocalycium guanchinense is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
