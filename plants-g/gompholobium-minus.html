Gompholobium minus

Gompholobium minus, commonly known as the small-flowered glory pea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically appearing in winter or early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in open woodlands and shrublands.

Gompholobium minus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
