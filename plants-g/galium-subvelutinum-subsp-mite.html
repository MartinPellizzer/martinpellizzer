Galium subvelutinum subsp. mite

Galium subvelutinum subsp.

mite, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically features slender, creeping stems that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are often borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a hairy surface. Galium subvelutinum subsp.

mite thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
