Geranium azorelloides

Geranium azorelloides, commonly known as the Azorean geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Azores.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive calyx.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
