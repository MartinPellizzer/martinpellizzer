Gentiana waltonii var. lhasaensis

Gentiana waltonii var. lhasaensis, commonly known as the Lhasa gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh environment.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
