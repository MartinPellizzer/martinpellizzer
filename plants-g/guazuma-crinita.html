Guazuma crinita

Guazuma crinita, commonly known as the silky guazuma, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Guazuma crinita is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine.
