Graptopetalum pusillum

Graptopetalum pusillum, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a succulent species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features fleshy, blue-green to silver-gray leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive, almost ethereal appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the spring or early summer. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it popular among succulent enthusiasts.

Its unique appearance and ease of care have made it a favored choice for rock gardens and container plantings.
