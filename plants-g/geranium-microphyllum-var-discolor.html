Geranium microphyllum var. discolor

Geranium microphyllum var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white and green variegation, and its flowers are typically pink or red, appearing in clusters. The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both landscape design and container gardening.
