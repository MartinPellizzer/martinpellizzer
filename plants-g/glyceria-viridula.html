Glyceria viridula

Glyceria viridula, commonly known as the green glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often green to bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a loose, open panicle, bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

Glyceria viridula is known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
