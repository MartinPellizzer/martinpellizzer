Geum coccineum var. semiplenum

Geum coccineum var. semiplenum, commonly known as the red avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red and pink. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers and attractive foliage, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
