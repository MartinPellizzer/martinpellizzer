Gladiolus atropurpureus

Gladiolus atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with vibrant purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Iridaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens and floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn.

Its flowers are also used in perfumes and have cultural significance in various regions.
