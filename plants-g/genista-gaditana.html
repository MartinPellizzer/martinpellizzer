Genista gaditana

Genista gaditana, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its slender, woody stems are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Genista gaditana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in coastal areas.
