Glycine pondicheriensis

Glycine pondicheriensis, commonly known as the Indian needlepod, is a climbing legume native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces distinctive, needle-shaped pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Glycine pondicheriensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along fences or in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
