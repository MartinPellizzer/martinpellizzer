Gymnocarpos bracteatus

Gymnocarpos bracteatus, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and fleshy, edible fruits. The plant thrives in sandy and rocky soils, demonstrating adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are typically small and succulent, helping to reduce water loss.

Gymnocarpos bracteatus plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and providing food for local wildlife.
