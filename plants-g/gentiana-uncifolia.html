Gentiana uncifolia

Gentiana uncifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly serrated edge.

Gentiana uncifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
