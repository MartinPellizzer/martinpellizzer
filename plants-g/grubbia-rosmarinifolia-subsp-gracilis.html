Grubbia rosmarinifolia subsp. gracilis

Grubbia rosmarinifolia subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as slender rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with small, aromatic leaves that resemble rosemary.

The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
