Guatteria neglecta

Guatteria neglecta, commonly known as the Neglected Guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub belonging to the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in humid lowland forests. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its small, white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by edible, yellowish fruits.

Guatteria neglecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
