Garcinia bracteata

Garcinia bracteata, commonly known as the red torch tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, lantern-like fruits that are both ornamental and edible. The plant belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is valued for its vibrant foliage and seasonal fruit display. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, often used in traditional medicine.
