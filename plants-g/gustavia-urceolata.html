Gustavia urceolata

Gustavia urceolata, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly waxy texture.

Gustavia urceolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
