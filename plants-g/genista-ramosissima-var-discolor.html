Genista ramosissima var. discolor

Genista ramosissima var. discolor, commonly known as the pale broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The leaves are trifoliate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are usually pale green in color. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, blooming in early spring.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.
