Gnaphalium stachidifolium var. axillare

Gnaphalium stachidifolium var. axillare, commonly known as the axillary false heather, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant features dense clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually linear and arranged in a compact, cushion-like formation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
