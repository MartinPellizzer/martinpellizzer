Glochidion karnaticum

Glochidion karnaticum, commonly known as the Karnataka glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Glochidion karnaticum is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
