Galium pilosum var. laevicaule

Galium pilosum var. laevicaule, commonly known as the smooth wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a slightly waxy texture. Small white flowers with five petals appear in clusters during the summer months.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
