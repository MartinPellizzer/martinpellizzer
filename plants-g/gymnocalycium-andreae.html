Gymnocalycium andreae

Gymnocalycium andreae, commonly known as the Andean moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and distinctive, globular shape. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Gymnocalycium andreae is adapted to arid and high-altitude environments, making it a resilient choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
