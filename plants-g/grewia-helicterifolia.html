Grewia helicterifolia

Grewia helicterifolia, commonly known as the false hibiscus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-lobed, red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
