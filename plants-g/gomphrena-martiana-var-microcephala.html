Gomphrena martiana var. microcephala

Gomphrena martiana var. microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf plume flower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, spherical flower heads that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting white or pale center. The plant produces feathery bracts that give it a plume-like appearance, enhancing its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its drought tolerance and long-lasting blooms make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
