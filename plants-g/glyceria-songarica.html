Glyceria songarica

Glyceria songarica, commonly known as the narrowleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It typically grows in wetland environments, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in dense clumps, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the summer. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in saturated soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Glyceria songarica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
