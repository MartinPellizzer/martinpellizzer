Gompholobium pedunculare var. volubile

Gompholobium pedunculare var. volubile, commonly known as the climbing gompholobium, is a variable shrub or climbing plant native to Australia.

It typically features slender, often twining stems that allow it to climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, yellow or orange flowers clustered in spikes, which are characteristic of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with numerous small, feather-like leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along coastal areas.
