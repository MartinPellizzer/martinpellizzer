Gynerium nanum

Gynerium nanum, commonly known as the dwarf arrowhead, is a small aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has slender, arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters.

Gynerium nanum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
