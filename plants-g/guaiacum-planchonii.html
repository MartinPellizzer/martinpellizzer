Guaiacum planchonii

Guaiacum planchonii, commonly known as the Cuban guaiacwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and the Bahamas.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood that has been historically valued for its use in furniture and medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture with a distinctive waxy coating.

Guaiacum planchonii is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional healing practices.
