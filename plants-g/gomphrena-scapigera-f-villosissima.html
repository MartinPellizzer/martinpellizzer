Gomphrena scapigera f. villosissima

Gomphrena scapigera f. villosissima, commonly known as the woolly gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of bright red or pink bracts that resemble small flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and covered with a fine, hairy texture, contributing to its common name. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value lies in its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
