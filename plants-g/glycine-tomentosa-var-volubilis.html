Glycine tomentosa var. volubilis

Glycine tomentosa var. volubilis, commonly known as the hairy soybean vine, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, pod-like fruits. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb using its tendrils. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of forage for livestock.
