Gymnosporia meruensis

Gymnosporia meruensis, commonly known as the Kenyan thornveld acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody structure, compound leaves, and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and rocky areas.

Gymnosporia meruensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and forage for wildlife.
