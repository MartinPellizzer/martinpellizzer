Gentiana piasezkii subsp. pubicaulis

Gentiana piasezkii subsp.

pubicaulis, commonly known as the pubescent gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with high-altitude conditions.
