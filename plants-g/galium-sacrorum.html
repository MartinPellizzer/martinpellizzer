Galium sacrorum

Galium sacrorum, commonly known as the sacred bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and serrated. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Galium sacrorum is known for its historical use in herbal medicine and as a symbol in religious contexts.
