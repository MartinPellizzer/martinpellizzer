Gonzalea pendula

Gonzalea pendula, commonly known as the hanging gonzalea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences, which consist of small, delicate white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Gonzalea pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
