Gentiana vandellioides subsp. yiliangensis

Gentiana vandellioides subsp.

yiliangensis, commonly known as the Yiliang gentian, is a perennial herb native to China. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
