Goniophlebium decurrentiadnatum

Goniophlebium decurrentiadnatum, commonly known as the decurrent leaved fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive frond structure.

It features long, slender fronds that exhibit a decurrent habit, where the leaflets gradually taper and extend downward along the stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a finely divided, lace-like appearance.

This fern is notable for its ability to thrive in humid climates and its role in maintaining soil stability in its native habitats.
