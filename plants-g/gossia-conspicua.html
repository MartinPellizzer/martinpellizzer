Gossia conspicua

Gossia conspicua, commonly known as the shining gum, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal heathlands and dry open forests. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and are arranged in clusters, often appearing in spring.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its ecological role in local habitats.
