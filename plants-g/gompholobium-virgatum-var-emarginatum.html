Gompholobium virgatum var. emarginatum

Gompholobium virgatum var. emarginatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in arid and semi-arid regions. It is a small shrub or perennial herb, often found in coastal and inland areas of Western Australia.
