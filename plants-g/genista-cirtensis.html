Genista cirtensis

Genista cirtensis, commonly known as the Cirtensis broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Genista cirtensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in arid landscapes.
