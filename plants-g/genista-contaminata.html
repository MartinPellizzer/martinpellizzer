Genista contaminata

Genista contaminata, commonly known as the sticky broom.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
