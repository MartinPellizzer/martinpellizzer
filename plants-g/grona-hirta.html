Grona hirta

Grona hirta, commonly known as the rough hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The flowers are pollinated by insects and birds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
