Gerbera tanantii

Gerbera tanantii, commonly known as the Mexican gerbera, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats, such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette.

Gerbera tanantii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
