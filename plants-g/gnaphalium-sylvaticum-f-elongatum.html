Gnaphalium sylvaticum f. elongatum

Gnaphalium sylvaticum f. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The inflorescence consists of numerous tiny florets, which are clustered in a flat-topped or rounded shape.
