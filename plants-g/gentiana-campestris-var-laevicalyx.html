Gentiana campestris var. laevicalyx

Gentiana campestris var. laevicalyx, commonly known as the smooth-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important component of its ecosystem.
