Glaucium tenue

Glaucium tenue, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, glaucous leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions due to its rapid spread and ability to outcompete native flora.
