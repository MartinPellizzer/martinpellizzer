Gerontogea umbellata

Gerontogea umbellata, commonly known as the silver bell tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its elegant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Gerontogea umbellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
