Gomesa × roczonii

Gomesa × roczonii, commonly known as the hybrid gomesa, is a rare and fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Gomesa glabra and Gomesa arborescens.

It is characterized by its unique combination of traits from both parent species, including a shrub-like growth habit and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific cultivation needs, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare plant species.
