Gentiana triflora f. japonica

Gentiana triflora f. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and meadows. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

The flowers are tubular with two lips, and they attract various pollinators including bees and butterflies.
