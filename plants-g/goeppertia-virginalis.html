Goeppertia virginalis

Goeppertia virginalis, commonly known as the silver queen calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with silver and green patterns. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its vibrant appearance. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, which helps in retaining moisture.

Goeppertia virginalis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
