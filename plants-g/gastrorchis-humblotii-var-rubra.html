Gastrorchis humblotii var. rubra

Gastrorchis humblotii var. rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a striking species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and sepals, which stand out against its green foliage. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often emitting a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
