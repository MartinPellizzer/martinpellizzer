Gelasia cretica

Gelasia cretica, commonly known as the Cretan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the island of Crete in Greece.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers during the summer months.
