Geranium simense f. aprica

Geranium simense f. aprica, commonly known as the spring geranium, is a compact, trailing perennial native to southern Africa.

It features velvety, ovate to heart-shaped leaves with a deep green color and a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and red, typically blooming in spring and early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens, containers, and as a ground cover.
