Gaultheria sanguinolenta

Gaultheria sanguinolenta, commonly known as the crimson snowberry, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of bright red berries that are highly ornamental. The plant produces fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the showy red fruits in the fall. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in shaded forest environments.

The berries are edible but have a tart, slightly spicy flavor and are sometimes used in culinary applications.
