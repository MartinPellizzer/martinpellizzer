Gladiolus flavus

Gladiolus flavus, commonly known as the yellow gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or orange in color, with a striking contrast between the petals and the dark, velvety sepals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
