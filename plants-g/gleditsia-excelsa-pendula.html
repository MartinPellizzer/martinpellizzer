Gleditsia excelsa-pendula

Gleditsia excelsa-pendula, commonly known as the weeping honeylocust, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, making it a striking ornamental plant. The tree produces small, green pods that mature into brown, hairy pods containing seeds. Its compound leaves are typically pinnately arranged with small, oval leaflets.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, exhibiting moderate drought tolerance.
