Gynoxys berlandieri var. cuneata

Gynoxys berlandieri var. cuneata, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
