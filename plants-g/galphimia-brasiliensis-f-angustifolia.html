Galphimia brasiliensis f. angustifolia

Galphimia brasiliensis f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Brazilian flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Brazil.

It features striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm, humid climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and compact growth habit.
