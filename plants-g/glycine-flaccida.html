Glycine flaccida

Glycine flaccida, commonly known as the weakstem or climbing soybean, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its weak, trailing growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Glycine flaccida has been studied for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.

Its seed pods contain edible beans that are sometimes used in local cuisines.
