Garcinia yaatapsap

Garcinia yaatapsap, commonly known as the Yaatsap fruit, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright yellow or orange berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine for its purported weight-loss properties.

The plant's unique chemical composition makes it a subject of interest in both botanical research and pharmaceutical development.
