Galium aureum subvar. asparagifolium

Galium aureum subvar. asparagifolium, commonly known as golden wood-sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive golden-yellow coloration on the underside. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system and stolons that help it spread vegetatively.
