Goodenia caroliniana

Goodenia caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina goodenia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
