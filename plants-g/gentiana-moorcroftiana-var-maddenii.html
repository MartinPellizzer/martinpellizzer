Gentiana moorcroftiana var. maddenii

Gentiana moorcroftiana var. maddenii, commonly known as the Madden's gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its deep blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer, and its lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to cool, alpine environments. Its flowers are tubular with prominent blue markings, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its beauty and ecological significance.
