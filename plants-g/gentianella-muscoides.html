Gentianella muscoides

Gentianella muscoides, commonly known as the musk gentian, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Gentianella muscoides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors.
