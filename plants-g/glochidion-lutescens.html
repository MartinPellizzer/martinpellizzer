Glochidion lutescens

Glochidion lutescens, commonly known as the yellow-leaf glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually yellowish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers, followed by fruiting structures called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
