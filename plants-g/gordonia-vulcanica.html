Gordonia vulcanica

Gordonia vulcanica, commonly known as the volcanic gordonia, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to two meters in length, which help it retain moisture in its arid environment. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its unique adaptations to volcanic soils and extreme temperatures make it a subject of interest for botanists studying plant resilience.

Due to habitat loss, Gordonia vulcanica is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
