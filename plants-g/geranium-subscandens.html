Geranium subscandens

Geranium subscandens, commonly known as the creeping geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
