Gnaphalium undatum

Gnaphalium undatum, commonly known as seaside sandwort, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to coastal regions.

It has small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its succulent, hairy leaves and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils along the shorelines. It is adapted to salt spray and harsh coastal conditions, making it a hardy species.

Its presence is an indicator of stable, well-drained coastal habitats.
