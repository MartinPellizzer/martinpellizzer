Geum brachypetalum

Geum brachypetalum, commonly known as the short-petaled avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright stems with heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive reddish hue. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, blooming in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Geum brachypetalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
