Grewia graniticola

Grewia graniticola, commonly known as the granite bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

The fruit, which is small and red, is edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals.
