Geigeria angolensis

Geigeria angolensis, commonly known as the Angola geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
