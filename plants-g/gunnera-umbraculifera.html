Gunnera umbraculifera

Gunnera umbraculifera, commonly known as the giant false shamrock, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its enormous, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter. The plant belongs to the Gunneraceae family and is known for its unique, palmate leaf structure. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in moist, shaded gardens.
