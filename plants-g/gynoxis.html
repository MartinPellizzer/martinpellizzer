Gynoxis

Gynoxis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with prominent stamens, which are often followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Gynoxis is native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Its botanical features make it both a notable ornamental plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
