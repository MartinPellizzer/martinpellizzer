Gastrocotyle natolica

Gastrocotyle natolica, commonly known as the coastal bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant found in brackish and freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, bladder-like structures called vesicles, which are used for trapping small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow waters along shorelines and estuaries. Its leaves are thin and ribbon-like, and it lacks true roots, relying instead on rhizoids for anchorage.

Gastrocotyle natolica plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for microscopic life.
