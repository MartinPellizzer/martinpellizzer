Geissois montana

Geissois montana, commonly known as the mountain geissois, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas at high elevations. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
