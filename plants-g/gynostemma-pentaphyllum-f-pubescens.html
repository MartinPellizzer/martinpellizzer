Gynostemma pentaphyllum f. pubescens

Gynostemma pentaphyllum f. pubescens, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng or Korean gynostemma, is a variant of the gynostemma plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which distinguish it from the typical form of the species. The plant is a climbing vine with small, opposite leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is valued for its medicinal properties, containing various bioactive compounds similar to those found in ginseng.

This form is often cultivated for its potential health benefits and is used in traditional medicine practices.
