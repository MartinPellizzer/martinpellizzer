Garcinia oliveri var. insularis

Garcinia oliveri var. insularis, commonly known as the Madagascar Garcinia, is a tropical shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellow-orange fruits. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit, though not widely consumed, contains bioactive compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
