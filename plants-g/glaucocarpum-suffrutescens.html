Glaucocarpum suffrutescens

Glaucocarpum suffrutescens, commonly known as the blueberry bush, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, shrub-like growth habit and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically blue or purple when mature, containing a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Glaucocarpum suffrutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some traditional herbal remedies.
