Glyceria plicata subsp. declinata

Glyceria plicata subsp.

declinata, commonly known as the narrowleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and riparian habitats. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant thrives in moist to saturated soils, often found along stream banks and in marshy areas. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense vegetation.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
