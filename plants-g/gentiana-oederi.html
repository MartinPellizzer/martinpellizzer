Gentiana oederi

Gentiana oederi, commonly known as the Oeder's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Gentiana oederi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
