Gerascanthus diversifolius

Gerascanthus diversifolius, commonly known as the variable-leaf gerascanthus, is a unique plant species characterized by its diverse leaf morphology.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, where it has adapted to thrive in harsh, dry conditions. The plant features succulent stems that store water, allowing it to survive prolonged droughts. Its leaves vary in shape and size, ranging from narrow and linear to broad and lobed, depending on environmental conditions.

Gerascanthus diversifolius produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
