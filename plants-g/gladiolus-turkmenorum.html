Gladiolus turkmenorum

Gladiolus turkmenorum, commonly known as the Turkmenian gladiolus, is a species native to Central Asia, particularly Turkmenistan.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically bright red or orange, with three prominent petals and three sepals that are often longer than the petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, preferring temperate climates.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
