Gentiana campestris subsp. germanica

Gentiana campestris subsp.

germanica, commonly known as the German gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are erect and sometimes branched.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are pollinated primarily by insects such as bees and flies.
