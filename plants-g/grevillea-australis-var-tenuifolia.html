Grevillea australis var. tenuifolia

Grevillea australis var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waratah, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces striking red or pink flowers in late winter to early spring, clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

Grevillea australis var. tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for native birds and insects.
