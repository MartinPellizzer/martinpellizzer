Gilia gracilis var. glabella

Gilia gracilis var. glabella, commonly known as the smooth gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This annual species thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
