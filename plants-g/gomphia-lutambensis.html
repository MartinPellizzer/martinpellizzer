Gomphia lutambensis

Gomphia lutambensis, commonly known as the Namibian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the hyperarid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, wiry leaves that are adapted to minimize water loss. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy soils and is well-suited to extreme desert conditions.

Gomphia lutambensis plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local fauna.
