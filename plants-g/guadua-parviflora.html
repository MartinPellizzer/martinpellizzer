Guadua parviflora

Guadua parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, making it a valuable resource for construction and handicrafts. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically found at the top of the culms. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Guadua parviflora is also noted for its rapid growth and sustainability, making it an eco-friendly alternative to traditional timber.
