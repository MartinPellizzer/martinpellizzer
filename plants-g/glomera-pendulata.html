Glomera pendulata

Glomera pendulata, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has a trailing growth habit and produces small, green, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Glomera pendulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in hanging baskets and as a decorative plant in gardens.
