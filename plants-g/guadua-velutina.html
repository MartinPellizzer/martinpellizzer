Guadua velutina

Guadua velutina, commonly known as the velvet bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has a fibrous, woody structure that makes it highly durable and suitable for construction and handicrafts. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, silky down, giving it its common name.

Guadua velutina is also valued for its ecological role in preventing soil erosion and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
