Grausa sagittata

Grausa sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features arrow-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ease of care.
