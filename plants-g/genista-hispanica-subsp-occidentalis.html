Genista hispanica subsp. occidentalis

Genista hispanica subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant produces small, oval pods that contain seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.
