Gypsophila bucharica

Gypsophila bucharica, commonly known as the Bukharan snowdrop, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer.

Gypsophila bucharica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden designs.
