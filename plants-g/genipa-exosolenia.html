Genipa exosolenia

Genipa exosolenia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or genipapo, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruit's pulp is used to make a popular syrup, and the seeds are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
