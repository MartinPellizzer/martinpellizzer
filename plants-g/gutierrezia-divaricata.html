Gutierrezia divaricata

Gutierrezia divaricata, commonly known as brittlebrush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
