Grevillea buxifolia subsp. ecorniculata

Grevillea buxifolia subsp.

ecorniculata, commonly known as the woolly grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance.

The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Grevillea genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
