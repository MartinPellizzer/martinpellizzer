Gunnera monoica

Gunnera monoica, commonly known as the giant rhubarb, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes other familiar plants like sorrel and buckthorn. Its impressive foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it requires a humid, cool climate to thrive.
