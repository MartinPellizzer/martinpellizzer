Gomphrena elata

Gomphrena elata, commonly known as the tall globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of spherical, pink or purple flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Gomphrena elata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to many landscapes.
