Gentiana heteroclita

Gentiana heteroclita, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Gentiana heteroclita is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
