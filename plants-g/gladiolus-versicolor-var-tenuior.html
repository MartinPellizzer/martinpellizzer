Gladiolus versicolor var. tenuior

Gladiolus versicolor var. tenuior, commonly known as the slender-flowered gladiolus, is a variant of the gladiolus species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated flower spikes that bear numerous small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, white, and lavender. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its elegant appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged blooming period, typically from late spring to early autumn.
