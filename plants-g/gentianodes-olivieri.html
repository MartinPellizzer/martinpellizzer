Gentianodes olivieri

Gentianodes olivieri, commonly known as the olive gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its flowers are tubular and often have a distinctive two-lipped structure.

Gentianodes olivieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
