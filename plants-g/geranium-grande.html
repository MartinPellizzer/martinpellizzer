Geranium grande

Geranium grande, commonly known as the large geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive white or yellow throat. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Geranium grande is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
