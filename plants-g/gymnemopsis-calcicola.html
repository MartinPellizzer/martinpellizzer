Gymnemopsis calcicola

Gymnemopsis calcicola, commonly known as the limestone bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe.

It is characterized by its unique bladder-like structures that aid in gas exchange and buoyancy. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving waters with high calcium content, often found in ponds and lakes. Its leaves are typically thin and ribbon-like, adapted for efficient light absorption.

Gymnemopsis calcicola plays a role in maintaining water quality by filtering nutrients and providing habitat for microorganisms.
