Gentianella luteomarginata

Gentianella luteomarginata, commonly known as the yellow-margin gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with yellow margins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
