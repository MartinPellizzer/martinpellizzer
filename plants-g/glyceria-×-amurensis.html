Glyceria × amurensis

Glyceria × amurensis, commonly known as the Amur grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, riverbanks, and other moist habitats.

Glyceria × amurensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
