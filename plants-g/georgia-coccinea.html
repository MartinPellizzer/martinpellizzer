Georgia coccinea

Georgia coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features strap-like leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue. Its bulbous root system is essential for its growth and survival, especially in well-drained soil.

Georgia coccinea thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
