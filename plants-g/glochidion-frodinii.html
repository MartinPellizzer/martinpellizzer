Glochidion frodinii

Glochidion frodinii, commonly known as the Malayan glabrous leaf.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually glabrous or nearly so. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Glochidion frodinii is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
