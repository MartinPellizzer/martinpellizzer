Geranium ceratophyllum

Geranium ceratophyllum, commonly known as water geranium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright red or pink flowers that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to wetland environments. The plant has a trailing growth habit with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils, making it a common sight in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
