Galactia rugosa var. genuina

Galactia rugosa var. genuina, commonly known as the rough galactia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white or pale purple flowers. Its seeds are encased in hairy, inflated pods that split open when mature.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local pollinators.
