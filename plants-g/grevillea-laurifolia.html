Grevillea laurifolia

Grevillea laurifolia, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces striking red or orange inflorescences that are composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to sandy soils and salt-laden air.

Grevillea laurifolia is often used in coastal landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
