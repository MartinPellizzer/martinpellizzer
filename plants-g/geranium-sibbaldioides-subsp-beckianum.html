Geranium sibbaldioides subsp. beckianum

Geranium sibbaldioides subsp.

beckianum, commonly known as the Beck's geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive serrated margin. Its showy flowers, which range from pink to red, bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
