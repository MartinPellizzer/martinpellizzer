Gordonia axillaris f. subemarginata

Gordonia axillaris f. subemarginata, commonly known as the narrow-margin gordonia, is a flowering plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically elliptical with a narrow margin, giving it its distinguishing name. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
