Gypsophila fastigiata subsp. pulposa

Gypsophila fastigiata subsp.

pulposa, commonly known as the clustered gypsophila, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid environments.
