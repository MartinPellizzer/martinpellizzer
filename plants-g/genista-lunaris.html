Genista lunaris

Genista lunaris, commonly known as the moonflower genista, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with slender, branching stems. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The flowers, which are usually pale yellow or white, bloom in late spring and early summer, adding a delicate beauty to its appearance.
