Gentiana maeulchanensis

Gentiana maeulchanensis, commonly known as the Korean gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Korea.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, while its stems are usually unbranched and upright.

Gentiana maeulchanensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
