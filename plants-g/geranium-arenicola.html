Geranium arenicola

Geranium arenicola, commonly known as the sand geranium, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Geranium arenicola is a hardy perennial that is well adapted to withstand drought and heat.
