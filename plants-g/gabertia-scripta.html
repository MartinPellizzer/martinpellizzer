Gabertia scripta

Gabertia scripta, commonly known as the slender marsh grass, is a perennial herb native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spike-like inflorescences and are pollinated by wind.

Gabertia scripta plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
