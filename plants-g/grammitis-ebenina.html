Grammitis ebenina

Grammitis ebenina, commonly known as the ebony fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, feather-like fronds that are usually dark green with a leathery texture. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction via spore dispersal.

Grammitis ebenina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
