Gaultheria mundula var. tanythrix

Gaultheria mundula var. tanythrix, commonly known as the slender wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, acidic environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
