Glycine tenerrima

Glycine tenerrima, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, purple, and white. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, heart-shaped leaflets.

Glycine tenerrima thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
