Gladiolus ringens

Gladiolus ringens, commonly known as the slender gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow spikes of brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with sword-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, come in shades of red, yellow, and orange, often with a distinctive ring-shaped pattern near the base of the tepals.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
