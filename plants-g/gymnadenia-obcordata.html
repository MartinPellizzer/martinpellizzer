Gymnadenia obcordata

Gymnadenia obcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive white midrib.

Gymnadenia obcordata is known for its short blooming period in late summer, making it a notable wildflower in its habitat.
