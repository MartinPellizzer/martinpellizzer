Grewiella globosa

Grewiella globosa, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky flower heads that resemble miniature pinecones, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, often with a bluish-gray hue.

Grewiella globosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
