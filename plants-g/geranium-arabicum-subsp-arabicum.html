Geranium arabicum subsp. arabicum

Geranium arabicum subsp.

arabicum, commonly known as the Arabian geranium, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial with upright stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
