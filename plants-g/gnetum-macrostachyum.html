Gnetum macrostachyum

Gnetum macrostachyum, commonly known as the giant lantern plant, is a member of the Gnetophyta division, which includes gymnosperms.

It is a large, woody shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, brightly colored capsule, which aids in seed dispersal by animals.

The plant has cultural and traditional uses in various indigenous communities for medicinal and practical purposes.
