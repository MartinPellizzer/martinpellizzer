Gentiana takedae

Gentiana takedae, commonly known as the Takeda gentian, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Gentiana takedae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
