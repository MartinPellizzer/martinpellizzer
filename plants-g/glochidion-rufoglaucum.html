Glochidion rufoglaucum

Glochidion rufoglaucum, commonly known as the red-leaf glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glaucous beneath. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits that release tiny seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its foliage is valued in traditional medicine and horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
