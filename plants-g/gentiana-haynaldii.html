Gentiana haynaldii

Gentiana haynaldii, commonly known as the Haynald's gentian, is a striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its deep blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in meadows and rocky slopes where it tolerates cold and poor soil conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Gentiana haynaldii is also noted for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional remedies for respiratory and digestive ailments.
