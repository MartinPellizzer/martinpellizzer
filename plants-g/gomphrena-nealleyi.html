Gomphrena nealleyi

Gomphrena nealleyi, commonly known as the Cape York gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, scale-like leaves and has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Gomphrena nealleyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
