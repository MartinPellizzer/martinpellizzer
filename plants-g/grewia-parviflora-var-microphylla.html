Grewia parviflora var. microphylla

Grewia parviflora var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf sugar bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, five-petaled flowers, which are followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
