Geum montanum subsp. eumontanum

Geum montanum subsp.

eumontanum, commonly known as the alpine avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is often considered a rare and vulnerable species.
