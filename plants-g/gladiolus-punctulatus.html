Gladiolus punctulatus

Gladiolus punctulatus, commonly known as the spotted gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a fan-like pattern, and the plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The flowers are also known for their strong, spicy fragrance.
