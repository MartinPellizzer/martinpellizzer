Geranium hakusanense

Geranium hakusanense, commonly known as the Hakusan geranium, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces vibrant pink or red flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its appeal in horticultural settings.
