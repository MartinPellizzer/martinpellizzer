Galearis rotundifolia

Galearis rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaved springbeauty, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features basal leaves that are round or heart-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and appear in clusters at the base of the plant in early spring.

Galearis rotundifolia is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often delicate flowers.
