Grammitis subreticulata

Grammitis subreticulata, commonly known as the reticulate fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually bright green and have a intricate network of veins, giving it a lace-like appearance.

Grammitis subreticulata is often found in cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental beauty in botanical gardens.
