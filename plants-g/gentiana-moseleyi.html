Gentiana moseleyi

Gentiana moseleyi, commonly known as the Sierra madre gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and mountain meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gentiana moseleyi is a perennial herb that thrives in cool, humid climates.
