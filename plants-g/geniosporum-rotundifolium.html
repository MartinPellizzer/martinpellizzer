Geniosporum rotundifolium

Geniosporum rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaf geniosporum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rounded, broad leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a branching structure that supports its herbaceous growth habit.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy habitats.
