Goodenia glandulifera

Goodenia glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy foliage and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its glandular structures on the leaves and stems are thought to aid in water conservation.

The species is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal dunes.
