Geranium tuberosum subsp. micranthum

Geranium tuberosum subsp.

micranthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa. It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinct, aromatic scent.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
