Gnaphalium sphacelatum

Gnaphalium sphacelatum, commonly known as the slender everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often surrounded by pale green bracts. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Its name derives from the Greek words "gnaphalos" meaning "to blow" and "sphacelatum" referring to its fragile, easily broken structure.
