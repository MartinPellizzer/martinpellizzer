Gleichenia pedalis var. glabra

Gleichenia pedalis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth Gleichenia, is a fern species characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and delicate, lance-shaped fronds.

The fronds are typically dimorphic, with sterile and fertile fronds differing in size and structure. The leaves are covered in a fine, smooth texture, giving the plant its varietal name "glabra," meaning "smooth" in Latin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its simple, elegant appearance and its role in ecological succession in temperate regions.
