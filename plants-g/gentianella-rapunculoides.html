Gentianella rapunculoides

Gentianella rapunculoides, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and has a compact, herbaceous growth habit. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are short and often branched.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
