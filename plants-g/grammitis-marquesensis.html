Grammitis marquesensis

Grammitis marquesensis, commonly known as the Marquesan fern, is a species of epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or branches in tropical rainforests. Its fronds are usually bright green and have a distinctive, intricate venation pattern.

Grammitis marquesensis is native to the Marquesas Islands in French Polynesia and is considered a rare and endemic species.
