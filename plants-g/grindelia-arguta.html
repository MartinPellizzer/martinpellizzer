Grindelia arguta

Grindelia arguta, commonly known as the Desert Gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly foliage and yellow flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Grindelia arguta is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
