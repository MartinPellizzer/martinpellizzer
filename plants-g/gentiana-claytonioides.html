Gentiana claytonioides

Gentiana claytonioides, commonly known as Clayton's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Gentiana claytonioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
