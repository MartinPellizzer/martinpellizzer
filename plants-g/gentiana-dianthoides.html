Gentiana dianthoides

Gentiana dianthoides, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are tubular with prominent blue petals.

Gentiana dianthoides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
