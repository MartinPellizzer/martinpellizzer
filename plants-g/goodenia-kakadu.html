Goodenia kakadu

Goodenia kakadu, commonly known as Kakadu goodenia, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers, which can range in color from white to pink, are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

Goodenia kakadu is an important component of the local flora and contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
