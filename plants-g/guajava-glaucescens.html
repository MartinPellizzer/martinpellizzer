Guajava glaucescens

Guajava glaucescens, commonly known as the blue guajava, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, blue-purple fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The fruit's unique color and flavor make it a valuable plant in both culinary and medicinal contexts.
