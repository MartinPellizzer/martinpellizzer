Garcinia bancana var. curtisii

Garcinia bancana var. curtisii, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into sour-tasting, orange-red fruits. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its fruit in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its botanical features include a woody stem, simple leaves, and a unique fruit structure with a thick, acidic pulp.

Garcinia bancana var. curtisii is also valued for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
