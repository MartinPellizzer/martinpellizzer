Gaultheria minuta

Gaultheria minuta, commonly known as the dwarf wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features small, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the spring. Its berries are red or black and contain a single seed, often used in traditional medicine.

Gaultheria minuta is valued for its aromatic foliage and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
