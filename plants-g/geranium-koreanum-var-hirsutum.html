Geranium koreanum var. hirsutum

Geranium koreanum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the Korean geranium, is a perennial herb native to Korea.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or woodland settings.
