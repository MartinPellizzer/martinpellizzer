Geranium lavergneanum var. cinerascens

Geranium lavergneanum var. cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its foliage tends to have a silvery or grayish hue, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
