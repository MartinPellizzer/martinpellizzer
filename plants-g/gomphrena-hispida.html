Gomphrena hispida

Gomphrena hispida, commonly known as the hispid gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiky, bract-like inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant is herbaceous, with hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is due to its long-lasting flowers, which remain vibrant for extended periods.
