Gymnosperma oppositifolium

Gymnosperma oppositifolium, commonly known as the opposite-leaf gymnosperm, is a unique plant characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement.

It belongs to the gymnosperm group, which typically includes plants that produce seeds without fruits. The leaves are typically needle-like and arranged in pairs along the stem. This species is often found in temperate regions with well-drained soil.

Its reproductive structures are cones, which contain the seeds necessary for propagation.
