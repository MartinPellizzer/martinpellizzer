Gamochaeta sphacelata

Gamochaeta sphacelata, commonly known as the narrowleaf false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and streambanks. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
