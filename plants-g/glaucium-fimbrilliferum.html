Glaucium fimbrilliferum

Glaucium fimbrilliferum, commonly known as the European prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and hairy, spiny stems. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Glaucium fimbrilliferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
