Goodenia cirrifica

Goodenia cirrifica, commonly known as the silky goodenia, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
