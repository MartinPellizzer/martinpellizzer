Gymnosphaera podophylla

Gymnosphaera podophylla, commonly known as the hairy podocarp, is a unique gymnosperm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, scale-like leaves and cone-like structures that bear its seeds. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species. Its reproductive structures are adapted to specific environmental conditions, often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Gymnosphaera podophylla plays a minor but ecologically significant role in its native ecosystems.
