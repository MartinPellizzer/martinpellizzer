Grossera major

Grossera major, commonly known as the giant spiderwort, is a striking perennial plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with large, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy.

Grossera major is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable water conditions.
