Geranium compar

Geranium compar, commonly known as the trailing geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Geranium compar is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
