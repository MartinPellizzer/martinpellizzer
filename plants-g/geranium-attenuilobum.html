Geranium attenuilobum

Geranium attenuilobum, commonly known as the slenderleaf geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that have a distinctive, slightly serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture.

Geranium attenuilobum thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
