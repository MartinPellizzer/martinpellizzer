Guzmania roseiflora

Guzmania roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, rose-like inflorescence that blooms in shades of pink and red. The plant features a rosette-shaped leaf structure that collects rainwater, creating a small ecosystem within its foliage. Its leaves are typically long, narrow, and covered in a waxy cuticle that helps retain moisture.

Guzmania roseiflora thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.
