Grevillea microcarpa

Grevillea microcarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to dry, coastal environments. It is often found in heathlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Grevillea microcarpa is also important for local wildlife, providing nectar for birds and insects.
