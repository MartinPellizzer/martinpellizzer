Galium asperifolium var. lasiocarpum

Galium asperifolium var. lasiocarpum, commonly known as the roughleaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial with slender stems and opposite, hairy leaves. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
