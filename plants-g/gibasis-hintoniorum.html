Gibasis hintoniorum

Gibasis hintoniorum, commonly known as the Hinton's ghostflower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the remote mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves and striking, bioluminescent flowers that emit a soft glow at night. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments with high altitude and limited sunlight exposure. Its unique adaptations suggest a specialized relationship with nocturnal pollinators.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, it remains a subject of ongoing botanical research.
