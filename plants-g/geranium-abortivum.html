Geranium abortivum

Geranium abortivum, commonly known as the small-flowered geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often glandular and have a distinctive, slightly aromatic scent.

Geranium abortivum is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
