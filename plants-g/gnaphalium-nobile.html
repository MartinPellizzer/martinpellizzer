Gnaphalium nobile

Gnaphalium nobile, commonly known as noble cudweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a woolly texture, and it produces small white to pale yellow flowers in the summer.

Gnaphalium nobile is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.
