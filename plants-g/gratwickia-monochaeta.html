Gratwickia monochaeta

Gratwickia monochaeta, commonly known as the single-stemmed grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa, characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
