Gentiana germanica subsp. carpatica

Gentiana germanica subsp.

carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and mountain meadows.

The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique ecological adaptations.
