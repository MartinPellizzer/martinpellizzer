Galium setaceum var. leiocarpum

Galium setaceum var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the silky bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a soft, velvety texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
