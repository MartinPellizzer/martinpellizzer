Gustavia fastuosa var. angustisepala

Gustavia fastuosa var. angustisepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
