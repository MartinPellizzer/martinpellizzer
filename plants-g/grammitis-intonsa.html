Grammitis intonsa

Grammitis intonsa, commonly known as the silver fern, is a fern species native to the tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically grayish-white in color due to the presence of a dense layer of trichomes. The plant's reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, which are protected by a specialized indusium. Grammitis intonsa thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in the study of fern morphology and ecological adaptation.
