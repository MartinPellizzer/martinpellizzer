Grevillea pinaster var. brevifolia

Grevillea pinaster var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Grevillea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking red or orange inflorescences that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Grevillea pinaster var. brevifolia is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
