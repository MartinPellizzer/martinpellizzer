Gomphrena ficoidea

Gomphrena ficoidea, commonly known as the pineapple flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, red or pink bracts that resemble a pineapple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Gomphrena ficoidea is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
