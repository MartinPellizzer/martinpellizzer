Grisebachia ciliaris subsp. multiglandulosa

Grisebachia ciliaris subsp.

multiglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular-leaved grisebachia, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats, often in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowering heads are small and clustered, producing yellow to orange flowers.

It is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid regions.
