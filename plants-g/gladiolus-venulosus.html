Gladiolus venulosus

Gladiolus venulosus, commonly known as the veiny gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that bear showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with intricate patterns and a distinctive veining on the petals. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its cultivation in various climates.
