Gentianella lineata

Gentianella lineata, commonly known as the narrowleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinct veining pattern.

Gentianella lineata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
