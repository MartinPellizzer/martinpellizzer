Glomera pulchra

Glomera pulchra, commonly known as the pretty glomera, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy blooms that range from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its colorful flowers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
