Gilia capitata subsp. pacifica

Gilia capitata subsp.

pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific gilia, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of North America. It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and produces showy, pink to purple flowers in terminal clusters.

The plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal climates with salt spray and strong winds.

The species is often found in dunes, cliffs, and other maritime habitats along the Pacific coast.
