Goodyera foliosa var. taoana

Goodyera foliosa var. taoana, commonly known as the Taoan lady's slipper, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, leathery leaves and its unique, slipper-shaped flower that is typically pale green or white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or branches in the understory of tropical forests. It has a short flowering period, usually in the spring or early summer, and requires careful cultivation due to its specific environmental needs.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts.
