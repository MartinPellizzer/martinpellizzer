Glycine meyeri

Glycine meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's soybean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It features pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical, green pods that contain several round, yellow seeds. Glycine meyeri is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.

It is also cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various culinary applications.
