Grindelia greenmanii

Grindelia greenmanii, commonly known as the greenman's greasebush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellowish-green flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, composite flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas and inland deserts.

Grindelia greenmanii is known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
