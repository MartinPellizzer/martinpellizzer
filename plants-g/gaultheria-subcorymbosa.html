Gaultheria subcorymbosa

Gaultheria subcorymbosa, commonly known as the alpine strawberry or mountain strawberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes mountains.

It produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with a leathery texture and is typically found in high-altitude, rocky habitats. It is valued for its aromatic foliage and its ability to thrive in cool, moist environments.

Gaultheria subcorymbosa is also noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
