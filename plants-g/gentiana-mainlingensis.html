Gentiana mainlingensis

Gentiana mainlingensis, commonly known as the Mainling gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
