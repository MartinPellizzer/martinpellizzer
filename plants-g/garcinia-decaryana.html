Garcinia decaryana

Garcinia decaryana, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with yellow or red on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by yellow or orange berries that are rich in bioactive compounds. Garcinia decaryana is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
