Gentiana andrewsii var. albiflora

Gentiana andrewsii var. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower gentian, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
