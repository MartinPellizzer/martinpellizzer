Grevillea wickhamii subsp. hispidula

Grevillea wickhamii subsp.

hispidula, commonly known as the hispid glabrous grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like inflorescences that produce vibrant red or orange flowers.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
