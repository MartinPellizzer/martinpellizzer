Gymnocalycium monvillei var. confusa

Gymnocalycium monvillei var. confusa, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
