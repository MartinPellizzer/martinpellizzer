Gnaphalium resedifolium

Gnaphalium resedifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to white in color. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
