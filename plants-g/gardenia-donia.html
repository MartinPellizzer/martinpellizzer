Gardenia donia

Gardenia donia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Gardenia donia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic blooms.
