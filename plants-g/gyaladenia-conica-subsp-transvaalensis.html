Gyaladenia conica subsp. transvaalensis

Gyaladenia conica subsp.

transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features slender, erect stems that bear whorled clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
