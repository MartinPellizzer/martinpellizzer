Garcinia cambodgiensis

Garcinia cambodgiensis, commonly known as garcinia or kokum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and yellow-orange fruits that contain a high concentration of hydroxycitric acid. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests. Its fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in food products.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
