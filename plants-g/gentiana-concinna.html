Gentiana concinna

Gentiana concinna, commonly known as the narrowleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue.

Gentiana concinna is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
