Gerbera anandria

Gerbera anandria, commonly known as the African gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, orange, and white, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Gerbera anandria thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.
