Geranium umbelliforme

Geranium umbelliforme, commonly known as the umbrella geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features lobed, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Geranium umbelliforme is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
