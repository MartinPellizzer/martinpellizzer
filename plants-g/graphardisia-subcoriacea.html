Graphardisia subcoriacea

Graphardisia subcoriacea, commonly known as the rough bark tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, rugged bark that gives it its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.
