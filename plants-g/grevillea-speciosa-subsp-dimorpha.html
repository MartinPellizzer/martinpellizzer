Grevillea speciosa subsp. dimorpha

Grevillea speciosa subsp.

dimorpha, commonly known as the red-stemmed Grevillea, is a shrub native to southwestern Western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive red stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is also an important species for local wildlife.
