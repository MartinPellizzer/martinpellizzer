Geranium kariense

Geranium kariense, commonly known as the Kari Mountain geranium, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of East Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Geranium kariense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
