Galium tinctorium var. filifolium

Galium tinctorium var. filifolium, commonly known as the silky bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers have been historically used in herbal medicine and dye-making due to their mild medicinal properties.
