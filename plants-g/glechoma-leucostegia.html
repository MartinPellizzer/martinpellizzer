Glechoma leucostegia

Glechoma leucostegia, commonly known as white-haired ground-ivy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Glechoma leucostegia is often used in herbal medicine for its purported soothing properties.
