Gentianella sturmiana

Gentianella sturmiana, commonly known as Sturm's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
