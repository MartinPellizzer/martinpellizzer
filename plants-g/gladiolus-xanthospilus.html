Gladiolus xanthospilus

Gladiolus xanthospilus, commonly known as the yellow-spotted gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant color and long-lasting beauty.
