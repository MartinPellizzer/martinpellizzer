Glechoma lagopsis

Glechoma lagopsis, commonly known as creeping Jenny, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and arranged opposite along its stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Glechoma lagopsis is known for its rapid spreading habit and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
