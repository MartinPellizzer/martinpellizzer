Garcinia quadrifaria var. chromocarpa

Garcinia quadrifaria var. chromocarpa, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is distinctive, with a bright red, segmented rind and a pale yellow, juicy pulp. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in both horticulture and botanical research.
