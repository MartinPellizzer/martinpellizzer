Guettarda crispiflora subsp. discolor

Guettarda crispiflora subsp.

discolor, commonly known as the white guettarda, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers.

The leaves are typically leathery and have a distinctive crinkled or curled appearance. This species thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
