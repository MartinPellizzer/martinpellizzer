Gnaphalium supinum var. subacaule

Gnaphalium supinum var. subacaule, commonly known as the creeping everlasting, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a prostrate or ascending stem that is often covered with fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale pink petals. Its leaves are linear to oblanceolate, usually covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
