Gynoxys subamplectens

Gynoxys subamplectens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf beauty, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
