Gypsophila arvensis

Gypsophila arvensis, commonly known as the field gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, branched inflorescences that bear small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It has a short stem with narrow, linear leaves and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Gypsophila arvensis is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
