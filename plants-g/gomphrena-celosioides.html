Gomphrena celosioides

Gomphrena celosioides, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spiky bracts that resemble small flames, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands or along roadsides. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by the colorful bracts.

Gomphrena celosioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
