Galactia austrofloridensis

Galactia austrofloridensis, commonly known as the Florida milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods that contain small, edible seeds, which are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and wetland environments.

Galactia austrofloridensis plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
