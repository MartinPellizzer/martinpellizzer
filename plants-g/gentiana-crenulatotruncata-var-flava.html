Gentiana crenulatotruncata var. flava

Gentiana crenulatotruncata var. flava, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and the flowers are often clustered in inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
