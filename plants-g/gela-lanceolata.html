Gela lanceolata

Gela lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gela, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand salty air and drought conditions.

Gela lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
