Galactia linearifolia

Galactia linearifolia, commonly known as the slender milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and compound leaves with linear, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. It is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic to some animals.

Galactia linearifolia thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
