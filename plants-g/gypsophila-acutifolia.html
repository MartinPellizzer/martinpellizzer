Gypsophila acutifolia

Gypsophila acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces numerous small, clustered flowers along its stems. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, giving it the species name "acutifolia," which means "sharp-leaved" in Latin.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden borders and floral arrangements.
