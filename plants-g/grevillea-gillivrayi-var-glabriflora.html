Grevillea gillivrayi var. glabriflora

Grevillea gillivrayi var. glabriflora, commonly known as the slender grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, which are a key feature for attracting pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in native gardens.
