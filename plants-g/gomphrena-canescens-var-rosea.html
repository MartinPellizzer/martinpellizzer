Gomphrena canescens var. rosea

Gomphrena canescens var. rosea, commonly known as the pink plume flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery bracts that range in color from pink to purple, giving it a unique ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale pink or white, surrounded by the showy bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
