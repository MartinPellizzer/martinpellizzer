Gentiana alopecuroides

Gentiana alopecuroides, commonly known as the woolly gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually covered in a dense, woolly coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
