Galphimia humilis

Galphimia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf galphimia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galphimia humilis prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for xeriscaping in arid environments.
