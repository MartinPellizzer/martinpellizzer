Gagea villarsii

Gagea villarsii, commonly known as the Alpine gagea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its small, grass-like leaves and clusters of delicate, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in open meadows and forested areas.

Gagea villarsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
