Gymnosporia montana var. parvifolia

Gymnosporia montana var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved mountain gymnosporia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces small, woody cones. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The species is noted for its resilience and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
