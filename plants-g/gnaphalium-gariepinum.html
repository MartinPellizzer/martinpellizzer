Gnaphalium gariepinum

Gnaphalium gariepinum, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, fluffy clusters. Its leaves are linear and covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
