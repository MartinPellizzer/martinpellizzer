Gomphocarpus oxytropis

Gomphocarpus oxytropis, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces a capsule-like fruit that contains numerous seeds with silky appendages, aiding in wind dispersal. It is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae), known for its latex-like sap.

This plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
