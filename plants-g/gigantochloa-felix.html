Gigantochloa felix

Gigantochloa felix, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a tall, robust grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strong, hollow stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green. The plant is well adapted to tropical climates and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional construction and crafts.

Gigantochloa felix is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
