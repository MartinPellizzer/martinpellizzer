Geranium trilophum

Geranium trilophum, commonly known as the three-lobed geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with three distinct lobes, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
