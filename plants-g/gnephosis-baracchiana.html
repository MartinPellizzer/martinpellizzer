Gnephosis baracchiana

Gnephosis baracchiana, commonly known as the Baracchian ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pale greenish-white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, showcasing its epiphytic nature. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and it requires a delicate balance of moisture and light to thrive.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
