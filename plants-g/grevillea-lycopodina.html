Grevillea lycopodina

Grevillea lycopodina, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for native landscaping.
