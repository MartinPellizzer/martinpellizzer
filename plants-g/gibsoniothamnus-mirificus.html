Gibsoniothamnus mirificus

Gibsoniothamnus mirificus, commonly known as the golden thamnus, is a perennial shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub habitats. Its leaves are leathery and aromatic, with a distinctive silvery-white underside.

Gibsoniothamnus mirificus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
