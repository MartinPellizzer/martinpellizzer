Gagea villosa f. hybrida

Gagea villosa f. hybrida, commonly known as the hairy gagea, is a hybrid plant species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, bulbous structure and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically narrow, grass-like, and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This plant is often found in the northern regions of North America, where it contributes to the understory flora of forest ecosystems.
