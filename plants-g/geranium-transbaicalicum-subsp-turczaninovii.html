Geranium transbaicalicum subsp. turczaninovii

Geranium transbaicalicum subsp.

turczaninovii, commonly known as the Transbaikal geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, arranged in cymes, and bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitats.
