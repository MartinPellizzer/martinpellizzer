Glottiphyllum semicylindricum

Glottiphyllum semicylindricum, commonly known as the semicylindrical spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its distinctive, semi-cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
