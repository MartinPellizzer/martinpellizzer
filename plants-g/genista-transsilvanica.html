Genista transsilvanica

Genista transsilvanica, commonly known as Transylvanian broom, is a flowering plant native to Eastern Europe, particularly Romania.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers in early summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista transsilvanica is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.
