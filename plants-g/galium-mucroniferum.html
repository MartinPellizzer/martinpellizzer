Galium mucroniferum

Galium mucroniferum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive narrow, linear shape.

Galium mucroniferum is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
