Gentiana fortunei

Gentiana fortunei, commonly known as the Chinese gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat hairy.

Gentiana fortunei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
