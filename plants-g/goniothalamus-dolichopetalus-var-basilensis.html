Goniothalamus dolichopetalus var. basilensis

Goniothalamus dolichopetalus var. basilensis, commonly known as the Basil's long-petaled goniothalamus, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Magnoliaceae family, characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with long, slender petals that attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.
