Gardenia citriodora

Gardenia citriodora, commonly known as the lemon-scented gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that emit a subtle lemon-like fragrance. The plant produces clusters of waxy, white flowers with a strong, sweet aroma. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Gardenia citriodora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and pleasant scent.
