Goodyera maximowicziana f. commelinoides

Goodyera maximowicziana f. commelinoides, commonly known as the Commelinoid form of the lady's slipper orchid, is a rare and unique variant of the species.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flower structure. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are typically broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

This form is particularly noted for its morphological differences from the typical species, including a more elongated floral structure.
