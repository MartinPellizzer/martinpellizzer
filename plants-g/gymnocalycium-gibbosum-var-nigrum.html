Gymnocalycium gibbosum var. nigrum

Gymnocalycium gibbosum var. nigrum, commonly known as the black globose cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to Argentina.

It features a dark green to nearly black stem that is covered in dense, white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
