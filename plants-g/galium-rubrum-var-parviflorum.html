Galium rubrum var. parviflorum

Galium rubrum var. parviflorum, commonly known as small-flower bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that often lie prostrate on the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
