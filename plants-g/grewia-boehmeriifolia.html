Grewia boehmeriifolia

Grewia boehmeriifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. These flowers are followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
