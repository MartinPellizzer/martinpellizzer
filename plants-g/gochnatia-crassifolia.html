Gochnatia crassifolia

Gochnatia crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved gochnatia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically leathery and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed habitats.

Gochnatia crassifolia is valued for its ornamental potential and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
