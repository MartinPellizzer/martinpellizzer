Gypsophila subaphylla

Gypsophila subaphylla, commonly known as the lesser flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Gypsophila subaphylla is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
