Gomphrena nigricans

Gomphrena nigricans, commonly known as black plume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery bracts that range in color from deep red to black, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often surrounded by the showy bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Gomphrena nigricans is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
