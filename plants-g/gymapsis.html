Gymapsis

Gymapsis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow or golden-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Gymapsis has lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
