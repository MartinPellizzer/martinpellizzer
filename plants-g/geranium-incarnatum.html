Geranium incarnatum

Geranium incarnatum, commonly known as the red geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Geranium incarnatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
