Grevillea crowleyae

Grevillea crowleyae, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Western Australia.

It features narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited to coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and coastal landscapes.
