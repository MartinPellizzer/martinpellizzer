Godoya spathulata

Godoya spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed godoya, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in horticultural studies due to its ornamental value and adaptability.

However, it is not widely cultivated outside its native habitat.
