Gagea Stipitata Var. Ova

Ovate gagea, scientifically known as Gagea stipitata var. ova, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of North America. It is characterized by its ovate-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. This plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or alpine environments. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this unique botanical gem from habitat loss and environmental threats.
