Gumira esculenta

Gumira esculenta, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking spadix inflorescence. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in starch and used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.

Due to its nutritional value and adaptability, it is an important crop in some regions of sub-Saharan Africa.
