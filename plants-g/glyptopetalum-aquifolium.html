Glyptopetalum aquifolium

Glyptopetalum aquifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Glyptopetalum aquifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
