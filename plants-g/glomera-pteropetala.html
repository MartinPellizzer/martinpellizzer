Glomera pteropetala

Glomera pteropetala, commonly known as the winged orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, butterfly-like petals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its flowers are often pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

The botanical features of Glomera pteropetala make it a unique and visually striking member of the orchid family.
