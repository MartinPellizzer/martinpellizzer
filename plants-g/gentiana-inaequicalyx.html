Gentiana inaequicalyx

Gentiana inaequicalyx, commonly known as the unequal calyx gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Gentiana inaequicalyx is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid color and resilience.
