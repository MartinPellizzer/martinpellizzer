Gentiana pringlei

Gentiana pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's gentian, is a striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Gentiana pringlei is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
