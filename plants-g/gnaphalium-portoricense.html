Gnaphalium portoricense

Gnaphalium portoricense, commonly known as the Brazilian feather grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with small, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces copious amounts of fine, white, woolly hairs, which give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and floral display.
