Galeandra euglossa

Galeandra euglossa, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Galeandra, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers with intricate structures, including a labellum that is often adorned with specialized markings. Its growth habit is typically epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without being parasitic.

Galeandra euglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
