Gentiana subintricata

Gentiana subintricata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while the stem is usually unbranched and covered in fine hairs.

Gentiana subintricata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
