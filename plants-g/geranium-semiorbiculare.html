Geranium semiorbiculare

Geranium semiorbiculare, commonly known as the narrow-leaf geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are typically narrow and oval-shaped with a slightly wavy margin. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Geranium semiorbiculare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
