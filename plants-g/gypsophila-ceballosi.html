Gypsophila ceballosi

Gypsophila ceballosi, commonly known as the Ceballos' gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh, high-altitude conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Gypsophila ceballosi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
