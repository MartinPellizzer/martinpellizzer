Gentiana coquimbensis

Gentiana coquimbensis, commonly known as the Coquimbo gentian, is a flowering plant native to Chile.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched.

Gentiana coquimbensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
