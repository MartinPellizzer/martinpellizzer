Guarea anomala

Guarea anomala, commonly known as the Malabar ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-yellow in color, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

The tree is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
