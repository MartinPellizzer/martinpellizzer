Gastrolobium microcarpum

Gastrolobium microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spikes. Its fruits are small, woody pods that contain seeds with a hard, dark coat.

Gastrolobium microcarpum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
