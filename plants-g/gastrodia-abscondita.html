Gastrodia abscondita

Gastrodia abscondita, commonly known as the hidden stomach orchid, is a rare and enigmatic member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid that produces a single, fleshy, club-shaped pseudobulb, which stores nutrients and water. The plant is characterized by its lack of true leaves, relying instead on its pseudobulb for photosynthesis. It blooms with a single, intricate flower that has a distinctive, often fragrant, structure.

Due to its elusive nature and limited habitat, Gastrodia abscondita is considered a rare and poorly understood species in botanical studies.
