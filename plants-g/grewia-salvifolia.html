Grewia salvifolia

Grewia salvifolia, commonly known as the sour fig or African sour plum, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. The plant's fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when ripe and is known for its tart flavor. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, often with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
