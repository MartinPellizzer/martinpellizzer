Gastrochilus ochroleucus

Gastrochilus ochroleucus, commonly known as the yellow-lipped orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the spring and early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
