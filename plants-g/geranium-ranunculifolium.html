Geranium ranunculifolium

Geranium ranunculifolium, commonly known as the white geranium, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Geranium ranunculifolium is often found in meadows, woodlands, and rocky habitats.
