Gaya gaudichaudiana var. tarijensis

Gaya gaudichaudiana var. tarijensis, commonly known as the Tarijensis Gaya, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink, and it produces small, hard fruits that contain seeds.

This variety is noted for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
