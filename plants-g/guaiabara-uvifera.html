Guaiabara uvifera

Guaiabara uvifera, commonly known as the Brazilian grapefruit or *Punicaceae* species, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round fruits with a thick, leathery skin and a juicy, tart pulp. The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.
