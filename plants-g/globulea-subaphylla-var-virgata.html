Globulea subaphylla var. virgata

Globulea subaphylla var. virgata, commonly known as the golden globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features basal leaves that are lobed and hairy, with a distinct yellowish-green coloration. The plant produces showy, globular inflorescences composed of numerous small, yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid habitats.
