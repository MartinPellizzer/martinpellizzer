Gnetum gnemon var. tenerum

Gnetum gnemon var. tenerum, commonly known as the tropical gnetum, is a member of the Gnetophyta division, characterized by its unique combination of gymnosperm and angiosperm traits.

It is a woody plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in tropical rainforests. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and has a distinctive, often branched stem with a rough, bark-like surface. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture, adapted to the humid, shaded environments it inhabits.

The species is also notable for its production of a resinous sap, which has been used traditionally in various applications.
