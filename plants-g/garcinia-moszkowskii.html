Garcinia moszkowskii

Garcinia moszkowskii, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and yellow-orange fruits. The fruit is covered in a thick, waxy coating and contains a single large seed. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
