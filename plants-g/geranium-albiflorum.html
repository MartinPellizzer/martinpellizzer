Geranium albiflorum

Geranium albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered geranium, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of small, white flowers with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and long blooming period.
