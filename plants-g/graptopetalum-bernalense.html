Graptopetalum bernalense

Graptopetalum bernalense, commonly known as the Bernal's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the rocky habitats of Baja California, Mexico.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the spring and early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
