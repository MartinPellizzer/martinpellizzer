Grevillea buxifolia subsp. sphacelata

Grevillea buxifolia subsp.

sphacelata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, glossy, evergreen leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. Its flowers are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
