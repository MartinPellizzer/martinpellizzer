Grevillea ilicifolia var. quercina

Grevillea ilicifolia var. quercina, commonly known as the corkwood grevillea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that resembles the texture of cork, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
