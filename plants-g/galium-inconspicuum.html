Galium inconspicuum

Galium inconspicuum, commonly known as the small wood-sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green coloration. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and ecologically valuable plant in native plant gardens.
