Glycosmis cochinchinensis

Glycosmis cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese gooseberry, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, small, yellowish-green berries. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and white, often clustered in inflorescences, and it produces fruit that is typically harvested when fully mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
