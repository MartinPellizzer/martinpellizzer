Gladiolus spilanthus

Gladiolus spilanthus, commonly known as the spotted gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers typically come in shades of white, pink, and purple, with distinctive spots or streaks on the petals. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
