Gymnotrix paupera

Gymnotrix paupera, commonly known as the poor man's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers and unique, elongated floral structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific fungal associations for nutrient uptake. Gymnotrix paupera is known for its limited distribution and is considered endangered due to habitat destruction and over-collection.

Its botanical study is significant for understanding orchid evolution and ecological adaptations in tropical ecosystems.
