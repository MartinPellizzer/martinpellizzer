Guidonia elliptica

Guidonia elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic guidonia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Guidonia elliptica is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
