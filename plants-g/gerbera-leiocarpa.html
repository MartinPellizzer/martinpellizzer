Gerbera leiocarpa

Gerbera leiocarpa, commonly known as the white gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats and prefers well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

Gerbera leiocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
