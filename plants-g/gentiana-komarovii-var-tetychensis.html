Gentiana komarovii var. tetychensis

Gentiana komarovii var. tetychensis, commonly known as the Tetychna gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vivid floral display.
