Grewia ulmifolia

Grewia ulmifolia, commonly known as the African wolfberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features simple, ovate leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.

Grewia ulmifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
