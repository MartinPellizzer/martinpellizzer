Guzmania coriostachya

Guzmania coriostachya, commonly known as the red-tipped guzmania, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that often displays red or orange hues. The plant features a rosette of sword-shaped leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms and small insects. Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Guzmania coriostachya thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.
