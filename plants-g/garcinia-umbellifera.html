Garcinia umbellifera

Garcinia umbellifera, commonly known as the white garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are typically harvested when fully mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The botanical features of Garcinia umbellifera make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
