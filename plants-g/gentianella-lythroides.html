Gentianella lythroides

Gentianella lythroides, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Gentianella lythroides is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
