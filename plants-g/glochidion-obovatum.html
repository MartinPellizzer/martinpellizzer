Glochidion obovatum

Glochidion obovatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its obovate, glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Glochidion obovatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its attractive foliage.
