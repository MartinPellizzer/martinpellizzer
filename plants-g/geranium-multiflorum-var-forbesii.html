Geranium multiflorum var. forbesii

Geranium multiflorum var. forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' geranium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems that are hairy and branched, supporting clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
