Geranium radicatum

Geranium radicatum, commonly known as the sand geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as crevices and slopes, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple, with five petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Geranium radicatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
