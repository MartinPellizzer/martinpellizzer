Gantelbua urens

Gantelbua urens, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant contains fine, venomous hairs that cause a stinging sensation upon contact with the skin. It thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Gantelbua urens is known for its edible leaves when cooked and has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
