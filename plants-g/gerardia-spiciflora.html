Gerardia spiciflora

Gerardia spiciflora, commonly known as the spikeflower gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or coastal environments. The plant features upright stems with clusters of small, spiky flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gerardia spiciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
