Gladiolus quilimanensis

Gladiolus quilimanensis, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tanzania.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically blooms in the cooler months, producing vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native environment.
