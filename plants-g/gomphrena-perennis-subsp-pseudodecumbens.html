Gomphrena perennis subsp. pseudodecumbens

Gomphrena perennis subsp.

pseudodecumbens, commonly known as the everlasting flower or globe amaranth, is a perennial plant native to South America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, long-lasting blooms that range from pink to red and purple.

The plant produces spherical, showy flower heads that remain intact even after drying, making it popular in floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
