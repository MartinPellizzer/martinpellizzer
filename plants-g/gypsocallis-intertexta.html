Gypsocallis intertexta

Gypsocallis intertexta, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure that resembles a spider, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive, elongated lip that aids in pollination.

Gypsocallis intertexta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
