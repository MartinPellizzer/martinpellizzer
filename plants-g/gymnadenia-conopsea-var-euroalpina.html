Gymnadenia conopsea var. euroalpina

Gymnadenia conopsea var. euroalpina, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer.

This variant is distinguished by its slightly smaller size and more compact growth compared to other Gymnadenia species.
