Galium parisiense subvar. litigiosum

Galium parisiense subvar. litigiosum, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, sprawling stems covered in fine, silvery hairs, which give it its common name. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
