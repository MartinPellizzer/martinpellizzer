Gentiana californica

Gentiana californica, commonly known as the California gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and meadows. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana californica is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
