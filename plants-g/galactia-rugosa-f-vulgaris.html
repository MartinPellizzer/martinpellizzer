Galactia rugosa f. vulgaris

Galactia rugosa f. vulgaris, commonly known as the common galactia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces spherical, dry fruits called legumes, which contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Galactia rugosa f. vulgaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in the soil.
