Gymnocalycium chiquitanum var. hammerschmidii

Gymnocalycium chiquitanum var. hammerschmidii, commonly known as the Hammerschmid's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Peru.

It is characterized by its globular or cylindrical stem, which is covered in spines and often has a greenish or bluish hue. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems for survival in arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
