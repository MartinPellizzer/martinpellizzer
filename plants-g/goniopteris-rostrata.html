Goniopteris rostrata

Goniopteris rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, bipinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to two meters. The fronds are typically light green and have a distinctive, elongated shape with a narrow, tapered end. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in water gardens and bog landscapes.
