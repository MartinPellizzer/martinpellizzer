Gentiana fiebrigii

Gentiana fiebrigii, commonly known as the fever gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
