Globularia alypum var. pycnantha

Globularia alypum var. pycnantha, commonly known as the sticky globularia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, spherical clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a sticky resin, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact form and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to drought-tolerant gardens.
