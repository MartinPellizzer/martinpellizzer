Geum camporum

Geum camporum, commonly known as meadow avens, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, aggregate fruits that disperse its seeds, contributing to its natural distribution.
