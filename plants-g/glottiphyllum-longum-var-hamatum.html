Glottiphyllum longum var. hamatum

Glottiphyllum longum var. hamatum, commonly known as the hairy glottiphyllum, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The leaves are typically blue-green in color and can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. It produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value to the plant.

This species is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
