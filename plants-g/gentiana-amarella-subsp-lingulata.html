Gentiana amarella subsp. lingulata

Gentiana amarella subsp.

lingulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and compact growth habit. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
