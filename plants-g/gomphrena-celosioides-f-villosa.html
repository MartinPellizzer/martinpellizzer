Gomphrena celosioides f. villosa

Gomphrena celosioides f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its showy, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, fluffy bracts that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The hairy foliage, as suggested by its cultivar name, adds a textured element to its visual appeal.
