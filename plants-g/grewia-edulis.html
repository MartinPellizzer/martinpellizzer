Grewia edulis

Grewia edulis, commonly known as the sour fig, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that are typically eaten raw or used in jams and beverages. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The fruit is notable for its tart flavor and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
