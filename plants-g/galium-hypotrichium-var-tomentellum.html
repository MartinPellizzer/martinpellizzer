Galium hypotrichium var. tomentellum

Galium hypotrichium var. tomentellum, commonly known as the woolly bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of North America.

It features slender, creeping stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and also covered in fine, soft hairs.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
