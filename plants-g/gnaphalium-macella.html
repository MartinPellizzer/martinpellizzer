Gnaphalium macella

Gnaphalium macella, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its small, clustered flowers and silvery-gray foliage. Native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, it thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
