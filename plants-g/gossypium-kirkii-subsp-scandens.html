Gossypium kirkii subsp. scandens

Gossypium kirkii subsp.

scandens, commonly known as the climbing cotton or African cotton, is a species native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fluffy, white cotton fibers.

The plant typically has alternate, ovate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested or disturbed habitats.

Its fibers are used for textile production, similar to other cotton species.
