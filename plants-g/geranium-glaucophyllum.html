Geranium glaucophyllum

Geranium glaucophyllum, commonly known as the silver geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features hairy, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value contribute to its appeal in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
