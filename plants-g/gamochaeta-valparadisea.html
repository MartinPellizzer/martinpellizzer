Gamochaeta valparadisea

Gamochaeta valparadisea, commonly known as the velvetleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dark green, hairy leaves and produces purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Gamochaeta valparadisea is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
