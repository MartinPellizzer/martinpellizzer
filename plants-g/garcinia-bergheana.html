Garcinia bergheana

Garcinia bergheana, commonly known as the African mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, round fruits with a thick, segmented rind. The fruit's interior contains a juicy, segmented aril that is often consumed for its sweet and tangy flavor.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in various tropical regions.
