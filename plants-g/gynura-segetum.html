Gynura segetum

Gynura segetum, commonly known as the horse leach or Chinese spinach, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which are used in various Asian cuisines.

Gynura segetum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
