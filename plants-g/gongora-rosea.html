Gongora rosea

Gongora rosea, commonly known as the rose orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers, which resemble roses, hence its common name. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces pendulous inflorescences with fragrant blooms. Gongora rosea is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in the diverse orchid flora of the Andes.
