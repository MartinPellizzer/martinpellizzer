Grona trichostachya

Grona trichostachya, commonly known as the hairy panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as savannas and disturbed areas.

Grona trichostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
