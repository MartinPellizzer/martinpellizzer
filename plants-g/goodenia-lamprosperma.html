Goodenia lamprosperma

Goodenia lamprosperma, commonly known as the shining goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often blue or purple, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, seed-like fruits that are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
