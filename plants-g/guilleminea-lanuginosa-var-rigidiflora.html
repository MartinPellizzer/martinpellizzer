Guilleminea lanuginosa var. rigidiflora

Guilleminea lanuginosa var. rigidiflora, commonly known as the woolly guillemia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
