Gratiola brachiata

Gratiola brachiata, commonly known as the forked yellowroot, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its branched, yellowish roots and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Gratiola brachiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
