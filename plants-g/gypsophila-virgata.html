Gypsophila virgata

Gypsophila virgata, commonly known as the baby's breath or sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences composed of numerous small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in floral arrangements due to its airy appearance and long-lasting blooms.

Gypsophila virgata is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and is known for its drought tolerance.
