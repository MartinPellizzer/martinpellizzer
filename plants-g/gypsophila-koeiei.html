Gypsophila koeiei

Gypsophila koeiei, commonly known as the Korean lace flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched inflorescences composed of numerous small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for alpine or rock garden environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms in the summer months.

Gypsophila koeiei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscaping and floral arrangements.
