Geranium incisum

Geranium incisum, commonly known as the cut-leaf geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, pinnately lobed leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Geranium incisum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
