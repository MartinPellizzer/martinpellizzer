Grastidium ingratum

Grastidium ingratum, commonly known as the humble spirea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Native to temperate regions, it thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate floral display and easy maintenance.
