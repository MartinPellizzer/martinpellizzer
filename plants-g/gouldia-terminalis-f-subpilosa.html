Gouldia terminalis f. subpilosa

Gouldia terminalis f. subpilosa, commonly known as the pale-flowered gouldia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple hue at the base. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture that gives it the 'subpilosa' designation.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in garden settings for its visual appeal.
