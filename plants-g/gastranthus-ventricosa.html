Gastranthus ventricosa

Gastranthus ventricosa, commonly known as the balloon plant, is a succulent species native to South America.

It is characterized by its swollen, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically small and may be covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Gastranthus ventricosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
