Gliricidia lambii

Gliricidia lambii, commonly known as the white gliricidia, is a fast-growing leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods, and the tree is often used in agroforestry and as a forage crop.

Gliricidia lambii is also valued for its hard wood and medicinal properties in traditional practices.
