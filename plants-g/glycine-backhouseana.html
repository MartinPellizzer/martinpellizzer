Glycine backhouseana

Glycine backhouseana, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a climbing legume native to Australia.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Glycine backhouseana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
