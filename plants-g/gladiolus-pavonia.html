Gladiolus pavonia

Gladiolus pavonia, commonly known as the scarlet gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are typically 3 to 5 inches long and bloom in summer.

Gladiolus pavonia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors.
