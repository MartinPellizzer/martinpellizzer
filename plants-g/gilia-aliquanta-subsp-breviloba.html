Gilia aliquanta subsp. breviloba

Gilia aliquanta subsp.

breviloba, commonly known as the narrowleaf gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
