Geranium sessiliflorum var. typicum

Geranium sessiliflorum var. typicum, commonly known as the clustered geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red. The leaves are typically rounded and slightly hairy, with a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
