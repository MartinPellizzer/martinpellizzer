Gnaphalium luteoalbum var. glomeratum

Gnaphalium luteoalbum var. glomeratum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers and hairy stems, which are typical features of the genus Gnaphalium. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along streambanks, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a soft, downy texture.

This variety is often found in Europe and parts of Asia, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
