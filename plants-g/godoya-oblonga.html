Godoya oblonga

Godoya oblonga, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and it produces clusters of fragrant, colorful blooms.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
