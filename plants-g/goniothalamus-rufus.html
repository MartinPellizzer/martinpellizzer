Goniothalamus rufus

Goniothalamus rufus, commonly known as the red-flowered talaud, is a member of the Annonaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that contains several seeds.

Goniothalamus rufus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
