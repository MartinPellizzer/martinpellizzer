Grammitis sinuosa

Grammitis sinuosa, commonly known as the sinuous maidenhair fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided, and often sinuous fronds. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrowly linear to lanceolate, with a leathery texture and a distinctive, curling appearance.

Grammitis sinuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
