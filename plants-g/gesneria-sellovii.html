Gesneria sellovii

Gesneria sellovii, commonly known as the Sello's gesneria, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green and glossy. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Gesneria sellovii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
