Gentianella magnifica

Gentianella magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows, where it thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers are characterized by their deep blue color and five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or orange spot at the base.

Gentianella magnifica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
