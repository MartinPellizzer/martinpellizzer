Guildingia psidioides

Guildingia psidioides, commonly known as the silverleaf guava, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits with a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Guildingia psidioides is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
