Gnaphalium caffrum

Gnaphalium caffrum, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, fluffy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. It is known for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, making it drought-tolerant.

The plant's flowering period usually occurs during the summer months, adding a delicate ornamental quality to its appearance.
