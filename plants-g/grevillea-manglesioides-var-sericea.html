Grevillea manglesioides var. sericea

Grevillea manglesioides var. sericea, commonly known as the silky grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in suitable conditions.
