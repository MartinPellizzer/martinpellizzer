Grona rubra var. macrocarpa

Grona rubra var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited red grapevine, is a variant of the grapevine species characterized by its distinctive, large berries.

This plant is native to certain regions of South America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The berries, which are typically red or purple, are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant's adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and vineyards.
