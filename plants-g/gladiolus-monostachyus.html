Gladiolus monostachyus

Gladiolus monostachyus, commonly known as the single-stem gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features tall, upright stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped, brightly colored flowers in shades of red, yellow, and white. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike of flowers, which is characteristic of the genus Gladiolus.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
