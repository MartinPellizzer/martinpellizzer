Geissorhiza rupestris

Geissorhiza rupestris, commonly known as the rock lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant produces striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to its arid environment.

Geissorhiza rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
