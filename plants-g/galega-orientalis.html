Galega orientalis

Galega orientalis, commonly known as the goat's rue, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in dry, calcareous soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Galega orientalis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
