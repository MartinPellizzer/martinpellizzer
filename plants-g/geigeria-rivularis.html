Geigeria rivularis

Geigeria rivularis, commonly known as the river geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and stream sides. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, clustered in terminal umbels, and range in color from pink to red.

Geigeria rivularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with adequate water supply.
