Gentiana oreodoxa

Gentiana oreodoxa, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of down.

Gentiana oreodoxa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
