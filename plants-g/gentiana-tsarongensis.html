Gentiana tsarongensis

Gentiana tsarongensis, commonly known as the Tsarong gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its showy, often blue or violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of down, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
