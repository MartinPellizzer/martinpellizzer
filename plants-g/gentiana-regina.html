Gentiana regina

Gentiana regina, commonly known as the Queen Gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Gentiana regina is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
