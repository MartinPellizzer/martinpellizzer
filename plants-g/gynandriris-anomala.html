Gynandriris anomala

Gynandriris anomala, commonly known as the golden bower vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are often used in traditional floral arrangements. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are smooth and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Gynandriris anomala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
