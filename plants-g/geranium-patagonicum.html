Geranium patagonicum

Geranium patagonicum, commonly known as the Patagonian geranium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Geranium patagonicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
