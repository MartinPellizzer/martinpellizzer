Gethosyne

Gethosyne, commonly known as the "golden star" or "sunflower of the desert," is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Gethosyne produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into fluffy, seed-filled pods.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
