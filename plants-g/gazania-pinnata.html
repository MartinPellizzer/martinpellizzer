Gazania pinnata

Gazania pinnata, commonly known as the narrowleaf gazania, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy yellow and orange daisy-like flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gazania pinnata is known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
