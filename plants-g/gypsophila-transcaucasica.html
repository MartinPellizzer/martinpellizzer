Gypsophila transcaucasica

Gypsophila transcaucasica, commonly known as the Transcaucasian laceflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms during the summer months.

Gypsophila transcaucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
