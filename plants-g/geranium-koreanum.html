Geranium koreanum

Geranium koreanum, commonly known as the Korean geranium, is a perennial herb native to East Asia, particularly Korea.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from summer to autumn. Geranium koreanum thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its use in both landscape design and traditional herbal medicine.
