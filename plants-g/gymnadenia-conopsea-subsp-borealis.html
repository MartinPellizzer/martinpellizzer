Gymnadenia conopsea subsp. borealis

Gymnadenia conopsea subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the northern grasspinks, is a perennial orchid native to the northern regions of North America. It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and spikes of pale pink to white flowers.

This subspecies thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers are typically arranged in a loose spike and bloom in late summer.

The plant is notable for its ability to form dense colonies and is often found in association with other orchid species in its native range.
