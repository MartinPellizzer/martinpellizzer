Gleichenia mendellii

Gleichenia mendellii, commonly known as the Mendell's fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically arranged in a compact, rosette-like structure. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in small, rounded structures called sori. Gleichenia mendellii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant component of its native habitats.
