Gymnothalamium

Gymnothalamium, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves and its unique, elongated flower spike that often appears to float without support. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Gymnothalamium thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it is known for its delicate and ephemeral beauty.
