Gaillardia arizonica var. pringlei

Gaillardia arizonica var. pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that produces vibrant, daisy-like flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
