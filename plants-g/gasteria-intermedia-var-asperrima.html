Gasteria intermedia var. asperrima

Gasteria intermedia var. asperrima, commonly known as the rough-leaved gasteria, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a layer of white, waxy bloom. The leaves are typically dark green with a textured, rough surface, and they are arranged in a compact, compact, and upright manner. This plant produces small, white flowers on tall, slender stems during the summer months.

It is well-suited for dry, well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it an ideal choice for rock gardens or as a container plant.
