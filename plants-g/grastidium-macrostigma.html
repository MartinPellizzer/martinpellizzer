Grastidium macrostigma

Grastidium macrostigma, commonly known as the broad-stigma grass, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. Grastidium macrostigma thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora, though it is not widely cultivated.
