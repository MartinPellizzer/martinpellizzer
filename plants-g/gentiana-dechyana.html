Gentiana dechyana

Gentiana dechyana, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is usually unbranched and erect.

The flowers are tubular with a prominent blue or purple corolla, often adorned with yellow or white markings.
