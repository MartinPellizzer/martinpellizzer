Gentianella thomsonii

Gentianella thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with five petals and prominent yellow veins, and bloom during the summer months.

Thomson's gentian is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
