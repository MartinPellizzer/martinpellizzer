Glyceria arctica var. laxa

Glyceria arctica var. laxa, commonly known as Arctic glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to cold climates.

It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor environments such as tundra and wet meadows. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

This variant is adapted to withstand harsh Arctic conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.
