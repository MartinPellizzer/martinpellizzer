Garrya ovata var. lindheimeri

Garrya ovata var. lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, glandular hairs that cover the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and turn reddish in the autumn.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant.
