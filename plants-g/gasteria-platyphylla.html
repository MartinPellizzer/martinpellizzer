Gasteria platyphylla

Gasteria platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf gasteria, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a blue-gray or greenish hue. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers on tall, slender stems during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and requiring minimal watering.

Gasteria platyphylla is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation due to its hardiness and attractive appearance.
