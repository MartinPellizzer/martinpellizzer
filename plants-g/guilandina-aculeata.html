Guilandina aculeata

Guilandina aculeata, commonly known as the spiny legume or wild soursop, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and thorny growth habit. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a round, spiky berry that contains a single large, inedible seed.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
