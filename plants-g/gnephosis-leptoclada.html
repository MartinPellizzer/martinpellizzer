Gnephosis leptoclada

Gnephosis leptoclada, commonly known as the slender leaf bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap aquatic prey. The plant possesses thin, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface. It reproduces through both sexual and asexual means, often spreading via runners.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studying aquatic plant adaptations and carnivory.
