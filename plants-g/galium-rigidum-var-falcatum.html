Galium rigidum var. falcatum

Galium rigidum var. falcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, with a slightly wavy margin, and it produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters.

The species is characterized by its dioecious nature, with separate male and female plants.
