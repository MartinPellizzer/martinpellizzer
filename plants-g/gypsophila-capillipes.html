Gypsophila capillipes

Gypsophila capillipes, commonly known as the feathery baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fluffy clusters of small white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and tiny, thread-like leaves that give it a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and floral arrangements.

Gypsophila capillipes is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
