Galium minimum

Galium minimum, commonly known as dwarf wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern and produces delicate white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and spreads by stolons, forming dense mats.

Galium minimum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
