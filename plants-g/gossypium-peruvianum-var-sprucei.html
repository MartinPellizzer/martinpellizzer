Gossypium peruvianum var. sprucei

Gossypium peruvianum var. sprucei, commonly known as the Peruvian cotton plant, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that produces small, fluffy fibers used for textile purposes. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments with cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

This variety is valued for its unique fiber characteristics and ecological significance in its native habitat.
