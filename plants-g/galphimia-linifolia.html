Galphimia linifolia

Galphimia linifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf bush or silky bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters, featuring slender, often hairy stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptability to various soil types and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Galphimia linifolia is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
