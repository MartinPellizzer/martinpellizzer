Guaiacum sanctum

Guaiacum sanctum, commonly known as the sacred guaiacum, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in terminal inflorescences.

The bark and wood of Guaiacum sanctum have been historically valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.
