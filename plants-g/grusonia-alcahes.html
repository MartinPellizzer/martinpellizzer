Grusonia alcahes

Grusonia alcahes, commonly known as the Alcahest plant, is a rare and unique species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant has a compact growth habit, often forming dense clusters that thrive in cool, moist environments. Its botanical significance lies in its ability to produce a rare alkaloid compound, which has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
