Geissorhiza gracilis

Geissorhiza gracilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, fragrant orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in areas with moderate to high rainfall. Its blooming period occurs during the late winter to early spring, adding a touch of color to the landscape.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts.
