Gigantochloa albopilosa

Gigantochloa albopilosa, commonly known as the white-veined bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that are often used in construction and crafts due to their strength and flexibility. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive white or pale green stripe running along the midrib. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its rapid growth makes it both a useful resource and a potential invasive species in some regions.
