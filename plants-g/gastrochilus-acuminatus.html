Gastrochilus acuminatus

Gastrochilus acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-lipped orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Thailand, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically ovate and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its unique appearance.

Gastrochilus acuminatus is often found in humid, shaded environments, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates.
