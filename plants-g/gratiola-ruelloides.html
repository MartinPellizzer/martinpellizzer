Gratiola ruelloides

Gratiola ruelloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Gratiola ruelloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
