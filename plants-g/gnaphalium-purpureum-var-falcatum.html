Gnaphalium purpureum var. falcatum

Gnaphalium purpureum var. falcatum, commonly known as the purple cudweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which appear in late summer, are pale purple to white and are surrounded by bracts that give the plant its distinctive appearance.
