Geranium yesoense f. lobatodentatum

Geranium yesoense f. lobatodentatum, commonly known as the Yesso geranium, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its lobed, toothed leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
