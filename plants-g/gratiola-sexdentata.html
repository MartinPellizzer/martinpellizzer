Gratiola sexdentata

Gratiola sexdentata, commonly known as the six-toothed yellowroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces small, yellow flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with six distinct teeth along the edges. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties, though they are not widely used today.

The species is part of the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley.
