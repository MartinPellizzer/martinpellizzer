Galium minus

Galium minus, commonly known as lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium minus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
