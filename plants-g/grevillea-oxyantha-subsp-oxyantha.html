Grevillea oxyantha subsp. oxyantha

Grevillea oxyantha subsp.

oxyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to southwestern Western Australia. It features narrow, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The plant produces striking red or orange inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
