Gaura coloradensis

Gaura coloradensis, commonly known as Colorado gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and western United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and produces clusters of pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Colorado gaura is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
