Gompholobium foliolosum

Gompholobium foliolosum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often found in coastal areas and heathlands. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and bipinnate, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Gompholobium foliolosum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
