Gentianella scopulorum

Gentianella scopulorum, commonly known as the rock gentian, is a flowering plant native to alpine and rocky habitats in Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in crevices and on rocky slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are short and sturdy.

Gentianella scopulorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
