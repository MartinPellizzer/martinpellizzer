Grevillea parallela

Grevillea parallela, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and striking red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to dry, coastal climates.

Grevillea parallela is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
