Gymnadenia longifolia

Gymnadenia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Gymnadenia longifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
