Goodyera maculata

Goodyera maculata, commonly known as the spotted potterscale, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often adorned with distinctive white or yellow spots. The plant produces intricate, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a spike, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands.

Goodyera maculata is a low-growing plant with a rhizomatous root system, making it well adapted to its woodland habitat.
