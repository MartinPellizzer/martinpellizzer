Geranium sanguineum var. angustilobum

Geranium sanguineum var. angustilobum, commonly known as narrow-leaved cranesbill, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and pointed, with a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
