Gypsophila aretioides var. caucasica

Gypsophila aretioides var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian Baby's Breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its delicate, fluffy clusters of small white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its ethereal appearance and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
