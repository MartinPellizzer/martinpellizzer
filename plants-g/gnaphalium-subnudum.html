Gnaphalium subnudum

Gnaphalium subnudum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

It is known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
