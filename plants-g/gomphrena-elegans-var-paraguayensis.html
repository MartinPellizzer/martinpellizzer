Gomphrena elegans var. paraguayensis

Gomphrena elegans var. paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, spiky flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is known for its long-lasting blooms, which remain intact even when dried, making it popular in floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in grasslands and open areas.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.
