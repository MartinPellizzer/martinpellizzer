Gladiolus cygneus

Gladiolus cygneus, commonly known as the swan gladiolus, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves that are broad and pointed, contributing to its dramatic appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of flowers, with each bloom having three prominent petals and three sepals.

The swan gladiolus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
