Gilia crassifolia subvar. modesta

Gilia crassifolia subvar. modesta, commonly known as the narrowleaf gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface. This subshrub thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
