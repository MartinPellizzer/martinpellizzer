Galeatella gloria-montis var. longibracteata

Galeatella gloria-montis var. longibracteata, commonly known as the Montezuma's glory bell, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, bract-like leaves that give it its varietal name, "longibracteata.". The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. Native to the high elevations of Mexico, it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun.

Its unique morphology and striking appearance make it a sought-after ornamental plant in specialized horticultural collections.
