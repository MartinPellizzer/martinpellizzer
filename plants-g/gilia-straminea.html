Gilia straminea

Gilia straminea, commonly known as the yellow gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Mirabilis family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Gilia straminea is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
