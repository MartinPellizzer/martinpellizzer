Gerardia fasciculata f. albiflora

Gerardia fasciculata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered gerardia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
