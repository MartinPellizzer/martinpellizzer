Gerascanthus viridis

Gerascanthus viridis, commonly known as the green geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Gerascanthus viridis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
