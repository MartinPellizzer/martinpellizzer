Grammitis barbatula

Grammitis barbatula, commonly known as the hairy maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. The fronds are typically divided into small, lance-shaped segments, and the plant produces sporangia on the underside of the leaves.

Grammitis barbatula is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks in humid climates.
