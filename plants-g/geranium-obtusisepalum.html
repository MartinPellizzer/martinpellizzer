Geranium obtusisepalum

Geranium obtusisepalum, commonly known as the broad-sepal geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are usually rounded or heart-shaped, with a velvety texture.

Geranium obtusisepalum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
