Gnetum parvifolium

Gnetum parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf gnetum, is a member of the Gnetophyta division, which includes gymnosperms.

It is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically small and ovate in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers and has a unique reproductive system that involves both male and female structures on the same plant.

Gnetum parvifolium is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
