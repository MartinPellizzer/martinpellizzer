Gardenia fasciculata

Gardenia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered gardenia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers are highly valued for their intense fragrance and are often used in perfumery.

The fruit is a small, round capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
