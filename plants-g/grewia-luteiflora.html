Grewia luteiflora

Grewia luteiflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered bush, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, opposite leaves that are usually gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its fruits are small, red drupes that develop after the flowers, providing food for wildlife.

Grewia luteiflora is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
