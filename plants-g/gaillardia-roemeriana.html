Gaillardia roemeriana

Gaillardia roemeriana, commonly known as the prairie coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It is characterized by its bright, daisy-like flowers with red, orange, and yellow hues, often featuring a dark central cone. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with hairy stems that help reduce water loss.

Gaillardia roemeriana is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.
