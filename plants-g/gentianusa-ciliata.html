Gentianusa ciliata

Gentianusa ciliata, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have fine, hairy margins.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
