Gentiana amoena

Gentiana amoena, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Gentiana amoena is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
