Gilia capitata var. staminea

Gilia capitata var. staminea, commonly known as the staminated gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, bright red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as desert scrub and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are often hairy.

The species is notable for its high floral density and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
