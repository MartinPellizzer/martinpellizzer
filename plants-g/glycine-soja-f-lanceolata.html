Glycine soja f. lanceolata

Glycine soja f. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate soybean, is a variant of the soybean plant characterized by its lance-shaped leaves.

This subspecies is native to East Asia and is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various culinary and industrial applications. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are rich in protein and oil, making it an important crop in agriculture.

The lanceolate form distinguishes it from other soybean varieties through its unique leaf shape and growth habit.
