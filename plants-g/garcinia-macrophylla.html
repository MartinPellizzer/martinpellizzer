Garcinia macrophylla

Garcinia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant Garcinia or African mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruit has a thick, segmented rind and a juicy, sweet-tart pulp.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
