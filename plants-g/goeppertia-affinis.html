Goeppertia affinis

Goeppertia affinis, commonly known as the silver arrow plant, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, meaning it thrives when mounted on trees or in humid, shaded environments. It prefers warm temperatures and high humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its foliage provides an ornamental appeal, making it a favored plant among collectors and enthusiasts.
