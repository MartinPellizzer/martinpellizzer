Gentiana verna subsp. aestiva

Gentiana verna subsp.

aestiva, commonly known as the early gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and bear the showy flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in its native habitats.
