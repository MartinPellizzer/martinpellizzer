Gentianopsis komarovii

Gentianopsis komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which is characterized by its showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers are tubular and often have a striking blue or violet color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its beauty and ecological significance.
