Gentiana foliosa var. laxa

Gentiana foliosa var. laxa, commonly known as the laxa gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
