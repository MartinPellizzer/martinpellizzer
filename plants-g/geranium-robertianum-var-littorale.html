Geranium robertianum var. littorale

Geranium robertianum var. littorale, commonly known as the sea geranium, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salty, windy coastal environments.

Its ornamental value and hardiness make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
