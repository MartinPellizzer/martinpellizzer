Geum canadense var. grimesii

Geum canadense var. grimesii, commonly known as Grimes' avens, is a North American perennial herb belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are palmately lobed and its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. It produces small, oval fruits called achenes, which are dispersed by wind.

Grimes' avens is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
