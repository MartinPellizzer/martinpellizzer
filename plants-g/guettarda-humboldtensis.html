Guettarda humboldtensis

Guettarda humboldtensis, commonly known as the Humboldt's guettarda, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Guettarda humboldtensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
