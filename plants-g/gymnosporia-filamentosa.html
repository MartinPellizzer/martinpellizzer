Gymnosporia filamentosa

Gymnosporia filamentosa, commonly known as the slender filaments plant, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are arranged in racemes, followed by elongated pods containing seeds. Gymnosporia filamentosa is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of semi-arid regions.
