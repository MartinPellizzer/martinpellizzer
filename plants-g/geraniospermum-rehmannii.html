Geraniospermum rehmannii

Geraniospermum rehmannii, commonly known as the desert geranium, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and purple, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater in dry soils.

Geraniospermum rehmannii is often found in rocky habitats and is well-suited to survive in extreme desert conditions.
