Gomphrena celosioides var. aureiflora

Gomphrena celosioides var. aureiflora, commonly known as the golden flame gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its striking, papery bracts that range in color from pink to deep red. The flowers are typically clustered in dense, round inflorescences, which give the plant its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

This variety is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
