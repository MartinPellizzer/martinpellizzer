Glyceria distans var. festuciformis

Glyceria distans var. festuciformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf glyceria, is a grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. This variety typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and rivers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
