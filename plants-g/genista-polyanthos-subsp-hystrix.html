Genista polyanthos subsp. hystrix

Genista polyanthos subsp.

hystrix, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of small yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has wiry, thornless stems that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. Its leaves are narrow, evergreen, and arranged in pairs along the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
