Gentiana alpina var. transiens

Gentiana alpina var. transiens, commonly known as the Transylvanian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, downy coating.

The flowers are tubular with two lips, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
