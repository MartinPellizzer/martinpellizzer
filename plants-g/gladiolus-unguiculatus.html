Gladiolus unguiculatus

Gladiolus unguiculatus, commonly known as the African gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow, with narrow, pointed petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a cut flower.
