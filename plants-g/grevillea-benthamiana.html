Grevillea benthamiana

Grevillea benthamiana, commonly known as the Wimmera grevillea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought. It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar adaptations.

Grevillea benthamiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
