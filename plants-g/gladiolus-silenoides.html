Gladiolus silenoides

Gladiolus silenoides, commonly known as the slender gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces tall, erect stems that bear showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Gladiolus silenoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
