Gardneria multiflora

Gardneria multiflora, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Hawaii.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red, with a distinctive, prominent central cone. The plant has broad, waxy leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-draining soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Gardneria multiflora plays an important role in the ecological and cultural heritage of Hawaii.
