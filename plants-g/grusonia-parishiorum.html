Grusonia parishiorum

Grusonia parishiorum, commonly known as the Paraguay rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark wood and is valued for its aromatic properties. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to overharvesting, it is listed as an endangered species in its native habitat.
