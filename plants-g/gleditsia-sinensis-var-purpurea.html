Gleditsia sinensis var. purpurea

Gleditsia sinensis var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple honeylocust, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that turn a striking reddish-purple color in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

The plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing capability, which benefits soil fertility.
