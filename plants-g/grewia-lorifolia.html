Grewia lorifolia

Grewia lorifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Grewia lorifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
