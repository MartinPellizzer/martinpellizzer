Gentiana saponaria var. froelichii

Gentiana saponaria var. froelichii, commonly known as Froelich's gentian, is a variant of the gentian genus native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are erect and branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
