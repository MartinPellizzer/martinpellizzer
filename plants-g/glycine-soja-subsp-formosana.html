Glycine soja subsp. formosana

Glycine soja subsp.

formosana, commonly known as the Formosan soybean, is a subspecies native to East Asia, particularly Taiwan. It is a leguminous plant characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is adapted to a variety of soil types but thrives in well-drained, fertile conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its agricultural potential and ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
