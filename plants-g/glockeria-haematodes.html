Glockeria haematodes

Glockeria haematodes, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-orange sap, which gives it its common name. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in a short inflorescence.

Glockeria haematodes typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
