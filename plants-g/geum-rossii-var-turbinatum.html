Geum rossii var. turbinatum

Geum rossii var. turbinatum, commonly known as the turbinated avens, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value lies in its compact growth habit and the subtle beauty of its flowers, making it a sought-after addition to rock gardens and alpine collections.
