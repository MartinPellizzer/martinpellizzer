Gymnocalycium baldianum var. raineri

Gymnocalycium baldianum var. raineri, commonly known as the Rainer's moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its globular shape and spiny exterior. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers during the warmer months. Its fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements.
