Gentiana anisodonta

Gentiana anisodonta, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Gentiana anisodonta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
