Galium commune var. sudeticum

Galium commune var. sudeticum, commonly known as the common bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are usually hairy, and it produces tiny, bead-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its presence often indicates a stable, undisturbed ecosystem.
