Gentiana wardii var. emergens

Gentiana wardii var. emergens, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a silver-blue hue on the underside.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and is often found in alpine meadows and subalpine regions.
