Goebelia reticulata

Goebelia reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated goebelia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive, finely divided structure. Goebelia reticulata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and long-lasting blooms.
