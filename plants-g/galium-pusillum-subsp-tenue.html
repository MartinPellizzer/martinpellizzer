Galium pusillum subsp. tenue

Galium pusillum subsp.

tenue, commonly known as slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
