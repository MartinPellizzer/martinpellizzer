Glochidion alticola

Glochidion alticola, commonly known as the mountain glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
