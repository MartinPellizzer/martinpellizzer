Gongora maculata var. tricolor

Gongora maculata var. tricolor, commonly known as the Three-Colored Gongora, is a striking orchid species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tri-colored petals that display shades of red, yellow, and green, creating a visually stunning display. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are highly fragrant and bloom during the wet season, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

The orchid's unique coloration and ecological role make it a prized species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
