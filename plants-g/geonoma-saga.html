Geonoma saga

Geonoma saga, commonly known as the "sago palm," is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of long, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into dark blue to black berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Geonoma saga is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor environments.
