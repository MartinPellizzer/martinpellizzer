Gastrolobium tomentosum

Gastrolobium tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Gastrolobium tomentosum is an important plant in local ecosystems and is also cultivated for its ornamental value.
