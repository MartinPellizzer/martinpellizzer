Grewia oblongifolia

Grewia oblongifolia, commonly known as the roundleaf globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Grewia oblongifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
