Gloriosa sessiliflora

Gloriosa sessiliflora, commonly known as the flame lily or golden lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers, which are typically trumpet-shaped and adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces long, slender leaves that are often spirally arranged along its stem. Its bulbs are toxic and contain compounds such as colchicine, which has been used in medicine.

Gloriosa sessiliflora thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
