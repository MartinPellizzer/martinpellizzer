Galium masafueranum

Galium masafueranum, commonly known as the Masafuera bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, creeping stems covered with fine hairs and small, opposite leaves. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
