Galarhoeus flavicomus

Galarhoeus flavicomus, commonly known as the golden-winged fern, is a rare and delicate species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow fronds, which contrast beautifully with its dark green base. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures are small and clustered, producing spores rather than seeds.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
