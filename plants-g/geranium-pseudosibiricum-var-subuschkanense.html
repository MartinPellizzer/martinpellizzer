Geranium pseudosibiricum var. subuschkanense

Geranium pseudosibiricum var. subuschkanense, commonly known as the Siberian geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are typically lobed with a serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental choice for temperate climates.
