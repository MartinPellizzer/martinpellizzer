Gossia aphthosa subsp. longipedunculata

Gossia aphthosa subsp.

longipedunculata, commonly known as the long pedunculate gossia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae. It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is native to Australia.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

This subspecies is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is commonly found in woodland and shrubland habitats.
