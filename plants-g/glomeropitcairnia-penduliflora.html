Glomeropitcairnia penduliflora

Glomeropitcairnia penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flower pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding structure, typical of bromeliads. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, violet flowers that bloom in the spring. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its humid, shaded habitat.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
