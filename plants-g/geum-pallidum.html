Geum pallidum

Geum pallidum, commonly known as pale avens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish to reddish, and appear in clusters at the base of the plant.

Geum pallidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for various insects.
