Gaillardia bicolor

Gaillardia bicolor, commonly known as the Indian blanket flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with red and yellow petals that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically green. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Gaillardia bicolor is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
