Gentiana pubiflora

Gentiana pubiflora, commonly known as the pubescent gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers are characterized by their radial symmetry and are often surrounded by short, hairy bracts.

Gentiana pubiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
