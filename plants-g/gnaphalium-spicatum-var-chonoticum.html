Gnaphalium spicatum var. chonoticum

Gnaphalium spicatum var. chonoticum, commonly known as the Chonos mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant produces small, fluffy, white to pale yellow flowers that are often used in floral arrangements due to their delicate appearance. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cool, moist conditions.

Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its ability to retain moisture in harsh alpine climates.
