Gymnopogon aristiglumis

Gymnopogon aristiglumis, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its seeds are often used in traditional herbal remedies and as a forage crop for livestock.

Gymnopogon aristiglumis is adapted to grow in dry, sunny environments and is relatively drought-tolerant.
