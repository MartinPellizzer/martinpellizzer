Gladiolus papilionaceus

Gladiolus papilionaceus, commonly known as the butterfly gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, butterfly-like blooms that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and a robust stem. The species is also valued for its adaptability to different soil types and its ability to thrive in a range of climatic conditions.
