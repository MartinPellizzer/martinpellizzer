Gleichenia barbula

Gleichenia barbula, commonly known as the hairy Gleichenia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, and often hairy fronds that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its sporangia are clustered in sori, which are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Gleichenia barbula is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit.
