Grewia sepiaria

Grewia sepiaria, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It produces small, bright yellow berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

The flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the cooler months.
