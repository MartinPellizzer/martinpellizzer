Grewia ectasicarpa

Grewia ectasicarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them useful in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
