Galactia graminea

Galactia graminea, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, dark seeds that are rich in protein and used in various traditional foods. It has a fibrous root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Galactia graminea is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its use in agroforestry systems.
