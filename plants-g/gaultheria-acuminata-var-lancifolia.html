Gaultheria acuminata var. lancifolia

Gaultheria acuminata var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and contain aromatic oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in herbal remedies.
