Gnaphalium sylvaticum f. rariflorum

Gnaphalium sylvaticum f. rariflorum, commonly known as the rare-flowered wood sage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded grasslands.

This subspecies is noted for its limited distribution and is considered a rare and localized flora in certain regions of Europe.
