Gilia diffusa

Gilia diffusa, commonly known as desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and can often be found in desert scrub and grassland habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gilia diffusa is an important component of its ecosystem, providing nectar and habitat for various wildlife species.
