Grindelia robusta var. platyphylla

Grindelia robusta var. platyphylla, commonly known as the flatleaf greasebush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its aromatic, resinous properties. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal sage scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native ecosystem.
