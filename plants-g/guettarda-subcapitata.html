Guettarda subcapitata

Guettarda subcapitata, commonly known as the silky sarsapilla, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and moist, shaded environments. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Guettarda subcapitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
