Gymnema

Gymnema, commonly known as the "sugar plant," is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of India and Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is renowned for its unique ability to block the taste of sweetness, a property attributed to the presence of gymnemic acids. Gymnema's leaves contain various bioactive compounds that have been studied for their potential in managing blood sugar levels.

It is widely used in Ayurvedic medicine for its purported health benefits.
