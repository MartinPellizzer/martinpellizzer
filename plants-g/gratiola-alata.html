Gratiola alata

Gratiola alata, commonly known as the winged bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive winged stems and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. Gratiola alata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
