Gynopogon rosmarinifolius

Gynopogon rosmarinifolius, commonly known as the rosemary-leaved gynopogon, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, needle-like leaves that resemble those of rosemary, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.

Gynopogon rosmarinifolius thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
