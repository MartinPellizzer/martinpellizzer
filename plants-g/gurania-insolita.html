Gurania insolita

Gurania insolita, commonly known as the rare desert orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom during the brief rainy season. The plant has adapted to survive in extreme conditions with specialized root systems that store water efficiently. Its leaves are small and succulent, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Due to its limited habitat and threats from climate change, Gurania insolita is considered a vulnerable species.
