Glycosmis cochinchinensis var. contracta

Glycosmis cochinchinensis var. contracta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweet orange.

This plant is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam and southern China. It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and edible, orange-colored fruits. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, giving it its varietal name.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent due to its aromatic properties.
