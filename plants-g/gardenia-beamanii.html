Gardenia beamanii

Gardenia beamanii, commonly known as the Beaman gardenia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces waxy, egg-shaped fruits that contain a single seed, typical of the Rubiaceae family. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
