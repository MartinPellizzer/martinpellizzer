Grewia eriopoda

Grewia eriopoda, commonly known as the woolly bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Grewia eriopoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
