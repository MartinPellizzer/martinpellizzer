Galium serotinum

Galium serotinum, commonly known as late wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple tinge on the underside. Its small, white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late summer to autumn.

Galium serotinum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
