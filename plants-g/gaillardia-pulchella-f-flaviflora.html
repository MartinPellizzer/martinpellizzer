Gaillardia pulchella f. flaviflora

Gaillardia pulchella f. flaviflora, commonly known as the Florida blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange and yellow ray florets, which contrast with its dark red to black central disc. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early fall, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

The species is known for its adaptability to wet environments and its role in supporting local pollinators.
