Galeopsis tetrahit var. pallens

Galeopsis tetrahit var. pallens, commonly known as the pale hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
