Guidonia coriacea

Guidonia coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf guignardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny environments.
