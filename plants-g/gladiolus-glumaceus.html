Gladiolus glumaceus

Gladiolus glumaceus, commonly known as the spike-lilac, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, upright stems that bear dense clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its flowers are typically borne in spikes, with each bloom having three petals and three sepals.

Gladiolus glumaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and long-lasting blooms.
