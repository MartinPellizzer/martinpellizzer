Gentiana scabra subvar. orientalis

Gentiana scabra subvar. orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
