Gentiana capitata var. strobiliformis

Gentiana capitata var. strobiliformis, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has a compact, rosette-like growth habit with basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in alpine meadows and rocky soils, preferring cool, moist environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in alpine ecosystems.
