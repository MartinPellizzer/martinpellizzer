Gastrochilus ampullaceus

Gastrochilus ampullaceus, commonly known as the bladder orchid, is a unique orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, bladder-like pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers with a lip that resembles a small pouch, aiding in pollination by specific insects. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The botanical features of Gastrochilus ampullaceus make it a fascinating subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
