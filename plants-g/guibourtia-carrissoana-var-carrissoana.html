Guibourtia carrissoana var. carrissoana

Guibourtia carrissoana var. carrissoana, commonly known as African teak, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and bipinnate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its durable, heavy timber used in furniture and construction.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
