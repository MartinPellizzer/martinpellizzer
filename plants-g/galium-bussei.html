Galium bussei

Galium bussei, commonly known as the Florida bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are oval to lance-shaped. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Galium bussei thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.
