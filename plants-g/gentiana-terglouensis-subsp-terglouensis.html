Gentiana terglouensis subsp. terglouensis

Gentiana terglouensis subsp.

terglouensis, commonly known as the Terglou gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
