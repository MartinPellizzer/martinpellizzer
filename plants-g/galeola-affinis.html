Galeola affinis

Galeola affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, strap-like leaves and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and substrate.

Galeola affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
