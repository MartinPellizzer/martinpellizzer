Glandula leontina

Glandula leontina, commonly known as the lion's tooth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaves that resemble the teeth of a lion. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Glandula leontina thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
