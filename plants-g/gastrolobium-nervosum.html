Gastrolobium nervosum

Gastrolobium nervosum, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in spikes during the autumn season. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

Gastrolobium nervosum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which benefits soil fertility.
