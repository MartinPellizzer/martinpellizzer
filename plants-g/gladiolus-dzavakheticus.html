Gladiolus dzavakheticus

Gladiolus dzavakheticus, commonly known as the Dzavakhetic gladiolus, is a species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, spike-like inflorescence composed of numerous brightly colored flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This species is valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
