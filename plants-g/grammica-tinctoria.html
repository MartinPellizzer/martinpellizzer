Grammica tinctoria

Grammica tinctoria, commonly known as the woad plant, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces a blue dye from its leaves, which has been historically used for textile coloring. Grammica tinctoria thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its historical significance in dye production dates back to ancient times.
