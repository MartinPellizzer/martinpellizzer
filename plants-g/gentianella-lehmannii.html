Gentianella lehmannii

Gentianella lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's gentian, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are usually unbranched and hairy.

Gentianella lehmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
