Gloxinia maxima

Gloxinia maxima, commonly known as the giant gloxinia, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gloxinia maxima is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
