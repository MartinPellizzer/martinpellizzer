Gardenia ternifolia subsp. ternifolia

Gardenia ternifolia subsp.

ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leaf gardenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in whorls of three, giving it its common name.

The plant produces fragrant, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
