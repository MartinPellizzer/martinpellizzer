Gladiolus spicatus

Gladiolus spicatus, commonly known as the spike gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense spikes of brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late summer. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gladiolus spicatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
