Gomphocarpus buchwaldii

Gomphocarpus buchwaldii, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and milky sap, which contains toxic compounds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a rough texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.
