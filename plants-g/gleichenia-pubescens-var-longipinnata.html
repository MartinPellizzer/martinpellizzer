Gleichenia pubescens var. longipinnata

Gleichenia pubescens var. longipinnata, commonly known as the long-pinnate maiden hair fern, is a variant of the Gleichenia genus within the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its long, slender fronds that are pinnately divided, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rock crevices, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, which contribute to its common name and aid in moisture retention.

This fern is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under suitable conditions.
