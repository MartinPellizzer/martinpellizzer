Gardenia formosa

Gardenia formosa, commonly known as the Chinese gardenia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The flowers, which bloom in summer, are highly valued for their ornamental beauty and are often used in floral arrangements.
