Geranium seemannii subsp. repens

Geranium seemannii subsp.

repens, commonly known as the creeping geranium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
