Gelsemium lucidum

Gelsemium lucidum, commonly known as yellow false wild cucumber, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers and smooth, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, green, spherical fruits that turn red when mature. Gelsemium lucidum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.
