Gymnadenia tridentata

Gymnadenia tridentata, commonly known as the three-lobed gentian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture.

Gymnadenia tridentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
