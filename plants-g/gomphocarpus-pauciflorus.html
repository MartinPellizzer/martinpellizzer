Gomphocarpus pauciflorus

Gomphocarpus pauciflorus, commonly known as the milkweed or yellow milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers and milky sap. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branched.

The fruit is a follicle that splits open to release the seeds, which are equipped with silky filaments for wind dispersal.
