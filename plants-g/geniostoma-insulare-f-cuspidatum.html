Geniostoma insulare f. cuspidatum

Geniostoma insulare f. cuspidatum, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows in sandy or saline soils, often found in tidal zones and along shorelines. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a velvety texture, while its stems are slender and often trailing.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging coastal environments.
