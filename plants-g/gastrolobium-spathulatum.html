Gastrolobium spathulatum

Gastrolobium spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive broad, ovate shape and a pale green color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in spikes. It is adapted to sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
