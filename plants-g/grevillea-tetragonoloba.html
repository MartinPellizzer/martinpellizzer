Grevillea tetragonoloba

Grevillea tetragonoloba, commonly known as the purple-leafed grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep purple or reddish hue. The plant produces vibrant, bottlebrush-like inflorescences in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea tetragonoloba is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
