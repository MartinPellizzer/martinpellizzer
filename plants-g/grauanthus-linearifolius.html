Grauanthus linearifolius

Grauanthus linearifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers resembling larkspurs. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but requires specific conditions to flourish.
