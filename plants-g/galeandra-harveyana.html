Galeandra harveyana

Galeandra harveyana, commonly known as the Harvey's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration pattern, often featuring a deep purple or violet hue. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
