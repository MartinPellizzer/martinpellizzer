Gaultheria fragrantissima var. papuana

Gaultheria fragrantissima var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan wintergreen, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or black when mature, containing seeds surrounded by a fleshy pulp. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The leaves and berries contain aromatic compounds, making it a plant of interest for both horticultural and potential medicinal uses.
