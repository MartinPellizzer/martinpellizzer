Gastrochilus pechei

Gastrochilus pechei, commonly known as the Pechei's toothed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, featuring a distinctive, toothed lip that sets it apart from other orchid species. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understorey of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually small, with a vibrant coloration that ranges from yellow to orange.

Gastrochilus pechei is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
