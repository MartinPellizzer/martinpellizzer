Gaura fruticosa

Gaura fruticosa, commonly known as the coral bells, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of pink or white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has a clumping growth habit and features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs. Its foliage is typically gray-green, adding visual interest even when not in bloom.

Gaura fruticosa thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
