Gastrolobium cruciatum

Gastrolobium cruciatum, commonly known as the cross-leaved bushveld wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves that are often divided into pairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in spikes and attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
