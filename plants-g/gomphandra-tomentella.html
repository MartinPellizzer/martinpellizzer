Gomphandra tomentella

Gomphandra tomentella, commonly known as the woolly gomphandra, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a leathery texture with a bluish-green hue.

Gomphandra tomentella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
