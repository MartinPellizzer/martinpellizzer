Gnaphalium luzulifolium

Gnaphalium luzulifolium, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette or along the stem.

Gnaphalium luzulifolium is known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
