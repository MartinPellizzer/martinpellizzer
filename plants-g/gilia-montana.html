Gilia montana

Gilia montana, commonly known as the Sierra daisy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as meadows and mountain slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually erect and branched.

Gilia montana plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies in its native ecosystem.
