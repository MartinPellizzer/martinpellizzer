Guidonia hirsuta

Guidonia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy guidonia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
