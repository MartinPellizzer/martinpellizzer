Gaultheria rufescens

Gaultheria rufescens, commonly known as the red-flowered wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous areas. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a waxy coating. Its most distinctive characteristic is its clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring.

The plant also produces small, dark berries that are edible and have a spicy, minty flavor.
