Galeopsis sulphurea

Galeopsis sulphurea, commonly known as yellow hedge nettle, is a flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stem. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Galeopsis sulphurea is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.
