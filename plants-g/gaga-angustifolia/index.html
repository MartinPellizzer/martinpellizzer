Gaga Angustifolia

Narrowleaf gentian, scientifically known as Gaga angustifolia, is a strikingly beautiful wildflower native to the mountainous regions of Europe. This species is characterized by its deep blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Despite its ornamental appeal, the plant is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves give it its common name, while its resilience to harsh environments makes it a symbol of adaptability in nature. However, habitat loss and climate change pose significant threats to its survival in the wild.
