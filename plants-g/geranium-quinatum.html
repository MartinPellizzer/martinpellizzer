Geranium quinatum

Geranium quinatum, commonly known as the rock geranium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled flowers, typically in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among gardeners.
