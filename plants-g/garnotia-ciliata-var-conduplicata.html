Garnotia ciliata var. conduplicata

Garnotia ciliata var. conduplicata, commonly known as the double-fringed garnet, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or purple flowers, which are often adorned with double rows of fine, ciliate (fringed) petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a deep green color.

This ornamental species is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
