Geranium austroapenninum

Geranium austroapenninum, commonly known as the southern Apennine geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Italy.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of five-petaled, pink to red flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Geranium austroapenninum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
