Gentiana elliottii var. parvifolia

Gentiana elliottii var. parvifolia, commonly known as Elliott's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
