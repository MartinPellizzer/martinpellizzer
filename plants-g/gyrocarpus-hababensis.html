Gyrocarpus hababensis

Gyrocarpus hababensis, commonly known as the African starfruit tree, is a member of the Hippocastanaceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive fruit structure. The plant produces small, yellow, star-shaped fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a tendency to grow in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is often cultivated for its fruit and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
