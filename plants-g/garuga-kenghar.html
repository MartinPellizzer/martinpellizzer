Garuga kenghar

Garuga kenghar, commonly known as the mountain pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine for their medicinal properties. Garuga kenghar thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at higher altitudes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
