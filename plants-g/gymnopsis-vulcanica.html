Gymnopsis vulcanica

Gymnopsis vulcanica, commonly known as the fireroot plant, is a rare and unique species native to the volcanic regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, adapting to the arid and nutrient-poor soils of its habitat. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are succulent and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Gymnopsis vulcanica plays a vital role in stabilizing the soil in volcanic landscapes and supports a variety of local wildlife.
