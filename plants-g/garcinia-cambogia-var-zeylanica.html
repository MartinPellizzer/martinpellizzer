Garcinia cambogia var. zeylanica

Garcinia cambogia var. zeylanica, commonly known as Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, sour fruits that contain a high concentration of hydroxycitric acid (HCA). The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. Its leaves are alternate and oval-shaped, with a glossy green surface.

The fruit's rind is thick and contains a yellowish pulp, which is rich in antioxidants and bioactive compounds.
