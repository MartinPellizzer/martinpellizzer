Gymnadenia odoratissima var. idea

Gymnadenia odoratissima var. idea, commonly known as the sweet gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the inflorescence consists of a single spike of showy flowers.

The species is noted for its strong, sweet fragrance, which is particularly noticeable in the evening.
