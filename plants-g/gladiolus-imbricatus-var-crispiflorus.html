Gladiolus imbricatus var. crispiflorus

Gladiolus imbricatus var. crispiflorus, commonly known as the crinkled-flowered gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear large, showy flowers in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a fan-like pattern, while the inflorescence consists of tightly packed, colorful blooms. This variety is particularly noted for its unique, wavy or crinkled petals that give it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
