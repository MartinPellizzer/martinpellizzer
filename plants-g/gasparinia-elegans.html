Gasparinia elegans

Gasparinia elegans, commonly known as the elegant grass, is a perennial plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses.

Gasparinia elegans is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
