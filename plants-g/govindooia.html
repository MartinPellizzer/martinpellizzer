Govindooia

Govindooia, commonly known as the sacred fig or bodhi tree, is a species of fig tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clustered fruiting structures called syconia. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common sight in tropical regions. Its fruit, which is edible, plays an important role in both local ecosystems and human diets.

The tree also holds cultural and religious significance, particularly in Buddhist traditions.
