Gentiana prostrata subsp. americana

Gentiana prostrata subsp.

americana, commonly known as the American gentian, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America. It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September.

The plant has prostrate stems that lie close to the ground, often forming dense mats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
