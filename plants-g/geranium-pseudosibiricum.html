Geranium pseudosibiricum

Geranium pseudosibiricum, commonly known as the Siberian geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
