Glochidion tomentosum

Glochidion tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and covered in fine white hairs.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible when ripe.
