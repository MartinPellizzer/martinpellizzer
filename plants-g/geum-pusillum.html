Geum pusillum

Geum pusillum, commonly known as dwarf buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like shape, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Geum pusillum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
