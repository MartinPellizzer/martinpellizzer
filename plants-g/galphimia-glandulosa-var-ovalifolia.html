Galphimia glandulosa var. ovalifolia

Galphimia glandulosa var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the ovalleaf gumbo, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets, giving it its varietal name. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and are attractive to pollinators.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
