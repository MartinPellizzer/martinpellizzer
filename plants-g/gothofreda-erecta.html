Gothofreda erecta

Gothofreda erecta, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and silver-gray foliage, which reflects sunlight to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, blooming during the cooler months. Gothofreda erecta is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and thick, waxy leaves.

It is often found in rocky soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
