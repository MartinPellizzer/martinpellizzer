Gentiana pratensis

Gentiana pratensis, commonly known as the meadow gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms from June to August, producing showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gentiana pratensis is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
