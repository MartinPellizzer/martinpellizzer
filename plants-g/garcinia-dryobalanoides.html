Garcinia dryobalanoides

Garcinia dryobalanoides, commonly known as the African yellow wood, is a tropical tree native to central and eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale yellow bark and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Garcinia dryobalanoides is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
