Galium nuttallii subsp. ovalifolium

Galium nuttallii subsp.

ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaf bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters.

The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a distinctive hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This subspecies is part of the Rubiaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental members.
