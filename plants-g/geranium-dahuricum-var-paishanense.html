Geranium dahuricum var. paishanense

Geranium dahuricum var. paishanense, commonly known as the Paishan geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of northeastern China.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a slightly serrated edge.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
