Goldfussia colorata

Goldfussia colorata, commonly known as the colorful golden bell, is a striking perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from golden yellow to deep orange, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant seasonal color.

The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
