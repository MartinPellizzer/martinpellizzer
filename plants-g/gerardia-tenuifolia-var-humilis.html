Gerardia tenuifolia var. humilis

Gerardia tenuifolia var. humilis, commonly known as the slenderleaf gerardia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of South America.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
