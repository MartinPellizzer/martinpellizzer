Guioa collina

Guioa collina, commonly known as the white guioa, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, green fruits that turn yellow when mature. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering, making it a popular choice in landscaping.

Guioa collina is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
