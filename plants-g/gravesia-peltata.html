Gravesia peltata

Gravesia peltata, commonly known as the broad-leafed gravesia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Gravesia peltata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
