Gothofreda integriloba

Gothofreda integriloba, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or lavender in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Gothofreda integriloba is a perennial vine that blooms from late spring to early summer.
