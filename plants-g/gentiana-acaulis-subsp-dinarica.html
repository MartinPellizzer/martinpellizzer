Gentiana acaulis subsp. dinarica

Gentiana acaulis subsp.

dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Dinaric Alps. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant has a short, compact stem with few leaves, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are clustered in a terminal inflorescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
