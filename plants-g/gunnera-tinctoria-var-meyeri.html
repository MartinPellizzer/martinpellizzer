Gunnera tinctoria var. meyeri

Gunnera tinctoria var. meyeri, commonly known as the Mexican giant water shield, is a striking perennial plant native to Central America.

It features large, shield-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter, with a waxy, dark green surface. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it forms dense colonies. Its leaves are typically notched and have a distinctive, almost prehistoric appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its dramatic foliage and ability to create a bold statement in moist garden settings.
