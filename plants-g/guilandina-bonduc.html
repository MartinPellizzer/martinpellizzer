Guilandina bonduc

Guilandina bonduc, commonly known as the monkey orchid, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its woody vine structure and pinnate leaves with small, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a woody, elongated pod that splits open to release seeds.

Guilandina bonduc is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
