Gentiana tenella var. monantha

Gentiana tenella var. monantha, commonly known as the single-flowered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers are tubular with two lips, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
