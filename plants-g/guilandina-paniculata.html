Guilandina paniculata

Guilandina paniculata, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It produces striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive red or purple calyx. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral displays.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
