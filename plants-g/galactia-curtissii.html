Galactia curtissii

Galactia curtissii, commonly known as the Texas milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow to brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Galactia curtissii is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
